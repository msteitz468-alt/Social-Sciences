### Claim 75: Midwest prairie transition and Serengeti analogy
- **Register:** finding
- **Claim:** By ~13,000–11,500 BC the midwest shifted from sodden spruce mosaic toward drying, droughts and Younger Dryas-exacerbated grasslands; mammoths, camels, horse, bison and others exploited new prairies that Mithen likens to a North American Serengeti.
- **Attribution:** Mithen 2003, ch.26
- **Grounding quote:** "Periodic droughts arrived at 11,500 BC, which were exacerbated by the onset of the Younger Dryas… The grazing mammals – mammoths, camels, horse, bison and many others – exploited the new prairies, eagerly turning them into a North American Serengeti."
- **Target pages:** [[megafaunal-extinctions]], [[younger-dryas]], [[clovis-culture]]
- **Action:** UPDATE
- **Notes:** Environmental context for later Clovis–mammoth associations in southwest.
