### C-8-001: Dominant relational schema produces ethnographically reported “values”
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues that prevalence of a relational schema leads members of a collective to adopt typical, repeated behavior patterns that ethnographers often describe as normative “values” orienting social life (e.g. Matsiguenga/Ojibwa sharing; Jivaro bellicosity; Tukano exchange obligation).
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-relation]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]], [[descola-philippe]]
- **Grounding quote:** "The prevalence of a relational schema in a collective leads its members to adopt typical behavior patterns, the repetition and frequency of which are such that ethnographers who observe and interpret them feel justified in describing them overall as normative “values” that orientate social life." (Ethos of Collectives)
- **Notes:** Framing of values as effects of relational schemas, not free-floating culture traits.

### C-8-003: Jivaro predation as moral horizon with nested exchange niche
- **Register:** THEORY / FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola argues Jivaro predatory assimilation is a moral horizon orienting many practices, not totalizing daily life; it tolerates minor schemas (Dravidian sister-exchange among cross-cousins) as islands of reciprocity that enable predation toward more distant affines—while affinity’s fracturing potential continually threatens that balance.
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-relation]], [[alliance-theory]], [[reciprocity]]
- **Grounding quote:** "The schema of predatory assimilation constitutes, rather, a moral horizon that orientates many fields of practice… Thus, the Jivaro kinship system, which is of the Dravidian type, is founded on the ideal model of an exchange of sisters between cross-cousins… indispensable for the development of a predatory attitude toward more distant affines."
- **Notes:** Ethnographic illustration of dominant/minor relational nesting.

### C-8-004: Tukano predation subordinated to exchange
- **Register:** FINDING / THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola reports that among Tukanos, wife-raids (often regularized into alliance) and rare distant killings occur, but predation is not the dominant schema; Makuna eaters/eaten relations (Århem) are construed as exchange modeled on affinity, not pure predation; Campa gift schema is set against a predation background managed by exchange with like neighbors.

- **Notes:** Århem cited as Descola’s ethnographic source; do not invent Århem thinker page.

### C-8-008: Collective vs. Benedict “patterns of culture”
- **Register:** THEORY / POSITION
- **Claim:** Descola contrasts his approach with Benedict’s: instead of preassigned group limits given abstract transcendent unity, one should map fields covered by schemas assembling collectives of variable size, whose frontiers reflect breaks between ways of being present in the world—avoiding essentialism and emic self-other markers as automatic analytic units.
- **Page targets:** [[collectives-including-nonhumans]], [[culture]], [[comparative-method]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "This way of proceeding is the reverse of that which Benedict adopts in order to reveal her “patterns” of culture; instead of casting one’s eye over a group with preassigned limits… it is better to seek out a field covered by certain schemas… the frontiers between which are not fixed by custom or by law but simply reflect the breaks that separate them from other ways of being present in the world."
- **Notes:** Benedict cited as foil; no Benedict page—fold under culture/comparative-method.

### C-8-037: Amazonian sealed dual animal classes block transfer to domestication
- **Register:** FINDING / THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola illustrates priority of identification with tropical SA dual modalities: provisional protection of tamed individuals vs. absolute subordination of European dogs/chickens—game cannot slip into domesticating relationship; transfer runs the other way (feral cattle become game), never game→domesticate.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** "it was more or less inconceivable that animals hunted as game could be slipped into a domesticating relationship… feral cattle that have reverted to a wild state have been turned into game animals by Amerindians, but there is no record of any game animals being turned into domesticated ones."
- **Notes:** Empirical support for C-8-036.

### C-8-038: Usual path of change: marginal schema subsumes preeminent class of beings
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues persistent total resistance is rare; more usually a preeminent class of beings is gradually subsumed into a previously marginal relational schema that becomes preponderant (e.g. Siberian protective attitude erodes person-to-person animist patterns into folklore)—but only if exceptional events inhibit the inhibitors of change, and only if terms are new or already weakened.
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-relation]], [[four-ontologies]], [[animism]]
- **Grounding quote:** "More usually, a class of existing beings that is preeminent… finds itself gradually subsumed into a relational schema that was previously marginal and that now comes to acquire a preponderant position… such a mutation can only come about if the mechanisms that inhibit change have themselves been inhibited by the consequences of events sufficiently exceptional…"
- **Notes:** Contingency + structural compatibility.

### C-8-039: Reconstruction constrained by limited architectonic plans
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola holds lasting relational transformation requires tumultuous openings to contingency, but reorganization follows limited organizing rules and compatibility principles—destruction is multifarious; reconstruction uses available materials and few architectonic plans; surface diversity is “ornamentation.”
- **Page targets:** [[four-ontologies]], [[structuralism]], [[modes-of-relation]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Destruction can be wreaked in a thousand different ways, but reconstruction can be achieved only with whatever materials are available and by following a limited number of plans that respect the architectonic constraints peculiar to any edifice. All the rest… is no more than ornamentation."
- **Notes:** Closes Ch.15; structuralist constraint on history.

### C-8-040: Against etiological explanations; comparativist difference questions
- **Register:** META / THEORY
- **Claim:** Epilogue: Descola criticizes anthropology’s fascination with etiological explanations (evolutionist genesis, descriptive history, adaptive function, archetypes) for failing the key question—why a social fact is present here not there, and why combinations cohabit or exclude; “pointless” questions (no totemic royalty; nonhumans not in parliaments; Inca/Pharaoh not eating enemies; shamans not sacrificing) are the ones that matter.
- **Page targets:** [[comparative-method]], [[anthropology]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "they do not really make it possible to answer the only question that matters: namely why is a particular social fact, belief, or custom present in one place but not in another?… Why is there no totemic royalty? Why are nonhumans not represented in parliaments… Those are… the questions that matter when one tries to account for differences in the ways of inhabiting the world…"
- **Notes:** Methodological manifesto for combinatorial ontology.

### C-8-041: Four identifications × six relations; not all 24 realized
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola restates the program: four modes of identification and six relational modes yield twenty-four theoretical configurations, but many are fanciful/utopian; he limits himself to types of compatibility and incompatibility rather than exhaustive chemistry-like systematization.
- **Page targets:** [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "I have chosen to examine four modes of identification and six relational modes, so, for the picture to be complete, it would be necessary to review the twenty-four configurations… some of those combinations are fanciful and only exist in the domains of Utopia or the pages of science fiction…"
- **Notes:** Scope limit of the combinatorial matrix.

### C-8-042: Animism and naturalism as antithetical identification schemas
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: animism emphasizes physical difference (dissimilar bodies) with similar interrelations (shared interiority); naturalism emphasizes physical continuity under natural laws with heterogeneous relations depending on capacity for interiority.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[naturalism-ontology]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[four-ontologies]], [[nature-culture-distinction]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Animism lays the emphasis on the physical differences between existing beings… while recognizing that they maintain similar interrelations… Naturalism, on the contrary, lays the emphasis on the physical continuity between the world’s elements… the better to note the heterogeneity of the relations that may bring them together…"
- **Notes:** Core ontology definition pair.

### C-8-043: Animism compatible with reversible subject–subject relations only
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues animist unrestricted intersubjective sociability supports only potentially reversible relations between equal-status subjects—predation, exchange, gift—whose realization does not alter identities; production, transmission, protection remain marginal because they create hierarchy/dependency and ontological disparity.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[gift-exchange]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the only structuring relations possible are those that operate with potentially reversible links between subjects… predation, exchange, or gift giving. Conversely, intransitive relations of the production, transmission, or protection type are bound to remain marginal… With gift giving, exchange, and predation one subject ratifies the other; with production, protection, and transmission, the subject establishes a dependent subject or a subordinate object."
- **Notes:** Compatibility matrix—animism column.

### C-8-044: Achuar predation, Tukano exchange, Campa gift leave interlocutor identities intact
- **Register:** THEORY / FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola illustrates: Achuar hunter’s predatory affinity actualizes without modifying ontological properties of the animal; likewise Tukano exchange and Campa gift; contrasts Modern production as agent imposing form on non-autonomous matter—hence animist artifacts (Wayana baskets) are transformed subjects, not ex nihilo craft production.
- **Page targets:** [[achuar]], [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[naturalism-ontology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "an Achuar hunter regards the animal that he pursues as an alter ego… he modifies neither the ontological properties of his interlocutor nor the nature of the relations… it is mistaken to speak of “craft production” in the case of animism: the artifacts here are… transformed subjects that preserve some of their original ontological attributes."
- **Notes:** Achuar page link warranted.

### C-8-045: Protection and transmission marginal/incompatible in animism
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: protection (control of reproduction/feeding) strips protected subjects of independence and can require a new ontology if generalized; transmission (living’s dependence on dead via differentiated lineages/ancestors) eliminates treating animals/plants as subjects and contradicts animist species-tribes re-creating existence through interaction—hence wipe dead from memory, destroy possessions.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]]
- **Grounding quote:** "protection cannot constitute a general relational schema suited to animism… As for transmission… it instantly eliminates the possibility of treating animals and plants as subjects… That is why it is necessary to wipe the dead from memory and to destroy their meager possessions…"
- **Notes:** Negative template of animism.

### C-8-046: Animist “absences” are not lacks
- **Register:** THEORY / POSITION
- **Claim:** Descola lists absences across animist territory (no exclusive livestock raisers, craft castes, ancestor cults, lineage moral persons, demiurges, material patrimonies, heredity obsession, arrow of time, wide filiation, deliberative assemblies)—observers read “lacks,” but they are prices of populating the world with subjects.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "By rejecting or marginalizing certain relations, animism provides a negative template of all that it rejects… Some perspicacious observers who have noticed those absences have interpreted them as lacks. But they are, of course, nothing of the kind."
- **Notes:** Anti-deficit model of nonmodern societies.

### C-8-047: Naturalism: attenuated historical transmission; no hegemonic relation
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues Modern naturalism’s historical consciousness is attenuated transmission (dead as benign puppets, not despotic ancestors); distinctive human interiority prevents any single relational mode from schematizing all interactions—relations split human–human vs. human–nonhuman.
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[nature-culture-distinction]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the singularity that is ascribed to humans on account of their distinctive interiority has the effect of preventing any relational mode from occupying a hegemonic position: some relations are deemed suitable for connections between humans, others for connections with nonhumans, but none has the power to schematize the principal interactions between all the world’s elements."
- **Notes:** Explains naturalism’s plural hybridity.

### C-8-048: Modern exchange/protection exclude nonhumans; commodity fetishism incomplete
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: mercantile exchange and state protection are central democratic values but do not extend to nonhumans as contractual subjects; capitalist naturalism disguises subordination via production of nonhumans (Marxian commodity fetishism: things link as if persons)—yet never grants things full autonomy; abstract entities (“growth,” “profit margin”) quasi-intentionalized imperfectly.
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[nature-culture-distinction]]
- **Grounding quote:** "One does not enter into a contract with plants, animals, machines, or genes, all of which are objects, not subjects, of transactions… Where commodity fetishism prevails, labor relations between persons tend to be seen as connections in which things become linked, unlike in animism…"
- **Notes:** Marx used as analytic language, not as target thinker required.

### C-8-049: Naturalism’s relational apartheid and failed totalizing relations
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues subject/object dissociation condemns naturalism to perpetual compromise and nostalgia for re-enchantment: transmission imperfect (history ≠ ancestors); gift marginalized to intimacy/charity; predation as self-constituting incorporation not central (resource ransacking forgets early-modern nature-otherness thesis).
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[gift-exchange]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[nature-culture-distinction]]
- **Grounding quote:** "in such a clearly apartheid regime, it is impossible to set up between all existing beings a schema of interaction with the synthesizing power and simplicity of expression of the relations that structure nonmodern collectives… a veneration for history is not the same as veneration for ancestors…"
- **Notes:** Critical diagnosis of Moderns.

### C-8-050: Desire for reciprocity with nature; naturalism’s plurality as strength
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola treats Romantic Naturphilosophie, neo-shamanism, New Age, cyborg aesthetics as symptoms of desire to recover plants/animals/objects as fellow citizens; but Modern nature speaks only via human intermediaries—no true contract; yet naturalism’s inability to unify under one relation yields fascinating hybrid plurality of postindustrial “would-be collectives.”
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[nature-culture-distinction]], [[animism]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the Moderns’ nature can emerge from its silence only by means of all-too-human intermediaries, so that no exchange, no negotiation, no contract with the host of inanimate beings is now conceivable… the contradictions of naturalism… are what give it its fascinating plurality."
- **Notes:** Not an attack on tech society per se (explicit).

### C-8-051: Totemic collectives: internal identity of composition; exchange outward
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: totemic humans/nonhumans share physical and moral attributes (identity as distinctive character and class equivalence); within-group relations lack dynamizing exterior difference, forcing projection to other totemic classes; exchange dominates between ontologically co-level groups (status parity, mutual dependence)—Australia as Lévi-Strauss’s generalized exchange model.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[gift-exchange]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[alliance-theory]]
- **Grounding quote:** "no veritable relations can exist between the members of a totemic group—at least none of the kind that stamp their vigorous mark on practices… it is hardly surprising that exchange should be the dominant schema into which their links are subsumed… Aboriginal Australia provided Lévi-Strauss with not only the most complete model of generalized exchange…"
- **Notes:** Totemism × exchange compatibility.

### C-8-052: Totemism excludes transmission and production (intichiuma as midwifery)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola denies equating totemic class perpetuation with patrimonial transmission: Dream-beings are prototypes transmitting themselves as attributes, not ancestral creditors; production (object fabrication) has no place—intichiuma rites are midwifery assisting propagation of affiliated nonhumans for other groups, not autonomous creation.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[animism]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the perpetuation of the physical and moral properties that each totemic class embodies… can in no way be assimilated to the transmission of a patrimony… humans here play the role of midwives assisting the birth of quasi fellow beings, not that of autonomous creators of objects…"
- **Notes:** Clears conflation with analogist ancestrality.

### C-8-053: Analogism: only internal relations; paradox of difference-seeking integration
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: analogical collectives are coextensive with the world—authentic partners exist only inside; outside beings must be absorbed into prepared sociocosmic places; paradox—analogism posits differences then integrates via correspondence/hierarchy, whereas totemism posits identity then seeks exterior difference for relations.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[totemism]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "While totemic collectives have to project themselves outward… analogical collectives can, on the contrary, establish links only within themselves… The paradox of totemism is that in principle it posits an identity between terms… Meanwhile, the paradox of analogism is that it posits in principle differences between terms…"
- **Notes:** Structural chiasmus totemism/analogism.

### C-8-054: Analogism dominated by transmission and protection (exchange as moderator)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola: analogical predominance of hierarchical transmission and cascading protection (plants/animals to pantheon); exchange appears as division of tasks (castes, Inca/Pharaoh tribute, descent groups) but moderates disparity via illusion of equivalence rather than serving as cardinal schematizing value—even unequal exchange integrates distant statuses.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[gift-exchange]], [[social-structure]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the relations that predominate are those best suited to cope with differences… transmission… protection… In many cases, these hierarchical relations take the form of a division of tasks through the medium of exchange… here the exchange is not so much a cardinal value that schematizes relationships; rather, it is a way of moderating the original disparity…"
- **Notes:** Analogism compatibility column.

### C-8-055: Functionalism as ideology of analogical collectives; colonial sampling bias
- **Register:** THEORY / POSITION
- **Claim:** Descola claims analogical collectives’ ideology is functionalism (each element contributes to stable totality); British functionalism’s sway partly reflected colonial fieldwork among African/Eastern analogical societies who self-explained as functional groups; animist American collectives were neglected for ostensible lack of institutional cohesion until structural anthropology.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[animism]], [[anthropology]], [[structuralism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the ideology of a collective of this type is bound to be functionalism… the sway that was exercised in the United Kingdom by the “functionalist” anthropological doctrine resulted partly from the circumstantial fact that British anthropologists tended to study… most of those stemmed from analogical collectives… animist collectives, above all those in the Americas, which were tacitly neglected by the functionalist school…"
- **Notes:** Strong reflexivity claim for anthropology discipline page.

### C-8-056: Heuristic status of the enterprise; structures not limitless
- **Register:** META / THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola accepts competence critiques (Weberian humility) and possible simplifications; hypotheses are heuristic; aim is to treat social facts via principles by which humans schematize experience and welcome nonhumans—forms neither limitless nor incommensurable; backs assumption of common structures against pure stylistic commentary on diversity.
- **Page targets:** [[descola-philippe]], [[four-ontologies]], [[comparative-method]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the hypotheses set out in this work are above all of a heuristic nature… those forms of relating to the world seem to me neither limitless nor incommensurable… In the present book, I back the converse assumption… that common structures may govern their organization."
- **Notes:** Closing epistemology.

### C-8-057: Program incomplete; structure before genesis is methodological priority only
- **Register:** META
- **Claim:** Descola admits collectives typology is too summary for kinship/network variation; many facets of experience unintegrated; hypothesis that identification/relationships partly define other schemas (configurations, temporality, categorization) needs rigorous justification; precedence of structural configuration over causal genesis is methodological priority, not a bar to studying causes/change mechanisms.
- **Page targets:** [[four-ontologies]], [[descola-philippe]], [[social-structure]], [[structuralism]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the precedence ascribed to the discovery of structural configurations over research into the causes of their genesis is no more than a methodological priority and that, however imperfect our understanding of structural configurations may still be, this should not be considered a reason to defer a study of the causes of their genesis."
- **Notes:** Aligns with Ch.15’s historical turn.

### C-8-058: Theory not a political model; no nonmodern wisdom export
- **Register:** META / THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola refuses that the theory should design new cohabitation regimes or reforms (though reform is “indispensable” given N–S disparity and biosphere degradation); Amazonian Indians, Australian Aboriginals, or Tibetan monks do not offer deeper present wisdom than late modernity’s “shaky naturalism”—each presence is a compromise between sensory/cognitive factors and historically formed schemas (final truncated continuation).
- **Page targets:** [[descola-philippe]], [[naturalism-ontology]], [[four-ontologies]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Its purpose is not to propose models of communal life… it would be mistaken to think that the Indians of Amazonia, the Australian Aboriginals, or the monks of Tibet can bring us a deeper wisdom for the present time than the shaky naturalism of late modernity."
- **Notes:** Anti-romantic political conclusion; ends the book.
