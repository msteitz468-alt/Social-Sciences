### C-4-001: Animism’s residual definition — shared interiority of nonhumans
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues that, stripped of sociological correlations and despite dubious past uses of the term, animism’s acceptable core is humans’ attribution to nonhumans of an interiority identical to their own; that soul enables nonhumans to follow human social/ethical norms and communicate with humans and among themselves.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[four-ontologies]], [[descola-philippe]]
- **Grounding quote:** “That characteristic is the attribution by humans to nonhumans of an interiority identical to their own. This attribution humanizes plants and, above all, animals, since the soul with which it endows them allows them not only to behave in conformity with the social norms and ethical precepts of humans but also to establish communicative relations both with humans and among themselves.” (Ch.6 opening)
- **Notes:** Program definition of animism as identification mode.

### C-4-003: Incomplete humanization — discontinuity by physicality (body)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues humanization under animism is incomplete: plants/animals remain distinct as “humans in disguise” by outward apparel (feathers, fur, scales, bark)—i.e., physicality; following Viveiros, difference is through bodies not souls.

- **Register:** THEORY

### C-4-015: Form = ethogram package (Ingold circumpolar)

### C-4-021: Metamorphosis as anamorphosis of perspectives
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola redefines metamorphosis: not unveiling or disguise but ultimate phase of a relationship where each party modifies viewpoint imposed by original physicality to coincide with how the other sees itself—“enter the skin” of the other; human sees animal as animal sees itself (as human); shaman is seen as animal. - **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[perspectivism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[modes-of-relation]]
- **Grounding quote:** “metamorphosis is not an unveiling or a disguise. What is involved is really not so much a metamorphosis as an anamorphosis.”
- **Notes:** Bridge into perspectivism section.

### C-4-025: Standard animism vs perspectivism’s extra clause
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola distinguishes: standard animism = humans say nonhumans perceive themselves as humans (similar interiorities despite different forms); perspectivism adds that nonhumans see humans as nonhumans (predators or spirits)—crossed inversion of viewpoints; that crossed inversion is *not* attested in all animist systems (Americas especially in myths; rarer in Asia).
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[perspectivism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “In ‘standard’ animism, humans maintain that nonhumans perceive themselves as humans…. To this, perspectivism appends an additional clause: humans claim that nonhumans see humans not as humans but as nonhumans…. this crossed inversion of the two points of view, which is a defining characteristic of perspectivism, is by no means attested in all animist systems.”
- **Notes:** Core Descola–Viveiros differentiation.

### C-4-036: Elkin’s inventory — totemism not unified marriage mechanism
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola reports Elkin’s 1930s inventory split Australian totemism into heterogeneous types, some playing no role in matrimonial alliances—ruining hope of systematic continent-wide link between totemic classifications and social organization; Lévi-Strauss criticized Elkin vehemently because that link was L-S’s objective; Descola’s interest is ontological characteristics Elkin reveals.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[structuralism]], [[social-structure]]
- **Grounding quote:** “Elkin’s inventory split Australian totemism into a multitude of apparently heterogeneous types and unambiguously showed that some of those types played no role at all in the functioning of matrimonial alliances…. Lévi-Strauss’s criticisms of him on this score were all the more vehement because that was precisely the objective that he himself had in mind.”
- **Notes:** Position: Descola vs L-S use of Elkin.

### C-4-037: Individual totemism (SE Australia) vs animist human–animal links
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola contrasts SE Australian individual totemism (sorcerer–reptile species: species as whole, alter ego; wound to totemic animal harms sorcerer) with animist shaman/guardian relations: latter involve individual creatures not whole species; animist persons remain distinct enabling dyadic relations, whereas Australian sorcerer’s person fuses with the adopted species.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[animism]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “in Australia a relationship is established with a species considered as an indissociable whole…. in animist systems, human and animal persons are clearly distinguished…. In contrast, an Australian sorcerer’s person seems completely fused with the animal species that he has adopted as his totem.”
- **Notes:** Cross-ontology contrast.

### C-4-038: Sexual and conceptional totemism
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola reports sexual totemism (SE tribes: sexes as exclusive totemic classes, e.g. bat/owl; shared life or shared affiliation) and Aranda/Aluridja conceptional totemism: child’s totem from place mother became aware of pregnancy (site of Dreamtime child-souls), independent of parents’ totems and of matrimonial subsections.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[social-structure]]
- **Grounding quote:** “The totem of each child is not determined by sex or filiation but by the place where the child’s mother became aware of her pregnancy…. An Aranda child will thus not necessarily have the same totem as its father, its mother, or its brothers and sisters.”
- **Notes:** Ontological person-definition vs purely classificatory emblem.

### C-4-039: Moiety/section/subsection totems — cosmological shared properties
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues class totems often overdetermine marriage classes but justifications reveal other principles: matri-moiety totemism as shared flesh/blood from eponymous birds; patri-moieties with kangaroo species + local cults; section/subsection totems as fourfold/eightfold cosmic partition “on the basis of the kinship … between human beings and the natural species”; shared properties more cosmological/ontological than strictly sociological.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[social-structure]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “most clearly have a classificatory dimension of a cosmological or ontological type rather than a strictly sociological one, the general idea being that there exist shared properties, even if they are only vaguely defined, between humans, nonhumans, and the totems that encompass them.”
- **Notes:** Softens pure sociology of totems.

### C-4-040: Clan totemism — matrilinear substance vs patrilinear place-genius vs native religious collectives
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola summarizes Elkin’s clan totemism: matrilinear = shared corporeal substance from totemic entity → strict exogamy and ban on eating totem (“something of the life of man is in the life of the totemic species”); patrilinear = coincidence of clan, local group, territory, shared spiritual genesis/sacred geography; native clan = co-conception/birth at same child-soul site, not exogamous, religious collective performing increase rites for shared-essence species.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[social-structure]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the totem ‘is more than a name or emblem; something of the life of man is in the life of the totemic species, and vice-versa.’ … In the case of a matrilinear clan, the relation … is of a substantial nature. But in a patrilinear clan it stems from an intimate solidarity … identical spiritual genesis and an identical sacred geography.”
- **Notes:** Two major hybridization modes prefigured.

### C-4-041: Multiple coexisting totems; against “Australian totemism is fiction”
- **Register:** POSITION
- **Claim:** Descola notes one person may link to five heterogeneous totem classes (NW southern Australia); Elkin’s deconstruction does not warrant concluding Australian totemism is pure anthropological fiction—resemblances matter: Elkin’s “oneness of life” shared by man and natural species; name stands for community of nature, not mere emblem.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** “is it legitimate to infer … that Australian totemism does not exist … but simply amounts to an anthropological fiction…? That would be to leap too hastily to a conclusion…. all the forms … are based on a belief in the ‘oneness of life, which is shared by man and natural species,’ … ‘the name stands for a community of nature between the group and its totem.’”
- **Notes:** Defends ontological unity against pure classificatory dissolution.

### C-4-042: Two major hybridizations — shared substance vs shared essence/child-souls
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola extracts from Elkin two major human–nonhuman hybridizations: (1) shared substance (flesh, blood, skin)—matrilinear moieties/clans, some subsection totemisms; (2) identical essence/principle of individuation via incorporation of child-souls from totemic sites—conceptional, religious, patrilinear clan totemism; individual sorcerer totemism combines both.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “two major varieties of hybridization between humans and nonhumans, totems included. One type refers to the sharing of one and the same substance (flesh, blood, skin)…. The other is based on an identical essence or principle of individuation that is engendered by the regular incorporation of the child-souls of a totemic site into both humans and nonhumans.”
- **Notes:** Prefigures totemic ontology = continuity of both physicalities and interiorities.

### C-4-043: Brandenstein — generic totem terms are body/substance vocabulary
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Per Brandenstein (via Descola), most Aboriginal generic terms for “totem” denote body parts or physiological substances (flesh/meat, skin, head, forehead/face, eyes, side, liver/temper, color); polysemic terms like ngurlu/ngarlgi = interior/temperament/identifying essence (sweat, odor, taste, voice, etc.); relational terms (“moiety,” “friend,” “same name”) much rarer—semantic field stresses shared physical identity.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the great majority of these generic terms refer to elements of a human or animal body or to physiological substances, some of which are explicitly regarded by the Aboriginals as vectors of a shared identity.”
- **Notes:** Linguistic evidence for ontological reading; “totem” etymology from Ojibwa flagged for later Algonquin section.

### C-4-044: Brandenstein’s combinatorial logic of class attributes
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola reports Brandenstein’s model: Australian totemic system governed by immanent logic; moieties by “quick”/“slow” (and related moral-physical pairs); four sections add “hot-blooded”/“cold-blooded”; eight subsections add “round”/“flat”—each subsection a combination of three properties defining behavior, humor, and dimension; marriage pairs complementary combinations; physical mismatch with type not strongly enforced.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[social-structure]], [[binary-opposition]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the whole Australian totemic system is governed by a single immanent logic whose most complete expression, in societies with subsections, is based on eight combinations of three pairs of primary properties…. a type of behavior (quick or slow), a type of humor (hot-blooded or cold-blooded), and a dimension or volume (round or flat).”
- **Notes:** Descola later doubts perfect continent-scale coherence but values insight.

### C-4-045: Totem names denote qualities, not zoological source of qualities
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola (via Brandenstein) claims animals (and plants) embody exemplary attributes of totemic class, and often “the animal is named after the quality which is its main characteristic, never the quality after the animal”—class names are property terms also naming eponymous animals, reversing L-S *Totemism* model that natural species differences supply model for human segmentation.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[structuralism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “in many cases ‘the animal is named after the quality which is its main characteristic, never the quality after the animal.’ … the names of the totemic classes are terms denoting properties that designate the eponymous animal also, rather than the reverse…. It therefore becomes difficult to maintain the interpretation of totemic classifications for Australia proposed in Totemism.”
- **Notes:** Direct challenge to structuralist totemism for Australia.

### C-4-046: Non-species totems and subtotem stacks vs intellectualist model
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola argues non-species totems (~15% of Brandenstein’s 524: human body parts, artifacts, meteors) break homology-of-natural-discontinuities if treated as spontaneous sensory species differences, but fit if they are iconic/metonymic labels of property classes; hierarchized secondary species under moiety principals (e.g. Nungar cockatoo/crow stack) are taxonomically redundant under L-S dichotomic-matrix function but make sense as weaker expressions of moiety attributes.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[structuralism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]]
- **Grounding quote:** “if we accept that these ‘non-species’ totem names are nothing but more or less iconic labels denoting a class of properties with which they have only a metonymic relationship, then the difficulty disappears…. From a strictly taxonomic point of view, all this involves a pointless redundancy that simply blurs the initial contrast.”
- **Notes:** Second difficulty for intellectualist totemism.

### C-4-047: Australian totemism = continuity of both physicalities and interiorities
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola concludes Brandenstein+Elkin support a mode of identification with interspecies continuity of *both* physicalities and interiorities: common material/behavioral properties and humors (temperament from physicality); interiority via child-souls from Dreaming sites or via class itself as vector of shared essence—each member (human and nonhuman) has same intrinsic species-defining characteristics of the totemic collective.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]]
- **Grounding quote:** “a mode of identification founded on an interspecies continuity of both physicalities and interiorities…. it is the class itself, with all its physical and moral attributes, that here constitutes the vector and symptom of a shared interiority.”
- **Notes:** Formula of totemic ontology opposite to animism’s continuous interiority / discontinuous physicality.

### C-4-048: Against pure patri-spiritual vs matri-substantial opposition
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola urges circumspection toward sharp opposition of patrilinear (shared spiritual identity from sites) vs matrilinear (shared maternal substance) totemism: combinations exist; hybridization occurs at both interiority and physicality levels with variable modalities across ~500 languages.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** “totemic hybridization seems everywhere to occur simultaneously at the levels of both interiority and physicality, albeit according to variable modalities.”
- **Notes:** Methodological caution before hybrid case studies.

### C-4-049: Mangarrayi/Yangman — semimoiety totems, warrwiyan, pre-differentiated world
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Per Merlan (via Descola), Mangarrayi/Yangman totemism associates with four patrilinear semimoieties (not eight marriage subsections); marragwa totems (plants, animals, phenomena, mythic, abstract) without formal major/subtotem hierarchy; Dream-beings warrwiyan already distributed by semimoiety place beings as landscape features; common origin/substance regulated by pre-existing categorization—myths do not start from undifferentiated continuum.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “mythical accounts relate not to an initial undifferentiated state but to a world already divided into substantive essences that were actualized as classes of particular entities thanks to the intervention of the Dream-beings.”
- **Notes:** Contrast setup for animist myths.

### C-4-050: Animist vs Australian mythologies of difference
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola contrasts: Amerindian myths = events introducing discontinuities among species from original continuum (retain shared interiority); Australian mythology = parthenogenesis within already constituted hybrid classes, multiplying species (including human varieties) still conforming to ontological type of their subdivision.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[totemism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[nature-culture-distinction]]
- **Grounding quote:** “Amerindian myths tell of separate events that occasioned the introduction of discontinuities between the species that had all been part of an original continuum…. But Australian mythology evokes a process of parthenogenesis unfolding actually within the classes of hybrids already constituted.”
- **Notes:** Load-bearing comparative architecture.

### C-4-051: Aranda identification — “same thing as himself”; intichiuma paradox
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Per Spencer & Gillen (via Descola), Aranda: “The totem of any man is regarded … as the same thing as himself” (photo = self = kangaroo); yet no Frazerian protection: killing own totem allowed (eat sparingly); myths of ancient preferential feeding on totem; intichiuma increase rites feed other groups—problem of deep identification plus complicity in destruction.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-relation]]
- **Grounding quote:** “‘That one,’ pointing to his photograph which we had taken, ‘is just the same as me; so is a kangaroo.’ … How can one both set up a relationship of profound identification between a human and a nonhuman and at the same time accept that the former brings about or is complicit in the destruction of the latter?”
- **Notes:** Sets up prototype/matrix solution.

### C-4-052: Dream-beings as hybrid prototypes, not metamorphic persons
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola’s solution: child-souls differentiated (some → humans, some → plants/animals); Dream-beings are original hybrid hypostases of physical/moral properties, not plants/animals becoming humans or reverse; individual of my totemic species is contingent expression of shared qualities from immutable matrix—not a subject for personal social relationship as in animism; killing does not destroy the matrix.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[animism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[modes-of-relation]]
- **Grounding quote:** “Dream-beings are thus not plants or animals that undergo metamorphosis and change into humans, nor are they humans that change into plants or animals. Rather, they are original and originating hybrids…. Far from mutually apprehending each other as subjects engaged in a social relationship, humans and nonhumans are merely particularized materializations of classes of properties that transcend their own existences.”
- **Notes:** Decisive animism/totemism relation-type contrast.

### C-4-053: Aranda inapatua / Numbakulla — constitutional hybridization (Moisseeff)
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Per Moisseeff (via Descola), Aranda origin myths distinct from Dream-being journeys: incomplete inapatua conglomerates of plants/animals; Numbakulla segment them with stone knives into human forms; each human then associated with nonhuman element from which separated—becomes totem; dual identification levels: common matter and Dream-being essence.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** “it was only once it was individualized out of the common mass of matter that each human being became associated with the nonhuman element, either an animal or a plant, from which it had at first been separated. This element … was to become its totem.”
- **Notes:** Reinforces constitutional hybridization.

### C-4-054: Totemic ontology exemplary in Australia but not unique; keep term “totem”
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola claims moral and physical continuity between human and nonhuman groups is most systematic in Australia but not unique; elsewhere many “totemic” institutions are merely denotative labels for social segments (L-S homology applies without substantive continuity); specialists’ replacement of “totem” by “Dream” for Australia over-narrows the concept—he rejects such semantic cosmetics.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[four-ontologies]], [[structuralism]]
- **Grounding quote:** “Nowhere else do we find such a vast gathering of peoples that has so systematically, explicitly, and uniformly developed the idea that there exists a moral and physical continuity between groups of humans and groups of nonhumans…. I am not in favor of such semantic cosmetics for, although the Australian formula of totemism is certainly remarkable … it is not at all unique.”
- **Notes:** Terminological policy for the four ontologies.

### C-4-055: Chickasaw clan particularities as attenuated totemic ontology
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola cites SE US Chickasaw (L-S “hybrids” of totemism and castes): clans/hamlets with repertories of behavior, diet, costume, temperament, subsistence, physical aptitudes said to derive from eponymous species (Puma, Wild-Cat, Red-Fox, Raccoon peoples)—animals as concrete paradigms of descent rather than Australian ideal prototype, yet still groups share physical and psychic dispositions with nonhumans as ontological class.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[levi-strauss-claude]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the Chickasaw attributed to each eponymous clan, or even each hamlet, definite particularities of behavior, diet, costume, temperament, means of subsistence, and physical aptitudes…. the general inspiration for the totemic mode of identification is preserved.”
- **Notes:** Extra-Australian residual totemism.

### C-4-056: Modes of identification non-exclusive generative matrices
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola restates that modes of identification are historical schematizations of experience, not empirical syntheses of institutions; each (animism, totemism, analogism, naturalism) can predominate while tolerating discreet presence of others—source of cultural idiosyncrasies.
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-identification]], [[four-ontologies]], [[analogism]], [[naturalism-ontology]], [[animism]], [[totemism]]
- **Grounding quote:** “modes of identification are ways of schematizing experiences that prevailed in certain historical situations and are not empirical syntheses of institutions and beliefs…. animism, totemism, analogism, and naturalism can each tolerate a discreet presence of other emerging modes.”
- **Notes:** Methodological META for the whole program; names all four.

### C-4-057: Ojibwa totam confusion — Long, clan -dodem vs pawagának
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Descola recounts origin of “totem/totamism” in John Long’s memoirs: Ojibwa companion used totam for personal guardian bear (pawagának type), not clan eponym; Ojibwa also have patrilinear animal-named clans with root -dodem (kinship/coresidence); anthropologists later split social vs individual totemism attributing Long’s error.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[animism]], [[anthropology]]
- **Grounding quote:** “Long mentions the term totam in the context of an anecdote in which one of his Ojibwa companions uses the word to refer to a bear that is apparently his own personal guardian spirit, not the eponymous animal of his clan.”
- **Notes:** Disciplinary history of the concept.

### C-4-058: Fogelson & Brightman — polyvalent Algonquian *o.te. / -dodem
- **Register:** POSITION
- **Claim:** Descola reports Fogelson & Brightman cast doubt on Long’s “mistake”: Long fluent, knew collective Iroquois totems; Father De Smet similar Potawatomi use of dodeme for dream guardian; Proto-Algonquian /*o.te./ “live together as collective”; terms cover clan, eponymous animal, kinship, friend, and (Fox) provider of supernatural power—same word for relative, clan animal, individual spirit depending on pragmatics.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[animism]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the Ojibwa, or the Potawatomi … were not as strict as anthropologists later were when it came to distinguishing between what the anthropologists called ‘social totemism’ and ‘individual totemism.’ … it is not at all incongruous to use one and the same word to refer sometimes to a clan relative, at other times to one’s clan’s eponymous animal, and at yet other times to one’s individual animal spirit.”
- **Notes:** Semantic bridge between complexes.

### C-4-059: Northern Algonquin animism with classificatory (not ontological) clans
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Descola places Ojibwa/northern Algonquins in animist mode (interiority of nonhumans as persons); their totemic groups lack Australian-style physical+spiritual continuity with eponymous species—governed by L-S metaphorical relation of discontinuities; northernmost Ojibwa lack clans; yet guardian spirit as species emissary and onomastic twinship can shift denotative totemism toward personalized nonhuman class without full Australian collective sharing.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[totemism]], [[structuralism]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the Ojibwa, and the northern Algonquins more generally, undoubtedly can be connected to an animist mode of identification…. As for the totemic groups among them, these are not characterized by the kind of continuity both physical and spiritual between humans and eponymous species that prevails in Australia…. Rather, they seem to be governed by the mechanism, detected by Lévi-Strauss, of a relationship that is metaphorical, not inwardly motivated, between two series of discontinuities.”
- **Notes:** Mixed/minor-mode presence of totemism in animist settings.

### C-4-060: Penobscot game totems — material dependence on eponymous species
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Per Speck (via Descola), Penobscot (no descent groups): 13 of 22 local bands named for abundant game on winter grounds; identification via preferred hunting/fishing dependence; parallel to Mistassini Cree guardian/game preference; Cree (and perhaps Penobscot) treat species as proprietors of hunting territories lent to humans—totemic names from accepted material dependence, not arbitrary natural/social discontinuity mapping; shows totemism in minor mode inside animist ontologies.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** “the totemic names were not based on some arbitrary correspondence between natural and social discontinuities but on an accepted material relationship of dependence reached between the human groups and the nonhuman groups that had imprinted the mark of their identity on to the territory…. totemism, as I have defined it, may be present, in a minor mode or potentially, in animist ontologies.”
- **Notes:** Closes Ch.7; supports non-exclusive modes thesis.
