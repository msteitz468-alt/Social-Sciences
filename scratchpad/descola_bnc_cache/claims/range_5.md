### C-5-032: Chain of being — continuous/discontinuous fit as typical analogist problem
- **Register:** THEORY

- **Grounding quote:** "The theory of the chain of being presents a particular intellectual problem that is probably typical of analogism: namely, how do the continuous and the discontinuous fit together?"

### C-5-036: Nahua four-component person (López Austin)
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Via López Austin (Nahuatl philology, Sahagún, modern Nahua ethnology): Nahua person has four principal components—*tonacayo* (“all our flesh,” body as substantial totality, also plants/maize); three “animist centers” *tonalli*, *teyolia*, *ihiyotl* governing life, movement, individuation, psychic functions in humans and nonhumans. - **Grounding quote:** "The Nahuas recognized the existence of four principal components in a human person (tonacayo, tonalli, teyolia, and ihiyotl)… López Austin defines the three other components as “animist centers”"
- **Notes:** Core ethnographic finding for Mesoamerican analogism; do not invent López Austin page.

### C-5-037: Tonalli as individualized destiny/heat/consciousness
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** *Tonalli* (head; spreads via blood): impalpable emanation (sometimes breath); when absent takes invisible body-shape, needs envelope—may lodge in animal/plant; “irradiation,” birth-day destiny, personal property; force as heat/light unique by day-sign and secret name; incorporated by ritual bath not present at birth; gives vigor, growth, body temperature, self-consciousness; temporary absence (drunkenness, sickness, dream, sex) or permanent (imminent death). Gods, plants, animals, inanimates also have *tonalli*. Parallels: Otomi *šũti*, Mesoamerican *sombra*, Tzotzil *ch’ulel* (intangible replica; nail/hair retain link).
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Tonalli may be translated as “irradiation,” but depending on the context, it may also mean “the destiny of a person according to the day of its birth”… Tonalli is not present at birth but has to be incorporated in a ceremony featuring a ritual bath"
- **Notes:** Dense person-component ethnography.

### C-5-038: Teyolia as heart-principle of animation (Christian anima assimilation)
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** *Teyolia* in heart but not the organ: part that goes to world of the dead; never leaves body in life; lodged in embryo by *calpulli* protective deities; source of sensibility, memory, “states of soul,” idea-formation (“to think” = “to make something inside alive”); controls lasting temperament typed as white/hard/sweet/bitter/sad/raw/cold heart. Highly individualized yet also in animals, plants, mountains. Quickly assimilated to Christian *anima*.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "This is the component of the human person that, through its permanence and properties, seems to correspond most closely to an idea of interiority… “to think” was “to make something inside alive.”"
- **Notes:** Closest analogical component to “interiority” category.

### C-5-039: Ihiyotl as dense luminous gas of influence and “face”
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** *Ihiyotl* linked with liver/bile but inorganic entities also have it; “breath”/face; dense luminous gas/aura of attraction and influence; present embryonically; survives death as dangerous exhalation; engenders directed feelings (desire, anger, appetite, harm); revitalized by air and food; as “face” reflects civic virtues and hierarchical status. Harder to classify than pure immaterial *tonalli*/*teyolia*—more concrete vapor (Chorti *hijillo* stench/outline).
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Ihiyotl… is described as an extremely dense luminous gas that emanates from a human, an animal, or an object and acts as an attractive force and seat of influence"
- **Notes:** Blurs interiority/physicality boundary.

### C-5-040: Analogism’s dominant feature — plural unstable components per entity
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Dominant feature of Nahua/any analogical system is not material/immaterial sorting but grouping within every entity of plurality of aspects whose coordination stabilizes identity, faculties, and “nature.” Infinite combinatorial variants make each human and nonhuman quasi-unique; obsessive attention to disparities (age, sex, skin, odor, heat, normality, vulnerability). Gradational continuum hot–cold for plants, animals, minerals, stars, foods, illnesses, deities.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "The dominant feature of Nahua ontology, as of any analogical system, is the grouping within every existing entity of a plurality of aspects the right coordination of which is believed to be necessary for the stabilization of that entity’s individual identity"
- **Notes:** Generalizable analogism property.

### C-5-041: Nahua analogism near animism yet structurally distinct
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Nahua world of trees begged forgiveness, dream-communicating interiorities, seems near animism but differs: (1) more liberal attribution—all entities have at least one subjectivity component; (2) every being different by plural component combinations—against animist unity of internal faculties; (3) humans and animals do not share same culture/ethics/institutions; (4) closed universe of destiny and uncontrolled correspondences vs animist freedom of action among similar interiorities.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[animism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[achuar]]
- **Grounding quote:** "In an analogical regime, humans and animals do not share the same culture, the same ethics, and the same institutions… In contrast to the freedom of action that animism allows… analogical worlds are burdened by the weight of destiny."
- **Notes:** Critical discrimination for four-ontology pages.

### C-5-042: Possession vs Amazonian hallucinogen use
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Nahua possession (*aacqui* intrusion; *cihualpipiltin* teyolia causing paralysis; pulque rabbit deities; peyote/mushrooms inhabited by deities taking over body) differs from Amazonian ayahuasca: not alien interiority establishing hold, but freeing interiority from physical receptacle to increase acuity—eventually enabling metamorphosis/viewpoint shifts.

- **Grounding quote:** "This is strikingly different from analogous practices in animist Amazonia. Here, the ingestion of hallucinatory substances such as ayahuasca… is in no sense supposed to introduce an alien interiority… On the contrary, it serves to free the interiority from its physical receptacle"
- **Notes:** Practice-level ontology contrast.

### C-5-043: Tonalism vs Nagualism vs animist metamorphosis
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Clarify terms: *nagual* for sorcerer transforming; *tona* for animal alter ego. *Tona/wayjel*: co-natal wild animal sharing temperament; fraction of human *tonalli* installs at birth—parallel destinies, often unknown identity (risk of hunting own double). Nagualism (López Austin): possession by sending *ihiyotl*/*nahualli* into other beings—not metamorphosis of appearance. Both export components (tonalli involuntary permanent vs ihiyotl intentional transitory); major contrast with animist metamorphosis where interiorities stay constant/unitary/autonomous and only corporeal envelopes change with viewpoint.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[animism]], [[perspectivism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "In metamorphosis, in contrast, the interiorities of both humans and nonhumans, identical in their dispositions, remain constant, unitary, and autonomous; only their corporeal envelopes change, in accordance with the point of view from which they are apprehended."
- **Notes:** Load-bearing for perspectivism vs analogism.

### C-5-044: Analogy as compensatory integration in singularity-saturated worlds
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Worlds of circulating plural components require pairing, structuring, classifying; analogy creates chains of solidarity across cosmos levels, body parts, social strata, occupations, weather, foods, deities, calendars, cardinal points. Macrocosm–microcosm is superficial effect (Foucault) yet humans serve as privileged hermeneutic standard; analogism not anthropomorphic overall model—too complex. Not universal: straggling body analogies everywhere, but only analogical ontologies systematize into ordered interdependent practical sets.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** "analogy serves as a compensatory procedure of integration, making it possible to create chains of solidarity and links of continuity leading in every direction… only analogical ontologies have managed to systematize these straggling chains of meaning into ordered and interdependent sets"
- **Notes:** Functional role of analogy in the mode.

### C-5-045: Hot/cold polarity as typical analogical classification
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Inclusive bipolar structures (hot/cold; sometimes dry/wet) are most obvious analogism indicators. Early Nahuas exhaustively used hot vs cold (celestial/light/masculine/above/strength/fire/eagle/day/13/life vs chthonic/dark/feminine/below/weakness/water/ocelot/night/9/death). Assignations are conventional properties not actual temperature (Yucatan oven heat → “cold” food via underworld analogy). Gradations for medical equilibrium; some plants dual-classed (Tzotzil); maize hot+cold (Totonac).
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[binary-opposition]]
- **Grounding quote:** "these perhaps constitute the most obvious indications of an analogical ontology. The early Nahuas made use only of the former [hot/cold], but they employed it regularly and exhaustively in order to divide all existing things into two classes."
- **Notes:** Classification mechanism; link [[binary-opposition]] carefully as analytic tool not Lévi-Straussian identity.

### C-5-046: Hot/cold origin debate — Foster vs López Austin
- **Register:** POSITION
- **Claim:** Foster argued Mesoamerican hot/cold is Hippocratic legacy via Spaniards; stimulated studies of Iberian grafts. López Austin defends pre-Hispanic autochthony: curious that only part of humor theory crossed (dry/wet little impact). Descola: if great qualitative inclusive classifications typify analogism, origin controversy is beside the point—structural convergence of two analogical contexts; Spaniards unsurprised by Mexican divination/medicine (“same methods as astrologers and doctors… among ourselves”). - **Grounding quote:** "If we accept that the great inclusive classifications based on pairs of perceptible qualities are one of the most typical features of analogical ontologies, then the controversy over the origin of the hot/cold polarity will seem quite beside the point."
- **Notes:** Historiographic position; method of structural comparison.

### C-5-048: Dogon extreme diversification and chains of doubles
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Dogon (Dieterlen/Griaule/Ogotemmêli): body; eight *kikinu* souls (four body souls—intelligent + crawling, each twinned male/female; four sex souls); eight clavicle seeds (primordial crops); many parcels of *ɲàma* vital force; animal double. Components mobile across ponds, sanctuaries, prohibited animals at rites of passage. ### C-5-049: African analogism more parsimonious on nonhuman interiors than Mesoamerica

### C-5-050: Analogical archipelago — shared properties summarized
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Analogism fragments interiority and physicality into multiple mobile partly extracorporeal components; unstable grouping produces flux of singularities. Humans privileged as reduced manageable model of world relations → concern for balance of humors, fear of intrusive identity or loss of essential element, specialists (signs, ritual, astrology, divination), batteries of symbols/emblems, computing systems. Spatial/temporal coordinates proliferate; long genealogies and ancestors central (absent from animism and totemism). Dual/quadripartite classifications organize predicates (hot/cold type)—class does not pre-specify ontology as Australian totemic dualisms do.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[animism]], [[totemism]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Interiority and physicality are here fragmented, in every being, into multiple components that are mobile and partly extracorporeal… All this stands in total contrast to animism and totemism, from which these cumbersome ancestral figures are absent."
- **Notes:** Synthesis section “Pairings, Hierarchy, and Sacrifice.”

### C-5-051: Hierarchy and Dumontian holism as analogical management tools
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Chain-of-being hierarchy broadly transposable to analogical ontologies (criteria vary); animism/totemism distinguish on horizontal plane without castes/classes/divinity accumulations. Dumont’s holism (part recapitulates whole; values subordinate place of beings to transcending totality)—Descola reinterprets not as modern/nonmodern contrast but as means *solely analogical ontologies* use to manage mass of singularities; also Chinese dominance reversals by level.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[social-structure]]
- **Grounding quote:** "holism is not so much a characteristic by which nonmodern societies as a whole are differentiated from modern individualism; rather, it is a means employed solely by analogical ontologies to make it possible to manage such a mass of singularities."
- **Notes:** Important critique/refunctioning of Dumont; no Dumont page—fold on analogism.

### C-5-052: Sacrifice co-distributed with analogical ontologies
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** As hypothesis: sacrifice present in analogical regions (Brahmanic India, West Africa, ancient China with political functions, Andes, pre-Columbian Mexico) and virtually unknown in totemic Australia and classic animist Amazonia/subarctic America. Domesticates-absent objection shifts problem to incompatibility of animal domestication with animism/totemism (deferred to Part V). Cashibo tapir feast and Ghiliak/Nivkh bear festival are animist but not sacrifices: no deity-directed victim, no status benefit expected, animal not substitutable.

### C-5-053: Sacrifice forges contiguity among singularities (Lévi-Strauss refined)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Citing Lévi-Strauss: sacrifice establishes relation of contiguity via successive identifications (sacrificant/sacrificer–victim–deity). Pointless in animism where animal already has human-like interiority as person. Necessary in analogism to link distant singularities without breaking chain of being. Descola refines Lévi-Strauss “reservoirs” model: victim is composite package of properties overlapping sacrificant, deity, and substitutes—decomposition against general splitting of beings enables identification by each actor with at least one property; African cases where beneficiary is not a person but a concept/force.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[animism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[structuralism]]
- **Grounding quote:** "making use of sacrifice to forge such a relationship of contiguity between initially separate entities may well seem necessary in an analogical ontology, in which all existing beings are singularities between which links need to be established."
- **Notes:** Develops/refines Lévi-Strauss; attributed theoretical conversation.

### C-5-054: Four modes on two axes of identification (fig. 2)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Distribution of four combinations of resemblances/differences organized on two vertical axes: (1) wide dichotomous separations, preeminence of continuity over discontinuity, inversion of hierarchical inclusion—animism (universal continuity of interiorities/“culture”) vs naturalism (universal continuity of physicalities/nature); (2) chromatic continuities pairing resemblances toward identity (totemism) with gradual differences toward continuity (analogism).
- **Page targets:** [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[animism]], [[naturalism-ontology]], [[totemism]], [[analogism]], [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "The distribution of the four combinations of resemblances and differences is organized on the basis of two vertical axes… in animism, the continuity of interiorities… Meanwhile, in naturalism it is the continuity of physicalities… the other axis… system of resemblances tending toward identity (totemism) and a system of gradual differences tending toward continuity (analogism)"
- **Notes:** Master schematic claim for theory + hub.

### C-5-055: Modes are schemas not exclusive cultural models
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Modes of identification are not cultural models or dominant habitus but schemas integrating experience, structuring perceptions/relations via body and intentionality. Principles ex hypothesi universal and non-exclusive—coexist potentially in all humans; one becomes historically dominant without preventing other modes infiltrating representations/actions/customs. Example: spontaneous European naturalists may still treat cat as ensouled, believe Jupiter’s orbit affects tomorrow, or identify totally with a local community—without becoming institutionally animist/analogical/totemic.
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-identification]], [[four-ontologies]], [[practice-theory]], [[descola-philippe]]
- **Grounding quote:** "modes of identification are not cultural models or locally dominant forms of habitus. Rather, they are schemas for integrating experience… they coexist potentially in all human beings."
- **Notes:** Anti-essentialist, anti-typological misuse warning; practice-adjacent.

### C-5-056: Totemism — continuity at class, discontinuity at system (fig. 3)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Apparent disappearance of discontinuity in totemism is perspective skew: original framework is totemic class with internal continuity of physical and moral properties shared by humans and nonhumans from one prototype; social life requires other similar groups on different property collections, complementary at tribe level—discontinuity as condition for segment integration into system.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** "It is at this point that discontinuity recovers its rights, as a condition for totemic segments to become integrated and to form a system by playing on their intrinsic differences"
- **Notes:** Resolves symmetry-axis objection.

### C-5-057: Analogism — initial atomism then recomposition (fig. 4)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Analogism reverse of totemism: initial general fragmentation/discontinuities is logical preliminary for vast recomposition reversing disjunctive atomism via correspondences and analogies weaving reassuring continuity.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Its initial state is certainly a general fragmentation of existing beings and their components. But that accumulation of discontinuities may be seen as a preliminary state of affairs that is logically necessary for the vast movement of recomposition"

### C-5-058: Animism — hierarchical encompassment of physical difference by relation
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** In animism, physical differences do not prevent intersubjective relations; imputation of human behavior to nonhumans via resembling interiorities annexes much of “nature” into social life—hierarchical encompassment where shared relationship counts more than physical difference between terms. Explains ethnographers’ emphasis on relational cosmologies and contrastive-position identities in Amerindian, Asiatic, Melanesian animist societies.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[modes-of-relation]], [[collectives-including-nonhumans]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "a movement of hierarchical encompassment in which the fact of sharing a mutual relationship counts for more than the physical difference between the terms thus brought together."
- **Notes:** Links identification to relation; modes-of-relation page.

### C-5-059: Naturalism — nature encompasses culture; museum as microcosm
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Reverse of animism: all beings depend on same physicochemical mechanisms and phylogenetic continuities; universality of nature encompasses them; natural history museums display decontextualized inventories with only classificatory inclusion as relation. Ethnography rooms break unifying taxonomic ambition—culture resists natural-science order; dissimilarities proliferate between nonhumans and humans and among human groups via culture.
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[nature-culture-distinction]], [[culture]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "In naturalism, quite the reverse attitude prevails… The universality of nature encompasses them all… culture and its products indicate their resistance to being set into an order dictated by the natural sciences."
- **Notes:** Institutional microcosm of naturalist schema.

### C-5-060: Totemism — symmetrical double identity of terms and relations
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Totemic schema perfectly symmetrical: humans and nonhumans in a class share identical interiority and physicality attributes; morphological difference insufficient for intra-class ontological discrimination; shared origin/kinship/similarity/inherence relations; groups equivalent as homologous elements of wider tribal collective. No incipient nature/culture duality—ethnographers forced into “socio-natural” periphrases (Merlan, Elkin, Glowczewski); Lévi-Strauss: “socio-natural image that is unique but fragmented.”
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[nature-culture-distinction]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Here there is no trace of that incipient ontological duality that animism introduced with its distinction between different physicalities and that naturalism turns into a dogma with its distinction between different interiorities. Nature and culture are completely beside the point"
- **Notes:** Strong claim for totemism vs dualism.

### C-5-061: Analogism — symmetry of differences; totalitarian coherence + hermeneutic liberty
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Analogism plays symmetry of differences not resemblances: particularized beings from dissimilar components muddling body/intentionality duality (interiority externalized; physicality spiritualized); weak ontological differences + pairing devices. Paradox: difference of principle between terms that otherwise resemble. Multiple possible correspondence paths → varied relations on same beings. Terms and relations interdependent: cosmologies perfectly integrated/coherent bordering totalitarian order while allowing inhabitants large hermeneutic liberty.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[four-ontologies]], [[interiority-and-physicality]]
- **Grounding quote:** "the cosmologies that it renders possible are so perfectly integrated and coherent that they border upon a totalitarian order while allowing each of their inhabitants a large measure of hermeneutic liberty."
- **Notes:** Political/hermeneutic dual face of analogism.

### C-5-062: Metaphor/metonymy distribution across four modes (Jakobson; fig. 5)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Using Jakobson’s metaphor (internal similarity between terms) vs metonymy (external similarity between relations): animism—absence of metaphorical physical resemblance compensated by metonymic relations; naturalism—absence of metonymic relational resemblance compensated by metaphorical material continuity; totemism—class-level metaphor conditions system-level metonymy; analogism—absence of metaphor leads to metonymy and vice versa (search relations among dissimilar terms; multiply dissimilar relations to find term similarity).
- **Page targets:** [[four-ontologies]], [[modes-of-identification]], [[structuralism]], [[binary-opposition]]
- **Grounding quote:** "In animism, the absence of metaphorical resemblances between existing beings… is compensated for by metonymy… In naturalism… the absence of metonymic resemblances… is compensated for by a metaphorical link… An absence of metaphor leads to metonymy and an absence of metonymy leads to metaphor"
- **Notes:** Synthetic semiotic grid; structuralist inheritance acknowledged.

### C-5-063: Classificatory mechanisms not mutually exclusive
- **Register:** META
- **Claim:** Ethnoscience customarily opposes attribute categorization (componential analysis) to prototype categorization (Rosch colors; Berlin ethnobiological taxonomies); Descola: use alternately by object plus other schemas (spatial contiguity, origins, activity spheres). Folk plant taxonomies often prototypical (French *chêne*); grouping also by habitat or provenance; *agrume* as multi-attribute class without clear prototype.
- **Page targets:** [[modes-of-identification]], [[four-ontologies]], [[comparative-method]]
- **Grounding quote:** "rather than regard those two cognitive mechanisms as mutually exclusive, it is more reasonable to suppose that we use them alternately, depending on the objects to be classified"
- **Notes:** Methodological prelude to mode-specific classification claims.

### C-5-064: Animism classifies by prototypical humanity (interiority)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Animist schema relies chiefly on prototypical generalization: prototype is humanity as composite of affects, intentionality, reflective consciousness, linguistic/technical aptitudes, arbitrary norms—human as overwhelming interiority, not biological species—best example of person class into which nonhumans are incorporated. Perceptually counterintuitive; works by substituting salient psychological trait (dreams; urge to impute intentionality) for salient appearance. Contextual: Achuar, Makuna, Naskapi still use inclusive ethnobiology of plants/animals by perceptible prototypes while sometimes treating them under interiority prototype.
- **Page targets:** [[animism]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[achuar]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "it is humanity… that provides the ontological model… it is a human with his overwhelming interiority, not a human as a biological species, that constitutes the best example of the class of persons"
- **Notes:** Classificatory signature of animism.

### C-5-065: Totemism classifies by abstract class prototype of shared properties
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Totemic identification also prototype-governed but more abstract: prototype is not Dream-being or principal totem as phenomenal object but core of physical and moral properties identifying members; one property (name-giving) synthesizes rest (Brandenstein “round”/“flat” clusters). Attributes complementary within class (not contrastive until full system); identification does not primarily list common properties but proceeds from prototype coherence.
- **Page targets:** [[totemism]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "in this case the prototype is even more abstract than that used by animism, for it does not exist as a phenomenal object… it is the core of physical and moral properties that identify each of its members"
- **Notes:** Completes totemic classification account.

### C-5-066: Analogism excels at classification by attributes (Hong fan example)
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Analogical identification classifies by attributes: every being decomposable into elements/characteristics pairable as opposites—table of correspondences = table of contrasts. Chinese *Hong fan*: five phases correlated with human activities and celestial signs as contrastive semantic fields, indefinitely extensible. Exhaustive analogical knowledge tends to require writing/graphics to expand attribute tables without taxing memory.
- **Page targets:** [[analogism]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "As for the analogical mode of identification, this mode does classify according to attributes and, indeed, excels at doing so… the repertory of analogies and contrasts is in principle limitless"
- **Notes:** Ties writing to analogical knowledge systems.

### C-5-067: Naturalism’s elementary attribute classification and racial taxonomies
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Naturalism operates by elementary attribute classification: humans = physicality + interiority; nonhumans = physicality − interiority. Refinements mostly on physicality—reinserting man into nature via anatomy/physiology after moral separation (Buffon, Linnaeus dichotomous physical features). Within-species distinctions also physicalized (Blumenbach’s five races by skin, hair, face)—classification by attributes still official in US categories (“Caucasian,” “African,” “American Indian”). National character tables existed without scientific pretension.
- **Page targets:** [[naturalism-ontology]], [[interiority-and-physicality]], [[nature-culture-distinction]], [[four-ontologies]]
- **Grounding quote:** "humans are what they are because they have a physicality plus an interiority; and nonhumans are what they are because they have a physicality minus an interiority… Blumenbach, for instance, classified humans into five races"
