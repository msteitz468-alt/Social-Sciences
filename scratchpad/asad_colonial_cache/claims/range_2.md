### R2-C001
- **Claim:** Current (c. 1970s) criticisms of colonial-period social anthropology admit that many anthropologists were liberal or even radical politically, but treat that fact as irrelevant to analyzing the subject's development or colonial place.
- **Register:** P
- **Attribution:** Wendy James (summarizing critical literature)
- **Grounding quote:** "Current criticisms of the social anthropology of the colonial period acknowledge that more than a handful of individual anthropologists were of liberal or even radical political outlook. But they usually maintain that this fact is not relevant for analysis of the development of the subject or its place in the colonial situation."
- **Wiki targets:** [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]], [[asad-anthropology-colonial-encounter-1973]], [[colonial-encounter]], [[anthropology]]
- **Notes:** Opens the essay against the "irrelevant liberalism" line.

- **Register:** P
- **Attribution:** Kathleen Gough (quoted by Wendy James)

### R2-C032
