### Claim 10: Six premises Levinson attributes to Whorf’s linguistic relativity
- **Register:** THEORETICAL
- **Target pages:** [linguistic-relativity], [cryptotype], [whorf-benjamin-lee], [whorf-language-thought-reality-1956]
- **Claim:** Levinson extracts six central tenets: (1) thought/perception have certain independence from language; (2) some thinking is deeply interconnected with language concepts; (3) some interconnections are backgrounded in grammatical distinctions, including covert concepts without direct surface exponents that highlight specific percepts; (4) such concepts exist beyond conscious awareness and may influence classifications/reactions; (5) grammatical distinctions form an interconnecting web—a partial take on the perceptual world linked to language and culture; (6) different languages’ takes are invaluable correctives to common sense.
- **Quote:** "1. Thought and perception have a certain independence from language (207–208). 2. Nevertheless some aspects of thinking are deeply interconnected with language and the concepts it provides (84–87). 3. Some of these interconnections are backgrounded because the concepts are implicit in grammatical distinctions. Some grammatical concepts are especially covert… 4. Nevertheless such grammatical concepts, especially when they are covert, exist beyond our conscious awareness and may influence our classifications and reactions… 5. These grammatical distinctions form an interconnecting web of concepts… 6. The ways in which languages offer different such takes make them invaluable correctives to our own commonsense views…"
- **Grounding:** slice ll. 79–110
