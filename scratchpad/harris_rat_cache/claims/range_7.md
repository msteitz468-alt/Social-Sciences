### C7-002: Harris — Durkheim vs Spencer on industrial society and the individual
- **Register:** theoretical (Harris’s reconstruction of Durkheim’s critique of Spencer)
- **Claim:** Harris reports that the superficial resemblance between Spencer’s military/industrial types and Durkheim’s mechanical/organic solidarity hides, for Durkheim, Spencer’s fundamental error: Spencer expected contractual individualism to expand until society was only voluntary private conventions, with social solidarity as spontaneous accord of individual interests.
- **Targets:** [[harris-marvin]], [[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968]]
- **Quote:** "According to Spencer, the sphere of contractual relationships is destined to expand to the point where the entire social organization will consist of nothing but a network of voluntary individual arrangements of convenience."

### C7-003: Harris — Spencer and Marx share a “withering of the state / individual over society” dream
- **Register:** theoretical / position (Harris’s juxtaposition)
- **Claim:** Harris argues there is a “curious similarity” between Spencer’s ultimate industrial phase and Marx’s stateless, classless utopia: both expected the triumph of the individual over society (Rousseauan dream), treating withering of the state as withering of the whole supra-individual sociocultural nexus of restraint.

- **Register:** theoretical (Harris’s reading of Durkheim)
- **Claim:** Harris holds that Durkheim’s distinctive contribution to cultural theory is the insistence that growth of division of labor / organic solidarity intensifies mutual dependence of individual and social group and expands legal-administrative apparatus and moral rules, rather than freeing the individual from social constraint.

- **Quote:** "According to Durkheim, the growth of the division of labor, and hence of organic solidarity, is accompanied by no weakening of the power of the social organism over the individual; there is rather an intensification of the mutual dependence of individual and social group."

### C7-006: Harris — Durkheim rejected racial determinism on methodological grounds
- **Register:** finding (Harris’s report of Durkheim’s Rules)
- **Claim:** Harris reports that Durkheim held sociocultural phenomena cannot be understood as psychobiological instincts or individual willful choices; diverse organizations occur in same-race societies and similarities across races, so racial determinism is an evasion of sociological problems requiring sociological method (unlike Boas’s different anti-racist route).
- **Targets:** [[harris-marvin]], [[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968]], [[anthropology]]
- **Quote:** "No social phenomenon, he argued, can be placed in indisputable dependence on race since the “most diverse forms of organization are found in societies of the same race, while striking similarities are observed between societies of different races” (1938:108). Unlike Boas, Durkheim rejected the appeal to racial factors largely on methodological grounds."
- **Approx locus:** slice L112–122; ~p. 470

- **Register:** theoretical / position (Harris)

### C7-009: Harris — Durkheim’s “social things” fail operationally (group-mind ontology)

- **Quote:** "The phenomenological status which Durkheim attributed to social things is operationally unacceptable.
