## Claims

### C2-001: Spencer–Darwin as products of Western phase plus cumulative science
- **Register:** theoretical-claim (Harris)
- **Claim:** Harris argues that treating Spencer’s and Darwin’s theories as inevitable products of a phase of Western history does not deny cumulative scientific advances; laissez-faire individualism and economic/political liberalism reinforced science via positive feedback, while Darwin’s 1859 materialist account of species origin broke theological authority in the life sciences.
- **Targets:** [[spencer-herbert]], [[harris-marvin]], [[social-evolutionism]], [[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968]]
- **Quote:** "Finally, in 1859, Darwin’s materialist explanation of the origin of species broke the authority of the theologians in the domain of the life sciences."
- **Approx locus:** Ch 5 opening; slice ~ll. 9–26; p. 108

### C2-002: Darwin’s advantage over Lamarck was deep geological time
- **Register:** theoretical-claim (Harris)
- **Claim:** Harris holds that Darwin’s main advantage over Lamarck was not merely a more advanced capitalist phase but the prior confrontation of theology and science over the age of the earth: Lamarck had to fight both organic and geological evolution.
- **Targets:** [[spencer-herbert]], [[social-evolutionism]]
- **Quote:** "It was here that Darwin’s main advantage over Lamarck resided. For Lamarck had to contend with the theologians not only on the question of organic evolution, but on the question of geological evolution as well."
- **Approx locus:** Ch 5 "Why did Charles Darwin succeed…"; ~ll. 34–52; p. 108–109

### C2-003: Hutton’s Vulcanism vs Wernerian Neptunism
- **Register:** fact (as Harris narrates)
- **Claim:** Harris reports James Hutton’s *Theory of the Earth* (1788) as the first sustained refutation of Biblical-flood geology; Hutton explained strata by cumulative natural processes (heat, pressure, weathering) against Werner/Jameson Neptunism (rocks precipitated from brine in creation stages).
- **Targets:** [[social-evolutionism]]
- **Quote:** "Hutton on the other hand avoided the issue of creation altogether and attempted to interpret geomorphological features in terms of the cumulative effect of natural physical and chemical processes such as heat, pressure, and various forms of physical and chemical weathering."
- **Approx locus:** Ch 5 "GEOLOGY SHOWS THE WAY"; ~ll. 76–102; p. 109–110

### C2-004: Buffon, Kant, and Lamarck’s deep chronology
- **Register:** fact / position (as Harris cites)
- **Claim:** Harris notes Buffon’s experimental cooling estimate (~75,000 years, restrained for scripture), Kant’s infinite-universe myriads of centuries, and Lamarck’s *Hydrogeology* (1802) proposal of several billion years—met with contempt; Lamarck saw resistance to deep time as the main obstacle to organic evolution.
- **Targets:** [[social-evolutionism]]
- **Quote:** "Lamarck’s proposal that the earth had existed for several billion years was greeted with even more contempt than his idea that men were descended from fish."
- **Approx locus:** Ch 5 "EIGHTEENTH-CENTURY PRECEDENTS"; ~ll. 108–138; p. 110

### C2-005: Catastrophism salvaged Biblical story; Lyell’s uniformitarianism converted Darwin
- **Register:** fact / theoretical-claim (Harris)
- **Claim:** Harris states that under Cuvier (1812) and Buckland (1823), multi-flood “catastrophism” salvaged Biblical chronology; the turning point was Lyell’s *Principles of Geology* (1830), whose uniformitarianism and expanded time converted Darwin (who carried Lyell on the Beagle) from scriptural middle-of-the-road respectability.
- **Targets:** [[social-evolutionism]]
- **Quote:** "It was Lyell’s uncompromising “uniformitarianism” and the consequent lengthening out of time that converted Darwin from a middle-of-the-road respecter of scriptural authority to a resolutely independent scientist."

### C2-006: Lyell rejected Lamarck but modeled struggle and extinction
- **Register:** position (Lyell via Harris) / theoretical-claim (Harris)

- **Targets:** [[race-and-culture-debate]], [[social-evolutionism]]
- **Quote:** "A faint image of the certain doom of a species less fitted to struggle with some new condition… is presented by the extirpation of savage tribes of men by the advancing colony of some civilized nation."

### C2-010: *Origin* as cultural consolidation, not ideology-free science
- **Register:** theoretical-claim (Harris) / position (contra Himmelfarb)
