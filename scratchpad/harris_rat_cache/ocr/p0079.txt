TYLOR'S DEPENDENCE ON
ARCHAEOLOGICAL DATA

Edward Tylor’s Researches into the Early History of Mankind
may once again serve to demonstrate the importance of pre-Darwinian
archaeological achievements for the theories of the period 1860—go.
It may be said without exaggeration that archaeological evidence is at
least as important as ethnographic evidence for Tylor's conclusions
concerning the overall uniformity of evolutionary change. The “three-
age” classification is employed throughout the book, as well as a

It has been especially the evidence of prehistoric archaeology

anthropological grounds. . . . The finding of ancient stone im-

such as Egypt, Assyria, India, China, Greece, etc., may be ad-

time belonged to the stone age [TYLOR quoted in OPLER 1964a:132].
