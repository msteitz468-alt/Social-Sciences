## Ch.9 Reddy — structure of a cultural crisis (cloth in France)

### C6-001
- **Register:** FACT
- **Claim:** Reddy’s essay is Ch.9 in Part V (“Historical transformations and commodity codes”) of Appadurai (ed.), *The Social Life of Things* (1986), titled “The structure of a cultural crisis: thinking about cloth in France before and after the Revolution,” authored by William M. Reddy.
- **Attribution:** volume structure / Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** PART V heading; CHAPTER 9 title and byline.
- **Targets:** [appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986, reddy-william-m, commodity]
- **Notes:** —

### C6-002
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Reddy argues the French Revolution of 1789–99 brought a general change in how French people thought about commodities: though France had centuries of money-based exchange and rapid commercial expansion without significant change in predominant modes of production (industrial capitalism “all but nonexistent” in 1789 and “still quite exceptional” in 1815), millions adopted a “capitalist mode of evaluating commodities,” involving a change in generally accepted notions of what a commodity was.
- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “The great French Revolution of 1789–99 brought a general change in the way French people thought about commodities… Yet millions of French people in the interim adopted what could only be called a capitalist mode of evaluating commodities. It is the object of this essay to show that this involved a change in the generally accepted notions of what a commodity was.”
- **Targets:** [commodity, commoditization, reddy-william-m, appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986]
- **Notes:** Core thesis: evaluation regime can shift without production-mode revolution.

### C6-003
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Reddy concentrates on cloth as an essential commodity and holds that the shift in commodity thought was conjoined with reconstruction of political institutions, social hierarchy, street-level relations, ideological control, and commercial dependence—without asserting causal priority among these.
- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “even in pursuing this modest goal, one is forced to recognize that political institutions, the social hierarchy… all had to undergo significant reconstruction in conjunction with this shift… (No suggestion about priority is implied here.)”
- **Targets:** [commodity, regimes-of-value, reddy-william-m]
- **Notes:** Cloth as cultural code / window, not isolated economic object.

### C6-004
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Reddy argues thought about commodities (like thought about persons and institutions) is “encyclopedic”: persons must organize vast detail under simple overarching categories and principles; this mental/cultural limit shapes what kinds of change can occur—including during the ancien régime’s political crisis.
- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “thought about commodities… is necessarily encyclopedic in scope… This limitation of the human mind… introduces limits to the kind of change that can occur…”
- **Targets:** [commodity, regimes-of-value, reddy-william-m]
- **Notes:** Theoretical frame for “structure of a cultural crisis.”

### C6-005
- **Register:** POSITION / FACT
- **Claim:** Reddy reports historiographic consensus that revolutionary conflicts cannot be mapped one-to-one onto social classes; the “revolutionary bourgeoisie” once credited with guiding patriot and Jacobin parties has “proved to be a phantom”; even class-conflict accounts concede antagonisms were expressed in a language that could not distinguish classes as such, so painstaking interpretive work on revolutionary language is prerequisite to explanation.
- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986 summarizing Doyle, Furet, etc.
- **Grounding:** “The conflicts of the revolutionary period can no longer be seen as pitting one social class against another… The revolutionary bourgeoisie… has proved to be a phantom… all historians now agree that extremely painstaking interpretive work on revolutionary and prerevolutionary language is a prerequisite…”
- **Targets:** [reddy-william-m, appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986]
- **Notes:** Positions historiography; cites Doyle *Origins*; Furet; Sewell.

### C6-006
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Reddy endorses reading the Revolution’s outbreak as a cultural crisis: old institutions (monarchy, guild/trade restrictions, Church and nobility privileges) had lost legitimacy; forms floated “detached and transmogrified”; normal political and trade guidelines lost credibility; economic and political crisis was cultural because routines and principles no longer held conviction in practice.
- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “Documenting the existence of such a crisis is not difficult… This was a cultural crisis of the first order… In just this sense the economic and political crisis was cultural in nature.”
- **Targets:** [reddy-william-m, commodity]
- **Notes:** Suggested slug: cultural-crisis (historical-analytic, not wiki-asserted).

### C6-008

### C6-009
- **Register:** FACT

- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986; bibliographic n.5
- **Grounding:** “Savary des Bruslons’s Dictionnaire universel du commerce, first published between 1723 and 1730 and reissued… at least six more times… used by real merchants in their day-to-day dealings.”

### C6-010
- **Register:** FACT

- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “He was a royal customs official based in Paris… France… riddled with internal customs barriers… any manufactured article entering Paris legally could be expected to have… seals of inspection… certificates showing what duties had been paid…”
- **Targets:** [reddy-william-m, commodity]
- **Notes:** Institutional embedding of commodity knowledge.

### C6-011

- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986
- **Grounding:** “His was a first attempt to systematize the everyday knowledge and beliefs of merchants, producers, government officials, and consumers about commerce… turn lore into knowledge…”
- **Targets:** [regimes-of-value, commodity, reddy-william-m]

- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986 summarizing Savary

- **Attribution:** Reddy 1986

### C6-041
- **Register:** THESIS
