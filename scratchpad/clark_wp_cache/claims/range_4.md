## Entity / label flags (do not equate)

1. **Battle-axe / Corded-ware / single-grave** = Clark’s archaeological-culture / ceramic–weapon labels (Late Neolithic pastoral groups). **Not** equated here with [[yamnaya-culture]] or modern genetic “steppe ancestry.” Clark derives barrow burial “from the steppe region further east” and places Battle-axe homeland between Vistula–Baltic–Dnieper; he does **not** use “Yamnaya.” **Four-way non-identity:** Battle-axe ≠ Corded Ware ≠ Yamnaya ≠ genetic populations (flag for [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]], [[pots-and-peoples-debate]]).
2. **Bell-Beaker** = Clark’s ceramic/archer package from Iberia and Rhenish routes; archaeological culture label only—not a genetic population ([[bell-beaker-culture]]; [[pots-and-peoples-debate]]).
3. **Corded-ware folk** (Clark’s phrase for highland SW German / Saale–Warthe BA descendants) = ceramic/cultural label; not genetic Corded Ware DNA cluster ([[corded-ware-culture]]).
4. **Urnfield / Lausitz / Hallstatt / La Tène** = culture-historical complexes; Clark links Lausitz to “basis of the Slav people” as Polish prehistorians’ view—flag ethnic reading ([[lausitz-culture]]; [[pots-and-peoples-debate]]).
5. **Minyan / Middle Helladic warriors** = Clark’s IE/Greek-language hypothesis via grey Minyan ware distribution; culture ≠ people automatic equation flagged.
6. **Slug mismatches:** Clark “Starčevo/Startevo/Starćevo,” “Danubian I (spiral-meander),” “Funnel-neck Beaker,” “Battle-axe,” “Beaker,” “Aunjetitz,” “Lausitz,” “Urnfield,” “Hötting,” “Knovíz,” “Villanovan,” “El Argar,” “Sesklo,” “Dimini,” “Boian,” “Cucuteni/Tripolje,” “Lengyel,” “Rössen,” “Michelsberg,” “Cortaillod,” “Chassey,” “Horgen,” “Seine–Oise–Marne,” “Fatyanovo,” “Remedello,” “Tarxien.” Map to wiki where listed; else free-text / NEW.

## Claims

### C4-001: european-neolithic-depends-on-spread-of-farming
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM
- **Claim:** Clark 1961 argues that settled life in Europe and the possibility of a distinct European civilization depended first on the spread of farming from focal territories of domestication and the adoption of this alien way of life by indigenous hunter-fishers.
- **Attribution:** Clark 1961
- **Targets:** [[clark-world-prehistory-1961]], [[clark-grahame]], [[neolithic]], [[neolithic-revolution]], [[world-prehistory]], [[ex-oriente-lux]]
- **Grounding quote:** “The beginnings of settled life in Europe and the possibility of a distinct European civilization depended in the first instance on the spread of farming from the focal territories… and on the adoption… by indigenous hunter-fishers.”

### C4-003: european-neolithic-later-than-sw-asia-contemporary-with-asian-metallurgy
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Clark states earliest European peasant communities began much later than in Palestine, Cilicia, Syria and northern Mesopotamia (though radiocarbon suggests no later than the Nile Valley), and were “Neolithic” (pre-metallurgy, flint/stone technology) while SW Asia already cast copper (later 5th millennium B.C.) and bronze (mid-4th).
- **Attribution:** Clark 1961 **(as of 1961 chronology)**
- **Targets:** [[clark-world-prehistory-1961]], [[neolithic]], [[three-age-system]], [[ex-oriente-lux]]
- **Grounding quote:** “settled life began much later than in Palestine, Cilicia, Syria and northern Mesopotamia… early peasant communities in Europe were ‘Neolithic’… contemporary with societies in western Asia conversant… with the casting of copper…”

### C4-004: northern-neolithic-adopts-megalithic-collective-burial-from-aegean
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM
- **Claim:** Clark argues northern “Neolithic” peoples, even when too poor for metal tools, adopted collective burial from the technologically more advanced Aegean and erected megalithic chamber tombs—illustrating their subordinate cultural status relative to richer Aegean societies.
- **Attribution:** Clark 1961
- **Targets:** [[clark-world-prehistory-1961]], [[neolithic]], [[diffusionism]], [[ex-oriente-lux]], [[diffusion-collapse-european-prehistory-debate]]
- **Grounding quote:** “they adopted the rite of collective burial from the technologically more advanced Aegean world… to erect megalithic chamber tombs.”

### C4-005: desiccation-challenge-explains-sw-asian-lead-over-europe
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM
- **Claim:** Clark theorizes SW Asia led Europe in farming because Late Pleistocene–Neothermal desiccation of rich hunting grounds challenged peoples ready to transform their relation to environment, whereas Europe’s gradual warming offered no comparable challenge and encouraged Mesolithic persistence of Old Stone Age ways.
- **Attribution:** Clark 1961
- **Targets:** [[clark-world-prehistory-1961]], [[neolithic-revolution]], [[mesolithic]], [[palaeolithic]], [[economic-prehistory]], [[clark-grahame]]
- **Grounding quote:** “an environmental change which precipitated the so-called Neolithic Revolution in one, encouraged in the other the persistence… of old ways of life… expressed in the Mesolithic cultures…”

### C4-006: farming-expansive-after-altithermal-wetting-three-european-zones
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM
- **Claim:** Clark holds the farming economy was inherently expansive but spread to North Africa, SE Europe and Inner Asia only after wetter Altithermal conditions returned near the focal region; Europe as a colonization field fell into three ecological zones—Mediterranean (easy penetration), temperate (selective/difficult), and circumpolar (only filtered Neolithic elements; hunting–fishing supreme).
- **Attribution:** Clark 1961
- **Targets:** [[clark-world-prehistory-1961]], [[neolithic]], [[economic-prehistory]], [[world-prehistory]]

### C4-030: aegean-maritime-trade-cyclades-west-mediterranean
