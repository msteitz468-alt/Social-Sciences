### C1.40 Darwin, sexology, Freud: unsettling natural fixed characters
- **Register:** FACT / THEORETICAL (history of ideas)
- **Claim:** Darwin’s *Descent of Man* (1874) sexual selection made sex a matter of observation/comparison; long-term effect unsettled for patriarchy by making patterns need explanation. - **Quote/grounding:** "Freud’s arguments wrecked the notion of natural fixed characters for the two sexes… Psychoanalysis thus produced detailed and sophisticated accounts of femininity and masculinity as psychological forms constructed by social processes." (lines 1275–1351)

### C1.44 Mid-century synthesis: Mead, Parsons, de Beauvoir
- **Register:** THEORETICAL (historiography)

### C1.46 Sex-role research boom underpins liberal feminism

### C1.47 Radical feminism: power analysis beyond sex roles
- **Register:** THEORETICAL (position recording)

### C1.48 Social reproduction and socialist-feminist capitalism analyses
