### C-3-001: Erésd agriculture and domestic animals
- **Register:** FINDING (attributed to Childe 1929 / site reports as he cites)
- **Claim:** Childe reports that Erésd-type painted-pottery communities were food producers: saddle querns at Erésd imply agriculture (grains not yet studied); flint “saws” interpreted as sickle-teeth; Priesterhügel animal bones include domestic cattle (Primigenius-related), sheep (possibly mouflon), swine, and Bezoar goat, plus abundant game.
- **Page targets:** [[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929]], [[childe-v-gordon]], [[archaeological-culture]], [[neolithic]]
- **Grounding quote:** "saddle querns have been reported from Erésd and prove the existence of some sort of agriculture… The animal bones from Priesterhiigel reveal the presence of domestic cattle… sheep… swine, and the Bezoar goat"
- **Notes:** Opens mid-Ch. VI; Erésd = painted pottery group of Upper Alt (Olt).

### C-3-002: Erésd weaving and absence of graves
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Childe attributes perforated clay loom-weights (and disk spindle-whorls) to weaving at Erésd-type sites, and states that no graves or skeletal remains are known from the twenty-six Upper Alt stations.
- **Page targets:** [[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929]], [[archaeological-culture]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Numerous round or pyramidal lumps of baked clay, carefully perforated, may have served as loom weights… No graves or skeletal remains have been found in connexion with any of the twenty-six stations along the Upper Alt."
- **Notes:** Burial rite for Erésd culture unknown in Childe’s dossier.

### C-3-003: Erésd settlement concentrates on Upper Alt löss strips

- **Claim:** Childe maps Erésd-type painted pottery thickly along the Upper Alt above the Kronstadt elbow and the Feketeügy (Schwarzbach); he explains the concentration by strips of elevated open löss, not copper lodes or through-routes (upper reaches “do not even lead anywhere”).

### C-3-011: Schroller’s chronology — Erésd later than Danubian II crusted ware
- **Register:** POSITION (Schroller) / CRITIQUE (Childe)
- **Claim:** Childe summarizes Schroller: painting in the Danube basin from Menghin’s “vorderasiatischer Kulturkreis” via Thessaly I (Sesklo) as Susa–el-Obeid “textile design” outpost; Erésd I equated with latest Polgár (Palliardi C) so Transylvanian/Moldavian/Ukrainian painted cultures would be later than Danubian II crusted ware and thus dependent. Childe objects on technique (carboniferous Danubian vs red Erésd), art principles, vessel repertoire, absence of perforated celt, and Thessalian equation (Erésd = Dimini II; crusted ware first in Thessaly III).
- **Page targets:** [[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929]], [[diffusionism]], [[culture-historical-archaeology]]
- **Grounding quote:** "Schroller’s chronological scheme is not without difficulty… In Thessaly Erésd is to be equated with period II (Dimini); crusted ware of Danubian II style appears first in the succeeding period III."
- **Notes:** Method: relative chronology via interregional ceramic equations.

### C-3-027: Danordic houses and heterogeneous burial rites
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** Architecture non-uniform: rectangular post-built houses at Nosswitz; pit-dwellings and square wattle-and-daub huts elsewhere. - **Grounding quote:** "Antoniewicz and Zurowski conclude that ‘Corded Ware’ was just a special funerary fabric, manufactured by the ‘Nordic’ villagers."
- **Notes:** Important Poland-specific interpretation of Corded–Nordic relation.
