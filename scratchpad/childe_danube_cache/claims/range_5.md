## Summary
Range closes Childe’s Early Bronze Age account of the **Aunjetitz (Únětice) culture** and its trade-route outposts, then sets his **Reinecke-based absolute chronology** (Periods IV–VI ≈ 1850–900 B.C. in his table) via Mycenaean/Byblos synchronisms. Ch.XIII builds Hungarian relative chronology from **stratified riverine tells** (Pécska A–F; Tószeg A/B1/B2/C/D), not typology alone; Phase B yields the earliest claimed **bridle-bit cheek-piece** and megaron-like houses. Ch.XIV maps five **Middle Bronze Age urnfield groups** on the Hungarian plain as amalgamations of Danubian, Perjamos, Corded, and prospector elements, with strong **Aegean/Anatolian** reflexions (cowries, fayence, fibulae, Minoan dress on idols, Trojan lids) and internal debate over early (Period V / 14th c.) vs late (Hallstatt / 1000–600) dating—Childe prefers **~1400–1000 B.C.** Ch.XV opens the parallel **Tumulus Bronze** upland province.
