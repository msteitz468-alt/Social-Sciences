## Claim R4-031: Production development = adaptation + expansion + intensification
- **Register:** theoretical-claim
- **Claim:** Kirch defines development of production systems as encompassing three components: (1) adaptation to environmental conditions; (2) expansion (more land cleared, herds enlarged, etc.); (3) intensification—increased labour input (permanent facilities or daily labour) for relative yield increase. - **Grounding quote:** "The term development must be understood here in a broad sense, as encompassing three major components: (1) adaptation (2) expansion, and (3) intensification. … intensification, which unlike expansion, involves an increased labour input (either for the construction of permanent facilities, such as pond-fields, or in the daily labour input to agriculture and fishing) to achieve a relative increase in yields."
