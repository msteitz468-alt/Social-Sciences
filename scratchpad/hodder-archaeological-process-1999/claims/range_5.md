## C5-001
- **Register:** theoretical claim
- **Claim:** Moves toward diversity, fluidity, and reflexivity in archaeology are not merely intellectual outgrowths of feminist, dialectical, or post-processual critique of processual archaeology; they must be contextualized within broader economic, social, and cultural processes in which archaeology and heritage are embedded.
- **Attribution:** Hodder 1999, Ch9 opening
- **Grounding quote:** "It is inadequate to see these themes as intellectual outgrowths or logical results of feminist or dialectical archaeology or of the post-processual critique of processual archaeology. … it is necessary for archaeologists to understand the broader economic, social and cultural processes within which archaeology and heritage are embedded."

## C5-074
