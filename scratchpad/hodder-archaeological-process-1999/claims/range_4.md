## C4-005
- **Register:** theoretical claim
- **Claim:** Geology (and related historical sciences) is historical because: (1) results are historically situated via pre-judgements; (2) laboratory experiment has a limited role relative to chronicling particular events (hypotheses about singular events not fully testable as general laws); (3) narrative/central themes organize data into intelligible wholes, not only subsumption under predictive generalizations.
- **Attribution:** Hodder summarizing Frodeman 1995; Latour and Woolgar 1986 cited on social factors in experimental science
- **Grounding quote:** “the aim in experimenting with, for example, chemical reactions is not to describe specific historical conditions… But in geology, at least part of the enterprise is to chronicle the particular events… Because of the singularity of these events, hypotheses are not testable… A third historical aspect of geological enquiry concerns the centrality of narrative… Geologists do not simply work by subsuming events under predictive generalizations. Rather, they try to integrate events into organized wholes which have an overall structure or story.”

## C4-027
- **Register:** methodological / theoretical claim
- **Claim:** Harris matrix reduces 3D soil relationships to simplified 2D codified relations (above/below/same as); actual stratigraphic understanding depends on interpretation and narrative (equivalent floors without contact; foundation deposits without visible cut; sherd joins)—so alternative matrices under competing hypotheses with annotated narratives are needed; Chadwick (1998) argues for more interpretive components (fills in earlier capped drains; pits dug while deposits still forming).
- **Attribution:** Hodder 1999; Harris et al. 1993; Chadwick 1998
- **Grounding quote:** “the Harris matrix approach assumes clear-cut contexts and clear-cut relations. Three-dimensional soil relationships are reduced to a simplified and codified two dimensions… understanding the stratigraphic relationships of a context is complex — it depends on interpretation and narrative… it is necessary to embed the matrix of stratigraphical relations in a narrative.
