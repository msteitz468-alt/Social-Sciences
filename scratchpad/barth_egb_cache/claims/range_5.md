### C1
- **Claim:** Ethnography/social anthropology has mainly aimed at describing and analysing separate social systems (mono-ethnic groups), partly under influence of the old national-romantic movement that stressed each people’s national characteristics and value systems.
- **Register:** THEORETICAL
- **Attribute:** Izikowitz 1969 (Neighbours in Laos)
- **Grounding:** Opening paragraph: “Ethnography or social anthropology has hitherto aimed mainly at describing and analysing separate social systems…”; national romantic movement as possible partial cause; defers history of ideas to specialists.
- **Links:** polyethnic-system; ethnic-boundary-theory
- **Notes:** Frames the volume’s turn from mono-ethnic to poly-ethnic analysis.

### C2
- **Claim:** When a group seeks to improve status and foreground its way of life, the problem becomes “neighbourhood” — relations of different peoples living next to each other — i.e. a turn from mono-ethnic to poly-ethnic groups.
- **Register:** THEORETICAL
- **Attribute:** Izikowitz 1969
- **Grounding:** “As soon as a group wishes to improve its status… it is faced with the problem of neighbourhood or — as I should like to call it — the relationship of different peoples living next to each other.” Leaves separate societies for “neighbouring groups — poly-ethnic groups.”
- **Links:** polyethnic-system; ethnic-boundary
- **Notes:** Izikowitz’s preferred term “neighbourhood” for inter-ethnic adjacency.

### C7

- **Register:** THEORETICAL / FINDING (comparative sketch)

### C27

- **Register:** THEORETICAL
- **Attribute:** Izikowitz 1969
- **Grounding:** Numbered three categories; “introspective… directed towards the group of identification”; situational identification.
- **Links:** ethnic-boundary; ethnic-ascription; diacritical-features
