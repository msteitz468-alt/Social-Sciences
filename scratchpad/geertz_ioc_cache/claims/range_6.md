## Chapter 13 — The Cerebral Savage: On the Work of Claude Lévi-Strauss
**Targets:** levi-strauss-claude · structuralism · geertz-clifford · interpretive-anthropology

### IOC6-001 | Opening: “savage” as key to anthropologist’s work | A/P | C16–32
Geertz opens: after three centuries of debate on what savages are, “we still don’t know.” For the anthropologist, “know what he thinks a savage is and you have the key to his work… All ethnography is part philosophy, and a good deal of the rest is confession.”
- **Q:** “All ethnography is part philosophy, and a good deal of the rest is confession.” (C31–32)
- **Register:** A (Geertz’s methodological credo)

### IOC6-002 | Lévi-Strauss: personal quest vs positive science | A | C34–62
Geertz presents dual face of Lévi-Strauss (Collège de France): (1) profession as personal quest/salvation, quoting *Tristes Tropiques* (“I owe myself to mankind…”; pebble/wave image); (2) ethnology as positive science—“ultimate goal of the human sciences is not to constitute man but to dissolve him”; invariants; reintegration of culture into nature/physico-chemical conditions.
- **Q:** “The ultimate goal of the human sciences is not to constitute man but to dissolve him.” (C54–55)
- **Targets:** levi-strauss-claude · structuralism

### IOC6-003 | Two faces of anthropology forced into confrontation | A/P | C64–83
In Lévi-Strauss the two faces—way of going at the world vs method for lawful empirical relations—are turned inward into confrontation (vs usual ethnologist avoidance). Power and appeal; also professional suspicion that “High Science” may defend a metaphysical position, ideology, moral cause. “Every man has a right to create his own savage… But to demonstrate that such a constructed savage corresponds to Australian Aborigines… is another matter.”
- **Q:** “Every man has a right to create his own savage for his own purposes… But to demonstrate that such a constructed savage corresponds to…” (C79–83)
- **Register:** P (critical caution, Marx comparison)

### IOC6-004 | *Tristes Tropiques* as Heroic Quest, not great anthropology | A | C85–102
*Tristes Tropiques* (1955): “very far from being a great anthropology book… is surely one of the finest books ever written by an anthropologist.” Design = standard Heroic Quest legend: departure from stultifying France → Brazilian jungles (Cuduveo/Caduveo, Bororo, Nambikwara, Tupi-Kawahib) → return exhausted with obligation to communicate.
- **Q:** “though it is very far from being a great anthropology book, or even an especially good one, is surely one of the finest books ever written by an anthropologist” (C88–90)
- **Targets:** levi-strauss-claude

### IOC6-005 | Spoiled tropics; delayed Columbus | A (Geertz retelling L-S) | C114–132
Sea journey as prelude; classical navigators sailed to Garden of Eden “spared the agitations of ‘history’”; Lévi-Strauss sails to a spoiled world destroyed by greed and cultural arrogance. Tropics “historical where once eternal, and social where once metaphysical.” Rio disappointing. “The tropics are not so much exotic as out of date.”
- **Q:** “The tropics are not so much exotic as out of date.” (C131–132)

### IOC6-006 | São Paulo 1935: “former savages”; four groups deeper into jungle | W/A | C134–170
By 1935 no native Indians near São Paulo; reservation Indians as “former savages.” Four groups: Caduveo (body tattoos as formal representation of decayed social organization); Bororo (reduced but village pattern/clan/religion); Nambikwara (simple bands → Rousseau social contract support); Tupi-Kawahib (uncontaminated and unstudied—“Crusoe country”).
- **Finding (attributed to L-S via Geertz):** Nambikwara political organization supports Rousseau’s social-contract theory (C164–167).

### IOC6-007 | Tupi-Kawahib climax: too savage to communicate | A | C172–213
Expectation of first-white-man gnosis; disappointment—could not communicate; “They were as close to me as an image seen in a looking-glass. I could touch, but not understand them.” Mistake of profession: belief some men more deserving of interest because manners astonish; when known, strangeness drops away.
- **Q:** “I could touch, but not understand them.” (C195–196)
- **Q:** “Between these two extremes, what are the equivocal cases which afford us [anthropologists] the excuses by which we live?” (C204–206)

### IOC6-008 | Anthropologist’s double infirmity; model vs phenomenological entry | A | C215–260
Either contaminated “true savages” (few left) or unintelligible pure ones. “I am the victim of a double infirmity: what I see is an affliction to me; what I do not see a reproach.” Escape: construct theoretical model of society from particles/debris corresponding to none observable, yet toward basic foundations of human existence—because mind of man is “at bottom, everywhere the same.” Not phenomenological storming of citadels (impossible) but intellectual reconstitution from filth-covered remains. *Tristes Tropiques* romanticism → *La Pensée Sauvage* (1962) “exultant sciencism.”
- **Q:** “the mind of man is, at bottom, everywhere the same” (C243–244)
- **Q:** “What a journey to the heart of darkness could not produce, an immersion in structural linguistics, communication theory, cybernetics, and mathematical logic can.” (C256–258)
- **Targets:** structuralism · levi-strauss-claude

### IOC6-009 | Customs as ordered systems; anthropology = study of thought | A (L-S via Geertz) | C264–281
From Caduveo tattoos idea: totality of customs forms ordered whole; limited number of systems; societies choose combinations from repertory. Ethnologist: describe surface patterns → reconstitute deeper structures → classify like Mendeleev table. “Anthropology is only apparently the study of customs, beliefs, or institutions. Fundamentally it is the study of thought.”
- **Q:** “Fundamentally it is the study of thought.” (C280–281)
- **Targets:** structuralism

### IOC6-010 | Science of the concrete; kaleidoscope; Boas on myth | A (L-S via Geertz) | C283–335
Savage “science of the concrete”: models not by abstract theory but by ordering perceived particulars into immediately intelligible wholes (kangaroos/ostriches, flood waters, moon phases). “Savage thought extends its grasp by means of imagines mundi.” Kaleidoscope metaphor: chips rearrange, patterns = relations not individual properties; limited transformations. Odds and ends from myth, ritual, magic, empirical lore—“residue of events.” Boas quote generalized: mythological worlds shattered and rebuilt from fragments; permutational shuffling of discrete concrete images.
- **Q:** “It fashions mental constructions which render the world intelligible to the degree that they contrive to resemble it.” (C298–299)
- **Q:** “Both anecdotal and geometric, it builds coherent structures out of ‘the odds and ends left over from psychological or historical process.’” (C314–315)

### IOC6-011 | Totemism as logical parallel, not nature worship | A (L-S) | C337–370
Totemism not autonomous institution; logical parallel between natural and cultural series—order of differences isomorphic. Bear/eagle/turtle vs clans A/B/C: not specific animal traits but sensible contrast. “History” casts up arbitrary terms; as ordered set they symbolize social structure. Meaning basis is “logical,” coincidence of form—not affective, historical, or functional. “Savage thought is frozen reason”; anthropology “like music and mathematics, ‘one of the few true vocations.’”
- **Q:** “Savage thought is frozen reason” (C368–369)
- **Targets:** structuralism · levi-strauss-claude

### IOC6-012 | Linguistics analogy; binary opposition as lingua franca | A (L-S via Geertz) | C372–418
Like language: units arbitrary; significance from ordered strings; ur-system subconscious; units defined by differences/pairs. Binary opposition “forms the basis of savage thought as it does of language”—communications systems. Any classification reducible to underlying paired oppositions; structures related by inversion/transposition/substitution; levels of social reality (women, gifts, ritual symbols) isomorphic as communication schemes.
- **Q:** “Binary opposition… forms the basis of savage thought as it does of language.” (C393–395)
- **Targets:** structuralism

### IOC6-013 | Geertz’s graded assessment of L-S socio-logic | P | C420–437
Some essays (totemism) “persuasive and enlightening as far as they go” but exclude metaphysical content/affective aura—so “not really so very far.” Totemism–caste reduction “intriguing if not precisely convincing.” Horse/dog/bird/cattle naming systems “triumphs of self-parody… farfetched enough to make even a psychoanalyst blush.” If eternal universal model of mind/brain can be built from debris, “this may well be the way to build it.”
- **Register:** P (critical hierarchy of examples)
- **Targets:** structuralism · levi-strauss-claude

### IOC6-014 | “Infernal culture machine”; intellectual closeness, physical distance | P | C441–479
L-S has made “an infernal culture machine”: “annuls history, reduces sentiment to a shadow of the intellect, and replaces the particular minds of particular savages… with the Savage Mind immanent in us all.” Circumvents Brazilian impasse (physical closeness/intellectual distance) with what he “always really wanted—intellectual closeness and physical distance.” Rejection of phenomenology (no continuity experience/reality; must repudiate experience) and existentialism (illusions of subjectivity). High Science and Heroic Quest are “very simple transformations” of one another—French Enlightenment universal rationalism. Real guru not de Saussure/Shannon/Boole/Wiener/von Neumann/Marx/Buddha but **Rousseau**.
- **Q:** “an infernal culture machine. It annuls history, reduces sentiment to a shadow of the intellect…” (C441–444)
- **Q:** “it is not de Saussure, or Shannon, or Boole, or Weiner, or von Neumann who is Lévi-Strauss’ real guru… but Rousseau.” (C477–479)
- **Targets:** structuralism · levi-strauss-claude · interpretive-anthropology (contrast)

### IOC6-015 | Search for Man not men; epistemological empathy; neolithic intelligence | A (L-S/Rousseau via Geertz) | C481–515
Like Rousseau: search not for men (doesn’t much care for) but Man. Bridge not personal confrontation (corrupts) but “experimental mind reading” / “epistemological empathy”—try on modes of thought from elsewhere. Needs obsessively detailed ethnography + “neolithic intelligence.”
- **Q:** “search is not after all for men, whom he doesn’t much care for, but for Man, with whom he is enthralled.” (C491–492)
- **Q:** “What one needs, aside from obsessively detailed ethnography, is a neolithic intelligence.” (C514–515)
- **Epigraph echo:** L-S “My intelligence is neolithic.” (C10)

### IOC6-016 | Rousseauian moralism: primary wild thought; neolithic middle ground; reform via anthropology | A (L-S) | C517–569
Savage modes primary, common to all; civilized science secondary/artificial. Best time = neolithic (*société naissante*): science of concrete produced agriculture, husbandry, pottery, weaving, etc. Should have kept middle ground between indolence and *amour propre*; reform by reminders of neolithic achievements → rational future. Anthropology legitimizing savage thought is agency of reform. “The golden age… is in us.”
- **Q:** “The golden age [wrote Rousseau] which blind superstition situated behind or ahead of us is in us.” (C564–565)

### IOC6-017 | Geertz’s closing critique: science or alchemy? | P | C573–612
After Freud/depth psychology, can we still believe “sovereignty of the intellect” as Cerebral Savage? Fieldwork tempts anthropologists where “the mind of man is no dry light.” Doctrine of changeless rational mind seems “merely quaint.” Questions: science or alchemy? real transformation or sleight of hand? genuine demolition of walls between minds or “elaborately disguised evasion” after failure to breach them? Prolegomenon to future anthropology—or “uprooted neolithic intelligence… shuffling the debris of old traditions in a vain attempt to revivify a primitive faith whose moral beauty is still apparent but from which both relevance and credibility have long since departed?”
- **Q:** “Is this transmutation science or alchemy?” (C600–601)
- **Q:** “shuffling the debris of old traditions in a vain attempt to revivify a primitive faith whose moral beauty is still apparent but from which both relevance and credibility have long since departed?” (C609–612)

## Chapter 14 — Person, Time, and Conduct in Bali
**Targets:** geertz-clifford · interpretive-anthropology · thick-description · webs-of-significance · participant-observation

### IOC6-019 | Human thought is consummately social | A | C636–641
“Human thought is consummately social: social in its origins, social in its functions, social in its forms, social in its applications. At base, thinking is a public activity—its natural habitat is the houseyard, the marketplace, and the town square.”
- **Q:** as above (C636–639)
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology · webs-of-significance

### IOC6-020 | Aim: Balinese person apparatus → culture analysis | A | C643–666
Inquiry into cultural apparatus by which Balinese define/perceive/react to individual persons; odd (from Western view) enough to illuminate general relations between person-perception, time experience, and affective tone of collective life.
- **Fieldwork frame:** Geertz Bali ethnography (implicit 1950s; notes ca. 1958)

### IOC6-021 | Culture vs social structure; avoid Hegelian/Marxist reduction | A | C670–694
Recent theorizing distinguishes culture and social structure to take ideational factors seriously without Hegelian (ideas as soul of history) or Marxist (ideas as shadows of organization) reductionism. Ideas as independent but not self-sufficient—act within social contexts. Marc Bloch merchants/Holbein/Erasmus/Luther quotation.
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology

### IOC6-022 | Thought as traffic in significant symbols; culture as orienting machinery | A | C712–730
Thought is not Ryle’s “secret grotto in the head” but “traffic in significant symbols”—objects (rituals, tools, idols, gestures, markings, images, sounds) with impressed meaning. Culture study = positive science; meanings discoverable empirically. Culture patterns = ordered clusters of significant symbols; culture = accumulated totality of such patterns—machinery to orient in otherwise opaque world.
- **Q:** “It is through culture patterns, ordered clusters of significant symbols, that man makes sense of the events through which he lives.” (C725–727)
- **Cross-ref:** Chapter 3 extrinsic theory of thought (note C2434–2440)
- **Targets:** webs-of-significance · thick-description · interpretive-anthropology

### IOC6-023 | Need scientific phenomenology of culture | A | C769–792
Structural analysis and personality theory need categories “already known.” Need systematic method to describe meaningful structure of experience as apprehended by representative members at a point in time—“a scientific phenomenology of culture.”
- **Q:** “a scientific phenomenology of culture.” (C792)
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology · thick-description

### IOC6-024 | Schutz: predecessors, contemporaries, consociates, successors | A | C796–916
Alfred Schutz disaggregation of “fellowmen”: **consociates** (share time + space; face-to-face; grow older together); **contemporaries** (share time not space; linked by cultural assumptions about typical behavior; graduation to anonymity); **predecessors/successors** (no shared time). Actor-oriented; culturally formulated; “Consociates, contemporaries, predecessors, and successors are as much made as born.”
- **Q:** “Consociates, contemporaries, predecessors, and successors are as much made as born.” (C916)
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology

### IOC6-025 | Six Balinese symbolic orders of person-definition | W | C920–934
(1) personal names; (2) birth order names; (3) kinship terms; (4) teknonyms; (5) status titles (“caste names”); (6) public titles (chiefs, rulers, priests, gods). Used alternatively by situation; “symbolic orders of person-definition.”
- **Caveat (Geertz):** schematizes; homogeneity overstated (note C2473–2486)

### IOC6-026 | Personal names: private residual identity | W/A | C938–993
All have personal names; rarely used; sacrilegious re forebears. Commoners (~90%): arbitrarily coined nonsense syllables; no pool of names; no naming-after; mononyms; duplication avoided within hamlet (*bandjar*); no nicknames/diminutives. Name = residual when other labels removed; intensely private; near end of life only self may know it. Mutes idiosyncratic “personality” for conventional enduring aspects.
- **Gentry exception (note):** Sanskrit names ornamental, not denotative of identity (C2488–2498)

### IOC6-027 | Birth order names: circular succession of forms | W/A | C997–1046
Wayan, Njoman, Made/Nengah, Ktut cycling; used for children and childless young adults; childless adults remain symbolically “children”—shame/embarrassment. Not genuine classes or psychological types; may not match actual birth rank (demography). Suggests births as “endless four-stage replication of an imperishable form”: men ephemeral, *dramatis personae* socially eternal.
- **Q:** “Physically men come and go as the ephemerae they are, but socially the dramatis personal’ remain eternally the same” (C1042–1044)

### IOC6-028 | Kinship terms: Hawaiian/generational; taxonomic not face-to-face | W/A | C1050–1111
Hawaiian/generational terminology; almost never vocative; referential only when kin info relevant. Children use same public terms for parents as outsiders. System as cultural map of social field; kin norms overridden by religion, politics, stratification. Personhood contribution: “immobilization of time through the iteration of form.”

### IOC6-029 | Kumpi reciprocity; cylindrical generations; unreality of progression | W/A | C1124–1178
Third generation up/down: *kumpi* = great-grandparent AND great-grandchild (reciprocal). Four-generation span = completed life ideal. Funeral homage stops at grandchildren; great-grandchildren “same age,” forbidden to pray to deceased. Image: cylinder of six bands not layer cake—spiritual relations among coexisting generations, not unrepeating historical process.
- **Q:** “what at first glance seems a very diachronic formulation… is, in fact, an assertion of the essential unreality—or anyway the unimportance—of such a progression.” (C1162–1165)

### IOC6-030 | Teknonymy: Father/Mother-of; procreational strata; steady state | W/A | C1180–1302
Dominant address for peasantry: Father/Mother-of → Grandfather/Mother-of → Great-grandparent-of as first child/grandchild/great-grandchild named. Identifies marital pair via procreation not surname. Parental couple = fundamental social building block (hamlet council seats, etc.). Procreational strata hierarchy: childless minors → fathers active citizens → grandfathers elders → great-grandfathers half-returned to gods. Not number of children but reproductive continuity. One defined by whom one is ancestral to, not ancestors (genealogical amnesia). Bateson’s “steady state.”
- **Q:** “an individual is not perceived in the context of who his ancestors were… but rather in the context of whom he is ancestral to.” (C1281–1284)
- **Q:** “what looks like a celebration of a temporal process is in fact a celebration of the maintenance of… a ‘steady state.’” (C1293–1297)
- **Cross-ref:** H. & C. Geertz, “Teknonymy in Bali” *JRAI* 1964

### IOC6-031 | Status titles: pure prestige; spiritual composition | W/A | C1316–1413
Titles (Ida Bagus, Gusti, Pasek, etc.) attach to **individuals** not groups; independent of structural role. Pure prestige: title dictates demeanor code (speech, posture, dress, eating, marriage, burial, cremation) regardless of personal opinion. Inequality is **religious**—reflection of divine order; title signals spiritual composition not wealth/power/reputation. From gods; rank from mythic “offenses” that debase titles—not moral sins in ethical sense; not historical causes but images of imperfect expression of divine pattern.
- **Q:** “A man’s title does not signal his wealth, his power, or even his moral reputation, it signals his spiritual composition” (C1362–1364)
- **Q:** “It would be difficult to conceive of anything further from the Balinese spirit than Machiavelli’s comment that titles do not reflect honor upon men, but rather men upon their titles.” (C1367–1370)

### IOC6-032 | Varna as hat trick correlating titles with cosmology | W/A | C1426–1506
Four Varna (Brahmana, Satria, Wesia, Sudra); Triwangsa vs Sudra. In Bali Varna labels **titles** not men—correlates prestige system with Hinduism and with ideal coincidence of spiritual worth and social standing (moderate in practice). Exceptions exist but “it is the rule and not the exceptions that the Balinese regard as truly illuminating.” Diversity of talent and historical process “superficial” vs location in standardized immortal status categories.
- **Usage:** Triwangsa addressed by title; Sudras by teknonym in hamlet; *Djero* politeness across hamlets; Varna for conceptualizing hierarchy (C1457–1486)

### IOC6-033 | Public titles / *linggih* (“seat”); doctrine of spiritual eligibility | W/A | C1508–1656
Sharp civic/domestic boundary; public sector as multiple discontinuous realms (hamlet, temple, irrigation society, regional government/worship). Officers absorb identity into titles (Klian, Pedanda, Anak Agung, etc.). **Doctrine of spiritual eligibility:** translocal seats for Triwangsa; local for Sudras; eligibility ≠ occupancy. Higher titles blend into god titles; gods nearly faceless—Dewa/Betara + place/function; no interest in divine personalities. Icons of nameless gods = purest expression of Balinese personhood concept.
- **Q:** “it is not what a man is as a man… that matters, but where he fits in a set of cultural categories which not only do not change but, being transhuman, cannot.” (C1612–1614)
- **Q:** “Genuflecting to them… the Balinese are not just acknowledging divine power. They are also confronting the image of what they consider themselves at bottom to be” (C1650–1653)

### IOC6-034 | Cultural triangle: depersonalizing personhood ↔ detemporalizing time | A | C1658–1735
Orders of person-definition depict nearly everyone as “stereotyped contemporaries, abstract and anonymous fellowmen”—“illuminating paradox… they are—in our terms anyway—depersonalizing.” Blunts three sources of temporality: consociates as perishing; weight of dead; impact on unborn. Favors “pure simultaneity” of anonymized contemporaries → “detemporalizing conception of time.”
- **Q:** “The illuminating paradox of Balinese formulations of personhood is that they are—in our terms anyway—depersonalizing.” (C1698–1699)
- **Q:** “Linked to their depersonalizing conception of personhood is a detemporalizing… conception of time.” (C1733–1735)

### IOC6-035 | Permutational calendar: punctual not durational time | W/A | C1737–1811
Two calendars: lunar-solar + permutational (ten concurrent day-name cycles). Main: five-, six-, seven-name cycles → 210-day product; also 35-, 42-, 30-day conjunctions. Classifies qualitative significance of days (like Friday-the-13th), not duration. Cycles “endless, un-anchored, uncountable… without climax. They don’t tell you what time it is; they tell you what kind of time it is.”
- **Q:** “They don’t tell you what time it is; they tell you what kind of time it is.” (C1810–1811)
- **Examples:** rainan (Boda-Klion offerings); Galungan (Boda-Ariang-Klion)

### IOC6-036 | Full/empty days; odalan; fortune; lunar-solar as supplement | W/A | C1813–1964
Holidays (~32; ~1/7 days) spasmodic → “full” vs “empty” days (“times/junctures” vs “holes”). ~20,000 temples; each *odalan* (“emergence” of gods, not anniversary). Secular uses: house-building, business, travel, harvest, spur-sharpening, puppet shows, war/peace; birth *odalan/otonan* indicates destiny; marriage *odalan* matching; medicine; meetings. Lunar-solar: 12 months, tithi/diwasa; double lunar day every 63 days via permutational; leap month every 30 months; *Njepi* (Day of Silence, 10th month new moon—not New Year renewal). Anchored loosely for agriculture; same classificatory “detemporalized” conception.
- **Q:** “What is ‘really real’ is the name… of the day… not its epiphenomenal reflection in the sky.” (C1934–1936)

### IOC6-037 | Ceremonialization; playful theatricality; aesthetic morality | A | C1966–2055
Third side of triangle: ceremonialization of social intercourse—render consociates as contemporaries via high formalization. Manners of “deep spiritual concern.” Style: “playful theatricality”—playfulness not lighthearted, theatricality almost forced. “Balinese social relations are at once a solemn game and a studied drama.” Art, religion, etiquette exalt outward form—“playing.” Radical aestheticism: social acts designed to please “as beauty pleases, not as virtue pleases.”
- **Q:** “Balinese social relations are at once a solemn game and a studied drama.” (C2019–2020)
- **Q:** “acts of courtesy are works of art… not rectitude… but sensibility.” (C2042–2045)

### IOC6-038 | *Lek* as stage fright, not shame | A | C2064–2127
Western “shame cultures” vs “guilt cultures” misleads. Balinese *lek* (translated “shame”) “has nothing to do with transgressions.” Should be “stage fright”: nervousness that aesthetic illusion of etiquette will fail, actor show through part, public standardized identity dissolve into personality. When it occurs, triangle collapses: ceremony evaporates, men become “unwilling consociates locked in mutual embarrassment.” *Lek* protects personhood concepts from individualizing force of face-to-face encounters.
- **Q:** “lek… ought therefore not to be translated as ‘shame,’ but rather… as ‘stage fright.’” (C2095–2099)
- **Q:** “What is feared… is that the public performance that is etiquette will be botched… and that the personality of the individual will then break through to dissolve his standardized public identity.” (C2112–2116)
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology · thick-description

### IOC6-039 | Absence of climax; vectorless now | A | C2129–2175
Social activities do not build to definitive consummations; quarrels blunted; monadic encounters; art starts/goes/stops; ritual often “getting ready and cleaning up”; Rangda-Barong ends in irresolution. “Balinese social life lacks climax because it takes place in a motionless present, a vectorless now.” Person, time, ethos “hooked together by a definable logic… not syllogistic; it is social.”
- **Q:** “Balinese social life lacks climax because it takes place in a motionless present, a vectorless now.” (C2168–2169)
- **Cross-ref:** Bateson & Mead *Balinese Character* on absence of climax

### IOC6-040 | Cultural integration as experiential reinforcement, not immanent meaning | A | C2177–2255
Against logicism (harmony as property of meanings themselves) and psychologism. Meaning imposed by men in society; “study of thought is… the study of men thinking.” Integration = interaction of effects of symbol systems on users—reinforcing circle of contemporizing, blunted aging, episodic events, ceremonialization, steady-state, taxonomic time. Weber’s *Sinnzusammenhang*. Subdominant counter-themes exist (witch beliefs, trance as inverses).
- **Q:** “The study of thought is, to borrow a phrase from Joseph Levenson, the study of men thinking” (C2207–2208)
- **Wittgenstein note:** sign’s life is its use (C2809–2811)

### IOC6-041 | Culture as octopus, not seamless web | A | C2276–2329
Discontinuities real; seamless-web notion as petitio principii as “shreds and patches.” Systems need not be exhaustively interconnected (Ashby). Image of cultural organization: **octopus**—tentacles separately integrated, poorly connected, yet viable. Analysis: significant symbols → clusters → octopoid systems of partial integrations/incongruencies/independencies. Culture moves by disjointed part-movements cumulating to directional change.
- **Q:** “It is rather more the octopus…” (C2301–2305)
- **Targets:** interpretive-anthropology · thick-description

### IOC6-042 | Change pressures from Indonesian nation-state (as of early 1965) | A | C2343–2415
Attacks on person-perception, time, or propriety would transform much of Balinese culture. Likely from without: Bali part of national state centered in Java/Sumatra. Sukarno as all-too-human aging-in-public consociate figure bursting traditional categories (charisma); crisis politics toward climaxes; nationalist historical narrative; urban informality/youth culture/populist equalitarianism threaten ethos side. Speculative but “not wholly groundless”; analysis of cultural concepts as active public forces should aid understanding change.

### IOC6-045 | The police raid narrative (rapport breakthrough) | W | C2911–3048

Village chief had arranged fight, claimed bathing in river; fined 300 rupiah (village raised collectively). Host defends Geertzes as American professors studying culture. Next morning: teased, accepted—“In Bali, to be teased is to be accepted.” Turning point for rapport; opened village; put Geertz onto cockfight as “emotional explosion, status war, and philosophical drama.”

### IOC6-047 | Cock as masculine symbol; double entendre; language | W/A | C3064–3097
Deep psychological identification of Balinese men with cocks; deliberate double entendre (works in Balinese as English). Bateson & Mead: detachable penises suggestion—Geertz neither confirms nor disconfirms from unconscious material; masculinity “about as indubitable… as… water runs downhill.” *Sabung* (cock; inscriptions from A.D. 922) metaphorically = hero, warrior, champion, candidate, bachelor, dandy, lady-killer, tough guy. Pompous man = tailless cock; desperate man = dying cock’s last lunge; stingy = held by tail; shy youth = fighting cock first caged. Court trials, wars, politics, inheritance, arguments compared to cockfights. Island shape as cock challenging “large, feckless, shapeless Java.”
- **Sex exclusion note:** cockfight single-sex male public activity; exception to Bali’s general “unisex” pattern (C4290–4302)

### IOC6-048 | Care of cocks; “I am cock crazy” | W | C3099–3136
Enormous time grooming, feeding (maize kernel by kernel), red pepper in beak/anus for spirit, ceremonial baths like infants, comb crop, spur trim, diamond-merchant inspection. Landlord: “I am cock crazy… We’re all cock crazy.”

### IOC6-049 | Animality inversion; blood sacrifice to demons | W/A | C3138–3194
Cocks also express inversion of human status—animality. Revulsion at animal-like behavior: babies not crawl; bestiality worse than incest; demons animal-form; tooth-filing puberty rite; eating/defecation private/disgusting; dogs treated with phobic cruelty. Identifying with cock = ideal self AND “Powers of Darkness.” Cockfight first a blood sacrifice to demons (chants/oblations); required before temple festivals; responses to illness/crop failure/eruptions; precedes Njepi (Day of Silence). Winner eats loser with mixed “social embarrassment, moral satisfaction, aesthetic disgust, and cannibal joy.” Loser of important fight may wreck family shrines/curse gods—“metaphysical (and social) suicide.” Heaven/hell compared to winning/losing mood.
- **Q:** “In identifying with his cock, the Balinese man is identifying not just with his ideal self, or even his penis, but also… with what he most fears, hates, and… is fascinated by—‘The Powers of Darkness.’” (C3159–3163)
- **Q:** “In the cockfight, man and beast, good and evil, ego and id, the creative power of aroused masculinity and the destructive power of loosened animality fuse in a bloody drama of hatred, cruelty, violence, and death.” (C3182–3185)

### IOC6-050 | Fight structure: ring, spurs, coconut timer, umpire | W | C3196–3312
*Tetadjen/sabungan*; ~50-ft ring; late afternoon to sunset; 9–10 matches (*sehet*); no main match; ad hoc matching (subdued, dissembling). Spurs (*tadji*): razor steel 4–5"; specialist affixers; lore (eclipses, dark moon, kept from women). Coconut sink timer (*tjeng* ~21 sec); handlers; cage if won’t fight. First solid spur blow → pick up; intervals for patching wounded; winner = who expires last if both die. Rules in palm-leaf manuscripts (*lontar*); umpire absolute authority—never questioned in Geertz’s observation; likened to judge/king/priest/policeman. Never open altercation about rules.
- **Focused gathering (Goffman):** cockfight as set engrossed in common activity flow (C3316–3330)

### IOC6-051 | Classical civic centrality vs modern semisecrecy | W | C3332–3348
Pre-Dutch 1908: bringing cock compulsory adult male citizenship duty; fight taxation major public revenue; princely patronage; *wantilan* central near council house, origin temple, market, signal tower, banyan. Today connection less openly stated but “intimate and intact.”

### IOC6-052 | Two bet systems: center even money, side never even | W | C3350–3471
Two *toh*: **toh ketengah** (center, axial, between principals)—always even money, large, collective coalitions (4–8 allies), quiet/furtive with umpire; **toh kesasi** (side, audience)—small, individual, shouting, never even money. Odds paradigm 10–9 through 2–1; underdog backers shout short number; favorite backers shout color-type. Side odds usually move toward shorter end; bulk 4–3 to 8–7. Large-center matches: frenzied last-minute side betting then “intense stillness.” No IOUs to betting opponents; pay before next match; no welshed bets heard.
- **Center bet data (57 matches):** range 15–500 ringgits, mean 85; trimodal (~35, ~70, ~175). Daily wage ~3 ringgits; fights ~every 2.5 days in area (C3381–3393)
- **~10%** of winner’s receipts to umpire/sponsors (note C4354–4360)

### IOC6-053 | Higher center bet → more even match → short side odds + volume | W/A | C3479–3550
Higher center bet → more care for fifty-fifty match (size, condition, spur handicapping, handlers). Stats: overall favorite wins 33/57 (1.4:1); center >60: 1.1:1; >100: 1:1; <40: 1.9:1. Higher center pulls side odds shorter and increases side volume. Center bet is “center of gravity” defining match **depth**.
- **Q:** “the center bet… defines it, signals what, following a notion of Jeremy Bentham’s, I am going to call its ‘depth.’” (C3548–3550)
- **Targets:** deep-play

### IOC6-054 | Bentham’s deep play; money as moral symbol not utility | A | C3571–3633
Bentham (*Theory of Legislation*): deep play = stakes so high that net pain for participants collectively; irrational; should be legally prevented. Yet men engage passionately. For Balinese (implicitly): money less measure of utility than “symbol of moral import.” Shallow games ≈ cash utility; deep games stake **status** (esteem, honor, dignity, respect)—symbolically affirmed or insulted, not actually altered (addicts aside). Money matters, which is why risking more also risks pride, poise, dispassion, masculinity publicly. Meaning imposition (Weber) compensates economic costs.
- **Q:** “in such play, money is less a measure of utility, had or expected, than it is a symbol of moral import, perceived or imposed.” (C3595–3597)
- **Q:** “It is at stake symbolically, for… no one’s status is actually altered by the outcome of a cockfight; it is only, and that momentarily, affirmed or insulted.” (C3605–3608)
- **Q:** “as (to follow Weber rather than Bentham) the imposition of meaning on life is the major end and primary condition of human existence, that access of significance more than compensates for the economic costs involved.” (C3630–3633)
- **Targets:** deep-play · interpretive-anthropology · webs-of-significance

### IOC6-055 | Status gambling vs money gambling hierarchy | W/A | C3634–3703
True material fortune shifts rare in large even-money matches; “real” downward changes among shallow-fight addict gamblers—despised by true cockfighters as fools. Sociomoral hierarchy at fights: women/children/poor at roulette/dice/pea-shell edges; small-match bettors; small/medium fighters; solid citizenry dominate large fights—“true cockfighter” (*bebatoh*, *djuru kurung*). Depth = migration of status hierarchy into cockfight body. Psychologically Aesopian male self; sociologically simulation of social matrix (villages, kin, irrigation, temples, castes). Prestige ascriptive—affirm/defend/celebrate, not seek. Goffman: “a status bloodbath.”
- **Q:** “What makes Balinese cockfighting deep is thus not money in itself, but what, the more of it that is involved the more so, money causes to happen: the migration of the Balinese status hierarchy into the body of the cockfight.” (C3683–3686)
- **Q:** “This apparent amusement and seeming sport is… ‘a status bloodbath.’” (C3701–3703)
- **Targets:** deep-play · geertz-deep-play-study

### IOC6-056 | Tihingan case: 17 structural rules of betting loyalty | W/A | C3705–3888
Village: Tihingan, Klungkung, SE Bali; four large patrilineal partly endogamous descent groups as factions; village vs neighboring villages in cockfight circuit. Rules (Geertz’s numbered 1–17):
1. Almost never bet against own kingroup cock; obliged to support, deeper fights more so.
2. Allied kingroups over unallied.
3. Village cock over outsider; circuit over outside circuit.
4. Distant cocks favored (wouldn’t bring bad one); “away games” mend ruptures home games exacerbate.
5. Almost all matches sociologically relevant; neutral matches dull/shallow.
6. Rarely same group/subfaction fight; outsiders rarely fight each other away.
7. *Puik* (institutionalized non-speaking hostility): heavy maniacal betting against opponent’s masculinity.
8. Center coalition = structural allies only; no outside money.
9. Borrow for a bet not in one—never economically at enemy’s mercy; debts to friends.
10. Don’t ask friend which way if neutrally positioned—strain if opposite.
11. Betting against grain = *mpura* (“pardon me”); bad, worse if large/frequent.
12. *Puik* often formally initiated by “pardon me” deep bet; ended by supporting other’s bird.
13. Cross-loyalty stickiness → wander off for coffee (like American swing voters).
14. Center bettors especially in deep fights = group leaders; solid citizens.
15. Explicit attitude: money secondary, self-balancing circulation; “Oh, God, please let me break even”; prestige wins utterly; remember demolitions not sums.
16. Must bet own group—else “too proud”; home team must bet outside cocks.
17. Peasants state: “like playing with fire only not getting burned”—activate rivalries in play form without open aggression.
- **Q:** “Fighting cocks… is like playing with fire only not getting burned.” (C3883–3884)
- **Targets:** geertz-deep-play-study · deep-play

### IOC6-057 | Formal paradigm: deeper match correlates | A | C3890–3929
**THE MORE A MATCH IS** between near status equals (and/or personal enemies) and between high status individuals → **THE DEEPER THE MATCH**. Deeper → closer cock-man identification (owner advances best cock); finer/more exact matching; greater emotion/absorption; higher bets, shorter outside odds, more overall betting; less “economic” more “status” view; solider citizens. Inverse for shallower (→ coin-spin/dice). No absolute upper limit; legends of classical lord/prince duels deeper than modern possible. Culture hero “The Cockfighter” prince spared because away at deep fight when family assassinated; returns to restore state—archetype of *ksatria* status virtue.
- **Targets:** deep-play · geertz-deep-play-study

### IOC6-058 | Cockfight makes nothing happen; art form of status semblance | A | C3943–3980
Auden “Poetry makes nothing happen” applied: allegorical humiliation day after day; **no one’s status really changes**—cannot ascend/descend ladder via cockfights. Only “aesthetic semblance” of mobility—“behind-the-mirror status jump.” Like art: practical consequences removed → sheer appearances where meaning articulated. “Really real” only to cocks; does not kill/castrate/reduce men, alter hierarchy, redistribute income significantly. Like *Lear*/*Crime and Punishment*: catches death, masculinity, rage, pride, loss, chance—orders them, makes meaning. Function: not assuage or heighten social passions primarily but **display** them “in a medium of feathers, blood, crowds, and money.”
- **Q:** “You cannot ascend the status ladder by winning cockfights; you cannot, as an individual, really ascend it at all.” (C3951–3953)
- **Q:** “its function is neither to assuage social passions nor to heighten them… but… to display them.” (C3977–3980)

### IOC6-060 | Atomistic structure; life in spurts; full/empty times | A | C4013–4048

- **Q:** “the Balinese live in spurts.” (C4035–4036)

### IOC6-061 | Reversal of *alus* everyday ethos; status as life and death | A | C4050–4086
Normal Balinese *alus* (polished, smooth)—avoid open conflict; cockfight portrays wild murderous instinctual cruelty. “A powerful rendering of life as the Balinese most deeply do not want it… set in the context of a sample of it as they do in fact have it.” Stratificatory angle: status relationships as life and death; hierarchy of pride = moral backbone (Polynesian titles + Hindu castes). Only in cockfight sentiments revealed in “natural colors”—mask demonstrates more than conceals. Jealousy/envy/brutality as much Bali as poise/grace/charm.
- **Q:** “What… the cockfight talks most forcibly about is status relationships, and what it says about them is that they are matters of life and death.” (C4071–4074)

### IOC6-062 | Not functionalist reinforcement: metasocial commentary; “story they tell themselves about themselves” | A | C4105–4115

### IOC6-063 | Culture as text; social semantics not social mechanics | A | C4117–4145
Shift analysis of cultural forms from organism/symptom/code/system analogies to **literary text**. “Saying something of something” (Aristotle)—problem of social **semantics**. Text beyond written/verbal: medieval *interpretatio naturae*, Nietzsche, Marx, Freud as precedents (not equally recommendable); idea theoretically undeveloped; cultural forms as imaginative works from social materials “yet to be systematically exploited.”
- **Q:** “If one takes the cockfight, or any other collectively sustained symbolic structure, as a means of ‘saying something of something’… then one is faced with a problem not in social mechanics but social semantics.” (C4124–4128)

### IOC6-064 | Emotion for cognitive ends; sentimental education | A | C4147–4163
Central feature obscured if treated as rite or pastime: **use of emotion for cognitive ends**. Vocabulary of sentiment (risk thrill, loss despair, triumph pleasure) teaches that of these emotions “society is built and individuals are put together.” Attending = “sentimental education”; learn ethos and private sensibility in collective text—and disquieting part: “the text… consists of a chicken hacking another mindlessly to bits.”
- **Q:** “Attending cockfights and participating in them is, for the Balinese, a kind of sentimental education.” (C4156–4158)
- **Q:** “the text in which this revelation is accomplished consists of a chicken hacking another mindlessly to bits.” (C4161–4163)

### IOC6-065 | Paradigmatic human event; art generates subjectivity | A | C4164–4231
Every people loves its own violence; cockfight is Balinese reflection on theirs. Northrop Frye/Aristotle *Poetics*: poet gives typical/universal; Macbeth for feeling after gaining kingdom/losing soul—Balinese go to cockfights to feel what composed aloof man feels when driven to fury of total triumph/defeat. Not “typical/universal” but **paradigmatic** human event—kind of thing that would happen if life were art. Art forms “generate and regenerate the very subjectivity they pretend only to display”—positive agents creating sensibility, not mere reflections. Color experience with light they cast.
- **Q:** “Quartets, still lifes, and cockfights are not merely reflections of a pre-existing sensibility analogically represented; they are positive agents in the creation and maintenance of such a sensibility.” (C4222–4225)
