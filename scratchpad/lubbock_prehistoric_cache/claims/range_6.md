## Summary
Closing stretch of Ch.XV argues that “savages” are highly skilled with weapons and simple crafts; that Stone Age Europe itself is subdivided (Palaeolithic cave-hunters vs Neolithic lake-dwellers); that simple arts were often **independently invented** under isolation rather than diffused; and that custom, morality, language, numeration, and religion vary so widely that lowest races allegedly lack abstract vocabulary, numbers past 3–4, and “true” religion—while remaining enslaved to witchcraft. Ch.XVI reconstructs the **primitive condition of man** (no pottery, bows, agriculture; spear and club only), rejects peopling-by-migration for gradual “creeping” diffusion, affirms **unity of the human race** with early racial differentiation, adapts Wallace on mind vs body under natural selection, and culminates in a **progressivist moral:** civilization multiplies population and happiness; the “free and noble savage” is false; science diminishes ignorance, sin, and suffering; Utopia is lawful destiny. Strong anti-degenerationist and anti-noble-savage thrust; Victorian hierarchy language is pervasive and must stay attributed.
