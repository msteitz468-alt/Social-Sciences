### R5-001: Evolutionary stage models fit Near East/Mesoamerica better than Europe
- **Claim:** Sherratt argues that social interpretations of the archaeological record have usually been cast within an implicit evolutionary framework that has been most successful for the Near East and Mesoamerica, where rapid social inequality followed agriculture; in Europe this approach has been less fruitful because complex urban societies appear relatively late despite farming from the seventh millennium BC, so the long intervening period cannot be described as a simple succession of increasingly ranked societies.
- **Type:** theoretical claim
- **Register:** attributed
- **Grounding:** "Interpretations of the archaeological record in social terms have usually been undertaken within an implicit evolutionary framework. The most successful of these studies have been those concerned with the Near East and Mesoamerica... In Europe, however, this approach has been less fruitful... the long intervening period cannot be adequately described by a simple evolutionary succession of increasingly ranked societies."

### R5-011: Three-phase summary model for central Plain (Fig. 10.2)
- **Claim:** Sherratt summarises central Great Hungarian Plain settlement and exchange 6000–3500 BC in three major phases: (1) Körös & AVK (6th mill.) — dispersed linear shoreline settlements, sheep predominate, fishing important, no formal cemeteries, obsidian becoming important; (2) Szakálhát & Tisza (early 5th mill.) — large aggregated often-burnt settlements, cattle predominate and wild cattle locally important, elaborately decorated pottery/cult objects, large quantity/variety of imports, small formal grave groups; (3) Tiszapolgár & Bodrogkeresztúr (late 5th/early 4th) — dispersed small coherent unditched settlements, cattle predominate, plain uniform pottery without local styles/no figurines, some stone/copper imports especially on Plain edge, large formal community cemeteries.
- **Type:** finding / descriptive synthesis
- **Register:** attributed (Sherratt’s regional synthesis)
- **Grounding:** Fig. 10.2 summary table: settlement, subsistence, material culture, exchange, cemeteries for the three phases.
