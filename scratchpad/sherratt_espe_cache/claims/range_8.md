### R8-013: Gold vessels before bronze sheet in temperate Europe
**Claim:** Beaten gold and silver vessels slowly appeared among temperate European “barbarian” societies in the second millennium; another ~800 years before Late Bronze Age successors acquired sheet bronzework for vessels and armour previously restricted to Aegean palace workshops. ### R8-035: Technologies that spread vs stayed urban

### R8-036: Secondary states vs Balkan non-linkage
**Claim:** Within Near East, raw-material suppliers developed hierarchies and manufacturing into secondary cores/states (Syria, highland Iran; Aegean from silver supplier to textile/wine/oil producer). ### R8-038: Kura-Arax highland bloc from Uruk contacts

### R8-039: Maikop as independent northern core for steppes

### R8-047: Demic diffusion vs indigenous adoption and language outcomes
**Claim:** Patterns of language distribution from farming spread depend on balance of demic diffusion vs indigenous adoption: expansion of a population group → linguistic dispersal then related differentiation; adoption by native populations → no necessary language transfer, possibly increased diversity as local dialects crystallise. Uniform dispersal predominates into empty or new environments and secondary specialised expansions (e.g. pastoralism). Classic successful archaeology–linguistics marriages: Polynesian and Bantu (demic, recent); Melanesia/New Guinea extreme diversity where adoption and earlier agriculture predominate (note 2).
**Type:** theoretical | **Attribution:** Sherratt & Sherratt | **Register:** attributed
