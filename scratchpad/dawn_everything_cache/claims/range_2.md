## Chapter 3 — Unfreezing the Ice Age

### C2-001 | THESIS | Prehistory origin stories function like myth
**Claim:** G&W argue that modern scientific accounts of “human origins” fulfil functions similar to creation myths (Greek/Polynesian mythic ages, Australian Dreamtime): a canvas for collective fears, desires, and obsessions about a time when new institutions could first arise.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “In some ways, accounts of ‘human origins’ play a similar role for us today as myth did for ancient Greeks or Polynesians, or the Dreamtime for indigenous Australians. This is not to cast aspersions on the scientific rigour or value of these accounts. It is simply to observe that the two fulfil somewhat similar functions.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[social-evolutionism]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
**Notes:** Opening of Ch.3; sets epistemological stance for Palaeolithic politics.

### C2-002 | FACT | Term ‘prehistory’ after Brixham Cave 1858
**Claim:** G&W state that “prehistory” only entered common use after 1858 discoveries at Brixham Cave (Devon): stone axes with remains of extinct fauna under sealed rock, sparking rethinking of human time-depth (“the bottom dropped out of human history”).
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (citing standard historiography of prehistory)
**Quote:** “The term ‘prehistory’ only came into common use after the discoveries at Brixham Cave in Devon in 1858, when stone axes, which could only have been fashioned by humans, were found alongside remains of cave bear, woolly rhinoceros and other extinct species… Suddenly, ‘the bottom dropped out of human history.’”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
**Notes:** Historical fact about disciplinary vocabulary.

### C2-003 | FINDING | Early African Homo populations more physically diverse than modern humans
**Claim:** G&W report (as of their synthesis of biological anthropology/genetics) that early human populations in Africa were far more physically diverse than living humans; modern humans are relatively uniform composites of an earlier mosaic; ancestral humans coexisted with smaller-brained species such as *Homo naledi*.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (reporting biological anthropology/genetics consensus they summarize)
**Quote:** “Rather than everyone starting out the same, then dispersing from East Africa in some Tower-of-Babel moment… early human populations in Africa appear to have been far more physically diverse than anything we are familiar with today.” / “Ancestral humans were not only quite different from each other; they also coexisted with smaller-brained, more ape-like species such as Homo naledi.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
**Notes:** FINDING as G&W’s report of scientific literature; date-stamp to their synthesis.

### C2-005 | THESIS | Sapient paradox is a red herring / evidence bias
**Claim:** G&W argue the “Upper Palaeolithic Revolution” / “Human Revolution” (~45,000 years ago) and the “sapient paradox” are largely illusions created by European research bias and preservation; Europe was late; complex symbolic behaviour appears earlier elsewhere (e.g. South African rock shelters ~80,000 bc; Panga ya Saidi beads/pigments ~60,000 years; Borneo/Sulawesi cave art older than Lascaux/Altamira).
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “In fact, it’s becoming increasingly clear that the whole problem is a mirage. The reason archaeological evidence from Europe is so rich is that European governments tend to be rich… Even as we write, a cave site on the coast of Kenya called Panga ya Saidi is yielding evidence of shell beads and worked pigments stretching back 60,000 years… If anything, then, Europe was late to the party.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[social-evolutionism]]
**Notes:** Methodological critique of Palaeolithic “revolution” narrative.

### C2-006 | THESIS | Ice Age Europe density of interaction, not genetic leap
**Claim:** G&W propose that apparent cultural efflorescence after modern humans enter Europe may relate to climate/demography: confined, game-rich valleys between tundra and forest; seasonal aggregation tracking mammoth, bison, deer; density of interaction (not absolute population) produced cultural bursts.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “Why there appears to be such a sudden cultural efflorescence, shortly after their arrival, may have something to do with climate and demography… While the absolute number of people may still have been startlingly small, the density of human interactions seems to have radically increased, especially at certain times of year. And with this came remarkable bursts of cultural expression.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
**Notes:** Explanation for Upper Palaeolithic European richness.

### C2-007 | POSITION | Boehm: humans uniquely anti-dominance political actors — then stuck in egalitarian Garden
**Claim:** G&W report Christopher Boehm’s position that humans inherit dominance-submission tendencies but uniquely deploy conscious counter-dominance (ridicule, shame, shunning, even assassination); politics = reflecting on alternative social paths. They then criticize Boehm for concluding that until agriculture humans lived only as egalitarian “societies of equals” for ~200,000 years — reinstalling a Garden of Eden then flip to hierarchy.
**Attribution:** Christopher Boehm (as summarized/critiqued by G&W); Graeber & Wengrow 2021 for the critique
**Quote:** “Carefully working through ethnographic accounts of existing egalitarian foraging bands… Boehm identifies a whole panoply of tactics collectively employed to bring would-be braggarts and bullies down to earth… Before 12,000 years ago, Boehm insists, humans were basically egalitarian, living in what he calls ‘societies of equals, and outside the family there were no dominators’.” / “So, according to Boehm, for about 200,000 years political animals all chose to live just one way… An odd insistence that for many tens of thousands of years, nothing happened.”
**Target pages:** [[egalitarian-society]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[social-evolutionism]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
**Notes:** G&W use Boehm’s politics insight against his evolutionary stasis.

**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (archaeological synthesis)

### C2-030 | THESIS | Seasonality wrecks band–tribe–chiefdom–state and simple/complex HG typologies
**Claim:** G&W argue you cannot speak of evolution from band to tribe to chiefdom to state if groups move fluidly among them habitually; seasonal dualism also wrecks simple vs complex hunter-gatherer types because diagnostic features of “complexity” appear in some seasons and are brushed aside in others by the same population.

### C2-032 | THESIS | Real question is not origins of inequality but ‘how did we get stuck?’

### C2-033 | POSITION | Clastres: societies against the state; Amazonian chiefs structurally powerless

### C2-034 | THESIS | No single seasonal pattern — only consistent alteration and awareness of alternatives

### C2-037 | THESIS | Forager ancestors as bold social-form experimenters
**Claim:** G&W argue remote forager ancestors were “much bolder experimenters in social form,” breaking and reassembling societies at different scales with different value systems seasonally; Eurasian/African/American agrarian festive calendars are distant echoes of those political freedoms.

### C2-038 | THESIS | Ethnography shows possibilities, not ancestral windows

### C2-039 | THESIS | How we got stuck: permanent power over others

### C2-040 | THESIS | Upper Palaeolithic ‘society’ spanned continents; cultural porosity
**Claim:** G&W argue UP material uniformities (Alps to Mongolia: tools, instruments, female figurines, ornaments, funeral rites) and long-distance individual travel suggest it may not make sense to speak of separate bounded societies like modern nations; “Society… spanned continents.”

### C2-041 | FINDING | Contemporary forager bands cosmopolitan (~10% close kin)
**Claim:** G&W report research on Hadza and Australian Martu: when forager bands gather into larger residential groups, primarily biological relations average ~10% of membership; most members drawn from wider, often distant, multi-language pools — true even for encapsulated groups.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (citing forager research)
**Quote:** “primarily biological relations constitute on average a mere 10 per cent of total membership. Most members are drawn from a much wider pool of individuals, many from quite far away, who may not even speak the same first languages.”

**Notes:** Supports fluid membership / freedom of movement.

### C2-042 | FINDING | Long-distance clan/moiety hospitality systems
**Claim:** G&W report Aboriginal Australians could travel halfway across the continent among different languages still finding totemic moieties (hospitality/marriage rules); a North American 500 years ago could travel Great Lakes to Louisiana bayous finding members of own Bear/Elk/Beaver clans obliged to host.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “Aboriginal Australians, for instance, could travel halfway across the continent, moving among people who spoke entirely different languages, and still find camps divided into the same kinds of totemic moieties that existed at home.”

### C2-043 | THESIS | Overall historical direction: social worlds grow more parochial (origin of cultures)
**Claim:** G&W argue that contrary to technology-makes-world-smaller intuition, as population rose, smaller proportions travelled far; after ~12,000 bc Mesolithic “cultures” become traceable beyond stone tools (fisherfolk, acorn gatherers, grain processing, cuisine as markers of difference); later micro-differentiation (Amazonia, PNG) — overall direction until recently is increasingly local allegiances, cultural inventiveness aimed at setting peoples off against neighbours, not globalization.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “If we survey what happens over time, the scale on which social relations operate doesn’t get bigger and bigger; it actually gets smaller and smaller.” / “Overall, though, what we observe is not so much the world as a whole getting smaller, but most peoples’ social worlds growing more parochial…”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[schismogenesis]], [[social-evolutionism]]
**Notes:** “Origin of cultures” as hardening boundaries; schismogenesis deferred to next chapter.

### C2-044 | THESIS | Cultural boundary hardening enables durable domination
**Claim:** G&W argue proliferation of bounded cultural universes contributed to more durable domination: mixed forager composition and cultural porosity enabled exit and seasonal demographic pulses alternating political arrangements; hard to exercise arbitrary power in January over someone equal again in July; “The hardening and multiplication of cultural boundaries can only have reduced such possibilities.”
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “Simply put, it’s difficult to exercise arbitrary power in, say, January over someone you will be facing on equal terms again come July. The hardening and multiplication of cultural boundaries can only have reduced such possibilities.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[schismogenesis]], [[state-formation]]
**Notes:** Mechanism linking culture-origins to getting stuck.

### C2-045 | THESIS | ‘Egalitarian society’ defined via shared paramount values equally distributed
**Claim:** G&W propose a working definition: an egalitarian society if (1) most people feel they ought to be the same in specifically important agreed ways, and (2) the ideal is largely achieved; alternatively, societies organized around key values (wealth, piety, beauty, freedom, knowledge, warrior prowess) are egalitarian when the paramount value is distributed equally — but different value systems create dilemmas for a universal label.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “we can speak of an egalitarian society if (1) most people in a given society feel they really ought to be the same in some specific way, or ways, that are agreed to be particularly important; and (2) that ideal can be said to be largely achieved in practice.”
**Target pages:** [[egalitarian-society]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
**Notes:** Conceptual groundwork before three freedoms.

### C2-046 | THESIS | Property/agriculture-inequality story is too broad to explain
**Claim:** G&W report conventional wisdom: agriculture enables surplus → specialists/warriors/priests → merchants/lawyers/politicians → private property → the state (Rousseau lineage); they say broad truth with little explanatory power — ~6,000 years between first Middle Eastern farmers and first states; many farming regions never produced such states; cereal farming→states is like calculus→atom bomb.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (critiquing conventional wisdom from Rousseau/Smith line)
**Quote:** “Roughly 6,000 years stand between the appearance of the first farmers in the Middle East and the rise of what we are used to calling the first states; and in many parts of the world, farming never led to the emergence of anything remotely like those states.”
**Target pages:** [[social-evolutionism]], [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
**Notes:** Anti-agricultural-revolution-as-inequality-origin.

### C2-047 | POSITION | Woodburn: only immediate-return foragers are truly egalitarian
**Claim:** G&W report James Woodburn’s position: Hadza and parallel societies (San, Mbuti, Pandaram, Batek) are only genuinely egalitarian cases extending equality to gender and age; based on ethos that no one should be in ongoing dependency; defining feature is lack of material surplus / “immediate return” (food eaten same day or next, never stored) vs “delayed return”; they self-consciously avoid stockpiling to avoid chains of captivity. **Attribution:** James Woodburn (via G&W); Graeber & Wengrow 2021 for critical framing

### C2-050 | FINDING | Nuer: rank relevant at marriage, not everyday command (Evans-Pritchard / Gough)
**Claim:** G&W report Evans-Pritchard’s Nuer as egalitarian paradigm (no government, personal independence) vs Kathleen Gough’s feminist critique: aristocrats/strangers/captives and differential access to cattle/women for “proper” marriage; yet G&W (with Evans-Pritchard) hold rank had almost no bearing outside marriage — no orders, no formal obeisance; women often not “properly” married (ghosts, woman-woman marriage), retaining sexual/parental autonomy.

### C2-051 | THESIS | Three basic freedoms (definitions)
**Claim:** G&W enumerate three freedoms they say appear to have been simply assumed among distant ancestors (barely conceivable today): (1) freedom to abandon one’s community knowing one will be welcomed in faraway lands; (2) freedom to shift back and forth between social structures depending on time of year; (3) freedom to disobey authorities without consequence. ### C2-052 | THESIS | Work/property focus of inequality-origins is misconstrued

### C2-062 | THESIS | Agricultural Argument and colonial dispossession of foragers

### C2-063 | FINDING | Calusa: non-agricultural kingdom of southwest Florida
**Claim:** G&W report Calusa (west Florida, Tampa Bay to Keys): capital Calos at Mound Key (~30 ha shell mounds); diet of fish, shellfish, marine animals, deer, etc.; war canoes extracting tribute (foods, skins, weapons, metals, slaves); ruler “Carlos” with gold diadem, exclusive throne, absolute will (insubordination punishable by death), secret renewal rituals, kneeling subjects, warrior-priest class, craft specialists/metallurgists; Spanish report of subject children’s deaths on ruler/principal wife’s death — G&W: sacred/divine kingship features; economy via rich fisheries, canals/ponds, largely permanent settlements though seasonal scattering continued.

### C2-064 | THESIS | ‘No True Scotsman’ fallacy of excluding complex foragers
**Claim:** G&W accuse adherents of agriculture-as-necessary-foundation for durable inequality of “No True Scotsman” (Antony Flew): redefining foragers who raid, stockpile, have kings/slavery as not “true” foragers or as proto-farmers/delayed-return subtypes — circular protection of “hunter-gatherers do not have aristocracies.” They define foragers as populations not relying primarily on biologically domesticated plants/animals; complex tenure, kings, slavery do not magically make them proto-farmers; reject “complex/affluent/delayed-return” as “savage slot” (Trouillot).
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (Flew; Trouillot)
**Quote:** “Foragers are populations which don’t rely on biologically domesticated plants and animals as their primary sources of food. **Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[social-evolutionism]], [[original-affluent-society]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]], [[egalitarian-society]]

### C2-069 | FINDING | Woodburn: sacred sphere exception to egalitarian property rules
**Claim:** G&W report Woodburn’s less-known insight: even assertive egalitarian immediate-return groups make one exception to no-orders / no-private-claim norms — ritual/sacred sphere. ### C2-070 | THESIS | Private property and the sacred share structure of exclusion
**Claim:** G&W argue in free societies, exclusive sacred items are often the only important absolute property; formal similarity between private property and the sacred — both structures of exclusion. Durkheim: sacred as “set apart,” clearest in Polynesian *tabu* (“not to be touched”); absolute private property (e.g. car against the whole world) sets object apart as sacred to a living individual. European oddity (Macpherson’s “possessive individualism”): absolute sacred quality of private property as paradigm for all human rights/freedoms (own your body as you own chattels).
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (Durkheim; C. B. Macpherson)
**Quote:** “In such societies, there turns out to be a profound formal similarity between the notion of private property and the notion of the sacred. Both are, essentially, structures of exclusion.” / “Just as every man’s home is his castle, so your right not to be killed, tortured or arbitrarily imprisoned rests on the idea that you own your own body…”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[indigenous-critique]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
**Notes:** Core private-property thesis of Ch.4.

### C2-071 | FINDING | Indigenous sacra as primary exclusive property (Lowie)
**Claim:** G&W report Robert Lowie on indigenous property: many important forms immaterial — magic formulae, stories, medical knowledge, rights to dances/patterns; weapons/tools/hunting territories often freely shared while esoteric powers for game reproduction individually owned; Kwakiutl heirloom feast-dish conveys berry-gathering rights and right to present while singing; Plains sacred bundles (objects + dances/rituals/songs) often only objects treated as private property — inherited, bought, sold.
**Attribution:** Robert Lowie (via G&W); Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “many of the most important forms of indigenous property were immaterial or incorporeal: magic formulae, stories, medical knowledge, the right to perform a certain dance, or stitch a certain pattern on one’s mantle.” / “Among Plains societies… sacred bundles… were often the only objects in that society to be treated as private property: not just owned exclusively by individuals, but also inherited, bought and sold.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[indigenous-critique]], [[egalitarian-society]]
**Notes:** Sacred exclusive property ethnography.

### C2-072 | FINDING | Ownership as domination and care; totemic non-consumption
**Claim:** G&W report: often true “owners” of land/resources are gods/spirits, humans caretakers or poachers; Amazonia paradigm of ownership/mastery = capturing animals then adopting as pets (violent appropriation → nurture); Amazonian ethnography finds almost everything has an owner (domination + care dual meaning); totemic systems: clan “owns” a species meaning members must not kill it and must promote its flourishing.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021 (Amazonian and totemic ethnography as reported)
**Quote:** “Often, the true ‘owners’ of land or other natural resources were said to be gods or spirits; mortal humans are merely squatters, poachers, or at best caretakers.” / “Each human clan is said to ‘own’ a certain species of animal… but what this means is precisely that members of that clan cannot hunt, kill, harm or otherwise consume animals of that species.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[indigenous-critique]]
**Notes:** Contrasts with Roman absolute property.

### C2-073 | THESIS | Roman Law abusus defines true property vs usufruct
**Claim:** G&W argue Roman Law (basis of most modern legal systems) uniquely minimizes care/share responsibility: three rights — *usus*, *fructus*, *abusus*; *usus*+*fructus* alone = usufruct, not true possession; defining feature of true legal property is option not to care for or to destroy at will.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “In Roman Law there are three basic rights relating to possession: usus (the right to use), fructus (the right to enjoy the products of a property…), and abusus (the right to damage or destroy)… The defining feature of true legal property, then, is that one has the option of not taking care of it, or even destroying it at will.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]]
**Notes:** Legal-historical claim about private property.

### C2-074 | FINDING | Aranda churinga initiation: sacred property and obedience via ritual violence
**Claim:** G&W report Western Desert Aranda initiation (via T. G. H. Strehlow): adult males as guardians of territories; *churinga*/*tsurinja* relics of territory-creating ancestors as legal title and Durkheim’s archetype of the sacred; initiation (circumcision then sub-incision) deliberately cruel to convert spoiled children into obedient “citizens” heirs of sacred traditions — ritual contexts invert free-and-equal ordinary life with exclusive sacred property, top-down hierarchy, dutiful obedience.
**Attribution:** T. G. H. Strehlow (quoted by G&W); Durkheim on churinga (via G&W); Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “The deliberate cruelty with which the traditional initiation rites are carried out at a later age is carefully calculated to punish insolent and lawless boys… and be fit heirs to the ancient sacred traditions of their clan.” / “It is only within such contexts that exclusive (sacred) forms of property exist, strict and top-down hierarchies are enforced, and where orders given are dutifully obeyed.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[egalitarian-society]]
**Notes:** Private property + domination confined to sacred ritual.

### C2-075 | THESIS | Private property origin as old as the sacred; question is how it ordered everything else
**Claim:** G&W conclude carefully coordinated ritual theatres (Göbekli Tepe, Poverty Point, Sannai Maruyama, Stonehenge, UP “princely” resting places) were exactly where exclusive property rights and demands for unquestioning obedience were likely made among otherwise free people; if private property has an “origin,” it is as old as the idea of the sacred (likely as old as humanity); pertinent question is not when this happened but how it came to order so many other aspects of human affairs.
**Attribution:** Graeber & Wengrow 2021
**Quote:** “such carefully co-ordinated ritual theatres, often laid out with geometrical precision, were exactly the kinds of places where exclusive claims to rights over property – together with strict demands for unquestioning obedience – were likely to be made, among otherwise free people. If private property has an ‘origin’, it is as old as the idea of the sacred, which is likely as old as humanity itself. The pertinent question to ask is not so much when this happened, as how it eventually came to order so many other aspects of human affairs.”
**Target pages:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]], [[the-state]]
