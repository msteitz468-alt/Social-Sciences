## Ch.9 — Hiding in Plain Sight (Teotihuacan, stranger-kings, Tlaxcala)

### C5-001: Chapter frame — indigenous social housing and democracy
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Graeber & Wengrow frame Ch.9 as recovering a neglected Mesoamerican strand of urban republics, large-scale social welfare, and indigenous democracy traceable to the Spanish conquest and beyond—against the expectation that early cities require overlords.
- **Grounding quote:** "bring to the surface this neglected strand of Mesoamerican social history: one of urban republics, large-scale projects of social welfare, and indigenous forms of democracy"
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[urbanization]], [[indigenous-critique]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** Ch.9 subtitle/intro program statement.

### C5-002: Mexica model Tenochtitlan on ruined Teotihuacan
- **Register:** FACT / FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W report that around AD 1150 the Mexica migrated south from Aztlán (location unknown) into the Valley of Mexico, later building the Aztec Triple Alliance capital Tenochtitlan; lacking their own urban tradition, they modelled Tenochtitlan on the ruined city they called Teotihuacan (“Place of Gods”).
- **Grounding quote:** "Lacking an urban tradition of their own, the Mexica modelled the layout of Tenochtitlan on that of another city they found, lying in ruins"
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan, Tenochtitlan, Mexica/Aztec Triple Alliance (no established society/site slugs).

### C5-003: Teotihuacan chronology and scale
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W state archaeology places Teotihuacan’s foundation around 100 BC and decline around AD 600; heyday ~eight centuries before the Mexica; conservative population estimates ~100,000 (perhaps five times Mohenjo-daro/Uruk-scale early Eurasian cities); at zenith ≥1 million across Valley of Mexico and surrounds considered it the world’s most important place.
- **Grounding quote:** "Its foundation dates to around 100 bc, and its decline to around ad 600… even conservative estimates place its population at around 100,000"
- **Links:** [[urbanization]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan.

### C5-004: “Weird” city — nonconformity to Mesoamerican kingship package
- **Register:** FINDING / POSITION
- **Claim:** G&W report specialists find Teotihuacan “weird”: it refuses expectations of how early Mesoamerican cities functioned; the Classic Maya “standard package” of kingship (royal temples, ball-courts, war/captive imagery, calendrical royal ancestor ritual, royal biographies—c. AD 150–900) is “strikingly absent” at Teotihuacan, as are analogues at Monte Albán, Tula, and arguably Cahokia.
- **Grounding quote:** "there's just something ‘weird’ about the place… In Teotihuacan, all this seems to have been strikingly absent."
- **Links:** [[chiefdom]], [[the-state]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Classic Maya, Tikal, Calakmul, Palenque, Monte Albán, Tula, Cahokia.

### C5-005: No ruler-dominating-subordinate imagery; equal-scale figures
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W claim among thousands of Teotihuacan images there is not a single representation of a ruler striking, binding, or dominating a subordinate (unlike Maya and Zapotec art); artists often made all figures in a scene the same size, frustrating searches for kingly figures; few written inscriptions; language of majority unknown (Maya and Zapotec minorities present).
- **Grounding quote:** "nowhere among some thousands of such images do we find even a single representation of a ruler striking, binding or otherwise dominating a subordinate"
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan.

### C5-006: No ball-court; tunnels not royal tombs
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W report ceremonial ball-courts are absent at Teotihuacan; tunnels around Pyramids of Sun/Moon and under Temple of the Feathered Serpent lead to chthonic labyrinths and mineral-crusted shrines, not royal tombs or robbed-out chambers.
- **Grounding quote:** "the ceremonial ball-court is also conspicuous by its absence at Teotihuacan… passages do not lead to royal tombs… but to chthonic labyrinths and mineral-crusted shrines"
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan; Aztec-imposed monument names (Pyramid of Sun/Moon, Way of the Dead) still used by archaeologists/tourists.

### C5-007: Pasztory — schismogenesis and anti-dynastic utopia
- **Register:** POSITION (Pasztory, via G&W) / THESIS
- **Claim:** Esther Pasztory argued highland Teotihuacan and lowland Maya enacted conscious cultural inversion (what G&W call schismogenesis) at urban-civilization scale: Teotihuacan art rejected ruler–captive tropes and aristocratic glorification, celebrated community values, and for centuries prevented “dynastic personality cults”; she and followers cast it as a utopian “Tollan for the people,” with early authoritarian drift reversed around AD 300 toward “collective governance” and more equal resource distribution.
- **Grounding quote:** "nothing less than a case of conscious cultural inversion – or what we’ve been calling schismogenesis… successfully prevented the emergence of ‘dynastic personality cults’"
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan; Pasztory as named scholar.

### C5-008: G&W thesis — self-conscious egalitarian urban self-government
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W argue all evidence suggests Teotihuacan at its height governed itself without overlords—as earlier Ukrainian mega-sites, Uruk-period Mesopotamia, and Bronze Age Pakistan cities did—but on a different technological base and larger scale; consensus among those who know the site best is self-consciously egalitarian organization; this is not as anomalous in world-historical or Mesoamerican terms as scholars assume (a “republican” tradition alongside the kingship package).
- **Grounding quote:** "All the evidence suggests that Teotihuacan had, at its height of its power, found a way to govern itself without overlords… a city organized along some sort of self-consciously egalitarian lines."
- **Links:** [[urbanization]], [[the-state]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Teotihuacan.

### C5-009: Maya stranger-kings in Teotihuacan regalia
- **Register:** FINDING / THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W report fifth-century AD Maya monuments (e.g. Tikal) show throne-seated figures in Teotihuacan-style dress and atlatls; Copán base-level temple burials with grave goods of stranger-kings and glyphs linking some to the Land of Cattail Reeds; Sihyaj K’ahk’ (“Born of Fire”) installed Teotihuacano “princes” who married local women; G&W argue these images reveal Maya concepts of royal power more than Teotihuacan kingship—logistics make a Teotihuacan military expedition 600+ miles unlikely; likely long-distance specialists (traders, soldiers of fortune, etc.) elevated as stranger-kings.
- **Grounding quote:** "the images and records from places like Tikal tell us more about Maya concepts of royal power than they do about Teotihuacan itself, where not a shred of compelling evidence for the institution of kingship has yet been found."
- **Links:** [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[chiefdom]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Tikal, Copán, Teotihuacan, stranger-kings; later Chichén Itzá–Tula analogy.

### C5-012: AD 300 turn — desecration and end of pyramid building/sacrifice

- **Grounding quote:** "the Temple of the Feathered Serpent was desecrated and its stores of offerings looted… At this point all new pyramid construction stopped permanently, and there is no further evidence of ritually sanctioned killing"

### C5-013: City-wide stone apartment “social housing”
- **Register:** FINDING / THESIS
- **Claim:** After AD 300, G&W report extraordinary urban resources went into stone masonry apartments for nearly the whole ~100,000 population—not only the privileged; blocks initially on ~3,600 m² plots; nuclear-family suites with drainage, plastered floors/walls, private porticoes; compounds ~60–100 people with central courtyard rituals (*talud-tablero* altars, murals); René Millon argued the apartment was invented as social housing for an increasingly crowded city; G&W claim many citizens enjoyed living standards rarely achieved across such a wide urban sector in any period, including our own—“Tollan of the people.”
- **Grounding quote:** "supplying high-quality apartments for nearly all the city’s population, regardless of wealth or status… few were deprived… a standard of living that is rarely achieved across such a wide sector of urban society in any period of urban history, including our own."
- **Links:** [[urbanization]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]

## Ch.10 — Why the State Has No Origin (definitions; three forms; cases)

### C5-026: “Origins of the state” as fool’s errand
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W open Ch.10 arguing the quest for “origins of the state” is almost as long-standing and contested as “origins of social inequality,” and “just as much of a fool’s errand”; nearly everyone today lives under state authority and past polities (Pharaonic Egypt, Shang China, Inca, Benin) are often called early states, but without consensus definition any inclusive definition risks meaninglessness.
- **Grounding quote:** "The quest for the ‘origins of the state’ is almost as long-standing, and hotly contested, as the pursuit of the ‘origins of social inequality’ – and in many ways, it is just as much of a fool’s errand."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** Chapter title thesis.

### C5-027: Bodin coins “the state”; von Ihering/Weber monopoly of force
- **Register:** FACT / THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W state the term “the state” entered common usage in the late sixteenth century via Jean Bodin; Rudolf von Ihering (late nineteenth c.) first systematically defined the state as any institution claiming monopoly on legitimate use of coercive force within a territory—definition later identified with Max Weber; works for modern states but fails historically (Hammurabi’s Babylon, Socrates’ Athens, William the Conqueror’s England either out or force-monopoly claims empty).
- **Grounding quote:** "a state should be defined as any institution that claims a monopoly on the legitimate use of coercive force within a given territory (this definition has since come to be identified with the sociologist Max Weber)."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[legitimate-domination]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Weber/Ihering genealogy of definition.

### C5-028: Marxist and functional “complexity” definitions critiqued
- **Register:** THESIS / POSITION
- **Claim:** G&W summarize Marxists defining states as protecting emerging ruling-class power/property (Rousseauan tradition)—returns Babylon/Athens/medieval England but reopens “exploitation” and rules out benevolent state for liberals; twentieth-century functionalists treat social complexity (four-level settlement hierarchy + full-time specialists) as ipso facto state, predicting monopoly of force/elites sooner or later—circular (“since states are complicated, any complicated social arrangement must therefore be a state”).
- **Grounding quote:** "Basically, all it says is that, since states are complicated, any complicated social arrangement must therefore be a state."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]], [[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Attacks evolutionary stage models.

### C5-029: Early cities break complexity→state assumption
- **Register:** THESIS / FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W argue Ch.8 already showed earliest cities need not be states (e.g. early Uruk); when top-down rule emerges in Mesopotamia it is among small “heroic” foothill societies averse to administration (NW Coast ethnographic parallel: Haida/Tlingit boastful warrior aristocracies without state apparatus or formal government); if monarchy/aristocracy/slavery can exist without a state, and complex irrigation/science/philosophy without a state, taxonomizing “state vs non-state” may not be the interesting question.
- **Grounding quote:** "If it is possible to have monarchs, aristocracies, slavery and extreme forms of patriarchal domination, even without a state (as it evidently was); and if it’s equally possible to maintain complex irrigation systems, or develop science and abstract philosophy without a state… then what do we actually learn… by establishing that one political entity is what we would like to describe as a ‘state’"
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[urbanization]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Clears ground for three-forms theory.

### C5-030: Landed property is legal claim backed by force, not soil
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** Against Rousseau’s “first enclosure” as origin of domination, G&W argue “landed property” is not material soil/rocks/grass but a legal understanding of exclusive access maintained by morality and threat of violence—illustrating von Ihering’s monopoly logic at sub-national scale; revolutions succeed when those ordered to shoot refuse.
- **Grounding quote:** "‘landed property’ is not actual soil, rocks or grass. It is a legal understanding, maintained by a subtle mix of morality and the threat of violence."
- **Links:** [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[the-state]], [[legitimate-domination]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Thought-experiment lead-in to three principles.

### C5-031: Three elementary forms of domination defined
- **Register:** THESIS (core definition)
- **Claim:** G&W propose three principles as possible bases of social power: (1) control of violence, (2) control of information, (3) individual charisma. Violence is most dependable (basis of uniform law); charisma most ephemeral; usually all three coexist. Thought experiment: Kim Kardashian’s diamonds require security (violence); if invulnerable, secrecy/information control; if also unable to keep secrets, only charisma (“deserves” exclusive goods). Knowledge hierarchies need not be “practical” (technical smelting or “names of twenty-seven hells”).

### C5-061: Bureaucracy does not begin as scale solution
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W reject Dunbar/Diamond-style claim that large populations require chiefs, police, executives, bureaucrats and coercive monopoly; farmers self-coordinate complex irrigation; urban self-governance often more participatory than today; emperors often uninterested in street-cleaning; first functional admin (lists, ledgers, audits) emerges in *ritual* contexts (Mesopotamian temples, Egyptian ancestor cults, Chinese oracle readings)—not practical information management past a complexity threshold.
- **Grounding quote:** "one thing we can now say with a fair degree of certainty is that bureaucracy did not begin simply as a practical solution to problems of information management, when human societies advanced beyond a particular threshold of scale and complexity."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[urbanization]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Attacks scale-determinist sociology.

### C5-062: Tell Sabi Abyad — Neolithic “village bureaucracy”
- **Register:** FINDING / THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W cite burnt one-hectare village Tell Sabi Abyad (Syria Balikh valley, c. 6200 BC, ~150 people): central granaries/warehouses; geometric clay tokens; miniature seals; archived clay stoppers in central office—miniature precursors to Uruk temple archives—yet *no* unusually large residence or rich burials; uniform dwellings. Specialized administrative control first appears in very small communities >1,000 years after Çatalhöyük founding and >2,000 years before anything city-like.
- **Grounding quote:** "the first systems of specialized administrative control actually emerged in very small communities… around 8,000 years ago… a one-hectare village… comprising perhaps around 150 individuals."
- **Links:** [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[the-state]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Tell Sabi Abyad; LOAD-BEARING bureaucracy-origins claim.

### C5-063: Ubaid cultural uniformity as equality technology
- **Register:** THESIS / FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W argue fifth-millennium BC “Ubaid” Middle East shows spread of admin tools alongside disappearance of outward individuality (standard tripartite houses; drab/uniform pottery; mechanical crafts; spatial control of female labour)—possibly birth of overt equality ideology *before* first cities, with administrative tools designed to prevent wealth accumulation and rank marking, not extract it.
- **Grounding quote:** "it is possible that we are witnessing the birth of an overt ideology of equality in the centuries prior to the emergence of the world’s first cities, and that administrative tools were first designed not as a means of extracting and accumulating wealth but precisely to prevent such things from happening."
- **Links:** [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[urbanization]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Ubaid.

### C5-064: Ayllu khipu equality vs Inca extractive khipu
- **Register:** FINDING / THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W compare Andean *ayllu* (uniforms, land redistribution, reciprocity accounting, renegotiable khipu debts cancelled yearly) to Neolithic village accounting ideals; under Inca, ayllus reduced to “conquered women,” khipu fixed/non-negotiable for labour debts, decimal household grouping (10…5000) ignoring local organization, invented make-work, punishment—Graffigny’s benevolent Inca bureaucracy mistook local self-organized benefits for imperial command. Sovereignty + administration lethal because equivalence language of equity produces permanent failures/peons.
- **Grounding quote:** "That is why sovereignty and administration make such a potentially lethal combination, taking the equalizing effects of the latter and transforming them into tools of social domination, even tyranny."
- **Links:** [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** MISC — ayllu, Inca; parallels Hebrew Prophets debt peonage, China, India.

### C5-065: Impersonal promises — freedom inverted into peonage
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W link three primordial freedoms (move, disobey, create/transform social relations) to etymologies: English “free” < Germanic “friend”; earliest word for freedom Sumerian *ama(r)-gi* “return to mother” (debt-freedom decrees releasing peons). Freedom to make promises becomes peonage/slavery when promises become impersonal, transferable, bureaucratized; “As money is to promises… state bureaucracy is to the principle of care: in each case we find one of the most fundamental building blocks of social life corrupted by a confluence of maths and violence.”
- **Grounding quote:** "As money is to promises, we might say, state bureaucracy is to the principle of care: in each case we find one of the most fundamental building blocks of social life corrupted by a confluence of maths and violence."
- **Links:** [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[the-state]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** LOAD-BEARING freedoms–bureaucracy synthesis.

### C5-066: Seeking origins of the state is chasing a phantasm

- **Grounding quote:** "seeking the origins of the state is little more than chasing a phantasm."

### C5-067: Early regimes seasonal; full-time specialists myth
- **Register:** THESIS / FINDING
- **Claim:** G&W argue almost none of regimes considered had standing armies or full-time priests/judges—government staffed by rotating workers with other occupations; government and warfare concentrated in seasons (Ice Age seasonal kingship performances; winter police powers of some clans); “play kingdoms” may have gained substance analogously to play farming → agriculture—or both driven by patriarchal household changes and decline of women’s power.
- **Grounding quote:** "almost none of the regimes we’ve been considering in this chapter were actually staffed by full-time specialists. Most obviously, none seem to have had a standing army."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Undermines surplus→specialists→state story.

### C5-068: State as totalitarian impulse to make ritual last forever
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W (via Fijian Cakaudrove sunrise kava ritual under a “confederacy of chiefdoms” ruler) argue cosmic claims of kingship often inverse to actual power; if “the state” means anything it is the totalitarian impulse behind making such ritual permanent—pyramids as attempts to eternalize power that only really existed in building seasons; Abrams: the state is the mask preventing us seeing political practice; attend to senses in which the state does *not* exist.
- **Grounding quote:** "If ‘the state’ means anything, it refers to precisely the totalitarian impulse that lies behind all such claims, the desire effectively to make the ritual last forever."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[legitimate-domination]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Fiji Cakaudrove; Abrams citation.

### C5-069: Modern state as contingent confluence now drifting apart
- **Register:** THESIS (chapter synthesis)
- **Claim:** G&W conclude what we regard as states are not historical constants or Bronze Age evolutionary results but confluence of three political forms—sovereignty, administration, charismatic competition—with different origins; modern states are one way they came together, with “the people” holding kings’ power, bureaucracies “for the people,” and aristocratic contests relabelled democracy/elections—nothing inevitable; currently planetary bureaucracies without global sovereignty, plus crypto/private security undermining state sovereignty; civilization and state are complex amalgams of differently originated elements now drifting apart—rethinking social evolution is rethinking politics.
- **Grounding quote:** "An origin for ‘the state’ has long been sought… but what we now regard as states turn out not to be a constant of history at all; not the result of a long evolutionary process that began in the Bronze Age, but rather a confluence of three political forms – sovereignty, administration and charismatic competition – that have different origins."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]], [[graeber-david]], [[wengrow-david]]
- **Notes:** LOAD-BEARING chapter conclusion.

### C5-070: “Civilization” etymology and true history (mutual aid, women’s knowledge)
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W argue “civilization” is wrongly equated with cities→states and authoritarian stratified packages (Egypt, Inca, Aztec, Han, Rome, Greece) built on sacrifice of three freedoms; Latin *civilis* meant political wisdom and mutual aid enabling voluntary coalition—*ayllu*/Basque qualities, not Shang/Inca courts. True civilization history starts with expansive culture areas/interaction spheres (Mauss direction); women’s work (textiles, beadwork geometry) at core of mathematical/technical knowledge later appropriated in stone by men; expansion of ambitious polities often accompanied women’s marginalization.
- **Grounding quote:** "The word ‘civilization’ derives from Latin civilis, which actually refers to those qualities of political wisdom and mutual aid that permit societies to organize themselves through voluntary coalition."
- **Links:** [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[urbanization]], [[indigenous-critique]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** Coda; MISC — civilis etymology claim as G&W state it.

### C5-071: Minoan Crete — possible female political rule / college of priestesses
- **Register:** FINDING / THESIS / POSITION
- **Claim:** G&W present Neopalatial Crete (c. 1700–1450 BC; Knossos ~25,000) as not fitting state-formation playbook: palaces, Linear A (undeciphered), industrial storage, yet no clear monarchy; pictorial art shows adult women larger scale, staffs of command, thrones, assemblies without male presiding, supernatural flanks—while men are athletes, tribute-bearers, subservient; imports with female cult flavour (Hathor-related); unfortified, art of play not war. Contrast Mycenaean mainland warrior aristocracies, Linear B small-scale admin, *wanax* limited sovereignty. Knossos “Throne Room” likelier council-head seat for succession of female councillors than male monarch. G&W conclude available evidence suggests system of female political rule—effectively a theocracy governed by a college of priestesses—and ask why researchers resist; *Femina ludens* / *Femina potens* vs heroic ego-politics.
- **Grounding quote:** "Pretty much all the available evidence from Minoan Crete suggests a system of female political rule – effectively a theocracy of some sort, governed by a college of priestesses."
- **Links:** [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]], [[dawn-of-everything-debate]]
- **Notes:** MISC — Minoan Crete, Knossos, Mycenae; scholarly resistance recorded as POSITION.

### C5-072: Chapter close — state formation means many things; three principles constrain variety
- **Register:** THESIS
- **Claim:** G&W close: “state formation” can mean honour-game gone wrong, ritual growth for feeding dead, industrial slaughter, male appropriation of female knowledge, or governance by priestess college—but logical and historical constraints on how power expands are the basis of the three principles (sovereignty, administration, competitive politics); even within constraints, more was going on than conventional state definitions allow; uneven evidence (wall paintings preserved at Minoan Crete/Teotihuacan/Çatalhöyük) warns against projecting modern nation-state onto bare stone of “lost civilizations.”
- **Grounding quote:** "The process usually called ‘state formation’ can in fact mean a bewildering number of very different things… there seem to be both logical and historical constraints on the variety of ways in which power can expand its scope; these limits are the basis of our ‘three principles’ of sovereignty, administration and competitive politics."
- **Links:** [[state-formation]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[the-state]], [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021]]
- **Notes:** End of assigned range; Ch.11 heading only.
