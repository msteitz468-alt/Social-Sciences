### C-5-001 | Cook’s Pacific and Arctic language/culture links
- **Register:** FINDING
- **Target pages:** meltzer-first-peoples-2009, global-colonization
- **Claim:** Meltzer 2009 reports that Captain James Cook (1774 Rapa Nui; 1778 Alaska) recognized shared language and material culture among widely separated Pacific islanders and among Alaskan and Greenland “Esquemaux,” concluding common origin via divergence rather than mere convergence of tools.
- **Grounding:** “Cook concluded that the peoples who were scattered across thousands of miles of Pacific Ocean must be related; likewise, those who inhabited the top of America, from Alaska to Greenland” (slice ~ll. 44–48).
- **Notes:** Opening analogy for Ch. 6 consensus problem.
