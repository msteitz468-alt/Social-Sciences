### R2-001: Tools as archaeological marker of human emergence
- **Register:** theoretical-claim
- **Claim:** Childe holds that man's emergence on the earth is indicated to the archaeologist by the tools he made; tools supplement physiological deficiencies for securing food and shelter, enabled by hand–eye correlation and brain constitution.

## Chapter V — THE NEOLITHIC REVOLUTION

### R2-035: Postglacial attitude shift — radical change after Ice Age
- **Register:** theoretical-claim
- **Claim:** Childe argues throughout Ice Ages man made no fundamental change in attitude to external Nature—content to take what he could get with improved methods; soon after Ice Age a few communities underwent a radical attitude change with revolutionary consequences; postglacial ~15,000 years generous estimate vs ~250,000 conservative for preceding era; in last twentieth of history man began controlling Nature by co-operating with her.
- **Attribution:** Childe (Man Makes Himself, ch. V)
- **Quote:** "Soon after the end of the Ice Age man's attitude (or rather that of a few communities) to his environment underwent a radical change fraught with revolutionary consequences for the whole species."
- **Wiki targets:** [[neolithic-revolution]], [[palaeolithic]], [[neolithic]], [[childe-v-gordon]]
- **Notes:** Absolute dates pre-radiocarbon — flag.

### R2-036: Definition of the first / Neolithic Revolution — control of food supply
- **Register:** theoretical-claim
- **Claim:** Childe defines the first revolution that transformed human economy as giving man control over his own food supply: planting, cultivating, and improving by selection edible grasses, roots, and trees; and taming/attaching animal species in return for fodder, protection, and forethought. Steps gradual and cumulative; some stand out as revolutions by Chapter I standards.
- **Attribution:** Childe (Man Makes Himself, ch. V)
- **Quote:** "The first revolution that transformed human economy gave man control over his own food supply. Man began to plant, cultivate, and improve by selection edible grasses, roots, and trees. And he succeeded in taming and firmly attaching to his person certain species of animal in return for the fodder he was able to offer, the protection he could afford, and the forethought he could exercise."
- **Wiki targets:** [[neolithic-revolution]], [[agricultural-revolution]], [[neolithic]], [[childe-v-gordon]], [[childe-man-makes-himself-1936]], [[social-evolutionism]], [[historical-materialism]]
- **Notes:** LOAD-BEARING DEFINITION — coining/popularization of Neolithic Revolution as economic transformation; keep fully attributed. Mentions "neolithic" revolution later in chapter as synonym for this first revolution.

- **Register:** position

- **Attribution:** Childe (Man Makes Himself, ch. V)
- **Quote:** "Many authorities now hold that cultivation is everywhere older than stock-breeding."
- **Wiki targets:** [[neolithic-revolution]], [[diffusionism]], [[childe-v-gordon]]
- **Notes:** Position among competing schools; not adjudicated as fact.

- **Attribution:** Childe (Man Makes Himself, ch. V)
