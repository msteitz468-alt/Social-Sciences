### C5-020

Rather… the discrepancy works to enhance honor…”

## C. Why Ethnography? — responsibility, Malinowski, Trouillot, Sahlins–Obeyesekere (2016 afterword)

### C5-033

## E. Appendix — Formulas and Themes of the Ghinnāwa

### C5-057
- **Claim:** Abu-Lughod characterizes the Awlad ʿAli ghinnāwa as traditional oral poetry; individuals draw from cultural repertoire or compose extemporaneously playing with familiar themes, phrases, structures—formulaic in a looser sense than Lord–Parry epic formulas, because rhyme is absent, meter need not be sustained long, and little pressure for rapid performance composition; still, images/phrases/themes recur and a specialized vocabulary (terms rare in ordinary speech) functions as convention/simple formula.
- **Register:** F/T (appendix technical)
- **Grounding quote:** “I thus characterize the poetic discourse as formulaic, but I mean something less rigid than the use of actual formulas, which Lord and Parry have shown to be key elements of the composition and recitation of traditional epic poetry… The ghinnāwa even has a specialized vocabulary, its most common and characteristic terms being ones rarely heard in ordinary speech.”
- **Location:** cache lines 275–277
- **Target pages:** [[ghinnawa]], [[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-study]], [[awlad-ali]]
- **Notes:** Core definition for concept page.

### C5-058
- **Claim:** Awlad ʿAli are not overly concerned with original vs. repeated poems (oral-poetry feature); authorship answers unreliable—poems claimed as “theirs” heard from others; “in the olden days” everyone composed, now few do and most repeat by rote; gifted individuals compose originals with remembered date/occasion (e.g., poem composed eight years earlier about a married woman loved).
- **Register:** F (appendix)
- **Grounding quote:** “Awlad ʿAli do not seem overly concerned with distinguishing between original poems and those heard and repeated… Often, just after someone had told me a poem was theirs I would hear it from someone else.”

- **Target pages:** [[ghinnawa]], [[awlad-ali]]
- **Notes:** Authorship norms.

### C5-059
