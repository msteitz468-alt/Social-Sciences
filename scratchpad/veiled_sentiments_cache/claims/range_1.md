### C1-024: Fieldwork reflexivity as intermediate position
- **Register:** theoretical
- **Claim:** Drawing on Maybury-Lewis, Abu-Lughod argued honest fieldwork accounts are essential for evaluating ethnographic facts; she rejected treating the anthropologist–host encounter as sole object of inquiry yet refused to ignore how personality, social location, intimacy, luck, theory, and method shape the product—rejecting fictions of objectivity and omniscience in the ethnographic genre.
- **Grounding quote:** "An honest account of the circumstances of fieldwork… is… both essential for the evaluation of the facts and interpretations… I do not believe that the encounter between anthropologists and their hosts should be the sole object of inquiry… However, to ignore the encounter… perpetuates the conventional fictions of objectivity and omniscience that mark the ethnographic genre."

### C1-034: Non-directive method; resistance to surveys; poetry discovery path
- **Register:** finding
- **Claim:** Powerlessness and preference prevented coercive structured interviews/door-to-door surveys; she rarely took notes or taped during speech (except later poetry collection), wrote from memory, asked questions when topics already under discussion; this enabled eliciting Bedouin conceptions and discovering poetry’s importance—structured advance design would have blinded her to both.
- **Grounding quote:** "My research was perhaps most profoundly affected by the non-directive approach I took… I rarely took notes or tape-recorded when they spoke (except later when I began collecting poetry) but rather wrote notes from memory… In this manner I was able to elicit freely the Bedouins’ conceptions of their social world, and I was led to the discovery of poetry’s importance in social life. Had I rigidly structured my research in advance, I would have been blind to both."
- **Location:** lines ~595–597
- **Target pages:** participant-observation, abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-study, ghinnawa
- **Notes:** Method commitment central to study page.

### C1-036: Gendered information flow asymmetry favors female ethnographer access to men’s news
- **Register:** finding
- **Claim:** She argued her access was more balanced than a man’s would be in sex-segregated society: hierarchy meant men spoke before women but not reverse; young/low-status men informed female kin about men’s affairs; conspiracy of silence excluded men from women’s world.
- **Grounding quote:** "the structure of information flow between the men’s and women’s worlds was not symmetrical. Because of the pattern of hierarchy, men spoke to one another in the presence of women, but the reverse was not true… young and low-status men informed mothers, aunts, grandmothers, and… wives about men’s affairs, whereas no one brought news to the adult men.
