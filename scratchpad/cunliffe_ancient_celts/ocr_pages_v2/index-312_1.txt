Chapter 1. Visions of the Celts
The most challenging work on the whole question of validity of the concept of ‘Celtic
is M. Chapman, The Celts: The Construction of a Myth (New York, 1992). It deserves
careful reading as an essential preliminary. Other writers have been quick to espouse a
politically correct disdain for the use of ‘Celt” though usually without Chapman’s depth
of scholarship.
