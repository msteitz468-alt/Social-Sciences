The developments in central southern Britain are in marked contrast to
those in the west and east. One possible explanation is that the central southern
zone was a border region between the metal-producing west and the warrior
élites of the cast. The instability of such a region, perhaps under threat of con-
tinuous raids from the eastern zone, may well have led to the need to develop
and maintain fortifications,

different socio-economic systems to the north, that created a 'marcher’ society.
