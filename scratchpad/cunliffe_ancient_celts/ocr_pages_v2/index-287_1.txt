THE CELTS IN RETREAT

Catuvellauni ruled by Cunobelin were dominant, while to the south the Atre-
bates held sway. Conflict between these two polities had been going on for some
while, and disputed territories seem to have changed allegiance more than
once, but the Catuvellauni were in the ascendancy and Tacitus could describe
Cunobelin as ‘great king of Britain”,

The treaties set up by Caesar, and no doubt later developments about which
we know little, established bonds of clientage between the native tribes and
Rome, and these were used to political effect. A number of British kings are
recorded as being among the admirers of Augustus, and during the first decades
of the first century ap several British rulers fled to Rome to ask for the help of
the emperor in establishing their claims to power. The last recorded refugee was
Verica, king of the Atrebates, whose flight, about Ap 42, was caused by internal
rivalries, possibly exacerbated by the increasing dominance of the Catuvel-
