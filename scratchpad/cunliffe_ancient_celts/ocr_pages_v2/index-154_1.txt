To attempt to understand the nature of a society through an observer-
imposed interpretation of its art is a dangerous game. But is it too spurious to
see in the tightly sprung tension of the art of the fourth and third centuries
something that would have appealed to the reckless warrior hordes, or in the
enigmatic faces which peer from the stylized foliage an image that had meaning
for those who believed in the shape-shifting of the gods?
