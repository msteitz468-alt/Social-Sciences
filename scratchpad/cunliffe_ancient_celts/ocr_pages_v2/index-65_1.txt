Soon to follow were the Phocacans from the Acolian coast. According to
Herodotus, they set sail in their pentekonters (warships), exploring the Adri-
atic region, Etrunia, Iberia, and Tartessos, establishing friendly relations with
the Tartessian King. Their route almost certainly took them along the north
shores of the west Mediterranean, where they would have become aware of the
Etruscan entrepots along the littoral of Provence and Languedoc, possibly to
the port of Rhode in north-west Spain, which may have been established as
carly as the eighth century. Years of seasonal sailing in these waters would have
