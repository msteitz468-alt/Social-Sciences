The Expansion of Celtiberian Culture

From its homeland in the north-east of the Peninsula, Celtiberian culture
spread to many other areas of the country. This is best exemplified by the extent
to which the Celtic language was spoken, as is demonstrated by the distribution
of place names with the suffix - briga or the prefix Seg- (Fig. 107).

iberian culture westwards to the territory of the Vettones in the fifth century ic,
