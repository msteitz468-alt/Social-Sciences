FURTHER READING

Chapter13. Celtic Survival

The best introduction to the survival of Celtic peoples and institutions is Nora
Chadwick, The Celts (Harmondsworth, 1971), Myles Dillon and Nora Chadwick, The
Celtic Realms (London, 1967), and Alwyn and Birey Rees, Celtic Heritage: Ancient
Traditions in Ireland and Wales (London, 1961). For the most part these works deal with
Ireland, Wales, and Scotland. For Brittany the most accessible sources are Nora
Chadwick, Early Brittany (Cardiff, 1969); L. Fleuriot, Les Origines de la Bretagne (Paris,
1982); and A, Chédeville and H. Guillotel, La Bretagne des saints et des rois V=X siécles
{Rennes, 1984). A taste of the quality of the surviving literary tradition is given by K, H,
Jackson in A Celtic Miscellany (rev. edn., Harmondsworth, 1971).
