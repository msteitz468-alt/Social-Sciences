From the middle of the third century the Celtic raids against the cities of the
Acgean coastal area began to intensify, and Eumenes | of Pergamum (263-41),
now the prime power in the west, decided to buy them off. His successor,
Attalus 1 (241-197), however, chose instead to stand his ground, eventually win-
ning a decisive battle at the Springs of Kaikos in about 233 sc. It was to
celebrate this victory that a monument was erected on the acropolis of Perga-

the ‘Dying Gaul',

In the later part of the third century Celts were used extensively as mer-
cenaries in the armies of the Seleucid and Ptolemaic rulers, though from the
fragmentary accounts that survive they appear to have been unruly and some-
what unreliable. One group who became demoralized at an eclipse of the moon
had to be returned with their wives and children to the Hellespont in case they
had decided to change sides. The incident is interesting, because it shows that
some at least of the Celtic mercenaries may have been arriving in fresh groups
from Europe. It was during this period that Celtic warriors were in action as far
afield as Egypt. In 218 Attalus Il employed a European tribe, the Aigosages, to
take part in his campaigns in Acolis and Phrygia and afterwards gave them land
on the borders of Phrygia, from where, under their own initiative, they began to
carry out extensive raids until the entire Celtic population was slaughtered by
Prusias of Bithynia,

In 191 B¢ the Romans were drawn into the political turmoil of Asia Minor as
allies of Pergamum against the Seleucid king Antiochus 111 At Magnesia, in 190,
the Seleucid army, together with its Galatian mercenaries, were soundly
defeated by a combined Roman and Pergamene force, and in the next year the
new Roman commander Cn. Manlius Vulso set out for central Anatolia to deal
with the Galatians in their home territory. In preparation for the onslaught the
Tolistobogii and the fighting men of the Trocmi rallied at Mount Olympos,
three days” travel west of Ancyra, while the Tectosages and the families of the
Trocmi made for Mount Magaba just cast of Ancyra. In a lightning strike Man-
lius defeated first the Tolistobogii and the Trocmi, selling 40,000 men, women,
and children as slaves, and then moved quickly on to defeat the Tectosages. In
