The social ‘transhumance’ embodied in the system created the context for
entire populations to move when the territory became too constricted either
for the population to grow or to provide suitable marcher zones around for
small-scale military activity. The situation is vividly demonstrated in one of the
last and most fully documented migrations—that of the Helvetii in 59 and 58
e, Although we see the situation develop only in the words which Julius Cae-
sar chooses to provide for us ( De Bello Gallico1), his account contains a ring of
truth.
