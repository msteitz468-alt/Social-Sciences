Armorican Iron Age is the prominence of
standing stones (stelae) scattered widely in
the landscape. Of the many hundreds which
are known, two types can be distinguished:
tall pillar-like stelae and shorter hemispheri-
cal stones, All were worked with some care to
create smooth surfaces, the tall types being
columnar, faceted, or ridged. A few, most
notably the low stela from Kermaria, near
Pont-L'Abbé, and the tall example from
