The exchange rate noted by Diodorus—an amphora of wine for a slave—
need not be taken too literally: it may have been little more than a neat literary
flourish, But if it is an accurate record of relative values, then comparative data
suggest that the Celts were getting a poor deal: in Rome the trader might expect
to get the equivalent of half a dozen amphorae of wine for a healthy Gaul.
