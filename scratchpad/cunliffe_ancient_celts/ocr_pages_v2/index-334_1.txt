9. Rhodian flagons

Rhodian flagons, which were probably made in Greek workshops in southern Italy, were used for pouring wine, In
the first half of the sixth century s a number of them were used. possibly as gifts, in the trading relationships that

were beginning to develop between the western Greek colonies and native communities in southern Spain and west-
«ern central Europe.

‘Wine manufactured in the Greek coastal zone of southern France was transported in distinctive amphorae. The
distribution of sherds of sixth century sc amphorae in western central Europe indicates the zone receiving wine as a
luxury product.
