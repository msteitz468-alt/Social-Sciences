Caesar adds one tantalizing detail to hisaccount of Celtic religion. “The Gauls

claim that they are all descended from Dis Pater; they say this is the tradition

handed down to them from the Druids.’ Dis Pater, the Roman god of the dead,
provides an understandable eponymous ancestor. As the first man alive, he was
first to die and thus to acquire control of the underworld, and it is to him that
the spirits of the dead return after their earthly life. In Celtic mythology and in
the Irish Insular tradition, as Donn ‘the brown or dark one’, he stands aside
from the other gods.
