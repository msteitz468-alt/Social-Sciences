Celtic mercenarie:

Celtiberians and the Classical World

The value of the Celtiberians as mercenary troops was well appreciated
by the Carthaginians, who employed substantial detachments in their
various campaigns. Celtiberians were also used in the Roman armies,
which entered Spain in 218 e at the beginning of the Second Punic
War, though they proved to be somewhat unreliable. By the end of the
second century, with Carthage defeated and Rome now developing its
hold over the Iberian lands, Romans and Celtiberians had become all
too familiar with each other’s strengths and weaknesses.
