# Range MAN claims — Vol II Ch XIX–XXI (SS of man + conclusion)
**Source lines:** vol2 1687–2946 (body + endnotes; claims from chapter prose)
**Actually covered:** Ch XIX–XXI main text (main-thread); endnotes sampled for citations only
**Register:** theoretical-work; race/sex claims as POSITION/THEORY

## Claims

### MAN-01 Sex differences parallel Quadrumana
- **Target pages:** [[secondary-sexual-characters]], [[sexual-selection]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Darwin catalogues human sex differences (size, strength, brow ridges, hairiness, voice, earlier female maturity) and maps them onto Quadrumana parallels (gorilla sagittal crest, male beards lighter than head hair, male boldness). Secondary sexual characters are highly variable within and between races; males have been “chiefly modified” since races diverged (Novara measurements).
- **Quote:** "We thus see how close is the parallelism between the sexual differences of man and the Quadrumana."

### MAN-02 Period sex-psychology claims (attributed only)
- **Target pages:** [[darwin-descent-of-man-1871]], [[sexual-selection]]
- **Register:** POSITION (period; do not promote)
- **Claim:** Darwin asserts man is more courageous, pugnacious, energetic, and has more inventive genius; absolute brain larger in man (relative size uncertain). Recorded as 1871 attributed position.
- **Quote:** "Man is more courageous, pugnacious, and energetic than woman, and has a more inventive genius."

### MAN-03 Law of battle over women
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[struggle-for-existence]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Among “barbarous nations,” contest for women (Australian war causes; North American wrestling for wives per Hearne; Guana late marriage until rivals conquered) is the human form of the law of battle, analogous to higher Quadrumana.
- **Quote:** "With barbarous nations, for instance with the Australians, the women are the constant cause of war"

### MAN-04 Race-relative standards of beauty; exaggerate peculiarities
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[race]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Each race prefers familiar features and admires characteristic points “carried to a moderate extreme”; no universal beauty standard. Possible inherited tastes → innate race ideals (speculative). Civilised “ugliness = approach to lower animals” is not general. Ornaments and body modification among “savages” express and exaggerate valued natural peculiarities.
- **Quote:** "The men of each race prefer what they are accustomed to behold… but they like variety, and admire each characteristic point carried to a moderate extreme."

### MAN-05 Different beauty standards select different racial types
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[race]], [[monogenism]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Continued selection of women by race-specific beauty standards would modify females and (via equal mammalian inheritance) often both sexes differently by race — Darwin’s mechanism for external racial differentiation deferred from Ch VII.
- **Quote:** "it is almost certain that the different races will have been differently modified, as each has its own standard of beauty."

### MAN-06 Interference with sexual selection (civilised and savage)
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[primitive-promiscuity]], [[lubbock-john]], [[mclennan-john-ferguson]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Civilised mating is diverted by mental charms, wealth, rank; beauty need not yield more descendants (except primogeniture fortunes). Among savages, four interferences: (1) communal/promiscuous marriage (Lubbock; Darwin doubts absolute present promiscuity but accepts specialists’ original communal marriage via kinship terms); (2) female infanticide; (3) early betrothals; (4) women held as slaves. Without choice, no sexual selection.
- **Quote:** "the chief causes are, firstly, so-called communal marriages or promiscuous intercourse; secondly, infanticide… thirdly, early betrothals; and lastly, the low estimation in which women are held, as mere slaves."

### MAN-07 Primeval conditions favoured sexual selection; some female choice
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Sexual selection acted most efficiently in a remote early period when man was more instinctive, less licentious/infanticidal/slavery-oriented than many modern savages; each male jealously guarded wife/wives. Women in savage tribes sometimes exercise choice of husbands. Racial differentiation via SS thus largely ancient — explaining why earliest records already show races nearly as distinct as now.
- **Quote:** "sexual selection has been by far the most efficient" (as cause of external racial differences)

### MAN-08 Hairlessness and beard via sexual selection (contra Wallace)
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[natural-selection]], [[secondary-sexual-characters]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Naked skin is no direct advantage (contra natural selection; Wallace’s guided-development alternative rejected for hairlessness). Women less hairy than men → secondary sexual character likely from sexual selection (analogy to denuded coloured skin patches in monkeys). Beard development/race differences treated under same agency.
- **Quote:** "No one supposes that the nakedness of the skin is any direct advantage to man, so that his body cannot have been divested of hair through natural selection."

### MAN-09 Skin colour via sexual selection
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[race]]
- **Register:** THEORY/POSITION
- **Claim:** Although sexes barely differ in skin colour (weakening simple SS inference), colour is a highly valued beauty element in all races; negroes “admire their own blackness”; monkey face-colour diversity offers analogy. Darwin treats jet-black skin as a plausible SS product.
- **Quote:** "It seems at first sight a monstrous supposition that the jet blackness of the negro has been gained through sexual selection; but this view is supported by various analogies"

### MAN-10 Main conclusion: descent from lower form
- **Target pages:** [[darwin-charles]], [[antiquity-of-man]], [[biological-anthropology]], [[natural-selection]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Main conclusion — man descended from less highly organised form — rests on embryology, homology, rudiments, reversions plus affinities, distribution, geological succession; “grounds… will never be shaken.” False facts harm science more than false views.
- **Quote:** "man is descended from some less highly organised form."

### MAN-11 Genealogy: hairy quadruped to Ascidian-like vertebrate
- **Target pages:** [[darwin-charles]], [[biological-anthropology]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Early human progenitor reconstructed as hairy tailed pointed-eared arboreal Old World quadruped classed with Quadrumana; deeper ancestry through marsupial, reptile/amphibian, fish-like, ultimately aquatic hermaphrodite vertebrate resembling marine Ascidian larvae.
- **Quote:** "man is descended from a hairy quadruped, furnished with a tail and pointed ears, probably arboreal in its habits, and an inhabitant of the Old World."

### MAN-12 Races as sub-species of one stock
- **Target pages:** [[monogenism]], [[race]], [[polygenism]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** After attaining manhood, man diverged into races/sub-species (Negro and European distinct enough for “good and true species” if unlabeled) yet share unimportant structural and mental details from a common progenitor who already “deserved to rank as man.” Not from single pairs.
- **Quote:** "he has diverged into distinct races, or as they may be more appropriately called sub-species."

### MAN-13 Intellectual advance via language–brain feedback
- **Target pages:** [[natural-selection]], [[moral-sense]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Intellect advanced under NS (language, weapons, tools, social habits → dominance). Language half-art/half-instinct reacted on brain (Chauncey Wright) producing inherited effect and further language improvement; higher powers (ratiocination, abstraction, self-consciousness) follow continued mental improvement under culture.
- **Quote:** "the continued use of language will have reacted on the brain, and produced an inherited effect"

### MAN-14 Moral sense summary (conscience from social instincts)
- **Target pages:** [[moral-sense]], [[natural-selection]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Moral being = one who compares past/future actions and approves/disapproves — greatest distinction from lower animals. Moral sense from (1) enduring social instincts (shared with animals; community-limited; NS-acquired) + (2) vivid human memory/foresight → conscience after passion yields to reflection. Praise/blame and sympathy; greatest-happiness as near standard; self-regarding virtues enter with advanced reason; “less civilised” reason errs into bad customs esteemed as virtues.
- **Quote:** "A moral being is one who is capable of comparing his past and future actions and motives"

### MAN-15 Sexual vs natural selection (final definition)
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[natural-selection]], [[secondary-sexual-characters]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Sexual selection = success of certain individuals over others of same sex in relation to propagation; natural selection = success of both sexes at all ages re general conditions of life. Two sexual struggles: male combat (females passive) vs charming opposite sex (females select). Inheritance laws determine sex/age of transmission.
- **Quote:** "Sexual selection depends on the success of certain individuals over others of the same sex in relation to the propagation of the species; whilst natural selection depends on the success of both sexes, at all ages, in relation to the general conditions of life."

### MAN-16 Religion / irreligion charge answered
- **Target pages:** [[darwin-charles]], [[darwin-descent-of-man-1871]]
- **Register:** POSITION
- **Claim:** Darwin anticipates “irreligious” denunciation; equates explaining species origin by descent with explaining individual birth by ordinary reproduction within a grand ordered sequence — not blind chance.
- **Quote:** "why it is more irreligious to explain the origin of man as a distinct species by descent from some lower form… than to explain the birth of the individual through the laws of ordinary reproduction"
