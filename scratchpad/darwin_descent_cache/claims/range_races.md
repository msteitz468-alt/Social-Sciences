# Range RACES claims — Vol I Ch VII On the Races of Man
**Source lines:** vol1 951–1063
**Actually covered:** full chapter body (main-thread extraction)
**Register:** theoretical-work (all attributed); race-science material recorded as POSITION/THEORY

## Claims

### RACES-01 Species-criteria applied to human races
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[monogenism]], [[polygenism]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Darwin applies ordinary natural-history criteria (amount/constancy of difference, sterility, absence of linking varieties, geographic distribution) to human races, viewing man “in the same spirit as a naturalist would any other animal.”
- **Quote:** "Now let us apply these generally-admitted principles to the races of man, viewing him in the same spirit as a naturalist would any other animal."

### RACES-02 Structural and mental race differences (period construct)
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[darwin-descent-of-man-1871]]
- **Register:** POSITION (period race typology)
- **Claim:** Darwin holds that races differ in hair, body proportions, lung capacity, skull form/capacity, brain convolutions, constitution, disease liability, and mental characteristics (especially emotional, partly intellectual) — recorded as his 1871 position, not wiki-voice fact.
- **Quote:** "There is, however, no doubt that the various races, when carefully compared and measured, differ much from each other… Their mental characteristics are likewise very distinct"

### RACES-03 Polygenist-style case a naïve naturalist might make
- **Target pages:** [[polygenism]], [[race]]
- **Register:** THEORY (dialectical setup)
- **Claim:** Darwin reconstructs the case for ranking races as distinct species: large multi-character differences, long constancy (e.g. ancient Egyptian negroes ~4000 years), climate adaptation, Agassiz’s congruence of human races with mammalian zoological provinces, Wallace’s Malay/Papuan line, and Murray’s allegedly distinct human lice (Pediculi) by race.
- **Quote:** "he would assuredly declare that they were as good species as many to which he had been in the habit of affixing specific names."

### RACES-04 Hybrid fertility evidence nearly valueless; Broca cited
- **Target pages:** [[polygenism]], [[race]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Darwin treats alleged sterility (Australian/Tasmanian women with Europeans; mulatto intermarriage) as weak or “almost valueless,” notes half-caste killing by pure blacks as confounder, and cites Bachman/Lyell for mulatto fertility comparable to parent races; even perfect fertility would not absolutely preclude specific rank under Pallasian domestication logic.
- **Quote:** "the evidence, however, on this head has now been shewn to be almost valueless."

### RACES-05 Against specific rank: fusion, variability, graduation
- **Target pages:** [[monogenism]], [[race]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Decisive anti-species arguments: races fuse when coexisting (Brazil, Chile, Viti, S. Africa); every racial character is highly variable; races graduate into each other, shown by endless disagreement among classifiers (Virey 2 … Burke 63 races).
- **Quote:** "the most weighty of all the arguments against treating the races of man as distinct species, is that they graduate into each other"

### RACES-06 Sub-species as proper rank; “race” by habit
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[monogenism]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Darwin recommends “sub-species” as the most appropriate rank, while expecting the habitual term “race” to persist.
- **Quote:** "the term “sub-species” might here be used with much propriety. But from long habit the term “race” will perhaps always be employed."

### RACES-07 Evolution dissolves monogenist–polygenist dispute
- **Target pages:** [[monogenism]], [[polygenism]], [[psychic-unity-of-mankind]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** For evolutionists, all human races descend from a single primitive stock (whether labeled species or not); once evolution is accepted the monogenist/polygenist dispute “will die a silent and unobserved death.” Non-evolutionists remain trapped without a workable species definition free of creation acts.
- **Quote:** "When the principles of evolution are generally accepted… the dispute between the monogenists and the polygenists will die a silent and unobserved death."

### RACES-08 Mental similarity across races; Tylor and Lubbock
- **Target pages:** [[psychic-unity-of-mankind]], [[tylor-edward-burnett]], [[lubbock-john]], [[race]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Darwin argues numerous unimportant bodily and mental similarities (tastes, dancing, music, tattooing, gesture-language, facial expression of emotion, convergent stone arrow-heads per Nilsson, megalithic burial) imply inheritance from common progenitors, not independent acquisition — strengthened by reading Tylor and Lubbock. Fuegian and “full-blooded negro” anecdotes illustrate mental similarity to Europeans.
- **Quote:** "He who will carefully read Mr. Tylor’s and Sir J. Lubbock’s interesting works can hardly fail to be deeply impressed with the close similarity between the men of all races in tastes, dispositions and habits."

### RACES-09 Early state when man first spread
- **Target pages:** [[antiquity-of-man]], [[lubbock-john]], [[civilization]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Spreading widely by sea preceded major racial divergence (same race never on distinct continents). Lubbock’s inventory of arts: earliest man had spear and club only; fire and maybe rafts probable; counting to ten improbable. Intellect/social faculties not “inferior in any extreme degree” to lowest savages, else man could not have dominated early struggle. Language (imperfect) needed for that dominance; no sharp line where “man” begins in a graduated series.
- **Quote:** "the spear… and the club… are the only things left."

### RACES-10 Races not from single pairs
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[natural-selection]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Like domestic sub-breeds, human races formed from many individuals varying in the same direction under shared conditions/selection, not from single ancestral pairs.
- **Quote:** "the races of man have been similarly produced, the modifications being either the direct result of exposure to different conditions, or the indirect result of some form of selection."

### RACES-11 Extinction of races: competition of tribe and race
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[social-darwinism]], [[struggle-for-existence]], [[darwin-charles]]
- **Register:** POSITION/THEORY (historically load-bearing; do not promote to wiki-voice)
- **Claim:** Darwin attributes partial/complete extinction of races chiefly to competition of tribe with tribe and race with race, not unfavourable physical conditions. Checks on savage tribes listed (famine, infanticide, war, etc.). When civilised nations contact “barbarians,” struggle is short except where climate aids natives; causes include cultivation, new diseases, liquor, “bewilderment” (Sproat), and grade of civilisation (Bagehot contrast with classical era). Parallel drawn to species extinction (fossil horse; New Zealand rat).
- **Quote:** "Extinction follows chiefly from the competition of tribe with tribe, and race with race."

### RACES-12 Formation: crossing, climate weak, use weak, NS weak on external race traits
- **Target pages:** [[race]], [[natural-selection]], [[sexual-selection]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** New races may form by crossing (Broca on Aryan/Hindoo; Santali–Hindu gradations). Skin colour not explained by climate (Pallas; Dutch in S. Africa unchanged 300 years; gypsies/Jews). Darwin’s own miasma–dark-skin correlation hypothesis fails his checks. Direct conditions, use/disuse, and correlation insufficient for global race differences. **Natural selection** cannot fix external racial differences because they appear of no direct service (intellectual/moral faculties excepted). Residual “spontaneous variations” possible.
- **Quote:** "not one of the external differences between the races of man are of any direct or special service to him."

### RACES-13 Sexual selection as chief agency of racial differentiation
- **Target pages:** [[sexual-selection]], [[race]], [[darwin-descent-of-man-1871]]
- **Register:** THEORY
- **Claim:** Having rejected climate, use, correlation, and natural selection for external race traits, Darwin nominates **sexual selection** as the remaining powerful agency (colour, hairiness, features), to be proved after reviewing the animal kingdom in Part II; he does not claim it explains all differences.
- **Quote:** "there remains one important agency, namely Sexual Selection, which appears to have acted as powerfully on man, as on many other animals."
