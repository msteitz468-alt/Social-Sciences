## Claim 1: Integrated survey rationale — mounds, watercourses, surface ceramics
- Register: METHOD
- Attribution: Adams 1981

## Claim 5: Mosaic assembly and prolonged stereo/lighting study of faint levees

- Attribution: Adams 1981

## Claim 7: Dual simultaneous mapping of watercourses and sites
- Register: METHOD
- Attribution: Adams 1981
- Summary: Two maps were elaborated simultaneously on one base: watercourses essentially photo-dependent from the outset; archaeological mapping started from unambiguously photo-located major mounds/junctions and was carried forward by prismatic-compass triangulation (~1½° accuracy with multiple readings) and odometer distances with three-arm vernier protractor.
- Quote: "two maps had to be elaborated simultaneously on a single base, of which only one, the watercourses, was essentially dependent on the aerial photographs from the outset. From there it was carried forward by triangulation with a prismatic compass (multiple readings permitting something on the order of 1½° accuracy) and measured odometer distances"
