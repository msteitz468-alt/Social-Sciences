### R5-009: Cucuteni-Tripolye C2 crisis and end
- **Target page:** anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007
- **Register:** finding
- **Claim:** Anthony dates two major Tripolye crises: C1/C2 transition shock c. 3300 BCE simultaneous with early Yamnaya horizon (abandonment of large regions near steppe border including almost entire South Bug valley; no surviving Cucuteni in Romania after two millennia); final erasure of Tripolye customs c. 2800–2600 BCE when early Yamnaya ended. - **Quote:** "The first major shock came at the transition from Tripolye C1 to C2 about 3300 BCE, simultaneously with the appearance of the early Yamnaya horizon. The second and final sweep of change erased the last remnants of Tripolye customs around 2800–2600 BCE"

### R5-010: Usatovo culture definition and dates

### R5-026: no Indo-European invasion of Europe conclusion Ch. 14

### R5-037: arid climate and marsh winter refuges drive settlement
- **Target page:** sintashta-culture
