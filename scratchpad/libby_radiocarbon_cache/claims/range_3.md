### R3-002: Catalogue organization — independent runs vs. braced remeasurements
- **Register:** method finding / lab procedure
- **Claim:** Multiple dates listed for a sample mean completely independent runs on separately extracted carbon portions, unless dates are linked by a brace, in which case they are remeasurements of a given sample (often different counter and/or re-extraction with acid).
- **Attribution:** Libby lab / ch. VI intro
- **Quote:** "The number of runs is indicated by the number of dates listed. These runs were completely independent, involving separate portions of the original sample from which the carbon had been extracted independently, unless the dates are linked together with a brace. In this case, the results are those obtained by remeasurement of a given sample, usually in a different counter, and frequently involving re-extraction of the sample with acid."

### R3-017: Nippur charcoal long-count experiment — Hammurabi floating chronology (C-752)
- **Register:** finding + method commentary
- **Claim:** C-752 charcoal roof beam from Nippur house fixed to Ibi-Sin/Shu-Sin (≈250 years before Hammurabi) was split into three portions and each counted ~1 month (3 months total) to test method sensitivity. Weighted mean gave ~4004±106 yr; preferred arithmetic mean 3945 ± 106 years (≈1993±106 B.C. as Libby converts). Error budget folds half-life uncertainty 0.54% and modern-wood assay 0.67%. Libby concludes younger of possible Mesopotamian calendars is strongly favored (allowable historical range 2374–1975 B.C.).
- **Attribution:** Libby lab analysis; material McCown excavation; submitted Donald E. McCown
- **Quote:** "a serious attempt was made to test the limit of sensitivity of the radiocarbon dating method. An important subject, the time of Hammurabi of Babylon, was selected… The arithmetical average … corresponds to an age of 3945 +106 years, or 1993+106 B.c. … we conclude that the younger of the possible calendars is strongly favored by the radiocarbon dating."
- **Line (in slice):** 241–328
- **Target pages:** [[radiocarbon-dating]] [[libby-radiocarbon-dating-1952]] [[libby-half-life]] [[exchange-reservoir]]

### R3-070: Counting error as minimum; half-life and modern assay in long runs
- **Register:** method
- **Claim:** Throughout the catalogue, printed ± errors are counting σ only (“certainly is a minimum error”). For high-precision Nippur and Mayan experiments, Libby explicitly combines counting error with 0.54% half-life uncertainty and 0.67% modern-wood assay uncertainty to produce final ±106 or ±110 year figures.
- **Attribution:** Libby lab / ch. VI Nippur & Mayan discussions
- **Quote:** "the errors indicated above are calculated solely on the basis of the square root of the total number of counts taken… It certainly is a minimum error. … an estimated 0.54 per cent error in the half-life of radiocarbon and 0.67 per cent in the assay of modern wood must be included."
- **Line (in slice):** 287–316; 2755–2763
