### R1-024: Fate of atmospheric C atoms → radioactive CO₂ → plants → animals
- **Register:** THEORETICAL

### R1-025: Ocean inorganic carbon as exchange reservoir; turnover ≪ C-14 lifetime
- **Register:** THEORETICAL
- **Claim:** Libby included dissolved sea carbon (CO₂, bicarbonate, carbonate) in the natural radiocarbon reservoir via exchange with atmospheric CO₂; turnover time for air CO₂ through this reservoir probably not in excess of 500 years (hardly exceeding 1000 years)—short compared with radiocarbon lifetime, so each C-14 atom makes the round trip several times and distribution should be nearly uniform (little vertical/latitudinal/longitudinal gradients) despite ~3.5× equatorial–polar neutron variation.
- **Attribution:** Libby, Ch. I Principles
- **Quote:** "The time for radioactive carbon dioxide in the air to distribute itself through this reservoir probably is not in excess of 500 years. This is the so-called 'turn-over' time for the life-cycle which has been widely discussed by geochemists. The estimates vary quite widely, but it does seem that this time can hardly exceed 1000 years. Since this is a time short as compared to the lifetime of radiocarbon, we conclude that any given radiocarbon atom will make the round trip several times in its lifetime, and we therefore predict that the distribution of radiocarbon throughout the reservoir will be quite uniform."
