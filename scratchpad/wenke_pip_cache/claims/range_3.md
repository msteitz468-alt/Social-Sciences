## Opening frame & deep chronology

### PIP-R3-001
- **Claim:** W&O reserve the label *Homo sapiens sapiens* (“people like us”) for only some humans after about 150,000 years ago, and state that only by about 30,000 years ago did *H. s. sapiens* alone constitute humanity.

- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "We reserve the ultimate accolade of “people like us,” Homo sapiens sapiens, for only some of the humans who lived after about 150,000 years ago, and it was not until about 30,000 years ago that we alone came to constitute humanity."

## Homo erectus grade: taxonomy, body, tools, economy

### PIP-R3-003
- **Claim:** W&O treat humans between ~1.5 and 0.5 mya as *Homo erectus* for narrative convenience, while noting the taxon is contested (possible restriction to East Asian forms vs African *Homo ergaster*; questions of stasis, European presence, and African membership).
- **Register:** POSITION / THEORETICAL CLAIM
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007 summarizing physical-anthropology debates
- **Grounding quote:** "In what follows here, all of the humans who lived between about 1.5 and 0.5 million years ago are called Homo erectus, but this designation is likely to change as these taxonomies are refined."
- **Wiki targets:** [[homo-erectus]], [[biological-anthropology]], [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]]
- **Notes:** Cache L135–165; also lists *H. antecessor*, *heidelbergensis*, etc. in note 6.

### PIP-R3-004

- **Register:** FINDING (descriptive morphology synthesis)
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "Individuals generally classed as Homo erectus averaged just over five feet… had a cranial capacity that ranged between about 850 and 1,300 cm3… low foreheads, large brow ridges, thick cranial bones, and almost no chins"
- **Wiki targets:** [[homo-erectus]], [[biological-anthropology]]
- **Notes:** Cache L129–134.

### PIP-R3-005
- **Claim:** Nariokotome boy KNM-WT 15000 (West Turkana, ~1.6 mya) is presented as a well-preserved *H. erectus*/ergaster-grade individual: ~1.6 m tall at ~11 years, cranial capacity 880 cm³, thick brow ridges, skeleton already similar to moderns—suggesting body form preceded modern mentality.

- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007 on KNM-WT 15000
- **Grounding quote:** "He was 1.6 meters (5 feet, 3 inches) tall… His cranial capacity, however, was 880 c3… His skeleton appears very similar to ours… our ancestors developed a body much like ours long before their mentalities equaled ours."
- **Wiki targets:** [[homo-erectus]], NEW:nariokotome / NEW:knm-wt-15000, [[biological-anthropology]]
- **Notes:** Cache L176–184; figure labels *H. ergaster* (erectus grade).

### PIP-R3-006
- **Claim:** Acheulian hand-axes (named after St. Acheul) associated with *H. erectus* appear in East Africa more than a million years ago, whereas earliest European Acheulian is cited ~600 kya; they require more manufacture steps than Oldowan choppers and may approach functionally optimal proportions—or be cores for flakes; function remains unclear.

- **Wiki targets:** [[homo-erectus]], [[palaeolithic]], [[archaeology]]

### PIP-R3-007

## Models of modern human origins

### PIP-R3-019
- **Claim:** Two basic competing models structure the chapter: (1) African Origins / Total Replacement / Noah’s Ark / Eve — modern humans evolve first only in Africa a few hundred thousand years ago or less, then replace other hominins worldwide with little/no genetic interchange; (2) Continuity / Multiregional Evolution / Candelabra — archaic populations outside Africa also contribute via regional continuity plus gene flow under shared hunter-forager selection, converging as one species by ~30 kya with regional physical differences.
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM (model exposition)
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007 (majority view = replacement as of their writing)
- **Grounding quote:** "One of these, which has become the majority view, is described variously as the African Origins, Total Replacement, Noah’s Ark, or Eve model… An alternative model, commonly known as the Continuity, Multiregional Evolution, or Candelabra model"
- **Wiki targets:** [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[stringer-chris]], [[evolutionary-archaeology]], [[biological-anthropology]]
- **Notes:** Cache L684–743; Figure 4.11 captions; Weidenreich 1930s multiregional vs Leakey African origins historiography (L744–749).

### PIP-R3-020
- **Claim:** Multiregional models face the problem that few nonhuman species evolve convergently over a wide range; proposed answer is shared cultural niche plus gene flow (estimates as low as one migrant/generation), which many still doubt can produce simultaneous similar *H. s. sapiens* from N China to S Africa.
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM / CRITIQUE
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "This presumed pattern of convergent human evolution over a wide area and through great spans of time remains a problem for the Multiregional Evolution model, but, as we shall see, the African Origins model also requires some assumptions."
- **Wiki targets:** [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[biological-anthropology]]
- **Notes:** Cache L750–763.

### PIP-R3-021
- **Claim:** Five testable implications of Total Replacement (as adapted from Frayer et al.): (1) earliest *H. s. sapiens* only in Africa first; (2) no anatomical genetic-mixing intermediates outside Africa ~400–30 kya; (3) earliest moderns outside Africa resemble African ancestors not locals, plus rapid replacement signal in fossils/archaeology along exit routes; (4) no anatomical continuity across replacement horizon (e.g. Zhoukoudian 300 kya → 50 kya → today); (5) some visible advantage explaining complete displacement (tech, skull, subsistence, site patterns).
- **Register:** THEORETICAL CLAIM (hypothesis operationalization)
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007 following Frayer et al. “Paleontological Test”
- **Grounding quote:** "First, the earliest known Homo sapiens sapiens should be found in Africa… Second… no anatomical evidence of genetic mixing… Third… earliest modern humans should resemble these African ancestors… Fourth… no evidence of anatomical continuity… Fifth, we should look for some evidence as to why Africans were able to displace well-adapted hominins"
- **Wiki targets:** [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]]
- **Notes:** Cache L888–916; W&O stress all five are equivocal (what counts as intermediate/rapid) (L917–933).

### PIP-R3-022
- **Claim:** W&O explicitly state their review of replacement tests ends inconclusively: reasonable scientists differ; hypotheses cannot be proven—only not disproven; “best” model = fit + parsimony.
- **Register:** POSITION / META
- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "this review ends inconclusively: Reasonable scientists differ on interpretations of this evidence… hypotheses cannot be “proven.” … all that science can aspire to is pointing us toward the current “best” model"
- **Wiki targets:** [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[archaeology]]
- **Notes:** Cache L938–940, 930–933.

## Molecular/genetic evidence (2007 horizon)

### PIP-R3-023
- **Claim:** Cann, Stoneking & Wilson mtDNA from 147 placentas concluded all living people descend from an African “mitochondrial Eve” ~140–290 kya (probably ~200 kya), with Africa identified by greatest living mtDNA variation (longest accumulation).
- **Register:** FINDING (genetic study as reported)
- **Attribution:** Cann, Stoneking & Wilson via Wenke & Olszewski 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "They concluded that everyone living today is descended from an African woman, a “mitochondrial Eve,” who lived between 140,000 and 290,000 years ago—probably about 200,000 years ago."

- **Attribution:** Frayer et al.; Melnick & Hoelzer as presented by W&O 2007
- **Grounding quote:** "Frayer and his colleagues conclude… “There is no statistically reliable mtDNA evidence for an African, or any other single, geographically centered origin for Eve.”"

- **Notes:** Cache L846–866.

### PIP-R3-025
- **Claim:** As of W&O 2007, Neandertal mtDNA from fossils (Krings, Ovchinnikov, Caramelli et al. in notes) showing >20 mtDNA differences from modern humans is presented as strong genetic support for Out of Africa: Neandertals (and likely most non-African archaic *Homo*) did not contribute to the modern gene pool.

- **Attribution:** Wenke & Olszewski 2007 on early Neandertal mtDNA studies

- **Notes:** Cache L867–874, 1825–1831. **Critical 2007-horizon flag:** later nuclear aDNA demonstrated limited Neandertal/Denisovan admixture—outside this range; do not promote W&O’s “no contribution” to wiki voice.

- **Notes:** Cache L1812–1831.
