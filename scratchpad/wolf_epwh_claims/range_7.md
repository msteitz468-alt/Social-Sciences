### C7-001: European Asian voyages sought Asia as imagined treasure house
**Claim:** Wolf states that European exploratory and mercantile voyages to America and Africa grew out of the search for sea routes to Asia, imagined as a treasure house of unlimited wealth; westward attempts continued from the Vivaldi brothers (1291) through Columbus, Cabot, and even Jean Nicolet (1638).

### C7-003: Sixteenth-century Asia held powerful tributary states; Europeans long confined to tidal footholds
