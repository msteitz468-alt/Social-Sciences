### R3-C02
- **ID:** R3-C02
- **Register:** CRITIQUE / META
- **Claim:** S&T hold that individuals appear in archaeological literature mainly as anonymous powerful agencies (e.g. a paramount chief inferred from Stonehenge, Silbury Hill, or Bush Barrow) or as artisan idiosyncrasy/creativity (Hill & Gunn); literary sources may rescue anonymity, but archaeology has predominantly concerned units larger than the individual—an absence they now assess via mediation of individual and society.
- **Quote/ground:** “Individuals have appeared in the archaeological literature as powerful but anonymous agencies, for example a 'paramount chief might be inferred from the construction of Stonehenge, Silbury Hill or the Bush Barrow… The work of an individual artisan may, perhaps, be recognized… but here individuality is reduced to idiosyncracy and creativity… archaeology has been predominantly concerned with units larger than the individual.”
- **Topics:** chiefdom inference; artisan individuality; scale
- **Wiki targets:** [[stonehenge]], [[chiefdom]], [[post-processual-archaeology]]
