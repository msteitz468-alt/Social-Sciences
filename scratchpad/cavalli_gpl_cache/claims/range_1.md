### C1-011: Parma research — drift predicted from demography vs local genetic variation
- **Register:** finding
- **Claim:** Main Parma research studied chance in evolution ("drift") using parish demographic records over ~three centuries in a region of highly variable population density (high plains, low mountains); village sizes and migration estimated from parish books; with few parents, chance causes large fluctuations in gene frequencies between villages; research predicted between-village variation due to drift from demography and compared it with local genetic variation.
- **Attribution:** Cavalli-Sforza 2000 (Parma work with collaborators)
- **Quote:** "My main research at Parma was the study of the role of chance in evolution… This research made it possible to predict the variation between villages due to drift on the basis of their demography, and compare it with local genetic variation."
- **Target pages:** [[genetic-drift]], [[cavalli-sforza-luigi-luca]], [[methods]]
- **Notes:** (as of Cavalli-Sforza 2000); foundational empirical program for drift.

### C1-031: Genetic definition of racism preferred over broad cultural intolerance

- **Attribution:** Cavalli-Sforza 2000
- **Quote:** "If these genetic differences were found to be genuinely important and could support the sense of superiority that one people can have over another, then racism is justified—at least formally. I find this genetic or biological definition of racism more satisfactory than others."

- **Notes:** definitional position.
