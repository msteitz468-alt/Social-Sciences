A starting reading/ingestion roadmap: **100 works each in sociology, anthropology,
and archaeology**, chosen to build a solid foundational base. This is the **user's
working checklist** (curated wish-list + ingest status). Per `CLAUDE.md` Step 6

partial). Also update `Structural_Sources.md` (acquisition/queue ledger) when the
work is listed or queued there.

**Marks:** ✅ full ingest · ⚠️ partial (component volumes/parts still open).

**Ingest status last reconciled:** 2026-07-10 (full pass) against `wiki/sources/` (264 source pages). Catch-up marks added for ingested works that had been left unchecked.

## How this list is built

- **Tiered by ingest priority within each discipline.** Tier 1 = ingest first (the
  foundational theory and defining works everything else links to — scaffold the
  `disciplines/`, `theories/`, and `thinkers/` pages from these). Later tiers deepen
  and broaden. Ingesting roughly in tier order means early pages have their link
  targets already on disk.
- **Both wiki layers are served.** Theoretical and programmatic works feed the
  knowledge layer (`theories/`, `thinkers/`, `concepts/`, `methods/`); classic
  ethnographies, excavation syntheses, and empirical studies feed the subject layer
  (`societies/`, `cultures/`, `sites/`, `phenomena/`) *and* earn `studies/` pages.
- **`[FLAG]` marks contested / partisan / fast-superseding works.** Per `CLAUDE.md`,
  these get heavy `reliability_notes`, their interpretive theses quarantined to
  `debates/` rather than asserted in wiki voice; archaeogenetic works additionally get
  "(as of Author Year)" date-stamps. The flag is a handling instruction, not a verdict
  on quality — several flagged works are field-defining.
- **`[X-disc]` marks cross-disciplinary works** already counted under another heading
  or straddling fields; ingest once, link from both.

## Selection bias — stated honestly

This is a **canon-weighted, anglophone-heavy** list: it privileges the founding
European/American theoretical traditions and the works that anglophone graduate
training treats as landmarks. It under-represents non-Western scholarship, the
Global South as a *producer* (not just object) of social science, and very recent
(post-2015) work still finding its status. That bias mirrors the fields' own historical
gatekeeping — worth making visible on the `disciplines/` reflexivity sections rather
than hiding. Ask and I'll add a fourth "global / decolonial supplement" column, or
rebalance any discipline toward contemporary, quantitative, or regional depth.

## Suggested ingest order across disciplines

Rather than finishing one discipline before starting the next, interleave the Tier 1s
so the wiki's `disciplines/`, `theories/`, and `thinkers/` scaffolding fills out evenly
and cross-disciplinary links (Marx, Durkheim, Weber, evolutionism, structuralism,
world-systems, the state-formation debate) have targets on both sides early. A workable
sequence: all three Tier 1 sets → all three Tier 2 sets → then deepen by whichever
discipline you want to lead with. Flag-marked works are best ingested *after* the
surrounding non-partisan literature exists, so their theses land as positions on a
populated `debates/` page rather than in a vacuum.
