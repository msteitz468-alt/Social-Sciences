# Accomplishment of Natural Growth

Lareau’s term for the dominant **working-class and poor** logic of childrearing in *[[lareau-unequal-childhoods-2011|Unequal Childhoods]]* — not a “deficit” label in her framing.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Lareau 2003/2011.)* Parents provide care, food, safety, and support for **children’s spontaneous growth**; children had more unstructured time with kin and multi-age peers; adults used more **directives** than negotiation; parents often **deferred** to school professionals. Exemplars include Tyrec Taylor (peer-centered free time), Katie Brindle (play left to children amid scarcity), Harold McAllister (short adult talk; institutional constraint). Not a cultural-deficiency label in Lareau’s framing — schools undervalued these skills.

## Semantic History

Paired with concerted cultivation as the book’s core typology; misread as cultural deficiency when stripped of Lareau’s structural account of scarce resources and institutional bias.

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic case comparison (e.g. Tyrec Taylor, Wendy Driver, and other working-class/poor families in the sample).

## Applications

Explains classed school–family mismatches; complements [[cultural-capital]] without reducing culture to taste.

## Critiques and Limitations

Ideal-type risk; within-class variation; strengths of natural growth (independence, kin networks) can be undervalued by schools.
