# Fatalistic suicide

## Definition as Coined

In footnote 25 at the end of the anomic chapter of *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*, [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] states that there is a type opposite to [[anomic-suicide]], as [[egoistic-suicide]] and [[altruistic-suicide]] are opposites: suicide from **excessive regulation** — “persons with futures pitilessly blocked and passions violently choked by oppressive discipline.”

Examples he offers: very young husbands; the married woman who is childless; historically, slaves under certain conditions and suicides from excessive physical or moral despotism. For completeness a fourth type should be set up, but he judged it of little contemporary importance and hard to document beyond those cases — “useless to dwell upon it,” though of historical interest. Name chosen to contrast with “anomy”: **fatalistic suicide**.

## Semantic History

Almost unused in Durkheim’s own later corpus as a developed type; later secondary literature sometimes revives it (prisons, total institutions, hyper-regulated settings). The dual-axis 2×2 (integration × regulation) is largely a *post hoc* systematization of his three main types plus this footnote.

## Operationalizations

Minimal in the primary text — illustrative cases only, no tables. Do not treat as a full empirical program of *Suicide*.

## Applications

- Completes the regulation axis with [[anomie]] / [[anomic-suicide]]
- Historiography of typology: shows Durkheim’s awareness of over- as well as under-regulation

## Critiques and Limitations

Underdeveloped by design; later uses often project 20th-c. institutional cases onto a residual 1897 note. Record as residual type, not as a peer chapter to egoistic/altruistic/anomic.
