# Mutuality of Being

> *(Attributed — [[sahlins-marshall|Marshall Sahlins]], *[[sahlins-what-kinship-is-2013|What Kinship Is—And Is Not]]* 2013; revised from Sahlins 2011 *JRAI*.)* The specific quality of kinship: kinfolk are persons who **participate intrinsically in each other's existence** — "members of one another."

## Definition as Coined

Sahlins proposes "mutuality of being" as a modest solution to "the 150-year-old anthropological problem of what kinship is." Kinfolk are "persons who participate intrinsically in each other's existence"; they are "members of one another." The formula is meant to cover constitution of kinship **by social construction as by procreation**, and to account for "the mysterious effectiveness of relationality" (phrase attributed to [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Eduardo Viveiros de Castro]]) — how relatives emotionally and symbolically live each other's lives and die each other's deaths.

Because kinship involves such transpersonal relations of being and experience, Sahlins places it "in the same ontological regime as magic, [[gift-exchange|gift exchange]], sorcery, and witchcraft." Where being is mutual, "experience is more than individual."

## Semantic History

| Layer | Meaning / use |
|---|---|
| Epigraphic ethnography Sahlins gathers | Palauan *kauchad* "mutual person(s)" (Smith 1981); Korowai "intersubjective belonging" (Stasch 2009); Ashanti lineage as "one person," *nipa koro* (Fortes 1969); Gawa *dala* and "transbodily being" (Munn 1986); Maori "kinship I" (Johansen 1954) |
| Philosophical / comparative ancestry Sahlins claims | Aristotle: kinship as "the same entity in discrete subjects"; Lévy-Bruhl "participation"; Leenhardt dual unities / dual substantives; Durkheim recovered as radical constructivist of mutual participation (against Schneider's reading) |
| Post-Schneider "relatedness" literature | Sahlins endorses constructivist orthodoxy (Carsten, Bamford & Leach, Franklin & McKinnon, etc.) that whatever is genealogically construed may also be socially constructed; mutuality is his *definitional* synthesis of that orthodoxy |
| Viveiros de Castro | "Mysterious effectiveness of relationality"; kinship–gift–magic as one animistic regime; Amazonian "non-biological theory of life" |

The term is Sahlins's coinage as a *definition*, not an emic native term (though he builds it from native glosses of mutual personhood).

## Operationalizations

Sahlins does **not** operationalize mutuality as a measurable variable. He labels Ch.1 a "Frazerian-style" uncontrolled comparison — ethnography exemplifies the idea rather than verifies it statistically. Content of mutuality as he develops it:

1. **Constitution** — how people become kin: procreation (variable substances, spiritual thirds, ancestral co-generation); feeding, co-residence, land-sharing, name-sharing, conversation, shared tabus, mutual suffering, compact/adoption.
2. **Transpersonal praxis** — mystical interdependence (Wilson on Nyakyusa); shared injury/responsibility; mourning as mutual death; spouse dual unities and couvade; agency and interest as "we-ness."
3. **Group scale** — lineages/clans as one entity in discrete subjects; classificatory kinship present from birth via ancestral co-generation (not "extension" of primary kin).
4. **Negative form** — sorcery/witchcraft as "negative kinship" (LiPuma on Maring: sorcerers cannibalize the life-force of their own kin); unrequited gift as life-drain (*kaihau* of the Maori *[[hau]]* per Johansen).

## Applications

*(Attributed — Sahlins 2013 unless noted.)*

- **Constructivist cases.** To Pamona parentage via nurturance (Schrauwers); Iñupiat naming over natal bonds (Bodenhorn); Kamea non-substance kinship (Bamford); Korowai *lambi-lelo* "become a relative" through visiting, cooperation, food-giving (Stasch); Fijian *kerekere* test — the one who gives is the real relative (Sahlins 1962).
- **Substance that is already social.** Ku Waru *kopong* "grease" from soil equalizes prenatal and postnatal kinship (Merlan & Rumsey); Schneider on American "blood" as constructed as "code."
- **Unilineal vs cognatic tendency.** Sahlins's generalization: unilineal descent groups tend to **build kinship into procreation** (ancestors as co-generators); cognatic/kindred orders favor **life-achieved** mutuality (Taylor on Achuar/Jivaro person-construction over the life course).
- **Gift and mana.** Johansen's Maori triple of kinship / fellowship / *[[mana]]*; gift exchange as creating fellowship = kinship (via Viveiros/Gregory); failed gift as rupture of mutual being.
- **Developmental parenthesis.** Sahlins equates Tomasello-style infant "shared intentionality" / "we-ness" with the kinship form, while denying that culturally specific kinship is innate — "Kinship may be a universal possibility in nature… [but] is always a cultural particularity."

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Method.** Sahlins himself flags uncontrolled comparison and illustrative (not verificatory) ethnography — weak for cross-cultural statistical claims; strong as conceptual synthesis.
- **Politics deferred.** He reserves "politics of kinship" (inequality, domination via gift asymmetries) for other occasions — mutuality can sound irenic if that side is omitted.
- **Relation to Schneider.** Mutuality is offered as what Schneider's followers recovered by attending to symbols and meanings after Schneider tried to close kinship studies; it is also positioned against genealogical/biogenetic orthodoxy (Scheffler–Lounsbury, Davis–Warner, Gellner, Morgan). See [[kinship-biology-vs-culture-debate]].
- **Sociobiology / EP.** Grouped with the "fictive kinship" orthodoxy; engagement is polemical and thin on evolutionary mechanisms.
- **Primary vs secondary sources.** Case material is cited ethnography, not re-fieldwork; reliability travels with the cited monographs.
