# Withering Away of the State

## Definition as Coined

[[engels-friedrich|Engels]] concludes *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]* ch. IX by arguing that the [[the-state|state]] has not existed from all eternity; societies managed without it; at a stage of economic development involving class cleavage the state became necessary; production is approaching a stage where classes hinder production and will fall as they arose — “The state inevitably falls with them. The society which organizes production anew on the basis of free and equal association of the producers will put the whole state machinery where it will then belong–into the museum of antiquities, next to the spinning wheel and the bronze ax.” This **withering-away** thesis is a theoretical prediction and political horizon, not an empirical finding.

## Semantic History

Central to later Marxist state theory and to criticisms of twentieth-century socialist states that did not “wither.” Distinct from anarchist immediate abolition and from Weberian analyses of the modern state’s rationalization (no withering built in). Interacts with [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]] debates: if the state can appear independent, does class abolition automatically dissolve it?

## Operationalizations

Not operationalized as a measurable variable in *Origin*; it is a historical-materialist inference from the state’s alleged class origin.

## Applications

*Origin* ch. IX; reception in Leninist and anti-Leninist Marxism; contrast with [[weber-max|Weber]]’s monopoly-of-force definition on [[the-state]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Falsified as near-term prediction by actually existing socialist states for many critics; defenders treat it as conditional on genuine classlessness and world scale. Record as POSITION, not wiki-voice future.
