# Bearer (Träger)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]*, **bearer** (*Träger*, support) designates the function of social agents as **supports of relations of production**, not as originating subjects of the process. [[balibar-etienne|Balibar]]: classes are “not the subjects of this mechanism but its bearers”; the economic relation of production is among three functionally defined terms — owner class / means of production / class of exploited producers — and “the definition of classes does not precede social relations of production but vice versa.” [[althusser-louis|Althusser]]’s historical-time outline: individuals appear as bearers of the process; the “authorless theatre” of [[structural-causality]] forces spectators first to be actors of a script they did not write. [[ranciere-jacques|Rancière]] distinguishes rigorous **personification** in *Capital* (subject as bearer of a relation) from residual anthropological subject/object reversal of the 1844 Manuscripts.

## Semantic History

Marx’s *Capital* uses *Träger* and related personification language; Althusserian reading radicalizes it as the anti-humanist replacement for Man as theoretical subject of history ([[theoretical-anti-humanism]], *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]* and *Reading Capital*). On the stage of reproduction, Balibar notes, individuals come forward masked (*Charaktermaske*). Brewster’s glossary links forms of historical individuality to the structure (see TRANSITION / BEARER cross-references in the Complete Edition apparatus). Later agency and subject-theory debates treat *Träger* as the emblem of structural Marxism’s evacuation of subjectivity — either as necessary scientific abstraction or as political-theoreticist error.

## Operationalizations

- **Personification ≠ anthropology.** Capitalist and worker as personifications of capital and wage-labour relations, not primordial subjects who invest essence in objects (Rancière’s contrast of 1844 and *Capital*).
- **Three-term relation.** Owner class / means / exploited producers — connection of “invisible threads,” not contract between pre-given legal subjects.
- **Reproduction.** Continuity of social relations reproduced through agents as function-bearers of the process as a whole.
- **Ideology interface.** Lived relation of agents to their conditions can misrecognize structure as intention or nature; scientific discourse thinks them as bearers.

## Applications

- Anti-humanist reading of class, capital, and the labour process in *Capital*.
- Transition analysis: modes define modes of historical individuality via the structure’s connections.
- Bridge between structural causality (structure in effects) and sociology of roles/functions without humanist ground.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Humanist and historicist Marxisms: bearer language cannot ground emancipation or ethical responsibility ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]).
- Risk of sociological reification if “bearer” is read as empirical role inventory rather than theoretical function in a combination.
- Gender, race, and other categorical memberships are under-specified in the 1965 economic *Träger* analysis (later work outside this volume).
