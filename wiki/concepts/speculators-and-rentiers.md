# Speculators and rentiers (Pareto)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* Ch XII, [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] split “capitalists” into non-uniform types and labelled two convenient poles **speculators (S)** and **rentiers (R)** (§§2233–34, 2310–2313):

- **R:** Savers living on fixed or virtually fixed incomes; secretive, cautious, timid; Class II residues often powerful.
- **S:** Opposite traits — expansive personalities, combination instincts, often underground holders of real power; Class I residues powerful.

Neither S nor R is typically adept at **force**; force-users form a third group that robs R easily and S with more difficulty (§2313).

## Semantic History

Economic typology inside the social-system chapter; maps onto [[lions-and-foxes]] and [[demagogic-plutocracy]] (“government by speculators”). Not the same as pure-economics *rentier* in all contexts — here a social-equilibrium actor type.

## Operationalizations

- **Cycle with conjuncture:** Rapid prosperity enriches the governing class in S (Class I) and depletes “gentlemen” R (Class II); depression reverses the mix (§§2310–2311).
- **Spoliation of savers:** Continuous historical nibble on savings by taxes, fiat, monopolies, protection, repudiations — “catastrophes” differ in degree, not kind (§§2312–2317).
- **Political colours:** S change party labels with power; “behind the scenes they join hands, speculators all” (§2313).
- **Protection and strikes:** Manufacturers raise wages and profits together by taking from small stockholders/savers under protection (§2327).
- **War paradox:** S profit from armaments and nationalism but fear war, which elevates courage over “subtle wiles” (§2328).
- **Anti-Semitism as derivation:** Treated as anti-speculator residue-clothing in places (attributed ILLUSTRATION/THEORY — not a wiki-voice claim about Jews).

## Applications

- Mechanism for [[circulation-of-elites]] under modern II-b regimes.
- Undulatory wealth curves (compound-interest impossibility over long spans via recurrent destruction) (§2316).
- Critique of unitary “capitalist” moral types and of progressive tax as S cover for manipulations (§§2326–2327).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Binary typology compresses heterogeneous economic roles.
- Risk of conspiracy-tone readings; Pareto stresses system effects more than single will (§2328).
- Sensitive ethnic illustrations must never be promoted to wiki voice.
