# Party of Order

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|The Eighteenth Brumaire]]* (1852), [[marx-karl|Marx]] uses **Party of Order** both as:

1. **Broad June 1848 coalition** — all classes and parties united against the Paris [[proletariat]] branded “Party of Anarchy,” of Socialism, of Communism; passwords “Property, Family, Religion, Order.”
2. **Parliamentary party of 1849–51** — principally the **Legitimist and Orleanist** coalition ([[class-fraction|class fractions]] of landed property and capital) holding government, army, and legislature after the legislative elections, doing “real business… under a Social, not a Political title… as the bourgeois class against the other classes.”

## Semantic History

Contemporary French political label that Marx theorizes as the political face of united bourgeois class rule under the parliamentary republic. After June, Marx argues, each successive group that challenges the narrowing circle of rulers is itself denounced as enemy of Order — until even Order’s high priests fall and Bonaparte enters as “Savior of Society.” Later historiography retains the name for the conservative majority of the Second Republic.

## Operationalizations

- Ministry of Barrot-Falloux; Changarnier’s military/National Guard command.
- Slogans and branding of opposition as “Socialism.”
- Dissolution under constitutional revision (1851) into original royalist components, then further fragments; loss of independent parliamentary majority; end with the coup: “Thus ended the party of Order, the Legislative Assembly and the February revolution.”

## Applications

- Case material for [[class-fraction]] and [[bonapartism]].
- Rhetoric of “order” vs “anarchy” as class-political weapon.
- Self-disarmament thesis: parliamentary Party of Order strengthens executive and army means that later destroy it.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Name is partly actors’ own and partly Marx’s analytic frame.
- Internal diversity (pure republicans, Bonapartist “mauvaise queue,” Mountain as opposition) means “Party of Order” is not a single organization throughout 1848–51 — periodize carefully.
