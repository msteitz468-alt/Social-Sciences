# Consequences-in-Action

## Definition as Coined

**Consequences-in-action** is Tavory and Timmermans’s (*[[tavory-timmermans-abductive-analysis-2014|Abductive Analysis]]*, 2014) name for the pragmatist-semiotic way of locating **meaning** and building **processual explanations** in qualitative analysis. Meaning is not a static code but the **effects** of sign–object relations on interpreters — effects (actions, thoughts, emotions) that can become signs for further iterations.

They define the direction of spiraling meaning-making — “the actual consequences-in-action that make the world meaningful” — as the joint outcome of the **object’s resistances/directions** and the interpreter’s **habits of thought and action**.

## Semantic History

- **Peirce / pragmatist maxim (1878 “How to Make Our Ideas Clear”):** to clarify a concept, spell out its conceivable practical bearings.
- **Peircean triad:** sign–object–interpretant; interpretant is foremost an **effect** completing meaning-making, not necessarily a cognitive gloss.
- **2014 method book:** named as analytic complement for variation (Ch. 5) and causality (Ch. 6); contrasted with coding to preconceived category definitions (e.g., “racism” as fixed code vs. asking what racialization **does** in subsequent interaction).

Unlike a **hermeneutic** approach that situates acts in symbolic layers, the pragmatic approach (as they state it) locates meaning in effects across chain iterations. Objects resist — not anything goes (realist dimension); interpretants depend on cultivated habits (training shapes what develops).

## Operationalizations

- Map what follows from people conversing, categorizing, diagnosing, refusing, etc.
- Build generalization by comparing similar chains across cases (semiotic connecting tissue).
- **Second-person explanation:** dialogue with actors’ interpretations without automatic acceptance — test by variation and valence in action.
- Extend to “causes behind causes” only when the chain can be extended empirically — not fixed a priori by favorite macro theory ([[extended-case-method]] foil).
- Illustrations in-source: Davis disability normalization strategies; Caplow Christmas gifting under anticipated in-law judgment; newborn-screening clinic sequences; Sewell on Bastille as transformative **through later interpretation**.
