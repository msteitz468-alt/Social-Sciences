# Chain Migration

> Network-mediated migration in which earlier movers create paths and niches for later movers. In [[tilly-charles|Tilly]]’s inequality program (*[[tilly-durable-inequality-1998|Durable Inequality]]* 1998; *[[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005|Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties]]* Ch. 10), chain migration is a prime site of **[[opportunity-hoarding]]**.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Tilly 2005 Ch. 10.)* Chain migration involves continuing contacts between origin and destination; migration is often circular in origin; later movers use information, credit, housing, and job access controlled by categorically bounded networks. Those networks can monopolize a value-producing resource (a trade niche, a hiring channel) without necessarily full [[exploitation]] of outsiders as labor for surplus extraction.

## Semantic History

Standard in migration sociology (chain/network migration) before Tilly; he embeds it in the four-mechanism durable-inequality schema (exploitation, opportunity hoarding, emulation, adaptation).

## Operationalizations

| Case (Tilly) | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Roccasecca–Mamaroneck Italians | Landscape gardening near-monopoly; little non-Italian labor |
| Same village → Lyon factories | Employment without the same ethnic monopoly |
| Johnstown East European Jews vs Gentiles | Complementary niches under capital dominance |
| Juárez maquiladoras | Employee-referral hiring as firm-internal hoarding |
| Professions | Licensing as state-backed hoarding |

## Applications

- Immigrant entrepreneurship and ethnic niches (Waldinger/Portes via Tilly)  
- Boundary activation among migrants ([[social-boundary]], festive identity)  
- Policy: intervene in destructive hoarding without “stamping out” all network migration (Tilly’s POSITION)

## Critiques and Limitations

- Natural-experiment comparisons (Mamaroneck vs Lyon) are illustrative, not randomized.  
- Normative ambiguity: trust networks vs exclusion.  
- Gendered and origin-country dynamics thinner in Tilly’s sketches than in specialist migration ethnography.
