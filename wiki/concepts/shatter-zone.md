# Shatter Zone

## Definition as Coined

In *[[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-2009|The Art of Not Being Governed]]*, [[scott-james-c|Scott]] treats upland mainland Southeast Asia ([[zomia]]) as a classic **shatter zone** and **region of refuge**: successive waves of state expansion, warfare, slavery, and tax pressure fragment and recombine populations in high-friction terrain, producing ethnic and political diversity as a byproduct of flight and evasion (attributed — Scott 2009). Zomia is a **state effect** — inconceivable except as the outside of state-making — not an archaic residue.

## Semantic History

Older geopolitical use for fragmented frontier belts; Scott’s densification ties shatter zones to maroon/runaway communities and to comparative refugia (Appalachia, etc.). Conclusion: upland societies as **reactive product** of valley state-making over the longue durée.

## Operationalizations

Friction of terrain; episodic state reach; multi-ethnic mosaics; mobility; oral and acephalous forms; secondary primitivism (flight *from* civilization as adaptive).

## Applications

Zomia program; comparative hill–valley frontiers (China southwest Han expansion as major peopling driver — Scott).

## Critiques and Limitations

May over-unify diverse highland histories; “shatter” is interpretive metaphor.
