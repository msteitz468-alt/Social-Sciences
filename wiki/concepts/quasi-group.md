# Quasi-Group

## Definition as Coined

For [[dahrendorf-ralf|Dahrendorf]] (*[[dahrendorf-class-class-conflict-1959|Class and Class Conflict]]*, Ch. V), a **quasi-group** is the aggregate of persons who share the same latent interests by virtue of their positions in an [[imperatively-coordinated-association|imperatively coordinated association]] — a recruiting field for conflict groups, not yet itself an organized group. An **interest group** is the organized formation that articulates those interests as manifest goals. Classes, in his theory, are a type of conflict group (interest group) emerging from such quasi-groups.

## Semantic History

Dahrendorf draws on prior usage (including Ginsberg) of quasi-group for loose aggregates, and specializes it to authority-based latent interests. The pair quasi-group / interest group is his operational bridge between structural positions and organized class conflict.

## Operationalizations

Identify position aggregates with shared latent authority interests; observe conditions under which they organize (leadership, communication, freedom of coalition, etc. — as specified in the theory's empirical conditions of group formation).

## Applications

Unions, parties, and employer associations are treated as interest groups recruited from authority-defined quasi-groups in enterprise and state.

## Critiques and Limitations

The same quasi-group may recruit multiple, competing interest groups (e.g., rival unions or parties); identity of quasi-group and interest group is a limiting case, not the norm.
