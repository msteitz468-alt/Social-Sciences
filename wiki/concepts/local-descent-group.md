# Local Descent Group

> Analytic unit in [[leach-edmund|Leach]]’s “Structural Implications of Matrilateral Cross-Cousin Marriage” (1951; *[[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961|Rethinking Anthropology]]* Ch. 3). When a clan or large lineage ceases to be localized, it generally ceases to be the **corporate unit that arranges marriage**; that unit is a set of males who are co-members of the lineage/clan **and** co-residents.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Leach 1951/61.)* In a unilaterally defined descent system:

> “Where a clan or large scale lineage, ceases, for one reason or another, to be a localized group, then, in general, it ceases to be a corporate unit for the purposes of arranging a marriage. The corporate group which does arrange a marriage is, in such circumstances, always a group of males who, besides being members of the same lineage or clan, share a common place of residence.”

**Local descent group** = that residence-sharing corporate marriage-arranging unit. It is the node in diagrams of **Kariera-type**, **Trobriand-type**, and **Kachin-type** marriage systems (restricted vs asymmetrical matrilateral exchange).

## Semantic History

- **1951 Curl Prize Essay:** introduced as a clarifying unit against loose talk of “clans” exchanging women when the practical exchangers are local.
- **Contrast with Lévi-Strauss:** Leach criticizes analyses that treat abstract descent lines or misidentified ethnic units (e.g. LS’s Haka Chin material misread as Kachin) as if they were the corporate parties to marriage.
- **Relation to *mayu–dama*:** Jinghpaw wife-giver/receiver categories operate between local descent groups in the ideal model; political rank and bridewealth map onto those inter-group ties ([[mayu-dama]], [[marriage-payments]]).
- **Not identical to “village”** or to the maximal lineage — localization + descent + corporate marriage function must coincide.

## Operationalizations

| Question | Local descent group answer (Leach) |
|---|---|
| Who gives/receives wives in Kachin-type systems? | Local descent groups in directional *mayu→dama* chains, not “the clan” as a whole if non-local |
| What does “MBD marriage” prescribe? | Category marriage between groups in fixed wife-giving direction (Needham: prescription, not mere preference — as Leach notes in 1961 Intro) |
| Comparative types | Kariera (symmetrical CCM / restricted); Trobriand-type; Kachin-type (asymmetrical matrilateral; no direct exchange of women between two groups) |

## Applications

- Core of Ch. 3’s mathematical “function” of marriage rules: infer political and economic implications (rank of wife-givers vs receivers; consumer-goods brideprice vs dowry direction).
- Comparative extensions: Batak *hulahula–anak boru*; Lovedu reverse hierarchy; Chinese material (Freedman later: thin evidence for systematic asymmetric local-group intermarriage — preference not prescription).
- Ties Jinghpaw [[ethnographic-algebra]] (ideal *gu/nam* marriage) to highland political structure (*gumsa* hierarchy).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Homans & Schneider (1955)** dismissed Leach’s material as exceptional; linked matrilateral CCM statistically to paternal authority — Leach unpersuaded.
- **Freedman (1958)** on Chinese cases: no evidence of systematic asymmetric local-descent-group wife-giving cycles.
- **OCR/table gaps** in some digital copies of Ch. 3 (Table III prestations) — prefer print for full prestation inventories.
- Construct is **etic and mid-century British**; do not equate with indigenous corporate labels without argument.
