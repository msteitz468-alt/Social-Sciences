# Anticipatory Socialization

## Definition as Coined

In [[merton-robert-k|Merton]]'s reference-group theory (*[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]* Chs. X–XI), **anticipatory socialization** is the process of taking on the values and orientations of a **group one does not yet belong to** but **aspires to join**, thereby easing transition and acceptance if mobility occurs — and producing strain or marginality if it does not.

## Semantic History

From military and occupational mobility analysis into general socialization and mobility research. Remains standard vocabulary.

## Operationalizations

Attitude change toward destination-group norms before status change; survey indicators of aspiration and identification.

## Applications

Military promotion; class mobility; professional training; immigrant incorporation analogies (careful: analogy, not identity).

## Critiques and Limitations

Assumes clear destination groups; underplays blocked mobility and oppositional identity formation.
