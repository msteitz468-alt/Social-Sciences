# Entity Processes

## Definition as Coined

[[abbott-andrew|Abbott]] (attributed; “What Do Cases Do?” in Ragin & Becker 1992; *[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]* ch. 4) proposes **entity process** links as a vocabulary for narratives about social units that are not fixed:

| Process | Content (Abbott, attributed) |
|---|---|
| Birth / death | Familiar from demography; for emergents (occupations, pressure groups) hard to conceptualize and measure |
| Merger / division | Easy to label, hard to model; central narrative events for organizations and jurisdictions |
| Microtransformation | Change at member level with or without macro identity change — turnover, internal metamorphosis, microstructural change |
| Macrotransformation | Change of emergent properties without concurrent micro change; or change that preserves relative structural place while transforming absolute character (e.g. “profession” over a century) |

These sit alongside other generic narrative links Abbott names (institutionalization and legitimation links after Berger & Luckmann; paradigm/style shifts; collective-action links).

## Semantic History

Part of relaxing GLR assumption 1 (fixed entities). The epilogue frames a generalized demography of organizations and social-level entities as one of four formal alternatives to standard methods; ch. 9 ([[things-of-boundaries]]) pushes further by deriving entities from boundaries rather than starting from pre-given units.

## Operationalizations

Historical and comparative case narratives; sequence alphabets that include entity-process states; network and vacancy models for constrained trajectories. Not a single statistical package procedure.

## Applications

Occupations and professions; social-work jurisdiction formation; organizational demography more broadly (programmatic, attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

Measurement of birth/death/merger for emergents remains underdeveloped (Abbott's own assessment, attributed). Easy labels can smuggle in essence-talk about entities that ch. 9 denies.
