# Polycentricity

*(Attributed — Vincent Ostrom et al.; applied by [[ostrom-elinor|Elinor Ostrom]] in *[[ostrom-governing-the-commons-1990|Governing the Commons]]*.)* Governance through **many centers** of semi-autonomous decision-making rather than a single Leviathan or pure market of private rights.

## Definition as Coined

In the California groundwater cases (Ch. 4), Ostrom describes emergence of a **polycentric public-enterprise** system — watermaster, districts, courts, state agencies — that halted pumping races through negotiated, multi-level institutions.

## Semantic History

Metropolitan reform debate (1960s) → Workshop tradition → CPR institutional change and nested enterprises (design principle).

## Operationalizations

Mapping overlapping limited-purpose governments and contractual arrangements; performance vs monocentric alternatives.

## Applications

Urban service delivery; groundwater; climate and multi-level environmental governance (later literature).

## Critiques and Limitations

Coordination costs; elite capture of fragments; not a substitute for all hierarchical functions.
