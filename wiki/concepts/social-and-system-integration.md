# Social and System Integration

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*):

- **Social integration:** reciprocity of practices between actors in circumstances of **co-presence**, understood as continuities in and disjunctions of encounters.
- **System integration:** reciprocity between actors or collectivities across **extended time-space**, outside conditions of co-presence.

Together they replace the micro/macro dualism.

## Semantic History

Giddens rejects "micro-" vs "macro-sociology" as a phoney war and as an unhappy division of labour (free agent vs structural constraint). Critiques Collins's microtranslation program. Earlier Durkheimian [[social-integration]] (intensity of collective life; *Suicide*) is a **different** concept — record affinity of problem, not identity of solution. [[forms-of-integration]] (other lineages) likewise distinct.

## Operationalizations

Trace modes of [[regionalization]] and time-space paths linking co-present encounters to remote institutional relations; compare tribal segmental fusion, class-divided city–countryside stretch, modern commodified time-space.

## Applications

- Articulates Goffmanian encounters with large-scale institutional analysis without dualism.
- Frames [[time-space-distanciation]] and intersocietal systems.

## Critiques and Limitations

Terminology overlaps with Lockwood's social/system integration (different apparatus) — flag when secondary literature enters; do not silently merge.
