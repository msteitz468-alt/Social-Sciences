# State Capitalism

## Definition as Coined

In Lenin's usage the concept has two linked lives, both documented from ingested primary sources and stated here in attributed voice.

**1917 — state-monopoly capitalism as threshold.** In *The Impending Catastrophe and How to Combat It* (Sept 1917) Lenin argued the war had transformed monopoly capitalism into "state-monopoly capitalism," and that this constituted "a complete material preparation for socialism, the threshold of socialism, a rung on the ladder of history between which and the rung called socialism there are no intermediate rungs." What German social-chauvinists called "war-time socialism" was, he argued, "war-time state-monopoly capitalism... penal servitude for the workers and protection for capitalist profits"; the same machinery directed by a revolutionary-democratic state would be "a step, and more than one step, towards socialism" ([[lenin-impending-catastrophe-1917]]).

**1921 — state capitalism as NEP strategy.** In *The Tax in Kind* (April 1921) Lenin restated his 1918 five-structure ladder of the Russian economy — patriarchal economy, small commodity production, private capitalism, state capitalism, socialism — arguing all five coexisted in Russia and that "state capitalism is nothing to fear in Russia; it would be a step forward," since the immediate enemy of socialist construction in a peasant country was petty-bourgeois anarchy of exchange, not organized capitalism. Concessions to foreign capital were defined as a form of state capitalism — "an economic alliance, a bloc, a contract with advanced finance capital" ([[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]).

## Semantic History

The 1917 usage names a stage capitalism itself reached under war conditions; the 1921 usage names a *policy instrument* a proletarian state deliberately employs. Lenin bridged the two with the claim that the class character of the state determines the meaning of the same economic machinery. Later usage diverged sharply: dissident-left and academic traditions applied "state capitalism" to the Soviet system itself — a reversal of Lenin's polemical intent that is not yet documented from ingested sources (as of 2026-07-16; Trotsky's rejection of the "state capitalism" label for the USSR is noted via [[service-trotsky-2009]] on [[russian-question-debate]]).

## Operationalizations

Lenin's 1917 markers: compulsory syndication, bank amalgamation under state control, abolition of commercial secrecy, rationing — measures he argued Germany already practiced "reactionary-bureaucratically" and a Soviet state would practice "revolutionary-democratically." The 1921 markers: concessions, leases, state-regulated exchange with the peasantry.

## Applications

The concept underwrites Lenin's argument in "Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?" (Oct 1917) that capitalism's own accounting apparatus — banks, syndicates, the grain monopoly, universal labour conscription — should be "wrested from the capitalists" rather than smashed, a single State Bank being "nine-tenths of the socialist apparatus" ([[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]). In 1921 it anchors the NEP retreat: the tax in kind and concessions as "two crutches" ([[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]; see [[new-economic-policy]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Within the ingested material, Lenin's own Tenth Congress self-criticism concedes the 1918–20 over-nationalization was "a patent mistake," and Baku Communists resisted the concessions policy. The deeper limitation is definitional: the concept's meaning flips with the analyst's judgment of the state's class character, which is precisely what opponents (Mensheviks, later left communists) disputed — making most assertions deploying it attributed positions rather than findings.

## Carr 1979 rejects the label for Soviet planning

In the closing perspective chapter of *[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979]]* (Ch. 19), [[carr-edward-hallett|Carr]] addresses critics who describe Soviet planned economy as "state capitalism." He finds the view **untenable**: "Capitalism without entrepreneurs, without unemployment and without a free market, where no class appropriates the surplus value produced by the worker, and profits play a purely ancillary role, where prices and wages are not subject to the law of supply and demand, is no longer capitalism in any meaningful sense." He calls Soviet planning "the material, economic half" of socialism and a major outcome of the revolution — while denying it realized Marx's free association of producers or proletarian self-emancipation. Record as **Carr's position**, not wiki adjudication; contrast [[russian-question-debate]] and Lenin's own earlier "state capitalism" transitional vocabulary ([[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]], [[new-economic-policy]]).
