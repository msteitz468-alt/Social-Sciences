# Witchcraft

## Definition as Coined

In *[[evans-pritchard-witchcraft-oracles-magic-1937|Witchcraft, Oracles and Magic among the Azande]]* (1937), [[evans-pritchard-e-e|Evans-Pritchard]] reported the Zande distinction: a **witch** injures others by an **inherent quality** — **no rite, no spell, no medicines**; the act is **psychic**. **Sorcerers** injure by **magic rites with bad medicines**. Zande *mangu* names the substance and the phenomenon; a witch is *boro mangu*. (Attributed: E-P's ethnographic definition as anthropology's type-case — not a universal ontology.)

Evans-Pritchard also stated (attributed) that witches as Azande conceive them "clearly cannot exist," yet the concept supplies a **natural philosophy** of relations between men and unfortunate events, a stereotyped means of reacting, and a system of values regulating conduct.

## Semantic History

European "witchcraft" carried diabolic and early-modern legal meanings. Africanist anthropology after E-P often reserved **witchcraft** for putative **innate/psychic** harm and **sorcery** for **learned ritual** harm — a contrast Zande usage (as E-P rendered it) underwrote. Later writers sometimes collapse the pair or treat "witchcraft" as any occult-harm idiom. Always date and attribute the operationalization.

## Operationalizations

**Azande (E-P, late 1920s), attributed:**

| Feature | Report |
|---|---|
| Substance | Oval blackish swelling/bag near liver/xiphoid; may contain plant seeds; E-P suggested small intestine in some states |
| Inheritance | Sex-linked unilinear: male witch → sons; female witch → daughters; may stay "cool" |
| Diagnosis living | Oracles ask situational questions, not "is X a witch forever?" |
| Diagnosis dead | Public autopsy of belly/intestines |
| Action | *Mbisimo mangu* (soul of witchcraft); nocturnal light; steals *mbisimo pasio*; limited range |
| Pace | Slow wasting typical; sudden acute illness may be sorcery |
| Objects | *Ahu mangu* shot into bodies; witch-doctor leeches extract |
| Social incidence | Commoner both sexes; Avongara fictionally immune; powerful commoners largely immune; children minor only |
| Agency | Others morally guilty; self often unaware; public cooling by blowing water on fowl's wing |

**Contrast class:** sorcery = technique + bad medicines. **Not:** denial of natural cause — see [[second-spear]].

### Gluckman densification (*Custom and Conflict*, 1955/56)

In Lecture IV, [[gluckman-max|Gluckman]] restated Evans-Pritchard’s intellectual logic (why this person’s misfortune; moral theory of the witch; oracles; secondary elaboration) and pushed a **sociological** thesis: accusations arise only in *certain* relationships, enabling fission and readjustment when contradictory principles clash (e.g. Zulu co-wives as mystical scapegoats for agnatic competition). He analogized modern “witch-hunts” that blame categories of persons for structural ills. Study: [[gluckman-custom-and-conflict-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Colonial ethnographic construction; abridgement and single-observer limits; exporting the Azande witch/sorcerer split as universal taxonomy; philosophical over-reading in the rationality debate. Emic *mangu* ≠ European witch stereotype. Gender access limited E-P's account of women's experience.
