# Differential Historical Time

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* Part Four (outline of a concept of historical time; “Marxism Is Not a Historicism”), [[althusser-louis|Althusser]] argues that the Marxist whole — a complex structured unity of relatively autonomous levels fixed in the last instance by the economy — is **incompatible with Hegelian historical time**. Hegelian time, as he reconstructs it, has two essential characteristics: (1) **homogeneous continuity** (a continuum for periodizing succession of dialectical totalities); (2) **contemporaneity / historical present** enabling an **“essential section”** (*coupe d’essence*) in which all elements coexist in one present immediately expressing the essence. Against this, each level of the Marxist whole (productive forces, relations of production, political superstructure, philosophy, sciences, aesthetics, etc.) has a **peculiar, relatively autonomous time and history**, differentially based on its type of articulation and dependence in the whole. **For each mode of production there is a peculiar time and history**; capitalist economic time is a complex non-linear “time of times,” constructed in concept from structures of production — “an invisible time, essentially illegible” as clock or life time.

## Semantic History

Brewster’s glossary entry **TIME** codifies: no simple unilinear time in which the social formation unfolds; each level and element has a different temporality; the totality is articulation of **dislocations** between temporalities; synchrony is the concept of structure (including static and dynamic/tendential elements), not co-presence of essence; diachrony properly applies only to the concept of the phase of [[dislocation-decalage|transition]]. Althusser treats the then-widespread **synchrony/diachrony** distinction as inheriting Hegelian ideological time when synchrony = essential section and diachrony = contingent succession in a homogeneous continuum. Distinct from Stocking’s disciplinary-historiography pair [[presentism-and-historicism]] (same word family, different object). Anti-historicism of Marxism is paired with [[theoretical-anti-humanism]] as double negative of the same ideological problematic.

## Operationalizations

- **No essential section** of the social formation: you cannot cut a single present in which all instances immediately express one essence.
- **Economic time of capital** produced from structures of production, circulation, turnover — not calendar chronology of events.
- **Balibar:** reproduction installs a fictive contemporaneity / system of synchronic dependencies (*Gleichzeitigkeit*); transition is a structured mode with special dislocation, not a gap in continuous time.
- **Political stake:** Hegelian present as absolute horizon yields “no Hegelian politics strictly speaking”; differential times open knowledge of non-contemporaneous levels.

## Applications

- Critique of radical historicism (Marx as Ricardo historicized / Hegelianized).
- Reading of *Capital*’s “ideal average” and England as illustration, not historical object.
- Anthropological and archaeological uptake of non-synchronous levels (relative autonomy of kinship, politics, ideology).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of freezing structure as static if differential times are not also thought with tendency and transition (Balibar’s chapters address this).
- Historicist Marxists (Gramscian absolute historicism in Althusser’s polemical target) reverse the charge: differential time severs theory from lived history.
- Empirical history still needs dating methods; the concept is epistemological for historical materialism, not a substitute for chronology.
