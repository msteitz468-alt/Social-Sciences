# Dramaturgical Action

## Definition as Coined

**Dramaturgical action** is [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]'s (attributed, *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]*) action concept, drawn from Goffman, for actors who **present a view of themselves** before a public — stylizing the expression of their own experiences so as to control the audience's access to the actor's subjective world. The corresponding validity aspect is **truthfulness/sincerity**; the world-relation is the **subjective (internal) world**.

## Semantic History

Goffman's dramaturgical analysis of self-presentation → Habermas's purified "dramaturgical model of action" as one of four sociological concepts of action, later aufgehoben in multi-claim [[communicative-action]]. Distinct from the wiki's broader [[dramaturgical-analysis]] theory page if present as a separate Goffman program.

## Operationalizations

Impression management; sincerity conditions of expressive speech acts; consistency of self-presentation over time (Habermas's preferred redemption path for truthfulness claims).

## Applications

Completes the three-world map (objective / social / subjective) in TCA's action typology.

## Critiques and Limitations

As a stand-alone model it risks reducing social interaction to performance without shared normative or cognitive validity; Habermas keeps it as a moment, not the whole.
