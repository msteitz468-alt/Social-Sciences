# Things of Boundaries

## Definition as Coined

[[abbott-andrew|Abbott]]'s thesis (attributed; “Things of Boundaries,” *Social Research* 62 [1995]: 857–82; *[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]* ch. 9):

> We should not look for boundaries of things, but for **things of boundaries**.

Social entities often come into existence when actors **tie** social boundaries together; boundaries and “sites of difference” are primary, durable “things” secondary. Related apparatus (attributed):

- **Sites of difference** — raw materials of distinction
- **Protoboundaries** — early/partial boundary formations
- **Yoking** — linking boundaries into an entity
- **Thingness** — endurance plus causal authority (what makes an entity solid)

Challenges the [[general-linear-reality|GLR]] starting point of fixed entities with variable properties; connects to a generalized demography of social entities ([[entity-processes]]).

## Semantic History

Adjacent to but not identical with Gieryn/Lamont [[boundary-work]] (rhetorical/evaluative demarcation of kinds of people or science/non-science). Abbott's focus is ontological: how occupational and professional entities form historically (social work 1870–1920; British medicine illustrations, attributed). Relates to network and structuralist images of interdependence ([[harrison-white|White]]) and to Abbott's professions work (*System of Professions*).

**Barth parallel (ethnicity):** [[barth-fredrik|Barth]]’s 1969 thesis that the ethnic unit is defined by the [[ethnic-boundary]] rather than the cultural stuff it encloses (*[[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-1969|Ethnic Groups and Boundaries]]*) is an earlier, ethnicity-specific version of boundaries-first analysis. Abbott generalizes the ontological priority of boundaries to professions and social entities broadly; do not equate the programs — Barth’s concern is ascription and polyethnic organization ([[ethnic-boundary-theory]]), Abbott’s is entity formation over time.

## Operationalizations

Historical case analyses of professional jurisdictions and protoboundaries rather than a single metric. Multi-dimensional strength of entities; types of occupational entity formation.

## Applications

Professions; social-work boundaries; any analysis tempted to treat occupations or organizations as natural kinds with essences. Epilogue links ch. 9 to the demographic alternative program (birth, death, amalgamation, division of social-level units).

## Critiques and Limitations

Not a claim that *no* entity has internal coherence or that all entities are only boundaries; Abbott presents boundaries-first as often the right starting point for temporal accounts of origins. Related theories of category construction (e.g. prototype approaches) are noted as conversation partners (attributed). Distinction from [[boundary-work]] should be kept clear in linking.
