# Double Hermeneutic

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: the intersection of **two frames of meaning** as a logically necessary part of social science — the meaningful social world as constituted by lay actors and the metalanguages invented by social scientists; constant "slippage" from one to the other in the practice of the social sciences (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Present from *New Rules of Sociological Method* (1976). Distinguishes social from natural science: concepts can become first-order when appropriated in social life (e.g. "capitalism," "unemployment," psychiatric categories).

## Operationalizations

Methodological awareness rather than metric; track concept migration between academy and lay discourse.

## Applications

- Practical connotations of social science and critique (*Constitution* Ch. 6).
- Mutual knowledge vs common sense; internal vs external critique.

## Critiques and Limitations

Some realists and positivists deny or minimize slippage; some relativists over-extend it to dissolve validity criteria (Giddens retains validity/credibility criteria).
