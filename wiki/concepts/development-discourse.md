# Development discourse

In [[escobar-arturo|Escobar]]'s usage (1995, attributed), **development discourse** is the historically produced [[discourse-foucauldian|discourse]] through which the industrialized West and postcolonial states imagined and remade Asia, Africa, and Latin America as "underdeveloped" after World War II. It is not merely talk about development policy but an encompassing cultural space that defines problems, authorizes experts, and organizes intervention.

## Definition as Coined

Escobar treats development analogously to Said's [[orientalism]]: a corporate institution for dealing with the Third World by making statements about it, authorizing views, teaching it, and restructuring it. The discourse has (attributed) three interlocking axes: **forms of knowledge** (concepts, theories, disciplines), a **system of power** that regulates practice, and **forms of subjectivity** fostered by the discourse (including "underdeveloped" identities).

## Semantic History

Before the critical turn, "development" named a self-evident goal (growth, modernization, later basic needs). From the late 1970s–80s, Foucauldian and postcolonial tools made it possible to analyze development as a colonizer of reality. [[ferguson-james|Ferguson]]'s *[[ferguson-anti-politics-machine-1990|The Anti-Politics Machine]]* (1990) analyzes development as a **conceptual apparatus** that constitutes Lesotho as an LDC object (four features: aboriginal economy, peasant society, national economy, governmentality) and organizes projects with depoliticizing [[instrument-effects]] — see [[anti-politics-machine]]. [[escobar-arturo|Escobar]]'s *[[escobar-encountering-development-1995|Encountering Development]]* (1995) offers a principal **global** statement of development as [[regime-of-representation]]; the Sachs circle uses related framings. "Developmentalism" sometimes labels the ideology; Escobar prefers discourse/regime analysis; Ferguson prefers apparatus/effects vivisection.

## Operationalizations

- Historical genealogy of problematizations (poverty, hunger, growth, population).
- Institutional analysis of planning, missions, and expert reports (World Bank, UN, national plans).
- Mapping of client categories (peasants, women, environment) as products of visibility.

## Applications

Organizes [[post-development]], the [[anthropology-of-modernity]], and critique of reformist "alternative development." Ferguson's Lesotho case ([[ferguson-anti-politics-machine-study]]) is the vault's primary ethnographic operationalization; Escobar's study is the global discourse charter. Used across critical development studies and political ecology.

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics argue discourse analysis underplays material scarcity and state capacity (see [[post-development-debate]]). The concept is contested and must stay in attributed voice when tied to Escobar's stronger claims (e.g., that development *produced* underdevelopment as outcome).
