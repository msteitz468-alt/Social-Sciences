# Utopian Realism

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (1990): even aboard the [[juggernaut-of-modernity|juggernaut]], one should not abandon attempts to steer. History supplies **no teleology and no guarantees**, but the heavily counterfactual nature of future-oriented thought — essential to modernity’s reflexivity — has a positive side: **the propagation of models of alternative futures might help them be realised**. This stance he called **utopian realism**.

Requirements he sketched for a critical theory without guarantees include sociological sensitivity to modernity’s institutional dynamics; awareness of connections between the local and the global; linkage of **emancipatory politics** (freedom from inequality/oppression) with **life politics** (self-actualisation, how we should live given abstract systems and global risks); and refusal of a rigid realism/utopia split while keeping a principle of immanence (transformative potentials within existing institutions).

## Semantic History

Positioned against both Marxist privileged revolutionary agent / master-slave dialectic simplifications and quietist acceptance of the juggernaut. Social movements (labour, free speech/democratic, peace, ecological — mapped loosely to institutional dimensions) *glimpse* futures but are not sole agents of utopian realism.

## Operationalizations

Normative-theoretical orientation, not a variable. Dimensions: local/global × emancipatory/life politics.

## Applications

- Political horizon of *Consequences* after the risk/juggernaut analysis.
- Contours of a possible post-modern *order* (post-scarcity, polyarchy beyond the nation-state, world without war, humanisation of technology/planetary care) offered as utopian-realist sketches, **not** as predictions or as a claim that we already live in postmodernity.

## Critiques and Limitations

May understate structural constraints and overstate reflexivity’s steering capacity; "Third Way" political reception of later Giddens is a separate debate from the 1990 theoretical sketch. Record as attributed program, not validated policy science.
