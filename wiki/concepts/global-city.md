# Global City

Analytical construct naming cities that function as strategic production sites for the specialized services and financial instruments through which economic globalization is coordinated and controlled. Associated primarily with [[sassen-saskia|Saskia Sassen]] and [[global-city-theory]].

## Definition as Coined

Sassen (from 1984; full statement 1991/2001) uses **global city** to designate cities deeply articulated with the global economy as sites that *produce* the capability for global control — advanced [[producer-services|producer services]], finance, management operations — rather than merely as large or historically important cities.

## Semantic History

- **World city** (Goethe; relaunched Hall 1966; Friedmann and Wolff 1982; Braudel *supervilles*): multi-century type of major international city. Sassen deliberately did *not* choose this term, because it lacks specificity to contemporary globalization.
- **Global city** (Sassen 1984→): names the contemporary structuring of the global in urban form. Most major global cities are also world cities; some global cities may not be classical world cities.
- Related neighbors: Castells's informational city; global-city region constructs — related architecture, not identical.

## Operationalizations

Producer-service and FIRE employment shares and concentration; cross-border financial and service transactions; foreign affiliates and advanced legal/accounting networks; property markets; earnings polarization indicators. Contested: headquarters counts alone (Sassen argues against reducing the concept to HQ tallies).

## Applications

New York, London, Tokyo as primary cases in *[[sassen-global-city-2001|The Global City]]*; extended by others to many cities worldwide (second-edition research literature).

## Critiques and Limitations

Over-extension of the label; three-city origin bias; debate over polarization as necessary effect; relationship to secondary cities and national systems. See [[sassen-global-city-study]] and [[hubs/theory/global-city-theory]].
