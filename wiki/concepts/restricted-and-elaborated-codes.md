# Restricted and Elaborated Codes

Concepts of [[bernstein-basil|Basil Bernstein]], adapted by [[douglas-mary|Mary Douglas]] in *[[douglas-natural-symbols-1970|Natural Symbols]]* as the analogue for ritual coding. Douglas's program: [[grid-group-analysis]]. Bernstein's own 1972 sociolinguistic primary statement: below. Contested reception: [[deficit-difference-debate]].

## Definition as Coined

Bernstein distinguished **restricted** and **elaborated** speech codes tied to family control systems (positional vs personal) and class/education. Restricted codes condense meaning through shared context and enforce group norms in interaction; elaborated codes require explicit verbalization and support individualized, abstract control. Douglas treated **ritual as a restricted code** and anti-ritual / inner-experience religion as aligned with elaborated-code pressures — a long-run prediction that medium and control system match.

### Bernstein's own 1972 primary statement

In "A Sociolinguistic Approach to Socialization; with Some Reference to Educability"
(in Gumperz & Hymes, eds., *Directions in Sociolinguistics*, 1972), Bernstein gives
the primary formulation: an **elaborated code** is one where "it is difficult to
predict across a representative range the syntactic options or alternatives"
(flexibly organized, wide vocabulary differentiation); a **restricted code** is one
where "it is much less difficult to predict... the syntactic alternatives"
(narrow-range, rigid organization). Codes are explicitly **not** a matter of
vocabulary/jargon and are explicitly **not evaluative** — each is functionally
fitted to its social matrix.

Codes are tied to two **family types**: **positional families** (decision-making
allocated by formal status — age, sex, generational rank; role-segregated; "closed"
communication systems) and **person-oriented families** (decisions on individual
psychological qualities; "open" communication, greater role discretion). Associated
modes of social control: **imperative** (restricted code, e.g. "Shut up"),
**positional appeal** (refers to status/subcultural norms, tends to produce shame),
and **personal appeal** (addresses individual motives, tends to produce guilt).
Bernstein invokes but explicitly distances the codes from Chomsky's
competence/performance distinction ("codes refer to performance not to
competence") and links restricted codes to Durkheimian **mechanical solidarity**
("we" over "I") and elaborated codes to **organic solidarity** ("I" over "we").

Bernstein links class to family type only as a hedged broad tendency ("broadly
speaking... we could expect"), not an identity claim, and explicitly denies that
restricted code implies linguistic or cognitive **deficit**: "it is orientation and
use of communicative modes that is in question, not fundamental capacity for
logical analysis and conceptual thought," citing Labov (1969a) approvingly. His
mechanism for class differences in school "educability" is cultural/institutional
mismatch between home and school codes, not innate capacity — see
[[deficit-difference-debate]] for the difference/interactional counter-positions
(Labov; Gumperz/Garfinkel on indexicality) and the debate over whether the popular
"deficit" reading ever fairly represented this position.

## Semantic History

- Bernstein 1960s–71: speech and later curriculum boundaries (*Class, Codes and Control*).
- Bernstein 1972: primary sociolinguistic statement in *Directions in Sociolinguistics* (Gumperz & Hymes, eds.), with explicit anti-deficit disclaimer.
- Douglas 1970/73: "the other side" of Bernstein's thesis — cosmology and ritual forms under social control; grid/group adapted from family-control and curriculum diagrams.
- Education debates later politicized "deficit" readings of restricted code — Bernstein's and Douglas's uses must stay attributed and not collapsed into popular deficit discourse; see [[deficit-difference-debate]] for the primary-source record of this contest, including Labov's 1969 anti-deficit challenge.

## Operationalizations

In Douglas: strength of ritual form ≈ restricted coding; contempt of ritual and demand for verbalized sincerity ≈ elaborated coding. Not a psychometric scale in *Natural Symbols* — comparative typology.

## Applications

Family control diagrams; Bog Irish vs reform Catholicism; academic and professional "ritual" of footnotes and pre-coded scholarly interaction (Douglas's own example); religious anti-ritualism of the 1960s.

## Critiques and Limitations

Speech-code theory is heavily contested in sociology of education; Douglas admits her adaptation is a "crude and limping parody" of Bernstein's subtlety; analogy ritual≈speech can over-extend.
