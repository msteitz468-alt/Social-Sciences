# Variability selection

**Richard Potts**'s hypothesis that hominin evolution was shaped by selection for **versatility under environmental variability** (wide amplitude, unpredictable climate/habitat change), not only adaptation to a fixed savannah or other single niche (attributed via [[gamble-clive|Gamble]] 2013 ch. 2 citations of Potts). Gamble uses it as a climate-evolution driver package for Terrae 0–2 alongside Milankovitch cyclicity and the tectonic trail.

## Definition as Coined

Variability selection favors traits that maintain function across shifting conditions — a "variability package" rather than narrow specialization to one stable habitat (attributed, Potts as summarized by Gamble 2013 ch. 2). Gamble pairs it with Milankovitch cyclicity (23/41/100/400 ka orbital bands; Mid-Pleistocene Revolution toward 100 ka cycles), deMenocal/Trauth-style African climate packets, and the **tectonic trail** (King–Bailey; East African rain shadow; Wall of Africa) as the environmental frame for Terrae 0–2 (attributed).

Box 2.2 / Grove simulations: high-variability regimes favor versatile alleles and strategies (attributed via Gamble). Dispersal models (dispersal vs displacement vs diaspora; bio-tidal refugia; Foley radiations) sit beside variability selection as organizing geography (attributed).

## Semantic History

Developed in palaeoanthropology / Quaternary science debates of the 1990s–2000s as an alternative to simple cooling-or-drying-drove-bipedalism narratives. Gamble 2013 is an archaeological-settlement application, not the primary Potts monograph.

## Operationalizations

Correlation of hominin first appearances and dispersals with intervals of high climate variability (pulses cited around 2.5 Ma, 1.8 Ma, etc. — as of sources Gamble cites). Bio-tidal shelf exposure/inundation as a spatial expression of the same variability.

## Applications

*Settling the Earth* chs 2 and 4–5 frame Terra 0–2 adaptive challenges and the three strides of Terra 2 through variability + tectonics + social brain, not progress teleology (attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

Competing habitat-specific models remain; correlation of variability peaks with speciation/dispersal is contested; absolute chronologies move. Always attribute and date-stamp (as of Gamble 2013 / cited papers).
