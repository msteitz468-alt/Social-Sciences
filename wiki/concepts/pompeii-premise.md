# Pompeii Premise

Name for treating archaeological deposits as **frozen moments of ongoing life** — complete systemic inventories interrupted in place — so spatial pattern maps directly onto past behavior. Anchors the Binford–Schiffer dispute over how the [[archaeological-record]] relates to living systems.

## Definition as Coined

[[binford-lewis|Binford]] used the Pompeii image to attack inferences (and, he argued, philosophical assumptions) that treat the record as a distorted copy of a once-living systemic arrangement. [[schiffer-michael-b|Schiffer]]’s [[formation-processes|formation-process]] program rejects naive Pompeii readings of most prehistoric deposits, while modeling transforms between [[systemic-context]] and [[archaeological-context]]. In Schiffer 1987, **Pompeii ≈ the extreme de facto refuse case**: nearly complete systemic inventories left at abandonment; most sites are depleted by curate behavior, scavenging, draw-down, and maintenance (attributed, Ch. 4, 12).

## Semantic History

Teaching shorthand for “don’t read floors as activity maps without formation-process control.” Rapid-burial catastrophe sites remain real limiting cases (environmental Ch. 8–9), not the general model. At Broken K, Schiffer argues abandonment was **not** catastrophic Pompeii despite substantial de facto refuse (attributed, Ch. 12).

## Operationalizations

Contrast de facto refuse and abandonment assemblages with secondary refuse and cleaned floors; model clean-up and McKellar residual primary refuse; ethnoarchaeological site structure (Binford Nunamiut) as anti-Pompeii empirical program.

## Applications

Critique of New Archaeology social inferences from room floors ([[hill-broken-k-pueblo-study]]); general method training; Hohokam de facto house samples as chronological resources when pathway is controlled.

## Critiques and Limitations

The label is partisan. Schiffer’s “distortion” language was itself accused of smuggling a Pompeii-like original. Careful formation-process work and middle-range ethnoarchaeology both oppose naive Pompeii reading while disagreeing on theory language.
