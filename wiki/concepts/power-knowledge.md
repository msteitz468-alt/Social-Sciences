# Power-Knowledge

## Definition as Coined

**Power-knowledge** (*pouvoir-savoir*) is [[foucault-michel|Foucault]]'s thesis (attributed) that the production of knowledge and the exercise of power are not separable: apparatuses that generate truth about an object simultaneously exert power over it, and relations of power are made operable through the knowledge they organize. In *[[foucault-history-of-sexuality-1978|The History of Sexuality]]* Vol. I, Foucault sets the book's object as defining "the regime of **power-knowledge-pleasure** that sustains the discourse on human sexuality." He states the strong form on confession: "truth is not by nature free — nor error servile — but... its production is thoroughly imbued with relations of power," calling for "a 'political history of truth.'" And on discourse: "it is in discourse that power and knowledge are joined together."

## Semantic History

The compound belongs to Foucault's mid-1970s turn from "archaeology" (rules of discourse) to [[genealogy]] (discourse within power). It reworks the received opposition — knowledge as disinterested, power as coercive — into a single hyphenated relation. In later reception "power/knowledge" became a slogan of poststructuralist social theory; critics complain the compound is asserted more than demonstrated, and that it risks reducing all knowledge-claims to power-effects. Foucault's own formulations are more specific: not that knowledge *is* power, but that the two "directly imply one another," with no exteriority between "techniques of knowledge and strategies of power" (the "rule of immanence").

## Operationalizations

Foucault does not measure power-knowledge; he exhibits it through cases. In *The History of Sexuality*:
- **[[confession]]** — the confessor/analyst "was the master of truth. His was a hermeneutic function"; the "agency of domination does not reside in the one who speaks... but in the one who listens and says nothing."
- **[[scientia-sexualis]]** — nineteenth-century medicine and psychiatry "constructed around and apropos of sex an immense apparatus for producing truth," which "grounded" the emerging "racisms of the state" in "truth."
- **The examination** (clinic, school, family) — proximity, observation, and the extraction of discourse producing "a knowledge... that above all causes [the subject] to be ignorant of himself."

## Applications

Power-knowledge underwrites the whole [[analytics-of-power]] and the concepts [[biopower]], [[deployment-of-sexuality]], and [[scientia-sexualis]]. Beyond Foucault it has been applied across the sociology of knowledge, science studies, criminology, and postcolonial theory.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Self-reference** (attributed): if all truth-production is power-imbued, the status of Foucault's own knowledge-claims is unstable.
- **Loss of the truth/validity distinction** (attributed): critics argue the concept cannot distinguish well-warranted from ideological knowledge, collapsing epistemology into strategy. Foucault's defenders reply that he analyzes *régimes* of truth, not the falsity of particular claims.
