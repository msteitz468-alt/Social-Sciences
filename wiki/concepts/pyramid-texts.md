# Pyramid Texts

The **Pyramid Texts** are the corpus of hieroglyphic ritual spells inscribed on the internal walls of certain late Old Kingdom royal pyramids (beginning with Unas, end of 5th Dynasty, and continuing in 6th Dynasty and queens’ pyramids). They are the earliest extensive body of ancient Egyptian religious literature.

## Definition as Coined

“Pyramid Texts” is an etic label for spells that speak to the dead king’s ascent, offerings, protection, and identification with gods — performed or activated in the tomb context. [[lehner-mark|Lehner]] (*[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|Complete Pyramids]]* 1997) treats them under “Tomb and Temple” as evidence for royal afterlife ideology bound to the [[pyramid-complex]] (attributed). A protective utterance (PT 1278–79) threatens anyone who lays a finger on “this pyramid and this temple which belong to me and to my double” (inscribed before portcullises in Pepi I’s pyramid).

## Semantic History

### Discovery

In Mariette’s last year (1880) Antiquities Service foreman Mohammed Chahin opened Pepi I’s pyramid at [[saqqara]] — first find of Pyramid Texts, rapidly copied by Emile Brugsch and unofficially by [[petrie-flinders|Flinders Petrie]]. Merenre entered just before Mariette’s death; Gaston Maspero then “attacked the entire front of the Memphite Necropolis, from Abu Roash to Lisht,” opening Unas (28 Feb), Pepi I/Neferirkare (13 Apr), Teti (29 May) — five “dumb” Saqqara pyramids “spoken” in less than a year (attributed, Lehner 1997 reporting Maspero). Brugsch brothers’ 1881 work covered Unas, Teti, Pepi I, Merenre, Pepi II.

### Editions and reception

Kurt Sethe’s definitive edition numbered 714 sayings/spells; 1925 finds in Pepi II and queens Neith, Iput, Wedjebten extended the count to 759; French South Saqqara work under Jean Leclant continued recovering fragments. Editions differ: Unas (oldest most complete) has only 283 of known texts plus unique ones; Ibi’s small pyramid includes spells unknown earlier — fluidity and individual repertoire. Archaeological/historical evidence leads scholars to date earliest *composition* after unification (references to the OK state). Later reception: FIP/MK officials’ tombs; subsumed into Coffin Texts; still in NK officials’ tombs, Book of the Dead, Late Period papyri; reworked in NK temple ritual; Late Period copies in OK style include known and unknown spells — implying the known OK corpus is a selection from a larger body.

## Unas: first appearance

*(Attributed — Lehner 1997.)*

Unas’s chambers are the first since Djoser to be decorated: alabaster reed-mat / wood-frame enclosure around the sarcophagus; gabled ceiling with golden stars on blue; remaining walls of burial chamber, antechamber, and part of the horizontal passage covered with vertical blue-painted hieroglyph columns — the earliest Pyramid Texts (283 of >700 known spells; some already ancient). Reed-mat motif and star ceiling frame the king as in a divine booth open to the sky. Pyramid smallest known OK royal (“Perfect are the Places of Unas,” 57.75 m square, 43 m high, 56°).

**Lehner’s priesthood-independence reading (INTERPRETATION):** etching sacred utterances inside the pyramid responds to visible decay of former kings’ monuments and cults — permanent spells would work without depending forever on an unreliable priesthood.

## Corpus architecture across reigns

| Stage | Location / carrier | Notes (Lehner 1997) |
|---|---|---|

| Teti | Chamber walls + first single band of PT on basalt sarcophagus | More damaged walls |
| Pepi I | Expanded wall coverage; green-painted hieroglyphs | Brugsch discovery site; MFAS restoration of thousands of fragments |

| Pepi II queens | Neith first queen with PT in chamber and passage; Wedjebten also | “Democratization” still royal-household, not private |
| Later | Napatan Aspelta sarcophagus (Nuri) carved with PT + Book of the Dead | Long afterlife of the corpus outside Egypt proper |

## Five categories of spells

Scholars (as summarized by Lehner) recognize five major categories:

1. **Dramatic Texts** — lament, offering ritual, crowns, equipment introduction, opening of mouth/statue rituals as recited speech and prescribed action; speakers/recipients may take god roles; formulation possibly 2nd–3rd dynasties.
2. **Hymns with Name Formulae** — set cult symbols/actions in mythical context via “in this thy name of…” or “as.”
3. **Litanies** — verse enumerations of names (e.g. Spell 220 hailing crown); Hymns/Litanies possibly 4th dynasty.
4. **Glorifications (*Sakhu*, “that which makes one into an *akh*”)** — largest part; oldest mention sand tomb and mudbrick [[mastaba]]; many youngest composed 5th–even 6th dynasty.
5. **Magical Texts** — short snake/dangerous-being protection spells; judged oldest (early Archaic) by speech form.

## Themes and cosmology

*(Attributed interpretive synthesis — Lehner 1997.)*

Overall theme like temple statue/relief programmes: eternal existence of the king in the Afterlife — not a fully coherent single treatment.

- **Solar sky emphasis:** scholars suspect Heliopolis as place of much conception/formulation. King boards the sun-god ship; flies as falcon/kite/goose or leaps as grasshopper; destiny includes Imperishable Stars (circumpolar, ~26°–30° above northern horizon in the pyramid zone). Long narrow north-sloping passages from burial chambers aim like telescopes at stars that neither rise nor set.
- **Sky as watery landscape:** large “fresh water” surface crossed on reed floats; canals, lakes, banks; Milky Way as beaten path of stars; Field of Reeds and Field of Offerings — a vision of the Nile Valley at inundation. Doorways exclusive to gods/kings may reflect restricted pyramid-temple doors.
- **Nut:** sky personified — head west swallowing setting sun/stars, loins east birthing them. PT 616: king given to mother Nut in her identity of the coffin, gathered in identity of sarcophagus, ascended to her in identity of the tomb — so sloping passages “descending” to burial chambers also “ascending” to Nut.
- **Osiris / chthonic dimension:** identification with Osiris presents underworld dimension; myths (Osiris pageant; Horus–Seth inheritance) only fragmented — as if the full story were too potent for outright telling. Spells call for recomposition of the royal body implying prior dismemberment (linked to Osiris killed by Seth, reconstituted by Isis).
- **Sethe vs ritual-script views:** free-form amalgamation of spells permanently enabling transformation/resurrection (Sethe); or funeral-ritual script — “words to be spoken,” dramatic opening-of-mouth form, pyramid-consecration texts; offering ritual performed in the mortuary-temple offering hall.

## Allen reading of Unas (chamber as cosmos)

James Allen’s reading (as reported by Lehner): narrative flows away from the direction hieroglyphs face to follow an inside-out rule — texts move from inside the tomb outwards. Two thematic sets (burial chamber vs antechamber). Western gable BC and east gable antechamber: apotropaic frames paralleling exterior causeway/vestibule protection scenes. Offering ritual on north wall BC. East antechamber gable includes the “Cannibal Hymn” — king flies through stormy sky living on fathers/mothers — to be read via *ka* as communicative life force (Creator → gods → king → parent/child), not literal cannibalism. Sequence matches Unas rising from sarcophagus through BC → antechamber → corridor: body remains as Osiris in Duat; *ba* proceeds toward sunrise; antechamber = Akhet between Duat and day sky; later Teti/Pepi corridor texts on marshes at the edge of Akhet where the king becomes effective *akh*.

## Pyramid, *ben-ben*, and solar symbolism

*(Attributed — Lehner 1997.)*

True pyramids as sun symbols via Heliopolis *ben-ben*; Atum cosmogony (PT 527 and related); rays as ramp by which the king mounts to the sun; older step pyramids as giant stairs; white limestone casing as near-blinding reflector (“picture-window”). Pyramid more than magical ascent device: place of physical and spiritual transformation tying king’s ascent to world-creation and daily sun rebirth. Egyptian word *mer* for pyramid has no obvious cosmic etymology (Edwards’s “place of ascension” derivation self-doubted).

## Operationalizations

Spell numbers; distribution by chamber (burial chamber / antechamber / corridor / sarcophagus band); comparison across reigns; linguistic dating vs archaeological context; chamber cosmology mappings (Duat / Akhet / day sky).

## Applications

Ideology of [[divine-king|divine kingship]]; ritual-sequence hypotheses for the [[pyramid-complex]]; dating and attribution of late OK pyramids at [[saqqara]]; genealogy into Coffin Texts and Book of the Dead (as scholarly tradition, not identity).

## Critiques and Limitations

Spells are not a single authored “book”; compilation and performance context are debated. Architecture alone does not prove which rituals were enacted. Always attribute interpretive readings (solar vs stellar emphasis, priesthood-independence thesis, Cannibal Hymn *ka* reading, etc.). Corpus known from walls is a selection; Late Period copies imply larger lost repertoire.
