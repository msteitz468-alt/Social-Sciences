# Commercialization of Feeling

## Definition as Coined

The core thesis of *[[hochschild-managed-heart-1983|The Managed Heart]]*: under advanced service capitalism, private capacities for managing feeling are **transmuted** into **[[emotional-labor|emotional labor]]** — standardized, supervised, and sold. Closing Part One, Hochschild writes that she has described an emotional system in normal private life; Part Two shows "what happens when a gift becomes a commodity and that commodity is a feeling" (attributed).

**Transmutation** (attributed): elements of the private system (emotion work, feeling rules, exchange of feeling) are taken into the marketplace, stretched into standardized social forms; the contribution of feeling becomes thinner and "more susceptible to estrangement."

Hochschild: capitalism did not invent turning feeling into a commodity, but "has found a use for emotion management, and so it has organized it more efficiently and pushed it further" — advertising the sincere smile, training it, supervising it, and linking it to corporate profit (attributed).

## Operationalizations

Empirical illustration via airline service competition (post-1978 deregulation dynamics), Delta training as high-demand benchmark, industry speed-up and worker slowdown revealing latent emotional-labor requirements, and comparative bill-collector work.

## Applications

- People-processing industries; "emotional proletariat" (afterword reception language).
- Gendered service: status enhancement and sexualized friendliness as commercialized "womanly" feeling skill (Ch 8).
- Comparable-worth politics: contact with difficult clients often unpaid premium (afterword cites Steinberg & Jacobs).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Thesis is interpretive political economy of feeling, not a single statistical model of commodification.
- Risk of totalizing: not all public-contact jobs equally engineer deep acting; within-category variation is large (Ch 7).
- Socialism footnote: managed enthusiasm ("hero of labor") shows the issue is recompense systems, not capitalism alone (attributed).
