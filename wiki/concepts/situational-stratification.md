# Situational Stratification

**Situational stratification** is [[collins-randall|Randall Collins]]'s term (in *[[collins-interaction-ritual-chains-2004|Interaction Ritual Chains]]*, 2004, Ch. 7) for hierarchy as it is **enacted and felt in face-to-face encounters** — as distinct from macro class categories and from categorical status-group deference. It is an application of [[interaction-ritual-chains|interaction ritual chain theory]] to late-modern stratification.

## Definition as Coined

Collins argues that macro categories of class, status, and power must be micro-translated into chains of situations. **Situational stratification** names the resulting hierarchy of who dominates attention, sets emotional tone, and walks away with higher [[emotional-energy|emotional energy]] in the encounter — which need not match formal rank or categorical identity.

He contrasts:

| Dimension | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| **Categorical deference** | Rituals that mark fixed status-group membership (class manners, estate regalia, gender/age etiquette of the Goffmanian mid-century) |
| **Situational deference** | Moment-to-moment yielding to whoever commands the scene (celebrity, energy, sexuality, local expertise, "being in" preferred scenes) |
| **D-power** | Deference / order-giving power — authority to give orders and receive compliance (organizational chain of command) |
| **E-power** | Efficacy / emotional power — capacity to dominate the emotional entrainment of the situation without formal office |

Collins argues (attributed) that late-modern public life has shifted toward situational and E-power dynamics even as class inequality persists: categorical deference rituals have thinned ("evaporated" in the language of his earlier [[deference-and-demeanor]] work with Joan Annett), while situational scenes, celebrity totems, and youth quasi-estates reorganize who gets attention.

## Semantic History

- **Weber:** class / status / party as macro dimensions — Collins's foil for micro-translation.
- **Collins 1975:** [[class-cultures]] as order-giving/order-taking and communication-network position; [[deference-and-demeanor]] as comparative history of status ritual.
- **Goffman:** deference and demeanor, civil inattention, the interaction order — raw material Collins re-reads as natural IRs whose categorical contents historically shift.
- **Collins 2004 Ch. 7:** "situational stratification" as the explicit label; economic class re-read via **Zelizer circuits** (multiple monies and relational earmarking) rather than a single market hierarchy; status-group boundaries via Lamont-style boundary work and formal–informal ritual continua.
- Adjacent wiki concept: [[categorical-inequality]] (Tilly) — complementary macro account of durable categories; Collins's situational layer is the micro enactment problem, not a rival name for the same thing.

## Operationalizations

Observational: who initiates and holds mutual focus; who gives vs takes orders (D-power); who leads emotional entrainment without office (E-power); formal vs informal ritual markers (clothing, space, access); celebrity/attention as sacred-object dynamics. Survey class measures, Collins argues, miss situational dominance and circuit-specific economic life.

## Applications

Attributed applications in IRC Ch. 7: upper-class formal rituals vs working-class observation of them; celebrity as totem; street codes and challenges to civil inattention; organizational slippage between D-power charts and E-power realities; historical decline of patrimonial household ritual and rise of picaresque/highway imagery of situational encounters. Cross-links to [[class-cultures]] and [[deference-and-demeanor]] for the longer Collins program.

## Critiques and Limitations

The concept inherits IRC's general measurement and scope issues: situational dominance is easier to illustrate than to scale; the historical claim of categorical-deference decline is culture-specific (late modern West) and can be overstated; E-power risked becoming a residual "charisma" label. Use as Collins's attributed framework, not as wiki-voice description of all hierarchy.
