# Non-logical conduct

## Definition as Coined

In *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* Ch II, [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] defined **logical actions** as those that “logically conjoin means to ends not only from the standpoint of the subject performing them, but from the standpoint of other persons who have a more extensive knowledge” — i.e. logical both subjectively and objectively. **All other actions** he called **non-logical** — “by no means the same as ‘illogical’” (§150).

Nearly all human actions *look* logical to the actor (Greek mariners treat sacrifice and rowing as equally logical means). The sociological point is the objective/subjective mismatch.

| Moment | Usage |
|---|---|

| Ch V–VI | Non-logical conduct expressed as [[residues]]; logical varnish as [[derivations]] |
| Later sociology | Textbook “non-logical action”; sometimes flattened to “irrationality” (a misreading Pareto rejected) |

Livingston’s English title *The Mind and Society* frames the work as thought–conduct–society relations (editor POSITION, 1935).

## Operationalizations

Pareto’s Class II table (§151) by objective end / subjective purpose:

| Genus | Objective end | Subjective purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No | No | Scant importance in humans (logical varnish) |
| 2 | No | Yes | e.g. magic when no real effect |
| 3 | Yes | No | Pure type in animals; rare as pure form in humans |
| 4 | Yes | Yes (but ends differ) | Species α (actor would accept objective end if known) / β (would reject) |

Further apparatus: constant trunk of inclination vs variable interpretations (weather-magic pattern); psychic state *A* as origin of non-logical actions vs reasoning as origin of logical ones (§161).

## Applications

- Foundation of [[paretian-sociology]]: induction “at the very first glance” finds non-logical actions play an important social part (§153).
- Logical actions numerous in arts/sciences and much of political economy among “civilized peoples” (§152) — residual domain still large.
- Language formation as non-logical “masterpiece” (practice before grammar) (§158).
- Bridge to residue classes: conflict of combination-tendency vs permanence of sensation-groups (§157) → [[combination-instinct]] / [[group-persistence]].
- Elite and political examples (auspices, missions, free competition vs monopoly) as genus 4α/4β illustrations (§159).

## Critiques and Limitations

- “Non-logical” easily misread as pejorative irrationality; Pareto’s genera include objectively effective conduct the actor does not intend.
- Classification depends on the analyst’s “more extensive knowledge” — historically relative.
- Later ideology and rational-choice theories reframe the same phenomena without Pareto’s taxonomy.
