# Trade-Union Neutrality

## Definition as Coined

**Trade-union neutrality** (also “neutralism”) names the principle that trade unions should remain **non-partisan** with respect to socialist parties: unions improve conditions within capitalism; the party handles socialism and the abolition of capitalist relations. In [[lenin-collected-works-vol-13-1962]], the slogan is the polemical target of [[lenin-vladimir]] after the London RSDLP Congress (1907) and the Stuttgart International Socialist Congress (August 1907).

Lenin reported that he himself had earlier **advocated** trade-union neutrality (including in pages of *What Is To Be Done?* stressing economic struggle and unions) and had not altered that view until London and Stuttgart led him to conclude that neutrality is **not defensible as a principle**. The only correct principle, he argued, is the **closest possible alignment** of unions with the Party — pursued perseveringly, without expelling those of different opinion or seeking mere formal “recognition.”

## Semantic History

| Stage (as Lenin narrates) | Content |
|---|---|
| Early European SD practice | Neutrality sometimes upheld to widen the field when the movement was undeveloped and bourgeois influence on unions less systematic |
| **Stockholm RSDLP (1906)** | Menshevik-majority congress adhered to trade-union neutrality |
| **Olympia Theatre meeting, 1905** (as *Znamya Truda* reported; Lenin uses) | Mensheviks: classical non-partisan delimitation party vs unions; Bolsheviks: **close unity**, Party leads unions; SRs: strictly non-partisan to avoid proletarian split |
| **Mannheim** | Kautsky (as Lenin/Zetkin frame) moves unions left — “considerable breach” in neutrality |
| **London RSDLP (1907)** | Necessity of working toward **partisanship** of unions |
| **Stuttgart (1907)** | Resolution that, Kautsky said, “puts an end to neutrality once and for all”; Beer amendment stressed socialist tasks of unions |
| **RSDLP C.C. resolution (pub. *Proletary* No. 21, Feb 1908)** | Whole Party: work **not** in spirit of neutrality but closest Party–union relations; SD solid units in unions; illegal unions where legal impossible |

After Stuttgart, Lenin held that only **Socialist-Revolutionaries and Plekhanov** still seriously defended neutrality among relevant actors — “very unsuccessfully.”

## Operationalizations

**Neutralist operationalization (Menshevik / classical form as Lenin frames it):**

- Party = socialism / abolish capitalist relations.
- Unions = improve conditions within capitalism; non-partisan; all workers of an occupation.
- Plekhanov’s Stuttgart amendment: “unity of the economic struggle should always be borne in mind” — Lenin: no serious significance for the essence of the dispute; did not restore neutralism.
- SRs: party propaganda “has its place **outside** the union”; non-partisanship to avoid splitting the proletariat.

**Anti-neutralist operationalization (Lenin / London–Stuttgart / C.C.):**

- Each organisation has its own sphere and acts independently **and** expands a common sphere with the Party.
- Independence of the economic sphere ≠ non-partisan character.
- Partisanship achieved by SD **work within** unions, not by obliging every member to join the Party.
- Campaign to win unions and subordinate them to Party leadership: Bolsheviks “frank and open,” Mensheviks (as Lenin frames) roundabout; do not lose sight of **organisational unity** of unions.
- Contrast with Menshevism is **not** repudiation of trade-union work but a different line of propaganda, agitation, and organisation toward socialist consciousness and revolutionary tasks of the [[proletariat]].

## Applications

- **Preface to Voinov (Lunacharsky) pamphlet (Nov 1907):** Lenin endorses revolutionary trade-union work against Menshevik neutralism; warns against SR presumption that Stuttgart endorsed *their* non-party line; corrects Voinov’s over-general attacks on German “orthodoxy” while lining up with him on essentials at Stuttgart.
- **“Trade-Union Neutrality” (*Proletary* No. 22, Feb/March 1908):** C.C. resolution; polemic vs SR *Znamya Truda* and Plekhanov; Richard Bell railway settlement in Britain as case of “improvement” renouncing struggle — linked by Lenin to neutrality theory putting unity for improvement ahead of unity for emancipation.
- **German experience (as Lenin cites):** neutrality strengthened opportunism without preventing Christian/Liberal unions; class contradictions and Russian revolution prototypes (mass strike, armed uprising) cut ground from under the theory.
- **Revolutionary syndicalism:** Lenin treats it as reaction to opportunism, reformism, and parliamentary cretinism; not a substitute for Party-aligned class organisation.

## Critiques and Limitations

**Lenin’s critique of neutrality (attributed):**

- Neutrality is **ideological cover** for bourgeois strivings to confine unions to petty activity within the existing order and keep them from socialism.
- Neutrality vs close ties to revolutionary SD “inevitably prefers” methods of improving conditions that **blunt** proletarian class struggle.
- Plekhanov’s claim that Marx would still favour neutrality in Germany rests (Lenin says) on a one-sided quotation ignoring the sum of Marx’s statements.
- “Neutrality in Bebel’s not revisionist sense” — Lenin: do not imitate Bebel when Bebel was wrong (agrarian programme Breslau 1895; Essen war distinctions; elevating neutrality to principle).
- SRs confuse **independence of sphere** with **non-party character** and suppress the issue that neutrality strengthens bourgeois influence over the proletariat.

**Limitations of this page’s evidence:** opponents (Plekhanov, SRs, Menshevik neutralists, Bebel) appear almost entirely **as framed by Lenin** in [[lenin-collected-works-vol-13-1962]]; Stuttgart text and Kautsky’s gloss are likewise mediated. Early European neutrality as widening-field strategy is Lenin’s own historical concession, not an independent multi-source finding.
