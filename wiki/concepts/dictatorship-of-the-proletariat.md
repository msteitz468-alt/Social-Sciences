# Dictatorship of the Proletariat

## Definition as Coined

[[marx-karl|Marx]] used the phrase for the transitional political rule of the working class after socialist revolution. In *[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|The State and Revolution]]* (1917), [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] makes it the load-bearing concept of [[marxist-theory-of-the-state|Marxist state theory]] as he reconstructs it:

> the proletariat organized as the ruling class

— a state that is still a state (special coercive apparatus) but of a new type, directed against the bourgeoisie, and constituted so that it “begins to wither away immediately.” **Attributed** — theoretical claim, not a wiki-voice finding.

Lenin (attributed) treats recognition of the dictatorship of the proletariat as the **touchstone** of Marxism: recognition of [[class-struggle]] alone is incomplete and can still be acceptable to the bourgeoisie; only linking class struggle to this transitional dictatorship is distinctive.

## Semantic History

- **Marxian usage:** transitional rule; Lenin dates the phrase’s prominence “after the Paris Commune,” but Chretien notes earlier Marx uses (1850, 1852) — philological caution.
- **Lenin 1917:** contrasts **dictatorship of the bourgeoisie** (including under democratic republics) with **dictatorship of the proletariat**. Democracy and dictatorship are not opposites: each is class democracy / class dictatorship. Attacks Second International silence, dilution, or postponement of the term (Kautsky charged with saying problems of the proletarian dictatorship can be “quite safely left” to the future).
- **Commune as concrete form:** Marx’s “political form at last discovered”; institutional marks (standing army → armed people; electable/recallable officials at workers’ wages; working body, not merely parliamentary). Democratic republic treated as the “nearest approach” among existing forms, but still not the DoP itself until the machine is smashed and replaced.
- **Later reception:** Soviet constitutional/party usage; Western Marxist, liberal, and anarchist critiques of the term as license for party monopoly — positions for [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]; not adjudicated here.

### *Left-Wing Communism* (1920, attributed)

In *[[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]]*, Lenin characterized the dictatorship of the proletariat as "a most determined and most ruthless war waged by the new class against a more powerful enemy," directed not only against the old ruling classes but against the "forces and traditions of the old society" continually reproduced by small-scale production. He defended strict centralized party discipline as a condition of waging this war, a position critics read as sitting in tension with the Commune-type democratic institutional programme (elected, recallable officials) associated with the concept in 1917 — recorded here as an internal tension, not adjudicated.

### October 1917–February 1918: the dictatorship exercised (attributed; [[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]])

Lenin applied the concept operationally in the months around the seizure of power. He framed the Republic of Soviets as a higher form of democracy than a bourgeois republic crowned by a Constituent Assembly, the only form capable of securing "the most painless transition to socialism" for the dictatorship of the proletariat, and — after the Constituent Assembly's Right-SR majority refused to recognize Soviet power — argued "the interests of this revolution stand higher than the formal rights of the Constituent Assembly," explicitly warning that "a few Bolshevik leaders" had strayed toward a formal-legal reading he rejected (see [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]). Defending forcible measures against critics who invoked "correct scientific theory" only to caution against action, he wrote that "the iron hand that destroys also creates" and insisted "we use the words 'dictatorship of the proletariat' in all seriousness and we shall effect that dictatorship." He denied Marxists ever intended to "introduce" socialism by fiat: "a long period of 'birth-pangs' lies between capitalism and socialism; that violence is always the midwife of the old society; that a special state... corresponds to the transitional period between the bourgeois and the socialist society, namely, the dictatorship of the proletariat" — citing the Paris Commune as itself "a dictatorship of the proletariat" whose downfall Marx and Engels partly attributed to insufficiently vigorous use of armed force. He defined "accounting and control" of labor and distribution via the Soviets as "the essence of socialist transformation," and outlined a punitive program against "parasites" (prison, compulsory labor, and — as one experimental method among several — summary shooting of "one out of every ten idlers... on the spot"). The Declaration of Rights of the Working and Exploited People (drafted by Lenin) proclaimed Russia a Republic of Soviets with the aim "to abolish all exploitation of man by man... to mercilessly crush the resistance of the exploiters"; when the Constituent Assembly's SR majority rejected it, the Bolshevik/Left-SR walkout and subsequent CEC dissolution decree followed.

### 1918, against Kautsky (attributed; [[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]])

Lenin defined dictatorship as "rule based directly upon force and unrestricted by any laws," and revolutionary proletarian dictatorship as "rule won and maintained by the use of violence by the proletariat against the bourgeoisie, rule that is unrestricted by any laws." Against [[kautsky-karl|Kautsky]]'s charge that the Soviet Constitution's disfranchisement of capitalists was arbitrary and undemocratic, Lenin argued genuine equality between exploiter and exploited is impossible — a "liberal" argument proceeds from majority/minority in the abstract, a "Marxist" argument from the class relation between exploiters and exploited — and that any state, even a democratic one, inevitably serves whichever class controls it. He claimed Soviet ("proletarian") democracy was "a million times more democratic than any bourgeois democracy," citing the enlistment of the exploited in administration and the abolition of the old bureaucracy, while noting bourgeois democracy's own record of suppressing minorities (Dreyfus; lynching in the US; Ireland/Ulster). He stated the disfranchisement of the bourgeoisie was not a pre-planned feature but "emerged of itself in the course of the struggle," pointing to bourgeois sabotage of the Soviets culminating in the Kornilov mutiny. Bolshevism, he argued, "has popularised throughout the world the idea of the 'dictatorship of the proletariat,'" translating the term "first into Russian, and then into all the languages of the world."

### 1918, Kautsky's counter-reading (attributed; [[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918]])

The opposing pole of the definitional dispute, now in its author's own words. Kautsky argued Marx's 1875 phrase describes "a condition, and not a form of government": in a real democracy where the proletariat is the "immense majority" (Communist Manifesto), its rule *is* the dictatorship of the proletariat — the Paris Commune, which Engels "expressly declared" to be the dictatorship of the proletariat, rested on universal suffrage, its first act an appeal to the electors. As a form of government, class dictatorship is impossible (a class can rule but not govern); it must become dictatorship of a party, then of a fraction — and where a party rules through peasant alliance, "a dictatorship of proletarians and peasants over proletarians." His conclusion: "By the dictatorship of the proletariat we are unable to understand anything else than its rule on the basis of democracy," and "Marx must not be cited by those who support dictatorship in preference to democracy." John H. Kautsky's 1964 introduction adds the scholarly caution that the "dictatorship" Kautsky attacked in 1918 (restricted suffrage, outlawed parties) predates and should not be conflated with mature totalitarianism (secondary scholarship, attributed).

### 1920–21: the Trade-Union Debate (attributed; [[soviet-trade-union-debate]])

In the controversy with Trotsky over the role of trade unions, Lenin argued the dictatorship of the proletariat cannot be exercised by an organization taking in the whole proletarian class, since the class remains "divided," "degraded," and "corrupted in parts" under capitalism; it can only be exercised by a vanguard, via a system of "transmission belts" running from vanguard party to advanced class to the mass of working people (in Russia, the peasantry) — with trade unions standing "between the Party and the government" as a "reservoir of the state power." He warned that ceding candidate-nomination and administration to the trade unions (as Shlyapnikov's "mandatory nominations" proposal implied) would be "fatal for the dictatorship of the proletariat," and — citing the Central Committee's own December 1920 "freedom of discussion" ruling — argued formal democratic rights inside the Party must still be subordinated to "the revolutionary interest." He held the state is inherently "a sphere of coercion," so renouncing coercion "in the epoch of the dictatorship of the proletariat" would be madness, describing the Party as "the leader, the vanguard of the proletariat, which rules directly." See [[bureaucracy]] for the "workers' state with a bureaucratic twist" formulation from the same debate.

### Kautsky 1919: democracy, Commune, and the failure of terrorist dictatorship (attributed; [[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]])

Extending his 1918 pamphlet, Kautsky held "class-dictatorship" as a form of government is nonsense (a class rules but does not govern as a person does); Jacobin terror failed to produce socialism; the 1871 Commune was democracy — "government of the people through the people" — not a Soviet prototype and not a terrorist regime; personal/factory dictatorship is incompatible with democracy including Soviet democracy; majority-over-minority is the only compulsion democracy concedes; Soviet "council" demarcation of workers is arbitrary foundation for dictatorial rule; despotism is the essence of dictatorship and cannot be reformed without abolishing dictatorship.

### Trotsky 1920: dictatorship as exceptional regime, party rule, and terror (attributed; [[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920]])

Trotsky's *Terrorism and Communism* defends the dictatorship of the proletariat as an exceptional transitional regime for abolishing private property in the means of production, necessary because the existence of the bourgeoisie is at stake and force decides. He links it tightly to [[red-terror]] (principled rejection of terrorism = rejection of proletarian supremacy) and to party monopoly: "the dictatorship of the soviets became possible only by means of the dictatorship of the party," which he denies is mere "substitution." Soviets are elastic class apparatus, not a constitutional *Notbehelf* for missing parties (against Kautsky as he constructs him); after victory they become organs of power while the party retains the final word. The same regime extends to the labour front ([[militarization-of-labour]]): the road to socialism as highest intensification of the state — the dictatorship as the state's "last brilliant flame" before melting into producing/consuming cooperation. Against Kautsky's "too soon" charge, Trotsky holds the young proletariat of a backward country took initiative along history's line of least resistance and must learn administration by riding the horse.

### 1921: dictatorship as "direction of policy" under NEP (attributed; [[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]])

Justifying the priority given to peasant production over immediate worker welfare under the New Economic Policy, Lenin denied this constituted a "renunciation" of the dictatorship of the proletariat, redefining it in this context as "the direction of policy by the proletariat," which must address the most urgent problem first. He restated the "principal enemy" as the petty-bourgeois element: "Either we subordinate it to our control and accounting, or it will overthrow the workers' power as surely and as inevitably as the revolution was overthrown by the Napoleons and the Cavaignacs who sprang from this very soil of petty proprietorship" — and defended continued suppression of Mensheviks and SRs as "Red, proletarian terrorism," an unavoidable feature of revolutionary conditions worldwide since 1914–18: "there is no middle course, no 'third' course, nor can there be any."

## Operationalizations

Not a survey construct. Lenin operationalizes via Commune institutions and via the *Gotha* analysis of transition (lower phase of communism still needs a state — even a “bourgeois state without the bourgeoisie” in his dialectical formulation for residual right/coercion — until the higher phase). Suppression of the minority (ex-exploiters) by the majority is presented as vastly simpler than the reverse, allowing a “semi-state.”

## Applications

- Core of [[marxist-theory-of-the-state]] and of Bolshevik revolutionary strategy as stated in 1917.
- Linked by Lenin to Russian **soviets** as Commune-type organs (argument promised for unfinished Ch. 7).
- Foil for reformist “capture the democratic state” programmes and for anarchist “no transitional state.”
- Distinct from Skocpol’s [[social-revolution]] (structural, state-building outcomes) — different research object.

## Critiques and Limitations

| Critique (position) | Thrust |
|---|---|
| Social-Democratic ([[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918|Kautsky 1918]], primary) | "Condition, not form of government"; minority dictatorship → Bonapartism/civil war; democracy is the proletariat's school and gauge |
| Social-Democratic ([[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919|Kautsky 1919]], primary) | Terror fails historically; Commune ≠ Soviet/terror license; despotism essence of dictatorship; free press vs Extraordinary Commissions |
| Trotsky 1920 (practitioner polemic) | DoP requires terror, party dictatorship, and labour compulsion in civil-war conditions; formal democracy is the enemy's weapon |
| Anarchist | Any “workers’ state” is still a state; abolish immediately |
| Liberal / Weberian | State defined by legitimate force, not class essence |
| Historical | One-party Soviet practice diverged from Commune programme |
| Philological | Lenin’s reconstruction of Marx is selective |

Each is a **position**, not wiki voice. Lenin’s text is itself a partisan reconstruction of Marx.
