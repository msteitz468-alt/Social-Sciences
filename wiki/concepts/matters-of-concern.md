# Matters of Concern

Latour’s contrast term to **matters of fact** in *[[latour-reassembling-the-social-2005|Reassembling the Social]]* (Fourth Source of Uncertainty): shift attention from settled, decontextualized facts to the assembled, controversial, cared-about gatherings of people and things.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Latour 2005.)* **Matters of fact** appear as bare objects once production and controversy have been deleted ([[black-box|black-boxed]]). **Matters of concern** are the same sorts of entities when treated as gatherings — risky, multi-connected, under dispute or care — without reducing “construction” to mere social projection. The methodological wager is to feed off controversies rather than wait for closure.

## Semantic History

Tied to Latour’s critique of “critique” as debunking and to later political ecology rhetoric; distinct from everyday “concerns” as attitudes only.

## Operationalizations

Track how an issue is assembled (spokespersons, instruments, forums); reopen black boxes into matters of concern for analysis.

## Applications

STS, public controversies, design/organization studies; ANT’s Fourth Source.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can blur any dispute into “concern”; critics see soft relativism or loss of truth criteria — Latour rejects that reading in-text.
