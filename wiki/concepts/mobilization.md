# Mobilization

## Definition as Coined

In Tilly's mobilization model (*FMR* 1978), **mobilization** is the extent of resources under the **collective control** of a contender (group); as a process, an increase in resources or in degree of collective control (**demobilization** = decline). Schematic (attributed): quantity of resources × probability of delivery ≈ mobilization; mobilization = f(organization); organization = catness × netness.

## Semantic History

Distinct from military "mobilization" and from purely psychological "activation." Resource-mobilization theorists (Oberschall, McCarthy & Zald) are adjacent; Tilly generalizes beyond formal SMOs.

## Operationalizations

Control of money, labor power, weapons, information, loyalties usable in claim-making. Not mere membership counts.

## Applications

Explaining capacity to act before opportunity triggers action; linking communal vs associational group structure to protest capacity.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Resources" inventory is open-ended; delivery probability hard to measure; model is deliberately primitive/static in *FMR* ch. 3–4.
