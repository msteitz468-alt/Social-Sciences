# Capitalist Discipline

## Definition as Coined

In *[[ong-spirits-of-resistance-1987|Spirits of Resistance and Capitalist Discipline]]* (1987), [[ong-aihwa|Aihwa Ong]] takes **discipline** as “the effect of the exercise of power in the interests of capitalist production,” so social control is traceable through cultural forms that enforce compliance **within and outside** economic enterprises. Expanded in Ch. 1: effects of power on the subjugated and enforced/induced compliance with political, social, and economic objectives considered rational and functional for capitalist production.

## Semantic History

- **Foucault (1970s):** productive power; discursive production of rituals, objects, and “truth”; disciplinary techniques implanted in bodies (docile bodies) — Ong applies this to TNC factory subjectivity.
- **Labor-process tradition (Braverman, Burawoy):** shopfloor control, consent, and separation of conception/execution — Ong accepts shopfloor rigor but **rejects** over-determined internal logic that banishes imaginative life from the factory; corporate culture and sexualized gender hierarchy matter.
- **Thompson (1967):** time-work-discipline transforming agrarian populations — Ong cites and limits transfer to peripheral Malay labor.
- **Ong 1987:** multi-scalar synthesis — household claims, *kampung* gender norms, state EOI labor law, Islamic moral surveillance, Japanese corporate familialism — not factory walls alone.

## Operationalizations

Not a single index. Ong tracks discipline via: work rules, targets, three-shift cycles, microscope surveillance; recruitment of young single “nimble fingers”; turnover designed against long tenure/unionization; *bapa angkat* and parents’ meetings; prayer-time and dress control; *khalwat* raids; dakwah models of womanhood; peer *malu* and self-regulation among [[neophyte-factory-women]].

## Applications

Primary case: Japanese electronics plants and [[sungai-jawa]] kampung society, Kuala Langat, **1979–80** ([[ong-spirits-of-resistance-study]]). Comparative teaching with other global-factory and EOI gender studies.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether “discipline” over-unifies heterogeneous controls; how far possession and micro-tactics count as resistance to discipline ([[spirits-of-resistance]]); state vs firm vs family weights vary by chapter. Keep date-stamped to Ong’s source.
