# Smash the State Machine

## Definition as Coined

In *[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|The State and Revolution]]*, [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] elevates Marx’s conclusion from 1848–51 and from the Paris Commune to the criterion of revolutionary Marxism:

> The working class cannot simply lay hold of the ready-made state machinery and wield it for its own purposes.

The task is to **break up / smash** that machinery (*zerbrechen*) and replace it with a Commune-type [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]. **Attributed** theoretical-strategic claim.

## Semantic History

### Marx (as Lenin quotes)

- ***Eighteenth Brumaire*:** all previous revolutions perfected the state machine instead of smashing it; parties that contended for power regarded possession of this immense state edifice as the principal spoils.
- **Letter to Kugelmann (12 April 1871) and 1872 *Manifesto* preface:** the next attempt of the French Revolution will be no longer, as before, to transfer the bureaucratic-military machine from one hand to another, but to smash it; “cannot simply lay hold” becomes the corrected programme.
- ***Civil War in France*:** Commune as first attempt to smash the bourgeois state machine and the political form that must replace it.

### Lenin 1917

- **Smash vs perfect:** prior revolutions (including bourgeois ones) improved the machine of class rule; proletarian revolution must destroy it.
- **Smash vs capture:** Second International “opportunism” (esp. Kautsky as Lenin constructs him) is charged with treating seizure/staffing of the existing state as enough — “we can quite safely leave” dictatorship problems to the future; when Pannekoek presses smash language (1912), Kautsky is charged with rejecting destruction of the state machine.
- **Smash vs abolish (anarchist):** Marxists and anarchists agree the bourgeois state must go; they disagree on the **transitional** proletarian state. Smash of the *bourgeois* machine ≠ immediate abolition of all political authority.

### Later vocabularies

Reformist “capture/use”; Weberian transformation of administration; Skocpolian finding that social revolutions *build* stronger states — different questions; see [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]].

## Operationalizations

Institutional marks Lenin draws from the Commune (attributed):

| Smash / suppress | Replace with |
|---|---|
| Standing army | Armed people |
| Privileged bureaucracy / high state pay | Electable, recallable officials at workers’ wages |
| Police as political force | Responsible, revocable agents |
| Purely parliamentary body | Working body (executive + legislative) |
| “National war instrument of capital against labor” | Working-class government / form for economic emancipation |

Not a measurement construct. Historical tests of whether 1917 “smashed” or rebuilt a stronger state belong to historiography — a different question from Lenin’s normative programme.

## 1917 institutional programme (attributed)

The April Theses and the associated 1917 texts (*[[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]]*) give the smash programme its most concrete institutional form for the Russian case: Lenin's draft Bolshevik catechism called for abolition of the standing army, police, and "the usual" bureaucracy, replacing officials with elected, instantly displaceable ones paid no more than a competent worker's wage, and for arming the whole people as a militia — explicitly presented as continuous with the Paris Commune model and as the defining mark separating the Bolshevik position from that of the SRs and Mensheviks, whom Lenin accused of hesitating before Commune-type measures.

## *Left-Wing Communism* qualification (1920, attributed)

*[[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]]* does not revise the smash thesis but extends it: Lenin argues that even after the bourgeois machine is smashed, the [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] must wage sustained "ruthless war" against habits and relations of small-scale production that continually regenerate capitalist and bureaucratic tendencies — the smashing of the old machine is thus presented as necessary but not sufficient; ongoing struggle against relapse is required.

## 1917–18 application and defense against Kautsky (attributed)

In *[[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]* ("Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?"), Lenin restated Marx's Paris Commune thesis — that the proletariat cannot simply "lay hold of" the ready-made state machine but must smash it and substitute a new one — presenting the Soviets of Workers', Soldiers' and Peasants' Deputies as a new-type state apparatus of the same kind the Commune had created. He held that capitalism's own accounting apparatus (banks, syndicates, the post, consumers' societies) should not be smashed but "wrested from the control of the capitalists" and subordinated to the Soviets — distinguishing what must be smashed (the coercive/bureaucratic machinery of class rule) from what should be seized and repurposed (large-scale organizational and accounting infrastructure). Reporting the October seizure to the Petrograd Soviet (Oct 25, 1917), he declared the old state apparatus "shattered to its foundations" and replaced by Soviet organizations.

In *[[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]*, Lenin defended the thesis against Kautsky directly: he credited Marx with attaching "enormous significance to smashing the bureaucratic machine," faulting Kautsky for ignoring bourgeois lawyers and bureaucrats as a distinct class interest, and cited Engels's 1891 remark that "superstitious belief in the state... has been carried over into the general thinking of the bourgeoisie and even of many workers" in Germany as diagnosing Kautsky's own refusal to let proletarian organizations (the Soviets) become state organizations.

In *[[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]* (Plan-of-Pamphlet notes), Lenin traced the historical trajectory of the "smashed" apparatus in practice: six months after October 1917, with the old bureaucratic apparatus "smashed from top to bottom," they felt "none of its evils"; by the Eighth Party Congress (March 1919) the Party Programme openly admitted "a partial revival of bureaucracy within the Soviet system"; by spring 1921 the evils of bureaucracy were "even more distinct and sinister" — see [[bureaucracy]] for the fuller account of this reversion and its argued economic roots.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Kautskyist / social-democratic:** smash language is Blanquist or anti-democratic; use parliament and civil service.
- **Anarchist:** smash must include *all* state forms immediately.
- **Historical-sociological:** successful [[social-revolution|social revolutions]] often produce more centralized bureaucratic states (e.g. Skocpol) — challenges empirical expectations of lasting anti-statism without refuting the normative claim on its own terms.
- **Practice gap:** post-October Soviet reconstruction of apparatus vs. Commune programme — record as open problem.
