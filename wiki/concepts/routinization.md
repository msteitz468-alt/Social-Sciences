# Routinization

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: the **habitual, taken-for-granted character** of the vast bulk of the activities of day-to-day social life; the prevalence of familiar styles and forms of conduct, both supporting and supported by a sense of [[ontological-security]] (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Master key of *Constitution* Ch. 2. Connects Goffmanian encounter management, Hägerstrand daily paths, and institutional reproduction via [[duality-of-structure]]. Distinct from Weberian "routinization of charisma" (different problem).

## Operationalizations

Time-space path notation; ethnography of daily trajectories; seriality of encounters.

## Applications

- Psychological: grounds trust/security.
- Institutional: recursive reproduction of structural properties without continuous discursive planning.
- Critical situations reveal routine by its collapse.

## Critiques and Limitations

May underplay deliberate innovation and conflict unless paired with contradiction/conflict analysis (Ch. 4–6).
