# Aṣl (Blood of Ancestry)

## Definition as Coined

[[abu-lughod-lila|Abu-Lughod]] reports that [[awlad-ali|Awlad ʿAli]] use **blood** (*dam*) in two related senses:

1. **Aṣl** — nobility of origin/ancestry; the link to the past through genealogy that underwrites collective cultural identity and moral worth.
2. **Garāba** — the blood of present relationship (kinship closeness), including agnatic *ʿaṣabiyya*-like solidarity and maternal ties of shared life (*ʿishra*).

Aṣl is a point of intense concern. It distinguishes Bedouin identity from Nile Valley Egyptian society and organizes claims among tribal groups (including relations to Mrābṭīn and other statuses). Genealogical narratives associate Awlad ʿAli, like other Saʿādi tribes of Cyrenaica, with Beni Suleim and Beni Hilal invasions; migration into Egypt is placed variously at the end of the seventeenth or eighteenth century in sources she cites.

## Semantic History

In the ethnography, aṣl is not a static pedigree fetish alone: moral qualities (generosity, autonomy, honor) are said to flow from noble blood, and loss of lifestyle does not erase identity if genealogical and moral criteria hold. Abu-Lughod's own acceptance as fieldworker turned partly on being recognized as Muslim, Arab, and of good aṣl (via her father's standing), showing the concept's everyday gatekeeping force.

## Operationalizations

Genealogical narration; marriage preferences (patrilateral parallel cousin); moral vocabulary of "origin"; status rankings between core lineages and clients; Bedouin vs. Egyptian contrast talk — ethnographic, not genetic testing.

## Applications

[[awlad-ali]] identity; [[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-study]]; caution against equating tribal ethnonym with genetic population. Related honor discourse: aṣl and [[hasham|ḥasham]] interact in claims about who must defer to whom.

## Critiques and Limitations

Emic political-moral construct; not a biological measure. Four-way non-identity: aṣl claims ≠ archaeological culture ≠ language family ≠ genetic cluster. Origin stories are contested historiography, not wiki-voice demography.
