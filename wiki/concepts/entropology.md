# Entropology

## Definition as Coined

**Entropology** (*entropologie*) is a coinage from the closing pages of [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss's]] *[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]* (1955): a play on "anthropology" proposing that the discipline's true object is the process by which civilization produces **entropy** — disorder and inertia — as its by-product. "Rather than *anthropology*, one ought to write *entropology* as the name of a discipline devoted to studying... this process of disintegration": every exchanged word and printed line, he argued, levels an information differential and hastens a universal running-down. It accompanies the book's famous line, "The world began without man and it will end without him."

## Semantic History

The term crystallizes the melancholy, civilization-critical strain of *Tristes Tropiques* — the mourning for indigenous worlds destroyed by Western expansion (the "melancholy liquidation of the assets of a dying culture," of the [[tupi-kawahib|Tupi-Kawahib]]). It is a **rhetorical and philosophical** coinage, not a methodological program; Lévi-Strauss never operationalized "entropology" as a research practice.

## Operationalizations

None in a technical sense. The concept survives as a much-cited image for anthropology as immanent critique of the modern West and for a pessimistic, thermodynamic view of cultural history.

## Applications

Frequently invoked in discussions of Lévi-Strauss's reflexivity, of anthropology's melancholy and its complicity in documenting what colonialism destroyed, and as an early gesture toward what became the 1980s critique of ethnographic authority (*Writing Culture*).

## Critiques and Limitations

As a scientific concept it is essentially a metaphor and is not treated as an analytic tool; its cosmic pessimism and the analogy between social and thermodynamic entropy are literary rather than demonstrated. Record it as a signature *idea* of *Tristes Tropiques*, attributed to Lévi-Strauss, not as an established framework.
