# Sib

## Definition as Coined

[[lowie-robert-h|Robert H. Lowie]] (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, 1920), following Professor Philbrick's terminological suggestion, adopted the Anglo-Saxon term **sib** for the unilateral kinship unit that resembles the family in being based on kinship but differs fundamentally by reckoning descent through **one parent only** (mother-sib or father-sib). Lowie argued existing nomenclature (gens, clan, etc.) was hopelessly confused and needed a fresh comparative term.

## Semantic History

Victorian writers used *gens*, *clan*, *septs*, etc., often equating them as stages of one evolutionary type ([[gens]] under Morgan–Engels). Lowie's *sib* is an **etic comparative construct** designed to separate the unilateral unit from the bilateral family and from evolutionary stage claims. Later anthropology more often says **clan** / **lineage** / **descent group**; *sib* survives in some kinship literature and as a historiographic marker of the Boasian social-organization program. Do not silently equate sib = gens = people = language = genes.

## Operationalizations

Lowie: unilateral membership; often (not always) [[exogamy]]; variable ties to [[totemism]]; may form higher-order groupings (phratries, etc.); historically secondary to the universal bilateral family. Trait lists vary by society — diversity of origin is part of Lowie's claim.

## Applications

Central organizing concept of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]* chs. on The Sib and History of the Sib; Crow Thick-lodge / Bad-leggings illustration in the Conclusion; critique of Morgan's sib-pattern monopoly model.

## Critiques and Limitations

Terminological reform did not fully stick (clan/lineage remained standard). Whether any single etic package covers all unilateral groups is contested. Lowie's historical reconstructions of sib origins are particularist hypotheses, not wiki-voice findings.
