# Demagogic plutocracy (Pareto)

## Definition as Coined

[[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] called the “very important type of government flourishing in our day” **government by “speculators”** — demagogic regimes that use intelligence and cunning chiefly to play upon **interests** (without necessarily ignoring sentiments): type **II-b** in his force/cunning typology (§§2275–2276). He also wrote of the **“pluto-demagogic system that is nowadays called democracy”** — an “indissoluble trinity” of bureaucrats, politicians, and speculators (§§2306–2309 region).

## Semantic History

Part of Ch XII’s government taxonomy; prepared by earlier contempt for plutocratic democracy in Livingston’s biographical framing and by *Trasformazione della democrazia* (1921, not yet ingested). Later elite and mass-society theories borrow the diagnosis without the residue apparatus.

## Operationalizations

- **Cost/production:** II-b governments are expensive yet often highly productive; surplus may yield prosperity or shrink to deficit (§2276).
- **Circulation:** Rapid, sometimes very rapid class-circulation — maximum under contemporary speculators (§2276).
- **Degeneration:** May become government by shrewd but cowardly individuals easily overthrown by violence (Greek democracies; fall of Roman/Venetian republics) (§2276).
- **Residue failure:** Speculator governments deficient in Class II and fail to exploit subjects’ [[group-persistence]] (§2454 n.).
- **Parallel to late Rome:** Modern demagogic plutocracy turns political “freedom” and anarchy into profits, as imperial monopolies did (§§2548–2550).

## Applications

- Analysis of protectionism, budget manipulation, party finance as S–politician–worker coalitions fleecing savers ([[speculators-and-rentiers]]).
- Link to [[circulation-of-elites]] under pluto-demagogic rule.
- Contrast with type-I force regimes and mixed I+II-b forms (§§2277–2278).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Polemical tone toward “democracy” as label — not a neutral constitutional typology.
- Empirical identification of “speculators” is impressionistic.
- Authoritarian afterlives of the critique are reception politics.
