# Performance Expectations

**Performance expectations** are anticipations of how useful or competent self and other will be at a collective task. In [[expectation-states-theory]], they are formed from salient status information (including gender status beliefs) and drive the power-and-prestige order of the group.

## Definition as Coined

Berger and colleagues treat performance expectations as often nonconscious organizers of who gets chances to perform, whose ideas are evaluated positively, and who influences decisions. Ridgeway applies them to gendered workplaces and homes: when gender is salient, status beliefs bias performance expectations and thus evaluation, influence, and self-assessment.

## Semantic History

Technical term of the expectation-states program; in popular speech "expectations" can mean norms or hopes — here the meaning is specifically **anticipated task competence/contribution** shaping interaction.

## Operationalizations

Laboratory measures of influence, speech, evaluation ratings; scope conditions of task and collective orientation. Gender applications use both experiments and organizational outcome patterns (Ridgeway 2011 Ch 3–4).

## Applications

Leadership double binds, glass-ceiling evaluation bias, maternal-wall competence penalties, household task competence assumptions.

## Critiques and Limitations

Depends on scope conditions; may understate identity, resistance, or institutional rule systems that constrain expectation effects. Lab-to-organization generalization is an ongoing research problem.
