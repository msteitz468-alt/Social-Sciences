# Costly Signaling (Religion)

## Definition as Coined

CSR adaptationist strand (White 2021): **costly signaling** (and related credibility-enhancing displays, CREDs) treats demanding religious practices as hard-to-fake signals of group commitment that support cooperation and deter free-riders.

## Semantic History

From behavioral ecology into religion (Sosis & Bressler commune longevity, etc.); competes with pure by-product accounts of ritual.

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic fieldwork, field experiments, economic games, historical statistics (White method table: extreme ritual alone often insufficient; with regular attendance more effective).

## Applications

Extreme rituals, initiation, asceticism; group survival hypotheses.

## Critiques and Limitations

Not all costly acts are signals; costs may be by-products of other motivations; free-rider problem underdetermines specific ritual forms.
