# Status to Contract

[[maine-henry-sumner|Henry Sumner Maine]]'s formula (*[[maine-ancient-law-1861|Ancient Law]]*, 1861, Ch. V):

> "the movement of the progressive societies has hitherto been a movement from Status to Contract."

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Maine 1861.)* **Status** = personal conditions in the Law of Persons derived from powers and privileges anciently residing in the Family — not conditions that are immediate or remote results of agreement. **Contract** = capacity of the individual to enter independent agreements with strangers to the family-group by which he is legally bound.

Progressive legal history is the gradual dissolution of family dependency and growth of individual obligation: slave, woman under perpetual tutelage, and son under *patria potestas* move toward contractual capacity. Illustrations run through Ch. VI–X (wills, property, contract, delict).

## Operationalizations

Not quantified. Demonstrated via Roman family law (*patria potestas*, agnation, perpetual tutelage of women), testamentary history, joint→private property, and the late rise of bare promissory contract.

## Applications

Core of *Ancient Law*; textbook private-law history; modernization and individualization narratives (often loosely).

## Critiques and Limitations

Applies to “**progressive** societies” as Maine defined them — ethnocentric progressive/stationary binary; “hitherto” is historical, not predictive law. Patriarchal starting point contested ([[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]). Do not equate with Spencer’s militant/industrial polarity or market liberalism as ideology.
