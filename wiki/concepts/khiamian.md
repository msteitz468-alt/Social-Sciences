# Khiamian

Etic archaeological phase/complex of the Levant bridging late [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] foragers and PPNA village groups. Named from **el-Khiam** projectile points. In [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]]'s sequence (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Birth of the Gods]]* 2000), the Khiamian is the horizon of the **[[revolution-of-symbols|revolution of symbols]]** (~9500 BC): new dominant symbols (woman/Goddess, bull) among hunter-gatherers still without a farming economy.

## Definition as Coined

Lithic-typological and culture-historical unit: assemblages with el-Khiam points and related forms, positioned after Natufian and before or at the onset of PPNA regional cultures (Sultanian, Aswadian, Mureybetian). Absolute dates and boundaries vary by author and calibration scheme — always date-stamp.

## Semantic History

- **Lithic type → phase:** from type-fossil points to a cultural phase in Levantine sequences.
- **Cauvin 1994/2000:** loads the phase with religious-symbolic primacy — birth of divinities before agriculture.
- **Later literature:** may treat Khiamian as short transitional PPNA-related horizon; symbolic package not universally accepted as religion.

## Operationalizations

Points typology; site stratigraphy (e.g. continuity claims at [[mureybet]]); presence/absence of morphological domestication; symbolic finds (figurines, bucrania).

## Applications

Cauvin's hinge between Natufian villages and first farmers; target for debates on whether ideology leads economy.

## Critiques and Limitations

Phase integrity, duration, and geographic scope contested; equating figurines with goddess religion is interpretive; four-way non-identity — Khiamian is not a people, language, or genetic population.
