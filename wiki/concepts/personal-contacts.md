# Personal Contacts (Job Finding)

Analytic category for **job information or placement obtained through a person the job-seeker already knows**, as opposed to formal intermediaries (advertisements, public/private agencies) or direct application to an employer not previously known. In *[[granovetter-getting-a-job-1974|Getting a Job]]*, Granovetter made this the central empirical object and split "informal" methods used by labor economists into **personal contacts** vs **direct application**.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Granovetter 1974, building on labor-economics trichotomies.)* **Formal means** = ads, public/private agencies (incl. executive search), university/professional placement — impersonal intermediary. **Personal contact** = someone known in a context originally *unrelated to job search* who informed the respondent or recommended him. **Direct application** = approaching a firm without formal or personal intermediary and without a prior specific-opening tip. Granovetter stressed that conflating contacts with direct application under "informal" obscures the sociological mechanism.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Labor economics, 1930s–60s | "Informal" methods (friends/relatives + direct application) vs formal agencies/ads |
| Granovetter GAJ 1974 | Clean trichotomy; personal contacts as network-mediated information flow |
| SWT 1973 / later networks | Contacts further stratified by [[tie-strength]] (weak vs strong) and work vs family-social origin |
| Social-capital era | Often folded into network [[social-capital]] / [[bridging-social-capital]] measures |
| GAJ Afterword 1994 | Nonsearch treated as near-proxy for contact matching; inequality agenda (returns to contacts by race/gender) |

## Operationalizations

GAJ interview and mail instruments: how respondent first heard of the job; relationship to informant; frequency of contact; work vs family-social origin; chain length to employer; whether contact "put in a good word." Appendix B: conservative formal coding; catalytic mutual friends not counted as chain links if parties already linked (biases chains short).

**Newton PTM headline rates (attributed, N≈282):** contacts ~56%; formal 18.8%; direct 18.8%. Among contact-users (N=153): work 68.7% / family-social 31.4%. Weak-frequency skew among interview contact-users (N=54): often 16.7% / occasional 55.6% / rare 27.8%.

## Applications

Primary evidence base for [[granovetter-getting-a-job-study]] and the labor-market half of [[strength-of-weak-ties-theory]]; comparative citations to blue-collar and academic labor markets in GAJ Introduction and Ch. 9; Afterword 1994 cross-national prevalence review; feeds [[network-inequality]] when contacts are unequally held or yield unequal returns.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Category collapses heterogeneous influence (mere tip vs sponsorship).
- Self-report of "how found" can misattribute mixed searches.
- Male PTM Newton rates are not universal base rates; later surveys show wide range by sample definition and nonsearch handling.
- Wage advantage of contact method often attenuates under multivariate controls (Afterword 1994 review).
