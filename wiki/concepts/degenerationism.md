# Degenerationism

**Degenerationism** is the doctrine that "savage" peoples are the degenerate descendants of an originally higher or divinely instructed condition — that human history's fundamental movement is downward (fall, decline) rather than upward (progress). In [[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Stocking's account]] it is the principal rival to progressive [[social-evolutionism|developmentalism]] in Victorian anthropology, and the two doctrines shared a decisive common premise: the essential [[psychic-unity-of-mankind|psychic unity]] of humankind. Claims here are recorded in **attributed** voice.

## Definition as Coined

The framework is rooted in biblical anthropology — the Genesis sequence of Creation, Fall, Flood, and the Tower of Babel — whose principle of temporal change was degenerationist and whose privileged evidence was linguistic relationship. On an antediluvian short chronology, archaeological evidence of highly civilized ancient societies (Egypt, Nineveh) left little time for savages to have *risen* but ample time for them to have *fallen* from a higher state, sustaining a degenerationist reading of history. Stocking notes (attributed) that by the early nineteenth century the concept had been "transvalued": earlier associated with a European fall from primitive cultural simplicity, it came to mean a savage fall from primitive religious grace.

## Semantic History

- **Evangelical and providentialist forms.** John Bird Sumner's *Treatise on the Records of the Creation* held that savagery resulted from post-Flood degeneration, producing (in Stocking's phrase) a "diverging ladder of degeneration" rather than a single ascending one. The colonial administrator [[unknown|Sir George Grey]] and the missionary Thomas Williams read the peoples they documented (Australian Aboriginal peoples; Fijians) in a Christian-degenerationist register — savagery as "moral evolution in reverse."
- **The set-piece debate.** In the 1860s **Archbishop Richard Whately** and the **Duke of Argyll** pressed the degenerationist challenge against the evolutionists: the "impossibility of the last step of all — the advance of savage man into the civilized, without external help." By insisting savages were "the degenerate descendants of far superior ancestors," they denied evolutionists the use of living savages as stand-ins for the prehistoric "missing link." Stocking argues (attributed) that [[lubbock-john|Lubbock]]'s shift toward a more rationalistic, favourable image of the savage between 1865 and 1870 was driven by the need to answer these critics and establish the possibility of *independent* savage progress.
- **Linguistic degenerationism.** Early comparative philology retained a degenerationist model of language change (languages decaying from older, structurally "more perfect" forms); [[muller-friedrich-max|Max Müller]]'s critique of evolutionary assumptions had, in Stocking's phrase, an "ambiguous double thrust" — partly anticipating Boas, partly echoing Christian degenerationism.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct; a historical-interpretive framework applied to ethnographic and archaeological data.

## Applications

Degenerationism supplied the theological alternative to evolutionary developmentalism and shaped colonial readings of "declining" peoples — Stocking shows how post-Darwinian degenerationist explanation of the Tasmanians' extermination (attributing it to their own "licentiousness") converted extermination into "decline," lightening the settlers' moral burden.

## Lubbock's anti-degenerationism (*Pre-Historic Times*)

*(Attributed — Lubbock 1872.)* In ethnographic and concluding chapters Lubbock denies general degradation of savages: simple arts once gained are hard to lose (citing Tylor); lowest living peoples are at least as advanced as ancestral humans at peopling; European contact can cause decline. Index: "Degradation of savage tribes denied." This is the **progressivist** pole opposite Whately/Argyll degenerationism Stocking emphasizes — not a refutation of all race hierarchy (Lubbock still ranks peoples). See [[lubbock-prehistoric-times-1872]], [[lubbock-john]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Evolutionists (Tylor, Lubbock) countered that independent savage progress was demonstrable and that survivals evidenced ascent from lower stages (see [[survivals]], [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]). Stocking's structural point (attributed): degenerationism and progressivism were mirror-image applications of the same premise of human psychic likeness — each explained apparently irrational custom by reference to a shared human nature, one falling, the other rising.
