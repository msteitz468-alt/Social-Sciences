# Four Wishes

## Definition as Coined

In the Methodological Note to *[[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-1918|The Polish Peasant]]* Vol I, Thomas and Znaniecki enumerate **"general patterns of wishes"** (attributed):

1. **Desire for new experience** — "for fresh stimulations."
2. **Desire for recognition** — "including, for example, sexual response and general social appreciation," secured by devices "ranging from the display of ornament to the demonstration of worth through scientific attainment."
3. **Desire for mastery**, or the **"will to power"** — "exemplified by ownership, domestic tyranny," etc., "capable of being sublimated to laudable ambition."
4. **Desire for security** — "based on the instinct of fear and exemplified negatively by the wretchedness of the individual in perpetual solitude or under social taboo."

**Semantic caution.** Many textbooks later list **new experience, security, response, and recognition** (with *response* replacing *mastery*). That received quartet is **not** identical to the Vol I Methodological Note list above. Keep the 1918 four (including mastery/will to power) distinct from later pedagogical reformulations; do not silently equate "response" with a fourth Vol I wish separate from recognition.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Polish Peasant 1918 | Motive scheme within Methodological Note for social psychology and social control |
| Interwar textbooks | Fixed "four wishes" teaching device |
| Mid-century critique | Seen as arbitrary, incomplete, or too biological-instinctual |
| Later SI / motive vocabularies | Largely replaced by vocabularies of identity, career, and situational meaning |

## Operationalizations

Used as a **coding heuristic** for life-histories and letters (which wish is frustrated or satisfied?). Not a validated scale in the 1918 work.

## Applications

- Reading immigrant letters for recognition and security under separation.
- Delinquency and "unadjusted" personality work in Thomas's later books (not yet ingested).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Arbitrary enumeration** — critics ask why four, and why these four.
- **Instinct residual** — security tied to fear, etc., can read as diluted instinct theory.
- **Absorbed / declining** as a live research tool; historically important for Chicago social psychology's teaching history.
