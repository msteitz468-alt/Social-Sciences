# Isolation Index

## Definition as Coined

Measure of **exposure/isolation**: the extent to which group members live in neighborhoods where they are the majority or rarely encounter the other group. In *[[massey-denton-american-apartheid-1993|American Apartheid]]*, black isolation is the average percentage black in the neighborhood of the average black resident — high values mean blacks rarely share neighborhoods with whites.

## Semantic History

Part of the exposure family of segregation measures; second dimension in [[hypersegregation]] (Massey & Denton).

## Operationalizations

Tract-based P*-style isolation in 1980 metro comparisons; historical series show black isolation rising with ghetto consolidation (Ch 2–3, attributed).

## Applications

Distinguishes even-but-exposed patterns from isolation; central to claims that black spatial isolation is historically unique in U.S. cities.

## Critiques and Limitations

Sensitive to group relative size; high isolation can partly reflect large minority share even with moderate unevenness.
