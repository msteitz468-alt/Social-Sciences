# Arts of Noticing

## Definition as Coined

In *[[tsing-mushroom-2015|The Mushroom at the End of the World]]* (Ch. 1), [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Tsing]] advances **arts of noticing** as the skills needed to see collaborative survival in damaged landscapes — against stories that only register progress or terminal ruin. Listening to and telling a "rush of stories" is presented as a method (even a science) whose object is contaminated diversity and whose unit of analysis is the indeterminate encounter *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Semantic History

Builds on multi-species and feminist STS attention practices; pairs with Tsing's critique of **scalability** — the ability of a project to expand without changing its framing assumptions — which modern science and progress narratives treat as a hallmark of knowledge and business *(Tsing 2015)*. Related earlier: [[ethnography-of-global-connection]] in *Friction*.

## Operationalizations

- Attend to matsutake, pickers, disturbance histories, and awkward collaborations that job/environment dualisms miss.
- Refuse nested, expandable scales; follow interrupting geographies and tempos.
- Practice natural history alongside ethnography; revitalize observation against algorithmic economy/ecology *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Applications

- [[tsing-mushroom-study]] · Method: [[multi-species-ethnography]]
- Contrast: plantation/scalability model vs matsutake "anti-plantations" *(Tsing 2015)*

## Critiques and Limitations

Can be read as soft method without political teeth; Tsing pairs noticing with [[salvage-accumulation]] analysis of how wealth is amassed. Arts of noticing are "archaic" from the standpoint of scalable science precisely because they cannot scale up without changing questions *(Tsing 2015)*.
