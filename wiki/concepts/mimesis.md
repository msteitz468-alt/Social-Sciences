# Mimesis

## Definition as Coined

In [[horkheimer-max|Horkheimer]] and [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]]'s usage,
**mimesis** is the imitative, assimilative relation to nature — becoming *like*
the object, losing oneself in it — as opposed to the instrumental relation that
masters the object by distancing itself from it. They define it against science:
"like science, magic pursues aims, but seeks to achieve them by mimesis—not by
progressively distancing itself from the object... The relation is one not of
intention but of relatedness" (attributed). Where the magician imitates the demon,
science reduces the world to fungible, interchangeable specimens: "representation
is exchanged for the fungible—universal interchangeability."

The concept's key claims (all attributed):

- **The taboo on mimesis.** Civilization advances by prohibiting the mimetic
  impulse: "civilization has replaced the organic adaptation to others and mimetic
  behavior proper, by organized control of mimesis, in the magical phase; and,
  finally, by rational practice, by work." "Uncontrolled mimesis is outlawed" —
  from "the religious prohibition on images" to "the social banishment of actors
  and gypsies."
- **Reason as mimesis unto death.** The instrumental reason that supplants mimesis
  is itself a mimesis — the subject masters rigid, despiritualized nature by
  imitating its rigidity: "**the ratio which supplants mimesis is not simply its
  counterpart. It is itself mimesis: mimesis unto death**." The self hardens
  itself into a thing to master a world of things; "the mathematical formula is
  regression handled consciously."
- **Art as preserved mimesis.** Art retains "the magic heritage" precisely by
  renouncing literal magical efficacy — a residue of the mimetic relation
  surviving inside instrumental civilization.
- **False projection as failed mimesis.** In the anti-Semitism excursus, mimesis
  is the counterpart of anti-Semitism's [[false-projection]]: "anti-Semitism is
  based on a false projection... it is probably the morbid expression of repressed
  mimesis." Fascist ritual (uniforms, drums, the leader's gestures) is "the mimesis
  of mimesis" — the organized, permitted release of the mimetic impulse
  civilization forbids, and "there is no anti-Semite who does not basically want to
  imitate his mental image of a Jew."

## Semantic History

Mimesis is a **migrated term**: an ancient aesthetic concept (Plato's suspicion of
imitative art; Aristotle's *mimēsis* as the basis of poetry and catharsis)
reworked by Adorno and Horkheimer into an anthropological and epistemological
category naming a whole pre-instrumental mode of relating to nature. In Adorno's
later work (*Aesthetic Theory*, *Negative Dialectics*) mimesis becomes the name for
what "identity thinking" represses and what the modernist artwork keeps alive — a
cognition that does not dominate its object. The term's two lives (aesthetic
representation; assimilative relation to nature) should be kept distinct: this page
records the *Dialectic of Enlightenment* sense.

## Operationalizations

None; the concept is philosophical and anthropological, drawing on the ethnology of
magic and mana (see [[domination-of-nature]]) and on psychoanalysis (mimicry, the
mimetic impulse in projection).

## Applications

Mimesis is the repressed other of [[instrumental-reason]] within the
[[dialectic-of-enlightenment]]; it grounds the reading of the
[[domination-of-nature]] as self-domination and supplies the key to
[[false-projection]] in the anti-Semitism excursus. It became a load-bearing term
in Adorno's aesthetics and in later critical-theory and anthropology of the senses.

## Critiques and Limitations

Recorded in attributed voice: critics find the concept elusive and shifting —
alternately an anthropological stage, an epistemic mode, an aesthetic capacity, and
a psychoanalytic impulse — and question whether so plastic a term can carry the
weight the argument places on it. Its romantic overtones (a lost, benign relation
to nature) sit awkwardly with the authors' refusal of any simple return to nature.
Defenders read it as a deliberately non-conceptual placeholder for what conceptual
domination excludes.
