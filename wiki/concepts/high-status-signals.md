# High-Status Signals

## Definition as Coined

[[lamont-michele|Michèle Lamont]] (1992) uses **high-status signals** for the keys to evaluative distinctions — the concrete traits people treat as proof of moral, socioeconomic, or cultural worth. Against survey research that builds cultivated dispositions into the instrument a priori, she argues open-ended interviews let respondents define which signals matter most. The comparative finding is relative **salience**, not a single universal list.

Examples from *[[lamont-money-morals-manners-1992|Money, Morals, and Manners]]* (attributed):

- **Moral:** honesty, sincerity, non-phoniness, consideration, work ethic, intellectual honesty, anti-*salaud* solidarity.
- **Socioeconomic:** income, material “progress,” power, independence, *patrimoine*, professional rank.
- **Cultural:** refinement, intelligence, *sens critique*, eloquence, expertise, pragmatism, cosmopolitanism, high-culture familiarity, self-actualization.

## Semantic History

Related to Weberian status honor, Goffmanian presentation, Kanter’s homosocial trust signals, and Bourdieuian [[cultural-capital]] / [[distinction]]. Lamont’s move is to treat moral and socioeconomic signals as **coequal** research objects with cultural refinement, and to treat which signals dominate as an empirical question varying by nation and occupation.

## Operationalizations

Interview prompts about people above/below self, preferred associates, workplace-valued traits, child-rearing values; inductive assignment to moral / socioeconomic / cultural standards; relational ranking. Workplace signals matter because gatekeepers frame others’ careers via informal inclusion and evaluation (attributed).

## Applications

[[lamont-money-morals-manners-study]]; Ch. 7 argument that many valued signals are class-skewed, so their use under meritocracy contributes to [[cultural-reproduction|reproduction]] without conscious class warfare.

## Critiques and Limitations

Self-reported evaluative talk ≠ observed hiring decisions; multi-loading signals blur type boundaries; sample-limited (white male UMC) list of signals should not be universalized in wiki voice.
