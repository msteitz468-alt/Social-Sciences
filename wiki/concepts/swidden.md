# Swidden

## Definition as Coined

In *[[geertz-agricultural-involution-1963|Agricultural Involution]]*, [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] treats **swidden** (shifting cultivation / slash-and-burn) as one of two major Indonesian agricultural **ecosystems**. It is land-extensive, polycultural, and designed as a “canny imitation” of tropical forest structure; nutrient cycling is largely plant-to-plant via forest fallow rather than via irrigated water medium.

## Semantic History

Standard ethnographic label (also shifting cultivation, slash-and-burn). Geertz embeds it in Stewardian systems comparison for **Outer Indonesia**, not a mere technique list. Harold Conklin’s Hanunóo work is a classic descriptive base for both Geertz and later political-ecological readings.

In *[[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-2009|The Art of Not Being Governed]]* (2009), [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]] (acknowledging Conklin and Michael Dove) re-reads swidden as **[[escape-agriculture]]**: a political-ecological choice that disperses population, diversifies crops, and frustrates state cadastre and grain seizure — often a **secondary adaptation** in the shadow of padi states, not an evolutionary stage “before” irrigated rice.

## Operationalizations

### Ecosystem type (Geertz via Conklin)

- Architecture of the plot (edge associations, stacked canopy, multi-crop sequence over ~two years)
- Closed-cover ideal; maladaptation when fallow shortens or cover breaks
- Population response: **dispersive** and relatively **inelastic** density ceilings — growth met by opening new land, not indefinite labor intensification on the same plot
- Contrast: Inner Indonesia [[sawah]] concentrates population

### Escape value vs state space (Scott 2009)

- **Appropriation-resistant:** mobile plots, polyculture, staggered harvests, forest cover — hard to measure, tax, and confiscate compared with permanent irrigated fields of simultaneous aboveground grain ([[legibility]]).
- **Political gradient (Jonsson via Scott):** foraging (most appropriation-proof) → swidden (resistant; temporary hierarchy possible) → fixed-field wet-rice (appropriable, hierarchical settlements).
- **Dual program:** supports both **[[state-evasion]]** (stay beyond valley reach) and **state prevention** (no concentrated surplus nerve-center for indigenous state crystallization).
- **Enclosure target:** modern colonial and postcolonial states aim to settle swiddeners, close commons, and replace swidden with legible cash monocrops or permanent fields — part of the “last great enclosure.”
- Pairs with roots/tubers, open common property, and ridge-top dispersal in Scott’s highland [[nonstate-spaces]] / [[zomia]] package.

## Applications

- Inner vs Outer Indonesia dualism; *[[geertz-agricultural-involution-study|Agricultural Involution]]*; tropical agricultural ecology (Geertz).
- Highland mainland SE Asia / [[zomia]] political ecology; *[[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-study|The Art of Not Being Governed]]* (Scott 2009).
- Comparative [[state-evasion]] and secondary primitivism arguments (Clastres-adjacent via Scott).
- **Achuar pioneering swidden** (Descola 1986/1994): *[[descola-in-the-society-of-nature-1994|In the Society of Nature]]* documents [[achuar]] *aja* clearings — slash-and-burn, ~60 cultigens/>150 varieties, manioc dominance, sexual division of labor, large unused manioc potential, biotope contrasts — with gardening magic (Nunkui) constitutive of practice ([[descola-society-of-nature-study|study]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Covers diverse practices; not all Outer Island agriculture is pure swidden; colonial and smallholder cash crops (rubber, tobacco, opium in some highland zones) overlay the ideal type.
- **Scott’s escape reading is attributed and contested:** not every swidden system is best explained as state-flight; ecological constraint, crop repertoire, and cultural preference remain alternative or complementary accounts. Functionalist over-reading of “escape value” is a standing critique of the 2009 program.
- Swidden can support temporary hierarchy and highland polities; it is appropriation-*resistant*, not appropriation-*impossible*.
