# Philosophy of Praxis

> [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]]'s prison name for Marxism in Part III of *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971|Selections from the Prison Notebooks]]*. **Attributed voice** throughout. Double status: **censorship euphemism** and **substantive reconception**.

## Definition as Coined

Editors (Hoare) note: the term entered Italy via **Antonio Labriola** (nexus of theoretical and practical activity; unity of philosophy and history). Gramsci uses it partly to deceive the prison censor, partly for its own theoretical sake. He rejects reducing Marxism to a sociology of "laws" (polemic on Bukharin's *Popular Manual*) or to a subordinate application of mechanical materialism or of Crocean idealism.

Load-bearing claims (attributed to Gramsci):

- Philosophy of praxis is **"sufficient unto itself"** — contains elements for a total conception of the world and an integral practical organisation of society (renewed "orthodoxy" as self-sufficiency, not dogmatic loyalty to theses).
- It is **absolute historicism**: "the absolute secularisation and earthliness of thought, an absolute humanism of history." Accent in "historical materialism" falls on **historical**, not on metaphysical "matter."
- It crowns a cultural past (Renaissance and Reformation, German philosophy, French Revolution, classical economics…): "a philosophy which is also politics, and a politics which is also philosophy" — nexus "Protestant Reformation plus French Revolution."
- Unity of elements: economics (value), philosophy (**praxis = relationship between human will/superstructure and economic structure**), politics (State ↔ [[civil-society]]).
- Against "primitive infantilism": every political/ideological fluctuation is **not** an immediate expression of structure.
- Relation to masses: philosophy of praxis works to renovate [[common-sense|common sense]] (*senso comune*), raising it toward critical "good sense," not to replace mass faith with pedantic systems alone.

## Operationalizations

- **Two tasks:** combat refined modern ideologies to constitute independent intellectuals; educate popular masses still in "medieval" culture — the second absorbs enormous strength (Gramsci).
- **Anti-Manual test:** does a presentation start from common sense and dialectic, or from positivist sociology + metaphysical matter?
- **Catharsis:** passage from economic-corporate to ethico-political moment (hegemony) as philosophical event.
- **Prediction:** not Laplacean social science; prediction as struggle and collective will (critique of statistical fatalism).

## Applications

- Part III of *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971]]* (Study of Philosophy; Problems of Marxism).
- Bridge concept for placing Gramsci in [[western-marxism]] beside [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]]'s method/totality (parallel anti-scientism; different apparatus).
- Critique of economism inside Marxist movements.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Euphemism status invites over-reading every "praxis" as deep theory.
- Absolute historicism accused (by structuralist and analytical Marxists) of relativism; Gramsci's own caution: historicising the philosophy of praxis itself is politically difficult without shaking action-needed convictions.
- Incomplete vs full critical edition (Gerratana 1975) — this wiki uses 1971 selection.
- Idealism-charge and materialism-charge both recur in the reception; record as positions, do not adjudicate in wiki voice.
