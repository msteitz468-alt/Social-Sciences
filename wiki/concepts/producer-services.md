# Producer Services

Intermediate services produced primarily as inputs to other firms and organizations — legal, accounting, management consulting, advertising, finance, insurance, real estate, and related business services — as opposed to consumer services sold mainly to households. In [[global-city-theory]], the advanced producer-services complex is the key agglomeration that specifies the production advantage of the [[global-city|global city]].

## Definition as Coined

Standard economic/urban category that Sassen elevates to a theoretical pivot: the specialized service firms that produce the inputs required to manage global production networks and markets.

## Semantic History

Grew with post-industrial and service-economy debates (cf. Bell); in global-cities research becomes the operational core of "command and control" infrastructure rather than a residual tertiary sector.

## Operationalizations

SIC/industry shares for business services and FIRE; location quotients by city; employment and earnings distributions within producer services (Sassen stresses that these sectors contain both high- and low-wage jobs).

## Applications

Comparative location of producer services in New York, London, Tokyo and national urban hierarchies (*[[sassen-global-city-2001|The Global City]]* Parts Two–Three).

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundary of "producer" vs consumer services is fuzzy (tourism, some retail); national classification systems differ; risk of treating the whole FIRE sector as high-skill when internal job structures are polarized.
