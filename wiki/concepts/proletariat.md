# Proletariat

## Definition as Coined

In the [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] (1848),
[[marx-karl|Marx]] and [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] use **proletariat** for "the modern working
class — a class of labourers, who live only so long as they find work, and who find work only
so long as their labour increases capital." Owning no [[mode-of-production|means of
production]], proletarians "must sell themselves piece-meal, are a commodity, like every other
article of commerce," and so are exposed "to all the fluctuations of the market." The
[[bourgeoisie|bourgeoisie]] both creates the proletariat and cannot exist without it; the
proletariat is "its special and essential product."

## Semantic History

The word revives Latin *proletarius* (the propertyless citizen counted only for offspring).
The *Manifesto* gives it a developmental arc: the proletariat begins as "an incoherent mass
scattered over the whole country, and broken up by their mutual competition," and through
concentration in factories, improved communications, and repeated struggle grows into
"combinations (Trades Unions)," then "a class, and consequently... a political party" — "the
self-conscious, independent movement of the immense majority." This trajectory from a class
merely *in* itself to a class *for* itself became a central concept in later Marxism. The
*[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|Eighteenth Brumaire]]* (1852) narrates the June 1848 defeat
of the isolated Paris proletariat and its subsequent retreat to the background of the
revolutionary stage — distinct from the [[lumpenproletariat|slum-proletariat]] base of
Bonaparte. Modern usage stretched the term across manual and, contested, white-collar and
service labour, and critics dispute where its boundaries fall.

## Operationalizations

The *Manifesto* marks the proletariat by propertylessness and dependence on wage-labour;
Engels's 1888 note (outside the ingested body) fixes it as the class of modern wage-labourers
who have no means of production of their own. Its formation is tracked through observable
militancy — machine-breaking, riots, unionization, the ten-hours' bill — escalating toward a
"national struggle between classes."

## Applications

The revolutionary subject of [[historical-materialism|historical materialism]]; half of the
Manifesto's two-class model; foundational to labour sociology and to theories of collective
action and class formation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Two *Manifesto* predictions about the proletariat are heavily contested: **immiseration** —
"the modern labourer... sinks deeper and deeper below the conditions of existence of his own
class" and "becomes a pauper" — which real-wage growth in the industrial economies did not
bear out; and its destined role as unified revolutionary agent of "the immense majority,"
undercut by reformist labour politics, national loyalties (contra "the working men have no
country"), and internal stratification of the working class. Claims are recorded as the
authors' attributed positions.
