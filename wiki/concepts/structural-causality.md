# Structural Causality

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* Part Four, chapter “Marx’s Immense Theoretical Revolution,” [[althusser-louis|Althusser]] argues that Marx’s scientific practice poses an unprecedented problem: **how a structure determines its elements** — the **effectivity of a structure on its elements** — for which classical philosophy lacked concepts. He names the answer **structural causality**. Its least metaphorical index in Marx is *Darstellung* (presentation/exposition): “the key epistemological concept of the whole Marxist theory of value, the concept whose object is precisely to designate the mode of presence of the structure in its effects, and therefore to designate structural causality itself.” Related formulations in the volume and its reception: **metonymic causality** (structure effective as an **absent cause** — present only in effects); Spinozist **immanent cause** (“the structure is immanent in its effects… the whole existence of the structure consists of its effects… nothing outside its effects”).

## Semantic History

Balibar’s Presentation lists “structural causality” as the volume’s recasting of dialectics, linked to Freudian concepts beyond the clinic. In Part One Althusser already treats *Darstellung* and its avatars as Marx’s answer to a question nowhere posed — effectivity of structure on elements — residual Hegelian forms in *Capital* being presence of that absence, not mere “flirtation.” Classical philosophy, Althusser claims, offered only two systems: (1) **mechanistic/transitive** Cartesian causality (cannot think whole→elements without distortion); (2) **Leibnizian expression** (dominating Hegel) — elements as phenomenal expressions of an inner essence (*pars totalis*), presupposing a spiritual whole, not a structure. Structural causality is neither. Surplus-value illustrates: not a measurable thing but “the concept of a relationship… visible and measurable only in its ‘effects’… present there, as a structure, in its determinate absence.” Theatrical metaphor: an **authorless theatre** whose spectators are first forced to be actors under a script they did not write. Post-1965 reception often equates the term with Althusser’s entire anti-humanist apparatus; in the text it is the specific answer to the causality problem opened by the object of *Capital*.

## Operationalizations

- **Presence in effects only.** Structure is not an exterior essence acting from outside; absence of the cause is the form of its interiority in effects.
- **Regional in global structure.** Economic phenomena determined by a regional structure inscribed in the global structure of a [[mode-of-production]] — complex deep space, not planar transitive space of classical political economy.
- **Links to last instance.** Works with [[structure-in-dominance]] and determination in the last instance: hierarchy of effectivity of a dominant structure over subordinate structures, not expressive co-presence.
- **Balibar’s uptake.** Classes as [[bearer-trager|bearers (*Träger*)]] of relations; combination of two connections (property / real appropriation) as the structure whose effectivity is at stake.

## Applications

- Theory of value and surplus-value as structure present only in effects.
- Critique of empiricist and historicist readings of *Capital*’s object (“ideal average” as concept of the real *Kerngestalt*, not statistical mean of England).
- Bridge from *For Marx*’s [[overdetermination]] of contradiction to the causality of the structured whole.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Theoreticism charge: causality without subjects evacuates politics and agency ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]).
- Metaphorical residue (theatre, machinery, metonymy) acknowledged by Althusser as search for an adequate concept Marx himself lacked.
- Spinoza as “only theoretician” who dared the problem is a polemical genealogy, not a historical-philological claim about Marx’s sources.
- Anthropological wing (Godelier et al.) inherits structural determination of instances; culturalist critics (e.g. Sahlins) treat last-instance economic structure as practical-reason deep structure.
