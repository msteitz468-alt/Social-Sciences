# Entanglement

## Definition as Coined

In *[[hodder-entangled-2012|Entangled]]* (2012), [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]] defines **entanglement** as the open-ended set of dependences and dependencies between humans and things, formalized as:

> Entanglement = (HT) + (TT) + (TH) + (HH)

— human–thing, thing–thing, thing–human, and human–human relations. The **defining aspect** is a double bind: humans depend on things that depend on humans. Entanglement is the **dialectic** of enabling **[[dependence-dependency|dependence]]** (reliance, contingency) and constraining **dependency** (entrapment). Program page: [[entanglement-theory]].

Hodder differentiates his term from near-neighbors: actor-networks, Renfrew’s material engagement, Heidegger’s equipmental totality, *chaînes opératoires*, and behavioral chains — related tools that do not by themselves capture sticky **dependency** and historical debt.

## Semantic History

| Strand | Contribution (as positioned by Hodder 2012) |
|---|---|
| Nicholas Thomas (1991) and colonial anthropology | “Entanglements” of objects in empire — HT-heavy, often weak on object chemistry |
| Appadurai / Kopytoff social life and [[cultural-biography-of-things]] | Things-in-motion and biographical careers |
| Materiality studies (Miller, Meskell, Keane, etc.) | Co-production of things and society — still often anthropocentric |
| Latour ANT / Ingold meshwork | Symmetry and flows; Hodder argues still underplay entrapment |
| Hodder 2012 | Program definition: formula + dependence/dependency dialectic + archaeological tracing |

Earlier post-processual Hodder emphasized meaningfully constituted material culture; *Entangled* shifts emphasis to **object-side** care without abandoning social interpretation.

## Operationalizations

- **Tanglegrams** — graphic maps of heterogeneous strings (e.g. Çatalhöyük brick/clay sequences).
- **Operational / behavioral chains** and life-history sequences — procurement through discard ([[life-history-method]]).
- **Type sequences and legacies** — how traits persist when entanglements shift.
- **Degrees** — length of links; integration (one pin drops the mainsail) vs redundancy; material vs semiotic entanglement (a pebble cache may be socially “thick” despite simple materials).

Entanglements are **not** cultures or closed systems: they may be partial, local, or hyper-connected; links are open-ended.

## Applications

- [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] clay, plaster, brick, cattle, pottery, houses (Hodder 2012).
- Plant and animal domestication as co-dependency traps.
- Modern analogies (cars, grids, sugar) for invisible long strings and forgetness.
- Selection and persistence of traits via [[fittingness]] within webs (Ch. 7).

## Critiques and Limitations

Hodder’s own Ch. 10 “mop-up”: HH dependence and power relatively underplayed; “objective” tanglegram vs situated judgment of failure; quantification vs contextual mess; definitional slipperiness of “thing.” External critiques of the wider materiality/entanglement literature (beyond this volume) are not yet primary-ingested.
