# Mixtec Codices

## Definition as Coined
The corpus of [[mixtec-culture|Mixtec]] pictorial screenfold manuscripts recording dynastic genealogies, marriages, wars, and rituals of the Postclassic Mixtec kingdoms — the largest body of pre-Conquest dynastic history surviving from the Americas.

## Semantic History
[[caso-alfonso|Alfonso Caso]] demonstrated their Mixtec origin and cracked their genealogical structure (from 1949); [[smith-mary-elizabeth|Mary Elizabeth Smith]] anchored their place signs to real communities, arguing in [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) that Codex Selden comes from Jaltepec in the Nochixtlán Valley and that the codices record regional points of view, not one national history.

## Operationalizations
Art-historical and ethnohistoric analysis: place-sign matching against colonial lienzos and maps; genealogical cross-correlation between manuscripts; correlation with [[archaeological-survey|survey]] and excavation data on Postclassic settlement.

## Applications
Primary evidence for Postclassic Mixtec social stratification and cacicazgo politics (Spores's analyses on [[mixtec-culture]]); the writing system is a semasiographic/pictorial system contrasting with [[zapotec-writing]] and Aztec conventions.

## Critiques and Limitations
Survivorship bias (a handful of manuscripts, elite-authored dynastic propaganda); readings depend on chains of identification that remain contested for several codices.
