# Last Glacial Maximum

## Definition as Coined

The **Last Glacial Maximum (LGM)** is the periodization label for the coldest, ice-volume peak of the most recent glacial cycle. In [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]] the LGM is placed **around 20,000 BC** and frames the book’s starting world: cold, dry, windy conditions; expanded ice sheets and deserts; lowered seas joining landmasses; and human communities concentrated in refugia after abandoning many previously inhabited regions.

## Semantic History

Quaternary science formalized LGM as a global ice-volume and sea-level lowstand concept (marine isotope stage conventions, ice cores, pollen, beetles). Archaeology adopted it as the baseline against which late-glacial warming, the [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]], Holocene onset, and [[post-glacial-recolonization]] are measured. Absolute calendar placement has been refined repeatedly; Mithen’s ~20,000 BC peak and subsequent milestones (ice-sheet melt by ~15,000 BC; strong fluctuations by ~12,000 BC; abrupt warming soon after 10,000 BC into the Holocene) are **as of his 2003 synthesis**, not a permanent lab consensus.

## Operationalizations

Cross-proxy markers used in Mithen’s narrative (environmental evidence class):

- **Ice sheets and glaciers:** northern ice margins; retreat after ~18,000 BC; glaciers gone from northern Germany by ~14,000 BC (as of Mithen 2003).
- **Sea level:** continental shelves exposed (Doggerland; Sundaland; Beringia; Tasmania–Australia–New Guinea join).
- **Vegetation/fauna:** polar desert in northern Europe; southern French/Spanish refugia hunting reindeer, horse, bison; Bordes’s “ice-age Serengeti” framing of French tundra.
- **Palaeotemperature proxies:** e.g. British beetles — southern Britain winters to −16°C and summers ~10°C at LGM (as of Mithen 2003).
- **Human geography:** abandonment of high latitudes; refugia and specialized architectures (e.g. Pushkari mammoth-bone dwellings ~250 km south of glaciers).

Radiocarbon and ice-core chronologies calibrate regional sequences; coastal LGM sites are often lost to post-glacial sea-level rise ([[coastal-adaptation]]).

## Applications

As of [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]]:

- **Global human history baseline:** starting condition for 20,000–5000 BC narrative of farming, towns, and civilization’s “coming of age.”
- **Europe:** polar-desert north; recolonization chronology after 15,000 BC (Housley et al. via Mithen); Bølling/Allerød/Younger Dryas vegetation sequence.
- **Americas peopling:** LGM ice sheets and ice-free corridor timing constrain Clovis-first vs coastal-route models ([[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]], [[clovis-culture]]).
- **Africa/Asia:** LGM coastal foragers (e.g. Tamar Hat Barbary sheep; Elands Bay shore migration); Yangtze and New Guinea environmental flip-flops into Holocene.
- **Megafauna:** LGM and terminal Pleistocene landscapes as backdrop for [[megafaunal-extinctions]] debates.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Date bands differ by region and method** (ice core vs radiocarbon calibration vs local pollen); do not mix uncalibrated and calibrated figures without stating method.
- Mithen’s round BC dates are narrative syntheses of early-2000s literature — supersession expected.
- LGM is an **environmental periodization**, not a culture; human adaptations vary sharply by refugium.
- Coastal and lowland LGM archaeology is **preservation-biased** toward higher ground; absence of coastal sites is not absence of coastal people.
