# Cosmopolitics

## Definition as Coined

**Cosmopolitics**, in Isabelle Stengers’s usage (as mobilized by [[de-la-cadena-marisol|Marisol de la Cadena]] in *[[de-la-cadena-earth-beings-2015|Earth Beings]]* 2015), is not Kantian cosmopolitanism (universal citizenship under shared reason) but a politics that **slows down reasoning**, resists the war on that which does not conform to modern knowledge, and makes room for **divergence** among practices and worlds (attributed via de la Cadena’s Epilogue and Story 3 uptake). De la Cadena’s **Mariano’s cosmopolitics** names the land struggle’s double register: lawyers and state law *and* Ausangate and in-ayllu obligations — more than one politics going on.

## Semantic History

Stengers develops cosmopolitics in philosophy of science and political ecology (e.g. “The Cosmopolitical Proposal,” 2005). Anthropological uptake (Holbraad, de la Cadena, and others) folds it into the [[ontological-turn]]. De la Cadena’s ethnographic version stresses partial connections and equivocation rather than pure theory: Mariano walks between lettered city and earth-beings; Nazario’s tourism/museum work is a later multicultural equivocation of related practices. **Ethnographic cosmopolitics** (Epilogue) is the reflexive claim on anthropology’s own practice.

## Operationalizations

No quantitative index. Analytic use: identify where modern politics translates socionatural collectives into “peasant” or “indigenous culture” while excess (tirakuna, being-in-ayllu speech) continues to operate; refuse to erase that excess as superstition or mere belief.

## Applications

- Story 3: Mariano between lawyers and Ausangate; coloniality of modern politics (Latour Modern Constitution + Quijano) excludes earth-beings from counting.
- Story 7 / Epilogue: munayniyuq power, rondas, and mining demo as cosmopolitical moments; *cosmovivir* / Mariano’s *sumaq kawsay* vs developmentalist Buen Vivir packaging.
- Epilogue program: anthropology as ethnographic cosmopolitics — slow down; cultivate epistemic disconcertment (Verran via de la Cadena); refuse same-ing (e.g. García’s education-against-superstition).
- Broader extractive-industry and indigenous-rights contexts where mountains/rivers as persons challenge liberal environmental law (as of de la Cadena’s framing; political uptake beyond the book not yet primary-sourced here).

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of political quietism if “many worlds” undercuts shared legal struggle; risk of romanticism if divergence is celebrated without power analysis. De la Cadena ties cosmopolitics to concrete land struggle, archive work, and tourism markets — not pure alterity. Stengers’s primary texts not yet ingested; claims route through de la Cadena 2015 unless otherwise attributed.
