# Ultimate Sacred Postulates

## Definition as Coined

For [[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]] (*[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*, 1979; definitive architecture in *[[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999|Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity]]*, 1999, Chs. 8–10, 13), **ultimate sacred postulates** (USPs) are the most invariant, typically non-material sentences of a [[liturgical-order]] — objectively unverifiable and absolutely unfalsifiable — to which congregations impute [[sanctity]] (unquestionableness). Examples: the Jewish Shema; Christian creedal sentences; Maring "Deceased ancestors persist as sentient and powerful beings." Only USPs are **sacred** in the restricted sense; other sentences are **sanctified** by association (graded, contingent).

Sanctity **flows** from USPs (by ritual association and, in literate traditions, theological exposition) to sentences with material and social terms — ethics, legitimate authority, categories, economic and ecological rules — certifying their correctness, naturalness, or binding force without making those lower-order sentences themselves "sacred" in the restricted sense.

## Semantic History

The dual-model vocabulary of *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* (1968/1984) already needed a way to say how spirit-and-ancestor sentences organize action without being operational ecological claims. The 1974/1979 formal analysis of ritual supplies USPs as the **apex of the cognized hierarchy**: vague, invariant, and therefore (Rappaport argues) adaptively flexible — reinterpretation of mid-level rules can change while USPs stay put. "Sanctity and Lies" (1979; earlier "Liturgies and Lies," 1976) places USPs in evolutionary argument: sanctification of convention as partial antidote to language's capacity for falsehood and alternative.

## Operationalizations

Not a survey index. Reconstructed from liturgical text and performance: identify the most invariant, non-material sentences; map how ritual association ties them to commissives, authorities, and taboos; assess whether sanctity is confined to USPs or has been **oversanctified** onto specific material interests ([[adaptive-structure]] / idolatry as maladaptation). Among the [[tsembaga]], military pledges and [[kaiko]] commitments are certified by association with ancestor postulates.

## Applications

- Hierarchical structure of [[cognized-model|cognized models]] (USP → cosmological axioms → rules → imported indications → secular knowledge).
- Indexical pledges in Maring dancing certified by USP association (hosts read dance contingents as future military support).
- Theory of convention: liturgical performance establishes acceptance; USPs naturalize and moralize accepted orders.
- Evolutionary/religious argument: material **vacuity** of USPs places them beyond falsification and preserves adaptive flexibility of the whole.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Adaptive" evaluation of sacred postulates may be read as functionalism-in-theology; others treat USPs as ordinary ideology. Vacuity of USPs is adaptive only if not filled by overspecific material claims (idolatry). Highest-order [[cybernetics-of-the-holy]] responses seek USP replacement — rare endogenously.
