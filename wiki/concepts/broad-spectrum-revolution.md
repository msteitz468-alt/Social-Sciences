# Broad Spectrum Revolution

## Definition as Coined

**(Attributed — [[flannery-kent-v|Kent V. Flannery]], “Origins and ecological effects of early domestication in Iran and the Near East,” in *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*.)**

Flannery named a mid–Upper Palaeolithic shift **before ~20,000 B.C.** (as of his 1969 chronology) in which Near Eastern foragers broadened subsistence to include progressively more fish, crabs, water turtles, molluscs, land snails, partridges, migratory waterfowl, and (possibly) wild cereal grains. The change is **not** simply “large game → small game” — ungulates could still dominate bone counts and meat weight — but a move from a **narrow** to a **broad spectrum** of edible wild products. That broad-spectrum collecting pattern, he argued, characterized cultures into ~6000 B.C. and was **the only context in which first domestication could take place**.

## Semantic History

- **1969 coining context:** Flannery’s ecological program paper in the London domestication seminar; integrated with [[binford-lewis|Binford]] density-equilibrium / optimal–marginal habitat logic.
- **Later career of the term:** Became a standard label in agricultural-origins teaching and research for pre-farming dietary diversification (regional applications and critiques after 1969 are outside this source’s scope — date-stamp later literature).
- **Relation to Childe:** Undercuts reading the [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] as the first major dietary leap; Flannery held early farmers were not clearly better nourished than broad-spectrum collectors.

## Operationalizations

*(As of Flannery 1969.)*

- Faunal: increase of fish, invertebrates, waterfowl alongside continued ungulate hunting; regional unevenness (e.g. *Helix* abundance contrasts) supports pressure in **marginal** zones.
- Botanical/technological pre-adaptations: ground stone (initially often ochre), storage pits (Zarzian Shanidar; increase 9000–7000 B.C. at Zawi Chemi, Karim Shahir, Mureybat, Ain Mallaha).
- Chronological frame: begins mid-UP, continues through early dry-farming (Ali Kosh Bus Mordeh still “broad spectrum” in diet structure).

## Applications

- **Near East vertical economy** (Zagros–Levant altitude mosaic) as setting for altitude-zone resource scheduling.
- **Harlan–Zohary logic:** cultivation may start **adjacent to**, not inside, densest wild cereal stands — broad-spectrum sedentary collectors (Natufian; Tell Mureybat wild cereals) reduce pressure to farm in the habitat core.
- **Ali Kosh (Deh Luran):** early dry-farming phases still draw most plant weight and meat weight from wild resources; cereals small seed % but large plant-food weight share (Helbaek/Flannery data as of 1969).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Absolute dates and site sequences are **1969 uncalibrated-era** snapshots.
- “Revolution” is Flannery’s rhetorical label for a long trend — not a single event.
- Causal link to population pressure / Binford packing is a **theoretical position**, not a multi-class convergent finding.
- Regional applicability outside SW Asia must be argued case by case (Flannery already analogized vertical economies in Mesoamerica and the Andes).

## Natufian densification ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]])

*(Attributed — 2013 symposium authors; densified with the culture page.)*

The [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] is the classic SW Asian test of BSR as farming prelude. Positions in the 2013 volume:

1. **BSR (Binford/Flannery tradition):** diet diversification as demographic–ecological prelude to farming.
2. **Henry specialized intensification:** large sedentary communities via specialized cereals/nuts + specialized gazelle hunting — **not** diversification as prime mover.
3. **Bar-Oz, Yeshurun & Weinstein-Evron (2013):** Carmel coastal gazelle rise is **within Early Natufian** and does not track climate — cultural hunting preference, not Younger Dryas effect.
4. **Rosen “Monocot Revolution” (2013 phytoliths):** resource re-ranking (forest/nut → grass-husk + wetland monocots); small-seeded weeds often exceed cereals; no clear Late Natufian cultivation in her samples.

Do not promote BSR as the sole Natufian→farming mechanism in wiki voice. Cross-links: [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]], [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[sedentism]].

## Bellwood densification — *First Farmers* (2005)

*(Attributed — [[bellwood-peter]], *[[bellwood-first-farmers-2005|First Farmers]]* 2005. Bellwood does **not** use Flannery’s exact phrase “Broad Spectrum Revolution” but summarizes the content within demographic-stress / packing models.)*

- **Cohen packing model (via Bellwood):** continuous late Pleistocene population growth under improving environments → dietary shift from large game toward marine resources, cereals, small game → eventual plant cultivation — classic broad-spectrum logic (position inventory in ch.2). Related: Binford (1968) / Flannery (1969) post-Pleistocene coastal packing → outflow to marginal zones → cereal cultivation likeliest at **edges** of wild plant ranges (higher supply stress than cores) (attributed positions).
- **Finding (Bellwood):** evidence that hunter-gatherer populations were growing in various Old World places after LGM and before agriculture is “quite strong for some regions” — significance highlighted for SW Asia (attributed).
- **Ohalo II (~19,000 BC, Sea of Galilee / Lake Lisan):** wild emmer, barley, pistachio, grape, olive; 1,500 m² camp; ≥3 oval pole-thatch huts; storage pits; flexed burials under stones; Upper Paleolithic blade toolkit + basalt bowls/pestles — early multi-resource exploitation baseline **millennia before** agriculture (attributed). Does not by itself produce agricultural lifeways (same caution as W&O / Mithen on this site).
- **Causal layering:** Bellwood treats causes as “layered in time” (sedentism, affluence/choice, co-evolution, environmental stress, population pressure, suitable candidates) — BSR-type dietary broadening is one strand, not a sole prime mover (attributed). Animal domestication later as wild meat declined is part of the Cohen-style sequence he inventories (position).
- **Comparative:** Jōmon Japan listed among rare long-term sedentary forager cases that **resisted** formal grain agriculture despite broad-spectrum affluence — see [[jomon-culture]].

Do not promote BSR as sole Natufian→farming mechanism. Cross-links: [[natufian-culture]], [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[domestication]]. Source: [[bellwood-first-farmers-2005]].

## Barker 2006 (*Agricultural Revolution in Prehistory*)

*(Attributed — [[barker-graeme|Graeme Barker]], *[[barker-agricultural-revolution-prehistory-2006|The Agricultural Revolution in Prehistory]]* 2006, Chs. 3–4, 8.)*

- **Kebaran baseline.** Most Kebarans broad-spectrum hunting, fishing, and gathering; increasing cereal reliance by Late Glacial close. Ohalo II (Sea of Galilee, c.19,000 BP): kidney-shaped huts, fish + nets, gazelle/deer/small game/waterfowl, carbonized morphologically wild barley (+ emmer, almond, olive, pistachio, grape, acorns), grass-seed starch on grinding stone; semi-sedentary seasonal reuse without storage facilities (attributed). Sr/Ca: Kebarans ate less plant food / more meat than Natufians (Schoeninger via Barker).
- **Natufian intensification.** Early Natufians as delayed-return collectors actively manipulating resources (plant-tending, fire for gazelle grazing, storage, communal gazelle kills) short of full cultivation; intensive wild-cereal collection consensus (Unger-Hamilton sickle microwear) (attributed). Shellfish calorie estimates fraught (Bailey: ~50,000 oysters ≈ one red deer) yet may be critical seasonal starvation food (method caveat).
- **Africa / Nile.** Wadi Kubbaniya AMS disproved 18k BP domestic cereals; genuine broad-spectrum diet with ≥25 wild plants (esp. nut-grass tubers), >100,000 fishbones, waterfowl, molluscs, game; regular harvest of nut-grass could increase yields (attributed). Early Holocene Sahara/Sahel broad-spectrum foraging before pastoralism.
- **Americas.** Late Pleistocene tropical lowlands more mosaic than modern rainforest enabling broad-spectrum foraging; Lake La Yeguada clearance ~11,000 BP as unconscious fitness enhancement for beneficial plants (Piperno & Pearsall via Barker) (attributed).
- **Do not promote as sole prime mover.** Barker embeds broad-spectrum / delayed-return collecting within long multi-path regional histories, not as single global mechanism. Cross-links: [[natufian-culture]], [[sedentism]], [[abu-hureyra]], [[barker-graeme]].
