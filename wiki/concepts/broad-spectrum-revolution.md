# Broad Spectrum Revolution

## Definition as Coined

**(Attributed — [[flannery-kent-v|Kent V. Flannery]], “Origins and ecological effects of early domestication in Iran and the Near East,” in *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*.)**

Flannery named a mid–Upper Palaeolithic shift **before ~20,000 B.C.** (as of his 1969 chronology) in which Near Eastern foragers broadened subsistence to include progressively more fish, crabs, water turtles, molluscs, land snails, partridges, migratory waterfowl, and (possibly) wild cereal grains. The change is **not** simply “large game → small game” — ungulates could still dominate bone counts and meat weight — but a move from a **narrow** to a **broad spectrum** of edible wild products. That broad-spectrum collecting pattern, he argued, characterized cultures into ~6000 B.C. and was **the only context in which first domestication could take place**.

## Semantic History

- **1969 coining context:** Flannery’s ecological program paper in the London domestication seminar; integrated with [[binford-lewis|Binford]] density-equilibrium / optimal–marginal habitat logic.
- **Later career of the term:** Became a standard label in agricultural-origins teaching and research for pre-farming dietary diversification (regional applications and critiques after 1969 are outside this source’s scope — date-stamp later literature).
- **Relation to Childe:** Undercuts reading the [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] as the first major dietary leap; Flannery held early farmers were not clearly better nourished than broad-spectrum collectors.

## Operationalizations

*(As of Flannery 1969.)*

- Faunal: increase of fish, invertebrates, waterfowl alongside continued ungulate hunting; regional unevenness (e.g. *Helix* abundance contrasts) supports pressure in **marginal** zones.
- Botanical/technological pre-adaptations: ground stone (initially often ochre), storage pits (Zarzian Shanidar; increase 9000–7000 B.C. at Zawi Chemi, Karim Shahir, Mureybat, Ain Mallaha).
- Chronological frame: begins mid-UP, continues through early dry-farming (Ali Kosh Bus Mordeh still “broad spectrum” in diet structure).

## Applications

- **Near East vertical economy** (Zagros–Levant altitude mosaic) as setting for altitude-zone resource scheduling.
- **Harlan–Zohary logic:** cultivation may start **adjacent to**, not inside, densest wild cereal stands — broad-spectrum sedentary collectors (Natufian; Tell Mureybat wild cereals) reduce pressure to farm in the habitat core.
- **Ali Kosh (Deh Luran):** early dry-farming phases still draw most plant weight and meat weight from wild resources; cereals small seed % but large plant-food weight share (Helbaek/Flannery data as of 1969).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Absolute dates and site sequences are **1969 uncalibrated-era** snapshots.
- “Revolution” is Flannery’s rhetorical label for a long trend — not a single event.
- Causal link to population pressure / Binford packing is a **theoretical position**, not a multi-class convergent finding.
- Regional applicability outside SW Asia must be argued case by case (Flannery already analogized vertical economies in Mesoamerica and the Andes).

## Natufian densification ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]])

*(Attributed — 2013 symposium authors; densified with the culture page.)*

The [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] is the classic SW Asian test of BSR as farming prelude. Positions in the 2013 volume:

1. **BSR (Binford/Flannery tradition):** diet diversification as demographic–ecological prelude to farming.
2. **Henry specialized intensification:** large sedentary communities via specialized cereals/nuts + specialized gazelle hunting — **not** diversification as prime mover.
3. **Bar-Oz, Yeshurun & Weinstein-Evron (2013):** Carmel coastal gazelle rise is **within Early Natufian** and does not track climate — cultural hunting preference, not Younger Dryas effect.
4. **Rosen “Monocot Revolution” (2013 phytoliths):** resource re-ranking (forest/nut → grass-husk + wetland monocots); small-seeded weeds often exceed cereals; no clear Late Natufian cultivation in her samples.

Do not promote BSR as the sole Natufian→farming mechanism in wiki voice. Cross-links: [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]], [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[sedentism]].
