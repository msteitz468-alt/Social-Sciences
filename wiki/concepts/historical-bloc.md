# Historical Bloc

> [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]]'s concept (*blocco storico*) in *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971|Selections from the Prison Notebooks]]*. **Attributed voice** throughout. Appears both as programmatic formula (structure–superstructure unity) and as condition of living representation (intellectuals–people).

## Definition as Coined

Two load-bearing formulations in the 1971 selection:

1. **Structure–superstructure unity** (Modern Prince / political notes): "Concept of 'historical bloc', i.e. unity between nature and spirit (structure and superstructure), unity of opposites and of distincts." Against economism, Gramsci holds that a new homogeneous politico-economic historical bloc is formed through conscious struggle and compromises that weld forces — not by waiting for economic "laws" to deliver favourable conditions.

2. **Leaders–led organic cohesion** (Problems of Marxism): If the relationship between intellectuals and people-nation, leaders and led, is organic cohesion in which "feeling-passion becomes understanding and thence knowledge (not mechanically but in a way that is alive)," then representation is real and "the shared life be realised which alone is a social force—**with the creation of the 'historical bloc'.**"

Together: a historical bloc is not a parliamentary coalition and not a base that "expresses" a superstructure. It is the lived unity of economic structure, political organisation, and cultural leadership — cemented by [[organic-intellectuals]] and [[hegemony]].

## Semantic History

- **Gramsci:** Fragmentary; sometimes near "ideological bloc," "national bloc," urban/rural blocs in Italian history without always using *blocco storico*.
- **Neo-Gramscian IR (Cox, Gill, etc.):** Historic bloc as world-order configuration of material capabilities, ideas, and institutions — productive extension, not notebook identity.
- **Cultural studies:** Articulation of class fractions + popular common sense under hegemony.

## Operationalizations

- Test of representation: do intellectuals feel/understand with the people-nation, or form a caste ("organic centralism")?
- Anti-infantilism rule: politics/ideology are not immediate expressions of structure (Gramsci: such a claim is "primitive infantilism" vs Marx's concrete works).
- Formation path: struggle for hegemony in [[civil-society]] + compromises among allied forces + eventual State phase requiring development of *all* superstructures.

## Applications

- Italian Risorgimento failures of popular-national bloc (adjacent analysis even when the exact term is sparse in the History notes).
- Critique of economism and of Bukharin's sociology of "laws" (*[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971]]* Problems of Marxism).
- Later world-systems / IR neo-Gramscian uses (attribute the extension).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Thin explicit definition density relative to [[hegemony]] — easy to inflate from few passages.
- Risk of circularity: bloc = whatever successfully coheres.
- Do not read IR "historic bloc" back into 1930s Italian notebooks without attribution.
