# Polyphony in Ethnography

Bakhtinian **polyphony** applied to ethnography: texts that sustain multiple independent voices rather than a single monologic ethnographic voice. Companion to [[dialogism-in-ethnography]] in *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* discussions of experimental form.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed to volume uses.)*

Multi-voiced arrangement as experimental ideal challenging [[ethnographic-realism]]’s omniscient monologue.

## Semantic History

Literary term → ethnographic experimental ideal in the 1980s.

## Operationalizations

Multi-authored or multi-voiced textual arrangements; italicized “direct speech” claims (Rosaldo criticizes in *Montaillou*).

## Applications

Clifford and others on experimental ethnography; Pratt on Shostak’s polyphonous mediation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Rosaldo: **false polyphony** — translation chains and editorial control still concentrate authority. Power and editing still concentrate in the ethnographer-author; polyphony can be aesthetic only. Rabinow on limits of heteroglossia.
