# Militarization of Labour

## Definition as Coined

In *[[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920|Terrorism and Communism]]* (1920), especially Chapter 8 and the embedded "Report on the Organization of Labour," [[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]] defines **militarization of labour** as the application of military-type methods — registration, mobilization, transfer, discipline, executive thoroughness, harsh insistence on execution — to economic organization under the workers' state. Only the army, he argues, had historically subordinated citizens so totally; with the civil-war "labour front" deciding Soviet survival, economic and trade-union bodies may demand army-level devotion (attributed).

> The introduction of compulsory labour service is unthinkable without the application, to a greater or less degree, of the methods of militarization of labour.

He distinguishes **proletarian** militarization (by the workers' will, under the [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]) from **bourgeois** or Arakcheev-style militarization (slavery against the workers' will). Evidence of consent he offers: Subbotniks (Communist Saturdays) — voluntary unpaid labour he calls historically unprecedented.

## Semantic History

- **1920 origin in practice:** Trotsky insists labour armies arose from practice (idle Third Army in the Urals), not prior theory; War Department experience in mass mobilization was reused because economic organs lacked it under private property (attributed).
- **Trade-union debate:** The same programme — unions as production organizers, compulsory membership, party final word, one-man management — is the antagonist position of the [[soviet-trade-union-debate]] as later reconstructed from Lenin's side (1920–21). *Terrorism and Communism* is the fullest primary statement of Trotsky's 1920 labour doctrine on this wiki.
- **War communism / NEP reception:** Later historiography and Žižek's foreword treat the chapter as the shocking core of the book — industrial mobilization on the terrain later associated with Stalinism — while some scholars (e.g. Lars Lih, as Žižek cites) reframe war communism as fortress recovery rather than a deliberate short-cut to full communism (secondary positions).
- **Stalin path debate:** Žižek notes Stalin's annotated copy of the book with approving notes; Service and others treat continuity of compulsion methods; Trotskyist tradition stresses class-nature of the state as the decisive differentia. **Not adjudicated here.**

## Operationalizations

| Instrument | Trotsky's 1920 specification (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Compulsory labour service | "He who works not, neither shall he eat"; free hiring permanently replaced; whole population as labour reservoir |
| War Department apparatus | First-period tool for registration, mobilization, formation, transfer |
| Labour tasks vs pure transfer | Prefer quotas (e.g. cubic sazhens of wood) when possible |
| Labour armies | Military units applied to fuel, cartage, building; March 1920 utilization figures reported by Trotsky (e.g. Third Army ~38% workers after cuts) |
| Trade unions | Organize production, discipline, distribution of workers; not wage struggle against the labour state; apparatus of repression against "undisciplined" elements |
| Wages / piecework / Taylorism | Retain money and goods wages tied to productivity; re-purpose Taylor methods for social product |
| One-man management | Against collegiate boards that hide responsibility; variants of worker + specialist combinations |
| Single economic plan | Four stages: survival (transport, bread, fuel, raw materials) → locomotive/machine-building → primary necessities → personal consumption |

**Core theoretical rebuttal:** That free labour is always more productive than compulsory labour is true, he grants, for the feudal→bourgeois transition; making it permanent for the bourgeois→socialist transition is a "liberal" or "Kautskian" prejudice. Productivity depends on **who compels whom, for what purpose** (attributed).

## Applications

- **Soviet Russia 1920:** Defence of war-economy compulsion against Menshevik congress resolutions ("Compulsory labour always is unproductive labour" — as Trotsky quotes them).
- **State theory:** "The road to socialism lies through a period of the highest possible intensification of the principle of the state" — the dictatorship as the state's "last brilliant flame" before withering into a producing/consuming commune (attributed; links [[marxist-theory-of-the-state]], [[withering-away-of-the-state]]).
- **Party–union–soviet triangle:** Party holds final word; unions under Communist leadership; soviets as power apparatus (Ch.7–8; see also [[vanguard-party]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Kautsky 1919 ([[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]], attributed):** Compulsory labour imposed on the bourgeoisie only; arbitrary "who is a worker?" demarcation makes the council idea foundation for dictatorial arbitrary rule; production collapse when expropriation is severed from organisation; morale not raised by merciless tribunals for labour discipline (Lenin quotes as foil).

### Other critiques

- **Menshevik / Kautskyist (as Trotsky constructs them):** Compulsion lowers productivity; militarization scatters labour; path to socialism without bread monopoly, market abolition, revolutionary dictatorship, or labour militarization.
- **Lenin 1920–21 (separate primary dossier):** In the [[soviet-trade-union-debate]], Lenin charged Trotsky's trade-union programme with "bureaucratic harassment," one-track thinking, and over-militarization of the unions — recorded on that debate page.
- **Later anti-Stalinist Trotsky:** The 1920 labour-state doctrine is among the materials critics (Service, Žižek, liberal historians) use to argue continuity with later forced industrialization; Trotsky's own 1930s theory of degenerated workers' state does not retract the 1920 text — career tension, not silent overwrite.
- **Evidence class:** Partisan programme by the war commissar who ran the Red Army and pushed labour armies. Productivity statistics and desertion comparisons are **his** reports. Do not promote to wiki voice without independent economic history.
