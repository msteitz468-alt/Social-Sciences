# Doing Difference

**Doing difference** is [[west-candace|West]] and [[fenstermaker-sarah|Fenstermaker]]'s
extension of [[doing-gender]] to the claim that **race**, **class**, and **gender** are
ongoing interactional *accomplishments* of categorical difference, produced through mechanisms
analogous to gender accountability. Key text in
[[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]] (Ch. 4); live dispute on
[[doing-difference-debate]].

## Definition as Coined

West and Fenstermaker proposed that the ethnomethodological apparatus developed for gender —
categorical membership, accountability to normative conceptions, situated accomplishment —
can analyze how multiple categorical differences are done, often simultaneously, in everyday
interaction. "Difference" here is not a trait list but the practical production of
categorically different persons and the legitimation of inequality.

They explicitly disclaim **equivalence of oppressions** while asserting a shared *process*
mechanism: race and class, like gender, are ongoing accomplishments whereby persons are held
accountable to normative conceptions attached to race category and class category (examples
in the 1995 paper include police stops, workplace uniforms, the Baby M trial, and the
Thomas–Hill hearings). Mathematical metaphors (additive/multiplicative oppression models)
are rejected as inadequate to simultaneity.

## Semantic History

Published 1995 in *Gender & Society* after earlier West–Fenstermaker–Zimmerman work on gender
inequality and power. Immediately occasioned a symposium (Collins, Maldonado, Takagi, Thorne,
Weber, Winant) and a West–Fenstermaker reply "(Re)Doing Difference." The term remains a
program slogan more than a standardized measurement construct.

## Operationalizations

Empirical chapters in the 2002 volume apply simultaneous race/class/gender accountability to
settings such as a UC Board of Regents meeting on affirmative action. No single quantitative
index; operationalization is interaction-analytic.

## Applications

Program extensions and critiques collected in
[[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]]; intersects debates on intersectionality
and racial formation (e.g., Winant as symposium contributor; see also
[[racial-formation-theory]] where relevant).

## Critiques and Limitations

Contested from publication: whether power and history are adequately theorized; whether race
and class are "done" like gender; whether a micro-interactional frame can carry structural
oppression. Full position map: [[doing-difference-debate]].
