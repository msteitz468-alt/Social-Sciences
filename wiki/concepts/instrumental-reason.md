# Instrumental Reason

## Definition as Coined

**Instrumental reason** is the [[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt School]]'s name — most
associated with [[horkheimer-max|Horkheimer]]'s *Eclipse of Reason* (1947) and
elaborated across
*[[horkheimer-adorno-dialectic-of-enlightenment-1947|Dialectic of Enlightenment]]*
— for reason reduced to the calculation of efficient means, indifferent to the
rationality of ends. Horkheimer and [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]] argue (attributed)
that Enlightenment fused knowledge with power: reading Bacon, "**power and
knowledge are synonymous**"; "what men want to learn from nature is how to use it
in order wholly to dominate it and other men. That is the only aim." Reason
becomes technique — "thinking objectifies itself to become an automatic,
self-activating process; an impersonation of the machine that it produces itself
so that ultimately the machine can replace it" — until "reason itself has become
the mere instrument of the all-inclusive economic apparatus."

Its core features (all attributed):

- **The reduction of thought to calculation and equivalence.** "Bourgeois society
  is ruled by equivalence. It makes the dissimilar comparable by reducing it to
  abstract quantities." "Number became the canon of the Enlightenment." Thought
  that once comprehended objects now merely subsumes them: "cognition is
  restricted to its repetition; and thought becomes mere tautology."
- **Self-preservation as the constitutive principle.** In the Juliette excursus:
  "self-preservation is the constitutive principle of science, the soul of the
  table of categories." Reason "posits no substantial goals," so "all affects are
  equally removed from its governance" — it is "neutral in regard to ends; its
  element is coordination."
- **Morality is underivable from reason.** Because formalized reason can ground no
  end, "the plain truth had no advantage over distortion, and rationalization none
  over the ratio, if they could prove no practical benefit." Adorno and Horkheimer
  read Kant, Sade, and Nietzsche as those who drew the honest conclusion: reason
  gives "no fundamental argument against murder." Sade and Nietzsche, "more
  merciful than the moralistic lackeys of the bourgeoisie," told the truth the
  optimistic Enlightenment concealed — that domination and reason are identical.
- **Cunning as reason's origin.** In the Odyssey excursus, instrumental reason is
  traced to Odyssean cunning (*mētis*): survival through calculation, adaptation,
  and self-denial — and, paradoxically, "**the ratio which supplants mimesis is
  not simply its counterpart. It is itself mimesis: mimesis unto death**" (see
  [[mimesis]]).

## Semantic History

The concept is the Frankfurt School's radicalization of [[weber-max|Weber's]]
[[rationalization]] and *Zweckrationalität* (means–ends rationality): where Weber
diagnosed an [[iron-cage|iron cage]] and disenchantment, Horkheimer and Adorno
locate domination in the very structure of reason and trace it back to the origins
of civilization, not merely to modern capitalism. [[marcuse-herbert|Marcuse]]
recast it as [[technological-rationality]] ("the technological a priori is a
political a priori"). [[mills-c-wright|C. Wright Mills]]'s parallel American formulation —
"rationality without reason," the growth of bureaucratic/functional rationality without a
corresponding increase in individual reason and freedom (see [[cheerful-robot]]) — draws on the
same Weberian root and cites Horkheimer directly, though it stays closer to Weber and does not
make Mills's totalizing indictment of reason as such. Habermas's later reconstruction distinguishes *instrumental*
(means–ends) from *communicative* rationality precisely to escape the Frankfurt
School's totalizing indictment of reason as such (see
[[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]]).

## Operationalizations

None; the concept is argued philosophically, through readings of Bacon, Kant,
Sade, Nietzsche, mathematics, and positivism. It converges with the wiki's
existing [[technological-rationality]] page (Marcuse's cognate); the two should be
read together, with instrumental reason as the parent formulation.

## Applications

Instrumental reason is the mechanism of the [[dialectic-of-enlightenment]] thesis:
it underlies the [[domination-of-nature]], the [[culture-industry]] (whose
"technological rationale is the rationale of domination"), and — in the paranoid,
projective form analysed in the anti-Semitism excursus — [[false-projection]]. It
is a foundational reference for the critical theory of science, technology, and
administration.

## Critiques and Limitations

Recorded in attributed voice: critics argue the concept conflates science and
reason as such with their social misuse; that positing reason as inherently
dominative leaves the critique no rational standpoint from which to speak (the
"performative contradiction" Habermas presses); and that the sweep from Odysseus to
Auschwitz is too total to discriminate among historically distinct rationalities.
Defenders reply that the diagnosis of a reason that optimizes means while going
silent on ends captures something real about bureaucratic, technocratic, and
market modernity. See [[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]].
