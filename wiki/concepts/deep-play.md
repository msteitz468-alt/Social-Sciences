# Deep Play

## Definition as Coined

[[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] (*[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]*; IoC Ch. 15) borrows Bentham’s **deep play** — stakes so high that participation is irrational by utilitarian calculus — to read the Balinese cockfight. Money becomes “a symbol of moral import”; status is symbolically affirmed or insulted, not structurally redistributed. The fight’s function, if any, is interpretive: “a Balinese reading of Balinese experience, a story they tell themselves about themselves.”

## Semantic History

Bentham → Geertz ethnographic keyword → general metaphor for high-stakes expressive culture.

## Operationalizations

Betting structure (center even-money status-matched fights vs side odds); man–cock identification; illegality; “status bloodbath”; culture as ensemble of texts read over the shoulder.

## Applications

*[[geertz-deep-play-study]]*; teaching [[thick-description]]. Companion Bali polity model: *[[geertz-negara-study|Negara]]* elevates status dramatization from cockfight to [[theatre-state]] court ceremony — continuous “status bloodbath” idiom at different scales.

## Critiques and Limitations

Over-coherence of “the Balinese”; gender/violence; aestheticization without changing structure (Geertz’s own point, turned against him as quietism).
