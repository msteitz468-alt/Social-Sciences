# Class Fraction

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|The Eighteenth Brumaire]]* (1852), [[marx-karl|Marx]] disaggregates the [[bourgeoisie]] into **fractions** (factions) defined by **material conditions of life** and **forms of property**, not by dynastic “principles.” Core case: **Legitimists** = large landed property (Bourbon political expression); **Orleanists** = high finance, large industry, large commerce — Capital (July-monarchy expression). “What… kept these two factions apart was no so-called set of principles, it was their material conditions for life—two different sorts of property… the old antagonism of the City and the Country, the rivalry between Capital and Landed property.”

A **class fraction** is thus a subdivision of a class with shared property-form interests strong enough to support distinct political identities, yet capable of joint class rule under specific political forms (for Marx: the parliamentary republic as [[party-of-order|Party of Order]]).

## Semantic History

The *Manifesto* already distinguishes aristocracy of finance, industrial bourgeoisie, petty bourgeoisie, etc.; the *Brumaire* makes **fractional analysis** the engine of a political narrative. Later Marxism (Poulantzas and others) formalized “class fractions” and “power bloc”; this page anchors the 1852 usage. Explicit negative case in Ch. II: pure “republican” bourgeois party was **not** a fraction held by common business interests but a coterie of republican-idea professionals.

**Bourdieu (*[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]*, 1979).** Secondary differences **within** classes are [[class-fraction|class fractions]] defined by **composition of capital** (economic vs cultural) and trajectory, not only by property form in Marx’s sense. Dominant-class fractions (teachers/intellectuals, professions, employers) and middle-class fractions (declining, executant, new petite bourgeoisie) structure lifestyle and political homology in [[social-space]]. Related problem-space to Marxian fractions; different operational basis (capital structure + habitus). Do not equate Marx 1852 fractions with Bourdieu 1979 occupational fractions without argument.

## Operationalizations

- Party labels mapped to property types (Legitimist/Orleanist; finance aristocracy under Fould).
- Superstructure claim: upon forms of property “a whole superstructure is reared of various and peculiarly shaped feelings, illusions, habits of thought and conceptions of life.”
- Breach between parliamentary/literary **representatives** and the commercial mass **represented**.
- **Bourdieu:** refined socio-occupational categories (INSEE-based but split — e.g. secondary vs higher teachers; public vs private cadres; cultural intermediaries); volume × composition of capital; lifestyle and opinion indicators.

## Applications

- Political sociology of ruling-class disunity and coalition.
- Explains why the parliamentary republic was, for Marx, the only form of **joint** bourgeois class rule — and why its collapse yielded [[bonapartism]].
- Refines [[class-struggle]] beyond a two-camp schematic.
- **Bourdieuian lifestyle and taste analysis** (*[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]*) — fraction-level correspondence maps; reconversion of threatened fractions.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of infinite fractional splitting without a stopping rule.
- Historians dispute how tightly 1848–51 party labels map onto property forms.
- “Landed property” as bourgeois vs feudal is itself a theoretical claim of the text (land “rendered thoroughly bourgeois” in standard readings).
