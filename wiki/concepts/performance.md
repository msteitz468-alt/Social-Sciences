# Performance (ethnography of speaking / folklore)

## Definition as Coined

In [[bauman-sherzer-explorations-ethnography-speaking-1974|Bauman & Sherzer (eds.) 1974]], **performance** is the **nexus** of ethnography-of-speaking analysis: the interplay between **resources** for speaking and individual **competence**, within particular situations. Performances have an **emergent** quality, structured by the situated and creative exercise of competence — not mere “use” residual after grammar.

## Semantic History

- **Hymes:** performance as a SPEAKING component and as the actualization of communicative competence (vs Chomsky’s residual “performance”).
- **Bauman & Sherzer 1974:** organizing theme of the volume — social structures largely **created in performance** by strategic manipulation of speaking resources (editors’ intro, attributed).
- **Folklore performance turn:** Bauman’s later *Verbal Art as Performance* (1977, not yet ingested) densifies accountability to an audience, keying, and emergent structure — flag for future ingest.

## Operationalizations

Event-centered recording and analysis; attention to audience, keying, genre, and emergence; rejection of treating artistic speech as mere ornament on referential grammar.

## Applications

Case studies throughout *Explorations* (Sections IV–V especially); [[ethnography-of-speaking]] method; verbal art.

## Critiques and Limitations

“Performance” risks inflation (everything is performance); requires ethnographic criteria for marked performance vs ordinary talk (Bauman later addresses this).


## Applications — *Explorations* case library (1974)

*(Attributed to chapter authors via [[bauman-sherzer-explorations-ethnography-speaking-1974|Bauman & Sherzer 1974]].)*

- **Emergence in formal events:** Irvine Wolof greeting strategies; Salmond Māori ritual encounter rule-play; Sherzer Cuna political metaphor.
- **Artistic continua:** Gossen Chamula heat taxonomy (ordinary → heated heart → pure words); Bricker Zinacanteco couplets; Kirshenblatt-Gimblett parable components (*moshl*/*nimshl*).
- **Ground-rules performances:** Bauman Quaker “Speaking in the Light”; Philips optional vs obligatory participation structuring time.
- **Editors:** performance as volume’s organizing theme — social structure created in performance.

## Smith 2006 — heritage as performance (attributed)

*([[smith-laurajane]], *[[smith-uses-of-heritage-2006]]*, esp. Ch. 2 and case chapters.)* Heritage is a **multilayered performance** — visiting, managing, interpreting, conserving — that embodies remembrance while negotiating place and belonging, and that simultaneously constitutes the idea of heritage framing those performances. Country-house visiting as identity performance (“knowing your place”; banality of grandiloquence). Industrial museums and Castleford festival as labour/community performance of remembering. Riversleigh tourism as performance of national landscape identity. Draws Bagnall and audience theory (Abercrombie & Longhurst) as Smith deploys them.
