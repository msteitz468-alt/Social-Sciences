# Racial Project

In [[racial-formation-theory]], a **racial project** is both an interpretation of race and a practical effort to distribute resources and organize social structures along racial lines. Racial formation is the society-wide synthesis of such projects.

## Definition as Coined

Omi and Winant: racial projects link the representational and the structural; they operate from everyday common sense to state policy. A project is “racist” (in their usage) when it creates or reproduces structures of domination based on essentialist racial categories — contested operational detail in reception.

## Semantic History

1986 coinage; widely applied to policies (e.g., colorblindness as hegemonic project), movements, and cultural representations.

## Operationalizations

Case analysis of policies, movements, and discourses as dual structure/representation moves — not a scale score.

## Applications

Colorblind racial ideology as hegemonic project; race-conscious movement projects; state racial policy.

## Critiques and Limitations

Breadth of the concept; criteria for “racist” project; relation to non-racial projects that have racial effects.
