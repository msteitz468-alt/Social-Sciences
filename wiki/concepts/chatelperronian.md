# Châtelperronian

Western European **transitional** industry at the Middle–Upper Palaeolithic boundary, often linked to late [[neanderthals]]. Stringer & Gamble (1993) favor reading it as **Neanderthal imitation** of incoming Modern technology rather than independent invention of the Upper Palaeolithic (*[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993]]*, attributed).

## Definition as Coined

Industry with **Châtelperron points** / curved, steeply blunted-backed knives and mixed Middle/Upper Palaeolithic traits: some MP features remain, but many tools are on well-struck **blades** (attributed, S&G 1993). Regional industry of southwest France and Spain in their synthesis; parallel late Neanderthal technological adoptions they cite include the **Uluzzian** (Italy) and **Szeletian** complex (eastern Europe) — similar age/form, mix of MP and UP techniques/types, fragmentary fossils consistent with Neanderthal origin in several scholars' readings (attributed, S&G 1993).

## Semantic History

### Authorship and dating (as of S&G 1993)

By 1993 the Châtelperronian was widely thought Neanderthal handiwork. Load-bearing associations:

- **Saint-Césaire** (1979): partial undoubted Neanderthal skeleton/skull in the higher of two Châtelperronian levels; TL ~**36,300** years ago (body text; appendix also lists related Early UP TL figures — treat as of 1993).
- **Arcy-sur-Cure / Grotte du Renne** (Leroi-Gourhan): levels from late Mousterian to earliest UP; distinctive Châtelperronian points; radiocarbon for level VIII ~**33,860** ya; associated carved bone and drilled animal canines; lower level X with dozen living surfaces, mammoth tusks and post-hole traces of stakes for hut construction. S&G caption: Châtelperronian point "possibly a Neanderthal imitation of Upper Palaeolithic technology" (attributed, 1993).

If Neanderthals made the Châtelperronian, S&G date their demise by disappearance of that industry and deduce Neanderthals were gone from western Europe by ~**31,000** years ago (attributed, 1993). Francis Harrold's review of French material is cited as supporting Neanderthal authorship stratigraphically; new dates showed overlap with the first "true" Upper Palaeolithic (attributed, 1993).

### Origins: local derivation vs intrusion vs imitation

1950s opinion (as S&G recount) divided:

- **François Bordes**: Châtelperronian points and technological style derived from late **Mousterian of Acheulean Tradition B** (MAT B) — the curved backed point/knife reminiscent of MAT B naturally backed knives.
- **Intrusionists**: unconvinced because of predominance of blanks from carefully prepared **blade cores** rather than Mousterian flakes.

S&G's **imitation position** (chs. 8–9): European transitional/UP-style industries associated with late Neanderthals were not independent local invention of Upper Palaeolithic technology. Reasons they give: (1) all European transitional industries are contemporary with or postdate earliest [[aurignacian|Aurignacian]] firmly associated with anatomically modern people (contrast African/Levantine PUP); (2) transition to blade-based Châtelperronian and campsite structures (Arcy) only occurs long after Moderns arrived in central Europe and Iberia — "Imitation rather than invention therefore seems the most plausible explanation for the change in Neanderthal behaviour" (attributed, S&G 1993).

They further argue Châtelperronian tools and rudimentary structures (Molodova, Arcy) show Neanderthal capacity for **emulation and change but not symbolism**: under contact 40–35 ka Neanderthals "imitated certain aspects of modern behaviour. But while they could emulate they could not fully understand" — structures more like "nests" than Modern symbolic "homes" at Kostenki or Dolní Věstonice (attributed, 1993). **This is a POSITION**, not wiki-voice fact; continuity and independent-invention readings remain live in the field's afterlife.

## Operationalizations

- Typological: Châtelperron point/backed knife; blade blanks; mixed MP/UP type lists.
- Biostratigraphic: Neanderthal fossils in Châtelperronian levels (Saint-Césaire; Arcy associations as argued).
- Chronological: TL/radiocarbon of late Neanderthal / early UP transition (as of 1993 apparatus).
- Comparative: Uluzzian / Szeletian as analogous "last Neanderthal" packages.

## Applications

- Key evidence in replacement vs continuity arguments about late Neanderthal cognition ([[behavioral-modernity]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).
- Stratigraphic interlacing with [[aurignacian]] (Roc de Combe, Piage alternating levels).
- Bridge claim between late [[mousterian]] (MAT B ancestry per Bordes) and incoming Modern package (per S&G imitation reading).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Authorship**: Neanderthal vs Modern manufacture still debated; association quality and reworking are recurrent issues.
- **Imitation vs independent evolution vs acculturation**: S&G's imitation is one strong position; Bordes local-derivation and gradual-continuity models are alternatives.
- **Cognitive inference**: "could emulate but not understand" is theoretical attribution, not a finding; later work (not in this source) has reopened Neanderthal symbolism.
- Dates (Saint-Césaire, Arcy, western Europe ~31 ka demise) are **as of 1993** and subject to supersession.

## Klein — *The Human Career* (2nd ed., 1999)

*(Attributed — [[klein-human-career-1999]], [[klein-richard]]; as of Klein 1999.)*

Klein stresses that the Châtelperronian — Mousterian-like stone tools plus Upper-Paleolithic
elements (endscrapers, burins, formal bone artifacts, pierced animal teeth, a post-built hut at
the Grotte du Renne, Arcy-sur-Cure) — is directly associated with [[neanderthals]] at
Saint-Césaire and Arcy. He favors **diffusion/acculturation** from the intruding
[[aurignacian|Aurignacian]] moderns over independent Neanderthal innovation, citing
interstratification of Châtelperronian and early Aurignacian layers, but calls it "a major puzzle
whose solution is important for closure on Out of Africa 2" (see [[modern-human-origins-debate]],
[[behavioral-modernity]]).
