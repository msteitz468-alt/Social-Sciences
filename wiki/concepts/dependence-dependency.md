# Dependence and Dependency (Hodder)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[hodder-entangled-2012|Entangled]]* (2012 Ch. 2, 5), [[hodder-ian|Hodder]] splits the verb “depend” into two analytic foci:

| Term | Sense | Role in the program |
|---|---|---|
| **Dependence** (pl. *dependences*) | Enabling **reliance** on things; also **contingency** (“it depends”) | Productive side — tools, symbols, materials allow humans to be, socialize, eat, think |
| **Dependency** (pl. *dependencies*) | **Constraint**, compulsion, co-constraint | Entrapment side — humans and things cannot manage without each other; limits development |

He uses *dependence/dependences* for the **general category** when both enabling and constraining relations are in view, and marks the distinction where productive reliance must be separated from lock-in. Their **dialectic** *is* [[entanglement]] ([[entanglement-theory]]): humans depend on things that depend on humans (and things on things).

**Not the same as** [[unilateral-dependence|Blau’s unilateral dependence]] (exchange/power alternatives in dyadic exchange). Different problem space: Hodder’s pair is HT/TT/TH material–social entanglement, not only HH compliance.

## Semantic History

Hodder explicitly borrows inflection from **dependency theory** (e.g. world-systems) and psychological co-dependency, and from everyday “reliance on,” then retools them for human–thing analysis. Related archaeological vocabularies he maps onto the pair include Skibo & Schiffer’s “dependency relationships” along behavioral chains. The enabling side connects to [[affordance]] and tool use; the constraining side to path dependence and maintenance labor.

## Operationalizations

Read any human–thing case for **both** sides:

- Domestication: nutrition and flock products (dependence) *and* tilling, herding, property, walls (dependency).
- Çatalhöyük walls: shelter (dependence) *and* continual rebuilding because mudbrick falls (dependency).
- Cars/grids: mobility and cold milk (dependence) *and* global mobilization to produce the appearance of stability (dependency).

## Applications

Central to every chapter of *Entangled*; formalized in the HT+TT+TH+HH formula; used to critique ANT “network” language for underplaying sticky dependency.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of stretching “dependency” from political-economy and clinical senses into every material constraint. Hodder notes (Ch. 10) that power asymmetries among humans (HH) are relatively underdeveloped relative to HT/TT strings. Distinction can blur in practice — he often keeps both under general *dependence*.
