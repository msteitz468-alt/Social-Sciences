# Classic Stage

Fourth New World historical-developmental stage in [[willey-phillips-method-theory-1958|Willey & Phillips]] (1958). At **hemispheric** scale they limit Classic to **Middle America and the central Andes** only.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Willey & Phillips 1958.)*

Earlier criteria retained: excellence in the great arts, climax in religious architecture, general florescence in material culture. **Added overriding criterion: urbanism** — “the threshold of civilization in so far as ‘civilization’ is defined as city life.”

Urbanism is defined **functionally** (capacity to organize large numbers of people), not only as house-to-house massed settlement — so Classic Maya of the Petén can be urban without proven compact cities. Attributes of civilization they list: outstanding public architecture, great art styles, class differentiations, codified intellectual systems (writing), science knowledge, formal deity hierarchies, widespread trade in luxury goods.

Also: Formative inventions drawn together into rich, diverse, yet unified patterns; strong regional ethnocentrism in art/architecture despite trade; pomp surrounding ruling classes.

## Semantic History

“Classic” was already Mesoamerican period vocabulary (and Andean climaxes). W&P’s move is evaluative at **hemispheric** scale: local climaxes (Pueblo Bonito, Mississippian, Coclé) may be “classic” within their areas but not Classic stage for the Western Hemisphere as a historic entity. Terminology “culture” vs “civilization”: they reserve **civilization** for maximum units on Classic and Postclassic stages.

## Operationalizations

| Area | W&P Classic exemplars (1958) |
|---|---|
| Middle America | Teotihuacan, Monte Albán, lowland Maya (Tikal etc.), Valley of Mexico Classic |
| Central Andes | Gallinazo–Mochica, Nazca, Tiahuanaco, Recuay, etc. |
| Outside nuclear America | Formative ceiling (SW, East, Intermediate, much of Amazonia) |

## Applications

- Still structures textbook periodization for Mesoamerica and Andes.
- Explicit argument that multiplicity of Formative regional strands enabled nuclear Classic — individually those strands would support only Mississippian- or Coclé-level cultures.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Evaluative hierarchy:** non-nuclear areas “fail” Classic by nuclear standards — a presentist civilizational ranking.
- Urbanism definition stretched to cover dispersed Maya settlement.
- Absolute chronology heavily revised by radiocarbon calibration and subsequent research.
- Stage is not a single culture or people (W&P: regionalism persists).
