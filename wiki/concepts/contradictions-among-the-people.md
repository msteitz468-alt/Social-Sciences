# Contradictions Among the People

## Definition as Coined

In the February 1957 speech *On the Correct Handling of Contradictions Among the People* (anthologized as ch. 4 of *[[mao-quotations-from-chairman-mao-1966|Quotations]]*), [[mao-zedong|Mao]] asserts that socialist society faces **two types of social contradictions**, "totally different in their nature":

1. **Between ourselves and the enemy** — antagonistic; a matter of drawing a clear line between enemy and ourselves.
2. **Among the people themselves** — generally non-antagonistic among working people; resolved by democratic methods: discussion, criticism, persuasion, education — "not by the method of coercion or repression."

"The people" and "the enemy" are **stage-bound political categories**: at the stage of building socialism, those who favour socialist construction are "the people"; those who resist or sabotage it are "enemies of the people."

## Semantic History

| Layer | Claim (attributed to Mao) |
|---|---|
| *On Contradiction* (1937) | Contradiction universal; methods of resolving differ by nature; some contradictions are openly antagonistic, others not; non-antagonistic can become antagonistic and vice versa |
| 1957 typology | Enemy vs people dualism for socialist China; lists intra-working-class, worker–peasant, worker/peasant–intellectual, working people–national bourgeoisie, and state–people contradictions (interests, democracy/centralism, leadership/led, bureaucracy) as among the people |
| National bourgeoisie | Dual character; contradiction with working class is exploiter/exploited (antagonistic in nature) but can be handled as among the people and resolved peacefully if policy is correct — or flip to enemy contradiction if mishandled |
| Six criteria (1957) | Words/actions should help unity of nationalities; socialist transformation/construction; people's democratic dictatorship; democratic centralism; Communist Party leadership; international socialist unity — **most important: socialist path and Party leadership** |
| Hungary 1956 lesson | Reactionaries inside a socialist country may exploit contradictions among the people (Mao's reading) |

Builds on [[dialectical-materialism]]'s contradiction vocabulary (as in Soviet/Chinese ML education) but applies it to **post-revolutionary governance** and policing the boundary of legitimate dissent.

## Operationalizations

- **Enemy contradictions:** dictatorship — suppress counter-revolutionaries; deprive certain exploiters of political rights for a period; compel obedience and labour reform (*people's democratic dictatorship* functions).
- **People contradictions:** democracy — obligatory administrative orders *plus* persuasion; criticism that takes "the stand of the people"; fight "poisonous weeds" with reasoned, non-metaphysical criticism (still mandatory criticism).
- **Error types Mao names:** Rightists who treat enemies as people; "Leftists" who treat people as enemies.

These are **political rules of classification**, not survey instruments. Who counts as "people" is a power-laden boundary — the concept's operational core and its principal danger.

## Applications

- CCP rectification and anti-rightist aftermath (historical context of 1957; outcomes not established by the speech alone).
- Later Cultural Revolution expansion of "enemy" categories — a **use history** that critical scholarship reads as collapse of the non-antagonistic safeguard (external literature).
- Comparative political theory: how revolutionary regimes categorize legitimate vs illegitimate conflict (link [[class-struggle]], [[ideology]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Boundary problem:** If "people" is defined by support for the Party's socialist path, opposition is definitionally pushed toward "enemy" — critics argue the democratic method is conditional on prior political loyalty.
- **Antagonism switch:** Mao admits improper handling can turn non-antagonistic into antagonistic; critics note the state's interest in that switch during campaigns.
- **Not social science:** Offers no independent evidence about actual Chinese class structure in 1957; it is a governing ideology of conflict management.
