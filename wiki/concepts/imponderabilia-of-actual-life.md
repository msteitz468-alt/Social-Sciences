# Imponderabilia of actual life

## Definition as Coined

Methodological concept coined by [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] in the Introduction to *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts of the Western Pacific]]* (1922). Phenomena that "cannot be recorded by questioning or documents alone but must be observed in full actuality": routine of a working day, care of body, manner of taking and preparing food, tone of conversational and social life, friendships and hostilities, how personal vanities and ambitions show in behaviour — "part of the real substance of the social fabric."

## Semantic History

| Moment | Sense |
|---|---|
| Malinowski 1922 | Second of three ethnographic avenues; paired with concrete statistical documentation and corpus inscriptionum |
| Later methods pedagogy | Absorbed into teaching of [[participant-observation]] as attention to everyday practice vs formal rules alone |
| Related later terms | Overlaps with later interest in tacit knowledge, habitus, and "actually existing" social life — without identity |

## Operationalizations

Malinowski recommended a systematic ethnographic diary; occasional "plunges into the life of the natives" (games, visits, walks) putting aside camera and notebook; close contact from village camping. Complements charts and tables rather than replacing them.

## Applications

- Charter element of [[participant-observation]] in anthropology
- Method design of [[malinowski-argonauts-study]]
- Contrast with interview-only or white-resident hearsay ethnography (Malinowski's polemic)

## Critiques and Limitations

What counts as "imponderable" is observer-dependent; diary-based claims are hard to audit; the posthumous *Diary* controversy (not yet ingested) complicates the published persona of the immersed ethnographer. Still a useful name for the everyday-behavior layer of fieldwork.
