# Nondecision

*(Attributed — Bachrach & Baratz; framed in [[lukes-steven|Lukes]], *[[lukes-power-radical-view-2005|Power: A Radical View]]*).* A **[[nondecision]]** is a decision that results in suppression or thwarting of a latent or manifest challenge to the values or interests of the decision-maker — the second face of power: control of **what is decided about**, not only who wins on the floor.

## Definition as Coined

Bachrach and Baratz criticized pluralist one-dimensional focus on overt decisions; their two-dimensional view adds mobilization of bias and nondecision-making. Lukes treats this as a major advance that still does not go far enough (needs the third dimension of preference-shaping).

## Semantic History

From “Two Faces of Power” (1962) and “Decisions and Nondecisions” (1963) through *Power and Poverty* (1970); central to Lukes’s three-map typology; related to Schattschneider’s mobilization of bias.

## Operationalizations

Identify issues kept off agendas; barriers to entry; anticipated reactions; Crenson’s air-pollution non-issues as Lukes’s prized empirical application.

## Applications

Community power studies; environmental non-politics; agenda-setting research.

## Critiques and Limitations

Hard to observe non-events; risk of circular inference; pluralist critics demanded operational criteria; Lukes still finds the view too behavioural.
