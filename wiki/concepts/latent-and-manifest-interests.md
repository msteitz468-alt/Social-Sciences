# Latent and Manifest Interests

## Definition as Coined

In Dahrendorf's [[conflict-theory|conflict theory]] (*[[dahrendorf-class-class-conflict-1959|Class and Class Conflict]]*, Ch. V), **latent interests** are the opposed role expectations structurally attached to positions of domination and subjection in an [[imperatively-coordinated-association|imperatively coordinated association]] — independent of whether incumbents consciously hold them. **Manifest interests** are those interests as articulated and organized by an interest group (union, party, association). The conversion of latent into manifest interests is the formation of conflict groups from [[quasi-group|quasi-groups]].

## Semantic History

The latent/manifest pair echoes broader mid-century sociological vocabulary (including Merton's latent/manifest functions) but is here applied specifically to *role interests* generating class conflict. It operationalizes the older Marxian distinction between class "in itself" and "for itself" without adopting Marx's property definition or revolutionary telos (see [[class-struggle]]).

## Operationalizations

Latent interests inferred from authority-role structure; manifest interests observed in platforms, demands, and organized collective action of interest groups.

## Applications

Explains why authority structures predict potential conflict even when organization is absent, and why organization (manifestation) is a variable condition, not automatic.

## Critiques and Limitations

Assigning "interests" independent of actors' awareness is a structuralist move open to the charge of imputing false consciousness; Dahrendorf treats latent interests as theoretical constructs, not psychological reports — a distinction that remains contested.
