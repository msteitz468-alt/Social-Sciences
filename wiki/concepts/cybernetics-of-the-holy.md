# Cybernetics of the Holy

## Definition as Coined

In *[[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999|Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity]]* (1999, Ch. 13), [[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]] names the **Cybernetics of the Holy** the closed adaptive loop by which religion participates in social self-regulation:

1. [[ultimate-sacred-postulates|Ultimate Sacred Postulates]] sanctify authorities, institutions, and directives.
2. The regulatory hierarchy shapes material and social conditions.
3. Conditions determine subjects' well-being.
4. Subjects as congregations re-establish (or refuse) USPs through ritual **acceptance** — so sanctified authority is ultimately contingent (usually mystified) on the governed.
5. Prolonged oppression may yield passive non-participation or active desecration/prophecy that strips sanctity from personnel, structures, or even USPs.
6. Authorities who wish to keep legitimacy must keep regulatory structures responsive.

> "The Cybernetics of the Holy is constituted of corrective actions initiated in response to pressure from those subordinate to regulatory hierarchies for the amelioration of unsatisfactory conditions prevailing within the systems governed by those hierarchies." (Rappaport 1999, attributed)

## Semantic History

Extends 1970s [[adaptive-structure]] and "Sanctity and Lies" arguments (*[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|EMR]]*) into an explicit cybernetic model of the Holy. Heavy [[bateson-gregory|Bateson]] inheritance (truth as system maintenance; graded adaptive responses). Contrasts with pure power: **"Power disrupts the Cybernetics of the Holy"** — colonial/foreign rule that does not depend on local liturgical sanctification breaks the loop (Ghost Dance, Melanesian millenarian movements as incomplete circuits).

## Operationalizations

**Graded desanctifying responses** (attributed ladder): lower-order reversible correctives (personnel, local directives) before structural change; highest-order responses seek to replace USPs themselves (endogenous wholesale USP replacement rare; often exogenous imposition). Time-lag is intrinsic: mystification and residual sanctity slow rapid divestment.

**Pathology (Ch. 14):** when ritual blesses power and other-worldly salvation without corrective engagement, sacred and numinous detach from regulation → **unholy**; family of lies (oppression, diabolical, idolatrous) marks broken holies.

## Applications

- Links [[ritual-regulation]] (Maring homeostat) to general political theology of legitimacy.
- Frames prophecy, reformation, and revolution as adaptive responses of different depth.
- Diagnoses state churches and fundamentalisms that overspecify or oversanctify material claims.

## Critiques and Limitations

Strong functional/cybernetic packaging of religion — contested by meaning-first and power-first theories. Historical cases (conversion, millenarianism) are illustrative, not a tested cross-cultural sample. Normative edge ("theory of correction") is explicit in the program. Record as attributed theory, not wiki-voice finding. Related debate spine: [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
