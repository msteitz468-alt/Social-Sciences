# Mediated and Unmediated Exchange

## Definition as Coined

In *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]* (esp. Ch. 7–9), Strathern analyzes Melanesian gift sociality through two exchange modes:

| Mode | Mechanism (attributed) | Examples she re-reads |
|---|---|---|
| **Mediated** | A person's influence on another is carried in a detachable **part** that passes between them (partibility) | Shell valuables, pigs in moka, kitoum/kula cycles, some bridewealth |
| **Unmediated** | Direct transmission or growth effects; persons do not detach parts of themselves in the same way; includes productive work as effect on partners | Feeding/growing, some sibling and conjugal work, certain ritual transmissions |

Unmediated exchange can appear as **"gift exchange without a gift"** — the paradox marks the absence of the object-gift while remaining inside gift (not commodity) sociality. Metaphoric vs metonymic gift functions (after Schwimmer, extended) further distinguish how prestige and substance move (e.g. moka metonymic disposal then metaphoric recovery of prestige).

## Semantic History

Part of Strathern's apparatus for Part Two synthesis; not an indigenous binary claimed for all Melanesia. Builds on and transforms Maussian [[gift-exchange]] and Gregory's gift/commodity contrast without reducing Melanesia to three obligations alone.

## Operationalizations

Comparative re-reading: Muyuw kitoum vs mwal/veigun; Hagen moka and pig personification; Trobriand wealth and *dala*; Sambia semen transactions mapped to mediated/unmediated; Daulo *wok meri*.

## Applications

[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study]]; Melanesian exchange ethnography; gender of valuables and marriage gifts.

## Critiques and Limitations

Etic pair; dense and easy to reify as typology checklist. Use with full study context.
