# Disenchantment

## Definition as Coined

**Disenchantment** (*Entzauberung*) is [[weber-max|Weber]]'s (attributed) name for the historical process in which magical and sacred powers are driven out of the world as reliable means of salvation and control, and religious-metaphysical worldviews are rationalized toward more systematic, abstract, and ethically consistent forms. In *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]*, [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]] treats disenchantment as the **religious-historical internal condition** of [[occidental-rationalism]]: it produces modern structures of consciousness (decentered world-relations, differentiated [[value-spheres]]) that later become institutionally effective.

Habermas (attributed) reads Weber as combining external (ideas/interests; carrier strata) and internal (logical-ethical rationalization of worldviews) analysis — a neo-Kantian double perspective of validity and efficacy.

## Semantic History

Weber's sociology of religion → mid-century secularization debate → Horkheimer/Adorno's darker reading of disenchantment as prelude to total [[instrumental-reason]] → Habermas's attempt to keep disenchantment's cognitive gains while resisting the totalization of reason as domination ([[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]]).

## Operationalizations

Interpretive reconstruction of worldview rationalization (magic → ethical prophecy → principled ethics; theodicy solutions; virtuoso vs mass religion); not a single variable.

## Applications

TCA's account of modern consciousness; bridge from cultural rationalization to the Protestant methodical conduct of life; background to formal law and science.

## Critiques and Limitations

Secularization and "disenchantment" theses are heavily revised in contemporary sociology of religion (re-enchantment, multiple modernities — not primary in this ingest). Habermas uses Weber reconstructively, not as settled social science.
