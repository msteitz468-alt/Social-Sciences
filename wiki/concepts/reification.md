# Reification

## Definition as Coined

### Lukács 1923 (primary Marxist sense for this wiki after HCC ingest)

In “Reification and the Consciousness of the Proletariat” (*[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]*), [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]] names **reification** (*Verdinglichung*) the process whereby, once the [[commodity]] form becomes the **universal structural principle** of society, a relation between people takes on the character of a **thing** and acquires a **“phantom objectivity”** — an autonomy that seems strictly rational and all-embracing so as to **conceal every trace** of its fundamental nature: the relation between people.

He treats Marx’s account of [[commodity-fetishism]] as the “basic phenomenon of reification”:

> It is only a definite social relation between men that assumes, in their eyes, the fantastic form of a relation between things. *(Marx, Capital I, as quoted by Lukács)*

**Dual structure (Lukács):**
- **Objective side:** a world of objects and relations between things (commodities, market movements) whose laws confront agents as invisible forces; knowledge can be used for advantage but the individual cannot modify the process by own activity.
- **Subjective side:** man’s activity becomes estranged, turns into a commodity subject to non-human objectivity of social “natural laws,” going its own way independently of man.

**Extensions beyond the factory:** progressive rationalisation and calculability (through Taylorism “into the soul”); bureaucracy and formal law (Weber used as diagnostic); journalism as apogee of reification; contemplative stance toward closed systems; time degraded to the dimension of space (“Time is everything, man is nothing… Quantity alone decides everything” — Marx via Lukács). Specialisation destroys the image of the whole → crisis of the specialised sciences.

### Berger & Luckmann 1966 (sociology-of-knowledge sense)

[[berger-peter-l|Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]] (1966): reification is the apprehension of human phenomena as if they were things — products of human activity forgotten as human products. A **modality of consciousness** and extreme of objectivation, available for institutions, roles, and identity generally — **not** limited to the commodity form. Do not merge this with Lukács’s capitalism-specific theory without argument.

### Giddens 1984 (discursive-ideological sense)

In *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, [[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] treats reification as a **discursive mode** that presents historically contingent social properties as fixed with the fixity of natural law — one main dimension of ideology — within [[structuration-theory]]. Distinct again from both Lukács and B&L.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Marx 1867 | [[commodity-fetishism]] — structural inversion under commodity production (Lukács’s explicit base) |
| **Lukács 1923** | Generalisation of fetishism into a theory of capitalist society’s objective forms and subjective stance; centrepiece of [[western-marxism]] |
| Löwy 2023 (reception) | Reification as Lukács’s major contribution, influencing Frankfurt School, Benjamin, Goldmann, Lefebvre, Debord |
| Berger & Luckmann 1966 | SoK modality; depoliticised relative to class structure (Marxist critique) |
| Giddens 1984 | Ideological-discursive presentation of contingency as nature |
| **Habermas TCA Vol. 1 (1981/1984)** | Reconstructs Lukács–Horkheimer–Adorno line; argues reification cannot be adequately treated within the philosophy of consciousness; reformulates the problem (Vol. 2) as system imperatives overriding communicative sociation rather than as "instrumental reason gone wild" — see [[theory-of-communicative-action]], [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld]] |
| Everyday use | “Treating people as things” — thinner than any of the above |

**Lukács 1967 self-criticism:** warns against equating reification/alienation with objectification as such (Hegelian error in *HCC*); insists labour and nature metabolism were missing; still treats the unmasking of reification as historically central. Löwy disputes that *HCC* simply identifies alienation with objectivity — reification/fetishism remain processes of social relations appearing as things.

## Operationalizations

Critical-interpretive, not a standard scale. Lukácsian markers: universal commodity exchange; sale of labour-power; rationalised labour process; calculability as governing principle; contemplative stance; fragmented specialisms; crisis as exposure of partial systems. B&L: role formulas (“I have no choice because of my position”). Giddens: ideological naturalisation in discourse.

## Applications

- **Critical theory of capitalism** (Lukács → [[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt]] / [[critical-theory]]): culture industry, instrumental reason, one-dimensionality as later elaborations in the reification family — **attributed** to those programs, not identical to 1923 Lukács.
- **Class consciousness:** proletariat shares reified immediacy with the bourgeoisie but is forced beyond it by the dialectic of its existence as commodity that can become self-conscious (Part III of the reification essay).
- **Sociology of knowledge / institutions** (B&L): taken-for-granted facticity of roles and institutions.
- **Ideology critique** (Giddens): naturalisation of contingency.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Contested lineage:** Marxist critics of B&L find reification depoliticised; phenomenological users under-cite Marx/Lukács.
- **Dialectics of nature / idealism charges:** Comintern critics (Rudas, Deborin) and later DIAMAT attacked *HCC* as idealist; see [[lukacs-dialectics-of-nature-debate]].
- **1967 authorial retraction:** identical subject–object and alienation=objectification errors; missing labour.
- **Scope:** Lukács’s theory is historically specific to **universal** commodity capitalism; applying it unchanged to all “thing-like” social appearances flattens the concept (his own episodic vs universal distinction).
