# Reification

## Definition as Coined

[[berger-peter-l|Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]] (1966):

> Reification is the apprehension of human phenomena as if they were things, that is, in non-human or possibly supra-human terms. Another way of saying this is that reification is the apprehension of the products of human activity as if they were something other than human products — such as facts of nature, results of cosmic laws, or manifestations of divine will. Reification implies that man is capable of forgetting his own authorship of the human world. … The reified world is, by definition, a dehumanized world.

They treat reification as a **modality of consciousness** and as an **extreme of objectivation** — possible on pretheoretical and theoretical levels; not only an intellectuals’ error. Through reification, the world of institutions appears merged with the world of nature. Formula for role reification: “I have no choice in the matter, I have to act this way because of my position.”

## Semantic History

Prior usages: Marxian fetishism of commodities ([[commodity-fetishism]]); Lukácsian *Verdinglichung* (not yet ingested). Berger and Luckmann give a sociology-of-knowledge formulation available for institutions, roles, and identity generally — not limited to the commodity form. They warn against treating reification as a historical fall from a golden age of non-reified consciousness; original apprehensions may already be reifying. **De-reification** is historically possible when social conditions make human authorship of the world visible again.

**Giddens (1984), distinct sense.** In *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, [[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] treats reification as a **discursive mode** that presents historically contingent social properties as fixed with the fixity of natural law — one main dimension of ideology in social life — within [[structuration-theory]]'s insistence that structure is always enabling and constraining, not an external thing-like force. Do not merge B&L's consciousness-modality with Giddens's ideological-discursive formulation without argument.

## Operationalizations

Modality of consciousness / interpretive diagnosis, not a scale in the source. Examples they discuss: marriage, law, roles, identity reified as fate.

## Applications

- Critical corrective within the [[sociology-of-knowledge]] (Conclusion: anti-reification as humanistic stake)
- Analysis of taken-for-granted institutional facticity
- Interface with [[alienation]] and Marxist critique without reducing reification to economic fetishism alone

## Critiques and Limitations

**Contested lineage:** Marxist critics may find B&L’s reification depoliticized (consciousness modality without class structure); phenomenological/constructionist users may under-cite Marx/Lukács. B&L place reification inside their dialectic rather than as a separate crisis theory of capitalism.
