# Divergent Evolution (cultural)

## Definition as Coined
In [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983), [[flannery-kent-v|Flannery]] and [[marcus-joyce|Marcus]] framed the [[zapotec-civilization|Zapotec]] and [[mixtec-culture|Mixtec]] as descendants of a common proto-Otomanguean ancestor ([[otomanguean-languages]]) whose cultures diverged over ~9,000 years — an explicitly biological-evolutionary analogy (adaptive radiation from a common stem) applied to two documented cultural lineages.

## Semantic History
The 1983 usage married the Romney/Vogt "genetic model" of Mesoamerican ethnology (which treated language, physical type, and culture as markers of a single historical unit) with processual comparative archaeology. The wiki flags that the underlying "genetic model" conflates the four categories the Evidence-Class Framework keeps apart; Flannery and Marcus's application rests principally on the linguistic reconstruction plus parallel archaeological sequences.

## Operationalizations
Paired regional sequences (Valley of Oaxaca vs. Mixteca Alta) built from [[archaeological-survey|full-coverage survey]], excavation, epigraphy, and [[ethnohistory]], compared period by period.

## Applications
The Cloud People itself is the type application; see the synthesis claims on [[zapotec-civilization]] and [[mixtec-culture]].

## Critiques and Limitations
Glottochronological divergence dates anchoring the model are contested (flagged in the source itself); the biological analogy risks treating cultural lineages as bounded, non-hybridizing units despite continuous interaction.
