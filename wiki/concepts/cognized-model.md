# Cognized Model

## Definition as Coined

In *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* ([[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]], 1968; elaborated in the 1984 Epilogue and in "On Cognized Models," *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*, 1979), the **cognized model** is the environment and its relevant relations **as understood by the actors** — including nonempirical agencies (spirits), ritual rules, and value-laden categories. It is paired with, not identical to, the [[operational-model]].

In the 1979 freestanding essay Rappaport states the opening premise: nature is seen through a screen of beliefs, knowledge, and purposes; humans act in terms of their **images** of nature, yet it is nature itself that nurtures or destroys them. Disparities between images and actual ecosystem structure are inevitable; adequacy of a cognized model is judged by **adaptive effectiveness**, not by isomorphic accuracy with the operational model.

## Semantic History

In the 1968 text the dual-model idea is foreshadowed by the contrast between native purposes (repaying ancestors and spirits) and **latent functions** (Merton) of ecological regulation. The terms are sharpened in Ch. 6, become the keyword essay **"On Cognized Models" (1979)**, and are restated in the **1984 Epilogue**. The pair entered textbook ecological anthropology and later debates over whether cognized models are "merely" ideological or can be evaluated for adaptive consequences without collapsing into economism. EMR Essay 1 already contrasts cognized vs operational **environment** in Polynesian island ecology.

## Operationalizations

Not a survey index: reconstructed from ethnography of ritual speech, taboo, spirit categories, and ancestor obligations among the [[tsembaga]], then generalized.

**Hierarchy of the cognized model (EMR 1979, attributed):** roughly five levels ordered by [[sanctity]], mutability, and specificity — (1) [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]] → (2) cosmological axioms → (3) rules of conduct → (4) imported indications (signals from environment/other actors) → (5) secular knowledge. [[liturgical-order|Liturgical orders]] organize meanings sequentially, simultaneously, and hierarchically.

**Reference values vs goal ranges:** reference values are set in the cognized model; goal ranges are independently established operational viability bounds. Maladaptive equilibration occurs when cognized reference values drive the system outside sustainable goal ranges ([[adaptive-structure]]).

## Applications

- Tsembaga ritual can regulate material variables **without** actors intending "homeostasis" — latent function relative to the cognized model (Merton; [[ritual-regulation]]; [[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-study]]).
- Maring spirit taxonomy and *rumbim*/[[kaiko]] cycle as structured cognized content (Red Spirits, Smoke Woman, etc. — date-stamped 1962–63).
- Modern inversion of knowledge hierarchy and money's "specious commensurability" as maladaptive reordering of meaning (Rappaport POSITION in EMR).

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics argue the distinction still subordinates meaning to systemic function, or that "cognized model" re-describes culture without interpretive depth comparable to [[interpretive-anthropology]]. Live dispute: [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
