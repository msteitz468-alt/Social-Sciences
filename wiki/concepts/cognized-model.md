# Cognized Model

## Definition as Coined

In *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* ([[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]], 1968; elaborated in the 1984 Epilogue and in "On Cognized Models," 1979), the **cognized model** is the environment and its relevant relations **as understood by the actors** — including nonempirical agencies (spirits), ritual rules, and value-laden categories. It is paired with, not identical to, the [[operational-model]].

## Semantic History

In the 1968 text the dual-model idea is foreshadowed by the contrast between native purposes (repaying ancestors and spirits) and **latent functions** (Merton) of ecological regulation. The terms **cognized model** and **operational model** are sharpened in Ch. 6 and become central in the **1984 Epilogue** (and Rappaport's 1979 essay "On Cognized Models"). The pair entered textbook ecological anthropology and later debates over whether cognized models are "merely" ideological or can be evaluated for adaptive/functional consequences without collapsing into economism.

## Operationalizations

Not a survey index: reconstructed from ethnography of ritual speech, taboo, spirit categories (*rawa* and related beings), hot/cold qualities, and ancestor obligations among the [[tsembaga]], then generalized. In 1984 Rappaport distinguishes **reference values** in cognized models from **goal ranges** in operational models — dysfunction when reference values fall outside goal ranges that systems can sustain.

## Applications

Used to argue that Tsembaga ritual can regulate material variables **without** actors intending "homeostasis" in the analyst's sense — regulation is a latent function relative to the cognized model (Rappaport invokes Merton). See [[ritual-regulation]] and [[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics argue the distinction still subordinates meaning to systemic function, or that "cognized model" re-describes culture without interpretive depth comparable to [[interpretive-anthropology]]. Live dispute: [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
