# Higher Immorality

## Definition as Coined

In *[[mills-power-elite-1956|The Power Elite]]* (1956), chapter 15, [[mills-c-wright|Mills]]
named the **higher immorality** the structural moral condition of the elite order. It "can
neither be narrowed to the political sphere nor understood as primarily a matter of corrupt men
in fundamentally sound institutions." It is "a systematic feature of the American elite; its
general acceptance is an essential feature of the [[mass-society]]." In the corporate worlds of
business, war-making, and politics, "the private conscience is attenuated — and the higher
immorality is institutionalized."

## Semantic History

Mills opposed the "bad apple" frame: when institutions are corrupting, many who work in them are
necessarily corrupted. Older codes of uprightness, he argued (attributed), no longer grip men and
women of the corporate era, nor have new codes replaced them with moral meaning for corporate
routines. Related phrases in the chapter include **structural immorality**, the **immorality of
accomplishment**, and **higher ignorance** (knowledge without democratic relevance). The diagnosis
caps the book's move from institutional anatomy to moral-political judgment.

## Operationalizations

Illustrative symptoms in Mills's text (attributed, mid-1950s examples): campaign funds and
blunted moral sensibility as structural requirements of fast political rise; white-collar crime;
relaxed public morality; cases that the public greets with "another one got caught today." The
underlying condition is systemic, not the small character twisted by a bad milieu alone.

## Applications

- Links [[power-elite]] structure to [[organized-irresponsibility]]
- Complements *White Collar* themes of alienation and political indifference at the mass end
- Provides the moral vocabulary through which New Left and later critics read Mills

## Critiques and Limitations

Charges of elitism or preacherly tone (parallel to critiques of *White Collar*'s craftsmanship
lament) apply here. Empirical "corruption studies" operationalize differently. Keep as
**attributed diagnosis**, not as a measured index of national morality.
