# The Submerged Tenth

## Definition as Coined

In [[du-bois-philadelphia-negro-1899|*The Philadelphia Negro*]] (1899),
[[du-bois-w-e-b|Du Bois]] used "the submerged tenth" for the **lowest social grade**
of the Seventh Ward Black community — the criminal, "vicious," and destitute
stratum he estimated at roughly a tenth of the population. His analytic point was
the mirror image of the label: because the community was internally stratified, it
**should not be judged by this bottom tenth** (attributed, Du Bois 1899).

## Semantic History

The phrase adapts a then-current reform trope of a "submerged" residuum of the
poor. Du Bois turned it into a **class-structural** claim about Black urban life
rather than a moral verdict. Four years later, in 1903, he coined its deliberate
inverse — the [[talented-tenth|"Talented Tenth"]], the college-trained leadership
stratum whose duty was to lift the masses. The two "tenths" bracket Du Bois's
stratification of the Black population from top to bottom. In later 20th-century
sociology the concept's descriptive territory was reoccupied by the
[[urban-underclass|"underclass"]] debate — with the recurring danger, which Du Bois
had already warned against, of reading structural position as moral failure.

## Operationalizations

Not a measured index. Du Bois derived the grade from his Seventh Ward canvass
(occupation, household, and conduct data); the "tenth" is an estimate, not a
verified proportion (the exact percentages were not confirmed in this web ingest).

## Applications

Anchors the structural reading of racialized urban poverty later developed on
[[black-urban-north]] and in the underclass literature.

## Critiques and Limitations

`contested: yes` — the moralized language of a "vicious" lowest class sits
uneasily with Du Bois's own structural argument, and later scholars debate how far
the grade scheme reflected Victorian respectability norms versus observed
stratification. The concept's afterlife also carries the risk Du Bois foresaw:
being weaponized to pathologize the whole community it was meant to defend.
