# Noreshi

## Definition as Coined

In [[chagnon-yanomamo-fierce-people-1968|Chagnon 1968]] (Ch. 2, intellectual environment), **noreshi** is an emic element of Yanomamö personhood: an **animal double / soul aspect** bound to the person. Chagnon described a dual patterning and complementary distribution in how noreshi relates to paternal/maternal lines (details as his mid-1960s field model — not independently verified in this ingest).

## Semantic History

Single-ethnographer English rendering in a student case study; later Yanomami ethnography (not ingested) may use different orthography or partition soul concepts differently. Keep Chagnon's noreshi dated to 1968.

## Operationalizations

Descriptive only in this source — tied to illness, vulnerability, and cosmological architecture (four-layer cosmos; *hekura* shamans) rather than to a measurement construct.

## Applications

- [[yanomamo]] — belief and ritual as of mid-1960s

## Critiques and Limitations

Thin relative to the political chapters; risk of over-systematizing a complex soul vocabulary from one observer. Not a load-bearing term in the ethics debate, but part of complete ethnographic coverage.
