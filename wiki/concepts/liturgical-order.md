# Liturgical Order

## Definition as Coined

In "The Obvious Aspects of Ritual" ([[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]], 1974; expanded *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|EMR]]*, 1979) and fully restated in *[[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999|Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity]]* (1999, Chs. 2–8), a **liturgical order** is the relatively invariant sequence of formal acts and utterances that constitutes a ritual as an event of a recognizable type. Without performance, a liturgical order is dead; performance **enlivens, realizes, and establishes** the order.

Formal definition of ritual (RRMH Ch. 2): *"the performance of more or less invariant sequences of formal acts and utterances not encoded by the performers."* Five features (encoding by other than performers; formality; invariance more or less; performance; formality vs physical efficacy). Uniqueness is in their **conjunction**. Ritual is "the basic social act" (Ch. 4) — social contract, morality, sacred, and a paradigm of creation are argued as intrinsic to the form.

## Semantic History

1974 *Cambridge Anthropology* → 1979 EMR → **1999 RRMH** book-length architecture: Ch. 2 form; Ch. 3 self-referential/indexical Maring messages; Ch. 4 performatives and basic social act; Ch. 5 form–substance / naturalization of convention; Chs. 6–8 time, eternity/communitas, simultaneity and hierarchy. Surfaces and formal entailments remain the method; final-causal system functions deferred to Chs. 13–14 ([[cybernetics-of-the-holy]]).

## Operationalizations

- **Five features** of ritual form (RRMH Ch. 2).
- **Self-referential vs canonical** messages; Peirce index/icon/symbol; digital representation of analogic processes; binary occurrence of ritual.
- **Acceptance** ≠ belief; obligation intrinsic to performance; meta-performative establishment of convention.
- **Three dimensions** of liturgical orders: sequential (time), simultaneous, hierarchical ([[ultimate-sacred-postulates]] → cosmological axioms → rules → environmental indices).
- Time out of time, tempo, frequency–bonding, eternity (Ch. 7); Yu Min *rumbim* as simultaneous hierarchical integration (Ch. 8).

## Applications

- Maring [[kaiko]] / *rumbim* as transduction and world-mending after warfare.
- Social contract and morality as entailments of form.
- Establishment and binding of [[logos-rappaport|Logoi]]; evolutionary response to lie and alternative.

## Critiques and Limitations

Formal necessity can look tautologous; symbolic anthropology may underplay polyvalent meaning here; political anthropology may underweight coercion (addressed via oppression/diabolical lies). Ecological [[ritual-regulation]] uses liturgical orders as homeostats; RRMH claims those effects rest on structural properties of the form, not only Maring ecology.
