# World-System Hegemony

## Definition as Coined

In [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems analysis]], **hegemony** is not only military dominance but systemic leadership: a core power organizes the rules and circuits of the [[capitalist-world-economy|world-economy]] for a historical period. Arrighi's 2004 chapter links hegemony to the prospects of [[antisystemic-movements|antisystemic movements]].

## Semantic History

Distinct from (though in dialogue with) Gramscian cultural hegemony and IR hegemonic-stability theory. Arrighi's systemic cycles of accumulation (*Long Twentieth Century*) are the full statement — not yet ingested.

## Operationalizations

Successive hegemonic cycles (Dutch → British → US); financial expansion phases; military capacity; consent of other core powers. Period boundaries are disputed.

## Applications

Arrighi 2004 on hegemony and movements; Amin 2004 on US-led triad imperialism as a third wave.

## Critiques and Limitations

State-centric vs capital-centric readings; China/end-of-US-hegemony debates; whether "hegemony" fits early modern Dutch case.
