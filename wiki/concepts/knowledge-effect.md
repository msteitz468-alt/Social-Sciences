# Knowledge Effect

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* Part One, [[althusser-louis|Althusser]] distinguishes a theory of the **history** of theoretical practice (how knowledges are produced, rupture, ideology→science) from the question of the **mechanism** by which an existing knowledge fulfils cognitive appropriation of its object. He proposes the name **knowledge effect** for that peculiarity of “those special products which are knowledges” — corresponding to what Marx called the “mode of appropriation of the world peculiar to knowledge.” The knowledge effect is not a Subject–Object guarantee (classical theory of knowledge) but a de facto effect of scientific (and, generically, theoretical) discourse.

## Semantic History

Balibar’s Presentation groups knowledge effects with [[symptomatic-reading]] and [[theoretical-practice]] as the package that replaces foundational epistemology. Althusser rejects founding the knowledge effect in an original real object, a Husserlian pre-reflexive life-world, or genetic psychology of gestures — “origin,” “original ground,” “genesis,” and “mediation” as nomads of empiricist ideology. He subdivides the generic object into at least **ideological knowledge effect** (recognition-misrecognition in a mirror relation) and **scientific knowledge effect**. An analogue **society effect** clarifies the problem: how a social formation produces the effect of its own existence for its agents. Later ISA work (1970, not this volume) reworks ideology’s recognition-misrecognition without superseding the 1965 knowledge-effect analysis of scientific discourse.

## Operationalizations

- **Synchrony / diachrony of proof.** Synchrony = organizational structure of concepts in the thought-totality (*Gliederung*); diachrony = succession of concepts in ordered proof. Knowledge effect arises in the “play” (mechanical sense) of the **unity-of-dislocation** between system and discourse.
- **Forms of order.** Mechanism lies in forms of order of scientific proof discourse, not in an origin story of discovery.
- **Written discourse.** Peculiarity of scientific discourse is to be written and to exist by reference to what is present as absence in each moment of its order — returning the problem of reading.
- **Circle opened by closures.** Unlike ideology’s closed circle, scientific discourse is “the circle perpetually opened by its closures themselves, the circle of a well-founded knowledge” (Althusser, June 1965).

## Applications

- Philosophical reading of *Capital* as production of knowledge of Marxist philosophy from its practical state.
- Distinction of scientific vs ideological effects of discourse without collapsing ideology into mere error.
- Macherey’s “work of concepts” as arrangement that produces scientific knowledge-effects rather than narrative of investigation.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Theoreticist risk: knowledge as production can underplay politics of theory (Althusser’s later self-correction: philosophy represents class struggle in theory).
- “Knowledge effect” never became as portable a slogan as overdetermination or ISA; often absorbed into theoretical practice.
- Mechanism sketch at the end of Part One remains programmatic rather than fully formalized.
