# Instrument-effects

**Instrument-effects** (Ferguson's term for a pattern he draws from [[foucault-michel|Foucault]]) are outcomes of planned institutions that are **two-edged**: they fail by the institution's own declared standards, yet produce regular, politically intelligible side-effects that serve other strategies of power.

## Definition as Coined

Ferguson (1990, Ch. 1 and Ch. 9, attributed) cites Foucault on the prison: the prison fails to reduce crime / reform inmates as advertised, but succeeds in producing a milieu of delinquency that is useful for surveillance and social control — effects that are at once instrumental and unintended relative to the official program. Foucault: one should reverse the problem and ask what is *served by* the failure of the prison (Ferguson quoting *Discipline and Punish*).

Applied to **"development"** in Lesotho: projects fail to transform rural production as planned, yet expand state bureaucratic reach and depoliticize poverty ([[anti-politics-machine]]). The instrument-effect is **two-fold**: institutional expansion of bureaucratic state power + conceptual/ideological depoliticization of poverty and the state. Planners may rank agricultural transformation primary and government expansion secondary; Ferguson argues the resultant intelligibility may reverse that ranking without anyone having plotted it.

## Semantic History

Rooted in Foucault's *Discipline and Punish* (Eng. 1977/1979); Ferguson crystallizes the phrase for development studies. Related later idioms: "effects of failure," "success of failure," "rendering technical." Not identical to simple "unintended consequences" — the point is **systematic intelligibility** of the side-effects, not random accident.

## Operationalizations

- Pair official project objectives with observed outcomes and concurrent state/political changes.
- Refuse success/failure evaluation as the analytic frame; ask what the apparatus *does*.
- Compare across cases for repeated patterns (Ferguson poses generalization as open).

## Applications

Analytic engine of [[ferguson-anti-politics-machine-study]] Part V; bridges Foucault's [[analytics-of-power]] to anthropology of development.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can slide into functionalist "everything serves power" if side-effects are assumed rather than demonstrated; Ferguson insists on case evidence (Thaba-Tseka). Keep attributed.
