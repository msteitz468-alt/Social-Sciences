# Metropole

Analytic term for the dominant pole of a colonial or global knowledge/power order — in [[connell-raewyn|Connell]]'s *[[connell-southern-theory-2007|Southern Theory]]* (2007), the rich capital-exporting countries of Europe and North America from which mainstream social science pictures the world while claiming universality.

## Definition as Coined

In imperial vocabulary, the **metropole** is the imperial centre relative to colonies. Connell loads the term for the **sociology of knowledge**: metropolitan social science embeds metropolitan viewpoints, problems, and audiences, and organises a division of labour in which (as Hountondji described for colonial science, per Connell) data-gathering happens in the periphery while theorising is reserved for the centre.

## Semantic History

- **Imperial administration / geography** — mother country vs. colonies
- **World-systems and dependency registers** — adjacent to [[core-periphery|core]] / [[centre-periphery|centre]] language
- **Connell 2007** — "global metropole" as the unmarked standpoint of general theory and of many globalisation sociologies; paired with relational "Southern" rather than a simple North/South map

## Operationalizations

Not a single index. In Connell's usage it is identified by wealth, capital export, institutional control of theory production (journals, curricula, granting bodies), and the habit of treating metropolitan experience as the default case of "society" or "modernity."

## Applications

- Critique of sociology's founding narratives and general theory (*Southern Theory* Part I) — [[global-difference]] under empire; classical canon as US postwar reconstruction
- Cases of Northern general theory: [[coleman-james]], [[giddens-anthony]], [[bourdieu-pierre]] as ethnosociology of metropolitan society framed as universal
- Settler-colony intellectual dependence (Australia as "branch office," Part II)
- Hountondji pattern: data in periphery, theory in metropole ([[hountondji-paulin]])
- Patterns of indigenous sociology, *Gharbzadegi*, [[prebisch-raul|Prebisch]] centre–periphery economics, Subaltern Studies, [[nandy-ashis|Nandy]] (Parts III–IV)
- "Studying up" the metropole-apparatus as research frontier (Ch. 10)

## Critiques and Limitations

"Metropole" can blur internal hierarchies (class, race, gender within the North) and differences among European/US formations; Connell partially offsets this by stressing *relations* and by noting that the metropole was itself remade by empire ([[nandy-ashis|Nandy]], as she cites). Pair with [[centre-periphery]] / [[core-periphery]] rather than equating all three without attribution. Not identical to world-systems "core" (trade/production hierarchy vs. knowledge institutions).
