# Duality of Structure

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]]'s Glossary (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, 1984): **structure as the medium and outcome of the conduct it recursively organizes**; the structural properties of social systems do not exist outside of action but are chronically implicated in its production and reproduction. Structure exists only as memory traces (organic basis of [[knowledgeability]]) and as instantiated in action; it is [[rules-and-resources|rules and resources]], not an external shell.

## Semantic History

Giddens contrasts **duality** with **dualism** (structure *versus* agency). Earlier formulations appear in *Central Problems in Social Theory* (1979) and *New Rules* (1976) — not yet ingested as primary. The term reorganizes classical problems of constraint and production (Marx, Durkheim, Weber) and Critical Turn reaction against [[structural-functionalism|Parsonsian]] system theory. Parallel but non-identical: Bourdieu's habitus–structure relation in [[practice-theory]] — do not equate duality of structure with habitus.

## Operationalizations

Programmatic theorem. Applied research sometimes codes "drawing on rules/resources" in interaction analysis or organizational studies; those operationalizations are secondary and variable.

## Applications

- Core of [[structuration-theory]] and [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] (Giddens position).
- Paired methodologically with analysis of strategic conduct vs institutional analysis (*Constitution* Ch. 6).
- Underwrites [[social-and-system-integration]], [[routinization]], and [[time-space-distanciation]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Recurrent charges (as positions, pending secondary ingest): abstractness; residual subjectivism or residual objectivism; thin empirical program. Giddens answers dualism by duality rather than by dissolving either pole.

[[sewell-william-jr|Sewell]] (*[[sewell-logics-of-history-2005|Logics of History]]*, ch. 4) calls the duality of structure "the main attraction of Giddens's theory" but argues its content is incoherent as Giddens states it: defining structure as wholly **virtual** while equating it with [[rules-and-resources|rules and resources]] is self-contradictory, since resources are actual (they exist in time-space). His reconstruction keeps the duality but recasts it as the mutual generation of virtual **[[transposable-schemas|schemas]]** and **actual resources** — "schemas are the effects of resources, just as resources are the effects of schemas" — and adds five axioms making the resulting structures transformable from within. See [[transposable-schemas]], [[agency]].
