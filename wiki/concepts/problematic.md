# Problematic (Althusser)

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[althusser-louis|Althusser]], *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*; Glossary.)*

A **problematic** is the definite theoretical structure — the system of questions, concepts, and necessary silences — that governs what can appear as an object of thought within a discourse. It is not a worldview in the weak sense, nor a mere aggregate of theses; it is the **organic system** that assigns each concept its place and makes certain questions possible and others unaskable.

Althusser credits the term’s decisive use to his friend **Jacques Martin** (dedication of *Pour Marx*). The Glossary links change of problematic to the [[epistemological-break]]: science is produced by making an epistemological break with ideology and constructing a new pattern (problematic).

**Vs totality (attributed, “On the Young Marx”):** Ideology is unconscious of its problematic; a problematic is not the same as a Hegelian expressive totality. Reading must recover the problematic that produces the answers — including what the text cannot say.

**Vs element-history:** Althusser rejects decomposing a text into “elements” (this bit Feuerbach, that bit Hegel) that are recombined teleologically into Marxism. The unit of analysis is the problematic as a whole; Marx’s early works are Feuerbachian *en bloc* in their problematic even when “original.”

## Operationalizations

Reading protocol (Althusserian): identify the questions a text *must* ask given its concepts; identify silences; refuse future-anterior history that reads the past only as preparation of the present science. “Symptomatic reading” (fuller in *Reading Capital*, not yet primary here) is the method twin of problematic analysis.

## Applications

- Periodizing Marx ([[epistemological-break]]).
- Distinguishing scientific [[historical-materialism]] from humanist ideology that may use overlapping words.
- Critiquing “inversion of Hegel” as if method could be extracted while leaving the Hegelian problematic intact.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Hermeticism:** if only the problematic authorizes questions, criticism from outside looks impossible (historicist objection).
- **Retrospective projection:** the “mature” problematic may be read back as the sole measure of the early work (humanist objection).
- **Thin popular use:** “problematic” as mild synonym for “problem” or “topic” empties the technical sense.
