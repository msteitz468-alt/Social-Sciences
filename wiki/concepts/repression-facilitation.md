# Repression and Facilitation

## Definition as Coined

Tilly (1978): **repression** is any action by another group which **raises** the contender's cost of collective action; **facilitation** lowers that cost; **political** when the other party is a government. Authorities may work on the target's **mobilization** (disrupt organization, freeze resources, ban assemblies) or directly on **collective action** (penalties, inaccessible targets). Tilly argues antimobilization is often more reliable for governments than raising action costs alone. Distinct from **power** (returns per unit of action). Threat often outweighs opportunity in shaping action (asymmetric model — attributed).

## Semantic History

Broader than police crackdowns alone — includes legal barriers, co-optation, subsidy of allies, toleration thresholds. Figures in *FMR* map repression, toleration, facilitation, and coercion.

## Operationalizations

Cost per unit of collective action; scale × power distributions of governmental repression (Tilly's diagrams).

## Applications

Why some groups act while equally aggrieved others do not; clustering of violence around polity entry/exit.

## Critiques and Limitations

Cost is multi-dimensional; subjective risk vs objective cost; facilitation by patrons can redefine interests (movement organization vs constituency).
