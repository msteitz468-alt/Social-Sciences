# Dysfunction

## Definition as Coined

In [[merton-robert-k|Merton]]'s codification of functional analysis (*[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]* Ch. III), **dysfunctions** are those **observed consequences** which **lessen** the adaptation or adjustment of a designated system — the counterpart of functions (consequences that contribute to adaptation/adjustment). An item may be functional for some units and dysfunctional for others; analysis seeks a **net balance** of consequences, not a global blessing of the status quo.

## Semantic History

Introduced to break the equation of functional analysis with celebration of existing structures. Merton argued that focus on functions alone can serve ultraconservative ideology, while focus on dysfunctions alone can serve radical ideology; rigorous analysis inventories both. Later textbook use often flattens "dysfunction" to "bad thing" without system reference.

## Operationalizations

Requires the same system-designation and objective-consequence rules as [[manifest-and-latent-functions]]. Accumulated dysfunctions and strains are Merton's internal route from functional analysis to **pressure for change**.

## Applications

Bureaucracy (rules functional for reliability, dysfunctional for flexibility); anomie and deviance as structured outcomes; science under political hostility.

## Critiques and Limitations

Same measurement and system-boundary problems as functional analysis generally; "dysfunction for whom?" must be answered or the term becomes rhetoric.
