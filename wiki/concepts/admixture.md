# Admixture (Population Mixture)

## Definition as Coined

**Admixture** is the mixing of previously differentiated populations, leaving in
descendants' genomes a mosaic of ancestry from distinct sources. In Reich's
(2018) synthesis it is elevated from a technical term to the organizing thesis of
human population history: mixture between highly differentiated populations is a
**recurrent** process, so that present populations are blends of past
populations that were themselves blends.

## Semantic History

Long a routine population-genetics concept (e.g. admixed populations such as
African Americans and Latinos), admixture was reframed by the aDNA revolution as
*the norm rather than the exception* across deep prehistory. Reich argues this
overturns both the folk model of primeval "pure" races and the mid-20th-century
archaeological retreat from migration ("pots are not people"): ancient DNA shows
repeated population turnover and mixture almost everywhere sampled.

## Operationalizations

Detected and dated with [[admixture-statistics]] (the Four-Population Test,
*f3*/*f4*, admixture graphs) and visualized with [[principal-component-analysis]].
An admixture signal establishes `associated_with`/mixture between source-related
populations; it does not by itself fix migration direction or social meaning.

## Applications

Archaic [[neanderthals|Neanderthal]] and [[denisovans|Denisovan]] admixture into
modern humans; the three-way formation of Europeans
([[western-hunter-gatherers]], [[early-european-farmers]], [[yamnaya-culture|steppe]]);
the [[ancestral-north-indians]]/[[ancestral-south-indians]] formation of South
Asians; sex-biased admixture in the genomics of inequality. See
[[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich (2018)]].

## Critiques and Limitations

The recurrent-mixture thesis, and Reich's claim that it dissolves "pure"
populations, is a strong interpretive frame — powerful but to be attributed, not
stated as settled cosmology. Admixture inference is model- and
reference-dependent, and vulnerable to unsampled [[ghost-population|ghosts]].
Crucially, genetic admixture ≠ cultural mixture ≠ language shift: a mixture event
carries no automatic implication about what languages people spoke or how they
identified.

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]]. Attributed; early nuclear aDNA horizon **(as of Stringer 2012)** — percentages and geographies have been refined by later work; do not promote absolute figures to wiki voice without later sources.*

### Pre-genomic admixture hypotheses

Hybridization interpretations of Mount Carmel (Skhul/Tabun) were proposed in the 1930s; McCown & Keith preferred a single variable population near Neanderthal–modern divergence rather than hybrids (as of Stringer 2012). Stringer: [[recent-african-origin|RAO]] never precluded modern–archaic interbreeding during dispersal; hybridization would not automatically mean same species — depends on scale/impact; if Neanderthals passed genes to moderns they are not “entirely extinct” at DNA level though the distinctive bodily population vanished (attributed).

### Neanderthal nuclear admixture (as of Stringer 2012)

Draft Neanderthal genome largely confirmed Out of Africa and Neanderthal distinctiveness (separation ~350 ka), but non-African modern genomes (Europe, China, New Guinea) lay slightly closer to Neanderthal sequence than African ones (as of Stringer 2012). Explanation: ancestors of Europeans, Asians, and New Guineans interbred with Neanderthals (or a Neanderthal-component population) in North Africa, Arabia, or the Middle East as they exited Africa ~60 ka; estimated Neanderthal input ~**2%** overall — surprisingly high to Out-of-Africa adherents (attributed). Yotova et al.: non-African X-chromosomes as much as ~**9%** Neanderthal-derived DNA at one locus (via Stringer). >200 genetic changes shared by modern humans to the exclusion of Neanderthals and chimpanzees (brain, skull/skeleton, skin/hair/sweat, energy, sperm) (as of Stringer 2012).

**Where and when:** Stringer argues against focusing only on late European contact (dying, low-diversity Neanderthals); admixture may have occurred earlier/warmer (possibly Levant ~120 ka Skhul-Qafzeh/Tabun era) (attributed). Advantageous light-skin genes apparently did not transfer from European Neanderthals; some European light-skin variants probably <15 ka; early Cro-Magnon linear physiques and Europeans’ relatively poor cold tolerance challenge simple European-hybridization narratives (as of Stringer 2012). Currat and Excoffier: the ~2% in French, Chinese, and New Guinean samples is not the same 2% — a measure of how **ineffective** hybridization was across separate events (biological, social, or demographic limits) (via Stringer). Stringer rejects Trinkaus’s claim that Neanderthal input is visible in most earliest European moderns (e.g. Lapedo Valley child ~27 ka); prefers ancestral modern variation or individual overlap (attributed).

### Denisovan and Melanesian archaic input

Long et al. diversity hotspot in Melanesia as exception to serial-founder decline from Africa (via Stringer). Denisovans related to living Melanesians: most plausible explanation (as of Stringer 2012) is Denisovan presence in SE Asia as well as Siberia, with pre-Melanesian migrants picking up ~**5%** Denisovan genes; New Guinea/Bougainville may have as much as ~**8%** total “archaic” genes (small Neanderthal component earlier in western Asia + larger Denisovan component later) (attributed). Abi-Rached et al.: some HLA immune-system variants in modern Eurasian populations possibly acquired via Neanderthal and Denisovan interbreeding (via Stringer). Australia not yet fully compared with Neanderthal/Denisovan genomes as of the book — important test bed (as of Stringer 2012).

### Within-Africa archaic admixture

Hammer et al.: ~**2%** archaic autosomal ancestry in sub-Saharan samples (Mandenka, Biaka, San), introgressed ~35 ka from an unknown African archaic possibly separate ~700 ka — possibly late heidelbergensis-like groups (via Stringer 2012). Stringer suspects some “archaic” DNA markers outside Africa may be African admixture carried out in dispersals, plus selection/drift — not only non-African hybridization (attributed).

### Serial founders vs pan-assimilation

Steady decline of genetic and cranial diversity with distance from Africa challenges assimilationist expectation of repeated diversity reversals wherever archaics mixed — except Melanesia (as of Stringer 2012). mtDNA/Y-DNA long suggested zero or near-zero Neanderthal–Cro-Magnon admixture, but they are only ~1% of DNA; autosomal data revealed more complex history (as of Stringer 2012). Occasional hybridization outside Africa with Neanderthals, Denisovans (heidelbergensis descendants?), SE Asian *erectus*, and perhaps floresiensis — ranked by probability/evolutionary distance — while rejecting Trinkaus’s claim that modern–Neanderthal interbreeding was the norm everywhere (as of Stringer 2012). Oase (Romania) H-O foramen, flat forehead, large molars used by Trinkaus as Assimilation evidence at >40 ka vanguard contact; Stringer finds teeth unlike Neanderthals too, origin unclear (attributed). Les Rois Aurignacian jaws: contested possible Neanderthal dental features / hybrid options (as of Stringer 2012).

### Perception and interbreeding outcomes

Stringer: long divergence implies deep differences in appearance, expression, body language, behavior, possibly smell; misreading risk with Neanderthals higher than within moderns — such factors as important as physical ones for interbreeding outcomes (as of Stringer 2012).
