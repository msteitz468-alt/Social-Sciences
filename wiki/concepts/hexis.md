# Hexis (Bodily Hexis)

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]*, "Belief and the body".)* The embodied dimension of [[habitus]]: political mythology realized in posture, gait, bearing, and the sexual division of bodily practice. Related to Mauss's techniques of the body and to Aristotelian *hexis*, reworked as social incorporation rather than mere skill or habit.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu.)* Bodily hexis is political mythology realized, em-bodied, turned into a permanent disposition — a durable way of standing, speaking, walking, and thereby of feeling and thinking. Implicit pedagogy can instil a whole cosmology through injunctions as insignificant as "sit up straight," inscribing fundamental cultural principles beyond consciousness and explicit statement.

> "Bodily hexis is political mythology realized, em-bodied, turned into a permanent disposition, a durable way of standing, speaking, walking, and thereby of feeling and thinking."

What is "learned by body," Bourdieu argued, is not something one has, like brandishable knowledge, but something one is. The body believes in what it plays at: it weeps if it mimes grief; it does not represent or memorize the past but enacts it. Relation to the body is a fundamental dimension of habitus, inseparable from relation to language and time, and not reducible to "body image" or "body concept."

**Application — Wacquant *[[wacquant-body-and-soul-2004|Body & Soul]]*.** [[wacquant-loic|Wacquant]] takes the learning-by-body thesis into the boxing gym: pugilistic dispositions as kinetically remodeled hexis; his own non–average-white-American hexis and French nationality as factors in gym acceptance; sexual temperance and bearing as part of [[bodily-capital]] ethic. See [[pugilistic-habitus]], [[carnal-sociology]].

## Semantic History

- **Classical *hexis* / scholastic habit.** Durable disposition; Pascal's automaton inclined by habit (proofs convince the mind; habit provides strongest proofs) — mobilized by Bourdieu while rejecting voluntary decision as origin of durable inclination.
- **Mauss — techniques of the body.** Socially transmitted uses of the body; Bourdieu radicalizes this as incorporation of political mythology and class/gender order.
- **Poincaré analogy.** Genealogies and maps are to practical social orientation as abstract maps are to practical sense of space — "a system of axes linked unalterably to our bodies" (cited in *Logic* ch. 1).
- **Bourdieu's gendered and classed body.** Full development in *Logic* Book I ch. 4 and Book II spatial/ritual analyses of the [[kabyle]] house and calendar; class body awaits *Distinction*.

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic description of deportment, spatial movement, and motor schemes — not scales:

- **Gendered posture ([[kabyle]]).** Man of honour walks at a steady, determined pace; woman expected slight stoop, eyes down; feminine virtue *lah'ia* (modesty, restraint) orients the female body downwards, inside, house; male excellence *nif* asserted upwards, outwards, toward other men. Same oppositions in ways of eating.
- **Division of labour as body schemes.** Three cardinal oppositions: inwards/downwards vs outwards/upwards; wet vs dry; continuous maintenance vs short discontinuous confrontation (ploughing, harvest, slaughter). Olive-gathering: man knocks down olives; woman stoops to gather — "woman gathers up what man casts to the ground."
- **Right/left, front/behind, house threshold.** Right hand to licit action, oaths, honour (*qabel*); left to awkwardness, ill omen, woman; dual house orientation so one enters and exits "on the right foot"; half-turn transformations between interior and exterior space.
- **Diffuse pedagogy.** Riddles, games of honour, gift-exchange participation, silent observation of men's assembly, parent–child relations — structural exercises transmitting *modus operandi* without discourse; social order imposed as temporal discipline on the body.
- **Symbolic power via bodies.** Collectively recognized capacity to neutralize or reactivate linguistic and muscular patterns (ceremonies marshalling practices and disposing bodies).

## Applications

- **[[kabyle]] sexual division of labour and space.** House cosmology (male dry/fire/high/day vs female moist/water/low/night); ploughman/harvester bodily protections (*arkasen*, leather apron) as meeting-point avoidance of antagonistic forces.
- **Honour as embodied.** Sense of honour inscribed in gaze, speech, walk — hexis as motor of challenge and gift dialectic (ch. 6).
- **Institution and *amor fati*.** Division of tasks induces vocation for assigned tasks, reinforcing classification systems that help produce the reality they appear to describe.
- **Modern class body** — gestured toward; full treatment awaits *Distinction*.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Over-interpretation of gesture.** Reading cosmology from posture risks totalizing ethnography; Bourdieu's own "economy of logic" warns against forced systematicity.
- **Ethnographic presentism.** Claims about Kabyle hexis must stay dated to Bourdieu's documentation moment and synthesis, not timeless "Kabyle body."
- **Agency and resistance.** *Amor fati* and compliant hexis can underplay counter-hexis, irony, or deliberate transgression — a common reception charge pending critical sources.
- **Mind–body dualism reverse.** Making body the deposit of social order can seem to re-naturalize domination even while denouncing naturalization.
