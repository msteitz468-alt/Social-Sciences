# Normalizing Judgement

## Definition as Coined

Second of the three "means of correct training" in *[[foucault-discipline-and-punish-1977|Discipline and Punish]]*. [[foucault-michel|Foucault]] describes a **micro-penality** of time, activity, behaviour, speech, body, sexuality — small punishments for small deviations — that ranks, differentiates, hierarchizes, homogenizes, and excludes. The reference is no longer (only) the law's binary permitted/forbidden but the **norm**: a standard that measures gaps, distributes ranks, and traces the abnormal.

Discipline, Foucault argues (attributed), "compares, differentiates, hierarchizes, homogenizes, excludes. In short, it normalizes." The **Norm** becomes a principle of coercion alongside (and often inside) the law — later generalized in the carceral chapter as the emergence of "judges of normality" throughout the social body.

## Semantic History

- **1975:** technical component of disciplinary training.
- **Relation to [[social-norm]]:** broader sociological concept of norms; Foucault's *normalizing* judgement is specifically the **punitive-corrective use** of the norm inside disciplinary institutions.
- **Relation to [[normal-and-pathological]]:** Canguilhem's history of the normal/pathological is part of Foucault's intellectual milieu (Foucault cites related problems); not the same page-object.
- Reception: "normalization" became a keyword of governmentality studies and disability studies — later layers not yet ingested.

## Operationalizations

- School marks, military drills, workshop fines, hospital regimens as micro-penalties
- Double system: gratification–punishment; ranking tables
- Production of gaps relative to an average or optimum

## Applications

Fuses with [[hierarchical-observation]] in the [[examination-foucauldian|examination]]. Extends into the [[carceral-archipelago]]'s continuous gradation from irregularity to crime. Central to [[foucault-discipline-and-punish-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of equating all ranking (grades, metrics, KPIs) with Foucault's classical-age apparatus without historical specification.
- Tension with emancipatory uses of norms (professional standards, rights baselines) — Foucault's focus is coercive normalization, not a complete sociology of norms.
