# Narrative Positivism

## Definition as Coined

[[abbott-andrew|Abbott]]'s name (attributed; *[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]*, especially chs. 4–6 and Epilogue) for a constructive alternative to [[general-linear-reality|general linear reality]]: a program that is **formal and generalizing** yet takes **narratives of cases** — ordered sequences of events — as the primary image of social process. It is *not* a rejection of quantification; it is reverse-engineered reform of what quantification formalizes.

Core commitments Abbott attributes to the program include:

- Social processes as **enchainment** of events with consequential **order**
- Possibility of **convergence** on typical plots or sequence types across cases
- Events as **complex particulars** (ensembles of attribute values), not free play of independent variables
- Methods: formal description and empirical categorization of sequences ([[sequence-analysis]], [[optimal-matching]], scaling), plus modeling families that respect time (time series, event history, game-theoretic interaction) — used for **pattern search** before (or instead of) net-effects causalism
- Explanation may be **historical/narrative** or **descriptive** (dimension reduction on trajectories), not only Hempelian covering-law “causal analysis”

## Semantic History

Abbott (epilogue, attributed) distinguishes the narrative program from three other relaxations of GLR: demographic flows of units, structural/network interdependence ([[harrison-white|White]]), and multivocality of meaning (ch. 2). He insists on disentangling dichotomies often conflated in the [[qualitative-quantitative-divide-debate]]: narrative vs analysis, interpretation vs positivism, constructionism vs realism, situated vs transcendent knowledge. “Narrative positivism” names his refusal to surrender formal methods to the variable ontology.

Related but distinct: [[ragin-charles|Ragin]]'s [[case-oriented-vs-variable-oriented]] and [[qualitative-comparative-analysis|QCA]] (configurational, often cross-sectional in classic form); [[lieberson-stanley|Lieberson]]'s internal critique of mock experiments (reform within variable ontology).

## Operationalizations

- Colligate [[event-occurrence-distinction|events vs occurrences]]; encode strings; distance and cluster ([[sequence-analysis]])
- Multicase narrative comparison via generic “plots” and [[entity-processes|entity-process links]]
- Identify [[turning-point|turning points]] as regime shifts between trajectories
- Time-horizon and Markovian assumptions treated as theoretically load-bearing, not defaults

## Applications

Careers, professionalization, welfare-state sequences, medical communities, interaction sequences (Heise ESA, Abell comparative narrative) as discussed in *Time Matters* (attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

Abbott reports (attributed, prologue/epilogue) that quantitative audiences often answered “we already know / no alternative,” and qualitative audiences treated the work as anti-quant propaganda then declared victory. Whether sequence distances identify generative processes remains disputed; narrative explanation is accepted by philosophers of history more readily than by many standard-methods colleagues (Abbott's report).
