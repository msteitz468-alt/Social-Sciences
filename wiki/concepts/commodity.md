# Commodity

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Book One, Part 1, [[marx-karl|Marx]] begins analysis of the capitalist [[mode-of-production|mode of production]] with the **commodity** — “an object outside us, a thing that by its properties satisfies human wants,” which appears as the elementary form of wealth where that mode prevails: “an immense accumulation of commodities.”

A commodity has a dual character:

1. **Use-value** — qualitative usefulness; the material content of wealth; realized in use or consumption.
2. **Value** (whose form of appearance is **exchange-value**) — quantitative relation of exchange; substance, Marx argues, is **abstract human labour**; magnitude is measured by **socially necessary labour-time** (→ [[labor-theory-of-value]]).

All theoretical claims on this page are **attributed to Marx** (*Capital* I) unless independently converged from other ingested sources.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Classical political economy (Smith, Ricardo) | Product for exchange; labour as source of value |
| Marx *Capital* I (1867) | Cell-form of bourgeois society; dual labour; value-form → money; fetishism |
| Later Marxism / sociology | Commodity form, commodification of life |
| Contrast: Simmel 1900 (`[[value]]`) | Subjective valuation and distance — different problem (do not merge) |
| Appadurai & Kopytoff 1986 (`[[appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986]]`) | Comparative [[commoditization]]: commodity as **phase/situation**, not capitalism-only product; [[regimes-of-value]]; [[singularization]] counterdrive |

## Operationalizations

As Marx develops them (*Capital* I, Ch. 1–3):

- **Substance of value:** abstract human labour (homogeneous labour-power expenditure), distinct from concrete useful labour that produces use-values.
- **Magnitude:** socially necessary labour-time — “the labour time required to produce any use-value under the conditions of production normal for a given society and with the average degree of skill and intensity of labour prevalent in that society.”
- **Value-form:** simple, expanded, general form of value; fully developed as the **money-form**.
- **Exchange:** private producers meet as owners of commodities; money as measure of value, medium of circulation, and money as such (hoard, means of payment, world money).

## Applications

- Starting point of *Capital* I’s entire architecture (preface: commodity-form as economic **cell-form**; force of abstraction as method).
- [[labor-power]] treated as a special commodity under capitalism.
- [[commodity-fetishism]] — social relations of producers appear as relations between things.
- Historical precondition for capital: generalized commodity production including labour-power (Parts 2 and 8).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Labour theory of value** contested by marginalist and later economics; record as Marx’s framework, not wiki-voice law.
- **Abstraction:** first chapters are deliberately difficult; Marx revised value-form and fetishism sections for the second German edition (1873 afterword).
- **Scope:** analysis of the simple commodity prepares capital; not a full market ethnography.

## Appadurai / Kopytoff comparative extension (1986)

*[[appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986|The Social Life of Things]]* (Appadurai ed., 1986) broadens the commodity beyond a capitalism-only product while remaining in conversation with Marx:

- **[[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]:** a commodity is any thing **intended for exchange**; the **commodity situation** is when exchangeability is the socially relevant feature — **phase**, **candidacy**, **context**. Types by destination, metamorphosis, diversion, and ex-commodities. Marx was right about **intensity** under industrial capitalism but wrong if commodities are treated as either fully present or absent. Barter is a special form of commodity exchange; the gift/commodity contrast is overdrawn *(attributed)*.
- **[[kopytoff-igor|Kopytoff]]:** [[commoditization]] as process; cultural marking and moral economy; [[singularization]] as counterdrive; slavery as processual person-commodity biography *(attributed)*.

Do **not** merge this comparative toolkit with Marx’s labour-value architecture or Polanyi’s [[fictitious-commodities]] — three related but non-identical problems. See [[regimes-of-value]], [[cultural-biography-of-things]], [[object-biography]].
