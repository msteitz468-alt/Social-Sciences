# Male Marriageable Pool Index (MMPI)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[wilson-the-truly-disadvantaged-1987|The Truly Disadvantaged]]* Chapters 3–4 (with Kathryn Neckerman; Ch 4 also Robert Aponte), the **male marriageable pool index** measures, for a race and age group, the **ratio of employed men to women** — operationalizing the idea that marriageability for men depends substantially on **employment**, so black male joblessness shrinks the pool of partners for black women and contributes to female-headed families and nonmarital births.

## Semantic History

Extends Moynihan/Clark/Rainwater-era concerns about black family structure with a labor-market mechanism against 1980s welfare-disincentive explanations (Murray). Aggregate racial construction (not race × class) is an admitted limitation in the text.

## Operationalizations

Census/employment aggregates by race, age, and sometimes region; related to female-headship and welfare trends in national and regional series (Wilson 1987 Ch 3–4).

## Applications

Central empirical support for Wilson’s claim that **joblessness**, not primarily AFDC generosity, drove black family change; feeds policy priority on black male employment.

## Critiques and Limitations

Aggregate race-only MMPI; marriageability is multi-dimensional (incarceration later emphasized — Wilson 2012 Afterword); cultural and gender-agency critiques; selection and reverse causation debates in family demography (record as positions when ingested).
