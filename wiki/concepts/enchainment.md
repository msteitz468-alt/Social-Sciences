# Enchainment (FACE)

In [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013), **enchainment** is one of four **FACE** social activities — **F**ragmentation, **A**ccumulation, **C**onsumption, **E**nchainment — that result in patterns in archaeological data (attributed, glossary). Enchainment links people through the social life of things (fragmented, circulated, kept) so that relations persist beyond co-presence.

## Definition as Coined

**FACE** = **F**ragmentation, **A**ccumulation, **C**onsumption, **E**nchainment — four social practices through which people arrange "cultural stuff" into patterned sets and nets of objects that give archaeological evidence its repeated forms (attributed, Gamble 2013 ch. 1 / glossary). Once materials are fragmented, the opportunity exists to **enchain** people through those fragments (attributed). Amplification of social engagement is achieved through the diversity and quantity of what is fragmented, accumulated, enchained, and consumed — "the FACE of the extended mind" (attributed).

Instruments vs containers are the two major sets of **material metaphors** that feed FACE practices: instruments (oldest, Terra 1) and containers (rise late Terra 2, dominate Terra 3 — boats, houses, baskets as keeping devices) (attributed, ch. 1).

## Semantic History

Belongs to British social archaeology's relational material-culture line; Gamble codifies FACE as a portable toolkit for deep-history reading and closes *Settling the Earth* by tying imagination’s evolution to FACE amplification across Terrae 1–2 before going-beyond becomes routine in Terrae 3–4 (attributed, ch. 9).

## Operationalizations

Refitting, long-distance transfers, ornament sets, gift exchange (shell armbands, obsidian), structured deposits — read as enchainment only under explicit interpretive argument (attributed). Pacific inter-island exchange is an explicit Terra 4 case of enchainment maintaining relations at a distance (attributed, ch. 8).

## Applications

Terrae 1–4 material culture interpreted as amplifying social networks; evolutionary advantage claimed for Terra 1 amplification of relational possibilities of stuff under variable climate (attributed, ch. 9).

## Critiques and Limitations

Interpretive; alternative formation-process and functional readings of the same patterns remain available. Not a substitute for site-level taphonomy. Horizon: as of Gamble 2013.
