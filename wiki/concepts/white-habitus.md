# White Habitus

**White habitus** is [[bonilla-silva-eduardo|Eduardo Bonilla-Silva]]'s adaptation of [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s [[habitus]] to the racial socialization of white Americans. He defines it (attributed) as "a racialized, uninterrupted socialization process that conditions and creates whites' racial taste, perceptions, feelings, and emotions and their views on racial matters," produced by whites' high levels of residential and social isolation from Black people.

## Definition as Coined

In Chapter 6 of *[[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-2018|Racism without Racists]]*, Bonilla-Silva argues that whites' near-total racial segregation — by his account even more complete than the segregation experienced by Black people — generates a shared "white culture of solidarity" with two central consequences: (1) in-group solidarity and a positive collective self-image, and (2) negative views of nonwhites, with Black people treated as the "racial antithesis" of whiteness (attributed). He builds the argument by analogy to subcultural theories others had applied to poor Black neighborhoods, insisting the same socialization logic must apply to whites' isolation.

## Semantic History

The concept extends [[habitus|Bourdieu's habitus]] — durable dispositions produced by social position — to the specifically *racial* dispositions of the dominant group, giving color-blind preferences (whom to befriend, marry, live near) the feel of natural, non-racial choice.

## Operationalizations

Bonilla-Silva infers white habitus from the gap between professed and enacted attitudes in the [[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-study|1997 college survey and 1998 Detroit-Area Study]] (attributed): large majorities of whites voiced tolerant attitudes, yet the overwhelming majority reported no close Black friends, no interracial romantic history, and white spouses — while not perceiving their own segregation as a racial phenomenon at all. Whites also systematically inflated acquaintances into "friends," and read Black clustering as "self-segregation" while not seeing equivalent white clustering.

## Applications

Explains why [[colorblind-racism|color-blind]] frames feel natural to whites, why professed tolerance coexists with segregated lives, and why even self-identified "racial progressives" retain color-blind residues. Bonilla-Silva grounds the account in social-identity and status-construction research from social psychology (attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

Relies on interpreting a professed-attitude/behavior gap as evidence of a durable racialized disposition; skeptics may read the same gap as ordinary social-desirability response bias rather than a distinct "habitus" (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]).
