# Holocene

The Holocene is the current interglacial epoch of the [[quaternary-glaciation|Quaternary]], spanning roughly the last 11 500 calendar years — the environmental stage on which the whole of agriculture, urbanism, and recorded history has played out. For [[environmental-archaeology|environmental archaeology]] it is both a chronological frame and an object of study: the period in which human societies moved from negligible to dominant influence on the global environment.

## Definition as Coined

"Holocene" (Greek *holos*, whole + *kainos*, recent — "wholly recent") was introduced by Paul Gervais in 1867 and adopted by the International Geological Congress at Bologna in 1885 for the post-glacial or "Recent" epoch. It was long defined by convention as beginning ~10 000 uncalibrated radiocarbon years BP.

## Semantic History

The epoch's boundary and meaning have drifted with dating technique. [[roberts-neil|Roberts]] ([[roberts-holocene-1998|1998]]) notes that the elegant round figure of 10 000 (uncalibrated) years was abandoned in favour of a calibrated onset — he adopts **11 500 Cal. yr BP** — precisely because the second edition moved entirely to a calendar (calibrated) timescale (see the book's Technical Box I, "Dating the onset of the Holocene"). In 2018 the International Commission on Stratigraphy formally fixed the base at 11 700 cal yr b2k via a Global Stratotype Section and Point in the NGRIP Greenland [[ice-core-analysis|ice core]], and subdivided the epoch into Greenlandian, Northgrippian, and Meghalayan ages (bounded by the 8.2 ka and 4.2 ka climatic events). The rise of the [[anthropocene]] proposal — that human impact now defines a new epoch — is the latest turn in the term's history.

## Operationalizations

Different traditions place and measure the boundary differently: the classic radiocarbon convention (~10 ka ¹⁴C BP), the calibrated calendar convention (~11.5–11.7 ka cal BP), the Greenland ice-core δ¹⁸O signal of abrupt warming at the end of the [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]], and pollen-stratigraphic boundaries (the [[blytt-sernander|Blytt–Sernander]] Preboreal). These do not all coincide, and mixing calibrated with uncalibrated ages across the boundary is a known precision error (Roberts 1998).

## Applications

The Holocene frames deep-time subject-matter pages in this wiki (see the [[holocene-chronology|Holocene chronology]] timeline). It is the backdrop for [[agricultural-revolution|agricultural origins]], the [[neolithic|Neolithic]], [[deforestation]], [[soil-erosion]], and [[desertification]], and for the study of [[holocene-climate-change|Holocene climate change]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Roberts (1998) stresses that the Holocene is not a period of climatic stasis: it contains real, sometimes abrupt, climatic variability (the early-Holocene thermal maximum, the 8.2 ka event, the [[holocene-climate-change|Little Ice Age]]). Treating it as a stable "natural baseline" against which to measure human disturbance is therefore misleading — a caution central to the [[environmental-determinism|environment-and-society]] debate and to conservation practice.
