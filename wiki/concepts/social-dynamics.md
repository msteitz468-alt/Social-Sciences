# Social Dynamics

## Definition as Coined

**Social dynamics**, for [[comte-auguste|Comte]], is the **theory of the natural progress of human society** (Martineau title of Book VI ch. VI) — the laws of succession of social states. It is the historical-developmental half of [[social-physics]], paired with [[social-statics]] (order/coexistence).

Comte bound dynamics tightly to the [[law-of-three-stages]]: progress is not random change but filiation of theological, metaphysical, and positive regimes (with subdivisions). He prescribed abstract historical method focused on the most advanced series and the most general facts (Book VI methodological and historical chapters). From the highest scientific point of view, he held, human development consists in educing more and more the characteristic faculties of humanity in comparison with animality (ch. VI).

## Semantic History

- Comtean half of sociology
- Not the same as twentieth-century "social dynamics" in systems theory or agent-based modeling — shared words, different programs
- Contributed vocabulary to later evolutionary and developmental sociologies that must be linked only with attribution — Spencer’s [[differentiation-and-integration]] / [[social-evolutionism]] is a **different master key** for social change (not three stages), though both are founding-era “progress” programs

## Operationalizations

Historical stages in Book VI (all **Comte's schema**, attributed):

| Chapters | Stage content |
|---|---|
| VII–IX | [[theological-military-system]]: fetichism → polytheism → monotheism (Catholic/medieval modification) |
| X | Metaphysical state and critical period of modern society (from 14th c.; negative doctrine; transitional dogmas) |
| XI | Rise of elements of the positive state; scientific and industrial materials of reorganization |
| XII | Revolutionary crisis; negative progression outruns positive; French Revolution as experiment |
| XIII–XV | Final estimate of method, doctrine, and social action of positive philosophy |

Rejects golden age / state of nature and pure event chronicle. "Predict the past" before the future (method chapters).

## Applications

Philosophy of history inside [[positivism]]; justification of positive reorganization as the next stage after revolutionary crisis; pairing Order (statics) and Progress (dynamics) as modern political problem.

## Critiques and Limitations

Linear progress, Eurocentrism, teleology toward the positive polity, and stage-forcing are the main later objections. Keep all stage claims in attributed voice.
