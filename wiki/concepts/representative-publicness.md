# Representative Publicness

Habermas’s term for the medieval/early modern type of publicity in which status and authority are *represented before* the people (coronation, court ceremony, church ritual), as distinct from the bourgeois public sphere’s critical publicity *by* private people assembled as a public.

## Definition as Coined

STPS §2: representative publicness is a status attribute of the rulers, not a social domain of critical debate. The bourgeois public sphere supersedes (without simply abolishing all residues of) this type.

## Semantic History

Analytic contrast within STPS; used in historical sociology of courts and estates.

## Operationalizations

Ceremonial and legal-status evidence vs press/club debate evidence.

## Applications

Periodizing European publicity; clarifying what is new in the bourgeois type.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether the binary oversimplifies early modern political communication.
