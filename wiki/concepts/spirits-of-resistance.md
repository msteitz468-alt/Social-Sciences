# Spirits of Resistance

## Definition as Coined

[[ong-aihwa|Ong]]’s title phrase for the dialectic between Malay factory women’s spirit-possession outbreaks and enforced industrial [[capitalist-discipline|discipline]]. *Hantu* visitations on the shopfloor are read not as cultural relic but as speaking to contemporary peasant-to-industrial experience — ultimately a claim “to be treated as human beings” (*manusia*), not an ideology of class struggle (Ong 1987, Ch. 9–10).

## Semantic History

**Emic layer:** *hantu*, *jinn*, *syaitan*, *datuk*/*kramat*, *toyol*, *pontianak*; *lemah semangat*; *kena hantu*; *bomoh* healing; pollution of “dirty” toilets vs “clean” production floors.

**Etic analytic move:** possession as **unconscious beginnings of a protest idiom** against labor discipline and male industrial control (dialogue with I.M. Lewis on possession as thinly disguised protest). Engaged with [[taussig-michael|Taussig]]’s devil/commodity mediation but **rejects** reading Malay *hantu* as elegant use-/exchange-value opposition as the primary local meaning.

**Managerial counter-vocabulary:** *penyakit histeria* / “mass hysteria”; biomedical causal stories (no breakfast, lack of sleep → ghosts); third-strike dismissal “for security reasons.”

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic episode description + national press cases, not possession-rate surveys. Signature numbers Ong reports: Sungai Way 1975 (**40** operators); 1978 microscope sections (~**120**; 3-day shutdown; goat sacrifice; “8,000 hours lost”); Penang late 1978 (**15** over 3 days); Pontian 1980 (**21**). Telok FTZ: recurring monthly residual attacks; microscope sections high-risk; *bomoh* every ~6 months; *kenduri* excluding operators.

Everyday companion repertoire (not possession alone): crying, slowdown, carelessness, temper, *tidak apa*, “female problems” exits, prayer-room exits — refuges that were also possession sites.

## Applications

[[ong-spirits-of-resistance-study]]; teaching type-case in anthropology of work, gender, and resistance studies; paired with Taussig devil contracts and with Ackerman’s Malacca factory possession notes.

## Critiques and Limitations

“Resistance” is Ong’s interpretive claim — discussable as affliction, contagion fear, medicalization, or managerial co-optation of ritual. Do not promote to wiki-voice universal mechanism. Date-stamp to late-1970s Malaysian FTZs and Ong’s 1979–80 Telok observations.
