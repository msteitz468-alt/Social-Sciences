# Procession

## Definition as Coined

Kirshenblatt-Gimblett and McNamara (1985) define procession as "a performance in motion through space," framed off from everyday movement by costume, props, scenography, music, and choreography, and designed to "compete with the existing environment... becoming for a time the dominant element." Schechner (1988:159–60): "In a procession... the event moves along a prescribed path, spectators gather along the route, and at appointed places the procession halts and performances are played... Usually a procession moves towards a goal."

## Semantic History

Formalized within 1980s performance theory (Schechner; Turner's multisensory reading of ritual) and the anthropology of public events — Handelman (1990) argued public events have comparable "forms" or "logics of design" distinct from their meanings, "devices of praxis" that "mediate persons into collective abstractions." Processions range along a continuum from unbounded, leaderless movement (the Mbuti molimo, per Turnbull) to fully fixed routes (the Via Dolorosa, its stations fixed by the Church only in the late 17th century). Davis (1986) on Philadelphia parades established that processional space is "structured and contested terrain" — participation can be exclusionary (African Americans barred from parades after the early 1770s).

## Operationalizations

Moore (2005) combined Bell's (1997) six ritual characteristics (formality, traditionalism, disciplined invariance, rule-governance, sacral symbolism, performance) with Handelman's technology-of-events to define five archaeologically tractable design-logic domains: **time, space, sound, roles, props**. This licenses the study of processional *form* in prehistory while disclaiming access to lost meaning; Moore explicitly declined to extrapolate precontact symbolic content from colonial/modern cases, citing Urton on the inextricability of Andean/Catholic syncretism.

## Applications

- **Andean ethnographic** (all with fieldwork attribution): Isbell's Yarqa Aspiy canal-cleaning at Chuschi; Gelles's Cabanaconde water-mayor ceremonies; Rasnake's Yura moiety-structured tropas and animate staffs; Bastien's Kaata New Earth ceremony mapped onto a body-metaphor of Mount Kaata; Sallnow's Qoyllur Rit'i pilgrimage with its "totemic topography" and role troupes (ukuku, machula, qhapaq colla, ch'unchos). Moore flagged the regional bias of this record toward southern Peru/Bolivia.
- **Ethnohistoric Inca**: Molina's ceremonial calendar (the Situa; Ccapac Raymi circuits between [[cuzco]]'s Haucaypata plaza, the suyus, and hilltop shrines, with [[huaca|huacas]] themselves processed); Guaman Poma's taxonomy of processions that militarily confront pestilence, tempest, and hail; Atahualpa's four-hour, four-kilometre entry into Cajamarca in November 1532 (Cieza de León); Betanzos on Huayna Capac's year-long royal tour, read by Moore as "dynamic diplomacy."
- **Material/iconographic**: [[moche-culture]] Sacrifice Ceremony and prisoner processions (corroborated by the Sipán tombs); the Chimú maqueta models from Huaca de la Luna with musicians, corteges, and sacrificial processions; Formative "axial" site plans (huaca-de-los-reyes, Purulén, Las Aldas, Sechín Alto) whose staircased, progressively restricted circulation Moore reads as processional architecture — see [[huaca-de-los-reyes]] and [[formative-ceremonial-architecture-debate]].
- **Funerary**: Moore's "Marching with the Dead" comparison of Inca, Chimú, and Moche funerals as multi-phase events with structured sound — noisy mourning fiestas, silent corteges (see [[soundscape]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

The Turnerian communitas reading of pilgrimage-procession is directly challenged by Sallnow (1987:205), whose Andean data show pilgrimage stimulating factionalism, intercommunity conflict, and ethnic segregation "not as perversions of a fraternalistic ideal but rather as the concomitants of the very extralocal, universal status that the shrines proclaim" — recorded as positions ([[turner-victor]] vs. Sallnow), not adjudicated. The design-logics approach deliberately brackets meaning; it recovers form and sensory structure only.
