# Relational Work

## Definition as Coined

In the 2017 afterword to *[[zelizer-social-meaning-of-money-1994|The Social Meaning of Money]]*, [[zelizer-viviana|Zelizer]] states that after identifying [[earmarking]] she developed **relational work** as central to all economic activity: people differentiate monetary transfers to match definitions of the relationship between parties — negotiating **matches (or mismatches)** among differentiated social ties, economic transactions, media of exchange, and variable meanings.

She distinguishes this from "simply generalized relational effort."

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |

| Zelizer 1994 (*Social Meaning of Money*) | Practices of earmarking money for relations — program precursor without the later label |
| Later Zelizer program / 2017 afterword | Named **relational work** as explanation of economic activity generally |
| *The Purchase of Intimacy* (2005) and after | Intimate payments and hostile-worlds critiques (not fully ingested here) |

## Operationalizations

Observable as earmarking choices: which medium (cash, gift certificate, tip, wage, relief voucher), which amount norms, which timing, which moral label, for which tie. Mismatches (wrong medium for the relation) produce anger, ridicule, or legal/ethical dispute.

## Applications

- Domestic allowances vs. gifts vs. wages.
- Tips and bonuses vs. salary.
- Welfare form of payment (EITC vs. cash assistance) as dignity of form.
- Intimate and sexual economies where payment form marks the relation.

## Critiques and Limitations

The 1994 book does not yet use "relational work" as a technical term; later texts carry the full apparatus. Do not back-project the full concept as if coined in 1994 — attribute the label to the later program while treating 1994 earmarking cases as the empirical base.
