# Symbolic Tokens

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (1990): **symbolic tokens** are media of interchange that can be "passed around" without regard to the specific characteristics of the individuals or groups that handle them at any particular juncture. They are one of two types of [[disembedding]] mechanism (with [[expert-systems]]).

**Paradigm case:** money ("money proper"), which Giddens treated as integral to the disembedding of modern economic activity and to international capital markets. He engaged classical and modern money theory (including Marx’s "universal whore" image of money as abstracting content of goods/services) as resources for the disembedding thesis, not as wiki-voice economics.

## Semantic History

Giddens’s label is specific to the 1990 disembedding apparatus. Related but distinct: Simmel on money; Polanyi on market exchange; later "token" usages in economic sociology. Keep attribution tight.

## Operationalizations

Illustrative (money, and other interchange media Giddens flags). Not a formal measurement scheme.

## Applications

- With expert systems, constitutes [[abstract-systems]].
- Explains how economic relations stretch across time-space independent of local personal knowledge of trading partners.
- Connects to [[trust]] as faith in the abstract capacity of the token system rather than only in known persons.

## Critiques and Limitations

Money is overdetermined in social theory; Giddens’s contribution is the placement of tokens *as disembedding mechanisms*, not a free-standing monetary theory. Contested if read as reducing all modern mediation to tokenisation.
