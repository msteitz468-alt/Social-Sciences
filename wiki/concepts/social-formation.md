# Social Formation

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Brewster Glossary of *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*, with Althusser’s interpolation; essays “Marxism and Humanism,” “Contradiction and Overdetermination.”)*

**Social formation** is the concept denoting “society” so-called — the **concrete complex whole** comprising economic practice, political practice, and ideological practice at a certain place and stage of development. [[historical-materialism|Historical materialism]] is the science of social formations.

Althusser’s interpolation in the Glossary: a concept denoting “society” so-called. The point of the technical term is to avoid the ideological notion of society as an expressive totality or as a collection of individuals, and to mark the object of science as a **structured combination of practices/instances** under [[structure-in-dominance]] and [[overdetermination]].

In “Marxism and Humanism,” the 1845 break installs “radically new concepts: the concepts of social formation, productive forces, relations of production, superstructure, ideologies, determination in the last instance by the economy, specific determination of other levels,” etc.

## Semantic History

| Layer | Content |
|---|---|
| Marxian usage | Concrete societies / “social formations” in historical writing |
| **Althusser 1960s** | Technical object of HM; multi-instance structure |
| *Reading Capital* (not yet primary) | Articulation of modes of production within a social formation (Balibar) — fuller treatment awaits that ingest |
| Anthropological structural Marxism | Precapitalist social formations as articulated modes; kinship dominant, etc. ([[structural-marxism]]) |
| Ordinary sociology | “Social formation” sometimes used loosely as synonym for society — loses Althusserian load |

## Operationalizations

Name a historical society as a combination of practices and instances; specify dominant instance and last-instance economic determination; analyze contradictions as overdetermined. Contrast with “mode of production” alone: a social formation may articulate more than one mode (program claim more fully in *Reading Capital*).

## Applications

- Replaces “man” / “human essence” as starting point of historical theory ([[theoretical-anti-humanism]]).
- Frame for political conjuncture analysis (balance of forces within a formation).
- Anthropological reanalyses of “primitive” societies as structured wholes rather than stages of a single unilinear essence.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Abstraction:** can float free of ethnographic or archival specificity (Sahlins-type critique of structural Marxism).
- **Last-instance economism** charge persists despite relative-autonomy talk.
- Without *Reading Capital*’s articulation apparatus, the *For Marx* concept is schematic — this page records the *For Marx* sense first.
