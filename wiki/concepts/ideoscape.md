# Ideoscape

One of [[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]'s five [[global-cultural-flows|-scapes]];
with [[mediascape]] it forms the pair of "landscapes of images." Attributed to
Appadurai.

## Definition as Coined

"Ideoscapes are also concatenations of images, but they are often directly
political and frequently have to do with the ideologies of states and the
counterideologies of movements explicitly oriented to capturing state power or a
piece of it" *(Appadurai 1996)*. They are "composed of elements of the
Enlightenment worldview" — "freedom, welfare, rights, sovereignty, representation,
and the master term *democracy*."

## Semantic History

Appadurai argues that the "diaspora of these terms and images across the world,
especially since the nineteenth century, has loosened the internal coherence that
held them together in a Euro-American master narrative," producing "a loosely
structured synopticon of politics" in which different nation-states organize their
political cultures around different inflections of the same keywords (invoking
Raymond Williams on keywords).

## Operationalizations

The circulation creates both **semantic** translation problems (lexical
equivalence across languages) and **pragmatic** ones (different genre-reception
conventions — Indian audiences reading political speech through Hindi-cinema
resonances, Korean audiences through Buddhist/neo-Confucian codings).

## Applications

Anchors the disjuncture thesis at the level of the state (siege "between
ideoscapes and technoscapes / financescapes / ethnoscapes / mediascapes").
Connects to [[deterritorialization|deterritorialized]] political movements and to
the analysis of the postnational.

## Critiques and Limitations

The boundary between [[mediascape]] and ideoscape is porous, as Appadurai
concedes; critics note the framework offers little account of *why* particular
keywords travel and take root where they do.
