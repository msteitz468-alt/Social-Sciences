# Deep human history

Programmatic label preferred by [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]] in *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013) over **prehistory**: "everything about the human past is history" (attributed, Gamble 2013 ch. 1). Aligns with historian Daniel Smail's neuro-history / deep-history arguments (Smail 2008, cited by Gamble) for a common narrative beyond nation-state shallow history.

## Definition as Coined

Deep human history integrates artefact, symbolic, genetic, and environmental archives across the six [[terrae|Terrae]], refusing a sharp cut that puts writing on one side of history and everything earlier outside it (attributed, Gamble 2013 Box 1.1 and intro). The written-history / material-prehistory distinction is treated as false; symbolic, genetic, and digital archives can impose a **veneer** on deep time if used uncritically (attributed, ch. 1). Two driving themes: **[[encephalization]]** and **[[global-colonization|global settlement]]** — and their disconnect (attributed).

## Semantic History

Part of a wider early-21st-century push (Smail 2008 neuro-history / psychotropic social participation, cited by Gamble; Shryock & Smail; archaeologists reframing "prehistory") against the literacy threshold as the start of history. Gamble's version is explicitly archaeological-geographical and settlement-centered, ending with Terra 5 reunions as the shallow-history re-entry into a deep settlement story (attributed, ch. 9).

## Operationalizations

Multi-archive synthesis (Box 1.1: artefact / symbolic / genetic / digital × Terra map); caution about recent **veneers** of climate, ecology, population, and ethnography colouring deep time ("ethnography with a shovel") (ch. 3); FACE and imagination as interpretive tools, not just data classes.

## Applications

Frames *Settling the Earth*; densifies [[world-prehistory]] historiography; pairs with [[global-colonization]] as the substantive process deep history narrates.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Deep history" can blur disciplinary standards (historical vs archaeological evidence classes). Gamble's usage is a framing choice, not a finding. Horizon: as of 2013.
