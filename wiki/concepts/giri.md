# Giri

## Definition as Coined

In *[[benedict-chrysanthemum-and-sword-1946|The Chrysanthemum and the Sword]]*, *giri* is the cluster of obligations Benedict presents as more burdensome and situational than *[[gimu]]*. *Giri* debts are to be repaid with **mathematical equivalence** to the favor received and have **time limits**. “Repaying giri” is full of malaise and is often defined as done **unwillingly** — “the repayment hardest to bear.”

### Two branches (Benedict’s chart)

**1. Giri-to-the-world** — duties within the circle of received *on* but outside core *gimu*:
- to liege lord
- to affinal family
- to non-related persons (gifts, favors, work parties)
- to insufficiently close kin (aunts, uncles, nephews, nieces) via common-ancestor *on*

**2. Giri-to-one’s-name** — a Japanese version of *die Ehre* (honor), **outside** the circle of *on*:
- duty to **clear** reputation of insult or imputation of failure (feud/vendetta — not reckoned as aggression)
- duty to admit no professional failure or ignorance
- duty to fulfill proprieties (respect behavior, not living above station, curbing emotion on inappropriate occasions)

## Semantic History

Emic term; Benedict’s two-branch analytic chart became the standard English teaching model and a target of later refinement. She discusses bright and dark faces of *giri*, revenge, suicide as name-clearing, and the Meiji-era elevation of *chu* that sidelined *giri* in official ethics while living *giri* persisted.

## Operationalizations

Obligation typology + narrative examples (Forty-Seven Ronin as *chu* vs *giri* drama; etiquette; professional face) rather than measurement. Linked tightly to [[shame-culture]] (audience, ridicule, *haji*).

## Applications

Benedict uses *giri* to explain revenge, meticulous politeness, sensitivity to insult, and the friction between obligation and the “circle of human feelings.”

## Critiques and Limitations

Over-systematization; class and historical variation; danger of explaining all Japanese conflict as *giri* drama. Attribute the dual chart to Benedict 1946.
