# Social information

## Definition as Coined

In the Preface and Ch 2 of *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]*, [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] framed the essay around **social information**: information about an individual concerning relatively **abiding** characteristics (not momentary moods), that is **reflexive and embodied** — conveyed by the very person it is about, through bodily expression in the immediate presence of receivers (attributed).

**Symbols** are signs frequently and steadily available and routinely sought. Subtypes (attributed):

| Sign type | Function |
|---|---|
| **Prestige symbols** | Establish special claims to prestige, honour, or desirable class position |
| **Stigma symbols** | Draw attention to a debasing identity discrepancy; break up an otherwise coherent picture |
| **Disidentifiers** | Break up a coherent (stigmatizing) picture in a *positive* direction desired by the actor — throw doubt on the virtual claim |

Fugitive (non-institutionalized) signs: **points** (prestige claims) vs **slips** (discrediting). Signs may be congenital or acquired, permanent or temporary; the same sign can mean different things across groups. Reliability is imperfect (false positives). The **"with" relationship** — who one is seen with — itself conveys social information (attributed).

**Visibility** is carefully distinguished from **known-about-ness**, **obtrusiveness**, **perceived focus**, and audience **decoding capacity** (attributed).

**Personal identity** (Ch 2): identity pegs plus unique combination of life-history items; biography as a single life-line despite multiplicity of role selves; organizational documentation; cognitive vs social recognition (attributed).

## Semantic History

Specialized Goffmanian usage (1963); related to but not identical with later psychology's "social information processing." Continuous with interaction-order concerns of *Presentation of Self* (primary not yet ingested).

## Operationalizations

Conceptual scheme of signs and biography; not metric in Goffman.

## Applications

Organizes the information-control half of the stigma scheme ([[passing]], [[covering]], techniques of control).

## Critiques and Limitations

Face-to-face and mid-century documentary focus; digital permanent records transform the information environment.
