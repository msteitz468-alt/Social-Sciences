# Cathedral of the Mind

Central metaphor of [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]]'s *[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* (1996) for the evolved architecture of cognition and its prehistory. Companion construct: [[cognitive-fluidity]] (theory); [[specialized-intelligences]] (chapels).

## Definition as Coined

Mithen (1996, attributed) compares the mind to a **cathedral** built over evolutionary time:

| Element | Cognitive content | Evolutionary phase (Mithen) |
|---|---|---|
| **Nave** | Generalized intelligence — domain-general learning and decision rules; slow, error-prone, limited complexity | Phase 1; survives as residual general intelligence |
| **Chapels** | [[specialized-intelligences]] (social, natural-history, technical, linguistic) | Phase 2; Romanesque isolation — thick walls, no access between chapels |
| **Doors/windows** *or* **superchapel** | Channels of cognitive fluidity; alternative design: Sperber’s module of metarepresentation expanding social intelligence | Phase 3; Gothic free flow of light and sound |

Each individual mind develops like a new cathedral from a genetic architectural plan under environmental influence. Evolution **tinkers** plans by natural selection and cannot return to the drawing board — hence historical joints remain visible in the modern mind (attributed). Schematic illustrations carry **no** claims about brain spatial localization (attributed caution).

## Semantic History

Teaching and popular-science metaphor specific to Mithen’s 1996 synthesis. Neighboring images in the wider literature: Cosmides & Tooby’s **Swiss army knife** (Phase-2 modular isolation); sponge and general-purpose computer (rejected by Mithen as inadequate for creativity); Fodor’s modular input systems vs non-modular central processes. Not an emic category and not a measurement construct.

## Operationalizations

Not quantified; organizes the three-phase narrative and book figures on the [[cognitive-fluidity]] theory page. Medieval-archaeology analogy (excavating building phases at San Vincenzo) structures how Mithen proposes to “date” nave, chapels, and openings from the archaeological record.

## Applications

Public and pedagogical presentation of modular-to-fluid cognitive evolution; entry point for [[cognitive-archaeology]]’s evolutionary-origins focus; pairs with play/drama framing of prehistory (Acts 1–4) in the same book.

## Critiques and Limitations

Metaphor risk: over-hardening “walls” between domains; cathedral teleology (cathedrals have a plan — evolution does not). Mithen stresses evolution has no redesign option (attributed). Contested as metaphor: low; contested as architecture: see [[cognitive-fluidity]] critiques.
