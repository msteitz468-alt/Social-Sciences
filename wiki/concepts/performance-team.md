# Performance Team

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]*, 1956) defined a **performance team** (or **team**) as "a set of individuals who co-operate in staging a single routine" — more fully, individuals whose intimate co-operation is required if a given projected **[[definition-of-the-situation|definition of the situation]]** is to be maintained (attributed). A team is a grouping **in relation to an interaction** (or series of interactions), not in relation to social structure or organization as such.

**Emergent team impression.** Whether members stage similar or dissimilar individual performances, a team impression arises as a third level of fact between the individual performance and the total interaction. For studying **[[impression-management|impression management]]**, Goffman argued the team and team-performance may be the best fundamental units of reference (drawing the team unit from von Neumann's game analysis of bridge).

**Not the same as:** clique (often protects from same-rank others), friendship, or action group pursuing ends by non-dramaturgical means. A rate-buster or a "flagrantly accessible" party guest can still be team-relevant as a threat to the collective definition.

### Structural features (attributed)

- **Reciprocal dependence:** any member can give the show away.
- **"In the know":** team-mates cannot maintain the audience front before one another; automatic **familiarity** (intimacy without necessary warmth).
- **Party line and unanimity:** public disagreement embarrasses the sponsored reality; prior consultation may be secret because independent agreement looks like truth.
- **Delayed sanctioning:** mistakes corrected after the audience is gone.
- **Secret society character:** what is secret is *how* they co-operate, even when membership is public.
- **Two-team interplay:** interaction often resolves into dialogue between performing team and audience-team; control of the setting often marks the "performing" side.
- **Director:** may have directive dominance distinct from dramatic dominance (star); may become marginal go-between between teams.

One-person "teams" and self-as-audience (conscience, self-deception) are limiting cases Goffman explicitly allowed.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |

| Goffman 1956 | Dramaturgical co-operation unit |

| Everyday use | "Team" as sports/management metaphor — not Goffman's criterion |

## Operationalizations

Map who must co-operate for a show to hold; who shares destructive information; who is allowed backstage. Not isomorphic with org-chart teams.

## Applications

Conjugal front before guests; staff before clients; officers before enlisted; nurses before patients; hotel management and maids (Shetland illustrations). Links: [[discrepant-roles]], [[communication-out-of-character]], [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Interaction-relative definition can underplay enduring organizations — Goffman redirected that to the establishment frame in the Conclusion.
- "Team" language can smuggle sports solidarity where Goffman meant reciprocal dramaturgical risk.
