# Monogenism

## Definition as Coined

**Monogenism** is the doctrine that all humans share a **single origin**, as against [[polygenism]] (separate origins or species). In pre-Darwinian British [[ethnology]], monogenism was the Prichardian default, often tied to biblical unity and environmentalist explanations of racial difference. After *[[darwin-descent-of-man-1871|The Descent of Man]]* (1871), evolutionary monogenism meant: all races descend from one primitive stock, whether or not classifiers call them species; the preferred rank for Darwin was **sub-species**, with “race” retained by habit.

## Semantic History

| Phase | Sense |
|---|---|
| Biblical / Prichardian | One creation; racial differences secondary (climate, habit) |
| Polygenist challenge (mid-19th c.) | Separate species/creations; hard hereditarian racism (Knox, Hunt, Morton) |
| Darwin 1871 | Evolutionist single stock; races as sub-species; monogenist–polygenist dispute “will die a silent and unobserved death” once evolution is accepted |
| Huxley’s “reconciliation” | Common origin so remote races had time to diverge — “new monogenism” absorbing polygenist observations of difference |
| Modern | Biological unity of *Homo sapiens*; reject fixed racial species — see [[race]], [[race-and-culture-debate]] |

## Operationalizations

Darwin’s *Descent* ch. VII operationalizes monogenism by natural-history criteria:

- **Against** specific rank: races fuse when coexisting; characters highly variable; endless classifier disagreement (2–63 “races”) shows graduation.
- **For** common descent: numerous unimportant structural and mental similarities (Tylor, Lubbock on shared tastes, gesture-language, convergent stone tools) unlikely to be independently acquired.
- Hybrid fertility evidence against specific distinctness judged strong (alleged sterility “almost valueless”).
- Each race **not** from a single pair — many individuals modified in the same direction.

## Applications

Axis of the Victorian [[victorian-ethnology-anthropology-schism-debate|Ethnological–Anthropological schism]]; transformed by Darwin into evolutionary unity-with-divergence. Feeds [[psychic-unity-of-mankind]] and the later Boasian rejection of race as mental destiny. Darwin’s residual racial hierarchy language (period construct) shows monogenism did not equal egalitarianism.

## Critiques and Limitations

Evolutionary monogenism could still underwrite hierarchical racialism (divergence + selection ranking “civilised” over “savage”). Stocking and others treat the post-Darwin settlement as absorbing polygenist *observations* into monogenist *origins*. Modern genetics confirms species unity but dissolves typological race — do not equate Darwin 1871 with post-2010 population genetics.
