# Public Sphere

Domain in which private persons assemble as a public and exercise critical judgment — in Habermas’s reconstruction, a historically specific category of bourgeois society (*Öffentlichkeit*), not a timeless synonym for “the state” or “the media.”

## Definition as Coined

In *[[habermas-structural-transformation-public-sphere-1989|The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere]]* (1962/1989), [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]] sorts multiple meanings of “public” and isolates the sense tied to **public opinion** as critical judge, publicity as a function of that opinion, and the public sphere as a domain between private society and public authority.

## Semantic History

German *Öffentlichkeit*; French *public* / *opinion publique*; English public opinion. Pre-bourgeois **[[representative-publicness]]**; classical **[[bourgeois-public-sphere]]**; modern mass-media publicity and PR. Post-Habermas: counterpublics, networked public spheres (not yet ingested).

## Operationalizations

Historical-institutional analysis in STPS; later deliberative-democracy and media research (not yet ingested).

## Applications

Constitutional publicity, press freedom, media critique, democratic theory.

## Critiques and Limitations

Bourgeois exclusions; idealization; translation debates; digital-era extension.
