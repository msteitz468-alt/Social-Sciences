# Performance Front

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]*, 1956) defined **front** as that part of the individual's performance which regularly functions in a general and fixed fashion to define the situation for observers — "the expressive equipment of a standard kind intentionally or unwittingly employed by the individual during his performance" (attributed).

### Components

| Element | Definition (Goffman, attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Setting** | Furniture, décor, physical layout, and background items supplying scenery and stage props; tends to stay put geographically |
| **Personal front** | Items intimately identified with the performer and expected to follow him: insignia, clothing, sex/age/race characteristics, size and looks, posture, speech, facial expression, gesture — some relatively fixed, some mobile |
| **Appearance** | Stimuli that tell of social statuses and temporary ritual state (formal activity, work, recreation, celebration) |
| **Manner** | Stimuli that warn of the interaction role the performer will expect to play (e.g. haughty vs meek) |

Observers typically expect **consistency** among appearance, manner, and setting; exceptions are analytically interesting. Fronts tend to be **selected, not created**: a given social front becomes institutionalized as abstract stereotyped expectations — a "collective representation" and fact in its own right. Taking an established **[[social-role|social role]]** usually means finding a front already established.

Fronts claim abstract facts (cleanliness, competence, integrity) claimable across many routines; a small vocabulary of fronts orients people across situations. Large organizations cut diversity at a few points so many statuses share a front.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Goffman 1956 | Full setting + personal front apparatus |
| Popular sociology | "Front" collapsed into "facade" or appearance only |
| Organizational studies | Branding, office design, dress codes as fronts |
| Digital | Profile layout as setting-analogue |

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic inventory of scenic and personal sign-equipment; architectural analysis of reception spaces; dress and demeanor coding. Not a single scale.

## Applications

Hotels, clinics, courts, shops, domestic hospitality; professional "scientific stage" of hospitals; hired luxurious settings (clubs). Links: [[front-and-back-region]], [[performance-idealization]], [[dramatic-realization]], [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of equating front with "fake" — Goffman's Santayana epigraph frames masks as cuticles, not non-hearts.
- Mid-century sign inventories (race, sex, class dress) are period materials; the analytical partition travels better than the examples.
