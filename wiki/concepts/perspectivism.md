# Perspectivism

## Definition as Coined

**Amerindian perspectivism** (Eduardo Viveiros de Castro, mid-1990s papers) holds that nonhumans are persons who see themselves as humans and that differences between kinds of being are differences of **body/perspective**, not of culture or soul. Related slogan: **multinaturalism** (many natures, one culture) versus naturalist **multiculturalism** (one nature, many cultures).

## Operationalizations (as Descola frames the distinction)

| | Standard [[animism]] (Descola) | Perspectivism (additional clause) |
|---|---|---|
| Nonhumans see themselves as humans | Yes | Yes |
| Nonhumans see humans as nonhumans (prey, spirits) | Not required | Yes — crossed inversion |
| Distribution | Amazonia, Arctic, SE Asia forests, etc. | Especially Amerindian myths; rarer in Asia per Descola |

Descola redefines metamorphosis as **anamorphosis**: coinciding with the other's viewpoint ("enter the skin"), not mere disguise. Physical difference is form/ethogram more than substance.

## Applications

Amazonian cosmology; ontological-turn debates; Descola four-ontology matrix; multi-species and "new animism" literature.
