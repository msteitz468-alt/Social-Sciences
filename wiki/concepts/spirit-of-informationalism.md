# Spirit of Informationalism

Castells’s Weber-echoing phrase for the cultural configuration of the [[network-enterprise|network enterprise]] and informational capitalism. See *[[castells-rise-of-network-society-1996|The Rise of the Network Society]]* Ch. 3. Methodological homage to Weber’s *[[protestant-ethic-thesis|Protestant Ethic]]*; substantive focus is [[informationalism]] as mode of development, not capitalism’s origin.

## Definition as Coined

Castells (attributed): the spirit of informationalism is **not** a new culture as a traditional value system (multiplicity of subjects and networks reject a unifying “network culture”), nor a single institutional charter. It is a common cultural code made of many cultures, values, and projects — a culture of the ephemeral and of each strategic decision; a multi-faceted virtual culture rearranging reality as a material force. Load-bearing formulation: “The ‘spirit of informationalism’ is the culture of ‘creative destruction’ accelerated to the speed of the optoelectronic circuits that process its signals. Schumpeter meets Weber in the cyberspace of the network enterprise.” Crystallizing any fixed network position as a cultural code sentences the network to obsolescence given informationalism’s variable geometry.

## Semantic History

Explicit dialogue with Weber’s spirit of capitalism; Schumpeter’s creative destruction; rejection of ultra-rationalist economics that treats activity as occurring in a cultural vacuum. Parallel method: historical individual unified from cultural significance (Weber), applied to organizational forms of informationalism.

## Operationalizations

Organizational-culture claims; not a survey construct. Read through practices of networking, multi-tasking flexibility, and rapid reconfiguration.

## Applications

[[network-society-theory]]; cultural analysis of the [[network-enterprise]]; contrast with industrial-era work ethics.

## Critiques and Limitations

Thin empirical operationalization relative to Weber’s religious materials; risk of aestheticizing precarious flexibility; diversity of network cultures may undercut any “spirit” unity (Castells builds that tension into the definition).
