# Expressions Given and Given Off

## Definition as Coined

In the Introduction to *[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life]]* (Edinburgh 1956), [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] developed the problem of how co-present persons acquire information about one another through **sign-vehicles**. Drawing on Gustav Ichheiser, he held that the individual must act so that he **intentionally or unintentionally expresses** himself, and others must in turn be **impressed** by him (attributed).

**1956 formulation (this edition):** Crucial facts lie beyond or concealed within the interaction; "true" attitudes, beliefs, and emotions can be ascertained only indirectly through **avowals** or what appears to be **involuntary expressive behaviour**. Activity in co-presence has a **promissory character** — others must accept the individual on faith. Only in social interaction do the objects of inference purposely facilitate and hinder the inferential process (attributed).

**Terminology note:** The famous textbook pairing **"expressions given" / "expressions given off"** is the standard secondary label for this intentional-vs-symptomatic channel distinction in Goffman's interaction program. The exact phrase pair is **not clearly present in the 1956 Edinburgh OCR** used for this wiki ingest; the conceptual distinction (intentional expression vs unwitting/involuntary signs; avowals vs symptomatic behaviour) **is** load-bearing in the Introduction. Later editions and secondary literature crystallize the given/given-off wording — treat the *slug* as the established concept name, the *1956 prose* as intentional/unintentional expression and promissory inference.

The same sign-accepting tendency that makes performers vulnerable to misunderstanding makes audiences vulnerable to **misrepresentation**: signs can attest to what is not there (Ch. I, attributed).

## Operationalizations

Separate designed messages from leakage (hesitation, affect displays, dress contradictions); audience suspicion directed at hard-to-fake cues. Lab "self-presentation" measures rarely capture both channels.

## Applications

First impressions; interviews; service encounters; any setting where trust rides on signs of unseen competence or character. Links: [[impression-management]], [[definition-of-the-situation]], [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Binary given/given-off can reify a clean split; Goffman also stressed calculation spectra and traditional role design without conscious intent.
- Edition/terminology drift: do not quote "given off" as 1956 Edinburgh wording without verifying the US 1959 text.
