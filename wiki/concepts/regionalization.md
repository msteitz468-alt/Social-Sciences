# Regionalization

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: the temporal, spatial or time-space **differentiation of regions** either within or between locales; important for counter-balancing the assumption that societies are always homogeneous, unified systems (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Not geographic "region on a map" alone — always structuration of social conduct across time-space. Incorporates Goffman front/back, urban ecology centre/periphery, and disciplinary partitioning (Foucault critical engagement).

## Operationalizations

Form of boundaries; character of ordering within larger systems; span/scale; enclosure/disclosure; front/back; centre/periphery and established/outsiders.

## Applications

- House rooms; hospital shifts (Zerubavel); school classrooms; shop floor; North/South Britain; world-economy cores.
- Links micro settings to institutional alignment via [[structural-principles]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Breadth of the term can blur distinct mechanisms (architectural partition vs class geography).
