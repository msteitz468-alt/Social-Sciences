# Seven Types of Ambiguity (Abbott)

## Definition as Coined

[[abbott-andrew|Abbott]]'s typology (attributed; “Seven Types of Ambiguity,” *Theory and Society* 26 [1997]: 357–91; *[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]* ch. 2) of ways the standard image of a survey item — one indicator standing for one real state of one real person — is often mistaken. Named after Empson's literary *Seven Types of Ambiguity*; applied to positivist measurement practice. Abbott defines **positivism** here operationally as commitment to measurement (with formalization of concept-level relations as a related layer).

| Type | Name | Content (Abbott, attributed) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Semantic | One indicator attached to more than one concept (years of school = education, culture exposure, reduced crime time…) |
| 2 | Locus | Indicator treatable as property of different social units (divorce rate as family vs community property) |
| 3 | Syntactic | Same indicator independent for one analyst, dependent for another |
| 4 | Durational | Ambiguity about the temporal span the indicator indexes |
| 5 | Narrative | Ambiguity about which narrative/plot the indicator is embedded in |
| 6 | Contextual | Meaning depends on the ensemble of other variables (from correlation-matrix indeterminacy) |
| 7 | Interactive / interactional | General indeterminacy of meaning in interaction |

Abbott argues (attributed) that standard fixes (new surveys, dummies, switching regressions) typically **exchange** one ambiguity type for another; his alternative starts from an indicator and maps the meanings attached to it across the positivist literature, treating ambiguity as **constitutive of social life**.

## Semantic History

Early ideas at SSHA Baltimore 1994; full paper 1997. Extends the univocal-meaning critique of [[general-linear-reality|GLR]] (assumption 3) into a research program on culture and multivocality. Empson is the literary source of the title; the sociological content is Abbott's.

## Operationalizations

Empirical program in ch. 2: systematic coding of literature using RELITEN and related GSS measures (~155 pieces), Jaccard clustering, periodization of how literatures trade syntactic for contextual ambiguity and drive ambiguity into interstices between studies (attributed findings from Abbott's analysis — single-study, not wiki-voice fact).

## Applications

Critique of survey culture; argument that positivist output can support quantitative inquiry into multiplicity of meaning (epilogue: qualitative audiences largely did not take up this offer, attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations
