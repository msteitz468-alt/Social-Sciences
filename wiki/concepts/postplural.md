# Postplural

## Definition as Coined

**Postplural** is [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]’s term (*[[strathern-partial-connections-1991|Partial Connections]]*, 1991) for a late twentieth-century anthropological **perception of the world** that succeeds simple pluralism. *(Attributed.)* Under plural perception, many cultures/perspectives multiply objects of knowledge. Under **postplural** perception, the relativizing effect of knowing other perspectives exist makes every approach feel **only ever partial**, while the recurrence of similar propositions across domains makes everything seem **connected** — partial *and* connected without a totalizing vista that any one perspective can claim.

Strathern states that the monograph artificially reproduces this postplural realization in its organization (self-scaling sections, remainders, jumps over gaps).

## Semantic History

Coined in the method register of late-1980s Melanesianist theory, not as a political slogan about multiculturalism. Distinct from:

- **Pluralism** as enumeration of more cultures/voices (Writing Culture’s polyphony can still be pluralist in form).
- **Postmodern pastiche** as free play of styles (Strathern wants connection-with-partition, not pure textual free-float).
- Later **ontological multiplicity** talk ([[ontological-turn]]) — related reception path via [[partial-connections]], but “postplural” is specifically her scale/perception diagnosis in PC.

## Operationalizations

Diagnostic of knowledge practice: when comparison seems both impossible (always more detail) and inevitable (same forms recur), analysts need images of **[[partial-connections]]** rather than maps that restore centers or taxonomies that restore genetic closure.

## Applications

- Organizing principle of *Partial Connections* ([[strathern-partial-connections-study]]).
- Bridge between [[writing-culture]] reflexivity and renewed [[comparative-method]] without holist units.

## Critiques and Limitations

Thin uptake as standalone keyword relative to “partial connections”; often absorbed into general talk of partiality. Not a claim that the world *is* postplural in wiki voice — a claim about anthropological perception in Strathern’s account.
