# Bodily capital

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Wacquant, *[[wacquant-body-and-soul-2004|Body & Soul]]*.)* **Bodily capital** names the boxer’s body as managed asset: physical resources requiring continuous maintenance (especially hands and face). The pugilist’s body is simultaneously **tool** (offensive weapon and defensive shield) and **target**. Successful pro practice requires an “extraordinarily efficient relation” to this capital — at the edge of rational management, yet not pure utility-maximization.

Wacquant argues the relation is a **pugilistic practical sense** — “corporeal thrift” acquired through long-term contact with athletes and coaches — neither full-knowledge maximizing calculation nor unmediated Foucaultian “dressage.” Knowledge of limits and tactics is practical “concrete science” (via Lévi-Strauss) drawn from daily training and hitting/being hit.

## Semantic History

Extends Bourdieu’s capital family ([[cultural-capital]], [[symbolic-capital]], etc.) to the organism as productive resource in a field. Related mid-century Chicago gym ethnography (Weinberg & Arond: body as “stock-in-trade”) is cited as continuous with Woodlawn observation. Not identical to generic economic “human capital.”

## Operationalizations

In *Body & Soul*, shown through practices, not scales:

- **Protection/fructification repertoire:** hand-wrapping, breathing, defensive tricks, creams/unguents, culinary regimens, roadwork.
- **Paradox:** use the body without using it up; navigate by eye between overtraining (shortens career) and under-discipline (injury / unexploited capacity) — precarious living conditions obstruct methodical plans.
- **Weight:** fight-weight surveillance; dangerous last-minute cuts; fighting up a class as handicap.
- **Time:** amateur overuse amputates capital before pro career; end-of-career labels (“washed up,” “dead meat”); trainer–manager optimizes return across fighter career, opponents’ trajectories, and promoters’ economic time.
- **Ideal-types:** Butch (frugality, solid job) vs Curtis (irregular hygiene, subproletarian insecurity).
- **Sacrifice cult:** food, sleep, sex as “Holy Trinity”; post-fight gendered capital shift (masculine controlled violence → feminine sexual exhibition).

## Applications

- [[wacquant-body-and-soul-study|Body & Soul]] — “Managing Bodily Capital”; fight-night weigh-in; Golden Gloves cut.
- Potential extension to other body crafts — mark as analogy until studied.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether “capital” over-economizes honor and care motives; convertibility outside the gym; gendering in a male gym — secondary critiques as ingested.
