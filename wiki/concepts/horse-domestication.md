# Horse Domestication

## Definition as Coined

**Horse domestication** names the process by which *Equus caballus* entered sustained human management. [[anthony-david-w|David Anthony]] (*[[anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007|The Horse, the Wheel, and Language]]*, 2007) insists on **staging** that the literature often collapses:

1. **Dietary/symbolic horse keeping** — possibly from ~4800 BCE in the Pontic-Caspian steppes (winter meat incentive; funeral head-and-hoof deposits with cattle/sheep).
2. **Riding or driving with bit control** — evidenced by [[bit-wear-analysis]] (Botai ~3700–3500 BCE; Anthony argues riding likely began earlier west of Botai in the Pontic-Caspian).
3. **Specialized breeds / size variability** — often dated ~2500 BCE; may reflect breed diversification, not first domestication.
4. **Cavalry / massed mounted archery** — Iron Age after ~1000 BCE; distinct from early mounted *raiding*.

Domestication for meat ≠ riding ≠ cavalry (Anthony 2007).

## Semantic History

Earlier size-variability methods (placing first domestication ~2500 BCE at sites like Csepel) are treated by Anthony as possibly late breed signals. Levine's age/sex models read both Dereivka and Botai as "wild" despite dissimilar profiles — Anthony treats those methods as ambiguous. The famous Dereivka "cult stallion" bit-wear case was **retracted** after AMS dating placed the specimen in the Scythian period (Anthony 2007; see [[dereivka]]).

## Operationalizations

- **Faunal:** NISP/MNI horse dominance; head-and-hoof ritual deposits; stalling chemical signatures (Olsen on Botai).
- **Pathology:** P₂ bevel ≥ ~3 mm and microwear matching experimental bitted horses ([[bit-wear-analysis]]).
- **Genetics (as reported by Anthony 2007):** highly diverse mare mtDNA vs. nearly homogeneous stallion Y-chromosome — many wild mares, few (possibly one) founding stallion lines.
- **Genetics (later layers):** post-2015 aDNA of horses is a separate research stream not in Anthony 2007 — do not back-fill.

## Applications

Steppe mobility models; [[yamnaya-culture]] wagon-and-riding package; [[botai-culture]] horse specialists; [[kurgan-hypothesis]] prestige and raiding capacity; Indo-European technical vocabulary for equids and vehicles (associated, not identical, with speaking communities).

## Critiques and Limitations

Organic bits leave different traces than metal; bitless control leaves little osteological signal; regional wild size gradients confound morphometrics; Botai people are **not** automatically Indo-European speakers (Anthony treats them as likely non-IE mounted foragers acquiring management ideas from western neighbors).
