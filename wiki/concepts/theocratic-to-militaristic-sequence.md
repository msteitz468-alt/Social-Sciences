# Theocratic-to-Militaristic Sequence

## Definition as Coined

In *[[adams-evolution-urban-society-1966|The Evolution of Urban Society]]* (1966), [[adams-robert-mcc|Robert McC. Adams]] frames early urban growth as a **homotaxial sequence of polity types** shared (as slightly variant patterns) by southern Mesopotamia and central Mexico:

1. **Theocratic polities** — temple-centered leadership, religious style intensity, Protoliterate Uruk / Classic Teotihuacan as type correlations.
2. **Militaristic polities** — formal militarism, bifurcation of religious and political institutions (Early Dynastic Mesopotamia; late Teotihuacan → Tula horizon in his correlation).
3. **Conquest states** — extended trade-and-tribute imperial realms (Akkadian; Aztec).

Figure 4 of the lectures graphs related **ramp / step / mixed** paradigms of early urban growth; Adams treats ramp and step as polar abstractions, preferring cumulative social differentiation (ramp-like) as the essay's main axis.

## Semantic History

Mid-century multilinear comparative ideal type for nuclear urban civilizations; related to but not identical with Childe's [[urban-revolution]] trait package or Service's theocracy-before-empire generalizations. Later primary-state literature often rejects any single ladder while still using temple→palace / ritual→military differentiation as regional narratives.

## Operationalizations

Adams operationalizes via **homotaxial phase correlation** (Figure 1 correlation set followed through the book): stylistic monumentality plateaus may align (Uruk–Teotihuacan "step") while class, military, and administrative differentiation continue as a ramp. Evidence classes: settlement survey, temple architecture, early texts (Mesopotamia), ethnohistory (Mexico).

## Applications

[[adams-evolution-urban-society-study]]; comparative [[state-formation]]; teaching contrast of Old and New World pristine urbanism under [[multilinear-evolution]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Two-case sample; 1960s chronologies; risk of forcing local sequences into a shared ladder; militarism and conquest are not unique endpoints and may interleave with theocracy earlier than the ideal type allows. Keep all claims attributed to Adams 1966.

## Homotaxial correlations (Adams 1966, attributed)

| Phase label | Mesopotamia (as of Adams) | Central Mexico (as of Adams) |
|---|---|---|
| **Theocratic polities** | Protoliterate [[uruk|Uruk]] — temple *en*, Eanna redistribution, writing/accounts under theocratic auspices; religious style intensity | Classic [[teotihuacan|Teotihuacan]] — monumental grid city, theocratic leadership with growing politico-military controls; Tlaloc as life-giving focus |
| **Militaristic polities** | Late Protoliterate militarism → Early Dynastic walls, *lugal*/*ensi*, palaces, assembly displacement by kings | End of Teotihuacan militarism → Tula/Toltec transition; dual rule; Tezcatlipoca/warrior-god ascendancy; religious–political bifurcation |
| **Conquest states** | Akkadian trade-and-tribute realm (Sargonids; Dilmun–Magan–Meluhha commerce; limited central continuity) | [[aztec-empire|Aztec]] Triple Alliance trade-and-tribute realm (*pochteca*, calpixque, palace redistribution); loosely knit beyond core |

**Not secularization:** Adams reads militarization as the rise of autonomous political goals of a warrior aristocracy still under religious conception, not simple desacralization of the state. **Rates differ, essentials parallel** (homotaxial method). Area difference: Mesopotamian social differentiation closer to smooth ramp A; Mexican sequence more pulsed (fragmentation and craft decline between Teotihuacan, Tula, Tenochtitlan peaks) → intermediate curve B preferred over pure ramp or pure step.
