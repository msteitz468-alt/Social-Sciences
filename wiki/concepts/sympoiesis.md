## Definition as Coined

Sympoiesis — "making-with" — was coined by M. Beth Dempster in her 1998 Master of
Environmental Studies thesis at the University of Waterloo. Dempster defined sympoietic
systems as "collectively-producing systems that do not have self-defined spatial or
temporal boundaries. Information and control are distributed among components. The
systems are evolutionary and have the potential for surprising change," contrasted with
autopoietic systems, which she characterized as "self-producing" autonomous units "with
self defined spatial or temporal boundaries that tend to be centrally controlled,
homeostatic, and predictable" (Dempster 1998, as relayed by Donna Haraway in
*(Haraway 2016)*, [[haraway-staying-with-the-trouble-2016|Staying with the Trouble]]).
Haraway reports the term reached her secondhand: "Katie King told me about M. Beth
Dempster's Master of Environmental Studies thesis," making Katie King the transmission
link between Dempster's coinage and Haraway's theoretical program *(Haraway 2016)*.

## Semantic History

Dempster's original coinage was a systems-theory term describing a class of biological
and ecological systems without fixed boundaries or central control. Haraway borrows the
term rather than claiming to coin it — she is explicit that "Dempster coins/borrows" is
the relevant genealogy, with Dempster credited as originator — and extends its scope
considerably beyond Dempster's systems-ecology use, making it the organizing concept of
her own philosophical program in *(Haraway 2016)*. In Haraway's hands sympoiesis becomes
a general alternative to self-organization/autopoiesis talk across biology, ecology, and
social theory: "Sympoiesis is a simple word; it means 'making-with.' Nothing makes
itself; nothing is really autopoietic or self-organizing," and she describes it as "a
word proper to complex, dynamic, responsive, situated, historical systems... a word for
worlding-with, in company" *(Haraway 2016)*.

Haraway is careful to state the relation as one of enfolding rather than simple
opposition: "Sympoiesis enfolds autopoiesis and generatively unfurls and extends it"
*(Haraway 2016)*. She also notes that Lynn Margulis's earlier work anticipated the idea
without the word — Haraway states that "[Margulis] would have chosen the term
sympoietic, but the word and concept had not yet surfaced" when Margulis described her
own symbiogenetic processes as "autopoietic," following Gaia theory with James Lovelock
*(Haraway 2016)*; see [[gaia-hypothesis]].

Haraway extends the sympoiesis frame with her own neologisms built on the same root:
"holoents" (proposed "as a general term to replace 'units' or 'beings,'" Haraway's
coinage), and descriptive compounds "symchthonic" and "symanimagenic," applied to the
[[chthulucene|Chthulucene]]'s "earthly ones" *(Haraway 2016)*. Elsewhere in the book she
states the concept even more strongly against its rival: "sympoesis enlarges and
displaces autopoesis and all other self-forming and self-sustaining system fantasies.
Sympoesis is a carrier bag for ongoingness, a yoke for becoming-with, for staying with
the trouble of inheriting the damages and achievements of colonial and postcolonial
naturalcultural histories" *(Haraway 2016)*.

## Operationalizations

Sympoiesis is not operationalized as a measurable variable in Haraway's usage; it
functions as a figurative-theoretical concept mobilized through illustrations rather
than tested empirically. Haraway cites, as illustrations rather than as independent
empirical findings, symbiogenesis research (Lynn Margulis's account of holobionts and
multi-genome fusion), model systems such as the Hawaiian bobtail squid–*Vibrio fischeri*
symbiosis and coral reef holobiomes, and collaborative projects she frames as "model
system[s] for sympoiesis" (the Crochet Coral Reef project; the Ako Project in
Madagascar) *(Haraway 2016)*. These are mobilized to make the concept vivid, not
presented as tests of it.

## Applications

Haraway applies sympoiesis as the master concept organizing her critique of
[[anthropocene|Anthropocene]]/[[capitalocene|Capitalocene]] framings and her positive
program of [[making-kin|making kin]] and multispecies "response-ability"
([[response-ability]]). She uses it to reframe [[gaia-hypothesis|Gaia]] itself: Haraway
argues "Gaia is a system mistaken for autopoietic that is really sympoietic" *(Haraway
2016)*, directly folding the Lovelock–Margulis hypothesis into her sympoiesis framework.
The concept also structures her account of fictional/speculative "sym fiction" ("the
genre of sympoiesis and symchthonia") in the book's closing Camille fabulation, linking
sympoiesis to [[sf-string-figures|SF]] as method *(Haraway 2016)*.

## Critiques and Limitations

As a concept transplanted from a graduate-student systems-ecology thesis into a
wide-ranging philosophical program, sympoiesis is contested territory: Haraway's
extension goes well beyond Dempster's original, bounded ecological-systems usage, and
the term's evidentiary basis in *(Haraway 2016)* rests on illustration (symbiogenesis
research, collaborative art-science projects) rather than argument that sympoiesis is
itself an empirically testable claim about the world. Because Haraway explicitly casts
sympoiesis as displacing autopoiesis as a general framework — not merely supplementing
it — critics working from systems theory or from Maturana and Varela's autopoiesis
tradition would regard the displacement claim as itself a contested theoretical move
rather than an established finding. The wiki has not (as of this ingest) located a
sustained scholarly critique literature engaging sympoiesis on these terms; flagged as a
gap in collection coverage.
