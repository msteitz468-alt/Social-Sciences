# Reflection Theory (of Knowledge)

## Definition as Coined

The doctrine — systematized by Lenin in *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* (written 1908, published 1909) — that sensations and ideas are "copies, photographs, images, mirror-reflections" of a material world existing independently of and prior to consciousness. Lenin attributed the formulation to Engels's habitual usage in *Anti-Dühring* and *Ludwig Feuerbach* (Gedanken-Abbilder, "mental images"), and defined matter correlatively as "the objective reality given to us in sensation" ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]).

Lenin's version has three linked commitments, all stated in attributed voice here: (1) objects exist independently of consciousness ("the earth existed prior to man"); (2) sensation *corresponds to* but is not *identical with* its object — Lenin fought Bazarov's formula "sense-perception is the reality existing outside us" as "the fundamental absurdity of Machism"; (3) practice — "experiment and industry," Engels's alizarin example — is the criterion that reflections are objectively faithful, though practice "can never... confirm or refute any human idea completely."

## Semantic History

- **1908–09.** Forged polemically against the Russian Machists ([[empirio-criticism-debate]]). Lenin distinguished his reflection doctrine both from Plekhanov's "hieroglyph"/symbol theory (a deviation, Lenin argued, that Bazarov exploited) and from Helmholtz's "theory of symbols," which he read as "shamefaced materialism" with Kantian lapses.
- **Extension to society.** In the same work Lenin extended reflection to [[historical-materialism]]: against Bogdanov's thesis of the "identity of social being and social consciousness," Lenin insisted "social consciousness *reflects* social being — that is Marx's teaching," illustrated by the peasant who sells grain on the world market without being conscious of the relations he thereby enters ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]).
- **Soviet codification.** The doctrine became a fixed component of official [[dialectical-materialism]] (see Stalin's 1938 codification), acquiring a dogmatic fixity the 1908 polemic did not yet have. Later Marxist and non-Marxist epistemologies of science largely abandoned the copy metaphor; that later history is not yet documented from ingested sources (as of 2026-07-16).

**The hieroglyph original, in situ (attributed):** the 1892 formulation is now primary — "Our sensations are in their way hieroglyphs which inform us of what is taking place in reality. The hieroglyphs do not resemble the events conveyed by them. But they can with complete fidelity convey... the relations existing between them" ([[plekhanov-notes-on-ludwig-feuerbach-1892]], Note 9). The original claims fidelity of *relations* without resemblance of *contents* — a structural-correspondence doctrine subtler than the "conventional signs" reading Lenin attacked or the copy doctrine he counterposed.

**The Plekhanov divergence, dual-documented (attributed):** Plekhanov's *Materialismus Militans* ([[plekhanov-materialismus-militans-1908]]) defends the "hieroglyph" theory — sensations as *signs*, not copies, of things-in-themselves, traced to Sechenov, with his own 1905/06 concession that the terminology was "ambiguous" — while Lenin's M&E calls the doctrine "an obvious mistake" (a judgment the Soviet edition of Plekhanov's own text records in its apparatus). The two founders of Russian anti-Machism thus held distinct epistemologies: sign-relation vs copy-relation. Both positions are now primary-sourced.

**A terminological caution from the opposing camp (attributed):** Bogdanov's *Empiriomonism* itself uses "reflection" — the physical world as a "reflection" of immediate experience within socially organized experience — with his own hedge about the term's looseness ([[bogdanov-empiriomonism-1906]]). This is not Lenin's copy-relation: for Bogdanov the "reflecting" medium is social organization, not the individual brain mirroring mind-independent matter. The shared word conceals opposed doctrines — a wikilink trap worth flagging.

## The 1914–15 Stratum (Private Notebooks)

Lenin noted privately (1914–15), in reading notes on Hegel's *Science of Logic* and *Lectures on the History of Philosophy* and in the independent 1915 fragment "On the Question of Dialectics" — none published in his lifetime; all first appeared in 1925–30 as part of what became *Philosophical Notebooks* — a treatment of cognition markedly more dialectical in emphasis than the 1908 formulation. Cognition, he wrote, "is the eternal, endless approximation of thought to the object. The reflection of nature in man's thought must be understood not 'lifelessly,' not 'abstractly,' NOT DEVOID OF MOVEMENT, not without contradictions, but in the eternal process of movement, the arising of contradictions and their solution" ([[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]]). Glossing Aristotle's *Metaphysics* on primitive idealism, he wrote that the mind's approach to a thing, "the taking of a copy (= a concept) of it[,] is not a simple, immediate act, a dead mirroring, but one which is complex, split into two, zig-zag-like, which includes in it the possibility of the flight of fantasy from life" — adding that "even in the simplest generalisation... there is a certain bit of fantasy," citing Pisarev on useful versus empty daydreaming ([[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]]). Reading Hegel's account of Life in the Logic, he framed the inclusion of Life in logic as intelligible "from the standpoint of the process of the reflection of the objective world in the (at first individual) consciousness of man and of the testing of this consciousness (reflection) through practice" (same source).

He also privately registered a debt the 1908 polemic did not credit: that "[d]ialectics is the theory of knowledge of (Hegel and) Marxism... to which Plekhanov, not to speak of other Marxists, paid no attention" ([[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]]; see also [[plekhanov-georgi]]).

**Continuity or tension — an open question, not adjudicated here.** These notes can be read as continuous with 1908 — "not a dead mirroring" elaborating rather than repudiating the copy-relation, since Lenin never renounces the independent-existence or practice-criterion commitments — or as marking a private shift toward a more Hegelian, self-consciously dialectical epistemology than the polemical *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* voice allows, one Lenin did not carry back into published doctrine before his death. Both readings are available from the primary material; the wiki records the tension without resolving it. The passages themselves were unpublished in Lenin's lifetime and reached print only via posthumous editorial assembly (see [[dialectical-materialism]] on the canonization history).

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Its operational edge in Lenin's text is the practice criterion: successful action on an object ("the proof of the pudding is in the eating," quoting Engels) counts as evidence that perception agrees with external reality; failure counts as evidence of perceptual error.

## Applications

Lenin deployed the doctrine to adjudicate the "crisis in modern physics" ("matter has disappeared"): only the *philosophical* concept of matter — objective reality outside mind — is binding, while every specific physical theory of matter's structure is a relative, historically superseded reflection; hence "with each epoch-making discovery... materialism has to change its form" (quoting Engels). He also derived his account of absolute and relative truth from it: absolute truth is "compounded from relative truths," with the limits of approximation historically conditional but the existence of objective truth unconditional ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

The contemporaneous critics are recorded on [[empirio-criticism-debate]]: the Machists held "elements" (sensations) to be the sole given, making the copy relation unverifiable; Bogdanov denied objective truth beyond socially organized experience. Lenin's own text concedes limits — the practice criterion is "never complete," and Dietzgen's formulations (which Lenin endorsed nine-tenths of) contain confusions Marx himself flagged. The theory's standing as epistemology of science is contested; the wiki records it as a position with a large historical footprint, not as a finding.
