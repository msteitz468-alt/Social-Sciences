# Labour-Power

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Part 2, Chapter 6, [[marx-karl|Marx]] defines **labour-power** (Arbeitskraft) as the aggregate of mental and physical capabilities existing in a living human being, which he or she sets in motion whenever producing use-values.

Under capitalism, labour-power becomes a **[[commodity]]**:

- Its **value** is determined by the labour-time necessary to produce the means of subsistence required for the labourer’s maintenance (and, under historical conditions, for the labourer’s family / replacement of the working class).
- Its **use-value** to the purchaser (the capitalist) is the capacity to produce value — and specifically more value than it itself contains (→ [[surplus-value]]).

**Attributed to Marx.** Spelling: wiki slug uses US “labor-power”; Marx’s English edition uses “labour-power.”

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| *Capital* I (1867) | Commodity form of human capacity to work; free double freedom |
| Later Marxism | Reproduction of labour-power; wage struggle |
| Neoclassical “labour” | Factor of production — different ontology |

## Operationalizations

Historical conditions Marx specifies for labour-power as commodity:

1. The labourer is **free** to dispose of labour-power as his or her own commodity.
2. The labourer is free in a second sense — **stripped of means of production** and means of subsistence, hence must sell labour-power to live.
3. That second freedom is the product of [[primitive-accumulation]] (Part 8), not a natural fact.

Value of labour-power includes a **historical and moral element** (standard of life), not only bare physical minimum.

## Applications

- Resolves the contradiction of the general formula of capital (M–C–M′): surplus cannot arise from circulation alone if equivalents exchange; it arises in production from the use of labour-power.
- Wage-form (Part 6) presents the value/price of labour-power as if it were the value/price of labour itself — a mystification.
- Factory legislation and the working-day struggle regulate the *use* of purchased labour-power (Part 3).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Contested as reducing human capacities to commodities (normative critique) vs. describing a real social form (analytic defense).
- Gendered unpaid reproductive labour is thin in *Capital* I’s formal definition — later feminist political economy thickens this (not yet ingested).
- Date-stamp: 1867 English industrial context for illustrations of subsistence and family wage.
