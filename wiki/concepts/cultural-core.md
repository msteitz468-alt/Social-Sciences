# Cultural Core

## Definition as Coined

[[steward-julian-h|Steward]] (*[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]*, Ch. 2): the **cultural core** is "the constellation of features which are most closely related to subsistence activities and economic arrangements. The core includes such social, political, and religious patterns as are empirically determined to be closely connected with these arrangements." **Secondary features** "may have great potential variability because they are less strongly tied to the core" and are "determined to a greater extent by purely cultural-historical factors — by random innovations or by diffusion — and they give the appearance of outward distinctiveness to cultures with similar cores."

## Semantic History

Stewardian neo-evolutionary / ecological anthropology (1950s). Later cultural materialism (Harris) reworked core-like priorities; political ecology and symbolic anthropology contested subsistence primacy. **Not** the same as world-systems [[core-periphery]].

## Operationalizations

- Define core empirically from technology–environment–behavior analysis ([[cultural-ecological-method]]).
- [[culture-type]] = similar cores + similar [[levels-of-sociocultural-integration]] across areas.
- Cross-cultural types consist of cultural cores of area types that recur independently (Steward Ch. 5).
- In advanced/industrial societies, core includes capital, credit, trade, and socially derived needs that became productive forces (Steward's extension).

## Applications

[[patrilineal-band]] core (hunting economy, descent, marriage, land tenure); Great Basin family-level subsistence fragmentation; irrigation-civilization primary vs secondary features (Ch. 11); Puerto Rico subcultural cores under national institutions.

## Critiques and Limitations

What counts as "core" is analytic choice; risk of tautology if core is whatever predicts the social form of interest; meaning systems, gender, and colonial power may be labeled secondary by fiat; advanced-society extension blurs the original subsistence focus.
