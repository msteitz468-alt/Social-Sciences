# Agency Detection Device (ADD / HADD)

## Definition as Coined

In CSR (White 2021), the **agency detection device** (ADD), earlier often **hypersensitive agency detection device (HADD)**, is the hypothesized cognitive bias to perceive agents in the environment under uncertainty — argued by some as a by-product basis for detecting invisible supernatural agents. Precursor: [[guthrie-stewart|Guthrie]]'s anthropomorphism thesis (*Faces in the Clouds*).

## Semantic History

Barrett popularized HADD; later literature often drops "hyper-" as ADD when hypersensitivity is questioned.

## Operationalizations

Biological motion tasks, point-light displays, threat-inducing and VR experiments (White method table): people sometimes detect agents in ambiguous situations, but threat does not clearly intensify detection — White reports that "hypersensitive" may be unwarranted.

## Applications

Links misfortune, ghosts, and monitoring gods to ordinary vigilance systems rather than a dedicated "God module."

## Critiques and Limitations

Empirical support for *hyper*sensitivity weak in White's survey; agency detection alone underdetermines full god concepts (needs ToM, MCI, cultural scaffolding).
