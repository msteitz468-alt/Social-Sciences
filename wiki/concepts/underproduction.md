# Underproduction

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]], *[[sahlins-stone-age-economics-1972|Stone Age Economics]]*, 1972, ch. 2.)* **Underproduction** names Sahlins' claim that primitive economies systematically **produce below their objective capacity** — "production is low relative to existing possibilities" — even while wants are met (underproduction is compatible with the [[original-affluent-society|"pristine affluence"]] of foragers). It is held to be intrinsic to the [[domestic-mode-of-production]]: production geared to livelihood halts once customary requirements are satisfied, leaving labor, land, and resources unused.

Sahlins identifies **three dimensions** of underproduction: (1) **underuse of resources** (land), (2) **underuse of labor-power**, and (3) **household ("domestic-group") failure** to meet its own subsistence needs.

## Semantic History

The term recasts, in a Marxian frame, older observations about "production for use" and the discontinuity of primitive labor (Firth 1929 on Maori work; Boeke's "dual economy"; colonial complaints of "lazy natives" who absorb productivity gains as leisure). Sahlins turns what earlier writers read as backwardness or irrationality into a structural property of a household-based economy oriented to use-value.

## Operationalizations

- **Underuse of resources:** swidden populations well below calculable carrying capacity — Naregu Chimbu at ~64% of potential, Kuikuru at ~7% (Carneiro); wild game and pasture exploited far below sustainable yield (Clark & Haswell); mongongo nuts left to rot in the Kalahari (Lee).
- **Underuse of labor:** short, intermittent working days (Kapauku ~2 hr/day, working alternate days by norm; Bemba and Toupouri work-diaries showing 40–45% of days largely non-working); age- and sex-based exemptions from production (Lele vs Bushong lifetime work-spans, per Douglas).
- **Household failure:** in Forde's Yako, Freeman's Iban, and Scudder's Mazulu Tonga, roughly 20–40% of households fell below the customary subsistence output in a given year — proposed as a structural, not accidental, feature.

## Applications

Underproduction is the empirical face of the [[domestic-mode-of-production]] and the premise of Sahlins' account of **intensification**: because the household underproduces and generates no surplus for collective ends (warfare, ceremony, large works), "the DMP's defects must be overcome, or else the society is overcome" — the opening for kinship pressure, chiefship, and the [[big-man]]. It also underwrites the [[original-affluent-society]] thesis: foragers could produce more but choose leisure once wants are met.

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Attributed.)* "Carrying capacity" is itself a contested construct, sensitive to assumptions about technology, fallow, and diet; "potential" output is a counterfactual. The three dimensions are illustrated from reworked ethnographic and statistical data of varying quality, gathered under colonial conditions. Whether underproduction is a universal structural law or a description of particular cases remains the framework's open question; stated in attributed voice.
