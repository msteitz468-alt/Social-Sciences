# Levantine Corridor

Geographic-cultural construct for the **core band of early Levantine neolithisation**: roughly the Jordan Valley – Damascus oasis – middle Euphrates alluvial strip. [[bar-yosef-ofer|Ofer Bar-Yosef]]'s label; central to [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]]'s (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Birth of the Gods]]* 2000) map of first agriculture among Sultanian, Aswadian, and Mureybetian groups after the [[khiamian|Khiamian]] symbolic shift.

## Definition as Coined

Not a political or ethnic corridor — an etic region of ecological opportunity and archaeological density for PPNA villages and early cultivation evidence.

## Semantic History

Used in Natufian/PPN literature for homeland vs periphery arguments; pairs with Mediterranean “core” Natufian models (see [[natufian-origins-core-periphery-debate]]).

## Operationalizations

Site distributions of early morphological domesticates, PPNA architecture, and related material culture within the band vs arid peripheries.

## Applications

Agricultural origins geography; diffusion baseline for PPNB expansion; contrast with hilly-flanks-wide nuclear zone models ([[braidwood-robert|Braidwood]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundaries fuzzy; northern Fertile Crescent monumental sites ([[gobekli-tepe]]) complicate simple corridor-only origin stories — record both positions.
