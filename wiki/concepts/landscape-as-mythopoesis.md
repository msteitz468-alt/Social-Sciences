# Landscape as Mythopoesis

## Definition as Coined

Tilley (1994, Ch. 2 synthesis) argues that in the ethnographic cases reviewed, **landscape is in substantial part a mythopoesis** — ordered by the same metaphoric/allegorical logics as myth — and that this cross-cuts hunter-gatherer vs cultivator and mobile vs sedentary dichotomies (*[[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-1994|A Phenomenology of Landscape]]*).

## Semantic History

Bridges symbolic anthropology / myth analysis with landscape archaeology inside [[post-processual-archaeology]].

## Operationalizations

Reading monument siting as claim on ancestral powers already resident in natural "megaliths" (outcrops, cliffs); Neolithic architecture as capture/control of land meanings.

## Applications

Part II prehistoric case studies — enculturation from Mesolithic landmark use to Neolithic monumental form.

## Critiques and Limitations

How far ethnographic myth–land logics transfer; under-determination for prehistory without texts.
