# Categorical Pairs (Paired Categories)

## Definition as Coined

[[tilly-charles|Tilly]] (1998): inequality-relevant categories typically operate as **paired, recognized, organized, unequal categories** — black/white, male/female, married/unmarried, citizen/noncitizen. Multi-religion or multi-race systems resolve into **bounded pairs** (Muslim/Jew, Muslim/Christian, Jew/Christian), each with its own boundary relations. Boundaries are incomplete (mixed or unclassifiable persons; situations where the distinction does not matter) but, where they apply, do organizational work producing durable resource differences.

## Semantic History

Builds on dual classification traditions (sacred/profane, in-group/out-group) but anchors the pair in **resource control and organizational problem-solving**, not only classification or identity.

## Operationalizations

Boundary audits in organizations; job-title segregation by binary category; citizenship dualism; pair-specific interaction scripts.

## Applications

Core unit in [[durable-inequality-theory]]; links to [[in-group-out-group]] and [[boundary-work]] without equating them.

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary framing may understate graded hierarchies and multi-dimensional intersection; Tilly treats intersection via multiple pair relations rather than a single multi-axis model.
