# Autopoiesis

## Definition as Coined

*Autopoiesis* ("self-production") was coined by the biologists Humberto Maturana and Francisco
Varela to describe living systems as "networks of productions of components that recursively,
through their interactions, generate and realize the network that produces them." [[luhmann-niklas|Niklas
Luhmann]] imported it into sociology in *[[luhmann-social-systems-1995|Social Systems]]* (1984)
to mark a shift from "self-organization" (the formation or change of structures) to
self-reference at the level of the **constitution of the elements themselves**: an autopoietic
system produces the very elements out of which it is produced.

The decisive move (Luhmann, attributed) is redefining the elements of a **social** system so
that they are not living beings but **[[communication|communications]]** — "vanishing events in
time that, in producing the networks that produce them, constitute emergent orders of
temporalized complexity." Different autopoietic system types are distinguished by their medium:
cells reproduce chemically, brains neurophysiologically, [[psychic-system|psychic systems]] and
social systems in the medium of **[[meaning]]**.

## Semantic History

The term migrated from theoretical biology (organisms, cells) to social theory, and the
migration is contested at its source: Maturana himself doubted that social systems are
autopoietic in his sense, since their "components" (people) are not produced by the network.
Luhmann's answer was to change the components — communications, not people. Luhmann also
departs from Maturana on the observer: Maturana's autopoiesis requires an external
observer-system to draw the system/environment distinction, whereas Luhmann makes
self-observation internal to autopoietic reproduction.

The concept carries a companion notion, **operational closure**: Luhmann redefines a "closed"
system not as one without an environment but as one that "create[s] everything that they use
as an element." Closure is not an end in itself but "the condition of possibility for
openness" — "all openness is based on closure" (Luhmann citing Edgar Morin). For meaning
systems, closure is specifically "the control of the system's own possibilities for negation
while producing its own elements."

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Autopoiesis functions as a criterion (does a system produce its
own elements?) and organizes description; it is not quantified. Luhmann applies it as a test:
communication exists "only" within the one autopoietic communication system of society.

## Applications

- The foundation of Luhmann's [[social-systems-theory]]: society and its function systems as
  autopoietic communication systems.
- **Operational closure** grounds his account of function-system autonomy (economy, law,
  science each closed under its own code — see [[functional-differentiation]]).
- Beyond Luhmann, autopoietic legal theory (Teubner) and organization/communication studies
  took up the concept.

## Critiques and Limitations

Maturana's own reservation — that "social autopoiesis" over-extends a biological concept —
remains the standard objection; critics charge the transfer is metaphorical rather than
rigorous. The near-tautological self-reference of the construct (systems defined by producing
what produces them) makes it hard to falsify. Luhmann answered that the very universality of
the concept is what lets a theory include itself in its object (see [[self-reference]]).
