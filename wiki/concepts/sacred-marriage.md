# Sacred Marriage (hieros gamos)

## Definition as Coined

[[frazer-james-george|Frazer]] treated the **sacred marriage** — ritual or mythical sexual union of god and goddess, or of human representatives (king and goddess/queen) — as a fertility rite promoting the growth of vegetation and the prosperity of the land (*[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|Golden Bough]]* ch. 11–12). At Nemi he hypothesized a marriage of the King of the Wood with Diana/Egeria. (Attributed.)

## Semantic History

*Hieros gamos* is a classical and history-of-religions term; Frazer's comparative expansion linked Near Eastern, classical, and "savage" fertility rites. Later scholarship historicizes specific cults (e.g. Mesopotamian) rather than treating one pan-human type.

## Operationalizations

Mythic divine couples; royal ritual cohabitation; seasonal May-bride customs as [[survivals]].

## Applications

Links [[divine-king]] to [[vegetation-spirit]] logic; tree-worship and Latin kingship chapters in *The Golden Bough*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Sexualized fertility theory over-applied; ethnographic reports often voyeuristic/colonial; local meanings of marriage rites not reducible to crop magic. See [[frazer-golden-bough-study]].
