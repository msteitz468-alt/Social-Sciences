# The Examination (Foucault)

## Definition as Coined

Third of the three "means of correct training" in *[[foucault-discipline-and-punish-1977|Discipline and Punish]]*. [[foucault-michel|Foucault]] (attributed): **"The examination combines the techniques of an observing hierarchy and those of a normalizing judgement. It is a normalizing gaze, a surveillance that makes it possible to qualify, to classify and to punish."** It establishes over individuals a visibility through which one differentiates and judges them. Highly ritualized: ceremony of power + form of the experiment; deployment of force + establishment of truth. "At the heart of the procedures of discipline, it manifests the subjection of those who are perceived as objects and the objectification of those who are subjected."

In the examination, [[power-knowledge]] becomes visible: superimposition of power relations and knowledge relations. Foucault asks who will write the history of the examination's rituals, methods, characters, marking systems — "in this slender technique are to be found a whole domain of knowledge, a whole type of power."

## Semantic History

- **1975:** technical fusion of observation and norm inside [[disciplinary-power]].
- Produces the **individual as describable, analysable object** and the **case** as documentary individuality — "descending individualization" (power becomes more individualizing as it becomes more anonymous and functional, inverting the ascending individualization of feudal/sovereign display).
- Related but distinct: [[confession]] in *History of Sexuality* Vol. I (truth-extraction through speech); the examination is more optical-documentary and multi-institutional (school, clinic, army, prison).
- Everyday "exam" (school test) is one historical form, not the whole concept.

## Operationalizations

| Feature | Effect (Foucault, attributed) |
|---|---|
| Visibility | Subject made object of knowledge |
| Documentation | Writing, registration, files, cumulative records |
| Case | Individual as object for a branch of knowledge and a branch of power |
| Ranking | Distribution around a norm |
| Ritual | Combines parade, test, and inquiry |

## Applications

Closes the "means of correct training" triad; essential to [[panopticism]]'s automatic power and to the prison's "file that was never closed." Study: [[foucault-discipline-and-punish-study]]. Links to human sciences as co-produced with disciplinary technology (four-rules thesis).

## Critiques and Limitations

- School/medical "examination" histories may contest Foucault's continuity claim across institutions.
- Not every assessment practice is disciplinary examination in his sense (peer review, democratic accountability differ in structure).
