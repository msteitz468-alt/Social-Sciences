# Combination instinct (Pareto Class I residues)

## Definition as Coined

Pareto’s **Class I residues** — the **instinct for combinations** — “intensely powerful in the human species and… one of the important factors in civilization” (§889). Taking the class as a whole: (1) propensity for combinations; (2) search for combinations deemed best; (3) propensity to believe they do what is expected (§889). Scientist and magician share the instinct; one combines under experimental control, the other by fantastic analogy (§889).

Livingston note: Italian *combinazione* is broader than English “combination” (deal, scheme, inspiration); progressive element vs [[group-persistence]] as conservative.

## Semantic History

Isolated inductively in purification and weather-magic nuclei (§§863–864) before full Class I taxonomy in Ch VI. In elite theory, Class I predominance marks cunning/speculator governing classes ([[lions-and-foxes]], [[circulation-of-elites]]).

## Operationalizations

Major genera (Ch VI):

| Code | Genera | Content (Pareto) |
|---|---|---|
| I-α | Generic combinations | Combinations without clear rational grounds (magic, lottery, Pliny remedies) |
| I-β | Similars or opposites | Association by likeness/contrast; magic *similia*; parody |
| I-β1–5 | Subtypes | Generic likeness; unusual/exceptional; awe/terror pairings; good-with-happy/bad-with-unhappy states; assimilation by consumption |
| I-γ | Mysterious workings | Occult powers of things/acts; oaths, ordeals, taboos |
| I-γ1–2 | Operations / names | Mysterious operations; name–thing linkings, sacred numbers |
| I-δ | Hunger for combining residues | Synthetic blends (true/good/beautiful/solidarity) |
| I-ε | Hunger for logical developments | Demand for logic *or* pseudo-logic → source of [[derivations]] |
| I-ζ | Faith in efficacy of combinations | Belief that combinations work |

Passive/active sides: subject to combinations vs produce/control them; Class II “cements” combination structures (§§890–891).

## Applications

- Civilization and discovery without pre-established design (alchemy→chemistry; domestication arguments, §§898–901).
- Progressive force in social equilibrium when balanced with Class II.
- Speculators / Class I leaders in [[circulation-of-elites]] and [[speculators-and-rentiers]].
- Modern halo of “Progress,” “Democracy,” universal suffrage as I-β4 + Class II personifications (§§932–936) — experimental absurdity ≠ settled social utility.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Very broad: can absorb almost any inventive or magical behaviour.
- “Instinct” language is 1916 psychology — Pareto also says residues are manifestations, not instincts themselves.
- Progressive/conservative mapping is ideal-typical, not a moral ranking in wiki voice.
