# Legitimate Domination (Three Pure Types)

## Definition as Coined

*Herrschaft* — translated by Gerth & Mills as **domination**, by Parsons as **authority** — is, for [[weber-max|Max Weber]], a relation in which a command is obeyed as if the ruled had made its content the maxim of their conduct. In *[[weber-politics-as-a-vocation-1919|Politics as a Vocation]]* (1919) Weber asks *"When and why do men obey?"* and answers with **three pure types of legitimate domination**, distinguished by the *inner justification* the ruled give for obeying (attributed voice throughout):

1. **Traditional** — the authority of the *"eternal yesterday,"* of mores "sanctified through the unimaginably ancient recognition and habitual orientation to conform"; exercised by the patriarch and the patrimonial prince.
2. **Charismatic** — the authority of "the extraordinary and personal gift of grace (*charisma*)," resting on personal devotion to a leader's revelation, heroism, or exemplary qualities; exercised by the prophet, elected war-lord, plebiscitarian ruler, or party leader (see [[charismatic-authority]]).
3. **Legal-rational** — authority "by virtue of *legality*," of belief in the validity of legal statute and rationally created rules and functional "competence"; exercised by the modern "servant of the state" and all bearers of power resembling him (see [[bureaucracy]]).

Weber stresses these are **pure types** — "rarely found in reality"; actual authority mixes them, and obedience is in practice also driven by "robust motives of fear and hope" and interests of every sort. Legitimacy is *belief in validity*, not moral correctness.

## Semantic History

The tripartite typology is Weber's, developed across his writings on *Herrschaft* c. 1913–1920 and given systematic form in *Economy and Society* (still queued for this wiki); the 1919 lecture is a compact public statement of it, focused on the charismatic type as "the root of the idea of a calling." The scheme became one of sociology's most durable analytical instruments. A key downstream development — the **routinization of charisma** (*Veralltäglichung*), by which charismatic authority, unstable because bound to one person, ossifies into traditional or legal-rational forms — is only implicit here (Weber notes the machine "deserts its own creator") and belongs to the fuller treatment.

## Operationalizations

- Read the **grounds of obedience** the ruled themselves offer (custom vs. personal faith vs. legality).
- Read the **administrative staff**: household retainers and vassals bound by tradition; personally devoted disciples/followers of a charismatic leader; salaried, examined, career officials under legal-rational rule.
- Read the **succession rule**: hereditary/customary; charismatic designation or acclamation; formal appointment by rule.

## Applications

The organizing frame for the comparative sociology of authority, political leadership, religious authority, and organizations. In this wiki it underlies [[charismatic-authority]], connects to [[bureaucracy]] as the paradigm of legal-rational administration, and grounds Weber's account of how the [[the-state|modern state]] concentrates and legitimates its [[monopoly-of-violence|monopoly of force]]. It contrasts with earlier classical accounts of power — [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]]'s psychologistic [[circulation-of-elites|circulation of elites]] and [[marx-karl|Marx]]'s class-based theory of the state — by locating legitimacy in *belief*.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Ideal-type status.** The pure types never appear pure; classifying real regimes forces judgment calls, and hybrid/transitional forms are the norm, not the exception.
- **Legitimacy as belief.** Critics (and later legitimation-crisis theorists such as Habermas) argue Weber's belief-centred definition brackets *why* beliefs in validity arise and whether they are manufactured — reducing legitimacy to the fact of obedience risks circularity.
- **Charisma's elasticity.** The charismatic type is the most powerful and the most abused (see [[charismatic-authority]]); its post-Weberian stretch to celebrities and brands drains the term.
- **Eurocentric exemplars.** Weber's illustrations lean on Occidental state formation; comparative applicability is debated.
