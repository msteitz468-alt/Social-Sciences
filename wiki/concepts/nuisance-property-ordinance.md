# Nuisance Property Ordinance

## Definition as Coined

Municipal **nuisance property** (chronic-nuisance) ordinances that cite landlords when a property generates too many police calls or listed nuisance activities within a time window, with fines, license penalties, or worse unless the landlord “abates” the nuisance. In *[[desmond-evicted-2016|Evicted]]* (Ch. “A Nuisance”), Desmond shows abatement often means **evicting tenants** — including after domestic-violence-related calls.

## Semantic History

Desmond situates ordinances in late-20th-century expansion of criminal justice and offloading of policing onto private actors (landlords, pawnshops). Sold as cost-saving manpower allocation; in practice a landlord–police–tenant triangle that can punish help-seeking.

## Operationalizations (Milwaukee, Desmond 2016)

- Threshold: often **≥3 calls/activities in 30 days** (Milwaukee Section 80-10 framing).
- 2008–09: Milwaukee PD issued a residential nuisance citation roughly **every 33 hours**.
- Top categories: “Trouble with Subjects,” noise; **domestic violence third** — DV incidents (mostly physical abuse or weapon) exceeded other assaults + disorderly conduct + drug crimes combined in the citation pattern Desmond reports.
- Abatement: Desmond reports roughly **8 in 10** property owners abated by eviction or threat of eviction if police contacted again (~83% eviction-response pattern in claims extraction).
- Racial geography: black neighborhoods cited at higher rates than white (e.g. 1 in 16 vs 1 in 41 eligible properties — Desmond 2016).

## Applications

Explains why tenants (especially DV survivors) may avoid calling 911; links police response to housing loss; civil-rights and due-process critiques (Fourth Amendment / Fair Housing arguments cited in Notes).

## Critiques and Limitations

Legal scholars and advocates argue ordinances violate due process and fair-housing protections; city “fixes” (e.g. carving out DV from lists) may fail when DV hides under other codes (battery, property damage) and when landlords still have eviction as cheapest abatement (Desmond’s analysis, attributed).
