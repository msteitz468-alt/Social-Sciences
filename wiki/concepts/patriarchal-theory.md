# Patriarchal Theory (Maine)

*(Attributed — [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]], *[[maine-ancient-law-1861|Ancient Law]]* Ch. V.)* Comparative jurisprudence, Maine claims, establishes the **Patriarchal Theory**: the primeval condition of the human race was patriarchal household organization. Legal testimony comes chiefly from Indo-European stocks (Romans, Hindus, Slavs).

## Definition as Coined

- Eldest male ascendant absolutely supreme over household (wife, sons, daughters, slaves); dominion extends to life and death in the archaic picture.
- **Unit of ancient society = the Family; of modern society = the Individual.**
- Ascending series: Family → Gens/House → Tribe → Commonwealth, conceived as common descent (often partly fictitious).
- Key Roman correlates: **agnation** (kinship traced exclusively through males; *Mulier est finis familiae*); **adoption** (preserve male line / potestas); **perpetual tutelage of women**; *patria potestas* over person and property; family as **corporation** with patriarch as representative.
- Civil law appears sparse because persons, property, and contract are absorbed in household subjection — explaining thin early contract law and the priority of tort/composition over true criminal law (Ch. X).

## Semantic History

Maine acknowledges Scriptural patriarchs and Locke–Filmer debates but grounds the theory in legal antiquities. Victorian rivals (M’Lennan; [[morgan-lewis-henry|L.H. Morgan]]; [[bachofen-johann-jakob|Bachofen]] mother-right) attacked patriarchal priority — noted already in the Everyman intro. Filed on [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]. Distinguish Maine’s Roman *gens* as **agnatic patriarchal house** from Morgan–Engels **gens** as matrilineal-type clan (see [[gens]]).

## Operationalizations

Survivals and early codes: agnation, adoption, potestas traces in Justinian, joint family property, restricted testation, Indian village community as organized patriarchal co-proprietorship (Ch. VIII).

## Applications

*Ancient Law* program; later village-community literature; foil for mother-right and Morgan–Engels sequences.

## Critiques and Limitations

Universal patriarchal origin contested; sampling heavily Roman/Indo-European; progressive/stationary binary; undervalues mother-right evidence claimed by rivals. Do not assert patriarchal priority in wiki voice.

## From Kuper, *Invention of Primitive Society* (1988)

*(Attributed — Kuper 1988, Ch. 2.)* Kuper reconstructs patriarchal theory as Maine’s foil for modern contractual society and as the first full constitution of anthropological [[primitive-society]]. Challenge sequence: Bachofen *Mutterrecht* (1861, little immediate impact); McLennan *Primitive Marriage* (1865) in direct reaction to Maine (exogamy, polyandry, inverted sequence, [[survivals]]); Morgan’s matriarchal version came to dominate the next generation. Maine’s late defence and monogenist commitments did not reverse the matriarchal turn. See [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
