# Actant

In Actor-Network Theory, an **actant** is whatever acts or is granted action in a network — human or nonhuman — as developed in Latour’s *[[latour-reassembling-the-social-2005|Reassembling the Social]]* (esp. Third Source: objects too have agency).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Latour / ANT.)* Borrowed from Greimasian narrative semiotics and generalized: agency is not reserved to intentional human subjects. Objects, instruments, texts, and microbes can be actants when they make a difference in a course of action. “Actor” and “actant” are often used interchangeably in ANT popularization; technical use stresses role in network over psychology.

## Semantic History

Semiotics → laboratory STS → general social theory in *Reassembling*. Adjacent to Descola’s more-than-human [[collectives-including-nonhumans]] but from a network/association apparatus, not four-ontology morphology.

## Operationalizations

Ask what makes a difference in a chain of action; follow mediators (transform) vs intermediaries (transport without change) — terminology densified from the book’s claims.

## Applications

Third Source of Uncertainty; object agency debates in sociology and anthropology.

## Critiques and Limitations

Anthropomorphism charges; political agency of things disputed; risk of flattening power differentials.
