# Primitive Accumulation

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Part 8, [[marx-karl|Marx]] defines **primitive** (or original) **accumulation** as the historical process that constitutes the pre-history of capital — not thrift of the industrious, but the **separation of the producer from the means of production**, creating on one side free wage-labourers and on the other owners of money and means of production.

> The secret: “the so-called primitive accumulation… is nothing else than the historical process of divorcing the producer from the means of production.”

**Attributed to Marx.** England is the “classic form.” Closing image of capital: “dripping from head to foot, from every pore, with blood and dirt.”

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Classical PE “previous accumulation” | Virtuous thrift → capital |
| Marx Part 8 | Expropriation, state violence, colonies |
| Later (Luxemburg, Harvey, etc.) | Ongoing accumulation by dispossession — extensions beyond Vol. I |

## Operationalizations / Historical mechanisms (Ch. 26–33)

As Marx reconstructs them (attributed historical synthesis):

1. **Expropriation of the agricultural population** — enclosures, clearances, dissolution of bands of feudal retainers, usurpation of church and common lands.
2. **Bloody legislation** against the expropriated (whipping, branding, enslavement, death) and statutes forcing down wages.
3. **Genesis of the capitalist farmer** and the home market for industrial capital.
4. **Genesis of the industrial capitalist** — usury and merchant capital; **colonial system**; public debt and modern taxation; protectionism; commercial wars; slave trade / plantation slavery as pedestal of European wage labour.
5. **Colonies (Wakefield)** — systematic colonisation: artificial land price to keep immigrants as wage-labourers; capital as a social relation requiring landless free labour.

## Applications

- Explains historical conditions for [[labor-power]] as commodity (double freedom).
- Completes *Capital* I’s arc: commodity analysis → capital → accumulation → historical origin.
- **Historical tendency** (Ch. 32): centralisation of capital and socialization of labour → “expropriators are expropriated” — **position**, not wiki-voice prediction.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Historians debate scale/timing of enclosures and the role of internal vs colonial accumulation.
- “Primitive” can misleadingly imply once-and-done; later literature treats dispossession as recurrent.
- Violence and colonial content are first-class historical claims in Marx’s text — attributed, full fidelity.
