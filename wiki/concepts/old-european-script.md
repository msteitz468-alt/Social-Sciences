# Old European Script (Gimbutas / Vinča signs)

## Definition as Coined

**Old European script** (also “Vinča script,” “sacred script”) is [[gimbutas-marija|Marija Gimbutas]]’s name for groups of linear and geometric signs on Neolithic and Copper Age objects of southeastern and central Europe, which she treats in *[[gimbutas-civilization-of-the-goddess-1991|The Civilization of the Goddess]]* Ch.8 as a **sacred** writing system used ~5300–4300 BCE (with roots in the early 6th millennium), developed for communication with deities rather than administration (attributed). She rejects equating the phenomenon solely with Vinča: nearly 100 sites, with main concentrations in Vinča–Tisza and Karanovo zones, plus Dimini, Cucuteni, Petrești, Lengyel, Butmir, Bükk, and Linear Pottery ceramics (her distribution; attributed).

## Semantic History

- **1874:** first inscribed finds at Turdaș (Tordos), early Vinča, Transylvania (as Gimbutas recounts).
- **1961:** Tărtăria plaques (early Vinča pit near Cluj) sparked “Sumerian imitation” debates; Gimbutas rejects Hood et al.’s uncomprehending-imitation reading.
- **Winn 1973/1981:** *Pre-writing in Southeastern Europe* — 210 recognizable Vinča signs from 29 sites, five core graphic elements (Gimbutas’s main catalogue base).
- **Gimbutas 1991:** upgrades “pre-writing” to full **sacred script** of its own kind in the climactic phase (~5300–4000 BCE); links disappearance to Kurgan-related disintegration of Karanovo/Vinča ~4300–4000 BCE; posits Aegean/Linear A/Cypriot continuities (Harold Haarmann ≥50 OE–Linear A parallels cited).
- **Mainstream epigraphy:** most specialists treat Vinča signs as symbols, ownership marks, or proto-writing — not fully accepted as a decipherable script; status remains **contested**.

## Operationalizations

| Element | Gimbutas 1991 claim (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Chronology | First half 6th mill. B.C. appearance; continuous ~two millennia; earlier than Sumerian by ~2,000 years (her C14 calibration) |
| Core inventory | ~30 core signs; >100 modified combinations |
| Carriers | Figurines, anthropomorphic vases, spindle whorls, loom weights, temple models, seals, libation vessels |
| Function | Dedicatory / Goddess epithets / votive formulas — **not** economic-legal administration |
| Development | Early phase ~6000–5300; climactic ~5300–4000 coalescing into “morphemographic (logo-syllabic)” system |
| End | “Virtually disappeared” with OE culture disintegration c. 4300–4000; survival claimed in Aegean |

## Applications

- Load-bearing prop for Old Europe as “civilization” (Preface + Ch.8): script + metallurgy + temples + towns.
- Contrast with Sumerian commercial-administrative writing and Mycenaean Linear B.
- Continuity theses to Cretan Hieroglyphic / Linear A / Cypriot (highly speculative).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Script vs symbol:** clustering of signs does not by itself prove language encoding; no bilingual text.
- **Chronology of “earliest writing”:** depends on accepting sign groups as writing and on 1991 14C frames.
- **Sumer comparison:** purpose-difference (sacred vs administrative) is interpretive; chronological priority claims are disputed.
- **Disappearance = IE invasion:** equates culture collapse with speech change — four-way non-identity violation if asserted as fact; file as [[kurgan-hypothesis]] / [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]] thesis.
- **Linear A parallels:** formal similarity ≠ genetic descent of scripts or languages.
