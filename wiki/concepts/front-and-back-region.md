# Front Region and Back Region

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]*, 1956) defined a **region** as any place bounded to some degree by barriers to perception (varying by medium — visual vs aural isolation). Given a performance as reference:

| Term | Definition (Goffman, attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Front region** | Place where the performance is given; fixed sign-equipment there is the setting part of [[performance-front]] |
| **Back region / backstage** | Place where the impression fostered by the performance is knowingly contradicted as a matter of course — preparation, rehearsal, relaxation, fabrication, dropping of front |
| **Outside** | Neither front nor back relative to a given performance; **outsiders** neither perform nor observe that show |

**Front-region standards** (attributed): (1) **politeness** — how the performer treats the audience in talk or gestural substitutes; (2) **decorum** — comportment while in visual/aural range but not necessarily talking (moral and instrumental requirements). Decorum is more pervasive ecologically than politeness.

**Back region functions** (attributed): assemble personal front; rehearse; correct errors; relax; hold **[[communication-out-of-character|staging talk]]** and treatment of the absent; keep secrets. Very commonly front and back are **adjacent** (kitchen/dining room; shop floor/storeroom); passages are controlled so the audience does not see backstage and outsiders do not walk into the wrong show.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Goffman 1956 | Spatial-perceptual regions relative to a performance |
| Everyday academic English | "Backstage" = informal/private; often loses team and barrier precision |
| Organizational ethnography | Kitchen vs dining room; green room; staff lounge |
| Digital | "Frontstage Instagram / backstage DMs" — analogy requiring re-argument of barriers to perception |

## Operationalizations

Floor plans and access control; observation of who may enter which spaces when; recording of backstage talk (with ethics constraints). Regions are **relative** to a performance — a kitchen is front for kitchen staff's show among themselves and back for the dining-room show.

## Applications

Shetland hotel kitchen/doors (Goffman's field illustrations); restaurants (Orwell waiter among sources Goffman cites); hospitals, schools, barracks, shops, domestic interiors. Links: [[audience-segregation]], [[performance-team]], [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Not a claim that backstage is the "true self" — Goffman treated official and out-of-character currents as dual involvements to be managed, not as real vs fake ontology.
- Open-plan and surveillance-heavy workplaces compress or eliminate classical back regions (Goffman already noted cases where "guards are always up").
