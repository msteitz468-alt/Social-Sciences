# Theoretical Practice

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[althusser-louis|Althusser]], “On the Materialist Dialectic,” *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*.)*

**Theoretical practice** is Althusser’s name for knowledge-production as a **practice** among practices — not contemplation of a given object by a given subject (the empiricist model he rejects), but a process of transformation that produces knowledge.

**General definition of practice (Althusser):** any process of transformation of a determinate given raw material into a determinate product, a transformation effected by a determinate human labour using determinate means of production.

He distinguishes **economic, political, ideological, and theoretical** practices as members of the social whole. Theoretical practice “falls within the general definition of practice” and includes both scientific theoretical practice and pre-scientific (ideological) theoretical practice (religion, art, ethical ideology, their “philosophies”).

### Generalities I, II, III

| Generality | Role in theoretical practice |
|---|---|
| **I** | Abstract, part-ideological / part-scientific generalities that are the **raw material** of the science |
| **II** | The **theory** of the science at a given moment — means of production of knowledge |
| **III** | Concrete **scientific** generalities that are the **product** (knowledge) |

**Concrete-in-thought vs real-concrete (attributed):** For mature Marx (as Althusser reads the 1857 Introduction), the theoretical abstract and concrete both exist in thought (I and III); the real-concrete “survives independently outside thought before and after.” Empiricism confuses knowledge of the object with a part of the object itself.

### Theory / “theory” / Theory

Althusser’s nomenclature in the essay:

- **theory** (scientific theoretical practice of a science)
- **“theory”** (the determinate theoretical system of a science at a moment — e.g. the “theory” of historical materialism)
- **Theory** (capital T): general theory — the Theory of practice in general = **dialectical materialism** / Marxist philosophy constituted in rupture with ideological philosophies

## Operationalizations

Epistemological schema, not a lab method: specify raw materials (concepts/ideologies being worked), theoretical means (problematics, protocols), and products (new concepts). The [[epistemological-break]] is the establishment of a scientific theoretical practice in a domain previously governed by ideological theoretical practice.

## Applications

- Reading Marx’s formation as transformation of problematics, not accumulation of “elements.”
- Distinguishing science of history ([[historical-materialism]]) from humanist ideology that may share words (“man,” “alienation”).
- Justifying specialized theoretical work inside a political movement (theory essential to practice — Althusser’s Party-conjuncture stake).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Theoreticism:** severs theory from class struggle and empirical constraint (Althusser’s later self-critique; English readers preface already notes silence on theory–practice fusion).
- **Idealism charge:** knowledge produced wholly in thought risks losing contact with the real-concrete (Althusser insists the real remains independent; critics deny the dualism works).
- **Empiricist counter:** science progresses by confrontation with data, not only transformation of Generalities.
- Do not equate with Bourdieu’s “theory of practice” / [[practice-theory]] — different programs.
