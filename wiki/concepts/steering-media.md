# Steering Media (Money and Power)

## Definition as Coined

**Steering media** are, in [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]'s [[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v2-1987|TCA Vol. 2]] (attributed), **delinguistified** mechanisms that coordinate action without recourse to communicative agreement. Money and power "attach to empirical ties; they encode a purposive-rational attitude toward calculable amounts of value and make it possible to exert generalized, strategic influence on the decisions of other participants while bypassing processes of consensus-oriented communication." Crucially, they do not merely simplify language but **replace** it: "the lifeworld is no longer needed for the coordination of action."

## Semantic History

The concept is developed through an immanent critique of [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]], who generalized "medium" from money to power, influence, and value-commitment. Habermas accepts money and power as genuine steering media but charges Parsons with **overgeneralization**: as the chain is extended, "the structural analogies to the money medium become increasingly unclear... in the end, merely metaphorical." His criterion: a genuine medium must be measurable, alienable, storable, and institutionally anchored (money in property/contract; power in office/law) so as to relieve actors of communicative justification. Money needs no legitimation; power requires organization **and** legitimation — an "asymmetry" Habermas says Parsons misses, since obedience requires "obligation," not mere "compliance." **Influence** and **value-commitment** fail these tests: they cannot be measured, alienated, or stored, and remain "second-order processes of consensus formation in language" — so they "cannot technicize the lifeworld."

## Operationalizations — media dualism

Habermas distinguishes two kinds of relief mechanism that emerge as value generalization increases the communicative burden:
- media that **replace** mutual understanding (money, power) — the true steering media; versus
- media/forms that **condense** it (reputation, moral–value commitment; and the mass media of communication, enabled by writing, print, and electronics), which remain tied to linguistic consensus.

Money becomes structure-forming only when it is an "intersystemic medium of interchange" (wage labor, taxation), which constitutes the capitalist economy as a monetarily steered subsystem; with the state's monopoly of legitimate force, organizational power is likewise assimilated "to the structure of a steering medium."

## Applications

Steering media are the engine of the "uncoupling" of [[system-and-lifeworld|system from lifeworld]]; their penetration into communicatively structured domains is the mechanism of the [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld]]. The money/power pairing also grounds Habermas's four system–lifeworld interchange roles: employee and consumer (economy), client and citizen (state).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Are money and power symmetrical?** (attributed): critics question treating administrative power as a medium "like" money, given its dependence on legitimation.
- **Norm-free systems?** (attributed): the claim that media-steered subsystems operate free of the lifeworld is charged with depoliticizing the economy and state (see [[system-and-lifeworld]]).
