# Culture Pattern

[[kroeber-alfred-l|Kroeber]]'s multi-level concept of **pattern** in culture (*[[kroeber-culture-patterns-processes-1963|Culture Patterns & Processes]]*, Ch. 8): arrangements that structure cultural content from near-universal schemes through systemic and whole-culture patterns to style patterns and the clustering of genius and invention.

## Definition as Coined

Kroeber (attributed) distinguished several kinds of pattern:

1. **Universal pattern** (or culture scheme) — near-universal topical inventory of what cultures cover (Wissler's scheme treated largely as a table-of-contents convenience, not a deep causal structure).
2. **Systemic patterns** — structured plans that can diffuse as wholes or homologues (examples he discussed: alphabet, plow agriculture, exclusive monotheism, kula-type exchange systems). Homology vs. analogy matters for historical inference.
3. **Total-culture patterns** — physiognomy or overall "configuration" of a whole culture; language (grammar vs. lexicon) as model for systemic vs. total organization.
4. **Style patterns** — channeled definiteness in arts, dress, manners; basic pattern under fashion variability; style exhaustion (e.g. his discussion of Greek geometry as a closed style run).

Related claims (attributed): whole-culture patterns show **climaxes** or spurts; **genius clusters** where style-patterns reach fruitfulness — not explained by race/heredity (contra Galton); a large share of congenital ability is "wasted" where culture does not provide a channel. **Simultaneous inventions** (§140) reinforce superindividual determination once culture patterns are ripe.

## Semantic History

Boasian "pattern" vocabulary predates and runs alongside Benedict's [[cultural-configuration|configuration]]. Kroeber's fourfold structural-historical scheme is broader than Benedict's single-label psychological integration ([[configurationism]]); in the Patterns chapter he accepted multi-strand characterizations (including Benedict's Apollonian/Dionysian, Dobu, Kwakiutl sketches as illustrations) while resisting restriction of "integrated" total patterns to rare cases and critiquing "basic personality structure" overreach. Later archaeology's "horizon styles," design-structure analysis, and "cultural logics" partly inherit the systemic/style levels.

## Operationalizations

- Trait-complex and element distribution as entry to systemic patterns.
- Stylistic series and climax dating in art and science history (*Configurations of Culture Growth*, 1944 — not yet primary-ingested).
- Comparison of whole-culture "physiognomy" with ethos/eidos ([[ethos-and-eidos]]).

## Applications

- Organizing culture history without unilinear stages.
- Linking invention clustering and simultaneous discovery to pattern fruitfulness ([[stimulus-diffusion]], Processes chapter).
- Dialogue with [[configurationism]] and culture-and-personality.

## Critiques and Limitations

Universal pattern schemes risk **laundry-list** emptiness. Total-culture physiognomy shares stereotype risks with ethos labels. Style-and-genius arguments can reintroduce **great-man** talk even while denying racial genius theories. Postwar anthropology moved toward meaning, practice, and power more than pattern morphology.
