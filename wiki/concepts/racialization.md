# Racialization

Process of extending racial meaning to a previously racially unclassified relationship, social practice, or group — a key mechanism within [[racial-formation|racial formation]].

## Definition as Coined

Omi and Winant deploy racialization inside the racial formation framework: race is made through historical projects that attach racial meaning to bodies, groups, and institutions.

## Semantic History

Used more broadly in British and U.S. race scholarship; in Omi–Winant it is tied specifically to formation via projects rather than only to prejudice or ideology.

## Operationalizations

Historical and discursive analysis of when/how groups or practices become “raced.”

## Applications

Immigrant groups “becoming racial”; policy categories; scientific racism’s classificatory schemes.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can blur into generic “social construction” without specifying agents and projects.
