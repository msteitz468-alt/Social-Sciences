# Patriarchal Alchemy

## Definition as Coined

Claudia von Werlhof (in *[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|MWS in the Longue Durée]]*, 2004) proposes **alchemy** as a longue-durée, interdisciplinary concept for analyzing the modern world-system: **patriarchal alchemy** seeks to create/replace life through transformation against nature (technology + theology of male creation), contrasting with older matriarchal/cooperative alchemy that cooperates with natural fertility. She treats capitalism as the latest stage of patriarchy and unpaid housework as permanent underground fundament of accumulation.

## Semantic History

Repurposes historical alchemy (Eliade, Jung, etc.) as social-theoretical key; unique (per von Werlhof) matriarchal/patriarchal alchemy distinction. Internal feminist critique of productivist world-systems political economy.

## Operationalizations

Procedures (*mortificatio*, *solve et coagula*, etc.) read as templates for modern science/technology and the "social machine"; housewifeization as modern phase; sexism argued as older/deeper than racism in this framework (attributed — contested).

## Applications

Gendering world-systems analysis; critique of technology-as-progress; linking violence against women/nature to accumulation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Highly metaphorical; risk of monocausal patriarchy narrative; empirical operationalization sparse in the 2004 chapter; position within feminist theory is program-building, not consensus finding. For a more structurally specified account of men's collective material advantage, compare [[connell-raewyn|Connell]]'s [[patriarchal-dividend]] within the [[gender-relations-theory|theory of gender relations]].
