# Law of Evolutionary Potential

Primary formulation: [[service-elman-r|Elman R. Service]], Ch. V of *[[sahlins-service-evolution-culture-1960|Evolution and Culture]]* (Sahlins & Service, eds., 1960), pp. 93–122. Later redeployed in *[[service-origins-state-1975|Origins of the State and Civilization]]* (1975). Co-editorial frame is joint Sahlins–Service; chapter authorship is Service’s (Note to the 1960 volume).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Service 1960.)* Dual formulation:

1. **“The more specialized and adapted a form in a given evolutionary stage, the smaller is its potential for passing to the next stage.”**
2. **“Specific evolutionary progress is inversely related to general evolutionary potential.”**

Service grounded the idea in the **Principle of Stabilization**: specific adaptation is ultimately self-limiting and nonprogressive; if all forms were fully adapted, evolution would halt. Total-system evolution continues because *less specialized* (generalized, hinterland, “backward”) forms retain potential for new adaptations or new environments. Hence the “contradictory-sounding propositions”: evolution of particular species/cultures occurs *because of* adaptation; evolution of the total system of life/culture occurs *in spite of* adaptation.

**Corollaries (Service 1960):**
- **Phylogenetic Discontinuity of Progress** — an advanced form does not normally beget the next stage; the next stage begins in a different line.
- **Local Discontinuity of Progress** — successive stages of progress are not likely to occur in the same locality (especially apt for culture, because cultures are often named for territories).

**Borrowed phrases (attributed use):**
- Thorstein Veblen (*Imperial Germany*, 1915): “**merits of borrowing**” and “**penalty of taking the lead**”
- Leon Trotsky (*History of the Russian Revolution*): “**privilege of historic backwardness**” and the **Law of Combined Development** (latecomers insert into advanced development, skipping intermediate stages)

Service held the pattern is expectable in culture as in biology (citing Huxley on specialization “grooves” and Simpson on evolutionary “opportunism”) but insisted it was **not a mere biological carryover** — cultural students had anticipated it (Morgan on stage-skipping by borrowing; Trotsky most explicit on the potential of backwardness). Diffusion and combined development differentiate cultural from biological phylogeny; the inverse law still applies.

## Semantic History

**1960 volume context.** Service opened by arguing mid-century neo-evolutionism was one-sidedly adaptationist (specific only) and that anthropologists fairly demand usable applications. LEP is the volume’s synthesis chapter: interaction of specific adaptation, dominance ([[law-of-cultural-dominance]]), and general progress ([[general-and-specific-evolution]]).

**Epistemology of “law.”** Service admitted illustrations cannot *prove* the law “true”; a law relates classes of phenomena “other things constant,” but nature has no constants. Criterion = **explanatory utility** (Cohen: “the repeatable escapes us if it is not identified”), not laboratory absolute truth.

**1975 redeployment (*Origins of the State*).** Service put LEP to a specific civilizational use: explaining why “**the first classic civilizations… did not fall; they were pushed**” — competitive displacement by newer, less-committed frontier or “marcher” societies (Old Kingdom Egypt, Indus, Teotihuacán, Andean and Mesopotamian centers; dynastic turnover as Sargon, Chou/Ch’in, Aztecs). Links to [[integrative-theory-of-the-state]] (a successful integrative bureaucracy is also a rigid one) and [[archaic-state-formation-pathways]].

**Carneiro 2003 (attributed):** restates the dual formulation and notes Spencerian anticipations (leapfrogging / non-vanguard advance) in the classical corpus.

## Operationalizations

The “law” resists quantitative measurement: tendency, “other things constant,” with adaptive *specialization/commitment* the operative but unquantified variable. Service offered:

- **Dominance vs potentiality** as endemic contest (much “struggle and warfare” readable as contest between these factors)
- Homogenization consequence: specific evolution often moves heterogeneity→homogeneity as a higher dominant form spreads ([[law-of-cultural-dominance]])
- Faster evolution → more discontinuous advance (generational leapfrog in science; “cult of youth” in rapidly changing societies)

## Applications

*(Attributed — Service 1960 illustrations.)*

| Domain | Illustration (Service’s reading) |
|---|---|
| Writing | Specialized Egyptian hieroglyphic/rebus system; alphabetic leap among East Mediterranean peoples without writing of their own |
| Science | Generational discontinuity; young scientists have “privilege of backwardness” |
| Age status | Stabilized cultures subordinate youth to age; rapid change elevates youth (U.S.; USSR/China “cult of youth”) |
| Mesoamerica | Ancestral stages need not underlie High Culture in the same region; Central Mexican “barbarian” invasions (Toltec, Chichimec, Aztec) |
| Agriculture | Forest origin of New World agriculture as likelihood suggestion, not proof |
| Paleolithic–Neolithic | Childe on overspecialized Magdalenians vs humbler southern groups |
| Classical Mediterranean | “Rise and fall” as local discontinuity, not cyclical mystery |
| Energy regimes | Japan advanced over China on coal/oil; China may leap to next energy stage (Mao’s “blank paper” metaphor cited) |
| Late industrializers | Can adopt best known standards (alphabet, metric, keyboards) without pioneering sunk costs |

**Present and future of America (1960, attributed speculation — not wiki-voice prediction):** Service argued advanced coal/oil nations have *less* potential for the next energy regime than underdeveloped regions; sketched a “Greek” decline model for the U.S. without extermination; analyzed three “reversals” of late industrialization (forced capital → socialist police state; industrialization under external opposition; **inverse cultural lag** — ideology/politics ahead of technology). Policy recommendation: aid full-world industrialization rather than containment; realistic caveat that the U.S. was unlikely to do so soon. Closes with Tylor 1881 on leaving the world better, and Service’s hope it “will still be intact.”

**1975 civilizational applications:** fall of archaic civilizations as push by less-committed rivals (see Semantic History).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Service’s own strongest caveats (1960 and 1975 “Caveat Lector”): LEP is **not** *the* cause of any rise or fall — only one generalizable factor among many specific historical causes (disease, drought, war), “other things constant.”
- Near-untestability; risk of *post hoc* labeling of winners as “backward/generalized” and losers as “overspecialized.”
- Progress/potential vocabulary inherits ethnocentric ranking risks even when framed thermodynamically.
- Lineal/Hegelian–Marxist self-contained-unit evolution is Service’s polemical foil (Bolshevik Russia as anomaly for “revolution in the most advanced countries”); recorded as his position, not wiki-voice political analysis.
- Later archaeology and post-processual critique of stage and leapfrog master-narratives.
