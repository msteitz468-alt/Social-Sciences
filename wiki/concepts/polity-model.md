# Polity Model

## Definition as Coined

Tilly's **polity model** (*FMR* 1978) is a static map of contention: a **government** exercising coercion within a population; **members** of the polity (high return on expended resources); **challengers** (low return); optional **coalitions**. All contenders try to realize interests by applying pooled resources; challengers seek entry into the polity, members seek to remain. Power = increasing rate of return on expended resources.

## Semantic History

Interest-group politics formalized for historical and cross-national use; not identical to Easton's systems "polity" or Dahl's polyarchy alone.

## Operationalizations

Identify government(s) then population under jurisdiction, or start from a population and map relevant governments. Membership changes detected via entries/exits and violence clustering around them (Tilly's hypotheses).

## Applications

Linking mobilization model (single contender) to multi-actor contention; analyzing democratization as expansion of polity membership.

## Critiques and Limitations

Skocpol: state reduced to one "government" node among contenders, underplaying administrative autonomy. Tilly notes the model is static and interest-light until elaborated.
