# Financescape

One of [[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]'s five [[global-cultural-flows|-scapes]].
Attributed to Appadurai.

## Definition as Coined

"It is useful to speak as well of financescapes, as the disposition of global
capital is now a more mysterious, rapid, and difficult landscape to follow than
ever before, as currency markets, national stock exchanges, and commodity
speculations move megamonies through national turnstiles at blinding speed, with
vast, absolute implications for small differences in percentage points and time
units" *(Appadurai 1996)*.

## Semantic History

The scape closest to the concerns of political economy and
[[world-systems-analysis]], but Appadurai's point is precisely that capital flow
does **not** synchronize with the movement of people, technology, images, or
ideas: "even an elementary model of global political economy must take into
account the deeply disjunctive relationships among human movement, technological
flow, and financial transfers."

## Operationalizations

Illustrated by the difficulty of comparing wages or real-estate costs across Tokyo
and New York without accounting for "the very complex fiscal and investment flows
that link the two economies through a global grid of currency speculation and
capital transfer."

## Applications

Anchors the disjuncture thesis: states are strained "between ideoscapes and
financescapes" (Appadurai's examples: Mexico, Brazil, where international lending
shapes national politics). Connects to [[deterritorialization]] of wealth.

## Critiques and Limitations

Political-economy critics argue that treating finance as one "landscape" among five
co-equal flows understates capital's structuring priority over the others (cf.
[[world-systems-analysis]]; [[globalization]]).
