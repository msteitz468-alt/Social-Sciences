# Structural Time

> Coined in [[evans-pritchard-nuer-study|Evans-Pritchard, *The Nuer* (1940)]], Ch. III. Bundles two coordinate ideas: **oecological time** and **structural time**, both resting on **structural distance**.

## Definition as Coined

Evans-Pritchard (attributed) distinguished two Nuer modes of reckoning duration. **Oecological time** is cyclical, tied to the seasonal and activity round — the year as "a period of village residence and a period of camp residence," the day told by "the cattle clock, the round of pastoral tasks." **Structural time** reckons distance not in units but in *relations between social groups*: "distance between events ceases to be reckoned in time concepts as we understand them and is reckoned in terms of structural distance." Its components are points of reference significant to local groups (year-names of floods, wars, famines), age-set distances, and lineage/kinship distances.

The Nuer, he argued, "have no expression equivalent to 'time'... they cannot... speak of time as though it were something actual, which passes, can be wasted, can be saved" — and so "never experience the same feeling of fighting against time" ("Nuer are fortunate"). Crucially, structural time is an **illusion of movement**: "the structure remains fairly constant and the perception of time is no more than the movement of persons... through the structure." Because genealogies are telescoped ([[segmentary-lineage-system]]), lineage depth stays constant — "the distance between the beginning of the world and the present day remains unalterable... Time is thus not a continuum, but is a constant structural relationship between two points, the first and last persons in a line of agnatic descent." His most vivid datum: "the tree under which mankind came into being was still standing in Western Nuerland a few years ago."

## Semantic History

The paired concept underpins later anthropology and sociology of time and stands behind constructs like the [[timeless-time|"timeless time"]] of network-society theory (a distinct, later coinage). "Structural time" is now a standard reference point in debates over whether "cold"/genealogical societies experience history differently, and a caution against projecting Western linear-continuous time onto other reckoning systems.

## Operationalizations

- **The cattle clock.** The day is told by pastoral tasks (byre-to-kraal, milking, herding out, return, evening milking, enclosure), not clock units.
- **Age-set and lineage distance.** Events are dated "six sets ago" or by generation-steps (*kath*), not by year-count; valid historical reckoning reaches "probably" no more than ~50 years, tradition ~10–12 generations.
- **Structural distance (the load-bearing device).** "Lineage time is... the structural distance between groups of persons... Structural time cannot be understood until structural distance is known." Structural distance is "the distance between groups of persons in a social system, expressed in terms of values," and can **invert physical distance**: "A Nuer tribe which is separated by forty miles from another Nuer tribe is structurally nearer to it than to a Dinka tribe from which it is separated by only twenty miles." The relativity of the home-term *cieng* (village/section/tribe depending on who asks) is its everyday expression.

## Applications

Structural time/distance is the temporal-spatial face of the [[segmentary-lineage-system|segmentary system]]: fission/fusion in political space and the constancy of genealogical depth in time are the same structure viewed two ways. It frames Evans-Pritchard's treatment of Nuer myth (not stratified — "one mythological event did not precede another") and of the shallow, structurally-anchored Nuer past.

## Critiques and Limitations

Evans-Pritchard himself flagged the boundary of his evidence on subjective time-experience ("our observations on the matter have been slight"). Later critics question whether the sharp "no abstract time" claim overstates the contrast with Western reckoning and reads structure where practice is messier — the same idealization charge levelled at the segmentary model ([[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]]). Stated here in attributed voice.

## From Leach, *Rethinking Anthropology* Ch. 6 (1953/61)

*(Attributed — Leach; Lévi-Strauss influence acknowledged.)* Two short *Explorations* essays: “Cronus and Chronos”; “Time and False Noses.”

- **Dual time.** Repetition/oscillation vs irreversible succession — not one continuum.
- **Cronus.** Myth as structural creation of **contraries** (sky/earth, male/female principles, Zeus vs polar opposites) and of the **beginning of becoming**; time as zig-zag of opposed yet allied categories (linked by Leach to R-B alternating generations and LS marriage-as-alliance).
- **Festivals create time.** Without festivals, named periods would not exist; societies create intervals in social life then measure them. Formality / masquerade / role reversal map onto Durkheimian sacred/profane phase shifts; role reversal ≈ sacred time played in reverse.

Not the same construct as Evans-Pritchard’s structural time (segmentary distance), but a related mid-century structural analysis of temporal symbolism. Source: [[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961]].
