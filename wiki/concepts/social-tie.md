# Social Tie

> One of three title objects of [[tilly-charles|Tilly]]’s *[[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005|Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties]]* (2005). Relational building block: **transactions** accumulate into **ties**; ties plus boundaries and identities organize durable inequality and politics.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Tilly 2005.)* Social processes start from **interpersonal transactions**. From recurring transactions, analysts (and participants) infer **social ties** — durable characteristics of connections among persons, groups, or social sites. Ties are not essences of individuals; they are properties of **relations**.

In the book’s architecture (Fig. 1-1 sense): **boundaries**, **ties**, and **identities** interlock — identities represent us–them and us–us relations; boundaries interrupt social fields; ties carry obligations, trust, exploitation, and opportunity.

## Semantic History

- Network sociology / relational sociology (White, Granovetter, etc.) — ties as edges.  
- Tilly’s durable-inequality program — categorical ties and organizational connections install [[exploitation]] and [[opportunity-hoarding]].  
- Contention program — catness × netness (*FMR*); brokerage as a relational mechanism.  
- 2005 collection — title-level synthesis: transactions → identities, boundaries, durable ties.

## Operationalizations

| Observation | Inference |
|---|---|
| Repeated exchanges, mutual aid, remittances | Within-boundary ties |
| Employment, clientelism, denigration across categories | Cross-boundary ties |
| Long-term risky cooperation (credit, migration chains) | [[trust]] networks as special tie clusters |
| Claim-making coalitions | Political ties among identities |

## Applications

- Chain-migration and niche formation ([[chain-migration]], [[opportunity-hoarding]])  
- Trust networks and democratization (insulation vs political integration — Chs. 4, 13)  
- Organizational inequality as patterned ties among categories  
- Social movement diffusion via brokerage and negotiated transfer (Ch. 15)

## Critiques and Limitations

- “Tie” can become residual for any connection; Tilly’s point is **transactional origin** and durability, not mere network diagramming.  
- Methodological individualists reject ties as primary units — Tilly’s POSITION against disposition monism.  
- Overlaps with network formalization without adopting full graph-theory machinery in this volume.
