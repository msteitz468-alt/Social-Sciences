# Boycott (Political Tactic)

## Definition as Coined

In Russian Social-Democratic usage during 1905–07, **boycott** named a political method of struggle against quasi-constitutional representative institutions of the old regime. [[lenin-vladimir]], in *Against Boycott* (June 26 / July 9, 1907; pamphlet *Concerning the Boycott of the Third Duma*), defined boycott as struggle **not within** a given institution but **against its emergence**, or more broadly against its becoming operative. He distinguished **active boycott** — not mere refusal to participate, but an attack on the old regime, openly linked in Social-Democratic literature of the Bulygin period with **armed uprising** — from **passive boycott** (abstention alone).

Lenin insisted that boycott was **not** a standing “line of tactics” identifying Bolshevism (as opposed to Menshevism), but a **special means under special conditions**. Confusing Bolshevism with “boycottism,” he wrote, was as mistaken as identifying it with “boyevism.”

## Semantic History

Boycott as refusal of markets or institutions predates Russian Marxism; in this source the operative meaning is the **Duma-era revolutionary tactic**. Key moments Lenin periodizes:

| Moment | Lenin’s framing (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Bulygin Duma (autumn 1905)** | Active boycott as fight to prevent the revolution being switched onto a monarchist-constitutional path; successful under sweeping mass upswing |
| **Witte Duma** | Boycott slogan as concentration and generalisation of sporadic mutinies/strikes of summer 1906 after December 1905 defeat |
| **First–Second Dumas** | Period of “constitutional illusions”; SDs still used the institutions for propaganda and agitation |
| **Third Duma (post–June 3, 1907)** | Boycott **cannot** have the same meaning: monarchist-constitutional turn is a fact; no grounds for proclaiming boycott without a new broad upswing |

The “living spirit” of the 1905–06 boycott campaign, Lenin argued, was the **fight against constitutional illusions**, not the word “boycott” alone. Mensheviks (including Plekhanov at Stockholm, as Lenin frames them) treated such a fight under nascent constitutionalism as anarchist nonsense.

## Operationalizations

Lenin’s criteria (Theses on attitude to the Third Duma, July 1907; *Against Boycott*):

1. **Not degree of reaction** — a Marxist does not boycott because an institution is reactionary, but because special struggle conditions (as Russian revolutionary experience showed) make boycott applicable.
2. **Active mass upswing** — successful boycott requires broad, universal revolutionary upswing moving toward straightforward assault (armed uprising); without mass unrest overflowing old legality, boycott cannot succeed.
3. **Warning function** — boycott warns proletariat and people against constitutional illusions and quasi-constitutional institutions granted by the old regime.
4. **Active vs passive** — active boycott = refusal + attack; passive boycott = abstention without offensive content.
5. **Traditions vs slogans** — preserve revolutionary traditions and study forms of struggle; do **not** treat reuse of a historical slogan as restoring that period’s conditions (Lenin citing Marx on 1848 traditions and the 1870 Address warning French workers re 1792).

Operational contrast in 1907: Socialist-Revolutionary and Teachers’ Congress arguments grounded boycott almost entirely in the Third Duma’s reactionariness and fear that participation would “sanction” the June 3 coup; Lenin rejected that grounding as insufficient.

## Applications

- **Bolshevik practice 1905–06 (as Lenin recounts):** boycott of Bulygin and Witte Dumas; Mensheviks vacillated (e.g. appealed to vote but not “for” the Witte Duma).
- **Against Boycott (1907):** Lenin concludes autumn 1907 offers **no grounds** for a Third Duma boycott slogan. Continue election preparation; do not refuse participation in reactionary representative institutions; explain December defeat → decline of liberty; do not renounce boycott for a **future** rising revolution; convert working-class upswings into sweeping assault on reaction’s foundations.
- **Even the “strongest Marxist” pro-boycott case** (partial industrial revival, planned textile strike) he treats as insufficient for the **immediate** slogan at the start of a partial upswing.
- **Internal Bolshevik episode (1908):** disagreement on Third Duma boycott was never as sharp as a split threat and did not map onto materialists vs Machists (Bazarov opposed boycott with Lenin).

## Critiques and Limitations

From this source alone, the concept’s load-bearing limits are Lenin’s own:

- **Condition-dependence** — without broad upswing, boycott fails or becomes empty repetition of slogans.
- **Not a permanent identity** — “boycottism” as faction brand misdescribes Bolshevism; Bolshevik/Menshevik tactical lines already differed in 1905 without boycott talk.
- **SR reduction** — grounding boycott solely in institution-reactionariness misses the Marxist criterion of struggle conditions.
- **Compromise problem** — Lenin (citing Engels against Blanquist absolute rejection of compromises) holds Marxism uses and fights compromises; boycott vs participation is not an absolute moral rule.
- **Source asymmetry** — SR, Menshevik, and Teachers’ Congress positions appear as Lenin frames them in *Against Boycott* and related pieces in [[lenin-collected-works-vol-13-1962]].
