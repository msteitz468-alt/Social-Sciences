# Courtesy stigma

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]*, 1963) described the tendency of stigma to **spread from the stigmatized person to close connexions** — the loyal spouse of the mental patient, the daughter of the ex-con, the parent of the disabled child — a **courtesy stigma** (stigma by courtesy of association). Those related through the social structure are obliged to share some of the discredit; one response is to embrace it and live in the world of the stigmatized connexion; another is avoidance or termination of the relation (attributed). Courtesy stigma can generate once-removed waves (e.g. child ostracized because father is an ex-convict — Ann Landers illustration in Goffman) (attributed).

Related: **tribal stigma** contaminates lineage members as a class; courtesy stigma is the general mechanism of associative spread.

## Semantic History

Standard term in family-caregiver, disability, and mental-illness research ("affiliate stigma" in later scales).

## Operationalizations

Later caregiver-burden / affiliate-stigma scales; Goffman remains conceptual.

## Applications

Family of patients; mixed-status couples; occupational courtesy among workers with the stigmatized; [[the-own-and-the-wise|the wise]] who stay inside the circle.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Courtesy" can understate coercion and structural discrimination against associates.
