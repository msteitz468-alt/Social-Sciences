# Time-Space Distanciation

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: the **stretching of social systems across time-space**, on the basis of mechanisms of social and system integration (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

In *[[giddens-consequences-of-modernity-1990|The Consequences of Modernity]]* (1990), Giddens reformulated the sociological "problem of order" (against Parsons) as how social systems **"bind" time and space** — i.e. time-space distanciation as conditions organising time and space to connect presence and absence. He argued modernity’s dynamism derives partly from the **separation of time and space** and their recombination in forms permitting precise time-space "zoning" of social life (empty time via mechanical clock and calendar; empty space separated from place). **Place** becomes "phantasmagoric" — locales thoroughly penetrated by distant influences.

## Semantic History

Developed across *A Contemporary Critique of Historical Materialism* (1981) and *Constitution* Ch. 3. Builds on and criticizes time-geography (Hägerstrand) and time-space convergence (Janelle). *Consequences* (1990) makes distanciation a **facilitating condition** of modernity’s institutional clusterings (with [[disembedding]] and reflexivity) and the starting point for defining [[globalization]] as intensification of worldwide social relations.

Distinct from Harvey’s [[time-space-compression]] (political-economic acceleration under flexible accumulation) — related problem-space, different apparatus; do not equate without argument.

## Operationalizations

Comparative institutional analysis of how far relations of co-presence are mediated by writing, money, electronic communication, organizational surveillance, cities, states.

## Applications

- Tribal vs class-divided (cities) vs modern capitalist-industrial stretch (*Constitution*).
- Separation of communication media from transportation (telegraph) as radical disjuncture.
- Power expandable via [[allocative-and-authoritative-resources]] across distance.
- Globalisation definition and critique of bounded "society" (*Consequences*).
- Trust in [[abstract-systems]] as condition of extended distanciation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of technological teleology if stretch is read as linear progress; Giddens stresses variable systemness and conflictual time-space edges.
