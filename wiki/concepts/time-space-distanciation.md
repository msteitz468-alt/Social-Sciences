# Time-Space Distanciation

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: the **stretching of social systems across time-space**, on the basis of mechanisms of social and system integration (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Developed across *A Contemporary Critique of Historical Materialism* (1981) and *Constitution* Ch. 3. Builds on and criticizes time-geography (Hägerstrand) and time-space convergence (Janelle). Later globalization/modernity literature (not yet ingested) extends the term.

## Operationalizations

Comparative institutional analysis of how far relations of co-presence are mediated by writing, money, electronic communication, organizational surveillance, cities, states.

## Applications

- Tribal vs class-divided (cities) vs modern capitalist-industrial stretch.
- Separation of communication media from transportation (telegraph) as radical disjuncture.
- Power expandable via [[allocative-and-authoritative-resources]] across distance.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of technological teleology if stretch is read as linear progress; Giddens stresses variable systemness and conflictual time-space edges.
