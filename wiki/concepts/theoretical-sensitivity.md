# Theoretical Sensitivity

## Definition as Coined

[[glaser-barney-g|Glaser]] and [[strauss-anselm-l|Strauss]] (1967) treat the analyst as needing capacity for theoretical insight into data and for rendering insight in conceptual form — not genius, but cultivated sensitivity. Ch. XI centers **insight** as the root source of significant theorizing, developed through systematic comparative analysis; personal and borrowed experience, immersion-with-detachment, and ongoing reformulation of perspective all feed the capacity. Later Glaser book *Theoretical Sensitivity* (1978) elaborates — **not yet ingested**; do not import 1978 apparatus into this page from outside knowledge.

## Semantic History

1967 theme of insight/sensitivity; the phrase becomes a GT keyword especially after later Glaser titles. Adjacent to Blumer's [[sensitizing-concept]] (concepts that guide looking) without identity: sensitivity is a property of the **analyst**; sensitizing concepts are a property of **concepts**.

## Operationalizations

Skill/disposition of the analyst, not a measurement index. Evidenced in memo quality, category/property density, and ability to use theoretical sampling without freezing early.

## Applications

Ch. XI and generation chapters of *Discovery*; initial theoretical sampling decisions (Ch. III) also invoke theoretical sensitivity.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can appear to reintroduce the "gifted observer" problem the method claims to systematize; authors answer with systematic comparative procedures as the chief safeguard against stopping development too soon.
