# Scale-Making

## Definition as Coined

In *[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]*, [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Tsing]] treats **scale** not as a neutral ladder from micro to global but as something **made** through projects that link or sever places, recruit collaborators, and authorize claims. Global connection requires scale-making work; “the global” is not a pre-given level *(attributed)*.

Linked claims (attributed):

- Capitalism must be studied **in friction**, through scale-making projects rather than as a finished planetary machine.
- Scientific and conservation universals (botany, parks, climate models, ITTO tropical timber regimes) succeed or fail as scale claims.
- Indonesian nature-loving produces national scale experience from international adventure forms (mountaineering, SAR, expeditions).
- CBNRM and related donor packages travel as **engaged universals** through Ford and other circuits — scale-shifts (e.g. community irrigation → community forestry per Coward) enable new alliances and cooptation risks.
- Method: attend **incompatible datasets** across village / province / capital rather than force global aggregation (Manggur campaign: three forest stories partially overlapping on “community”).

## Semantic History

Sits with critical geography’s production-of-scale literature and multi-sited ethnography debates; Tsing’s contribution is ethnographic demonstration via Indonesian environmental, scientific, and capital projects.

## Operationalizations

Tracking claims to global/national/local legitimacy in nature-loving, conservation science, activist campaigns, finance spectacles, and donor model travel.

## Applications

Globalization ethnography; environmental politics; critique of seamless globalism and pure localism; link to [[traveling-universals]].

**Scalability vs scale-making (*Mushroom*, 2015):** In *[[tsing-mushroom-2015|The Mushroom at the End of the World]]*, Tsing develops a related but distinct critique of **scalability** — the ability of a project to expand without changing its framing assumptions (plantation model; scalable science). Arts of noticing and matsutake “anti-plantations” refuse nested, expandable research frames; matsutake commerce depends on scalability *in ruins* rather than pure nonscalability *(Tsing 2015)*. See [[patchiness]], [[arts-of-noticing]], [[tsing-mushroom-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Overlaps geography’s scale debates — keep Tsing’s ethnographic sense distinct from cartographic scale unless explicitly linked. Not a complete theory of the world-system; complementary to [[world-systems-analysis]] macros. Do not collapse *Friction*’s scale-making (who claims global/national/local) with *Mushroom*’s scalability (expansion without frame change) — related, not identical.
