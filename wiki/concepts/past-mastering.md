# Past Mastering

## Definition as Coined

**Past mastering** is [[meskell-lynn|Lynn Meskell's]] organizing term (attributed,
[[meskell-nature-of-heritage-2012|*The Nature of Heritage*]] 2012) for the struggle to come to terms
with a past that is "contested, conflictual, and multiply constituted." The phrase deliberately
echoes the German **Vergangenheitsbewältigung** ("coming to terms with the past") while insisting
that in South Africa the process is **unfolding and not-yet-historical** — a "future perfect,"
ongoing project rather than a settled reckoning. It takes its cue from Paul Gilroy's *Postcolonial
Melancholia* (2005), which Meskell uses as an epigraph.

## Semantic History

- Coined against the backdrop of the German model of *Vergangenheitsbewältigung*, but repurposed for
  a **recently transitioned** society (South Africa's democratic transition dates to 1994), where the
  reckoning is visibly "in process."
- In Meskell's conclusion the term **expands** beyond the processing of history and archaeology to
  include territory, conservation, natural resources, public goods, and "private pains" — an
  open-ended labor rather than a completed task.

## Operationalizations

Meskell's cases (attributed, 2012), centered on [[kruger-national-park|Kruger National Park]]:

- The recurring institutional metaphor of **"Skukuza"** ("to sweep/strip clean") — from the original
  removal of indigenous inhabitants to the literal burning-clearance of the Masorini site — read as a
  mechanism of past mastering.
- Heritage recast serially since 1994 as burden, then asset, then burden again — "made and unmade"
  as the state alternately mobilizes and abandons it.
- [[negative-heritage|Negative heritage]] (forced removals, burial-site destruction) as the material
  a nation must master.

## Applications

Analytic frame for post-conflict and post-colonial heritage generally; Meskell argues South Africa
offers a "critical distillation" of global political-heritage developments condensed into roughly a
decade. Links to [[heritage]], [[negative-heritage]], [[social-memory]], and
[[critical-heritage-studies]].

## Critiques and Limitations

As a coinage tied to one author's monograph, "past mastering" has not yet accumulated an independent
literature; its openness (extending to "private pains" and conservation) is both its analytic reach
and a risk of over-extension. Recorded as Meskell's attributed construct.
