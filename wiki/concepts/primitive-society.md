# Primitive Society

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[kuper-adam|Kuper]], *[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|The Invention of Primitive Society]]* 1988.)* **Primitive society** names not an empirical type discovered in the field but a **disciplinary prototype** that crystallized with anthropology itself in the 1860s–70s and persisted across evolutionism, diffusionism, functionalism, and structuralism. In Kuper’s reconstruction, the prototype bundled assumptions about early social organization — kinship as the skeleton of society, clan/gens structure, regulated marriage/exogamy, collective property or status, “primitive religion” (often [[totemism]]) — as the **mirror image** of modern society.

Kuper’s canonical five late-century propositions (as he reconstructs consensus):

1. Most “primitive” societies ordered by kinship relations.
2. Kinship organization based on descent groups.
3. Descent groups exogamous and related by marriage exchanges.
4. Primeval institutions preserved as fossils in ceremonies and kinship terminologies.
5. With private property, descent groups withered and a territorial state emerged — the ancient-to-modern transition.

## Semantic History

| Period | Sense (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Pre-1860 | Scattered traveler/philosophical “savage” images; not yet the anthropological package |
| 1860s–70s | Legal-evolutionist monographs (Bachofen, [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]], Fustel, Lubbock, McLennan, [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]], [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]]) crystallize a novel sociological version |
| Late 19th c. | [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]]/[[frazer-james-george|Frazer]] adjudicate Maine vs rivals; matriarchal exogamous descent groups + totemism package |
| Classical–postwar | Boasian critique, Rivers, [[descent-theory]], [[alliance-theory]] **transform** rather than abandon the prototype (Kuper) |
| Late 20th c. | Mainstream cultural/social anthropology shifts object toward “culture”; biological-evolutionary anthropology occupies other margins (Kuper’s closing diagnosis) |

Related but not identical: [[primitiveness]] (broader evaluative register). Ethnographic societies must never be silently equated with the prototype (four-way non-identity).

## Operationalizations

Classificatory kinship schedules; unilineal descent-group models; totemism–exogamy complexes; segmentary lineage diagrams; elementary structures of exchange — each treated in the literature as a key to “primitive” social structure. Kuper treats these as puzzle-solving tools of the tradition, not neutral measures of a natural kind.

## Applications

- Organizing foil for Victorian debates on family, property, citizenship, and the state.
- Technical core of social anthropology via kinship studies (“Kinship is the central discipline of anthropology… what the nude is to art” — Fox, as cited by Kuper).
- Target of Boasian anti-evolutionism and later Schneiderian critique of kinship.
- Central object of [[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988]].

## Critiques and Limitations

*(All attributed — Kuper 1988 unless noted.)*

- **Invented object / illusion:** “the history of the theory of primitive society is the history of an illusion. It is our phlogiston, our aether.” No fossils of social organization; human societies cannot be traced to a single origin and ranked as a time series of social forms.
- **Mirror image:** modern society defined by territorial state, monogamous family, private property → primitive society imagined as nomadic, blood-ordered, promiscuous, communist; mentality magic → religion → science.
- **Lawyer construction:** pioneers often lawyers treating marriage, family, property, and the state as legal questions; Roman law as shared case-study — not natural history, not strict Darwinism (un-Darwinian anthropology paradox).
- **Persistence despite contradiction:** basic assumptions contradicted by ethnography and evolutionary logic (Westermarck, Boas, Malinowski stated difficulties); yet the fantasy was manipulated for over a century.
- **Three strengths** (Kuper): (1) ultimate social concerns (state, citizenship, family); (2) specialized puzzle-solving tradition; (3) endless transformations accommodating any special interest.
- **Political multivalence:** usable by imperialists, nationalists, anarchists, Marxists; blood vs soil equated with race and citizenship; reformist message that forms are not fixed.
- **No single ethnographic refutation:** models provided analytical short-cuts; Schneider’s Yap “double descent” reanalysis exemplifies theory constraining what counts as data.
- **Four-way non-identity:** the construct is not a language family, genetic population, archaeological culture, or self-conscious people.
