# Agromanagerial Despotism

## Definition as Coined

In *[[wittfogel-oriental-despotism-1957|Oriental Despotism]]* (1957), [[wittfogel-karl-a|Wittfogel]] uses **agromanagerial despotism** for the political order of **[[hydraulic-society]]**: absolute power whose managerial core is large-scale water control and related public works, not industrial apparatus rule. He treats it as essential and (so far as known) specific to hydraulic society (Ch. 10).

## Semantic History

Near-synonym refinement of **[[oriental-despotism]]** that shifts emphasis from geographic "Orient" to **agrarian managerial** structure. Contrasted with feudal limited service/vassalage/fief and with modern total-managerial (Communist/Fascist) states — Wittfogel's "total power" is the broader category; "totalitarian" is reserved for modern apparatus states (third-printing note).

## Operationalizations

State management of irrigation/flood works; organizational monopoly; weak proprietary counterweights; fused religious authority — see [[hydraulic-society]].

## Applications

Label for Pharaonic Egypt, imperial China, Inca, and other cases Wittfogel classifies as hydraulic.

## Critiques and Limitations

Inherits the contested causal link of the [[hydraulic-hypothesis-debate]]; "managerial" may overstate water bureaucracy relative to military and ideological power in many cases.
