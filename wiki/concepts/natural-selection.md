# Natural Selection

## Definition as Coined

[[darwin-charles|Darwin]]'s mechanism whereby heritable variations advantageous under the [[struggle-for-existence]] are preserved. In *[[darwin-descent-of-man-1871|The Descent of Man]]* (1871) he restated the contrast with [[sexual-selection]]: natural selection depends on success of **both sexes, at all ages, relative to general conditions of life**; sexual selection on success of individuals of the **same sex relative to propagation**.

Applied to man, Darwin held that NS acted on corporeal structure (erect posture and consequences; dominance via tools, sociality, intellect) and especially on **intellectual and moral faculties**, including multi-level (tribe-level) selection for courage, fidelity, and sympathy. He largely **denied** that NS fixed the external differences between human races (colour, hair, features), because those traits appeared of no direct service — opening the door to sexual selection (ch. VII).

## Semantic History

| Phase | Sense |
|---|---|
| *Origin* (1859) | Species-level evolutionary mechanism; man only hinted |
| *Descent* (1871) | Explicit application to man: body, mind, morals; limits re race exteriors; civilised-nation checks on elimination |
| Spencer / Sumner | “Survival of the fittest” transferred to society → [[social-darwinism]] (partly retrospective label) |
| Wallace | Independent co-discoverer; later limited NS on human mind/hairlessness |
| Modern biology | Genetic, population-genetic restatement — date-stamp; not identical to 1871 wording |

## Operationalizations

In *Descent*: Malthusian increase + checks (war, famine, infanticide per McLennan, disease); variability and inheritance (Galton); use/disuse and correlation as adjuncts; Wallace’s point that after mind develops, bodily NS weakens because tools and mutual aid buffer environmental change. Civilisation **checks** elimination of the weak (asylums, poor-laws, medicine) yet may favour bodily development via food — a tension Darwin refused to resolve by abandoning sympathy.

## Applications

- Human dominance and erect-posture suite (ch. IV).
- Intellectual advance and [[moral-sense]] via individual + tribe-level selection (ch. III, V).
- Explicitly **not** primary explanation of racial external differences (ch. VII).
- Sociological transfer and critique: [[struggle-for-existence]], [[social-darwinism]], [[societal-selection]].

## Critiques and Limitations

*Descent* ch. V’s discussion of civilised nations checking elimination of the weak was later mined for eugenic and “social Darwinist” programs Darwin himself partly refused (he would not suppress sympathy). Tribe-level moral selection anticipates later group-selection debates. The 1871 text still includes Lamarckian “inherited habit” alongside NS. Modern population genetics re-grounds human variation without Victorian racial typology — see [[race]], [[race-and-culture-debate]].
