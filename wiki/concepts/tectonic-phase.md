# Tectonic Phase

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]], *[[renfrew-prehistory-making-human-mind-2007|Prehistory: The Making of the Human Mind]]* 2007.)* The **tectonic phase** names the period after modern humans are in place as a species — roughly the last ~60,000 years in Renfrew's outline — during which **diverse cultural trajectories** (sedentism, farming, monuments, valuables, cosmos appropriation, writing, early states) reshape human life. On his account, **genome change is no longer the main driver of cognitive capacity**; change runs through learning, symbols, and **[[material-engagement]]**.

## Semantic History

"Tectonic" evokes constructive building/rearrangement rather than biological speciation. Complements [[speciation-phase]]. In 2007 Renfrew places even Upper Palaeolithic cave art as a **localized tectonic** episode, not proof of a completed single "human revolution" that ends the explanatory problem (see [[sapient-paradox]]). Aligns with the second cognitive-archaeology subfield: varying cognitive devices along different post-dispersal trajectories.

## Operationalizations

Comparative multi-trajectory charts (Near East, Europe, East Asia, Americas, etc.) rather than a single progress ladder. Horizon **as of Renfrew 2007**.

## Applications

Frames Chs. 6–10 of *Prehistory* 2007: prehistory of mind; constructing community; worldly goods; appropriating the cosmos; writing/history. Resolves the sapient paradox via post-sedentism materializations rather than further neurological revolution.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of underplaying ongoing selection and population structure; multi-trajectory charts can still smuggle Western Asia–Europe privilege; genetics/chronology as of 2007. Keep attributed.
