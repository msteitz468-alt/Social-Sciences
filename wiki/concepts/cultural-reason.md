# Cultural Reason

## Definition as Coined

[[sahlins-marshall|Marshall Sahlins]] (attributed, *[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]*, 1976) uses **"cultural reason"** — also "the symbolic or meaningful" — for the paradigm opposed to [[practical-reason]]: the position that "human action in the world is to be understood as mediated by the cultural design, which gives order at once to practical experience, customary practice, and the relationship between the two." Its founding thesis: "meaning is the specific property of the anthropological object. Cultures are meaningful orders of persons and things." Because these orders are systematic, they "cannot be a free invention of the mind"; anthropology's task is to discover the system.

The decisive move is that cultural reason **encompasses** practical reason: "As opposed to all these genera and species of practical reason, this book poses a reason of another kind, the symbolic or meaningful... it is culture which constitutes utility." Utility is not a pre-cultural ground on which culture is built; what counts as useful is specified by a prior symbolic scheme "which is never the only one possible."

## Semantic History

Sahlins traces cultural reason through two convergent traditions. The **American line** runs from [[boas-franz|Boas]] — whose move "from physics to ethnology" led him to conclude that "the seeing eye is the organ of tradition," perception mediated by cultural pattern — through Ruth Benedict's "orienting logic" and patterns of culture. The **European line** runs from [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]'s sociology of knowledge (which Sahlins judges "a sociological theory of symbolization, but not a symbolic theory of society") to French [[structuralism]] via [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]], who refused the Durkheimian separation of social morphology from collective representation — "his appropriation of the social by the symbolic." The Saussurean model of value grounds the whole position: as with *sheep*/*mouton*, no language is "a mere nomenclature"; meaning is constituted differentially within the system, and "the arbitrariness of the symbol is the indicative condition of human culture," extending beyond the linguistic sign to cultural propositions generally (see [[saussure-ferdinand-de]], [[culture]]).

## Operationalizations

Cultural reason is a demand on method rather than a measure: symbolic analysis must attend to *content* and internal meaningful relations, not merely to instrumental effect. Sahlins's methodological aphorism is that a theory should be judged "as much by the ignorance it demands as by the 'knowledge' it affords" — the practical-reason theories fail because they demand ignorance of the very meanings that constitute their objects. In his historical anthropology (*[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*, 1985) cultural reason becomes the claim that "structure — the symbolic relations of cultural order — is an historical object" (see [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]]).

## Applications

The concept's showcase application is the demonstration that even Western capitalism is organized by a symbolic code, not by pure utility: [[la-pensee-bourgeoise]]. Its reflexive edge is [[totemism]] — Sahlins argues Western evolutionary/biological social science "may be the highest form of totemism," explaining human society by distinctions between species, an empirical "origin myth" of descent from savages.

## Critiques and Limitations

Because cultural reason is one side of a live paradigm dispute, its promotion of the symbolic over the material is contested by materialist and Marxist anthropologists (see [[historical-materialism]], [[structural-marxism]]). Sahlins insists the position is not idealism — material forces have real effects — but that their social *form* cannot be deduced from their material nature. Within the culturalist camp, later critics (e.g. from [[practice-theory]] and the [[eventful-sociology|eventful]] program) pressed him on whether "structure" conceived as a singular, purely symbolic order adequately credits material resources and actors' creativity — reservations recorded on [[sahlins-marshall]] and [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]].
