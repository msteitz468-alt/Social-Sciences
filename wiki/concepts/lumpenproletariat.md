# Lumpenproletariat

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|The Eighteenth Brumaire]]* (1852), [[marx-karl|Marx]] analyzes the **slum-proletariat** (this edition’s English for *Lumpenproletariat*) as the “undefined, dissolute, kicked-about mass” — ruined rogues, dismissed soldiers, convicts, beggars, and “dregs of the bourgeoisie” — organized in the **Society of December 10** (from 1849) under Bonapartist agents. Bonaparte constitutes himself “Chief of the Slum-Proletariat”; only here, Marx claims, he finds interests matching his own and “the only class upon which he can depend unconditionally.” Related Ch. I image: “the slums, organized as Guarde Mobile” in the June 1848 coalition against the Paris [[proletariat]]; “the refuse of bourgeois society” as “holy phalanx of Order.”

## Semantic History

German *Lumpen* (rags) + proletariat. Present in *Manifesto*-era vocabulary; the *Brumaire* gives the fullest classical portrait as **political base of personal rule**, not as a productive class. Later Marxist and sociological uses range from street underclass to pejorative dismissal of non-organized poor; contemporary sociology largely abandoned the term’s moralizing load while retaining interest in informal/marginal strata (reception not fully ingested).

## Operationalizations

- Membership of Society of December 10 and similar client networks.
- Military rank-and-file bought with “liquor and sausages” / cash bounties (Marx’s coup narrative).
- Contrast with industrial [[proletariat]]: not defined by wage relation to capital as productive labor-power.

## Applications

- [[bonapartism]] and executive independence ([[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]]).
- Class maps of counter-revolution and “Order” coalitions.
- Cautionary case for equating “the people” with a single progressive subject.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Polemical catalogue:** the long list of occupations is rhetorical as well as analytic; risk of moralizing the poor.
- **Boundary problems:** who counts as lumpen vs. unemployed workers, casual labor, or peasantry remains contested.
- **Edition wording:** “slum-proletariat” vs. standard “lumpenproletariat” — same concept slug.
