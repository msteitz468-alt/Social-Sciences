# Vernacular Globalization

The [[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]] argument that globalization proceeds through
**local appropriation ("indigenization")**, not homogenizing diffusion — developed
paradigmatically through the decolonization of Indian cricket
(*[[appadurai-modernity-at-large-1996|Modernity at Large]]*, ch. 5). Attributed to
Appadurai. (The exact phrase "vernacular globalization" belongs to Appadurai's
later work; this page uses it for the argument stated here as indigenization.)

## Definition as Coined

Globalization "does not necessarily or even frequently imply homogenization or
Americanization"; imported forms are indigenized "at least as rapidly as forces
from various metropolises are brought into new societies" *(Appadurai)*. Key
analytic device: **hard vs. soft cultural forms** — "Hard cultural forms are those
that come with a set of links between value, meaning, and embodied practice that
are difficult to break and hard to transform. Soft cultural forms… permit
relatively easy separation of embodied performance from meaning and value."

## Semantic History

Sharpens the [[homogenization-heterogenization-debate|homogenization/
heterogenization]] argument of ch. 2 into a worked case. Cricket is treated as a
**hard** form — Victorian/puritan in its moral architecture — whose Indianization is
therefore the more striking.

## Operationalizations

The Indian cricket case: princely, corporate, and state patronage; a talent
reservoir beyond urban elites; **vernacular radio and television commentary** and a
"sports pidgin" that "linguistically domesticated" the game; vernacular cricket
magazines; the construction of a "postcolonial male spectatorship." Appadurai reads
indigenization as "a product of collective and spectacular experiments with
modernity" — his **"means of modernity"** — explicitly posed as an alternative to
Ashis Nandy's claim that cricket is "really" an Indian game by deep
structural affinity.

## Applications

Sharjah cricket as a "different global ecumene" ("After Sharjah, all cricket is
Trobriand cricket… From the perspective of Sharjah, it is the Etonians who seem like
Trobrianders today"). Feeds the [[globalization]] page's heterogenization pole and
the [[mediascape]] concept. Comparable to the divergent national *sequences* of the
consumer revolution (ch. 4).

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics of the indigenization thesis argue it can understate the power asymmetries
and market structures that shape which forms travel and on whose terms; the
hard/soft distinction is heuristic rather than sharply operationalized.
