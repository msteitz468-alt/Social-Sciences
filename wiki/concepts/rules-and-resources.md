# Rules and Resources

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary — **Structure:** rules and resources, recursively implicated in the reproduction of social systems. Structure exists only as memory traces, the organic basis of human [[knowledgeability]], and as instantiated in action (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Rejects structure as visible pattern "outside" action (common macrosociological usage) and as pure constraint. Resources subdivided into [[allocative-and-authoritative-resources]]. Modalities link structure to interaction (interpretive schemes, facilities, norms — Ch. 1 apparatus).

## Operationalizations

Institutional analysis brackets actors' skills to treat institutions as chronically reproduced rules and resources; analysis of strategic conduct reverses the bracket.

## Applications

- Definitional core of [[duality-of-structure]].
- Study of [[structural-principles]] as deepest rule-resource organization of societal totalities.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Rules" risk legalistic reading Giddens rejects; "virtual" existence of structure contested by realists and ethnographers.

[[sewell-william-jr|Sewell]] (*[[sewell-logics-of-history-2005|Logics of History]]*, ch. 4) presses exactly these points and reformulates the pair: he replaces **"rules"** with **[[transposable-schemas|schemas]]** (to avoid the legalistic connotation and cover the informal, unconscious cultural procedures anthropology studies) and splits **resources** into **human** and **nonhuman** (his rework of the [[allocative-and-authoritative-resources|allocative/authoritative]] distinction), insisting resources are *actual*, not virtual — which is why he holds Giddens's all-virtual definition incoherent. See [[transposable-schemas]].
