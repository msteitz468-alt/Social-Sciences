# Role-Set

## Definition as Coined

[[merton-robert-k|Merton]] defined the **role-set** as that **complement of social relationships** in which persons are involved simply because they occupy a particular social status — e.g., the teacher's role-set includes relations to pupils, colleagues, principal, PTA, etc. He distinguished this sharply from **multiple roles** attached to **different statuses** of the same person (the latter array is a [[status-set]]). Structural source of conflict: role-set members typically occupy **different** social positions and thus hold distinct interests (he noted alignment on this structural fact with theorists as disparate as Marx, Spencer, Simmel, Sorokin, and Parsons). The concept generates the research problem of **articulation mechanisms** that reduce role-set conflict — and residual conflict when mechanisms fail.

## Semantic History

Introduced as a middle-range theoretical image generating researchable problems: articulating conflicting expectations within a role-set; structural mechanisms that reduce role-set conflict; residual conflict. Became standard role theory vocabulary.

## Operationalizations

Structural analysis of expectation conflict; observational and organizational studies of role articulation (later literature).

## Applications

Bureaucracy; professions; family–work; any status with heterogeneous role-partners.

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundary of a "status" can be arbitrary; mechanisms of articulation variously specified; overlaps later network and identity approaches.
