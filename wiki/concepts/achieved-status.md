# Achieved Status

A social position acquired through individual action or performance, as opposed to [[ascribed-status|ascribed status]]. In *[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-1967|The American Occupational Structure]]*, education and occupational destinations are the principal achieved statuses in the path model.

## Definition as Coined

Lintonian achieved status: position gained by effort or choice. Blau and Duncan measure educational attainment, first full-time job, and current occupation (SEI) as achievements structured by origins.

## Semantic History

Paired with ascription in mid-century theory; status-attainment research quantified how much "achievement" is predicted by ascription.

## Operationalizations

Years/levels of schooling; SEI of first job; SEI of 1962 occupation; related career contingencies (migration, marriage) treated as intermediate variables.

## Applications

[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-study]]; human-capital and status-attainment traditions.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Achievement" language can naturalize unequal outcomes; education is jointly determined by ascription and institutional sorting; later critics preferred class, rent, or network vocabularies over the achievement–ascription frame.
