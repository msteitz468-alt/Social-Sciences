# Neolithic Sensory Revolution

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed, [[tilley-christopher|Tilley]] 2010 Ch. 2 / earlier 2007 paper in Whittle & Cummings eds.)* If the Neolithic involved new modes of thought, a fundamental part of that shift was stimulated by **fresh forms of sensory experience of place and landscape**. Extensive, more permanent woodland clearance altered experiential conditions: "If there was a Neolithic 'revolution', it entailed a sensory revolution."

## Semantic History

Distinct from Childe's economic [[agricultural-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]]. Tilley frames Neolithic change as ideology/mind mediated by material and sensory change (indigenous selective adoption position). Restated comparatively in *[[tilley-interpreting-landscapes-2010|Interpreting Landscapes]]* Ch. 10 as post-Mesolithic forest clearance enabling new monument–landform relations across study areas.

## Operationalizations

Mesolithic forest world (attributed reconstruction): vision curtailed to tens of metres; sound/smell/touch primary; landform contours masked. After clearance: vision dominant for orientation; hills, ridges, coombes, coasts become readable; hilltop enclosures and long barrows participate in new vistas and intervisibility.

## Applications

Organizing thesis for Part I of *Interpreting Landscapes* and for linking Mesolithic forest dwelling to Neolithic monumentality ([[tilley-interpreting-landscapes-study]]). Cross-links: [[phenomenological-archaeology]], [[path]], [[place-as-lived]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Depends on reconstructed vegetation and modern sensory analogy; "revolution" may overstate continuity of late Mesolithic clearance; causal arrow sensory → ideology is theoretical. Keep attributed.
