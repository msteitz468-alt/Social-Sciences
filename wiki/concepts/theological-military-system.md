# Theological-Military System

## Definition as Coined

In Book VI of the *Positive Philosophy*, [[comte-auguste|Comte]] analyzed the long pre-positive history of European society as a **theological and military system** — spiritual power ordered by theology, temporal power ordered by military polity — developing through the theological stages of the [[law-of-three-stages]] (Martineau titles of ch. VII–IX):

1. **Fetichism** (ch. VII) — first theological phase; beginning of the theological-military system  
2. **Polytheism** (ch. VIII) — development of the system  
3. **Monotheism** (ch. IX) — age of monotheism; modification of the system (Rome as precondition; Catholic/medieval dual power)

The construct is an **etic philosophy-of-history category**, not an observed self-description of historical actors.

## Semantic History

- Core of Comtean [[social-dynamics]] historical program  
- Dissolution treated in ch. X (metaphysical-critical period) and crisis in ch. XII  
- Not identical to later "military society" / "industrial society" typologies without attribution — especially Spencer’s [[militant-type-of-society]] / [[industrial-type-of-society]], which key organizational constitution to war vs peaceful industry rather than to Comte’s theological stages  


## Operationalizations

Abstract historical review of "principal phases"; concentration on advanced European series; linkage of spiritual form (theology) to temporal form (military/feudal). Monotheism includes Comte's praise of medieval separation of spiritual and temporal power as preparation for later dual organization under positive auspices (ch. IX). Decline: critical period from fourteenth century; Jesuit resistance; Protestant and philosophical negative doctrines; industrial and scientific anti-military tendencies (ch. X–XII).

## Applications

- Stages proof of the three-stage law in Book VI  
- Contrast pair for modern industrial/scientific society and positive reorganization  
- Explains Comte's reading of revolutionary crisis as incomplete until spiritual (positive) reorganization replaces theology  

## Critiques and Limitations

Eurocentric stage model; military-theological couple as over-unifying; mute on non-European paths and on conflict within stages. All historical stage claims remain attributed to Comte.
