# Anomic suicide

## Definition as Coined

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] (*[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*, Book II ch. 5) assigns the name **anomic suicide** to suicide resulting from man’s activity lacking regulation and the consequent suffering — the product of [[anomie]]. Society is not only a force of attachment ([[social-integration]]) but a power **controlling** individuals ([[social-regulation]]); the way regulative action is performed relates to the social suicide-rate.

Distinguished from:

- [[egoistic-suicide]] — no basis for existence *in* life (integration)
- [[altruistic-suicide]] — basis appears *beyond* life (over-integration)
- [[fatalistic-suicide]] — opposite pole on the regulation axis (excessive regulation; footnote)

## Semantic History

1897 type name; often collapsed into everyday “anomie” talk. Keep the regulation mechanism distinct from mere social isolation (egoism).

## Operationalizations

| Form | Durkheim’s evidence (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Economic crises** | Vienna 1873–74, Paris Bourse 1882, bankruptcy spikes paralleling suicide rises |
| **Prosperity crises** | Italian post-unification boom; Prussian post-1866/1870; Paris expositions — “fortunate crises” raise suicide like disasters |
| **Poverty as protection** | Low rates in poor regions he cites; French depts: more independent means ↔ more suicides |
| **Occupational** | Trade/industry high vs agriculture (old regulative forces); possessors of comfort stricken (Table XXIV) |
| **Domestic anomy** | Widowhood crisis; divorce/separation frequency parallel to suicide (Europe, Swiss cantons, French depts) |
| **Conjugal anomy** | Divorce weakens matrimonial regulation; husbands lose preservation where divorce is common; wives’ preservation may rise — “marriage more favorable to the wife the more widely practiced divorce is, and vice versa” |

Causal formula for crises: not poverty as such but **disturbance of collective order**.

## Applications

- [[durkheim-suicide-study]]; modern rise classed with egoistic as **morbid**
- Remedy: same as egoistic at the structural level — restore collective forces ([[occupational-group]]); conjugal anomy partly independent (divorce/indissolubility antinomy; sex interests opposed in his analysis)

## Critiques and Limitations

- Sex-asymmetric divorce theory is heavily period-gendered and contested.
- Poverty-protection claim does not hold as a general modern finding.
- Occupational tables are 19th-c. classification.
- Mixed types (anomic–egoistic, etc.) in Book II ch. 6 under-specified for later operationalization.
