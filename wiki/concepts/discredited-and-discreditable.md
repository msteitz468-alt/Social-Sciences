# Discredited and discreditable

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]*, 1963) splits the stigma situation by information state:

- **Discredited** — differentness known about already or evident on the spot. Main problem: **managing tension** in social contacts (often mutual careful disattention; acting as if known differentness were irrelevant).
- **Discreditable** — differentness neither known about by those present nor immediately perceivable. Main problem: **managing information** — "To display or not to display; to tell or not to tell; to let on or not to let on; to lie or not to lie; and in each case, to whom, how, when, and where" (attributed).

A particular person typically experiences both. Ch 2 focuses the second issue under the heading **[[passing]]** (attributed).

**Paradigm (attributed):** the ex-mental patient may face not open prejudice but *unwitting acceptance* by people prejudiced against the kind of person he can be revealed to be — accepting treatment based on false suppositions.

## Semantic History

Standard Goffmanian pair; reused in disability, HIV, mental-illness, and LGBTQ disclosure literatures.

## Operationalizations

Binary analytic situations; later research treats concealability/visibility as continua.

## Applications

Organizes Ch 1–2 of *Stigma*; pairs with [[covering]] (tension reduction when stigma is already known or apparent).

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary underplays partial knowledge, rumor, and institutional records; still useful as ideal types.
