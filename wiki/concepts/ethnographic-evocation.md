# Ethnographic Evocation

Tyler’s alternative to representation: post-modern ethnography should **evoke** a world rather than claim to represent or document it as an object. Paired with [[post-modern-ethnography]] in *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* (1986).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed to Tyler 1986.)*

Evocation as cooperative, fragmentary, therapeutic aesthetic integration — not correspondence between text and cultural object. Opposite pole to natural-history documentary claims of [[ethnographic-realism]].

## Semantic History

Literary/phenomenological term specialized as ethnographic program in Tyler’s essay.

## Operationalizations

Aesthetic-epistemic writing goal; no completed Tylerian exemplar by his own Supplement.

## Applications

Tyler’s manifesto; experimental writing teaching.

## Critiques and Limitations

See [[writing-culture-debate]]; charges of abandoning evidence standards; Asad’s power limits.
