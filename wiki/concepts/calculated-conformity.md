# Calculated Conformity

Concept from [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]], *[[scott-weapons-of-the-weak-1985|Weapons of the Weak]]* (1985), especially Chapter 7 on routine compliance and the partial transcript.

## Definition as Coined

Scott argued that in public, power-laden settings in [[sedaka|Sedaka]] “a carefully calculated conformity prevails for the most part.” Apparent acquiescence is often **strategic** — necessary where open defiance is dangerous or futile — rather than proof of [[false-consciousness]] or full [[hegemony|hegemonic]] internalization. Resistance often “covers its tracks”; the public performance of deference is a **partial transcript** of a fuller offstage critique (gossip, character assassination, private class analysis).

> “By its calculated prudence and secrecy it preserves, for the most part, the onstage theater of power… Deference and conformity, though rarely cringing, continue to be the public posture of the poor. For all that, however, one can clearly make out backstage a continuous testing of limits.”

## Semantic History

Prefigures the public/hidden transcript vocabulary of *Domination and the Arts of Resistance* (1990; see vault scaffold [[public-transcript]] / concurrent source page if present). Bridges Goffmanian presentation-of-self and class analysis (Scott cites Goffman on the power of those without much to lose; Hegel’s lord–bondsman on power precluding knowledge of what the poor really think).

## Operationalizations

- *Pak Turut* party followers affiliating with UMNO for majority-side access to work and petty patronage.
- Explicit benefit formulas for party choice (“If I enter UMNO I can latch on to work from a rich man…”).
- Public silence coexisting with nicknames, offstage bitterness, and low-profile economic sabotage.
- Methodological rule: do not infer ideological support from faithful compliance; need evidence from contexts least constrained by power.

## Applications

[[scott-weapons-of-the-weak-study]]; [[everyday-forms-of-resistance]]; critiques of [[hegemony]] and [[false-consciousness]] built on Sedaka.

## Critiques and Limitations

How observers distinguish calculated from sincere consent; risk of reading *all* compliance as resistance (definitional inflation if decoupled from Scott’s filters).
