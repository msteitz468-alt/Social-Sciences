# Sororate

## Definition as Coined

The **sororate** links a husband to his wife's sister — classically after the wife's death, though simultaneous marriage with a woman and her sister also occurs. [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]] (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*) and later notes (1947 Corrections, citing Granet) distinguish **sororate** proper (deceased wife's sister) from **sororal polygyny** (simultaneous).

## Semantic History

Victorian comparative marriage catalogues → Boasian and later kinship typology. Terminology refined mid-20th c.

## Operationalizations

Simultaneous vs sequential; obligatory vs preferred; relation to bridewealth and co-wife hierarchy.

## Applications

Marriage/polygyny chapters of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Lumping simultaneous and sequential forms obscures different institutional logics.
