# Boundary Work

## Definition as Coined

In *[[lamont-money-morals-manners-1992|Money, Morals, and Manners]]*, [[lamont-michele|Michèle Lamont]] uses **boundary work** for the pervasive activity of drawing evaluative lines between kinds of people. New Yorker scorn for Midwestern “parochialism,” French critiques of American puritan moralism, businessmen’s anti-intellectualism, and cultural specialists’ anti-materialism are treated as **instances of one social process** — not incommensurable national characters or mere political attitudes. Boundary work produces and defends [[symbolic-boundaries]]; it is shaped by available cultural repertoires and by remote and proximate structural situations.

## Semantic History

Gieryn’s original use concerns scientists’ rhetorical demarcation of science from non-science. Lamont 1992 is the primary ingested source for the cultural-sociology sense applied to everyday status assessment and class cultures. Related but not identical: Bourdieuian strategies of [[distinction]] (more capital-field-centered); Goffmanian impression management (interactional face-work rather than national/occupational pattern comparison). Distinct again: [[abbott-andrew|Abbott]]'s [[things-of-boundaries]] (*[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]* ch. 9) — an ontological thesis that social *entities* (professions, jurisdictions) often form from boundary activity, not a comparative typology of evaluative exclusion talk.

## Operationalizations

Comparative interview analysis of what interviewees exclude, admire, and rank; occupational contrasts (social/cultural specialists vs for-profit workers); national multicausal explanation (repertoires + public sector, mobility, diversity, education systems, intellectuals).

## Applications

[[lamont-money-morals-manners-study]]; explanation of national differences without modal-personality “national character”; workplace as site where boundary concepts shape hiring, mentoring, and informal training.

## Critiques and Limitations

As a process label, boundary work can become residual for any exclusion talk; Lamont’s contribution is **structured comparison** of content, strength, and autonomy of standards — not the bare claim that people draw lines. Agency vs structure: she positions between pure tool-kit voluntarism and habitus determinism (choices among repertoires, but repertoires and environments channel choice).
