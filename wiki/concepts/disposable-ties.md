# Disposable Ties

## Definition as Coined

[[desmond-matthew|Matthew Desmond]] (*American Journal of Sociology* 2012; *[[desmond-evicted-2016|Evicted]]* 2016, especially Ch. 12): **disposable ties** are relationships people in deep poverty form rapidly when family fails — often with near-strangers met in institutions that manage the poor (shelters, welfare offices, food pantries, eviction-court waiting areas) — that meet urgent needs (shared housing, money, favors) but are unstable, exhausting, and readily discarded. In *Evicted*: “It was next to impossible for people to survive deep poverty on their own. If you could not rely on your family, you could reach out to strangers, make disposable ties” (attributed).

## Semantic History

- **Classic poverty ethnography** — Carol Stack, *[[stack-all-our-kin-1974|All Our Kin]]* (1974; [[stack-all-our-kin-study]]): durable [[personal-kindred|personal kindreds]] and multi-household [[domestic-network|domestic networks]] organized by obligatory [[swapping]] and [[child-keeping]] in The Flats (late 1960s–early 1970s). This is the durable-kin baseline Desmond contrasts.
- **Granovetter [[strength-of-weak-ties]]:** weak ties as bridges for information — different mechanism and class location from disposable ties.
- **Desmond 2012/2016:** under eviction and extreme scarcity, kin webs thin or refuse; crisis generates **intense, short-lived** ties that can end badly and erode trust. Desmond also notes stated support networks should be treated skeptically (giving inflates self-worth; receiving diminishes it); ethnography under eviction crisis compares claimed vs actual support (Notes).

## Operationalizations

Primarily ethnographic identification (shared residence after eviction; rapid resource pooling; abrupt rupture). Survey network modules can mis-measure if they capture nominal rather than activated ties.

## Applications

Central to *Evicted* Part Two (Arleen–Crystal and related cases after forced moves). Links [[eviction]] to network churn and to institutions that co-locate the unstably housed.

## Critiques and Limitations

Concept is Desmond-specific analytic; not yet stress-tested against multi-city replications in this wiki. Risk of overgeneralizing Milwaukee crisis networks; risk of reading disposability as cultural preference rather than structural necessity (Desmond’s own framing resists culture-of-poverty reduction).
