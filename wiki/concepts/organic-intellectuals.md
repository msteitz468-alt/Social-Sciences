# Organic Intellectuals

> [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]]'s term from "The Intellectuals" in *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971|Selections from the Prison Notebooks]]* (1971 Eng.). **Attributed voice** throughout.

## Definition as Coined

Gramsci asks whether intellectuals are an autonomous social group or whether every social group has its own specialised category of intellectuals. He answers with two historically important forms of formation:

1. **Organic intellectuals.** Every social group that comes into existence on the terrain of an essential function in economic production "creates together with itself, organically, one or more strata of intellectuals which give it homogeneity and an awareness of its own function not only in the economic but also in the social and political fields." The capitalist entrepreneur creates the industrial technician, the specialist in political economy, organisers of a new culture and legal system, etc. These are "for the most part 'specialisations' of partial aspects of the primitive activity of the new social type."

2. **Traditional intellectuals.** Categories that appear continuous across modes of production (clergy, literary men, philosophers, artists) and claim inter-class independence, while actually attaching to successive historical class formations.

Load-bearing formula (attributed):

> "All men are intellectuals, one could therefore say: but not all men have in society the function of intellectuals."

*Homo faber* cannot be separated from *homo sapiens*; every person participates in some conception of the world. The "function" of the intellectual is organisational and connective. Intellectuals are the dominant group's "deputies" exercising the **subaltern functions of social [[hegemony]] and political government** — spanning spontaneous consent in [[civil-society]] and the coercive apparatus of political society/the State. (Here "subaltern functions" means subordinate *roles* of deputies, not "[[subaltern]] classes.")

The new intellectual, Gramsci argued (linking *Ordine Nuovo*), is not the man of eloquence but "constructor, organiser, 'permanent persuader'" — proceeding from technique-as-work to technique-as-science and a humanistic conception of history.

## Semantic History

- **Prison notebooks (1929–35):** Functional/class-organic definition against the myth of a free-floating intelligentsia; peasantry often lacks "organic" intellectuals of its own and depends on traditional urban types (lawyers, priests, notaries).
- **Hoare & Nowell Smith (1971):** Frame the schema as challenging Social-Democratic division of labour (Kautsky: bourgeois intellectuals supply theory to non-intellectual masses) and as developing a Leninist fusion of workers and intellectuals inside the party, extended to the class as a whole.
- **Cultural studies / New Left:** Stuart Hall and others rework organic intellectuals as cultural producers articulating popular common sense toward counter-hegemony.
- **Anthropology / postcolonial reception:** Mediation role of intellectuals in colonial encounter and nationalist leadership (adjacent to [[subaltern]] historiography debates) — not Gramsci's primary Italian frame.

Do not collapse Gramsci's class-functional sense with later "public intellectual" or media-celebrity usage.

## Operationalizations

- **Organicity (*organicità*):** degree of connection of an intellectual stratum to a fundamental social group; gradation of superstructural functions from structure upward.
- **Two superstructural levels:** civil society (private organisms; hegemony) and political society/State (direct domination) — intellectuals operate on both.
- **Party:** channels organic intellectuals of a class and links the class to sections of traditional intellectuals.
- **School:** instrument through which intellectuals of various levels are elaborated; dual classical/vocational systems encode class selection (Italy: rural South → functionaries/professionals; urban North → industrial technicians).

## Applications

- Risorgimento analysis: Moderates' hegemony over intellectuals; Action Party's failure to elaborate its own organic cadre (*[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971]]* Notes on Italian History).
- Working-class strategy: escape economism/corporatism toward hegemony by elaborating directive organic intellectuals (*Ordine Nuovo* model).
- [[historical-bloc]]: organic cohesion leaders–led requires intellectuals who feel/understand with the people-nation, not a detached caste.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Fragmentary notebook form leaves the organic/traditional boundary porous (engineers? teachers? party cadres?).
- Reception sometimes romanticises "organic" as authenticity and damns "traditional" as betrayal — sharper than Gramsci's historical account of assimilation struggles.
- Gender and colonial intellectual formations largely outside this selection's frame.
