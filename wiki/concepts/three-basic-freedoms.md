# Three Basic Freedoms

## Definition as Coined

In *[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|The Dawn of Everything]]* (2021), [[graeber-david|Graeber]] and [[wengrow-david|Wengrow]] propose three **basic forms of social liberty** that one might actually put into practice (attributed; restated most clearly in the Conclusion):

1. **Freedom to move away or relocate** from one's surroundings (exit; knowledge that one will be welcomed elsewhere).
2. **Freedom to ignore or disobey commands** issued by others.
3. **Freedom to shape entirely new social realities**, or to **shift back and forth** between different ones (including seasonal oscillation of political forms).

An earlier formulation in the Ice Age / property chapters (attributed) lists the same cluster as: abandon community knowing welcome in faraway lands; shift social structures by season; disobey authorities without consequence — with the puzzle restated as when it became impossible simply to **laugh kings out of court**.

They argue (attributed) that the first two freedoms often scaffolded the third: so long as exit and disobedience were assumed, kings were "play kings" and seasonal police were "play police." The master historical question of the book is not the origin of inequality but **how we got stuck** — how these three freedoms receded so that most people today can barely comprehend living by them.

## Semantic History

Assembled in *Dawn* from anthropological commonplaces (fission–fusion, exit, refusal of chiefs, seasonal dual organization, constitutional reform) into an explicit triad. Intellectual lineage: anarchist anthropology + comparative ethnography/archaeology; engaged with James C. Scott on agrarian kingdoms abolishing disobedience more easily than exit. **Not** Isaiah Berlin's negative/positive liberty; **not** Sen's capabilities approach — different problem-space (deep-time political self-consciousness vs. liberal political philosophy).

Competes as organizing vocabulary with "equality" (which G&W treat as a late, often empty term once stage theory banished Indigenous interlocutors to the bottom of the ladder — see [[indigenous-critique]]).

## Operationalizations

Qualitative comparative checklist, not quantified indices. Applied (as G&W's readings) to Upper Palaeolithic seasonality, Pacific Coast foragers, Neolithic "play farming," early cities without kings, Teotihuacan social housing, Tlaxcalan republics, Hopewell–Cahokia–post-Mississippian sequences, Osage and Haudenosaunee constitutional politics.

## Applications

- Organizing device for *Dawn* chs. 3–11 and Conclusion.
- Paired with [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]] (what accumulates when freedoms recede).
- Frames critique of scale=hierarchy assumptions ([[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]], popular big history).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Normative triad may privilege cases that illustrate freedoms and underweight coercion within the same societies (NW Coast slavery is discussed in *Dawn* but remains a hard test).
- "Freedom" as etic master term can erase emic political vocabularies.
- Seasonal-shift formulation and "create new social realities" formulation are not always kept distinct — reception sometimes flattens them.
- Contested: [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].
