# Covering

## Definition as Coined

At the close of *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]* Ch 2, [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] defined **covering** as the effort of persons ready to admit a stigma (often because it is known or immediately apparent) to keep it from **looming large** — to reduce tension, ease withdrawal of covert attention from the stigma, and sustain spontaneous involvement in the official content of interaction (attributed). Means often resemble [[passing]] techniques; what conceals from the unknowing may ease matters for those in the know. Many who rarely pass routinely cover (attributed).

**Covering vs passing (attributed):** passing targets **visibility** / known-about-ness (conceal the fact); covering targets **obtrusiveness** (the fact may be known, but interaction is structured so it does not dominate). Visibility ≠ known-about-ness ≠ obtrusiveness — Goffman separates these carefully (Ch 2) (attributed).

**Types sketched (attributed):** covering incidental standards that would spotlight the failing; covering the central failing itself; covering by managing the structure of interaction (e.g. diverting attention, staging help as ordinary).

## Semantic History

Goffman 1963 technical coinage; later disability and LGBTQ writing reuse "covering" (sometimes with legal/policy extensions beyond face-to-face focus).

## Operationalizations

Impression-management tactics for known stigma; not a scale in Goffman.

## Applications

Visible disability etiquette; accent or name management that reduces tribal markers without full concealment; professional self-presentation after known hospitalization.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can read as pressure to minimize difference for normals' comfort — later identity politics reverse the demand (cf. Ch 3 codes against "normification").
