# Emotional Energy

**Emotional energy (EE)** is [[collins-randall|Randall Collins]]'s term, developed fully in *[[collins-interaction-ritual-chains-2004|Interaction Ritual Chains]]* (2004), for a **long-term, continuous** feeling of confidence, enthusiasm, and social initiative that successful [[interaction-ritual|interaction rituals]] deposit in participants and that failed, coercive, or exclusionary rituals drain. EE is the central motivational currency of [[interaction-ritual-chains|interaction ritual chain theory]].

## Definition as Coined

Collins distinguishes short-term **dramatic** emotions (anger, joy, fear, embarrassment) from undramatic **long-term** tones or moods. He names the long-term outcome continuum **emotional energy**:

> "I refer to these long-term outcomes as 'emotional energy' (EE). It is a continuum, ranging from a high end of confidence, enthusiasm, good self-feelings; down through a middle range of bland normalcy; and to a low end of depression, lack of initiative, and negative self-feelings. Emotional energy is like the psychological concept of 'drive,' but it has a specifically social orientation." (Collins 2004, Ch. 3)

High EE, on his account, is confidence and enthusiasm **for social interaction** — initiative in setting the level of emotional entrainment, the personal side of Durkheimian ritual solidarity. Low EE is drained or depressed affect, avoidance, and alienation from group purposes and symbols. Among IR outcomes (alongside solidarity, sacred symbols, and moral feelings), EE is the share that the individual carries away into the next situation.

Collins argues that IR is an **emotion transformer**: transient emotions of many kinds enter as ingredients; mutual focus and rhythmic entrainment intensify them into shared excitement ([[collective-effervescence]]); outcomes include long-term EE and sometimes aggressive emotions (righteous anger at violations).

## Semantic History

- **Durkheim (1912):** [[collective-effervescence]] as extraordinary group stimulation — short-burst assembly energy, not yet a personal long-term continuum.
- **Goffman / Garfinkel:** undramatic emotional tones of ordinary order (that "nothing out of the ordinary is happening") and righteous anger when the mundane sacred is breached.
- **Collins 1975:** *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology]]* speaks of emotional "tone," "charge," and ritual solidarity as resources in conflict, but does not yet center the EE continuum.
- **Collins 2004:** EE becomes the theory's master currency — stratified by power and status rituals, reinvested across IR chains, proposed as the common denominator of rational choice, and (in the book's closing formula) the answer to "what human beings are" (EE-seekers).

Related but distinct: Hochschild's [[emotion-work]] / [[emotional-labor]] concerns managed feeling rules and surface/deep acting, not a continuum of ritual-charged confidence.

## Operationalizations

Collins's Ch. 3 appendix proposes measuring EE and its antecedents via self-report, body and initiative, eyes, voice (including rhythmic coordination / FFT), hormones, and face — and notes that EE decays (often within hours to a few days) after the charging ritual, so **recency** of high-intensity IRs matters for attraction to the next situation. Independent operationalization remains contested: critics argue EE is often inferred from the very solidarity and initiative outcomes it is meant to explain.

## Applications

Within IRC theory (all attributed):

- **Stratification of EE:** order-givers in power rituals tend to gain or sustain EE; order-takers lose it; central status-group members gain EE, marginal/excluded persons lose it; EE cumulates positively or negatively across chains and is situation-specific (professional confidence vs sexual EE, etc.).
- **Markets and choice:** EE as common denominator among alternative IRs; material resources constrain which rituals are accessible; material markets embed in IR-generated trust and motivation.
- **Thinking:** charged symbols and anticipated talk carry EE into solitary thought; creative intellectual work clusters in high-EE network positions (linking to Collins's *Sociology of Philosophies*).
- **Sex, manners, individuality:** sexual IR as high mutual-focus entrainment; private/carousing rituals (e.g. historical tobacco) as EE technologies; modern introversion and the cult of the individual as products of particular IR chains, not escapes from the EE model.
- **[[situational-stratification]]:** E-power (emotional/situational dominance) vs D-power (deference/order-giving authority).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Circularity risk** if EE is only read off outcomes (initiative, solidarity) it predicts.
- **Measurement** remains multi-channel and program-level rather than a settled scale.
- **Mediated and solitary experience** strain a co-presence-first model; Collins allows internalized symbols and private rituals but privileges bodily assembly for full entrainment.
- **Unification charge:** folding markets, sex, thought, and personality into EE-seeking may over-unify distinct processes (parallel to critiques of Collins's 1975 conflict synthesis).

All framework uses of EE on this page remain **attributed** to Collins unless independent multi-study convergence is later documented.
