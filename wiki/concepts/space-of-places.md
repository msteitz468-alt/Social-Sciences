# Space of Places

Castells’s counterpart to the [[space-of-flows|space of flows]]: organization of experience and meaning in contiguous locales. In the [[network-society|network society]], he argues, dominant functions organize in the space of flows while people largely live in the space of places — producing a **structural schizophrenia** between the two spatial logics. See *[[castells-rise-of-network-society-1996|The Rise of the Network Society]]* Ch. 6 and 2010 preface.

## Definition as Coined

A place (attributed Castells): a locale whose form, function, and meaning are self-contained within the boundaries of physical contiguity. People still live in places; but because function and power are organized in the space of flows, experience related to places becomes abstracted from power, and meaning is increasingly separated from knowledge. Cultural and social meaning is defined in place terms; functionality, wealth, and power are defined in terms of flows. Unless cultural, political, and physical bridges are built between the two forms of space, society may head toward “parallel universes whose times cannot meet.”

**Temporal polarity:** timeless time belongs to the space of flows; time discipline, biological time, and socially determined sequencing characterize places — “flows induce timeless time, places are time-bounded.”

## Semantic History

Paired opposition within Castells’s spatial theory; not a free-standing school concept outside the network-society program. Cases (e.g. Belleville, Tokyo World City Fair / Aoshima resistance) illustrate local meaning vs global networking.

## Operationalizations

Neighborhoods; local identity; mega-city dualism (global connection / local disconnection); European elite rehabilitation of historic centers vs American Edge City pattern.

## Applications

[[network-society-theory]]; urban dualism and exclusion; identity politics of place (developed further in Vol. II — not ingested).

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary can understate hybrid places-in-flows; policy uptake of “place” vs “flows” sometimes loses Castells’s bridging prescription.
