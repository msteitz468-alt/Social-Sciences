# Instruments and containers

In [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013), technology is sorted into two major sets of **material metaphors** — **instruments** and **containers** — that give material form to emotions and bodily sensations through metaphorical substitution and feed the social practices of [[enchainment|FACE]] (attributed, ch. 1).

## Definition as Coined

- **Instruments:** the oldest artefacts from [[terrae|Terra 1]]; extensions of bodily action (after William H. Walker’s usage as cited by Gamble) (attributed).
- **Containers:** the other large set — wrapping, holding, housing, boat-hulls; serve **keeping** (storage, possession, secrets) and rise in importance during the third stride of Terra 2, dominating Terra 3 (attributed). The boat is the container necessary to reach Sahul and later island worlds (attributed).

Social technologies rely on making connections — bringing things and people into association — for practical effect and aesthetic affect (attributed).

## Semantic History

Pairs with FACE and [[kinshipping]] (container metaphor for kinship roles). Distinct from Clark’s Mode 1–5 manufacturing taxonomy (also used in the book as a separate axis).

## Operationalizations

Mode/technounit complexity can be scored within either set; presence of composite instruments and of container classes is used as a coarse Terra 2→3 signal (attributed).

## Applications

Frames the technology half of the brain–settlement–stuff disconnect; supports kinship-as-container argument for Terra 3 (attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary is analytical; many artefacts are both (e.g. hafted projectiles). Chronology of "first containers" is research-sensitive. Horizon: as of Gamble 2013.
