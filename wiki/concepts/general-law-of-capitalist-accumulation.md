# General Law of Capitalist Accumulation

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Chapter 25, [[marx-karl|Marx]] states an **absolute general law of capitalist accumulation**: the greater the social wealth, the functioning capital, the extent and energy of its growth, and therefore also the absolute mass of the proletariat and the productiveness of its labour, the greater is the industrial reserve army; the same causes that develop the expansive power of capital develop the labour-power at its disposal; the relative mass of the industrial reserve army increases with the potential energy of wealth; etc. In compressed form he asserts the **accumulation of misery** corresponding to the accumulation of capital — “be his payment high or low.”

**This is Marx’s theoretical position, not a wiki-voice finding.** Empirical illustrations (England 1846–66, agricultural proletariat, Ireland) are date-stamped documentation as he cites it.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| *Capital* I Ch. 25 | Law internal to accumulation; relative surplus population |
| Later reception | “Immiseration thesis” — often simplified or refuted by rising real wages |
| Revisionist / social-democratic | Contested by welfare capitalism evidence |

## Operationalizations

Mechanisms Marx chains:

1. Accumulation converts [[surplus-value]] into additional capital.
2. [[organic-composition-of-capital|Organic composition of capital]] tends to rise (c grows faster than v).
3. Relative demand for labour falls → [[industrial-reserve-army]].
4. Wage movements constrained by reserve-army dynamics.
5. Illustrations: British wealth vs pauperism/housing/nutrition stats; agricultural gangs; Irish depopulation with rising rents.

## Applications

- Organizes Part 7 of *Capital* I.
- Feeds political claims about polarization (affinity with *Manifesto* two-class thesis, with richer mechanism).
- [[class-struggle]] over Factory Acts and wages appears inside this structural frame.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Historical trajectories of real wages, welfare states, and middle strata challenge simple immiseration — record as later critique, not silence Marx’s claim.
- Law is stated under assumptions of capitalist production’s pure form; national differences matter (Ch. 22 on wages).
- Ch. 25 §5 is illustration, not a modern probability sample.
