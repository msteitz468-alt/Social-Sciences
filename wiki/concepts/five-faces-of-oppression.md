# Five Faces of Oppression

Young’s multi-criteria account of **oppression** in *[[young-justice-politics-difference-1990|Justice and the Politics of Difference]]* (Ch. 2) — the book’s most cited analytic device.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Young 1990, Ch. 2.)* Oppression is a **structural** condition of **social groups** (not mere aggregates or associations; groups are relational, often “thrown,” fluid and cross-cutting), not only individual tyranny. The **five faces**:

1. **Exploitation** — structural transfer of the results of labor of one group to benefit another (class, gender, racialized menial labor); not fixable by redistribution alone.  
2. **Marginalization** — expulsion from useful participation in social life (e.g. underclass, many elderly, severely disabled); produces dependency and non-distributive harms.  
3. **Powerlessness** — lack of authority, status, and sense of self especially among nonprofessional workers; contrasts with professional privileges of respectability and autonomy.  
4. **Cultural imperialism** — universalization of a dominant group’s experience as the norm; the Other is marked, stereotyped, rendered invisible as subject; double consciousness.  
5. **Violence** — systemic vulnerability of group members to random attacks and harassment as a social practice, not only isolated crimes.  

A group is oppressed if it suffers **one or more** of these faces. Complements but is not reducible to the [[distributive-paradigm]].

## Semantic History

Charter statement 1990; widely anthologized in feminist and social-justice teaching. Adjacent to later recognition theory ([[misrecognition]]) without identity.

## Operationalizations

Heuristic for diagnosing group oppression in policy and movements; not a single quantitative index.

## Applications

Social-movement claims; workplace hierarchy; cultural stereotype analysis; violence against women/minorities as structural.

## Critiques and Limitations

Overlap among faces; group-boundary disputes; risk of checklist use without structural analysis.
