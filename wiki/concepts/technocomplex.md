# Technocomplex

Highest **rank** (but often large **size**) in [[clarke-david-l|Clarke]]'s hierarchical model of archaeological entities (*[[clarke-analytical-archaeology-1978|Analytical Archaeology]]*): a group of cultures linked by shared families of artefact-types under common environmental, economic, and technological conditions — not by shared specific types as in a culture or culture group.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Clarke, Definitions and ch. 8, 1978 ed.)*

> "A group of cultures characterized by assemblages sharing a polythetic range but differing specific types of the same general families of artefact-types, shared as a widely diffused and interlinked response to common factors in environment, economy and technology."

Clarke added indicative affinity bands: negligible shared **specific types** (perhaps 5% or less) but residual medium affinity in **type families** (perhaps 30–60%). He stressed larger size but **lower rank** than culture or culture group — a gross artefact complex as vector of particular sociocultural, technological, economic, and environmental stances.

## Semantic History

Clarke coined the term because archaeologists were already using labels like Tardenoisian, Gravettian, Palaeo-Indian, Mousterian, Acheulean, and Chopper-chopping-tool complex at wildly different ranks, often calling technocomplex-scale packages "cultures." The term is a hygiene device: force rank declaration before comparison. It is **not** a revival of the three-age system or a pure subsistence taxonomy (hunter-gatherer / pastoral / agrarian categories may cut across technocomplexes).

## Operationalizations

- **Positive criteria:** shared type families; environmental/economic/technological co-response; wide diffusion; polythetic assemblage patterns.
- **Negative criteria:** not requiring genetic/collateral culture relatedness (unlike culture group); not requiring high specific-type identity.
- **Examples Clarke treats at this rank (attributed):** Acheulean and chopper-tool complexes as huge loose alignments; loose uses of Mousterian, Gravettian, Palaeo-Indian, Tardenoisian as often technocomplex-level technologies in similar contexts (sometimes subsuming true culture-status entities).

## Applications

- Correcting multilevel name confusion in Paleolithic and later prehistory.
- Linking [[polythetic-classification]] to adaptive/technological packages without equating them to peoples.
- Semantic "activity kit" common ground (Clarke's archaeological semantics) connecting technocomplex type complexes to ecological regularity — illustrated partly via Binfords' Mousterian factor analysis.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Affinity percentages are indicative, not universal cut-offs.
- Risk of reifying adaptive packages as quasi-cultures if rank discipline lapses.
- Sparse uptake relative to "culture"; many archaeologists still use industry/complex without Clarke's formal definition.
- Identity caution: technocomplex ≠ language family ≠ genetic population ≠ ethnic group.
