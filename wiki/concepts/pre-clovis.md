# Pre-Clovis

**Etic chronological/classificatory term** for human presence in the Americas **before** the Clovis archaeological horizon. Not a single culture, people, or migration. Central hinge of the [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]].

## Definition as Coined

“Pre-Clovis” names a **relative chronological claim** (older than Clovis), not a diagnostic toolkit. Candidates have ranged from robust ([[monte-verde]] MV-II) to rejected or unresolved (Calico, many early bone “tools,” extreme Pedra Furada readings). Meltzer 2009 notes the term is a **misnomer in South America** since Clovis fluted points, strictly speaking, reach only as far south as Panama.

## Semantic History

*(Attributed densification — [[meltzer-david]], *[[meltzer-first-peoples-2009]]* 2009.)*

- Under Clovis-first orthodoxy (Haynes 1960s: secure Clovis ¹⁴C in a narrow **11,500–11,000 BP** slice; corridor opening ~12,000 BP), pre-Clovis was hypothetical and held to a high bar.
- **Alex Krieger** (1953) warned against a new 10,000-yr “allowed antiquity” dogma; by 1964 tallied **fifty** N/S American sites he thought predated Clovis. **MacNeish 1976:** 35 sites (almost completely different list); **Morlan 1988:** 5. Critics read ~90% attrition as reason for skepticism; Meltzer: failure of specific sites ≠ proof humans absent pre-Clovis—“Whether the first Americans came… before Clovis… and whether we have detected their traces are independent issues.”
- **“Pre-Clovis credibility decay curve”:** purported ancient sites burst with fanfare then collapse as artifacts prove natural, dating experimental/unreliable, or deposits mixed—dozens/scores failed scrutiny, breeding cynicism.
- **Higher bar than African deep antiquity:** ready acceptance of Senga (Zaire) ~2.3 Ma tools vs noisy debate over Monte Verde (~12,500 BP) and Meadowcroft (~14,250 BP)—American antiquity politics and skepticism culture (Meltzer contrast).
- **As of ~book era (dozen years before 2009):** pre-Clovis shifted from hypothesis to **accepted reality** after Monte Verde 1997—“it's a whole new archaeological world as a result.” Individual early sites still require case-by-case evaluation.

## Operationalizations

### Three criteria (Hrdlička 1907 / Folsom 1927 lineage, as Meltzer states)

1. **Undeniable human traces** (artifacts or skeletal remains).
2. **Undisturbed geological deposits** in proper stratigraphic position.
3. **Indisputable radiometric ages** (speared bison no longer suffice as the sole clock).

Not different from Africa’s earliest-culture standards, yet extraordinarily hard to meet in the Americas. **Jackie Robinson Rule:** a pre-Clovis site must be utterly unimpeachable—convince skeptics with room to spare. Criteria not unfair: Clovis long met them; Folsom succeeded under less refined methods; many pre-Clovis failures reflect site problems, not criteria rigidity.

**Expected toolkit:** Americas should yield relatively complex tools/features of anatomically modern *Homo sapiens* of the last ~100 ka—not Senga-like earliest-culture starters; “primitive-looking” recent tools can reflect poor stone supply. Pre-Clovis need not look like Clovis.

**Methods:** stratigraphy + [[radiocarbon-dating]] (preferred; non-radiocarbon methods tried with mixed results—Hueyatlaco U-series/fission-track vs ¹⁴C conflict as cautionary tale). Steno’s law with real-world mixing (burrows, shrink-swell, redeposition) keeps Holmes/Hrdlička-style caution partially appropriate.

## Applications

| Site / claim class | Status in Meltzer 2009 weighing |
|---|---|
| [[monte-verde]] MV-II ~12,500 ¹⁴C BP | Accepted after 1997 visit; 2008 seaweed consolidates |
| [[meadowcroft]] ~14,250 ¹⁴C BP (Adovasio conservative) | Serious candidate; coal-contamination debate; limbo without final report |
| Pedra Furada older phases | Nature likely produced “artifacts” (cliff-fall geofacts); TL failed to confirm human burning |
| Taima-Taima | Association/age unresolved; limbo |
| Big Eddy, Cactus Hill, Saltville, Chesrow, Paisley, Pendejo, Topper, etc. (~20 contenders) | Newly reported/under analysis; **none fully accepted** by N American community as of 2009 |
| Calico / Leakey | Failed scrutiny (credibility decay) |

**Implications Meltzer stresses:** even a few millennia of pre-Clovis change understanding of HG colonization of empty continents, population history, genetic/linguistic clocks, and post-1492 tragedy calibration—not mere turf war. Pre-Clovis presence challenges strict Martin overkill timing but does not alone refute all overkill variants (Martin: Clovis is first unmistakable projectile-point hunting of now-extinct large mammals). Pre-Clovis complexes of South and North America show **no obvious historical/technological links** (as of 2009); MV toolkit very different from Clovis—two traditions/pulses or outgrowths of one colonization, call either way until gaps fill.

**Genetics (as of Meltzer 2009 only):** Torroni-style “genetics confirms pre-Clovis” overstated—haplogroups cannot be directly dated; archaeology gives minimum ages, molecular clocks maximum ages; arrival window after ~40 ka but before 12,500 (MVII) if then-current estimates hold; later mtDNA/NRY clusters ~18–14 ka could narrow the window. Use `associated_with`, not identity, across genes/cultures/languages/peoples.

## Critiques and Limitations

- The label collapses very different assemblages and ages; accepting *some* pre-Clovis does not validate *all* early claims.
- “Pre-Clovis ≠ Bigfoot”: existence not required by theory alone—evidence required (Meltzer).
- Media distorts controversy dynamics; legitimate multi-reading of evidence under-explained (Meltzer *caveat lector* as participant).
- One site can establish antiquity (Folsom; Yana analogy)—but post-Monte Verde confirmation boom was slower than post-Folsom because early sites are rare, subtle, and drowned on coasts.
- Four-way non-identity throughout.
