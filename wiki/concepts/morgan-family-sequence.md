# Morgan's Family Sequence (Five Forms)

## Definition as Coined

[[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]] (attributed, *[[morgan-ancient-society-1877|Ancient Society]]*, Part III) argues that "five different and successive forms" of the family can be distinguished, "each having an institution of marriage peculiar to itself." He states plainly that the sequence "is, in part, hypothetical … Its complete establishment must be left to the results of future ethnological investigations." The forms, in order:

1. **Consanguine family** — the intermarriage of brothers and sisters, own and collateral, in a group. Not observed (extinct by Morgan's account) but inferred from the **Malayan** system of consanguinity (Hawaiian terms), treated as its fossil. Morgan posits a still-earlier state of promiscuity but flags it as beyond positive knowledge (and notes Darwin's doubt).
2. **Punaluan family** — groups of sisters married in common to each other's husbands, or brothers to each other's wives ([[group-marriage]]). Core evidence: the Hawaiian custom of **[[punalua]]** (via missionary informants Andrews, Bishop, Bingham); corroborated by Caesar on the Britons and a North American "sororate" survival. Morgan locates the germ of the [[gens]] in the punaluan group's female line.
3. **Syndyasmian (pairing) family** — marriage between single pairs without exclusive cohabitation, dissolvable at will. Exemplified (per Morgan) by contemporary Iroquois and, at the Middle Status, Aztec/Maya practice.
4. **Patriarchal family** — one man with several wives; Morgan downplays polygamy and locates the form's real feature in **paternal power** (Hebrew and Roman *patria potestas*). This is his explicit rebuttal of [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]]'s thesis that the patriarchal family was the *original* human family — which, Morgan says, "leaves at least four entire ethnical periods untouched."
5. **Monogamian family** — single pairs with exclusive cohabitation, guaranteeing paternity. "The monogamian family owes its origin to property." Evidenced via Tacitus on the Germans, the Homeric Greeks (a "low type" — wife seclusion without reciprocal male chastity), and Roman marriage law.

## Semantic History

The sequence rests on the **survival/fossil** method: because "systems of consanguinity are neither adopted, modified, nor laid aside at pleasure," a classificatory terminology outlives the marriage practice that produced it and can be read as evidence for an extinct family form — "the form of the family advances faster … than systems of consanguinity, which follow to record the family relationships." The two ultimate terminological types are [[classificatory-system-of-relationship|classificatory]] (Malayan, Turanian/Ganowánian — produced, Morgan argues, by "plural marriages in the group") and [[descriptive-system-of-relationship|descriptive]] (Aryan/Semitic/Uralian — produced by single marriage). The scheme extends and systematizes the argument of *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity]]* (1871).

## Operationalizations

Each form is diagnosed by the marriage institution it rests on and by the kinship terminology it is claimed to have produced: the consanguine family by the Malayan (generational) system; the punaluan by the Turanian/Ganowánian classificatory system and the surviving Hawaiian punalua term; the syndyasmian by dissolvable pairing (Iroquois/Aztec practice); the monogamian by the Aryan descriptive system and guaranteed paternity. The operative rule is the **fossil/lag** principle: read a present-day terminology as evidence for the (earlier) marriage form that generated it, since terminology changes more slowly than practice. See [[classificatory-system-of-relationship]].

## Applications

The sequence links Morgan's kinship data to his account of [[mother-right]] (female-line descent as archaic, the paternity of children being uncertain under group marriage) and to his property argument (property drove the shift to male descent and the monogamian family, lowering women's status — the point [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] developed into the "world-historic defeat of the female sex"). See [[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884]].

## Critiques and Limitations

`contested: yes` — this is the paradigm case of discredited unilineal kinship evolution. Twentieth-century anthropology rejected universal primitive promiscuity, the consanguine and punaluan stages, and group marriage as a universal phase, while retaining the insight that kinship terminologies encode historical information (see [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]). The evidentiary chain is uneven: the punaluan stage rests on morally loaded, secondhand missionary testimony about Hawaii, and much of the sequence is Morgan's own acknowledged conjecture. Every stage is [ATTRIBUTED] to Morgan; none is wiki-voice fact. Morgan's founding dispute with J. F. McLennan over the origin of classificatory kinship is at [[morgan-mclennan-debate]].
