# The New Racism

**The new racism** is [[bonilla-silva-eduardo|Eduardo Bonilla-Silva]]'s term for the post–civil rights racial *structure* of the United States — the set of "subtle, institutional, and apparently nonracial" practices that reproduce white privilege after the collapse of Jim Crow's overt racial order (attributed). It is the structural counterpart to the ideology of [[colorblind-racism|color-blind racism]]: the new racism is *how* racial inequality is reproduced; color-blind racism is *how it is explained away*.

## Definition as Coined

In *[[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-2018|Racism without Racists]]*, Bonilla-Silva characterizes the new racism by five elements (attributed): (1) increasingly covert racial discourse and practices; (2) avoidance of overt racial terminology paired with growing white claims of "reverse racism"; (3) a racial political agenda that eschews direct racial reference; (4) the invisibility of most mechanisms that reproduce racial inequality; and (5) the rearticulation of some Jim Crow–era practices. He argues minorities "should fear less the angry men with white hoods… than the men with suits and their 'smiling discrimination'" (attributed).

## Semantic History

Bonilla-Silva notes the term "new racism" and the "declining significance of race" framing were made prominent in the late 1970s by William Julius Wilson — a historical/bibliographic point, not an endorsement of Wilson's class-over-race thesis, which Bonilla-Silva contests structurally (attributed). He treats the new racism as dominant but not total: overt Jim Crow–style racism persists cyclically, and he reads the Trump moment as a resurgence analogous to the Reagan era rather than a displacement of the new-racism regime (attributed).

## Operationalization and Mechanisms (attributed, per Bonilla-Silva)

- **Housing** — not showing units, racial steering, quoting higher rents/prices to minorities (see [[residential-segregation]]).
- **Employment** — "smiling face" discrimination, informal white-network hiring, steering skilled minorities into dead-end jobs.
- **Politics** — racial gerrymandering, at-large elections, runoff and anti-single-shot devices operating as disenfranchisement.
- **Social control** — mass incarceration, policing, and capital-punishment disparities.

Bonilla-Silva presents supporting statistics (audit studies, segregation indices, incarceration and wealth gaps) as attributed evidence, not independently established wiki-voice facts.

## Applications

Anchors his account of why severe racial inequality persists in a nominally color-blind society, and — projected forward — of the [[latin-americanization-of-race|Latin Americanization]] of the U.S. racial order.

## Critiques and Limitations

As with his broader [[racialized-social-system|structural theory]], critics ask whether "structure" is specified sharply enough to distinguish new-racism mechanisms from non-racial market and institutional processes (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]).
