# Standardization (Culture Industry)

## Definition as Coined

**Standardization** is [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]] and
[[horkheimer-max|Horkheimer]]'s term, paired with
[[pseudo-individualization]], for the interchangeable, formula-governed structure
of [[culture-industry]] products beneath their surface variety. In
*[[horkheimer-adorno-dialectic-of-enlightenment-1947|Dialectic of Enlightenment]]*
(attributed): "under monopoly all mass culture is identical"; the industry achieves
"the standardization and mass production" of culture, "sacrificing whatever involved
a distinction between the logic of the work and that of the social system." Details
are "ready-made cliches to be slotted in anywhere"; "as soon as the film begins, it
is quite clear how it will end." The graded differences between products serve
"classifying, organizing, and labeling consumers" rather than any real variety.

## Semantic History

The concept comes from Adorno's popular-music research ("On Popular Music," 1941),
where **standardization** (the fungible harmonic and structural template of the hit
song) is the counterpart of the surface novelties of [[pseudo-individualization]].
*Dialectic of Enlightenment* extends it across film, radio, and magazines. In media
economics the descriptive core (format standardization under industrial production)
was widely adopted even where Adorno's aesthetic verdict was rejected.

## Operationalizations

None philosophically; Adorno's music sociology attempted structural analysis of
song forms. Illustrative, not an independent finding.

## Applications

Standardization and [[pseudo-individualization]] are the paired mechanisms of the
[[culture-industry]]; together they specify [[reification]] and
[[commodity-fetishism]] in the sphere of mass culture and prefigure debates over
format, genre, and algorithmic "personalization."

## Critiques and Limitations

Recorded in attributed voice: critics argue the thesis understates genre variation,
audience appropriation, and the aesthetic achievements of popular forms. Defenders
note its purchase on formula-driven, data-optimized content industries.
