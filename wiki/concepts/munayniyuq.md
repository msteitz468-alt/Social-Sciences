# Munayniyuq (Owner of the Will)

## Definition as Coined

**Munayniyuq** (Quechua: roughly “one who has/owns *munay*,” the will that shapes others’ lives) is the title concept of Story 7 in [[de-la-cadena-marisol|Marisol de la Cadena]]’s *[[de-la-cadena-earth-beings-2015|Earth Beings]]* (2015). In Turpo conversations (Pacchanta, 2003), **Apu Ausangate** is the supreme munayniyuq among [[earth-beings|earth-beings]] — orders other Apus, can prevent hail and make potatoes grow through *despachos*. The **hacendado** is also munayniyuq: “He who makes us do what he likes” — arbitrary punishment, forced labor, confiscation, sexual violence (attributed narrative). Munayniyuq thus names a **meeting of ontologically different wills** across lettered power and tirakuna (attributed — de la Cadena 2015).

## Semantic History

John Earls (1969) already linked mistis/Wamanis as munayniyuq in Andeanist literature (as cited by de la Cadena). De la Cadena expands: munayniyuq **overlaps and exceeds** *gamonalismo* (local boss-rule); residual munay continues after land reform in bureaucratic wait, literacy barriers, and extractive state practice. Related Spanish/Andeanist frames (gamonal, patrón) do not exhaust the earth-being side of the term.

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic: who can order lives without reciprocal negotiation; who is paid with *despachos* vs bribes/sheep gifts; ronda campesina as collective practice that can contest present munayniyuq (state, thieves, local officials) without being simple liberal representation.

## Applications

- Hacienda Lauramarca peonage and Mariano’s land struggle.
- Post-reform biopolitics of abandonment and mining threat to Ausangate/Sinakara.
- Contrast with in-ayllu leadership (personero speaks *from* ayllu; ronda chief as anti-munayniyuq knot, e.g. Julián Rojo forced return — attributed cases).

## Critiques and Limitations

Single primary source in vault; risk of reifying “will” as metaphysics. De la Cadena ties the term to concrete violence and institutions, not pure cosmology. Gendered dimensions noted in endnotes (not fully expanded in main text).
