# Sodality

## Definition as Coined

In [[service-elman-r|Service]]'s *[[service-primitive-social-organization-1971|Primitive Social Organization]]*, a **sodality** is a social group distinguished from a **residential group** by the absence of coresidence as the defining basis of membership. Service’s working gloss (2nd-ed preface): “a non-residential association with some corporate functions.” He needed the term against the cumbersome alternative and **refused to yield** when critics denied his definition because [[lowie-robert-h|Robert Lowie]] had defined sodality differently (Lowie’s sense closer to voluntary association; Service calls Lowie’s definition equally arbitrary and less useful for his evolutionary problem).

**Pan-tribal sodalities** are the specifically tribal additions to band-like means of solidarity: they integrate multiple residential communities into a larger society. Service: “To ask what these are is to ask what a tribe is.” Most usual: **clans**, then age-grade associations, [[secret-society|secret societies]], and special-purpose sodalities (curing, warfare, ceremony). Sodalities can exist in band society under special circumstances but are few, limited in scope, and not pan-tribal.

## Semantic History

- Older anthropological “sodality” / “association” (Lowie *Primitive Society* and others).
- Service elevates the residential/non-residential contrast and makes **pan-tribal** scope the evolutionary marker of the tribal level ([[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]).
- At the **chiefdom** level, Service argues sodalities decline in relative importance as hierarchical residential organization, rank, and redistribution take over integrative work.
- Related: unilineal **clans** as nonlocal kinship sodalities requiring name and putative common descent (vs local [[patrilocal-band]] membership).

## Operationalizations

| Level | Sodality role (Service, attributed) |
|---|---|
| Band | Limited; inverse to residential-group strength (e.g. Central Australian formal sodalities where residence is most scattered) |
| Tribe | Pan-tribal sodalities *define* the level; kinship (clans) and non-kin (age-grades, societies) types |
| Chiefdom | Relative decline; integration via permanent central redistribution and rank |

Service also treats sodalities as having **latent political integrating functions** even when overt purposes are ceremonial or special-interest. Structure–function relations differ for sodalities vs residential groups (residential groups more demographically/habitat-constrained).

## Applications

- Diagnosing tribal vs band organization in Service’s model.
- Comparative study of clans, age-sets, and secret societies as political-integrative devices without permanent chiefship.
- Critique of pure habitat ecology: sodalities mediate between geography-demography and the rest of organization.

## Critiques and Limitations

Definitional fight with Lowie tradition; “pan-tribal” assumes a coherent tribe; many ethnographically labeled tribes are colonial units; not all larger non-state societies fit the pan-tribal-sodality template; Freudian/voluntary-association senses of sodality persist in other literatures.
