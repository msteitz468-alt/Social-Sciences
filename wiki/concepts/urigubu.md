# Urigubu

## Definition as Coined

**Urigubu** is a Trobriand (Kiriwinian) term for a major class of **harvest gift / presentation**, reported by [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] for fieldwork **1915–18**. In *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]* it figures in the political economy of chieftainship: polygamous wives brought harvest tribute that financed redistribution. In *[[malinowski-coral-gardens-1935|Coral Gardens]]* **Vol. I Ch. VI** it is stated as **customary law**: a man presents a large share of garden produce (often framed as about half) to his **sister’s husband**, conveyed by mass carriers for impressiveness and festivity after garden display. In **Vol. II** it is densified in the crop and legal-economic lexicon (selected *taytu* for *urigubu*; *sawili* carriers’ cry; North/South semantic shift).

## Semantic History

- **Argonauts 1922:** political-economic function — harvest presentations underwriting ranked chieftainship; part of the gift continuum (customary payments).
- **Coral Gardens 1935 Vol. I:** full **customary-law** treatment — who owes whom; *gubakayeki* personal plots vs obligation plots; polygamy multiplies chiefly inflow; companion gifts (*kovisi*, *taytupeta*, *likula bwayma*, *youlo*, *takola*); Document III corrects early field errors that treated *urigubu* as simple community "tribute."
- **Coral Gardens 1935 Vol. II:** terminological precision — selected *taytu*, harvest-gift set, North/South meaning drift.
- **Weiner 1976 (restudy, Kiriwina 1971–72):** [[weiner-annette-b|Annette Weiner]] reinterprets the yams a man receives through his wife as **reciprocal, not tribute** (contra Malinowski 1929's "permanent tributary obligation" and Powell). On her reading the husband is obligated to *reciprocate* by helping his wife accumulate [[womens-wealth|women's wealth]], and wife-givers monitor and enforce the return; the wife is "titular owner" of the yams. She also narrows the emic term itself — reporting *urigubu* proper covers pork, coconut/areca, and betel pepper (or substitutes for a yam garden), not the exchange-garden yams — and treats rank-linked presentations (*bopokala*, *guyapokala*) as carrying "positive exchange feedback for the original donors." Recorded as a position on [[weiner-malinowski-trobriand-debate]]; see [[weiner-women-of-value-study]].

## Operationalizations

Identified by native term in harvest and kinship contexts; not reducible to "tax," "rent," or undifferentiated "tribute" without losing emic distinctions. Basketfuls of *taytu* are the practical value unit (Documents give heap and *kayasa* basket totals). Display, comparison, and kinship shame/renown enforce compliance ([[malinowski-coral-gardens-study]]).

## Applications

- [[trobriand-islanders]] social organization and economy (1915–18 documentation).
- [[gift-exchange]] continuum beside [[kula]] and [[gimwali]].
- Chiefly finance and storehouse (*bwayma*) politics in [[malinowski-coral-gardens-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Single classical ethnographer's map; regional variation flagged by Malinowski himself; gender and valuation revised by Weiner's 1971–72 restudy (tribute→reciprocity; see above and [[weiner-malinowski-trobriand-debate]]). Do not equate *urigubu* with Kula *vaygu'a* — different circuits and objects.
