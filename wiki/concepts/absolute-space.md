# Absolute Space

In Lefebvre’s *[[lefebvre-production-of-space-1991|The Production of Space]]*, **absolute space** is a historically produced form of social space — **not** simply Newtonian absolute space as empty physical container (though he engages that philosophical tradition as ideology of emptiness).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Lefebvre.)*

> “Absolute space was made up of fragments of nature located at sites which were chosen for their intrinsic qualities (cave, mountaintop, spring, river), but whose very consecration ended up by stripping them of their natural characteristics and uniqueness. … Absolute space, religious and political in character, was a product of the bonds of consanguinity, soil and language, but out of it evolved a space which was relativized and historical.”

Producers of space (peasants, artisans) are not the managers/owners (priests, warriors, scribes, princes). Absolute space survives as bedrock of historical space and basis of representational spaces; internal dialectic of full and empty (e.g. cathedral nave / political nucleus). Architectonics chapters develop body, gesture, marking, and the organic/biomorphic persistence of absolute residues within relative space.

## Semantic History

Reworks philosophical “absolute space” into a **historical-social** category within [[production-of-space]]. Distinct from Descartes/Newton *res extensa* as pure empty container.

## Operationalizations

Historical-interpretive (sacred geography, agrarian landscapes, early political centres). Not a survey construct.

## Applications

Ch. 1 periodization; Ch. 3–4 passage from absolute toward historical and [[abstract-space|abstract]] space (Greek Logos–Cosmos vs Roman Law/property illustrations as theory-laden illustrations).

## Critiques and Limitations

Can read as stadial; specialist historical and archaeological literatures may contest particular illustrations.
