# Differentiation and Integration

## Definition as Coined

For Spencer, **evolution** is the trend toward increasing **differentiation** (specialization of functions) coupled with **integration** (interdependence of structurally differentiated parts and coordination of their functions). Andreski (1969) treats this pair as the master key of the *Principles of Sociology*. proposed_by: [[spencer-herbert]] · part_of: [[social-evolutionism]]

In social application: from relatively homogeneous simple groups toward heterogeneous structures (classes, organs, offices, industries) whose parts depend on one another more tightly (“consensus of functions” tightens as evolution advances).

## Semantic History

Andreski traces the idea’s economic root to Adam Smith on the division of labour, generalized by Spencer beyond economics to a cosmic and social law. Related but not identical: Comtean [[social-dynamics]] (progress of intellectual stages); **[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]'s division of labour** (*[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|The Division of Labor]]*, 1893), which re-registers the same structural pair as a **moral** fact — differentiation of function produces **[[organic-solidarity]]** (interdependence) rather than dissolving cohesion, driven by rising **[[dynamic-density]]** — while explicitly *rejecting* Spencer's contractual-utilitarian reading of what interdependence entails (see [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]); and later “differentiation” in modernization and systems theory. The pair can be used descriptively without Spencer’s universal evolutionism; that detachment is common after the evolutionary school’s decline.

## Operationalizations

Spencer operationalizes via comparative sequences:

- **Structural:** simple → specialized organs; sorcerer-priest-doctor → separate professions; undivided political-industrial rule → separated spheres.
- **Compositional:** simple → compound → doubly/trebly compound societies ([[social-compounding]]).
- **Industrial:** sex and skill division of labour; household → guild → factory; communal/gild regulation → freer contract (*Principles* Part IV).
- **Political/ecclesiastical:** headship, ministries, hierarchies as graded differentiation of the regulating system.

## Applications

Organizes the entire *Principles*: Part I states the formula in organic terms; Parts II–IV apply it to political, ecclesiastical, and industrial institutions. Militant activity differentiates a fighting/regulative part from an operative/commissariat part; industrial activity multiplies specialized productive and distributive organs.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Universal cosmic claim is outside social-science evidence.
- “Advance” language smuggles progress; Spencer also allowed regression and metamorphosis under war.
- Integration can mean coordination **or** centralization — the militant type’s tight binding is not the industrial type’s free interdependence; conflating them is a standing misreading risk.
- Not to be equated silently with Comte’s [[law-of-three-stages]] or with biological speciation.
