# Sociology of Associations

Latour’s name in *[[latour-reassembling-the-social-2005|Reassembling the Social]]* for ANT’s general social theory: the opposite of a **sociology of the social** that uses “society” or “the social” as an unexplained resource.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Latour 2005.)* **Sociology of the social** treats the social as a special domain or glue that explains politics, science, law, etc. **Sociology of associations** treats the social as what is produced when associations are made — to be traced, not assumed. The task is to *reassemble* the social by following actors’ own world-building.

## Semantic History

Programmatic binary structuring the whole book (Introduction; Conclusion: from society to collective).

## Operationalizations

Five uncertainties + three moves (localize global; redistribute local; connect sites); keep the social flat.

## Applications

Charter framing for [[actor-network-theory]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics of ANT often reject the binary as a straw “critical sociology”; Latour’s foil is partly Bourdieu-style critical sociology as he reconstructs it.
