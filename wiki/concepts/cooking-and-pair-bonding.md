# Cooking and Pair-Bonding

*(Attributed — Wrangham 2009, Chs. 6–7.)* Social half of the [[cooking-hypothesis]]: cooking restructures time, creates a monopolizable evening meal, and underwrites pair-bonds and a gendered [[household-division-of-labor|household division of labor]] that Wrangham links to patriarchy as well as mutual provisioning.

## Definition as Coined

Two linked theses:

1. **Time / division of labor.** Soft cooked food frees ~four hours of chewing daily versus a great-ape-like raw diet. That free time makes a hunting–gathering [[sexual-division-of-labor|sexual division of labor]] workable: women process staples; men can hunt long hours because a cooked meal waits. On raw food, Wrangham argues, the classic Washburn household bargain fails — unsuccessful hunters would lack evening chewing time for plant calories.
2. **Pair-bond as meal protection.** Cooking ends individual self-sufficiency (Perlès, Goudsblom, Symons as he cites them). Cooked food is social, camp-centered, and stealable; pair-bonds function as a "protection racket" / special friendship that secures a woman's food against bullying and secures a man's cooked meal. Economics (feeding) primary, sex secondary.

## Semantic History

Develops Brillat-Savarin's cooking→hunting→women cook sketch and challenges the meat-only Washburn & Lancaster family origin story. Mobilizes Charlotte Perkins Gilman (*Women and Economics*) on household labor enabling male wealth without female independence. Distinct from Morgan/Engels [[pairing-family]] (Victorian stage label) and from evolutionary-psychology pair-bond literature not ingested here — same English words, different discourse.

## Operationalizations

*(As Wrangham reports cross-cultural and ethnographic secondaries.)*

- Murdock & Provost (1973): women predominantly/almost exclusively cook in **97.8%** of 185 societies; cooking the most female-biased of 50 activities. Apparent exceptions = community/men's-house cooking by men vs family cooking still by women (Samoa, Marquesas, Chuuk).
- Even highly egalitarian Vanatinai (Lepowsky): women do all domestic cooking.
- Collier & Rosaldo: universal wife cooking obligation (as Wrangham cites).
- Calorie shares in nine forager groups: women 16–57% (avg ~1/3); he stresses mutual criticality over averages.
- Comparative: nonhuman primate adults rarely share food; human households are "little economies."

Sensitive illustrations (Inuit cooking under threat; Tiwi polygyny and wife-beating; wife theft for domestic labor; Mbuti bachelor theft) are **Wrangham's secondary use** — attributed program evidence, not wiki-voice ethnography of those societies.

## Applications

- Alternative to [[hunting-hypothesis]] origin of the family.
- Densifies [[sexual-division-of-labor]] and [[marriage]] with an evolutionary-cooking mechanism.
- Feeds [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]] as a third pole: not just who hunted/gathered, but whether **cooking** is the missing condition for either story.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Grand narrative from secondary ethnography risk overgeneralizing "patriarchy" and wife-cooking as evolutionary destiny.
- Feminist and cultural-anthropology readers may reject biological underwriting of domestic inequality even while accepting cooking's dietary importance.
- Cooperative-breeding, grandmothering, and multi-male food-sharing models (not developed in this book) compete as social-evolutionary packages.
- Keep every causal claim attributed; do not promote to wiki voice.
