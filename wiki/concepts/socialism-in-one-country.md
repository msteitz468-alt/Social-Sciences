# Socialism in One Country

## Definition as Coined

**Socialism in one country** is the mid-1920s Soviet doctrinal formula — associated above all with [[stalin-joseph|Stalin]] (December 1924) and supported by Bukharin — holding that the USSR could construct a complete socialist society on its own territory without waiting for proletarian revolution in advanced Europe. It competed with the internationalist insistence (Trotskyist and earlier Bolshevik common sense) that isolated backward Russia could not finish the socialist project alone (see [[permanent-revolution]]).

Documented here primarily from [[carr-edward-hallett|Carr]] 1979 (*[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979]]*), with cross-links to Service biographies and Trotsky materials already on the wiki.

## Semantic History

- **December 1924 (Stalin):** Carr reports Stalin's formulation and stresses its **psychological and national** impact — a declaration that the revolution's fate was not hostage to western proletariats that had failed to rise.
- **Attribution fight:** Carr calls the later attempt to root the doctrine in [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] **spurious** — an example of Stalin grounding authority in the master rather than accurate Lenin philology (Carr's thesis).
- **Opposition indictment:** The united opposition's "declaration of the 83" (May 1927, mainly Trotsky-drafted, per Carr) charged leaders with substituting a "petty-bourgeois theory of socialism in one country" for Marxist analysis of the proletarian dictatorship in the USSR.
- **Comintern:** Fifth-congress "Bolshevization" drew reinforcement from the doctrine: the USSR as unique exemplar of successful socialist revolution. After it became official ideology, Carr argues, international revolution was reconceived as directed "from above," with **defence of the USSR** as overriding interest.
- **Trotsky 1928:** From Alma-Ata exile, Trotsky's critique of the Comintern draft programme attacked socialism in one country as responsible for Comintern policy disasters (Carr).
- **Carr's structural reading:** The doctrine "perfectly fitted" Stalin and "pointed back to an old Russian national exclusiveness, rejected by Marx as well as by Lenin." Its **limitations** were "plainly revealed" by the absence of a trained proletariat growing up within bourgeois society — least of all in backward Russia. Practically it underwrote catch-up industrialization and means-of-production priority ([[five-year-plan]]).

## Applications

- Banner of the majority against the [[left-opposition]] and residual internationalism.
- Organizing ideology for [[comintern]] "Bolshevization" and later third-period tactics.
- Structural pair with [[permanent-revolution]] on [[russian-question-debate|the Russian Question]] lineage (nature of the USSR).
- Frame for Carr's "revolution from above" judgment on industrialization and [[collectivization]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Internal (Trotsky/opposition):** Petty-bourgeois nationalism; abandons permanent/international revolution; responsible for China and western Comintern failures (positions as reported by Carr).
- **Carr:** National exclusiveness vs Marx/Lenin; spurious Lenin attribution; hybrid-revolution dilemma only deferred, not solved.
- **Wiki caution:** Do not state "Lenin held socialism in one country" in wiki voice; Carr explicitly rejects that attribution. Earlier Lenin positions on NEP, state capitalism, and world revolution remain on [[lenin-vladimir]] and primary-source pages.
