# Speciation Phase

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]], *[[renfrew-prehistory-making-human-mind-2007|Prehistory: The Making of the Human Mind]]* 2007.)* The **speciation phase** is Renfrew's label for the long evolutionary stretch in which ancestral hominin species develop the **biological and early cultural capacities** of modern humans — tool skill, language capacity, self-awareness — culminating in *Homo sapiens* and the so-called human revolution / out-of-Africa dispersals. In this phase, **genetic change still bears on cognitive capacity**.

## Semantic History

Pairs with Renfrew's complementary [[tectonic-phase]] (post-dispersal cultural diversification largely without major genetic cognitive upgrades). Aligns with the first of two cognitive-archaeology subfields Renfrew names in 2007: development of pre-sapient cognitive capacities. Related but not identical to literature on [[behavioral-modernity]] and the Upper Palaeolithic "human revolution."

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Anchors: fossil/genetic human origins narrative **as of Renfrew 2007** (pre-aDNA scale); archaeological record of early tools, fire, possible language precursors. Date-stamp genetic claims.

## Applications

Organizes Part II of *Prehistory* (2007) and the Prospect research agenda (model group dynamics; document physical evolution and cultural adaptations; DNA reaching before out-of-Africa). Cross-links: [[cognitive-archaeology]], [[sapient-paradox]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary speciation/tectonic split may over-smooth African MSA gradualism and Neanderthal capacities; genetics **as of Renfrew 2007** is superseded for many demography claims (aDNA post-2010, as of later literature). Keep attributed.
