# Ethnoscape

The first and most widely borrowed of [[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]'s five
[[global-cultural-flows|-scapes]]. All claims attributed to Appadurai.

## Definition as Coined

"By ethnoscape, I mean the landscape of persons who constitute the shifting
world in which we live: tourists, immigrants, refugees, exiles, guest workers,
and other moving groups and individuals" *(Appadurai 1996)*. He does not deny
that relatively stable communities and kinship networks persist, "but it is to
say that the warp of these stabilities is everywhere shot through with the woof
of human motion." The term is **deliberately ambiguous**: it names both the
brute demographic fact of mass movement and the perspectival dilemma this poses
for ethnography — "the *ethno* in ethnography takes on a slippery, nonlocalized
quality."

## Semantic History

Coined against the anthropological trope of the bounded, territorialized,
"historically unselfconscious" community. "The landscapes of group identity — the
ethnoscapes — around the world are no longer familiar anthropological objects,
insofar as groups are no longer tightly territorialized, spatially bounded,
historically unselfconscious, or culturally homogeneous" *(Appadurai)*. In
Ch. 3 ("Global Ethnoscapes: Notes and Queries for a Transnational Anthropology")
Appadurai proposes substituting "ethnoscape" for older wholes such as "villages,
communities, and localities." The term has since circulated widely in migration,
diaspora, and media studies, often detached from the disjuncture thesis.

## Operationalizations

Appadurai calls for a **"cosmopolitan ethnography" or "macroethnography"** that
studies moving populations without presupposing the authority of the Western
experience. He revises [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s [[habitus]]: improvisation
"no longer occurs within a relatively bounded set of thinkable postures but is
always skidding and taking off, powered by the imagined vistas of mass-mediated
master narratives"; where once improvisation was snatched from the "glacial
undertow of habitus," now "habitus has to be painstakingly reinforced in the
face of life-worlds that are frequently in flux."

## Applications

Deployed via vignettes — the Meenakshi Temple across Madurai and Houston, Mira
Nair's *India Cabaret*, Sri Lankan refugees in South India and Switzerland. Feeds
the method program of [[global-cultural-flows]] and the analysis of
[[deterritorialization]]. Appadurai pairs it with the **history/genealogy**
distinction: history "leads you outward" to larger interactions, genealogy
"leads you inward" to embedded dispositions.

## Critiques and Limitations

Evocative but loosely operationalized; critics note it resists measurement and
can become a descriptive relabeling of "migration." Like the other scapes, it is
strong on flux and weak on the durable structures (states, capital, law) that
channel movement.
