# Proletarian Internationalism

## Definition as Coined

In Marcel van der Linden's long-view essay (in *[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|MWS in the Longue Durée]]*, 2004), **proletarian internationalism** is restricted to solidarity activities **from below** (not state or party-diplomatic "internationalism" alone), treated as **multiform** rather than a single organizational lineage (e.g. Internationals only).

## Semantic History

From nineteenth-century labor and socialist practice through national trade-union internationals to post-1960s transition. Distinct from liberal internationalism and from purely party-state socialist internationalism.

## Operationalizations

Van der Linden (attributed) offers:

- **Seven economic motives** for cross-border solidarity (plus political motives and ambivalence — national means often first).
- **Five stages:** (1) pre-1848 self-demarcation; (2) 1848–1870s subnational internationalism (London-centered); (3) 1870s–1890s transition; (4) 1890s–1960s national internationalism; (5) since 1960s second transition undermining national forms; speculative **transnational internationalism** as next stage.
- Link to long waves of capitalism and types of trade-union internationalism.

## Applications

History of labor movements; [[antisystemic-movements]]; world-systems analyses of oppositional politics.

## Critiques and Limitations

Stage models compress regional diversity; "from below" boundary is porous; gender and informal labor may be under-weighted in classical internationalist archives.
