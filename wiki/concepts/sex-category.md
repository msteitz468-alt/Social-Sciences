# Sex Category

**Sex category** is the middle term in [[west-candace|West]] and [[zimmerman-don-h|Zimmerman]]'s
(1987) three-way distinction among **sex**, **sex category**, and **gender**, foundational to
the [[doing-gender]] program.

## Definition as Coined

West and Zimmerman defined **sex** as a determination made through the application of socially
agreed biological criteria for classifying persons as females or males (genitalia at birth,
chromosomal typing before birth — criteria that need not agree). **Sex category** is placement
achieved through application of those criteria, but *in everyday life* categorization is
established and sustained by the socially required **identificatory displays** that proclaim
membership in one category or the other. One's sex category *presumes* one's sex and stands
as proxy for it in many situations, yet sex and sex category can vary independently: it is
possible to claim membership in a sex category even when the sex criteria are lacking.
**Gender**, by contrast, is managing situated conduct in light of normative conceptions of
attitudes and activities appropriate for one's sex category.

## Semantic History

The triad was offered to replace the coarser sex/gender binary of 1970s teaching, which
collapsed everyday categorization with either biology or achieved "role." It draws on
[[ethnomethodology]] (Garfinkel; Kessler & McKenna's "gender attribution process"; Sacks's
membership categorization and the "if-can" test) without equating sex category with gender
performance. Later [[doing-difference]] work treats analogous categorical membership work
for race and class.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct in the original article. Empirical work in the program observes
identificatory displays, categorization troubles, and accountability sequences in interaction
(medical settings, meetings, households).

## Applications

Anchor concept for [[west-zimmerman-doing-gender-study]]; used throughout
[[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]]; linked to [[garfinkel-agnes-study]]
as the classic case making categorization work visible. Ridgeway (2011) adopts the term for
social labeling that triggers gendered practices, and pairs it with process-focused
[[sex-categorization]] inside [[gender-as-primary-frame]] (*[[ridgeway-framed-by-gender-2011|Framed by Gender]]*).

## Critiques and Limitations

Critics of "Doing Difference" argue that treating race and class as parallel "category"
accomplishments can flatten historical and structural specificity (see
[[doing-difference-debate]]). The sex/sex-category/gender split itself is widely adopted in
sociology even among scholars who reject the broader program's micro-focus.
