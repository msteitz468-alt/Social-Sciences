# Enskilment

## Definition as Coined

In *[[ingold-perception-of-the-environment-2000|The Perception of the Environment]]* (2000), [[ingold-tim|Ingold]] places **skill** and **enskilment** at the center of what is usually called culture. Cultural variation "consists, in the first place, of variations in skill"; skills are "grown, incorporated into the human organism through practice and training in an environment" and are "thus as much biological as cultural" (General introduction / blurb, attributed). **Enskilment** names the developmental process: learning inseparable from doing within dwelling — Jean Lave's "understanding in practice" against a "culture of acquisition" (Ch. 23).

## Semantic History

Contrasts with:
- **Enculturation** as transfer of content or rules between heads (OFT/dual-inheritance analogues of genes; Ch. 2).
- **Technology** as application of prior design or as applied science (Part III).
- **Intelligence** as an internal device delivering plans for mechanical execution (Ch. 23).

Aligns with Gibson's **education of attention**: generations introduce novices into contexts that afford selected opportunities and scaffolding; "nothing, strictly speaking, is 'handed down' at all" as a corpus of representations (Chs. 8, 19). Novices become skilled by eventually *dispensing with* rules and maps — "map discarded once country known" (Ch. 23). Part III treats tool-use, craft, speech, and song as varieties of skilled practice sharing properties of care, judgment, dexterity, and situational awareness (five dimensions previewed and developed in Ch. 19).

## Operationalizations

Ethnography of apprenticeship and craft (Telefol bilum, basketry, weaving; Chs. 18–19); developmental accounts of tool-use and speech; critiques of "anatomically modern human" packages that split innate anatomy from acquired culture (Ch. 21); industrial skill that absorbs clock-time into *tekhnē* (Ch. 17).

## Applications

Craft and design anthropology; education research; landscape [[wayfinding]]; multi-species attention practices (conceptual kinship with [[arts-of-noticing]]). Weaving paradigm: form generated in morphogenetic engagement with materials, not transcription of design (Chs. 18–19).

## Critiques and Limitations

Hard to operationalize for large-n comparison; may underplay coercive training and power in "scaffolding"; conversation with [[practice-theory|habitus]] (durable dispositions vs skill-growth-in-attention) remains open. Ingold's five dimensions of skill are analytic, not a validated psychometrics battery.
