# Psychic System

## Definition as Coined

A **psychic system** is, in [[luhmann-niklas|Luhmann]]'s theory, a system of **consciousness**:
an [[autopoiesis|autopoietic]] system "based on consciousness, not on life," whose element is
the **thought** ("Thoughts are necessary in order to arrive at thoughts"). It operates in the
medium of **[[meaning]]** and is operationally distinct from both the organic/nervous system
(which mediates all its environmental contact) and from social systems. Consciousness "cannot
be directly transferred or shared" between psychic systems; only [[communication]] bridges them,
and only indirectly, via language, which "transfers social complexity into psychic complexity"
without psychic processes ever being linguistic — thinking is not "internal dialogue."

Individuality, on this account, is redefined **through self-reference, not substance**: it "can
be destroyed... but it cannot be modified" — the circular closure of a self-referential
reproduction, not a thing or an essence.

## Semantic History

The concept is Luhmann's device for **removing "the individual" from the foundations of
sociology** and relocating human beings — as organic + psychic + social interpenetration — to
the **environment** of society. This is his methodological anti-humanism: psychic and social
systems are "co-evolved and mutually irreducible" — "no consciousness revolves around
communication and no communication around consciousness." Against Husserl, Luhmann agrees
consciousness is temporalized (no duration, constantly self-replacing — anticipating Derrida's
*différance*) but denies it is transcendental, since it is only one autopoietic system-reference
among several (life, consciousness, communication). He treats **emotions** functionally as an
internal "immune system" of the psychic system — reactions that "grip body and consciousness
when the autopoiesis of consciousness is in danger" — not as representations of the environment.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Luhmann offers the (attributed) hypothesis that "social systems
that can enlist more complex psychic systems need less structure."

## Applications

- The environmental correlate of every social system; grounds the [[interpenetration]] account
  of psychic/social co-evolution.
- Supports Luhmann's claim that the proper object of sociology is communication, not individual
  behaviour: "it is in principle false to assume that individuals are... more directly
  observable than social systems."

## Critiques and Limitations

Placing persons in society's environment and reducing "the individual" to a closure-effect is
the sharpest point of the humanist objection to Luhmann. Critics also question whether treating
consciousness as strictly sealed off from communication can account for shared understanding at
all. All claims here are Luhmann's, in attributed voice.
