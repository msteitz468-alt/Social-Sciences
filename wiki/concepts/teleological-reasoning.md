# Teleological Reasoning

## Definition as Coined

CSR (White 2021 ch. 3, 5): a bias toward seeing things in the world as **having a purpose** and being made for that purpose — proposed cognitive foundation for creator deities, design arguments, and "everything happens for a reason" reasoning about life events.

## Semantic History

From developmental work on children's promiscuous teleology (Kelemen) into explanations of natural-world religious cognition.

## Operationalizations

Developmental experiments on artifact vs natural-object purpose; adult reasoning under speeded conditions; cross-cultural extensions.

## Applications

Nature-of-the-world chapter; misfortune and providence; design stance toward cosmos.

## Critiques and Limitations

Teleology is domain-sensitive; education and culture modulate expression; not identical to monotheistic creation doctrine.
