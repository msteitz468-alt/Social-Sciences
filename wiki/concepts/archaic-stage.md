# Archaic Stage

Second New World historical-developmental stage in [[willey-phillips-method-theory-1958|Willey & Phillips]] (1958). Americanist construct — not a synonym for European Mesolithic.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Willey & Phillips 1958.)*

> The stage of migratory hunting and gathering cultures continuing into environmental conditions approximating those of the present. With the extinction of the large Pleistocene mammals… dependence on smaller and perhaps more varied fauna. There is also an apparent increase in gathering…

Key clarifications in the 1958 revision:

- **Polished stone** is **not** a pan-hemispheric stage criterion (Andover 1956 technological split rejected for hemispheric use).
- **Agriculture** may appear in early Archaic contexts; mere presence is not primary. Agriculture becomes developmentally decisive only when **dominant** and socially integrated into stable settlement — the sine qua non of [[formative-stage|Formative]]. Lack of agriculture is **no longer** a negative criterion of Archaic.
- **Preformative** (1955) is **dropped**; early plant domestication and “slow-footed agricultural revolution” material is redistributed between late Archaic and Formative.
- No upper/lower Archaic pan-New World split.

## Semantic History

“Archaic” was already standard in eastern North American archaeology (pre-pottery and early pottery foragers). W&P elevated it to a **hemispheric developmental stage** and reassigned Desert/Cochise/Basketmaker II-type complexes, Early Woodland-with-pottery in some cases, etc. Regional period systems (Eastern Archaic, Desert Archaic, California, Andean preceramic) continue in parallel; “Archaic” as hemisphere-wide stage is a mid-century synthesis device, often replaced by local sequences today.

## Operationalizations

| Feature | Archaic (W&P) | Formative (W&P) |
|---|---|---|
| Environment | Near-modern | Near-modern |
| Subsistence | Hunting-gathering (+ optional early cultigens) | Agriculture or comparable effective economy + sedentary village |
| Settlement | Migratory / less stable | Well-established sedentary villages |
| Pottery | May appear (e.g. Early Woodland) | Usually associated, not definitional alone |

## Applications

- Reclassification of Desert culture, Cochise, and related complexes as Archaic rather than “early farming.”
- Framing California and Northwest Coast ethnographic florescences: W&P long preferred Archaic for non-agricultural cases, then allowed non-ag Formative where subsistence effectiveness and sedentism match (tensions in their own text — see Formative page).
- Temporal snags they note (as of 1958): early Archaic radiocarbon (Danger Cave, Modoc, Graham Cave) overlapping late Lithic Plains — stage priority vs pure chronology.

## Critiques and Limitations

Stage vs period confusion; enormous internal diversity; later research rewrote plant-domestication timelines and coastal adaptations. Keep all W&P regional assignments **attributed and dated 1958**.
