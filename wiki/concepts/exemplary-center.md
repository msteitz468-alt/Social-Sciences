# Exemplary Center

## Definition as Coined

In *[[geertz-negara-1980|Negara]]* (1980), [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] used **exemplary center** for the classical Balinese court-capital as the living **model** of cosmic and political order — a center lesser houses, temples, and villages imitated at reduced scale, rather than a command post administering continuous territory. The more consummate the king as icon, he argued, “the more exemplary the center. The more exemplary the center, the more actual the realm.” Closely tied to the [[theatre-state]] thesis and to Geertz’s [[models-of-and-for|model of / model for]] formula: ceremony does not merely depict order; under a type-and-token hierarchy of sensuous expressions, imitation is actualization — “To visualize was to see, to see to imitate, and to imitate to embody.”

## Semantic History

**Myth of origin.** Geertz recounts Balinese origin narratives of Majapahit conquest and the Kepakisan line as charters that locate present courts as diminished copies of a brighter past center — the past as standard (echoing Bateson on Balinese temporal orientation). **Sinking** applies both genealogically ([[sinking-status]] within *dadia*) and spatially (cadet houses and peripheral realms “go out” from the core).

**Pyramid / house of cards.** Representationally the negara looks like a hierarchy descending from an apex (Klungkung as nominal “capital” of traditional Bali among competing south-Bali courts: Tabanan, Badung, Gianyar, Karangasem, Bangli, Mengwi). Institutionally Geertz stresses fragility, confederacy, and competitive replication — not a single hydraulic despotism.

**Ritual ecology.** In the irrigation system, Pura Batu Kau functions as a state-legitimized ceremonial cycle above the rice line of which each *subak* mounts timed replications — an “exemplary center” pattern in agricultural ritual, not only in court politics (*[[geertz-negara-study]]*).

**Notes on terminology.** Geertz held that wild terminological variation (*bedugul*, *pekasih*, etc.) reflects the replica logic: any off-center term can name a diminished version of a brighter center; failure to see this feeds false *dorpsrepubliek* vs oriental-despot poles.

## Operationalizations

Indicators in Geertz’s reconstruction: palace as temple / microcosm (e.g. Klungkung *puri* plan ca. 1905); nested *sekti* / *murti* stages of power-imaging; competitive scale of cult (cremation towers 1–11 roofs; mass attendance); Klungkung titular primacy without effective monopoly; multi-lord confederations; directional symbolism (mountain/sea, *jero*/*jaba*).

## Applications

Organizing image for the [[theatre-state]] and for south-Bali multi-court politics. Comparative resonance with Southeast Asian [[mandala]] polities (radiating allegiance, no Hadrian’s wall frontiers) — **associated, not identical**: Geertz’s case is Balinese-specific in *dadia*, *subak*, and *puputan* detail; mandala literature is broader mainland/island synthesis.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether “exemplary” understates extractive and military practices; whether model-and-copy over-reads Indic cosmology into messy politics; whether the center is more colonial/Indological projection than emic constant — live questions in Indonesian studies.
