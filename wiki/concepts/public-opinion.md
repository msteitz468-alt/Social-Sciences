# Public Opinion

Category at the center of STPS’s closing chapters: historically the opinion of a public engaged in rational-critical debate; under structural transformation, often reduced to a **fiction of constitutional law** or a **social-psychological aggregate** of attitudes.

## Definition as Coined

Habermas reconstructs the prehistory of *opinion publique* / *öffentliche Meinung* and argues empirical opinion research liquidates the classical concept when it equates public opinion with mass/group attitudes stripped of institutional-political publicity.

## Semantic History

Eighteenth-century critical standard; nineteenth-century liberal anxiety; Tarde, Bryce, Dicey; Lazarsfeld and polling culture; Habermas’s attempt at sociological re-clarification linking opinion to agencies of domination and rules of public discussion.

## Operationalizations

Polls and attitude scales vs institutional analysis of media, parties, and constitutional publicity — the gap is Habermas’s point.

## Applications

Democratic theory, polling critique, media sociology.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether classical public opinion was ever inclusive; whether polls can be reintegrated with deliberative norms (Habermas later returns to this — not ingested).
