# Technoscape

One of [[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]'s five [[global-cultural-flows|-scapes]].
Attributed to Appadurai.

## Definition as Coined

"By technoscape, I mean the global configuration, also ever fluid, of technology
and the fact that technology, both high and low, both mechanical and
informational, now moves at high speeds across various kinds of previously
impervious boundaries" *(Appadurai 1996)*. Its "odd distribution" is "increasingly
driven not by any obvious economies of scale, of political control, or of market
rationality but by increasingly complex relationships among money flows, political
possibilities, and the availability of both un- and highly skilled labor."

## Semantic History

Coined to break the assumption that technology diffuses along a single
developmental gradient (contrast [[modernization-theory]]). Less independently
cited than [[ethnoscape]] or [[mediascape]], it survives mainly within discussions
of the full [[global-cultural-flows|scapes framework]].

## Operationalizations

Illustrated by a Libyan steel complex drawing on Indian, Chinese, Russian, and
Japanese interests, and by India simultaneously exporting "waiters and chauffeurs
to Dubai and Sharjah" and "software engineers to the United States." The
technoscape's relation to the [[ethnoscape]] and [[financescape]] is "deeply
disjunctive and profoundly unpredictable."

## Applications

Anchors the disjuncture thesis: states are under particular siege "where there are
radical disjunctures between ideoscapes and technoscapes" (small countries lacking
production/information technology).

## Critiques and Limitations

The weakest-defined of the five for measurement purposes; critics note it collapses
distinct phenomena (production location, technology transfer, labor mobility) into
one "landscape."
