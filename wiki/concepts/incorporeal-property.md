# Incorporeal Property

## Definition as Coined

**Incorporeal property** denotes exclusive rights in **non-material** goods — ceremonial privileges, songs, names, offices, ritual knowledge — as opposed to land and chattels. [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]] (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, Property) treats such rights as widespread in simpler societies and as a decisive counter to crude "primitive communism" slogans.

## Semantic History

Legal incorporeal hereditaments → ethnographic extension (Plains, Northwest Coast, Oceania) → later economic anthropology of intangible assets and intellectual property analogies. Distinct from Marxian "primitive communism" debates.

## Operationalizations

What can be owned/sold/inherited; who validates title; relation to rank and associations (e.g. Crow/Hidatsa ceremonial privileges; NW Coast privileges in rank chapter).

## Applications

Property and Rank chapters of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*; critique of primitive communism.

## Critiques and Limitations

Label can blur ritual office, copyright-like exclusivity, and prestige goods — specify local categories.
