# Play Farming

## Definition as Coined

[[graeber-david|Graeber]] and [[wengrow-david|Wengrow]] (*[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|Dawn of Everything]]*, chs. 6–7) use **play farming** — framed through Plato's **Gardens of Adonis** (ephemeral ritual plantings vs. serious eighth-month agriculture) — for cultivation practiced as **seasonal, ritual, experimental, or reversible** activity without committing a society to full agrarian dependence, private-property lock-in, or irreversible hierarchy.

They argue (attributed):

- Knowledge of cultivation and plant properties often long preceded any "Agricultural Revolution" as popularly narrated (multi-millennial lag before morphological domestication in the Fertile Crescent as they synthesize it).
- Many Neolithic and Holocene peoples **switched in and out** of farming ("ecology of freedom," adapted from Bookchin's phrase in their usage) or maintained low-level food production as the Holocene **norm**.
- Farming did **not** automatically mean private property or inequality; some of the first farming communities were relatively free of ranks; stratification and violence can be more entrenched among groups **least** dependent on crops.
- Innovation often lived in a zone of **ritual play** that also functioned as a scientific laboratory (women's botanical knowledge emphasized).

**Closing thesis of ch. 6 (attributed):** there was no single Agricultural Revolution switch from Palaeolithic forager to Neolithic farmer; "play farming" and mode-switching fill the gap; the phrase Agricultural Revolution is a semantic snare.

## Operationalizations

Archaeological signatures of partial/seasonal cultivation, delayed domestication, continuity of foraging, settlement without rigid ranking — **as interpreted by G&W** from specialist sources. Not a formal cross-cultural coding scheme in *Dawn*.

## Applications

- *Dawn* chs. 6–7 (Fertile Crescent, Göbekli context, Çatalhöyük revision, multi-centric domestication, Amazonia, Eastern Woodlands, China, Linear Pottery contrasts).
- Linked to [[three-basic-freedoms]] (capacity to shift social/economic regimes).
- American Hopewell / post-Mississippian "play farming" return in ch. 11.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Specialists may reject the degree of voluntary "avoidance" vs. ecological constraint and risk.
- Risk of romanticizing Neolithic agency and gendering innovation without primary excavation control.
- Case-level disputes → [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].
