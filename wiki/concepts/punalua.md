# Punalua

## Definition as Coined

**Punalua** (Hawaiian) enters anthropology via [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]’s use of the Hawaiian custom and terminology in *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity]]* (1871) as a key to the **Malayan** [[classificatory-system-of-relationship|classificatory system]] and to his **conjectural solution** for the origin of classificatory relationship. Emic term; etic evolutionary load added by Morgan (and later Engels).

## Semantic History

Emic Hawaiian kinship/marriage vocabulary → Morgan’s evolutionary mechanism for Malayan terminology → [[group-marriage]] stage in *Ancient Society* / Engels *Origin* → contested and largely abandoned as universal stage evidence.

## Operationalizations

Morgan links shared-spouse arrangements among related persons to terminological mergers in Malayan schedules.

## Applications

Part III Malayan discussion and General Results; Marxist family evolution.

## Critiques and Limitations

Ethnographic accuracy of nineteenth-century Hawaiian reports debated; overextension to world history rejected ([[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]).
