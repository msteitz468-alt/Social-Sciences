# Passing

## Definition as Coined

In *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]* Ch 2, [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] treats **passing** as management of **undisclosed discrediting information about self** — the situation of the **[[discredited-and-discreditable|discreditable]]** who is received as normal or as a member of an ordinary category (attributed). Reverse passing (concealment of *creditable* facts) occurs but is set aside as not relevant to the essay (attributed).

Goffman argued that where stigma is sociologically interesting, the person is neither always exposed nor always safely hidden: **near-universal occasional passing** appears among the discreditable; extremes of total exposure or total invisibility fail to cover the empirical range (attributed). Learning to pass can be a phase of the [[moral-career|moral career]] (attributed).

**Spatial partitioning (attributed):** by social identity — forbidden, civil, and back places; by personal identity — places where one is known personally vs anonymous. Contingencies include blackmail varieties, double lives, biographical discontinuity, confession obligations, and psychic costs (anxiety, dual attachment, hyper-attentiveness) (attributed).

**Techniques of information control (attributed sample):** conceal or obliterate stigma symbols; use **disidentifiers**; present a lesser or alternate stigma; divide the world (tell all to a few, nothing to many); voluntary distance and geographic discontinuity; staged voluntary disclosure (moving from discreditable to discredited as a moral-career phase of unlearning concealment).

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Racial "passing" (US) | Presenting as white / another racial category |
| Sexuality / gender | Concealment of gay or trans identity |
| Goffman 1963 | General information-control strategy across stigma types |
| Disability / illness | Concealable conditions |

Contested when generalization flattens power differences across race, sexuality, and disability.

## Operationalizations

Disclosure management; visibility/concealability; later "outness" scales invert the frame.

## Applications

Criminal records, mental-hospitalization history, stigma of origin, disability, sexuality — as Goffman illustrates from secondary sources. Closely paired with **[[covering]]** (reduce obtrusiveness of known/visible stigma without full concealment).

## Critiques and Limitations

Ethics of concealment vs authenticity politics; structural surveillance (records, databases) limits individual passing; racial-passing literatures often refuse dissolution into general stigma theory without race-specific analysis.

## The Ethnomethodological Sense (Garfinkel)

Independently of Goffman, [[garfinkel-harold|Harold Garfinkel]] used **passing** in his
[[garfinkel-agnes-study|Agnes case study]] (Chapter 5 of *[[garfinkel-studies-ethnomethodology-1967|Studies in Ethnomethodology]]*, 1967) for the continuous practical work of securing an
elected sex status against the risk of disclosure. Garfinkel stressed the active,
non-episodic character of the work: "it would be incorrect to say of Agnes that she has
passed. The active mode is needed: she is passing" (attributed). Where Goffman's dramaturgical
treatment frames passing as episodic impression management, Garfinkel argued (attributed) that
Agnes's passing was ongoing and developmental, exposing a "troubled relationship" between
routine, trust, and [[accountability|accountable]] rationality that the dramaturgical model
does not capture — making passing, for ethnomethodology, a window onto sex status as a
[[practical-action|managed achievement]] rather than a natural fact.
