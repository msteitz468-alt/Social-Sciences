# Black Box (STS)

In laboratory studies and related STS, a **black box** is a fact, device, or package of claims that has been stabilized so that users no longer open its internal history — they take the output as given. *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* develops the process by which construction work disappears into taken-for-granted facticity; later Latour writing (not yet primary-ingested) popularizes the term further.

## Definition as Coined

As facts move up the [[statement-types|statement-type]] scale and modalities are deleted, production circumstances (failed assays, local criteria, investments, controversies) drop out of view. The statement can then be used as an unproblematic input — a black box — until challenge **reopens** it (demodalization, artefact accusations).

## Semantic History

Cybernetic/engineering "black box" metaphor migrated into STS; *Laboratory Life* and subsequent Latour are primary vectors in social science. Related to reification and to textbook science vs. research-frontier science.

## Operationalizations

Ask what a paper or practice treats as needing no further justification; track historical reopening of once-closed facts.

## Applications

TRF becoming "what it is"; instruments used without re-deriving their calibration history; textbook statements.

## Critiques and Limitations

Metaphor can obscure partial openings and routine maintenance; "black-boxing" is not always permanent or complete.
