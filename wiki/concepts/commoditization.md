# Commoditization

## Definition as Coined

In *[[appadurai-social-life-of-things-1986|The Social Life of Things]]* (1986), **commoditization** is a **process of becoming**, not an all-or-none state.

**[[kopytoff-igor|Kopytoff]]:** expansion (a) for each thing, by making it exchangeable for more other things; (b) for the system, by making more kinds of things more widely exchangeable. Every exchange system has a drive toward **optimum commoditization** within its exchange technology; money’s introduction typically expands internal commoditization and merges spheres *(Kopytoff)*.

**[[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]:** the **commodity situation** is when a thing’s exchangeability (past, present, or future) is its socially relevant feature — disaggregated into commodity **phase**, **candidacy**, and **context**. Types: by destination, by metamorphosis, by diversion, and ex-commodities. Modern industrial capitalism is the most intensely commoditized type of society, but degree comparison is complex under this definition *(Appadurai)*.

> "the commodity is not one kind of thing rather than another, but one phase in the life of some things." *(Appadurai, agreeing with Kopytoff)*

## Operationalizations

- **Phase / candidacy / context** (Appadurai).
- **Moral economy** of what may enter commodity marking (Kopytoff).
- Paths vs diversions; enclaving as controlled restriction of commoditization.
- **Partial commoditization** (Bayly on Indian cloth retaining qualities of makers/exchangers even when monetized).

## Applications (SLOT)

- Slavery as temporary full commoditization of persons *(Kopytoff)*.
- Qat’s cultural-restriction breakdown → fuller commodity career *(Cassanelli)*.
- Relics: theft/sale as temporary commoditization to escape gift-debt *(Geary)*.
- Metal as completed commoditization when circulating as raw-material ingots *(Renfrew)*.
- Carpets: imperfect commoditization + authenticity-driven resingularization *(Spooner)*.

## Tsing / matsutake chain (2015)

*[[tsing-mushroom-2015|Tsing]]* tracks partial and brief commoditization along the Oregon–Japan matsutake chain: freedom trophies → buyer theater → export inventory → Japanese gift re-embedding. Lead-firm accumulation depends on translating noncapitalist and peri-capitalist value into legible inventory without fully proletarianizing production ([[salvage-accumulation]]) *(Tsing 2015)*. Related: [[gift-exchange]], [[alienation]], [[tsing-mushroom-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Overlaps with Marxist “commodification” and Polanyian fictitious commodities — keep registers distinct. Appadurai’s broad definition deliberately blurs sharp gift/commodity dualism; that blur is a thesis, not wiki-voice law.
