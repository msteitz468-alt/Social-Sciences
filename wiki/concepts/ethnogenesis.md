# Ethnogenesis

## Definition as Coined

**Ethnogenesis** names the emergence and remaking of ethnic groups and identities over time — a standard anthropological term with many schools (primordialist, instrumentalist, constructivist). On this wiki the densified program is [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]]’s **radical constructionist** case for mainland SE Asian hills in *[[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-2009|The Art of Not Being Governed]]* (2009, Ch.7; study [[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-study]]): ethnic identities in the hills are **politically crafted** to position groups in competition for power and resources — especially vis-à-vis the lowland state and other hill peoples — “not in circumstances of their own choosing.” All substantive claims below are **attributed** to Scott (and sources he cites) unless flagged as general usage.

Scott’s prefatory defense: all identities are socially constructed (Han, Burman, American, Danish…); radical constructionism is not denigration of ethnonational struggle; minority identities are often first imagined by powerful states yet may become passionately lived.

## Semantic History

- **General anthropology:** Ethnogenesis covers fusion of maroons and refugees into new peoples, colonial tribalization, nationalist mythmaking, and gradual cultural drift. Related vocabularies: Barthian boundary maintenance, instrumental ethnicity, Hobsbawmian invented tradition.
- **Scott 2009 program (Zomia hills):** Ch.7 makes ethnogenesis the political counterpart of [[escape-agriculture]] and [[orality-as-strategy]]. Key moves: (1) trait-based “tribe” fails empirical tests; (2) **states make tribes** more than tribes make states — tribe as secondary form and “module of rule”; (3) hill identity plasticity is a **resource**, not confusion; (4) for most of long hill history, identities served **statelessness** (“antistate nationalism”) as much as autonomy or later nation-state aspiration; (5) valley “ethnicity” is itself often a **recipe for state-making** (padi + Indic/Theravada formulas + written language) that ingathers multi-origin manpower.
- **Semantic drift caution:** Do not equate Scott’s radical constructionism with the claim that hill peoples “are not real.” He holds named self-conscious identities exist as creative political projects; the only defensible departure for who is X/Y is actors’ self-designations.

## Operationalizations

| Scott 2009 diagnostic | Content (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Trait-bundle test fails | Language ≠ race ≠ culture ≠ history; colonial censuses operationalized “race” as mother tongue under a dogma Leach called “nonsense”; bilingualism/trilingualism the hill rule |
| Exonyms and administrative tribes | Most “tribal” designations exonyms (derogatory or geographic); tribe as module of rule — classify non-peasants, appoint responsible chiefs, pacify zones |
| Repertoire / bandwidth | Precolonial hill people often lacked “proper” ethnic labels; plural identities deployed by context; change = shift in relative frequency of repertoire performance (Lehman, Leach, Keyes “ethnic amphibians”) |
| Centripetal valley vs centrifugal hills | Valley systems homogenize (wet-rice routines, class, Theravada/Indic formulas); hills fabricate further difference (Jonsson; Lieberman 1600–1840 valley centralization vs hill heterogeneity) |
| States make tribes | Antonym of tribe is peasantry (subject cultivator); colonial and imperial systems prolific tribe-makers (Rome, Tang, Dutch *adat*, Han *tusi*, British sawbwa reliance) |
| Manpower absorption | Hill and valley societies absorb migrants, captives, adoptees; genealogical legerdemain naturalizes newcomers; Akha “massively replenished” while remaining Akha (von Geusau) |
| Maroon ethnogenesis model | Cossacks, Surinam maroons, Seminoles, Roma — fused from disparate beginnings by niche and persecution; SE Asia runaways analogized |

**Colonial census illustration (Burma 1911/1931):** cadastral survey for land, census for peoples; baroque complexity defeated order; a single “Kachin” village could host six mother tongues with Jingpaw as lingua franca.

## Applications

- **[[zomia]] / hill–valley coevolution:** Identity flux as product of fracture-zone state-making (flight, slave raids, altitude niches, intermarriage) — see [[shatter-zone]], [[state-evasion]], [[nonstate-spaces]].
- **Padi-state ingathering:** Burman/Tai polities as pioneer elites confecting multi-origin subjects at wet-rice cores; most Shan/Burman/Thai commoners partly ex-hill; “Hanness” as long-term state-based ingathering (*negeri*, *muang* membership formulas: religion + language + fealty + padi, not genealogy). See [[southeast-asian-state-formation]], [[state-formation]], [[grain-hypothesis]].
- **Case types Scott emphasizes:** Kachin↔Shan passage (Leach); Karen amphibiousness; Zhuang officialization under PRC minority policy reversing Sinicization; Karenni/Kayah invention around teak monopoly; Lisu “active forgetting” of history as identity strategy (Jonsson).
- **[[orality-as-strategy]]:** Oral plasticity of genealogy and history enables ethnogenetic maneuver that fixed texts impede.
- **[[legibility]]:** Stable named units of identity and place as state effects linked to writing and census.

## Critiques and Limitations

Keep as **Scott’s position (2009)**, not wiki-voice law of ethnicity:

- **Specialist pushback expected** on over-construction, underplaying long-run linguistic/cultural continuity, and analogy from Cossacks/maroons to specific Zomia groups.
- **Not all ethnogenesis is state-evasion** — market niches, missionary projects, millenarian fusion, and ordinary cultural change also create peoples.
- **Self-designation rule** does not dissolve power: states still impose categories that later become lived (Zhuang; colonial tribes).
- **Four-way non-identity:** Ethnic labels ≠ languages ≠ genetic populations ≠ archaeological cultures — Scott’s flux argument strengthens, does not cancel, the wiki’s non-identity rule.
