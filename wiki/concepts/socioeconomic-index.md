# Socioeconomic Index (SEI)

Continuous score of occupational status used to place detailed census occupations on a socioeconomic hierarchy. The **Duncan SEI**, used throughout *[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-1967|The American Occupational Structure]]*, predicts prestige from the educational and income composition of occupations.

## Definition as Coined

Duncan's socioeconomic index assigns each occupation a score reflecting the typical education and income of its incumbents, aligned with occupational prestige scales. Blau and Duncan treat SEI as the metric of "occupational status" in path models and regressions.

## Semantic History

Prestige studies (NORC and successors) → Duncan's 1961 SEI → Blau–Duncan 1967 mass use → later updates (MSEI, TSEI, ISEI/SIOPS internationally). "SEI" in U.S. stratification usually means a Duncan-type socioeconomic score, not raw prestige alone.

## Operationalizations

Occupation title → census code → SEI score. First job, current job, and father's occupation all scored on the same metric for correlation and path analysis.

## Applications

[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-study]]; status-attainment research program; comparative mobility when similar scales exist.

## Critiques and Limitations

Collapses class relations into a continuous gradient; prestige/SEI may not capture power, ownership, or labor-market segments; scores are historically specific to the education–income–occupation structure of the base period.
