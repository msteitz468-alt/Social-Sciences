# Dislocation (Décalage)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]*, **dislocation** (*décalage*) names the **offset or non-correspondence** between elements that empiricist and historicist problematics assume to be one-to-one or self-contemporaneous. Brewster’s glossary: empiricist/historicist problematics assume one-to-one mapping; Althusser/Balibar treat the social formation as articulation of dislocations between differential temporalities and between instances. Dislocation is not mere failure of fit but a **positive structural feature** — of complex unity under [[structure-in-dominance]], and especially of **forms of transition**.

## Semantic History

Althusser uses *décalage* for theoretical dislocation (e.g. between system and discourse in the [[knowledge-effect]]; between Marx’s answer and an unposed question). In the historical-time outline it is the articulation of non-contemporaneous times of levels. Balibar’s Part Five makes it load-bearing for **transition**: manufacture as transitional form has as its essence the chronological and structural dislocation between capitalist **property** relations and specifically capitalist **productive forces** (formal subsumption without real subsumption; craft skill still linking worker and means in the real-appropriation connection while property is already capitalist). Transition is itself a mode of production characterized by a special type of dislocation rather than a hiatus of destructuration between two structured periods. Related to but not identical with [[overdetermination]] (specification of contradiction by the whole) and [[differential-historical-time]] (peculiar times of levels).

## Operationalizations

- **Base / superstructure:** gaps between law and economic relation (or custom and law) as possible dislocations — especially visible in transition periods; law is second in order of analysis.
- **Two connections of the combination:** homology (capitalism) vs non-homology (precapitalist and transitional forms).
- **Knowledge:** unity-of-dislocation between conceptual system and order of discourse produces the knowledge effect.
- **Not quantity→quality shortcut:** structure is not transformed merely by its own functioning; transition takes structure as object of transformation (Balibar).

## Applications

- Manufacture → large-scale industry as transitional analysis in *Capital*.
- Theory of transition as structured phase (glossary TRANSITION): dislocation within the mode and between mode and other levels of the social formation.
- Critique of primitive accumulation as if it were already a completed transition theory (Establet: genealogy of elements, not autonomous development then fusion).

## Critiques and Limitations

- If overused, every mismatch becomes “dislocation” and the term loses diagnostic force.
- Requires the dual-connection model of combination; without it, reduces to vague non-synchrony.
- Political questions of agency in transition are underdetermined by the structural description alone.
