# Naturalness of Religion

## Definition as Coined

CSR thesis (White 2021): many forms of religious thought are **maturationally natural** — they arise readily from ordinary cognitive systems — whereas science and much formal theology are harder, "unnatural" cultural achievements (McCauley's naturalness of religion / unnaturalness of science contrast).

## Semantic History

Boyer *Naturalness of Religious Ideas* (1994); McCauley 2000 essay; Barrett natural foundations. Distinct from theological claims that religion is innate revelation.

## Operationalizations

Developmental readiness; cross-cultural recurrence; dual-process gaps ([[theological-correctness]]); comparison with scientific training costs.

## Applications

Explains easy acquisition of agentive supernatural concepts and difficulty of sustained scientific atheism or abstract theology without institutions.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Natural" risks reifying WEIRD developmental patterns; secularization and non-theistic traditions challenge simple naturalness; adaptationist strands claim some religion is hard-won cultural evolution, not pure natural by-product.
