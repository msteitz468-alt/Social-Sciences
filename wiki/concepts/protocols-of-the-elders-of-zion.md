# Protocols of the Elders of Zion

The *Protocols of the Elders of Zion* (also *Protocols of the Learned Elders of Zion*) is a **forged** document purporting to be the secret minutes of a Jewish conspiracy for world domination. It is the supreme textual vehicle of the [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]] in [[cohn-norman|Norman Cohn]]'s *[[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-1967|Warrant for Genocide]]* (1967).

## Definition as Coined

Not a genuine coinage but a fabrication: a pastiche presented by publishers as authentic Jewish leadership plans. Cohn (attributed) and the Berne-trial tradition treat it as a proven forgery. **Main literary source (Cohn, documented):** Maurice Joly's 1864 *Dialogue aux Enfers entre Machiavel et Montesquieu* — a liberal satire on Napoleon III, not a Jewish text — with ~160 parallel passages and roughly two-fifths of the Protocols dependent on Joly, often in the same order. Machiavelli's amoral statecraft is rewritten as the secret plan of "Elders of Zion." Additional streams: Goedsche/Retcliffe "Rabbi's Speech" lineage; messianic/Antichrist framing (esp. Sergey Nilus); other antisemitic fictions.

## Operationalizations

1. **Philological** — parallel-passage demonstration (Cohn Appendices 2–3).
2. **Edition history** — languages, publishers, abridgments, print runs (Cohn reports e.g. ~120k early German copies; dozens of zur Beek editions by 1933).
3. **Political use** — who printed, funded, cited, and acted under its spell.
4. **Reception** — sincere belief vs. cynical instrumentalization (Cohn contrasts Rosenberg-type true believers with Goebbels-type cynics).

## Applications

Central case for modern political forgeries, conspiracy culture, and genocidal ideology. Links: [[antisemitism]], [[genocide]], [[holocaust-and-modernity-debate]], [[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-study]].

## Post-1945 Afterlife (Wistrich 2010, attributed)

[[wistrich-robert|Wistrich]] (*[[wistrich-lethal-obsession-2010|A Lethal Obsession]]*) extends Cohn's dissemination history forward: first Arabic translations mid-1920s; Nasser's public endorsements (1958 recommendation to journalist R.K. Karanjia; his brother's 1968 Arabic edition) and Egyptian state distribution; Soviet propagandists' pseudo-Marxist adaptations (Ivanov, Yevseyev, Begun — a 1972 Soviet embassy journal in Paris reprinted a 1906 czarist pamphlet with "Zionist" substituted for "Jew," drawing a 1973 French conviction); the **Hamas Charter's article 32 citing the Protocols directly**; Jordanian school-curriculum use and Saudi UN ambassador Baroody's 1970s advocacy; Iranian textbooks teaching Zionism "through a Protocols prism" and Ahmadinejad's Protocols-style 2008 UN speech; a 2002 Egyptian Ramadan TV dramatization; post-Soviet Russian revival (1993 Holy Trinity Lavra bestseller edition); distribution at Durban 2001; and transmission to Japan via White Russian officers (1920). Wistrich's judgment that no single text matches its influence on twentieth-century antisemitism is his attributed assessment.

## Critiques and Limitations

**Authenticity is not a live scholarly dispute** — the text is a forgery. Live questions: (a) precise Russian authorship (Rachkovsky/Okhrana hypotheses vs. residual mystery — Cohn leaves a residual); (b) **causal weight** — how much the Protocols moved policy versus rationalized it; (c) relation to racial antisemitism (fusion product, not a substitute). Never treat Protocols content as evidence about Jews or Judaism.
