# Specialized Intelligences

Etic analytical construct in [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]]'s [[cognitive-fluidity]] model (*[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]*, 1996): relatively insulated **domain-specific** mental capacities — the “chapels” of the [[cathedral-of-the-mind|cathedral of the mind]] — that process particular problem types with little cross-domain access until cognitive fluidity evolves.

## Definition as Coined

Mithen (1996, attributed) posits specialized intelligences including at least:

| Intelligence | Domain content (Mithen 1996) | Illustrative archaeological correlate (attributed) |
|---|---|---|
| **Social** | Theory of mind, alliance, status, intentionality of persons | Group size / grooming–language arguments (Dunbar); social complexity without technological reflection |
| **Natural-history** | Animals, plants, tracks, landscape geography; prediction from current cues | Old World colonization; Neanderthal glacial survival; hunting success without dedicated tool diversity |
| **Technical** | Artifact manufacture, fracture dynamics, mental templates, physical causality of materials | Handaxes; Levallois; limited bone/ivory; few composite tools; multi-task stone |
| **Linguistic** | Language as specialized capacity; initially social-only in Early Humans | Endocast/hyoid/vocal-tract arguments; gossip residue in modern talk (Dunbar) |

Inspired by Howard Gardner’s multiple intelligences and Cosmides & Tooby’s Darwinian algorithms, reworked for archaeological periodization (attributed). Cosmides & Tooby’s “Swiss army knife” is Mithen’s preferred Phase-2 image; he accepts modularity as key but claims it unlocks prehistory “in a very different way” than pure EP modular isolation (attributed).

## Semantic History

“Multiple intelligences” (Gardner) and “domain-specific modules” (Fodor; evolutionary psychology) predate Mithen. **Specialized intelligences** in this wiki sense means Mithen’s *archaeologically staged* suite, not Gardner’s educational list and not IQ factor structure. Developmental “intuitive psychology / biology / physics / language” (Leslie, Baron-Cohen, Atran, Spelke, etc., as Mithen cites) supply the modern-mind residue of the chapels.

## Operationalizations

Mithen operationalizes presence and isolation via material culture: tool typology, innovation rates, and multi-material use (technical); environmental range and hunting pattern (natural history); group-size and social-learning proxies (social); symbolic/artistic production and cross-domain metaphor (fluidity, not specialization alone). **All such mappings are interpretive bridges** — attributed, not direct measures. Phase-2 isolation is diagnosed by *paradoxes*: modern-level skill in one domain coexisting with absence of behaviors that would require combining domains (e.g. handaxe skill without art; track-reading capacity without intentional visual symbols).

## Applications

- **Common ancestor / chimpanzee analogy (Ch.5):** social intelligence present; technical and natural-history intelligence limited or micro-domain only; social–tool “brick wall.”
- **First toolmakers (Ch.6):** Oldowan may enhance social learning without full technical/natural-history domains; *H. habilis* as elaborated common-ancestor design.
- **Early Humans / Neanderthals (Ch.7–8):** full multi-chapel Phase 2; consciousness “trapped” in social chapel (Humphrey-based argument).
- **Upper Palaeolithic (Ch.9):** art = technical + social + natural-history processes combined; religion as domain-mixing (Boyer); adornment as technical–social.
- **Agriculture (Epilogue):** four propensities from recombined pairs of intelligences.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether domains were as sealed as claimed; whether four (or three-plus-language) is the right inventory; whether archaeology can diagnose “isolation” versus absence of externalization; European bias in diagnosing Neanderthal “lack.” Contested: yes. Links to theory page: [[cognitive-fluidity]].
