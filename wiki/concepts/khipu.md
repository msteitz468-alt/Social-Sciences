## Definition as Coined

Khipu (also rendered quipu; Quechua for "knot") is the Andean knotted-cord device used to record information. As of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]], Inca lords kept vast information — census, tax, and imperial history — on quipu: a length of cord held horizontally from which numerous yarns of various colors were vertically suspended, with hierarchical dependent strings. Numerical data were encoded by knot types; other information by cord length, color, and hierarchical position. The system disintegrated after foreign conquest with the royal court.

## Semantic History

Colonial chroniclers noted professional accountants (**quipukamayoqs**) among the permanently subsidized technical tier of the Inca state (Moseley 2001). Modern scholarship securely establishes khipu as a decimal accounting technology within labor-tax and tribute economies that substituted for currency; whether khipu also encoded narrative, historical, or linguistic content beyond quantitative and categorical records remains contested and is not settled by the material ingested here. Scarre ed. 2013 likewise treats khipu primarily through Inca administrative/numeric function.

Preceramic and earlier coastal "knotting" as textile technique (Huaca Prieta looping/knotting — Moseley Ch. 5) is a craft practice, **not** evidence that knotted-cord *recording systems* existed in the Preceramic; do not collapse the two.

## Operationalizations

As of Moseley 2001:

- **Structure:** main horizontal cord; pendant colored yarns; secondary strings in hierarchy.
- **Numeric:** knot types for decimal quantities (goods, labor obligations, census-like counts).
- **Non-numeric channels:** color, length, position — content mapping debated.
- **Circulation:** *chaskis* (relay runners on mit’a service) carried quipu and messages to and from Cuzco along the road network.
- **Statecraft coupling:** decimal administration (units of 10 → 100 → … → Hunu Kuraka "Lord of 10,000") presupposed inventory technologies including khipu; staple finance vs wealth finance accounting drawn attention of chroniclers via quipukamayoqs.

## Applications

- **[[inca-empire|Inca]] imperial administration:** census after conquest of a province (people, livestock, fields, pastures); labor obligations (agricultural tax, mit’a, textile tax); movement of information with *chaskis*.
- **Museum and ethnohistoric corpora:** surviving khipu collections and colonial descriptions — full decipherment program not covered in ingested sources.
- **Comparative:** emblematic of Andean administrative sophistication without alphabetic writing.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Full semantic range contested: numeric/accounting role well established; narrative/linguistic claims require specialist sources not yet ingested — keep attributed and open.
- Post-conquest disintegration and Spanish framing of Inca "history" (Moseley: cyclical Andean time vs lineal Spanish history; colonial reports more reliable on organization than on dynastic narrative) limit what later readers can recover from khipu-mediated memory.
- Do not project Inca khipu back onto earlier archaeological cultures without cord evidence.
