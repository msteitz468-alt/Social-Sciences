## Definition as Coined

Khipu (also rendered quipu; Quechua for "knot") is the Andean knotted-cord device used to record information. As of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]], Inca lords kept vast information — census, tax, and imperial history — on quipu: a length of cord held horizontally from which numerous yarns of various colors were vertically suspended, with hierarchical dependent strings. Numerical data were encoded by knot types; other information by cord length, color, and hierarchical position. The system disintegrated after foreign conquest with the royal court.

## Semantic History

Colonial chroniclers noted professional accountants (**quipukamayoqs**) among the permanently subsidized technical tier of the Inca state (Moseley 2001). Modern scholarship securely establishes khipu as a decimal accounting technology within labor-tax and tribute economies that substituted for currency; whether khipu also encoded narrative, historical, or linguistic content beyond quantitative and categorical records remains contested and is not settled by the material ingested here. Scarre ed. 2013 likewise treats khipu primarily through Inca administrative/numeric function.

Preceramic and earlier coastal "knotting" as textile technique (Huaca Prieta looping/knotting — Moseley Ch. 5) is a craft practice, **not** evidence that knotted-cord *recording systems* existed in the Preceramic; do not collapse the two.

## Operationalizations

As of Moseley 2001:

- **Structure:** main horizontal cord; pendant colored yarns; secondary strings in hierarchy.
- **Numeric:** knot types for decimal quantities (goods, labor obligations, census-like counts).
- **Non-numeric channels:** color, length, position — content mapping debated.
- **Circulation:** *chaskis* (relay runners on mit’a service) carried quipu and messages to and from Cuzco along the road network.
- **Statecraft coupling:** decimal administration (units of 10 → 100 → … → Hunu Kuraka "Lord of 10,000") presupposed inventory technologies including khipu; staple finance vs wealth finance accounting drawn attention of chroniclers via quipukamayoqs.

## Applications

- **[[inca-empire|Inca]] imperial administration:** census after conquest of a province (people, livestock, fields, pastures); labor obligations (agricultural tax, mit’a, textile tax); movement of information with *chaskis*.
- **Museum and ethnohistoric corpora:** surviving khipu collections and colonial descriptions — full decipherment program not covered in ingested sources.
- **Comparative:** emblematic of Andean administrative sophistication without alphabetic writing.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Full semantic range contested: numeric/accounting role well established; narrative/linguistic claims require specialist sources not yet ingested — keep attributed and open.
- Post-conquest disintegration and Spanish framing of Inca "history" (Moseley: cyclical Andean time vs lineal Spanish history; colonial reports more reliable on organization than on dynastic narrative) limit what later readers can recover from khipu-mediated memory.
- Do not project Inca khipu back onto earlier archaeological cultures without cord evidence.

### From D'Altroy 2002

[[daltroy-the-incas-2002|D'Altroy (2002)]] expands the administrative and historiographic picture while keeping narrative encoding open.

**No alphabetic writing.** As of 2002, D'Altroy states the Incas had no writing system “that we have been able to recognize and decipher”; history was oral, with *amautas* (poet-historians) and *khipu kamayuq* reciting royal pasts — descendants favoring their ancestors. *Khipu* (and painted sticks, woven designs, painted boards) were precise flexible recording techniques borrowed from Andean traditions going back almost a thousand years (Wari knot-records already in use).

**Corpus and structure.** Only about 400 *khipu* known archaeologically as of 2002, partly because Spaniards destroyed as many as they could find; most finds coastal (dry preservation). Structure (Locke 1923; Ascher & Ascher 1981): primary cord with multi-colored knotted pendants (cotton, occasionally wool); decimal positional system — units farthest from primary cord, then tens, hundreds…; figure-8 knot for 1; long knots for 2–9; granny for ten; zero as absence of knot (Urton 1997); largest known position 10,000; superior strings can sum pendants; hierarchical tree-like organization. Arithmetic was done with pebbles/grain/counters, not on the *khipu* itself (Rowe).

**Uses.** Numerical data: census, warehouse contents, royal flocks, tax obligations, land measurements, military organization, calendrics. Also aided genealogies, conquest sequences, myths, and literary works; each *khipu* accompanied by an oral account memorized by the *khipu kamayuq* (office often father–son); Incas professionalized and ranked specialists; Spaniards allowed *khipu* testimony in court as reliable. Storage accountants tracked in/out of *qollqa*; Guaman Poma depicts a Huánuco Pampa storehouse account delivered on *khipu* to Thupa Inka; post-collapse Mantaro *khipu kamayuq* lists ordered goods by cultural significance. Census: separate provincial *khipu* by sex-specific life-stage categories; annual report at December solstice festival (possible independent check every 3–5 years). Elite hostages/students in [[cuzco]] learned *khipu* accounting. *Chaski* relay messengers (every ~6–9 km; ~240 km/day per Rowe) carried *khipu* with oral messages (Estete, Garcilaso). Cobo: over one thousand record-keeping specialists needed in Cuzco for shrine lore, prayers, rituals, and sacrifices ([[zeqe-system]]).

**History and power.** Atawallpa’s victorious faction reportedly killed historians and destroyed *khipu* so a new *pachakuti* could begin unburdened by the past (Callapiña et al. via D'Altroy). Even *khipu*-derived accounts could present conspicuously different visions by royal kin group (e.g. Probanza de Qhapaq Ayllu listing Thupa Inka Yupanki’s conquests). 1569 testimony ordered conquests by *suyu* quarter (Chinchaysuyu clockwise…), sometimes prioritizing status of conquered over event chronology — linear European history needs intercalation; structure embedded cultural logic of power and space as well as time. As of 2001/2002, Urton’s Chachapoyas cache of 32 knot-records is cited as a provisional calendrical/tribute-cycle interpretation (knot total ~3,005 ≈ ~3,000 local taxpayers) — single-study, assumption-laden. Full code still not cracked as of D'Altroy 2002; *t’oqapu* textile design-as-writing is not widely accepted.
