# Intichiuma

## Definition as Coined

**Intichiuma** is the term [[spencer-baldwin|Spencer]] and [[gillen-frank|Gillen]] recorded (1899) for Arrernte (Central Australian) totemic increase ceremonies intended to promote the multiplication of the totemic species (attributed). The reports became central data for evolutionary theories of [[totemism]] and [[magic]]. Spencer and Gillen observed the ceremonies as part of the **Engwura** — a multi-month Arunta initiation ceremony they witnessed over roughly three months at Alice Springs, November 1896 — and glossed intichiuma as functionally aimed at "increasing the number of the animal or plant after which the totem is named," i.e., an increase rite for the total food supply (attributed; [[stocking-after-tylor-1995|Stocking]] summarizing Spencer and Gillen, *The Native Tribes of Central Australia*, 1899).

## Semantic History

Drawn from an Arrernte term, the word entered anthropological literature as the paradigm case of "increase rites" and was deployed by Frazer, Durkheim, and Freud in rival theories of totemism. [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] used the Arunta intichiuma/totemism material as a "crucial experiment" for *The Elementary Forms of the Religious Life*, treating totemism as constitutive of Aboriginal social organization and as evidence for religion's function of periodically reaffirming collective social sentiments (attributed). [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] (1913) estimated that half of all anthropological theoretical work since 1899 had drawn on Spencer and Gillen's book, and nine-tenths had been "affected or modified by it" (attributed). Later scholarship has questioned the accuracy and framing of the original transcription.

## Applications

## Critiques and Limitations

The emic accuracy and armchair uses of the Spencer–Gillen material are contested; see [[stocking-after-tylor-1995|Stocking (1995)]]. T. G. H. Strehlow, fluent in Western Aranda, later criticized the Spencer–Gillen ethnography underlying the intichiuma reports as "defaced" by "many serious inaccuracies," attributing errors to reliance on pidgin-speaking intermediaries (attributed, Strehlow 1971 via Stocking; see [[spencer-baldwin]] for the fuller reliability dispute, flagged as a candidate future `debates/` page and not adjudicated here).
