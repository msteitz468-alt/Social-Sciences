# Tournaments of Value

## Definition as Coined

[[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]] (1986) takes the [[kula]] as paradigm of **tournaments of value**:

> "Tournaments of value are complex periodic events that are removed in some culturally well-defined way from the routines of economic life."

Participation is a privilege of power and an instrument of status contests; the “currency” is set apart by cultural diacritics; stakes include not only fame/rank but disposition of central tokens of value; outcomes matter for mundane power; strategic skill is success at diversions and subversions of conventionalized paths *(Appadurai)*.

Term stimulated by McKim Marriott’s “tournaments of rank” (1968) *(Appadurai note)*.

## Operationalizations

Markers *(Appadurai)*: cultural removal from routine exchange; elite/peer participation; calculated path-making and diversion; increment often reputation rather than industrial profit; articulation with mundane economy variable (do not isolate tournaments as Baudrillard tends to).

Analogues he sketches: Western art world; commodity futures pits as “meta-fetishization” arenas; potlatch; relic trade; Central Asian *buzkashi*.

## Applications

- Kula paths, *keda*, *kitoum* conversions *(Appadurai via Munn, Damon, Campbell)*.
- Eastern Solomon *-murina* as large-scale commemorative distribution fusing economic and spiritual value *(Davenport — related ceremonial scale, not labeled tournament by Davenport)*.
- Contrast: Gell’s Muria display as **collective, non-competitive** incorporation of goods rather than Melanesian-style tournament *(Gell)*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Ideal type; not every ceremonial distribution is a tournament. Keep distinct from ordinary high-price markets unless status/token criteria are themselves at stake.
