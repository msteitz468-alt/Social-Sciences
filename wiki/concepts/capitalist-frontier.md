# Capitalist Frontier

## Definition as Coined

In *[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]* Ch. 1, [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Tsing]] analyzes **capitalist frontiers** (*zones-frontières du capitalisme*) as particular edges where expansive extraction appears as wilderness-like deregulation. Frontiers arise in interstices of collaboration among legitimate and illegitimate partners — military and bandits, gangsters and firms, builders and spoilers — tending to erase separations between law and theft, governance and violence, use and destruction *(attributed)*.

Key attributed claims:

- Frontiers are **not natural borders** discovered at the edge; they are **projects** that fabricate geographic and temporal experience of the “not yet” mapped or regulated — including the unplanned as a planned effect.
- Frontiers **make resources** (and frontier subjects): gold, birds’ nests, incense wood, ironwood, plywood logs, pulp plantations appear as inventable objects between monoculture plantation order and wild frontier plunder — each side constitutive of the other.
- Accumulation here is less “primitive” in the archaic sense than **wild** — proliferation out of control (cancer/nuclear metaphor in Tsing’s opening).
- The frontier is a **traveling theory** / foreign form requiring translation into Kalimantan (US 1849 gold rush, Far West, Sarmiento’s Latin American frontier, Turner’s nation-making frontier, postwar techno-frontier of endless development).
- Legal and illegal extraction are **systemic complements**, not opposites; *penyingso* and similar carriers move frontier culture.
- “Tragedy of the commons” discourse can itself become a **tragedy** when it licenses emptying landscapes of people and non-timber values (dipterocarp simplification; industrial timber boom from the 1970s via Japanese *sogo shosha* and New Order arrangements including APKINDO / Bob Hasan).

Empirical window in *Friction*: South Kalimantan under and after the New Order — roads that empty territory, armed property simulacra (e.g. swallow-nest claims), water-pump gold mining and frontier masculinity, 1997 smoke/fires, cascading ecological sterilizations, confiscations at Kalawan, sensory assault of an out-of-control landscape. Commodity-chain interlude: Kalimantan coal to India held together by frictional cultural economies (sign-language port loading, bribes, gangs after army withdrawal).

## Semantic History

Builds on conquest models, Turner, Sarmiento, development techno-frontier, and “salvage frontier” / zone of captation language. Tsing’s contribution is ethnographic-theoretical: frontier as **scale-linked cultural production** under Indonesian developmentalism and crisis, not stage theory of empty land.

## Operationalizations

Case ethnography and historical sequence (Kalimantan was not always a frontier). Resource lists; plantation vs wild pair; legal-illegal pairing; crisis continuity with “development.”

## Applications

Resource frontiers in Indonesia and comparative gold-rush / Amazon analogies Tsing herself stages. Links to [[economy-of-appearances]] when frontier wealth must be staged for investors (Bre-X).

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of reifying frontier as universal stage; Tsing stresses contingency, imagination, and nonlinear leaps. Keep distinct from international state borders. Meratus highland cases must not be collapsed with West/Central Kalimantan Dayak–Madurese conflict news Tsing reports as elsewhere.
