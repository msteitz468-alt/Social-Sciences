# Communication Out of Character

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]*, Ch. V, 1956) argued that underneath the **[[working-consensus|working consensus]]** run less apparent currents which, if officially communicated, would contradict and discredit officially projected definitions. Surreptitious communications are **not** claimed as more "real reality" than official ones; dual involvement must be carefully managed (attributed).

Four types:

| Type | Sense (Goffman, attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Treatment of the absent** | Backstage derogation (or secret praise) of the audience; mock role-play; different terms of address vs reference; last-moment staging that profanes without quite being caught |
| **Staging talk** | Shop talk about sign equipment, lines, audiences, disruptions, post-mortems — shared dramaturgical climate across roles |
| **Team collusion** | Secret communication during the show that does not threaten the fostered illusion: staging cues, high signs, coded phrases, derisive collusion, knowing looks |
| **Realigning actions** | Temporary unofficial realignments the audience *may* half-receive: feelers, guarded disclosures, double-talk, fraternization, unbending, line-crossing, confession/group therapy, convivial sparring |

Team collusion: "any collusive communication which is carefully conveyed in such a way as to cause no threat to the illusion that is being fostered for the audience" (attributed). Performers give surreptitious expression to **multiple versions of reality**, each tending to be incompatible with the others.

## Semantic History

Feeds later work on backstage talk, emotional labor's hidden transcripts, and workplace resistance — often without Goffman's four-type structure.

## Operationalizations

Record backstage vs frontstage reference terms; code staging cues; observe unofficial feelers that can be disavowed.

## Applications

Service trades; conjugal teams; civil service and political performance; hospitals and schools; convivial interaction among equals as a laboratory of temporary betrayals. Links: [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]], [[performance-team]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of reading out-of-character talk as the only authentic layer; Goffman rejects that ontology. Ethical issues in studying collusion and derogation.
