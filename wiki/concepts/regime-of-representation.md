# Regime of representation

In [[escobar-arturo|Escobar]]'s *[[escobar-encountering-development-1995|Encountering Development]]* (attributed), a **regime of representation** is the organized system through which a part of the world is made knowable, speakable, and intervenable — for development, the postwar regime that produced the [[third-world|Third World]] as underdeveloped. Representation here is not passive depiction but productive of social reality (in a Foucauldian register).

## Definition as Coined

Escobar uses the term for the ensemble linking: (1) forms of knowledge that construct objects (poverty, GNP, the peasant, the Third World woman); (2) techniques and institutions of power (planning, missions, projects); (3) forms of subjectivity (identities of developed/underdeveloped). The development regime of representation is his central object.

## Semantic History

"Regime of representation" draws on cultural studies and poststructuralist language about how media and expertise frame the world; Escobar specializes it to development's historical formation after 1945 and to the possibility of a [[post-development|postdevelopment]] regime.

## Operationalizations

Case chapters operationalize the concept via food/hunger planning, rural development, women-in-development, and sustainable development — each a local center where representation and power meet.

## Applications

Key to [[development-discourse]] and [[escobar-encountering-development-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Same class of critiques as discourse-centered development theory: risk of idealism; ambiguity of "representation" for readers expecting semiotic or media theory only (see [[post-development-debate]]).
