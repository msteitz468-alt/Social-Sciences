# Taste for Necessity

Bourdieu's term in *[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]* for the working-class ethos that **makes a virtue of necessity** — preferring the functional, the substantial, and the immediately gratifying, and rejecting formal aesthetic refinement as pretension.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, Distinction, “The Choice of the Necessary.”)* What statistics record as systems of needs is “nothing other than the coherence of the choices of a habitus.” The taste of necessity favours filling, economical foods and practical interiors; it is not a simple deduction from current income (foremen with higher average household income than skilled workers still show worker-like consumption structures in Bourdieu’s comparisons). The **principle of conformity** — “Who does she think she is?” / “That’s not for the likes of us” — polices pretension, especially among men in culture, language, and clothing.

## Semantic History

Contrasts with the taste of liberty/luxury and with Kantian disinterestedness. Related to older ideas of popular realism but theorized as **dominated aesthetic**: recognition of the dominant hierarchy even in refusal, not an autonomous popular art. Distinct from romantic celebrations of working-class culture.

## Operationalizations

Food budgets and dish preferences; décor adjectives (clean, tidy, easy to maintain); clothing and body-care indices; refusal of formal experimentation in art; conformity statements; class contrasts in photo aesthetics.

## Applications

Anchors the third pole of *Distinction*’s class-habitus typology; pairs with effects of domination (substitutes, educational classifier, recognition without knowledge). Used in later class-culture and consumption research.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of essentializing “the” working class; under-representation of unskilled workers and exclusion of farmers in the main sample; may underplay internal diversity, youth subcultures, and later omnivorous working-class practices. Always date-stamp to documentation moment.
