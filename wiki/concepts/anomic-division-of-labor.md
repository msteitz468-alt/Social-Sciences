# Anomic division of labor

## Definition as Coined

In *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|The Division of Labor in Society]]* (1893, Book III), [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] identified the **anomic division of labor** as the first of three **abnormal forms** — cases where specialization fails to produce solidarity "because the relations of the organs are not regulated, because they are in a state of anomy" (attributed). It is *not* the mere existence of harmful occupations (which he excluded as "differentiation pure and simple," not a true division of labor); it is the absence of the rules that normally coordinate differentiated functions. Durkheim treated it as pathology illuminating physiology: "Pathology, here as elsewhere, is a valuable aid of physiology."

This 1893 sense — a **regulatory deficit between differentiated social functions/organs** — is the textual origin of Durkheim's *anomie* (see [[anomie]]), distinct in unit of analysis from the individual-appetite anomie of *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]* (1897).

## Semantic History

| Work | Sense of anomie |
|---|---|
| *Division of Labor* (1893) | Deficient regulation **between functions/organs** — industrial crises, class conflict |
| *Suicide* (1897) | Deficient regulation of **individual desires/aspirations** — see [[anomic-suicide]] |

Both share "deficient regulation/normlessness"; the 1893 form is structural/organizational, the 1897 form appetitive/individual.

## Operationalizations

Durkheim's illustrative cases (attributed):

- **Industrial and commercial crises / business failures** ("From 1845 to 1869, failures increased 70%") as "partial breaks in organic solidarity."
- **Capital–labor conflict**: as industry specializes (medieval guild fraternity → 15th-c. separation of masters and workers into distinct "orders" → large-scale factory industry, e.g. the Van-Robais works with 1,692 workers), class antagonism intensifies rather than solidarity.
- **Fragmentation of science** ("Alexandrianism") as an epistemic analogue — specialists losing the "lively sense of unity" ([[comte-auguste|Comte]]).

Causal account: regulation is normally the spontaneous crystallization of repeated, prolonged contact ("contiguity") between functions; anomie occurs where contact is too intermittent, or where the market has grown so vast that "production becomes unbridled and unregulated," producing periodic crises.

## Applications

- One of the three abnormal forms alongside the [[forced-division-of-labor|forced]] form and an under-coordinated third form — all failures to realize [[organic-solidarity]], not refutations of it.
- Durkheim's rebuttal (attributed) of [[comte-auguste|Comte]]/Espinas's view that the division of labor is inherently "dispersive": apparent decline of cohesion is the *substitution* of organic for mechanical solidarity plus a **regulatory** deficit, not decay from specialization as such.
- The "worker as machine" complaint is, for Durkheim, a symptom of the anomic form, not of specialization normally functioning; the remedy is better regulation, not layering general education atop machine-like labor.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Rests on secondary historical sources on French guilds/industry (footnoted, not Durkheim's own data) — attributed.
- The claim that adequate regulation would dissolve capital–labor conflict is a normative-functionalist bet criticized by conflict theorists (cf. Simpson's Marxist objection that regulation short of abolishing the profit system cannot cure it — see [[durkheim-division-of-labor-study]]).
