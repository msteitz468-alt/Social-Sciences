# Singularization

## Definition as Coined

In [[kopytoff-igor|Kopytoff]]’s SLOT essay (1986), **singularization** is culture’s **counterdrive** to [[commoditization]]: commoditization homogenizes value through extended exchangeability; culture discriminates, setting some things apart as unique, sacred, non-exchangeable, or narrowly exchangeable *(Kopytoff)*.

> "To be a non-commodity is to be 'priceless' in the full possible sense of the term, ranging from the uniquely valuable to the uniquely worthless."

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Kopytoff 1986 | Processual opposite of commoditization; Durkheimian sacred; moral economy |
| Appadurai 1986 | Enclaved commodities; decommoditization from above; less rigid binary (middle zones) |
| Later consumer studies | Often narrowed to personalization — keep Kopytoff’s moral-economy sense primary |

## Operationalizations

Mechanisms *(Kopytoff, attributed)*:

- **Public/cultural preclusion** from commodity status (symbolic inventory: monuments, ritual objects, chiefly insignia).
- **State/royal monopolies** expanding sacred power onto objects (albino elephants of Siam; British whales; African spotted-cat regalia; Star of India as crown jewel).
- **Restricted commodity spheres** (Tiv prestige sphere; kula closed sphere of armbands/bracelets — high singularization).
- **Terminal commoditization** — sale allowed once, resale prohibited (prescription medicines; medieval indulgences).
- **Individual singularization** — personal attachment, heirlooms, “pet” objects in complex societies.
- **Sacralizing diversions** of former trade goods into ritual paraphernalia (Suku European mugs).

## Applications

- Slave career: reinsertion as singularization after commodity phase *(Kopytoff)*.
- Eastern Solomon sacred canoes and ritual bowls withdrawn from sale after aesthetic/ritual transformation *(Davenport)*.
- Relics oscillating between person-status and commodity phase *(Geary)*.
- Bayly: kingship as mediator of singularization–commoditization for Indian cloth *(Bayly)*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Singularization ≠ automatic high prestige (lowly non-tradeables exist, e.g. Aghem manioc). Scalar, not binary. Appadurai flags middle-zone cases that permanently commoditize singularities *(Appadurai)*.
