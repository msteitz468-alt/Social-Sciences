# Egoistic suicide

## Definition as Coined

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] (*[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*, Book II chs. 2–3) names **egoism** the state in which the individual ego asserts itself to excess against the social ego, and **egoistic** the special type of suicide springing from **excessive individualism** / **insufficient [[social-integration|integration]]**.

Master formula: *suicide varies inversely with the degree of integration of the social groups of which the individual forms a part* — religious, domestic, and political. Excessive individualism not only removes a protective collective force but itself generates a suicidogenic inclination. Private-life shocks appear as causes; the individual yields because society has made him a ready prey.

Mechanism (attributed): as groups weaken, personal goals outweigh community goals; civilized “social man” (superimposed on physical man) loses objective foundation; collective currents of depression and “social malaise” form; the agonizing question becomes “to what purpose?”

## Semantic History

Coined as a technical type in 1897; later often loosely used for “loneliness suicide.” Keep Durkheim’s sense tied to **group integration**, not mere private unhappiness. Contrasts with [[altruistic-suicide]] (excessive integration) and is kindred to but independent of [[anomic-suicide]] (regulation axis).

## Operationalizations

| Domain | Durkheim’s comparative claim (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Religion** | Protestant rates > Catholic; Jews generally lower still; not dogma about suicide but strength of confessional society (common practices, free inquiry) |
| **Family** | Age-corrected unmarried higher than married; **children** more protective than conjugal society alone; family density inverse to departmental suicide; childless wives aggravated in France |
| **Political society** | Popular wars/revolutions/elections that concentrate passions lower suicide; not all wars (dynastic campaigns weaker) |
| **Education** | Correlates with suicide within confessions but is “innocent” — learning and suicide both track loss of religious cohesion |

Method tools: multi-country confession tables; Swiss canton nationality controls; **coefficient of preservation**; rejection of matrimonial selection as principal cause of married immunity.

## Applications

- [[durkheim-suicide-study]] core type
- Modern rise: Book III treats egoistic (with anomic) development as the **morbid** currents of the 19th century
- Remedy: restore group grip — religion/family/nation protect as integrated societies, not by special dogmas; [[occupational-group]] as modern substitute when those weaken

## Critiques and Limitations

- Confessional rankings are period moral statistics; later demography has refined and challenged them.
- Sex claims (woman “less impregnated with sociability”) are attributed period theory.
- Ecological leap from group rates to individual egoism remains a standing methodological debate.
- England’s low Anglican rate is Durkheim’s own confirming exception (more integrated Protestant church) — treat as his argument, not independent validation.
