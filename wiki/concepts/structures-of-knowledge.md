# Structures of Knowledge

## Definition as Coined

In the Fernand Braudel Center / [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]] program, **structures of knowledge** are the organized ways of knowing — disciplines, the two cultures (science vs humanities), conceptual fashions — treated as *structures of the historical system*, not free-floating ideas. Wallerstein's introduction (2004): knowledge is "itself both systemic and historical."

## Semantic History

Overlaps with [[sociology-of-knowledge]] but system-historicized at world scale. Part II of the 2004 volume is devoted to constructed knowledge (Collins, Mamdani, Santos, Abu-Lughod, Aymard, Wallerstein, Trouillot).

## Operationalizations

Histories of concepts (Wallerstein on post-1945 social-science vocabularies); area-studies regimes (Mamdani); epistemologies of the South (Santos); North Atlantic universals (Trouillot).

## Applications

Unifying frame for Part II of *The Modern World-System in the Longue Durée*; program aim to overcome the two-cultures split.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of functionalizing all knowledge as system-reproduction; underplays internal scientific autonomy; culture-wars and science-wars reception is polarized.
