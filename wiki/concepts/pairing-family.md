# Pairing Family

## Definition as Coined

The **pairing family** (Engels/Morgan: *Paarungsfamilie*) is the transitional household form in which one man and one woman pair for a more or less extended period, but without the exclusive, indissoluble, male-dominated character of civilization’s [[monogamy|monogamous family]]. Engels places it in upper savagery / lower–middle barbarism, still typically under [[mother-right]], with marriage easily dissolved and children remaining with the mother.

## Semantic History

A Victorian stage label between [[group-marriage]] and patriarchal/monogamous forms. Not identical to modern “pair-bond” evolutionary psychology or to Christian companionate marriage — those are different discourses.

## Operationalizations

Relative sexual exclusivity vs group marriage; bride-price and consent customs as signs of pairing; continued matrilineal reckoning; absence of full paternal power (*patria potestas*).

## Applications

*Origin* ch. II: pairing family as the form within which wealth accumulation and the overthrow of mother-right become possible, paving the way for monogamy as property form.

## Critiques and Limitations

Stage placement and universality are contested ([[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]). Ethnographic pair bonds appear under many kinship regimes without implying Morgan’s ladder.
