# Stimulus Diffusion

[[kroeber-alfred-l|Kroeber]]'s term for a culture-history situation in which **diffusion and invention enter equally**: knowledge that something exists or that a problem has been solved elsewhere stimulates local invention of a different mechanism for a similar result. Also called **stimulus invention** or **idea diffusion**. Primary vault treatment: *[[kroeber-culture-patterns-processes-1963|Culture Patterns & Processes]]*, Culture Processes §154.

## Definition as Coined

Kroeber (attributed) distinguished ordinary diffusion — both principle and mechanism transferred (e.g. Australian ballot, automobile) — from cases where the mechanism is hard to accept or not fully learned: the **idea** may fail, *or* it may penetrate while the receiving culture "devises another mechanism for the desired result." Thus reinvention is stimulated by contact.

> "There is one type of situation in culture history that is interesting because diffusion and invention enter into it equally. It can be called either stimulus invention or idea diffusion."

> "Stimulus diffusion accordingly is also stimulus invention. Therein lies its interest."

He held frequency may be **undercounted**: results look original; historical factors are forgotten; occurrences may be more numerous than the record shows.

Cases he treated as illustrations (attributed, not primary ethnography for this vault): **porcelain** (idea of Chinese ware stimulating European reinvention of materials/methods); **Sequoyah** and the Cherokee syllabary (stimulus of writing without wholesale transfer of English orthography); **Vai** script (Doalu Bukere) under similar stimulus logic; further probable but unproven candidates listed cautiously.

## Semantic History

Named to split the false alternative "pure diffusion vs. pure independent invention" that structured evolutionist and diffusionist polemics. Fits [[historical-particularism|Boasian]] insistence on history without requiring either unilinear stages or pan-Egyptian/pan-Pacific hyperdiffusion. Remains a standard culture-history concept in archaeology and historical anthropology (often without Kroeber's label).

## Operationalizations

Requires evidence of **contact or knowledge of foreign achievement** plus **local mechanical novelty**. Harder to prove than trait diffusion; Kroeber treated many cases as probable rather than demonstrated. Contrasts with reduction-segregation invention (§155: alphabet, calendar splits) and ordinary cumulative invention.

## Applications

- Culture-process taxonomy alongside persistence, invention, loss, and external change/acculturation.
- Explaining partial borrowings and "independent" inventions that are historically prompted.
- Softening ultra-diffusionism and ultra-evolutionism alike ([[diffusionism]], [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Proof standards are high and often unmet (Kroeber admitted). Risk of **post-hoc** "stimulus" stories wherever two similar inventions exist. Does not itself theorize power, refusal, or colonial force in contact — those enter more in his acculturation chapter.
