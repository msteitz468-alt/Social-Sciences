# Surface Acting and Deep Acting

## Definition as Coined

In *[[hochschild-managed-heart-1983|The Managed Heart]]* Ch 3, [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]] argues that in both forms the actor has learned to **intervene** — feelings do not simply erupt (attributed):

| Form | Intervention | Analogy |
|---|---|---|
| **Surface acting** | Shape the outward appearance of a feeling | Raised eyebrow, forced smile; Coquelin-school "art of the surface"; Goffmanian [[impression-management]] |
| **Deep acting** | Create the inner shape of a feeling | Stanislavski Method: emotion memory; the "if" / "as if"; trained imagination |

Two routes to deep acting (attributed): (1) **direct exhortation** of feeling ("don't feel," "muster gratitude") — limited, addresses capacity to duck a signal; (2) **indirect Method** — retrain imagination so feeling follows from reconstructed perception (Stanislavski: never seek the passion for its own sake; think of what went before).

Hochschild: whether separating "me" from face (surface) or "me" from feeling (deep) counts as **estrangement** depends on outer context — honorable on stage, often useful in private life, potentially alienating when placed in unacknowledged profit-and-loss employment (attributed).

## Semantic History

| Source | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Stanislavski, *An Actor Prepares* | Method vs surface school; emotion memory; sincerity beyond true-seeming feeling |
| Goffman | Rich on surface display / traffic rules of interaction; Hochschild: weak on deep acting (Appendix A) |
| Hochschild 1983 | Applies both to private emotion work and commercial emotional labor; corporate training as deep-acting school |
| Post-1983 org. psych. | SA/DA as measurable strategies linked to burnout, job satisfaction |

## Operationalizations

Primary text: qualitative typology + training-room and interview illustration. Later: survey subscales of surface acting (faking) vs deep acting (reappraisal) — not in Hochschild 1983.

## Applications

- **Private:** trying to feel what a funeral or wedding demands; fighting off love; psyching up for a party.
- **Commercial:** Delta smile training, passenger-as-guest/"as if in your living room," anger reframing so the worker's anger is pathologized rather than the situation (attributed FINDINGs from Ch 6–9).
- Link to [[cynical-and-sincere-performance|Goffman's cynical/sincere performance]]: deep acting aims to produce something closer to sincere display by changing the inner feeling.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Stanislavski analogy is illustrative; workers are not professional actors and lack artistic autonomy.
- Corporate "deep acting" can be more intrusive than surface faking — success at deep acting may deepen estrangement from a prior sense of authentic feeling (Ch 9 stances; attributed).
- Binary SA/DA underplays mixed strategies and collective emotional labor (team tone-setting).
