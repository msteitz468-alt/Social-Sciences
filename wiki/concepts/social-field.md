# Social Field (*Champ*)

Bourdieu's concept of a relatively autonomous social space structured by the distribution of forms of capital and by struggles over legitimate principles of vision and division. In *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* the field appears in the theoretical apparatus — relation of [[habitus]] to objective structures; games "in themselves"; artistic and educational examples of misrecognized economy — **more than as a fully developed topology of modern fields**. Those monographs (art, science, law, etc.) are not yet ingested; claims here stay within what *Logic* states or assumes.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu.)* A field is a network or configuration of objective relations between positions defined by distributions of capital and by stakes agents invest in under [[illusio]]. Social fields, products of long slow autonomization, are games "in themselves" not "for themselves": unlike explicit games (pitch, board, rules, referee) that advertise their arbitrariness, entry is by birth not quasi-contract, and investment is total and largely unconscious.

> "By contrast, in the social fields, which are the products of a long, slow process of autonomization, and are therefore, so to speak, games 'in themselves' and not 'for themselves', one does not embark on the game by a conscious act, one is born into the game, with the game"

Being product of a particular class of objective regularities, habitus tends to generate all the "reasonable," "common-sense" behaviours (and only these) possible within those regularities, positively sanctioned because objectively adjusted to the logic characteristic of a particular field whose objective future they anticipate — and to exclude "extravagances" ("not for the likes of us"). An institution, even an economy, is complete only if objectified both in things (logic of a field) and in bodies (durable dispositions to recognize and comply with field demands).

## Semantic History

- **Physical/social-space metaphors and Weberian spheres.** Specialized cultural-production fields are Bourdieu's distinctive development; distinguish from Lewinian psychological field theory and from generic "fieldwork."
- **Scientific field legitimacy (Preface).** Problems and methods constituted as highly legitimate induce respectability and encouragement; sacred texts as tools of recognized power via interpretation; mythologies as powers at stake in social struggles.
- **Economic field condition of capital (ch. 8).** Wealth can function as capital only in relationship with a specifically economic field, presupposing economic institutions and specialized agents; ancient [[kabyle]] economy, Bourdieu argued (citing Finley, Polanyi, Sahlins lines of argument), lacked elementary instruments of that institution.
- **Relatively autonomous fields free dominants from personal strategies.** Mechanisms that impose necessity allow those who command them to dispense with costly person-to-person domination strategies; full institutionalization allows quasi-perfect commensurability via titles (nobility, property, educational qualifications).
- **[[cultural-capital]] as field-relative.** Cultural competence becomes cultural capital only when inserted into objective relations between systems of economic production and systems producing producers (education–family).
- **[[strategic-action-field|Strategic action fields]] (Fligstein & McAdam 2012) — related, not identical.** *[[fligstein-mcadam-theory-of-fields-2012|A Theory of Fields]]* states intense affinity with Bourdieu's project but builds a different apparatus: [[social-skill]] and the "existential function of the social" instead of habitus/capital topology; continuous incumbent–challenger jockeying and SM-style [[episode-of-contention|episodes of contention]]; mandatory embedding in lattices of other fields and the state-as-SAF-system; [[internal-governance-unit|IGUs]]. Use `associated_with` / contrast — **do not equate** *champ* with SAF. See [[strategic-action-fields-theory]].

## Operationalizations

- **Position in a space of capitals.** In *Logic*, mostly theoretical; correspondence analysis and field maps belong mainly to *Distinction* and later field studies — not yet ingested.
- **Habitus–field attunement.** Reasonable behaviours; [[doxa]] as immediate adherence of habitus to field; practical faith as entry condition (rites, examinations).
- **Institutionalization of capital.** Degree of objectification of capital distinguishes modes of domination (personal vs mechanism-mediated).
- **Scientific and artistic fields** as examples of autonomized games and of denial of economy taking refuge in art/culture.

## In *Homo Academicus* (1984)

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, [[bourdieu-homo-academicus-1984]]; see [[bourdieu-homo-academicus-study]].)* *Homo Academicus* applies field theory to the [[academic-field|academic field]], framed through Kant's "conflict of the faculties" (*Le conflit des facultés*): the higher, temporally dominant faculties (theology, law, medicine) hold government's confidence and least autonomy, while the "lower faculty" (rational/empirical science) is left to scholarly reason alone. Bourdieu argues the university field is homologous to the field of power but retains its own logic, distributing along a **chiasmatic structure** — one pole scientifically dominant but socially dominated (letters, sciences), the other scientifically dominated but temporally dominant (law, medicine) — which explains differential weight given to research versus teaching across faculties. Cross-link: [[academic-field]].

## In *The State Nobility* (1989)

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, [[bourdieu-state-nobility-1996]]; see [[bourdieu-state-nobility-study]].)*
The fullest field monograph now ingested: the [[grandes-ecoles]] shown to constitute a genuine
field with detectable **field effects** ("the transformations undergone by the École Normale and
the École Polytechnique following the creation of the ENA"), structured **chiasmatically** and
homologously to the [[field-of-power]] — the concept of a meta-field of capitals here receives
its first full theoretical and empirical elaboration. The "On Method" appendix supplies the
method's clearest statement: field construction precedes measurement, and random sampling is
rejected for **structural sampling** (representativity as structural homology). See
[[field-of-power]] and [[correspondence-analysis]].

## Applications

- **Honour and agrarian social space ([[kabyle]]).** Not a modern "field" in the full sense, but a configuration of capital (fighting strength, economic, symbolic) and stakes of *nif*/*h'urma* with practical kinship networks — the ethnographic base against which modern fields are contrasted.
- **Educational and cultural production.** Educational qualifications as objectified cultural capital with conventional fixed value; artistic field as island of disinterestedness (alluded).
- **Science as field of belief.** Participant objectification uses membership of the science field to objectify other games of belief.
- **Full field monographs**: [[bourdieu-homo-academicus-1984|Homo Academicus]] and [[bourdieu-state-nobility-1996|The State Nobility]] now ingested; *Rules of Art* etc. still queued.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Boundary of a field.** How to decide where a field ends is a classic reception problem; *Logic* offers autonomization narrative more than decision rules.
- **Autonomy degree.** Relative autonomy can become a free parameter explaining any residual variance.
- **Circularity of capital and field.** Positions defined by capital distributions while capital value depends on field — acknowledged theoretical circle; critics ask for independent anchors.
- **Thinness in this source.** *Logic* is not a field monograph; over-reading modern field topology from honour-economy chapters would violate source limits.
