# Industrial Type of Society

## Definition as Coined

Spencer’s polar opposite to the [[militant-type-of-society]]. In the *Principles* industrial-type chapter he argued that organization proper to peaceful industrial life is based on:

- **Voluntary cooperation** (not compulsory).
- Relations of **contract** (not status).
- Government that is mainly **negatively regulative** — restraining aggression rather than prescribing the how/where/when of private activities.
- The state as defender of **individuality** rather than owner of the individual.
- Greater **plasticity**, private associations, free exchange, and (as remote tendency) looser federal arrangements.

proposed_by: [[spencer-herbert]] · contrasts_with: [[militant-type-of-society]] · part_of: [[social-evolutionism]]

Qualifications Spencer stated: pure industrialism was still little experienced; industrial traits are often **hidden** by residual militancy; **industrialism ≠ mere industriousness** and is not identical with socialism (he treated authoritarian industrial regimentation as militant in principle). Habitual **mingling** of types in real societies.

## Semantic History

Not the same as later “industrial society” in modernization theory or economic-sector definitions without attribution. Spencer’s “industrial” means the constitutional pattern of peaceful productive cooperation, not factories alone — though factory and free-labour chapters in Part IV supply the sustaining-system detail. Relation to Maine’s [[status-to-contract]] is **affinity of language** (status/contract appear in Spencer’s militant/industrial contrast); do not merge without argument. Maine’s formula is a comparative-jurisprudence thesis about family-derived personal Status (*[[maine-ancient-law-1861|Ancient Law]]* 1861); Spencer’s is a polar constitutional type for whole societies under war vs peaceful industry. See [[maine-henry-sumner]], [[militant-type-of-society]].

## Operationalizations

Trait cluster (Spencer): voluntary cooperation; contract; negative regulation; representative and justice-oriented political forms; free labour; decline of ceremonial propitiation; women’s labour less “drudge”-like where men are not absorbed in war/hunt (his comparative claim); character traits he associated with industrialism (independence, enterprise, less uncritical loyalty/faith — attributed characterology). Metamorphosis: prolonged peace approaches the type; war reverts.

## Applications

*Principles* Part II industrial-type chapter; Part IV free labour and contract as correlatives of the industrial constitution; ecclesiastical prospect of weakened sacerdotal power under industrialism; political widening of councils/representation under industrial conditions (vs. militancy narrowing them).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Spencer’s own Britain was mixed; Andreski notes weakness on contemporary industrial civilization.
- Normative valorization of the industrial pole (liberal anti-statism) shapes the type.
- Polar construct; incomplete realization expected by Spencer himself.
- Not a wiki-voice prediction that peace produces liberal democracy.
