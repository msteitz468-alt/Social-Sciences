# Mother-Right and Father-Right

> [[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]]’s structural reworking in *[[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-1950|African Systems of Kinship and Marriage]]* (1950). Distinct from Victorian evolutionary [[mother-right]].

## Definition as Coined

In ASKAM, **mother-right** and **father-right** name contrasting **jural patterns** in marriage and affiliation — rights over wife and children; which kin receive marriage payments and hold residual claims; how the unit of mother-and-children is incorporated — **not** a unilinear historical sequence from matriarchy to patriarchy.

Radcliffe-Brown’s ideal-type poles (attributed):

**Mother-right (Nayar-type reconstruction):** matrilineal lineage (*taravad*) as joint corporate property unit; husbands not resident as jural household heads; *sambandham* visiting unions without husband’s jural rights over wife/children; parental family as co-resident household absent; jural/religious/property relations confined to the female line. “In the purest form of the system a man does not acquire by marriage any rights of possession over his wife and her children.” Sibling group remains the permanent unit; rights over children rest with mother and her brothers and sisters.

**Father-right (Roman *manus*/*patria potestas* type):** possessive rights over the woman ceremonially transferred from father/guardian to husband (with *coemptio*-like payment); children under father’s power; mother’s kin no rights over children. “Possession of a woman, and therefore of the children of her body, are surrendered by marriage to her husband and his kin. … The jural bonds between a woman and her siblings are severed by her marriage.”

He held mother-right and father-right are opposites yet **varieties of a single type**: extreme emphasis on matrilineal or patrilineal lineage, both contrasting with cognatic systems. Differentiating principle is not strength of the mother–child bond (near-universal intimacy) but which marriage solution accommodates claims of husband vs woman’s siblings.

| **R-B 1950 ASKAM** | Structural typology for comparative analysis of rights transfer at marriage; four ideal types with cognatic and [[double-descent]] poles |
| Later kinship theory | Prefers finer terms (matriliny, patriliny, complementary filiation, residence rules); avoids mother-/father-right as totalizing labels |

### R-B’s position against evolutionary mother-right schemes

**Position/theoretical — not fact:** Radcliffe-Brown attacked the famous speculation that earliest society was matriarchy/mother-right as promiscuity with unknown father, or kinship through females only with no social fatherhood. He held: no known societies past or present of that kind; it is “a pure product of imagination” (citing Robertson’s *History of America*). Classic mother-right exemplars (Nayar, Menangkabau, Khasi) are advanced, literate, cultivated peoples — “typical instances of mother-right are found, not amongst the more primitive peoples, but in advanced or relatively advanced societies” — undercutting stage sequencing. Evolutionary uses of the vocabulary belong on [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]; do not assert mother-right → father-right as wiki-voice history.

## Operationalizations

| Diagnostic | Mother-right pole | Father-right pole |
|---|---|---|
| Rights over wife at marriage | Minimal / none to husband | Transfer of *mund*-like possession to husband (and kin) |
| Rights over children | Mother and her sibling group | Husband and his agnatic kin |
| Sibling group of woman after marriage | Remains primary jural unit | Jural bonds severed; affectionate MB often remains |
| Marriage payment | May be small/absent, or dual forms | Often large; can fix child affiliation (“cattle beget children”) |
| Domestic group | Parental family may be weak/absent as jural household | Parental/patrilineal extended household common |
| Mother-and-children unit | Incorporated in woman’s sibling group | Incorporated in brothers + wives + children group |

**Four ideal types (R-B framework):** (1) father-right, (2) mother-right, (3) purely cognatic systems (Lozi approximation), (4) double lineage systems (Yakö). African cases are mostly approximations and “qualified” systems.

**Payment dual forms:** R-B reported societies where full payment → father-right children; small/no payment → mother-right children (Brass; Nyamwezi *kukwa*/*butende*) — payment operationalizes the contrast *within* one society (see [[marriage-payments]]).

## Applications

ASKAM mappings (attributed):

- **Nearest African mother-right:** Ashanti (Fortes) — continued solidarity of sibling group of one flesh and blood; parental family exists but is not the sole standardized domestic form; lineage property and female-line religious bonds; undergoing European-influenced modification. Fortes: not equal double descent (*ntoro* ≠ corporate patri-sib).
- **Qualified Central African mother-right (Richards):** lower Congo type A (male matrilineage keeps property/cult but surrenders certain rights over marrying women); Bemba enlarged domestic group of man, wife, married daughters + husbands/children with later fission; Ila “mixed,” farther toward father-right despite matrilineal clans. R-B/Fortes/Richards: compromise systems show more tensions than clearly defined jural systems; mother-right per se not more difficult than father-right.
- **Father-right Nguni (Swazi, Zulu):** possession of children by marriage payment; infancy with mother; full incorporation in father’s lineage at adolescence; parallel timing to Ashanti/Congo post-adolescence shift toward mother’s brothers under matriliny.
- **Qualification of crude father-right:** Gluckman following Evans-Pritchard — even in strongly patrilineal Nuer and Zulu, child’s social position is determined *through the mother*; children of one mother more closely connected than same-father different-mother children; intimate personal bond still with mother (Roman *materfamilias* analogy).
- **Double descent:** Yakö and Nuba as neither pure mother- nor father-right but cross-segmentation of rights (see [[double-descent]]).
- **Cognatic foil:** Lozi minimal unilineal emphasis (Gluckman).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Binary risks reintroducing evolutionary baggage even when authors intend structural types.
- Ethnographic cases are mixed (“qualified” systems); prefer specific rights analysis (who may discipline whom; who inherits; who receives payments; residence careers).
- Nayar type is a compiled secondary reconstruction for typology, not ASKAM fieldwork.
- Fortes’s complementary filiation analysis and later descent-vs-alliance debates supersede mother-/father-right as total labels for many specialists.
- Link evolutionary uses strictly to [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]] and historical [[mother-right]]; never promote stage schemes to wiki voice.
