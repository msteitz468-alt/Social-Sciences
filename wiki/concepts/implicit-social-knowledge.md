# Implicit Social Knowledge

## Definition as Coined

[[taussig-michael|Taussig]] defines **implicit social knowledge** (ISK) as “an essentially inarticulable and imageric nondiscursive knowing of social relationality,” and asks how history enters healing as sorcery through that knowing (attributed). It is less a stock of beliefs than techniques for interpreting “shades of meaning of social situations” (he invokes Henry James’s “situation”): interpretation enters the situation it reads (attributed).

In the Putumayo, he claimed, sensitivity to **envy** is “as ever-present and as necessary as the air we breathe” — foundation of shamanic discourse and a sixth-sense antenna for social danger (attributed).

## Semantic History

- **Ch. 24 History as sorcery.** Colonial and preconquest dead, Capuchin crusades, rubber memory, and highland–lowland song activate pasts as magical forces; Benjaminian dialectical images as shocks that set thinking in motion (attributed).
- **Ch. 25 Envy.** Envy as discourse and purgation (yagé as epic theater); uncertainty of accusation; Christmas violence and market *sal*/*serrucho* figures as Taussig recounts.
- **Chs 27–28 Montage / healer.** ISK is jointly constructed in shaman–patient difference: shaman’s mute certainty and patient’s voice produce “credible impossibility” of visions (attributed). Contrast with Lévi-Strauss’s shamanism-as-inverted-psychoanalysis and with Evans-Pritchard’s lucidity that can invite totalizing misreadings (attributed critiques).

Adjacent but not identical to [[habitus]] (Bourdieu) or Raymond Williams’s “structure of feeling” (Taussig cites Williams elsewhere in the Andes moral-topography discussion).

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic: envy talk, sorcery imputation, yagé night form (interruptions, song, vomiting), memory of rubber and conquest. Not a survey scale.

## Applications

- [[yage]] healing of colonists and Indians
- [[taussig-shamanism-colonialism-study]] methodological program
- Critique of academic “ritual of explanation” that orders chaos into authority (attributed)

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Hard to pin down by design.** An “inarticulable” object resists standard operationalization; risk of unfalsifiable depth claims.
- **Fieldwork positionality.** Taussig as patient/participant in yagé nights is part of the data — strength for montage method, limit for external reliability.
