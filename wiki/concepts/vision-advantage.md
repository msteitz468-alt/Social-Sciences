# Vision Advantage

*(Attributed to [[burt-ronald-s|Burt]], *[[burt-brokerage-and-closure-2005|Brokerage and Closure]]* 2005.)* The hypothesized **active ingredient** in returns to network [[brokerage]]: people whose networks span [[structural-holes|structural holes]] sit at higher risk of **detecting and developing good ideas** because information and practice are more homogeneous within groups than between them.

## Definition as Coined

Burt argues (attributed) that brokerage’s performance association is not magic of the hole itself but a **vision** mechanism: early access to diverse information, beliefs, and behavior; option to select and synthesize alternatives; better sight of how to implement. He likens the mechanism to “adding radar to aircraft, or an MRI to medical care.”

## Semantic History

Builds on the 1992 [[structural-holes]] program’s information benefits; *Brokerage and Closure* names and centers **vision advantage** as stylized fact 2 (mechanism of brokerage returns), distinct from mere contact volume.

## Operationalizations

Ego-network [[network-constraint|constraint]] (low = more holes) correlated with evaluation/promotion/compensation and with measures of idea quality and uptake in manager samples (Burt 2005, Chs. 1–2 — single-program multi-sample evidence).

## Applications

Corporate managers (supply-chain, investment banking, electronics, multi-source evaluation programs); entrepreneurial bridging; critique of network-service business models that optimize volume.

## Critiques and Limitations

Causal identification is difficult (networks as context, not actors); reverse causation and personality confounds discussed by Burt; creativity claims are program-internal and contested outside network sociology.
