# Ḥasham

## Definition as Coined

In *[[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-1986|Veiled Sentiments]]*, [[abu-lughod-lila|Abu-Lughod]] analyzes Awlad ʿAli **ḥasham** as **voluntary deference** to those higher in the honor hierarchy. She calls it the **honor of the weak**: those who cannot fully realize autonomy-based honor (notably women and juniors) achieve moral worth by showing modesty toward seniors, especially senior male agnates. Coerced obedience is scorned; voluntary deference is honorable.

In daily parlance, forms of the root *ḥashama* cluster around two poles: an **internal state** (discomfort, shyness, embarrassment, shame in the presence of the more powerful — often "ashamed from/in front of" particular others) and a **voluntary set of acts** conforming to the modesty code (veiling and modest dress; downcast eyes; formal posture; restraint in eating, smoking, talking, laughing, joking). Closely linked is *ʿagl* (reasonableness/self-mastery): the ideal woman is *ʿāgla* — soft-spoken, knowing when to listen; the negative poles are willful (*gāwya*) or sexually shameless (*qḥaba*).

## Semantic History

Modern standard Arabic dictionaries gloss related forms as modesty, shame, shyness, propriety — but Abu-Lughod, following Riesman on Fulani analogues, warns that convenient English terms create false knowledge. She rejects Antoun-style dictionary etymologies of *maḥāshim*/pudenda for local analysis, citing Abu-Zahra's critique that classical glosses may not match dialectal use. Among Awlad ʿAli as documented 1978–80, meaning is sought in **everyday hierarchical practice**, not classical lexica. Distinct from Benedict's [[shame-culture]] binary and Elias's [[threshold-of-shame]].

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic, not psychometric:

- Who *taḥashsham* from whom (young wives asked this to map community hierarchy)
- Smoking as male-to-male index of ḥasham; **veiling as female-to-male index**
- Silence and mood shift when respected adult men enter women's space
- Avoidance and sexual/generational segregation justified as comfort of the weak

Sexual modesty and the veiling matrix (who covers before whom) are treated as instances of hierarchical respect, not free-floating "Islamic" custom alone.

## Applications

Load-bearing for [[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-study]]; gender hierarchy among [[awlad-ali]]; comparative Mediterranean honor/modesty discussions; feminist ethnography of deference as moral agency of dependents.

## Critiques and Limitations

Emic term given heavy etic theoretical work (hierarchy + ideology of honor). Single-community specification; not a universal Muslim women's psychology. Honor-killing logic (insubordination destroying family moral standing) is Abu-Lughod's interpretation of rare extreme cases, not wiki-voice prevalence.
