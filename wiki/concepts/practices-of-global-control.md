# Practices of Global Control

Sassen's term for the work of running a global economic system: the specialized services, financial innovations, management and control operations, and organizational networks through which dispersed economic activity is coordinated. The [[global-city|global city]] is theorized as a *production site* for this capability, not merely a place where headquarters "reside."

## Definition as Coined

In *[[sassen-global-city-2001|The Global City]]*, "practices of global control" and related phrases ("capability for global control," "work of running a global economic system") name the practical infrastructure of globalization — much of it outside the formal corporation, in specialized service markets.

## Semantic History

Part of Sassen's 1980s–90s conceptual architecture; becomes a "comfortable heuristic" by the 2001 edition (her phrasing). Adjacent to but distinct from state-centered regulation and from simple HQ geography.

## Operationalizations

Studied via producer-service growth, financial-market internationalization, cross-border affiliate networks, and the occupational structure supporting them (including low-wage servicing jobs).

## Applications

Explains why capital mobility and firm dispersal increase, rather than decrease, demand for certain urban agglomerations.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Control" can be read as conspiratorial or totalizing; Sassen's use is infrastructural and production-oriented. Empirical scope depends on observable service and finance complexes.
