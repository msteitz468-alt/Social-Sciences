# Poison Oracle (*benge*)

## Definition as Coined

In Evans-Pritchard's Azande ethnography, the **poison oracle** (*benge*) is the premier Zande oracle (*soroka*): poison is administered to fowls while questions are put; the fowl's death or survival is the answer. A **second test** (*gingo*) after the first (*bambata sima*) confirms — for a valid verdict one fowl must die and the other live. Men consult about witchcraft, adultery, journeys, hunting, marriage, homestead sites, and political affairs. Decisions on a **prince's** orders have the force of law. (Attributed: E-P 1937, Ch. VIII–IX; glossary App. I.)

E-P reported *benge* also has a "soul" accounting for power to see what a man cannot see. Chemical analysis of his sample (Prof. R. Robinson) found an alkaloid related to strychnine; botanical identity was undetermined. Formerly poison was also given to human beings.

## Semantic History

"Oracle" is etic gloss for Zande *soroka* — techniques supposed to reveal what cannot be discovered (or not for certain) by experiment and logical inference alone. Lesser oracles in E-P's ranking:

| Oracle | Zande | Mechanism (as reported) |
|---|---|---|
| Poison | *benge* | Strychnine-type poison to fowls |
| Termites | *dakpa* | Branches of two trees into termite runs |
| Rubbing-board | *iwa* | Wooden instrument operated by specialist |
| Three-sticks | *mapingo* | Pile of three small sticks |

Witch-doctors are treated as a kind of oracle but **below** *benge* and *dakpa*; highest compliment is prophesying "just like *benge*." Comparative divination literature treats *benge* as a type-case of poison-ordeal / animal-medium divination.

## Operationalizations

**Procedure (summary, E-P):** obtain fowls and poison; early-morning bush seance; address oracle; map death/survival to yes/no; double-test for validity; control for bad poison (*andegi*) and operator virtue. **Male monopoly** in this account; women excluded from *benge*. Taboos (e.g. sexual relations before consultation) condition efficacy.

**Secondary elaborations of failure** (Ch. IX, attributed): bad poison, breached taboos, witchcraft corrupting the oracle, operator error — explanations that absorb contradiction **without abandoning** oracular authority. Creed: "the poison oracle does not err," backed by princely power and tradition.

**Accusation protocol:** name must rest on *benge* (or at least termites') verdict — else ridicule or violence. Wing of the oracle-killed fowl is sent (often via prince's deputy) to the named person to cool witchcraft.

## Applications

Central decision engine of the Zande witchcraft complex in [[evans-pritchard-witchcraft-oracles-magic-study]]; links accusation, vengeance after death, and daily planning. E-P himself consulted oracles as method. Feeds [[rationality-debate]] on system coherence under apparent falsification.

## Critiques and Limitations

Colonial constraint on judicial/lethal uses; E-P as participant-observer who used oracles (methodologically consequential); animal-killing ordeal ethics in later reception; abridgement may thin full 1937 procedural detail; forest gathering of *benge* not observed (failed Congo trip).
