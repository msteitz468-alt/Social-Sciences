# Radicalised Modernity

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (1990) argued that rather than entering **post-modernity** as then popularly labelled, "we are moving into one in which the consequences of modernity are becoming more **radicalised** and **universalised** than before." He reserved "post-modern" for a *possible future institutional order* beyond high modernity — "quite distinct from what is at the moment called by many 'postmodernity.'"

**Table 2** in *Consequences* contrasts conceptions of post-modernity (PM) with **radicalised modernity (RM)** across multiple dimensions (knowledge, progress, epistemology, etc.). Giddens’s preferred diagnosis of the present is RM / **high modernity**, not an epochal postmodern break.

## Semantic History

Parallel to Beck’s [[reflexive-modernization|reflexive modernization]] and [[risk-society]] second-modernity thesis: both reject simple "postmodern" exit and insist on *intensified* modernity. Giddens’s accent falls on institutionalised reflexivity, trust/risk, and time-space; Beck’s on manufactured hazards and individualisation. Consolidated (without full merger) in *Reflexive Modernization* (Beck, Giddens, Lash, 1994).

Do **not** equate with Scott’s [[high-modernism]] (state high-modernist planning ideology) — different concept, different literature.

## Operationalizations

Periodising/diagnostic, not a measurement construct. Indexed via globalisation intensity, abstract-system dependence, high-consequence risk profile, reflexive self-identity projects.

## Applications

- Core position on [[postmodernity-epochal-break-debate]].
- Frames [[globalization]] as inherent to modern institutions, not a post-modern novelty.
- Grounds [[utopian-realism]] politics under high-consequence risk.

## Critiques and Limitations

Contested whether "radicalisation" adequately captures cultural/aesthetic postmodern claims (Lyotard, Baudrillard) or political-economic "late capitalism" readings (Jameson, Harvey). Eurocentric periodisation risks. All such critiques are **positions**.
