# Post-Modern Ethnography

[[tyler-stephen-a|Stephen Tyler]]’s programmatic label in *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* (1986) for an ethnography that abandons realist documentary claims and scientific rhetoric in favor of **[[ethnographic-evocation|evocation]]** — from “document of the occult” to “occult document.”

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed to Tyler 1986.)*

Cooperative fragmentary discourse producing evocation and therapeutic aesthetic integration rather than representation/correspondence. Discourse over text; dialogue/polyphony; reject observer–observed split. Critique of generalization, verification, experiment, truth as correspondence, natural-history [[ethnographic-realism]].

## Semantic History

Part of the broader post-modern turn; Rabinow critically frames post-modernism as a new canon in the same volume. Editors note Tyler draws on pre-modern sources and is “unclassifiable.” Distinct from ACC’s broader [[experimental-moment]] catalogue (which includes realist experiments).

## Operationalizations

Writing practice / aesthetic-epistemic stance. Tyler’s Supplement: **no completed instance** yet; only works “in the right spirit” (Crapanzano, Tedlock, Majnep–Bulmer).

## Applications

Tyler’s manifesto essay as the volume’s radical pole.

## Critiques and Limitations

Asad (power/stiffness); Rabinow (institutions; new canon); incomplete exemplars by Tyler’s own admission; see [[writing-culture-debate]].
