# Teknonymy

## Definition as Coined

**Teknonymy** (Tylor): naming or addressing a parent by reference to the child. [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]] (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, Kinship Usages) reviews Tylor's evolutionary/statistical treatment and argues for **convergent multiple sources** rather than a single developmental law.

## Semantic History

Tylor's 1889 paper linked teknonymy to parent-in-law avoidance and evolutionary sequences; Boasian distributional critique (Lowie) demotes the stage reading while keeping the descriptive term.

## Operationalizations

Presence/absence; who is renamed; relation to avoidance taboos; statistical association with other customs (Tylor) vs historical particularism (Lowie).

## Applications

Kinship Usages chapter of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Lowie: same surface form can arise from different local histories — do not treat as one-stage marker.
