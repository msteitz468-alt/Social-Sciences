# Communication (Luhmann)

## Definition as Coined

For [[luhmann-niklas|Luhmann]], **communication is the elementary operation of social systems**
— the basal process "that produces their elements." His most consequential thesis: society
"consists of communications, not of human beings" or actions. Communication is *not* a
transmission of content from sender to receiver (Luhmann rejects the "transmission" metaphor as
smuggling in too much ontology). It is a **synthesis of three selections**:

1. **Information** — a selection from a range of referential possibilities;
2. **Utterance** (*Mitteilung*) — a selection of how/whether to impart it;
3. **Understanding** (*Verstehen*) — the observation of the **difference** between information
   and utterance.

"The inclusion of this difference [between information and utterance] is what makes
communication communication." Understanding completes the communicative act; **acceptance or
rejection** is a further, distinct selection, not part of the event. Communication is basally
self-referential (each connecting communication tests whether the prior one was understood) and
its smallest unit is "the smallest unit that can be negated."

## Semantic History

Luhmann breaks with the whole action-theoretic tradition (Weber, Parsons) that made sociality a
special case of action. He reverses it: **action is a secondary self-simplification** the
communication system uses to observe and steer itself — "Actions are artifacts of processes of
attribution... the results of observing observers." Attributing action to whole individuals is
"unrealistic" but functional (it reduces complexity and lets the reversible flow of
communication be punctualized into irreversible, connectable events — see [[social-action]]).

Communication is held to be improbable in three ways — that it is understood, that it reaches
beyond those present, and that it is accepted — and three classes of **media** evolve to
overcome these: **language** (understandability), **dissemination media** (writing, printing,
broadcasting — reach), and **symbolically generalized media** (truth, love, money/property,
power/law — acceptance). Luhmann insists communication is not consensus-production and, in
fact, "requires dissent in order to continue" — universal consensus would end the system's
[[autopoiesis]].

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Communication cannot be observed directly, only inferred; the
system must flag communication *as action* in order to observe itself.

## Applications

- The elemental unit of [[social-systems-theory]]; every social system is a communication
  system.
- Grounds Luhmann's rejection of [[symbolic-interactionism]] and of Habermasian communicative
  action as a normative ground (see [[luhmann-habermas-debate]]).
- Distinguishes society (all communication) from interaction systems (communication among the
  co-present).

## Critiques and Limitations

Making communication rather than acting persons the unit of the social is the crux of the
anti-humanism objection: critics hold it dissolves agency, intention, and the speaking subject.
The three-selection definition also competes with rival triads Luhmann explicitly sets aside
(Bühler's language functions; Austin's speech acts), which he faults for asking which function
dominates rather than how communication's unity arises at all.
