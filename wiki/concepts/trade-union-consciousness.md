# Trade-Union Consciousness

## Definition as Coined

In *[[lenin-what-is-to-be-done-1902|What Is to Be Done?]]* (1902), [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] argued that the working class, **exclusively by its own effort**, is able to develop only **trade-union consciousness** (*trade union consciousness*): the necessity of combining in unions, fighting employers, and striving to compel the government to pass necessary labor legislation, etc. He contrasted this with **Social-Democratic consciousness**, which he held could only be brought to workers [[consciousness-from-without|from without]].

He placed systematic 1890s strikes as **class struggle in embryo** but, taken by themselves, only **trade-union** struggles—not yet Social-Democratic—because workers were not and could not be conscious of the irreconcilable antagonism of their interests to the whole modern political and social system. Spontaneous development of the labor movement, he claimed, is pure and simple trade unionism (*Nur-Gewerkschaftlerei*), and “trade unionism means the ideological enslavement of the workers to the bourgeoisie.” Working-class trade-union politics he later called “precisely working class **bourgeois** politics.”

## Semantic History

| Layer | Sense |
|---|---|
| Spontaneous element as embryonic consciousness | 1860s–70s “riots” / machine-breaking as despair outbursts; 1890s strikes as greater flashes of consciousness but still trade-union |
| Trade-union vs Social-Democratic politics | Factory exposures and economic struggle as levers of economic struggle; **alone**, trade-union not SD work |
| “Economic struggle against employers and government” | Martynov’s formula (as Lenin reads it) = quintessence of [[economism|Economism]] = trade-union politics |
| “Giving the economic struggle a political character” | Lenin: struggle for economic reforms—what trade unions do (Webbs cited) |
| English / West European comparison | Phrases about fighting for selves and children not “future generations” / socialism as weapons of bourgeois promoters of pure trade unionism (Hirsch et al. as Lenin frames them) |
| Kautsky block Lenin quotes | Socialist consciousness not *urwüchsig* from class struggle; introduced *von Aussen* |

Lenin insisted *Rabochaya Mysl*’s “politics always obediently follow economics” is wrong if “politics” means Social-Democratic politics (workers’ economic struggle can connect to bourgeois, clerical, etc. politics) but true if it means only trade-union politics—common striving for governmental measures that **alleviate** but do not **abolish** labor’s subjection to capital.

## Operationalizations

Indicators Lenin used (attributed, not wiki-voice measures):

- Organizing factory/condition “exposures” and strikes for better terms of sale of labor power.
- Confining leadership ideal to the **trade-union secretary** rather than the “tribune of the people.”
- Elevating economic struggle as the “most widely applicable” method of drawing masses into politics.
- Organizational forms: wide public trade unions vs. compact [[professional-revolutionary|organization of revolutionaries]] (he wanted both **distinguished**, not fused).

He did **not** reject trade-union work: Social-Democrats, he argued, should support wide unions open beyond party members; economic (factory) exposures remain an important lever “as long as capitalism… exists.” The critical claim is that **trade-union activity alone does not generate Social-Democratic [[class-consciousness]]**.

## Applications

- **Dual task of Social-Democracy (Lenin):** lead struggle for better sale terms **and** for abolition of the system that compels the propertyless to sell themselves; economic exposures must not become the predominant activity.
- **Lassalle illustration:** historical service as diverting German workers from spontaneous Progressive trade-union/co-operative path—only after multi-year struggle against spontaneity.
- **Ideal leader contrast:** Robert Knight–type trade-union secretary vs. Wilhelm Liebknecht–type political tribune; Martynov’s contrasts with *Iskra*, Lenin argued, de facto reduce SD to trade unionism.
- **Press division of labor:** all-Russian SD paper for political exposure; trade-union **pamphlets** (not local trade newspapers under illegality) to keep trade-union detail in proportion.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Economist counter-position (strongest form as Lenin reports targets):** workers liberating themselves from leaders; mass movement determining tasks; political agitation as superstructure on economic struggle; *Iskra* overestimates ideology and seeks liberal allies without patience for workers to accumulate forces. These deny that trade-union consciousness is an ideological “enslavement” requiring combat.
- **Non-identity with later uses:** later sociological and ethnographic deployments of “trade-union consciousness” are not the same construct; this page records **Lenin’s 1902** usage. Adjacent wiki discussions of class experience vs imputed revolutionary consciousness live on [[class-consciousness]].
- **Four-way care:** “bourgeois politics” here is Lenin’s ideological diagnosis of spontaneous trade unionism, not a sociological claim that union members form a bourgeois class.
- **Evidence class:** theoretical-programmatic claim from a factional pamphlet; empirical histories of Russian strikes and union legalization (Zubatov etc.) enter only as Lenin frames them.

## Lenin 1920: work inside reactionary unions, "that is where the masses are" (attributed)

In *[[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920|"Left-Wing" Communism]]* (1920), Lenin revisited trade-union questions against German "Left" Communists (citing K. Horner) who gave "an unqualified negative" to whether Communists should work within reactionary or "yellow" trade unions of "the Legien type," and who proposed abandoning existing unions for a new "Workers' Union." Lenin argued trade unions inevitably display "a certain 'reactionism'" under the dictatorship of the proletariat, since they are transitional institutions bequeathed by capitalism (organized by craft/trade rather than revolutionary program) — but that Communists must not withdraw from them, instead patiently working within even "the most reactionary" trade unions to win over the backward masses, because "**that is where the masses are to be found**." He offered as historical precedent the pre-revolutionary case of Bolshevik infiltration of Zubatov's police-sponsored "Black-Hundred" workers' assemblies (naming Comrade Babushkin, later shot in 1906) to reach workers the Party could not otherwise contact. As evidence that even "backward" trade-union masses were moving toward organization, he cited (from the Swedish paper *Folkets Dagblad Politiken*, March 1920) British trade-union membership growth from 5.5 million (end 1917) to 6.6 million (end 1918) to an estimated 7.5 million (end 1919). He described the Bolsheviks' own organizational structure — the Party (611,000 members per the April 1920 Congress) linked to trade unions (over 4 million members, formally non-Party but Communist-led) and the Soviets — as the model by which a vanguard [[vanguard-party|party]] guides state policy without requiring formal control of the unions themselves.

## Kollontai 1921: unions as builders, not schools (attributed; [[kollontai-workers-opposition-1921]])

The Workers' Opposition rejected the whole schools-of-communism frame from the left: unions are not preparatory institutions in which workers "learn" under party and specialist direction, but the "basic class collectives" through which the class itself must create the communist economy — with a staged transfer of economic administration to an All-Russian Congress of Producers. Kollontai read the school metaphor as a deferral device ("too early... they have not yet learned enough," as she glossed Lenin's position). The Solidarity editors (1961, secondary commentary) counter that the unions in question were already party-controlled, making the disputed transfer more symbolic than structural.

## Lenin 1920–21: "school of communism" and the trade-union controversy (attributed)

In the trade-union controversy with Trotsky and Bukharin debated at the Tenth Congress (recorded in *[[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]* and *[[soviet-trade-union-debate]]*), Lenin elaborated trade-union consciousness as a *pedagogical* stage below full political class-consciousness: unions are "a school: a school of administration, a school of economic management, a school of communism," standing "between the Party and the government" as "a link between the vanguard and the masses" and a "'reservoir' of the state power," and "comprise the whole organised proletariat" — explicitly not government departments. He rejected Trotsky's proposal to "shake up" the unions administratively and Bukharin's formula of the unions as simultaneously "a school, on the one hand, and apparatus, on the other" as eclectic, insisting the union's present function in every respect (including industrial-management participation) remains that of a **school**, not yet a management apparatus. He distinguished this "school of communism" model from syndicalism, which "hands over to the mass of non-Party workers... the management of their industries," rendering "the Party superfluous" (see [[vanguard-party]]). Trade-union consciousness on this account is real but partial: "we have no other mainstay but the millions of proletarians, who may not be class conscious, are often ignorant, backward and illiterate, but who, being proletarians, follow their own Party" (see [[class-consciousness]]) — the union's task being to raise this partial consciousness through the discipline of administration and management practice rather than to substitute for the Party's political leadership.
