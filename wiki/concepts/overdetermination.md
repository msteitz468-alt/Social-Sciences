# Overdetermination

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[althusser-louis|Althusser]], “Contradiction and Overdetermination,” *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]* 1965 / essay 1962.)*

**Overdetermination** names the claim that Marxist contradiction is **never simple** (one pure principal contradiction whose terms contain their own resolution, as Althusser reads Hegel) but is **always specified** by the total structure of the social body in which it is found — by its formal conditions of existence and even by the instances it governs. The contradiction determines and is determined “in one and the same movement” by the various levels and instances of the [[social-formation]] it animates.

Althusser’s load-bearing formulation:

> the “contradiction” is inseparable from the total structure of the social body in which it is found, inseparable from its formal conditions of existence, and even from the instances it governs; it is radically affected by them, determining, but also determined in one and the same movement, and determined by the various levels and instances of the social formation it animates; it might be called overdetermined in its principle.

He notes the term is “borrowed from other disciplines” (Freud’s dream-work vocabulary of condensation/displacement is the usual referent; in this essay the clinical analogy is lightly marked, then expanded in the Glossary and in “On the Materialist Dialectic”). He uses it “in the absence of anything better, both as an index and as a problem.”

**Contrast with Hegel (attributed):** A Hegelian contradiction is “never really overdetermined.” Apparent complexity in the *Phenomenology* is cumulative internalization around a single centre of consciousness; in the *Philosophy of History*, concrete determinations (law, religion, economy, arts) reflect a unique internal Principle rather than decentred effective determinations. Marxist contradiction requires “circles with another centre” — relative autonomy of instances.

**Historical illustration (attributed):** Russia 1917 as accumulation of all then-possible contradictions (the “weakest link”); overdetermination can run toward historical **block** (e.g. Wilhelmine Germany) or **revolutionary rupture**, but never appears in a pure state.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Freud (dream-work) | Condensation and displacement of psychical intensity (source of the loan-word) |
| **Althusser 1962/65** | Specificity of Marxist contradiction vs Hegel; structure–superstructure complex |
| Althusser Glossary (Brewster / L.A.) | Condensation/fusion vs displacement as modes of overdetermination of contradictions in the social formation |
| Later Marxism / anthropology | Applied in [[structural-marxism]] to precapitalist modes; often thinned to “multiple causes” |
| Causal modeling (distinct) | [[multiple-conjunctural-causation]] (Ragin etc.) is a different research tradition — do not silently equate |

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Diagnostic markers in Althusser’s use:

- Capital–labour never “pure”: always specified by State, dominant ideology, religion, national past, world conjuncture, uneven development.
- **Two ends of the chain:** determination in the last instance by the economic [[mode-of-production]] *and* [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state|relative autonomy]] / specific effectivity of superstructures (“the lonely hour of the last instance never comes”).
- In “On the Materialist Dialectic”: principal/secondary contradictions and aspects (via Mao); phases of displacement, condensation, explosive fusion.

## Applications

- **Political analysis of conjunctures** (Lenin’s “current moment”; 1917 as type case).
- **[[structural-marxism]]** — articulated social formations; kinship/politics “functioning as” relations of production where economy is determinant last-instance but not always dominant.
- **Critique of economism** and of expressive totality (superstructure as mere phenomenon of essence).

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Attributed positions.)*

- **Sahlins** (*[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]*): structural-Marxist reduction of production relations to abstract productive requirements still refuses the symbolic constitution of social reality — overdetermination of levels does not escape “practical reason” if the economic remains last-instance determinant of cultural content.
- **Humanist Marxism:** treats the anti-expressive, anti-subjective framing as evacuation of praxis and agency (see [[marxist-humanism-debate]]).
- **Later Althusser:** theoreticism self-criticized; the category’s descriptive political force can outrun its grounding in a full theory of instances (Althusser’s own worry in the 1962 essay).
- **Term drift:** casual use as “many factors matter” loses the structure-in-dominance claim.
