# Soviet Archaeology

## Definition as Coined

**Soviet archaeology** names the institutional and theoretical development of archaeology in the USSR (and, by extension in some surveys, related Eastern Bloc practice). [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]] (*[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]]* 2006) treats it as a major non-Anglophone stream of [[functional-processual-archaeology|early functional-processual]] and culture-historical work, redistributed in the 2nd edition across culture-historical, early functional-processual, and recent chapters rather than isolated in a single "Soviet" chapter as in 1989.

## Semantic History

Early Soviet years: revolutionary reorganization of museums and academies; Marr's Japhetic theory influencing some archaeological linguistics; 1930s turn to socio-economic formations and critique of "bourgeois" artifact chronology; postwar ethnogenesis and ethnically framed prehistories; post-1991 reassessments and renewed culture-historical ethnicity themes (Trigger on post-communist conditions).

## Operationalizations

Marxist periodization of prehistory; large-scale state excavations; ethnogenesis models linking archaeological cultures to modern nationalities; technical excellence in field method often noted alongside theoretical orthodoxy constraints (Trigger's balanced survey — attributed).

## Applications

Trigger uses Soviet cases to show: (1) functional-processual aims under historical materialism; (2) nationalist/ethnogenetic culture-history under Stalinist and later conditions; (3) post-Cold War return of ethnicity as key concept worldwide, not only in the former USSR.

## Contested Issues

How far theory was genuine Marxist science vs political control; status of Marrism; ethics of ethnogenesis archaeology; relation to Kossinna-style ethnic mapping. Record as positions; Trigger is one major Anglophone guide, not the only.
