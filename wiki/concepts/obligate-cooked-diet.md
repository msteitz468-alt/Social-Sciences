# Obligate Cooked Diet

*(Attributed — Wrangham 2009.)* Thesis that humans are **biologically dependent on cooked food** — not merely culturally fond of it. "We are cooks more than carnivores."

## Definition as Coined

Humans are adapted to cooked food "in the same essential way as cows are adapted to eating grass, or fleas to sucking blood." Dependence arose, on Wrangham's fossil reading, at the habiline → *Homo erectus* transition, not in recent prehistory. Anatomy (small guts, jaws, teeth) is effect of that adaptation, not independent cause of cooking preference.

## Semantic History

Challenges (1) folk intuition that cooking is luxury (Marco Polo Mongol raw-blood stories; Howell's exaggerated Inuit raw diet); (2) Darwinian and structuralist views that fire is late culture without biological necessity (Lévi-Strauss / Leach: people cook "to show they are men and not beasts"); (3) raw-foodist ideology (Howell's "living enzymes"; Shelley's 1813 anti-cooking moralism). Aligns with Tylor (1870): cooking practiced by every known society; fireless-ancestor myths are fantasy (Frazer).

## Operationalizations

*(Findings as Wrangham reports them — single-source.)*

- **Giessen raw-food study:** BMI falls as raw share rises; energy deficiency; ~50% of women with high raw diets amenorrheic; 82% of long-term raw-foodists still include some cooked food.
- **Evo Diet (2006):** raw "evolutionary" diet produced weight loss despite other health markers.
- **Cooked vegetarians vs meat-eaters:** no body-weight difference — cooking status matters more than meat vs plant in his contrast.
- **No documented long-term survival on raw *wild* food** (castaways, lost explorers cook when they can; Valero banana case unverified exception).
- **Anatomical suite:** small mouth, weak jaws (MYH16), smallest relative molars among primates, small stomach/colon — suited to energy-dense soft food, not ape-style bulk raw forage.
- **Protein ceiling ~50% of calories** + low fat in tropical game → plant carbs essential; small-gutted humans could not flourish on raw plant staples alone.

## Applications

- Anchors [[cooking-hypothesis]] dating via biology rather than hearths alone.
- Frames modern industrial softness/processing as amplification of an ancient energy preference (Epilogue).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Raw-foodist samples are modern, self-selected, often high-quality produce — Wrangham uses their *failure* as evidence of obligate cooking, but critics may stress sample bias differently.
- Ethnographic "universality" of cooking is strong; the evolutionary *timing* of dependence is the contested leap (see [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]]).
- "Obligate" is Wrangham's strong framing; short-term raw survival (weeks) is acknowledged.
