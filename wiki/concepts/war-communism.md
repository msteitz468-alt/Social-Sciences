# War Communism

## Definition as Coined

**War Communism** names the Soviet economic and political emergency regime of the Civil War years (roughly mid-1918 to early 1921): grain surplus appropriation (*prodrazvyorstka*), far-reaching nationalization, labour armies and compulsion ([[militarization-of-labour]]), and suppression of free private trade. It was succeeded by the [[new-economic-policy]]. The term is conventional and partly retrospective; practices varied over time.

## Semantic History

- **1918–21 practice:** Emerged under civil war, blockade, and multi-front fighting; justified as wartime necessity and as steps toward socialism.
- **Avrich 1970:** Treats War Communism as the systemic context of the [[kronstadt-debate|Kronstadt rising]] — industrial collapse, deurbanization, black market, roadblocks, peasant war (Antonov/Tambov and many local risings), Petrograd hunger and strikes (*[[avrich-kronstadt-1921]]*, attributed).
- **Trotsky 1920:** Defends labour militarization and related compulsion as transition tools while the civil war still raged (*[[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920]]* — overlapping instruments, not identical label).
- **Lenin 1921:** Self-criticism at the Tenth Congress of over-nationalization as "a patent mistake"; tax in kind replaces surplus appropriation (*[[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]*).

## Operationalizations

| Instrument | Role under War Communism (as described in Avrich/Lenin materials) |
|---|---|
| *Prodrazvyorstka* | Forced grain collection from peasantry |
| Nationalization | Industry under state; limited private trade |
| Labour compulsion / armies | Mobilize labour-power on military model |
| Roadblocks / trade bans | Suppress free exchange |
| Political monopoly | One-party rule; repression of rival socialists |

## Applications

- Context of [[kronstadt-debate]] and Petrograd strikes of February 1921.
- Immediate predecessor of [[new-economic-policy]]; Avrich: NEP outlines formulated weeks before Kronstadt, hastened by the rising.
- Labour doctrine in [[militarization-of-labour]] and trade-union controversy ([[soviet-trade-union-debate]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Peasant and worker revolt:** Mass risings and Kronstadt treated by critics as verdict on the regime (Avrich's "crisis of War Communism").
- **Bolshevik self-critique:** Lenin conceded economic overreach while tightening political monopoly as NEP began.
- **Label ambiguity:** "War Communism" can smuggle teleology (wartime only vs socialist blueprint); date and content contested among historians — attribute periodizations.

## Carr 1979 synthesis

[[carr-edward-hallett|E. H. Carr]], *[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979|The Russian Revolution: From Lenin to Stalin]]* (1979), Ch. 3, supplies a structural reading (all attributed to Carr):

- **Timing/name:** Drastic policies "later known by the ambiguous name of war communism" from summer 1918; food first — food detachments; decree of 11 June 1918 for committees of poor peasants under Narkomprod.
- **Two elements:** (1) concentration of economic authority, centralization, large units, unified planning — built on foundations that partly "stood the test"; (2) flight from commercial/monetary forms (rationing, payments in kind, production for direct use) — **decisively rejected by NEP**. The second element failed especially against backward peasant agriculture (>80% of population).
- **Industry:** Decree of 28 June 1918 nationalizing major industry; Vesenkha/glavki; specialists retained at higher pay; Moscow lost ~44.5% and Petrograd ~57.5% of population (Carr's figures).
- **Labour:** One-man management; militarization and labour battalions (Trotsky, early 1920); concentration camps for severest cases.
- **Ambivalent party memory:** Emergency necessity vs. advance into socialism (Bukharin on "revolutionary disintegration of industry" as historically necessary stage). Divergences reappeared in NEP debates.
