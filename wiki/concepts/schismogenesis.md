# Schismogenesis

## Definition as Coined

[[bateson-gregory|Gregory Bateson]] coined **schismogenesis** for processes of **progressive differentiation** through interaction: complementary (e.g. dominance–submission escalating) or symmetrical (e.g. rivalry escalating). Primary monographs (*Naven*, etc.) are not yet ingested here; the term enters this wiki substantially via *[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|The Dawn of Everything]]* (2021).

[[graeber-david|Graeber]] and [[wengrow-david|Wengrow]] revive and extend it (attributed) as:

> societies in contact ending up joined within a **common system of differences**, even as they attempt to distinguish themselves from one another.

Classic analogy they cite (via [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]]): Athens and Sparta as antitypes (sea/land, democratic/oligarchic, etc.). Applied programmatically to **California foragers vs Northwest Coast** ranked slave-holding societies — potlatch vs anti-potlatch ethos, slavery vs its rejection — as mirror images rather than independent ecological adaptations or successive [[mode-of-production|modes of production]]. Also used for Fertile Crescent upland–lowland contrasts and for culture-area politics of values more generally.

## Semantic History

- **Bateson (1930s):** Iatmul *naven*; culture-contact dynamics; later cybernetic reception.
- **Mid-century:** limited mainstream anthropological use outside Bateson circles.
- **Sahlins:** Athens–Sparta / culture-as-antithesis formulations G&W cite.
- **G&W 2021:** comparative historical engine for regional diversity and for the [[indigenous-critique]] (self-definition against European "way of money").

## Operationalizations

Qualitative: identify bordering societies with inverted values/institutions and argue mutual self-definition. Requires (ideally) historical interaction evidence, not trait lists alone. Competes with environmental determinism, optimal-foraging reduction, and single-ladder evolutionism.

## Applications

- *Dawn* ch. 2 (definition; Indigenous–European contact).
- *Dawn* ch. 5 (California / NW Coast paradigm; slavery and "capturing societies").
- *Dawn* ch. 6 (upland–lowland Fertile Crescent).
- *Dawn* ch. 10–11 (admin valleys vs heroic highland princelings; culture areas in "getting stuck").

## Critiques and Limitations

- Easy to overfit: any contrast can be labeled schismogenic without process evidence.
- Bateson's complementary/symmetrical types often flattened in popular uptake.
- Primary Bateson texts not yet in collection — this page is G&W-heavy until *Naven* / *Steps* are ingested.
