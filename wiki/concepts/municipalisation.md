# Municipalisation (Agrarian)

## Definition as Coined

**Municipalisation** (agrarian), in Russian Social-Democratic debate 1905–07, means converting **confiscated privately owned (especially landlord) lands** into the property or disposal of **large local self-governing bodies** (Zemstvo-scale / regional), while **leaving peasant allotment lands in private ownership**. [[lenin-vladimir]] identifies **P. Maslov** as initiator of municipalisation in Russia and as the theorist who “revised” Marx by repudiating [[absolute-rent]].

The **Stockholm (April 1906) RSDLP programme**, as Lenin describes it, embodied dualism: under victorious development of the revolution — nationalisation of forests, waters, and colonisation lands **plus** municipalisation of privately owned lands; under unfavourable conditions — **division** of landlords’ lands as private property; present peasant/small-owner holdings recognised. Authors’ main argument (John): fear of alienating peasantry / counter-revolutionary Vendée if peasants’ **own** allotments were nationalised. Maslov’s municipalisation (exempt peasant lands) replaced an earlier “X” scheme transferring “if possible” all lands to Zemstvos.

Lenin’s legal-political contrast: difference from [[land-nationalisation]] is **not** central vs local administration, but whether private ownership is retained for one land category (**“agrarian bimetallism”**) or abolished completely.

## Semantic History

| Usage | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Maslov municipalisation** | Programme form: landlord estates to local bodies; allotments private; rent question reduced to who (local vs central) gets rent |
| **Western “municipal socialism”** | Mensheviks (Kostrov, Larin at Stockholm, as Lenin frames) equated land municipalisation with Fabian-style local reforms; Lenin (citing Engels on Fabians) treats that as petty-bourgeois opportunism diverting from class rule and state structure |
| **Kautsky’s different sense** | (Letter to Shanin; letter published by Maslov, as Lenin/Shanin present it): confiscated big estates continued as large-scale agriculture by municipalities/larger orgs or rented to producers’ associations — **not** Menshevik dual tenure; Kautsky declined to impose property form on peasants |
| **Cossack “municipalisation”** | Lenin’s polemical real-world model: army/caste regional landownership as reactionary isolation, not democratic bulwark |
| **Larin “best outcome”** | Popular Menshevik pamphlet sums best outcome as all land as **state property** turned over to local self-government for cultivators — which Lenin identifies as **nationalisation**, showing municipalisation’s practical muddle |

Semantic drift Lenin polices: treating municipalisation as more “flexible,” more “democratic,” or a “guarantee against restoration.”

## Operationalizations

**Programme operationalization (Stockholm / Maslov line as Lenin reconstructs):**

1. Confiscation of landlords’ estates (shared with Bolshevik nationalisers).
2. Confiscated private lands “placed at the disposal of large local self-governing bodies” — ownership muddled; Lenin: owner can only be the state; central parliament must define disposal.
3. **Exemption of allotment land** (~138.8 of ~280m dessiatins suitable land in Lenin’s European Russia synthesis) as private.
4. Forests/waters/colonisation lands of “national importance” already to central state even in Menshevik programme — Lenin: enormous areas already “nationalised” on paper.
5. Maslov budget demagogy (as Lenin ridicules): nationalisation = rent for army/navy; municipalisation = rent for “needs of the population” (parenthesis muddling absolute vs differential rent).

**Political operationalization claimed by municipalisers (via Lenin):**

- Local bodies as “bulwark against reaction.”
- Flexibility for diverse regional conditions and “process of the revolution” (John).
- Avoidance of peasant Vendée against nationalisation of allotments.
- Tsereteli’s Second Duma cluster of pro-municipalisation arguments (rent for people’s needs; unemployed; model farms; free lease in crisis; democracy bulwark “like Cossacks”; fear of allotment-alienation counter-revolution).

## Applications

- **Stockholm Congress programme** — official RSDLP dual system Lenin attacked in 1907–08.
- **Second Duma SD speeches** — only Ozol and Tsereteli’s second substantially defended municipalisation (per Lenin); “nationals” pushed regional stocks — Lenin: municipalisation fed nationalist-federalist splits of the peasant movement.
- **Restoration comparison (Lenin):** after municipalisation, restoration need only transfer municipal lands (or rent) by paper; after nationalisation, must smash millions of new farms.
- **Incompatibility claim (Lenin):** economically rivets medieval allotment ownership; politically stops at regional democracy — incompatible with real victory as conquest of power by proletariat and peasantry.

## Critiques and Limitations

**Lenin’s critique (attributed, *Agrarian Programme* Ch. IV):**

- **Reactionary slogan:** “idealises the medieval isolation of the regions” and dulls need for a **centralised** agrarian revolution.
- **Theoretical dualism:** deny absolute rent → private vs state ownership “makes no difference” (only who gets differential rent) → mechanical private allotments + public confiscated land.
- **No restoration guarantee:** law expresses classes holding state power; paper municipal title cannot stop surrender after restoration. Relative guarantee = deepest revolution; republic > local self-government.
- **Local democracy without central power** = Cadet-style deal with reaction.
- **Fabian analogy:** bourgeoisie tolerates municipal reforms only off the foundations of rule; half the best land under “local government” as if horse trams is demagogy.
- **Less flexible than nationalisation;** farther from revolutionary peasant nationalisation than Trudoviks; closer to liberal-landlord preservation of allotments.

**Limitations:** Maslov, Plekhanov, John, Kostrov, Larin, Tsereteli, Kautsky appear **as Lenin presents them** in [[lenin-collected-works-vol-13-1962]]. Full positions: [[nationalisation-vs-municipalisation-debate]].
