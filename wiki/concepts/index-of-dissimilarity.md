# Index of Dissimilarity

## Definition as Coined

Standard measure of **evenness** of residential distribution between two groups. As used by [[massey-douglas-s|Massey]] and [[denton-nancy-a|Denton]]: the percentage of blacks who would have to move to achieve evenness with whites across tracts (or blocks in historical series). Score of 0 = perfect evenness; 100 = complete separation.

## Semantic History

Long standard in U.S. segregation research (Taeuber & Taeuber *Negroes in Cities* and earlier); one of five dimensions in the [[hypersegregation]] framework.

## Operationalizations

Computed at block or tract level; historical series in *American Apartheid* Ch 2–3 track black–white D from late 19th century through 1980. Threshold language in the book: D under ~60 often treated as moderate; many large northern metros had black–white D in the 70s–80s in 1980 (attributed).

## Applications

Cross-city and over-time comparison; contrast black vs Hispanic/Asian segregation; component of hypersegregation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Insensitive to relative group size (unlike isolation); depends on areal unit (MAUP); does not identify cause (discrimination vs income vs preference).
