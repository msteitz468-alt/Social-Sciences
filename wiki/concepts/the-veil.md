# The Veil

## Definition as Coined

In *[[du-bois-souls-of-black-folk-1903|The Souls of Black Folk]]* (1903), [[du-bois-w-e-b|Du Bois]] uses **the Veil** as the barrier that separates Black Americans from the “other world” of white America — shutting out true self-consciousness while conferring a painful “second-sight.” He signs the Forethought as “bone of the bone and flesh of the flesh of them that live within the Veil,” and organizes the book’s later movement as stepping “within the Veil” to show religion, sorrow, and greater souls.

Origin vignette (ch. I): in a New England schoolhouse, a girl’s refusal of his visiting-card revealed that he was “shut out from their world by a vast veil.”

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| *Souls* (1903) | Spatial-spiritual barrier; second-sight; worlds “within and without” |
| Later commentary | Often paired or conflated with double-consciousness; sometimes treated as pure metaphor of segregation |
| Du Bois later works | Continues and expands (not yet ingested) |

Across *Souls* the Veil appears as: childhood exclusion; barrier to Opportunity (Tennessee hills); “Veil of Race” over the South’s journey from feudalism; shadow over the first-born child; and that which Sorrow Songs and culture strive to rend.

## Operationalizations

Not measured. Empirical correlates in the book are institutional: Jim Crow cars, segregated Teachers’ Institutes, dinner eaten alone at the commissioner’s house, dual villages, physical color-line on Southern maps, funeral cortege insults.

## Applications

- Frame for [[double-consciousness]] (see through the other world’s revelation)
- Narrative structure of *Souls* (outside → Black Belt → within: church, elegy, Crummell, fiction, songs)
- Education under the Veil (ch. IV–VI); hope that America “shall rend the Veil” (ch. XIV)

## Critiques and Limitations

- Metaphor can be aestheticized, losing the material color-line (debt, ballot, school fund, lynching) that Du Bois also documents.
- “Second-sight” as gift can be romanticized; in *Souls* it is inseparable from contempt, pity, and closed Opportunity.
