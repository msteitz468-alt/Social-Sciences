# Data Constraint on Interpretation

## Definition as Coined

**Data constraint on interpretation** is [[trigger-bruce-g|Bruce Trigger]]'s thesis that, although social and subjective factors powerfully shape archaeological questions and preferred answers, the **gradual accumulation of archaeological data** exercises a growing constraint on interpretation — increasing the objectivity of research and its value for understanding the human past. It is the load-bearing middle path in *[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|A History of Archaeological Thought]]* between naive positivism and hyper-relativism.

## Semantic History

Trigger develops the idea against 1970s processual dismissals of disciplinary history and against extreme postprocessual claims that interpretations only mirror transient values. He draws on Collingwood's insistence that historical problems require the history of thought about them, and on a materialist-Marxist appreciation of relativism that still affirms real knowledge of the past (preface, *HAT* 2nd ed.).

## Operationalizations

- Comparative historiography: which interpretive schemes survive contact with expanding datasets (e.g. general outline of prehistory refined for a century while *explanations* turn over — Trigger ch. 10).
- Rejection of pseudoscience/hyperdiffusion/extraterrestrial claims as failing to account for the record **in totality** (Trigger attributed).
- Nested theory levels: high-level theory, middle-ranging theory, and data interact rather than free interpretation from evidence.

## Applications

Organizing principle of *HAT*: social context of archaeologists (esp. middle classes in the West) explains much of theoretical succession, but data constraint explains cumulative gains and why some colonialist and nationalist schemes eventually fail.

## Contested Issues

Postprocessual and strong constructivist positions dispute that data "constrain" in Trigger's robust sense. Johnson (*[[johnson-archaeological-theory-2011|Archaeological Theory]]*) recommends Trigger for historical perspective while disagreeing with his assessment of the field's present. The thesis is **Trigger's position**, not wiki-voice epistemology.
