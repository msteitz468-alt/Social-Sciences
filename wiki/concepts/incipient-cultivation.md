# Incipient Cultivation (and Animal Domestication)

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[braidwood-robert|Braidwood]], *[[braidwood-prehistoric-men-1959|Prehistoric Men]]* 3rd ed. 1959.)*

Within the **food-producing stage**, Braidwood distinguishes eras. The **era of incipient cultivation and animal domestication** is the **first** era — preceding the **era of the primary village-farming community**. He repeatedly marks the empirical base as **slight**: the era is set up "mainly by playing a hunch," because the archaeological gap between late food-collecting assemblages and full villages is poorly filled. Candidate Near Eastern assemblages he discusses include the **[[natufian-culture|Natufian]]** (Palestine caves; sickles, bone industry, possible dog/goat) and **Karim Shahir** (Iraqi hill country).

## Semantic History

- **Braidwood program:** transitional analytic slot between food-collecting stage and village-farming; natural-habitat nuclear area as setting.
- **Later terms:** Proto-Neolithic, PPNA, "low-level food production," cultivation-without-domestication continua — related but not identical vocabularies; do not silently equate.
- **Wiki usage:** historiographical Braidwood periodization term; pair with modern multi-proxy domestication sequences without asserting his 1959 absolute dates in wiki voice.

## Operationalizations

Sickle gloss, groundstone, animal age/sex profiles, morphological domestication markers, architecture short of full village permanence — Braidwood's own 1959 evidence is limited and explicitly provisional.

## Applications

Frames [[natufian-culture]] and pre-Jarmo Zagros materials; bridges [[neolithic-revolution]] event language and gradualist transitions.

## Critiques and Limitations

Era may be a residual category for "not yet village"; Natufian food-producer status remains debated in his text and after; absolute dates (e.g. Natufian ~5000 B.C. guesses in places, elsewhere ~7750 B.C. dog claim) are (as of 1959) and superseded.
