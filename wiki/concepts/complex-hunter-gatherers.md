# Complex Hunter-Gatherers

## Definition as Coined

**Complex hunter-gatherers** names an etic analytical category for forager societies that display organizational and material features once assumed to require agriculture — multi-season or year-round residence, substantial dwellings, food storage, cemeteries, specialized crafts (including pottery or polished stone), long-distance exchange, and sometimes ranked or contested inequality — while remaining dependent primarily on wild resources. In [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]] the category organizes global late-glacial and early Holocene cases rather than a single ethnographic type.

## Semantic History

Victorian and evolutionary schemes treated “hunter-gatherer” as simple, mobile, and pre-political. Mid–late 20th-c. archaeology (Northwest Coast ethnography; affluent-forager debates; Mesolithic cemetery studies; Natufian and Jomon research) forced a graded vocabulary: simple/egalitarian mobile bands vs complex/affluent/sedentary foragers. Mithen (2003) deploys the frame comparatively across the Levantine [[natufian-culture|Natufian]], European late Mesolithic (Lepenski Vir, Skateholm, [[ertebolle-culture|Ertebølle]], Oronsay), Japanese [[jomon-culture|Jōmon]], Amazonian riverine economies, and other cases — without equating them as one people or developmental stage.

## Operationalizations

Indicators commonly bundled (as of Mithen 2003’s synthesis, not a single formal index):

- **Settlement:** multi-season or permanent villages; substantial houses (e.g. Natufian stone-footed huts; Jōmon Initial villages; Lepenski Vir trapeze dwellings).
- **Subsistence:** intensive wild plants, fish, shellfish, or migratory salmon; storage pits; coastal/lagoon specialization ([[coastal-adaptation]]).
- **Mortuary:** formal cemeteries, grave goods, possible status or violence signals (Skateholm, Vedbæk, Téviec).
- **Craft and exchange:** forager pottery (Jōmon earliest; Ertebølle coarse wares; Yangtze/Amazon early ceramics); ground axes; exotic raw materials.
- **Inequality:** debated case-by-case; Mithen often notes egalitarian signatures (e.g. Uenohara dwelling similarity) alongside wealth/violence claims.

Absence of morphological plant/animal domestication does **not** disqualify a case; presence of limited management (“wild gardening”) may be scored as complex foraging rather than full farming.

## Applications

As of [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]]:

- **Southwest Asia:** Natufian villages (e.g. Eynan/Ain Mallaha), Ohalo II plant intensification, Abu Hureyra pre-farming occupation, Hallan Çemi — sedentary or semi-sedentary foragers before and during the path into [[pre-pottery-neolithic|Pre-Pottery Neolithic]].
- **Europe:** late Mesolithic cemeteries and marine economies (Ertebølle, Skateholm, Tybrind Vig); Iron Gates Lepenski Vir; Franchthi Mesolithic co-presence with early farmers.
- **East Asia:** Jōmon as world’s earliest pottery culture with sedentary villages on wild woodland and coastal resources; Yangtze pottery among early cultivators/foragers.
- **Americas:** Monte Verde as early settled foragers; later Amazon pottery and specialized riverine economies; NW Coast salmon complexity as ethnographic analogy for Jōmon (attributed).
- **Comparative:** Mithen links Damdama (Ganges) foragers to Uenohara, Koster, and Skateholm as parallel part-year sedentism with mortuary claims on land.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Heterogeneous bundle:** sedentism, inequality, storage, and craft need not co-occur; lumping risks reifying a stage.
- **Ethnographic analogy** (Northwest Coast, Saami swan symbolism, etc.) is hypothesis-generating, not evidence — Mithen uses it as such.
- **Sedentism ≠ farming** and **pottery ≠ farming** (Jōmon, Sahara, Ertebølle); the category can blur into early horticulture or animal management without clear boundaries ([[horticulture]], [[animal-domestication]]).
- **Violence and inequality** readings from cemeteries are interpretive and contested.
- Dates, genetic ancestry, and peopling models (as of Mithen 2003) predate the aDNA revolution — keep regional claims attributed and date-stamped.
- Four-way non-identity: complex-forager *assemblage* ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ self-conscious people.
