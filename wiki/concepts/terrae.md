# Terrae (Gamble deep-history worlds)

[[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s six-part periodization of **homonin global settlement** in *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013). Each **Terra** is a "world" of settlement whose boundaries are drawn by **where** and **how far** hominins lived and linked, not solely by skull morphology or stone-tool mode (attributed, Gamble 2013 ch. 1).

## Definition as Coined

Gamble uses **Terrae** (plural of Latin *terra*, land/world) for six successive deep-history stages whose boundaries are drawn by **the changing distribution of archaeological evidence in time and space**, initially ignoring tool types and skull shape (attributed, Gamble 2013 ch. 1). They are a "snapshot of the current state of research" and subject to revision (attributed).

| Terra | Span (as of Gamble 2013) | Land % of present ~149M km² (T1–T5 table) | Duration share (T1–T5) | Character (attributed summary) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Terra 0 | 10–3.3 Ma | (pre-table; larger low-latitude world) | — | Miocene apes → early bipeds; key issue **bipedalism not encephalisation**; African bipeds (e.g. *Orrorin* ~6 Ma, *Ardipithecus* 4.4 Ma, Laetoli 3.7 Ma) |
| Terra 1 | 3.3–1.8 Ma | 24% | 45% (1.5 Ma) | Mode 1 / Gona 2.5 Ma (as of 2013); brains >400 cm³; Africa + SW Asia tectonic extension; small operational areas |
| Terra 2 | 1.8 Ma–50 ka | 44% | 53% (1.75 Ma) | Old World in **three strides** (1.8–0.8 / 0.8–0.2 / 200–50 ka); brains >900 then >1200 cm³; **bio-tidal** shelves; rigid bounds (~no Sahul, rarely >55°N); Moviusland northern belt |
| Terra 3 | 50–4 ka | 94% | 1.4% (46 ka) | *H. sapiens* only by end Pleistocene; [[going-beyond]] into Sahul, Siberia, Americas (~42M km² new); low-density FGH; [[kinshipping]] / storage / FACE amplification |
| Terra 4 | 4–1 ka | 95% | 0.09% (3 ka) | Domestication-led island/seascape settlement (Pacific, Madagascar, Iceland 874 CE, etc.); **veneer** through which older Terrae are seen |
| Terra 5 | after ~1400 CE | 100% | 0.02% (600 yr) | European "discovery" as **reunion** with already-settled earth; little first-time land; displacement/diaspora |

Low sea level can add ~22 million km² of shelves (offset by ice); Terrae are not continuously occupied even at equable moments — settlement histories express climatic tolerances (attributed).

## Semantic History

Introduced for this book as a cartographic alternative to progress-staged "Ages of Man" and pure industry sequences. Related to but distinct from *[[gamble-timewalkers-1994|Timewalkers]]*' Ancients / Pioneers / Moderns colonization guide and from standard ESA/MSA/LSA or Lower/Middle/Upper Palaeolithic labels. Terra 3–4 roughly map onto *Timewalkers*' Moderns + Holocene maritime phases; Terra 5 is the colonial-reunion frame absent from the 1994 guide.

## Operationalizations

- Geographic settlement extent and barriers (water, mountains, latitude; bio-tidal shelf inundation/exposure).
- Archive types available (artefacts, symbols, genes, digital — Box 1.1 map onto Terrae).
- Social reach: [[going-beyond]], [[kinshipping]], [[enchainment|FACE]], instruments vs containers.
- Climate drivers for early Terrae: [[variability-selection]], Milankovitch / MIS, tectonic trail (chs 2–4).

## Applications

Structures the entire argument of *Settling the Earth*; densifies [[global-colonization]] and [[world-prehistory]] timelines; supplies the periodization for Gamble densify after *Timewalkers* and *Palaeolithic Societies of Europe*.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Boundaries are analytical, not natural kinds; Terra 2 alone spans ~1.75 Myr of heterogeneous hominins and industries.
- Absolute dates, land-area percentages, and genetic clocks are **as of Gamble 2013**; aDNA and redating revise many anchors.
- Risk of reifying "worlds" as discrete stages rather than overlapping settlement systems.
- Table 1.1 header says "five" Terrae while listing T1–T5; T0 is treated separately in the narrative (source inconsistency to note, not resolve).
