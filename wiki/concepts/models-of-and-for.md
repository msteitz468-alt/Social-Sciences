# Models of / Models for

## Definition as Coined

In “Religion As a Cultural System” (*[[geertz-interpretation-of-cultures-1973|IoC]]* Ch. 4), [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] (attributed) holds that cultural patterns have an “intrinsic double aspect”: they are **models of** reality (they represent, map, or express how things are) and **models for** reality (they provide programs or blueprints for how to act). Sacred symbols fuse a people’s **ethos** (tone, character, quality of life, moral/aesthetic style) with their **world view** (picture of the way things in sheer actuality are).

## Semantic History

Portable pair in cultural theory. [[sewell-william-jr|Sewell]] later treats *disjunction* between models of and models for as a mechanism of cultural change.

## Operationalizations

Geertz’s five-clause definition of religion: a system of symbols that establishes moods and motivations by formulating a general order of existence and clothing it with factuality so that those moods/motivations seem uniquely realistic — religion as both model of and model for.

## Applications

Religion and ideology essays; Sewell’s historical sociology of structure/culture/event.

## Critiques and Limitations

May overstate fusion; needs theory of mismatch and contest (Sewell; practice theory).
