# Validity Claims

## Definition as Coined

**Validity claims** (*Geltungsansprüche*) are, for [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]] (attributed in *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]*), the criticizable claims that a speaker raises with a speech act and that a hearer can accept or reject with a "yes" or "no." In full [[communicative-action]], three claims are raised **simultaneously**:

| Claim | World-relation | Pure mode (analytic) |
|---|---|---|
| **Truth** (*Wahrheit*) | objective world (states of affairs) | constative / assertive speech |
| **Rightness** (*Richtigkeit*) | social world (interpersonal relations, norms) | regulative speech |
| **Truthfulness / sincerity** (*Wahrhaftigkeit*) | subjective world (inner nature, intentions) | expressive speech |

Habermas (attributed): "validity claims cannot be rejected or accepted without reason"; the "yes/no" response takes the interaction "out of the sphere of mere caprice, sheer conditioning, or adjustment." Illocutionary force is not an irrational push opposite propositional content; it **specifies which claim** is raised, how, and for what, and motivates the hearer through a rationally motivated binding (*Bindung*).

## Semantic History

- **Speech-act theory (Austin, Searle).** Illocutionary force and sincerity conditions provide the raw materials; Habermas generalizes validity beyond propositional truth to the pragmatic level of utterances.
- **Universal / [[formal-pragmatics]].** 1970s essays propose the three-claim structure as general pragmatic presuppositions of speech oriented to understanding.
- **TCA Vol. 1, Ch. I and III.** Full statement: three worlds; pure modes vs everyday multi-claim speech; distinction of power claims (imperatives) from normative rightness; refined communicative action as coordination *only* through criticizable claims (not through perlocutionary success).
- **TCA Vol. 2.** Validity claims remain the background structure of [[lifeworld]] reproduction; media-steered subsystems "bypass" them.

## Operationalizations

Not measured. Distinctions used analytically:

- **Criticizable vs non-criticizable coordination** — agreement based on reasons vs forced accord, conditioning, or delinguistified media ([[steering-media]]).
- **Power claim vs rightness claim** — imperatives relate to the objective world via success; regulative speech relates to a shared social world via norms.
- **Warranty (*Gewähr*)** — the speaker's offer to redeem the claim if challenged.

## Applications

- Analytic core of [[communicative-action]] and [[communicative-rationality]].
- Maps onto the four sociological action concepts of Ch. I.3: [[teleological-action]]/[[strategic-action]] privilege objective-world success; [[normatively-regulated-action]] privileges rightness; [[dramaturgical-action]] privileges sincerity; communicative action carries all three.
- Discourse ethics: practical discourse redeems rightness claims under idealized conditions.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Idealization / counterfactual status** (attributed): everyday speech rarely approximates unconstrained redemption of claims; critics treat the three claims as philosophical stipulation rather than reconstructible competence.
- **Sincerity claim** (attributed): truthfulness is harder to redeem discursively than truth or rightness — Habermas treats it as validated in consistency of conduct over time, not only in argument.
- **Power inside "communication"** (Foucauldian line, attributed): classifying some force as "mere power claims" may sanitize domination that works through the form of validity itself.
