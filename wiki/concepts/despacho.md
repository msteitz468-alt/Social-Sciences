# Despacho (Andean Offering / Pago)

## Definition as Coined

A **despacho** (from Spanish *despachar*, to send or dispatch) is a packet of goods — contents depending on purpose — offered to [[earth-beings|earth-beings]] (and sometimes other powers) in Andean practice. In *[[de-la-cadena-earth-beings-2015|Earth Beings]]* (2015), [[de-la-cadena-marisol|Marisol de la Cadena]] stresses that Mariano and Nazario Turpo were known for the effectiveness of their dispatches: what they contained, how and from where they were sent, and the words used (attributed — Preface). She resists reducing despacho to “religion” or mere “offering”: it can be **pago** (payment) that enters political negotiation with Ausangate alongside lawyers — and can uncomfortably echo gamonal bribe logics (attributed — Story 3).

## Semantic History

Long-standing Andean *pago a la tierra* / mountain-payment practices; colonial extirpation of idolatry targeted related *guacas*. Late-20th/21st-century **chamanismo andino** and New Age tourism standardize “despacho workshops”; presidential multicultural theater (Toledo’s 2001 Machu Picchu ceremony) and museum display (NMAI) further translate despacho into spirituality/culture slots. De la Cadena’s analytic interest is the **partial connection** among these translations and in-ayllu practice that does not require Christian *iñi*/faith in the same way relations with Jesus do (attributed — Nazario contrast with Padre Antonio).

## Operationalizations

Types noted in the ethnography (attributed): full burned offerings vs “raw” (unburned) tourist despachos at Machu Picchu; *k’intu* coca presentation as related everyday welcome to tirakuna; museum despacho as **representation** rather than relation (Ausangate photo is not Ausangate). Efficacy evaluated locally by consequences (hail, crops, political outcomes) — not by belief affirmation.

## Applications

- Mariano’s land struggle: despachos with earth-beings + lawyers (Story 3).
- Nazario’s tourism labor and Toledo ceremony (Stories 5–6).
- NMAI collaboration and *suq’a* repatriation despachos (Story 6).
- Mining conflict: environment frame may win while tirakuna are withdrawn (Epilogue).

## Critiques and Limitations

Tourism raw despachos and museum spirituality captions risk emptying relational practice into performance. Leftist “defense of spiritual beliefs” and New Age sacred-mountain talk still, on de la Cadena’s account, translate excess as belief under one Nature (position). Comparative Andeanist ritual literature (Allen et al.) not yet co-ingested here.
