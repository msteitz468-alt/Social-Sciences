# Making Out

## Definition as Coined

Machine operators' own term for the game of earning incentive pay under piece rates — "a sequence of stages — of encounters between machine operators and the social or nonsocial objects that regulate the conditions of work" (Burawoy). Winning means beating the rate up to the informally enforced ceiling (140% at Allied, 1975; $1.25/hour at Geer, 1945); companion vocabulary includes "gravy" and "stinker" jobs, "goldbricking" (coasting on guaranteed base pay when a rate is impossible), "quota restriction" and "banking"/"kitties" (holding surplus output against future bad shifts), and "chiseling" (reallocating recorded time).

## Semantic History

Donald Roy documented the game covertly at "Geer Company" (1944–45; Chicago dissertation "Making Out on the Shop Floor"), showing output restriction was rational play, not Mayo-style "nonlogical belief." [[burawoy-michael|Burawoy]]'s restudy of the same shop (*[[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-1979|Manufacturing Consent]]*, fieldwork 1974–75) migrated the emic term into a theory of consent: "The very activity of playing a game generates consent with respect to its rules... consent rests upon — is constructed through — playing the game." Playing constitutes workers as individuals, redistributes conflict laterally, and obscures and secures surplus value; the game is organized and refereed with management's participation, so it is not autonomous worker resistance (contra Mayo, Homans, Castoriadis — attributed).

## Operationalizations

Burawoy measured it directly from production records (plantwide average ~133.5% in early 1975; distributional clustering at 140% or ~80% as a diagnostic of legitimation crisis) and specified crisis types (system, legitimation, motivational) by the game's failure modes. Uncertainty must be "minimal" but real — too little or too much destroys the game.

## Applications

Core mechanism on [[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-study]]; anchor case for consent-side positions in the [[labor-process-debate]]; standard citation in the sociology of work for games, effort bargains, and shop-floor culture.

## Critiques and Limitations

Single-shop evidence base (two studies, same site); piece-rate machine shops are a shrinking labor-process type; the generalization from making out to consent under monopoly capitalism is the study's contested move, not the ethnographic description ([[labor-process-debate]]).
