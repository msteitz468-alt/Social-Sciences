# Tar

*Tar* (plural *utar*) is the [[tiv|Tiv]] concept of "the territory occupied by a lineage
[[segmentary-lineage-system|segment]]" — analyzed by [[bohannan-paul|Paul Bohannan]] as a
fundamental Tiv category in *[[bohannan-justice-judgment-tiv-1957|Justice and Judgment
Among the Tiv]]* (1957) and in his earlier *Tiv Farm and Settlement* (1954).

## Definition as Coined

A *tar* is a territory associated with, defined by, and named after the agnatic lineage
segment (*ipaven*, of the *nôngo*/lineage) that inhabits it. Bohannan reports that within a
minimal *tar* about 83 per cent of resident males are agnatic members of the associated
lineage. Two adjacent *utar* whose lineages share a common ancestor combine into a larger,
inclusively named *tar*, and so on up to *Tar Tiv* — "Tivland" — so that the segmentary
lineage genealogy and the map of the country are the same image. Tiv have no place names
(other than for streams and hills) except the names of their *utar*.

## Semantic History

*Tar* is a Tiv word; Bohannan's analysis (attributed) turns on its being a *territory-as-
social-group*, not land-as-substance (*nya*, "earth"/"soil"). He also documents a second,
esoteric register: in the secret language of the *mbatsav* (witch-elders), *tar* names a
magical instrument — the owl pipe (*imborivungu*) or the "father's head" (*ityough ki
ter*) — so that "repairing the *tar*" shifts between the jural and the ritual sense
depending on register. The colonial period further loaded the term, as the indigenous *tar*
was overlaid by the British "clan"/administrative district.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Bohannan "operationalizes" *tar* ethnographically by mapping
lineage segments onto territory — reporting, for a minimal *tar*, that about 83 per cent of
resident males are agnatic members of the associated lineage — and by tracing how *utar*
nest upward to *Tar Tiv*, the whole country.

## The two senses of "repairing the tar"

Bohannan's key move (attributed) is that the Tiv phrase "to repair the *tar*" has two
linked meanings, the poles of Tiv social welfare:

- a **governmental/jural** sense — to "sit and listen quietly and dispassionately to all
  sides of a dispute, then to give a just decision"; arbitration, the work of the
  **[[jir]]**;
- a **religious/mystical** sense — in the secret language of the *mbatsav* (witch-elders),
  to perform ceremonies for fetishes such as the owl pipe (*imborivungu*) or the "father's
  head" (*ityough ki ter*), connected to the fertility, health, and prosperity of the group
  and, in belief, requiring human life (*tsav*, witchcraft substance).

*Justice and Judgment* treats only the first ("government") aspect; Bohannan planned a
second study on the *tsav*/witchcraft aspect.

## Applications, Critiques, and Limitations

*Tar* supplies the jurisdictional logic of the *jir* (a case is normally heard in the
defendant's *tar*; theft is tried where committed) and the spatial frame for the whole
analysis. Bohannan cautions that *tar* is not "earth" or "soil" (*nya*) — a territory-as-
social-group, not land as substance — and that British administrators, misreading the
acephalous segmentary system through a chiefly template, distorted its political meaning.
