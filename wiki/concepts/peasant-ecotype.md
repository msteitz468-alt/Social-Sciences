# Peasant Ecotype

## Definition as Coined

In *[[wolf-peasants-1966|Peasants]]* (1966, Ch. 2), [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] defines a peasant **ecotype** as a system of energy transfers between man and environment in production. Master contrast:

- **Paleotechnic** — human/animal muscle; cultivator and non-cultivator typically live off the same crop (cultivator consumes what is also transmitted as tax/sale).
- **Neotechnic** — industrial-scientific energy and inputs; Second Agricultural Revolution forms (horticulture, dairy, mixed farming, tropical cash crops) tying the household to industrial markets and states.

**Paleotechnic classification criterion:** degree of land use over time (plus labor requirement, hoe vs animal plow, growing-season length). **Five major paleotechnic forms:**

1. **Long-term fallowing / swidden** (fire clearing + hoe)
2. **Sectorial fallowing** (2–3 years plant, 3–4 fallow; hoe/digging stick) — restricted influence; set aside
3. **Short-term fallowing / Eurasian grain farming** (plow + draft animals)
4. **Permanent cultivation / hydraulic systems** (assured water supply)
5. **Infield–outfield** and analogues — restricted influence; set aside

Of these, **swidden, short-term fallowing, and hydraulic** are of major evolutionary importance.

## Semantic History

Vocabulary resonates with Lewis Mumford's paleotechnic/neotechnic adapted to agrarian production; First/Second Agricultural Revolution framing. Sits near [[cultural-ecology]] / [[cultural-core]] without reducing peasantry to environment alone — [[types-of-domain|domain]] remains definitional for peasantry.

## Operationalizations

- Energy source and tool system; degree of land use over time; hoe vs plow; growing-season length.
- Paleotechnic five-form inventory (three major) vs neotechnic industrial linkage.
- Implications for surplus size, population density, and who captures [[fund-of-rent]].

## Applications

- **Swidden cycles (Wolf 1966):** Petén Maya often 1 yr use / 4 yr rest; N Yucatán ~10 yr fallow; Hanunóo min 7–8 yr; abandonment also from grasses (Totonac/Philippines) or insects (Amazon). Yields can match hydraulic rice (Yagaw Hanunóo vs Tonkin) or exceed plow averages (Tepoztlán). Carrying-capacity estimates often far above present density.
- **Hydraulic:** dry-zone and SE Asian wet rice; high labor intensity and densities; Millon on irrigation and authority (cited).
- **Eurasian grain:** Mediterranean ard vs northern wheeled plow; Russian steppe expansion.
- **Complementary goods:** *zadruga*, Indian *jajmani* (Rampur), medieval specialists.
- **Markets:** sectional vs network; inelasticity; price scissors; labor markets; Haitian *pratik*.
- Pairs with [[peasant-dilemma]] strategies and [[cultural-ecology]] comparative work (e.g. Geertz Java).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Binary paleo/neo compresses intermediate commercialized systems.
- Survey-level regional inventory, not primary ecological measurement.
- Swidden–state relationship remains contested in later archaeology (not adjudicated here).
