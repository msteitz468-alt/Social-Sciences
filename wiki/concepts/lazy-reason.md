# Lazy Reason

## Definition as Coined

[[de-sousa-santos-boaventura|Boaventura de Sousa Santos]] names **lazy reason** (*razão indolente*) the dominant Western modern mode of knowing that wastes the experience of the world — especially the Global South and non-hegemonic knowledges — by producing absences (making alternatives non-credible or non-existent). His alternative is **cosmopolitan reason**: sociology of absences, sociology of emergences, and the work of translation. Core slogan (attributed): *global social justice is not possible without global cognitive justice*.

## Semantic History

Formulated at length in "A Critique of Lazy Reason: Against the Waste of Experience" (*[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|MWS in the Longue Durée]]*, 2004). Related to Santos's later "epistemologies of the South" monographs (not yet ingested). Dialogues with coloniality of power (Quijano), world-systems [[structures-of-knowledge]], and decolonial critique.

## Operationalizations

Santos distinguishes forms of lazy reason and modes of nonexistence (e.g. ignorance, residuality, inferiority, local particularity, unproductiveness — attributed typology). Remedies: **ecology of knowledges**, ecologies of temporalities/recognition/trans-scale/productivity; diatopical hermeneutics; translation among contact zones.

## Applications

Critique of Northern social science and development discourse; World Social Forum–adjacent knowledge politics; bridge from [[world-systems-analysis]] knowledge critique to Southern epistemologies.

## Critiques and Limitations

Scope can become all-encompassing; criteria for "absence" vs genuine disagreement need case-by-case argument; primary monographs not yet in vault for full architecture.
