# Category Formation

*(Attributed — McAdam, Tarrow & Tilly 2001.)* Relational mechanism creating **social categories** — sets of sites sharing a boundary that distinguishes and relates them to at least one excluded set — thereby reshaping the identities in whose names people make claims.

## Definition as Coined

Category formation proceeds via three submechanisms:

1. **Invention** — authoritative drawing of a boundary and prescription of cross-boundary relations.
2. **Borrowing** — import of a categorical package existing elsewhere.
3. **Encounter** — contact between previously separate networks competing for resources, interactively generating boundary definitions.

None creates complete perimeters or homogeneous populations; mixed cases and variable conformity survive. Formation takes time in discrete increments but powerfully affects claim-making identities (women, citizens, co-religionists, property owners, etc.).

## Semantic History

DOC Part II (esp. Ch. 5 South Asia / South Africa). Adjacent to Tilly’s later [[categorical-inequality]] and [[social-boundary]] / [[social-boundary-mechanism]] programs (*Durable Inequality*; *IBST*) — related but not identical: DOC scopes category formation as a contention mechanism; DI/IBST generalize categorical organization of inequality and boundary change.

## Operationalizations

Emergence or hardening of public categorical pairs in episodes; documentary and ethnographic evidence of invention, borrowing, and encounter.

## Applications

*(DOC attributed illustrations:)* Hindu–Muslim escalation (Panipur, Ayodhya, Hyderabad materials); South African identity politics; polarization processes that include category formation (French Revolution Mountain/Girondin; Maluku Christian–Muslim wars 1998–2000).

## Critiques and Limitations

Overlap with [[identity-shift]] and social-boundary mechanisms; risk of redescription; four Ch. 5 mechanisms (brokerage, category formation, object shift, certification) are a **sample**, not a comprehensive closed model (authors’ own caveat).
