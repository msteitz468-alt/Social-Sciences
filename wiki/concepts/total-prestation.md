# Total Prestation

## Definition as Coined

**Total prestation** (*prestation totale*) is [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]]'s term in *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* (1925) for permanent contractual exchanges between clans or groups in which men, women, children, ritual, and services are put on a communal basis — not mere transfers of goods. Cunnison's translator's note retains French **prestation**: any thing or series of things given freely or obligatorily as a gift or in exchange, including services and entertainments as well as material things.

Mauss distinguishes:

1. **Simpler total prestation** — permanent contracts between groups without exaggerated rivalry (much of Polynesia, Africa, Malaya, South America, etc., as he then mapped it).
2. **Agonistic total prestation** — competitive, usurious, extravagant struggle among nobles for hierarchical position through the clan chief — which he **proposes to call the [[potlatch]]**.

Total prestation, fully understood, carries **three obligations**: to give, to receive, and to repay (not only the obligation of return).

## Semantic History

| Moment | Sense |
|---|---|
| Mauss & Davy earlier work | Contractual gifts; potlatch as North American/Melanesian extreme |
| Mauss 1925 *Gift* | Total prestation as general type; potlatch as agonistic subtype; gift regime as phase between total prestation and pure individual contract / money market |
| Later anthropology | Often collapsed into "gift exchange" or "reciprocity"; Sahlins and others rework the obligation triad without always keeping *prestation* |
| English reception | Cunnison keeps *prestation*; later Halls translation sometimes softens terminology — this wiki's primary ingest is Cunnison |

## Operationalizations

Not quantified. Indicators in Mauss's prose: group (not only individual) contracting; inclusion of persons/ritual/services with goods; permanence of obligations; in the potlatch subtype, rivalry, destruction, and rank stakes.

## Applications

- **Samoa** (via Turner, Krämer): exchange of oloa (masculine/husband-linked property) and tonga/toga (feminine mats and valuables) at birth, marriage, puberty, funerals, trade; fosterage channels; honour/mana of wealth and absolute obligation to return under penalty of losing mana.
- **Maori** (via Best, Hertz papers): [[hau]] of [[gift-exchange|taonga]] explains the force of return within a total-prestation logic.
- **Phase model (Ch. II conclusion):** spirit of gift-exchange characterizes societies that have passed pure total prestation (clan-to-clan) but not yet pure individual contract, money market, fixed price, and coined money.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Evolutionary residual:** the "phase between total prestation and market" framing can read as unilinear developmentalism even as Mauss denies absence of markets.
- **Type vs case:** "total prestation" is an etic umbrella; ethnographic cases (Samoa, Andaman, Kula, potlatch) differ sharply — Mauss's own subtypes (simple vs agonistic) acknowledge this.
- **Secondary evidence:** built from others' field reports, not Mauss's fieldwork.
