# Abangan

## Definition as Coined

In [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]’s *[[geertz-religion-of-java-1960|The Religion of Java]]* (fieldwork 1952–54 at [[javanese-modjokuto|Modjokuto]]), **abangan** names the first of three religious **variants**. Geertz: it “represent[s] a stress on the animistic aspects of the over-all Javanese syncretism and [is] broadly related to the peasant element in the population.” Core ritual: the [[slametan]]. He insists the label is **emic** — “not constructed types, but terms and divisions the Javanese themselves apply.”

## Semantic History

Pre-existing Javanese usage elevated by Geertz to comparative analytic type for Indonesian studies. Later political and scholarly uses (aliran politics, New Order religious classification, revisionist critique) shifted meanings; treat post-1960 senses as dated and attributed when vault has them. Spelling *abangan* follows Geertz 1960.

## Operationalizations

Geertz operationalizes abangan via:

- **Ritual:** slametan cycles (birth, circumcision/marriage, death; calendrical, village, intermittent)
- **Belief:** spirit taxonomy (*memedi*, *lelembut*, *tujul*, *demit*, *danjang*, etc.); *pètungan* numerology; *tjotjog*
- **Specialists:** *dukun* curing and sorcery; contrast with santri *modin*/mosque personnel
- **Organization:** neighborhood territorial base; Permai as modern abangan cult/party-linked form
- **Contrast:** doctrine vs ritual stress vs [[santri]]; animistic vs Hindu-Javanese vs Islamic strands of syncretism

## Applications

*[[geertz-religion-of-java-study|The Religion of Java]]*; Indonesian religious stratification and party analysis; comparative syncretism.

## Critiques and Limitations

Geertz’s own text stresses porous boundaries and **mixed** individuals. Later critics contested reification and political stereotyping of “abangan.” Do not assert the category as a timeless natural kind in wiki voice.
