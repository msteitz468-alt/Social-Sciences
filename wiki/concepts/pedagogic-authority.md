# Pedagogic Authority

*(Attributed — [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]] & [[passeron-jean-claude|Passeron]], [[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|*Reproduction*]], Book I §2.)* The authority (abbreviated **PAu**) that any [[pedagogic-action]] necessarily presupposes as the social condition of its exercise. PAu is the second pillar of the theory of [[symbolic-violence]]: PA can succeed only where it is already invested with a legitimacy that conceals the arbitrary power grounding it.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed.)* Proposition 2: PA "necessarily implies, as a social condition of its exercise, pedagogic authority (PAu) and the relative autonomy of the agency commissioned to exercise it." PAu works through [[misrecognition]]:

> PAu, "by the mere fact of being misrecognized as such, is objectively recognized as a legitimate authority."

Misrecognition here is not a psychological failure but a *structural precondition*: a PA that disclosed its own violence would be self-destructive (the "paradox of Epimenides the liar" applied to pedagogy — a proof *per absurdum* that "every PA requires as the condition of its exercise the social misrecognition of the objective truth of PA"). PAu is thus "the chief instrument of the transubstantiation of power relations into legitimate authority." Its recognition, the authors argue (distancing themselves from [[weber-max|Weber]]'s psychological-genesis account of legitimacy), "is never more total than when it is totally unconscious" — like the acceptance of a currency.

## Semantic History

- **Delegated authority.** Every PA-exerting agency commands PAu "only in its capacity as the mandated representative of the groups or classes whose cultural arbitrary it imposes" — a *limited delegation*. The authors extend this against [[weber-max|Weber]]'s theory of **charisma**: there is "no intrinsic strength of the true idea"; "the successful prophet is the one who formulates for the groups... a message which the objective conditions... have predisposed them to attend to" — the prophet "always preaches to the converted."
- **No normative content.** PAu "has no normative content": it does not depend on the transmitter's actual technical or charismatic qualification, and it "tends to eliminate any question of the informative efficiency of the communication" — even near-zero-information messages sustain the pedagogic relation.
- **School authority (SAu).** In an institutionalized [[relative-autonomy-of-the-educational-system|educational system]], PAu is objectified as *school authority* (SAu) by a two-step delegation, dispensing individual teachers from having to win their own authority. This produces the "paradox of professorial charisma": institutional SAu is misrecognized as the teacher's *personal* authority. Bourdieu and Passeron quote [[weber-max|Weber]] ("The priest dispenses salvation by virtue of his office...") and [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] ("The teacher, like the priest, has a recognized authority, because he is the agent of a moral body greater than himself") — a load-bearing priest/teacher homology.

## Operationalizations

- **Magisterial language and distance.** The lecture setting (platform, chair) imposes distance "even if [the lecturer] did not wish to"; "magisterial language, a status attribute which owes most of its effects to the institution... is able to appear as an intrinsic quality of the person."
- **The "ruse of academic reason."** The institution lets the teacher believe he serves himself (personal freedom, charisma) while he serves "a function of social conservation which academic reason cannot recognize."
- **Professorial ideology of student incapacity.** As with Weber's priest, responsibility for communication failure falls on the students, not the institution — the *exposé* and the student dissertation form a "functional couple" whose latent function is "preventing precise measurement of comprehension."

## Applications

- **Pedagogic communication ≠ pure communication.** Because it presupposes PAu, "the relation of pedagogic communication... is not reducible to a pure and simple relation of communication"; recognition of the transmitter, not just cognitive understanding, conditions internalization as *formation*.
- **The examination** consecrates and legitimates, leading the [[self-elimination|self-eliminated]] to count themselves "among those who fail" (Book II ch. 3).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Circularity of misrecognition.** If recognized authority is legitimate *because* misrecognized, the concept can seem to render itself unfalsifiable — a standing charge; see [[cultural-reproduction-debate]].
- **Relation to Weber and Gramsci.** PAu's mapping onto [[charismatic-authority|charismatic]]/[[legal-rational-authority|legal-rational]] authority and onto Gramscian hegemony is generative but disputed; the authors perform only the Weber contrast themselves.
