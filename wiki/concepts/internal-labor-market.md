# Internal Labor Market

## Definition as Coined

Doeringer and Piore's construct (as quoted by Burawoy): "an administrative unit, such as a manufacturing plant, within which the pricing and allocation of labor is governed by a set of administrative rules and procedures," distinguished from the external labor market. Burawoy's six conditions: a differentiated job structure; institutionalized vacancy posting and application; nonarbitrary selection criteria; on-the-job training; means of generating commitment to the firm; means of maintaining laid-off employees' allegiance.

## Semantic History

Coined in institutional labor economics as an efficiency account (adaptation to enterprise-specific skills). [[burawoy-michael|Burawoy]] (*Manufacturing Consent*, 1979) resituated it: at Geer/Allied the bidding system (from the 1947 job-classification scheme to twelve labor grades and plant-wide bidding by 1975) "internalizes the features most characteristic of the external labor market — the competitive individualism of 'free and equal' laborers," dissolves worker–management tension into worker–worker tension, and coordinates capital and labor's interests through seniority rewards — masking and securing surplus value rather than merely allocating skill. He rejects Doeringer/Piore's skill-specificity account (hard to square with deskilling) and Richard Edwards's "bureaucratic control" reading (which leaves unexplained how rule-following legitimizes power): rules matter because they confine uncertainty and offer workers real, narrow choices — "it is participation in choosing that generates consent" (all attributed).

## Operationalizations

Job ladders/labor grades, posted-bid systems, seniority-scaled benefits (pensions from 1959, supplementary unemployment benefits from 1956, vacation scales, bumping rights at Allied), and mobility rates within vs between firms.

## Applications

[[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-study]] (consent mechanism); labor economics of firm wage structures; segmented-labor-market theory; positions in the [[labor-process-debate]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Efficiency vs control vs consent readings remain contested; Burawoy's own evidence is one monopoly-sector plant; Black workers' bidding-discrimination complaints at Allied (reported, unadjudicated) mark the construct's silence on ascriptive sorting.
