# Space of Flows

Castells’s concept for the **material support of simultaneous social practices** articulated through flows of capital, information, technology, organizational interaction, and symbols — contrasted with the [[space-of-places|space of places]] of everyday experience. Canonical treatment: *[[castells-rise-of-network-society-1996|The Rise of the Network Society]]* Ch. 6; restated in the 2010 preface.

## Definition as Coined

Castells (attributed): “the space of flows is the material organization of time-sharing social practices that work through flows. By flows I understand purposeful, repetitive, programmable sequences of exchange and interaction between physically disjointed positions held by social actors.” 2010 formulation: “the material support of simultaneous social practices communicated at a distance,” involving production, transmission, and processing of information flows and localities as nodes of communication networks.

**Three layers (attributed):**

1. **Circuit of electronic exchanges** — microelectronics, telecom, computer processing, broadcasting, high-speed transportation based on IT; material basis for strategically crucial network-society processes. No place exists by itself; positions defined by exchanges of flows.
2. **Nodes and hubs** — the space of flows is *not placeless*; structural logic is placeless but places are connected by electronically powered networks. Some places are exchangers (hubs); some are coordinators (nodes). Example networks: global financial centers; advanced services.
3. **Spatial organization of dominant managerial elites** — residential/leisure/work spaces of the actors who exercise directional functions; cultural codes and interpersonal networks of the elite.

**Global city hook:** Castells, engaging Sassen, holds that “the global city is not a place, but a process” by which centers of production/consumption of advanced services and ancillary local societies connect in a global network while downplaying hinterland linkages.

## Semantic History

Developed from *The Informational City* (1989, not yet ingested) and full theorization in 1996. Related to but distinct from Sassen’s [[global-city]] (production of control capacity *in* places). Opposed to end-of-cities / pure electronic-cottage theses.

## Operationalizations

Advanced-services location; new industrial space (electronics division of labor); mega-cities (globally connected / locally disconnected); metropolitan regions; elite spatial organization and “architecture of the end of history” / architecture of nudity.

## Applications

[[network-society-theory]]; [[urbanization]]; dialogue with [[global-city-theory]]; media and finance geography.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of dualism (flows vs places) oversimplifying hybrid spaces; empirical identification of “nodes” can reify hierarchy; 1990s case base. Castells himself stresses places remain and that bridges between logics must be built.
