# Correspondence (Ingold)

## Definition as Coined

**Correspondence** is [[ingold-tim|Tim Ingold]]'s term (developed in
*[[ingold-making-2013|Making]]*, 2013, and his later work) for the way lives, materials
and things "answer to" one another as they proceed **along** their paths — a
**joining-with** rather than an *interaction between* pre-formed entities. Making, on
this account, is a correspondence between the practitioner and the material: the maker
attends and responds to what the material is doing, and form grows from that ongoing
answering (attributed, Ingold).

## Semantic History

*(Enriched from [[ingold-making-2013|Making]] during integration.)* Correspondence is
the positive counterpart to Ingold's critique of [[hylomorphism]] and of "interaction"
models (which presuppose already-bounded agents connected across gaps — the network
rather than the [[meshwork]]); it generalizes the [[wayfaring|wayfarer's]] answering
attention to the whole field of life.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct — a relational stance opposed to "interaction." Where
interaction joins closed entities across a gap, correspondence is the ongoing mutual
answering of lines that run *alongside* one another in the [[meshwork]].

## Applications

- **Dance of animacy vs. dance of agency** (Ch. 7): Ingold contrasts his correspondence
  ("dance of animacy" — lives answering one another as they move) with Andrew Pickering's
  "dance of agency" between already-bounded human and material agents, which he reads as
  still an *interaction* between closed entities.
- **The transducer**: the maker's tool (pencil, trowel, brush) is a **transducer** that
  channels the correspondence between practitioner and material, not an instrument that
  imposes a plan.
- **Excavation as correspondence**: archaeological digging is figured as a "line of
  correspondence" with the ground — a joining-with, continuous with
  [[knowing-from-the-inside]] (attributed, Ingold).

## Critiques and Limitations

As with Ingold's other oppositional pairs, "correspondence vs interaction" is a
programmatic contrast whose analytic payoff and empirical testability have been
questioned. *(Enriched during integration.)*
