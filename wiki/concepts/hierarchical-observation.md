# Hierarchical Observation

## Definition as Coined

First of the three "means of correct training" in *[[foucault-discipline-and-punish-1977|Discipline and Punish]]* Part Three, chapter 2. [[foucault-michel|Foucault]] (attributed): "The exercise of discipline presupposes a mechanism that coerces by means of observation; an apparatus in which the techniques that make it possible to see induce effects of power, and in which, conversely, the means of coercion make those on whom they are applied clearly visible."

Classical-age construction of "observatories" of human multiplicity — "eyes that must see without being seen" — a minor art of light and the visible preparing a new knowledge of man, alongside the major technology of the telescope.

## Semantic History

Component term inside [[disciplinary-power]]; perfected and generalized in [[panopticism]]. Not a free-standing school of thought. In reception, often collapsed into "surveillance" generally — keep the **hierarchical** and **architectural/organizational** sense (ranks of supervisors; nested gazes; buildings that see).

## Operationalizations

- Military camps, schools, workshops: continuous and functional surveillance built into architecture and rank
- Relays of observation (supervisor observed by higher supervisor)
- Ideal limit: the Panopticon, where the hierarchy collapses into a single unverifiable center

## Applications

Combined with [[normalizing-judgement]] in the [[examination-foucauldian|examination]]. Feeds study [[foucault-discipline-and-punish-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Thin as a standalone concept outside Foucault's triad; most of its force lives in combination with norm and examination. Not a claim that all observation is disciplinary (scientific observation, mutual peer gaze differ).
