# Middle-Ranging Theory (Trigger)

## Definition as Coined

**Middle-ranging theory**, in [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]]'s (*[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]]* 2006, ch. 9) usage, is an **umbrella category** for the mid-level theoretical work that connects archaeological observations to higher-order explanations. It **includes but is not identical to** Binford's [[middle-range-theory-archaeology|middle-range theory]] (actualistic/ethnoarchaeological bridges from statics to dynamics). Trigger treats Binford middle-range as one type within a broader typology of middle-ranging concerns.

## Semantic History

Binford coined and popularized "middle-range theory" in archaeology for formation-process and ethnoarchaeological inference. Trigger deliberately uses **middle-ranging** (and related "middle-level" language in ch. 1) to avoid collapsing all mid-level work into the Binford program, and to place mid-level work in a three-tier scheme with [[high-level-theory-archaeology|high-level theory]] and data under his [[data-constraint-on-interpretation|data-constraint]] epistemology.

## Operationalizations

Trigger's ch. 9 survey groups multiple mid-level enterprises (exact typology in claims: formation processes, ethnoarchaeology, experimental archaeology, historical analogy, cross-cultural regularities, etc. — as Trigger enumerates). Binford MRT is explicitly **item one** under Trigger's broader umbrella, not the whole.

## Applications

Supports Trigger's "pragmatic synthesis": competing high-level programs can share middle-ranging tools; convergence happens partly at mid-level where data bite hardest.

## Contested Issues

Whether "middle-ranging" is a useful general category or dilutes Binford's specific methodological claim. Keep the two pages cross-linked and unmerged.
