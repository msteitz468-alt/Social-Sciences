# Economy of gratitude

## Definition as Coined

In *[[hochschild-second-shift-1989|The Second Shift]]*, [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]] argued that the interplay of spouses' gender ideologies implies an interplay of **gratitude**: how each wants to identify shapes what will seem like a gift. Example contrast: a man who thinks it unmanly for his wife to earn more may treat "bearing it" as *his* gift; another may treat her higher salary as *her* gift ("When my wife began earning more than me I thought I'd struck gold!"). "When couples struggle, it is seldom simply over who does what. Far more often, it is over the giving and receiving of gratitude" (Ch. 2, attributed).

## Semantic History

Extends the gift-exchange of feeling from *[[hochschild-managed-heart-1983|The Managed Heart]]* into marital accounting under the [[stalled-revolution|stalled revolution]]. Adjacent: "going rate" comparisons to other men (Holt case — Evan claims Nancy should feel lucky because other husbands do less). In clashing couples, appreciation becomes a "dead letter office" — thanks sent to the wrong address (Ch. 14, attributed). The major social revolution of women's employment enters private marriage "through the different appraisals of such 'gifts'" (attributed).

## Operationalizations

Analytic, not a scale:

- What each partner treats as credit-worthy sacrifice
- Asymmetric honor (e.g. putting husband through school counted; reciprocal support for wife's degree rarely observed — Ch. 15)
- [[economy-of-gratitude|Debits/credits]] that tip the balance between spouses' [[gender-strategy|gender strategies]] — women's lower wages, divorce risk, and cultural subordination make many wives feel "lucky" if husbands share "some," muting demands for full equality (Ch. 13, attributed)

## Applications

- **Tanagawa:** salary shaming manhood → inverted gratitude when Nina out-earns Peter
- **Steins:** clashing sacrifices (career hours vs family) → scarcity of gratitude
- **Alstons:** his/hers of sharing — equal time ≠ equal gratitude for primary parenting
- Structural tip: economy of gratitude helps explain why many believe in sharing but few fully practice it

## Critiques and Limitations

- Interpretive reconstruction of meaning; not independently measured gifts
- Risk of psychologizing wage and legal inequalities that Hochschild also names as structural
- Heterosexual dual-earner focus of the source
