# State Evasion

## Definition as Coined

**State evasion** (paired with **state prevention**) is [[scott-james-c|Scott]]’s master frame in *[[scott-art-of-not-being-governed-2009|The Art of Not Being Governed]]* for practices that put distance between people and the padi state’s demand for concentrated, legible, taxable subjects. Not developmental lag or residual primitivity, but **purposeful, reactive statelessness** — social forms adapted to a world of states while remaining outside their firm grasp (attributed — Scott 2009 conclusion).

**Evasion** = keeping out / getting out (flight, maronnage, secondary primitivism).  
**Prevention** = organizing so that a state cannot easily form or grab hold (dispersal, acephaly, “jellyfish” structure, “Divide that ye be not ruled”).

## Semantic History

Builds on Scott’s peasant-resistance corpus and on Clastres (*Society Against the State* — book epigraph). Inverts civilizational narratives that cast hill peoples as “pre-” padi, pre-town, pre-literacy. Conclusion thesis: better as **post–irrigated rice, postsedentary, postsubject, perhaps postliterate**.

## Operationalizations

| Domain | Examples (Scott 2009, attributed) |
|---|---|
| Agro-ecology | [[escape-agriculture]] — [[swidden]], roots/tubers, dispersed fields that raise friction of *appropriation* |
| Settlement | Location in high-friction terrain; mobile/ephemeral villages |
| Social structure | Weak hierarchy, fission, gumlao-type egalitarianism vs gumsa/Shan hierarchy |
| Culture | Orality as strategy; thin historical baggage; ethnolinguistic amphibiousness |
| Politics | Millenarian exit options; resistance to permanent chiefship |

## Applications

[[zomia]] highland–valley dialectic; deep-time hinterlands in *Against the Grain* ([[nonstate-spaces]]); comparative refugia (Appalachia, maroons, etc. as Scott cites).

## Critiques and Limitations

Functionalism risk (every hill trait read as anti-state design); highland states and hierarchies exist; post-1945 technology changes the balance of power (Scott’s own scope limit).
