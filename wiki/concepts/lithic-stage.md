# Lithic Stage

First of five New World **historical-developmental stages** in [[willey-phillips-method-theory-1958|Willey & Phillips]] (1958). Americanist etic construct — **not** identical to Old World [[palaeolithic|Palaeolithic]].

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Willey & Phillips 1958.)*

- **Geochronological definition:** adaptation by immigrant societies to late glacial and early postglacial climatic and physiographic conditions in the New World; working criteria are artifact associations with geological deposits or plant/animal remains reflecting those conditions.
- **Economy:** pre-eminently hunting (including extinct Pleistocene fauna), fully migratory pattern assumed from the nature of finds — with explicit circularity caution; other economic patterns not precluded.
- **Technology:** rough- and chipped-stone traditions; **not** grinding and polishing; bone/horn poorly preserved.
- **Social inference:** extremely hazardous; small-scale kinship organization postulated; simplicity ≠ uniformity.
- **Terminology:** “Lithic” preferred to “Paleo-Indian”/“Paleo-American” because those imply a Paleo/Neo-Indian twofold split; W&P place the grand hinge later, between [[archaic-stage|Archaic]] and [[formative-stage|Formative]].
- **Subdivision:** single stage retained (1955 “Early Lithic” core-flake vs blade split abandoned as independent stages); “lower” percussion stage (Krieger) remains an “intriguing hypothesis” not erected as a separate stage.

## Semantic History

Regional “Paleo-Indian” vocabularies (Clovis, Folsom, Yuma/Eden-Scottsbluff) predate W&P; they reorganized them as one developmental stage in a hemispheric scheme. Later research (pre-Clovis debates, coastal routes, aDNA — [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]], as of Reich 2018 and subsequent work) has revised absolute chronology and peopling models; the **stage label** survives mainly as historiographic Americanist periodization, partly replaced by regional sequences and calibrated chronologies.

## Operationalizations

| Criterion (W&P 1958) | Content |
|---|---|
| Environment | Late glacial / early postglacial associations |
| Fauna | Extinct large herbivores often co-occur |
| Lithics | Chipped specialized points (Clovis, Folsom, Eden-Scottsbluff, etc.) |
| Settlement | Thin sites, kills, blowouts; few houses |

Exemplars they cite (as of 1958 dates): Clovis older + mammoth; Folsom ~8000 B.C. + extinct bison; Eden-Scottsbluff ~4–5k B.C.; High Plains best known; fluted points near-continental N. America; Magellanic and other South American early complexes with few specific N. American fluted resemblances.

## Applications

Hemisphere-scale synthesis chapters; teaching surveys of “earliest Americans”; contrast term when arguing Archaic–Formative as the real developmental hinge.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Chronology and peopling models outdated (radiocarbon calibration, pre-Clovis, genetics).
- Hunting bias from kill-site sampling — W&P flag circularity themselves.
- Stage ≠ absolute period: “belated” Lithic cultures may persist beside Archaic.
- Four-way non-identity: Lithic “cultures” are material constructs, not peoples or languages.
