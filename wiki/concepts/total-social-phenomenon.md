# Total Social Phenomenon

## Definition as Coined

In the Introductory to *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* (1925; Cunnison tr.), [[mauss-marcel|Marcel Mauss]] proposes to call early-society social phenomena that are not discrete **total social phenomena**: each contains "all the threads of which the social fabric is composed." In them, "all kinds of institutions find simultaneous expression: religious, legal, moral, and economic," plus an aesthetic aspect and morphological types.

Evans-Pritchard's introduction (Cunnison 1966) glosses the same idea: archaic exchanges are "total social movements or activities… at the same time economic, juridical, moral, aesthetic, religious, mythological and socio-morphological"; "'Total' is the key word of the Essay."

In the Conclusions Mauss insists research should concern **wholes** — systems in their entirety — not static skeletons dissected into rules, myths, and values: "Nothing in our opinion is more urgent or promising than research into 'total' social phenomena." The advantage is generality and especially **realism**: social facts "in the round."

## Semantic History

| Moment | Sense |
|---|---|
| Durkheimian [[social-fact]] | External, constraining collective ways of acting/thinking/feeling — related parent, not identical |
| Mauss 1925 *Gift* | Total social phenomena as multi-dimensional concrete activities (gift systems as type-case) |
| Later French anthropology / sociology | Holistic method slogan; Lévi-Strauss and others rework "total social fact" vocabulary (translations and later literature vary between *phenomenon* and *fact*) |
| Economic anthropology | Used to refuse isolating "the economic" from kinship, religion, and politics — feedstock for substantivist readings (see [[substantivism]]) |

## Operationalizations

Operationalized comparatively rather than as a survey index: an institution counts as "total" when Mauss can show simultaneous legal, religious, economic, and status functions in the same acts (e.g. [[potlatch]] as total; [[kula]] as extreme gift exchange). Not a measurement construct.

## Applications

- **[[gift-exchange]] / [[total-prestation]]** — type-cases of total phenomena in *The Gift*.
- **[[potlatch]]** — Mauss explicitly: "the potlatch is more than a legal phenomenon; it is one of those phenomena we propose to call total."
- Method: careful comparison confined to documented areas so local colour is not stripped (Mauss's stated method).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Armchair holism:** the "total" reconstruction is Mauss's synthesis from secondary ethnography and legal texts — not field observation of wholes (EP praises this; later fieldworkers sometimes reverse the priority).
- **Scope inflation:** almost any institution can be redescribed as multi-dimensional; the concept risks becoming a methodological slogan rather than a discriminating analytic.
- Relation to Durkheim's [[social-fact]]: total social phenomenon emphasizes **simultaneity of institutional threads** more than exteriority/constraint alone; treat as developed_from Durkheimian holism, not as synonym.
