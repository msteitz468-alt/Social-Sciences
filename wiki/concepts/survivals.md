# Survivals (doctrine of survivals)

## Definition as Coined

[[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]] defined survivals in *[[tylor-primitive-culture-1871|Primitive Culture]]* (1871, Vol. I ch. III–IV) as:

> "processes, customs, opinions, and so forth, which have been carried on by force of habit into a new state of society different from that in which they had their original home, and they thus remain as proofs and examples of an older condition of culture out of which a newer has been evolved." (Tylor 1871)

A survival is a culture-trait that persists after the conditions that made it functional have passed — it is meaningful in an earlier stage, vestigial or merely customary in the present. Tylor's stock examples: the greeting "God bless you" after a sneeze (a survival of soul-belief), children's games and rhymes, ordeals and divination, and superstitions surviving as folklore. (Attributed voice: this is Tylor's evolutionist device, not a wiki-voice law.)

## Semantic History

The concept is the evidential engine of Tylor's [[social-evolutionism|evolutionism]]: survivals are the "milestones along the road" of cultural development, letting the analyst reconstruct vanished earlier stages from residues embedded in the present — a biological-vestige analogy (like the human coccyx). Tylor distinguished mere survivals (harmless persistences) from **revivals** and from survivals that become actively mischievous (his critique of the "occult sciences," [[magic]], and spiritualism as survivals of primitive animism intruding into modern life — the reform edge of his anthropology). The term entered general usage; "survival" in the Tylorian sense is now a period concept, largely abandoned as an analytic because it presupposes the unilinear developmental scale it was meant to demonstrate (a circularity critics flagged).

## Operationalizations

A trait is diagnosed as a survival when it is **functionally unintelligible or anomalous in its present context** but intelligible as functional in a hypothesized earlier stage. This requires an independent reconstruction of the stage sequence — which the [[comparative-method]] supplies, and which is exactly what makes the reasoning vulnerable to circularity.

## Roots in the folklore/antiquarian tradition (Stocking 1987)

Stocking ([[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|*Victorian Anthropology*]], 1987) traces the doctrine's ancestry to the earlier British "popular antiquities" tradition (attributed): Reverend Henry Bourne's *Antiquitates Vulgares* (1725), reworked by Joseph Brand in 1774, explained peasant customs as survivals of pagan or Catholic superstition surviving in oral tradition after Reformation-era suppression — a purgative, degenerationist reading of folk custom rather than a developmental one (see [[degenerationism]]). The 1830s–1850s British folklore movement (Croker, Keightley, Wright, and William John Thoms, who coined the term "folk-lore" in 1846) collected peasant tales and customs as evidential residues; Thomas Keightley in the 1830s posed the question later central to social evolutionism — whether similarities among legends resulted from diffusion/transmission or from independent formation grounded in "the original sameness of the human mind" (an early statement of [[psychic-unity-of-mankind]]). Brand's fragmentary *Popular Antiquities* material became, within a few years, integral source material for Tylor's *Primitive Culture* — directly connecting the antiquarian/folklore tradition's survivals-as-superstition-residue to Tylor's survivals-as-evolutionary-evidence (attributed).

## Maine's survivals vs. Tylor's (Stocking 1987)

Stocking distinguishes (attributed) [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]]'s use of "survivals" — inherited legal remnants — from Tylor's. Maine's concept derived from Savigny's historical jurisprudence and rested on a **demonstrable, documented historical connection** between an ancestor institution and its surviving descendant (e.g. Roman-law "remnants" traceable to a reconstructible parent institution via the [[comparative-method]]). Tylor's survivals, by contrast, rested on no such documented descent — their evidential warrant was instead the **psychic unity of mankind**, which licensed inferring an earlier rational function for an apparently meaningless present custom without requiring any actual historical link between the earlier and later bearers of the custom. "For Tylor, the methodological utility of survivals depended on the psychic unity of man... for Maine, their utility depended on an underlying historical connection" (Stocking 1987, attributed). The two "survivals" concepts are thus evidentially unrelated despite the shared name — one diffusionist/documentary, the other evolutionist/psychological.

## Later fate: Radcliffe-Brown's rejection and Rivers's persistence (Stocking 1987)

[[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]], breaking from his teacher [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]], rejected survival-explanation outright unless grounded in a hypothesis about the custom's *present* function: in his Durkheimian view, the "conservatism" of human culture that lets outmoded customs persist was itself the thing needing explanation, not an adequate explanation on its own (attributed; Stocking 1987) — the functionalist objection already recorded above under Critiques. Rivers himself, even after his 1911 "conversion" from evolutionism to diffusionism, remained heavily dependent on survivals in the sense recorded above (his kinship "fossils"): customs with no present function, explicable only by their utility in a prior social state — showing, per Stocking, real methodological continuity between Tylorian evolutionism and the "neo-ethnological" diffusionism that displaced it.

## Applications

Survivals let Tylor treat folklore, custom, superstition, ritual, and children's play as data for the deep history of culture, founding the anthropological study of folklore and much of the comparative study of religion and custom. [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]]'s *[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|The Golden Bough]]* is built substantially on survival-reasoning: European May rites, harvest customs, fire-festivals, and peasant "relics of tree worship" are read as residues of [[vegetation-spirit]] and [[divine-king]] stages (see [[frazer-golden-bough-study]]).
## Critiques and Limitations

Functionalists (Malinowski especially) attacked the concept head-on: a persisting custom that critics call a "survival" almost always has a **present function** — nothing truly survives without doing current work — so labelling it a survival substitutes conjectural history for analysis of what the custom does now. Boasians attacked the underlying developmental scale. The concept is also circular (it presupposes the stage-sequence it is used to evidence) and unfalsifiable (any anomaly can be relabelled a survival). It survives mainly as a historical marker of how founding anthropology reasoned. See [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]] and [[evolutionism-diffusionism-debate]].

## Rivers' "fossil" use

[[rivers-w-h-r|W. H. R. Rivers]] gave survival-reasoning its most systematic kinship
application (attributed): in *[[rivers-melanesian-society-1914|The History of Melanesian
Society]]* (1914) he held that "in systems of relationship we have, **like fossils, the hidden
indications of ancient social institutions**." Anomalous terminological correspondences were
read as residues of extinct marriage forms — the [[cross-cousin-marriage]] pattern, the
[[gerontocracy|gerontocratic]] marriages — reconstructed via the [[genealogical-method]].
Notably, Rivers' method converted survivals from a device of *evolutionary* stage-reconstruction
(Tylor) into a device of *diffusionist* culture-history, while remaining vulnerable to the same
functionalist objection: his own Tavua (Fiji) case shows fossil terminology outlasting the
practice it supposedly evidences.
