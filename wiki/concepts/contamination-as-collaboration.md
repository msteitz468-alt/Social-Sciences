# Contamination as Collaboration

## Definition as Coined

In *[[tsing-mushroom-2015|The Mushroom at the End of the World]]* (Ch. 2), [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Tsing]] answers how a gathering becomes a "happening" greater than the sum of its parts with **contamination**: encounters change who we are as we make way for others; purity is not an option; changing with circumstances is the stuff of survival under [[precarity]] *(Tsing 2015)*.

Against popular U.S. fantasies equating survival with conquest, she argues staying alive for every species requires livable collaborations across difference — which leads to contamination *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Semantic History

Resonates with [[friction]] (awkward engagement of difference) from *[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]* (2005) but centers multi-species encounter and purity critique. Contaminated diversity is the research object of her "rush of stories" method *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Operationalizations

| Domain (Tsing 2015) | Contaminated diversity example |
|---|---|
| Oregon forests | Fire exclusion + logging → lodgepole thickets that fruit matsutake after 40–50 years |
| Picker histories | Mien and other groups made through imperial, refugee, and U.S. encounters |
| Economy/ecology theory | Critique of self-contained individual maximizers in neoclassical economics and population genetics |

Collaboration is already polluted by histories of encounter including extermination and imperialism; category names (ethnicity, species) should commit analysts to tracing assemblages in which categories gain only momentary hold *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Contamination metaphor can be read as underplaying domination. Tsing insists on both creative and destructive sides of encounter and does not treat contamination as automatically benign.
