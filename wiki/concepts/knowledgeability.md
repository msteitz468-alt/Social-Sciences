# Knowledgeability

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: everything which actors know (believe) about the circumstances of their action and that of others, drawn upon in the production and reproduction of that action, including **tacit as well as discursively available** knowledge (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Central rejoinder to structural sociologies that treat actors as cultural dopes. Bounds: unconscious; unacknowledged conditions; unintended consequences. **Mutual knowledge** (how to go on) shared by lay actors and observers is condition of valid description.

## Operationalizations

Not IQ. Ethnographic recovery of practical mastery; accounts for discursive layer only.

## Applications

- Willis example: oppositional pupils more knowledgeable about school authority on some dimensions (*Constitution* Ch. 6).
- [[double-hermeneutic]] and mutual knowledge vs common sense distinction.

## Critiques and Limitations

"How much" knowledgeability is always contestable; risk of over-rationalizing agents.
