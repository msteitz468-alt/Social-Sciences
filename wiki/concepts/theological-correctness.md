# Theological Correctness

## Definition as Coined

[[barrett-justin-l|Barrett]]'s term (White 2021): **theologically correct** ideas are those people explicitly endorse when they have time to reflect and that align with official doctrine; **theologically incorrect** ideas are the more intuitive representations that surface under cognitive load, time pressure, or strong emotion — not judgments of true doctrine, but labels for reflective vs online cognition.

## Semantic History

CSR dual-process application to religion; related to "practiced naturalness" vs maturational naturalness (White ch. 2).

## Operationalizations

Speeded judgment tasks; God-concept studies where Christians affirm omnipotence reflectively but reason as if God has limited attention online (White ch. 2–3, 7).

## Applications

Shows self-report belief ≠ everyday reasoning (Evidence-Class Framework: self-report vs behavior); explains coexistence of karma and luck, anger at God, etc.

## Critiques and Limitations

"Correctness" language can sound normative; operationalization depends on which theology is taken as reference; cultural variation in orthodoxy institutions.
