# Legal-Rational Authority

## Definition as Coined

For [[weber-max|Weber]], **legal-rational rule** (*legale Herrschaft*) is one of the three pure types of [[legitimate-domination|legitimate domination]]. Its ground of legitimacy is belief in the **validity of legal statute** and of rationally created rules, and in the functional competence of persons elevated by those rules — obedience is owed to the *impersonal order*, not to the person of the ruler (Ch.3 §2–§3 of *[[weber-economy-society-1921|Economy and Society]]*, attributed throughout).

Weber states that legal rule rests on interconnected ideas: (1) that any law can be enacted for any end by procedure or agreement; (2) that every body of law is a consistent system of abstract rules deliberately established; (3) that the typical person in authority is himself subject to an impersonal order, orienting commands to it; (4) that the person who obeys obeys *as a member* and obeys the *law*; (5) that members obey the office-holder only in his capacity as holder of that office, within a defined sphere of competence (Ch.3 §3).

The purest administrative form of legal rule is [[bureaucracy]] (Ch.3 §4) — monocratic, documented administration by free, salaried, examined career officials separated from ownership of the means of administration. *[[weber-politics-as-a-vocation-1919|Politics as a Vocation]]* (1919) gives the compact public statement: authority "by virtue of *legality*," of belief in legal statute and functional competence, exercised by the modern "servant of the state."

## Semantic History

English reception often renders the type as "legal-rational **authority**" (Parsons) or as one of three "types of legitimate **domination**" (Gerth & Mills; Roth–Wittich). Keith Tribe's 2019 translation prefers **legal rule** / **legal rulership**, rejecting "authority" as a systematic equivalent of *Herrschaft* (see [[domination-herrschaft]], [[weber-interpretive-sociology-debate]]). This wiki keeps the familiar classificatory label "legal-rational authority" for findability while treating Weber's claim-structure in Tribe's "rule" vocabulary where quoting the 2019 text.

The type is the political-administrative face of the broader process of [[rationalization]] and of [[formal-and-substantive-rationality|formal rationality]] — rule by abstract, calculable norms rather than by personal grace or sacred tradition.

## Operationalizations

- **Ground of obedience:** belief that a command is valid because enacted under recognized procedure / statute.
- **Form of the staff:** salaried, examined, hierarchical [[bureaucracy|officials]] with defined competence (contrast retainers, disciples, prebendaries).
- **Relation of ruler to order:** even the highest office-holder is, in the pure type, subject to the impersonal legal order (ministers and elected heads may be only partially pure).
- **Sphere of competence:** obedience limited to the official's jurisdiction, not total personal submission.

## Applications

Organizing frame for the modern [[the-state|state]], large firms, parties, churches, and armies insofar as they administer through legal offices. In this wiki it pairs with [[bureaucracy]] as pure form, contrasts with [[traditional-authority]] and [[charismatic-authority]], and supplies the legitimacy-claim side of Weber's account of mass democracy and expert officialdom (including the *Beamtenherrschaft* problem in *Politics as a Vocation*).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Ideal type.** Weber insists pure legal-rational rule never appears historically pure; real regimes mix traditional and charismatic residues.
- **Formal vs. substantive.** Formal legality can serve any end; critics (and Weber himself on the "iron cage" of modern life) stress that formal rationality of law and administration does not guarantee substantive justice or freedom — see [[formal-and-substantive-rationality]].
- **Democracy and leadership.** Legal-rational bureaucracy can hollow out political leadership; Weber's late politics pairs legal-rational staff with [[plebiscitary-leader-democracy|plebiscitary]] selection of leaders.
- **Translation.** Whether "authority," "domination," or "rule" best names the type is part of the live reception dispute ([[weber-interpretive-sociology-debate]]).
