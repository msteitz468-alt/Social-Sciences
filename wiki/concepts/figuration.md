# Figuration

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Elias, civilizing-process program.)* A **figuration** is a web of interdependent people whose reciprocal constraints and power balances shape both social structure and individual psychical make-up. Elias uses the term (and related language of configurations/interweaving) to avoid the false choice between "individual" and "society" as separate substances: people always already exist in shifting interdependencies ([[civilizing-process]], *[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]*).

## Semantic History

In the 1939 work, configuration/interdependence language is load-bearing even where later program vocabulary "figuration" is more explicit. Postwar figurational sociology made the term a school label. Distinct from Bourdieu's [[social-field]] (homology of positions and stakes within a relatively autonomous game) though both reject atomistic individualism — do not equate without argument.

## Operationalizations

Historical-process case analysis of interdependence chains (e.g., warrior competition → court → state monopolies); power-balance descriptions; lengthening chains of interdependence under pacification.

## Applications

Unit of analysis for [[civilizing-process]] research; court society as figuration ([[court-society]]); monopoly struggles as changing power ratios within figurations.

## Critiques and Limitations

Ambiguity relative to "network," "system," or "field"; risk of re-describing any social relation as a figuration without added explanatory power (critical position — see [[civilizing-process-debate]]).
