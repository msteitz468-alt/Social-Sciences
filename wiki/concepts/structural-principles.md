# Structural Principles

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: principles of organization of **societal totalities**; factors involved in the overall institutional alignment of a society or type of society (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Ch. 4 typology (as Giddens presents): tribal (tradition–kinship); class-divided (city–countryside); capitalist (state–economy disembedding; surveillance). Related to **contradiction** (opposition of structural principles that depend on and negate each other).

## Operationalizations

Comparative historical analysis of institutional alignment — not a coefficient.

## Applications

- Analysing social change via episodes, intersocietal systems, time-space edges, world time (Ch. 5) rather than evolutionary covering laws.
- Clustering of institutions and identity/legitimacy criteria of "societies."

## Critiques and Limitations

Typology risks reifying societal types; empirical boundaries contested.
