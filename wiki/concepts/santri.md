# Santri

## Definition as Coined

In [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]’s *[[geertz-religion-of-java-1960|The Religion of Java]]*, **santri** is the second religious variant: “a stress on the Islamic aspects of the syncretism,” associated “in a broad and general way” with the trading element (and with richer peasants who complete the pilgrimage and found schools). Dual sense in Geertz: (1) the Islamic **variant**; (2) a religious **student** in the pondok system.

## Semantic History

Emic Javanese/Indonesian term; Geertz’s dual usage (variant vs student) is load-bearing for educational chapters. Later Indonesian education and political vocabulary retained and reworked the term.

## Operationalizations

Geertz:

- **Orthopraxy:** careful execution of prayers, Fast, Pilgrimage; Friday service; *zakat* practices
- **Ideology:** **kolot** (conservative) vs **modéren** (modernist, e.g. Muhammadijah) oppositions — fate/work, totalistic/narrowed religion, syncretic/purist, experience/behavior, scholastic/pragmatic
- **Institutions:** pondok/pesantrèn, langgar, mosque, tarèkat; madrasah and modern religious schools; Ministry of Religion / KUA marriage-divorce; party ecology (Masjumi, NU, PSII)
- **Social base:** larger traders; village *hadji* leadership; not all traders are santri

## Applications

Anthropology of Indonesian Islam; *[[geertz-religion-of-java-study|Religion of Java]]* study; aliran politics.

## Critiques and Limitations

Association with trade is probabilistic; rural santri strong; boundary with [[abangan]] is a live dispute in later literature.
