# Communicative Rationality

## Definition as Coined

**Communicative rationality** is [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]'s term (attributed) for the rationality of speech and action oriented to **reaching understanding** — the capacity of speaking and acting subjects to raise, accept, or reject **criticizable [[validity-claims]]** with reasons. In *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|The Theory of Communicative Action, Vol. 1]]* he contrasts it with **cognitive-instrumental** rationality (mastery of an objective world through technically and strategically successful action). Communicative rationality is procedural and multi-dimensional: it encompasses propositional **truth**, normative **rightness**, and subjective **truthfulness/sincerity**, not only efficiency of means.

Habermas (attributed) ties the concept to a **decentered** modern understanding of the world (via Piaget): once objective, social, and subjective worlds are differentiated, speakers can relate utterances simultaneously to all three and redeem the corresponding claims in argumentation. Albrecht Wellmer's gloss, which Habermas cites approvingly, calls this **discursive rationality** — a meta-level procedural standard for dealing with incoherence and dissension, not a "parasitic" substantive standard of one cultural domain.

## Semantic History

- **1970s.** Prepared in Habermas's essays on universal/[[formal-pragmatics|formal pragmatics]] and the ideal speech situation; still partly transcendental in cast.
- **TCA Vol. 1 (1981/1984).** Systematic statement: rationality as use of knowledge in speech and action; three world-relations; argumentation types; defense against Winch-style relativism and against reducing reason to [[instrumental-reason|instrumental reason]].
- **TCA Vol. 2 (1981/1987).** Communicative rationality becomes the normative standard against which [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld|colonization of the lifeworld]] is measured — the "inner logic" of resistance, not "impotent rage of nature in revolt."
- **Later.** Discourse ethics and deliberative-democracy theory operationalize the concept as procedures of discursive will-formation (not ingested here as primary texts).

## Operationalizations

Not measured as an index. Operationalized analytically by:

1. whether coordination rests on illocutionary uptake of criticizable claims rather than on force, reward, or media steering;
2. whether participants can take a "yes/no" position with reasons;
3. whether cultural rationalization has differentiated cognitive, moral-practical, and aesthetic-expressive value spheres without collapsing them into purposive calculation.

Habermas stresses (attributed) that the concept is **historically situated** — formal-pragmatic structures become empirically available as the [[lifeworld]] rationalizes — not a free-floating transcendental given.

## Applications

- Foundation of the whole [[theory-of-communicative-action]].
- Reconstructive standard for modern pathologies (loss of meaning / loss of freedom re-read as selective rationalization and colonization).
- Normative backbone of discourse ethics and deliberative democracy (later program).
- Explicit alternative to first-generation [[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt School]] totalization of [[instrumental-reason]] — see [[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Idealization** (attributed to critics): the ideal of unconstrained consensus underplays power, exclusion, and strategic mimicry of communicative form (Foucauldian, feminist, and systems-theoretic objections).
- **Formalism** (Habermas partly concedes): procedural universalism "does not consider the price that communicative reason has to pay for its victory in the coin of concrete ethical life" (*Sittlichkeit*).
- **Eurocentrism charge** (attributed): the decentration story and the modern world-understanding as universal developmental threshold remain contested in postcolonial and anthropological debates (Winch line; later literature not ingested).
- **Burden of proof:** Habermas himself states that asserting universal communicative rationality incurs a heavy reconstructive and empirical burden — TCA is his attempt to discharge it via the history of sociological theory rather than pure transcendental deduction.
