# Hydraulic Density

## Definition as Coined

In *[[wittfogel-oriental-despotism-1957|Oriental Despotism]]* Ch. 6, [[wittfogel-karl-a|Wittfogel]] analyzes **hydraulic density** — the degree to which large-scale water control sits at the center of economy and government. He distinguishes **core**, **margin**, and **submargin** of hydraulic societies and discusses shifts in density (including conquest and collapse cases).

## Semantic History

Internal typology within Wittfogel's single hydraulic type; related to (but not identical with) Steward's irrigation-civilization density discussions. Not a modern quantitative hydrology metric.

## Operationalizations

Qualitative: relative importance of government-managed water works; frequency of subtypes; decreasing importance of hydraulic economy proper at margins; historical movement between density positions (e.g. Wittfogel's Russia / Tatar and Spanish Reconquista narratives — attributed).

## Applications

Explains variation among societies Wittfogel still classes as "hydraulic" or Oriental-despotic without requiring identical irrigation intensity everywhere.

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundary criteria can be elastic; risk of reclassifying inconvenient cases as "marginal" post hoc — flagged in [[hydraulic-hypothesis-debate]].
