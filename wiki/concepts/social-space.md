# Social Space

Bourdieu's relational construct of the overall structure of social positions — distinct from specialized [[social-field|fields]] of practice, but homologous with the space of lifestyles. In *[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]*, the principal dimensions are **volume of capital**, **composition of capital** (economic vs cultural), and **trajectory**.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, Distinction, Part II.)* Social class is not defined by a single property (not even the most determinant) but by “the structure of relations between all the pertinent properties.” Primary differences between classes derive from overall volume of capital; secondary differences within classes (class fractions) derive from asset structure. Economic and cultural capital distributions are often **chiastic** among dominant fractions (teachers high culture/lower economic vs employers the reverse). Geographical space compounds social distance from scarce assets. Bourdieu breaks with one-dimensional “social ladder” mobility imagery.

## Semantic History

*Distinction* is the major empirical statement; later field theory specializes spaces of positions within domains (art, science, housing) while social space names the global topology. Related to but broader than Weberian status groups; reworks class/*Stand* into one relational geometry. Distinct from Blau space or other multidimensional stratification models in mechanism (capital forms + habitus).

## Operationalizations

Occupational class fractions with indicators of income, education, father’s occupation, trajectory; correspondence analysis of lifestyle variables superimposed on position space; secondary INSEE tables for economic capital and mobility.

## Applications

Frames all of *Distinction*’s lifestyle and political homologies; basis for reconversion analysis; template for later national “fields of power” and lifestyle maps.

## Critiques and Limitations

Fraction coding is theory-laden; capital “volume” is a theoretical artifact whose conversion rates are fought over; gender, race, age often secondary; freezes a struggle into a survey snapshot (Bourdieu’s own caution).
