# Utopia (Utopian Mentality)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[mannheim-ideology-and-utopia-1936|Ideology and Utopia]]* (Part IV), [[mannheim-karl|Mannheim]] defines a **utopian** state of mind as one incongruous with the reality in which it occurs — oriented toward objects that do not exist in the actual situation — but **not** every reality-transcending orientation counts:

> Only those orientations transcending reality will be referred to by us as utopian which, when they pass over into conduct, tend to shatter, either partially or wholly, the order of things prevailing at the time.

**[[ideology|Ideologies]]**, on his pairing, may also be situation-transcendent, but they tend to **maintain** the existing order or fail to transform it when enacted. Both are reality-transcending; they differ in relation to the order and in historical function. Who labels a complex “utopia” vs “ideology” is itself political: dominant strata, he argues, call unrealizable-in-principle what may be only unrealizable *within their order* (**relative** vs **absolute** utopia). Realization is often only a **retroactive** criterion.

**Utopian mentality** names the case where utopia permeates the whole range of experience, action, and outlook of a group — not isolated wish-images. Wishful thinking as mere complementary fantasy is distinguished from oppositional utopia.

## Semantic History

- Pre-Mannheim literary/philosophical “utopia” (More, etc.) is treated as a descriptive tradition Mannheim’s constructive abstraction partly sets aside for sociological analysis.
- Mannheim’s four modern **forms** (ideal-types, Weberian caution — not pure embodiments) map successive then coexisting counter-utopias:
  1. **Orgiastic chiliasm** of the Anabaptists / Münzer line — oppressed strata; absolute presentness (*Kairos*); revolution as value; lowest-strata social base after Peasant Revolt clarification.
  2. **Liberal-humanitarian idea** — bourgeoisie and intellectual class; formal infinite-future regulative goal; culture/freedom/personality; unilinear progress; toning-down of sudden change.
  3. **Conservative idea** — originally no utopia (harmony with mastered reality); counter-utopia arises under progressive attack; past as value-creator; organic growth vs invention; quietism risk.
  4. **Socialist-communist utopia** — Janus-faced synthesis under multi-sided attack; shares remote freedom/equality with liberalism while specifying capitalist breakdown; economic-social structure as absolute reality; assimilates conservative determinism into progressive horizon; Marx vs Bakunin as decisive for end of pure Chiliastic utopianism.
- Closing claim (attributed): complete disappearance of the utopian element would leave man a “mere creature of impulses,” a “thing,” losing the will to shape history and therewith the ability to understand it — whereas unmasking ideology is a crisis mainly for certain strata.

Later everyday English “utopian” often severs this sociological pair and pejorative sense.

## Operationalizations

Qualitative historical-sociological typology; constellation analysis of successive then coexisting forms; time-sense structure of each mentality. Not operationalized as a survey construct in this source.

## Applications

- Diagnosis of modern political styles and their knowledge forms (cross-link Part III styles of thought).
- Bridge from ideology theory to historical sociology of intellectual and political movements.
- Later critical theory and sociology of intellectuals inherit fragments (utopia’s “disappearance,” intellectuals as carriers) without always retaining Mannheim’s full four-form apparatus.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Boundary ideology/utopia is, Mannheim concedes, value-laden and hard; retroactive realization criterion limits predictive use.
- Historical carriers (Anabaptists, etc.) are schematic ideal-types — specialist historiography may contest detail.
- Pejorative political use of “utopian” collapses his technical distinction.
- Berger & Luckmann’s redefinition of [[sociology-of-knowledge]] demotes utopia/ideology analysis from exclusive SoK centre without refuting the typology as history of ideas.
