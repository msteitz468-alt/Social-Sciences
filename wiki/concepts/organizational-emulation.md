# Organizational Emulation (Tilly)

## Definition as Coined

[[tilly-charles|Tilly]] (1998): **emulation** is the copying of established organizational models and/or categorical distinctions from one setting to another. With [[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]], one of two **reinforcing** mechanisms that cement [[categorical-inequality]] installed by [[exploitation]] and [[opportunity-hoarding]]. Relies chiefly on **scripting** (transferable scripts of roles and relations).

## Semantic History

**Not** Veblen's [[pecuniary-emulation]] (status competition via display). Tilly's sense is organizational isomorphism of categorical structures — closer to diffusion/isomorphism literatures than to conspicuous consumption.

## Operationalizations

Adoption of job titles, rank systems, and category matches across firms, armies, schools, colonies; borrowed scripts of gender or race relations in new workplaces.

## Applications

Military/academy codes; firm hierarchies modeled on other firms; colonial administrative categories transferred across territories; governments imitating other governments' forms of inequality; nationalism transplanting categorical scripts; health-care professional scripts diffused across organizations (Tilly Ch. 6–7).

## Critiques and Limitations

Overlap with institutional isomorphism (DiMaggio/Powell not central in this text); scripting vs local invention hard to separate in practice.
