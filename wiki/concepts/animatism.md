# Animatism

## Definition as Coined

[[marett-r-r|R. R. Marett]] proposed **animatism** (also "pre-animism") as a stratum of religious feeling prior to [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]]'s [[animism]]: a diffuse sense of impersonal supernatural power or awe (akin to Melanesian [[mana]]) preceding belief in individual spirits (attributed).

## Semantic History

The term entered debates over the origins of religion in the 1900s and remains associated with Marett's revision of Tylorian intellectualism. Its later use is limited; it survives mainly as a historiographic marker in [[stocking-after-tylor-1995|Stocking's account]] of the "Frazerian moment."

(Attributed — [[stocking-after-tylor-1995|Stocking, *After Tylor*]], 1995.) Marett introduced the term at the British Association meeting, autumn 1899, drafting the paper on Jersey from a "small bundle of extracts" compiled by his wife Nora Kirk. He argued primitive religion was both "wider" and "vaguer" than [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]]'s [[animism]] ("belief in spiritual beings") — savage religion was "something not so much thought out as danced out." His 1904 *Folk-Lore* critique of [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]]'s magic→religion→science sequence extended the same move against intellectualism, charging that Frazer's "purely analytical method" had reified classificatory categories into a "pseudo-genetic" historical sequence, and calling Frazer's rationalism an instance of "the psychologist's fallacy." Marett's positive alternative rooted magico-religious behavior in emotion and "imperative willing" — the spell as a "spiritual projectile" — explicitly invoking William James's essay "The Will to Believe." He combined animatism with **"negative mana"** (drawing on R. H. Codrington's Melanesian *mana*) into a composite "tabu-mana formula" naming both aspects of supernatural power under the category "magico-religious."

## Operationalizations

Not operationalized quantitatively; a typological/developmental construct.

## Applications

Invoked to interpret [[mana]] and other impersonal-power concepts as pre-animistic.

## Critiques and Limitations

Critiqued as another speculative evolutionary origin-story; see [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]].
