# Polythetic Classification

Analytical classification rule that [[clarke-david-l|David L. Clarke]] made central to *[[clarke-analytical-archaeology-1978|Analytical Archaeology]]*: archaeological entities (artefact-types, assemblages, cultures, culture groups, technocomplexes) are **polythetic sets**, not monothetic key-character taxa.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Clarke, Definitions / Sokal & Sneath 1963 framing.)*

**Polythetic:** an aggregate of entities or systems in which (i) each individual possesses a large but unspecified number of the attributes of the aggregate, (ii) each attribute is possessed by large numbers of these individuals, and (iii) **no single attribute is both sufficient and necessary** for membership.

**Monothetic** (contrast): possession of a unique set of attributes is both sufficient and necessary for membership.

Clarke argued that no two artefacts in a type share completely identical attribute sets, so exclusive monothetic attribute-list definitions fail; prehistorians had often pretended monothetic types and cultures, obscuring that most archaeological groupings are polythetic.

## Semantic History

- **Numerical taxonomy (biology):** Sokal & Sneath formalized mono-/polythetic aggregates for clustering and phenetic classification.
- **Clarke 1968:** imported the distinction as the structural principle of the entire archaeological entity hierarchy — not a specialist side method but the default ontology of types and cultures.
- **Later archaeology:** "polythetic" becomes standard caution against trait-list essentialism; operational numerical clustering uptake remains uneven; culture-history practice often remains de facto monothetic in textbook type fossils.

## Operationalizations

| Level (Clarke) | Polythetic content |
|---|---|
| Artefact-type | Recurrent range of attribute states within a polythetic set; affinity often ~30–60% |
| Assemblage | Associated contemporary artefact-types (not all types in every assemblage) |
| Culture | Polythetic set of comprehensive artefact-type categories recurring in assemblages in a limited area |
| Culture group | Shared multistate types with differing states across transform cultures |
| Technocomplex | Shared type *families*, not same specific types (specific-type affinity often ≤5%; type-family ~30–60%) |

Clarke also described type polythetic sets as nucleated constellations: core clusters of tightly covarying attributes, essential attributes at moderate correlation, outer penumbra of sporadic attributes.

## Applications

- Reform of type and culture definition in [[clarke-analytical-archaeology-study|Analytical Archaeology]].
- Critique of *fossile directeur* / monothetic culture packages in [[culture-historical-archaeology]].
- Bridge to [[numerical-taxonomy]] and multivariate typology (Beaker attributes, Hallstatt swords, Acheulean handaxes as illustrations).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Polythetic membership rules still require theory-laden choice of attributes and affinity thresholds.
- Pure polythesis is rare (Clarke noted functional nuclei, e.g. all scrapers have retouch, prevent full polythesis).
- Does not by itself supply social interpretation of clusters — only classification hygiene.
