# Theoretical Anti-Humanism

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[althusser-louis|Althusser]], “Marxism and Humanism,” *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*.)*

**Theoretical anti-humanism** is Althusser’s name for the claim that mature Marxist science does **not** take “man,” “the human essence,” or philosophical anthropology as its theoretical foundation. It is a thesis **about theory**, not a moral program against persons.

> Strictly in respect to theory, therefore, one can and must speak openly of Marx’s theoretical anti-humanism, and see in this theoretical anti-humanism the absolute (negative) precondition of the (positive) knowledge of the human world itself, and of its practical transformation. It is impossible to know anything about men except on the absolute precondition that the philosophical (theoretical) myth of man is reduced to ashes.

**Humanism as ideology (attributed):** In the couple “humanism–socialism,” socialism is a scientific concept; humanism is ideological — it can designate real relations and serve as a practical slogan, but does not give knowledge of essences. Socialist or “class” humanism may be a legitimate **ideological** form in a socialist social formation without being a theoretical foundation of historical materialism.

**Corollary:** Recognizing humanism as ideology does not “dissipate” ideology’s existence; ideology is an objective social reality (lived relation; largely unconscious structure) that survives even under socialism (Althusser in this essay — **not** the later ISA theory of interpellation).

## Operationalizations

Theoretical rule of reading: refuse explanations that begin from human nature, species-being, or alienation-as-essence; begin from concepts of [[social-formation]], productive forces, relations of production, superstructure, ideologies. Demotes [[alienation]] from key of Marxism to a concept of the Early Works / residual later uses.

## Applications

- Charter for [[structural-marxism]]’s anti-subjectivist social theory.
- Critique of ethical-humanist readings of *Capital*.
- Distinction between practical-ideological slogans and scientific concepts (Complementary Note on “real humanism”).

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Positions — see [[marxist-humanism-debate]].)*

- **Humanist Marxism:** evacuates emancipation, ethics, and the lived experience of oppression; technocratic.
- **Historicist / praxis traditions** (Sartrean, Gramscian, some Lukácsian receptions): theory without a subject cannot account for revolutionary agency.
- **Althusser’s own later self-criticism** of theoreticism softens the isolation of theory but does not restore anthropological humanism as foundation.
- **Not identical to** “anti-humanism” in structuralism generally (Lévi-Strauss) or in post-structuralism — related family, different theses.
