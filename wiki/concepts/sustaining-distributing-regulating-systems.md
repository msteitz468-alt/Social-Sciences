# Sustaining, Distributing, and Regulating Systems

## Definition as Coined

Spencer’s three great **systems of organs** in the [[social-organism]] (*Principles* Part I):

| System | Social content (Spencer) | Organic analogue (Spencer) |
|---|---|---|
| **Sustaining** | Industrial/productive structures that feed the aggregate | Alimentary |
| **Distributing** | Roads, markets, commercial and communicational channels | Vascular / circulatory |
| **Regulating** | Governmental-military control structures (and related) | Nervous / neuro-motor |

He also described an **industrial regulating** apparatus that becomes partly autonomous at higher stages (guilds, unions, coordination of industries) — a third regulating strand inside the sustaining sphere. proposed_by: [[spencer-herbert]] · part_of: [[social-evolutionism]]

Order of development (Spencer): outer/inner contrasts and sustaining arrangements appear early; distributing channels elaborate with complexity; regulating systems are originated and improved especially by **war** (corporate action against other societies).

## Semantic History

Direct ancestor of later “subsystems” talk (economy, polity, integration) without one-to-one mapping. Structure-functional division of labour among institutional spheres absorbs the intuition. Cybernetic “control and communication” readings (Andreski) emphasize the regulating system’s afferent/efferent homologues (intelligence, orders, roads, wires).

## Operationalizations

- **Sustaining:** division of labour, slavery/serfdom/free labour as how the operative part is organized; localization of industries.
- **Distributing:** roads (militant roads for troop/supply movement vs commercial roads), fairs, trade, currency/credit evolution (Part IV).
- **Regulating:** political heads, ministries, military systems, law, revenue; ecclesiastical structures as specialized moral-regulative organs ([[priesthood]]).

## Applications

Architectonic for the *Principles*: Part II ≈ regulating (political); Part III ≈ ecclesiastical regulative-moral; Part IV ≈ sustaining (and industrial regulation). Militant type maximizes binding of sustaining to regulating (commissariat); industrial type allows sustaining system more self-direction under negative political regulation.

## Critiques and Limitations

Core of organicism critique if taken as proof rather than map. Systems can reify boundaries that are historically porous. Spencer’s war-origin thesis for government is a strong functional claim — record as attributed, not as the only modern theory of state origins.
