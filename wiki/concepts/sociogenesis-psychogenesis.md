# Sociogenesis and Psychogenesis

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Elias, *The Civilizing Process*.)* **Sociogenesis** is the long-term formation of social structures, standards, and figurations; **psychogenesis** is the formation of individual psychical structures (drive controls, fears, habitus). Elias's "sociogenetic ground rule" claim: individuals in their short development pass through abbreviated forms of processes their society traversed over centuries; the psychogenesis of adult make-up in civilized societies cannot be understood independently of the sociogenesis of civilization.

## Semantic History

Programmatic pair in the book's subtitle (*Sociogenetic and Psychogenetic Investigations*). Stands against psychology that treats adult personality as socially unformed and history that treats institutions without affect structures.

## Operationalizations

Parallel reading of manners standards (social) and childhood socialization demands (psychical); court/state process narratives linked to changes in aggressiveness and shame.

## Applications

Organizing frame of *[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]*; Part Two (psychical standard shifts) + Part Three (social monopolies) synthesized in Part Four.

## Critiques and Limitations

Recapitulation analogy risks over-statement; cross-cultural tests sparse in the 1939 base; relation to developmental psychology debated.
