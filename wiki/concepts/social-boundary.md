# Social Boundary

> Analytic construct for **us–them** divides that organize interaction. [[tilly-charles|Tilly]] (*[[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005|Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties]]* 2005, Ch. 9) synthesizes Abbott-style field interruptions with Lamont & Molnár’s symbolic component and a four-part operational complex.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Tilly 2005, drawing Abbott 1995.)* Social boundaries **interrupt, divide, circumscribe, or segregate** distributions of population or activity within social fields — spatial distributions, temporal sequences, and webs of interpersonal connections.

**Minimal definition:** any contiguous zone of contrasting density, rapid transition, or separation.

A **fully social** boundary (Tilly) typically includes:

1. Relations **within** each side  
2. Relations **across** the line  
3. Shared **representations** of the distinction (symbolic boundary component — Lamont & Molnár 2002 via Tilly)  
4. (Implied) reification by at least some actors on each side  

Boundary **change** = formation, transformation, activation, and suppression. Mechanisms that precipitate or constitute change: [[social-boundary-mechanism]].

## Semantic History

| Layer | Use |
|---|---|
| Ethnic/group boundaries (Barth et al.) | Interactional maintenance of ethnic difference — [[ethnic-boundary]] |
| Cultural sociology | [[boundary-work]] (Lamont) — evaluative standards drawing lines |
| Organizational / network analysis | Abbott 1995 “things of boundaries” |
| Tilly 2005 | General social-boundary change as causal problem; inventory of mechanisms; link to inequality and citizenship |

Do not equate “boundary” with “group essence”; Tilly treats boundaries as relational and variable in salience.

## Operationalizations

| Indicator | Example (Tilly) |
|---|---|
| Festive identity display | Bolivian Fiesta of Our Lady of Copacabana in Buenos Aires |
| Salience shift | Peaceable co-residence → killing across a line |
| Category swap | Yesterday’s enemies become allies; new enemies emerge |
| Site-specificity | Boundary organizes some social sites, not others |

Within-boundary transactions (mutual aid, remittances) and cross-boundary transactions (exploitation, denigration) are analytically distinct from the boundary itself.

## Applications

- Immigrant adaptation and niche formation ([[chain-migration]], [[opportunity-hoarding]])  
- Citizenship inclusion/exclusion ([[citizenship]])  
- Ethnic cleansing and nationalism (imposition → activation pathways)  
- Occupational sex-typing and organizational ins/outs ([[categorical-inequality]])  
- Political identities as boundary + relations + stories ([[political-identity]])

## Critiques and Limitations

- Inventory of mechanisms is **preliminary** (Tilly’s own caveat) — not exhaustively verified.  
- Risk of residualizing every distinction as “boundary.”  
- Production context: comparative historical sociology of the 1990s–2000s; illustrations span Latin America, Europe, soccer violence, genocide — uneven depth.
