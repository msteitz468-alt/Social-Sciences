# Discrepant Roles

## Definition as Coined

[[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (*[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]*, Ch. IV, 1956) argued that one overall objective of any **[[performance-team|team]]** is to sustain the definition of the situation its performance fosters — over-communicating some facts and under-communicating others. Facts that would discredit the fostered impression are **destructive information**; audiences must not acquire it (attributed).

Taking a performance as reference, three crucial roles by function are **performers**, **audience**, and **outsiders**, with expected correlations to information possessed and regions of access. Congruence is seldom complete. Additional vantage points that complicate the simple relation, when often taken and clearly significant, are **discrepant roles** (attributed).

### Secret types (function-relative)

| Type | Sense (Goffman, attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Dark secrets** | Facts the team knows and conceals, incompatible with its image (double secrets: the fact and that facts are hidden) |
| **Strategic secrets** | Intentions/capacities concealed so audience cannot adapt |
| **Inside secrets** | Possession marks membership; objectifies social distance |
| **Entrusted secrets** | Kept because of relation to the team (loyalty to role) |
| **Free secrets** | Someone else's secret one could disclose without discrediting one's own image |

### Principal discrepant roles (attributed)

| Role | Core idea |
|---|---|
| **Informer** | Pretends team membership, acquires destructive information, sells out the show (traitor if once sincere; spy if joined to inform) |
| **Shill** | Appears ordinary audience member but is in league with performers; models desired response |
| **Spotter** | Audience-side impostor checking standards in the public's interest |
| **Shopper** | Competitor's agent in the audience (e.g. rival store) |
| **Go-between / mediator** | Double-shill; learns both sides' secrets; arbitrator, agent, marriage-broker, foreman, chairman |
| **Non-person** | Present but not performer or audience (classic: servant; also very young/old/sick; technicians) |
| **Service specialist** | Constructs/repairs clients' shows; privileged information without shared risk of presenting the show; discretion ethics |
| **Training specialist** | Teaches impression-building while playing future audience |
| **Confidant** | Receives confession that the impression was merely an impression (friendship, not fee) |
| **Colleague** | Same routine to same *kind* of audience, not simultaneous teammates; community of face; renegade and backslider as disaffection modes |

## Semantic History

Typology rarely taught complete; "shill," "non-person," and "backstage" travel independently. Service-specialist discretion feeds professional-ethics literatures.

## Operationalizations

For a given show, map who has unexpected information or region access relative to feigned role.

## Applications

Espionage and industrial security; retail and carnival; domestic service; professions (lawyer, therapist, accountant); collegial groups. Links: [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Ideal-typical catalogue; roles combine in practice. Period examples (race, gender, colonial service) need date-stamping.
