# Social integration

## Definition as Coined

In *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*, [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] treats **integration** as the degree to which individuals are **bound into** social groups — the intensity of collective life circulating in an aggregate. “The state of integration of a social aggregate can only reflect the intensity of the collective life circulating in it.” One of two master axes of his suicide typology (with [[social-regulation]]).

Low integration → [[egoistic-suicide]]; high (excessive) integration → [[altruistic-suicide]].

## Semantic History

Rooted in *Division of Labor* themes of solidarity (mechanical/organic) — full cross-link on that ingest. Later sociology (social capital, network density, belonging scales) partly inherits the problem under other names; do not equate measures without argument.

**Giddens (1984) redefinition — different concept.** In *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, [[social-and-system-integration|social integration]] means reciprocity of practices under **co-presence** (paired with system integration across extended time-space), replacing micro/macro dualism within [[structuration-theory]]. That is **not** Durkheim’s intensity-of-collective-life axis. Use [[social-and-system-integration]] for Giddens; keep this page for the Durkheimian (and derivative survey) lineage.

## Operationalizations (*Suicide*)

| Domain | Indicator (as Durkheim uses it) |
|---|---|
| Religious society | Extent and firmness of common credo/practices; free inquiry concessions weaken cohesion |
| Domestic society | Family density (members per household); presence of children; marital status × age coefficients |
| Political society | Crises that concentrate activity and reduce self-regard (popular wars, revolutions, charged elections) |

Religion, family, and nation preserve against egoistic suicide **as integrated societies**, not by special dogmas: “The details of dogmas and rites are secondary. The essential thing is that they be capable of supporting a sufficiently intense collective life.”

## Applications

- [[egoistic-suicide]] · [[altruistic-suicide]]
- Book III: modern states too distant for continuous integration; family weakened; need [[occupational-group]] as nearer continuous milieu
- “Man cannot become attached to higher aims and submit to a rule if he sees nothing above him to which he belongs.”

## Critiques and Limitations

- Integration is inferred from proxies, not directly measured.
- Military “integration” as altruistic cause can reverse the valence of the same structural feature.
- Later network measures may not capture Durkheim’s moral-collective intensity.
