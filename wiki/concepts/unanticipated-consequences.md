# Unanticipated Consequences

## Definition as Coined

[[merton-robert-k|Merton]]'s early (1936) analysis of the **unanticipated consequences of purposive social action** supplied the intellectual root of later **latent function** analysis. In *[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]* Ch. III he clarified relations: not all unanticipated consequences are latent functions — only those that are functional for a designated system; unanticipated consequences may also be latent **dysfunctions** or irrelevant.

## Semantic History

From Merton's AJS article into general sociology and policy studies ("unintended consequences"). Broader than latent functions; often popularized without the functional-analysis apparatus.

## Operationalizations

Historical and case analysis of policy and institutional action; requires comparison of stated aims with observed outcomes and system referents.

## Applications

Social policy; organizational reform; scientific and technological change; self-fulfilling and suicidal prophecies as special cases of definition-driven dynamics.

## Critiques and Limitations

Hindsight bias in labeling consequences unanticipated; multiple actors with different intentions.
