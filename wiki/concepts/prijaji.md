# Prijaji (Priyayi)

## Definition as Coined

[[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] (*[[geertz-religion-of-java-1960|The Religion of Java]]*): **prijaji** “originally referred only to the hereditary aristocracy which the Dutch pried loose from the kings of the vanquished native states and turned into an appointive, salaried civil service.” By mid-century Modjokuto extension: white-collar elite conserving court etiquette, classical arts, and Hindu-Buddhist mysticism — “stressing the Hinduist aspects and related to the bureaucratic element.”

## Semantic History

Rank term → status–cultural **variant** in Geertz. Traditional marks (parasols, deep bow, court titles) nearly gone by fieldwork; respect for educated remains. Acculturation of this group fed the secular nationalist elite of the Republic, yet “prijaji style of life remains the model… for the entire society” (Geertz’s claim). Modern orthography often **priyayi**.

## Operationalizations

- **Worldview:** *alus*/*kasar*, *lair*/*batin*, *rasa*; etiquette (*andap-asor*, indirection, *étok-étok*); linguistic levels
- **Arts:** *wajang* (shadow play) and psychological interpretation; gamelan, tembang, djogèd, batik; contrast with *kasar* popular arts
- **Mysticism:** sects and disciplines (*ngèsti*, *semèdi*, *tapa*); guru–murid; this-worldly power of inner cultivation
- **Social:** bureaucracy, teachers, clerks; literati vs intelligentsia split; nationalist organizational milieux

## Applications

Javanese stratification and cultural hierarchy; *[[geertz-religion-of-java-study|Religion of Java]]*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Blurs rank, occupation, and religious style; Geertz documents **mixed** types (e.g. NU leader with prijaji cultural style). Later work contests treating prijaji as a religious “variant” parallel to santri.
