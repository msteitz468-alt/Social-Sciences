# Age Societies

## Definition as Coined

**Age societies** (age-grades, age-classes) are associational units organized by **age** (often male warrior grades), central to Heinrich Schurtz's *Altersklassen und Männerbünde* (1902) and to [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]]'s recovery of non-sib grouping in *[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]* (esp. Plains Indian age-societies; theory-of-associations chapter).

## Semantic History

Schurtz → Lowie Americanization of the program → later Africanist age-set literature (e.g. Masai) and separate "secret society" traditions. Distinguish age-set systems from one-off initiations and from [[sib]] membership.

## Operationalizations

Entry rules; purchase vs automatic advancement; corporate property and ritual; relation to military and police functions (Plains).

## Applications

Associations and Theory of Associations chapters of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*; Crow Fox/Lumpwood illustration in Conclusion.

## Critiques and Limitations

Schurtz's broader evolutionary scheme for associations is not endorsed wholesale by Lowie; treat Schurtz's typology and Lowie's cases separately.
