# Diacritical Features (Ethnic)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-1969|Ethnic Groups and Boundaries]]* (1969), [[barth-fredrik|Barth]] distinguishes the cultural contents of ethnic dichotomies into two analytic orders:

1. **Overt signals or signs — diacritical features** that people look for and exhibit to show identity — often dress, language, house-form, or general style of life.
2. **Basic value orientations** — standards of morality and excellence by which performance is judged.

Only features actors themselves regard as significant count. Ecologic variation may exaggerate differences; some features are used as emblems, others ignored; in some relationships radical differences are played down and denied. One cannot predict from first principles which features will be organizationally relevant.

## Semantic History

“Diacritical” evokes distinctive marks (as in linguistics). Barth applies it to ethnic signaling within an organizational theory of [[ethnic-boundary|boundaries]]. Later boundary sociology uses related language of cues and signals ([[symbolic-boundaries]]).

## Operationalizations

Identify which dress, language, ritual, virtue standards, or economic practices actors treat as membership signals:

| Case (1969 volume) | Illustrative diacritica / signals (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Pathan | Doing Pashto (virtue package); *izzat*; institutions of hospitality, *jirga*, purdah |
| Coast Sámi | Lappish language as secret/domestic code; stigma syndrome of signs (Eidheim via Goffman) |
| Highland Chiapas | Language, clothing; pueblo-specific dress/market products (Zinacantan hats, etc.) |
| Fur nomads | Few diacritica vs Baggara after pastoralization; language may still reveal Fur origin |
| Kohistani contact | Archaic dress features over-communicating independence |

## Applications

Central to distinguishing ethnic organization from mere cultural diversity (Blom’s negative case: stereotypes about mountain-peasant style do **not** become ethnic diacritica organizing Norwegian interaction).

## Critiques and Limitations

Which features count as diacritical can shift historically; risk of reducing ethnicity to superficial markers if the structure of [[ethnic-ascription]] and value standards is ignored.
