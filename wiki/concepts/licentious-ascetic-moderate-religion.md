# Licentious–ascetic–moderate religion

## Definition as Coined

In *[[martineau-how-to-observe-1838|How to Observe Morals and Manners]]* (1838), Part II Ch. I, [[martineau-harriet|Harriet Martineau]] argued that religion, in the wide sense the traveller must recognize, takes **three kinds** — **Licentious**, **Ascetic**, and **Moderate** — not divided by sect boundaries (pagan / Muslim / Christian) but by **spirit**:

| Kind | Martineau’s definition (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Licentious** | Worship of unspiritualized nature — material objects and primitive passions; always ritual; tends to leave the worshipper satisfied with a low moral state and selfish indulgence |
| **Ascetic** | Despises nature and worships artificial restraints; also ritual; spiritual vices (pride, vanity, hypocrisy) under asceticism are as fatal as sensual indulgence under licentiousness |
| **Moderate** | Worship of spiritualized nature — God in the material universe and the disciplined human mind; least ritual; drops rites as it advances; "Religion in its purity is not a pursuit, but a temper" |

She held that highest manifestations of the moderate order appear in Christian countries, while analogous aims also exist among some Muslims and pagans — a **period ranking**, attributed, not wiki voice.

## Semantic History

An 1838 traveler’s etic typology for comparative observation of religious *sentiment*, not a confessional taxonomy. It organizes her observational program (churches, clergy, superstitions, suicide) and reappears when she links religious type to class condition and polity. Later sociology of religion developed different typologies (church–sect, church–sect–cult, Weberian types); Martineau’s triad is historical scaffolding, not current standard classification.

## Operationalizations

- **Things before persons** for religion: places of worship, clergy condition, popular superstitions, holy days (*How to Observe*, Ch. I Religion).
- **Polity co-variation** (her claim): licentious tendency → poorer classes liable to slavery; ascetic → hardship and want; moderate → freedom and comfort; absolutism common under licentious or ascetic forms; democratic self-government possible only under moderate religion of the majority — attributed theoretical scheme, not established finding.
- **Suicide** character and number as indicators of religious sentiment (see [[suicide]]).

## Applications

Organizes Part II Ch. I of *How to Observe* and feeds her Domestic State discussion of asceticism and licentiousness as coexisting extremes (she argued they rise and fall together).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Etic spirit-types collapse vast confessional diversity.
- Christian-centered ranking of "highest" moderate religion is a period evaluative frame.
- Causal links from religious type to slavery, despotism, and democracy are speculative comparative illustrations, not controlled comparisons ([[unknown]] Galton controls).
- Superstition and suicide indicators require careful interpretation and later statistical methods ([[moral-statistics]]) that Martineau does not supply.
