# Concrete Totality

## Definition as Coined

In “What is Orthodox Marxism?” and throughout *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]*, [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]] elevates **concrete totality** (*konkrete Totalität*) as the governing category of Marxist method. Against empiricist and revisionist idolatry of isolated “facts,” he holds that facts become knowledge only within a system and a historical process; dialectics “insists on the concrete unity of the whole.”

Load-bearing formulations (attributed):

- “It is not the primacy of economic motives in historical explanation that constitutes the decisive difference between Marxism and bourgeois science, but the **point of view of totality**.”
- “The primacy of the category of totality is the **bearer of the revolutionary principle in science**.”

“Concrete” here is **not** the empirical individual (person, people, or psychological consciousness). Bourgeois historiography, Lukács argues, mistakes the abstract empirical for the concrete; the concrete is the **relation to society as a whole** — production relations, class structure, historical process (Marx: relations of worker/capitalist, tenant/landlord, etc.).

## Semantic History

| Layer | Sense |
|---|---|
| Hegel | Concrete universal; truth as the whole — philosophical precursor Lukács openly renovates |
| Marx | Method of rising from abstract to concrete (*Grundrisse*); totality of production relations |
| **Lukács 1923** | Totality as revolutionary-methodological principle; weapon against Bernstein’s “facts” and natural-science model of social knowledge |
| Lukács 1967 | Affirms restoration of totality against SD “scientism”; **retracts** elevating totality *over* economic priority as Hegelian distortion |
| Frankfurt / Critical Theory | Totality as critical category (often with reification); later Habermasian and systems-theoretic displacements |
| Structural Marxism | Different totality (Althusserian structure in dominance) — do not equate without argument |

## Operationalizations

Methodological, not a variable. Procedures Lukács opposes: treating statistics and “pure” economic data as self-interpreting; specialist isolation of law, economics, etc. as closed systems; natural-scientific experiment as model for social knowledge under capitalism (which itself produces reified, quantifiable “facts”).

Positive orientation: relate partial phenomena to the historical process and to the whole of production; see “facts” as products of a definite epoch (capitalism) whose immediate form already encodes capitalist structure.

## Applications

- **Orthodox Marxism essay:** method vs results; critique of revisionism and opportunism.
- **[[reification]] essay:** specialised sciences and formal systems lose the whole; crisis as limit of partial rational systems.
- **[[class-consciousness]]:** false consciousness diagnosed by failure to strike the heart of the totality even when class-typical thoughts are thought through.
- **Luxemburg reception:** *Accumulation of Capital* praised for restoring total process (imperialism, world market) against incomplete *Capital* and Centre Marxism.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **1967 self-criticism:** totality without labour/metabolism and without economic priority becomes idealist; can underwrite subjectivist revolutionism.
- **Empirical social science:** “totality” resists operationalisation and can license unfalsifiable holism — a standing charge from analytic and positivist critics.
- **Political:** claims to speak for the whole can underwrite party monopoly of “total” knowledge (organisation essay tensions).
