# Object Shift

*(Attributed — McAdam, Tarrow & Tilly 2001.)* Mechanism: **change in the targets** (objects) of collective claims — and thus in relations between claimants and those objects — during an episode.

## Definition as Coined

> "Object shift means alteration in relations between claimants and objects of claims, as when shifting parties and their brokers in Panipur moved up the Pakistani administrative hierarchy looking for allies and thus promoted militarization of their local conflict."

Short-run effects appear inside strategic interaction (e.g. rival gangs uniting against police). Longer-run shifts also occur as institutional environments change (e.g. parliamentarization redirecting claims toward elections, demonstrations, lobbying). Differs from [[brokerage]] by centering on claim-making and always altering answers to “who’s who” questions about targets.

## Semantic History

DOC Ch. 5 mechanism inventory (with brokerage, category formation, certification). Related to but not identical with [[scale-shift]] (level of coordination) and [[identity-shift]] (who we/they are).

## Operationalizations

Documented retargeting of claims across parties, agencies, or institutions; repertoire change correlated with new objects.

## Applications

*(DOC attributed illustrations:)* South Asian local conflicts escalating up administrative hierarchies; South African multi-target transitions; Europeanization appeals by Spanish dissidents.

## Critiques and Limitations

Co-occurs with scale and identity shift; isolation is analytic. Sample of four Ch. 5 mechanisms is not a closed comprehensive model (authors).
