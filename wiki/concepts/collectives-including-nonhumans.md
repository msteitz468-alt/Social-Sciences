# Collectives (Including Nonhumans)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* (Part IV), [[descola-philippe|Descola]] argues that "society" as an exclusive human aggregate facing an asocial nature is a **naturalist export**. He proposes **collectives** — practical forms of aggregation that may include animals, plants, spirits, places, and artifacts according to the governing [[modes-of-identification|ontology]].

## Semantic History

Adjacent to [[latour-bruno|Latour]]'s collectives and actor-networks but generated from the four-ontology matrix rather than from STS. Reacts against Durkheimian sociocentrism (ontology subordinated to social morphology) and against Lévi-Straussian classification that keeps nonhumans as signs for human groups.

## Operationalizations (four morphologies)

| Ontology | Collective form (Descola) |
|---|---|
| [[animism]] | **Monospecific tribes** — each species a people with its culture; humans one collective among others; anthropogenic ethos |
| [[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]] | **Exclusive human societies** + **asocial nature**; anthropocentric |
| [[totemism]] | **Hybrid complementary segments** — socio-natural classes (cosmogenic Dreaming actualization) |
| [[analogism]] | **One mixed hierarchical cosmos-society** — not hybrid segments but encompassment of differences |

A collective is also a **field of [[modes-of-relation|relational schemas]]** (gift, predation, protection…) with a dominant ethos and subordinate niches (Ch.14–15).

## Applications

Amazonian multi-species sociality; Australian totemic segments; Andean/Chipaya hierarchical cosmos; critique of applying "society vs nature" to non-naturalist regimes.

## Critiques and Limitations

Morphologies are schematic; real systems hybridize. Risk of reading Descola's types as census categories rather than ideal-typical combinatorial extremes.
