# Performative and Prescriptive Structures

## Definition as Coined

[[sahlins-marshall|Marshall Sahlins]] (attributed, *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*, 1985, ch. 1, section "Performative Structures") draws an ideal-typical distinction — paralleling [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]]'s mechanical/statistical models — between two ways structure and action relate:

- **Prescriptive structures**, in which institutional forms determine the appropriate acts: circumstance is assimilated *to* the categories. The type case is Radcliffe-Brownian corporate descent-group systems (e.g. Australian Aboriginal society), where the order specifies in advance who may do what.
- **Performative structures**, in which customary acts precipitate the social forms: the categories are generated *in* action. "If friends make gifts, gifts make friends"; Hawaii, the Eskimo, and "our own" society are Sahlins's examples.

## Semantic History

The distinction refines Sahlins's reconciliation of structure and history. Performative and prescriptive orders have different **historicities**: performative orders (Hawaii) "assimilate themselves to contingent circumstance" and value departures from precedent, making them historically "hot" (in Lévi-Strauss's sense); prescriptive orders "assimilate circumstance to themselves," denying contingency, and are historically "cold." Crucially, both types can coexist within a single society, in different domains — "evenementially hot areas" alongside closed ones. The concept connects to [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]] (the third term where categories meet events) and to the [[duality-of-structure]] and [[habitus]] on which Sahlins draws.

## Operationalizations / Applications

Sahlins's showcase is **Hawaii** (see [[hawaiians]]): a "performative structure" lacking determinate prescribed kinship groups, "constituted by the acts that signified it." His striking gloss — "Love is the infrastructure (as [Godelier] might say)" — reads Hawaiian political order as a "political economy of love," in which sexual/marital acts, freely pursued, nonetheless reproduce a non-arbitrary structure of customary distinctions (men/women, chiefs/people, gods/mortals): "Social system is thus constructed out of passion, structure out of sentiment." He credits Bourdieu's [[habitus]] ("structuring structures") and Durkheim's "everything contractual is not in the contract" as scaffolding, and generalizes the point to kinship's varied modes of relating to time (Crow-Omaha "freezing," elementary reproduction of alliance, complex systems' generational discontinuity, the Ilongot's lived ideology of self-invention over an unrecognized structural constraint).

## Critiques and Limitations

The pairing is explicitly ideal-typical: real societies mix performative and prescriptive domains, and the "hot/cold" mapping is a heuristic, not a taxonomy of whole societies (Sahlins elsewhere flags the hot/cold contrast as "crude"). The risk is reifying a spectrum into a binary; Sahlins mitigates it by locating both poles within single societies. The Hawaiian material is his attributed ethnohistorical reconstruction (see [[sahlins-obeyesekere-cook-debate]]).
