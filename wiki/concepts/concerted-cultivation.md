# Concerted Cultivation

Lareau’s term for the dominant **middle-class** logic of childrearing in *[[lareau-unequal-childhoods-2011|Unequal Childhoods]]*.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Lareau 2003/2011.)* Parents actively **foster and assess** children’s talents, opinions, and skills through organized leisure, continuous reasoning-heavy talk, and interventions with schools and other institutions. In her sample, middle-class children learned to perform for adults, negotiate, and feel **entitled** to customize institutional interactions (e.g. doctor visits, school placements). Exemplars include Garrett Tallinger’s multi-sport schedule and Alexander Williams’s language training.

Contrasts with [[accomplishment-of-natural-growth]].

## Semantic History

Charter coinage 2003; widely used in education and family sociology; often paired with natural growth as a binary (Lareau presents them as ideal types with variation).

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic observation of daily schedules, language, and institutional encounters; not a survey scale in the original study.

## Applications

Explains middle-class children’s institutional ease; mechanism of class inequality beyond money alone.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of middle-class normativity; resource-intensity of concerted cultivation; race and gender cross-cuts.
