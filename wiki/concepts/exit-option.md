# Exit (option)

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[hirschman-albert|Hirschman]] 1970.)* In *[[hirschman-exit-voice-loyalty-1970|Exit, Voice, and Loyalty]]*, the **exit option** is: "Some customers stop buying the firm's products or some members leave the organization… As a result, revenues drop, membership declines, and management is impelled to search for ways and means to correct whatever faults have led to exit." Exit is the characteristic **economic** recuperation mechanism — neat (binary), impersonal, and indirect (recovery as unintended by-product of market signals). Paired with [[voice-option|voice]]; modulated by [[loyalty]]. Parent framework: [[exit-voice-loyalty]].

## Semantic History

Pre-Hirschman economics treated customer switching as ordinary demand response, without theorizing it as one of two alternative **recuperation channels for the declining organization itself**. Hirschman elevated "exit" as repertoire item, analyzed its failure modes (too little signal; too much too soon; industry-wide diversion; cream-skimming of voice agents), and showed that political culture often brands exit as desertion or treason rather than feedback.

## Operationalizations

- **Quality-elasticity of demand** with price and costs held constant (random efficiency lapse, not calculated skimping).
- Management reaction function: no reaction for small revenue drops; recovery for intermediate drops; bankruptcy if losses too large.
- Alert vs inert customer mix: alert customers start feedback; inert customers provide cushion.
- Loyalist path points: XAL (exit absent loyalty), TX (threat of exit), XWL (exit with loyalty).
- Hybrids: **boycott** (exit consummated to force policy change; promise of re-entry); clamorous resignation under protest (public-goods exit as voice-from-without).

## Applications

*(All attributed illustrations from EVL.)* Competitive firm–customer markets; Nigerian railways vs trucks; public/private schools; Wall Street rule / stockholder exit; party switching and third parties; AFL-CIO no-raiding; Latin American exile as "conspiracy in restraint of voice"; American frontier, success-as-exit-mobility, and Black Power's rejection of individual exit; non-resignation of U.S. officials over Vietnam policy.

## Critiques and Limitations

Exit can recuperate *or* comfort monopoly; quasi-perfect competition with quality latitude can wipe firms out before recovery (second-best logic). From [[public-good|public goods]], full exit is often impossible — private exit leaves residual consumption of the shared outcome. Preferred use of exit atrophies the art of [[voice-option|voice]] — "a central point of this book" (Hirschman).
