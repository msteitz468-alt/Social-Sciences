# Modes of Religiosity

## Definition as Coined

[[whitehouse-harvey|Whitehouse]]'s theory (White 2021 summary): religious transmission clusters into two **modes** — **imagistic** (low-frequency, high-arousal rituals; episodic memory; intense small-group cohesion) and **doctrinal** (high-frequency, low-arousal rituals; semantic memory; orthodoxy and large-scale anonymous communities) — linking ritual dynamics to socio-political scale.

## Semantic History

From PNG cult ethnography to general cognitive theory of religious transmission; refined with experimentalists and historians (White).

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic coding; historical/archaeological quantitative samples; experiments on identity fusion and shared dysphoria (White ch. 4, 9–10). White reports imagistic practices associated with small-scale bonding; doctrinal with agricultural intensity and larger formations.

## Applications

Explains variation in ritual systems without single "function of ritual"; pairs with ritual form theory ([[lawson-e-thomas]], [[mccauley-robert-n]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Dichotomy may oversimplify mixed systems; historical tests forced model revision (White).
