# Interiority and Physicality

## Definition as Coined

The dual analytical axes of [[descola-philippe|Descola]]'s [[modes-of-identification|modes of identification]] (*[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]*, Ch.5):

- **Interiority** — "a range of properties recognized by all human beings and partially covers what we generally call the mind, the soul, or consciousness: intentionality, subjectivity, reflexivity, feelings, and the ability to express oneself and to dream," plus breath, vital energy, shared essence or origin.
- **Physicality** — "external form, substance, the physiological, perceptive and sensorimotor processes," constitution and way of acting insofar as these reflect morphological and physiological characteristics — not bare matter alone but the visible/tangible expression of dispositions (including ethograms).

Descola insists the distinction is **not** a simple ethnocentric projection of Western mind/body dualism: "it is a distinction that all the civilizations about which we have learned something from ethnography and history have, in their own fashions, objectivized." Continuity *or* discontinuity of each axis between self and nonhumans yields the four ontologies.

## Semantic History

Builds on Mauss's separation of a universal sense of self from culturally variable theories of the person; engages Strathern/Leenhardt/LiPuma on dividual and composite persons without abandoning reflective individuation as a necessary (not sufficient) condition. Developmental psychology and dualist intuitions in dreams, dissociation, and ritual are cited as ordinary experiential supports for a felt split — without Tylor's causal dream→soul story.

## Operationalizations

| Ontology | Interiorities | Physicalities |
|---|---|---|
| [[animism]] | continuous (shared souls/culture) | discontinuous (form/ethogram; substance often continuous) |
| [[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]] | discontinuous (human mind/culture unique) | continuous (one material nature) |
| [[totemism]] | continuous within totemic class | continuous within class (hybrid socio-natural image) |
| [[analogism]] | discontinuous (singularities) | discontinuous (singularities linked by analogy) |

## Applications

Architecture of [[four-ontologies]]; Descola–Viveiros debate on body as differentiator ([[perspectivism]]); re-reading of [[animism]] and [[totemism]]; naturalist animal-minds and animal-rights tensions as internal pressures on discontinuity of interiority.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether "all civilizations" objectivize the same dual plane is itself contested; axes may smuggle dualism back under anti-dualist banners. Descola's claim is comparative-instrumental, not a metaphysical dualism of substances.
