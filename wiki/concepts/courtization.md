# Courtization

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Elias.)* **Courtization** (courtisation of warriors) is the long process by which members of the warrior upper class are drawn into dependence on princely/royal courts, transforming their conduct, affect controls, and status competition. Private warrior violence gives way to restrained, observation-heavy court life; etiquette and favour become weapons. Central to the empirical bridge between feudal warrior society and the manners standards tracked in Part Two of *[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]*.

## Semantic History

Pairs with "bourgeoisification of courtly people" and "courtization of bourgeois elements" as double movement in early modern Europe (Elias). Related to but not identical with Norbert Elias's later *The Court Society* monograph (expand when ingested).

## Operationalizations

Residence at court; dependence on royal monopoly of force/tax; elaboration of civilité/courtoisie; decline of free feud rights.

## Applications

Explains why upper-class manners codes tighten as courts monopolize opportunities; links [[court-society]] to [[self-constraint]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Gendered and class-limited documentary base; variation across European courts; colonial court analogues not in the 1939 core.
