# Political Participation

## Definition as Coined

Engagement in electoral and governmental politics — voting, campaign and party work, contacting, contributing, attending meetings, protest. In *[[putnam-bowling-alone-2000|Bowling Alone]]* ch. 2, one domain of [[civic-engagement]].

## Semantic History

Verba, Nie, and related political-science programs formalized modes of participation mid-century; Lipset *Political Man* treated class bases of electoral politics ([[class-voting]]). Putnam's contribution is multi-decade **trend** synthesis and linkage to social-capital decline, not a new typology of modes.

## Operationalizations

Official turnout; Roper Social and Political Trends items (party work, officer-holding, petition, speeches, meetings); campaign finance vs time; interest and knowledge items. Putnam stresses time-based vs checkbook participation.

## Applications

- *Bowling Alone* ch. 2 trend claims (turnout 1960→1996; Roper 1973–94 suite).
- Democracy outcomes chapter (internal/external civic effects).
- Comparison with Lipset on class and regime without equating designs ([[lipset-political-man-study]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Turnout series are period-sensitive (later 21st-c. rebounds); protest and online politics may be undercounted as "participation"; professionalization confounds membership counts. Decline interpretation contested ([[bowling-alone-debate]]).
