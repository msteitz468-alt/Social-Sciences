# Symbolic Universe

## Definition as Coined

[[berger-peter-l|Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]] (1966): **symbolic universes** are the highest level of [[legitimation]] — bodies of theoretical tradition that integrate different provinces of meaning and encompass the institutional order in a symbolic totality. They provide the matrix within which all human experience can be consistently conceived; the institutional order is legitimated *sub specie universi*.

Functions they attribute:

- **Nomic** (ordering) protection against chaos, the “night side” of reality, and terror
- Integration of biography and identity (including death and marginal situations) into a meaningful whole
- Historical canopy over the institutional order
- Frame for therapy (keeping deviants in) and nihilation (liquidating outsider realities)

## Semantic History

Related to earlier notions of worldview, cosmos, and ideology, but technical in B&L as the top tier of a four-level legitimation scheme. Maintenance machineries: mythology, theology, philosophy, science. Social organization: experts, monopolies (e.g. medieval Christendom as ideal type of monopolistic universe), pluralism and competition, ideology as power-linked legitimation.

## Operationalizations

Analytic totality, not a survey item. Later sociology of religion (including Berger’s own later work, not yet ingested) operationalizes related ideas via secularization and pluralization debates.

## Applications

Religious cosmologies; totalizing political ideologies; identity theories (“psychologies”) as part of universe-maintenance; conversion as migration between universes (via [[plausibility-structure]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Can be read as over-systematizing culture into a single “universe”; B&L themselves analyze pluralism, sub-universes, and conflict between experts. Empirical specification of “a” society’s symbolic universe is always interpretive.
