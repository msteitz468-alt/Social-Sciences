# Archaeological Horizon

Integrative unit formalized for American archaeology by [[willey-gordon-r|Willey]] and [[phillips-philip|Phillips]] (1958): **wide space, short time** — the spatial counterpoise to [[archaeological-tradition|tradition]].

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Willey & Phillips 1958.)*

> The horizon may be defined as a primarily spatial continuity represented by cultural traits and assemblages whose nature and mode of occurrence permit the assumption of a broad and rapid spread.

Units linked by a horizon are assumed **approximately contemporaneous** — with the caveat that cultural horizons unsupported by independent dating may have depth and “slope” (time required for markers to spread).

## Semantic History

- **Horizon style** (Uhle; formalized by Kroeber for Andean archaeology): wide distribution of a recognizable art style over little time — “horizontal stringers” tying regional columns on the time chart.
- W&P **generalize** beyond art style: specialized artifact types, widely traded objects, new technologies, unusual burials, ritual assemblages — any markers of rapid spread of ideas.
- Rouse urged stricter, extracultural (distributional) use of horizon for pure temporal correlation; W&P keep a partly “genetic” (culture-linked) horizon while agreeing pure area chronologies should rest on independent dating when possible.
- Still standard Americanist vocabulary (e.g. “Hopewellian horizon,” “Tiahuanaco horizon,” “Southern Cult horizon”).

## Operationalizations

| Use | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Cross-dating phases | Shared horizon markers ≈ rough synchrony |
| Chart construction | Horizontal ties across regional sequences |
| Limits | Horizon style needs aesthetic complexity many cultures lack; cultural markers may slope in time |

## Applications

- Andean ceramic and art-style horizons (White-on-red, Tiahuanaco, etc.).
- Eastern North America: Hopewellian interaction / Southern Cult as horizon phenomena in W&P’s Formative–threshold discussions.
- Mesoamerican Formative–Classic style spreads as chronological stringers.

## Critiques and Limitations

W&P themselves: horizons based only on cultural criteria are weak pure-time tools; correlations often rest on assumed contemporaneity more than independent dates. Processual critique later attacked horizon-as-migration default. The concept survives as a **descriptive** integrative tool, not an explanation of why traits spread.
