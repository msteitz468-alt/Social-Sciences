# Social Capital (civic / Putnam–Coleman tradition)

Community- and network-level **social capital** as popularized in U.S. political sociology: social networks plus norms of reciprocity and trustworthiness that enable coordination. Primary ingested source: [[putnam-bowling-alone-2000|Putnam, *Bowling Alone* (2000)]].

> **Not** Bourdieu's convertible network resources. The Bourdieusian lineage lives on [[social-capital]]. Do not silently equate the two.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Putnam 2000, synthesizing prior uses.)* Social capital refers to connections among individuals — social networks and the norms of reciprocity and trustworthiness that arise from them. Civic virtue is most powerful when embedded in dense reciprocal networks; isolated virtue is not social capital. Putnam notes independent coinages (Hanifan 1916; Jacobs; Loury; Coleman; others) each stressing productivity of social ties.

## Semantic History

- **Hanifan (1916):** goodwill, fellowship, sympathy, social intercourse in a social unit — Progressive rural-school reformer.
- **Coleman:** structural resources for action (obligations, trustworthiness, information channels, norms) — rational-choice-compatible.
- **Putnam (*Making Democracy Work*; *Bowling Alone*):** civic-community stock with public-goods effects; decline narrative for the United States; [[bonding-social-capital]] vs [[bridging-social-capital]].
- **Drift:** policy "social capital" often flattens power and exclusion — see ch. 22 dark side and [[bowling-alone-debate]].

## Operationalizations

Membership density, meeting attendance, voting and campaign work, church attendance and parish groups, workplace socializing, informal visiting/card-playing, volunteering and philanthropy, social trust items, honesty/reciprocity indicators. Putnam triangulates rather than relying on one index (Appendix I).

## Applications

- Italian regional government performance (*Making Democracy Work* — not fully ingested).
- U.S. civic decline and revival agenda (*Bowling Alone*).
- Education, neighborhoods, prosperity, health, democracy outcome chapters (Section IV).

## Critiques and Limitations

Measurement validity of "joining"; formal vs informal; online association; baseline nostalgia; inequality and who was excluded from mid-century civic life — [[bowling-alone-debate]]. Distinction from [[social-capital|Bourdieu]] is mandatory on every use.
