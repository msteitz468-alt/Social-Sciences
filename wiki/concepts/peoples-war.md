# People's War

## Definition as Coined

In materials anthologized under *[[mao-quotations-from-chairman-mao-1966|Quotations]]* chapters 5–9, [[mao-zedong|Mao]] defines revolutionary war as **a war of the masses**:

> The revolutionary war is a war of the masses; it can be waged only by mobilizing the masses and relying on them. ("Be Concerned with the Well-Being of the Masses…," 1934)

The "true bastion of iron" is not fortification but "the millions upon millions of people who genuinely and sincerely support the revolution." Guerrilla forces and main Red Army forces "complement each other like a man's right arm and left arm"; without popular guerrillas the main force is "a warrior with only one arm."

Migrated term: from CCP military doctrine into comparative revolutionary studies, COIN literature, and left political movements.

## Operationalizations

From the anthology (doctrinal, not measured):

- Mobilize masses; attend to well-being as condition of political support.
- Combine main forces with armed people in base areas.
- Party leads the gun; political work; army–people and officer–men relations (chs. 12–14).
- Strategic vs tactical time-horizons (paper tiger speech, Wuchang 1958 explanatory note).

## Applications

- Chinese revolutionary civil war and anti-Japanese war (as claimed victory theory).
- Later reference point for Vietnam and other rural revolutions (external comparative literature).
- Links [[mass-line]] (political mobilization) and [[lenin-theory-of-imperialism]] (enemy characterized as imperialist/reactionary system).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Asymmetric costs:** Doctrine understates civilian suffering and coercion inside base areas and campaigns; maxims are not body counts.
- **Context dependence:** Built for semi-colonial agrarian China; export is analogy unless re-specified.
- **Paper-tiger rhetoric:** Motivational and strategic framing; not a prediction model with error bands.
- **Single anthology source:** Full military writings and critical military history not ingested.
