# Ex-human

## Definition as Coined

In *[[biehl-vita-2005|Vita]]* (2005), [[biehl-joao|João Biehl]] uses **ex-human** (attributed) for people left in places like [[vita-porto-alegre|Vita]] who are still living but no longer counted as full persons — cut from family, medicine, and productive life while remaining entangled with all three. He writes that Vita is "a place in the world for ex-humans," and that he adopted the term reluctantly after Catarina said "I am an ex" and referred to herself as an "ex-wife" and her kin as "my ex-family" (attributed, Biehl 2005).

The construct is **migrated**: it begins in Catarina's emic self-description ("ex-") and is elevated by Biehl into an analytic for the social form of life that precedes biological death.

## Semantic History

- **2005 (*Vita*).** Biehl distinguishes *ex-human* from Agambenian "bare life": language and desire continue; the point is not mute biological residual but a form of life still speaking from inside institutions that were supposed to nurture humanness.
- He also flags the risk that a "philosophical-sounding" label can generate distance and participate in the discursive regimes that let the Other die — yet keeps the term because it relative-izes generic humanness and illuminates contingency (attributed).
- Related to **[[social-death]]** (before biological death comes social death) and **[[zone-of-social-abandonment]]** (the places that produce and house the ex-human).

## Operationalizations

Ethnographic, not a survey index: self-description as "ex-"; institutional placement outside family/clinic; absence of continuous care; continued speech, writing, and desire (Catarina's dictionary).

## Applications

- *[[biehl-vita-study|Vita]]* — type case Catarina and the Vita infirmary population (documentation 1995–2003).
- Comparative potential: other post-asylum dump-sites and informal long-term care under democratization and pharmaceutical citizenship (not yet independently ingested).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of reifying a philosophical category from one life history.
- Contested relation to bare-life and social-death literatures — Biehl's own hesitation is part of the concept's record.
- Single-source base in this vault; keep claims attributed until independent cases converge.
