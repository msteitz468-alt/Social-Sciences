# Habitus

Central concept of [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s [[practice-theory]]. Its **first systematic statement is *[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline of a Theory of Practice]]* (1972)**, ch. 2 (see Original formulation under Semantic History); the fullest statement in the wiki's sources is Book I, ch. 3 of *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (1980/1990), which rewrites *Outline*'s apparatus, with ethnographic grounding in [[kabyle]] ritual, honour, and matrimonial strategy (Book II) and comparative Béarn succession material.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, Logic of Practice, ch. 3.)* Habitus designates systems of durable, transposable dispositions — structured structures predisposed to function as structuring structures — that generate and organize practices and representations objectively adapted to their outcomes without conscious aiming at ends or express mastery of the operations required to attain them. Practices so generated are, in Bourdieu's formulation, objectively "regulated" and "regular" without being products of obedience to rules, and can be collectively orchestrated without a conductor.

> "the conditionings associated with a particular class of conditions of existence produce habitus, systems of durable, transposable dispositions, structured structures predisposed to function as structuring structures, that is, as principles which generate and organize practices and representations that can be objectively adapted to their outcomes without presupposing a conscious aiming at ends or an express mastery of the operations necessary in order to attain them."

As an acquired system of generative schemes, Bourdieu argued, habitus makes possible all thoughts, perceptions, and actions inherent in the particular conditions of its production — and only those. Its "infinite yet strictly limited generative capacity" was meant to transcend the antinomies of determinism/freedom, conditioning/creativity, consciousness/unconscious, and individual/society. The most improbable practices are excluded as unthinkable by immediate submission to order that inclines agents to "make a virtue of necessity."

## Semantic History

**Elias (prior usage in this wiki's primary sources).** In *[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]* (1939; English rev.), [[elias-norbert|Norbert Elias]] uses **habitus** (restored in the revised English edition's editorial practice) for the social moulding of drives and the apparatus of self-restraint that becomes “second nature” under monopolies of force and lengthening chains of interdependence. Elias links habitus change to [[sociogenesis-psychogenesis|socio- and psychogenesis]] and to [[figuration|figurations]]—not to Bourdieu's later field/capital architecture. **Do not equate Elias's habitus with Bourdieu's;** treat as related problem-space (durable social moulding of conduct) with distinct theories.

**Bourdieu (consolidating usage).**

- **Scholastic / Aristotelian *habitus*.** In the Preface of *Logic*, Bourdieu retrieves the body schema as *principium importans ordinem ad actum* — an ordering principle capable of orienting practices at once unconsciously and systematically — against intentionalist or structuralist accounts of [[kabyle]] ritual coherence.
- **Against dualisms.** Against Sartre, Bourdieu charged that refusing durable dispositions leaves each action as an antecedent-less confrontation of subject and world; the social world is instead "meaning-made-thing" and "meaning-made-body." Against Lévi-Strauss's eternal logical categories of the unconscious mind, he argued schemes of thought form through dialectic of social structures and structured, structuring dispositions. Ignoring that dialectic, he claimed, yields fetishism of social laws and agents as mere *Träger* of structure.
- **Original formulation (*[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline]]*, 1972).** The canonical definition first appears in *Outline* ch. 2 ("Structures and the habitus"), in wording nearly identical to the later *Logic* version but keyed to *environment* rather than *conditions of existence*:
  > "The structures constitutive of a particular type of environment … produce habitus, systems of durable, transposable dispositions, structured structures predisposed to function as structuring structures, that is, as principles of the generation and structuring of practices and representations which can be objectively 'regulated' and 'regular' without in any way being the product of obedience to rules, objectively adapted to their goals without presupposing a conscious aiming at ends … and, being all this, collectively orchestrated without being the product of the orchestrating action of a conductor."

  Several load-bearing formulations are *Outline*'s coinages: the "**conductorless orchestration**" of a group's or class's practices; the "durably installed generative principle of **regulated improvisations**"; habitus as "**history turned into nature**, i.e. denied as such," with the corollary that "the 'unconscious' is never anything other than the forgetting of history which history itself produces" (**genesis amnesia**); habitus as an "immanent law, *lex insita*, laid down in each agent by his earliest upbringing"; and "**bodily hexis** is political mythology realized, em-bodied." *Outline* also states the conditioned-freedom answer to determinism directly ("the conditioned and conditional freedom it secures is as remote from a creation of unpredictable novelty as it is from a simple mechanical reproduction of the initial conditionings"), against Chomsky's determinism/creativity dilemma.
- **Consolidation (Outline → Logic).** *Le sens pratique* / *Logic of Practice* (1980/1990) gives the systematic ch. 3 definition (quoted above under Definition as Coined) and couples habitus to [[practical-sense]], [[hexis]], [[doxa]], [[illusio]], and capital forms.
- **Primary vs. secondary habitus (*[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]*).** The education work develops the concept's *genetic* dimension: [[pedagogic-work]] produces a **primary habitus** ("characteristic of a group or class, which is the basis for the subsequent formation of any other habitus") in the family; the school's **secondary** pedagogic work has a productivity that is "a function of the distance" between the habitus it targets and this primary habitus — so "the success of all school education... depends fundamentally on the education previously accomplished in the earliest years of life." Habitus formation is *irreversible* (Husserl on the non-fungibility of a formation), which is why class-differentiated primary habitus translates into unequal school outcomes.
- **Post-Bourdieusian diffusion.** Widespread adoption in cultural sociology, education, and anthropology; measurement and "determinism" debates largely await *Distinction* and secondary literature not yet ingested.
- **Craft specification — [[pugilistic-habitus]] (*[[wacquant-body-and-soul-2004|Body & Soul]]*, Wacquant 2004).** [[wacquant-loic|Wacquant]] applies habitus to Chicago boxing: durable bodily/mental schemata of the competent fighter, fabricated through implicit collective pedagogy and sparring; limiting case of practice whose logic unfolds “directly in bodily gymnastics.”

## Operationalizations

In *Logic*, habitus is **reconstructed from ethnographic and strategic regularities**, not attitude scales:

- **Generative reconstruction.** Preferential marriage is treated not as obedience to a norm or unconscious model but as a reproduction strategy in a system of strategies generated by habitus (Preface). Ritual and matrimonial variants are read as improvisation on schemes, not execution of an explicit model (Book II).
- **Codification gradient.** Bourdieu argued schemes of the habitus are objectified in codified knowledge (sayings, prohibitions, proverbs, regulated rites) to very different degrees by domain — densest near agricultural activity, weaving, pottery, cuisine; thinner for divisions of day or life-course.
- **Class and homology.** A social class (in-itself) is simultaneously a class of similar conditions of existence and a class of individuals with the same habitus; singular habitus within a class stand in homology — diversity within homogeneity. Homogamy is the paradigm of choices through which habitus reinforces itself.
- **Hysteresis.** Misfit of dispositions to new objective chances (Marx's Don Quixote example) is the operational signature of habitus as past conditions of production of practice, not pure present adaptation.
- **Later quantitative sociology** (credentials, tastes, correspondence analysis) operationalizes class habitus mainly via *[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]* — **now ingested**.

## In *Distinction* (1979/1984)

*(Attributed — Bourdieu.)* Class conditionings produce class [[habitus]] that generate systematic lifestyle choices across domains (art, food, sport, décor, body, politics). Figure scheme: conditions of existence → habitus → lifestyle. Practice formula: *[(habitus)(capital)] + field = practice*. Three pole-types organize the book’s taste analysis: dominant [[aesthetic-disposition]] (distance from necessity), petit-bourgeois [[cultural-goodwill]], working-class [[taste-for-necessity]]. Habitus also generates schemes of classification of the social world (Conclusion: knowledge without concepts; classification struggle). Hysteresis / “Don Quixote effect” appears when dispositions lag structural change ([[reconversion-strategies]]). See [[hubs/studies/bourdieu-distinction-study]].

## Applications

- **[[kabyle]] honour, gift, assembly, and ritual.** Sense of honour as disposition inscribed in postures and language; assembly arbitration presupposing orchestration of habitus rather than a code applied by monopoly of force; gift and challenge sequences as regulated improvisation under urgency; parallel-cousin and distant marriage as strategies of material and [[symbolic-capital]]; ploughing/harvest rites as application of generative schemes of reunion and separation.
- **Béarn succession and marriage.** Dispositions and "tastes" for partners that rule out misalliance without direct calculation; "socially approved love" as love of one's social destiny along paths of free choice; strategies of reproduction as "socially constituted instinct" rather than pure calculation or mechanical necessity.
- **Institutions made flesh.** Habitus enables agents to inhabit institutions; king, banker, priest as monarchy, capitalism, Church made flesh through incorporation (*Logic* ch. 3).
- **Class habitus and lifestyles (*Distinction*).** Full survey program of homologous lifestyle spaces, fraction variants, and political opinions — continuous capital distributions reappear as discontinuous, misrecognizable lifestyle hierarchies.
- **Scientific and artistic fields** appear mainly as examples of field–habitus attunement and [[illusio]] in *Logic*; *Distinction* maps cultural production/consumption homology in more detail.

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Positions — to be thickened when critics are ingested.)*

- **Determinism charge.** Critics read "only those" practices inherent in conditions of production as residual structuralism. Bourdieu's own reply in *Logic* is conditioned/conditional freedom remote from both pure novelty and simple mechanical reproduction, plus hysteresis and strategy under uncertainty — still contested.
- **Measurement.** Independent operationalization of "dispositions" without circular reconstruction from the practices they explain remains difficult; *Logic* itself is ethnographic-theoretical, not psychometric.
- **Micro-mechanisms.** How exactly schemes transfer across domains (sport analogy; scheme transfer in Book II) can look underspecified relative to cognitive science or interactionism.
- **Who knows the "correct" fit?** Reconstructing habitus from observed regularities risks retrospective functionalism — a charge Bourdieu anticipates via [[practical-sense]]'s non-logical logic and economy of logic, but does not dissolve for critics.
- **Relation to rational-actor models.** Bourdieu rejected pure rational-actor and pure mechanical models of sequential interaction (gift example); whether habitus "strategies" smuggle teleology back in is a standing debate in [[practice-theory]] reception.
- **Toolkit as lighter alternative.** [[swidler-ann|Swidler]]'s [[cultural-toolkit]] concept covers the same problem-space (culture as capacity shaping practice, not rule-following) but claims less durability and more plurality/switchability than habitus — cultures constitute *multiple* selves held with variable mastery rather than one deeply structured disposition. The two are frequently contrasted in the sociology of culture. See [[culture-in-action]].
