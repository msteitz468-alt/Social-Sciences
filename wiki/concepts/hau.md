# Hau

## Definition as Coined

**Hau** enters social theory as a **migrated** term: Maori emic vocabulary recorded by Elsdon Best from informant **Tamati Ranaipiri**, then made load-bearing by [[mauss-marcel|Marcel Mauss]] in *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* (1925, Ch. I) as the answer to his research question — *what force in the thing given compels return?*

### Emic text (as Mauss quotes it)

Ranaipiri explains that if A gives *taonga* to B, and B later receives a return gift from C for that *taonga*, the second gift is the **hau** of the first and **must** be returned to A; keeping it risks illness or death. "Such is hau, the hau of personal property, the hau of the taonga, the hau of the forest."

### Mauss's etic reading (attributed)

Mauss interprets: *taonga* and personal possessions have a hau, a spiritual power; the thing given is not inert — "even when abandoned by the giver, it still forms a part of him." The hau "wants to return to the place of its birth." Hence: **to give something is to give a part of oneself**; to receive is to receive part of someone's spiritual essence; to keep without return is dangerous. The bond created by things is a bond between persons. This, he argues, motivates obligatory circulation of wealth, tribute, and gifts in Samoa and New Zealand and illuminates [[gift-exchange]] and [[total-prestation]] including [[potlatch]].

Hertz had flagged related notes; Mauss found the Ranaipiri text among Hertz's papers.

## Semantic History

| Moment | Sense |
|---|---|
| Maori (as of Best's documentation) | Spirit/power of forest, game, personal property — multi-domain |
| Mauss 1925 | Key mechanism of gift obligation; general theory of return |
| Sahlins 1972 (primary-ingested) | *Hau* = the **yield/product** of a gift, not a mystical spirit; the return owed to the original giver ("one man's gift should not be another man's capital") — see below |

## Operationalizations

Not measured. Mauss treats hau as explanatory mechanism for obligation; ethnographic tests would require Maori (and comparative) reanalysis beyond this source.

## Applications

- Core of *The Gift* Ch. I answer to the force-in-the-thing question.
- Extended analogically to power in Northwest Coast valuables, Trobriand *vaygu'a*, Germanic *wadium*, and personified gifts in Hindu materials (attributed comparative extensions).
- Downstream social theory (Blau, Bourdieu on gift/time/misrecognition) cites Mauss's gift apparatus; hau itself is less often the carried term than the **three obligations** and total prestation.

### Sahlins's reinterpretation (attributed — primary)

*(Attributed — [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]], *[[sahlins-stone-age-economics-1972|Stone Age Economics]]*, "The Spirit of the Gift," 1972.)* Sahlins rejects Mauss's mystical reading. On his account the **hau of a taonga is its "yield" — the product or return it generates when put to use**, "just as the hau of a forest is its productiveness." The Ranaipiri passage's crucial **three-party** structure (A gives to B; B trades with C; C's return is the taonga's yield, owed back to A) then makes plain economic sense: "one man's gift should not be another man's capital, and therefore the fruits of a gift ought to be passed back to the original holder." He cross-validates this against the *whangai hau* rite (priests eat some birds so the forest's fertility/hau "returns") and the transmission of *tapu* magic through a third party. His synthesis: *hau* is a **"total" (undifferentiated economic/religious) category** of fecundity, so on the "spiritual specifics" Mauss "was very likely mistaken. But in another sense, more profound, he was right." Against Sahlins stand [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] (the hau is the natives' own rationalization masking an unconscious structural logic), Firth (Mauss conflated distinct types of hau; witchcraft, not hau, punishes non-return), and Johansen (the word may simply mean *countergift*/utu). See [[gift-exchange]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Single-text load-bearing:** the general theory rests heavily on one informant passage and the reconstruction of its third-person "obscurity" — a text now read at least four incompatible ways (Mauss, Lévi-Strauss, Firth, Sahlins).
- **Emic ≠ etic:** Ranaipiri's forest/taonga cosmology is not automatically a universal spirit-of-the-gift.
- **Secondary chain:** Best → Hertz papers → Mauss; not Mauss's field interview.
- Do not promote any one reading (mystical spirit *or* Sahlins's "yield") to wiki voice; the meaning is genuinely contested (`contested: yes`).

## Sahlins 2013 — unrequited gift as life-drain (*kaihau*)

*(Attributed — [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] 2013 via Johansen 1954 on Maori.)* Failed counter-gift ruptures kinship fellowship: gift is *mate* (weakening/death) to receiver who cannot assert by counter-gifts; recipient who fails "steals a little of the giver's life" — *kaihaus* the gift (consumes the gift as *hau*, draining the giver). Placed in the kinship–gift–magic regime of [[mutuality-of-being]]; gifts may also generate inequality/domination (politics deferred). Links: [[gift-exchange]] · [[mutuality-of-being]].
