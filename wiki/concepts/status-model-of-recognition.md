# Status Model of Recognition

Fraser’s model of recognition in *[[fraser-honneth-redistribution-or-recognition-2003|Redistribution or Recognition?]]*: recognition harms are **status** injuries.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Fraser.)* Misrecognition is an institutionalized relation of **status subordination** produced by cultural value patterns that deny some actors the standing of full partners in interaction. Remedies target those institutions and patterns (parity of participation), not only interpersonal esteem or therapeutic identity repair.

**Disambiguation:** Not Bourdieu’s [[misrecognition]] (*méconnaissance*). Not identical to Honneth’s moral-psychological [[struggle-for-recognition]] grammar (though both use “recognition”).

## Semantic History

Fraser’s alternative to “identity model” readings of recognition politics; key stake in the debate with Honneth.

## Operationalizations

Ask which institutionalized value patterns impede peer status; link to legal, cultural, and organizational rules.

## Applications

Gender, race, sexuality, and cultural-minority claims framed as status not only as resource claims.

## Critiques and Limitations

Honneth: may sever recognition from its moral-experiential core. Others: status language may under-specify material power.
