# Exogamy

## Definition as Coined

**Exogamy** (McLennan) is the rule of marrying **outside** a specified social group. Complementary term: endogamy (marrying within). In [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]]'s *[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, exogamy is treated as a variable feature often associated with the [[sib]] but not its definitional essence and not a universal evolutionary stage marker.

## Semantic History

Victorian evolutionism (McLennan, Morgan) made exogamy central to conjectural histories of marriage and the gens. Boasian and later anthropology retained the term as a descriptive rule while stripping universal-stage packaging. Group boundaries (what one must marry out of) are historically specific — always state the unit.

[[white-leslie-a|Leslie A. White]] (*[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|The Evolution of Culture]]*, Chs. 4–5) gives a mid-century **culturological** reconstruction: prehuman families had strong endogamous tendencies; to unite families into larger cooperative groups, sociocultural systems "invented" the incest concept and prohibited it. Incest is defined as sexual union of persons in too close a *social* relationship (unit varies by society); it is meaningful only for [[symboling|symboling]] humans. White rejects instinct, inbreeding-deterioration (Morgan), and psychological origin theories as unable to explain cross-cultural variation (e.g. parallel-cousin prohibited, cross-cousin preferred). He follows Tylor: tribes faced the alternative of "marrying-out and being killed out." **Exogamy and endogamy are inseparable** — "marry out but not too far" — effectiveness of the cooperative group depending on size × solidarity (which vary inversely). Sequence claims: incest prohibitions and exogamy precede clan organization.

## Operationalizations

Specify: (1) the unit prohibited (sib, moiety, village…); (2) whether unilateral or other; (3) sanctions; (4) relation to incest rules and to preferential mating (e.g. cross-cousin). Lowie separates marriage prohibitions, preferential mating, and sib exogamy as analytically distinct. White adds: cooperative-group size and solidarity as functional determinants; culturological vs psychological explanation as a register choice.

## Applications

Sib chapters and marriage chapters of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*; Morgan–McLennan debates; [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]; White Chs. 4–5 origin of human social systems; Kariera→Arunta expansion examples (White).

## Critiques and Limitations

"Exogamy" alone does not identify function (alliance, incest avoidance, group identity). Silent equation of all out-marriage rules as one institution is a comparative error. White's "invention of incest" and primitive ideal-type of mutual-aid kinship are attributed theoretical reconstructions, not ethnographic consensus; later alliance theory and genetic inbreeding models re-open bio/psych channels White closed.
