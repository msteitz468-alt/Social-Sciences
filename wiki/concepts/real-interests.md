# Real Interests

*(Attributed to [[lukes-steven|Lukes]], *[[lukes-power-radical-view-2005|Power: A Radical View]]*).* Interests that would be held or pursued if agents were freer from others’ power — required for the **third dimension** of power (preference-shaping). Distinct from Dahrendorf’s [[latent-and-manifest-interests]] (role expectations in ICAs).

## Definition as Coined

One-dimensional power presupposes a liberal conception of interests (revealed preferences); two-dimensional a reformist one; three-dimensional requires a **radical** conception allowing that wants may themselves be products of power. 2005: real interests are a function of explanatory purpose/framework (materialist, rational-choice, capabilities, etc.), not a final moral oracle.

## Semantic History

Tied to critiques of revealed-preference liberalism and to debates over [[false-consciousness]]. Lukes 2005 answers paternalism and counterfactual-coherence critiques by purpose-relative ascription and empirical illustrations (e.g. women’s collectives; pollution–employment trade-offs).

## Operationalizations

Counterfactuals; comparative cases of reduced power; capability approaches (Nussbaum cited); material interests under class-compromise frameworks (Przeworski cited).

## Applications

Third-dimension power analysis; ideology critique; development and gender capability work as cited by Lukes.

## Critiques and Limitations

Accused of elitism/paternalism; infinite regress of third powers C; identity of the counterfactual agent; Lukes softens but does not abandon the construct.
