# Ritual Regulation (of Ecological Systems)

## Definition as Coined

[[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]]'s central thesis in *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* and the 1967 *Ethnology* paper reprinted in *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*: among the [[tsembaga]], **ritual** (especially the cycle culminating in the [[kaiko]]) **regulates** relations among people, pigs, and gardens and articulates the local ecosystem with the regional system of other local populations. He frames ritual as both **homeostat** (maintaining variables in viable ranges) and **transducer** (translating state changes in one subsystem into effects in another).

**Eight effects** (*Pigs* Ch. 1 thesis list, attributed): (1) people–pigs–gardens / fallow and forest; (2) pig slaughter and pork distribution; (3) nondomesticated-animal consumption; (4) possible marsupial conservation; (5) redispersion of people and land; (6) warfare frequency; (7) severity of fighting; (8) exchange of goods and personnel among local groups.

Religious ritual is defined in the 1967/EMR short paper as prescribed performance of conventionalized acts manifestly directed toward nonempirical agencies, while attention focuses on **empirical effects** — ritual as part of the behavioral repertoire of an aggregate of organisms adjusting to its environment (attributed).

## Semantic History

1967 *Ethnology* précis → 1968 *Pigs* monograph → 1970s textbook icon → sustained critique as functionalist teleology / vulgar materialism → EMR "Ills of Functionalism" (1977/1979) + "Adaptive Structure" + formal ritual essays → 1984 Epilogue defense and refinement (formal vs final cause, units, negative feedback). Related to but not identical with Stewardian [[cultural-ecology]] (Rappaport is more systems/cybernetic). EMR formal analysis claims ecological regulation rests on structural properties of [[liturgical-order]] (indexical pledges, binary transduction of continuous states, sanctified commissives).

## Operationalizations

Timing of rumbim planting/uprooting and kaiko relative to pig herd, garden stress, truce length, and warfare; quantitative appendices support the operational side. Latent-function language (Merton) bridges cognized and operational models. In [[adaptive-structure]], ritual cycles are loci of regulation in egalitarian hierarchical systems (alongside immutability of creeds and seasonal festivals as time-dependent regulation).

## Applications

Type-case for arguing against Homans-style claims that ritual has no practical effect on the external world. Feeds debates on functionalism, adaptation, and the autonomy of meaning. Sanctified military pledges in Maring dancing (indexical contingent size → future support) link regulation to [[sanctity]] / [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Core of [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]]: circularity, selection of confirming variables, nutritional reanalyses, political agency underplayed, comparison limits beyond Maring. EMR records partial concessions on 1968 homeostat logic while rejecting the collapse of ecological anthropology into vulgar materialism.
