# Colligation

## Definition as Coined

In [[abbott-andrew|Abbott]]'s sequence-methods program (attributed; “Event Sequence and Event Duration,” 1984; *[[abbott-time-matters-2001|Time Matters]]* prologue, chs. 3–6, Epilogue), **colligation** is the narrative version of **conceptualization**: assembling particular **occurrences** into abstract **events** at a coherent narrative level so sequences can be compared without mixing incompatible indicators.

Philosophy-of-history lineage: Collingwood, Gallie, Mink treat colligation as central to historical understanding; Abbott (ch. 3, attributed) places this literature against Hempelian covering-law accounts of explanation and against the ANOVA-shaped causalism he reads in Durkheim's *Suicide*. Epilogue: colligation and **central subjects** are the historical equivalents of conceptualization (including classification and measurement) and units of analysis.

## Semantic History

Whewell in philosophy of science; narrative historians and philosophers of history; Abbott's import into sociological sequence measurement after the Wilensky/order-statistics problem. Related plot/emplotment language (Barthes kernels; Hayden White) appears in Abbott's discussion of what cases “do” narratively (ch. 4).

## Operationalizations

- Choose event alphabet and which empirical happenings count as instances ([[event-occurrence-distinction]]).
- Hold a **single coherent narrative level** (avoid once-only vs many-times mix).
- Trade off order statistics, medians, and rates when occurrence timing distributions differ by event frequency.
- Support generic “plots” and [[entity-processes|entity-process links]] for multicase narrative comparison.

## Applications

Prerequisite step for rigorous [[sequence-analysis]] of professionalization, careers, and institutionalization. Underpins [[turning-point]] identification (what counts as a regime vs a turn depends on colligated narrative structure).

## Critiques and Limitations

Under-specified colligation silently biases sequence orderings. Abbott's own discarded *System of Professions* chapters illustrate the cost (attributed, prologue). Colligation is theory-laden — not a neutral preprocessing step.
