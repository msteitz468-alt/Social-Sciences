# State-Church (White)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|The Evolution of Culture]]* Part Two, [[white-leslie-a|Leslie A. White]] coins **state-church** for the special political mechanism that civil society requires after the [[agricultural-revolution|Agricultural Revolution]] — not two separate entities but **aspects of one coordinative, integrative mechanism** (secular-civil-military and ecclesiastical-clerical-theological). Ch. 13:

> To accomplish all this, the new society would have to have a special mechanism, a special political device, for coordination, integration, regulation, and control. Such a special mechanism was developed. We shall call it the state-church.

> What the brain and central nervous system are to biological evolution, the state-church is to sociocultural evolution.

White's seven-step sequence to first civil societies ends with (6) state-church coordinating parts and **coercing food producers to yield surplus** and (7) dominant vs subordinate class + breakdown of tribal society. Forms may be structurally fused, distinct, or overlapping; he cites Spencer that originally church and state were one. From the sociocultural-system standpoint the church is "more properly a political institution of integration and control" even when analyzed as religion from the individual psyche.

## Semantic History

White's hyphenated construct is specific to his evolutionary synthesis (civil society after agriculture). It sits adjacent to sociological theories of church and state, Marxian superstructure, and archaeological accounts of temple-palace complexes (Childe; Near Eastern and Mediterranean cases White uses). Later anthropology rarely keeps the exact hyphenated term; temple-economy and early-state literature cover related ground without White's full energy narrative.

## Operationalizations

Forms and functions catalogued comparatively (despotic and other political forms; church property and social control; education as political process) rather than a single quantitative index.

## Applications

Bridge between Agricultural Revolution (Ch. 12) and economic structure of higher cultures (Ch. 14) / theology and science (Ch. 15) in White's sequence up to Rome.

## Critiques and Limitations

Conflating "state" and "church" can erase historically specific church-state separations and non-theocratic polities; evolutionary packaging is contested ([[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]). Use as White's analytic composite, not as a universal type asserted in wiki voice.
