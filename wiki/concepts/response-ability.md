## Definition as Coined

[[haraway-donna|Donna Haraway]] coins "response-ability" (hyphenated, playing on
"responsibility") in *Staying with the Trouble* as the cultivated capacity to respond —
a capacity central to what she calls "staying with the trouble." She glosses her
neologism Terrapolis as "the SF game of response-ability" *(Haraway 2016)*,
[[haraway-staying-with-the-trouble-2016|Staying with the Trouble]].

Haraway theorizes response-ability as differentially distributed rather than universal:
"we are all responsible to and for shaping conditions for multispecies flourishing... but
we are not all response-able in the same ways." Cultivating response-ability, in her
account, is a practice of collective knowing and doing — an "ecology of practices" —
that she contrasts explicitly with Hannah Arendt's account (relayed via Valerie
Hartouni) of Adolf Eichmann's "thoughtlessness," his inability to make present to himself
what was not himself: "In passion and action, detachment and attachment, this is what I
call cultivating response-ability... The answer to the trust of the held-out hand: think
we must."

## Semantic History

Response-ability is coined within this book and elaborated across nearly every chapter,
making it one of the text's load-bearing terms. Haraway draws on Thom van Dooren's
*Flight Ways* (on whooping cranes and Hawaiian crows/'Alalā) to argue that mourning is
intrinsic to cultivating response-ability, extending the capacity beyond the human by
treating corvid grief as evidence that non-human animals also participate in
response-able relations.

The concept is explicitly linked to [[despret-vinciane|Vinciane Despret]]'s notion of
"rendering capable" — the idea that observer and observed mutually capacitate one
another through relational encounter — which Haraway treats as a philosophical precedent
for response-ability. Haraway also credits Carla Hustak and Natasha Myers, in their work
on orchid-bee "involutionary momentum," with proposing "a feminist ethic of
'response-ability'" as an alternative to neo-Darwinian "selfish gene" readings of
orchid-insect mutualism — crediting them alongside her own use of the term. In Chapter 5,
Haraway extends response-ability to the biomedical case of DES and Premarin (equine
estrogen), coining "viral response-ability" to describe the "leaks and eddies" of
hormones and accountability crossing species and institutional boundaries: "Leaks and
eddies are everywhere. These leaks and eddies might help open passages for a praxis of
care and response—response-ability—in ongoing multispecies worlding on a wounded terra."

## Operationalizations

Response-ability is not operationalized as a measurable variable in Haraway's text; it
functions as a normative and figurative capacity assessed narratively (through case
material) rather than through an index or instrument.

## Applications

Haraway applies response-ability across a wide range of illustrative cases: racing-pigeon
practices and citizen-science projects; the activist history of Premarin-farm reform
(HorseAid's documentation of PMU-farm conditions from 1986/1988, the 1995 formation of
the industry group NAERIC, and continued activist calls to end PMU farming), framed as
an example of "viral response-ability" distributing accountability across contract
farmers, activists, mares, and consumers; and the Camille Stories fable in Chapter 8,
where a child's genetically and developmentally linked bond with an endangered migratory
species is presented as enabling heightened multispecies response-ability. Haraway also
situates the Camille fable's five-generation horizon against the Haudenosaunee "seventh
generation" principle of responsibility, acknowledging this as an appropriation of that
principle for her own narrative purposes.

## Critiques and Limitations

The claims record documents Haraway's own qualification that response-ability is
unevenly distributed and cannot be treated as a uniform capacity across actors; it does
not surface external scholarly critique of the term specifically within this source.
Haraway's contrast with Eichmann's "thoughtlessness" positions response-ability as an
ethical achievement rather than a given, implying failure modes (thoughtlessness,
disconnection) as the concept's structural opposite, though this is Haraway's own framing
rather than an independent critique.
