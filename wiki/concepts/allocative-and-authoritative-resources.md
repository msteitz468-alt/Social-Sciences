# Allocative and Authoritative Resources

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*):

- **Allocative resources:** material resources involved in the generation of power, including the natural environment and physical artifacts; derive from human dominion over nature.
- **Authoritative resources:** non-material resources involved in the generation of power, deriving from the capability of harnessing the activities of human beings; result from the dominion of some actors over others.

## Semantic History

Power as transformative capacity is inherent in agency; resources expand power across time-space. Related to **dialectic of control** (two-way character of power as control). Not identical to Bourdieu's capital types.

## Operationalizations

Comparative analysis of material control vs command over persons/organization/surveillance in types of society (tribal, class-divided, capitalist).

## Applications

- Institutional orders; change and power (Ch. 5); disciplinary surveillance as authoritative resource expansion.

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary may under-specify cultural/symbolic resources (compare symbolic capital).
