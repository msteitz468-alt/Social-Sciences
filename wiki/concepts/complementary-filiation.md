# Complementary Filiation

> Core analytical concept associated with [[fortes-meyer|Meyer Fortes]], developed ethnographically for the [[tallensi|Tallensi]] in *[[fortes-web-of-kinship-tallensi-1949|The Web of Kinship among the Tallensi]]* (1949). Study: [[fortes-web-of-kinship-study]]. Contrasts with [[double-descent]] (two corporate unilineal groups) and with pure bilateral kindred models.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed to Fortes.)* In a system of [[unilineal-descent|unilineal descent]], **complementary filiation** is the structural weight of filiation through the parent who does *not* transmit corporate group membership. For patrilineal Tallensi, that means the **maternal parent and uterine/matrilateral kin**: *soog* (uterine kinship), the mother’s-brother / sister’s-son complex (*ahab* / *ahay*), and the wider extra-clan web spun through women. These ties are **personal and non-corporate** — they do not found a second clan — yet they are not residual “bilateral leftovers.” Fortes argued they supply the primary **centrifugal** force of dispersion and segmentation that balances the **centripetal** stability of patriliny.

Native maxim Fortes reports: “It is women who cause kinship to spread widely.”

## Semantic History

- **1949 ethnographic base.** *Web of Kinship* states the cognatic/agnatic distinction, the *soog* complex, the MB–ZS nuclear relationship, and the centripetal/centrifugal synthesis without always using the later textbook label; the structural idea is fully present.
- **Later Fortes essays / textbooks.** “Complementary filiation” becomes the standard name in mid-century British kinship teaching for the non-corporate other-parent line in unilineal systems.
- **Contest.** Alliance theorists and critics of descent theory argued the concept smuggled bilaterality into unilineal models or underplayed marriage exchange; defenders treated it as the correct structural completion of unilineal corporate theory. The dispute is historiographic and theoretical — record positions, do not adjudicate in wiki voice.
- **Leach *Pul Eliya* (1961) adjacent critique.** Not a direct assault on the Tallensi *soog* complex by name, but the monograph’s program rejects treating matrilateral/affinal weight as residual completion of a unilineal frame: in Pul Eliya there is no unilineal corporate frame to “complete”; brothers rival as co-heirs while affines maximise co-operation; *Rethinking Anthropology* (same year) is the explicit [[affinity]] vs complementary-filiation polemic. See [[leach-pul-eliya-study]], [[descent-theory]].
- **Not double descent.** [[double-descent]] assigns corporate unilineal groups through *both* sexes; complementary filiation does not.

## Operationalizations

| Indicator (Tallensi as type case) | Corporate unilineal? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Maximal lineage / clan membership | Yes (agnatic) | Inheritance, succession, *bɔyar* cult |
| *soog* uterine bond | No | Intimacy, aid, witchcraft potentiality, *tyuk* |
| *ahab*–*ahay* (MB–ZS) | No (personal; uses MB’s lineage powers) | Gifts, fosterage, ritual shares, marriage intermediary |
| Father’s sister (*ba-pok*) | Limited | “Female father”; weaker jural force than avunculate |
| Kindred as closed unit | No | Fortes: kindred-as-unit does not exist among Tallensi |

## Applications

- **[[tallensi]]** (Fortes 1949) — type case: patriliny + strong complementary maternal web.
- Comparative teaching: any unilineal system where the “other” parent’s kin carry jural, ritual, or affective load without forming a second corporate descent group (attribute case by case).
- Linked institutions: [[family]], [[marriage]], [[avunculate]]; method: [[genealogical-method]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Naming lag.** The 1949 monograph’s vocabulary is “cognatic/matrilateral/uterine” and centrifugal force; later label “complementary filiation” should not be back-projected as if Fortes used it as a chapter title.
- **Theory wars.** Descent vs alliance readings of the same African materials remain contested; keep claims attributed.
- **Not universal bilaterality.** The concept presupposes a dominant unilineal corporate principle; it is not a claim that all kinship is bilateral.

## From Kuper, *Invention of Primitive Society* (1988)

*(Attributed — Kuper 1988, Ch. 10.)* Fortes contrasted **descent** and **complementary filiation**: corporate descent groups tend to carry legal/public enforcement; complementary line carries religion, morality, conscience, and sentiment (1953 UDG essay). Leach later charged that Fortes “disguises” ties of affinity under complementary filiation (*Man* exchange). Source: [[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988]]; [[descent-theory]].

## From Leach, *Rethinking Anthropology* (1961)

*(Attributed — Leach; Fortes/Goody positions as Leach reports them.)* Primary Leach texts: Preface, Ch. 1 (Malinowski Memorial Lecture as “rejoinder to Fortes”), Ch. 2 Intro Note, Ch. 5.

**Leach’s charge.** Fortes recognizes affinity’s importance but “disguises” it under complementary filiation. Fortes (1959b) reaffirmed complementary filiation as “a function of affinal relations” and as “the kinship reciprocal of affinal relationship in the marriage tie” — which Leach says confuses (1) a concept that only makes sense with unilineal descent with (2) inter-individual marriage ties, missing enduring **group** alliance categories such as Jinghpaw *[[mayu-dama|mayu/dama]]* and Lakher *patong*.

**Genealogy of the concept (Leach).** Complementary filiation is in one view the inverse of Malinowski’s “sociological paternity” in the matrilineal Trobriand context; Fortes made it cornerstone theory because Tallensi and Ashanti both approach double-unilineal patterns while he rejected labelling them double-descent systems — so the concept protects the unilineal thesis. Leach: category distinctions are contrived; Goody’s “complementary descent rather than dual descent” continues the tautology (adjective “complementary” meaningless without “descent”).

**Heresy.** If Trobrianders treat father–son as like male cross-cousins and brothers-in-law (not like mother–child), accept that; calling it filiation is delusion. Complementary filiation may have utility for intermediate cases but must not be universalized; prefer algebraic/topological formulation of extremes ([[butterfly-collecting|anti-typology]]).

**Ch. 5 ban.** For Kachin–Gauri–[[lakher|Lakher]] materials, terms “complementary filiation,” “complementary descent,” and “double unilineal descent” are all **inappropriate** and yield false conclusions; differences are in the **marriage institution**, not descent form. Jack Goody (1959) and Fortes (1959b) denounced Leach — record as positions. Source: [[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961]]; [[affinity]].
