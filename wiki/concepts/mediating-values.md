# Mediating Values

## Definition as Coined

In complex structures too large for most members ever to interact directly, [[blau-peter-m|Blau]] argues that **shared values serve as the mediating mechanism** extending social transactions beyond face-to-face contact: "common values of various types can be conceived of as media of social transactions that expand the compass of social interaction and the structure of social relations through social space and time." The analogy is to **money** as the medium of economic exchange (Boulding: assets "customarily exchanged for a wide variety of other assets... wanted mainly because of a belief in their continuing ability so to be exchanged"): values, like money, function as a shared context, as mediating links, and as a store of value.

Blau's signature is a **fourfold typology** of mediating values, each the medium of one **facet of social structure**, organized by [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]]'s particularism/universalism criterion:

| Mediating value | Medium of | Structural facet |
|---|---|---|
| **Particularistic** values | solidarity (unite ingroup, segregate outgroups) | social **integration** |
| **Universalistic** standards | exchange and status differentiation (like money) | **differentiation** / distribution |
| **Legitimating** values | authority independent of persons ("not the man in the White House but... the office") | **organization** |
| **Opposition** ideals | reorganization; extend dissent to all whom conditions predispose | social **change** |

## Semantic History

Blau reaches value-consensus late and instrumentally — the opposite of [[structural-functionalism|Parsons]], who begins from a shared value system. For Blau values are not the starting point of order but a *medium* that exchange requires once structures outgrow direct interaction; he explicitly notes their function is "methodological as well as substantive," while cautioning that not all social constraint derives from shared values (power, exchange, mobility, and opposition also operate).

## Operationalizations

Not measured directly; identified by which facet of structure a value sustains and by the [[institutionalization]] mechanisms that transmit it (historical formalization plus socialization).

## Applications

The bridge in [[social-exchange-theory]] from micro exchange to macrostructure: mediating values carry [[indirect-exchange]] across the collectivity, ground the four types of social [[institutionalization|institutions]] (integrative, distributive, political, counterinstitutional), and — via the "counterinstitutional component" of every cultural heritage — supply even radical [[opposition]] with legitimation from within the prevailing culture.

## Critiques and Limitations

- The mapping of four value types onto four structural facets is elegant but schematic; critics question whether social values sort so cleanly.
- This is precisely the macro joint Blau later (1986) judged the book's weakest — the derivation of value-mediated macrostructure from micro exchange he came to think required a *different* conceptual scheme, not an extension of the micro one.
