# Ontological Security

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: **confidence or trust** that the natural and social worlds are as they appear to be, including the basic existential parameters of self and social identity (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

*Constitution* Ch. 2 draws critically on Erikson (trust/mistrust) and on the anxiety of **critical situations** (e.g. Bettelheim's camps) that strip routine. Sustained primarily by [[routinization]] and tact in encounters, not by continuous discursive reflection. Later Giddens modernity writings (not yet ingested) extend the concept to late-modern self-identity.

## Operationalizations

Theoretical in 1984. Subsequent applied work operationalizes "ontological security" in housing, IR, and mental-health literatures — secondary.

## Applications

- Explains affective investment in daily routines and front-region performances (not mere cynical role-play).
- Links micro encounters to institutional continuity.
- Front regions cannot be pure façades if ontological security is to be sustained (*Constitution* Ch. 3 vs Goffman dramaturgy).

## Critiques and Limitations

Psychological borrowing contested; risk of functionalist reading (routine exists "for" security). Giddens treats security as condition and outcome of routine, not system need.
