# Minimally Counterintuitive Concepts (MCI)

## Definition as Coined

[[boyer-pascal|Boyer]]'s account (as summarized by White 2021): religious concepts that are **minimally counterintuitive** — they breach a small number of intuitive ontological expectations (physical, biological, or psychological) while remaining otherwise intuitive — enjoy a transmission advantage over fully intuitive or massively counterintuitive concepts.

## Semantic History

Developed within cognitive anthropology and CSR; related to "structured imagination" and domain-specific intuitive ontology. Distinct from merely "bizarre" or maximally counterintuitive theology.

## Operationalizations

Recall and memory experiments coding concept types (White ch. 4 notes mixed results across 15 coded MCI types; cognitive architecture often more complex than assumed).

## Applications

Explains cultural success of ghosts, gods, spirits as agents with strategic limited violations (invisible yet minded, etc.).

## Critiques and Limitations

Empirical memory advantage mixed (White); theological systems include highly counterintuitive doctrines sustained by institutions, not only MCI attractors; [[theological-correctness]] gaps show reflective theology diverges from intuitive MCI packages.
