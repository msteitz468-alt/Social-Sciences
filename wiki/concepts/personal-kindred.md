# Personal Kindred

## Definition as Coined

A **personal kindred** is an ego-centered set of kin. In *[[stack-all-our-kin-1974|All Our Kin]]*, [[stack-carol|Stack]] specifies that in [[the-flats|The Flats]] membership is governed by a **folk system of parental rights and duties**: **essential kin** actively accept responsibility and reciprocal obligation; mere **relatives** may be genealogically known without being "really kinfolk." Friends who "you count on" may receive kin terms and enter the kindred. Billy (emic): "We help each other out and that's what kinfolks are all about."

## Semantic History

Built on comparative kinship (Goodenough on social parenthood; Fox/Keesing on kindreds). Stack's contribution is empirical specification under urban poverty and the essential-kin vs relative distinction, plus recruitment into multi-household [[domestic-network|domestic networks]].

## Operationalizations

Genealogies plus observation of who actually swaps, keeps children, and is sanctioned; not pure descent reckoning. Half-siblings can have non-identical personal kindreds.

## Applications

[[stack-all-our-kin-study]] Ch. 4; feeds domestic-network analysis (networks recruit from overlapping personal kindreds).

## Critiques and Limitations

Ego-centered construct can obscure network-level (non-ego) organization — which is why Stack pairs it with domestic network. Folk vs legal parenthood dual system is setting-specific (welfare/courts).
