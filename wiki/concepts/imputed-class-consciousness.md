# Imputed Class Consciousness

## Definition as Coined

**Imputed class consciousness** (*zugerechnetes Klassenbewusstsein*; *zugerechnetes Bewusstsein*) is [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]]’s term in *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]* for the consciousness **objectively appropriate** to a class’s typical position in production — the rational reactions that can be **imputed** to that position when it is related to society as a [[concrete-totality|totality]]. It is explicitly **not** the empirical psychological consciousness of individual proletarians or of the proletariat as a mass-psychological average.

> Class consciousness consists of the appropriate and rational reactions “imputed” [zugerechnet] to a particular typical position in the process of production. *(Lukács, “Class Consciousness,” 1923)*

The English edition apparatus glosses the term as the category of **possible** consciousness — what the class **can** know from its structural place — versus what it currently thinks.

## Semantic History

- **1923 *HCC*:** dialectical distance between imputed and psychological consciousness is the space of political education, crisis, and organisation; opportunism is defined as mistaking the latter for the former.
- **Relation to Lenin (disputed):** Lukács 1967 equates his meaning with Lenin’s implant of socialist consciousness “from outside” trade-union struggle (*What Is to Be Done?*). Löwy (2023) argues that *HCC* itself still presents rise to imputed consciousness as a process through the class’s own struggle (with vanguard assistance), whereas *Tailism and the Dialectic* (c. 1925) hardens the Kautskyan/Lenin 1902 “from outside” line.
- **Reception:** becomes a flashpoint for debates on party, substitutionism, and whether Western Marxism’s subject is the empirical working class or a philosophical construction.
- **1967 retraction:** Lukács says he could not progress beyond the imputed category; left as pure intellectual result, it remains contemplative — a “miracle” if it becomes praxis.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Grounded in **objective possibility**: infer what thoughts and feelings agents would have if able to assess both their immediate situation and its bearing on the whole structure of society. Number of such situations is limited by types of position in production.

Pre-capitalist epochs (Lukács): agents could not become conscious of true driving forces **even by virtue of an imputed consciousness** — economic articulation was not available as pure form. Capitalism makes pure class consciousness (economic form) possible for the first time.

## Applications

- Analytic distinction on [[class-consciousness]] pages and party-theory arguments in *HCC*’s organisation essay (internal proletarian strata as stages of consciousness penetration).
- Critique of Social-Democratic and “vulgar Marxist” politics that treat spontaneous economic consciousness as sufficient.
- Negative diagnostic for other classes: for petty bourgeoisie/peasantry, full imputed consciousness would reveal hopelessness of particularism — hence their consciousness stays borrowed or ideological.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Political danger:** can underwrite elite certification of “true” class interest against workers’ stated views.
- **Philosophical danger (1967 Lukács):** remains idealist/contemplative without labour as model of praxis and without concrete mediation into organisation and struggle.
- **Historical:** composition in a messianic-revolutionary conjuncture (1919–22) colours the concept’s urgency; later Lukács disowned the package while the term continued in Western Marxist debate.
