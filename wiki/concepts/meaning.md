# Meaning (Sinn)

## Definition as Coined

In [[luhmann-niklas|Luhmann]]'s [[social-systems-theory|systems theory]], **meaning** (*Sinn*)
is the medium in which both [[psychic-system|psychic]] and social systems operate — "the
indispensable, undeniable form of their complexity and self-reference." Following Husserl,
Luhmann defines meaning as the **actualization of one possibility against a horizon of other
co-given possibilities**: "a surplus of references to other possibilities of experience and
action... all else is indicated marginally as the horizon of an 'and so forth.'" Meaning is
therefore, in form (not content), the *processing* of [[complexity]] — it simultaneously
forces selection and preserves the excluded possibilities as still-available.

Luhmann distinguishes **three meaning dimensions**, each with a twofold horizon, none reducible
to another:
- **Factual/objective** — this versus something else (internal/external horizon).
- **Temporal** — past versus future; the present as the span in which change becomes
  irreversible.
- **Social** — ego versus alter; the horizon of consensus/dissent.

## Semantic History

Luhmann's usage departs from the hermeneutic tradition, which opposes meaning to
*meaninglessness* (*Sinnlosigkeit*). For Luhmann meaning is **unnegatable** — "any attempt to
negate meaning on the whole would presuppose meaning, would have to occur in the world. Thus
meaning is an unnegatable category, a category devoid of difference." Crucially he shifts the
reference point of meaning "from the transcendental subject" to "the empirical operations of
self-referential systems" (Knodt) — meaning is no longer a subject's achievement but a
system's medium. Meaning is basally unstable (a "compulsion to self-alteration"), and its
"auto-agility" is "[[autopoiesis]] par excellence." Meaning is not a sign: signs exclude
self-reference, whereas meaning is both world-referring and self-referential, and is the
*precondition* for any sign.

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct. Meaning-constituted (not physical or territorial) boundaries are,
for Luhmann, the general form of a social system's boundary; every [[communication]] "employs
the system/environment difference and thereby contributes to determining or changing the
system's boundaries."

## Applications

- The common medium that lets psychic and social systems **[[interpenetration|interpenetrate]]**
  while each protects its own autopoiesis.
- Grounds the theory of structure as generalized expectation (symbolic generalizations condense
  into expectations), and the account of the "world" as the ultimate, unnegatable horizon of
  all meaning.
- Luhmann claims meaning supersedes the classical *animal sociale* (human-nature) grounding of
  society.

## Critiques and Limitations

Detaching meaning from the subject and from intention is precisely what humanist and critical
readers (notably [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]) reject: it evacuates the interpreting, reasoning
agent (see [[luhmann-habermas-debate]]). The claim that meaning is strictly unnegatable and
"without difference" is also contested as a philosophical stipulation rather than a finding.
All claims here are Luhmann's, in attributed voice.
