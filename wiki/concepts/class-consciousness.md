# Class Consciousness

## Definition as Coined

In the essay “Class Consciousness” (1920; revised in *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]* 1923), [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]] defines **class consciousness** as the appropriate and rational reactions **“imputed” [zugerechnet]** to a particular typical position in the process of production — **neither the sum nor the average** of what individual members of the class think or feel. Historically significant actions of the class as a whole are, he argues, determined in the last resort by this consciousness, and can be understood only by reference to it.

The definition sits inside [[historical-materialism]]: class position is fixed by place in production; the real motor forces of history are independent of men’s psychological consciousness of them (Engels, as Lukács reads him); yet consciousness is not denied a decisive role — men act consciously, but that consciousness is typically **[[false-consciousness|false]]** and must be investigated as a stage of the historical totality, not scored true/false abstractly.

> Class consciousness consists of the appropriate and rational reactions “imputed” [zugerechnet] to a particular typical position in the process of production. This consciousness is, therefore, neither the sum nor the average of what is thought or felt by the single individuals who make up the class. *(Lukács 1923, “Class Consciousness”)*

See also the specialised page [[imputed-class-consciousness]] for the *zugerechnet* category and its afterlife.

## Semantic History

| Layer | Sense |
|---|---|
| Marx/Engels tradition | Class interest, ideology, spontaneous vs socialist consciousness (Lenin *What Is to Be Done?* is a parallel pole, not identical to Lukács) |
| **Lukács 1923** | Structural / imputed vs psychological; only bourgeoisie and proletariat as pure classes under capitalism; proletarian consciousness uniquely fuses theory and practice and aims at abolishing class society |
| Opportunism (as Lukács targets it) | Equates actual psychological state of workers with class consciousness of the proletariat |
| Survey / political psychology (later) | Attitude scales, “class identity” items — different operational family |
| **Wright *[[wright-class-counts-1997|Class Counts]]*** | Strictly **micro** ordinary consciousness (identity + interest items), not Lukácsian imputed consciousness. Class structure **limits** consciousness; class formation **selects** practices; struggle can **transform** structure. Comparative finding (project surveys): ideological polarization **Sweden > United States > Japan**; bourgeois coalitions form via authority (US) vs credentials (Japan); [[mediated-class-locations]] weight own job vs spouse more in Sweden than US for married women's identity. See [[wright-class-counts-study]]. |
| [[mannheim-karl|Mannheim]] / SoK | Adjacent false-consciousness and ideology debates; not a restatement of imputed consciousness |
| [[hegemony|Gramscian]] reception | Consent and common sense as cultural organisation of class power — related problem-space, different apparatus |

Lukács 1967 self-criticism: he “could not progress beyond” imputed consciousness; the category risked remaining contemplative unless a “miracle” converted it into revolutionary praxis. Löwy (2023) stresses that in *HCC* itself the rise to imputed consciousness is a **dialectical process** through struggle (with vanguard assistance), not a pure “from outside” implant — contrast *Tailism and the Dialectic*.

## Operationalizations

**Lukács:** category of **objective possibility** — what thoughts/feelings agents **would** have if they could assess their situation and its interest for immediate action and for the structure of society as a whole. Not a psychometric construct. Practical triad: objective economic totality ↔ imputed class consciousness ↔ real psychological thoughts.

Later empirical “class consciousness” research (identity scales, party ID, subjective class) should not be silently equated with Lukács’s category.

## Applications

- **Bourgeoisie (Lukács):** can clarify particular interests but cannot grasp the **totality** without self-negation; barrier is objective class situation → false consciousness that, under defensive pressure, becomes “mendacious.” Planned-economy readiness among progressive bourgeois strata read as capitulation of bourgeois consciousness before the proletariat’s.
- **Proletariat:** superiority “exclusively” in ability to see society from the centre as a coherent whole; theory and practice coincide; ideology is weapon and objective, not banner. Fate of revolution depends on **ideological maturity** of the proletariat.
- **Other classes:** petty bourgeoisie transitional, imagines itself above classes; peasantry “class and not a class” (Marx’s potato-sack formula as Lukács uses it) — borrowed ideologies; full consciousness would reveal hopelessness of particularist aims.
- **Organisation:** Communist Party as organised form of correct class consciousness; workers’ councils as overcoming of reification; critique of separation of economic from political struggle.

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Authoritarian risk:** imputed consciousness can license vanguard substitution for empirical workers (intensified in *Tailism*, per Löwy).
- **1967 self-criticism:** contemplative residue; missing labour/metabolism; messianic subjectivism.
- **Empirical sociology:** the concept resists survey operationalisation; using it as a moral/political yardstick against workers’ reported views is a known abuse.
- **Non-identity with Gramsci:** [[hegemony]] organises consent across civil society; Lukács’s category is still centred on production-position and revolutionary subject.

## Scott / Sedaka (*Weapons of the Weak*, 1985)

*(Attributed — [[scott-james-c]].)* Scott treated class consciousness as lived and contested in [[sedaka|Sedaka]] through divergent rich/poor histories of the Green Revolution, folk vocabulary of exploitation, and [[everyday-forms-of-resistance]] — not as imputed revolutionary consciousness awaiting a party. He argued against ranking “trade-union consciousness” as merely the obstacle to revolution (Lenin as Scott reads him) and extended Lukács-adjacent concerns about class experience without equating Sedaka’s poor with a classical proletariat. See [[scott-weapons-of-the-weak-study]], [[hegemony]], [[false-consciousness]].
