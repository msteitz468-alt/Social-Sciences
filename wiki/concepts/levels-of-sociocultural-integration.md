# Levels of Sociocultural Integration

## Definition as Coined

[[steward-julian-h|Steward]] (*[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]*, Ch. 3; earlier 1950/1951 article) proposed **levels of sociocultural integration** as an operational concept: cultures and societies are organized at successive scopes of social relations — classically **nuclear family**, **folk / multifamily community**, and **state / national** — with qualitative as well as quantitative change between levels. He insisted this is **not** Morgan/Tylor unilinear sequence ([[social-evolutionism]]); levels are analytic tools for comparing systems and for analyzing interaction *inside* complex societies (localized/"vertical" and horizontal segments).

## Semantic History

Neo-evolutionary complexity language without savagery/barbarism/civilization labels. Steward re-read Redfield's "folk society" as an integrational **level**, not only a [[culture-type]]. Lineage and clan may mark intermediate integration levels without being single culture types (Ch. 9).

**Service elaboration (primary-ingested):** [[service-elman-r|Service]] (*[[service-primitive-social-organization-1971|Primitive Social Organization]]*, 1962/1971) reworks levels as the **[[band-tribe-chiefdom-model|band / tribe / chiefdom]]** sequence of social organization under Sahlins’s general vs specific evolution. His band level (familistic economy/polity/religion), tribal level ([[sodality|pan-tribal sodalities]]), and [[chiefdom]] level (permanent redistributional coordination) are general-evolutionary classes, not identical to Steward’s family / folk / state operational set — they specialize the levels idea for political-social integration. Service also reconsiders Steward’s “family level” Western Shoshone and Eskimo as possibly once more band-like under aboriginal conditions.

## Operationalizations

- Compare societies at the same level (family-level [[great-basin-shoshoneans]] vs family-level Eskimo: same level, different types).
- Analyze how national institutions reorganize local levels (Ch. 4; Puerto Rico; Inca/Spanish layered replacement).
- Acculturation: family-level groups vs multifamily tribes vs state-segment communities differ in how national systems penetrate them; Steward held the family level is often hardest to change as a *pattern*, while multifamily tribes on reservations face traumatic dependency.
- Developmental eras of early civilizations named by organizational levels, not metal ages (Ch. 11).

## Applications

Great Basin family level; bands as supra-family integration; lineage → multiclan village; national systems and Puerto Rico subcultures; national-character studies needing level distinctions. Service’s band/tribe/chiefdom applications: foraging type-cases; neolithic tribal integration; ranked redistributive polities ([[hubs/theory/band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Level boundaries blur; "folk" and "national" are historically loaded; risk of smuggling stages back in under operational labels; composite bands may "skip" lineage level (Steward's own caveat) — sequence is not mandatory.
