# Sinking Status

## Definition as Coined

In *[[geertz-negara-1980|Negara]]* (1980), [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] described **sinking status** as the structural principle of Balinese **gentry** descent: within a *dadia* (patrilineal house / descent group), lateral and junior lines “sink” in title and prestige relative to the **core primogeniture line** over generations. Rank correlates with closeness to the living core-line head. Cadet lines “go out” (*jaba*) from the core, which remains *jero* (inside) — the same inside/outside idiom that structures commoner/gentry (*wong jaba* / *wong jero*, Sudra / *triwangsa*). Figure in *Negara*: “Gentry Descent: The Principle of Sinking Status.”

## Semantic History

Geertz presents sinking status as the **institutional counterpart**, in kinship idiom, of the general cultural pattern of the [[exemplary-center|exemplary center]] and diminished replicas. He stresses it is **not** autonomous genealogical process: genealogies were manipulated to rationalize current power and justify prestige claims; “the actual reasons [rank differences] came into existence had rather more to do with the workings of politics than with the logic of kinship.” Completely **absent in commoner dadias**, where genealogies were not kept and sub-dadias not formally ranked.

Distinct from Indian varna/caste as closed hereditary estates: Geertz treats Balinese title systems as graded proximity to exemplary cores, weakly articulated with karma ideology (notes). Related origin myths pair spatial branching of royal power from a unitary center with genealogical sinking-status legends for title groups.

## Operationalizations

| Element | Geertz’s description (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Unit | Gentry *dadia*; nested sub- / sub-sub-dadias (“Chinese boxes”) |
| Core | Living head of primogeniture line; *puri* / *Dalem* as exemplary apex |
| Sink | Cadet *puri*, *jero gde*, plain *jero* — four-tier house-name hierarchy in Tabanan case |
| Marriage | Preferential endogamy / FBD patterns supporting corporate particularism |
| Politics | Who belongs where is contestable → prestige politics, sometimes bloodshed |
| Charisma | Even meanest peripheral house “shared, if dimly,” in the *Dalem*’s force as exemplary center |

## Applications

Organizes internal ruling-class anatomy in the classical [[negara]] (Ch. 2–3 of *Negara*): articulates with **clientship** and **alliance** as the other two pillars of “national” politics. Tabanan 1891–1906 reconstruction: dual kingship, maps of capital and countryside houses, perbekel chains. Complements 1950s [[balinese-tabanan]] economic ethnography (*Peddlers and Princes*), where aristocratic *seka* and firm-type enterprise are the post-conquest afterlife of this hierarchy.

## Critiques and Limitations

Area specialists debate how general the dadia/sinking-status model is across Bali (mountain vs lowland; Bali Aga vs *wong Majapahit* narratives). Geertz’s Tabanan focus and mid-century informant base shape the reconstruction. Not a measurement scale for survey use without re-operationalization.
