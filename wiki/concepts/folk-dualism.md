# Folk Dualism

## Definition as Coined

In CSR (White 2021), **folk dualism** is the ordinary intuition that minds (or personhood) can exist somewhat independently of bodies — a cognitive foundation proposed for afterlife beliefs, ghosts, and spirit possession.

## Semantic History

Related to philosophical mind–body dualism but as a *developmental/intuitive* stance (Paul Bloom and others in White's citations), not Cartesian theory. Contrasts with and also feeds Tylorian [[animism]] as intellectualist origin story — CSR relocates dualism from primitive philosophy to early-emerging intuition.

## Operationalizations

Death and afterlife reasoning tasks with children and adults; cross-cultural person–body reasoning (Cohen et al.; Astuti & Harris Madagascar, per White).

## Applications

Chapter 6 afterlife; possession (Cohen *Mind Possessed*); reincarnation personal identity (White).

## Critiques and Limitations

Not universal in reflective philosophy; cultural death exposure modulates reasoning (Astuti & Harris); "dualism" label may oversimplify graded person–body concepts.
