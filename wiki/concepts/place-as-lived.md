# Place (Lived Place)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-1994|A Phenomenology of Landscape]]* (1994), [[tilley-christopher|Tilley]] treats **place** as humanized, experienced location — contrasting abstract **space** of spatial science. Places gather meaning through body, path, narrative, and landmark relations.

## Semantic History

Philosophical/geographic lineage (phenomenology; humanistic geography) brought into British prehistoric archaeology as alternative to distributional and site-catchment location. Distinct from Giddens [[locale]] (interaction setting) and Castells [[space-of-places]] (network society).

## Operationalizations

Monument/locale relations to outcrops, coasts, ridges; chamber orientations; approach views; intervisibility; path sequences between places.

## Applications

Mesolithic flint-scatter locales and Neolithic monument siting in SW Wales, Black Mountains, Cranborne Chase ([[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-study]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether modern place-experience licenses prehistoric meaning claims; under-determination by sparse Mesolithic evidence.
