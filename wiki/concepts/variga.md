# Variga

> Principal analytic source in this wiki: [[leach-pul-eliya-1961]] / [[leach-pul-eliya-study]] ([[leach-edmund]], fieldwork 1954). Society: [[pul-eliya]].

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Leach 1961.)* Sinhalese *variga* (“variety, kind”) labels endogamous categories. Dictionary “caste” words: *jati* and *variga* ≈ variety/kind; *kula* ≈ colour/*varna*. Villagers distinguish *variga* from *kula* (e.g. same *kula*, different *variga*). Ordinary expectation: marriage and land rights align with variga; but Leach redefines endogamy operationally as a **recognition rule** — a woman’s paramour cannot be husband/father of her children unless recognised as local variga member — not an absolute rule that every man marries within variga.

## Semantic History

- **Vernacular / caste literature:** poorly treated in mid-century Ceylon sociology (Ryan footnote; Pieris); Leach treats Nuvarakalaviya variga as regionally distinctive.
- **Colonial political institution:** not mere Kandyan feudal survival. Served definite political function under British **indirect rule** through the late nineteenth century until **1938**, when D.R.O. functions moved from hereditary Ratemahatmaya to civil-service bureaucrat; sanctions collapsed; Pul Eliya court never met after 1939; younger generation ridiculed it by 1954.
- **Leach analytic:** variga as relatively **corporate landholding group** — court decides membership; aggregate title interest in variga-village lands; land rights and residence, not descent, ultimate basis of status. Translation as “sub-caste” needs caution.

## Operationalizations

| Indicator | Content (Leach 1954 / 1961) |
|---|---|
| Membership knowledge | List of village names (Map A contiguous field) |
| Court (*variga sammutiya* / sabha) | Unofficial; Ratemahatmaya hereditary head; titles Mohottala, Badderala, Lekama |
| Typical cases | *Binna* husband accused as outside-variga; local man accused of incest with classificatory sister — economic disputes reframed as sexual sin / pollution |
| Outcomes | Fine (= admission fee for desirable outsiders) or expulsion of undesirable woman/kin |
| Dogma maintained | Only classificatory cross-cousins (*massina–nana*) may legitimately cohabit (as fiction) |

**Appendix 1 case (Arlis):** Vanni-Vadda variga court admits a known Karava carpenter via staged “Goyigama origin” testimony, reduced fine, feast, and certificate — every householder in the variga shares a token of the fine so all are committed to the verdict.

## Applications

- Marriage field of [[pul-eliya]] (≈50% mates in four daily-contact villages within the variga network).
- Explains how water/land disputes could be fought as endogamy/incest cases while Ratemahatmaya controlled Korala patronage through titled electors.
- Supports Leach’s thesis: **variga membership tested by affinity and property claims, not descent** — anyone acceptable as landowner and brother-in-law is treated as “our variga.”

## Critiques and Limitations

NCP Dry Zone 1954; organisation elsewhere in Sri Lanka may differ. Court records and admission rituals are political-ritual products involving known fictions — not transparent ethnicity tests. After formal collapse, residual endogamy and kinship recognition may persist without the court; Leach holds nothing in kinship structure *per se* keeps the corporate variga in being once the politico-legal context disappears.
