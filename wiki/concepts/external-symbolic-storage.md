# External Symbolic Storage

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — [[donald-merlin|Merlin Donald]], *Origins of the Modern Mind*, 1991; developed archaeologically by [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]] in *[[renfrew-figuring-it-out-2003|Figuring It Out]]* 2003.)* **External symbolic storage (ESS)** names systems outside the individual nervous system that hold, organize, and transmit symbolic content — from notational marks, maps, and standardized weights to full writing, archives, coinage, and modern electronic media. For Donald, ESS is central to **theoretic culture**; for Renfrew it is archaeologically diagnosable **before** literacy in material-symbolic form.

## Semantic History

| Author | Move |
|---|---|
| Donald 1991 | Stage model episodic → mimetic → mythic → theoretic; theoretic culture depends on ESS (writing paradigmatic) |
| Renfrew 2003 | Accepts ESS; inserts **material-symbolic** phase; pre-writing ESS as "analogue computers" (e.g. Stonehenge alignments, power symbols, monuments, valuables); writing (esp. alphabetic) enables cultures reasonably called theoretic |
| Later MET / extended mind | Cognition partly offloaded into things — continuous with ESS without requiring Donald's full stage package |

## Renfrew's archaeological expansion (attributed, 2003)

**Pre-writing / material-symbolic ESS**

- Monuments and landscape markers as repositories of shared memory (e.g. Orkney: Quanterness, Ring of Brodgar).
- Prestige valuables and ranked goods as repositories of value.
- Possible early notation (Marshack's notched bone/antler claims — calendrical ESS; contested in the wider literature).
- Seals and counters; Çatalhöyük stamp seals as possible anticipation of written signs (Renfrew's sketch).
- Artefacts as **foci for shared recollection** (not wholly "outside" minds — they prompt and validate communal memory).

**Writing-era / theoretic ESS**

- Early writing ~3000 BC Mesopotamia and Egypt; ~1500 BC China (as of Renfrew's outline); ideograms → syllabic → alphabetic (Levant, then Greece/Italy) enabling mass literacy in Europe/W. Asia.
- Archives as type-case: **Ebla** palace tablets systematically shelved.
- Organization and domination: writing as state tool; public inscriptions; restricted literacy as power (Homeric **"baneful signs"** / Bellerophon episode — secret lethal message the carrier cannot read).
- Democracy link (ancient Greece): high literacy and public decrees; ostraka as material ballots.
- Maps/diagrams as co-equal ESS mode (possibly more basic than linear text).
- Money, paper currency, electronic media: progressive **dematerialization** of ESS; future archaeology of mind may find digital storage "lost in the ether."

## Operationalizations

Diagnostic caution (aligned with [[cognitive-archaeology]]): form alone does not prove storage function — use-context and assemblage matter. Oral religions and Vedic retention show ESS is not the only long-term cultural memory path.

## Applications

- Donald 1991 → theoretic culture.
- Renfrew 2003 → resolves part of the [[sapient-paradox]] once sedentism densifies material-symbolic ESS; Ch. 6 tracks ESS into the present.
- Textbook cognitive archaeology (measurement, planning devices, depiction) continuous with this apparatus.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of equating any durable mark with "storage" or literacy.
- Stage models are heuristic; multi-origin trajectories (Renfrew himself: transitions 3–4 multiple places/times).
- Oral and embodied memory can carry complex culture without durable external stores.
- Marshack-style notation claims remain contested; do not promote to wiki-voice calendrics without independent evaluation.

## Renfrew *Prehistory* (2007)

*(Attributed — *[[renfrew-prehistory-making-human-mind-2007]]*, 2007.)* Writing is treated as the chief device that opens Donald's **theoretic** stage by externalizing memory, while Renfrew stresses that calendrics, weights, monuments, and money already function as ESS **before or beside** phonetic literacy. Alphabet (Levantine origin, Greek expansion) and Chinese script are parallel paths to widespread theoretic culture; money (first coinage, Lydia/Asia Minor tradition) is a non-literary ESS of value. History is prehistory plus the clarity and organizational power of the written word — not a different human species.
