# Statement Types (Laboratory Life)

Latour and Woolgar's typology, in *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* Ch. 2, of how scientific claims sit between speculation and taken-for-granted fact. Central to their account of **fact construction** and reverse **dismantling**.

## Definition as Coined

The fictional observer posits a five-fold scheme. In their initial continuum framing, **higher type numbers are more fact-like**:

| Type | Character (as L&W develop it) |
|---|---|
| Type 5 | Taken-for-granted facts; often unstated among insiders; tacit background (newcomers force them into speech) |
| Type 4 | Explicit textbook-style assertions ("A has a certain relationship with B") without heavy modalities |
| Type 3 | Research-literature statements still carrying modalities and/or attributions |
| Type 2 | More heavily qualified claims |
| Type 1 | Conjectures or speculations (paper endings, private discussion) |

**Modalities** (qualifications, citations-as-doubt, author-attachment) act like "price tags" on statements. Deleting modalities can enhance facticity; adding them can demote a fact toward conjecture or artefact. Conversational examples in the book show rapid type shifts (e.g. an unstated Type 5 assumption reopened as Type 2 by "we cannot be sure").

## Semantic History

1979 laboratory-ethnography formulation. Feeds later Latour on trials of strength and [[black-box|black-boxing]]. Related to linguistic modality and to SSK interest in knowledge stabilization — not identical to either.

## Operationalizations

Code papers, drafts, talk, and referee reports for modalities and certainty markers; track a claim (e.g. TRF structure) across years and statement types; treat citation and document-loading as weight.

## Applications

TRF(H) case ([[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-study]]); conversational microprocessing of facts (Ch. 4); textbook vs. research-frontier prose.

## Critiques and Limitations

Heuristic scale; fuzzy boundaries; risk of reifying a ladder as the only path to facticity. Authors themselves note empirical difficulties verifying a clean continuum.
