# Culturalism

[[appadurai-arjun|Appadurai]]'s term (*[[appadurai-modernity-at-large-1996|Modernity
at Large]]*) for the **conscious mobilization of cultural difference** in the
service of identity politics, as distinct from culture or ethnicity as tacit,
lived givens. Attributed to Appadurai.

## Definition as Coined

Culturalism names "ethnicities… mobilized by or in relation to the practices of the
modern nation-state" — it "is the conscious mobilization of cultural differences in
the service of a larger national or transnational politics" *(Appadurai)*. Where
"culture" and "ethnicity" imply the natural, unconscious, and tacit, culturalism
makes cultural difference "a conscious object," worked through classification, mass
mediation, mobilization, and entitlement.

## Semantic History

Grounded in Appadurai's distinction of **"culture" as adjective, not noun**: "the
most valuable feature of the concept of culture is the concept of difference, a
contrastive rather than a substantive property." He distinguishes **Culture 1** (the
open-ended archive of differences) from **Culture 2** (the subset "mobilized to
articulate the boundary of difference," i.e. group identity). Culturalism is the
politics of Culture 2. Positioned against [[weber-max|Weber]]'s primordialist
extension of kinship, and adjacent to Barthian
[[ethnic-boundary-theory|boundary theory]].

## Operationalizations

Identified in movements from African-Americans, Dalits, and native Hawaiians to
Sikh, Basque, and Québécois nationalisms; often transnational, linked to
"diasporic public spheres." Culturalist movements can be self-expressive and
autonomy-seeking or negative and eliminationist; primordial sentiment is frequently
**"deliberately fostered by various nation-states… to construct homogeneous
subjects of the state."**

## Applications

The analytic core of "Life after Primordialism" (ch. 7): modern ethnic mobilization
is a form of the [[imagination-as-social-practice|work of the imagination]], not the
eruption of ancient attachments — see the
[[primordialism-constructivism-ethnicity-debate|primordialism debate]]. Connects to
the crisis of the nation-state (ch. 8).

## Critiques and Limitations

The term overlaps with "identity politics" and "ethnonationalism"; critics ask
whether the culture/culturalism line can be drawn cleanly, given that even "tacit"
ethnicity is always to some degree mobilized and represented.
