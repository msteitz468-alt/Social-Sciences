# Postclassic Stage

Fifth New World historical-developmental stage in [[willey-phillips-method-theory-1958|Willey & Phillips]] (1958), primarily illustrated from Middle America and Peru; endpoint = European conquest.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Willey & Phillips 1958.)*

Marked by:

1. **Breakdown of old Classic regional styles** — wide interregional transfer of total art and architectural styles (vs Classic trade in objects/isolated ideas); many transfers reasonably read as movements of large groups, often with military force.
2. **Continuing or increased urbanism** — best known native American cities (archaeology + ethnohistory); politico-religious nuclei; craft and produce specialists; Aztec independent artisans/merchants vs Inca state employees.
3. **Militarism and secularism (trends, not absolutes)** — more fortifications and fortified communities; decrease in number/size/elaboration of pyramid-mounds as clue to waning religious authority; aesthetic decline, standardization, mass production in some regions. Warfare and non-religious authority existed earlier; Postclassic is **ascendancy** of these trends.

Peruvian Postclassic (their span): roughly A.D. 1000–1532 (Tiahuanaco horizon and later; Chimu and other kingdoms; Inca expansion). Middle American exemplars include Tula, Mayapan, Aztec, etc.

## Semantic History

Standard Mesoamerican period term; W&P extend the developmental reading (militarism/secularism/urbanism) as hemisphere-relevant for nuclear civilizations. Later research complicated “decline” narratives, ethnic migration readings of style transfer, and absolute dates.

## Operationalizations

| Trend | Archaeological signals (W&P) |
|---|---|
| Style leveling | Interregional total-style transfers |
| Militarism | Fortifications, fortified towns |
| Secularization | Fewer/less elaborate religious monuments; mass-produced art |
| Urban form | Planned city layouts (esp. Peru Postclassic vs haphazard Classic accretion) |

## Applications

- Terminal prehispanic nuclear American sequences.
- Comparative notes on empire formation (Inca, Aztec) as Postclassic political scale.

## Critiques and Limitations

“Aesthetic decline” and secularization are value-laden readings. Migration+military mechanisms for style transfer are hypotheses. Scheme ends with conquest — colonial archaeology is outside their stage series. As with Classic, limited applicability outside nuclear America.
