# Younger Dryas

## Definition as Coined

The **Younger Dryas** is a late-glacial climatic stadial — an abrupt return toward colder/drier conditions in the North Atlantic realm and many mid-latitude regions — interrupting the Bølling–Allerød warming before the Holocene. In Levantine archaeology it is routinely synchronized with mid-to-late [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] occupation and invoked (contestedly) as a driver of settlement contraction, subsistence change, or the path to cultivation.

## Semantic History

Named in European pollen sequences for the alpine plant *Dryas octopetala*; generalized via ice-core and other proxies. Near Eastern archaeology imported the event as a causal candidate for Natufian–Neolithic transitions from the 1980s onward (Henry; Bar-Yosef tradition). By 2011–2013, reviews (Blockley & Pinhasi; Maher, Banning & Chazan — cited in Bar-Yosef & Valla’s 2013 preface) stressed that coarse climate scales poorly test economic causation and that studies can contradict.

## Operationalizations

- Global/regional climate proxies (ice cores, speleothem δ18O, lake levels).
- Site-level paleoenvironment (e.g., Napierala on Baaz Rockshelter fish/YD influence; Ashkenazi landscape reconstruction at Eynan).
- Synchronism with archaeological phases via [[radiocarbon-dating]] — precision limits are a standing problem (Grosman chronological scheme).

## Applications

- **Henry (2013):** evaluates YD role in Natufian adaptations ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).
- **Bar-Yosef & Valla preface:** call for habitat-by-habitat response tests rather than gross climatic forcing.
- **Agricultural origins debates:** YD stress as push toward cultivation vs. continuity models that downplay climate.

## Critiques and Limitations

Correlation ≠ causation; Levantine mosaic habitats may respond differently; diagenetic loss of plant remains and sparse dates weaken tests (Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013). Evidence-class rule: environmental proxies license `concurrent_with` until mechanisms are stated.

### Levantine multiproxy detail (Henry 2013, attributed)

- Global BA from ~14.7 ka cal BP; Greenland YD ~12.9–11.6 ka; **Levantine YD ~13–11 ka cal BP**, longer (~2000–2200 yr) and more gradual termination (~500 yr) than ice-core YD (Henry).
- Soreq Cave: progressive colder/drier peaking 13.2–11.4 ky then Early Holocene warm-moist (Bar-Matthews et al. via Henry).
- Henry multiproxy: BA peak ~1°C below modern, precipitation ~21–196 mm above modern; YD temps ~3.5–4.5°C below modern, precipitation only 0–50 mm above modern — impact framed as **cold more than drought**.
- Counter-cases: Napierala brown trout at Baaz; Ashkenazi Eynan habitats; Conard Late>Early Anti-Lebanon intensity under YD interval.

## After the Ice densification (Mithen 2003)

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven]], *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice]]* 2003; calendar BC as Mithen’s narrative.)*

- **Ice-core sequence:** gradual irregular warming 20,000→~12,700 BC; warm late-glacial interstadial (Bølling/Allerød peaks) to ~10,800 BC; cold/dry **Younger Dryas**; second sharp rise at **9600 BC** = Pleistocene–Holocene transition (Greenland δ¹⁸O; attributed, ch. 2).
- **European vegetation:** Allerød birch–poplar–pine to northern Germany/Britain/S Scandinavia by ~11,500 BC; YD from ~10,800 BC restores open tundra with *Dryas octopetala*; tree pollen returns abruptly ~9600 BC (attributed, ch. 13).
- **British beetle palaeotemperatures:** Bølling winters ~0–1°C / summers ~17°C; YD winters to ~−17°C at 10,500 BC matching ice-core cold (attributed).
- **Levantine social effect:** Early Natufian villages collapse by ~10,800 BC — Mallaha ramshackle/abandoned, Hayonim and Abu Hureyra deserted; first sedentism experiment fails; food shortage from overharvest of wild gardens **plus** climate (attributed).
- **Farming after YD end:** Jericho founded ~9600 BC when summer droughts end and Jordan-valley floods renew ‘Ain es-Sultan (attributed).
- **Not sole domestication driver:** Mithen’s Göbekli/Karacadağ reading — wheat domestication may be by-product of ceremonial ideology rather than people “struggling against the harsh conditions of the Younger Dryas” alone (theoretical position).
- **Americas:** YD after ~11,000 BC disrupts North American communities; Lake Agassiz eastward drainage switch ~11,000 BC hypothesized as possible YD cause via North Atlantic circulation (attributed, ch. 26); midwest prairie transition droughts exacerbated by YD.
- **Historiography:** Victorian Lubbock (1865) already listed solar radiation, earth’s axis, ocean currents among climate causes — but lacked multiple glaciations and the YD (attributed).

Environmental proxy → `concurrent_with` until mechanism stated. Source: [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003]].

## Meltzer 2009 densification (Americas / late-glacial climate)

*(Attributed — [[meltzer-david]], *[[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|First Peoples in a New World]]* 2009. Ages radiocarbon BP unless noted; YD as he frames ~11,000–10,000 BP.)*

### Mechanism (Broecker-style thermohaline)

Meltzer follows Wallace Broecker on Atlantic meridional overturning: warm salty Gulf Stream north; near Iceland/Greenland surface water cools, sinks, and drives deep southbound flow equal to ~100 Amazon Rivers. Large freshwater injection can allow winter freezing, prevent sinking, and collapse thermohaline circulation. Named for tundra flower *Dryas octopetala*; Greenland ice cores average annual air temperature during YD about **−52°F**. Switching conveyor scrambles atmospheric:ocean ¹⁴C ratios—radiocarbon clock can run too slow or too fast—complicating dating of YD-age first-American sites.

**Lake Agassiz hypothesis:** when Agassiz drained south via Mississippi, climate was unaffected; by **11,000 BP** Laurentide retreat opened an eastern outlet, suddenly routing ~**9,500 km³** of very cold fresh water into the North Atlantic via St. Lawrence, shutting down thermohaline circulation for ~the next thousand years. Meltzer notes **considerable evidence** that thermohaline shutdown caused the YD, but **controversy over the cause of the shutdown**: expected geological evidence of Agassiz eastward drainage (outlet channel or flood debris dated 11,000 BP) has **not been found**—opening space for alternatives, most controversially an extraterrestrial impact.

**YD not an LGM replay:** reduced ice climatic influence; CO₂ near interglacial (~265 vs ~190 ppm LGM); extreme seasonality; Greenland temperatures fell ~**27°F** in decades (vs ~41°F LGM plunge). Across North America, YD climates were a **mosaic** (northernmost U.S. cooler/locally drier; SE relatively warmer/wetter; midlatitude interior relatively warm/temperate)—“no modern equivalent in North America.” Vegetation lagged and reversed rapidly in places (e.g. pine ~300 km west into southern Great Lakes in little more than a century). YD end: by 10,000 BP ice lobe reblocks Agassiz eastern outlet; thermohaline re-establishes; Greenland +**15°F** in a decade. Broecker via Meltzer: if freshwater-triggered, YD was a **“freak event”** unique in glacial history—possible contributor to why megafauna that survived prior cycles succumbed this time (climate-side thesis; not exclusive of human role—see [[megafaunal-extinctions]], [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]]).

**Radiocarbon plateaus:** 900 radiocarbon years from 10,900–10,000 BP ≈ **1,360 calendar years** with then-current calibrations—Late Paleoindian “settling in” slower than raw ¹⁴C suggests (Meltzer Ch. 9).

### Firestone impact hypothesis — Meltzer’s critique (as of 2009)

| Claim package (Firestone et al. positions) | Meltzer’s assessment (thesis/findings) |
|---|---|
| Firestone & Topping 2001 supernova over Great Lakes ~12,500 ya; megafauna kill; ¹⁴C ages off by up to 40,000 yr | Radiocarbon experts: “highly problematical” / “difficult to take seriously”; 40 ka dating error unnoticed since 1950 implausible |
| *Cycle of Cosmic Catastrophes* (popular) multi-comet version | “Long on anecdote, short on evidence, weak on fact”; cold scientific / warm apocalyptic-reader reception |
| 2007 AGU + Kennett: comet ~11,000 ya as YD trigger; NSF press release before peer evaluation | Extraordinary (esp. ET) claims need extraordinary proof; firewall between “was there impact?” and “what consequences?”; press-first science inverts procedure |
| Black Mat at Clovis sites: iridium, nanodiamonds, ET helium fullerenes, soot | Specialists: scenario “contrived” (Koeberl); Gabrielli says cited Greenland iridium showed no such thing; algae can concentrate iridium (Kring); claimed elements require incompatible body types—“Frankenstein” meteorite (Pintar & Ishman) |
| Crater via ice cushion / Hudson Bay / Carolina Bays | No crater identified; Hudson Bay still ice-buried; no meteoritic material from Carolina Bays; bays not all same age or timed to supposed impact |
| Continent-scale wildfires | Thermal radiation zero below horizon (undercuts Europe-wide fire from NA blast); Upper Midwest lake cores near proposed ground zero lack noticed soot/charcoal spikes |
| Solves YD + extinctions + post-Clovis population collapse | No evidence of immediate post-Clovis collapse—on well-dated plains, Folsom follows Clovis with seeming boom (see [[folsom-site]]); extinctions not shown simultaneous/coincident with impact; global conflagration should have killed far more life; invisible impact melting ice sheets is faith, not science (as of Meltzer 2009) |

Meltzer rejects simplistic causal claims that YD onset caused extinctions (“overchill”), Clovis fragmentation, or forced Florida Paleoindians into furs: severe plunge was high-latitude; vegetation lagged; mobile Paleoindians routinely adapted—“the notion that the Younger Dryas somehow caught Paleoindians flat-footed seems overly dramatic if not simplistic” (Eren: environments moved to people more than people to environments).

## From Roberts, *The Holocene* (1998)

*(Attributed — [[roberts-holocene-1998]].)*

Roberts synthesizes the Younger Dryas as a late-glacial cold reversal (13,000–11,500 Cal. yr BP; the **Loch Lomond stadial** in Britain, following the **Windermere interstadial**). [[coleopteran-analysis|Beetle]] data (Coope) show summer temperature rose ~1°C/decade at the interstadial's onset then cooled into the YD; Scotland regrew an ice cap with permafrost features. Richard Fairbanks' (1989) dated Barbados reefs recorded a slow-down in [[sea-level-change|sea-level rise]] during the YD, and Greenland ice cores register a **7°C warming in ~50 years** at its end (Mayewski et al. 1996). The favoured mechanism (Broecker et al. 1990) is a shut-down of the North Atlantic thermohaline "conveyor," possibly triggered by diversion of Laurentide meltwater to the North Atlantic — the event "bucks the [[milankovitch-cycles|Milankovitch]] trend." Its human/agricultural consequences are treated separately (see [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]], [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]]).

## Cauvin densification (2000 Postscript)

*(Attributed — *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]]* Postscript, as of ~1998.)* At Abu Hureyra latitude, Younger Dryas desiccation (wild cereals disappearing) became significant only around **10,600 BC**, not the then-standard **11,000 BC**. Impact moderate (almond and terebinth persist reduced). Cauvin’s **position:** this phase did **not** play an important causal role in triggering the Neolithic Revolution; it drove populations out of territories becoming desert (e.g. El Kowm, Sinai).
