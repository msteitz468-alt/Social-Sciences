# Impression Management

## Definition as Coined

In *[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life]]* (1956), [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] treated **impression management** as the set of practices by which individuals and **[[performance-team|teams]]** guide and control the impressions others form of them — sustaining a projected **[[definition-of-the-situation|definition of the situation]]** before an audience. The Preface states the report's concern: how the individual "presents himself and his activity to others, the ways in which he guides and controls the impression they form of him, and the kinds of things he may and may not do while sustaining his performance before them" (attributed).

Goffman distinguished:

- **Defensive practices** — strategies the individual/team uses to protect **own** projections.
- **Protective practices** (tact) — strategies used to save **another's** projected definition.

Together they safeguard the impression fostered before others. Few impressions would survive, Goffman argued, if receivers did not exert tact in reception.

Chapter VI organizes the **arts** of impression management around disruptions ("incidents") and three classes of remedy: (1) defensive measures by performers; (2) protective measures by audience and outsiders; (3) measures performers take so that others *can* employ protective measures ("tact regarding tact").

### Defensive attributes (performers)

| Attribute | Goffman's sense (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Dramaturgical loyalty** | Do not betray team secrets; do not exploit the front region for a private show; perform enthusiastically; be taken in enough to avoid sounding hollow; resist over-attachment to the audience that costs the team |
| **Dramaturgical discipline** | Appear immersed while remaining affectively dissociated enough to handle contingencies; manage face and voice; presence of mind; self-control; stick to the team's affective line |
| **Dramaturgical circumspection** | Foresight and prudence: choose members and audiences; limit team/audience size; adapt to information conditions and props; prepare agendas, protocol, rehearsals, briefings |

### Protective practices (audience)

Voluntary non-entry into uninvited regions; knocks/coughs as warnings; civil inattention; etiquette that avoids scenes; "not seeing" slips; collusion at crises; extra consideration for beginners; nested awareness that can produce a "communion of glances" when the dramaturgical structure is briefly laid bare.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Goffman 1956/59 | Core of dramaturgical handbook; establishment-level techniques |
| *Stigma* 1963 | Identity-threat management (passing, covering) as specialized impression work — [[passing]], [[covering]], [[social-information]] |
| Organizational sociology | Service work, professional front, corporate image management |
| Social psychology (later) | Explicit self-presentation scales and lab paradigms — often thinner than Goffman's team/region apparatus |
| Digital culture | Profiles, curated feeds — analogy-heavy; not Goffman's indoor co-presence model without re-argument |

## Operationalizations

Goffman: formal description of techniques and contingencies in natural establishments. Later research: observational coding of service encounters; survey self-presentation scales; experimental self-presentation; online behavioral traces. Operationalizations that drop team, region, and tact systematically under-specify the 1956 concept.

## Applications

- Any social establishment studied as impression system (hotel, hospital ward, classroom, shop, court).
- Service and care work; professional etiquette and "common front" before clients (Hughes, cited by Goffman).
- Continuity with stigma management (*[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]*) and institutional self-work (*[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]*).
- Study page: [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]]; theory: [[dramaturgical-analysis]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Read as recommending cynicism or reducing morality to stagecraft — Goffman's "merchants of morality" dialectic is analytical, not a how-to guide.
- Under-specifies structural power and inequality as causes (though status fronts and service hierarchies fill the illustrations).
- Co-presence model travels poorly to purely mediated interaction without explicit bridging.
