# Timeless Time

Castells’s concept for the **dominant temporality of the [[network-society|network society]]**: time compressed, desequenced, or made simultaneous in dominant functions, coexisting with biological and clock time of everyday life. Canonical: *[[castells-rise-of-network-society-1996|The Rise of the Network Society]]* Ch. 7; restated 2010 preface.

## Definition as Coined

Castells (attributed): timeless time is “the kind of time that occurs when in a given context, such as the network society, there is systemic perturbation in the sequential order of the social practices performed in this context.” It is not the only form of time, but **the time of power** in the network society. Operationally: compression of occurrence of phenomena toward instantaneity *or* introduction of random discontinuity in sequence — elimination of sequencing creates undifferentiated time that is, in Castells’s phrasing, eternity (mixing of tenses in a forever universe). Dialogue with Leibnizian relational time and with Harvey on time–space compression (qualified).

**Domains (attributed illustrations):** global financial markets (“global casino”); flex-time and the [[network-enterprise]]; diversification/shortening of working time; life-cycle “social arrhythmia”; denial of death in medical/cultural practice; “Instant Wars” with smart weapons; virtual time under [[real-virtuality]].

**Counter-time:** “glacial time” (from Lash and Urry) — slow planetary/intergenerational environmental time — confronts timeless time as a fundamental struggle of the network society.

## Semantic History

Industrial clock time (Taylorism; time = money under capitalism) as predecessor dominant form. Relation to Innis’s space–time regimes noted by Castells without claiming lineage. 2010 preface links timeless time to perpetual multi-tasking via wireless and to failed Instant Wars (Iraq/Afghanistan) that nonetheless retain the project.

## Operationalizations

Finance turnover; media simultaneity; contingent work schedules; career unpredictability; medical control of biological clocks; warfare tempo.

## Applications

[[network-society-theory]]; work and media chapters; environmental-movement time politics.

## Critiques and Limitations

Metaphor risk (is desequenced time “timeless”?); elite bias of who lives timeless time vs who remains on the assembly line (Castells acknowledges coexistence); measurement independent of theory remains thin.
