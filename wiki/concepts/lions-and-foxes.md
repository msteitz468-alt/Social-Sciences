# Lions and foxes (Pareto)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* Ch XII–XIII, [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] analysed ruling classes by their use of **force** versus **cunning** and by the balance of **[[combination-instinct|Class I]]** and **[[group-persistence|Class II]]** residues. The tradition labels these poles **lions** (force, courage, Class II-heavy) and **foxes** (cunning, combination, Class I-heavy) — Machiavellian vocabulary aligned with Pareto’s types (explicit fox imagery e.g. §2316 n.; lions→foxes succession under fraud/diplomacy in force chapters).

**Government types (§§2274–2276):**

| Type | Means | Governing-class residues | Circulation | Cost / production |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Physical force + religious/similar sentiments | Class II > Class I | Slow | Cheap; low economic stimulus; crystallization ideal |
| II-a | Intelligence/cunning on **sentiments** | Class I > Class II | Slow | Inexpensive; stupefying; prey to force |
| II-b | Intelligence/cunning on **interests** | Class I > Class II | Rapid (max under “speculators”) | Expensive; often highly productive |

## Semantic History

Built on residue Classes I–II; applied historically to Rome, Athens, Sparta, Macedonia, medieval republics, modern Europe. English reception popularized “lions and foxes” as elite-theory shorthand, sometimes detached from the full type table.

## Operationalizations

- Authority passes from lions to foxes as fraud/diplomacy replace force; reverse when force-capable Class II elements “shake off the yoke” of combination-experts (§§2319, 2549).
- **Maximum utility of command:** Class I in leaders + Class II in subordinates (obedience as religion) (§§2427–2455).
- Neither pure S nor R types are force-adept; a third force-using group can rob both (§2313).
- Force contingency: where shrewdness rules, Class I pays; where force decides, Class II pays (§§2526–2528).

## Applications

- Core mechanism language of [[circulation-of-elites]].
- Modern II-b = [[demagogic-plutocracy]] / government by [[speculators-and-rentiers|speculators]].
- Historical ILLUSTRATIONS: Caesar/Augustus after late-Republic S triumph; Reformation as Class II force vs Italian Class I cunning; Athens vs Macedon (attributed patterns, not wiki-voice history).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Dualism oversimplifies mixed regimes (Pareto himself stresses mixtures §2277).
- Zoomorphic labels risk cartoon reduction of residue theory.
- Political afterlives (including authoritarian readings) are reception, not treatise verdicts.
