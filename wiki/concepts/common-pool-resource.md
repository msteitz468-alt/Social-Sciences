# Common-Pool Resource (CPR)

*(Attributed to [[ostrom-elinor|Ostrom]], *[[ostrom-governing-the-commons-1990|Governing the Commons]]* 1990.)* A **CPR** combines **difficulty of excluding** beneficiaries with **subtractability** of resource units — unlike pure [[public-good|public goods]] (non-subtractable) or private goods (easy exclusion).

## Definition as Coined

Ostrom distinguishes the **resource system** (fishery, forest, irrigation system, groundwater basin) from **resource units** (fish, timber, water) appropriated from it. Appropriators face collective dilemmas of provision (maintaining the system) and appropriation (allocation of units).

## Semantic History

Resource economics and Hardin’s commons metaphor; Ostrom systematizes CPRs as a class of institutional problems distinct from pure public goods ([[collective-action-theory|Olson]]).

## Operationalizations

Small-scale CPRs in one country, ~50–15,000 persons heavily dependent on the resource (Ostrom 1990 sampling frame).

## Applications

Inshore fisheries, irrigation, alpine meadows/forests, groundwater — long-enduring and failed cases in *Governing the Commons*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Global commons and large-N systems outside the 1990 frame; construct boundaries debated relative to common property vs open access.
