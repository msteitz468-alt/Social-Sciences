# Disembedding

## Definition as Coined

In *[[giddens-consequences-of-modernity-1990|The Consequences of Modernity]]* (1990), [[giddens-anthony|Anthony Giddens]] defined **disembedding** as "the 'lifting out' of social relations from local contexts of interaction and their restructuring across indefinite spans of time-space." He treated it as one of three sources of modernity’s dynamism (with the separation/recombination of time and space, and the [[reflexive-modernization|reflexivity of modernity]]).

Giddens distinguished two types of **disembedding mechanisms**:
1. **[[symbolic-tokens]]** — media of interchange passable without regard to the specific characteristics of the individuals who handle them (money as the paradigmatic case).
2. **[[expert-systems]]** — systems of technical accomplishment or professional expertise that organise large areas of material and social environments.

Together, symbolic tokens and expert systems constitute **[[abstract-systems]]**.

## Semantic History

Giddens positioned disembedding against classical sociological talk of "differentiation" or "functional specialisation," which he argued understates how modernity *restructures* relations across time-space rather than merely diversifying units within a bounded system. The concept extends his earlier [[time-space-distanciation]] apparatus from *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]* (1984). Related later vocabularies (e.g. "disembedding" in market sociology, Polanyi-adjacent uses of embedding/disembedding) should not be silently equated with Giddens’s 1990 definition.

**Counterpart:** [[reembedding]] — the re-anchoring of disembedded relations in local contexts of action (which may support or undermine abstract systems).

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct in Giddens 1990. Indexed theoretically via money/markets, professional/technical systems, and the trust relations that sustain them at a distance.

## Applications

- Explains how modern institutions span the globe while reshaping intimate life.
- Grounds Giddens’s claim that **modernity is inherently globalising**.
- Frames [[trust]] in abstract capacities (systems) as the condition of extended time-space distanciation.
- Links to [[access-points]] where lay actors meet system representatives ([[faceless-commitments]] meet facework).

## Critiques and Limitations

Disembedding is a programmatic theoretical claim, not an empirical finding. Critics of Giddens’s modernity program often charge abstractness and thin ethnographic specification of how "lifting out" actually works in particular locales; those critiques should be filed as **positions** when secondary literature is ingested. Do not treat disembedding as synonymous with market commodification alone — Giddens’s second mechanism (expert systems) is co-equal.
