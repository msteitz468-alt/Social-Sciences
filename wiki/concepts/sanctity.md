# Sanctity (Rappaport)

## Definition as Coined

[[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]] (*[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*, 1979; full restatement in *[[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999|Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity]]*, 1999, Chs. 9–10) uses **sanctity** in a restricted sense: *the quality of unquestionableness imputed by congregations to postulates that are objectively unverifiable and absolutely unfalsifiable*. He distinguishes:

| Term | Role (attributed to Rappaport) |
|---|---|
| **Sacred** | Discursive aspect of religious phenomena; quality of discourse rather than of objects discourse is "about" |
| **Sanctity** | That part of the holy expressible in language; faces conscious reason |
| **Numinous** | Non-discursive / emotional aspect (Otto-lineage) |
| **Holy** | Inclusive category: sacred + numinous |

Unquestionableness of [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]] follows, he argues, from **invariance** of the canons that express them in [[liturgical-order|liturgical orders]]. To sanctify is to **certify**: sanctified truth is a third member of a set with logical necessity and empirical truth ([[bateson-gregory|Bateson]] on validity as a function of acceptance).

## Semantic History

Built on *Pigs*-era claims that spirit sentences organize action, formalized in 1970s ritual essays (EMR), then **systematically restated** in RRMH 1999. Ch. 9 grounds unquestionableness in liturgical **acceptance** and **certainty** via canonical invariance; Ch. 10 distinguishes the **sacred** (USPs only) from the **sanctified** (contingent, graded expressions). Evolutionary load: language enables lies and **alternative** (Babel); sanctity is partial adaptive stabilizer. Family of falsehoods expands (ordinary, Vedic, oppression, diabolical, idolatrous, Gnostic). Contrasts sanctity with [[deutero-truth]]. **Oversanctification** / Tillich idolatry = hierarchical maladaptation ([[adaptive-structure]]; RRMH Ch. 14).

## Operationalizations

- Locate USPs vs sanctified-but-not-sacred regulatory sentences; comparative invariance (punctiliousness, ubiquity, perdurance).
- Trace ritual association as vehicle flowing sanctity to ethics, authority, ecological rules.
- Map the [[cybernetics-of-the-holy]] loop: sanctify → regulate → well-being → re-accept or desecrate.
- Cybernetic desanctification / **unholiness**: sacred and numinous detached from corrective function.

## Applications

- Hierarchical organization of [[cognized-model|cognized models]] and [[liturgical-order|liturgical orders]] by sanctity, mutability, specificity.
- Maring pledges and ancestor postulates as sanctified commissives.
- Binding of [[logos-rappaport|Logoi]]; modern idolatry and power-pollution of sanctity (RRMH Ch. 14 POSITION).

## Critiques and Limitations

Restricted definition excludes many folk/theological uses of "sacred." Critical theory may read certification as ideology-stabilization. RRMH's normative ecological-Logos close is contested. See [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
