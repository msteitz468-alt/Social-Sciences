# Policy-Planning Network

Domhoff’s term for the organizational network through which the corporate rich formulate, legitimize, and transmit policy frameworks into government (*[[domhoff-who-rules-america-2022|Who Rules America?]]*, esp. Ch. 4–5, 7).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Domhoff.)* The **policy-planning network** includes major foundations, think tanks, policy-discussion organizations, and allied university/nonprofit institutes that are funded and overseen disproportionately by corporate/upper-class sources. It is distinct from day-to-day lobbying (special-interest process) and from electoral campaigning, though linked to both.

## Semantic History

Core of Domhoff’s elaboration of how a power elite shapes *what is on the agenda* beyond campaign donations alone. Related to but not identical with Mills’s “higher circles” of celebrity and advisors.

## Operationalizations

Foundation grants; board composition of think tanks; membership in groups such as (historically) CED, Business Roundtable, CFR-type discussion organizations — edition-specific lists.

## Applications

Explains moderate-conservative policy shifts, opinion-shaping (Ch. 5), and government staffing pipelines (Ch. 7).

## Critiques and Limitations

“Network” boundary can expand or contract with the argument; progressive foundations and counter-networks may be underweighted; causality from plan → statute is contested by pluralists.
