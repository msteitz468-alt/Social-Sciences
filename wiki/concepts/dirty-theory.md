# Dirty Theory

Methodological ideal named by [[connell-raewyn|Raewyn Connell]] in *[[connell-southern-theory-2007|Southern Theory]]* (especially ch. 9–10): theory that stays **grounded** in place, history, and empirical corrigibility — as against pure general theory that pretends to speak from a cleared, featureless site (*terra nullius* of theory).

## Definition as Coined

Connell contrasts metropolitan general theory's "cleared building site" / "featureless dance floor" (her metaphors for Coleman-style abstraction) with theory that accepts the "dirt" of particular landscapes, dispossession histories, and multi-centred evidence. Dirty theory is not anti-generalisation: it seeks generalisation that remains open to correction from research — including research from the periphery — and that refuses Kuhnian "normal science" as routine puzzle-solving under a protected metropolitan paradigm.

## Semantic History

- **Connell 2007** — coined in the Antipodean land discussion and world-scale chapter; plays against "pure theory" and against closed ethnophilosophy
- Echoes (not identity) with [[grounded-theory|grounded theory]] as empirical discipline — Connell's target is geopolitics of theory, not only coding procedures
- Related metaphors: *terra nullius* of theory; ethnosociology of the metropole dressed as universal science

## Operationalizations

Stance, not index: (1) name the metropole and empire when they structure the problem; (2) treat land/place as part of social order where the case requires it; (3) keep theory corrigible by peripheral research; (4) build multi-centred networks rather than monologue or sealed mosaic.

## Applications

- Critique of Coleman / Giddens / Bourdieu general theory in *Southern Theory* ch. 2
- Yolngu land / Yirrkala as case where land is *part of* social structure
- Program for social science on a world scale (ch. 10)

## Critiques and Limitations

"Dirty" can be misread as anti-theoretical localism; Connell explicitly wants world-scale dialogue and unification *without* metropolitan monologue. The metaphor's force is geopolitical and epistemological, not a rejection of abstraction as such.
