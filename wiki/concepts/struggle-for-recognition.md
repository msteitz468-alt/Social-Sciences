# Struggle for Recognition

Honneth’s core concept (full monograph not yet primary-ingested); applied and defended in *[[fraser-honneth-redistribution-or-recognition-2003|Redistribution or Recognition?]]* (2003).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Honneth, as used in the 2003 exchange.)* Social conflicts are rooted in experiences of **disrespect** — withheld recognition. A moral grammar of recognition (intimate care/love, legal respect, social esteem/achievement) structures claims. Under capitalism, struggles over distribution are interpreted as conflicts within a **recognition order**, not as a separate sphere wholly outside recognition.

## Semantic History

Hegelian lineage via Critical Theory; *Struggle for Recognition* (1992/95) is the book-length statement. The Fraser exchange is the main primary filing of Honneth’s view in this wiki until that monograph is ingested.

## Operationalizations

Read social movements and class conflicts for recognition claims (legal inclusion, esteem, care); map achievement principle and legal equality as capitalist recognition norms.

## Applications

Critical Theory renewal; critique of pure resource-distributive justice; dialogue/conflict with Fraser dualism.

## Critiques and Limitations

Fraser: risks reducing political sociology to moral psychology and capitalism to culture/recognition alone.
