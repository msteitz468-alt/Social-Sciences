# Cycles of Credit

Latour and Woolgar's political-economy model of scientific motivation and strategy in *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* Ch. 5: scientists seek **credibility**, not merely reward-credit in the Mertonian sense, and convert one form of credibility into another in ongoing cycles.

## Definition as Coined

They distinguish **credit as reward** (recognition, prizes, citations as ends) from **credibility** as a convertible resource enabling further fact-production. Data, arguments, publications, reputation, money, students, and equipment circulate; success is ability to sustain conversion so that credible information can be demanded and supplied. Career trajectories, group structure, and technician labor are analyzed within this economy.

## Semantic History

1979 alternative to pure reward-system sociology of science; anticipates later capital metaphors in STS and resonates (without identity) with Bourdieuian field capital — not equated here without primary source bridge.

## Operationalizations

CV and position analysis; tracking grant → equipment → data → paper → recognition → grant; group hierarchy and technician contribution.

## Applications

Salk laboratory careers; strategies in TRF race (Guillemin vs. Schally and others, as narrated).

## Critiques and Limitations

May underplay curiosity, ideology, or institutional constraint outside credit conversion; depends on interview/CV reconstruction; "credibility" can become an all-explaining solvent.
