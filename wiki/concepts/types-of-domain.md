# Types of Domain

## Definition as Coined

In *[[wolf-peasants-1966|Peasants]]* (1966, Ch. 2), [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] identifies three traditional types of **domain** affecting peasantry:

1. **Patrimonial domain** — control of land occupants by lords who inherit the right as members of kinship groups/lineages; tribute for occupance; may be pyramided (higher lords over lower over peasants). Wolf avoids the loaded label "feudal." Peasant at base of pyramid sustains it with surplus as labor, kind, or money. Often heavily ceremonialized personal lord–peasant reciprocity (Homans on English villagers; *Piers Plowman* compact image).

2. **Prebendary domain** — not heritable lineage domain but grants of income (**prebends**, Weber) to officials as servants of a centralized state. Two land/income variants: (a) rights to draw tribute as office; (b) tax farming or salary from centralized revenue. Characteristic of Sassanid Persia, Ottoman, Mughal India, traditional China. Greater centralization than patrimonial domain; officials still skim (Weber estimate cited: even best case ≤40% of Chinese revenue reached the center). Ceremonial cloaks the sovereign as cosmic steward; district magistrate as magician (Fei).

3. **Mercantile domain** — land as private property, commodity bought/sold for profit; rent as interest on capitalized land (Maine's competition rent); mortgage and foreclosure. Polanyi: land as commodity is a legal fiction (land is not produced for sale). Still a domain asserting oversight and tribute (rent), but reckoned as money capital.

4. **Administrative domain** (modern extension Wolf discusses) — state-socialist and reform forms that reorganize peasant surplus capture without classical lordship: Soviet **kolkhoz** / **sovkhoz** (private plots vs landless state-farm labor), Chinese communes, Mexican **ejidos**. Not a pure fourth "traditional" type but the postwar reorganization of domain under revolutionary and developmental states.

Domains **coexist** in mixes; relative importance defines organizational profile (e.g., medieval Europe N of Alps: patrimonial dominant with prebendary grants). Wolf warns against treating the sequence patrimonial → prebendary → mercantile as a unilinear evolutionary ladder. **Rent capitalism** (Bobek, via Wolf) models commercialized surplus extraction without full industrial transformation — relevant to Orient/Latin America profiles vs NW Europe.

## Semantic History

Weber's patrimonialism/prebend + Maine + Polanyi + agrarian history, synthesized as comparative domain typology for peasantry. Distinct from Marxian ground-rent theory though compatible as description of surplus form. Homans on gift reciprocity and Fei on the Chinese magistrate-as-magician supply the ceremonial legitimation half of domain.

## Operationalizations

- Who holds heritable vs office-based vs marketable vs state-administrative claims?
- Form of tribute: labor / kind / money / tax / interest / collective delivery.
- Ceremonial legitimation load (personal lord–peasant compact vs cosmic sovereign vs market contract vs party-state ideology).
- Mix ratios and historical transitions; official skimming under prebendary systems (Weber estimate cited: even best case ≤40% of Chinese revenue reached the center).

## Applications

- Comparative agrarian political structure without forcing "feudalism" as universal stage.
- Conditions for [[fund-of-rent]] intensity and for [[peasant-dilemma]] strategy shifts.
- Link to state strength vs officialdom (prebendary skimming).
- Structural precondition for [[closed-corporate-peasant-community]] under certain prebendary/colonial regimes.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Ideal types; hybrid empires compressed in short treatment.
- Administrative domain is sketched via secondary stats (e.g. Johnson on Soviet private plots), not primary fieldwork.
- Gendered access to domain rights under-specified.
