# Value Spheres

## Definition as Coined

**Value spheres** (*Wertsphären*) are [[weber-max|Weber]]'s (attributed) name for the culturally differentiated domains of modern life — science, law and morality, art, eroticism, economy, politics, and so on — each of which develops an **inner logic** (*Eigengesetzlichkeit*) and can stand in irreconcilable tension with the others ("polytheism of values"). In *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]*, [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]] treats the differentiation of value spheres as the cultural side of modern rationalization: cognitive-instrumental, moral-practical, and aesthetic-expressive rationalities become separable.

Habermas's strategic claim (attributed): Weber *describes* this multi-dimensional cultural rationalization but, bottlenecked by an action theory centered on [[purposive-rationality]], *analyzes societal rationalization* too narrowly — missing how moral-practical and aesthetic dimensions could rationalize communicatively.

## Semantic History

Weber's *Zwischenbetrachtung* on religious rejections of the world → Parsons's differentiation theory → Habermas's three-world / three-validity reconstruction of cultural modernity → debates on whether "value spheres" reify bourgeois cultural divisions (Marxist and cultural-studies critiques, attributed).

## Operationalizations

Typological; Habermas maps spheres onto validity dimensions (truth / rightness / authenticity) rather than treating them as ontologically separate "worlds of life."

## Applications

Explains modern cultural differentiation; supports TCA's thesis of **selective rationalization** (cognitive-instrumental institutionalization outrunning communicative rationalization of the lifeworld).

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of culturalist essentialism of spheres; feminist and postcolonial critiques of the bourgeois public mapping of culture (attributed, not primary in this ingest).
