# Neheh and Djet

## Definition as Coined

**Neheh** and **djet** are the two ancient Egyptian terms conventionally translated "eternity," which [[assmann-jan|Jan Assmann]] analyzes as two distinct modes of time: *neheh* — cyclical, recurring time, generated by the movement of the heavenly bodies, associated with the sun, "becoming," and the scarab; *djet* — unchanging permanence, "time at a standstill," associated with the earth, stone, the mummy, and Osiris. In Assmann's reading, linear-irreversible historical time occupies neither category but the dimension both negate ([[assmann-mind-of-egypt-1996|Assmann 1996]]).

## Semantic History

The word pair is Egyptian and long predates modern analysis; Egyptological treatments (Assmann's own *Zeit und Ewigkeit* 1975 among them) turned it into an analytic contrast. In *The Mind of Egypt* it carries the book's architecture: ritual repetition sustains *neheh*; monumental stone construction realizes *djet*; and Egypt's alleged aversion to historical (linear) time is read against both.

## Operationalizations

Readings of solar hymns, Pyramid and Coffin Texts, pyramidion inscriptions, and the mud-brick/stone building dichotomy (stone reserved for the sacred/eternal).

## Applications

Assmann's account of pyramid symbolism, canonization as "institutionalized permanence," and his broader claim that Egyptian culture was organized to foil linear time — connecting to Lévi-Strauss's "cold society" idea, which Assmann adapts and qualifies.

## Critiques and Limitations

Positions recorded: the neat cyclical/static binary is Assmann's systematization of usage that Egyptologists note is fluid in the sources; the inference from temporal vocabulary to a whole culture's historical consciousness is an interpretive leap resting on elite textual evidence.
