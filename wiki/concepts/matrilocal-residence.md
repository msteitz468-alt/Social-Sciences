# Matrilocal Residence

## Definition as Coined

**Matrilocal residence**: the married couple lives with or near the **wife's** people. In [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]]'s *[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, a key variable of family organization, analytically distinct from matrilineal descent ([[sib]] mother-sib) and from the "matriarchate."

## Semantic History

Comparative residence typology (matrilocal, patrilocal, neolocal, bilocal, avunculocal, etc.) became standard in 20th-c. kinship. Do not equate matrilocal residence with female political dominance.

## Operationalizations

Initial vs permanent residence; bride-service periods; correlation (or not) with mother-sibs — Lowie treats correlations as empirical, not necessary.

## Applications

Family and Position of Woman chapters of *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]*.

## Critiques and Limitations

Residence labels compress messy household cycles; always date and specify.
