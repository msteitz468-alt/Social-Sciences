# Underlife

## Definition as Coined

**Underlife** is [[goffman-erving|Goffman]]'s name, in *[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]* Essay 3, for the tissue of [[secondary-adjustment|secondary adjustments]] through which inmates of a public mental hospital (and, by extension, members of organizations generally) introduce distance between themselves and the institution's expectations. The essay's subtitle: "A Study of Ways of Making Out in a Mental Hospital."

When the set of secondary adjustments is considered together — not as isolated infractions — they "comprise what can be called the underlife of the institution" (attributed). Essay 3 conclusions include the general claim: **whenever worlds are laid on, underlives develop** (attributed).

Underlife is not merely rule-breaking; it is a structured set of practices, places, relationships, and exchange systems that allow a self to survive under conditions designed to schedule and redefine it. Goffman links this to a view of the self as a **stance-taking entity**, with identity protected "in the cracks" of the organizational schedule (attributed).

## Operationalizations

Essay structure (as in source):

- **Part One:** action and being; commitment/attachment; primary vs secondary adjustment; underlife as concept.
- **Part Two:** hospital underlife — make-dos; working the system; free places, territories, stashes, transport; coercion and exchange; patrons; bond relations.
- **Part Three:** conclusions — underlives under laid-on worlds; vicious circle when alienative acts are read as psychosis; removal activities; self as stance-taking.

Field site in the essay: **Central Hospital** (Goffman's anonymized large public hospital materials, continuous with St. Elizabeths fieldwork; ~7,000 patients).

## Applications

- Primary case: large public mental hospital underlife.
- Comparative asides to prisons and other total institutions.
- Theoretical payoff: sociological account of the self as withholdable and partitionable under totalizing conditions — link to Goffman's broader self theory and to [[symbolic-interactionism]].

## Critiques and Limitations

- Single-hospital ethnographic depth plus literature — not multi-site systematic comparison.
- Underlife of *staff* is thinner than inmate underlife.
- Historical: 1950s hospital ecology (wards, grounds, town parole, cigarette economies) differs from later psychiatric settings.
- Goffman's own caveat on underlife observational bias and weak reactive solidarity among patients at Central Hospital.
