# Going beyond

Central analytical phrase of [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013): the imaginative ability to **go beyond** immediate co-presence and create societies in which people **lived apart yet stayed in touch** — argued as the social-cognitive base that made *Homo sapiens* effective world settlers (attributed, Gamble 2013, esp. chs 1, 6).

## Definition as Coined

Load-bearing motif of *Settling the Earth*: humans increase social complexity by extending it through time and place — they can **go beyond** the physical presence that constrains other primates and so achieve a **[[release-from-proximity|release from social proximity]]** (Gamble 1998; Rodseth et al. 1991, as cited 2013) via materials linked to senses and emotions (attributed, ch. 1). Imagination moves from the experiential to the **relational** through symbols and metaphors (e.g. a handaxe standing for an unseen ancestor) (attributed).

Three imaginative developments (attributed, no forced sequence): (1) relating to people when not present; (2) treating objects similarly to people (two-way animate/inanimate agency); (3) creating geographies of other times and spaces (Eden, Lilliput, Orientalism, shared Palaeolithic past). **Keeping** (storage, possession, secrets) and **[[kinshipping]]** are the paired concepts that describe the capacity to go beyond and create such imaginary geographies (attributed after Said 1978; Shryock, Trautmann & Gamble 2011).

## Semantic History

Builds on *Palaeolithic Societies of Europe* (1999) network scales / release-from-proximity and *Timewalkers* (1994) colonization-without-teleology. In 2013 the phrase titles Chapter 6 ([[terrae|Terra 3]]) and closes the book’s synthesis: going beyond becomes regular in Terrae 3–4 but imagination evolves earlier via [[enchainment|FACE]] amplification in Terrae 1–2 (attributed, ch. 9). Ocean navigation is cast as the same accomplishment — social skills first, then geography (attributed, ch. 8).

## Operationalizations

Archaeologically inferred via long-distance transfers, ornaments, structured burials, boats/containers, seafaring, and other markers of extended networks — always attributed and period-stamped. Contrasted with Terra 2 limits: rare long stone transfers, little ocean voyaging, no Sahul peopling, rarely >55°N (attributed, chs 5–6).

## Applications

Explains Terra 3+ range expansion (Sahul, Siberia, Americas, later Pacific) where **[[encephalization]]** alone (largely earlier) does not — the major **disconnect** between brain growth and settlement/technology (attributed, ch. 1; cf. [[sapient-paradox]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Theoretical package; risk of reading absence of UP-style material culture as absence of "going beyond." Absolute timings and behavioral-modernity checklists remain contested. Horizon: as of Gamble 2013.
