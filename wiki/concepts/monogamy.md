# Monogamy

## Definition as Coined

**Monogamy** in ordinary usage is marriage of one spouse at a time. In [[engels-friedrich|Engels]]’s *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]*, monogamy is specifically the **civilized family form** that crystallizes with [[private-property]] and father-right: exclusive cohabitation under male supremacy, designed to produce undisputed heirs of body, historically accompanied (Engels argues) by hetaerism/prostitution and the double sexual standard. He contrasts this with the [[pairing-family]] and with proletarian unions where property motives drop away.

## Semantic History

Legal-religious monogamy (Roman, Christian, Islamic debates over plurality) ≠ Morgan–Engels evolutionary monogamy ≠ modern demographic “monogamous marriage rates.” Do not collapse these registers. Feminist theory reworks Engels’s link between monogamy and property without accepting his stage ladder.

## Operationalizations

- Legal: one spouse; adultery law asymmetry.
- Engels historical: monogamy as index of civilization and of class society; “first class oppression” of male over female coeval with class opposition (attributed slogan-level claim).

## Applications

*Origin* ch. II (family) and ch. IX (synthesis with state and commodity production). Institution page [[family]] carries the Comtean and Engelsian theoretical treatments side by side.

## Critiques and Limitations

Cross-cultural prevalence of polygyny, serial monogamy, and non-property motives for pair marriage undercut any single origin story; genetic and archaeological kinship data (when used) must be date-stamped and not equated with Engels’s narrative. See [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
