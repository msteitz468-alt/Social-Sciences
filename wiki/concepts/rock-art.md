# Rock Art

## Definition as Coined

**Rock art** denotes paintings and engravings on natural rock surfaces. In African archaeology it is both an evidence class and an interpretive minefield: images are mute on language and self-ascribed identity, and ethnographic analogy (especially San/Bushman analogy in southern Africa) is powerful but must be flagged (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Semantic History

Phillipson’s Ch. 4 survey treats southern and south-central African rock art as part of later mode-5 / LSA cultural landscapes, with interpretive traditions drawing on San ethnography (as of Phillipson 2005). North African and Saharan rock art sequences (pastoral and earlier) form a parallel literature often periodised separately.

## Operationalizations

- **Style and superimposition** sequences for relative chronology.
- **Direct dating** rare; many chronologies are relative or by associated deposits.
- **Interpretive frames:** hunting magic, shamanism/trance (southern Africa debates), territorial marking, historical narrative — all **attributed positions**, not wiki-voice meanings.

## Applications

- Southern African painted traditions in Phillipson’s regional MSA/LSA chapter (as of Phillipson 2005).
- Links to [[khoe-san]] ethnography as **analogy**, not identity of ancient painters with modern groups without argument.
- Sahara pastoral imagery in Holocene food-production discussions (cross-chapter).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Ethnographic-present and analogy traps.
- Colonial collection and aesthetic framing histories.
- Dating poverty relative to lithic sequences.
- Four-way non-identity: art style ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ “a people.”

### Handbook densification (*Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology*, 2013)

*(Attributed — Connah Ch. 2; Wadley Ch. 25; Mitchell southern-Africa Holocene chapters; as of ~2012.)*

- **Historiography:** Abbé Breuil believed southern African rock art ultimately “descended from European cave-painting”; *White Lady of the Brandberg* advanced extreme diffusionism even for its time (Connah).
- **Ethnographic turn:** Connah credits Vinnicombe and Lewis-Williams with showing meaning can be extracted from southern African rock art using relevant San ethnography, revitalizing rock-art studies globally within a broader post-processual/cognitive turn after environmental-determinist processualism lost favour.
- **Shamanistic model as heuristic:** Wadley records Lewis-Williams’s neuropsychology + ethnography model as widely used and heuristic, while detractors (Solomon; McCall) contend it is too restrictive.
- **Southern African resolution (Mitchell):** San concepts (Kalahari ethnography and /Xam archive) illuminate shamanic healing, animal control, rain-making, and cosmic tiers; other levels (ethology, body phenomenology, gender/initiation, female puberty among Khoe-speaking San painters) also warrant study; Solomon’s creation-myth alternative judged exaggerated by Mitchell. Surviving paintings may reach ~3500–3000 BP (Western Cape, Maloti-Drakensberg) and perhaps ~6000 BP in Zimbabwe; open-air engravings may be older; **AMS paint dating remains urgently needed**.
- **Sahara:** Roundhead rock art as early Holocene hunter-gatherer symbolic world (Barich); later pastoral cattle imagery participates in “cattle cult” circulation (di Lernia) — separate regional literature, not a single pan-African style.
- **Apollo 11:** Earliest figurative art in southern Africa from late MSA Apollo 11 (~27 kya)—painted slabs of animals, geometrics, possible therianthrope resembling Holocene hunter-gatherer rock art—still unique, awaiting corroboration (Lombard citing Vogelsang et al.).

**Identity caution:** Art style ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ a people. San ethnography is **analogy** for Holocene southern painting traditions, not proof that MSA or Saharan painters were “San.”

## Renfrew 2003 layer

*(Attributed — *[[renfrew-figuring-it-out-2003|Figuring It Out]]*.)* Upper Palaeolithic Franco-Cantabrian cave art (Altamira, Lascaux, Chauvet ~30 kya "if radiocarbon dates are to be believed") is Renfrew's prime illustration of **mythic** culture in the Donald/Renfrew stage scheme — foci for narrative — while remaining geographically limited; not treated as sufficient to trigger the Holocene acceleration explained via [[material-engagement]].
