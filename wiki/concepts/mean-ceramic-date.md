# Mean Ceramic Date

Dating formula in North American **[[historical-archaeology]]**, attributed by [[deetz-james|Deetz]] (1996) to **Stanley South**: for each of 100+ pottery types with known manufacture spans, take the midpoint manufacturing date, weight by relative fragment quantity, and average to approximate the midpoint of site occupation. South tested the formula on collections with known occupation dates and reported close matches.

## Definition as Coined

A single-date summary of an assemblage's ceramic chronology, complementary to **terminus post quem** (e.g., creamware after Josiah Wedgwood c. 1762) and pipestem-bore formulas (Harrington; refined by [[binford-lewis|Binford]]).

## Semantic History

Part of the 1960s–70s quantification of historic-site dating. Deetz treats disagreement between mean ceramic date and independent occupation dates as potentially **behavioral** information (not only error): at **[[parting-ways|Parting Ways]]**, a cellar with mid-19th-c TPQ yielded mean ceramic date **1794** vs actual mean occupation ~**1822**, read as poverty and secondhand ceramics.

## Operationalizations

Sherd counts × type midpoints → weighted mean; requires well-dated European (esp. English) type series.

## Applications

Eastern North American colonial and early national sites; pedagogical staple in Deetz 1996 Ch. 1.

## Critiques and Limitations

Assumes type midpoints and count weights track occupation use-life; secondhand goods, curated heirlooms, and dumping lag bias the mean; English-type series less portable worldwide without recalibration.
