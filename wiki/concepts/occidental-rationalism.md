# Occidental Rationalism

## Definition as Coined

**Occidental rationalism** is [[weber-max|Weber]]'s (attributed) problem-concept for the historically unique combination of rationalizations that, in his view, crystallized in the modern West: modern empirical science, rational law and administration, capitalist enterprise with free labor, rational bureaucratic domination, and a methodical conduct of life. In *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]* Ch. II, [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]] reconstructs Weber as the classic who broke with philosophy-of-history and evolutionist premises yet still treated modernization as a **universal-historical process of rationalization** open to empirical investigation without empiricist erasure of rationality aspects.

Habermas (attributed) stresses that Weber employed a **complex but largely unclarified** concept of rationality in the religious-historical disenchantment story, while analyzing modern *societal* rationalization under a **restricted** idea of [[purposive-rationality]].

## Semantic History

Weber's comparative sociology of religion → mid-century "modernization" readings → Habermas's reconstructive use as the empirical-theoretical problem any sociology-as-theory-of-society must face on three levels (metatheoretical action concepts, methodological Verstehen, empirical modernization-as-rationalization). Contested as Eurocentric master narrative in later postcolonial and global-historical sociology (not fully ingested here).

## Operationalizations

Weber's checklists of institutional forms (capitalism, bureaucracy, formal law, experimental science, professional politics, etc.); Habermas's re-reading via differentiation of cultural [[value-spheres]] and selective societal rationalization.

## Applications

Anchor of TCA Vol. 1's history-of-theory path; foil for expanding rationalization beyond purposive rationality; link to diagnoses of loss of meaning and loss of freedom.

## Critiques and Limitations

Habermas accepts Weber's problem while rejecting the constriction of societal rationalization to purposive rationality; external critiques target the "unique West" framing itself.
