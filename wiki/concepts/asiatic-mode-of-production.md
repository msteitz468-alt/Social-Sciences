# Asiatic Mode of Production

## Definition as Coined

The **Asiatic mode of production (AMP)** is a Marxian category for an agrarian social formation distinct from classical slavery, feudalism, and capitalism — typically associated with state or communal property forms and village communities under a superior authority. In *[[wittfogel-oriental-despotism-1957|Oriental Despotism]]* Ch. 9, [[wittfogel-karl-a|Wittfogel]] reconstructs the **rise and fall** of AMP theory inside Marxism and Soviet orthodoxy, tying it to his own hydraulic/Oriental-despotism analysis.

## Semantic History

Wittfogel (1957, attributed narrative): classical economists' Oriental society → Marx/Engels on Asia → later Marxist elaborations → Soviet conferences and political suppression when AMP threatened unilinear feudal→capitalist→socialist schemes and analyses of Russia/China. Later anthropologists (e.g. [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]]) folded related issues into a broader **[[tributary-mode-of-production]]**, criticizing the East–West despotism contrast — a different constructive move, not a confirmation of Wittfogel.

## Operationalizations

Variable across authors: state hydraulic works + dispersed villages; absence of private landed property; tax/rent fusion; stagnant "Asiatic" reproduction — all contested operational packages. Wittfogel's preferred operationalization is institutional **[[hydraulic-society]]**, not the AMP label alone.

## Applications

Historiography of Marxist theory; debates on China, India, Russia, and "Asiatic restoration" (Wittfogel Ch. 10 on Lenin/Soviet bureaucracy — attributed Cold-War argument).

## Critiques and Limitations

Empirical adequacy of a single "Asiatic" mode; Eurocentric naming; political uses in Stalinist and anti-Stalinist polemics; superseded or redescribed in many later frameworks ([[tributary-mode-of-production]], multiple tributary trajectories).
