# Dialogism in Ethnography

Application of Bakhtinian **dialogism** to ethnographic writing: texts that stage dialogue between ethnographer and interlocutors rather than monologic realist authority. Discussed across *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* (Clifford on experimental forms; Tyler; Rabinow on limits of dialogic solutions).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed to volume uses of Bakhtin/dialogic forms.)*

Dialogue as form and as ideal of coeval encounter (related to Fabian’s coevalness critique, separate ingest). Pratt on personal narrative recuperating face-to-face authority; Shostak *Nisa* as polyphonic mediation.

## Semantic History

From literary theory into ethnographic experiment in the Critical Turn.

## Operationalizations

Multi-voice transcripts, dialogue-centered monographs, quoted interlocutor authority.

## Applications

Shostak *Nisa*, Dwyer, Crapanzano, Clifford’s experimental trends; Willis dialogue-as-evidence (Marcus).

## Critiques and Limitations

Asad: dialogue does not erase power asymmetry. Rosaldo: false polyphony via translation chains. Rabinow: limits of dialogic/heteroglossic solutions as full answer to authority crisis.
