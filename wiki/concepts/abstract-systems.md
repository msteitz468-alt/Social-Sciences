# Abstract Systems

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (1990) grouped **[[symbolic-tokens]]** and **[[expert-systems]]** under the term **abstract systems**. Trust in abstract systems takes the form of **[[faceless-commitments]]** — faith sustained in the workings of knowledge of which the lay person is largely ignorant.

## Semantic History

Specific to Giddens’s late-1980s/1990 modernity program. Not Luhmann’s systems theory (though Giddens engages Luhmann on trust/risk); not "abstracted empiricism" (Mills).

## Operationalizations

Umbrella theoretical term. Empirical purchase via cases of money, professional services, transport, medicine, markets.

## Applications

- Explains extended [[time-space-distanciation]] under modernity.
- Frames transformation of intimacy: personal life is reworked *with* abstract systems, not merely diminished by them.
- Access points are junctions of vulnerability and trust-building for abstract systems.

## Critiques and Limitations

Highly general; critics may charge that "abstract systems" flattens institutional diversity. Keep in attributed voice.
