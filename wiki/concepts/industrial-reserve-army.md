# Industrial Reserve Army

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Chapter 25, [[marx-karl|Marx]] argues that capitalist accumulation progressively produces a **relative surplus population** — a mass of labourers superfluous to capital’s average needs for self-expansion — which he also calls the **industrial reserve army**, “kept in misery in order to be always at the disposal of capital.”

It is both a **product** and a **lever** of accumulation: machinery and rising [[organic-composition-of-capital|organic composition]] free workers; the reserve holds down wages and supplies sudden expansion.

**Attributed to Marx.** Part 4 already names the “monstrosity” of an industrial reserve army in connection with machinery.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| *Capital* I Ch. 25 (and foreshadowed in machinery ch.) | Relative surplus population internal to accumulation |
| Malthusian population theory | Absolute overpopulation vs means of subsistence — Marx’s foil |
| Later labour economics / sociology | Structural unemployment, underemployment — partial analogues |

## Forms (Ch. 25 §4)

As Marx classifies them:

| Form | Sketch (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Floating** | Urban/industrial centres; workers sometimes repelled, sometimes attracted |
| **Latent** | Rural population continually on the point of passing into urban proletariat |
| **Stagnant** | Irregular employment; maximum labour-time and minimum wages |
| **Pauperism** | Hospital of the active labour-army and dead weight of the industrial reserve |

## Operationalizations

- Wages regulated not by absolute population (Malthusian) but by expansion/contraction of the reserve army relative to active demand for labour.
- Ireland illustration (Ch. 25 §5 F): absolute depopulation with continuing relative surplus population and low wages — attributed counter to simple Malthusian cure-by-depopulation.
- England illustrations (Ch. 25 §5 A–E): pauper counts, casual labour, agricultural gang labour as empirical colour — mid-19th-c. documentation as Marx cites it.

## Applications

- Central to the [[general-law-of-capitalist-accumulation]].
- Links [[class-struggle]] over the working day and wages to structural unemployment internal to accumulation.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Contested by full-employment growth theories and by welfare-state periodization.
- “Kept in misery” thesis is a strong attributed claim — not promote to wiki voice as universal fact.
- Gender and informal care work thin in the 1867 typology.
