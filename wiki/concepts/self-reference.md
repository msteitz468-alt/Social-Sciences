# Self-Reference

## Definition as Coined

In [[luhmann-niklas|Luhmann]]'s [[social-systems-theory|systems theory]], **self-reference** is
"the unity that an element, a process, or a system is for itself" — a unity that must be
*produced*, not presupposed as substance. A system is self-referential "if it itself
constitutes the elements that compose it as functional unities and runs reference to this
self-constitution through all the relations among these elements." Luhmann narrows his opening
axiom "there are systems" to "there are self-referential systems": systems that "establish
relations with themselves and... differentiate these relations from relations with their
environment." Referring becomes **observing** (Luhmann via George Spencer Brown) when a
distinction is used to acquire information about what it indicates.

Luhmann's canonical **three forms of self-reference** (attributed):

1. **Basal self-reference** — the distinction element/relation; the "self" is an element (an
   event, a [[communication]]). The minimal form, without which [[autopoiesis|autopoietic]]
   reproduction of temporalized systems would be impossible.
2. **Reflexivity** (processual) — the distinction before/after; a process (e.g. communication
   about communication) with increasing selectivity.
3. **Reflection** — the distinction system/environment; the system indicates *itself* against
   its environment. Only here does self-reference become full system-reference; it issues in
   **self-observation** and **self-description**.

## Semantic History

Self-reference in logic and mathematics named a source of paradox; in cybernetics it named
feedback. Luhmann transforms it into the constitutive operation of meaning-based systems while
rejecting both roots: he denies causal circularity at the level of vanishing events (against
cybernetic feedback theories) and treats paradox as something systems must "de-tautologize" or
"asymmetricize" (via time, externalization, hierarchy) rather than resolve. **Pure** self-
reference ("relating only and exclusively to itself") is held impossible — self-reference is
always merely "accompanying." Luhmann's later work develops the reflection form into the
theory of **second-order observation** (observing observers), whose seed is here: "the unity of
difference is defined by the observer, not by his object," and an observer cannot observe the
distinction it is currently observing *with* (the blind spot).

## Operationalizations

Not a measurement construct; a structural criterion and descriptive apparatus.

## Applications

- Grounds [[autopoiesis]] and the closure/openness account in [[social-systems-theory]].
- Self-description explains how function systems represent themselves — money for the economy,
  the state for politics, *Bildung* for education (see [[functional-differentiation]]).
- The reflection form underlies Luhmann's claim that a **rational self-description of society as
  a whole** is blocked in a functionally differentiated society with no apex.

## Critiques and Limitations

The apparatus is charged with being self-immunizing: a theory built on self-reference can
absorb objections as further instances of itself, which critics say makes it hard to falsify.
Luhmann embraced this — a universalistic theory "appear[s] as [its] own object" — turning the
reflexivity that troubles critics into the theory's claimed advantage.
