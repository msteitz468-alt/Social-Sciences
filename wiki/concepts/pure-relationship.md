# Pure Relationship

## Definition as Coined

In *[[giddens-consequences-of-modernity-1990|The Consequences of Modernity]]* (1990), [[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] analysed the **transformation of intimacy** under modernity: personal trust becomes a *project* of mutual disclosure; modern "relationships" are organised around authenticity and ongoing negotiation rather than pre-modern honour/sincerity codes alone. He argued personal life is **transformed, not merely diminished**, by [[abstract-systems]] — intertwined with them rather than simply colonised.

The full label **pure relationship** is developed more programmatically in *Modernity and Self-Identity* (1991): a relationship entered into for its own sake, for what can be derived by each person from a sustained association with another, and continued only in so far as it is thought by both parties to deliver enough satisfaction. The 1990 book supplies the trust/intimacy architecture (self as reflexive project; friendship honour→authenticity; romantic love as transitional) that the 1991 text names and extends. **Attribute carefully by year.**

## Semantic History

Builds on the 1990 claim that the search for self-identity uses expert knowledge and globalised experience rather than mere narcissistic withdrawal (contra Lasch’s narcissism thesis as Giddens reads it). Parallel to Beck on individualisation of the intimate sphere — related but not identical.

## Operationalizations

Mostly theoretical; later family/relationship research sometimes operationalises "pure relationship" criteria (contingency, equality norms, disclosure) — secondary.

## Applications

- Links micro intimacy to macro disembedding and [[globalization]].
- Feeds life-politics side of [[utopian-realism]].
- Connects [[ontological-security]] to personal trust as actively achieved.

## Critiques and Limitations

Criticised as describing a privileged, Western, middle-class ideal as if general; underplays structural inequality, gendered care burdens, and non-voluntary ties. The 1990 text itself disentangles community, kinship, friendship, and sexual intimacy — do not collapse them. Full critical literature not yet ingested.
