# Friction

## Definition as Coined

[[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing]] (*[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]*, 2005) names **friction** the quality of interconnection across difference through which global aspirations take practical hold. Cultures, she argues (attributed), are continuously coproduced in interactions that are strange, unequal, unstable, and creative. Capitalism, science, and politics depend on global connections and universal dreams, but those universals only become effective in the “sticky materiality of practical encounters.”

Load-bearing metaphors (all attributed):

- A wheel turns because it meets the road; in air it goes nowhere. Rubbing two sticks produces heat and light.
- Roads facilitate movement **and** confine routes — ease of travel is also a structure of confinement.
- Friction is **required** to keep global power moving (“where the rubber meets the road”); it is not only drag.
- Friction is **not synonymous with resistance**. Hegemony is made *and* unmade in friction. Difference can disturb, dysfunction, or spark insurrection (“the mouse stuck in the elephant’s trunk”).
- The industrial rubber commodity chain itself is a history of frictions (conquest, colonial botany, peasant strategies, war, technoscience) that teleological progress stories erase.

Against 1990s stories of a frictionless global era of unimpeded flows, Tsing insists movement is always socially informed — by shoes, buses, security searches, informal segregation, coercion, and desire not to move.

## Semantic History

Charter formulation in *Friction* (Princeton 2005; French 2020 La Découverte). Distinct from physics, “friction of distance,” or casual “social friction.” Became a widely cited charter term in anthropologies of globalization and environmental politics. Later multi-species / ruins work (*Mushroom*, 2015, not yet ingested) continues related concerns under different master metaphors.

## Operationalizations

Heuristic and ethnographic, not a scalar index. Operationalized through Indonesian cases: frontier extraction and resource-making; Bre-X and speculative performance ([[economy-of-appearances]]); nature-loving under the New Order; Meratus weedy social-natural forests; environmental and *adat* mobilizations; **collaboration without consensus** (Manggur and CBNRM circuits) where productive misunderstanding and partial overlap enable joint action.

## Applications

- How [[traveling-universals]] and [[scale-making]] projects actually work.
- Critique of seamless-flow models of [[globalization]].
- Frontier capitalism ([[capitalist-frontier]]).
- Method: [[ethnography-of-global-connection]] tracks zones of cultural friction as transient encounter-spaces studied by “patchwork” rather than pre-planned village holism.

## Critiques and Limitations

Can become a catch-all for any messy encounter — specificity requires named parties, scales, and outcomes. Reception sometimes reads Tsing as overly optimistic about collaborative productivity (position; not a wiki verdict). Does not replace macrosociological accounts of capital; it ethnographizes their practical making.
