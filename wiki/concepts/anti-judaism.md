# Anti-Judaism

**Anti-Judaism**, in the analytical sense developed at length by [[nirenberg-david|David Nirenberg]] in *[[nirenberg-anti-judaism-2013|Anti-Judaism: The Western Tradition]]* (2013), names a Western (and, he argues, also Islamic) **habit of thought**: the use of figures of Judaism, Jews, and “[[judaizing|Judaizing]]” to make sense of the world — of power, money, scripture, law, body, letter, reason, and revolution — often independent of the presence or behavior of living Jews. It is **not** identical with modern racial or political [[antisemitism]], though the two interact; and it is not reducible to a continuous hatred that simply “causes” the Holocaust. See [[anti-judaism-continuity-debate]].

## Definition as Coined

Nirenberg (attributed) frames the object as the history of *thinking about Judaism* / the *Judaism of thought*: why diverse cultures, including many with no Jews among them, thought so much with Jewish figures; what work that thinking did; how it constrained later possibilities of thought; and how it affected possibilities of existence for living Jews.

Following Marx’s insight into the [[jewish-question]] (as Nirenberg reads it), he treats “Judaism” not only as the religion of a people but as a **category** — ideas and attributes with which non-Jews make sense of and criticize their world. Anti-Judaism is thus “one of the basic tools with which [the Western] edifice was constructed,” not an archaic irrational closet (Nirenberg, attributed).

Earlier Christian-theological usage of “anti-Judaism” (as opposed to racial antisemitism) pre-exists Nirenberg; his contribution is the long-span intellectual-history program that treats the category as a **mask** giving form to transforming concepts.

| Phase | Use (attributed / conventional) |

| Theological / church history | Pre-modern Christian hostility labeled “anti-Judaism,” often distinguished from modern racial “antisemitism” |
| Nirenberg 2013 | Long-span history of *thinking with* Judaism across Egypt → modernity, including Islam; mask/genealogy method |
| Relation to antisemitism | Overlap without collapse — see [[anti-judaism-continuity-debate]] |

## Method (Nirenberg, attributed)

Three programmatic deviations from mid-twentieth-century professional history’s “liberty”/agency enthusiasm:

1. Scarcely treating thoughts and actions of self-identified Jews — focus is how mostly **non-Jews** thought with/about Judaism.
2. A history of **thought** granted power against pure interest-explanations (Weber’s “switchmen” cited).
3. Attention to **continuity** of form (mask) alongside transformation of content — against both teleology and radical presentist cutting.

Provocative juxtaposition without simple causality: gospel attacks on Pharisees’ “empty wisdom” and Goebbels’s 10 May 1933 “The age of rampant Jewish intellectualism is now at an end.” Relation possible; not implication or necessity.

Core causal formula on the Holocaust (Ch. 13, attributed): the West’s long history of Jewish questions **did not cause** the Final Solution or make it inevitable; but the Holocaust was **inconceivable without** that history.

## Operationalizations

Not a survey construct. Operationalized as close reading of how corpora deploy Jewish figures to solve non-Jewish problems:

| Domain | Exemplary “Jewish” problem (Nirenberg’s cases) |
|---|---|
| Scripture / hermeneutics | Letter vs. spirit; Law; Pharisee as hypocrite |
| Politics / power | Royal “Jew-love”; fiscality; *servi camerae*; expulsion as state extension |
| Economy | Usury, contract, commerce, capital as Judaizing |
| Conversion / purity | Conversos; *limpieza de sangre*; incomplete conversion as Judaizing residue |
| Reason / critique | Formalism, intellectualism, mediation, “Jewish mathematics” |
| Revolution | Emancipation as limit-case of citizenship; regeneration |

## Cross-tradition applications (from the book)

- **Egypt / empire:** Manetho’s Osarseph/Moses; five-point misanthropy tradition; Alexandrian politics — tiny pre-Christian “islands” later enlarged by projection (Nirenberg, attributed).
- **Early Christianity:** Paul on “to Judaize”; letter kills; gospel dualisms; Acts supersession.
- **Early Church:** Chrysostom’s “Judaizing disease”; Ambrose; Augustine’s protected exile / “Slay them not.”
- **Islam:** *tahrif*; hypocrite ≈ Judaizer; inclusion/exclusion as structuring principle (potential internal to Islam, not “essential antisemitism” — Nirenberg, attributed).
- **Medieval Europe / Spain / Reformation:** Jews and princely power; 1391 conversions; Inquisition and purity of blood; Luther’s trajectory from mild 1523 to 1543 elimination program (Nirenberg declines Holocaust-architect anachronism).
- **Shakespeare / confessional politics / Enlightenment:** *Merchant of Venice*; Hobbes and Christian Hebraism; Spinoza, Voltaire, Kant’s “euthanasia of Judaism.”
- **Revolution / philosophy / modernity:** French Revolutionary citizenship debates; German idealism’s struggle with Judaism; Marx 1844; Weber–Sombart; Nazi “Jewish intellectualism”; Arendt and Horkheimer/Adorno in the epilogue.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Risk of **over-intellectualizing** persecution if used alone (material and political histories of violence need parallel evidence classes).
- Risk of **teleology** — Nirenberg himself rejects heavy causal bridges from gospels to genocide.
- Living Jewish self-understandings and internal diversity are not the book’s primary archive — a deliberate scope limit, not a finding about Judaism itself.
- Companion explanatory axes: Cohn’s [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]] warrant ([[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-study]]); Bauman’s modernity/bureaucracy ([[holocaust-and-modernity-debate]]).
