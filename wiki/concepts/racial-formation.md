# Racial Formation

Core concept of [[racial-formation-theory]]: the sociohistorical process by which racial categories are created, inhabited, transformed, and destroyed through linked structural and representational practices.

## Definition as Coined

Omi and Winant (*[[omi-winant-racial-formation-2015|Racial Formation in the United States]]*, 1986/2015) define racial formation as that ongoing process; race is neither essence nor illusion but a fundamental principle of social organization continually remade.

## Semantic History

Coined for the 1986 program; generalized across U.S. race sociology and ethnic studies; sometimes loosely used as synonym for “social construction of race” without the project/structure linkage the authors emphasize.

## Operationalizations

Analyzed via trajectories of racial politics, institutional policies, movements, and everyday racial projects — not a single quantitative index.

## Applications

U.S. postwar racial politics; colorblind hegemony; comparative extensions (Winant and others — not fully ingested).

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of over-extending “everything is a racial project”; tension with intersectional accounts that refuse race primacy — engaged but not dissolved in the third edition.
