# Social fact

## Definition as Coined

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]’s definition of sociology’s object, stated programmatically in *[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|The Rules of Sociological Method]]* (1895), Chapter I. Not everything that happens “in society” is a social fact — eating, sleeping, and private reasoning interest society but belong to biology or psychology if they have only the individual as substratum (attributed).

**Load-bearing formula** (*Rules* Ch. I, closing definition, Halls/Lukes):

> A social fact is any way of acting, whether fixed or not, capable of exerting over the individual an external constraint; or, which is possible everywhere it is general over the whole of a given society whilst having an existence of its own, independent of its individual manifestations.

Two criteria (attributed):

1. **Exteriority** — ways of acting, thinking, and feeling exist **outside** the individual consciousness (received through education, law, custom, language, currency, professional practice).
2. **Constraint (coercion)** — they are invested with a compelling power that asserts itself when one resists (legal sanctions, public conscience, ridicule and social distance, practical impossibility of speaking another language or using other currency, ruin if one ignores economic techniques).

He extends the class beyond “physiological” ways of functioning to **morphological ways of being** — political divisions, population distribution, housing design, communication networks — which likewise impose themselves and are consolidated forms of acting ([[social-morphology]]).

Collective **currents** of opinion (e.g. enthusiasm, indignation, pity in assemblies; suicidogenetic currents studied in *Suicide*) are social facts even when not crystallized as rules (attributed).

## Semantic History

| Moment | Usage |
|---|---|
| *Division of Labor* (1893) | Method enacted: solidarity known via external index (law); causes in the social environment |
| *Rules* (1895) | Dual criteria; morphological facts included; “[[treat-social-facts-as-things|treat as things]]” |
| 1901 Preface | Social life made entirely of **representations**; consciousness not a substance; constraint as *indicator*, not philosophy of force; synthesis *sui generis* from association |
| *Suicide* (1897) | Social suicide-rate as paradigm *sui generis* fact |
| Subsequent letters (Lukes volume) | Social facts are psychological *sui generis* — not non-psychological; collective psychology ≠ individual psychology enlarged |
| Later reception | Core of [[social-realism|Durkheimian social realism]]; reification charges; methodological-individualist rivals ([[methodological-individualism-debate]]) |

## Operationalizations

- **Legal codes and fixed institutions** — preferred “solid” entry points (*Rules* Ch. II).
- **Suicide-rate** and other [[moral-statistics]] — constancy, variation, lawful covariation.
- **Morphological measures** — volume, density, segmentary structure ([[dynamic-density]], [[social-types]]).
- **External indices** for invisible states (law → solidarity in *Division of Labor*).

Method rule: [[treat-social-facts-as-things]] — discard prenotions; define by external characters; prefer crystallized forms.

## Applications

- *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-study|Division of Labor]]* — solidarity as social fact via legal index.
- *[[durkheim-suicide-study|Suicide]]* — rate as *sui generis* fact; elimination of extra-social causes.
- [[normal-and-pathological|Normality]] judgments relative to [[social-types|social type]] (crime as normal; pathological intensity).
- Explanation rule: determining cause among **antecedent social facts**, not states of individual consciousness; function sought in relation to a **social end** (*Rules* Ch. V).

## Critiques and Limitations

Attributed lines: reification / “group mind” (Durkheim’s 1901 reply: no substantial soul; phenomena *sui generis*); underplaying meaning and agency ([[structure-vs-agency-debate]], [[interpretive-sociology]]); ecological inference from rates; constraint criterion may not fit all collective patterns equally; later individualist and emergence framings restate the problem ([[methodological-individualism-debate]]).
