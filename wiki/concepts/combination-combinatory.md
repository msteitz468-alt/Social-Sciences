# Combination / Combinatory (Mode of Production)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* Part Five, [[balibar-etienne|Étienne Balibar]] defines a **mode** as “a system of forms which represents one state of the variation of the set of elements which necessarily enter into the process considered.” The **combination** (*Verbindung*) of a [[mode-of-production]] is not a simple relation among factors but **the relationship between two distinct connections and their interdependence**:

1. **Property connection** — separation/property between labourer, means of production, and non-worker appropriating surplus labour.
2. **Real (material) appropriation connection** — *wirkliche Aneignung*: real appropriation of means of production by the producer in the labour process (appropriation of nature), distinct from ownership of surplus product.

Invariant elements: (1) worker; (2) means of production (object of labour; means of labour); (3) non-worker; plus connections (A) property and (B) real appropriation. Balibar stresses: this is **not a combinatory in the strict sense** — only places and relations would change while natures of factors stayed fixed — because the nature of the elements themselves is transformed by the combination.

## Semantic History

Althusser’s Part One already distinguishes *Verbindung* / *Gliederung* (articulated combination of a structured whole) from empiricist aggregation. Brewster’s glossary entry **COMBINATION/COMBINATORY** codifies the dual vocabulary. In capitalism the two connections take homologous form as **separations** (worker stripped of property *and* of ability to set social instruments to work alone), which is why Marx sometimes confounds them; earlier modes and notional “natural community” do not share that homology. Variation of the combination reconstitutes modes including notional ones (simple commodity production; foreseeable socialist conditions). Anthropological structural Marxism (Godelier, Terray, Meillassoux) inherits combination-of-elements as apparatus for precapitalist modes — reception already on [[structural-marxism]] and Sahlins’s critique.

## Operationalizations

- **Table of invariants** as research grid for any mode (not a classification of societies as wholes).
- **Double function of the capitalist** — exploiter (property) and organizer of production (real appropriation) — as index of technical vs social division of labour in one structure.
- **Classes as [[bearer-trager|bearers]]** of the three-term economic relation, not pre-given subjects of contract.
- **Transition / manufacture:** non-correspondence of the two connections (formal subsumption without real subsumption) is the essence of certain transitional forms — see [[dislocation-decalage]].

## Applications

- Balibar’s reconstruction of historical materialism’s basic concepts in *Reading Capital*.
- Distinction of Asiatic, feudal, capitalist, etc., as different combinations, not stages of an expressive historical time.
- Establet’s plan of *Capital*: Vol. 1 sub-articulations rewritten as capitalist labour process / reproduction of conditions and relations of production / organization of productive forces.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Strict-combinatory misreading flattens history into a matrix of slots (Balibar explicitly rejects this).
- Sahlins et al.: combination still organizes analysis around production requirements, underplaying symbolic constitution of social forms.
- Empiricist “which societies fall under this mode?” demand yields model-ideology; Balibar/Althusser answer with ideal average as concept of the real, not classification key.
