# Discursive Consciousness

## Definition as Coined

Giddens Glossary: what actors are able to **say**, or to give verbal expression to, about social conditions, including especially the conditions of their own action; awareness which has a discursive form (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution]]*).

## Semantic History

Upper layer of Giddens's stratification model, paired with [[practical-consciousness]] and bounded by the unconscious. Related to **rationalization of action** — competent actors' capacity to keep in touch with grounds of what they do and supply reasons if asked.

## Operationalizations

Accounts, interviews, and explicit justifications access discursive consciousness; they do **not** exhaust [[knowledgeability]] (much remains practical).

## Applications

- Method: do not equate verbal report with full knowledgeability.
- Critique and social science can expand discursive reach over practical knowledge ([[double-hermeneutic]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of interview methodology treating discursive accounts as complete maps of action.
