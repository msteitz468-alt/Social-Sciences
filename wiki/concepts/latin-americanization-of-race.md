# Latin Americanization of Race

The **Latin Americanization thesis** is [[bonilla-silva-eduardo|Eduardo Bonilla-Silva]]'s projection (Chapter 9 of *[[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-2018|Racism without Racists]]*) that the historically bi-racial (white/nonwhite) U.S. order is evolving into "a complex and loosely organized triracial stratification system similar to that of many Latin American and Caribbean nations" (attributed). It is offered explicitly as a heuristic hypothesis about a future order — expected to take several decades and offered partly in the hope it can be derailed — not as an established finding.

## Definition as Coined

Bonilla-Silva proposes three emerging strata (attributed; his Figure 9.1 is labeled preliminary and revisable):

| Stratum | Illustrative groups (per Bonilla-Silva) |
|---|---|
| Whites | Traditional whites; new white immigrants; assimilated white Latinos; lighter-skinned multiracials; assimilated urban Native Americans; a few Asian-origin people |
| Honorary whites | Most light-skinned Latinos (most Cubans, segments of Mexicans/Puerto Ricans); Japanese, Korean, Chinese, and Asian Indian Americans; most Middle Eastern Americans; most multiracials |
| Collective black | Dark-skinned Latinos; Vietnamese, Cambodian, Hmong, Laotian, and (tentatively) Filipino Americans; Black Americans; new West Indian and African immigrants; reservation-bound Native Americans |

He stresses "categorical porosity" (individual mobility between strata, e.g. through intermarriage) alongside "pigmentocracy" (group rank-ordering by phenotype and culture), so the map is meant for group- not individual-level prediction. He flags several placements — Filipinos, Arab Americans, Chinese Americans — as uncertain. (An internal tension: Filipinos sit in the "collective black" stratum in Figure 9.1 but on the higher-earning side of his income table — a discrepancy he himself notes.)

## Semantic History / Reasoning

Bonilla-Silva transposes six features of Latin American racial stratification onto the U.S. (attributed): hierarchical mestizaje oriented toward "whitening"; a buffering intermediate stratum; colorism/pigmentocracy within strata; *blanqueamiento* (whitening) as ideology and process; a national "mestizaje" myth that suppresses racial discourse; and nationality/culture ("we are all Americans") as the preferred idiom that keeps race off the political agenda. He gives five drivers: changing demography (minorities approaching a numeric majority); the [[the-new-racism|new racism]] and [[colorblind-racism|color-blind racism]]; the globalization of race relations; Census/political dynamics that may end official racial data collection ("the secret of race in Latin America"); and the "racial retreat" from affirmative action.

## Operationalizations

Reviewed as provisional support (attributed): income (2000 PUMS) tracking the honorary-white/collective-black divide; Latino self-classification as "white"; racial-attitude data in which intermediate groups hold more pro-white attitudes than whites themselves; and intermarriage, offspring classification, and residential-segregation patterns. Bonilla-Silva stresses most datasets do not disaggregate by subgroup or skin tone, so the evidence is suggestive only.

## Applications / Debates

Projects five consequences: softened "us vs. them" politics; deeper entrenchment of color-blind racism; harder documentation of racial inequality if racial statistics cease; a durable but more "diffused" Black identity; and a more secure white supremacy with a narrower forum to contest it. Bonilla-Silva argues **against** [George] Yancey's competing prediction of a binary black/nonblack order, giving four reasons most Latinos and Asians will not simply "become white" (long U.S. presence, the "epidermic capital" requirement, segmented assimilation, and stable ethnic enclaves) — a live scholarly disagreement (attributed).

## Critiques and Limitations

`contested: yes` — the thesis is a forward projection resting on cross-sectional, coarsely disaggregated data; Yancey and others predict a different (binary) trajectory, and the stratum placements are admittedly provisional.
