# Status-Set

## Definition as Coined

[[merton-robert-k|Merton]]'s **status-set** is the array of **distinct social statuses** a person occupies (e.g., physician, spouse, church member, citizen), each generating its own [[role-set]]. Complements role-set theory: conflict may arise **within** a role-set or **between** statuses in a status-set.

## Semantic History

Paired with [[role-set]] in Merton's middle-range apparatus; standard in role theory textbooks. Distinct from Weberian [[status-group]] (Stand) — do not equate.

## Operationalizations

Status inconsistency / crystallization research (adjacent traditions); organizational dual-role analysis.

## Applications

Professional identity; work–family; political participation of multiple-status actors.

## Critiques and Limitations

List of statuses is open-ended; dynamics of status-set change under-theorized relative to role-set articulation mechanisms.
