# Three Elementary Forms of Domination

## Definition as Coined

In *[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|The Dawn of Everything]]* ch. 10 (2021), [[graeber-david|Graeber]] and [[wengrow-david|Wengrow]] propose three principles as possible bases of social power (attributed):

1. **Control of violence** — most dependable; basis of uniform law and sovereignty (Weberian monopoly of legitimate force as one institutionalization).
2. **Control of information / knowledge** — administration, official secrets, lists, stores, scribal or esoteric expertise (bureaucracy).
3. **Individual charisma** — most ephemeral; competitive political field, heroic *agons*, larger-than-life persuasion (not identical to Athenian assembly democracy; G&W note Greeks often treated elections as aristocratic and sortition as democratic).

**Load-bearing mapping (attributed):** "Just as access to violence, information and charisma defines the very possibilities of social domination, so the modern state is defined as a combination of **sovereignty, bureaucracy and a competitive political field**."

Their claim that **[[the-state|the state]] has no origin** means (attributed): the three forms have separate historical origins and need not fuse; modern nation-states are an amalgam that came together at a certain point and may be coming apart (e.g. planetary bureaucracies without global sovereignty). Looking at "birth of the state," one may be seeing different mixes crystallize — not embryonic modern states.

### First- and second-order regimes (as G&W classify)

| Order | Pattern (attributed) | Illustrative cases they discuss |
|---|---|---|
| First-order | One form dominates | Olmec (charisma/theatre); Chavín (knowledge); Natchez / Shilluk (sovereignty alone) |
| Second-order | Pairwise fusion | Egypt/Inca (sovereignty + administration); Mesopotamia (admin + heroic); Classic Maya (heroic + sovereignty) |

They reject teleology that these forms were bound to combine as eighteenth-century nation-states; rival developmental sequences are possible (Administration → Charisma by [[schismogenesis]] → Sovereignty, etc.).

## Semantic History

Builds on Weberian force / administration / charisma differentiation ([[legitimate-domination]], bureaucracy) while refusing a necessary modern synthesis and a single evolutionary threshold. Engages Clastres-style society-against-the-state and Scott-style state-space critiques without equating all non-state life with freedom. Sacred origins of property, police, and kingship (Hocart and others they cite) feed the sovereignty and knowledge poles.

Contrasts with [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model|Service]], [[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model|Fried]], Engels on [[state-formation]], and popular big-history state origins.

## Operationalizations

Case-by-case archaeological/historical diagnosis: which forms are present? G&W apply this across Mesopotamia, Egypt, Andes, Mesoamerica, Shang (as complication), and village-level bureaucracy origins (e.g. Tell Sabi Abyad, Ubaid "technologies of equality" as they read them).

## Applications

Central thesis of *Dawn* ch. 10; organizes critique of unitary [[state-formation]]; pairs with loss of [[three-basic-freedoms]] in ch. 11 / Conclusion (care–violence confusion; temple welfare and captive labour as cascading traps).

## Critiques and Limitations

- May understate how often the three forms co-occur in mutual reinforcement.
- "Charismatic politics" as *domination* can blur coercion and persuasion.
- Case assignments (Olmec, Teotihuacan, Inca, etc.) are G&W's readings of specialist literatures — disputed on [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].
