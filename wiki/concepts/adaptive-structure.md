# Adaptive Structure (and Its Disorders)

## Definition as Coined

In "Adaptive Structure and Its Disorders" ([[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]], *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*, 1979 — expansion of the second half of his 1977 Friedman-response essay), **adaptation** refers to processes through which living systems maintain **homeostasis** under short-term environmental fluctuation and, by transforming their own structures, under long-term nonreversing environmental change. **Living systems** include organisms and associations (families, clans, tribes, states, societies, ecosystems) that have distinct processes as units responding to perturbation.

**Adaptive structure** is the hierarchical organization of those processes — not mere stratification of power, but ordered relations along dimensions including specificity, response time, duration, reversibility, [[sanctity]], value, and meaning. Rappaport later identifies adaptiveness with **general systemic (structural) homeostasis** rather than with any single variable: if orderly adaptive structure is maintained, organic function, meaning, and meaningfulness are maintained.

**Maladaptations**, on this account, are **factors internal to systems** that interfere with homeostatic responses — anomalies of hierarchy and self-regulation — rather than exogenous stressors alone (though maladaptations can produce stress). Analogy: disease as "dis-ease" (Young & Rowley).

## Operationalizations

**Modes of self-regulation** (Rappaport): (1) **immutability** (e.g. creeds held eternally true); (2) **operations** (Piaget — reversible logical/mathematical); (3) **time-dependent** regulation (circadian, traffic lights, seasonal festivals); (4) **variable-dependent / cybernetic** mode.

**Romer's Rule** (as used): evolutionary/self-organizing changes in components function in the self-regulation of more inclusive systems — ask what a structural change *maintains unchanged*.

**Maladaptive anomaly types** (attributed inventory): cybernetic failure (detection/transmission/interpretation of signals); premature override / time aberrations; over-segregation; hyper-coherence; hierarchical maldistribution of organization; **usurpation / overspecification** (lower-order special purposes capture higher-order places); **oversanctification / idolatry** (Tillich + adaptive falsity — absolutizing the relative).

## Applications

- Egalitarian systems: ritual cycles as loci of regulation without discrete chiefs ([[ritual-regulation]]; Maring).
- **[[cybernetics-of-the-holy]]** (RRMH Ch. 13): closed loop of USP sanctification, regulation, well-being, and congregational re-acceptance — adaptive structure as religious-political cybernetics.
- Industrial/modern critique (POSITION, EMR + RRMH Ch. 14): oversanctification/idolatry; lies of oppression and diabolical lies; inversion of knowledge; law–meaning dissonance; money as Gnostic-adjacent fragmenting force; "post-modern science and natural religion" toward ecological [[logos-rappaport|Logos]].
- Normative **theory of correction** — graded responses from reversible correctives to USP replacement; revolution when milder responses fail.

## Critiques and Limitations

Teleology charges; political economy may find industrial critique under-specified; RRMH climax is programmatically religious as well as analytic (Hart foreword). Dual models still apply: cognized sanctity hierarchies misaligned with operational viability → maladaptive equilibration. See [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
