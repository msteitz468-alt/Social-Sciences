# Status Consistency (and Status Inconsistency)

## Definition as Coined

**Status consistency** (also status crystallization) is the degree to which an individual’s positions on multiple dimensions of stratification align. **Status inconsistency** is the mixed-rank pattern (e.g., high education, low ethnic status). [[lenski-gerhard|Lenski]] developed the construct in 1950s research and restates it in *[[lenski-power-and-privilege-1966|Power and Privilege]]* (Ch. 4; industrial applications in Ch. 12) as a structural property of multidimensional distributive systems.

## Semantic History

Moves stratification analysis beyond single hierarchies; later political-behavior research linked inconsistency to liberal/radical politics (Lenski and others — detail to be filled from primary articles when ingested). Distinct from Merton’s [[status-set]] (role theory) though compatible.

## Operationalizations

Correlation among rank dimensions; typology of consistent vs. inconsistent profiles. Industrial societies said to increase the *necessity* of multidimensional analysis as power bases decorrelate.

## Applications

- **1966 restatement (Ch. 4, 12):** industrial societies increase the *necessity* of multidimensional analysis as power bases decorrelate; status inconsistency linked (attributed) to liberal/radical political leanings relative to consistent high or low ranks.
- **Later citations:** frustration/revolution theories and political-behavior research (e.g. Coleman’s survey of rising-expectations / status-inconsistency accounts of revolt — see [[revolution]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

Measurement of “rank” across dimensions; causal claims from inconsistency to politics contested; whether inconsistency is a stable trait or a research artifact of chosen dimensions.
