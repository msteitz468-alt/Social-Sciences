# Vegetation Spirit

## Definition as Coined

Building on Wilhelm Mannhardt, [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]] treated much of European peasant custom and Mediterranean cult as evidence of **spirits of vegetation** — tree-spirits, corn-mothers, corn-spirits in animal form — whose life is annually killed and renewed in harvest and spring rites (*[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|Golden Bough]]* tree-worship chapters; ch. 28; ch. 45–49). Human or animal "representatives" of the corn-spirit may be slain or eaten ([[dying-and-rising-god]], eating the god). (Attributed.)

## Semantic History

Mannhardt → Frazer pipeline founded a major folklore-anthropology research program later dismantled or heavily revised by functionalist, historical, and structural approaches to agrarian ritual.

## Operationalizations

Last sheaf customs; animal embodiments of the corn; mock killing of harvest figures; identification of classical gods with crop cycles.

## Applications

Bridge between Nemi oak-king and Adonis/Attis/Osiris cycles; European fire-festivals and harvest folklore.

## Critiques and Limitations

Single-spirit monism for diverse agrarian rites; [[survivals]] circularity; quality of folklore reports. See [[frazer-golden-bough-study]], [[dying-and-rising-god]].
