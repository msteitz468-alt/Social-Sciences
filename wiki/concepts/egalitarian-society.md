# Egalitarian Society

## Definition as Coined

In [[fried-morton-h|Fried]]'s *[[fried-evolution-of-political-society-1967|The Evolution of Political Society]]* (1967), an **egalitarian society** is one in which there are **as many positions of prestige in any given age-sex grade as there are persons capable of filling them** — the number of valued statuses adjusts to the number of able persons. Stronger form: the society has **no means of fixing or limiting** who can exert power; as many as can wield power (strength, influence, authority) may do so, without necessity of a dominance order of paramountcy.

**Negative definition:** simple human societies lack both **ranking** (fewer valued statuses than capable persons) and **stratification** (differential rights of access to basic resources). “Egalitarian” is relative, not absolute identity — age, sex, and ephemeral ability differences remain; leveling mechanisms check permanent gaps.

## Semantic History

- **Political slogan heritage:** Fried notes the word’s programmatic/political coloration and retains it for relative absence of formal ranking and stratification.
- **Service band level:** partial overlap with [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model|Service]]’s band — familistic foraging organization — but Fried’s cut is status/resource access, not integrative sodalities. He adjudicates Steward vs Service on family-level / patrilineal / composite bands within the egalitarian chapter.
- **Later anthropology:** “egalitarian” becomes standard for many foraging societies; debates on gender hierarchy, delayed-return foragers, and storage societies re-open how “equal” the type is.

## Operationalizations

From EPS Chs. 2–3 (attributed):

| Dimension | Fried’s content |
|---|---|
| Status | Achieved and ascribed both present; status ≠ hierarchy; egocentric vs sociocentric (after Service) |
| Prestige | Symbolic/ideological; hunter success can yield prestige **without** political power (!Kung arrow ownership randomizes success) |
| Economy | Reciprocal; generalized reciprocity core (Sahlins continuum); balanced/negative peripheral; open access to basic productive resources |
| Law | **No law** in Fried’s working sense (vs Hoebel, Pospisil); Australian *djugururu* as ideology not validated law |
| Leadership | Authority, situational, transient; rejects state-as-family / *patria potestas*; !Kung “headman” as corporate charter not hierarchy |
| Conflict | Not over land; sex/labor roles major; rare escalation; no military command path to ranking/state |
| Habitat | Ethnographic cases often **marginal**; Pleistocene simple societies often were not — extrapolation hazard |

## Applications

Type-cases Fried inventories: Eskimo, !Kung, Australian groups (Murngin, Walbiri, Tiwi), Montagnais-Naskapi, Siriono, Shoshone/Paiute, Andamanese, Semang, Pygmies, Yahgan, Tehuelche, and others. Transition into human egalitarian society: **sharing** (with Sahlins/Service) as paramount invention versus primate non-sharing.

Among the "Pygmies," [[turnbull-colin-m|Turnbull]]'s [[mbuti|Mbuti]] ethnography ([[turnbull-forest-people-study|*The Forest People*]], 1957–58) supplies a much-cited concrete case: a net-hunting band with no chiefs, councils, or courts, maintaining order through **ridicule, shaming, and the threat of ostracism** rather than authority. The **Crime of Cephu** — a hunter who set his net ahead of the others to steal the communal catch, sanctioned by being called "Animal," denied a seat, denounced at the *kumamolimo*, and restored only on capitulating and sharing his meat — is a standard illustration of egalitarian levelling of a would-be free-rider (attributed to Turnbull's single-band fieldwork).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Marginal-habitat sampling of living cases.
- Contact composites ([[composite-hunting-band]]) and fur-trade “family hunting territories” (Leacock vs Speck) as secondary situations.
- Whether pure open access and absence of law hold under restudy.
- Gender and age inequalities may be under-weighted by the prestige-count definition.
- **Graeber & Wengrow (2021), attributed:** *[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|Dawn]]* argues that once stage theory answered the [[indigenous-critique]], "egalitarian" was emptied into a residual label for bottom-ladder societies no longer treated as equal political interlocutors; they prefer [[three-basic-freedoms]] and seasonal flexibility over equality-as-master-category. Also: egalitarian ethos can deny individual difference *or* celebrate quirks so thoroughly that ranking becomes inconceivable. Contested: [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].
