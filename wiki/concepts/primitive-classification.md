# Primitive Classification

## Definition as Coined

In « De quelques formes primitives de classification » (1903), [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] and [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]] analyse **primitive classification** as systems of classes that are hierarchical, totalizing, and (they argue) modeled on social morphology — not as the spontaneous work of the individual mind. They distinguish such moral/religious **[[symbolic-classification|symbolic classifications]]** from practical/technological schemes of distinction. Load-bearing claim (attributed): "The first logical categories were social categories; the first classes of things were classes of men… Moieties were the first genera; clans, the first species."

**Period language:** "Primitive" is the authors' evolutionary frame (1903); retain as historical title/term of art, not as a wiki-voice developmental stage.

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |
|---|---|
| Durkheim & Mauss 1903 | Hierarchical totalizing classifications continuous with science; social in form and origin |
| Commentators (Gehlke, Alpert) | Forerunner / initial formulation of *Elementary Forms* category thesis |
| Needham 1963 | Analytical object retained; causal thesis rejected; English title of the essay |
| Lévi-Strauss *La Pensée sauvage* (1962; Needham notes) | "Primitive classification" as central concern of structural anthropology — different theory of mind |
| Later symbolic classification studies | Comparative dualisms, right/left, correlative cosmologies without D&M causality |

## Operationalizations

- **D&M:** Map social divisions (moiety, marriage class, clan) onto divisions of things, space, time, rites; read kinship idioms of logical relation as evidence; construct evolutionary series simple → complex (Australia → Zuñi → China).
- **Needham residual program:** Structural concordance of symbolic and social orders by descent/alliance type; inventory of categories (Mauss); formal correspondence not required.
- **Not:** technological distinctions alone (water/air/ground animals as practical groups).

## Applications

- Founding case for sociological study of [[collective-representations|collective representations]] as knowledge structures ([[sociology-of-knowledge]]).
- Template for later dual symbolic classification and right/left research (via Hertz line; Needham).
- Bridge text between *Année* morphology and *[[durkheim-elementary-forms-1912|Elementary Forms]]* theory of categories.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Causal society → classification thesis: **contested** — see [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]] and [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]] Critiques (Needham: fallacious method, circularity, source misuse, unevidenced sentiment and evolution).
- Evolutionary "primitive → scientific" frame and colonial ethnography as evidence base.
- China case: authors themselves lack proof of clan/region historical link.
- Term "primitive classification" can reify a stage; better analytic label for ongoing work is often [[symbolic-classification]].
