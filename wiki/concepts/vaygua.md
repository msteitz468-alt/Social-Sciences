# Vaygu'a (vaygua)

## Definition as Coined

Trobriand / Massim **emic** term for **native valuables** — objects of parade and renown, not ordinary use-goods — as reported by [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] in *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]* (fieldwork 1915–18). Principal Kula classes: **mwali** (white shell armshells) and **soulava** (red spondylus-disc necklaces). Other permanent vaygu'a include stone blades, *kaloma* belts, *doga* pendants.

## Semantic History

| Moment | Sense |
|---|---|
| Massim emic (1915–18) | High valuables for display, ceremonial exchange, offerings to spirits |
| Malinowski 1922 Ch. XXII | Explicitly **not money/currency** — not medium of exchange or measure of value; "cumulative possession" via circulation |
| Later gift theory | Type of renown-object / inalienable or circulating valuable |

## Operationalizations

Tracked by direction of movement in the [[kula]]; partnership path ("whose hand?"); temporary holding; *tanarere* display; use in yolova offerings to spirits and in mortuary contexts. Value from renown, history of ownership, and competitive desire rather than utilitarian scarcity-within-use (Malinowski, attributed).

## Applications

- Core object-class of the [[kula]]
- Contrast class for [[gimwali]] utilities
- [[gift-exchange]] and economic-anthropology teaching
- Study: [[malinowski-argonauts-study]]; society: [[trobriand-islanders]]

## Critiques and Limitations

Later specialists debate gender of wealth and whether "not money" overstates differences from other store-of-value practices. Periodize to 1915–18 documentation.
