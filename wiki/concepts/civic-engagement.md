# Civic Engagement

Participation in public and community life — political, associational, religious, workplace, informal, and altruistic — treated as the empirical face of [[civic-social-capital]] in [[putnam-bowling-alone-study|*Bowling Alone*]].

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Putnam 2000.)* Putnam tracks multiple domains of Americans' involvement with politics, civic organizations, religion, workplace connections, informal socializing, altruism, and trust. The book's title image: more Americans bowl, but fewer bowl in leagues — parallel play without the social capital of organized association.

## Semantic History

Tocquevillian [[voluntary-association]] and democratic theory long predate Putnam; *Bowling Alone* made multi-indicator **decline** of civic engagement a late-century research program. Contested measurement and interpretation: [[bowling-alone-debate]].
