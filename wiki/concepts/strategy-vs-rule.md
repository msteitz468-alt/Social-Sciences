# Strategy vs Rule

*(Attributed — [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]].)* Analytic opposition at the center of [[practice-theory]]: regularities of social practice are not to be explained primarily as products of agents' obedience to **rules** (juridical, ethical, grammatical, or "unconscious" structural codes), but as products of **strategies** generated by [[habitus]] and [[practical-sense]] under temporal pressure — regulated improvisation oriented toward material and symbolic interests, often experienced as duty or feeling rather than calculation.

## Definition as Coined

In *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (Book II Introduction and kinship/matrimonial chapters), Bourdieu argues that objectifying the relationship to the object leads scientific practice to abandon overt or implicit **legalism** and the language of rules and rituals; the movement **from rule to strategy** is the same as that which situates analysis at the point of generation of practice, grasping it (with Marx) "as concrete human activity, as practice, in a subjective way."

A **rule**, in the usages he criticizes, may mean (and illicitly conflate):

1. a **statistical regularity** (what recurs with measurable frequency);
2. a **scientific model** that fits that regularity;
3. a **conscious norm** agents are said to follow.

Sliding from a model that *fits* to a rule that *guides* — or to unconscious regulating by a mysterious cerebral or social mechanism — is, he holds, the commonest way of "sliding from the model of reality to the reality of the model."

A **strategy**, in his strongest formulation, is **not** a consciously calculated "winning move" in chess or fencing, nor pure legal inventiveness bending rules. Superficially different family responses (e.g. Béarn succession manoeuvres; [[kabyle]] marriage choices) have their common origin in the habitus, product of structures it tends to reproduce. Their principle is "neither calculating reason nor the mechanical determinations of economic necessity, but the dispositions inculcated by the conditions of existence" — a socially constituted instinct that causes objectively calculable demands of an economy of practices to be experienced as duty or irresistible feeling. Agents often "obey the impulses of feeling or the injunctions of duty more than the calculations of interest" even when thereby conforming to the economy of the system of constraints.

Second-order strategies may still use the **vocabulary of the rule**: agents secure profits of conformity by disguising strategies and interests under the veil of obedience to the rule (officialization; "logical conformism").

## Semantic History

- **Legalism / jural anthropology** — kinship and custom treated as codes agents apply; Needham's questions on the language of law (as Bourdieu endorses them); Radcliffe-Brown–style jural rules.
- **Structuralist "rule"** — preferential vs prescriptive systems; Lévi-Strauss oscillation between norm language and model language; structuralist "unconscious" as naturalized finality.
- **Bourdieu's shift — the founding text is *[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline]]* (1972), ch. 1** ("The objective limits of objectivism"): the whole argument — "from the mechanics of the model to the dialectic of strategies," "the fallacies of the rule" — is stated here first and reworked in *Le sens pratique* (1980). *Outline*'s diagnosis of "rule" as an evasive omnibus term: "this hospitable notion, which can suggest at once the law constructed by science, the transcendent social norm and the immanent regularity of practices, enabled its user to escape from the dilemma of mechanism or finalism." *Outline* coins the official/practical-kinship distinction — "**It is practical kin who make marriages; it is official kin who celebrate them**" — and the tempo thesis, "**To abolish the interval is also to abolish strategy**." Kabyle parallel-cousin marriage figures as the test case against Lévi-Strauss's elementary structures ("a sort of scandal … only to those who have internalized the categories of thought which it disturbs").
- **Post-Bourdieusian uptake** — "strategy" sometimes re-read as rational-choice calculation (a reading Bourdieu explicitly rejects) or diluted to vague agency-talk; keep the *Logic* sense distinct.

## Operationalizations

How the distinction is used in analysis (attributed uses in *Logic*):

- Reconstruct the **system of principles** agents put into practice when identifying socio-logically matchable partners, rather than a pure algebra of marriage rules.
- Treat matrimonial, inheritance, fertility, and educational strategies as an inseparable set of **reproductive strategies** transmitting powers and privileges.
- Read land pre-emption, vengeance, and parallel-cousin obligation as honor- and interest-oriented practices, not as applications of *qanun* or custom as efficient cause.
- In gift and honour exchange: substitute the dialectic of strategies in time for the mechanics of the reciprocity cycle ([[gift-exchange]]).
- Flag social science's greatest use of rule-language precisely where it is most inadequate — social formations where objective conditions are constant and practices are largely entrusted to automatisms of the habitus.

## Applications

- **Kabyle kinship and marriage** — even extreme obligation of parallel-cousin marriage needs no ethical/juridical rule as explanatory principle; continuum from close to distant marriage as strategy space maximizing integration/security vs alliance/prestige ([[kabyle]]).
- **Béarn land and matrimonial strategies** — succession "rules" as legalistic vocabulary reducing family strategies; patrimony-protecting practices generated by habitus of the heir.
- **Gift and honour** — tempo, deferral, and uncertainty as strategic resources, not automatic rule-following.
- **Ritual** — practical schemes generating variants; official knowledge over-collected by legalist ethnography.
- **Debate stake** — central position in [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] (Bourdieusian path): neither structural determinism by rules/codes nor subjectivist voluntarism.

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Recorded as standing charges; not adjudicated here.)*

- **Residual determinism** — if strategies are products of habitus reproducing structures, critics ask how much "strategy" remains; Bourdieu answers via regulated improvisation and imperfect attunement, but the charge recurs.
- **Rational-choice misreading** — "strategy" invites economistic or game-theoretic reduction that *Logic* polemically rejects; careful attribution required.
- **Who sees the strategy?** — objectivist reconstruction of interests agents misrecognize raises the same authority problem as [[misrecognition]] generally.
- **Rule not abolished** — Bourdieu grants that conducts of honour are *also* experienced as obedience to rules or values; official norms and second-order conformity strategies remain part of the object. The target is making the rule the *principle of production* of practice.
- **Scope** — formulated against kinship anthropology and structuralism; extension to highly juridified modern fields requires the later field/capital apparatus (partly in *Logic*, fuller elsewhere — not all ingested).
