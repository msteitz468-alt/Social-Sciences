# Traveling Universals

## Definition as Coined

[[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Tsing]] (*[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]*, 2005) analyzes **universals** as practical projects that **travel** through difference. She writes of **engaged universals**: they take content and force in particular historical conjunctures; they mobilize partisans; they never fully fulfill their promise of sameness everywhere *(attributed)*. One can study universals not as truths or lies but as “sticky links.”

Key attributed moves:

- Postcolonial theory challenges scholars between traps of the universal and of cultural specificity — both colonial knowledge stratagems — yet social science has under-historicized the **production of the universal** in colonial encounter (invoking Spivak’s double bind: the universal as what we cannot not want).
- Environmental universals (science, nature, rights) can serve empire **and** liberation; Indonesian environmentalists used science/modernity languages against an anticommunist New Order state.
- Natural universals and global scale (Ch. 3): generalization in botany (Linnaeus), parks (Muir lineage), climate models, and failed ITTO-style regimes — how “nature” is made to speak globally.
- Collaboration packages (CBNRM, *masyarakat adat*/AMAN, charismatic North–South packages such as Chico→Chipko stories) travel as engaged universals with contingent African/Asian outcomes (e.g. CAMPFIRE debates recorded as positions).

## Semantic History

Related to [[north-atlantic-universals|Trouillot’s North Atlantic universals]] (particulars projecting themselves as standards) but not identical: Tsing emphasizes **travel, translation, engagement, and Indonesian cases** more than North Atlantic conceptual genealogy alone. Converses with postcolonial theory, STS, and critical development studies.

## Operationalizations

Cases of scientific/conservation universals, prosperity packages, freedom/rights languages, nature-loving, and donor community-management models in Indonesian forest politics.

## Applications

Anthropology of science and environment; globalization studies; method for studying universals ethnographically ([[ethnography-of-global-connection]]); critique of both naïve universalism and pure cultural relativism as analytic endpoints.

## Critiques and Limitations

“Universal” can blur moral universals, scientific generalizations, and policy packages — keep case types distinguished. Tsing does not reject all universal aspirations; she ethnographizes their sticky practical life.
