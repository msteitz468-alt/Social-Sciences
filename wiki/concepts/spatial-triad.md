# Spatial Triad

Lefebvre’s three dialectically related moments of social space in *[[lefebvre-production-of-space-1991|The Production of Space]]*. Core of the [[production-of-space]] program.

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Lefebvre 1974/1991.)*

| Moment | Labels | Register |
|---|---|---|
| **Spatial practice** | *pratique spatiale* — **perceived** | Production and reproduction; locations and spatial sets of each social formation; ensures continuity and cohesion; competence/performance relative to social space. “The spatial practice of a society secretes that society's space… it produces it slowly and surely as it masters and appropriates it.” Under neocapitalism: daily routine associated with urban routes/networks despite extreme separation of places. |
| **Representations of space** | *représentations de l'espace* — **conceived** | Conceptualized space of scientists, planners, urbanists, technocratic subdividers, social engineers; tied to relations of production and the order they impose; knowledge, signs, codes. “This is the dominant space in any society (or mode of production).” Tends to verbal sign systems. |
| **Representational spaces** | *espaces de représentation* — **lived** | Space directly lived through images and symbols; inhabitants and users; clandestine/underground side of social life and art. “This is the dominated — and hence passively experienced — space which the imagination seeks to change and appropriate.” “Representational space is alive: it speaks.” Affective kernel: Ego, bed, house; square, church, graveyard. |

> “The perceived-conceived-lived triad (in spatial terms: spatial practice, representations of space, representational spaces) loses all force if it is treated as an abstract 'model'.”

## Semantic History

Canonical statement: *La production de l'espace* (1974); anglophone uptake after Nicholson-Smith 1991. Later geography shortens to **perceived / conceived / lived**. Related to phenomenological lived body but tied to production and mode-of-production analysis. **Not** Bourdieu’s [[social-space]].

## Operationalizations

Interpretive framework for plans vs everyday use; architecture as conceived vs users’ lived space (architects inhabit graphic blueprints; users’ space is concrete, childhood-originated). Risk: checklist application that loses dialectics.

## Applications

Charter tool of [[production-of-space]]; medieval illustration (routes / Ptolemaic-Christian cosmology / village church and pilgrimage); Renaissance perspective as representation of space dominating religious representational space; critique of Le Corbusier and expert visual space.

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundary ambiguity; translation disputes; “lived space” romantic residual; hard to code in surveys without reducing to three boxes.
