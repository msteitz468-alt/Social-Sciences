# Reconversion Strategies

Bourdieu's term in *[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]* for the practices by which agents and class fractions **convert, reinvest, or redeploy forms of capital** when the structure of [[social-space|social space]] shifts (credential inflation, sectoral change, morphological growth or decline of occupations).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, Distinction, Part II.)* Reproduction strategies form a systematic set; reconversion transforms asset structure. Movements in two-dimensional social space may be **vertical** (same capital composition, change in volume) or **transverse** (change in composition — e.g., trading economic for cultural capital via schooling). Declining classes may escape collective decline via occupational reconversion; diploma inflation triggers intensified educational investment and competitive displacement (“change to conserve”).

## Semantic History

Extends educational-mobility concerns of *The Inheritors* / *Reproduction* into a general theory of capital conversion under structural change. Related to hysteresis (habitus attuned to earlier conditions — “Don Quixote effect”) when reconversion lags.

## Operationalizations

French morphological change c. 1954–1975 (schooling boom, growth of culture-intensive crafts and new petite bourgeoisie); fertility × mobility tables; educational strategies by class fraction; occupational category shifts.

## Applications

Explains new middle-class growth, teacher/cultural-intermediary trajectories, and threatened traditional petite-bourgeois reaction. Portable to other credential societies with care about period.

## Critiques and Limitations

Conversion “rates” are themselves stakes of struggle (not fixed prices); strategy language can sound more intentional than habitus allows; requires good historical occupational data.
