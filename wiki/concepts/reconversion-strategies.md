# Reconversion Strategies

Bourdieu's term in *[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]* for the practices by which agents and class fractions **convert, reinvest, or redeploy forms of capital** when the structure of [[social-space|social space]] shifts (credential inflation, sectoral change, morphological growth or decline of occupations).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Bourdieu, Distinction, Part II.)* Reproduction strategies form a systematic set; reconversion transforms asset structure. Movements in two-dimensional social space may be **vertical** (same capital composition, change in volume) or **transverse** (change in composition — e.g., trading economic for cultural capital via schooling). Declining classes may escape collective decline via occupational reconversion; diploma inflation triggers intensified educational investment and competitive displacement (“change to conserve”).

## Semantic History

Extends educational-mobility concerns of *The Inheritors* / *Reproduction* into a general theory of capital conversion under structural change. Related to hysteresis (habitus attuned to earlier conditions — “Don Quixote effect”) when reconversion lags.

*[[bourdieu-state-nobility-1996|The State Nobility]]* (1989) folds reconversion into the full
system of [[modes-of-reproduction|reproduction strategies]] and extends it to elite formation
and the [[field-of-power]] (attributed — Bourdieu):

- **Sanctuary schools** (new management schools, INSEAD-type institutions, lesser business
  schools) absorb bourgeois demand displaced by intensified academic competition — "roundabout
  routes" that convert academic failure into rehabilitated, marketable capital.
- Conversion pressures are "subjectively experienced as 'changes of taste or vocation.'"
- Reconversion is distinguished from mere mobility: it is a **structural shift** in capital
  composition (displacements *between* fields via capital conversion), not only vertical
  movement within a field; both depend on the "exchange rate" among capitals, which is itself a
  stake of struggle in the field of power.
- The school-mediated mode's *numerus clausus* produces overproduction of "bourgeois-rights"
  holders and "catch-up" reconversion strategies (May 1968 as collective subversion of the
  educational institution is Bourdieu's example).

## Operationalizations

French morphological change c. 1954–1975 (schooling boom, growth of culture-intensive crafts and
new petite bourgeoisie); fertility × mobility tables; educational strategies by class fraction;
occupational category shifts. Elite-scale: sanctuary-school recruitment and HEC/ENA/management-
school expansion as reconversion channels for the business bourgeoisie (*State Nobility*,
1960s–80s French data).

## Applications

Explains new middle-class growth, teacher/cultural-intermediary trajectories, and threatened traditional petite-bourgeois reaction. Portable to other credential societies with care about period.

## Critiques and Limitations

Conversion “rates” are themselves stakes of struggle (not fixed prices); strategy language can sound more intentional than habitus allows; requires good historical occupational data.
