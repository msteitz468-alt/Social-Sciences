# Continental Axis (Diamond)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[diamond-guns-germs-steel-1997|Guns, Germs, and Steel]]* Ch. 10 (“Spacious Skies and Tilted Axes”), [[diamond-jared-m|Diamond]] argues that **continental orientation** shapes historical trajectories: **east–west axes** (Eurasia) allow faster spread of domesticates and technologies across similar day-length and climate bands; **north–south axes** (Americas, Africa) force packages to cross latitudinal climate belts, slowing or blocking diffusion. All claims **attributed to Diamond 1997**.

## Semantic History

Geographic shape of continents is an old map fact; Diamond’s move is to make **axis orientation** a major **ultimate** cause of differential food-production spread and, downstream, of unequal power packages. Contested as over-simple physical geography — see [[guns-germs-steel-geographic-determinism-debate]].

## Operationalizations

| Axis type | Exemplar | Diamond’s claimed effect |
|---|---|---|
| East–west | Eurasia | Rapid Fertile Crescent package spread to Europe/Asia |
| North–south | Americas, Africa | Slow crop/livestock/tech transfer; repeated independent domestication |

Also linked to “preemptive domestication” (rapid spread prevents independent re-domestication of same wild ancestors).

## Applications

Epilogue factor set (2): rates of intracontinental diffusion. Used to explain Eurasian lead vs American/African lags in GGS synthesis.

## Critiques and Limitations

Specialists note many non-axis barriers (mountains, deserts, cultural borders) and counter-examples of rapid north–south transfer; the mechanism is a **thesis**, not measured law. Does not replace case-level archaeology of adoption.
