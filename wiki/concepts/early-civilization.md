# Early Civilization

## Definition as Coined

In *[[trigger-understanding-early-civilizations-2003|Understanding Early Civilizations]]* (2003), [[trigger-bruce-g|Bruce G. Trigger]] summarily defines **early civilization** as **the earliest and simplest form of class-based society** — a natural comparative category (not a Weberian ideal type) within which similarities and differences can be studied. Class displaces kinship as the principal organizer of social relations; states are present; writing is neither necessary nor sufficient. All definitional and comparative claims on this page from Trigger 2003 are **attributed**.

## Semantic History

"Civilization" has a long Enlightenment-to-Victorian career (see [[civilization]], [[savagery-barbarism-civilization]]). Trigger’s **early civilization** is a late-twentieth-century comparative-archaeological narrowing: not civilization as moral progress, but a stage of sociopolitical complexity (class + state) short of later preindustrial empires and industrial forms. Related vocabularies include [[pristine-state]], [[archaic-stage]], Childe’s [[urban-revolution]], and Fried’s [[stratified-society]] → state path. Trigger explicitly rejects Steward’s arid-irrigation unilinear “nuclear” model as the most influential and most pernicious comparative frame for the category.

## Operationalizations

Trigger operationalizes the category via seven best-documented cases as of ~2001:

| Case | Region | Political form (Trigger) |
|---|---|---|
| Egypt | north-africa | [[territorial-state]] |
| Mesopotamia | mesopotamia | [[city-state]] system |
| Shang China | china | (treated in his typology; territorial leanings in synthesis) |
| Valley of Mexico / Aztecs | mesoamerica | city-state system + hegemonic tributary overlay |
| Classic Maya | mesoamerica | city-state system |
| Inka | andes | territorial state |
| Yoruba | west-africa | city-state system |

## Applications

- Organizes Trigger’s 2003 comparison of sociopolitical, economic, and cognitive institutions.
- Anchors wiki links for [[kingship]], [[wealth-appropriation]], [[state-formation]], [[urbanization]].
- Supplies the structural formula Trigger concludes with: roughly **one class structure · two political forms · one key religious set**, with economy and personal values freer to vary.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Evolutionary framing attracts relativist critique (which Trigger addresses in ch. 3).
- Case set and ~2001 evidence horizon limit generalizability; later primary-state literature multiplies pathways and revises case detail.
- Binary city/territorial typology may under-differentiate federations and intermediate forms.
- “Early civilization” remains an etic analytical construct — not an emic self-designation of the peoples compared.
