# Faceless Commitments

## Definition as Coined

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (1990) distinguished:

| Type | Definition (attributed) |
|---|---|
| **Facework commitments** | Trust relations sustained by or expressed in social connections established in circumstances of **co-presence** |
| **Faceless commitments** | Development of **faith** in [[symbolic-tokens]] or [[expert-systems]] ([[abstract-systems]]) |

Trust in systems takes the form of faceless commitments: faith sustained in knowledge of which the lay person is largely ignorant. Faceless commitments are linked *ambiguously* with facework — neither simply replacing personal trust nor floating free of it.

## Semantic History

Part of Giddens’s reworking of trust against purely interpersonal or purely rational-choice models (contrast [[trust|Coleman’s bet-structure trust]] on the same concept page’s multi-tradition structure).

## Operationalizations

Theoretical dual. Basic facework with strangers in modernity includes Goffman’s **civil inattention** (trust as "background noise").

## Applications

- Structural condition of extended [[time-space-distanciation]].
- Explains coexistence of anonymous settings and intensive personal "relationships" projects.
- Access points mediate the two forms of commitment.

## Critiques and Limitations

Binary may oversimplify hybrid trust (platforms, brands, influencers). Keep as Giddens’s analytical cut, not exhaustive typology of all trust.
