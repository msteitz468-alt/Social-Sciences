# Kula

Emic institution of the Massim (eastern New Guinea), documented by [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] in *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts of the Western Pacific]]* (fieldwork 1915–18; published 1922). The term is **emic** (Boyowan / Massim); Malinowski's analytic construction of the Kula as a closed ring is **etic** synthesis — he argued no native held the integral map (Ch. III).

## Definition as Coined

As Malinowski defined it (Ch. III): a form of **extensive inter-tribal exchange** on a **closed circuit** of islands. Two kinds of articles only travel in opposite directions: **soulava** (long necklaces of red spondylus shell) one way; **mwali** (white shell armshells) the other. Partners exchange, hold briefly, and pass on — "once in the Kula, always in the Kula." Associated: canoe-building and sailing; *mwasila* magic; mythology; mortuary distributions; and ordinary [[gimwali|gimwali]] trade under ceremonial cover.

**Types** (Ch. XIX): overseas Kula between distant communities; Kula between contiguous communities; inland Kula within a Kula community. **Uvalaku**: big competitive ceremonial expeditions (vs *Kula wala*, ordinary smaller sailings); outbound uvalaku carries no vaygu'a.

## Operationalizations

Partnership rules (*karayta'u*); geographic direction of valuables; ownership as temporary trusteeship; solicitory gifts (*pokala*, *kaributu*, *basi*); inland vs overseas; uvalaku ceremonial; *tanarere* display of results; association with so'i mortuary feasts and gushes of valuables.

## Applications

- Founding case for [[gift-exchange]] and substantivist economic anthropology ([[formalist-substantivist-debate]])
- Classic teaching case for [[participant-observation]] ethnography of a complex institution
- Society base: [[trobriand-islanders]] and Massim partners
- Study: [[malinowski-argonauts-study]]

## Critiques and Limitations

- Single-ethnographer reconstruction of a multi-island system; SE arc less observed
- Later Massim work revises gender, scale, and politics of valuables — [[weiner-annette-b|Weiner]] (1976) reframes kula as men's pursuit of ephemeral individual **renown**, the counterpart to women's enduring [[reproduction-regeneration|regenerative]] value ([[weiner-women-of-value-study]]); the wider SE-arc revision still not ingested
- Colonial disruption of chieftainship already noted by Malinowski (Ch. XIX)
