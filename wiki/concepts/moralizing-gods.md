# Moralizing Gods

## Definition as Coined

In CSR/cultural-evolution literature (White 2021 ch. 8): **moralizing gods** are supernatural agents represented as monitoring and caring about human moral behavior — central to accounts of the rise of large-scale **prosocial religions** (Norenzayan and colleagues).

## Semantic History

Competes with pure by-product accounts that treat god concepts as MCI/ToM packages without cooperation function; Baumard & Boyer "moral religions" strand also in White.

## Operationalizations

Cross-cultural databases; economic games; historical timing of moralizing deities vs social complexity (debated).

## Applications

Explains (attributed) correlation of certain god concepts with anonymous large-scale societies; links to [[costly-signaling]] and credibility-enhancing displays (CREDs).

## Critiques and Limitations

Causal direction contested (gods cause complexity vs complexity produces moralizing gods); small-scale societies also have moral norms without Big Gods; WEIRD and historical sampling issues.
