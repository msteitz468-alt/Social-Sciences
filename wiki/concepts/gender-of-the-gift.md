# Gender of the Gift

## Definition as Coined

Strathern's program question (preface and throughout *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]*):

> "To ask about the gender of the gift, then, is to ask about the situation of gift exchange in relation to the form that domination takes in these societies. It is also to ask about the 'gender' of analytical concepts, the worlds that particular assumptions sustain."

Corollaries she states: one cannot read gender off objects in advance, **not even when women appear as the items gifted**; "the action is the gendered activity." Conventional anthropology treated gift exchange as gender-neutral control of assets; she argues Melanesian gift behavior is itself gendered, with power in how gendering enables some persons to transact at others' expense.

## Semantic History

Title concept for the 1988 book; folds feminist gender analysis into Maussian gift theory and Melanesian ethnography. Distinct from Du Bois's [[the-veil]] or generic "gender gift" phrasing.

## Operationalizations

Who can transact what; male/female classification of valuables; marriage movements; women's wealth; domination as particular excess acts ([[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study]]).

## Applications

Study page and hub; [[feminist-anthropology]]; post-Mauss [[gift-exchange]].

## Critiques and Limitations

Book-length construct — cite the study, not free-standing universal law of gifts.
