# Organic Composition of Capital

## Definition as Coined

In *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), Chapter 25, [[marx-karl|Marx]] defines the **composition of capital** in two senses:

1. **Value-composition** — ratio of [[constant-and-variable-capital|constant capital (c)]] to variable capital (v).
2. **Technical composition** — ratio of mass of means of production to mass of living labour that sets them in motion.

He calls the value-composition **organic composition** insofar as it is determined by the technical composition and mirrors changes in the latter. “Wherever I refer to the composition of capital, without further qualification, its organic composition is always understood.”

**Attributed to Marx.**

## Semantic History

| Context | Sense |

## Operationalizations

- Accumulation with rising productivity tends to raise c relative to v (relative diminution of the variable part).
- **Concentration** of capital (growth of individual capitals with accumulation) vs **centralisation** (expropriation of capitalist by capitalist; competition and credit) — both reshape composition and scale.
- Bridge to production of a relative surplus population (→ [[industrial-reserve-army]]).

## Applications

- Core of the [[general-law-of-capitalist-accumulation]].
- Machinery chapters (Part 4) supply the technical mechanism that raises composition.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Empirical measurement of “organic” composition is disputed.
- Link to long-run profit rate is incomplete without later volumes.
