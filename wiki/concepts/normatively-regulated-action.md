# Normatively Regulated Action

## Definition as Coined

**Normatively regulated action** is [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]'s (attributed, *[[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|TCA Vol. 1]]*) sociological action concept for members of a group who orient their action to **common values** and act in compliance with (or deviation from) **norms** that establish legitimate interpersonal relations. The corresponding world-relation is the **social world**; the corresponding validity aspect is **normative rightness**.

Habermas locates the concept in the [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]–[[parsons-talcott|Parsons]] line (normative integration) as one of four one-sided concepts that [[communicative-action]] integrates by carrying truth, rightness, and truthfulness at once.

## Semantic History

Drawn from classical sociology of norms and roles; in TCA it is analytically purified as a model that privileges the social world, just as teleological action privileges the objective world and dramaturgical action the subjective.

## Operationalizations

Role compliance, legitimate order, internalization of norms — the standard structural-functional toolkit, reframed as one validity aspect among three.

## Applications

Intermediate step toward communicative action; later connected (Vol. 2) to the normative background of the [[lifeworld]] and the [[linguistification-of-the-sacred]].

## Critiques and Limitations

As a stand-alone model it cannot handle strategic action or cognitive truth claims; Habermas treats it as incomplete rather than false.
