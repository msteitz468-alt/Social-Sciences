# Paretian social equilibrium

## Definition as Coined

In *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* Ch XII, [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] treated chosen social elements as a **social system** (§2066) and defined the **state of equilibrium** (label *X*) as a state such that if it is “artificially subjected to some modification different from the modification it undergoes normally, a reaction at once takes place tending to restore it to its real, its normal, state” (§§2067–2068). Without that peculiarity, form would be “a mere matter of chance.”

“Equilibrium” is a **label** borrowed from mechanics, not a sentimental or metaphysical term; meaning is fixed by his definitions (§2076). Successive states *X1, X2…* at intervals when considered elements have completed their action form the line of equilibrium (§§2069–2070).

## Semantic History

Rooted in Pareto’s engineering dissertation and pure-economics equilibrium (*Manuale*). Extended from economic molecules (tastes + obstacles) to social molecules harbouring [[residues]], [[derivations]], interests, and proclivities (§2079). Ch XIII applies Class I/II proportions and historical undulations as equilibrium factors.

## Operationalizations

- **Element groups (§2060):** (1) soil/climate/flora/fauna/geology; (2) external in space (other societies) and time (prior situations); (3) internal — race, residues, proclivities, interests, knowledge, derivations.
- **Interdependence** among elements; anti-dissolution forces in enduring societies (§2061).
- **Quantitative ideal:** equations equal unknowns — practically insuperable in full; useful for local properties (§2062).
- **Residues as indirect account** of many environmental influences (§§2063–2064).
- **Composition of forces:** real movement follows residue-force resultant, not derivation resultant (§§2087–2088).
- **Dependence first type** (direct among residue groups) vs **second type** (indirect via maintenance of equilibrium) — second type linked to rhythmical social movements (§§2088–2090).
- **Cycles of interdependence** among residues, interests, derivations, and heterogeneity/class-circulation (Ch XII).
- **Utility composition:** utilities of/for individuals and communities; heterogeneous utilities; maxima (Ch XII) — kept distinct from experimental truth ([[truth-utility-distinction]]).

## Applications

- Framework for [[circulation-of-elites]], force/cunning regimes, protectionism’s mediate effects, S/R composition.
- Historical waves: crystallization vs free initiative as successive equilibrium paths (Ch XIII).
- Methodological caution: derivations’ LE validity “usually has little to do with their influence on the social equilibrium” (§2086).

## Critiques and Limitations

- Mechanical metaphor criticized as reification or static bias (despite dynamic *X1, X2…* language).
- Unmeasured residue “indices” limit empirical test.
- Europe/Mediterranean confinement (§2065).
