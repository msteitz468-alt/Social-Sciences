# Working-Class Authoritarianism

## Definition as Coined

[[lipset-seymour-martin|Seymour Martin Lipset]] (*[[lipset-political-man-1960|Political Man]]*, Ch IV, 1960) argued that **extremist and intolerant movements in modern society are more likely to be based on the lower classes than on the middle and upper classes**. "Authoritarianism" here names predispositions — preference for simple solutions, intolerance of difference, susceptibility to demagogic and extremist appeals — that Lipset attributed disproportionately to lower-class social situations, not to a fixed psychological type alone. See study [[lipset-political-man-study]]; debate [[working-class-authoritarianism-debate]].

## Semantic History

Lipset's chapter synthesizes survey findings (including authoritarian-personality research such as the F-scale tradition), historical patterns of lower-class support for communism and fascism-related movements, and observations on fundamentalist religion. The label "working-class authoritarianism" became a standard (and contested) term in political sociology and political psychology. Later usage sometimes collapses Lipset's structural account into pure personality psychology or into a simple "workers vote extremist" claim he himself qualified by country and by democratic norms (e.g. Britain).

## Operationalizations

Lipset links lower-class position to: low education; economic and psychological insecurity; isolation from heterogeneous communication; family and workplace authority patterns; concrete rather than abstract cognitive style. Indicators include party/movement support, tolerance items, and religious extremism. Critics stress education as confound, measurement of "class," and selection on available mid-century surveys.

## Applications

- Comparative support bases of communism, fascism, and populist extremism (*Political Man* Chs IV–V).
- Conditions under which lower-class democratic loyalty is strengthened (unions, parties, education, economic security).
- Contrast with middle-class fascism and status-anxiety accounts (Ch V).

## Critiques and Limitations

Live dispute: [[working-class-authoritarianism-debate]]. Main lines of criticism (positions): ecological and educational confounds; romanticization vs pathologization of workers; later evidence of right-wing authoritarianism across classes; historical contingency of 1930s–50s cases.
