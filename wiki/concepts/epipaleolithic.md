# Epipaleolithic

## Definition as Coined

**Epipaleolithic** is the regional period/industry label used in SW Asian prehistory for late Pleistocene microlithic hunter-gatherer cultures preceding the Neolithic — roughly the Levantine counterpart to European “Mesolithic,” though not identical in date or content. Major southern Levantine entities include Kebaran, Geometric Kebaran, and [[natufian-culture|Natufian]]; arid zones add Mushabian, Ramonian, and others (Goring-Morris tradition).

## Semantic History

Adopted to avoid forcing European Mesolithic terminology onto Near Eastern sequences with different chronology and early sedentism. Culture-historical subdivision (microlith typology) remains the primary sorting language even after radiocarbon and behavioral approaches densified the record (Bar-Yosef; Henry; Goring-Morris & Belfer-Cohen).

## Operationalizations

Microlith typology (non-geometric vs geometric; lunates), technology (bladelet production systems), settlement pattern, and absolute dates. Boundaries (e.g., Geometric Kebaran → Early Natufian) are contested where admixture and sloping occupations confuse assemblages (el-Wad Layer B; Hayonim terrace).

## Applications

- Framework for [[natufian-culture]] as terminal Epipaleolithic.
- Multi-entity maps of core vs periphery (Goring-Morris & Belfer-Cohen 2013).

## Critiques and Limitations

Etic industry labels ≠ peoples; risk of equating tool style with ethnicity or language; radiocarbon gaps and site-formation issues limit phase purity.
