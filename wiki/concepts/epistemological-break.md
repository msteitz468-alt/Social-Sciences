# Epistemological Break (*coupure épistémologique*)

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Brewster Glossary summarizing Althusser; Althusser’s application in *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*.)*

The **epistemological break** is a concept introduced by **Gaston Bachelard** for the leap from the pre-scientific world of ideas to the scientific world — a radical break with the whole pattern and frame of reference of pre-scientific (ideological) notions and the construction of a new pattern ([[problematic]]). Related usages appear in history-of-ideas work by Georges Canguilhem and (transformed) Michel Foucault (Althusser’s Letter to the Translator).

**Althusser’s application to Marx:** Marx’s rejection of the Hegelian and Feuerbachian ideology of his youth and the construction of the basic concepts of dialectical and historical materialism in the mature works. In *For Marx* the break is placed at **1845** (*The German Ideology* / *Theses on Feuerbach*), not at 1843 (against Della Volpe/Colletti) and not as a continuous “maturation” of the young Marx into *Capital*.

> In 1845, Marx broke radically with every theory that based history and politics on an essence of man. … This rupture with every philosophical anthropology or humanism is no secondary detail; it is Marx’s scientific discovery.

The unique rupture (Althusser) has three indissociable elements: (1) new concepts (social formation, productive forces, relations of production, superstructure, ideologies, determination last instance, etc.); (2) radical critique of theoretical pretensions of every philosophical humanism; (3) definition of humanism as ideology.

## Operationalizations

A historiographic and theoretical **periodization rule**, not a measurement: identify when a discourse replaces an ideological problematic with a new conceptual system. Althusser’s test for Marx is the abandonment of essence-of-man anthropology for concepts of [[social-formation]], [[mode-of-production]], etc.

## Applications

- Charter for reading *Capital* as science of history, not as ethics or philosophical anthropology.
- Underwrites [[theoretical-anti-humanism]] and the demotion of [[alienation]] as key concept of Marxism.
- Anthropological [[structural-marxism]] inherits the break vocabulary when reclassifying precapitalist formations as articulated modes of production.

## Critiques and Limitations

*(Attributed / position recording.)*

- **Continuist / humanist readers** of the Early Works treat the break as artificial surgery that discards Marx’s critical-emancipatory core.
- **Historicists** reject the science/ideology dualism as positivist residue (Althusser himself later flags a “positivist half-silence” on philosophy ≠ science).
- **Empirical historiography of Marx’s texts** often finds more gradual conceptual change than a single 1845 event — the break is a **theoretical thesis**, not an uncontested archival finding.
- Do not confuse with Kuhnian “paradigm shift” without argument; Althusser’s lineage is Bachelardian.
