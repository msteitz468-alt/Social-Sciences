# Residues (Pareto)

## Definition as Coined

In *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]*, [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] named the relatively constant element *a* in social theories and conduct **residues**, the variable element *b* **[[derivations]]**, and *c* **derivatives** — stressing that nothing follows from etymology; the labels mean only *a*, *b*, *c* (§868). Livingston’s note: a residue is “what is left” after eliminating variables; roughly “principle underlying a non-logical action or reasoning.”

Residues **correspond to** certain instincts/sentiments but **are not** those instincts: they are **manifestations** of sentiments, “just as the rising of the mercury in a thermometer is a manifestation of the rise in temperature” (§875). They do not exhaust appetites, tastes, or especially **interests**, which remain separate social forces (§§851–854).

## Operationalizations

**Six classes** (§888 table):

| Class | Name | Detail pages / notes |
|---|---|---|
| I | Instinct for combinations | [[combination-instinct]] — progressive, inventive |
| II | Group-persistences (persistence of aggregates) | [[group-persistence]] — conservative cement |
| III | Need of expressing sentiments by external acts (activity) | Genera: combinations of acts; religious ecstasies (Ch VII) |
| IV | Residues connected with sociality | Uniformity, pity, self-sacrifice, hierarchy, asceticism (Ch VII) |
| V | Integrity of the individual and appurtenances | Equilibrium resistance, equality-as-privilege, restoration/vengeance (Ch VIII) |
| VI | The sex residue | Sex as social residue ≠ mere appetite; compounds with religion/asceticism (Ch VIII) — attributed THEORY only |

Pareto held residues are indefinite vs scientific principles *A* (§§870–874). Analytic isolation, he argued, is convenience; individuals act by composite sentiment groups (§876). Derivatives can themselves become residues of further phenomena (§877).

## Applications

- Explaining constant cores under variable myths, theologies, and political doctrines (purification/baptism, weather-magic, “Progress,” “Democracy”).
- Ranking causal weight: residues > derivations for equilibrium (Ch IX §1402; Ch XII §§2081–2085).
- Elite composition: Class I vs II dosage in ruling/subject classes → [[circulation-of-elites]], [[lions-and-foxes]].
- Policy implication (Ch XI): exploit residues; attacking manifestations may strengthen them; efforts to modify residues often fail.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Taxonomy criticized as unfalsifiable redescription.
- Class VI and other sensitive material must stay dated and attributed.
- Interests deliberately left outside residues — incomplete if read as full psychology of action.
- “Thermometer” metaphor: residues indicate sentiments but Pareto still speaks elliptically of residues determining equilibrium.
