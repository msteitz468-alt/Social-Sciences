# Bonapartism

## Definition as Coined

**Bonapartism**, as analyzed in [[marx-karl|Marx]]’s *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|Eighteenth Brumaire]]* (1852) — Marx does not always use the later school term, but the case of Louis Bonaparte is its classic source — is the form of rule in which the **executive [[the-state|state]]** rises above the contending classes and parliamentary institutions, personified by a leader who presents himself as savior of “Order,” while still resting on determinate class bases (especially the allotment [[peasantry-as-class|peasantry]] and the [[lumpenproletariat|slum-proletariat]] / Society of December 10). Immediate result of 2 December 1851: “triumph of Bonaparte over parliament, of the Executive over the Legislative power, of force without phrases over the force of phrases.”

## Semantic History

From the analysis of Louis-Napoléon to a general category in Marxist and political-sociology vocabularies for authoritarian executives that claim to stand above class while securing bourgeois property or social order. Later usages (Second Empire historiography; twentieth-century “Bonapartist” regimes) stretch the term; this page anchors the **1852 Marxian case**. Near-neighbor without identity: Weber’s [[plebiscitary-leader-democracy]] (leader + machine + mass acclamation) — different theory of legitimacy, overlapping problem-space.

## Operationalizations

In the *Brumaire* (as Marx reports):

- Personalization of “Order” after June 1849; Society of December 10 as organized slum-proletariat base.
- Bourgeois cry “Rather an End With Fright, Than a Fright Without End”; industrial bourgeoisie’s Circus “Bravo” to the coming coup.
- Coup technique: bank funds, military bribes, night arrests, restored universal suffrage decrees.
- State machine “fully independent” under the second Bonaparte — qualified: “does not float in the air.”

## Applications

- [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]] and [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state-debate]].
- Political sociology of coups, military republics, and “saviors of society.”
- Class analysis of personal rule ([[class-fraction]], [[party-of-order]] collapse).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Case vs. type:** whether 1851 France generalizes is contested.
- **Agency of the adventurer:** Marx stresses structural necessity of the coup’s success *and* ridicules Bonaparte’s farcical methods — tension recorded, not resolved here.
- **Gendered/classed polemic:** “Crapulinsky,” “adventurer,” “three swindlers” are polemical POSITION language, not neutral description.
