# Fractal Person

## Definition as Coined

The **fractal person** is [[wagner-roy|Roy Wagner]]’s image for a person who is **never a unit standing in relation to an aggregate**, but **always an entity with relationship integrally implied** — self-similar relational form at every scale rather than individual-plus-society hierarchy. *(Attributed — Wagner’s formulation as received in this vault and in Strathern’s acknowledgement.)*

## Semantic History

| Strand | Content |

| Strathern reception | *[[strathern-partial-connections-1991|Partial Connections]]* shamelessly appropriates the provocation **as imagery without mathematical apprehension**; 2004 Preface: fractals as “numerical metaphors,” not math; parallel Cantor-dust / self-scaling book form |

**Not the same as** [[fractal-recursivity]] (linguistic-anthropology label for scale-invariant language ideology patterns) — different literature, different load-bearing claims.

## Operationalizations

Heuristic for Melanesianist personhood and for method: person/relationship form recurs at “part” and “whole” scales so that cutting a relation still leaves person-form (remainder), not a leftover fragment of a prior complete individual.

## Applications

- Wagner–Strathern Melanesianist dialogue ([[strathern-partial-connections-study]], [[wagner-roy]]).
- Companion to [[dividual]] (*Gender of the Gift*) without identity: dividual = composite of relations; fractal person = self-scaling relational entity image.
- Reception pages that treat Melanesian personhood as impossible twin to Amazonian perspectivism.
