# Publicity (*Publizität* / critical vs manufactured)

In Habermas, **publicity** names both the principle that political domination should be subject to public critical reason (Kantian bridging principle between politics and morality) and, in its transformed form, the attribute of whatever attracts public attention through advertising and public relations.

## Definition as Coined

STPS tracks the shift from publicity as a *function of public opinion* to publicity as an *attribute of objects* staged for the public — “publicity work,” manufactured acclamation.

## Semantic History

Kant *Publizität*; bourgeois press publicity; 20th-c. PR and mass-media publicity. Habermas’s normative charge attaches to the former.

## Operationalizations

Institutional requirements of open debate vs metrics of media exposure and campaign publicity.

## Applications

Press law, parliamentary publicity, advertising-state relations, critique of plebiscitary mass democracy.

## Critiques and Limitations

Whether critical publicity was ever more than ideology; digital publicity forms.
