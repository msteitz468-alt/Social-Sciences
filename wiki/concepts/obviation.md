# Obviation

[[wagner-roy|Roy Wagner]]'s term for the effect of differentiating (inventive) symbolization; introduced in *[[wagner-invention-of-culture-1975|The Invention of Culture]]* (1975) and developed at book length in *Lethal Speech: Daribi Myth as Symbolic Obviation* (1978), which Wagner describes as the third part of an "unintended trilogy" with *Habu* (1972). All claims attributed to Wagner.

## Definition as Coined

Where conventional symbolization draws a contrast between symbol and thing symbolized, obviation is the effect whereby differentiating symbols **assimilate or encompass what they symbolize**, collapsing the distinction — the symbol "stands for itself." Invention is "the obviator of conventional contexts and contrasts": its total effect is to merge subject and object, transforming each in terms of the other (an exchange Wagner calls "objectification," noting the parallel with Nancy Munn's "objectivate" in *Walbiri Iconography*, 1973). Wagner characterizes obviation as "the extended, or processual form of trope" — where *Habu* was "about" metaphor, *Lethal Speech* is "about" obviation.

## Applications

**Strathern *Partial Connections* (1991):** *(Attributed.)* In the Center and Periphery stretch, Strathern engages Wagner’s **obviational analysis** (recursive processual form; *Symbols that Stand for Themselves*; Barok materials) as a way Melanesian imagery prefigures and elicits forms — a precursor path via [[structuralism]] that she neither simply revives nor abandons when arguing for self-scaling partial connections. See [[strathern-partial-connections-study]], [[wagner-roy]], [[fractal-person]].

Wagner also deploys obviation as the master pattern of intellectual history: a cultural or theoretical program undercuts and supersedes its own founding assumptions through its own success. In Ch. 6 he argues "the evolutionism of Tylor and Morgan… obviated itself in the years between 1870 and 1895," that anthropological paradigms succeed one another by self-obviation (diachronic/diffusionist anthropology 1871–1922 → synchronic-systemic anthropology 1922–1972), and that "a progressively relativizing Culture progressively obviates its own interests and activities" — a scheme he offers as a rewriting of Kuhn's *Structure of Scientific Revolutions* "from the standpoint of invention."

## Critiques and Limitations

- The concept is tightly bound to Wagner's [[invention-and-convention]] dialectic and has had its main afterlife within Melanesianist and ontological-turn circles (Strathern, Viveiros de Castro) rather than as a general analytic tool.
- As with the parent framework, obviation is presented programmatically, not as a testable claim; its diagrammatic formalization in *Lethal Speech* has been applied mainly by Wagner and his students.
