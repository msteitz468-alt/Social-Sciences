# Microphysics of Power

## Definition as Coined

In *[[foucault-discipline-and-punish-1977|Discipline and Punish]]*, [[foucault-michel|Foucault]] calls for a **micro-physics of power** — analysis of power at the scale of minute material details, gestures, distributions, and bodily forces — rather than only at the scale of law, sovereignty, or class possession of the state. Closely related phrases in the same work: **political anatomy**, **political economy of the body**, and (in the panopticism chapter) a "physics of a relational and multiple power."

General proposition (attributed): systems of punishment sit within a political economy of the body; even "lenient" penality targets the body's forces, utility, and docility. Power relations have an immediate hold on the body: they invest it, mark it, train it, torture it, force it to carry out tasks, to perform ceremonies, to emit signs. This hold is also a knowledge relation — [[power-knowledge]].

## Semantic History

- **1975:** method-scale term for the disciplinary analysis in D&P.
- **Relation to [[analytics-of-power]]:** the analytics (stated most programmatically in *History of Sexuality* Vol. I) is the general non-juridical account of power; microphysics is the **scalar commitment** that studies power in capillary techniques.
- **Reception:** used widely in body sociology and STS; sometimes diluted into any "small-scale" politics. Keep Foucault's sense: techniques that simultaneously produce knowledge of and power over bodies.

## Operationalizations

Not measured. Exhibited through:

- Scaffold vs. timetable (Damiens / Faucher) as two investments of the body
- Military drill, school rank, hospital architecture as technico-political registers
- Hierarchical observation, examination, Panopticon as optical micro-techniques
- Prison labour as transformation of the convict into a regular "part" of a machine

## Applications

Underwrites [[disciplinary-power]], [[docile-bodies]], [[panopticism]], and the four methodological rules of the study [[foucault-discipline-and-punish-study]]. Informs later vault uses of Foucault (e.g. Ferguson on apparatus and [[instrument-effects]]).

## Critiques and Limitations

- **Missing macro?** (attributed): critics argue microphysics under-weights state, capital, and law as structural powers. Foucault's reply-in-practice: law and state are terminal forms; the analysis starts from techniques that make those forms operable.
- **Verification:** micro-historical claims are hard to falsify; treat as genealogical interpretation.
