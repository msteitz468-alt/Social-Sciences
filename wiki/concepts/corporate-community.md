# Corporate Community

Domhoff’s term for the organized nationwide network of large US corporations and financial institutions that forms the organizational core of the corporate rich (*[[domhoff-who-rules-america-2022|Who Rules America?]]*).

## Definition as Coined

*(Attributed — Domhoff, WRA 8th.)* The **corporate community** is not merely “big business” as a residual category but a **connected community** of major corporations and financial institutions linked through ownership, interlocking directorates, and shared organizational memberships (e.g. Business Roundtable / related peak associations in his analyses). Duality of persons and groups: the same people sit on multiple boards. Domhoff synthesizes interlock findings (e.g. high share of Fortune 500 firms in the interlock network in studies he cites for 2010–11) as evidence the community is nationwide. He rejects treating the military-industrial sector or high-tech as fully autonomous separate communities. First empirical pillar of [[class-domination-theory]].

## Semantic History

Central from the 1st edition (1967) through the 8th (2022); boundary of “who counts” as community member is research-operational (size, finance, interlocks) and edition-updated.

## Operationalizations

Interlocking directorates; ownership networks; trade associations; inclusion criteria for “large” firms — see WRA Ch. 2 evidence base (attributed).

## Applications

Foundation for linking upper class (Ch. 3) and policy-planning network (Ch. 4) into Domhoff’s power elite.

## Critiques and Limitations

Boundary and interlock-threshold debates; fragmentation of capital fractions may be understated; comparative portability outside the US is limited in the charter text.
