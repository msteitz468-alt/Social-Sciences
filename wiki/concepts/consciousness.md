# Consciousness (social)

> Reworked as a graded "chain of consciousness" by [[comaroff-jean|Jean]] and [[comaroff-john-l|John L. Comaroff]] in *[[comaroff-of-revelation-and-revolution-1991|Of Revelation and Revolution]]* (1991).

## Definition as Coined

In social theory "consciousness" names the awareness and knowledge through which people apprehend their world. The Comaroffs criticize "the post-enlightenment western tradition" — including mainstream sociology and anthropology — for a **binary** image of social consciousness: "all or none, true or false, present or absent," and for treating it as *content* (knowledge) rather than *form* (modes of knowing).

## The chain of consciousness (attributed)

Against the binary, the Comaroffs situate social knowledge along a **continuum** — a "chain of consciousness" — running from the unseen, submerged, and unrecognized to the seen, apprehended, and cognized. The **[[hegemony|hegemonic]]** pole is the naturalized and mute; the **[[ideology|ideological]]** pole is the articulate and contested. Hegemony stands to ideology "as form to content," a matter of degree, not kind (they draw an analogy to Barthes on code vs. message). The most important zone for [[historical-anthropology]] is the **liminal middle**: a domain of "partial recognition … inchoate awareness … ambiguous perception," from which "silent signifiers and unmarked practices may rise to the level of explicit consciousness" or recede back into the taken-for-granted. This middle is also, they argue, the wellspring of "the poetics of history, the innovative impulses of the [[bricolage|bricoleur]] and the organic intellectual."

## Semantic History

The concept carries a Marxian lineage — Marx and Engels's "contradictory consciousness" (glossed by [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]] as the gap between the world as hegemonically constituted and as practically apprehended), and the whole problem of [[false-consciousness]]. The Comaroffs' move is to make consciousness *gradient* rather than a switch, aligning it with [[practical-consciousness|Giddens's practical]] and [[discursive-consciousness|discursive]] consciousness and with [[habitus|Bourdieu's]] orthodoxy/heterodoxy.

## Operationalizations

Diagnostically, the analyst tracks *movement* along the chain — what becomes remarked and contestable (moving toward the ideological pole) and what sediments into unremarked "custom" (toward the hegemonic). The [[colonization-of-consciousness]] is precisely such movement engineered from outside.

## Applications

The theoretical hinge of *[[comaroff-revelation-revolution-study]]*; broadly borrowed in the anthropology of colonialism, resistance, and everyday life.

## Critiques and Limitations

The gradient model is hard to operationalize independently of the analyst's reading, and — as with the wider program — the *consciousness of the colonized* is largely inferred from a European archive (see [[historical-ethnography]]). Distinguish from [[false-consciousness]]: the point is not error about interests but the form within which awareness is possible.
