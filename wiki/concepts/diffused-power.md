# Diffused Power

## Definition as Coined

[[mann-michael|Michael Mann]] (*[[mann-sources-of-social-power-vol1-1986|SSP Vol. I]]*, 1986, Ch. 1): **diffused power** spreads in a spontaneous, unconscious, decentered way, producing similar practices that embody power relations without explicit command — often experienced as natural, moral, or self-evident common interest.

> "Diffused power, however, spreads in a more spontaneous, unconscious, decentered way throughout a population, resulting in similar social practices that embody power relations but are not explicitly commanded. … To use Adam Smith's famous metaphor, the principal instrument of power in a market is an \"Invisible Hand,\" constraining all, yet not controlled by any single human agency."

Requires a **universal infrastructure** (markets, literacy, coinage, class/national culture) rather than particular authoritative organizations. Cross-classified: intensive+diffused ≈ general strike; extensive+diffused ≈ market exchange.

## Semantic History

Mann's complement to [[authoritative-power]] within the [[iemp-model]] and [[power-networks]] apparatus. Related to but not identical with hegemony or Foucaultian micro-power (different theoretical lineage). Class and nation solidarity are treated as diffused power forms; transcendent ideology often organizes largely diffused extensive/intensive practices (Christian ecumene).

## Operationalizations

Uncommanded conformity; organizational outflanking of subordinates by market or normative fields; literacy, coinage, simplified scripts, and class/national culture as carriers; immanent ruling-class morale as diffused upper-class identity.

## Applications

Attributed to Mann in *SSP* Vol. I:

- **Iron Age cosmopolitanism (Chs. 6–8)** — Broader symbiosis of diffused power under chariot-age decentralization; literacy, trade, coinage grow diffuse universal identities at expense of particularistic local ones; Assyrian upper-class "nationalism" and Persian international upper-class ideology as immanent morale.
- **Greece/Phoenicia** — Markets and multistate exchange without central authoritative redistribution.
- **Christianity (Chs. 10–12)** — Ecumene combining extensive and intensive power, largely diffuse rather than authoritative; Christendom supplies extensive infrastructure "by ideological means… without a state," enabling intensive local economic dynamism under [[normative-pacification]].
- **Capitalism (Chs. 14–15, Vol. II preview)** — World market as extensive+diffused outflanking of national working-class movements; British national economy allowing growth to flow diffusely under universal legislation and coinage.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of residual category for "everything not commanded"; keep tied to Mann's examples. Diffused power can still be highly coercive in effect (market discipline, normative exclusion of outsiders to the ecumene).
