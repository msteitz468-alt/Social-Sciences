# Anti-Ritualism

Key foil and explanandum of [[douglas-mary|Mary Douglas]]'s *[[douglas-natural-symbols-1970|Natural Symbols]]* (1970), developed from the 1968 Blackfriars lecture "The Contempt of Ritual." Program: [[grid-group-analysis]]. Study: [[douglas-natural-symbols-study]].

## Definition as Coined

Douglas used **anti-ritualism** for the modern (and recurrent) rejection of fixed ritual forms in favor of sincerity, inner experience, ethical universalism, and spirit-over-letter religion — exemplified by 1960s student revolt, currents in post–Vatican II Catholicism, and Protestant interiorization. She refused to rank anti-ritualism as spiritual progress; she asked which **social environments** produce it.

## Semantic History

- Stimulus: late-1960s contempt of "dead ritual" and "meaningless forms."
- Douglas links anti-ritualism to Bernstein's elaborated code and personal control systems ([[bernstein-basil]], [[restricted-and-elaborated-codes]]) — analogy, not identity.
- Opposed pairs in the book: ritualist / anti-ritualist; condensed / diffuse symbols; positional / personal control.

## Operationalizations

Treat ritual vs anti-ritual as variable strength of boundary maintenance in religious form (Douglas: analogous to strong vs weak boundary maintenance in Bernstein's curriculum). Locate cases on [[grid-and-group]].

## Applications

Bog Irish Friday abstinence vs elite Catholic reform; millenarian enthusiasm; Western counterculture; church worries about clothes stolen by ethical sensitivity without institutional form (Ch. 10).

## Critiques and Limitations

Can sound conservative if read as defending ritual against reform; Douglas's claim is comparative (social location of styles), not a blanket defense of hierarchy. Reformers' own accounts of dead ritual remain competing evidence.
