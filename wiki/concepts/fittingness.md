# Fittingness

## Definition as Coined

In *[[hodder-entangled-2012|Entangled]]* Chapters 6–7, [[hodder-ian|Hodder]] develops **fittingness** to cover two dictionary senses of “fit” that he insists are interdependent:

1. **Functional adaptation / [[affordance]]** — things work together (handles and hands, paste properties and firing, pin and shackle).
2. **Acceptability / coherence** — things are accepted when they cohere through abstraction, metaphor, mimesis, or bodily resonance within a wider entanglement.

Traits (pots, planes, pins, house forms) are **selected for** and **persist** when they are fitting within a taut web of human–thing dependences — not solely because they maximize reproductive fitness, resource return, or replicative success. Fittingness thus retools Darwinian language of variation and selection for [[entanglement-theory|entanglement theory]].

“Fit” language appears earlier in Ch. 1–3 (materials fit into each other); Ch. 6 makes it a formal concept.

## Operationalizations

- Pottery paste preparation multi-factor spaces.
- Organizational diagrams of mutually reinforcing activities.
- Resonance tests (abstract sound–shape; decorated face pot vs undecorated cooking ware at [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]]).
- Evolutionary persistence: wheel requires draught animals and roads; piano requires industrial and musical entanglements absent in the Mesolithic.

## Applications

- **Çatalhöyük face pot** — unique decorated vessel linking human faces (eyeless, like plastered skulls) and bull heads; never copied because pottery then was entangled with domestic sheep/goat fat processing, not with history-making wild-animal symbolism (Hodder 2012).
- **Later decorated pottery** — becomes fitting when houses, hearths, serving, and productive display reorganize (West Mound).
- **Plane design** (Lemonnier) — efficient odd-shaped planes not adopted because they did not *feel* safe — coherence side of fit.

## Critiques and Limitations

Risk of post-hoc storytelling (anything that persisted was “fitting”). Hodder ties the concept to empirical chain reconstruction to reduce that risk. Boundary with adaptive “fitness” remains contested among evolutionary archaeologists. Power: who decides what coheres is a positioned judgment (flagged in Ch. 10).
