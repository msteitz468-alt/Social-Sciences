# Social Compounding

## Definition as Coined

Spencer’s account of **social growth** by the joining of social groups into larger aggregates: the primitive social group compounds with others; compound societies may re-compound into **doubly compound** and further **trebly compound** aggregates. He held that stages of compounding are sequential — higher compounds presuppose lower — and that war is a principal cause of durable political integration, though industrial and other ties also bind. proposed_by: [[spencer-herbert]] · part_of: [[social-evolutionism]]

Linked classification in “Social Types and Constitutions”: societies typed **primarily** by degree of composition (simple, compound, doubly-, trebly-compound) and **secondarily** by predominant activity (militant vs industrial).

## Semantic History

Compositional typology sits beside evolutionary progress talk; later political sociology’s “state formation,” “scale,” and multi-level governance problems rework related questions without Spencer’s full formula. Not identical to mere population growth (Spencer distinguished growth by unit multiplication from growth by union of groups).

## Operationalizations

- Simple group → compound (cluster of groups under some common regulation) → doubly compound (compounds of compounds) → trebly compound (e.g. ancient empires in his illustrations).
- Political integration chapters: conquest, federation, and subordination of local heads as compounding mechanisms.
- Regulating system compounds as local centres become subordinate to general centres.

## Applications

Frames political headship at successive compound stages; ecclesiastical hierarchies paralleling political scale; industrial structures that can cross political boundaries (distributing/sustaining systems) even as regulating systems track political compounds.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Sequential-stage claim is evolutionary theory, not demonstrated necessity.
- Ethnographic labels for “simple” groups are Victorian comparative constructs.
- Conquest-driven compounding understates other integration paths; Spencer includes industrial cooperation but weights war heavily.
