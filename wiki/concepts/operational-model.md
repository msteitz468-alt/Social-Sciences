# Operational Model

## Definition as Coined

For [[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]] (*[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]*, 1968/1984; "On Cognized Models" in *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]*, 1979), the **operational model** is the **observer's** model of how the local and regional systems work in material terms — trophic exchanges, pig–garden competition, population, warfare, trade — using measurable variables. It is deliberately distinguished from the people's [[cognized-model]].

## Semantic History

Part of 1960s–70s systems/human-ecology vocabulary; "operational" signals measurement and ecosystem specification (local ecosystem vs regional system). EMR Essay 1 already contrasts cognized vs operational **environment** in Pacific island ecosystems. Later critics read the pair as privileging Western science over indigenous knowledge; Rappaport insisted both models are necessary and that cognized models can be evaluated for **adaptive effectiveness**, not only accuracy.

## Operationalizations

In the Tsembaga study: garden yields, energy expenditure, diet logs, pig census, [[carrying-capacity]] estimates, ritual-cycle timing relative to herd and fallow — see appendices of *Pigs* and [[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-study]]. Goal ranges (viability bounds on operational variables) are established independently of cognized reference values; misalignment is a maladaptation risk ([[adaptive-structure]]).

## Applications

Allows claims that ritual has "practical results on the external world" (contra Homans-style internal-only functionalism) without claiming that Tsembaga *intend* ecological management in scientific terms. In the Friedman response (EMR "Ills of Functionalism"), operational/systemic analysis is defended as non-identical with vulgar materialism or economism of ritual.

## Critiques and Limitations

Measurement error, short fieldwork window, and disputed nutritional arithmetic (App. 11 / McArthur) threaten operational conclusions; interpretive anthropologists contest the priority of operational variables. See [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].
