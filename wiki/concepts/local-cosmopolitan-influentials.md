# Local and Cosmopolitan Influentials

## Definition as Coined

In *[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]* Ch. XII ("Patterns of Influence"), [[merton-robert-k|Merton]] distinguished **local** and **cosmopolitan** influentials in a community study (Rovere): locals whose influence rests on dense local interpersonal integration, cosmopolitans whose influence rests on specialized expertise and extra-local orientation. Types differ in media use, association patterns, and paths to influence.

## Semantic History

Classic two-step flow / opinion-leader adjacent typology; "local/cosmopolitan" entered organizational sociology and elite studies with varied meanings — keep Merton's community-influence sense distinct from Gouldner's later local/cosmopolitan professional orientations unless explicitly bridged.

## Operationalizations

Interviews and reputational methods in a single community; media-exposure patterns. Case-based, not a national sample.

## Applications

Mass communication and personal influence; community power; professional orientation (later extensions).

## Critiques and Limitations

Single-community scope; reputational bias; historical specificity of mid-century small-city media ecology.
