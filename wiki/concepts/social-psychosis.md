# Social psychosis

## Definition as Coined

[[biehl-joao|João Biehl]] introduces **social psychosis** in *[[biehl-vita-2005|Vita]]* (2005) as a working concept for Catarina's condition: "those materials, mechanisms, and relations through which the so-called normal and minimally efficient order of social formations is effected and of which Catarina is a leftover" (attributed). In the Conclusion he states that social psychosis "encompasses the manner in which diagnosis and treatment of various mental and affective disturbances fluctuate in concert with political and economic forces and the dying of social ties" (attributed) — not a pure social-construction thesis that denies biology, but a name for how kinship, psychiatry, pharmaceuticals, and economy jointly form what appears as "madness."

## Semantic History

- **2005.** Coined against both naive biomedical realism and pure constructivism: Biehl refuses to dissolve Catarina's suffering into "only" social construction, while refusing to accept the psychiatric career that made her abandonable.
- Dialogues with Lacan, Deleuze, and clinical anthropology without collapsing into any single school's technical psychosis concept — Biehl's term is ethnographic-political, not a DSM equivalent.
- Related later work on pharmaceuticalization and biological citizenship may refine the construct; date-stamp to *Vita* until then.

## Operationalizations

Case reconstruction: fluctuating diagnoses (psychosis → affective disorder), polypharmacy, family proxy-psychiatry, economic "common sense" of impossibility of care, eventual genetic finding (MJD/SCA3) that reopens but does not erase the social career.

## Applications

- *[[biehl-vita-study|Vita]]* Parts Two–Five — Catarina as type case of how "psychotic" experience is given form by institutions and then leftover.

## Critiques and Limitations

- Working concept from one monograph; clinical readers may reject the label as metaphorical misuse of "psychosis."
- Must not be read as denying organic disease — Biehl's later MJD finding is part of the same argument, not a refutation of it.
