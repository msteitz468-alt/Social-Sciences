# Private Property

## Definition as Coined

In [[engels-friedrich|Engels]]’s *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]*, **private property** is not mere personal use-possession but the historical emergence of alienable, heritable wealth (especially herds, then land and money capital) held by individual families against the [[gens]], enabling class division, father-right inheritance, [[monogamy]] as heir-guarantee, and finally [[the-state|the state]] as organ of the possessing class. Distinct from the *Manifesto*’s slogan focus on **bourgeois** property, though continuous with [[historical-materialism]].

## Semantic History

Roman law, liberal natural-rights theory, and socialist critique use “property” differently. Engels’s origin story is a Victorian evolutionary reconstruction; legal anthropology and institutional economics later disaggregated property rights bundles without that ladder.

**Maine (*Ancient Law* Ch. VIII).** *(Attributed — [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]].)* Separate occupancy theories of property’s origin are late projections; **joint ownership** is the archaic form; father holds as trustee of the family; Indian village community is organized patriarchal co-proprietorship (compare Roman [[gens]]); “the most important passage in the history of Private Property is its gradual separation from the co-ownership of kinsmen.” Source: [[maine-ancient-law-1861]].

## Operationalizations

Movable wealth in cattle; land severalty; money, interest, mortgage; slave property; testamentary disposition — markers Engels associates with civilization (*Origin* ch. IX).

## Applications

Links kinship chapters to political chapters: property splits the gens, demands heirs, produces monogamy and the state. Compare [[primitive-accumulation]] (Capital I) for capitalism’s historical origin — different object, related expropriation theme.

## Critiques and Limitations

Archaeology of “property” is evidence-class sensitive (material ≠ legal title ≠ ideology). Unilinear path from communal gens to private property is contested. Always attribute origin claims to Engels/Morgan.

**Sacks (1974) in *[[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974|Woman, Culture, and Society]]*.** *(Attributed.)* Clarifies Engels: private property = goods/resources with *productive* potential (esp. domesticated animals, cultivated land), not personal effects; earliest private productive property (domestic animals) assimilated into male-associated ownership patterns that undermined communal order — not "greedy male nature." Sacks holds that women's decline tracks the privatization of productive property and the shift of women's work from social/public to private family maintenance; she modifies Engels for nonclass societies (public labor stretch; women can be social adults and wives at once).

**Lowie (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, Property):** crude “primitive communism” fails ethnographic survey — individual property of some sort is general; land tenure varies by subsistence; chattels and especially **[[incorporeal-property]]** (names, songs, ceremonial privileges) are exclusive and heritable in many simpler societies. Inheritance rules are diverse (not one stage sequence). Reaffirmed 1947: individual property seems a general phenomenon of primitive society.
