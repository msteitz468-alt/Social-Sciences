# Housing Audit Study

## What It Does

Paired or systematically assigned **testers** (matched on qualifications, differing by race) inquire about housing; analysts measure differences in availability, terms, steering, and courtesy. Used in *[[massey-denton-american-apartheid-1993|American Apartheid]]* Ch 4 to show ongoing discrimination after the Fair Housing Act.

## Procedure

HUD **Housing Market Practices Survey (1977)** and **Housing Discrimination Study (1989)** are the major federal applications reanalyzed in the book; local audits also cited.

## Assumptions and Limitations

External validity to unsampled agents; ethical/logistical constraints; does not replace structural analysis of segregation indices.

## History

Fair-housing enforcement and social-science tool from the 1970s onward; central empirical support for Massey & Denton's "continuing causes" chapter.

## Exemplary Applications

[[massey-denton-american-apartheid-study]] Ch 4 (1977 HMPS; 1989 HDS; Yinger and related analyses).

## Debates

How much residual segregation is preference vs discrimination; audit net incidence vs gross.
