# Multiple-Classification Analysis (MCA)

A multivariate technique for categorical predictors, closely related to multiple regression with dummy variables, that reports grand means and adjusted deviations for each category of each predictor. Blau and Duncan use MCA extensively in *[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-1967|The American Occupational Structure]]* (Chapter 4 and throughout subgroup analyses).

## What It Does

Shows how mean occupational status (or another outcome) differs across categories of origin, education, race, region, etc., before and after statistical adjustment for other factors.

## Procedure

Specify dependent variable and set of categorical predictors → estimate additive model → report unadjusted and adjusted category effects / eta and beta analogues (as presented in the OCG analyses).

## Assumptions and Limitations

Additivity; category coding choices; same causal caveats as observational regression.

## History

Developed and popularized in the Michigan ISR survey tradition; Blau–Duncan 1967 is a major sociological showcase.

## Exemplary Applications

[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-study]] (race, region, farm, family, marriage analyses).

## Debates

MCA vs full regression/path specifications; interpretation of "net" category effects under omitted-variable bias.
