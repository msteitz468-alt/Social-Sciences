# Constant Comparative Method

## What It Does

The **constant comparative method of qualitative analysis**, formulated by [[glaser-barney-g|Glaser]] and [[strauss-anselm-l|Strauss]] (*[[glaser-strauss-discovery-grounded-theory-1967|Discovery of Grounded Theory]]*, 1967, Ch. V), is a strategy for generating theory by **jointly coding and analyzing** qualitative data. Each incident is coded into as many categories as relevant and **compared** with previous incidents (and later with properties of categories), driving development of properties and hypotheses while theory remains a **process**.

Glaser and Strauss situate it among general approaches to qualitative analysis:

1. **Code first, then analyze** to convert qualitative data into crudely quantifiable form for provisional hypothesis testing (they associate this with systematic "proof" aspirations).
2. **Generate theoretical ideas** by inspecting data and writing memos without full explicit coding (creative but less systematic).
3. **Constant comparative method** — their third path: explicit coding and analytic procedures for more systematic generation, used jointly with [[theoretical-sampling]], without fully adhering to coding-for-proof (which they argue hinders discovery).
4. **Analytic induction** (Lindesmith, Cressey, Znaniecki lineage) — generates and aims to prove a limited causal theory of a specific behavior by reformulating hypotheses against negative cases; G&S contrast it with CCM (CCM suggests many categories/properties without universality claims; needs saturation, not all available cases of one defined type).

They claim CCM is designed to aid generation of theory that is integrated, consistent, plausible, close to the data, and clear enough to be partially operationalized for later testing — **not** to guarantee that two independent analysts get the same results.

## Procedure

Four stages (continuously growing; earlier stages remain in operation):

| Stage | What happens |
|---|---|
| **1. Comparing incidents applicable to each category** | Code each incident into as many categories as emerge or fit; track comparison group. **Basic rule:** while coding an incident for a category, compare it with previous incidents in the same and different categories. **Second rule:** stop coding and write a memo on ideas. Categories may be constructed or "in vivo." |
| **2. Integrating categories and their properties** | Comparison units shift from incident–incident to incident–properties; properties integrate with each other and with related categories into a denser theory. |
| **3. Delimiting the theory** | Reduction for parsimony and scope; solidify boundaries of the theory; theoretical saturation limits further coding on saturated categories. |
| **4. Writing the theory** | Rearrange memos and notes; "fracture" the narrative story for clear theoretical integration while saving apt illustrations. |

Running example in Ch. V: nurses' calculation of patients' **social loss** — properties of recalculation as more is learned about the patient.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- Theory is process until saturated; generation and provisional testing are not done in the same CCM pass (theoretical sampling does not yield data extensive enough for testing).
- Depends on analyst skill and [[theoretical-sensitivity]].
- Qualitative information broadly defined: observations, interviews, documents, articles, books.

## History

Chapter V of *Discovery* (1967); continuous with comparative-analysis chapters (II–IV, VI). Later grounded-theory schools rename and re-package stages (open/axial coding, etc.) — **collection gap**; do not equate 1967 CCM with later textbook packages without primary sources.

## Exemplary Applications

Dying-research illustrations in *Discovery* (*Awareness of Dying* not yet ingested). Ch. VI assesses other comparative studies (Swanson, Blauner, Redfield, Eisenstadt, Goffman, Shibutani & Kwan, Etzioni, Geertz, Evans-Pritchard, Park/Simmel, etc.) against an eight-question "accounting scheme" for how comparison is used (accurate evidence, empirical generalization, specifying concepts, generating theory, etc.).

## Debates

[[generation-vs-verification-debate]]; relation to analytic induction; relation to Blumer's [[exploration-and-inspection]] and [[sensitizing-concept]] (affinities — flexible concepts close to the world — without identity of method). Tavory & Timmermans (*[[tavory-timmermans-abductive-analysis-2014|Abductive Analysis]]*, 2014) report later GT open/axial/selective coding packages and appropriate comparison for **variation** and abductive casing, while arguing that coding primacy under an inductivist philosophy reifies process and underdelivers theory construction (see [[abductive-analysis]]).
