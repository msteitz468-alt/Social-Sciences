# Potassium-Argon Dating

## What It Does

**Potassium-argon (K-Ar) dating** dates volcanic rock by the decay of radioactive potassium-40 (⁴⁰K) to argon-40 (⁴⁰Ar), with a half-life of about **1.3 billion years**. Colin Renfrew and Paul Bahn present it as one of the principal absolute methods for the Paleolithic—especially appropriate for early hominin sites in Africa that can be up to ~5 million years old (*[[renfrew-bahn-archaeology-2012|Archaeology]]*, 6th ed. 2012, Ch. 4; attributed). The clock is zeroed at rock formation when volcanic activity drives off prior argon.

**Argon-argon (⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar)** laser-fusion variants need smaller samples, including single crystals (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

In their method hierarchy, for periods **before radiocarbon’s range**, K-Ar (or Ar-Ar), uranium-series, and fission-track matter most; radiocarbon remains the main tool for the last ~50,000 years (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Procedure

As Renfrew & Bahn describe:

1. Sample volcanic rock/mineral from strata above and/or below archaeological deposits.
2. Measure ⁴⁰K and accumulated ⁴⁰Ar (or use neutron-irradiated ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar protocols with laser fusion on small samples).
3. Calculate time since the volcanic event that zeroed the argon clock.
4. Bracket archaeology between dated tuffs/lavas—a **“chronological sandwich”** typical of East African Rift Valley sites (attributed phrasing).

**Related confirmation methods they discuss:**

- **Fission-track dating** of uranium-bearing minerals and glass (zircon, apatite, volcanic/manufactured glasses) has been used at early Paleolithic sites such as Olduvai to confirm K-Ar and other results (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).
- **Uranium-series dating** (²³⁸U/²³⁵U daughters ²³⁰Th, ²³¹Pa) covers roughly **500,000–50,000 years**, outside radiocarbon’s range and especially valuable in **Europe lacking volcanic rocks for K-Ar**—dating travertine, cave speleothems, and (with uptake problems) teeth (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).
- **Paleomagnetic reversals** (most recent complete reversal ~780,000 years ago) build sequences with K-Ar control and check other methods at African early-hominin sites and Atapuerca (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Assumptions and Limitations

- **Volcanic contexts only.** No direct dating of bone, pottery, or non-volcanic sediments by classic K-Ar.
- **Young-age floor.** Restricted to rock no more recent than about **100,000 years** in their presentation; not a method for late prehistoric or historic archaeology (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).
- **Accuracy.** Rarely better than **±10%**; counting errors illustrated e.g. Olduvai Tuff IB 1.79 ±0.03 Ma (~2% counting error in that example) (as of textbook).
- **Indirect archaeology.** Dates the volcanic event, not the cultural occupation; association and stratigraphy must link the two.
- **Geographic complementarity.** Where volcanics are scarce (much of Europe), uranium-series and other methods substitute (attributed).

## History

K-Ar entered archaeological chronology with deep-time African paleoanthropology and Quaternary geology. Renfrew & Bahn treat it as post-WWII “radioactive clock” practice alongside radiocarbon, uranium-series, and fission-track (attributed synthesis). Multi-method programs (K-Ar + fission-track + paleomagnetism + ESR + cosmogenic nuclides) characterize later textbook cases such as Atapuerca.

## Exemplary Applications (as textbook reports)

- **Olduvai Gorge:** tuffs dated by K-Ar; Tuff IB 1.79 ±0.03 Ma; fission-track confirmation of early Paleolithic results (attributed as R&B present them).
- **Hadar and other Rift Valley sites:** chronological sandwiches for early hominins.
- **Vesuvius AD 79 pumice:** Ar-Ar test gave AD 72 ±94—a known-age check of the method (as of textbook).
- **Atapuerca multi-method program:** K-Ar among microfauna, ¹⁴C, uranium-series, ESR, paleomagnetism, and cosmogenic burial dating supporting occupation >1 million years; Gran Dolina TD6 ~800 ka–1 Ma (*Homo antecessor*); Sima del Elefante TE9 ~1.1–1.2 Ma (oldest securely dated human occupation in Europe *as they present it*); Sima de los Huesos chronology with U-series/ESR constraints (attributed synthesis, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Debates

- **How old is European occupation?** Atapuerca ages underwrote arguments for pre-500 ka Europe against more conservative resistance—reported as textbook case, not adjudicated here.
- **Single-method vs multi-method.** Fission-track, paleomagnetism, and U-series/ESR are used to cross-check K-Ar; Renfrew & Bahn favor correlation of methods for Paleolithic chronology (attributed position).
- **Precision vs question.** ±10% ages at multi-million-year scales still resolve broad evolutionary questions while remaining too coarse for short-term cultural history (see [[absolute-dating]] on precision and research design).
