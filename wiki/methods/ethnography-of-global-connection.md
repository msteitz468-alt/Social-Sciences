# Ethnography of Global Connection

## What It Does

A research design for studying **globalization as practical, frictional engagement** rather than as abstract system or bounded village. Charter text: *[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]* (2005). Tracks how capitalism, science, and politics depend on global connections that only take hold in sticky practical encounters ([[friction]]).

## Procedure

Tsing’s repertoire (attributed, from *Friction*):

1. **Start in the middle** — refuse abstract universals as self-realizing truths; enter specific situations.
2. **Zones of cultural friction** — focus where engagement is hard; words mean different things across a divide; these zones are transient and reappear at new events.
3. **Patchwork** — renounce a fully pre-planned design; results may not look like classical ethnography but remain ethnographic as products of the ethnographer’s learning.
4. **Multi-locale triangulation** — follow affinity chains (Jakarta ↔ provincial capitals ↔ Meratus; nature-lovers; activists; commodity chains).
5. **Track scale-making and engaged universals** — who claims global/national/local, with what collaborators ([[scale-making]], [[traveling-universals]]).
6. **Attend incompatible stories** — e.g. village / province / capital forest narratives; do not force premature aggregation.
7. **Write distress at the center** — place ecological and social crisis in analytic view rather than romantic hybridity alone; “economy of the viewpoint of despair” as necessary counterweight.

Field practice in *Friction*: deep Meratus immersion 1979–81; later shorter seasons 1986, 1994, 1997, 2000; collaborative nature-lovers research; failed corporate interviews; pseudonyms for private persons/villages.

## Assumptions and Limitations

Assumes connection is observable in concrete collaborations and ruptures. Limitations: incomplete corporate access; uneven depth; French/English edition issues for quotation; complementary to, not substitute for, world-systems macrosociology or the full Marcus multi-sited program.

## History

Emerges from 1990s–2000s anthropology of globalization and multi-sited ethnography debates; *Friction* is a major empirical-theoretical charter. Extends Tsing’s earlier argument (*Diamond Queen*, 1993) that apparently isolated cultures are shaped in long regional/global networks — then reverses the harder claim that global forces are aggregates of local/global interactions. *[[tsing-mushroom-2015|The Mushroom at the End of the World]]* (2015) keeps multi-locale commodity-chain following and scale critique while extending the connection motif into [[multi-species-ethnography]] and [[salvage-accumulation]] across matsutake patches *(Tsing)*.
