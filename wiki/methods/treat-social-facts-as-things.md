# Treat social facts as things

## What It Does

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]’s first and most basic rule of sociological method, stated in Chapter II of *[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|The Rules of Sociological Method]]* (1895): **consider [[social-fact|social facts]] as things** — as realities external to the observer’s (and the actor’s) preconceptions, to be approached from outside by definition, observation, and comparison rather than by introspection or ideological analysis of ideas (attributed).

The rule is methodological before it is metaphysical. In the 1901 Preface he insists social life is made up of **representations** and that “consciousness” names a collection of phenomena *sui generis*, not a substance; “things” means resistance to the will and independence from the method that studies them, not inert matter (attributed).

## Procedure

Summary resolution from *Rules* Ch. II (attributed to Durkheim):

1. **Discard prenotions** — Baconian *praenotiones* / *idola*: common-sense and everyday practical ideas about law, morality, the family, the state that substitute for the things themselves and turn sociology into ideology or art.
2. **Proceed from things to ideas**, not ideas to things — facts are subject matter, not mere illustrations of prior concepts.
3. **Define the object by external, observable characteristics** shared by a class of phenomena, not by the inner meaning actors attach or by the observer’s ideal.
4. **Prefer solid, crystallized forms** where available (codes of law, established statistics, fixed morphological arrangements) as the most objective point of entry, then relate freer currents of opinion to them.
5. **Study social facts from outside** — as the chemist studies compounds — because they are external to individual consciousness and invested with coercive power (Ch. I definition).

Related operational toolkit: [[comparative-method]] (Ch. VI demonstration); [[moral-statistics]] (*Suicide*); law as external index of solidarity (*Division of Labor*).

## Assumptions and Limitations

| Assumption / threat | Note |
|---|---|
| **Objectivity via exteriority** | Assumes social facts leave external marks sufficient for science |
| **Anti-introspection** | Rules out (as *sufficient*) the actor’s conception as explanation — contested by interpretive sociology ([[verstehen]], [[interpretive-sociology]]) |
| **Reification charge** | Critics read “things” as hypostasis of Society; 1901 Preface replies that social facts are representations, not a substantial soul |
| **Prenotions return** | Scientific concepts still mediate observation; the rule disciplines, does not eliminate, conceptual work |
| **Power of definition** | Who frames the “external characteristic” shapes the object (reflexivity concern) |

## History

Enacted before codified: *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|The Division of Labor]]* (1893) already studies solidarity via its external legal index and locates causes in the social environment. *Rules* (1895) names and generalizes the rule; *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]* (1897) applies it to rates. The 1901 Preface defends the formula after “lively controversy.” English transmission: defective Solovay–Mueller 1938; Halls 1982 / Lukes 2013 revisions (this wiki’s base). Lineage: Bacon against *idola*; [[comte-auguste|Comte]]’s demand for positive social science; Durkheim’s break with encyclopedic system-building and with utilitarian reduction of social order to individual ideas.

Martineau’s earlier [[things-before-discourse]] is a related anti-discourse observational priority in the English founding era — not the same formula, but a cousin discipline of looking past talk to institutions and records.

## Exemplary Applications

- *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-study|Division of Labor]]* — law as index of solidarity types.
- *[[durkheim-suicide-study|Suicide]]* — social suicide-rate as *sui generis* fact; elimination of extra-social “apparent causes.”
- Program frame: [[social-realism]].

## Debates

- [[methodological-individualism-debate]] — whether social facts reduce to individuals’ beliefs and actions.
- [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] — constraint and exteriority vs. making history.
- [[weber-interpretive-sociology-debate]] — exterior “things” vs. subjectively meaningful action.
- Reification / “group mind” charges (1901 Preface responses; later Simpson and others).
