# Thermoluminescence Dating

## What It Does

**Thermoluminescence (TL) dating** dates crystalline minerals that have been fired—pottery, baked clay, burnt stone or soil. Natural radiation traps energy in crystal lattices until heating to ≥500 °C releases thermoluminescence and zeros the clock. Archaeological firing resets that clock; laboratory reheating measures accumulated TL to estimate time since last heating. Colin Renfrew and Paul Bahn present TL among post-WWII absolute “trapped-electron” methods that **overlap [[radiocarbon-dating]] and can go earlier** (*[[renfrew-bahn-archaeology-2012|Archaeology]]*, 6th ed. 2012, Ch. 4; attributed). Their summary table places useful range up to ~100,000 BP (as of 2012 textbook).

Related techniques they group with TL:

- **Optical (OSL) dating:** similar principle, but dates minerals **exposed to light** (bleaching zeros the clock); burial restarts electron accumulation from soil radiation; lab stimulation with visible light measures luminescence (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).
- **Electron spin resonance (ESR):** similar to but less sensitive than TL; usable on materials that decompose when heated; most successful application for **tooth enamel**, which starts trapping energy after burial; precision ~10–20% (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Procedure

As Renfrew & Bahn describe TL:

1. Sample fired crystalline material (pottery, terracotta, baked clay, burnt flint/stone/soil).
2. Measure accumulated trapped-charge luminescence by controlled reheating in the lab.
3. Measure **background radiation at the find spot** (capsule or portable counter)—dose rate is essential to convert luminescence signal into age.
4. Compute age from accumulated dose / annual dose rate.

They note TL is used when radiocarbon is unavailable (inorganic fired materials, or contexts without suitable organics) (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

OSL procedure (same chapter): minerals bleached by sunlight at deposition; burial accumulates dose; optical stimulation reads the signal. ESR targets tooth enamel (and similar) without destructive heating of the whole specimen in the same way as TL.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- **Last heating event.** TL dates the last firing ≥500 °C, not necessarily first manufacture if the object was reheated.
- **Dose rate known.** Background radiation must be measured *at the find spot*; misestimation of dose rate scales the age.
- **Precision.** Renfrew & Bahn state precision is **rarely better than ±10% of the age** for TL; ESR ~10–20% (attributed).
- **OSL shares TL’s background-radiation complications** (attributed).
- **Ranking.** In their chapter summary, TL and ESR are “less precise or special-purpose” relative to radiocarbon (for the last ~50 ka) and to K-Ar / uranium-series for deeper Paleolithic time (attributed position).

## History

Renfrew & Bahn place TL, optical dating, and ESR in the suite of absolute methods developed alongside radioactive clocks after WWII. They treat them as complementary where organics for ¹⁴C are missing or where ages extend beyond radiocarbon’s practical range (attributed synthesis, 2012). Radioactive decay remains, in their theoretical framing, the only completely regular time-dependent process uninfluenced by environment; trapped-electron methods require careful local dose-rate control (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Exemplary Applications (as textbook reports)

- **Jemaa (Nok) terracotta head:** TL age 1520 ±260 BC—presented as the first reliable chronological position for Nok terracottas (attributed finding as R&B report it).
- **Nauwalabila I (Australia):** OSL with TL and ¹⁴C; artifact-bearing sands OSL ~53,000–60,000 BP, with implications they discuss for first human occupation of Australia (as of 2012 textbook).
- **Early humans / cross-checks:** ESR at Atapuerca and Krapina (~130 ka Neanderthals with ESR in their uranium-series discussion) as multi-method checks (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).

## Debates

- **Special-purpose vs routine absolute method.** Textbook ranks TL/ESR below radiocarbon for routine later-period work and below K-Ar/U-series for deep Paleolithic volcanic/cave contexts, while valuing them when materials force their use.
- **Australian first-occupation OSL ages.** High OSL ages for early Australian sites (Nauwalabila) enter broader debates about peopling timelines; Renfrew & Bahn report the ages and implications as of 2012 without treating them as settled wiki-voice chronology.
- **Multi-method correlation.** Their general stance favors correlating trapped-electron ages with radiocarbon, uranium-series, and stratigraphy rather than relying on single TL/OSL/ESR determinations (see [[absolute-dating]]).

### Stringer & Gamble — *In Search of the Neanderthals* (1993)

*(Attributed — [[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993]]; as of 1993.)*

TL on **burnt flint** is presented as extending absolute chronology for the Middle Palaeolithic beyond radiocarbon’s ~40–50 ka practical limit. Key Levant cases they report: TL (and confirming ESR) dates of ~60 ka for levels adjoining the Neanderthal burial at [[kebara]]; much older TL ages for [[qafzeh]] and [[skhul]] modern-human-associated levels — used to argue that Moderns in the Near East are not simply later descendants of local Neanderthals but can predate or coexist with them (POSITION feeding [[recent-african-origin]] / [[modern-human-origins-debate]]). Also cited: Pontnewydd (Wales) burnt flint ~200 ka cross-checked with U-series on flowstone (as of Stringer & Gamble 1993). **Horizon:** absolute figures are 1993-vintage; later re-dating may revise site chronologies — keep attributed.

### Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*(Attributed — [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]], [[stringer-chris]]; as of Stringer 2012.)*

**Principle.** Accumulated radiation-damage energy in sand or flint released by laser (OSL) or heating (TL); signal zeroed when strongly heated in a hearth; with environmental dose rate yields burial/heating age. Landmark applications Stringer recounts: TL on heated flints dating the [[kebara]] Neanderthal burial ~**60 ka**; first application to [[qafzeh]] early modern material ~**90 ka** — more than twice the generally expected age — reordering Levant moderns older than late Neanderthals (with ESR confirmation at Skhul/Tabun). **[[blombos-cave]]** MSA chronology uses four dating methods including luminescence on quartz grains and heated stone tools — one of the best-dated African MSA sites. Moroccan/Algerian MP *Nassarius* bead sites dated by luminescence or U-series to ~80–100 ka. Bohunician ~45 ka by radiocarbon and luminescence.
