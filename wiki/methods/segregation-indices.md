# Segregation Indices

## What It Does

Family of quantitative measures of how two (or more) groups are distributed across areal units of a city or metro. *[[massey-denton-american-apartheid-1993|American Apartheid]]* uses a **five-dimension** suite: unevenness ([[index-of-dissimilarity]]), isolation ([[isolation-index]]), clustering, centralization (Duncan-style), concentration — jointly defining [[hypersegregation]].

## Procedure

Compute indices from census tract (or block) counts by race; compare metros; track historical series where data allow. Hypersegregation rule (1980 analysis): ≥4 dimensions with scores > 60.

## Assumptions and Limitations

Evenness ≠ exposure; high minority share affects isolation; ecological fallacy risk when inferring individual motives; historical data quality varies.

## History

Taeuber tradition for D; Massey & Denton multi-dimensional program (*Social Forces* / *Demography* papers) synthesized in the 1993 book.

Dimensionality and cutpoints; interpretation of decline/rise post-1980 (later literature).
