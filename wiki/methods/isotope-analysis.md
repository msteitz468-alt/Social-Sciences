# Isotope Analysis

**Isotope analysis** in archaeology measures ratios of stable (and sometimes radiogenic) isotopes in organic and inorganic materials to reconstruct **climate**, **diet**, **mobility/provenance**, and **residue chemistry**. Renfrew & Bahn 2012 deploy the method across environmental archaeology (Ch. 6), subsistence (Ch. 7), exchange/characterization (Ch. 9), and bioarchaeology (Ch. 11). It is complementary to [[paleoethnobotany]], [[zooarchaeology]], [[bioarchaeology]], and [[radiocarbon-dating]] (itself a radioisotope method treated separately).

## What It Does

| Domain | Typical systems | Archaeological questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Paleoclimate** | ¹⁸O/¹⁶O in foraminifera, ice, speleothems; H isotopes in ice | Glacial–interglacial cycles; storminess; local cave climate |
| **Long-term diet** | ¹³C/¹²C and ¹⁵N/¹⁴N in bone collagen and tooth enamel | C3 vs C4 vs marine; trophic level; maize uptake; carnivory |
| **Plant/meat contribution** | Sr/Ca in bone (with caution) | Vegetarian vs meat emphasis; ranked access |
| **Mobility / geology** | Sr (and O) in tooth enamel | Local vs non-local childhood residence; exogamy |
| **Residues** | C/N isotopes of charred pot contents | Legume / terrestrial non-legume / marine plant groups |
| **Provenance** | Pb, Sr, O, C isotopes (metals, marble, shell, ivory) | Source characterization when trace elements fail |
| **Environment proxies** | C isotopes in rock varnish | Aridity gradients (stratification difficulties) |

Textbook slogan for diet work: **“we are what we eat”** — collagen and enamel integrate food intake over years; only archaeology supplies which species.

## Procedure

(As synthesized in Renfrew & Bahn 2012, attributed.)

1. **Select tissue and question.** Collagen (decadal diet) vs enamel (childhood diet/mobility; seasonal resolution via laser ablation); foraminifera/ice for climate; metals/marble for source.
2. **Sample and pretreat.** Clean, demineralize or extract collagen as required; control for diagenesis and contamination.
3. **Mass spectrometry.** Measure target ratios (δ¹³C, δ¹⁵N, δ¹⁸O, ⁸⁷Sr/⁸⁶Sr, Pb isotopes, etc.).
4. **Interpret against baselines.** Food-web end-members (local fauna/flora), geological isoscapes, marine vs terrestrial enrichment patterns.
5. **Integrate multi-proxy.** Combine ¹³C + ¹⁵N (e.g. Cuello Maya food web); pair Sr isotopes with aDNA (Eulau); never treat isotopes as a replacement for plant/animal macroremains.

### Diet pathway logic (textbook)

- **C3 plants** (trees, temperate grasses) vs **C4** (tropical grasses including maize) vs **marine** producers pass distinct ¹³C signatures up the food chain.
- **¹⁵N** rises with trophic level (low ≈ plant-heavy agriculture; high ≈ marine or heavy meat); coral-reef shellfish can be a low-N anomaly requiring carbon confirmation.
- **Sr/Ca** rises with plant (and mollusk) contribution, falls with meat — complement, not replacement, for carbon isotopes; sediment contamination requires caution.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- Isotopes answer **class-level** diet questions; species-level menus need [[paleoethnobotany]] and [[zooarchaeology]].
- New World maize (C4 into C3 landscapes) is ideal; Near Eastern C3 domesticates into C3 environments are poorly discriminated by carbon alone.
- Modern growth rates, food-web baselines, and isoscapes are assumptions when transferred to deep time.
- Short-term meals (stomachs, feces) and elite texts/art are different evidence classes from lifetime bone chemistry.
- Destructive sampling conflicts with conservation and repatriation ethics for human remains.

## History

- Earth-science oxygen-isotope paleoclimatology (deep-sea cores; ice cores Vostok, EPICA, GRIP/GISP2) provides global climatic background Renfrew & Bahn treat as prerequisite for local environmental archaeology.
- Archaeological bone-collagen diet studies (e.g. Tauber’s Danish Mesolithic–Neolithic marine-to-terrestrial shift) demonstrate rapid dietary change at farming transitions even on coasts.
- New World maize uptake studies (Orinoco; Ontario ~50% maize by ~AD 1400) “revolutionize” detecting food production (textbook wording).
- Residue isotope work (Hastorf & DeNiro, Upper Mantaro) identifies tubers when morphology fails.
- Mobility applications mature with Sr enamel (Eulau non-local women); characterization uses Pb/Sr/O isotopes for metals and marble where trace elements are ambiguous.
- Neanderthal collagen ¹³C/¹⁵N interpreted as nearly exclusive terrestrial herbivore carnivory vs broader modern-human diets including aquatic foods (French researchers, per textbook).

## Exemplary Applications

| Study / case | Finding (per Renfrew & Bahn 2012) |
|---|---|
| **Deep-sea & ice cores** | ¹⁸O tracks ice volume and temperature; modern CO₂ highest in ≥440,000 years |
| **Tauber / NW Europe** | Rapid Mesolithic marine → Neolithic terrestrial diets |
| **Ontario / Orinoco maize** | C4 maize dependence quantified |
| **Cuello (Belize)** | Maize ~35–40% of Preclassic Maya (and food dogs) diet |
| **Ambrose & DeNiro Africa** | Forager / pastoralist / farmer dietary separation |
| **Georgia coast (Larsen)** | Isotopes + pathology: maize transition impacts, stronger on females |
| **Eulau** | Sr enamel: patrilocal exogamy with aDNA kinship |
| **Chalcatzingo Sr/Ca** | Ranked meat access vs jade grave goods (contamination caution) |
| **Lead isotopes in metals** | Source characterization beyond trace elements |

## Debates

- How completely Neanderthals were carnivorous (isotopic readings vs other proxies).
- Reliability of Sr/Ca under diagenesis.
- Whether Mesolithic–Neolithic isotopic shifts equal cereal farming or broader terrestrial reorganization.
- Sampling ethics on ancestral human remains.
- Characterization: when isotopes beat trace elements for pottery/metals (smelting/scrap confuse elemental signatures).

## From Roberts, *The Holocene* (1998)

*(Attributed — [[roberts-holocene-1998]].)*

Roberts traces oxygen-isotope palaeothermometry from Harold Urey's "geological thermometer" and Cesare Emiliani's foraminiferal δ¹⁸O stages, noting the key **revision** that deep-sea δ¹⁸O is controlled primarily by **ice volume**, not temperature directly (ice sheets lock up light ¹⁶O) — so marine δ¹⁸O is a glaciation index, whereas in lake sediments, ice, and speleothems temperature dominates (see [[oxygen-isotope-stratigraphy]]). He also uses **carbon isotopes** (δ¹³C) to distinguish C4 plants (tropical grasses, maize) from C3, applied to bone to reconstruct past diet (e.g. early maize consumption at Tehuacán) and to reconstruct past salinity and rainfall.
