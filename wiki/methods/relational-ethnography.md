# Relational Ethnography

## What It Does

A fieldwork stance associated with [[desmond-matthew|Matthew Desmond]] (and programmatic writing with Mustafa Emirbayer) that treats **poverty and inequality as relationships** connecting advantaged and disadvantaged actors — e.g. landlords and tenants — rather than as traits of poor people or sealed poor places. In *[[desmond-evicted-2016|Evicted]]*, Desmond sought a process binding rich and poor in mutual dependence and struggle; **[[eviction]]** was that process (About This Project, attributed).

## Procedure

As practiced in *Evicted* (design facts):

1. Identify a **relational process** (eviction) rather than a quarantined “poor population.”
2. Embed on **both sides**: live among tenants; shadow landlords (Tobin’s trailer park; Sherrena/Quentin’s North Side portfolio).
3. Long-duration [[participant-observation]]: court, moves, shelters, churches, AA, funerals, births; digital recording of quotations; official documents.
4. Integrate with **survey and administrative** data (MARS; court study; PD nuisance/911; property records) so magnitude and mechanism inform each other.
5. Reflexively manage positionality, ethics, and rare interventions.

Desmond’s working definition of ethnography (About This Project): understand people by allowing their lives to mold your own as fully as possible — rapport, long stretches of time, observing and experiencing, working and playing alongside, recording until you begin to move, talk, think, and feel something like they do.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- **Positionality:** White researcher in black inner city is “not uniquely exposed to threats but uniquely shielded from them” (Desmond); police interactions, tenant protection, and stiffened self-presentation are validity issues.
- **Gender:** Long time with women in homes raised partner suspicion; reciprocity expectations (“nothing is free”).
- **Intervention:** Policy of minimal intervention abandoned at least twice; Desmond reports one case where advising Sherrena not to call the sheriff on Arleen changed the eviction trajectory.
- **Narrative form:** Third-person story reconstruction from fieldnotes/recordings; composite risk exists even when events are claimed as witnessed.

## History

Programmatic critique of structural-only vs individual-deficiency dualism and of ethnographies of the poor “in quarantine” (About This Project). Related article: Desmond, “Relational Ethnography,” *Theory and Society* 2014 (cited in Notes). Complements but does not replace classic urban [[participant-observation]] (Whyte, Anderson, Duneier traditions).

## Exemplary Applications

[[desmond-evicted-study]] — dual embedding with landlords and tenants around eviction; multi-method triangulation.

## Debates

Mixed-methods authority vs pure ethnography; generalizability from Milwaukee; ethics of dual loyalty to landlords and tenants; public-sociology narrative style vs academic report genre.
