# Ice-Core Analysis

Ice-core analysis reconstructs past climate and atmospheric composition from cores drilled through ice sheets and glaciers, where annual snowfall accumulates and is preserved as datable layers. It is documented by [[roberts-neil|Roberts]] ([[roberts-holocene-1998|1998]], Technical Box II, "Ice cores and climate change") as one of the highest-resolution and most influential archives of [[holocene-climate-change|Holocene]] and glacial climate.

## What It Does

The oxygen-isotope ratio (δ¹⁸O) and deuterium ratio (δD) of the ice vary with the temperature at which the snow formed, giving a palaeothermometer. Air bubbles trapped in the ice preserve samples of the past atmosphere, allowing direct measurement of greenhouse-gas concentrations (CO₂, CH₄). Dust, sea-salt, sulphate (volcanic and industrial), and other impurities record atmospheric circulation, volcanism, and — in recent layers — human pollution.

## Procedure

Cores are recovered by deep drilling (e.g. the Greenland GRIP and GISP2 cores to bedrock, the Antarctic Vostok core). Chronology is established by counting annual layers (visible stratigraphy, seasonal δ¹⁸O and chemistry cycles) near the top, and by ice-flow modelling and correlation at depth. Trapped gases are extracted under vacuum; isotopes are measured by mass spectrometry.

## Assumptions and Limitations

The method assumes preserved annual layering and known relations between δ¹⁸O and temperature. Deep layers are thinned and disturbed by ice flow, and the trapped-gas age is younger than the enclosing ice (firn close-off offset). Records are geographically restricted to ice-bearing regions and register the regional-to-hemispheric atmosphere rather than conditions at any archaeological site.

## History

Dansgaard's isotope work in the 1960s established the palaeothermometer; the GRIP and GISP2 Greenland cores (early 1990s) and the Vostok Antarctic core transformed Holocene and Quaternary climatology. Roberts (1998) singles out the Greenland Summit cores as one of the most spectacular data advances between his book's two editions, notably in revealing the abruptness of late-glacial climate change (see [[younger-dryas]]).

## Exemplary Applications

Ice cores fixed the formal base of the [[holocene|Holocene]] (the NGRIP GSSP), documented the near-decadal onset of Holocene warming, and provided the CO₂/CH₄ records central to understanding [[milankovitch-cycles|orbital]] and abrupt climate change.

## Debates

The abruptness of climate transitions recorded in the cores fuelled debate over ocean-circulation (thermohaline) triggering of events like the [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] — a mechanism Roberts (1998) presents in attributed voice.
