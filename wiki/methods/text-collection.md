# Text Collection (Native-Language Texts)

## What It Does

Systematic recording of dictated or written native-language texts — myths, family histories, speeches, procedural knowledge — as the primary ethnographic record, translated and analyzed afterward. [[codere-helen|Codere]] (1966) credits [[boas-franz|Boas]] with originating the method *for ethnographic purposes* (not just linguistics), as a deliberate solution to observer contamination: texts preserve what is important "to him" — the informant — rather than what a visitor would notice or distort.

## Procedure

Standardize an alphabet for the unwritten language; train a literate native collaborator (Boas taught [[hunt-george|George Hunt]] to write Kwak'wala in 1893); collect texts continuously (Hunt produced material for four decades, yielding over three thousand published pages); perform grammatical analysis as a prerequisite to translation; translate with repetitive cross-checking of doubtful points against the collaborator and other speakers. Boas did the final Kwak'wala editing and translation himself.

## Assumptions and Limitations

Boas's stated warrant (1935 preface): tales "probably contain all that is interesting to the narrators," so the corpus yields the people's own thought-world — including unconscious meanings, which Boas held more reliable than conscious "secondary explanations" (folk etymology being the paradigm case). Codere's counterpoint (1959): the method under-documents "informal culture" (jokes, gossip, byplay), and the sheer bulk of myth in the Kwakiutl record reflects both myth's social centrality *and* what dictation selects for. The collaborator layer is constitutive, not incidental — the Kwakiutl corpus is a Boas–Hunt co-production.

## History

Introduced within the Jesup-era Northwest Coast program (Boas's Kwakiutl work, 1886–1943). Boas repudiated his own first decade of hurried survey publications (1886–c.1895) in favor of the text-based record of 1897 and after. [[codere-helen|Codere]] cites Dell Hymes (1965) as later, independent endorsement of the texts' value.

## Exemplary Applications

*Kwakiutl Texts* (Boas & Hunt 1905, 1906); *Ethnology of the Kwakiutl* (1921); *The Religion of the Kwakiutl* (1930); *Kwakiutl Culture as Reflected in Mythology* (1935) — the comparative test against *Tsimshian Mythology* (1916). See [[boas-kwakiutl-ethnography-study]].

## Debates

Feeds the reliability side of the [[potlatch-interpretation-debate]] (what the texts record vs. what syntheses claimed) and the authorship question around [[hunt-george|Hunt]]'s role.
