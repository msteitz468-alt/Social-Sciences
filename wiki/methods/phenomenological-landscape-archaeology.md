# Phenomenological Landscape Archaeology

Field-interpretive method for studying how **monuments and locales sit in and act on the experienced landscape** — walking, looking, relating architecture to topography — associated with [[tilley-christopher]]'s *[[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-1994|A Phenomenology of Landscape]]* (1994) and [[phenomenological-archaeology]].

## What It Does

Fills the gap Tilley calls "middle-range" between the **site plan** and the **regional distribution map**: neither mole-in-the-trench nor aircraft-over-the-map, but on-the-ground relations among places, paths, and dominant natural features.

## Procedure

*(As demonstrated in Tilley 1994 Parts I–II.)*

1. **Theoretical framing** of space/place/landscape and bodily perception (Ch. 1).
2. **Ethnographic conceptual background** (not direct models) for land–myth–power relations (Ch. 2).
3. **Regional walking survey** of Mesolithic findspots and Neolithic monuments, recording:
   - relations to coast, rivers, rock outcrops, spurs, escarpments, ridges;
   - chamber/passage orientations and approaches;
   - intervisibility among monuments;
   - alignments to cursus/enclosures where relevant.
4. **Long-term comparison** Mesolithic locales → Neolithic monumentalization as process of enculturing land.

## Assumptions and Limitations

Assumes some continuity of **topographic structure** as meaningful reference even when vegetation/detail is unknown. Explicitly refuses direct ethnographic transfer. Mesolithic "locales" are often invisible surface scatters — method for Neolithic upstanding monuments is stronger than for Mesolithic presence.

## History

Emerges within Anglophone [[post-processual-archaeology]] as a landscape and embodiment strand. [[tilley-christopher|Tilley]]'s *[[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-1994|A Phenomenology of Landscape]]* (1994) is the vault's flagship demonstration; related earlier critique appears in Shanks & Tilley theory volumes (queued). Later work formalizes viewsheds (GIS) and reconceptualizes dwelling ([[dwelling-perspective]]) without identical method packages.

## Exemplary Applications

Pembrokeshire portal dolmens/chambered cairns; Black Mountains Cotswold–Severn long cairns; Cranborne Chase earthen long barrows, Dorset Cursus, Hambledon Hill complex ([[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-study]]).

## Debates

Relation to [[processual-archaeology|processual]] site-catchment models; subjectivity critiques within [[processual-postprocessual-debate]]; later dwelling/taskscape programs ([[dwelling-perspective]]).
