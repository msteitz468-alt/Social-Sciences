# Ethnographic Algebra

> Analytic method branded by [[leach-edmund|E. R. Leach]] in “Jinghpaw Kinship Terminology: an experiment in Ethnographic Algebra” (*J.R.A.I.* 75, 1945; reprinted with critical Introductory Note in *[[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961|Rethinking Anthropology]]* 1961, Ch. 2). Deduce the simplest terminology consistent with a handful of **ideal-rule hypotheses** about an idealized society; treat actual behaviour as a remote approximation of that ideal.

## What It Does

Given a kinship terminology that looks “extremely complex,” the method asks: what **minimal ideal social order** would make those terms simple and consistent to a person living inside it? The output is:

1. A set of structural **hypotheses** (descent, residence, exogamy, marriage direction, etc.).
2. **Rules** of term assignment (Occam constraints; age/sex/residence discriminations).
3. **Definitions** mapping reciprocal term-pairs onto the ideal pattern.
4. An explicit claim that structural analysis of kinship systems discusses **ideal behaviour**, not the statistical **norm** of actual behaviour.

## Procedure

*(Summary resolution — Leach 1945 as he presents it.)*

1. **Separate three levels:** (i) actual individual behaviour; (ii) the average of such patterns = the **norm**; (iii) the native’s own description = the **ideal**. Short fieldwork risks equating (ii) with (iii).
2. **State ideal-society hypotheses** (Jinghpaw case: patrilineal descent; patrilocal marriage; exogamy of the patrilineal-patrilocal group; polygyny allowed / polyandry not; immediate marriage of women at puberty as simplifying exaggeration; man marries from mother’s original group and not older than himself; woman never given back to mother’s original group — i.e. matrilateral preference + FZD ban).
3. **Apply economy rules** (never two terms where one serves; discriminate only when failure would violate hypotheses or incest bans).
4. **Generate term definitions** from childhood outward (Malinowski’s “biographical approach” — which Leach later partly repudiates).
5. **Check against practice:** village composition, ultimogeniture, five-clan mythology, *gumsa*/*gumlao* politics — showing how ideal *[[mayu-dama]]* frames cognition even when behaviour is a remote approximation.

Preferred marriage is between *gu* and *nam* in Jinghpaw categories — **not** “MBD marriage” as an English genealogical gloss. The rule is ideal behaviour with mythological sanction, not a statistical norm or conjectural history.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- **Ideal ≠ norm ≠ practice.** Terminology relates specifically to idealized social order; relation to actual behaviour is “tenuous.”
- **No general/normal form of e.g. MB–ZS behaviour** may be assumed a priori (contra a reading of Radcliffe-Brown 1940) — it must be demonstrated.
- **Translation hazard:** mapping native terms to “primary English equivalents” displaces focal meanings (*tsa* = MB + WF + others, not a pure “mother’s brother” role).
- **1961 self-critique:** 1945 Rule 2 (elementary family as universal of paramount importance, Malinowskian) led into **self-contradiction**; Leach would drop it and rewrite Rule 5; the Fortes [[complementary-filiation]] critique in Ch. 1 reflects this shift.
- **Not Morganian conjectural history** and not pure R-B structural analysis as Leach understands R-B’s formal rigidity claim.
