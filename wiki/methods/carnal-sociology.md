# Carnal sociology

## What It Does

**Carnal sociology**, as [[wacquant-loic|Loïc Wacquant]] develops it in *[[wacquant-body-and-soul-2004|Body & Soul]]*, is a sociology not only *of* the body (as object) but *from* the body: the researcher’s organism, sensibility, and incarnate intelligence become tools of inquiry and vectors of knowledge. The aim is to clasp the carnal dimension of existence — “the taste and the ache of action” — that established social-science approaches typically mute.

Wacquant presents the book as both sociological-pugilistic *Bildungsroman* and scientific experiment demonstrating that the social agent is first a being of flesh, nerves, and senses (citing young Marx’s *leidenschaftliches Wesen*). He insists the research was *not* initially designed to prove the method: need to master an unanticipated transformative experience drove the theme.

## Procedure

Summary resolution (wiki, not manual):

1. **Initiatory immersion** — enter as apprentice; seek moral and sensual conversion to the craft’s appetites and competencies.
2. **Theoretically arm** the immersion — [[habitus]], [[hexis]], [[practical-sense]], [[practice-theory]] prevent mere native reportage (Wacquant’s condition *sine qua non*).
3. **Document meticulously** — field diary of events, interactions, conversations (Woodlawn: ~2,300 pages over ~3.5 years).
4. **Prefer “observant participation”** in the craft’s natural habitat over solicited public self-presentation (ecological-fallacy critique).
5. **Retranslate** practical comprehension into writing that conveys kinetic culture (photographs, notebook fragments, narrative/analysis braid).

### Exemplary application — Woodlawn (*Body & Soul*)

- Apprentice boxer (“Busy Louie”) at [[woodlawn-boys-club|Woodlawn Boys Club]] (Aug 1988–Feb 1992).
- Competed Golden Gloves 1990; sparred; corner work; ~30 cards.
- **Triple challenge:** learn the craft; grasp ghetto relations from that location; account for intensely corporeal practice.
- Object shift: gym as window for 16 months → craft as study object after inner-circle induction.
- Native understanding as necessary condition of a science of this “social art” (Durkheim: pure practice without theory).

## Assumptions and Limitations

- Social agents are carnal; social order inscribes itself in bodies (Wacquant citing Bourdieu, *Pascalian Meditations*).
- Practical mastery yields knowledge inaccessible to detached interviewing alone.
- Access, body type, gender, age, and risk constrain which universes admit the method.
- Distinct from generic [[participant-observation]] by explicit **bodily conversion** and rhetorical demand to restitute carnal experience.
- Protective gatekeepers can end immersion (DeeDee after Gloves: “You got enough to write your damn book now”).

## History
