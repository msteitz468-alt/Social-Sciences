# Social Structure and Anomie (Merton, 1938 / STSS VI–VII)

Landmark theoretical essay (original 1938; expanded in *[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|Social Theory and Social Structure]]* Chapters VI–VII) that reworked Durkheimian [[anomie]] into a **structural theory of deviance**: pressure toward deviation arises from the disjunction between **cultural goals** and **institutionalized means**, differentially distributed by class structure.

## Question and Context

Why does the frequency and type of deviant behavior vary by position in the social structure, without reducing deviance to individual pathology? Merton wrote against psychologistic accounts that located "faulty operation" of social structures in biological or personality defects alone.

## Design and Methods

Not a primary survey or ethnography. Merton constructed an analytic typology and illustrated it with U.S. cultural materials, crime and occupational patterns, and secondary observations. Continuities chapter (VII) reviews subsequent literature and refinements. Unit of analysis: the cultural–social structure articulation and modal adaptations of positioned actors.

## Findings

(Attributed to Merton.)

- Cultural goals and institutional norms vary independently in relative emphasis; imbalance (especially goal emphasis without equivalent means emphasis) produces anomie.
- Five **modes of adaptation**:

| Mode | Goals | Means |
|---|---|---|
| Conformity | accept | accept |
| Innovation | accept | reject/substitute |
| Ritualism | reject/abandon | accept |
| Retreatism | reject | reject |
| Rebellion | replace | replace |

- American society, he argued, chronically over-emphasizes monetary success while class structure restricts legitimate avenues — generating pressure toward **innovation** (including crime) especially in lower strata, with other modes patterned differently.

## Theoretical Impact

Founding text of strain theory in criminology; central middle-range exemplar; redefined [[anomie]] for U.S. sociology (must not be silently equated with Durkheim 1897 — see concept page).

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Class-crime assumptions reanalyzed; cultural goals less monolithic than assumed; opportunity structures (Cloward & Ohlin) and control theories as rivals; gender and race undertheorized in original. `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — core insight (structure shapes deviance modes) remains generative; specific empirical claims heavily revised.

## Current Standing

Still taught as classic typology; live research program under "strain" labels with modified mechanisms. Primary text for this wiki: STSS Chs. VI–VII via [[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968]].
