# Governing the Commons — study

Landmark **comparative institutional analysis** by [[ostrom-elinor|Elinor Ostrom]] (*[[ostrom-governing-the-commons-1990|1990]]*). Not classical ethnography; a theory-driven synthesis of field cases on self-governance of [[common-pool-resource|CPRs]].

## Question and Context

Can users of shared natural resources craft durable institutions for collective action without relying only on Leviathan or privatization? Framed against Hardin, Olson, and PD policy metaphors in late-20th-century resource politics.

## Design and Methods

Small-scale CPRs within one country; **50–15,000** persons heavily dependent on the resource (fisheries, grazing, groundwater, irrigation, communal forests). Compares long-enduring systems (Ch. 3: Törbel, Japan, Spanish huertas, Philippine zanjeras), institutional supply/change (Ch. 4: California groundwater basins), and failures/fragilities (Ch. 5: Turkish fisheries, Sri Lanka, Nova Scotia, etc.). Ch. 6 framework incomplete in the ingested OCR scan. Evidence class: secondary cases + workshop materials; attributed throughout.

## Findings

*(Attributed to Ostrom.)* Self-governance is possible under structured conditions; eight [[design-principles-cpr|design principles]] recur in robust cases and are largely absent in failures; [[polycentricity|polycentric]] public-enterprise systems can end pumping races without full privatization or central ownership; Alanya fishery illustrates assignment rules crafted by fishers themselves; metaphors from Hardin/Olson/PD can license harmful policy when local institutions are ignored.

## Theoretical Impact

Founded the modern CPR / commons-governance research program ([[commons-governance-theory]]); 2009 Nobel context.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Later large-N and meta-analyses test design principles with mixed support; critiques of selection and secondary sources; IAD elaborations. Status: **partially-supported** program, not single-study replication.

## Current Standing

Core syllabus text in political science and environmental governance.
