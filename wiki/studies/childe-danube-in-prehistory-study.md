# The Danube in Prehistory (as a study)

Landmark comparative synthesis treated as a research act: Childe's assembly of closed finds into a culture-historical sequence for the Danube world and his methodological definition of culture. Source: [[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929]]. Theory: [[culture-historical-archaeology]].

## Question and Context

How to order the prehistoric remains of Central Europe so that cultures stand in relative (and, where possible, absolute) sequence, and so that the Danube's role as a corridor between the ancient East Mediterranean and north-western Europe can be stated. Interwar European archaeology had rich local museums and uneven publication; Childe aimed at a synthetic order of cultures rather than a national prehistory.

## Design and Methods

- **Unit of analysis:** [[archaeological-culture]] (Preface).
- **Evidence:** published closed finds and museum collections; author notes uneven exploration and unvisited local museums.
- **Sequence:** stratigraphy preferred; typology under stated conditions; cross-dating by "accidental" imports.
- **Absolute chronology:** Aegean / Egyptian / Mesopotamian synchronisms — thin before the Copper Age; Childe's table (e.g. Aunjetitz and Hungarian phases) is a 1929 construction.
- **Exposition order:** cultures in chronological order; traits from closed finds first; external relations second (Preface plan).

## Findings

*(All attributed — Childe 1929.)*

1. **Method charter** — culture / people / race / movements / influences / survivals / accidental agreements (see [[archaeological-culture]]).
2. **Corridor** — Danube as shortest highway NW Europe ↔ Aegean/Ancient East; for >2000 years SE influence flowed north; Period VI inverts the flow.
3. **Culture sequence (summary)** — epipalaeolithic north residual; Neolithic from SE (Vinča, Danubian I expansion on loess); Period II complexes (Lengyel–Jordansmühl, Polgár, painted pottery); Nordic/Corded and Beaker currents; Copper Age Hungary; Early Bronze Aunjetitz; Hungarian BA and urnfields; Tumulus; Lausitz and Alpine urnfields; late Scythian limited impact.
4. **Britain** — first linked via Beaker-folk trade (amber–tin–Adriatic); later attenuated Rhine transmission.

## Theoretical Impact

Made the English definition of archaeological culture standard; supplied the culture-sequence backbone later rewritten by radiocarbon but still named in textbooks. Anchors [[culture-historical-archaeology]] as a research program.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- Absolute chronology largely superseded (radiocarbon revolution).
- Culture = people assumption contested by processual archaeology and by aDNA (sometimes support, sometimes refute demographic readings).
- Childe himself flags reversible chronological choices (Vinča priority, Tószeg vs Aunjetitz, Nordic synchronisms) in the Epilogue.
- `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — the method and many labels survive; the 1929 clock and many migration stories do not.

## Current Standing

Foundational **historiographical** document for European prehistory and for the concept of archaeological culture; not a current chronological handbook. Read with Identity Cautions on every culture page and with later absolute dates where ingested (e.g. Reich 2018 on Corded Ware / Bell Beaker).
