# Doing Gender (West & Zimmerman, 1987)

Landmark theoretical article in *Gender & Society* that founded the [[doing-gender]] research
program. Reprinted as Chapter 1 of [[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]].
This `studies/` page treats the article as a reference-point research act; deep analysis:
[[hubs/studies/west-zimmerman-doing-gender-study|Studies Hub]].

`replication_status: not-applicable` — the piece is a conceptual/programmatic intervention,
not a single empirical finding set; later empirical applications and the
[[doing-difference-debate]] constitute its afterlife rather than classical replication.

## Question and Context

Against 1970s classroom sex/gender teaching, role theory, and Goffman's gender-display model,
West and Zimmerman asked how gender is produced as a situated, accountable feature of
everyday interaction. Manuscript ~1977; repeated journal rejection; first public empirical
use in Fenstermaker's *Gender Factory*; published 1987 under Judith Lorber's editorship.

## Design and Methods

Not a survey or ethnography of one site. Method: ethnomethodologically informed theoretical
reconstruction, using Garfinkel's [[garfinkel-agnes-study|Agnes]] case and everyday examples
(e.g., ambiguous sex categorization; Kessler & McKenna on gender attribution; Sacks's
if-can test) to make visible the work of categorization and accountability that "normal"
incumbents take for granted. Original publication: *Gender & Society* 1(2): 125–151 (1987);
Note 1 records a 1977 ASA paper version. `replication_status: not-applicable` — conceptual
charter, not a single empirical estimate.

Deep reconstruction: [[hubs/studies/west-zimmerman-doing-gender-study|Studies Hub]].

## Findings

See frontmatter `key_findings` (all attributed). Core conceptual product: the sex /
[[sex-category]] / gender triad and the definition of doing gender as accountable
accomplishment under omnirelevant sex category — "at the risk of gender assessment," not
necessarily conformity to ideals. Closing argument: appropriate doing gender legitimates
institutional arrangements as natural; social change requires interactional *and*
institutional levels.

## Theoretical Impact

Organized a generation of gender-as-interaction research in U.S. sociology; vocabulary
absorbed into education, work/organizations, and medical sociology; extended by West &
Fenstermaker to race/class ([[doing-difference]]); dialogue and friction with Butlerian
performance theory and intersectional/structural critiques.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- Role-theory readers who reduced the paper to "micro" role enactment (early rejection theme)
- "Doing Difference" symposium and reply — [[doing-difference-debate]]
- Ongoing debates on power, structure, and change within the 2002 volume (esp. Ch. 9–11)
- Relation to Garfinkel's Agnes afterlife (disclosure that Agnes had taken estrogen) is
  downstream of [[garfinkel-agnes-study]], not of West & Zimmerman's own fieldwork

## Current Standing

Canonical in sociology of gender; still actively taught and cited. Program status: **active**,
with contested multi-category extension.
