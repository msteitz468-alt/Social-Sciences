# Kinship and Marriage among the Nuer (Evans-Pritchard, 1951) — Study Summary

Middle volume of the Nuer trilogy. Source: [[evans-pritchard-kinship-marriage-nuer-1951]]. Hub: [[hubs/studies/evans-pritchard-kinship-marriage-nuer-study]]. Society: [[nuer]]. Complements *[[evans-pritchard-nuer-study|The Nuer]]* (1940) and *[[evans-pritchard-nuer-religion-study|Nuer Religion]]* (1956); ASKAM 1950 local-community essay ([[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-study]]) previews Ch.I.

## Question and Context

*(Attributed — Evans-Pritchard 1951, Preface.)* How do marriage, family, and kinship work among the Nuer once political structure is set aside as a separable problem? *The Nuer* had taken village kinship for granted; this volume supplies the domestic system — prohibited degrees, marriage process, family forms, and kinship categories — that make local communities of kith also communities of kin.

## Findings

*(Attributed — Evans-Pritchard 1951; documentation moment 1930s.)*

1. **Local community as kinship network.** Every villager can trace *mar* to every other; lineage (*thok dwiel*) is not coextensive with the residential cluster. *Buth* = agnatic relation between collateral lineages; *mar* = interpersonal kinship (paternal and maternal).
2. **Marriage prohibitions / incest (*rual*).** Wide cognatic and affinal range; clan exogamy with spear-name logic; age-set “daughter” ban; sanctions ladder from *kwol* to ox + leopard-skin chief. Incest secondary to marriage-prohibition logic in EP’s framing.
3. **Marriage process.** Courtship → ceremonies (*larcieng*, *ngut*, *mut*) → bridewealth → completion by birth of child. Dual institution: cattle + rites.
4. **Bridewealth.** Historical ideal ~**40** cattle; usual ~**20–30** after rinderpest and colonial stop of Dinka raids; floor ~**16**. Creates “children of the cattle”; **pater** = man in whose name cattle paid, whether genitor or not. Parallel to bloodwealth (also ideal 40).
5. **Union typology.** Simple legal marriage (standard); polygynous complex; **[[ghost-marriage]]**; [[levirate]] (not remarriage — widow remains dead man’s wife); widow-concubinage; unmarried concubinage; woman-marriage (woman as pater).
6. **Family and kinship system.** Dual pull of father’s lineage vs mother’s people; “bulls” vs *gaatnyiet*; elastic-band extension of kinship with social contacts; kinship and political systems interconsistent but not identical.

## Theoretical Impact

Landmark case for ghost-marriage, true levirate vs widow inheritance, cattle bridewealth as jural instrument, and pater/genitor distinction under agnatic descent. Feeds [[unilineal-descent]], [[marriage]], [[marriage-payments]], [[segmentary-lineage-system]].

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Shares the afterlife of the Nuer political corpus ([[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]]); later kinship theory challenged corporate-descent models generally. Status: **reanalyzed-contested**.
