# Stigma (as a study)

Analysis of [[goffman-erving|Erving Goffman]]'s *Stigma: Notes on the Management of Spoiled Identity* (1963) as a research act. Bibliographic record: [[goffman-stigma-1963]].

Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/goffman-stigma-study|Studies Hub]].

## Question and Context

Goffman asked what the scattered social-psychological and clinical literature on persons "disqualified from full social acceptance" yields when marked off for **sociology** — especially under a conceptual scheme of **[[social-information|social information]]** (the information the individual directly conveys about himself). He aimed to unify stigma material, relate it to [[deviance]], and supply an economical descriptive language (Preface, attributed).

**Intellectual setting:** postwar American sociology and social psychology; decade of work after Lewin, Heider, Dembo, Barker, Wright (especially Beatrice Wright, *Physical Disability*, 1960); clinical studies of deformity; MacIver Lecture (Southern Sociological Society, Louisville, 13 April 1962); Center for the Study of Law and Society, UC Berkeley, under a President's Committee on Juvenile Delinquency grant. Continuity with *[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]* ([[moral-career]] of the mental patient) and with interaction-order work after *Presentation of Self* (1959 — primary not yet ingested). Earlier summary version in Greenblatt, Levinson & Williams, *The Patient and the Mental Hospital* (1957).

**Epigraph:** Nathanael West, *Miss Lonelyhearts* — "Desperate," born without a nose — frames human stakes before analysis (placed at Ch 5 / front of Penguin reading order).

## Design and Methods

Conceptual essay, not single-site ethnography.

| Element | As conducted (attributed / FACT of form) |
|---|---|
| Form | Five-chapter progressive scheme (social identity → personal identity/information → ego identity/alignments → self and other → deviance boundary) |
| Evidence | Secondary literature + quoted first-person and clinical cases; no St. Elizabeths-style field campaign |
| Analytic tools | Ideal types; relational definitions; information-state distinctions; spatial partitions; moral-career patterns |
| Unit | Face-to-face mixed contacts; information control; group-alignment politics of ego identity |

**What the design can do:** extract shared situational dynamics across heterogeneous stigma categories. **What it cannot:** category-specific political economy, probability samples, or clinical etiology.

## Findings

All attributed to Goffman unless noted.

### Ch 1 — Stigma and social identity

- Virtual vs actual social identity; stigma as deeply discrediting discrepancy and as **relationship** of attribute to stereotype.
- Double perspective: **[[discredited-and-discreditable|discredited]]** (tension) vs **discreditable** (information).
- Three types: body; character; tribal (plus class-as-tribal footnote for Britain).
- Normals; discrimination; **stigma-theory** ideology; shame; problem of acceptance; mixed contacts as primal scenes.
- **[[the-own-and-the-wise|The own and the wise]]**; **[[courtesy-stigma]]**; normalization vs normification.
- **[[moral-career]]** of persons with a stigma: two initial socialization phases; four timing patterns (inborn; protective capsule; late onset/discovery; alien-community then second socialization); affiliation cycles; retrospective turning points.

### Ch 2 — Information control and personal identity

- Social information (reflexive, embodied, abiding); prestige symbols, stigma symbols, **disidentifiers**.
- Visibility ≠ known-about-ness ≠ obtrusiveness; decoding capacity.
- Personal identity (identity pegs + unique biography); biographical others; cognitive vs social recognition.
- **[[passing]]** as management of undisclosed discrediting information; techniques of information control; spatial partitioning (forbidden/civil/back places).
- **[[covering]]**: keep known/apparent stigma from looming large without full concealment.

### Ch 3 — Group alignment and ego identity

- Social / personal / ego identity division of labor.
- Inevitable ambivalence; stratification of the own; professional presentations and codes (against complete passing, minstrelization, and normification).
- In-group and out-group alignments; politics of identity; "good adjustment" as psychiatric/normal demand (cheerfully same as normals while voluntarily withholding from awkward situations).

### Ch 4–5 — Self and other; deviations and deviance

- Continuum of precariousness; third solution of passing/covering/tacit cooperation under identity norms.
- **Normal deviant**; normal and stigmatized as **perspectives**, not two piles of persons; everyone plays both roles in some connexions.
- "The stigmatized and the normal are part of each other"; situations can be proven the fool.
- Social functions vary by stigma type (formal social control; competitive exclusion; courtship narrowing — last via Matza suggestion).
- Ch 5: deviation vs stigma; **social deviants** / deviant community as putative core of a deviance field; neighbouring categories (in-group deviant, isolate, licensed sick/high-status deviators, quietly disaffiliated).

## Theoretical Impact

Foundation text for stigma research across sociology, social psychology, disability studies, and health. Vocabulary still live: passing, covering, courtesy stigma, discredited/discreditable, spoiled identity. Bridges interaction order to labeling/deviance traditions. Links: [[symbolic-interactionism]], [[deviance]], [[moral-career]], *[[goffman-asylums-study|Asylums]]*.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

`replication_status: not-applicable` — conceptual scheme, not a single experimental finding. Critique lines (positions; expand when sources ingested): disability studies vs "normals"; structural stigma underplayed; tribal stigma thin relative to critical race scholarship; period character/sexuality lists; quantitative measurement leaves interaction detail behind. No Mead–Freeman-style single restudy overturns the book.

## Current Standing

Canonical mid-century interactionist treatment of spoiled identity. Contemporary research is often not "Goffmanian" in method (scales, multilevel models, intersectionality) but remains conceptually downstream. Read with *Asylums* for institutional careers and with later structural-stigma work for what face-to-face management cannot explain.
