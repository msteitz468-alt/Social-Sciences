# A Forest of Kings — Study Analysis

**Source record:** [[schele-freidel-forest-of-kings-1990]]

## Question and Context

Can the ancient Maya be written as **political history** — named kings, dated wars, dynastic strategy — rather than as timeless ceremonial civilization? Schele & Freidel (1990) answered yes for a general and scholarly public, deploying the post-[[proskouriakoff-tatiana|Proskouriakoff]] / post-[[knorosov-yuri|Knorosov]] decipherment to narrate lowland Maya history from Preclassic kingship origins through Terminal Classic collapse and northern Chichen florescence.

## Design and Methods

- **Evidence classes mixed:** hieroglyphic monuments (historical events, accessions, wars), iconography (masks, lintels, pottery scenes), and excavation (especially Freidel’s [[cerros]]).
- **Narrative method:** chapters combine technical paraphrase of texts with **reconstructed scenes** (dialogue, interior monologue). Per source FLAG: reconstructed scenes are interpretive devices, not primary data.
- **Chronology:** Thompson (GMT) correlation framework as then standard; Long Count dates converted to CE.
- **Unit of analysis:** royal courts and emblem-glyph polities — elite scale; commoners appear as constituency and labor backdrop.

## Findings

*(All attributed to Schele & Freidel 1990; later epigraphy often revises names and some links.)*

1. **Preclassic kingship:** Cerros Structure 5C-2nd program = public *ahau* cosmology by ~50 BCE.
2. **Early Classic war:** Tikal under Great-Jaguar-Paw / Smoking-Frog horizon conquers or subordinates Uaxactun (AD 378 frame); Teotihuacan-linked war ritual appears.
3. **Seventh-century “star wars”:** Caracol, Naranjo, Calakmul, Tikal locked in Venus-associated warfare and hierarchical patronage.
4. **Late Classic courts:** Palenque theology of First Mother and succession; Yaxchilan *cahalob* (secondary lords) under Bird-Jaguar; Copan crisis after 18-Rabbit’s capture by Quirigua.
5. **North and end:** Chichen Itza as new political form; southern collapse as failure of literate divine-kingship order.

## Theoretical Impact

Canonical popular vehicle for **historical Maya archaeology**; shaped museum, textbook, and public narratives of named kings. Intellectual sibling to *The Blood of Kings* (Schele & Miller 1986). Downstream chronicle form: [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008]].

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Name and glyph revisions:** Many 1990 nicknames (Great-Jaguar-Paw, Ah-Cacaw, 18-Rabbit, etc.) replaced by fuller phonetic readings in later corpora (Martin & Grube 2008 generation).
- **Over-narrativization:** Critics treat dramatized scenes as exceeding evidence; defenders treat them as pedagogical hypotheses.
- **Political model:** Hierarchical [[overkingship]] is clearer in Martin & Grube than in the 1990 prose; do not read 1990 as the final Great Game model.
- **Collapse:** Multi-causal and crisis-of-faith framing anticipates later debates; paleoclimate and LIDAR demography (post-1990) densify other causal classes — see [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]].
- **replication_status:** `reanalyzed-contested` — core historical program stood; many details superseded.

## Current Standing

Foundational **historiographic** artifact of the decipherment era’s public face; still cited for Cerros kingship argument and for the style of Maya political narrative. Use with Martin & Grube / Coe & Houston for updated dynastic facts; keep Schele & Freidel claims date-stamped **(as of 1990)**.

### Empirical anchors densified this ingest

- **[[cerros]]:** Freidel project; Structure 5C-2nd *ahau* program ~50 BCE (authors).
- **[[tikal]] / [[uaxactun]]:** AD 378 war; Smoking-Frog / Curl-Snout political structure (1990 nicknames); authors reject simple Teotihuacan military takeover.
- **[[caracol]] / [[calakmul]] / [[naranjo]] / [[dos-pilas]]:** 556–562 Tikal defeat; seventh-century hierarchical wars; Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau Naranjo revival; 695 Tikal recovery.
- **[[palenque]]:** Pacal–Chan-Bahlum succession theology; ruling queens Kanal-Ikal and Zac-Kuk (authors’ reading).
- **[[yaxchilan]]:** Lady Xoc bloodletting; Bird-Jaguar *cahalob* network.
- **[[copan]] / [[quirigua]]:** 18-Rabbit capture 738; Yax-Pac ecological and noble-power crisis.
- **[[chichen-itza]]:** unified cosmopolitan city vs sequential Toltec conquest (POSITION 1990).
- **Collapse:** multi-strand crisis of literate divine kingship — see [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]].
