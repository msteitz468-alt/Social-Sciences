# Sources of Social Power Vol. I (study)

Landmark comparative-historical synthesis by [[mann-michael|Michael Mann]] (*[[mann-sources-of-social-power-vol1-1986|1986]]*), treated here as a research act: theory construction plus long-run secondary narrative of agrarian power.

Program depth lives in Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/iemp-model]] and Thinkers Hub [[hubs/thinkers/mann-michael]] (no separate Studies Hub — research act is theory-program synthesis, not a classic single-site study).

## Question and Context

Can a general sociology of power avoid unitary "society" models and economic monism while still explaining stratification, states, empires, religions, and European dynamism? Mann answers with the [[iemp-model|IEMP]] organizational model and a history from prehistory to 1760.

## Design and Methods

- Unit of analysis: overlapping [[power-networks]], not national societies.
- Evidence class: secondary historical, archaeological, and classical scholarship (mid-1980s).
- Method: comparative narrative punctuated by organizational "spurts"; neo-episodic (Gellner), Weberian messiness discipline; logistics measurement (e.g. ~80–90 km ancient unsupported march).
- **Not** primary excavation, survey, or ethnography — reliability is that of a theorist's synthesis.

## Findings

Attributed to Mann (load-bearing):

1. Societies are multiple overlapping IEMP networks, not totalities.
2. Most prehistoric peoples evade stable power; [[social-caging]] makes civilization/stratification/state rare and local.
3. [[multi-power-actor-civilization]] and [[empire-of-domination]] (via [[compulsory-cooperation]]) alternate as high-power agrarian forms; Rome pioneers territorial empire.
4. Ideological power as transcendence vs immanent morale — Christian ecumene reorganizes late Roman world; comparative world religions (Ch. 11).
5. European dynamism = accident + two macropatterns + capitalism / Christendom / states; [[organic-national-state]] and inter-national (not transnational) capitalism by 1760.
6. No general historical materialism; class roles vary by infrastructure and phase; class and nation share diffuse-message preconditions.

## Theoretical Impact

Standard multi-source power framework in historical sociology; [[infrastructural-power]]/[[despotic-power]] pair entered general state theory; standing dialogue partner for Tilly/Skocpol/Wallerstein generation.
