# The Elementary Forms of Religious Life (as a study)

Analysis of *[[durkheim-elementary-forms-1912|Les formes élémentaires de la vie religieuse]]* (1912; Swain 1915) as a research act — distinct from the bibliographic source page and the [[durkheim-emile|thinker page]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/durkheim-elementary-forms-study]]. All substantive findings in **attributed voice**.

## Question and Context

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] proposes to study "the most primitive and simple religion which is actually known" in order to explain **the religious nature of man** — an essential and permanent aspect of humanity — not antiquarian curiosities. Sociology, he argues, explains actual realities near us; archaic religion is chosen because its organization is simplest and can be explained without elements borrowed from a prior religion. Against the charge that comparing Australian cults to Christianity insults the latter, he holds that institutions cannot rest on pure error: "there are no religions which are false" in the sense that all answer conditions of human existence; science must go under the symbol to the reality it represents (attributed).

The book continues the [[social-realism|social-realist]] program of *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|Division of Labor]]*, *Rules*, and *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*: explain social facts by social facts; treat collective forces as real. It also advances a **sociology of knowledge**: the categories of the understanding are social in origin.

## Design and Methods

| Element | As conducted |
|---|---|
| **Unit** | Religion as a system of beliefs and rites in a society; inductive generalization from one "elementary" type |
| **Case** | Australian totemism (clan totems, churinga, intichiuma, etc.) as reported by colonial ethnographers |
| **Method** | Define religion by essential characteristics → eliminate rival elementary theories (animism, naturism) → describe totemic beliefs and rites → explain origins → generalize to religion and knowledge |
| **Evidence** | Second-hand ethnography (Spencer & Gillen, Strehlow, Howitt, Fison, etc.); comparative asides (America, etc.) |
| **Not used** | Durkheim's own fieldwork; survey statistics; experimental design |

**Critical design features:** (1) **Single well-made experiment** logic — one carefully analyzed case licenses general laws, like biology of protoplasm. (2) **Evolutionary primitivism** — "simplest" society as window on permanent human nature (period frame). (3) **Definition first** — sacred/profane + church before gods.

## Findings (attributed — summary)

- **Definition of religion:** dualism of sacred and profane things; beliefs and rites; unified moral community (church). Magic lacks a church.
- **Animism and naturism rejected** as accounts of elementary religion.
- **Totemism** is the elementary religion; clan and totem structure Australian social and religious life as Durkheim reads it.
- **Totemic principle / sacred force** is society hypostatized; emblem concentrates collective force.
- **[[collective-effervescence|Collective effervescence]]** in assembly produces the experience of forces outside the individual.
- **Soul, spirits, gods** extend the same social genesis.
- **Ritual:** negative cult (interdicts, asceticism); positive cult (sacrifice, imitation, commemoration); piacular rites (mourning, expiation); sacredness is ambiguous (pure/impure).
- **Categories of understanding** (time, space, genus, causality, force, personality) have religious/social origins.

## Theoretical Impact

Founds the sociology of religion as a core sociological subfield; supplies the sacred/profane dualism and effervescence vocabulary of later theory (functionalism, structuralism, ritual studies, cultural sociology). Thickens [[collective-conscience|collective conscience]] into **[[collective-representations|collective representations]]** and the sacred. Anchors debates on functional explanation of religion, the social construction of knowledge, and the use of "primitive" cases. The **categories of the understanding** thesis continues and enlarges the 1903 program of *[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Primitive Classification]]* (with Mauss) — Gehlke, Alpert, and Needham treat the earlier essay as initial formulation; see [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]] and [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]].

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Ethnographic base:** Spencer & Gillen and related sources are colonial, salvage-era, and theory-laden; later Aboriginal anthropology revises totemism, clan structure, and ritual description (attributed reception).
- **Single-case induction:** generalizing from Australian totemism to all religion is contested (too narrow; Australia not "simplest"; totemism not universal elementary form).
- **Society as God:** charge of reducing religion to social worship / reifying society.
- **Sacred/profane dualism:** too sharp for many ethnographic cases (ambiguity Durkheim partly grants in piacular chapter).
- **Evolutionary and colonial frame:** "primitive," hierarchy of mental functions — period discourse, not wiki-voice science.
- `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — not a statistical study; afterlife is ethnographic reanalysis and theoretical critique.

## Current Standing

Still a reference point for sociology of religion, ritual theory, and cultural sociology; read as attributed theory + dated ethnography. Deep treatment: [[hubs/studies/durkheim-elementary-forms-study]].
