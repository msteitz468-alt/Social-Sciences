# Hacınebi Tepe and the Test of World-Systems (Stein) — as an Object of Analysis

Bibliographic record: [[stein-rethinking-world-systems-1999]]. This page analyzes the
*research act* — how [[stein-gil|Gil Stein]] designed the [[hacinebi|Hacınebi]] excavation
as a deliberate test of [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]] applicability to the
[[uruk-expansion|Uruk expansion]], and how the case has fared. It is the empirical
counterpart to [[algaze-uruk-expansion-study|Algaze's Uruk-world-system study]], and the two
together frame the [[uruk-world-system-debate]].

## Question and Context

Stein asked whether the [[uruk-expansion|Uruk expansion]] transformed and dominated its
Anatolian periphery, as an [[informal-empire|informal-empire]] / world-system reading
predicts — and set out to test it where the model's asymmetries should be most visible, in
the zone of primary contact rather than the Mesopotamian heartland. Hacınebi was chosen
because its stratigraphy offered both a **pre-contact baseline** (local phases A/B1) and a
**contact phase** (B2) with an apparent resident Uruk quarter — permitting a comparison of
local society "before and after" contact *and* a side-by-side comparison of the Uruk and
local quarters at the same moment. The work reacts against [[algaze-guillermo|Algaze's]]
model specifically and against the wider import of dependency-theory categories into
archaeology.

## Design and Methods as Conducted

A six-season (1992–1997) single-context excavation with a full specialist battery:
[[radiocarbon-dating|calibrated radiocarbon]] chronology, ceramic seriation, archaeobotany,
zooarchaeology with age/sex and butchery analysis, lithic analysis, geochemical bitumen
sourcing, and — the evidentially decisive technique — **INAA of sealing clays** to source
the two seal-using groups' clay. The design's power is that it operationalizes an abstract
theoretical dispute into concrete, independently measurable predictions: Stein specifies
**eight world-system correlates** and **eight [[distance-parity|distance-parity]]
correlates** (production intensification vs. continuity, asymmetric vs. symmetric exchange,
sudden vs. no complexity increase, dendritic vs. continuous settlement, and so on) and reads
the Hacınebi stratigraphy against both. The unit of analysis is the site and its two
communities; the inference to the wider Uruk network is by explicit gradient argument, not
extrapolation from the single case.

## Findings (attributed)

Stein reports (attributed): (1) the pre-contact local community was **already** a complex,
hierarchically organized society — monumental architecture, stamp-seal administration,
long-distance prestige exchange, on-site metallurgy — before any Uruk contact; (2) the
contact-phase Uruk enclave was a small minority, spatially segregated, that coexisted
**peacefully** (no fortification, weapons, or destruction) for 300–500 years; (3) the two
groups maintained **two parallel administrative systems** (indigenous stamp seals vs. Uruk
cylinder seals, bullae, a tablet), distinct diets and butchery practices, and each raised its
own animals — INAA showing the Uruk sealings' clay split between on-site local clay and
non-local clay matching the Susa area, i.e. two communities, one keeping homeland ties; (4)
local production showed **no** reorientation toward export surplus, no introduced irrigation
or craft specialization, and continuity in lithic technology across the contact horizon; (5)
there was no sudden increase in local complexity attributable to contact. Stein concludes the
enclave was an autonomous [[trade-diaspora|trade diaspora]] that "dealt with its host
community as an equal," surviving "at the sufferance of the local rulers," and that the case
lets him "reject the world-system model's assumptions of core dominance, asymmetric exchange,
and trade as the prime mover."

## Analytic and Rhetorical Moves

Three moves carry the argument. (1) **Symmetric research design** — by securing a pre-contact
baseline, Stein can separate "complexity present during contact" from "complexity caused by
contact," and answers his own explicit question ("Does correlation imply causation here?")
in the negative for both Hacınebi and Arslantepe. (2) **Multi-class convergence** — the
parity claim does not rest on ceramics (which he insists index style, not ethnicity) but on
the convergence of independent evidence classes: architecture, sealing systems, INAA
geochemistry, faunal diet/butchery, and archaeobotany all pointing the same way. (3)
**Gradient framing** — rather than universalize Hacınebi, Stein embeds it in a distance
gradient (coercive near-home Syrian colonies → autonomous far outposts), which pre-empts the
"unrepresentative single site" objection while conceding the site-to-site variation the
debate turns on.

## Theoretical Impact

The study is the principal empirical challenge to [[algaze-guillermo|Algaze's]] Uruk
world-system and the reference case for arguing that [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]]
vocabulary imports unwarranted dependency assumptions into pre-capitalist prehistory. It
supplied the [[distance-parity|distance-parity model]] and the archaeological
[[trade-diaspora|trade-diaspora]] concept to a literature (the Rothman and Postgate edited
volumes) organized around exactly that question, and, with Frangipane's Arslan Tepe work,
pushed Algaze to six self-corrections in his 2005 edition.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

`replication_status: not-applicable` — a single excavation project, not a replication of a
prior finding, though it is itself framed as a test of Algaze's claims. The standing
objection is one of **scale/representativeness**: Hacınebi is one far-edge site, and its
demonstrable local symmetry is logically compatible with system-wide asymmetry — which is
[[algaze-guillermo|Algaze's]] "decay with distance" rejoinder (with Matthews 2003 and Kohl
2001 judging Stein and Frangipane to have overcorrected). Because the excavator is also the
theorist and the interpretation rests on his own dig, the primary data's independence from
the theoretical claim is a live reliability consideration — mitigated by the number of
independent evidence classes that converge. Full positions on [[uruk-world-system-debate]].

## Current Standing

Hacınebi is settled as a *fact* — a genuine, long-lived, materially distinct Uruk enclave
inside a complex indigenous settlement — and contested as an *interpretation*: whether it
refutes the Uruk world-system or merely marks its expected far-edge decay. Its research
design is widely treated as the model for how to test interregional-interaction claims
archaeologically, cited on all sides of the debate.
