# Primitive Classification (as a study)

Analysis of Durkheim & Mauss, « De quelques formes primitives de classification: contribution à l'étude des représentations collectives » (*Année Sociologique* VI, 1903; Needham tr. 1963) as a research act — distinct from the bibliographic [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|source page]]. All substantive findings in **attributed voice**. Critiques drawn especially from [[needham-rodney|Rodney Needham]]'s Introduction in the English edition. Related later development: [[durkheim-elementary-forms-study|*Elementary Forms*]] (1912) on categories of the understanding.

## Question and Context

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] and [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]] ask what could have served as the **model** for complex systems of classification that every society possesses and that are cultural products "not to be found in nature." Against logicians and psychologists who treat the **classificatory function** as simple, innate, or instituted by individual powers alone, they argue that methods of scientific thought are "veritable social institutions whose origin sociology alone can retrace and explain" (attributed). They distinguish **moral/religious [[symbolic-classification|symbolic classification]]** (their object) from practical **technological** distinction-making (means of getting animals by water/air/ground, etc.), which they set aside as unsystematized and action-oriented.

The essay is framed as a contribution to the study of **[[collective-representations|collective representations]]** — early sociology of knowledge / sociology of logic, a decade before *[[durkheim-elementary-forms-1912|Elementary Forms]]* generalized the social origin of the categories (time, space, class, causality, etc.). Intellectual foil: [[unknown|J. G. Frazer]]'s opposite reading (men divided into clans by a pre-existing classification of things); D&M reverse the arrow: "they classified things because they were divided by clans" (attributed).

**Production context:** *Année Sociologique* collective work (Durkheim ~45, Mauss ~31); pure literary secondary research — no fieldwork. Evidence base: Howitt, Fison, Spencer & Gillen, Roth, Curr, Haddon, Cushing, Dorsey, de Groot, Doolittle, Young, and others (Needham later documented extensive bibliographic and citation errors in the French original).

## Design and Methods

| Element | As conducted |
|---|---|
| **Unit** | Systems of classification (classes, hierarchies, totalities) as collective representations |
| **Cases** | (1) Australian type — moieties, marriage classes, totemic clans; (2) other Australian variation (Arunta etc.); (3) Zuñi + Siouan regional/camp classifications; (4) Chinese correlative cosmology |
| **Sample** | Convenience sample of published ethnography/sinology available c. 1902; Australia treated as "most simple" known systems |
| **Method** | Morphological comparison of social divisions with divisions of things, space, and time; evolutionary series from simple → complex |
| **Period** | Published sources mostly 1870s–1901; essay 1903 |
| **Authors' field role** | None — armchair synthesis |

**Method commitments (attributed):** start from "most rudimentary" classifications; treat classification as hierarchical, totalizing, and speculative (to "make intelligible the relations which exist between things"), not primarily practical; read kinship idioms of logical relation ("same flesh," genus/species as family) as evidence of social origin.

**Needham's methodological judgment (position):** intensive limited-region comparison is preferable to Frazerian scatter; but D&M fail **concomitant variation** tests, ignore negative instances, assume single mode of classification at a time, and use unevidenced evolutionary sequences and source misrenders (see Critiques).

## Findings

All attributed to Durkheim & Mauss unless labeled as Needham.

### Load-bearing theoretical claims

1. **Classificatory function is not individual/innate.** Modern class concepts have history (Aristotle vs Plato); "prehistory" of classification is mental indistinction (metamorphosis, person–totem identity, sympathetic correspondences). The individual mind, left to itself, produces only fragmentary distinctions (right/left, past/present, resemblance) — not hierarchical classification (attributed).

2. **Society as model and as material of classes.** "Society was not simply a model which classificatory thought followed; it was its own divisions which served as divisions for the system of classification. The first logical categories were social categories; the first classes of things were classes of men… Moieties were the first genera; clans, the first species" (attributed).

3. **Logical hierarchy = social hierarchy.** Nesting of classes mirrors nesting of groups (sub-clan → clan → moiety → tribe); "the unity of knowledge is nothing else than the very unity of the collectivity, extended to the universe" (attributed).

4. **Logical ties as social ties.** Same-class things are "relatives," "same flesh"; pre-eminence of genus over species analogous to totem/chief/owner over subordinate (attributed).

5. **Affectivity.** How things are *divided among* classes is governed by sentimental affinities (collective *âme*), not pure understanding; species are "sentimental attitudes" as much as objects of knowledge (attributed). Needham: this factor is introduced late and without ethnographic evidence.

6. **[[sociocentrism|Sociocentrism]].** The center of first schemes of nature is not the individual (anthropocentrism) but the group — "better… called sociocentrism" (attributed).

7. **Science as demotion of social emotion.** "The history of scientific classification is, in the last analysis, the history of the stages by which this element of social affectivity has progressively weakened, leaving more and more room for the reflective thought of individuals" — yet the cadre of classification (habit of co-ordinated/hierarchized groups) survives (attributed).

8. **Program.** Same method for time, space, cause, substance, modes of reasoning — sociology of the categories of the understanding (attributed); fulfilled in part by *Elementary Forms*.

### Comparative case findings (attributed; second-hand)

| Case | Claimed pattern |
|---|---|
| **Australian type** | Moieties / marriage classes / totemic clans divide the universe; moiety = genus, class/clan = species; things assigned to social divisions; extends to rites, burial, dreams, message sticks |
| **Examples** | Port Mackay bipartite cosmos; Wakelbura four-class universe; Mount Gambier ten-clan inventory; Wotjobaluk sub-/tertiary/mortuary totems + spatial horizon figure |
| **Arunta & variation** | Extreme clan segmentation; "disintegration" of integrated classification; intermediates (Chingalee, Moorawaria) |
| **Zuñi** | Seven regions / clans / colors / prey animals; evolutionary story 4→6→7; Winter–Summer moieties; "mytho-sociologic" cosmos (via Cushing) |
| **Sioux** | Omaha camp circle (tribal space); Osage–Kansa–Ponka progressive orientation; Dakota quarters without clans |
| **China** | Animals, eight powers/trigrams, five elements, sexagenary cycle, *feng-shui* — same formal principles, more abstract/learned; **no proven** historical link of regions/hours to clans (authors admit gap; offer weak quasi-totemic analogies) |

**Technological vs symbolic:** Practical groupings (water/air/ground animals) are admitted as always present but "not schemes of classification" and are excluded from the analysis (attributed).

## Theoretical Impact

- **Early isolation of classification as sociological object** — Needham: cardinal achievement is conceiving the analytical notion of classification for sociological enquiry, whatever the causal failure.
- **Bridge to *Elementary Forms*** — Gehlke, Alpert, and Needham treat the essay as initial formulation of ideas later developed in 1912 (categories, totemism, collective representations).
- **[[sociology-of-knowledge]]** — Merton later lists Durkheim/Granet among classical SoK currents; Berger & Luckmann program is a later redefinition of the field, not continuous with this essay alone.
- **British/Dutch anthropology of [[symbolic-classification]]** — Hertz right/left line; Leiden school; Needham's own dual symbolic classification program; Lévi-Strauss *La Pensée sauvage* (1962) takes "primitive classification" as central concern (Needham notes).
- **Année method capital** — intensive limited comparison + totality/system precept vs Frazerian scatter (Needham's positive residual).

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Primary critique in this ingest: **Needham 1963 Introduction** (same volume). Positions (attributed to Needham unless noted):

1. **Metamorphosis / "mental confusion" do not prove absence of classificatory concepts** — mystical man–totem identity is not cognitive incapacity.
2. **Kinship idiom of logic does not prove social origin of the faculty** — metaphor and etymology ≠ genesis.
3. **No necessary formal correspondence** society → classification: Port Mackay vs Wakelbura (same social type, different concordance); Arunta negative instance explained away by "later development"; Zuñi game-distribution anomalies patched by unspecified "modifications."
4. **Petitio principii / evolutionary assumptions** without evidence (moieties before clans; Wotjobaluk clan before space; Omaha sequence; Mount Gambier).
5. **Concomitant variation not tested**; negative instances ignored (Evans-Pritchard's general charge against Durkheimian method).
6. **Chinese sources misrendered** (Young, Doolittle); "probable" inauspicious same-year marriage is wishful; "families" idiom smuggled for eight divisions.
7. **Sentiment as dominant characteristic** introduced without evidence; Lévi-Strauss: sentiment explains nothing (reverse causality risk).
8. **No logical necessity** of causal connexion society → classification; alternatives: common principles structuring both; reverse tilt (classification organizes society).
9. **Category of the understanding vs content of collective representations** — Gehlke/Dennes line: if "mind" = cognitive faculties, social origin of categories is circular (space needed to see groups as spatial model); if mind = culture-variable ideas, comparative study cannot yield origin of the *faculty*.
10. **Overall verdict (Needham):** "logically fallacious and methodologically unsound" — yet residual theoretical value high (analytical notion of classification; research program).

**Replication status:** `reanalyzed-contested` — not a statistical study to "replicate"; the causal thesis is widely contested; the comparative *object* (symbolic classification) remains active research territory. Worsley (1956, Groote Eylandt) is flagged by Needham as reanalysis of Durkheimian knowledge theory; not ingested here.

**Debate page:** [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]].

## Current Standing

- **As causal theory of the categories:** largely abandoned in Needham's form of the critique; *Elementary Forms* version remains a classic attributed position within Durkheimian [[social-realism]] and is still taught as a reference thesis, not as an established finding.
- **As research charter for symbolic classification:** alive — dual classification, right/left, correlative cosmologies, structural concordance by descent/alliance type (Needham's constructive residual).
- **As historical document:** key *Année* text; under-read mid-century (Needham's motive for translation); now standard in classical-theory syllabi often via *Elementary Forms* rather than the 1903 essay itself.
- **Evidence class caution:** all ethnographic claims are second-hand colonial ethnography and period sinology; four-way non-identity applies (Australian "totemic societies," Zuñi, Sioux, "China" are etic constructs in this text).

## Historiography

- Primary: *Année* 1903 French; Needham 1963 English (corrected apparatus).
- Needham's Introduction is itself a major secondary source on the essay's reception and flaws.
- Intellectual biography of Mauss's share vs Durkheim's not resolved in Needham (no clear division of labor; *Année* "collective conscience").
- Link to *Elementary Forms* is consensus among commentators (forerunner / initial formulation); exact textual filiation not re-audited in this ingest.
