# Land Filled with Flies (Wilmsen, 1989) — Study Summary

Landmark mixed historical–ethnographic–archaeological study that anchors **Position B (revisionist / enmeshment)** in the [[kalahari-debate]]. Bibliographic record: [[wilmsen-land-filled-with-flies-1989]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/wilmsen-land-filled-with-flies-study]]. Author: [[wilmsen-edwin-n]].

## Question and Context

*(Attributed — Wilmsen 1989, Introduction and Ch.1.)* What accounts for the presence of peoples labeled "Bushmen"/San who appear to practice an aboriginal foraging economy inside modern southern African states? Wilmsen answers by reconstructing San history inside indigenous and colonial political economies rather than treating them as remote evolutionary survivors. The study reacts against isolationist and *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]*–era uses of San ethnography as a window onto human origins, and against the [[foraging-mode-of-production]] / [[original-affluent-society]] package when those rest on a pristine-forager premise.

He states explicitly that the book is **not an ethnography** in the sense of excising a bounded society from history: San-speakers were "ethnograpized" (after Clammer) more thoroughly than Bantu-speaking coresidents and cut from a multi-ethnic formation they co-constructed over centuries.

Unit of analysis: San-speakers **within** a wider system of production, tenure, and ethnicity. Jack Parson's question — what accounts for "Bushmen" in a modern state? — redirected attention to colonial and precolonial archives.

## Findings

*(All attributed — Wilmsen 1989 unless noted.)*

### 1. Isolation is historically produced

Forager appearance is a function of relegation to a **rural underclass** under colonial (and earlier indigenous) social policies, not of residual Paleolithic autonomy. Conceptual isolation of "Bushmen" as an undifferentiated enclave among more "advanced" Africans was a prerequisite to administrative isolation. The ethnonyms Bushman / Basarwa / San are treated as **class and administrative categories** more than as stable ethnic entities; "San" as polite substitute changed little in practice.

### 2. Deep regional integration (archaeology and history)

The Recovered Past and Recorded Past chapters reconstruct Early Iron Age and later pastoral/settlement hierarchies (including western sandveld loci such as **Nqoma**, Divuyu, and related sequences) and nineteenth-century trade corridors (cattle, ivory, feathers; Orlam/Nama control of Atlantic-side routes; Walvis Bay–Lake Ngami). San-speakers appear as herders, traders, raiders, clients, and tributaries — not only as pure hunters. Merchant-capital penetration and later colonial labor regimes consolidate underclass position (cattle-post clearances, reenserfment episodes, mine-labor substitution).

### 3. Tenure, kinship, and production relations (CaeCae)

Wilmsen maps Zhu **nqore** (wiki: [[nore|n!ore]]) and **haro** (wiki: [[hxaro]]) into a multi-ethnic entitlement field overlapping Herero and Tswana systems. Inherited tenure privileges access to hunted meat and livestock accumulation; private ownership of means of production (tools, stock, guns, horses) is stressed. At CaeCae 1980 he reports **66%** of Zhu households owning no cattle or goats while **11%** (homesteads 1–3) held **~64%** of Zhu cattle; livestock-owning households rose only 27%→33% (1969–80) while mean cattle per owning household doubled 2.5→5.1. Class characteristics — differential accumulation, surplus extraction by *nqorekau*/*xhaiha* leadership, outmigration of excluded homesteads — are argued to be inherent in Zhu relations as engaged with regional history, not only recent impositions.

### 4. Energetics vs political economy (vs Lee)

July camp calories can **agree** with Lee (~2,140 / revised 2,355 vs Wilmsen ~2,294 for comparable July series) while year-round extrapolation is rejected: forager monthly means range roughly **1,408–3,114 kcal/person/day**; vegetables **60–98%** of forager budgets. Status, horses/rifles/dogs, and tenure structure returns more than raw time allocation. Lee's craft-time / store-food remedy for seasonal weight loss is rejected as mis-specifying underclass budgets (foragers spent little on food cash despite larger shortfalls).

### 5. Physiology as political economy

Seasonal mean weight amplitude **~3%** (1968–69, Lee/Howell data reanalyzed) vs **~6%** (1975–76, Wilmsen); independent/pastoral Zhu **~10% heavier** than foragers at all times; forager drought losses roughly **2×** pastoral/independent; weight-for-height ~**75%** of local Bantu for foragers vs **~83%** for independent/pastoral Zhu. Food-relief intervention rapidly raised mean weights. Fertility: completed fertility and TFR higher under agropastoral than pure-foraging conditions in his series. Bodies are read as expressing structural underclass position, not desert genetic destiny alone (with Hausman & Wilmsen growth data and national nutrition survey remote-area disadvantage).

### 6. Closing policy position

"Bushmen"/Basarwa/San talk is useless unless recognized as **class categorization**; fascination with a fixed forager image obscures present and future. Equal opportunity and exit from Remote Area underclass conditions require more than cultural preservation of a foraging type.

## Theoretical Impact

Primary monographic warrant for Kalahari revisionism; forced hunter-gatherer studies to treat contact depth, class, and colonial political economy as first-order variables. Interlocks with [[anthropological-political-economy]] ([[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] epigraph program) and polices [[ethnographic-analogy]] from living San to the Pleistocene. Directly densifies Position B of the [[kalahari-debate]] and Position 3 of the [[original-affluent-society-debate]].

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Isolationist replies (esp. Solway & Lee 1990 "Foragers, Genuine or Spurious?") contest whether long contact dissolves foraging autonomy or coexists with majority hunting-and-gathering and indigenous institutions ([[nore]], [[hxaro]], leveling). Lee 1979 already anticipates weight disputes by attributing larger mid-1970s losses to cash/sedentarization stress. Status: **reanalyzed-contested** — live debate, not laboratory replication failure. Do not adjudicate in wiki voice.

## Current Standing

Still the standard cited revisionist monograph. Must be read against primary isolationist ethnography ([[lee-kung-san-study]]) rather than secondary paraphrase. Hub depth: [[hubs/studies/wilmsen-land-filled-with-flies-study]].
