# Deep Play: Notes on the Balinese Cockfight — Study

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/studies/geertz-deep-play-study|Studies Hub]]

Landmark essay (*Daedalus* 1972; *[[geertz-interpretation-of-cultures-1973|Interpretation of Cultures]]* Ch. 15). Author: [[geertz-clifford]]. Exemplar of [[thick-description]] and [[interpretive-anthropology]].

## Question and Context

What is the Balinese cockfight as cultural form, and what does reading it as a **text** yield beyond gambling economics or simple social reinforcement?

## Design and Methods

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Site | Small Balinese village (~500), relatively remote; April 1958 arrival |
| Access narrative | Studied invisibility until shared flight from police raid on illegal school-fund cockfight makes ethnographers “real” |
| Methods | Participant observation of fights, betting, talk; interpretive reading |
| Form | Essayistic thick description, not monographic apparatus |

## Findings

*(Attributed to Geertz.)*

1. **Deep play.** Bentham’s category: stakes so high net utility fails; yet passion persists because meaning (Weber) compensates economic cost; money as moral symbol.
2. **Man and cock.** Cocks identified with owners’ masculinity and with demonic powers; animal rage expressed under cultural control.
3. **Betting dualism.** Center bets even-money, status-matched; side bets odds-based; rules of loyalty and alliance (Tihingan materials).
4. **Status drama without redistribution.** Outcomes affirm or insult status symbolically; social structure not rearranged.
5. **Interpretive function.** Not primarily functionalist reinforcement: “a story they tell themselves about themselves”; culture as ensemble of texts read over the shoulder.

## Theoretical Impact

Public face of Geertzian interpretation; anthologized teaching set-piece for culture-as-text and thick description.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

`reanalyzed-contested` — over-generalization of “the Balinese”; gender and violence; quietism (displays status without changing it); Swidler-type peak-performance bias.

## Current Standing

Still the most-cited single Geertz ethnographic performance analysis.
