# Warrant for Genocide — Study

Source: [[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-1967]]. Thinker: [[cohn-norman]].

## Question and Context

How did a ludicrous forgery and a long-running demonological fantasy become a **warrant for genocide**? Writing after the Eichmann trial and drawing on a 1945 encounter with Nazi literature and SS interrogations, Cohn asks why Jews were hunted with a fanaticism he treats as distinct even within WWII's broader civilian mass murder — and answers by reconstructing the career of the [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]] and its supreme vehicle, the *[[protocols-of-the-elders-of-zion|Protocols of the Elders of Zion]]*.

**Origin of the research problem (Cohn, attributed).** Months of reading in a Nazi library (winter 1945) plus contact with SS under investigation left him with the suspicion that extermination sprang from quasi-demonological superstition. By 1961, he judged, no thorough study yet linked the myth's post-French-Revolution formation, the Protocols, civil-war massacres, interwar global spread, and Hitler's ideology into one arc.

## Findings

*(All attributed to Cohn, 1967/Serif foreword.)*

### The myth
1. **Quasi-demonological core.** The deadliest antisemitism — massacre and attempted genocide — turns on the conviction that *all* Jews form a conspiratorial body set on ruining and dominating mankind, not merely on ordinary prejudice or interest-conflict.
2. **Subterranean producers.** "Crooks and half-educated fanatics" (Goedsche, Osman-Bey, Nilus, and kin) can change history when crisis lets their fantasies capture mass publics and states.
3. **Enabling crises.** Without WWI, the Russian Revolution and aftermath, the Protocols would have stayed obscure; without the great slump they would not have become a government creed — yet disasters alone, Cohn argues, would not have produced Auschwitz without the myth.

### The text
4. **Forgery and plagiarism.** The Protocols are forged; their main source is Joly's 1864 *Dialogue aux Enfers* (Machiavelli vs. Montesquieu as satire on Napoleon III), with ~160 parallel passages and roughly two-fifths of the Protocols text dependent on Joly, often in order. Non-Joly material includes messianic/Antichrist frames and other antisemitic fictions (Rabbi's Speech lineage).
5. **Edition politics.** Russian editions (Krushevan, Butmi, Nilus; Union of the Russian People) embed the text in pogromist and apocalyptic politics; *The Times* (Graves, 1921) is the classic public unmasking.

### Dissemination and violence
6. **Russia.** Launched among *pogromshchiki*; Kishinev 1903 (Cohn: 45 killed, ~10,000 destitute); Black Hundreds; Nicholas II's circle until Stolypin inquiry ("Drop the Protocols"); White-army editions across civil-war fronts; Cohn reports **over 100,000** Jews killed in associated civil-war massacres (e.g. Fastov detail).
7. **Germany.** 1920 German edition (zur Beek/Hausen); huge print runs (Cohn: ~120k first wave; dozens of editions by 1933); Ludendorff circle; Rathenau assassination in an atmosphere that cast him as an "Elder"; temporary mid-1920s ebb then Nazi revival.
8. **World.** Britain (*Jewish Peril*, *Morning Post*) until *Times* unmasking curtails mainstream respectability; US Henry Ford / *Dearborn Independent* → *The International Jew*; France, Poland, multi-language diaspora networks.
9. **Racism fusion.** *Völkisch* racism (Marr, Fritsch, Chamberlain, Austrian swastika cults, Thule → DAP) reinterprets Elders as racial antagonists; fusion converts conspiracy fantasy into an **extermination** warrant in Cohn's account.
10. **Hitler and modeling.** By 1923 Hitler's thinking is "permeated" by the Protocols; Wisliceny testimony and *Mein Kampf*/prophecy materials (as Cohn cites) show leaders treating world history as struggle against the conspiratorial Jew; Cohn (with Arendt/Poliakov as he reads them) stresses Nazis **modeling** themselves on the imaginary Elders' ruthlessness.
11. **Propaganda stages.** Myth used to gain power, justify terror, justify war, justify genocide, delay surrender (Ch. 9).
12. **Trials and international.** Berne trial establishes forgery in court for Swiss plaintiffs; appeal technicalities later misused by publishers; wartime collaborators (e.g. Darquier, Preziosi, Endre in Cohn's cases) show the myth traveling with occupation power.

## Theoretical Impact

Standard English-language scholarly reference on the Protocols; major source for treating conspiracy-myth antisemitism as a distinct strand in Holocaust ideology. Cross-cuts [[holocaust-and-modernity-debate]]: Cohn supplies a **myth/ideology warrant** thesis complementary to Bauman's modernity/bureaucracy and Goldhagen's eliminationist culture — not a substitute for either.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Plagiarism demonstration:** widely accepted; appendices make the Joly apparatus checkable.
- **Authorship detail:** Rachkovsky/Okhrana two-stage hypotheses remain evidential, not courtroom-closed; later archival work (beyond this ingest) has refined the Russian production story.
- **Causal weight:** How much the Protocols *moved* policy versus *rationalized* it remains debated; Cohn argues fanaticism "largely inspired" by the myth stands behind massacre "as a whole" while listing careerist, looting, and sadistic motives too.
- `replication_status: partially-supported` — core textual claim robust; broader Holocaust-causal weighting still open.

## Current Standing

Still the classic account of the Protocols' career in English; Serif reprints and multi-language translations (Cohn: nine languages by the mid-1990s) mark durable demand. **Studies Hub** not built this session (single primary source; no full reception monograph ingested) — flagged as warranted-eventually if a reception volume is ingested.
