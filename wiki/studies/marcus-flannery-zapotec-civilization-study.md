# Zapotec Civilization (study)

Source record: [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996]].

Landmark Thames & Hudson synthesis of the Oaxaca long-term project — a **reference-point research synthesis** (excavation + full-coverage settlement survey + epigraphy + ethnohistory), not a single-season field report. Does not meet Studies Hub criteria for a second full hub excavation (the method charter hub already exists for *[[flannery-early-mesoamerican-village-study|The Early Mesoamerican Village]]*; this is the civilization-scale narrative companion).

## Question and Context

How did urban society and a Zapotec state evolve in the Valley of Oaxaca from Late Ice Age foragers to Classic civilization, without reducing the sequence to unilinear evolutionary stages? Framed for Renfrew/Sabloff’s New Aspects of Antiquity series as a generalizable case of early civilization.

## Design and Methods

- **Regional survey:** Valley of Oaxaca Settlement Pattern Project ([[blanton-richard]], [[kowalewski-stephen]], Feinman et al.) supplies population ranges and hierarchy tiers.
- **Excavation:** Michigan Human Ecology Project at [[san-jose-mogote]], [[guila-naquitz]], [[tierras-largas]], and related Formative sites; colleagues’ work at [[fabrica-san-jose]] (Drennan), [[tomaltepec]] (Whalen), [[cuicatlan-canada]] (Spencer/Redmond), canal systems (O’Brien et al.).
- **Epigraphy / iconography:** Caso-era corpus re-read (Danzantes, Building J, Stelae 12–13, royal tombs/urns).
- **Ethnohistory:** 16th-c. Spanish questionnaires and noble interrogations (Cociyoeza/Cociyopii era) via direct historical approach preferred over loose ethnographic analogy for Period III.
- **Theory:** [[action-theory-archaeology]] program; Greek [[synoikism]] and Hawaiian state formation as **flagged analogies**.

## Findings

*(All attributed to Marcus & Flannery 1996.)*

1. **Egalitarian to ranked Formative:** Tierras Largas prestige without hereditary rank → San José phase Earth/Sky dualism, sumptuary gradients, cranial deformation → Guadalupe hypogamy/feasting alliances → Rosario maximal [[chiefdom]] (three-tier hierarchy, buffer zone, temple burning, Monument 3 writing).
2. **[[synoikism]] of Monte Albán (c. 500 BCE):** Unoccupied mountain → Early MA I ~5,280 → Late MA I ~17,242 with walls; three founding clusters (Blanton); Etla core abandonments; dual defense/power motives; authors prefer SJM-led confederacy.
3. **State institutions by MA II:** Four-tier hierarchy emerging; palace/two-room temple/ballcourt package; Building J conquest list; piedmont canal strategy as system-posed provisioning fix.
4. **Expansion triad:** Colonize underpopulated zones; conquer resistors (Cuicatlán skull rack/fortress); diplomacy with Teotihuacan (barrio, Lapida de Bazán, style continuity = anti-takeover).
5. **Classic multipolar defense:** IIIa valley ~115,000; peer [[jalieza]]; 38 fortifiable sites hold ~⅔ of population after frontiers roll back; urns as royal ancestors; 12 Jaguar inauguration package.
6. **Evolution without stages:** Institutions and actor–system transitions are milestones; stages retained only as comparative shorthand ([[action-theory-archaeology]]).

## Theoretical Impact

Anchors the Flannery–Marcus Oaxaca model in world archaeology teaching literature; feeds later *[[flannery-marcus-creation-inequality-2012|Creation of Inequality]]* comparative arguments; supplies the standard English-language narrative against which disembedded-capital (Blanton), external-threat, and ecological-functionalist alternatives are weighed.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Founding models:** Blanton disembedded-capital confederacy vs Flannery–Marcus SJM-led synoikism — **open positions** (also in *Cloud People*).
- **Absolute chronology:** 1996 radiocarbon/ceramic dates need later calibration updates; phase labels more stable than year numbers.
- **“Empire” scale:** Authors themselves call peak ~20,000 km² “a kind of empire” but modest vs Aztec — interpretive, not consensus label.
- **Ethnic equation:** “Identifiably Zapotec” from script/religion is their claim; four-way non-identity still required.
- Epigraphic revisions (Urcid and others) post-date or parallel the synthesis — monument reuse and name-vs-date readings continue to revise Building J/Danzantes contexts.

## Current Standing

Still a primary English synthesis for Oaxaca urban origins; must be read with *[[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]]* (primary seminar detail), later survey monographs, and ongoing epigraphy. Methodologically continuous with *[[flannery-early-mesoamerican-village-1976|The Early Mesoamerican Village]]* multi-scalar program.

## Historiography

Builds on Caso–Bernal–Acosta Monte Albán excavations; Michigan Formative program; Settlement Pattern Project. Series editors Renfrew & Sabloff frame it as model regional survey+excavation synthesis. Related: *Cloud People* (1983); *Creation of Inequality* (2012); Coe & Koontz survey chaptering in [[coe-koontz-mexico-2013]].
