# Primitive Society (Lowie study)

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/studies/lowie-primitive-society-study|Studies Hub]]

## Question and Context

What is the actual form of "primitive" social organization once Victorian unilinear schemes are set aside? Writing after Boas's methodological critique and amid a flood of new monographs, [[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]] set out to replace [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]'s *Ancient Society* (1877) ladder with a historically minded comparative survey of marriage, family, kinship usages, the [[sib]], women's status, property, associations, rank, government, and justice. Theoretical frame: [[historical-particularism]]. Polemical targets: unilinear evolutionism and the atomistic "all units = sibs" picture of lower culture. Secondary recovery program: Heinrich Schurtz on age- and sex-based associations (*Altersklassen und Männerbünde*, 1902).

## Design and Methods

**Type:** comparative synthesis, not single-site ethnography. **Unit:** social-organizational institutions and groups across societies. **Evidence class:** ethnographic (secondary literature plus Lowie's Crow and related fieldwork). **Method:** historical-comparative reasoning — distribution of traits; diffusion as *vera causa* without sole cause; independent development allowed; stage-laws formally abjured (Conclusion). **Scope:** social organization, not full culture. Chapter sequence: Marriage → Polygamy → Family → Kinship Usages → Sib → History of the Sib → Position of Woman → Property → Associations → Theory of Associations → Rank → Government → Justice → Conclusion.

## Findings

All attributed to Lowie (1920; 1947 retrospective where noted):

- **Mottled diversity.** Primitive society is not dull uniformity of a single sib pattern but a variety of units — family, sib, association, rank, territory — combined "in a purely capricious fashion."
- **Family universal and prior.** The bilateral individual family (spouses + immature children) is an omnipresent unit; kinship terms and customary law recognize both sides; only escape for deniers is flight to a remote past. The unilateral [[sib]] is historically secondary and geographically restricted; simpler cultures are often sibless (Swanton on North America among other evidence).
- **Marriage without stage ladder.** Polygyny often permitted but minority practice; polyandry restricted; sexual license and hospitality are not equal [[group-marriage]]; Morgan's promiscuity/consanguine packages fail chronological and ethnographic tests. [[levirate]], [[sororate]], [[cross-cousin-marriage]], bridewealth, capture (demoted), and residence rules ([[matrilocal-residence]], [[patrilocal-residence]]) are variable preferential complexes.
- **Kinship usages.** Prescribed conduct for kin types; [[avunculate]]; parent-in-law taboos; privileged familiarity/joking; [[teknonymy]] as convergent, not single-stage (vs Tylor).
- **Sib.** Unilateral unit (Lowie's term after Philbrick) vs bilateral family; types and higher-order groupings; [[totemism]] not definitional essence; mother-sib not necessary world prior to father-sib; Dakota terminology does not prove former universal sib.
- **Women's status.** Theory vs practice diverge; no true matriarchate; matriliny ≠ female rule; economic interpretations and stage correlations fail as laws.
- **Property.** Crude primitive communism rejected; land, chattels, and [[incorporeal-property]] (songs, names, privileges) are real; inheritance rules diverse.
- **Associations.** Case chapters (Andaman, Australia, Masai, Banks Islands, Pueblo, Crow, Hidatsa) + theory chapter: Schurtz scheme useful for facts, wrong as unilinear law; sex dichotomy not universal; Plains [[age-societies]] often purchase/property systems.
- **Rank, government, justice.** Bravery, shamanism, wealth, caste produce stratification; political organization exists in "rude" cultures (gerontocracy, chiefs, African monarchy, Iroquois-type councils, police societies); justice mixes torts and crimes; territorial bond coexists with kinship (Ifugao extreme of kin-as-judicial).
- **Anti-progress-law.** Processes (prestige, conformity, cooperation) recur; social forms are incommensurable for objective grading; Maine's [[status-to-contract]] is formal rather than substantial for freedom; cultures grow mainly by chance-contact borrowing; no people need reenact Western stages ("mock-law of social evolution"); civilization as "shreds and patches" (1947: not pure atomism).

## Theoretical Impact

Became the standard American statement of anti-stage social organization for a generation (Sapir 1920 reception). Fed [[historical-particularism]] applied to kinship and polity. Anchors particularist Position on [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]] and [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]. Engaged Rivers (wanting more diffusion), Kroeber (sterility charge), Malinowski (praise), Morgan admirers (denunciation) — see 1947 Preface.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

Rivers: valuable, under-diffusionist. Kroeber: honest, comparatively sterile. Later neo-evolutionists: particularism under-produces recurrent pathways. Functionalists: different anti-Morgan path via present function. "Shreds and patches" misread as pure cultural atomism — Lowie 1947 clarification. `replication_status: not-applicable`.

## Current Standing

Absorbed anti-unilinear and multi-unit picture; ethnographic details and some origin reconstructions dated; still the classic American charter against Morgan's social-organization ladder. Read with later kinship theory as subsequent layers.
