# Great American City — Study Summary

Landmark multi-method synthesis of the **Project on Human Development in Chicago Neighborhoods (PHDCN)** and cognate Chicago Project components. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/sampson-great-american-city-study]]. Source: [[sampson-great-american-city-2012]]. Thinker: [[sampson-robert-j]].

## Question and Context

Do neighborhoods still structure inequality, crime, civic life, altruism, mobility, and power in a supposedly “flat,” globalized, community-lost world? Sampson writes against placelessness narratives and against purely individualistic selection accounts, while reconstructing the intellectual history of [[neighborhood-effects]] research and the making of a large multi-method project in the [[chicago-school|Chicago School]]’s city. Foreword by [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]].

## Design and Methods

- **343 neighborhood clusters (NCs)** (~8,000 people each); **80 NCs** stratified on SES×race/ethnicity for intensive work.
- **Community Survey** 1995: n=8,782 adults across all 343 NCs (residents as informants).
- **Longitudinal Cohort Study**: seven age cohorts in the 80 NCs; wave-1 participation ~75%; retention 87%/76% waves 2–3 (1994–2002).
- **[[systematic-social-observation]]**: ~23,816 face-blocks; video coding of physical/social disorder.
- **KeyNet** elite/key-informant networks (1995; 2002 panel).
- **[[lost-letter-technique]]** behavioral altruism (2002; 2010 partial replication).
- Administrative series (crime, births, incarceration, later foreclosures/CHA).
- Measurement philosophy: **[[ecometrics]]** (with [[raudenbush-stephen|Raudenbush]]).
- Critical engagement with **MTO** experiments (not original fielding).

Unit of analysis spans individuals, neighborhoods, interneighborhood dyads, and citywide structure. Detail: [[hubs/studies/sampson-great-american-city-study]].

## Findings

*(Attributed — Sampson 2012; program includes earlier PHDCN papers.)*

- **Durable place:** relative neighborhood inequality highly persistent; crisis and CHA transformation reorganized landscapes without erasing deep structure (2010 revisit).
- **[[concentrated-disadvantage]]:** ecological concentration of poverty and correlated deficits predicts violence, incarceration ecology, and compromised wellbeing; magnified by segregation.
- **[[collective-efficacy]]:** cohesion/trust + shared expectations for informal control; varies widely; associated with lower violence and disorder; partially mediates structure→violence; reciprocal dynamics with prior crime.
- **Disorder / broken windows:** observed disorder more a product of structure/land use and low CE than an independent cause of predatory crime; **perceived** disorder shaped by racial/immigrant composition beyond SSO and can reinforce inequality (looking-glass neighborhood).
- **Organizations:** nonprofit/service density predicts collective civic events; civic action transformed/spatially concentrated rather than simply “lost.”
- **Altruism & cynicism:** letter-drop and related behavioral measures vary ecologically; with CE and [[legal-cynicism]], relate to homicide and teen births.
- **Spatial logic:** neighbors of neighborhoods matter (spatial lags/multipliers); ecological dissimilarity of black vs white neighborhood environments.
- **Selection as process:** mobility structured by distance, income/race homophily, and social climate (e.g. perceived disorder); **structural sorting** — neighborhoods choose people.
- **Higher-order structure:** migration flows and elite leadership ties interlock; CE and org density shape leadership networks.

## Theoretical Impact

Re-centers [[urban-sociology]] on multi-level context; standard reference for collective efficacy and neighborhood-effects debates; dialogue with Wilson, broken-windows criminology, MTO experimental literature, and Chicago School legacy. Ten analytic principles (ch. 3) codify the program’s method-theory stance.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

See [[neighborhood-effects-debate]]; [[broken-windows-debate]]. Experimental (MTO) and cross-city literatures continue to condition claims about magnitudes and mechanisms. Sampson cites Stockholm comparison, UK item replications, and meta-analytic support; Latin American and other caveats appear in his comparative discussion. `replication_status: partially-supported` reflects a large program with extensive follow-on research rather than exact replication of every coefficient.

## Current Standing

Core text of contemporary neighborhood-effects research; still widely cited for theory of context, ecometrics, and collective efficacy. PHDCN data remain a public research infrastructure beyond the book.
