# 1177 B.C. (Cline) — Synthesis Study

**Source page:** [[cline-1177-bc-2014]] · **Author:** [[cline-eric-h]]

## Question and Context

What ended the interconnected Late Bronze Age civilizations of the Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean after centuries of peak international contact, and why is **1177 BCE** a useful date-marker? Cline writes for the Turning Points series as a narrative synthesis against monocausal invasion models and against treating collapse as a single-year catastrophe.

## Design and Methods

Not a primary excavation. Cline integrates:

1. **Egyptian royal inscriptions** (esp. Merneptah ~1207 BCE and Ramses III year 8 ~1177 BCE on Sea Peoples).
2. **Diplomatic/economic archives** (Amarna; Hattusa; Ugarit merchant and royal tablets).
3. **Archaeological destruction horizons** across Greece, Anatolia, Cyprus, Levant, and related regions, with attention to warfare vs earthquake markers.
4. **Secondary scientific literatures** on drought proxies and archaeoseismology ("earthquake storms").

Unit of analysis: the **international system** (multiple polities), not a single state. Scale: macro/regional-transregional.

## Findings

All attributed to **Cline 2014** (or to specialists he cites, so labeled).

### Chronological shorthand
- **1177 BCE** = eighth year of Ramses III; major Sea Peoples land-and-sea attack on Egypt. Cline treats it as scholarly shorthand for a multi-decade process (~1225–1175 or as late as ~1130 BCE), analogous to AD 476 for Rome — convenient, not a single-day end.
- Earlier Sea Peoples-related activity under Merneptah ~**1207 BCE**.

### Sea Peoples
- Modern collective label; Egyptians named separate groups (Peleset, Tjekker, Shekelesh, Shardana, Danuna, Weshesh, etc.) with varied dress/gear — multi-origin coalition, not one ethnos.
- Homeland unknown (hypotheses: Sicily/Sardinia/Italy, Aegean, western Anatolia, Cyprus, Eastern Mediterranean); no departure site identified.
- Not accepted as monocausal destroyer of the LBA world.

### Prior international system (~1500–1200 BCE)
- Dense diplomatic, commercial, transport, and communication networks among Egypt, Hittites, Mitanni/Assyria, Babylon, Canaanite polities (incl. Ugarit), Cyprus (Alashiya), and Aegean Minoan/Mycenaean centers — documented especially via Amarna, Hattusa, and Ugarit archives.
- Strategic tin (via Afghanistan routes in Cline’s presentation) compared rhetorically to modern oil (Carol Bell attributed).

### Destructions / transformations (selected)
- **Ugarit:** destroyed/abandoned early 12th c.; rich late archives (enemy ships letters; international correspondence). Schaeffer’s earthquake damage now dated ~1250+ with rebuild before final end.
- **Hattusa:** public buildings burned after emptying; Seeher (via Cline): abandonment before torch; Kashka more likely than Sea Peoples for final burn; empire weakened by drought, famine, trade interruption.
- **Mycenaean palaces** (e.g. Pylos ~1180): loss of palaces, Linear B, *wanax* (Maran via Cline); Pylos region long depopulated (Davis via Cline).
- **Mesopotamia:** some destructions by Elamites (e.g. Shutruk-Nahhunte) — not Sea Peoples.
- Regional unevenness and ambiguous agency at many sites (internal revolt vs external attack often undetermined).

### Causal model (“perfect storm”)
- Factors reviewed: foreign attack, social uprising, natural catastrophe (earthquake storm ~1225–1175 BCE per archaeoseismology Cline cites), drought/famine (textual grain pleas; newer proxies Drake, Kaniewski et al.), systems collapse, changes in warfare (Drews — reported, not adopted as sufficient).
- **Thesis:** no single factor sufficient; multiplier and domino effects in an interdependent system; cutting networks cascades failure. Complexity theory flagged as promising (Bell 2006; Johnson).
- Sandars (1985) quoted: earthquakes/famines/droughts/invasions may play parts but “are not enough.”

### Aftermath
- Smaller Early Iron Age polities; loss of LBA international elite framework (Van De Mieroop via Cline); continuity uneven (e.g. Neo-Assyrian trajectories); Phoenicians, Philistines, Israelites, later Greek polities among regenerations; alphabet and iron-use expansions as post-collapse developments in Cline’s narrative.

## Theoretical Impact

Became a standard public and pedagogical reference for multi-causal LBA collapse and for systems-interdependence analogies. Feeds [[late-bronze-age-collapse]], [[societal-collapse]], and [[late-bronze-age-collapse-causes-debate]].

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- Chronological precision of 1177 BCE is conventional (Egyptological chronology); absolute dates remain debated in specialist literature.
- Relative weight of climate vs invasion vs systems failure remains open (debate page).
- Synthesis horizon ~2013–14 (as of Cline 2014); later aDNA, refined radiocarbon, and paleoclimate work may refine or challenge parts (update when ingested).

## Current Standing

Widely cited popular-scholarly synthesis; core multi-causal stance aligns with much specialist caution against Sea Peoples monocausality. Treat detailed site-by-site claims as secondary until cross-checked against excavation reports.
