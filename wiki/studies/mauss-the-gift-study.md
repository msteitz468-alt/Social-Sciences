# The Gift — Study

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/mauss-the-gift-study|Studies Hub — The Gift]]. Source: [[mauss-the-gift-1925]]. Author: [[mauss-marcel]].

## Question and Context

*Essai sur le don* (1925) asks: in archaic societies, **what force in the thing given compels the recipient to make a return?** Mauss isolates prestations that appear voluntary, disinterested, and spontaneous but are "in fact obligatory and interested" — "formal pretence and social deception" cloaking obligation and economic self-interest. Against both utilitarian "economic man" and the claim that archaic societies lack markets, he offers a comparative morphology of gift systems as [[total-social-phenomenon|total social phenomena]].

Intellectual setting: *Année Sociologique* school after WWI; collaboration and rivalry with Davy on contract; Malinowski's *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]* (1922) as fresh Melanesian feedstock; Boas school Northwest Coast corpus; Indo-European legal history (Huvelin et al.). Evans-Pritchard (Cunnison intro) calls it the first systematic comparative study of gift exchange and of its function in articulating social order.

## Design and Methods

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Unit of analysis | Systems of gift / total prestation, not a single society |
| Evidence classes | Secondary ethnography; legal and epic texts; philology |
| Core areas | Polynesia, Melanesia, Northwest America; Indo-European codes as "survivals" |
| Method | "Careful comparison" confined to well-documented areas; abstains from diffusion history in Ch. II |
| Fieldwork | **None by Mauss** for this essay — armchair synthesis (EP: "without leaving his flat in Paris") |

Scale: **macro** comparative; ethnographic depth is borrowed. Mauss corrects and re-synthesizes fieldworkers (notably Malinowski on Trobriand exchange) from comparative leverage — EP treats this as a methodological feat; later specialists sometimes treat it as overreach.

## Findings

All **attributed** (Mauss 1925 / Cunnison):

1. **Voluntary appearance, obligatory structure** — gifts "in theory voluntary but in fact given and repaid under obligation."
2. **[[total-social-phenomenon|Total social phenomena]]** — gift systems simultaneously religious, legal, moral, economic, aesthetic, morphological.
3. **[[total-prestation|Total prestation]]** — permanent group contracts involving persons, ritual, and goods; **[[potlatch]]** = agonistic subtype.
4. **[[hau]]** (Maori via Ranaipiri/Best) — spirit of the *taonga* remains part of the giver and compels return; "to give something is to give a part of oneself."
5. **Three obligations** — to give, to receive, to repay (refusal ≈ declaration of war).
6. **[[kula]] and potlatch as extremes** — Kula as extreme gift exchange; potlatch as "monster child" of the gift system; honour, credit, renown-valuables (*Renommiergeld*).
7. **Indo-European survivals** — Rome (*nexum*, person/thing law), Hindu *danadharma*, Germanic *Gaben*/*wadium* as traces of gift regimes before pure market contract.
8. **Normative program (Conclusions — POSITION)** — revive mutuality, professional groups, social insurance; "in order to trade, man must first lay down his spear"; Round Table allegory of redistributed common riches.

## Theoretical Impact

- Charter text of the **anthropology of the gift** and a foundation for economic anthropology's non-formalist wing ([[substantivism]] draws on Mauss/Malinowski/Thurnwald lineage).
- Supplies the vocabulary ([[total-prestation]], three obligations, potlatch-as-type) for Lévi-Strauss on reciprocity, Sahlins on the spirit of the gift, Bourdieu on gift/time/[[misrecognition]], Blau on gift and power (secondhand layers already on vault).
- Method icon of **comparative holism** — and of its risks (armchair totalization).

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

| Line of critique | Position (strongest form) |
|---|---|
| Hau over-generalized | Ranaipiri's text is forest/game cosmology, not a universal mechanism of the gift (later Sahlins et al. — flag for primary ingest) |
| Evolutionary residue | Phase model (total prestation → gift exchange → individual contract/market) smuggles unilinear history |
| Secondary ethnography | Corrections of Malinowski and Boas-based potlatch types need field restudy |
| Normative Conclusions | Civic/socialist program is position, not demonstrated finding |
| Structuralist rewrite | Lévi-Strauss treats exchange as constructed object/cycle, bracketing Mauss's phenomenological force-in-the-thing (Bourdieu later defends temporal strategy against that rewrite — see [[gift-exchange]]) |

`replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — not a lab replication target; continuously re-read and partially overturned in mechanism claims while remaining a reference point for the field.

## Current Standing

Still **required reading** across anthropology and sociology; concept apparatus is live; many mechanism claims (universal hau, Indo-European potlatch traces) are specialist-contested. Vault now holds **primary Cunnison text** rather than secondhand Blau/Bourdieu citations alone.

## Historiography

Published in *L'Année Sociologique* n.s. 1 (1923–24 [1925]); French book form in *Sociologie et Anthropologie* (1950) with Lévi-Strauss introduction; English Cunnison 1954/1966 (this ingest); later Halls translation with Mary Douglas intro. EP introduction is itself a major reception document (totality, moral vs mechanical exchange).
