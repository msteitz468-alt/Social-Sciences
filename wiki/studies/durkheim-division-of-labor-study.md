# The Division of Labor in Society (as a study)

Analysis of *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|De la division du travail social]]* (1893) as a research act — its question, design, evidence, and afterlife — distinct from the bibliographic [[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|source page]] and the [[durkheim-emile|thinker page]]. Durkheim's doctoral thesis and first book. All substantive findings in **attributed voice**.

## Question and Context

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] asked what binds members of modern, highly differentiated societies together — "what are the bonds that unite men?" Against the classical economists (Adam Smith onward), who treated the division of labor as an engine of productivity, and against moralists who feared specialization as a source of social dissolution, Durkheim proposed that the division of labor's true function is **moral**: it is the principal source of social solidarity once societies grow too large and differentiated for solidarity by resemblance. He denied that civilization as such is morally progressive (citing rising suicide and crime alongside industrial growth), reframing the division of labor as an object for a positive **science of moral facts** rather than economics or ethics-as-doctrine (see [[social-realism]]).

## Design and Methods

| Element | As conducted |
|---|---|
| **Unit of analysis** | Whole societies / social types (macro), compared across historical and evolutionary stages |
| **Core method move** | Solidarity is "a completely moral phenomenon which... does not lend itself to exact observation," so Durkheim substitutes an **external index — law** — and studies solidarity through it |
| **Classification** | Legal rules sorted by **sanction**: [[repressive-law|repressive]] (penal, expiatory) vs. [[restitutive-law|restitutive]] (restoring relations) — rejecting jurists' public/private division as unscientific |
| **Evidence** | Comparative coding of ancient codes (Pentateuch, Laws of Manu, Twelve Tables, Salic/Burgundian/Visigothic law); 19th-c. ethnology and craniometry (Le Bon, Waitz); population/urban statistics; Civil Code provisions |
| **Causal reasoning** | Covariation inference (a constant cause cannot explain a variable effect); the Darwinian struggle-for-existence mechanism; social-morphological cause ([[dynamic-density]]) |
| **Rhetoric** | Argument by biological/organic analogy; systematic polemic against named predecessors ([[spencer-herbert|Spencer]], [[comte-auguste|Comte]], [[unknown|Maine]], [[unknown|Tarde]]) |

No fieldwork or original survey data; the empirical base is comparative-historical and secondary.

## Findings (attributed)

### The function of the division of labor (Book I)

Solidarity is indexed by law. **[[mechanical-solidarity]]** (solidarity by resemblance) is indexed by **[[repressive-law]]**: crime "offends strong and defined states of the [[collective-conscience]]," and punishment expiates the wounded sentiment. **[[organic-solidarity]]** (solidarity by differentiated interdependence) is indexed by **[[restitutive-law]]**. Durkheim's signature claim: the division of labor *simultaneously increases solidarity and individuates persons*. The [[collective-conscience]] weakens in **volume, intensity, and determinateness** as organic solidarity grows — evidenced by the historical retreat of penal law, religion, and proverbs — while one region intensifies: the [[moral-individualism|cult of the individual]]. Against [[spencer-herbert|Spencer]], contract rests on [[non-contractual-elements-of-contract|non-contractual social conditions]] (see [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]).

### The causes (Book II)

The division of labor grows with the **volume and [[dynamic-density|moral/dynamic density]]** of society — more people in more intense interaction. Denser society intensifies the **struggle for existence** (Darwin), which specialization resolves *pacifically*: differentiated rivals occupy different niches and survive side by side. Durkheim rejects the classical-economic explanation (specialization sought for happiness/utility) via covariation and the Weber–Fechner bound on pleasure, and rejects Spencer's environmental-diversity account — insisting the cause is social-morphological, not psychological. Density is the *determining cause*, not a permissive means.

### The abnormal forms (Book III)

| Form | Failure |
|---|---|
| [[anomic-division-of-labor|Anomic]] | Inter-functional relations unregulated — industrial crises, capital–labor conflict |
| [[forced-division-of-labor|Forced]] | Function assigned by constraint/inheritance, not aptitude — "there cannot be rich and poor at birth without... unjust contracts" |
| Third (under-coordinated) | Functional activity insufficiently continuous — specialization without adequate work/coordination |

All three are **pathologies** of a normally solidarity-producing division of labor; the remedy is better regulation and just conditions, not less specialization.

### Conclusion

Morality "enunciate[s] the fundamental conditions of social solidarity"; "man is a moral being only because he lives in society." The emergent common morality of modern societies is [[moral-individualism]] — respect for the human person — and the task of advanced societies is a continuing "work of justice."

## Theoretical Impact

- Founding statement of Durkheimian **[[social-realism|social realism]]** and of the **function-vs-cause** and **normal-vs-pathological** distinctions later central to structural-functionalism.
- Introduced durable vocabulary: [[mechanical-solidarity|mechanical]]/[[organic-solidarity|organic]] solidarity, [[dynamic-density|dynamic density]], [[anomie]] (here in its earliest, inter-functional sense), the [[non-contractual-elements-of-contract|non-contractual element in contract]].
- The occupational-group remedy (developed in the 1902 [[occupational-group|second-edition preface]]) feeds Durkheimian corporatism and reappears in *[[durkheim-suicide-study|Suicide]]*.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

`replication_status: partially-supported`. The **core thesis** (that modern integration rests on interdependence, and that markets rest on a moral/legal order) endures; several **specific empirical claims** have been challenged or overturned:

- **The legal-evolution claim overturned** — later legal anthropology (e.g. work following Barnes, and Sheleff/Malinowski) found restitutive/reciprocal sanctions widespread in simple societies and penal law not simply dominant there, undermining Durkheim's repressive→restitutive sequence and his code tallies. Demote this specific claim from any wiki-voice reading.
- **Mechanical/organic dichotomy** — criticized as too clean; real societies mix both, and the evolutionary ranking of "social types" is dated.
- **Race/craniometric sources** (Le Bon, Waitz) and era-bound anthropological generalizations ("paternal love is unknown in a great many societies") are discredited period apparatus — attributed, never wiki voice; four-way non-identity applies.
- **Simpson's Marxist critique (1933)** — Durkheim conflated the division of labor "in society" with the division of labor "in the workshop" (Marx's category of capitalist production), underestimating monopoly and making his regulatory remedy inadequate short of abolishing the profit system (a position; see [[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|source page]] reliability note).
- **Reification** — the "society *sui generis*" ontology draws the standard hypostasis objection.

## Current Standing

Still a graduate and undergraduate landmark — for the solidarity typology, the sociology of law, the concept of anomie, and the non-contractualist critique of utilitarian social theory. Read today more for its framework, method, and the abnormal-forms/anomie material than for its evolutionary legal history, which the field has largely discarded. A **Studies Hub deep analysis** reconstructs the design, evidence, rhetoric, and full afterlife at graduate resolution: [[hubs/studies/durkheim-division-of-labor-study|The Division of Labor in Society — Deep Analysis]] (the hub's exemplar).

## Historiography

Primary: *De la division du travail social* 1893 (this wiki: George Simpson English, Free Press 1933). Editorial framing: Simpson's "Preface to the Translation" and partisan "An Estimate of Durkheim's Work" (1933) — a Marxist-inflected secondary reading, flagged as such. Relations to *Rules* (1895) and *[[durkheim-suicide-study|Suicide]]* (1897) run forward from here — this book already states, four years early, the "explain the social by the social" rule and treats the suicide rate as a social indicator.
