# Capital, Volume I (as a study)

## Question and Context

[[marx-karl|Karl Marx]]’s *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867; English 1887 ed. Engels) asks: what is the **economic law of motion** of modern society under the capitalist [[mode-of-production|mode of production]]? It continues the 1859 *Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy* and supplies the scientific core that the [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] had stated programmatically.

Object (1867 preface): the capitalist mode of production and corresponding relations of production and exchange; **England** as classic ground of illustration.

## Design and Methods

- **Method of abstraction:** economic forms analyzed without microscope; commodity as cell-form.
- **Critique of political economy:** classical PE (esp. Ricardo) as last scientific bourgeois form while class struggle latent; post-1830 “vulgar” economy as apology (1873 afterword — attributed periodization).
- **Individuals as personifications** of economic categories and class relations.
- **Empirical base:** English factory inspectors, Blue Books, public health reports, tax and trade series, parliamentary inquiries; Ireland census and emigration figures — **as Marx cites them**, mid-19th century, not re-verified modern datasets.
- **Genre:** mixed theoretical treatise + historical monography (working day; accumulation illustrations; primitive accumulation).

`replication_status: not-applicable` — landmark theoretical work with documentary illustration, not a survey experiment.

## Findings

**Attributed to Marx (*Capital* I):**

1. **Value and commodity** — dual character of the commodity and of labour; value as abstract labour / SNLT; money as finished value-form; [[commodity-fetishism]].
2. **Capital and surplus-value** — M–C–M′; [[labor-power]] as commodity; [[surplus-value]] from unpaid labour; [[constant-and-variable-capital]]; absolute vs relative surplus-value.
3. **Working day** — class struggle over limits; Factory Acts as compulsory limitation; branches without legal limits documented via inspectors.
4. **Relative surplus-value** — co-operation, manufacture, machinery; machinery creates industrial reserve population.
5. **Wages** — wage-form mystifies value of labour-power as price of labour.
6. **Accumulation** — simple reproduction of the capital-relation; conversion of surplus-value into capital; rising [[organic-composition-of-capital]]; [[industrial-reserve-army]]; [[general-law-of-capitalist-accumulation]].
7. **Illustrations** — England 1846–66 wealth vs worker conditions; agricultural proletariat and gang-system; Ireland post-1846 depopulation, farm centralisation, rents up / relative surplus population remains.
8. **Primitive accumulation** — expropriation of agricultural population; bloody legislation; colonies, public debt, protection; Wakefield on free colonies; historical tendency that expropriators are expropriated (**position**).

## Theoretical Impact

- Foundation text of Marxist political economy and a pillar of sociology’s **conflict tradition**.
- Thickens [[historical-materialism]] beyond the *Manifesto* with value theory and accumulation dynamics.
- Enduring concepts: commodity fetishism, labour-power, surplus-value, reserve army, primitive accumulation.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- Transformation problem and value–price relation (later volumes / secondary literature).
- Immiseration and reserve-army theses vs long-run real wage growth and welfare states.
- Historiography of enclosures, Ireland, and colonial accumulation.
- Method: abstraction vs historical specificity debates (German Historical School contemporaries; later).
