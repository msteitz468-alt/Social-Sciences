# Tsing, *The Mushroom at the End of the World* — Study

Landmark multi-locale, multi-species ethnography of matsutake commerce and ecology under capitalist precarity. Summary resolution; depth in [[hubs/studies/tsing-mushroom-study]].

Source: [[tsing-mushroom-2015]]. Author: [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt]]. Society: [[open-ticket-oregon]]. Methods: [[multi-species-ethnography]], [[ethnography-of-global-connection]]. Companion earlier study: [[tsing-friction-study]].

## Question and Context

How is livelihood and collaborative survival possible in landscapes and economies already ruined by industrial progress narratives? Tsing tracks matsutake to open imagination beyond both progress and pure decline, asking how capitalism looks without assuming progress and how ecology looks without romantic anti-modern nature *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Design and Methods

- **Field window:** matsutake seasons 2004–2011 in the United States, Japan, Canada, China, and Finland; interviews also Denmark, Sweden, Turkey *(Tsing 2015)*.
- **Collaboration:** Matsutake Worlds Research Group (Timothy Choy, Lieba Faier, Elaine Gan, Michael Hathaway, Miyako Inoue, Shiho Satsuka, Tsing) — experiment in always-in-process collaborative ethnography; categories develop with research *(Tsing 2015)*.
- **Ethics:** most individual names pseudonymous; Open Ticket is a composite place-name; cities named, local countryside place names generally avoided *(Tsing 2015)*.
- **Form:** riot of short chapters as open-ended assemblage mimicking [[patchiness]]; photographs as parallel argument *(Tsing 2015)*.
- **Object:** follow mushrooms, pickers, buyers, traders, foresters, scientists, and nonhumans (pines, fungi, disturbance regimes) across gift–commodity chains and forest histories.

## Findings

*(all attributed to Tsing 2015)*

1. **Precarity as earthwide condition** — life without the promise of stability; vulnerability to others; dual ecological and employment precarity after Hiroshima/postwar development contradictions.
2. **Third nature / patchiness** — what lives despite capitalism; mosaics of open-ended assemblages; capitalism without progress looks patchy because wealth concentrates by appropriating unplanned patch value.
3. **Arts of noticing** — method for contaminated diversity and indeterminate encounter against scalable science and progress blinders.
4. **Contamination as collaboration** — purity is not an option; survival requires work across difference already polluted by history.
5. **Scalability vs nonscalability** — plantation model of smooth expansion; matsutake forests as anti-plantations; commerce and ecology depend on interactions between scalability and its undoing in ruins.
6. **Salvage accumulation** — lead firms amass capital without controlling production conditions; pericapitalist patches; supply chains as translation machines (Oregon freedom trophies → Japanese inventory/gifts).
7. **Open Ticket freedom assemblage** — ethnicized camps; war-haunted performances of freedom; no local capital accumulation; buyers' theater.
8. **Gift–commodity toggle** — matsutake begins and ends as gift; few hours as fully alienated inventory enable exporter/importer accumulation.
9. **Unintentional design / resurgence** — multi-species forest histories (Japan red pine, Oregon lodgepole after fire exclusion, Yunnan, Finland); science as translation across knowledge patches; satoyama "matsutake crusaders"; latent commons and ordinary assets without redemptive ending.

## Theoretical Impact

Reference point for multi-species ethnography, anthropology of capitalism without progress assumptions, salvage and peri-capitalist value, disturbance ecology in social theory, and precarity as more than labor-market category. Extends *[[tsing-friction-study|Friction]]*'s global-connection program into multi-species and ruins registers while keeping scale critique and translation across difference.
