# Çatal Hüyük (Mellaart 1961–65) — study

> Summary of the research act. Deep analysis: **[[hubs/studies/mellaart-catal-huyuk-study|Studies Hub]]**. Bibliographic record: **[[mellaart-catal-huyuk-1967]]**.

The excavation campaign and 1967 monograph through which [[mellaart-james|James Mellaart]] established [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] as the emblematic Anatolian Neolithic “town.” Findings attributed to Mellaart 1967 (and named specialists) unless noted.

## Question and Context

Postwar Near Eastern prehistory, armed with [[radiocarbon-dating|C-14]], was relocating early farming from riverine “cradles” to upland and multi-foci zones. Mellaart’s Konya Plain survey and Çatalhöyük dig asked what a large seventh-millennium settlement looked like in plan, economy, art, and cult — and whether Anatolia belonged at the centre of the [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] story rather than as a periphery of Mesopotamia.

## Design and Methods

**Fieldwork:** Four seasons **1961–1965** under the British Institute of Archaeology at Ankara; site formally reached 1958 (Mellaart, Alan Hall, David French). Horizontal area clearance preferred over deep trenches because of wall-paintings and the goal of building complexes; virgin soil not reached. Building-levels numbered from the top (O–X); Level VI split into VIA and VIB.

**Dating:** Series of radiocarbon dates plus annual plaster-layer counts as duration check; Mellaart places Levels X–II roughly **c. 6500–5700 BC** over ~800 years (mid-1960s laboratory framework — later calibration revises absolute dates).

**Specialists:** Hans Helbaek (plants); Dexter Perkins Jr / Pierre Ducos (animals); Mlle Ferembach (human remains typology). Unit of analysis: buildings and building-levels within the excavated western/edge areas of the East Mound (full mound not cleared).

## Findings (attributed, Mellaart 1967)

| Finding | Evidence base | Later status |
|---|---|---|
| Large Neolithic “town” (~32 acres East Mound; 15 m deposit) | survey + area excavation | Scale refined; still major site |
| Roof-access cellular plan; no streets; outer wall/defence | Levels II–VI plans | Largely supported; access model standard |
| ≥40/139 rooms as cult “shrines” | decoration/find criteria | **Contested** — house/shrine continuum (Hodder project) |
| Goddess + bull religious system | reliefs, paintings, statues | **Contested** — goddess universalism challenged; imagery recontextualized |
| Secondary burial + vulture excarnation | skeletal condition; vulture paintings | Partial reanalysis; mechanisms debated |
| Early farming on plain with crops from elsewhere (Helbaek) | carbonized plants | Direction refined; plain agriculture confirmed in outline |
| Domestic sheep/goat early; aurochs hunting important | fauna (Perkins/Ducos) | Revised by later zooarchaeology |
| Long-distance materials (obsidian, pigments, copper/lead) | finds + regional sources | Trade/exchange models updated |

## Theoretical Impact

Fixed Çatalhöyük as the visual and narrative type-site for Neolithic complexity, art, and “early urbanism” in textbooks and public culture. Supplied the iconographic archive for [[mother-goddess]] and goddess-spirituality receptions and for [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]]’s later ideology-before-economy synthesis. Became the **baseline the [[hodder-ian|Hodder]] project restudied** — methodologically ([[reflexive-fieldwork]], [[interpretation-at-the-trowels-edge]]) and interpretively (shrine/house, entanglement of clay/cattle).

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Restudy program:** Çatalhöyük Research Project from 1993 (Hodder) reopened the site with multi-specialist reflexive recording; microstratigraphy (e.g. Wendy Matthews) undermined sharp shrine/house dualism.
- **Chronology:** Calibrated sequences and later radiocarbon programs supersede bare 6500–5700 BC figures.
- **Religion:** Contextual and practice-based readings reduce mother-goddess totalization; multivocal heritage (goddess tours vs national archaeology) recorded on [[catalhoyuk]].
- **replication_status: reanalyzed-contested** — core material presence (paintings, dense housing, farming) stands; classification and social interpretation heavily revised.

## Current Standing

Still required reading as the founding synthesis and as a **historiographic object**: how a 1960s culture-historical dig produced a global Neolithic mythos. Use for attributed 1960s claims and type-images; use Hodder-project and specialist monographs for current lifeways and chronology. Deep treatment: [[hubs/studies/mellaart-catal-huyuk-study]].
