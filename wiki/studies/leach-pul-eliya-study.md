# Pul Eliya — Study

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/leach-pul-eliya-study|Studies Hub]]. Source: [[leach-pul-eliya-1961]]. Author: [[leach-edmund]]. Society: [[pul-eliya]].

## Question and Context

How do kinship and marriage actually relate to land holding and land use in a small irrigated Dry Zone village — and what does a meticulous **case history** do to the mid-century British theory of [[unilineal-descent|unilineal descent groups]] and to synthetic “typical village” accounts of Ceylon?

[[leach-edmund|Leach]]’s *Pul Eliya* (1961) is framed as the **antithesis** generated by a successful thesis. Leach states that British social anthropology’s most important theoretical development of 1935–54 enlarged understanding of unilineal descent groups (Radcliffe-Brown; Evans-Pritchard *The Nuer*; Fortes 1953 summary). Against that program — and against ideal-type North Central Province village descriptions (Ievers, Codrington, Pieris, Ryan’s “Vanniaveva”) — he offers a single-village micro-study: 146 persons, ≈135 irrigated acres, land-record depth to ≈1890.

Same-year twin: *[[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961|Rethinking Anthropology]]* (method and affinity polemics). Later self-label (via Gudeman & La Fontaine): *Highland Burma* “idealist” vs *Pul Eliya* “materialist.”

## Design and Methods

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Unit of analysis | Single village social system as **particular case history** (not regional sample) |
| Population | 146 residents (1954); Goyigama-dominated Dry Zone irrigation village |
| Fieldwork | ≈6 months June–Dec 1954; brief Aug 1956 revisit; concurrent comparative touchstone: N. O. Yalman’s longer Dry Zone fieldwork |
| Methods | Participant observation; compound/marriage genealogies; maps of field strips and house-site compounds; reconstruction of title/succession ≈1890–1954; labour and harvest organisation |
| Scale | Micro (named individuals, compounds, plots) with meso links ([[variga]], neighbouring tanks) |
| Funding | Leverhulme; Wenner-Gren; Ford Foundation personal grant for preparation |

**Method charter (attributed — Leach).** Social facts fall into two classes: **statistical** (economics, demography — require survey) and **jural/particular** (law, custom — require observed application and precedent). Prior NCP accounts describe ideal types; Pul Eliya exemplifies rules in singular particularity. Numerical quantities in the book are particular figures, not regional averages. Thesis sentence: jural rules and statistical norms are separate frames of reference, and **the former should always be considered secondary to the latter**.

**Reliability facts.** Short fieldwork by classical standards — Leach cites agreement with Yalman as partial defence. Name-code protocol encodes compound residence/birth and diga/binna. OCR source text has moderate noise; core arguments are clear.

## Findings

*(Attributed to Leach, 1961.)*

### Locality, not descent

- **Unilineal descent is not a factor** in the Pul Eliya situation. Corporate continuity without exclusive on-going corporations (no lineages, secret societies, or sects) rests on **territory**: village tank, gamgoda house-site area, Old Field with baga/pangu/elapata.
- Answer to Fortes’s question why descent rather than locality bases corporate groups: *in Pul Eliya, locality does*.
- Sinhalese may hold a **patrilineal ideology** without patrilineal lineage corporations (contra Ryan/Pieris-style ge-group readings).
- Leach refuses a simple bilateral-vs-unilineal typology: “sceptical of all typologies”; Pul Eliya “does not belong to either ‘type’.”

### Kinship as property talk

- “Kinship systems have no ‘reality’ at all except in relation to land and property. What the social anthropologist calls kinship structure is just a way of talking about property relations which can also be talked about in other ways.”
- “Marriage unifies; inheritance separates; property endures.”
- Economic relations are **prior** to kinship; kinship continuity is adaptive to the economic situation, not intrinsic.
- “Kinship solidarity” is an **image of the pattern of landholding**, not vice versa (illustrated by applications for scattered *badu* house-sites once co-residence ceases to be essential).

### Pavula, variga, compounds

- **[[pavula]]**: flexible “effective” kindred/political faction in a kinship idiom; not a fixed patrilineage. Ideal sibling solidarity vs effective *massina* (affine) alliance.
- **[[variga]]**: endogamous kind/variety linking villages; relatively corporate land-residence group under British indirect rule until ≈1938 court collapse. Membership tests turn on **affinity and property claims**, not descent.
- **Compounds**: 39 family heads in 13 compound groups (1954 Table I); full membership ≈ variga + Old Field land + compound (land outranks house alone).

### Traditional and non-traditional tenure

- **Old Field (*paraveni*)**: baga divisions, pangu shares, issara strips; traditional rights often in irrigation-ditch length (water) more than fixed ground area; water scarcity elevates Vel Vidane.
- **Plot succession ≈1890–1954**: inheritance fragments; near-kin marriage consolidates; brothers opposed as co-heirs, affines solidary; diga/binna, dowry, gift, mortgage (*ukas*), *ande* sharecropping, trader sales as transfer mechanisms.
- **Sinakkara freehold** and **badu Crown lease** (1935 LDO) expand irrigated area without raising productive capacity of the tank; restructure factions around private channels; elite capture; traditional tenure *imposes* solidarity, English freehold models bring prosperity **and** dissension.

### Labour regimes

- Primary title is uneven; labour and *ande*/*ukas* redistribute product among kinsmen.
- **Old Field**: scarcity of water and harvest labour → consistent affinal *kayiya* patterning.
- **Tank fishing** and **chena**: where economic scarcities do not force co-operation, kinship manifestations are random/incidental.

### Structure as statistical, not transcendental

- Social structure in the book is a **statistical notion** — a social fact like a suicide rate: normal rather than normative; by-product of many actions of which participants are neither wholly conscious nor wholly unaware.
- Continuity entity is not “Pul Eliya society” as corporate moral order but **Pul Eliya itself** (tank, gamgoda, Old Field layout hard to alter for technical reasons of irrigated rice).

## Theoretical Impact

- Major empirical lever against [[descent-theory]] and against treating kinship as isolatable equilibrium-legal structure.
- Explicit critique of Evans-Pritchard (*Nuer* ecology/structure halves autonomous), Fortes (lineage and locality “functionally discrete”), Mitchell (lineages vs villages), Radcliffe-Brown / *African Systems* (kinship system as self-subsistent reality).
- Speculative extension: the same microscopic method applied to “unilineal” societies might show “the structure of unilineal descent groups” as a **total fiction** — illuminating, like other theological ideas, but still a fiction.
- Paired with *Rethinking Anthropology* and *Highland Burma* model theory in Leach’s career arc; Kuper’s historiography treats Pul Eliya as the locality-not-descent counter-case.

## Current Standing

Teaching classic of property-centred kinship analysis and of anti-typological case-history method in British social anthropology. Read with *Rethinking Anthropology* and against Fortes’s 1953 unilineal-descent summary.

## Historiography

Produced at Cambridge under postwar British Africanist descent orthodoxy as foil; funded by Leverhulme, Wenner-Gren, Ford; engages Ceylon administrative/historical land literature as ideal-type foil. Colonial and post-colonial state land categories (Crown lease, freehold registration, 1938 D.R.O. reform ending variga-court sanctions) are part of the ethnographic object.
