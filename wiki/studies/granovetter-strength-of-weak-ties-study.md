# The Strength of Weak Ties (Granovetter, 1973)

Landmark mixed theoretical–empirical article that made **bridging weak ties** central to sociological network theory. Source record: [[granovetter-strength-of-weak-ties-1973]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/granovetter-strength-of-weak-ties-study]].

## Question and Context

How can micro-level interpersonal interaction generate macro-level patterns — diffusion, [[social-mobility|mobility]], community organization, cohesion — without a black-box leap? Granovetter answered: through the structure of interpersonal networks, via one limited aspect of dyads — [[tie-strength]]. Written against (a) theory that leaves micro and macro unjoined, (b) sociometry trapped in small groups, (c) network models that implicitly privilege strong ties.

## Design and Methods

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design type | Mixed: formal qualitative network theory + secondary reanalysis + original survey subsample |
| Unit of analysis | Dyadic ties; egocentric networks; community-level network topology (hypothesized) |
| Original sample | Random sample of recent PTM job changers, Boston suburb; total sample 282; personal-interview random subsample 100; contact-frequency base N=54 among job-via-contacts; path-length N=64 |
| Strength measure | Frequency of contact at time of job tip: often (≥2×/week), occasionally (>1×/year but <2×/week), rarely (≤1×/year) |
| Secondary materials | Davis sociograms; Newcomb triads; Rogers diffusion literature; Coleman–Katz–Menzel medical innovation; Kerckhoff hysterical contagion; Milgram/Korte small-world; Rapoport & Horvath junior-high sociogram; Gans *Urban Villagers*; Keyes Charlestown |
| Limits | Frequency = one of four definitional dimensions; positive/symmetric ties only; West End has no original network matrix |

## Findings

*(All attributed to Granovetter 1973.)*

1. **Overlap / forbidden triad.** Stronger A–B ties imply more shared third-party ties; the triad with two strong legs and an absent base is rare — supporting evidence from Davis (90% of 651 sociograms) and Newcomb balance processes.
2. **Bridges are weak.** Under the forbidden-triad logic, no strong tie is a bridge except under unlikely conditions; all bridges and local bridges are weak ties.
3. **Diffusion.** Whatever diffuses reaches more people and greater social distance via weak ties; strong-tie rumor chains recirculate inside cliques. "Marginal" early innovators may be rich in weak ties invisible to best-friend sociometry (Kerckhoff & Back multi-basis choices).
4. **Small-world.** Interracial chains more often completed when first White→Black step was to an acquaintance (50%) than to a friend (26%) (Korte data).
5. **Sociometric reach.** Rapoport & Horvath: tracing lower-ranked friends reached more people than tracing top-ranked friends.
6. **Jobs.** Of contact-mediated job finds: 16.7% often, 55.6% occasionally, 27.8% rarely (N=54) — skew to weak end; structure over pure motivation. Paths short: 39.1% direct from known employer; 45.3% one intermediary; 12.5% two; 3.1% more (N=64). Long paths resembled formal channels (no influence).
7. **Community.** West End non-mobilization reconstructed as clique fragmentation without bridging weak ties; Charlestown contrast (organizations + local work). Proposed principle: more local bridges → more capacity for concert.
8. **Transitivity.** Transitivity is a function of tie strength, not a universal small-group law (vs DHL model); weak ties enable intergroup and quasi-group analysis.

## Theoretical Impact

- Founding portable statement of [[strength-of-weak-ties-theory]]; charter for sociological [[network-analysis]] beyond small groups.
- Seed for later mobility, diffusion, social-capital, and economic-sociology programs (many not yet ingested).
- Forced community and urban sociology to take relational structure seriously alongside subculture ([[west-end-network-fragmentation-debate]]).

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Gans 1974 exchange** — networks necessary not sufficient; history, politics, culture, subneighborhood scale; some bridges existed (see debate page).
- **Measurement:** frequency proxy; weak-tie under-sampling in sociometry; Light's "most ties are weak so base rates expect weak-tie flow" objection noted by Granovetter as inconclusive without baseline networks.
- **Later literature (reception note, not primary-ingested here):** extensive citations; conditional support — weak ties for novel information; strong ties for trust/influence; Burt structural holes reframe bridging; some job-search studies find strong-tie advantages for certain outcomes. Hence `replication_status: partially-supported` rather than unqualified replication.

## Current Standing

Among the most cited articles in late-20th-century sociology; core teaching text in network sociology. Empirical point estimates (16.7/55.6/27.8%) are historical single-study results — cite attributed, not as timeless rates. The **structural intuition** (bridging weak ties integrate; strong-tie worlds can fragment) remains generative.
