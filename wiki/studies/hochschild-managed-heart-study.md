# The Managed Heart (as a study)

Analysis of [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Arlie Russell Hochschild]]'s *The Managed Heart: Commercialization of Human Feeling* (1983) as a research act. Bibliographic record: [[hochschild-managed-heart-1983]].

Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/hochschild-managed-heart-study|Studies Hub]].

## Question and Context

Hochschild asked what workers do when the product of labor is a **state of mind** in others, and what it costs when private systems of feeling management are sold under corporate control. She joined three discourses rarely held together: labor (post-industrial service / deskilling), display (Goffmanian interaction rules), and emotion theory (what emotion is and how it can be managed) (attributed).

**Intellectual setting:** critical-turn U.S. sociology; service-sector expansion; dialogue with [[mills-c-wright|Mills]] (*White Collar*, [[personality-market]]), [[marx-karl|Marx]] ([[alienation]]), [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] ([[impression-management]], surface acting), Braverman (deskilling), Bell (post-industrial encounters), Stanislavski (deep acting), and Freud (signal function of anxiety extended to emotion generally).

**Origin:** Foreign Service childhood (diplomatic smiles "from below"); Delta recurrent-training room and the notebook coinage of "emotional labor" (2012 preface, attributed).

## Design and Methods

| Element | As conducted (attributed / FACT of form) |
|---|---|
| Stance | "Not intended as an empirical report, or not simply as that" — illustrated ideas; point, illustrate, comment |
| Pool 1 | 261 UC Berkeley students (1974): open prompts on deep emotion and changing situation vs feeling |
| Pool 2 | Delta as high-demand benchmark (Southern, nonunion, strong training/service reputation): observed Initial + Recurrent Training; interviews with ~20 officials, 7 supervisors (group), trainers, advertising agents; 30 years of ads on microfilm |
| Pool 2b | Pan Am recruiting observation (SF); open-ended interviews 3–5 hrs with 30 Bay Area FAs (25 women, 5 men; multi-carrier; avg age 35, avg 11 years experience) |
| Pool 3 | Mini-project: 5 bill collectors (including Delta billing head) — "toe and heel" contrast |
| Macro | Appendix C: 1970 Census occupational coding for jobs "most calling for" emotional labor |

**Why Delta:** exaggerates company demands and worker constraints to build a benchmark (attributed). **Gender design:** FA work allows same-job male/female comparison; men ~15% of FAs (attributed).

**What the design can do:** found a vocabulary and show mechanisms of commercial emotion management. **What it cannot:** probability-sample prevalence of burnout; full industry variation; clinical etiology.

## Findings

All attributed to Hochschild unless noted.

### Core concepts

- **[[emotional-labor]]** vs **[[emotion-work]]** (exchange vs use value).
- **[[surface-and-deep-acting]]**; corporate training as deep-acting school.
- **[[feeling-rules]]** and misfeeling; pinches as rule detection.
- Private system = emotion work + feeling rules + gift exchange of feeling → **[[commercialization-of-feeling|transmutation]]** when sold.
- **[[status-shield]]**; gender makes "two jobs of one" in public-contact work.

### Empirical poles

- Flight attendants: enhance customer status; induce trust/care; disguise fatigue/irritation; smiles "on them but not of them."
- Bill collectors: deflate status; maintain suspicion; anger as occupational resource ("soft collect" vs hard).

### Scale estimates (Appendix C, 1970)

- ~**38.1%** of all jobs; ~**55.2%** of employed women; ~**27.7%** of employed men in jobs most calling for emotional labor.

### Human costs (Ch 9)

Three stances toward the work (attributed): (1) over-identify → burnout risk; (2) separate "true" from "on-stage" self → chronic split; (3) healthy distance / estrangement from the role as coping — each with its own risk. Cultural celebration of unmanaged/"authentic" feeling rises as the managed heart expands.

## Theoretical Impact

Founded the research program and keyword of **emotional labor**; organized the sociology of emotions' applied wing; bridged microsociology of feeling with political economy of service and gender. 2012 preface cites massive academic and popular uptake.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- Concept **widely extended** (sometimes diluted) in organizational psychology, nursing, teaching, care work, digital service.
- **Comparable-worth** and burnout literatures treat emotional labor as undercompensated skill (afterword cites Steinberg & Jacobs; Erickson testimony).
- Debates on **gender essentialism**, measurement (SA/DA scales), and whether unpaid care should share the label.
- `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — not a failed lab replication; a generative concept with contested boundaries and operationalizations.

## Current Standing

Canonical. Still the reference point for defining emotional labor; later work multiplies cases and measures while returning to Hochschild's three criteria when rigor is demanded. Domestic companion *[[hochschild-second-shift-1989|The Second Shift]]* (1989) is now ingested ([[hochschild-second-shift-study]]; hub [[hubs/studies/hochschild-second-shift-study]]) — unpaid care and [[stalled-revolution]] using the same feeling-rules / emotion-work toolkit. *Time Bind*, *Outsourced Self* remain queued.
