# The Civilizing Process (study)

**Hub:** [[hubs/studies/elias-civilizing-process-study|Studies Hub — Civilizing Process]]

Landmark mixed historical-comparative and theoretical study by [[elias-norbert|Norbert Elias]] (*[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]*, 1939). Dual empirical tracks: (1) series of manners texts showing standard-shifts; (2) long-run European [[state-formation]] and [[court-society]] narrative. Founding study of the [[civilizing-process]] research program.

## Question and Context

How did Western upper-class manners and psychical habitus change over centuries, and how do those changes connect to the formation of states and monopolies of force? Posed against static "civilization" self-images and against psychology detached from long social process.

## Design and Methods

- **Manners series:** Comparative quotations from medieval to early modern/modern conduct literature (table, natural functions, bedroom, sexual relations, aggression), read for advancing thresholds of shame and prohibition.
- **State/court narrative:** Secondary historiography plus process reconstruction of feudalization, elimination contests, royal centralization (France weighted).
- **Unit of analysis:** Figurations and standards, not survey samples of living respondents.
- **Dates:** Research and writing in the 1930s; published 1939 — **not** an ethnography of a living community; `fieldwork_dates` records the documentary synthesis moment.

## Findings

*(Attributed — Elias.)* Directional civilizing of Western upper-class manners across table, body, bedroom, sexual relations, and aggressiveness; *courtoisie* → *civilité* → *civilisation* stages; Erasmus 1530 as *civilité* landmark; rising [[threshold-of-shame]]; free warrior competition → dual force/tax [[monopoly-mechanism]]; [[royal-mechanism]] and [[courtization]]; pacification → [[self-constraint]] as second nature; unplanned order from [[figuration|figurations]]; spurts/counter-spurts (not pure linear progress).

## Findings (summary)

*(Attributed — Elias.)* Directional civilizing of manners; rising shame thresholds; link of pacification and self-constraint; monopoly and royal mechanisms; unplanned long-run order. Detail on hub page.

## Theoretical Impact

Founded process-figurational sociology; re-specified Weberian monopoly of force as historical process; influenced historical sociology of the body, violence, sport, and the state.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

`replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` — not a survey replication target; subject to sustained theoretical and historical reanalysis (teleology, Eurocentrism, decivilizing violence, source bias). See [[civilizing-process-debate]] and Studies Hub.

## Current Standing

Canonical reference point; active research program continues; core empirical series still cited and criticized.
