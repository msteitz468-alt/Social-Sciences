# Partial Connections — Study

Source: [[strathern-partial-connections-1991]]. Thinker: [[strathern-marilyn]]. Related study: [[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study]]. Concepts: [[partial-connections]] · [[postplural]] · [[fractal-person]]. Debates: [[writing-culture-debate]]. Methods: [[comparative-method]].

## Question and Context

After *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]* and amid the [[writing-culture|Writing Culture]] moment, how can anthropology still do **cross-cultural comparison** — especially for Melanesia — without restoring holist societies-as-units, without surrendering to pure textual pastiche, and without letting either “theory of writing” or “regional data” swallow the other? [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]’s *[[strathern-partial-connections-1991|Partial Connections]]* (1991) answers by making **proportion, scale, and partial fit** the shared problem of ethnographic writing *and* comparative method. Study object: not a single society at a fieldwork moment, but the **culture of anthropological argument** staged through Melanesian and English materials.

## Findings

*(Attributed to Strathern, 1991/2004.)*

### Scale, complexity, postplural

- Complexity is intrinsic to ethnography and comparison; making it visible requires simplification that also makes description hard.
- Scale-switching (domaining and magnification) creates a **multiplier effect** *and* **information loss**; if loss is proportionate to gain, amount of information stays constant; complexity **replicates** at every level of detail.
- Anthropology has moved from plural to **[[postplural]]** perception: multiple perspectives make every approach partial; recurrence of similar propositions makes everything seem connected — the book artificially reproduces that realization in its organization.
- Images needed: not taxonomy or regional map alone, but self-similar/intermittent forms (coastline, Koch curve, **Cantor’s dust**).

### Writing after Writing Culture

- [[ethnographic-evocation|Tyler’s evocation]] opposes representation and appears to leave no place for classical comparison; yet Tyler’s therapeutic journey still integrates the writer/reader as traveller — a **hidden aesthetic form** Strathern finds insufficient.
- Realization that one speaks about cultures only through cultures dismantles comparative scaffolding into internal referencing of monographs; Strathern seeks a path beyond both holism and pure pastiche.

### Feminism, cyborg, partial

- Feminism and anthropology meet as **awkward partition**, not complementary halves of one person; each is only partially enacted.
- Haraway’s **cyborg** (whole image, not image of a whole): connection without common origin or organic totality; **compatibility without comparability**; partial = incomplete + partisan.
- Anthropology–feminism as **prosthetic** mutual extension.

### Comparison without holist units

- Cross-cultural surveys still locate traits “in” societies even when modelling interdependence (Galton’s problem / network autocorrelation) — societies get counted.
- Units of comparison are the problem: e.g. “initiation” is non-unitary at close scale.
- **[[partial-connections]]**: relate by partition and remainder, not by recovering seamless wholes; cyborg observes on its own scale(s).

### Melanesian imagery and writing anthropology

- Wantoat trees/flutes, Barok figure–ground, flutes as levels without independent substrate: Melanesian handling of imagery as self-scaling and cut-based.
- Historical completeness of trade/transmission would not eliminate analogy; “if only one knew the history” is a Western historicist temptation.
- Complexity not rankable as evolutionary social elaboration (contra Feil-type rankings of relational complexity).
- **Melanesian cyborg** vs Haraway: components cut from *same* conceptual material (shell/matrilineage, man/bamboo), centered in the person’s perceptual circuit.
- Persons extend persons; remaindering reproduces grounding sociality; writing anthropology likewise produces infinite complexity and more gaps from complexity.

## Theoretical Impact

Key methodological companion to *Gender of the Gift*; coined/stabilized [[partial-connections]] for later [[ontological-turn]] and de la Cadena-style co-labor. Major post–[[writing-culture]] statement on what comparison can still be. Provoked Wagner’s [[fractal-person]] and reciprocal Melanesianist theory conversation. Feeds [[comparative-method]] critique without abandoning comparison.

Active reference for postplural comparison, ontological-turn method talk, and Strathern’s oeuvre next to *Gender of the Gift* and *After Nature*. Read as attributed method theory with ethnographic illustrations, not as primary ethnography of named societies at a single date.
