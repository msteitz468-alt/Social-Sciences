# Boas's Kwakiutl Ethnography

The [[boas-franz|Boas]]–[[hunt-george|Hunt]] Kwakiutl corpus (~175 publications, 1886–1943, plus the unfinished summary manuscript) treated as a single research act, in the form fixed by [[codere-helen|Helen Codere]]'s edition, *[[boas-kwakiutl-ethnography-1966|Kwakiutl Ethnography]]* (1966). It is the reference ethnographic corpus of the Northwest Coast and the evidentiary base beneath [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]]'s and [[benedict-ruth|Benedict]]'s famous readings of the [[potlatch]].

## Question and Context

Boas's stated aim was a complete written record of an alien way of life disclosing the "innermost thoughts" and "mental life" of the people — with unconscious meanings counted more reliable than conscious "secondary explanations." No general method treatise: the program is visible in the corpus itself and in his 1909 introduction (reproduced by Codere), where he repudiates his own first decade of hurried BAAS survey publications (1886–c.1895) as superseded by the 1897 and 1909 monographs.

## Design and Methods

- **[[text-collection|Native-language text collection]]** as the backbone: Hunt, taught to write Kwak'wala in 1893, produced most of the >3,000 published pages of texts; Boas did final editing and translation with iterative cross-checking.
- **Repeat fieldwork**: twelve Northwest Coast trips, 1886–1930 (five exclusively Kwakiutl), first winter-ceremonial observation 1895, final trip at age 72 in 1930; institutional vehicles ran from the BAAS committee (Hale, 1888) through the 1893 World's Fair (Putnam) to the Jesup North Pacific Expedition (1897–).
- **Participant standing**: 1951 Kwakiutl informants remembered Boas living with the people, potlatching, and dancing in ceremonial dress; he held potlatch names, and his 1930 letter to Benedict complains that feast obligations were leaving him "broke."

## Findings (attributed)

- **Potlatch**: mechanics of blanket currency (50¢ single blanket), scaled loan-interest (to 100% at a year), name-pawning ("selling a slave"), the career sequence of names validated by distributions, coppers as high-denomination instruments (named coppers valued to 7,500 blankets, Fort Rupert 1893), property destruction and grease feasts as rivalry moves. Boas's summary: "the interest-bearing investment of property."
- **Social organization**: the [[numaym]] as 658 named seats across thirteen tribes; contested primogeniture; marriage as property-and-privilege transfer ("obtained in war"), including fictitious marriages to move privileges.
- **Winter ceremonial**: the eyewitness Fort Rupert record of Nov 15–27, 1895 (three tribes combined); hamatsa excitement-and-taming cycle; staged wounding/resurrection theater with documented trick mechanisms; the sacred/profane season and name duality; Boas's diffusion argument that the Cannibal complex is a recent Bella Bella/Rivers Inlet import (18th-century southward spread of the ceremonial, an oral-tradition-based, century-level inference).
- **Religion**: graded shamanic practitioners; the shaman-skeptic first-person narrative "I desired to learn the ways of the shaman" (Boas 1930) by his "principal informant," secret name Qa'sElid — told four times (1897–1930) with "vacillating attitude" between fraud-exposure and belief; this is the case [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] made famous as "Quesalid" ([[effectiveness-of-symbols]]). Codere separately speculates a witchcraft eyewitness account is "presumably George Hunt" — a distinct attribution thread, not merged here.
- **War**: dated raid narratives (1840s–1856) with head-taking and "killing to wipe one's eyes"; Boas's interpretive judgment that the people were "at heart... not warlike," with rivalry channeled into property.

## Theoretical Impact

Evidentiary base for [[mauss-the-gift-1925|Mauss's *The Gift*]] (potlatch as agonistic total prestation), [[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Benedict's Dionysian Kwakiutl]], Lévi-Strauss's symbolic-efficacy and art analyses, and the numaym's later career in kinship theory. Within Boas's own program it is the flagship of [[historical-particularism]]'s method commitments.

## Critiques, Reanalyses, and Replications

- **Codere (1950s–1966)**: potlatch escalation is historical, shaped by fur-trade wealth, depopulation, and the potlatch ban; scholarship misused the 57-year corpus as homogeneous. Her *Fighting with Property* (1950) is not yet ingested. See [[potlatch-interpretation-debate]].
- **Benedict's selection**: the "megalomaniac paranoid" configuration reads Boas's materials selectively (noted on [[benedict-patterns-of-culture-study]]).
- **Authorship**: how much of the corpus is Hunt's is a live question ([[hunt-george]]).
- The corpus documents a memory culture already transformed — Boas's informant record and Codere's edition both say so explicitly.

## Current Standing

The corpus remains the indispensable primary record; its syntheses (Benedict, and to a degree Mauss) are contested, the primary texts are not. `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested` refers to the interpretive superstructure, not the documentary base.
