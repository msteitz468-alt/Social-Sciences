# Sultanian

PPNA-horizon archaeological culture of the **southern Levant**, named from Tell es-Sultan ([[jericho|Jericho]]). In [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (2000), one of three Khiamian-descended corridor cultures (with Aswadian and [[mureybetian-culture|Mureybetian]]), corresponding to what Kenyon called PPNA at Jericho when that label still meant a local phase more than a pan-Levant culture.

## Definition and Construct History

Kenyon’s basal Neolithic at Jericho (wall, tower, round mud-brick houses) became the Sultanian in regional culture-history. Cauvin stresses stratigraphic lacuna between early Natufian and Sultanian at Jericho (no late Natufian/Khiamian on-site in his account).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material:** plano-convex mud-brick round houses; paved floors; collective stone wall (~3 m) and tower (~10 m base diameter, ~8.5 m preserved height) at Jericho as supra-domestic architecture; PPNA lithics; early agriculture in corridor context. **Language/genes:** unknown.

## Distribution and Chronology

Southern Levant (Israel/Jordan zone in Cauvin’s map); ~9500–8300 BC in his calibrated tables.

## Lifeways

Village life with monumental collective construction at Jericho; plant cultivation appearing in the PPNA package (as of his synthesis).

## Relations and Successions

Sibling to Mureybetian and Aswadian; later southern PPNB receives diffusion from northern Early PPNB in Cauvin’s Part II model (position).

## Identity Cautions

Not an ethnonym; Jericho tower function debated (defense, ritual, other).

## Historiography

Textbook PPNA image largely Sultanian/Jericho; northern sites later decentre sole-origin stories.
