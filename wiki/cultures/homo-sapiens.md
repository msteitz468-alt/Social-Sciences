# Homo sapiens

**Note on register:** this page summarizes the received teaching account in
[[fagan-durrani-people-of-the-earth-2022|Fagan & Durrani (2022)]], a decade-lagging
textbook; modern-human origins is a fast-moving, contested field. Genetic and dating
claims are date-stamped and attributed; the live disputes are on
[[modern-human-origins-debate]]. This page treats the *species* as an analytic construct
in the archaeological record, not as an ethnographic subject.

## Definition and Construct History

*Homo sapiens* — anatomically modern humans — is the sole surviving hominin. Fagan &
Durrani frame its origin as a resolved-in-favour-of-Africa but still-contested problem
(see [[modern-human-origins-debate]]): the older **multiregional** model (parallel
evolution from regional *[[homo-erectus|H. erectus]]* populations) has given way to a
predominantly **out-of-Africa (replacement)** account, itself now softened into an
**assimilation/admixture** model given ancient-DNA evidence of interbreeding with
[[neanderthals|Neanderthals]] and [[denisovans|Denisovans]] (attributed — Reich 2010;
Prüfer et al. 2014).

The oldest fossils assigned to the species are from **Jebel Irhoud, Morocco**, re-dated to
~315,000 BP (attributed — Hublin et al. 2017), a find the textbook uses to argue the
African record is biased toward East Africa merely by where excavation has concentrated;
earlier East African fossils include Omo Kibish (~195,000 BP) and Herto (*H. sapiens
idaltu*, ~160,000 BP).

## Anatomical / Genetic Signature

Anatomical markers: high rounded cranium, reduced brow, chin, gracile skeleton. Genetic
signature (date-stamped, attributed): African origin ~200,000+ BP on mtDNA coalescence;
non-African populations carry a few percent [[neanderthals|Neanderthal]] ancestry, and
Oceanian/some Asian populations additional [[denisovans|Denisovan]] ancestry, from
interbreeding events during the dispersal out of Africa (as of Reich 2010, per Fagan &
Durrani 2022). aDNA speaks to populations and admixture, **not** directly to language,
culture, or ethnicity (four-way non-identity — see [[culture]]).

## Distribution and Chronology

African origin by ~315,000 BP; a first, impermanent dispersal into the Levant (Skhul,
Qafzeh, ~120,000–80,000 BP) that apparently retreated; sustained dispersal into Eurasia
from ~50,000 BP; Sahul by ~65–55,000 BP; the Americas via
[[peopling-of-the-americas|Beringia]] by ~16,000 BP. Behavioral markers — pigment use,
beads, engraving — accumulate in Africa well before 50,000 BP (e.g. [[blombos-cave|Blombos]]),
which Fagan & Durrani read as favouring a **gradual, mosaic** emergence of
[[behavioral-modernity]] over a sudden "cognitive revolution."

## Lifeways

Across the span of this page's construct, *H. sapiens* lifeways run the full documented
range of the human past — Late Pleistocene foraging, the [[upper-paleolithic-revolution|Upper
Paleolithic]] florescence of art and complex technology, post-glacial broad-spectrum
foraging, the [[agricultural-revolution|origins of farming]], and every subsequent social
form. The species is the bearer of, not a single lifeway, but the entire cultural sequence
charted on [[world-prehistory-timeline]]; specific lifeways belong on the society, culture,
site, and phenomenon pages, not here.

## Relations and Successions

`descends_from` *[[homo-heidelbergensis|Homo heidelbergensis]]* (or an African variant
sometimes split as *H. rhodesiensis*); `associated_with` [[neanderthals]] and
[[denisovans]] through documented admixture — an evidenced genetic relation, not identity.
Bearer of the [[upper-paleolithic-revolution|Upper Paleolithic]] industries in Eurasia and
of essentially all subsequent [[archaeological-record|archaeological cultures]].

## Identity Cautions

The species is a biological taxon; it is **not** a culture, a language, or a people.
"Behavioral modernity" is a contested threshold, not a switch. Regional fossil and genetic
samples are unevenly recovered (East-Africa excavation bias; sparse aDNA in warm climates),
so distribution maps track sampling as much as past reality.

## Historiography

The multiregional-vs-out-of-Africa debate (Wolpoff vs. Stringer) and its aDNA-era
resolution into admixture models are treated at [[modern-human-origins-debate]]. Fagan &
Durrani's account is a textbook synthesis; primary archaeogenetic treatment in this vault
runs through [[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich (2018)]]. Date-stamp all genetic claims, which
supersede rapidly.

### Tattersall — *Masters of the Planet* (2012)

*Source: [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012]], [[tattersall-ian]]. **As of Tattersall 2012** — pre–Hublin et al. Irhoud re-dating for fullest claims; nuclear aDNA incomplete; do not overwrite later Reich/Stringer/Hublin layers as if current.*

**African origin fossils (2012 horizon).** Tattersall places confidence in African birth of *H. sapiens* on (1) earliest plausible fossils and (2) living-population DNA convergence. First bony structure “exactly/almost exactly” like today: NE Africa — Omo Kibish rocks then ~**195 ka** (fragmentary skull, plausible *H. sapiens* not matching any living population exactly); [[herto]] trio firmly **155–160 ka**, adult with high voluminous vault and small face retracted beneath the front. Together: distinctive basic *H. sapiens* cranial anatomy by ~**200–160 ka**, coinciding with then molecular coalescence estimates (as of Tattersall 2012). Omo artifacts “nondescript”; Herto handaxes + prepared-core flakes (latest recorded African handaxes) at the Acheulean → MSA transition — cultural start “more of a whimper than a bang.” MSA origin ~**300–200 ka** most likely **before** recognizable *H. sapiens* — biology/culture disconnect (**thesis**).

**Irhoud / “archaic *H. sapiens*” critique.** He rejects the wastebasket label “archaic *Homo sapiens*” for large-brained non-Neanderthal forms lacking the reduced/retracted face. [[jebel-irhoud]] crania (may be >160 ka as of his text) with Mousterian-like kit: brain volumes ~1,305 and 1,400 cc; small lower face but **forwardly positioned** facial skeleton, prominent brows, retreating forehead — “none presents as obvious variety of *H. sapiens*” (Tattersall 2012 morphological **position**). **Post-2012 Hublin et al. reassignment/re-dating is not this horizon** — keep Irhoud modernity contested and date-stamped; page frontmatter already carries the later ~315 ka claim separately.

**Anatomical ≠ cognitive modernity lag (load-bearing thesis).** Body form and symbolic cognitive system acquired at **different times**; earliest anatomical *H. sapiens* appear cognitively indistinguishable from Neanderthals and contemporaries. Birth of anatomical *H. sapiens* may precede first pierced *Nassarius* bead by as much as ~100 kyr — a huge biological event (skull/postcranial features, brain growth, almost certainly internal organization) via short-term developmental reorganization, not gradual becoming (**thesis**). Symbolic/linguistic potential is framed as **exaptation**/byproduct of that reorganization (~200 ka as he dates it), lying fallow until culturally “discovered” — preferred stimulus **language invention** (possibly by children in a small African community) rather than silver-bullet FOXP2, larynx-only models, Cro-Magnon-only late gene, or ToM alone. Full architecture belongs on [[behavioral-modernity]] and [[modern-human-origins-debate]] (do not duplicate here).

**African MSA symbolic evidence (pointer).** Earliest firm symbolic intimations from Africa/environs: arguable Skhul shells/pigments >100 ka; firmer Aterian *Nassarius* ~80 ka; best MSA adornment at [[blombos-cave]] ~76 ka beads + engraved ochre ~77 ka; bone tools missing from contemporary European Neanderthal kits; Pinnacle Point marine resources/pigments/bladelets from ~164 ka and silcrete heat-treatment ~72 ka as near-exception multi-stage planning; [[klasies-river]] functional space division >100 ka and near-modern remains 80–90 ka; Diepkloof decorated eggshell ~60 ka; EKP beads ~40 ka at MSA–LSA transition (all as of Tattersall 2012). Southern drought after 80–60 ka makes Blombos/Pinnacle Point only moderately likely direct northern antecedents — more a pan-African quickening on a predisposition born with physical *H. sapiens* (**thesis**).

**Levant foray and later takeover.** Thoroughly modern Qafzeh skeleton ≥100 ka alongside nonstandard big-brained forms, all with [[mousterian-industry]]; Skhul ~100 ka high vaults but unretracted faces — hybrid-with-Neanderthal rejected; hybrid-with-N. African Aterian-like populations floated as “more intriguing” (**position**). Early Levant foray not permanent; by ~60 ka Neanderthals back in charge; rapid world takeover awaited symbolic behaviour patterns. Molecular (as of 2012): ≥14 African lineages; non-Africans as African subsets; low diversity + bottleneck(s) ~75–60 ka (Toba scenario debated; MIS 4 cold certain). Dispersals after ~60 ka: coastal Asia to Australia ≥50 ka (ocean crossing); Europe ~40 ka; N. Siberia above Arctic Circle by ~21 ka — culturally enabled where “cold-adapted” Neanderthals shunned extremes. Behaviorally modern *H. sapiens* produces a **hominid monoculture** as Neanderthals, late *H. erectus*, and Flores hobbits yield (**thesis**/reflexivity). Expansion by demographic spread and intensification feedback, not deliberate expeditioneering.

**Genetics note.** mtDNA/nuclear living-population and early aDNA claims here are **(as of Tattersall 2012)**; admixture percentages and maps on this page’s Genetic Signature section follow later Reich-layer sources.
