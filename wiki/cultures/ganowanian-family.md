# Ganowanian Family (Morgan)

## Definition and Construct History

**Ganowanian** is [[morgan-lewis-henry|Lewis Henry Morgan]]’s **etic construct** in *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity]]* (1871) for the “family” of **American Indian** peoples he believed shared a common **classificatory system of relationship** (type case: Seneca-Iroquois) and, by inference, historical connection. The name and classification **fell into disuse** (Tooker 1997); they are **not** modern linguistic stocks (e.g. Algonquian, Athabaskan, Uto-Aztecan).

`construct_status: obsolete` as language-family science; retained here as a **history-of-anthropology object**.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

- **Linguistic (Morgan):** shared classificatory kinship plan, not comparative sound laws.
- **Material / genetic:** none claimed here; four-way non-identity — do not equate Ganowanian with a people, genes, or archaeological culture.

## Distribution and Chronology

Morgan applied the label across North American (and related) Indigenous nations from which he obtained schedules; chronological depth is **conjectural** (Asiatic origin argument).

## Lifeways

Not a lifeway unit — a **terminological** grouping.

## Relations and Successions

Morgan `associated_with` Turanian classificatory systems for Asiatic-origin inference — **association, not identity**. Type society in the wiki: [[iroquois]] (Seneca).

## Identity Cautions

Does **not** name a self-conscious people, a valid modern language family, or a genetic population. Indigenous nations in Morgan’s tables retain their own names and histories.
