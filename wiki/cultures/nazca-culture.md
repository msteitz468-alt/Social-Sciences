## Definition and Construct History

Nazca names the south-coast Peruvian archaeological culture of the Early Intermediate Period, famous for polychrome pottery, fine textiles, and desert geoglyphs (Nazca Lines). As of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]], the small Nazca drainage never supported more than ~25,000 people; ceramic style core is Ica and Nazca, with coastal distribution Chincha–Acarí. EIP onset is defined by **polychrome slip painting** ~200 BC as the Early Horizon drought ended, succeeding [[paracas-culture]] resin painting. Scarre ed. 2013 uses 200 BC–AD 650.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Ceramics (eight phases, Moseley 2001):** Nazca 1 preserves Paracas mythical content + realistic birds/fish/fruit; 2–4 "Monumental" bold simple plants/animals/people/demons; 5 "Proliferous" (bodiless heads, cluttered backgrounds); 6–7 militaristic motifs, elite portraits, some Moche-like design (possible maritime contact); 8 design breakup, disjoint figures. Chronology problematic — ¹⁴C overlap; Phase 8 estimated end c. AD 600 but some associated dates AD 755 or later.

**Textiles:** more fine fabrics from core valleys than any other Peru region (Moseley); grow from Paracas tradition; technical characteristics more precocious than north; motifs often on fabric before ceramics; **massive alpaca wool** use — north coast lacked comparable wool expenditure for almost a thousand years. Wool esteem + seated burials link Nazca to south highland sphere; wool source probably sierra herds, Ayacucho region with close coastal relationships (Huarpa symbiosis).

**Cahuachi (Silverman via Moseley):** ~40 mounds along riverside ~150 ha; virtually all built Phase 3; many visitors, few permanent residents — more akin to Casma Sechín complex than Moche capital. Great Temple 20 m high encasing natural hill with wedge-shaped adobes; basal enclosure with 47 × 75 m plaza. Separate kancha-enclosure + mound facilities by many groups; platforms adobe facades over trash from ritual feasting — mound size commemorated ceremonial lavishness. Corporate construction declined end Nazca 3; then great mortuary ground and votive place; looting severe. Silverman: built/used by people living elsewhere; cyclical ritual gatherings. If platforms reflect politics: Moche centralized rule vs Nazca **confederacy/maximal ayllu** (every *parcialidad* asserts identity by separate mound).

**Geoglyphs (Kosok & Reiche; Aveni via Moseley):** brushing away dark desert varnish to expose lighter sediment; large figures by small crews (Aveni: 16,000 m² cleared in about a week). Exceptional concentration on ~200 sq. km Nazca pampa: ~1,300 km of straight lines (to 20+ km), ~300 geometric figures; linear+geometric cover ~3.6 million m² (~2% of pampa). Aveni team: no statistically significant celestial or directional correlations; 62 ray centers interconnected oasis-to-oasis; glyphs densest near irrigated land; younger cross older — brief use then forgotten; trapezoids often point upstream near rivers (symbolic water link). ~36 animal drawings (birds, killer whales, monkey, spider, fox/llama, human, plants) — mostly single non-crossing outline for walking. Multiple functions; calendrical significance suspected not demonstrated. Not centralized plan — each group erected its own; individuality with cultural coherence without large-scale integrated power = Nazca hallmarks. Scarre records more speculative theories with skepticism (alien strips, balloons, etc.).

**Linguistic / genetic:** none in sources.

## Distribution and Chronology

Río Nazca formed by eight short drainages across pampa converging ~35 km inland; surface runoff disappears mid-drainage then reappears — **separate oases**, not a unified valley. Habitation on terraced hillsides by irrigated floodplains; first four phases clustered above/below dry midpoints. Phase 5 settlement change after short severe drought beginning **AD 560**: people moved up some tributaries >1,000 m; along Río Nazca into normally dry sections with shallow subsurface water — sunken gardens or long aquifer tunnels (qanat-like) proposed; scant evidence of tunneling until Spanish times in other valleys (Moseley). Influence Phase 2 to Dos Palmas (Pisco), Phase 3 to Tambo Viejo (Acarí), coinciding with Cahuachi florescence.

## Lifeways

Dispersed oasis farming; highland wool and mineral exchange (Huarpa supplies wool, copper, minerals; coast salt and marine products — Moseley). Periodic pilgrimage aggregation at Cahuachi. Geoglyph labor as group-level ritual investment without imperial command structure.

## Relations and Successions

- Succeeds [[paracas-culture]]; precedes Middle Horizon [[wari-culture]] sphere expansion.
- Symbiosis with **Huarpa** (Ayacucho) ~AD 200–600; Huarpa terracing and Nawinpukio foundations for later Wari empire-building.
- Political contrast with [[moche-culture]] (centralized dual capital vs confederated mounds/geoglyphs).
- Geoglyph tradition wider (Lambayeque to N. Chile) but Nazca pampa exceptional concentration.

## Identity Cautions

Nazca ≠ single state ≠ language ≠ genes. Population ceiling ~25,000 is Moseley’s estimate. Geoglyph function remains multi-hypothesis; do not assert astronomy or hydrology as wiki-voice purpose. Phase chronology still problematic.
