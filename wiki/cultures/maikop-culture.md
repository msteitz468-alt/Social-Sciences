# Maikop Culture

## Definition and Construct History

The **Maikop culture** is an etic North Caucasus elite-kurgan construct of the late Eneolithic / early Bronze Age, named from the spectacular 1897 chieftain’s mound. [[anthony-david-w|David Anthony]] (*[[anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007|The Horse, the Wheel, and Language]]*, 2007) re-dates Maikop to **Middle/Late Uruk (~3700–3100 BCE)** — about a thousand years earlier than older Troy II / Ur death-pits correlations — and treats it as the **filter** through which southern urban innovations (including possibly wagons and new metal alloys) first entered the Pontic-Caspian steppes. Local Caucasian origins (continuity from Svobodnoe) plus southern technology define the package. Not a steppe “Kurgan culture” ethnic identity — Anthony explicitly rejects Gimbutas’s expansion of “Kurgan” to include Maikop.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Elite wealth (Maikop chieftain’s kurgan, ~3700–3400 BCE):** Timber-partitioned chamber; two female attendants + male with gold/silver cups, arsenical bronze tools, silver-sheathed staffs with lost-wax cast gold/silver bulls; tunic with **68 golden lions and 19 golden bulls**; turquoise/carnelian/gold beads; five-rosette gold diadem. Lion–bull iconography Mesopotamian (lions not native to North Caucasus); turquoise likely NE Iran or Amu Darya; red carnelian western Pakistan (Anthony 2007).

**Phases:** Early Maikop ~**3700–3400 BCE** (Maikop type site, Galugai); late **Novosvobodnaya/Klady** ~**3400–3000 BCE** with stone chamber graves. Klady k.31 gr.5 alone: 15 heavy bronze daggers, a **61 cm sword** (“oldest sword in the world” per Anthony), three sleeved axes, two hammer-axes, carnelian/lapis/gold ornaments including gold-sheathed human molar.

**Wagons / wheels:** Traces of ≥2 solid wooden disc wheels in late Maikop (Novosvobodnaya) kurgan at **Starokorsunskaya** k.2 on the Kuban — possibly among the oldest wheels in Europe; Evdik k.4 gr.23 cauldron with schematic yoke-wheel image deep in Kalmyk steppes. Anthony: Maikop-Novosvobodnaya **may have introduced wagons** into the Eurasian steppes (alternative path: from Europe ~3500–3300 BCE).

**Horses:** Bones at settlements (Galugai 3%), graves (Inozemstvo jaw), art (19-horse frieze Klady k.28; engraved horse on Maikop silver cup resembling Przewalski). Chernykh suggested riding began in the Maikop period — Anthony **reports** Chernykh’s suggestion; Anthony’s own riding-origin model prioritizes Pontic-Caspian west of Botai. Horses appear regularly in Maikop and ETC Caucasus ~3500–3000 BCE.

**Southern tech on local base:** Continuity from Svobodnoe in settlement locations, lithics, some ceramics; wheel-made pottery, high-nickel arsenical bronzes, sleeved axes, cylinder seals from the south. Archaic cylinder seal at Krasnogvardeiskoe anchors Uruk-period dating.

**Linguistic:** Possible Caucasian speech for late Novosvobodnaya; contact with Repin and late [[khvalynsk-culture|Khvalynsk]] PIE-dialect speakers on the steppe frontier as the setting for archaic Caucasian–PIE loans. If Proto-Kartvelian lay south of the range with ETC/Kura-Araxes (~3500–2200 BCE), relations with PIE would be **indirect through Maikop** (Anthony). Cultural frontiers (pots, loom weights, metals, ornaments) stayed sharp despite interaction.

## Distribution and Chronology

| Horizon | cal BCE (Anthony 2007) |
|---|---|
| Pre-Maikop Svobodnoe / Meshoko farmers | ~4400–4300 |
| Early Maikop | ~3700–3400 |
| Late Maikop / Novosvobodnaya | ~3400–3000 |
| Collapse with Late Uruk exchange | ~3100 |

Geography: North Caucasus piedmont and Kuban; steppe contacts densest on lower Don (**Konstantinovka** fortified settlement — Anthony allows a small Maikop colony: ~90% local cord-impressed ceramics, 10% red-burnished Maikop wares, loom weights, copper chisels). Gold/turquoise/carnelian stayed in the Caucasus; little southern wealth shared with steppe clans — steppe imported **patterns** (sleeved axes, tanged daggers) mostly made locally.

## Lifeways (as evidenced)

Agricultural villages in the pre-Maikop horizon (Svobodnoe sheep:goat 5:1 suggests wool); Maikop itself known primarily from elite kurgans and settlement finds with metallurgy and exotic trade. Possible Maikop demands on the steppes (Anthony’s list): Sherratt’s **Cannabis** narcotics hypothesis; **wool** (weavers at Konstantinovka); **horses** (horse bones widespread in Transcaucasia after ~3300 BCE ETC; Mezhlumian: horses at 10/12 later fourth-millennium Armenian sites; bit wear claimed at Mokhrablur).

## Relations and Successions

- `developed_from` local Caucasian (Svobodnoe) origins + southern Uruk-period technology.
- `associated_with` early Sredni Stog / Suvorovo–Novodanilovka fringe (~4000 BCE): Svobodnoe pot in Novodanilovka grave; Balkan copper ring at Svobodnoe; green serpentine Caucasian axes in early Sredni Stog.
- `associated_with` [[yamnaya-culture]] genesis: Konstantinovka/Evdik contact may have stimulated Repin → early Yamnaya including wagons; steppe emulation of Maikop metal types densest 3350–3100 BCE.
- `preceded_by` / filter for southern innovations into steppes ~3700–3500 BCE.
- Ends when Late Uruk long-distance exchange collapsed ~3100 BCE (southern valuables dried up; ETC peoples burned Arslantepe and Hacinebi; Sé Girdan abandoned).

## Identity Cautions

- Culture ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ people. Maikop ≠ PIE; Anthony keeps Caucasian vs steppe speech distinct at a sharp material frontier.
- Reject equating Maikop with Gimbutas’s broad Kurgan culture.
- Wagon introduction via Maikop is **one of two** paths Anthony allows (vs European Bronocice/Baden path).
- Chernykh’s Maikop riding origin is reported, not Anthony’s preferred model.
- Wealth of type-site and Klady is elite mortuary display, not a census of Maikop society.

## Historiography

1897 type-site discovery; Soviet and Russian Caucasian archaeology; Chernykh metallurgy; radiocarbon redating (Klady, Galugai) that pulled Maikop back to Uruk time. Anthony 2007 is the vault’s primary synthesis for Maikop’s steppe-filter role.
