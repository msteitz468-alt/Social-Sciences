# Keros-Syros Culture

## Definition and Construct History

**Keros-Syros** is Renfrew's (1972) name for the middle, floruit phase of Early Cycladic material culture — the classic “Metal Age” / EB2 package. Defined by recurrent cemetery associations (close-proximity analysis with Sterud) and settlement finds; not by a pure geographic Pelos-vs-Syros split (attributed, *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|Emergence]]* Chs 9, 11).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material:** sauceboats; folded-arm marble figurines; Syros-type graves; frying-pan vessels (sun/sea/sex motifs in Renfrew's symbolic discussion); increased metallurgy; fortified sites (e.g. Kastri); late Kastri/Amorgos groups. Settlements include Chalandriani, Dhaskalio, Mt Kynthos, Naxos sites (attributed).

**Linguistic / genetic:** not claimed.

## Distribution and Chronology

Northern and central Cyclades cemeteries especially; stratigraphically between Phylakopi O and Phylakopi I at Melos where evidence allows. Later than [[grotta-pelos-culture]] on Renfrew's reading. Absolute dates within his third-millennium calibrated framework (as of 1972).

## Lifeways

Markedly richer graves and metal use than Grotta-Pelos; craft production (figurines, metal); fortification and dagger complex feed Renfrew's social-ranking and warfare discussions ([[chiefdom]] as provisional EBA label he later softens in 2010 preface). Longboat imagery later compared to Neolithic rock art at Strophilas (2010 retrospect).

## Relations and Successions

[[grotta-pelos-culture]] → Keros-Syros → [[phylakopi-i-culture]] / later groups. Inter-Aegean exchange and “international spirit” of EB2 (sauceboat world) link Cyclades to Crete and mainland without requiring Near Eastern transplant of civilisation ([[exchange]], [[ex-oriente-lux]] rejected as prime mover).

## Identity Cautions

Etic culture construct. Folded-arm figurines dominate modern art markets; Renfrew treats them as culture diagnostics and symbolic/projective evidence, not ethnic labels. Kastri-group Anatolian affinities remain among residual contact problems he lists as not fully explained by trade alone (Ch. 21).

## Historiography

Core of Renfrew 1972 Cycladic synthesis; 2010 preface cites Doumas, Rambach, Broodbank, Barber, Karantzali, and new Dhaskalio/Kavos work as major post-1972 densification.
