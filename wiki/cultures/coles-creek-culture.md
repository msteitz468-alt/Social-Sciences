# Coles Creek Culture

Late Woodland archaeological construct of the **lower Mississippi River valley**, treated in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] Ch. 5 as a key case of **platform-mound hierarchical societies** that set the stage for Mississippian chiefdoms — including examples with **heavy foraging (acorns) for centuries after chiefdoms appeared**, challenging maize-as-prerequisite models.

## Definition and Construct History

Etic culture-historical label for lower Mississippi Late Woodland platform-mound communities. **Not** identical to later classic Mississippian material culture, though ancestral in regional sequence.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

Platform mounds at major settlements; multi-stage mounds supporting buildings; Lake George: mound building before 800 CE, increasing thereafter; largest Mound C with burials/charnel — foreshadowing Mississippian ancestor-mound complexes (as of Milner 2021).

## Distribution and Chronology

Lower Mississippi River valley in the Late Woodland / late first millennium CE, transitioning into Mississippian sequences (as of Milner 2021). Related Late Woodland centers such as [[toltec-mounds]] in Arkansas illustrate plaza-and-platform plans of the same horizon.

## Lifeways

Hierarchical organization with platform mounds; continued reliance on wild plants including acorns alongside or instead of heavy maize for centuries (Milner) — load-bearing case that **agriculture is not necessary** for institutionalized inequality in the Eastern Woodlands.
