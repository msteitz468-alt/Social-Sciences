# Phylakopi I Culture

## Definition and Construct History

**Phylakopi I** names Renfrew's (1972) third Early Cycladic culture, after the **First City** of Phylakopi on Melos — the only well-stratified third-millennium settlement sequence in the Cyclades available to him (Mackenzie Daybooks + published sequence: pre-City / Phylakopi O → First City → Second City). He proposes “Phylakopi I culture” for First City finds and “Phylakopi II” for the fortified Second City (attributed, *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|Emergence]]* Chs 9, 12).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material:** town plan of First City; duck vase; conical pyxis; Melian bowl; kernos; rock-cut tombs; ceramic links to Anatolian EB3 and Middle Helladic. Local derivation stressed over wholesale immigration (attributed).

**Linguistic / genetic:** not claimed.

## Distribution and Chronology

Southern Cyclades focus (type site Melos). Grotta-Pelos underlies Phylakopi I at Phylakopi; Phylakopi I develops without interruption into MBA Phylakopi II (matt-painted, Minyan, Kamares; first fortification of Phylakopi = MM Ia / earlier MH synchronism) (attributed). Closes Renfrew's third-millennium Cycladic independence narrative before full Minoan–Mycenaean integration.

## Lifeways

Nucleated town vs earlier dispersed cemetery-settlement pattern; craft and exchange embedded in Melian landscape (obsidian history of the island is longer than Phylakopi I alone).

## Relations and Successions

End of Renfrew's EC sequence before second-millennium palace world ([[minoan-civilization]], [[mycenaean-civilization]]). Renfrew's own later Phylakopi excavations (1974–76) and *An Island Polity* (1982) post-date and densify this type site.

## Identity Cautions

Culture name = settlement phase name; not an ethnos. “End of Cycladic independence” is Renfrew's culture-historical framing of MBA integration, not a political narrative of conquest alone.

## Historiography

1972 construct from British School Phylakopi publications + Renfrew's synthesis; later Melos survey/excavation revises population and polity models (Cherry; Renfrew & Wagstaff 1982) — flagged in Renfrew's 2010 preface as part of the settlement-studies revolution that outdated Ch. 14 estimates.
