# Classic Maya

## Definition and Construct History

**Classic Maya** labels the lowland (and related highland) Maya city-state / kingdom tradition of Mesoamerica at its inscriptional and architectural climax, continuous with deeper Preclassic roots. As synthesized by [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston, *The Maya* (9th ed. 2015)]], the **Late Preclassic** (~400/300 BC–AD 250) is already a “proto-Classic”: nearly the full cultural package is present except vaulted stone architecture and the full elaboration of calendar and script on stone monuments. The **Classic** itself is operationally the span when lowland Maya used the [[long-count|Long Count]] on monuments (roughly AD 250–900 in conventional teaching chronologies).

Earlier textbook scaffolding ([[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007|Wenke & Olszewski 2007]]) already rejected single-empire models and stressed independent states with shared tradition; Coe & Houston densify that picture with post-decipherment dynastic history (Martin & Grube “Great Game”), Preclassic Mirador urbanism, and a multi-causal Terminal Classic.

**Construct status:** conventional etic archaeological culture + historical civilization reconstructed from material culture **and** deciphered texts — still **not** a single ethnos, language, or genetic population.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

### Material (as of Coe & Houston 2015)

Temple-pyramids, palaces, ballcourts, stelae and altars, corbel vaults (Classic), stucco masks, fine polychrome ceramics, jade and shell regalia, reservoirs and wetland engineering. Urban form is typically **“colloidal”** — density declining from ceremonial cores to house-mound peripheries — not a Teotihuacan-style grid ([[tikal]], Ceibal, Dzibilchaltun maps).

### Linguistic

Living Mayan languages form a family with good fit between language distribution and archaeological Maya area (except Wastek). Classic hieroglyphic language = **Ch'olti'an**. “Yukatekan” (languages) ≠ “Yucatecan” (area). See [[maya-hieroglyphic-writing]].

### Genetic

Not a definitional criterion. Living Mayan-speaking populations continue after Classic political collapse — continuity of people/language ≠ continuity of Classic royal order.

## Distribution and Chronology

**Areas (C&H):** Northern (Yucatan plains), Central (Peten and adjacent lowlands), Southern (highlands and Pacific piedmont) — ecological and cultural sub-regions, not empires.

| Span | Sketch (attributed C&H 2015) |
|---|---|
| Early–Middle Preclassic | Village farming; Soconusco ceramics; Olmec interaction; Ceibal Real-Xe caches; Mamom / Nakbe public architecture |
| Late Preclassic | [[el-mirador]] capital scale; [[san-bartolo]] murals/writing; [[kaminaljuyu]] highland peak; “proto-Classic” |
| Early Classic | Long Count monuments; [[teotihuacan]] contacts (esp. Tikal AD 378 *entrada* — contested meaning); Tzakol ceramics |
| Late Classic | Dynastic apogee: [[tikal]], [[calakmul]], [[palenque]], [[copan]], Usumacinta courts, [[bonampak]] murals |
| Terminal Classic c. 800–925 | Southern “Great Collapse”; northern Puuc / [[chichen-itza]] florescence — see [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]] |
| Postclassic | [[mayapan]], coastal trade, highland K'iche'/Kaqchikel states; Spanish Conquest 16th–17th c. (Tayasal 1697) |

**Agriculture:** milpa maize–beans–squash; bajo margins; terracing; raised fields in places; chultuns in the north; nixtamal processing as Mesoamerican dietary foundation.

## Lifeways

**Politics:** Neither Morley’s “Old Empire” nor pure equal city-states. Emblem Glyphs title [[kuhul-ajaw|k'uhul ajaw]] of named kingdoms; Martin & Grube hierarchical model — overkingship, *y-ajaw* vassalage, foreign-witnessed accessions; [[tikal]]–[[calakmul]] rivalry as organizing spine of Late Classic history.

**Ideology:** Layered cosmos; Maize God and Hero Twins mythic cycle; bloodletting; Period-Ending rites; kings as holy mediators. Ballgame ([[mesoamerican-ballgame]]) as sport, ritual, and political theatre.

**Contact-period baseline (ethnohistory, Yucatan-heavy):** Ranked class society (*almehen* nobility; *halach winik* / *batab*); milpa and cacao; long-distance coastal trade (Putun/Chontal); obsessive warfare. C&H treat missionary sources as essential for interpreting Postclassic and even Classic remains — **interpretive bridge**, not identity of Classic = Conquest Maya.

## Relations and Successions

- **associated_with:** [[olmec-culture]] (Formative models, jade, early monuments — degree of “mother culture” debated); [[teotihuacan]] (Early Classic military-iconographic horizon).
- **preceded_by / developed_from:** Preclassic Mirador Basin and highland centers.
- **succeeded_by:** Postclassic kingdoms and continuous Mayan-speaking societies into the present (~5 million people as of C&H 2015 demography sketch) — see [[zinacantan]] as one ethnographic window.

## Identity Cautions

1. **“Maya” ≠ one empire.** Series of kingdoms with shared script, calendar, and elite culture.
2. **Four-way non-identity.** Archaeological Classic Maya ≠ single ethnos ≠ genes ≠ modern pan-Maya identity (latter is partly modern political formation — C&H note pan-Maya identity stronger now than in the past).
3. **Collapse ≠ disappearance of people.** Southern royal centers fail; languages and peoples continue.
4. **Ethnographic analogy** from Zinacantan or Chan Kom to Classic courts is hypothesis-generating only when flagged.

## Historiography

- **Morley “Old Empire” / “New Empire”** — rejected.
- **Thompson** non-historical script era — overturned by phonetic decipherment (Knorosov) and historical hypothesis (Proskouriakoff).
- **Coe** *The Maya* multi-edition series (1966→2015 with [[houston-stephen|Houston]]) as barometer of the field; 9th ed. integrates Martin–Grube political history, Preclassic revolutions, paleoclimate droughts, and remote-sensing settlement hints (as of Coe & Houston 2015).
- Prior wiki layer: Wenke & Olszewski textbook; Trigger city-state notes — retained as secondary scaffolding.

### Coe & Houston 2015 densification (this ingest)

## Densification from Sharer & Traxler 2006

Comprehensive reference densification from [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler, *The Ancient Maya*, 6th ed. 2006]] (Stanford). Claims below are **attributed** to that mid-2000s synthesis; they densify rather than replace Coe & Houston 2015.

### Setting and identity (S&T 2006)

- **Area:** ~324,000 km² covering Yucatan, Guatemala, Belize, and western Honduras/El Salvador; three geographic zones — Pacific coastal plain, highlands, lowlands — with high internal ecological diversity. Maya civilization is placed inside nuclear [[mesoamerica]], interacting with [[olmec-culture]], Oaxaca, [[teotihuacan]], Tula, and southeastern neighbors — not an Old World transplant (S&T reject Atlantis/alien/colony myths; cite Stephens 1841 as anticipating indigenous builders).
- **Languages:** several million speakers of ~28 [[mayan-languages|Mayan languages]] as of their writing; languages and family/community lifeways survived Conquest even as elite institutions, codices, and script use were crushed (disease as greatest accidental tragedy; deliberate colonial destruction of ritual and books).
- **Living continuity:** ethnography of contemporary communities is treated as a reconstruction source *where* traditional agriculture, organization, and belief vestiges persist — always as analogy, not identity with Classic courts. Looting for the antiquities market is framed as the greatest destroyer of archaeological context.

### Periodization and political arc (S&T 2006)

| Span (approx., as of S&T 2006) | Sketch |
|---|---|
| Archaic–Early Preclassic | Coastal villages; early pottery (Barra–Locona–Ocos); maize expansion; multiethnic lowland colonization |
| Middle Preclassic | Markers of complex society; [[nakbe]] monumental core; Pacific polities; [[kaminaljuyu]] irrigation |
| Late Preclassic | First lowland states at [[el-mirador]] scale; southern Maya writing/stelae; Terminal Preclassic decline |
| Early Classic | Expansion of states; [[tikal]] dynasty; 378 *entrada* horizon with [[teotihuacan]]; [[copan]]/[[quirigua]] founding; [[calakmul]]–[[caracol]] pressure; 562 Tikal defeat |
| Late Classic | “Great Game” dynastic wars; Calakmul ascendancy then Tikal resurgence (695); Petexbatun ([[dos-pilas]]); Usumacinta ([[yaxchilan]], [[piedras-negras]]); western ([[palenque]], [[tonina]]); SE (Copan–Quirigua 738) |
| Terminal Classic | Heartland political demise ca. 800–900; northern florescence ([[uxmal]], [[chichen-itza]]); multi-causal process — see [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]] |
| Postclassic | [[mayapan]]; east-coast trade; Peten lakes ([[tayasal]]); highland K’iche/Kaqchikel; Spanish Conquest to 1697 |

**Politics:** never a single empire. Divine kings ([[kuhul-ajaw]]) titled by [[emblem-glyph|Emblem Glyphs]]; hierarchical overlordship and *yajaw* vassalage; peer-polity competition; Marcus-style cycles of consolidation vs fragmentation. Population tables (Rice & Culbert via S&T) imply dual peaks (Late Preclassic and larger Late Classic) with hinterland densities that, combined with urban figures, yield author estimates of “tens of millions” in the central lowlands — **attributed estimate**, contested and pre-LIDAR.

### Economy and society (S&T 2006)

- **Subsistence:** simple swidden-only models rejected; terracing, raised fields, gardens, irrigation, and [[milpa]] in combination; water management (reservoirs, *chultuns*, *cenotes*) critical in many lowland zones.
- **Trade cycles:** prestige and utilitarian long-distance goods; control of overland/river then Terminal Classic seaborne bulk routes (salt, cotton, cacao) reshaped power toward the north.
- **Households and settlement:** multi-building compounds (*naah* / *naahlil*); [[settlement-pattern-archaeology]] as foundation; elite bias of texts; nonelite majority poorly represented in Classic inscriptions.
- **Ideology:** layered cosmos; bloodletting and royal Period-Ending rites; ballgame ([[mesoamerican-ballgame]]); calendar package ([[long-count]], [[calendar-round]], [[tzolkin]], [[haab]]) and [[maya-hieroglyphic-writing]].
