# Clovis Culture

North American late Pleistocene archaeological construct defined by **fluted lanceolate projectile points** and associated toolkits, long treated as the continent’s first culture under “Clovis First.” Account follows [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]] (pre-aDNA; post-1997 Monte Verde panel).

## Definition and Construct History

Larger fluted points with shorter flutes and ground bases, associated with mammoth at Dent and **Blackwater Draw** near Clovis, New Mexico, defined Clovis; Blackwater Draw showed Folsom/bison above Clovis/mammoth. By the 1970s radiocarbon dates indicated no Clovis older than **11,500 BC**, and with no accepted earlier settlement Clovis was cast as first Americans via Beringia and the ice-free corridor. Paul Martin fused Clovis First with **overkill** of megafauna. Mithen reports the 1997 Monte Verde expert panel as ending Clovis First as viable while leaving Clovis as a real, widespread toolkit horizon. Mithen’s own synthesis: people already continent-wide by ~11,500 BC; Clovis points invented (likely in eastern forests) and spread rapidly as an **idea**, making people archaeologically “visible.”

**Etic construct** named from a New Mexico town — not an ethnonym.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as of Mithen 2003):**

- **Diagnostics:** Clovis fluted points (larger; flute to mid-point; ground bases); caches with red ochre (Anzick: 100+ tools including fine points with two young adults).
- **Faunal associations:** mammoth kill/scavenge sites (Naco eight points with one mammoth; Lehner Ranch twelve points with eight mammoths); many non-southwest sites show small game, turtles, plants, fish — **opportunistic foragers**, not exclusive big-game specialists.
- **Symbolic:** regular burials nearly unknown; Fishbone Cave cedar-bark shroud; exotic colored raw materials may exceed pure utility.
- **End:** last points ~10,000 BC as mammoth and other megafauna disappear and cultures diversify.

**Linguistic:** Greenberg’s equating proto-Amerind ~11,500 BC with Clovis is a **contested position** reported by Mithen — not a culture=language identity.

**Genetic:** Greenberg–Turner–Zegura three-migration package (as of mid-1980s / Mithen 2003) is classical genetics/dentition — superseded frameworks exist; do not treat as current aDNA consensus.

## Distribution and Chronology

- **Span:** none older than ~11,500 BC in 1970s–2003 accepted Clovis dates; ends ~10,000 BC.
- **Geography:** “all-pervasive” across North America with minor variants in Mithen’s contrast to more diverse South America; related fishtail points in South America lack Clovis flute.
- **Relative:** Folsom stratigraphically later at type sequence; Denali tools in Alaska dated no older than 11,000 BC — after Clovis threshold.

## Lifeways (Settlement and Subsistence)

Mithen stresses **opportunism**: berries (Shawnee-Minisink), caribou (Debert), rabbits/geese/turkeys (Lubbock Lake), trout/eggs/clams (Old Humboldt); salt-lick mega-bone sites without Clovis points. Gary Haynes: some mammoth bonebeds resemble drought die-offs with limited scavenging. Continent-wide presence before Clovis visibility implies thin, leap-frogging communities with wide social links (Mithen reconstruction).

## Relations and Successions

- **Debate hub:** [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]] — Clovis First vs pre-Clovis coastal routes; [[monte-verde|Monte Verde]] ~12,500 BC.
- **Megafauna:** [[megafaunal-extinctions]] — overkill vs climate vs multi-causal.
- **Succeeded by** diversified Holocene hunter-gatherer traditions; Folsom bison hunters in Plains sequence.
- ****Not equal to** First Americans as a genetic population label in later aDNA literature (as of post-2010s aDNA; Mithen 2003 layer pre-aDNA).

## Significance

Clovis remains the most archaeologically **visible** late Pleistocene toolkit in North America and the historical baseline every peopling model must explain — whether as first culture, spreading fashion among existing peoples, or both.

## Identity Cautions

- **Archaeological culture ≠ people ≠ language ≠ genes.**
- Fluted-point makers need not be a single migration or ethnicity.
- “Clovis First” is a **peopling model**, not a synonym for the material culture.
- Pre-aDNA genetic and three-wave linguistic equations are period positions (as of Mithen 2003).

## Historiography

Named from Blackwater Draw/Clovis, NM; 1950s San Pedro valley mammoth associations; 1970s Clovis First orthodoxy; Martin overkill; decades of pre-Clovis claims (Meadowcroft, Bluefish, Pedra Furada, Calico) with uneven acceptance; 1997 Monte Verde panel as watershed in Mithen’s narrative. Post-2003 aDNA and new sites further revise peopling — densify from later sources without silently overwriting this 2003 horizon.

## Fagan (1987/2004) — complementary account

*[[fagan-great-journey-1987|Fagan]] narrates the same construct, largely
consistent with Mithen (attributed throughout; radiocarbon dates mostly
uncalibrated in Fagan — treat as approximate).*

- **Diagnostic technology.** The [[fluted-point|Clovis point]] made by a careful
  biface-reduction sequence ending in the channel flute struck from a prepared
  base; knappers favored fine-grained exotic stone (Alibates dolomite up to
  ~300 km distant, Knife River chalcedony, Yellowstone obsidian). Toolkits are
  conserved and portable — at Murray Springs only ~36 finished tools, a ~6:10,000
  tool-to-flake ratio (attributed, Fagan).
- **Chronology.** Appeared on the Great Plains ~11,500 BP, flourished only ~500
  years to ~11,000 BP; twenty-one Lehner/Murray Springs dates average
  11,000 ± 200 BP (attributed, Fagan).
- **Hunting disputes.** Single mass kills (Saunders on the 13-mammoth Lehner
  family) vs. solitary animals over years (Vance Haynes) vs. many "kill sites"
  being unbutchered natural deaths (James Judge); Frison's replica-spear
  experiments on dead African elephants imply cooperative atlatl hunting.
- **Origins.** Clovis points are absent in Alaska/Siberia — read by Fagan as a
  distinctively American (perhaps eastern) invention, not an arctic import; he
  leaves open indigenous Plains development vs. descent from arctic hunters moving
  south as the [[ice-free-corridor]] opened.
- **Succession.** On the Plains, Clovis is succeeded by [[folsom-site|Folsom]],
  Scottsbluff, Hell Gap and other complexes, "all apparently descended from the
  fluted Clovis point" (see [[paleoindian]]).
- **Extinctions.** The Clovis-megafauna coincidence anchors the
  [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]] — see also [[megafaunal-extinctions]].
- Source: [[fagan-great-journey-1987]] ch. 9.

## Meltzer 2009 densification

*(Attributed to [[meltzer-david]], *[[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|First Peoples in a New World]]* 2009, esp. Ch. 8. Appends a parallel dated layer — do not delete Mithen/Fagan sections above. **Dating convention:** Meltzer refuses cal BP for late Pleistocene because calibration is multi-valued; all ages below are **radiocarbon years BP** as in the book [C-1-030–031].)*

### Discovery and naming

Edgar B. Howard staked the Clovis/[[blackwater-draw|Blackwater Draw]] pond deposits after 1932 gravel-crew bone finds; Aug. 1933 International Geological Congress visitors agreed association of larger, less refined fluted points with extinct elephant and bison. Jesse Figgins’s Dent, Colorado announcement came two weeks too late for naming rights — the field speaks of Clovis, not Dent (C-6-001–003). Same summer, W. A. Johnston floated Bering land bridge + Mackenzie corridor; Howard and Antevs linked 20–15 ka dry Bering + eastern Rockies corridor to estimated Clovis age (pre-radiocarbon ages) (C-6-004). Relative stratigraphy: Clovis points continent-wide and below Folsom — older; absolute ages later (C-3-041).

### Chronology (radiocarbon BP as Meltzer)

- Classic framing: Clovis almost **11,500 BP**; Folsom at least ~**10,800 BP** by early 1960s (C-1-022).
- Oldest Clovis on Plains/SW ~**11,570 BP** (Aubrey, TX) to ~**10,800 BP**; earliest Clovis follows opening of viable [[ice-free-corridor]] (C-6-027).
- Type-site initial occupation ~**11,300 BP** spring-fed pond setting (C-6-056).
- Eastern reliable Clovis/Clovis-like dates fewer; oldest Shawnee-Minisink (PA) **10,940 BP**; many East 10,600–10,200 BP contemporaneous with Plains Folsom (C-6-027).
- Waters & Stafford: nearly a dozen sites Montana (Anzick) to Arizona (Lehner) to PA date ~**10,900 BP ±~century** — essentially contemporaneous within ¹⁴C bounds. Rapid radiation **≤~500 radiocarbon years**; may stretch to ~**1,000 calendar years** under early [[younger-dryas]] radiocarbon plateaus — still among fastest prehistoric expansions (C-6-027). Net expansion ~10–20 km/year (C-5-060).
- Haynes 1964 cluster 11,500–11,000 BP underpinned corridor-timed Clovis-first (C-3-043) — now a position on [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]], not identity with the material culture.

### Geographic breadth and footprint

Unlike Folsom (mostly plains), Clovis sites span Pacific NW conifer forests, desert SW, western plains, SE mixed forests, near-tundra Northeast — no subsequent North American occupation so widespread amid deglacial upheaval (C-6-005). Anderson et al.: **>13,000** Clovis points from ~**1,500** locations (Texas ~550); great majority **isolates** without other tools/bones (Brose: groups “ate nothing and lived alone”). Density high E US (Tennessee, Ohio, Cumberland valleys); sparse Great Basin, Columbia/Colorado plateaus, northern plains/Rockies, uppermost/lowermost Mississippi Valley (C-6-025). Staging-area vs quarry-magnet vs sampling-bias readings of SE concentrations (Anderson vs Meltzer); lower Mississippi scarcity may be burial under Holocene floods (C-6-026). Essential pattern: **broad not deep** — highly mobile, low density, **leapfrogging** not wave settlement (C-6-026).

### Toolkit and fluting

**Diagnostics.** Lanceolate fluted points; base and partial sides ground smooth; flutes from base partially up one or both faces. Classic Clovis: parallel sides, flutes usually <½ face length, relatively long/thick; Texas freshly minted ~slightly over 10 cm × 3 cm × 7.5 mm; base size often uniform (points fitted to hafts) (C-6-010). Gainey-type eastern Clovis-like variant (C-6-091). Continuity of fluting into Folsom (C-6-094).

**Origin of fluting.** No Siberian Clovis fluted points (Howard 1935 USSR trip); ~50 Alaskan fluted points (mostly far north, surface/younger associations); ~60 former corridor; few secure ¹⁴C, those that exist **<10,500 BP** — too young for southbound Clovis colonizers; stylistically more like later fluted types. Meltzer: fluting invented **south of Laurentide/Cordilleran ice**, with northward “migratory backwash” (C-6-006). Origin candidates: Plains/SW (oldest known fluted) vs East (Mason diversity-as-age; more and more varied points) — eastern points clearly predating western not yet found as of 2009. [[adovasio-james|Adovasio]]: unfluted Miller lanceolate from [[meadowcroft|Meadowcroft]] Stratum IIa as fine fluted-point precursor (C-6-007).

**Why flute?** Failure rates 10–20% Clovis (nearly 40% Folsom); fluting thins/weakens; bloodletting and performance ideas weak; Frison & Bradley: may not enhance performance; Meltzer leans style/symbolic prowess/pre-hunt ritual; red ocher staining on some points (C-6-008–009).

**Generalized toolkit.** Large bifaces (transport blanks); end/side scrapers; gravers/spurs; spokeshaves/notches; knives/flake tools; regional: long blades/polyhedral cores on southern plains/SE (rich at Gault); Ellis et al. eastern Great Lakes vs New England frequency differences (C-6-011). Bone/ivory rods ~30 cm (dozen+ western sites; underwater Florida); Murray Springs mammoth-bone shaft wrench; functions debated (foreshafts, pressure tools, etc.) (C-6-012). Perishables scarce; Adovasio: Clovis-age twined bags/mats/baskets in Great Basin dry caves; nets expected but not yet found — net hunting low-expertise, multi-age/gender (C-6-013). Limited “art” (scored rods; ≤ few dozen engraved stones mostly Gault; no European-scale portable/cave art) (C-6-014).

**Stone economy.** Almost exclusive high-quality chert/jasper/chalcedony/obsidian; direct bedrock quarry procurement (some bifaces ≥20 cm, >600 g); Alibates → Drake cache NE Colorado **585 km**; 300–400 km common on western plains and open NE parkland/tundra; closed richer forests often local (~50 km) with occasional exotics (Gainey cores from Upper Mercer, OH, ~380 km) (C-6-015–017). Quarry-workshop debitage (Thunderbird >10,000 pieces/level); heat treatment 165–300°C (C-6-018). Far-from-quarry conservation/recycling to ~4–5 cm nubs (C-6-019).

### Caches, dwellings, mobility

**≥20 Clovis caches**, mostly West — bifaces/blades for transport; often isolated; stone from hundreds of km distant. Richey-Roberts (East Wenatchee): ~40 artifacts, points to 21×6 cm, red ocher (C-6-020). **Anzick** (Montana): >100 stone artifacts + rods with ocher-covered child — grave goods, not utilitarian cache (C-6-021). Collins: return-and-resupply knowledge; Meltzer counters late-Paleoindian rarity of caches + Folsom long-distance stone without caches → landscape-learning **outbound depots** for explorers who could not predict next outcrop (C-6-022–023).

Dwellings temporary (postmolds); Thunderbird oval ~9×12 m; Upper Midwest/NE concentrations ~4–6 m diameter as family households (Nobles Pond semicircle); single-use sites vs revisits at permanent resources (Gault, Thunderbird); no Clovis “rendezvous” yet found (C-6-024).

### Subsistence breadth (not exclusive megafauna specialists)

Traditional specialized-hunter explanation for continental speed (“a mammoth was a mammoth”) summarized then undercut (C-6-028–029). Kill-site search bias after Folsom cast Paleoindians as specialized top predators (C-3-040). Secure megafauna associations: Lehner 13 mammoths serial ~10,950 BP; Murray Springs mammoth + 11 bison ~10,900 BP (impact fractures more on bison points); Colby meat caches; partial butchery at water settings recurring; unsuccessful Naco/Escapule; ambiguous Dent/Domebo/Miami; eastern mastodon rare (Kimmswick exception) (C-6-056–064). Opportunistic scavenging thesis (Judge) and Vance Haynes disputed drought overlay recorded (C-6-058). Cannon & Meltzer: **eastern diets broader**, less large-mammal-dominated (C-6-065). Plants (Shawnee-Minisink flotation), turtles, fish/shellfish contexts, SE deer/elk expectation, NE caribou (Vail), Far West water-edge (Buhl isotopes) (C-6-070–075). Elephant-hunting risk and Hadza failure rates undercut exclusive specialization (C-6-066–068). No massive Clovis environmental destruction (C-6-096). Overkill implications: [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]] — Grayson & Meltzer 76→14 secure kills, **only mammoth + mastodon**.

### Relation to pre-Clovis and peopling models

Clovis is an **archaeological culture** (etic construct), not automatically the first Americans. [[monte-verde|Monte Verde]] ~12,500 BP non-Clovis character and distance force earlier entry and coastal-route rethink; Clovis after 11,500 BP may be separate radiation/pulse or technology within a peopled landscape — Meltzer **rejects** simple “diffusion of fluting among pre-Clovis population” as full account while accepting pre-Clovis reality (C-5-047–048; [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]] Meltzer 2009 section). Pre-Clovis complexes lack coherent inter-American links; MV ≠ Clovis (C-5-044). Minimum two Pleistocene migrations: Paleoindian + Paleoarctic (C-5-045). Solutrean Atlantic origin rejected (time gap, missing inventory, convergence) (C-5-008–014). Nenana broadly similar technology but no fluted points; re-dated contemporaneous ~11,300 BP not clear precursor (C-5-019–020). Clovis points as continent-wide style zone / social glue for bold colonizers (C-5-084).

### Identity Cautions (Meltzer layer)

- Fluted-point horizon ≠ single ethnos, language family, or genetic population.
- “Clovis First” is a peopling **model**, historically contingent on kill-site search and Haynes-era ¹⁴C cluster.
- Genetics as of Meltzer 2009 (mtDNA A–D/X, NRY C/Q, bottlenecks) constrain Asian origin, not Clovis-as-founders identity — later aDNA (Anzick etc.) lives on peopling debate Reich layer.
- South America: strict Clovis fluted points only to Panama; “pre-Clovis” is a misnomer there (C-1-008).

### Historiography (Meltzer layer)

Howard/Dent naming race; corridor proposal 1933; kill-site bias stereotyping big-game specialists; Haynes 1964 Clovis-first; Martin overkill fusion; pre-Clovis credibility decay and Monte Verde 1997 watershed; Solutrean fringe revival post-Kennewick; landscape-learning and bold-explorer reframing of caches and radiation.
