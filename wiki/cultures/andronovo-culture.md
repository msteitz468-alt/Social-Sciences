# Andronovo Horizon

## Definition and Construct History

The **Andronovo horizon** is the principal Late Bronze Age archaeological complex of the steppes **east of the Urals** to the Altai/Tien Shan and south toward the Amu Darya, dated by [[anthony-david-w|David Anthony]] (*[[anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007|The Horse, the Wheel, and Language]]*, 2007) roughly **1800–1200 BCE** (Alakul from ~1900–1800). Two principal subgroups — **Alakul** and **Federovo** — often co-occur in the same sites, houses, and cemeteries and are **not easily separate ethnic groups** (Alakul slightly earlier; Federovo more cremation and complex stone constructions). Anthony treats Andronovo (with Srubnaya west of the Urals) as growing from the **Potapovka–[[sintashta-culture|Sintashta]] complex**, continuing Sintashta/Petrovka customs but without chariot burials. “Horizon” rather than single culture emphasizes shared LBA package over ethnic unity.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Metal and mining:** Tin-bronzes from Zeravshan and probably upper Irtysh; major copper mines at **Uspenskyi** (S of Karaganda) and **Dzhezkazgan** (one mine 1,500×500×15 m); Karaganda-region production estimated **30–50,000 metric tons** smelted copper in the Bronze Age. **Karnab** (Uzbekistan) and **Mushiston** (Tajikistan) tin mines (Boroffka & Parzinger 1997–99): Andronovo pottery, dates 1900–1300 BCE (oldest Karnab ~1900–1750); Sarazm probably abandoned when Andronovo mining began ~2000 BCE. Tin was the most important Near Eastern BA trade commodity (Mari: tin worth 10× silver).

**Petrovka precursor / eastern offshoot of Sintashta (~1900–1750 BCE):** Ceramics stratified above Sintashta at Ust’ye etc.; oldest sites on Ishim in N Kazakhstan (Petrovka II); spread south into central Kazakhstan and to **Tugai** on the Zeravshan >1,200 km — intentional migration for trade/loot/glory, not accidental herding drift. Most Petrovka metal **tin-bronze** (unlike Sintashta arsenical bronze); chariot burials gradually cease. Tugai: copper-smelting ovens, cloth-form shell-tempered Petrovka pottery (two talc-tempered pots from Ural steppes), cattle/sheep/goat but no horse eating; Sarazm IV cups. **Zardcha-Khalifa** grave near Sarazm: male with ram, Namazga VI pots, BMAC bronzes, gold trumpet earrings, four Sintashta-type disk cheekpieces, **two bronze bar bits (oldest known metal bits)**, horse-figure pin — probable northern immigrant horse dealer with chariot-team equipment.

**Southern imports north:** Wheel-made Namazga VI pottery **12%** of sherds on two house floors at Pavlovka Federovo settlement in N Kazakhstan — **2,000 km** north of Bactria.

**Linguistic:** Indo-Iranian and Proto-Iranian probably spread with Andronovo material culture (Anthony); Old Indic evolved and separated from developing Iranian in Zeravshan–Bactrian contact zone; by 1600 BCE eastern Iran/former BMAC towns abandoned; Mitanni Old Indic (~1500 BCE) and Punjab RV compilers share ethnolinguistic population origin in Andronovo/Tazabagyab/coarse-incised-ware hybrid cultures of Bactria-Margiana (attributed model).

## Lifeways (as evidenced)

Pastoral and mining economies; kurgan and camp sites in middle/upper Zeravshan after 1800; Tazabagyab irrigation farming hybrid on the southern fringe. Continued Sintashta/Petrovka customs without chariot burials. Integration with declining BMAC: after ~1800, people living just outside crumbling walls and at least some inside “were probably closely related” (Anthony) — attributed social reading of pottery distributions.

## Relations and Successions

- `developed_from` Potapovka–[[sintashta-culture|Sintashta]]; Petrovka as intermediate eastern offshoot.
- Parallel western LBA: **Srubnaya** (Timber-Grave) from Urals to Dnieper, also from Potapovka/late Abashevo via Pokrovka ~1900–1750 into Srubnaya 1800–1200.
- `associated_with` BMAC classic phase contact then post-BMAC countryside integration.
- `associated_with` Seima-Turbino forest-steppe metal horizon (partly contemporary; tin-bronze spears/axes; lost-wax from BMAC direction).
- `associated_with` Okunevo (western Altai, successor of late [[afanasievo-culture|Afanasievo]]) via Petrovka eastern contacts.
- Linguistic daughters: northern Iranian path vs southern Old Indic contact-zone path (Anthony).

## Identity Cautions

- Horizon ≠ single people. Alakul and Federovo co-residence blocks simple ethnic dualism.
- Culture ≠ language ≠ genes. “Indo-Iranian spread with Andronovo” is Anthony’s attributed correlation, not proven speech from pots.
- Mining-scale estimates are order-of-magnitude archaeological estimates.
- Petrovka often lumped as “Sintashta-Petrovka”; keep chronological and metallurgical distinctions (arsenical vs tin bronze; chariot phase).
- Dates follow Anthony 2007 synthesis of CIS radiocarbon after 1991 boom.
