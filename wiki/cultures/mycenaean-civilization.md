# Mycenaean Civilization

## Definition and Construct History

**Mycenaean** denotes the Late Bronze Age palace-centered archaeological culture of mainland Greece (and related Aegean contexts), named from the citadel of [[mycenae]]. It is an **etic archaeological-civilizational construct**: shared architecture (megaron palaces), pottery styles (LH), administrative practices (Linear B), and elite exchange — not a single self-named ethnos continuous with classical "Greeks" without argument.

[[cline-eric-h|Cline]] (2014) treats Mycenaean palaces as participants in the Eastern Mediterranean international system and as major casualties of the [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].

**Renfrew 1972 (attributed).** *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|Emergence]]* pairs Mycenaean with Minoan as the second-millennium palace outcome of third-millennium mainland + Aegean processes (Eutresis/Korakou/Tiryns culture frames; Lerna House of the Tiles; fortifications; anti-immigration reading of EH origins). Palace principalities are “minor states,” not empire; foundations (ranking, metal, ships, [[mediterranean-polyculture]], craft) already EBA. 2010 preface: no “Coming of the Greeks” migration package required for the civilisation story.

**Dickinson 1994 (attributed).** *[[dickinson-aegean-bronze-age-1994|The Aegean Bronze Age]]* densifies MH→LH material culture (pottery homogeneity from Argolid centre; fresco as traditionalising Minoan inheritance; weapons/armour; chamber/tholos burial; Linear B land/wool economy). Softens total palace-[[redistribution]] models; warns against equating Ahhiyawa with Mycenae and overestimating Mycenaean eastern commercial role. Collapse framed as cumulative systems process; Postpalatial isolation by ~1000; Dark Age as true birth of “the Greeks” (his framing).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

- **Material:** fortifications, tholos tombs, palatial complexes, LH ceramics, prestige imports.
- **Linguistic:** Linear B administrative Greek (as deciphered); loss of Linear B with palace collapse.
- **Genetic:** not established on this page from Cline 2014 alone.

## Distribution and Chronology

Mainland Greece and Aegean connections; peak palatial period roughly 14th–13th centuries BCE; destructions/abandonments cluster near the end of the 13th / early 12th century BCE (site-by-site dates vary).

## Lifeways

Palace-administered agriculture, craft, and redistribution (Linear B); warfare and elite competition; long-distance contacts with Crete, Anatolia, Cyprus, Levant, Egypt (as of Cline's synthesis of finds and texts).

## Relations and Successions

`associated_with` [[minoan-civilization]] (influence, conquest narratives for Crete — attributed, contested detail). Part of LBA international system destroyed/transformed in [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].

## Identity Cautions

Mycenaean material culture ≠ Homer's Achaeans without argument; Linear B Greek ≠ classical Greek ethnicity; destruction ≠ total population replacement.

## Historiography

Culture-historical naming from Schliemann onward; Linear B decipherment (Ventris) transformed the construct; collapse debates continue (see [[late-bronze-age-collapse-causes-debate]]).
