## Definition and Construct History

**Recuay** is a vibrant Early Intermediate Period corporate style and sierra political sphere of the **Callejón de Huaylas** and upper **Río Santa** (Ancash, Peru). Moseley (as of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|2001]]) treats it as a powerful mountain neighbor of coastal [[moche-culture|Moche]], known mainly through looted white kaolin ceramics, stone sculpture, and elite tombs rather than a fully mapped territorial state. The construct is material-style + burial architecture—not a documented ethnonym or single capital city.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Ceramics (as of Moseley 2001):** White kaolin wares; negative/positive painting; three-dimensional modeling; standardized iconography emphasizing men in arms, trophy heads, males with females, serpents/felines/condors, double-headed creatures.

**Sculpture:** (1) Tenon heads—Chavín carry-over, realistic male heads presumed kuraka warriors; (2) freestanding ~1 m squat figures with oversized heads (Schaedel: two phases; ~one-third women; males with clubs/shields/trophy heads).

**Tombs:** Elite subterranean slab-roofed mausoleums (Willkawain multi-room crypts). **Pashash** (Grieder & Bueno): tomb under three-room shrine with 277 fine items in three offerings; possible ancestral shrine; larger complex includes 15-m “Caserón” platform terrace ~30 m wide—major Recuay center, unclear if capital or one of equals.

**Linguistic / genetic:** [[unknown]].

## Distribution and Chronology

Callejón de Huaylas and headwaters of the Santa; elite exchange zone into the lower Santa and adjacent coastal valleys. EIP contemporaneity with Moche; Recuay ceramics at Moche settlements in the lower Santa; Moche tombs with Recuay vessels—elite exchange beyond folk [[vertical-archipelago|verticality]] (as of Moseley 2001).

## Lifeways

Sierra kuraka militarism and ancestor veneration are iconographically prominent (warriors, trophy heads, mausoleums). Vertical and/or trade links to the coast. Detailed household economy and settlement hierarchy beyond Pashash/Willkawain are under-specified in Moseley’s EIP chapter.

## Relations and Successions

- **associated_with:** [[chavin-culture]] (tenon-head tradition carry-over—not identity).
- **Exchange peer:** Moche elites (ceramic interchange in lower Santa).
- **Regional context:** One of several EIP sierra polities (with Cajamarca, Marcahuamachuco/Crisnejas, Kuelap/Amazonas sphere) neighboring coastal states.
- Do not merge Recuay with Wari, Chavín, or Moche political identities.

## Identity Cautions

Recuay names a corporate style and archaeological culture, not a proven language or genetic population. “Capital vs one of equals” at Pashash remains open. Looting biases the ceramic sample toward elite funerary art.

## Historiography

Schaedel on sculpture phases; Grieder & Bueno at Pashash; Moseley’s 2001 synthesis for coastal–sierra exchange framing. Source: [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001]].
