# Aunjetitz (Únětice) Culture

*(Childe 1929 spelling Aunjetitz; modern Únětice.)* Early Bronze Age culture of Bohemia–Moravia and adjacent regions; load-bearing in Childe's Early Bronze Age chapter (*[[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929|Danube]]*).

## Definition and Construct History

Defined from cemetery and hoard assemblages: characteristic pottery, metal forms, amber, and trade connections. Classic culture-historical package in [[culture-historical-archaeology]].

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as Childe):** Aunjetitz ware; copper/bronze types; amber route connections toward Italy; copper sources argued from Slovakia/Transylvania; social specialization without a full "warrior class" in his reading; outposts/variants (Veselé, Gata, Straubing, Silesia as he maps them).

**Linguistic / genetic:** none in source.

## Distribution and Chronology

Bohemia–Moravia core with related groups in adjacent Central Europe. Absolute dates: Childe's synchronism table (Byblos, Vapheio, Mycenaean rapiers, etc.) — **1929 construction**, superseded.

## Lifeways

Metal-using agricultural societies with long-distance "pedlar" trade (amber, metals) as Childe reconstructs.

## Relations and Successions

Links to Hungarian Early Bronze (Tószeg/Perjámos) — chronological priority disputed even in Childe's Epilogue (reversible vs Marschwitz/proto-Aunjetitz). Succeeded/abutted by Tumulus and later urnfield complexes in his sequence.

## Identity Cautions

Etic culture label; not a people-name; not IE language proof. Do not merge with genetic steppe narratives without separate evidence.

## Historiography

Únětice remains a standard EBA label; absolute dating and social models updated repeatedly since 1929.
