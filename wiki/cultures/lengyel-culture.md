# Lengyel Culture

Middle Neolithic archaeological culture of **east-central Europe** (Carpathian basin and north into Poland), known for **settlement layouts, longhouses, enclosures, and graves** in the post-LBK world. **Etic construct.**

## Definition and Construct History

Culture-historical label densified from *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996|Whittle 1996]]* Ch. 6 (longhouse lives) — material detail from claims integration.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

Longhouses (including late trapezoidal wall-supported houses without internal posts), ditch/palisade enclosures and roundels, formal graves (settlement-dispersed at Brześć Kujawski / Oslonki; large cemeteries at Aszód, Zengővárkony). Not equated with language or genes.

## Distribution and Chronology

East-central Europe in the middle Neolithic after early LBK expansion; exact calendrical spans region-dependent (mid-1990s ¹⁴C framework). After 5000 BC Whittle maps **Lengyel** across Transdanubia, Slovakia, Czech Lands, parts of S/central Poland, Bavaria/adjacent Austria; **Stichbandkeramik (SBK)** as far W as the Rhine, interleaved/preceding Lengyel in Bohemia/S Poland; Hinkelstein and Grossgartach Rhineland; **Rössen** succeeding across much of Germany; Villeneuve-Saint-Germain/Cerny N France as related post-LBK geography.

## Lifeways

Village and enclosure life continuing longhouse architectural tradition; Whittle reads social values through settlement and burial (attributed). Continuity package post-LBK (as of Whittle): longhouses remain (shape/construction change); some settlements more nucleated/bounded; many formalised ditched/palisaded enclosures; still decorated pots and stone adzes/axes; copper only gradual; dead in/around settlement/cemeteries; same broad subsistence — some Transdanubian Lengyel even **more wild game** than LBK; changes as strategy to **maintain/reinforce** existing Neolithic way of life, not wholesale new society.

## Relations and Successions

- **developed_from / associated_with:** [[linear-pottery-culture]] longhouse world.
- **associated_with:** [[funnel-beaker-culture|TRB]] later trajectories in some regions (Bellwood/others link TRB via Lengyel — position, not identity). Brześć late Lengyel ¹⁴C overlaps early TRB in Kujavia; early TRB largely sandy/morainic zones complementary to black-soil islands (Sarnowo ~20 km SW of Brześć).
- Middle Neolithic ditched enclosures elsewhere may keep alive **cultural memory** of later LBK / earlier Lengyel enclosure tradition (Whittle Ch. 7 position).

## Identity Cautions

Culture name ≠ ethnicity. Enclosure ≠ fortress by default — ritual/aggregation readings exist.

## Historiography

Central European culture history; densified via Whittle 1996.

## Whittle densification — Brześć Kujawski, Oslonki, enclosures (1996)

*(All claims attributed to [[whittle-alasdair]], *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996|Europe in the Neolithic]]* 1996, Ch. 6–7. Mid-1990s cal BC; pre-aDNA. Lengyel = etic material-culture construct.)*

### Brześć Kujawski and Oslonki (Kujavia, central Poland)

Mid–later fifth millennium BC: Brześć Kujawski and Oslonki (OCR variants Ostonki) ~10 km apart on fertile black-soil low ridges partly surrounded by water; major sites in the vicinity per surface survey. **Trapezoidal longhouses**, large pits, graves dispersed through settlement (beside houses, abandoned pits); Oslonki encircled by a ditch with houses inside; long use (Oslonki possibly slightly earlier); Brześć up to six house-plan superimpositions in plots.

Late Lengyel trapezoidal longhouses: broad front, narrow rear; walls of closely set posts/planks supporting the roof — **no internal postholes**; up to 30 m; N–S rows. Basic unit: longhouse + surrounding space (~20 m radius) with large loam pit and work areas. House 56 (site 4) maintained over time with renewal; 5 burials/phase mature men/women + child cranium; House 41→42 similar; tortoise/shellfish live-storage pit; copper smelting pit; House 41 and first phase of 56 ended in flames — possibly deliberate burning; tortoise pit became adolescent grave.

**Subsistence very broad:** domesticates (cattle most important + pigs/sheep) + cereals + hunting game/birds + fish + tortoises/shellfish + butchered beaver for skins; seasonal abundance rhythms. Whittle prefers not “farmers vs foragers” labels but a herding–hunting–cultivation–collecting–foraging spectrum — broader than LBK as usually seen (possibly better bone preservation). Non-local chocolate flint >150 km SE, Jurassic Kraków flint >250 km S, copper SW/S sources.

**Permanence:** Krusza Zamkowa model of frequent residential relocation vs stable long-term model for these sites; Whittle: **radiating/tethered mobility** most appropriate — pre-eminent places with greatest aggregations sanctified by household burials; longhouse as metaphor for human life continuity; individual occupants may come/go seasonally; TRB chronological overlap may circumscribe mobility orbit. Late Lengyel Brześć group = end of long “Danubian” tradition; TRB origins debate (indigenous Mesolithic vs Danubian offshoots) left open.

### Large Transdanubian / S Slovak sites and sanctification of place

Aszód: strategically sited Galga valley; 20–25 ha pits/graves; few small houses; graves in linear groups of ~30 separated 10–20 m, totals in hundreds; goods diverse (pots, stone, boar jaws, *Spondylus*); ~1/3 no goods; cenotaphs. Also Lengyel type-site; Zengővárkony (~400 graves in clusters); Mórágy-Tűzkődomb (structured pit deposits incl. female figurine; child skulls/skeletons in pots; dog burials; copper ornaments; hammer-axes; ditch system may link to enclosure phenomenon). Whittle: not legitimate to infer marked social difference from rich graves alone — striking is **sanctification of chosen places by the dead**; need not be permanently occupied.

### Enclosures and roundels

SBK/early Lengyel formalised enclosures: **Svodín** (S Slovakia) — large settlement with longhouses + graves >500 m; after settlement already existed, roundel circular ditch >60 m diam., probably 4 opposed entrances, 2 inner palisade rings; longhouses radial **outside**, no structures inside; replaced by larger double circular ditches >140 m diam., 4 entrances with out-turned terminals, 3 inner palisades with narrow in-turned entrances; ditches to 5 m deep; outermost ditch terminal to innermost palisade entrance 40 m = ≥5 graded visual experiences; V-ditches quick loess silt, recut 4–5 times; primary use maybe only as long as wood endured; settlement continued as enclosure decayed. Nearby Nitriansky Hrádok ~30 km; Bučany ~70 km NW.

**Těšetice-Kyjovice** (Morava basin): outer roughly circular palisade ~110×125 m, 4 opposed entrances; inner roundel ditch ~60 m + 2 palisade rings; early Lengyel after SBK occupation; **no Lengyel settlement within outer palisade**; special deposits (painted pottery + human skull; wall daub by W entrance). Other Morava-basin enclosures: Němčičky, Křepice, Vedrovice, Bulhary. Variation: Kamegg; Friebritz 2 / Künzing-Unternberg connecting terminals; Osterhofen-Schmiedorf three concentric; Glaubendorf 2, Gauderndorf, Hornsburg 3; Vochov non-opposed entrances; Quenstedt Elbe-Saale 5 concentric palisades (no ditches — not formally a “roundel” but graded experience); **Žlkovce** large Lengyel ~30 ha, outer palisade 350–400 m, central oval palisade >75 m renewed ≤6 times each enclosing ≤1 substantial longhouse — blurs roundel vs less formal.

### Whittle on enclosure meaning

Discounts fortification as primary explanation (would expect more through Neolithic/Copper Age); social pre-eminence/labour-mobilisation models awkward vs spacing and short-lived regional specificity. More promising: cult centres/meeting places, time-marking (cardinal orientations); consecration of place, ancestors, symbolic communal cohesion; mobility still important — symbolic capital in places increases territorial attachment **and** enables continued mobility; ordered approach/entry as metaphor for regularities of comings and goings year-to-year and generation-to-generation. Monuments not mere reflection of stabilised longhouse life but part of its **maintenance** — encourage tethered-mobility rhythms/obligations. Evolutionary explanations (strife, differentiation, population density) fail the short-lived regional nature of the phenomenon. Settlement continuity: Lengyel–Polgár same Vistula tributaries as LBK S Poland; few areas show significant settlement-density increase (Kujavia possible exception); notable is **nucleation** — population redistribution not overall density rise.
