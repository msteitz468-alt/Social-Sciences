# Minoan Civilization

## Definition and Construct History

**Minoan** is Evans's etic label for Bronze Age Crete's palace-centered culture (from mythical Minos). Construct rests on palace architecture, fresco styles, ceramic sequences, and scripts (Cretan hieroglyphic, Linear A). [[cline-eric-h|Cline]] (2014) places Minoans in early LBA international contacts and treats their transformation under Mycenaean influence as part of the longer LBA story leading toward systemic collapse.

**Renfrew 1972 (attributed).** *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|The Emergence of Civilisation]]* treats Minoan (with Mycenaean) palace society as Europe’s first civilisation and locates its **foundations in third-millennium Crete and the wider Aegean**: EM sequence (Knossos well, Lebena, Vasiliki, Myrtos, tholoi, early metallurgy/seals), [[mediterranean-polyculture]], craft specialisation, and social ranking culminating in first palaces around 2000 BC — via local [[multiplier-effect]] process, not Near Eastern transplant. Prepalatial Crete densified in his Ch. 6; palace redistribution and writing as palace accounting in later process chapters.

**Dickinson 1994 (attributed).** *[[dickinson-aegean-bronze-age-1994|The Aegean Bronze Age]]* is the full-BA thematic handbook: Prepalatial → First/Second/Third Palace → Postpalatial crafts, settlement hierarchy, burial (communal long-use tombs), exchange (Crete as Near Eastern channel in Second Palace; Keftiu diplomacy), and religion (multi-god, peak sanctuaries, elite cult; rejects Evans mother-goddess monism). Palace-tied crafts (fresco, seals, Linear A/B administration) collapse with palaces. Absolute chronology and Thera debate date-stamped to mid-1990s.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

- **Material:** multi-story palaces, frescoes, marine-style ceramics, peak sanctuary and port sites.
- **Linguistic:** Linear A undeciphered; later Linear B (Greek) at Knossos under Mycenaean-period administration.
- **Genetic:** not claimed from Cline 2014 alone.

## Distribution and Chronology

Crete and Aegean islands; Neopalatial peak mid-2nd millennium BCE; Thera eruption chronology disputed; mainland Mycenaean takeover/influence narratives for later palace phases are attributed and debated.

## Lifeways

Intensive agriculture; craft and trade (including copper/tin bronze networks); maritime connectivity.

## Relations and Successions

`associated_with` [[mycenaean-civilization]]; Aegean partner in Eastern Mediterranean exchange. Not identical to "Sea Peoples" or to classical Cretans.

## Identity Cautions

Minoan ≠ a known ethnonym; Linear A language unknown; palace destruction phases must not be silently equated with [[sea-peoples]] without site-level argument.

## Historiography

Evans's reconstruction heavily shaped the construct; later excavation and chronology revisions (including Santorini dating disputes) continually revise timelines Cline inherits as secondary synthesis.
