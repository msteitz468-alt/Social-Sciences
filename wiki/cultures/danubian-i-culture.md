# Danubian I Culture (Childe)

*(Attributed — Childe 1929, *[[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929|The Danube in Prehistory]]*, esp. Ch. IV.)* First major Neolithic farming culture of the Middle Danube / loess corridor in Childe's system — the naming ancestor of later Linear Pottery / LBK discussions. **Etic construct.**

## Definition and Construct History

Childe divides Danubian I into **Ia** and **Ib** (stroke-ornamented / *Stichbandkeramik* and related), with further Rhenish and Rössen styles treated as related or successive. Defined by a recurring package of pottery decoration, shoe-last celts, settlement form, and ornaments — a textbook application of [[archaeological-culture]].

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as Childe):** linear / spiral-meander pottery (Ia); shoe-last celts; disk maces; *Spondylus* ornaments; small loess settlements / *Hackbau* horticulture; house plans (e.g. Lissdorf). Ib and Rössen/Rhenish styles as regional/developmental variants he discusses.

**Linguistic / genetic:** not available in 1929 source.

## Distribution and Chronology

Loess band from Middle Danube expanding north and west toward Galicia, central Germany, Rhine, Belgium, northern France (Epilogue summary). Absolute dates: Childe's relative Period only — modern absolute chronology differs.

## Lifeways

*(Attributed.)* Small, relatively short-lived farming settlements on loess; horticultural *Hackbau*; material package consistent with expanding peasant colonisation from the SE in his narrative (linked to Vinča I horizon).

**Clark 1961 densification** (*[[clark-world-prehistory-1961|World Prehistory]]* ch. 6): Danubian I (spiral-meander ware) peasants occupy a vast loess zone from south of Lake Balaton nearly to the Oder estuary and from Rhine/Maas to Vistula/Upper Dniester; cultural uniformity implies comparatively rapid colonization from the Middle Danube along rivers; early C-14 places them in South Germany and Dutch Limburg before the end of the 5th millennium B.C. (uncalibrated). Package: emmer, barley, einkorn, peas, beans, flax; farmyard animals; no weaving or metallurgy; long rectangular houses 20–50 m; palisaded villages; shifting cultivation with rebuilds; *Spondylus* ornaments; single-grave cemeteries (attributed). Later "barbarization" on poorer soils and Rössen specialization are successive stages in his outline — still culture-historical labels, not genetic populations.

## Relations and Successions

- **Associated with** first Neolithic SE package ([[vinca-culture]] Vinča I as southern counterpart/horizon).
- Succeeded in Childe's sequence by Period II complexes (Lengyel–Jordansmühl, Polgár, etc.).
- Later "Nordic," Corded, and Beaker currents overlay or abut Danubian-derived populations in his story.

## Identity Cautions

Not a self-named people; not a race; not a language. Childe's "movement of people" reading of the expansion is a culture-historical inference, not a genetic result. Do not silently equate with LBK or with modern ethnic labels.

## Historiography

Central named culture of interwar Central European Neolithic synthesis; label "Danubian" largely replaced in later literature by more local culture names (LBK, etc.) while the expansion narrative was rewritten with radiocarbon and aDNA.
