# Grotta-Pelos Culture

## Definition and Construct History

**Grotta-Pelos** is [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]]'s etic archaeological-culture name (1972) for the earliest major phase of his Early Cycladic sequence. He prefers named **cultures** defined by recurrent associations over a purely period-based EC I–II–III scheme imposed by analogy with Crete or the mainland (attributed, *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|Emergence]]* Ch. 9).

The construct joins two assemblages Renfrew argues are contemporary and of one people: **Pelos-type cist-grave** funerary sets and **Grotta / Phylakopi O** domestic burnished wares (tubular lugs; pithoi with raised incised bands). Incised pyxides etc. are chiefly funerary, so domestic and cemetery shapes rarely co-occur; surface survey of settlements adjoining Pelos cemeteries supports the equation (attributed).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (Renfrew 1972):** rolled-rim burnished bowls; cist graves; schematic / Plastiras / Louros figurines; Plastiras and Kampos groups; marble kandilai and simple bowls; limited metal. Diagnostic grave types almost never co-occur with Keros-Syros diagnostics in the same burial (exception: Apollona 38 B, possibly heirloom kandila with dagger).

**Linguistic / genetic:** not claimed.

## Distribution and Chronology

Central and southern Cyclades cemeteries; domestic material best documented at Grotta and Phylakopi pre-City. Stratigraphically earlier than Phylakopi I; earlier than [[keros-syros-culture]] on metal frequency, Phylakopi sandwiching, and seriation (attributed). Absolute dates within Renfrew's ~3200–2100 BC third-millennium framework (as of 1972 calibration horizon).

## Lifeways

Small communities; cemetery wealth modest vs later Keros-Syros; Final Neolithic local origins stressed against Anatolian diffusion packages (attributed).

## Relations and Successions

Preceded by Final Neolithic / Attic–Kephala horizons in Renfrew's account; succeeded by [[keros-syros-culture]]; later [[phylakopi-i-culture]] at Melos. Part of the third-millennium foundation for [[minoan-civilization|Minoan]]/[[mycenaean-civilization|Mycenaean]] complexity via [[multiplier-effect]] (program-level, not this culture alone).

## Identity Cautions

Etic construct — not an ethnonym or “people” in the ethnic sense. Renfrew rejects treating Grotta-Pelos and Keros-Syros as rigid contemporary ethnic co-residents with sealed grave-good codes; he reads the split as largely chronological (attributed).

## Historiography

Defined in *[[renfrew-emergence-of-civilisation-1972|The Emergence of Civilisation]]* (1972). Later Cycladic work (Doumas, Rambach, Broodbank, etc.) densifies cemeteries and chronology; Renfrew's 2010 preface notes continued refinement while keeping the culture-process program.
