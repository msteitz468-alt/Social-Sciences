# Natufian Culture

Terminal Pleistocene Levantine archaeological tradition of intensive foragers — often semi-sedentary in the Mediterranean “core” — immediately preceding Pre-Pottery Neolithic food production. Named by Dorothy Garrod after Wadi en-Natuf (Shukbah Cave); classic type-assemblage from [[el-wad|el-Wad]] Layer B on Mount Carmel. The modern research corpus is organized by the two Bar-Yosef & Valla symposium volumes (1991; [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013|2013]]).

## Definition and Construct History

The **Natufian** is an **etic archaeological-culture / cultural-complex** construct defined by microlithic technology (especially lunates), groundstone, bone industry, scaphopod (Dentalium-group) shell beads, architecture and burial practices in varying combinations — not by a self-name or observed ethnicity.

**Construct history (attributed).**

- **Garrod / Neuville (1930s):** defined Natufian from Carmel–Judean assemblages; Dentalium shells as a hallmark (restated Kurzawska et al. 2013).
- **Bar-Yosef school:** formalized Early/Late (and later Final) phasing; “homeland” or Mediterranean **core area** (Carmel–Galilee–Upper Jordan Valley) vs arid **periphery**.
- **Valla:** refined Final Natufian by very small abrupt-backed lunates; long-term [[eynan-ain-mallaha|Eynan]] program.
- **Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris (2013):** argue the core-area Natufian has been wrongly treated as a **monolithic** Late Epipaleolithic culture; better viewed as a **cultural complex** with ~3000 years of intra-/inter-regional and chronological facies, multi-tradition origins, and more than a base-camp vs hunting-station dichotomy ([[natufian-origins-core-periphery-debate]]).
- **[[henry-donald-o|Henry]] (2013):** dual northern and southern Early Natufian cores and a four-stage expansion model synchronized (imperfectly) with Bølling–Allerød and [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] ([[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]]).

Textbook dates ~12.5–10.5 kya (as of [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007|Wenke & Olszewski 2007]]) reflect older uncalibrated conventions; calibrated schemes in the 2013 volume stretch the entity to roughly **15.5–11.0 ka cal BP** (Henry) or **~15–11.5 ka cal BP** with Early ~15–13.6 and Late ~13.1–11.5 (Grosman PeakPoint method) — schemes disagree on Early→Late placement (~13.7 ka cal BP Henry vs other estimates).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

| Architecture | Circular/oval stone-footed semi-subterranean structures; packed earth floors; light brush/hide superstructures hypothesized (Bar-Yosef & Valla preface); massive Early Natufian hamlets (el-Wad, Eynan, Wadi Hammeh 27) |
| Burial | Primary and secondary interments; Early ornamented burials; Late skull removal and cemetery–settlement shifts (Belfer-Cohen tradition); burials almost confined to Mediterranean phytogeographic zone (exception Azraq 18) |

**Linguistic / genetic.** Unknown from this evidence class. Do not equate Natufian material culture with later Semitic or any genetic population.

## Distribution and Chronology

**Geography.** Levantine arc from northern Levant (Anti-Lebanon, Qadisha, Damascus province, Homs Gap, Dederiyeh) through Mount Carmel–Galilee–Hula–Jordan Valley “core” into Transjordan steppes (Wadi Hasa, Azraq, Wadi al-Qusayr) and Negev–Sinai (Terminal Ramonian / Natufian / Harifian). Northern Levant shows **Late denser than Early** occupation in Anti-Lebanon foothills — opposite the classic southern “heartland” Early-hamlet / Late-ephemeral pattern (Conard et al. 2013) — so regional models should not be projected pan-Levant.

**Chronology (positions, not adjudicated).**

**Phasing labels:** Early / Late / Final Natufian (Mediterranean); Terminal Ramonian–Early Natufian, Late Natufian, **Harifian** (Negev–Sinai).

## Lifeways

**Subsistence.** Intensive wild-plant use (cereals, nuts, wetland monocots) plus hunting (often gazelle-specialized in Mediterranean zone). Positions:

1. **[[broad-spectrum-revolution]] (Binford/Flannery tradition):** diet diversification as demographic–ecological prelude to farming.
2. **Henry specialized intensification:** large sedentary communities via specialized cereals/nuts + specialized gazelle hunting — not diversification as prime mover.
3. **Bar-Oz, Yeshurun & Weinstein-Evron (2013):** Carmel coastal gazelle rise is **within Early Natufian** and does not track climate — cultural hunting preference, not YD effect.
4. **Rosen “Monocot Revolution” (2013 phytoliths):** Early forest/nut ranking → Late grass-husk + wetland monocots; small-seeded weeds often exceed cereals; **no clear Late Natufian cultivation** in her samples; YD as resource re-ranking.
5. **Site diversity:** Baaz as hunting camp without sickle gloss; Dederiyeh burnt building with *Pistacia*/almond/wild wheat; Eynan wetland avifauna (Simmons) and multi-habitat catchment (Ashkenazi).

**Settlement and [[sedentism]].** Multi-season to year-round base-camps with architecture, storage, cemeteries in core; higher residential mobility and logistical ranges in steppic periphery (Goring-Morris & Belfer-Cohen; Olszewski on Hasa). Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris reject simple permanent-base vs temporary-station dichotomy. Seasonality of house occupation remains open (Valla 1998; 2013 preface). [[commensalism-archaeology|Commensal]] house mice used as sedentism proxy (Tchernov) — Weissbrod et al. (2013) find Natufian *Mus* frequencies often below ethnoarchaeological sedentary benchmarks and urge taphonomic caution.

**Social signaling.** Shell bead networks, green-stone and basalt exchange, obsidian from Cappadocia, art, and burial ornamentation document inter-site “networking” (preface agenda). Late Natufian: fewer shells in graves, more Red Sea scaphopods in habitation — mobility/exchange shift (Kurzawska et al.).

**Dog.** Early human–canid association (e.g. ʿAin Mallaha puppy burial in textbook synthesis); dog as oldest SW Asian animal domesticate trajectory — distinct from plant domestication.

## Relations and Successions

- **Preceded by:** Geometric Kebaran in Mediterranean zone (**continuity contested** — Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris); Mushabian→Ramonian→Terminal Ramonian in Negev/Sinai (**stronger continuity**).
- **Enables / path toward:** PPNA farming villages; Huzuq Musa as Final Epipaleolithic / earliest PPNA Jordan Valley facies with mixed markers (Nadel & Rosenberg). Harifian as arid-zone Late/Final offshoot coinciding broadly with YD, then abandonment.
- **Compared with:** other early sedentism cases; Mesoamerican sedentism–agriculture order (textbook comparative theme).
- **Not identity with:** later [[uruk-culture|Uruk]]/Sumerian or any Semitic ethnolinguistic group.

## Identity Cautions

- **Etic culture-historical construct** (or multi-facies complex), not an ethnonym.
- **Four-way non-identity.** Natufian ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ “first farmers” continuous into later civilizations.
- **Sedentism ≠ agriculture.** Intensive wild-plant use and multi-season base-camps precede morphological domestication.
- **Core ≠ whole.** Periphery and northern Levant invert or complicate heartland periodizations.
- **Henry vs BSR vs climate models** are positions — see debates.

## Historiography

**Evidence quality.** Rich multi-site material record (Henry: >70 sites); poor plant preservation and sparse 14C remain structural gaps (Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013 preface). Site-formation (brush superstructures, floor accumulation, admixture of Geometric Kebaran into Natufian layers at el-Wad/Kebara) limits phase purity.

**Major research programs.** Garrod Carmel; Perrot then Valla at Eynan; Bar-Yosef at Hayonim; Weinstein-Evron/Kaufman/Yeshurun at el-Wad terrace; Edwards at Wadi Hammeh 27; Grosman at Hilazon; Nadel/Lengyel/Bocquentin at Raqefet; Conard TDASP Anti-Lebanon; Goring-Morris Negev–Sinai.

**Key sources.** [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013|Bar-Yosef & Valla (eds.) 2013]] (primary densification); Bar-Yosef & Valla 1991; [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]]; [[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996]] (cognitive “point of no return” reading).

**Outstanding.** Post-2013 aDNA of Levantine Epipaleolithic/Neolithic populations; refined Bayesian 14C models; systematic starch/phytolith programs.

## Mithen 1996 — “point of no return”

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven]] citing Bar-Yosef & Belfer-Cohen in *[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* Epilogue.)* Natufian mobile→sedentary shift as a **point of no return**: sedentism relaxes mobility constraint on population growth, making higher food production inevitable when terminal-Pleistocene arid crises hit. Cognitive precondition is already-present [[cognitive-fluidity|cognitive fluidity]]. See [[agricultural-revolution]].

## After the Ice densification (Mithen 2003)

*(Attributed throughout — [[mithen-steven]], *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice]]* 2003, chs. 4–7; calendar BC as Mithen’s calibrated narrative; pre-aDNA horizon.)*

- **Early Natufian villages:** Mithen presents planned year-round villages in Mediterranean oak woodland (~12,300–10,800 BC), exemplified by [[eynan-ain-mallaha|‘Ain Mallaha]]; [[bar-yosef-ofer|Ofer Bar-Yosef]] calls the culture the “point of no turning back” toward farming (attributed).
- **Material package (as of Mithen’s synthesis):** subterranean floors, low drystone walls, brush/hide roofs; boulder basalt mortars for plant processing; lunate microliths for sickles/arrow barbs (fashion as well as function); sickle-gloss blades (Perrot dwelling 131); gazelle-carved or geometric bone sickle handles (possible symbolic harvest tech); dentalium and other shell exchange (including a Nile-origin bivalve ≥500 km).
- **Dog:** elderly woman–puppy burial at Mallaha and multi-human/dog graves at Hayonim as true domestication (size reduced vs wolf), enabled by permanent village waste niches (attributed).
- **Village distinctiveness:** jewellery raw materials and biological markers (Hayonim shorter stature; high third-molar agenesis rare at Mallaha) read as limited intermarriage between neighbouring settlements (attributed).
- **Sedentism evidence:** Lieberman cementum bands on gazelle teeth — warm- and cold-season kills at Natufian sites → year-round occupation (position; open to critique).
- **Cereals still wild:** all known Natufian cereal grains wild; first domesticates ≥1 millennium later at Abu Hureyra, Tell Aswad, [[jericho|Jericho]] — sickle-harvesting wild stands possibly for up to ~3,000 years without morphological domestication (attributed).
- **Origins of villages:** Valla (via Mauss Arctic ethnography) — extend Kebaran winter aggregations until year-round residences under improving climate; not forced by overpopulation (Mithen: Natufian skeletons reasonably healthy; site-number jump earlier at Kebaran ~14,500 BC).
- **Younger Dryas collapse (~10,800 BC):** Mithen reconstructs abandoned/ramshackle Mallaha, deserted Hayonim and Abu Hureyra; Late Natufians mobile; “experiment of sedentary village life… ultimately failed” after ~2,000 years — villages survive as ancestral memory and secondary reburial gatherings (attributed narrative).
- **Expansion then stress:** lunates spread to Arabian deserts and Euphrates before abandonment (homeland fission + food shortfall migrants); Cope’s 4:1 male-biased large-gazelle culling → size reduction over ~500 years; overcutting wild cereals/nuts plus YD climate; Late burial shifts (less grave ornament, secondary burials/missing skulls) as collapse of sedentary prestige control toward more egalitarian mobile society (strong interpretation — keep attributed).
- **PPNA path:** Natufian camping at Jericho spring (crescent tools; possible meagre cereal planting) precedes permanent farming village ~9600 BC (attributed).

**Identity cautions unchanged:** Natufian ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ continuous “first farmers.” Source: [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003]].

## Braidwood 1959 — incipient-era assemblage

*(Attributed — [[braidwood-robert]], *[[braidwood-prehistoric-men-1959|Prehistoric Men]]* 3rd ed.)* Braidwood places the Natufian among candidate assemblages of the **[[incipient-cultivation|era of incipient cultivation and animal domestication]]** — the thin archaeological prelude to primary village-farming in the **[[nuclear-area|nuclear Near East]]**.

- Upper layers of Palestine caves (Mount Carmel cited); flint traces also in Syria and Lebanon; date guesses in his text range around mid-holocene conventions then in flux (as of 1959).
- ~80% of Carmel Natufian flints microlithic; **sickle blades** identified by cutting sheen; sickle hafts in bone; ground/pecked stone; rich bone industry (points, harpoons, fish-hooks, awls, beads); burials some multiple; gazelle dominate fauna (drier climate reading); possible domesticated dog (and more recent report of goat); Coon “basically Mediterranean” physical assessment pending McCown.
- Formerly thought only cave dwellers; open-site traces beginning to appear in his account.
- **Food-producer status:** most food still hunted/collected (including land/freshwater shells); he treats Natufian + [[karim-shahir|Karim Shahir]] as *incipient* by hunch, not proven full food production.

Construct remains etic; Braidwood’s absolute dates and dog/goat claims are period positions. Debate: [[oasis-natural-habitat-debate]].

## Cauvin densification (2000)

*(Attributed — *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]]* ch.2; calibrated dates as of his tables.)*

- **Dates:** Natufian ~12,500–10,000 BC as first pre-agricultural villages of the Levant.
- **Sedentism without farming:** permanent villages (architecture solidity, heavy equipment, burial abundance — e.g. [[eynan-ain-mallaha|Eynan]]); not all Natufians villagers (coastal camps; arid-zone seasonality uncertain).
- **Economy:** broad-spectrum hunting-gathering; aquatic resources aid year-round occupation at lake/river edges; **not** specialised cereal farmers — intensive wild cereals only when locally abundant (Abu Hureyra 11–10k BC); after desiccation, other plants mass-exploited. Rejects Henry “cultural filter” gazelle specialisation as universal.
- **Toolkit:** sickles, grinding, even hoe/adze forms already exist but are not yet agricultural specialists (wood, ochre uses); Neolithic needs no wholly new tool kit until plough/traction — originality is **social framework** of villages/cemeteries.
- **Art:** essentially zoomorphic; sparse anthropomorphs (inventory of ~five proposed representations).
- **Succession:** toward [[khiamian|Khiamian]] (10,000–9500 BC) then PPNA triad — Cauvin’s path into the [[revolution-of-symbols]].
