# Afanasievo Culture

## Definition and Construct History

The **Afanasievo culture** is an etic Early Bronze Age / late Eneolithic construct of the **western Altai and Minusinsk** region, treated by [[anthony-david-w|David Anthony]] (*[[anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007|The Horse, the Wheel, and Language]]*, 2007) as the product of a **long-distance migration** (~2000 km) of people with **Repin-type** material culture from the middle Volga–Ural / Don steppes, two–three centuries before the classic [[yamnaya-culture|Yamnaya]] horizon. It is the easternmost early kurgan-and-stock package in Anthony’s narrative and the archaeological hook for the **Pre-Tocharian** separation in his branch-order model. Cultures are analytical constructs, not self-named peoples.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (Anthony 2007):** Fully developed **kurgan rituals** and Repin–Yamnaya-related material culture introduced into the Altai; earliest **domesticated cattle, sheep, and horses** in the Altai (Balyktyul sample: sheep-goat 61%, cattle 12%, horses 8%); ~**100 metal objects**, including three sleeved copper axes of classic Volga-Ural Yamnaya type that E. N. Chernykh recognized as **western types with no local Altai precedents**. Shared steppe ritual geography: sacrifices and broken pottery on the **southwestern side of the kurgan** also appear in Yamnaya and even Usatovo — linking far west and far east. Intermediate path: only three kurgan cemeteries (Ubagan I, Verkhnaya Alabuga, Karagash) across north Kazakhstan while indigenous [[botai-culture|Botai-Tersek]] occupied the open steppe.

**Repin source package:** Middle Don Repin site — **55% horse** among mammal bones; pottery type spread Don–Volga, stratified under Yamnaya (Cherkasskaya), north to Samara; Kyzyl Khak Repin antelope camp ~3700–3600 BCE.

**Linguistic:** Anthony accepts J. P. Mallory and Victor Mair’s book-length argument that Afanasievo migration **detached Tocharian** from PIE; possible material bridge via Tarim “mummies” 1800–1200 BCE (supine raised knees, ledged graves) and shared solar-sign/headdress motifs with late Afanasievo Altai grave paintings (~2500 BCE) and Zaghunluq (~1200 BCE). “If Mallory and Mair were right, as seems likely…” (Anthony 2007) — explicitly theoretical.

**Genetic / osteology:** Skulls said to resemble Yamnaya/western types (morphological analogy only in this source).

## Distribution and Chronology

| Element | Anthony 2007 dates / span |
|---|---|
| Appearance in Altai | ~**3700–3400 BCE** (Repin–late Khvalynsk period) |
| Flourishes until | ~**2400 BCE** |
| Distance from Ural frontier | ~**2000 km** east |
| Sequence in IE model | Pre-Tocharian separation **before** classic Yamnaya late-PIE expansion (~3300 BCE) |

Northern Kazakhstan intervening steppe largely unsettled by the migrants; Botai-Tersek indigenous. Later contact: Petrovka (LBA) probably in touch with **Okunevo**, successor of late Afanasievo in the western Altai.

## Lifeways (as evidenced)

Stockbreeding (sheep-goat dominant at Balyktyul) with cattle and horses; kurgan burial; western-type metallurgy without local Altai antecedents — intrusive pastoral package. Late Afanasievo pastoralists, if the Tocharian link holds, may have been among the first to take herds from the Altai southward into the Tien Shan (Anthony, following Mallory & Mair).

## Relations and Successions

- `descends_from` / migration from **Repin** (and late [[khvalynsk-culture|Khvalynsk]] period context), not from Botai.
- `associated_with` [[yamnaya-culture]] material and ritual parallels (axes, kurgan SW-side sacrifices) without chronological identity — Afanasievo starts earlier than classic Yamnaya.
- `associated_with` Tocharian hypothesis (Mallory & Mair; Anthony agrees “as seems likely”).
- `succeeded_by` Okunevo in western Altai; later LBA networks ([[andronovo-culture|Petrovka/Andronovo]]) touch Okunevo.
- Anthony’s migration cascade: (1) Repin→Afanasievo ~3700–3500 BCE = Pre-Tocharian; (2) early Yamnaya ~3300 BCE scatters late PIE; … (5) late Yamnaya/Poltavka→[[sintashta-culture|Sintashta]] for Indo-Iranian precursors.

## Identity Cautions

- Culture ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ people. Afanasievo ≠ “Tocharians” without argument; the Tocharian link is a **linguistic–archaeological hypothesis**, not a reading of texts from Altai graves.
- Do not equate pots/axes with genetic population; Chernykh’s western metal types and skull resemblance are independent evidence classes, each limited.
- Intermediate emptiness of north Kazakh steppe undercuts simple continuous demic carpet — leapfrogging migration with sparse intermediate cemeteries.
- Dates and Tocharian bridge motifs follow Anthony 2007; later aDNA and Tarim work may revise (date-stamp).

## Historiography
