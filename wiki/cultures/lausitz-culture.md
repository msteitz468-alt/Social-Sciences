# Lausitz (Lusatian) Culture

*(Childe 1929: Lausitz urnfields east of the Elbe.)* Late Bronze Age urnfield complex covering much of the area between Elbe and Vistula and adjacent uplands (*[[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929|Danube]]*, Ch. XVI).

## Definition and Construct History

Childe follows Seger's phases **A, B, C, D** (aligned with Czech Earlier/Later Lausitz, Silesian, Platenice; North German Aurith, Görlitz, Billendorf for later phases). Defined primarily from cremation urnfields and associated metal/pottery types.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as Childe):** cremation with ustrinum; *Seelenloch* urns; pottery development wart → fluting; settlement evidence including Buch megaron houses; socketed celts; southward expansion into Hungary read as conquest package (socketed celt + sword + fluted ware) in Period VI narrative.

**Linguistic / genetic:** none in source — later ethnogenesis claims not to be projected back as wiki voice.

## Distribution and Chronology

Elbe–Vistula lowlands, Moravian lowlands, parts of Lower Austria and Slovakia (Childe); phases A–B his main concern for the Bronze Age core. Absolute dates: 1929 relative/synchronism only.

## Lifeways

Urnfield burial communities; mixed farming; metal use; Childe's invasion language for Hungarian impact is culture-historical inference.

## Relations and Successions

Origin arguments involving Aunjetitz + Hungarian stimuli (as he weighs them); North Alpine urnfields as related/amalgam; links to Switzerland–Rhine–Britain transmission in later chapters; Period VI downstream pressure toward Balkans/Macedonia in Epilogue.

## Identity Cautions

Etic urnfield package; **not** automatically "early Slavs" or any modern nation. Conquest language is Childe's 1929 narrative.

## Historiography

Lusatian/Lausitz remains a conventional late BA label; ethnic readings were heavily politicized in the 20th century — keep attributed and cautious.
