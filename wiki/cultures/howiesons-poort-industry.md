# Howiesons Poort Industry

## Definition and Construct History

**Howiesons Poort** (often Howieson’s Poort) is a southern African lithic industry/facies within the broader Middle Stone Age / mode-3–5 continuum, famous for **backed bladelets and geometric microlithic forms** that prefigure later mode-5 technology. [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] treats it as a key illustration that sophisticated composite-tool technology and other “modern” cultural markers appear in sub-Saharan Africa **well before** their relatively sudden appearance in Europe (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Material Signature

Backed microliths/bladelets suitable for hafting; part of the broader African trajectory Phillipson describes from mode-3 prepared cores toward mode-5 microlithic composite tools (as of Phillipson 2005; see [[lithic-technological-modes]]). Specific type lists and raw-material preferences are site-level and should be densified from excavation reports.

## Distribution and Chronology

Southern Africa, with classic stratified sequences at coastal and near-coastal sites including [[klasies-river]] and related MSA caves. Absolute ages often depend on luminescence and uranium-series methods because much of the span is beyond reliable radiocarbon; Phillipson flags many ≥40 ka 14C ages as **minimum** rather than absolute (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Lifeways

Hunter-gatherer; Phillipson links backed microliths continent-wide to probable arrow tips/barbs and bow-and-arrow hunting (especially with poison) as a revolutionary technology — a general mode-5 argument that includes but is not limited to Howiesons Poort (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Relations and Successions

- Embedded in southern African MSA sequences that also include sites such as [[blombos-cave]] (symbolic/engraved materials — Phillipson is cautious about over-reading single-site “firsts”).
- Temporal priority of African microlithic/composite technology relative to European Upper Palaeolithic is a load-bearing claim of Phillipson’s Ch. 4 synthesis (as of Phillipson 2005).
- Industry ≠ population: do not equate Howiesons Poort makers with any modern ethnolinguistic group without independent argument.

## Identity Cautions

Construct is a **facies/industry label**, not a people. “Behavioral modernity” packages that use Howiesons Poort as a trait list are theoretical frames — keep attributed (see [[behavioral-modernity]], [[recent-african-origin]]).
