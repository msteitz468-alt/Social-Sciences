## Definition and Construct History

Chavín is an archaeological-culture / horizon label for the religious and iconographic complex centered on [[chavin-de-huantar|Chavín de Huántar]] in the northern Peruvian sierra, and for the wider spread of associated imagery and craft markers across northern and central Peru during the **Early Horizon**. As of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]], the sanctuary was founded by ~800 BC; the horizon is **technically** defined when Chavín artistic influence appears in Ica Valley ceramic arts ~400 BC, broadly coincident with another protracted rainfall plunge. Scarre ed. 2013 dates the Early Horizon c. 400–200 BC. The construct rests on shared iconographic, architectural, textile, and metallurgical traits — not a single polity, language, or population.

Moseley frames the cult as rising after the fall of Sechín Alto and other Initial Period coastal centers (~900 BC drought abandonment): beliefs associated with sunken courts and U-shaped facilities persisted in the highlands and at Chavín; later cult spread during dry times **cross-cut old social boundaries** and fostered flow of ideas, products, and people within what became the **northern sphere** of Andean civilization — a **counter-pole** to the southern Pukara–[[paracas-culture]] sphere.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Architecture and oracle (Moseley 2001, citing Burger, Lumbreras):**

- Site at 3,177 m, confluence of Mosna and Wacheksa. Burger phases: **Urabarriu** ~800 BC (~500 people, 6 ha) → middle 500–400 BC (~doubled, 15 ha) → **Janabarriu** 400–200 BC (2,000–3,000 people, 42 ha urban core) then decline.
- **Castillo** <1/10 magnitude of Sechín Alto platform but fine masonry; ~quarter hollow with labyrinth of slab-roofed galleries, drains, vents; Lumbreras et al. hydrological/acoustical analysis: flushing water could make the temple "roar."
- **Old Temple** (Urabarriu): ~109 × 73 m, ~15 m high, U-plaza with circular sunken court; dual processions in tinku fashion; central **Lanzón** stela ~4.5 m (Smiling/Snarling God — clawed figure, feline face, tusk canines, snake hair); floor stone above allowed unseen attendant to speak — Lanzón as oracle for privileged supplicants (proposition).
- **New Temple** (after 500 BC): expanded southern wing; rectangular plaza with 50 m square sunken court; **black-and-white portal** with male hawk-headed and female eagle-headed winged anthropomorphs each with two staffs — gender dualism and tinku; tenon heads; cornice dual animal processions.
- **Raimondi Stela** (~2 m): Lanzón god after centuries of evolution as forward-facing **[[staff-god|Staff God]]** with dual *vara* uniting dual spheres of society and cosmos; Moseley concurs with Lumbreras that it stood above/behind the black-and-white portal. Greater focus on one paramount deity than multi-mound Casma/Supe pantheons.
- **Tello Obelisk:** dual caymans with manioc, gourd, pepper, other cultigens — possible mythical crop donors.

**Horizon media (not exported stone monuments):**

- Cult exported as word/pilgrimage model (Burger; later Pachacamac secondary-oracle analogy), not as local stone/ceramic export; pure Janabarriu pottery only near type site; influence via stirrup-spout shapes and plastic ornament (combing, rocker-stamping, circle rows, feline mouths).
- **Textiles:** heddle weaving replaces twining for ornate cloth; coastal cotton + dyed camelid wool; tapestry, discontinuous warps, painted/tie-dye/batik — Conklin: Chavín-related coastal fabrics lack regional ceramic-style distinctions = purest horizon marker. **Karwa** (Paracas Peninsula): several hundred painted cotton panels, ≥25 Staff Gods, often female-gendered (possible satellite oracle).
- **Metallurgy:** Early Horizon northern revolution — soldering, sweat welding, repoussé, Au-Ag-Cu alloys — hallmark of northern Peru tradition vs Titicaca and Ecuador/Colombia.
- **Obsidian:** use at Chavín increased five-hundred-fold in Janabarriu; >90% from Huancavelica ~470 km south.
- Iconography includes San Pedro cactus-carrying supernaturals (figure caption, Moseley).

**Geographic limits (Moseley):** northern frontier Sechura Desert; highlands Pacopampa; eastern Kotosh Chavín Phase veneer; coast diminishes south of Río Mala (limited Ocucaje); sierra south to Atalla (Huancavelica) and Chupas (Ayacucho).

**Linguistic / genetic:** none in ingested sources.

## Distribution and Chronology

Northern–central Peru Early Horizon. Droughts: coastal Initial Period centers abandoned from ~900 BC (Titicaca sediment cores); second protracted drought ~400–200 BC concurrent with cult spread (contributing/enabling mobility and class formation — not sole `caused_by`). Scarre handbook dates align on 400–200 BC horizon core; Moseley’s sanctuary foundation ~800 BC is earlier than horizon onset by definition.

Antecedents: Sechín Alto, U-shaped centers, Kotosh chambers, *plazas hundidas*. Related but distinct: Pukara (Titicaca archaic state); [[paracas-culture]] (southern sphere with limited Chavín ceramic contact).

## Lifeways

Burger residential data (via Moseley): during 400–200 BC drought, people closer to the Castillo enjoyed better diet and more camelid protein than settlement-margin residents — social differentiation. **Kuntur Wasi** shaft tombs with gold crowns, earspools, pectorals (kuraka-type offerings) plus Paracas necropolis gentry = spreading class formation; Moseley partly invokes circumscription theory under drought. Not primarily a residential urbanism model like later Moche capital — pilgrimage/oracle center with growing attendant population.

## Relations and Successions

- Follows Initial Period coastal collapse and highland persistence of U/sunken-court forms.
- Precedes EIP [[moche-culture]] (secular kuraka-oriented coastal ideology contrast) and [[nazca-culture]] / Paracas–Nazca southern trajectory.
- Staff God revitalized at Tiwanaku Gateway of the Sun (Moseley; Williams via Scarre) — `associated_with`, not unbroken ethnic identity (`preceded_by`/`followed_by` for time only where appropriate).
- Dual-organization / tinku architecture links to [[dual-organization]].

## Identity Cautions

Chavín ≠ empire ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ Paracas ≠ Moche. Horizon markers are stylistic/ideological; pure Janabarriu ceramics do not blanket the influence zone. Karwa female Staff Gods do not make Paracas "Chavín people." Cerro Sechín dismemberment imagery is Initial Period, not Chavín proper — comparative links only.
