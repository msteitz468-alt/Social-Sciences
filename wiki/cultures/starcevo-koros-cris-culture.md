# Starčevo–Körös–Criș Culture

Early Neolithic archaeological complex of the **central Balkans and Carpathian basin** (Starčevo / Körös / Criș facies), defined by early farming material culture and settlement forms of the first generations after farming’s appearance in SE Europe. **Etic construct** spanning national research traditions (Serbian Starčevo, Hungarian Körös, Romanian Criș).

## Definition and Construct History

Culture-historical label for related early Neolithic assemblages on the Hungarian plain and adjacent Balkans. Scaffold densified from *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996|Whittle 1996]]* Ch. 3 (and related SE chapters) — full material signature and chronology to be integrated from claims.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material:** Early pottery traditions, mixed farming economy with SW Asian domesticates, settlement patterns ranging from tells to open sites (regional variation). Details attributed from Whittle and predecessors.

**Linguistic / genetic:** No identity equation with language or genes. Four-way non-identity.

## Distribution and Chronology

Hungarian plain and central Balkans in the early–middle 6th millennium BC (calibrated horizon as of mid-1990s syntheses). Related to Thessalian and Karanovo early Neolithic sequences by interaction and shared farming package elements — not automatic ethnic identity.

## Lifeways

Early farming communities with forager-derived social values emphasized in Whittle’s indigenous-lean narrative (attributed). See [[farming-spread-europe-debate]].

## Relations and Successions

- **associated_with:** early SE European farming, [[karanovo]], [[vinca-culture]] (successor/neighbour).
- **Upstream package:** SW Asian domesticates via Greece/Balkans.
- **Downstream:** middle Neolithic tell and Vinča worlds.

## Identity Cautions

Pottery facies ≠ people ≠ language ≠ genes. National naming (Starčevo/Körös/Criș) reflects research history, not three separate ethnē.

## Historiography

Culture-historical Balkan archaeology; densified here first via Whittle 1996 Cambridge World Archaeology survey.

## Whittle densification (1996)

*(All claims attributed to [[whittle-alasdair|Alasdair Whittle]], *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996|Europe in the Neolithic]]* 1996, mainly Ch. 3 with plain sequence notes in Ch. 4. Chronology mid-1990s cal BC; pre-aDNA. Material-culture complex ≠ people ≠ language ≠ genes.)*

### Chronology and distribution (as of Whittle 1996)

Whittle places Neolithic presence as far as the Hungarian plain from around **~6000 BC** onward (as of his radiocarbon framework), after Macedonia/Crete soon after 7000 and Thessaly by ~6500. He maps many sites in Thessaly and the Maritsa valley; scatters up Struma/Vardar, down Morava into the Danube; **Starčevo** classic on the left bank near Belgrade; further into southern Hungary and the **Tisza–Körös–Criș** network; forest-steppe east of the Carpathians—patterns formed **gradually over generations**, people widespread but not everywhere.

### Settlement form: mobility and thin ribbon sites

Whittle reports early Tisza–Körös–Criș occupations as often **thin, ribbon-like** on terrace/island edges above floodplains—repeated shifting occupations; large pits recurrent. Possibilities of **movement, impermanence, and seasonal shifts** are clearest here (though not unique). He contrasts northerly Starčevo–Körös brief occupations and **pit-dwellings/huts** suited to mobility with more solid southern tell architecture ([[karanovo]], Anza)—the tell as a product of distinctive relationships, not an inevitable Neolithic lifestyle (**position**). Distinguishes dispersed single-level “flat” sites from ordered tells; warns against automatic sedentism equations.

### Material and subsistence (attributed)

Early SE package he summarises for this world: small communities; river/stream/old lake-basin locations; abundant often painted pottery—but **Starčevo–Körös–Criș** with **less painting and more impressed/incised** decoration vs painted Greece/central-southern Bulgaria (Karanovo I–II); Anza phase I white-on-red then Starčevo style. Pots more for preparation/presentation of food/drink than cooking (**position**). Sheep/goat usually most common among domesticates and killed young (imported fauna—not indigenous SE stock); **local domestication of cattle and pigs** likely; at Starčevo-type spectra, larger quantities of wild game, birds, and fish; Körös fish drying/cleaning noted. Floodwater/seasonally wet plots weaken any hard cereal–full-sedentism link (**theory**).

### Transition models and the Gorges

Whittle’s preferred model for SE beginnings is **indigenous forager acculturation**: mobile foragers adopt SW Asian-derived herding, crops, and potting to support an existing sharing ethic—not independent local domestication of sheep/cereals, and not a complex pre-Neolithic sedentary Gorges “village” model he finds chronologically and interpretively weak (see [[lepenski-vir]]). Compromise if pure indigenous reading fails: **limited sea colonisation** into later dense areas (Thessaly, central-southern Bulgaria) with **population continuity** around including much of the Danube valley and Tisza–Körös network; porous frontier exchanges of foodstuffs, know-how, marriage partners; DNA research (as of 1996) he judged unlikely to yield clear descent patterns—**pre-aDNA silence**, later aDNA must be date-stamped separately.

He reinterprets spectacular late Iron Gates developments as possible **conceptual resistance** (sacred centre) either to incoming Neolithic populations or to indigenous acculturation of neighbours—unsuccessful; later phase III reoccupation with tradition dislocation (**position**).

### Downstream plain sequence (Ch.4 notes)

Post–Starčevo–Körös on the N Hungarian plain: from ~5500 BC Linear Pottery settlement to northern margins (still dispersed); after Szakálhát, earlier 5th mill. Tisza–Herpály mounds + open sites; after ~4500 BC Tiszapolgár re-dispersal. Vinča trajectory and plain cycles are compared, not equated (see [[vinca-culture]]). Criș then LBK east of the Carpathians he allows as possible secondary colonisation compatible with earlier SE acculturation (**position**).

### Identity cautions (Whittle layer)

Ceramic facies and culture names are etic constructs; goods and styles cross “cultural” boundaries; no equation with language, genes, or self-conscious people. National Starčevo / Körös / Criș naming is research history, not three ethnē.
