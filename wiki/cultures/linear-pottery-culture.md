# Linear Pottery Culture (LBK)

Central European early Neolithic archaeological culture (*Linearbandkeramik*, LBK) defined by **band-decorated pottery**, timber long houses, and loess-oriented farming with cattle emphasis. Naming ancestor of Childe’s [[danubian-i-culture|Danubian I]] discussions; modern LBK is the operational construct in [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]].

## Definition and Construct History

By **5500 BC**, Mithen states, a new farming culture emerged from the fringes of the Hungarian plain — the Linearbandkeramik — and spread with astonishing speed east into Ukraine and west across Poland, Germany, the Low Countries, and eastern France. Defined by band-decorated pottery, three-aisled timber long houses with wattle-and-daub, and cattle-rich mixed farming. Culture-historical archaeology (including Childe’s Danubian sequence) earlier mapped the same phenomenon; LBK is the standard later label. **Etic construct.**

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as of Mithen 2003):**

- **Pottery:** linear band decoration (definitional).
- **Houses:** long houses ~12 m (sometimes much larger); three-aisled posts; household as key social unit; abandoned intact when household dies → low mounds (“dead” houses matching “dead” families); floors often destroyed by later ploughing.
- **Uniformity:** near-identical houses from Cuiry-lès-Chaudardes (Paris basin) to Miskovice (Czech Republic) nearly 1,000 km and centuries apart — frontier mentality explanation (Mithen).
- **Economy:** fertile-soil farming; cattle emphasis; polished stone axes as frontier exchange goods.
- **Burial:** village-adjacent cemeteries; often only tooth enamel survives; ages/sexes together; axes/adzes/arrows/shells with men, grinding stones/awls with women; little evidence of wealthy individuals or elaborate ritual.

**Linguistic:** Renfrew’s claim that proto-Indo-European spread with Neolithic farmers is a **linked debate position**, not a property of LBK pots.

**Genetic:** classical blood-group wave-of-advance (Cavalli-Sforza) vs mtDNA “tiny ripple” (Sykes et al.) as of Mithen 2003 — see debate page; not LBK = one gene pool.

## Distribution and Chronology

- **Origin zone:** Hungarian plain fringes ~5500 BC.
- **Spread:** ~25 km per generation westwards by filling fertile soils then leap-frogging poorer ones (Mithen); east to Ukraine.
- **Frontier:** contact with late Mesolithic foragers including [[ertebolle-culture|Ertebølle]]-related groups ~4400 BC (exchange, intermarriage scenarios).

## Lifeways (Settlement and Subsistence)

Village farmers on loess; household production; polished-axe exchange at forest frontiers; cemeteries without strong hierarchy signals in Mithen’s sketch. Relationship to indigenous Mesolithic people is the core of [[farming-spread-europe-debate]] (Zvelebil indigeneity thesis vs migration models).

## Relations and Successions

- **Upstream:** Greek/Balkan early farming from western Asia (boats with seed-corn and livestock ~7500 BC).
- **Parallel label:** [[danubian-i-culture]] (Childe 1929 culture-historical form).
- **Broader genetic/cultural construct:** [[early-european-farmers]] in later archaeogenetic literature — do not silently equate without attribution.
- **Downstream influence:** Scandinavian early Neolithic long barrows said to echo LBK long houses (Mithen).

## Significance

LBK is the type-case of rapid temperate-European agricultural expansion and the empirical core of demic vs cultural diffusion arguments for the European Neolithic.

## Identity Cautions

- Pottery+long-house package ≠ single ethnicity, language, or migration event.
- Zvelebil’s indigenous-descendant claim and immigrant-wave claims are **positions** — both can be partly true regionally.
- Four-way non-identity strict.
- Do not merge LBK with all “first farmers of Europe” (Greece and Mediterranean Cardial differ).

## Historiography

Childe Danubian I; mid-20th-c. LBK systematics; processual settlement archaeology; Zvelebil Mesolithic continuity school; Cavalli-Sforza genetic geography; Sykes mtDNA critique; Renfrew language-farming hypothesis — all refracted through Mithen 2003’s popular synthesis. Post-2010 aDNA transformed the debate — densify from later sources with explicit date-stamps; do not overwrite this page’s 2003 horizon silently.

### Sherratt densification (1997)

*(Attributed — [[sherratt-andrew]], *[[sherratt-economy-society-prehistoric-europe-1997]], Ch.2–3, 10–12. )* Early loess/moist-soil horticultural restriction; groundwater fixed-plot models; later secondary-products horizons restructure settlement beyond primary LBK/AVK village patterns on the Hungarian Plain (AVK split, regional styles, Bükk trade). Bandkeramik opium-poppy associations appear in Ch.16 intoxicants discussion — botanical claims as of his cited evidence. Culture ≠ people/language/genes.

### Renfrew 1987 — rapid farming transformation in the wave of advance

*(Attributed — [[renfrew-colin]], *[[renfrew-archaeology-and-language-1987|Archaeology and Language]]*).* LBK figures as a **rapid** regional transformation within the European farming wave of advance (contrast slow Scandinavian neolithic). One of several named early-farming cultural transformations he maps onto a speculative linguistic genealogy under the [[anatolian-hypothesis]] — association only, not culture=language identity. Demic preference over pure mesolithic creation of LBK (contra Zvelebil-type Position B on [[farming-spread-europe-debate]]).
