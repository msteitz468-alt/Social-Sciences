## Definition and Construct History

*Homo ergaster* (“work man”) is [[tattersall-ian|Ian Tattersall]]’s preferred name for **early African *Homo erectus*-grade** hominids — the first fossils he treats as clearly meriting membership in genus *Homo* on a modern-leaning body plan. In [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012|*Masters of the Planet* (2012)]], the name is traced to a **~1.5 Ma East Turkana mandible (1975)**; the iconic specimens are the **KNM-ER 3733** cranium (East Turkana, ~1.8 Ma, found 1975) and especially the nearly complete adolescent skeleton **KNM-WT 15000** (“**Turkana Boy**” / Nariokotome), west Lake Turkana, ~**1.6 Ma** (found 1984).

**Synonymy debate with [[homo-erectus]].** Eugène Dubois’s 1894 *Pithecanthropus* (now *Homo*) *erectus* from Trinil, Java, anchors the classic Asian species (long low vault, strong brow, ~950 cc skullcap; Trinil ~1 Ma–700 ka; eastern Asian group including Peking Man ~1.8 Ma to as recently as ~40 ka in Tattersall’s 2012 frame). Ernst Mayr’s mid-century “middle stage” concept encouraged motley 1.9–0.4 Ma attributions by intermediate age. **Tattersall prefers *Homo ergaster* for early African forms** rather than folding them automatically into worldwide *H. erectus* (attributed naming preference). This page treats *H. ergaster* as Tattersall’s **African early erectus-grade taxon**; relationship to Asian *H. erectus* is **`associated_with` / grade continuity**, not asserted identity unless a given author synonymizes them. Construct status **contested** reflects that live split-vs-lump dispute ([[species-concept]]).

**What counts as *Homo*.** Tattersall holds that fossils truly meriting *Homo* appear after ~2 Ma with features shared specifically with later humans; he is unconvinced by expansive *H. habilis* / “early *Homo*” allocations of australopith-grade anatomy. Wood & Collard (1999), as he reports, working outward from *H. sapiens*, would exclude *habilis*/*rudolfensis* from *Homo* — reinforcing his sense that *ergaster* is where the genus becomes morphologically coherent (positions recorded, not adjudicated).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

### Cranium and face

**KNM-ER 3733** (until mid-1980s the iconic early *ergaster* cranium): face **not strongly projecting** like an ape; somewhat **protruding nose** (departure from flat midface of apes/australopiths); vault ~**850 cc**, above KNM-ER 1470, near younger Trinil; with other East Turkana material shows that by **1.8 Ma** some East African hominids had reached a new grade exemplified by *H. erectus* — “something that deserved consideration as a member of the genus *Homo*” (as of Tattersall 2012).

### Turkana Boy skeleton (KNM-WT 15000)

- Died face-down in lakeside swampy mud ~1.6 Ma; covered before scavengers — first time a single *H. ergaster* individual’s full build known; only reasonably complete East African *ergaster* skeleton.
- **~5′3″ at death**; if modern growth schedule, projected ~6′1″ / ~150 lb adult — but **dental growth increments** under microscopy indicate only **~8 years old** despite tooth eruption/bone knitting like a modern ~12-year-old → **fast, more apelike developmental schedule**; already completed most growth; improbable he would have hit six feet. Still tall/slim relative to australopiths.
- Died with **~880 cc** brain near adult size for the individual.
- **Obligate biped**: long legs, near-modern proportions; contrasts with Lucy’s short-legged, climbing-compatible package; arboreal “have your cake and eat it” ambiguities gone; committed to upright striding far from tree shelter ([[bipedalism]]).
- Pelvis **narrower** as legs lengthened; arms longish vs modern humans but far from apes; shoulder socket faced out (not up) but a bit more forward — limited throwing speculation only.
- Few hand/foot bones on the Boy; large East Turkana footprints confirm long stride and basically modern foot ~**1.5 Ma**.
- Limb shafts with **much thicker walls** than modern humans (including ancient hunter-gatherer *H. sapiens*) → immense strength / higher activity; shaft-wall thickness has “plummeted” since (as of Tattersall 2012).

### Development and brain growth

Rapid developmental timetable treated as typical for *H. ergaster*/*H. erectus* (Java juvenile *H. erectus* ~1 year old already ~72–84% of adult species brain average — ape-like pattern) (as of Tattersall 2012). Adult brains larger absolutely than bipedal apes but not much larger relative to greater body size; less elevated above the mammalian brain–body curve than *H. sapiens*. Tattersall: for all physical similarities to us, on the cognitive side they were **neither bipedal apes nor modern humans** (attributed).

### Speech and symbol (negative evidence)

- Endocast outlines **Broca’s area** on left frontal lobe → once fueled speech speculation; Tattersall notes Broca’s area also serves memory/executive functions; one necessary feature ≠ speech.
- Thoracic vertebral canal **average for a primate** (unlike unusually broad canal in modern humans and Neanderthals, linked to fine breathing control for speech); alternative reading pathological narrowing is controversial.
- **No archaeological evidence** of symbolic mental manipulation associated with the *ergaster*/*erectus* group across its long tenure (as of Tattersall 2012). Language affiliation: **unknown / not modern**.

### Body-plan origin thesis

Tattersall finds **no convincing morphological intermediate** between australopith/“early *Homo*” and the Turkana Boy — discordant with Evolutionary Synthesis gradualism. He hypothesizes a **minor regulatory/timing mutation** altering gene expression, opening new adaptive avenues (Goldschmidt “hopeful monster” rehabilitated via stickleback and related examples) — few intermediates expected on fossil timescales (**thesis**, not sequenced genetics) (attributed, Tattersall 2012).

## Distribution and Chronology

- **East Africa core:** Turkana Basin ([[koobi-fora]] East Turkana; Nariokotome west); related early erectus-grade material across East Africa. Timeline table: first *Homo* of modern body proportions **1.9–1.6 Ma** (as of Tattersall 2012).
- **Out of Africa 1:** hominids leave Africa to [[dmanisi]] (Georgia) ~**1.8 Ma** with small brains (600–775 cc), short stature (~4′10″–5′2″), modern-leaning body form closer to the Boy than to australopiths, and **Oldowan-like** tools — not improved technology. Java dates ~1.8–1.6 Ma for *H. erectus* jointly put early exit beyond reasonable doubt in Tattersall’s 2012 frame (attributed). Dmanisi fossils are usually discussed under *H. erectus*/*Homo georgicus* labels; anatomical comparison to African *ergaster* is `associated_with`, not automatic synonymy.
- Environments at ~1.6 Ma: grasslands widespread but Serengeti-style open savannas still hundreds of ka ahead; **mosaic** of grassland patches, trees, gallery forest, lake-margin swamp.

## Lifeways

### Technology

Initially, Turkana Boy’s kin made stone tools **identical in concept** to those at Gona almost a million years earlier — technological **stasis** despite new body type and larger brains ([[oldowan-industry]]). **[[acheulean-industry|Acheulean]]** handaxes appear in Africa ~**1.78 Ma** (as of Tattersall 2012) — **after** *H. ergaster* appears; invention within a pre-existing species, neural potential present before expression (exaptation pattern Tattersall stresses). Olorgesailie and other landscapes show Acheulean production at scale later; raw-material cognition (“see” form in the rock) is not equated with modern symbolic processing.

### Diet, gut, and fire

Narrow-hipped *ergaster* (vs australopith broad abdomens) implies **gut reduction** as trade-off for brain expansion → high-yield foods (animal proteins/fats among savanna mammals) even early in expansion (attributed). Endurance-running / persistence-hunting scenarios reported as research positions requiring tracking cognition and hairless sweating; molecular-clock estimates on head vs pubic lice (~3–4 Ma split) used to argue body-hair loss well before the Boy (parasite data “may be a bit controversial”). Water-transport problem: no direct evidence of containers; Turkana-Boy-era activity sites typically **near water**.

**[[control-of-fire]]:** direct evidence for *H. ergaster* **almost entirely circumstantial** — singed bones ~1.8 Ma Swartkrans; scorched clay balls ~1.4 Ma Chesowanja at campfire temperatures but not definitive controlled fire; earliest substantial hearth ash layers ~**800 ka** Israeli site (as of Tattersall 2012). Tattersall still finds a “**mildly compelling**” case that cooking/fire enabled the savanna lifestyle (predator deterrent; huddling sociality) — **thesis**, not demonstrated hearths at 1.6 Ma.

### Sociality and vulnerability

Poor climbers without big jaws/slashing canines faced diverse savanna predators with only modestly improved toolkit; one Kenyan *ergaster* frontal shows **carnivore tooth-marks** above an orbit. Tattersall infers transcendence of chimp-level social limits and reduced group size relative to high-predation australopith troops (predator ecology: prey outnumber predators); group size guesses (“a dozen? twenty?”) remain speculative; male–female bonds for infant support hypothetical; mothers likely lacked fur for clinging infants. Midwifery from narrower pelvis + neonatal head remains **speculation**.

## Relations and Successions

- **Preceded (chronologically / competitively) by** [[australopithecus]] and gracile/“early *Homo*” forms including [[homo-habilis]]; australopiths (including [[paranthropus]]) linger after recognizable *Homo* and eventually lose out (Tattersall thesis).
- **`associated_with` [[homo-erectus]]** as the broader erectus-grade; African early material = *ergaster* in Tattersall’s preference; Asian classic = *erectus*; Dmanisi small-brained dispersers compared to both.
- **Coexistence:** Ileret and related Turkana Basin finds show multi-lineage *Homo* after 1.6 Ma under various labels — Tattersall criticizes dumping diverse crania into a single worldwide *H. erectus* middle-grade construct ([[species-concept]]).
- Technology path: [[oldowan-industry]] → later [[acheulean-industry]] within African early *Homo* populations.
- Does **not** equal language-bearing modern humans; symbolic behavior postponed to much later *H. sapiens* narrative in this book.

## Identity Cautions

*Homo ergaster* is a **taxonomic preference** for African early erectus-grade fossils — an etic morphological construct, **not** a people, language, archaeological culture, or genetically sampled population. **Do not equate** *H. ergaster* with [[homo-erectus]] as identity; use `associated_with` / grade language unless a source explicitly synonymizes. **Do not equate** the taxon with Oldowan or Acheulean industries — tools are invented by individuals within pre-existing species (Tattersall’s rule) and first appear with bipedal apes, not with *Homo*. Turkana Boy is **one adolescent skeleton**, not a population mean. Speech, midwifery, endurance hunting, and habitual fire remain **inferred or speculative** layers over weak direct evidence (as of Tattersall 2012). Four-way non-identity enforced.

## Historiography

Naming and splitting African vs Asian erectus-grade fossils tracks larger fights over Mayr-style single-species gradualism vs bushy phylogenies that Tattersall champions throughout *Masters of the Planet*. The 1984 Turkana Boy discovery transformed body-plan knowledge beyond cranial “grade” arguments based on 3733 alone. Subsequent debate has continued over growth age (the ~8-year dental estimate), speech anatomy (vertebral canal), and whether *ergaster* should be sunk into *H. erectus*. Parallel wiki layers on [[homo-erectus]] (Leakey & Lewin 1977; Klein 1999; Stringer; Wenke & Olszewski) already note African *ergaster* vs Asian *erectus* distinctions — this page is the dedicated Tattersall-2012 *ergaster* node.
