## Definition and Construct History

*Orrorin tugenensis* is a ~**six-million-year-old** fossil taxon discovered in **northern Kenya in 2000** and popularized as “**Millennium Man**.” [[tattersall-ian|Ian Tattersall]] presents it in [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012|*Masters of the Planet* (2012)]] alongside [[sahelanthropus]] and [[ardipithecus]] as one of the late-Miocene/early-Pliocene candidates for early membership in the human family. The known sample comprises **fragmentary jaws and teeth** plus **limb bones** that are **believed (not demonstrated)** to belong to the same species (as of Tattersall 2012).

Like other “earliest hominid” claims of this period, *Orrorin* is judged largely on whether its postcrania support **upright locomotion** — the criterion Tattersall says most unites current earliest-hominid contenders after dental criteria proved non-unique to Hominidae (attributed, Tattersall 2012). Molecular systematists usually place the human–chimpanzee split ~**5–7 Ma** (gorillas peeling off a couple of million years earlier); *Orrorin* at ~6 Ma sits inside that window (as of Tattersall 2012).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Dentition (as of Tattersall 2012).** Thick-enameled, squarish, not-too-large **molars**; **small upper canine**. These dental features align with traditional “hominid” dental markers (thick enamel, reduced canine), but Tattersall stresses such markers can be matched outside Hominidae.

**Postcrania and locomotion.** **Femora** are broken at the critical morphology for diagnosing bipedality but are described as **consistent with upright locomotion**. The **humerus** shows **strong climbing-muscle attachment**; a **curved finger bone** indicates retained arboreal capacity. The resulting picture is a creature combining possible ground bipedality with climbing ability — not an obligate savanna strider like later [[homo-ergaster]].

**No genetic or linguistic signature.** No tools or other material culture are attributed to *Orrorin* in this source.

## Distribution and Chronology

Found at a **number of localities** in northern Kenya dated to about **six million years ago** (as of Tattersall 2012). Paleoenvironmental reading: **dryish evergreen forest**; associated fauna noted for **absence of grassland ruminants** — reinforcing Tattersall’s point that very early putative hominids are recovered from **wooded or mixed** settings rather than open grassland forced bipedality (as of Tattersall 2012).

## Lifeways

Direct behavioral evidence is absent. Inference from habitat and anatomy (attributed, Tattersall): a forest-dwelling primate with climbing specializations and possible upright terrestrial locomotion; diet not detailed beyond thick-enameled molars consistent with relatively hard or abrasive plant foods. No stone-tool association.

## Relations and Successions

- Chronologically between claimed [[sahelanthropus]] (~7 Ma) and [[ardipithecus]] (*Ar. kadabba* 5.8–5.2 Ma; *Ar. ramidus* 4.4 Ma).
- Tattersall treats association of jaws/teeth with limb bones as **belief, not demonstration** — a caution for single-species reconstruction.
- Later, *Australopithecus anamensis* (~4.2–3.9 Ma) is, for Tattersall, the earliest form **beyond a shadow of doubt** specialized for upright bipedality; that package is more securely known than *Orrorin*’s incomplete femora (see [[australopithecus]], [[bipedalism]]).
- No asserted linear descent *Orrorin* → later hominids; early diversity and possible multiple upright lineages are the framing thesis (attributed).

## Identity Cautions

*Orrorin* is a **fragmentary morphological construct**, not a people, language, or genetically defined population. “Millennium Man” is media/popular labeling, not an ethnonym. **Same-species association of dental and postcranial elements is not demonstrated** (as of Tattersall 2012). Bipedality is **consistent with** femoral morphology but not sealed by complete diagnostic anatomy. Do not equate *Orrorin* with [[australopithecus]] or with any living population. Four-way non-identity applies.

## Historiography

Discovered in 2000, *Orrorin* is part of the early-twenty-first-century expansion of the pre-australopith record that Tattersall contrasts with the pre-1990s ceiling of ~3–4 Ma. It enters debates over bipedality criteria, forest vs. savanna origin stories, and species association from mixed elements.
