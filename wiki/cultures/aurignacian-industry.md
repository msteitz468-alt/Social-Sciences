## Definition and Construct History

The **Aurignacian** is a conventional Early Upper Palaeolithic industry complex of Europe (with Near Eastern extensions), classically linked to blade/bladelet technology, bone/antler projectile points, ornaments, and early portable art. Replacement programs treat it as the archaeological proxy for anatomically modern Cro-Magnon entry into Europe; that equation is a **position**, not identity (see [[aurignacian]] concept page; [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

This page holds the industry as culture construct and densifies from *[[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999|The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe]]* (1999). Classificatory/semantic history: [[aurignacian]].

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material.** As of Gamble 1999 synthesis (citing Hahn, Knecht, White, Kozlowski, and others):

- **Bone/antler points** as trademark of undisputed Aurignacian UP — absent from leaf-point assemblages and Mousterian; of 381 early split-based points (France/Belgium/Germany), Knecht found 97% antler; made similarly over large areas (attributed).
- **Blade *chaîne opératoire*** (Geißenklösterle): multi-stage prepared-core production; Aurignacian often opportunistic unipolar on local small nodules (95% local Ach-valley hornstein; only 1% >20 km); Gravettian later more standardized, bipolar, indirect percussion (Hahn via Gamble).
- **Ornaments and music:** earliest deliberate piercing at Bacho Kiro level 11 earlier than Chatelperronian Arcy (White via Gamble); basket-shaped beads ~3 hours each (White experimental); Geißenklösterle bone flutes AMS ~36.8 Kyr bp — possibly oldest musical instruments in Europe (Hahn & Münzel via Gamble). Formal music/depiction use additional **symbolic** resources stretching social life beyond knapping’s mutual environment (Gamble position).
- **Shells and distant transfers:** EUP shells regularly exceed the ~100 km landscape-of-habit limit (e.g. Kostenki Aurignacian shells from Black Sea ~500 km) (Floss, Taborin via Gamble).

No linguistic or genetic signature attaches to the industry label.

## Distribution and Chronology

### Gamble 1999 chronology and the 33 Kyr hinge (attributed)

*Source: [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999]], [[gamble-clive]].*

Gamble’s outline (compiling Allsworth-Jones, Anikovich, Kozlowski, Oliva, Rigaud, Svoboda, et al.) places earliest UP ~45–33 Kyr bp (Proto-Aurignacian/Bachokirian SE ~43; earliest Aurignacian MW 43–33; later Aurignacian all regions 33–25). Division at **33 Kyr bp** follows Desbrosse and Kozlowski 1988. Gamble’s own load-bearing chronological claim: after **33 Kyr** the record changes not only in regional space–time material-culture constellations but in the **structure of locales as social occasions**; two structural changes he flags are **settlement hierarchies** and **pronounced inter- and intra-regional material-culture variation** (landscape of habit vs social landscape distinction developed in Ch.7) (attributed).

The period **60–33 Kyr** is a long, **polythetic** transition far from “human revolution” expectations — whether read as replacement or continuity (Clarke 1968; Stringer & Gamble 1993 via Gamble 1999). Kozlowski multi-aspectual model (via Gamble): considerable continuity 60–30 Kyr; after ~30 Kyr a definite rupture — only four aspects cross that timeline including UP blade technology and Aurignacian toolkit (attributed).

Hahn C14 medians (via Gamble): transitional technocomplexes ~39 Kyr; earliest Aurignacian ~35 Kyr; Aurignacian I ~31.1 Kyr. Late Mousterian/transitional/Early Aurignacian meridional (N–S) isochrons with older eastern dates; Proto-Aurignacian zonal (rapid movement); east–west Aurignacian migration not yet proved (Straus via Gamble) (attributed).

**Regional notes (as of 1999):** Swabian Alb — Aurignacian earliest UP after Hengelo; low site densities; few open locales near Vogelherd/Geißenklösterle. Empty/infrequently used central Europe may explain early Aurignacian at Geißenklösterle as movement into rarely visited space, not dense competition (Gamble position). North European Plain and much of Italy 30–25 Kyr lack substantial EUP occupation.

## Lifeways

### Transfers and co-residence with late Neanderthals (Gamble 1999, attributed)

- **Raw materials:** earliest UP shifts toward finer-grained flint often from greater distances (l’Arbreda abrupt Aurignacian-over-Mousterian change). Turq Aquitaine: Mousterian + Chatelperronian — no material >16 km >6% of assemblage; earliest Aurignacian transfers greater proportions from further afield (50–80 km up to 30% in flint-poor areas). Bacho Kiro Aurignacian 53% flint from >120 km vs Mousterian level 13 only 13% over same distance (Kozlowski via Gamble).
- **Network structure:** Aurignacian points to high-density all-channel transfer structure and directed transfers (provisioning places / focusing on individuals) vs MP simple distance fall-off; none of these changes environmentally forced (Gamble position). Aurignacian “excels at extension” beyond face-to-face society across a **social landscape** — contrast with Chatelperronian wheel-shaped, high-mobility short-stay patterning (Gamble model).
- **Coexistence:** Chatelperronian coexists with Aurignacian in SW Europe; co-existence minimally ~43–36 Kyr bp; early Cantabrian/Catalan Aurignacian dates may leave Neanderthals in a Dordogne “pocket” while incomers initially skirt them — conditions for imitation/emulation (Hublin et al. via Gamble). Straus/Gamble caution: only significant Chatelperronian fossil is St Césaire — we do not know who made early French/Cantabrian Aurignacian; MP/Chatelperronian/Aurignacian distinctions can blur in Spain.
- **Fauna:** Abri Pataud Aurignacian reindeer hunting does **not** imply organizational capabilities very different from Mauran Mousterian bison (Spiess, Enloe, Patou-Mathis via Gamble) — differences for Gamble lie in social-network resources, not hunting skill alone.
- **Assemblage richness:** break between Chatelperronian and Aurignacian richness (Simek & Price; Harrold via Gamble); major evenness change later Gravettian.

## Identity Cautions

Aurignacian is a **technological–stylistic construct**, not a people, language, or genetic population. Do not silently equate Aurignacian = Cro-Magnon = all European early moderns; fossil–industry links are sparse and regionally uneven. Leaf-point “transitional” assemblages and Bachokirian labeling show polythetic boundaries.

## Historiography

Classic culture-history and replacement archaeology used Aurignacian as Moderns’ European package (Stringer & Gamble 1993 layer on [[aurignacian]]). Gamble 1999 reframes it within network scales: release from proximity, extended networks, social landscape after the ~33 Kyr hinge — without reducing the transition to pure population replacement. Dates and site associations heavily revised post-1999 (AMS, Bayesian modelling, re-excavation); retain 1999 apparatus as dated position.
