# Globular Amphora Culture

Late Neolithic / Copper Age archaeological culture of north-central and eastern Europe, named from **globular amphora** pottery. In [[sherratt-andrew|Andrew Sherratt]]’s *[[sherratt-economy-society-prehistoric-europe-1997|Economy and Society in Prehistoric Europe]]* framing, Globular Amphora (GAC) forms a **Hamburg–Kiev band** after ~3500 BC that, together with Pit-Grave/[[yamnaya-culture|Yamnaya]] steppe packages, reorganises former Cucuteni–Tripolye and North European Plain worlds and helps set conditions for [[corded-ware-culture|Corded Ware]] dispersed tumulus societies.

## Definition and Construct History

GAC is a conventional culture-historical construct (pottery form, burial practices including animal deposits, settlement signatures). Sherratt uses it as a **horizon actor** in the post-3500 BC east–west reorientation of temperate Europe: not a single migrating “people,” but a package network linking northern plains and forest-steppe margins. Gimbutas wave models treat GAC as amalgam under Kurgan dominance — a different equation; keep positions separate (see [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]]).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (Sherratt-attributed):**

- **Globular amphora** ceramic form; long-distance stylistic cohesion across a Hamburg–Kiev band.
- **Paired / double ox burials** as draught-signal within the wider SPR traction complex (with Baden and related).
- **Spatial politics:** participation in dividing former Cucuteni–Tripolye territory with Pit-Grave groups after ~3500 BC (Sherratt intro synthesis).
- **Drinking-vessel chain:** Troy II–Baden–GAC–Corded Ware–Bell Beaker liquid vessels as a demand for explanation (alcohol package vs milk after vessel-function split).

**Linguistic / genetic:** not established in Sherratt’s essays. He explicitly refuses simple culture = language equations in the Indo-European chapter with E.S. Sherratt. Later aDNA studies (post-1997) must be attributed and date-stamped separately — never silently equated with GAC pots.

## Distribution and Chronology

Sherratt’s band model: after 3500 BC, GAC stretches roughly **Hamburg to Kiev**, overlapping and succeeding late TRB zones and abutting steppe Pit-Grave expansions. Chronology sits between mid-fourth-millennium traction horizon and third-millennium Corded Ware crystallisation (regional absolute dates vary).

## Lifeways

Read as more mobile / livestock-emphasising than mature tell Neolithic or early TRB horticultural cores; ox traction and amphora-hosted liquids participate in the secondary-products and hospitality packages. Not full steppe pastoral nomadism — a forest-plain hybrid in Sherratt’s economic geography.

## Identity Cautions

- **GAC ≠ Indo-Europeans.** Association claims are positions (Gimbutas, others), not wiki-voice facts.
- **GAC ≠ Yamnaya.** Concurrent and interacting packages; Sherratt keeps steppe Sub-Neolithic + Caucasian/Near Eastern innovations as a distinct Pit-Grave formation pathway.
- Animal burials are ritual/economic signals, not automatic proof of full pastoral mode of production.
- Four-way non-identity: pots ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ people.
