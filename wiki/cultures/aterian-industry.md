# Aterian Industry

## Definition and Construct History

The **Aterian** is a North African / Saharan mode-3 (prepared-core) lithic industry conventionally distinguished by **tanged or pedunculated tools** and often associated with desert and desert-margin settlement. In [[phillipson-african-archaeology-2005]], [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] places the Aterian within the pan-African trend toward regional specialisation after the Acheulean/Sangoan, under the former “Middle Stone Age” / Middle Palaeolithic terminological umbrella (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Material Signature

Tanged points and related forms are the classic diagnostic; technology is mode-3 prepared-core (J. G. D. Clark) rather than a unique inventiveness package (as of Phillipson 2005). As with other named industries, definitional boundaries are conventional and may cut continuous variation (Phillipson’s general industry-construct caution).

## Distribution and Chronology

Phillipson’s continental survey places Aterian occurrences across the Sahara and North Africa, stratified over earlier industries at some oasis and desert-margin sites (e.g. Egyptian Western Desert contexts he cites). Many ages lie beyond secure radiocarbon; treat chronologies as **approximate** and regionally contested (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Lifeways

Associated with hunter-gatherer settlement under fluctuating late Pleistocene aridity; specific subsistence packages are site-dependent and not generalised to the industry construct in Phillipson’s synthesis.

## Relations and Successions

- Parallel to other African mode-3 industries (e.g. southern African MSA sequences including [[howiesons-poort-industry]]).
- Not to be equated with a language family or genetic population.
- Northern Africanists often use European/Mediterranean period terms (Middle Palaeolithic) for the same technological horizon (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Identity Cautions

Aterian ≠ “the first modern humans of North Africa” as an ethnic identity. Fossils and industries must be linked with `associated_with` language only when the source argues association.

## Historiography

## Oxford Handbook densification (Mitchell & Lane 2013)

*Attributed — Elena Garcea (Ch. 29), Christian Tryon (Ch. 8) in [[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]; **as of handbook ~2012**.*

Garcea defines the **Aterian** as a widespread North African regional unit comprising **tanged and ventrally retouched points**, bulbar basal thinning, and bifacial retouch; it is almost absent from the Nile Valley. Earliest Moroccan dates ~**122–121 kya**; Adrar Bous span ~**150–45 kya** assumed; recent dates concur the Aterian ends by ~**40 kya** at the latest. Morphological continuity, Garcea argues, links Early MSA makers (Jebel Irhoud, Haua Fteah) to late MSA Aterian makers; Irhoud similarities with Skhul/Qafzeh support out-of-Africa authorship. No Middle/Upper Pleistocene Sahara fossils, so Saharan Early MSA makers are inferred as anatomically modern humans who crossed the Sahara to Mediterranean coasts (**position**, as of handbook ~2012). Industry ≠ genetic population ≠ language family.

On arid adaptation, Garcea reports Kharga/Dakhleh long sequences from Late Acheulean through Early MSA to abundant Aterian (Kharga easternmost abundant zone; Dakhleh <100 to >40 kya). Aterian foragers appear in **drier spells** than Early MSA predecessors, settling near scarce water (Bir Tarfawi/Bir Sahara; Uan Tabu Aterian deposit OSL 61±10 kya). The Sahara was largely abandoned after ~60 kya until early Holocene reoccupation, often at the same sites. At Jebel Gharbi, Aterian ~60±20 kya U/Th lasting to ~40 kya, with aquifer springs enabling occupation in dry periods; demographic contraction ~40–20 kya across most of North Africa.

Garcea endorses the Brooks/McBrearty/Barham–Mitchell view that African MSA toolkits differ from European Middle Palaeolithic mainly by scarcity of side-scrapers and importance of **points as projectile armatures** of composite tools — expressing cognitive sophistication and regional social organization, with North Africa sharing point production while expressing characteristic cultural units (Aterian as one such identity package). Tryon’s inventory figure (Ch. 8) uses an Aterian point from Oued Djebbana/Bir-el-Ater as the type exemplar.

### Tattersall — *Masters of the Planet* (2012)

*Source: [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012]], [[tattersall-ian]]. **As of Tattersall 2012.** Industry ≠ species ≠ definitive Out-of-Africa population.*

**Definition and Sahara conduit.** Tattersall treats the Aterian (after Bir el Ater, Algeria) as an MSA variant with unique types, notably **tanged points** (hafted spear tips; conceivably late arrowheads). Long thought recent; by 2012 known at quite ancient sites → speculation that earliest producers played a role in initial *H. sapiens* exodus. Sahara not always a barrier: wettest greening ~**130–120 ka** (last European interglacial) could conduit northward expansion — though he also holds reasons to think Aterians themselves may have remained effectively in Africa longer term (attributed).

**Fossil associations and non-identity.** Generally unwise to associate a particular hominid exclusively with a toolkit. N. African remains so far associated with early Aterian fall in his dubious “archaic *H. sapiens*” category: Dar-es-Soltan II (Morocco) partial cranium maybe ≥110 ka; Contrebandiers child’s cranium reconstructed not standard-issue modern; [[jebel-irhoud]] may be >160 ka with toolkit said to resemble Neanderthal Mousterian though the hominids do **not** look Neanderthal and, for Tattersall, do not present as obvious *H. sapiens* (see site page). Absolute certainty that Aterians or Irhoud people could not exchange genes with mainstream anatomical moderns is unavailable; still, he asserts Aterians “certainly did not play a role in the **definitive** *Homo sapiens* exodus that later populated the world” (**thesis**, Tattersall 2012). Dual-corridor scenario ~120 ka (Aterians east then north across Sinai; sub-Saharan groups via Nile into Levant) with possible interbreeding at Skhul/Qafzeh is floated as speculative scenario, not field consensus.

**Beads and late contraction.** Early putative *Nassarius* beads include a single shell at an Aterian site in Algeria of uncertain age (slender/arguable); firmer bead evidence ~**80 ka** at other Aterian sites (e.g. dozen perforated *Nassarius* at Moroccan Grotte des Pigeons far from shell source — trade suggested; holes not proven human-made though pigment/polish may indicate wear). Extreme aridity ~60 ka severely hit Sahara Aterians; by ~**40 ka** Aterian limited to few Mediterranean coastal outposts (as of Tattersall 2012) — aligns in direction with Garcea’s ~40 kya end date above without equating the two syntheses.
