# Mureybetian

PPNA-horizon archaeological culture of the **middle Euphrates**, defined from [[mureybet|Mureybet]] and related assemblages. [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (2000) treats it as one of three regional cultures (with Sultanian and Aswadian) descending from the [[khiamian|Khiamian]], within Ofer Bar-Yosef’s [[levantine-corridor|Levantine corridor]] — not as a single undifferentiated “PPNA culture.”

## Definition and Construct History

Etic culture-historical unit. Kenyon’s PPNA named a chronological horizon at Jericho; Cauvin and colleagues split contemporary regional facies. Mureybetian = northern corridor facies with continuous sequence claims from forager to farmer at the type-site.

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (as of Cauvin 2000):** middle Euphrates PPNA lithics and architecture; early agricultural evidence in sequence; early bucrania in symbolic repertoire. **Language/genes:** unknown; do not equate.

## Distribution and Chronology

Middle Euphrates northern Syria; Cauvin’s ~9500–8300 BC PPNA band (calibrated as of 2000). Early PPNB derives by internal evolution from Mureybetian in his model.

## Lifeways

Village settlement; hunting-gathering into cultivation along the corridor; symbolic practices including bull deposits.

## Relations and Successions

`preceded_by` Khiamian/Natufian; `succeeded_by` Early PPNB; `associated_with` Sultanian/Aswadian as sibling corridor cultures — not identity.

## Identity Cautions

Not a people or language. Construct status conventional within French/Levantine culture-history.

## Historiography

Anchored in Cauvin’s Mureybet program; flooded type-site limits re-excavation.
