# Kulli Culture

## Definition and Construct History

An archaeological culture of southern Balochistan (Las Bela, Kolwa, and parts
of the Makran), contemporary with the Mature Harappan and defined by
distinctive stone architecture, unique figurines, and painted pottery.
Documented here from [[wright-ancient-indus-2010|Wright (2010)]] (drawing on
Dales 1976, Franke-Vogt 2000, and J.-F. Jarrige). It is an **etic analytical
construct**, not an observed people; whether it is a genuinely separate culture
or a regional variant of the Indus is disputed (see Identity Cautions).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material.** Distinct stone (not brick) construction; unique figurines and
pottery, but incorporating borrowed Indus design elements indicating contact.
Settlements range from large to small; Nindowari reaches about 50 hectares.
Many Kulli sites occupy strategic positions on mountain tops and hills
overlooking valleys and passes (Franke-Vogt 2000).

**Linguistic / Genetic.** Unknown; not equated with any language or population.

## Distribution and Chronology

Distributed across Las Bela and parts of the Makran, between the Indus coastal
outposts of [[balakot]]/[[sutkagen-dor|Sutkagen-dor]]–[[sotka-koh|Sotka-koh]].
Strong local traditions persisted until roughly 2400 BCE, when an overlay of
Indus material appears; Kulli-style pottery and figurines appear at
[[nausharo|Nausharo]] Period IV (radiocarbon c. 2200–2000 BCE) in northern
Balochistan (as of Wright 2010).

## Lifeways

Agropastoral, with craft production and evidence of exchange with the Indus,
Iran, and (via the coast) the Arabian Peninsula.

## Relations and Successions

- **Associated with (not identity):** [[harappan-civilization]] via shared
  design elements and trade; positioned as an intermediary on overland routes.

## Identity Cautions

Wright reads the Kulli material as sufficiently distinct that it "may represent
an entirely separate culture" — a **"wedge of cultural resistance"** between
continuous Harappan settlements requiring diplomatic negotiation
(`associated_with`, not identity). Possehl (2003), by contrast, treats Kulli as
a local Indus variant rather than a separate culture. This is a live
construct-status dispute; the page keeps `construct_status: conventional`
pending resolution. Do not equate the Kulli material culture with a language,
a genetic population, or a self-conscious people.
