# Aswadian

PPNA-horizon archaeological culture of the **central Levant** (Damascus oasis), defined primarily at **Tell Aswad** (Henri de Contenson soundings). In [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|2000]]*), the middle member of the three Khiamian-descended corridor cultures — with [[sultanian-culture|Sultanian]] (south) and [[mureybetian-culture|Mureybetian]] (north) — inside Bar-Yosef’s [[levantine-corridor|Levantine corridor]].

## Definition and Construct History

Etic mono-site (as of Cauvin 2000) culture construct: “defined at present by this one village.” Farmers of the PPNA horizon established Aswad a little before 9000 BC; the tell covers ≥7 ha though the initial settlement was much smaller (area not accurately estimated from soundings).

## Material / Linguistic / Genetic Signature

**Material (Cauvin 2000):** small semi-subterranean round houses (2–3 m) with flanking cylindrical pits (possible silos); mud brick floors/platforms; reed-and-clay walls/roofs inferred from burned debris; multi-notched **Aswad points** derived from Khiam points; large finely denticulated lustred sickle-blades (best-represented tool class, tied to agriculture); baked-clay conical “pawns” and animal figurines. **Van Zeist** botanical work cited for domestic emmer at Aswad. **Language/genes:** unknown.

## Distribution and Chronology

Damascus oasis / eastern Anti-Lebanon margin; PPNA band overlapping Cauvin’s ~9500–8300 BC corridor map.

## Lifeways

Village farming early in the PPNA sequence; architecture and sickles as agricultural package markers.

## Relations and Successions

Sibling corridor culture to Sultanian and Mureybetian; later Damascus-area PPNB (e.g. Tell Ramad in Cauvin’s later chapters) as successor horizon — association not identity.

## Identity Cautions

Mono-site definition is fragile; not an ethnonym. Sounding-based excavation limits settlement reconstruction.

## Historiography

Cauvin 2000 is the wiki’s primary synthesis source; Contenson’s field reports are the primary excavation base (not separately ingested).
