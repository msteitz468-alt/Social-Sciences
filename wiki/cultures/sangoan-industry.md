# Sangoan Industry

## Definition and Construct History

The **Sangoan** is an African stone-artefact grouping named after Sango Bay on the western shore of Lake Victoria (Cole 1967 via Phillipson). In *African Archaeology* (3rd ed.), [[phillipson-david-w|David W. Phillipson]] treats Sangoan assemblages as a **final manifestation of the Acheulean tradition** and discusses them with the [[acheulean-industry]] rather than as an independent “culture” equivalent to a people (as of Phillipson 2005).

Like other named industries, the construct is **etic and conventional**: Phillipson warns that prehistorians have often imposed compartments on continuous ranges of variation in time and space (as of Phillipson 2005). No hominid fossils are **incontrovertibly** associated with Sangoan assemblages; Phillipson assumes makers of archaic *Homo sapiens* type (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Material Signature

Phillipson characterises Sangoan assemblages over a wide area from Botswana to Ethiopia and Sudan as featuring **massive pointed core-tools** plus lighter retouched flakes. In much of the Congo Basin they are the earliest human settlement yet found (as of Phillipson 2005, citing Mortelmans, Van Noten and others).

Earlier interpretation (e.g. J. D. Clark 1970) that the Sangoan was a forest/woodworking adaptation is, Phillipson states, **no longer tenable**: many sites belong to semi-arid conditions when equatorial forest was less widespread and dense (as of Phillipson 2005; McBrearty 1987 cited).

## Distribution and Chronology

Distribution is pan–sub-Saharan in a broad belt; absolute chronology is often weak. At sites such as [[kalambo-falls]], late Acheulean/Sangoan-related horizons once dated by radiocarbon near the method’s limit have been revised older by uranium-series and regional comparison (as of Phillipson 2005). Treat all Sangoan dates as **approximate** and method-dependent.

## Lifeways

Direct behavioural reconstruction is thin. Phillipson folds Sangoan into the late Acheulean behavioural package (expanded settlement, more efficient environmental use) without equating the industry with a single subsistence system or ethnolinguistic group (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Relations and Successions

- `preceded_by` / final facies of [[acheulean-industry]] (Phillipson’s framing).
- Succeeded regionally by mode-3 “Middle Stone Age” industries (see [[lithic-technological-modes]], [[howiesons-poort-industry]], [[aterian-industry]]).
- `associated_with` genus *Homo* only; not with robust australopithecines as primary makers (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Identity Cautions

Sangoan ≠ a people, language, or genetic population. It is a **material-culture construct** applied to assemblages with shared technological traits. Regional labels (e.g. Charaman in some Zimbabwe sequences) may or may not map cleanly onto the same construct — record local usage when densifying from site monographs.
