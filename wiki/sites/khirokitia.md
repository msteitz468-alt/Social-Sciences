# Khirokitia

Type-site of the Cypriot **aceramic Neolithic** village phenomenon. For [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|2000]]*, ch. 15), a principal case of **arrival of farmers on the Mediterranean littoral and in Cyprus** — seaborne diffusion of the Neolithic package beyond the Levantine mainland.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Long French mission (incl. Alain Le Brun); well-published relative to many mainland tells. Cauvin uses the site’s enclosed plan and architecture as diffusion evidence.

## Occupation Sequence

Aceramic Neolithic village with stone round houses and fortification/rampart features. Absolute dates as of Cauvin 2000 need calibration date-stamps against later Cypriot chronologies.

## Significance

Island colonisation by farming groups implies organised maritime capacity and motivation — central to Cauvin’s “Great Exodus” diffusion chapters. Challenges purely gradual local forager adoption models for Cyprus (as of his synthesis).

## Key Debates

Timing of first Cypriot settlement relative to mainland PPNB; demic vs cultural transmission; relation to earlier Akrotiri phase finds (post-Cauvin literature may revise — flag).

## Identity Cautions

“Khirokitia culture” is an archaeological construct, not a known ethnonym.

## Cauvin 2000 diffusion context

*(Attributed — *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]]* ch.15 + Postscript.)*

- Cyprus peopling staged: earlier primary sites (Shillourokambos, Tenta) with PPNB-affinity lithics precede classic Khirokitia culture; sea-borne colonisation with mainland (northern Syria/middle Euphrates) affinities.
- **Chronology correction (Postscript):** 1997 text had placed first Cyprus Neolithic with “Great Exodus” ~7500 BC, discounting earlier 14C because domestic cattle seemed too early on the island; **Halula cattle ~7700 BC** allows earlier mainland cattle and moves first Cyprus occupation roughly contemporary with early Halula / PPNB southern expansion — chronological adjustment without theoretical rewrite.
