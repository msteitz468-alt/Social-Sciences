# Nabta Playa

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Nabta Playa** in the Egyptian Western Desert is a key early Holocene locality in discussions of Sahara–Nile sedentism and early food production. [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] draws on Wendorf and colleagues for its place in the North African/Sahara sequence (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Occupation Sequence

Phillipson places Nabta within early Holocene wetter-phase occupation of the eastern Sahara, part of the broader trend toward permanent settlement and, later, herding/cultivation discussed in his Chs. 5–6 (as of Phillipson 2005). Exact phase names and cattle-domestication claims in the specialist literature are contested; keep all food-production interpretations **attributed** and date-stamped.

## Significance

Illustrates Phillipson’s argument that final Pleistocene–early Holocene sedentism and specialised foraging in the Nile–Sahara zone provided a base for later herding and cultivation — without treating “Neolithic” as a uniform package or calendar stage (he is wary of the label; as of Phillipson 2005).

## Key Debates

Early cattle domestication vs hunting/management at Nabta is a live specialist dispute beyond Phillipson’s survey resolution — do not promote to wiki voice from this source alone.
