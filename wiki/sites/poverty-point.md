# Poverty Point

Major Late Archaic earthwork complex in northeastern Louisiana — type site of [[poverty-point-culture]]. Densified from [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner, *The Moundbuilders* (2021)]] Ch. 3.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Recognized and excavated across the twentieth century; treated by Milner as the premier Late Archaic mound/earthwork center of the lower Mississippi. Site destruction and agricultural leveling threaten many Eastern Woodlands monuments generally (Milner Ch. 1 preservation theme).

## Occupation Sequence

Late Archaic monumental construction by hunter-gatherers with extensive exchange networks. Concentric ridges and mounds form a planned ceremonial/residential landscape on a scale unmatched among many Archaic contemporaries. **Not** a maize-agricultural Mississippian center.

## Significance

- Anchors deep time-depth of **mound/earthwork construction** in the Eastern Woodlands before Adena–Hopewell and Mississippian florescence.
- Comparative case against equating monuments with agriculture or hereditary [[chiefdom|chiefdoms]] (Milner: complex relative to peers, without permanent hierarchical domination of later chiefdom type).
- Links: [[watson-brake]] (earlier Archaic mounds), [[eastern-woodlands-chronology]], [[moundbuilder-myth]] (refutation context: Native-built monuments of great age).

## Key Debates

Social organization (egalitarian aggregation vs ranking); function of ridges; precise dating and duration; relation to earlier Archaic mounds.
