# Lepenski Vir

Mesolithic–Early Neolithic settlement on the Danube Iron Gates (Serbia), famous for trapezoidal houses and monumental stone sculpture. In this vault, first densified via [[handsman-russell|Russell Handsman]]'s *[[gero-conkey-engendering-archaeology-1991|Engendering Archaeology]]* chapter **"Whose Art Was Found at Lepenski Vir?"** (1991) — a critique of gender relations and power in archaeological interpretation and exhibition of the site, not a primary excavation report.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Major Yugoslav excavations under Dragoslav Srejović; popular and scholarly framing as “Europe’s first monumental sculpture” (Srejović 1972 and related publications Handsman cites). The 1986 Southampton City Art Gallery exhibit “The Art of Lepenski Vir” (with World Archaeological Congress) displayed 91 pieces of monumental sculpture, altars, and ritual instruments from stratified settlements on the Danube. Handsman (1991) treats exhibition design, renaming (e.g. pestles as sceptres; “Black Obelisk”), and narrative as part of the knowledge-production problem. Excavation quality and phasing must be checked against primary reports before wiki-voice chronological claims.

## Occupation Sequence

Eight habitation levels reported in literature Handsman uses; trapezoidal “houses” with hardened limestone plaster floors (some burnished red/white); stone-lined hearths, altars, spherical boulders; focus for inhumations and reburial of skeletal parts, especially heads (Tringham 1971 as cited). Absolute dates remain disputed in secondary literature — attribute.

## Significance

Anchor site for Iron Gates forager monumentalism; for this wiki’s knowledge layer, a case of **how gender and power enter archaeological art narratives**. Handsman argues the exhibit answers “whose art?” as Males’ Art: Srejović’s architect-magician male ritual specialist; sculpture names (Adam, Sorcerer, Barbarian, First Father, Old Chief, Founder of the Tribe) vs women only as Lady of Lepenski Vir, Mermaid, First Mother, Vulva — art *about* women that cannot empower them as makers. Discourse is treated as productive of gender inequality (Foucault/Said), not a neutral mirror of the past. Program link: [[feminist-archaeology]].

## Key Debates

Gender and interpretive power; whose labor/art is credited; relation to goddess/“Old Europe” receptions (Gimbutas cited in Handsman’s network); present museum/exhibition framings; communal surplus and dual art traditions in Handsman’s re-reading (attributed POSITION).

## After the Ice densification (Mithen 2003)

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven]], *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice]]* 2003, European Mesolithic chapters; does not overwrite Handsman gender critique above.)*

- **Setting ~6400 BC:** sedentary hunter-gatherer fishermen’s village of trapeze-shaped dwellings on the Iron Gates Danube, with canoes, nets, carved boulders — flourishing while ‘Ain Ghazal suffered economic collapse (narrative contrast) (attributed).
- **Excavation:** Dragoslav Srejović discovered and excavated 1966–1971 during pre-dam survey; related sites Hajdučka, Vodenica, Padina, Vlasac — some seasonal, or Lepenski Vir a ceremonial centre (positions) (attributed).
- **Health paradox:** architectural/artistic creativity coexists with undernourished children, rickets, enamel hypoplasia — periodic food shortage (attributed).
- **Mortuary/construction rites:** infants buried and sealed in dwelling floors; adult jawbones between hearth stones; no secular/sacred division for builders (attributed).
- **Radovanović:** river-symbolism reading of sculpture/architecture (via Mithen) (position).
- **Farming interface:** farmers leap-frog Balkans; settlements within ~50 km first stimulate then collapse Mesolithic culture as pottery and farming foods replace wild staples (attributed).
- **Generational split (Mithen interpretation):** elders elaborate larger doorway sculptures to resist farmers; younger people trade and adopt sheep/cattle/wheat that fill wild-food gaps then demote wild foods to supplements (strong position).
- **Complex HG class:** with Oleneostrovski, Skateholm, Ertebølle, Oronsay — late Mesolithic sedentism/aggregation/cemeteries before Neolithic absorption (attributed).
- **Labour contrast:** Mesolithic few-hours foraging knowledge economy vs Nea Nikomedeia hard agricultural labour including child weeding (Mithen’s comparative framing).
