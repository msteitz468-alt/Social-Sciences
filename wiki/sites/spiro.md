# Spiro

[[mississippian-culture|Mississippian]] ceremonial center in eastern Oklahoma, famous for the **Craig Mound** mortuary assemblage and [[southeastern-ceremonial-complex|SECC]]-related materials. Treated in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] Ch. 6 (box feature).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Commercially looted in the 1930s (Pocola Mining Company tunnel mining of Craig Mound) — a textbook disaster for provenience (Milner Ch. 1 lists Spiro among major destruction cases). Subsequent archaeological work and museum reconstruction of assemblages only partly recover context.

## Occupation Sequence

Mississippian mound center on the western edge of the Mississippian world; elite burial wealth (shell, copper, elaborately engraved objects) often disproportionate to modest residential scale in synthetic accounts. Craig Mound served as a major elite mortuary facility, not a simple single-event tomb only.

## Significance

- Key evidence for long-distance exchange, elite ideology, and SECC art.
- Cautionary case for **looting and context loss**.
- **Mesoamerican contacts (Milner position):** despite occasional exotic comparisons (e.g. items sometimes read as Mesoamerican-linked), Milner concludes there was **“not much”** in the way of direct Mesoamerican contact — parallel development and selective long-distance exchange within the Eastern Woodlands matter more (attributed).

## Key Debates

Craig Mound as chiefly tomb vs multi-phase ritual deposit; SECC as “cult” vs style complex; Mesoamerican influence vs independence.
