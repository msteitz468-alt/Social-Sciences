# Río Seco

Preceramic mound settlement (Río Seco del León) on an isolated coastal desert stretch north of the Chancay Valley — Moseley's key "maize-free control" case for the [[maritime-foundations-hypothesis]].

## Discovery and Excavation History

Systematically tested by Wendt (1964), reported by Moseley from Wendt and [[lanning-edward|Lanning (1967)]]. Midden ~11.8 ha with 1–1.5 m of accumulated refuse; two freestanding artificial mounds each ~3 m high, each begun as large corporate-labor masonry rooms subsequently filled to raise an eminence. Lanning (1967) reports one platform with two superimposed sets of filled rooms and a masonry summit structure in its final phase; the mounds are apparently not terraced, and access routes, ground plans, and orientations are unreported.

## Occupation Sequence

Cotton Preceramic maritime settlement; relative dating only (textile-sequence cross-reference to [[ancon|Ancón–Chillón]]; no absolute dates in the source). Moseley (Epilogue) suggests it was deserted around the Gaviota phase, as was El Paraíso — hedged. Wendt's testing indicates **2,500–3,000 interments** in the refuse, though this need not represent total mortality if some burials lie outside the settlement.

## Significance

Because cultivation was effectively impossible at this barren desert location, Río Seco anchors Moseley's argument that platform-mound construction and dense population did not require agriculture. Contrasted with [[aspero]] (minor maize present): monumental corporate labor occurs at both, so maize cannot be the driver of settlement size or monumentality (attributed to Moseley). Its midden volume is also used as a comparative baseline against [[el-paraiso|El Paraíso's]] surprisingly thin refuse.

## Key Debates

Part of the maritime-vs-agrarian foundations debate ([[maritime-foundations-hypothesis]]); no site-specific dispute recorded.

## Historiography

Rests on Wendt (1964) and Lanning (1967) as reported by [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|Moseley 1975]]; primary reports not ingested. Dating weakly anchored.
