# Quirigua

## Discovery and Excavation History

Motagua site controlling jade/obsidian trade corridor. Peabody and Guatemalan projects. [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]] narrate its rebellion against Copan.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]].)*

- Client or subordinate to Copan until K'ahk' Tiliw’s rebellion from AD 725.
- 4 May AD 738: capture and beheading of Copan’s 18 Rabbit — political inversion celebrated on Quirigua monuments.
- Distinctive zoomorph altars and exceptionally tall stelae.

## Significance

Case study in Classic dynastic warfare and secondary-center agency; Motagua economic corridor.

## Key Debates

- Trade control vs dynastic ideology as drivers of the Copan–Quirigua war.

## Densification from Sharer & Traxler 2006

*(Attributed — [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]]. Complements Coe & Houston synthesis above.)*

### Founding as Copan/Tikal Motagua subsidiary (426)

Sharer & Traxler report Zoomorph P (Looper & Schele) recounts the same founding events as [[copan]] Altar Q: Quirigua founded in **426** by an *ajaw* nicknamed **Tok Casper** under authority of **K’inich Yax K’uk’ Mo’**. Archaeological and historical evidence suggests [[tikal]] orchestrated the 426 Copan takeover **and** establishment of Quirigua as Copan’s subsidiary controlling the Motagua Valley (jadeite, Ixtepeque obsidian, Caribbean gateway). For the next ~300 years Copan controlled Motagua via Quirigua. Early Classic settlement: Group A hilltop and floodplain (Stela U 478; Monument 26 ca. 493); possible founder crypt under early Acropolis east side (jade-inlaid notched teeth like Hunal); founder may have come from Tikal with the overlord. River-capital location type (with Yaxchilan, Piedras Negras, Lamanai). Pyrite mirrors among prestige goods at Quirigua. Cacao plantations inferred from effigies/residues at Copan, Quirigua, Lubaantun among others.

### Independence after 738 and monumental program

K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Yopaat recorded inauguration under supervision (*u kabi*) of Copan’s Waxaklajuun Ub’aah K’awiil — then in **738** captured and sacrificed the 13th Copan ruler; inscriptions also claim capture of Copan’s **patron gods**. Thereafter Quirigua expanded ~a century from Motagua control; **most Main Group construction post-738**; Altar L references Smoke Imix of Copan. Exemplar of warfare ideology: subordinate lord sacrificed overlord and became *k’uhul ajaw*, yet the **Copan polity continued** — victorious kings allowed or encouraged perpetuation of defeated royal houses. Size ≠ power illustration: small capital defeating larger overlord. Structure density well below most Classic sites; central Late Classic population estimates only 1,200–1,600. Public vs private spatial contrast noted (with Lubaantun). *Hotun* (quarter-*k’atun*) period-ending practice shared with Piedras Negras. Creation text: Stela C (Monument 3) describes primordial three-stone hearth at Orion’s belt. Distance numbers: Stela F records a date calculated ~91,683,930 *tuns* (>90 million years) earlier than 9.16.10.0.0; Stela D secondary series ~411,863,930 *tuns* (>400 million years) before 9.16.15.0.0.

### Terminal Classic fate and Motagua corridor

Copan’s weakening triggered by loss of the thirteenth ruler and Quirigua’s breakaway. Trade-economy models list Quirigua with Palenque, Piedras Negras, Yaxchilan, and Copan as Motagua/Usumacinta riverine sites vulnerable when seaborne Yucatan routes rose. Earthquake position (Motagua Fault): may have affected Quirigua and Copan but inhabitants rebuilt — insufficient alone for Classic demise. Terminal Classic arrivals at Copan and Quirigua appear **after** demise of centralized order — more result than cause. Copper bells/ornaments at Quirigua Terminal Classic or Early Postclassic. Cortés’s 1524–25 march passed near the Quirigua vicinity without it being a major living capital visited.
