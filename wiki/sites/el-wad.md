# el-Wad (Cave and Terrace)

Mount Carmel cave-and-terrace complex; Dorothy Garrod’s type site for the [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] (named after Wadi en-Natuf / Shukbah discovery sequence, with el-Wad central to early definition). Renewed terrace excavations by Mina Weinstein-Evron, Daniel Kaufman, Reuven Yeshurun and colleagues re-mapped spatial organization, Late Natufian burials, refuse maintenance, and ornament production ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Garrod’s 1929–33 campaign defined Natufian Layer B; later work and archival reanalysis (Weinstein-Evron) showed Geometric Kebaran admixture within thick Natufian deposits — a taphonomic caution for “continuity” claims (Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris 2013). Post-70-years terrace excavations (Weinstein-Evron et al. 2007) form the basis of 2013 spatial, osteological, zooarchaeological, and ornament chapters.

## Occupation Sequence

Early to Late/Final Natufian on the terrace and cave; multi-phase spatial reorganization. Absolute dates densify from claims inventory.

## Significance

Type-assemblage and long research history; modern work tests refuse management, burial demography, and long-distance green-stone exchange at a core-area site.

## Key Debates

- Mechanical mixture of Geometric Kebaran and Natufian microliths in Layer B (Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris) — [[natufian-origins-core-periphery-debate]].
- Refuse maintenance as evidence of domestic organization (Yeshurun et al.).
- Gazelle specialization timing on Carmel coast (Bar-Oz, Yeshurun & Weinstein-Evron 2013) — [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]].

## Densified findings from Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013

*(Attributed — [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]].)*

- **Spatial organization (Weinstein-Evron, Kaufman & Yeshurun):** Early/Middle/Late Natufian terrace phasing; Wall I / Structure II; alternating habitation and burial use of space through time.
- **Late Natufian burials (Bachrach et al.):** 15 individuals; skull removal practices; stature, robusticity, diet, pathology dataset for last Natufian inhabitants of the terrace.
- **Refuse (Yeshurun et al.):** Structure II fauna NISP 1769; primary refuse inside dwelling with limited external cleaning — implications for domestic organization and occupation intensity.
- **Green stone pendants (Bar-Yosef Mayer, Porat & Weinstein-Evron):** green apatite; Late Natufian assignment; unique form; raw material from Rift sources; green-color symbolism argument.
