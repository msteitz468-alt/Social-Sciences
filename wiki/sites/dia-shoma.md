# Dia (Dia Shoma / Dia Urban Cluster)

Large multi-mound settlement complex in the Middle Niger / Inland Niger Delta, treated by R.J. McIntosh (*[[mcintosh-ancient-middle-niger-2005|Ancient Middle Niger]]*, 2005) as a peer **[[urban-cluster|Urban Cluster]]** to [[jenne-jeno|Jenné-jeno]]. Earlier handbook densification (MacDonald): Dia Shoma Horizon I (800–400 BCE) ≥19 ha; apogee c. AD 1000 ~34 ha + satellites; early ceramics largely from Tichitt Tradition.

**Identity caution:** Archaeological complex ≠ ethnic Dia people of later ethnography ≠ language ≠ genetic population. Use `associated_with` for any ethnic or imperial equation (Ghana/Mali/Songhay).

## McIntosh 2005 densification

### Excavation history

Exploratory 1986–87 units at Mara and larger Shoma (~49 ha including wall trench). 1998–2004 Malian–Dutch–French–English program: twelve further units into the Dia Urban Cluster (Bedaux et al. 2001). Illustrates danger of anchoring chronology on few test units: preliminary sequence seemed to match Jenné-jeno phases with little earlier material; later seasons **pushed foundation well back into the first millennium BCE**.

### Occupation sequence (as of McIntosh 2005)

- **Five horizons** at Shoma with two abandonments: sporadic/seasonal 300 BCE–AD 300; and 12th–14th centuries CE.
- Earliest horizon ¹⁴C **800–300 BCE** falls in a radiocarbon plateau (half-millennium calibration uncertainty).
- Early levels: Méma/Tichitt LSA sherds (few Kobadi; ~30% Faita).
- Fineware begins Horizon I and continues strongly through AD 300–800 and 800–1100 — majority run ~**2,000 years** at Shoma, so Fineware is **not** a tight chronometer here (unlike Jenné-jeno Fineware ~250 BCE–AD 350).
- Eight units at the ~**100 ha** Dia–Shoma–Mara complex do not preclude urban proportions by **500 BCE**.
- By AD 800–1100: specialized herders and fisherfolk; “frenzy of burial states” possibly ethnic insignia (Bedaux et al. 2001: 844–46).

### Hinterland survey

1986 **50% randomized** survey matched to Jenné design (Haskell et al. 1988): sites cleave more tightly/linearly to waterways than Jenné’s satellite-orbit pattern; earlier regional abandonment wave than Upper Delta.

### Significance and identity cautions

Peer [[urban-cluster]] demonstrating multi-centric Middle Niger urban tradition. Not equated with historic ethnic “Dia,” Ghana capital debates, or Mali Empire. See also MacDonald handbook densification on [[jenne-jeno]] (Fadugu vs Markadugu).
