# Kahun (Lahun pyramid town)

Middle Kingdom planned settlement associated with the pyramid complex of Senwosret II at Lahun (Fayum region). Entered this wiki primarily via [[kemp-barry-j|Kemp]]’s *[[kemp-ancient-egypt-2018|Anatomy of a Civilization]]* (2018) as the **type-site of the prescriptive grid town** — contrast case to self-organized [[amarna|Amarna]].

## Discovery and Excavation History

Classic excavations by Petrie produced the famous town plan and papyri. Full excavation history awaits dedicated source ingest; Kemp uses the published plan as a model-community exemplar.

## Occupation Sequence

Founded as a pyramid town / administrative settlement in the 12th Dynasty; main archaeological signature is the orthogonal street grid and house-size hierarchy of the planned phase.

## Significance

1. **Model community** in Kemp’s provider-state argument — bureaucracy made spatial.
2. **Two-level social map** — large houses vs small houses / granary logistics (Kemp 2018 Ch. 5, attributed).
3. **Foil to Amarna** — planned prescription vs desert-bay self-organization.

## Key Debates

How far Kahun represents general Middle Kingdom urbanism vs a special-purpose royal foundation.

## Lehner densification (1997) — Illahun / Hetep-Senwosret

*(Attributed throughout — [[lehner-mark]], *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|The Complete Pyramids]]* 1997. Kahun = pyramid town of Senwosret II at Illahun/Lahun; ancient name **Hetep-Senwosret** (“May Senwosret be at Peace”).)*

### Pyramid context

Senwosret II built overlooking the Hawara Channel opening to the Fayum near Illahun — choice reflects growing MK importance of the Fayum. First of the giant mudbrick pyramids: core around reserved four-step yellow limestone stump; radial/cross limestone walls form compartments filled with mudbrick; upper part also mudbrick; fine limestone casing bottom course in foundation trench; cobble-filled drainage trench; base 106 m, slope 42°35′, height 48.6 m. Complete departure from north-entrance norm: narrow vertical shaft at east end of south side ([[petrie-flinders|Petrie]] spent months finding it); anti-robbery disguise; only royal burial remnant a gold uraeus. Osirian redesign (Stadelmann): circuit corridor creates subterranean “island”; well and grove as Osiris symbols; nicked limestone inner enclosure nods to Djoser/Archaic enclosures.

Valley temple location known but plan destroyed; immediately NW lay foundations of part of the pyramid town.

### Town plan and economy

- Footprint is “one of the basic documents for the study of the history of Egyptian urbanism.”
- Estimated population several thousand: **220 small houses** west/south; **9–10 substantial urban estates** northeast for senior officials.
- [[kemp-barry-j|Kemp]] compared large households to Meketre tomb models (porticoed house, bakery, brewery, carpentry, weaving, granary).
- Collectively, large Illahun granaries could store enough grain to feed **5,000–9,000** people annually; inhabitants of small dwellings “must have worked for and in the large households in return for their sustenance.”
- Dieter Arnold’s workforce estimate for Amenemhet III’s Dahshur mudbrick pyramid (~5,000) is the same order as Kemp’s Illahun grain-ration figure — Lehner notes the congruence as a scale check for MK pyramid logistics.

### Treasure of Illahun

1913 Brunton and Petrie found the “Treasure of Illahun” in a recess of the plundered tomb of princess Sit-Hathor-Iunet (daughter of Senwosret II, aunt of Amenemhet III) — diadem, mirror, pectorals with cartouches of Senwosret II and Amenemhet III.
