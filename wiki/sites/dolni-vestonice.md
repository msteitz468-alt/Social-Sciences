# Dolní Věstonice

Open-air Pavlovian/Gravettian complex in the Pavlov Hills, southern Moravia; densest Upper Palaeolithic case for Gamble’s “extension of social life”—burials, kilns, figurines, large gatherings—in [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999|Gamble 1999]], Ch.7. Treated with Pavlov as **Dolní Věstonice–Pavlov**.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Landscape dominated by the Dyje river and Pavlovské limestone hills (~550 m); ~100 km to Wachau/Willendorf; ~150 km to Moravian Gates/southern Poland; calcareous preservation of bone and organics (as of Gamble 1999). Systematic excavations began with Absolon (1924–38); Nazi-period Bohmers stratigraphy; Cold War soil focus; Klíma’s 1950s settlement approach; post-1985 rescue at DV II/III; Milovice 1986. Famous finds include the 1925 clay female figurine (“hood”/slant-cut eyes) and 1936 ivory “portrait” head; many Absolon finds destroyed in a 1945 castle fire. Klíma framed great loess sites as permanent settlements with earliest buildings and organized community life (possibly matriarchal)—positions Gamble records rather than endorses as wiki voice.

## Occupation Sequence

**Svoboda chronology (via Gamble 1999):**

| Phase | Uncal. span (as of Gamble 1999) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Early Pavlovian | 30–27 ka bp | |
| Evolved Pavlovian | 27–24 ka bp | major DV–Pavlov use |
| Willendorf/Kostenki | 24–20 ka bp | shouldered points Don→Moravia/Austria/S. Poland; mammoth-bone houses, storage pits, carvings |

Groningen C14 moving sums: DV II/III (west) consistently **older** than classic DV I + Pavlov I. Evolved Pavlovian settlement extends almost **3 km** between Pavlov and Dolní Věstonice (200–300 m ASL, two clusters). DV I and Pavlov I (<500 m apart) share occupation phase with thousands of clay figurine fragments and semi-subterranean huts on levelled platforms; DV II has hearths, burials, lithics, bone tools, refitting but fewer fired-clay objects. Mammoth-bone middens at DV I/II (human vs natural origin debated). Open-air UP burials at Sunghir, Kostenki, and DV–Pavlov are treated as a cultural development, not merely a preservation change (Gamble 1999, attributed).

## Significance

### Densest UP open locale

Of eleven DV–Pavlov lithic density samples, six >100 pieces/m² and none <10/m²—exceptionally high vs earlier open locales (sample of 80); Gamble reads a quantifiable increase as largest locales shift from gatherings to **social occasions**. Comparable high densities at Predmostí, Willendorf II 9, Milovice vs small German Gravettian cave assemblages. After 33 ka bp and especially after climatic downturn ~28 ka bp, Gravettian NC/NE mammoth middens include DV I–II (>150 MNI), Predmostí (>1,000), Pavlov, Milovice, Spadzista, Kostenki 1/1, etc.; Soffer (via Gamble): kills **and** natural “mammoth graveyards,” generally lack cut marks but carnivore-gnawed—collected as much as hunted; Moravian mineral licks as attractors; mammoth bone not burnt/stored in pits at Moravian locales (contrast Kostenki 1/1 and Central Russian Plain post-LGM).

### Setting up home and raw materials

Platforms 4–5 m diameter cut into loess; post holes and central hearths (DV I); mammoth-bone rings (Milovice); DV II large reoccupied hearths with tent-like posts; Pavlov I possible paired huts; permanence argued beyond seasonal gathering; mixed ages/sexes (infant plant-food “mush” at DV II; scattered human remains); fibre technology (textile/basketry impressions, possible nets in burnt clay—Adovasio, Soffer, Klíma via Gamble); flint from southern Poland; radiolarites from Slovakia/Austria. Kozlowski/Svoboda (via Gamble): after 33 ka bp Gravettian raw-material specialization—stable distant high-grade procurement; Moravia 30–20 ka bp southern Polish flints more common, fewer sources, coinciding with large river settlements (Predmostí, DV–Pavlov, Milovice). Gamble treats long-distance stone and familiar tool styles as **social objects** symbolizing distant hunting grounds and other occasions—agency stretched in the social landscape—not mere information transmission (attributed).

### Kilns and “exploding life”

Vandiver et al. (via Gamble): thousands of clay pellets/figurine fragments at DV I and Pavlov I (rare elsewhere); local loess suitable for kiln firing but many pieces show **intentional thermal shock**—process of making/firing (exploding in kilns) valued over durable product; place-specific skills differ between DV I and Pavlov I only 500 m apart; not “precocious pottery invention” but loess as active agent in performance. Hearths/kilns are hallmarks of DV–Pavlov and most EUP locales, contrasting earlier MP locales such as Mauran that often lack even simple hearths.

### Burials as detaching rituals

Open-air burial tradition: repeated locale use, multiple burials, extensive ochre/ornament/clothing; ~34 individuals at DV by 1995 plus Pavlov finds; six complete bodies (as of Gamble 1999).

- **DV III:** tightly flexed gracile female under mammoth scapulae.
- **DV XVI:** male flexed toward hearth with infant mush nearby.
- **Triple burial DV XIII–XV:** two adolescent males + central pathological individual; ochre on heads and central pelvis; charcoal/branches briefly fired then smothered; nearby limestone rod possibly lunar-calendar notations (29 marks)—reconstruction attributed in source.

Gamble reads DV–Pavlov burials as **detaching rituals** with *au revoir* character (dead enter extended network as ancestors), contrasting Neanderthal burial as *adieu*. Ochre on head (interaction focus) and groin (birth/blood attaching symbolism, attributed reading); digging (ochre, hut platforms, graves, kiln loess) as primary rhythm of attaching/detaching; permafrost ~80 cm as physical limit beneath surface of fire-transforming action. Beads/shells abundant with Gravettian burials (DV, Sunghir, Balzi Rossi) vs rare pre-30 ka burials (White via Gamble).

### Programmatic contrast

Lower Palaeolithic [[bilzingsleben|Bilzingsleben]] = gathering; Upper Palaeolithic Dolní Věstonice = social occasion based on built environment of huts, graves, ovens, middens (Gamble 1999 typology). Attachment at DV also uses extended-network resources—objects as independent agents, fireplaces and ochre as attention foci—beyond the intimate/effective-only negotiation of Bilzingsleben and [[maastricht-belvedere|Maastricht-Belvédère]] (attributed).

## Key Debates

- **Permanence of occupation** — Klíma permanent settlement vs seasonal aggregation models.
- **Mammoth middens** — hunting vs collection from natural death sites (Soffer).
- **Triple burial and fired clay** — ritual/detachment readings (Gamble) vs other mortuary/taphonomic accounts.
- **Kiln thermal shock** — intentional performance vs failed firing.
- **Lunar rod** — speculative notation reconstruction.
- **Cultures field** — Pavlovian/Gravettian technocomplex labels; frontmatter retains loose industry link; four-way non-identity: industry ≠ people.

## Historiography

Eighty years of excavation map onto political regimes (Absolon; Nazi stratigraphy; Cold War soils; post-1985 rescue). Gamble 1999 makes DV–Pavlov the European type-case for **release from proximity** and extended networks in the mid Upper Palaeolithic.
