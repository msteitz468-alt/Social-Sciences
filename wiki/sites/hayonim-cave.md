# Hayonim Cave and Terrace

Karstic cave and adjacent terrace in the Lower Galilee; major [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] occupation excavated by [[bar-yosef-ofer|Ofer Bar-Yosef]] (cave) and later by Valla and others (terrace). Cave and terrace lie ~20 m apart but were excavated by different teams; a stratigraphic gap leaves contemporaneity unresolved (Munro in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Bar-Yosef’s Natufian cave work sits atop a long Paleolithic sequence (Acheuleo-Yabrudian through Late Natufian). Terrace excavations (Valla et al.) produced separate publication streams. Munro (2013) tests whether fauna indicate a single multi-function site vs. separate occupations.

## Occupation Sequence

Early Natufian construction of built features; Late Natufian reduction of construction with continued burial and special activities (caches along NE wall). Chert procurement territories analyzed for Late Natufian terrace (Delage 2013).

## Significance

Core-area multi-component site for architecture, mortuary practice, and lithic economy; method case for integrating cave–terrace datasets.

## Key Debates

- Cave vs terrace: one site with functional differentiation, or non-contemporaneous occupations (Munro 2013).

## Densified findings from Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013

*(Attributed — [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]].)*

- **Chert (Delage):** Late Natufian terrace procurement territory ~2,500 km² scale argument; raw-material economy of a core-area site.
- **Fauna (Munro):** tests cave vs terrace as one multi-function site vs separate non-contemporaneous occupations; stratigraphic gap leaves contemporaneity open.
- **Scaphopods (Kurzawska et al.):** Hayonim largest sample (4536 of 5829 multi-site total); Mediterranean *Antalis* dominate; Red Sea taxa appear only in Late contexts.
