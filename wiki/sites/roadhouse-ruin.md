# Roadhouse Ruin

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Early Classic** masonry hamlet/habitation in the Horseshoe project area, excavated by [[lower-verde-archaeological-project-study|LVAP]]. Like [[scorpion-point-village|Scorpion Point Village]], data recovery showed the site **much more extensive and complex** than survey suggested, driving contract modifications (*[[whittlesey-vanishing-river-1997|Vanishing River]]* Ch. 1, 13, 17). Primary Classic-period case for domestic architecture in the print synthesis; detail on CD-ROM Volume 1.

## Occupation Sequence

LVAP Classic / Period 3 sample (attributed to Deaver; Klucas et al.; Ciolek-Torrello):

- Oval rooms stratigraphically earlier than rectangular rooms; stone-robbing and wall evolution (upright siltstone slabs → stacked basalt/granite/siltstone).
- Compound/patio organization; metrics in Table 13.2 (overview).
- Decorated pottery extremely scarce: **>17,000 sherds** with only **18 decorated** vessels/sherds reported (all nonlocal; residual pre-Classic buff among them) — almost no indigenous Classic decorated tradition at the site.
- Interaction shift in white wares: Tusayan White decreases, Little Colorado White increases (Flagstaff → northeast interaction, as interpreted).
- Beaver remains reported from Classic Roadhouse (Whittlesey Ch. 3 paleoenvironmental note) — local riparian indicator.

Project-wide, late Classic is poorly represented; confluence area largely abandoned; Classic occupation concentrates in Horseshoe Basin and upper Camp Creek.

## Significance

Balances Pre-Classic Scorpion Point in LVAP’s household and settlement arguments; anchors Classic demography models (project-area Classic peak ~**750** people in Ciolek-Torrello’s estimates) and shows Classic lower Verde ceramic poverty relative to Phoenix Basin expectations under simple core–periphery trait export.

## Key Debates

Relationship to Phoenix Basin and Tonto Basin Classic trajectories; whether low decorated-ware counts mean isolation, different social networks, or sampling; platform mounds and fortified sites in the *wider* lower Verde settlement pattern (regional Ch. 14) vs this site’s hamlet scale.

## Historiography

LVAP 1997 synthesis and companion volumes; earlier CAWCS/CAP survey mentions in Ch. 4.
