## Discovery and Excavation History

Hane is a coastal sand-dune/beach-terrace settlement on Ua Huka Island, Marquesas, first excavated by Yosihiko Sinoto in 1966, and subsequently reexcavated and redated by Anderson and Sinoto (2002, redating ten original samples) and by Conte and Molle (2012, a fresh excavation with a new AMS radiocarbon series) (attributed, [[kirch-patrick-vinton|Kirch]] 2017). The site preserves complex, reworked stratigraphy including two buried stone pavements. As a site excavated and redated across three separate campaigns spanning nearly fifty years, using progressively more rigorous dating methods, Hane is unusually well documented as a case study in the evolution of Pacific dating practice itself, even though its earliest excavation predated modern AMS techniques.

## Occupation Sequence

Hane's dating history is itself a case study in the short-versus-long chronology debate over East Polynesian settlement (see [[pacific-settlement-chronology-debate]]):
- Sinoto's original excavation (1966) suggested occupation as early as AD 300–600.
- Anderson and Sinoto (2002) redated the sequence using ten samples; none returned dates earlier than AD 1000.
- Conte and Molle (2012) obtained a fresh AMS radiocarbon series from the lowest deposits, giving a date range of AD 900–1000.

The earlier, older dates are now recognized as artifacts of the "old wood" in-built-age problem in bulk-charcoal radiocarbon samples; the current, AMS-based determination on short-lived identified samples supersedes them.

## Significance

Hane's finds — diverse one-piece fishhook styles (figured by Suggs 1961) and basalt adzes with incipient tangs (triangular, trapezoidal, and quadrangular cross-sections) — form part of the diagnostic "Archaic Phase" Eastern Polynesian assemblage type as defined by Sinoto (1970/1983/1996b). The site's successive redatings, progressively compressing its occupation span and moving it later in time, exemplify the broader mid-to-late-20th-century "short chronology" revision of East Polynesian settlement dates, associated with Atholl Anderson (a co-author of the site's 2002 redating) and the "chronometric hygiene" approach he and Matthew Spriggs pioneered (Spriggs and Anderson 1993).

## Key Debates

Hane's dating history is a paradigm case within the broader [[pacific-settlement-chronology-debate]]: Sinoto's original "long chronology" dates supported an early first-millennium-AD settlement of the Marquesas, while successive AMS redatings (Anderson and Sinoto 2002; Conte and Molle 2012) place the site's earliest secure occupation no earlier than about AD 900–1000, consistent with the now-dominant short-chronology consensus for Marquesan and wider East Polynesian settlement.
