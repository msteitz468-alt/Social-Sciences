# Hacılar

Neolithic (and later) settlement in southwestern Anatolia excavated by [[mellaart-james|James Mellaart]]. Entered this wiki primarily as **comparandum** in Mellaart’s *[[mellaart-catal-huyuk-1967|Çatal Hüyük]]* (1967) and related Anatolian Neolithic narratives — not yet from a dedicated Hacılar monograph ingest.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Mellaart’s Hacılar campaigns (1950s–60s) established the site as a key SW Anatolian sequence from aceramic Neolithic through later painted-pottery phases. Full excavation history and specialist results await dedicated source ingestion.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Stub resolution.)* Aceramic levels cited by Mellaart as among the earliest plant-domestication evidence in Anatolia (with Beidha, Ali Kosh — mid-1960s framework ~7000 BC). Later levels famous for painted pottery and figurines in secondary literature.

## Significance

1. **Aceramic farming node** on the Anatolian plateau in multi-foci Neolithic maps.
2. **Burial contrast with [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]]:** Mellaart (1967) states extramural burial at aceramic Hacılar vs under-platform intramural secondary burial at Çatalhöyük.
3. **Figurine/pottery comparanda** for Çatalhöyük goddess and ceramic discussions (Cauvin and textbook syntheses).

## Key Debates

Chronology and culture-historical links to central Anatolian sequences (including Çatalhöyük) remain research-front; do not equate Hacılar and Çatalhöyük as one “people.”
