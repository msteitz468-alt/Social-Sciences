# Meadowcroft Rockshelter

Stratified rockshelter in southwestern Pennsylvania (~50 km west of Pittsburgh) excavated by **James Adovasio**; a central **pre-Clovis candidate** in the [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]]. Densified from [[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|Meltzer 2009]] Ch. 4 (as-of-2009 assessment) with a thinner [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]] position layer. Not a primary excavation report.

**Identity caution:** A rockshelter sequence is not a people, language, or genetic population.

**Date hygiene:** Ages as Meltzer reports are **radiocarbon years BP (uncalibrated)**.

## Discovery and Excavation History

*(Attributed — Meltzer 2009.)* Excavated **1973–~1978** by Adovasio’s interdisciplinary team with meticulous techniques (critics concede superb fieldwork). **4.6 m** of sediment in **11** natural strata; **52** ¹⁴C dates from **31,400 BP to Historic**, nearly all in stratigraphic order (four minor Holocene flip-flops). Despite **>100** publications, Meltzer stresses the lack of a **comprehensive final report** tying all data, artifact–date–stratum relations—keeping the site in “archaeological limbo” (as of 2009). Adovasio compared the controversy to the old Trenton debates.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Adovasio via Meltzer 2009.)*

- **Middle Stratum IIa:** bracketed **11,300–12,800 BP** with an **unfluted Miller lanceolate** point—sometimes floated as a fine fluted-point precursor (Adovasio’s note, via Meltzer on fluting origins).
- **Lower Stratum IIa:** six dates **12,800–16,175 BP**; ~**700** genuine artifacts (bone punch, debitage, small blades, uni/bifacial knives, gravers) of exotic stone (NY, OH, WV, PA sources). Adovasio’s **conservative average** for human presence: **~14,250 BP**.
- **Later components:** Holocene Archaic/Woodland through Historic materials complete the sequence; only the pre-Clovis claims are load-bearing for peopling debates.

## Significance

Together with [[monte-verde]] and other candidates, Meadowcroft forced the field to treat Clovis-first as a **hypothesis under challenge**. Meltzer: even if pre-Clovis only adds a few millennia (e.g. Meadowcroft ~14,250 years), stakes include hunter-gatherer colonization of empty continents, population history, and calibration of genetic/linguistic change—not mere academic turf. As of Meltzer 2009, however, Meadowcroft had **not** achieved Monte Verde’s acceptance status: the exception proving that if a site is not convincing before general pre-Clovis acceptance, it is no more convincing after. Meltzer also notes that, except Meadowcroft and Paisley, few eastern/western caves show regular habitation before ~10,500 BP (basement culture usually Late Paleoindian/Dalton).

## Key Debates

### Coal-contamination critique (Haynes vs Adovasio)

*(Positions — as of Meltzer 2009.)* **C. Vance Haynes** suspected Pennsylvania coal-country ancient carbon (dust/groundwater) inflated Meadowcroft ages. **Adovasio:** nearest coal 800 m away; no coal particles/spores in samples; multi-lab consistency and stratigraphic order argue against contamination; geologists say groundwater never within 10 m of deepest occupation; Paul Goldberg microstratigraphy found no saturation/coal. Haynes dismissed Goldberg without explanation and demanded short-lived seeds/nuts dates; Adovasio refused as lose-lose (endless spiral of tests). Live methodological dispute when Meltzer wrote; not resolved in that book.

### Paleoenvironmental anomaly

Skeptics argued ~14,250 BP Meadowcroft should show near-glacial conditions (Laurentide within ~150 km) yet organic samples show temperate deciduous taxa (oak/hickory/walnut; white-tailed deer, flying squirrel, passenger pigeon)—not boreal/extinct megafauna. Meltzer: sample tiny (278 bones lower IIa, 11 identifiable; 11.9 g plants from glacial levels of 1.4 M fragments)—“hopelessly inadequate basis to judge whether the anomaly is real, or a freak of sampling.” Adovasio team cites low elevation and south aspect.

### Place among post-Monte Verde contenders

Meltzer lists ~20 North American contenders by Adovasio’s count (Big Eddy, Cactus Hill, Saltville, Chesrow, Paisley, Pendejo, Topper, etc.); **none fully accepted** by the North American community as of 2009. Healthy skepticism still warranted. See [[pre-clovis]], [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]].

### Mithen 2003 layer

Mithen notes coal-region contamination critiques and Adovasio’s re-tests finding no contamination (as of mid-1990s literature Mithen uses). Treat absolute chronology as **disputed**.

## Historiography

Meadowcroft is a case study in **superb excavation without full monographic closure**: high technical quality, contested ¹⁴C association, paleoenvironmental sampling limits, and sociology of endless “one more test” demands (Adovasio–Haynes explosion after the Dallas Monte Verde toast, as Meltzer narrates). Contrast with [[monte-verde]], which met the same high bar via peat seal, multi-lab ages, and elite visit. Status as of Meltzer 2009: serious candidate, not closed case.

**Milner 2021:** still reports Meadowcroft as the best-known eastern pre-Clovis candidate (stone tools, burned basketry in lowest levels) while noting that **skepticism remains** despite excavation quality and many radiocarbon dates — consistent with the disputed standing above (as of Milner 2021).
