# Dereivka

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Dereivka** is a multi-component locus near the Dnieper Rapids (Ukraine): a large **Dnieper-Donets II** communal cemetery and a later **[[sredni-stog-culture|Sredni Stog]]** settlement that became the type case in Western debates on horse domestication and riding. D. Ya. Telegin’s mid-20th-century excavations (including the 1964 head-and-hoof stallion deposit with two dogs) supplied the bone series V. I. Bibikova analyzed and that [[gimbutas-marija|Marija Gimbutas]] used in her Kurgan-invasion narrative. [[anthony-david-w|David Anthony]] and Dorcas Brown’s [[bit-wear-analysis|bit-wear]] work (published 1991, **retracted for this specimen 2000**) made Dereivka internationally famous — then infamous — when AMS dates showed the “cult stallion” was a **Scythian-era intrusion**.

## Occupation Sequence

| Component | Dates (Anthony 2007) | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| DDII cemetery | Oldest graves ~5200–5000 BCE after ¹⁵N correction on human bone | Among largest DDII cemeteries: **173** individuals; communal multilayer pits, ochre, feast debris |
| Sredni Stog settlement (phase IIa) | **4200–3700 BCE** (ten radiocarbon dates) | Three excavated Sredni Stog structures; horses **63%** of animal bones |
| Intrusive pit (stallion + dogs) | Combined ~**800–200 BCE** (Scythian) | OxA 7185 tooth 410–200 BCE; Ki 6962 skull bone 790–520 BCE; not recognized as later in 1964 |

Sredni Stog cemetery at the site was small (Anthony: 12 single graves vs 173 DDII individuals) — part of the broader shift from communal pits to individual graves and early low kurgans.

## Significance

1. **Horse economy (Eneolithic, still valid as faunal pattern):** Bibikova’s count — 2,408 horse bones, MNI ≥51, more than half the mammals butchered, ~9,000 kg of meat; horses 63% mammal NISP, cattle 16%, red deer 10%. Anthony links elevated horse use vs DDII to colder climate ~4200–3800 BCE and domestic-horse maintenance advantages. This is **dietary and economic** evidence, not by itself proof of riding.
2. **Failed early-riding claim:** Anthony & Brown 1989 molded the cult-stallion P2s (bevels 3.5 and 4 mm, deep enamel abrasion); only one clearly worn horse among five surviving P2s. 1991 *Scientific American* / *Antiquity* announcements of riding ~2000 years earlier than then known were overturned by direct AMS: the deposit is Scythian, placed in a pit dug into the Eneolithic settlement. 2000 *Antiquity* retraction. **Eneolithic riding at Dereivka cannot be dated by this specimen.**
3. **Historiographic fulcrum:** Bibikova (1967) domestic; Bökönyi and Nobis agreed; Gimbutas used domesticated Dereivka horses for Kurgan waves 4200–3200 BCE; Western archaeology largely rejected massive Kurgan invasions in the 1980s (Häusler); Levine (1990) argued age/sex ratios fit wild hunting; Anthony’s bit-wear then reopened and again closed the riding claim for this site. Mallory (1989) retained a steppe homeland more cautiously (via Anthony).

## Key Debates

- Horse domestication vs hunting at Eneolithic Dereivka — faunal abundance vs age/sex arguments (Levine); symbolic domestication claims elsewhere (e.g. [[khvalynsk-culture]]) do not transfer automatically.
- [[bit-wear-analysis]] methodology and the Dereivka retraction; contrast with Botai/Kozhai 1 results Anthony still treats as early riding evidence (~3700–3500 BCE) in northern Kazakhstan ([[botai-culture]]).
- Relation to [[sredni-stog-culture]], Suvorovo–Novodanilovka elites, and [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]] / Gimbutas Kurgan narrative.
- Radiocarbon hygiene: freshwater reservoir effect on human-bone dates at Rapids cemeteries ([[radiocarbon-dating]]).
