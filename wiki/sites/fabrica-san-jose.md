# Fábrica San José

## Discovery and Excavation History

Small Formative village in the Etla arm of the Valley of Oaxaca, excavated at household scale by **Robert Drennan** within the Michigan Oaxaca program. Synthesized in [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996|Marcus & Flannery 1996]] as a **third-order** community in the [[san-jose-mogote|San José Mogote]] hierarchy.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Marcus & Flannery 1996 citing Drennan.)*

- **Guadalupe phase (850–700 BCE):** Alliance-building evidence includes **hypogamy** — high-status San José Mogote women buried at satellite villages — plus feasting signatures (e.g. dog-feast contexts discussed at regional scale).
- **Rosario phase (700–500 BCE):** Est. **50–80 persons** (10–16 households). No residences matching SJM high-status compounds, but relatively large houses, sumptuary goods, and cranial deformation. High-status households show higher bowl / Socorro Fine Gray frequencies (entertainment interpretation); lower-status households more jars and drab wares. Salt production by boiling saline spring water; fiber spinning; leatherwork tools. Supplied SJM with salt and travertine; still linked by hypogamous marriage.
- **End of Rosario / MA I transition:** Among Etla communities losing population with SJM’s core abandonment at Monte Albán’s founding.

## Significance

Type case for **rank gradients below the paramount center**: how chiefdom integration worked through marriage, craft/salt provisioning, and ceramic status markers without full state institutions. Feeds [[chiefdom]], [[hereditary-rank]], [[settlement-hierarchy]].

## Key Debates

Survey bias: decorated gray ware underestimates low-status Rosario population (Marcus & Flannery methodological note).
