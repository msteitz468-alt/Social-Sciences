## Discovery and Excavation History

Highland ceremonial center at the Mosna–Huachecsa confluence (Ancash). Tello established Chavín as a horizon-defining "mother culture" in early Peruvian archaeology; later work (Lumbreras, Burger, and others) refined Old Temple / New Temple sequences and gallery systems ([[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]]; [[scarre-human-past-2013]]).

## Occupation Sequence

U-shaped / multi-phase temple growth; internal stone-slab galleries; sunken courts (circular earlier, rectangular later in Moseley's synthesis). The **Lanzón** and related images of the clawed staff-bearing deity ([[staff-god]]) support oracle/pilgrimage readings (Rowe and others, attributed). Moseley stresses export of *cult* more than bulk goods — a pilgrimage/oracle model for Early Horizon integration. Northern-sphere connections (Cupisnique coast, Kuntur Wasi, Pacopampa) vs southern sphere (Paracas, Pukara) frame the horizon's dual geography.

## Significance

Type site of the Early Horizon religious-iconographic complex conventionally labeled [[chavin-culture]]. Technological and artistic associations (textiles, metallurgy) radiate with the cult; political unity is **not** demonstrated.

## Key Debates

- Oracle center vs regional capital vs elite pilgrimage node.
- How far "Chavín influence" implies religious conversion, exchange, or elite emulation.
- Dating revisions post-2001 may compress or shift the classic Early Horizon window — date-stamp "(as of Moseley 2001 / Scarre 2013)."
