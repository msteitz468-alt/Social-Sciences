## Discovery and Excavation History

Kot Diji lies in the Rohri Hills, Sindh. Per Possehl (2002), the Pakistan Department of Archaeology excavated the site in 1955 and again in 1957, working two areas: Area A, a mound rising approximately 12 m, and Area B, a Lower Town. The excavation is a mid-20th-century, state-department-directed campaign; Possehl does not detail its stratigraphic recording methods in this chapter beyond the resulting phase sequence and destruction evidence.

## Occupation Sequence

Kot Diji is the type-site for the Early Harappan phase Possehl and the broader field term "Kot Dijian" — the site's own sequence defines the phase name used civilization-wide, including at [[harappa]] (Period 2) and other Early Harappan contexts. The Kot Dijian occupation at the type-site itself ends in a massive burning event, evidenced archaeologically as a destruction horizon. Possehl also notes a possible Transitional-stage occupation bridging the Kot Dijian and Mature Harappan phases at the site. Calibration status for the underlying chronology is not stated in the source.

## Significance

Beyond its role as the Kot Dijian type-site, Possehl records a notable absence at Kot Diji: no imported luxury materials such as lapis lazuli or turquoise have been recovered there, in contrast to some other Indus-tradition sites with longer-distance exchange connections. This absence is treated as a data point on the site's economic/exchange position within the broader [[harappan-civilization]] network rather than as evidence of isolation per se.

## Key Debates

The burning-horizon end to Kot Diji's Kot Dijian occupation is one of several site-specific destruction/discontinuity episodes relevant to broader questions about the Early-to-Mature Harappan transition, though Possehl does not extend this single-site event into a civilization-wide "invasion" or "conflict" narrative in this chapter. Kot Diji's Early Harappan sequence, like those at [[rakhigarhi]] and [[kalibangan]], reinforces the "continuity beneath discontinuity" pattern Possehl documents for the transition into the Mature Harappan.
