# Xochicalco

Epiclassic hilltop center in western Morelos, mapped by Kenneth Hirth (Pennsylvania State University) and characterized by [[sources/coe-koontz-mexico-2013|Coe & Koontz (2013)]] as "Maya-influenced and perhaps even Maya-directed," forming "a kind of bridge between Classic and Post-Classic central Mexico."

## Discovery and Excavation History

Kenneth Hirth's site mapping documented a defensively terraced hilltop with a planned network of stone causeways and guarded access ramps, compared by Coe & Koontz to Classic Maya sacbeob (causeways) such as those at Tikal. The site was founded before AD 700 and remained active throughout the Epiclassic, with extensive contacts to the Maya area, Zapotec/Mixtec Oaxaca (see [[cultures/zapotec-civilization]]), and Classic central Veracruz.

## Occupation Sequence

The **Temple of the Feathered Serpent**, a talud-tablero platform with an unusually tall talud, bears reliefs of undulating Feathered Serpents with cut-shell symbols echoing Teotihuacan's Temple of Quetzalcoatl, enclosing seated lord figures wearing a war-serpent headdress descended from Teotihuacan iconography. Coe & Koontz suggest (attributed) no highland Mexican prototype exists for the seated lord figures, which likely derive from Late Classic Maya jade-plaque rulers, though the raised body-edge treatment recalls reliefs at [[sites/el-tajin]]. A monumental glyphic group on the temple's talud reads (Teotihuacan-style) as "9 Reptile Eye" — also found at [[sites/cacaxtla]] — surmounted by a house glyph; Alfonso Caso believed this glyph equivalent to "9 Wind," the later name of the culture hero Quetzalcoatl, a reading other epigraphers regard with less certainty (**[POSITION]**, see [[thinkers/caso-alfonso]]). An underground observatory cave near the main plaza has a man-made vertical shaft through which sunlight reaches the cave floor on the two annual zenith-passage days, paralleling similar chambers recently discovered at [[sites/teotihuacan]]. Xochicalco's main I-shaped ball court shares exact dimensions with the later northern ball court at Toltec Tula.

## Significance

Xochicalco is cited by Coe & Koontz as a possible contributor to Teotihuacan's Epiclassic-era decline, through siphoning of trade and migrants from the collapsing metropolis — one strand of the unresolved Teotihuacan-collapse **POSITION** set (see [[sites/teotihuacan]]).

## Key Debates

- **[POSITION]** Caso's "9 Reptile Eye" = "9 Wind"/Quetzalcoatl epigraphic identification is contested among epigraphers.
- Xochicalco's role (siphoning rival vs. incidental beneficiary) in Teotihuacan's collapse is unresolved.
