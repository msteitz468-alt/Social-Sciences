# Karim Shahir

Open-air site in the Iraqi hill country used by [[braidwood-robert|Braidwood]] as a type **[[incipient-cultivation|incipient-era]]** assemblage in *[[braidwood-prehistoric-men-1959|Prehistoric Men]]* (3rd ed. 1959) — earlier and thinner than **[[jarmo|Jarmo]]**, with a probable gap still separating the two in his sequence.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Documented in Braidwood’s mid-century Iraq program alongside Jarmo and related sites (M’lefaat; Solecki’s Zawi Chemi near [[shanidar|Shanidar]]). The 1959 popular book is not an excavation report; depth here is Braidwood’s public synthesis only.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Braidwood 1959.)*

- **Temporary open site / encampment** on a hill; **one very shallow level** — not long-lived architecture of village type.
- Assemblage dominated by **chipped flint**; ground stone, unbaked clay, shell, bone present; “architecture” minimal.
- No direct vegetable-food evidence reported in the popular account; animal bones show hunting continuum.
- Paired with **[[natufian-culture|Natufian]]** as candidate for the era of **incipient cultivation and animal domestication** — Braidwood’s “hunch” that hints point toward food production, while most subsistence remains collected/hunted.

## Significance

Fills (imperfectly) the pre-Jarmo slot in the **[[hilly-flanks]]** sequence; underwrites the claim that full village-farming has a thin archaeological prelude. Related open sites he notes as slightly more “settled in”: M’lefaat and **Zawi Chemi Shanidar** (Solecki; early radiocarbon cited ~8900 ± 300 B.C. as of 1959).

## Key Debates

[[oasis-natural-habitat-debate]]; status of Natufian/Karim Shahir as food-producers vs intensive collectors; gap to Jarmo.

## Historiography

Known in this vault primarily via Braidwood 1959; specialist reanalysis outstanding.

### Roux (*Ancient Iraq*, 1992, attributed)

*(Attributed — [[roux-ancient-iraq-1992|Roux 1992]], who spells the site "Karim-Shehir" — same site, canonical slug retained here as an alias.)* Roux places the site 10 km east of Chemchemal: a single occupation level covering roughly 2 acres, with microlithic flint tools, flint sickle blades, chipped and ground stone hoes, and milling stones; an irregular pebble pavement suggests hut floors, though no clear building plans survive. He treats it as roughly contemporary with Mlefaat and separated from Zawi Chemi Shanidar by a gap of roughly a thousand years, interpreting the site as a semi-nomadic camp representing an early step toward sedentism, within the broader Mesolithic-to-Neolithic transition window (c. 9000–7000 BCE per his chapter's Mesolithic framing).
