# Etowah

Major [[mississippian-culture|Mississippian]] multi-mound center in northwestern Georgia. Cited in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] (Ch. 6–8) among principal Southeastern chiefdom centers; also in contact-period narratives of Spanish exploration.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Long-known mound center; classic Southeastern archaeology case for platform mounds and elite mortuary display ([[southeastern-ceremonial-complex|SECC]]).

## Occupation Sequence

Mississippian chiefdom capital with platform mounds and ranked burials; occupations into the late prehistoric/contact interface.

## Significance

Southern Mississippian peer of [[moundville]] and [[cahokia]] (smaller than Cahokia); SECC iconography; contact-period documentary intersections.

## Key Debates

Political centralization; chronology of mound stages; Spanish-era identification of polities.
