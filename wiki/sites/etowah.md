# Etowah

Major [[mississippian-culture|Mississippian]] multi-mound center in northwestern Georgia. Cited in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] (Ch. 6–8) among principal Southeastern chiefdom centers; also in contact-period narratives of Spanish exploration.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Long-known mound center; classic Southeastern archaeology case for platform mounds and elite mortuary display ([[southeastern-ceremonial-complex|SECC]]).

## Occupation Sequence

Mississippian chiefdom capital with platform mounds and ranked burials; occupations into the late prehistoric/contact interface.

## Significance

Southern Mississippian peer of [[moundville]] and [[cahokia]] (smaller than Cahokia); SECC iconography; contact-period documentary intersections.

## Key Debates

Political centralization; chronology of mound stages; Spanish-era identification of polities.

## Pauketat 2007 additions

*(Attributed — [[pauketat-chiefdoms-delusions-2007]].)*

- **Chronology problem (fact + his reading):** conventional Early Etowah phase begins A.D. 1000, but four of seven radiocarbon dates from Early Etowah features intercept at A.D. 1160–1238 (one-sigma range of all seven: A.D. 899–1275; King 2003) — Pauketat argues true beginnings may postdate ~1150, which also destabilizes the proposed A.D. 1200–1250 "Early Savannah" hiatus (dated by absence of evidence). Preceding **Woodstock culture** built defensive walls — "endemic warfare and resource stress" backdrop (Knight & Steponaitis); King (2003) reads early Etowah-area chiefdoms as alliances of once-hostile groups, a Late Woodland "solution to endemic warfare."
- **Wilbanks-phase boom (A.D. 1250–1375):** post-hiatus reconsolidation with new stages on Mounds A and C, paved rock-lined plaza, compound walls, palisade and moat, and up to five secondary centers (King 2005). [POSITION] King's dual-processual "network strategy" reading vs. Pauketat's transregional-preeminence reading built on adoption of the Cahokia-rooted **Morning Star / bird-man legend** (Brown 2005, after Radin and Hall) and fictive-kin gifting of **Braden A copper plates** south to Lake Jackson and Rood-phase sites — political history rather than the older SECC "exchange corridor" framing.
- **Fall:** palisade burned ~A.D. 1375 (likely sacked); bird-man imagery is "few to nonexistent" in later Lamar complexes — the legitimating story "intentionally forgotten" (his gloss). Reconsolidation at the King site as de Soto's "Coosa"; post-1350 South Appalachian phases fold into the **Lamar** ceramic phenomenon, which appears to acquire ethnic reality only after 1550 (Hally).
