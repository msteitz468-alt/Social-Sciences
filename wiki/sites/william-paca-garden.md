# William Paca Garden

Eighteenth-century formal garden in **Annapolis, Maryland**, associated with **William Paca** (signer of the Declaration of Independence; Maryland governor). Originally laid out in the **1760s**, the garden disappeared over the centuries and was **reconstructed in the 1960s–1970s** after archaeological investigations revealed garden features. [[leone-mark-p|Mark P. Leone]] made it the classic demonstration that elite American landscape can operate as **[[ideology]]** (rules of perspective naturalizing order) rather than mere taste — central case in *[[leone-critical-historical-archaeology-2010|Critical Historical Archaeology]]* Ch. 6 (reprint of the 1984 essay "Interpreting Ideology in Historical Archaeology: Using the Rules of Perspective in the William Paca Garden in Annapolis, Maryland").

## Discovery and Excavation History

Garden features recovered archaeologically before Historic Annapolis–era reconstruction; the restored garden is what Leone first saw in **1981** (taken there by Annapolis contacts as Archaeology in Annapolis was forming). Excavation quality of the reconstruction-era work and the restored fabric's relationship to 1760s surfaces are part of the interpretive problem: the object of analysis is a **heritage landscape**, not a pristine colonial surface. Leone's method combines plan, optical geometry, documentary context on Paca, and comparison to Renaissance/Baroque planned landscapes (Boboli, Villa Lante, Villa d'Este).

## Occupation Sequence

Colonial elite townhouse garden (Paca era, mid–late 18th c.) → later alteration and loss of formal garden → 20th-c. archaeological recovery and reconstruction → ongoing tourist and educational use within historic Annapolis.

## Significance

*(Attributed — Leone 1984 / 2010.)* Leone argued planned Renaissance–Baroque–Georgian landscapes are designed to **work on the viewer as participant**, not merely to display taste. The Paca Garden's perspective geometry teaches a natural, lawful visual order that **naturalizes hierarchical social order** while Paca's wealth rested on slavery — ideology masking the contradiction between professed liberty and slaveholding. Links [[georgian-worldview]] patterning (order, control of nature, symmetry) to capitalist/class ideology rather than only to cognitive "worldview." For [[critical-archaeology]], the garden is the empirical anchor of "excavating ideology."

## Key Debates

Taste vs. ideology readings of Georgian landscapes; how far restored fabric can support 18th-c. ideology claims; relation of Annapolis critical program to Deetz–Glassie Georgian synthesis (pattern shared; mechanism rewritten). See [[critical-archaeology]], [[marxist-archaeology]], [[ideology]].

## Historiography

Canonical example in Anglophone theory surveys (Hodder & Hutson on ideology naturalization; Johnson textbook map of Leone/Annapolis). Primary densification in this vault: Leone 2010 reprint + framing. Full reconstruction archaeology monographs and Historic Annapolis archives remain outstanding.
