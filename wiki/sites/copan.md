# Copan

## Discovery and Excavation History

Southeastern Classic capital. Long research history (Peabody, Honduran, Harvard Fash projects, etc.). [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]] stress dynastic founding, Teotihuacan-linked founder iconography, and catastrophic defeat by Quirigua.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]].)*

- **Preclassic:** Uir phase (c. 800–400 BC) Olmecized ceramics; Motagua jade sources nearby (blue-green jade of Olmec taste in Sierra de las Minas — 2001 discoveries cited).
- **Founding:** Yax K'uk' Mo' accedes AD 426; Hunal tomb; isotopic work points toward Caracol connections rather than Teotihuacan birthplace for the founder (as of C&H synthesis) even as Teotihuacan warrior imagery is used.
- **Late Classic:** 18 Rabbit (Waxaklajuun Ub'aah K'awiil) beheaded 4 May AD 738 after Quirigua rebellion under K'ahk' Tiliw (from 725). Hieroglyphic Stairway as dynastic manifesto (text labels Temple 25 in C&H — verify against specialist “Temple 26” convention).
- Ecological stress and deforestation enter [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]] discussions for the Copan pocket.

## Significance

Southeastern hinge of the Classic world; founder ideology; exemplar of dynastic warfare ([[quirigua]]); long epigraphic sequence.

## Key Debates

- Founder origins (local/Caracol/Teotihuacan imagery).
- Collapse ecology in a river valley setting.

## Densification from Sharer & Traxler 2006

*(Attributed — [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]]. Complements Coe & Houston synthesis above; note founder-origin tension with C&H’s Caracol-leaning isotopic reading.)*

### Research history, setting, and density

Known since 1576 (García de Palacios); Galindo’s 1834 royal tomb excavation (first reported Maya royal tomb); Stephens/Catherwood; Maudslay from 1885 opened the era of scientific Maya archaeology; Peabody 1891–94; Morley on monuments and valley settlement; Carnegie from 1935 (Acropolis river diversion, Hieroglyphic Stairway, Ball Court); Gordon Willey Copan Valley Project 1975–77; Instituto Hondureño phases 1977–85; later North Group/Group 8N-11, Copan Mosaic Project, Acropolis tunnels. Deciphered capital name **Oxwitik**. Positioned near the eastern margin of the southern highlands as a highland–lowland trade-route capital (with [[tonina]] on the northwest highland margin). Central Copan has the highest construction density of any Maya site in their Rice & Culbert summary tables (1,449 structures/km²) — reflecting valley confinement. Greater Copan Valley Late Classic peak ~18,000–25,000 (about one-fourth of greater Tikal). Uncounted-structure correction factors for population estimates: Copan +39–50% (vs Tikal ~+10%).

### Founding (426–427), founder identity, and Early Classic sequence

Sharer & Traxler argue archaeological and historical evidence that **[[tikal]] orchestrated** the 426 takeover of Copan and establishment of [[quirigua]] as Motagua subsidiary (jadeite, Ixtepeque obsidian, Caribbean gateway). Altar Q: **K’uk’ Mo’ Ajaw** took K’awiil at *wi’te’ naah* (5 Sept 426), became **K’inich Yax K’uk’ Mo’**, arrived Oxwitik 8 Feb 427 as *ochk’in kaloomte’* (same title as Siyaj K’ak’); sponsor likely Siyaj Chan K’awiil II; *wi’te’ naah* location unspecified (possibly Tikal). Supporting threads: Hombre de Tikal text (406) names a K’uk’ Mo’ during Yax Nuun Ayiin I’s reign; Altar Q portrait shows Teotihuacan warrior regalia; **Hunal Tomb** elderly male (55–70), healed combat injuries, Teotihuacan-style shell platelet helmet; **strontium isotopes: childhood/young adult in the Tikal region of the Petén**, final years at Copan; NAA vessels from Tikal region (2), Central Mexico (5), Copan Valley (11), Kaminaljuyu-region sources. *(Wiki note: Coe & Houston 2015 synthesis above leans toward Caracol connections for founder isotopes — treat as competing readings as of their respective dates.)* Early Classic sequence includes Margarita Tomb (likely founder’s queen / Ruler 2’s mother); Ruler 4 K’altuun Hix; Ruler 7 Balam Nehn (accession 524; named on Caracol Stela 16); monument breakage ca. 553–564 possible disruption (authors float possible Calakmul/ally strike contemporaneous with Tikal’s 562 defeat); Moon Jaguar Ruler 10 (553–578) ends Early Classic era; Rosalila temple over founder’s core dedicated to the founder over 400 years later (legitimacy through ancestor cult).

### Economy, labor, and strong-state model

Carrelli’s Early Classic Acropolis estimates: founder (ca. AD 426–37) required >175,000 person-days for initial platform/buildings — about one month/year from every adult male in the Copan Valley — with labor burden rising under Ruler 2. Aoyama’s research: dynastic state managed Ixtepeque obsidian blade-core distribution. Authors classify Copan with Tikal and Calakmul as best Classic **strong-state** examples (capital qualitatively different — palaces, tombs, temples, dynastic monuments). Preclassic Los Achiotes ball court earliest in the Copan Valley; Late Preclassic hilltop defensive shift in the valley; Early Preclassic Group 9N-8 pottery with pan-Mesoamerican motifs (not proof of Olmec political control). Rosalila cache: twelve elaborate eccentric flint figures probably once royal scepters; textile fragments with the flints.

### AD 738 crisis and Terminal Classic path

[[quirigua]]’s K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Yopaat recorded inauguration under supervision (*u kabi*) of Copan’s Waxaklajuun Ub’aah K’awiil, then in **738** captured and sacrificed the 13th Copan ruler; Quirigua inscriptions also claim capture of Copan’s patron gods. North Group associated (after Ashmore/Harrison cosmogram readings) with Waxaklajuun Ub’aah K’awiil’s apotheosis after the Quirigua sacrifice. Authors’ collapse path for Copan: violence/warfare played **much less** causal role than in Petexbatun — deforestation, soil erosion, and loss of confidence in rulers mattered more; weakening triggered by loss of the thirteenth ruler and Quirigua’s breakaway, then power-sharing that decentralized authority, culminating in Ukit Took’s failure to succeed Yax Pasaj Chan Yopaat. Drought not treated as critical for Copan demise (unlike reservoir-dependent Tikal). Terminal Classic arrivals at Copan and Quirigua appear **after** demise of centralized order — more result than cause. Earliest Maya-area metal: gold-copper figurine legs (Costa Rica/Panama origin) at Copan.
