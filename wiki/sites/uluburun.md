# Uluburun Shipwreck

## Discovery and Excavation History

Found in 1982 by a Turkish sponge diver (copper “metal biscuits with ears” = oxhide ingots). Excavated underwater by George Bass and Cemal Pulak (Institute of Nautical Archaeology). [[cline-eric-h|Cline]] (2014) treats Uluburun as vindication of Bass’s earlier Cape Gelidonya program (~1200 BCE wreck), which had been met with skepticism about LBA east–west trade scale.

## Occupation Sequence

Single wreck event ~1300 BCE (as of Cline 2014 synthesis). Not a settlement sequence.

## Significance

- **Microcosm of the LBA “first global age”** (Cline attributed): multi-origin cargo (Cyprus-region copper, tin from distant sources, Near Eastern and Aegean goods) on what appears a Canaanite ship, with personal items suggesting at least two Mycenaeans aboard.
- Material evidence class for international trade that texts (Amarna, Ugarit) also document — independent convergence on interconnection before [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].
- Anchors debates on royal gift vs private merchant vs “shopping expedition” models of LBA exchange.

## Key Debates

Ownership and voyage purpose · tin sources · absolute dating refinements · relation to later Cape Gelidonya and Point Iria wrecks as evidence that some trade continued near 1200 BCE. See [[cline-1177-bc-study]], [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].
