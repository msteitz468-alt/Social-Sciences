# Heit el-Ghurab (Giza workers' town)

Old Kingdom settlement south of the Giza plateau — often called the **“Lost City of the Pyramid Builders”** or the town behind the **Wall of the Crow** — excavated by [[lehner-mark|Mark Lehner]]’s Giza Plateau Mapping Project. Primary wiki source: Lehner, *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|The Complete Pyramids]]* (1997); later GPMP monographs not yet ingested.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Until the late 1980s, reconstruction of the living pyramid community rested mainly on tomb scenes, temple towns, and surface traces. Lehner’s tables of “recent explorations” list 1988–89 Yale work on “workmen’s barracks” at Giza and continuous GPMP settlement archaeology thereafter (as of the 1997 book). Earlier misidentification of long galleries west of Khafre as “workmen’s barracks” is corrected toward **royal workshop** function once settlement evidence south of the Wall of the Crow is in hand (Lehner 1997, attributed).

## Occupation Sequence

Fourth Dynasty construction-era occupation associated with provisioning the Giza workforce and permanent support staff. Lehner links harbours and the Main Wadi mouth (defined by causeways and the huge southern boundary wall, possibly begun under Khufu) to a zone of settlement, storage, and possibly palace functions at the foot of the plateau (1997). Fine phasing and post-OK reuse require later GPMP publications.

## Significance

1. **Type site for Old Kingdom pyramid labour archaeology** — empirical counterweight to purely textual or Classical (Herodotus) workforce images.
2. **Provisioning complex:** bakeries (*bedja* bread moulds; egg-carton baking pits under ash) and adjacent **fish-processing** buildings (“loaves and fishes” evidence) (Lehner 1997).
3. **Urbanism of the provider state** — comparative link to planned pyramid towns such as [[kahun]] and to Kemp’s model-community program ([[kemp-barry-j]]).

## Key Debates

- **Who lived here?** Rotating conscript crews vs. permanent specialists vs. mixed population — Lehner’s synthesis favors permanent craftsmen plus large rotating peasant crews at plateau scale; site-level demography remains interpretive (POSITION).
- **Galleries as barracks vs. other functions** — functional attribution of long gallery blocks has shifted with excavation (INTERPRETATION, dated to 1997 state of knowledge).
- **Scale of Giza workforce** — site is evidence for *organization and diet*, not a direct headcount; numerical models (20–30k range, two crews of 2,000, etc.) are separate calculations (see [[giza]]).

## Historiography

Entered the wiki as a scaffold during the Lehner 1997 ingest. The 1997 book captures an early mature phase of GPMP results; densify and date-stamp aggressively when full Heit el-Ghurab monographs are ingested. Do not treat popular “workers were not slaves” press framings as the scholarly claim set — record Lehner’s **conscript + craftsmen** model as attributed position.

## Lehner densification (1997)

*(Attributed — [[lehner-mark]], *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997]]*, Living Pyramid / loaves-and-fishes chapters.)*

- **Area A and bakery evidence:** cache of large bell-shaped *bedja* pots; baking pits; mould counts used to model ration output.
- **Fish processing:** low troughs/shelves; ashy fibrous deposits with microscopic catfish and *Synodontis* bone — protein complement to bread.
- **Wall of the Crow / wadi mouth:** landscape package with harbours, quarries, and ramp-material (*tafla*) sources; ~9 million cu. m of stone moved at Giza over three generations (Lehner’s figure).
- **Menkaure-era dating links** and sealings/titles where reported — treat individual epigraphic points as FINDINGS of the 1997 synthesis pending specialist corpus.
- Comparative: Menkaure valley-temple town trajectory toward dense “sacred” occupation; Illahun/[[kahun]] as better-preserved planned pyramid-town type for MK.
