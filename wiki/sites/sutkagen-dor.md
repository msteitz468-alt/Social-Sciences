# Sutkagen-dor

## Discovery and Excavation History

The westernmost known Indus settlement, in the Dasht valley of the Makran coast
near the modern Iran–Pakistan border. First recorded by Major E. Mockler (1876)
and later worked by George Dales. Documented here from
[[wright-ancient-indus-2010|Wright (2010)]].

## Occupation Sequence

A lower town with a walled "citadel" built of locally available stone. Together
with its twin [[sotka-koh|Sotka-koh]], described by Dales and Lipo (1992:110)
as "virtually identical — both being in major valleys that provided access from
the seacoast to the inland Kej Valley... both had commanding positions for
controlling any movement of people or goods along the valley routes."

## Significance

An Indus-founded port/entrepôt on the maritime route between Gujarat and the
Persian Gulf (Magan/Oman). Wright (attributed) reads Sutkagen-dor and Sotka-koh
as conscious trader strategies to control new trade avenues, part of the Indus
engagement with [[meluhha|Meluhha]]–Magan–Dilmun Gulf trade. Both were
abandoned near the end of the Urban period (c. 1900 BCE).

## Key Debates

Its founding and abandonment feed the trade-organization discussion
([[indus-polity-debate]]) and the [[indus-transformation-debate|transformation]]
of Gulf trade networks.
