# Qafzeh

Cave near Nazareth, Israel: early [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern]] humans in Middle Palaeolithic context; keystone for Levant chronology. Core locus in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) ch. 5–6. Dates below are **as of Stringer & Gamble 1993**.

## Discovery and Excavation History

As of Stringer & Gamble (1993), René Neuville excavated Qafzeh 1933–35, recovering seven fossil hominids (two Upper Palaeolithic, the rest Middle Palaeolithic). Bernard Vandermeersch resumed work 1965–79, yielding remains of at least another fourteen Middle Palaeolithic individuals. As at [[skhul|Skhul]], several men, women and children appear deliberately buried.

Notable contexts they report:

- **Qafzeh 11** (child): burial accompanied by the skull and antlers of a large deer (presumably a complete head).
- **Qafzeh 9** (young adult): probable double burial with a six-year-old child across its feet.

S&G note the samples were early recognized as modern morphology with Middle Palaeolithic industry (“proto-Cro-Magnoids” in the 1950s), but only Vandermeersch’s detailed study made their importance clear once dating caught up. Plate captions in the 1993 book call Qafzeh the largest early modern sample then known worldwide.

## Occupation Sequence

**(As of Stringer & Gamble 1993.)** TL on burnt flints from early modern burial levels ~**92 ka**; ESR on associated mammal teeth **120–100 ka**. S&G stress the implications: Qafzeh early moderns ~3× older than European Cro-Magnons and ~30 ka older than the [[kebara|Kebara]] Neanderthal burial (~60 ka). Small-mammal stratigraphy had already suggested main Qafzeh modern levels older than [[tabun|Tabun]] level C (Neanderthal woman).

Upper Palaeolithic levels are also present (Neuville’s two UP fossils). The site thus spans the anatomy/industry mismatch that structures Levant debates rather than a simple Neanderthal→modern cultural succession.

## Significance

- With Skhul, primary Levantine evidence that **modern anatomy precedes Neanderthals locally** under 1980s–90s absolute dating — a load-bearing result for [[recent-african-origin]] as framed by Stringer & Gamble (position-laden synthesis, 1993 horizon).
- Comparative morphology (e.g. Qafzeh 9 vs [[shanidar|Shanidar]] 1): both with Middle Palaeolithic artifacts, but the modern Qafzeh individual is probably more ancient than the Shanidar Neanderthal (as of S&G 1993 figure caption).
- Tropical body proportions and modern pelvic morphology contrast with cold-adapted Middle Eastern Neanderthals (Kebara “cold body in a warm cave” argument).
- Archaeology of earliest Levantine moderns is S&G’s exhibit that modern physique and modern behaviour need not share a single origin centre ([[behavioral-modernity]]).
- Cann–Stoneking–Wilson 1987 mtDNA ~100 ka dispersal maps onto Skhul/Qafzeh ages in their 1993 narrative (**pre-aDNA genetics; provisional**).

## Key Debates

- **Three models after moderns proved older than Amud/Kebara Neanderthals** (S&G ch.5 box): (1) overlap zone of European Neanderthal and African modern lineages 120–40 ka; (2) moderns first (≥100 ka), Neanderthals arriving ~70 ka with glacial cooling, possibly displacing early moderns; (3) single variable regional population with Qafzeh and Kebara as extremes.
- **Burial intentionality and symbolism:** deer-head and double burial among stronger Middle Palaeolithic claims; S&G still distinguish complete skeletons from modern-style symbolic burial in general (position).
- **Proto-Cro-Magnon gap** to European Aurignacian moderns (~60 ka if TL/ESR stand).
- Relation to African early moderns (Omo Kibish 1, Klasies) as part of a morphological transition before 100 ka (S&G African synthesis).
- Ongoing absolute-dating revision (post-1993) can reorder coexistence arguments — date-stamp all uses.

### Klein — *The Human Career* (2nd ed., 1999)

*(Attributed — [[klein-human-career-1999]], [[klein-richard]]; as of Klein 1999.)*

Klein dates the near-modern Qafzeh (and [[skhul]]) humans by TL and ESR to ~120–80 ka — **predating**
the Levantine [[neanderthals]] of Kebara/Amud (~65–47 ka). He reads them as an Out-of-Africa range
extension during the mild Last Interglacial, later displaced by Neanderthals migrating in as climate
deteriorated (~71 ka) — a "reversal" of the European sequence. Crucially, Klein argues these
near-moderns were behaviorally like the Neanderthals ("cognitively human, but not cognitively modern
in the sense that all living people are") — a key plank of his anatomy-before-behavior thesis (see
[[behavioral-modernity]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]], [[stringer-chris]]; as of Stringer 2012.*

**TL breakthrough (~1988).** First application of thermoluminescence on heated hearth flints at Qafzeh gave ~**90 ka** — more than twice the generally expected ~40 ka age — supporting or exceeding rodent relative dating (Bar-Yosef had proposed up to ~70 ka). ESR/TL suite with Skhul yields ~**90–120 ka** for the early moderns, older than Kebara (~60 ka TL) and Amud Neanderthals. “African” dental traits shared with Skhul (Stringer, Compton, Humphrey).

**Symbolism ~100 ka.** Pierced shells and red ochre potentially associated with modern burials; one child covered by huge deer antlers; possible woman+child double burial. Together with Skhul, these are Stringer’s oldest accepted **symbolic burials** (as of 2012). Armitage et al. at Jebel Faya (UAE) tools look “African” and unlike contemporaneous Skhul/Qafzeh industries — possible separate early Arabia vs Levant dispersals of uncertain fate (position).

**Behavioural modernity debate.** Klein treats Qafzeh/Skhul as anatomically modern but behaviourally like Neanderthals (MP industry); Stringer records expanding MSA symbolic evidence (Blombos, shells, ochre) that challenges Klein’s hard ~50 ka neural event while still distinguishing early Levantine moderns from later European figurative art (~40 ka). EQ peak (~5.3–5.4) shared with Cro-Magnons. Possible early admixture window with Neanderthals in the Levant ~120 ka floated for the autosomal ~2% signal.

### Tattersall — *Masters of the Planet* (2012)

*Source: [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012]], [[tattersall-ian]]. **As of Tattersall 2012.***

**Assemblage mix.** After Omo/Herto, growing African inventory of unequivocal modern morphology; none indisputably as early as the more/less complete skeleton buried ≥**100 ka** (latest reckoning in his text) at Jebel Qafzeh — clearly *H. sapiens*, as is an adolescent nearby. **Same site:** larger numbers of big-brained hominids that are **not** standard *H. sapiens* and **not** Neanderthals. All associated with [[mousterian-industry]] kits more or less identical to those of Neanderthals documented in Israel the same period (Neanderthal sites in the region from at least ~160 ka to ~45 ka). Thoroughly anatomically modern Qafzeh individuals reinforce a mixed/newly mixing population notion and local association of anatomical moderns with Mousterian — **industry ≠ species** (attributed).

**Identity options (positions).** Brushing nonstandard forms under “archaic *H. sapiens*” evades the issue. Possibilities Tattersall weighs: (1) entirely distinct lineage otherwise unrecorded; (2) “more intriguing”: curious anatomy from interbreeding between moderns and N. African Aterian-like populations ([[jebel-irhoud]], Dar-es-Soltan) — **not** with Neanderthals. Dual-corridor greened-Sahara scenario ~120 ka (Aterians east then north; sub-Saharan Nile corridor) offered as speculative encounter frame; future DNA may test (as of 2012 hope).

**Behaviour and failed foray.** Whoever the Qafzeh moderns were, not demonstrably behaving significantly differently from Neanderthals. Early anatomical moderns’ foray outside Africa ultimately unsuccessful; by ~60 ka Neanderthals seem back in charge of the Levant until much later cognitive/technological superiority. Initial excursion fortuitous under benevolent climate; later retreat/local extinction quite likely under extreme aridity ~60 ka cold snap. Rapid world takeover awaited symbolic behaviour patterns (**thesis**). See [[skhul]], [[homo-sapiens]], [[behavioral-modernity]], [[global-colonization]].
