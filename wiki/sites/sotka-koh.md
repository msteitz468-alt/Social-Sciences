# Sotka-koh

## Discovery and Excavation History

An Indus port settlement in the Shadi Kaur valley of the Makran coast, worked
by George Dales in the 1960s. Documented here from
[[wright-ancient-indus-2010|Wright (2010)]].

## Occupation Sequence

A lower town with a walled "citadel" of local stone, the near-twin of
[[sutkagen-dor|Sutkagen-dor]] — Dales and Lipo (1992:110) call the pair
"virtually identical," both positioned to control movement of goods between the
seacoast and the inland Kej valley.

## Significance

An Indus-founded coastal entrepôt on the maritime route toward Magan (Oman) and
the Persian Gulf. Founded by Indus settlers who brought Harappan material
culture; abandoned near the end of the Urban period (as of Wright 2010). Part of
the Indus [[meluhha|Meluhha]]–Magan–Dilmun trade system.
