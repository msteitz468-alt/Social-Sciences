# Ali Kosh (Deh Luran plain, SW Iran)

Early dry-farming village on the Deh Luran plain, south-western Iran — empirical spine of [[flannery-kent-v|Kent V. Flannery]]’s 1969 ecological model of Near Eastern [[domestication]] (with Hole, Neely, and Helbaek botanical work).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Excavated in the 1960s Deh Luran program (Hole, Flannery, Neely). As of Flannery’s seminar paper in *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*, full seed and bone counts were reserved for the final monographs; the 1969 paper gives abbreviated phase summaries. Excavation quality is modern multidisciplinary for its era (systematic plant and animal recovery) but chronology is **1960s uncalibrated radiocarbon** — treat all absolute ages as of Flannery 1969.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Flannery 1969.)*

| Phase | Approx. dates (as of 1969) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Bus Mordeh** | 7500–6750 B.C. | Early dry-farming + caprines; wild resources major in diet |
| **Ali Kosh** | 6750–6000 B.C. | Continues dry-farming complex |
| **Mohammad Jaffar** | 6000–5600 B.C. | More fallow-land plants; legumes shift toward fodder role |

Domestic inventory (early phases): emmer wheat, two-row hulled barley, goats, sheep. Wild plants include small legumes (*Astragalus, Trigonella, Medicago*), wild barley, *Aegilops*, *Lolium*, *Prosopis*, etc. Wild fauna: gazelle, onager, pig, aurochs, birds, fish, mussels.

**Bus Mordeh diet structure (Flannery 1969):** cereals ~3% of carbonized seeds but ~1/3 plant-food weight; small wild legumes ~94% seeds / ~1/3 plant weight; domestic caprines ~67% bones but only ~1/3 meat weight; wild ungulates ~25% bones / >60% meat weight — hunting and wild plants are major strategies, not supplements.

## Significance

- Type case that early “food producers” need not be better nourished or more diet-stable than collectors under erratic dry-farming rainfall.
- Demonstrates anthropogenic vegetation change (weeds, *Prosopis* increase, legumes → fodder) that can block return to prior gathering.
- Anchors Flannery’s **surplus-banking** reading of early caprine herding and his [[broad-spectrum-revolution]] continuity into early farming.
- Settlement preference for high water-retention / swamp-margin lands in early dry-farming spread arguments.

## Key Debates

- Absolute chronology and phase boundaries revised by later calibrated radiocarbon — date-stamp 1969 figures.
- Relation to [[oasis-natural-habitat-debate]] and [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]] via Flannery’s marginal-innovation model.
- Site ≠ people ≠ language ≠ genes.

## Historiography

Primary vault depth from Flannery 1969 in Ucko & Dimbleby; textbook mentions (W&O 2007) secondary. Full Deh Luran memoirs remain outstanding for specialist reanalysis.

## Bellwood *First Farmers* densification (2005)

*(Attributed — [[bellwood-peter]], *[[bellwood-first-farmers-2005|First Farmers]]* 2005.)*

- Listed with early agricultural sites near good water (advantages comparable to Levant spring/lake/river loci).
- By ~8000 BC (PPNB horizon in the Levant), Ali Kosh shows PPNB-like architecture and economies; goat herding; **claimed** sheep domestication (as of Bellwood’s synthesis).
- Located on the dry **Deh Luran Plain** outside major wild cereal ranges — so agriculture **had to be introduced**, analogous (for Bellwood) to farming entering Europe and Egypt from outside primary homeland zones rather than purely local invention on the spot.
