# Hilazon Tachtit Cave

Western Galilee cave with Late [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] specialized mortuary use. Dubreuil & Grosman (in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]) analyze the life history of macrolithic tools from the site.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Excavated under Leore Grosman; internationally known for a richly furnished Late Natufian burial often interpreted as a ritual specialist (claims outside this volume — attribute and date-stamp when those papers are ingested). The 2013 chapter focuses on groundstone use-wear and tool biographies.

## Occupation Sequence

Late Natufian ritual/burial-focused occupation rather than a full residential base-camp (as framed in the literature; densify from claims).

## Significance

Case for specialized Late Natufian ritual loci; groundstone life-history method exemplar.

## Key Debates

- Interpretation of the primary burial as “shaman” — contested symbolic reading; keep attributed.

## Densified findings from Dubreuil & Grosman 2013

*(Attributed — [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]].)*

- Macrolithic (groundstone) tool life-histories at a specialized Late Natufian burial cave — use-wear and curation as ritual-economy evidence.
- Scaphopod beads present in multi-site Kurzawska et al. sample; Red Sea taxa in Late contexts.
