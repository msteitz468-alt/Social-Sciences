# Herto (Middle Awash, Ethiopia)

Major Middle Awash locality yielding early [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern human]] crania (~160–154 ka as commonly cited), including material Tim White and colleagues assigned in part to *Homo sapiens idaltu*. Central African-origin fossil evidence in the [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Discovery and Excavation History

Recovered by the Middle Awash research program led by Tim White and colleagues (early 2000s announcements). Wiki coverage so far is **secondary** via [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]] and earlier textbook synthesis ([[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]]); not yet from primary excavation reports.

## Occupation Sequence

Late Middle Pleistocene; fossils intermediate between African archaic *Homo* and fully modern morphology in many treatments (as of Wenke & Olszewski 2007; Stringer 2012). Stringer (2012) calls them some of the **oldest and most massive** individuals of our species ever discovered.

## Significance

- Anchors early African *H. sapiens* morphology well before the main non-African expansion (~60–50 ka genetic estimates as of Stringer 2012).
- Supports [[recent-african-origin]] fossil chronology alongside [[omo-river|Omo Kibish]], [[klasies-river]], Jebel Irhoud (see [[jebel-irhoud]]), etc.
- Subspecies name *idaltu* is a taxonomic **position**, not settled wiki-voice classification.

## Key Debates

How “modern” the crania are; relationship to later MSA behavioral packages ([[behavioral-modernity]]); role in multi-wave vs single-wave African origin models. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Oxford Handbook densification (Mitchell & Lane 2013)

*Attributed — Sahnouni, Semaw & Rogers (Ch. 22), Marta Lahr (Ch. 23), Robert Foley (Ch. 20), Laura Basell (Ch. 27) in [[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]; **as of handbook ~2012**.*

Lahr reports Late Middle Pleistocene fossils from two Ethiopian sites — Omo Kibish and Herto, dated to c. **200 and 160 kya** respectively — as combining high vault, facial position, and chin that “clearly place these specimens within the modern human evolutionary line.” Foley similarly treats earliest *H. sapiens* samples Omo Kibish (~194 kya) and Herto (~160 kya) as combining modern and robust traits, with continuity within Africa likely in eastern Africa. Sahnouni et al. stress that **Herto ~160 kya has both Acheulean and MSA** with *H. sapiens* — underlining complex associations (or lack thereof) between hominin type and stone-tool technology; Mode 1–3 chronology synthesis lists Herto as a key overlap case. Basell notes Herto crania cutmarks suggest **mortuary practice, not formal burial** — a distinction for symbolic-behaviour checklists. Subspecies *H. sapiens idaltu* remains a taxonomic **position**; industry co-occurrence must not be read as identity between Acheulean/MSA and a single people.

### Tattersall — *Masters of the Planet* (2012)

*Source: [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012]], [[tattersall-ian]]. **As of Tattersall 2012.***

**Earliest anatomical moderns package.** With Omo Kibish (~**195 ka** rocks in his text), Herto (N. Ethiopia) anchors the first traces of people with bony structure exactly/almost exactly like today in NE Africa. Trio of crania including fairly complete child and adult; best considered *H. sapiens* though differing in few details; adult shows characteristic high voluminous vault with small face retracted beneath the front. Firmly dated **155–160 ka**. Together with Omo: distinctive basic *H. sapiens* cranial anatomy established by ~**200–160 ka**, coinciding with then molecular anthropologists’ origin dates from time-to-coalescence (as of Tattersall 2012).

**Cultural whimper.** Herto assemblage: handaxes + prepared-core flakes — latest recorded handaxes in Africa; at the end of a complex prolonged Acheulean → MSA transition. Cultural start “more of a whimper than a bang”; MSA stirrings of the kind later read as symbolic not expressed until after Omo/Herto times as then known. MSA origin ~300–200 ka most likely before recognizable *H. sapiens* — biology/culture disconnect (**thesis**). Reinforces that industry co-occurrence (Acheulean + MSA + early *sapiens*) is association, not identity. Anatomical modernity here precedes the symbolic/linguistic package that, for Tattersall, drives later [[global-colonization]] — see [[homo-sapiens]], [[behavioral-modernity]].
