# Herto (Middle Awash, Ethiopia)

Major Middle Awash locality yielding early [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern human]] crania (~160–154 ka as commonly cited), including material Tim White and colleagues assigned in part to *Homo sapiens idaltu*. Central African-origin fossil evidence in the [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Discovery and Excavation History

Recovered by the Middle Awash research program led by Tim White and colleagues (early 2000s announcements). Wiki coverage so far is **secondary** via [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]] and earlier textbook synthesis ([[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]]); not yet from primary excavation reports.

## Occupation Sequence

Late Middle Pleistocene; fossils intermediate between African archaic *Homo* and fully modern morphology in many treatments (as of Wenke & Olszewski 2007; Stringer 2012). Stringer (2012) calls them some of the **oldest and most massive** individuals of our species ever discovered.

## Significance

- Anchors early African *H. sapiens* morphology well before the main non-African expansion (~60–50 ka genetic estimates as of Stringer 2012).
- Supports [[recent-african-origin]] fossil chronology alongside [[omo-river|Omo Kibish]], [[klasies-river]], Jebel Irhoud (see [[jebel-irhoud]]), etc.
- Subspecies name *idaltu* is a taxonomic **position**, not settled wiki-voice classification.

## Key Debates

How “modern” the crania are; relationship to later MSA behavioral packages ([[behavioral-modernity]]); role in multi-wave vs single-wave African origin models. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].
