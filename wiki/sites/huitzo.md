# Huitzo

## Discovery and Excavation History

Northern Etla Formative center treated in [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996|Marcus & Flannery 1996]] as a **frontier / rival** node relative to [[san-jose-mogote|San José Mogote]] — possibly wholly or partly autonomous in Rosario times.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Marcus & Flannery 1996.)*

- **Guadalupe phase (850–700 BCE):** Built monumental platforms (e.g. Structure 4: pyramidal platform ~2 m high, >15 m wide, earth core, stone facing) with possible one-room temples — elite sponsorship comparable to SJM Structure 8.
- **Rosario phase:** ~3-ha village at extreme north Etla “as if monitoring the frontier.” Structure 2: large temple platform (stone and adobe), possibly >20 m long, oriented 8° north of east; skeleton of man 35–40 under retaining wall, crushed by adobes — possible sacrificial incorporation.
- **Monte Albán founding transition:** Listed among Etla communities participating in population loss / reorganization associated with [[monte-alban]]’s rise (*Cloud People* / 1996 narrative).

## Significance

Illustrates multi-polity competition before [[synoikism]]: not a single-center valley. Feeds [[chiefdom]], [[settlement-hierarchy]], [[san-jose-mogote]].

## Key Debates

Degree of autonomy from San José Mogote remains **uncertain** in the source (wholly/partly autonomous — authors’ open wording).
