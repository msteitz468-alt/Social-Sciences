# Calakmul

## Discovery and Excavation History

Campeche mega-site; Mexican National Institute projects including Ramón Carrasco Vargas’s Chiik Nahb work. [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]] emphasize its role as Tikal’s great rival in the Martin–Grube political reconstruction.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]].)*

- Middle Preclassic roots; by Early Classic building an alliance network. Martin & Velásquez: dynasty may derive from southern Quintana Roo. Structure II renovated early 5th century.
- **Golden age:** Yuknoom the Great from AD 636; hegemony over Piedras Negras, Dos Pilas, El Perú and attacks on Tikal.
- **Defeat:** August AD 695 Yich'aak K'ahk' (“Fiery Claw”) defeated by Jasaw Chan K'awiil of [[tikal]]; Calakmul never recovered prior eminence. Fiery Claw’s tomb in Structure II (~AD 700) with rich offerings including Teotihuacan War God headdress.
- **Chiik Nahb murals (2004+):** market scenes — maize, atole, salt, etc. — rare window on non-royal urban economy.
- After 695, orientation toward Río Bec north; Late Classic architectural influence from that zone.

## Significance

Co-protagonist of the Classic lowland “Great Game” with [[tikal]]; Snake-polity hegemony model; market murals diversify the elite-ritual picture of [[classic-maya]] cities.

## Key Debates

- Hierarchical vs peer-polity models of Classic politics.
- [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]] regional trajectories.

## Densification from Sharer & Traxler 2006

*(Attributed — [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]]. Complements Coe & Houston synthesis above.)*

### Scale, location, and Kan origins

Sharer & Traxler place Calakmul ~35 km from the Guatemalan border north of [[el-mirador]]; civic core ~2 km² (~1,000 structures); mapped residential >20 km² (~6,250 structures); estimated population ~50,000 — on a par with [[tikal]] among largest Late Classic capitals. They report **117 stelae** (largest total at any Maya site), many eroded soft limestone. Str. II buries a Late Preclassic quadrangle — Calakmul **survived** the Terminal Preclassic unlike El Mirador. Stela 114 (AD 435, 9.0.0.0.0) is the earliest dated Calakmul monument. Joyce Marcus identified the **Kan** (snake-head) emblem. Authors float that Calakmul may have been a Late Preclassic dependency of El Mirador that became the new seat of the Kan dynasty after the original capital’s downfall. Late Classic codex-style vases listing nineteen Snake kings with Preclassic-scale inauguration dates (from 396 BC) are treated as a possible retrospective of El Mirador Basin rulers — identity remains an “enigma.” Patron *way* associated with a deer-serpent. Causeway-extent estimate for the Calakmul realm ~4,000 km². Marcus & Flannery: secondary centers form a hexagonal lattice ~35 km average radius. High ground at the Caribbean–Gulf drainage divide (with Tikal and El Mirador) is stressed as commanding E–W Peten trade and bajo agriculture; canal noted as landscape investment.

### Early Classic expansion and the 562 victory over Tikal

As of Sharer & Traxler Table 7.3 and narrative: **Tuun Kab Hix** defeated by [[yaxchilan]] 537, oversaw [[naranjo]] accession of Aj Wosal 546; **Sky Witness** oversaw Los Alacranes 561, alliance with [[caracol]](?), defeated Tikal and Wak Chan K’awiil **562** (star war on Caracol Altar 21; Martin’s reading of victor outlines as Calakmul). Early Classic strategy: alliances nearly surrounding Mutul, key flip of Caracol from Tikal ally. Commercial rivalry included Río Hondo–Caribbean routes challenged by Tikal’s Río Azul takeover. Becan Early Classic moat/rampart dated as likely response to Calakmul expansion; population contracted after fortification — authors suggest possible fall to Calakmul before Late Classic Río Bec revitalization.

### Apogee under Yuknoom the Great; failure to forge a single state

After Sky Witness (last known mention 572), next four Kan kings reigned over half a century of peak power. **Scroll Serpent** (from 579) best known for 611 long-distance campaign against [[palenque]] — “axing of Lakamha” — demonstrating Calakmul as paramount after Tikal’s defeat. **Yuknoom Ch’een II** (“Yuknoom the Great,” 636–ca. 686): victories over Tikal 657, 677, 679; extensive overlordship (Dos Pilas, Cancuen, Moral Reforma, etc.). Authors stress that Calakmul did **not** completely subdue Tikal or forge a single unified lowland state — may not have been the objective given territory size and ideology preserving defeated polities’ continuity. Co-optation over annexation: subordinates acknowledged overlords (*yajaw*); e.g. Moral-Reforma Stela 4 (Martin) shows Muwaan Jol taking the headband thrice — independent 661, under Calakmul 662, under Palenque ~30 years later.

### Client wars and the 695 reverse

Calakmul–Caracol alliance strained by Caracol–Naranjo enmity; **Yuknoom Head** conquered Naranjo 631. After Naranjo’s 680 star-war victory over Caracol and subsequent extinguishing of Naranjo’s house, Calakmul sponsored restoration via **Lady Six Sky** of [[dos-pilas]] (682); her son K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Chaak acceded 693 as *yajaw* of **Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’**. In 648 Calakmul defeated Dos Pilas and flipped B’alaj Chan K’awiil into vassalage, splitting Tikal’s royal house. **695:** Jasaw Chan K’awiil I of Tikal “brought down the flint and shield” of Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’; nothing heard of him after 695; Tomb 4 under Str. II (1997) may be his. Prestige plummeted; **Split Earth** named on Tikal tomb bones may be a Tikal-installed vassal; **Yuknoom Took’ K’awiil** (by 702–ca. 736) sponsored ~20 monuments but power was broken — further Tikal victories 736 (and against Waka/Naranjo 743–44). Last kings of the sequence extend to 909 in the synthesis tables. Terminal Classic: drastic population drop at former peak capitals; Río Bec/Chenes zone north of declining Calakmul saw prosperity increase (possible southern refugees); landlocked trade-bypass models treat Calakmul as a classic inland capital losing income as seaborne Yucatan routes rose ([[chichen-itza]]).

## Densification from Martin & Grube 2008

*(Attributed — [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008|Martin & Grube 2008]]; [[martin-simon|Martin]] & [[grube-nikolai|Grube]]. Complements Coe & Houston and Sharer & Traxler densifications above.)*

### Snake kingdom, capital problem, and scale

Martin & Grube present the “Kingdom of the Snake” as one of the most important Classic polities: in the 6th–7th centuries it eclipsed arch-rival [[tikal]] and built a widespread [[overkingship]] felt in the farthest corners of the Maya world. They stress a **capital identification problem**: Calakmul was certainly the Late Classic seat, but earlier power may have resided further north, perhaps at Dzibanche; they currently lack evidence for Snake kings *at Calakmul* before about 630, though Calakmul itself was a Preclassic centre of magnitude (architecture deep in Structure 2). Early Snake references include Yuknoom Ch'een I on Dzibanche’s captive stairway (dates uncertain, some possibly 5th century). Joyce Marcus’s assignment of the snake-head emblem to Calakmul is confirmed, they report, by Late Classic place-names **Uxte'tuun** (“Three Stones”) and **Chik Nahb** and residual *k'uhul kaanul ajaw* readings. Site scale: >11 sq miles (~30 km²), ≥117 monuments — local chalky limestone badly erodes the historical record. A 109-year monumental gap coincides with Snake flowering known from foreign texts — either buried/destroyed stelae or a dynasty not then resident at Calakmul. Early Classic Stela 114 (AD 435) celebrates a king with a **Bat** emblem, not Snake — underscoring non-identity of site and Snake dynasty before the Late Classic reconstitution.

### Sky Witness 562 through Yuknoom Ch'een II’s network

**K'altuun Hix** supervising [[naranjo]] Aj Wosal’s accession (546) is their first concrete southern evidence of Snake patronage. **Sky Witness** (active ≥561–572) installs a ruler at Los Alacranes (561), displaces Tikal’s patronage of [[caracol]], and is the likely victor of the 562 star war on Tikal (damaged Altar 21 outlines fit Sky Witness, not Caracol’s Yajaw Te' K'inich). The ensuing 130-year Tikal Hiatus mirrors Snake peak strength. **Scroll Serpent** (from 579) campaigns west against Palenque (599, 611) — among the furthest known Maya expeditions — while supervising Caracol events; no standing Scroll Serpent monuments survive at Calakmul itself. **Yuknoom Head** reasserts ascendancy with the 631 star war on Naranjo and is first described as Snake king “at Uxte'tuun,” a phrasing Martin & Grube read as possible capital differentiation.

**Yuknoom the Great (Yuknoom Ch'een II, 636–686)** anchors the golden age: up to ~18 personal stelae; palace-complex programmes; struggle with a resurgent Tikal split into dual Mutal factions. Sequence they give: 650 seizure of [[dos-pilas]] / ousting of Bajlaj Chan K'awiil (who becomes key Snake *yajaw* and “anti-Tikal” founder); 657 star war on Tikal subordinating Nuun Ujol Chaak; 672 Tikal reconquest of Dos Pilas; 677 Calakmul reverse; likely combined Calakmul–Dos Pilas victory 679. After Naranjo’s 680 reverse against Caracol and rapid dynastic extinction, they argue Calakmul orchestrated the 682 dispatch of Bajlaj’s daughter Lady Six Sky to Naranjo. Sample of Yuknoom’s [[overkingship]]: El Perú (K'inich Bahlam; Calakmul princess marriage), La Corona as near-fiefdom, Cancuen over three generations (installations 656, 677), Uxul 662, Moral-Reforma child-king 662, Piedras Negras links 662/685. Failure to fully subdue Tikal, they conclude, would prove costly.

### 695 reverse and dismantling of the imperium

**Yich'aak K'ahk'** (“Claw of Fire”; formal enthronement 686) inherits Dos Pilas and El Perú loyalties and receives the five-year-old K'ahk' Tiliw of Naranjo as *yajaw* (693). In August 695 Jasaw Chan K'awiil of Tikal “brings down” his flint and shield and captures a Calakmul patron deity; Martin & Grube treat this as closing Calakmul’s golden age — hegemony relying on military prestige, with explicit overlordship statements nearly vanishing afterward. Yich'aak K'ahk'’s fate is uncertain; Tomb 4 under Structure 2B (owner’s plate names him) is a leading candidate for his burial. Brief “Split Earth” (by November 695) may be legitimate king or Tikal-sponsored pretender. **Yuknoom Took' K'awiil** mounts a monument surge (702, 731) and retains some Dos Pilas / El Perú / Naranjo ties, yet a Tikal altar (c. 733–736) shows a bound Calakmul lord. Tikal’s victories over El Perú (743) and Naranjo (744) constitute, in their words, “the final dismemberment” of Snake [[overkingship]] “imperium.” Late sequence is sketchy (Wamaw K'awiil at Quirigua 736; Bat emblem returns; Bolon K'awiil by 771); no 10.0.0.0.0 (830) at Calakmul; Chan Pet “witnesses” Seibal 849; last rally Stela 61 Aj Took' (preferred 909).

## Densification from Schele & Freidel 1990

*(Attributed — [[schele-freidel-forest-of-kings-1990|Schele & Freidel 1990]], mainly Ch. 5 “Star Wars in the Seventh Century” and endnotes. 1990 nicknames retained; later standard phonetic readings in parentheses where well established, following Martin & Grube and related work. Star-war / Venus-timing claims are **theory/position of Schele & Freidel**, not wiki-voice fact.)*

### Early Classic reach and anti-Tikal envelopment

Schele & Freidel treat Calakmul as still strong by the Late Preclassic and Early Classic, **most probably the inheritor of [[el-mirador]]’s northern power** and a long-term rival of [[tikal]] (C5-013; endnote C10-020: Stuart’s observation that El Mirador pyramids are visible from Calakmul summits, with Calakmul “very probably a significant player in the demise of El Mirador”). On **Yaxchilan Lintel 35** (Structure 12), a vassal of a Calakmul king nicknamed **Cu-Ix** (as of Schele & Freidel 1990; from a Cauac-in-hand-Ix glyph) participates in ritual at Yaxchilan on 9.5.2.10.6 (16 January 537) (C5-014). **Naranjo Stela 25** dates 9.5.12.0.4 (7 May 546)—presumed accession of Naranjo Ruler I (later Aj Wosal in Martin & Grube)—*u cab* “in the territory” of Cu-Ix, Ahau of Calakmul; the authors suggest Calakmul was important if not instrumental in installing that [[kuhul-ajaw]] (C5-015). They propose an **envelopment strategy** against Tikal involving Calakmul north, [[caracol]] south, [[naranjo]] east, and perhaps Yaxchilan west (C5-016; C10-039).

### Kan II alliance, Naranjo subjugation, and Site Q

On 9.9.5.13.8 (9 January 619) **Lord Kan II** of Caracol (as of Schele & Freidel 1990; later K’an II) performed an unidentified action “in the land of” a Calakmul ahau—read by the authors as the start of a fateful bond against Tikal and Naranjo, surrounding intended victims with a ring of enemies (C5-012; [[overkingship]]). After Kan II’s decisive star-war defeat of Naranjo (27 December 631; Venus Eveningstar first appearance as authors’ favored timing—**theory**), the forced Naranjo Hieroglyphic Stairs record a follow-up *k’uxah* (“to torture” or perhaps “to eat”) on a Naranjo notable “in the land of” the Calakmul king—Calakmul short-term beneficiary of the Caracol alliance (C5-021).

**Jaguar-Paw** (as of Schele & Freidel 1990; later Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’ / “Claw of Fire”): birth listed 9 October 649 on looted **Site Q Glyphic Panel 6**; authors label panels “Calakmul?” (C5-032). Around 9.11.10.0.0 young Jaguar-Paw participates with **Flint-Sky-God K** of [[dos-pilas]] (as of 1990; later B’alaj Chan K’awiil) in ceremony at Yaxha; 25 February 683 another ritual on Lake Petexbatún near Dos Pilas—authors infer significant connection/possible alliance (C5-033). Jaguar-Paw accedes as Calakmul king **6 April 686** (9.12.13.17.7); accession “was seen (*yilah*)” by Flint-Sky-God K at **Nab Tunich** (Calakmul toponym), also recorded at El Perú with Mah-Kina-Balam; commemorated on Dos Pilas Stela 13 next to Hieroglyphic Stairs 2 (C5-034). A looted pot shows Jaguar-Paw kneeling in subordination before a Dos Pilas lord—authors offer “godfather,” non-interference, or coercion scenarios without deciding (C5-035).

**Site Q / snake Emblem identification (C10-038):** Marcus/Miller linked the snake Emblem to Calakmul; Mathews coined “Site Q” for the looted corpus; Graham found Cleveland/Fort Worth stelae butts at El Perú (shifting many monuments); Stuart & Houston re-argue snake EG as Calakmul via dual EG at El Perú, Jaguar-Paw birth/accession distribution, and *nab tunich*. Schele & Freidel **follow the Calakmul ID** but explicitly admit evidence “still not indisputable” (Site 2 / unknown city remains possible). Endnote C10-048 flags that some epigraphers still question the snake EG ID.

### 695 reverse and aftermath

Schele & Freidel frame **Ah-Cacaw** of Tikal (as of 1990; later Jasaw Chan K’awiil) capturing **Jaguar-Paw of Calakmul on 8 August 695**—158 days after Temple 33-1st finish (after 9.13.3.0.0 / 3 March 695). They label the campaign **Tlaloc-Venus war** in form (like Caracol vs Naranjo), but note it was timed by the **13-katun anniversary** of Stormy-Sky’s Stela 31 war event rather than “usual” celestial stations—two days after solar zenith passage (C5-055; **historical-cycle timing as authors’ theory**). Thirteen days later Calakmul captives are displayed/humiliated; Structure 5D-57 plaster shows Ah-Bolon-Bakin (ally/vassal of the captured king), not Jaguar-Paw himself (C5-056). Captives sacrificed in Temple 33 dedication; Temple 1 Lintel 3 glosses *hubi* “was brought down” in the capture sequence (C5-057, C5-072). Authors explain the northward target: Calakmul–Caracol alliance in the Naranjo war and earlier encirclement made Jaguar-Paw the object of Tikal wrath while Naranjo descendants struck south at Caracol’s neighbors (C5-059).

Within a year Ah-Cacaw attacks Calakmul again, taking **Ox-Ha-Te Ixil Ahau** in the land of **Split-Earth** (as of 1990)—king who apparently succeeded Jaguar-Paw; captive incised on bones in Ah-Cacaw’s tomb (C5-063). Schele & Freidel argue Calakmul was **so huge and far north** that it survived the king’s defeat “without major effect”; by the next period ending after Jaguar-Paw’s death it was already erecting stelae again (C5-062)—a softer “collapse” reading than later Martin–Grube “final dismemberment” of Snake [[overkingship]], which remains a separate synthesis.
