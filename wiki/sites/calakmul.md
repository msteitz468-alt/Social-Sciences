# Calakmul

## Discovery and Excavation History

Campeche mega-site; Mexican National Institute projects including Ramón Carrasco Vargas’s Chiik Nahb work. [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]] emphasize its role as Tikal’s great rival in the Martin–Grube political reconstruction.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]].)*

- Middle Preclassic roots; by Early Classic building an alliance network. Martin & Velásquez: dynasty may derive from southern Quintana Roo. Structure II renovated early 5th century.
- **Golden age:** Yuknoom the Great from AD 636; hegemony over Piedras Negras, Dos Pilas, El Perú and attacks on Tikal.
- **Defeat:** August AD 695 Yich'aak K'ahk' (“Fiery Claw”) defeated by Jasaw Chan K'awiil of [[tikal]]; Calakmul never recovered prior eminence. Fiery Claw’s tomb in Structure II (~AD 700) with rich offerings including Teotihuacan War God headdress.
- **Chiik Nahb murals (2004+):** market scenes — maize, atole, salt, etc. — rare window on non-royal urban economy.
- After 695, orientation toward Río Bec north; Late Classic architectural influence from that zone.

## Significance

Co-protagonist of the Classic lowland “Great Game” with [[tikal]]; Snake-polity hegemony model; market murals diversify the elite-ritual picture of [[classic-maya]] cities.

## Key Debates

- Hierarchical vs peer-polity models of Classic politics.
- [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]] regional trajectories.

## Densification from Sharer & Traxler 2006

*(Attributed — [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]]. Complements Coe & Houston synthesis above.)*

### Scale, location, and Kan origins

Sharer & Traxler place Calakmul ~35 km from the Guatemalan border north of [[el-mirador]]; civic core ~2 km² (~1,000 structures); mapped residential >20 km² (~6,250 structures); estimated population ~50,000 — on a par with [[tikal]] among largest Late Classic capitals. They report **117 stelae** (largest total at any Maya site), many eroded soft limestone. Str. II buries a Late Preclassic quadrangle — Calakmul **survived** the Terminal Preclassic unlike El Mirador. Stela 114 (AD 435, 9.0.0.0.0) is the earliest dated Calakmul monument. Joyce Marcus identified the **Kan** (snake-head) emblem. Authors float that Calakmul may have been a Late Preclassic dependency of El Mirador that became the new seat of the Kan dynasty after the original capital’s downfall. Late Classic codex-style vases listing nineteen Snake kings with Preclassic-scale inauguration dates (from 396 BC) are treated as a possible retrospective of El Mirador Basin rulers — identity remains an “enigma.” Patron *way* associated with a deer-serpent. Causeway-extent estimate for the Calakmul realm ~4,000 km². Marcus & Flannery: secondary centers form a hexagonal lattice ~35 km average radius. High ground at the Caribbean–Gulf drainage divide (with Tikal and El Mirador) is stressed as commanding E–W Peten trade and bajo agriculture; canal noted as landscape investment.

### Early Classic expansion and the 562 victory over Tikal

As of Sharer & Traxler Table 7.3 and narrative: **Tuun Kab Hix** defeated by [[yaxchilan]] 537, oversaw [[naranjo]] accession of Aj Wosal 546; **Sky Witness** oversaw Los Alacranes 561, alliance with [[caracol]](?), defeated Tikal and Wak Chan K’awiil **562** (star war on Caracol Altar 21; Martin’s reading of victor outlines as Calakmul). Early Classic strategy: alliances nearly surrounding Mutul, key flip of Caracol from Tikal ally. Commercial rivalry included Río Hondo–Caribbean routes challenged by Tikal’s Río Azul takeover. Becan Early Classic moat/rampart dated as likely response to Calakmul expansion; population contracted after fortification — authors suggest possible fall to Calakmul before Late Classic Río Bec revitalization.

### Apogee under Yuknoom the Great; failure to forge a single state

After Sky Witness (last known mention 572), next four Kan kings reigned over half a century of peak power. **Scroll Serpent** (from 579) best known for 611 long-distance campaign against [[palenque]] — “axing of Lakamha” — demonstrating Calakmul as paramount after Tikal’s defeat. **Yuknoom Ch’een II** (“Yuknoom the Great,” 636–ca. 686): victories over Tikal 657, 677, 679; extensive overlordship (Dos Pilas, Cancuen, Moral Reforma, etc.). Authors stress that Calakmul did **not** completely subdue Tikal or forge a single unified lowland state — may not have been the objective given territory size and ideology preserving defeated polities’ continuity. Co-optation over annexation: subordinates acknowledged overlords (*yajaw*); e.g. Moral-Reforma Stela 4 (Martin) shows Muwaan Jol taking the headband thrice — independent 661, under Calakmul 662, under Palenque ~30 years later.

### Client wars and the 695 reverse

Calakmul–Caracol alliance strained by Caracol–Naranjo enmity; **Yuknoom Head** conquered Naranjo 631. After Naranjo’s 680 star-war victory over Caracol and subsequent extinguishing of Naranjo’s house, Calakmul sponsored restoration via **Lady Six Sky** of [[dos-pilas]] (682); her son K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Chaak acceded 693 as *yajaw* of **Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’**. In 648 Calakmul defeated Dos Pilas and flipped B’alaj Chan K’awiil into vassalage, splitting Tikal’s royal house. **695:** Jasaw Chan K’awiil I of Tikal “brought down the flint and shield” of Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’; nothing heard of him after 695; Tomb 4 under Str. II (1997) may be his. Prestige plummeted; **Split Earth** named on Tikal tomb bones may be a Tikal-installed vassal; **Yuknoom Took’ K’awiil** (by 702–ca. 736) sponsored ~20 monuments but power was broken — further Tikal victories 736 (and against Waka/Naranjo 743–44). Last kings of the sequence extend to 909 in the synthesis tables. Terminal Classic: drastic population drop at former peak capitals; Río Bec/Chenes zone north of declining Calakmul saw prosperity increase (possible southern refugees); landlocked trade-bypass models treat Calakmul as a classic inland capital losing income as seaborne Yucatan routes rose ([[chichen-itza]]).
