# Scorpion Point Village

## Discovery and Excavation History

Major **Pre-Classic** habitation excavated by the [[lower-verde-archaeological-project-study|Lower Verde Archaeological Project]] (SRI) in Horseshoe Basin. Survey under-estimated size; Phase 1 work showed a continuous village landscape rather than three small sites. The historical **Horseshoe Dam construction camp** was built directly on the site, extensively modifying the surface (*[[whittlesey-vanishing-river-1997|Vanishing River]]* Ch. 1, 13, 17). Full feature catalogues are on LVAP CD-ROM Volume 1; the 1997 overview synthesizes household, ceramic, and chronological results.

## Occupation Sequence

As phased by Deaver and the settlement chapters (attributed, as of 1997):

1. **Late Pioneer / Colonial through Santa Cruz** — bulk of occupation; longest continuous stretch among LVAP pre-Classic sites; up to ~**300** pit houses estimated site-wide (58 excavated in reported sample); site area >**11 ha**.
2. **Hiatus ~AD 950–1000** — roughly fifty years at end Santa Cruz / start Sacaton; Cow Wallow and Middendrum permanently abandoned in the wider sample while Scorpion Point later reoccupied.
3. **Sacaton reoccupation** — smaller aggregated area; ballcourts reused (cemetery/cooking contexts reported); more intermittent occupation; abandonment of project-area pre-Classic sequence by ~AD 1125 frame.

Mean house floor area: Colonial **16.62 m²** vs Sedentary **21.19 m²** (site subsample slightly lower). Ten pre-Classic courtyard groups identified across Scorpion Point and CTC (six Colonial at Scorpion Point, spatially more isolated; Sedentary courtyards more tightly clustered in Locus A).

## Significance

- Keystone LVAP site for Pre-Classic domestic organization, chronology, and [[hohokam-core-periphery-model|core–periphery]] critique.
- **Ballcourts:** Court 1 ~20×9 m; Court 2 only ~2×4.6 m — Whittlesey’s “haphazard and least-cost” participation contrast with larger courts elsewhere in the same “periphery.”
- **Ceramics:** buff ware only **4.2%** of assemblage (vs CTC 14.3%; lower Verde pre-Classic total ~7.7%) — weak monothetic “Hohokam” signature by decorated-ware criterion.
- **Cotton:** **18%** ubiquity in flotation — among the higher LVAP signals; read as possible production above local need (Teague threshold cited by Van West & Altschul).
- Mortuary: secondary pit/urn cremations with broken vessels/palette *and* Sacaton-age extended inhumations in midden — cremation complex present but not exclusive.

## Key Debates

How “Hohokam” the site is under trait-list definitions; mobility vs residential stability across the hiatus; cotton as exchange crop vs local use. All positions attributed to LVAP authors/cited specialists.

## Historiography

Known primarily through LVAP 1997 and companion volumes; earlier CAP/CAWCS survey context in Ch. 4.
