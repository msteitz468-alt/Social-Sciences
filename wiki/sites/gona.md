# Gona

Locality in the Afar region of Ethiopia anchoring early **[[oldowan-industry|Oldowan]]** stone-tool evidence at about **2.6 million years ago**, as synthesized by Manuel Domínguez-Rodrigo (Ch. 21) in *[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013|The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology]]* (2013).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Handbook treatment is secondary (as of ~2012 literature). Gona is cited as the type of early Mode-1 assemblage used to define the beginning of the archaeological record of hominin culture, distinct from chimpanzee tool use in form and archaeological visibility (Domínguez-Rodrigo, attributed).

## Occupation Sequence

Plio-Pleistocene Oldowan contexts; detailed stratigraphy belongs in specialist reports beyond this handbook page.

## Significance

- Anchors the **start of the archaeological record** of intentional flaked-stone technology in Africa (~2.6 mya as of handbook synthesis).
- Frames debates on early hominin carcass access (hunting vs scavenging) discussed via later Olduvai sites such as FLK Zinj — **positions**, not settled wiki-voice facts (Domínguez-Rodrigo Ch. 21).
- Complements [[olduvai-gorge]], [[omo-river]], and South African cave sequences ([[sterkfontein]], [[taung]]) in continental deep-time coverage.

## Key Debates

Makers of the earliest Oldowan (which hominin taxa) remain **open**; technology ≠ species identity (Sahnouni et al. Ch. 22 on Mode 2 likewise).

## Historiography

Known through late-20th/early-21st-century Afar research; vault densification is handbook-level until primary monographs are ingested.

## Oxford Handbook densification (Mitchell & Lane 2013)

*Attributed — Domínguez-Rodrigo (Ch. 21), Sahnouni et al. (Ch. 22), Christian Tryon (Ch. 8) in [[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]; **as of handbook ~2012**. Page already seeded from this handbook; densifies without re-counting the ingest.*

Domínguez-Rodrigo: oldest known Oldowan stone tools come from Gona in the Afar and date to **2.6 mya** (Semaw et al. 1997, 2003). Early Oldowan knapping at Gona shows fine-grained raw-material selection and cut-marked bones by 2.6 mya; intentional reoccupation is argued at EG 10 Gona. Tryon independently reports preferential selection of fine-grained lavas at Gona and Lokalalei despite rarity in local cobble bars. Sahnouni et al.: Gona also yields large handaxes/picks ~**1.7 mya** in the Early Acheulean package (with Kokiselei 1.76 mya and Konso ~1.7 mya). Thus Gona anchors both Mode 1 start and Early Mode 2 presence — not a single static industry. Makers of the earliest Oldowan remain debated (*A. garhi* at Bouri vs early *Homo*); industry ≠ species.
