# Hattusa (Boğazköy)

## Discovery and Excavation History

Capital of the [[hittite-empire]], excavated primarily as Boğazköy/Hattusa by German-led projects since the early twentieth century. Archives recovered here underwrite Hittite political history and LBA international relations (including Egyptian correspondence and treaties).

## Occupation Sequence

Hittite capital through Old, Middle, and New Kingdom phases; imperial peak 14th–13th centuries BCE under kings such as Suppiluliuma I and later Tudhaliya IV. Capital abandoned in the early twelfth century BCE as the central empire dissolved; successor polities persisted elsewhere (e.g., Carchemish).

## Significance

Anchors Anatolian side of the LBA great-power system reconstructed by [[cline-eric-h|Cline]] (2014); its end is a central data point for [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].
