# La Cotte de St Brelade

Cliff-headland cave complex on Jersey (Channel Islands); Middle Palaeolithic case for landscape use, raw-material logistics, and possible drive-hunting bone piles in [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999|Gamble 1999]], Ch.5.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Gamble’s 1999 synthesis deploys La Cotte primarily via Callow (raw materials) and Scott (1986a) on bone piles rather than a full excavation narrative. Excavator names and campaign years are not densified in the claims extracted for this page.

## Occupation Sequence

Middle Palaeolithic occupations spanning cold-stage landscapes when sea levels and headland topography differed from the modern island setting (as of Gamble 1999). Layers 3 and 6 carry the bone piles discussed below. Burning is reported without formal hearths—placed with other open/cave locales that lack convincing fireplace structure (contrast Grotte Lazaret as Gamble’s near-exception).

## Significance

### Raw materials (Callow via Gamble)

No flint on Jersey; nearest outcrop now submerged ~20 km north (requires ~25 m sea-level fall). Callow argues sources used were always close, probably **within ~3 km** (beach pebbles), with knapping flake:retouched ratios matching Geneste’s local-use expectations (<10 km). As sea levels changed, different flint sources seem to have been used but remained near the cave (Gamble 1999, attributed).

### Refitting

Among Cziesla’s three refit types, La Cotte is cited for **modification** refits (*Anpassungen*): specialized long sharpening flakes n=2,195, of which **only one** could be refitted—evidence of intensive maintenance activity without easy conjoin of blanks to tools (Gamble 1999, attributed). Production-sequence refits are more characteristic of [[maastricht-belvedere|Maastricht-Belvédère]], Vaufrey, and Biache.

### Bone piles and drives (Scott via Gamble)

In layers 3 and 6, mammoth and woolly rhino remains form bone piles with few stones among them. Scott (1986a:183 via Gamble) argues the headland was used for **two separate gatherings** when a small herd of prime mammoths with a few rhinos was driven over the cliff; selected bones were then dragged up beneath the overhang. Gamble files this as organized game procurement / performance of acquiring at a landscape locale—still within Neanderthal effective-network negotiation rather than symbolic “place” architecture (attributed position).

Source: [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999]].

## Key Debates

- **Intentional drive vs natural accumulation** — Scott’s stampede/drive model is the load-bearing attributed interpretation; alternatives (natural falls, mixed taphonomy) remain live in the wider literature and are not adjudicated here.
- **Dating of levels** — not densified from Gamble 1999 claims for this page.
- **Local vs distant stone** — Callow’s near-source model vs submerged-outcrop distances under different sea levels.
- **Fire without hearths** — burning present; structured fireplaces absent in Gamble’s account.

## Historiography

La Cotte is a classic Channel Islands Middle Palaeolithic sequence; Gamble 1999 uses it as a **landscape-of-habit** vignette (local stone, headland topography, episodic mass-kill inference) rather than as a culture-historical type site. All claims **(as of Gamble 1999)**.
