# Tomaltepec

## Discovery and Excavation History

Formative-to-Monte Albán I community east of [[monte-alban|Monte Albán]], excavated by **Michael Whalen** and synthesized in [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996|Marcus & Flannery 1996]] as evidence for the **piedmont strategy** — top-down canal-era administration with elite families posted outside the capital.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Marcus & Flannery 1996 / Whalen.)*

- **Rosario:** Only ~50–75 persons yet an adobe public structure >3 m high (probable modest temple) with four dedicatory burials; some L-shaped adobe houses while neighbors remained wattle-and-daub.
- **Late Monte Albán I (Ic):** Transformed into a **5–8 ha** community with public buildings, plastered courtyards, elite households; may have administered 7–8 villages along nearby piedmont streams. Hierarchy: Tier 3 under Monte Albán (~400 ha) and Tier 2 towns. Elite tomb under floor: woman <40, adult, child ~12; **37 pottery vessels** including bridgespout jars and human effigies — hypogamy signal paralleling [[fabrica-san-jose|Fábrica San José]]. Courtyard **two-chambered kilns** (60–80 cm pits) for reducing-atmosphere gray ware — hinterland craft specialization under high-status direction.

## Significance

Shows how Monte Albán’s urban provisioning problem (after [[synoikism]]) was solved by **elite-directed piedmont intensification**, not only capital-centric production. Links [[settlement-hierarchy]], [[state-formation]], [[zapotec-civilization]].

## Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas (2022) — a possible San José-phase house-on-platform

*(Attributed — [[blanton-ancient-oaxaca-2022|Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas]], *Ancient Oaxaca: The Monte Albán State*, 2nd ed. 2022, citing Whalen 1981.)* The authors note Whalen's (1981) excavation at Tomaltepec of a possible house-on-platform dating to the **San José phase** (~1000 BC) — earlier than the Rosario-phase public building documented above — adding a further, still-tentative data point to the site's occupation sequence ahead of its later Monte Albán I administrative role.
