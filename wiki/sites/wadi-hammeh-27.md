# Wadi Hammeh 27

Open-air Early [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] base-camp in the foothills overlooking the Jordan Valley (Jordan). Edwards et al. (in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]) present it as only the third such Mediterranean-zone open-air base-camp after Jericho and Eynan, and as the eastern margin of the traditional Natufian “core area” / “homeland.”

## Discovery and Excavation History

Presented while still under excavation at the 1989 Natufian conference (Edwards 1991); final report completed by 2013 (Edwards 2013). Multi-specialist chapters cover fauna, plants, bone, art (Major), and architecture.

## Occupation Sequence

Relatively short Early Natufian time-slice (~500 years per Edwards et al. 2013) compared with multi-millennial core-area sequences — still long for a hunter-gatherer settlement. Rebuilds of modest stone-and-brush huts with coherent material culture.

## Significance

Tests the geographic extent of the “homeland” package (architecture + burials + art + groundstone + bone industries). Without Wadi Hammeh 27, Edwards argues, there is little reason to extend the core concept east of the Jordan.

## Key Debates

- Core-area boundaries and whether “homeland” remains a useful construct (challenged at the 2009 conference; Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris) — [[natufian-origins-core-periphery-debate]].

## Densified findings from Edwards et al. / Major 2013

*(Attributed — [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]].)*

- Early Natufian open-air base-camp on eastern core margin; occupation estimate ~12–12.5k cal BC / ~500-year time-slice (Edwards et al.).
- Distant basalt sourcing; large bone sickle set; Helwan-rich lithics with negligible microburin use; wild barley flotation; gazelle-dominated fauna with high juveniles.
- Art (Major): 66 portable + monumental items; ~¼ of published Natufian art corpus; structure-interior clustering; possible narrative imagery.
- Positioned by Henry (2013) within Stage 2 northern-core expansion under late Bølling–Allerød.
