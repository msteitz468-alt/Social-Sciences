# Troy (Hisarlık)

## Discovery and Excavation History

Hisarlık excavations from Schliemann through Korfmann transformed a Homeric toponym into a stratified archaeological sequence. Early excavation quality is uneven; later work recovered lower-town extent and refined LBA phases.

## Occupation Sequence

LBA phases Troy VI–VII are those linked in popular and some scholarly literature to Homeric memory and to end-of-Bronze-Age turmoil. Cline discusses destruction evidence including weapon finds in some levels (warfare signatures) versus earthquake hypotheses at related sites.

## Significance

Western Anatolian node between Aegean and Anatolian worlds; symbolic and archaeological case in collapse and "Trojan War" historicity debates — keep literary tradition separate from archaeological levels unless argued.
