# Toltec Mounds

Late Woodland mound center in central Arkansas — for Milner (2021) the **most impressive mound center of its time** in the Eastern Woodlands sequence before classic Mississippian florescence. Box/main treatment in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] Ch. 5.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Named “Toltec” historically (misattribution, not Mexican Toltec identity — etic place name). Modern archaeology treats it as Late Woodland Arkansas River ceremonial center.

## Occupation Sequence

Eighteen mounds around two plazas, habitation areas, defensive embankment and ditch; positioned on abandoned Arkansas River channel (Mound Pond) — wetland focus shared with later Mississippian centers. Rectangular platforms sometimes with feast refuse (white-tailed deer).

## Significance

Bridge from Late Woodland hierarchical centers to Mississippian plaza-and-mound plans; wetland siting pattern; feast archaeology of emerging rank.

## Key Debates

Degree of ranking; relation to [[coles-creek-culture]]; continuity into Mississippian.
