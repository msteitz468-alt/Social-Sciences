# South Dorset Ridgeway

Chalk escarpment ridge inland of the Dorset coast, treated by [[tilley-christopher]] as "The Beach in the Sky" in *[[tilley-interpreting-landscapes-2010|Interpreting Landscapes]]* (2010, Ch. 5; earlier in *Metaphor and Material Culture* 1999).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Long history of Wessex monument archaeology (Maiden Castle etc.). Tilley's contribution is walking phenomenology relating ridge monuments to Chesil Beach and the Isle of Portland.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed, Tilley 2010.)* Mesolithic settlement confined to Portland and Fleet shores; Chesil pebbles as ritual/exchange materials. Neolithic: Maiden Castle enclosure designed for views from the Ridgeway; bank barrows as cultural doubles of the beach. Bronze Age: densest linear barrow cemetery in Britain along the ridge; east-to-west processional path; sea-visibility thresholds structure barrow groups.

## Significance

Load-bearing metaphor (attributed): three bank barrows as **beaches in the sky** — *imagenes mundi* objectifying coastal form inland. Anchors chalk strand of [[geological-identity]] and [[ritual-landscape]].

## Key Debates

Tilley subordinates elite "Wessex culture" territorial readings to topographic identity claims (attributed position). Cross-links: [[stonehenge]] (comparative chalk), [[monumentality]], [[long-mound]].
