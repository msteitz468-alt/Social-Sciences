## Discovery and Excavation History

Aspero lies at the mouth of the Río Supe — the coastal counterpart to interior [[caral|Caral]] in the same drainage. Early digging disturbed deep midden; later work situates it in the Supe Preceramic monumental cluster ([[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001]]; [[scarre-human-past-2013]]).

## Occupation Sequence

Moseley describes ~half-a-dozen sizeable platforms within ~15 ha of dark midden — a maritime settlement signature distinct from Caral's interior profile. Small maize cache and isolated cobs contrast with Caral's reported scarcity of maize; marine protein dominates coastal middens. Platform chronology: some huacas likely began and ended earlier than others (accretional, not single-pulse). Tropical *Spondylus* beads indicate long-distance coastal exchange.

## Significance

Type case for the **[[maritime-foundations-hypothesis]]**: sedentary maritime communities erecting public platforms before ceramic-using intensive irrigation farming. Paired with Caral, it anchors debates on fishing–farming symbiosis in the Supe drainage (Shady's exchange model vs Moseley's maritime calorie base — both attributed).
