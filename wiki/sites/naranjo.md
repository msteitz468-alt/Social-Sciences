# Naranjo

[[classic-maya]] capital in eastern Petén, Guatemala, just west of the Belize border and about 50 km east of [[tikal]]. As of [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]], Naranjo controlled a critical segment of Tikal’s access toward the Belize River, swung between [[calakmul]] clientage and local wars with [[caracol]], and is a premier case of dynastic extinction and restoration through a foreign royal woman (Lady Six Sky of [[dos-pilas]]).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Sharer & Traxler (2006) report discovery by Teobert Maler in 1905. As of their synthesis the site had never been subjected to systematic archaeological investigation, though Belize River valley dependencies had been excavated and Vilma Fialko’s project was documenting remains. The civic-ceremonial core is about 1 km²; the Main Group sits on the eastern edge of a *bajo*; a Hieroglyphic Stairway stands near the center. Looting has devastated the site. Political history is therefore disproportionately epigraphic. Location between the Holmul and Belize/Mopan tributaries indicates control of a critical Tikal-to-Caribbean corridor—one reason both Tikal and Calakmul cared about Naranjo’s allegiance.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]] unless noted.)*

- **Early Classic Calakmul clientage:** Stela 25 notes that Aj Wosal’s accession (546) was under the auspices of Tuun K’ab Hix, king of Calakmul—implying Naranjo was already a client of the emerging dominant Kan power. Naranjo and Caracol were longtime enemies. Sharer & Traxler place Naranjo among sites Tikal sought for Belize River access in the strategic geography of the Tikal–Calakmul rivalry; Yaxha’s Late Classic decline is judged most likely the work of Naranjo among competing candidates (Calakmul, Caracol, Naranjo).
- **Caracol attacks and Calakmul conquest 631:** In 626 Caracol’s Kan II launched two attacks into Naranjo territory with no immediate Calakmul response—a strain on the Calakmul–Caracol alliance. New Calakmul king Yuknoom Head conquered Naranjo in 631; the defeated and captured Naranjo ruler was tortured (and the event recorded) as Calakmul settled the threat to its Caracol alliance.
- **680 star war vs Caracol; dynastic extinction:** In 680 Naranjo exacted revenge, defeating Caracol in a star war and forcing K’ak’ Ujol K’inich II (658–ca. 680) to flee—opening Caracol’s century-long hiatus. Soon after, Naranjo’s own ruling house disappeared; Sharer & Traxler judge it highly likely Calakmul retaliated by crushing Naranjo.
- **Lady Six Sky restoration 682 and K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Chaak:** Calakmul apparently sponsored restoration: in 682 Lady Six Sky, daughter of Dos Pilas vassal B’alaj Chan K’awiil, was chosen to revive the fallen polity. Her arrival is repeatedly recorded on later monuments; she ruled ~a decade as regent (never formally inaugurated) but performed calendrical rituals of a king and is portrayed trampling captives (Stela 24 etc.). K’ak’ Tiliw Chan Chaak was born 688 and acceded 693 at age 5 as *yajaw* of Calakmul’s Yuknoom Yich’aak K’ak’. The episode is a standing example that Classic polities could survive king-loss if the [[kuhul-ajaw]] institution and legitimacy claims persisted, including female-line transmission.
- **Defeat by Tikal 744:** Yik’in Chan K’awiil of Tikal, after defeating Calakmul (736) and Waka/El Perú (743), defeated Naranjo in 744. The defeat was more complete than Waka’s: Tikal captured Naranjo’s king and, per wooden lintels, recorded processions of Naranjo (and Waka) patron deities after victory—capture of local gods along with rulers.
- **Terminal Classic periphery:** Xunantunich (Belize, Mopan Valley) was secondary within Naranjo’s realm; research by Leventhal & Ashmore (as cited) proposed late independence from Naranjo; Awe 2002 Panel 1 likely carries a Xunantunich emblem glyph. Xunantunich stelae date AD 820–849; by AD 900 royal authority failed and the site was abandoned. Naranjo’s own Terminal Classic fate is less fully detailed in Sharer & Traxler than these dependency trajectories.

## Significance

Naranjo sits at the intersection of geography (Belize River access), hierarchical politics (Calakmul *yajaw* status shared with Caracol and Dos Pilas), and dynastic ideology (restoration via Lady Six Sky rather than annexation). It supports Sharer & Traxler’s argument that Maya states often left defeated polities with identity and kings—or, when a house was extinguished, restored a legitimate line—because extinguishing kingdom identity threatened a world order of continuous [[kuhul-ajaw]] seats. Capture of patron deities by Tikal after 744 is key evidence that local gods were war prizes. Epigraphically it is also a node in the Caracol–Calakmul alliance story (626–631, 680).

## Key Debates

- **Calakmul as restorer vs punisher:** 631 conquest and post-680 crushing vs 682 sponsored restoration—how Kan strategy balanced alliance management and dynastic ideology.
- **Lady Six Sky’s status:** Regent performing kingship without formal inauguration; implications for gender and the [[kuhul-ajaw]] office.
- **Authorship of Yaxha’s decline:** Naranjo vs Calakmul vs Caracol as primary agent.
- **Looting and incomplete archaeology:** How far political history can be trusted when the urban core is devastated and never systematically excavated (as of 2006).
- **Xunantunich independence:** Secondary center breakaway at the end of the Classic vs continued Naranjo sphere.

## Historiography

As of Sharer & Traxler 2006, Naranjo is a classic “text-rich, dirt-poor” (relatively) capital: Maler’s early recording, heavy looting, Fialko’s documentation, and dependency archaeology in Belize substitute for a full site monograph. Lady Six Sky’s monuments made the site central to discussions of female political power and dynastic restoration. Tikal’s 744 victory narrative (king + patron gods) is known largely from the victor’s side. Claims about 631 torture of a captive king, Calakmul retaliation after 680, and Xunantunich’s late independence are attributed findings/interpretations in the 2006 synthesis. Primary synthesis source for this page: [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006]].

## Densification from Martin & Grube 2008

*(Attributed — [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008|Martin & Grube 2008]]; [[martin-simon|Martin]] & [[grube-nikolai|Grube]]. Complements Sharer & Traxler densification above.)*

### Aj Wosal under Snake patronage; First Hiatus and 631

Martin & Grube locate Naranjo midway between Holmul and Mopan drainages, with access toward Belize and the Caribbean; fortunes always shaped by war among [[tikal]], [[caracol]], and [[calakmul]]. Stelae cover ≥345 years in three periods interrupted by lacunae after major defeats. Early historical rulers include Tzik'in Bahlam (maternal grandfather of Tikal’s Chak Tok Ich'aak on two Tikal stelae) — Early Classic genealogical ties to Mutal.

**Aj Wosal Chan K'inich** (35th successor; ~546–615+): Stela 25 records his 546 inauguration under the “supervision” of **K'altuun Hix** of the Snake polity — earliest surviving example of one king sanctioning another’s rule, and first sign of northern Snake power. Martin & Grube assume lifelong client loyalty; his death (after Stela 25, 615) sets a disastrous course. Between Aj Wosal and K'ahk' Tiliw (38th) two obscure kings hold power. **K'uxaj** suffers *jubuy* defeats by Caracol’s K'an II (May and August 626); coup de grâce is the **December 631 star war** delivered not by Caracol but by Calakmul’s **Yuknoom Head** — proof, they argue, that Aj Wosal’s affiliations had been repudiated. The hieroglyphic stairway (K'an II, for 642) long read as Caracol occupation of Naranjo may instead have been carved at Caracol and later moved; a matching fragment was found at Caracol itself.

**K'ahk' Skull Chan Chaak** (by ≥644) attacks Caracol’s Uxwitza' in **680** with a star-war defeat (Caracol text); if Naranjo then suffered reverse revenge, that would explain why the entire ruling line promptly disappears.

### Lady Six Sky, K'ahk' Tiliw, and the 744 Tikal star war

On **27 August 682** Lady Six Sky, daughter of [[dos-pilas]]’s Bajlaj Chan K'awiil, “arrives here” — arrival as dynastic foundation/refoundation — and three days later rededicates a dynastic shrine. Never invested as Naranjo ruler (she always carries Mutal/Dos Pilas emblem), she assumes kingship prerogatives including captive-trampling stelae (type otherwise nearly limited to Calakmul). Son **K'ahk' Tiliw Chan Chaak** born 688; no father named — deliberate break with old patriline; rhetorical alignment with Aj Wosal and his mother. Accession **May 693** as 38th ruler at age five: long a figurehead for Lady Six Sky. Scarred Stela 1 substitutes “the lord of Yich'aak K'ahk', Holy Lord of Calakmul” where the king’s name would stand — explicit *yajaw* of Calakmul. Martin & Grube treat the whole renewal as Snake-sponsored: for the rest of the Classic, Naranjo’s line traces kingship from Mutal’s chief opponent via Dos Pilas.

Campaigns in the boy-king’s name begin the day after accession; 695 Yellow Rabbit raid near Tikal (captive Siyaj K'awiil) is read as possible diversion in the wider Tikal–Calakmul war of that year. Mature K'ahk' Tiliw wars on Yaxha (706, 710); supervised accessions at Ucanal and probably Yootz (712); lives to ≥731; Lady Six Sky dies ~741 (Dos Pilas memorial bench). **Second Hiatus:** heir **Yax Mayuy Chan Chaak** incumbent in **744** when Tikal’s Yik'in Chan K'awiil launches a star war on **Wak Kabnal** (“Six Earth Place”), city of the Square-nosed Beastie — presumably the capital. Temple IV lintel: capture of personal god and giant palanquin/throne; Stela 5 (128 days later) shows Yax Mayuy trussed at Yik'in’s feet, “probably about to meet his death.”

### Late Classic recovery and Terminal end

K'ahk' Yipiiy Chan Chaak (~746) briefly interrupts silence; further 34-year gap may reflect continued Tikal pressure (748 capture of a Naranjo–Holmul–Yaxha-axis lord). **K'ahk' Ukalaw Chan Chaak** (son of K'ahk' Tiliw and Lady Unen Bahlam; accession 755) sets all five known stelae together for 780 — possible lost monuments or circumscription by resurgent Tikal. Itzamnaaj K'awiil (790–810) returns to regular monuments and renews war on Yaxha despite blood kinship through his Yaxha mother. Last known king **Waxaklajuun Ubaah K'awiil** (Stela 32, 814; Ucanal ceremony 820; quantified tribute 815). Cycle 10 Stela 9 after 830; Xunantunich stelae to ~830 may be twin capital or provincial claim as central authority falters.

*associated_with:* Snake [[overkingship]] (Aj Wosal; K'ahk' Tiliw *yajaw*); Dos Pilas female-line implant; wars with [[caracol]] and 744 reverse by [[tikal]].

## Densification from Schele & Freidel 1990

*(Attributed — [[schele-freidel-forest-of-kings-1990|Schele & Freidel 1990]], Ch. 5 and endnotes. 1990 nicknames retained; later standard forms in parentheses—e.g. **Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau** → Lady Six Sky; **Smoking-Squirrel** → K’ahk’ Tiliw Chan Chaak. **Venus/star-war timing is theory/position of Schele & Freidel**, not wiki-voice fact.)*

### Early Classic Calakmul patronage and Caracol subjugation

Naranjo Stela 25 (9.5.12.0.4 / 7 May 546)—presumed accession of **Ruler I**—is *u cab* “in the territory” of **Cu-Ix**, Ahau of [[calakmul]]; Schele & Freidel suggest Calakmul was important if not instrumental in installing him (C5-015; later Martin & Grube: Aj Wosal under K’altuun Hix). Authors float that after Tikal’s 562 defeat Ruler I may have reached out to [[tikal]]; a high-status Tikal woman buried under Structure 5G-8 with a polychrome bowl rim-text naming Ruler I of Naranjo as original owner—marriage or gift exchange hypothesized; the occasion may have angered [[caracol]] (C5-020—**hypothesis**).

**Caracol under Lord Kan II** (as of 1990) then breaks Naranjo:
- 28 May 626 first full-scale campaign (Venus Morningstar stationary—**authors’ favorable-timing claim**);
- 4 May 627 second event / “ballgame” captive disposal; death of a Naranjo figure 4 October 630 (possibly aged Ruler I);
- **27 December 631** decisive star war (Venus Eveningstar first appearance over Naranjo—**theory**);
- 4 March 636 further attack and capture of lord **18-Rabbit** (1990 nickname);
- 24 November 637 Kan II’s first-katun rites recorded **on Naranjo’s Hieroglyphic Stairs**;
- **6 December 642** stairs dedication forced as subjugation monument—last public written record for ~40 years (C5-017–C5-019, C5-022–C5-023, C5-027).

Follow-up *k’uxah* on a Naranjo notable “in the land of” the Calakmul king places Calakmul as short-term beneficiary of the Caracol alliance (C5-021). Endnote C10-013 notes Naranjo Altar 1 among cases where numbered-successor counts can start from pre-creation mythological ancestors.

### Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau and Smoking-Squirrel restoration

**Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau** (“Six Celestial Lord,” as of Schele & Freidel 1990; later literature **Lady Six Sky**) of [[dos-pilas]] arrives at Naranjo; first sacred rituals begin **30 August 682** (9.12.10.5.12), three-day rites culminating in pyramid dedication—authors read as marriage founding a new royal house after Caracol destruction. They note **116 days earlier** Ah-Cacaw (Jasaw Chan K’awiil) had resurrected kingship at Tikal, treating both events as results of the Dos Pilas–Naranjo marriage politics (C5-037). The arrival-action *hom* glyph resembles Stormy-Sky’s Uaxactún-conquest hand on Tikal Stela 31, but authors **reject a “destruction of buildings” reading** here—action leads to pyramid dedication and reestablishment of royal house; the pyramid uses the Naranjo [[emblem-glyph]] as part of its proper name (C5-038). Endnote **C10-041:** Proskouriakoff (1961b) identified the Naranjo woman with a Tikal Emblem as mother of the next king; Berlin, Molloy & Rathje, Marcus followed a Tikal-marriage model; **Mathews (1979)** matched her father’s name to **Flint-Sky-God K of Dos Pilas**—Houston & Mathews: Dos Pilas king sent daughter to Naranjo; next king carried the **Naranjo** Emblem, not Dos Pilas. Schele & Freidel accept Mathews.

**Smoking-Squirrel** (as of 1990; later K’ahk’ Tiliw Chan Chaak): born **6 January 688** (9.12.15.13.7); accedes **31 May 693** at age five (9.13.1.3.19); scribes never acknowledge his father; Proskouriakoff realized he was most likely the child of Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau (C5-039). He pairs accession-anniversary monuments with his mother’s; father probably local modest rank; capitalizes on maternal grandfather Flint-Sky-God K—pedigree treated as more historically important even than sonship of Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau (C5-040; [[kuhul-ajaw]] legitimacy via female line and Dos Pilas celebrity).

### Ucanal–Yaxha wars, Venus theory, and revenge on Caracol stairs

First victim **Ucanal** (hilltop capital south of Lake Yaxha, west bank Mopan)—authors judge probably a Caracol ally on the route into Petén. Campaign begins **20 June 693** (eve of summer solstice; Eveningstar last gleam—**authors’ timing**); captive Kinichil-Cab; Lady Wac-Chanil-Ahau stands on his body on **Stela 24**. Further attacks 14 Sept and 12 Dec 693; **1 February 695** main battle takes Shield-Jaguar of Ucanal, with a Dos Pilas lord attending (C5-041). Authors argue Smoking-Squirrel timed kingship declaration, first war, and second Ucanal victory by Venus stations—“star of war glinted brightly for Naranjo” (C5-042—**theory**). Prestigious captives kept alive years for ritual humiliation; Kinichil-Cab and Shield-Jaguar reappear 698–702; Shield-Jaguar last appears **22 June 712** after ~18 years—authors argue long captivity disrupted succession (C5-043).

**23 March 710:** war on Yaxha (Venus last as Morningstar; Jupiter/Saturn stationary—**theory**); 8 June multi-planet alignment ritual with Yaxha prisoner; **12 April 711** war at Sacnab (“Clear Lake”) when Venus again Morningstar (Stela 23) (C5-044). Period ending 9.14.0.0.0 celebrated first appearance of Venus as Eveningstar at Naranjo, Copan, and Tikal—authors see widespread Venus rituals (C5-045). Campaign ends **16 February 713** with first-katun accession anniversary; Stela 2 (war) adjacent to mother’s Stela 3; links own anniversary to the same anniversary of Ruler I (victim of Caracol 81 years earlier)—completing a “circle of defeat and triumph” (C5-046). Fought with support of grandfather Flint-Sky-God K and probably **Shield-God K** (mother’s half-brother), who became Dos Pilas ruler 27 March 698—securing the Lake Yaxha corridor between Naranjo and Petexbatún (C5-047; [[overkingship]]). Costume on Stela 2 read as Late Classic version of Tikal Venus-war garb; timing framed as neutralizing ancestor’s defeat (C5-048—**spiritual-charter theory**).

Once Caracol’s Petén power ended, Smoking-Squirrel **dismantled the hated Caracol stairs**, reset them in illegible order as a “nonsense chronicle,” and transported one glyph block to the Ucanal ballcourt center alley—likely with sacrificial rituals involving defeated Ucanal lords (C5-049). Parallel Tikal recovery under Ah-Cacaw uses similar reclamation tools; little mutual mention between Tikal and Naranjo—authors speculate Dos Pilas meddling in Naranjo sowed distrust between logical allies (C5-050).
