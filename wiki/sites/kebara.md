# Kebara

Mount Carmel cave, Israel: Neanderthal remains (including hyoid) and Mousterian sequence; pivotal absolute dates for Levant Neanderthal–modern ordering. Core locus in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) ch. 4–5. Dates below are **as of Stringer & Gamble 1993**.

## Discovery and Excavation History

As of Stringer & Gamble (1993):

- **1931:** Upper Palaeolithic fossils recovered.
- **1964:** a Neanderthal child.
- **1984:** spectacular burial skeleton virtually intact from lower jaw to pelvis; skull and upper jaw missing except one tooth — presumed originally present and removed by later human or animal activity. S&G call it the most complete trunk and upper-limb Neanderthal skeleton then known.

The find anchors late-twentieth-century reassessments of Neanderthal postcrania (pelvis, thorax, hyoid) after Boule-era distortions.

## Occupation Sequence

**(As of Stringer & Gamble 1993.)** Thermoluminescence on burnt flints from levels adjoining the Neanderthal burial ~**60 ka**, confirmed by ESR on animal teeth from equivalent levels. That age is younger than [[qafzeh|Qafzeh]]/[[skhul|Skhul]] early moderns (~100–90+ ka) and sits within S&G’s colder-period Neanderthal occupation of the Levant after earlier modern presence — one of the three models they outline for the corridor.

Industry: Middle Palaeolithic (Mousterian) tools with the Neanderthal burial — the same technological grade as at much older Qafzeh moderns, so tools do not diagnose species here (S&G emphasis). Upper Palaeolithic levels overlie/are also present in the longer site history.

## Significance

- **Dating lever:** ~60 ka Neanderthal vs older Levantine moderns reordered succession assumptions that underwrote local Neanderthal→modern evolution ([[modern-human-origins-debate]]).
- **Cold body in a warm cave:** S&G argue Kebara upper-limb and trunk proportions (pelvic width vs estimated height) most resemble cold-adapted modern populations (Lapps, Inuit) — even more pronounced — so Middle Eastern Neanderthals were fundamentally cold-adapted, unlike tropical-proportioned Skhul/Qafzeh early moderns and European Cro-Magnons (attributed).
- **Pelvis:** longest superior pubic ramus then known; Yoel Rak’s description — internal birth-canal volume not enlarged; lengthening widens pelvis frontally. Undermines earlier gestation/large-baby hypotheses; Rak revives locomotion/hip-function explanations (positions via S&G 1993).
- **Hyoid:** first Neanderthal hyoid recovered; small relative to robust body, virtually indistinguishable from modern. Experts split: similar vocal skill vs bone uninformative (pig hyoids also similar) — live speech debate (Crelin & Lieberman history; S&G synthesis).
- **Massive mandible** among the largest known Neanderthal jaws (S&G 1993).

## Key Debates

- Chronological relation to Skhul/Qafzeh and Tabun under TL/ESR vs older relative sequences.
- Whether Kebara Neanderthals **replaced/displaced** earlier Levantine moderns (~70 ka entry model) or merely overlap in a corridor without clean succession (S&G three options).
- Speech anatomy (hyoid + cranial base) vs archaeological proxies for language.
- Burial: intentional interment with later skull removal vs other taphonomic paths; S&G’s broader caution on equating complete skeletons with modern symbolic burial.
- Technology–biology non-identity (MP tools at both Kebara Neanderthal and older Qafzeh moderns).
