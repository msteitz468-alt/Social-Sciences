# Nevalı Çori

PPNB settlement and cult architecture in SE Anatolia (Turkey), excavated by Harald Hauptmann before flooding by the Atatürk Dam. For [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|2000]]*), a key witness to **neolithisation of Anatolia** and to sophisticated anthropomorphic cult sculpture in the PPNB symbolic package. Art lineage later linked (by others) to [[gobekli-tepe|Göbekli Tepe]] (~30 km).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Hauptmann 1983–91; site now flooded. Publication and museum pieces (Urfa) underpin Cauvin’s Anatolian chapters and plate references. Modern comparative work often pairs Nevalı Çori with Göbekli Tepe (Schmidt); Cauvin’s 2000 main text predates full Göbekli impact (Postscript may note newer finds — attribute carefully).

## Occupation Sequence

PPNB village with distinct **cult building** and monumental sculpture (T-capitals, human bas-relief). Rectangular architecture horizon of the northern PPNB expansion in Cauvin’s diffusion model.

## Significance

Shows SE Anatolia participating in PPNB ideology and settlement, not as a passive recipient of a purely Levantine economic package. Supports Cauvin’s claim that symbolic/religious elaboration accompanies neolithisation.

## Key Debates

Relation to Göbekli Tepe chronology and function; domestic vs ceremonial balance; how far Anatolian PPNB is demic diffusion vs local adoption.
