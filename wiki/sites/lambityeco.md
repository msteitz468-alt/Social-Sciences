# Lambityeco

## Discovery and Excavation History
Valley-floor site near Tlacolula, Valley of Oaxaca, excavated by [[paddock-john|John Paddock]] (1961–1976). Its Monte Albán IV occupation made it the type case in [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) for what happened in the valley after the urban decline of [[monte-alban|Monte Albán]].

## Occupation Sequence
Main florescence ca. 600–800 CE (Monte Albán IV as then defined), a prosperous small center with elite residences, ancestor friezes, and salt production, abandoned before the Late Postclassic.

## Significance
Paddock read Lambityeco's stucco portraits of named couples above tombs as genealogical legitimation by local lords after Monte Albán's fall — evidence for the devolution of power to secondary centers ([[state-formation]] in reverse; see [[zapotec-civilization]]).

## Key Debates
The definition and dating of "Monte Albán IV" as a period rests heavily on this site; the volume itself flags the IIIb–IV boundary as problematic.
