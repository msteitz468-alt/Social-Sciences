# Skhul

Mount Carmel cave remnant (es-Skhul), Israel: early [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern]] sample with Middle Palaeolithic tools. Core Levant locus in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) ch. 5. Dates below are **as of Stringer & Gamble 1993**.

## Discovery and Excavation History

As of Stringer & Gamble (1993), 1930s Anglo-American excavations uncovered remains of at least ten men, women and children at Skhul, with a smaller sample at the larger cave of [[tabun|Tabun]] nearby in Wadi el-Mughara. Resources available from Mount Carmel (~500 m) and the coastal plain are part of their site setting.

Apparent burials they report: **Skhul 4** on right side with flexed arms and legs; **Skhul 5** on back, head on chest, flexed legs, with a large wild-boar lower jaw in the arms. Similar Levalloiso-Mousterian artifacts at Skhul and Tabun led early scholars to assume near-contemporaneity.

McCown & Keith treated Skhul as the predominantly modern-looking end of one Mount Carmel population continuum with the Neanderthal-like Tabun woman (historical position, via S&G). The Skhul and [[qafzeh|Qafzeh]] samples were called “proto-Cro-Magnoids” in the 1950s; S&G stress that inadequate dating long blocked understanding of their real importance.

## Occupation Sequence

**(As of Stringer & Gamble 1993.)** Early moderns at Skhul dated by ESR on mammal teeth at ~100–80 ka, and older still by TL on burnt flints — roughly coeval with Qafzeh’s early modern levels and **older** than the [[kebara|Kebara]] Neanderthal burial (~60 ka TL/ESR). This overturned progressive models that made Skhul/Qafzeh ~40–45 ka descendants of ~50–60 ka Tabun Neanderthals.

S&G’s broader Levant narrative places possible Skhul-related modern visits around ~90 ka within a longer alternation of Neanderthal-like and modern occupations (see [[tabun]] occupation sequence). Whether Skhul represents a lasting founding population for later Eurasians or a premature “Out of Africa 2” pulse is left open in their text (position).

## Significance

- Together with Qafzeh, the type Levantine demonstration that **anatomically modern humans used Middle Palaeolithic technology** — breaking the European equation Middle Palaeolithic = Neanderthal / Upper Palaeolithic = Cro-Magnon (as of S&G 1993).
- Morphology S&G summarize as “decidedly modern” vs Tabun/Kebara: high short rounded vault; hollowed cheeks; tropical body proportions (males ~1.83 m, females ~1.70 m); modern-type pelvis; fewer fractures/stress signs than Neanderthals; average brain ~1550 ml.
- Anchors corridor arguments for African-derived moderns adjacent to Africa by ~100 ka, aligning (in S&G’s synthesis) with Cann–Stoneking–Wilson 1987 mtDNA dispersal timing — a **1993 genetic horizon**, provisional and later revised.
- Grave-associated fauna (wild-boar jaw at Skhul 5; cf. deer at Qafzeh 11) is among the rare claims for Middle Palaeolithic “grave goods” S&G still flag as relatively harder to dismiss than many European claims (attributed caution).

## Key Debates

- **Age relative to Neanderthals** (Tabun, Kebara, Amud) and implications for evolution-from vs replacement-of Neanderthals ([[modern-human-origins-debate]]).
- **Proto-Cro-Magnon derivation:** if ~60 ka older than European Cro-Magnons, is there a necessary connection? S&G note the gap as problematic (position).
- **One population vs two** with Tabun (McCown–Keith continuum vs separate lineages vs hybrids).
- **Behavioural modernity lag:** anatomically modern but not yet “us” archaeologically (S&G phrasing); modern behaviour’s geographic source remains uncertain even if anatomy is African-linked (as of 1993).
- Body-proportion explanations: S&G favour climatic adaptation over Wolpoff’s long-distance locomotion model for Skhul/Qafzeh long legs (attributed).

### Klein — *The Human Career* (2nd ed., 1999)

*(Attributed — [[klein-human-career-1999]], [[klein-richard]]; as of Klein 1999.)*

Klein groups Skhul with [[qafzeh]] as near-modern humans dated by TL/ESR to ~120–80 ka, predating the
Levantine [[neanderthals]] of Kebara/Amud — anatomically near-modern but, in his reading,
behaviorally indistinguishable from Neanderthals, supporting his anatomy-before-behavior argument
(see [[behavioral-modernity]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]], [[stringer-chris]]; as of Stringer 2012.*

**Dating reordering (ESR/TL with Grün, Schwarcz et al.).** Skhul early moderns at least as old as Qafzeh; continuing work places Skhul/Qafzeh ~**90–120 ka** — older than Kebara and Amud Neanderthals, so they **could not have evolved from those late Neanderthals**; late Neanderthals were in the Levant *after* early moderns. Intentional burials with European-Neanderthal-type tools; Stringer’s morphology: Skhul much more modern than Tabun/Amud. Dental work (Stringer with Compton and Humphrey; strengthened by Irish and Bailey) shows Skhul and Qafzeh early moderns have “**African**” dental traits.

**Symbolism (as of Stringer 2012).** At ~**115 ka**, Skhul has the oldest known symbolic burial (man clasping massive wild-boar lower jaw), plus shell beads and heated pigments — among the oldest shell-bead and pigment evidence. No symbolic burials older than Skhul/Qafzeh ~100 ka are accepted in Stringer’s survey (Herto suggestive of ritual treatment of remains). Synchrotron dental maturation: Skhul/Qafzeh early moderns at slow recent-human rates vs faster Neanderthals. EQ reaches highest values (~5.3–5.4) in Skhul/Qafzeh and Cro-Magnons.

**Admixture hypothesis.** Stringer floats possible modern–Neanderthal interbreeding in the Middle East ~120 ka (Skhul–Qafzeh / Tabun era) as an alternative to only late European contact for the ~2% Neanderthal autosomal signal (as of Stringer 2012). Caspari & Lee: Skhul/Qafzeh old:young adult ratios match low Neanderthal survivorship, not later Cro-Magnon longevity — cultural/social factors, not pure biology. Klein uses Skhul’s MP industry association for anatomy-before-behaviour; Stringer situates that claim within expanding African MSA symbolic evidence that undercuts a hard ~50 ka switch.

### Tattersall — *Masters of the Planet* (2012)

*Source: [[tattersall-masters-of-the-planet-2012]], [[tattersall-ian]]. **As of Tattersall 2012.***

**Morphology.** Mugharet-es-Skhul (W slope Mount Carmel, few dozen miles from [[qafzeh]]): remains of ten adults/juveniles probably ~**100 ka**. More uniform than Qafzeh but odd: high rounded vaults, brains ~**1,450–1,590 cc**; fairly heavily built faces **unretracted**, jutting before the vault, transverse bar of bone instead of vertical forehead. Pre-WWII describers wrote large volume yet identity conclusions “spectacularly obscure” (via Tattersall).

**Against Neanderthal hybrid.** Geographic temptation high (minutes’ stroll from [[tabun]]; Neanderthals seem in residence close in time though not proven exact contemporaries). Biologically: Neanderthals and moderns built on fundamentally different plans; hybrids should exhibit traits of both parents — “certainly not what we are seeing at Skhul” (**position**, Tattersall 2012). Alternative he finds “more intriguing”: interbreeding between moderns and descendants of N. African Aterian-like populations ([[jebel-irhoud]], Dar-es-Soltan) rather than with Neanderthals — closer recent African origins, less differentiated pair (**scenario**, not adjudication).

**Industry and behaviour.** Lithic context Mousterian, more or less identical to Levantine Neanderthal kits — **industry ≠ species**. Not demonstrably behaving significantly differently from Neanderthals (anatomy-before-behaviour alignment with Klein as reported). Picture complicated by recently reported pigments and shells apparently pierced for stringing: among oldest proposed symbolic signals (>100 ka) but evidence “slender and a bit arguable” — two shells perforated (possibly natural) at weakest points, *Nassarius* genus later widely used in better-substantiated ornamental contexts; shells collected far along the Mediterranean shore (special objects / long-distance exchange implication) (as of Tattersall 2012). Early Levant foray ultimately unsuccessful; by ~60 ka Neanderthals back in charge until later cognitively modern re-entry. See [[behavioral-modernity]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[aterian-industry]].
