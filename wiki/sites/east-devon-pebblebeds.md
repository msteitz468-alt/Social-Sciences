# East Devon Pebblebeds

Unique multi-coloured **pebble** heathland ridge in East Devon with no British equivalent, studied by [[tilley-christopher]] in *[[tilley-interpreting-landscapes-2010|Interpreting Landscapes]]* (2010, Ch. 6). Prior archaeological research was sparse (Tilley: "black hole" for sixty years).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Tilley's 2004–2007 walking and writing while dwelling in the landscape; Carter excavations of cairns provide rare excavation control. Preface notes subsequent co-directed Pebblebeds Project excavations (Andy Jones) — post-book densification pending.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed, Tilley 2010.)* Sparse Mesolithic–Neolithic on the heath itself; richer off-heath. High Peak ancestral hill; Hembury materials include crushed Bunter temper. First monuments are EBA round barrows/pebble cairns — heath as reserved funerary zone. Cosmological reading: Exe as river of death vs Otter as river of life; pebbles as ancestral gift; colour process ontology.

## Significance

Prime case for [[geological-identity]] and sensory aesthetics of stone. Links [[neolithic-sensory-revolution]] clearance landscapes to a non-chalk geology.

## Key Debates

Aesthetic/ancestral readings of pebbles are interpretive (attributed); modern beauty responses flagged by Tilley himself as potentially anachronistic.

## Historiography

Research gap until Tilley; ongoing excavation project may revise cairn chronologies — date-stamp claims as of 2010 book.
