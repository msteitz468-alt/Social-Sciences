# Mureybet

Middle Euphrates tell in northern Syria — key long-sequence site for [[cauvin-jacques|Jacques Cauvin]]'s model of neolithisation. Flooded by the Tabqa Dam reservoir after excavation. Type anchor for the **[[mureybetian-culture|Mureybetian]]** PPNA regional culture and for early **bull/bucranium** symbolism in a still non- or newly farming context (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]]*).

## Discovery and Excavation History

French-led excavations under Cauvin established a deep stratigraphic sequence used throughout *Birth of the Gods* as evidence of **in situ** cultural evolution from Natufian/Khiamian hunter-gatherers through PPNA farming without requiring external invasion at the origin point. Excavation quality is mid–late twentieth-century Near Eastern open-area/sounding work; flotation was not always systematic (Cauvin notes recovery limits for plant remains at related sites). Site now underwater — no further fieldwork.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Cauvin 2000 synthesis.)* Continuous local development claimed: late foragers → [[khiamian|Khiamian]] symbolic innovations (including bucrania) → Mureybetian village/farming → contribution to Early PPNB of the northern Levant. Absolute BC dates follow Cauvin’s calibrated tables (date-stamp; later schemes may shift).

## Significance

Empirical backbone of the [[revolution-of-symbols]] thesis: bull skulls and related deposits appear in a hunting-gathering economic context on the eve of farming. Counterweight to models that place first villages only in the southern Levant or only in Braidwood’s hilly flanks.

## Key Debates

- Continuity vs gap interpretations of Euphrates sequences.
- Whether bucrania = bull divinity.
- Plant-domestication timing at the site vs corridor peers ([[jericho]], Aswad, Netiv Hagdud).

## Identity Cautions

Mureybetian is an archaeological culture construct, not an ethnonym or language.

## Cauvin 2000 detail densification

*(Attributed — *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]].)*

- **Khiamian IB–II:** Khiam points; round/oval houses; diversified hunting/fishing/gathering; aurochs skulls with horns buried in houses/clay benches from ~10,000 BC; bull horns repeatedly in walls — deliberate deposits; cattle rarely hunted (technique/audacity) so bucrania ≠ food refuse.
- **Phase III ~9500–8700 BC:** farming only from **IIIB after ~9000 BC**. Female figurines in IIIA (eight stone/clay; no masculine). House XLVII-type differentiated round houses (sleeping platform, storage cells, earliest architecture-integrated geometric fresco). End IIIB: first rectangular constructions (cell-silos); coexistence with small round shelters; Jerf el Ahmar rounded-corner transitional houses.
- **Sequence claim:** continuous local evolution Natufian–Khiamian–Mureybetian without external invasion at the origin point — backbone of [[revolution-of-symbols]] chronology.
