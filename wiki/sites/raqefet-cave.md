# Raqefet Cave

Mount Carmel cave with Late [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] occupation and cemetery reported by Lengyel, Nadel, and Bocquentin in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]. Complements el-Wad as a Carmel mortuary and occupation locus.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Modern multi-specialist excavations; 2013 chapter synthesizes Natufian results. Broader literature (outside this volume) includes claims of flower-lined graves — treat as attributed and date-stamped when those sources are ingested.

## Occupation Sequence

Primarily Late Natufian occupation and burial activity (densify from claims).

## Significance

Core-area Late Natufian mortuary practice; links to Bocquentin’s field-anthropology approach also applied at Eynan.

## Key Debates

- Ritual elaboration and plant use in burials (positions densify from claims and later publications).

## Densified findings from Lengyel, Nadel & Bocquentin 2013

*(Attributed — [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]].)*

- Late Natufian cave occupation with cemetery and bedrock mortars.
- Part of Carmel mortuary landscape alongside el-Wad; Bocquentin field-anthropology approach shared with Eynan program.
- Red Sea scaphopods in Late Natufian contexts (Kurzawska et al., multi-site study).
