# Riversleigh

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Riversleigh** (northwest Queensland) is internationally famous as a **fossil field** and **World Heritage** property valued for Cenozoic vertebrate palaeontology. [[smith-laurajane|Laurajane Smith]] (*[[smith-uses-of-heritage-2006|Uses of Heritage]]* 2006, Ch. 5) does not re-excavate the site; she analyses how Riversleigh is **constituted as heritage** — scientific mastery narratives, Australian landscape myth, tourism, and [[waanyi|Waanyi]] cultural claims to **country**. Excavation quality for fossils is a palaeontological matter; this page's social-science load is the **heritage discourse**, not the fossil systematics. Smith also reports qualitative work with palaeontologists (one research season 1998) and a visitor survey (n=92) at the Riversleigh Fossil Centre and “D site.”

## Occupation Sequence

- **Palaeontological deep time:** Cenozoic vertebrate fossil assemblages underwriting **World Heritage** natural listing (**1994** as Smith reports) as living research sites of scientific universal value; continued excavation framed as conservation of scientific values.
- **Waanyi country:** living cultural landscape; fossils known prior to palaeontological “discoveries”; archaeological dates for Indigenous occupation to at least **15,000 BP** *(as Smith reports)*; management rights contested.
- **Modern tourism/science:** Fossil Centre (Mount Isa); “D site” visitation; many domestic tourists unaware of WHS status, seeking “real Australia” / national antiquity.

## Significance

Type case in Smith for **[[dissonant-heritage]]** under World Heritage: palaeontological and national landscape discourses authorize one sense of place while Waanyi meanings and control claims constitute another. Demonstrates the Australian **[[authorized-heritage-discourse|AHD]]** of landscape (bushman/pioneer myths, masculinity, rugged testing-ground, terra nullius residues after Mabo/Wik) as performed and contested. Palaeontological disciplinary identity (macho field culture, naming, storytelling) is authorized by and authorizes “Australian heritage” values marketable to tourism. Locals appropriate “country” / custodianship discourse while rehearsing AHD character narratives. **Dissonance** centers on **who controls meanings** and thus management and resources (fossils, land, tourism, sovereignty).

## Key Debates

- Natural vs cultural heritage framing; who speaks for Riversleigh.
- Fossil return vs scientific sample retention ([[waanyi]] position vs palaeontology).
- National landscape myth vs Indigenous prior sovereignty evidenced in cultural sites.
