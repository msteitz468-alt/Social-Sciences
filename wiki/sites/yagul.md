# Yagul

## Discovery and Excavation History
Hilltop-and-piedmont center in the eastern (Tlacolula) arm of the Valley of Oaxaca, excavated principally by [[paddock-john|John Paddock]] and colleagues in the 1950s–1960s. Reported in [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) for its Monte Albán I and II occupations and its Postclassic florescence.

## Occupation Sequence
Occupied from Monte Albán I; a major fortified urban center in Monte Albán V, when Paddock and others saw it as one of the petty polities of the "balkanized" Postclassic valley alongside [[mitla|Mitla]].

## Significance
Key evidence in the Postclassic "Mixtec impact" debate — whether its polychrome ceramics and palace architecture mark Mixtec presence or Zapotec adoption of an international style (positions recorded on [[mixtec-culture]] and [[zapotec-civilization]]).

## Key Debates
See the Paddock vs. Flannery/Marcus dispute recorded from [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]].

## Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas (2022) — Period I tombs

*(Attributed — [[blanton-ancient-oaxaca-2022|Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas]], *Ancient Oaxaca: The Monte Albán State*, 2nd ed. 2022.)* Four Monte Albán Period I tombs excavated at Yagul — at the time a fifth-rank village 35 km east of Monte Albán in the Tlacolula Valley — contained many vessels for liquids and a Cocijo-element brazier similar to those found at Monte Albán and [[monte-negro|Monte Negro]] (Chadwick 1966), evidence the authors cite for the spread of a shared Period I ritual/ceramic tradition across the valley's settlement hierarchy even at low-rank centers.
