# Makapansgat

A fossil-bearing cave-breccia site in the Makapan Valley, Limpopo, South Africa, one of the "Cradle of Humankind"–type australopithecine localities. It is significant twice over: as a source of *[[australopithecus|Australopithecus africanus]]* fossils, and as the type case of a **discredited behavioral inference** from a bone assemblage.

## Discovery and Excavation History

The lime-quarry breccias yielded fossil fauna and, from 1947, australopithecine remains recovered under [[dart-raymond|Raymond Dart]] and the University of the Witwatersrand. Excavation quality is constrained by the site's origin as a commercial limestone quarry — much material came from blasted, decalcified dump breccia rather than controlled context, a reliability fact that bears directly on the interpretive dispute below. Later, more controlled work (Phillip Tobias and others) refined the stratigraphy and dating.

## Occupation Sequence

The australopithecine-bearing deposits (Member 3 / "Grey Breccia") are dated by biostratigraphy and paleomagnetism to roughly 3–2.6 Ma (approximate). The bones are a **faunal accumulation in a cave/sinkhole context**, not an occupation floor — the nature of the accumulating agent is the whole question.

## Significance

At Makapansgat, Dart proposed the **[[osteodontokeratic-culture|osteodontokeratic culture]]** (1949, 1957): that the differential frequencies of skeletal parts and the modifications on the bones showed *A. africanus* selecting and manufacturing tools from bone, tooth, and horn, and living as a predatory hunter (feeding his broader [[hunting-hypothesis|killer-ape reading]] of human origins). The site is thus the origin point of one of the discipline's most consequential over-interpretations.

## Key Debates

[[brain-ck|C.K. Brain]]'s actualistic study of goat bones scavenged by dogs at Hottentot villages showed the Makapansgat part-frequency profile could arise from carnivore ravaging and density-mediated [[taphonomy|preservation]] alone, with no hominid selection — a founding demonstration of [[taphonomy]] and [[actualistic-studies|actualistic method]]. [[binford-lewis|Binford]] (*[[binford-bones-1981|Bones]]*, 1981) treated Makapansgat and the osteodontokeratic claim as the historical paradigm of reading behavior off a bone assemblage without independent warrant. The australopithecine fossils themselves remain a secure part of the *A. africanus* record; the tool-culture reading is regarded as obsolete. See [[osteodontokeratic-culture]] and [[hunting-scavenging-debate]].
