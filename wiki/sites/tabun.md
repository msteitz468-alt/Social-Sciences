# Tabun

Mount Carmel cave (et-Tabun) in the Wadi el-Mughara, Israel: long Middle Palaeolithic sequence and classic Neanderthal-associated fossils. Core Levant locus in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) ch. 5. Absolute ages below are **as of Stringer & Gamble 1993** (TL/ESR horizon) and have been revised since.

## Discovery and Excavation History

As of Stringer & Gamble (1993), 1930s Anglo-American excavations on Mount Carmel uncovered a smaller fossil sample at Tabun than at neighbouring [[skhul|Skhul]], including the partial skeleton of a woman and an isolated larger (male?) mandible from layer C. Both Tabun and Skhul were associated with similar Levalloiso-Mousterian industries, which led early scholars to treat the caves as near-contemporaries. Interwar Mount Carmel work (with Crimea and Central Asia) helped set what S&G still regarded in 1993 as the southern geographical limit of distinctive Neanderthal fossils.

McCown & Keith’s two-volume report treated Tabun and Skhul as one highly variable population: Skhul predominantly modern-looking, Tabun woman lightly built but Neanderthal-like; they proposed a Levantine “progressive” Neanderthal — less specialized than classic Europeans, not a direct evolutionary intermediate (historical position, via S&G 1993). Later alternatives S&G record: missing link between Neanderthals and moderns; hybrids of separate populations; or chronological frameworks that uncouple the two samples.

## Occupation Sequence

**(Chronology as of Stringer & Gamble 1993 — ESR/TL; supersedable.)**

S&G report ESR dating of the Tabun sequence from ~200 ka to ~90 ka — roughly double traditional age estimates — with the Tabun woman in level C probably ~110 ka, roughly coeval with or slightly before the early moderns at [[qafzeh|Qafzeh]]. Their revised occupational narrative if ESR is correct:

1. Hand-axe makers in an earlier interglacial ~200 ka.
2. Early Neanderthals (including the Tabun woman) during/just after the last interglacial ~110 ka.
3. Possible early modern visits related to [[skhul|Skhul]] ~90 ka.
4. Regional Neanderthals camping in colder periods (e.g. [[kebara|Kebara]] ~60 ka).
5. Neanderthal use of the broader Mount Carmel area ending by ~45 ka (judging from Amud); moderns back in the Levant to stay by ~37 ka (Ksar Akil “Egbert” child, as they report).

Large-mammal analyses had long supported Tabun preceding Skhul, but small-mammal studies suggested Tabun C was *younger* than main early-modern levels at Qafzeh — already contradicting a simple Neanderthal→modern succession before absolute methods (as of S&G 1993). Radiocarbon’s ~40 ka ceiling had left the critical window untestable until TL/ESR/U-series.

Outside Europe’s flake→blade progressivism, Garrod’s Tabun (with Rust’s Yabrud) shows Amudian / pre-Upper Palaeolithic (PUP) blade assemblages appearing and disappearing within long Middle Palaeolithic sequences (Ronen’s term, via S&G 1993) — so blade technology is not a reliable Ancient/Modern marker.

## Significance

- Anchors the **Neanderthal pole** of the Middle Eastern fossil dichotomy: Neanderthal-like remains at Tabun, [[shanidar]], Amud, [[kebara]] vs modern-looking [[skhul]] and [[qafzeh]] (as of S&G 1993).
- Tabun woman exemplifies Neanderthal cranial traits (long broad low skull, strong brows even in females) and the extended pubic ramus first recognized in her pelvis (S&G 1993).
- With Skhul/Qafzeh, forces the field’s chronological models of coexistence, succession, and replacement under [[recent-african-origin]] vs [[multiregional-evolution]] ([[modern-human-origins-debate]]).
- Illustrates technology–biology non-identity: long Mousterian sequence and intermittent PUP blades without equating industry to species.

## Key Debates

- **Tabun–Skhul relationship:** one variable population (McCown & Keith) vs two groups vs hybrids vs re-dated non-contemporaneity (positions as of / reported in S&G 1993).
- **ESR chronology vs older progressive models** that put Tabun Neanderthals at 50–60 ka and Skhul/Qafzeh moderns as their 40–45 ka descendants.
- **Which layer holds which industry and fossil** (Tabun B vs C reassignments in some models that put moderns first and Neanderthals arriving ~70 ka).
- **Burial vs complete-skeleton survival:** S&G (Gamble’s carnivore model) argue complete bodies at Tabun/Shanidar track low carnivore cave use more than symbolic burial (position).
- Implications for [[recent-african-origin]] and the Middle East as corridor rather than European-style cul-de-sac.
