# Rehman Dheri

## Discovery and Excavation History

A Pre-urban/Early Harappan town on the Gomal Plain near the Indus–Zhob
confluence, excavated by F. A. Durrani (1988). Documented here from
[[wright-ancient-indus-2010|Wright (2010)]].

## Occupation Sequence

About 22 hectares, enclosed by a wall, with a main NW–SE street axis from its
earliest levels — an early example of planned layout. Kot Diji-style ceramics
(a Pre-urban style elsewhere) continued to be produced there until at least
2300 BCE, and Indus-associated new ceramic types are largely absent.

## Significance

Wright reads Rehman Dheri (attributed) as evidence that the Gomal region
**retained its indigenous culture and was not eclipsed by the Indus
civilization** — a frontier or "edge-zone" settlement that maintained local
traditions even as urbanism consolidated in the alluvial heartland. Beads
(lapis lazuli, turquoise) and figurines nonetheless show Central Asian
connections. It is one of the boundary cultures Wright cites for a picture of
the Indus as an expansive system engaging in negotiated affiliations rather
than uniform political control (see [[indus-polity-debate]]).

## Key Debates

Feeds Wright's edge-zone / "negotiated affiliation" reading of Indus political
organization ([[indus-polity-debate]]).
