# Ausangate

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Ausangate** is a major peak of the Cordillera Vilcanota (Cuzco Department, Peru), not primarily known as an excavated archaeological type-site in this vault. This page records it as documented in [[de-la-cadena-marisol|Marisol de la Cadena]]’s *[[de-la-cadena-earth-beings-2015|Earth Beings]]* (2015) via co-labor with Mariano and Nazario Turpo of nearby [[pacchanta|Pacchanta]] / Ocongate.

## Occupation Sequence

Continuous runakuna engagement in the ethnographic and historical record of the book (mid-20th-century land struggle through 2000s tourism). Not a stratified dig sequence.

## Significance

In de la Cadena’s analysis (attributed), Ausangate is simultaneously a **mountain** and an **[[earth-beings|earth-being]]** (*tirakuna* / Apu): a participant in conversation, politics, and obligation whom modern institutions cannot recognize without reduction. It is the geographic and relational pivot of the Turpos’ practice and of Mariano’s [[cosmopolitics]] “between lawyers and Ausangate.”

## Key Debates

Four-way non-identity: mountain (material) ≠ Apu (earth-being) ≠ “sacred mountain” as cultural belief about one Nature. De la Cadena’s claim is ontological participation within [[ayllu]] relations; critics of the [[ontological-turn]] re-read the same as symbolism or religion. Record as positions; do not equate in wiki voice.

## Identity Cautions

This page does **not** assert that Ausangate “is a person” in wiki voice. It records de la Cadena–Turpo co-labored claims and the dual mountain/earth-being geometry of the ethnography. Archaeological culture labels are not applied.
