## Discovery and Excavation History

Pachacamac, at the mouth of the Lurín Valley on Peru’s central coast, is among the best-known multi-period ceremonial cities of the Andes. Spanish accounts describe the sacking of the wooden idol of the creator/earthquake deity Pachacamac in a darkened chamber of a mound-top temple by Pizarro’s brother. Moseley (as of [[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|2001]]) synthesizes ethnohistory and archaeology rather than a single excavation campaign: the site as oracle city, pilgrimage economy, and political node of Ichma and earlier Lima spheres.

## Occupation Sequence

**Lima culture foundation (EIP, as of Moseley 2001):** By ~AD 200 stronger settlement and monumental focus in the central Rímac; Pachacamac founded near the shore as a notable Lima center with elite compounds; core of the large “Sun” huaca and “Pachacamac Temple” probably of this time; numerous burials suggest sacrosanct status. Moseley presumes the oracle cult and priesthood began then and endured more than a millennium through petty kingdoms and great states. Along with the Island of the Sun in Lake Titicaca, Pachacamac remained one of the most sacred places in the Inca realm until the Spanish sack.

**Middle Horizon:** Numerous tomb accompaniments exhibit [[wari-culture|Huari/Wari]] influence of an **ideological** nature—no skeletal evidence of Ayacucho people colonizing the oracle center; no highland administrative centers of Huari type on site (as of Moseley 2001). Regional religious cults (Chavín earlier; Pachacamac) exemplify **nodal/dispersed** extensive organization beyond pure politics/economics.

**Late Intermediate Period / Ichma:** Ichma territory embraced Rímac and Lurín with the oracle at Pachacamac; architectural florescence of sanctuaries and elite compounds; temple of Pachacamac as supreme *[[huaca|huaca]] adoratorio* amid a cosmopolitan pantheon (some sanctuaries possibly for foreign deities). Close Inca–Ichma relationship included military alliance to attack and defeat northern adversary [[chimu-culture|Chimor]] (as of Moseley 2001).

**Inca Late Horizon:** Topa Inca had a shrine erected dedicated to the cult of the Sun; other pantheon representatives present. One Spaniard reported many coastal populations paid tribute to Pachacamac, not Cuzco. Hierarchical priesthood (bishop/archbishop-like ranks by ability not inheritance); satellite shrines for offspring/kindred among tithe-paying populations; full-time resident clergy not very large but **mortuary population enormous**—prime burial place for commoners and kurakas alike (as of Moseley 2001).

## Significance

Pan-Andean oracle and pilgrimage center rivalling the Island of the Sun; creator of humans and agricultural plants; lord of earthquakes; cult members considered him superior to sun and moon (attributed ethnohistory). Commanding oracle drawing devotees from Ecuador through Bolivia; pilgrimage costly (food, coca, fabrics garnered by clergy). Political order held sway over señoríos in Lurín and Rímac valleys. Anchors central-coast religious geography across EIP–LH and the Inca–Ichma anti-Chimor alliance narrative.

## Key Debates

- Exact foundation date of the oracle vs later ethnohistoric projection.
- Degree of political power vs purely religious influence over coastal tribute.
- Nature of Middle Horizon “Huari influence” without colonization (style/ideology vs empire).
- Identification and dating of specific platforms (Sun Temple components vs Pachacamac Temple).
