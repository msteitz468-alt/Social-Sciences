# Moundville

Major [[mississippian-culture|Mississippian]] multi-mound center on the Black Warrior River, west-central Alabama. Box feature and main text in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner 2021]] Ch. 6; earlier textbook layers cite Peebles’s mortuary ranking work.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Long-excavated classic of Southeastern archaeology; mortuary analyses (Peebles and successors) made it a type case for ranked Mississippian society. Milner treats mound-construction labor as within local capacity, especially with hinterland help — parallel to his [[cahokia]] labor argument.

## Occupation Sequence

Mississippian **mound-and-plaza** center with strong evidence of **ranked burial treatment**. Platform mounds supported elite and ceremonial architecture. Over its history the balance of residential vs ceremonial use has been debated (occupied capital vs more vacant ceremonial phases — specialist literature beyond Milner’s summary).

## Significance

- Second-tier (after Cahokia) in many textbook rankings of Eastern Woodlands complexity.
- Classic mortuary archaeology of rank short of state.
- Links: [[southeastern-ceremonial-complex]], [[chiefdom]], [[hereditary-rank]].

## Key Debates

Degree of political centralization; urban vs vacant ceremonial models; SECC iconography and exchange.
