# Jebel Irhoud (Morocco)

North African cave/rockshelter with early *Homo* fossils long placed near the archaic–modern boundary. Appears in African origin sequences (e.g. Irhoud among “borderline moderns” in [[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993]]; revisited in [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]]).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Known since mid-20th-century finds; subsequent campaigns refined context. **Dating and morphological interpretation have moved substantially** after 2012 in the wider literature (Hublin et al. re-dating) — claims on this page from Stringer 2012 / S&G 1993 must stay **horizon-stamped** and not silently overwritten by later work until those papers are ingested.

## Occupation Sequence

Middle Stone Age associations; fossils used in arguments for early African modernization and possible North African continuity (Irhoud → later Maghreb series in some treatments — attributed).

## Significance

- Extends early *sapiens*-related morphology to **northwest Africa**, complicating purely East African “Garden of Eden” origin stories (Stringer 2012 acknowledges multi-area African complexity vs his earlier single-center preference).
- Supports pan-African or multi-regional-within-Africa models of modern origin under [[recent-african-origin]].

## Key Debates

Age; degree of modernity; relation to Levantine early moderns ([[skhul]], [[qafzeh]]); post-2012 chronological revisions. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].
