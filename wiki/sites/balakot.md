# Balakot

## Discovery and Excavation History

A coastal settlement on Sonmiani Bay in Las Bela (Balochistan), about 2.7
hectares, excavated by George Dales in the 1970s. Documented here from
[[wright-ancient-indus-2010|Wright (2010)]] (drawing on Dales 1979, Belcher
2003).

## Occupation Sequence

Pre-urban levels show pottery with northern Balochistan/Amri affinities plus a
distinct **Nal** type (canister-shaped, painted floral/zoomorphic polychrome
motifs). After a roughly 200-year abandonment the site was reoccupied in the
Urban period with typical Indus pottery.

## Significance

- **Marine subsistence.** About half the recovered fauna is fish; subsistence
  combined cattle/sheep/goat pastoralism, limited hunting, and shellfishing,
  with barley and jujube as plant foods.
- **Shell-bangle production.** Segregated workshops by shell type — clam-shell
  bangles chipped/ground one-per-shell on a mudbrick platform (large debris
  suggests export production); conch-shell bangles sawn with bronze saws,
  multiple per shell (Kenoyer 1997b). Some stoneware bangles (a craft otherwise
  restricted to Mohenjo-daro and Harappa) also reached Balakot.
- **Fish-processing station (attributed, Belcher 2003).** Belcher proposes
  Balakot was resettled in the Urban period specifically to exploit fish and
  shellfish, with fish likely salted for preservation and trade.

## Occupation Sequence and Debates

Balakot lies within the zone where [[kulli-culture|Kulli]] material appears; its
reoccupation and craft-export role feed the Indus trade-organization discussion
([[craft-specialization]], [[indus-polity-debate]]).
