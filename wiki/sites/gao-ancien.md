# Gao Ancien (Old Gao)

**Gao Ancien** is the archaeological settlement complex of **Old Gao** on the Middle Niger in modern Mali — the urban core associated with the historic entrepôt of Gao in Arabic and West African historiography. Primary in-vault treatment: [[insoll-timothy|Timothy Insoll]], *[[insoll-islam-archaeology-history-gao-1996|Islam, Archaeology and History: Gao Region (Mali) ca. AD 900–1250]]* (1996 BAR / Cambridge Monographs in African Archaeology 39), based on 1993 fieldwork and a full review of earlier research.

**Identity cautions:** The archaeological site is **not** identical to the later Songhay Empire, to modern Songhay ethnicity, or to any single language community. Use `associated_with` for historical Gao/Songhay labels; four-way non-identity applies. Pair with sister site [[gao-saney]] (cemetery/tell complex ~northeast of Gao).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Insoll 1996 Ch. 2 reviews colonial and post-independence work (Mauny and others; survey by Seydou Dawa 1985; French–Malian missions). Insoll’s **1993** season (Ch. 3) provides the monograph’s primary excavation data at Gao Ancien (imported ceramics, glass, beads, architecture — details densified from claims). Earlier research quality varies: some colonial cemetery digs at Gao-Saney were poorly recorded; Insoll re-synthesizes and re-excavates contexts where possible.

## Occupation Sequence

Focus window **ca. AD 900–1250** (monograph title range): Islamic-period urbanism, mosque/burial evidence, and trade assemblages. Earlier and later occupation exists regionally; Insoll’s analytical centre is the medieval entrepôt phase. Radiocarbon and import-dating as of mid-1990s — **date-stamp**.



## Occupation Sequence (attributed — Insoll 1996)

Insoll’s 1993 trench (6 × 6.5 m; 19 Sep–11 Oct 1993) yielded a five-period sequence (depths to ~1.85 m). Chronology as of mid-1990s C14 + import associations:

| Period | Approximate date (Insoll 1996) | Material character |
|---|---|---|
| **5** | from ~6th/7th–9th c. | Base levels; no structures in trench |
| **4** | late 9th–11th c. | Banco/puddled-mud structures; ~20 drainpipe fragments (flat-roof drainage); hippopotamus ivory cache (~53 tusks reported; fauna also lists ivory fragments); ~1.5 kg slag; possible fire destruction at end |
| **3** | 11th–12th c. | Peak **imports**: glazed pottery, glass (~147 frags in finds discussion), beads; banco structure with copper door fittings; thin import-rich layer |
| **2** | late 12th–13th c. | Fired-brick building (floor + column 1.8×0.6×0.7 m; bricks ~25×25×7 cm) |
| **1** | late 13th/14th–modern disturbance | Angular banco/banco-brick walls; disturbed; fewer imports; continued Muslim burial indicators |

**C14** (lab codes as published): GX-21656, GX-20623, GX-21657 — use with Insoll’s period assignments; note tension he flags between some C14 and import dates.

**Urban role (attributed):** Gao Ancien develops as an **Islamized quartier** by late 12th–early 13th c. — trade-facing, import-rich, mosque with fired-brick mihrab (Mauny’s mosque re-located ~100 m NE in 1993 survey), Muslim cemeteries nearby — **not** a uniformly Muslim city. Contrasts with Gadei (harbour/Sorko associations; few imports) and Koima (right bank; less Islamized burial). Manufacturing evidence weak vs [[gao-saney]] (little slag/spindle/crucibles at Ancien; trade indicators strong).

**Pottery corpus:** 8083 sherds recorded at Gao Ancien (one wash-loss exception). Dominant classes (identified assemblage): red-slipped ~38%, comb-stamped ~37%, channelled ~12%, painted ~7% (Insoll App. 7).

## Significance (densified)

- Empirical anchor for [[islamic-archaeology]] in the western Sahel: multi-domain Islamization (burial, architecture, imports), not mosque-only.
- Complements [[jenne-jeno]] Inland Delta urbanism with a historically named northern entrepôt more tightly tied to Arabic-source trade geography.
- Dual-settlement model with [[gao-saney]] (cemetery + manufacturing/tell vs Ancien mercantile/elite).

## Significance

- Type case for **[[islamic-archaeology]]** in West Africa beyond mosque-monument tourism: trade goods, diet/fauna (appendices), burial, urban form.
- Node in **trans-Saharan** and **inter-regional** West African exchange (Insoll 1996 chs. 5–6).
- Complements Inland Niger Delta urbanism at [[jenne-jeno]] (McIntosh program) with a more northerly, historically named entrepôt.

## Key Debates

- Relationship of archaeological Gao to Arabic-source “Gawgaw” and later Songhay political narratives — associate, do not equate without argument.
- Chronology of Islamization vs. trade intensification (Insoll ch. 7 positions).
