# Sibudu Cave

Rock shelter in KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, central to late Pleistocene **Middle Stone Age** debates on technology, cognition, and subsistence. Primary vault synthesis: Marlize Lombard, Ch. 26 of *[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013|The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology]]* (2013), reporting work by Wadley, Backwell, and others (**as of handbook ~2012**).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Modern excavations under Lyn Wadley produced high-resolution MSA sequences used for experimental archaeology (adhesives), use-wear, and multi-proxy subsistence studies. Handbook claims are secondary.

## Occupation Sequence

Still Bay / Howiesons Poort and post-HP MSA layers (ages as reported by Lombard: bone points 64–61 kya; beads ~71 kya; HP arrow arguments 60–64 kya). Continuity and flexibility after Howiesons Poort are stressed against simple “behavioural reversal” models (Lombard, attributed).

## Significance

- **Bone-tool industry** alongside [[blombos-cave|Blombos]] overturns older dismissals of MSA bone as mixing/aberration.
- **Compound adhesives** (~70 kya) — experimental replication implies deliberate chemical/thermal control (Wadley et al. via Lombard).
- **Mechanically projected weapons** — multi-stranded evidence for stone-tipped arrows (and by implication bows) in HP contexts at Sibudu and Umhlatuzana (Lombard & Phillipson via Lombard).
- **Remote capture** — Wadley’s snare/trap argument from faunal structure (~61–70 kya) as enhanced working-memory proxy (interpretive; attributed).

## Key Debates

Cognitive-modernity checklists vs demographic/behavioral flexibility models (see [[behavioral-modernity]]; Barham Ch. 24). Projectile and snare inferences are **stronger as multi-proxy arguments than as single-find proof**.

## Historiography

Part of the post-2000 southern African MSA revolution densifying African priority for many “modern” behaviors. Companion sites: [[blombos-cave]], [[klasies-river]], [[howiesons-poort-industry]], [[still-bay-industry]].

## Oxford Handbook densification (Mitchell & Lane 2013)

*Attributed — Marlize Lombard (Ch. 26), Lyn Wadley (Ch. 25), Christian Tryon (Ch. 8) in [[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]; **as of handbook ~2012**. Page already seeded from this handbook; densifies residual claims without re-counting the ingest.*

Lombard: bone points 64–61 kya (one possible arrowhead); six *Afrolittorina africana* shells (three perforated) ~71 kya may be beads; notched bone ~60 kya. Direct evidence for ochre-enriched **compound adhesives** for hafting begins ~70 kya at Sibudu and continues to final MSA ~35 kya — artisans as competent chemists/pyrotechnologists by ≥70 kya without necessarily invoking symbolism (Wadley et al. via Lombard). Multi-stranded evidence for stone-tipped **arrows** (and by implication bows) from Howiesons Poort contexts at Sibudu and Umhlatuzana 60–64 kya. Wadley’s multistranded snare/trap argument (~61–70 kya) from forest taxa, high small-mammal frequencies, high taxonomic diversity, and small carnivores as non-selective capture. Micromorphology and seeds show sedge-covered sleeping areas/bedding ~50–60 kya and hearth sweeping/dumping from at least ~58 kya. Post-Howiesons Poort at Sibudu shows flexibility comparable to recent hunter-gatherers rather than simple behavioural reversal. Tryon: ochre/haematite traces on ~70 ka Still Bay points at Sibudu as soft-hammer evidence.
