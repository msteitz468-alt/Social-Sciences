# Jalieza

## Discovery and Excavation History

Major secondary (and later peer) urban center on a mountain ridge in the southern Valle Grande of the Valley of Oaxaca. Primary treatment in this wiki’s collection is the Settlement Pattern Project demography synthesized in [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996|Marcus & Flannery 1996]] (*Zapotec Civilization*; study [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-study]]). Knowledge base is survey-led; treat population figures as **range estimates**, not census counts.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Marcus & Flannery 1996 / Settlement Pattern Project.)*

- **Monte Albán IIIa:** Jalieza covers an estimated **408 ha** with population on the order of **~12,835**, approaching [[monte-alban|Monte Albán]]’s own ~16,500 — one of three Tier-1 population concentrations (with Monte Albán and the Dainzú–Macuilxóchitl–Tlacochahuaya–Guadalupe cluster in Tlacolula). Authors float spectacular growth as a possible second valley example of [[synoikism|synoikism]] / urban relocation.
- **Defense context:** Part of a IIIa landscape in which ~38 fortifiable sites held nearly two-thirds of an estimated valley population ~115,000 after outer provinces rolled back.
- **Monte Albán IV:** After Monte Albán’s decline, Jalieza is described as the **largest city in the valley** in Period IV multipolar reorganization.

## Significance

Anchors the argument that Classic Zapotec urbanization was not permanently mono-centric: peer cities and defense-driven redistribution reshaped the valley after imperial frontiers contracted. Links to [[settlement-hierarchy]], [[state-formation]], and [[zapotec-civilization]].

## Key Debates

Whether IIIa growth is true synoikism or in-place agglomeration remains an **attributed hypothesis** in Marcus & Flannery 1996, not a demonstrated Greek-style multi-village relocation.
