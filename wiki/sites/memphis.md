# Memphis

Lower Egyptian capital and Ptah cult centre near the apex of the delta; traditional administrative counterpart to Upper Egyptian Thebes. Densified from [[wilkinson-toby|Wilkinson]] *[[wilkinson-rise-fall-ancient-egypt-2010|Rise and Fall]]* (2010) political narrative.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Poor preservation relative to desert cemeteries; known from texts, scattered excavations, and nearby Saqqara necropolis. Wilkinson treats Memphis as recurring seat of northern power across dynasties.

## Occupation Sequence

Early Dynastic–Old Kingdom capital ideology; intermittent capital status; Late Period and Ptolemaic administrative roles before Alexandria’s rise.

## Significance

Balance of “Two Lands”; Ptah temple as northern religious pole (Kemp enclosure-wall model of temple as citadel).

## Key Debates

Archaeological invisibility of palace vs cemetery bias.

## Historiography

Stub from Wilkinson 2010; specialist Memphis monographs not yet ingested.

## Wilkinson densification (2010)

*(Attributed — [[wilkinson-toby]], *[[wilkinson-rise-fall-ancient-egypt-2010]].)*

Recurring northern capital and prize of reunifiers (e.g. Ahmose after Hyksos); administrative counterweight to [[thebes-egypt|Thebes]]; later Saite and Persian-period politics play out across Memphis–delta axis before Alexandria supersedes it under the Ptolemies.
