# Giza

Old Kingdom royal necropolis and plateau complex west of modern Cairo; the three Fourth Dynasty pyramids of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure and their associated temples, Sphinx, elite mastabas, and workers’ settlement. Primary densification from [[lehner-mark|Mark Lehner]], *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|The Complete Pyramids]]* (1997), with earlier stubs from [[wilkinson-toby|Wilkinson]] 2010, [[kemp-barry-j|Kemp]] cross-links, Assmann, and Connah.

## Discovery and Excavation History

Exploration is continuous since medieval Arabic entry into the Great Pyramid and early modern European visitors (Greaves, Norden, Davison). Industrial-scale clearance and dynamite-era work under Vyse and Perring (1837) left lasting damage and documentation of chambers; Lepsius’s Prussian expedition and Mariette’s Antiquities Service institutionalized Egyptian archaeology; Petrie at Giza established measured survey against pyramidological claims (Piazzi Smyth). Twentieth-century concessions (Reisner, Junker, Hassan, et al.) excavated elite cemeteries and temples. Modern settlement and landscape archaeology is dominated by the **Giza Plateau Mapping Project** under [[lehner-mark|Lehner]] (Sphinx mapping 1979–83; settlement work from 1988; ongoing), with Egyptian campaigns including rediscovery of Khufu’s satellite pyramidion (Hawass) (attributed, Lehner 1997).

## Occupation Sequence

1. **Fourth Dynasty peak (three generations):** Khufu → (interruption: Djedefre at [[abu-roash]]) → Khafre → Menkaure on the Mokattam Formation limestone plate; SE corners of the three main pyramids nearly aligned on a NE–SW “Giza diagonal” (~43° E of true N) following the geological strike so bases stay approximately level (Khafre’s base ~10 m higher than Khufu’s) (Lehner 1997).
2. **Close of Giza royal program:** Khentkawes’s mastaba-like tomb; subsequent Fifth Dynasty royal focus shifts toward [[saqqara]] / [[abusir]] (Lehner 1997, attributed on dynasty-transfer framing).
3. **Later residual use:** New Kingdom Sphinx cult (*Horemakhet*), Khaemwaset and other restorations, Graeco-Roman repairs, medieval quarrying of casing, modern tourism.

## Significance

1. **Apogee of the giant stone pyramid** and formalization of the E–W [[pyramid-complex]] (mortuary temple, long causeway, valley temple, satellite and queens’ pyramids, orthogonal elite [[mastaba]] streets) (Lehner 1997).
2. **Type landscape for Old Kingdom labour and provisioning** — harbours, Wall of the Crow / Main Wadi settlement zone, and [[heit-el-ghurab|Heit el-Ghurab]] workers’ town (Lehner/GPMP; Kemp provider-state comparative use).
3. **Global reception icon** of ancient Egypt; standing target of alternative-history claims that Lehner’s archaeological synthesis is written against (1997 subtitle frame).

## Key Debates

- **Workforce size and status:** Herodotus’s 100,000 (re-read by Lehner as possible annual rotation) vs. “generally accepted” **20,000–30,000** peak infrastructure figure; permanent craftsmen vs. **peasant conscript crews** organized as crew → gang → *zaa*/phyle → division — all **attributed positions**, not wiki-voice (Lehner 1997).
- **Ramp and finishing systems:** straight-on, wrap-around, and lean-to ramp hypotheses; levering for the last few courses (Arnold, Stadelmann as reported by Lehner); NOVA experiment as experimental analogy.
- **Sphinx dating and identity:** Lehner’s mapping supports Fourth Dynasty association with Khafre’s complex; Dream Stela and later cult layers; rejects non-matching Orion-belt star-map claims (Bauval as reported and rejected, Lehner 1997).
- **Purpose beyond tomb:** solar/ascension ideology ([[pyramid-texts]] tradition later; *akhet* horizon reading of Sphinx-temple solstice alignment) vs. purely political display — keep as interpretive positions.

## Historiography

Entered the wiki as a Wilkinson 2010 narrative stub; densified from Assmann (symbolic *akhet* / *djet*) and Connah (African civilizational framing). **Lehner 1997** is the first ingested source whose author is the principal modern settlement archaeologist of the plateau — treat construction, labour, and complex layout claims as attributed synthesis as of 1997; GPMP monographs and Lehner & Hawass *Giza and the Pyramids* remain future primary digests.

## Lehner densification (1997)

*(Attributed throughout — [[lehner-mark]], *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|The Complete Pyramids]]*, esp. catalogue Giza chapters and “Living Pyramid.”)*

### Plateau layout and survey

Lehner describes the Mokattam Formation plate as ideal building ground; Maadi Formation south supplied stone and *tafla* for ramps/supports; nummulite banks and Member II/III hard–soft sequences are visible in the Sphinx body and head. Cardinality was principal: Khufu’s sides deviate under 5′ from true north. Cross-complex alignments (diagonal through subsidiary pyramids and temple fronts; Sphinx Temple south wall with Khafre’s south face) are within cord-survey tolerance over ~1 km of slope. Speculative cosmic motive (toward Heliopolis / Abydos) is flagged as speculation; Bauval’s Orion map is rejected for unmatched stars/pyramids.

### Khufu (Great Pyramid)

~2.3 million blocks; classic internal sequence (descending passage, subterranean chamber, ascending passage, Grand Gallery, Queen’s Chamber, King’s Chamber with relieving chambers); “air shafts”; boat pits and reconstructed solar boat; queens’ pyramids; Hetepheres deposit (as reported). Ancient name associations encode *akhet*/horizon ideology in textual contexts (Lehner). Stadelmann’s rate calculation (~340 blocks/day if ~23-year reign) frames labour modelling.

### Khafre and the Great Sphinx

Pyramid with surviving upper casing (tan patina; apex courses not flush — finishing stress). Mortuary and valley temples in hard stone; Sphinx carved from member beds in the Khafre quarry zone. Lehner’s 1979–83 photogrammetric/hand mapping (with Ulrich Kapp) produced drawings and later digital models (~2.5 million surface points). From the Sphinx Temple, summer-solstice sunset between the two largest pyramids forms a monumental *akhet* (horizon) hieroglyph — whether by design or chance is left interpretive. Multi-period veneer repairs from pharaonic through modern; Baraize 1926– clearance poorly published.

### Menkaure and the close of the Fourth Dynasty

Reduced pyramid mass with enlarged temple emphasis (size-ratio shift). Temple graffiti preserve gang names (“Friends of Menkaure,” “Drunkards of Menkaure”) and phyle labels — primary evidence for workforce organization. Settlement remains near the valley temple (including later “sacred slum” densification of the pyramid-town trajectory — Lehner 1997).

### Labour model (linked to Heit el-Ghurab)

Lehner’s synthesis: two crews of ~2,000 for core quarry/haul/set work; infrastructure and support may raise totals to **~20–25,000**; generally accepted **20–30,000** range; mass of unskilled labour as **peasant conscripts** in phyles, with skilled permanent craftsmen — **positions**, not established fact. See [[heit-el-ghurab]], [[bureaucracy]], [[the-state]].

## Wilkinson Part I densification (2010)

*(Attributed — [[wilkinson-toby]], *[[wilkinson-rise-fall-ancient-egypt-2010]]* Chs.3–5.)*

Fourth Dynasty pyramid program as expression of absolute royal power and command economy (taxation, corvée labour, elite provisioning). Giza marks the “cultural zenith” of the first cycle before OK withering under internal and external pressures (Wilkinson’s collapse framing — contested in specialist literature; keep attributed).

## Assmann densification (1996/2002)

*(Attributed — [[assmann-jan]], *[[assmann-mind-of-egypt-1996|The Mind of Egypt]].)*

Assmann reads the pyramids symbolically: the pyramid form is aniconic — it "does not stand for anything visible, it makes something visible," symbolizing *akhet* (horizon) — and Giza's pyramids stand for "the very foundation of the state itself," monumentalizing [[neheh-and-djet|djet]]-permanence in stone. He frames the Old Kingdom as the "Age of the Pyramids," arguing pyramid-building created (not merely occupied) the epoch, and floats the analogy of "ethnogenesis by means of building" — nationwide labor forging cultural unity. He also notes Prince Khaemwaset (son of Ramesses II, high priest of Ptah) restored Fourth Dynasty monuments at Giza and Saqqara — in Assmann's phrase "the first known antiquarian and conservationist in human history."

## Connah 2016 densification (*African Civilizations* Ch. 2)

*(Attributed — [[connah-graham]], *[[connah-african-civilizations-2016|African Civilizations]]* 3rd ed. 2016.)*

Connah treats the Giza pyramid group (Menkaure and Khafre illustrated) as archaeological evidence of **centralized authority and monumental labour mobilization** on the lower Nile, within a wider Egypt chapter arguing that Egyptian urbanism and state formation are **African** achievements continuous with continental trajectories of complexity — not a non-African exception. Pyramids sit beside planned towns ([[kahun]]), fortresses (Buhen), the short-lived capital [[amarna]], Deir el-Medina, and Red Sea ports (Berenike) as complementary faces of complexity. Under the book’s repeated factor template, Giza indexes elite control of productive Nile land, craft/organization, ideology, and population pressures rather than a diffusionist “civilization from outside Africa” frame (attributed).
