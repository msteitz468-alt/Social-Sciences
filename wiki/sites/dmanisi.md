## Discovery and Excavation History

Dmanisi, in the Republic of Georgia (44°N), has produced a human mandible, Oldowan-like
pebble tools and flakes, and a fossil fauna that is exclusively Eurasian in composition
— evidence Klein treats as making a migration out of Africa "indisputable" (Klein,
[[klein-human-career-1999]]). Excavation personnel and years are not specified in the
source consulted for this page; see [[unknown]].

## Occupation Sequence

Potassium-argon and 40Ar/39Ar dating fix an underlying basalt at 2.0–1.8 Ma.
[[paleomagnetic-dating]] combined with mammalian biostratigraphy constrains the mandible
itself to one of two candidate windows: 1.95–1.77 Ma or 1.19–0.99 Ma (as of Klein 1999).
The mandible's morphology — a rounded symphysis and a molar-size reduction pattern —
more closely resembles later Homo (post-1 Ma) than [[homo-erectus]]'s African
counterpart H. ergaster, which Klein reads as favoring the younger age estimate (~1 Ma)
despite the older basalt date; this reading is Klein's own interpretive position, not a
settled fact.

## Significance

Dmanisi is the earliest well-attested evidence of hominins outside Africa in the source
consulted, documenting an "Out of Africa 1" dispersal associated with early [[homo-erectus]]
or a closely related early Homo population, well before the much later dispersal of
anatomically modern humans treated in the [[modern-human-origins-debate]]. Klein is
explicit, however, that Dmanisi does not by itself establish an early, deep penetration
into the wider Eurasian landmass: "Dmanisi does not show that people had penetrated the
farther reaches of Eurasia long before 1 my ago" (Klein 1999).

## Key Debates

The central dispute recorded in the source is chronological rather than behavioral:
whether the older basalt-based age (~1.95–1.77 Ma) or the younger mandible-morphology-
based age (~1.19–0.99 Ma) better dates the hominin occupation, a live tension between
independent dating methods (radiometric vs. biostratigraphic/morphological) that Klein
resolves provisionally in favor of the younger estimate.
