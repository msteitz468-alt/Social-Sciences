# Dhar Tichitt

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Dhar Tichitt** (Mauritania) is a classic escarpment settlement complex in the West African early food-production record. [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] includes it in the Sahara/West African early farmers discussion as a major archaeological anchor for sedentary/agro-pastoral communities south of the desert (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Occupation Sequence

Phillipson’s synthesis places Tichitt among mid-to-late Holocene food-producing communities of the southwestern Sahara margins, with stone architecture and pottery sequences that prefigure later complex societies of the West African savanna (as of Phillipson 2005). Fine-grained phase chronology belongs in specialist reports.

## Significance

- Early West African sedentism/food production outside the Nile corridor.
- Bridge between Saharan Holocene sequences and later sudanic kingdoms treated in Ch. 8 ([[jenne-jeno]], ancient Ghana, etc.).
- Complements [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]]’s urbanism focus with deeper food-production time-depth from Phillipson.
