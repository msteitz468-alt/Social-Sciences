# Dhar Tichitt

## Discovery and Excavation History

**Dhar Tichitt** (Mauritania) is a classic escarpment settlement complex in the West African early food-production record. [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] includes it in the Sahara/West African early farmers discussion as a major archaeological anchor for sedentary/agro-pastoral communities south of the desert (as of Phillipson 2005).

## Occupation Sequence

Phillipson’s synthesis places Tichitt among mid-to-late Holocene food-producing communities of the southwestern Sahara margins, with stone architecture and pottery sequences that prefigure later complex societies of the West African savanna (as of Phillipson 2005). Fine-grained phase chronology belongs in specialist reports.

## Significance

- Early West African sedentism/food production outside the Nile corridor.
- Bridge between Saharan Holocene sequences and later sudanic kingdoms treated in Ch. 8 ([[jenne-jeno]], ancient Ghana, etc.).
- Complements [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]]’s urbanism focus with deeper food-production time-depth from Phillipson.

## Key Debates

Crop packages, climate forcing, and continuity into historic polities are interpretation-heavy — keep attributed.

### Handbook densification (*Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology*, 2013)

*(Attributed — Peter Breunig, Ch. 38, and Joanna Casey, Ch. 41, in *[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013|Mitchell & Lane 2013]]*; as of ~2012.)*

- **Tichitt–Oualata–Néma complex:** Southeastern Mauritania escarpment sites of second–early/mid first millennium BC **agropastoral** economy; no known local precursors — economy may have arrived by migration as a package (Breunig). Plateau sites include large stone house/farm structures up to **~90 ha** — among the **oldest villages of West Africa**, implying social organization different from small hunter-gatherer groups.
- **Pearl millet:** Cultivated from the sequence beginning c. **1800 BC** (not a local transition from gathering alone). Complements Tilemsi Valley early pearl-millet evidence further east (third millennium BC — Breunig).
- **Inland Niger Delta connection:** Mid-second millennium BC agropastoralists migrated from the north (including Tichitt region: Ndondi–Tossokel, Windé Koroji), interacting with Kobadi fishermen who lacked domestic animals/crops — different origins and subsistence economies side by side (Breunig).
- **Sahel three-phase frame:** (1) second-millennium BC massive pastoral immigration + settled food-producing cores; (2) first-millennium BC crisis; (3) renewed flowering with new crops, settlement forms, craft specialization, iron smelting — basis for first states later (Breunig). Tichitt anchors Phase 1 for the western Sahel.
- **Stone to Metal Age (Casey via de Maret):** Tichitt/Néma sequences also appear in later metallurgy-transition discussions (e.g. MacDonald et al. 2009 on Dhar Néma).

**Identity caution:** Escarpment archaeological complex ≠ ethnonym ≠ language family. Package-migration interpretation is **attributed**, not proven demic identity of builders.
