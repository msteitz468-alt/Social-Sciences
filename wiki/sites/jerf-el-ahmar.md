# Jerf el Ahmar

Mureybetian PPNA settlement on the Syrian middle Euphrates upstream of [[mureybet|Mureybet]]. For [[cauvin-jacques|Cauvin]] (*[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Birth of the Gods]]* Postscript, as of ~1998/2000), the best illustration of the **circular → rectangular** architectural transition and of cooperative settlement planning, with evidence for **pre-domestic agriculture** (barley) and a possible subterranean social-religious nodal house (Stordeur).

## Discovery and Excavation History

Excavations continued through the late 1990s with publications Cauvin integrates in the English Postscript. Quality: modern research excavation with botanical program (Willcox weed-suite methods referenced). Site later affected by Euphrates dam landscapes like peers.

## Occupation Sequence

*(Attributed — Cauvin 2000 Postscript after Stordeur.)* Same stratum can show simple round cells (sometimes agglutinated), large internally divided round houses (cf. Mureybet House XLVII), rectangular houses with rounded corners, and fully rectangular buildings (some subdivided, some with antae/courtyards). Rear walls of slope houses bonded into terrace construction → preconceived multi-house collective building. Large wholly subterranean divided round house interpreted as storage plus nodal social/perhaps religious centre for surrounding surface houses. Hunting profile differs from contemporary Mureybet (more equid/gazelle; less aurochs emphasis) — Cauvin notes a slight adjustment to prior Mureybetian subsistence generalisations.

## Significance

Supports Cauvin’s claims that (1) architectural and social complexity intensify **before** full morphological domestication packages, (2) PPNA is a period of diverse local experiments, and (3) ideology-and-cooperation are visible archaeologically in the corridor. Cauvin also links small stone-tablet “pictograms” to deep roots of later urban criteria (collective works; authority). Paired with [[gobekli-tepe|Göbekli Tepe]] in later literature as ideology-before-economy evidence (including Mithen’s uptake).

## Key Debates

Pre-domestic vs domestic botanical status; function of subterranean building; how far “pictograms” prefigure writing (Cauvin’s long-range suggestion — position only).
