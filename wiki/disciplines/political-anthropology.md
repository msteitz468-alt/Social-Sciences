# Political Anthropology

## Scope

[[political-anthropology|Political anthropology]] is the anthropological study of political systems, authority, law, conflict, and the state — and of **order without the state** — comparatively and ethnographically. In its British mid-century charter form, it treated political organization as an abstracted aspect of [[social-structure|social structure]], comparable across cultures once cultural idiom is stripped to functional terms. Adjacent to [[political-sociology]] (which typically starts from modern states, parties, and classes), political anthropology’s classical archive is non-Western and especially African fieldwork, later expanded to peasants, postcolonial states, social movements, and global governance.

## Historical Development

- **Precursors:** Travellers’ accounts; legal historians (Maine, Vinogradoff); evolutionary origin-of-the-state theories; Lowie’s *Origin of the State* (editors of APS note Lowie as partial exception to their stricture on anthropologists).
- **Fieldwork revolution charter (1940):** *[[fortes-evans-pritchard-african-political-systems-1940|African Political Systems]]* — Group A/B typology, Radcliffe-Brown force definition, mystical values of office, eight African cases. See [[fortes-evans-pritchard-african-political-systems-study]].
- **Postwar expansion:** Dense monographs (Nuer trilogy, Tallensi kinship volumes, Gluckman and Manchester process/conflict school, Barth on pathans, etc.). **Primary-ingested:** [[leach-edmund|Leach]]'s *[[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-1954|Political Systems of Highland Burma]]* (1954) — structure as model; [[gumsa-gumlao-oscillation]]; highland SE Asia type-case ([[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-study]]). **US neo-evolutionary political typology (primary-ingested):** [[service-elman-r|Service]]'s *[[service-primitive-social-organization-1971|Primitive Social Organization]]* (1962/1971) — [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]; [[chiefdom]]; pan-tribal [[sodality|sodalities]] (Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]); and [[fried-morton-h|Fried]]'s *[[fried-evolution-of-political-society-1967|Evolution of Political Society]]* (1967) — [[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]]; [[egalitarian-society]] / [[rank-society]] / [[stratified-society]]; [[pristine-state]] / [[secondary-state]] (Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]]; debate [[service-fried-political-typology-debate]]).
- **Critical turn onward:** Critique of segmentary models; Marxist and dependency approaches to African states; postcolonial critiques of anthropology’s colonial production; feminist political anthropology; resistance and “weapons of the weak”; anarchist anthropology (Clastres and after). Collection thin after the 1940 charter.
- **Anthropology of development / bureaucratic state (1990):** [[ferguson-james|Ferguson]] *[[ferguson-anti-politics-machine-1990|The Anti-Politics Machine]]* — Lesotho Thaba-Tseka project; [[anti-politics-machine]]; [[depoliticization]]; decentralization-as-centralization; [[instrument-effects]] of planned intervention ([[ferguson-anti-politics-machine-study]]).
- **Peasantry and political economy (mid-1960s):** [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] *[[wolf-peasants-1966|Peasants]]* (1966) — Foundations series primer defining peasantry by surplus transfer under domain; coalitions and movements as political organization of cultivators (see [[peasant]], [[types-of-domain]], [[peasant-movements]]).
- **Amazonian chronic warfare ethnography (1968):** [[chagnon-napoleon|Chagnon]] *[[chagnon-yanomamo-fierce-people-1968|Yanomamö: The Fierce People]]* — graded violence scale, alliance ladder (trade→feast→women), village fission; society [[yanomamo]]; study [[chagnon-yanomamo-fierce-people-study]]; ethics/representation afterlife [[chagnon-tierney-yanomami-ethics-debate]].

## Methods

[[comparative-method]] (type comparison); [[participant-observation]] and political case ethnography; genealogical and lineage mapping; analysis of feud, court, and ritual; colonial archival cross-check (variable by author).

## Relations to Adjacent Disciplines

- **[[political-sociology]]:** Shared objects (state, authority, conflict); different archives and scales; APS explicitly claims “comparative politics.”
- **[[anthropology]] parent:** Subfield of social/cultural anthropology; classical British institutionalization.
- **History of Africa / colonial history:** Necessary corrective to synchronic SF; not substituted by APS alone.
- **Political philosophy:** APS editors reject normative and conjectural-historical philosophy as scientific method.

## Current State

*Friction* (Tsing 2005) densifies multi-locale environmental and *adat* politics under/after the New Order via [[tsing-friction-study]]. *Mushroom* (Tsing 2015) densifies refugee/veteran freedom politics, Forest Service regulation, and supply-chain state transformations via [[tsing-mushroom-study]] / [[open-ticket-oregon]].


Active fronts (not yet collection-covered): postcolonial state ethnography; bureaucracy and documents; violence and vigilantism; transnational governance; indigenous sovereignty movements; reanalysis of classic segmentary cases with new archives.

## Reflexivity

Political anthropology’s charter texts were produced under colonial governments for audiences that included administrators (Indirect Rule). That does not automatically falsify ethnographic content, but it shapes research questions (order, chiefship, pacification), access, and the “tribe” as unit. The subfield’s later self-critique of colonial entanglement is part of its content. WEIRD sampling is less the issue than **colonial African sampling** and under-representation of other world regions in the early canon.

**2026-07-09:** [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] *[[wolf-europe-people-without-history-1982|EPWH]]* (1982) — global political economy of social labor; tributary/kin-ordered modes; slave-trade and fur-trade reorganizations of polities; mode-vs-market dispute with [[world-systems-analysis]].
