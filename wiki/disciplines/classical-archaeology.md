# Classical Archaeology

Subfield of [[archaeology]] devoted to the material remains of Greek, Roman, and related Mediterranean societies, historically closely tied to classical philology, art history, and elite education. In this vault's framing it is a **text-aided** archaeology that Trigger treats as among the "first archaeologies," parallel to Egyptology and Assyriology.

## Scope

Art, architecture, inscriptions, cities, and everyday material culture of the classical Mediterranean and its extensions; chronological and regional specialisms within that world. Boundary with prehistoric archaeology of the same regions is historically sharp (elite classical curriculum vs museum prehistory) and later contested (prehistoric Aegean; processual/classical rapprochement).

## Historical Development

*(Attributed to [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]] 2006, ch. 2 and later synthetic chapters.)*

- **Renaissance antiquarianism** recovers classical monuments and texts as models.
- **Eighteenth-century** art-historical and Grand Tour formations (Winckelmann stream) institutionalize classical archaeology as historical archaeology of elite culture — epigraphy, art, architecture.
- **Nineteenth–twentieth centuries:** large excavations (Olympia, Delos, Roman provinces); tension between art-museum connoisseurship and stratigraphic field method ([[wheeler-mortimer|Wheeler]] tradition partly bridges).
- **Late twentieth century:** Trigger reports rapprochement with prehistoric/processual and postprocessual agendas (social archaeology of the classical world; landscape; identity) as part of "theoretical convergence" and historical–prehistoric dialogue (*HAT* ch. 9).

## Theoretical Landscape

Long generically culture-historical (Trigger: classical archaeology was culture-historical *avant la lettre* in focusing named peoples and elite culture sequences). Later open to [[processual-archaeology|processual]], [[post-processual-archaeology|postprocessual]], and heritage frameworks. Relation to [[antiquarianism]] is ancestral.

## Methods

Epigraphy; art-historical attribution; architectural recording; increasingly full excavation standards and scientific dating shared with other archaeologies.

## Current State

Still institutionally distinct in many universities; collection coverage in this vault is **thin** (secondary via Trigger only).

## Reflexivity

Classical archaeology's elite and colonial collecting histories (marbles, Grand Tour, museum acquisitions) are standing ethical content. Nationalist uses of classical pasts (Greece, Italy, imperial Rome as model) parallel [[nationalism-and-archaeology]] elsewhere.
