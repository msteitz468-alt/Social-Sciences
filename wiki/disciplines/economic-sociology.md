# Economic Sociology

## Scope

**Economic sociology** studies economic action, markets, money, firms, and inequality as social phenomena — shaped by networks, culture, institutions, and power — rather than as a domain fully explained by formal economic models alone. This page is seeded by the ingest of [[zelizer-viviana|Zelizer]]'s *[[zelizer-social-meaning-of-money-1994|The Social Meaning of Money]]*; the subfield's full map (markets, firms, finance, development) remains thin until further sources are ingested.

Parent: [[sociology]].

## Historical Development

| Era | Markers (partial; collection still thin) |
|---|---|
| Classical / founding | Marx, Weber, Simmel on economy and money; not yet a named subfield |
| Mid-century | [[polanyi-karl|Polanyi]] [[embeddedness]] and [[substantivism]] |
| New economic sociology (1980s–) | Granovetter network embeddedness; White, Burt, Fligstein, Zelizer cultural/relational programs |
| Field-theoretic markets (1990s–2012) | Fligstein markets-as-fields; *[[fligstein-mcadam-theory-of-fields-2012|A Theory of Fields]]* (with McAdam) generalizes to [[strategic-action-fields-theory]] |

## Theoretical Landscape

- **[[embeddedness]]** — Polanyi institutional; Granovetter network; Fligstein–McAdam **field-to-field** embedding.
- **[[multiple-monies-approach]]** — Zelizer; [[multiple-monies]], [[earmarking]].
- Market as institution: [[market]] (Weberian scaffold already present).
- **[[strategic-action-fields-theory]]** — markets, industries, and finance as [[strategic-action-field|SAFs]] (mortgage/MBS illustration in Fligstein & McAdam 2012 Ch. 5); [[social-skill]]; [[internal-governance-unit|IGUs]].
- Related: [[institutional-economics]], [[social-exchange-theory]], [[gift-exchange]], [[social-field]] (Bourdieu contrast).
- **[[piketty-capital-dynamics]]** — historical wealth/income inequality; β, r > g, patrimonial capitalism (Piketty 2013/2014; political-economy bridge).

## Methods

Historical and archival analysis of economic culture (Zelizer 1994); network analysis of economic action (Granovetter program — [[granovetter-getting-a-job-study]], [[granovetter-strength-of-weak-ties-study]], [[granovetter-embeddedness-1985]], [[new-economic-sociology]]); ethnography of markets and firms (not yet dense in this collection); [[historical-wealth-accounts]] and fiscal [[top-income-shares]] for long-run distribution (Piketty program — see [[piketty-capital-twenty-first-century-study]]).

## Relations to Adjacent Disciplines

Boundary disputes with neoclassical and institutional economics over the autonomy of "the economic"; with cultural anthropology over gift/commodity; with social psychology over economic decision-making.

## Current State

Active; money, finance, platforms, and intimate economies are live fronts. Collection coverage remains **weak** outside selected monographs.

## Reflexivity

The "new" economic sociology was largely U.S.- and Europe-centered in its classic statements; Zelizer's 1994 evidence is U.S. historical. WEIRD and national-case selection bias apply until comparative cases are ingested.
