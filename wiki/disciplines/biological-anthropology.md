# Biological Anthropology

The evolutionary and biological wing of [[anthropology]] — human origins, primate comparative context, human biological variation, and (increasingly) archaeogenetics. Also called physical anthropology in older usage. Parent overview: [[anthropology]].

## Scope

Objects: hominin fossils and archaeology of early behavior; living nonhuman primates as comparative models; human growth, nutrition, demography, and disease; genetic variation and population history. Distinct from cultural anthropology's primary focus on meanings and social forms, though the boundary is porous (forager studies, medical anthropology, evolutionary approaches to culture).

## Historical Development

- **Founding / classical eras:** Evolutionary inclusion of man in *[[darwin-descent-of-man-1871|Darwin, Descent of Man]]* (1871) — homology/embryology/rudiments, mental and [[moral-sense|moral]] continuity, [[sexual-selection]], races as sub-species ([[monogenism]]) — founding text for evolutionary [[biological-anthropology|human biology]], entangled with Victorian race typology the subfield later had to renounce (see [[race-and-culture-debate]]). Parallel stream: "physical anthropology" as race typology and skeletal measurement.
- **Postwar expansion:** functional anatomy, Washburn's "new physical anthropology," primatology, and the [[hunting-hypothesis|Man-the-Hunter]] evolutionary narrative (*[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]* 1968 as cultural–biological meeting ground).
- **Critical turn–contemporary:** molecular methods, aDNA revolution, behavioral ecology, costly critiques of sociobiology and of androcentric origin stories ([[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]]). Dietary and expensive-tissue models ([[expensive-tissue-hypothesis]]; [[cooking-hypothesis]]) reframe what funded encephalization.

## Theoretical Landscape

Founding primary ingest for evolutionary inclusion of man: Darwin's *[[darwin-descent-of-man-1871|Descent of Man]]* (1871) — see [[darwin-charles]], [[natural-selection]], [[sexual-selection]], [[moral-sense]]. Later program text: Wrangham's *[[wrangham-catching-fire-2009|Catching Fire]]* ([[cooking-hypothesis]]; debate [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]]). Adjacent forager streams: [[hunting-hypothesis]], [[hunter-gatherer]], [[original-affluent-society]], [[sexual-division-of-labor]]. Broader cultural-evolution pages ([[cultural-ecology]], [[social-evolutionism]]) are mostly cultural-anthropology lineages.

## Methods

Comparative anatomy; excavation and dating of fossil/archaeological contexts; field primatology; human biology surveys; genetics and aDNA (now primary-ingested via [[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich 2018]] — see the [[archaeogenetics]] subfield page and [[ancient-dna]], [[admixture-statistics]] methods). Wrangham's methodological wager in *Catching Fire*: when archaeological fire traces are faint, **anatomical adaptation** to cooked diet can date cooking — a contested method commitment (see debate).

## Current State

Active fronts (not yet covered by primary sources here): aDNA and population history; niche construction; cooperative breeding; costly signaling and food sharing; fire and cooking timing; WEIRD limits in evolutionary psychology. Collection has only begun.

## Reflexivity

Biological anthropology inherits the colonial and racializing history of physical anthropology; postwar reform did not erase the need for reflexive method. Evolutionary origin stories (Man-the-Hunter; cooking-and-patriarchy) encode gender and species politics — record as content. aDNA and "population" language risk reifying races if not carefully framed (Evidence-Class Framework: genes ≠ peoples ≠ cultures).

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]], [[stringer-chris]]; as of Stringer 2012.*

**Modern human origins as a major research focus.** Stringer states modern human origins blossomed over ~40 years as a central paleoanthropological problem: partial answers exist to “when” and “where,” but still little on the “whys”; future work needs dated multi-proxy fossil, archaeological, and environmental records beyond western Europe and parts of Africa. The book is a first-person research-program history of [[recent-african-origin]] from minority (~1984) to mainstream, then revision under autosomal Neanderthal/Denisovan genomes toward predominantly — but **not purely** — recent African origin ([[assimilation-model]] adjacency; [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

**What genetics does and does not say about living diversity (as of Stringer 2012).** Old racial categories (“Negroid,” “Caucasoid,” “Australoid,” “Mongoloid”) largely abandoned as not meaningful descriptors of biological variation levels; individuals mixed across gene histories; African populations may contain as much genetic variation as the rest of the world combined. Skin pigmentation: original African ancestral skin darkly pigmented; selection for lighter skins at low UV; favored European light-skin mutations young (one important change ~11 ka on some estimates) and partly different from north Asian light-skin mutations. Blue-eye European variant <20 ka. Drift, founder effect, and “surfing” on expanding waves can produce distinctive non-African gene frequencies without selection. On regional IQ/brain-quality claims: Stringer declines deep treatment but argues any cognitive differences evolved in last 50 ka should show high within-Africa variation rather than uniformly low African IQ as some studies report; IQ tests measure limited aspects; environment contributes strongly. mtDNA commercial ancestry testing controversial (small fraction of ancestry; comparative sampling uneven, Africa poorly sampled). Study of >1,000 markers in 113 African populations → fourteen groups closely matching cultural/language affiliations; central African “pygmies,” Sandawe, Hadza, Khoisan shared ancestors ~40 ka — **association only; genes ≠ languages** (four-way non-identity).

**Reflexivity note.** Stringer’s own multivariate 1970s skull work (no Neanderthal ancestry for Cro-Magnons; early moderns cluster with modern counterparts) and cephalic-index history (racist misuse as “primitiveness”) are recorded as production context for the RAO program.
