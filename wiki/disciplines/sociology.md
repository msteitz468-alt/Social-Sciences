# Sociology

## Scope

As named and framed by [[comte-auguste|Auguste Comte]] in the *Cours de philosophie positive* (English: [[comte-positive-philosophy-martineau-1853|Martineau, *Positive Philosophy*]]), **sociology** (also **[[social-physics]]**) is the science of social phenomena — the last and most complex of the fundamental sciences. Comte treated it as both the completion of positive philosophy and the intellectual basis for reorganizing modern society after the revolutionary crisis.

Later classical redefinitions now in the collection: [[spencer-herbert|Spencer]]'s [[social-evolutionism|evolutionary-comparative sociology]] (*[[spencer-principles-of-sociology-andreski-1969|Principles of Sociology]]*); [[tocqueville-alexis-de|Tocqueville]]'s comparative political sociology of the democratic social state; [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]'s professionalizing definition via [[social-fact|social facts]] and limited-problem monographs (*[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]*); [[veblen-thorstein|Veblen]]'s institutional / economic sociology of the [[leisure-class]]; [[simmel-georg|Simmel]]'s [[formal-sociology|formal sociology]] of the forms of [[sociation]] and his philosophical sociology of the [[money-economy]] (*[[simmel-philosophy-of-money-1900|The Philosophy of Money]]*); [[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]]'s [[paretian-sociology|logico-experimental sociology]] of residues, non-logical conduct, and elite circulation (*[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]*); [[du-bois-w-e-b|Du Bois]]'s sociology of race and the color-line (*[[du-bois-souls-of-black-folk-1903|The Souls of Black Folk]]*); [[weber-max|Weber]]'s historical sociology of [[rationalization]] and the [[protestant-ethic-thesis]] (*[[weber-protestant-ethic-1905|The Protestant Ethic]]*); [[mead-george-herbert|Mead]]'s [[social-psychology|sociological social psychology]] (*[[mead-mind-self-society-1934|Mind, Self, and Society]]*). These extend and contest the Comtean founding frame rather than simply continuing it.

## Historical Development

### Founding Era (to c. 1890)

Comte argued (Introduction ch. I) that celestial and terrestrial physics and organic physics had entered the positive stage, while social subjects were still handled by theological and metaphysical methods (divine right, sovereignty of the people, etc.). He set establishing social physics as the principal aim of the *Positive Philosophy*. Book VI develops:

- the necessity of the science and dual problem of **Order and Progress** (ch. I–II);
- method and place in the encyclopedic hierarchy (ch. III–IV);
- [[social-statics]] and [[social-dynamics]] (ch. V–VI);
- a staged historical interpretation of Europe's past: [[theological-military-system]] (fetichism → polytheism → monotheism) → metaphysical-critical period → revolutionary crisis → positive reorganization (ch. VII–XV).

The name "sociology" appears in Comte's usage as the preferred designation for social physics (Martineau/Comte Book VI). Precursors (Montesquieu, Condorcet, political arithmetic, etc.) are treated by Comte as incomplete anticipations — Book VI ch. II.

[[martineau-harriet|Harriet Martineau]] (*[[martineau-how-to-observe-1838|How to Observe Morals and Manners]]*, 1838) contributes a distinct English founding-era **method** program: trained [[social-observation]] of morals and manners, [[things-before-discourse]], philosophical/moral/mechanical requisites, and departmental checklists — before she later condensed Comte for English readers (1853). Precursor-methodologist and transmitter, not a Comtean system-builder in the 1838 work.

[[spencer-herbert|Spencer]] (*[[spencer-principles-of-sociology-andreski-1969|Principles of Sociology]]*, 1876–1896) contributes the major English founding-era **system**: society as [[social-organism]]; evolution as [[differentiation-and-integration]]; growth by [[social-compounding]]; [[sustaining-distributing-regulating-systems|sustaining / distributing / regulating]] architecture; polar [[militant-type-of-society|militant]] vs [[industrial-type-of-society|industrial]] types; world-wide [[comparative-method|comparative]] reconstruction of political, ecclesiastical ([[priesthood]]), and industrial institutions. Master key differs from Comte’s [[law-of-three-stages]] while sharing the demand for a science of society (see [[social-evolutionism]]; Andreski 1969 on Comte–Spencer division of labour).

Tocqueville (ingested separately) contributes a different founding-era program: comparative political sociology of democratic social state, not Comtean encyclopedic hierarchy.

[[marx-karl|Marx]] and [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] contribute a further, antagonistic founding-era program. The [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] (1848) founds the discipline's **conflict tradition**: where Comte and (later) Durkheim center order, integration, and consensus, [[historical-materialism|historical materialism]] centers [[class-struggle|class struggle]] and the [[mode-of-production|mode of production]] as the motor of social change. *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte]]* (1852; study [[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-study]]) supplies the tradition’s classic political-sociological case — [[class-fraction|class fractions]], [[bonapartism]], [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]], [[peasantry-as-class|peasantry]], [[men-make-their-own-history|structure/agency]]. *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867) supplies the scientific critique of political economy — [[commodity]], [[labor-theory-of-value]], [[surplus-value]], [[labor-power]], [[industrial-reserve-army]], [[primitive-accumulation]] — analyzed as [[marx-capital-volume-1-study]]. Engels’s *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State]]* (1884/1891) extends the program into kinship, the [[gens]], [[private-property]], and [[state-formation]] via Morgan — dual production thesis; [[monogamy]] and “first class oppression”; state as class organ with [[withering-away-of-the-state]] horizon. Claims remain attributed throughout; unilinear kinship sequence contested ([[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]); *Capital* II–III and secondary reception not yet ingested.

[[sumner-william-graham|William Graham Sumner]] (Yale, professor from 1872) founds the
**American academic sociology of custom**, teaching one of the first U.S. courses under the
name "sociology" from [[spencer-herbert|Spencer]]'s *The Study of Sociology*. His late
masterwork *[[sumner-folkways-1906|Folkways]]* (1906) supplies a micro-foundational
counterpart to Spencer's macro-evolutionism: society as the sediment of unconscious custom.
Its coinages — [[folkways]], [[mores]] (revived as English technical vocabulary),
[[ethnocentrism]], [[in-group-out-group|in-group/out-group]], [[antagonistic-cooperation]],
[[societal-selection]] — became permanent sociological equipment, outlasting the
[[social-darwinism|social Darwinism]] and evolutionary racial hierarchy in which Sumner
embedded them. His thesis that custom, not reason or legislation, is the fundamental
societal force ("the mores can make anything right") makes him the American founding-era
theorist of the anti-voluntarist intuition also reached, differently, by
[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]'s [[social-fact|social fact]].

### Classical Era (c. 1890–1920)

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] professionalized academic sociology in France and founded **[[social-realism|social realism]]** — the science of [[social-fact|social facts]] as realities *sui generis*. His doctoral thesis *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|The Division of Labor in Society]]* (1893) recast the division of labor as the moral basis of modern solidarity, tracing the shift from [[mechanical-solidarity|mechanical]] to [[organic-solidarity|organic]] solidarity through law as an external index ([[repressive-law|repressive]] vs. [[restitutive-law|restitutive]]), grounding it in [[dynamic-density|dynamic density]], distinguishing a social fact's *function* from its *cause*, and diagnosing [[anomic-division-of-labor|anomic]] and [[forced-division-of-labor|forced]] pathologies. *[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|The Rules of Sociological Method]]* (1895; 2nd ed. 1901) codifies the program: dual definition of the social fact (exteriority + constraint, including [[social-morphology|morphology]]); [[treat-social-facts-as-things]]; [[normal-and-pathological|normal/pathological]] relative to [[social-types|social types]]; explain social facts by prior social facts via the inner social environment; demonstrate by [[comparative-method|concomitant variations]] (see [[hubs/theory/social-realism|Theory Hub]]). The limited-problem demonstration *[[durkheim-suicide-study|Suicide]]* (1897) applies the rules: rates as *sui generis* facts; [[moral-statistics]]; typology of [[suicide]] by [[social-integration]] and [[social-regulation]]; [[anomie]]; reform via [[occupational-group|occupational groups]]. Against [[spencer-herbert|Spencer]] he argued order rests on [[non-contractual-elements-of-contract|non-contractual social conditions]], not contract ([[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]). He kept the demand for social science as science (relation to [[positivism]]) while rejecting encyclopedic system-building and much of Comtean philosophy of history. Standing method dispute: [[methodological-individualism-debate]].

[[veblen-thorstein|Veblen]] (*[[veblen-leisure-class-1899|Theory of the Leisure Class]]*, 1899) founded a strand of American [[institutional-economics|institutional economics]] / economic sociology around [[conspicuous-consumption]], the [[leisure-class]], and related concepts — speculative-comparative theory, not moral-statistical monography.

[[simmel-georg|Simmel]] (*[[simmel-philosophy-of-money-1900|The Philosophy of Money]]*, 1900) founded **[[formal-sociology|formal sociology]]** — society as [[sociation]] (reciprocal interaction), sociology as the study of the recurrent *forms* of association abstracted from their contents — and, in the same work, a philosophical sociology of the [[money-economy|money economy]] and modern culture ([[value]], [[exchange]], [[objective-culture]], the [[tragedy-of-culture|tragedy of culture]], the [[blase-attitude|blasé attitude]]). Speculative social philosophy rather than empirical study (Durkheim reviewed it, 1901, as "social philosophy" bordering on "illegitimate speculation").

[[pareto-vilfredo|Pareto]] (*[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|Trattato di Sociologia generale / The Mind and Society]]*, 1916/1923; Eng. 1935) founded a third classical grand program: **[[paretian-sociology|logico-experimental general sociology]]** of [[non-logical-conduct]], [[residues]] and [[derivations]], [[paretian-social-equilibrium|social equilibrium]], and the [[circulation-of-elites]] (including [[lions-and-foxes]], [[demagogic-plutocracy]], [[speculators-and-rentiers]]). He rejected dogmatic Comtean/Spencerian “religions” of sociology while keeping the demand for experimental science of society ([[logico-experimental-method]]; [[truth-utility-distinction]]).

[[du-bois-w-e-b|W. E. B. Du Bois]] (*[[du-bois-souls-of-black-folk-1903|The Souls of Black Folk]]*, 1903; *[[du-bois-essays|Essays]]*, 1897–c. 1906 pieces) founded a classical American sociology of race from within the color-line: [[double-consciousness]], [[the-veil]], [[the-color-line]] as the century’s problem; [[conservation-of-races]] (1897 Academy race ideal); full [[talented-tenth]] education program; mixed-method diagnosis (Freedmen’s Bureau history, Black Belt monography, Atlanta Conference statistics, [[black-urban-north|Black North]] urban observation, Southern peonage/group-economy political economy); the [[black-church]] as social centre and ethics of slavery/emancipation; [[sorrow-songs]] as national cultural gift; and the [[washington-du-bois-debate|Washington–Du Bois]] leadership fight. Genre-hybrid (essay, history, observation, fiction) rather than pure moral-statistical monography — *Souls* analyzed as [[du-bois-souls-study]].

[[mead-george-herbert|George Herbert Mead]] (*[[mead-mind-self-society-1934|Mind, Self, and Society]]*, lectures c. 1900–1930, published posthumously 1934) founds the **sociological social psychology** strand at the University of Chicago — the [[social-psychology|subfield]] treating [[the-social-self|mind and self]] as products of the social process of communication ([[gesture|gesture]], [[significant-symbol|significant symbol]], [[taking-the-role-of-the-other|role-taking]], the [[generalized-other|generalized other]], the [[the-i-and-the-me|"I"/"me"]]). Mead's [[pragmatism|pragmatist]] [[social-behaviorism|social behaviorism]] is the intellectual source of [[symbolic-interactionism|symbolic interactionism]] (named by his student Herbert Blumer, 1937). This is the wiki's first Chicago-tradition and first micro-interactionist source; the book is a posthumous editorial reconstruction (see [[mind-self-society-authorship-debate]]).

[[thomas-william-isaac|W. I. Thomas]] and [[znaniecki-florian|Florian Znaniecki]] (*[[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-1918|The Polish Peasant in Europe and America]]*, Vol I 1918; full work 1918–20) supply the wiki's first **Chicago empirical monograph**: [[attitude-and-value|attitude–value]] social psychology, [[definition-of-the-situation]], [[four-wishes]], [[personal-documents-method|personal documents]] (family letter series), and the [[primary-group]] organization of the [[polish-peasants|Polish peasant]] under migration. Analyzed as [[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-study]] (Studies Hub: [[hubs/studies/thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-study]]). Founds the documentary side of the [[chicago-school|Chicago School]]; Vols II–V (life-record, reorganization, American disorganization) not yet in the collection.

[[weber-max|Max Weber]] (*[[weber-protestant-ethic-1905|The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism]]*, 1904–5/1920) founds a classical **historical and economic sociology of [[rationalization]]** and the [[protestant-ethic-thesis|Protestant-ethic thesis]]: elective affinity between [[inner-worldly-asceticism|ascetic Protestant ethics]] and the [[spirit-of-capitalism|spirit of modern capitalism]], mediated by the [[calling-beruf|calling (*Beruf*)]]; [[ideal-type]] method; closing [[iron-cage]] diagnosis. Mixed historical-documentary study analyzed as [[weber-protestant-ethic-study]]; standing controversy on [[protestant-ethic-debate]]. Weber also founds this wiki's **[[political-sociology|political sociology]]** strand through the lecture *[[weber-politics-as-a-vocation-1919|Politics as a Vocation]]* (1919): [[the-state|the state]] defined by its [[monopoly-of-violence|monopoly of legitimate violence]]; the three types of [[legitimate-domination|legitimate domination]] ([[charismatic-authority|charismatic]] included); [[bureaucracy|expert officialdom]]; [[plebiscitary-leader-democracy|plebiscitary leader-democracy]]; and the political ethics of [[ethic-of-responsibility|responsibility]] vs. [[ethic-of-ultimate-ends|ultimate ends]]. The late systematic statement is *[[weber-economy-society-1921|Economy and Society]]* Part I (1921 instalment; Tribe 2019): [[interpretive-sociology|sociology as science of social action]] ([[verstehen]], four [[types-of-social-action]]); economic sociology ([[economic-action]], [[formal-and-substantive-rationality]], [[market]]); full [[types-of-legitimate-rule|Herrschaft typology]]; unfinished class/[[status-group|Stand]] fragment. Reception dispute: [[weber-interpretive-sociology-debate]]. Hub treatments: [[hubs/thinkers/weber-max|Weber biography]], [[hubs/theory/types-of-legitimate-rule|types of legitimate rule]].

The ethnographic Chicago School (Park, Burgess, Thomas) remains thin; Cooley and Sumner are ingested; Marx/Engels enter via the *Manifesto*, *Brumaire*, *Capital* I, and *Origin of the Family* (conflict tradition + state origin + kinship); *Capital* II–III and reception literature remain.

### Postwar Expansion (c. 1945–1968)

[[mills-c-wright|C. Wright Mills]] is the wiki's first postwar primary source and its first
voice of the emerging **Critical Turn**. Trained by the German refugee scholar
[[gerth-hans|Hans Gerth]] (a student of Karl Mannheim), Mills carried the Marx–Weber
tradition into a radical anatomy of American stratification. *[[mills-white-collar-1951|White
Collar: The American Middle Classes]]* (1951; study: [[mills-white-collar-study]]) analyzes the
displacement of the propertied [[old-middle-class]] by the salaried, propertyless
[[new-middle-class]], introducing the [[personality-market]], the [[status-panic]], the
[[managerial-demiurge]], the [[the-big-split]], the [[cheerful-robot]], and the "politics of the
[[new-middle-class-politics-debate|rearguard]]." Against a crude materialism Mills insisted that
"between consciousness and existence stand communications" ([[mass-society]]), and he placed
[[political-indifference]] at the center of postwar politics. His stratification analysis is
Weberian (class, [[status-group|status]], power as distinct dimensions; see
[[social-class-weberian]]); his diagnosis of work extends Marxian [[alienation]] to salaried
labor against the ideal of [[craftsmanship]]. *[[mills-power-elite-1956|The Power Elite]]* (1956;
study: [[mills-power-elite-study]]) extends the analysis upward to the [[power-elite]] of corporate,
military, and political command. *[[mills-sociological-imagination-1959|The Sociological Imagination]]*
(1959) is his methodological and programmatic manifesto: it names the discipline's promise — the
[[sociological-imagination|quality of mind]] that connects [[personal-troubles-and-public-issues|personal
troubles to public issues]], biography to history to social structure — and mounts a two-front attack on
the era's dominant styles, [[grand-theory]] (Parsons's high abstraction) and [[abstracted-empiricism]]
(Lazarsfeld-style survey research), as twin "withdrawals from the tasks of the social sciences"
([[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]]). It also carries Mills's diagnosis of the
[[cheerful-robot]] and "rationality without reason," and his account of social science as the
[[reflexive-sociology|reflexive]], value-committed, publicly-addressed craft of the "classic tradition."

[[parsons-talcott|Talcott Parsons]], *[[parsons-social-system-1951|The Social System]]* (1951), supplies the wiki's charter for postwar **[[structural-functionalism]]** — the dominant American theoretical synthesis of the era: the [[social-system]] and [[social-role|status-role]], the [[pattern-variables]], [[functional-prerequisites]], [[socialization]] and internalized [[value-orientation|value-orientations]], [[deviance]] and [[social-control]], and the field-founding [[sick-role]] of medical sociology. It is at once the era's central theory and the principal target of the wiki's [[conflict-theory|conflict-theoretic]], interactionist, and reflexive holdings (Dahrendorf, Mills, Gouldner); on its capacity to theorize change see [[functionalism-and-social-change-debate]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/structural-functionalism]].

[[merton-robert-k|Robert K. Merton]], *[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|Social Theory and Social Structure]]* (1968 enlarged ed.), supplies the complementary **Columbia** charter of the same settlement: [[middle-range-theory]] as research policy (a fourth path relative to [[grand-theory]] and [[abstracted-empiricism]]; [[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]]); codified functional analysis ([[manifest-and-latent-functions]], [[dysfunction]], functional alternatives); the [[merton-social-structure-and-anomie-study|anomie/strain typology]]; [[reference-group]] / [[relative-deprivation]] / [[role-set]]; the [[self-fulfilling-prophecy]]; and the [[scientific-ethos|ethos of science]] (plus Puritanism–science extension of the [[protestant-ethic-debate|Weberian affinity thesis]]). Thinkers Hub: [[hubs/thinkers/merton-robert-k]]; Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/middle-range-theory]].

[[dahrendorf-ralf|Ralf Dahrendorf]], *[[dahrendorf-class-class-conflict-1959|Class and Class Conflict in Industrial Society]]* (1959), supplies the wiki's principal postwar **[[conflict-theory|conflict theory of class]]**: society as Janus-headed (integration vs coercion faces); class as authority-based conflict groups in [[imperatively-coordinated-association|imperatively coordinated associations]]; [[institutionalization-of-class-conflict|institutionalized]] industrial and political conflict in "post-capitalist" societies; Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/conflict-theory]]. Competes with Marxian property class theory and with integration-only models ([[consensus-vs-conflict-debate]]).

[[mannheim-karl|Karl Mannheim]], *[[mannheim-ideology-and-utopia-1936|Ideology and Utopia]]* (1929/1936 English composite), supplies the wiki’s principal **classical** charter for the [[sociology-of-knowledge]]: particular/total and special/general [[ideology]]; [[utopia|utopian mentality]] (four modern forms); [[relationism]]; [[free-floating-intelligentsia]]; scientific politics and styles of thought. Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/sociology-of-knowledge]]; thinker hub [[hubs/thinkers/mannheim-karl]]. Transmission into English via [[wirth-louis|Louis Wirth]] and Edward Shils.

[[berger-peter-l|Peter L. Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Thomas Luckmann]], *[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|The Social Construction of Reality]]* (1966), supply the wiki’s principal **postwar theoretical** charter for the redefined [[sociology-of-knowledge]] and [[social-construction-of-reality]]: dialectic of [[externalization-objectivation-internalization|externalization, objectivation, and internalization]]; [[institutionalization]] and [[legitimation]]; [[primary-socialization]] / [[secondary-socialization]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/social-construction-of-reality]]. Bridges [[social-psychology|Meadian social psychology]] to a full sociology of institutions and meaning-order (Durkheimian facticity + Weberian subjective meaning); redefines the field against Scheler/Mannheim toward everyday knowledge.

### Later eras

The **Critical Turn** (c. 1968–1990) enters the wiki with [[marcuse-herbert|Herbert Marcuse]]'s
*[[marcuse-one-dimensional-man-1964|One-Dimensional Man]]* (1964), the first
[[critical-theory|Frankfurt-School]] source — a diagnosis of "advanced industrial society" as a
[[one-dimensionality|one-dimensional]] order that contains opposition through affluence,
[[technological-rationality|technological rationality]], and [[repressive-desublimation]] rather
than terror. Marcuse's revision of Marxism on the integrated working class ([[revolutionary-subject-debate]])
and his fusion of Marx, Weber, Hegel, and Freud opened the wiki's critical-theory holdings; the
Institut für Sozialforschung program (Horkheimer, Adorno) is now partly present via *[[horkheimer-adorno-dialectic-of-enlightenment-1947|Dialectic of Enlightenment]]*. Berger & Luckmann’s 1966 constructionist charter (above) is late Postwar Expansion but its vocabulary is widely absorbed into Critical-Turn and contemporary work.

[[gouldner-alvin-w|Alvin W. Gouldner]], *[[gouldner-coming-crisis-1970|The Coming Crisis of Western Sociology]]* (1970), supplies the wiki's major **sociology of sociology** and the diagnosis that Western Academic Sociology and Marxism — the two "halves" of Western sociology after binary fission — face structural crisis. Core apparatus: [[domain-assumptions]], [[theoretical-infrastructure]], [[methodological-dualism]], and the program of [[reflexive-sociology]]; extended critique of [[structural-functionalism|Parsonian structural-functionalism]] (entropy under Welfare-State and generational pressure; rival infrastructures of Goffman, ethnomethodology, exchange, New Left). Standing dispute: [[coming-crisis-of-western-sociology-debate]].

The Critical Turn's **comparative-historical** wing enters with [[skocpol-theda|Theda Skocpol]]'s *[[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-1979|States and Social Revolutions]]* (1979), the founding statement of the state-centered [[structural-theory-of-revolution|structural theory of revolution]] and of the "bring the state back in" agenda. Building on [[moore-barrington|Barrington Moore Jr.]]'s agrarian-class comparativism but reasserting the [[state-autonomy|autonomous state]], it modeled rigorous small-N [[comparative-method|comparative-historical analysis]] and made the state a first-class variable across [[political-sociology]] (Studies Hub [[hubs/studies/skocpol-states-social-revolutions-study]]; comparison [[france-russia-china-revolutions]]).

The Critical Turn's **practice-theory** wing enters with [[bourdieu-pierre|Pierre Bourdieu]]'s *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (French 1980; English c. 1990) — [[practice-theory]] as double break with objectivism and subjectivism; [[habitus]], [[practical-sense]], [[symbolic-capital]], [[symbolic-violence]]; Kabyle and Béarn empirical programs (Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/practice-theory]]; Thinkers Hub [[hubs/thinkers/bourdieu-pierre]]; society [[kabyle]]; debate [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]). The apparatus is extended to institutions in *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]* (with [[passeron-jean-claude|Passeron]], 1970), founding text of this wiki's **[[sociology-of-education]]** subfield: [[symbolic-violence]], [[pedagogic-action]], [[cultural-arbitrary]], [[cultural-reproduction]], [[self-elimination]] (Studies Hub [[hubs/studies/bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-study]]; debate [[cultural-reproduction-debate]]). Related monographs (*Outline*, *Distinction*, *The State Nobility*) remain queued.

The Critical Turn's **emotions and work–family** wing enters with [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Arlie Russell Hochschild]]: *[[hochschild-managed-heart-1983|The Managed Heart]]* (1983) — [[emotional-labor]], [[feeling-rules]], commercialization of feeling (Studies Hub [[hubs/studies/hochschild-managed-heart-study]]); *[[hochschild-second-shift-1989|The Second Shift]]* (1989, with Anne Machung) — [[second-shift]], [[stalled-revolution]], [[gender-strategy]], dual-earner [[family]] under [[work-family-conflict]] (Studies Hub [[hubs/studies/hochschild-second-shift-study]]).

The Contemporary **sociology of gender and masculinities** wing enters with [[connell-raewyn|R. W. Connell]]'s *[[connell-masculinities-1995|Masculinities]]* (1995) — the [[gender-relations-theory|relational theory of gender]] (gender as a structure of practice: power/production/cathexis) and the field-founding concept of [[hegemonic-masculinity]], with [[patriarchal-dividend]], [[gender-order]], and [[body-reflexive-practice]], grounded in a [[connell-masculinities-lifehistory-study|life-history study]] of four groups of Australian men (Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/gender-relations-theory]]; debate [[hegemonic-masculinity-debate]]). ## Relations to Adjacent Disciplines


Comte located sociology **after** biology in the hierarchy and insisted social science depends on the whole prior encyclopedic scale. He distinguished abstract social physics from concrete histories, and social science from immediate political art — while still aiming at reorganization. He rejected introspective psychology as a route to social knowledge. Boundary with political economy: Comte treated the Economists as critical agents who showed governments cannot direct industry, yet charged political economy with individualism and organic impotence (Book VI ch. X). Durkheim and Veblen rework the economy–society boundary differently (anomic market regulation; leisure-class consumption). Boundary with history: abstract social physics vs. concrete chronicle.


**Structuration (Critical Turn).** [[giddens-anthony|Anthony Giddens]]'s [[structuration-theory]] (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, 1984) reorganizes structure–agency via the [[duality-of-structure|duality of structure]]; see also [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] and [[hubs/theory/structuration-theory]].

## Reflexivity

Sociology's Comtean birth is openly a **reorganization project** after the French Revolution and what Comte called intellectual anarchy — not a value-neutral academic specialty. The historical method privileges a European "elite of humanity" path; statics privileges the "white race" as chief object of the scientific society-concept (attributed). Gendered hierarchy in social statics ([[family]] doctrine) shaped early images of the social organism. Colonial and comparative worlds appear mainly as inferior stages relative to the advanced series. Tocqueville's America material and Durkheim's "lower societies" framings carry their own positionality; Veblen's 1899 race typology is dated race-science (attributed, not fact). Spencer’s *Principles* compile travellers’/missionaries’ reports under Victorian “savage/barbarian/civilized” vocabulary and include race-mixture constitutional claims — attributed dated discourse, not findings. Pareto’s *Trattato* confines illustration to Europe/Mediterranean (§2065), polemizes against “pluto-demagogic” democracy, and carries dated sex/race illustrations under attributed voice only. Du Bois writes as subject of the color-line, not as European “elite of humanity” observer — a structural corrective within the classical era, though *Souls* still centers male leadership types and 1903 institutional facts.
