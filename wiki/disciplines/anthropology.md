# Anthropology

## Scope

Anthropology claims as its object the whole range of human ways of life — biological, cultural, linguistic, and material — across all times and places, and the comparative study of what is universal versus particular in them. In its four-field North American organization it comprises cultural (social) anthropology, biological anthropology, [[linguistic-anthropology|linguistic anthropology]], and archaeology. The founding move, made canonically by [[tylor-edward-burnett|E. B. Tylor]], was to constitute **[[culture]]** as a single lawful phenomenon ("that complex whole which includes knowledge, belief, art, morals, law, custom...") studiable by natural-science method.

## Historical Development

- **Founding era (1830–1890).** Emerges from ethnology, natural history, and the debate over human unity (monogenism vs. polygenism). Victorian [[social-evolutionism|cultural evolutionism]] — Tylor's *[[tylor-primitive-culture-1871|Primitive Culture]]* (1871), [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]'s *Ancient Society* (1877) and *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-study|Systems of Consanguinity]]* (1871), Spencer, Lubbock, McLennan — reconstructs a universal ladder from savagery to civilization using the [[comparative-method]] on traveller and missionary reports ("armchair anthropology"). *(Attributed — [[stocking-george-w|Stocking]] 1987.)* British institutional history in this era ran from Prichardian [[ethnology]] (the Ethnological Society, 1843, monogenist and humanitarian-adjacent) through the [[victorian-ethnology-anthropology-schism-debate|1860s schism]] with James Hunt's rival, polygenist Anthropological Society (1863), to the 1871 "compromise" founding of the Anthropological Institute of Great Britain and Ireland. Institutionalization stayed weak relative to France (Broca's École d'Anthropologie, six chairs, 1876) and the United States (Bureau of Ethnology, 1879): Tylor's Oxford readership dates only to 1884 and Haddon's Cambridge post to 1895, and Stocking estimates no more than a dozen full-time professional anthropologists existed in Britain before 1900. Stocking periodizes the discipline's subsequent trajectory as classical evolutionism's dominance (1860s–1900s) giving way to a "neo-ethnological" diffusionist reaction (Rivers, c. 1911–1920s), then functionalism and structural-functionalism (from 1922–23), a long mid-century "dehistoricization," and, from the late 1960s, an interpretive/hermeneutic turn (US) or "epistemological break" from function to meaning (Britain) — see [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]], [[evolutionism-diffusionism-debate]].
- **Classical era (1890–1920).** Professionalization: Tylor's Oxford chair (1896); [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]]'s *[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|Golden Bough]]* (1890; expanded through 1915; abridged 1922) becomes the public face of comparative religion and [[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion|intellectualist]] evolutionism; [[boas-franz|Boas]] at Columbia mounts the [[historical-particularism|historical-particularist]] reaction against evolutionism (*[[boas-mind-of-primitive-man-1938|Mind of Primitive Man]]*, 1911/1938); the Torres Strait expedition (1898) begins systematic fieldwork.
- **Fieldwork revolution (1920–1945).** [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] (*[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]*, 1922; fieldwork 1915–18) and [[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]] (*[[radcliffe-brown-andaman-islanders-1922|The Andaman Islanders]]*, 1922; fieldwork 1906–08) institutionalize intensive fieldwork and functionalism, displacing conjectural evolutionary history — with Frazer as the emblematic armchair target. *Argonauts* is primary-ingested (method charter + [[kula|Kula]] ethnography of the [[trobriand-islanders|Trobriands]]/Massim). *[[malinowski-coral-gardens-1935|Coral Gardens]]* Vol. II (1935) is primary-ingested (ethnographic theory of language / [[context-of-situation]]; garden magic and legal-economic lexicon; Vol. I still absent). *Andaman Islanders* is primary-ingested (Great Andaman [[social-structure]] + systemic "social physiology" interpretation; [[andamanese]]; [[radcliffe-brown-andaman-islanders-study]]).
- **Postwar expansion (1945–1968).** Neo-evolutionism reopens evolutionary questions without unilinear ladders: [[steward-julian-h|Julian Steward]]'s *[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]* (1955) formulates [[multilinear-evolution]] and [[cultural-ecology]] (program hub: [[hubs/theory/cultural-ecology]]); [[white-leslie-a|White]]'s *[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|The Evolution of Culture]]* (1959) states [[universal-evolution]] / [[culturology]] (hub: [[hubs/theory/universal-evolution]]); [[service-elman-r|Service]]'s *[[service-primitive-social-organization-1971|Primitive Social Organization]]* (1962/1971) states the [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]] of political-social levels (hub: [[hubs/theory/band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]); [[fried-morton-h|Fried]]'s *[[fried-evolution-of-political-society-1967|Evolution of Political Society]]* (1967) states the rival [[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]] (hub: [[hubs/theory/egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]]; debate [[service-fried-political-typology-debate]]). Sahlins & Service *Evolution and Culture* (1960) remains outstanding. In political anthropology, [[leach-edmund|Leach]]'s *[[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-1954|Political Systems of Highland Burma]]* (1954) critiques equilibrium models and theorises [[gumsa-gumlao-oscillation]] among highland [[kachin|Kachin]] and [[shan|Shan]] polities ([[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-study]]; [[political-anthropology]]).
- **Postwar expansion — symbolic/structural pollution theory:** [[douglas-mary|Mary Douglas]]'s *[[douglas-purity-and-danger-1966|Purity and Danger]]* (1966) reorients comparative pollution and [[taboo]] around classification and social order ([[purity-pollution-theory]]; [[douglas-purity-and-danger-study]]) — Oxford lineage from [[evans-pritchard-e-e]], against medical materialism and residual "primitive mentality."
- **Postwar expansion — ethnicity as organization:** [[barth-fredrik|Fredrik Barth]] (ed.) *[[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-1969|Ethnic Groups and Boundaries]]* (1969) founds [[ethnic-boundary-theory]] — ethnic groups as categories of [[ethnic-ascription]] maintained by [[ethnic-boundary|boundary]] processes, not culture-content inventories ([[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-study]]; Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/ethnic-boundary-theory]]).
- **Postwar → critical turn — the Manchester School and symbolic anthropology:** [[gluckman-max|Gluckman]]'s [[manchester-school|Manchester School]] (Rhodes-Livingstone Institute) shifted analysis from equilibrium to conflict, process, and the [[extended-case-method|extended case]]; his student [[turner-victor|Victor Turner]] carried this into ritual, founding the processual wing of [[symbolic-anthropology]]. *[[turner-forest-of-symbols-1967|The Forest of Symbols]]* (1967) and *[[turner-ritual-process-1969|The Ritual Process]]* (1969) — Ndembu ritual — supply the concepts [[dominant-symbol]], [[liminality]], and [[communitas]] ([[social-drama]]; [[symbolic-exegesis]]; society [[ndembu]]).
- **Critical turn — cultural materialism as named program:** [[harris-marvin|Marvin Harris]]'s *[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|The Rise of Anthropological Theory]]* (1968) names [[cultural-materialism]] — techno-environmental/techno-economic research priority as the sociocultural analogue of natural selection — and mounts a partisan historiography of anthropology from the Enlightenment through mid-1960s rivals (Boas, culture-and-personality, structuralism, British SA, emic new ethnography). Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/cultural-materialism]]. Emic/etic metalanguage: [[emic-and-etic]].
- **Critical turn — anthropology and colonialism:** [[asad-talal|Talal Asad]] (ed.) *[[asad-anthropology-colonial-encounter-1973|Anthropology and the Colonial Encounter]]* (1973) is the vault's charter collection for the [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]] — determination thesis (the colonial world shapes how anthropology apprehends its objects); [[james-wendy|Wendy James]]'s "reluctant imperialist"; sponsorship/[[applied-anthropology]]; [[indirect-rule]] cases; [[wilson-godfrey|Godfrey Wilson]]/RLI; standing debate page.
- **Critical turn — the temporal/epistemological critique:** [[fabian-johannes|Johannes Fabian]]'s *[[fabian-time-and-the-other-1983|Time and the Other]]* (1983) argues (attributed) that anthropology systematically denies its contemporaries [[coevalness]] via [[allochronism]] (the [[ethnographic-present]]; [[visualism]]; evolutionist "temporal slope" vocabulary), reframing the discipline's colonial entanglement as *epistemological* ("geopolitics has its ideological foundations in chronopolitics") — a theoretical complement to Asad's determination thesis and a precursor to *Writing Culture* (1986).
- **Critical turn — experimental moment / Writing Culture:** Clifford & Marcus (eds.) *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* (1986) and Marcus & Fischer *[[marcus-fischer-anthropology-as-cultural-critique-1986|Anthropology as Cultural Critique]]* (1986) name the ethnographic **[[experimental-moment|experimental moment]]** and the **[[crisis-of-representation]]**: ethnography as writing under contested [[ethnographic-authority]]; dual modern promise of diversity documentation plus **[[anthropology-as-cultural-critique|cultural critique]]**; [[repatriation-of-anthropology|repatriation]] and techniques of [[defamiliarization]]. Program: [[writing-culture]]; debate: [[writing-culture-debate]]; Hub: [[hubs/theory/writing-culture]].
- Later eras: the interpretive turn, practice theory, and further decolonizing critique (see also [[historical-anthropology]], [[wolf-europe-people-without-history-1982]]).

- **Critical turn — historiography of the discipline’s object:** [[kuper-adam|Adam Kuper]]’s *[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|The Invention of Primitive Society]]* (1988) argues that successive schools (evolutionism, diffusionism, functionalism, structuralism) all addressed a single invented prototype of [[primitive-society]] crystallized in the 1860s–70s; kinship studies were its technical core; British [[descent-theory]] and Lévi-Straussian [[alliance-theory]] were transformations of that illusion. See also [[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]], [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].

## Theoretical Landscape

**[[culture-and-personality]] national-character extension:** [[benedict-ruth|Ruth Benedict]]'s *[[benedict-chrysanthemum-and-sword-1946|The Chrysanthemum and the Sword]]* (1946) scales [[configurationism]] to wartime Japan via [[culture-at-a-distance]] ([[benedict-chrysanthemum-study]]; [[national-character-debate]]).

**[[structuralism|Structuralism]]:** [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss's]] program — cultural phenomena as sign systems of [[binary-opposition|oppositions]] on the model of structural linguistics — reshaped the postwar discipline. Its kinship face, [[alliance-theory]] (*[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures of Kinship]]*, 1949), took marriage exchange rather than descent as the generative principle ([[restricted-exchange]] / [[generalized-exchange]]; [[exchange-of-women]]), and *[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]* (1955) is an early reflexive text anticipating the 1980s critique of ethnographic authority. Structuralism was in turn challenged by the practice turn ([[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]], [[practice-theory]]) and post-structuralism; its vocabulary survives its program. Harris's *Rise* (ch. 18) is a cultural-materialist counter-reading (attributed), not a neutral summary.

**[[cultural-materialism]]:** Harris (1968) reasserts anthropology as nomothetic science of history under techno-environmental determinism; redesignates Steward's [[cultural-ecology]] as applied CM and White's evolutionism as incomplete strategy. Status contested; ecological and archaeological materialisms partially absorbed priorities under other labels.

**The two-paradigm frame ([[symbolic-anthropology|symbolic]]/culturalist):** [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] (*[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]*, 1976) recast a century of the discipline's theory as one recurrent opposition — [[practical-reason]] (utilitarian, ecological, and Marxist accounts of culture as material effect) versus [[cultural-reason]] (culture as a meaningful symbolic order that constitutes utility) — mapping [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]], [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]], [[steward-julian-h|Steward]], and [[white-leslie-a|White]] to the first and [[boas-franz|Boas]], [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]], and [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] to the second. In *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]* (1985) he turned the culturalist program toward history ("structure is an historical object"; see [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]], [[mytho-praxis]]).

Founding cultural evolutionism ([[social-evolutionism]]) was overthrown by Boasian [[historical-particularism]] (see [[boas-franz]], [[race-and-culture-debate]]), then succeeded by functionalism, neo-evolutionism ([[cultural-ecology]], [[multilinear-evolution]]), structuralism, cultural materialism, and interpretive/symbolic anthropology. Tylor's [[animism]] and Frazer's [[sympathetic-magic]] / magic–religion–science sequence ([[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion]]) compete with Durkheimian, functionalist, and symbolic accounts of religion. See [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]], [[frazer-golden-bough-study]].

## Methods

The [[comparative-method]] is anthropology's founding analytic; Tylor also pioneered cross-cultural statistical correlation (1889), from which the Galton's-problem critique of case non-independence derives. Boas attacked the naïve evolutionary use of comparison and insisted on historical reconstruction and four-field independence of race, language, and culture (*[[boas-mind-of-primitive-man-1938|Mind]]*). Intensive fieldwork ([[participant-observation]]) became the signature data-collection method with Malinowski's *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]* charter (three avenues; [[imponderabilia-of-actual-life]]; native's point of view). Radcliffe-Brown's *[[radcliffe-brown-andaman-islanders-1922|Andaman Islanders]]* (same year) pairs residence with a program of systemic interpretation (meaning vs origin; compare social systems/types) — the structural pole of British functionalism ([[structural-functionalism]]; [[social-structure]]). The [[genealogical-method]] — [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]]' technique of deriving kinship, marriage, and social organisation from systematically collected pedigrees (Torres Strait 1898; *[[rivers-melanesian-society-1914|Melanesian Society]]*, 1914) — made kinship a rigorous field science and remains standard. [[cultural-ecological-method|Cultural ecology]] (Steward) analyzes technology–environment–behavior relations and the [[cultural-core]] to explain cross-cultural regularities without unilinear stages (*[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]*).

## Relations to Adjacent Disciplines

Anthropology overlaps [[sociology]] (shared theory of the social; anthropology's traditional focus on non-Western and small-scale societies is a colonial-era division of labour now contested), history, biology, and linguistics. The four-field structure keeps it uniquely spanning the natural-science/humanities divide.

## Reflexivity

Anthropology's founding categories — "savage," "barbaric," "primitive," "lower races" — were forged inside Victorian colonialism and an evolutionary hierarchy that ranked the world's peoples by proximity to European civilization. The discipline's early evidence base was overwhelmingly the reports of colonial administrators, missionaries, and travellers. Reckoning with this production context, and with the discipline's complicity in and critique of empire, is treated as content throughout this wiki, not as apology.
