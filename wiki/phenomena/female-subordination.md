# Female Subordination (the secondary status of women)

The cross-cultural pattern by which women's roles, tasks, and products are accorded less prestige than men's, and women are excluded from a society's highest offices of authority. Whether the pattern is a genuine *universal* — and how to explain it — is the founding problem of [[feminist-anthropology|feminist anthropology]] and the subject of the [[nature-culture-gender-debate]]. Recorded here as a **claimed** universal, not a wiki-voice fact.

## Definition and Scope

[[ortner-sherry|Ortner]] (1972/74) distinguished three analytically separate things: (1) the *cultural evaluation* of women as secondary; (2) *ideological/symbolic variation* in how women are conceived across cultures; and (3) women's *actual on-the-ground powers*, "often at variance with cultural ideology." The phenomenon named here is level (1) — a fact of cultural valuation — which Ortner argued must be understood before levels (2) and (3). It is explicitly **not** the claim that women are everywhere powerless in practice.

The editors of *[[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974|Woman, Culture, and Society]]* (1974) stated the universalist claim in programmatic form: all contemporary societies are to some extent male-dominated; degree and expression of female subordination vary greatly, but "sexual asymmetry is presently a universal fact of human social life." That formulation is a **position** of the early program, not wiki-voice fact — see [[nature-culture-gender-debate]]. The same volume's second half documents systematic female power, associations, chiefship, and networks *within* asymmetry.

## Measurement

[[ortner-female-to-male-1972|Ortner]] proposed that any one of three data types suffices as evidence in a given society: explicit devaluing ideology; symbolic devices such as [[ritual-pollution|menstrual defilement]]; or rules barring women from the realm where "the highest powers of the society are felt to reside" (e.g. exclusion from the most sacred rite or highest council). Critics note that what counts as "highest" and how prestige is scored are themselves analyst's judgments. Sanday (1974, same volume) operationalized female status in the **public domain** with multi-indicator scales (power, authority, deference) separate from domestic respect.

## Patterns and Findings (attributed)

- [[ortner-sherry|Ortner]] held the secondary status to be a "true universal," illustrated by cases usually counted favorable to women (e.g. the matrilineal Crow, where women held ritual offices yet the most sacred bundle was tabooed to their touch — per Lowie).
- [[rosaldo-michelle|Rosaldo]] tied it to the [[domestic-public-dichotomy|domestic/public]] division; [[chodorow-nancy|Chodorow]] to the socialization consequences of women's monopoly of early child care.
- Volume empirical cases (1974) show wide *variation within* claimed asymmetry: Mende female paramount chiefs (Hoffer); Ijaw associations (Leis); Chinese uterine-family power (Wolf); Engelsian public-labor continua (Sacks); matrifocal networks (Tanner, Stack).
- Critics ([[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]], MacCormack, Collier & Yanagisako) argued that the apparent universality is partly an artifact of Western categories, and that "gender" and its valuation vary too much to constitute a single explanandum.

## Explanatory Theories

Biological-determinist / sociobiological accounts (rejected by Ortner and mainstream anthropology); symbolic ([[nature-culture-distinction|nature/culture]]); structural ([[domestic-public-dichotomy|domestic/public]]); psychoanalytic (reproduction of mothering); and later materialist and historicized accounts that deny a single cause. Stated side by side, attributed.

## Interaction

Interacts with [[family|kinship and family]] organization, the gender division of labor, [[marriage]], and religion/ritual authority. Its explanation is disputed in the [[nature-culture-gender-debate]].

## Debates

See [[nature-culture-gender-debate]] for the dispute over whether the phenomenon is universal at all and whether nature/culture is a valid frame for it.
