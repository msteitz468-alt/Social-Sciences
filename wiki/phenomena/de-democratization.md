# De-Democratization

> Decline of **protected consultation** — Tilly’s process measure of democracy — after regimes have moved toward broader, more equal, binding, protective citizen–government relations. Principal source: *[[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005|Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties]]* Ch. 13 (conjectural program).

## Definition and Scope

*(Attributed — Tilly 2005.)* Democracy combines relatively **broad** and **equal** public participation, **binding consultation** of participants regarding state policies/resources/personnel, and **protection** from arbitrary state action. **Protected consultation** is the product of those dimensions. **De-democratization** is their reversal.

Tilly stresses: most regimes that took significant steps toward democracy over the last two centuries later de-democratized at least temporarily. Democracy is not a permanent cave of refuge.

## Measurement

- Continuous dimensions of capacity and protected consultation (Fig. 13-1 regime typology: high/low capacity × democratic/undemocratic).  
- Case rosters of reversals (20th-c. Europe: Greece, Italy, Germany, Spain, Portugal, Vichy France; Latin America; post-Soviet recessions — as Tilly cites Freedom House and comparative literature).  
- Eight processes under three headings (categorical inequality; trust networks; public politics / citizen–agent relations): reversal of **any** promotes de-democratization; democratization requires at least one process under each of the first two headings **and all four** under the third (Tilly’s most general claim in the chapter).

## Patterns and Findings

*(Attributed — labeled **conjectures** by Tilly, not established findings.)*

- Organizing major political actors around **categorical inequality** boundaries and writing those boundaries into participation rules undermines protected consultation.  
- Insulation of **trust networks** from public politics (or their capture as clientelist machines) can block or reverse democratization.  
- High governmental capacity can facilitate either democratic protection or authoritarian control depending on trajectory.  
- Inequality and democratization interact; widespread [[categorical-inequality]] threatens democracy (also *Durable Inequality* 1998).

## Explanatory Theories

- Tilly mechanism–process account (Chs. 3, 13) vs modernization monisms that treat democratization as irreversible developmental stage.  
- Links to [[durable-inequality-theory]] when governments are parties to categorical inequality.  
- Related but distinct from purely economic development–democracy correlations ([[economic-development-and-democracy]]).
