# Durable Inequality

Long-lasting, systematically reproduced inequalities in access to valued resources that map onto **[[categorical-inequality|social categories]]** rather than fluctuating only with individual traits. Primary theorization in this wiki: [[tilly-charles|Tilly]], *[[tilly-durable-inequality-1998|Durable Inequality]]* (1998) / [[durable-inequality-theory]].

## Definition and Scope

**Durable** here means institutionalized and self-reproducing across time and organizational sites — not merely large at one moment. Tilly's claim (attributed): such durability depends heavily on **[[categorical-pairs|paired categories]]** and the four mechanisms of [[exploitation]], [[opportunity-hoarding]], [[organizational-emulation|emulation]], and [[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]].

## Measurement

No single global index in the charter text. Illustrative measures: stature/nutrition by class; wage and job-segregation gaps; niche monopoly of trades; citizenship rights differentials; intergenerational transmission of categorically organized advantage.

## Patterns and Findings

Attributed to Tilly (1998): (1) large inequalities correspond mainly to categorical pairs; (2) controllers of resources install categories to solve organizational problems; (3) beliefs reinforce more than they independently cause initiation; (4) matching interior organizational ranks to exterior categories multiplies durability; (5) intervention leverage is organizational redesign, not attitude change alone.

Related phenomena: [[social-mobility]], [[residential-segregation]], [[concentrated-poverty]].

## Explanatory Theories

| Theory | Claim (attributed) |
|---|---|
| [[durable-inequality-theory]] (Tilly) | Four mechanisms + categorical pairs |
| Human capital / status attainment | Individual skills and family background (Tilly treats as incomplete without organizational mechanisms) |
| Prejudice/discrimination monism | Attitudes drive inequality (Tilly: reinforce, rarely initiate) |
| Classical Marxist class exploitation | Surplus extraction (Tilly generalizes exploitation beyond wage labor) |
| [[gender-as-primary-frame]] (Ridgeway) | Gender as categorical status inequality re-inscribed via primary-frame interaction and lagging beliefs at innovation sites — dialogues with Tilly/Jackman on categorical and status embedding (*[[ridgeway-framed-by-gender-2011|Framed by Gender]]*) |

## Interaction

Connects to [[state-formation]] and democracy (categorical inequality as threat to broad equal citizenship — Tilly Ch. 8); to migration and ethnic niches; to gender–household–firm circuits; to [[political-conflict-theory|contention]] when excluded categories mobilize.

## Debates

Weight of categories vs continuous class gradients; primacy of exploitation vs hoarding; whether "durable" understates historical reversals (franchise expansion, civil rights). Positions to be expanded with reception literature.
