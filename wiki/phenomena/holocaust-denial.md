# Holocaust Denial

## Definition and Scope

The claim that the Nazi genocide of the Jews did not happen, was vastly exaggerated, or was fabricated by Jews for political/financial gain. Distinct from *revisionist historiography* (normal scholarly correction): denial's defining move is conspiracist — the evidence itself is alleged to be forged, which makes denial an application of the [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]] rather than a historical position. "Softer" relativization and Holocaust inversion (casting Israelis as Nazis) border the phenomenon and are contested boundary cases (see [[new-antisemitism-debate]]).

## Measurement

Monitored via denialist publishing/web activity, survey belief items, and prosecutions where denial is criminalized (Germany, Austria, France). Measures disagree about where minimization ends and denial begins.

## Patterns and Findings

All attributed to Wistrich (2010) unless otherwise noted; single-source seed.

**Origins and the French "laboratory."** Wistrich calls France "the main intellectual laboratory for Holocaust denial" since 1945 (his characterization). Maurice Bardèche's *Nuremberg ou la terre promise* (1948) framed the Holocaust as Jewish propaganda; Paul Rassinier (a Buchenwald survivor and ex-Communist) denied an extermination policy from the early 1950s; Robert Faurisson (University of Lyon) declared the gas chambers a "historical lie" from 1978, his 1980 apologia carrying a preface by Noam Chomsky. The far-left publishing house La Vieille Taupe (Pierre Guillaume, Serge Thion) gave denial an anarcho-Marxist strand alongside the far-right one.

**Anglophone and German networks.** Harry Elmer Barnes and David Hoggan (US); Arthur Butz (*The Hoax of the Twentieth Century*, 1975, a Northwestern engineering professor); Richard Verrall/"Harwood" (*Did Six Million Die?*, 1974, National Front); Willis Carto's Institute for Historical Review (1979) and *Journal of Historical Review* (1980), which gave denial a pseudo-academic apparatus; David Irving, whose 2000 libel suit against Deborah Lipstadt ended with Justice Gray's ruling that Irving is "unequivocally anti-Semitic" and a Holocaust denier — the paradigm legal adjudication of the phenomenon; Ernst Zündel, Thies Christophersen, Wilhelm Stäglich, Germar Rudolf, and the "Leuchter Report" (1989), demolished by Jean-Claude Pressac's forensic study of the gas chambers the same year.

**State sponsorship.** Nasser-era Egypt employed ex-Nazi propagandists (Johann von Leers as "Omar Amin") and Nasser himself endorsed denial to a German interviewer; Mubarak-era state-subsidized press ran denial columns; Syrian state media and Bashar al-Assad ("exaggerated by the West," 2006) relativized it; the USSR suppressed the Jewish specificity of Nazi victims (no Jewish monument at Babi Yar) while sponsoring "Zionist-Nazi collaboration" literature. Iran under Ahmadinejad made denial state foreign policy: the December 2006 Tehran "Review of the Holocaust" conference (David Duke, Faurisson, Töben, Neturei Karta attendees), the Hamshahri cartoon contest (1,200+ entries, 61 countries), a state "World Foundation for Holocaust Research" under Mohammed Ali Ramin, and repeated "myth" statements by Ahmadinejad, Rafsanjani, and Khamenei. Roger Garaudy, convicted in France (1998), was feted across the Arab world and received by Iran's top leadership.

**Palestinian and Islamist variants.** Mahmoud Abbas's 1982 Moscow dissertation minimized Jewish deaths ("even fewer than one million") and alleged Zionist-Nazi collusion — never retracted; Hamas's February 2000 statement called the Holocaust "baseless"; Hezbollah's Nasrallah praised Garaudy and called the gas chambers "Jewish fabrications" (1998), years before Ahmadinejad. A 1999 American University of Beirut survey found 45% of respondents believed the Holocaust never happened; a University of Haifa poll (2009) found 40.5% of Israeli Arabs agreed, up from 28% three years earlier.

**"Selective negationism" and softer forms.** Michael Shafir's term (cited by Wistrich) for post-communist Eastern Europe: mass killing acknowledged but national perpetration denied (Romania), alongside rehabilitation of collaborators (Tiso, Antonescu, Pavelić) and "double genocide"/competitive-martyrdom framings. Ernst Nolte's Historikerstreit relativization is treated as a "half-embrace."

**Criminalization.** France's Gayssot Law (1990, used against Faurisson and Garaudy), German and Austrian prosecutions (Irving imprisoned in Austria); Britain has no denial statute — the free-speech boundary remains a live policy dispute.

## Explanatory Theories

Wistrich (attributed) reads denial as the post-1945 form of the conspiracy myth: since the Holocaust delegitimized open antisemitism, denying it both rehabilitates the ideology that produced it and re-accuses Jews of world-manipulating fraud. Cohn's demonological-myth framework supplies the mechanism.

## Interaction

Feeds and is fed by [[antisemitism]]; entangled with free-speech debates over memory laws; state sponsorship (Iran's 2006 Tehran conference, as of Wistrich 2010) links it to interstate politics.

## Debates

Boundary with legitimate historiography; criminalization vs. free speech; whether Holocaust inversion belongs to the phenomenon — see [[new-antisemitism-debate]].
