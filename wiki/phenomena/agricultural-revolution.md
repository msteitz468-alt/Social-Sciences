# Agricultural Revolution

## Definition and Scope

In *[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|The Evolution of Culture]]* (1959), [[white-leslie-a|Leslie A. White]] treats the **Agricultural Revolution** as the decisive energy transformation of cultural history within his volume's arc: domestication of plants (especially cereals) and animals harnesses solar energy in new form, breaking the ceiling of human-organismic energy that limited "primitive" (human-energy) cultures (Ch. 12; Summary Ch. 16). Adjacent archaeological language: V. Gordon Childe's Neolithic Revolution (food production) and Urban Revolution (cities/states) — White cites Childe but organizes the argument under his thermodynamic [[universal-evolution]] program.

Not identical to any single regional Neolithic; White's construct is a **universal-evolutionary type** illustrated by Old World (and comparative) materials.

## Measurement

White operationalizes primarily via energy regime (human vs plant/animal) and consequent social-ideological reorganization, not modern paleobotanical metrics. Later archaeology measures domestication morphologies, settlement, isotopes, and genetic evidence (as of post-2010 archaeogenetic literature) — independent evidence classes (material/genetic) to be kept distinct from White's theoretical packaging.

## Patterns and Findings

Attributed to White: agricultural energy enables surplus, population growth, division of labor intensification, property and class, **[[state-church]]**, commercial exchange, and new ideological forms (theology; beginnings of science). Primitive culture as a whole (Part One) is the human-energy baseline he contrasts.

## Explanatory Theories

- **White [[universal-evolution]] / [[energy-law-of-cultural-evolution]]:** energy threshold is primary.
- **Childe:** archaeological revolutions (Neolithic/Urban) with technology and surplus. Primary coining in *[[childe-man-makes-himself-1936|Man Makes Himself]]* (1936): first revolution = "control over his own food supply" (cultivation + stock); measured by population kinks; process not catastrophe. In *[[childe-dawn-european-civilization-1958|Dawn]]* (6th ed., attributed) he places the **Neolithic Revolution** in SW Asia and argues temperate European food-gatherers could not become food-producers by themselves — cereals and stock arrived with immigrant shepherds and cultivators; the **Urban Revolution** concentrates Oriental surplus that Aegean and barbarian Europe later tap. Absolute dates and many demic routes are (as of Childe 1936–58) and partly superseded; the vocabulary remains field-standard.
- **Steward [[cultural-ecology]] / irrigation civilizations:** multilinear paths, not one global curve.
- **Contemporary Neolithic research:** multiple independent centers, mosaic timelines — challenges single-revolution rhetoric while retaining food-production as a major transition.
- **Leakey & Lewin "psycho-materialism" reading** (*[[leakey-lewin-origins-1977|Origins]]* 1977, attributed): the authors call the invention of agriculture "the most significant event in the history of mankind" and argue that sedentary farming, by permitting the accumulation of possessions that must be defended, called forth a "psycho-materialism" that is — in their view — the root of organized human conflict and war, in explicit contrast to the mobile, low-accumulation [[hunter-gatherer]] pattern. This is central to their claim that war is a product of the farming transition, not of innate biological aggression (see [[human-aggression-killer-ape-debate]]). They cite pre-agricultural sedentary villages (Lepenski Vir on the Danube; Ain Mallaha in the Upper Jordan Valley) and Owens Valley Paiute wild-plant irrigation as transitional cases, and note world population rose from under ten million at the revolution's onset to over four billion by 1977.
- **Binford packing / mobility-security model** (*[[binford-in-pursuit-of-the-past-1983|In Pursuit of the Past]]* 1983, attributed): critiques oasis, settling-in, **Garden of Eden** sedentism, and the **“Slug Principle”** (effort-minimizing sedentism when food is abundant). Mobility is a security option among hunters; demographic **packing** segments ranges, shortens return intervals, forces hunters down the body-size chain toward plants; Flannery’s “broad spectrum revolution” re-read as **broad spectrum depression**. Plant agriculture does not solve the animal-protein packing problem. Later self-critique notes residual “little Gardens of Eden” assumptions in his own 1968 model (*New Perspectives* Post-Pleistocene Adaptations). Rival origin theories (Flannery, Hassan, Cohen, Hayden, packing) assign different meanings to the same archaeological pattern — hence need for [[middle-range-theory-archaeology|middle-range]] warrant independent of origin narratives.

- **Mithen cognitive-fluidity epilogue** (*[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* 1996, attributed): agriculture ~10 kya in independent centres is a **direct consequence** of [[cognitive-fluidity|cognitively fluid]] minds meeting terminal-Pleistocene environmental change — not a knowledge/technology threshold (natural-history intelligence ≥1.8 mya; Wadi Kubbaniya / Abu Hureyra plant intensity), not a global population crisis, climate necessary but not sufficient (Early Humans survived ice ages without farming). Four mental propensities: intensive plant tools (technical × natural history); plants/animals as prestige media (social × natural history; Hayden competition; Soffer storage-as-power); social relationships with plants/animals (domestication as social-like relations); plants/animals as manipulable artifacts (fire management). [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] sedentism as Bar-Yosef & Belfer-Cohen “point of no return.” “Seeds” of agriculture laid in the mind at the Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition; Neolithic is cultural **epilogue** to mind evolution. Early Near Eastern farmers show poorer health than forager forebears — farming as compulsion/opportunity structure, not progress. See [[cognitive-fluidity]].

## Debates

[[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]; archaeological debates on agricultural origins — promoted primary pair: [[oasis-natural-habitat-debate]] (oasis/propinquity vs Braidwood [[hilly-flanks]] / [[nuclear-area]]); packing/demographic-ecological alternatives densified from Binford 1983 above; climate and social models remain open.

### Braidwood — natural habitat / hilly flanks (*Prehistoric Men* 1959)

*(Attributed — [[braidwood-robert]], *[[braidwood-prehistoric-men-1959|Prehistoric Men]]* 3rd ed.)* The **food-producing revolution** is a technological-economic stage threshold: food-gathering/collecting → food-producing (later industrial). Preconditions = cultural receptiveness + promising environment; cause still unknown. **Oasis** rain-belt-retreat theory is stated in full and rejected as too simple. Positive case: **[[hilly-flanks]]** zone as **[[nuclear-area]]** where wild wheats, barley, and potential domesticates co-occur; climate then ≈ now; earliest villages (e.g. [[jarmo]]) and incipient candidates ([[natufian-culture]], [[karim-shahir]]) sit there rather than in pure oases or lower alluvium. Civilization later flowers in river lands via irrigation after farming spreads from the flanks — food production necessary but not sufficient for civilization. See [[incipient-cultivation]], [[neolithic-revolution]].

### Natufian foragers and the path to SW Asian food production ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013|Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013]])

The [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] is the standard terminal-Pleistocene forager complex immediately preceding Levantine PPNA farming. Positions in the 2013 symposium (not adjudicated):

- **Climate / Younger Dryas:** classic models link Late Natufian stress under YD to cultivation; Henry (2013) argues Levantine YD is longer and more cold- than drought-driven, and that Natufian emergence and Early→Late (~13.7 ka cal BP) **disconnect** from YD breakpoints — yet cold may still compress nut masts and force cereal storage; Harifian tracks harsh YD then collapses. Site-level counter-cases (Baaz trout; Ashkenazi Eynan habitats) and methodological skepticism (preface; Maher et al. 2011) — see [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]] and [[younger-dryas]].
- **Origins complexity:** Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris treat Natufian as multi-tradition complex, not simple *in situ* Geometric Kebaran→homeland expansion — see [[natufian-origins-core-periphery-debate]].
- **Subsistence without cultivation:** Rosen phytolith “Monocot Revolution” — Late grass/wetland monocots and small-seeded weeds without clear Late Natufian cultivation; Bar-Oz et al. gazelle specialization as cultural preference within Early Natufian, not YD climate tracking.
- **Sedentism first:** multi-season architecture and storage before morphological domestication — links [[sedentism]] and [[domestication]] as separable variables (also W&O; Mithen point-of-no-return).

### Wenke & Olszewski — *Patterns in Prehistory* (2007)

[[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007|Wenke & Olszewski]] densify the **origins-of-agriculture** debate as a mid-2000s world-prehistory survey (ch. 6; attribute as of 2007).

**Scope and definitions.** Systematic plant cultivation is recent (<1% of genus *Homo* existence); all Pleistocene societies shared hunting-gathering subsistence despite tool and brain-size change (attributed). Independent agricultural economies appeared in widely separated regions roughly between ~10,000 and 3,500 years ago (SW Asia wheat/barley; China rice/millet; Mexico maize; Andes potatoes) (attributed). Historically, largest complex civilizations rested mainly on six plant genera: wheat, barley, millet, rice, maize, potatoes (attributed pattern claim). **Agriculture** = systematic, purposeful human modification of plant/animal environments to increase productivity; **cultivation** (Blumler & Byrne) = growing plants (need not be domesticated); **domestication** (Rindos) = coevolutionary mutualism in which a taxon diverges from its gene pool under symbiotic protection/dispersal (attributed). Rindos position: human intention is neither archaeologically recoverable nor logically necessary; counter-position: intentional selection sped domestication almost from the beginning (attributed).

**Sedentism and consequences.** In SW Asia sedentary communities may appear ~1,000 years before domesticated wheat/barley; in Mesoamerica several plants were domesticating millennia before sedentary/agricultural villages (attributed). Village life correlates with higher fertility, more epidemic disease, family-based economic units, material accumulation, and strong "inertia" once established (Danish isotope shift from marine to agricultural diet after 4000 BC despite nearby marine richness — Tauber via W&O) (attributed).

**Rejected myths.** Single-place genius diffusion, extraterrestrial teaching, or farming-as-easier than hunting: farmers often have less leisure than hunter-gatherers; worldwide near-simultaneity and multi-millennial transitions make single-invention scenarios improbable (attributed; original-affluent-society theme).

**Causal model inventory (positions, as of W&O 2007).** Three recurrent factors: post-Pleistocene climate change; long-term population growth; accumulating technology/knowledge. Childe **oasis** hypothesis (end-Pleistocene drying forced propinquity) — weakened by wild cereal geography and early domestication timing; later climate-role models (McCorriston & Hole; Moore & Hillman) revive climate without full oasis package (attributed). **Natural habitat** (Peake & Fleure; Braidwood): cumulative interaction in wild-wheat/barley zones (Jarmo ~6500 BC type); Harlan 1966 wild-emmer harvest (~year's grain in weeks for a family) implies least incentive to farm in richest natural habitats (attributed). Packing / demographic-ecological models (cf. [[binford-lewis]]); Hayden resource-reliability / r-selected shift and feasting models; Richerson, Boyd & Bettinger — Pleistocene climates "hostile to agriculture," Holocene stability/moisture/CO₂ favoring plant-focused economies; Hodder symbolic *domus* domestication of the wild; Flannery broad-spectrum themes as surveyed (attributed). Ohalo II (~19 kya) shows wild barley/emmer use millennia before agriculture without producing agricultural lifeways then (attributed).

**Link to complexity.** Leisure-time explanation of civilization rejected: many agricultural groups never became states; Peru may have evolved early complexity without primary agriculture (attributed, ch. 7). Regional packages densify multi-centric pattern (Egypt SW Asian cereals; Indus; North China millets; Andean potatoes/maize after ~4000 B.C. — as of W&O 2007). See [[neolithic-revolution]], [[neolithic]], [[domestication]].

## After the Ice densification (Mithen 2003)

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven]], *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice]]* 2003; multi-regional narrative 20,000–5000 BC.)*

- **Rubicon framing:** Mithen treats 20,000–5000 BC as history’s “coming of age”; by 5000 BC many people farmed with domesticates, permanent villages/towns, and craft/priest/chief specialists — the foundational transition from hunting-gathering (attributed, ch. 1).
- **Fertile Crescent start:** narrative begins where first farmers, towns, and civilizations will arise (attributed); forest-steppe wild relatives of wheat, barley, peas, lentils, flax as cradle landscape (Zohary via Mithen).
- **Mechanism (plants):** wild vs domestic = shattering vs “wait for the harvester” (often single-gene); sickle-harvest + reseeding as selection pathway; Hillman & Davies simulations ~20–250 harvest–resow cycles to non-brittle wheat under ideal-to-realistic conditions (attributed).
- **Sedentism without farming first:** Early [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] villages as Bar-Yosef’s “point of no turning back”; not forced by overpopulation (attributed); YD collapses first sedentism experiment (~10,800 BC) before Holocene PPNA farming at [[jericho|Jericho]] ~9600 BC.
- **Ideology path:** Cauvin mother-goddess/bull thesis largely rejected as cult label, but ideology-before-economy gains support from Jerf el Ahmar / [[gobekli-tepe|Göbekli Tepe]]; Mithen links Karacadağ wild wheat (closest relative of modern domestic wheat ~1997 genetics — **date-stamp**) to ceremonial feeding — domestication possibly accidental by-product of gatherings, not only YD stress (theoretical position).
- **Multi-pathway Europe:** farmers leap-frog Balkans; [[lepenski-vir|Lepenski Vir]] florescence then collapse within ~50 km of farming settlements; hard agricultural labour contrasted with Mesolithic few-hours foraging (attributed, European chapters).
- **New Guinea parallel:** Flannery-style reliability (secure seasonal plant yields enabling social aggregation) as explanation for Kuk Swamp wetland investment — social as much as nutritional motives (attributed).
- **Health/progress:** early Near Eastern farmers poorer health than forager forebears; farming as compulsion/opportunity structure, not simple progress (consistent with Mithen 1996 cognitive epilogue already on this page).

Do not collapse multi-center packages into a single global event. Cross-links: [[domestication]], [[sedentism]], [[neolithic-revolution]], [[younger-dryas]].

## Ucko & Dimbleby 1969 densification

*(Attributed — *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969]]* seminar.)*

- **Ucko & Dimbleby:** Neolithic Revolution not a single catastrophic change; process over several thousand years with regional special characters; multi-origin recurrence; first agricultural settlement events dated ~9000–7000 B.C. (as of 1969).
- **Flannery:** early dry-farming not clear dietary improvement or stability gain; real consequences are means of production, labour division, stratification foundations, environmental irreversibility — see [[broad-spectrum-revolution]], [[flannery-kent-v]].
- **Darlington vs multi-regional papers:** Nuclear Zone monogenesis POSITION vs independent yam/chili/*Phaseolus*/Chinese packages in the same volume.
- **Piggott:** Europe’s initiation still owed to peoples, crops, herds from across the Hellespont/Aegean (*ex oriente lux* not mirage — 1969 POSITION); food refuse socially selected.

## From Roberts, *The Holocene* (1998)

*(Attributed — [[roberts-holocene-1998]].)*

Roberts treats agricultural origins as **independent innovations** across several regions with almost no overlap in domesticate lists — the Near East (wheat, barley; sheep, goat, cattle, pig), Mesoamerica (maize), China (rice at Ho-mu-tu, ~7500 Cal. yr BP), and probably highland New Guinea (Kuk swamp field drainage, ~6800 Cal. yr BP) — largely rejecting single-centre diffusionism (Griffith Taylor, Sauer). The **tempo differed sharply**: in the Near East the full package was adopted within a few centuries (Abu Hureyra's abrupt shift from >80% gazelle to ~70% sheep-goat, Legge 1996), whereas Mesoamerican maize took >3,500 years to become the staple. He stresses that environmental change alone does not explain why farming arose when and where it did (see [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]]), and that early European agriculture ([[deforestation|landnám]] clearance, the LBK loess frontier) spread partly by demic diffusion (van Andel and Runnels 1995).

## Cauvin densification — *Birth of the Gods* (2000)

*(Attributed — [[cauvin-jacques]], *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|The Birth of the Gods and the Origins of Agriculture]]* 2000; dates calibrated as of his tables.)*

- **Scope:** Near East = Levant + Anatolia; ~12,000–6300 BC staged transition; first synthesis stressing **calibrated** Near Eastern Neolithic dates (with Jacques Evin).
- **Sequence thesis:** [[revolution-of-symbols|Revolution of symbols]] (~9500 BC [[khiamian|Khiamian]] Woman/Goddess + Bull) **precedes** farming economy (~9000 BC) among PPNA corridor cultures ([[sultanian-culture|Sultanian]], [[aswadian-culture|Aswadian]], [[mureybetian-culture|Mureybetian]]). Farming is the **second step** of neolithisation.
- **Geography:** first cultivation restricted to Bar-Yosef’s [[levantine-corridor|Levantine corridor]] (Jordan–Damascus–middle Euphrates), not the whole hilly flanks at once.
- **Anti-prime-movers:** rejects general Near Eastern population-pressure explanation (larger but fewer settlements = regrouping, not pan-regional demographic explosion); climate/Younger Dryas desiccation not an important causal trigger of the Revolution (Postscript: YD significant ~10,600 BC at Abu Hureyra latitude; pushes people from desertifying zones).
- **PPNB package diffusion:** Early PPNB from northern Levant/Mureybetian evolution; middle PPNB cultural reunification (rectangular architecture, herding, perfected cultivation, [[skull-cult|skull cult]]); “Great Exodus” to Mediterranean, [[khirokitia|Cyprus]], Jezirah, and pastoral nomadism.
- **Definition:** keeps “Neolithic” as a **state** of productive economy (vs Watkins process definition); pre-agricultural sedentary villages are psycho-social precursors but not yet Neolithic in Cauvin’s sense.
- **Standing:** ideology-before-economy remains a research pole; full Goddess–Bull theology contested (see [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[mother-goddess]]).

### Sherratt — fourth-millennium second transformation (1997)

*(Attributed — [[sherratt-andrew]], *[[sherratt-economy-society-prehistoric-europe-1997]]. )* Beyond the initial farming transition, Sherratt models a **fourth-millennium BC** western Old World transformation — [[secondary-products-revolution|secondary products]], elite consumables, and core–periphery interaction with Near Eastern urbanism — as comparable in magnitude to the first agricultural revolution and as largely its consequence. Europe’s later distinctiveness (high livestock relative to humans; alcohol hospitality) is traced to this conjunction. See [[neolithic-revolution]], [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]].

### Diamond — *Guns, Germs, and Steel* (1997, attributed)

[[diamond-jared-m|Diamond]] makes the **rise and unequal spread of food production** the central ultimate bridge to guns, germs, and steel: independent centers vs late adopters vs non-adopters; founder-crop packages; push factors for farming; continental endowment of domesticable species; and **[[continental-axis]]** rates of package diffusion. Treat as popular secondary synthesis (~1990s evidence horizon), not primary agricultural archaeology. Debate: [[guns-germs-steel-geographic-determinism-debate]]. Source: [[diamond-guns-germs-steel-1997]].

## Scott densification — progress-narrative inversion (*Against the Grain*, 2017)

*(Attributed — [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]], *[[scott-against-the-grain-2017|Against the Grain]]* 2017. Political-scientist synthesis of specialist Near Eastern archaeology; positions, not wiki-voice findings. Do **not** collapse with [[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|Graeber & Wengrow]] as the same program — adjacency only: [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].)*

### Against the ascent-of-man package

- **Well-being inversion (position):** Agriculture was assumed a great step forward in well-being, nutrition, and leisure; Scott argues **something like the opposite was initially the case** (Preface). Hunters and gatherers “have never looked so good” (diet, health, leisure); agriculturalists “have never looked so bad” on the same measures — Sahlins-adjacent reappraisal; see [[original-affluent-society-debate]] (Introduction; Ch.3).
- **Drudgery (via Boserup et al.):** With possible exception of flood-recession (*décrue*) agriculture, farming was far more onerous than hunting and gathering; foragers shift to agriculture under population pressure or coercion, not opportunity (Introduction; Ch.2–3). Fixed-field cereal farmers confined to one starch plant’s annual metronome vs forager multi-web tempos; late Neolithic as “deskilling” / contraction of knowledge and diet (Ch.2).
- **Long lag:** ~4,000 years between key plant/animal domestications and first agrarian economies based on them; states only ~3100 BCE in Tigris–Euphrates, more than four millennia after crop domestication and [[sedentism]] — challenge to automatic cultivation→village→state sequences (Preface; Introduction; Ch.1). See [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[state-formation]].
- **Sedentism before farming / wetland towns:** Sedentism and early towns as products of wetland abundance, not irrigation and states; southern alluvium towns with little agriculture (Ch.1). States did **not** invent irrigation or crop domestication — both prestate; states later amplify via “state landscaping” (Introduction).

### Health, diet, and zoonoses of crowding

- **Paleopathology (symposium quote via Scott):** Nutritional stress becomes common only after high sedentism, density, and agricultural reliance — higher mortality; porotic hyperostosis / cribra orbitalia (iron-deficiency related); more severe enamel hypoplasia and infectious-disease pathologies (Ch.3).
- **Iron and cereal diets:** Preagricultural omega-6/omega-3 fatty acids facilitate iron uptake; cereal diets lack those fatty acids and inhibit iron uptake — anemia signatures under late Neolithic wheat/barley/millet, especially reproductive-age women; beriberi, pellagra, riboflavin deficiency, kwashiorkor attributed to Neolithic foodways (Ch.3). Coeval hunter-gatherers several inches taller on average; Abu Hureyra forager phase ~192 plant taxa (Ch.3).
- **Infant mortality:** ~40–50% among most early agriculturalists from diet-weakened vulnerability plus novel infections (Ch.3).
- **Demographic bottleneck then surge:** World population ~4 million in 10,000 BCE rising only to ~5 million by 5,000 BCE despite Neolithic sedentism/agriculture — then twentyfold growth to >100 million over next five millennia; Scott proposes the transition among the most lethal epidemiological periods; Mesopotamia a focal point of chronic/acute infections (Ch.3).
- **Crowd diseases as “civilizational effect”:** Virtually all infectious diseases from microorganisms specifically adapted to *H. sapiens* arose only in the past ~10 kya (many past ~5 kya) — cholera, smallpox, mumps, measles, influenza, chicken pox, perhaps malaria — from urbanism and agriculture; measles persistence needs ~300,000 population scale (Faroes case) (Ch.3). Epidemic disease as “loudest silence” in Neolithic archaeology; sudden unexplained abandonments likelier epidemics than gradual climate/salinization alone (position) (Ch.3).
- **Zoonoses of the *domus*:** Multispecies resettlement camp (livestock + commensals + humans) as first massive pathogen surge across the species barrier; shared-disease counts with domesticates (see [[animal-domestication]]); feces concentration and reinfection; mobile foragers leave parasites behind (Ch.3). *Note:* no dedicated `emerging-infectious-diseases` concept page yet — material filed here and under [[sedentism]] / [[animal-domestication]].

### Causation stance (not a new prime mover)

Scott rejects Boserupian “backs-to-the-wall” for Mesopotamia/Fertile Crescent (cultivation in abundance not scarcity; flood-retreat may invalidate pure toil premise) but accepts that planting/stock as **dominant** modes were avoided as long as possible because of work and fragile simplified landscapes; declines to adjudicate multi-millennial drivers (Younger Dryas “pistol” at Abu Hureyra contested). Accepts [[broad-spectrum-revolution]] archaeological reality while jury out on its causes; for agriculture proper “the jury is in” on avoidance-until-pressure logic (Ch.2–3). Full position: [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]].
