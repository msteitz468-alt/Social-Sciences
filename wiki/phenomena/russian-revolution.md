# Russian Revolution

## Definition and Scope

The political and social upheavals of 1917 in the Russian Empire that overthrew the Tsarist autocracy (February) and brought the Bolsheviks to power (October), and the subsequent civil-war and early-Soviet transformations through the late 1920s. This page's narrative spine for **1917–1929** is primarily [[carr-edward-hallett|E. H. Carr]]'s *[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979|The Russian Revolution: From Lenin to Stalin]]* (1979), cross-linked to Bolshevik primary texts, [[avrich-kronstadt-study|Avrich on Kronstadt]], and Service biographies.

Carr treats 1917–1929 as a single arc: dual power and October → civil war and [[war-communism]] → [[new-economic-policy|NEP]] → succession and [[left-opposition|opposition]] defeats → planning, [[five-year-plan|First Five-Year Plan]], and [[collectivization]] as "revolution from above." Broader comparative theory (e.g. [[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-study]]) is linked but not substituted for this narrative.

## Patterns and Findings

### Carr's opening frame (attributed)

Carr called 1917 a turning-point that "may well be assessed… as the greatest event of the twentieth century," the "first open challenge to the capitalist system," both consequence and cause of capitalism's decline, yet rooted in specifically Russian conditions (stagnant rural economy, peasantry, post-1890 foreign-capital industrialization, RSDWP 1897).

### 1905 and February–October 1917

Carr analyzes **1905** as three uncoordinated strands (liberal-constitutional, worker/Soviet, peasant) easily repressed. **February 1917** repeated those strands under war-weariness: Tsar abdicated; Provisional Government and Petrograd Soviet ([[dual-power]]); Carr qualifies dual power as understating a "total dispersal of authority" (local Soviets, factory committees, land seizures, soldiers' committees, army disintegration).

[[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]]'s April return and April Theses shattered the dual-power compromise (shared initially by Kamenev and [[stalin-joseph|Stalin]]): not a completed bourgeois revolution but transition toward Soviet power of workers and poor peasants. October 25/November 7 OS/NS: Red Guard coup; Provisional Government collapsed; Second Congress of Soviets (Bolshevik majority 399/649) adopted peace, land, and Sovnarkom decrees. January 1918: Constituent Assembly prevented from re-assembling — "the revolution had turned its back on the conventions of bourgeois democracy" (Carr).

### Civil war, two worlds, War Communism

Civil war (to spring 1920 for White defeat; Polish war 1920) hardened what Carr calls the stereotype of **two worlds** — capitalist and revolutionary — in irreconcilable contradiction. [[war-communism]] combined centralization/planning elements that partly endured with anti-market, requisition, and labour-compulsion elements rejected by NEP. [[red-terror]] and White terror entered the vocabulary; Cheka founded weeks after October.

### NEP and the 1920s contradictions

[[new-economic-policy|NEP]] (10th congress, March 1921) — tax in kind, market, "commanding heights" retained — was accepted as Kronstadt was crushed (Carr: revolt increased readiness for both NEP and faction ban). Recovery produced the [[scissors-crisis]] (1923) and later grain-marketing crises, splitting the party over industrialization vs peasant-oriented market.

### Succession, opposition, planning

Lenin's illness and Testament; Stalin as general secretary (April 1922); defeat of Trotsky then of the united [[left-opposition]] (1926–27); rise of [[socialism-in-one-country]] as official doctrine. [[five-year-plan|Planning]] and forced industrialization, then mass [[collectivization]] (1929–), closed the NEP era.

### Carr's closing judgment (Ch. 19, attributed)

Carr's synthesis: the revolution had a **hybrid and ambiguous** character. Industrialization and planning realized what he calls the "material, economic half" of socialism (he **rejects** reducing the planned economy to mere [[state-capitalism]]). But it was not Marx's free association of producers, nor the dictatorship of the proletariat as self-emancipation of workers, nor Lenin's transitional democratic dictatorship of workers and peasants: industrial and agrarian revolutions were **from above**. Socialism in a backward country with a weak proletariat could not be the socialism predicated on advanced-country proletarian unity. In advanced capitalist democracies the Russian model was largely destructive; in backward non-capitalist countries it proved "more pervasive and more productive" as leader of revolt against western capitalist domination. "Its record was flawed and ambiguous. But it has been the source of more profound and more lasting repercussions throughout the world than any other historical event of modern times."
