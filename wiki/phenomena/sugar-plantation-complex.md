# Sugar Plantation Complex

## Definition and Scope

The historically evolving system linking cane cultivation, mill processing, coerced and free labor, shipping, refining, and metropolitan consumption of sucrose — treated by [[mintz-sidney-w|Mintz]] in *[[mintz-sweetness-and-power-1985|Sweetness and Power]]* as the production side of a single production–consumption circuit. Related institution: [[plantation]]; industrial image: [[factory-in-the-field]].

Not identical to a language, a people, or a genetic population (four-way non-identity): it is an economic-institutional complex.

## Measurement

Mintz works with trade and duty series, planter and political-economy pamphlets, consumption estimates, and secondary economic history rather than a single index. Metropolitan per-capita lb/year and caloric share are his principal consumption measures; production is tracked by geography of industry, slave trade, and mill technology.

## Patterns and Findings

*(Attributed to Mintz 1985 unless noted as his citation.)*

- **Geographic succession:** New Guinea domestication → India crystallization → Arab Mediterranean (Sicily, Spain, Maghrib, etc.) → Spanish/Portuguese Atlantic islands → New World dominance after the late sixteenth century; British Caribbean (Barbados and after) as major supplier to England.
- **Labor:** early New World plantations staffed mostly with enslaved Africans; post-emancipation indenture (India, China, etc.) and legislation constraining freed people's alternatives (as Mintz recounts for British West Indies).
- **Dual structure:** mass export agro-industry coexists with small-scale traditional brown sugars (*raspadura*/*rapadou*, Jamaican loaves) consumed by the poor — observed by Mintz in Jamaica and Haiti.
- **Metropole link:** without external consumers, land, labor, and capital would not have been funneled into cane at Caribbean scale; production and consumption are "neatly interdigitated."
- **Andean/Colombian agribusiness pole (Taussig 1980):** southern Cauca Valley cane expansion over Afro-Colombian free-peasant tree agriculture; piecework wage cutting; devil-contract barrenness lore; 2009 afterlife as biofuel sugar under neo-liberal short contracts ([[taussig-devil-commodity-fetishism-study]]; society [[cauca-valley-afro-colombians]]). Complements Mintz's Caribbean–British circuit rather than duplicating it.

## Explanatory Theories

- **Mintz:** meaning and power in food; drug foods and proletarian diet; plantation as industrial form.
- **Taussig:** plantation as cosmogonic site of use-value vs exchange-value conflict; cane as fetishised "Green Monster."
- **Adjacent:** [[world-systems-analysis]] / [[capitalist-world-economy]] (core–periphery commodity circuits); [[anthropological-political-economy]] (social labor, historical interconnection); Williams thesis on capitalism and slavery (engaged as literature Mintz cites).

## Interaction

Enables mass [[sweetness]] in European diets; articulates with tea/coffee/chocolate trades; feeds [[proletarian-hunger-and-drug-foods]]; intersects slavery, indenture, free trade vs mercantilist preference, and modern HFCS/sweetener competition (Mintz's late chapters). In Cauca (Taussig), intersects free-peasant moral economy, Liberal/Conservative land politics, and trade-union militancy of the 1970s.

## Debates

Capitalism vs slavery timing; how early "industrial" the plantation was; British case vs other European sugar paths; gender and domestic labor in consumption (later food studies).
