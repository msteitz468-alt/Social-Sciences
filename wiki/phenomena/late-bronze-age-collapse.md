# Late Bronze Age Collapse

## Definition and Scope

The **Late Bronze Age collapse** denotes the multi-decade end of the interconnected palace-centered and imperial systems of the Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean roughly between the late thirteenth and mid-twelfth centuries BCE. [[cline-eric-h|Cline]] (2014) treats **1177 BCE** (Ramses III year 8; major Sea Peoples confrontation with Egypt) as convenient scholarly shorthand — analogous to AD 476 for Rome — for a process spanning approximately 1225–1130 BCE, not a single-year cataclysm.

This page is the **case-specific** phenomenon page; general theory of collapse lives on [[societal-collapse]].

## Measurement

- Site destruction layers and abandonment (with care: earthquake vs warfare signatures).
- End of palace administrations and scripts (e.g., Linear B; local cuneiform practices at Ugarit).
- Disruption of long-distance trade (tin, copper, luxury goods).
- Contemporary texts: Ugarit last letters; Egyptian Merneptah and Ramses III inscriptions; Hittite records where they cease.
- Regional unevenness is expected — not every site fails the same way or year.

## Patterns and Findings

Primary synthesis ingested: [[cline-1177-bc-2014|Cline 2014]] — mechanism claims **attributed**.

**Dickinson 1994 (attributed, Aegean focus).** *[[dickinson-aegean-bronze-age-1994|The Aegean Bronze Age]]* rejects invasion and mono-catastrophe (including Thera as Second Palace terminator) as primary engines; prefers cumulative **cultural process** for Mycenaean systems collapse and notes dual Aegean collapse episodes vs Near Eastern trajectories. Mid-1990s horizon — pair with Cline for Eastern Mediterranean multi-causal synthesis.

### Prior system

Cline reconstructs a [[late-bronze-age-international-system]] (~15th–early 12th c. BCE) linking Egypt, Hittites, Mitanni then Assyria, Babylon, Canaanite city-states (including [[ugarit]]), Cyprus (Alashiya), and Aegean [[minoan-civilization|Minoan]]/[[mycenaean-civilization|Mycenaean]] centers through diplomacy (Amarna “Great Powers,” dynastic marriage, greeting-gifts), commerce (copper, tin, prestige goods; shipwrecks such as [[uluburun]]), and multi-archive correspondence (Amarna, Hattusa, Ugarit).

### Process timing

Civilizations still flourishing c. 1225 BCE had begun to vanish by 1177 BCE and were almost completely gone by c. 1130 BCE in Cline’s summary. Destructions are **not simultaneous**; some Levantine horizons (Megiddo, Lachish debates) may fall as late as ~1130. Egypt under Merneptah (1207) and Ramses III (1177) records victories against named foreign groups but emerges weakened; Ramses III assassinated 1155 (Harem Conspiracy — forensic support Cline reports).

### Selected site outcomes (Cline synthesis)

| Region / site | Outcome (attributed to excavators via Cline) |
|---|---|
| [[ugarit]] | Violent destruction c. 1190–1185 under Ammurapi; not reoccupied ~650 years; Yon: attack not final earthquake; late letters on enemy ships |
| [[hattusa]] | Early 12th-c. end; public buildings emptied then burned (Seeher); Kashka likelier than Sea Peoples after drought/famine/trade stress |
| [[pylos]] | Palace fire c. 1180; agent unknown; long regional depopulation (Davis) |
| [[mycenae]] / Tiryns | Quake damage c. 1250 and later fire/retrenchment; continued reduced occupation at some sites |
| [[troy]] VIIA | Fire c. 1190–1180; warfare markers; agent disputed |
| Cyprus | Plural agents and hybridization models revise simple Aegean-invasion story |
| Mesopotamia | Some destructions by Elamites (Shutruk-Nahhunte), not Sea Peoples |
| Philistia | Mixed: violent replacement at some sites, peaceful transitions at others (Yasur-Landau hybridization thesis) |

### What was lost / what regenerated

Loss of palace administrations, Linear B and the *wanax* in Greece (Maran); end of cuneiform practice in the Levant with Ugarit’s fall; dismantling of elite international networks (Van De Mieroop via Cline). Iron Age regenerations include Neo-Hittite states, Phoenicians, Philistines, Israelites, later Greek polities; alphabet and expanded iron use in Cline’s aftermath narrative. Dever (via Cline): Canaan’s “Dark Age” as catalyst of a new age, not mere void.

## Explanatory Theories

| Position | Core claim | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sea Peoples monocausal invasion | Mobile invaders destroy LBA centers | Older popular narrative; Cline rejects as sole cause |
| Drought/famine primary | Climate stress drives collapse | Textual famine + proxies (Kaniewski, Drake, Finkelstein/Langgut/Litt as of Cline 2014) — contributory, not sufficient alone |
| Earthquake storm | Seismic cluster ~1225–1175 BCE | [[archaeoseismology]]; contributory; many sites reoccupied at reduced level |
| Internal rebellion | Local revolts end palaces | Hard to prove widely (Hazor as exemplum) |
| Trade-cut / overdependence | Cutting routes or over-reliance on long-distance trade | Singer, Monroe; tin≈oil (Bell) |
| Sherratt decentralization | Sea Peoples as structural outcome of trade evolution subverting palaces | [[sherratt-susan]]; Bell demurs as wholesale cause |
| Drews warfare-change | Changes in warfare explain burnings | Reported; not Cline’s preferred sole cause |
| Renfrew systems collapse | Admin/elite/economy fail; settlement shift; century-scale | Cline adopts as outcome description |
| Multi-causal "perfect storm" | Concurrent stressors + multiplier/domino + interdependence | **Cline 2014 preferred synthesis**; complexity/hyper-coherence with caveat |

## Historiography

Maspero-era narratives built destruction-migration stories from epigraphy alone. Late twentieth- and early twenty-first-century work (Sandars; Drews; conference literature; Cline’s synthesis as of 2014) multiplies causes and stresses the prior globalized system. Collection coverage: one major synthesis (Cline 2014); primary excavation monographs and post-2014 paleoclimate/aDNA updates not yet ingested.
