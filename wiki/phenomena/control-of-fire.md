# Control of Fire

Deliberate production, maintenance, and use of fire by hominins. Load-bearing for the [[cooking-hypothesis]]; **dating is the core empirical fault line** of the [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]].

## Definition and Scope

Control means more than opportunistic use of natural fire: the ability to maintain and (eventually) make fire for warmth, light, protection, and cooking. Wrangham treats cooking as effectively inseparable from permanent fire control — an unlikely long delay between capturing fire and cooking.

## Measurement

| Evidence class | What it shows (as Wrangham 2009 surveys) | Limits |
|---|---|---|
| Secure late hearths (≤~250 ka) | Abri Pataud ~40 ka; Abri Romani >60 hearths to 76 ka; Vanguard Cave >93 ka; Klasies 60–90 ka; Sodmein 109–127 ka; Kalambo ~180 ka; Hayonim ~250 ka | Dates *H. sapiens* span, not origin of control |
| Middle Pleistocene | Beeches Pit ~400 ka (Gowlett); Schöningen ~400 ka spears + fires (Thieme); Gesher Benot Ya’aqov **~790 ka** burnt seeds/wood/flint clusters — oldest "confident" control in his survey | Still far later than *erectus* origin on his clock |
| Lower Paleolithic hints (1–1.5 Ma) | Swartkrans burnt bone; Chesowanja heated clay; Gadeb rocks; Koobi Fora colored patches | Possibility only; lightning/natural fire alternative |
| Biological proxies | Small guts/teeth/jaws of *H. erectus* as cooked-diet adaptation | Inference, not direct fire trace |

**Preservation biases** (as Wrangham argues): meat can cook without charring bone; Hadza one-use fires leave little; limestone caves erode; some recent occupations lack fire signs despite expected use — so **absence of evidence is not evidence of absence**.

## Patterns and Findings

- Ethnographic: cooking and fire as human universals (Tylor; Andamanese fire as human distinctiveness via Radcliffe-Brown epigraph).
- Behavioral package Wrangham attaches to early fire: ground sleep (Turkana Boy context), reduced tree dependence, predator deterrence, hair loss + endurance running enabled later, self-domestication around the hearth (Ch. 8 — all attributed).

## Explanatory Theories

- **[[cooking-hypothesis]]:** permanent control by ~1.9–1.8 Ma, dated via anatomy.
- **Late-fire positions:** regular cooking only with UP or mid-Middle Pleistocene (see debate Position C).
- **Symbolic accounts:** Lévi-Strauss on raw/cooked as nature/culture — orthogonal to dating.

## Interaction

Fire control interacts with diet ([[obligate-cooked-diet]], [[energy-theory-of-cooking]]), encephalization ([[expensive-tissue-hypothesis]]), time budgets and [[sexual-division-of-labor]], pair-bonds ([[cooking-and-pair-bonding]]), and (on Wrangham's reading) ground sleep, hair loss, and endurance running. Links forager ethnography of hearths to paleoanthropological origin stories without equating living foragers with Pleistocene populations.

## Debates

Primary standing dispute: [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]] (early biological dating vs late archaeological minimalism vs meat-first [[hunting-hypothesis]]). Adjacent: [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]] on social packages once fire is assumed.

## Klein — *The Human Career* (2nd ed., 1999)

*(Attributed — [[klein-human-career-1999]], [[klein-richard]]; as of Klein 1999. A more skeptical
counterpoint to the cooking-hypothesis dating of early fire.)*

Klein is cautious about early controlled fire. He treats the occasional **burned bones first
appearing in Swartkrans Member 3** as, at most, "incipient human control over fire" — not
locally concentrated and with no associated hearths — and does not accept secure evidence for
routine, controlled hearth fire until considerably later in the *Homo erectus*/archaic-*Homo*
record. This tempers strong readings (e.g. [[cooking-hypothesis|Wrangham's]]) that place habitual
cooking-grade fire at the base of the genus (see [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]], [[swartkrans]]).
