# Work–family conflict

## Definition and Scope

**Work–family conflict** here names the clash between the urgency systems of paid employment and of family care when both adults (or a single adult) hold jobs without institutional redesign of either sphere. In *[[hochschild-second-shift-1989|The Second Shift]]*, [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]] treated this not as private personality failure but as the lived form of a [[stalled-revolution|stalled revolution]]: women changed; workplaces and many men lagged (attributed). Related but not identical: the [[second-shift|second shift]] (unpaid load after paid work) and [[leisure-gap|leisure gap]] (unequal free time).

Scope of the ingested source: U.S. heterosexual dual-earner couples with young children, 1976–88 fieldwork, middle-class skew. Other forms (single parents, same-sex couples, parents of older children) are noted as also facing second-shift issues but not equally sampled.

## Measurement

| Approach | Role in this wiki's evidence |
|---|---|
| Time-use diaries (Szalai, Robinson, Walker & Woods, etc.) | Document double day / leisure inequality (Appendix synthesis) |
| Intensive couple interview + home observation | Mechanisms: strategies, myths, gratitude (Hochschild primary) |
| Task inventories and share percentages | Study-internal operationalization of sharing |
| Secondary child-development reviews (NAS, Hoffman) | Effects of maternal employment — mixed/reassuring as cited |

Self-report of "balance" can diverge from observed load ([[family-myth|family myths]]).

## Patterns and Findings

**Convergent (attributed, multi-study time-use base):** working women in mid-20th-c. industrial societies often carried more combined paid+unpaid hours than men; husbands of employed wives did not fully compensate.

**Hochschild study (single-study, attributed):** women more torn; ~20% of men shared equally; women more daily rigid tasks and managerial responsibility; class shapes *how* strain bites (money/day care vs career clocks) while dual gender ideals run across class; sharing associated with happier marriages in her sample; unresolved conflict managed by myth or separation.

**Not wiki-voice:** that maternal employment generally harms children (source cites literature against consistent ill effects while insisting care work is undervalued).

## Explanatory Theories

| Account | Claim (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Stalled revolution (Hochschild) | Incomplete gender change; structural + cultural lag |
| Gender strategy | Micro plans linking ideology, feeling, and action |
| Economy of gratitude | Conflict over recognition of gifts, not only tasks |
| Pocketbook / balancing | Relative earnings reshape power and home work unevenly; not simple "who earns more does less" |
| Job culture vs family culture | Expanding job culture crowds care norms |
| Gender as primary frame (Ridgeway 2011) | Family-devotion vs work-devotion schemas + ideal-worker structures make conflict especially acute for mothers; status beliefs and sex categorization organize household labor and feed [[maternal-wall]] (*[[ridgeway-framed-by-gender-2011|Framed by Gender]]*) |

## Debates

- How much has the leisure gap closed since the 1980s? (Method and cohort dependent.)
- Is the primary deficit male participation, workplace policy, or both?
- Does equalizing hours without valuing care produce "workaholism à deux" (Hochschild's third tension)?
- Sampling: WEIRD / middle-class dual-earner bias in classic studies including this one.
