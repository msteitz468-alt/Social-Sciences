# Juvenile Delinquency

## Definition and Scope

Youth law-breaking and related conduct as a social problem. In *[[park-burgess-city-1925|The City]]* Ch V, [[park-robert-e|Park]] treats delinquency as bound to community organization and urban social control, not only individual pathology: "**Delinquency is, in fact, in some sense the measure of the failure of our community organizations to function**" (attributed).

## Measurement

Juvenile/morals court records; case studies (Park cites studies claiming ~50% of delinquencies from broken homes — attributed report); area coincidence with mobility/deterioration; gang mapping (Thrasher ~1000 Chicago gangs — program reference); Burgess/Buchan "delinquency triangle" (home of girl, home of companion, place of delinquency) distinguishing neighborhood, mobility, and promiscuity forms.

## Patterns and Findings

(Attributed.)

- Family/neighborhood folkway control undermined by cities, industry, transport/communication.
- Auto, press, motion pictures as disorganizing instrumentalities (Park's strong formulations).
- Migration (including Negro northward migration as discussed by Park) emancipating yet disorganizing for communities and demoralizing for younger generation.
- Slums as adventure spaces rivaling playgrounds; gangs may form character more than church/school outside home.
- Reform: re-education as finding group/vocation/plan realizing fundamental wishes; delinquency primarily **group** not individual problem.
