# Priesthood

## Definition and Scope

Specialized religious functionaries and the organized ecclesiastical systems they form. In Spencer’s *Principles of Sociology* Part III (Andreski abridgement), the priesthood is treated as an evolving social institution from undifferentiated origins to hierarchical churches.

## Cross-Cultural Variation

*As Spencer illustrated (19th-c. comparative compilation; attributed, not modern ethnography):*

- **Undifferentiated stage:** patriarchal or chiefly roles fuse political and sacred functions; sorcerer–priest–doctor as one person in simple tribes (*Principles* Parts I and III).
- **Genesis (Spencer):** propitiation of powerful dead (ghost-theory of dead chiefs) → specialized deputies, kin of chiefs, or castes of priests; deputation and inheritance elaborate the role.
- **Polytheistic / monotheistic priesthoods:** Spencer linked polytheism to fission and conquest (multiple cults/gods); monotheism to competition and dominance of one cult — with incomplete purity of monotheism noted.
- **Hierarchies:** ecclesiastical organization tends to **isomorphism** with political structure (compound societies → compound church hierarchies); monastic and graded clerical ranks as further differentiation.
- **Social bond:** shared cult as cohesion; priests in war; spiritual vs temporal power struggles; industrialism and independent ethics erode sacerdotal control without abolishing “mystery” (Spencer’s agnostic close in the abridged prospect chapter).
- **Moral influence:** Spencer stressed **obedience** and cult maintenance over what modern readers call private morals; labour discipline, ascetic rules, taboos as ecclesiastical products.

## Theoretical Treatments

- **Spencer ([[social-evolutionism]]):** Ecclesiastical institutions differentiate from political headship over time; form part of the wider [[sustaining-distributing-regulating-systems|regulating]] and moral-cohesive apparatus; track [[social-compounding|compounding]] and shift with [[militant-type-of-society|militant]] vs [[industrial-type-of-society|industrial]] activity. Ghost-propitiation origin is Spencer’s theory, not wiki-voice history of religion.
- **Comte (ingested separately):** [[theological-military-system]] stages fetichism → polytheism → monotheism as intellectual-social states — **related evolutionary-religious narrative, different master key**; do not merge priesthood pages with Comtean stages without attribution.
