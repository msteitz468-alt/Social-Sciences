# Court Society

## Definition and Scope

**Court society** is the figurational order centred on a princely or royal court: a dense web of interdependent nobles, officials, and attendants competing under the ruler's eyes for favour, rank, and resources. In [[elias-norbert|Elias]]'s analysis (*[[elias-civilizing-process-1939|The Civilizing Process]]*; later *The Court Society*), it is a historically specific institution mediating between feudal warrior society and modern state societies — the social site of [[courtization]] and of tightening manners codes ([[threshold-of-shame]], civilité).

## Cross-Cultural Variation

Elias's core cases are Western European (French royal court central; comparative English, German territorial, Italian). Other court traditions (imperial China, Ottoman, colonial vice-regal) are not the 1939 evidence base — flag as open comparative extensions.

**Balinese classical court (Geertz 1980, attributed).** *[[geertz-negara-study|Negara]]* reconstructs the *puri* as [[exemplary-center]] of a [[theatre-state]]: palace as temple/microcosm; competitive cremation and status pageantry; gentry houses ranked by [[sinking-status]]; etiquette and honor as political law of alliance. Distinct from Elias’s Versailles path (monopoly of force + courtization of warriors): Geertz stresses pomp as primary business and institutional *dispersion* rather than progressive monopolization. Not a substitute for Elias — a comparative SE Asian case.

## Theoretical Treatments

- **Elias (attributed):** Court as figuration of mutual observation and dependence; etiquette as weapon and constraint; warrior affects remodelled into courtly self-control; link to [[monopoly-mechanism]] and [[royal-mechanism]].
- **Geertz (attributed):** Court as site of exemplary display; “poetics of power”; king as still point and impresario (*[[geertz-negara-1980|Negara]]*).
- Other court historiographies (art, diplomacy, household accounts) enter as they are ingested — not collapsed into Elias.
