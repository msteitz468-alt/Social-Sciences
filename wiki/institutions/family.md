# Family

## Definition and Scope

This page holds **[[comte-auguste|Comte]]'s social-static doctrine of the family** from Book VI ch. V of the *Positive Philosophy* ([[comte-positive-philosophy-martineau-1853|Martineau]]), plus later theoretical and ethnographic densifications (Maine, Lowie, Engels/Morgan, Thomas–Znaniecki, Lynds, Hochschild, Zelizer, Portes–Rumbaut, Anderson, and African structural analysis via *[[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-1950|ASKAM]]* 1950). It is still not a full HRAF-style inventory of living kinship systems. Comte treated the family as the elementary unit of social science and as the germ of the social organism.

## Cross-Cultural Variation

Partial: [[polish-peasants|Polish peasant]] family organization as documented by Thomas & Znaniecki (early 20th c. materials) is a dense documentary family case beyond evolutionary theoretical sequences. [[middletown|Middletown]] (Lynd & Lynd, fieldwork 1924–25) adds an industrial Midwestern nuclear-household case: romance ideology vs thin companionship, rising divorce, classed birth-control knowledge, youth autonomy via school/car/movies, and housework commercialization (attributed, *[[lynd-middletown-1929|Middletown]]*). Not documented from Comte source alone. Comte's claims are universalizing program statements about "all ages" and "all forms of marriage," not HRAF-style comparison.

**African comparative densification (ASKAM 1950, mid-century fieldwork moments):** structural types range from father-right Nguni polygynous “houses,” cognatic Lozi villages without corporate lineage, Ashanti matrilineal sibling-group primacy with complementary fatherhood, Bemba matrilineal extended domestic groups, Yakö/Nuba double-descent households, to Nuer segmentary-patrilineal clusters built by bridewealth and levirate. See Theoretical Treatments (ASKAM) and [[marriage]]; not a full ethnographic inventory of living African families.

## Theoretical Treatments

### Maine (attributed — *Ancient Law*, 1861)

[[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]]’s [[patriarchal-theory]]: archaic society’s unit is the **Family** under absolute male ascendant power (*patria potestas*), not the free individual; Family → Gens → Tribe → Commonwealth. Members except the head live in **status** (no independent property or contract capacity); progressive legal history emancipates them toward **[[status-to-contract|contract]]**. Agnation, adoption, perpetual tutelage of women, and the family as corporation are Roman-centred correlates. Source: [[maine-ancient-law-1861]]. Contested on [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].

### Lowie (attributed — *Primitive Society*, 1920)

[[lowie-robert-h|Lowie]] argued that the **bilateral individual family** (husband, wife, immature children) is an **omnipresent** social unit for the ethnographic present: kinship terminology recognizes both parental lines (Morgan himself vs McLennan); customary law usually assigns functions on both sides; spouses form a universal economic partnership. Unilineal naming ([[sib]] membership, matronymy/patronymy) does not erase the other side’s jural and affective roles. The family is **chronologically prior** to the unilateral sib; simpler cultures are often sibless. Form varies (residence [[matrilocal-residence|matrilocal]]/[[patrilocal-residence|patrilocal]], polygyny/polyandry as minority practices, looser divorce) without dissolving the unit. Source: [[lowie-primitive-society-1920]] · [[lowie-primitive-society-study]]. Opposes Morgan-school submersion of the family in gens/group-marriage stages ([[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]).

### Comte (attributed — full fidelity on hierarchy)

In Book VI ch. V Comte argued:

1. **True social unit.** Society is not composed of individuals; "the true social unit is certainly the family," reducible if necessary to the elementary couple. Every system must be composed of elements of the same nature as itself.
2. **Germ of the social organism.** The family is intermediate between individual and species; by it "Man comes forth from his mere personality, and learns to live in another." Association always requires diversities and inequalities.
3. **Two orders of relations.** The sociological theory of the family reduces to (a) subordination of the sexes, which institutes the family, and (b) subordination of ages, which maintains it.
4. **Sex hierarchy.** Comte asserted the "natural subordination of the woman" as fundamental principle of marriage "under all forms of marriage, in all ages," to be based by Positive philosophy on knowledge of the individual and social organism. He claimed biological philosophy teaches radical physical and moral sex differences; compared women especially to a state of "perpetual infancy" relative to men; held sex equality "incompatible with all social existence" because each sex has special permanent functions; asserted women's "relative inferiority" for continuous intense mental labour and "radical inaptitude" for government; assigned women superior spontaneous sympathy and a moderating social function while male reason supplies "necessary and invariable preponderance."
5. **Crisis of the family.** Contemporary assaults on the family are "alarming symptoms" of temporary social disorganization; only Positive philosophy can re-establish the family conception on an immutable foundation.
6. **Ages and continuity.** Parent–child subordination is the type of wise social co-ordination and school of social life for obedience and command; domestic organization establishes social perpetuity and respect for predecessors.

These claims are **theoretical positions of Comte**, not findings and not wiki voice. They are recorded because they are load-bearing for early sociological imagery of the social organism and for later feminist and critical reception.

### Spencer (attributed — program only in this ingest)

In *Principles of Sociology* (Andreski abridgement), Spencer held that **development of the family stands first** in the sociological order of inquiry: forms of sexual relation (promiscuity, polyandry, polygyny, monogamy; exo- and endogamy), then parental and filial relations, in their effects on race maintenance and public life (Part I, Scope). Andreski omitted much Domestic Institutions material; this page does **not** yet hold Spencer’s full domestic-institution doctrine. Sex division of labour under militant vs industrial conditions appears in Part IV industrial chapters as attributed illustration, not as kinship system inventory.

### Engels / Morgan (attributed — *Origin of the Family*, 1884/1891)

[[engels-friedrich|Engels]], reworking [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]], treats the family as an **active historical form** that advances with society while kinship terminologies lag as passive records (Morgan; Marx extends the lag thesis to political/juridical/religious systems). Program: history is dual production of means of life **and** of human beings; social form depends on labor stage and family stage.

**Evolutionary sequence** (THEORY, not finding):

1. **Consanguine family** — reconstructed from Hawaiian kinship; extinct as living form.
2. **[[group-marriage|Punaluan / group marriage]]** — sets of co-husbands/co-wives; alleged origin of the matrilineal [[gens]]; fourth edition notes Morgan over-generalized this as a necessary universal stage.
3. **[[pairing-family]]** — unstable pair bond, easy dissolution, typically under [[mother-right]]; form of barbarism; America as “classic soil.”
4. **Patriarchal family** — transitional (e.g. Roman *familia* with *patria potestas*); contains slavery/serfdom “in germ” (Marx via Engels).
5. **[[monogamy|Monogamous family]]** — form of civilization; male supremacy; purpose to produce undisputed heirs of [[private-property]]; accompanied (Engels argues) by hetaerism/prostitution and sexual double standard.

**Mechanism of mother-right’s overthrow:** pastoral wealth and male ownership of herds → demand for agnatic heirs → father-right and monogamy of the woman; Engels calls this “the world historical defeat of the female sex.” **First class oppression** coincides with oppression of the female sex by the male in monogamy (Engels; cell-form of civilized contradictions).

**Bourgeois vs proletarian marriage:** property-conditioned marriage of convenience among possessors; among the propertyless proletariat, Engels claims foundations of typical monogamy (property, male legal supremacy) fall away and sex-love can become the rule. **Future:** with social ownership of means of production, family ceases as economic unit; public child-rearing; monogamy “realized also for men” as prostitution’s economic basis disappears — theoretical prediction, not finding.

Evidence class: second-hand ethnography (Morgan) + classical/literary history + kinship-term inference. See [[iroquois]] for the pairing-family/classificatory type case **as of mid-19th-c. documentation**. Full unilinear ladder is contested: [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]. Source: [[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884]].

### Radcliffe-Brown & Africanist structural analysis (attributed — ASKAM 1950)

*[[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-1950|African Systems of Kinship and Marriage]]* densifies the **elementary family** as analytic unit and African domestic-group variation without replacing this page’s theoretical history.

**Radcliffe-Brown (Introduction):**

1. **Elementary family** — father, mother, and their children — is the basic unit of kinship structure; all kinship/affinity ties are traced through parents, siblings, spouse, or children. Most mature men belong to two elementary families (as son/brother and as husband/father), generating first-, second-, and third-order relationships.
2. **Compound families** — remarriage with children from prior unions, and especially African **polygyny**: full siblings vs half-siblings are generally socially important; mother-and-children units appear as separate “houses” (*indlu*) in Zulu/Swazi polygynous compounds.
3. **Pater ≠ genitor** — social fatherhood usually fixed by marriage (*pater est quem nuptiae demonstrant*); African regions with woman–woman marriage (woman as *pater*, lover as genitor); descent in the essay is social, not biological.
4. **Extended domestic groups** — patrilineal extended family (sons remain, bring wives); Bemba matrilineal extended family (man, wife, daughters, daughters’ husbands and children) that fissions when a man leaves parents-in-law with wife and children.
5. **Structural units** — full sibling group of one flesh and blood as universal unit; mother–children as more extended unit; under mother-right the mother–children unit is jurally in the woman’s sibling group, under father-right in a group of brothers with wives and children ([[mother-right-and-father-right]]).
6. **Range** — wide-range vs narrow-range recognition as a primary classifier of kinship systems; [[classificatory-system-of-relationship|classificatory terminology]] facilitates wide range.
7. **Colonial change** — first effects of administration, missions, and economy destroy kinship obligations; new policeman/priest sanctions “much less effective” than kinsmen speaking with ancestral authority (position/diagnosis).

**Gluckman (Lozi vs Zulu):** Lozi — unstable parental family; father in law is the **genitor**; no corporate unilineal group; child “belongs to both sides”; free choice of residence as grown child; wives not inherited. Zulu — stable legal family; cattle transfer rights to *all* children of wives to husband and agnates whoever the genitors; corporate agnatic lineage; levirate/sororate reinforce permanence. Shows how “family” jural content co-varies with [[unilineal-descent]] and [[marriage-payments]].

**Fortes (Ashanti):** matrilineal corporate *abusua* with complementary paternity; mother–child and uterine sibling bonds strongest; *wofa* (MB) sole legal authority in segment; father has duties of support/education without legal custody after divorce; levirate/heirship in segment (*yafunu*); not equal [[double-descent]].

**Richards (Central Bantu):** matrilineal family-structure types (father–daughter grand-family among Bemba–Bisa; Mayombe–Kongo bride-removal with children’s return to MB; Yao–Cewa sororal cores; Ila cattle mixes) — uneasy two-sided balance under matriliny; “qualified” mother-right systems show more tension than pure poles.

**Forde (Yakö) / Nadel (Nuba):** elementary family nucleus of household under [[double-descent]]; polygyny with separate wives’ houses; half-siblings share patri-group but diverge matrilineally (Yakö); dual membership and incomplete dual forms (Nyaro vs Tullishi).

Full claims on [[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-study]]; society pages for case peoples.

## Key Studies

[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-study|Morgan, *Systems of Consanguinity* (1871)]] is the founding comparative study of kinship **terminology systems** (descriptive vs classificatory; schedules; conjectural family evolution). Comte Book VI ch. V and Engels *Origin* are theoretical reconstructions. Morgan’s *Ancient Society* / full *League of the Iroquois* await primary ingest.



### Thomas & Znaniecki (attributed — *Polish Peasant* Vol I, 1918)

[[thomas-william-isaac|Thomas]] and [[znaniecki-florian|Znaniecki]] analyze the **[[polish-peasants|Polish peasant]] family** as the core [[primary-group|primary group]] of their monographic baseline (*[[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-study]]*):

1. **Family-group vs marriage-group.** Larger family includes blood- and law-relatives to a variable limit (often fourth degree); marriage-group is the pair with children. Unity is a living social group, not genealogical tree or ancestor cult.
2. **Solidarity.** Irreducible assistance and control; marital norm of **respect**, not romantic love; parental authority as group representation; community opinion reinforces family.
3. **Marriage standpoints.** Familial (universal marriage, partner control), community/religious/professional, economic (land, dowry, class, America), sexual (sex subordinated to institution; staged rise of love-selection).
4. **Disintegration under modern change.** Isolation of marriage-group and individualization under emigration, industry, and class mobility; letters as performances of solidarity under separation ([[personal-documents-method|bowing letter]]).
5. **Economy.** Land as familial temporary management; tendency to economic "advance" individualizes property and narrows solidarity.

Claims are **documentary-analytic positions about early twentieth-century Polish peasant materials**, not cross-cultural universals. American immigrant family disorganization is program for later volumes not in the Vol I ingest.

### Hochschild (attributed — *The Second Shift*, 1989)

[[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]] (with Anne Machung) analyzes the **two-job family** as the private site of a [[stalled-revolution|stalled revolution]]: women's mass paid employment without matching male domestic adaptation or workplace redesign (*[[hochschild-second-shift-study]]*; source [[hochschild-second-shift-1989]]). Core constructs: [[second-shift]], [[leisure-gap]], [[gender-strategy]] (traditional / transitional / egalitarian), [[family-myth]], [[economy-of-gratitude]]. Fieldwork 1976–88 on 50 dual-earner couples with young children plus home observation. Findings (attributed): women more often carry the unpaid load and managerial responsibility; ~20% of men in the sample shared equally; husband willingness to share associated with happier marriages regardless of gender-role ideology; myths and gratitude economies manage unresolved tension. Class shapes how strain bites (money/day care vs career clocks) while dual ideals of traditional care vs 50-50 run across class. Not a cross-cultural inventory — U.S. heterosexual dual-earner focus, middle-class skew. Links to [[work-family-conflict]].

### Middletown note

[[middletown|Middletown]] (Lynd & Lynd) remains the wiki's industrial Midwestern nuclear-household case (1924–25): romance vs thin companionship, housework commercialization, classed birth control — earlier dual pressures before mass maternal employment of Hochschild's period.

### Zelizer (attributed — *The Social Meaning of Money*, 1994)

[[zelizer-viviana|Zelizer]] analyzes **domestic monies** as socially differentiated currencies inside the household (*[[zelizer-social-meaning-of-money-study]]*; source [[zelizer-social-meaning-of-money-1994]]). Core constructs: [[multiple-monies]], [[earmarking]]. For U.S. households c. 1870–1930 she reconstructs contests over the housewife's income (dole vs allowance vs joint-control ideals), class-inverted control of the pay packet, pin money as supplementary "not real" wages, and children's monies. Dimensions: allocation, timing, uses, quantity. Transfer modes: payment, entitlement, gift. Later restated: earmarks of women's monies can enforce dependency even as they are contested. Not ethnography of living families — historical-cultural reconstruction from print, law, and advice literature. Links to [[money-economy]] and [[economic-sociology]].

### Portes & Rumbaut (attributed — *Legacies*, 2001)

[[portes-alejandro|Portes]] and [[rumbaut-ruben|Rumbaut]] treat the **immigrant family** as the hinge between first-generation [[modes-of-incorporation]] and second-generation paths (*[[portes-rumbaut-legacies-study]]*; source [[portes-rumbaut-legacies-2001]]). Intergenerational language/culture gaps produce [[dissonant-acculturation]] (role reversal risk), [[consonant-acculturation]], or [[selective-acculturation]] (often with fluent bilingualism and community support). Intact two-parent structure and co-ethnic community resources are framed as social capital against race, hourglass labor markets, and inner-city countercultures. CILS parental survey: high educational ambition for children alongside fear of American school/peer "permissiveness." Not a general theory of kinship — U.S. gateway-city immigrant families in the mid-1990s.

### Fortes (attributed — *Web of Kinship*, 1949)

[[fortes-meyer|Fortes]] analysed the [[tallensi|Tallensi]] **polygynous joint family** and homestead as one social entity (*yir*) whose composition is a [[developmental-cycle-of-domestic-group|developmental cycle]] of growth, fission, and reintegration — not a fixed elementary vs extended type (1934 sample of 61 domestic units). Architecture (gateway, *zoy*, granaries, women’s *dug* apartments) mapped agnatic integration against matricentral differentiation. The domestic family was the matrix of all genealogical ties and the focal field where centripetal patriliny and centrifugal matrilateral kinship ([[complementary-filiation]]) met; parent–child bonds were morally absolute yet generationally tense; sibling grades (*soog* / *sunzo*) structured co-residence and property. Source: [[fortes-web-of-kinship-tallensi-1949]]; study: [[fortes-web-of-kinship-study]].

## Debates

- Gender hierarchy in classical sociology (Comte natural subordination vs Engels historical-defeat thesis — different mechanisms, both attributed).
- Family as unit of analysis vs individual.
- [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]] — whether the Morgan–Engels sequence is world history.
- Property, monogamy, and women’s oppression (feminist partial retentions of Engels).
- Dual-earner second shift and stalled revolution (Hochschild): who owes what at home when both earn; myth vs equality.
- Domestic monies and gendered earmarking (Zelizer): who controls household cash; allowance vs entitlement; inequality enforced by monetary form.
- Inner-city kinship under street code (Anderson): [[decent-and-street]] family orientations; decent daddy; grandmother as transitional safety net; mating game under joblessness (*[[anderson-code-of-the-street-study]]*).
- African family jural content and marriage stability (Gluckman Lozi–Zulu; R-B mother-/father-right typology; Fortes Tallensi joint-family cycle) — how “the family” changes with [[unilineal-descent]], [[double-descent]], [[complementary-filiation]], and [[marriage-payments]] (*[[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-study]]* · *[[fortes-web-of-kinship-study]]*).
