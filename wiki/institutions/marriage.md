# Marriage

Recurrent social form joining persons (and typically kin groups) with jural, economic, sexual, and often ritual consequences. *[[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-1950|African Systems of Kinship and Marriage]]* (1950) is a major comparative charter for African marriage as a **developing process** involving payments and inter-group alliance.

## Definition and Scope

[[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]] defined marriage for comparative purposes as a **rearrangement of social structure**: existing ties (especially the bride’s to her natal family) are changed; new relations are created between spouses, each spouse and the other’s kin, and often between the two bodies of kin. Marriage, like birth, death, or initiation, is a recurrent structural moment regulated by custom.

Key definitional moves in the ASKAM Introduction (attributed):

1. **Not constituted by sexual intercourse.** “Marriage is a social arrangement by which a child is given a legitimate position in the society, determined by parenthood in the social sense.”
2. **Union, not contract, between spouses.** Agreement between families to give a daughter and make [[marriage-payments|marriage payments]] is a preliminary contract; the marital relationship itself is a union entered into, normally lifelong in ideal, not a commercial contract between husband and wife.
3. **Legality without the state.** In Africa, R-B argued, political authority is typically not what makes a marriage legal; legality is produced by series of transactions/formalities involving both kin bodies, commonly including a payment of goods or services from bridegroom to bride’s kin.
4. **Alliance of two bodies of kin.** Africans (in his comparative generalization) do not conceive marriage as based on romantic love; marriage is a compact between two kin bodies with common interest in the union and offspring. Modern English/American marriage (state legality, romantic love, women’s changed economic position) is a recent special development and illegitimate as the sole “civilized” standard for comparison.
5. **Rights transfer.** Central feature is transfer of control/protection (*mund*-like) over the woman from her kin to husband and his kin, while natal kin retain protective claims against ill-treatment; payment is the objective instrument of that legal transfer, not wife-purchase (see [[marriage-payments]]).
6. **Child-bearing as primary “value.”** An African marries *liberorum quaerendorum gratia*; barrenness may require return of payment or provision of another woman to bear children (linking to [[levirate]]/sororate).

Scope on this page: comparative institution as theorized and illustrated in ASKAM (mid-20th-c. African ethnography + classical foils). Not a world HRAF inventory; not modern state-law marriage alone.

## Cross-Cultural Variation

ASKAM’s African sample spans several structural poles (attributed case reports):

| Pattern | Illustration | Marriage rules / process notes |
|---|---|---|
| Father-right + cattle payments | Nguni ([[zulu]], [[swazi]]); Nilotic cattle complex | “Cattle beget children”; bride integration/avoidance; permanent alliance ideals; levirate/sororate |
| Cognatic, no corporate lineage | [[lozi]] (Gluckman) | Degree prohibitions among cognates; high divorce; object to sororal polygyny; child “belongs to both sides” |
| Matrilineal unilineal / mother-right approx. | [[ashanti]] (Fortes); [[bemba]] / Central Africa (Richards) | *tiri nsa* via mother’s brother; no wife’s-sister marriage (Ashanti); Bemba man attaches to wife’s family at least temporarily |
| Double descent | [[yako]] (Forde); [[nuba]] Nyaro/Tullishi (Nadel) | Dual exogamy poles; marriage money interacts with patri- and matri-rights |
| Segmentary patriliny | [[nuer]] (Evans-Pritchard) | Bridewealth, ghost-wives, levirate, concubinage as they build local clusters |
| Anomalous FBD permission | Tswana (Schapera) | Patrilineal lineage + MBD preference + FBD allowed — “decidedly exceptional” vs Nguni disgust at “marrying sisters” |

**Preferential / prohibited marriage (R-B functional theory):** customs function to preserve or renew kinship structure — disruptive marriages forbidden; preferential marriages reinforce existing relations. Cross-cousin marriage (usually MBD) common where MB relations are compatible with wife’s-parent roles; Ashanti formerly preferred MBD (declining with structural change per Fortes). Wife’s brother’s daughter marriage renews lineage alliance under patriliny (unity of lineage group). True [[levirate]] vs widow inheritance; sororate vs sister-replacement vs sororal polygyny — see [[levirate]].

**Affinal regulation:** mother-in-law avoidance and [[joking-relationship|joking]] as paired modes of managing separation and alliance after marriage; Nguni bride renaming and service period as temporary disequilibrium repair.

**Woman–woman marriage:** in several African regions a woman may go through a marriage rite with another woman and stand as *pater* to the wife’s offspring by an assigned lover (R-B Introduction fact claim).

## Theoretical Treatments

### Radcliffe-Brown / structural-functionalism (ASKAM Introduction)

- Marriage as structural rearrangement and social function within a working kinship system.
- Comparison + analysis over pseudo-historical origins.
- Mother-right / father-right / cognatic / double-lineage typology for jural variation ([[mother-right-and-father-right]]).
- Marriage payments as multi-aspect legal instruments ([[marriage-payments]]).
- Avoidance and joking as solutions to affinal conjunction/disjunction ([[joking-relationship]]).
- Incest defined narrowly as sexual intimacy within the parental family; marriage prohibitions not reducible to incest feelings (often reverse: sex felt wrong *because* marriage is forbidden).
- Position against evolutionary mother-right schemes and against using modern English marriage as universal standard.

### Gluckman (Lozi–Zulu)

[[gluckman-max|Gluckman]] tied marriage stability, divorce rates, sororate/levirate viability, and bridewealth logic to presence/absence of **corporate lineage**. Zulu: cattle transfer absolute rights in children to husband’s agnates; approve sororal polygyny; object to divorce as destroying permanence. Lozi: no corporate unilineal group; object to marriage with classificatory sister of wife; high divorce; genitor-based fatherhood. Classic demonstration that marriage rules co-vary with descent organization (attributed).

### Fortes (Ashanti)

Matrilineal corporate *abusua*; legal marriage via *wofa* and *tiri nsa*; complementary paternity/*ntoro* without equal double descent; politics of chiefly marriage alliances; decline of MBD preference under change (attributed).

### Fortes (Tallensi — *Web of Kinship*, 1949)

Patrilineal [[tallensi|Tallensi]] marriage (attributed, 1930s–40s): marriage founds the joint-family reproductive and economic partnership while remaining structurally opposed to each spouse’s prior kinship ties; sexual rights of the husband carry a **corporate clan interest**; co-wives adjust along *dug* (matricentral room) lines; the wife retains ties to her paternal family and lineage; *poyasama* intermediary of dual loyalty articulates the two clans (“our person and also their person”); genitor/pater ideally coincide under legitimate marriage; levirate and rudimentary sororate link widowhood to sibling equivalence and lineage continuity. Complements Ashanti matrilineal case above. Source: [[fortes-web-of-kinship-tallensi-1949]]; study: [[fortes-web-of-kinship-study]]; concepts: [[complementary-filiation]] · [[developmental-cycle-of-domestic-group]].

### Forde / Nadel (double descent)

Marriage and affiliation partitioned across patri- and matri-groups; Forde on Yakö marriage money, divorce, and physiological vs marriage-derived social fatherhood; Nadel on dual exogamy and incomplete dual forms (attributed).

### Earlier traditions (wiki cross-links only)

Morgan–Engels evolutionary marriage stages, Maine patriarchal family, Lowie bilateral family — see [[family]], [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]; not restated as ASKAM findings.

### Wrangham / cooking hypothesis (2009)

*(Attributed — *[[wrangham-catching-fire-2009|Catching Fire]]*.)* Evolutionary-biological account: pair-bonds and marriage as economic exchange around **cooked camp meals** — protection against food theft, husband’s access to a wife’s cooking, wife’s access to male provisioning and male communal authority. Economics (feeding) primary over sex. Cross-cultural near-universal of female domestic cooking (Murdock & Provost as cited) is load-bearing evidence. Distinct discourse from ASKAM jural analysis and from Morgan–Engels stages; see [[cooking-and-pair-bonding]], [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]]. Sensitive illustrations (wife-beating, wife theft for labor) are Wrangham’s secondary ethnography — not wiki-voice law of marriage.

- Component essays as used in claims integration: Gluckman Lozi–Zulu; Fortes Ashanti; Forde Yakö; Nadel Nuba; Evans-Pritchard Nuer; Richards Central Africa; Schapera Tswana; Kuper Swazi; Wilson Nyakyusa (and others in the volume).
- [[abu-lughod-writing-womens-worlds-study]] — Awlad ʿAli 1980s narrative ethnography of polygyny, [[patrilateral-parallel-cousin-marriage|patrilateral parallel-cousin marriage]], and wedding practice as particular histories rather than ethnic traits ([[writing-against-culture]]).
- [[edin-kefalas-promises-study]] — Philadelphia–Camden late 1990s/early 2000s ethnography of 162 low-income unmarried mothers: marriage revered but deferred under a high economic and relational bar (“promises I can keep”); nonmarital childbearing sequenced before marriage (attributed; Edin & Kefalas 2005/2011).

### U.S. low-income contemporary (Edin & Kefalas, attributed)

In *[[edin-kefalas-promises-i-can-keep-2005|Promises I Can Keep]]*, poor unmarried mothers in eight Philly/Camden neighborhoods insist marriage means **lifelong commitment** and refuse it until dual standards are met: material respectability (jobs, savings, often a “real” wedding) and relational trust (fidelity, maturity, fear of male control after the wedding). Authors argue the poor avoid marriage not because they denigrate it but because they revere it — better nonmarital children than divorce that makes marriage “a piece of paper.” Marriage’s **symbolic** significance has grown as its practical monopoly on sex, cohabitation, and childbearing eroded (authors with Cherlin framing). Class difference is less in marriage *values* than in ability to reach the “white picket fence” standard. See also [[nonmarital-childbearing]], [[being-there-mothering]], [[male-marriageable-pool-index]].

## Debates

- **Wife-purchase vs bridewealth:** colonial legal doctrine vs anthropological redescription ([[marriage-payments]]); still live in policy and feminist critique.
- **Mother-right evolutionary schemes:** R-B’s position rejects earliest matriarchy-as-promiscuity as imagination; evolutionary debate page [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
- **What makes marriage “legal”:** state registration vs kin prestations; dual marriage forms by payment size.
- **Corporate lineage and divorce:** Gluckman’s Zulu–Lozi thesis — how far generalizable beyond Central/Southern Africa.
- **Double descent vs complementary filiation:** Fortes against equal dual-descent Ashanti readings ([[double-descent]]); Tallensi *Web of Kinship* as type case of complementary filiation without second corporate descent group ([[complementary-filiation]] · [[fortes-web-of-kinship-study]]).
- **Affinity as group alliance vs complementary filiation:** Leach (*Rethinking* 1961) — enduring *mayu/dama*-type ties must not be reduced to individual in-law reciprocals of filiation ([[affinity]] · [[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961]]).
- **Universal definition of marriage:** Leach — marriage is Maine’s “bundle of rights”; *Notes and Queries* legitimacy definition fails on [[polyandry]] and dual Sinhalese institutions; Gough (1959) repudiates — positions, not wiki-voice verdict.
- **Incest vs marriage prohibition:** R-B’s separation of problems; Tallensi “copulation and marriage are not the same thing.”
- **Avoidance function:** incest-prevention theories (rejected by R-B) vs structural distance/respect analysis ([[joking-relationship]]).
