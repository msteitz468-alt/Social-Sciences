## Burt — *Structural Holes: The Social Structure of Competition* (1992) — ingested 2026-07-09

Ronald Burt's landmark recasts competition as social structure: advantage flows from **[[structural-holes|structural holes]]** — gaps between non-redundant contacts — that a network spans, brokered by the **[[tertius-gaudens|tertius gaudens]]** ("the third who benefits," from [[simmel-georg|Simmel]]) and measured by **[[network-constraint|network constraint]]**. The **[[structural-holes-theory|structural-holes theory]]** supplies the network-*brokerage* strand of social capital, distinct from [[social-capital|Bourdieu's]] classed capital, [[social-capital-coleman|Coleman's]] closure account, and [[social-capital-theory|Putnam's]] civic tradition — and it disputes Coleman directly in the new **[[closure-vs-brokerage-debate]]**. Two original quantitative studies anchor it ([[burt-structural-holes-study]]): 77 US product markets (holes → profit margins) and 284 managers (holes → promotion, *but* contingent on legitimacy — the returns reverse for women and entry-rank men). See [[burt-ronald-s]]. Theory/Thinkers/Studies Hub pages flagged as warranted-but-deferred pending further Burt sources.

The **founding monograph of economic anthropology**: [[firth-raymond|Firth]]'s economic companion to *We, the Tikopia*, from the same 1928–29 [[tikopia|Tikopia]] fieldwork — no money, prices, or wage labour, yet scarcity, choice, and capital in modified form; Hicks's "substantial vs. formal" categories; [[spheres-of-exchange]]; reciprocity refined into three quantitative types; chiefs as redistributive integrators; *tapu* as economic regulation.
