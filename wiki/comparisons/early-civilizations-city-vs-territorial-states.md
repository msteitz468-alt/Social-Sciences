# Early Civilizations: City-States vs Territorial States (Trigger 2003)

Comparison drawn from [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]]'s *[[trigger-understanding-early-civilizations-2003|Understanding Early Civilizations]]* (2003). All claims **attributed to Trigger**. Study: [[trigger-understanding-early-civilizations-study]].

## The two-type claim

Trigger argues that among early civilizations only **two** basic political forms occur: [[city-state|city-states]] and [[territorial-state|territorial states]]. They are **stable alternatives, not stages** — neither evolves into the other within early civilization; both are later replaced by more complex preindustrial forms. Polity type, he holds, shapes economic behaviour at least as much as environmental difference.

## Ideal-type contrasts (attributed)

| Dimension | City-state pattern | Territorial-state pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Scale | Smaller polities / peer systems | Larger unitary territories |
| Settlement | Higher urban density; farmers often in cities | Dispersed rural majority; elite/admin centres |
| Administration | More [[heterarchy|heterarchical]] elements | Hierarchical bureaucratic and/or delegational |
| Exchange | Broader market access; independent merchants | Stronger central control of exotics and elite production |
| Kingship sacralization | Present; often weaker under close urban observation | Often stronger where rulers remote |
| Law | Detailed codes more with hegemonic city-state systems | Less codified in some territorial cases |
| Labour forms | Slavery more prominent (comparative note) | Bonded free labour / corvée stronger (comparative note) |

## Case assignments (Trigger’s readings)

| Case | Assignment (attributed) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mesopotamia | City-state system | Classic peer-polity urbanism |
| Classic Maya | City-state system | [[classic-maya]] |
| Valley of Mexico / Aztecs | City-state system + tributary hegemony | Aztec “empire” ≠ territorial state ([[aztec-empire]]) |
| Yoruba | City-state system | [[yoruba]] |
| Egypt | Territorial state | Low early urbanism relative to city-state systems |
| Inka | Territorial state | [[inka-empire]] |
| Shang China | Territorial leanings in synthesis | [[shang-civilization]] — specialist debate on extent |
