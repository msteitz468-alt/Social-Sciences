# Post-Processual Archaeology — Deep Analysis

> Theory Hub deep analysis. Summary: [[post-processual-archaeology]]. Link hub by full path `[[hubs/theory/post-processual-archaeology]]`.

**Scope.** This analysis densifies the program from three complementary primaries: (1) [[shanks-michael|Shanks]] & [[tilley-christopher|Tilley]]’s critical/metatheoretical charter *[[shanks-tilley-social-theory-and-archaeology-1987|Social Theory and Archaeology]]* (Polity 1987) — theory-as-practice, [[logic-of-contingency]], material culture as signifying practice, anti-evolutionism, politics of theory; (2) their critical practice charter *[[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987|Re-Constructing Archaeology]]* (1987/1992) — positivism/value-freedom, [[fourfold-hermeneutic]], [[redemptive-aesthetic]], [[social-archaeology]] ontology, style and modern-MC analyses, 1992 replies to critics; (3) [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]]’s field-method charter *[[hodder-archaeological-process-1999|The Archaeological Process]]* (Blackwell, 1999) — recursive excavation, interpretation at the trowel’s edge, multivocality. Cross-links: vault’s processual charter (*[[binford-binford-new-perspectives-1968|New Perspectives]]* 1968); [[lucas-gavin|Lucas]] fieldwork historiography (2002); Tilley landscape phenomenology (1994). *Symbols in Action* (1982) and *Reading the Past* (1986) remain outstanding or partially mediated. **All programmatic claims in attributed voice.**

## Intellectual Origins and Context

Post-processual archaeology named itself against [[processual-archaeology|New Archaeology]]. Hodder’s 1999 definition (attributed):

> "By postprocessual archaeology I mean a group of views based on a critique of processual archaeology (Hodder 1982b; Shanks and Tilley 1987). These views could crudely be described as interpretive and self-reflexive as opposed to processual archaeology’s emphasis on science and the objective."

The cluster was never a single school: Marxist and dialectical-Marxist (Leone; McGuire), feminist (Gero and Conkey), interpretive (Tilley), structurationist (Barrett), phenomenological (Thomas; Gosden). The load-bearing shared commitment Hodder retains is that **material culture is meaningfully and historically constituted**.

**What it reacted against (as Hodder frames 1999):**

1. **Universal method + universal theory.** Early processualism treated not only cultural process but **field method** as universal: middle-range theory of record formation (Binford 1977; Schiffer 1976), regional ecological designs, intensive survey/sampling/screening — coupled to CRM codification and computers.
2. **Practice lagging theory on theory-ladenness.** Sophisticated accounts already admitted data are theory-laden; recording forms and manuals still split “objective facts” from later “subjective interpretation” (Barker tradition; Joukowsky-type manuals Hodder quotes).
3. **Untheorized fieldwork.** Ethnography had undergone postcolonial reflexive scrutiny (*Writing Culture* et al.); archaeological trenches remained “sheltered,” techniques treated as self-evident under contract pressures.
4. **Closed professional authority.** Heritage multivocality, indigenous claims, popular and alternative pasts, and global information networks made “these are the objective data” an insufficient closer of debate.

**Disciplinary moment.** Critical Turn and early Contemporary eras: end of high modernist confidence in a single scientific archaeology; contract archaeology’s standardization boom; rise of heritage as political economy; digital networks; postcolonial and feminist challenges inside and outside the academy. Hodder notes that by the 1990s even processualism had split (cognitive-processual; neo-Darwinian) and Schiffer could speak of “a thousand archaeologies” — a **double crisis** of object (what archaeology is for) and objective method.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

| Claim (attributed) | Commitment | Evidence class / operationalization |
|---|---|---|
| Material culture meaningfully constituted | Meaning, agency, historical context central | Ethnoarchaeology of symbols; contextual reading |
| Data = objects–contexts–interpretations in dialectical relation | No radical description/interpretation split | Figs. 5.1–5.2 in Hodder 1999 |
| Interpretation at the trowel’s edge | Recovery and recording already interpretive | Bodily decisions; floor vs fill screening intensity |
| Hermeneutic spiral / accommodative fitting | Part–whole coherence + correspondence in dialectic | Haddenham ditch re-interpretation; Building 1 paradox |
| Recursive vs linear method | Feedback during excavation, not only after | Specialist tours; fast-track finds; fluid DB |
| Multivocality | Multiple legitimate stakes in the past | Heritage; web database; multi-team windows |
| Sciences inside hermeneutic accommodation | Not anti-science | ¹⁴C, micromorphology, Ice Man multi-strand narrative |
| Globalism: emancipatory or dominating | Same networks, opposite politics | Çatalhöyük local–global case |

**Redefinition of the debate (Hodder 1999 Ch. 5 close):** rather than subject versus object, “the central issue is the use of data as evidence for interpretation.”

**Four themes** under the twelve practical steps (Ch. 11):

1. **Reflexivity** — examine effects of archaeological assumptions and actions on communities involved (including other archaeologists); diaries, video, embedded anthropologists.
2. **Relationality / contextuality** — meaning is relational; findings related to context of knowledge production and to interrelations of contexts and artefacts.
3. **Interactivity** — circuits of information among specialists, excavators, databases, publics.
4. **Multivocality** — multiple pathways and communities; hypertext; multi-team methods.

## The Empirical Program

### Haddenham as reasoning laboratory

Hodder’s Ch. 3 case is not offered as a finished culture-history of a site but as a **document of how archaeologists reason**. Pre-understandings of a “causewayed enclosure” made excavators “see” causeways and Neolithic pottery that were not there, and fail to see a ditch that was. Accumulating contradictions (horse bone, missing southern ditch, leaching, radiocarbon) forced a new whole: northern ditch late Iron Age/Roman; southern the Neolithic enclosure; variability recast as social differentiation (longer segments, more recuts, special deposits, higher cattle %) rather than simple segmentary society. Figure 3.3’s **hermeneutic spiral** links prior knowledge, excavation, analysis, site interpretation, and wider comparisons.

**Method lesson (attributed):** most archaeology depends on **accommodative** fitting of evidence despite Binford’s critique of ad hoc procedures; coherence of theory and correspondence with data cannot be cleanly separated because “seeing” is theory-laden — yet data can still confront and break a whole.

### Çatalhöyük as method laboratory

[[catalhoyuk]] under Hodder (from 1993) operationalizes the program. Building 1 floor/wall paradox (floors both later and earlier than wall C under successive readings) exemplifies stratigraphic **fitting** under incomplete evidence. Space 107 dump vs activity-area sampling shows recovery intensity tracking interpretation.

### Multiscalar windows (British practice; Ice Man)

Ch. 8 argues for non-commensurable scales (event vs structure) held together without reducing one to the other. The Ice Man (as of Spindler 1993 literature Hodder cites) is a multiscalar narrative hinge between individual death and long-term *domus*→*agrios* framings — with staged self-critique of grand narrative temptation. British practice-phenomenology is engaged and critiqued (meaning/practice splits; universal bodies).

## Method Commitments

### Four numbered principles (Hodder 1999 Ch. 5)

1. Accept in practice that interpretation constructs data; description and interpretation cannot be radically separated; much archaeology is not experimental science — interpretations are built in a **fitting** process.
2. Meaning is **relational** — everything depends on everything else.
3. Meaning is both **general and local** (specialist general knowledge + local contextual fitting).
4. Return emphasis to the **point of excavation**; excavator is not a technician; digging is destructive and method depends on interpretation of what will be found.

### Twelve practical steps at Çatalhöyük (Hodder 1999 Ch. 11)

| # | Step | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lab specialists visit trenches every 1–2 days | Mutual context and ID for sampling/interpretation |
| 2 | Priorities not off-the-shelf sampling formulae | Sampling tracks changing site interpretation |
| 3 | Cross-cutting “objects” (refuse, burning, food…) | Break tray-universal categories; force interaction |
| 4 | Fast-track selected materials + digital plots | Feedback during dig; trowel’s-edge interpretation |
| 5 | Integrated networked database | Query across field/lab; conclusions momentary |
| 6 | Diaries in DB cross-referenced to contexts | Situate coded data in assumptions of production |
| 7 | Video clips keyword-linked to contexts | Visual relativizing of “data”; force explanation |
| 8 | Embedded anthropologists of knowledge production | Destabilize unrecognized assumptions; local impact |
| 9 | Web database for multivocal access | Open participation; career/IP tension acknowledged |
| 10 | Hypertext multimedia narratives | Non-linear evidence–interpretation pathways |
| 11 | VR reconstruction as public front-end to DB | Experiment + non-specialist access |
| 12 | Multi-team multi-method area windows | Different traditions → different “Çatalhöyüks” welcomed |

### Natural sciences and IT

Hodder is **not anti-science**: Ch. 6 places natural-science procedures inside a generalized hermeneutic accommodation of strands (geology as hermeneutic model via Frodeman; multi-stranded sexing, wood, micromorphology, ¹⁴C outlier handling). Ch. 7–10: computers first drove codification under CRM; newer flexible relational systems, multimedia, and networks *can* support reflexivity — or reinforce elite closure. Emancipatory vs dominating use is a political choice, not a technological destiny.

## Major Debates and Rivals

Primary rival program: [[processual-archaeology]] — see [[processual-postprocessual-debate]] for dual strongest-form statements.

Internal and adjacent:

- **Adams & Brooke (1995):** postprocessualism produced no methodology — Hodder accepts and answers with the 1999 project.
- **Relativism charge:** Hodder reframes toward evidence, coherence, correspondence, fruitfulness; rejects pure subjectivism while denying clean subject/object split.
- **British single-context recording:** both resource and problem — formalizes units but can technicise diggers and freeze codes (Hodder engages DUA 1975 practice; vault also has Lucas on [[single-context-recording]]).
- **Lucas (2002):** extends reflexivity toward material praxis and critiques neglect of bodily encounter.
- **Heritage “past as play”:** Ch. 9 warns diversity rhetoric can serve global commercial homogenization; local rights and grounded difference as counter.

## What Survived

Even where the full twelve-step package is rare:

- **Vocabulary:** context, meaning, agency, multivocality, reflexivity, heritage stakeholders — routine in training and CRM ethics codes.
- **Recording experiments:** diaries, digital photogrammetry, integrated databases, specialist–field feedback loops appear in many research projects without full philosophical framing.
- **Heritage practice:** indigenous consultation, multiple narratives in museums, contested monuments — institutionalize multivocality beyond theory seminars.
- **Hybridization:** “processual-plus,” cognitive archaeology, and scientifically intensive interpretive projects show the dichotomy softened in practice — which Hodder’s “non-dichotomous thinking” and “go with the flow” already anticipated as both risk and opportunity.
- **Fieldwork historiography:** Lucas and others treat the reflexive moment as a chapter in how the [[archaeological-record]] is conceived — permanent vault content even if the brand “post-processual” ages.

## Current Status and Research Frontiers

Status remains **active** as a living family of interpretive, critical, and reflexive approaches rather than a single party line. Research frontiers (as of Hodder 1999 primary, vault note 2026-07-10): scaling reflexivity into developer-funded archaeology; integrating biomolecular evidence without re-essentializing peoples; digital multivocality after the web's actual political economy; decolonizing curricula and collections; material-praxis and symmetrical/ontological turns that partly supersede, partly inherit, the interpretive agenda. Outstanding primary for earlier charters: *Symbols in Action*, *Reading the Past*, Shanks & Tilley; for later Hodder: *Entangled* (2012) already in `raw/`.

**Quote to hold the method thesis:**

> "Interpretation occurs at the trowel’s edge."

**Quote to hold the political thesis:**

> "The new technologies… provide not a solution but an opportunity and a challenge… They can be used to create emancipation… Or they can be used to exclude, close off, and dominate."

## Strand note — critical charter (Shanks & Tilley 1987)

Primary densification: *[[shanks-tilley-social-theory-and-archaeology-1987|Social Theory and Archaeology]]* — [[shanks-michael]], [[tilley-christopher]], [[logic-of-contingency]]. Companion practice charter: *[[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987|Re-Constructing Archaeology]]* (1987/1992).

**What this strand supplies that Hodder 1999 does not (attributed):** metatheory of theory-as-practice (against theory-as-methodology and middle-range empiricism as cure); thirteen-point textual critique of processual social archaeology; necessity vs contingency; ideology as subject-constituting signifying practice; material culture as active symbolic order in political relations; critique of abstract chronological time and neo-evolutionism ("stability rather than social change needs explaining"); politics of theory (WAC, museums, *National Geographic*, cultural capital); formal Appendix theses including call for archaeological poetics. **From RCA specifically:** positivism and value-freedom as ideology (Chs. 2–3); [[fourfold-hermeneutic]] (Ch. 5); [[social-archaeology]] ontology (Ch. 6); [[redemptive-aesthetic]] museum program (Ch. 4); style-as-ideology TRB case and beer-can modern MC (Chs. 7–8); 1992 Appendix four-charge reply (subjectivity, relativism, fiction, impotency). **What it does not supply:** recursive field method, trowel’s-edge recording package, Çatalhöyük laboratory — those are Hodder 1999’s load.

**Bridge quote (S&T 1987 Preface, attributed):** "No amount of excavation, survey, ethnoarchaeological work or so-called 'middle-range' empiricism will cure the perceived fundamental isolation of past from present."

**Bridge quote (S&T 1987 Appendix 6.5, attributed):** "Archaeology is nothing if it is not critique."

**Bridge quote (RCA Ch. 5, attributed):** archaeology and history are "the most difficult of all disciplines because the process of acquiring a historical understanding involves a **quadruple act of interpretative endeavour**."
