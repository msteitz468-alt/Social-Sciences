## Intellectual Origins and Context

Structural Marxism is a product of the French **Critical Turn** conjuncture of the early-to-mid 1960s — post-Twentieth Congress de-Stalinization, the Sino-Soviet rupture, the crisis of PCF intellectual culture, and the high moment of [[structuralism]] (Lévi-Strauss, Lacan at ENS from 1964, Canguilhem’s history of science). [[althusser-louis|Louis Althusser]], *agrégé répétiteur* at the École Normale Supérieure, had already staged seminars on the Young Marx (1961–62), the origins of structuralism (1962–63), and Lacan and psychoanalysis (1963–64). The 1964–65 seminar on *Capital* was, in [[balibar-etienne|Balibar]]’s later Presentation, a “recapitulation and reinvestment” of those results: a collective philosophical reading of Marx’s scientific work that would appear, with *Pour Marx*, as the founding pair of Maspero’s *Théorie* series in November 1965.

The program **reacted against** a short list of load-bearing alternatives:

1. **Hegelian and humanist Marxism** — expressive totality; history as alienation and reappropriation of Man; return to the 1844 Manuscripts as the “secret” of *Capital* (the target of both *For Marx*’s anti-humanism and Rancière’s Part Two).
2. **Economism / mechanistic base–superstructure** — economy as sole immediate cause; superstructures as pure reflection (the foil against which last-instance determination and relative autonomy are defined).
3. **Empiricist history of “elements” and of political economy** — treating classical categories as continuous with Marx’s, or treating England as the real object of *Capital*.
4. **Radical historicism of Marxism** — Gramscian “absolute historicism” and the reading of Marxism as Ricardo historicized / Hegelianized (Althusser’s Part Four, “Marxism Is Not a Historicism”).
5. **Foundational theory of knowledge** — Subject–Object guarantees; replaced by [[symptomatic-reading]], [[theoretical-practice]], and [[knowledge-effect]].

Debts are as polemical as the foils. **Bachelard** supplies the epistemology of rupture (transformed into the [[epistemological-break]] in Marx). **Spinoza** is preferred to Hegel for thinking science/ideology discontinuity and later for the immanent cause that names [[structural-causality]]. **Freud** loans “overdetermination” and the metaphor of the symptom. **Lenin** and **Mao** (*On Contradiction*) supply political conjunctural thinking. **Marx** is read as the founder of a science whose philosophy exists first in the *practical state* inside *Capital* and must be produced as theory by reading. The ENS circle — Balibar, [[ranciere-jacques|Rancière]], [[macherey-pierre|Macherey]], [[establet-roger|Establet]], with Duroux, Linhart, and others — made the program multi-author from the start; *Reading Capital* is not Althusser’s monologue with annexes.

Colonial and party production contexts matter. Althusser wrote as a PCF philosopher intervening in the Party’s theoretical culture; the “scientific” self-presentation of the break was also a political weapon against humanist and historicist revisions. Anthropological structural Marxism would later re-describe ethnographies produced under colonial conditions without always generating new fieldwork — a reflexivity the wiki records without collapsing the theoretical claims into biography.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

The dual charter divides labor without dividing the program.

### From *For Marx* (philosophical apparatus)

Althusser’s essays of 1960–64, collected in 1965, install the vocabulary that *Reading Capital* will operationalize on *Capital* itself:

- **[[epistemological-break]] (~1845).** Marx breaks with the ideological problematics of the Early Works (Feuerbachian anthropology; Hegelian expressive dialectic). Periodization: Early / Works of the Break / Transitional / Mature. The 1844 Manuscripts are a *threshold*, not the essence of Marxism.
- **[[overdetermination]].** Marxist contradiction is never Hegel’s simple contradiction; it is always specified by the structured whole. Condensation/fusion vs displacement; 1917 as type case; block vs rupture.
- **[[structure-in-dominance]] / determination in the last instance.** Economy determinant *in the last instance*; which instance is *dominant* varies; superstructures have relative autonomy and specific effectivity. The “lonely hour of the last instance never comes.”
- **[[theoretical-practice]] (Generalities I–II–III).** Knowledge is production, not extraction of essence from the real. Theory (capital T) = dialectical materialism as theory of practice — a definition Althusser later retracts as unilateral.
- **[[problematic]].** The unit of theoretical analysis (via Jacques Martin); ideology is unconscious of its problematic.
- **[[theoretical-anti-humanism]].** Mature Marxism does not ground history in Man; humanism is ideology (and may remain a practical slogan).
- **[[social-formation]].** Concrete complex whole of economic, political, and ideological practices — object of historical materialism.

### From *Reading Capital* (philosophy of *Capital* + basic concepts of HM)

**Part One (Althusser)** poses “what is it to read?” and answers with a guilty philosophical reading: *Capital* is questioned for the specificity of its *object* and of its *discourse*, not for economic content already defined outside it. **[[symptomatic-reading]]** makes lacunae and the “discourse of the silence” perceptible; dual reading against the Early Works makes “invisible survivals” visible. **[[knowledge-effect]]** names the mechanism of cognitive appropriation by ordered scientific discourse (*Gliederung*) — ideological recognition-misrecognition vs scientific knowledge effect — distinct from the history of how knowledges are produced.

**Part Two (Rancière)** tracks **critique** from 1844 to *Capital*. Two structures of *Entfremdung*: anthropological subject/object reversal vs *Capital*’s materialization of the relation in the thing and the worker as [[bearer-trager|bearer]] of the wage-labour relation. Where Marx’s rigour slackens, anthropological schemas re-enter under the same words.

**Part Three (Macherey)** analyzes the **process of exposition** as work of concepts — autonomous scientific arrangement, not narrative of investigation (*Forschungsweise* vs *Darstellungsweise*).

**Part Four (Althusser)** is the philosophical core of the object of *Capital*: merits and errors of classical economics; **[[differential-historical-time]]** against Hegelian essential section; Marxism as **anti-historicism** paired with anti-humanism; the “immense theoretical revolution” as rupture of object, not mere inversion of method; **[[structural-causality]]** as effectivity of structure on elements, indexed by *Darstellung*, structure immanent in effects, authorless theatre; “ideal average” as concept of the real (*Kerngestalt*), not empiricist mean of England.

**Part Five (Balibar)** reconstructs **basic concepts of historical materialism**: [[mode-of-production]] as [[combination-combinatory|combination]] of invariants (worker / means / non-worker) linked *twice* by property and real appropriation; classes as bearers; reproduction as triple continuity and synchrony of social capital; **transition** as itself a structured mode with special [[dislocation-decalage|dislocation]] (manufacture as formal without real subsumption).

**Part Six (Establet)** maps the **plan of *Capital*** as order of concepts; brackets primitive accumulation as genealogy posing a problem for transition theory, not that theory itself.

### Architecture in one sentence (attributed)

A science of history, founded by an epistemological break, thinks social formations as complex structured wholes under structure-in-dominance and structural causality; reads its own texts symptomatically; treats agents as bearers of combinations of production relations; and refuses both expressive historical time and Man as theoretical subject.

## Key Texts and Proponents

| Text | Year | Move it makes | Reception |
|---|---|---|---|
| Althusser, *For Marx* (*Pour Marx*) | 1965 (essays 1960–64) | Installs break, overdetermination, structure in dominance, theoretical practice, anti-humanism | Charter of philosophical structural Marxism; English Brewster 1969 |
| Althusser et al., *Reading Capital* (*Lire le Capital*) | 1965 seminar/book; 1968 abridged; 1996/2015 complete | Operationalizes apparatus on *Capital*; structural causality; combination; anti-historicism | Co-charter; abridgment politics (Rancière/Macherey/Establet cut then restored); Brewster + Fernbach English |
| Godelier, *Rationality and Irrationality in Economics* | 1966/1972 | Anthropological program; rationality of “irrational” economies | Bridge philosophy → economic anthropology |
| Terray, *Marxism and “Primitive” Societies* | 1969/1972 | Guro etc. as articulated modes | Sahlins’s principal critical case |
| Meillassoux, *Anthropologie économique des Gouro* etc. | 1960s–70s | Lineage mode / domestic community | Enduring economic-anthropology reference |
| Sebag, *Marxisme et structuralisme* | 1964 | Early fusion | Sahlins: “essentially Boasian” |

Proponents are a **network**, not a school with a single master: Althusser as director and philosophical pole; Balibar as systematizer of HM concepts; Rancière, Macherey, Establet as specialized *Capital* readers; Godelier/Terray/Meillassoux as anthropological wing. Later breaks (Rancière’s anti-Althusserianism; Althusser’s own self-criticisms) are part of the program’s history, not external noise.

## The Empirical Program

Structural Marxism’s “empirical” program is uneven by design.

**Hits (as the program counted them).** (1) A re-periodization of Marx’s oeuvre that made the 1844 Manuscripts *problematic* rather than foundational — reshaping Marxist philosophy curricula for a generation. (2) A vocabulary for conjunctural politics (overdetermination, fusion, last instance) that could think 1917 without reducing it to pure economic contradiction. (3) Balibar’s combination grid as a non-evolutionist way to compare modes without assuming unilinear stages. (4) Rancière’s fine-grained demonstration that mature Marx still *slides* into anthropological discourse — a finding internal to the Althusserian project that complicates any pure “break” mythology. (5) Anthropological reanalyses that forced economic anthropology to state whether kinship “is” or “functions as” relations of production.

**Misses and anomalies.** (1) Little new ethnographic fieldwork in the philosophical wing; the anthropological wing often re-described existing colonial-era materials. (2) The science/ideology dualism proved brittle under political stress (1968 and after) and under Althusser’s own self-criticism of theoreticism. (3) Structural causality remained partly metaphorical (theatre, machinery, metonymy) — Althusser admits Marx lacked an adequate concept and that the age did not provide one. (4) Transition theory is powerfully *posed* (manufacture, primitive accumulation as genealogy) but not fully *delivered* as a predictive or historiographic engine. (5) Sahlins’s anomaly: if Guro father–son cooperation is “really” economic requirement in kinship form, the symbolic reason for *that* form is evacuated — cultural reason returns as critique.

## Method Commitments

1. **Read as philosophers.** Question discourse–object unity and epistemological status; do not take the object as already defined by economics or narrative history.
2. **Symptomatic attention to silences.** Progress by what “sounds hollow”; lacunae are data.
3. **Knowledge as production.** Generalities I–II–III; no empiricist extraction of essence; no origin-ground of the knowledge effect in the life-world.
4. **Structure before consciousness.** Agents’ understandings are ideological or practical; scientific concepts reconstruct the combination and its effectivity.
5. **Differential times.** No essential section; synchrony as concept of structure, not co-presence of essence.
6. **Edition and seminar as method.** *Reading Capital* is multi-authored and incomplete by design (“mere beginnings of a reading”); the 1968 abridgment and later restoration are political-editorial events that change which claims circulate.

## Major Debates and Rivals

- **[[marxist-humanism-debate]].** Theoretical anti-humanism vs socialist/humanist Marxisms (Fromm, early Marx revival, praxis philosophies). *Reading Capital* intensifies the anti-humanist and anti-historicist poles.
- **Historicism.** Gramsci, Croce-derived Italian traditions, and “absolute historicism” as Althusser’s targets; reverse charge that anti-historicism is positivist.
- **Western Marxism.** Lukácsian reification and cultural critique vs Althusserian science of history — rival recoveries of Marx inside [[western-marxism]]’s broad umbrella.
- **Structuralism.** Attraction and tension: Lévi-Strauss as interlocutor and limit; Althusser’s 1968 self-critique of “structuralist” theoreticism.
- **Cultural reason vs practical reason (Sahlins).** Anthropological wing’s combination apparatus vs symbolic constitution of social reality.
- **Processual / post-processual archaeology.** Selective uptake of Marxism without evolutionary necessity (Shanks & Tilley).
- **Internal self-criticism.** Philosophy as Theory of theoretical practice → philosophy as class struggle in theory (late 1960s); later aleatory materialism (not primary here).

## Critiques and Responses

**Theoreticism.** The charge that the program substituted theoretical practice for political practice and sealed science from the masses. Althusser’s own prefaces and glossary interpolations partly concede the point while defending the necessity of theoretical autonomy.

**Evacuation of the subject.** *Träger* language and authorless theatre are read by critics as denial of agency and ethics. Program response (attributed): scientific discourse must replace Man as *theoretical* subject; practical ideology and political struggle remain real — but this dual-register answer never fully satisfied humanist opponents.

**Spinozism / anti-Hegelianism.** Accused of smuggling metaphysics; Althusser answers that classical philosophy offered only transitive and expressive causalities, so a third concept had to be produced.

**Anthropological reductionism (Sahlins).** Response within the wing varied; the philosophical wing largely did not answer Sahlins on his terrain. The wiki records both positions without adjudication.

**Edition politics.** Cutting Rancière, Macherey, and Establet in 1968 made “Althusser and Balibar” the English-language face of the book for decades; Complete Edition restoration reopens the multi-author structure as historically primary.

## What Survived

Even as “structural Marxism” declined as a named school after the 1970s, durable residues remain:

1. **Overdetermination and last instance** — portable Marxist vocabulary across sociology, cultural studies, and state theory.
2. **Relative autonomy of instances** — still a default anti-economistic move.
3. **Symptomatic reading** — generalized (sometimes loosely) as attention to textual silences and problematics in the humanities.
4. **Mode of production as research construct** — Balibar’s combination logic and Wolf’s later parsimonious modes both refuse pigeonholing societies while keeping production relations central (different programs, shared allergy to stage typologies).
5. **Anti-humanist caution** — “Man” as theoretical ground of history remains suspect in large parts of social theory even where Althusser is not cited.
6. **Transition as structured** — formal/real subsumption and non-correspondence of connections survive in historical sociology of capitalism’s emergence.
7. **Seminar form of theory** — multi-author theoretical intervention as a genre (less a concept than a practice that *Reading Capital* exemplifies).

What largely did **not** survive as a package: Theory with a capital T as definition of philosophy; confidence in a clean science/ideology dualism; the full anti-historicist refusal of continuous historical narrative; the claim that structural causality had been adequately conceptualized.

## Current Status and Research Frontiers

**Status: declining as named program; absorbed vocabulary.** Live frontiers that still touch this archive:

- Economic anthropology and multipolar production analysis after the formalist–substantivist and structural-Marxist waves.
- Value-form theory and *Capital* reading groups that partly re-run 1965 questions without Althusserian branding.
- Ideology theory after ISA (pending primary ingest) — must not be back-projected onto *For Marx* / *Reading Capital*.
- Postcolonial and feminist critiques of bearer-language and of the Eurocentric mode lists (Asiatic mode debates).
- Archaeological theory’s selective Marxism (contingency vs necessity).

In this wiki the dual charter is now primary; the Theory Hub can be updated when Godelier monographs or the ISA essay are ingested. The anthropological wing’s empirical cases remain the largest coverage gap relative to the philosophical density of 1965.

### How the dual charter should be used (editorial)

Treat *For Marx* and *Reading Capital* as **complementary, not redundant**. *For Marx* is the essayistic charter of contradiction, break, and anti-humanism; *Reading Capital* is the seminar’s demonstration that those theses reorganize the *object* of *Capital* and the basic concepts of historical materialism. Filing ISA/interpellation under either volume is a bookkeeping error. Filing Balibar’s combination under “Althusser said” without attribution to Part Five is a second error. Filing Rancière’s residual-anthropology finding as if it refuted the break, without noting that it is an *internal* Althusserian result, is a third. The multi-author structure is content: each part owns a problem (reading; critique; exposition; object; HM concepts; plan).

### Relation to adjacent programs in this wiki

- **[[historical-materialism]]:** structural Marxism claims to be HM’s scientific self-consciousness, not a rival ontology; whether that claim holds is a position on the [[marxist-humanism-debate]] and among historicist Marxisms, not a wiki-voice verdict.
- **[[structuralism]]:** shared anti-humanist and anti-empiricist climate; divergent objects (kinship/myth systems vs social formations and *Capital*). Godelier’s “Marx was a structuralist” is a wing slogan, not Althusser’s self-description in 1965.
- **[[western-marxism]]:** often listed as the cultural-Hegelian recovery of Marx against which Althusser defines himself; the label is historiographic and contested.
- **[[practice-theory]] / [[structuration-theory]]:** later Critical Turn answers to structure/agency that partly absorb and partly refuse *Träger* language — different primary bases.
- **Wolf’s modes:** research-orienting parsimony after the structural-Marxist wave; not the same combination grid, but a related refusal of stage pigeonholing.

### Collection coverage note (hub)

Strong: philosophical charter (*For Marx*, *Reading Capital* Complete Edition). Moderate: critical reception (Sahlins). Weak: Godelier monographs as primary; Terray/Meillassoux full texts; ISA 1970; Althusser’s later aleatory materialism; non-French national receptions (Latin American Althusserianism, British *New Left Review* debates) as primary sources.

### Counterfactual note (program-level)

Without the ENS seminar form and the Maspero *Théorie* series, Althusser’s essayistic interventions in *For Marx* might have remained a one-author philosophical revision of Marxism. The multi-author *Reading Capital* is what turned a set of theses into a **research program** with specialized tasks (critique history, exposition, object, HM concepts, plan) and a generation of readers trained to reproduce the method. Without the 1968 abridgment, English-language reception would likely have been less “Althusser-and-Balibar-centric” and more visibly plural; the Complete Edition partly undoes that path-dependency for future scholarship. Without the anthropological wing, structural Marxism would be a chapter in the history of Marxist philosophy rather than a force in economic anthropology’s 1970s debates — and Sahlins’s critique would lack its principal target.

## Verbatim Passages with Analysis

**1. Guilty reading (*Reading Capital* Part One).** “But as there is no such thing as an innocent reading, we must say what reading we are guilty of. … To read *Capital* as philosophers is precisely to question the specific object of a specific discourse, and the specific relationship between this discourse and its object.” Analysis: the “crime” is not bias to be confessed away but the constitutive non-innocence of any theoretical reading; innocence is the ideology of transparency. The question “what is it to read?” is not preparatory — it *is* the philosophical object.

**2. Symptomatic silence.** “A ‘symptomatic’ reading is necessary to make these lacunae perceptible, and to identify behind the spoken words the discourse of the silence.” Analysis: silence is not lack of speech but another discourse; the method is Freud-shaped but epistemological. It licenses reading Marx against Marx without psychologizing him.

**3. Structural causality / *Darstellung*.** “It can be entirely summed up in the concept of ‘*Darstellung*’, the key epistemological concept of the whole Marxist theory of value, the concept whose object is precisely to designate the mode of presence of the structure in its effects, and therefore to designate structural causality itself.” And: “the structure is immanent in its effects… nothing outside its effects.” Analysis: *Darstellung* is stolen from ordinary “presentation” and made the name of a causality type classical philosophy lacked; Spinozist immanence blocks both exterior mechanical cause and interior expressive essence.

**4. Authorless theatre.** “…the theatre whose spectators can, on occasion, be spectators only because they are first of all forced to be its actors, caught by the constraints of a script and parts whose authors they cannot be, since it is in essence an authorless theatre.” Analysis: the most famous metaphor of anti-humanist structure; spectators/actors = agents as bearers; authorlessness = no constitutive subject of the structure. Critics hear fatalism; Althusser hears scientific description of constraint.

**5. Combination (Balibar).** “What Marx called a combination is not therefore a simple relationship between the ‘factors’ of any production, but the relationship between these two connections and their interdependence.” Analysis: the dual-connection thesis is the technical heart of the HM reconstruction; without it, “mode of production” collapses into factors-list or stage-label.

**6. Transition (Balibar).** “The transition cannot be a moment of destructuration, however brief. It is itself a movement subject to a structure which has to be discovered. … they are therefore themselves modes of production.” Analysis: blocks both empty interregnum and pure quantity→quality dialectic; transition is a research object with its own combination (often non-homologous connections).

**7. Break / anti-historicism.** “Marxism is, in a single movement and by virtue of the unique epistemological rupture which established it, an anti-humanism and an anti-historicism.” Analysis: the double negative is deliberate; humanism and historicism are treated as the same ideological problematic’s two faces (Man and continuous Time as expressive grounds).
