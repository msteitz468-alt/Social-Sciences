# Four Ontologies (Descola) — Deep Analysis

[[descola-philippe|Philippe Descola]]'s *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* (Gallimard 2005; Chicago English 2013) is the systematic statement of a comparative anthropology of **ontology**: a claim that human ways of detecting continuity and discontinuity between self and other beings, along two planes — [[interiority-and-physicality|interiority and physicality]] — yield four stable combinatorial formulas, each generating characteristic collectives, moralities, and relational styles. The program retains Lévi-Straussian structural ambition while overturning the nature/culture opposition Lévi-Strauss had made foundational. It became a charter text of the early-twenty-first-century "ontological turn" without being reducible to that label.

## Intellectual Origins and Context

The program has three roots Descola himself narrates.

**First, Achuar ethnography.** Acknowledgments open with the [[achuar]] of Ecuadorian Amazonia as the people who "initially propelled" him to "question earlier certainties." In the gardens and hunting territories of the Achuar, plants were treated under consanguine templates (women and crops as mother–child) and game under affine templates (men, prey, and spirit masters as relatives by marriage). Continuity of sociality with nonhumans was not a poetic metaphor but ordinary practice. That experience made the universal [[nature-culture-distinction]] unusable as an analytic baseline.

**Second, structuralism retained and revised.** Descola's Achuar thesis was directed by [[levi-strauss-claude|Claude Lévi-Strauss]]. He claims fidelity to the *spirit* of the method — limited combinatorial repertoires, transformational comparison, structures of relations — while disagreeing on "details," notably the nature/culture hinge and the classificatory dissolution of [[totemism]]. Against the late-twentieth-century swing that treated structural analysis as "icy objectivism" nobody "ever held," he insists that abandoning dualism must not abandon the search for morphologies of practice.

**Third, a dialogue network.** Anne Christine Taylor, Eduardo Viveiros de Castro ([[perspectivism]]), [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]], and Tim Ingold are named as decade-long interlocutors. The book is thus both a personal magnum opus and a node in a French and Amazonian conversation about modernity, hybrids, and nonhuman persons — published just as Anglophone anthropology was absorbing "the ontological turn."

The polemical context is the exhaustion of culturalism as residual category: non-Western cosmologies treated as "beliefs about nature" while naturalism's dualism remained the unexamined frame. Descola's neo-Copernican claim (Sahlins's Foreword) is that other people's worlds do not revolve around ours.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

### 1. Identification upstream of taxonomy and society

Descola defines [[modes-of-identification|identification]] as the elementary capacity to "apprehend and separate out some of the continuities and discontinuities" between self and environment — "well upstream from the categorizations of beings and things that taxonomies reveal." It is prepredicative (Husserlian language) rather than an empirical natural-history judgment. Crucially, **ontology is analytically prior to sociology**: stabilized relational systems are subordinate to systems of properties attributed to beings. That reverses the Durkheimian default in which cosmologies are projections of social morphology.

### 2. Interiority and physicality (not mind/body as Western dualism)

The two axes:

> The vague term "interiority" refers to a range of properties recognized by all human beings and partially covers what we generally call the mind, the soul, or consciousness: intentionality, subjectivity, reflexivity, feelings, and the ability to express oneself and to dream… Physicality, in contrast, concerns external form, substance, the physiological, perceptive and sensorimotor processes…

Descola insists this is **not** a simple ethnocentric export of Cartesian dualism: every documented civilization objectivizes some such dual plane, even when persons are dividual or composite. Continuity or discontinuity of *each* axis between humans and nonhumans yields four cells.

### 3. The four ontologies (Figure 1)

| | Physicalities continuous | Physicalities discontinuous |
|---|---|---|
| **Interiorities continuous** | **[[totemism]]** (ontological) | **[[animism]]** |
| **Interiorities discontinuous** | **[[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]]** | **[[analogism]]** |

**Animism** (restored): attribution to nonhumans of an interiority identical to humans'; discontinuity by physicality — "humans in disguise" differentiated by feathers, fur, scales, bark; form and ethogram more than substance. Culture extends to nonhumans. Amerindian myths narrate emergence of natural discontinuities from an original cultural continuum — inverting the naturalist story of culture emerging from nature.

**Naturalism**: reverse of animism — continuity of physicalities (one material world under natural laws) + discontinuity of interiorities (mind, culture, language as human prerogative). This is the formula that *produces* the modern nature/culture dualism. Viveiros's gloss (endorsed with terminological caution): naturalism is multiculturalist; animism multinaturalist.

**Totemism (ontological)**: continuity of *both* interiorities and physicalities within pre-differentiated hybrid classes — not Lévi-Strauss's homology of *differences* between natural and social series, but a "socio-natural image, single but fragmented" (Descola recovering what Lévi-Strauss set aside). Australia (Dreaming; Elkin inventory; Brandenstein semantics) is exemplary; Algonquin cases show hybrid presence inside animist regimes.

**Analogism**: discontinuity of *both* axes — a world of singularities recomposed by analogy, correspondence, hierarchy, and sacrifice (Great Chain of Being; Nahua component ontologies; West African systems). Collective form is one mixed hierarchical cosmos-society, not hybrid segments.

### 4. Modes of relation cross-cut identification

[[modes-of-relation|Modes of relation]] — give, take, exchange, produce, protect, transmit — are analytically independent of identification. Within one animism, Jivaro/Achuar predation, Tukanoan balanced exchange, and Campa/Algonquin sharing can each dominate as moral horizon. Production is not universal human–world relation. Collectives are fields of relational schemas with dominant ethos and subordinate niches.

### 5. Heuristic status and relative universalism

Preface and Epilogue insist hypotheses are heuristic; secondary-source reach outside Amazonia requires Weberian humility. Method is **relative universalism** — relational comparison free of naturalism's particular universalism — not a reform program exporting Amazonian wisdom to the climate crisis.

## The Empirical Program

### Hits

- **Amazonian animism** is the program's strongest empirical success: Achuar garden/hunt sociality; Makuna/Wari' self-seeing of animals as cultural persons; multi-species collectives; predation/exchange/gift triad as relational variation under one identification.
- **Genealogical demolition of naturalist universalism** (Ch.3): landscape perspective, *phusis*, creation theology, autonomy of Nature and Culture, dualism as anthropology's default — a history of the West as one cosmology among others.
- **Australian ontological totemism**: recovery of Dreaming as continuous cosmogenic framework and of class-level fusion of humans and nonhumans against pure classificatory reading.
- **Analogist dossiers** (Nahua; West Africa; chain of being): dense secondary synthesis showing double-discontinuity cosmologies as a *type*, not residual exoticism.
- **Naturalism's internal heterodoxies** (animal culture, language, rights, land ethic): Descola's claim that these press but do not yet exit the mode is a sharp presentist diagnosis.

### Misses and anomalies

- **Secondary-source dependence** outside Amazonia is structural; Australian, Nahua, African, and Siberian materials are filtered through specialized literatures Descola cannot fully control (his own caveat).
- **Hybridity and non-exclusivity** of modes (Algonquin totems inside animism; Siberian transitions) soften the four cells — strength as realism, weakness as typology.
- **China** is noted (correlative cosmology) but deliberately underdeveloped relative to Mexico — a gap in the analogist cell.
- **Change theory** (Ch.15) sketches paths (gift-animism → protective analogism via pastoralism; taming ≠ protodomestication) without a full historical mechanism — structure-before-genesis remains methodological priority more than demonstrated dynamics.
- **Perspectivism boundary**: whether crossed inversion of viewpoints is rare outside Amazonia is asserted from incomplete Asian comparison.

| Claim | Supporting evidence | Challenging evidence | Current standing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nature/culture is local to naturalism, not universal | Achuar, Chewong, Cree, Aboriginal, Nahua cases; Ch.3 genealogy | Some dualisms appear outside "the West"; translation risk | widely influential; contested at margins |

| Heterodoxies do not exit naturalism | animal rights still dualist problem-space | multi-species ethnography claims exit | open as of 2005+ |

## Method Commitments

- Two axes as comparative instruments, not metaphysical substances.
- Elementary schemas of practice "upstream" of habitus and of instituted norms (Bourdieu engaged, not discarded).
- Structural morphology: limited range of relations enables typology of compatibilities.
- Relative universalism vs particular (naturalist) universalism.
- Heuristic models; Weberian humility on secondary sources.
- Sensitive refusal to convert the book into political blueprint (Epilogue).

## Major Debates and Rivals

1. **Vs universal nature/culture** — Ortner, early feminist anthropology (used the dualism); Strathern and MacCormack (critiqued it); Descola generalizes the critique into a positive fourfold alternative (see [[nature-culture-gender-debate]], [[nature-culture-distinction]]).
2. **Vs Lévi-Straussian totemism** — classification retained as *one* reading; ontological fusion restored as another combinatorial cell.
3. **Vs/alongside Viveiros perspectivism** — shared Amazonian ground; Descola: crossed inversion is optional graft, not definition of all animism.
4. **Vs pure practice/anti-structure** — retains morphology against agency-only anthropology.
5. **Vs Latourian ANT** — allied on hybrids and collectives; Descola insists on finite combinatorial ontology rather than open network description alone.
6. **Vs multi-species / political ecology export** — Epilogue: Amazonian or Tibetan "wisdom" is not a portable solution to biosphere crisis; each ontology is a historical compromise.

## Critiques and Responses

- **Typological reification:** four boxes flatten history. Descola's reply: schemas are not exclusive total models; hybrids allowed; heuristic.
- **Amazonia-centrism:** strongest cases are where he did fieldwork. Reply: Weberian caveat; invitation to correct.
- **Eurocentric axes:** interiority/physicality smuggle dualism. Reply: axes are analytic, cross-culturally objectivized, not Cartesian substances.
- **Political quietism:** refusing reform export disappoints engaged readers. Reply: theory's job is comparative grammar, not cosmopolitical design.
- **Ontological turn excesses:** later "ontology" talk sometimes abandons evidence. Descola's book is more structural-comparative than speculative metaphysics — a distinction worth preserving.


## Verbatim Key Passages

**On the two axes (Ch.5):**

> The vague term "interiority" refers to a range of properties recognized by all human beings and partially covers what we generally call the mind, the soul, or consciousness: intentionality, subjectivity, reflexivity, feelings, and the ability to express oneself and to dream… Physicality, in contrast, concerns external form, substance, the physiological, perceptive and sensorimotor processes… So physicality is not simply the material aspect of organic and abiotic bodies; it is the whole set of visible and tangible expressions of the dispositions peculiar to a particular entity…

Rhetoric: Descola inoculates against the charge of Cartesian projection by claiming cross-cultural objectivation of the dual plane, then operationalizes it combinatorially. The move is classic structuralist — reduce surface diversity to a small matrix — under an anti-dualist banner.

**On animism restored (Ch.6):**

> That characteristic is the attribution by humans to nonhumans of an interiority identical to their own. This attribution humanizes plants and, above all, animals… This similarity of interiorities justifies extending a state of "culture" to nonhumans… these, as it were, humans in disguise… are distinct from humans precisely by reason of their outward apparel of feathers, fur, scales, or bark—in other words, their physicality.

Rhetoric: "humans in disguise" inverts naturalist common sense (animals as incomplete humans) into animist common sense (animals as differently clothed persons). "Culture" is deliberately re-exported to nonhumans to break naturalism's monopoly on the term.

**On naturalism as reverse of animism (Ch.8):**

> the counterpart of animism is not totemism, as I had earlier supposed, but naturalism. For the naturalist schema reverses the formula of animism: on the one hand, articulating a discontinuity of interiorities and a continuity of physicalities and, on the other hand, reversing their hierarchical order, with the universal laws of matter and life providing naturalism with a paradigm…

Rhetoric: self-correction of Descola's earlier work models the structural method — terms redefine by position in a system of transformations, not by dictionary inheritance.

**On ontology before sociology (Ch.5 close):**

> I have accordingly had to reject the sociocentric assumption and opt for the idea that sociological realities (stabilized relational systems) are analytically subordinate to ontological realities (the systems of properties attributed to existing beings). That is the price that has to be paid if animism and totemism are to be reborn with new meanings.

Rhetoric: "price to be paid" frames a Kuhnian stake — Durkheimian sociology of religion cannot host the rebirth. The sentence is the program's declaration of independence from the Année sociologique default.

**On Epilogue anti-export:**

> it would be mistaken to think that the Indians of Amazonia, the Australian Aboriginals, or the monks of Tibet can bring us a deeper wisdom for the present time than the shaky naturalism of late modernity… none of those compromises, however worthy of admiration some may be, can provide a source of instruction valid for all situations.

Rhetoric: blocks both primitivist nostalgia and scholarly prophetic wishful thinking. The theory's hospitality is cognitive (better grammar of diversity), not political (ready-made cohabitation regime).

## Extended Architecture: Collectives and Morals

Part IV maps each ontology to a collective morphology and a "metaphysics of morals":

| Ontology | Collective | Subject/epistemic problem |
|---|---|---|
| Animism | Monospecific "tribes" (each species a people) | Body-otherness; food-soul dilemma; invasive self |
| Naturalism | Exclusive human societies + asocial nature | Mind-otherness; culture-in-nature problem; thinking reed |
| Totemism | Hybrid complementary segments (Dreaming classes) | Dream-subjects; singularizing fused classes |
| Analogism | One mixed hierarchical cosmos-society | Diffuse subjectivities; amalgamation and politics of signatures |

Descola's labels **anthropogenic** (animism), **anthropocentric** (naturalism), and **cosmogenic** (totemism) mark different directions of world-production. Analogism's hierarchical encompassment (Dumont is an acknowledged resource) organizes difference without collapsing into either hybrid segments or exclusive humanity.

## Extended Architecture: Relations and Histories

Part V's six relational schemas are not a second typology competing with the four ontologies but a cross-cutting grammar. The Amazonian triad — Jivaro predation, Tukano exchange, Campa/Algonquin sharing — is Descola's best demonstration that **identification does not determine relation**. Ch.15's historical sketches (Chukchee Caribou-Man; Exirit-Bulagat Lord Bull; Amazonian taming vs Old World domestication) argue that identification tends to precede and constrain relation, that gift-animism can tip toward protective analogism under pastoral hierarchy, and that Amazonian pet-keeping is not protodomestication on a naturalist developmental ladder.

Compatibility matrix (program-level): gift and predation highly developed under animism; protection under pastoral/analogist hierarchy; production under naturalism; transmission under totemic actualization. The matrix is the book's least ethnographically footnoted apparatus and its most generative for later comparative work — also its most vulnerable to "just so" charges.

## Institutional and Pedagogical Afterlife

Beyond monographs, the four regimes were staged as a visual installation at the Musée du quai Branly (Paris), translating combinatorial ontology into museum pedagogy. Teaching uptake is high: the 2×2 table is now a standard advanced-undergraduate device for "beyond nature/culture" modules, often paired with Viveiros and with multi-species ethnography (Tsing and others — related but distinct programs). The risk of pedagogical flattening (four boxes as census categories) is real; Descola's own non-exclusivity and hybridity clauses are the corrective that classrooms often drop.

## Relation to the Broader Ontological Turn

Descola's program is **not identical** to the Holbraad–Pedersen "ontological turn" in British social anthropology, nor to Viveiros's perspectivism, nor to Latour's modes of existence. Shared: refusal to treat non-Western cosmologies as mistaken beliefs about a single nature. Different: Descola's finite combinatorial structuralism vs open-ended recursive "ontography"; Descola's two axes vs Viveiros's body-perspective emphasis; Descola's comparative table vs Latour's network and diplomatic programs. For this wiki, keep [[four-ontologies]] as Descola's research program; use [[perspectivism]] for Viveiros; do not merge into a single "ontology" page.

## What Survived

What entered lasting anthropological vocabulary from this program:

1. **Nature/culture as local naturalist formula** — taught as standard critique, not fringe.
2. **Animism restored** as relational ontology (with Bird-David, Viveiros, Hallowell lineage) rather than Tylorian deficit religion.
3. **Interiority/physicality grid** as portable teaching device — even critics use the table.
4. **Collectives including nonhumans** — convergence with multi-species ethnography and ANT, different generative path.
5. **Perspectivism/animism distinction** — productive research question, not settled doctrine.
6. **Structuralist revival under anti-dualist banner** — proof that morphology of practice need not equal nature/culture universalism.

What did **not** survive whole: confidence that four cells are exhaustive and stable; hope that the book alone would reorganize all comparative anthropology; any simple political ecology of "becoming animist."


## Worked Comparative Miniatures

**Achuar garden and hunt (animism).** Women address plants as children; men address game and spirit masters as affines. The same kinship grammar that organizes human marriage organizes nonhuman attachment. Garden and forest are not wild vs domestic dual zones but regions of different social partners. Predation toward distant affines and game coexists with reciprocity islands inside the local kindred — relation variation under one identification.

**Australian Dreaming classes (totemism).** Existing beings actualize Dreaming prototypes by duplication/formation rather than generational filiation alone. Clan–species links are ontological fusions within hybrid classes, not merely heraldic labels for human groups. Descola uses this against pure classificatory dissolution without reinstating Frazerian mystical participation as evolutionary stage.

**Nahua component person (analogism).** Multiple components, destinies, nagual/tona co-essences, and directional/humoral correspondences recompose a world of singularities. Hierarchy and sacrifice mediate discontinuities. Descola's point is structural: this is the same combinatorial cell as the Great Chain of Being, not a Mesoamerican exotic.

**Modern animal rights (naturalism under stress).** Singer, Regan, Leopold, and French legal "animal person" debates remain inside naturalism: they renegotiate the *boundary* of who has interiority-like moral status while leaving continuous physical nature and discontinuous mind as the problem-space. Descola reads this as heterodoxy *within* the mode, not exit from it — a claim dated to 2005 and open to multi-species challenge.

**Siberian pastoral tip (history of structures).** From Caribou-Man / Rich-Forest alliance logic (animist gift/affinity with wild beings) toward Lord Bull protective hierarchy (ancestors, celestial bull, grace that may be withheld) — a sketched path from animist to analogist collective form under livestock dominance. Identification change trails practical regime change more slowly than functionalist accounts expect.
