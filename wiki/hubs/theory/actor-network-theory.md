# Actor-Network Theory — Deep Analysis

> Theory Hub. Summary: [[actor-network-theory]]. Charter intro: *[[latour-reassembling-the-social-2005|Reassembling the Social]]* (2005). Precursor empirical: *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]*.

All programmatic claims **attributed** to Latour 2005 unless noted.

## Intellectual Origins and Context

Latour presents ANT as the “unusual meaning” of *social* and *social explanation* that puzzled readers of science studies more than the laboratory findings themselves. Genesis: primate sociology teaching with Shirley Strum; synthesis after Callon, Law, Akrich, Mol, Hennion et al.; crash course for organization studies (Czarniawska); Clarendon Lectures at Saïd Business School (Woolgar connection). The book is an opinionated systematic introduction, not a neutral survey.

**Reacted against:** “Sociology of the social” — Society as special domain/glue that explains science, law, religion, organizations. Critical sociology’s ready-made Society (Bourdieu-style as Latour reconstructs the foil). Pure constructivism that brackets nonhumans. Jumping to levels (micro/macro) that slash a flat map of associations.

**Developed from:** STS laboratory and network studies; *Laboratory Life* inscription/[[black-box]] practice generalized to all associations.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

### Sociology of the social vs sociology of associations

> Latour aims “to show why the social cannot be construed as a kind of material or domain and to dispute the project of providing a ‘social explanation’ of some other state of affairs.”

“Social” broke when it named both a *movement of assembling* and a *type of ingredient*. Redefine by original re-associating sense so connections can be traced again — faithful to sociology as science of living together, with better tools.

### Five sources of uncertainty (Part I)

| # | Source | Method stake |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | No group, only group formation | Trace spokespersons, anti-groups, boundaries, professionals; performative not ostensive groups |
| 2 | Action is overtaken | Action is not transparent/owned; distributed among many |
| 3 | Objects too have agency | Nonhumans as [[actant|actants]]; not backdrop |
| 4 | Matters of fact vs [[matters-of-concern]] | Reopen black boxes into gatherings under dispute/care |
| 5 | Writing risky accounts | Textual accounts can fail; quality tests for ANT texts |

**Mediators / intermediaries:** intermediaries transport without change; mediators transform. If an intermediary mutates into a mediator, the setup becomes unpredictable. Silk/nylon and Durkheim totem examples appear as teaching boxes in Part I.

### Three moves (Part II) — keep the social flat

1. **Localize the global** — the “global” is produced in local sites of connection.  
2. **Redistribute the local** — face-to-face is framed by silent mediators (doors, windows, hardware); inter-objectivity holds “interaction.”  
3. **Connect sites** — after flattening, connectors and circulating formats become visible; transaction costs of moving/assembling must be paid.

> “an actor-network is what is made to act by a large star-shaped web of mediators flowing in and out of it… attachments are first, actors are second.”

### Conclusion: society to collective

Reassemble a *collective* of heterogeneous entities rather than invoke Society. Political and scientific composition of the common world (compositionism) is the horizon — densified from conclusion claims in range_5.

## Method Commitments

Follow the actors; slow tempo; cartography of controversies; flat default for the *observer*; infra-language quality tests; risky accounts; mediators over causes.

## Major Debates and Rivals

- Critical sociology of domination (Society explains)  
- Strong programme SSK (social explanation of science without full object agency)  
- Managerial/organizational capture of ANT  
- Political quietism charges  

Latour’s dialog interlude (“On the Difficulty of Being an ANT”) stages misunderstandings (everything is a network; no critique; etc.).

## Critiques and Responses

*(Positions.)* Descriptive flatness; insufficient inequality analysis; relativism; jargon. Latour: better maps of composition enable better politics of the common world than premature Society explanations.

## What Survived

Vocabulary: actor-network, actant, mediator/intermediary, matters of concern, follow the actors, black box (from STS). Used across organization studies, geography, anthropology, design — often thinned of full five-uncertainty method.

## Current Status and Research Frontiers

Active program language; post-ANT and “after networks” literature; climate/political ecology extensions in Latour’s later work (not this book’s primary job). Wiki next: *Science in Action*, Callon primary texts.
