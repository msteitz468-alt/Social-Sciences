# Cognitive Fluidity — Deep Analysis

> Theory Hub deep analysis of [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]]'s modular-to-fluid model of mind evolution, charter text *[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* (1996). Summary page: [[cognitive-fluidity]]. All programmatic claims in **attributed voice**.

## Intellectual Origins and Context

Mithen wrote at the intersection of two late-twentieth-century research programs. On the archaeological side, **[[cognitive-archaeology]]** had been pressing beyond New Archaeology's relative silence on mind — Renfrew's cognitive-processual agenda, Thomas Wynn's Piagetian reading of handaxes, and a growing willingness to ask what ancestral minds were doing rather than only how bodies looked and tools functioned. On the psychological side, the modular mind of Jerry Fodor, Howard Gardner's multiple intelligences, and the evolutionary psychology of Leda Cosmides and John Tooby offered a content-rich alternative to blank-slate and single general-purpose computer models of mind. Mithen's preface narrates the personal hinge: Fodor after a 1988 Trinity Hall lunch, a 1994 Santa Barbara dinner with Cosmides and Tooby, and conversations that flipped Merlin Donald's 1991 *Origins of the Modern Mind* — psychologist using archaeology as props — into an archaeologist setting the agenda for psychology (attributed, Mithen 1996).

The polemical targets are clear. Creationist sudden-mind claims are rejected outright. Sponge and general-purpose computer analogies are treated as psychological flat-earth society. Pure Swiss-army-knife modularity is accepted as half the story but charged with failing the "imagination dilemma": if modules are sealed, how does almost unlimited metaphor and creativity arise? Cosmides's own knife analogy, Mithen notes, seems to falsify pure isolation. Modern hunter-gatherer ethnography (Bird-David, Ingold, Gellner as he cites them) shows social and natural domains intimately conflated — not Pleistocene modules running in parallel boxes. Developmental psychology (Greenfield; Karmiloff-Smith's representational redescription; Carey and Spelke's mapping across domains; Rozin's accessibility; Sperber's module of metarepresentation) supplies the missing bridge from domain-specific skill to cross-domain thought. The critical-turn moment is thus: take modular EP seriously *and* take Upper Palaeolithic archaeology seriously, then make the mismatch between large Neanderthal brains and limited symbolic culture into the central puzzle rather than an embarrassment.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

### The three phases

Mithen states the framework explicitly (Ch.4):

> Phase 1. Minds dominated by a domain of general intelligence — a suite of general-purpose learning and decision-making rules. Phase 2. Minds in which general intelligence has been supplemented by multiple specialized intelligences, each devoted to a specific domain of behaviour, and each working in isolation from the others. Phase 3. Minds in which the multiple specialized intelligences appear to be working together, with a flow of knowledge and ideas between behavioural domains.

Phase 3 is **cognitive fluidity**. The phases are a *hypothetical framework* for guiding the study, not a proven phylogeny; he hesitantly invokes recapitulation only for broad scaffolding, with Gouldian cautions against racist misuse and against strict correspondence (attributed).

### Cathedral architecture

The [[cathedral-of-the-mind]] metaphor maps phases onto building history. Phase 1 is a single **nave** of general intelligence — usable anywhere but only for simple, slow, error-prone learning. Phase 2 adds **chapels** of specialized intelligence (Romanesque isolation: thick walls, no access between chapels). Phase 3 opens doors and windows, or builds a Sperberian **superchapel**, so that Gothic light and sound (knowledge and ways of thinking) flow freely. Evolution, like a medieval builder, can only tinker: "Evolution does not have the option of returning to the drawing board and beginning anew; it can only ever modify what has gone before" (attributed). Figures are explicitly not brain maps.

### Specialized intelligences (the chapels)

Assuming Phase 2 minds were hunter-gatherers, Mithen posits at least three chapels that survive as modern intuitive psychology, biology, and physics, plus linguistic intelligence as a fourth of unclear architectural relation:

1. **Social intelligence** — mind-reading, alliance, status (Humphrey's social function of intellect; Byrne and Whiten Machiavellian intelligence; Baron-Cohen mindblindness as selective impairment).
2. **Natural-history intelligence** — animals, plants, landscape geography; prediction from tracks, seasons, cues (Atran folk taxonomy; Keil essences).
3. **Technical intelligence** — fracture dynamics, mental templates, artifact design (Spelke intuitive physics; handaxe and Levallois skill).
4. **Linguistic intelligence** — initially a **social language** (Dunbar, Aiello): grooming-time substitute, social information vehicle — not yet a general-purpose channel for tools and animals.

### The Early Human paradox (Phase 2 diagnosed)

Act 3 (c. 1.8 mya–100 kya) is Mithen's "no-man's land": anatomical change and climatic flux with technological near-stasis after the handaxe and one major innovation (Levallois ~250 kya). He groups *H. erectus*, archaic *Homo*, *H. heidelbergensis*, and Neanderthals as **Early Humans** sharing a generic architecture (similarities > differences). Eight behavioural puzzles drive the diagnosis. Technical intelligence looks modern (symmetry, templates, Levallois expertise modern students rarely match) yet Early Humans ignore worked bone/antler/ivory, make multi-task rather than purpose-dedicated tools, limit composite tools to few components, and show Isaac's "minor, directionless change" across environments (Binford: handaxes without patterned environmental covariation). Natural-history intelligence looks powerful (Old World colonization; Neanderthal glacial survival) while social intelligence does not "reflect" into technology as modern foragers' social worlds do. Language may be complex as *social* language after the second brain spurt (500–200 kya) yet not deliver tool and hunting discourse. The architecture that fits: **strong chapels, solid walls**.

Neanderthal consciousness (Ch.8), on Humphrey's account extended by Mithen, is trapped in the social chapel: skilled toolmaking and foraging may run with "rolling consciousness with swift memory loss," analogous to petit-mal gaps or modern craftspersons' inability to verbalize skill — so the difficulty of imagining "what it is like" to be a Neanderthal is predicted by the model, not evidence against it (Nagel's bat argument as setup; attributed).

### Cognitive fluidity and the cultural explosion (Phase 3)

Between ~60 and 30 kya Mithen sees not one global bang but regional **cultural sparks** (Australia colonization ~60–50 kya; Near East blades ~50–45 kya; Europe ~40 kya; confident global art after ~30 kya). Alternative archaeological explanations (social restructuring, economic specialization, a technological invention, origin of language as sole cause) are consequences or underestimates of Early Human complexity. His cause:

> My explanation of the big bang of human culture is that this is when the final major re-design of the mind took place. It is when the doors and windows were inserted in the chapel walls, or perhaps when a new 'superchapel' was constructed.

**Art** requires three processes already present in Early Humans but in *different* domains: planning a preconceived form (technical), intentional communication about displaced referents (social), attributing meaning to marks not associated with their referent (natural history — track reading). Art is their seamless combination. Earliest pieces (Hohlenstein-Stadel lion-man ~30–33 kya; Chauvet ~30 kya) show high skill — "nothing primitive" — because the components pre-existed; only the connection was missing. Neanderthals under extreme adaptive stress produced no art: "They lacked the capacity to do so" (attributed capacity argument, not leisure argument).

**Religion** (via Boyer and Tylor as Mithen cites): supernatural beings violate intuitive biology/physics while remaining intentional agents — domain-mixing Early Humans could not form. Anthropomorphic images, deep-cave ritual loci, and investment burials (Sungir) are archaeological hints, not proofs, of first religious ideologies.

**Anthropomorphism, totemism, complex tools, adornment, information-storing art, and a second global colonization pulse** complete the suite of multi-domain products. Science, in the Ch.11 synthesis, shares three properties with art and religion as fluidity products: hypothesis generation (already in specialized domains), tools and artificial memory systems (D'Errico), and cross-domain metaphor/analogy as "tools of thought" (Fodor's passion for analogy; Kuhn; Dennett).

### Mechanism: language and consciousness

Chapter 10's proposition: "once Early Humans started talking, they just couldn't stop." Social language carries non-social snippets (from general-intelligence domain interfaces and incomplete chapel isolation). Recipients decode snippets in social intelligence — the social chapel is invaded. Selection favors exploiting invasions for hunting and tool decisions and for generating more non-social talk; Mithen guesses social → general-purpose language between ~150 and 50 kya. Social intelligence expands into Sperber's **module of metarepresentation** (superchapel). Consciousness shifts from predicting others to managing a multi-domain database. Female provisioning under extended infant dependency is a strong selective pressure for first integrating social and natural-history intelligence; technical integration follows. No brain-size increase is required — "new connections rather than new processing power."

### Two-step timing and Early Modern half-way house

Partial fluidity among Early Modern *H. sapiens sapiens* ~100 kya: Skhul/Qafzeh animal parts in graves and more efficient seasonal hunting (social × natural history) with tools still Neanderthal-like (technical isolated); southern African ochre, rare burial, blades, Katanda bone harpoons as "drifts" in and out of fluidity not yet fixed. Full technical integration 60–30 kya is staggered parallel evolution with "evolutionary momentum." Mithen adopts single African origin with replacement, sympathetic to limited hybridization (attributed position; pre-aDNA horizon).

### Long-run oscillation (Ch.11)

Charted over 65 my of primate evolution, specialization and generalization **oscillate**: hard-wired specialized modules in plesiadapiforms → general intelligence ~56 mya (omomyids/adapids; Milton diet) → social specialization from ~35 mya → australopithecine brain hiatus (fuel and cooling) → bipedalism relaxes constraints (Wheeler, Falk radiator) → meat and gut reduction (Aiello and Wheeler) → natural-history and technical domains at 1.8–1.4 mya with *H. erectus* and handaxes → social language → fluidity. The specialized intermediate phase is necessary: complex systems require modules developed separately then glued (programmer analogy; Mark Lake on general evolutionary switching). Superficial chimp–human similarity is an architectural fallacy.

### Agriculture as epilogue

Around 10 kya independent farming is a **direct consequence** of fluidity-era thinking meeting terminal-Pleistocene climate — not a knowledge threshold (natural-history intelligence ≥1.8 mya; Wadi Kubbaniya and Abu Hureyra plant intensity), not global population crisis, climate necessary but not sufficient (Early Humans survived ice ages without farming). Four mental changes: (1) intensive plant tools (technical × natural history); (2) plants/animals as prestige media (social × natural history; Soffer mammoth-bone dwellings; Hayden competition); (3) social relationships with plants/animals (domestication as social-like relations; Humphrey's gardener; Natufian dog burial); (4) plants/animals as manipulable artifacts (fire management). Natufian sedentism is Bar-Yosef and Belfer-Cohen's "point of no return." "While the seeds for agriculture may have been first planted 10,000 years ago, they were first laid in the mind at the time of the Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition."

### Dark potential of fluidity

Boxed essays treat racist attitudes and humour as capacities of domain-mixing: Early Humans could not think humans as animals or artifacts; fluid minds *can* (without compulsion). Record as attributed theoretical claims about potential, not as findings about Upper Palaeolithic racism.

| Claim (Mithen 1996) | Supporting evidence he cites | Challenging / later pressure | Current standing (wiki practice) |

| Agriculture from fluidity × climate | Natufian; four propensities; paleopathology of early farmers | Packing, oasis, climate-first models; multi-center diversity | One attributed position among many on [[agricultural-revolution]] |

What the program **explained that predecessors could not** (on its own terms): the lag between modern anatomy and modern culture; Neanderthal large brains without UP package; first art's high quality; why metaphor defines modernity; why agriculture is cognitive epilogue not cognitive mutation.

**Anomalies and failures:** sealed-domain assumption under later symbolic claims; European-weighted "explosion"; pre-aDNA replacement narrative; limited treatment of East Asian and Australian sequences; hard testability of chapel isolation.

## Method Commitments

- Material culture as diagnosis of cognitive architecture (middle-range bridging).
- Explicit use of evolutionary psychology, developmental psychology, and primate analogy — with stated limits (6-mya gap; no over-import of chimp tools into Act 1).
- Paradoxes (modern in one respect, archaic in another) as evidence, not noise.
- Voice: theory always attributed; ethnographic analogy flagged when used for "inside" meanings of art.

## Major Debates and Rivals

- **[[modern-human-origins-debate]]:** Mithen's single African origin with limited-hybridization sympathy; fluidity as competitive edge in replacement — one cognitive layer on the origins debate, not a substitute for genetics/fossils.
- **Processual vs interpretive archaeology:** shares Renfrewian scientific recovery of mind; rejects unstructured empathy; differs from Renfrew's later material-engagement emphasis by prioritizing internal architectural redesign.
- **Pure evolutionary psychology:** accepts modules, rejects sealed modern mind; modern forager domain conflation is evidence *for* fluidity, not against evolved structure.
- **Gradualist / continuous-modernity models:** rival readings of African MSA and late Neanderthal behavior.
- **Donald mimetic/mythic/theoretic stages:** neighboring periodization; different architecture story.

### Verbatim passages (load-bearing)

**Three phases (Ch.4):**

> Phase 1. Minds dominated by a domain of general intelligence — a suite of general-purpose learning and decision-making rules. Phase 2. Minds in which general intelligence has been supplemented by multiple specialized intelligences, each devoted to a specific domain of behaviour, and each working in isolation from the others. Phase 3. Minds in which the multiple specialized intelligences appear to be working together, with a flow of knowledge and ideas between behavioural domains.

Analysis: the wording commits Mithen to *isolation* as a design feature of Phase 2, not mere absence of evidence for symbol. That is the strong claim critics attack when they find limited Early Human ornament: any symbol would count as a crack in the wall. Followers who treat Phase 2 as "mostly specialized" without isolation softens the theory into ordinary multi-domain psychology.

**Imagination dilemma (Ch.1):**

> If the mind is indeed constituted by numerous specialized processes each dedicated to a specific type of behaviour, how can we possibly account for one of the most remarkable features of the modern mind: a capacity for an almost unlimited imagination?

Analysis: this is the hinge that turns EP modularity against itself. The book is not anti-modularity; it is anti-*sealed* modularity as a complete account of modernity. Cognitive fluidity is the proposed resolution — and art, religion, science, racism-potential, and humour are all sold as consequences of that resolution.

**Big bang redesign (Ch.9):**

> My explanation of the big bang of human culture is that this is when the final major re-design of the mind took place. It is when the doors and windows were inserted in the chapel walls, or perhaps when a new 'superchapel' was constructed.

Analysis: "doors/windows" vs "superchapel" keeps mechanism underdetermined — honest relative to Ch.4's open architectural questions. The cultural explosion is *defined* as the archaeological signature of Phase 3, so the risk of circularity is real: material novelty both motivates and "proves" the redesign. Mithen mitigates by arguing that the *components* of art pre-exist in separate domains (handaxe form + social communication + track-reading), so quality of first art is predicted, not surprising.

**Agriculture's mental seeds (Epilogue):**

> while the seeds for agriculture may have been first planted 10,000 years ago, they were first laid in the mind at the time of the Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition. It is this key epoch and not the period of the birth of agriculture that lies at the root of the modern world.

Analysis: this is a periodization claim as much as a causal one. It demotes the Neolithic from "cognitive revolution" status (contra some popular narratives) and promotes the UP as the root of modernity — aligning the book with archaeologists who treat the Middle/Upper transition as the decisive behavioral break, while still needing Holocene ecology for farming itself.

**Manifesto close (Ch.11):**

> And so, if you wish to know about the mind, do not ask only psychologists and philosophers: make sure you also ask an archaeologist.

Analysis: institutional claim for cognitive archaeology's autonomy. The book's dual audience (general reader + notes for scholars) performs the same boundary work: archaeology is not illustration for psychology but a co-equal source of constraint on theories of mind.

### Empirical hits in more detail

**Hit 1 — lag anatomy/culture.** Modern brain size long before UP package is the book's central archaeological fact-pattern. The second brain spurt (500–200 kya) without correlating cultural revolution is especially load-bearing: if intelligence scaled with cubic centimeters, Act 3 should look different. Fluidity without further enlargement is an elegant fit (attributed).

**Hit 2 — Neanderthal skill without art.** Levallois difficulty and glacial survival undercut "not smart enough" and "too busy surviving" stories. Capacity-without-connection is a sharper middle-range hypothesis than raw IQ ranking (attributed).

**Hit 3 — first art quality.** Chauvet and Hohlenstein-Stadel as non-primitive first art supports recombination over gradual symbol evolution from scribbles (attributed). This remains a powerful teaching point even if earlier African marks complicate the timeline.

**Hit 4 — Early Modern half-way house.** Skhul/Qafzeh ritual + hunting with Neanderthal-like tools is a non-binary intermediate — better than all-or-nothing modernity — and anticipates later "mosaic" behavioral-modernity models (attributed).

**Miss 1 — African MSA density.** Later work on Howiesons Poort, Blombos, and related assemblages pressures the European-weighted explosion (post-1996 literature; flag when ingested).

**Miss 2 — Neanderthal symbol claims.** If Bruniquel, eagle talons, and pigments indicate more symbol than 1996 allowed, Phase 2 walls need thinning or Neanderthals need reclassification toward partial fluidity (positions).

**Miss 3 — testability of social-only language.** Endocasts and hyoids do not distinguish social-only from general-purpose language; the mechanism is almost entirely theoretical.


## What Survived

Regardless of whether every chapel wall holds, several elements persist in subsequent work and teaching:

1. **Cognitive fluidity** as a named target for what modern minds do that modular accounts alone cannot.
2. **Architecture over brain size** — large brains are not cultural modernity; connections matter.
3. **Domain recombination** as a generative account of art, religion, anthropomorphism, and science-like analogy.
4. **Agriculture as cognitive epilogue** — mental capacities predate Holocene farming.
5. **Archaeology of mind as agenda-setting** — material culture can constrain models of cognition, not only illustrate them.
6. The **cathedral** and **Swiss army knife** images as durable pedagogy inside [[cognitive-archaeology]].

## Current Status and Research Frontiers

Active as a reference model in cognitive archaeology, public science, and evolutionary psychology of culture. Frontiers that re-open the 1996 synthesis: African behavioral modernity timelines; Neanderthal symbolic packages; aDNA admixture and demography; material-engagement and extended-mind theories (Renfrew/Malafouris) that relocate "architecture" partly outside the skull; experimental and developmental tests of domain isolation; climate–cognition couplings for agricultural origins. The wiki should densify these as sources are ingested, preserving Mithen 1996 as a dated position rather than overwriting it.
