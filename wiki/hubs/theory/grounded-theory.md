## Intellectual Origins and Context

Grounded theory, as a **named method-theory program**, is a mid-1960s American sociological intervention into the postwar theory–research gap. Glaser and Strauss open with an "ironic conjunction of careers": Glaser trained at Columbia — the orbit of Merton's middle-range theory and Lazarsfeld's quantitative methodology — while Strauss came from the "Chicago tradition" (1920s–1950s) of down-to-earth qualitative research that, in their own barbed summary, was associated with less-than-rigorous methodology and unintegrated presentation of theory. The partnership is not biographical color; it is the program's self-positioning: neither tradition, they argue, had closed the "embarrassing gap between theory and empirical research" that Blumer had commented on in 1941 and that Merton had optimistically addressed in 1949.

What the program **reacted against** is as important as what it inherited:

1. **Verification primacy.** Attempts to close the gap had concentrated on methods for *testing* theory — with success — while generation remained secondary or lost. Canons of sampling, coding, reliability, validity, indicators, and "proof" derived from quantitative verification, G&S argue, freeze energy needed for discovery.
2. **Logico-deductive and grand theory.** Theory generated by logical deduction from a priori assumptions; "grand theory" so influential that many researchers treat it as synonymous with theory and thus as of little research relevance. Their Epilogue polemic targets Merton's 1964 capsule on theory outrunning systematic empirical research: speculative generation illustrated by apt cases, "systematic" equated with quantitative, qualitative demoted as impressionistic or merely clinical.
3. **Serendipity as insufficient.** Merton's "theoretic functions of research," they claim, reach grounded *modification* of theory via unanticipated anomalous findings (serendipity) but not purposeful discovery of grounded theory — discovery left on a surprise basis rather than designed as research strategy.
4. **Verification-side answers to Blumer's gap.** They read Blumer's critique of the theory–data gap (and his later critique of operationalism) as having thrown weight toward verification problems rather than toward *how to generate* theory beyond the formula of gifted mind + rich experience + data.
5. **Descriptive qualitative work without theory generation.** Chicago-style ethnography valued but, in their diagnosis, often under-theorized or unverified in presentation.

**Production context.** The book was made possible by Public Health Service Research Grant NU-00047 (Division of Nursing, Bureau of State Services–Community Health). Sister monographs under the grant using the same methods/position: *Awareness of Dying* (Glaser & Strauss, Aldine 1965), *The Nurse and the Dying Patient* (Jeanne C. Quint, Macmillan 1967), and forthcoming *Time for Dying* (trajectory of dying). Clinical hospital settings — awareness contexts, social loss, lingering death — supply the program's running illustrations. That is not incidental: applied criteria in Ch. X (fit, understandability, generality, partial control) are written for practitioners (nurses, doctors) as much as for sociologists.

**Disciplinary moment.** *Discovery* appears in 1967, at the hinge of postwar expansion and the critical turn: Parsonsian systems theory and Bureau-style survey research still dominate U.S. sociology's prestige structure (the same dual target Mills attacked in 1959 — see [[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]]), while interactionist and qualitative currents reorganize. Grounded theory offers neither Mills's classic-craft polemic alone nor Blumer's naturalistic method alone; it offers **named procedures** for generating theory that claim scientific status without equating science with verification protocols.

## Core Claims and Conceptual Architecture

### Discovery of theory from data

The basic theme (Ch. I): discovery of theory from data systematically obtained from social research. Every chapter formulates processes of research for generating theory. Generating grounded theory is "a way of arriving at theory suited to its supposed uses," contrasted with theory generated by logical deduction from a priori assumptions.

> The basic theme in our book is the discovery of theory from data systematically obtained from social research.

They refuse a logical conflict between verifying and generating during a research project, but diagnose a **primacy conflict** reflecting opposition between desire to generate and trained need to verify — with verification primary on the contemporary scene.

### Fit and work; five jobs of theory

Theory in sociology, for G&S, should: (1) enable prediction and explanation of behavior; (2) be useful in theoretical advance; (3) be usable in practical applications (understanding and some control); (4) provide a perspective/stance toward data; (5) guide and provide a style for research on particular areas. Theory is thus a **strategy for handling data**, providing modes of conceptualization for describing and explaining. Categories and hypotheses must be clear enough for later verification and operationalization where appropriate; understandable to sociologists of any viewpoint, students, and significant laymen.

> By "fit" we mean that the categories must be readily (not forcibly) applicable to and indicated by the data under study; by "work" we mean that they must be meaningfully relevant to and be able to explain the behavior under study.

**Analysis of commitment.** Fit blocks forcing data into imported schemes; work blocks empty formalization. Together they reverse the speculative license (Epilogue) that assumes theory fits until disproven and that data should be made to fit theory. The five jobs refuse a pure "theory for theorists" or pure "application manual" split: generation is scientific *and* practical.

### Comparative analysis as general method

Comparative analysis is a general method, as experimental and statistical methods are (all use the logic of comparison), usable for social units of **any** size — not restricted to large-scale units (organizations, nations, world regions) as some usages imply. G&S use it strategically for **generation**. Other uses of comparison they inventory (Ch. II and especially Ch. VI): accurate evidence, empirical generalization, specifying and clarifying concepts, verifying theory — legitimate but not their primary purpose here.

### Theoretical sampling

> Theoretical sampling is the process of data collection for generating theory whereby the analyst jointly collects, codes, and analyzes his data and decides what data to collect next and where to find them, in order to develop his theory as it emerges. This process of data collection is controlled by the emerging theory, whether substantive or formal.

Initial decisions rest on general perspective and problem area, not preconceived framework; "local" concepts give foothold. Sampling strategies include maximizing and minimizing differences among comparison groups, selecting "slices of data," varying depth on core categories, and continuing until **theoretical saturation**:

Saturation means that no additional data are being found whereby the sociologist can develop properties of the category. As he sees similar instances over and over again, the researcher becomes empirically confident that a category is saturated.

**Analysis of commitment.** This inverts verification sampling: the population is not fixed in advance; the emerging conceptual structure governs the path. Cost: non-statistical generalizability and dependence on analytic judgment. Payoff claimed: efficient development of dense, relevant theory rather than representative description of a pre-fixed universe.

### Substantive and formal theory

**Substantive theory** is grounded in research on one particular substantive area (work, delinquency, medical education, mental health, dying care). **Formal theory** addresses a conceptual sociological domain. G&S argue formal theory *can* be generated directly from data but is "most desirable, and usually necessary," to start from substantive theory — which provides stimulus, initial categories/properties, and modes of integration. One-area rewriting alone yields inadequate formal theory; multi-area comparative analysis is the path. They warn against logico-deductive elaboration that produces the "freelance clerk" of formal categories without grounding.

Slug caution: this wiki's [[formal-theory-grounded]] is **not** Simmelian [[formal-sociology]] and not mathematical formalization.

### Constant comparative method

Four stages (Ch. V), continuously growing, earlier stages remaining in operation:

1. Comparing incidents applicable to each category (code into as many categories as possible; compare while coding; stop and memo).
2. Integrating categories and their properties (comparison shifts to incident vs properties).
3. Delimiting the theory (parsimony, scope, saturation).
4. Writing the theory (fracture the story for theoretical integration; save illustrations).

CCM is designed for systematic generation — more disciplined than pure memo inspection, not constrained to coding-for-proof. It is **not** designed to guarantee identical results from two independent analysts. It cannot simultaneously serve provisional testing and discovery in one pass (theoretical sampling does not yield extensive enough coding for tests). Analytic induction is distinguished as a fourth approach aiming at limited universal causal proof via negative cases.

### Credibility, application, insight

**Credibility** (Ch. IX): generation methods have been discredited as unsystematic; G&S answer with levels of plausibility for different tasks, comparative correction of data inaccuracies, integration correcting bad inferences, presentation that lets readers assess, and colleague judgment. **Application** (Ch. X): four interrelated properties — fit, understandability (analytic *and* sensitizing categories), generality across diverse daily situations, partial control via controllable and access variables — illustrated from *Awareness of Dying* (awareness context as prime controllable variable). **Insight** (Ch. XI): root source of significant theorizing is the sensitive insights of the observer; systematic comparative analysis develops insights into theory; the researcher's perspective is never finished.

### Epilogue architecture: no license for speculative theory

Against Merton's 1964 pages, they list licenses speculative theory enjoys when data fail (forcing indices; theory never threatened when data missing; rough approximations discounted; fragment testing of many-variable theories; relevance assumed because tests are rare). Grounded generation, they claim, allows none of this: inability to find data for a speculative theory should disqualify it; theory should fit the data, not data the theory.

### Worked conceptual example: social loss

Ch. V's running illustration shows the architecture in miniature. The category **"social loss"** of dying patients, they report, emerged quickly from comparing nurses' responses to potential deaths — appraisals of loss to family, occupation, or society ("He was so young," "He was to be a doctor," "She had a full life"). Constant comparison then generated **properties**: high vs low social loss; care tending to vary positively with degree of loss; some attributes seen immediately (age, ethnic group, social class), others learned over time (occupational worth, marital status, education); perceived loss changeable as new attributes are learned; ward/hospital conditions clustering degrees of loss. The example is offered as method teaching, not as a freestanding verified finding for this wiki's society pages — the primary monographs remain uningested.

### Formalization path: status passages and cooling the mark out

Ch. IV shows substantive → formal movement. Dying as **nonscheduled status passage** prompts a formal theory of status passages with dimensions (institutionally prescribed sequence; transitional status as social-system time tactic; regulation; undesirability; inevitability; clarity of statuses), then comparison across types (engagement, defendant/commitment, polio recovery). Separately, they recommend generating formal theory by extending published theory through theoretical sampling of forgotten comparisons — Goffman's "On Cooling the Mark Out" is their case: ask what comparisons the author threw away, suggested but did not follow, analysis implies, or the reader's reflections suggest. This is G&S's mechanism for **incorporating and transcending** others' theories without logico-deductive grand synthesis.

### Library and quantitative generation (Part II)

Ch. VII treats the library and document **caches** as primary data media for generation — field–library analogues, theoretical sampling of locales and sources, fiction as functional synonyms for some purposes, sociological writing itself as data, non-library archives. Ch. VIII argues quantitative data's generation capacities have been underdeveloped because discovery is treated as byproduct of verification monographs written in hedging verification rhetoric. Their style: saturate core indices with two-variable runs; use consistency and summation indices; then **elaboration analysis** (Lazarsfeld) repurposed to specify conditions, causes, consequences, intervening variables — for denser theory, not only for causal proof. They relax significance-test barriers when theoretical relevance governs ordering. Part II is load-bearing for the claim that grounded theory is not a qualitative ghetto; reception history (outside this primary text) largely sidelined the quantitative chapter.

### Credibility canons (Ch. IX, compressed)

Verification canons (sampling, reliability, validity, indicators, frequency distributions) are improper sole criteria for flexible generation research. Criteria should rest on actual strategies of collecting, coding, analyzing, presenting — and on how readers receive the theory. The fieldworker trusts theory "in his bones" as systematically ordered lived knowledge without claiming sole plausible analysis; teams build shared theory through talk, memos, debate. Immersion-with-detachment and trust conditions field access. Imported preconceived formal theory yields footnotes, not discovery; insecure fieldworkers flee to "compulsive scientism." Conveying credibility requires abstract framework plus vivid data-in-relation-to-theory, and codified procedures (CCM) so readers can track the data–theory link. Multiple comparison groups raise credibility and delimit generality.

## The Empirical Program

*Discovery* is a **method book** whose empirical program is double: (1) claim that generation can be proceduralized; (2) show generation via dying-research and comparative-study assessments.

**What it claimed to explain that predecessors could not.** How to close the theory–research gap from the *theory-generation* side with teachable strategies; how qualitative (and quantitative) data systematically yield integrated theory that fits and works; how sampling can be theory-driven mid-stream; how applied theory can meet practitioner criteria without becoming ad hoc advice.

**Hits (as program self-assessment).** Diffusion of GT vocabulary across qualitative sociology, nursing, education, management; institutionalization of theoretical sampling and saturation language; legitimation of qualitative research as theory-generating, not merely exploratory prep for surveys.

**Misses and anomalies (honest).** The book under-specifies audit trails that later IRBs and journals demanded; CCM's refusal of inter-analyst identity of results sits uneasily with later reliability cultures; "induction" rhetoric invited critiques (not in this primary text) that all observation is theory-laden; the dying illustrations are recycled without the reader of *Discovery* alone being able to evaluate the monographs' evidence quality; Ch. VIII's quantitative generation path was less taken up than the qualitative chapters; post-1967 schisms suggest the joint program was unstable as a single orthodoxy. Ch. VI's accounting scheme for assessing comparative studies is powerful as a typology but remains an internal scoring device — it classifies others' monographs by G&S's generation criteria rather than engaging those authors' own purposes on their own terms. Simmel-style essay writing (they name Goffman and Riesman as valuable but under-integrated) is both foil and resource: insight without their preferred density of comparative grounding.

## Method Commitments

1. **Generation-first canons** for the discovery phase: sampling, coding, credibility, presentation suited to discovery, not only verification.
2. **Joint** collection, coding, and analysis ([[theoretical-sampling]] + [[constant-comparative-method]]).
3. **Theory as process** until saturated and written; developmental, open to reformulation.
4. **Comparison** as the engine across field, library, and quantitative secondary analysis.
5. **Plural data media:** field observation/interviews, document caches, fiction as functional synonyms for some purposes, survey/secondary quantitative data for theoretical elaboration (Lazarsfeld elaboration analysis **repurposed** for generation, with relaxed significance-test barriers when theoretical relevance governs).
6. **Credibility without verification monopoly:** comparative correction, integration, reader presentation, degrees of rigor matched to task.
7. **Application design:** include controllable and access variables; categories both analytic and sensitizing.
8. **Insight disciplined:** [[theoretical-sensitivity]] is cultivated capacity, not pure genius — but still person-dependent.

## Major Debates and Rivals

### Generation vs verification

The program's home debate: [[generation-vs-verification-debate]]. G&S do not reject verification; they reject its exclusive primacy and the export of verification canons into generation work. Foil: verification-first quantitative tradition and speculative theory that waits for (and often escapes) test.

### Grand theory and abstracted empiricism

Adjacent to Mills ([[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]]): G&S share hostility to a priori grand schemes and to empiricism that never generates theory, but supply **procedures** rather than Mills's classic-craft ethos alone. Formal *grounded* theory is their alternative to grand theory — conceptual scope without speculative license.

### Analytic induction

Ch. V rival/sibling: analytic induction seeks limited universal causal proof; CCM seeks many plausible categories/properties without universality or proof claims.

### Blumerian naturalistic method

Affinity with [[exploration-and-inspection]] and [[sensitizing-concept]]: respect for the empirical world, flexible concepts, hostility to premature operationalism. Difference: G&S offer staged coding/sampling rules and an explicit generation–verification division of labor; Blumer resists protocol-like method language.

### Post-1967 internal schisms (flag only)

Glaser vs Strauss/Corbin coding architectures; constructivist GT — **not in primary base**; list as reception frontiers.

## Critiques and Responses

| Critique (as position) | G&S response (1967) |
|---|---|
| Qualitative generation is unsystematic / impressionistic | Explicit procedures; comparative correction; credibility chapter; degrees of plausibility |
| Science requires verification and inter-subjective identity of results | Generation and verification both scientific; CCM does not guarantee identical dual-analyst results — by design |
| Flexible sampling is biased | Bias relative to statistical representation is the wrong standard for generation; theoretical relevance is the criterion |
| Speculative theory is efficient and tests will catch errors | Licenses protect speculative theory; no data for a theory should disqualify it; reverse fit direction |
| Too applied / nursing-grant provincial | Application criteria are features of good grounded theory, not a demotion; dying is a strategic research site |
| Later: GT became a coding recipe | Outside 1967 text; 1967 insists theory is process and insight + comparison, not mechanical coding |

## What Survived

Even where the joint 1967 program fractured, large pieces colonized qualitative methodology:

1. **The phrase "grounded theory"** as badge of legitimacy for qualitative work that claims more than description.
2. **Theoretical sampling** and **saturation** as stop-rules and design language (often loosened from G&S's technical sense).
3. **Constant comparison** as analytic habit (often detached from full four-stage + theoretical sampling package).
4. **Substantive vs formal** scope talk in qualitative theory-building.
5. **Fit / work / relevance** as criteria against imported frameworks.
6. **Memoing** and concurrent analysis as craft norms.
7. **Permission structure:** dissertations and funded projects can claim theory *generation* as aim, not only hypothesis test.
8. **Interdisciplinary export:** nursing, education, information systems, management, social work — beyond sociology proper.
9. **The theory–research gap diagnosis** restated for late-20th-century methods wars.

What often **did not** survive intact: the equal dignity of Ch. VIII quantitative generation; the sharp Epilogue anti-speculative ethic; the joint Glaser–Strauss voice without later orthodoxy fights; the full credibility apparatus of Ch. IX versus checklist "trustworthiness" rubrics.

## Current Status and Research Frontiers

**Status: active** — one of the most widely cited methodological programs in qualitative social science, with multiple living branches (collection holds only 1967).
