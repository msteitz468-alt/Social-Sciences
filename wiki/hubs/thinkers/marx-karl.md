# Karl Marx — Deep Analysis

> Thinkers Hub deep analysis. The summary page [[marx-karl]] owns overview, contributions, genealogy, critiques, and historiography at normal resolution; this page owns the depth. Built from the five Marx primaries ingested to date (Manifesto, Brumaire, Capital I–III) plus the Engels editorial apparatus, Mandel's introductions, and the receptions already in this wiki. The 1844 Manuscripts, *German Ideology*, *Grundrisse*, and *Theories of Surplus-Value* are not yet ingested; sections that would rest on them say so.

## Formation and Training

Karl Marx (Trier 1818 – London 1883) trained in law and then philosophy at Bonn and Berlin, taking a doctorate (Jena, 1841) on Democritus and Epicurus. The wiki's primary layer picks him up later: by Engels's account (preface to *Capital* II), Marx began serious economic study **in Paris in 1843**, reading "the great English and French economists" plus the Germans Rau and List. Two points from that account are load-bearing for the priority disputes below: Marx and Engels did not know of Rodbertus before 1848, and by 1847 (*The Poverty of Philosophy*; the Brussels lectures published as *Wage-Labour and Capital*) Marx already held the outline of a surplus-value analysis. The formation that shows through every ingested text is double: Hegelian dialectics turned onto political economy, and the polemical habit of developing doctrine *against* named opponents — Proudhon, Bauer, the "true socialists" of Manifesto Chapter III, later Rodbertus, Malthus, Say, Overstone, Destutt de Tracy.

The other formation was documentary. Marx did no fieldwork; his empirical base was the British Museum — Blue Books, factory inspectors' reports, parliamentary committee evidence, bank-act hearings. This matters for how the wiki weights his claims (see The Empirical Base).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Ancestors, acknowledged.** [[quesnay-francois|Quesnay]] — Marx credits the *Tableau économique* as the first analysis of social reproduction and calls Physiocracy "the first systematic conception of capitalist production"; the [[reproduction-schemas]] are its self-conscious heir. Adam Smith and Ricardo — the constant reference points of all three Capital volumes: Marx works *through* their categories (natural price → [[price-of-production]]; fixed/circulating capital; rent), preserving what he takes to be the "esoteric" Smith against the "exoteric" and charging both with the conflations (fixed/circulating vs constant/variable; "Smith's dogma" of v+s) that, on his account, buried the origin of surplus-value. Hegel — the method's source, acknowledged in Capital I's afterword (ingested layer).
- **The collaborator.** [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] is not merely co-author (Manifesto) but the posthumous *constructor* of half the corpus: Volume 2 assembled from manuscripts II–VIII, Volume 3 from a single gap-ridden draft over nine years, with Chapter 4 of Volume 3 written by Engels outright and Part Five arranged from "a disordered jumble of notes." Any statement "Marx says" about Capital II–III passes through this filter; Engels signed his interventions, and twice (Vol. 2 Ch. 15; Vol. 3 apparatus) disagreed with Marx's own text in print.
- **Enemies as structure.** Each major work is organized against a target: rival socialisms (Manifesto), great-man historiography of the coup (Brumaire), classical and vulgar political economy (Capital I), the Mercantile System and the circulation illusion (Capital II Part One), Ricardian value theory's unsolved profit-rate contradiction (Capital III Part Two), the currency school (Part Five), the trinity of vulgar economics (Part Seven).
- **Students and descent.** Marx trained no students in the academic sense; the genealogy runs through parties and texts — Kautsky, Bernstein, Lenin, Luxemburg, the Austro-Marxists — and through the twentieth-century re-readings ingested here: [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]]'s totality-Marx, [[althusser-louis|Althusser]]'s anti-humanist Marx of the [[epistemological-break]], Mills's and Marcuse's revisions, Collins's conflict-sociology Marx.

## Career Phases

1. **1843–1849, formation and revolution.** Paris–Brussels economic studies; the Manifesto (1848) states the program — history as [[class-struggle]], the [[mode-of-production]] as ground, bourgeoisie/proletariat polarization — in mobilizing register.
2. **1850–1863, exile journalism and the laboratory of 1848.** The *Brumaire* (1852) turns defeat into theory: structure and agency ("men make their own history, but not under conditions of their own choosing"), [[class-fraction|class fractions]], [[bonapartism]] and the [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]].
3. **1863–1867, the great drafts.** Per Engels, the material for Volumes 2 and 3 was drafted mainly 1863–1867 — the Volume 3 manuscript in 1864–65, *before* Volume 1 was published — while Marx simultaneously founded and ran the International. The published order (I 1867, II 1885, III 1894) inverts the order of composition; the "unfinished" later volumes are in fact earlier strata.
4. **1867–1883, revision and abandonment.** After 1870, illness and new interests (Russian rural statistics, agronomy, mathematics, the Morgan notebooks) fragment the work. Manuscript VIII (1878) reworks the reproduction schemas; the ground-rent Part was to be rebuilt on Russian data as Volume 1 was built on England — never done. Chapter 52, "Classes," breaks off after a page at the question "what makes a class?": the corpus ends, literally, mid-sentence at its sociological foundation.

## Major Works and Their Moves

| Work | Year | Move it makes | Reception |
|---|---|---|---|

| Capital III | 1894 (draft 1864–65) | Surface forms: average profit, falling rate, credit, rent, trinity formula | The transformation controversy from Engels's prize essays to Sraffa ([[transformation-problem-debate]]) |

## The Empirical Base

Marx's evidence classes were **textual/archival throughout**: government statistics, parliamentary evidence, factory-inspector and public-health reports, the financial press, plus period scholarship (Morgan via the notebooks; Russian statistics unused). Three characteristics deserve critical weight:

1. **England as "classic ground."** The generalizations rest on the most advanced capitalist case, by design — a one-case strategy Marx defends theoretically (the more developed shows the less developed its future), but a selection frame nonetheless. The planned Russian counter-case for rent was never executed.
2. **Illustrative arithmetic, not data.** The famous tables (five capitals, two departments, differential rent) are worked examples; Marx and Engels themselves flag the ratios as chosen for convenience. They carry the *logic*, not measurements — a register the wiki keeps by never citing schema numbers as findings.
3. **Advocacy sources read against the grain.** The bank-act hearings and Overstone testimony in Volume 3 Part Five are hostile-witness material, quoted at length and mocked; the "Confusion" manuscript Engels could not even reconstruct. The strongest empirical stretches (the working day in Vol. I; danger and economy in constant capital in Vol. 3 Ch. 5) rest on inspectorates with their own institutional filters.

The posthumous volumes add a second-order empirical problem: **the text itself is a reconstruction.** Engels's prefaces are unusually candid inventories — which chapters existed, which he wrote, where the manuscript "petered out." Source criticism of *Capital* II–III is therefore not a hostile operation but built into the edition; this wiki carries it in each source page's reliability notes.

## Reception, Politics, and Ethics

Marx was a revolutionary organizer (Communist League; First International 1864–72) as well as a scholar, and every reception is also political. The ingested layers give: Engels's construction of "Marxism" after 1883 (prefaces, the priority defenses against Rodbertus's circle, the prize-essay adjudications); Second International orthodoxy and Bernstein's revision (via Mandel); Lenin's *State and Revolution* elevating the Brumaire line into the smash-the-state program; Lukács's methodological orthodoxy ("orthodoxy refers exclusively to method"); Althusser's split of young from mature Marx; the postwar sociological domestications — Mills keeping alienation but dropping proletarianization, Marcuse's integrated working class, Collins extracting a de-teleologized conflict sociology.

The ethics record, stated factually: the ingested texts contain period language and figures that require flagging — the "half-savage hunting peoples" phrasing (Vol. 2), the "Jews in the pores of Polish society" simile and reproduced antisemitic-stereotype quotation (Vol. 3), "savage" comparisons via Senior and Tylor. Nirenberg's reading of "On the Jewish Question" (ingested via *Anti-Judaism*) charges the 1844 Marx with exploiting rather than historicizing the capitalism-Jewishness link — recorded as Nirenberg's attributed position, the 1844 primary not yet ingested. Marx's polemical register itself (Engels's Loria pages included) is part of the record: adjudication by invective, brilliant and unfair by turns.

## Legacy and Counterfactual Significance

Which moves were structural, which personal? The **existence of surplus-value** was structural: Engels's own preface concedes Smith, Ricardo, the 1821 pamphleteer, and the Ricardian socialists all had it in some form; someone would have systematized exploitation economics. What looks personal is the *architecture*: the labour/labour-power distinction; the insistence that realization (Vol. 2) and distribution (Vol. 3) be derived from production rather than juxtaposed to it; the fetishism series ending in the trinity formula. Without Marx, nineteenth-century socialism plausibly consolidates around Proudhonist, Lassallean, or Ricardian-socialist economics — under-consumptionist and distribution-focused; the conflict tradition in sociology loses its economic spine; and there is no reproduction-schema apparatus for Leontiev-style aggregation to descend from (if one accepts Mandel's lineage claim — a position). Conversely, much that circulates as "Marxism" is demonstrably Engels's and the editors': the finished shape of Volumes 2–3, the historical reading of the value law, the "scientific socialism" systematization — the Marx–Engels problem is a genuine historiographical object, visible in this wiki's own source pages.

## Historiography and Primary Sources

The corpus's stratigraphy is the central historiographical fact: Volume 1 the only authorized text (2nd ed. the last Marx revised); Volume 2 a compilation of eight manuscripts spanning 1865–1878; Volume 3 an 1864–65 draft edited 1885–1894; *Theories of Surplus-Value* published by Kautsky (1905, "rather unsatisfactory" per the Penguin apparatus); the 1844 Manuscripts and *Grundrisse* twentieth-century publications that fueled the humanism debate ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]). Receptions ingested: Lukács (1923), Althusser (1965), Mills (1951), Marcuse (1964), Collins (1975), Lenin (1917), Mandel (1978/1981), Nirenberg (2013). Biographies not yet ingested; the MEGA critical edition not represented. Reputation trajectory in this collection: from founder-polemicist (1848–52 texts) to system-builder (Capital) to permanently contested estate — every major twentieth-century reading ingested so far is a *selective claim* on the corpus, usually organized around which texts count as the "real" Marx.
