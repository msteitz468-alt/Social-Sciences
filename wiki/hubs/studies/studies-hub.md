# Studies Hub

Deep analyses of landmark studies — the research acts that became reference
points. Depth here only — ordinary summaries live in `wiki/studies/`.

**Division of labor:** the `studies/` page owns the summary (question, design,
findings, impact, critiques, standing); the hub page owns the depth (design
reconstructed and critiqued, fieldwork as conducted, findings with evidence
behind each, full afterlife of critique / reanalysis / replication).

Each hub page links its summary via `study_page`; the summary carries a
reciprocal link back. Link hub pages by full path:
`[[hubs/studies/[study-slug]|...]]`. A bare slug wikilink always means the summary.

Slug must match the `studies/` summary including its `-study` suffix.

---

## Selection criteria

Studies that founded a method or subfield, anchor a standing debate, or carry
an instructive failure (a famous finding that collapsed). A study page in
`studies/` is the prerequisite; the hub page is for the subset that repays
excavation-grade attention.

---

## Analysis standard

Single source of truth (create on first hub ingest, then lock):

`templates/study-analysis-template.md`

- **3,500–5,500+ words**
- Design summary table; findings table (finding | evidence | later status)
- Reconstruct the study as conducted, not as advertised
- Verbatim load-bearing passages with rhetorical analysis
- Afterlife in full (every major critique, restudy, replication as positions)
- Nine canonical body sections (see `CLAUDE.md` Studies Hub)
