# Racialized Social System

The structural theory of racism developed by [[bonilla-silva-eduardo|Eduardo Bonilla-Silva]], first stated programmatically in "Rethinking Racism: Toward a Structural Interpretation" (*American Sociological Review*, 1997) and applied throughout *[[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-2018|Racism without Racists]]*. It provides the theoretical foundation for his analysis of [[colorblind-racism|color-blind racism]].

## Core Claims

Bonilla-Silva argues (attributed) that a *racialized social system* is a society in which economic, political, social, and ideological structures are partially structured by the placement of actors in racial categories. Such systems allocate differential rewards along racial lines, so that the dominant race benefits materially and symbolically. Racism, on this view, is not a bundle of prejudiced attitudes or "irrational" beliefs but the ideological armor of a racial structure — which is why racial domination survives the decline of open bigotry.

## Intellectual Origins

The program defines itself against the dominant U.S. **prejudice/attitudinal paradigm** (racism as individual, irrational belief measured by survey items) and against the older **[[ethnicity-paradigm|ethnicity paradigm]]** that assimilated race to ethnicity and predicted the fading of racial distinctions (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]). It is broadly aligned with [[racial-formation-theory|racial formation theory]] ([[omi-michael|Omi]] & [[winant-howard|Winant]]) and the structural tradition running back to [[du-bois-w-e-b|Du Bois]].

## Key Proponents and Texts

Bonilla-Silva is the central figure; the 1997 *ASR* article is the charter statement, developed in *White Supremacy and Racism in the Post–Civil Rights Era* (2001) and popularized in *Racism without Racists*.

## Empirical Program

Applied to interview and survey data (the [[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-study|1997 college survey and 1998 Detroit-Area Study]]) to show how ordinary whites reproduce and rationalize a racial order they describe as nonracial.

## Method Commitments

Favors combining structural/historical analysis with in-depth interviewing over attitude surveys alone; treats discourse as data on ideology (see [[white-habitus]]).

## Critiques and Responses

Critics question whether "structure" is specified precisely enough to be falsifiable and whether reading color-blind talk as system-maintaining ideology overstates its coherence (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]). Content to be enriched from the extracted claims.

## Current Status

Active; a standard reference point in the sociology of race and a chief structural alternative to attitudinal accounts of racism.

## Reflexivity

Bonilla-Silva presents the theory as engaged, anti-racist sociology and is explicit that his structural framing carries a political program (see the Conclusion of *Racism without Racists* and [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]).
