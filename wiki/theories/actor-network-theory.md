# Actor-Network Theory (ANT)

Research program associated with Latour, Callon, Law, and others: follow actors and associations rather than explain with a ready-made “social.” Systematic introduction: *[[latour-reassembling-the-social-2005|Reassembling the Social]]* (2005). Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/actor-network-theory|Theory Hub]].

**Disambiguation:** Not Castells’s [[network-society-theory]]; not Descola’s [[collectives-including-nonhumans]] (adjacent more-than-human aggregates, different apparatus).

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — Latour 2005 unless noted.)*

1. **[[sociology-of-associations|Sociology of associations]]** vs **sociology of the social.** “Social” is not a material domain that explains other states of affairs; it is a movement of assembling/reassociating. Social explanation of law/science/religion is often counterproductive once the social sciences succeeded.
2. **Follow the actors; feed off controversies.** When Society-as-glue fails, slow down and map uncertainties rather than jump to structure.
3. **Five sources of uncertainty:** (1) no group, only group formation (spokespersons, anti-groups, boundaries, professionals); (2) action is overtaken (distributed); (3) objects too have agency ([[actant|actants]]); (4) matters of fact vs [[matters-of-concern|matters of concern]]; (5) writing risky accounts.
4. **Mediators vs intermediaries.** Mediators transform what they transport; intermediaries transport without transformation (until they mutate). **Attachments first, actors second:** an actor-network is made to act by a star-shaped web of mediators.
5. **Flat social / three moves.** Default observer position is flatland: (1) localize the global; (2) redistribute the local; (3) connect sites — so scaling labor becomes visible.
6. **Society → collective.** Prefer *collective* (heterogeneous reassembly) over Society as already-assembled frame. **FLAG:** Latour’s “collective” ≠ Descola’s [[collectives-including-nonhumans]].

## Intellectual Origins

STS laboratory and network studies (Callon, Latour, Law); reaction against using “the social” as unexplained explainer; engagement with organization studies (Clarendon/Saïd lectures genesis). Precursor empirical: *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* / [[laboratory-studies]].

## Empirical Program

Case-following: controversies, technical objects, laboratories, organizations, publics. Method: “follow the actors”; deploy controversies; write accounts that risk failure.

## Method Commitments

Trace associations; keep the social flat; feed off controversies; include nonhumans; treat explanations that invoke “Society” as what needs explaining.

## Critiques and Responses

*(Positions.)* Charges of descriptive flatness, political quietism, managerial capture, insufficient structure/power analysis; Latour’s dialog interlude addresses common misunderstandings (ANT as “everything is a network,” etc.).

## Current Status

Active across STS, organization studies, geography, anthropology; vocabulary (actant, black box, matters of concern) widely borrowed even where full program is not.

## Reflexivity

Written as opinionated synthesis for doctoral/organization audiences; Clarendon Lectures genesis. Production context: post-SSK debates and Latour’s long teaching of sociology to engineers.
