# Universal Evolution (White)

Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/universal-evolution|Theory Hub — Universal Evolution]]

## Core Claims

All claims below are **White's** (or Steward's framing of White), not wiki-voice laws of history.

1. **Culture = symbol-dependent extrasomatic system.** Culture is "an extrasomatic, temporal continuum of things and events dependent upon symboling" — tools, institutions, beliefs, language, etc. Only humans symbol; man and culture originate together (*[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|Evolution of Culture]]*, Ch. 1).
2. **[[culturology]].** The science of culture must explain cultural phenomena culturologically (by other cultural facts and systemic regularities), not by reduction to individual psychology (Ch. 1; cf. Durkheimian social facts).
3. **[[energy-law-of-cultural-evolution]].** "Culture advances as the amount of energy harnessed per capita per year increases, other factors remaining constant"; efficiency of tools is the complementary factor (Ch. 2).
4. **Evolution ≠ history ≠ functionalism.** Evolution treats temporal sequences of cultural forms (generalizing); history treats particular events in time and place; functionalism treats nontemporal structure-function relations. All three are legitimate but distinct (Ch. 1; earlier essays).
5. **Two great energy regimes in this volume.** Primitive culture runs almost exclusively on human organismic energy (~1/20 horsepower per capita in a self-reproducing society, White's figure) and is kin-based, relatively egalitarian in White's ideal-type. The Agricultural Revolution harnesses solar energy through plants and animals, enabling surplus, civil society, **[[state-church]]**, class, and commercial economy (Part Two).
6. **Continuity with classical evolutionism.** White rejects the label "neoevolutionism": he holds the *principle* of cultural evolution is continuous with Tylor 1881, though demonstration and data differ (preface).

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against Boasian and related **anti-evolutionism** (Laufer's denunciation of cultural evolution; particularism; *Kulturkreis* and clerical antievolution; functionalist indifference to long-run development). Recovered and re-armed nineteenth-century evolutionism with thermodynamics, a culturological epistemology, and archaeological synthesis (Childe and others). Steward later labeled this pole **universal evolution** (laws of culture-in-general) versus his own **[[multilinear-evolution]]**.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[white-leslie-a|Leslie A. White]] — *Science of Culture* (1949); *Evolution of Culture* (1959).
- Adjacent: V. Gordon Childe (archaeological energy and revolution language); later Michigan and neo-evolutionary networks (Carneiro et al.).
- Dialogic rival: [[steward-julian-h|Steward]] — *[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]* (1955).

## Empirical Program

Explains long-run increases in cultural complexity by energy capture and technological efficiency; organizes ethnographic and archaeological materials into a human-energy vs. agricultural-energy sequence up to Rome. Limitations: coarse global curve; under-specification of local ecological paths (Steward's critique); progress language and primitive ideal-type invite relativist and political critique. See hub for claims-vs-evidence matrix.

## Method Commitments

Comparative and evolutionary synthesis; culturological explanation; thermodynamic framing; distinction of evolution from history and from functional analysis. Not intensive single-society ethnography as the primary warrant.

## Critiques and Responses

Recorded on [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]] (Position 4) and the hub. Steward: universal laws do not explain particular cultures. Particularists: similarities often diffuse. Functionalists: origin stories are conjectural. White's response trajectory: diffusion does not refute evolution (independent development under like conditions); evolution is not history; energy provides a measurable comparative axis.

## Current Status

Status **absorbed**: energy and subsistence remain central in cultural materialism, ecological anthropology, and much archaeological theory; White's single universal curve and strict culturological exclusion of psychology largely do not. The vocabulary of "universal vs multilinear evolution" survives as a historiographic contrast.

**Harris on White (attributed, *[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|Rise]]* ch. 22).** [[harris-marvin|Harris]] credits White with formulating the cultural-materialist strategy "under the pseudonym of evolutionism" but argues White failed to convert energy/"law" statements into operational **covariance** propositions about particular cases, producing "vapid generalizations." Harris rejects Sahlins & Service's phylogeny analogy for culture and their "progress" label for thermodynamic efficiency, while accepting energy criteria as measures of adaptation when programmatic. See [[cultural-materialism]] · [[hubs/theory/cultural-materialism]].

**Carneiro 2003 (attributed).** [[carneiro-robert-l|Carneiro]] situates Whitean universal evolution within the midcentury revival: White undercut Boasian anti-evolutionism and supplied energy as measure/mover; Steward labeled White/Childe “universal evolution” for dealing with culture-as-a-whole stages after particular unilinear reconstructions failed. White averaged environments as a constant for culture-as-a-whole formulas (Steward rejected this as evasion). On [[general-and-specific-evolution]], White held both specific and general to be evolution and equated specific≈multilinear / general≈unilinear — a mapping Carneiro contests. White rejected “neo-evolutionism” as a label. See [[social-evolutionism]], [[multilinear-evolution]], [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]].

**Sahlins & Service 1960 (primary).** White’s Foreword to *[[sahlins-service-evolution-culture-1960|Evolution and Culture]]* endorses Sahlins’s dual-aspect resolution: White’s own program maps onto **general** evolution (absolute progress of culture-as-a-whole); Stewardian multilinear work maps onto **specific** evolution. Sahlins (Ch. II) treats White as the principal generalist of the mid-century revival and argues the history-vs-evolution dispute dissolves once both aspects are allowed. Kaplan’s [[law-of-cultural-dominance]] and Service’s [[law-of-evolutionary-potential]] operationalize general advance and its discontinuity with specialized centers.

## Reflexivity

White wrote as a mid-century American theorist reclaiming science-as-generalization against Boasian particularism and against religious and political hostility to evolution. His Summary valorizes primitive kinship society (freedom, equality, production for use) against civilizational coercion — a moral-political inflection inside a thermodynamic narrative. The unfinished Fuel Revolution volume would have brought industrial capitalism into the same frame; its absence leaves the arc truncated at Rome.

**Continuity with Spencer, per Johnson (attributed, 2011).** Johnson's textbook synthesis situates White's energy-based universal evolution within the same broader family as the Victorian "progress from less to more complex organization" reading of evolution popularized by [[spencer-herbert|Herbert Spencer]] (see [[social-evolutionism]]) — while cautioning that Darwinian archaeologists (see [[evolutionary-archaeology]]) sharply distinguish this progressive, energy-line reading of "evolution" from Darwin's own non-directional, non-progressive natural selection. White's own claimed continuity is with Tylor's principle rather than with Spencer's organism-analogy specifically, but Johnson groups both within the directional cultural-evolutionary family later challenged as a whole by the [[cultural-evolutionism-debate|postprocessual critique]].
