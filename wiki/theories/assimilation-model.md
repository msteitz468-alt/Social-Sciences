# Assimilation Model (Modern Human Origins)

A middle-range position in the [[modern-human-origins-debate]]: **African primary origin** of [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern humans]] combined with **geographically variable archaic admixture** after dispersal — between pure replacement [[recent-african-origin|Recent African Origin (RAO)]] and classic [[multiregional-evolution|multiregional evolution]]. Principal formulations associated with Fred Smith and Erik Trinkaus (Assimilation proper) and with Günter Bräuer’s “Out of Africa + Hybridization.” Distinct from sociological [[segmented-assimilation]] (immigration/incorporation theory).

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — primarily as of Stringer, *Lone Survivors*, 2012 summarizing the continuum.)*

1. **African primacy retained.** Modern anatomy and most living human ancestry originate in Africa; Africa is not merely one equal center among many (contrast classic Multiregionalism).
2. **Archaic contribution expected.** Expanding modern populations mixed with residual archaics ([[neanderthals]], [[denisovans]], and possibly other Eurasian groups) rather than replacing them genetically without remainder.
3. **Wide continuum of gene-flow intensity.** Stringer (2012) maps Assimilation near the center of a left–right continuum: pure RAO (total non-African replacement) → Bräuer “mostly Out of Africa” → Smith–Trinkaus Assimilation (if archaic gene flow were the rule, non-African input could approach ~50%) → classic Multiregionalism (no special African role).
4. **Fossil mosaics as positive evidence.** Specimens such as the Oase early moderns (Romania; possible hybridization signals) are read as consistent with assimilation rather than as noise (as of Stringer 2012 reporting Trinkaus and others).
5. **Genetics post-2010 as partial vindication of modest assimilation.** Early nuclear aDNA (~2% Neanderthal in non-Africans; higher Melanesian archaic totals with Denisovan input — as of Stringer 2012) moved pure-replacement RAO toward “mostly Out of Africa,” which Stringer still labels RAO when archaic assimilation remains under ~10% of the genome.

## Intellectual Origins

Developed as a **middle path** once pure replacement and pure multiregional poles polarized the 1980s–1990s debate. Bräuer’s hybridization-friendly African-origin variant pre-dated or paralleled Stringer’s stricter species/replacement emphasis; Smith and Trinkaus’s Assimilation model named the center ground (as of Stringer 2012 historiography). Reacts against both total-replacement overstatement and multiregional denial of African primacy.

## Key Proponents and Texts

Fred Smith · Erik Trinkaus · Günter Bräuer (related hybridization formulation) · reception and continuum mapping in [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012|Stringer 2012]] · fossil case material including Oase (as discussed by Trinkaus et al., via Stringer).

## Empirical Program

- Morphological mosaics and “transitional” features outside Africa interpreted as admixture, not local anagenesis alone.
- Later nuclear aDNA of Neanderthals and Denisovans treated as independent genetic evidence of limited archaic contribution (as of Stringer 2012 horizon).
- Geographic patterning of admixture (shared Neanderthal input in non-Africans; Melanesian Denisovan extra) used to constrain when and where mixing occurred.

## Method Commitments

Combines fossil morphology with (increasingly) genetics; species taxonomy may remain dual (separate *H. neanderthalensis* etc.) even if limited hybridization is accepted — Stringer (2012) holds that modest hybridization does not yet force lumping all later Pleistocene humans as one species.

## Critiques and Responses

- **From pure RAO:** pan-assimilation would predict repeated diversity reversals where archaics mixed in; Stringer (2012) argues serial decline of genetic/cranial diversity with distance from Africa challenges strong assimilation except the Melanesian hotspot.
- **From multiregionalists:** assimilation may be read as a watered multiregionalism; multiregionalists historically rejected African primacy and “replacement” framing.
- **Horizon risk:** admixture percentages and source populations are fast-moving science — date-stamp all numerical claims.

## Current Status

As of post-2010 aDNA, modest assimilation / “mostly Out of Africa” is widely treated as the empirical compromise; pure multiregionalism and pure zero-contribution replacement are minority extremes. Live debate: how much archaic DNA, which populations, fitness effects, and whether taxonomy should follow genetics. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]], [[recent-african-origin]], [[admixture]], [[ancient-dna]].

## Reflexivity

Assimilation naming is partly rhetorical — it allows former replacement advocates and continuity advocates to claim partial victory. Stringer (2012) explicitly keeps “RAO” for modest archaic input under ~10%, illustrating how program labels absorb contrary evidence rather than dissolving.
