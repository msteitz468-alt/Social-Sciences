# Cognitive Fluidity

Middle-range theory of human cognitive evolution proposed by [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]] in *[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* (1996). Frames the mind as a **[[cathedral-of-the-mind|cathedral]]**: a nave of general intelligence, chapels of [[specialized-intelligences|specialized intelligences]], and — only with modern humans — doors/windows (or a Sperberian “superchapel”) allowing **cognitive fluidity**, the cross-domain flow of knowledge.

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/cognitive-fluidity|Theory Hub — Cognitive Fluidity]]

## Core Claims

*(All attributed to Mithen 1996 unless noted.)*

1. **Three-phase architectural history.** Phase 1: minds dominated by domain-general intelligence (general-purpose learning and decision rules). Phase 2: general intelligence plus multiple specialized intelligences working **in isolation**. Phase 3: specialized intelligences working **together**, with flow of knowledge between domains — **cognitive fluidity**.
2. **Cathedral metaphor.** Developing minds are built from genetic architectural plans under environmental influence; evolution tinkers without redesign from scratch. Phase 2 chapels have thick walls; Phase 3 is Gothic light and sound between chapels.
3. **Early Human (Phase 2) profile.** *H. erectus*, archaic *Homo*, *H. heidelbergensis*, and Neanderthals share a generic “Early Human” architecture: strong technical intelligence (handaxes, Levallois), sophisticated natural-history intelligence (Old World colonization; glacial survival), powerful social intelligence, and language that remains essentially **social** — with poor integration across non-social domains. Explains technical skill *and* cultural stasis (Isaac’s “directionless change”).
4. **Cultural explosion as Phase 3 signature.** Between ~60–30 kya (staggered regional “sparks,” not one global bang), art, personal adornment, complex multi-material tools, religious ideologies, and a second global colonization pulse mark full cognitive fluidity. First art already shows high skill — not gradual evolutionary scribbles — because component processes already existed in separate domains.
5. **Language and consciousness as mechanism.** Once social language begins carrying non-social “snippets,” selection drives social → general-purpose language (~150–50 kya in Mithen’s guess); social intelligence expands into Sperber’s module of metarepresentation; consciousness shifts from social prediction toward a multi-domain mental database. No further brain-size increase required.
6. **Two-step fluidity.** Partial integration (social × natural history) among Early Modern Humans ~100 kya (Skhul/Qafzeh animal-in-grave ritual; improved hunting prediction; technical still isolated); full technical integration 60–30 kya produces the Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition.
7. **Exaptive high culture and agriculture.** Art, religion, and science exploit fluid mappings (animals as persons; objects as intentional; metaphor/analogy as tools of thought). Agriculture ~10 kya is a **cultural epilogue**: four propensities from fluidity (intensive plant tools; prestige via plants/animals; social relations with non-humans; plants/animals as manipulable artifacts) meet terminal-Pleistocene climate — not a knowledge threshold or global population crisis.

## Intellectual Origins

**developed_from:** [[cognitive-archaeology]] (evolutionary-origins focus; Thomas Wynn as early stimulus) and evolutionary psychology / modularity theory — Jerry Fodor (*Modularity of Mind*), Howard Gardner (multiple intelligences), Leda Cosmides & John Tooby (*The Adapted Mind*), Annette Karmiloff-Smith (representational redescription), Paul Rozin (accessibility), Dan Sperber (module of metarepresentation), Margaret Boden / Arthur Koestler (creativity as cross-domain combination). **reacted_against:** sponge and single general-purpose computer models of mind; pure Swiss-army-knife modularity that cannot explain imagination and metaphor; gradualist equating of brain size with continuous cultural progress; explanations of the Upper Palaeolithic that treat social reorganization, technology, or “origin of language” alone as sufficient causes.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]], *The Prehistory of the Mind* (1996) — charter text.
- Related program neighborhood: [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]]’s cognitive-processual archaeology; Merlin Donald’s *Origins of the Modern Mind* (1991) — Mithen reverses psychologist-led use of archaeology.

## Empirical Program

On Mithen’s account the model explains: million-year toolkit monotony despite large brains; Neanderthal technical skill without art; absence of worked bone/ivory and purpose-dedicated tools among Early Humans; Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition lagging anatomical modernity by ≥40 kyr; “half-way” Early Modern behavior (Near East, southern Africa); Holocene agriculture without a new brain architecture. Claims rest on mid-1990s fossil and archaeological literature — **pre-aDNA**; timings and species inventories stay date-stamped (as of Mithen 1996).

## Method Commitments

Middle-range bridging from material culture to cognitive architecture; heavy use of evolutionary-psychology models, developmental psychology, and primate analogy (explicitly limited for the 6-mya common ancestor). Archaeology is to set the agenda for mind research, not merely supply props for psychologists (attributed manifesto).

## Critiques and Responses

Live and subsequent challenges (record as positions; do not adjudicate): European bias in “big bang” periodization; earlier African behavioral modernity denser than 1996 synthesis allowed; sealed-domain assumption for Neanderthals contested by later symbolic claims; language-first mechanism untested; post-1996 aDNA revises replacement/hybridization balances. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Current Status

Active vocabulary in cognitive archaeology and public science; cathedral/fluidity metaphors widely cited even where authors reject phase dating. Overlaps [[cognitive-archaeology]], evolutionary psychology of culture, and [[cognitive-science-of-religion]] (Boyer-style domain-violation accounts of supernatural beings).

## Reflexivity

Written as accessible Thames & Hudson prose (1996; Phoenix 1998 paperback in this vault’s OCR). Anglo-European Palaeolithic heavily weighted; evolutionary psychology near peak influence. Boxed essays treat racist attitudes and humour as *potential* products of fluidity (capacity, not compulsion) — attributed and not promoted to wiki voice. Production context shapes which “chapels” are named and which ethnographic analogies appear (Aboriginal art, San rock art, modern foragers).
