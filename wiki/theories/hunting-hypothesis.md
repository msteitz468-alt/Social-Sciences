# The Hunting Hypothesis (Man the Hunter thesis)

The thesis — most fully stated by **[[washburn-sherwood|Sherwood Washburn]]** and **C. S.
Lancaster** in "The Evolution of Hunting," and by **William Laughlin** in the same volume —
that *hunting* was the formative adaptation of the human career, shaping human biology,
psychology, and society. It gives the *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]*
volume its title and its most contested content.

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — Washburn & Lancaster 1968; Laughlin 1968.)*
- **Hunting as master adaptation.** "Human hunting... is far more than a technique... It is a
  way of life... In a very real sense our intellect, interests, emotions, and basic social
  life — all are evolutionary products of the success of the hunting adaptation." Laughlin
  called hunting "the master behavior pattern of the human species."
- **Timescale.** The genus *Homo* is ~600,000 years old, agriculture <1% of that; "the origin
  of all common characteristics must be sought in preagricultural times."
- **Cooperation, division of labour, family.** Big-game hunting "put a maximum premium on
  cooperation among males"; "when males hunt and females gather, the results are shared... the
  habitual sharing between a male, a female, and their offspring becomes the basis for the
  human family."
- **Incest taboo and exogamy** follow from the economics of the hunting family and from
  demographic constraints (it "takes something on the order of 100 pairs," ~500 people, to
  keep sex ratios workable).
- **Psychology and aggression** (the most contested part). "Men enjoy hunting and killing...
  War has been far too important in human history for it to be other than pleasurable for the
  males involved"; "in man there are no effective submission gestures," so human killing lacks
  the biological inhibitions of other carnivores.

## Intellectual Origins and Context

The program grew from mid-century physical anthropology and the new primatology (Washburn's
own field), reacting against tool-centred accounts of human origins. Laughlin's version made
tools secondary: "Tools did not make the man, man made tools in order to hunt." It converged
with the popular "killer ape" writing of the period (Ardrey) while claiming a firmer
empirical base in primate behaviour and forager ethnography (it cites [[lee-what-hunters-do-study|Lee's]]
!Kung range data).

## Key Proponents and Texts

- **[[washburn-sherwood|Sherwood L. Washburn]] & C. S. Lancaster**, "The Evolution of Hunting"
  (in *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]*, 1968) — the canonical statement.
- **William S. Laughlin**, "Hunting: An Integrating Biobehavior System and Its Evolutionary
  Importance" (1968) — hunting as "the master behavior pattern," analysed as five patterned
  activity series.
- Antecedents in Washburn's "Tools and Human Evolution" (1960) and the "new physical
  anthropology" (1951); popular contemporaries in Robert Ardrey's "killer ape" writing.
- Critical counter-texts: Sally Slocum, "Woman the Gatherer" (1975); Nancy Tanner & Adrienne
  Zihlman, "Women in Evolution" (1976).

## Empirical Program

The thesis draws on primate behaviour, the fossil and archaeological record, and forager
ethnography. Laughlin analysed hunting as five patterned activity series (programming the
child, scanning, stalking, killing, retrieval) and pointed to selection for intelligence via
successful hunters' and headmen's disproportionate reproduction (Xavante and Anaktuvuk case
data). Its claims about male hunting supplying the dietary base, however, ran against the same
volume's finding that *gathering* supplies most calories in most foraging societies (see
[[sexual-division-of-labor]], [[original-affluent-society]]).

## Method Commitments

Comparative primatology plus explicit, self-acknowledged **speculation** about deep time
("The Pleistocene way of life can only be known by inference and speculation"). This reliance
on [[ethnographic-analogy|ethnographic and primate analogy]] is both the program's method and
its chief vulnerability.

## Major Debates and Rivals

- **"Woman the Gatherer."** From the 1970s, feminist anthropologists (Sally Slocum 1975;
  Nancy Tanner & Adrienne Zihlman) attacked the androcentrism of the thesis: if gathering
  (women's work) supplied most calories, then gathering, tool-carrying, and female social
  strategies are at least as plausible engines of hominization. See
  [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]].
- **Internal dissent.** [[steward-julian-h|Steward]], in the same volume, denied hunting is
  "a primary diagnostic characteristic of social types" or "even a single causal factor."
- **Aggression.** Turnbull and Schneider challenged the aggression claims on the floor of the
  symposium, citing Mbuti non-aggression (see [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]]).
- **Archaeological self-evidence critique (Binford 1981, 1983).** In *[[binford-bones-1981|Bones: Ancient Men and Modern Myths]]* (1981) — restated for a general audience in *[[binford-in-pursuit-of-the-past-1983|In Pursuit of the Past]]* — [[binford-lewis|Binford]] attacks the practice that treats early-man sites with animal bones + stone tools as self-evident evidence of hunting meals ([[hunting-hypothesis]] as archaeological inference). From [[actualistic-studies|actualistic]] wolf-den and [[nunamiut|Nunamiut]] control data he argued the [[olduvai-gorge|Olduvai]] fauna records marrow-**scavenging**, not hunting, and deflated [[dart-raymond|Dart]]'s [[osteodontokeratic-culture|osteodontokeratic]] / killer-ape reading via multi-agent [[taphonomy]] (Brain; waterhole actualism). This is a middle-range critique of how hunting claims are *warranted from the record*, not primarily a biological rival to Washburn–Lancaster — see [[hunting-scavenging-debate]].
- **Food-sharing as rival prime mover ([[food-sharing-hypothesis]]).** [[isaac-glynn|Glynn Isaac]]'s home-base/food-sharing model, popularized in [[leakey-richard|Leakey]] & Lewin's *[[leakey-lewin-origins-1977|Origins]]* (1977), grants hunting "an important part" but ranks it *secondary* to the sharing of gathered and hunted food at a home base as the human-making adaptation (attributed, Leakey & Lewin 1977). Origins explicitly rejects the hunting-derived "innate aggression" reading — see [[human-aggression-killer-ape-debate]].

## Critiques and Responses

Critics fault the thesis for reading a mid-century masculine ideal into human nature, for
projecting speculative continuities across half a million years, and for empirical strain
against the gathering-centred subsistence data. Defenders (Hamburg) reframed the aggression
claims as a "carry-over problem" — traits adaptive in the foraging past now maladaptive —
rather than a celebration of violence.

## Current Status

`contested`. The strong biological-psychological claims (innate male aggression, hunting as
sole engine of human nature) are largely rejected in current biological anthropology; the
weaker claim — that foraging and food-sharing shaped human sociality — survives, now usually
framed to include gathering and cooperative provisioning by both sexes. The thesis endures as
a landmark and as a cautionary case in the history of the discipline.

## Reflexivity

The program's masculine framing is itself content: it crystallized a 1960s image of "Man the
Hunter" that the discipline's subsequent feminist turn used to examine anthropology's own
androcentrism. Its afterlife is inseparable from the [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]].

**Cooking-hypothesis critique (Wrangham 2009).** *[[wrangham-catching-fire-2009|Catching Fire]]* steel-mans Man-the-Hunter (meat share in forager diets; deep butchery archaeology; cooperation/travel/brain attractions) then argues it is incomplete: (1) two fossil steps (~2.5 Ma and ~1.9–1.8 Ma) should not share one cause; (2) meat may explain habilines but not *Homo erectus* small jaws/teeth poorly suited to tough raw game; (3) raw-food time budgets make a hunting–gathering household bargain unworkable without cooking. Wrangham retains meat for step one and assigns [[cooking-hypothesis|cooking]] to step two. See [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]].
