# New Economic Sociology

Research program in sociology that treats **core economic processes** — labor markets, firms, exchange, trust, malfeasance — as structured by social relations, against mid-century decline of U.S. economic sociology and against purely atomized models of economic action. Granovetter retrospectively named *[[granovetter-getting-a-job-1974|Getting a Job]]* (1974) an early exemplar and *[[granovetter-embeddedness-1985|Embeddedness]]* (1985) the theoretical charter (GAJ 2nd ed. preface).

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — program-level.)*

1. **Embeddedness (network sense):** Economic action is embedded in ongoing networks of social relations (Granovetter 1985), not performed by atomized actors.
2. **Core, not periphery:** Sociology should address central economic outcomes (e.g., how jobs are filled), not only "social aspects" residual to economics.
3. **Against twin atomizations:** Both under-socialized (pure self-interest) and over-socialized (fully norm-governed) models erase relational structure.
4. **Mechanism-rich labor markets:** Job matching often runs through personal contacts and information networks ([[granovetter-getting-a-job-study]]), not only formal search or prices.

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against: (a) the empirical sterility of Parsons & Smelser *Economy and Society* (1956) abstraction; (b) the fade of mid-century industrial sociology into organization theory; (c) neoclassical job-search and market models that leave the matching mechanism black-boxed. Drew on White-school network analysis, Polanyi's [[embeddedness]] (shifted from institutional to network register), and ethnographic/industrial case work.

## Empirical Program

Labor-market contacts and careers ([[granovetter-getting-a-job-study]]); trust and order in markets (illustrations in 1985 essay); later firm, finance, and development cases across the wider field (collection still thin).

## Method Commitments

[[network-analysis]]; mixed survey/interview designs; historical and organizational case studies; willingness to challenge neoclassical baselines with relational mechanisms.

## Critiques and Responses

Conceptual stretch of "embeddedness" (multiple incompatible senses); risk of network reductionism vs culture/institutions (Zelizer, Fligstein correctives); U.S.-centric classic statements.

## Current Status

Active subfield label under [[economic-sociology]]; vocabulary absorbed even where practitioners reject a single "program." Collection: GAJ + Embeddedness ingested 2026-07-10; Zelizer, Fligstein & McAdam, and others already present as adjacent.

## Reflexivity

Program self-consciously U.S. academic; male PTM Newton sample of GAJ is a narrow empirical base for a program that later claimed general economic relevance — later work (and Granovetter's own Afterword) qualifies scope.
