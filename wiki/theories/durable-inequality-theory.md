# Durable Inequality Theory (Tilly)

Research program of [[tilly-charles|Charles Tilly]] explaining **[[durable-inequality|durable inequality]]** as the outcome of **[[categorical-inequality|categorical pairs]]** installed and cemented by four organizational mechanisms: **[[exploitation]]**, **[[opportunity-hoarding]]**, **[[organizational-emulation|emulation]]**, and **[[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]]**. Charter text: *[[tilly-durable-inequality-1998|Durable Inequality]]* (UC Press, 1998; Ross Lectures, UCLA 1995).

Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/durable-inequality-theory]].

## Core Claims

All **attributed** to Tilly (1998):

1. **Categorical, not individual, correspondence** — large inequalities map mainly onto paired categories (male/female, black/white, citizen/noncitizen), not individual propensities or performances alone.
2. **Organizational problem-solving** — controllers of value-producing resources install categorical distinctions to solve coordination, recruitment, and surplus-allocation problems; multiple parties (including some victims) acquire stakes.
3. **[[exploitation]]** — powerful, connected people command resources, enlist effort of outsiders, and exclude those outsiders from the full value added by that effort.
4. **[[opportunity-hoarding]]** — members of a categorically bounded network acquire access to a resource that is valuable, renewable, subject to monopoly, supportive of network activities, and enhanced by the network's modus operandi (often without major exploitation of outsiders).
5. **[[organizational-emulation|Emulation]]** and **[[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]]** generalize and lock in categorical inequality across and within organizations.
6. **Beliefs secondary** — prejudice and ideology reinforce more than they independently initiate durable inequality.
7. **[[interior-exterior-categories|Interior/exterior category matching]]** — organizations that map internal ranks onto exterior categories (race, gender, citizenship) produce especially durable inequality.

## Intellectual Origins

- **developed_from:** Marxist exploitation (generalized beyond capitalist wage labor); Weberian closure; Tilly's own organizational and contention work (*[[tilly-from-mobilization-to-revolution-1978|FMR]]* catness×netness); affinities with Stinchcombe's economic sociology (Tilly acknowledges).
- **reacted_against:** individual-difference explanations (ability, ambition, human capital as sufficient); pure discrimination/attitude models; status-attainment without organizational mechanisms.
- **Dialogue:** Zelizer (acknowledgment reader); Harrison White workshop network; immigration and niche literatures (Waldinger, Model, Anderson).

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[tilly-charles|Tilly]], *Durable Inequality* (1998) — charter.
- Related earlier/adjacent Tilly: state formation, contention, *Work Under Capitalism* (with Chris Tilly, forthcoming at writing).
- Research community uses opportunity-hoarding and categorical-inequality vocabulary widely in stratification and economic sociology (as of later literature — not fully ingested here).

## Empirical Program

Illustrative, comparative-historical and contemporary cases rather than a single design: stature/nutrition by class (Sandhurst vs Marine Society); Italian chain migration (Roccasecca–Mamaroneck); ethnic niches and entrepreneurship; gender at work and household–firm boundary; South African racial categories; Catholic Emancipation and citizenship politics; firm internal labor markets. Evidence class mix: historical, ethnographic report, secondary quantitative — all **illustrations of mechanism**, not a single sample inference.

## Method Commitments

Mechanism-centered explanation (recurrent causal sequences of general scope); relational/organizational units over individual attributes; historical-comparative illustration; skepticism toward attitude surveys as primary engines of inequality change.

## Critiques and Responses

| Position | Claim | Tilly-side response (attributed, 1998) |
|---|---|---|
| Human capital / status attainment | Skills and family background explain inequality | Much apparent individual difference is consequence of categorical organization; human-capital change works mainly via reorganization of opportunity |
| Prejudice / discrimination monism | Attitudes drive inequality | Beliefs reinforce the four mechanisms but exercise little independent influence on initiation |
| Classical Marxist exploitation | Class surplus under capitalism is the core | Exploitation spring from Marx but generalized; hoarding by nonelites is co-installing mechanism |
| Cultural / boundary traditions | Meaning and identity are independent engines | Historically accumulated culture differentiates category types — open problem Tilly names; resource control remains primary in the book |
| Methodological caution | Illustrations ≠ proof | Tilly presents claims as **working hypotheses** inviting verification, refinement, refutation |

**Politics and intervention (Ch. 7–8, attributed):** inequality's politics is the special case when a government is a party; states create/back categorical pairs (citizen/noncitizen, married/unmarried); democracy = broad relatively equal citizenship + protection from arbitrary state action + collective control of personnel/policy; widespread categorical inequality threatens democracy twice (exclusion incentives + visible markers). Four futures: More of the Same; Balkanization; Material Equalization; New Categories. Three intervention implications: attitude change weak; human-capital alteration works via opportunity organization; workplace/boundary reorganization has largest leverage. Affirmative action focused only on hire-point decisions is too narrow.

## Current Status

**Active** — opportunity hoarding and categorical inequality are standard stratification vocabulary; the four-mechanism kit remains a reference frame for organizational inequality research. Links to later contentious-politics and democracy work. Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/durable-inequality-theory]].

## Reflexivity

Written from a senior historical sociologist's comparative kit; illustrations skew Euro-American and firm/migration cases; "value added" language carries economic assumptions; Tilly's stated preference for "material equalization" is **POSITION**, not wiki-voice endorsement. Collection coverage: charter text ingested; reception literature not yet ingested.

## From *Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties* (2005)

*(Attributed — Tilly; heavy self-reuse of DI 1998 flagged.)* Essay collection restates the four mechanisms (Chs. 5–6, 10) with:

- **Maze** metaphor vs ladder of individual ranks; five elementary social configurations; scripting × local knowledge in ties.  
- **Relational origins** (Ch. 6): fifth family of inequality explanation against competitive sorting; category formation via encounter/imposition/negotiation/transfer.  
- **Changing forms** (Ch. 7): historical shift in value-producing resources (land/coercion → capital/information/science/media).  
- **Unequal knowledge** (Ch. 8): control of production, distribution, and access as inequality axes; universities as hoarding sites.  
- **Boundaries** (Ch. 9): [[social-boundary-mechanism]] inventory couples to categorical inequality installation.  
- **Chain migration** (Ch. 10): prime [[opportunity-hoarding]] / [[chain-migration]] application.  
- **Democracy** (Chs. 12–13): [[citizenship]] + [[protected-consultation]]; inequality ↔ [[de-democratization]] **conjectures**.
