# Structuralism (Anthropological)

Structuralism is the research program, founded by [[levi-strauss-claude|Claude Lévi-Strauss]], that treats cultural phenomena — kinship terminologies, marriage rules, myths, totemic classifications, food, art — as **sign systems** whose intelligibility lies in relations of opposition between elements, on the explicit analogy of structural linguistics. It dominated French human science in the 1950s–60s and reshaped anthropology's treatment of kinship (via [[alliance-theory]]), myth, and cognition before being challenged by the practice turn and post-structuralism. Its *vocabulary* and several results survive their program (`status: absorbed`).

## Core Claims (attributed)

Lévi-Strauss argued:

- **Meaning is relational.** "Human societies... never create absolutely, but merely choose certain combinations from an ideal repertoire" — customs form systems, like a periodic table of possible combinations (*[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]*, on Caduveo art).
- **The method is linguistic.** Kinship analysis should follow the model of structural phonology: reduce a mass of empirical variation to a few underlying oppositions. In the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]* conclusion he claims "there are only three possible elementary kinship structures... constructed by means of two forms of exchange... [depending] upon a single differential characteristic," the harmonic/disharmonic character of the regime.
- **Structures are unconscious.** The order the analyst recovers is not the actors' conscious rule; native systematizations (e.g. the Murngin subsection scheme) may be "a theory elaborated by the natives... rather than an expression of the reality."
- **Binary opposition** is the elementary operation of mind (nature/culture, raw/cooked, giver/taker), grounded in a [[psychic-unity-of-mankind|psychic unity]] of humankind.
- **Exchange as communication.** "The emergence of symbolic thought must have required that women, like words, should be things that were exchanged" — kinship, economics, and language are three modalities of one communicative logic (attributed; and contested — see Critiques).

## Intellectual Origins

By Lévi-Strauss's own account (*[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]*), the method predates his encounter with linguistics: his "three mistresses" — **geology, Marxism, and psychoanalysis** — share one lesson, that "to understand is to reduce one type of reality to another; ... true reality is never the most manifest." Reading a geological stratum, a Marxian infrastructure beneath events, or a latent content beneath a symptom are the same operation. To this he added [[mauss-the-gift-1925|Mauss's]] theory of reciprocity and the **[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Durkheim–Mauss]]** program on collective classification, and — decisively — the structural linguistics of **Saussure** and, above all, **Roman Jakobson**, whom he thanked for the "theoretical inspiration" behind the *Elementary Structures*. The program defined itself against **Sartrean existentialism and phenomenology** (which posit continuity between lived experience and the real, where structuralism insists on their discontinuity), displacing Sartre as the prestige position of the French field (see [[levi-strauss-claude]]).

## Empirical Program

Structuralism's central empirical success was kinship: the *Elementary Structures* organized the world's positive marriage systems under two forms of exchange (see [[alliance-theory]], [[restricted-exchange]], [[generalized-exchange]]), and the *[[levi-strauss-structural-anthropology-1958|Structural Anthropology]]* essays added the **[[atom-of-kinship|atom of kinship]]** and the model of **[[myth]]** as **mythemes** ("bundles of relations"; the Oedipus analysis — "the purpose of myth is to provide a logical model capable of overcoming a contradiction"). It applied the same logic to shamanic healing (the **[[effectiveness-of-symbols|effectiveness of symbols]]**), to art (split representation), and — in *[[levi-strauss-savage-mind-1962|The Savage Mind]]* — to cognition and classification: the **[[science-of-the-concrete|science of the concrete]]**, **[[bricolage]]**, and the dissolution of **[[totemism]]** into a logic of classification by difference. Its ambition was to explain via a small formal apparatus rather than accumulation of cases — the *Elementary Structures* rested on "more than 7,000 books and articles" but claimed to be "complete" by treating a discrete set of principles, not an exhaustive corpus.

## Method Commitments

Analyse systems, not conscious rules; privilege the model over the event; seek invariant relations beneath variable content; treat formal (even mathematical) modelling as legitimate (André Weil's group-theoretic appendix to the *Elementary Structures*); read across domains (kinship ≈ language ≈ myth) as variants of one combinatorial logic. The "Social Structure" essay (*Structural Anthropology*, ch. XV) states the doctrine of **models** explicitly: social structure is not empirical reality but models built from it, distinguished as **mechanical vs statistical** and conscious vs unconscious; native models (e.g. apparent moiety dualism, "Do Dual Organizations Exist?") are often "rationalizations" masking the real (concentric/triadic) structure; society is communication at three levels — women, goods, messages — the "order of orders."

## Critiques and Responses

- **The practice critique.** [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]] argued structuralism mistakes "the model constructed to account for practice for the principle of practice," detemporalizes exchange, and substitutes the rule for the strategy — the founding move of [[practice-theory]] (see [[strategy-vs-rule]], [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]).
- **The feminist critique.** The "women, like words, ... exchanged" formula was read (Gayle Rubin, "The Traffic in Women," 1975) as treating women as objects, not subjects, of exchange — see [[exchange-of-women]]. Lévi-Strauss's own text partly anticipates this: woman "has remained at once a sign and a value."
- **The prescriptive/preferential dispute.** [[needham-rodney|Needham]] recast the *Elementary Structures* as about prescriptive systems specifically; Lévi-Strauss rejected the reduction (see [[prescriptive-preferential-debate]]).
- **The writing/logocentrism critique.** Derrida's *Of Grammatology* (1967) made Lévi-Strauss's Nambikwara "writing lesson" (see [[nambikwara]]) the exemplary case of Western "logocentrism," reading its nostalgia for speech-without-writing as itself metaphysical.
- **The cultural-materialist front (Harris).** [[harris-marvin|Marvin Harris]] (*[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|Rise of Anthropological Theory]]* ch. 18 — attributed) reads French structuralism as mentalist idealism continuous with Durkheimian group-mind and Maussian reciprocity elevated above techno-economy; he critiques binary/linguistic models and alliance-theory empirics (e.g. Purum as he presents them) and labels Lévi-Strauss's Marxism a "Zen" or Hegelian variant. Record as Harris's partisan counter-reading; primary Lévi-Strauss pages remain authoritative for the program's own claims ([[cultural-materialism]] · [[hubs/theory/cultural-materialism]]).
- **The historicizing critique (Sahlins).** [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] worked within the structuralist tradition while pressing it toward history. In *[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]* (1976) he distinguished "two structuralisms" — a naturalist one (structure as an expression of material/biological constraint) and a genuinely cultural one (structure as a meaningful order) — and defended the second against the Marxist charge that structuralism is embarrassed before history ("*c'est un embarras de richesse*"). In *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]* (1985) he argued that "structure — the symbolic relations of cultural order — is an historical object," rejecting a purely synchronic Saussurean model ("yin-yang structuralism, without a Book of Changes") in favor of an "internal diachrony" of the categories, put at risk and revalued in the [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]]. See [[cultural-reason]].
- **The denial-of-coevalness critique (Fabian).** [[fabian-johannes|Johannes Fabian]] (*[[fabian-time-and-the-other-1983|Time and the Other]]*, 1983 — attributed) charged that structuralism "preempts" the question of [[coevalness]] through "a radically taxonomic approach," eliminating Time "as a significant dimension of either cultural integration or ethnography." He read Lévi-Strauss's claimed symmetry of history-in-time / anthropology-in-space as a rhetorical "trick" that substitutes *diachrony* for *history* and relocates culture in Foucault's atemporal "tabular space" ("Lévi-Strauss' thought does not inhabit a world; it lives in a matrix"); its "ostensibly achronic stance turns out to be a flagrant example of [[allochronism|allochronic]] discourse." He further read Lévi-Strauss's key term for fieldwork — *observation* — as evidence of a "visual root metaphor of knowledge" ([[visualism]]).
- **The existentialist front.** Structuralism defined itself against Sartre: *The Savage Mind* closes with the polemic against Sartre's *Critique of Dialectical Reason*, denying History and dialectical reason their privileged status and calling to "dissolve," not enshrine, the humanist subject (see [[levi-strauss-sartre-debate]]). Lévi-Strauss there conceded the *Elementary Structures* had underplayed the difference between exchange-as-praxis and the conscious rules codifying it — a concession the practice critique would press.

## Current Status

As a totalizing program structuralism is `absorbed` rather than `active`: few practise it whole. But its results and vocabulary are durable — the alliance/descent distinction, binary opposition, the analysis of myth and classification, and the kinship-as-communication frame remain standard reference points, and post-structuralism, practice theory, and cognitive anthropology all define themselves partly in relation to it.


## Afterlife: Descola's structural revival (2005)

[[descola-philippe|Descola]]'s *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* revives structuralist comparativism — limited combinatorial repertoires, transformational logic, morphology of relations — while **overturning** the nature/culture opposition Lévi-Strauss made foundational and partially **recovering** ontological [[totemism]] against pure classificatory dissolution. Descola (trained under Lévi-Strauss) presents this as fidelity to method against the spirit of dualism. The [[four-ontologies|four ontologies]] (animism, totemism, analogism, naturalism) are generated from two axes ([[interiority-and-physicality]]); Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies]]. See also structuralism hub note on Descola as lineage that overturns the hinge.

## Afterlife: Viveiros de Castro's "post-structural anthropology" (2014)

A second reactivation runs through the [[ontological-turn]]: [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]'s *[[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]* (2009/2014) rereads the *Mythologiques* via [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]] — "two structuralisms," a "structuralism without structure" governed by "perpetual disequilibrium" (the unequal twins) — and recasts [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] as "a philosopher of Amerindian thought," structuralism itself as "a structural transformation of Amerindian thought." See [[perspectivism]], [[multinaturalism]].

## Archaeological Applications: Glassie and Leroi-Gourhan (attributed — Johnson 2011)

*(Johnson 2011, Ch.6 — see [[johnson-archaeological-theory-2011]].)* Johnson reports
Saussurean structuralism's application within archaeology by two figures: **Henry
Glassie**, who applied structuralist analysis to Virginia vernacular house plans
(reading floor-plan and facade elements as a system of oppositions, on the linguistic
model), and **André Leroi-Gourhan**, who applied structuralist analysis to Palaeolithic
cave art (reading animal and sign motifs as elements of a systematic, patterned code
rather than isolated hunting-magic images). Both extend the Lévi-Straussian program's
central method — reducing surface variation to underlying oppositional structure —
into material-culture domains outside kinship and myth proper.

## Peircean Semiotics as an Alternative (attributed — Johnson 2011)

Johnson notes that Charles Peirce's semiotics (the sign–object–interpretant triad, and
the icon/index/symbol typology) has gained archaeological use as an alternative to the
disputed Saussurean/structuralist **textual analogy** for understanding meaning in
material culture — addressing critiques that treating artefacts as a "text" imports
assumptions (fixed authorial meaning, linguistic-style systematicity) that may not hold
for non-linguistic material signs. See [[post-processual-archaeology]] for the broader
interpretive-archaeology context in which this alternative arose.

## Reflexivity

Structuralism universalized the mind's operations at the moment (postwar decolonization) when anthropology's colonial framing was under attack; *Tristes Tropiques* is itself an early reflexive text, indicting the West's destruction of the worlds anthropology studies and anticipating the 1980s *Writing Culture* critique of ethnographic authority. The program's own claim to read structures "the natives" do not consciously hold sits in tension with the reflexive humility of *Tristes Tropiques* — a tension internal to Lévi-Strauss's work.

## Its archaeological application — see structuralist-archaeology (Reading the Past, 2003)

*(Attributed — [[hodder-reading-past-2004|Hodder & Hutson, Reading the Past]], 3rd ed., ch. 3.)* Hodder & Hutson's extended treatment of structuralism's archaeological uptake — formal (symmetry) analysis, Lévi-Straussian and Leroi-Gourhan-style meaning-assignment, case studies (Tilley on Nämforsen, McGhee on Thule, structured deposition), and the critique that Saussurean semiotics, unlike Peirce's triadic sign, "leave[s] no room for agency" — is filed on the dedicated page [[structuralist-archaeology]] rather than densified here, to keep this page to the parent anthropological program.
