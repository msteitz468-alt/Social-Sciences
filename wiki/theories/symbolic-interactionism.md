# Symbolic Interactionism

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/theory/symbolic-interactionism|Theory Hub]]

> **Scope note.** Classical foundations — *[[mead-mind-self-society-1934|Mind, Self, and Society]]* (1934), Cooley's *[[cooley-human-nature-social-order-1902|Human Nature and the Social Order]]* (1902), Thomas & Znaniecki's *[[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-1918|Polish Peasant]]* (1918) — plus [[goffman-erving|Goffman]]'s *[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]* (1956), *[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]* (1961), and *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]* (1963), and [[blumer-herbert|Blumer]]'s *[[blumer-symbolic-interactionism-1969|Symbolic Interactionism: Perspective and Method]]* (1969), the methodological manifesto. All programmatic claims in **attributed voice**. Still thin: Iowa school (Manford Kuhn), labeling theory as a named program. Goffman does not self-label as Blumerian SI in 1956; placement is by problem-space and reception — see [[dramaturgical-analysis]]. `originated_by` names Mead (intellectual source) and Blumer (namer and organizer). The named school is a [[disciplines/sociology|Critical-Turn-era]] development built on classical foundations.

## Core Claims

The school's canonical three premises are **Blumer's** (1969 Ch. 1), attributed; their substance is **Mead's**.

- **Action follows meaning.** Human beings "act toward things on the basis of the meanings that the things have for them" — physical objects, persons, categories, institutions, ideals, others' activities, situations (Blumer 1969).
- **Meaning is interactional.** Meaning "is derived from, or arises out of, the social interaction that one has with one's fellows" — not intrinsic to the thing (realism) and not a mere psychical accretion from private psyche (Blumer 1969).
- **Meaning is interpreted, not merely applied.** Meanings "are handled in, and modified through, an interpretative process" — [[self-indication]] and the [[the-i-and-the-me|"I"/"me" conversation]] (Blumer; Mead).
- **Self as process.** The human being is an object to himself; the [[the-social-self|self]] is reflexive process, not ego/attitudes/internalized norms as structure (Mead via Blumer Ch. 2).
- **[[social-object|Objects]] are social products.** Anything designateable; nature = meaning for the actor; worlds of objects differ across groups (Mead via Blumer).
- **Society as [[joint-action|joint action]].** Fitting together of diverse participants' acts; society as vast number of occurring joint actions, not a self-operating system (Blumer; Mead's "social act").

## Intellectual Origins

Deepest root: [[mead-george-herbert|Mead]]'s [[social-behaviorism|social behaviorism]] and [[pragmatism|pragmatist]] action theory. Parallel classical root: [[cooley-charles-horton|Cooley]] ([[looking-glass-self]], primary group). Chicago documentary and urban ethnography ([[thomas-william-isaac|Thomas]], [[znaniecki-florian|Znaniecki]], [[park-robert-e|Park]]) and [[formal-sociology|Simmelian]] form-analysis. Mead never used "symbolic interactionism" — **Blumer coinage (1937)** in an offhand article in *Man and Society*; he later called it a "somewhat barbaric neologism" that "somehow caught on" (*[[blumer-symbolic-interactionism-1969|1969]]* Ch. 1). The 1969 volume is Blumer's own systematization: "to a major extent I must bear full responsibility," especially for methodology.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- **[[cooley-charles-horton|Cooley]]** — looking-glass self, primary group (*[[cooley-human-nature-social-order-1902|Human Nature]]* 1902).
- **[[thomas-william-isaac|Thomas]] & [[znaniecki-florian|Znaniecki]]** — [[attitude-and-value]], [[definition-of-the-situation]], [[personal-documents-method]] (*[[thomas-znaniecki-polish-peasant-1918|Polish Peasant]]*). Blumer's appraisal (1969 Ch. 6) accepts the subjective–objective interaction core and human documents while rejecting "laws of social becoming" and finding that documents do not decisively test abstract theory.
- **Mead** — self, mind, meaning (*[[mead-mind-self-society-1934|Mind, Self, and Society]]* 1934). Canonization as SI scripture is a reception fact — [[mind-self-society-authorship-debate]]; [[mead-cooley-self-origin-debate]].
- **[[blumer-herbert|Herbert Blumer]]** — coined the name (1937); *[[blumer-symbolic-interactionism-1969|Symbolic Interactionism]]* (1969): three premises, root images, [[exploration-and-inspection]], critique of variable analysis, [[sensitizing-concept|sensitizing concepts]], attitude and polling critiques. Students he names as persistent advocates of the volume: Tamotsu Shibutani, Howard Becker.
- **[[goffman-erving|Erving Goffman]]**, *[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|Presentation of Self]]* (1956) — [[dramaturgical-analysis]], [[impression-management]], front/team/region apparatus; see [[goffman-presentation-of-self-study]]. *[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]* (1961) — institutional ethnography; see [[goffman-asylums-study]]. *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]* (1963) — spoiled identity, [[passing]], [[covering]], [[social-information]]; see [[goffman-stigma-study]]. Thinkers Hub: [[hubs/thinkers/goffman-erving]].
- **[[berger-peter-l|Berger]] & [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]]** (*[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|Social Construction of Reality]]*, 1966) — **not** Blumerian SI, but name Mead/SI as main source for their social-psychology of internalization; bridge outward. See [[social-construction-of-reality]].
- Still thin: Iowa school (Manford Kuhn); labeling theory as named program.

## Empirical Program

Characteristic method: naturalistic close study of interaction — [[participant-observation]], life histories, documents, recovery of how actors define situations. Blumer's pair [[exploration-and-inspection|exploration and inspection]] (1969 Ch. 1) is the explicit SI methodological program: flexible acquaintance-building plus intensive examination of analytic elements; acute informed participants over unobservant samples. Thomas & Znaniecki's letter series are the classical **documentary** arm. Goffman's *Presentation of Self* is the **formal dramaturgical** arm (handbook of establishment performances; Shetland illustrations); St. Elizabeths year is the first full institutional PO in this lineage; *Stigma* is the complementary **conceptual** arm — literature synthesis of mixed contacts and information control without a second field campaign. The tradition is strong on micro meaning-making and weak (by standing charge) on macro power and economy — Blumer answers in part by treating large organization as interlinked joint actions mediated by definition, not as self-running systems.

## Method Commitments

(Attributed to Blumer 1969 unless noted.)

1. Respect the nature of the empirical social world — methods are instruments subservient to that world, not imported protocols that replace firsthand acquaintance.
2. Take the standpoint of the acting unit; recover the actor's world of [[social-object|objects]].
3. Study social interaction as a **formative process**, not a neutral medium for psychological or structural factors.
4. Study the **career of the act** (and of joint action), not only initiating factors and terminal rates.
5. Prefer [[exploration-and-inspection|exploration and inspection]] over pre-mapped variable protocols; treat most sociological concepts as [[sensitizing-concept|sensitizing]], not definitive.
6. Variable analysis and public-opinion polling have legitimate limited uses but, Blumer argues, systematically omit intervening interpretation and mis-sample organic social structure (1969 Ch. 7, 12).

## Major Debates and Rivals

- **vs. structural-functionalism / system sociology.** Parsons-style complementarity of expectations as *the* form of interaction is parochial (Blumer Ch. 2); society is not a self-operating system. See [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] (Blumer position).
- **vs. variable-analytic / survey protocol.** Interpretation cannot be reduced to an intervening variable; bare factor relations omit the "here and now" of definition (Blumer Ch. 7).
- **vs. behaviorism.** "Symbolic" marks the break from S–R (see [[social-behaviorism]]).
- **vs. attitude psychology as explanation of the act.** Attitude fails as scientific concept and as controller of the act (Blumer Ch. 4).
- **Internal:** Cooley vs Mead on self-origin — [[mead-cooley-self-origin-debate]]; MSS authorship — [[mind-self-society-authorship-debate]].

## Critiques and Responses

| Charge | Response (attributed / tradition) |
|---|---|
| Astructural (power, class, institutions) | Structure exists but enters action only via interpretation; institutions are ongoing joint actions (Blumer); Goffman on total institutions |
| Soft / unscientific method | Exploration/inspection claimed as rigorous respect for the object; empirical world "talks back" (Blumer Ch. 1) |
| Unstable canonical text (Mead) | Reception fact; Blumer owns his systematization; philological Mead scholarship ongoing |
| Underplays coercion | Blumer notes joint action may rest on duress, compromise, necessity — not only shared values — but critics still find power thin |

## What Survived / Current Status

**Active** tradition (Society for the Study of Symbolic Interaction; *Symbolic Interaction*). Core assumptions — interpretive actor, social self, situated meaning, joint action — have diffused into everyday-life sociology, deviance, emotion, identity, qualitative methodology, and (selectively) into constructionist programs. [[sensitizing-concept|Sensitizing concepts]] and naturalistic design language outlived mid-century polemics against survey sociology. Adjacent method-theory: Glaser & Strauss [[grounded-theory]] (1967; Chicago-trained Strauss in the partnership) codifies generation procedures ([[theoretical-sampling]], [[constant-comparative-method]]) that share Blumerian hostility to premature a priori schemes without being identical to [[exploration-and-inspection]] — see [[hubs/theory/grounded-theory]].

## Reflexivity

The school's foundation is a reception artifact: scripture its founder did not write ([[mind-self-society-authorship-debate]]), filtered through **Blumer's** selective reading. Production context of the 1969 volume: mid-century American sociology's quantification turn — Blumer writes as defender of naturalistic/Chicago methods against the variable paradigm, funding/dissertation gatekeeping that rewards pre-mapped protocol, and operationalism. His Mead is a pedagogical reconstruction for sociology, not archival philology.
