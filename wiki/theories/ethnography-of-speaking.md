# Ethnography of Speaking

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/theory/ethnography-of-speaking|Theory Hub]]

Research program founded by [[hymes-dell|Dell Hymes]] (named 1962; communication scope 1964) for the systematic ethnographic study of **ways of speaking** as cultural systems that organize diversity. Method page: [[ethnography-of-communication]]. Charter synthesis for this wiki: *[[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974|Foundations in Sociolinguistics]]* (1974).

## Core Claims

All attributed to Hymes (*Foundations*):

1. **Patterns proper to speech.** Speech activity has organization that cannot be recovered by separately correlating grammar, personality, social structure, or religion.
2. **Frame = ethnography of communication.** Not linguistics and not "language" alone, but a community's communicative economy — means, purposes, selection patterns, hierarchies.
3. **[[communicative-competence|Communicative competence]].** Knowledge of how to conduct and interpret speech (including appropriateness and silence) exceeds grammatical competence of an ideal speaker-hearer.
4. **Descriptive units.** [[speech-community|Speech community]]; speech situation / [[speech-event]] / speech act; [[speaking-model|SPEAKING]] component heuristic; ways of speaking; speech economy.
5. **Sociolinguistics as mode of description.** Success depends on a specific descriptive mode (parallel to how linguistics succeeded relative to folklore); "sociolinguistic description" = ethnography of speaking.
6. **Socially constituted linguistics.** Social function gives form to how linguistic features are encountered; start from social functions; reverse the isolation of pure referential structure (Ch. 10's radical orientation).
7. **Self-liquidation.** If the program succeeds, "sociolinguistics" as special name should liquidate into a reconstructed linguistics (and ethnography) that already includes social constitution of speech.

## Intellectual Origins

- **reacted_against:** Autonomous American structural linguistics (form without society); Chomskyan idealization of homogeneous competence as the whole of theory; correlational "language and society" that leaves both departments unchanged.
- **developed_from:** [[sapir-edward|Sapir]] (vitalization of linguistics; late writings as sociolinguistic standpoint); Boas; Jakobson (functions/components); Malinowski ([[context-of-situation]] lineage); Prague/Firth process traditions; Kenneth Burke (dramatism, action vs motion, multi-function language); engagement with Marx (fetishism analogy for language abstracted from social roots).
- **Allies:** [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] (interaction order as core discovery of sociolinguistics); Gumperz; Labov (socially realistic linguistics); folklore performance turn; education research (classroom ethnography).

Hymes explicitly equates **ethnography of speaking** with **sociolinguistic description** and treats **ethnography of communication** as the wider frame (channels and codes beyond speech alone).

## Empirical Program

*Foundations* is idea-heavy; illustrations (Wishram, Paliyan silence, Chinookan, Gbeya, bilingual classrooms, folklore performance, etc.) are **heuristic taxonomy fodder**, not a single primary study. The program's empirical demand is: describe a community's sociolinguistic system as one describes a language or culture — via events, repertoires, rules of co-occurrence and alternation (Ervin-Tripp), and comparative etics of speaking (noting HRAF-era neglect of speaking as topic).

## Method Commitments

- [[ethnography-of-communication]] as data-collection/analytic frame
- Units: [[speech-community]], [[speech-event]], [[speaking-model]]
- Jointly ethnographic and linguistic description; message form kept with context
- Emic validation under etic grids (Conklin/Goodenough cultural-grammar ideal)
- Formal statement of rules for comparison (not only narrative ethnography)

## Critiques and Responses

- **Formal linguistics:** Charges that sociolinguistics confuses competence with performance or abandons science for ethnography. Hymes responds: complementary explanatory adequacy; grammar remains necessary but not sufficient; performance reclaimed as accomplishment.
- **Checklist SPEAKING:** Pedagogical reduction. Hymes: mnemonic ≠ theory; components are relational.
- **Correlational sociolinguistics:** Gross correlation of language features with social categories misses "structure as organization of diversity." Hymes wants integrated theory of sociolinguistic description.
- **New-discipline temptation:** He prefers merger/recasting of linguistics and ethnography over a permanent third department (Ch. 4).

## Current Status

`status: active` as absorbed vocabulary and method in linguistic anthropology, interactional sociolinguistics, educational ethnography, and performance studies. The self-liquidation of the label "sociolinguistics" did not fully occur; generative and ethnographic programs remain institutionally distinct. See [[communicative-competence-debate]].

## The 1972 Landmark Anthology

*[[gumperz-hymes-directions-sociolinguistics-1972|Directions in Sociolinguistics]]* (Gumperz & Hymes, eds., 1972) is the program's landmark **anthology** — a multi-author collection of independently authored chapters, distinct from *Foundations* (1974), which is Hymes's own continuous programmatic essays. The volume collected and named several of the "directions" sociolinguistics subsequently branched into: [[interactional-sociolinguistics|interactional sociolinguistics]] and [[code-switching|code-switching]] (Gumperz's Introduction; Blom & Gumperz on metaphorical switching), [[ethnomethodology|ethnomethodology]] and [[conversation-analysis|conversation analysis]] (Garfinkel, Sacks, Schegloff), [[variationist-sociolinguistics|variationist sociolinguistics]] (Labov's NYC dialect tradition), and Bernstein's restricted/elaborated **codes**. Hymes's own chapter 1, "Models of the Interaction of Language and Social Life," gives the fullest 1972-era componential statement of [[speaking-model|SPEAKING]] and the speech-community/situation/event/act taxonomy, predating and feeding the 1974 restatement.

## Reflexivity

The program is a Critical-Turn product of U.S. anthropology/linguistics (SSRC Committee on Sociolinguistics; Center for Urban Ethnography), arguing for inclusion of diversity, education, and non-standard speakers against homogeneous competence ideals. Hymes links scientific and "liberation" goals (unequal competence opportunities). Production context: invited papers revised into a book dedicated to Sapir; special debt to Goffman — record as network facts, not as proof of claims.
