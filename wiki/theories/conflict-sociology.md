# Conflict Sociology (Collins's Weberian-Durkheimian Synthesis)

**Conflict sociology** is [[collins-randall|Randall Collins]]'s programmatic synthesis, stated in *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology: Toward an Explanatory Science]]* (1975), of the [[conflict-theory|conflict tradition]] ([[marx-karl|Marx]], [[weber-max|Weber]]) with a microsociology of ritual and emotion drawn from [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] and [[goffman-erving|Goffman]]. It differs from the [[conflict-theory|Dahrendorf/postwar authority-conflict program]] by grounding macro-conflict in the dynamics of face-to-face encounters and by aiming explicitly at a cumulative, propositional **explanatory science**.

Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/conflict-sociology]].

*Seeded from Collins 1975. His later restatements (Weberian Sociological Theory 1986; Interaction Ritual Chains 2004) are not yet ingested.*

## Core Claims

All in **attributed** voice (Collins 1975).

1. **Conflict anthropology and method.** Human beings are "sociable but conflict-prone animals"; because violent coercion is always a latent, zero-sum resource, every empirical area can be analyzed by reducing it to typical interactions among self-interested, emotionally susceptible actors, inventorying the material resources that shape those interactions, and hypothesizing that resource inequality yields advantage-taking. "Life is basically a struggle for status."
2. **Order-giving and order-taking.** The archetypal social-causal act is "a man giving orders to another." Occupational position on the order-giving/order-taking continuum (upper/middle/working), together with communication-network position, is the primary determinant of [[class-cultures|class cultures]] — outlooks, deference styles, and sociability — rather than income or property alone.
3. **[[interaction-ritual|Interaction ritual]] as microfoundation.** Following Durkheim's *Elementary Forms*, copresence + a common focus of attention + stereotyped gesture/sound generates a common mood that reifies the symbols attended to ("Durkheim's social density principle," proposition 4.7). Stratification is reproduced in a "conversational market" where people trade conversational and non-conversational resources for status and deference.
4. **Organizations as arenas of conflict.** Reviving what he takes to be Weber's misread organizational theory, Collins treats organizational structure as "the network of power relations" and analyzes control through coercive, material, and normative (ritual) sanctions and their administrative devices (surveillance, rules, information control).
5. **The state as organized violence; [[geopolitical-theory-of-the-state|geopolitics]].** "The state is, above all, the army and the police." State expansion and collapse turn on heartland resources, weapon/supply/administration types, and single- vs. multi-front (peripheral vs. interior) position; conquest among comparably organized rivals produces rapid overextension (Napoleon, Hitler — under 15 years each).
6. **Explanatory-science program.** Sociology can become cumulative and testable; the book states its theory "semiformally" as numbered causal propositions, most explicit for stratification, organizations, and politics.

## Intellectual Origins

- **developed_from / reacted_against:** the [[conflict-theory|conflict tradition]] "of Machiavelli, Marx, and their successors," extended by [[weber-max|Weber]]'s multi-dimensional conflict theory; [[dahrendorf-ralf|Dahrendorf]]'s (1959) revision of Marx (authority, not property, as the general case). Collins **reacted_against** [[structural-functionalism|Parsonian functionalism]], which he charges "emasculated" Weber's conflict nominalism into a reified social system.
- **Micro sources:** [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] on ritual solidarity (reworked as [[interaction-ritual]]); [[goffman-erving|Goffman]] on the interaction order; the interpretive/[[interpretive-sociology|ethnomethodological]] insight that social reality is constructed in interaction — borrowed for its "conceptual reconstruction" while its anti-generalizing stance is rejected.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[collins-randall|Randall Collins]] — *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology]]* (1975), the program's foundational statement in this wiki; Ch. 4 co-authored with Joan Annett.

## Empirical Program

Collins organizes existing research — organizational sociology (Weber, Etzioni, Crozier, Michels), stratification and mobility, comparative-historical politics ([[moore-barrington|Barrington Moore]], [[lenski-gerhard|Lenski]]), and the sociology of science — as applications of one stratification-and-organization theory plus a cross-cutting social psychology of interaction. On his own 1975 self-assessment, the evidence is strongest for organizations and class cultures, weaker for the microsociology-to-stratification linkage and for wealth/mobility. Lenski’s *[[lenski-power-and-privilege-1966|Power and Privilege]]* ([[ecological-evolutionary-theory]]) is the comparative technology-and-wealth model Collins treats as empirically strongest for wealth (while still “most loosely connected” to his own micro program).

## Method Commitments

Ranked causal propositions; controlled comparison (Durkheim's suicide/solidarity model) as the "basic method of explanatory science"; multi-method combination (historical, survey, participant and personal observation); fact/value separation defended against both positivist technicism and relativist historicism.

## Critiques and Responses

The micro-macro linkage is, by Collins's own admission, "largely hypothetical"; critics question whether prose propositions constitute a testable theory, and whether reducing ethnicity, education, and sex stratification to one conflict model over-unifies distinct processes. See [[consensus-vs-conflict-debate]] for the broader framing dispute.

## Current Status

`status: active` — Collins's micro-founded conflict theory, the interaction-ritual mechanism, and the geopolitical theory of the state all remain live research programs, developed in his own later work and in historical and micro sociology. Distinct from, but allied to, the [[conflict-theory|Dahrendorf program]].

## Reflexivity

*Conflict Sociology* is a product of the critical turn: written as American sociology's Cold War suppression of the conflict tradition eased and the 1960s movements revived Marxian and Weberian conflict theory. Collins writes as an explicit partisan of a value-detached explanatory science, a stance he attributes above all to Weber — itself a methodological-political position, not a neutral vantage.
