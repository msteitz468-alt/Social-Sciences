# Marxist Theory of the State

## Core Claims

*(All attributed — primarily as reconstructed by [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] in *[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|State and Revolution]]*, drawing on [[marx-karl|Marx]] and [[engels-friedrich|Engels]].)*

1. The [[the-state|state]] arises where society is split into **irreconcilable** [[class-struggle|class antagonisms]]; it is a power seemingly above society that keeps conflict within “order,” not a neutral ethical universal (contra Hegel) and not a reconciler of classes.
2. Its distinctive means are “**special bodies of armed men**,” prisons, bureaucracy, and taxes — a public force alienated from the self-acting armed population.
3. As a rule the state is the state of the economically dominant class; exceptional periods of near-class balance can yield temporary independence of state power (Bonapartism, absolute monarchy — link [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]], [[bonapartism]]).
4. The modern representative / democratic republic remains a form of **bourgeois class rule** (“dictatorship of the bourgeoisie”) until the machine is destroyed.
5. The proletarian revolution must **[[smash-the-state-machine|smash]]** that machine (not perfect or merely staff it) and replace it with a [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat|dictatorship of the proletariat]] — Commune / soviet-type organs.
6. That transitional state itself [[withering-away-of-the-state|withers away]] as classes disappear; the bourgeois state is **abolished**, not withered; anarchist immediate abolition of all authority is rejected (*On Authority*).
7. Economic basis (*Gotha Critique*): lower phase of communism still needs residual right and a semi-state; higher phase (“to each according to his needs”) opens the door to complete withering.

## Empirical Program

As **theory of strategy and political form**, not a survey or causal-identification program. Historical anchors in Lenin’s text: 1848–51 France, Paris Commune 1871, (promised, unfinished) 1905/1917 Russia. Later comparative revolution sociology (e.g. Skocpol) tests structural conditions of [[social-revolution|social revolutions]] with different premises — **cross-link, do not merge**.

## Method Commitments

- Close reading and selective quotation of the Marxist canon.
- Conjunctural political polemic (1917 dual power, world war).
- Historical examples as **lessons for tasks**, not independent archival historiography.
- Explicit refusal of “free people’s state” and of treating democracy as classless.

## Current Status

Active reference programme in Marxist political thought and in histories of 1917; contested or set aside in mainstream political sociology, which more often starts from Weber, Tilly, or pluralism. The **concepts** (DoP, smash, withering) remain analytical objects even where the strategic programme is not adopted. Later Marxist state theory (Miliband, Poulantzas, etc.) reworks the problem-space — not yet primary-ingested here.

## Application: 1917 and the Dual Power (attributed)

In April–June 1917 (*[[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]]*: April Theses, "The Dual Power," Draft Platform, April Conference), Lenin applied the programme to the concrete conjuncture: the Provisional Government and the Soviets constituted [[dual-power|two powers]] that "cannot coexist" for long; the question of state power, not of specific reforms, was "the basic question of every revolution." He proposed replacing the demand for a "bourgeois parliamentary republic" with a "democratic proletarian-peasant republic" functioning "without police, without a standing army, and without a privileged bureaucracy," explicitly rejecting rival Bolshevik formulations invoking the Commune or "republic of Soviets" language as premature or "unsatisfactory" at that stage of drafting. He distinguished Bolshevism from both Second-International "Social-Democratism" (attributed to [[plekhanov-georgi|Plekhanov]] and [[kautsky-karl|Kautsky]]) and from anarchism, which he characterized as negating the need for any transitional state.

## Application: *Left-Wing Communism* (1920, attributed)

In *[[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]]*, Lenin restated the [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] as "a most determined and most ruthless war waged by the new class against a more powerful enemy," including against the spontaneous, daily, hourly regeneration of capitalism out of small-scale production — extending the state-smashing programme into a claim about the *duration and difficulty* of the transition, and defending centralized party discipline as a practical requirement of that war rather than a departure from Commune-type democracy (a claim disputed by critics who read it as license for bureaucratic oligarchy within the party itself).

## Application: Last Writings and the Realized State (1922–23, attributed)

*[[lenin-last-writings-1923]]* (On Co-operation; Better Fewer, But Better; the Letter to the Congress) records Lenin's own retrospective judgment that the post-1917 [[the-state|state]] apparatus remained substantially "a survival of the past" rather than the smashed-and-rebuilt Commune-type organ of 1917 theory — the old machinery, in his phrase, "only slightly anointed with Soviet oil." He proposed reforming the Workers' and Peasants' Inspection (Rabkrin) to check [[bureaucracy]] and warned against Great-Russian chauvinism among state officials. This material feeds the practice-vs-programme tension already flagged in Reflexivity below, rather than revising the core theoretical claims.

## Application: The Renegade Kautsky (1918, attributed)

In *[[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]*, Lenin sharpened the theory's central polemical target — [[kautsky-karl|Kautsky]]'s "fundamental contrast" between "democratic and dictatorial methods," which Lenin called a renunciation of Marxism exceeding Bernstein's revisionism. He defined [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] as "rule based directly upon force and unrestricted by any laws," crediting this as the definition Kautsky should have derived from Marx's own usage (the 1875 *Critique of the Gotha Programme* formula: "the state can be nothing but the revolutionary dictatorship of the proletariat") but evaded by trivializing it as a "little word." He argued dictatorship does not necessarily abolish democracy for the ruling class itself, only for the class against which it is directed (citing ancient slaveowner "democracy"), and that bourgeois democracy, though a genuine historical advance over feudalism, "always remains, and under capitalism is bound to remain, restricted, truncated, false and hypocritical, a paradise for the rich and a snare and deception for the exploited." Quoting Engels (letter to Bebel, March 28, 1875: "so long as the proletariat still needs the state, it does not need it in the interests of freedom but in order to hold down its adversaries") and Engels's introduction to *The Civil War in France* ("the state is nothing but a machine for the oppression of one class by another... in the democratic republic no less than in the monarchy"), Lenin rebutted Kautsky's Soviets-must-not-become-state-organizations argument — which he traced to Menshevik figures (Martov, Axelrod, Stein) — on the grounds that the state itself "is nothing but a machine for the suppression of one class by another," so the Soviets embracing all wage-workers logically must become the new state apparatus once decisive class battles are underway. He argued exploiters retain practical advantages (money, connections, education, military experience) for a prolonged period after a successful uprising, so "the transition from capitalism to communism takes an entire historical epoch" — extending the theory's transitional-period claim into an explicit temporal caution against expecting rapid withering.

## Application: Kautsky's *Terrorism and Communism* (1919, attributed)

In *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]]*, Kautsky argues that majority-over-minority is the only compulsion compatible with democracy; personal dictatorship and Soviet "democracy" as actually practised are incompatible; free press is essential against corruption; despotism is the essence of dictatorship; and the road of cannons/terror leads not to higher forms of life but to ruin — the world revolution proceeds only through democracy and humanity. Direct counter-position to Trotsky 1920 and to smash-only readings of the transition. See [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]], [[red-terror]].

## Application: Trotsky's *Terrorism and Communism* (1920, attributed)

In *[[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920]]*, [[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]] extends the civil-war DoP beyond Lenin's 1917 Commune programme: formal democracy is a class form and natural-law metaphysics; the Constituent Assembly path is historically closed under imperialism; [[red-terror]] is the method of breaking the enemy's will when resistance is ferocious; the dictatorship of the soviets requires the dictatorship of the party; and the road to socialism runs through "the highest possible intensification of the principle of the state" — the dictatorship as the state's "last brilliant flame" — including [[militarization-of-labour]] (compulsory labour service, labour armies, production-unions under a single plan). Marx's *Civil War in France* is enlisted against Kautsky's democratic reading of the Commune. This is practitioner-state theory under multi-front war, not a restatement of *State and Revolution*'s semi-state imagery alone — see [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]].

## Application: Bureaucracy and the Economic Foundations of Withering (1921, attributed)

In *[[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]* (Plan-of-Pamphlet notes), Lenin cross-referenced *[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|The State and Revolution]]* on "the economic foundations for the withering away of the state" and extended the concept under NEP conditions: there are also "economic foundations... for the withering away of bureaucracy" — but he stated plainly "the economic basis of socialism is not yet there," identifying the actual present basis as "commodity exchange with the peasantry." This qualifies the theory's withering-away claim (Core Claim 6) with a concrete, dated admission that the material preconditions for withering remained unmet four years after the seizure of power — see [[withering-away-of-the-state]] and [[bureaucracy]] for the fuller argument.

## Reflexivity

Produced as wartime revolutionary theory under dual power in Russia, 1917 — not as armchair system-building. The pamphlet’s democratic-commune imagery and subsequent Soviet state form are often held apart by historians; this wiki records both the text’s claims and the need for separate evidence on practice. Chretien’s 2014 apparatus is partisan; primary claims track Lenin’s words. “Special bodies of armed men” and some DoP dating are flagged as Lenin’s synthesis/selection, not pure Engels/Marx quotation.
