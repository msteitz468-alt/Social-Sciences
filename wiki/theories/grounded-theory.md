# Grounded Theory

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/theory/grounded-theory|Theory Hub]]

> **Scope note.** This page is grounded in Glaser & Strauss, *[[glaser-strauss-discovery-grounded-theory-1967|The Discovery of Grounded Theory]]* (1967). Later Glaser–Strauss splits (Glaserian vs Straussian coding packages), Corbin/Strauss manuals, and constructivist grounded theory (Charmaz) are **not yet ingested** — record them only when sources enter. All programmatic claims in **attributed voice**.

## Core Claims

All claims below are **attributed** to [[glaser-barney-g|Glaser]] and [[strauss-anselm-l|Strauss]] (1967) unless noted.

1. **Discovery over deduction.** Theory is generated from data systematically obtained in research. Generating and verifying are both scientific; postwar sociology, they argue, overemphasized verification and logico-deductive schemes.
2. **Fit and work.** Categories must be readily (not forcibly) applicable to and indicated by the data (**fit**); they must be meaningfully relevant to and able to explain the behavior under study (**work**).
3. **Jobs of theory.** Theory should enable prediction and explanation; advance sociology theoretically; be usable in practical applications; provide a perspective/stance toward data; and guide research style on particular areas.
4. **Comparative analysis as generation engine.** Comparative analysis is a general method (like experimental and statistical methods), applicable to social units of any size — not only large-scale cross-national comparison — used here for **generating** theory.
5. **[[theoretical-sampling|Theoretical sampling]].** Data collection is controlled by the emerging theory: jointly collect, code, analyze; decide next data and where to find them. Stop a category at [[theoretical-saturation|theoretical saturation]].
6. **Substantive → formal.** [[substantive-theory|Substantive theory]] (one empirical area) is the strategic link to [[formal-theory-grounded|formal theory]]; formal theory can be generated directly but usually should start from substantive; one-area "rewriting" alone is inadequate formal theory.
7. **[[constant-comparative-method|Constant comparative method]].** Four stages of joint coding and analysis generate categories and properties; theory is process until saturated and written.
8. **Flexible data.** Qualitative *and* quantitative data can generate theory; library/document caches and secondary analysis are first-class media (Chs VII–VIII).
9. **Application properties.** Practical grounded theory must *fit*, be *understandable* to lay participants, be *general* across diverse situations in the area, and allow *partial control* via controllable and access variables (Ch. X).

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against:

- The postwar primacy of **verification** methodology and the prestige of logico-deductive / [[grand-theory|grand]] schemes.
- The theory–research gap Blumer diagnosed (and, G&S argue, addressed too much from the verification side) and Merton addressed via serendipitous *modification* of theory rather than purposeful **discovery**.
- Speculative theory "illustrated" by apt cases and protected by licenses when tests fail (Epilogue polemic with Merton 1964 on anomie).

Authors' training (preface): Glaser — Columbia (Merton middle-range / Lazarsfeld quantitative methodology); Strauss — Chicago tradition (down-to-earth qualitative research). Empirical matrix of illustrations: PHS nursing grant dying research (*Awareness of Dying*, 1965; *Time for Dying*; Quint nurse study).

Affinities with [[chicago-school]], [[symbolic-interactionism]], Blumerian [[sensitizing-concept]] / [[exploration-and-inspection]] — without reducing GT to SI.

## Empirical Program

*Discovery* is methodological; its "empirical program" is the claim that generation procedures can be systematized and taught. Payoffs claimed: denser, more relevant substantive theories (e.g., awareness contexts in dying); path from substantive to formal theory; applied theory usable by practitioners. Ch. VI reviews comparative monographs for how far they generate vs merely verify or illustrate. Full assessment of the dying monographs awaits their ingest.

## Critiques and Responses

From *Discovery*'s own foil structure (primary rebuttals not yet ingested):

- **Unsystematic / impressionistic:** G&S answer with explicit procedures and degrees of plausibility for different tasks.
- **Not science without verification:** They refuse to divorce generation from verification but reverse primacy for the generation phase.
- **Non-replicable coding:** They concede CCM does not guarantee identical results across analysts — by design for creative generation.
- **Later literature** (partially ingested via foil): recipe-ization of GT; induction purity debates; Glaser vs Strauss/Corbin orthodoxy fights; constructivist revisions.
- **Tavory & Timmermans (*[[tavory-timmermans-abductive-analysis-2014|Abductive Analysis]]*, 2014):** argue (attributed) that GT became the standard inductive path and that its **inductivist legacy has thwarted rather than advanced** theory construction. Virtually all how-to books and software, they claim, try to lead to thematic analysis through GT and struggle with that legacy. The problem is not coding/memo precepts per se but their anchorage in a general inductive view of science that structures research design: induction strengthens generalizations from repeated observations but **cannot specify what to focus on or invent new framings**. They report GT’s late-1980s dominance via Strauss’s *Qualitative Analysis for Social Scientists* and Strauss & Corbin’s *Basics*, then a split into “classic” Glaser (inductive formal theories; positivist heritage) and “interactionist” Strauss/Corbin (interpretative; researcher as instrument). Against advocates who rebrand GT as [[abduction|abduction]], they argue rebranding does not purge inductivist design — Strauss mentioned abduction only once in a footnote; Glaser, they say, never broached it. Their alternative is [[abductive-analysis]] (theoretical pluralism + method-driven surprise).

## Current Status

**Active** as a major qualitative-research tradition worldwide, with internal schisms after the 1967 joint program. This collection holds the founding text plus a major 2014 methodological critique/repositioning ([[abductive-analysis]]); textbook GT manuals and Charmaz-style constructivist GT remain coverage gaps.

## Reflexivity

Joint Columbia–Chicago authorship is itself a methodological claim (closing the theory–research gap). Production funded by nursing/community-health research on dying — clinical settings shape examples (awareness contexts, social loss, trajectories). Mid-century gendered "the analyst" language and U.S. hospital ethnography are period facts. Later textbook GT often flattens 1967 generation emphasis into coding recipes — flag when secondary sources enter. Epilogue's sharp polemic with Merton is part of the book's rhetorical politics, not a neutral survey of Columbia sociology.
