# Circulation of elites

## Core Claims

All claims **attributed** to Pareto in *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* (Ch XII–XIII primarily; elite themes also prepared earlier).

1. **Ruling and subject classes.** Pareto treated societies as stratified into a governing minority and a subject mass; the governing class maintains itself by force, cunning, and manipulation of residues/interests, and is open (to varying degrees) to recruitment from below (§§ on force and class composition, Ch XII).

2. **Residue mix is load-bearing.** He argued that the balance of **[[combination-instinct|Class I]]** and **[[group-persistence|Class II]]** residues in ruling vs subject classes is a principal factor in [[paretian-social-equilibrium|social equilibrium]] and historical transformation (Ch XIII, §§2412+).

3. **[[lions-and-foxes]].** Governments relying chiefly on physical force and religious/similar sentiments tend to show Class II predominance in the governing class (slow circulation, cheap, low economic stimulus, crystallization ideal); governments relying on intelligence and cunning show Class I predominance — subtype II-a on sentiments, II-b on interests (“government by speculators”) (§§2274–2276).

4. **Optimum command structure.** Efficiency is greatest, he argued, when combination-instincts predominate in leaders and group-persistence in subordinates (obedience as “religion”); reverse combinations are disastrous (§§2427–2455).

5. **[[speculators-and-rentiers]].** Economic-psychological types S (speculators: expansive, combination-ready) and R (rentiers: fixed-income, Class II-heavy) fill governing classes differently in prosperity vs depression; modern plutocracy is S-dominated (§§2233–34, 2310–2328).

6. **[[demagogic-plutocracy]].** The “very important type of government flourishing in our day” is demagogic / government by speculators — expensive, often highly productive, rapid circulation, risk of shrewd cowardice open to overthrow by force (§§2275–2309).

7. **Waves, not linear progress.** Crystallization and free initiative succeed each other; social movements progress in undulations (Rome → later Empire crystallization; modern analogies) (Ch XIII).

## Intellectual Origins

- Embedded in [[paretian-sociology]]’s residue/derivation and equilibrium architecture; earlier anticipations in *Les Systèmes socialistes* (not yet ingested).
- **reacted_against:** Unilinear democratic progress narratives; unitary “capitalists” as a moral type; explanations of government form by constitutions alone without residue composition.
- **competes_with:** [[historical-materialism]]’s class-struggle motor (Pareto’s S/R and residue classes cut across “capitalist” as a single actor).

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[pareto-vilfredo]] — *Mind and Society* Ch XII–XIII
- Later elite theory (Mosca, Michels, Mills, etc.) is adjacent reception — not yet ingested; do not equate Mosca’s *ruling class* with Pareto’s circulation without attributed argument.

## Empirical Program

Historical-comparative illustrations (Athens, Sparta, Macedonia, Rome Republic/Empire, medieval republics, modern France/Italy/Germany). No systematic elite-mobility datasets in this source. Protectionism and party finance appear as mechanisms accelerating circulation (§§2208 f., 2327).

## Method Commitments

Same [[logico-experimental-method]] as the parent program: residue indices imagined as quantitative; concrete governments treated as mixtures of ideal types I / II-a / II-b.

## Critiques and Responses

Attributed later critiques: cynicism or anti-democratic bias; under-specification of “elite”; fascist appropriation; neglect of mass democratic institutions as more than demagogy. Pareto’s response pattern: restate truth≠utility of Class II beliefs and the productive side of II-b regimes.

## Current Status

`status: active` as a classical middle-range vocabulary in political sociology (elite recruitment, circulation, lions/foxes metaphors), usually detached from full residue taxonomy.

## Reflexivity

Written from a Lausanne/Italian elite vantage hostile to contemporary “pluto-demagogic” democracy (Pareto’s term). Speculator/rentier language is economic-psychological typology, not ethnic essentialism — but the treatise’s anti-Semitism-as-anti-speculator illustration (§ ranges in claims) must stay attributed and not generalized. Production amid pre- and post-WWI European elite crises.
