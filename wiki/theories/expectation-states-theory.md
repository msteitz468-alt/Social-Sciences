# Expectation States Theory

**Expectation states theory** (status characteristics theory and related branches) is a long-running research program, associated especially with Joseph Berger and colleagues, explaining how status information organizes influence and evaluation in task-focused interaction. In this wiki it is currently scaffolded primarily via [[ridgeway-cecilia-l|Ridgeway]]'s gender applications in *[[ridgeway-framed-by-gender-2011|Framed by Gender]]* (2011); fuller primary ingest of Berger-program texts will densify this page.

## Core Claims

**Attributed** to the Berger program as summarized and used by Ridgeway (2011):

- In collective task situations, actors form **performance expectations** for self and other.
- **Status characteristics** (specific or **diffuse**) become bases for those expectations when they are **salient** and culturally linked to competence.
- Resulting expectation states shape the **power and prestige order** (opportunities to perform, evaluations, influence).
- Gender often functions as a **diffuse status characteristic** favoring men on general competence in contemporary U.S. cultural beliefs (Ridgeway's application).

## Intellectual Origins

Postwar small-group and status research; Stanford expectation-states laboratory tradition. Ridgeway develops **[[status-construction-theory]]** as an account of how status beliefs arise and uses expectation-states mechanisms inside **[[gender-as-primary-frame]]**.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- Berger, Cohen, Zelditch and collaborators — core program.
- Wagner & Berger reviews; Ridgeway's gender applications and *Framed by Gender*.

## Empirical Program

Laboratory experiments on influence, evaluation, and leadership; extensions to organizations and gender. Exemplary applications in Ridgeway Ch 3–4 synthesis.

## Method Commitments

Standardized experimental designs measuring influence and performance evaluation; formal graph-theoretic models in the core tradition; cultural status beliefs as inputs.

## Critiques and Responses

Scope conditions (task orientation, collective orientation) limit generalizability; critics of lab external validity; Ridgeway addresses scope by linking to workplace and household contexts with mixed methods evidence.

## Current Status

Active; major infrastructure for sociological social psychology of status and for Ridgeway's gender-frame program.

## Reflexivity

Program historically U.S. laboratory-centric; WEIRD samples; strong internal validity, contested external validity. Gender applications inherit both the precision and the sampling limits of the experimental base.
