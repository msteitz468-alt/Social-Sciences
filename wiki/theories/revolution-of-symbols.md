# Revolution of Symbols (Cauvin)

Middle-range theory of Near Eastern **neolithisation** associated with [[cauvin-jacques|Jacques Cauvin]]: a **révolution des symboles** — the emergence of new dominant religious symbols (woman/[[mother-goddess|Goddess]] and bull) among still-foraging Khiamian groups — is the **primary** transformation; cultivation and herding follow as a second step. Canonical statement: *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|The Birth of the Gods and the Origins of Agriculture]]* (2000).

## Core Claims

*(All attributed to Cauvin 2000 unless noted.)*

1. **Symbols before economy.** Around 9500 BC, Levantine hunter-gatherers of the [[khiamian|Khiamian]] promote new dominant symbols: the woman (Goddess) and the bull (often as bucrania). Subsistence is still hunting and gathering; the bull is economically insignificant as a staple.
2. **New religion / divinities.** Cauvin reads this as the birth of personalized divinities and a structured symbolic system that humanises art and installs a metaphysical gap between god and man — dynamic, releasing human initiative.
3. **Agriculture as second step.** Regular farming appears ~9000 BC among PPNA groups of the [[levantine-corridor|Levantine corridor]] (Sultanian, Aswadian, Mureybetian). Farming is enabled by the prior mental change, not vice versa.
4. **Rejection of classical materialist derivation.** Cannot derive the symbolic recasting from transformed material infrastructure; ecological and purely economic models miss the anthropological foundation of the “desire to change.”
5. **Diffusion of a package.** PPNB cultural unity (lithics, rectangular architecture, herding, cultivation, [[skull-cult|skull cult]]) expands from a northern Levantine origin through Anatolia, southern Levant, Mediterranean, Cyprus, and eastward — modalities of diffusion require behavioural explanation.
6. **Braidwood re-read.** “Culture was not ready” is reinterpreted as religious/mental readiness rather than techno-economic lag alone.

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against socio-economic and ecological primacy in Anglophone prehistory (oasis; hilly-flanks settling-in; demographic pressure as sole drivers). Drew on Leroi-Gourhan’s structuralist treatment of prehistoric symbolism and Annales interest in *mentalités* / *l’imaginaire*. Retained Childe’s [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] label while relocating its essence from food production to mind and religion.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[cauvin-jacques]] — *Naissance des divinités…* (1994/1997); English Watkins translation 2000 with Postscript.
- Field base: [[mureybet]]; dialogue with Mellaart’s [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] for later “explicit theology”; Hauptmann’s [[nevali-cori|Nevalı Çori]]; later readers pair with [[gobekli-tepe|Göbekli Tepe]].

## Empirical Program

- Stratigraphic/cultural sequences: Natufian → Khiamian → PPNA triad → PPNB phases.
- Iconography: female figurines, bucrania, later mural theology at Çatalhöyük as developed form.
- Settlement and subsistence: villages and first morphologically domestic cereals after symbolic shift.
- Expansion maps for Early/Middle/Late PPNB.

## Method Commitments

Culture-historical phasing + symbolic/contextual reading of art and ritual; calibrated radiocarbon narrative (as of Cauvin 2000); diffusion models requiring specified human behaviour. Not experimental cognition; not aDNA.

## Critiques and Responses

- **Goddess/Bull package:** figurines may not equal mother-goddess religion; Göbekli imagery often read as wild danger/male animals rather than fertility goddess (Mithen/Schmidt positions).
- **Causation:** correlation of early symbols with later farming does not prove mental cause of agriculture; alternative social (feasting, competition) and multi-causal models remain open — see [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]].
- **Chronology and coverage:** 1990s site sample; Postscript claims newer finds support the thesis — treat as author’s update, not independent verification.
- **Softened uptake:** many scholars accept **ideology-before-economy** as a researchable hypothesis while rejecting Cauvin’s full theological reconstruction.

## Postscript adjustments (as of Cauvin 1998/2000)

Cauvin states new fieldwork **confirms** basic ideas with detail corrections only: YD timing ~10,600 BC (weak causal role); Jerf el Ahmar architectural/pre-domestic barley diversity (slight Mureybetian subsistence adjustment); Göbekli Mureybetian industry; Halula cattle revising Cyprus chronology; Ganj Dareh not PPNA peer. Definitional: “Neolithic” = productive-economy **state**; *l’imaginaire* = image-forming mental models, not “fiction.”

## Current Status

**Contested / partially absorbed.** The phrase “ideology before economy” remains a standing pole in Near Eastern Neolithic debate; the detailed Goddess–Bull theology is widely treated as over-interpretation. Links to [[cognitive-archaeology]] programs (Watkins, Mithen) that keep mind and symbol central without Cauvin’s full system.

## Reflexivity

French Near Eastern prehistory tradition; CNRS institutional base (Jalès); English edition aimed as corrective to Anglophone socio-economic “fatalism” (Cauvin’s framing). Translation by a sympathetic specialist (Watkins) shapes Anglophone reception. Gendered reading of prehistoric religion participates in longer mother-goddess historiography (Gimbutas-adjacent debates) even when Cauvin’s case is regionally specific.
