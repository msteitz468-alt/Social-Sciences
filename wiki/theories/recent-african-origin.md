# Recent African Origin (Out of Africa / Replacement)

The research program arguing that **[[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern humans]]** evolved first in Africa and dispersed, **largely replacing** archaic humans (including [[neanderthals]]) elsewhere. Also labeled Out of Africa 2, Noah's Ark, Eve, or (in [[gamble-clive|Gamble]]'s *Timewalkers* rhetoric) "Gardeners." Principal mid-1990s book-length statement co-authored by [[stringer-chris|Stringer]] and [[gamble-clive|Gamble]]: *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993). Competes with [[multiregional-evolution]]. Live dispute: [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — Stringer & Gamble 1993 and allied literature as they present it. Pre-nuclear aDNA horizon throughout.)*

1. **African anatomical primacy.** Modern human anatomy appears first in Africa (consistent modern package from ~100 ka as of S&G 1993: shorter rounder skull, smaller brow/face, chin, lighter build); non-African moderns derive from African (or Africa-adjacent) dispersal. Africa alone can support a multiregional-style "Neanderthal phase" in modern ancestry; elsewhere the trail leads back to Africa as single continental centre for physical origins (S&G 1993).
2. **Out of Africa 2 vs Out of Africa 1.** *Homo erectus* left Africa ~1 Ma (Out of Africa 1). A second wave of anatomically modern *H. sapiens* disperses sometime between ~130 and 50 ka (Out of Africa 2), replacing Ancients including Neanderthals (S&G terminology 1993; program developed ~15 years before the book by Stringer, Andrews, and others).
3. **Neanderthals as non-ancestral Eurasian specializations.** Neanderthals went their own evolutionary way over ≥200 ka — away from Cro-Magnons in face, nose, and body proportions — and did **not** evolve into living Europeans; their role in Europeans-to-come was minimal (S&G 1993). Occasional morphological "hybrid" hints treated as exceptions; gene flow minimal (1993 position — **superseded horizon** for absolute zero contribution).
4. **Genetics support recent single origin (1993 horizon).** Cann–Stoneking–Wilson 1987 mtDNA: African "Eve"/lucky mother ~200 ka, greatest African diversity, dispersal ~100 ka. Nuclear DNA / Cavalli-Sforza: primary African/non-African split; ancestral African population ~100 ka. Global low diversity and within-region clocks used to argue living variation could arise within ~150 ka (as of S&G 1993). **Flag:** pre-nuclear aDNA; absolute calibrations contested even then; post-2010 aDNA revises "complete replacement" genetics.
5. **Anatomy ≠ behaviour.** Earliest African and Levantine moderns "looked like us… but they were not us." Modern behaviour (symbolically organized culture, art, ornament, structured "homes," global colonization after ~40 ka) is a completely different order of complexity; replacement is the only plausible explanation given absence of gradual change from Ancient to Modern ways of life (S&G 1993). Source of modern behaviour less certain than African anatomy (possible SE Asia or Middle East export scenarios left open).
6. **Mechanism: competition and demography, not genocide.** Population growth and economic competition for resources; gradual displacement to marginal environments ("whimper rather than a bang"); Zubrow simulation — ~2% higher Neanderthal mortality → extinction within ~1,000 years. Limited interbreeding conceivable but not required (S&G 1993).

**Horizon note:** Post-2010 nuclear aDNA demonstrates limited Neanderthal/Denisovan admixture. Treat 1993 "minimal/no genetic contribution" formulations as historical positions; current consensus is often framed as **mostly** out of Africa with small archaic contribution — do not promote either extreme in wiki voice without dated sources.

## Intellectual Origins

Reacts against multiregional/continuity models (Weidenreich lineage; Coon; Brace; Wolpoff–Thorne) and against treating Neanderthals as a universal "phase" of human evolution or as Boule–Vallois "presapiens" relicts coexisting with European modern ancestors. Builds on Howells "Noah's Ark" single-origin framing, African fossil moderns, Levant absolute dating (TL, ESR, U-series) that broke the assumed Neanderthal→modern succession, and molecular phylogeography of the 1980s–early 1990s. S&G note that when Out of Africa 2 took shape ~1980 from fossils/dating, living-population osteology and early blood-group genetics gave little support — mtDNA and nuclear trees later supplied independent genetic ballast (as of 1993 narrative).

## Key Proponents and Texts

[[stringer-chris]] · Peter Andrews · [[gamble-clive]] (archaeological half of the 1993 synthesis) · *In Search of the Neanderthals* (1993) · earlier Howells single-origin framing · mtDNA Eve papers (Cann, Stoneking, Wilson 1987 as of 1993 citations) · Cavalli-Sforza nuclear trees (as cited 1993) · later Klein "Out of Africa 2" behavioural formulation (see [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

## Empirical Program

*(As of Stringer & Gamble 1993 — date-stamp all findings.)*

- **African sequence:** Bodo/Broken Hill Ancients → Ngaloba, Jebel Irhoud, Florisbad transitional/"borderline Moderns" → Omo Kibish 1, Klasies River Mouth (120–70 ka), Border Cave (provenience contested); Dar-es-Soltan Aterian moderns as possible North African continuity hint Irhoud→Afalou/Taforalt (S&G still frame Africa as primary).
- **Levant chronology:** Skhul/Qafzeh early moderns ~100+ ka (TL/ESR) before or coeval with some Neanderthals (Tabun C ~110 ka; Amud/Kebara later); three models of overlap/succession/single variable population — S&G favour corridor overlap and African-linked moderns; Middle East as springboard for later global dispersal.
- **Europe:** Aurignacian Moderns vs late Mousterian/Châtelperronian Neanderthals 40–30 ka; Châtelperronian as imitation not invention; Vindija incomplete as intermediate; Cro-Magnon metrics not regionally continuous from Neanderthals.
- **Far East / Australasia:** Zhoukoudian Upper Cave ~25 ka more Cro-Magnon-like than ancient–modern Chinese intermediate; Ngandong late Javanese Ancients; Australia dual-origin (Thorne–Wolpoff) vs Brown *in situ* diversity — S&G lean against strong Java continuity.
- **Genetics (1993):** mtDNA Eve ~200 ka (range debated 400–60 ka); nuclear African primary split ~100 ka; diversity clocks ≤150 ka for living variation.
- **Behaviour:** structured living space, art/ornament/display after ~40 ka; global colonization pattern; Neanderthal "tool-assisted" vs Modern symbolically organized culture.

## Method Commitments

Cladistic/morphometric separation of Ancients vs Moderns; absolute dating (TL, ESR, U-series, radiocarbon) to reorder relative sequences that once assumed Neanderthal→modern succession; genetic coalescence (mtDNA maternal lineages; nuclear hierarchical clustering) as independent test; archaeological comparison at campsite, landscape, and colonization scales; rejection of Boule-style ape-like Neanderthal criteria while retaining separate-species (or separate-trajectory) conclusion "for entirely different reasons" — special nature of Neanderthal fossils, not simian characters (S&G 1993).

## Critiques and Responses

**Multiregional critiques (as of 1993, reported by S&G):** Regional continuity traits in East Asia and Australia; mtDNA rate non-constancy and PAUP indeterminacy; pattern could fit *erectus* spread 1 Ma; Wolpoff moral/scientific critique of replacement as "story of Cain" / "Killer Africans"; gene-flow models maintain species unity without allopatric speciation.

**S&G responses (1993):** African fossil and genetic primacy; Levant dates break simple local evolution; facial reduction under braincase explains modern faces better than two-stage Neanderthal intermediate path; "transitional" industries as imitation; colonization scale/timing too great for continuity; replacement ≠ genocide (demographic attrition); mtDNA controversially used for complete replacement while nuclear DNA less definitive on intermixture — already a hedge within the 1993 program.

**Later horizon (record, do not overwrite 1993):** Post-2010 aDNA supports limited archaic admixture without restoring equal multiregional contribution; "mostly out of Africa" assimilation hybrids absorb some multiregional points. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Current Status

Still active as the backbone of "mostly out of Africa" synthesis; strict total-replacement genetics of 1993 are obsolete. Status remains **contested** at the level of how much archaic contribution, when behavioural modernity crystallized, and how to weight regional fossil signals. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012|Chris Stringer, *Lone Survivors* (2012)]]. All claims attributed; date-stamp **(as of Stringer 2012)**. This layer densifies the classic RAO narrative and Stringer’s own post-aDNA reformulation; it does **not** overwrite the 1993 Stringer & Gamble layer above.*

### Classic RAO narrative (Ch.1, as of Stringer 2012)

Stringer restates the **classic RAO / Replacement** package he and allies developed from ~1984 and that became dominant by the millennium turn (attributed):

- African origin of *H. erectus* and *H. sapiens* (and perhaps *H. heidelbergensis*); *erectus* from a *habilis*-like form nearly 2 Ma; **Out of Africa 1** ~1.7 Ma.
- African *erectus* handaxes ~1.5 Ma; later *heidelbergensis* in southern Europe/Britain ~500–600 ka; *heidelbergensis* split ~300–400 ka into Eurasian Neanderthals vs African modern ancestors by ~130 ka.
- Modern origin recent and restricted (possibly rapid, e.g. East Africa); Middle East ~100 ka; Australia by ~60 ka; Europe only ~35 ka after African Later Stone Age/complex behaviors ~50 ka; Cro-Magnons replace Neanderthals via superior technology.
- Under pure RAO, regional (“racial”) variation develops only during/after African dispersal; apparent continuity outside Africa is parallel evolution or coincidence, not archaic gene transmission (attributed).

Stringer prefers the label **Recent African Origin (RAO)** over “Out of Africa” because earlier African dispersals (Out of Africa 1, 2, etc.) and “into Africa” events are known (attributed). He notes Ch.2 chronology updates Europe entry to ~45 ka vs the classic ~35 ka sketch (internal synthesis update, not a contradiction to force).

**Fossil-program variants:** Stringer and Günter Bräuer developed fossil-based recent African origin; Bräuer was less inclined to treat *H. sapiens* as a newly evolved species and more open to post-dispersal hybridization with Neanderthals — both welcomed 1987 mtDNA (attributed).

**Dental RAO support:** Christy Turner’s “Out of Asia” dental scenario was rejected by Stringer with Compton and Humphrey (plus Irish and Bailey): African origin remains most likely for living dental variation; [[skhul]]/[[qafzeh]] early moderns show “African” dental traits (as of Stringer 2012).

### Out of Africa 1 challenged; Out of Africa 2 reevaluated

[[dmanisi]] (~1.75 Ma, small-brained skulls, basic tools) challenges the view that the first exit required larger brains or better tools (as of Stringer 2012). Combined with [[homo-floresiensis]], some (Dennell, Roebroeks and others, via Stringer) propose a pre-*erectus* Eurasian phase ~2 Ma with later reentry to Africa — orthodoxy of Out of Africa 1 under pressure (attributed positions). African origin of *H. sapiens* remains mainstream; reevaluation of Out of Africa 2 proceeds via [[herto]], Oase (possible hybridization), Chinese early *sapiens* (e.g. Tianyuan feet/shoes), and expanded Neanderthal genetics (as of Stringer 2012).

### Serial founder effect and living diversity

Hundreds of autosomal studies show **greatest diversity in Africa**; non-Africans are essentially a subset of that variation (as of Stringer 2012). DNA diversity declines steadily with overland distance from Africa (lowest in Arctic Europe, Americas, Polynesia, Australasia) — a **serial founder** signature also mirrored in *Helicobacter pylori* and in cranial diversity (most forensic regional cranial differences drift-generated; exceptions include cold-adapted Buryat/Eskimo head-face form) (attributed findings). Steady diversity decline **challenges pan-assimilation** expectations of repeated diversity reversals where archaics mixed in — with **Melanesia** the important exception (hotspot → Denisovan input) (as of Stringer 2012). Effective ancestral size for moderns maybe ~10,000 breeding individuals (female *N*e from mtDNA sometimes <5,000); Huff, Jorde et al. ~20,000 at *erectus* time still too small for continent-wide continuous spread (attributed).

### “Mostly Out of Africa” — continuum with Assimilation

Stringer (as of 2012) places his updated stance near Bräuer’s Out of Africa + Hybridization or a **modest** [[assimilation-model|Assimilation]] model: if archaic assimilation remains modest (<~10% of the genome), **“mostly Out of Africa”** is still RAO. He maps a **continuum**: pure RAO (total non-African replacement) → Bräuer hybridization → Smith–Trinkaus Assimilation (center; up to ~50% non-African if archaic gene flow is the rule) → classic Multiregionalism (Africa no special role). Pure RAO was reinforced by fossils, archaeology, mtDNA/Y-DNA, and early Neanderthal mtDNA; autosomal Neanderthal/Denisovan genomes **halted and partly reversed** the pure-replacement pendulum (as of Stringer 2012).

RAO, he argues, is not only African morphology and most genes but also shared patterns of modern **behavior**. **“Modernity” was a composite** assembled at different times and places, not a single package unique to one African origin event (attributed). He revises single-origin gradualism in Africa: dating and variation imply talking about **origins (plural)**, with fission–fusion, possible multi-species coexistence, and partial absorption of late archaics; Africa’s size, tropical position, and larger continuous populations explain modern success better than a unique special pathway (as of Stringer 2012). He acknowledges that 1980s RAO advocacy sometimes oversimplified multiregional views amid polarization, and that he once favored a rapid single-area African “Garden of Eden” (attributed reflexivity).

**Body-louse proxy:** Stoneking; Toups et al. body-louse mtDNA origin ~80–170 ka as proxy for regular clothing/bedding in Africa before diaspora (as of Stringer 2012).

### McBrearty & Brooks link to RAO

McBrearty and Brooks (via Stringer 2012) link behavioral and morphological species origin to **early** MSA developments, not late MSA/LSA changes near the exodus — cautioning against implying Africa left behind after the “necessary” exit innovations.

## Reflexivity

European Neanderthal debate is entangled with identity and "who counts as ancestor"; Stringer & Gamble 1993 preface flags that scientific claims about Neanderthals never fully escape contemporary opinions about race, heritage, and human unity (attributed). Closing manifesto: difference without Boule/Wells moral condemnation; Neanderthals "as human as us, but… a different brand of humanity"; investigate the "curtain" between Ancients and Moderns rather than rank winners and losers (attributed). Continuity vs replacement tracks older monogenism/polygenism afterlives — date-stamp and attribute. *Lone Survivors* (2012) adds Stringer’s own admission of polarizing oversimplification in the 1980s struggle and his move from pure Garden-of-Eden rapidism to “mostly Out of Africa” with plural African origins (attributed).
