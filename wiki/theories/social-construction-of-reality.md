# Social Construction of Reality

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/social-construction-of-reality|Theory Hub — Social Construction of Reality]]

## Core Claims

All claims below are **attributed** to [[berger-peter-l|Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]], *[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|The Social Construction of Reality]]* (1966).

1. **Title thesis.** Reality is socially constructed; the [[sociology-of-knowledge]] must analyse the process in which this occurs. “Reality” = quality of phenomena recognized as having being independent of our volition; “knowledge” = certainty that phenomena are real and have specific characteristics — sociological, not ultimate ontological, definitions.
2. **Object of knowledge.** The discipline must concern itself with **whatever passes for knowledge** in society, especially **common-sense knowledge of everyday life**, not only intellectual ideologies (redefinition vs Scheler/Mannheim).
3. **Dialectic.** Society is a human product; society is an objective reality; man is a social product — [[externalization-objectivation-internalization|externalization, objectivation, internalization]].
4. **Objective reality.** From biological world-openness, humans produce social order via [[habitualization]] and [[institutionalization]] (reciprocal [[typification]] of habitualized actions by types of actors); [[legitimation]] and [[symbolic-universe|symbolic universes]] maintain the order; [[reification]] is extreme objectivation that forgets human authorship.
5. **Subjective reality.** [[primary-socialization|Primary]] and [[secondary-socialization|secondary socialization]] induct individuals; conversation and [[plausibility-structure|plausibility structures]] maintain subjective reality; alternation/conversion requires new social bases.
6. **Central sociological question** (their synthesis of Weber and Durkheim): how subjective meanings become objective facticities — how *Handeln* produces a world of *choses*.

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against a narrow sociology of knowledge focused on ideology, intelligentsia, and false consciousness — the classical program whose English charter is [[mannheim-karl|Mannheim]]’s *[[mannheim-ideology-and-utopia-1936|Ideology and Utopia]]* (now primary-ingested; see [[sociology-of-knowledge]] and [[hubs/theory/sociology-of-knowledge]]). Built on Marx (activity/world; ideology), Durkheim (objective facticity), Weber (subjective meaning), [[mead-george-herbert|Mead]] / [[symbolic-interactionism]], philosophical anthropology (world-openness), and above all [[schutz-alfred|Alfred Schutz]] (phenomenology of the life-world).

## Empirical Program

The 1966 book is theoretical. Sketch pay-offs: religion, identity, institutions, distribution of knowledge, deviance/therapy, pluralistic competition of universes. Later constructionist, interpretive, and qualitative research absorbed the vocabulary unevenly.

| Claim area | Supporting style of work | Challenging concerns | Standing in collection |
|---|---|---|---|
| Everyday knowledge as sociological object | Phenomenology; later ethnomethodology (not yet ingested) | Narrow knowledge/ideology specialists | Programmatic |
| Institution from habitualization | Historical/qualitative institutional studies | Power-first / conflict theories of institutions | Programmatic |
| Plausibility structures | Sociology of religion; conversion studies | Materialist accounts of belief | Programmatic |

## Method Commitments

- Part One: phenomenological description of everyday life (brackets on causal/genetic/ontological claims).
- Parts Two–Three: genetic sociology of objective and subjective reality.
- Names-in-Notes policy: main text as continuous theoretical argument; genealogy in apparatus.
- Compatible with interpretive and qualitative traditions; not a survey methodology.

## Critiques and Responses

- **Relativism:** Sociologist “stuck with quotation marks” — cannot adjudicate ultimate validity; studies what *passes for* real. Critics see soft relativism; B&L insist on facticity of the constructed world.
- **Power/materialism:** Micro genesis (A–B) and conversation may underplay coercion, class, and state; universe-maintenance section partly answers via monopoly, ideology, police of definitions.
- **Relation to Durkheimian [[social-realism]]:** Shares facticity of institutions; rejects treating that facticity as non-human or non-produced (anti-[[reification]]).
- **Relation to [[positivism]]:** Not Comtean encyclopedism or pure external measurement first; starts from meanings and common sense.

## Current Status

**Active** as absorbed vocabulary across sociology (institution, legitimation, socialization types, plausibility, social construction) even where the full dialectic is not used. Constructionism diversified (STS, gender, race, science studies) beyond B&L’s 1966 frame. Primary STS extension now held: Latour & Woolgar *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* applies construction to **natural-scientific facts** via [[laboratory-studies]] (distinct object from everyday [[reality-of-everyday-life]]); 1986 Postscript drops "social" from the subtitle as residual category.

## Reflexivity

Postwar American–European synthesis (New School / Frankfurt orbits; Alps 1962 project origin). Émigré phenomenological sociology after Schutz. Main text’s anonymity of sources is a rhetorical politics of theory-building. Production context: contest with structural-functional orthodoxy and with narrow Wissenssoziologie — not yet a full Critical-Turn conflict theory.
