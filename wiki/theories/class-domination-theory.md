# Class Domination Theory

Domhoff’s name (in the WRA tradition) for the claim that the United States is characterized by **class domination** by the corporate rich organized through a [[corporate-community|corporate community]], social upper class, and [[policy-planning-network|policy-planning network]] — the empirical program of *[[domhoff-who-rules-america-2022|Who Rules America?]]*. See also [[domhoff-who-rules-america-study]] and [[power-elite-vs-pluralism-debate]].

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — Domhoff, WRA 8th ed. 2022 unless noted.)*

1. **Corporate community exists** as a nationwide network of large corporations and financial institutions (e.g. Domhoff reports ~87.5% of Fortune 500 interlocked in 2010–11 analyses he synthesizes).
2. **Upper class** (~top 0.5% in his framing) is socially organized (schools, clubs, Social Register-type indicators) and **intertwined** with the corporate community: stock ownership, family offices/holding companies, and assimilation of managers.
3. **Policy-planning network** (foundations, think tanks, policy-discussion groups such as CFR, CED, Business Roundtable, plus ultraconservative obstruction networks like Heritage/AEI/Federalist Society) is financed and directed from that base.
4. **Power elite (Domhoff)** = institutionalized leadership group at the **intersection** of social upper class, corporate community, and policy-planning network — integrating class and organizational insight. **Not** identical to Mills’s corporate–military–political triad; Domhoff’s US picture is **economic-network ascendancy** over military and (relative to Europe) over state/church.
5. **Domination processes:** special-interest, policy-planning (ranked even more important for general domination), opinion-shaping, and electoral/candidate processes — open and studyable, not conspiracy.
6. **Indicators:** who benefits, who governs, who wins (see [[power-indicators]]); Domhoff claims the power elite scores high on all three.
7. **Paradox:** individual rights can expand while class domination increases (he argues 1970–2020).

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against pluralist political science and “no one governs” images; developed from Mills/Hunter power-structure concerns with stronger **class** and **network** operationalization and continuous empirical updating across editions.

## Empirical Program

Board interlocks; biographical upper-class indicators; foundation and think-tank funding; cabinet/appointee origins; legislative/issue outcomes; electoral system structure. Strength: multi-indicator, multi-decade updating. Limits: US-centric; network boundary judgments; 2020s party labels are analytic periodization.

## Method Commitments

Power-structure research: organizational and network evidence over pure reputational community-power; explicit [[power-indicators]]; anti-conspiracy stance.

## Critiques and Responses

See [[power-elite-vs-pluralism-debate]]. Domhoff’s response pattern: demand indicator evidence; distinguish middle levels from the top; update for party realignment without abandoning class domination.

## Current Status

Active in political sociology and critical power research; standard foil/partner to pluralist and state-autonomy programs in US politics teaching.

## Reflexivity

Longitudinal single-author research program; editions track political economy (New Deal coalition → civil rights realignment → 2020s). Production context is US academy and progressive critique of corporate power — claims remain attributed empirical-theoretical synthesis.
