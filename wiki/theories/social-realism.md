# Social realism (Durkheimian)

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/social-realism|Theory Hub — Social realism]]

## Core Claims

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]]’s research program treats **society as a reality *sui generis*** — a level of phenomena produced by **association**, irreducible to individual minds, external to and constraining of individuals (all claims attributed). Methodological codification in *[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|The Rules of Sociological Method]]* (1895; 2nd ed. 1901):

- **[[social-fact|Social facts]] as things** — studied from outside through external characteristics and solid indices ([[treat-social-facts-as-things]]); discard Baconian prenotions / *idola*.
- **Social explanation of social facts** — “The determining cause of a social fact must be sought among antecedent social facts and not among the states of the individual consciousness” (*Rules* Ch. V). Principal causal locus: constitution of the **inner social environment** ([[social-morphology]], volume and [[dynamic-density]]).
- **Function vs. cause** — organ independent of function; efficient cause and function investigated separately; function sought in relation to a **social end**.
- **[[normal-and-pathological|Normal vs. pathological]]** — relative to a [[social-types|social type]] and phase; average type = healthy type; crime is normal (presence), though intensity may be pathological.
- **Proof** — comparative method as sociology’s indirect experiment; **concomitant variations** as the chief procedure (*Rules* Ch. VI).

In the 1901 Preface and later letters he insists social life is made of **[[collective-representations|representations]]** and that collective psychology is a psychology *sui generis* — not a substantial group soul, not individual psychology enlarged.

## Intellectual Origins

Extends [[comte-auguste|Comte]]’s demand for a positive science of society ([[positivism]]) while **rejecting** encyclopedic system-building, Comte’s single-Humanity teleology, and much philosophy of history. Durkheim later told Deploige that what others called his “social realism” came from **Comte, Spencer, and Espinas** (not Schaeffle), and that the sociology/psychology distinction owed first to **Boutroux** (“every science must explain its own principles”) plus Comtean level-irreducibility (letters, Lukes volume — attributed self-report).

Reacted against: utilitarian/contractualist foundations ([[spencer-herbert|Spencer]], classical political economy; [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]); psychological/individualist reduction and imitation theory (Tarde); pure historical nominalism; teleological “explanation by purpose.”

1895 “watershed”: Durkheim reported that the lecture course of that year — under Robertson Smith’s influence — revealed religion’s capital role and forced restart of prior research (Deploige reply).

## Empirical Program

- Comparative-historical coding of legal codes ([[repressive-law]] / [[restitutive-law]]).
- Social-morphological argument from volume/density to specialization.
- Flagship moral-statistical demonstration: *Suicide* ([[durkheim-suicide-study]]).
- Classification / early sociology of knowledge: *Primitive Classification* with Mauss ([[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]]) — social origin of the classificatory function; [[primitive-classification]]; [[sociocentrism]].
- Religious / cognitive deepening: *Elementary Forms* ([[durkheim-elementary-forms-study]]) — totemism as elementary religion; [[collective-effervescence]]; categories of the understanding as collective representations.
- Subsequent writings defend comparison against historical scepticism (Seignobos debate) and reject economic monocausality while accepting “causes that escape consciousness” (Labriola/Marxism review).

## Critiques and Responses

| Critique (position) | Durkheim’s / school response (attributed) |
|---|---|
| Reification / ontology | 1901: consciousness not substantial; social life = representations; *sui generis* synthesis |
| Eliminates mind | Social facts are mental *sui generis*; all sociology is a psychology of a new kind |
| Constraint too harsh | Constraint is an *indicator* of exteriority; often not felt when conformity is willing |
| Underplays agency/meaning | Rival programs: [[interpretive-sociology]], individualism ([[methodological-individualism-debate]]) |
| Evolutionary type ranking | Later anthropology abandons ladder; morphology tools partly survive |

## Current Status

`status: active` as ancestor program: vocabulary (social fact, function, anomie, solidarity, morphology) and the rule “explain the social by the social” remain foundational even where strong *sui generis* ontology is qualified. Structural-functionalism absorbed function/cause and normal/pathological distinctions; multilevel and emergence languages restate the non-reduction claim.

## Reflexivity

Forged in the Third Republic’s project of a secular, solidaristic civic morality — science of moral facts oriented to diagnosis and reform, not pure value-neutrality. Comparative base drew on 19th-c. ethnology now partly discredited; “lower societies” language is period apparatus. The program’s autonomy claim for sociology is also a **professionalization politics** (Bordeaux/Sorbonne, *Année sociologique*).
