# Configurationism

Research program within American [[historical-particularism|Boasian]] [[anthropology]], associated above all with [[benedict-ruth|Ruth Benedict]]'s *[[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Patterns of Culture]]* (1934). Its organizing claim is that a whole culture is patterned like a personality — an integration of institutions around a dominant psychological orientation selected from the range of human possibility. All core claims below are stated in **attributed voice**: they are Benedict's theoretical program, not wiki-voice findings. Deep treatment at [[hubs/theory/configurationism|the Configurationism theory hub]].

## Core Claims

Attributed to [[benedict-ruth|Benedict]]:

1. **Cultures are integrated wholes.** "A culture, like an individual, is a more or less consistent pattern of thought and action"; heterogeneous items of behaviour are drawn into "congruous shape" by characteristic purposes. Cultures are "more than the sum of their traits."
2. **Selection from the great arc.** Culture-building is like language: from the vast "great arc" of possible human motivations, each culture selects and elaborates a few segments, as each language selects a few of the sounds the vocal tract can make ([[the-great-arc-of-culture]]). What one culture elaborates, another ignores.
3. **The configuration gives meaning.** A dominant "psychological set" ([[cultural-configuration]]) organizes ritual, kinship, economy, and emotion alike; the same institution (e.g. the Kula, a fertility rite, a mourning custom) carries different meaning inside different configurations.
4. **Incommensurability and relativism.** Because cultures pursue different ends "they are travelling along different roads… essentially they are incommensurable," which underwrites Benedict's [[cultural-relativism]] and her [[cultural-relativity-of-normality|relativity of the normal]].
5. **Not universal, not biological.** Benedict insisted configurations are "local and do not correlate with known relationships of the various groups" — not racially or linguistically transmitted (the Hopi share the Apollonian Pueblo pattern though linguistically Shoshonean; temperament distribution is roughly constant and culture selects from it).

## Intellectual Origins

Developed from Boasian [[historical-particularism]] and [[cultural-relativism]], against the atomistic **trait-and-element analysis** of classical/armchair anthropology. Benedict explicitly credited a wider holist movement: Wilhelm Stern's "configuration" psychology, Wilhelm Worringer's contrast of non-gradable "artistic intentions," **Gestalt psychology** ("the whole determines its parts… their very nature"), Wilhelm Dilthey's relativity of *Weltanschauungen*, and Oswald Spengler's contrasting civilizational "destiny-ideas" (his Apollonian/Faustian pair) — while rejecting Spengler's organicist biological life-cycle and his treatment of stratified modern society as a homogeneous "folk culture." She credited [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]]'s functionalism for restoring living context to traits, but faulted him for generalizing Trobriand patterns onto "the primitive world": "Anthropologists are turning from the study of primitive culture to that of primitive cultures."

## Key Proponents and Texts

[[benedict-ruth|Ruth Benedict]], *[[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Patterns of Culture]]* (1934), building on her papers "Psychological Types in the Cultures of the Southwest" (1928) and "Configurations of Culture in North America" (1932). The program is the anthropological wing of the wider [[culture-and-personality]] movement ([[mead-margaret|Mead]], Kardiner, Linton). [[sapir-edward|Sapir]]'s work on culture as patterned is a cognate influence.

## Empirical Program

Three configurational cases, each built on one prior fieldworker's corpus:

- **Apollonian [[zuni|Zuni]]** — moderation, ceremonialism, distrust of individualism and excess (Benedict's own Southwest fieldwork, with Bunzel and Cushing).
- **"Paranoid" [[dobu|Dobu]]** — institutionalized treachery, sorcery, and suspicion ([[fortune-reo|Fortune]]'s *Sorcerers of Dobu*).
- **Dionysian / "megalomaniac" [[kwakiutl|Kwakiutl]]** — self-glorification and rivalry, epitomized by the [[potlatch]] ([[boas-franz|Boas]]'s Northwest Coast corpus).

Benedict noted (a point in the program's favour, she argued) that none of the three was fieldworked with the configurational hypothesis in mind — the reading was applied afterward.

## Method Commitments

Holistic, [[comparative-method|comparative]], interpretive characterization of a culture's dominant orientation, requiring **both** psychology (a behaviour is a standing human potentiality) **and** history/diffusion (why it is selected here and suppressed there): "one cannot make the one do the service of the other." Benedict credited [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]] with reversing the naive causal order — understand vengeance *from* the blood-feud institution, not the institution from an assumed drive.

### Relation to Kroeber pattern theory

[[kroeber-alfred-l|Kroeber]] (*[[kroeber-culture-patterns-processes-1963|Culture Patterns]]*, Ch. 8) treats total-culture "physiognomy" and multi-strand characterization as available for cultures generally; he cites Benedict's Apollonian/Dionysian and related sketches as illustrations while preferring multi-term descriptions and multi-level [[culture-pattern|pattern]] morphology (universal / systemic / total / style) over a single psychological label. Dialogue, not identity, with [[configurationism]].

## Critiques and Responses

- **Over-integration.** The program's central vulnerability: it reads internally diverse, conflicted societies as governed by a single psychological key, and treats non-conforming material (Zuni's two licensed-license occasions; the Dionysian content of the taken scalp) as contained exceptions rather than counter-evidence. Benedict herself warned against cutting "every culture down to the Procrustean bed of some catchword characterization" and conceded some cultures are un-integrated ("scattered"), yet the three showcase cases are strongly typed.
- **Psychiatric labels on whole peoples.** Casting Dobu as "paranoid" and Kwakiutl as "megalomaniac paranoid" imports clinical vocabulary onto cultures; later critics read this as caricature.
- **Empirical contestation of the type.** Benedict's Apollonian Zuni was later challenged (e.g. Li An-che 1937) as understating Zuni anxiety, conflict, and coercion — an attributed later-scholarship dispute recorded on the [[zuni|Zuni]] and [[benedict-patterns-of-culture-study|study]] pages.

## Current Status

Status **absorbed**. The whole-culture personality typology is largely rejected, but the holist insight — that cultures are patterned wholes, that traits mean what the configuration makes them mean — persists in the concept of ethos, in symbolic and interpretive anthropology, and in the broader vocabulary of "a culture." See [[hubs/theory/configurationism#what-survived|What Survived]].

## Reflexivity

*Patterns of Culture* was a mass-market book (translated into fourteen languages; hundreds of thousands of copies) that carried [[cultural-relativism]] and the anthropological [[culture]] concept into public discourse. Benedict wrote in 1934 amid rising European nationalism; her closing plea for tolerance of the deviant and of "coexisting and equally valid patterns" is legible as an anti-racist, anti-nationalist intervention as much as a scientific conclusion — part of the program's history of knowledge, and part of its later vulnerability to culture-war re-litigation.
