# Cultural Transmission Theory

Research program associated with [[cavalli-sforza-luigi-luca|Cavalli-Sforza]] and
[[feldman-marcus|Marcus Feldman]] formalizing how **[[cultural-transmission]]**
works as an evolutionary process parallel to genetic inheritance. Popular
restatement: Cavalli-Sforza, *Genes, Peoples and Languages* (2000), chapter 6.
Related (not identical) programs: Boyd & Richerson dual inheritance; Durham
coevolution — link when those sources are ingested; do not silently merge.

Deep analysis (when built): [[hubs/theory/cultural-transmission-theory]].

## Core Claims

(All attributed to Cavalli-Sforza & Feldman / Cavalli-Sforza 2000 — not wiki voice.)

1. **Culture as cumulative transmitted information** — customs and technologies
   learned from others, allowing knowledge to pile up beyond one lifetime.
2. **Multiple transmission modes** — vertical (parent–child), horizontal (peers),
   oblique (teachers/elders); mode mix predicts conservatism vs rapid spread.
3. **Analogy with limits** — cultural "mutation," selection, and drift metaphors
   are useful but cultural transmission is faster and multi-parental relative to genes.
4. **Gene–culture interaction** — technological and demographic cultural change
   redirects genetic evolution (expansions, density, disease environments).
5. **Language as worked example** — linguistic evolution illustrates cultural
   evolutionary dynamics and couples to genetic geography when demic spreads
   carry both genes and languages ([[demic-diffusion]]).

## Intellectual Origins

Reaction against (a) pure genetic determinism of behavior and (b) anthropological
reluctance to model culture quantitatively; stimulated by Cavalli-Sforza's African
Pygmy fieldwork observing teaching and skill transmission (as of Cavalli-Sforza 2000).

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[cavalli-sforza-luigi-luca]], [[feldman-marcus]] — 1981 monograph; 2000 ch. 6
- Field collaborators (e.g. Barry Hewlett on Aka transmission — cited 1986)

## Empirical Program

Aka Pygmy cultural-transmission inventories; African cultural-variation models
(Guglielmino et al. 1995, cited in Cavalli-Sforza 2000); gene–language tree
comparisons (chs. 4–5 context).

## Method Commitments

Population-genetic style recursion equations; ethnographic/survey measurement of
who teaches whom; comparative linguistics as cultural phylogeny (contested).

## Critiques and Responses

- **Meaning and power:** sociocultural anthropology argues packets-and-rates miss
  ideology, inequality, and interpretive depth.
- **Linguistic macro-phylogenies:** many historical linguists reject deep families
  used in gene–language plots (Greenberg, Nostratic, etc.).
- **aDNA era:** demic vs cultural diffusion tests improved with ancient DNA;
  cultural-transmission *formalism* still used in cultural evolution subfield.

## Current Status

Active in cultural evolutionary studies and gene–culture coevolution; vocabulary
partly absorbed into dual-inheritance teaching; not the dominant paradigm in
mainstream sociocultural anthropology.

## Reflexivity

Built by population geneticists extending their toolkit to culture — strengths
in formal clarity, risks of genetic metaphor overreach and of underplaying
colonial/mission contexts of "traditional society" case studies.
