# Cooking Hypothesis

*(Attributed — [[wrangham-richard|Wrangham]] 2009.)* Research program holding that **cooking and the control of fire**, not meat-eating alone, made humans biologically and socially what they are. Humans are "the cooking apes": adapted to cooked food as cows are to grass. Related concepts: [[obligate-cooked-diet]], [[energy-theory-of-cooking]], [[expensive-tissue-hypothesis]], [[cooking-and-pair-bonding]], [[control-of-fire]]. Standing dispute: [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]].

## Core Claims

*(All attributed — Wrangham, *Catching Fire*.)*

1. **Transformative origin of *Homo*.** The control of fire and cooked meals gave rise to the genus *Homo* (more precisely, for Wrangham, to *Homo erectus* from habilines) — one of the great transitions in the history of life.
2. **Multi-domain effects.** Cooking increased food value and changed bodies, brains, use of time, and social lives; humans became consumers of external energy, dependent on fuel.
3. **Two-step path.** Meat-eating (with stone tools from ~2.6 Ma and habilines ~2.3 Ma) explains the first step; cooking explains the second (~1.9–1.8 Ma): *Homo erectus* small jaws/teeth, reduced gut, near-modern body, and further brain growth.
4. **Obligate cooked diet.** Humans cannot thrive long-term on raw wild foods under natural conditions; cooking is necessity, not luxury.
5. **Energy mechanism.** The primary importance of cooking is net energy gain (gelatinization, denaturation, softening / lower digestive cost) — not only safety, taste, or symbolism.
6. **Social package.** Soft cooked food frees ~four hours of chewing per day, makes a hunting–gathering [[sexual-division-of-labor|sexual division of labor]] workable, and ties pair-bonds/[[marriage]] to camp meals and food protection.

## Intellectual Origins

Wrangham positions the program against:
- **[[hunting-hypothesis|Man-the-Hunter]]** (popular since the 1950s; Washburn & Lancaster 1968) — meat as single impetus; incomplete because it ignores gathered-food economies, the two-step fossil sequence, and *erectus* dental reduction.
- **Darwinian late-fire assumption** — fire as greatest discovery after language but irrelevant to *how* we evolved (controllers already human).
- **Lévi-Strauss / Leach** — cooking as symbolic nature/culture boundary without biological necessity.
- **Prior cooking advocates** (Coon, Brace, Perlès, Goudsblom, Symons, Fernández-Armesto) — valued cooking but lacked a nutritional mechanism.

Positive anchors: Brillat-Savarin's cooking→hunting→household sketch (1825); [[expensive-tissue-hypothesis|Aiello & Wheeler (1995)]] gut–brain trade-off, which Wrangham revises by assigning cooking to the *erectus* step.

## Empirical Program

| Domain | Evidence Wrangham mobilizes (as he reports it) |
|---|---|
| Anatomy | Small mouth, jaws, molars, stomach, colon vs primate expectations; MYH16 ~2.5 Ma; australopithecine large guts vs human reduction |
| Nutrition | Ileal starch/protein digestibility cooked vs raw; Secor grinding/cooking cost cuts (~12% each); Oka soft-pellet rats |
| Raw-foodist / survival | Giessen raw-food study (BMI, amenorrhea); Evo Diet; no long-term raw wild survival cases |
| Archaeology | Gesher Benot Ya’aqov ~790 ka; Beeches Pit / Schöningen ~400 ka; late secure hearths; Lower Paleolithic hints disputed |
| Time budgets | Ape chewing 5–6+ hrs/day vs human ~5%; Hadza/forager hunting hours vs Ngogo chimps |
| Cross-cultural | Murdock & Provost: women cook in 97.8% of societies; forager calorie shares by sex |

## Method Commitments

- **Biological dating of cooking:** archaeology of fire is too biased/faint to fix an origin; anatomical adaptation to cooked diet marks when cooking became permanent.
- **Comparative primatology + forager ethnography** as analogical evidence for time budgets and social structure (ethnography is secondary).
- **Energy as the master variable** linking diet quality, expensive tissue, and social time.

## Critiques and Responses

See [[cooking-hypothesis-debate]]. Main fault lines Wrangham himself steel-mans: late secure fire (Upper Paleolithic or ~200 ka Brace position); natural-fire dismissal of early sites; preference for hunting as master adaptation. Social/gender claims (cooking as patriarchy-enabling "protection racket") are especially contested and must stay attributed.

## Current Status

`contested`. Influential in popular and some biological-anthropology discussion of diet and human evolution; archaeological consensus on early fire remains unsettled; the social package is a minority evolutionary narrative beside cooperative-breeding and other models. Theory Hub **deferred** (single popular synthesis).

## Reflexivity

A trade book by a male primatologist advancing a program in which women's domestic cooking is both evolutionary cornerstone and "trapped" subservience — the gender politics of the thesis are content, not incidental. Ethnographic cases are cherry-picked secondaries supporting a grand narrative; date-stamp and attribute. Collection context: first bio-anth program text in a vault stronger on cultural theory and forager studies ([[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968]]) than on paleoanthropology.
