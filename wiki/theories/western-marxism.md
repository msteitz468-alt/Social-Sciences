# Western Marxism

## Core Claims

All in **attributed** voice. “Western Marxism” is a **retrospective school label** (popularised mid-20th century) for a family of Hegelian-cultural, anti-positivist Marxisms that developed partly outside and against Second International and Stalinist orthodoxy. Two charter primaries are now ingested: [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]], *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]* (1923), and [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]], *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971|Selections from the Prison Notebooks]]* (1971 Eng.; *Quaderni* 1929–35).

1. **Method over results (Lukács).** Orthodox Marxism “refers exclusively to method”; even total empirical refutation of Marx’s individual theses need not end orthodoxy. Removing the dialectic yields opportunist evolutionism (Bernstein).
2. **[[concrete-totality|Totality]] as revolutionary principle.** The decisive difference from bourgeois science is the point of view of totality; facts are moments of historical process, not natural-science “pure” data.
3. **[[reification]] and the commodity form.** Universal commodity structure remoulds objective forms and subjective stances of bourgeois society; [[commodity-fetishism]] is the basic phenomenon.
4. **[[class-consciousness]] / [[imputed-class-consciousness]].** Class consciousness is imputed to production positions, not polled from individuals; proletarian consciousness uniquely can grasp the social whole and fuse theory with practice.
5. **[[hegemony]] and [[civil-society]] (Gramsci).** Ruling-class power organises spontaneous consent through private organisms; State = political society + civil society = hegemony protected by the armour of coercion; Western strategy as [[war-of-position]]; [[organic-intellectuals]]; [[passive-revolution]]; [[philosophy-of-praxis]] as absolute historicism against economism and Manual sociology.
6. **Culture, ideology, later school.** Frankfurt [[critical-theory|Critical Theory]] radicalises reification into culture industry and instrumental reason. These are **developments**, not identical to 1923 Lukács or 1929–35 Gramsci.

## Intellectual Origins

- Defeat of Central European revolutions (Hungary 1919, Germany 1918–23), Comintern formation, and crisis of Second International Marxism (Kautsky, Bernstein).
- Recovery of Hegel for Marx (against “Hegel as dead dog” and against scientistic Social Democracy).
- Reacted against: economism, contemplative bourgeois thought, natural-science model of social knowledge, (for Lukács) insufficient subject–object dialectic in Engels’s popularisations → [[lukacs-dialectics-of-nature-debate]].

## Empirical Program

Primarily philosophical-political and cultural analysis rather than survey or excavation. Empirical contact points: political crisis of 1917–23; later Frankfurt empirical projects; cultural-studies operationalisations of hegemony — each must be attributed to its authors.

## Method Commitments

Dialectical totality; historicisation of “facts”; unity of theory and practice; critique of reified specialisms; (Lukács 1923) proletarian standpoint as solution to antinomies of bourgeois thought — **later partly retracted by Lukács himself**.

## Critiques and Responses

- **Comintern / DIAMAT:** idealism; denial or demotion of dialectics of nature; subjectivism (Rudas, Deborin, Zinoviev).
- **Structural Marxism ([[structural-marxism|Althusserian]]):** Western Marxism as historicist/humanist deviation.
- **Authorial self-criticism (Lukács 1967):** identical subject–object; alienation=objectification; missing labour; totality over economics.
- **Feminist / postcolonial / race critiques:** class-subject and European revolutionary conjuncture as unmarked universals — later debates, not settled in *HCC*.

## Current Status

Active as a historiographical and curricular category; many of its concepts (reification, hegemony, culture industry) circulate beyond Marxist parties. Status of the 1923 identical subject–object thesis is largely **abandoned** even by admirers; reification and totality remain live critical tools.

## Reflexivity

The label “Western” encodes a Cold War and geographic frame (Western Europe vs. Soviet Marxism) that can erase non-Western Marxisms and internal Soviet debates. *HCC* itself was written by a Hungarian Communist in Vienna exile — “Western” is retrospective packaging. Collection coverage: strong on Lukács 1923 charter; Gramsci partial; Althusserian **counter-tradition** now primary via *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]* (structural Marxism is not the same school as Western Marxism — they compete on humanism, historicism, and Hegel).
