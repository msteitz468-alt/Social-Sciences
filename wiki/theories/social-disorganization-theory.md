# Social Disorganization Theory

## Core Claims

(Attributed; *Polish Peasant* program + *[[park-burgess-city-1925|The City]]*.)

1. Primary-group and local-community controls (family, neighborhood, folkways) are undermined by urban growth, industrial division of labor, transport/communication, and migration.
2. Thomas's **individualization** = Park's **social disorganization** (two sides of one process).
3. **[[juvenile-delinquency|Delinquency]]** measures failure of community organization under rational (not traditional) control schemes (Park).
4. Ecological concentration: zone of deterioration / high-mobility areas show clustered pathology rates (Burgess; McKenzie on transitional vice locations).
5. Reform implication: treat delinquency as **group/environment** problem — find group and vocation realizing fundamental [[four-wishes|wishes]] — not only individual moral defect (Park).
6. Immigrant mutual-aid "control organizations" can buffer shock of new environment (Park Ch VI).

## Intellectual Origins

Thomas & Znaniecki's disorganization/reorganization narrative; Park's primary→secondary contact thesis; Burgess's metabolic expansion model; ecological distribution of "problem" rates.

## Key Proponents and Texts

[[thomas-william-isaac|Thomas]] & [[znaniecki-florian|Znaniecki]]; [[park-robert-e|Park]]; [[burgess-ernest-w|Burgess]]; later Shaw & McKay (flagged, not ingested). Sampson (2019) notes *The City* as seed of the tradition.

## Empirical Program

Letters and life-histories (*Polish Peasant*); community organization essays and ecological indices (*The City*); later delinquency mapping (reception).

## Method Commitments

Case documents; area rates; community studies; [[ecological-method]].

## Critiques and Responses

"Disorganization" as pejorative/ethnocentric; ordered alternatives and structural exclusion underplayed; reworked as collective efficacy / neighborhood effects (Sampson reception). Burgess already argued moderate disorganization can be normal metabolism toward reorganization.

## Current Status

`absorbed` — term contested; place-based crime and neighborhood research continue the problem-space under new labels.

## Reflexivity

Framed partly for social control and Americanization; indexes often from middle-class reform standpoint; period race and "junk" metaphors attributed only.
