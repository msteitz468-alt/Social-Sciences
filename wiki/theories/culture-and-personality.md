# Culture and Personality

Research program in American cultural anthropology (roughly 1920s–1950s) linking cultural institutions to individual personality formation and developmental experience. *[[mead-coming-of-age-samoa-1928|Coming of Age in Samoa]]* (1928) is a founding public case on adolescence; *[[mead-sex-and-temperament-1935|Sex and Temperament]]* (1935) is the program's major comparative statement on [[sex-temperament]]. Deeper school history expands as Benedict, Kardiner, and others are ingested.

## Core Claims

All **attributed** to the program's practitioners (here primarily [[mead-margaret|Mead]] / Boas framing):

1. Much of what looks like human nature is response to cultural restraints and opportunities ([[boas-franz|Boas]] foreword to Mead 1928).
2. Developmental stages (notably [[adolescence]]) vary in emotional tone across cultures; [[storm-and-stress]] is not a biological universal (Mead 1928).
3. Comparative ethnography of child-rearing and youth can test claims that psychology derived from Western cases alone.
4. Standardized personality differences between the sexes are cultural creations of early conditioning; “human nature is almost unbelievably malleable” (Mead 1935) — see [[sex-temperament]], [[mead-sex-and-temperament-study]].

## Intellectual Origins

Developed from [[historical-particularism]] and [[cultural-relativism]]: if cultures are historically unique totalities, personality is molded differently in each. Reacted against racial psychology, Hall-style universal developmental crisis, and natural sex-temperament essentialism.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[mead-margaret]] — *Coming of Age in Samoa* (1928); *Sex and Temperament* (1935); Manus and later works expand when ingested.
- [[boas-franz]] — programmatic endorsement (Samoa foreword; *Sex and Temperament* dedication).
- Field network: [[fortune-reo]], [[bateson-gregory]] (Sepik orientation).
- [[benedict-ruth|Ruth Benedict]] — *Patterns of Culture* (1934); *[[benedict-chrysanthemum-and-sword-1946|The Chrysanthemum and the Sword]]* (1946) national-character / culture-at-a-distance charter.
- Abram Kardiner, Ralph Linton, Cora Du Bois — expand when ingested.

## Empirical Program

Landmark cases now on the wiki: [[mead-coming-of-age-samoa-study]] / [[manua-samoans]]; [[mead-sex-and-temperament-study]] / [[arapesh]], [[mundugumor]], [[chambri]]; [[benedict-patterns-of-culture-study]]; [[benedict-chrysanthemum-study]] / [[japanese-people]] (wartime [[culture-at-a-distance]] [[national-character]]). The program organized a generation of culture-at-a-distance and national-character work (later contested — [[national-character-debate]]).

## Method Commitments

Intensive ethnography of childhood/adolescence and sex-role socialisation; problem-oriented multi-case comparison with Western socialisation; sometimes psychoanalytic vocabulary (later phases). *Sex and Temperament* exemplifies unequal-depth three-case contrast design with partner division of labour.

## Critiques and Responses

Critiqued for over-homogenizing "a culture," thin samples, national-character wartime excesses (*[[benedict-chrysanthemum-and-sword-1946|Chrysanthemum]]*; [[national-character-debate]]), Samoa contestation ([[mead-freeman-debate]]), and *Sex and Temperament*'s “too beautiful” pattern / depth imbalance. Status **absorbed**: the insight that development and sex-role ideals are culturally variable survives in psychological anthropology, childhood studies, and gender studies even where the mid-century program name has faded.

## Current Status

Absorbed into psychological anthropology, medical anthropology, childhood studies, and (via popular teaching) gender studies; rarely a self-named school after the 1960s Critical Turn.

**Harris critique (attributed).** *[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|Rise of Anthropological Theory]]* chs 15–17 treat culture-and-personality (pre-Freudian configurationism, Freudian/neo-Freudian, national character, Whiting-style psychocultural chains) as among the strategies **least suited** to explaining cultural evolution and institutional regularities; personality, for Harris, is not their origin. He re-reads some Whiting-style causal chains as "backing into" techno-environmental explanation. Record as Harris's position, not wiki-voice verdict ([[harris-marvin]] · [[cultural-materialism]]).

## Reflexivity

The program's public success (Mead as household name) shaped American debates on sex, education, and sex roles; that popularity is part of its history of knowledge, including vulnerability to culture-war re-litigation and to slogan reduction of complex ethnography.
