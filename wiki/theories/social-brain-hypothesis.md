# Social brain hypothesis

Research program associated especially with **Robin Dunbar**: primate (and by extension hominin) **neocortex / brain size** tracks **social network complexity** and group size more tightly than purely ecological demands alone (attributed to Dunbar program as used by Gamble). In *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013), [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]] deploys social-brain maps, predicted group sizes for extinct hominins, and the costly-brain / social-collaboration frame as one driver package beside climate and technology (attributed, Gamble 2013 chs 1–2).

## Core Claims

(All attributed. Primary source in this vault is Gamble's 2013 application, not Dunbar monographs.)

- Primate (and hominin) brain size scales with social group size / network complexity more tightly than with ecological problem-solving alone (Dunbar program as used by Gamble).
- Grooming-time constraints cap group size; language / bonding mechanisms substitute for fingertip grooming (Aiello & Dunbar line, attributed).
- Gamble maps nested intentionality / theory-of-mind levels and predicted group sizes onto extinct hominins and [[terrae|Terrae]] (attributed, chs 1–2); ties to [[intimate-effective-extended-networks]] and [[going-beyond]].
- Encephalisation is **not** automatically locked to better tools or wider range — the Terra 2 disconnect (attributed, ch. 1).

## Intellectual Origins

Primate grooming-time limits, neocortex-ratio studies, expensive-tissue hypothesis (Aiello & Wheeler — diet/cooking as enablers, attributed ch. 2), and language-as-cheap-grooming (attributed via Gamble's citations).

## Key Proponents and Texts

Dunbar and collaborators (primary literature largely un-ingested here); archaeological uptake in Gamble 1999 and especially *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013).

## Empirical Program

Correlate cranial capacity / EQ with reconstructed social scales; map nested network layers; use archaeological proxies (transfer distance, ornaments) only as secondary, attributed signals.

## Method Commitments

Comparative primatology + palaeoanthropological brain-size estimates; climate/variability ([[variability-selection]]) as co-drivers with social selection.

## Critiques and Responses

Group-size "magic numbers" contested; brain-size reconstructions for fossils uncertain; social vs ecological selection not mutually exclusive. Date-stamp all numerical predictions (as of sources cited; Gamble 2013 horizon).

## Current Status

Active in evolutionary anthropology and some cognitive archaeology; still middle-range and debated.

## Reflexivity

WEIRD and living-primate samples shape baseline curves; archaeological applications risk circularity when material culture is both evidence for and consequence of "social complexity."
