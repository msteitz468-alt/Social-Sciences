# Entanglement Theory

Research program associated with [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]]'s *[[hodder-entangled-2012|Entangled: An Archaeology of the Relationships Between Humans and Things]]* (2012). Organizes archaeology and material-culture analysis around **[[entanglement]]** as the dialectic of enabling **[[dependence-dependency|dependence]]** and constraining **dependency** among humans and things. Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/entanglement-theory|Theory Hub]]. Field laboratory: [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]]. Prior Hodder program: [[post-processual-archaeology]] (*[[hodder-archaeological-process-1999|The Archaeological Process]]* 1999 method charter).

## Core Claims

*(Attributed to Hodder 2012.)*

1. **Formal definition.** Entanglement is the sum of four classes of dependence/dependency: human–thing (HT), thing–thing (TT), thing–human (TH), and human–human (HH). Written as:
   > Entanglement = (HT) + (TT) + (TH) + (HH)

2. **Dialectic of dependence and dependency.** *Dependence* is enabling reliance (and contingency — “it depends”); *dependency* is constraint and entrapment. Entanglement is the productive/constraining double bind in which humans depend on things that depend on humans (and on other things).

3. **Objectness of things.** Things are not only social props or signs. They are temporary bundles of matter, energy, and information with presence; they are not isolated, not inert, endure over different temporalities, and are often forgotten as background. Their “stand-in-the-wayness” resists and forms human projects.

4. **Fittingness, not fitness alone.** Traits are selected and persist because they are **[[fittingness|fitting]]** within taut webs of mutual dependence (affordances, resonances, organizational coherence) — not solely by reproductive fitness, resource efficiency, or replicative success in transmission.

5. **Directional intensification without teleology.** Since dependence on made things became an evolutionary pathway, entanglements tend to lengthen and intensify (path dependence; fixing that adds further strings). Rate of change can rise even as entrapment deepens — a paradox addressed via open complex systems.

6. **Traceable archaeologically.** Specific local entanglements can be mapped with tanglegrams, [[life-history-method|operational/behavioral chains]], type sequences, spatial association, and legacy analysis — not only with meaning-first interpretation.

## Intellectual Origins

Hodder frames *Entangled* as a shift of gaze: from how things make society possible to **things themselves and their multiple connections** (the Lepenski Vir reconstruction with an absurd concert piano as pedagogical device). He places the book in the “return to things” across humanities and social sciences (Appadurai’s social life of things; materiality studies; Latour’s symmetry; Brown’s thing theory; Heideggerian equipment; cognitive/extended mind archaeology; behavioral chains; dual inheritance) while charging most of these strands with remaining **anthropocentric** — they still ask what things do for humans and under-engage chemistry, engineering, decay, and archaeometric materials analysis.

Genealogically the program **developed_from** mature [[post-processual-archaeology]] (meaningfully constituted material culture; theory-laden data; [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] as laboratory) but **reacted_against** purely semiotic readings and against network metaphors that erase sticky **dependency**. It also reworks Darwin-inspired cultural-evolution tools (variation, selection, persistence) through fittingness rather than reproductive success alone.

## Empirical Program

Cases in the 2012 book are **illustrations of the framework**, not free-standing excavation reports:

- **Çatalhöyük** — clay/plaster winning and maintenance; unfired mudbrick walls that fall; brick-making sequences; cattle/sheep–goat co-dependencies; Level VII cooking pottery and clay-ball multi-tasking; undecorated cooking pots vs one-off face pot; later West Mound decorated pottery as co-fitting with house/hearth/production shifts.
- **Domestication** — non-shattering cereals and flock care as co-dependency traps (labor, property, tools, walls).
- **Analogical modern strings** — cars (parts, roads, fuel), electricity grids, sugar (Mintz), planes — to show length, integration, and forgetness of links.
- **Mesolithic contrast** — piano at Lepenski Vir as impossible entanglement.

What the program claims to explain that meaning-only or fitness-only accounts underplay: **why** traits persist or fail (fittingness of the whole web); **why** humans keep investing in maintenance; **why** path dependence and directional growth of entanglement appear in deep time.

## Method Commitments

- Treat TT and TH dependences as first-class (not only HT meaning or HH sociality).
- Use **tanglegrams** and chain methods to make invisible strings visible when things go wrong or when archaeologists reconstruct production sequences.
- Integrate natural sciences/archaeometry into theoretical debate about things (not only social theory).
- Prefer historically specific, open-ended entanglements over closed “cultures” or systems — while still allowing language of integration, redundancy, and complex systems for degrees of entanglement.
- Analyze change as **co-fitting** of traits within webs, not transmission or optimality alone.

## Critiques and Responses

*(Positions as framed in Hodder 2012, especially Ch. 10 self-critique; external literature beyond the book not yet primary-ingested.)*

- **ANT / relationalism:** Hodder credits Latour with taking things seriously but argues “network” (and even meshwork) underplays sticky entrapment and independent material processes (ice melt, salts, decay) that are not only translations.
- **HBE / evolutionary archaeology:** Valued for quantifying trade-offs and long-term de-centering of the human; rejected when analysis stops at efficiency or reproductive/replicative success without the wider entanglement.
- **Human-centered social science:** Underplays objectness; Hodder answers by object-side themes (not isolated, not inert, different temporalities, forgetness).
- **Self-noted limits (Hodder):** Relative under-emphasis on HH dependence and on power/agency; tension between “objective” tanglegrams and positioned interest in what counts as “going wrong”; messiness that cannot be mopped up; definitional problems of “thing.”

## Current Status

Active program charter as of the 2012 text; vocabulary of entanglement, human–thing dependence, and tanglegram-style analysis has circulated widely in archaeology and material-culture studies (reception literature not yet fully ingested here). Status field: **active**. Related vault programs: [[post-processual-archaeology]], [[practice-theory]], [[dwelling-perspective]].

## Reflexivity

The book is written by the long-term director of a high-profile Neolithic project; Çatalhöyük cases double as method laboratory and as exemplars of the theory — production context shapes which entanglements are visible (well-funded multi-specialist excavation). Ethical closing chapters use global warming, sustainable “fixing,” and vulnerability under material entrapment; these are **positions**, not wiki-voice policy. Bridging culture and biology via “the material” as third term is Hodder’s synthetic claim, achieved by selective reworking of rival theories.
