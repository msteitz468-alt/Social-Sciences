# Multiregional Evolution

Research program holding that living humans descend from **regionally continuous** archaic populations (*Homo erectus* and later archaics including [[neanderthals]]) connected by **gene flow**, so modern traits evolve in parallel across the Old World rather than solely via African replacement. Chief rival to [[recent-african-origin]]. Live dispute: [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

**Source caution:** Densified primarily from *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|Stringer & Gamble 1993]]* as **opponent's synthesis** of the multiregional position plus named Wolpoff/Thorne/Brace claims they report. Primary multiregional monographs not yet ingested — treat formulations as attributed via S&G unless noted.

## Core Claims

*(Attributed — as presented by proponents and by Stringer & Gamble 1993 as foil. 1993 horizon.)*

1. **Regional anatomical continuity** links archaic and modern populations in Europe, East Asia, Australasia, and elsewhere: local features persist (e.g. Neanderthal large noses → modern Europeans; Javanese *erectus* cheek bones / frontal flattening → modern Australians; East Asian shovel-shaped-incisor and facial continuity arguments in the wider debate).
2. **Gene flow maintains species unity.** Isolation was never sufficient for allopatric speciation; genes circulated among all Pleistocene human populations. Multiregional evolution is **not** independent speciation or Coon's extreme staggered-race form (often repudiated). Thorne & Wolpoff "centre and edge" model: interbreeding between wide-ranging Ice Age populations always sufficient to prevent full speciation — even the radical suggestion that only one known species (*Homo sapiens*) has existed since the early Ice Age (via S&G 1993).
3. **Neanderthals in European ancestry.** Neanderthals (especially early forms and Middle Eastern *H. s. palestinus*-type samples in older taxonomies) contribute to modern people rather than pure evolutionary dead ends (Weiner & Campbell "spectrum hypothesis" and continuity camp via S&G). Continuity favourers read the Neanderthal face as a **logical intermediate** between big projecting archaic faces and small tucked modern European faces via two-stage reduction (cheek musculature then front teeth), not as a specialized dead end (via S&G 1993).
4. **Out of Africa 1 is the founding dispersal.** *Homo erectus* dispersal from Africa ~1 Ma begins the process culminating in modern human races; subsequent evolution is worldwide and multiregional, not a second total replacement (proponents via S&G).
5. **Genetics can be reread as deep.** Early Cann–Wilson mtDNA Eve ~400 ka was multiregional-compatible (late *erectus*); even post-1987 African-Eve trees are attacked on rate constancy — pattern could fit 1 Ma *erectus* spread as well as recent moderns; PAUP cannot conclusively prove African origin from then-available data (multiregionalists via S&G 1993).

## Intellectual Origins

Weidenreich's 1943 world-wide modern-race model (from Zhoukoudian *H. erectus* work) underpins later Coon, Brace, and Wolpoff (S&G historiography). Deeper roots: de Mortillet, Schwalbe (*Homo primigenius*), Gorjanović-Kramberger (Krapina), Hrdlička "Neanderthal phase." Brace's 1964 "The fate of the 'Classic' Neanderthals" revived regional continuity against Boule-style "catastrophism"/expulsion; "culinary revolution" explained loss of Neanderthal facial/dental specializations via improved stone tools reducing selection for large front teeth used as a vice (Brace via S&G). Named program of the 1980s (Wolpoff–Thorne) reacts against single recent African origin and total-replacement models.

**Coon caution:** Coon's *The Origin of Races* (1962) developed Weidenreich and was accused of racism for claiming African and Australian populations made the *erectus*→*sapiens* "transition" much later than Europe and mainland Asia (S&G 1993). Multiregionalists typically repudiate Coon's ranking of transitions while retaining polycentric continuity + gene flow.

## Empirical Program

*(Claims as multiregionalists press them, reported via S&G 1993 and later textbook layers.)*

- **Europe:** Neanderthal face/path as intermediate; "progressive" Levantine Neanderthals (McCown & Keith historical reading); late less-robust samples (e.g. Vindija) claimable as intermediates toward early Moderns (S&G reject remains as incomplete/late).
- **East Asia:** Continuity from Zhoukoudian / Dali-type archaics to modern East Asians (S&G counter: Upper Cave more Cro-Magnon-like; Dali/Maba neither African-style borderlines nor simple Peking Man continuity).
- **Australasia:** Thorne–Wolpoff dual-origin model — gracile eastern island-hopping Asian route vs robust Ngandong/Java western route retaining "mark of ancient Java"; Peter Brown's *in situ* late Pleistocene diversification as alternative (S&G note bulk of then-available Australian genetics supports one basic origin).
- **Genetics:** Attack mtDNA clock constancy and African-root certainty; early ~400 ka Eve calibration; demand that middle Pleistocene Europe/Asia fossils not be severed from living regional patterns.
- **Behaviour (continuity reading):** Gradual accrual of Upper Palaeolithic traits (Clark & Lindly dimmer-switch vs S&G switch/creative-explosion); Châtelperronian as local Neanderthal capacity not mere imitation (contested).

## Method Commitments

Phenetic continuity readings of regional trait lists; skepticism of pure replacement genetics and of cladistic separation that overweights specialized Neanderthal characters; emphasis on gene flow as unfalsifiable-looking glue ("Let the genes do the walking, if the bones won't play" — S&G's critical motto for multiregional flexibility, 1993). Preference for single polytypic species over multiple late Pleistocene *Homo* species.

## Critiques and Responses

**Replacement-camp critiques (S&G 1993):** African moderns earliest; genetics show recent African coalescence; Neanderthal specializations take them *away* from Cro-Magnons; facial reduction under braincase explains all modern faces without Neanderthal intermediate stage; "transitional" industries as imitation after Moderns arrive; global colonization after ~40 ka too rapid/extensive for continuity; multiregional gene-flow arguments hard to test or disprove.

**Multiregional rhetorical and scientific pushback (Wolpoff via S&G):** Continuity with long-lasting geographic contact and cooperation is "a more satisfying interpretation… than a scientific rendering of the story of Cain" based on one population quickly, completely, and most likely violently replacing all others — "making war and not love"; if true, implications "not pleasant" (Wolpoff quoted 1993). Popular image: Wolpoff "sees a Neanderthal every morning in his shaving mirror." 1990 AAAS: continuity accuses replacement of justifying extinction; replacement accuses continuity of treating racial differences as original and historical.

**Later horizon (record only):** Post-2010 aDNA supports limited archaic admixture without restoring equal multiregional contribution; classical equal-weight multiregionalism is minority; elements survive in assimilation/hybrid models. Do not adjudicate in wiki voice.

## Current Status

Classical equal-weight multiregionalism is minority; elements survive in assimilation/hybrid models. Status remains **contested** as a named program and as a set of regional-continuity claims still pressed against pure replacement. See [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]]. Claims as **Stringer’s historiography and critiques** of Multiregionalism (as of Stringer 2012) — not primary Wolpoff/Thorne monographs. Does not overwrite the 1993 layer above.*

### Intellectual lineages as Stringer presents them

- **Franz Weidenreich (1930s, Zhoukoudian):** *H. erectus* → *H. sapiens* across the whole Old World range (~1 Ma: Africa, China, Indonesia, perhaps Europe); regional variation as roots of modern “racial” differentiation (e.g. flat faces/prominent cheekbones China → modern Orientals; robust projecting faces Java → Australian Aborigines) (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Carleton Coon:** five largely independent *erectus* lineages → Capoid, Negroid, Caucasoid, Mongoloid, Australoid races — contrasted with Weidenreich’s gene/idea-exchange network; Coon held Africa “indifferent kindergarten,” Europe and Asia “principal schools” (attributed via Stringer; Coon ranking remains race-science-contaminated historiography).
- **C. Loring Brace:** global “neanderthaloid” phase; front-teeth-as-tools produced midface/incisor/skull morphology until Upper Paleolithic tools ~35 ka lifted dental demands (attributed).
- **Wolpoff, Thorne, Wu (1984 revival):** continuity so complete that all *erectus* and regional descendants = one species *H. sapiens*; no discrete “origin” of the modern form; local features via continuity, global features via interbreeding network (e.g. chin from Africa, high forehead from China, each spreading by interbreeding) (as of Stringer 2012).

### Saint-Césaire as replacement evidence against continuity

The Saint-Césaire partial skeleton associated with the **Châtelperronian** (transitional Middle→Upper Paleolithic traits in SW France) was widely expected (1960s–70s) to prove Neanderthal Phase / Multiregional continuity as an evolutionary intermediate. Klein and Stringer expected Neanderthal makers of UP-style tools **without** transitional morphology proving multiregional continuity. Skeleton identified as a rather typical Neanderthal — relief for Stringer’s doctoral rejection of Euro continuity; [[wolpoff-milford|Wolpoff]] argued transitional features overemphasized; Randy White suggested cultural change preceded morphological change (Brace’s Neanderthal Phase). Once Neanderthal nature was accepted, Saint-Césaire supported **Replacement in western Europe**: Châtelperronian ~35 ka ¹⁴C as local Neanderthal strand parallel to Aurignacian moderns entering ~40 ka (Klein, Mellars, Vandermeersch via Stringer 2012).

### Mungo / Australian aDNA critique

2001 claims of ancient mtDNA from Australian fossils (Willandra Lakes, Kow Swamp; including **Mungo 3** as outgroup) were used to support Multiregional or Assimilation models (one author: multiregionalist Alan Thorne) (as of Stringer 2012 reporting that study). Stringer, Alan Cooper, and Matthew Collins criticized exceptional recovery rates given desert heat; some DNA from storage-box scraps after reburial/cremation; nonstandard protocols; reanalysis with more comparative sequences showed Mungo 3 was **not** an outgroup and did **not** seriously challenge recent African origin (as of Stringer 2012).

### *Homo floresiensis* and single-species Multiregionalism

Stringer reports that multiregionalists wedded to a single species *H. sapiens* over the last ~2 Ma preferred pathology / “village idiot” / dental-fillings claims for [[homo-floresiensis]] rather than a distinct species; he judges short-term profile-raising damaged their and paleoanthropology’s reputation, while evidence grows strongly for a genuine distinct species (as of Stringer 2012).

### Classic Multiregional claims “comprehensively” rejected (Stringer’s position)

Stringer (as of 2012) quotes Wolpoff and co-authors (1994): earliest “modern” humans are not Africans; no specific admixture with Africans or replacement; continuous regional skeletal feature combinations from earliest to living peoples. He argues classic Multiregionalism’s claims — earliest moderns not African, Africa no special role, regional skeletal continuity linking *erectus* to living peoples — have been **pretty comprehensively shown to be false** by the evidence in the book (position, not wiki-voice verdict). He rejects the move that modest archaic ancestry (~5%) “proves” Multiregionalism: such input rarely appears in single-marker studies; large genomic comparisons with archaic genomes were needed to detect hybridization (as of Stringer 2012). Limited archaic admixture is recorded under [[assimilation-model]] / “mostly Out of Africa” RAO, not as restoration of equal-weight Multiregionalism (see [[recent-african-origin]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]]).

## Reflexivity

Continuity vs replacement tracks older polygenism/monogenism and race-science afterlives — handle with date-stamps and attributed voice (Stringer & Gamble 1993 preface on heritage and identity; Coon racism controversy). Wolpoff's moral framing of replacement as Cain/"Killer Africans" and S&G's reply (difference without Boule/Wells condemnation; no winners/losers) show both sides cast the other's model as politically as well as scientifically charged (as of 1993). Stringer 2012 densifies Weidenreich–Coon–Brace–Wolpoff lineages as **historiography from the replacement camp** — primary multiregional monographs remain thin in the collection.
