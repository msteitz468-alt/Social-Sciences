# Early Functional-Processual Archaeology

Trigger's umbrella for **pre- and mid-twentieth-century** archaeological programs that asked **how people lived** — economy, environment, social function — rather than only mapping cultures in time and space. Distinct from (and ancestral to) 1960s [[processual-archaeology|New Archaeology / processualism]], which radicalized the functional-processual interest under neoevolutionist and positivist banners.

## Core Claims

*(Attributed to [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]] 2006, ch. 7 and synthetic passages.)*

1. **Third complementary interest.** Prehistoric archaeology from Scandinavia onward carried three interests: cultural evolution ([[nineteenth-century-evolutionary-archaeology|evolutionary]]), regional culture-history ([[culture-historical-archaeology]]), and **how people lived** (functional-processual).
2. **Environmental and economic reconstruction.** Early strands include environmental functionalism, economic prehistory ([[clark-grahame|Grahame Clark]]), and ecological/settlement archaeology (Steward lineage; Willey settlement pattern).
3. **Soviet and Marxist streams.** Soviet archaeology after the 1930s and [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]'s Marxist phase supply class, production, and social-evolution frames that are functional-processual in Trigger's sense, not culture-historical trait lists alone.
4. **Americanist internal critique.** [[taylor-walter-w|Taylor]]'s [[conjunctive-approach]] attacks culture-historical chronicle from within American archaeology — proto-processual functionalism.
5. **Bridge to 1960s processualism.** Trigger treats post-1960 [[processual-archaeology]] as the behavioral/scientific radicalization of this longer functional-processual current; [[post-processual-archaeology]] later reweights it toward meaning and culture.

## Intellectual Origins

Social anthropology (functionalism); economic history; Soviet Marxism; cultural ecology ([[steward-julian-h|Steward]]); dissatisfaction with migration/diffusion as pseudo-explanation.

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[clark-grahame]] — economic/ecological prehistory (*[[clark-archaeology-and-society-1957|Archaeology and Society]]*).
- [[childe-v-gordon]] — Marxist and economic phases (*Man Makes Himself*, *What Happened in History*).
- Soviet archaeology (Ravdonikas, Artsikhovsky, et al. — as surveyed by Trigger).
- [[taylor-walter-w]] — *[[taylor-study-of-archeology-1948|A Study of Archeology]]*.
- Settlement/ecology: Steward; Willey Virú; Braidwood; etc. (Trigger survey).

## Empirical Program

Economic site reports; pollen/ecological reconstruction; settlement-pattern survey; Soviet socio-economic periodization; conjunctive site analysis.

## Method Commitments

Function and system over trait lists; environment and economy as primary; ethnographic and anthropological analogy carefully (and variously) used; later, explicit hypothesis-testing under New Archaeology.

## Critiques and Responses

Culture-historians saw functional aims as speculative; processualists later dismissed earlier functional work as insufficiently scientific; postprocessualists charged ecological functionalism with reductionism. Trigger's category is historiographic — it groups diverse programs that primary sources name differently.

## Current Status

`absorbed` into ordinary archaeological practice (economy, settlement, ecology are routine) and into the named successors [[processual-archaeology]] and strands of [[post-processual-archaeology]].

## Reflexivity

Trigger ties functional-processual growth to professionalization, welfare-state science funding, and Cold War intellectual climates (including Soviet vs Western Marxist paths). Source: [[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006]].
