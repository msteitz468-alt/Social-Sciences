# Interpretive Anthropology

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/theory/interpretive-anthropology|Theory Hub]]

Research program associated above all with [[geertz-clifford|Clifford Geertz]]’s *[[geertz-interpretation-of-cultures-1973|The Interpretation of Cultures]]* (1973) and its decade-later sequel *[[geertz-local-knowledge-1983|Local Knowledge]]* (1983). Overlaps the broader [[symbolic-anthropology]] movement (Turner, Douglas, Schneider) but is distinct in its semiotic definition of culture, essayistic method, and claim that ethnography is [[thick-description|thick description]].

## Core Claims

*(Attributed to Geertz, IoC 1973, unless noted.)*

1. **Semiotic culture.** Culture as “webs of significance”; analysis is interpretive, not law-seeking.
2. **Thick description.** Ethnography reconstructs stratified hierarchies of meaning (Ryle’s wink example).
3. **Culture as context, not cause.** Culture is the ensemble of texts/symbols within which action is intelligible — not an independent causal force in the manner of a variable.
4. **Models of / models for.** Sacred symbols and cultural systems shape both how the world is seen and how one should act.
5. **Public culture.** Meanings are public, not locked in private mental states.

## Intellectual Origins

Reacted against Tylor’s omnibus culture concept, behaviorist thin description, and (in “Cerebral Savage”) Lévi-Straussian [[structuralism]] as cerebral combinatorics. Drew on Weber, Ryle, Langer, Ricoeur-adjacent textual metaphor, and Harvard DSR / Parsons while turning systems theory toward local meaning.

## Empirical Program

Java and Bali ethnography (religion, ritual change, cockfight, personhood); new-states political essays (Indonesia and comparative); ideology analysis. Method: [[thick-description]].

## Empirical Program (from Local Knowledge, 1983)

The 1983 sequel extends the program along two fronts: a meta-argument about social science ([[blurred-genres|the refiguration of social thought]]) and the treatment of further domains as *cultural systems*.

| Domain | Essay | What Geertz claims |
|---|---|---|
| Meta / method | Blurred Genres | Genre-mixing; turn from "laws and instances" to "cases and interpretations"; game/drama/text analogies ([[blurred-genres]]) |
| Understanding | From the Native's Point of View | Understanding via symbolic forms, not empathy; [[experience-near-experience-distant]]; *lek* as stage fright; Moroccan *nisba* |
| Moral imagination | Found in Translation | "The social history of the moral imagination"; relativism apprehended *through* the interfering glosses, not behind them |
| Common sense | Common Sense as a Cultural System | Common sense as historically constructed cultural system; five "quasi-qualities" ([[common-sense]]) |
| Art | Art as a Cultural System | Art as semiotic, against formalism and functionalism; art as "sensibility" ([[art-as-a-cultural-system]]) |
| Power | Centers, Kings, and Charisma | Charisma as nearness to the active center; royal progresses (Elizabeth I, Hayam Wuruk, Mulay Hasan) ([[charisma]]) |
| Modern thought | The Way We Think Now | Ethnography of modern thought; disciplines as "forms of life"; thought spectacularly multiple as product, singular as process |
| Law | Local Knowledge: Fact and Law | Law as "legal sensibility" / [[local-knowledge]]; *haqq*/*dharma*/*adat* compared; law as "social imagination" |

## Method Commitments

- [[thick-description]] as ethnography’s defining task
- Essay as natural genre of cultural analysis (“spurts” not cumulative curve)
- Generalize **within** cases, not across them to laws
- Public meanings; culture as context not causal power (program claim)
- Textual metaphor: social action as readable manuscript / ensemble of texts

## Current Status

Active as vocabulary (**thick description**, culture as meaning) even where full program is contested or absorbed into eclectic cultural analysis. Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/interpretive-anthropology]]. Adjacent critical-turn ethnography of **sentiment as conventional discourse** — [[abu-lughod-lila|Abu-Lughod]] *[[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-1986|Veiled Sentiments]]* (1986) cites Geertz among others while pushing dual poetic/ordinary registers as political ([[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-study]]).

**Reflexive extension from within (Rabinow 1977):** [[rabinow-paul|Paul Rabinow]], a student
in [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]'s Sefrou project, kept the semiotic culture concept and
[[webs-of-significance|webs of significance]] but turned them onto the fieldwork encounter in
*[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977|Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco]]* (1977) — arguing
that anthropologist and informant *both* spin such webs, that "facts are made, not found,"
and that the webs become "at least partially intertwined" in dialogue. This
[[reflexive-fieldwork|reflexive]] move is both continuous with the interpretive program and a
step toward the crisis-of-representation critique of it (see [[writing-culture-debate]]).

**Afterlife in the experimental moment (Marcus & Fischer 1986):** *[[marcus-fischer-anthropology-as-cultural-critique-1986|Anthropology as Cultural Critique]]* treats interpretive anthropology (Geertz as major spokesman) as the 1960s critique that first shifted practice toward symbols and meanings; method and political critiques of ethnography "caught up" only in the later **[[experimental-moment|experimental moment]]** / [[writing-culture]] configuration. ACC both draws on and pressures the program — experiential ethnographies of person/self/emotion extend interpretive aims, while political-economy "[[knowable-communities|knowable communities]]" answer the standing charge that interpretive work elides power and history.

## Reflexivity

US prestige anthropology 1960s–70s; Indonesia/Morocco fieldwork capital converted into general theory essays; IAS/Chicago essay form; new-states discourse inside modernization conversation. Geertz’s “not a power” culture definition shaped a generation of meaning-centered work and the backlash against it.
