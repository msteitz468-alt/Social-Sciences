# Four Ontologies (Descola)

[[descola-philippe|Philippe Descola]]'s research program in comparative ontology, stated fully in *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* (2005 French; 2013 English). He argues that human ways of schematizing continuity or discontinuity of [[interiority-and-physicality|interiorities and physicalities]] between self and other beings yield **four** stable combinatorial formulas — **[[animism]]**, **[[totemism]]**, **[[analogism]]**, and **[[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]]** — each with characteristic [[collectives-including-nonhumans|collectives]], moralities, and [[modes-of-relation|modes of relation]].

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies|Four Ontologies — Theory Hub]] (architecture claim by claim, empirical program, debates, What Survived).

## Core Claims (attributed)

Descola argues:

1. **Nature/culture is not universal.** The dualism that organizes modern Western cosmology is the *local* formula of [[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]], not the baseline against which other "cultures" are measured.
2. **Identification is pre-taxonomic.** Upstream of folk taxonomies and instituted norms sit elementary schemas that detect continuity/discontinuity of **interiority** (mind, soul, intentionality, shared essence) and **physicality** (form, substance, ethogram, corporeal dispositions).
3. **Four combinatorial cells exhaust the matrix:**
   - **[[animism]]** — continuity of interiorities + discontinuity of physicalities
   - **[[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]]** — discontinuity of interiorities + continuity of physicalities (reverse of animism)
   - **[[totemism]]** (ontological) — continuity of *both* within pre-differentiated hybrid classes
   - **[[analogism]]** — discontinuity of *both*, order restored by correspondences, hierarchy, sacrifice
4. **Ontology precedes sociology.** Stabilized relational systems (institutions, "society") are analytically subordinate to systems of properties attributed to existing beings — reverse of the Durkheimian sociocentric default.
5. **[[modes-of-relation|Modes of relation]] cross-cut identification.** Give, take, exchange, produce, protect, transmit organize practical attachments; not every relation is equally compatible with every ontology.
6. **The program is heuristic.** Ethnographic and historical examples sketch a more economical comparative grammar than nature/culture dualism; structural configurations take methodological priority over genetic history without denying change.

## Intellectual Origins

- **[[structuralism]] / [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]]:** Descola retains structural comparison, limited combinatorial repertoires, and transformational thinking while overturning the nature/culture hinge Lévi-Strauss made foundational and partially recovering ontological totemism Lévi-Strauss dissolved into classification.
- **Achuar fieldwork:** Living with the [[achuar]] of Ecuadorian Amazonia "propelled" the questioning of dualist certainties; primary monographic base now ingested as *[[descola-in-the-society-of-nature-1994|In the Society of Nature]]* ([[descola-society-of-nature-study|study]]; Fr. 1986). Earlier definition of animism as reverse of Lévi-Straussian totemism is corrected in the 2005 book as still smuggling nature/society in.
- **Dialogue partners:** Eduardo Viveiros de Castro ([[perspectivism]]); [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]] (hybrids, collectives, *We Have Never Been Modern*); Tim Ingold (close, critical distance); Anne Christine Taylor.
- **Against:** Universal wilderness/domestication dichotomies; evolutionist stages of continuity; culturalism that treats non-Western cosmologies as residual "belief."

## Empirical Program

Comparative cases organized by ontology (attributed to Descola's synthesis):

| Ontology | Exemplary cases (as used in BNC) |
|---|---|
| Animism | [[achuar]]/Jivaroan Amazonia; Makuna, Tukano; Chewong; Cree/Ojibwa; Siberian hunters |
| Totemism | Australian Aboriginal systems (Elkin inventory; Dreaming; Brandenstein); Algonquin hybrids |
| Analogism | Nahua/Mesoamerican component ontologies; Bambara, Dogon, Samo; Great Chain of Being; classical China (noted) |
| Naturalism | Modern West (genealogy Ch.3); internal challenges from ethology, animal rights, ecology |

Fieldwork depth is highest for Amazonia; outside that zone the program relies on secondary ethnography — Descola's own Weberian caveat (Preface).

## Method Commitments

- Two axes of identification as comparative instruments (not Cartesian substances reified).
- Structural morphology of practices "upstream" of habitus and of instituted norms.
- Relative universalism: relational comparison free of particular (naturalist) universalism.
- Heuristic models; structure-before-genesis as *methodological* priority.
- Modes of identification and modes of relation analyzed separately then recombined (compatibility matrix).

## Critiques and Responses

Standing discussion points in the wider "ontological turn" (not all internal to BNC):

- Secondary-source reach outside Amazonia; risk of typological reification.
- Relation to Viveiros de Castro: Descola treats [[perspectivism]] as a *graft/corollary* of animism (crossed inversion of viewpoints), not coextensive with all animism. [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] rejects the subsumption in the other direction — perspectivism is "not a type but a concept," and "the very idea of identification is an analogist idea," so the fourfold typology's method instantiates one of its own cells (see [[ontological-turn-debate]]; [[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]).
- Whether four cells over-constrain ethnographic hybridity (Descola allows non-exclusive minor modes — e.g. Algonquin totemic features inside animism).
- Political export: Epilogue refuses to treat non-Western ontologies as ready-made reform programs for late-modern ecological crisis.

## Current Status

Part of the broader [[ontological-turn|ontological turn]] (with Viveiros [[perspectivism]]/[[multinaturalism]] and Strathern) — related programs, not identical. See [[ontological-turn]] for the umbrella.

**Active.** Organized a generation of comparative cosmology, Amazonian and Arctic work, museum/exhibition experiments (e.g. Musée du quai Branly), and debate with perspectivism, ANT, and multi-species ethnography. Status on this page: active research program with one primary source ingested.

## Reflexivity

Written from within naturalism to relativize it. Comparative ambition inherits structuralism's universalizing confidence while claiming anti-ethnocentric intent. Colonial secondary literature (Spencer & Gillen, early Algonquin ethnography, etc.) is reused as evidence — filter with documentation dates and construct status on society/culture pages.
