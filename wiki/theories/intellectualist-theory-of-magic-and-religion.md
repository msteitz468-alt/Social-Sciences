# Intellectualist Theory of Magic and Religion

## Core Claims

All claims in **attributed voice**.

1. **Intellectual origin.** For [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]] and [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]], magic and religion begin as *theories* — attempts to explain and act on nature — not as social glue (Durkheim) or anxiety management (Malinowski).
2. **[[sympathetic-magic]] as false science.** Frazer: Law of Similarity + Law of Contact → homoeopathic and contagious magic; association of ideas misread as real causal connection (*[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|Golden Bough]]* ch. 3–4).
3. **Magic → religion → science.** Frazer: when the magician's coercion fails, the religious attitude (submission to personal gods) arises; science later restores impersonal order. The three are successive "theories of thought" (ch. 4; Farewell to Nemi).
4. **[[animism]] as minimum religion.** Tylor: religion = belief in spiritual beings, elaborated from soul-inference.
5. **[[survivals]] as evidence.** Present customs of "lower races" and European peasants reconstruct earlier stages of the universal mind ([[psychic-unity-of-mankind]]).

## Intellectual Origins

reacted_against: purely theological or purely allegorical readings of myth; ethnocentric dismissal of "superstition" without systematic analysis. developed_from: Victorian [[social-evolutionism]], British empiricist associationism, classical scholarship, [[comparative-method]].

## Key Proponents and Texts

- [[tylor-edward-burnett]] — *[[tylor-primitive-culture-1871|Primitive Culture]]* (1871): [[animism]], [[survivals]], [[culture]].
- [[frazer-james-george]] — *[[frazer-golden-bough-1922|The Golden Bough]]* (1890–1922): [[sympathetic-magic]], magic–religion–science, [[divine-king]], [[dying-and-rising-god]].

## Empirical Program

Comparative catalogue of magic, taboo, kingship, and vegetation ritual worldwide, ordered to display developmental sequences. Exemplary application: [[frazer-golden-bough-study]].

## Method Commitments

[[comparative-method]] on second-hand sources; [[survivals]]; no participant observation; psychic unity licenses independent parallel development.

## Critiques and Responses

- **Boas / particularism:** similar traits ≠ parallel stages ([[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]).
- **Functionalists:** present function over conjectural origin; magic as anxiety-handling (Malinowski), not failed physics.
- **Durkheimian:** religion is social, not individual philosophy (*Elementary Forms*).
- **Evans-Pritchard:** "If I were a horse" reasoning; internal rationality of belief systems (Azande).
- **Dying-god critique:** single-type construct fails under closer case analysis (J.Z. Smith et al.).

## Current Status

status: **abandoned** as a research program; vocabulary ([[sympathetic-magic]], [[taboo]], scapegoat) survives stripped of unilinear ranking. Historiographically central.

## Reflexivity

Produced inside colonial knowledge circuits: missionaries, administrators, and travellers as data sources; "savage/civilized" ranking as analytic frame; Cambridge classicist authorship without fieldwork. The theory's intellectualism mirrored its authors' class position as library philosophers reconstructing "primitive thought."
