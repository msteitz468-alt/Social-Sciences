# Descent Theory (Kinship)

**Descent theory** is the mid-twentieth-century British social-anthropological program that took **unilineal descent groups** — lineages and clans — as the central institutions of politics, property, and jural identity in many non-state societies. In [[kuper-adam|Kuper]]’s historiography (*[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|Invention of Primitive Society]]*, Ch. 10), it is a **phoenix from the ashes**: a transformation of classical [[primitive-society]] models after the crisis of Rivers, enacted by Fortes and Evans-Pritchard under a Radcliffe-Brownian banner while reviving deep assumptions of the older anthropology.

## Core Claims (attributed)

- Corporate **unilineal descent groups** order rights in persons, land, and office ([[unilineal-descent]], [[segmentary-lineage-system]]).
- **Segmentary opposition** can produce ordered politics without a state (canonical Nuer case — [[evans-pritchard-e-e]], *The Nuer*).
- Analytic split between **domestic** and **politico-jural** domains; **[[complementary-filiation]]** (Fortes) carries morality/sentiment while descent groups carry jural public enforcement.
- Kinship terminologies largely sidelined (Malinowskian inheritance) until [[alliance-theory]] revived “relationship terminologies.”
- APS dichotomy: Group A (centralized government) vs Group B (stateless — either tiny kinship-coterminous polities or segmentary lineage systems) — preserves Maine/Morgan opposition of kinship-ordered primitive vs territorial state (Kuper).

## Intellectual Origins

*(Attributed — Kuper 1988.)* Not an inevitable outgrowth of Malinowski and Radcliffe-Brown. Fortes and Evans-Pritchard were young Africanists with long fieldwork on loosely organized tribes without centralized authority — experience different from earlier functionalists. Both trained at LSE under Malinowski, later at Oxford under Radcliffe-Brown (chair 1937); reacted against Malinowski while drawing on R-B.

**Three Malinowskian assumptions** (Kuper): (1) ignore kinship terminologies; (2) universal domestic family with crucial father role; (3) clan as political outgrowth of family elements, coexisting with person-to-person family ties (not evolutionary series).

**R-B “godfather”:** Fortes and EP appointed R-B as sponsor; Kuper judges Fortes’s Gifford/Tonga fireplace anecdote unpersuasive as history (term “lineage” adopted; model not Gifford’s). Main cited R-B text: 1935 “Patrilineal and matrilineal succession” — unilineal corporate groups efficient for status transmission; patri/matri functionally similar (contra old evolutionists).

**Durkheimian segmentary whole:** primitive society as equivalent parts hung by shared values/exchanges; Durkheim’s “clans” equated with R-B corporate descent groups — more structural than Malinowski’s individualism (Kuper).

## Empirical Program

African segmentary and unilineal systems (Nuer, Tallensi, etc.); 1950s extensions (divorce rates, witchcraft, ancestor worship, “segmentary state,” conical clan, Kachin). Later New Guinea Highlands critique (Barnes et al.: African moulds fit awkwardly). Leach *Pul Eliya* (1961): locality not descent as basis of corporate grouping; “structure of unilineal descent groups” possibly “a total fiction.”

## Method Commitments

Structural-functional fieldwork; genealogical method (Rivers inheritance, purified); jural/normative models of group structure; model-first analysis (risk of forcing data — Schneider/Yap example in Kuper’s conclusion).

## Critiques and Responses

- **Ideology vs behaviour (Kuper, Holy, Verdon, etc.):** segmentary opposition rarely observed in action; Nuer lack vocabulary matching the model — “Even the Nuer are not like *The Nuer*” (Kuper). EP early essays treated Nuer as transitional from pure lineage to territorial polity; *The Nuer* relocates lineage as values/idiom that need not match practice. See [[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]].
- **Richards:** clarity bought at expense of permanence and multi-principle grouping.
- **Leach:** complementary filiation disguises affinity; self-interest and territory over descent ideology.
- **Alliance theory:** marriage exchange generative, not descent.
- **Schneider:** “kinship” as Western cultural construct; Yap double-descent reanalysis.
- **1970s retreat:** failed outside British school; neo-Marxist “lineage mode of production” (Meillassoux/Rey) did not catch on (Kuper).

## Current Status

Contested / partly absorbed: descent remains a descriptive tool; the mid-century **paradigm** of descent-as-skeleton-of-primitive-society is no longer orthodoxy. Kuper (1982 ARA; 1988 book) pronounced the lineage model exhausted. See closing diagnosis that mainstream anthropology abandoned “society” for “culture.”

## Reflexivity

Produced in late-colonial Africanist institutions (International African Institute, Oxford); models traveled as teaching orthodoxy; critique of the model is also a critique of anthropology’s object-construction ([[primitive-society]]). Kuper: retained Victorian prototype as a **model**, disclaiming evolutionist cast.
