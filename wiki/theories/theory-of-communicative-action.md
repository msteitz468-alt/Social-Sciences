# The Theory of Communicative Action

Deep analysis: [[hubs/theory/theory-of-communicative-action|Theory Hub]].

Both volumes are now ingested: [[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v1-1984|Vol. 1]] (foundations) and [[habermas-theory-of-communicative-action-v2-1987|Vol. 2]] (system/lifeworld and colonization).

## Core Claims

All claims are [[habermas-jurgen|Habermas]]'s, in attributed voice.

1. **Communicative turn.** Sociology as theory of society needs a concept of [[communicative-action]] — coordination through illocutionary force and hearers' yes/no to criticizable [[validity-claims]] — not only a teleological/[[strategic-action|strategic]] actor model. This is a paradigm shift from purposive activity to communication (prepared philosophically in Vol. 1; derived genetically via Mead/Durkheim in Vol. 2 as [[linguistification-of-the-sacred]]).
2. **[[communicative-rationality|Communicative rationality]].** Rationality is not exhausted by cognitive-instrumental success. Speech and action oriented to understanding embody a multi-dimensional, procedural rationality (truth / rightness / truthfulness) reconstructible by [[formal-pragmatics]].
3. **Three-level rationality problem.** Any sociology that is a theory of society faces rationality at metatheoretical (action concepts), methodological (Verstehen and validity), and empirical-theoretical (modernization as rationalization) levels.
4. **Two-level society.** "Conceive of societies simultaneously as systems and lifeworlds": [[lifeworld]] (social integration via communication/norms) vs [[system-and-lifeworld|system]] (functional integration via [[steering-media]] of money and power).
5. **Colonization.** System imperatives penetrate communicatively structured domains → [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld]]; [[juridification]] as test case; new social movements along system/lifeworld seams.
6. **Reconstructed critical theory.** Escape the dead end of *[[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate|Dialectic of Enlightenment]]* by replacing totalized [[instrumental-reason]] with communicative reason; reformulate [[reification]] as system–lifeworld pathology rather than as fate of subject-centered reason.

## Intellectual Origins

Three lineages at once:

- **[[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt School]] [[critical-theory]]** — which Habermas argues stalled by generalizing reification into a total critique of instrumental reason.
- **Sociological classics** — [[weber-max|Weber]]'s [[occidental-rationalism]] and [[disenchantment]]; [[marx-karl|Marx]] on commodity form; [[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] on solidarity; Mead on communication; [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]] as both resource and foil.
- **Linguistic turn** — speech-act theory (Austin, Searle), Bühler, Wittgensteinian themes, formal pragmatics vs intentionalist semantics.

Vol. 1 path: history of theory with systemic intent (Weber → Lukács → Horkheimer/Adorno) + Intermediate Reflections on action and communication. Vol. 2 path: Mead/Durkheim → system/lifeworld → Parsons critique → Weber/Marx reconstruction → colonization.

## Empirical Program

Primarily **rational reconstruction** and history of theory, not new survey research. Testable diagnoses include: differential crisis forms of capitalist vs bureaucratic-socialist modernity; juridification of family and school as colonization; new social movements (ecology, peace, feminism, regional autonomy) as resistance; reinterpretation of Weber's loss of meaning/freedom as selective rationalization and colonization rather than fate of purposive reason *tout court*.

## Method Commitments

- Formal-pragmatic reconstruction of competences.
- Developmental-logic analogies (Piaget decentration; Kohlberg-adjacent moral development).
- Two-level methodology: participant (hermeneutic) perspective on the lifeworld + observer (systems) perspective — against collapsing either into the other (objection to late Parsons and to Luhmann).
- History of theory as systematic exploration, not antiquarianism.

## Major Debates and Rivals

- **vs systems theory** ([[luhmann-habermas-debate|Luhmann]], late Parsons): rejects dissolving lifeworld into autopoietic communication or subordinating action theory to systems theory.
- **vs first-generation Frankfurt School:** [[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]] — communicative vs instrumental reason as critical standard.
- **vs Marx:** labor theory of value "economistically foreshortens" critique; reification needs system/lifeworld frame.
- **vs subject-centered philosophy of consciousness:** paradigm shift to intersubjective linguistic communication (Vol. 1 Ch. IV climax).

## Critiques and Responses

- Idealization / rationalism (feminist, poststructuralist, Foucauldian).
- System/lifeworld dualism naturalizes economy and state.
- Eurocentrism of the Occidental rationalization narrative.
- Underplaying non-linguistic practice, embodiment, and affect (later practice-theory and feminist critiques).

Habermas's standing reply (attributed): formal pragmatics is reconstructive not foundationalist; the idealizations are counterfactual pragmatic presuppositions already operative in everyday speech, not utopian blueprints.

## Current Status

Central reference point of late-20th-century social theory; living afterlife in discourse ethics, deliberative democracy, public-sphere research, and critical social theory. Competes with and is partly absorbed into pragmatist, neo-institutionalist, and critical-realist programs. See Theory Hub for depth.

## Reflexivity

TCA is a document of West German critical theory after the collapse of Marxist philosophy-of-history guarantees. Habermas is explicit that "communicative action," like Marx's abstract labor, is a historically self-referential abstraction that "becomes true in practice" only under modern conditions of decentered world-understanding. The work is written against both neoconservative defense of tradition and growth-critical abandonment of the "project of modernity."
