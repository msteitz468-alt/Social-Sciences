## Overview

Charles Kimberlin "Bob" Brain (b. 1931) is a South African palaeontologist and, with [[binford-lewis|Lewis Binford]], a founder of modern **[[taphonomy|taphonomy]]** — the study of how bone assemblages form. His *The Hunters or the Hunted? An Introduction to African Cave Taphonomy* (1981) appeared the same year as Binford's *[[binford-bones-1981|Bones]]* and, from independent evidence, reached a convergent methodological conclusion: bone assemblages read as records of hominid behavior are often the work of carnivores and geological processes.

## Contributions

- **Actualistic reanalysis of the osteodontokeratic claim.** Brain's study (1967, 1969) of goat bones scavenged by dogs around Hottentot villages showed that the differential skeletal-part frequencies [[dart-raymond|Dart]] read as deliberate tool selection at [[makapansgat|Makapansgat]] could be produced by ordinary carnivore ravaging plus **density-mediated attrition** — denser bones survive, softer ones are destroyed — with no hominid selection required. This is a founding demonstration of [[actualistic-studies|actualistic method]] and a decisive blow to the [[osteodontokeratic-culture|osteodontokeratic culture]] hypothesis.
- **South African cave stratigraphy.** Brain established the well-resolved stratigraphy of [[swartkrans|Swartkrans]], making it a benchmark for taphonomic method in the southern African australopithecine caves.
- **Carnivore accumulation.** Brain argued much of the australopithecine and early-hominid bone in these caves reflects leopard and other carnivore predation and denning (the "hunted," not the "hunters") — inverting Dart's predatory killer-ape reading (see [[hunting-hypothesis]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Brain worked within the South African palaeoanthropological tradition (Broom, Robinson, Dart's legacy at the Witwatersrand) and became, alongside Binford, the reference point for taphonomic reasoning. Documented training relations not in the ingested source.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Brain's density-mediated-attrition framework was later formalized and extended (e.g., by R.L. Lyman) into quantitative bone-density studies; his leopard-predation model for Swartkrans is debated in detail but his general taphonomic caution is now standard. His convergence with Binford from different data (African caves vs. Nunamiut/Olduvai) is often cited as independent confirmation of the taphonomic turn.

## Historiography

*The Hunters or the Hunted?* is a canonical text of the [[hunting-scavenging-debate|hunting-versus-scavenging]] and taphonomy literatures, read alongside Binford's *Bones* as the paired 1981 foundations of the field.
