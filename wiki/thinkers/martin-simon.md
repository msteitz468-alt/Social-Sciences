# Simon Martin

## Overview

British Maya epigrapher and political historian; co-author with [[grube-nikolai|Nikolai Grube]] of [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008|*Chronicle of the Maya Kings and Queens*]] (2000; 2nd ed. 2008), the standard post-decipherment narrative of Classic dynastic politics. Primary densification in this vault is from that chronicle; fuller intellectual biography awaits dedicated sources.

## Contributions

- **Hierarchical Classic politics:** With Grube, reconstructs overking–vassal (*y-ajaw*) networks, foreign-witnessed accessions, and the [[tikal]]–[[calakmul]] rivalry as the organizing spine of Late Classic political history (attributed — Martin & Grube 2008).
- **Dynastic biographies:** Reign-by-reign accounts for eleven kingdoms from monuments and archaeology (see source page).
- **Emblem Glyph / holy lord framework:** Operationalizes [[emblem-glyph]] polities and [[kuhul-ajaw]] titles as the units of Classic political geography.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Part of the post-[[schele-linda|Schele]] / [[stuart-david|Stuart]] / [[houston-stephen|Houston]] generation of logosyllabic epigraphers who turned Proskouriakoff’s dynastic method into full historical narrative. Collaborator of [[grube-nikolai|Nikolai Grube]]. Detailed teacher/student links not established from the *Chronicle* alone.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Overkingship and “hegemony” readings of relationship glyphs remain models, not uncontested facts — alternative flatter multipolar reconstructions exist in the literature (record positions on site and culture pages; do not adjudicate here).
- Individual reign reconstructions and war narratives are only as strong as the underlying glyph readings and dating (Thompson correlation assumed).

## Historiography

*Chronicle* is widely cited as the accessible synthesis of Martin–Grube political geography; textbooks ([[coe-houston-the-maya-2015]], [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006]]) already summarize its “Great Game” frame. This page densified primarily from Martin & Grube 2008.
