# Peter Mitchell

Professor of African Archaeology, University of Oxford; Tutor and Fellow in Archaeology, St Hugh’s College. Leading synthesizer of southern and continental African archaeology; co-editor (with [[lane-paul]]) of *[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013|The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology]]* (2013). Author of *The Archaeology of Southern Africa* (CUP 2002; 2nd ed. 2024) — **not yet ingested** in this vault (acquired PDF was a mislabeled Pitcher monograph; correct copy still needed).

## Overview

Mitchell’s program places African archaeology inside world prehistory rather than as a residual “sub-Saharan” appendix: deep human origins, MSA behavioral complexity, Holocene hunter-gatherer diversity, herding and farming expansions, and second-millennium polities are treated as continental research agendas with global theoretical stakes. The 2013 handbook (ed. with Lane) is the vault’s multi-author method-and-region scaffold for Africa, densifying single-author surveys by [[phillipson-david-w|Phillipson]] and [[connah-graham|Connah]].

## Contributions

- **Handbook editorship:** with [[lane-paul]], structured the field into method/practice, hominin origins, hunter-gatherers, food production, towns/states, and modern-world-system historical archaeology (*[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]*).
- **Southern African synthesis:** *The Archaeology of Southern Africa* (2002/2024) — outstanding for ingest when a correct text is acquired.
- **Comparative Africa–world connections:** *African Connections* (2005) — not yet ingested.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Oxford African archaeology chair tradition; collaborates widely across Africanist specialisms represented in the handbook (Connah, Phillipson, Insoll, Chirikure, Smith, and many regional specialists). Reciprocal densification with [[connah-graham]], [[phillipson-david-w]], [[insoll-timothy]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Handbook chapters are specialist-authored; Mitchell’s editorial frame is synthesis and agenda-setting, not a single empirical thesis. Single-author southern African claims await the monographs themselves.
