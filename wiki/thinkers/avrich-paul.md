# Paul Avrich (1931–2006)

## Overview

American historian of Russian anarchism and mass revolt; Senior Fellow at Columbia's Russian (later Harriman) Institute. *[[avrich-kronstadt-1921|Kronstadt 1921]]* (Princeton 1970) is the wiki's first full ingest of his work — the standard English scholarly account of the March 1921 rising.

## Contributions

**Kronstadt 1921.** Independent archival reconstruction of the sailors' revolt: spontaneous mass protest under free-soviets slogans against [[war-communism]]; Secret Memorandum shows emigre hopes without proving White direction; NEP hastened not caused by the rising; suppression concurrent with faction ban and monopoly consolidation. See [[avrich-kronstadt-study]], [[kronstadt-debate]].

**Broader program** (not yet ingested): monographs on Russian anarchists and related revolts; Kronstadt presented as byproduct of that research program (Acknowledgments).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained at Columbia under Geroid T. Robinson, Henry L. Roberts, and Michael T. Florinsky (Acknowledgments). Engages Ida Mett and George Katkov as pioneering Kronstadt studies; libertarian memoirists (Berkman, Goldman, Serge) and Soviet military accounts (Pukhov) as sources to be triangulated.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Soviet historiography rejected his non-conspiracy reading. Libertarians sometimes wanted a harder martyr narrative; Trotskyists a harder necessity narrative. Post-Soviet archives may revise composition and casualty detail; his middle synthesis remains the English-language reference point as of the ingested text.

## Historiography

Harriman Institute series; Guggenheim and other fellowships for anarchism/mass-revolt research. Reputation as sympathetic but source-critical historian of the anti-authoritarian left.
