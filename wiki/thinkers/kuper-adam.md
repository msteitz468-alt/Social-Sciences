# Adam Kuper

British social anthropologist and historian of anthropological theory. Best known in this wiki (as of the 1988 ingest) for the critique of lineage theory and the book-length argument that “[[primitive-society|primitive society]]” was an invented, continually transformed disciplinary object rather than an empirical discovery.

## Overview

*[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|The Invention of Primitive Society: Transformations of an Illusion]]* (Routledge, 1988) grew from Kuper’s 1982 *Annual Review of Anthropology* essay on lineage theory (preface). Written Leiden and London 1982–7; earlier Maine/Morgan/Durkheim passages in journals; totemism chapter from a Henry Myers lecture. Explicitly “a story with a moral”: remove the constitution of primitive society from anthropology’s and political theory’s agenda.

## Contributions

- **Historiography of kinship theory:** reconstructed how legal evolutionism, totemism debates, Melanesian diffusionism, [[descent-theory]], and [[alliance-theory]] reworked shared assumptions about clan, exogamy, and “primitive” social structure as **transformations of an illusion**.
- **Critique of lineage model:** pronounced classical segmentary/lineage orthodoxy exhausted (1982 ARA; densified 1988 Ch. 10; see [[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]]). Famous formula: “Even the Nuer are not like *The Nuer*.”
- **Concept work:** treated [[primitive-society]] as an etic disciplinary construct and political foil (mirror image of modern society); three strengths of the model (ultimate concerns; puzzle-solving; transformability).
- **Alliance historiography:** argued [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]]’s cross-cousin marriage theory (*Folklore in the Old Testament* Vol. 2) was a more important direct source for Lévi-Strauss than Jakobson or Mauss alone (attributed — Kuper).
- **Closing diagnosis:** mainstream anthropology abandoned the primitive — and “society itself” — for the anthropology of **culture** (Tylor/Frazer line rather than Morgan/Rivers); hope to render the construct obsolete “in all its protean forms.”

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Engages (critically) [[maine-henry-sumner]], [[morgan-lewis-henry]], [[boas-franz]], [[rivers-w-h-r]], [[radcliffe-brown-a-r]], [[malinowski-bronislaw]], [[fortes-meyer]], [[evans-pritchard-e-e]], [[levi-strauss-claude]], [[leach-edmund]], [[needham-rodney]], and the Boasian and British functionalist traditions.
- Methodological frames: Kuhn paradigms; I.B. Cohen “transformations”; Lévi-Strauss mythic inversion — used to model anthropology’s own history as hermetic structural transformations that can circle back to start.
- Preface readers: Alan A. Stone, Gerhard Baumann, Isaac Schapera.
- Distinguish from [[kuper-hilda|Hilda Kuper]] (Swazi ethnography) — different scholar.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(To densify as reception sources are ingested.)* The book is itself a position in debates over whether classical kinship theory can be salvaged after Schneider and post-lineage critique. Kuper’s Frazer→Lévi-Strauss path and “illusion” framing are contested by readers who defend residual comparative value of descent/alliance tools.
