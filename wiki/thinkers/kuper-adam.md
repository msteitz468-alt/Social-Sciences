# Adam Kuper

British social anthropologist and historian of anthropological theory. Best known in this wiki for (1) the participant-observer historiography of [[british-social-anthropology|British social anthropology]] (*[[kuper-anthropology-and-anthropologists-2015|Anthropology and Anthropologists]]*, 1973; 4th ed. 2015) and (2) the critique of lineage theory and the book-length argument that “[[primitive-society|primitive society]]” was an invented, continually transformed disciplinary object (*[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|The Invention of Primitive Society]]*, 1988).

## Overview

Kuper entered the “tribe” as a King’s College, Cambridge research student in 1962, under the deep Fortes–Leach rift (Africanist / Radcliffe-Brownian vs Malinowskian–structuralist camps). Aunt [[kuper-hilda|Hilda Kuper]] (Malinowski student) first interested him in anthropology; elders he came to know well included [[fortes-meyer|Meyer Fortes]], [[schapera-isaac|Isaac Schapera]], [[richards-audrey|Audrey Richards]], [[douglas-mary|Mary Douglas]], and [[gellner-ernest|Ernest Gellner]]. After fieldwork years in Botswana and Uganda he returned in 1970; Schapera invited him to write an account of modern British social anthropology — the book that made his early reputation and, in revised form, remains a standard introduction to the British school.

## Contributions

### Historiography of the British school (*Anthropology and Anthropologists*)

- Frames the school as an **ethnography of natives who study natives** — personal rivalries, career structures, institutions, and Bourdieu’s “anthropological field” shaping the books.
- Baseline **1922**: Rivers’s death; *Argonauts* and *Andaman Islanders*; Lugard’s *Dual Mandate*; modernist “year one.”
- Narrates Malinowski vs Radcliffe-Brown parties; 1930s–40s shift function → structure; colonial elective affinity and [[applied-anthropology]]; postwar “charisma to routine” (ASA 1946; chair map); Leach and neo-structuralists; 1970s paradigm/identity crisis and cosmopolitan turn.
- **Fourth edition (2015)** treats the classical project as something of an **obituary**: distinctive British movement ~1920s–early 1970s, then absorption into cosmopolitan social anthropology.
- First edition’s **hostile reception** (attributed): [[mair-lucy|Lucy Mair]] refused to read it; [[gluckman-max|Gluckman]] furious correspondence; [[firth-raymond|Firth]] “not amused”; Oxford faction hostility / ~two decades “samizdat”; Roy Willis in *Man* (1975) defended desacralizing the founding fathers.

### Historiography of kinship theory (*Invention of Primitive Society*, 1988)

- Reconstructed how legal evolutionism, totemism debates, Melanesian diffusionism, [[descent-theory]], and [[alliance-theory]] reworked shared assumptions about clan, exogamy, and “primitive” social structure as **transformations of an illusion**.
- Critiqued lineage orthodoxy (1982 ARA; 1988 Ch. 10; [[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]]): “Even the Nuer are not like *The Nuer*.”
- Treated [[primitive-society]] as etic disciplinary construct and political foil.
- Closing diagnosis: mainstream anthropology abandoned the primitive — and “society itself” — for the anthropology of **culture**.

### Colonialism position (within *Anthropology and Anthropologists* Ch. 4)

Kuper argues (attributed **position**) that critics found no consistent pattern of shocking spy-collaboration; he favors analysis of elective affinity and loose symbiosis (Malinowski–Indirect Rule; IAI/Rockefeller; RLI) over conspiracy narratives — while granting the colonial setting as formative. Recorded on [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Formation:** Cambridge 1962– under Fortes/Leach dualism; Malinowski lineage via Hilda Kuper; Southern Africa fieldwork orientation (Schapera).
- **Engages (critically):** [[maine-henry-sumner]], [[morgan-lewis-henry]], [[boas-franz]], [[rivers-w-h-r]], [[radcliffe-brown-a-r]], [[malinowski-bronislaw]], Fortes, Evans-Pritchard, [[levi-strauss-claude]], Leach, [[needham-rodney]], and the British functionalist tradition as a whole.
- **Methodological frames for historiography:** Kuhn paradigms (explicit in the 2015 crisis chapter); I.B. Cohen “transformations”; Lévi-Strauss mythic inversion (*Invention*).
- Distinguish from [[kuper-hilda|Hilda Kuper]] (Swazi ethnography) — different scholar.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- *Anthropology and Anthropologists* read by some elders as irreverent personality gossip; by Willis and later readers as necessary desacralization.
- *Invention* is itself a position in debates over whether classical kinship theory can be salvaged after Schneider and post-lineage critique; “illusion” framing contested by those defending residual comparative value of descent/alliance tools.
- Colonial chapter’s relative-autonomy tilt competes with stronger determination theses (Asad, New Left, African intellectual critiques) — see debate page.
