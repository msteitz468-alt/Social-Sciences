# Vladimir Ilyich Lenin

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/lenin-vladimir|Thinkers Hub — Lenin]]

## Overview

Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov (**Lenin**, 1870–1924), Russian revolutionary Marxist, principal leader of the Bolshevik wing of Russian Social Democracy and of the October 1917 seizure of power. This page synthesizes the wiki’s **primary-text** holdings (Essential Works excerpts, *State and Revolution*, CW selections, etc.) with the first non-Soviet scholarly biography ingested: Robert Service, *[[service-lenin-2000|Lenin: A Biography]]* (2000). Program claims from Lenin’s own writings remain **attributed** to Lenin; life-course, personality, archival governance detail, and historiographic verdicts from Service remain **attributed** to Service.

## Contributions

### Political economy of Russian capitalism (1899, now ingested in full)

From *[[lenin-development-of-capitalism-in-russia-1899|Development of Capitalism in Russia]]* (attributed): home market created by capitalism itself through the differentiation of the peasantry and the social division of labour; peasant commune as petty-bourgeois field of disintegration; three stages of capitalist industry. **Composition history:** begun in earnest in prison after Lenin's arrest in connection with the St. Petersburg League of Struggle for the Emancipation of the Working Class, continued and finished in Shushenskoye exile — a January 2, 1896 prison letter already sketches the book's two-part plan ("A — the general theoretical part"; "B — application to Russian facts" via Zemstvo statistics). Published 1899 under the pseudonym "Vladimir Ilyin"; the 2nd edition (1907/8) added the "two paths" agrarian preface (Prussian vs. American types of capitalist agrarian development, later folded into the CW 13 agrarian-programme material above) and a census-based addendum reworking the 1897 national census into class categories. Lenin defended the book's realization/home-market theory against Marxist critics in **"Uncritical Criticism"** (1900, as "Vladimir Ilyin," Nauchnoye Obozreniye), replying to P. Skvortsov; the same article names Peter Struve and Mikhail Tugan-Baranovsky as representatives of a "new critical trend" in Russian Marxism, situating the dispute as a Russian echo of the Bernstein revisionism controversy and staking out his own definition of Marxist "orthodoxy" against it. See source page.

### Party and consciousness (1902)

From *What Is to Be Done?* (attributed): [[trade-union-consciousness]] vs [[consciousness-from-without]]; [[economism]]; [[professional-revolutionary]] / [[vanguard-party]] — see [[spontaneity-vs-consciousness-debate]].

### Reaction years: Duma, International, agrarian programme (1907–08)

From [[lenin-collected-works-vol-13-1962]] (attributed):

- **[[boycott-tactic|Boycott]]:** active boycott only under broad upswing toward uprising; **participate** in Third Duma for exposure (reject Cadet coalitions).
- **Stuttgart 1907:** against “socialist colonial policy”; against [[trade-union-neutrality]]; war crisis as opening for revolutionary struggle (as he frames the Congress poles).
- ***Preface to Twelve Years*:** legal Marxism → Economism → Menshevism as continuous Right tendency; defence of 1902 organisational line.
- **Agrarian programme:** two bourgeois agrarian paths (Prussian/American types); peasant movement and [[land-nationalisation]] vs [[municipalisation]] (Maslov et al.) — see [[nationalisation-vs-municipalisation-debate]]; Marx’s [[absolute-rent]] as theoretical stake; critique of agrarian revisionism (*Critics of Marx* continuation chapters).
- **1917 Postscript** (printed with the agrarian programme in CW 13): re-situates nationalisation under state-monopoly capitalism (later frame).

### Founding polemic (1894)

From [[lenin-what-the-friends-of-the-people-are-1894]] (attributed): the anti-Narodnik codification of [[historical-materialism]] as science (the Marx–Darwin analogy; material vs ideological relations), dialectics deflated to "the scientific method in sociology," the Voronezh-budget demonstration of peasant differentiation, and the closing 1894 forecast of the Russian worker leading "the victorious communist revolution." Written at 24; hectographed illegally; Part II lost.

### Philosophy: the Machist controversy (1908–09)

From [[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]] (attributed): the [[reflection-theory|reflection theory of knowledge]] — matter as "objective reality given in sensation," practice as criterion, absolute truth compounded of relative truths — forged against Bogdanov and the Russian Machists ([[empirio-criticism-debate]], [[bogdanov-alexander]]). Written in emigration (London/Geneva, 1908) while contesting Bogdanov's influence within the Bolshevik faction; "Ten Questions to a Lecturer" (May 1908) was its polemical trailer.

### 1917: April Theses and dual power

From [[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]] (attributed): arrived Petrograd April 3, 1917 (O.S.) via the sealed-train transit through Germany negotiated by Fritz Platten (which he defended publicly against collusion charges); delivered the April Theses April 4, published in Pravda April 7 over initial resistance inside his own party (Kamenev's Pravda objection; answered in "Letters on Tactics"). Coined [[dual-power]]; re-founded the party's program on the Commune-type state, land nationalisation, and Soviet power ("we are not Blancists" — majority first); named and rejected "Trotskyism: no tsar, but a workers' government" at the Petrograd City Conference.

### Insurrection, October, and the dissolution (Sept 1917–Jan 1918)

From [[lenin-impending-catastrophe-1917]] and [[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]] (attributed): the economic program (five control measures; [[state-capitalism]] as "threshold of socialism"); "Marxism and Insurrection" and the CC letters from hiding (incl. the resignation threat); "Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?"; the October 25–26 decrees; the Constituent Assembly theses and dissolution; the Brest-Litovsk three-faction split. The underground logistics (aliases "K. Ivanov"/"Huttunen," the Smilga letter marked "burn after reading") are primary-documented.

### The renegade polemic (1918) and the party crisis (1920–21)

From [[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]] (attributed): the definitional defense of dictatorship against [[kautsky-karl|Kautsky]] — "rule based directly upon force and unrestricted by any laws." From [[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]: the [[soviet-trade-union-debate]] against Trotsky, Bukharin, and the Workers' Opposition; the Tenth Congress factions ban; the NEP founding (tax in kind, concessions, the five economic structures).

### Comintern strategy (1920)

From [[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]] (attributed): the retrospective codification of Bolshevik tactics for the young Communist parties — iron discipline, compromises ("no compromises" as "childishness"), work in reactionary trade unions and parliaments, the "fundamental law of revolution," winning masses beyond the vanguard.

### Last writings (1922–23)

From [[lenin-last-writings-1923]] (attributed): the NEP re-foundation ("On Co-operation" — co-operatives plus proletarian power as "necessary and sufficient"; a "radical modification in our whole outlook on socialism"); "Our Revolution" on reversing the customary historical sequence; the Rabkrin articles on the unreformed state apparatus ("Better fewer, but better"); and the "Letter to the Congress" (Testament) — CC expansion, the Stalin–Trotsky split risk, the January 4, 1923 addition urging Stalin's removal as General Secretary, and the nationalities dictation laying responsibility for the Georgian affair on Stalin and Dzerzhinsky. Dictated through secretaries during his final illness; suppressed in the USSR until 1956.

### Philosophical Notebooks — private reading notes across three strata (1895; 1914–15; 1912–16)

From [[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]] (attributed; these are Lenin's **private** notes, never published in his lifetime — posthumously assembled 1929–33 across several *Lenin Miscellany* volumes, collected as CW Vol. 38 in 1961): three dated strata. **1895** — a youthful (age 25) conspectus of Marx and Engels's *The Holy Family* and of Feuerbach's book on Leibniz, written during Lenin's first stay abroad. **1914–15 (Bern exile)** — the core Hegel program: a September 1914 note on the collected works of Feuerbach and Hegel sets the reading list; the conspectus of Hegel's *Science of Logic* runs to December 17, 1914 ("End of the Logic"); Lenin worked at the Bern Cantonal Library; the fragment "On the Question of Dialectics" (1915) crystallizes the reading ("dialectics is the theory of knowledge of (Hegel and) Marxism"); a conspectus of Aristotle's *Metaphysics* follows in 1915. **1912–16** — ongoing tracking of the Avenarius/Mach literature and reviews of Hegel scholarship (Plenge, Ferd. J. Schmidt, R. B. Perry, Aliotta), continuing the *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* fight in notebook form well past the 1909 book's publication. First published in installments 1929–33; standard English edition CW 38 (1961). See [[dialectical-materialism]], [[reflection-theory]], [[plekhanov-georgi]], [[marx-karl]].

### Imperialism (1916/1917)

From *Imperialism* (attributed): monopoly stage; five features; [[finance-capital]] — see [[lenin-theory-of-imperialism]].

### Theory of the state (1917/1918)

From *State and Revolution* (attributed): irreconcilability; smash; [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]; withering — see [[marxist-theory-of-the-state]], [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]].

**Kronstadt (March 1921).** Lenin called the rising "the flash which lit up reality better than anything else" (quoted in [[avrich-kronstadt-1921]]); concurrent Tenth Congress advanced tax-in-kind/NEP and the faction ban. Independent narrative: [[kronstadt-debate]].

## Life-course and personality (as documented in Service 2000)

Archival biography *[[service-lenin-2000|Lenin: A Biography]]* (Robert Service, 2000; Pan 2002 paperback) is the wiki’s first non-Soviet scholarly life of Lenin. Service used post-1991 central party archives and uncut family/doctors/bodyguards memoirs. All judgments below are **Service’s** unless marked as documentary fact he reports.

### Family and formation

- **Birth and status (fact, Service):** Born 10 April 1870 (O.S.), Simbirsk; parents Ilya Nikolaevich Ulyanov (inspector then director of popular schools; hereditary noble) and Maria Alexandrovna Blank. Eight Ulyanov children (two died in infancy).
- **Ancestry (Service):** Maternal Blank line partly Jewish (great-grandfather Moshko Blank converted; Service stresses Moshko as *anti-semite*, against Soloukhin-type determinism); maternal German/Swedish Lutheran traces (Grosschopf); paternal Ulyanovs Astrakhan commercial family, ethnic mix uncertain (Russian / possible Volga non-Russian). Service: family were “new Russians” of diverse ancestry seeking incorporation into the Imperial official order — and ultimately failed after Alexander’s hanging.
- **Alexander Ulyanov (fact + Service thesis):** Hanged 8 May 1887 for the failed 1 March regicide plot. Service **rejects** the cult story that sixteen-year-old Vladimir said “we must not take that road” (anti-terrorist Marxist conversion); prefers Kashkadamova’s line that he said Sasha “couldn’t act in any other way,” then moved through agrarian-terrorist-idea circles before Marxism.
- **Education (Service):** Deep-but-narrow Classical gimnazia (Kerenski as headmaster — father of 1917 premier); gold medal 1887; Kazan University expulsion Dec 1887 after student unrest; external first-class law degree St Petersburg; Chernyshevski “ploughed him over and over”; *Uncle Tom’s Cabin* early favourite before Marx/Plekhanov.
- **Samara–Petersburg–Siberia (fact, Service):** Union of Struggle; arrest Dec 1895; Shushenskoe exile (“Siberian Italy”); married [[krupskaya-nadezhda|Nadezhda Krupskaya]] 10 July 1898; *Development of Capitalism in Russia* published as “Vladimir Ilin” (1899). Service: age ~30 already a “Lenin” in temperament — bookish, total confidence, pariah after Alexander, used family and wife as organisational support.

### Emigration, party, war

Service’s Part Two narrative (Iskra, 1903 Bolshevik/Menshevik split, 1905, second emigration, Bogdanov and *Materialism and Empirio-criticism*, Inessa Armand, wartime defeatism, Zurich) feeds the existing primary-text sections above. Service’s distinctive theses: Lenin as factional artist and “political warrior”; émigré disconnect from Russian masses; basic assumptions stable from the early 1890s while strategy shifted; Malinovski/Okhrana and Shmidt-money episodes as organisational texture.

### 1917–21: power, civil war, NEP (Service)

- **Counterfactual (Service position):** April Theses, October insistence, Brest-Litovsk, and NEP were strategical shifts without which the USSR would not have been established and consolidated as it was; colleagues could run the state when he was ill, but these pivots were his.
- **One-party state (Service vs *WITBD* teleology):** Controverts the claim that party-as-supreme-state-organ was already prescribed in 1902; archival evidence (Service) points to improvisation in 1918–19 from long-held assumptions (leadership, infallible policies, [[vanguard-party]]) — “one of the great malignancies of the twentieth century” created more by off-the-cuff measures than grandiose planning, yet “far from a complete accident.”
- **Terror (Service):** Preferred to overdo force; bloodshed “gave him no sleepless nights”; quoted to Gorki on the impossibility of calibrating necessary blows; Cheka, food dictatorship, and Civil War telegrams as institutional legacy. See [[red-terror]].
- **NEP and Kronstadt (Service):** Tax-in-kind Politburo 8 Feb 1921; concurrent Tenth Congress faction ban and Kronstadt suppression; Service calls Lenin’s *Pravda* framing of Kronstadt among his “most disgraceful travesties.” Independent scholarly narrative: [[kronstadt-debate]], [[avrich-kronstadt-1921]]. See [[new-economic-policy]].

### Illness, Testament, death (Service)

- Strokes 1922–23; secret “Letter to the Congress” (Testament) Dec 1922–Jan 1923: expand CC; Stalin as GenSec has unlimited power, “too crude,” propose removal; Trotsky ablest but administrative/self-confident; Zinoviev/Kamenev October “no accident.” Primary text: [[lenin-last-writings-1923]].
- Stalin’s phone abuse of Krupskaya (Dec 1922); Lenin’s March 1923 break-relations letter; Twelfth Congress — Testament not fully mobilised; Stalin kept GenSec.
- Died 21 Jan 1924, Gorki; mausoleum and cult (Service: Stalin prime advocate; Krupskaya and sisters colluded as “high priestesses”). Service closing line: “Lenin is not quite dead, at least not yet.”

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Claimed debts:** [[marx-karl|Marx]], [[engels-friedrich|Engels]]; Kautsky (quoted 1902; enemy 1916–17); Hilferding/Hobson (via *Imperialism*); Russian SD interlocutors (Plekhanov, Maslov, Economists, Mensheviks as Lenin names them); Stuttgart International debates.
- **Service’s non-Marxist streams (attributed):** Russian agrarian-socialist terrorists; Chernyshevski; Tkachëv/Nechaev (private admiration Service reports); childhood *Uncle Tom’s Cabin*; Gleb Uspenski on peasantry; later Machiavelli (via 1922 Molotov letter) and chance contacts (e.g. Gapon).
- **Household/organisational partners:** [[krupskaya-nadezhda]]; sisters Anna and Maria; mother Maria Alexandrovna as logistics base in exile years (Service).
- **Trained under / students:** not a university *Doktorvater* line; party training network (Service, Mawdsley & White).
- **Rivals named across ingested works:** Narodnik economists; Economists; Bernstein/Millerand; Mensheviks/municipalisers; Kautsky; Cadets/Octobrists; Bogdanov; later Stalin as succession antagonist.
- **Downstream Chinese reception (cross-link only):** [[mao-zedong|Mao]]'s *[[mao-quotations-from-chairman-mao-1966|Quotations]]* treats Marx–Engels–Lenin–Stalin as guides to action — see [[maoism]].


### Party elite and Central Committee (as documented in Mawdsley & White 2000)

Mawdsley and White treat the 1917–23 "revolutionary elite" (N=78 CC members/candidates) as Lenin's "old party guard" — the thin layer he told Molotov (1922) still determined proletarian policy. Their Ch. 1 epigraph is Lenin's 9th Congress (1920) gloss of [[democratic-centralism]] as congress verifying, removing, and naming the CC. His Testament (Dec 1922) proposed expanding the CC to 50–100 members for stability and against Stalin–Trotsky conflict. See [[soviet-central-committee]], [[mawdsley-white-soviet-elite-study]].

### Relation to Trotsky (as documented in Service 2009 and Service 2000)

Recruited [[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]] to *Iskra* in 1902 ("the Pen"); opponent after the 1903 split (Trotsky sided with Martov; his 1904 *Our Political Tasks* attacked Lenin's "substitutionism" — see [[vanguard-party]]); rivals through the "unifier" years; convergent from 1917 — Service (*Trotsky*, 2009) reads the April Theses as Lenin's substantive move toward Trotsky's [[permanent-revolution]] schema, uncredited (Service's position). Lenin's testament (Dec 1922–Jan 1923) called Trotsky "the ablest person" in the CC but overly administrative, and its codicil urged Stalin's removal as General Secretary. Their 1905 formulas differed on stages and the peasantry; at Brest-Litovsk Lenin's immediate-peace line defeated Trotsky's "neither war nor peace." Civil War: Trotsky as Military Affairs commissar / "Trotsky Train"; 1920–21 trade-union debate Lenin vs Trotsky militarisation; late 1922 tactical alliance on foreign-trade monopoly and Georgia. See [[service-trotsky-2009]], [[service-lenin-2000]].

### Relation to Stalin (as documented in Service 2000)

[[stalin-joseph]] as Nationalities commissar and GenSec (1922); Service’s narrative of autonomisation vs Union, Georgian affair, medical/poison promise, Krupskaya abuse, Testament, and cult manufacture — see Stalin page and hub. Primary Testament: [[lenin-last-writings-1923]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Social-Democratic / Menshevik / Kautskyist critiques of substitutionism, smash, dictatorship — see debate pages.
- Historical: Soviet practice vs pamphlet ideals.
- **Source asymmetry:** many opponent positions still mediated by Lenin’s framing until their own works are ingested.
- Agrarian: municipalisation and “critics of Marx” positions require independent reading of Maslov, David, etc.
- **Soviet canonization (Stalin 1938, attributed):** *[[stalin-dialectical-and-historical-materialism-1938|Dialectical and Historical Materialism]]* elevates Lenin's *Materialism and Empirio-Criticism* (1908–09; **now ingested** — [[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]) to the epistemological pillar of official [[dialectical-materialism]]: matter as "objective reality given to us in sensation," consciousness as reflection of being, "the brain is the organ of thought." With MEC ingested (2026-07-16), Lenin-as-philosopher is now primary-documented; the *Philosophical Notebooks* (CW 38) remain un-ingested.
- **Service 2000 reappraisal (position, not verdict):** Rejects Soviet/Trotskyist hagiography; rejects pure-doctrine (Harding), pure-psychopath (Pipes), late liberal reformer (Lewin/Cohen), wholly alien-to-Russia (Solzhenitsyn/Volkogonov), and Jewish-determinism (Soloukhin). Affirms ideology’s causal role and personal impact; attributes to Lenin lasting institutional and ethical-political legacies (dictatorship, terror, vanguard, one-party mono-ideology). Contested by other historians; recorded here as Service’s case.

## Carr 1979 — 1917–1923 political narrative

[[carr-edward-hallett|Carr]] (*[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979]]*) supplies a continuous secondary narrative of Lenin's last political years (attributed; complements primary CW pages and [[service-lenin-2000]]):

- **April–October 1917:** Arrival shatters dual-power compromise; April Theses; *State and Revolution* (party scarcely mentioned); October CC decision (Zinoviev/Kamenev dissent); Sovnarkom; Constituent Assembly dissolution.
- **Brest and civil war:** "Shameful" peace vs Left Communists (Bukharin); builds Red Army via Trotsky/specialists; poor-peasant committees as "October in the countryside"; labour discipline, piece-rates, Taylorism, one-man management.
- **Comintern:** Drive for Third International from 1914; founding 1919; *Left-Wing Communism* (1920) on parliament/unions; Warsaw 1920 optimism Carr judges costly.
- **NEP and tenth congress:** Tax in kind; "defeat" and "retreat—for a new attack"; faction ban; "dictatorship of the party" formulations.
- **Last days (Ch. 7):** Strokes (May 1922; Dec 1922; March 1923 speech loss); Testament critiques of Stalin, Trotsky, Zinoviev, Kamenev, Bukharin; Georgia intervention; death 21 January 1924.
- **Carr's book frame:** 1920s as transition personified from "revolution of Lenin" to "revolution of Stalin" — over-simplification that still names a real process.
