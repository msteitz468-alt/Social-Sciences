# Ashis Nandy

Indian psychologist and political/cultural critic (b. 1937). In this vault he is treated mainly via [[connell-raewyn|Connell]]'s *[[connell-southern-theory-2007|Southern Theory]]* (2007, ch. 8): *The Intimate Enemy* and related work as theory of colonialism's **psycho-cultural** violence and of how empire remade the **metropole** as well as the colony.

## Overview

As Connell reports Nandy: colonialism is not only political economy but a "state of mind" and intimate cultural relation; it restructured British masculinity and culture as well as Indian life. Gandhi appears as a figure who refused the player/counter-player terms of colonial modern politics. Nandy criticises the developmentalist science-state. Connell values the psycho-cultural insight and pairs it with institutional analysis (Subaltern Studies, Veena Das on Partition and Bhopal, limits of meaning under extreme violence).

## Contributions (as mediated by Connell 2007)

- Colonialism as mutual (asymmetric) cultural reconstitution — metropole affected, not only periphery
- Intimate Enemy framework for domination and resistance
- Critique of developmentalism and of science as pure emancipation

## Intellectual Genealogy

Indian postcolonial and post-Emergency intellectual field as Connell maps it; dialogue with Subaltern Studies and with feminist/ecological critiques (e.g. Shiva, as Connell notes). Primary Nandy corpus not yet ingested here.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Connell's closing note on ch. 8: psycho-cultural insight *plus* institutional analysis — neither alone suffices. Independent evaluation awaits primary ingest.
