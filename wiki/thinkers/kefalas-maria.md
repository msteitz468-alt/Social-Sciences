# Maria Kefalas

American sociologist; co-author with [[edin-kathryn|Kathryn Edin]] of *[[edin-kefalas-promises-i-can-keep-2005|Promises I Can Keep]]*, the multi-neighborhood ethnography of low-income unmarried mothers in Philadelphia and Camden.

## Overview

Kefalas’s contribution in the ingested source is collaborative fieldwork and co-analysis: nearly half of interviews and intensive fieldwork in five of eight neighborhoods conducted by the co-authors themselves (Appendix A). The book’s central interpretive claims — high marriage standards, semi-planned early fertility, “being there” as mothering valor — are joint.

## Contributions

- Co-developed the *Promises* argument that poor women put motherhood before marriage not from marriage-rejection but from protecting marriage’s meaning while seeking purpose through children ([[edin-kefalas-promises-study]]).
- Field methods: neighborhood intermediary networks, snowball and street recruitment, stratified race/ethnicity × age sampling.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator of Edin; interlocutor with Wilsonian structural accounts and culture-of-poverty debates via the *Promises* empirical program.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Same study-level limitations as Edin (sample, women’s-side accounts, city context) — see [[edin-kefalas-promises-study]].

## Historiography

Primary co-authored monograph *Promises I Can Keep* (2005; 2011 preface).
