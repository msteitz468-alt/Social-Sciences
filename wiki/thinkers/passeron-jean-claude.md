# Jean-Claude Passeron

## Overview

Jean-Claude Passeron (b. 1930) is a French sociologist, co-author with [[bourdieu-pierre|Pierre Bourdieu]] of the two founding texts of the French sociology of education — *Les Héritiers* (*The Inheritors*, 1964) and *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|La reproduction]]* (*Reproduction*, 1970) — and, with Monique de Saint-Martin, of the 1965 studies on pedagogical communication (*Rapport pédagogique et communication*). This vault's first primary ingest of his work is *Reproduction* (Nice English 1977; 2nd ed. 1990), co-authored with Bourdieu.

Passeron's later career diverged from Bourdieu's. In *Le raisonnement sociologique* (1991) he set out an epistemology of the social sciences as a "non-Popperian" natural language of proof, distinct from Bourdieu's programme — a break that makes the joint 1960s work a genuinely two-authored project rather than a Bourdieu monograph with a co-signatory.

## Contributions

From *Reproduction* (attributed jointly to Bourdieu & Passeron):

- **[[pedagogic-action]]** as "the imposition of a cultural arbitrary by an arbitrary power" — the founding proposition of the theory of [[symbolic-violence]].
- **[[cultural-arbitrary]]** — the content imposed by education, arbitrary because underivable from any universal principle.
- **[[pedagogic-authority]]** and **[[pedagogic-work]]** — the misrecognized legitimacy and the prolonged inculcation that make pedagogic action durable.
- **[[cultural-reproduction]]** — the school's reproduction of the class distribution of [[cultural-capital]], and through it the class structure.
- **[[self-elimination]]** — class-differentiated withdrawal from education before examination.
- The *more geometrico* propositional method of Book I, which Passeron's later epistemological work makes it tempting to attribute especially to him — though the division of authorial labour is not documented in the text.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Because *Reproduction* was received (and often criticized) as "Bourdieu's" book, Passeron's specific contribution is under-recorded in the Anglophone reception — a historiographical gap. The misreadings Bourdieu catalogues in the 1990 preface (mechanical determinism, neglect of change and of resistance) attach to the joint work; Passeron's own later methodological writing implicitly answers some of them by rejecting nomological ("law-like") readings of social-scientific claims. Full assessment awaits ingestion of *Le raisonnement sociologique* and *The Inheritors*.
