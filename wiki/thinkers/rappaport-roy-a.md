# Roy A. Rappaport

> Deep biography: [[hubs/thinkers/rappaport-roy-a|Thinkers Hub]]. Landmark study: [[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-study]]. Sources: [[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968]] · [[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979]] · [[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999]].

## Overview

Roy Abraham Rappaport (1926–1997) was an American cultural anthropologist whose *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* (1968; enlarged 1984) made the [[tsembaga|Tsembaga Maring]] pig-ritual cycle the type-case for **ritual as ecological regulation**. *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]* (1979) developed formal ritual theory, dual models, and sanctity. The posthumous *[[rappaport-ritual-religion-making-humanity-1999|Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity]]* (CUP 1999; foreword Keith Hart) is the late **summa**: religion as co-extensive with language and culture; ritual as the basic social act; [[cybernetics-of-the-holy]]; [[logos-rappaport|Logos]]/logoi; and the breaking of the Holy under modern power, idolatry, and law–meaning inversion. Fieldwork Oct 1962–Dec 1963 under NSF/Columbia with A. P. Vayda as PI.

## Contributions

- **Ritual regulation.** Tsembaga ritual (esp. [[kaiko]]) as homeostat and transducer — [[ritual-regulation]] (*Pigs*; 1967 *Ethnology*).
- **Cognized / operational models.** Dual models of environment — [[cognized-model]], [[operational-model]] (*Pigs* / EMR 1979).
- **Formal ritual theory.** Ritual = performance of more or less invariant sequences not encoded by performers; five features; self-referential vs canonical messages; acceptance ≠ belief; meta-performatives — [[liturgical-order]] (EMR; RRMH Chs. 2–5 full restatement).
- **Sanctity architecture.** [[sanctity|Sanctity]] as unquestionableness of unfalsifiable postulates; [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]]; sacred vs sanctified; [[deutero-truth]] vs sanctity (RRMH Chs. 9–10).
- **Time, hierarchy, Holy.** Liturgical construction of time, eternity, communitas; hierarchical liturgical dimension; numinous + sacred = Holy (RRMH Chs. 6–8, 12).
- **Adaptive structure & Cybernetics of the Holy.** Hierarchical adaptation; maladaptation as anomaly; closed loop of sanctity and corrective acceptance — [[adaptive-structure]], [[cybernetics-of-the-holy]] (RRMH Chs. 13–14).
- **Logos and the modern crisis.** Comparative logoi; oversanctification/idolatry; lies of oppression and diabolical lies; law–meaning dissonance; "post-modern science and natural religion" (RRMH Chs. 11, 14) — [[logos-rappaport]].
- **Quantitative ecological ethnography.** *Pigs* appendices; [[carrying-capacity]].
- **Response to critics.** EMR "Ills of Functionalism"; 1984 Epilogue — [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Columbia / Vayda human ecology (1960s); interlocutors Fried, [[harris-marvin|Harris]], [[mead-margaret|Mead]], Vayda, Paul Collins. RRMH dedication: Robert Levy, [[wolf-eric-r|Eric Wolf]], Mervyn Meggitt, Keith Hart. Heavy [[bateson-gregory|Bateson]] (deutero-learning, cybernetics, grace, map/territory). Peirce (index/icon/symbol); Austin (performatives); Otto/James/Durkheim (numinous/sacred); Turner (communitas). Between Stewardian [[cultural-ecology]] and systems approaches; Critical Turn interlocutors Friedman, [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Ecological functionalism / teleology charges; nutritional reanalyses; meaning-first critiques; power-first critiques of the Cybernetics of the Holy as soft legitimacy theory. RRMH intensifies the **normative** edge (reconciliation of science and religion; ecological Logos) — already flagged in Hart's foreword.
