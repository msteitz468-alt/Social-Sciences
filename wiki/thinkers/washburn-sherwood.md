# Sherwood L. Washburn (1911–2000)

American biological anthropologist, founder of the "new physical anthropology" and, with C. S.
Lancaster, author of "The Evolution of Hunting" — the fullest statement of the
[[hunting-hypothesis|"Man the Hunter" thesis]] in *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the
Hunter]]* (1968).

## Overview

*(Attributed — Washburn & Lancaster 1968.)* Washburn recast physical anthropology as an
experimental, evolutionary, behavior-oriented science integrated with primatology and
genetics. In "The Evolution of Hunting" he argued that hunting was "far more than a
technique... a way of life" whose success "dominated the course of human evolution," shaping
human intellect, emotions, the family, the [[sexual-division-of-labor|sexual division of
labour]], and (most controversially) a male psychology of aggression and the pleasure of
killing.

## Contributions

- **The "new physical anthropology"** (1951) — the reorientation of the field from
  static typology toward evolutionary process, function, and behaviour.
- **Primate field studies** (baboons, with [[devore-irven|DeVore]]) — establishing primate
  social behaviour as a comparative resource for human-origins research.
- **The [[hunting-hypothesis]]** — the argument (with Lancaster) that the hunting adaptation is
  the master key to human nature; a landmark and a lightning rod (see
  [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]]).
- In the symposium's discussions he argued against scavenging as an evolutionary stage prior to
  hunting and stressed the newly appreciated persistence of primate mother-offspring bonds.
- **Tool use and [[bipedalism]]** — [[leakey-richard|Leakey]] & Lewin's *[[leakey-lewin-origins-1977|Origins]]* (1977) reports Washburn's hypothesis that tool use was "the major force" driving hominids to habitual upright walking (attributed, Washburn per Leakey & Lewin 1977); the authors treat it as one factor among several in the multi-causal origin of [[bipedalism]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Washburn built the postwar Berkeley program in biological anthropology and trained a generation
of primatologists and human-evolution researchers, [[devore-irven|Irven DeVore]] among them. His
integration of fieldwork, anatomy, and evolutionary theory reshaped the discipline; his
speculative reach into human psychology drew the sharpest criticism.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Attributed.)* The aggression and androcentrism of the hunting thesis became a principal
target of feminist anthropology's "Woman the Gatherer" critique (Slocum 1975; Tanner &
Zihlman) and was contested at the symposium itself (Turnbull, Schneider). Current biological
anthropology retains Washburn's methodological program while rejecting the strong
hunting-as-sole-engine and innate-male-aggression claims (see [[hunting-hypothesis]]). A
contemporaneous popular challenge came from [[leakey-richard|Leakey]] & Lewin's
*[[leakey-lewin-origins-1977|Origins]]* (1977), which subordinated hunting to
[[food-sharing]] as the human-making adaptation (see [[food-sharing-hypothesis]]) and
attacked the hunting-derived "innate aggression" reading (see
[[human-aggression-killer-ape-debate]]).

## Historiography

Washburn is a standard reference for the history of physical anthropology and primatology. A
fuller treatment awaits ingestion of his methodological papers and the Berkeley record.
