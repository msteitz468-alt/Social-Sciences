## Overview

Rakhaldas Banerji (also spelled "R.D. Banerji" and "Rakal Das Banerji" in the
scholarly literature — treat as aliases pending a canonical-spelling check across the
wiki) was an Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) Superintendent who excavated
[[mohenjo-daro]] during the 1919–20 field season, one year before Daya Ram Sahni's
first season at Harappa. Birth and death dates are not given in the source ingested
here and are left `[[unknown]]`.

## Contributions

Banerji was D.R. Bhandarkar's successor as Superintendent Archaeologist. During his
1919–20 season at Mohenjo-daro, he correctly identified a Buddhist stupa on the site
(dated to roughly 150–500 CE) and noted the site's double-mound layout. His recovery
of a flint scraper led him to conclude the underlying settlement was substantially
older than the stupa, prompting further excavation; he subsequently found stamp seals
below the stupa level — the discovery that, together with parallel work at Harappa,
led toward [[marshall-john]]'s 1924 synthesis announcing the Indus Civilization.
Possehl credits Banerji as one of the discovery's key pioneers, alongside Marshall,
Daya Ram Sahni, Madho Sarup Vats, and K.N. Dikshit.

## Historiography

Banerji's 1919–20 recognition of Mohenjo-daro's antiquity — made a year before
Bhandarkar's earlier 1911–12 visit to the site had dismissed it as modern — is
recorded by Possehl as the pivotal on-the-ground discovery moment that, combined with
the parallel Harappa excavations under Sahni, enabled Marshall's 1924 public
announcement of the Indus Civilization.
