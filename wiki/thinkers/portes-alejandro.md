# Alejandro Portes

Cuban-born U.S. sociologist; co-author with [[rumbaut-ruben|Rubén G. Rumbaut]] of *[[portes-rumbaut-legacies-2001|Legacies]]* (2001) and a principal architect of [[segmented-assimilation|segmented assimilation]] and [[modes-of-incorporation|modes of incorporation]] theory in immigration sociology. CILS co-director (with Rumbaut).

## Overview

Portes's program links immigrant economic incorporation, ethnic enclaves (especially Cuban Miami), and second-generation adaptation. *Legacies* is the major co-authored empirical statement of the second-generation arm of that program.

## Contributions

- **[[segmented-assimilation]]** — multiple possible assimilation paths for the new second generation (mainstream, ethnic mobility, downward).
- **[[modes-of-incorporation]]** — government, society, and co-ethnic community as context for converting human capital.
- **Acculturation typology** — [[dissonant-acculturation]], [[consonant-acculturation]], [[selective-acculturation]].
- **CILS** — [[children-of-immigrants-longitudinal-study]] with Rumbaut; study [[portes-rumbaut-legacies-study]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator: [[rumbaut-ruben]]. Engages classical assimilation traditions, Gans on second-generation decline, urban underclass / labor-market dualism literatures. Full training lineage not yet documented from ingested sources (`trained_under: unknown`).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Segmented-assimilation predictions and the weight of downward assimilation are contested in later immigration sociology (to be expanded as rival sources are ingested). Miami/Cuban enclave empirics sometimes treated as exceptional rather than general.

## Historiography

*Legacies* (2001) and *Immigrant America* (multiple editions) are the public face of the program; journal articles on modes of incorporation and enclaves predate the CILS monograph.
