# C. Vance Haynes

North American geoarchaeologist central to [[clovis-culture|Clovis]] chronology and to critical evaluation of purported pre-Clovis sites. Named among the 1997 [[monte-verde|Monte Verde]] site-visit party (with [[meltzer-david|Meltzer]], [[dillehay-tom|Dillehay]], and others). Densified from [[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|Meltzer 2009]] (radiocarbon BP as Meltzer uses). **Not** Gary Haynes (separate figure in overkill debate).

## Overview

Haynes appears in the peopling literature as a rigorous standards-setter for stratigraphic and radiocarbon context — associated with defending high bars for pre-Clovis acceptance until site-quality criteria are met, and with the 1960s Clovis-first / ice-free-corridor synthesis that organized a generation of research.

## Contributions

### Clovis chronology and corridor model (1964)

Meltzer reports Haynes (1964) published secure ¹⁴C for half-dozen Clovis sites in a narrow **11,500–11,000 BP** slice; none >12,000 BP; none with pre-Clovis despite suitable underlying sediments. Haynes linked ~**12,000 BP** [[ice-free-corridor]] opening (after ~15,000 yr closed) to Clovis south of the ice — progenitors in Alaska ~12,500 BP, rapid continental spread <1,000 yr after corridor (C-3-043). This is a **position** in the [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]], foundational to mid-century Clovis-first.

### Geoarchaeology of Clovis kill/camp sites

- **Lehner Ranch** (San Pedro Valley): excavated after Haury; 13 mammoths (mostly calves/young adults), 13 Clovis points, scrapers/knives/chopper; serial killing of animals isolated from herds ~**10,950 BP** (C-6-059).
- **Murray Springs** (~16 km N of Lehner, ~**10,900 BP**): ≥1 mammoth and nearby 11 bison; camp with mammoth “drumstick,” bison filets, weapon repair; Haynes recovered points, bone shaft wrench, ~**15,000** manufacturing/resharpening flakes (C-6-060, C-6-012).
- Clovis type-site drought overlay: hints of brief severe Clovis-time western drought — strongly disputed (incl. Vance Holliday); 3 points in spring conduits read as offerings implying active/recent springs (C-6-058).
- Fagan layer: accepts rapid Clovis movement but rejects Martin population boom-crash; adds climatic overlay (falling water tables concentrating game) — see [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]].

### Pre-Clovis critique standards

- **Tule Springs, NV:** artifacts, “broken” megafauna, 28,000 BP date collapsed under blue-ribbon work — “hearths” organic blackening with unburned snails; bone breakage from spring-vent trampling/groundwater; only secure artifacts <12,000 BP. Haynes still saw “good indications” of people before 12,000 BP in 1964 but by 1969 cooled — “seeds of skepticism” (C-3-044).
- **Calico Hills:** with NGS evaluation team, argued “artifacts” are geofacts from natural gravel crusher; “hearth” not heated (C-3-050).
- **[[meadowcroft|Meadowcroft]]:** foremost among skeptics suspecting PA coal-country ancient carbon contaminated ¹⁴C; dismissed Goldberg microstratigraphy finding no saturation/coal without explanation (per Meltzer); demanded short-lived seeds/nuts dates [[adovasio-james|Adovasio]] refused as lose-lose (C-3-064). Dallas Museum La Caverna vote aftermath: Haynes asked Adovasio for one more seed date → Adovasio explosion (C-3-079).
- **Monte Verde 1997:** site visit participant; initially part of consensus MVII archaeological and 12,500 years old. Later claimed only six unequivocal artifacts (N. American Paleoindian-point lookalikes); Meltzer treats as red herring (C-3-078, C-3-080). Haynes quote on site visits: “one day on the site… worth all the words” (C-3-077).
- Standards tradition Meltzer formalizes as unmistakable artifacts + secure geology + reliable ages — “finally met at Monte Verde in 1997” (C-1-042, C-3-047).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Clovis geoarchaeology network (Haury, San Pedro valley); interlocutor of Adovasio (Meadowcroft), Dillehay (Monte Verde), Meltzer (visit statement, dating debates). Distinct from **Gary Haynes** (elephant taphonomy / overkill defense with Fiedel) — do not conflate.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Positions on individual sites are empirical claims — record as attributed, not as wiki-voice verdicts. Meltzer’s narrative is double-edged: Haynes-type standards protected the field from geofacts and mixed deposits, yet post-visit Monte Verde second thoughts and Meadowcroft coal critique are contested by site excavators and by Meltzer’s own acceptance of Monte Verde. Corridor-timed Clovis-first chronology was revised by pre-Clovis acceptance and corridor habitability timing (as of Meltzer 2009 geology).
