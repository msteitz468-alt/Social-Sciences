# Paul Bahn

## Overview

Paul G. Bahn (b. 1953) is a British archaeologist and co-author, with [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]], of the standard textbook *[[renfrew-bahn-archaeology-2012|Archaeology: Theories, Methods, and Practice]]* (1st ed. 1991; 6th ed. 2012). Within that volume he is the co-architect of a global survey of field methods, preservation case studies, Palaeolithic art boxes, and public/heritage archaeology. Fuller independent biography is not yet in the vault; densification here rests on the co-authored textbook.

## Contributions

- **Textbook synthesis.** Six editions of *Archaeology: Theories, Methods, and Practice* with Renfrew make Bahn a principal pedagogical voice for Anglophone method and theory teaching. Chapters and box features treat survey, excavation, dating, environmental and subsistence archaeology, technology, exchange, bioarchaeology, explanation, public archaeology, and CRM as a unified curriculum (attributed, Renfrew & Bahn 2012).
- **Palaeolithic art / cognitive illustration.** The textbook's art and depiction material (used inside [[cognitive-archaeology]] chapters) draws on Bahn's longstanding Palaeolithic-art expertise as reported in the co-authored synthesis (attributed).
- **Public and heritage archaeology.** [[public-archaeology]], [[heritage-management]], and [[cultural-resource-management]] chapters (with Renfrew) survey identity politics, NAGPRA/repatriation, looting, war damage, UNESCO, and career paths (attributed).
