# Mark Lehner

American Egyptologist and field archaeologist; long-time director of the **Giza Plateau Mapping Project (GPMP)**. Best known outside specialist literature for *[[lehner-complete-pyramids-1997|The Complete Pyramids]]* (Thames & Hudson 1997), a synthetic survey of Egyptian pyramid building, exploration history, and construction archaeology.

## Overview

Lehner’s program combines **monument-scale recording** (including multi-year mapping of the Great Sphinx, 1979–83) with **settlement archaeology** of the people who built and served the pyramids — especially the Heit el-Ghurab (“Lost City”) workers’ settlement south of the Giza plateau. *The Complete Pyramids* packages that field knowledge with a whole-corpus catalogue from Predynastic antecedents through Late Antiquity.

## Contributions

- **Giza as lived landscape:** pyramids, Sphinx, harbors/causeways, elite mastabas, and workers’ town as one planned system rather than isolated monuments — geology (Mokattam strike / Giza diagonal), cardinality, and complex formalization at [[giza]] (attributed, Lehner 1997 and GPMP program).
- **Sphinx recording:** five-year architectural survey (1979–83) with photogrammetry (Ulrich Kapp) and hand mapping; later digital surface models; *akhet*/horizon reading of solstice sunset from the Sphinx Temple between the two largest pyramids (INTERPRETATION, Lehner 1997).
- **Construction and labour synthesis (“Living Pyramid”):** quarries, transport, ramp and levering hypotheses, survey/alignment (including diagonal control to the pyramidion), workforce organization (crew–gang–phyle–division; ~20–30k accepted range as POSITION), and pyramid towns as landlord/estate institutions (*niut*/*hut*, *hut ka*, Palermo Stone colonization figures for Sneferu) (attributed, Lehner 1997).
- **Settlement archaeology at [[heit-el-ghurab]]:** bakeries (*bedja*), fish processing, Wall of the Crow landscape package — empirical base for provisioning arguments.
- **Experimental archaeology:** NOVA pyramid-building experiment with Roger Hopkins and Egyptian masons (small pyramid ~9 m base / ~6 m high, 186 stones, 44 workmen) testing hauling, levering at the top, and feasibility against “impossible statistics” rhetoric (Lehner 1997).
- **Whole-corpus catalogue:** Predynastic antecedents ([[hierakonpolis]], [[abydos]]) through Djoser ([[saqqara]]), Sneferu ([[meidum]], [[dahshur]]), Giza, [[abusir]], MK ([[lisht]], Illahun/[[kahun]]), NK and Nubian/Late Antiquity pyramids.
- **1984 radiocarbon sampling** of organic material in pyramid fabric with Robert Wenke (University of Washington) — method note, not a full re-dating of the OK chronology in the 1997 book.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborations and interlocutors named in or around the 1997 synthesis include Robert Wenke (1984 radiocarbon), Roger Hopkins (NOVA), Zahi Hawass and Egyptian Antiquities colleagues, and a wider Egyptological network ([[petrie-flinders|Petrie]], Reisner, Borchardt, Lauer, Stadelmann, Dieter and Felix Arnold as reported predecessors/interlocutors). [[kemp-barry-j|Barry Kemp]] cites Lehner among collaborators on Egyptian urbanism and provider-state archaeology; Lehner in turn cites Kemp’s Illahun grain-ration estimates alongside Arnold’s MK workforce figures.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Popular pyramid literature and alternative-history claims (lost civilizations, non-tomb functions as exclusive purpose) are a standing reception context; Lehner’s 1997 volume explicitly positions archaeological construction and ritual-complex evidence against “ancient mysteries” framing in its subtitle. Specialist debate continues on ramp systems, workforce scale/status, and fine chronology — record as positions, not wiki-voice verdicts.
