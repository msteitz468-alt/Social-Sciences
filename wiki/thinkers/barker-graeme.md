# Graeme Barker

## Overview

British archaeologist specializing in the comparative archaeology of agricultural origins and early farming systems. Trained at Cambridge under Eric Higgs’s early-history-of-agriculture research project; later held posts including at the University of Leicester and Cambridge. Global synthesis *[[barker-agricultural-revolution-prehistory-2006|The Agricultural Revolution in Prehistory]]* (2006) is his major statement on why foragers became farmers.

## Contributions

- **Global comparative forager→farmer synthesis (2006):** multi-regional independent husbandry at the Pleistocene–Holocene boundary; argues against simple large-scale [[demic-diffusion]] as the primary explanation of agricultural spread (engages [[bellwood-peter]], [[early-farming-dispersal-hypothesis]]); stresses forager world-views, stress-preservation decisions, mutualism/commensalism in [[domestication]], and cosmological shifts (animism→theism) alongside economy. See [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]], [[agricultural-revolution]].
- **European early farming:** *Prehistoric Farming in Europe* (1985); Ph.D. on central Italian transitions under Higgs.
- **Field programs** linking landscape archaeology to subsistence transitions (N. Africa, Jordan, Borneo).

## Intellectual Genealogy

**Teachers / formation:** Eric Higgs and the British Academy early-history-of-agriculture project at Cambridge (Barker’s own account, 2006 preface). **Peers and interlocutors** named as formative in the 2006 acknowledgements include (among others) Robin Dennell, Clive Gamble, David Harris, Charles Higham, Ian Hodder, Martin Jones, Steve Mithen, Colin Renfrew — intellectual network rather than formal lineage. **Students:** not documented from this source alone.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

As of the vault, Barker 2006 is secondary synthesis dated to mid-2000s evidence; regional chronologies and demic/admixture genetics have been revised by aDNA (post-2010). His anti-demic stance should be read as a **position** against [[bellwood-first-farmers-2005]]-style farming/language dispersal, not as wiki-voice adjudication. Regional specialists will contest any single-author global survey’s coverage depth.
