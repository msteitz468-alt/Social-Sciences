# Marilyn Strathern

Dame Marilyn Strathern (b. 1941) is a British anthropologist of Melanesia (Mount Hagen / Melpa, Papua New Guinea) and a major theorist of gender, exchange, and personhood. Primary-ingested: *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]* (1988) — study [[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study]]; hub [[hubs/studies/strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study|Studies Hub]].

## Overview

Strathern's career combines Highlands ethnography with systematic critique of Western analytical categories. With Carol MacCormack she argued that nature/culture is not a cross-cultural universal frame ([[nature-culture-gender-debate]]). *The Gender of the Gift* elaborates a Melanesian alternative sociality — [[dividual]] persons, gift vs commodity root metaphors, [[mediated-unmediated-exchange]], and domination as particular acts of excess — while treating anthropological writing itself as experimental form.

## Contributions

- **Nature/culture critique** (*Nature, Culture and Gender*, 1980): Western folk categories cannot ground universal female subordination (with MacCormack; against early [[ortner-sherry|Ortner]]).
- **[[dividual|Dividual]] personhood** (*Gender of the Gift*, 1988): persons as composite of relations; social microcosm; androgyne/individual alternation.
- **[[gender-of-the-gift|Gender of the gift]]**: gift exchange as gendered activity; gender of analytical concepts.
- **[[mediated-unmediated-exchange]]**: partible mediated gifts vs direct unmediated effects within gift sociality.
- **Feminist–anthropological mutual interrogation**: feminism as critical distance on anthropology and vice versa — neither simply subsumed.
- Later (not yet primary-ingested): *Partial Connections*; *After Nature* (English kinship, NRTs).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Dense, deliberately anti-summary style is itself a critical target; defenders read it as refusal of Western analytic shortcuts. Risk of reifying Melanesia as inverse of the West is a standing caution. Her critique remains the standard counterweight to Ortner in gender-and-culture teaching.
