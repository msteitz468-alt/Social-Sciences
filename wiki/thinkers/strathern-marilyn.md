# Marilyn Strathern

Dame Marilyn Strathern (b. 1941) is a British anthropologist of Melanesia (Mount Hagen / Melpa, Papua New Guinea) and a major theorist of gender, exchange, personhood, and **comparative method after reflexivity**. Primary-ingested: *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]* (1988) — study [[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study]]; hub [[hubs/studies/strathern-gender-of-the-gift-study|Studies Hub]] — and *[[strathern-partial-connections-1991|Partial Connections]]* (1991; AltaMira updated ed. 2004/5) — study [[strathern-partial-connections-study]].

## Overview

Strathern's career combines Highlands ethnography with systematic critique of Western analytical categories. With Carol MacCormack she argued that nature/culture is not a cross-cultural universal frame ([[nature-culture-gender-debate]]). *The Gender of the Gift* elaborates a Melanesian alternative sociality — [[dividual]] persons, gift vs commodity root metaphors, [[mediated-unmediated-exchange]], and domination as particular acts of excess — while treating anthropological writing itself as experimental form. *Partial Connections* extends the method problem: **scale, proportion, and [[partial-connections|partial connections]]** after [[writing-culture]], using Haraway’s cyborg, Cantor-dust imagery, and Melanesian trees/flutes to reopen comparison without holist units ([[postplural]] perception).

## Contributions

- **Nature/culture critique** (*Nature, Culture and Gender*, 1980): Western folk categories cannot ground universal female subordination (with MacCormack; against early [[ortner-sherry|Ortner]]).
- **[[dividual|Dividual]] personhood** (*Gender of the Gift*, 1988): persons as composite of relations; social microcosm; androgyne/individual alternation.
- **[[gender-of-the-gift|Gender of the gift]]**: gift exchange as gendered activity; gender of analytical concepts.
- **[[mediated-unmediated-exchange]]**: partible mediated gifts vs direct unmediated effects within gift sociality.
- **Feminist–anthropological mutual interrogation**: feminism as critical distance on anthropology and vice versa — neither simply subsumed; in PC, awkward **partition** and prosthetic extension rather than complementary halves.
- **[[partial-connections]] / [[postplural]]** (*Partial Connections*, 1991): relations through partition; comparison after Writing Culture without taxonomy/map holism; scale-switching multiplies and loses information so complexity keeps its own scale; Melanesian cyborg vs Haraway’s machine-hybrid.
- Later (not yet primary-ingested): *After Nature* (English kinship, NRTs; “merographic” connection distinguished in PC 2004 Preface).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Dense, deliberately anti-summary style is itself a critical target; defenders read it as refusal of Western analytic shortcuts. Risk of reifying Melanesia as inverse of the West is a standing caution. Her critique remains the standard counterweight to Ortner in gender-and-culture teaching.

## Historiography

*Gender of the Gift* remains the gift/gender/personhood landmark; *Partial Connections* is the method/comparison landmark. Primary base for this vault: 1988 UC Press + 1991/2004 AltaMira ingests. ## Reception in Sahlins, *What Kinship Is* (2013)

*(Attributed — [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] 2013.)* Hagen socialization: child's mind becomes visible when showing feeling for related persons and appreciating interdependence/reciprocity — a "gloss of mutuality" on unequal relationships (*Gender of the Gift* 1988, as cited). Feeding/eating as relational (not mere self-replacement); husband knows wife's menstrual magic through the appearance of his skin. Sahlins places Strathern within the constructivist orthodoxy that [[mutuality-of-being]] synthesizes; also curb on over-enthusiasm for mutuality as total fusion.
