# Nancy Fraser

## Overview

Nancy Fraser is an American critical theorist (New School) working on justice, feminism, and capitalism. This page is grounded primarily in her contributions to *[[fraser-honneth-redistribution-or-recognition-2003|Redistribution or Recognition?]]* (2003) with [[honneth-axel|Axel Honneth]]. Debate: [[redistribution-recognition-debate]].

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Fraser in the 2003 exchange.)*

- **Two-dimensional justice:** neither redistribution nor recognition alone; both are necessary and irreducible.
- **[[status-model-of-recognition]]:** recognition injustice as status subordination (institutionalized patterns of cultural value), not only damaged identity psychology.
- **[[participatory-parity]]:** normative standard — justice requires social arrangements that permit all adult members to interact as peers.
- **Perspectival dualism:** class (distribution) and status (recognition) as analytically distinct but practically entwined orders in capitalist society.
- Against Honneth: refuses to subsume distribution under a single recognition order or reduce political sociology to moral psychology.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Frankfurt-school critical theory; feminist theory; dialogue with Habermas; exchange partner and rival of Honneth in this volume. Related to but distinct from Young’s [[politics-of-difference]] (difference/oppression vs Fraser’s two-dimensional justice).
