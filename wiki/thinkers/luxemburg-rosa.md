# Rosa Luxemburg

## Overview

Rosa Luxemburg (1871–1919), Polish-German Marxist theorist and revolutionary of the SDKPiL and German SPD/USPD/Spartacus tradition, is treated here primarily as she appears in [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]]’s *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]*: the essays “The Marxism of Rosa Luxemburg” (January 1921) and “Critical Observations on Rosa Luxemburg’s ‘Critique of the Russian Revolution’” (January 1922). Full primary ingest of her monographs is **not** yet done. Claims about her own positions are **attributed via Lukács’s reading** (or standard bibliographic identification), not as wiki-voice reconstructions of her corpus.

## Contributions

**As praised by Lukács (1921 essay):**

- **Totality method.** Luxemburg’s *Accumulation of Capital* continues the unfinished total process of Marx’s *Capital* — imperialism, world market, and non-capitalist strata as necessary moments — against incomplete economic systems and against Austro-Marxist/Centre treatments (Bauer et al.) that Lukács reads as Proudhonist fatalism plus ethics.
- **Anti-revisionism.** Reform or Revolution and related work as defence of revolutionary dialectic against Bernsteinite evolutionism — aligned with Lukács’s orthodoxy-as-method.
- **Mass strike and class action** as living total process (referenced in the political essays’ orbit).

**As criticised by Lukács (1922 essay on her *Russian Revolution* critique):**

- Overestimation of **organic** historical development / underestimation of conscious party intervention.
- Insufficient grasp of agrarian and national questions as “impure” necessities of the Russian conjuncture.
- Treatment of soviets, democracy, and freedom that, in Lukács’s view, risks making freedom an abstract value and underestimating dictatorship’s function-change of legality.
- Spontaneism as a residual danger (sharpened further in *Tailism and the Dialectic*, per Löwy — not Luxemburg’s own page’s primary source).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Second International Marxism; Polish and German party struggles; opponent of Bernstein and of war credits; murdered January 1919 after the Spartacus uprising.
- Lukács’s wartime acquaintance with her work (1967 preface); she becomes both model of totalising Marxism and, posthumously, object of Bolshevik-aligned correction in *HCC*.
- Later reception: feminist, anti-colonial, and council-communist reclamations of Luxemburg often reverse Lukács’s 1922 corrections — **positions**, not settled here.

- **Scott's reclamation (1998):** [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]], in *[[scott-seeing-like-a-state-1998|Seeing Like a State]]* Ch. 5, reads Luxemburg (with Kollontai) as the anti-[[high-modernism|high-modernist]] pole against Lenin's *What Is to Be Done?* blueprint: revolution as "a vibrant part of life in flesh and blood," unplannable by a vanguard, and "the errors made by a truly revolutionary labor movement... historically infinitely more fruitful and valuable than the infallibility of the best of all possible 'central committees.'" Her 1918 warning that suppressing free elections, press, and assembly would make public life "fall asleep" is quoted at length; Scott frames her epistemology of learning-by-experience as [[metis]]. He also notes what she shared with Lenin: no "sociology of the party" — neither imagined the party's interests diverging from workers'. Attributed positions via Scott's readings of "Mass-Strike," "Organizational Questions," and "The Russian Revolution."

### In the reproduction-schemas debate (via Mandel 1978)

Ernest Mandel's introduction to *[[marx-capital-volume-2-1885|Capital, Volume II]]* (Fernbach translation, 1978) situates Luxemburg's *Accumulation of Capital* (1913) as the source of the [[reproduction-schemas-debate]]. Luxemburg argued that the accumulated (capitalized) portion of surplus-value cannot be realized through sales to workers (whose purchasing power derives from capital), to other capitalists (who cannot enrich the capitalist class as a whole by trading among themselves), or to "third persons" whose revenue itself derives from surplus-value; realization of accumulated surplus-value therefore requires external, non-capitalist markets. She concluded that capitalism, by progressively destroying the non-capitalist milieu it depends on for this realization, undermines the conditions of its own growth — its "absolute limit." Contemporary critics — Otto Bauer, Anton Pannekoek, G. Eckstein, Helene Deutsch, and later Nikolai Bukharin (*Imperialism and the Accumulation of Capital*) and Henryk Grossmann — disputed her solution, none of them established with wiki pages here.

Mandel's own adjudication (attributed to Mandel, not endorsed in wiki voice) is three-leveled: (1) Luxemburg illegitimately poses a "many capitals"/competition-level realization problem within the "capital in general" level of abstraction where, per Mandel, no such problem can arise by definition; (2) several later mathematical models show full realization of surplus-value is theoretically possible even under a rising organic composition of capital, contra her claim that "pure" capitalism is impossible — though Mandel also faults her "harmony theorist" critics for over-claiming smooth reproduction is thereby guaranteed; (3) historically, Mandel judges Luxemburg "fundamentally correct" that capitalism was born in, and has continuously plundered, a non-capitalist milieu — a contribution to anti-colonial and anti-imperialist literature he calls unequalled since. Mandel's verdict: "we cannot say baldly that she is right or that she is wrong... she certainly poses relevant questions... which Volume 2 does not and cannot answer." Luxemburg's own *Accumulation of Capital* remains uningested as a primary source; this section relies entirely on Mandel's secondary account.

### Relation to Trotsky (via Service 2009)

Occasional political ally of [[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]] (both opposed SPD indulgence toward bourgeois parties at the 1907 London Congress; both in wartime anti-war politics) but privately scathing about his conduct in the party-unity disputes — writing to Luise Kautsky that "The good Trotsky is more and more exposed as a rotten fellow." Her murder after the January 1919 Spartacist uprising (with Liebknecht and Jogiches) reached Moscow as a shock; the Bolshevik leadership had not been consulted on the rising ([[service-trotsky-2009]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Lukács’s double gesture — celebrate method, correct revolution theory — is itself a historiographical object. Löwy notes that *HCC* still attempts a Luxemburg–Lenin synthesis, whereas *Tailism* reduces her to pure spontaneism. Without her primary texts ingested, this page does not adjudicate accumulation theory or the Russian Revolution critique on independent grounds.
