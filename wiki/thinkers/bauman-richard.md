# Richard Bauman

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist and folklorist; co-editor with [[sherzer-joel|Joel Sherzer]] of *[[bauman-sherzer-explorations-ethnography-speaking-1974|Explorations in the Ethnography of Speaking]]* (1974), the first major multi-case volume of ethnographies of speaking under the Hymes–Gumperz program. Central to the **performance** turn linking folklore and the ethnography of speaking. With [[briggs-charles-l|Charles Briggs]], co-author of the Locke/Herder chapter in [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language* (2000)]], reading modern language philosophy as [[language-ideology|language ideology]].

## Contributions

- Co-editing *Explorations* (Austin 1972 conference → 1974 volume): framework of speech community as organization of diversity; resources; ground rules; **performance** as nexus.
- Bridging [[ethnography-of-speaking]] and verbal-art / folklore performance analysis.
- With Briggs (*Regimes* ch. 4): **metadiscursive practices** as “the capacity of discourses to represent and regulate other discourses” (regimentation of dominant discourses); Locke (purified rational-scientific language; monoglot public sphere) vs Herder (*Naturpoesie*, *Volk*, national spirit in language) as convergent “one voice in one language” projects that naturalize elite positions.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Program:** [[hymes-dell|Dell Hymes]] (mentor figure for volume); collaboration with [[sherzer-joel]]; later with [[briggs-charles-l]].
- Network overlaps [[gumperz-john-j]], Labov, Ferguson (SSRC Committee on Sociolinguistics); SAR 1994 language-ideologies seminar cohort.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Survey-volume editorship compresses individual case depth; performance theory after 1974 is a larger Bauman corpus not fully ingested here. Locke/Herder reading is historical-semiotic interpretation, not intellectual history from philosophy departments.
