# G. W. Dimbleby

Environmental archaeologist at the Department of Human Environment, Institute of Archaeology, London; co-editor with [[ucko-peter-j|Peter J. Ucko]] of *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|The Domestication and Exploitation of Plants and Animals]]* (1969).

## Overview

Dimbleby’s co-authored introduction with Ucko situates [[domestication]] studies as necessarily environmental and multi-disciplinary, critiques desiccation-centered narratives of Near Eastern agricultural origins, and stresses process, multi-regional recurrence, and the revision of Childe’s [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] under new dating and paleoenvironmental evidence (as of Ucko & Dimbleby 1969).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Ucko & Dimbleby 1969.)* Joint editorial framing of the seminar: standardize taxonomic nomenclature for the volume; assemble environmental, botanical, zoological, and archaeological papers; argue that overall aridity signals at sites need not equal overall climatic aridification (human land-use can extend near-desert conditions — Raikes cited).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Institute of Archaeology (London) human-environment tradition; seminar network of 1968. Detailed training lineage not yet in the vault.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Period environmental claims (pollen sequences, absolute ages) are snapshots as of late 1960s; later work revises regional climate histories — attribute and date-stamp.
