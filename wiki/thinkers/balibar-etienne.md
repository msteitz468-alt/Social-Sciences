# Étienne Balibar (b. 1942)

## Overview

French philosopher; student and collaborator of [[althusser-louis|Louis Althusser]] at the École Normale Supérieure. Principal author of **Part Five** of *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* (*Lire le Capital*, 1965) — “On the Basic Concepts of Historical Materialism” — the volume’s systematic reconstruction of [[mode-of-production]], the [[combination-combinatory|combination of elements]], reproduction, and transition. Co-editor of later complete editions; author of the 2015 Complete Edition **Presentation** (seminar history, edition history, four intertwined projects of the book). Later work on citizenship, race, and borders is outside this primary ingest.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — *Reading Capital* Part Five and Presentation.)*

- **Mode of production as epistemological break** with the philosophy of history; generality of the concept incompatible with dogmatic or empiricist idealism.
- **[[combination-combinatory|Combination]] of invariants:** worker / means of production / non-worker, linked **twice** by property connection and real material appropriation — not a strict combinatory; capitalist homology of two separations vs precapitalist non-homology.
- **Classes as [[bearer-trager|bearers (*Träger*)]]** of relations of production, not pre-given subjects; three-term economic relation owner class / means / exploited producers.
- **Reproduction** as triple continuity (individual capitals → social capital; non-economic conditions; successive production); simple reproduction as analysis of general formal conditions; synchrony as system of dependencies (*Gleichzeitigkeit*).
- **Transition** as itself a structured mode of production, not irrational hiatus or quantity→quality transformation of functioning alone; **manufacture** as transitional form whose essence is [[dislocation-decalage|dislocation]] between capitalist property and craft productive forces (formal vs real subsumption).
- **Presentation (2015):** situates *Lire le Capital* at the conjunction of re-reading Marx, [[structural-causality]], [[symptomatic-reading]] / knowledge effects, and theoretically anti-humanist Communist politics; records seminar 1964–65 and edition variants (1965 full / 1968 abridged / later restoration).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **trained_under:** [[althusser-louis]] (ENS; co-prepared *Capital* seminar with Rancière, Duroux).
- Circle: [[ranciere-jacques]], [[macherey-pierre]], [[establet-roger]]; exchanges noted with Canguilhem, Lacan, Bettelheim, Meillassoux, Lévi-Strauss (Presentation).
- Debts in Part Five: Marx (*Capital*, 1859 Preface, forms of pre-capitalist production); Althusser’s prior papers; critical use of Engels, Luxemburg, Lenin.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Shares structural-Marxist charges of theoreticism and anti-humanism ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]).
- Anthropological uptake and Sahlins’s critique target the combination apparatus generally.
- Balibar’s later political philosophy partially reopens subject/citizenship questions outside the 1965 frame — not primary-ingested here.
