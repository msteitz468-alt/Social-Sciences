# Émile Durkheim

## Overview

Émile Durkheim (1858–1917), French sociologist who professionalized academic sociology in France and established the study of **[[social-fact|social facts]]** as realities *sui generis*, external to and constraining of the individual — the founder of **[[social-realism|Durkheimian social realism]]**. This page rests on *[[durkheim-division-of-labor-1893|The Division of Labor in Society]]* (1893), *[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|The Rules of Sociological Method]]* (1895/1901), *[[durkheim-suicide-1897|Suicide]]* (1897), *[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Primitive Classification]]* (1903, with [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]]), and *[[durkheim-elementary-forms-1912|The Elementary Forms of Religious Life]]* (1912). proposed: [[social-fact]] · [[treat-social-facts-as-things]] · [[mechanical-solidarity]] · [[organic-solidarity]] · [[sacred-and-profane]] · [[primitive-classification]] · studied: [[suicide]] · applies: [[moral-statistics]] · [[comparative-method]]

## Contributions (from *Primitive Classification*, with Mauss, 1903)

All attributed; research-act page: [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]]. Debate: [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]].

- **[[primitive-classification]] / [[symbolic-classification]]** — hierarchical, totalizing classifications of things are social products, continuous with science; distinguished from technological distinctions.
- **Classificatory function as social institution** — not a spontaneous individual faculty; first logical categories were social categories; moieties = first genera, clans = first species.
- **[[sociocentrism]]** — group, not individual, as center of first schemes of nature.
- **Program for sociology of categories** — time, space, cause, substance, reasoning open to the same method — developed at book length in *Elementary Forms*.
- Co-author [[mauss-marcel]]; Needham 1963 critique of the causal demonstration is the main English reanalysis in the collection.

## Contributions (from *Rules of Sociological Method*, 1895/1901)

- **[[social-fact]]** — dual definition: exteriority + constraint; includes morphological “ways of being” ([[social-morphology]]).
- **[[treat-social-facts-as-things]]** — first and most basic rule: discard prenotions (*idola*), define by external characters, prefer crystallized data.
- **[[normal-and-pathological]]** — normality = average type for a [[social-types|social species]] at a developmental phase; crime is normal; pathological *intensity* distinct from presence.
- **[[social-types]]** — morphological classification as middle ground between historical nominalism and philosophical monism of Humanity.
- **Explanation rules** — cause among antecedent social facts (not individual consciousness); function distinct from efficient cause; inner social environment (volume + [[dynamic-density]]) as primary causal locus.
- **Proof** — [[comparative-method|comparative method]] / concomitant variations as sociology’s indirect experiment; sociology as autonomous science.
- **1901 reformulations** — social life as [[collective-representations|representations]]; no substantial collective soul; constraint as indicator; synthesis *sui generis* by association.
- **1895 watershed** (self-report to Deploige, 1907) — religion’s capital role discovered that year under Robertson Smith’s influence; prior research had to be restarted. Genealogy self-report: social realism from Comte, Spencer, Espinas; method autonomy from Boutroux + Comte.

## Contributions (from *Suicide* so far)

- **[[social-fact]]** — social realities external to the individual, studied as things; the social suicide-rate as paradigm case (*Suicide* Preface; cf. *Rules* 1895).
- **Scientific definition of [[suicide]]** — death from a positive or negative act of the victim, known to produce that result; object of sociology = the **rate**, not private motives.
- **Social typology of suicide** — [[egoistic-suicide]], [[altruistic-suicide]], [[anomic-suicide]], residual [[fatalistic-suicide]]; dual axes of [[social-integration]] and [[social-regulation]].
- **[[anomie]]** — insufficient moral regulation of desires under modern economic and domestic conditions; chronic industrial anomy.
- **[[collective-conscience]] / collective representations** — suicidogenetic currents as real collective forces (non-hypostasized).
- **[[moral-statistics]]** — comparative rates, coefficients of preservation, elimination of extra-social factors, distrust of “apparent causes.”
- **[[occupational-group]]** — proposed modern secondary group restoring integration and regulation; occupational decentralization.

In the Preface he polemically rejected system-building “pure sociology” without limited problems: the sociologist must take clearly circumscribed groups of facts, with history, ethnography, and statistics as auxiliaries.

## Contributions (from *Elementary Forms*, 1912)

All attributed — see [[durkheim-elementary-forms-study]] and [[hubs/studies/durkheim-elementary-forms-study]].

- **Definition of religion** — unified system of beliefs and practices relative to **[[sacred-and-profane|sacred things]]** (set apart and forbidden), uniting adherents into a **Church** (moral community); religion eminently collective; magic lacks a church.
- **[[totemism|Australian totemism]] as elementary religion** — experimental case (second-hand ethnography); totem as name and emblem; totemic principle as impersonal sacred force.
- **Society as the reality of the sacred** — the totemic god is the clan/society thought under the form of the emblem; "the idea of society is the soul of religion."
- **[[collective-effervescence]]** — religious ideas born in assembled, heightened collective emotion; cult periodically remakes the gods and the group.
- **[[collective-representations]]** and **categories of the understanding** — time, space, class, causality, force, totality have social (and religious) origins; man is double (individual + social).
- **Ritual system** — negative cult (interdicts, asceticism); positive cult (sacrifice, imitation, commemoration); piacular rites; ambiguity of pure/impure sacred.

## Contributions (from *Division of Labor*, 1893)

- **[[mechanical-solidarity]] / [[organic-solidarity]]** — the master typology: solidarity by resemblance (indexed by [[repressive-law]]) gives way to solidarity by differentiated interdependence (indexed by [[restitutive-law]]) as societies evolve; the division of labor's true *function* is moral (producing solidarity), not economic.
- **Law as external index of solidarity** — since solidarity is invisible, study it through the classification of legal rules by sanction (see [[comparative-method]], [[social-fact]]).
- **[[collective-conscience]]** — defined here with its variables (volume, intensity, determinateness, content), and its historical weakening as organic solidarity grows.
- **[[dynamic-density]]** — the social-morphological *cause* of the division of labor (volume + moral density), resolving an intensified struggle for existence by specialization; the methodological seed of "explain the social by the social."
- **[[non-contractual-elements-of-contract]]** — the anti-[[spencer-herbert|Spencer]] argument that contract rests on prior social conditions (see [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]).
- **Abnormal forms** — the [[anomic-division-of-labor|anomic]] (origin of his *anomie*), [[forced-division-of-labor|forced]], and under-coordinated forms as pathologies of organic solidarity.
- **[[moral-individualism]]** — the cult of the person as the emergent common morality of modern societies; the practical remedy of [[occupational-group|occupational groups]] (1902 preface).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **reacted_against:** [[spencer-herbert|Spencer]]'s contractualist/utilitarian theory of industrial society (Division of Labor Book I ch. 7; see [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]]); [[unknown|Henry Maine]] on the origin of primitive penal law; [[unknown|Gabriel Tarde]]'s imitation theory; the classical-economic (Smithian) reduction of the division of labor to productivity; purely psychological/racial explanations; encyclopedic system-building without proof; teleological “explanation by purpose”; Comte’s single-Humanity historical method as *substitute* for comparison; Seignobos-style historical scepticism about comparing institutions (1908 debate).
- **developed_from / relation to:** [[comte-auguste|Comte]] and [[positivism]] — kept the demand for a positive science of society and credited Comte for holding that cooperation presupposes society's prior existence, while rejecting encyclopedic system-building and much Comtean philosophy of history; *Division of Labor* is dedicated to his teacher Émile Boutroux; *Rules* is the fuller method statement. Self-report (Deploige 1907): “social realism” from Comte, Spencer, Espinas; each-science-explains-its-principles from Boutroux; religion turn from Robertson Smith (not Wundt).
- **Collaborators on *Suicide*:** [[mauss-marcel|Marcel Mauss]] (tables from ~26,000 French judicial records); N. Ferrand (maps); statistical access via G. Tarde as Chief of the Bureau of Legal Statistics (practical cooperation despite theoretical clash). *Année sociologique* (from 1898) as collective instrument of the program (*Rules* 1901 Preface). Co-author with Mauss of *[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Primitive Classification]]* (1903).
- **trained_under / trained:** Boutroux at the École normale (credited for autonomy-of-sciences maxim); institutional Bordeaux → Sorbonne trajectory (timeline in Lukes edition). Students/school: [[mauss-marcel|Mauss]] (nephew; *Année* circle) and denser genealogy pending.
- **Debates:** [[methodological-individualism-debate]] · [[durkheim-spencer-contract-debate]] · [[structure-vs-agency-debate]] · [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]]

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Classic later lines (positions, not wiki-voice verdicts) — detail on [[durkheim-suicide-study]]: ecological inference; data quality of moral statistics; four-type scheme; period sex and “lower societies” framings; underplaying of clinical psychiatry. Simpson (1950) already notes refined statistics and socio-psychology while retaining the work as prototype of systematic social investigation.

On *Division of Labor* (detail on [[durkheim-division-of-labor-study]]): George Simpson's partisan 1933 "Estimate" charges that Durkheim conflated the division of labor "in society" with the division "in the workshop" (a Marxist objection), that his social realism reifies society, and that his later work overgeneralizes from mechanical-solidarity societies to "society" as such. Later legal anthropology overturned the specific repressive→restitutive legal-evolution claim; the mechanical/organic dichotomy and evolutionary "social types" are treated as dated. His comparative base drew on discredited race/craniometric sources (Le Bon, Waitz) — attributed period apparatus, never wiki voice.

## Historiography

Primary for this wiki so far: *Suicide* (Spaulding & Simpson); *Division of Labor* (Simpson); *Rules* (Halls/Lukes 2013, with subsequent methodological texts); *Primitive Classification* (Needham 1963 English of 1903 *Année* essay, with Mauss); *Elementary Forms* (see that ingest’s translation note). Simpson’s *Suicide* introduction frames social-causation classic and social realism. Lukes’s *Rules* introduction maps live method questions (emergence, normal/pathological, explanation). Needham’s Introduction is the main English critique of the 1903 classification essay. Full Thinkers Hub biography still open as a dedicated deep page.

**Afterlife (Collins).** [[collins-randall|Randall Collins]]'s *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology]]* (1975) reworks Durkheim's ritual theory of solidarity (from *Elementary Forms*) into a general micro-mechanism, [[interaction-ritual|interaction ritual]] — formalized as "Durkheim's social density principle" — which he makes the microfoundation of conflict theory and later develops into *Interaction Ritual Chains* (2004). Collins reads Durkheim's [[collective-effervescence|collective effervescence]] as a conflict weapon, not only a source of consensus.
