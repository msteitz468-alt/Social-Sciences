# Gordon Hillman

British archaeobotanist central to reconstructing late Pleistocene–early Holocene plant use and pathways to cereal [[domestication]] in Southwest Asia (and comparative work elsewhere). Stub from secondary citation in [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]].

## Overview

Mithen credits Hillman’s flotation and botanical analyses at [[abu-hureyra|Abu Hureyra]] (with Andrew Moore’s excavation) and related work for models of wild cereal exploitation, sickle selection, and the chronology of morphological domestication (attributed, Mithen 2003). Also associated with archaeobotany at [[wadi-kubbaniya]] in Mithen’s Nile chapters.

## Contributions

- Empirical archaeobotany underwriting non-shattering / sickle-selection narratives of cereal domestication (as of Mithen 2003 synthesis).
