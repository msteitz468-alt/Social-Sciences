# Clive Gamble (b. 1951)

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/gamble-clive|Thinkers Hub — Clive Gamble]].

British Palaeolithic archaeologist and prehistorian; professor (later at Southampton and elsewhere in a long UK academic career) known for **social and ecological interpretations of deep-time hunter-gatherers**, for reframing **[[world-prehistory]]** around **[[global-colonization|global colonization]]**, and for a pan-European program that reconstructs **Palaeolithic societies** from individual networks rather than ethnographic band typologies. *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013) restates the global project as **[[deep-human-history]]** structured by six **[[terrae|Terrae]]** and the evolution of **imagination** ([[going-beyond]], [[kinshipping]], [[enchainment|FACE]]).

## Overview

Four ingested monographs define the wiki's Gamble layer. *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (with [[stringer-chris|Chris Stringer]], 1993) is a dual anatomy–archaeology RAO/replacement synthesis. *[[gamble-timewalkers-1994|Timewalkers]]* (1994) reorganizes world prehistory around global colonization and **[[exaptation]]**. *[[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999|The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe]]* (1999) succeeds and replaces *The Palaeolithic Settlement of Europe* (1986): it argues that Childe's judgement that Palaeolithic social life is unrecoverable is wrong about the *approach*, not the data, and rebuilds a social archaeology from the **individual**, **[[intimate-effective-extended-networks|intimate / effective / extended networks]]**, and **[[palaeolithic-rhythms|rhythms]]** linking **[[locale|locales]]** and regions (attributed, Gamble 1999). *[[gamble-settling-the-earth-2013|Settling the Earth]]* (2013) is the mature global synthesis: six Terrae, climate/variability and social-brain drivers, archaeogenetic veneers, and the claim that people lived apart yet stayed in touch (attributed).

## Contributions

### From *The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe* (1999; this ingest)

- **Pulling aside the Palaeolithic curtain** — naturalistic artists' reconstructions and formation-process debates made a *natural* prehistory comfortable and drew a curtain across *society* as object; the interpretative curtain later shifted onto the Middle–Upper / Neanderthal–Modern transition rather than being scrapped (attributed, ch. 1).
- **Society as unifying focus** — four convictions: same explanatory standards on both sides of the curtain; archaeology as anthropology of past societies beyond ethnography; social life central to stasis and change; archaeology belongs among the social sciences (attributed, ch. 1).
- **Individual and networks** — bottom-up model: ego-based **intimate** (~5; emotional resources), **effective** (~20; material), **extended** (100–400; symbolic), with personal/global scales beyond; magic numbers recast as networks not institutions (attributed, ch. 2; [[intimate-effective-extended-networks]]).
- **[[release-from-proximity]]** — hallmark of human social evolution: social relations stretched beyond continuous co-presence via objects and symbols (after Giddens / Rodseth et al.; attributed, chs. 2, 7–8).
- **Locales / rhythms / regions** — analytic bridge replacing pure ecological settlement bridging of 1986; rhythms (acquiring, making, walking, performing, attending) generate social life; landscape of habit vs **[[social-landscape-palaeolithic|social landscape]]** (attributed, ch. 3).
- **First European societies (500–300 ka)** — gatherings and local networks at sites such as [[boxgrove]], [[bilzingsleben]], [[schoningen|Schöningen]]; biface *[[chaine-operatoire|chaîne opératoire]]* as performance of making (attributed, ch. 4).
- **Neanderthal societies** — strong intimate/effective networks; weak or absent extended/symbolic networks; life in landscapes of habit; co-operation as routine negotiation not external principle; "original caring society" metaphor for emotional intensification without full symbolic self-definition (POSITIONS, attributed, ch. 5).
- **~33 ka structural hinge** — polythetic Middle→Upper transition; after ~33 ka settlement hierarchies and regional material-culture variation reorganize; directed long-distance transfers, ornaments, structured burials, art as extension of social life and **power** (attributed, chs. 6–7; cases include [[kostenki]], [[dolni-vestonice]]).
- **Neither stomach-led nor brain-dead** — program close: move beyond calories/tool-maintenance and beyond pure mentalist assertion (attributed, ch. 8).

### From *Timewalkers* (1994; already ingested)

- **Colonization-centered world prehistory** — *why people were everywhere* rather than *why civilization was not universal* (attributed).
- **Ancients / Pioneers / Moderns** guide; **[[exaptation]]** applied to range expansion; purpose without progress (attributed).
- Gardeners / Sons of Noah framing of [[modern-human-origins-debate]] (POSITION; attributed).

### From *Settling the Earth* (2013; this densify)

- **[[deep-human-history]]** preferred to "prehistory"; multi-archive enquiry (artefacts, symbols, genes, digital) (attributed, ch. 1).
- **Six [[terrae|Terrae]]** (10 Ma → after 1400 CE) as settlement worlds; land-area vs duration contrast (T3 settles ~94% land in ~1.4% of T1–T5 time) (attributed).
- **Encephalisation ↔ settlement disconnect:** brain growth 2 Ma–200 ka without lockstep tech/range expansion; lag of concepts and global settlement into Terra 3; dialogue with Renfrew’s [[sapient-paradox]] (attributed, ch. 1).
- **Imagination / [[going-beyond]] / [[kinshipping]] / keeping** — live apart, stay in touch; [[release-from-proximity]] restated for global scale (attributed).
- **[[enchainment|FACE]]** and **[[instruments-and-containers]]** as social technologies of materials (attributed).
- Drivers: [[variability-selection]] (Potts), tectonic trail, bio-tidal shelves, [[social-brain-hypothesis]] (Dunbar) (attributed, chs 2–3).
- Terra 3 south (Sahul ~45 ka short chronology as of 2013; Flores exception) and north (mammoth steppe, Beringia/Americas, UP critique) (attributed, chs 6–7).
- Terra 4 seascapes (Pacific, Caribbean, Madagascar, Iceland); Terra 5 as **reunion** not discovery (attributed, chs 8–9).
- Dedicated to [[binford-lewis|Lewis Binford]] (paratext).

### From *In Search of the Neanderthals* (with Chris Stringer, 1993)

- Dual anatomy–archaeology replacement synthesis; Ancients' campsite/landscape scales; Mousterian facies critique; burial taphonomy rule; social reading of [[behavioral-modernity]] (attributed; see prior ingest).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Engages [[binford-lewis|Lewis Binford]] on middle-range theory, planning depth, and Mousterian land-use while insisting patterns must also be read socially; rejects Bordes's sedentary Mousterian tribes ([[binford-bordes-mousterian-debate]]); draws on [[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (structuration, co-presence, distanciation), network sociology, primate social studies (Dunbar social brain), Strum & Latour (complex vs complicated), and [[ingold-tim|Ingold]] (taskscape). Explicitly rebuts [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]] (1951) on the impossibility of Palaeolithic social reconstruction. *Settlement* (1986) supplied the nine-region ecological frame that *Societies* (1999) retools with social rhythms. *Settling* (2013) adds Potts variability selection, Smail deep history, Barnard/Shryock kinshipping, and mid-2010s archaeogenetics as veneers. ## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Dating horizon:** absolute dates, industry chronologies, and species inventories are **as of the source year** (1993/1994/1999/2013) — pre-full aDNA for the early books; 2013 still pre-Reich-scale ancient genomics — treat each as a historical snapshot.
- **Network cutoffs and Neanderthal "weak extended network"** are **positions** contested by later art, ornament, and admixture evidence.
- **Complex vs complicated / release-from-proximity / going-beyond** package is theoretical; risk of reading absence of UP-style material culture as absence of social complexity.
- Terrae boundaries and land-area table are analytical conventions, not natural kinds.
- Does not replace site monographs; continental/global synthesis inherits uneven excavation quality.
