# George Peter Murdock (1897–1985)

American anthropologist, architect of the **cross-cultural statistical method** and of the
**Human Relations Area Files (HRAF)** and *Ethnographic Atlas*. In *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man
the Hunter]]* (1968) he contributed the global inventory that opened the volume.

## Overview

*(Attributed — Murdock 1968.)* Murdock's Chapter 2, "The Current Status of the World's Hunting
and Gathering Peoples," took an **organizational view** equating hunting-gathering with the
[[band-tribe-chiefdom-model|band]] level. Adopting "a strict definition," he **excluded**
mounted hunters, sedentary fishermen, and incipient tillers, and enumerated **27 surviving
groups** worldwide "(as of Murdock 1968)," region by region — a coverage map of the world's
remaining foragers. He observed that the largest well-documented concentration was aboriginal
North America (predominantly bilateral societies), where — owing to Morgan's influence —
ethnographers had produced kinship data for ~85% of tribes versus ~15% in Africa.

## Contributions

- **Cross-cultural comparative method** and the **[[comparative-method|Ethnographic Atlas]]**
  coding scheme (used by [[lee-what-hunters-do-study|Lee]] for his 58-society subsistence
  sample) — the statistical, HRAF-based approach to testing hypotheses across societies.
- **Global forager inventory** (1968) — a strict-definition census of surviving
  hunter-gatherers, feeding the wiki's [[hunter-gatherer]] coverage map.
- In the "cultural type" discussion he argued hunter-gatherers show "a surprisingly narrow
  range of variation" in social organization — "except for the Australians."

## Intellectual Genealogy

Murdock stands in the comparativist, quantifying wing of American anthropology, distinct from
the Boasian particularists; his statistical program is a foil to the intensive single-society
fieldwork of the [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowskian]] tradition. He built institutions (HRAF,
the *Atlas*, *Ethnology*) more than a student lineage; documented training relations are not in
the ingested source.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Attributed.)* The cross-cultural method is perennially charged with **Galton's problem**
(treating historically related cases as independent) and with the validity of coding rich
ethnography into single variables. At the symposium his strict subsistence definition of
"hunter-gatherer" was itself questioned as excluding culturally similar part-cultivators (see
[[hunter-gatherer]]).

## Historiography

Murdock is a standard reference for comparative method, HRAF, and mid-century social-structure
theory. A fuller treatment awaits ingestion of *Social Structure* (1949) and the *Ethnographic
Atlas*.
