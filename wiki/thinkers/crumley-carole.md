# Carole L. Crumley

## Overview

Archaeologist/anthropologist associated with the concepts of **[[heterarchy]]** (1987 definition widely cited) and [[historical-ecology|historical ecology]]. In the Social Sciences Wiki, Crumley’s heterarchy formulation is the standard definitional source on [[heterarchy]]; R.J. McIntosh (*[[mcintosh-ancient-middle-niger-2005|Ancient Middle Niger]]*, 2005) applies heterarchical organization to Middle Niger urbanism.

## Contributions

- **Heterarchy (1987):** structures are heterarchical when elements are unranked relative to one another or may be ranked in multiple ways (Crumley 1987: 158, as quoted on [[heterarchy]]).
- Participation in historical-ecology research program (with Balée, Erickson, and others — see [[historical-ecology]]).

cited_by: [[mcintosh-roderick-j]], Johnson (2011 multilinear evolution densifications), [[yoffee-norman]], [[trigger-bruce-g]] (city-state heterarchical elements), Connah, Monroe, Sinclair handbook chapters.

Definitional anchor more than a monographic “school”; reputation travels with the heterarchy citation network in complex-society archaeology.

## McIntosh 2005 reception

*Ancient Middle Niger* is a major empirical application of Crumley’s [[heterarchy]] to early urbanism at [[jenne-jeno]] Urban Clusters. Also aligned with [[historical-ecology]] vocabulary.
