# George R. Milner

## Overview

North American archaeologist specializing in the **Eastern Woodlands**, especially the midwestern Mississippian and earlier mound-building societies. Author of *[[milner-moundbuilders-2021|The Moundbuilders: Ancient Societies of Eastern North America]]* (Thames & Hudson, 2004; 2nd ed. 2021), the principal regional synthesis ingested here. The Preface of that work locates his fieldwork in the Midwest as far south as Kentucky and acknowledges the coverage bias that follows.

## Contributions

- Regional synthesis of Paleoindian through contact-period Eastern Woodlands, organized around **mound-building societies** as the narrative spine.
- Explicit separation of **Mississippian** (material-culture label) from **chiefdom** (sociopolitical category) — not freely interchangeable (as of Milner 2021).
- Labor-cost arguments against assuming that large mounds (e.g. Monks Mound at [[cahokia]]) required vast coerced populations.
- Attention to **warfare intensity** variation over time, **native-plant** cultivation before maize dominance, and **wetland** resources in chiefdom economies.
- Bioarchaeological and settlement perspectives on late prehistoric conflict (e.g. Norris Farms No. 36) and post-contact depopulation.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

As a single-author popular-academic synthesis, the book’s periodizations and chiefdom readings are **Milner’s judgments** competing with other regional specialists (e.g. denser Cahokia demographic estimates in other schools). Midwest fieldwork bias is author-stated. Detail densified from this source should be date-stamped “(as of Milner 2021)” and not overwrite site monographs or aDNA papers without attribution.

## Historiography

First edition 2004; second edition 2021 revised for peopling/aDNA, Archaic, warfare, Mississippian belief, and expanded contact chapter (as of Milner 2021). Thames & Hudson series packaging targets educated general and undergraduate audiences while remaining citable in the regional literature.
