# Michael Burawoy

## Overview

British-born Marxist sociologist of work, long at Berkeley; ethnographer of the capitalist labor process across sites and regimes. *[[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-1979|Manufacturing Consent]]* (1979), his Chicago dissertation-book from ten months as a machine operator at "Allied," asked why workers work as hard as they do and answered that consent is manufactured at the point of production ([[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-study]]).

## Contributions

- **Consent at the point of production:** the game of [[making-out]], the [[internal-labor-market]], and the [[internal-state]] obscure and secure surplus value; "hegemony... is born in the factory" (developing Gramsci's "Americanism and Fordism") — the consent-side charter of the [[labor-process-debate]] against Braverman.
- **Relations in production** (shop-floor relations) as analytically distinct from relations of production; the despotic → hegemonic periodization of work regimes (extended in *The Politics of Production*, 1985 — not yet ingested).
- **The restudy as method:** the Geer→Allied thirty-year comparison with Donald Roy's 1944–45 dissertation site; overt participant observation with records access; defense of single-case generalization (part-as-totality; extension part-to-whole) — seedbed of his later codified [[extended-case-method]].
- **Comparative labor process:** the Zambian copper appendix (colonial color bar, "lashing," Zambianization) arguing workers "respond to the structures in which they are immediately enmeshed," not imported ethnic consciousness.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Zambia fieldwork (1968–72) under Jaap van Velsen tied him to the Manchester School (Gluckman, Epstein, Mitchell, Kapferer — all deployed in the appendix); Chicago Ph.D. under [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]], with Przeworski's Marxism seminar and Zeitlin's 25-page referee critique shaping the book. Theoretical resources: Marx (labor/labor-power), [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]] ([[hegemony]]), Marcuse ("repressive satisfactions"), Baldamus, Polanyi, Selznick, Habermas. Primary foils: Braverman, Mayo/human relations, Goldthorpe et al., Doeringer & Piore, Richard Edwards, Castoriadis.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Single-case generalization and reliance on Roy's covert baseline are the standing methodological critiques (Burawoy's own caveats included); the [[labor-process-debate]] contests whether consent or coercion/deskilling dominates; gender is thin in the 1979 study by his own account. Later career turns (*The Politics of Production*, the extended-case methodology essays, public sociology as ASA president, 2004) are not yet primary-ingested.

## Historiography

*Manufacturing Consent* is a standard graduate text in the sociology of work and a fixture of the labor-process literature; *The Politics of Production* and the ECM essays are priority follow-on ingests (see [[extended-case-method]] coverage note).
