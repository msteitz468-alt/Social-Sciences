# Leslie A. White

Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/white-leslie-a|Thinkers Hub — Leslie A. White]]

## Overview

Leslie Alvin White (1900–1975) was the principal mid-twentieth-century American champion of **cultural evolution** after the Boasian eclipse of Victorian stage theory. Trained in a climate of anti-evolutionism, he broke with it and built a program he called **[[culturology]]** — the science of culture as an extrasomatic, symbol-dependent system governed by thermodynamic regularities. *[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|The Evolution of Culture]]* (1959) is his major book-length synthesis: culture advances as energy harnessed per capita (and the efficiency of tools) increases, from human-energy "primitive" systems through the Agricultural Revolution to the state-church civilizations of the Old World down to Rome. Carneiro and Brown (2007 foreword) treat him as pivotal in restoring generalizing theory to anthropology.

**From Carneiro 2003 (attributed historiography):** White is cast as the figure who “pruned away the dry, sterile undergrowth of anti-evolutionism” without fully innovating the next generation’s midcentury program — that fell to Sahlins, Service, and others. White accepted Sahlins’s [[general-and-specific-evolution]] both as evolution (“specific evolution is just as much evolution as general evolution,” 1960 foreword) and equated specific≈multilinear / general≈unilinear. He rejected the label **“neo-evolutionism”** as “misleading and unjustifiable” (the “neo-whiskey” quip); White’s students often avoided the word. On history vs evolution, White held that history particularizes unique events while evolution generalizes form-sequences — a dispute with Kroeber at the Darwin centennial. White also treated uni- and multilinear interpretations as complementary aspects of one process. Trained: [[carneiro-robert-l|Robert L. Carneiro]] (among others).

## Contributions

- **[[symboling]]** as the qualitative threshold between human and subhuman mind; culture defined as the extrasomatic continuum dependent on symboling (*Evolution of Culture*, Ch. 1).
- **[[culturology]]**: explanations of cultural phenomena in cultural terms (not psychological reduction), parallel to Durkheim's social facts (*Evolution*, Ch. 1; *Science of Culture* 1949).
- **[[energy-law-of-cultural-evolution]]**: culture advances as energy harnessed per capita per year increases, other factors constant; tools multiply efficiency of energy use (Ch. 2).
- **[[universal-evolution]]** of culture-as-a-whole (Steward's label for White's program): evolution vs. history vs. functionalist nontemporal analysis as three interpretive modes.
- Part Two developmental sequence: Agricultural Revolution → civil society → **[[state-church]]** composite → class stratification, commercial economy, theology and science (Chs. 12–15).
- **Foreword to *[[sahlins-service-evolution-culture-1960|Evolution and Culture]]* (1960)** — now primary: diagnoses Boasian antievolutionism (Laufer, Herskovits, Boas as catalyst not sole cause; capitalism/status-quo climate); endorses Sahlins’s [[general-and-specific-evolution]] as both fully evolution; equates **specific ≈ multilinear**, **general ≈ unilinear**; previews Harding (adaptation creativity→conservatism), Kaplan ([[law-of-cultural-dominance]]), Service ([[law-of-evolutionary-potential]]). Students Sahlins and Service acknowledge generic continuity with White and specific discontinuity in the volume’s Note.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Absorbed then rejected** Boasian anti-evolutionism as a graduate student (preface); polemical targets include Berthold Laufer, Lowie, and clerical *Kulturkreis* antievolutionism (Schmidt).
- **Continuity claimed** with [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]] and nineteenth-century evolutionism in *principle* (explicitly rejects the label "neoevolutionism").
- **Dialogue / contrast** with [[steward-julian-h|Julian Steward]]'s [[multilinear-evolution]] and [[cultural-ecology]] (Steward treats White as the "universal evolution" pole).
- **Adjacent** archaeological evolutionism of V. Gordon Childe (energy, agricultural revolution, urbanism) cited throughout Part Two.
- **Students / Michigan lineage**: University of Michigan teaching of "Evolution of Culture" over decades; foreword ties revival of evolutionary anthropology to White more than any other single figure (Carneiro & Brown 2007). [[service-elman-r|Service]] reports hearing cultural evolution first from White (then a different version from Steward); PSO is dedicated to White and Steward and cites White’s *Evolution of Culture* among mid-century revival books while focusing on social-organization levels ([[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Steward and others: universal energy laws explain that culture changes, not particular cultural forms under local conditions ([[multilinear-evolution]], [[cultural-ecology]]).
- Particularist and relativist traditions: stage language, progress evaluation, and "primitive" as residual human-energy type remain contested ([[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]).
- Later [[cultural-materialism]] ([[harris-marvin|Harris]], *[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|Rise]]* 1968 ch. 22 — attributed) and processual archaeology absorb energy/subsistence priorities while often dropping White's culturological exclusion of psychology and his single-curve universalism. Harris credits White with stating the materialist strategy but charges failure to apply it to specific cases and lack of operational covariance.
- Rhetorical valorization of primitive kinship society (equality, mutual aid, production for use) in the Summary is attributed advocacy within the evolutionary frame, not ethnographic consensus.

**McCarthy-era political attacks and the Marxism question (attributed — Johnson 2011).**
Johnson (2011, Ch.9) reports that White was attacked in the McCarthy era for his
politics, on the argument that Marxism is itself one form of cultural-evolutionary
theory and vice versa — mid-twentieth-century US cultural evolutionism often carried
implicit or coded Marxist content. Johnson notes the further irony that Maurice Bloch
accused White of *understating* his own Marxism, i.e. of not going far enough in
acknowledging the affinity, even as White faced political attack for the association.
See [[historical-materialism]], [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]].

## Historiography

Primary self-account in the 1959 preface (conversion from Boasian anti-evolution; Michigan course; planned trilogy of which only this volume appeared). 2007 Carneiro & Brown foreword situates White against "barefoot empiricism" and documents the mid-century return of evolutionary questions. *[[sahlins-service-evolution-culture-1960|Evolution and Culture]]* (1960) is the principal student-generation charter he prefaced.
