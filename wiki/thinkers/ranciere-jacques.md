# Jacques Rancière (b. 1940)

## Overview

French philosopher; ENS student in Althusser’s circle; co-preparer of the 1964–65 *Capital* seminar. Author of **Part Two** of *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* — “The Concept of Critique and the Critique of Political Economy: From the 1844 Manuscripts to *Capital*” — the volume’s most sustained comparison of the anthropological discourse of 1844 with the scientific discourse of *Capital*. Later break with Althusserianism and turn to aesthetics and the politics of equality are outside this primary ingest.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — *Reading Capital* Part Two.)*

- Takes *Capital*’s subtitle “A Critique of Political Economy” as requiring two questions: continuity of Marx’s concept of **critique**, and connection between *Capital* and the first 1844 plan for a critique of political economy.
- Argues “critique” names Marx’s activity at every stage, but was worked out explicitly only c. 1842–1845 as the central concept of his thinking.
- Reconstructs **two structures** of *Entfremdung*: (1) 1844 — subject (worker) invests object with essence; alien entity (capital) becomes subject; worker reduced to object of his object; (2) *Capital* — *Veräusserlichung* as materialization of the relation in properties of the thing and subjectification of the thing as automaton; worker as **bearer** of the wage-labour relation, not primordial subject of the process.
- **Personification** in the strict *Capital* sense = function of the subject as bearer for the relation of production (*Darstellung* / mise en scène of the subject) — invalidating slides where Marx reverts to anthropological subject/object reversal (e.g. certain Volume Three passages on surplus-value → profit).
- Where conceptual rigour slackens, anthropological models re-emerge around the same vocabulary that once named Feuerbachian concepts — residual discourse, not the secret of *Capital*.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **trained_under:** [[althusser-louis]]; seminar circle with [[balibar-etienne]], Yves Duroux, et al.
- Interlocutors in Part Two: Marx 1844/*Capital*/Vol. 3; Feuerbach; Ricardo; Lacan (mise en scène borrowing); Della Volpe school as foil on continuity of object.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Part Two is itself an Althusserian intervention; Rancière’s later anti-Althusserian work re-evaluates the science/ideology and mastery claims of this period — not yet primary-ingested.
- Humanist readers reverse the evaluation: residual anthropology is continuity of emancipatory critique, not lapse.
