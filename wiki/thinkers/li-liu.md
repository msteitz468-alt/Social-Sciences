# Li Liu

## Overview
Li Liu is a Chinese-born archaeologist, Sir Robert Ho Tung Professor in Chinese Archaeology at
Stanford University (Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures). Her work synthesizes
Chinese-language excavation data with anthropological theories of agricultural origins and
state formation, and she is a leading Anglophone voice for a **multi-regional, processual**
account of early Chinese social evolution.

## Contributions
With [[chen-xingcan|Xingcan Chen]] she co-authored [[liu-chen-archaeology-of-china-2012|*The
Archaeology of China: From the Late Paleolithic to the Early Bronze Age* (2012)]], the current
standard Cambridge World Archaeology synthesis of the field. Her *The Chinese Neolithic* (2004)
advanced a competitive, multicentric model of Longshan-period chiefdoms; she has also published
extensively on millet and rice domestication and on the [[erlitou-culture|Erlitou]] state.

## Intellectual Genealogy
Works closely with [[chen-xingcan|Xingcan Chen]] (Institute of Archaeology, Chinese Academy of
Social Sciences); situates her synthesis in dialogue with, and revision of,
[[chang-kwang-chih|K. C. Chang]]'s mid-century framework.

## Critiques and Reappraisals
Her processual, anthropological framing is sometimes contrasted with the historiographical,
text-anchored tradition dominant within Chinese archaeology (see
[[chinese-civilization-origins-debate]]).

## Historiography
Contemporary; reputation rests on the 2004 and 2012 syntheses as reference works in the field.
