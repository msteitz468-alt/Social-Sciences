# Marcel Mauss

## Overview

Marcel Mauss (1872–1950), French sociologist and anthropologist, nephew and closest collaborator of [[durkheim-emile|Émile Durkheim]], central figure of the *Année Sociologique* school. After Durkheim's death he was the leading figure in French sociology (Evans-Pritchard). Ingested primary works on this vault: *[[mauss-general-theory-magic-1902|A General Theory of Magic]]* (with Hubert, 1902–03), joint *[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Primitive Classification]]* (1903), and sole-authored *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* (1925; Cunnison English). Other major lines (Eskimo seasonal morphology with Beuchat, sacrifice with Hubert, techniques of the body, person/*moi*, unfinished projects on prayer, money, and the state) remain **not yet primary-ingested**.

Evans-Pritchard stresses Mauss's self-subordination after WWI: editing dead colleagues' manuscripts and reviving the *Année* (1923–24) rather than maximizing sole-authored output — enacting, EP suggests, the essay's theme that one belongs to others.

## Contributions

### From *Primitive Classification* (1903, with Durkheim)

All attributed jointly:

- Classificatory function as sociological problem; first logical categories as social categories.
- Concepts: [[primitive-classification]], [[symbolic-classification]], [[sociocentrism]], [[collective-representations]].
- Study: [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]].

### From *A General Theory of Magic* (1902–03, with [[hubert-henri|Hubert]])

All attributed jointly:

- Magic as **social phenomenon** defined by circumstance (private, secret, outside cult, verging on the prohibited), against Tylor–Frazer intellectualism; the magician as social status made by public opinion.
- **[[mana]]** promoted to general analytic category — "a category of collective thinking" filling the residue left by sympathy, property, and demonology; "the sacred is a species of the genus mana."
- Belief as collective, a priori, unfalsifiable; magic as genealogical precursor/protector of technique and science.
- Source + study: [[mauss-general-theory-magic-1902]] · [[mauss-hubert-general-theory-magic-study]]. Canonical critique: Lévi-Strauss 1950 (mana as floating signifier).


### From *Sacrifice* (1898, with Hubert)

All attributed jointly — see [[hubert-mauss-sacrifice-study]] and [[hubs/studies/hubert-mauss-sacrifice-study|Studies Hub]]:

- Unitary scheme of [[sacrifice]] as communication between [[sacred-and-profane|sacred and profane]] via destroyed victim.
- Role triad: [[sacrifier]] / sacrificer / victim; general functions [[sacralization]] and [[desacralization]].
- Social-function thesis: sacred things are social things; abnegation nourishes collective forces.
- Source: [[hubert-mauss-sacrifice-1898]].

### From *The Gift* (1925)

All attributed:

- First systematic comparative study of [[gift-exchange]] as articulation of social order (EP's evaluation).
- [[total-social-phenomenon|Total social phenomena]]; [[total-prestation]]; [[potlatch]] as agonistic subtype; [[hau]] as Maori-derived mechanism of return; three obligations (give, receive, repay).
- Re-synthesis of [[kula]] (Malinowski) and Northwest Coast potlatch (Boas school) as extremes of gift systems; Indo-European legal "survivals."
- Normative Conclusions: mutuality, professional groups, social insurance; "in order to trade, man must first lay down his spear" (POSITION).
- Study + hub: [[mauss-the-gift-study]] · [[hubs/studies/mauss-the-gift-study|Studies Hub]].

### "Techniques of the Body" (1934) — via Asad 1993

*(Attributed to [[asad-talal|Asad]], [[asad-genealogies-of-religion-1993|Genealogies of Religion]], ch. 2 — the essay itself not yet primary-ingested.)* Asad reads the essay as Mauss's move away from Durkheim's *homo duplex*: the body as man's "first and most natural instrument," with "[[habitus]]" coined for learned, socially variable bodily aptitudes ("These 'habits'... vary especially between societies, educations, proprieties and fashions, prestiges") — a notion Asad says Bourdieu later popularized without crediting Mauss. Asad highlights the essay's closing proposal that mystical states rest on body techniques ("I think that there are necessarily biological means of entering into 'communion with God'"), which he develops into a non-dualist account of embodied religious formation (see [[discipline]], [[ritual]]); he cites pain-threshold research (Melzack & Wall 1982) as empirical support for the Maussian view.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Classification essay:** Needham 1963 — logically fallacious as causal proof, high residual value — [[social-origin-of-categories-debate]].
- **Gift:** hau over-generalization; evolutionary residue in phase model; armchair method; potlatch type inflation; structuralist rewrite (Lévi-Strauss) vs practice-theoretic defense (Bourdieu); [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]]'s "Spirit of the Gift" (*[[sahlins-stone-age-economics-1972|Stone Age Economics]]*, 1972) re-reads the [[hau]] as the gift's "yield" and the gift as the primitive social contract — see [[mauss-the-gift-study]] Critiques and [[gift-exchange]].
- **Biography / politics:** Jewish scholar under Nazi occupation of Paris; EP reports mental collapse after WWII losses (including Halbwachs); handle factually, not sensationally.

## Clifford's reading (*The Predicament of Culture*, 1988)

*(Attributed — [[clifford-james|James Clifford]], in [[clifford-predicament-of-culture-1988|The Predicament of Culture]].)* Mauss never conducted fieldwork himself, but Clifford identifies him as the dominant oral-teaching influence on nearly every major French ethnographer before the mid-1950s (excepting Lévi-Strauss). He co-founded the Institut d'Ethnologie in Paris (1925, with Paul Rivet and Lucien Lévy-Bruhl) and taught its fieldwork-methods course; his posthumous *Manuel d'ethnographie* (1947, compiled by Denise Paulme from course notes) is a documentary checklist rather than a single method, urging fieldworkers to construct "series and not panoplies" and modeling the team-research ideal later taken up by [[griaule-marcel|Marcel Griaule]]'s multi-observer expeditions. Clifford reads Mauss's concept of the "total social fact" as productively ambiguous: it licenses synecdochic, partial cultural description (any part implicates the whole) without offering a principled criterion for which part to privilege — likened to "Nietzsche's vision of infinite interpretations." Biographically, Clifford notes Mauss was a Dreyfusard socialist who wrote for *L'Humanité* (contrasted with "his rather austere uncle" Durkheim), and records Mauss's remark to student Alfred Métraux, "Taboos are made to be violated" (c. 1925) — per Métraux, the seed of Georges Bataille's later theory of transgression.

## Historiography

Standard portrait: nephew-collaborator, *Année* editor, *Essai sur le don* as masterwork, more empirical than Durkheim (Dumont/EP). Needham: do not force Durkheim-vs-Mauss split for 1903. English *Gift* reception shaped by Evans-Pritchard (Cunnison) and later Mary Douglas (Halls). No Thinkers Hub entry yet — warranted when more of the sole-authored corpus is ingested.
