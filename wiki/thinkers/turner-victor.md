# Victor Turner

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/turner-victor|Victor Turner — Thinkers Hub]].

British cultural anthropologist (1920–1983), the central figure of the processual wing of
[[symbolic-anthropology]] and the theorist who made **[[liminality]]** and **[[communitas]]**
into portable analytic concepts. Trained in the [[manchester-school|Manchester School]] under
[[gluckman-max|Max Gluckman]], Turner did fieldwork among the [[ndembu]] of Northwestern
Zambia (1950–1954) through the Rhodes-Livingstone Institute; his Ndembu ritual corpus became
one of the most closely analyzed bodies of ethnographic material in the anthropology of
religion. The two works ingested here —
*[[turner-forest-of-symbols-1967|The Forest of Symbols]]* (1967) and
*[[turner-ritual-process-1969|The Ritual Process]]* (1969) — carry his theory from Ndembu
ritual to a general account of social process.

## Overview

Turner came to anthropology after wartime non-combatant service; "reared in the orthodox
social-structuralist tradition of British anthropology," he gradually broke from a purely
structural view toward a processual and symbolic one — "the 'social' is not identical with the
'social-structural'." His career runs from the [[extended-case-method|extended-case]] village
studies of *Schism and Continuity* (1957), through the ritual-symbol analyses of the two
ingested books, into the performance-oriented "processual symbology" of *Dramas, Fields, and
Metaphors* (1974) and his collaboration with theatre director Richard Schechner. He converted
to Roman Catholicism in 1958, and the concerns of ritual, pilgrimage, and communitas run
through both his scholarship and his life.

## Contributions

- **[[dominant-symbol|Theory of the ritual symbol]]** — dominant vs. instrumental symbols;
  the three properties (condensation, unification of disparate significata, polarization
  between an ideological and a sensory pole); multivocality.
- **[[symbolic-exegesis|Three levels of meaning]]** — exegetical, operational, positional —
  and the method of detecting where a symbol's stated meaning contradicts its use.
- **[[liminality]]** — the elaboration of van Gennep's liminal phase into a theory of
  "betwixt and between," structural invisibility, anti-structure, and the *sacra*.
- **[[communitas]]** — the unstructured, egalitarian bond of persons outside structure, in
  three forms (existential, normative, ideological); the structure/communitas dialectic.
- **[[social-drama]]** — the processual unit (breach, crisis, redress, reintegration/schism)
  linking incident to structure.
- **The color triad** (white/red/black) as a primitive classification grounded in bodily
  experience (see [[dominant-symbol]], [[primitive-classification]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained under** [[gluckman-max|Max Gluckman]] at Manchester; a product of the
  [[manchester-school|Manchester School]] and the Rhodes-Livingstone Institute.
- **Developed from** [[van-gennep-arnold|Arnold van Gennep]] (rites of passage) — the single
  most important source for the liminality/communitas theory.
- **Reacted against** the equilibrium bias of British [[structural-functionalism]]
  (Radcliffe-Brown) while retaining structural analysis; engaged and partly resisted
  [[structuralism|Lévi-Strauss]] (accepting binary oppositions, insisting on the emotional and
  processual dimension symbols carry).
- **Contemporaries in [[symbolic-anthropology]]:** [[geertz-clifford|Clifford Geertz]]
  (interpretive wing) and [[douglas-mary|Mary Douglas]] (classification/pollution wing), whose
  *[[douglas-purity-and-danger-1966|Purity and Danger]]* pollution hypothesis
  ([[ritual-pollution]]) Turner adopts and refines in "Betwixt and Between."
- **Methodological debt to** [[wilson-monica|Monica Wilson]] (the dedicatee of *The Forest of
  Symbols*), whose insistence on native exegesis Turner made a methodological charter.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- The extension of **liminality** and **communitas** from small-scale ritual to complex
  societies drew the standing charge of overgeneralization, which Turner answered in his 1977
  Foreword to *The Ritual Process* (see [[communitas-scale-debate]]).
- Interpretive symbol analysis (multivocality) is charged with being hard to falsify.
- The **communitas** concept is read by some as importing a romantic value-preference into
  analysis; its relation to Durkheim's [[collective-effervescence]] is much discussed.
- Later reflexive/"Writing Culture" critique questioned the analyst's authority to read
  another people's symbols — the very move (Turner's *positional* level) on which his method
  rests.
- **Rosaldo (attributed).** In *[[rosaldo-culture-truth-1993|Culture and Truth]]* (1989) [[rosaldo-renato|Renato Rosaldo]] credited Turner's social-drama/case-history method with advancing processual analysis but argued his Ndembu conclusions subordinated the human process his own material dramatized to structural "control mechanisms," and that the four-phase social-drama model was imposed without reference to Ndembu narrative conceptions.

## Historiography

Turner's reputation has, if anything, grown since his death, carried well beyond anthropology:
"liminality" and "communitas" are now standard vocabulary in ritual studies, performance
studies, theology, history, and literary criticism. Edith Turner — his wife and lifelong
fieldwork collaborator — continued and reinterpreted his work on ritual and communitas. The
Ndembu corpus, unusually rich in named informants and native exegesis, remains a reference
point; his fieldwork conditions and the ethnographic-present question are examined on the
[[turner-forest-of-symbols-study]] and [[turner-ritual-process-study]] pages and in the hub
analysis.
