# Mark S. Granovetter

American sociologist; principal architect of the **[[strength-of-weak-ties]]** argument (1973) and, later, of the "new economic sociology" via the [[embeddedness]] program (1985 — not yet ingested). At the time of the AJS article he was at Johns Hopkins University; the paper grew from discussions with [[harrison-white|Harrison White]] at Harvard.

## Overview

Granovetter's early program used [[network-analysis|interpersonal network analysis]] to bridge micro interaction and macro patterns — diffusion, mobility, community organization, cohesion. *[[granovetter-strength-of-weak-ties-1973|The Strength of Weak Ties]]* (1973) is the wiki's first Granovetter primary; it reworks dissertation labor-market contact data and a large secondary literature into a portable structural claim: **bridging weak ties** integrate individuals into opportunity structures and communities, while strong-tie density can fragment the larger structure.

## Contributions

- **[[tie-strength]]** as a multidimensional construct (time, emotional intensity, intimacy, reciprocal services).
- **Forbidden triad / bridge theorem** (attributed): under the overlap hypothesis, strong ties are almost never bridges; bridges are weak.
- **Labor-market illustration:** job information disproportionately flows through infrequent contacts (see [[granovetter-strength-of-weak-ties-study]]).
- **Community organization corollary:** bridging weak ties as necessary infrastructure for mobilization (West End vs Charlestown reconstruction; debated with Gans — [[west-end-network-fragmentation-debate]]).
- **Programmatic agenda** for network models that include weak ties and quasi-groups, against small-group sociometry confined to strong ties (contrast DHL transitivity models).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **trained_under:** [[harrison-white|Harrison White]] (acknowledged origin of the paper in discussions; White's structuralist network program is the immediate intellectual environment).
- **Dialogue partners / readers of early drafts (per acknowledgments):** Ivan Chase, James Davis, William Michelson, Nancy Lee, Peter Rossi, Charles Tilly.
- **Intellectual debts named in the article:** Homans (*The Human Group* — interaction and friendship); Heider/Newcomb cognitive balance; Rapoport network models; Davis clustering; Milgram small-world; Bott, Barnes, Mitchell network anthropology; Gans *Urban Villagers*; Jacobs *Death and Life*.
- **Later lineage:** [[structural-holes-theory|structural holes]] ([[burt-ronald-s|Burt]] 1992, ingested — Burt was another of [[harrison-white|White]]'s students and reinterprets the weak-ties result: the causal agent is the [[structural-holes|structural hole]] a tie spans, not the tie's weakness); social-capital network measures (Lin et al., not yet ingested); embeddedness and markets (Granovetter 1985, not yet ingested); new economic sociology.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Gans 1974:** weak ties overestimated for the West End; networks necessary but not sufficient; history, timing, political connection, and class culture also required ([[west-end-network-fragmentation-debate]]).
- **Measurement:** frequency-only operationalization of strength; positive/symmetric assumption; weak ties under-sampled in sociometry (Granovetter himself flags these).
- **Later research program:** the job-finding "weak ties win" finding has been refined and sometimes reversed for particular outcomes (strong ties as trust/influence channels; [[structural-holes|Burt's structural holes]] as a reframing of bridging — see [[closure-vs-brokerage-debate]]). Treat later status on the study page as the literature develops; do not promote 1973 point estimates to wiki voice.
