# David Harvey (1935– )

## Overview

British-born Marxist geographer, among the most widely read theorists of capitalist
space. Harvey moved from a positivist spatial science (*Explanation in Geography*, 1969)
to [[historical-materialism|historical-materialist]] analysis (*Social Justice and the
City*, 1973), and built a body of work reconstructing Marx's political economy as an
explicitly *geographical* theory — in which the production, destruction, and reordering
of space is internal to capital accumulation rather than a passive backdrop. This wiki
holds him primarily through *[[harvey-condition-postmodernity-1989|The Condition of
Postmodernity]]* (1989).

## Contributions

*(Attributed throughout — these are Harvey's theoretical claims.)*

- **The "spatial fix."** In *The Limits to Capital* (1982) Harvey argued that capitalism
  displaces its crises of [[flexible-accumulation|overaccumulation]] temporally (credit,
  long-term investment) and spatially — absorbing surplus capital and labour through
  geographical expansion and new infrastructure. Because overcoming spatial barriers
  requires *fixing* capital in immovable places (railways, ports, cities), the fix is
  self-limiting: today's spatial solution becomes tomorrow's barrier, to be devalued
  through "creative destruction."
- **[[time-space-compression|Time-space compression]].** Harvey's best-known coinage
  (*The Condition of Postmodernity*, 1989): capital's drive to reduce the turnover time
  of circulation and to "annihilate space through time" periodically revolutionizes the
  objective qualities of space and time, forcing changes in how societies represent the
  world. He reads both modernism (a response to compression c. 1847–1918) and
  postmodernism (a response to a post-1972 round) through this mechanism.
- **[[flexible-accumulation|Flexible accumulation]].** Harvey's name for the post-1973
  regime succeeding [[fordism|Fordism]], drawing on [[regulation-theory|regulation
  theory]] while insisting the underlying Marxist logic of accumulation is unchanged.
- **The postmodernism thesis.** That postmodern culture is the cultural-experiential
  registration of flexible accumulation, not an autonomous epochal break — the central
  argument of *The Condition of Postmodernity* (see
  [[postmodernity-epochal-break-debate]]).

In this book Harvey's stated method is to read cultural and aesthetic shifts against
political-economic history rather than as an internal history of ideas — treating Marx's
account of capitalist modernization ("all that is solid melts into air") as "the missing
link" explaining both modernist and postmodernist sensibility.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Harvey works within the Marxist tradition ([[marx-karl|Marx]], and in this book engages
[[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]] on Fordism, the [[regulation-theory|regulation school]] of
Aglietta and Lipietz, and Lefebvre's theory of the [[production-of-space|production of space]]). He positions his
argument against [[foucault-michel|Foucault]] and Giddens (for dispersing power away from
the "genuine political economy" that money welds together), against Lyotard's and
Baudrillard's postmodernism, and against the "disorganized capitalism" thesis of Offe and
of Lash and Urry. He aligns with Fredric Jameson's reading of postmodernism as "the
cultural logic of late capitalism."

**Lefebvre Afterword (1991, attributed to Harvey):** Harvey’s Afterword to the Nicholson-Smith translation of *[[lefebvre-production-of-space-1991|The Production of Space]]* frames the English edition as overdue access to a major French intellectual activist; sketches Lefebvre’s PCF years, 1958 left exit, Nanterre, and the 1968–74 urban sequence culminating in POS; and reads the book as a preliminary, “detonating,” intensely political enquiry rather than a closed system. He does **not** restate [[time-space-compression]] in that Afterword — keep POS reception and *Condition of Postmodernity* mechanisms distinct.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Attributed.)* Critics — including feminist geographers (Doreen Massey, Rosalyn Deutsche)
writing shortly after the book — charged that Harvey's totalizing political economy
subordinates gender, race, and difference to class, reproducing the very "meta-narrative"
closure postmodern critics warned against; that his account of the Fordism→flexible-
accumulation transition overstates its coherence and periodizing sharpness (a criticism
Harvey partly anticipates, siding "somewhere in between" the Piore–Sabel and Pollert
positions); and that his readings of postmodern art and film are dismissive. Harvey's
influence nonetheless grew: *time-space compression* and *the spatial fix* entered
general social-science vocabulary, and later work (*A Brief History of Neoliberalism*,
2005; *accumulation by dispossession*) extended the same framework.

## Historiography

Harvey's reputation rests on a long, consistent research program rather than a single
restudied study; the standard account treats *The Limits to Capital* (1982) as the
theoretical engine and *The Condition of Postmodernity* (1989) as its most influential
application. His retrospective self-accounts and widely circulated lecture course on
*Capital* have made him a central figure in the transmission of Marxist political economy
to recent generations.
