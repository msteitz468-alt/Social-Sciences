# Lewis Henry Morgan

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/thinkers/morgan-lewis-henry|Thinkers Hub]]

## Overview

Lewis Henry Morgan (1818–1881), American lawyer and ethnologist, founder of **comparative kinship** as a systematic science and principal architect of Victorian kinship evolutionism. *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity and Affinity of the Human Family]]* (1871) established the descriptive/classificatory typology and global schedule method; *Ancient Society* (1877, not yet ingested) totalized savagery–barbarism–civilization stages later reworked by [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] in *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]* (1884). This page now rests on *Systems* primary text (plus Engels reception), not Engels alone.

## Contributions

- **[[kinship-schedule-method|Kinship schedules]].** ~200 kin-type questionnaire; field and missionary/correspondent fills; ~139 terminologies (Tooker); Smithsonian tables.
- **Two radical systems.** [[descriptive-system-of-relationship|Descriptive]] (Aryan–Semitic–Uralian) vs [[classificatory-system-of-relationship|classificatory]] (Ganowanian/Turanian principal; Malayan lower; Eskimo third) — *Systems* General Results.
- **Seneca-Iroquois type case.** Classificatory plan as documented mid-19th c. among [[iroquois|Haudenosaunee/Iroquois]]; indicative features tracked across Native North America.
- **Conjectural evolutionary solution.** Classificatory systems explained via antecedent customs including Hawaiian [[punalua]], communal family, tribal exogamy reform, then pair marriage and property-based “civilized” family that overthrows classificatory terms (attributed; contested — [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]).
- **Asiatic-origin argument.** Shared Turanian–Ganowanian plan → common stock; peopling inference (superseded as proof method).
- **Gentile/family stages (fuller in *Ancient Society*).** [[gens]], [[mother-right]], [[group-marriage]], [[savagery-barbarism-civilization]] — partly previewed in *Systems*, completed later.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Field:** Seneca consultants; North American nations on schedule tours.
- **Institutional:** Joseph Henry / Smithsonian.
- **Cohort:** Lubbock, Tylor, McLennan, Bachofen.
- **Used by:** Marx (Ancient Society précis); Engels (*Origin*).
- **Rivals:** McLennan; Bachofen (as framed in Engels’s research history).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Unilinear ladder, group marriage universalism, and kinship proof of peopling rejected in mainstream anthropology. Terminology analysis and schedule method retained. [[ganowanian-family|Ganowanian]] construct obsolete. Dual reception: academic revision vs Marxist canonization.

**Lowie (*[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]*, 1920):** the major American book-length critique of Morgan’s social-organization scheme — mottled diversity vs single [[sib]] pattern; family prior and universal vs gens monopoly; no world group-marriage/matriarchate stages; associations (Schurtz) missing from Morgan’s atomism; diffusion undercuts stage-laws. Lowie: the book “inevitably grew into a persistent critique of Morgan.” See [[lowie-primitive-society-study]] · [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].

## From Stocking, *Victorian Anthropology* (1987)

*(Attributed — [[stocking-george-w|Stocking]] 1987; source [[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987]].)* Stocking reports Meyer Fortes's paradoxical claim that [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]] was in fact the founder of *American* cultural anthropology, while the true founder of *British* social anthropology was the American Morgan — whose kinship-and-social-organization approach was "rediscovered" by [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]] and, in Fortes's words, "took deep root in British anthropology." *Ancient Society* served as the standard theoretical reference work for U.S. Bureau of Ethnology anthropologists for roughly twenty years after the Bureau's 1879 founding by John Wesley Powell. Leslie White carried the Morganian evolutionary tradition forward in interwar American anthropology and devoted considerable effort to biographical research on Morgan; a student of White's later revisited the fundamental assumptions of evolutionary thought, questioning the "unilinear" stereotype long attached to Morgan and the other classical evolutionists.

## From Kuper, *Invention of Primitive Society* (1988)

*(Attributed — Kuper 1988, Ch. 3.)* Kuper calls Morgan the most influential developer of the anthropological idea of [[primitive-society]]. Influence became a barrier to fresh reading: Engels appropriated him early; [[boas-franz|Boas]] made him the special target of anti-evolutionism; two generations of American anthropologists fought over Morgan theses and often misrepresented him. Kuper reconstructs: Iroquois *League*; discovery of classificatory system among Ojibwa; global kinship schedules; *Systems of Consanguinity* (classificatory vs descriptive; Turanian/Malayan/Ganowanian); McLennan attack; *Ancient Society* ethnical periods and property; accidental invention of kinship studies as technical core of anthropology. Dual legacy: Marxist (Engels) and American anthropological battleground. Source: [[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988]]; debate [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
