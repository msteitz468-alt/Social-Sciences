# Jeffrey R. Parsons

## Overview

**Jeffrey R. Parsons** (1939–2021), University of Michigan archaeologist, was one of the principal architects of full-coverage regional settlement survey. He directed the Texcoco, Chalco-Xochimilco, and Zumpango regional surveys that, with [[sanders-william-t|William T. Sanders]]'s Teotihuacan Valley work, completed coverage of the Basin of Mexico, and co-authored the project synthesis [[sanders-parsons-santley-basin-of-mexico-1979|*The Basin of Mexico*]] (1979). His later work extended survey method to the Andes and to "aquatic" resources (salt-making, lake exploitation) in the Basin.

## Contributions

- **Regional survey method**: operationalized systematic full-coverage surface survey — sherd-density-based population estimation, standardized site typologies — across thousands of km² ([[settlement-pattern-archaeology]], [[archaeological-survey]]).
- **The Texcoco, Chalco-Xochimilco, and Zumpango surveys** (1967–1973): the monograph series underlying the 1979 synthesis and its demographic reconstructions.
- Co-author of the Basin synthesis's model of [[population-pressure]]-driven [[state-formation]] (see [[sanders-basin-of-mexico-study]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Formed within the Basin of Mexico survey tradition initiated by Sanders (and behind him [[steward-julian-h|Julian Steward]]'s [[cultural-ecology]] and Gordon Willey's Virú Valley settlement-pattern program); long collaboration with Sanders and [[blanton-richard|Richard Blanton]], whose Valley of Oaxaca surveys transplanted the method.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The survey program's population estimates and its materialist reading of the record drew the critiques recorded at [[sanders-basin-of-mexico-study]] and [[hydraulic-hypothesis-debate]]; Parsons's method itself became the disciplinary standard for highland Mesoamerican and Andean survey.
