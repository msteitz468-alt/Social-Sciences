# Elinor Ostrom

American political scientist; co-founder of the Workshop in Political Theory and Policy Analysis (Indiana University); 2009 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences (with Oliver Williamson) for analysis of economic governance, especially the commons. This summary rests primarily on *[[ostrom-governing-the-commons-1990|Governing the Commons]]* (1990).

## Overview

Ostrom challenged the policy orthodoxy that common-pool resources must be either privatized or state-controlled. She showed, through institutional analysis of enduring and failed self-governing systems, that appropriators can craft rules solving supply, commitment, and monitoring problems under specified conditions.

## Contributions

- **[[common-pool-resource|CPR]] framework** — resource systems vs resource units; subtractability + difficulty of exclusion.
- **[[design-principles-cpr|Design principles]]** for long-enduring CPR institutions.
- **[[polycentricity|Polycentric]]** public-enterprise arrangements (e.g. California groundwater).
- Empirical program linking theory to comparative cases ([[ostrom-governing-the-commons-study]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Engages Hardin, Olson ([[collective-action-theory]]), prisoner’s-dilemma models; institutional economics and workshop tradition (Vincent Ostrom on polycentricity). Reacts against metaphor-driven Leviathan/privatization prescriptions.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Scope limited to small-to-medium CPRs within one country; design principles not a checklist guarantee; OCR- and secondary-case dependence in the 1990 book; later IAD framework elaborates multi-level rules (not fully ingested here).

## Historiography

Central to commons scholarship and development policy. Wiki coverage: one primary (1990). Thinkers Hub candidate after further primaries.
