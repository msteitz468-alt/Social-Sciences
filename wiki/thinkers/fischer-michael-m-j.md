# Michael M. J. Fischer

American anthropologist (b. 1946); contributor to *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* ("Ethnicity and the Post-Modern Arts of Memory"); co-author with [[marcus-george-e|George Marcus]] of *[[marcus-fischer-anthropology-as-cultural-critique-1986|Anthropology as Cultural Critique]]* (1986). Later affiliated with anthropology and science and technology studies at MIT (as reported on ACC jacket/related apparatus).

## Overview

Fischer joins Marcus in theorizing anthropology's [[experimental-moment|experimental moment]] and dual promise of diversity documentation plus cultural critique. In *Writing Culture* he reads ethnic autobiography and literary ethnography as models for post-modern ethnographic composition. In ACC he co-authors the full program (crisis of representation, repatriation, defamiliarization techniques) and, in the Appendix, develops "ethnicity as text and model" (dreaming/transference; multi-voice experiment; ethnic memory as future-oriented).

## Contributions

*(Attributed; from WC 1986 and ACC 1986.)*

- **WC essay (Ethnicity and the Post-Modern Arts of Memory):** ethnicity reinvented each generation (Chinese-American ≠ Chinese in America); **bifocality** / family of resemblances; ethnic autobiography as laboratory of post-modern composition — tactics of **transference** (Arlen), **dream-work** (Kingston/Chin *Woman Warrior*), **alternative selves** (Mingus; Golden vs Mead), **inter-reference** (Chicano bilingual), **ironic humor** (Momaday, Silko, Vizenor, Welch); models for ethnographic cultural criticism; closing: not much fully bifocal ethnography yet.
- With Marcus: [[anthropology-as-cultural-critique]] program; survey of experimental ethnographies of person/self/emotion and political-economy embedding; [[repatriation-of-anthropology]]; two techniques of [[defamiliarization]].
- Appendix (ACC): ethnicity as text/model; multi-voice writing experiment (1986); ethnic memory oriented to the future.
