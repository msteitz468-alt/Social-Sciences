# Dan Sperber

## Overview

Anthropologist/philosopher whose **epidemiology of representations** is treated by White (2021) as a foundational metaphor for CSR: just as epidemiologists study disease distribution given bodies, culture-theorists must study the distribution of ideas given minds. *Explaining Culture* (1996); earlier *Rethinking Symbolism* (1975).

## Contributions

- Naturalistic culture theory: representations propagate, transform, and stabilize under cognitive and ecological constraints — not pure imitation or blank-slate socialisation.
- Bridge from cognitive science into anthropology that CSR pioneers used against cultural determinism.
- Not exclusively a "religion" theorist; CSR inherits his general mind–culture apparatus.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Influences CSR network: [[boyer-pascal]], [[whitehouse-harvey]], [[mccauley-robert-n]]. Contrasts with strong Geertzian symbol-system autonomy ([[interpretive-anthropology]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Epidemiology metaphor can underplay power, institutions, and deliberate pedagogy; cultural-evolution formalisms later re-specified transmission.
