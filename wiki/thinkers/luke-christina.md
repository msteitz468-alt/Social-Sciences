# Christina Luke

Archaeologist (as of 2006: research fellow, Department of Archaeology, Boston University; later teaching roles noted in subsequent literature outside this vault). Co-editor of [[brodie-kersel-luke-tubb-archaeology-cultural-heritage-antiquities-trade-2006|Archaeology, Cultural Heritage, and the Antiquities Trade]] (2006); co-author of the Ulúa Valley plunder/market chapter (with John S. Henderson), the Looting Lydia landscape chapter (with Christopher H. Roosevelt), and the volume conclusion on collecting contexts (with [[brodie-neil|Neil Brodie]]).

## Overview

Luke's 2006 footprint is **case-study and landscape**: Mesoamerican Ulúa Valley marble vessels and related antiquities on the market; Lydian tumulus landscapes in western Turkey destroyed for portable wealth; plus editorial framing with Brodie on why people collect.

## Contributions

- **Ulúa Valley market analysis** (attributed, Luke & Henderson 2006) — linking looted Honduran landscapes to collector demand for specific object classes.
- **Lydian landscape destruction** (attributed, Roosevelt & Luke 2006) — tumuli and settlement looting as archaeological-landscape erasure, not isolated object loss.
- **Co-editorship and conclusion** with Brodie on social/cultural contexts of collecting.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Boston University archaeology; co-work with Henderson (Ulúa) and Roosevelt (Lydia); editorial team with Brodie, [[kersel-morag-m|Kersel]], [[tubb-kathryn-walker|Tubb]].

## Contributions (densified)

### Ulúa Valley (with Henderson, ch. 7)
Lower Ulúa, NW Honduras: long-term pillage of polychromes, jades, and especially **marble vases**; Travesía downtown reduced from hundreds of mounds (Stone 1941) to ~25 trench-riddled mounds; forgeries a major market fraction. Legal tools cited: Pre-Columbian Monumental Act 1972; CPIA Honduras MOU (12 Mar 2004); NSPA/McClain/Schultz. **Not** the same case as Mortensen’s [[copan]] management study.

### Looting Lydia (with Roosevelt, ch. 8)
2001 survey of Lydian/Persian tumuli: **397** inspected, **90%** looted, **18%** destroyed; Lydian Hoard Met→Turkey 1993 as restitution success that still left landscape knowledge loss unaddressed.

### Conclusion (with Brodie, ch. 17)
Social and cultural contexts of collecting — aestheticization, networks, due diligence gaps.
