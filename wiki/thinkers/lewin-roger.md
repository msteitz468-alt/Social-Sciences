## Overview

Roger Lewin (b. 1944) is a science journalist and editor associated with *New Scientist*, who co-authored *Origins* (1977) with paleoanthropologist [[leakey-richard]], translating Leakey's fieldwork and evolutionary arguments for a general readership.

## Contributions

As co-author of *Origins*, Lewin helped articulate the book's central attributed thesis: that human ancestors were "strongly cooperative creatures" whose sharing of food and labor, rather than an innate killer instinct, drove human evolution. The book argues explicitly against the "killer ape" position associated with [[ardrey-robert]], [[lorenz-konrad]], and [[dart-raymond]] (see [[human-aggression-killer-ape-debate]]), attributing human warfare instead to agricultural surplus, materialism, and ideology rather than biological aggression.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Co-author relationship with [[leakey-richard]] on *Origins*; role as science-journalist collaborator translating a working paleoanthropologist's field-derived arguments into a synthetic popular/academic text.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

As a work of science journalism co-authored with a practicing researcher, *Origins* blends attributed scientific argument with polemic (particularly against the killer-ape school); readers should weigh its claims as advocacy for a position within an active 1970s debate rather than settled consensus.
