# Robert S. Santley

## Overview

**Robert S. Santley** (1946–2010), University of New Mexico archaeologist, was the junior co-author of [[sanders-parsons-santley-basin-of-mexico-1979|*The Basin of Mexico*]] (1979), trained under [[sanders-william-t|William T. Sanders]] at Penn State. Within the synthesis he carried much of the quantitative and spatial analysis (settlement typology, [[central-place-theory|central-place]] applications, sampling experiments). He later directed the Matacapan project in the Tuxtlas (Veracruz), pursuing Teotihuacan's Gulf Coast connections and obsidian-based political economy.

## Contributions

- Spatial and locational analysis of Basin settlement data, including the Appendix E sampling experiment testing what probabilistic sampling would have missed relative to full-coverage survey ([[archaeological-survey]]).
- Obsidian exchange and craft-production arguments linking [[teotihuacan|Teotihuacan]]'s growth to resource control (developed further in his post-1979 work).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Penn State student of Sanders; the third generation of the [[steward-julian-h|Stewardian]] [[cultural-ecology]] survey lineage that runs Steward → Willey/Sanders → Parsons/Blanton/Santley.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

His strongly materialist, exchange-centered readings of Teotihuacan influence (esp. at Matacapan) were contested in later Gulf Coast scholarship; the Basin synthesis critiques are recorded at [[sanders-basin-of-mexico-study]].
