# Susan Keech McIntosh

## Overview

Archaeologist of the Middle Niger; co-director with [[mcintosh-roderick-j|Roderick J. McIntosh]] of the [[jenne-jeno|Jenné-jeno]] excavations and related work that established the ceramic/radiocarbon sequence for early Inland Niger Delta urbanism. In *[[mcintosh-ancient-middle-niger-2005|Ancient Middle Niger]]* (2005) R.J. McIntosh relies on their joint sequence (Phases I/II–V; founding ~250 BCE) and on S.K. McIntosh’s ceramic and metallurgical analyses.

## Contributions

- **Ceramic chronology** for Jenné-jeno and the Upper Inland Niger Delta (Upper IND Fineware → Painted Ware → Fine Channeled and Impressed Ware phases — as reported in McIntosh 2005).
- Co-excavation and interpretation of urban clustering, craft/metallurgy, and pre-Islamic interregional exchange.
- Broader theoretical writing on horizontal complexity / [[heterarchy]] in African complex societies (cited in handbook densifications; see [[state-formation]], [[urbanization]]).
