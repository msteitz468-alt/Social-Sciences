# Donna Haraway

Donna Jeanne Haraway (b. 1944) is an American feminist science-studies scholar and cultural theorist, long based in the History of Consciousness Department at the University of California, Santa Cruz. Trained originally in biology (her PhD adviser was the ecologist G. Evelyn Hutchinson at Yale), she became one of the most influential figures in feminist science and technology studies, and later a charter voice in [[multispecies-studies]]. Her **cyborg** and **situated knowledges** vocabulary entered anthropological method debates in the late 1980s; her later companion-species and Chthulucene work reoriented her toward more-than-human relations. Her prose is deliberately figurative and boundary-crossing — a style that is itself part of her argument that the categories through which we know (nature/culture, human/animal, organism/machine) are made, not given.

Two source streams feed this page: [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]'s engagement in *[[strathern-partial-connections-1991|Partial Connections]]* (claims below marked as via Strathern), and the primary ingest of *[[haraway-staying-with-the-trouble-2016|Staying with the Trouble]]* (2016).

## Overview

*(Cyborg/knowledges material attributed via Strathern's reading.)* Haraway's cyborg is neither body nor machine as a single system: parts form no organic totality; connections are partial; identities are permanently partial; "one is too few but two are too many." She opposes the "god-trick" of infinite unlocated vision to embodied, partial objectivity; feminist knowledge is interest-group situated, not cumulative from nowhere. Irony holds contradictions that do not resolve into wholes. Organicism and purity outside the artificial are rejected as logics of domination.

## Contributions

**Feminist science studies (earlier corpus, partly via Strathern):**
- **Cyborg image** → Strathern's permission structure for [[partial-connections]]: compatibility without common origin; a whole image that is not an image of a whole; prosthetic mutual extension of anthropology and feminism. In *Staying with the Trouble* Haraway recasts the cyborg as one member of a "queer litter" rather than "the Chief Figure of Our Times" *(Haraway 2016)*.
- **Situated knowledges / god-trick** → critique of unmarked, unlocated vision; objectivity as situated, accountable, partial.

**Multispecies and Chthulucene program (*Staying with the Trouble*, 2016):** Haraway introduces or consolidates a dense interlinked vocabulary, each treated on its own page in attributed voice —
- **[[companion-species]]** and **[[naturecultures]]** (from her earlier *Companion Species Manifesto* and *When Species Meet*): beings "relentlessly becoming-with," refusing human exceptionalism without invoking posthumanism, and the refusal of the nature/culture binary.
- **[[chthulucene]]**: a renaming of the present against the [[anthropocene]] and [[capitalocene]] (see [[naming-the-anthropocene-debate]]).
- **[[sympoiesis]]** ("making-with," a term she borrows from M. Beth Dempster), **[[making-kin]]** and the contested slogan "Make Kin Not Babies," **[[response-ability]]**, and **[[sf-string-figures|SF]]** as method.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Haraway trained under the ecologist G. Evelyn Hutchinson at Yale — a lineage she foregrounds, since her biological formation underwrites her later claim that biology's own frontier (symbiosis, developmental systems, holobionts) undercuts the individualist organism. She was an interlocutor for [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]] (who repurposes the cyborg for Melanesian imagery and for method, distinguishing "Melanesian cyborgs" of her own making). Her multispecies and Gaia interlocutors include [[stengers-isabelle|Isabelle Stengers]] (cosmopolitics; "intrusive Gaia"), [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]] (the "Earthbound," whom she also critiques for relying on Carl Schmitt's political theology), [[despret-vinciane|Vinciane Despret]] (the "virtue of politeness," "rendering capable"), and [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Anna Tsing]] (arts of living on a damaged planet). She names Ursula K. Le Guin, Hannah Arendt, Thom van Dooren, and the biologist Lynn Margulis (symbiogenesis) as recurring resources. She sits adjacent to, but is not identical with, [[actor-network-theory]] and the [[ontological-turn]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Strathern notes the cyborg can be "friendly" or serve control by machines/others (the Ong factory example in *Partial Connections*); Melanesian analogizing is Strathern's move, not Haraway's ethnography. On the later work, Haraway's punning, mythopoetic style draws the charge of obscurity. Her most contested move in *Staying with the Trouble* is the "Make Kin Not Babies" argument (see [[making-kin]] and [[naming-the-anthropocene-debate]]): her call to pair reproductive justice with a deliberate long-run reduction of human numbers has been criticized by reproductive-justice, Indigenous, and postcolonial scholars as echoing population-control and eugenic discourses that disproportionately target the Global South. Haraway states her intent is the opposite — feminist, anti-racist, non-coercive — and the tension is recorded, not adjudicated, on the debate page.

## Historiography

Haraway's reputation moved from feminist science studies (the cyborg era) through animal/companion-species work to her current standing as a founding figure of multispecies studies and the environmental humanities. In this vault she is present via Strathern's *Partial Connections* (1991/2004) and de la Cadena's *Earth Beings* citation chains, now supplemented by the primary ingest of *Staying with the Trouble* (2016). Her earlier landmark monographs (*Primate Visions*, *Simians, Cyborgs, and Women*, *When Species Meet*) are not yet primary-ingested.
