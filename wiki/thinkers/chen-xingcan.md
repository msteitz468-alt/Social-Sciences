# Xingcan Chen

## Overview
Xingcan Chen is Senior Fellow and Deputy Director of the Institute of Archaeology at the
Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, and Professor in its Graduate School Department of
Archaeology. He is both a working prehistorian and a historian of the discipline of Chinese
archaeology.

## Contributions
Co-author with [[li-liu|Li Liu]] of [[liu-chen-archaeology-of-china-2012|*The Archaeology of
China* (2012)]] and *State Formation in Early China* (2003). His *History of Chinese Prehistoric
Archaeology (1895–1949)* is a standard reflexive account of how the discipline formed under
[[chinese-civilization-origins-debate|nationalist and diffusionist pressures]] — from
Andersson's painted-pottery diffusionism to [[li-chi|Li Chi]]'s indigenist program at
[[anyang|Anyang]].

## Intellectual Genealogy
Longtime collaborator of [[li-liu|Li Liu]]; institutionally rooted in the Institute of
Archaeology (CASS), the successor tradition to the Academia Sinica program founded by
[[li-chi|Li Chi]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals
As a historian of the discipline, Chen has documented the political entanglements of Chinese
archaeology — a reflexive contribution rather than a contested one.

## Historiography
Contemporary; a bridging figure between the Chinese-language excavation tradition and Anglophone
processual archaeology.
