# Saskia Sassen

Dutch-American sociologist of cities, globalization, and the territorial organization of the world economy. Best known for [[global-city-theory|global city theory]] as formulated in *[[sassen-global-city-2001|The Global City]]* (1991; 2nd ed. 2001).

## Overview

Sassen argues that economic globalization does not dissolve cities but reconstitutes a small set of them as strategic sites for the production of the specialized inputs — finance, legal, accounting, management consulting, and related [[producer-services|producer services]] — that make cross-border control and market coordination possible. New York, London, and Tokyo are her primary comparative cases in *The Global City*.

## Contributions

- Named and specified the [[global-city]] as distinct from the older "world city" construct (Hall, Friedmann, Braudel).
- [[global-city-theory]]: seven-hypothesis model — dispersal + integration feed central functions; outsourcing to specialized [[producer-services]]; agglomeration of complex service firms; services (not HQ counts) specify advantage; [[transnational-urban-system|transnational urban systems]]; inequality; informalization.
- **Practices of global control** as production problem: work of producing inputs for managing global production systems and financial marketplaces ([[practices-of-global-control]]).
- Empirical program: FDI and services trade; financial internationalization and crises; producer-service location; city-to-city networks; employment/earnings; class/[[spatial-polarization|spatial polarization]].
- Landmark study: [[sassen-global-city-study]]; deep analysis [[hubs/studies/sassen-global-city-study]]; theory hub [[hubs/theory/global-city-theory]]; debate [[global-city-model-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Adjacent / interlocutors:** world-city literature (Peter Hall; John Friedmann); urban political economy and restructuring ([[castells-manuel|Castells]], Harrison, Fainstein, Marcuse); world-systems-adjacent urbanists (Abu-Lughod); financial geography and producer-services research absorbed in the 2001 edition.
- **Castells dialogue (from Castells Vol. I ingest):** Castells cites Sassen on advanced services and the global city, reframes the global city as “not a place, but a process” within the [[space-of-flows]], and shares the anti-end-of-cities stance while centering network morphology ([[network-society-theory]]). Complementary, not identical programs.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

2001 Epilogue maps six critique clusters (construct/measurement, finance/digitalization, producer services–manufacturing, polarization, state/politics, convergence). Sassen clarifies polarization (not middle-class disappearance; not sole globalization cause), rejects New-York-likeness readings, and **concedes** underdevelopment of the state and politics of global finance. See [[global-city-model-debate]].

## Historiography

Founder of global-cities research; dual reception (policy/mayoral status claims and social-justice critiques of inequality). Collection holds only *The Global City* (2nd ed.); *The Mobility of Labor and Capital* (1988) and *Territory, Authority, Rights* (2006) not yet ingested.
