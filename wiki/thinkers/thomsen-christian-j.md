# Christian Jürgensen Thomsen (1788–1865)

Danish museum archaeologist; curator who established the [[three-age-system]] (Stone–Bronze–Iron) as a practical ordering of northern European antiquities. On [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]]'s account (*[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]]* 2006), Thomsen's relative-dating breakthrough — using closed finds and museum arrangement — ends the chronological impasse of text-poor [[antiquarianism]] and founds prehistoric archaeology as a cumulative science.

## Overview

Thomsen reorganized the Danish royal collection by material ages, published the scheme for a wider public (1836 *Ledetraad*), and trained the next generation including [[worsaae-jens-j-a|J. J. A. Worsaae]]. The three-age system became the basic period vocabulary of European prehistory.

## Contributions

*(Attributed via Trigger 2006.)*

- **[[three-age-system]]:** Stone, Bronze, Iron as successive periods for northern antiquities.
- **Closed finds / association:** objects found together constrain contemporaneity — foundation of relative dating before radiocarbon.
- **Museum as laboratory:** seriation and public display as research method.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Scandinavian antiquarian offices and nationalist interest in the northern past (Trigger: middle-class and patriotic context after Napoleonic wars). Student/successor: Worsaae. Downstream: Montelius typology; Lubbock's English popularization of Palaeolithic/Neolithic within the Stone Age.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Later subdivided Stone Age (Palaeolithic/Neolithic); absolute dates transformed by radiocarbon; the **logic** of relative dating by association remains. Nationalist uses of the ordered northern past are part of the scheme's political context (Trigger).
