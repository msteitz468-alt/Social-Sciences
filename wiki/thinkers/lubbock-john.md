# John Lubbock (Lord Avebury) (1834–1913)

English banker, Liberal MP, polymath naturalist, and founding-era prehistoric archaeologist / evolutionary ethnologist. One of the five major figures of British classical [[social-evolutionism]] (with [[tylor-edward-burnett]], [[mclennan-john-ferguson]], [[maine-henry-sumner]], [[spencer-herbert]]). Primary-ingested here: *[[lubbock-prehistoric-times-1872|Pre-Historic Times]]* (1865; 1872 enlarged edition). *The Origin of Civilisation* (1870) remains secondary via [[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Stocking]].

## Overview

Lubbock made prehistoric archaeology legible as a science linking geology and history for English readers. In *Pre-Historic Times* he divides European prehistory into four epochs and **coins** the labels **[[palaeolithic|Paleolithic]]** and **[[neolithic|Neolithic]]** for the two Stone Ages, popularizing the northern European [[three-age-system]] (Thomsen, Nilsson) while synthesizing Swiss lake-dwellings, Danish shell-mounds, Palaeolithic caves and Somme drift, and North American earthworks. Long chapters on "modern savages" without metal supply ethnographic analogy for prehistoric lifeways — classic founding-era method, with Victorian racial hierarchy language throughout (attributed only).

## Contributions

### From *Pre-Historic Times* (primary, attributed)

- **Four-epoch classification** — Drift/Paleolithic; polished Stone/Neolithic; Bronze; Iron — applied "for the present" to Europe (Ch. I).
- **[[palaeolithic]] / [[neolithic]] coinage** — "This we may call the 'Paleolithic' period" / "'Neolithic' period" (Ch. I); Paleolithic = man coeval with mammoth, cave bear, woolly rhinoceros; Neolithic = polished stone, no utilitarian metal except gold ornaments.
- **Method** — assemblage/co-occurrence over single finds; statistical tables of barrows and lake finds; geological analogy for human deep time ([[antiquity-of-man]]); bronze weapons not Roman (multi-point argument).
- **Regional syntheses** — Swiss *Pfahlbauten*; Danish *kjøkkenmøddinger* (Steenstrup/Worsaae debate); American mound-builders; Quaternary fauna; cave art (Dordogne); Boucher de Perthes / Somme vindication.
- **Ethnographic analogy** — metal-less living peoples as "what the opossum and the sloth are to the geologist" for the antiquary (attributed); anti-[[degenerationism|degenerationism]] for simple arts; independent invention of simple weapons; progressivist moral conclusion (civilization multiplies happiness; "noble savage" false).

### From Stocking on *Origin of Civilisation* (secondary, attributed Stocking 1987)

Religion and marriage synthesis; early claim that lower races have no religion; McLennan polyandry as exceptional; colonial anthropology linked to domestic reform; Ethnological Society / Anthropological Institute presidency (1864/1871).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Archaeological:** Thomsen, Nilsson, Worsaae; Keller (Swiss lakes); Steenstrup; Boucher de Perthes; Evans, Prestwich; Christy (Dordogne companion).
- **Evolutionary circle:** Tylor, McLennan, Huxley, Maine, Spencer; Darwin network (prefaces, family connections).
- **Downstream:** Palaeolithic/Neolithic vocabulary permanent in archaeology; sociocultural evolutionism of the book largely absorbed/abandoned (Stocking: least remembered of the five among sociocultural anthropologists).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Armchair compilation (Fison's "clerk at a pound a week" jab, via Stocking).
- Europe-first periodization; living peoples ranked as living fossils.
- Progress narrative and racial hierarchy language — historical artifact, not findings.

### Trigger on Lubbock as evolutionary synthesizer (2006)

*(Attributed — [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]], [[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]] ch. 5.)* Trigger places Lubbock at the center of [[nineteenth-century-evolutionary-archaeology]]: neighbor of Darwin; *Pre-Historic Times* and *Origin of Civilisation* as the English synthesis linking Palaeolithic archaeology to unilinear cultural evolution; **break with Enlightenment psychic-unity universalism** by tying progress to European biological as well as cultural preeminence; template for colonialist archaeologies that treated indigenous societies as static and change as external. Trigger's social-context thesis: mid-Victorian middle-class interests under British hegemony shape the synthesis — not only "scientific advance."
- *Origin of Civilisation* theoretical program did not survive as research tradition; archaeological period terms did.

**In Gamble *Timewalkers* (1994).** [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]] uses Lubbock as a foil for progressive/adaptive optimism: the view that natural laws of improvement license sanguine hopes for the future, and "creeping weeds"/human-drift images of prehistoric expansion — positions Gamble rejects in favor of purposive, often exaptive colonization (attributed, *[[gamble-timewalkers-1994|Timewalkers]]* ch. 1, 6, 11). Also names Lubbock among creators of a prehistoric past who sought "living prehistory" in present indigenous societies (attributed, ch. 11).
