# Paul V. Kroskrity

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist (UCLA); central organizer of the [[language-ideology]] research program. Editor of [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language* (2000)]], which collects the 1994 SAR advanced seminar on language ideologies. Long-term fieldworker among the [[arizona-tewa|Arizona Tewa]].

## Contributions

- Programmatic framing of **[[language-regimentation|regimes of language]]** and multi-level language-ideological analysis (intro to *Regimes*).
- Arizona Tewa case: indigenous purism, ethnic identity, critique of earlier Dozier representation (*Regimes* ch. 9).
- Co-editor of earlier *Language Ideologies* collections (with Schieffelin & Woolard) — densify when ingested.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Part of the post-Hymes generation of US linguistic anthropology linking [[ethnography-of-speaking]], Silversteinian semiotics, and political anthropology of identity. Seminar cohort: Irvine, Gal, Silverstein, Bauman, Briggs, Hill, Philips, Schieffelin, Errington.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Densify from reception literature; program-internal debates concern scope of “ideology” and relation to political economy.

## Historiography

Primary self-positioning in *Regimes* introduction and Tewa chapter; secondary histories of the language-ideology boom still thin in this vault.
