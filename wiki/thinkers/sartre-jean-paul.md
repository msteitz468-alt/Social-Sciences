# Jean-Paul Sartre

## Overview

Jean-Paul Sartre (1905–1980), French philosopher and the leading figure of postwar **existentialism** and phenomenology, and — in his late *Critique of Dialectical Reason* (1960) — of an existential Marxism grounding social knowledge in historical **praxis**. In this vault he appears as the polemical antagonist of [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss's]] "History and Dialectic" chapter, the "total intellectual" whom [[structuralism]] displaced as the prestige position of the French field. This page is a network stub grounded in that engagement; no Sartre primary text is ingested.

## Contributions

- **Existentialism / phenomenology** (*Being and Nothingness*, 1943): consciousness, freedom, and the primacy of the situated subject.
- **Existential Marxism** (*Critique of Dialectical Reason*, 1960): dialectical reason rooted in historical praxis; **totalization**; History as the medium of human self-constitution — the target of Lévi-Strauss's critique.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Lévi-Strauss (attributed) charged Sartre with giving History and the (sociologized) Cartesian *cogito* a privileged, "mythical" status, with an ethnocentric relegation of societies "without history," and with resting his account of dialectical social reality on "secondary incidentals of life in society" (strikes, queues, football matches). See the [[levi-strauss-sartre-debate]] for the full exchange. The structure/subject dispute Sartre anchored was itself later displaced by the practice turn ([[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]], [[practice-theory]]).
