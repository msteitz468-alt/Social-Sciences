# Margaret W. Conkey (b. 1944)

American archaeologist; co-editor with [[gero-joan-m|Joan M. Gero]] of *[[gero-conkey-engendering-archaeology-1991|Engendering Archaeology]]* (1991). With [[spector-janet|Janet Spector]] (1984), author of the agenda-setting call for an archaeology of gender. Advisory editor, Blackwell Social Archaeology series ([[hodder-ian|Hodder]] general editor).

## Overview

Conkey’s introduction with Gero frames **engendering** as studying gender systems in prehistory *and* in archaeological practice, using women as a neglected entry point into sex/gender analysis. Methodologically she insists gender attribution with “fixity” is neither end nor means of feminist archaeology. Her Magdalenian chapter reorients Upper Paleolithic interpretation toward **contexts of action and power** in material culture — not “revealing” Magdalenian women with skeletal certainty, but restoring people as active agents in Ice Age social life.

## Contributions

*(Attributed from *Engendering* 1991 and works cited.)*

- **Conkey & Spector 1984** program call (cited throughout the volume as the unfulfilled agenda).
- **Co-editing *Engendering Archaeology***: three aims (expose bias / find women and gender relations / problematize gender and difference); rejection of biological determinism; gender as historically contingent construction; dual object past + practice.
- **Magdalenian gender (ch.3):** conceptual reorientation of European UP archaeology; beyond-the-households / aggregation contexts; connectedness and undervalued cordage; critique of dehumanized adaptation narratives and UP as monolith; Cueto de la Mina Level B as procedural entry.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborates with Gero and Spector; series network with Hodder, Leone, Shennan, Trigger. Draws on women’s history (Scott; Gordon, Buhle, Dye) and [[feminist-anthropology]] (Moore, Strathern, Rosaldo & Lamphere). Positions program to draw on [[post-processual-archaeology]] social/symbolic openings without reducing feminism to that school.

## Later Contribution — Conkey and Gero (1997), per Johnson (2011)

*(Attributed; see [[johnson-archaeological-theory-2011]].)* With Gero, Conkey (1997) distinguished the theoretically diverse "archaeology of gender" from specifically **feminist archaeology**, defined via four criteria: (1) the inseparability of politics and knowledge-products; (2) rationality as a "mythical conflation" never realized in practice; (3) association with an "intimate," nuanced cognitive style over categorical thinking; (4) a challenge to basic disciplinary arrangements (Conkey and Gero 1997, as quoted by Johnson). See [[feminist-archaeology]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Deep Paleolithic gender systems remain underdetermined; “contexts of action” is a research orientation more than a closed reconstruction. Later work (not yet ingested) extends her Paleolithic and feminist programs.
