# Friedrich Engels

## Overview

Friedrich Engels (1820–1895), German political theorist, businessman, and revolutionary, co-author with [[marx-karl|Karl Marx]] of the [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] (1848) and co-founder of [[historical-materialism|historical materialism]]. After Marx’s death he became the doctrine’s principal editor, popularizer, and clarifier. His *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State]]* (1884; substantially revised 4th ed. 1891/92) extends historical materialism into kinship, the [[gens]], [[private-property]], and [[state-formation|the origin of the state]], reworking [[morgan-lewis-henry|Lewis H. Morgan]]’s *Ancient Society* (1877) with Marx’s précis notes. *The Condition of the Working Class in England* (1845) is not yet ingested.

## Contributions

### From the *Manifesto* (with Marx)

- [[class-struggle]] thesis; [[mode-of-production]] framework; [[bourgeoisie]] / [[proletariat]]; ideology thesis.
- Editorial custodian of the 1888 English *Manifesto* (Samuel Moore) ingested in this wiki.

### From *Origin of the Family* (1884/1891) — all attributed

- **Dual production thesis.** Determining factor in history is production and reproduction of immediate life: (1) means of existence; (2) human beings themselves. Social institutions are conditioned by the stage of labor **and** of the [[family]]; at low productivity, kinship dominates; rising productivity generates [[private-property]], classes, and [[the-state|the state]] as the content of written history.
- **Morgan ladder + materialist mechanism.** Adopts [[savagery-barbarism-civilization]] periodization by food-production criteria; maps family forms ([[group-marriage]] → [[pairing-family]] → patriarchal → [[monogamy]]) and gentile constitution onto it; supplies economic treatment Morgan lacked.
- **Mother-right and monogamy.** Accepts [[mother-right]] as historically prior; ties its “overthrow” to male-owned herds and heirship — “the world historical defeat of the female sex.” Claims the first class oppression coincides with oppression of women in monogamous marriage.
- **Gens and state.** Reconstructs the [[gens]] (type: mid-19th-c. [[iroquois]] via Morgan; Greek, Roman, Celtic, German cases) as pre-state political form; defines the state as product of irreconcilable class antagonisms, with territorial membership and a special public force distinct from the self-armed people; normally the state of the economically dominant class; exceptional relative independence when classes balance (absolute monarchy, Bonapartism, Bismarck) — see [[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]].
- **[[withering-away-of-the-state]].** With classes, the state falls; free association of producers places state machinery in the “museum of antiquity” beside spinning-wheel and bronze axe.
- **Historiography of kinship science.** 1891 preface narrates [[bachofen-johann-jakob|Bachofen]] → McLennan → Lubbock → Morgan; polemic against English anthropologists’ “killing by silence.”

All theoretical claims remain **attributed**, never wiki-voice findings. Victorian unilinear sequence is contested: [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].

### As editor of *Capital*, Volume 3 (1894) — and author within it

Nine years of editorial work on Marx's single, gap-ridden 1864–65 draft, documented candidly in the 1894 preface: **Chapter 4 is Engels's own composition** (title only in the manuscript); Ch. 3 was worked up via Samuel Moore's mathematical résumé; Part Five (credit) defeated three completion attempts and was finally arranged from "a disordered jumble of notes" with Chs. 33–35 compiled by Engels; the ground-rent chapters were reordered; substantive additions are bracketed and initialled. The preface also runs the **prize-essay survey** (Lexis, Schmidt, Fireman, Wolf, Loria, Stiebeling) on how an average profit rate can form without violating the law of value — the opening round of the [[transformation-problem-debate]]. Engels's **Supplement and Addendum (1895)** contains his own essays: the historical reading of the law of value (simple commodity production as historically real, his reply to Sombart and Schmidt) and "The Stock Exchange" (financialization and colonization notes) — Engels's positions, not Marx's text. He intended to edit *Theories of Surplus-Value* as "Volume 4" but died first (Kautsky published it 1905). See [[marx-capital-volume-3-1894]].

### As editor of *Capital*, Volume 2 (1885/1893)

Engels compiled *[[marx-capital-volume-2-1885|Capital, Volume 2]]* from Marx's manuscripts II–VIII (1865–1878), taking Manuscript II (1870) as base and Manuscript VIII (1878) for Part Three, on Marx's deathbed commission to "make something" of the material. His prefaces document the construction in detail; he states his own interventions total under ten printed pages and are purely formal. But his editorial voice is substantive in places — signed "F.E." footnotes include one (Ch. 15) judging that Marx's turnover calculations "contain many errors and contradictions" and ascribed "undeserved significance" to the setting-free of money capital. The 1884 second-edition preface also rebuts the **Rodbertus priority dispute** (the charge that Marx plagiarized surplus-value from Rodbertus) with a biographical and theoretical case culminating in the Lavoisier/Priestley analogy: predecessors found surplus-value; Marx identified what it was. These prefaces are key documents both for the Marx–Engels editorial relationship and for source criticism of Capital II–III.

### Feminist-anthropology reception (Sacks 1974)

In *[[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974|Woman, Culture, and Society]]*, Karen Sacks ("Engels Revisited") reconstructs *Origin* as nearly the only materialist account of women's status varying by economic and political relations; she holds Engels's main ideas correct despite ethnographic errors, stretches "social/public labor" for nonclass societies, rejects a pure evolutionary package, and argues women can be **social adults and wives simultaneously**. Her Mbuti–Lovedu–Pondo–Ganda continuum illustrates public labor as the key status variable. This is a *position* in feminist anthropology's materialist wing, not a wiki-voice vindication of Engels.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Collaborator:** [[marx-karl|Karl Marx]] — *Manifesto*; Marx’s 1880–81 précis of Morgan as the occasion of *Origin*; Engels cites Marx on family containing slavery/serfdom in germ and on property differences inside the gens.
- **Developed from:** [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]] (*Ancient Society*; Iroquois gens); classical and Germanic sources for Greece, Rome, Celts, Germans (Engels’s own additions).
- **Reacted against:** Bachofen’s religious lever for mother-right; McLennan’s legalistic exogamy scheme; Hegelian state-as-moral-idea (explicitly in *Origin* ch. IX); racial explanations of Germanic “rejuvenation” of Europe.
- **Adjacent:** Victorian [[social-evolutionism]] (Tylor, Lubbock, Spencer) — related family of stage theories, not identical.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Unilinear kinship evolution** largely abandoned as world history (group marriage stage, universal mother-right, single gens→state path); see [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
- **Marx–Engels problem:** post-1883 systematizations sometimes read as hardening Marx into determinist “scientific socialism”; *Origin* is a key late-Engels text in that debate.
- **Withering-away thesis:** twentieth-century socialist states that did not wither are the central empirical/political counter; defenders treat the thesis as conditional on classlessness.
- Feminist receptions often retain the monogamy–property–women’s oppression link while discarding the prehistoric ladder.
- Ethnographic quality: Engels did no kinship fieldwork; Iroquois material is Morgan’s mid-19th-c. documentation under colonial conditions.

### Reception in Lenin, *State and Revolution* (1917 — attributed)

[[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] (*[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|State and Revolution]]*) treats Engels as co-equal canonical authority on the state: long quotations from *Origin* (irreconcilability; public power; withering horizon), *Anti-Dühring* / *Socialism: Utopian and Scientific*, *Housing Question*, *On Authority* (vs anarchists: revolution as “the most authoritarian thing there is”; Commune needed armed authority), the Bebel letter, and the critique of the Erfurt Programme (democratic republic; opportunism; local self-government without appointed bureaucracy). Lenin’s dual formula — bourgeois state **abolished**, proletarian state **withers** — is his gloss on Engels, central to [[marxist-theory-of-the-state]] and [[withering-away-of-the-state]]. “Special bodies of armed men” is Lenin’s synthesis, not a verbatim Engels catchphrase (editor’s caution in the Haymarket edition).

### Reception in Lenin, *Materialism and Empirio-Criticism* (1908/1909 — attributed)

[[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]]'s *[[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909|Materialism and
Empirio-Criticism]]* treats Engels (via *Anti-Dühring* and *Ludwig Feuerbach*, neither yet
independently ingested) as the primary authority for [[dialectical-materialism]]'s
epistemology, quoting him at length: matter/objects exist independently of and prior to
consciousness, with sensations as "images" or "reflections" (*Gedanken-Abbilder*) of those
objects; consciousness and thought are "products of the human brain"; the **alizarin
example** — the dye was a Kantian "thing-in-itself" until organic chemistry synthesized it
from coal tar, becoming a "thing-for-us" — as "the most telling refutation" of the
unknowable thing-in-itself, and the **criterion-of-practice** passage ("before there was
argumentation there was action... the proof of the pudding is in the eating"), which Lenin
called the materialist theory of reflection "presented with absolute clarity." Lenin quoted
Engels's *Anti-Dühring* on **absolute and relative truth** ("sovereignty of thought" realized
only through humanity's "unending... succession of generations") and on **freedom and
necessity** ("freedom is the appreciation of necessity... Freedom does not consist in an
imaginary independence from natural laws, but in the knowledge of these laws"), and on
**space and time** as objective "basic forms of all being" ("being out of time is just as
gross an absurdity as being out of space"), each cited against Bogdanov, Bazarov, and other
Russian "Machists." Lenin also quoted Engels's methodological dictum that "with each
epoch-making discovery even in the sphere of natural science, materialism has to change its
form" — treating revision of materialism's form (not its substance) as required by new
physics, not as revisionism. See [[dialectical-materialism]] for the fuller reconstruction.

### 1917 wartime and imperialism-theory reception (attributed)

Per [[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]], Lenin cited Engels's remark (c. 1890) that once
one reaches "the trust," capitalist "planlessness disappears," applying it to the wartime
"military state" as evidence that state-coordinated planning under [[finance-capital]] could
intensify rather than relieve exploitation — part of Lenin's state-monopoly-capitalism
argument; see [[lenin-theory-of-imperialism]].

### Further Lenin reception, 1894–1918 (attributed)

- **What the "Friends of the People" Are (1894, [[lenin-what-the-friends-of-the-people-are-1894]]).** Lenin quotes Engels's *Anti-Dühring* to argue that Marx's "negation of the negation" characterization of capitalism's expropriation of the expropriators followed, rather than substituted for, prior historical-factual proof — i.e. Engels is Lenin's source for reading dialectic as a secondary characterization of an independently-established historical process, not a deductive method (see [[dialectical-materialism]]). Lenin also quotes Engels's *The Housing Question* (*Zur Wohnungsfrage*) at length: German rural domestic industry, combined with kitchen-gardening and agriculture, formed the broad basis of Germany's large-scale industry, tying wage-workers to their locality (worsening exploitation) while also spreading the industrial revolution and the revolutionary working-class movement across rural Germany rather than confining it to the cities — so that a "victorious rising" in German cities would only be possible once the rural districts too were "ripe for revolutionary change." Lenin draws the parallel to Russia's domestic-industry-tied peasantry.
- **Renegade Kautsky (1918, [[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]).** Lenin quotes Engels's letter to Bebel (March 28, 1875): "so long as the proletariat still needs the state, it does not need it in the interests of freedom but in order to hold down its adversaries, and as soon as it becomes possible to speak of freedom the state as such ceases to exist" — and Engels's Introduction to Marx's *The Civil War in France*: "the state is nothing but a machine for the oppression of one class by another… in the democratic republic no less than in the monarchy." He also quotes Engels's essay on authority (cited generically, not by title, in this slice) on the Paris Commune: "A revolution is certainly the most authoritarian thing there is… Would the Paris Commune have lasted more than a day if it had not used the authority of the armed people against the bourgeoisie?" — and Engels's 1891 remark that "superstitious belief in the state… has been carried over into the general thinking of the bourgeoisie and even of many workers" in Germany, which Lenin applies to Kautsky's refusal to let proletarian organizations (the Soviets) become state organizations. Lenin credits Engels with the maxim that "the disarming of the workers was the first commandment for the bourgeoisie" after the French pattern, and states that Marx and Engels "repeatedly emphasised" that the first task of every victorious revolution is to smash the old army and build a new one.
- **"Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?" (Oct. 1917, [[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]).** Lenin invokes Engels's concept of "latent socialism," predicting that once state power passes to the oppressed classes, "for every ten thousand overt and concealed enemies of working-class rule… there will arise a million new fighters who had been politically dormant."
