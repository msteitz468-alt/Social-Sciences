# John L. Comaroff

> Documented from *[[comaroff-of-revelation-and-revolution-1991|Of Revelation and Revolution, Vol. 1]]* (1991). South African–born anthropologist; with [[comaroff-jean|Jean Comaroff]] a leading practitioner of the [[historical-anthropology|historical anthropology]] of colonialism.

## Overview

John L. Comaroff is an anthropologist of law, politics, and colonialism in southern Africa. His early work (*Rules and Processes*, 1981, with Simon Roberts) reconceived dispute and rule in Tswana political life. With [[comaroff-jean|Jean Comaroff]] he authored the two-volume *[[comaroff-of-revelation-and-revolution-1991|Of Revelation and Revolution]]* (1991, 1997) and the methodological essays collected in *Ethnography and the Historical Imagination* (1992).

## Contributions

- Co-developer of **[[colonization-of-consciousness]]** and **[[the-long-conversation]]**.
- Extended the analysis of **[[southern-tswana|Southern Tswana]]** chiefship, law, and politics into the historical study of colonial power (see *[[comaroff-revelation-revolution-study]]*).
- With Jean Comaroff, articulated a program of **[[historical-anthropology]]** insisting that structure and agency, sign and material practice, be analyzed together over time.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained at the University of Cape Town and the London School of Economics, in the southern-African tradition running from [[schapera-isaac|Isaac Schapera]] and [[gluckman-max|Max Gluckman]]. Long at the University of Chicago; later Harvard. His work is inseparable from his lifelong collaboration with [[comaroff-jean|Jean Comaroff]].
