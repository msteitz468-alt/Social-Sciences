# Jens Jacob Asmussen Worsaae (1821–1885)

Danish field archaeologist who demonstrated and internationalized [[thomsen-christian-j|Thomsen]]'s [[three-age-system]] through excavation and publication. [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]] (*[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]]* 2006) treats Worsaae as the key agent carrying Scandinavian prehistoric method into European archaeology and as a politically aware interpreter of Danish interest in the past after the British bombardments of Copenhagen and the French Revolutionary era.

## Overview

Where Thomsen worked primarily through museum ordering, Worsaae tested period sequences in the field, wrote for international audiences, and held senior state antiquarian posts. He figures in kitchen-midden research and in debates that shaped early economic and environmental readings of the Danish past.

## Contributions

*(Attributed via Trigger 2006.)*

- Field validation of Stone–Bronze–Iron succession.
- International popularization of Scandinavian method (*Primeval Antiquities*).
- Political historiography of Danish archaeology's social context (his own later comments on consolation and national past — as reported by Trigger).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained under/with Thomsen; dialogue with Nilsson, Steenstrup (middens); influence on Lubbock and British prehistorians. Downstream culture-historical and evolutionary uses of the three-age scaffold.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Later absolute chronology and culture labels supersede early schemes; Worsaae's nationalist framing is reflexive content. Methodological core (field + typology + association) absorbed into ordinary practice.

## Historiography

Canonical "second founder" of Scandinavian prehistoric archaeology beside Thomsen. Source: [[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006]].
