# Mary Douglas

## Overview

British social anthropologist (1921–2007), trained under [[evans-pritchard-e-e|E. E. Evans-Pritchard]] at Oxford. Best known for *[[douglas-purity-and-danger-1966|Purity and Danger]]* (1966), which reoriented the comparative study of pollution and [[taboo]] around cultural classification and social order ([[purity-pollution-theory]]; [[douglas-purity-and-danger-study]]). Fieldwork among the Lele of the Kasai (monograph 1963) informed the comparative program; she reported that writing about Lele food rules took the best part of ten years and that the book's dirt-and-contagion argument first cohered in the 1950s. Later work developed grid/group analysis (*Natural Symbols*, 1970), consumption (*The World of Goods*, 1979), risk (*Risk and Culture* with Aaron Wildavsky, 1982), and a revised biblical scholarship (*In the Wilderness*, 1993; *Leviticus as Literature*, 1999).

## Contributions

- **[[matter-out-of-place|Dirt as matter out of place]]** — dirt as residual of classification, not absolute substance; "Where there is dirt there is system."
- **[[ritual-pollution|Pollution as social and symbolic system]]** — instrumental and expressive levels; powers inhering in the structure of ideas.
- **[[anomaly]]** — interstitial cases attract taboo, danger, or creative ritual.
- Comparative re-reading of magic/miracle, body symbolism, pollution–morals links, sex pollution under contradictory gender systems, and dirt-affirming ritual (see study page).
- **[[grid-and-group|Grid and group]]** (*[[douglas-natural-symbols-1970|Natural Symbols]]*, 1970; Pelican 1973) — two dimensions of classification scope/coherence and bounded pressure generating cosmologies and ritual styles ([[grid-group-analysis]]; [[douglas-natural-symbols-study]]).
- **[[natural-symbols|No natural symbols]]** — body media are selected and socially naturalized; seek natural systems of symbolizing.
- **[[anti-ritualism]]** — contempt of ritual has social locations (elaborated-code / weak-bond), not pure spiritual progress.
- **Self-correction on Leviticus:** 2002 preface confesses a "major mistake" in Ch. 3 of *Purity and Danger* and points to later covenant/body–altar readings.

- **trained_under:** [[evans-pritchard-e-e]] (Oxford). Acknowledgements: structural approach "made its direct impact on me" through Evans-Pritchard's analysis of the Nuer political system (1940); frameless-chassis analogy for systemic analysis without central organs of government.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Biblical specialists contested 1966 Leviticus; Douglas herself substantially revised it (2002 preface; 1990s biblical books) — three mistakes she named: circularity of anomaly reading; absence of positive social-system implications for biblical Hebrews; wrong acceptance that God made "abominable" creatures.
- "Primitive"/"modern" contrast in Ch. 5 carries period language even as she dismantles "primitive mentality."
- 2002: original taboo examples mainly conservative; would balance with radical taboos.
- Grid/group and risk work after 1970 are distinct programs — do not collapse them into 1966.

## Historiography

*Purity and Danger* sold ~200 copies in its first years; Norman Franklin called it a potential "sleeper" — Douglas (2002) reports he was right. She frames unfashionability in a 1960s anti-structure cultural moment ("praising structure and control"). Hub intellectual biography not built this session (summary only).
