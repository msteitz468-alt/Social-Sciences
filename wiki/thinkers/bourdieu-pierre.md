# Pierre Bourdieu

> Graduate-level intellectual biography: [[hubs/thinkers/bourdieu-pierre|Thinkers Hub — Pierre Bourdieu]].

## Overview

Pierre Bourdieu (1930–2002) was a French sociologist and anthropologist whose [[practice-theory]] sought a third path between objectivist structuralism and subjectivist philosophies of the subject. Ingested primary sources in this vault are *[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline of a Theory of Practice]]* (French *Esquisse*, 1972; Nice English 1977) — the founding statement of [[practice-theory]] — its expanded reworking *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (French *Le sens pratique*, 1980; Nice English c. 1990), *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]* (with Passeron, 1970), and *[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]* (French 1979; Nice English 1984). The first three revisit Algeria/Kabyle and Béarn fieldwork and the school; *Distinction* is the large-n French class-and-taste study.

In the Preface, Bourdieu situates his generation between [[levi-strauss-claude|Claude Lévi-Strauss]]'s scientific intellectual style and Jean-Paul Sartre's politically committed "total intellectual," and ties the theoretical project to late-1950s/early-1960s Algeria: scientific analysis of Algerian society under colonial war was, he wrote, inseparable from understanding the social bases of the independence struggle and from refusing racist contempt for local tradition.

## Contributions

From *Logic of Practice* (attributed):

- **[[practice-theory]]** as double break with native experience *and* with the observer's scholastic point of view; critique of the theoretical error that sets up the model of practice as the principle of practice.
- **[[habitus]]** — durable, transposable dispositions generating regulated improvisation without a conductor or explicit rules.
- **[[practical-sense|Practical sense]] (*sens pratique*)** — feel for the game; practice's irreversible time vs the detemporalizing calendar, map, and genealogy of science.
- **[[hexis|Bodily hexis]]** — political mythology realized in posture, gait, speech; sexual division of labor incorporated.
- **[[doxa]], [[illusio]], [[misrecognition]]** — taken-for-granted world, investment in field stakes, collective denial that makes domination and gift economies viable.
- **[[symbolic-capital]] and [[symbolic-violence]]** — denied capital; gentle, misrecognized modes of domination vs overt violence.
- **Strategy vs rule** in kinship and land (Kabyle parallel-cousin marriage; Béarn *adot* and primogeniture) and in ritual (generative schemes of reunite/separate; Kabyle house as inverted world).
- **Socio-analysis** — every genuine sociology is inseparably analysis of the analyst's position (*proposed* a style of [[reflexive-sociology]] distinct from Gouldner's).

From *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]* (with [[passeron-jean-claude|Passeron]], 1970; attributed):

- **A general theory of [[symbolic-violence]]** built *more geometrico*: all [[pedagogic-action]] is "the imposition of a [[cultural-arbitrary]] by an arbitrary power," working through misrecognized [[pedagogic-authority]] and durable [[pedagogic-work]].
- **[[cultural-reproduction]]** — the school reproduces the class distribution of [[cultural-capital]], and through it the class structure, via [[self-elimination]] and the certification effect, under the guise of meritocracy ([[relative-autonomy-of-the-educational-system|relative autonomy]]). Founding text of the [[sociology-of-education]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-study|Studies Hub]].

From *[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]* (1979/1984; attributed; French survey 1963 + 1967–68, n=1,217):

- **Taste as classification.** “Taste classifies, and it classifies the classifier” — cultural consumption legitimates social differences ([[distinction]]).
- **[[social-space]]** — relational topology of positions by volume, composition, and trajectory of capital; homologous lifestyle space via [[correspondence-analysis]].
- **Class-habitus taste typology:** [[aesthetic-disposition]] / [[pure-gaze]] (dominant distance from necessity); [[cultural-goodwill]] (petit-bourgeois recognition without ease); [[taste-for-necessity]] (working-class virtue of necessity).
- **[[reconversion-strategies]]** under structural change (schooling boom, diploma inflation, new petite bourgeoisie).
- **Politics in social space** — opinion competence, media, left/right mapped onto the same capital axes; classification struggle as dimension of class struggle.
- Study + deep analysis: [[bourdieu-distinction-study]] · [[hubs/studies/bourdieu-distinction-study]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

External critiques of determinism, residual structuralism, gender, colonial ethnography, and the capital metaphor are **not yet ingested** as primary sources. *Logic* itself answers the determinism charge with hysteresis, strategy, and regulated improvisation. Record further positions as sources arrive. Ethics of wartime ethnography: Bourdieu states that photographs and data sometimes came from houses damaged by French military expulsion — a production-context fact for the [[kabyle]] empirical base.

## Historiography

- Primary apparatus here: *[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline of a Theory of Practice]]* (1972/1977), *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (1980/1990), *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]* (with Passeron, 1970), and *[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]* (1979/1984).
- Queued, not ingested: *The Inheritors*; *The State Nobility*; Algeria monographs *Travail et travailleurs en Algérie* (1963) and *Le Déracinement* (1964); “forms of capital” essays.
- English reception via Nice translations shaped Anglophone "practice theory" and cultural-capital vocabulary (*habitus*, field, capital, distinction).
- In American cultural sociology, [[swidler-ann|Swidler]] draws on Bourdieu's [[practice-theory|practical logic]] (culture as capacity, not rule) in building [[culture-in-action|toolkit theory]] — citing *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]*'s "practice has a logic which is not that of the logician" and the point that contradictory schemas coexist because they are rarely confronted — while claiming her [[cultural-toolkit|repertoire]] is more plural and switchable than [[habitus]]. Attributed to Swidler.

## Engagement — Lamont *Money, Morals, and Manners* (1992)

[[lamont-michele|Michèle Lamont]] (*[[lamont-money-morals-manners-1992|Money, Morals, and Manners]]*; [[lamont-money-morals-manners-study]]) builds on Bourdieu’s claim that shared cultural style contributes to class reproduction and that actors compete over definitions of high-status signals, while arguing that *[[bourdieu-distinction-study|Distinction]]* underestimates moral signals, overgeneralizes Parisian intellectual cultural exclusivism, treats socioeconomic status as definitionally supreme, and centers [[habitus]] on proximate structural conditions at the expense of national repertoires and macrostructure. Lamont’s “boundary approach” keeps plural [[high-status-signals]] (moral, socioeconomic, cultural) as an empirical question rather than presupposing [[cultural-capital]] as the main axis of [[distinction]] (all attributed to Lamont 1992).
