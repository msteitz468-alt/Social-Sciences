# Donald P. Green

American political scientist (b. 1961), known within this wiki as co-author, with
[[shapiro-ian|Ian Shapiro]], of *[[green-shapiro-pathologies-1994|Pathologies of
Rational Choice Theory]]* (1994) — the book that opened the
[[rational-choice-controversy-debate|rational choice controversy]] in political
science.

## Overview

At the time of the book Green was a political scientist at Yale University working
on political behavior, public opinion, and quantitative methodology. In *Pathologies*
he and Shapiro appraise the empirical record of applied rational choice theory,
arguing its failures are [[method-driven-research|method-driven rather than
theory-driven]]. Green's methodological commitments — later prominent in his work on
field experiments — inform the book's insistence that theory be disciplined by
empirical test.

## Contributions

Co-developed, with Shapiro, the diagnostic vocabulary of
[[method-driven-research|methodological pathologies]] (post hoc theory development,
formulating tests the theory cannot fail, arbitrary domain restriction, projecting
evidence from theory) and the contrast between method/theory-driven and
**problem-driven** research.
