# Charles Darwin

> Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/darwin-charles|Thinkers Hub — Darwin]].

## Overview

Charles Darwin (1809–1882) is treated here for his place in the formation of evolutionary anthropology and [[biological-anthropology]], not as a general biography of his botanical or geological work. *On the Origin of Species* (1859) made common descent and [[natural-selection]] the default natural-history frame; *[[darwin-descent-of-man-1871|The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex]]* (1871) applied that frame to humankind — bodily continuity, mental continuity, the evolution of the [[moral-sense]], the classificatory status of [[race|human races]], and the long comparative development of [[sexual-selection]]. Historiographically (per [[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Stocking]]), classical sociocultural evolutionism was not a simple transfer of biological Darwinism into society, yet Darwin’s time-depth, naturalism, and *Descent*’s dense engagement with [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]], [[lubbock-john|Lubbock]], and [[mclennan-john-ferguson|McLennan]] fixed him inside the Victorian “science of man.”

## Contributions

### Descent of man (1871) — primary ingest

Darwin stated three objects: (1) whether man descends from a pre-existing form; (2) the manner of his development; (3) the value of differences between the “so-called races of man.” He took [[antiquity-of-man]] as already demonstrated (Lyell, Lubbock, Boucher de Perthes) and deferred man–ape morphological continuity largely to [[huxley-thomas-henry|Huxley]].

**Bodily continuity.** Homology, embryology, and rudiments supply the case when affinities/distribution/succession (usable for groups of species) are unavailable for a single species. Man is built on the mammalian plan; early development is shared with other vertebrates; rudiments (ear muscles, nictitating fold, coccyx, lanugo, etc.) mark descent. Erect posture and its structural consequences, plus tools and sociality, explain human dominance under [[natural-selection]].

**Mental continuity.** “No fundamental difference between man and the higher mammals in their mental faculties” — difference of degree, not kind: shared emotions, curiosity, imitation, memory, reason, tool use; language as art grounded in instinctive tendency, co-evolving with brain; sense of beauty and belief in spirits continuous with animal display and (via Tylor) dream-derived animism.

**Moral sense.** Social instincts + intellect → conscience; within-tribe altruism paradox solved partly by **tribe-level selection** (patriotic, sympathetic tribes conquer); praise/blame and reciprocity as within-tribe aids; complex moral sense later ruled by reason, religion, instruction, habit. See [[moral-sense]].

**Races.** Against ranking races as distinct species: fusion, variability, graduation. Preferred rank: **sub-species**; evolutionary [[monogenism]] dissolves the monogenist–[[polygenism|polygenist]] dispute. External racial differences not well explained by climate, use/disuse, or natural selection on serviceable traits → **[[sexual-selection]]** as chief agency (beauty standards differing by race; hairlessness; skin colour). Period racial typology and “savage”/“civilised” ranking are Darwin’s Victorian constructs — recorded as attributed positions, not wiki-voice findings.

**Sexual selection.** Dual mechanism: male combat and female choice; [[secondary-sexual-characters]] across the animal kingdom; application to human sex differences and racial exteriors (Part II + ch. XIX–XX).

**Civilised nations and selection (ch. V).** Darwin, following Greg, Wallace, and [[galton-francis|Galton]], held that civilisation checks elimination of the weak, which “must be highly injurious to the race of man,” yet **refused** to abandon sympathy — “the noblest part of our nature.” This passage is a root of later [[social-darwinism]] and eugenics discourse but is not identical with laissez-faire Sumnerism.

### Stocking’s historiographical placement (secondary)

Stocking argues classical sociocultural evolutionism had independent utilitarian and comparative-method roots; Darwinism contributed time-depth and the demand for naturalistic explanation more than a social mechanism. *Descent* nonetheless cites Lubbock, Tylor, and McLennan extensively; Darwin was sceptical of McLennan’s original promiscuity; after hearing *Primitive Culture* he urged Tylor toward moral evolution. Beagle Fuegian encounter recorded as visceral doubt about common humanity under a still-Lamarckian biocultural “race” category (Stocking).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Upstream:** Malthus (population pressure); Lyell (deep time); Lamarck (descent tradition Darwin partly rejects); comparative anatomy (Hunter, Owen — Owen contested); Humboldtian travel natural history.
- **Peers / allies:** [[huxley-thomas-henry|Huxley]] (*Man’s Place in Nature*); A. R. Wallace (independent NS; later divergence on human mind and hairlessness); Haeckel (genealogy of man — Darwin said Haeckel’s 1868/70 book almost made *Descent* unnecessary).
- **Anthropological interlocutors:** [[lubbock-john]], [[tylor-edward-burnett]], [[mclennan-john-ferguson]], [[spencer-herbert]] (evolutionary psychology priority partly conceded), [[galton-francis]] (inheritance, genius, civilised selection).
- **Downstream:** evolutionary anthropology and paleoanthropology; debates over group selection and evolutionary ethics; contested legacy in race science and eugenics (see [[race-and-culture-debate]], [[social-darwinism]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Wallace:** body little modified after mind; guided development for human peculiarities (hairlessness) — Darwin counters with sexual selection.
- **Polygenist race science:** Darwin’s monogenism undercut separate-species racism but retained hierarchical period language and extinction-of-races naturalism.
- **Social Darwinism / eugenics:** selective appropriation of ch. V; Stocking distinguishes classical evolutionist optimism from later Galtonian “unnatural selection” pessimism.
- **Boasian and later anthropology:** reject race–mind equation and unilinear “savage” ranking; retain deep-time evolutionary frame for biology of *Homo* (see [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]], [[race-and-culture-debate]]).
- **Modern biology:** sexual selection revived; moral-sense account rewritten in gene–culture and multilevel-selection vocabularies; date-stamp all contemporary claims.

## Historiography

Publication of *Descent* (1871) coincides, in Stocking’s narrative, with the founding of the Anthropological Institute — Darwinian unity of humankind under natural developmental law helping resolve the decade’s Ethnological–Anthropological schism ([[victorian-ethnology-anthropology-schism-debate]]). Primary source for this page’s *Descent* claims: [[darwin-descent-of-man-1871]]. Historiographical frame: [[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987]]. Hub depth: [[hubs/thinkers/darwin-charles]].

Downstream in popular paleoanthropology, [[leakey-richard|Richard Leakey]] & Roger Lewin's *[[leakey-lewin-origins-1977|Origins]]* (1977) opens with Darwin's *Descent* reasoning that Africa was probably the human cradle (because the gorilla and chimpanzee, man's nearest living allies, are African) and states the East African fossil record "strongly supports Darwin's hunch" (attributed, Leakey & Lewin 1977). They also credit Darwin's *Descent* argument that canine-tooth reduction came early — though they hold Darwin reversed the true causal order, since small canines (as in [[ramapithecus|Ramapithecus]]) preceded rather than followed tool use.
