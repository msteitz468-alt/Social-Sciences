# Paulin Hountondji

Beninese philosopher (b. 1942), treated in this vault primarily through [[connell-raewyn|Raewyn Connell]]'s reading in *[[connell-southern-theory-2007|Southern Theory]]* (2007, ch. 5). Hountondji is central to Connell's account of colonial and postcolonial **extraversion** of African knowledge production.

## Overview

As Connell reports Hountondji: colonial science established a pattern — data-gathering and application in the colony, theorising in the metropole — carried into the postcolonial world. Most social science, Connell argues, still follows that pattern. Hountondji also criticises **ethnophilosophy** (collective "African philosophy" as inventory of folk wisdom) in favour of philosophy as individual, critical, written practice with **third-person responsibility**; and he works toward **endogenous knowledge** agendas (including CODESRIA-linked discussions Connell cites).

## Contributions (as mediated by Connell 2007)

- Diagnosis of scientific **extroversion** (theory reserved for the metropole)
- Critique of ethnophilosophy as a dead end for African philosophy/social science
- Endogenous knowledge as constructive alternative to pure import of Northern concepts *and* to sealed traditionalism

## Intellectual Genealogy

Placed by Connell in African debates after Tempels and alongside Akiwowo's indigenous-sociology project (which Hountondji's framework helps evaluate). Full primary ingest of Hountondji's corpus is not yet in this vault — claims above are **attributed via Connell**.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Connell uses Hountondji both to support Southern theory's anti-extraversion thesis and to discipline nativist closures of "indigenous knowledge." Independent reception of Hountondji beyond Connell awaits further sources.
