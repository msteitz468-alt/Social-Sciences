# Marcus W. Feldman

## Overview

Marcus W. Feldman is a theoretical biologist and population geneticist at
Stanford, long-time collaborator of [[cavalli-sforza-luigi-luca|Luigi Luca Cavalli-Sforza]]
on the quantitative theory of **[[cultural-transmission]]** and gene–culture
coevolution. Treated here primarily as he appears in Cavalli-Sforza's *Genes,
Peoples and Languages* (2000) and related program citations.

## Contributions

- Co-author with Cavalli-Sforza of *Cultural Transmission and Evolution: A
  Quantitative Approach* (Princeton, 1981) — formal models of vertical,
  horizontal, and oblique cultural transmission.
- Joint work on cultural transmission theory and observation (e.g. *Science*
  1982 with Chen and Dornbusch, as cited in Cavalli-Sforza 2000 bibliography).
- Later collaborations on genetic dating and human evolutionary trees (e.g. with
  Goldstein, Ruiz-Linares, Cavalli-Sforza — as of citations in Cavalli-Sforza 2000).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Cultural-evolution formalisms are often criticized by sociocultural anthropologists
as underspecifying meaning, power, and historical contingency — a standing
interdisciplinary tension, not a personal misconduct record.
