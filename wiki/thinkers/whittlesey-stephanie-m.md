# Stephanie M. Whittlesey

## Overview

American Southwest archaeologist; lead editor and major author of *[[whittlesey-vanishing-river-1997|Vanishing River]]* (1997), the overview/synthesis volume of the [[lower-verde-archaeological-project-study|Lower Verde Archaeological Project]] (Statistical Research, Inc.). Contributes landscape theory, research history, Yavapai/Apache ethnohistory and archaeology, ceramic production/distribution, and the critique of Hohokam core–periphery models.

## Contributions

- Landscape framework for LVAP (Ch. 2, 19).
- Synthesis of central Arizona research history (Ch. 4).
- Yavapai ethnohistory and archaeological identification (Ch. 5–6).
- Ceramic production and distribution theory (Ch. 11).
- Rethinking Pre-Classic Hohokam core–periphery (Ch. 15).

## Intellectual Genealogy

CRM/Southwest archaeology networks; collaboration with [[ciolek-torrello-richard|Richard Ciolek-Torrello]] and [[altschul-jeffrey-h|Jeffrey H. Altschul]] at SRI; engagement with Reid and others on migration/ethnicity (Ch. 16).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

LVAP positions are mid-1990s snapshots (as of Whittlesey et al. 1997); later chronological refinements and tribal collaboration protocols post-date this volume.

## Historiography

Known in this vault primarily through the 1997 LVAP synthesis.
