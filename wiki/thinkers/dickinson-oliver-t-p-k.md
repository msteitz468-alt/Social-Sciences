## Overview

Oliver T. P. K. Dickinson is a British Aegean prehistorian, best known in this wiki for *[[dickinson-aegean-bronze-age-1994|The Aegean Bronze Age]]* (Cambridge World Archaeology, 1994), a thematic textbook synthesis of settlement, economy, crafts, burial, exchange, and religion across the Minoan–Mycenaean world (~3300–1000 BC). Earlier co-compiler (with Hope Simpson) of a major BA site gazetteer (1979); author of work on Mycenaean origins (1977).

## Contributions

### From *The Aegean Bronze Age* (1994)

Thematic synthesis of Aegean BA (~3300–1000 BC): terminology/chronology; environment; Neolithic→BA populations; settlement and economy; arts and crafts; burial; overseas exchange; religion; conclusions. Accepts [[renfrew-colin|Renfrew]]’s 1972 civilisation definition for the peak BA while arguing against invasion, simple foreign “influence,” and mono-catastrophe explanations; prefers **cultural process** (status, exchange, ideology, resource base) with events as catalysts (attributed). Softens totalising palace-[[redistribution]] models; treats craft specialists as mostly part-time; surveys Minoan and Mycenaean material systems through post-palatial decline. See [[dickinson-aegean-bronze-age-1994]], [[minoan-civilization]], [[mycenaean-civilization]], [[late-bronze-age-collapse]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Engages [[renfrew-colin|Renfrew]] (1972 civilisation definition; process over invasion), Warren & Hankey chronology debates, Halstead on economy, Broodbank on island archaeology, and the intensive-survey generation. Skeptical of invasion/colonisation and simple “influence” models for culture change (Conclusions, attributed).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Textbook horizon is early 1990s; absolute chronology (Thera) and survey data have moved since — date-stamp claims to Dickinson 1994.

## Historiography

Standard Anglophone student introduction to Aegean BA material culture and themes through the mid-1990s.
