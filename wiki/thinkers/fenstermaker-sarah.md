# Sarah Fenstermaker

U.S. sociologist (b. 1949); early adopter and co-developer of the [[doing-gender]] framework;
co-editor with [[west-candace|Candace West]] of *[[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002|Doing Gender, Doing Difference]]* (2002).

## Overview

Fenstermaker's *The Gender Factory* analysis of household labor was the first major published
empirical use of the then-unpublished West–Zimmerman formulation and, per the 2002
Introduction, helped push the article into *Gender & Society*. She co-authored the power,
inequality, and "Doing Difference" extensions and several empirical chapters (Regents meeting;
second-parent adoptions with Susan Dalton; Butler comparison with Molly Moloney).

## Contributions

- Empirical bridge from [[doing-gender]] theory to household labor
- Co-originator of [[doing-difference]]
- Institutional-change cases (adoption practice) testing how accountability standards shift

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaboration with [[west-candace|West]] and [[zimmerman-don-h|Zimmerman]]; engagement with
feminist and anti-racist critique (symposium and reply). Per the 2002 Acknowledgments,
household-division analysis was first conceived in her Northwestern dissertation (1976).
Dorothy E. Smith situates the collective project within research-disciplined feminist
sociology.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Co-target of [[doing-difference-debate]]; replies in "(Re)Doing Difference" and Ch. 11 of the
2002 volume.

## Historiography

Self-narrated program history in the 2002 Introduction; *The Gender Factory* as hinge between
manuscript and publication.
