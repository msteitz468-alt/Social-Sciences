# Oscar Montelius (1843–1921)

Swedish prehistorian who refined [[typological-method|typological]] [[seriation]] for European later prehistory and advanced the *[[ex-oriente-lux|ex oriente lux]]* synthesis: civilizing innovations radiate from the Near East into Europe on a short chronology. [[trigger-bruce-g|Trigger]] (*[[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006|HAT]]* 2006, ch. 6) treats the **Montelian synthesis** as a pillar of culture-historical archaeology and notes subjective as well as scientific supports (including colonial justifications that cast western Europeans as true heirs of Middle Eastern civilizations).

## Overview

Montelius's detailed Bronze Age typologies and cross-dating via Mediterranean contacts structured European prehistoric chronology until radiocarbon and calibration dismantled the short chronology ([[renfrew-before-civilization-1973|Renfrew 1973]]; already in vault).

## Contributions

*(Attributed via Trigger 2006; primary Montelius not yet ingested.)*

- Systematic typology/seriation for European metal ages.
- *Ex oriente lux* diffusionist chronology linking Europe to Egypt/Aegean anchors.
- Bridge from Scandinavian relative dating to continent-scale culture-history.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Scandinavian three-age tradition; dialogue with Childe (who used and later revised Orientalist frames); Russian/Gorodtsov and other national typological schools as parallels (Trigger). Downstream: [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]'s *Dawn*; radiocarbon revolution as rebuttal of absolute dates not of all typological method.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Radiocarbon calibration collapsed short Orientalist chronology; independent European developments re-evaluated. Trigger adds reflexivity: Montelian scheme resonated with conservative anti-creativity moods and colonial Middle East politics, not only with closed finds.

## Historiography

Standard name for typology + *ex oriente lux* European prehistory. Source: [[trigger-history-archaeological-thought-2006]].
