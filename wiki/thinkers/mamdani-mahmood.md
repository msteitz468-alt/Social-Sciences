# Mahmood Mamdani

Mahmood Mamdani (b. 1946) is a Ugandan political theorist and Africanist. Seeded from "Africa and African Studies" in *[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|The Modern World-System in the Longue Durée]]* (2004).

## Overview

Mamdani argues the weakness of African studies is the relative absence of an African voice — the field often resembles a "settler representation of the native." He links colonial indirect rule to anthropological knowledge of ethnic subjects, and traces postindependence university politics (ban on anthropology; political economy as nationalist mother discipline; CODESRIA). Not an argument that only Africans may study Africa.

## Contributions

- Area-studies critique from the side of the "areas."
- Colonial knowledge/power: direct vs indirect rule; racial beneficiaries vs ethnic subjects.
- Institutional history of African universities and continental research (CODESRIA).
