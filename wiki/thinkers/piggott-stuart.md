# Stuart Piggott (1910–1996)

British prehistorian (Edinburgh); provided the concluding paper to the 1968 London domestication seminar published in *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*.

## Overview

Piggott’s conclusion (kept in seminar style by the editors) treats agricultural origins and transmission through the sociological contrast of **innovating** vs **conserving** societies (drawing on Margaret Hodgen’s historical work on pre-industrial England), stresses that plant and animal remains in settlements and graves are **socially selected** “fossilized behaviour” (Childe’s phrase) rather than random nutritional samples, and reaffirms European indebtedness to Near Eastern crops and herds against nationalist indigenous-origin excesses (*ex oriente lux* as “not an illusion” for Europe’s first farming — as of Piggott 1969).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Piggott 1969 conclusion in Ucko & Dimbleby.)*

- **Innovators / conservers:** useful for thinking about adoption and transmission of domestication packages; links to Margaret Hodgen on technological receptivity, “new blood,” and broken custom in pre-industrial England — extended to prehistoric agricultural adoption.
- **Food refuse as social fact:** settlements and graves are Childe’s “fossilized behaviour,” not random nutritional samples; “Custom, not Calory is King” — tabus, grave selection, meal conventions bias plant and animal assemblages (pork prohibitions, offal attitudes, Celtic fish tabus as analogies).
- **Pottery and cooking:** advanced cooking for agricultural diets linked to ceramic development; container not above contents; composite bow requires boiling vessels (Rausing) → invention among food-producing peoples.
- **Diffusion stance for Europe:** with minor exceptions, Europe’s initiation of agriculture still owed to peoples, crops, and herds from across the Hellespont and Aegean; *ex oriente lux* not mirage; warns against nationalist indigenous-origin excesses (Tartaria tablets as psychology as much as prehistory) — **1969 POSITION**.

## Intellectual Genealogy

British culture-historical prehistory; dialogue with [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]; seminar interlocutor with [[ucko-peter-j|Ucko]], [[dimbleby-g-w|Dimbleby]], and the 1968 participants. Fuller genealogy awaits monographs.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

European near-eastern dependence is a **1969 position** later complicated by multi-regional models, radiocarbon calibration battles, and indigenous domesticate packages outside SW Asia. Record as attributed stance, not wiki-voice global history.
