# Philippe Descola

French anthropologist (b. 1949); Laboratoire d'anthropologie sociale / Collège de France lineage. Primary-ingested: *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* (2005/2013). Earlier Achuar monographs not yet primary-ingested.

## Overview

Descola's comparative program replaces the universal [[nature-culture-distinction|nature/culture opposition]] with four [[modes-of-identification|modes of identification]] — [[animism]], [[totemism]], [[analogism]], [[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]] — generated from combinations of continuity/discontinuity on [[interiority-and-physicality|interiority and physicality]]. Fieldwork among the [[achuar]] of Ecuadorian Amazonia supplied the "wake-up call" against dualist certainties; the 2005 book scales that insight into a structural comparative anthropology of cosmologies, [[collectives-including-nonhumans|collectives]], and [[modes-of-relation|relations]]. He presents the work as remaining faithful to Lévi-Strauss's *method* while overturning the nature/culture hinge and revising totemism's ontological side.

## Contributions

- **[[four-ontologies]]** — fourfold combinatorial schema of identification; Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies]].
- **Critique of naturalism's universality** — Western dualism as one ontology among others (Ch.3 genealogy of the Great Divide).
- **Animism restored** — shared interiority + discontinuous physicality (form/ethogram); culture extended to nonhumans; [[perspectivism]] as optional graft.
- **Totemism as ontology** — continuity of both axes within hybrid classes (Australia exemplary), against pure classificatory dissolution.
- **[[analogism]]** — double discontinuity recomposed by correspondence, hierarchy, sacrifice.
- **[[collectives-including-nonhumans]]** — aggregation beyond human-only "society."
- **[[modes-of-relation]]** — give/take/exchange/produce/protect/transmit with compatibility matrix.
- **Relative universalism** — comparative method that refuses naturalist particular universalism without collapsing into pure particularism.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Heuristic scope and secondary-source reliance outside Amazonia (Descola's own caveat). Relation to Viveiros de Castro (perspectivism vs standard animism; "not a type but a concept" — see [[ontological-turn-debate]]). Typological reification risk. Epilogue refuses non-Western ontologies as ready-made ecological politics for the global North — a self-limitation that some political-ecology readers contest.
