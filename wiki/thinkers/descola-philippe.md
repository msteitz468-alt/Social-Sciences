# Philippe Descola

French anthropologist (b. 1949); Laboratoire d'anthropologie sociale / Collège de France lineage. Primary-ingested: *[[descola-in-the-society-of-nature-1994|In the Society of Nature]]* (1994; Fr. 1986) and *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* (2005/2013).

## Overview

Descola’s program begins in long-term Achuar ethnography and scales to a comparative anthropology of cosmologies. The Achuar monograph combines quantified ecological/economic measurement with symbolic analysis to show that “nature” is socialized through the same relational templates as the house — without accepting a universal [[nature-culture-distinction|nature/culture dualism]]. *Beyond Nature and Culture* generalizes that insight into four [[modes-of-identification|modes of identification]] — [[animism]], [[totemism]], [[analogism]], [[naturalism-ontology|naturalism]] — generated from continuity/discontinuity of [[interiority-and-physicality|interiority and physicality]]. He presents the comparative work as remaining faithful to Lévi-Strauss’s *method* while overturning the nature/culture hinge.

## Contributions

- **[[descola-society-of-nature-study|Achuar native ecology]]** — equal heuristic weight to quantitative production/time data and qualitative cosmology; house–garden–forest–river as practical-symbolic worlds; consanguinity (gardens/Nunkui) vs affinity (game mothers/[[anent]]); under-exploitation with high diet adequacy (attributed findings of the 1986/1994 study).
- **[[four-ontologies]]** — fourfold combinatorial schema of identification; Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/four-ontologies]].
- **Critique of naturalism’s universality** — Western dualism as one ontology among others.
- **Animism restored** — shared interiority + discontinuous physicality; culture extended to nonhumans; [[perspectivism]] as optional graft.
- **[[collectives-including-nonhumans]]** and **[[modes-of-relation]]** — aggregation and give/take/exchange/produce/protect/transmit matrices.
- **Relative universalism** — comparative method refusing naturalist particular universalism without pure particularism.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Heuristic scope and secondary-source reliance outside Amazonia in BNC (Descola’s own caveat). Typological reification risk. Relation to Viveiros de Castro (perspectivism vs standard animism — see [[ontological-turn-debate]]). Achuar monograph’s non-mission sample and 1970s ethnographic present require date-stamping when later political history is at issue. Epilogue of BNC refuses non-Western ontologies as ready-made ecological politics for the global North.
