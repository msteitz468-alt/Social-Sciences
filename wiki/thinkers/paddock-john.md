# John Paddock

## Overview
American-born archaeologist long resident in Oaxaca (Centro de Estudios Oaxaqueños), excavator of [[yagul|Yagul]] and [[lambityeco|Lambityeco]] and definer of the Ñuiñe ("hot country") style of the Mixteca Baja. In [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) he wrote the topics on Yagul, Lambityeco, the Oaxaca barrio at [[teotihuacan|Teotihuacán]], the Ñuiñe centers, and the Postclassic "Mixtec impact" on the Valley of Oaxaca.

## Contributions
- Excavation of [[lambityeco]] (Monte Albán IV) and [[yagul]]; identification and naming of the Ñuiñe style/tradition of the Mixteca Baja.
- Paddock argued for a substantial Postclassic Mixtec intrusion into the Valley of Oaxaca ("Mixtec impact"), a position the volume's editors explicitly contested — see the debate recorded on [[mixtec-culture]] and [[zapotec-civilization]].

## Intellectual Genealogy
Student and continuator of the Oaxaca tradition of [[caso-alfonso|Alfonso Caso]] and [[bernal-ignacio|Ignacio Bernal]]; longtime interlocutor (and frequent dissenter) within the [[flannery-kent-v|Flannery]]–[[marcus-joyce|Marcus]] seminar circle.

## Critiques and Reappraisals
His maximalist reading of Mixtec presence in the Valley of Oaxaca was recorded as a minority position in [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] itself.

## Historiography
Documented here chiefly through his contributions to [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983).
