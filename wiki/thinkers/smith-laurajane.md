# Laurajane Smith

## Overview

Archaeologist and critical heritage scholar. At the publication of *[[smith-uses-of-heritage-2006|Uses of Heritage]]* (Routledge 2006) she was Senior Lecturer in Cultural Heritage Studies and Archaeology at the University of York; she had previously taught Indigenous Studies at the University of New South Wales, Sydney, and worked as a cultural heritage consultant. The 2006 book is the charter statement of her program: the **[[authorized-heritage-discourse|Authorized Heritage Discourse (AHD)]]**, heritage as **cultural process** (memory, performance, identity, intangibility, dissonance, place), and multi-sited qualitative documentation of how people *use* heritage — both conservatively and to contest received identities.

## Contributions

*(Densified from Smith 2006 ingest — attributed.)*

- **[[authorized-heritage-discourse]]:** names and analyses the dominant Western professional discourse that equates heritage with old, monumental, aesthetic material fabric; naturalizes expert authority; and promotes consensual national narratives.
- **Heritage as process, not thing:** sites and objects are cultural tools facilitating acts of remembering and identity work; "all heritage is ultimately intangible" *(Smith 2006, conclusion — attributed)*.
- **Case program:** English country house as banal authorized performance; Riversleigh World Heritage / [[waanyi|Waanyi]] dissonance over fossils vs country; industrial museums and labour remembering; [[castleford|Castleford]] community heritage after deindustrialization; Indigenous politics of **control** over heritage process.
- Institutional reading of Venice Charter, World Heritage Convention, Burra Charter, and UNESCO intangible-heritage instruments as authorizing apparatuses of the AHD (with openings for challenge).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Engages critically with the English "heritage industry" critique ([[unknown|Hewison]], [[unknown|Wright]], Lowenthal's anti-heritage animus) while arguing that critique shares conceptual space with the AHD.
- Draws on discourse analysis (Fairclough), memory studies (Wertsch, Samuel's theatres of memory), performance/place theory, Tunbridge & Ashworth on dissonance, and Indigenous heritage politics (including her own earlier work, 2004).
- Field lineage: CRM/cultural heritage consultancy + Indigenous studies (UNSW) → York cultural heritage studies.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(To densify as secondary literature is ingested.)* Smith's strong characterization of the AHD is self-noted as overlooking nuance and internal expert dissent; the program nonetheless became a central foil and vocabulary for critical heritage studies after 2006.
