# Joseph Errington

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist (Yale); in [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language*]] ch. 5 analyzes the **authority of Indonesian** under the New Order — an “unnative” national language lacking a primordial ethnic community of native speakers, yet central to modern Indonesian culture and state development.

## Contributions

- Dual grounds of Indonesian authority: **standardist** (institutionally grounded state standard vs ethnic languages; Gellner-style development) and **exemplarist** (covert qualities resonant with colonial Javanese courtly elites).
- Historical arc: Malay lingua franca → van Ophuysen standard Malay / Balai Pustaka → 1928 Sumpah Pemuda (*satu bangsa / satu bahasa / satu nusa*) → New Order language development.
- Weberian bureaucratic vs charismatic authority as frame for “asymmetrical complementarity” of state standardism and traditional elite exemplarism; dual lexical development (Old Javanese/Sanskrit + English esoterica).
