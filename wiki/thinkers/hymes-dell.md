# Dell Hymes

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/thinkers/hymes-dell|Thinkers Hub]]

## Overview

Dell Hathaway Hymes (1927–2009) was an American linguistic anthropologist, folklorist, and sociolinguist who founded the research program of the **[[ethnography-of-speaking|ethnography of speaking]]** / **[[ethnography-of-communication|ethnography of communication]]** and coined **[[communicative-competence]]** as a counter-concept to Chomskyan linguistic competence. *[[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974|Foundations in Sociolinguistics: An Ethnographic Approach]]* (1974) is his major programmatic collection: revised invited papers arguing that linguistics must become a social science of communicative conduct.

Hymes's doctorate was in linguistics with substantial anthropology and folklore; he describes a "mixed and troublesome intellectual and professional heritage" — anthropological philology, undergraduate socialist aspirations, and historiography of anthropology/folklore/Amerindian linguistics — set against a generation of linguists who, in his view, pursued formal goals while abandoning human realities (*Foundations* Introduction).

## Contributions

### Program

- **[[ethnography-of-speaking]]** (1962) and **[[ethnography-of-communication]]** (1964): ethnography as basis; communication as scope; sociolinguistic description as mode of linguistic description.
- **Three themes of *Foundations*:** (1) a mode of organization of language within communicative conduct requiring a new mode of description; (2) multidisciplinary study of language (linguistics necessary, not sufficient); (3) reconsideration of the foundations of linguistics itself — reconstruction as sociolinguistics.

### Concepts and apparatus

- **[[communicative-competence]]** — four aspects: systemic potential, appropriateness, occurrence, feasibility; creativity requires appropriateness rules.
- **[[speech-community]]** — shared rules for conduct and interpretation of speech, not "a language."
- **[[speech-event]]** (with situation and act) — central descriptive starting point.
- **[[speaking-model|SPEAKING]]** — mnemonic for event components (heuristic, not the theory itself).
- **Ways of speaking** / **speech economy** — primitive terms for repertoire organization.
- **Socially constituted linguistics** — radical orientation among three he distinguishes (social-as-well-as-linguistic; socially realistic; socially constituted).

### Applied and allied domains

- Bilingual education as sociolinguistic subject (rules of speaking, not only language codes).
- Folklore and poetics as essential contributors (performance, genre, Burkean dramatism).
- Education, literacy, and unequal competence opportunities as scientific-and-social stakes.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Generative linguists treated sociolinguistic critique as external to "real" linguistics; Hymes insisted it arises from linguistics' own practice (Part Three of *Foundations*).
- Risk of SPEAKING checklist pedagogy diluting the program.
- The hoped-for self-liquidation of "sociolinguistics" into ordinary linguistics did not fully occur — institutional dual track persists.
- Later applied "communicative competence" often thinned ethnographic force (see concept page).

Ethics/misconduct: none established from this source; Amerindian philology and urban ethnography contexts share the discipline's general colonial/power entanglements — treat under discipline reflexivity, not personal scandal.

### The 1972 statement: "Models of the Interaction of Language and Social Life"
