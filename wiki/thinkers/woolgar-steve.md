# Steve Woolgar

British sociologist of science; co-author with [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]] of *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-1979|Laboratory Life]]* (1979/1986). In that collaboration Latour conducted the Salk fieldwork; Woolgar co-developed the analysis and writing (later Brunel University affiliation noted in Acknowledgements). Subsequent Woolgar work on reflexivity, representation, and virtual society is not yet primary-ingested.

## Overview

Woolgar is one of the two named authors of the charter laboratory ethnography. The book's reflexive apparatus — the fictional "observer," attention to how texts constitute objectivities, and the 1986 Postscript's defense of fallibility — is continuous with Woolgar's later programmatic writing on reflexivity in STS (cited in the Postscript itself, e.g. Woolgar 1982, 1983).

## Contributions

- Co-architect of *[[latour-woolgar-laboratory-life-study|Laboratory Life]]* arguments: inscription, statement types, credit cycles, fact/artefact splitting.
- **Reflexivity program:** insists that laboratory studies should not claim privileged access to "what science really is" while ironizing scientists' accounts (Postscript; cf. Woolgar 1982 on instrumental vs. reflexive ethnography).

## Intellectual Genealogy
