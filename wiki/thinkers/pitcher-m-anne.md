# M. Anne Pitcher

## Overview

M. Anne Pitcher is a political scientist of African political economy. At the time of *[[pitcher-transforming-mozambique-2002|Transforming Mozambique]]* (2002) she was Associate Professor of Political Science and African Studies coordinator at Colgate University; she is best known for work on Mozambique, Lusophone colonial economies, and party politics and economic reform across African democracies. Her scholarship sits in the comparative-historical institutionalist tradition and the "state-in-society" current associated with Joel Migdal and [[scott-james-c|James Scott]].

## Contributions

Pitcher coined **[[transformative-preservation]]** to name the process by which a surviving ruling party (Frelimo) uses market restructuring to preserve and redirect — rather than surrender — state and elite power during a post-socialist transition. Her earlier *Politics in the Portuguese Empire* (1993) analyzed the colonial cotton economy; her later *Party Politics and Economic Reform in Africa's Democracies* (2012) extended the party-and-market theme comparatively.
