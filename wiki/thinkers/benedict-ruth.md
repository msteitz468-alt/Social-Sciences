# Ruth Benedict

## Overview

Ruth Fulton Benedict (1887–1948) was a leading figure of the American [[historical-particularism|Boasian]] school and, with [[mead-margaret|Margaret Mead]], a founder of the [[culture-and-personality]] movement. Her *[[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Patterns of Culture]]* (1934) is the defining statement of **[[configurationism]]** — cultures as integrated wholes selecting from “the great arc” of human possibility. Her *[[benedict-chrysanthemum-and-sword-1946|The Chrysanthemum and the Sword]]* (1946) scales that method to a modern nation under wartime [[culture-at-a-distance]] conditions and becomes the charter text of **[[national-character]]** anthropology applied to Japan.

## Contributions

- **[[configurationism]] / [[cultural-configuration]]:** *Patterns of Culture* — Apollonian [[zuni|Zuni]], “paranoid” [[dobu|Dobu]], Dionysian [[kwakiutl|Kwakiutl]]; personality writ large; [[cultural-relativity-of-normality]].
- **[[culture-and-personality]]:** Program co-builder with Mead; child-rearing and ethos as keys to adult pattern.
- **Wartime applied anthropology:** June 1944 OWI assignment; *Chrysanthemum* synthesizes hierarchy ([[taking-ones-proper-station]]), obligation (*[[on-obligation|on]]*, *[[gimu]]*, *[[giri]]*), [[shame-culture]], and child-life curve into a Japan national portrait aimed at policy (propaganda, surrender, Occupation).
- **Ethics of difference:** “World made safe for differences” — U.S. American, France French, Japan Japanese — without forced uniformity (Ch.1 of *Chrysanthemum*).
- **Public anthropology:** Both major books reached large non-specialist audiences.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained under** [[boas-franz|Franz Boas]] at Columbia.
- **Colleague network:** [[sapir-edward|Edward Sapir]]; [[mead-margaret|Margaret Mead]] (student, collaborator, lifelong interlocutor); wartime circle including Mead, Gregory Bateson, Clyde Kluckhohn, Conrad Arensberg, Alexander Leighton.
- **Trained / influenced:** Mead as the most famous student; configurationism and culture-and-personality shaped a generation of psychological anthropology.
- **Sources she used for Japan:** Embree *Suye Mura*; Japanese and Western literature; films; Japan-reared informants (incl. Robert Hashima); E. H. Norman among readers; comparative Pacific/Asia.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- *Patterns:* over-integration of cultures; psychiatric labels on whole peoples; Li An-che restudy of Zuni (expand when ingested).
- *Chrysanthemum:* no Japan fieldwork; national essentialism; shame/guilt binary; obligation chart over-systematized; wartime state commission and War Relocation–linked informants ([[national-character-debate]]).
- Enduring respect for literary synthesis, teaching power, and the historical importance of applied anthropology’s wartime moment — often separated from endorsement of national-character method.
