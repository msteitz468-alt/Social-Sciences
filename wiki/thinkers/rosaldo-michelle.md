# Michelle Zimbalist Rosaldo

## Overview

Michelle Z. Rosaldo (1944–1981) was an American anthropologist, co-founder of [[feminist-anthropology|feminist anthropology]] as co-editor (with [[lamphere-louise|Louise Lamphere]]) of *[[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974|Woman, Culture, and Society]]* (1974) and author of its theoretical overview. Her **[[domestic-public-dichotomy|domestic/public opposition]]** is one of the two organizing arguments of that volume (alongside [[ortner-female-to-male-1972|Ortner's]] nature/culture thesis). Her career was cut short by a fatal fall during Ilongot fieldwork in 1981. The 1974 overview is now primary-ingested; *Knowledge and Passion* is not.

## Contributions

- **The domestic/public dichotomy** (1974 overview): Rosaldo argued that women's association with the **domestic** sphere (organized around mothers and children) and men's with the **public** sphere generates and explains sexual asymmetry — men as locus of cultural value and authority, women often with informal **power** without legitimated authority. Status lowest when spheres are firmly split and women isolated under one man's household authority; most egalitarian when spheres are weakly differentiated and men share domestic life. See [[domestic-public-dichotomy]].
- **Women as strategists within asymmetry:** Using Ilongot, Merina, Yoruba, Eastern European Jewish, and other illustrations, she showed that acknowledging universal male authority does not entail denying women's systematic influence.
- **Self-critique** ("The Use and Abuse of Anthropology," 1980): Rosaldo later criticized the search for universal *origins* of gender asymmetry — including her own domestic/public frame — arguing for historically and culturally situated analysis instead, a pivotal move in the [[nature-culture-gender-debate]].
- **Ilongot ethnography** (*Knowledge and Passion*, 1980): emotion, "self," and headhunting — not yet primary-ingested. Renato Rosaldo's *[[rosaldo-ilongot-headhunting-1980|Ilongot Headhunting]]* (1980) cites it as the extended analysis of **[[liget]]** (anger/passion); the two books are companion products of their joint 1967–69/1974 fieldwork, hers on the cultural idiom of emotion, his on society and history.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The domestic/public dichotomy was criticized (by [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]], MacCormack, and others, and eventually by Rosaldo herself) for projecting a Western public/private split onto societies that do not divide social life that way — the central charge of the [[nature-culture-gender-debate]].

## Historiography

Rosaldo is remembered both for launching feminist anthropology's universalist phase and for helping dismantle it before her death. Primary source now on the wiki for the 1974 overview: [[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974]]. Renato Rosaldo drew on their joint [[ilongot|Ilongot]] fieldwork — and on his grief at her death — to build the argument of *[[rosaldo-culture-truth-1993|Culture and Truth]]* (1989), including his critique of [[imperialist-nostalgia|imperialist nostalgia]] in which he counts their own earlier ethnographic writing as complicit.
