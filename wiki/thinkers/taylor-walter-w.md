# Walter W. Taylor (1913–1997)

American archaeologist; author of *[[taylor-study-of-archeology-1948|A Study of Archeology]]* (AAA Memoir 69, 1948), the charter critique of Americanist culture-historical practice and the program statement for the **[[conjunctive-approach|conjunctive approach]]**.

## Overview

Taylor's 1948 book argued that mid-century Americanist archaeology claimed anthropological identity while practicing thin historical chronicle — presence/absence trait lists and time–space placement without multi-dimensional **[[cultural-context-archaeology|cultural contexts]]**. He defined archaeology as a **method** for producing cultural information ("neither history nor anthropology" as autonomous discipline), distinguished historiography (context construction) from cultural anthropology (comparative study of culture), and outlined procedures for site-level conjunctive analysis. Watson's 1983 foreword to the CAI reprint and Taylor's own 1968 foreword frame the work as a major proto-processual statement that was under-followed in monographic practice for decades (attributed reception).

## Contributions

*(From *A Study of Archeology* 1948 — attributed.)*

- **[[conjunctive-approach]]:** primary goal = elucidation of cultural conjunctives (associations, affinities) to construct cultural context; comparative extra-local work is means not end until context is built.
- **Archaeology as method:** specialized techniques for gathering cultural information; becomes historiography at the level of integrated cultural contexts; becomes anthropology when contexts support comparative study of culture.
- **Culture concept for archaeology** and **empirical vs cultural** typology/classification (type/class; form; Linton use/function/meaning; Kluckhohn configuration).
- **"More interpretation, not less":** labeled inference and hypothesis-testing required if the aim is history or culture.
- **Report hierarchy:** problem → description/interpretation → local chronology → **cultural context (most concern)** → extra-local relations → optional special problems.
- **Critique of Americanist leaders' publications** (Kidder, Haury, Roberts, Webb, Ritchie, Griffin, et al.): aims stated as anthropology/history vs practice as comparative chronicle and trait-list "cultures."

## Intellectual Genealogy

Dialogue with Americanist culture-history and Midwestern taxonomy; anthropological culture theory (Boas, Linton, Kluckhohn, Rouse, Wissler). Formal teachers/students not documented in the ingested primary (`[[unknown]]`). Later reception ties him to New Archaeology without equating conjunctive approach and full systems processualism (Watson 1983 — attributed).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Criticized for polemical tone and feasibility of data demands; Taylor (1968) refused to revise the "polemic" chapter. Practice lagged theory — few monographs fully enacted the 1948 outline (historiographic claim). Processual archaeology later absorbed context/quantification demands while shifting the theoretical frame.
