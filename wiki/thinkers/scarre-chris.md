# Chris Scarre

## Overview

Christopher Scarre is a British archaeologist (Professor of Archaeology, Durham
University; earlier at the McDonald Institute, Cambridge) best known as a synthesizer
and editor of world prehistory rather than as a single-theory program-builder. He edited
*[[scarre-human-past-2013|The Human Past: World Prehistory and the Development of Human
Societies]]* (Thames & Hudson; 1st ed. 2005, 3rd ed. 2013), the standard multi-author
university survey in the post-[[clark-grahame|Clark]] [[world-prehistory]] textbook genre,
and co-authored *Ancient Civilizations* with Brian Fagan. His own field research centers
on the Neolithic of Atlantic Europe — megalithic monuments, their construction, and the
societies that raised them.

## Contributions

- **Editorial synthesis of world prehistory.** As editor of *The Human Past* he
  assembled region-specialist chapters into a single global chronological-ecological
  narrative, foregrounding independent multi-regional trajectories of food production and
  complex-society formation over single-origin diffusion (see [[world-prehistory]]).
- **European megalith studies.** Work on the [[megalith|megalithic]] monuments of western
  France and the British Isles — their chronology, architecture, and the labour and social
  organization implied by their construction.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

As with any single-volume world survey, *The Human Past* is periodically criticized for
the unevenness inherent in multi-author coverage and for textbook lag behind the
current research front — critiques Scarre's own editorial apparatus (Key
Controversy boxes, chapter bibliographies) partly anticipates by flagging live disputes
rather than smoothing them over.
