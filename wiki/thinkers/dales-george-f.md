# George F. Dales, Jr.

## Overview

George Franklin Dales, Jr. (1927–1992) was an American archaeologist of the Indus civilization. He directed the 1964–65 excavations at [[mohenjo-daro]] (first major radiocarbon sampling there) and mentored [[kenoyer-jonathan-mark]]; the Harappa Archaeological Research Project grew from his and colleagues’ work. [[kenoyer-ancient-cities-indus-1998|Kenoyer 1998]] is dedicated in part to his memory and cites Dales throughout on chronology, brickwork, ceramics, trade debate, and Harappa cemetery finds (often as Dales & Kenoyer).

## Contributions

*(Attributed via Kenoyer 1998.)*

- **First 14C series at Mohenjo-daro.** Dales collected the first radiocarbon samples from the **latest** Mohenjo-daro levels (1964–65) but could not reach the earliest levels because of the high water table; Kenoyer notes that no further major excavations there followed, so the city’s origins remain obscure.
- **Baked-brick size change.** Kenoyer reports that Dales observed a **gradual decrease in absolute size of baked bricks** over time at Mohenjo-daro, paralleling HARP’s three-phase gateway sequence at Harappa (E/ET: earliest 7 × 14 × 28 cm → final 5 × 12 × 24 cm). Dales could not confirm the Mohenjo-daro pattern because later structures reused earlier bricks.
- **Harappa program and cemetery.** U.C. Berkeley excavations 1986–1990 and HARP continuity; Dales & Kenoyer publications underwrite Harappa cemetery documentation — mud-brick-lined graves, coffin stains, rosewood/cedar wood reported by Wheeler and later teams, sex-patterned grave goods (left-arm shell bangles and throat amulets on adult females; rare gold on male burial 196a), and infant/mother figurine associations.
- **Ceramics.** Kenoyer cites Dales’s specialized ceramic studies and Dales & Kenoyer 1989 for Harappa cemetery pottery design change across the Harappan Phase (plant/geometric early → simpler middle → late forms) and for kiln/craft contexts; absence of sealings/graffiti on pottery used to argue pottery was not directly elite-controlled at large cities.
- **Trade debate participant.** Kenoyer engages Dales among scholars (with Lamberg-Karlovsky, Kohl, Possehl) on whether long-distance Indus–Gulf–Mesopotamia trade ran through intermediary middlemen or more direct mercantile contact.
- **Catalogue objects.** Kenoyer’s 1998 catalogue attributes boat seal/tablet, inscribed bangle/rod, horned masks, and cemetery ornaments to Dales 1965–68 and Dales & Kenoyer publications.
- **Massacre critique lineage.** Dales’s mid-century critique of Wheeler’s “massacre” reading at Mohenjo-daro is part of the modern consensus trajectory (with later bioarchaeology; see [[mohenjo-daro-massacre-debate]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained:** [[kenoyer-jonathan-mark]] (Berkeley Ph.D. 1983; HARP field leadership).
- **Colleague circle:** [[fairservis-walter|Walter A. Fairservis, Jr.]]; postwar American Indus program; HARP co-direction later with [[meadow-richard|Richard H. Meadow]].
- **Institutional:** University of California, Berkeley excavations at Harappa preceding formal HARP 1993–97 seasons.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Brick-size seriation at Mohenjo-daro remained unconfirmed in Dales’s own work (reuse, water table). Cemetery demographic and gender inferences from Dales & Kenoyer contexts are sample-limited and reopened by later bioarchaeology. Full evaluation of Dales’s monographs awaits primary ingest.

## Historiography

Memorialized in Kenoyer 1998 dedication and preface; primary excavation reports (Mohenjo-daro 1964–65; Harappa seasons) and co-authored craft/cemetery papers remain the base texts. Kenoyer’s book is the main secondary pathway in this wiki session.
