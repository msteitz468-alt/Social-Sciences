# Gordon R. Willey

American archaeologist (1913–2002); co-author with [[phillips-philip|Philip Phillips]] of *[[willey-phillips-method-theory-1958|Method and Theory in American Archaeology]]* (1958), the principal mid-century formalization of **unit concepts** and New World **historical-developmental stages** in Americanist archaeology. Often ranked among the discipline-makers of New World prehistory; settlement-pattern work (Virú Valley) and later handbooks not yet primary-ingested here.

## Overview

*Method and Theory* (University of Chicago Press, 1958) rewrites their 1953 and 1955 *American Anthropologist* papers plus an introduction: archaeology as anthropology; descriptive [[culture-historical-integration]] vs explanatory [[processual-interpretation]]; component–phase–horizon–tradition unit system; five-stage hemispheric synthesis (Lithic → Postclassic). Dedicated with Phillips to James A. Ford. Special thanks to Albert C. Spaulding and Irving Rouse.

## Contributions

*(From primary ingest of *Method and Theory* 1958.)*

- **Archaeology as anthropology:** “American archaeology is anthropology or it is nothing” (Maitland paraphrase); methods rest on anthropology as more science than history and on society **and** culture as subject matter.
- **Operational levels:** observation → [[culture-historical-integration]] (descriptive) → [[processual-interpretation]] (explanatory regularities); convergence with cultural anthropology at the top.
- **Unit hierarchy:** [[archaeological-component]] → [[archaeological-phase]] (primary practicable unit) → maximum culture/civilization; integrative [[archaeological-horizon]] (space) and [[archaeological-tradition]] (time — Willey’s long interest from Peruvian ceramic traditions).
- **Historical-developmental stages:** [[lithic-stage]], [[archaic-stage]], [[formative-stage]], [[classic-stage]], [[postclassic-stage]] — hybrid historical + developmental synthesis of New World prehistory; Classic/Postclassic “civilization” reserved for nuclear America (Middle America + central Andes).
- **Methodological cautions:** phase ≠ demonstrated society; extracultural dating preferred for area chronologies; period ≠ stage; Preformative dropped; Formative not a Neolithic revolution.

- **Reacted against / corrected:** pure cultural superorganic; chronicle without context (Taylor’s charge, partially accepted); over-broad “tradition”; maize/manioc-locked Formative; Paleo/Neo-Indian twofold split.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

New Archaeology radicalized “process” beyond W&P’s culture-historical base while inheriting regularity-seeking language. Post-processual and social archaeologies criticized stage schemes as civilizational ranking. Settlement archaeology (including Willey’s own Virú work) is often treated as a bridge **out** of pure trait-list culture-history — that corpus is not yet in this vault. Absolute chronologies and peopling models in Part II are 1958-state-of-knowledge.
