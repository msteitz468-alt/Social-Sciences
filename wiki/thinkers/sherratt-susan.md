# Susan Sherratt

## Overview

Susan Sherratt is a British archaeologist (formerly Ashmolean Museum, Oxford; later University of Sheffield) working on Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean Bronze Age exchange and socio-economic change. **Not** the same person as [[sherratt-andrew|Andrew Sherratt]] (Secondary Products Revolution).

## Contributions (as used in Cline 2014)

- **Modern analogies:** “Genuinely useful analogies” between ~1200 BCE and contemporary global economic interdependence and fragmentation (cited in Cline’s preface).
- **Sea Peoples as structural phenomenon (1998 position via Cline):** final step replacing centralized Bronze Age politico-economic systems with decentralized Iron Age city-states and entrepreneurs; product of long-term expansion of international trade that subverted palace command economies; origin of the peoples secondary to the socio-economic change.
- Engages debates where Carol Bell and others reject wholesale palace→entrepreneur replacement as *the* cause of collapse ([[late-bronze-age-collapse-causes-debate]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Aegean/Eastern Mediterranean LBA specialist literature; interlocutor with Bass, Artzy, Bell, Muhly, and systems-oriented collapse discussions in Cline’s synthesis.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Bell (via Cline): simplistic to treat LBA→IA solely as palace exchange replaced by entrepreneurial trade; Ugarit lacks evidence that decentralized entrepreneurs caused destruction. Cline records both sides without adjudicating.
