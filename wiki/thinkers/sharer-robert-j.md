# Robert J. Sharer

## Overview

American Mesoamericanist archaeologist; Shoemaker Professor of Anthropology at the University of Pennsylvania and Curator of the American Section, University of Pennsylvania Museum (as credited in [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]]). Principal author of successive Stanford editions of *The Ancient Maya*, the standard comprehensive Anglophone reference after the Morley–Brainerd lineage.

## Contributions

- Thorough rewriting and expansion of *The Ancient Maya* (4th 1983, 5th 1994, 6th 2006 with [[traxler-loa-p|Loa P. Traxler]]) integrating post-decipherment history with settlement archaeology and ethnohistory (as of 2006).
- Field research and monographs on [[quirigua]] and related southeastern lowland topics (titles as listed on the 6th-edition jacket/credits).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Edition lineage:** inherits and supersedes Morley (1946/47) and Morley–Brainerd (1956) *Ancient Maya* text tradition; co-author Traxler on the 6th edition.
- **Network:** works in dialogue with the epigraphic and settlement-archaeology generations cited throughout the 2006 synthesis (Proskouriakoff, Knorosov, Schele, Martin, Grube, and others as attributed on topic pages).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Comprehensive-reference format compresses specialist debate; claims should be treated as Sharer & Traxler’s mid-2000s synthesis, not as primary data. Post-2006 LIDAR, climate proxy series, and epigraphy revise some settlement and collapse detail — date-stamp when updating from later sources.

## Historiography

The *Ancient Maya* series itself is a primary document of shifting Maya scholarship (Morley empire model → culture-history → process and decipherment). Edition history is summarized on [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006]].
