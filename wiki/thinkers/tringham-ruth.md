# Ruth E. Tringham

Archaeologist of prehistoric southeast Europe; *Engendering Archaeology* chapter **"Households with Faces"** argues that gender is investigable in architectural remains and that household archaeology must populate houses with gendered actors — not treat the household as a faceless economic unit (“faceless blobs”).

## Overview

Tringham opens with the South Carolina “Women and Production in Prehistory” conference “Aha” moment: she arrived convinced gender was invisible in deep prehistoric architecture, then reworked research strategy to ask what women were doing in the houses she studied. She distinguishes remedial feminism (add women) from transformative approaches; engages Soviet and Gimbutas precursors critically; develops fire archaeology and house-burning hypotheses at Opovo; stages dialogue with Gimbutas, [[hodder-ian|Hodder]], Sherratt, and her own earlier work. Conkey’s Magdalenian “beyond-the-households” aggregation concept is a different scale object — do not collapse with Tringham’s architectural household.

## Contributions

- Gender challenge to household/architectural archaeology (“faces”).
- Opovo SE European prehistoric household organization program (as framed 1991).
- Critique of faceless economic household models.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Volume network with Gero & Conkey; household archaeology debates; interpretive archaeology interlocutors (Hodder et al.).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Household concept contested (Hastorf notes elsewhere that it can still work for Andean compounds); architectural gender inference remains model-dependent.
