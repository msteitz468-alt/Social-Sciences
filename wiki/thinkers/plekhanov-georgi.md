# Georgi Plekhanov

## Overview

Russian Marxist theorist, conventionally called "the father of Russian Marxism"; founder of the Emancipation of Labour group (1883) and co-editor of the old *Iskra* (1901–03). Formerly documented here only through Lenin's and Bogdanov's engagements, this page now rests on his five major works (1885–1910), from the founding polemic to the final codification, including — *The Development of the Monist View of History* ([[plekhanov-monist-view-of-history-1895]], published legally as "N. Beltov," the name his opponents' polemics address) and *Fundamental Problems of Marxism* ([[plekhanov-fundamental-problems-of-marxism-1908]]).

## Contributions

In the philosophical register, Lenin treated Plekhanov as the leading Russian materialist: *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* repeatedly deploys Plekhanov's arguments against Kantianism (the "ichthyosaurus" argument — "the object existed long before the subject appeared") and notes that the would-be-Marxist Machists attacked Plekhanov as a proxy for Engels. Lenin nonetheless corrected him twice: for the "hieroglyph"/symbol theory of sensation (a deviation from Engels's "copies, images, mirror-reflections") and for a misreading of Carstanjen on "experience" ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]; see [[empirio-criticism-debate]], [[reflection-theory]]).

### Our Differences (1885, in his own words)

The founding text ([[plekhanov-our-differences-1885]], attributed): the break with Narodnaya Volya — capitalism already developing in Russia (the statistical case that fathered Lenin's method), the commune disintegrating into classes, conspiratorial seizure rejected ("cannot be stopped by any 'seizures of power' by any secret society"), the "Peruvian despotism" warning against minority-seizure socialism, and the five-thesis program with its two-stage perspective. Marxism as "the algebra of the revolution."

### The Monist View (1895, in his own words)

From [[plekhanov-monist-view-of-history-1895]] (attributed): the history-of-ideas staircase (French materialists → Restoration historians → utopian socialists → German idealism → Marx, whose discovery is placed "on a par with the discovery of Copernicus"); the naming and defense of the term **"dialectical materialism"** against "economic materialism" ("Marx never called himself an economic materialist"); the mediated prime-mover scheme (geography → productive forces → social relations, direct dependence becoming indirect); "economy is far from being a prime cause... itself a consequence, a 'function' of the productive forces"; freedom as known necessity; and the case against Mikhailovsky's subjective sociology, traced genealogically Bruno Bauer → Lavrov → Mikhailovsky, with the counter-thesis that "the kingdom of reason" arrives "only when the 'crowd' itself becomes the hero of historical action." The book fights the same anti-Narodnik war as Lenin's 1894 *What the "Friends of the People" Are* — same targets, convergent arguments, now independently documented from both authors.

### Fundamental Problems of Marxism (1908, in his own words)

His last systematic work ([[plekhanov-fundamental-problems-of-marxism-1908]], attributed): the Spinozism thesis — Marx and Engels's materialism as Feuerbach's de-theologized Spinozism, with unity-not-identity of thought and being; the **five-term formula** expanding base/superstructure (productive forces → economic relations → socio-political regime → social psychology → ideologies); the leaps essay against gradualism; dialectic as the "logic of contradiction." Written in 1908, the peak of the Machist fight, yet his own text touches it only in a brief protest against "new bases" for Marxism — the heavy anti-Bogdanov framing of the ingested edition belongs to Riazanov's 1928 preface, which also corrects Plekhanov's Feuerbach formulation in place ("not perfectly correct"). With the Monist View, his first and last major statements now bracket the corpus in primary.

### On the Role of the Individual in History (1898, in his own words)

The structure/agency classic ([[plekhanov-role-of-the-individual-1898]], attributed): individuals "can change the individual features of events... but they cannot change their general trend"; the "optical illusion" of great-man causation; talent as socially produced, its influence conditioned by epochal need and social access; great men as "beginners"; and the anti-quietist answer to the determinism-paralyzes charge. With this, the four ingested Plekhanov works span his codifying (1895), applying (1898), polemical (1908–10), and summarizing (1908) registers.

### Materialismus Militans (1908–10, in his own words)

His anti-Machist polemic ([[plekhanov-materialismus-militans-1908]], attributed): the reply to Bogdanov's 1907 Open Letter, in three letters — the "outside Marxism" exclusion, the Engels-fidelity defense against the "Holbachian" charge, the hobgoblin reductio of social-organization objectivity, and the defense (with self-correction) of the hieroglyph theory of sensation. This fills the gap the *Fundamental Problems* ingest exposed: the real Plekhanov anti-Machism, in his voice, distinct from both Riazanov's 1928 framing and Lenin's reflection doctrine.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Within ingested material: co-member of the 1901–02 *Iskra* board with Axelrod, Zasulich, Martov, Potresov, and Lenin, which briefly allied with the liberal Struve ([[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]]). Lenin cites Plekhanov circa 1900–03 — "when he was a Marxist and a revolutionary" — as having demanded Bernstein's expulsion from German Social-Democracy, and quotes his maxim that revolutionaries must be "well shod on all four feet."

## Critiques and Reappraisals

After 1914 Plekhanov took a "defencist" position on the war, and in 1917 edited *Yedinstvo*, supporting the Provisional Government and continuation of the war. Lenin's Vol. 24 writings attack him continually — as having called Lenin's April Theses "raving," as a "social-chauvinist," and as exemplifying "consistency of betrayal of socialism" ([[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]], attributed; these are partisan characterizations by his chief antagonist, recorded as positions, not verdicts). The trajectory Lenin narrates — foundational materialist theorist to wartime social-patriot — is itself the standard Soviet framing and awaits an independent source.

Even after the break, Lenin's respect for the philosopher persisted: in 1921 he attributed to "the late Plekhanov" the maxim "truth is always concrete, never abstract" and urged party members to study "all of Plekhanov's philosophical writings" as the best exposition of Marxist philosophy, proposing a dedicated volume ([[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]], attributed). Lenin's 1894 *What the "Friends of the People" Are* already treated Plekhanov's *Our Differences* as the founding Russian Social-Democratic work ([[lenin-what-the-friends-of-the-people-are-1894]]). In 1918 Lenin recalled that Plekhanov at the 1903 congress had himself defended disfranchising capitalists and dispersing a counter-revolutionary parliament ([[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]).

Bogdanov's side of their dispute is now primary-sourced ([[bogdanov-empiriomonism-1906]], attributed): the Book Three preface critiques "Bel'tov's" thing-in-itself position as a "golden mean" that leaves the problem unresolved, and his "reaction" theory of ideology as eclectic — while claiming his own system satisfies Plekhanov's stated materialism criterion.

## Lenin's private marginalia (1914–15/1920s reading, per [[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]])

Lenin's *Philosophical Notebooks* — reading notes never published in his lifetime, posthumously assembled (1929–33; CW 38, 1961) — preserve a harsher private verdict than his published praise of Plekhanov "the materialist." Lenin noted privately (undated fragment, associated with the 1914–15 Bern Hegel notebooks) that Plekhanov "wrote on philosophy (dialectics) probably about 1,000 pages" (*Beltov* + the anti-Bogdanov polemics + the anti-Kantian works + *Fundamental Problems*), yet on Hegel's large *Logic* specifically, "its thought (i.e., dialectics proper, as philosophical science)" amounted to "nil!!" He judged the unity/identity of opposites in Plekhanov (and, he added, in Engels) treated merely as a "sum-total of examples" for popularization rather than as a law of cognition and of the objective world, and — in "On the Question of Dialectics" (1915) — stated that "dialectics is the theory of knowledge of (Hegel and) Marxism," an aspect to which "Plekhanov, not to speak of other Marxists, paid no attention." Conspecting Plekhanov's own monograph on Chernyshevsky (undated, likely 1914–15), Lenin turned Plekhanov's Feuerbach-derived charge of insufficient dialectic back on its author, writing beside Plekhanov's diagnosis of a "shortcoming" in Chernyshevsky: "This is also the shortcoming of Plekhanov's book on Chernyshevsky." These are Lenin's private judgments — one side of the Lenin–Plekhanov epistemological divergence already documented above (the hieroglyph correction, the Carstanjen misreading) — not adjudicated verdicts; Plekhanov's own philosophical corpus (*Fundamental Problems*, the Chernyshevsky monograph) is not yet independently primary-ingested to test the charge from his side.

## Historiography

The *Monist View* and *Fundamental Problems* are now primary-ingested — both in Soviet-era editions whose corrective apparatus (the Monist View's footnotes; Riazanov's 1928 preface and notes) documents doctrinal drift across two strata of curation. Still un-ingested: *Our Differences*, the Feuerbach commentary Lenin cites in M&E, "On the Role of the Individual in History," the *Materialismus Militans* anti-Machist letters, and non-Soviet biographical scholarship.
