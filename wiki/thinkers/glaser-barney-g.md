# Barney G. Glaser

## Overview

Barney G. Glaser (1930–2022) was an American sociologist, co-author with [[strauss-anselm-l|Anselm L. Strauss]] of *[[glaser-strauss-discovery-grounded-theory-1967|The Discovery of Grounded Theory]]* (1967), the founding statement of [[grounded-theory|grounded theory]]. The book's preface presents him as Columbia-trained (Merton middle-range / Lazarsfeld quantitative methodology orbit) partnered with Chicago-trained Strauss — an "ironic conjunction" meant to close the theory–research gap from both sides.

## Contributions

From the 1967 joint text (attributed to Glaser & Strauss where the book speaks jointly):

- Co-formulated grounded theory as **generation** of theory from data (vs verification of a priori theory).
- Co-developed [[theoretical-sampling]], [[constant-comparative-method]], and the [[substantive-theory|substantive]] / [[formal-theory-grounded|formal]] theory distinction.
- Emphasized that quantitative data can generate theory (Ch. VIII), not only verify it — a Columbia-legible claim inside a generation program.
- Co-authored dying research under PHS nursing grant NU-00047 (*Awareness of Dying*, 1965 — not yet ingested) that supplies *Discovery*'s main illustrations.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Later methodological literature criticizes recipe-ization of GT, claims about induction purity, and post-split orthodoxy fights. *Discovery* anticipates resistance from verification-first canons (Ch. IX; Epilogue). Tavory & Timmermans (*[[tavory-timmermans-abductive-analysis-2014|Abductive Analysis]]*, 2014) place Glaser on the “classic”/positivist-heritage pole of the GT split and argue he never broached Peircean abduction — against later “abductive GT” rebranding.
