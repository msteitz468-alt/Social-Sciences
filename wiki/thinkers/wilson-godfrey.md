# Godfrey Wilson

British social anthropologist (1908–1944), first Director of the **Rhodes-Livingstone Institute** (Northern Rhodesia), student of [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]], husband of [[wilson-monica|Monica Wilson]]. Died in WWII service. This page is grounded in Richard Brown's essay in *[[asad-anthropology-colonial-encounter-1973|Anthropology and the Colonial Encounter]]* (1973); Wilson's primary texts are **not yet ingested**.

## Overview

Wilson's RLI directorship is a charter episode for the later [[manchester-school|Manchester School]] (institutional succession under [[gluckman-max|Max Gluckman]]). Brown reconstructs relations among colonial government, mining capital, and academic anthropology as a concrete **knowledge and power** case — funders paid a form of "hidden taxation" rather than hiring handmaidens; the institute was servant of neither government nor mines, yet research did not occur in a vacuum (mine/admin blocks; later Epstein copperbelt parallel).

## Contributions

*(Attributed via Brown 1973.)*

- **RLI founding context:** Governor Young, Colonial Office obstruction, Ormsby-Gore, Hailey; board structure without scientists; funding from colonial governments, British South Africa Company, and copper companies.
- **Research programme:** Wilson insisted *he* propose the programme (reluctantly agreed by trustees who wanted native-policy utility); urbanisation and social change; [[participant-observation]]; *Economics of Detribalization* (RLI Papers 1941–42).
- **Field conflicts:** urban research in Broken Hill African locations; Copperbelt mines withdrew permission after war outbreak; Broken Hill manager allowed continuation; fraternisation and administrative tension; CO/wartime resignation 1941.
- **Intellectual stance:** Malinowski seminar formation; Marxist influence on refusing sharp SA/sociology/history splits and bringing economic/administrative factors into Central African analysis; Christian pacifist objectivity.
- **Succession:** Gluckman's seven-year plan and academicisation of the RLI after Wilson.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Central case on [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]]: used by Brown to test handmaiden vs reluctant vs relative-autonomy readings against archive and career evidence. Radical critics (Goddard, Banaji, Gough, et al.) are Brown's polemical backdrop.
