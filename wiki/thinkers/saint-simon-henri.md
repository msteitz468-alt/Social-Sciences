# Henri de Saint-Simon

## Overview

Claude Henri de Rouvroy, comte de Saint-Simon (1760–1825), French social thinker associated with early industrial and organizational schemes for post-Revolutionary society. Appears in this wiki first as the senior associate of the young [[comte-auguste|Auguste Comte]] (relations roughly 1817–1822/24).

## Contributions

Not yet documented from primary Saint-Simon sources. Secondary note from Frederic Harrison's introduction to Martineau's *Positive Philosophy*: Saint-Simon's optimistic, humanitarian reformism impressed Comte's bent toward social reorganization, while Comte rejected Saint-Simon's shallowness, vanity, and later mystical theologism; the 1822 prospectus is treated by Comteans as already independent of Saint-Simon. Martineau's condensation of the *Cours* (primary for this wiki's Comte pages) does not systematically treat Saint-Simon in the Introduction range extracted at ingest.

One documented reception: in the [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] (1848), [[marx-karl|Marx]] and [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] class Saint-Simon (with Fourier and Owen) as a **Critical-Utopian** socialist — credited with perceiving class antagonism and producing "the most valuable materials for the enlightenment of the working class," but faulted for substituting inventors' "social science" and model experiments for the real historical movement of the [[proletariat|proletariat]], and for appealing "to society at large, without distinction of class."
