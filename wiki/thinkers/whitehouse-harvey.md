# Harvey Whitehouse

## Overview

Anthropologist and CSR program-builder. White (2021) presents him as moving from immersive ethnography (*Inside the Cult*, PNG) to a cognitively grounded theory of religious transmission — [[modes-of-religiosity|modes of religiosity]] (imagistic vs doctrinal) — tested with experimental psychologists and historians. PhD supervisor of [[white-claire]].

## Contributions

- **[[modes-of-religiosity]]** — imagistic (low frequency, high arousal, episodic memory, small-group cohesion) vs doctrinal (high frequency, low arousal, semantic memory, large-scale orthodoxy) packages linking ritual dynamics to socio-political scale.
- Early CSR ethnography: *Inside the Cult* (1995).
- Later: identity fusion and extreme ritual bonding; multi-method field programs (surveys, experiments, qualitative work).
- Institutional voice for scientific study of religion (AAR address quoted by White).

## Intellectual Genealogy

**trained:** [[white-claire]]. Collaborators/peers in White's map: [[mccauley-robert-n]], [[barrett-justin-l]], [[boyer-pascal]], Emma Cohen, Dimitris Xygalatas.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Modes model refined against historical and archaeological tests (White: theory modified in light of evidence). Imagistic/doctrinal typology risks over-dichotomizing ritual systems; White presents it as empirically productive, not closed.
