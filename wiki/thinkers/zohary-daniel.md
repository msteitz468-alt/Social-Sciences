# Daniel Zohary (1926–2016)

Israeli plant geneticist and archaeobotanical collaborator; contributed “The progenitors of wheat and barley in relation to domestication and agricultural dispersal in the Old World” to *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*.

## Overview

Zohary’s seminar paper maps wild progenitors of wheat and barley to domestication and Old World agricultural dispersal — a foundational botanical frame for Near Eastern founder crops later expanded in *Domestication of Plants in the Old World* (with Maria Hopf). Treat 1969 claims as period genetics/biogeography; later molecular work revises centers and pathways — date-stamp.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Zohary 1969 in Ucko & Dimbleby; details filled from extraction.)*

- Identifies and discusses wild progenitors of cultivated wheat and barley relative to archaeological domestication.
- Links progenitor geography to agricultural dispersal in the Old World.
- Contributes botanical constraints on where and how cereal domestication could begin (natural-habitat logic related to, but not identical with, [[braidwood-robert|Braidwood]]’s [[hilly-flanks|hilly flanks]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Plant-genetic and archaeobotanical networks of the 1960s domestication seminars; later collaboration with Maria Hopf (who also contributes a Jericho plant-remains paper in the same volume). Training links not yet documented here.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Progenitor maps and dispersal narratives from 1969 are provisional relative to post-1990s genetics; keep “(as of Zohary 1969)” on specific taxon claims.
