# Frédéric Engel

## Overview

French-Peruvian archaeologist whose wide-ranging survey and excavation of maritime settlements (from the mid-1950s) built much of the empirical base for the Andean coastal preceramic. Per [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|Moseley (1975)]], Engel's 1957 survey found "more than forty early sites," a figure subsequent work at least doubled; his excavation of [[el-paraiso|El Paraíso]] (1967) documented "a masonry complex comparable in size to later Andean and Near Eastern preliterate cities."

## Contributions

- **Cotton Preceramic horizon marker** — Engel (1957b) proposed the presence of cotton artifacts as the marker defining the Cotton Preceramic Stage (adopted by Moseley; see [[andean-chronology]]).
- **El Paraíso excavation** — cleared and partly restored one ~50 m square complex, documenting five to six construction/remodeling stages (attributed); estimated the whole could house 1,500–3,000 people.
- **Asia site (1963)** — excavated the Asia cemetery (49 mat-wrapped burials, unequal grave goods, 8 severed "trophy" skulls, two possible trepanations), a key source for preceramic mortuary and social-differentiation inferences.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Part of the mid-century central-coast survey generation alongside [[bird-junius|Bird]], [[lanning-edward|Lanning]], and [[patterson-thomas|Patterson]]; his site inventory and El Paraíso data feed directly into Moseley's synthesis.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Moseley (1975) accepts Engel's excavation data but revises his interpretation of [[el-paraiso|El Paraíso]] as basically residential, arguing for a non-residential regional function.

Densified from [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|Moseley 1975]] only; Engel's primary reports not directly ingested.
