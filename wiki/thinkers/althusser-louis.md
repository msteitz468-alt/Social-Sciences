# Louis Althusser (1918–1990)

French Marxist philosopher, principal theorist of [[structural-marxism]]. This page is a scaffold created during the ingest of [[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Sahlins's *Culture and Practical Reason*]] and *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*, which engage the Althusserian program critically; his primary works are not yet ingested.

## Overview

*(Attributed, via Sahlins's use.)* Althusser reread [[marx-karl|Marx]] to purge Marxism of Hegelian humanism, positing an "epistemological break" between the young (philosophical) and the mature (scientific) Marx, and modeling society as a **structured whole** of relatively autonomous levels (economy, politics, ideology) in which the economy is "determinant in the last instance." With Étienne Balibar he produced *Reading Capital* (1965), the touchstone applied by anthropologists (Terray, Godelier) to precapitalist societies.

## Contributions

- **Determination in the last instance / the structured social formation**, the framework Sahlins engages and rejects (see [[structural-marxism]]).
- **The young/mature Marx distinction**, which Sahlins invokes against a simple materialist reading — noting Marx's own thought "cannot be simply assimilated to the received empirico-materialism in anthropology."
- A distinction Sahlins borrows in passing: "to recognize an important fact, to see it, is not the same" as developing the concept of it.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Through *Reading Capital* Althusser shaped the anthropological structural-Marxism of Terray, Meillassoux, and [[godelier-maurice|Godelier]], and thereby became a chief polemical target for [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]], who reads the program's reduction of production relations to "an abstract shadow structure of productive requirements" as "a refusal of the symbolic constitution of social reality." Formal training relations are not documented in the ingested sources.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Sahlins's objection is that Althusserian Marxism, for all its anti-empiricism, still treats the material/economic as determining and the symbolic as derivative — reproducing "practical reason" at the level of high theory (see [[practical-reason]]). Broader reappraisals of Althusser (the abandonment of "theoreticism," and the biographical catastrophe of 1980, when he killed his wife Hélène Rytmann and was confined rather than tried) lie outside the ingested sources and are noted here only for the record.
