# Louis Althusser (1918–1990)

## Overview

French Marxist philosopher, long associated with the École Normale Supérieure and the French Communist Party; principal theorist of [[structural-marxism]]. Primary sources now ingested: *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]* (*Pour Marx*, 1965) and *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* (*Lire le Capital*, 1965; Verso 2015 Complete Edition) — the dual philosophical charter of the program. The 1970 Ideological State Apparatuses essay is **not** yet primary-ingested; do not file ISA/interpellation claims from these volumes.

## Contributions

### From *For Marx* (attributed)

- **[[epistemological-break]]** in Marx ~1845: science of history vs Feuerbachian/Hegelian ideological problematics of the Early Works; periodization Early / Break / Transitional / Mature.
- **[[overdetermination]]** of contradiction: Marxist contradiction always specified by the structured whole; never Hegel’s simple contradiction; 1917 as type case of fusion; block vs rupture.
- **[[structure-in-dominance]]** and **determination in the last instance** by the economy, with **relative autonomy** and specific effectivity of superstructures (“lonely hour of the last instance never comes”).
- **[[theoretical-practice]]** and **Generalities I–II–III**: knowledge as production; Theory (capital T) = dialectical materialism as theory of practice (later self-corrected).
- **[[problematic]]** (via Jacques Martin): unit of theoretical analysis; ideology unconscious of its problematic.
- **[[theoretical-anti-humanism]]**: mature Marxism does not ground history in Man; humanism is ideology (may be practical slogan); see [[marxist-humanism-debate]].
- **[[social-formation]]** as concrete complex whole of economic, political, ideological practices — object of [[historical-materialism]].
- **Materialist theatre** (Piccolo Teatro / Brecht essay); **1844 Manuscripts** as threshold, not the secret of *Capital*.

### From *Reading Capital* (attributed — Parts One and Four)

- **[[symptomatic-reading]]**: guilty philosophical reading that makes lacunae and the “discourse of the silence” perceptible; dual reading of *Capital* against Early Works.
- **[[knowledge-effect]]**: mechanism of cognitive appropriation by ordered scientific discourse (*Gliederung*), distinct from history of knowledge production; ideological vs scientific knowledge effects.
- **[[structural-causality]]**: effectivity of a structure on its elements; *Darstellung* as mode of presence of structure in its effects; structure immanent in effects (Spinozist); “authorless theatre.”
- **[[differential-historical-time]]**: each level and each mode has a peculiar time; no Hegelian essential section; Marxism as anti-historicism paired with anti-humanism.
- **Object of *Capital***: not England as impure example but capitalist [[mode-of-production]] in its conceptual *Kerngestalt* (“ideal average”); rupture with classical political economy’s object and planar causality.
- **Seminar director** of the 1964–65 ENS collective reading that produced the multi-author volume ([[balibar-etienne]], [[ranciere-jacques]], [[macherey-pierre]], [[establet-roger]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Formation context:** PCF philosopher under dogmatism then post-Twentieth Congress “liberation”; Sino-Soviet rupture; intervention against humanist return to Early Works.
- **Debts:** Bachelard (break); Canguilhem; Spinoza (science/ideology; structural causality foreshadowing); Lenin; Mao *On Contradiction*; Freud (overdetermination; symptomatic metaphor); critical Engels; selective Gramsci (target of anti-historicism in *Reading Capital*).
- **trained:** ENS circle of *Reading Capital* — [[balibar-etienne]], [[ranciere-jacques]], [[macherey-pierre]], [[establet-roger]] (among others named in Presentation: Duroux, Linhart, Milner).
- **Anthropological uptake:** Terray, Meillassoux, [[godelier-maurice|Godelier]] → structural Marxism in anthropology.
- **Chief anthropological critic in this wiki:** [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] (*[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]*).
- **Relation to [[western-marxism]]:** rival recovery of Marx (anti-historicist, anti-Hegelian, anti-humanist) rather than Lukácsian cultural charter.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Humanist and historicist Marxisms: theoreticism; evacuation of subject and ethics ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]).
- Self-criticisms: theoreticist silence on theory–practice fusion; philosophy redefined (1968–) as class struggle in theory, not Theory of theoretical practice (glossary interpolation / Avertissement track).
- Sahlins / culturalist anthropology: last-instance economic determination as practical-reason deep structure.
- Biographical catastrophe of 1980 (killing of Hélène Rytmann; confinement rather than trial) — factual record; not used here to adjudicate theoretical claims.
- Later work (ISAs, aleatory materialism) reshapes the program — await primary ingest.
- **Archaeological uptake (secondary via Leone 2010):** [[leone-mark-p|Mark Leone]]'s [[critical-archaeology]] operationalizes Althusserian ideology for U.S. historical archaeology and public tours ("Toward a Critical Archaeology," 1987, cites Althusser 1969/1971 among others). The ISA essay itself remains un-ingested as primary; file Leone's uses as **reception**, not as substitute for Althusser's own text.
