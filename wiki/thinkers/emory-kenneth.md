# Kenneth P. Emory

## Overview

Kenneth P. Emory was a Bishop Museum ethnologist and archaeologist, per Kirch (*On the Road of the Winds*, 2017), whose career spanned the Bayard Dominick Expeditions of the 1920s through the postwar radiocarbon and stratigraphic revolutions in Pacific archaeology. He was a member of the Bayard Dominick Expedition's Hawaiian team in 1920, the first field project in Polynesia, working at Hanakauhi, Haleakalā Crater, Maui.

## Contributions

- **First radiocarbon date for Polynesia** — was the first to respond to Willard Libby's 1950 call for Pacific charcoal samples, sending a sample from the Kuli'ou'ou rockshelter, O'ahu, dated to A.D. 1004 ± 180 (reported February 19, 1951). Emory's own reaction, quoted by Kirch (via Krauss 1988:338): "Boy, was I excited... Immediately it opened a whole new vista of possibilities." This event is presented by Kirch as the founding methodological moment for chronometric Pacific archaeology; see [[radiocarbon-dating]].
- **Fishhook-based [[seriation|seriation]] sequence for Hawai'i** (Emory, Bonk, and Sinoto 1959) — with William J. Bonk and Yosihiko Sinoto, defined a fishhook-based (rather than pottery-based, since Eastern Polynesia lacks pre-contact pottery) artifact sequence for Hawaiian prehistory.
- **Adoption of genuine stratigraphic control** — in 1954, per Kirch, was persuaded by Yosihiko Sinoto to abandon arbitrary six-inch-level excavation in favor of stratigraphic excavation — a methodological turning point in Pacific archaeology.
- **Marquesas-centric dispersal-center model** (attributed, theoretical claim, later revised/superseded) — with Sinoto, extended research from the Society Islands (disappointing stratification) to the Marquesas; excavations at the Hane dune site, Ua Huka (1963–65), led to a model positing the Marquesas as the dispersal center for Eastern Polynesian settlement (Emory and Sinoto 1965; Sinoto 1966, 1970).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborated directly with [[te-rangi-hiroa|Te Rangi Hiroa]] on the joint 1934 Mangarevan Expedition. Worked closely with Yosihiko Sinoto, who is credited by Kirch with persuading Emory toward stratigraphic rigor. Was not Te Rangi Hiroa's successor as Bishop Museum director (Alexander Spoehr succeeded Hiroa in 1951) — Kirch's source explicitly flags this as a point not to conflate.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The Marquesas-centric dispersal-center model is recorded by Kirch's source material as later contested/superseded by subsequent settlement-pattern and phylogenetic research (see [[green-roger]], [[near-oceania-remote-oceania]]); this wiki flags it here as a historically important but revised position, per the Contradiction and Replication Protocol, pending direct confirmation from later chapters of Kirch's synthesis.

## Historiography

Primary source in this vault: Kirch, *On the Road of the Winds* (2017), which treats Emory's radiocarbon initiative as the pivotal event separating comparative-ethnology-era Pacific prehistory from chronometric archaeology.
