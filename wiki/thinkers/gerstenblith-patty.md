# Patty Gerstenblith

Professor of law at DePaul University College of Law (as of 2006 contributor note); leading US legal scholar on cultural heritage protection, the [[illicit-antiquities-trade]], and implementation of international conventions in domestic law. Author of chapter 3 in [[brodie-kersel-luke-tubb-archaeology-cultural-heritage-antiquities-trade-2006|Brodie et al. 2006]], "Recent Developments in the Legal Protection of Cultural Heritage."

## Overview

Gerstenblith's contribution densifies the vault's legal layer of the antiquities debate: **McClain doctrine**, National Stolen Property Act applications, CPIA mechanics, sentencing guidelines for cultural-heritage crime, and post-2000 case law — the machinery that turns UNESCO norms into enforceable US outcomes.

## Contributions

- **US cultural-heritage law synthesis** (attributed, Gerstenblith 2006) — recent case developments, NSPA/McClain, CPIA, Archaeological Resources Protection Act interactions, sentencing.
- Recurrent cited authority in heritage-ethics literature (also referenced from Renfrew/Cuno poles).

## Intellectual Genealogy

US cultural-property bar and academic law; dialogue with archaeologists (AIA, IARC network) and with museum counsel on the other side of acquisition disputes.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Cultural-internationalist critics (e.g. [[cuno-james]]) treat expansive source-nation ownership doctrines and import restrictions as nationalist retentionism — positions on [[antiquities-market-looting-debate]], not wiki-voice verdicts on Gerstenblith's legal analysis.

## Contributions (densified — ch. 3)

### Legal holdings densified (as of Gerstenblith 2006)
- Demand-side thesis in legal key: ultimate purchasers (museums/collectors) finance looting; civil/criminal consequences in market states depress demand.
- **McClain** and **Schultz**: NSPA recognizes foreign national ownership laws for archaeological objects as "stolen" property when knowing trafficking is proven.
- **CPIA** vs NSPA relationship; Steinhardt phiale and related cases as reported.
- Cultural heritage **sentencing guideline §2B1.5** (ARPA/NAGPRA valuation of archaeological value).
- UK 2003 Dealing in Cultural Objects (Offences) Act vs Swiss due-diligence culture as comparative notes.
- Crisis tests: Taliban 2001; Iraq 2003 aftermath — progress and regime deficiencies under shock.
