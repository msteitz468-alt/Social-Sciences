# James R. Arnold (1923–2012)

Physical chemist and collaborator of **[[libby-willard-f|Willard F. Libby]]** in the development of **[[radiocarbon-dating]]**. Libby's first-edition preface names Arnold and **[[anderson-e-c|E. C. Anderson]]** as the collaborators without whom "certainly nothing would have been done" (attributed, Libby 1952 preface). Co-author of the 1949 *Science* papers that established natural radiocarbon and early dating results.

## Overview

Arnold appears in *[[libby-radiocarbon-dating-1952|Radiocarbon Dating]]* as a core Chicago collaborator on assay and early known-age checks. Later career (beyond this source) included cosmochemistry; this page records only the radiocarbon-development role as documented in the Libby monograph.

## Contributions

- Co-development of natural radiocarbon detection and dating assays (attributed, Libby 1952 prefaces and ch. III citations to Arnold & Libby 1949).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained/collaborated under Libby at Chicago on the C-14 program (as described in Libby 1952).
