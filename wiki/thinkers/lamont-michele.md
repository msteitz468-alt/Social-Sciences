# Michèle Lamont

## Overview

Michèle Lamont (b. 1957) is a Quebec-born sociologist of culture, inequality, and evaluation. The primary source ingested in this vault is *[[lamont-money-morals-manners-1992|Money, Morals, and Manners]]* (1992), the comparative interview study that established [[symbolic-boundaries]] and [[boundary-work]] as central objects of U.S. cultural sociology. Study analysis: [[lamont-money-morals-manners-study]]; deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/lamont-money-morals-manners-study]].

At the time of the *Money, Morals, and Manners* fieldwork she presented herself as a Quebecer outsider-insider to both French and American cultures (four years in France, five in the United States) and contacted respondents on Princeton University letterhead as an assistant professor. She defines the study’s problem as the criteria through which upper-middle-class people discriminate worthy from less worthy persons — recovered inductively rather than presupposed as [[cultural-capital|high-culture capital]].

## Contributions

From *Money, Morals, and Manners* (attributed):

- **[[symbolic-boundaries]]** — lines drawn when categorizing people; three-standard typology (moral, socioeconomic, cultural) whose *relative salience* varies by nation, region, occupation, and trajectory.
- **[[boundary-work]]** — the pervasive activity of drawing and defending those lines; national character tropes and occupational mutual scorn treated as instances of one process.
- **[[high-status-signals]]** — concrete keys to evaluation (honesty, money, brilliance, refinement, self-actualization, etc.) recovered from open-ended talk rather than survey a priori categories.
- **Boundary approach** — do not predefine the content of distinction; analyze multiple contents and variable forms; treat boundary *strength* and *autonomy* across standards as empirical.
- **Inequality mechanism** — class-related signals used under meritocratic guise can reproduce inequality; only strong, widely shared boundaries convert differentiation into hierarchy (against automatic poststructural hierarchy-from-difference).
- **Critique of Bourdieu** — builds on shared-style reproduction; rejects underestimation of moral signals, Parisian cultural-boundary overgeneralization, and habitus as proximate-only (see [[lamont-money-morals-manners-study]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Design limits Lamont herself states: white male main sample; minority/non-native exclusion; college threshold; intentional specialist overrepresentation; non-pure-random final selection; exploratory female n=15. Later Lamont work (race, immigration, dignity, evaluation — not ingested here) extends and retools the 1992 frame; reception is program-building more than single-study falsification. `replication_status` on the study page is `reanalyzed-contested` in the sense of ongoing extension and theoretical contest with Bourdieuian cultural capital, not a classic failed replication.
