# Roy Wagner

## Overview

Roy Wagner (1938–2018) was an American anthropologist of Melanesia (the **Daribi** of Papua New Guinea) and a symbolic/semiotic theorist whose *The Invention of Culture* (1975; rev. 1981) reframed "culture" as an act of invention performed reciprocally by ethnographer and studied alike. He enters this vault through [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]'s *[[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]*, which names Wagner — with [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]] and [[latour-bruno|Latour]] — as one of the discipline's genuine "post-structuralists," those whose work has the deepest affinities with [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]]. All claims here are attributed.

## Contributions (as received via Viveiros de Castro)

- **Convention and invention** (*The Invention of Culture*): two reciprocal modes of symbolization — a "collectivizing, conventional (literal)" mode and a "differentiating, inventive (figurative)" mode; cultures differ in which they treat as innately *given* and which as humanly *constructed*. Western ontology takes the body/nature as given and the soul/culture as constructed; many indigenous ontologies invert this (soul given, body constructed) — a claim Viveiros de Castro folds into [[perspectivism]] and [[multinaturalism]].
- **Reverse anthropology** and the "invention" concept — the studied invent the anthropologist as the anthropologist invents them.
- **Obviation**: a dialectical figure-substitution model of symbolic action.
- **The fractal person** ("never a unit standing in relation to an aggregate... always an entity with relationship integrally implied," 1991) — which Viveiros de Castro reads through Deleuzian multiplicity and [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]]'s partial connections.
- Wagner's fieldwork dictum, "their misunderstanding of me was not the same as my misunderstanding them" (Daribi), which Viveiros de Castro calls "the best definition of culture ever proposed" and ties to his method of [[controlled-equivocation]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Fieldwork:** the Daribi of Papua New Guinea.
- **Affinities / reception:** grouped by [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] with [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]] and [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]] as post-structuralist anthropologists; his convention/invention "duplex" read as a [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuzian]] reciprocal-presupposition structure.
