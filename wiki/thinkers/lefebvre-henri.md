# Henri Lefebvre

## Overview

Henri Lefebvre (1901–1991) was a French Marxist philosopher and sociologist whose work on everyday life, the urban, and the production of space became foundational for critical human geography after the 1970s. This page is grounded in *[[lefebvre-production-of-space-1991|The Production of Space]]* (1974 French / 1991 Nicholson-Smith) and [[harvey-david|David Harvey]]’s Afterword biography in that edition. Earlier urban writings and the *Critique of Everyday Life* series are named but not yet primary-ingested.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Lefebvre in POS unless noted.)*

- **Space as social product.** “(Social) space is a (social) product” — against empty container, pure mental category, or passive locus of relations. See [[production-of-space]] and [[hubs/theory/production-of-space|Theory Hub]].
- **[[spatial-triad|Spatial triad]].** Spatial practice (perceived), representations of space (conceived), representational spaces (lived).
- **History of space.** [[absolute-space|Absolute]] → historical accumulation → capitalist [[abstract-space|abstract space]], with [[differential-space|differential space]] as emergent alternative.
- **Unitary critique of fragmented spatial knowledge** as political/technocratic use of knowledge under neocapitalism.
- **Urban sequence (broader corpus, Harvey).** Seven books 1968–1974 on urbanization culminating in POS; city superseded by production of space; co-founded *Espace et Société* (circle including younger figures such as [[castells-manuel|Castells]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Marxist tradition:** [[marx-karl|Marx]] (broad production concept; *Grundrisse*/*Capital*), [[historical-materialism]], Gramscian [[gramsci-antonio|hegemony]] applied to space; [[western-marxism|Western Marxism]] reception network.
- **Formative (Harvey Afterword):** *jeune philosophes* vs Bergsonism; Spinoza, Hegel, Nietzsche, Heidegger; Dada/surrealism (Tzara, Breton, Aragon); PCF 1928–1958 (break “by the left” after 1956); Strasbourg then Nanterre sociology chairs; argument against Althusserian structuralism and Foucault’s detachment from everyday life.
- **Anglophone reception:** [[harvey-david|Harvey]] (1991 Afterword; spatial fix / time-space compression as related but distinct tools).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Common later critiques (secondary until ingested): under-operationalization of the triad; universalizing history; gender/colonial blind spots; tension between diagnostic power on abstract space and utopian differential horizon. Harvey: POS is a preliminary, “detonating,” intensely political enquiry, not a closed system.

## Historiography

Harvey relies on Lefebvre’s *La somme et le reste* (1959) and Rémi Hess, *Henri Lefebvre et l’aventure du siècle* (1988). French reception preceded the 1991 English translation that catalyzed the anglophone spatial turn. Wiki’s first primary filing is the 1991 text.
