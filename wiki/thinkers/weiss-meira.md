# Meira Weiss

## Overview

Meira Weiss is an Israeli anthropologist (Gender Studies and Medical Sociology, Hebrew University of Jerusalem; chairperson of the Israeli Anthropology Association as of the *Commodifying Bodies* bio). Her contribution to *[[scheper-hughes-wacquant-commodifying-bodies-2002|Commodifying Bodies]]* analyzes the **Yemenite Children Affair** as a “drama of commodification in two acts”: (1) 1950s medicalization of new immigrants by the Israeli state; (2) late-1990s/early-2000s biomedical/DNA verification of lineage via exhumed remains and second-generation claimants. Related program (volume bio): medicalization, militarism, individualism/collectivism, body as convergence of discourses; book on the National Institute of Forensic Medicine in progress; *The Chosen Body* forthcoming Stanford UP (as of 2002).

## Contributions

### Culture of the chosen body *(attributed — Weiss)*

Weiss argues Israeli society has regulated bodies as part of constructing collective identity — the **“culture of the chosen body”** (Weiss 2001): pre-state “new person” embodied as pioneer (*halutz*) — secular, strong, masculine, healthy, physically perfect. Despite Zionist egalitarian ideology, ethnic stratification (Ashkenazim over Mizrachim) was maintained and strengthened in 1950s mass immigration absorption (via Shafir 1990 on earlier Yemenite arrivals remaining subservient).

### Act I — 1950s transit camps and medicalization *(attributed — Weiss; state and parent testimonies distinguished)*

- Operation **“On Eagles’ Wings”** airlifted some **50,000** Yemenite Jews to Israel 1949–50 (of these, 36,000 in 1949). First homes: eight enormous tent transit camps (*ma’abarot*); poor hygiene, food shortage, malaria; masses of Yemenite babies and children taken for medical treatment.
- Weiss reports public estimates of ~**1000** disappearances from hospitals serving transit-camp patients; a governmental investigation committee was established in **1995** and accepted petitions for exhumation and DNA matching.
- Pattern described: children taken for treatment; days later parents notified of death. Weiss: in most cases parents accepted loss fatalistically at the time; **today activists say** hundreds were kidnapped and adopted by Ashkenazi families (many in the USA). Official documents (voting cards, conscription requests) mailed after alleged “death” led families to believe the child alive.
- **Distinguish carefully (Weiss’s map of positions):**
  - **State/agency sources** (e.g. Haim Sadok, Jewish Agency): malaria, night deaths, overwhelmed nursing (1949: 250 nurses for over 7,000 very ill Yemenite babies) — “it would be amazing if there weren’t lost babies.” Ben-Gurion diary via Sadok: “The Yemenite children are dying like flies.”
  - **Yemenite community accusations (positions, not wiki-voice fact):** **kidnapping** (Yigal Yosef: mothers as “baby machines” / commodity suppliers for childless Holocaust survivors; parents accuse sales abroad for weapons money) and **human experimentation** — Weiss labels experimentation allegations “difficult to substantiate,” while citing medical literature that treated Yemenites as disease sources and autopsy coronary-intima studies on ~60 Yemenite infants/children (1956–66).
  - **Official commissions (as Weiss reports findings):** 1968 committee — 316 of 342 investigated children died; 1988 Shalgi committee (findings 1994) — 75 of 505 disappearances unaccounted for, “no indication they were victims of illegal adoption or other criminal activity.”
  - **Staff testimony on the adoption side (position):** nurse Rosa Oushinnsky told the governmental committee she took healthy Yemenite babies to city hospital and returned without them — “given for adoption, mostly in the USA.” Weiss notes 1998–9 interviews with Yemenite Federation of Los Angeles and US Yemenites “have confirmed the accusation of kidnapping for the purpose of adoption” (her claim about those interviews).
- Ethnography of objectification: children’s houses bar parents for “hygiene”; deaths announced by first name only; ambulance transfers without name-tags; hospital staff use room/bed numbers as “more reliable” than Yemenite names — “Nameless, the children became blank slates, objects…” Weiss analogizes Zionist-state attitude to colonial medical “civilizing” of “primitives” (via Comaroff).

### Methods 1996–1999 *(descriptive — Weiss)*

Interviews, observations, textual analyses: 20 family relatives of allegedly abducted children (approached at National Institute of Forensic Medicine during blood/DNA testing); observation of **ten full-day exhumations**; Institute staff interviews; key informants including Tzila Levin, Ora Shifris, attorney Rami Tsubeiri, four anonymous followers of Rabbi Uzi Meshulam; ~**61 hours** of 1995 commission-of-inquiry protocols; official medical records and letters to families. All interviews Hebrew, taped.

### Act II — DNA, exhumation, Levine–Omessi *(attributed — Weiss)*

- High Court petition for genetic profiles; cost politics (petitioners ~NIS300/person vs Hiss estimate ~$1500/person for reliable profiles).
- **Tzila Levine** story: adoptee seeking origins; Margalit Omessi claimed recognition from baby photo. **Lab conflict:** Hebrew University geneticist Hasan Khatib reported 99.9% mother–daughter certainty (nuclear genomic DNA); Institute for Forensic Medicine negative (mitochondrial DNA). Dec 1997 Hebrew University admitted Khatib’s positive result was incorrect (personal favor; no prior human maternity/paternity DNA experience). Hiss: mtDNA can negate with complete certainty; genomic DNA never completely certain. **Despite scientific ruling,** Levine and Omessi continued as mother and daughter — loss of confidence in scientific authority fueled by political and ethnic resentment (Weiss).
- **Ten graves exhumed — not empty** (contrary to some community belief). Age estimates used Caucasian 1990s children’s charts; Yemenite elder disputed skeletal age (“Our children were smaller then”). Incomplete remains re-associated “like a jigsaw puzzle.” After DNA purification, lab workers shifted from serial numbers to “the child.” Matching never 100% positive; negative results treated as 100% certain.
- Exhumed remains and DNA samples **commodified** in cost-containment terms; commercial language in Birmingham lab correspondence (“delivering the goods,” payment terms); mini-sequencing recommended as cheaper pre-test.

### Analytic conclusions *(attributed — Weiss)*

Parents and children lived ~50 years in limbo; discourse of commodification/subjugation/discrimination became central to Yemenite identity. Early 1950s as liminal national rite: sidelocks cut, tefillin confiscated, youth taught to despise “primitive” parents. Genetic identification for Yemenites = desire to recover primordial “lineage”/ethnic roots — **interpretation completely different** from scientific meaning of genetic ID. Affair as rupture in Israeli body politic: shift from one collectivist interchangeable “chosen” body to many ethnic imperfect bodies; melting-pot doctrine aimed at effacing ethnic traditions under Ashkenazi hegemony. Biotechnologies novel in themselves, but meaning-in-use reintroduces social/ethnic/political; ultimate identity defined via tradition and kinship “ontologies,” not biotech alone. Parallel: 1996 Ma’ariv report that Israeli blood bank quietly destroyed Ethiopian Israelis’ donations (HIV/hepatitis-B/malaria rates cited); placards “One People, One Blood.”

## Intellectual Genealogy

Framed within *Commodifying Bodies* medical-anthropology conversation (Scheper-Hughes, Lock, Sharp, Appadurai on commodity-as-exchange). Colonial medicine analogy via Comaroff. Israeli ethnic stratification via Shafir and related sociological literature. Volume bio places her work at medicalization, militarism, and the body as discourse convergence.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The Yemenite Children Affair remains contested in Israeli public culture. This page **does not adjudicate** kidnapping vs loss-in-chaos vs official “no criminal activity” findings: it records Weiss’s two-act commodification frame and keeps activist accusations, staff testimony, and official commission results in attributed voice. Experimentation claims stay labeled as Weiss labels them (difficult to substantiate where she says so).
