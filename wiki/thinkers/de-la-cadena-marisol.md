# Marisol de la Cadena

Peruvian-born anthropologist; major Andeanist contributor to the anthropological [[ontological-turn]]. Best known in this vault for *[[de-la-cadena-earth-beings-2015|Earth Beings]]* (2015; Lewis Henry Morgan Lectures 2011), co-labored with Mariano and Nazario Turpo of [[pacchanta|Pacchanta]]. Earlier work includes *Indigenous Mestizos: The Politics of Race and Culture in Cuzco, Peru, 1919–1991* (Duke, 2000) — not yet ingested.

## Overview

De la Cadena practices ethnography as **[[co-laboring|co-labor]]** with interlocutors whose worlds include [[earth-beings|tirakuna / earth-beings]] (e.g. Ausangate) that modern political and scientific institutions cannot recognize without reduction to “belief” or “culture.” She draws on [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]’s [[partial-connections]], [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]’s [[controlled-equivocation]], and Isabelle Stengers’s [[cosmopolitics]] to argue for ethnographic practice that neither dismisses nor romanticizes ontological divergence.

## Contributions

- **[[earth-beings]]** as analytic translation of *tirakuna* — other-than-human participants in runakuna life and politics, not symbols of “nature.”
- **Being-in-ayllu** — retheorizes [[ayllu]] as socionatural relational mode (runakuna + tirakuna + animals + plants), not only kin corporation (via Justo Oxa’s weaving metaphor and Turpo practice).
- **Land struggle as equivocation** — Mariano Turpo’s fight against hacienda Lauramarca translated as “peasant movement” while excess (earth-beings, in-ayllu speech) remained unthinkable to modern politics; lawyers and Ausangate “need each other.”
- **[[munayniyuq]]** — owner of the will: Ausangate and hacendado as ontologically different wills shaping runakuna lives; residual munay in post-reform state.
- **Andean shamanism under multiculturalism** — Nazario Turpo’s work as *chamán andino* for tourism and at the National Museum of the American Indian as comedy of equivocations (*suq’a* ≠ ancestors; spirituality slot).
- **Ethnographic cosmopolitics** — program for anthropology that slows down reasoning and makes room for divergent worlds (Stengers); Sinakara lesson that environmental victory can withdraw tirakuna.
- **[[co-laboring]]** — method named against hierarchical “collaboration.”

## Intellectual Genealogy

Affiliations (as of *Earth Beings*): [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]] (partial connections); [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] (controlled equivocation; perspectival anthropology); Isabelle Stengers (cosmopolitics; divergence); Donna Haraway (“more than one, less than many”); Andeanist ethnography (Catherine Allen and others); political theory (Rancière, Clastres) reworked against purely modern frames. Earlier Cuzco race/culture work (*Indigenous Mestizos*) not yet mapped from primary text in this vault.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(To densify from reception literature when ingested.)* Standard risks of ontological-turn ethnography apply as external positions: romanticization of alterity; difficulty of political translation when earth-beings cannot enter liberal law as subjects; dependence on co-labor mediation. De la Cadena herself centers translation’s gaps and refuses both dismissal and celebration of “belief.”
