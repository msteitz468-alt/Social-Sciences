# Harriet Martineau

## Overview

Harriet Martineau (1802–1876) was an English writer, political economist, and early methodologist of social observation. In *[[martineau-how-to-observe-1838|How to Observe Morals and Manners]]* (1838) she argued that the observer of men and manners needs the same trained preparation as any physical-science student, and she codified philosophical, moral, and mechanical requisites for that work — including the priority rule [[things-before-discourse]] and the five observational departments (Religion, General Moral Notions, Domestic State, Idea of Liberty, Progress). She is also the English publicizer and condenser of [[comte-auguste|Auguste Comte]]'s *Cours* ([[comte-positive-philosophy-martineau-1853|Martineau, *Positive Philosophy*, 1853]]). This page is drawn primarily from the 1838 method treatise at first dedicated Martineau ingest; biography beyond that source and the Comte condensation remains thin.

## Contributions

- **[[social-observation|Systematic social observation]]** — Treated "Morals and Manners" as a domain requiring aims, principles, trained **sympathy**, and mechanical aids (query lists, journal, note-book), not casual tourist judgment (*How to Observe*, Parts I and III).
- **[[things-before-discourse]]** — Begin with **Things** (institutions and records); use the **discourse of persons** as commentary (Part II intro).
- **Happiness criterion and anti–Moral Sense** — Relative amount of human happiness as the broad evaluative test; conscience grows from influences, not an innate universal Moral Sense (Part I, Phil. Req.).
- **Circumstances form prevalent virtue and vice** — National character shaped by "gigantic" non-individual circumstances; do not bitter-censure individuals for participating in prevalent faults (Part I, Sec. IV).
- **Five observational departments** plus [[licentious-ascetic-moderate-religion|religion typology]], liberty indicators (police, press, education, persecution for opinion), progress via fraternity/charity hierarchy, and [[suicide]] as religious-sentiment indicator.
- **Comte transmission** — Later condensation of the *Positive Philosophy* (1853); chronological reverse of the 1838 method book, which precedes her work on Comte.
