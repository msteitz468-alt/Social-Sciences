# Robert R. Alford

## Overview

Robert R. Alford, with [[friedland-roger]], authored ch. 10 of *The New Institutionalism
in Organizational Analysis* (1991), "Bringing Society Back In: Symbols, Practices, and
Institutional Contradictions." The pair's earlier collaboration, "Powers of Theory"
(Alford and Friedland 1985), is cited within the chapter as the source of its
three-levels-of-analysis (pluralist/managerial/class) schema, and Alford and Friedland
(1985) is separately cited elsewhere in the volume (Scott and Meyer, ch. 5) for labeling
the view of society as functionally differentiated, vertically connected sectors
converging on the nation-state the "managerial" worldview.

## Contributions

The full theoretical content of ch. 10 — the definition of [[institutional-logics]], the
five central institutional orders and their logics, the institutional-contradictions
argument, and the critical engagement with Skocpol, Foucault, Douglas, Granovetter, and
Geertz — is documented on [[friedland-roger]], the joint chapter's fuller entry. The
ingested source does not distinguish Alford's individual contribution from Friedland's
within the co-authored text.

## Intellectual Genealogy

See [[friedland-roger]] for the chapter's genealogy. The "managerial worldview" label
(Alford and Friedland 1985), applied in Scott and Meyer's ch. 5 to describe the
nation-state-centered account of societal sectors, is the one piece of the pair's prior
work independently cited by another chapter of this volume.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

See [[friedland-roger]] for the documented engagement with [[dimaggio-paul]] and
[[powell-walter-w]]'s work and the chapter's use by DiMaggio and Powell (ch. 1) as an
interpretive frame for the volume's case-study chapters.
