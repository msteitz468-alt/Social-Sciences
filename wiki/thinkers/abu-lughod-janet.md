# Janet L. Abu-Lughod

Janet Lippman Abu-Lughod (1928–2013) was an American urban and historical sociologist. Seeded from "Continuing American Provincialism and the Rest of the World" in *[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|MWS in the Longue Durée]]* (2004).

## Overview

Abu-Lughod periodizes postwar US scholarly engagement with "the rest of the world" (developmentalism → area studies → dependency → globalization "babble") and praises Wallerstein 1974 while criticizing residual Eurocentrism. *Before European Hegemony* (1989) reconstructs a thirteenth-century multi-centered world system (not yet ingested).

## Contributions

- Critique of US provincialism and empty "globalization" rhetoric.
- Pre-1500 multi-hierarchy world system (thirteenth-century urban archipelago).
- Global cities research with historical depth and decentering.
