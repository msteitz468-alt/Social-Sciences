# Eduardo Bonilla-Silva

## Overview

Puerto Rican–American sociologist (b. 1962), a leading figure in the contemporary U.S. sociology of race and the scholar most associated with the concept of [[colorblind-racism|color-blind racism]]. Trained at the University of Wisconsin–Madison (adviser Charles Camic), he taught at the University of Michigan and Texas A&M before moving to Duke University; he served as president of the American Sociological Association (2018). His signature books are *[[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-2018|Racism without Racists]]* (2003; 5th ed. 2018) and *White Supremacy and Racism in the Post–Civil Rights Era* (2001).

## Contributions

- **[[racialized-social-system|Structural theory of racism]]** — In "Rethinking Racism: Toward a Structural Interpretation" (*ASR*, 1997), Bonilla-Silva recast racism as a property of a *racialized social system* rather than a matter of individual prejudice or false ideas (attributed). Societies become racialized when their economic, political, social, and ideological structures are partly organized along racial lines, allocating rewards to a dominant race.
- **[[colorblind-racism|Color-blind racism]]** — the dominant post–civil rights racial ideology, anatomized through four frames (abstract liberalism, naturalization, cultural racism, minimization of racism) and a characteristic style of racial talk (attributed).
- **[[the-new-racism|The new racism]]** — the covert, institutional, apparently nonracial regime of racial domination that replaced Jim Crow (attributed).
- **[[white-habitus]]** — the racialized socialization, born of white residential and associational isolation, that shapes whites' racial tastes and perceptions (attributed).
- **[[latin-americanization-of-race|Latin Americanization thesis]]** — a projected shift from a bi-racial to a tri-racial U.S. stratification order (attributed).

## Intellectual Genealogy

PhD, University of Wisconsin–Madison (adviser: Charles Camic). His structural theory positions itself against the dominant U.S. prejudice/attitudinal paradigm and the older [[ethnicity-paradigm|ethnicity paradigm]] (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]). It is broadly consonant with [[racial-formation-theory|racial formation theory]] ([[omi-michael|Omi]] & [[winant-howard|Winant]]) and the structural race tradition running back to [[du-bois-w-e-b|W. E. B. Du Bois]]; his concept of [[white-habitus|white habitus]] adapts [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s [[habitus]]. Chapter 10 of the 5th edition was written in collaboration with his former student Robert L. Reece.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*Racism without Racists* is widely taught and heavily cited but controversial: critics contest whether ordinary color-blind talk should be read as *racism*, and whether interview coding demonstrates ideology as strongly as Bonilla-Silva claims; his own stance is unapologetically that of an engaged, political sociologist (see [[structural-vs-attitudinal-racism-debate]]).
