# Justin L. Barrett

## Overview

Psychologist and CSR pioneer. White (2021) credits Barrett (with Lawson 2000) among the first uses of the phrase "cognitive science of religion" in print, and centers him on [[theological-correctness|theological (in)correctness]] and popularization of hypersensitive/agency detection accounts of god concepts.

## Contributions

- **[[theological-correctness]]** — discrepancy between reflective, doctrine-aligned statements and intuitive online reasoning (e.g., God as limited agent under time pressure).
- **Natural foundations of religion** (2000 *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* essay).
- Collaboration on modes summaries and cross-cultural person–body reasoning (with Cohen et al., per White).

## Intellectual Genealogy

CSR network: [[boyer-pascal]], [[whitehouse-harvey]], [[mccauley-robert-n]], [[white-claire]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

HADD/"hypersensitive" agency detection: White reports experimental work suggesting people detect agents in ambiguous situations but that threat does not intensify detection — so "hypersensitive" may be unwarranted (method table, ch. 4). Theological-correctness findings remain a core CSR demonstration of dual-process religious cognition.
