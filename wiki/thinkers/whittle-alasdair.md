# Alasdair Whittle

British prehistorian (School of History and Archaeology, University of Wales, Cardiff, as of the 1996 imprint), specializing in the **European Neolithic** — social values, settlement, monuments, and long-term process from forager to farmer worlds.

## Overview

Whittle’s *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996|Europe in the Neolithic: The Creation of New Worlds]]* (1996) is the vault’s first full ingest of his continental synthesis: a Critical-Turn / early Contemporary reframing of Neolithic Europe that privileges **indigenous forager continuity**, **slow settling-down**, and **sacred/ancestral ideology** over demic wave-of-advance and surplus-first models. Named manuscript readers include [[bradley-richard|Richard Bradley]] and Douglass Bailey; the book succeeds his own *Neolithic Europe: a survey* (1985) as a complete rewrite for Cambridge World Archaeology.

## Contributions

*(Attributed from *[[whittle-europe-in-the-neolithic-1996]]* 1996 unless noted.)*

### Program claims

- **Indigenous Neolithic histories:** In nearly all areas, largely indigenous people create Neolithic ways of life (Preface; Ch. 10).
- **Forager values prolonged:** Sharing, cooperation, solidarity, mutuality, honour — grounded in Mesolithic ethics; community continually re-created by gatherings and materiality.
- **Piecemeal becoming Neolithic:** Farming and sedentism gradual; not instant mixed farmers; many Neolithics, not one package event.
- **Sacred and ancestral “new worlds”:** Spirits, ancestors, descent, origins, and time structure a conceptual order distinct from undivided forager cosmos, even while social ethic continues.
- **Against hierarchy-first readings:** Little established within-group difference; Varna gold/cenotaphs and Mediterranean copper/idols read toward ancestral veneration, identity, fertility/regeneration rather than simple elite monopoly (Ch. 4, 9).

### Method stance

Calendrical chronology from calibrated radiocarbon and tree-rings where available; interpretation obligatory — the book is “a prolonged conditional sentence”; selection over encyclopaedia (Preface). Explicitly flags DNA as future research for LBK origins (**pre-aDNA** horizon).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Cardiff / British Neolithic research; dialogue with [[bradley-richard]], Julian Thomas, Marek Zvelebil, [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]] (rival demic/language packages he rejects as necessary), [[sherratt-andrew|Andrew Sherratt]] (secondary products as acceleration he reweights), and continental excavators listed in the 1996 acknowledgements. On [[farming-spread-europe-debate]] he densifies **Position B** (indigenous adoption) against Position A demic orthodoxy; overlaps hybrid regional partitioning for limited colonisation special cases only.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Mid-1990s calibrated ¹⁴C framework; **pre-genome-wide aDNA**. Later demography (post-2010 EEF) reopened demic strength especially for SE→central European early farmers — treat 1996 indigenous LBK and basin-wide continuity claims as **dated positions**, not silent overwrite. Sacred/ancestral and slow-becoming arguments remain influential in British social archaeology of the Neolithic independent of demographic revision. Hierarchy-downplaying readings of Varna and Millaran enclosures compete with stratification models still live in the field.
