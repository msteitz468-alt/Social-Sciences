# Michael E. Moseley

## Overview

American Andean archaeologist whose synthesis *The Incas and Their Ancestors* ([[moseley-incas-and-their-ancestors-2001|Moseley 2001 rev.]]) organizes Central Andean prehistory around ecological adaptations, climate shocks (Quelccaya ice cores), and an Inca-derived model of statecraft (ayllu, mit’a, dual organization, huaca cult, corporate style). Best known for the **[[maritime-foundations-hypothesis|Maritime Foundations of Andean Civilization]]** argument: that exceptional marine productivity on the arid Peruvian coast supported early sedentary life and monumental construction **before** intensive irrigation agriculture became the caloric base (1975 book; restated 2001).

## Contributions

- **Maritime Foundations hypothesis** — coastal fisherfolk and Maritime-Oasis economies as organizational base for Preceramic monumental centers, challenging agriculture-first models (attributed; see theory page).
- **Adaptive dualism** — sustained contrast of **Arid Montane** and **Maritime-Oasis** trajectories as parallel developmental paths culminating (2001 framing) in highland empires vs Chimor/coastal states before Inca unification; drought tips demographic balance toward the sierra (as of Moseley 2001).
- **Inca-as-model** — reading ethnohistorically documented institutions ([[ayllu]], [[mita-labor|mit’a]], [[mitmaq]], [[dual-organization]], [[huaca]], [[vertical-archipelago|verticality]]) backward as interpretive scaffold for earlier archaeological societies (methodological commitment of the 2001 book; attributed).
- **Intensive vs extensive states** — Middle Horizon typology: intensive polities (e.g. coastal Moche) submerge local populations under state ideology; extensive polities plant isolated nodes among local populations (e.g. Tiwanaku northern Titicaca; debated Huari) (as of Moseley 2001).
- **Climate–culture narrative** — AD 562–94 drought and ~AD 1100 multi-century dry phase as triggers for Middle Horizon turnover, Tiwanaku ridge-field collapse, LIP pukaras, and Chimor hydraulic crisis (attributed synthesis; not sole environmental causation in wiki voice).
- **Chan Chan and Chimor** — major synthesizer of *ciudadela*, craft demography, audiencia, and inter-valley canal failure narratives; dual-trajectory climax Chimor vs Incas.
- **Epilogue politics of heritage** — Anthropocene framing; verticality unrecognized by colonial/modern states; “Andean civilization is the orphan of the ancient world”; critique of “sustainable” agrarian development myths (advocacy positions, as of Moseley 2001).

## The Maritime Foundations argument (1975)

*(From primary ingest of [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|MFAC 1975]]; study-act analysis at [[moseley-maritime-foundations-study]].)*

- **Preadaptation to irrigation** — the plants, work force, and mobilizing institutions needed to open the desert each existed before agricultural dependence, so irrigation and its managing authority developed unusually fast (attributed).
- **Corporate authority precedes irrigation** — managerial authority arose from voluntary maritime cooperation, then took control of irrigation, inverting the hydraulic model in which irrigation forces villages to surrender autonomy (attributed).
- **Demographic deviation model** — a positive-feedback account of population-driven autogenous subsistence change (lomas foraging → fishing → farming), advanced against Lanning's lomas-desiccation "prime mover" and against "Protestant-ethic" more-food-for-less-labor explanations (attributed).
- **Industrial vs food cultigens** — cotton and gourd (for nets and floats), not food crops, drove Preceramic cultivation (attributed).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Ph.D. Harvard 1968; trained under [[willey-gordon-r|Gordon R. Willey]], with whom he co-authored the pivotal 1971 [[aspero|Aspero]] restudy; entered central-coast fieldwork through [[patterson-thomas|Thomas Patterson]] and built on (while disputing) [[lanning-edward|Edward Lanning's]] synthesis. Drew on the coastal preceramic record of [[bird-junius|Junius Bird]] and [[engel-frederic|Frédéric Engel]]. Later dialogue with ethnohistory (Murra's verticality/archipelago; kuraka/ayllu literature) and with Chan Chan fieldwork; cites and synthesizes Lumbreras, Isbell, Shimada, Goldstein, Donnan, Bauer, Silverman, and others.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Maritime-foundations claim contested by Norte Chico (Caral/Supe) and broader scholarship on early cultivation/irrigation—record positions on [[maritime-foundations-hypothesis]].
- Inca-model projection risks ethnographic present on EIP/Middle Horizon societies.
- Huari as religion vs empire vs Moseley’s middle ground remains an open field debate.
- Climate-first collapse narratives require multi-factor checks (politics, revolt hypotheses at Tiwanaku and Batán Grande torchings).
- Post-2001 chronology, population-genetic studies, and site reports may supersede 2001 phase dates—date-stamp aggressively.
