# Iris Marion Young

## Overview

Iris Marion Young (1949–2006) was an American political philosopher and feminist theorist. This page is grounded primarily in *[[young-justice-politics-difference-1990|Justice and the Politics of Difference]]* (1990; 2011 reissue with Allen foreword).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Young 1990.)*

- **Displaces the [[distributive-paradigm|distributive paradigm]]** of justice (allocations of material goods) toward decision-making, division of labor, and culture as structural conditions of justice.
- **[[five-faces-of-oppression|Five faces of oppression]]:** exploitation, marginalization, powerlessness, cultural imperialism, violence — criteria for social groups under oppression.
- **[[politics-of-difference]]:** emancipation through affirming group specificity rather than assimilation to a homogeneous public; heterogeneous public and group representation.
- **City life** as a normative ideal of togetherness-in-difference (vs fusion community ideal).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Critical theory, feminist theory, and social-movement practice (US new social movements of the 1970s–80s as empirical horizon). Interlocutors include Rawlsian distributive justice, civic-public impartiality ideals, and community-communitarian fusion ideals (as foils in the book).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Positions — densify from secondary sources.)* Related to but not identical with the Fraser–Honneth [[redistribution-recognition-debate]] (Fraser cites Young among anti-dualist interlocutors in the 2003 exchange). Critiques of group representation design; liberal defenses of impartiality and merit.

## Historiography

Danielle Allen’s 2011 foreword stresses prescience of the urban ideal and justice as conditions for nondomination and nonoppression (human flourishing).
