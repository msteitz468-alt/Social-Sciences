# David Graeber (1961–2020)

American anthropologist, anarchist theorist, and public intellectual. Known for value theory, debt as social relation, bureaucracy critique, and — with [[wengrow-david|David Wengrow]] — the deep-time political synthesis *[[graeber-wengrow-dawn-of-everything-2021|The Dawn of Everything]]* (2021, posthumous). Primary ingest so far is limited to *Dawn*; earlier monographs (*Debt*, *Fragments*, Madagascar ethnography) are not yet in the collection — treat career phases other than *Dawn* as stub-level.

## Overview

Graeber trained and taught in cultural/political anthropology (Yale; later LSE). He combined ethnographic and historical comparison with an explicit anarchist political project: recovering evidence of non-state and anti-hierarchical social possibilities. *Dawn* (with Wengrow) extends that project into Upper Palaeolithic through early urban archaeology, arguing (attributed) against Rousseau/Hobbes "origins of inequality" frames and stage-evolutionary big history.

## Contributions (from *Dawn of Everything*, attributed joint G&W unless noted)

- **[[indigenous-critique]]** — reconstruction of 17th–18th c. Indigenous (esp. Wendat/Kandiaronk) critique of European society as a source for Enlightenment equality/freedom discourse, later inverted into the myth of progress.
- **[[three-basic-freedoms]]** — freedom to move away, to disobey, to reshape social relations — as comparative baseline lost or constrained under modern states.
- **[[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]]** — control of violence, control of knowledge/information, charismatic politics — combinable into "the state" but historically separable.
- **Seasonal / protean politics** — foragers and early societies shifting hierarchical and egalitarian arrangements seasonally rather than occupying fixed evolutionary stages.
- **[[schismogenesis]]** (via Bateson) — mutual differentiation of neighboring societies (e.g. NW Coast vs California).
- **[[play-farming]] / Gardens of Adonis** — cultivation maintained in ritual/play registers long before (or instead of) full agrarian dependence.
