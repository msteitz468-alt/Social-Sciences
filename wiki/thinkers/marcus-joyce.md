# Joyce Marcus

## Overview

Joyce Marcus (b. 1948) is an American archaeologist and epigrapher (University of
Michigan), a specialist in Mesoamerican — especially **Zapotec** and **Maya** —
civilizations and a long-term collaborator of **[[flannery-kent-v|Kent V. Flannery]]**.
Her work joins settlement-pattern archaeology, epigraphy, and ethnohistory, and — with
Flannery — helped establish **[[cognitive-archaeology]]**, the study of ancient belief
and cosmology by processual means.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Flannery and Marcus 2012 and their earlier Oaxaca work.)* With Flannery,
Marcus reconstructed **Zapotec cosmology** from 16th-century Spanish eyewitness accounts
and combined it with subsistence and settlement data in Formative Oaxaca, demonstrating
that ancient religion could be studied with the explanatory ambitions of processual
archaeology. In *[[flannery-marcus-creation-inequality-2012|The Creation of Inequality]]*
(2012) she and Flannery advance a general comparative argument that hereditary
inequality is **created** by human agents manipulating pre-existing social logic —
kinship, ancestor ritual, debt, and prestige competition — rather than being an
automatic consequence of agriculture or surplus, and that societies repeatedly resist
and reverse it.

## Intellectual Genealogy

No teacher/student relationships are documented in the material ingested here. Marcus is
part of the Michigan processual milieu and is most closely associated intellectually
with her collaborator [[flannery-kent-v|Kent Flannery]]; their joint work bridges
subsistence-settlement processualism and [[cognitive-archaeology|cognitive archaeology]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Not documented in the material ingested here. The comparative argument of *The Creation
of Inequality* is contested where it bears on the origins-of-inequality and
origin-of-the-state debates (see [[origins-of-hereditary-inequality-debate]]); those
disputes concern the joint Flannery–Marcus program rather than Marcus specifically.
