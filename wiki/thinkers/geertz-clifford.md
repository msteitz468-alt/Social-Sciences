# Clifford Geertz (1926–2006)

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/thinkers/geertz-clifford|Thinkers Hub]]

American cultural anthropologist. Primary sources now ingested: *[[geertz-religion-of-java-1960|The Religion of Java]]* (1960), *[[geertz-agricultural-involution-1963|Agricultural Involution]]* (1963), *[[geertz-peddlers-princes-1963|Peddlers and Princes]]* (1963), *[[geertz-interpretation-of-cultures-1973|The Interpretation of Cultures]]* (1973), *[[geertz-negara-1980|Negara: The Theatre State in Nineteenth-Century Bali]]* (1980), and *[[geertz-local-knowledge-1983|Local Knowledge]]* (1983). The 1973 collection is the programmatic statement of [[interpretive-anthropology]] ([[thick-description]], [[webs-of-significance]], [[models-of-and-for]], *[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]*); *Negara* densifies the program into a full polity model ([[theatre-state]], [[exemplary-center]]); the 1983 sequel extends into new "cultural systems" ([[common-sense]], [[art-as-a-cultural-system|art]], law) and the "[[blurred-genres|refiguration of social thought]]".

## Overview

Geertz’s early career is Indonesia-centered team ethnography and ecological-historical synthesis; his later career is the leading figure of **interpretive / symbolic anthropology**. Continuity across phases: refusal of single-label characterizations of complex societies; attention to meaning, etiquette, and art; civilization-scale claims built from local materials. The 1960 religion monograph maps three emic variants ([[abangan]], [[santri]], [[prijaji]]) around the [[slametan]]; the 1963 involution study applies [[cultural-ecology]] to explain Java’s labor-absorbing wet-rice path under Dutch colonialism; the same year’s *Peddlers and Princes* compares bazaar and aristocratic routes to firm-type enterprise in Modjokuto and Tabanan.

## Contributions

- **Javanese religious typology** — abangan / santri / prijaji as structured internal diversity of a Muslim-majority civilization (*[[geertz-religion-of-java-study|Religion of Java]]*).
- **Slametan as core ritual** — territorial communal feast binding neighbors and spirits.
- **[[agricultural-involution]]** — labor-absorbing sawah intensification without take-off; sugar–sawah “centaur”; shared poverty (*[[geertz-agricultural-involution-study|Agricultural Involution]]*).
- **[[bazaar-type-economy]] / [[firm-type-economy]]** — organizational contrast of *pasar* vs *toko*/*perusahaan*; peddler vs prince entrepreneurial paths; Janus-faced pretake-off entrepreneur (*[[geertz-peddlers-princes-study|Peddlers and Princes]]*; comparison [[modjokuto-tabanan-economic-modernization]]).
- **[[interpretive-anthropology]]** (*IoC* 1973) — [[thick-description]]; [[webs-of-significance]]; [[models-of-and-for]]; culture as context not power.
- **[[deep-play]]** — *[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]* cockfight as text.
- **[[theatre-state]] / [[exemplary-center]] / [[sinking-status]] / [[negara]]** — *[[geertz-negara-study|Negara]]* (1980): classical Balinese court as spectacle-centered polity; poetics of power (“make inequality enchant”); hub [[hubs/studies/geertz-negara-study]].
- **The [[blurred-genres|refiguration of social thought]]** (*Local Knowledge* 1983) — genre-blurring and the turn from "laws and instances" to "cases and interpretations"; the game/drama/text analogies.
- **[[experience-near-experience-distant]]** — understanding via symbolic forms, not empathy ("From the Native's Point of View"); *lek* as stage fright; the Moroccan *nisba*.
- **Cultural-systems essays** — [[common-sense]] and [[art-as-a-cultural-system|art]] as cultural systems; charisma as nearness to the active center ([[charisma]]).
- **[[local-knowledge]]** — law as "legal sensibility" / local knowledge; *haqq*/*dharma*/*adat* compared (foundational for [[legal-anthropology]]).
- **Reflexive late work** — *Works and Lives* on ethnography as authored text.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained at Harvard DSR under [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]]; field formation in MIT Modjokuto Project (with Hildred Geertz and team). Ecological debt to [[steward-julian-h|Steward]] and Conklin in the involution book. Chicago New Nations committee; from 1970 Institute for Advanced Study, Princeton. Vault critics/appraisers: Swidler (toolkit foil); Sewell (historicizing repair of synchronic cultural analysis). Ran the **Sefrou (Morocco) research project** into which his Chicago student [[rabinow-paul|Paul Rabinow]] was recruited; Rabinow's *[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977|Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco]]* (1977) took Geertzian [[webs-of-significance|webs of significance]] and turned them reflexively onto the fieldwork encounter (see [[reflexive-fieldwork]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

**Typology and involution** reanalyzed-contested in area studies (see study pages). **Interpretive program** (secondhand): critics charge overstated cultural coherence and weak causal links to action (Swidler); Sewell urges keeping symbols while restoring diachrony and internal difference. Writing Culture turned textual sensitivity onto ethnographic authority.

**Sewell’s appraisal (attributed).** Calls Geertz “the ambassador from anthropology”; rejects discarding him as idealist; objects to synchronic gaze; proposes exploiting models-of/models-for disjunction for cultural change.

**Rosaldo's critique (attributed).** In *[[rosaldo-culture-truth-1993|Culture and Truth]]* (1989) [[rosaldo-renato|Renato Rosaldo]] both credited Geertz's "refiguration of social thought" and faulted his early work for "thick descriptions and thin conclusions" — case histories rich in conflict (the 1912 Morocco/Cohen episode) reduced in the conclusion to "systematic misunderstanding," and culture defined as "a set of control mechanisms." He read Geertz's "Thinking as a Moral Act" as saturated with a Weberian "passionate detachment" that foregrounds power at the expense of cultural meaning.

**Marcus on complicity (attributed).** In "The Uses of Complicity" (1997; *[[marcus-ethnography-thick-and-thin-1998|Ethnography Through Thick and Thin]]*, primary-ingested), [[marcus-george-e|Marcus]] rereads the cockfight "fable of rapport" (fieldwork 1958) as the source case of [[complicity]] inside rapport: Geertz's "anthropological irony" concedes the mutually self-interested fiction beneath rapport, but Geertz "pulled back" from its reflexive implications — read by Marcus not as ambivalence but as "commitment to the frame of reference in which anthropology could be done." Marcus also credits "Deep Play" as the genre-model whose story-opening rhetoric became a "now perhaps, dully repetitive" disciplinary strategy.

**Asad's critique of the religion definition (attributed).** In *[[asad-genealogies-of-religion-1993|Genealogies of Religion]]* (1993, ch. 1), [[asad-talal|Asad]] takes "Religion as a Cultural System" (1966) as the emblematic universal definition of [[religion]] and argues it fails both internally and historically: "symbol" is used inconsistently (sometimes an aspect of reality, sometimes of its representation); the "intrinsic double aspect" of culture patterns imports a Parsonian dualism that "creates a logical space for defining the essence of religion"; belief-as-prior-mental-state reflects a privatized modern Christian conception (against the medieval record in which power and *disciplina* implanted dispositions — Augustine's "Let constraint be found outside; it is inside that the will is born"); the claim that ritual generates conviction is internally inconsistent; religion "is indeed now optional in a way that science is not," collapsing the perspectivist symmetry; and the "modest" disorder-orientation view "comes to resemble the conception Marx had of religion as ideology." Asad also critiques Geertz's Indonesia "end of ideology" essay: modern liberal rights-language is "profoundly moralistic," not post-ideological (1993, ch. 6). See [[definition-of-religion-debate]].

## Clifford's reading (*The Predicament of Culture*, 1988)

*(Attributed — James Clifford, no relation, in [[clifford-predicament-of-culture-1988|The Predicament of Culture]], Ch. 1 "On Ethnographic Authority.")* Clifford treats "Deep Play: Notes on the Balinese Cockfight" (1972/1973) as the paradigm case of **interpretive authority** — one of four modes of [[ethnographic-authority]] he identifies (experiential, interpretive, dialogical, polyphonic). Following Ricoeur's "model of the text," Geertz's textualization converts Balinese behavior into a readable corpus and generates a "generalized author" ("the Balinese") standing in for the culture's meaning; the essay's opening "fable of rapport" — the police-raid anecdote establishing Geertz's shift from alienated "nonperson" to complicit insider — Clifford reads as a rhetorical device authorizing subsequent interpretive omniscience. Clifford also quotes a 1968 Geertz passage on fieldwork's necessary reliance on the productive "fiction" (not falsehood) of a shared moral community — "fiction, not falsehood, that lies at the very heart of successful anthropological field research," rendering fieldwork "continuously ironic" — and treats it as marking "the beginning of the end of innocence in fieldwork." See [[interpretive-anthropology]].
