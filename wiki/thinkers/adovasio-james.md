# James M. Adovasio

North American archaeologist best known for long-term excavation of [[meadowcroft|Meadowcroft Rockshelter]] (Pennsylvania) and for defending its pre-Clovis radiocarbon sequence against contamination critiques. Densified from [[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|Meltzer 2009]] (radiocarbon BP as Meltzer uses); secondary layers Mithen 2003.

## Overview

Adovasio is a principal figure in the pre-Clovis controversy: Meadowcroft’s deep dates, if accepted, place people in eastern North America millennia before [[clovis-culture|Clovis]]. Meltzer treats excavation quality as conceded even by critics, while final comprehensive reporting and ¹⁴C contamination disputes left the site in “archaeological limbo” as of 2009. Also contributes perishables expertise (nets, textiles) to Clovis-age technology discussions.

## Contributions

### Meadowcroft Rockshelter (Meltzer 2009)

- Excavated 1973–~1978 with interdisciplinary team; **meticulous techniques** (critics concede superb work); 4.6 m sediment, 11 natural strata; **52 ¹⁴C dates** from 31,400 BP to Historic, nearly all in stratigraphic order (4 minor Holocene flip-flops) (C-3-061).
- Middle Stratum IIa bracketed 11,300–12,800 BP with unfluted **Miller lanceolate** point; lower IIa six dates **12,800–16,175 BP** with ~700 genuine artifacts (bone punch, debitage, small blades, uni/bifacial knives, gravers) of exotic stone (NY, OH, WV, PA). Adovasio’s **conservative average human presence ~14,250 BP** (C-3-062). Miller as fine fluted-point precursor (C-6-007).
- Paleoenvironmental anomaly debate: skeptics expected near-glacial taxa ~14,250 BP (Laurentide within ~150 km) yet sample shows temperate deciduous taxa — sample tiny (278 bones lower IIa, 11 identifiable; 11.9 g plants from glacial levels of 1.4 M fragments); Adovasio team cites low elevation/south aspect; Meltzer: hopelessly inadequate basis to judge anomaly (C-3-063).
- **Haynes coal-contamination critique:** Adovasio — nearest coal 800 m, no coal particles/spores, multi-lab consistency and stratigraphic order, groundwater never within 10 m of deepest occupation; Goldberg microstratigraphy no saturation/coal. Haynes dismissed without explanation and demanded short-lived seeds/nuts dates Adovasio refused as lose-lose (C-3-064).
- Despite >100 publications, lack of comprehensive final report (all data, artifact–date–stratum relations) keeps Meadowcroft in limbo as of Meltzer 2009; Adovasio compared controversy to Trenton (C-3-065).

### Monte Verde visit sociology and Pedra Furada

- January 1997 Monte Verde visit participant orbit; Dallas Museum La Caverna forced vote aftermath: Stanford misspoke toasting Meadowcroft; [[haynes-c-vance|Haynes]] asked for one more seed date; Adovasio exploded, refused endless spiral of tests, stormed out — “Meadowcroft’s antiquity was settled as far as most other professionals and I were concerned” (C-3-079).
- Dec 1993 Pedra Furada site visit with Meltzer and [[dillehay-tom|Dillehay]] — free-fall geofact critique of older phase (C-3-067).

### Perishables and Clovis-age fiber technology

Meltzer reports Adovasio on Clovis-age twined bags, mats, burden baskets, trays mostly from Great Basin dry caves (e.g. Danger Cave); no Clovis nets yet found but Adovasio expects them — net hunting deep-rooted, low expertise, nonconfrontational, can kill large animals without great strength, routinely involving “females, juveniles, and the old” (C-6-013).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Interlocutor of Haynes (dating dispute), Dillehay/Meltzer (site visits), broader pre-Clovis community. Fieldwork centered at Meadowcroft.
