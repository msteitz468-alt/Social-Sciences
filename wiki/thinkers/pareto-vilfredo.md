# Vilfredo Pareto

## Overview

Vilfredo Federico Damaso Pareto (1848–1923), Italian engineer, mathematical economist, and sociologist, is the author of the *Trattato di Sociologia generale* (1916; 2nd ed. 1923), known in English as *[[pareto-mind-and-society-1935|The Mind and Society]]* (Livingston ed., 1935). In that work he proposed a [[logico-experimental-method|logico-experimental]] sociology of [[non-logical-conduct]], [[residues]] and [[derivations]], [[paretian-social-equilibrium|social equilibrium]], and the [[circulation-of-elites]]. proposed: [[paretian-sociology]] · proposed: [[circulation-of-elites]]

He did not do ethnographic fieldwork; the *Trattato* rests on comparative historical illustration (chiefly Europe and the Mediterranean basin, §2065) and on a prior career in pure economics.

## Contributions

Drawn from *The Mind and Society* (1935 English of the 1923 Italian) unless noted:

- **[[logico-experimental-method]]** — Pareto argued for a sociology guided only by experience and observation, contingent within known time/space/experience, using definitions as mere labels and successive approximations rather than the certain or absolute (*Trattato* Ch I).
- **[[non-logical-conduct]]** — He defined logical actions as means–ends conjunctions that hold both subjectively and objectively, and non-logical actions as the rest (not “illogical”); he held non-logical conduct plays a large social part (Ch II).
- **[[residues]] / [[derivations]]** — He analysed non-logico-experimental theories into a relatively constant element *a* (residues: manifestations of sentiments) and a variable element *b* (derivations: reasonings that justify *a*), with derivatives *c* (Ch V–X).
- **Six residue classes** — especially [[combination-instinct|Class I (combinations)]] and [[group-persistence|Class II (group-persistences)]], plus activity, sociality, integrity, and sex (Ch VI–VIII).
- **[[truth-utility-distinction]]** — He insisted logico-experimental “truth” of a doctrine and its social utility are distinct and may conflict.
- **[[paretian-social-equilibrium]]** — Society as an interdependent “social system” whose form reacts with its elements; equilibrium defined by restorative reaction to artificial modification (Ch XII).
- **[[circulation-of-elites]]**, **[[lions-and-foxes]]**, **[[speculators-and-rentiers]]**, **[[demagogic-plutocracy]]** — Ruling-class composition by Class I/II residue mix; force vs cunning; economic types S/R; modern “government by speculators” (Ch XII–XIII).

Earlier works (*Cours*, *Manuale*/*Manuel*, *Systèmes socialistes*) founded his reputation in mathematical economics and elite/socialist critique; they are not yet ingested as primary text in this wiki.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Training:** Turin Polytechnic (engineering; dissertation on equilibrium index functions of solid bodies); railway and mining engineering; free-trade politics in Italy; Florence intellectual circle; succession to Walras’s chair of political economy at Lausanne (1894) via Pantaleoni’s introduction (Livingston biographical note, 1935).
- **Relation to [[comte-auguste]] / [[positivism]]:** Pareto rejected dogmatic Comtean “positive” sociology as “as dogmatic as Bossuet’s *Discourse on Universal History*” — “two different religions” — and claimed a purely experimental sociology “after the fashion of chemistry, physics” (§6). Shared demand for scientific sociology; rejected encyclopedic dogma and humanitarian metaphysics.
- **Relation to [[spencer-herbert]]:** Same rejection of dogmatic evolutionary sociologies as religions (§6); later critiques Spencerian origin-stories as unproven residue-hunts (§§885–886).
- **reacted_against:** Assumption that important social conduct is logical; identification of experimental truth with social utility; theologies, metaphysics, and ethical systems treated as science; pure economic determinism and social Darwinism as pseudo-sciences (Ch V).
- **Economics → sociology:** Engineering and pure-economics equilibrium language migrated into the social-system model of Ch XII; he claimed to have dropped free-trader “cloak” when dealing with science (*Manuale* self-reference, §90 n.).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Method:** Residue classification is indefinite by design (Pareto’s own claim, §§870–874); critics treat it as unfalsifiable taxonomy.
- **Politics:** Livingston attributed to Pareto “utter contempt for plutocratic democracy” and a March-on-Rome “I told you so” (1935 biographical note — POSITION of the editor, not wiki-voice character judgment). Association with Italian fascism is a contested afterlife (not adjudicated here from the *Trattato* alone).
- **Sampling:** Self-confinement to Europe/Mediterranean (§2065); historical illustrations (Rome, Athens, modern Italy/France) are not systematic comparative samples.
- **Sex/race/colonial material:** Class VI and race language in the text are attributed 1916-era discourse; not findings about peoples.
- **Reception:** American “Pareto vogue” of the early 1930s (Henderson seminar, DeVoto, Canby) — Livingston’s priority claims are reception historiography, not settled priority.
