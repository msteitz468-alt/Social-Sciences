# Merlin Donald

## Overview

Merlin Donald is a Canadian psychologist and cognitive scientist whose *Origins of the Modern Mind* (1991) proposed a staged evolutionary model of culture and cognition — **episodic → mimetic → mythic → theoretic** — widely cited in [[cognitive-archaeology]]. In this wiki he enters primarily as an **interlocutor of archaeologists** ([[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]], [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]]), not yet via primary ingest of his monographs. The densest Renfrew-facing treatment is *[[renfrew-figuring-it-out-2003|Figuring It Out]]* (2003).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — as summarized and revised in Renfrew 2003.)*

| Phase (Donald) | Characteristic (as Renfrew states) | Associated forms / species anchors Renfrew uses |
|---|---|---|
| **Episodic culture** | Primate cognition; event-bound awareness | e.g. *Australopithecus* |
| **Mimetic culture** | Non-verbal modelling; tool handling & imitation | *Homo erectus*; handaxes; possible teaching without fully modern speech (Renfrew cites David L. Clarke) |
| **Linguistic / mythic culture** | Fully modern speech; narrative, myth | Early *Homo sapiens*; UP cave art as narrative foci |
| **Theoretic culture** | **[[external-symbolic-storage]]**, esp. writing | Early Near Eastern/Egyptian writing; Greek alphabetic literacy; maps, diagrams, weights |

Donald treats writing as the prime device of ESS enabling theoretic culture: information stored outside the body/brain, with retention beyond an individual's life.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Psychology / cognitive science → archaeological uptake in 1990s–2000s cognitive archaeology. Reciprocal links: [[renfrew-colin]], [[mithen-steven]], [[cognitive-archaeology]], [[cognitive-fluidity]].

### Renfrew's revision (*Figuring It Out*, attributed)

Renfrew accepts the broad architecture but argues Donald "overlooked a crucial point": the gap between UP **mythic** hunter-gatherer culture and urban **theoretic** culture. He inserts **material-symbolic** (table: "Symbolic-material") **culture** after mythic and before theoretic:

- Triggered by the **sedentary revolution** and denser **[[material-engagement]]**.
- Already uses ESS in non-written form (monuments, valuables, alignments, power symbols).
- Most of houses, deities, ranking, commodities, and exchange achieved **without writing**.
- Only later does writing (esp. alphabetic) permit cultures Renfrew calls theoretic (Greeks; Chinese from Qin; later Near Eastern states).

Revised sequence (Renfrew's table, compressed; date anchors are **his earliest-known heuristics**, multi-origin for later transitions): episodic → mimetic → mythic → **symbolic-material** → theoretic. Stages are **inclusive** of predecessors (literate societies still live largely by material-symbolic systems).

### Mithen's relation

Mithen treats Donald as a rival periodization of transitions he explains via **[[cognitive-fluidity]]** (modularity → cross-domain fluidity), reversing psychologist-led use of archaeology. See Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/cognitive-fluidity]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Stage models risk unilinear progress narratives; Renfrew softens this with multiple trajectories for transitions 3–4.
- Species–stage mappings and absolute dates are contested.
- Oral cultures complicate theoretic = literacy.
- Renfrew's material-symbolic insertion and Mithen's modularity model are **positions**, not wiki-voice replacements of Donald.

## Renfrew *Prehistory* (2007) reception

*(Attributed — [[renfrew-colin]], *[[renfrew-prehistory-making-human-mind-2007]]*, 2007.)* Same revision architecture as *Figuring It Out* (2003): Donald's episodic → mimetic → mythic → theoretic sequence accepted, with **material-symbolic** culture and pre-writing [[external-symbolic-storage]] inserted before full theoretic literacy. Used to organize the [[tectonic-phase]] account of mind after the [[sapient-paradox]].
