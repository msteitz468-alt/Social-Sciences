# Ian Shapiro

Political theorist (b. 1956), based at Yale University, known within this wiki as
co-author, with [[green-donald|Donald P. Green]], of
*[[green-shapiro-pathologies-1994|Pathologies of Rational Choice Theory]]* (1994).

## Overview

Shapiro works primarily in democratic theory and the philosophy of social science.
In *Pathologies* he and Green argue that the case for a **problem-driven** rather than
a [[method-driven-research|method- or theory-driven]] political science follows from
the empirical weakness of applied rational choice work — a normative-methodological
argument that connects to Shapiro's broader defense of an empirically disciplined,
democratically relevant social science.

## Contributions

With Green, articulated the critique of universalist theoretical ambition in political
science and the case for [[method-driven-research|problem-driven inquiry]]; anchored
the [[rational-choice-controversy-debate|rational choice controversy]].
