# Marcellin Boule (1861–1942)

French paleontologist whose early-20th-century reconstruction of the La Chapelle-aux-Saints Neanderthal shaped the popular and scientific **image problem** of [[neanderthals]] — bent-kneed, brutish, pathologically primitive — later revised by mid-century reexaminations. Historiographic figure in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* ch. 1 (attributed). Also co-architect (with Henri Vallois) of the **presapiens** model that expelled Neanderthals from ancestry while keeping Europe central to modern origins.

## Overview

In 1908 Abbés Jean and Amédée Bouyssonie and Abbé Bardon discovered a nearly complete Neanderthal skeleton at La Chapelle-aux-Saints; on Abbé Breuil's advice it went to Boule at the Museum of Natural History, Paris, not to de Mortillet's succession at the École d'Anthropologie (Stringer & Gamble 1993 historiography). Boule's three monographs (*Annales de Paléontologie*, October 1911–March 1913) "set new standards for describing and reporting human fossils" and dominated human evolutionary studies up to and just beyond his death in 1942 (S&G 1993).

## Contributions

*(As of Stringer & Gamble 1993 historiography — not Boule primary texts.)*

- **La Chapelle monographs (1911–1913).** Exhaustive description that became the century's type image of the Neanderthal; classification as *Homo neanderthalensis* (separate species, following William King) largely because he assessed the skull as outside expected *H. sapiens* variation — chimpanzee / Chapelle / modern Frenchman comparison series (Boule via S&G 1993).
- **Intellectual faculties judgment.** Boule and colleagues claimed the endocast indicated "rudimentary intellectual faculties"; large Neanderthal brain size used ironically against intelligence (Anatole France's smaller brain cited) (via S&G 1993). Early-20th-c. view that large occipital = "baser" functions and small frontal = deficient reason later repudiated (S&G historiography).
- **Stooped, simian reconstruction.** Vertebral column and limb bones showing "numerous simian characters" and "less perfect bipedal or upright carriage"; short legs; comparison of Chapelle posture with an Australian Aborigine whom Boule would have ranked among "lowest races" — S&G flag the comparison as racist and the slouched posture as inaccurate: "no evidence that the Neanderthals stood with the slouched posture depicted here" (S&G 1993).
- **Authorized popular image (1909).** *L'Illustration* / *Illustrated London News* reconstruction: excessively hairy "Stone Age mugger" "quite obviously… exiled from the mainstream of human ancestry" (S&G 1993). Contrasted with Keith's 1911 "Not in the 'Gorilla' stage" noble reconstruction of the same skeleton — Keith later reversed after Piltdown and joined the expulsion camp (S&G 1993).
- **Presapiens model (Boule–Vallois).** Modern features had long gestation; Neanderthals a relict from an ancient common ancestor (probably ≥1 Ma); Cro-Magnons derived from small-browed gracile European ancestors coexisting with Neanderthals — **Europe remains central** to modern origins while Neanderthals are replaced (via S&G 1993). Distinct both from living multiregional continuity and from African replacement; a historical third structure in [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

French paleontology / Museum of Natural History and Institut de Paléontologie Humaine tradition; collaborator/successor strand with Henri Vallois on presapiens. Foil for later rehabilitations of Neanderthal posture and intelligence (Straus & Cave and others; S&G composite portrait "a long way from Boule's Neanderthal"). Continuity camp (Brace 1964) defined itself partly against Boule-style "catastrophism"/expulsion (via S&G). Replacement camp ([[stringer-chris|Stringer]] & [[gamble-clive|Gamble]]) retains separate-species/non-ancestral conclusion but "for entirely different reasons than Boule's" — special Neanderthal fossil characters, not ape-like features (S&G 1993).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Posture and pathology.** Mid-century and later restudies revise subhuman conclusions. Widespread idea that Boule's peculiarities were pathology he ignored — a more recent study (as of S&G 1993) concludes Boule's interpretations, though flawed by present standards, were reasonable for their time and that he was aware of some pathologies; he judged the "Old Man" degenerate on criteria other than arthritis. Full list of maladies: degenerative joint disease (skull, jaw, spine, hip, feet), rib fracture, extensive tooth loss with abscesses — all before age 40 (via S&G 1993).
- **Locomotion.** None of the distinctive Neanderthal skeletal features S&G survey (squatting facets, hand grip, shoulder, pelvis/hip emphasis, ankle/foot) indicate Boule-style bent-kneed apelike posture (S&G 1993). Yoel Rak's return to different walking (Kebara pelvis) is a separate, limited claim — not restoration of Boule's simian carriage.
- **Cultural afterlife.** Fictional portrayals track scientific mood: Rosny-Aîné *La Guerre du Feu* (1911) and Wells *The Grisly Folk* (1921) unflattering/expulsionist — Wells explicitly has True Men hunt down and exterminate Neanderthals, echoing Boule ("as well might we try to dream and feel as a gorilla dreams and feels"); Golding *The Inheritors* (1955) and Asimov *The Ugly Little Boy* (1958) rehabilitative after Boule's death and Chapelle rethink; Auel *Clan of the Cave Bear* (1980) sympathetic with research base (S&G 1993 cultural analysis).
- **Moral load of replacement models.** S&G insist their 1993 replacement conclusion does **not** carry the moral implications of Boule's theories nor Wells/Quennells negative judgment — Neanderthals "as human as us, but… a different brand of humanity" (S&G 1993).

## Historiography

Seeded and densified from secondary historiography in Stringer & Gamble 1993 ch. 1 and related passages, not Boule primary texts. Canonical cautionary case of reconstruction bias (theoretical priors, comparative racism of the era, pathology). Remains indispensable for the genealogy of Neanderthal expulsion and for distinguishing **why** later workers exclude Neanderthals from living European ancestry (African replacement + morphology) from **why Boule did** (simian characters, rudimentary intellect, stooped posture).
