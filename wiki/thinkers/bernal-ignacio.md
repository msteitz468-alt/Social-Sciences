# Ignacio Bernal

Mexican archaeologist, known from Coe & Koontz (2013) for excavation work in Oaxaca.

## Overview

Ignacio Bernal directed 1966 excavations at Dainzú (12.5 mi/20 km southeast of Oaxaca
City), a Late Preclassic/Classic site, uncovering a roughly 150-ft (45 m) Monte Albán II
platform faced with 50 low-relief carved stones, mostly depicting ball players in
protective gear (helmets, knee guards, gauntlets) — it remains unclear whether victors
or the vanquished are depicted. Bernal is also quoted by Coe & Koontz characterizing the
Mixteca homeland as "a succession of very small, rather prosperous valleys surrounded by
large areas of nearby desert."

## Contributions

Excavation and interpretation of Dainzú's ball-player relief program (see
[[concepts/mesoamerican-ballgame]]); descriptive characterization of the Mixtec
homeland's geography, cited in [[cultures/mixtec-culture]].
