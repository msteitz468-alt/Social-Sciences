# Alexandra Kollontai

## Overview

Russian revolutionary, the most prominent woman in the early Bolshevik leadership (People's Commissar of Social Welfare, 1917), theorist of women's emancipation and the family, and — as documented here — co-leader and chief theorist of the Workers' Opposition of 1920–21. This page currently rests on a single primary source, *The Workers' Opposition* ([[kollontai-workers-opposition-1921]]), plus its 1961 Solidarity editorial apparatus; her feminist and family-sociology writings, for which she is at least equally significant, are not yet ingested (as of 2026-07-16) — the page will need substantial expansion when they arrive.

## Contributions

*The Workers' Opposition* (1921, attributed): the argument that the communist economy can be built only by "the whole class" through its unions, not by the vanguard party or bourgeois specialists — "Only the vanguard of the class can create revolution, but only the whole class can develop through its everyday experience the practical work of the basic class collectives" ([[soviet-trade-union-debate]], [[vanguard-party]]). Her theory of [[bureaucracy]] as "a direct negation of mass self-activity," argued against Zinoviev's misery explanation and Trotsky's "good sides of bureaucracy," is among the earliest inside-Bolshevik analyses of Soviet bureaucratization — the Solidarity editors (1961, attributed) rank it more radical than the later anti-Stalin oppositions while criticizing its class-infiltration causal theory for exempting the party's own structure.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Within ingested material: co-leader of the Workers' Opposition with Shlyapnikov (whose December 1920 report her pamphlet quotes) and Lutovinov; antagonist of Lenin, Trotsky, Zinoviev, and Bukharin in the trade-union controversy. Lenin charged the text with "petty-bourgeois anarchism" (reported via the 1961 editors), and the Tenth Congress banned it along with the faction.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Recorded critiques so far are the contemporaneous Bolshevik condemnation (the "syndicalist and anarchist deviation" resolution — [[lenin-tax-in-kind-1921]]) and the Solidarity editors' structural critique (her Marxist class-origin framework blinded her to party structure as a cause of bureaucracy; the unions she would have empowered were already party-controlled, citing Brinton). The editors also note that she denounced the Kronstadt rising and remained a loyal Bolshevik — later a Soviet diplomat (not documented from ingested sources). Her feminist thought's large reappraisal literature awaits its sources.

## Historiography

Single primary source in partisan transmission (banned text → Pankhurst's *Workers' Dreadnought* → Solidarity pamphlet). Biographical detail beyond the 1920–21 episode is deliberately thin pending independent sources.
