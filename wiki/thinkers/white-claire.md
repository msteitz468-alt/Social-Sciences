# Claire White

## Overview

Associate Professor in Religious Studies at California State University, Northridge; White (2021) notes she was appointed to the first tenure-track position in the [[cognitive-science-of-religion|cognitive science of religion]] at a U.S. religious-studies department. PhD training at the Institute of Cognition and Culture, Queen's University Belfast, under [[whitehouse-harvey|Harvey Whitehouse]] (committee: Jesse Bering, Emma Cohen, Tom Lawson, Paulo Sousa). Author of the field's first full introductory textbook, *[[white-introduction-csr-2021|An Introduction to the Cognitive Science of Religion]]* (Routledge, 2021).

## Contributions

- **Textbook synthesis of CSR** — frames the program's history, core assumptions ([[fractionation-of-religion|fractionation]], intuitive vs reflective belief, [[theological-correctness]]), research questions, methods, and topical domains (world, afterlife, agents, morality, ritual).
- **Reincarnation cognition** — empirical/theoretical work on cognitive foundations of reincarnation beliefs (White 2016, cited in the textbook).
- Positions CSR as complementary to interpretive study of religion rather than a replacement (textbook voice, attributed).

## Intellectual Genealogy

**trained_under:** [[whitehouse-harvey]]. Network of CSR pioneers she surveys: [[boyer-pascal]], [[barrett-justin-l]], [[guthrie-stewart]], [[mccauley-robert-n]], [[lawson-e-thomas]], [[sperber-dan]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Textbook adopts a "principle of generosity" in labeling CSR contributors — may understate internal fragmentation. Not a primary theory-builder of MCI/HADD/modes; her distinctive empirical footprint is denser on afterlife/reincarnation than on ritual form theory.
