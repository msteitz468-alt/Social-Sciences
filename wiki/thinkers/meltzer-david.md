# David J. Meltzer

North American archaeologist (Southern Methodist University) specializing in the peopling of the Americas, Paleoindian archaeology, geoarchaeology, and late Pleistocene megafaunal extinction debates. Primary densification from *[[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|First Peoples in a New World]]* (2009); earlier secondary layer from [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|Mithen 2003]].

## Overview

Meltzer is both a synthesist of the first-Americans problem and a participant in its hinge episodes: he helped organize and took part in the 1997 expert visit to [[monte-verde|Monte Verde]] that shifted pre-Clovis from hypothesis to widely accepted reality (as he narrates, 2009), co-authored major critiques of Paul Martin’s overkill model with [[grayson-donald|Donald Grayson]], and directed renewed work at the [[folsom-site|Folsom]] type locality. *First Peoples* rebuilds his 1993 *Search for the First Americans* from the ground up after Monte Verde, the Kennewick flashpoint, and a decade of genetic and paleoclimatic work (attributed, Meltzer 2009 preface).

## Contributions

### Standards of proof and historiography

Meltzer frames peopling research as a century-and-a-half cycle of extraordinary claims and high-bar tests, from the American Paleolithic / Holmes–[[hrdlicka-ales|Hrdlička]] orthodoxy through Folsom (1927) to pre-Clovis candidates. His standing methodological thesis: extraordinary antiquity claims require extraordinary proof (contamination, context, human agency, and age firewalls) — illustrated by his “fingerprint the crew” response to MacNeish’s Pendejo Cave fingerprint claim (attributed, Meltzer 2009).

### Pre-Clovis and routes

He treats pre-Clovis presence as established by 2009, with [[monte-verde]] MV-II (~12,500 radiocarbon years BP in his usage) as the decisive case, while insisting individual early sites still fail or pass case by case ([[meadowcroft]], Calico, Pedra Furada, etc.). On routes he weighs the [[ice-free-corridor]] against coastal pathways after Cordilleran retreat, skeptical of corridor-as-only-path models tied to classic Clovis-first timing (attributed, Meltzer 2009). He rejects Solutrean–Atlantic origins for Clovis as chronologically and adaptively unsupported (attributed).

### Colonization process

Beyond “who/when,” Meltzer develops **how** hunter-gatherers colonize an empty, no-analogue continent: landscape learning (stone, water, plants, climate, risk), demography of small founding groups, leapfrog mobility, and the eventual **settling-in** that diversifies point styles and diets as packing rises (attributed, Meltzer 2009 chs. 7, 9).

### Clovis and extinctions

He densifies [[clovis-culture]] as a real, widespread late Pleistocene horizon — high-quality exotic stone, fluting, caches, broad diet — without equating Clovis points with a single founding people or with exclusive big-game specialization (attributed). With Grayson he argues Martin’s blitzkrieg overkill fails against kill-site tallies (alleged associations pruned to a small set, secure kills essentially mammoth and mastodon) and extinction chronologies — see [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]] (attributed, Meltzer 2009 ch. 8).

### Dating convention in the 2009 book

Meltzer explicitly prefers **radiocarbon years BP** (present = 1950) for late Pleistocene ages in this volume rather than calendar-calibrated BP, citing calibration instability in the relevant range (attributed, Meltzer 2009 ch. 1). Integrators must not silently convert his figures to cal BP.

### Ethics and the living past

Chapter 10 treats NAGPRA, Kennewick, disease asymmetry at contact, and cooperative alternatives (e.g., On Your Knees Cave). He records deep rifts between some archaeologists and Native communities without adjudicating land claims; omits traditional origin narratives for lack of expertise while noting political stakes (Deloria) of peopling chronologies (attributed, Meltzer 2009). Cross-link [[ancient-dna-repatriation-ethics-debate]] and [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Genetics and some linguistic migration clocks in *First Peoples* are **(as of Meltzer 2009)** and are superseded for molecular consensus by post-2010 aDNA (Anzick, Kennewick genome, Reich-lab syntheses) already layered on [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]] — do not treat Meltzer 2009 genetics as current.
- Overkill defenders (e.g. Gary Haynes, Stuart Fiedel in the literature Meltzer engages) contest the Grayson–Meltzer pruning — record as positions on [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]].
- Site-by-site pre-Clovis assessments continue to be revised after 2009 (new sites, re-dating); Meltzer’s 2009 verdicts are dated archaeological judgments.
