# Timothy R. Pauketat

American archaeologist (University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign), the leading maximalist interpreter of [[cahokia]] and the [[mississippian-culture|Mississippian]] world.

## Overview

Pauketat built his career on American Bottom fieldwork and CRM-era salvage data, arguing that Cahokia was founded in a rapid, deliberate "big bang" around 1050 CE and constituted a genuine city — a political-religious phenomenon that spread people, cults, and practices across mid-continental North America. His *Chiefdoms and Other Archaeological Delusions* (2007) attacked the neo-evolutionary [[chiefdom]] typology as an analytical straitjacket, proposing "historical processualism" — practice-theory-inflected attention to events and agency — in its place.

## Contributions

- The **big-bang model** of Cahokia's origin (~1050 CE): rapid plaza and mound construction, in-migration, and new ceremonialism, argued from ceramic seriation and construction stratigraphy (Pauketat's position; contested — see [[cahokia]] and the population/urbanism dispute against minimalist readings such as Milner's).
- Critique of [[chiefdom]] as a category (`reacted_against` neo-evolutionary typology).
- Synthesis of [[cahokia]]'s Mound 72 mortuary data as state-like theatrical violence.

## Intellectual Genealogy

PhD under **Henry Wright** at the University of Michigan (1991) — trained inside the neo-evolutionary school whose chiefdom typology he attacks ([[pauketat-chiefdoms-delusions-2007]] notes many leading chiefdom theorists share the Michigan lineage: Sahlins's 1970 Hawaii seminar trained Brumfiel, Earle, and Kus). Works within the post-processual/practice-theory turn as applied to North American archaeology; named his program [[historical-processualism]]. Recounts being encouraged by James B. Griffin that "criticizing each other is the way archaeology improves itself." Excavated four pyramids at and around [[cahokia]] (plus documented Mounds 11 and 51) — the empirical base of his mound-renewal and big-bang arguments.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Minimalists (e.g., George Milner) regard Pauketat's population estimates (10,000–15,000+ at Cahokia proper) and urban framing as inflated; the dispute is live and recorded on the Cahokia population-urbanism debate page.
