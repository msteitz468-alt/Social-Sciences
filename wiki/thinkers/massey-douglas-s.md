# Douglas S. Massey

## Overview

American sociologist and demographer; co-author with [[denton-nancy-a|Nancy A. Denton]] of *[[massey-denton-american-apartheid-1993|American Apartheid]]* (1993), the landmark statement that extreme black–white [[residential-segregation]] and [[hypersegregation]] are primary structural causes of the [[urban-underclass|urban underclass]]. Research funded by NICHD (1980-census segregation project and consequences project); Guggenheim fellowship 1990–91 for the manuscript (as of the book's preface).

## Contributions

- Co-developed and popularized the five-dimension [[hypersegregation]] construct (with Denton; *Demography* 1989 paper extended in the book).
- Argued that [[residential-segregation]] concentrates poverty and mediates economic restructuring effects emphasized by [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]] — see [[underclass-and-segregation-debate]].
- Synthesized historical ghetto construction (violence, covenants, federal housing policy) with contemporary audit and census evidence — study page: [[massey-denton-american-apartheid-study]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator: [[denton-nancy-a]]. Engages [[wilson-william-julius]], Kenneth B. Clark (*Dark Ghetto*), Myrdal, Taeubers, Kerner Commission tradition. Chicago / NORC institutional context for the NICHD projects (preface).
