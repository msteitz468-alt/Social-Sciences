# Michael Omi

American sociologist and ethnic-studies scholar, co-author with [[winant-howard|Howard Winant]] of *[[omi-winant-racial-formation-2015|Racial Formation in the United States]]* (1986; 3rd ed. 2015), the charter text of [[racial-formation-theory]].

## Overview

Omi co-developed the racial formation framework: race as an ongoing sociohistorical process linking structure and representation through [[racial-project|racial projects]], against ethnicity-, class-, and nation-only reductions.

## Contributions

- Co-founder of [[racial-formation-theory]] and core concepts [[racial-formation]], [[racial-project]], [[racialization]].
- Critique of the [[ethnicity-paradigm]] of race and analysis of [[colorblind-racism|colorblind racial ideology]] as hegemonic project (with Winant).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborative program with Winant across three editions; engages Du Bois, Gramscian hegemony, social constructionism, and postwar U.S. racial politics. Detailed biography not in this source — `trained_under` left [[unknown]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

See [[racial-formation-theory]] and [[colorblind-racism]] debates; theory widely used and also contested for scope of “racial project” and periodization of colorblind hegemony.

## Historiography

Canonical in U.S. race sociology and ethnic studies since 1986; 3rd edition reworks paradigms and adds colorblind/Obama analysis.
