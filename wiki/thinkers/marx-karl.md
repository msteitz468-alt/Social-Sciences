# Karl Marx

## Overview

Karl Marx (1818–1883), German philosopher, political economist, and revolutionary, principal author of the [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|Communist Manifesto]] (1848), *[[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-1852|The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte]]* (1852), and *[[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|Capital, Volume I]]* (1867), and with [[engels-friedrich|Friedrich Engels]] founder of [[historical-materialism|historical materialism]]. He is a canonical founder of classical sociology’s **conflict tradition**. This page is built from the *Manifesto*, the *Brumaire*, and *Capital* I; the 1844 manuscripts and *Capital* II–III are not yet ingested.

## Contributions

From the *Manifesto* (with Engels):

- History as [[class-struggle|class struggles]]; [[mode-of-production|mode of production]] as primary; [[bourgeoisie]] / [[proletariat]] dialectic; ideology thesis.
- **Asiatic / Oriental formations (via secondary historiography).** Marx and Engels wrote on Asiatic society and related categories; the full primary dossier is not yet ingested. [[wittfogel-karl-a|Wittfogel]] (*[[wittfogel-oriental-despotism-1957|Oriental Despotism]]* Ch. 9) reconstructs Marx's [[asiatic-mode-of-production]] usage and later Soviet fights over it — treat as Wittfogel's attributed historiography until Marx's Asia writings are primary-ingested.

From the *Eighteenth Brumaire* (attributed; study [[marx-eighteenth-brumaire-study]]):

- **[[men-make-their-own-history|Structure and agency]]** — men make history under unchosen conditions; tradition weighs on the living.
- **[[historical-costume]]** — revolutions borrow past names and costumes; 1848–51 as farce after 1789’s tragedy.
- **[[class-fraction|Class fractions]]** and the [[party-of-order|Party of Order]] — Legitimist land vs Orleanist capital; parliamentary republic as joint bourgeois rule.
- **[[bonapartism]]** and **[[relative-autonomy-of-the-state]]** — executive independence when the bourgeoisie abandons political rule; state machine perfected by successive overturns.
- **[[peasantry-as-class|Peasantry]]** (potato-bag; cannot represent themselves) and **[[lumpenproletariat]]** (Society of December 10) as bases of personal rule.

From *Capital* I (attributed throughout):

- **Critique of political economy** — commodity as cell-form; force of abstraction; England as classic ground; aim to lay bare the economic law of motion of modern society.
- **[[labor-theory-of-value]]** — abstract labour; socially necessary labour-time; [[commodity-fetishism]].
- **[[labor-power]]** as commodity; **[[surplus-value]]** (absolute and relative); **[[constant-and-variable-capital]]**.
- Working-day struggle and Factory Acts as regulation of absolute surplus-value.
- Relative surplus-value via co-operation, manufacture, machinery.
- **[[organic-composition-of-capital]]**, **[[industrial-reserve-army]]**, **[[general-law-of-capitalist-accumulation]]**.
- **[[primitive-accumulation]]** as historical expropriation (not thrift); colonial system, public debt, protection; historical tendency that expropriators are expropriated (**position**).

From Engels’s *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]* (posthumous use of Marx’s notes — attributed via Engels):

- **Morgan précis (1880–81).** After Marx’s death Engels found Marx’s extensive extracts from [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]’s *Ancient Society*; *Origin* is framed as executing Marx’s intended restatement of Morgan in materialist terms.
- **Quoted extensions.** Engels cites Marx: systems of consanguinity lag like political/juridical/religious systems; the modern family contains slavery and serfdom “in germ”; property differences within one gens transform unity of interest into antagonism among members.

Theoretical claims remain **attributed**, never wiki-voice laws of history.

### Periodization as reception — Althusser’s *For Marx* (attributed)

*(Not Marx’s self-periodization; a 1960s theoretical thesis.)* [[althusser-louis|Althusser]] (*[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*) periodizes Marx as Early Works (1840–44; Kantian-Fichtean then Feuerbachian humanism; 1844 Manuscripts as extreme Feuerbach–Hegel synthesis), works of the [[epistemological-break]] (~1845: *Theses on Feuerbach*, *German Ideology*), transitional (1845–57), and mature (1857–83: *Capital*). He argues mature Marxism is [[theoretical-anti-humanism|theoretically anti-humanist]] and that continuous “young Marx is part of Marxism” readings confuse ideology with science ([[marxist-humanism-debate]]; [[structural-marxism]]). The 1844 Manuscripts themselves are not yet primary-ingested in this wiki.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Collaborator:** [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] — *Manifesto* co-author; editor of English *Capital* and later volumes; *Origin* as fulfillment of Marx’s Morgan project.
- **Reacted against:** rival socialisms (*Manifesto* Ch. III); classical and vulgar political economy (*Capital* afterword 1873); Malthusian population dogma (reserve-army / Ireland discussion); mere “surprise” or great-man accounts of the 1851 coup (*Brumaire*).
- Continuity with 1859 *Zur Kritik*; illness delayed *Capital* (1867 preface).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- *Manifesto* predictions (immiseration, polarization, imminent revolution) did not unfold as forecast.
- *Capital* I: value theory contested; immiseration/general law contested by wage and welfare history; enclosure/colonial historiography revised.
- *Brumaire*: historiography of 1848–51 and peasant politics contested; state-autonomy readings diverge ([[relative-autonomy-of-the-state-debate]]).
- Weberian and other sociologies contest reduction of status/power to class.
- Political uses of Marx’s name after his death are reception, not entailments of 1848/1852/1867 texts.
- **Postwar amendment ([[mills-c-wright|Mills]]).** In *[[mills-white-collar-1951|White Collar]]*
  (1951) C. Wright Mills extended Marxian [[alienation]] from the wage-worker to the salaried
  [[white-collar]] employee (the [[personality-market]]), but rejected the Marxian expectation of
  [[new-middle-class-politics-debate|proletarianization]], arguing propertylessness had coincided
  with rising, not falling, living standards, and amending the base/superstructure relation:
  "between consciousness and existence stand communications" ([[mass-society]]).
- **Western-Marxist revision ([[marcuse-herbert|Marcuse]]).** In *[[marcuse-one-dimensional-man-1964|One-Dimensional
  Man]]* (1964) Marcuse argued the working class had been *integrated* into advanced industrial
  society rather than pauperized into revolt, so it no longer functions as capitalism's
  "grave-digger" — leaving critical theory without a "demonstrable agent of change"
  ([[revolutionary-subject-debate]]). He read Marx's *Grundrisse* "Fragment on Machines" as
  anticipating that full automation would explode the value form, and held that emancipation
  requires transforming the [[technological-rationality|technological structure itself]], not
  only ownership ([[critical-theory]], [[one-dimensionality]]).
