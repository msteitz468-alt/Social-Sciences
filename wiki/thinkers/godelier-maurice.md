# Maurice Godelier (b. 1934)

French anthropologist, the leading exponent of [[structural-marxism]] within [[economic-anthropology]]. This page is a scaffold created during the ingest of [[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Sahlins's *Culture and Practical Reason*]] (and referenced in *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*); his primary works are not yet ingested.

## Overview

*(Attributed, via Sahlins's use.)* Godelier sought to reconcile [[marx-karl|Marx]] and [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]], arguing famously that "Marx was a structuralist" (1972) and developing the concepts of the **articulation of modes of production** and of thought as itself part of the infrastructure ("the ideal in the real"). His fieldwork among the Baruya of Papua New Guinea grounded his analyses of production, exchange, and domination in non-market societies.

## Contributions

- **Structural-Marxist economic anthropology** — the analysis of precapitalist societies as articulated modes of production. Sahlins situates Godelier's study of the Mbuti and his concept of "stereotypic reproduction" within the program he critiques (see [[structural-marxism]], [[mode-of-production]]).
- The claim, noted approvingly by Sahlins as evidence of the "attraction of opposites" between Marxism and structuralism, that Marx himself was a structuralist.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Godelier worked in the orbit of [[althusser-louis|Althusser]]'s reading of *Capital* and of Lévi-Straussian structuralism, and stands as the anthropological figure against whom [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] most directly develops his critique of "practical reason" in Marxist form. In *Islands of History* Sahlins turns a Godelierian phrase to his own ends ("Love is the infrastructure, as Godelier might say," of Hawaiian society — see [[performative-and-prescriptive-structures]]). Formal training relations are not documented in the ingested sources.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Sahlins's objection to Godelier is the general one against structural Marxism: that treating kinship or religion as "functioning as" relations of production reduces meaningful order to an "abstract shadow structure of productive requirements" and refuses "the symbolic constitution of social reality" (see [[cultural-reason]], [[practical-reason]]). Godelier's later work (*The Enigma of the Gift*, 1996) moved toward questions of the sacred and the inalienable, engaging Mauss.
