# Pierre Macherey (b. 1938)

## Overview

French philosopher; associated with Althusser’s ENS circle (returned from outside the École to present at the seminar). Author of **Part Three** of *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* — “On the Process of Exposition of *Capital* (The Work of Concepts)” — on the order of concepts (wealth, commodity, value) as scientific arrangement rather than narrative of discovery. Later work on literary production and Spinoza is outside this primary ingest.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — *Reading Capital* Part Three.)*

- Defines the **process of exposition** as arrangement of discourse following the strict movement of scientific knowledge: not the movement of appearance/investigation (*Forschungsweise*), but autonomous formulation of knowledge governed by particular laws.
- Analyzes Marx’s **starting-point** (wealth / commodity): wealth as ideological starting-point that must be worked; commodity as site where contradiction appears; **value as constructed concept**, not empirical given.
- Treats *Capital*’s order as a **work of concepts** that can look like “systematic disorder” relative to empiricist or dogmatic expectations of linear clarity — scientific order is not pedagogic ease.
- Aligns with [[symptomatic-reading]] and [[theoretical-practice]] (Generalities) without being a mere application note: exposition is where the knowledge-object is produced in discourse.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Circle of [[althusser-louis]]; seminar order placed his paper between parts of Rancière’s presentation (restored in complete editions).
- Debts: Marx’s distinction exposition/investigation; Althusserian problematic of science vs ideology.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Narrower object than Althusser’s Part Four or Balibar’s Part Five; sometimes under-cited in reception relative to structural causality and mode of production.
- Literary-theoretical later work retools “production” for texts — continuity and break with 1965 not primary-ingested here.
