# Gary King

## Overview

Gary King is an American political scientist and quantitative methodologist at Harvard University, best known within this wiki as the lead author of *[[king-keohane-verba-designing-social-inquiry-1994|Designing Social Inquiry]]* (1994), the text that reframed qualitative research design around the statistical logic of inference.

## Contributions

King's methodological program treats [[causal-inference]] and [[descriptive-inference]] as governed by a single logic across quantitative and qualitative work. In *Designing Social Inquiry* he and his co-authors advance [[observable-implications]] as the connective tissue between theory and data, and argue for maximizing the leverage of a research design.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Co-authored *Designing Social Inquiry* with [[keohane-robert]] and [[verba-sidney]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The design prescriptions King co-authored drew a substantial rebuttal literature; see [[king-keohane-verba-single-logic-debate]] and [[qualitative-quantitative-divide-debate]].
