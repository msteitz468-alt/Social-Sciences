# Edward P. Lanning

## Overview

American Andean archaeologist whose *Peru Before the Incas* (1967) was, per [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|Moseley (1975)]], the first synthesis of the early coastal materials within a broader account of Andean prehistory. Moseley's own work is "the direct outgrowth of Lanning's survey and research on the central coast," even as Moseley disputes Lanning's interpretive frame.

## Contributions

- **Central-coast preceramic survey** — located and periodized the [[ancon|Ancón–Chillón]] preceramic sites (Encanto phase, ~3600 BC; the Cotton Preceramic sequence); excavated the Yacht Club and Pampa sites.
- **Andean periodization** — supplied the stage/phase framework Moseley builds on and modifies (see [[andean-chronology]]).
- **Lomas desiccation hypothesis** — argued lomas fog-vegetation was ~10× more extensive early and that climatic desiccation drove abandonment of foraging at the close of the Encanto phase (attributed; contested — see below).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained [[moseley-michael|Michael Moseley]] in central-coast fieldwork context; through [[patterson-thomas|Thomas Patterson]] (Lanning's collaborator) shaped the research program Moseley entered. Moseley credits Lanning's synthesis while rejecting its assimilation of Peruvian data to Mesoamerican/Mesopotamian models "where the origins of civilization and plant domestication are seen as evolving jointly."

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Mesoamerican-analogy framing** — Moseley's disagreements are "basic and numerous," centered on Lanning's cross-regional analogy.
- **Lomas desiccation** — challenged by Craig & Psuty (1968, 1971) on geomorphological grounds; Moseley "remain[s] in doubt," judging the evidence tenuous. See [[ancon]] Key Debates.
