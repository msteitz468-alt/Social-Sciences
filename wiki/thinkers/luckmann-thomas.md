# Thomas Luckmann

## Overview

German-American sociologist (1927–2016), co-author with [[berger-peter-l|Peter L. Berger]] of *[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|The Social Construction of Reality]]* (1966). Luckmann brought a strong phenomenological and sociology-of-religion formation into the collaborative program that redefined the [[sociology-of-knowledge]]. At the time of the joint treatise: University of Frankfurt; Preface notes thanks to Friedrich Tenbruck and Benita Luckmann, and mentions his concurrent work on *The Invisible Religion* (not yet ingested).

## Contributions

Joint architect, with Berger, of the [[social-construction-of-reality]] program (attributed to the 1966 text):

- Redefinition of [[sociology-of-knowledge]] toward everyday knowledge and the social construction of reality
- Three-moment dialectic of [[externalization-objectivation-internalization]]
- Genetic theory of [[institutionalization]] and [[legitimation]] / [[symbolic-universe|symbolic universes]]
- Socialization theory ([[primary-socialization]], [[secondary-socialization]]) and [[plausibility-structure|plausibility structures]]
- Anti-[[reification]] humanistic stake of the Conclusion

See [[hubs/theory/social-construction-of-reality]] for depth. Individual Luckmann monographs are outside current collection scope.
