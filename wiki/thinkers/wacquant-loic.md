# Loïc Wacquant

## Overview

Loïc Wacquant is a French-American sociologist known for **[[carnal-sociology]]** — theoretically armed ethnography that takes the body as tool of inquiry — and for structural analyses of the post-Fordist ghetto, advanced marginality, and the carceral state. *[[wacquant-body-and-soul-2004|Body & Soul]]* (2004; French *Corps et âme*) is his apprentice-boxer ethnography of the [[woodlawn-boys-club|Woodlawn Boys Club]] (Chicago South Side, Aug 1988–Feb 1992), conducted while collaborating with [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]] at the University of Chicago. He is a principal collaborator and interpreter of [[bourdieu-pierre|Pierre Bourdieu]] in the Anglophone world (*An Invitation to Reflexive Sociology*, 1992). Acknowledgments in *Body & Soul* also credit UChicago teachers (Wilson, Coleman, Comaroffs, Przeworski, Steinmetz), Harvard Society of Fellows, and the MacArthur Fellows Program. Born trajectory noted in acknowledgments: Century Fellowship brought him from Nouméa, New Caledonia, to Chicago.

## Contributions

From *Body & Soul* (attributed):

- **[[carnal-sociology]]** / observant participation — sociology *from* the body; initiatory immersion; triple challenge (craft, ghetto, corporeal practice).
- **[[pugilistic-habitus]]** and gym as island of order/virtue, school of morality, sanctuary vs street.
- **[[bodily-capital]]** — body as tool+target; corporeal thrift; career-time management.
- Ghetto reconstruction against disorganization and underclass frames as ethnoracial prison / exploitation (bridge to later urban monographs).
- Empirical demonstration of Bourdieu’s learning-by-body thesis in a U.S. urban craft world.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Reception of carnal sociology and the apprentice-boxer project (gender/masculinity of the site; generalizability; romanticization; narrative vs science; analyst racial position) — record as secondary literature is ingested. *Body & Soul* presents itself as partial, provisional answer to the third challenge.
