# Alfred Schutz

## Overview

Austrian-American phenomenological sociologist (1899–1959). Not yet represented by a primary work in this collection; enters the wiki as the decisive intellectual source for Part One of Berger and Luckmann's *[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|The Social Construction of Reality]]* (1966). Berger and Luckmann state that their analysis of the foundations of knowledge in everyday life is based on Schutz's work (Notes): the [[reality-of-everyday-life|reality of everyday life]], [[typification]], and the [[stock-of-knowledge|social stock of knowledge]].

## Contributions

*(Stub — no Schutz primary source ingested. Content here is attributed via Berger & Luckmann's use.)*

Schutz developed a phenomenological sociology of the **life-world** (*Lebenswelt*), analyzing how ordinary actors constitute a shared world of everyday reality through typifications and a socially distributed stock of knowledge. Berger and Luckmann treat this analysis as philosophical prolegomena to their sociology of knowledge proper (Parts Two and Three of the 1966 treatise).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Influences:** Husserlian phenomenology; [[weber-max|Weberian]] [[verstehen|interpretive]] sociology (as reworked by Schutz — detail awaits primary ingest).
- **Trained / shaped:** [[berger-peter-l|Peter Berger]] and [[luckmann-thomas|Thomas Luckmann]] (among others in the New School orbit).
- **Relation to programs:** underwrites the phenomenological opening of [[social-construction-of-reality]]; links to [[interpretive-sociology]]. Schutz's phenomenology of the natural attitude and background expectancies is also the decisive source (alongside Parsons) for [[garfinkel-harold|Harold Garfinkel]]'s [[ethnomethodology]].
