# Nikolai Grube

## Overview

German Maya epigrapher; co-author with [[martin-simon|Simon Martin]] of [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008|*Chronicle of the Maya Kings and Queens*]] (2000; 2nd ed. 2008). Named among Coe’s younger-generation epigraphers in [[coe-breaking-the-maya-code-2012|Coe 2012]]; primary densification here is from the *Chronicle*.

## Contributions

- **Joint political narrative:** With Martin, the eleven-kingdom dynastic chronicle that made hierarchical Classic politics (overkings, *y-ajaw* vassals, Tikal–Calakmul competition) the default teaching frame (attributed — Martin & Grube 2008).
- **Epigraphic program:** Part of the logosyllabic generation that converted monument texts into reign sequences, war histories, and royal genealogies for sites including [[tikal]], [[calakmul]], [[naranjo]], [[caracol]], and the Usumacinta courts.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Post-[[schele-linda|Schele]] epigraphic generation alongside [[stuart-david|David Stuart]], [[houston-stephen|Stephen Houston]], and others; collaborator of [[martin-simon|Simon Martin]]. Detailed training chain not established from the *Chronicle* alone.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Same as for Martin: overkingship is a model; site-level narratives depend on contested readings and incomplete corpuses. Record dissent on site pages rather than here.

## Historiography

Co-signature of the Martin–Grube synthesis; German-language and field-project work beyond the *Chronicle* is not yet ingested.
