# Roberta Gilchrist

British archaeologist; major synthesizer of **[[feminist-archaeology|gender archaeology]]** in the Anglophone critical turn. *[[gilchrist-gender-and-archaeology-1999|Gender and Archaeology: Contesting the Past]]* (Routledge 1999) surveys the field ~fifteen years after Conkey & Spector 1984 and densifies the multi-author charter *[[gero-conkey-engendering-archaeology-1991|Engendering Archaeology]]* (1991). Background in **medieval European archaeology**; taught gender archaeology at the University of Reading from 1996 (structure of the 1999 book grew from that teaching).

## Overview

Gilchrist frames gender archaeology as evolving **symbiotically** with feminist theory while not always conducted by feminists or women: paradigm shift from ungendered/male-biased narratives (pre-1970s) through visibility of women (1980s–early 1990s) to difference, masculinity, embodiment, and queer-inflected readings (late 1990s). She distinguishes **Americanist** (anthropological/processual; labour and prestige) and **European** (identity and cultural construction) traditions and insists there is **no single method** — only an imperative to do gender archaeology well (rigour, multi-source evidence, suspicion of essentialism and cross-cultural stereotypes; Wylie-aligned).

## Contributions

*(Attributed from Gilchrist 1999.)*

- **Survey synthesis** of gender archaeology’s first ~15 years: feminist waves mapped onto archaeological practice; equality → difference catalysis.
- **Definitions and glossary** that stabilize vault vocabulary: gender as cultural interpretation of sexual difference; [[androcentrism]]; [[gender-attribution]]; performance (Butler); queer theory; sex as partly historically perceived.
- **Methodological program:** multi-source readings (spatial + iconographic + environmental + analogic); critical ethnographic/historical analogy (Wylie); gender attribution not the sole gate; standpoint-style attention to everyday relations without relativism.
- **Empirical case — [[contested-garden|medieval English castle]]:** phenomenological gender-space analysis of seclusion, hall/chamber, gardens, *hortus conclusus*, and lifecycle against the castle-as-masculine-bastion stereotype; six multi-source cases (Portchester, Clarendon, Castle Rising, Chepstow, Pickering, Carisbrooke); seclusion as prestige as well as control.
- **[[gender-lifecourse]]:** gender cumulative across a lifetime; embodiment of ageing; time–space–performance.
- Engagement with **[[butler-judith|Butler]]** performativity, queer theory (as disruption, not gay visibility archaeology), masculinist studies, and cognitive/medical challenges to pure social constructionism — without abandoning feminist science critique.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Dialogue with [[conkey-margaret-w]], [[gero-joan-m]], [[spector-janet]], [[wylie-alison]], [[tringham-ruth]], [[moore-henrietta-l]]; draws on [[post-processual-archaeology]] openings (body, space, meaning) and feminist philosophy of science. Cites Strathern on incompleteness of feminist debate; Foucault on sexuality as historical construction; Merleau-Ponty on embodied perception for the castle chapter.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Gilchrist’s 1999 book is a field synthesis from a medieval specialist — prehistoric cases are largely reviewed rather than original fieldwork. Later queer and intersectional archaeologies extend beyond her frame; her own earlier nunnery monographs are not yet ingested here and would densify the medieval gender-space program.
