# Johann Jakob Bachofen

## Overview

Johann Jakob Bachofen (1815–1887), Swiss jurist and classical scholar, author of *Das Mutterrecht* (1861). In the 1891 preface to *[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]*, [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] treats Bachofen as the pioneer who first replaced vague talk of primitive promiscuity with evidence for mother-right and pre-monogamous sexual orders in classical literature — while rejecting Bachofen’s religious-mystical explanation of the shift to father-right.

## Contributions

As reported by Engels (attributed):

- Collected classical passages as **proofs** of an order prior to monogamy in which women as well as men had multiple partners without moral offense; of descent reckoned in the female line; and of a higher social position for mothers as the only certain parents.
- Interpreted Aeschylus’s *Oresteia* as drama of mother-right versus rising father-right (Furies vs Apollo/Athena) — a reading Engels called “one of the best and finest passages” while criticizing Bachofen’s belief that divinities wrought the change.
- Held that the advance from “hetaerism” to monogamy and mother-right to father-right was driven by **religious conception**, not by material life-conditions — the point Engels inverted.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Pioneer for:** the [[mother-right]] problem that McLennan and Morgan reworked; Engels’s family chapters depend on this problem-space even where they reject Bachofen’s mechanism.
- **Critiqued by Engels for:** making religion the lever of world history; “mysticism.”

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Later anthropology separated ethnographic matriliny from Bachofen’s world-historical *Mutterrecht* package; see [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]]. This page does not yet rest on *Das Mutterrecht* itself.

## Historiography

Known in this wiki so far through Engels’s 1891 historiographic preface, not through primary Bachofen ingest.

George Stocking (*[[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Victorian Anthropology]]*, 1987) confirms Bachofen as the main proponent of the "matriarchy" idea, which he derived from the mythological realm of the Amazons; *Das Mutterrecht* (1861) was formulated **independently** of the parallel British debate — [[mclennan-john-ferguson|John Ferguson McLennan]], who advanced a similar thesis on the historical priority of matriliny and [[primitive-promiscuity]] from an unrelated evidentiary base, did not learn of Bachofen's work until 1866. Stocking's account thus treats Bachofen and McLennan as convergent but unconnected sources of the Victorian matriarchy thesis, distinct from the British Maine–McLennan institutional dispute over patriarchal versus matrifocal priority.
