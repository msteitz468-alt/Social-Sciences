# Barrington Moore Jr.

## Overview

Barrington Moore Jr. (1913–2005) was an American political sociologist at Harvard, a founder of modern comparative-historical sociology. Recorded here as a **genealogy anchor** rather than from an ingested source: he is the teacher in whose comparative-historical program [[skocpol-theda|Theda Skocpol]] worked, and *[[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-1979|States and Social Revolutions]]* (1979) names his *Social Origins of Dictatorship and Democracy* (1966) as the most notable contemporary model for its method. No Moore work is yet ingested; this page will be filled when one is.

## Contributions

- **Comparative historical analysis of political development.** *Social Origins of Dictatorship and Democracy: Lord and Peasant in the Making of the Modern World* (1966) compared England, France, the United States, China, Japan, and India to argue that the class relations of the agrarian transition (the fate of the landed upper classes and the peasantry, and the commercialization of agriculture) channeled societies onto divergent political routes to modernity — bourgeois-democratic, fascist, or communist. Associated dictum: "No bourgeois, no democracy."
- **Agrarian class structure as the pivot of macro-historical outcomes** — the analytic move Skocpol both extended and revised (she added the autonomous [[state-autonomy|state]] and the international context her teacher's frame underweighted).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained** [[skocpol-theda|Theda Skocpol]] (and shaped a generation of comparative-historical sociologists at Harvard).
- His agrarian-class comparativism is the immediate predecessor of Skocpol's [[structural-theory-of-revolution|structural theory of revolution]]; she situates her book alongside Moore's, Eric Wolf's *Peasant Wars of the Twentieth Century*, and John Dunn's *Modern Revolutions*.
