# Noam Chomsky

**Noam Chomsky** (b. 1928), MIT linguist and founder of **generative grammar**, is the dominant figure of late-20th-century theoretical linguistics. His program holds that the human capacity for language rests on an innate, species-specific **[[universal-grammar|Universal Grammar]]** — a biological endowment underlying the surface variation of the world's languages.

> This page is a minimal summary built from a single ingested source (Everett 2008), which engages Chomsky as an antagonist. It is not a full treatment of his linguistics or his political work; deeper coverage awaits ingestion of primary generative-linguistics sources.

## Overview and Relevance Here

In the ingested source, Chomsky matters as the theoretical target of [[everett-daniel|Everett]]'s Pirahã argument. In **Hauser, Chomsky & Fitch (2002, *Science*)**, recursion is singled out as the possibly-unique core of the human language faculty ("the faculty of language in the narrow sense") — the property that makes linguistic creativity possible. Everett's claim that [[piraha|Pirahã]] lacks sentential [[recursion]] is framed (by Everett) as a potential falsification of this thesis. Everett reports Chomsky's response as a flat denial that such languages exist, and as a redefinition of recursion that Everett rejects; Chomsky and others also reply that recursion is a *capacity* the brain makes available but that need not be used in every language. See [[piraha-recursion-debate]].

Everett additionally contrasts Chomsky's **deductive, non-fieldwork** method with the inductive descriptivism of the [[sapir-edward|Sapir]]–[[boas-franz|Boas]] tradition, arguing (attributed) that generativism pulled linguistics toward psychology and away from anthropology and field research.

## Contributions

- **[[universal-grammar|Universal Grammar]]** and generative grammar: language as a biological, largely innate module, with cross-linguistic variation "largely insignificant" at the deep level — the view [[everett-daniel|Everett]] says his career was built on and now rejects.
- **[[recursion]]/Merge** as the computational heart of language (Minimalist Program); the Hauser–Chomsky–Fitch (2002) claim that recursion is the plausibly-unique core of the human language faculty.
- A **deductive, cognitive-science** methodology for linguistics — the pole Everett contrasts with inductive fieldwork.
