# David W. Anthony

## Overview

David W. Anthony is an American archaeologist whose *[[anthony-horse-wheel-language-2007|The Horse, the Wheel, and Language]]* (2007) is the principal full-length archaeological synthesis of the steppe (Pontic-Caspian) hypothesis for Proto-Indo-European origins. He combines historical-linguistic chronology (wool and wheel vocabularies), Pontic-Caspian and European prehistory, experimental and archaeological **bit-wear** evidence for early horse riding, and modern migration/frontier theory. Primary Position A voice on [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]]. Research partner Dorcas Brown co-developed [[bit-wear-analysis]] and co-directed steppe fieldwork.

## Contributions

*(from *Horse, Wheel, and Language* 2007 primary ingest)*

### Steppe homeland chronology and synthesis

Anthony argues that it is now possible to solve who spoke PIE, where, and when by combining reconstructed vocabulary with archaeology. He places the homeland on the Pontic-Caspian steppes (southern Ukraine and Russia), following [[gimbutas-marija|Marija Gimbutas]] and J.P. Mallory, with a maximum reconstructable window ~4500–2500 BCE. Late PIE after ~4000–3500 BCE is fixed by wool and especially wheel vocabularies ([[linguistic-palaeontology]]); [[yamnaya-culture]] is treated as the classic late-PIE social world of wagon-using mobile pastoralists. He sequences out-migrations to match IE branch order (Pre-Anatolian west ~4200–3900; Pre-Tocharian east ~3700–3300; western IE via Danube/Corded Ware pathways; Indo-Iranian via Sintashta).

### Horse domestication and riding

With Brown, he claims multi-decade research on where and when people began keeping tamed herds; horseback riding began in the steppes long before chariots, despite chariotry preceding cavalry in organized states. Sequence he insists on: (1) symbolic/dietary horse keeping possibly from ~4800 BCE; (2) riding (bit control from behind) evidenced at Botai-Tersek ~3700–3500 BCE and likely earlier in Pontic-Caspian; (3) specialized breeds/size variability ~2500 BCE; (4) effective mounted archery/cavalry only ~1000 BCE. Developed archaeological [[bit-wear-analysis]] from 1985 (Clayton fluoroscopy → P₂ focus; SEM; organic-bit riding experiments; Ukrainian and Kazakh collections). Published Dereivka bit wear 1991 and retracted it 2000 after AMS redating of the cult stallion to Scythian intrusion — methodological self-correction. Positive Botai-Tersek results retained. Rejects Hun/Mongol militarized-nomad analogies for the Eneolithic: Bronze Age bows too long for effective mounted archery; war bands not armies; seasonal tribal raiding more like neighborhood gangs able to disrupt harvests.

### Language-shift and frontier models

Not pure conquest: [[elite-recruitment]] (preferred over Renfrew's "élite dominance") and **franchising** metaphor for IE expansion — open patron-client hierarchies, guest-host (*ghos-ti-*), oath contracts (*h1óitos*), public feast/praise-poetry theater. Persistent robust material-culture frontiers as rare but critical correlates of language borders; two conditions for persistence: large-scale ecotones and long-distance migrant stop-lines. Migration theory beyond overpopulation: push/pull, scouts, chain migration, leapfrogging, charter groups. Samara Valley Project: Bronze Age steppe pastoralism self-sustaining without cultivated grain or Iron Age states (Peschanyi Dol camps; Krasnosamarskoe midwinter dog sacrifice).

### Radiocarbon hygiene

All BCE dates in the 2007 book are calibrated (OxCal); flags freshwater-reservoir effect from fish-heavy steppe diets (high ¹⁵N) that can make human-bone dates centuries too old; uses provisional Bonsall–Cook-style corrections with in-text warnings.

### Methodological stance

Affirms "pots are not people" while arguing language correlates with material culture at long-lasting **bundles** of opposed customs, not single artifact types. Uses "horizon" not ethnicized culture labels for Yamnaya. Acknowledges bad history of archaeology–linguistics fusion (raw material for bigotry) but argues modern density of archaeological facts constrains narrative; 19th-c. Aryan fantasies (e.g. Madison Grant) were sparse-linguistic + racist + social Darwinist, not archaeological. "A convincing narrative about the speakers of Proto-Indo-European must today be pegged to a vast array of archaeological facts."

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Steppe-homeland line:** engages [[gimbutas-marija|Marija Gimbutas]] (Kurgan package refined and narrowed, not simply repeated; rejects broad "Kurgan culture") and [[mallory-j-p|J. P. Mallory]] (*[[mallory-in-search-of-the-indo-europeans-1989|In Search of the Indo-Europeans]]* 1989 now primary-ingested — credited with Gimbutas as thirty-year Pontic-Caspian advocates; Anthony narrows Yamnaya core, adds wool/wheel termini and franchising beyond Mallory’s 1989 survey).
- **Deep prehistory interlocutors:** [[childe-v-gordon|V. Gordon Childe]] (European Neolithic diversity against pan-European Neolithic PIE); [[sherratt-andrew|Andrew Sherratt]] ([[secondary-products-revolution]] — wool sheep + wagons fragment retained; Near Eastern package thesis "quickly fell apart").
- **Rival homeland:** [[renfrew-colin|Colin Renfrew]]'s [[anatolian-hypothesis]] / first-farming dispersal — principal alternative Anthony targets; accepts Renfrew's "elite dominance" *label* as a historical lesson while renaming/retooling the mechanism and rejecting Anatolian farming as full PIE homeland.
- **Frontier and clientage:** Fredrik Barth (Pathan–Baluch; ethnicity at boundaries); Eric Wolf (fonts of tradition); Labov, Nettle & Hill on language territories and ecotones; Johanna Nichols on the steppes as linguistic spread zone.
- **Soviet/post-Soviet steppe archaeology:** Telegin (Sredni Stog), Merpert (Yamnaya origins), Rassamakin (debated splits), Zaibert/Olsen (Botai), and the post-1991 CIS radiocarbon boom Anthony synthesizes after Iron Curtain lifting.
- **Veterinary/experimental collaborators:** Hilary Clayton; Dorcas Brown; SUNY Cobleskill riding program.
- **Later reweighting:** aDNA work ([[reich-david]]; as of Reich 2018) reweights demography in a steppe-compatible direction without replacing Anthony's linguistic-archaeological argument.

Affiliated methodologically with both [[culture-historical-archaeology]] (horizons, culture definition, technological style) and [[processual-archaeology]] (migration models, isotope diet, experimental middle-range work on bits) without belonging to a single school label.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Written **pre-2015 aDNA**; demographic scale of steppe expansion into Corded Ware Europe is better constrained after Haak/Allentoft/Reich (as of Reich 2018). Layer genetics from later sources; do not attribute aDNA claims to Anthony 2007.
- Reservoir-effect corrections for human-bone dates are Anthony's provisional scale — treat absolute cemetery chronologies as method-dependent.
- Homeland and branch-order details remain interpretive; four-way non-identity applies throughout.
- Dereivka retraction shows early bit-wear claims can fail dating control; Botai riding conclusions remain contested among zooarchaeologists (Levine et al. vs Olsen/Anthony).
- Franchise-not-invasion rhetoric sits in tension with violent Old Europe and raid-ideology chapters — critics may see selective soft-pedaling of demic/violent pathways.
- Sintashta-as-Indo-Iranian-crucible and steppe chariot priority over Near East are author arguments, not field consensus (Anthony himself notes gauge/function debates).
