# Howard Winant

American sociologist of race and racism, co-author with [[omi-michael|Michael Omi]] of *[[omi-winant-racial-formation-2015|Racial Formation in the United States]]* (1986; 3rd ed. 2015).

## Overview

Winant co-developed [[racial-formation-theory]], treating race as a fundamental axis of social organization continually made and remade through linked structural and cultural [[racial-project|racial projects]].

## Contributions

- Co-author of racial formation framework; related later work on global racial politics (*The World Is a Ghetto* — not yet ingested).
- Analysis of postwar U.S. racial trajectory: democratic “Great Transformation,” racial reaction, and colorblind hegemony.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Long collaboration with Omi; engages Gramsci, Du Bois, and comparative racial theory. Training genealogy not established from this source.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Program reception and critiques recorded on [[racial-formation-theory]].

Winant contributed to the 1995 *Gender & Society* symposium on West and Fenstermaker's
"Doing Difference" (reprinted in [[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]]).
In that exchange he treated the paper as a promising extension of ethnomethodology toward
hegemony and contradictory identity, while arguing that structure remains too thin and that
neither classic ethnomethodology nor conceded actor autonomy adequately explains oppositional
difference and change — position filed on [[doing-difference-debate]].

## Historiography

With Omi, among the most-cited U.S. race theorists of the late 20th century; third edition updates for neoliberalism, colorblindness, and the Obama years.
