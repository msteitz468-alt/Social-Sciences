# Antonio Gramsci

> Italian Marxist, co-founder and leader of the PCI, imprisoned by Fascism from 1926; author of the *Quaderni del carcere* (1929–1935). Primary-ingested here via *[[gramsci-selections-from-prison-notebooks-1971|Selections from the Prison Notebooks]]* (Hoare & Nowell Smith 1971). Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/gramsci-antonio|Thinkers Hub]].

## Overview

Antonio Gramsci (1891–1937) reconceived Marxist politics around **[[hegemony]]** — the organisation of consent and intellectual–moral leadership through [[civil-society]], "protected by the armour of coercion" — rather than around coercion or economic determination alone. Written under prison censorship in fragmentary notebooks, his concepts ([[organic-intellectuals]], [[passive-revolution]], [[war-of-position]], [[historical-bloc]], [[philosophy-of-praxis]], [[organic-crisis]], [[subaltern]]) became charter materials for [[western-marxism]], cultural studies, and large stretches of political anthropology and sociology.

## Contributions

- **[[hegemony]]** — spontaneous consent + prestige of the dominant group; State = political society + civil society; factory-born hegemony in Americanism; crisis when ruling class is only "dominant," not "leading."
- **[[organic-intellectuals]]** — every fundamental class creates intellectuals who give it homogeneity and direction; "all men are intellectuals… but not all… have the function of intellectuals."
- **[[passive-revolution]]** — revolution-restoration from above (Risorgimento, Europe, fascism/Americanism as open questions).
- **[[war-of-position]]** vs war of manoeuvre — Western civil-society "trenches" vs Eastern gelatinous civil society (1917).
- **[[philosophy-of-praxis]]** — Marxism as integral historicist philosophy; anti-economism; anti-Bukharin "popular sociology."
- **[[subaltern]]** classes — methodological criteria for fragmentary history under the State.
- **"Americanism and Fordism"** — new type of worker; high wages + force; "**Hegemony here is born in the factory**" → charter for [[burawoy-michael|Burawoy]]'s *[[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-1979|Manufacturing Consent]]*.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Formation (editorial biography, Hoare 1971):** Sardinia; Turin University (philology; with Togliatti); influences include Labriola (philosophy of praxis), Croce (later target), Salvemini (Southern Question), Pastore; *Ordine Nuovo* nucleus with Tasca, Terracini, Togliatti.
- **Political rivals inside Italian socialism/communism:** Bordiga (left/sectarian maximalism, as Hoare frames the dispute); Tasca (right/"liquidationism"); Comintern pressures; Gramsci's leadership line as synthesis against both, on Hoare's account.
- **Western Marxism co-originator** with [[lukacs-georg|Lukács]] (*[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|HCC]]* 1923) — parallel anti-scientistic, culture-conscious Marxisms; different problematics (reification/totality vs hegemony/civil society).
- **Transmission:** Togliatti/PCI editions; 1971 English selection; Raymond Williams, Stuart Hall; [[comaroff-jean|Comaroffs]] into anthropology; Subaltern Studies / [[spivak-gayatri-chakravorty|Spivak]] reworkings of *subalterno*.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Notorious difficulty of establishing "what Gramsci meant" — fragmentary form + censorship euphemism + partisan editions.
- Charges of culturalism (underplaying political society/coercion) vs charges of still-too-Leninist party theory — both track selective reading.
- Eurocommunist vs revolutionary-left battles over [[war-of-position]] as strategy.
- Postcolonial and feminist expansions of subaltern/hegemony go beyond notebook scope — attribute the extensions.
- **Scott's inversion (1990):** [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]], in *[[scott-domination-arts-resistance-1990|Domination and the Arts of Resistance]]*, argued the hegemony thesis mislocates the constraint on subordinates — they are freer at the level of thought (offstage, in the [[hidden-transcript]]) and more constrained at the level of action — and that neither thick nor thin readings of consent survive the record of subordinate dissent and rebellion. Positions filed at [[hegemony-resistance-debate]].
