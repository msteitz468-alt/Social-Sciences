# Irven DeVore (1934–2014)

American anthropologist and primatologist, co-organizer (with [[lee-richard-b|Richard B.
Lee]]) of the 1966 *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]* symposium and
co-editor of its volume.

## Overview

*(Findings, from Man the Hunter 1968.)* DeVore trained under [[washburn-sherwood|Sherwood
Washburn]] and made his early reputation in baboon field studies before turning, with Lee, to
hunter-gatherers; he was among the workers who conducted !Kung fieldwork presented at the
symposium. In the volume's closing discussion he framed the meeting as a needed
"rapprochement" between archaeology and social anthropology and cautioned against synthetic
"culture type" generalizations, while affirming the value of generalizations that "apply to
most hunter-gatherers most of the time."

## Contributions

- **Co-organizing and editing *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]* (1968)** —
  the field-defining collection of hunter-gatherer studies.
- **Primate behavioral ecology** — baboon field studies (with Washburn) that helped make
  primate social behavior a comparative resource for reconstructing human evolution (a move
  the [[hunting-hypothesis|hunting hypothesis]] relied on).
- **Skeptic of the "cultural type."** In discussion he warned that "synthetic statements
  concerning hunter-gatherers as a 'culture type' may now seem further away than before."

## Intellectual Genealogy

A student of [[washburn-sherwood|Washburn]]'s in the physical-anthropology/primatology line,
DeVore partnered with the ecological-materialist [[lee-richard-b|Lee]] to bridge biological
and cultural anthropology in the study of foragers. Later students at Harvard carried the
program into human behavioral ecology (documented training relations not in the ingested
source; `trained` left `[[unknown]]`).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Attributed.)* As co-editor, DeVore shares in the volume's mixed legacy: it launched
hunter-gatherer studies but also the androcentric "Man the Hunter" framing later criticized
(see [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]]). His own contribution leaned toward caution
about over-general "types."

## Historiography

DeVore is a standard reference for the founding of hunter-gatherer studies and for postwar
primatology. A fuller treatment awaits ingestion of his primatological work and the
Harvard behavioral-ecology record.
