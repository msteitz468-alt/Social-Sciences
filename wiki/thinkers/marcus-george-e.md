# George E. Marcus

American cultural anthropologist (b. 1946); co-editor with [[clifford-james|James Clifford]] of *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* (1986); co-author with [[fischer-michael-m-j|Michael M. J. Fischer]] of *[[marcus-fischer-anthropology-as-cultural-critique-1986|Anthropology as Cultural Critique]]* (1986). Initiator/organizer of the Santa Fe SAR Advanced Seminar that produced *Writing Culture*; professor at Rice University at the time of these volumes; editor of the journal *Cultural Anthropology*.

## Overview

Marcus is a principal organizer of the Critical-Turn "experimental moment" in ethnographic writing. In *Writing Culture* he presses multi-locale, world-system embedding and critiques salvage/redemptive historicizing tropes. In *Anthropology as Cultural Critique* he and Fischer restate anthropology's dual promise (document diversity + cultural critique at home), survey experimental ethnography, and theorize [[repatriation-of-anthropology|repatriation]] and two techniques of [[defamiliarization]].

## Contributions

*(Attributed; from WC 1986 and ACC 1986.)*

- Co-organization of the SAR seminar and *Writing Culture* Afterword on careers and writing (dissertation conservatism vs post-dissertation experiment; textualization orality→inscription; literary therapy stakes).
- **WC chapter:** salvage vs redemptive modes as ahistorical historicizing; multi-locale ethnography (ideal without market/capitalism ethnographic exemplars yet) vs strategically situated single-locale; extended Willis *Learning to Labour* reading (oppositional school culture → factory accommodation; Ethnography/Analysis split).
- With Fischer: dual-promise program; [[crisis-of-representation]]; [[experimental-moment]]; [[knowable-communities]] (via Williams); political-economy embedding of local ethnography; [[repatriation-of-anthropology]]; [[epistemological-critique]] and [[cross-cultural-juxtaposition]] as techniques.
- Appendix work-in-progress (ACC): Galveston business dynasties triangulated with Tonga/Tallensi materials ("right stuff" elite culture).

### From *Ethnography Through Thick and Thin* (1998; primary-ingested)

*(All attributed; essays 1980–1997 with a 1998 Introduction — see [[marcus-ethnography-thick-and-thin-1998]].)*

- The **[[multi-sited-ethnography]]** program in full: the multi-locale proposal (1989's "Imagining the Whole" against the "fiction of the whole" — the ulterior macro-context guaranteeing ethnographic facticity; the Bakhtinian **chronotope shift** "from a place, to places"); the 1991 six "requirements" for modernist ethnography (problematize space/time/voice; dialogic exegesis; bifocality via [[fabian-johannes|Fabian]]'s coevalness; critical juxtaposition); the canonical 1995 statement with the six tracking strategies; the **multi-sited research imaginary** distinguished from literal multi-sited fieldwork.
- **[[complicity]]** replacing rapport (1997), through the genealogy of Geertz's cockfight irony, Clifford's collaborative ideal, and Rosaldo's [[imperialist-nostalgia]]; the **circumstantial activist** persona; explicit distancing from Nader's "studying up" for elite ethnography (POSITION).
- **Typology of reflexivity ideologies** (1994): null/confessional; Bourdieusian-methodological (with [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s recorded "nihilistic… narcissistic self-criticism" polemic against Marcus, and Marcus's counter that Bourdieu is "tone-deaf" to subjective self-criticism); locational — "politics of location," his preferred variant, generative of "messy texts"; feminist positioning (Haraway as fullest version). Essential vs ideological reflexivity (via Graham Watson). See [[crisis-of-representation]].
- **Empirical traces**: Tongan elites on the world-system periphery (fieldwork 1972–75/78; see [[tonga]]); dynastic fortunes as an "unseen world" (Kaluli *mama* analogy via Schieffelin/Feld — self-flagged as ethnographic analogy; critique of Marx's [[commodity-fetishism]] as discouraging grounded study of mystification); eccentricity as a viable, non-clinical dynastic subject-position.
- **Professional-culture diagnoses** (1997): the first/second-projects career pattern (culture-area dissertation → post-tenure dispersed phenomena); the "loss of a discursive community" ("nothing like the old debates of kinship" — muted uptake of the [[sahlins-obeyesekere-cook-debate]] as his index); anthropology and cultural studies as adjacent power/knowledges; "oblique critique" worked through feminist economics.
- **Documentary facts**: Institute for Advanced Study year 1982–83 (invited via Geertz's interest in Marcus & Cushman 1982, "Ethnographies as Texts"), where the *Writing Culture* seminar was planned with Clifford and *Anthropology as Cultural Critique* drafted with Fischer; Rice graduate pedagogy (the five-problem advice program; Villaveces-Izquierdo's Colombia dissertation as worked multi-sited example).
