# Paul S. Martin

## Overview

Paul S. Martin (1928–2020) was an American geoscientist and Quaternary
paleoecologist at the University of Arizona, best known for the **Pleistocene
overkill hypothesis**: the argument that the wave of late-Pleistocene
extinctions of large mammals in the Americas was driven primarily by human
hunting as [[clovis-culture|Clovis]] foragers spread rapidly across a continent
of prey with no prior experience of humans. Densified from
[[fagan-great-journey-1987|Fagan]] and, principally, [[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|Meltzer 2009]] Ch. 8 (radiocarbon BP as Meltzer uses).

## Contributions

### Overkill / blitzkrieg model (as of Meltzer 2009)

- Attributed late Pleistocene extinctions **solely to human hunting**. Core formula Meltzer quotes: “Large mammals disappeared not because they lost their food supply but because they became one.” Mostly megafauna (≥~45 kg / ~100 lb); small Aztlan rabbit and diminutive pronghorn still “large enough”; smaller prey of no interest (“catch-and-release”) (C-6-030).
- **Scenario numbers:** ~100 Clovis hunters exit southern [[ice-free-corridor]] just over 11,500 BP into naive fauna; 1-in-4 hunters bag trophy/week; local populations decimated; slow breeders can’t replace even ~⅓ slaughter; human population doubles every 20 years (**3.4%/yr** — Pitcairn/Bounty analogy); killing wave **16 km/yr**; within **350 years** ~**600,000** people reach Gulf of Mexico; **1,200 years** to Tierra del Fuego; wake: tens of thousands of fluted points, **>100 million** rotting herbivore carcasses; carnivores boom then bust (C-6-031; also Fagan layer).
- Fused Clovis-first peopling with extinction timing — overkill requires no pre-Clovis humans else defensive behaviors or earlier extinctions; Clovis is “the time of unmistakable appearance of paleoindian hunters using distinctive projectile points” and only then evidence of predation on now-extinct large mammals (C-6-033–034).
- **On kill-site scarcity:** scarcity proves overkill — few centuries per area → poor preservation odds; tidy Clovis retrieved points from carcasses (C-6-039).
- Rejected near-time asteroid/space-rock causation for extinctions (*Twilight of the Mammoths*) (C-6-049).
- Overkill celebrity as conservation parable / moral homily (C-6-081, C-6-092).

### Made human arrival causal

Made the arrival of humans a *causal* rather than merely coincidental factor in
the disappearance of mammoth, mastodon, horse, and other genera — the central
claim disputed in the [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Martin's model became the pole around which the extinction debate organized:
climate-change and multi-causal explanations (Graham, Lundelius, Guthrie) and the
[[grayson-donald|Grayson]]–[[meltzer-david|Meltzer]] “Requiem for North American Overkill”
defined themselves against it. Early 1980s symposium poll: overkill-alone nearly only Martin’s hand (C-6-042). Disciplinary pattern Meltzer reports: archaeologists/vertebrate paleontologists tend climate; ecologists/zoologists tend humans.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Recorded as positions on [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]] (Meltzer 2009 densification):

- **Demography/foraging:** HG growth generally <~1.5%/yr; army-like wave not forager-like; landscape knowledge demands; prey learn predators swiftly; Monte Verde pre-Clovis undercuts timing (C-6-032–033).
- **Kill-site census (Grayson & Meltzer):** 76 alleged → **14** secure; **only mammoth + mastodon** of 35 extinct genera; horses abundant in fossil record with no secure kills (C-6-037–038).
- **Epistemology:** hypothesis that predicts lack of evidence because evidence lacking; double standard vs abundant NZ moa kills Martin cites, and vs bison surviving 11,000 years of intensive hunting (C-6-039–041).
- **Consistency:** Martin’s pre-Clovis bar (“absence… has limits”) applied by critics to overkill’s missing genera (C-6-041).
- **Fiedel & Gary Haynes defense** of overkill against “Requiem” (14 sites “phenomenally rich”; preservation thresholds) — Meltzer replies focus remains two genera only (C-6-043–047).
- **Meltzer’s thesis:** overkill wrong as sole primary cause; climate explanations still need genus-level work (C-6-055). Coup de grâce hunting on already-stressed herds remains open without blitzkrieg (C-6-054).

The dispute remains open; see debate page. Do not promote Meltzer’s verdict as wiki voice.
