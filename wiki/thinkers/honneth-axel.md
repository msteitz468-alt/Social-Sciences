# Axel Honneth

## Overview

Axel Honneth is a German critical theorist (Frankfurt Institut) whose recognition theory is a major contemporary Critical Theory program. This page is grounded primarily in his contributions to *[[fraser-honneth-redistribution-or-recognition-2003|Redistribution or Recognition?]]* (2003) with [[fraser-nancy|Nancy Fraser]]. Full *Struggle for Recognition* monograph is not yet primary-ingested. Debate: [[redistribution-recognition-debate]].

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Honneth in the 2003 exchange.)*

- **Recognition as master frame:** experiences of social injustice are rooted in withheld recognition; distribution conflicts are a species of recognition conflict under capitalism’s recognition order.
- **Capitalist recognition order** and conflicts over distribution (labor, achievement, legal respect, love/care spheres as developed in his broader program — detailed in this exchange as he applies it).
- Against Fraser: resists irreducible dualism of redistribution vs recognition; prioritizes moral-psychological and social-integration dimensions of Critical Theory.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Frankfurt Critical Theory; Hegelian recognition; dialogue with Habermas; exchange with Fraser. Hans Joas named as long-term interlocutor in the book’s dedication.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Fraser’s rejoinder charges reduction of political sociology to moral psychology and of capitalism to a recognition order. Later literature continues the dualism vs monism dispute.

## Historiography

The 2003 exchange is the standard paired presentation of Honneth vs Fraser for teaching.
