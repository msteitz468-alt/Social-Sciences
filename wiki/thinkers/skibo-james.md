# James M. Skibo

American archaeologist; collaborator of [[schiffer-michael-b|Michael B. Schiffer]] on experimental and technological-change research within [[behavioral-archaeology|behavioral archaeology]].

## Overview

Known in this vault primarily as coauthor of the provisional framework for explaining technological change (Schiffer and Skibo 1987; reprinted as Ch. 22 of *[[schiffer-behavioral-archaeology-first-principles-1995|Behavioral Archaeology: First Principles]]*) and as a full-manuscript reader of Schiffer’s 1987 formation-processes synthesis.

## Contributions

- **Performance characteristics / performance matrix** — with Schiffer: technical choices → formal properties → performance characteristics (heating effectiveness, impact resistance, thermal shock, abrasion, portability, ease of manufacture/maintenance); polar effects force design compromises; performance matrices compare weightings across artifact life-history activities to explain replacement of forms (attributed, Schiffer and Skibo 1987/1995 Ch. 22).
- **Experimental program** on Archaic–Woodland fiber- vs mineral-tempered pottery (with Schiffer and Reid): magnitude of temper effects on manufacture, cooling, heating, portability, impact, thermal shock, and abrasion — concluding Late Archaic designs weighted ease of manufacture and portability, later Woodland designs heating effectiveness and longer use-lives (attributed, Schiffer and Skibo 1987; Skibo, Schiffer, and Reid 1989).
- Later people-and-things synthesis with Schiffer (2008; outstanding as primary ingest).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Behavioral archaeology / Arizona experimental tradition; coauthor network with Schiffer (and K. C. Reid on fiber-temper preservational experiments).
