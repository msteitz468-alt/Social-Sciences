# Don H. Zimmerman

U.S. sociologist and ethnomethodologist; co-author with [[west-candace|Candace West]] of
"Doing Gender" (1987), the founding statement of the [[doing-gender]] program. Also
collaborated on early West–Fenstermaker–Zimmerman extensions on gender inequality
([[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]], Ch. 2).

## Overview

Zimmerman's contribution anchors the program in ethnomethodological resources (membership
categorization, accountability, the incompleteness of rule manuals) rather than role theory
or trait psychology.

## Contributions

- Co-formulator of sex / [[sex-category]] / gender and of gender as accountable accomplishment
- Link between conversation/ethnomethodological sociology and feminist gender theory

## Intellectual Genealogy

[[ethnomethodology]] circle (named among Garfinkel's early collaborators on the
[[ethnomethodology]] page); collaboration with [[west-candace|West]] (and with Wieder on
related ethnomethodological work, as cited in the 1987 article). Co-author of the Ch. 2
gender-inequality extension with Fenstermaker and West.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Shared program critiques under [[doing-difference-debate]] and later elaborations; Zimmerman
is less central to the race/class extension papers than West and Fenstermaker.
