# Edward Hallett Carr

## Overview

British historian and former diplomat whose multi-volume *History of Soviet Russia* (1950–1978: *The Bolshevik Revolution, 1917–1923*; *The Interregnum, 1923–1924*; *Socialism in One Country, 1924–1926*; *Foundations of a Planned Economy, 1926–1929*) became a standard Anglophone institutional narrative of the early Soviet regime. The short synthesis *[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979|The Russian Revolution: From Lenin to Stalin, 1917–1929]]* (Free Press 1979) distills that research for general readers — "substantially a new composition," without footnotes. Also author of *What Is History?* (1961) — not yet ingested.

## Contributions

From the 1979 short history (all **Carr attributed**):

- **Periodization:** 1917–1929 as embryo of later Soviet history; personifying a transition from "the Russian revolution of Lenin to the Russian revolution of Stalin" as useful over-simplification.
- **Structural dualisms:** Two worlds (capitalist vs revolutionary); dual power as dispersal of authority; War Communism's two elements (centralization that endured vs anti-market flight that NEP rejected); market vs managed economy through the 1920s ([[scissors-crisis]]).
- **International:** [[comintern]] as Russian-harnessed international; diplomacy–revolution tension; China and western failures.
- **Climax:** Forced industrialization and [[collectivization]] as **revolution from above**; Stalin as "most ruthless despot Russia had known since Peter" and also "a great westernizer" (Peter the Great analogy).
- **Verdict:** Hybrid, flawed, ambiguous revolution that nonetheless produced the "material, economic half" of socialism and world-historical repercussions especially in the non-capitalist world — rejecting pure [[state-capitalism]] labels for the planned economy.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Standard critiques of Carr's Soviet history (not fully ingested as independent sources here) include under-weighting terror and ideology relative to structural economic constraint, and a "view from the Kremlin" institutional focus. Record as positions when counter-histories (Figes, Conquest tradition, archival revisionism beyond Service) are ingested; do not adjudicate on this page.
