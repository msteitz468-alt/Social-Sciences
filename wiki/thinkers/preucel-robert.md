# Robert W. Preucel

Network summary — enriched from [[preucel-mrozowski-contemporary-archaeology-2010]].

## Overview

Robert W. Preucel is an American archaeologist of the Pueblo Southwest and the principal architect
of a **semiotic, pragmatist archaeology**. He co-edited both editions of *Contemporary Archaeology
in Theory* (the first, 1996, with Ian Hodder; the second, 2010, with Stephen Mrozowski, subtitled
*The New Pragmatism*), and is a leading advocate of applying Charles Sanders Peirce's semiotics to
archaeological interpretation.

## Contributions

Preucel's central move is to treat archaeological interpretation as an **inherently semiotic
process** — sign production in an endless Peircean chain, where methods like analogy and
middle-range theory are themselves signs (with Alexander Bauer, "Archaeological Pragmatics," 2001).
In *Archaeological Semiotics* (2006) he allied archaeology with the Chicago-school "pragmatic
anthropology" of indexicality, arguing a Peircean semiotic is more general than the Saussurean model
underwriting structuralism because it incorporates language, practice, and material culture alike.
With Mrozowski he framed the **[[archaeological-pragmatism|"new pragmatism"]]** — archaeology as
irreducibly both science and socially engaged practice. See [[pragmatism]].

Draws on American [[pragmatism]] (Peirce, Dewey, Rorty) and on linguistic-anthropological semiotics

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The pragmatist/semiotic program is contested as either a relabeling of post-processualism or a
genuinely distinct epistemology; the depth of the semiotic machinery lives in *Archaeological
Semiotics* (2006), not yet ingested as a monograph. Recorded here from a single source.
