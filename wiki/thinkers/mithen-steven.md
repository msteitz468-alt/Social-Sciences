# Steven Mithen

British prehistoric archaeologist and cognitive archaeologist. Charter cognitive synthesis: *[[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996|The Prehistory of the Mind]]* (1996), proposing the [[cognitive-fluidity]] model. Major public world-prehistory narrative: *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice: A Global Human History 20,000–5000 BC]]* (2003). Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/mithen-steven|Thinkers Hub]].

## Overview

Mithen works at the intersection of Palaeolithic–Mesolithic archaeology, evolutionary psychology, and cognitive science, then scales that mind-model into a **global Holocene narrative**. In the 1996 preface he identifies formative events (Fodor after a 1988 prompt by Sir John Lyons; 1994 Santa Barbara dinner with Cosmides, Tooby, and Jochim; draft readers including Richard Bradley, Dick Byrne, and Clive Gamble). *Thoughtful Foragers* (1990) applied cognitive-decision models to Mesolithic hunter-gatherers. *After the Ice* (2003), written largely on a British Academy Research Readership (from Oct 2001) at the University of Reading, assumes a creatively modern mind by ~50 kya and asks what happened next under post-LGM global warming (attributed, Mithen 2003 preface/ch.1).

## Contributions

- **[[cognitive-fluidity]]** — three-phase cathedral model of mind evolution (generalized → specialized/isolated → fluid); archaeological diagnosis of phases (attributed, Mithen 1996).
- **[[specialized-intelligences]]** / **[[cathedral-of-the-mind]]** — social, natural-history, technical, and linguistic “chapels.”
- Integration of Palaeolithic material culture with modularity / EP frameworks inside [[cognitive-archaeology]].
- **Agricultural epilogue (1996) → global history (2003):** farming as consequence of fluid minds × Holocene opportunity in the 1996 Epilogue; *After the Ice* expands this into a **continent-contrast** account of the 20,000–5000 BC transition (attributed, Mithen 2003).
- **Narrative method:** modern John Lubbock time-traveller device (echoing Victorian John Lubbock’s *Prehistoric Times*, 1865) to write lived-experience history without abandoning endnote apparatus (attributed, Mithen 2003).
- Densifies [[complex-hunter-gatherers]], [[natufian-culture]], [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]], multi-pathway [[domestication]], and post-glacial [[mesolithic]] diversity across all inhabited continents.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Modular-to-fluid model debated within cognitive archaeology (sealed Early Human domains; European “big bang” bias; African MSA symbolism). *After the Ice* inherits early-2000s dating, pre-aDNA genetics, and peopling models — treat sequences as **as of Mithen 2003**. The Lubbock device has been praised for accessibility and flagged as literary anachronism (Renfrew TLS; Scotsman — reception quotes on source apparatus). Record further critiques as they enter the collection; do not adjudicate here.

## Historiography

Johnson situates Mithen in the cognitive-evolutionary strand of [[cognitive-archaeology]] ([[johnson-archaeological-theory-2011]]). Renfrew & Bahn cite *Prehistory of the Mind*; Renfrew’s TLS review of *After the Ice* treats it as a major world-archaeology synthesis. **Thinkers Hub:** [[hubs/thinkers/mithen-steven]] built on the two ingested monographs (1996 + 2003); *Thoughtful Foragers* still outstanding for Mesolithic decision-model depth.
