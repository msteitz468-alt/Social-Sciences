# Judith T. Irvine

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist (University of Michigan); major architect of the [[language-ideology]] program. With [[gal-susan|Susan Gal]], author of “Language Ideology and Linguistic Differentiation” ([[irvine-gal-language-ideology-linguistic-differentiation-2000|2000]]) in [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language*]] — the essay that crystallizes [[iconization]], [[fractal-recursivity]], and [[erasure-semiotic|erasure]] as analytic processes. Her 1989 definition of language ideology (cultural system of ideas about social and linguistic relationships, with moral and political interests) is one of two charter definitions of the program (alongside [[silverstein-michael|Silverstein]] 1979).

## Contributions

- **Semiotic toolkit (with Gal, 2000).** Three processes by which ideologies construct linguistic differentiation; applications to language change, scholarly description, and political authorization. Africa and Europe as primary comparative sites.
- **Nguni *hlonipha* / clicks case.** Draws on Herbert 1990 and her own 1992 work: clicks enter Nguni via respect register as icons of foreignness; Bantu/Khoi contrast recurs within Nguni; European and folk erasures of political *hlonipha* and Khoi–Nguni complexity.
- **Senegal linguistic description.** 19th-c. European maps, grammars, and race-conquest narratives of Fula, Wolof, Sereer, and Cangin as long-lasting “culture of linguistics” of the region; interaction of European and African ideologies.
- **Speech-community critique (with Gal).** Shift attention from community as consensus unit to ideologically mediated **differentiation**, boundaries, and conflict (Implications section of 2000 essay).
- Earlier ethnography-of-speaking work (Wolof) links to the Hymes program ([[bauman-sherzer-explorations-ethnography-speaking-1974]] network).

## Intellectual Genealogy

US linguistic anthropology; collaboration with Gal; SAR 1994 seminar cohort (Kroskrity, Silverstein, Schieffelin, Woolard, Bauman, Briggs, et al.). Interlocutor with Silverstein on ideology and indexicality; cites Peirce, Barth on ethnicity, Labov on Martha’s Vineyard as ideologically mediated change.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The three-process toolkit is widely adopted; risk of checklist application noted on concept pages. The 2000 cases rely on secondary historical linguistics and ethnography (Herbert, Lunt, Friedman, 19th-c. Senegal grammars) as well as Irvine’s Senegal expertise — case findings are Irvine & Gal’s synthesis, not new fieldwork reports.
