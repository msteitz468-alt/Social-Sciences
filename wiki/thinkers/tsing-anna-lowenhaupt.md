# Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing

## Overview

US cultural anthropologist (b. 1952), long associated with the University of California, Santa Cruz (and later multi-sited institutional affiliations including Aarhus). Major figure in the anthropology of globalization, environmental politics, multi-species ethnography, and life in capitalist ruins. This page densifies from two ingested monographs: *[[tsing-friction-2005|Friction]]* (2005) and *[[tsing-mushroom-2015|The Mushroom at the End of the World]]* (2015). *In the Realm of the Diamond Queen* (1993) is not yet ingested.

## Contributions

### Ethnography of global connection (*Friction*, 2005)

Landmark multi-locale study ([[tsing-friction-study]]) arguing that global connection works through [[friction]] — awkward, unequal, creative and destructive engagement of difference — not frictionless flow. Program vocabulary:

| Concept | One-line (attributed) |
|---|---|
| [[friction]] | Sticky practical encounters that enable and interrupt global power |
| [[capitalist-frontier]] | Projects that make deregulated extractive landscapes and resources |
| [[economy-of-appearances]] | Speculative capital’s dependence on spectacular performance |
| [[scale-making]] | How local/national/global levels are claimed and produced |
| [[traveling-universals]] | Engaged universals that travel only through difference |

Empirical nodes: [[meratus-dayaks|Meratus]] social-natural forests; Indonesian nature-lovers (*pecinta alam*); New Order and post-1998 environmental/*adat* movements; Japan–Indonesia timber political economy; Bre-X and frontier speculation.

### Multi-species salvage capitalism (*Mushroom*, 2015)

Landmark multi-locale multi-species study ([[tsing-mushroom-study]]) following matsutake through Oregon, Japan, China, Finland, and Canada. Program vocabulary:

| Concept | One-line (attributed) |
|---|---|
| [[precarity]] | Life without the promise of stability; vulnerability to others as earthwide condition |
| [[salvage-accumulation]] | Lead firms amass capital by appropriating value produced without capitalist control |
| [[arts-of-noticing]] | Attention practices that make patchy multispecies survival visible |
| [[contamination-as-collaboration]] | Transformative encounter across difference as ordinary condition of survival |
| [[patchiness]] | Mosaic of open-ended assemblages against scalable uniformity |
| [[unintentional-design]] | Patterned multi-species landscapes without a single planner |

Empirical nodes: [[open-ticket-oregon|Open Ticket]] freedom assemblages; Japan–US gift/commodity matsutake chains; pine–fungus disturbance histories; satoyama restoration; science as translation.

### Earlier work (not yet ingested)

- *In the Realm of the Diamond Queen* (1993) — Meratus ethnography of gender, marginality, and state; *Friction* extends its network argument.

## Intellectual Genealogy

*Friction* acknowledgements record conversations with [[appadurai-arjun|Arjun Appadurai]], [[clifford-james|James Clifford]], [[haraway-donna|Donna Haraway]], James Scott, Ann Stoler, [[rosaldo-renato|Renato Rosaldo]], Nancy Peluso, and others. Tsing and Haraway are close interlocutors in the [[multispecies-studies]] program: Haraway draws on Tsing's "arts of living on a damaged planet" in *[[haraway-staying-with-the-trouble-2016|Staying with the Trouble]]* (2016), and both were among the participants who coined [[plantationocene|Plantationocene]] at Aarhus in 2014. *Mushroom* emerges from the Matsutake Worlds Research Group (Timothy Choy, Lieba Faier, Elaine Gan, Michael Hathaway, Miyako Inoue, Shiho Satsuka, Tsing) and dialogues with feminist STS, political ecology, and (critically) Gibson-Graham and Hardt/Negri. Mentors/students not fully reconstructed from these sources.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*Friction* and *Mushroom* are generally treated as positive methodological charters rather than failed-replication cases. Reception themes (positions): optimism about productive misunderstanding and contaminated collaboration; lyric form vs. political-economy hardness; relation of multi-species turn to the earlier global-connection program; whether ruins analytics romanticize abandonment.
