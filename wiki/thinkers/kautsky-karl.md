# Karl Kautsky

## Overview

German-Austrian Marxist theorist, editor of *Die Neue Zeit*, and the leading doctrinal authority of the Second International — "the pope of Marxism" in the standard characterization. Primary Kautsky now includes *[[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918|The Dictatorship of the Proletariat]]* (1918), *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919|Terrorism and Communism]]* (1919), and John H. Kautsky's 1964 introduction to the former — plus Lenin's and Trotsky's hostile engagements.

## Contributions

**Terror and democracy (1919).** *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919|Terrorism and Communism]]* develops the 1918 democracy thesis into a natural history of revolutionary violence and a detailed Commune-vs-Soviet contrast; full treatment on [[red-terror]] and below.

Within ingested material: Lenin's *What Is to Be Done?* (1902) built its consciousness-from-without argument on a long approving Kautsky quotation ([[consciousness-from-without]]); *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* cites Kautsky's *Ethics* against Kantian epistemology; and [[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920|"Left-Wing" Communism]] quotes at length Kautsky's 1902 *Iskra* article "The Slavs and Revolution" — written, Lenin says, when Kautsky was "still a Marxist and not a renegade" — predicting the revolutionary centre would shift to Russia.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Codifier of the SPD's Erfurt Programme (1892), whose draft Engels criticized in an 1891 text Lenin repeatedly invokes on the state and bureaucratism ([[lenin-collected-works-vol-24-1964]]). Leader, with Haase and Ledebour, of the wartime "Centre" trend and later of the Independent Social-Democratic Party's right wing.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

From 1914 Lenin made Kautsky the personification of the "Centre": "a model of utter bankruptcy as a Marxist" (1917), leader of the trend that vacillated between social-chauvinism and internationalism; *The State and Revolution* (1917) devotes its final polemic to him, and [[lenin-left-wing-communism-1920]] charges him and Otto Bauer with failing to apply "our theory is not a dogma, but a guide to action" at the revolutionary moment ([[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]] records the substantive dispute). These are Lenin's characterizations, recorded as positions. Kautsky's own side of the exchange is now represented via *[[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918]]* and *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]]* (see below).

### In his own words (1918)

From [[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918]] (attributed): "No Socialism without democracy" — socialism and democracy are both means to the abolition of exploitation and oppression, and he stated he would "throw Socialism overboard" if emancipation were better served otherwise. His reading of Marx's 1875 "dictatorship of the proletariat": a *condition* arising from proletarian majority in a real democracy, not a form of government — the Paris Commune, Engels's own exemplar, rested on universal suffrage. His "maturity" theory made democracy the school of proletarian capacity and the gauge of class strength; minority seizure of power leads to "Jesuitism" or "Bonapartism," and civil war is socialism's "deadly foe." On Russia: "the last of middle-class, and not the first of Socialist revolutions," whose real legacy would be entrenched peasant private property and a coming peasant–worker antagonism. He predicted the party dictatorship could become personal dictatorship (quoting Lenin's own April 1918 words) and that capitalism would re-arise in Russia.

### Standing and biography (John H. Kautsky, 1964, and self-report)

Per the 1964 introduction (attributed): personal acquaintance of Marx and Engels and "principal literary executor" of both; founder-editor of *Die Neue Zeit* for thirty-five years; held in "awesome esteem" by pre-1914 Russian Marxists of all camps (Shachtman, quoted). Kautsky's own 1930/31 retrospective records decades of friendship with Axelrod, Plekhanov, Zasulich, Martov — and Lenin, Trotsky, and Luxemburg. He broke with the SPD majority over war credits, joined the USPD, and was isolated even there by his anti-Bolshevik stand; the 1919 English edition's own ILP editor distanced himself in the preface.

### The 1918 exchange (from Lenin's side)

Kautsky's *The Dictatorship of the Proletariat* (Vienna, 1918) — his critique of Bolshevik rule as a "dictatorial" departure from democratic socialism — drew Lenin's book-length reply, now ingested ([[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]). As reconstructed through Lenin's quotations (the only textual access so far): Kautsky argued the "little word" dictatorship in Marx's Gotha Programme could not license rule by force unbound by law; that the Soviets, "the most all-embracing form of proletarian organisation," should remain a class combat organisation and not become state organs; that the Constituent Assembly's dispersal destroyed "the democracy which the Russian people had won"; and that imperialism is a *policy* of industrial capitalism toward agrarian territory (against Lenin's monopoly-stage definition). Lenin's counter-positions live on [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] and [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]. Lenin also invoked Kautsky's own earlier works against him — *The Road to Power* (1909) and the Basle Manifesto (1912) forecasting revolution from war, and his 1902 "The Slavs and Revolution." **Balance note:** the propositions in this subsection are Lenin's paraphrases; Kautsky's pamphlet itself is now ingested (see "In his own words" above), which confirms the broad outline of Lenin's rendering while restoring the argumentative structure Lenin stripped away. J. H. Kautsky additionally flags translation inaccuracies in the English text Lenin's quotations pass through.

### In his own words (1919) — *Terrorism and Communism*

From *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]]* (attributed; Eng. 1920 Kerridge). Starting point: Social Democracy's attitude to Bolshevik methods. Dual structure: (1) natural history of revolutionary terrorism via French Revolution → **failure of terrorism**; (2) 1871 Paris Commune as Bolshevik-claimed prototype, which he holds was **not** a Soviet Republic, seated opposition, and practised almost no terror. Ch.VII: industrial civilisation and the proletariat humanise conduct; planned state terror is regression; world war brutalised masses and revived anti-Marxist "theory of power." Ch.VIII: expropriation without organisation produces chaos; "dictatorship of the proletariat" as motto for absolution from anti-Marxist methods; personal dictatorship incompatible with democracy (including Soviet democracy); free press sole check on corruption; despotism essence of dictatorship; Soviet terror the **inevitable result of Communist methods**; White terror does not justify Red; Mensheviks and Left SRs as hostages; closing — world revolution only through "democracy and humanity," not cannons. See [[red-terror]], [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]].

### Trotsky's reply (1920) — still a filter

Trotsky's *[[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920]]* answers this book. His constructions of Kautsky (revolution without terrorism as delusion-turn; economic "pseudo-Marxism"; Austrian school portrait) can now be checked against Kautsky's own text rather than standing alone. Both remain partisan.

## Historiography

Primary Kautsky now includes *Dictatorship* (1918) and *Terrorism and Communism* (1919). Lenin and Trotsky polemics remain major filters for the post-1917 reputation; broader scholarly reappraisal still thin in the collection.
