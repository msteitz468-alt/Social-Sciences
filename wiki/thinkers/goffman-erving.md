# Erving Goffman

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/thinkers/goffman-erving|Thinkers Hub]]

## Overview

Erving Goffman (1922–1982) was a Canadian-born American sociologist whose microsociology of face-to-face interaction and institutional selfhood became one of the most cited programs in postwar sociology. This page is built from three mid-career pillars: *[[goffman-presentation-of-self-1956|The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life]]* (Edinburgh 1956; US ed. 1959), *[[goffman-asylums-1961|Asylums]]* (1961), and *[[goffman-stigma-1963|Stigma]]* (1963). Later interaction-order works remain queued. Goffman taught at Berkeley and later at the University of Pennsylvania; he was president of the American Sociological Association at his death.

## Contributions

### From *Presentation of Self* (1956 Edinburgh monograph; Shetland illustrations from PhD fieldwork)

- **[[dramaturgical-analysis]]** — handbook perspective treating social establishments as scenes of performance and **[[impression-management|impression management]]**.
- **[[performance-front]]** — setting + personal front (appearance, manner); front as collective representation.
- **[[front-and-back-region]]** — regions bounded by barriers to perception; politeness and decorum vs backstage contradiction of the show.
- **[[performance-team]]** — dramaturgical co-operation unit; reciprocal dependence; team secrets.
- **[[working-consensus]]** — temporary honoring of definitional claims, not candid harmony.
- **[[given-and-given-off]]** — intentional/unintentional expression; promissory co-presence (textbook "given/given off" label).
- **[[cynical-and-sincere-performance]]**, **[[dramatic-realization]]**, **[[performance-idealization]]**, **[[audience-segregation]]**, **[[discrepant-roles]]**, **[[communication-out-of-character]]**, **[[mystification]]**.
- Closing dialectic: performers as "merchants of morality."

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained under:** PhD Chicago; teachers named in *Presentation* Acknowledgements — C. W. M. Hart, W. L. Warner, E. C. Hughes. Formal `trained_under` slugs remain `[[unknown]]` until thinker pages exist for those teachers.
- **Formal sociology:** Simmel-style justification for mixed illustrations (*Presentation* Preface).
- **Occupations tradition:** Hughes lineage visible in dirty work, professional front, colleague ethics.
- ***Asylums* base:** NIMH Laboratory under John Clausen; St. Elizabeths access.
- ***Stigma* base:** Center for the Study of Law and Society, UC Berkeley; MacIver Lecture 1962.
- **Reception lineage:** placed after Mead–Blumer SI and Simmelian form analysis; influenced labeling, organizational total-institution research, stigma studies; anti-psychiatry-adjacent public debates as positions.
- **Sociolinguistics / ethnography of speaking:** [[hymes-dell|Dell Hymes]] (*[[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974|Foundations in Sociolinguistics]]*, 1974) records a special debt — "this book would not exist without his intervention" — and treats Goffman's interaction-order concerns as lying at sociolinguistics' core (attributed, Hymes).
- [[gumperz-john-j|Gumperz]]'s Introduction to *[[gumperz-hymes-directions-sociolinguistics-1972|Directions in Sociolinguistics]]* (1972) independently credits Goffman (1965) as one of the founders — alongside Garfinkel and Cicourel — of the **interactionist approach** to social theory that underpins [[interactional-sociolinguistics|interactional sociolinguistics]]: the view that communication is a two-step process in which speakers evaluate context via cultural background and interlocutor knowledge before selecting norms and strategies, rather than following fixed social rules (attributed, Gumperz).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Recorded as positions, not verdicts (detail on study/hub pages):

- **Dramaturgy:** stage metaphor over-read as ontology of fakeness; cynicism charge vs sincere pole and audience tact; micro without macro power.
- **Gouldner 1970:** dramaturgy as Functionalism's entropy infrastructure — appearances, tact, "soul-selling," accommodation (see [[gouldner-coming-crisis-1970]]).
- ***Asylums* method and tone:** assistant role; unpublished notes; anti-psychiatry uptake vs sociology-of-self framing.
- ***Stigma* standpoint:** "we normals"; structural thinness relative to later structural-stigma work.
- **Generalizability of [[total-institution]]:** one ideal type across monasteries, ships, prisons, asylums.
- **Historical supersession:** mid-century examples are period materials even where concepts travel.

## Reception in structuration theory

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|Constitution of Society]]*, 1984) draws heavily on Goffman's encounter order, tact, front/back regions, and co-presence while criticizing the lack of a theory of motivation/unconscious and any residual dramaturgical emptiness of the social world — supplying [[routinization]] and [[ontological-security]] as the missing hinge. See [[structuration-theory]].

## Historiography

Textbook Goffman is dramaturgy + total institutions + stigma. With all three mid-career pillars ingested, the wiki summary and **[[hubs/thinkers/goffman-erving|Thinkers Hub]]** can treat that cluster from primaries. Late-career frame and public-order works remain coverage gaps. Edition note: *Presentation* primary here is **1956 Edinburgh**, not 1959 US.

### Gouldner's reading (1970; attributed)

[[gouldner-alvin-w|Gouldner]]'s *[[gouldner-coming-crisis-1970|Coming Crisis]]* (Ch. 10) treats Goffmanian dramaturgy as a symptom of Functionalism's entropy and as an infrastructure rival: exaltation of appearances over essences; society as co-presence cemented by *tact*; "sociology of soul-selling"; secondary adjustments within bureaucracies rather than structural change; ambiguous rebel-critical yet accommodating of power. Now readable against the *Presentation* primary (merchants of morality; working consensus; protective tact) rather than only as second-order characterization.

**Later program link:** [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]] (*[[hochschild-managed-heart-1983|The Managed Heart]]*, 1983) treats Goffman as rich on surface [[impression-management]] and thin on deep acting and corporate profit from engineered feeling (attributed); see [[surface-and-deep-acting]], [[emotional-labor]], [[hubs/studies/hochschild-managed-heart-study]].
