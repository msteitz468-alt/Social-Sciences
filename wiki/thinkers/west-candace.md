# Candace West

U.S. sociologist; with [[zimmerman-don-h|Don H. Zimmerman]], author of the 1987 article
that launched the [[doing-gender]] research program; with [[fenstermaker-sarah|Sarah
Fenstermaker]], co-developer of "[[doing-difference]]" and co-editor of the 2002 consolidation
volume [[fenstermaker-west-doing-gender-doing-difference-2002]].

## Overview

West's program work relocates gender from individual traits and static roles into situated
interaction and institutional accountability structures, in explicit dialogue with
[[ethnomethodology]] and feminist sociology.

## Contributions

- Co-originator of [[doing-gender]] and the sex / [[sex-category]] / gender distinction
- Co-author of empirical applications (e.g., cosmetic-surgery accounting with Diana Dull;
  UC Regents meeting analysis with Fenstermaker)
- Extension of the framework from gender alone to simultaneous categorical differences

## Intellectual Genealogy

`developed_from:` [[garfinkel-harold]] and ethnomethodology; engagement with Goffman on
gender display (critical); long collaboration with [[zimmerman-don-h|Zimmerman]] and
[[fenstermaker-sarah|Fenstermaker]]. Per the 2002 Acknowledgments, original work on gender
accomplishment emerged from research on interruption in conversation (UCSB dissertation,
1977). Publication path for the core article ran through Judith Lorber's *Gender & Society*.
Early supporters named include Erving Goffman, Dorothy Smith, and Barrie Thorne among others.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Central target of the 1995 "Doing Difference" symposium ([[doing-difference-debate]]);
program responses collected in the 2002 volume.

## Historiography

Program history is narrated by Fenstermaker and West in the 2002 Introduction (manuscript
from 1977; rejections; *Gender Factory* pathway; 1987 publication).
