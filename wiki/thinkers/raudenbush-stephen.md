# Stephen W. Raudenbush

## Overview

Statistician and social scientist; major methodological collaborator with [[sampson-robert-j|Robert J. Sampson]] on the Project on Human Development in Chicago Neighborhoods. Co-developer of **[[ecometrics]]** and of the multi-level measurement of **[[collective-efficacy]]** (*Science* 1997 with Sampson and Earls). Known more broadly for hierarchical linear modeling (with Bryk). In Sampson’s account of PHDCN institutionalization (*[[sampson-great-american-city-2012|Great American City]]* ch. 4, attributed), Raudenbush served as a **scientific director** alongside Sampson, Reiss, and Moffitt under principal investigator Felton Earls.

## Contributions

- **Ecometrics** (with Sampson): ecological constructs need measurement standards analogous to psychometrics but are **not** mere aggregates of individual traits; without ecometric strategy, multilevel studies overemphasize the individual component (GAC, attributed).
- **Collective efficacy** multi-level reliability and *Science* 1997 violence models.
- **Disorder program (with Sampson):** [[systematic-social-observation]] of public disorder; concentrated poverty and mixed land use forecast observed disorder; CE predicts lower observed disorder and violence after controls; critique of strong broken-windows claims (disorder often *is* crime/ordinance violation — “crime causes crime”); perceived disorder shaped by racial composition beyond observed SSO.
- **Spatial CE–homicide typology** (with Morenoff & Sampson): high-high / low-low / off-diagonal clusters linking efficacy and homicide.
- **Developmental disadvantage** (with Sharkey & Sampson): inverse-probability-of-treatment weighted estimates that severe [[concentrated-disadvantage]] reduces African American children’s later verbal ability (>4 points; larger for ages 6–9 at baseline).

Synthesized in *Great American City* ([[sampson-great-american-city-study]]; [[hubs/studies/sampson-great-american-city-study]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Quantitative methodology (HLM/psychometrics) meeting Chicago urban sociology / criminology and PHDCN “big science” design. Collaborative program with Sampson is the main urban-sociology face of the work.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Reception travels mainly with the collective-efficacy, ecometrics, and [[neighborhood-effects-debate]] literatures; experimentalist critiques of observational neighborhood models apply to the joint program.

## Historiography

Secondary via Sampson’s project history (GAC ch. 4) and the HLM / ecometrics methods literature; Wilson’s GAC foreword highlights theoretically derived ecometric measurement of ecological settings.
