# J. Eric S. Thompson

## Overview

English-born Maya archaeologist and epigrapher (1898–1975) who dominated Anglophone Maya studies from the Carnegie Institution era through the 1960s. According to [[coe-breaking-the-maya-code-2012|Coe’s *Breaking the Maya Code* (2012)]], Thompson combined lasting calendrical discoveries with a methodological orthodoxy that treated non-calendrical glyphs as largely **ideographic** and the monuments as non-historical — a position Coe ranks as the principal **brake** on phonetic decipherment until the Knorosov–Proskouriakoff hinge. Primary filing for this densification: Coe 2012 Ch. 5–6 and synthesis chapters; cross-check [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006]] and [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015]]. See [[maya-script-decipherment-debate]].

## Contributions

### Calendrics and structural discoveries (as reported by Coe 2012)

Coe credits Thompson with durable advances that survive the collapse of his ideographic program:

- Defense of the **Goodman–Martínez–Thompson (GMT)** correlation of Long Count to Christian chronology against Spinden-type alternatives; Coe states time proved Goodman, Martínez, and Thompson right.
- **Glyph G** as a cycle of nine alternating signs related to the Aztec **Nine Lords of the Night**.
- Recognition of an **819-day** ceremonial cycle (7×9×13) linked to world directions, colors, and God K / K’awiil.
- “Count forward” / “count back” Distance Number indicators and the 15-tun period glyph.
- Successful readings of common affixes including *te/che* (“tree/wood,” also a numerical classifier) and *tu* (3rd-person possessive turning cardinals into ordinals) — advances Coe says have generally held up.

### Institutional position

Thompson worked at Chichén Itzá under Carnegie/Morley (left after 1926), at Chicago’s Field Museum with fieldwork in British Honduras and among living Maya, then as Carnegie staff researcher (1936–1958 program dissolution). Coe portrays him as the Anglophone gatekeeper of glyph interpretation in the mid-century “Club” of Carnegie Maya research.

### *Maya Hieroglyphic Writing* and the Catalog

Thompson’s 1950 *Maya Hieroglyphic Writing: Introduction* and 1962 *Catalog of Maya Hieroglyphs* became the standard reference apparatus. Coe argues the Catalog mixed calendrical, iconographic, and unproven assignments into a “hodgepodge” that impeded systematic phonetic analysis even as it remained indispensable for sign numbers (T-numbers still cited). Thompson’s popular *Rise and Fall of Maya Civilization* projected a peaceful, theocratic, calendar-obsessed Maya that Coe and later epigraphers treat as ethnographically and epigraphically obsolete.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Network:** Carnegie circle with [[morley-sylvanus-g|Sylvanus G. Morley]], A.V. Kidder, and staff including [[proskouriakoff-tatiana|Tatiana Proskouriakoff]] (initially as artist).
- **Reacted against / suppressed (per Coe):** phonetic proposals from Brasseur through Whorf to [[knorosov-yuri|Yuri Knorosov]]; historical readings later demonstrated by Proskouriakoff.
- **Opposed by (post-1952):** Knorosov’s logosyllabic program; North American defenders such as [[kelley-david-h|David H. Kelley]]; eventually the Schele–Lounsbury–Stuart generation.
- **Personal model:** Coe stresses lifelong friendship with Jacinto Cunil of Socotz; Coe reinterprets Cunil’s mysticism against Thompson’s “High Church Anglican” calendar-priest ideal.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Coe’s historiography is **partisan**. He compares Thompson’s grip on the field to Athanasius Kircher’s century-long blockage of Egyptian decipherment and details the 1953 *Yan* review of Knorosov as Cold War rejection (“Marxist hoax”) rather than technical refutation. That characterization is a **position** on [[maya-script-decipherment-debate]], not wiki-voice fact.

Separable points that later syntheses also record:

- Many of Thompson’s **calendrical** results remain foundational.
- The **non-historical / non-phonetic** package for monumental texts was overturned by Proskouriakoff’s structural method (1960) and Knorosov-line phonetics refined after ~1975.
- Thompson eventually accepted aspects of the historical reading (Coe and Sharer & Traxler both note late accommodation of Proskouriakoff-type patterns).
