# François R. Valla

## Overview

French prehistoric archaeologist; principal excavator of the Final Natufian at [[eynan-ain-mallaha|Eynan (Ain Mallaha)]] and co-editor with [[bar-yosef-ofer|Ofer Bar-Yosef]] of the two major Natufian symposium volumes (1991; [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013|2013]]). Known for spatial analyses of Natufian domestic structures and for questioning simple seasonality readings of Natufian settlement (Valla 1998, cited in the 2013 preface).

## Contributions

- **Eynan / Mallaha program.** Multi-decade French–Israeli excavations; Final Natufian structure analyses in the 2013 volume (with Khalaily, Samuelian, Bocquentin, and others).
- **Settlement and seasonality.** Raised issues of how long houses were inhabited, abandonment, and mobility terminology (residential / logistical / radial) — agenda restated with Bar-Yosef in the 2013 preface.
- **Editorial synthesis.** With Bar-Yosef, structured the 2013 volume geographically (northern Levant → southern Levant site clusters → general reviews) without imposing a single overall conclusion.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Long collaboration with Bar-Yosef; Eynan team includes Hamoudi Khalaily, Nicolas Samuelian, Fanny Bocquentin, and specialists in fauna, bone, and geochemistry.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Seasonality “guesses” and core-area assumptions are contested within the Natufian research community; densify as chapter-level positions are integrated.

## Historiography

Biographical depth limited in the 2013 volume; excavation reports (e.g., Valla et al. 2007 *Mitekufat Haeven*) are primary for the Eynan program.
