# Nikolai Danielson (N.-on)

Russian Narodnik economist, known in the polemical literature by his pseudonym **N.-on** (Nikolai-on) — co-translator (with Lopatin) of the first foreign-language edition of Marx's *Capital* Vol. I (Russian, 1872) and Marx's and Engels's long-term Russian correspondent, yet the chief theoretical target of the first generation of Russian Marxists. The wiki knows him almost entirely **through his opponents' quotations** — no work of his is ingested.

## Overview

See summary above; his position as reconstructed by critics:

In *Sketches on Our Post-Reform Social Economy* (1893) and earlier articles (Slovo, 1880), Danielson argued that capitalism in Russia was an artificial, state-fostered growth that ruined the peasantry and destroyed its own home market: surplus-value could not be realized domestically, foreign markets were already taken, and Russia should therefore develop "people's production" on the basis of the village commune. He held capitalist agriculture "almost completely absent" where communal tenure prevailed.

## Contributions (as polemical target)

- [[plekhanov-our-differences-1885|Plekhanov (1885)]] attacked the commune-socialism premise a decade before the *Sketches*.
- Lenin's [[lenin-what-the-friends-of-the-people-are-1894|Friends of the People (1894)]] treats N.-on (with V.V./Vorontsov) as Narodnik economics' most serious representative.
- [[lenin-development-of-capitalism-in-russia-1899|The Development of Capitalism in Russia (1899)]] is structured against him chapter by chapter: the realization theory of Ch. I, the differentiation data of Ch. II, and the factory-statistics critique of Ch. VII each answer a specific N.-on claim (underconsumptionism; commune homogeneity; the "shrinking home market" reading of the 1880s worker statistics).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Lenin conceded N.-on's seriousness (he had "admitted the need of raising the problem of the differentiation of the peasantry" in 1880) while charging him with retreating from his own data. The positions recorded here are Lenin's and Plekhanov's constructions; ingest of the *Sketches* would allow first-hand restatement.

## Historiography

His decades-long correspondence with Marx and Engels (not ingested) is a standard primary source on Marx's own late views on Russia and the commune — the irony that Marx's Russian translator became "N.-on" is a fixture of the literature on the Narodnik–Marxist dispute.
