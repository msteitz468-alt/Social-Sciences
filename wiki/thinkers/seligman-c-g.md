# C. G. Seligman

## Overview

Charles Gabriel Seligman (1873–1940), British anthropologist of the Cambridge school, teacher of [[malinowski-bronislaw|Bronislaw Malinowski]] and of [[evans-pritchard-e-e|E. E. Evans-Pritchard]]. *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts of the Western Pacific]]* is dedicated to "my friend and teacher Professor C. G. Seligman, F.R.S." Author of *The Melanesians of British New Guinea* (1910), which Malinowski cites as baseline Massim ethnography. Evans-Pritchard (App. IV of the Azande abridgement) names him as teacher and recalls practical field counsel (quinine; keep off women). Stub grounded from *Argonauts* and E-P App. IV only; fuller biography awaits primary ingest.

## Contributions

- Cambridge-school Melanesian ethnography; *Melanesians of British New Guinea* (1910) as regional baseline for Malinowski's Massim work.
- Teacher/patron role for Malinowski (named in dedication and method debts of the Introduction).
- Teacher of Evans-Pritchard (named in Azande fieldwork reminiscences).
