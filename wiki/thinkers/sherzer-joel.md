# Joel Sherzer

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist; co-editor with [[bauman-richard|Richard Bauman]] of *[[bauman-sherzer-explorations-ethnography-speaking-1974|Explorations in the Ethnography of Speaking]]* (1974). Known especially for ethnography of speaking among the **Cuna** (San Blas), including analyses of longhouse speech events and political-verbal skill (as presented in the volume).

## Contributions

- Co-editing *Explorations*: synthesis of second-stage ethnography of speaking.
- Cuna case materials in the volume: speech events, metaphor, chiefly discourse (attributed to Sherzer’s chapters).
- Collaboration with Darnell on methodological notes for the field (cited in volume preface lineage).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Program under [[hymes-dell]] charter; co-editor with [[bauman-richard]]; dialogue with [[gumperz-john-j]] and conference network.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Cuna materials are fieldwork-based case studies; not universal models. Later Sherzer monographs outstanding for densification.
