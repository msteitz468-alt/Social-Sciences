# A. Nigel Goring-Morris

## Overview

Israeli prehistoric archaeologist specializing in Levantine Epipaleolithic sequences, especially the Negev–Sinai “periphery,” and in the relationship between Geometric Kebaran, Mushabian/Ramonian, and [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] entities. Co-author with [[belfer-cohen-anna|Anna Belfer-Cohen]] of major 2013 reappraisals of Natufian phases, facies, and core–periphery models ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).

## Contributions

- **Periphery sequences.** Defined and refined Mushabian and Ramonian entities preceding local Early Natufian in Negev/Sinai — complicating pan-Levantine unilinear stories.
- **Core–periphery and origins.** With Belfer-Cohen, argues Natufian origins involve intermingling of varied Middle Epipaleolithic traditions, not simple *in situ* evolution from core-area Geometric Kebaran alone; Early Natufian dates from south Transjordan support multi-regional emergence.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Hebrew University; long collaboration with Belfer-Cohen.
