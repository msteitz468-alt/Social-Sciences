# Louise Lamphere

## Overview

Louise Lamphere is an American anthropologist, co-editor (with [[rosaldo-michelle|Michelle Z. Rosaldo]]) of *[[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974|Woman, Culture, and Society]]* (1974) — the charter volume of [[feminist-anthropology|feminist anthropology]] — and author of its essay "Strategies, Cooperation, and Conflict Among Women in Domestic Groups." Ph.D. Harvard 1968; early fieldwork among the Navajo; 1971–72 London pilot on working-class women; later industrial/workplace ethnography.

## Contributions

- **Co-editorship of *Woman, Culture, and Society*** (1974): with Rosaldo, framed the program that made sexual asymmetry and women's agency central anthropological problems (Preface Nov 1973; Stanford 1971 course lineage).
- **Strategies in domestic groups** (1974): critiques male-centered developmental-cycle and marriage analyses; distinguishes power, authority, influence, and unassigned power; ties women's strategies to domestic power structure and the developmental cycle.
  - Where **spheres are integrated** and authority relatively egalitarian (Navajo matrilocal grandfamilies; some foraging cases), women pursue cooperative "economic" strategies with female kin.
  - Where **spheres are separated** and men hold household authority (patrilocal extended houses, polygyny), women pursue "political" influence-through-men and often conflict with co-wives and mothers-in-law.
- **Navajo ethnography**: cooperation, residence, and domestic organization (*To Run After Them*, 1977) — not yet primary-ingested beyond the 1974 essay.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The domestic-group focus of her 1974 essay participates in the volume's [[domestic-public-dichotomy|domestic/public]] frame, later criticized (including by Rosaldo herself) for projecting Western public/private splits — see [[nature-culture-gender-debate]]. Her strategy typology remains a usable middle-range tool even where the universal frame is dropped.

## Historiography

Remembered primarily as co-architect of the 1974 feminist-anthropology charter and as a Navajo and workplace ethnographer. Primary source for this page: [[rosaldo-lamphere-woman-culture-society-1974]].
