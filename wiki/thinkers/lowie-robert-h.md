# Robert H. Lowie

## Overview

Robert Harry Lowie (1883–1957), Austrian-born American anthropologist, was a leading student of [[boas-franz|Franz Boas]] and a principal architect of the American **[[historical-particularism|historical-particularist]]** program applied to **social organization**. *[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]* (1920; 1947 reissue with new preface) is his major comparative statement: a sustained critique of [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]'s unilinear evolutionism and a reconstruction of "primitive" society as historically particular combinations of family, [[sib]], associations, rank, and territorial ties — **mottled diversity** rather than a single sib-pattern ladder. Long associated with the American Museum of Natural History and later the University of California, Berkeley. This summary rests primarily on *Primitive Society* as ingested; Crow monographs and *History of Ethnological Theory* are not yet ingested.

## Contributions

From *[[lowie-primitive-society-study|Primitive Society]]* (attributed):

- **Anti-unilinear social organization.** Stage-laws for family, gens, marriage, and associations fail distributional tests; independent development occurs but does not reproduce fixed stage series; diffusion "lays the axe to the root" of historical laws.
- **[[sib]] as analytic term.** Unilateral kinship unit (after Philbrick's suggestion) distinguished from bilateral family and from Morgan's stage-packaged gens; mother-sib not world-prior to father-sib by necessity.
- **Family priority and universality.** Bilateral individual family as omnipresent unit; sib secondary and restricted — main propositions Lowie still defended in 1947.
- **Associations program.** Incorporated Schurtz's age/sex grouping facts without Schurtz's unilinear psychology; Plains [[age-societies]] as graded/purchase systems; clubs and [[secret-society|secret societies]] as first-class structure.
- **Property and rank.** Rejection of crude primitive communism; [[incorporeal-property]]; stratification by bravery, shamanism, wealth, caste in "rude" cultures.
- **Political and jural institutions.** Government and justice exist without waiting for Morgan's late stages; territorial and kinship bonds coexist.
- **"[[shreds-and-patches|Shreds and patches]]."** Civilization as planless borrowed complex and anti-"mock-law of social evolution" close; 1947 clarification against pure-atomism misreading.
- **Method slogan.** "Whatever else the investigator of civilization may do, he must be a historian."

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Contemporaries split: Rivers found the book valuable but insufficiently diffusionist; Kroeber praised honesty over Morgan's "brilliant illusions" but regretted limited generalization; Sapir hailed the break with classical evolutionism; Morgan admirers denounced it. Later neo-evolutionists and structural-functionalists charged Boasian particularism with under-producing theory (different remedies). The "shreds and patches" phrase was widely misread as pure cultural atomism — Lowie denied that reading in 1947 while still denying that all culture elements are necessarily organically bonded. Museum-era ethnography of Native North America carries colonial production context to be expanded on society pages as monographs are ingested.

## Historiography

Primary self-historiography in the 1947 Preface to *Primitive Society* (reception, standing propositions, method hopes including area-historical reconstruction and Tylor-style statistics / Murdock). Standard histories of anthropology treat the book as the American social-organization charter of the anti-stage generation.
