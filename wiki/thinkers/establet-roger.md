# Roger Establet

## Overview

French philosopher/sociologist; former ENS philosophy student associated with Althusser’s circle. Contributed **Part Six** of *[[althusser-reading-capital-1965|Reading Capital]]* — “Presentation of the Plan of *Capital*” — after following the 1964–65 seminar from a distance; provides a structural map of the articulations of Marx’s volumes. Later educational sociology (with Christian Baudelot) is outside this primary ingest.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — *Reading Capital* Part Six.)*

- Argues *Capital*’s articulations cannot be read off the table of contents: the work is new in concepts **and** organization; two prejudiced misreadings are Hegelian volume-title order and “from model to model” under modern disciplinary preoccupations.
- Reconstructs **articulations** of the volumes from Marx’s own plan statements and the necessity of the order of concepts (not pedagogic convenience alone).
- Rewrites Vol. 1 sub-articulations via labour-process terms: production of surplus-value / accumulation (reproduction of conditions); absolute / relative surplus-value (relations of production / organization of productive forces).
- **Brackets Part Eight** (*So-Called Primitive Accumulation*) for plan purposes: genealogy of elements posing a problem for transition theory, **not** that theory itself; treating it as transition would imply autonomous development of elements then fusion into structure.
- Separates the theoretical domain of **definition** of a mode’s labour process from the domain of **transition** between modes (where famous RP/PF determination theses apply differently).
- Names **competition** as the non-elaborated theoretical field of Vols 1–2 — an empty place that denotes both ideological discourse and a problem for further science.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- Circle of [[althusser-louis]]; assumes Balibar’s labour-process/combination results; Althusserian [[symptomatic-reading]] of Marx’s self-presentation of the plan.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Most specialized of the six parts; reception thinner than Althusser/Balibar.
- Plan reconstruction is itself theory-laden (structural-Marxist criteria of scientific order).
