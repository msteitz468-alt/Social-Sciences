# Michael Mann

Michael Mann (b. 1942) is a British-American historical sociologist, long associated with the London School of Economics and later UCLA, best known for the multi-volume *[[mann-sources-of-social-power-vol1-1986|The Sources of Social Power]]* and the [[iemp-model|IEMP model]] of overlapping power networks.

Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/mann-michael]].

## Overview

Mann's program rejects the unitary concept of "society" as a bounded social system and reconstructs history as the interrelations of four organizational sources of power — **I**deological, **E**conomic, **M**ilitary, and **P**olitical — treated as overlapping sociospatial networks rather than "factors" or "levels" of a totality. Volume I (1986) covers agrarian societies to A.D. 1760; later volumes treat industrial modernity (not yet ingested here).

## Contributions

- **[[iemp-model|IEMP / sources of social power]]** — four organizational means of social control; logistics and communication as measurement of power capacity.
- **[[power-networks]]** — societies as multiple overlapping and intersecting networks, not unitary systems.
- **[[social-caging]]** — alluvial and other ecological-social "cages" that fix populations into permanent power relations (esp. early civilization).
- **[[infrastructural-power]] / [[despotic-power]]** — dual dimensions of state power (developed especially in the 1984 *AES* article; used throughout SSP).
- **[[compulsory-cooperation]]** — dialectic of early empires of domination.
- **[[multi-power-actor-civilization]]** — civilizations as multi-actor fields (Mesopotamia, Greece, medieval Europe) vs. centralized empires of domination / territorial empires.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Developed from / in dialogue with:** [[weber-max|Max Weber]] (organizational power, multi-factor caution, ideal-types); Gellner (neo-episodic change); military logistics traditions (e.g. Lattimore radii); engagement with Marxian class analysis without economic monism.
- **reacted_against:** unitary systems theory ([[parsons-talcott|Parsons]] and successors); nation-state = society models; unilinear social evolutionism; pure economic determinism; pure military or ideological monisms.
- **Dialogue partners in the comparative-historical field:** [[tilly-charles|Charles Tilly]], [[skocpol-theda|Theda Skocpol]], [[wallerstein-immanuel|Immanuel Wallerstein]], [[moore-barrington|Barrington Moore]] (program constellation; specific engagements densified from Vol. I claims).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Critiques of Mann typically target: secondary-source dependence of the grand narrative; Eurocentric weighting of the "European dynamic" chapters; treatment of gender and household power; residual residualism vs. fuller cultural theory; and (for later volumes not ingested here) modern case applications. Positions recorded as they appear in ingested material; full reception history on the hub page.
