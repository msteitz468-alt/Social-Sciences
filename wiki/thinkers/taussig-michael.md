# Michael T. Taussig

Australian-born anthropologist of capitalism, magic, and colonial violence; long associated with Columbia University. Best known for reading Latin American devil lore as mediation of [[commodity-fetishism|commodity fetishism]] and for analyzing Putumayo rubber terror and yagé healing as a single field of colonial image-work.

> Deep intellectual biography: [[hubs/thinkers/taussig-michael|Thinkers Hub]].

## Overview

Taussig arrived in Colombia in 1969 and conducted recurrent fieldwork among Afro-Colombian free peasants and cane workers of the southern Cauca Valley from about 1970, and across southwest Colombia’s Putumayo highland–lowland shamanic circuits through 1985 (Author’s Note to the 1987 book: almost five years total in periods from one month to two years). *[[taussig-devil-commodity-fetishism-1980|The Devil and Commodity Fetishism in South America]]* (1980; 30th anniversary ed. 2010) yoked Marx’s fetishism of commodities to plantation devil contracts and (via June Nash and others) Bolivian tin-mine devil (Tio) rites. *[[taussig-shamanism-colonialism-wild-man-1987|Shamanism, Colonialism, and the Wild Man]]* (1987) densifies the program into historical terror (Putumayo rubber boom) and medical-anthropological montage (yagé healing). Later works (*Mimesis and Alterity*; fictocritical experiments) push further into mimesis and ethnographic poetics — only partly covered by current ingests.

## Contributions

- **Devil contracts and baptism of money** as cultural critiques of wage labour and capital’s barren fertility ([[devil-contract]], [[baptism-of-money]]; study [[taussig-devil-commodity-fetishism-study]]).
- **[[tio|Tio]] / mine devil** as spirit-owner of minerals under capitalist mining — synthesised especially from June Nash (Nash not yet a wiki thinker page).
- **[[space-of-death]], [[culture-of-terror]], [[epistemic-murk]]** as analytic vocabulary for colonial and dictatorial violence ([[putumayo-rubber-terror]]; study [[taussig-shamanism-colonialism-study]]).
- **[[wild-man]]** as colonial and curative image; colonial mirror of production ([[mimesis]] of attributed savagery).
- **[[yage|Yagé]] montage** and **[[implicit-social-knowledge]]** as anti-positivist medical anthropology of healing.
- Programmatic challenge to functionalist reductions of magical belief and to omniscient academic historicist narrative (Benjaminian method).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Heavy secondary dependence for Bolivia in 1980; risk of over-generalising two regions as one structural contrast.
- Marxist use-/exchange-value binary and peasant-mode framing contested in later economic anthropology.
- 1987 literary-modernist style frustrates readers seeking standard rubber-boom demography; romanticization risk around wildness even while diagnosing colonial creation of the image.
- 2009 self-critique (devil book anniversary) of analytic distance and underdeveloped attention to narrative form and *dépense*.
- Regional specialists may revise highland–lowland composites (collection gap).
