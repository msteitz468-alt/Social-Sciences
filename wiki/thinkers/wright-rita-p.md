# Rita P. Wright

## Overview

American anthropological archaeologist (New York University; MacArthur Fellow)
specializing in the Indus civilization, craft production, and gender in
archaeology. Series editor of Cambridge's *Case Studies in Early Societies*.
Field research in Afghanistan, Iran, and Pakistan, including landscape and
settlement survey around [[harappa|Harappa]].

## Contributions

- *The Ancient Indus* (2010) — synthesis of Indus urbanism, economy, and
  society ([[wright-ancient-indus-2010]]), notable for its comparative
  Mesopotamian framing and social-network reading of city–countryside relations.
- Editor of *Gender and Archaeology* (1996) and co-editor of *Craft and Social
  Identity* (1998) — programmatic works for gender archaeology and
  [[craft-specialization]] studies.
