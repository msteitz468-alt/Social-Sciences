# Michel-Rolph Trouillot

Michel-Rolph Trouillot (1949–2012) was a Haitian anthropologist and historian of the Caribbean whose work centers **power in the production of history** and the provincialization of North Atlantic categories. Major densify from *[[trouillot-silencing-the-past-1995|Silencing the Past]]* (1995; 2015 ed. with Carby foreword); earlier seed from “The North Atlantic Universals” in *[[wallerstein-modern-world-system-longue-duree-2004|Wallerstein ed. 2004]]*.

## Overview

Trouillot argues that history is never only what happened or only what is said to have happened, but the uneven relation between material sociohistorical process ([[historicity-1-and-2|historicity 1]]) and narrative ([[historicity-1-and-2|historicity 2]]); that **[[historical-silences|silences]]** enter at four moments of production; and that events such as the [[haitian-revolution|Haitian Revolution]] could be [[unthinkable-history|unthinkable]] even as they happened. Later work names [[north-atlantic-universals]] as particulars that project North Atlantic experience as world standard.

## Contributions

### Production of history (*Silencing the Past*, 1995)

- Program: study **how history works**, not essentialist “what history is.”
- Four moments of silence: sources → archives → narratives → retrospective significance.
- Power **constitutive** of the story; begins at the source (Foucault adjacency: how power happens).
- Case strategies: Sans Souci (reposition sparse evidence); Haitian Revolution (make unthinkable silences speak); Columbus (conflicts under abundance).
- Concepts: [[production-of-history]], [[historical-silences]], [[historicity-1-and-2]], [[unthinkable-history]], [[formulas-of-silence]], [[columbus-commemorations]].

### North Atlantic universals (2002–2004 formulation)

- [[north-atlantic-universals]] — substantive vs classificatory universals; West as project (with Glissant). Seeded from Wallerstein 2004 volume.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Family:** Dinner-table historiography under father **Ernst Trouillot** and uncle **Hénock Trouillot** (National Archives director; co-author with Catts Pressoir of the first historiography book Michel-Rolph read). Formed under Duvalier terror: people as “complaisant hostages of the pasts they create,” yet refusal of escape-from-history.
- **Roundtables:** David W. Cohen’s International Roundtable in History and Anthropology (from 1985); Ch.1–2 from Paris 1986 / Bellagio 1989 papers. Writing on production of history as distinct topic from **1981**.
- **Johns Hopkins:** Homewood seminars; colleagues including Sara S. Berry, Sidney W. Mintz, Katherine Verdery, Brackette F. Williams; Institute for Global Studies in Culture, Power and History.
- **Named interlocutors (Acknowledgments):** Arjun Appadurai, Joan Dayan, among others.
- **Dialogue:** Foucault on power’s “how”; Bourdieu on the unthinkable; adjacency to [[historical-anthropology]], [[world-systems-analysis]], postcolonial critique — not membership in a single school.
- **Haitian intellectual community:** Michel Acacia, Pierre Buteau, Jean Coulanges, Lyonel Trouillot, Evelyne Trouillot-Ménard, Drexel Woodson (as listed).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- *Silencing the Past* became a graduate staple across anthropology, history, American Studies, cultural studies (Carby 2015: required reading; “With the Texts” seminar).
- “Unthinkable” thesis is live interpretive ground — see [[unthinkable-haitian-revolution-debate]].
- Risk in reception: collapse to “history is written by winners” slogan; Trouillot rejects both naive positivism and pure constructivism.
