# Gilles Deleuze

## Overview

Gilles Deleuze (1925–1995) was a French philosopher whose work — alone (*Difference and Repetition*, 1968; *The Logic of Sense*, 1969) and jointly with the psychoanalyst **Félix Guattari** (the two-volume *Capitalism and Schizophrenia*: *Anti-Oedipus*, 1972; *A Thousand Plateaus*, 1980; and *What Is Philosophy?*, 1991) — has been read by anthropologists as a resource for a "post-structural" theory of difference and relation. He enters this vault as the decisive philosophical interlocutor of [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Eduardo Viveiros de Castro]], whose *[[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]* reads the *Mythologiques* "on the basis of *A Thousand Plateaus*" and casts Amerindian [[perspectivism]] as "the becoming-Amazonian of Deleuze."

This page summarizes the Deleuzian apparatus **as anthropologists (chiefly Viveiros de Castro) appropriate it** — not as a full account of Deleuze's philosophy on its own terms. All claims are attributed.

## Contributions (as received in anthropology)

- **Multiplicity**: individuation as "non-taxonomical differentiation," "the mode of existence of pure intensive difference" (drawn by Deleuze from Riemann and Bergson); a system at *n–1* dimensions. Viveiros de Castro links it to [[wagner-roy|Wagner]]'s "fractal person" and [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]]'s "partial connections."
- **The virtual and the actual**: the virtual as ontologically primary but to be constructed/achieved; Viveiros de Castro inverts this for Amazonia, where the virtual/relational is the *immediate* condition and stable actual identities must be worked for.
- **Becoming**: neither metaphor, metamorphosis, filiation, nor production — "Becoming and multiplicity are the same thing." Central to Viveiros de Castro's reading of Amazonian "becoming-jaguar" and transversal [[perspectivism|shamanism]].
- **Rhizome vs. tree**: "the tree is filiation, but the rhizome is alliance, uniquely alliance" — Viveiros de Castro's warrant for making affinity/alliance, not filiation, the generic Amazonian relation.
- **Disjunctive synthesis / inclusive disjunction**: relation grounded in divergence rather than identity — "another name for becoming."
- **"The point of view creates... the subject itself"** (*The Fold*): the formula Viveiros de Castro takes as the philosophical basis of perspectivism.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Collaborator:** Félix Guattari (co-author of *Capitalism and Schizophrenia*; not a separate page in this vault — the joint "Deleuze & Guattari" corpus is treated here).
- **Reworked by anthropologists:** [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] (perspectivism as "becoming-Amazonian of Deleuze"); read alongside [[latour-bruno|Latour]] (actor-network as rhizome), [[wagner-roy|Wagner]], and [[strathern-marilyn|Strathern]].
- **Relation to structuralism:** Viveiros de Castro (with Maniglier) reads Deleuze's philosophy as "an extreme effort to deterritorialize [[structuralism]]" — extending, not rejecting, its insights.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Viveiros de Castro's own reservation targets *What Is Philosophy?* (with Guattari), which he and Skafish read as a "conservative, Eurocentric turn" restricting the concept (and hence philosophy) to Europe and casting non-Western thought as mere "figures," "religions," or "wisdoms." Viveiros de Castro sidesteps rather than confronts this Eurocentrism, exploiting the concept's non-referentiality to grant Amerindian cosmology full standing as thought (see [[ontological-turn-debate]]).

## Historiography

Deleuze appears in this vault only through the anthropological reception documented in *Cannibal Metaphysics*; his philosophical corpus is not primary-ingested. The page is deliberately scoped to that reception.
