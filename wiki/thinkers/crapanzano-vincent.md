# Vincent Crapanzano

American anthropologist (b. 1939); contributor to *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* with "Hermes’ Dilemma: The Masking of Subversion in Ethnographic Description." Named among seminar “innovators” in ethnographic writing (with Rabinow, Rosaldo, Fischer). Prior experimental monographs *Tuhami* (1980) and *Waiting* (1985) are cited across the experimental moment but not yet ingested as sources.

## Overview

Crapanzano’s WC essay is a rhetorical analysis of how ethnographic description promises not to lie while performing interpretive tricks that establish authority — the Hermes/trickster model that sharpens Clifford’s [[partial-truths]] definition.

## Contributions

*(Attributed to Crapanzano in Writing Culture 1986.)*

- Ethnographer as **Hermes**: messenger, boundary-crosser, trickster, hermeneut — promising not to lie, never undertaking to tell the whole truth.
- Provisional field interpretation vs definitive published presentation.
- Close readings: **Catlin** O-Kee-Pa (hyptotyposis, pathopoeia, credibility crisis); **Goethe** Roman carnival (theatrical deixis, allegory of individual destiny); **Geertz Deep Play** (puns, entry tale, generalized “Balinese,” pronoun erasure, ungrounded subjectivity, over-the-shoulder interpretive hierarchy).
- Thesis: authorial authority **masks subversion** that description performs.
- Hermes’ partial truth is structural, not a correctable method defect alone.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Seminar innovator; engages [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]] as target of rhetorical critique; experimental monographs feed Marcus & Fischer’s catalogue in [[anthropology-as-cultural-critique]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Geertz defenders may read Deep Play analysis as uncharitable; see [[writing-culture-debate]]. Tyler cites Crapanzano among works in the “right spirit” of post-modern ethnography without completed Tylerian exemplars.
