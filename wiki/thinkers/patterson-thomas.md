# Thomas C. Patterson

## Overview

American archaeologist and, from the 1960s, a central-coast collaborator who gave [[moseley-michael|Michael Moseley]] the opportunity to work on early coastal sites (1966–67). Later a prominent theorist of the political economy of archaeology; this page reflects only his early Andean fieldwork as seen through [[moseley-maritime-foundations-1975|Moseley 1975]].

## Contributions

- **Preceramic demography** — Patterson (1971) calculated a ~2,900% demographic increase in the [[ancon|Ancón–Chillón]] area from the Lithic Stage to the introduction of pottery, which Moseley extrapolated as "for every Encanto hunter there were thirty Gaviota fishermen."
- **Central-coast chronology** — with Moseley (1968) established Late Preceramic and Early Ceramic culture sequences and dated the Huaca La Florida style ~1750–1650 BC.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator with [[lanning-edward|Lanning]]; opened the central-coast research program to Moseley, whom he co-authored with. Later work in Marxist and political-economy archaeology falls outside this ingest.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

His demographic-increase figures feed Moseley's "demographic deviation" model; treated as attributed single-study estimates, not settled facts.
