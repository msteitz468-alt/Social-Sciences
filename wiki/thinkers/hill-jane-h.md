# Jane H. Hill

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist (University of Arizona); contributor to the [[language-ideology]] program. In [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language*]] ch. 7, analyzes ideological complexity of promising in American presidential politics — dual metapragmatic discourses of **truth** and **theater** under a personalist ideology of intending agents.

## Contributions

- *Regimes* ch. 7: Bush “Read my lips: No new taxes” as staged straight talk; 1990 tax raise read under truth discourse as broken promise; promising as overdetermined masculine performance; personalism reproduced via journalistic blame.
- Earlier Mexicano language-ideology work (gendered nostalgia for “respect”) cited in Kroskrity’s intro.

## Intellectual Genealogy

SAR 1994 language-ideologies seminar; US linguistic anthropology.
