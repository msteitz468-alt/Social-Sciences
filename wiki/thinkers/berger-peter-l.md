# Peter L. Berger

## Overview

Austrian-born American sociologist (1929–2017), co-author with [[luckmann-thomas|Thomas Luckmann]] of *[[berger-luckmann-social-construction-1966|The Social Construction of Reality]]* (1966) — the postwar treatise that redefined the [[sociology-of-knowledge]] around everyday knowledge and founded the [[social-construction-of-reality]] research program. Also a major figure in the sociology of religion (works beyond this ingest not yet in the collection). At the time of the joint treatise: Graduate Faculty, New School for Social Research.

## Contributions

With Luckmann (all attributed to the 1966 joint text unless noted):

- **Program:** Reality is socially constructed; sociology of knowledge analyses the process — whatever *passes for* knowledge, especially common-sense knowledge of [[reality-of-everyday-life|everyday life]].
- **Dialectic:** [[externalization-objectivation-internalization|Society is a human product / objective reality / man is a social product]].
- **Objective order:** [[habitualization]] → [[institutionalization]] (reciprocal [[typification]]); [[legitimation]] and [[symbolic-universe|symbolic universes]]; [[reification]] as forgetfulness of authorship.
- **Subjective order:** [[primary-socialization]] / [[secondary-socialization]]; [[plausibility-structure|plausibility structures]]; conversation as reality-maintenance; identity in dialectic with society.
- **Disciplinary placement:** Dual character of society (Durkheimian facticity + Weberian meaning); sociology as humanistic empirical science in conversation with history and philosophy.

Deep program analysis: [[hubs/theory/social-construction-of-reality]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Formative:** [[schutz-alfred|Alfred Schutz]] (phenomenological sociology of the life-world — Part One debt). Preface also notes Weber via Carl Mayer; Durkheim via Albert Salomon; thanks to Kellner, Pullberg, Kurt Wolff, Anton Zijderveld, Brigitte Berger.
- **Collaborator:** [[luckmann-thomas|Thomas Luckmann]] (joint 1962–66 project).
- **Theoretical ingredients they name:** Marx (activity/world; ideology), Durkheim, Weber, [[mead-george-herbert|Mead]] / [[symbolic-interactionism]], philosophical anthropology (Plessner, Gehlen).
