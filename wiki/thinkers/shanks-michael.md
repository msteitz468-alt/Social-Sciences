# Michael Shanks

British archaeologist and co-architect (with [[tilley-christopher|Christopher Tilley]]) of a major Anglophone **critical / post-processual** theoretical program in the mid-1980s. Primary-ingested: *[[shanks-tilley-social-theory-and-archaeology-1987|Social Theory and Archaeology]]* (Polity 1987 / UNM 1988) and *[[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987|Re-Constructing Archaeology]]* (CUP 1987; Routledge 2nd ed. 1992).

## Overview

Shanks co-taught Cambridge Archaeology seminars (1986) that became *Social Theory and Archaeology*. With Tilley he argued that archaeology remains "deeply empiricist and antitheoretical" despite the new archaeology; that the past–present gap cannot be closed by more excavation or middle-range empiricism alone; and that archaeology must become **cultural critique** and **political practice** in the present. *Re-Constructing Archaeology* extends the program into positivism/value-freedom critique, museum aesthetics, hermeneutics, social ontology, and two material-culture analyses. Named among originators of [[post-processual-archaeology]] (with [[hodder-ian|Hodder]], Tilley, and associates). Deep Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/post-processual-archaeology]].

## Contributions

*(Attributed; densified from the 1987 co-authored volumes.)*

### From *Social Theory and Archaeology* (1987)

- **Theory as practice, not methodology.** Against reducing theory to methods for more objective recovery of the past; theory as reflection, critique, and performance mediating past and present (Ch. 1; Appendix §1).
- **Logic of contingency** vs logic of necessity in social archaeology ([[logic-of-contingency]]; Ch. 2) — drawing on Laclau & Mouffe against totalizing social logics and evolutionary rational substrata.
- **Thirteen-point critique** of processual social archaeology (reified variables, functionalism, occlusion of the individual, middle-range without social process, neo-evolutionary facilitation).
- **Subject as social trace**; ideology as signifying practice reproducing the social totality (Ch. 3; Althusserian framing as adapted).
- **Material culture as active signifying practice** — symbolic order in political and ideological relations (Ch. 4; densifies [[material-culture]]).
- **Critique of abstract chronological time** and of evolutionary stage models / systems prime-movers (Chs. 5–6); stability rather than change needs explaining (Appendix 5.1).
- **Politics of theory:** no neutral archaeology; validity assessed by intention, interest, politics, morality; archaeology as critique (Ch. 7; Appendix §6); call for archaeological poetics of how the past is written.

### From *Re-Constructing Archaeology* (1987/1992)

- **Past–present mediation** and critique of commodified/allochronic time; archaeology as cultural capitalism and spectacular CRM (Ch. 1).
- **Positivism of the 'new' archaeology** — naturalism, phenomenalism, hypothetico-deductive prestige (Ch. 2).
- **Value-freedom as ideology** importing capitalist values via economic archaeology, systems, cultural evolution, mathematization (Ch. 3).
- **[[redemptive-aesthetic]]** for museums — against commodity, aesthetic, narrative, heritage-myth, and hero-archaeologist modes (Ch. 4).
- **[[fourfold-hermeneutic]]** expanding Giddens's double hermeneutic; dialectics beyond pure hermeneutics (Ch. 5).
- **Object of [[social-archaeology]]** — MC patterning ↔ practices/strategies/structures; agency, structuration, ideology (Ch. 6).
- **Style as ideology** — TRB ceramics at Fjälkinge No. 9 (Ch. 7); **modern design under constraint** — British/Swedish beer cans (Ch. 8).
- **Radical pluralism**; feminist and Indigenous stakes; 1992 Appendix replies to subjectivity/relativism/fiction/impotency charges (Ch. 9 + App.).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Co-author / collaborator:** [[tilley-christopher]].
- **Interlocutors named in the 1987 volumes:** [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]], [[giddens-anthony|Anthony Giddens]], Mike Rowlands, Grant Chambers; seminar chairs including Colin Renfrew, Matthew Johnson; 2nd-ed reception cluster (Kristiansen, Watson, *NAR* 22: Bender, Herschend, Nordbladh, Olsen, Renfrew, Trigger, Wenke).
- **Draws on (as S&T present them):** Laclau & Mouffe; Althusser on ideology; Giddens on structuration/double hermeneutic; Foucault/Lacan on subject; Critical Theory (Adorno, Horkheimer, Habermas early); Benjamin, Barthes, Derrida as used.
- **Positioned against:** positivism and functionalism as twin pillars of 'new' archaeology; middle-range empiricism as cure for past–present isolation; evolutionary stage models; neutral/disinterested archaeology; theory-as-pharmacopoeia of methods; value-freedom; reified museum/heritage presentation.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Reception of Shanks & Tilley is inseparable from the broader [[processual-postprocessual-debate]]: critics charge relativism, under-specification of method, and polemical caricature of processualism; supporters treat the volumes as founding critical charters.
