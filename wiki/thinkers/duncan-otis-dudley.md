# Otis Dudley Duncan

Otis Dudley Duncan (1921–2004) was an American sociologist and demographer whose quantitative program reshaped mid-century stratification research. With [[blau-peter-m|Peter M. Blau]] he co-authored *[[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-1967|The American Occupational Structure]]* (1967), the founding statement of [[status-attainment]] research, and he is the namesake of the [[socioeconomic-index|Duncan socioeconomic index (SEI)]] used to score occupations on a continuous status scale.

## Overview

Duncan's contribution in the 1967 collaboration is inseparable from the book's technical core: occupational status measurement, path analysis of the stratification process, and the integration of mobility tables with multivariate models. The OCG design and SEI apparatus made national occupational mobility statistically tractable at a scale mobility-table tradition alone had not achieved.

## Contributions

- Co-architect of the Blau–Duncan path model of occupational achievement ([[blau-duncan-american-occupational-structure-study]]).
- Developer/popularizer of the socioeconomic index linking education and income composition of occupations to a status score.
- Methodological standardization of regression, path coefficients, and mobility-distribution analysis in U.S. stratification.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator: [[blau-peter-m]]. Intellectual context: postwar survey research, demography, and the Chicago–Michigan quantitative sociology nexus (OCG analysis spanned Chicago and Michigan). Subsequent status-attainment work (e.g. Wisconsin model associated with William H. Sewell Sr. and colleagues) extended the program; keep distinct from [[sewell-william-jr|William H. Sewell Jr.]] (historian).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Critiques of status-attainment (class structure vs continuous status; omission of labor-market dualism and networks; gender exclusion of the OCG male sample) attach to the program Duncan co-founded; record them on [[status-attainment]] and the study page rather than as personal charges.
