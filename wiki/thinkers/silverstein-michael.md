# Michael Silverstein

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist (University of Chicago); principal architect of a Peircean, semiotic [[linguistic-anthropology]] and of the modern concept of **[[language-ideology|linguistic ideologies]]** (1979). Contributor to [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language*]] with the major theoretical essay linking Whorf to the linguistic imagination of nationality and the [[monoglot-standard]].

## Contributions

- 1979 definition of linguistic ideologies as rationalizations of perceived language structure and use — charter citation for the program.
- Indexical order, metapragmatics, and regimentation of denotation (densify from primary essays as ingested).
- *Regimes* ch. 3: Whorfianism re-read through nationalist deixis and monoglot public language; dialogue with Benedict Anderson.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Chicago linguistic anthropology; deep engagement with Peirce, Whorf, and Jakobson; interlocutor across the 1994 SAR seminar cohort.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Prose density and technical apparatus often noted; empirical reach of monoglot-standard thesis debated in plurilingual contexts (addressed comparatively elsewhere in *Regimes*).
