# Annette Lareau

## Overview

Annette Lareau is an American sociologist of education and family life. This page is grounded primarily in *[[lareau-unequal-childhoods-2011|Unequal Childhoods]]* (2003; 2nd ed. 2011). Study: [[lareau-unequal-childhoods-study]].

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Lareau.)*

- **[[concerted-cultivation]]** vs **[[accomplishment-of-natural-growth]]** as classed childrearing logics.
- Empirical demonstration that middle-class parenting transmits institutional advantages (language, entitlement, interventions) while working-class/poor parenting yields different skills and constraints — a cultural mechanism of inequality complementary to [[cultural-capital]] / Bourdieu.
- Race crossed with class: class often dominates patterning of daily organization; race remains consequential in institutional encounters.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Bourdieu ([[habitus]], [[cultural-capital]], [[cultural-reproduction]]); US ethnography of family and school; dialogue with Willis-style class culture work.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Positions.)* Sample size and generalizability; essentializing class cultures; under/over-weighting race; “deficiency” misreadings of natural growth (Lareau resists deficiency framing).

## Historiography

2nd edition Part IV (follow-up of focal children into young adulthood) is a major restudy moment for the original thesis.
