# Donald K. Grayson

Zooarchaeologist / Quaternary scientist known for critical reanalysis of late Pleistocene extinction chronologies and, with [[meltzer-david|David Meltzer]], for the “requiem for overkill” critique of [[martin-paul|Paul Martin]]’s blitzkrieg model. Densified from [[meltzer-first-peoples-2009|Meltzer 2009]] Ch. 8 (and Monte Verde visit note). Dates as Meltzer: radiocarbon BP.

## Overview

Grayson’s work emphasizes which extinct taxa actually have secure terminal dates and secure cultural associations — a methods-first challenge to narrative overkill models. With Meltzer he produced the kill-site census that is the empirical centerpiece of the anti-blitzkrieg position on the [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]].

## Contributions

### Kill-site census and “Requiem for North American Overkill”

- With Meltzer, systematically vetted alleged Clovis kills (including fluted point near fossil bone): **76 sites**. Criteria: points/tools in positions suggesting death; compelling butchery; stacking/arrangement unexplained by natural causes (C-6-037).
- **Results:** only **14 of 76** show humans as agents of death/dismemberment; in those 14 only **mammoth and mastodon** — no secure Clovis kills of the other **33** extinct genera despite abundant horse fossils, etc. (C-6-038). Four of fourteen clustered in San Pedro Valley (possibly same group).
- “Requiem for North American Overkill” declared overkill dead; Fiedel & Gary Haynes vigorous defense (applauded scrupulous vetting; charged misrepresentation/outmoded data; “doubletalk”) (C-6-043).
- Epistemological line Meltzer associates with the collaboration: rare hypothesis that predicts lack of evidence only because evidence is lacking (C-6-039, C-6-082).

### Island extinctions multi-causal; climate research program

- POSITION on New Zealand/Pacific: not hunting alone — fires, rats/dogs, diseases combined; island life highly susceptible (tiny islands, easily destroyed ecosystems, small non-replenishable populations; flightless birds) (C-6-035). Limits transfer of island overkill analogies to continents.
- Climate hypotheses need genus/species-specific responses (Grayson et al. via Meltzer) — climate underdeveloped relative to overkill’s neat story (C-6-053).

### Monte Verde 1997 statement

Meltzer reports *American Antiquity* statement after the January 1997 Monte Verde visit was drafted by **Grayson & Meltzer**, signed after cooling-off, urging others to judge Dillehay’s volumes themselves (C-3-078). Grayson thus appears in both extinction and peopling-reception bookkeeping.

### Great Basin fauna (secondary mention)

Meltzer Ch. 2 notes Great Basin sky-island connectivity literature involving Brown and Grayson (C-2-043) — faunal biogeography credentials beyond overkill polemic.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator of [[meltzer-david|Meltzer]] on overkill and Monte Verde statement; interlocutor of [[martin-paul|Paul Martin]]; opposed in print by Fiedel & Gary Haynes. Network of Quaternary zooarchaeology and Paleoindian studies.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Overkill defenders’ responses (14 sites “phenomenally rich”; <250 kg preservation claims; Signor-Lipps; eastern Holocene carcass argument) belong on [[pleistocene-overkill-debate]] as positions — Meltzer replies recorded there (C-6-044–048). Do not treat “Requiem” as field-wide consensus; resolution_status remains open. Post-2009 radiocarbon and aDNA databases may reframe terminal chronologies — date-stamp updates.
