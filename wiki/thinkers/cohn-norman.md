# Norman Cohn

Norman Rufus Colin Cohn (1915–2007), British historian of millenarian and demonological traditions; best known for *The Pursuit of the Millennium* (1957) and for *[[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-1967|Warrant for Genocide]]* (1967), the classic scholarly history of the *[[protocols-of-the-elders-of-zion|Protocols of the Elders of Zion]]* and the [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]].

## Overview

Cohn's program connects medieval and early-modern apocalyptic and demonological fantasies to modern political mass murder. In the Serif foreword to *Warrant* he describes a "subterranean world" of pathological fantasies produced by crooks and half-educated fanatics that can, under crisis, capture multitudes and become state power. *Warrant for Genocide* is his major modern-history study: he argues (attributed) that a quasi-demonological conspiracy myth, not ordinary interest-conflict alone, supplied a **warrant for genocide** under war, revolution, and depression.

## Contributions

- **Protocols scholarship:** reconstruction of sources (esp. Joly *Dialogue aux Enfers* plagiarism), Russian Okhrana/Nilus milieu, White-army and interwar dissemination, Nazi use — [[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-study]].
- **Myth of Jewish world-conspiracy:** analytic object linking medieval Antichrist/Jew tropes to modern political [[antisemitism]].
- **Millenarian historiography:** *Pursuit of the Millennium* already listed on [[millenarianism]]; this ingest densifies the modern political-myth side of his corpus (Nilus Antichrist frames; Rosenberg's apocalyptic cast of mind).
- **Methodological stance:** treats "uninviting" subterranean literature as historically consequential — not beneath scholarly notice.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Full training genealogy not in the ingested source. *Warrant* acknowledgements place him in conversation with Boris Nicolaevsky (Berne trial witness; archives), James Parkes and Leon Poliakov (antisemitism/Protocols), and chapter critics including Carsten, Higham, Laqueur, Mosse, Schapiro; research infrastructure includes the Wiener Library and Stanford CASBS. Gluckman and others cite Cohn on millenarianism ([[millenarianism]]). Dialogue with later Holocaust sociology runs through wiki cross-links to [[bauman-zygmunt]] and [[holocaust-and-modernity-debate]], not through personal training ties.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*Warrant* was widely translated (Cohn: nine languages) and remained in print decades later. Subsequent historiography has expanded Okhrana and Nazi-agency archives; Cohn's plagiarism demonstration and dissemination narrative remain standard reference points. His weighting of myth relative to bureaucracy (Bauman) or eliminationist culture (Goldhagen) is a live comparative question — see debate page — not a settled ranking.
