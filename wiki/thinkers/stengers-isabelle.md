## Overview

Isabelle Stengers is a Belgian philosopher of science, trained as a chemist, known for her collaboration with Ilya Prigogine (*Order Out of Chaos*) and her scholarship on Alfred North Whitehead and Gilles Deleuze. As with [[despret-vinciane]], this page is built entirely from her role as a cited interlocutor in Donna Haraway's *Staying with the Trouble* (2016); all claims are Haraway's relay of Stengers's positions, attributed accordingly *(Haraway 2016)*.

## Contributions

Haraway credits Stengers — described as "chemist, scholar of Whitehead and Deleuze, unruly feminist philosopher" — with the concept of "cosmopolitics": the position that "decisions must take place somehow in the presence of those who will bear their consequences" *(Haraway 2016)*. Haraway states this framing gives her "courage": "Isabelle Stengers's sense of cosmopolitics gives me courage" *(Haraway 2016)*, and situates it within "feminist communitarian anarchism" and a Whiteheadian philosophical lineage.

Haraway also relays Stengers's account of Gaia as "intrusive" — a fearful, non-nurturing power that "intrudes on our categories of thought," is indifferent to human intentions, and "puts into question our very existence" *(Haraway 2016)*; Haraway attributes to Stengers the specific phrase "multi-faced Gaïa" for this framing, distinguishing it from Lovelock and Margulis's original Gaia hypothesis (see [[gaia-hypothesis]]).

Haraway attributes to Stengers, drawing on Félix Guattari, the image of "com-menting" — thinking-with as becoming-with, relaying a cat's-cradle/string-figure pattern between players without demanding fidelity, only "a particular kind of loyalty": "com-menting, if it means thinking-with, that is becoming-with, is in itself a way of relaying ... the relay is now in your hands, together with the demand that you do not proceed with 'mechanical confidence'" (Stengers, quoted by Haraway) *(Haraway 2016)*.

Haraway records that the Camille Stories (Chapter 8 of *Staying with the Trouble*) originated in a 2013 writing workshop, "Narration Spéculative," at Cerisy, France, part of a colloquium Stengers organized on "gestes spéculatifs" ("speculative gestures"), co-written initially with filmmaker Fabrizio Terranova and [[despret-vinciane]] *(Haraway 2016)*. With Despret, Stengers co-authored *Women Who Make a Fuss: The Unfaithful Daughters of Virginia Woolf*; Haraway also relays, via Despret, a parable Stengers originated about twelve camels and inheritance, used to theorize "starting from" a heritage rather than merely applying it (see [[despret-vinciane]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

The source documents Stengers's collaboration with Ilya Prigogine (*Order Out of Chaos*, cited by title) and scholarly engagement with Whitehead's philosophy, but not formal teacher/student relationships. Her closest documented partnerships in this material are with [[despret-vinciane]] (co-authorship) and with [[haraway-donna]], who names Stengers among her key interlocutors for "tentacular thinking" alongside [[latour-bruno]] and [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt]] *(Haraway 2016)*.
