# Gary M. Feinman

## Overview

MacArthur Curator of Anthropology at the Field Museum of Natural History; long-term collaborator of [[blanton-richard|Richard Blanton]] and [[kowalewski-stephen|Stephen Kowalewski]] in the Valley of Oaxaca regional surveys and a principal architect (with Blanton) of [[collective-action-theory]] and the corporate/network "dual-processual" framework in archaeology.

## Contributions

- Co-author of the Valley of Oaxaca Settlement Pattern Project syntheses and of *[[blanton-ancient-oaxaca-2022|Ancient Oaxaca]]* (1999; 2nd ed. 2022), which reframes [[monte-alban]]'s founding through [[collective-action-theory]].
- With Blanton, Kowalewski, and Peregrine (1996), proposed the dual-processual distinction between corporate and network (exclusionary) political strategies — an influential alternative to unilinear neoevolutionist typologies.
- With [[nicholas-linda-m|Linda Nicholas]], conducted household-scale excavations (Ejutla, El Palmillo) linking domestic craft production to regional economies — an argument for market exchange in prehispanic Mesoamerica.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained at the University of Michigan and CUNY (PhD 1980, City University of New York, under the broader Michigan-linked Oaxaca projects' orbit); career-long collaboration network with Blanton, Kowalewski, Nicholas, and Marcus/Flannery's Oaxaca cohort — often on the opposite side of the [[monte-alban]]-founding debate from [[marcus-joyce|Joyce Marcus]] and [[flannery-kent-v|Kent Flannery]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Critics of collective-action readings of Monte Albán (notably Marcus and Flannery's actor-centered, conquest-oriented account) argue the confederation/cooperation model underplays coercion and elite agency; the dispute is recorded at [[monte-alban]] and [[collective-action-theory]], not adjudicated here.
