# Anthony Giddens

British sociologist and social theorist; principal architect of **[[structuration-theory|structuration theory]]** and a major late-twentieth-century theorist of **[[radicalised-modernity|radicalised (high) modernity]]**, [[trust]], risk, and [[globalization]]. In this vault the primary ingested bases are *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]* (1984) and *[[giddens-consequences-of-modernity-1990|The Consequences of Modernity]]* (1990). Earlier programmatic works (*New Rules*, *Central Problems*) and later writings (*Modernity and Self-Identity*, Third Way politics) are not yet fully ingested as primary sources.

## Overview

Giddens's Critical Turn project reorganizes classical and contemporary social theory around the **[[duality-of-structure|duality of structure]]**: structure as medium and outcome of the practices it recursively organizes. He rejects structure–agency dualism in favor of mutual constitution, while retaining [[knowledgeability|knowledgeable]] agents, [[routinization]], [[time-space-distanciation|time-space]], and institutional analysis.

*Consequences of Modernity* (West Lectures, Stanford 1988; published 1990) extends that apparatus into a **discontinuist** institutional analysis of modernity: three dynamisms ([[time-space-distanciation]], [[disembedding]], reflexivity); four institutional dimensions (capitalism, industrialism, surveillance, military power); [[trust]] in [[abstract-systems]]; [[globalization]] as inherent to modern institutions; and the claim that we inhabit **[[radicalised-modernity|radicalised modernity]]** (a "juggernaut"), not postmodernity as then popularly labelled.

## Contributions

**Structuration program (*Constitution* 1984):**
- **[[structuration-theory]]** — research program and conceptual architecture.
- **[[duality-of-structure]]** — structure as rules and resources instantiated in practice.
- Stratification model of the agent: [[discursive-consciousness]], [[practical-consciousness]], unconscious; [[ontological-security]] grounded in routine.
- **[[social-and-system-integration|Social and system integration]]**; [[time-space-distanciation]], [[locale]], [[regionalization]].
- **[[double-hermeneutic]]** — mutual interpretive interplay of social science and lay knowledge.
- Critique of functionalism, evolutionism, and hard methodological individualism (see [[methodological-individualism-debate]]; [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]).

**Modernity program (*Consequences* 1990):**
- **Discontinuist modernity** — pace, scope, and intrinsic institutional form separate modern from traditional orders; evolutionist "story lines" obscure this.
- **[[disembedding]]** via [[symbolic-tokens]] and [[expert-systems]] (= [[abstract-systems]]); counterpart [[reembedding]].
- **[[trust]]** apparatus: [[faceless-commitments]] vs facework; [[access-points]]; environments of trust/risk (pre-modern vs modern).
- **[[ontological-security]]** extended to high-consequence risk and basic trust (Erikson/Winnicott).
- **[[globalization]]** as intensification of worldwide social relations; four dimensions mapping institutional modernity; critique of IR monism and [[world-systems-analysis|Wallerstein]] economic monism.
- **[[juggernaut-of-modernity]]**; **[[high-consequence-risks]]**; four adaptive reactions; **[[utopian-realism]]**.
- **[[radicalised-modernity]]** vs epochal postmodernity — position on [[postmodernity-epochal-break-debate]].
- Transformation of intimacy / self as reflexive project (feeds [[pure-relationship]] fully named in 1991).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

**Connell (*Southern Theory* 2007, attributed):** [[connell-raewyn|Connell]] reads *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]* as general theory whose duality-of-structure concept arises from internal European/North American antinomies; the tribal / class-divided / class-society grid is a form of grand ethnography that can refer to empire via "time-space edges" without naming imperialism; colonisation is largely untheorised. Connell groups Giddens with Coleman and Bourdieu as cases of Northern general theory (see [[southern-theory]]). Attributed critique, not wiki-voice verdict.


Standard critical lines (secondary literature still thin in vault): alleged abstractness and thin empirical program; residual subjectivism or objectivism depending on the critic; underdeveloped culture, gender, and material inequality relative to feminist and cultural theory; political reception of later "Third Way" writings separate from the theoretical system. Record each as **position**. Standing debate homes: [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]; [[postmodernity-epochal-break-debate]].

[[sewell-william-jr|William H. Sewell Jr.]] (*[[sewell-logics-of-history-2005|Logics of History]]*) adopts duality of structure but argues Giddens leaves "structure" underspecified and resources-as-virtual contradictory; reworks via [[transposable-schemas|schemas]] (Attributed).

## Historiography

*Constitution* consolidates the 1970s program; *Consequences* began as the Raymond Fred West Memorial Lectures (Stanford, April 1988). Reception history and biographies not yet ingested. Giddens was a close reader of drafts of [[bauman-zygmunt|Bauman]]'s *[[bauman-modernity-holocaust-1989|Modernity and the Holocaust]]* (1989).

## Engagement — DiMaggio & Powell, *The New Institutionalism in Organizational Analysis* (1991)

[[dimaggio-paul|DiMaggio]] and [[powell-walter-w|Powell]] ([[powell-dimaggio-new-institutionalism-1991]]) adopt a structurationist idiom for [[organizational-field|organizational fields]] — describing field formation as a "structuration" process (DiMaggio 1983) — and credit Giddens's distinction between practical and discursive consciousness, along with his account of routine as a control on diffuse anxiety, as resources for [[new-institutionalism]]'s theory of practical action (attributed). They critique Giddens, however, for offering "little... to explain why some interactions go better than others or why routines create particular stable patterns" — his emphasis on "knowledgeable agents" (against the "cultural dope" model) is not, on their reading, matched by an account of the sources of that knowledge.

## Gamble 1999 — Palaeolithic application

*(Attributed — [[gamble-clive]], [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999]]; as of Gamble 1999. Application of Giddens’s concepts to deep-time archaeology, not a Giddens primary text.)*

[[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]] selects **[[structuration-theory|structuration]]** as the social-theory base for *The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe*: structure not external but contained in action; **agency** as capacity for action applies to Moderns, [[neanderthals]], and *Homo heidelbergensis*; duality of structure constraining and enabling. He uses Giddens’s **social integration** vs **system integration**; **practical vs discursive consciousness**; **ontological security** as trust grounded in intimate-network face-to-face investment; **routinization** of day-to-day social life as the bulk of the **landscape of habit**. “Release from proximity” — stretching relations across time/space beyond presence (Giddens 1984:35; Rodseth et al. 1991) — is the hallmark transition toward extended networks and complicated Cro-Magnon society. See [[intimate-effective-extended-networks]], [[release-from-proximity]], [[modern-human-origins-debate]].
