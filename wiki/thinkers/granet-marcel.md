# Marcel Granet

## Overview

Marcel Granet (1884–1940), French sinologist and Durkheimian sociologist, applied the methods of the Année Sociologique to classical Chinese texts, treating ritual, poetry, and kinship terminology as evidence for the social structures of ancient China. In this vault he appears as [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss's]] principal interlocutor and target for China in the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures of Kinship]]*. This page is a network stub grounded in that engagement, pending direct ingestion.

## Contributions

- Reconstructed ancient Chinese marriage and kinship from ritual texts, proposing an archaic system of **marriage categories** and reading the temple **chao–mu** ordering of ancestral tablets as a survival of an eight-class, alternating-generation marriage system.
- With Hodson and Held, among the few precursors Lévi-Strauss credited with "any inkling" of the [[restricted-exchange]]/[[generalized-exchange]] distinction (Granet's *chassé-croisé* and deferred-exchange terminology).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

In the *Elementary Structures* Lévi-Strauss drew heavily on Granet's Chinese data while overturning his **evolutionary sequencing** (four-class → eight-class → modern freedom): following Fêng, Lévi-Strauss argued the kinship nomenclature was the *result*, not the cause, of the mourning-grade system, and that Granet had reversed the causal direction and projected generation-stratification onto a mythical archaic past. Lévi-Strauss found Granet's chao–mu reconstruction logically incompatible with a functioning marriage system — "we are launched on a hazardous course but it leads nowhere" — while still crediting the underlying intuition of a structure based on alternating generations. Francis L. K. Hsu independently disputed Granet's chao–mu reading on textual grounds.
