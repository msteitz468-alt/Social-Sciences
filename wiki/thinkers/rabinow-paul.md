# Paul Rabinow

American anthropologist (b. 1944); author of *[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977|Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco]]* (1977) and contributor to *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]* with "Representations Are Social Facts: Modernity and Post-Modernity in Anthropology." Named among seminar innovators in ethnographic writing; also the volume’s principal **meta-critic** of textualism. Marcus’s Afterword credits Rabinow with calling for ethnography of anthropology’s professional culture.

## Overview

Rabinow’s WC essay offers a critical sociology of the textualist moment, drawing on Foucault and Bourdieu; frames representations as social facts rather than free literary play; stages Geertz vs Clifford; and advances **critical cosmopolitanism**.

## Contributions

*(Attributed to Rabinow in Writing Culture 1986.)*

- **Representations as social facts** — not free floating literary choices.
- Regime of truth (Foucault); anthropologize the West without anti-Occidentalism.
- Geertz vs Clifford staging; textualist meta-anthropology; four modes of authority; limits of dialogic/heteroglossic solutions.
- Jameson post-modernism (pastiche, mode rétro, textuality) applied to meta-ethnography — post-modernism as risk of **new canon**.
- Academic politics: Bourdieu; **corridor talk**; tenure micropolitics of the textual movement.
- Strathern/feminist anthropology mapped against experimentalists; four-position map.
- **Critical cosmopolitanism** as alternative orientation.
- Studying-up (Lyautey Morocco) as research direction.
- Prior *Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco* cited across WC/ACC as experimental precedent (Marcus: redemptive posture that explodes pure-subject illusion).

## Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco (1977)

Rabinow's earlier book — from 1968–69 doctoral fieldwork near **Sefrou** and in the
Middle Atlas village of **[[sidi-lahcen-lyussi]]**, done under [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]'s
Sefrou project — is a founding statement of [[reflexive-fieldwork|reflexive fieldwork]].
Taking [[ricoeur-paul|Ricoeur]]'s formula "the comprehension of the self by the detour of
the comprehension of the other" as its motto, it argues that ethnographic **facts are made,
not found** (doubly mediated by the anthropologist's presence and the informant's demanded
self-reflection — the "doubling of consciousness"), that a degree of **symbolic violence**
against informants is inherent in the field situation, and that the best informants are
marginal "insiders' outsiders." It is the direct precursor to his *Writing Culture*
intervention. Study: [[rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]] · deep analysis:
[[hubs/studies/rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]] · source:
[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977]].
