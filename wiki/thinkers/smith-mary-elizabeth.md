# Mary Elizabeth Smith

## Overview
American art historian (University of New Mexico), the leading authority of her generation on the [[mixtec-codices|Mixtec codices]] and the decipherment of Mixtec place signs. In [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) she wrote the topics on the Mixtec writing system, the earliest Mixtec dynastic records, Codex Selden's provenience, and regional points of view in the codices.

## Contributions
- Smith's method matched codex place signs to colonial lienzos and maps, anchoring the codices' dynastic histories ([[ethnohistory]]) to real Mixtec communities.
- She argued Codex Selden derives from the Valley of Nochixtlán (Jaltepec), reorienting readings of its dynastic narrative.

## Intellectual Genealogy
Trained in the Tulane art-historical school of Donald Robertson, working alongside the Caso tradition of codex studies ([[caso-alfonso|Alfonso Caso]]).
