# Christopher Chase-Dunn

Christopher Chase-Dunn (b. 1944) is an American sociologist in the [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]] tradition, associated with comparative world-systems theory (CWST) and research on semiperipheral development. Seeded from his 2004 chapter on paths toward global socialism.

## Overview

Chase-Dunn (2004) distinguishes structural globalization from the neoliberal "globalization project"; argues organizational innovations often emerge from the [[semiperiphery]]; and sketches a strategy of World Party education, semiperipheral democratic socialism, and mid-term UN/condominium governance through hegemonic-rivalry obstacles toward global democratic government. All claims attributed.

## Contributions

- CWST: nested exchange networks / comparative world-systems.
- Semiperipheral development as locus of transformative innovation.
- Programmatic link of [[antisystemic-movements]] to global democracy strategies.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Student of and collaborator in the Wallersteinian tradition; quantitative and comparative extensions of world-systems research.
