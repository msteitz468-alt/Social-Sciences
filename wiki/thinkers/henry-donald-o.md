# Donald O. Henry

## Overview

American prehistoric archaeologist; major ecological interpreter of the [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] and of the role of the [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] in Levantine forager adaptations. Associated with rejecting gradual pre-Natufian “broad spectrum” paths as the sole explanation of Natufian sedentism in favor of specialized intensive plant use plus specialized gazelle hunting (as presented in textbook syntheses and in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).

## Contributions

- **Younger Dryas and Natufian.** 2013 chapter evaluates climatic forcing on Natufian economy and settlement (attributed; densify from claims).
- **Specialized subsistence model.** Contrasts with Binford/Flannery broad-spectrum tradition as explanatory prime mover for Levantine sedentism.
- **Southern Jordan fieldwork.** Early Natufian and related occupations in steppic zones contributing to multi-regional chronology debates.

## Intellectual Genealogy

University of Tulsa; engaged with Bar-Yosef school debates on chronology and climate.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Climate-causation claims contested by other Levantine researchers (e.g., Maher, Banning & Chazan 2011, cited in the 2013 preface); positions side by side on culture and debate pages.
