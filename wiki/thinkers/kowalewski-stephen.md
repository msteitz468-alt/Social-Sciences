# Stephen A. Kowalewski

## Overview
American archaeologist (University of Georgia), a principal of the Valley of Oaxaca Settlement Pattern Project's full-coverage regional survey. In [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) he wrote the valley-floor settlement-pattern topics for every period from the Formative through Monte Albán V, plus the rank-size comparison of [[monte-alban|Monte Albán]] and [[teotihuacan|Teotihuacán]] hierarchies.

## Contributions
- Period-by-period valley-floor settlement data for the Valley of Oaxaca ([[archaeological-survey]]), the empirical backbone of demographic and political reconstructions of the [[zapotec-civilization|Zapotec]] sequence.
- Application of the rank-size rule to compare regional settlement hierarchies below Monte Albán and Teotihuacán.

## Intellectual Genealogy
Collaborator of [[blanton-richard|Richard Blanton]] on the Monte Albán and regional surveys; part of the [[flannery-kent-v|Flannery]]–[[marcus-joyce|Marcus]] Oaxaca seminar network.

## Zapotec Civilization (Marcus & Flannery 1996) — SPP demography

*(Attributed — [[marcus-joyce|Marcus]] & [[flannery-kent-v|Flannery]], *[[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-1996|Zapotec Civilization]]* 1996; Settlement Pattern Project numbers associated with Kowalewski and colleagues; study [[marcus-flannery-zapotec-civilization-study]].)*

Kowalewski is named among principals of the Valley of Oaxaca Settlement Pattern Project (with Blanton, Feinman, Finsten, Nicholas) that surveyed the full **~2150 km²** valley 1974–80 after the Monte Albán city survey. Marcus & Flannery use SPP demography as the quantitative backbone of their synthesis:

- Final prehispanic Valley of Oaxaca population **>160,000**; Province of Coyolapan (Central Valley System + surrounding mountains) estimated **350,000–367,000** Zapotec speakers.
- **Monte Albán IIIa** valley population ~**115,000** across 1075 known communities — demographic center of gravity shifts to Tlacolula (~510 communities) and Valle Grande (~455), with only ~110 in Etla/central regions.
- Tier structure and peer cities (Monte Albán ~16,500; Jalieza ~12,835; possible DMTG Tlacolula cluster ~12,292) and the count of **38** fortified/defensible IIIa sites housing almost two-thirds of valley population.
- Commercial intensification signals (mass gray-ware production; kiln wasters at Tlacochahuaya, Guelavia, Cuilapan, Yatzeche, Trinidad de Zaachila; chert core/flake production especially Tlacolula).

Marcus & Flannery note SPP surface estimates and excavation estimates sometimes disagree, yet projects agree most of the time — Kowalewski’s period-by-period valley-floor series remain the demography layer against which excavation cases are scaled.

## Ancient Oaxaca 2nd edition (Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas 2022)

*(Attributed — Blanton, Feinman, Kowalewski & Nicholas, [[blanton-ancient-oaxaca-2022|Ancient Oaxaca: The Monte Albán State]], 2nd ed., 2022.)*

Kowalewski is co-author of the 2022 volume and, with [[blanton-richard|Blanton]], supervised the Valley of Oaxaca regional survey completed in 1980 (2,150 km², 2,700+ sites), extending the demographic settlement-hierarchy work reported in Marcus & Flannery (1996). The book credits him with directing the later Central Mixteca Alta survey (Kowalewski et al. 2009) and the Coixtlahuaca survey (Kowalewski and Recorrido Arqueológico de Coixtlahuaca 2021), extending the full-coverage-survey dataset beyond the Valley of Oaxaca into the Mixteca Alta highlands. The 2022 book frames the combined highland Oaxaca survey corpus (Valley of Oaxaca, Nochixtlán Valley, Mixteca Alta, and adjacent areas) as "one of the largest 'macroregional' archaeological datasets in the world."
