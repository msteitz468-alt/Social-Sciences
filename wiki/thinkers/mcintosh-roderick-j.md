# Roderick J. McIntosh

## Overview

American archaeologist of West Africa’s Middle Niger, principal architect (with [[mcintosh-susan-keech|Susan Keech McIntosh]]) of the [[jenne-jeno|Jenné-jeno]] excavation and hinterland program that established early inland-savanna urbanism as an indigenous trajectory. In *[[mcintosh-ancient-middle-niger-2005|Ancient Middle Niger]]* (2005) he frames clustered, non-citadel cities as products of a [[self-organizing-landscape|self-organizing landscape]] and of [[heterarchy|heterarchical]] specialist corporations rather than coercive centralization.

## Contributions

*(Densified from McIntosh 2005 ingest.)*

- **City without Citadel:** urban scale and functional diversity without demonstrable palace/citadel or nested state hierarchy at early Jenné-jeno (attributed).
- **[[urban-cluster]] / polynucleated sprawl:** satellite mounds and functionally complementary sites as the urban form, not a single nucleated tell alone.
- **[[pulse-model]]:** model of successive specialist accommodation under floodplain/climate pulse dynamics.
- **[[historical-ecology]] application:** Middle Niger landforms, paleoclimate phase shifts, and risk-buffering subsistence as co-evolving with social complexity.
- Comparative lens on Mesopotamia, Nile, and northern China urban origins (Ch. 6).

Deep study analysis: [[hubs/studies/mcintosh-jenne-jeno-study|Jenné-jeno program — hub]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborates closely with [[mcintosh-susan-keech|Susan Keech McIntosh]] (ceramic sequence, co-excavation). Engages [[crumley-carole|Carole Crumley]] on [[heterarchy]]; situates program against citadel-seeking colonial/early archaeology and against external-stimulus (trans-Saharan trade) origin models for West African cities. Series editor dialogue with Rita P. Wright on alternative urbanisms. Comparative engagement with [[adams-robert-mcc|Robert McC. Adams]] Mesopotamian urban hinterland work.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

[[connah-graham|Connah]] (2016) accepts the importance of the heterarchy alternative but cautions that it often depends on *absence* of hierarchy evidence rather than positive proof, and that excavation samples remain small relative to site size — premature to conclude stratification was absent (attributed; see [[jenne-jeno]], [[heterarchy]]).

## Historiography

Reputation built on the 1977+ Jenné-jeno campaign that broke the “silent millennia” of savanna prehistory (as framed by Connah and the McIntoshes). Primary synthesis for teaching: *Ancient Middle Niger* (2005). Earlier field reports and *Peoples of the Middle Niger* (1998) feed the same program.
