# Herbert J. Gans

American urban sociologist and planner-critic. Enters this wiki first through [[granovetter-mark-s|Granovetter]]'s reanalysis of *The Urban Villagers* (1962) and the 1974 AJS exchange on whether network structure explains the West End's failure to fight urban renewal ([[west-end-network-fragmentation-debate]]).

## Overview

Stub for link integrity. Primary monographs not yet ingested; claims about the West End on other pages are attributed to Granovetter's reading of Gans or to Gans's 1974 commentary as captured in the SWT source PDF — not to a full *Urban Villagers* ingest.

## Contributions

*(From 1974 commentary only, attributed.)* Defended multi-factor explanation of West End non-mobilization (history, timing, political connection, class culture, subneighborhood identity) while conceding partial role for weak-tie scarcity; argued participant observation may under-detect weak ties but that bridging roles (shopkeepers, bartenders) were noted in the ethnography.
