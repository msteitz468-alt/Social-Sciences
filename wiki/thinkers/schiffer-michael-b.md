# Michael B. Schiffer (b. 1947)

American archaeologist; principal architect of **[[behavioral-archaeology|behavioral archaeology]]** and of the book-length synthesis of **[[formation-processes|formation processes]]** (*[[schiffer-formation-processes-1987|Formation Processes of the Archaeological Record]]*, 1987).

## Overview

Schiffer’s program holds that the cultural past is knowable only when the nature of archaeological evidence — including how people and nature transform materials — is understood (attributed, Schiffer 1987 preface). He distinguishes [[systemic-context|systemic]] from [[archaeological-context|archaeological]] context, classifies cultural formation processes (reuse, deposition, reclamation, disturbance), develops refuse typologies and discard equations, and treats the archaeologist’s own procedures as formation processes.

## Contributions

- **Transformation theory / behavioral archaeology** — with [[reid-j-jefferson|J. Jefferson Reid]] and others; nomothetic c-transforms and n-transforms; three tenets: formal/spatial/quantitative/relational transforms; creation of non-behavioral patterning; regularities as laws (attributed, Schiffer 1987 Ch. 1).
- **[[refuse-types]]** — primary, secondary, residual primary (McKellar Principle), de facto refuse, curate behavior interface with Binford.
- **[[old-wood-problem]]** — systematic antiquity bias in wood radiocarbon (1982/1986/1987 Ch. 11 Hohokam).
- **Broken K reanalysis** — ceramic formation processes undermine direct social factor interpretations ([[hill-broken-k-pueblo-study]]).
- **Identification checklist** (1983 AmAnt → 1987 Ch. 10) — multi-trace deposit analysis.
- Later people-and-things broadening (Skibo and Schiffer 2008; secondary via Johnson 2011).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained / network:** Arizona behavioral-archaeology circle; Reid urged the 1987 book (1982). Acknowledgments include Wilcox, Sullivan, Dean, Wilk, South, Shipman; Skibo among full-manuscript readers (attributed, 1987 preface).
- **Dialogue/dispute with [[binford-lewis|Lewis Binford]]** — shared middle-range / formation concerns; contested branding and philosophy of C-transforms vs [[pompeii-premise]] (see Johnson 2011 secondary; Binford ethnoarchaeology in vault).
- **Reacted against** correlates-only New Archaeology social inference (Hill 1970 as exemplar).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Binford rejected the “behavioral archaeology” label and attacked distortion-of-a-living-system framing (attributed secondary). Critical fieldwork writers (Lucas et al.) place formation-process theory still inside representational models of the record. Vocabulary of refuse types and formation processes is now standard even where the program label is less used.

## Historiography

Primary ingest of *Formation Processes* (1987) densifies this page beyond Johnson textbook reportage. *Behavioral Archeology* (1976) and Skibo & Schiffer (2008) still outstanding for full program history.
