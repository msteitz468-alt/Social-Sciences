# Paul Kirchhoff

## Overview

Paul Kirchhoff is credited by Coe & Koontz (2013) as the anthropologist who first proposed the
name "Mesoamerica" to designate the culture area comprising ancient Mexico and the Maya region,
"including much of the great constriction that separates the masses of North and South
America." Biographical detail beyond this coinage is not available from the ingested source and
is flagged `[[unknown]]` pending a dedicated biographical source.

## Contributions

- Coined **Mesoamerica** as a culture-area concept, now the standard term of art for the region
  spanning ancient Mexico and the Maya lowlands/highlands (Coe & Koontz 2013).
