# Manuel Castells

Spanish sociologist of cities, technology, and global capitalism; principal architect of [[network-society-theory|network society theory]] in *The Information Age* trilogy (1996–98; Vol. I 2nd ed. with 2010 preface). Summary page. **Thinkers Hub deferred** — single volume ingested; full intellectual biography needs *Urban Question*, *Informational City*, and Vols II–III plus secondary biography.

## Overview

Castells argues that around the end of the second millennium a new social form — the [[network-society|network society]] — emerges from the interaction of the information-technology revolution, the restructuring of capitalism, and the crisis of industrial statism. Networks become the dominant social morphology; space is reorganized as a [[space-of-flows|space of flows]] articulated with (and often dominating) the [[space-of-places|space of places]]; time is compressed into [[timeless-time|timeless time]]. Capitalism persists and expands under a new mode of development he calls [[informationalism]]. Vol. I builds the architecture of economy, work, culture, space, and time; identity, movements, and the state are deferred to later volumes (not yet ingested).

## Contributions

From *[[castells-rise-of-network-society-1996|The Rise of the Network Society]]* (attributed):

- **Program:** [[network-society-theory]] — Theory Hub [[hubs/theory/network-society-theory]]; study page [[castells-rise-network-society-study]].
- **Concepts:** [[network-society]], [[informationalism]], [[mode-of-development]], [[space-of-flows]], [[space-of-places]], [[timeless-time]], [[real-virtuality]], [[network-enterprise]], [[informational-city]], [[spirit-of-informationalism]].
- **Economy:** informational / global / networked triad; productivity and financial-globalization analysis; network enterprise and East Asian comparative organization.
- **Work:** G-7 employment transformation; rejection of jobless-society as main story; flex-timers and capital-global / labor-local thesis.
- **Culture:** culture of real virtuality; 2010 mass self-communication update.
- **Method:** triangulation of convergent secondary sources and official statistics; anti-determinist technology stance (“Technology does not determine society: it embodies it. But nor does society determine technological innovation: it uses it.”).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Vol. I positions against technological determinism, end-of-cities, and jobless-society theses. External critiques (periodization, Northern bias, grand-theory status) recorded on the theory page; fuller map pending secondary literature. Dialogue with [[global-city-theory]]: complementary scales — Sassen on production of control in places; Castells on process/space of flows.

## Historiography

Program-builder for network/information-society sociology from the late 1980s–1990s; dual reception as analytical toolkit and policy vocabulary. Collection holds only Vol. I of *The Information Age* in this session. 2010 preface revalidates the architecture after the 2008 crisis and Web 2.0.
