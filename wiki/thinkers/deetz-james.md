# James Deetz

American historical archaeologist; principal popularizer of **[[historical-archaeology]]** as the archaeology of everyday **[[material-culture]]** in early America. Author of *[[deetz-in-small-things-forgotten-1996|In Small Things Forgotten]]* (1977; expanded 1996). Ph.D. Harvard; taught at Brown, UC Berkeley (Distinguished Teaching Award), University of Virginia; earlier appointments included Harvard, William and Mary, UC Santa Barbara, and the University of Cape Town (1996 author note).

## Overview

Deetz joined documentary history to patterned material culture — ceramics, gravestones, vernacular houses, refuse — to recover cognitive and social change among non-elite Anglo-Americans and African Americans. His **1969** meeting with [[glassie-henry|Henry Glassie]] (Toledo) re-centered his American Material Culture course; by **1972** the course form stabilized; **1975–77** produced the first book. Later career shifted research to the **Chesapeake** ([[flowerdew-hundred|Flowerdew Hundred]] monograph 1993); the **1996** ISTF revision incorporates that work and a full African American archaeology program. Landmark case: **[[parting-ways|Parting Ways]]** (1975–78).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — [[deetz-in-small-things-forgotten-1996]] / [[deetz-in-small-things-forgotten-study]].)*

- **Definitions of HA:** literacy-based contrast with prehistory; popular global definition (European expansion since the 15th c. and Indigenous interaction) — later cited in [[orser-charles-e|Orser]]'s definitional lineage; insistence that African American, Asian American, and post-contact Native American archaeology are integral.
- **[[georgian-worldview]]** and three-period Anglo-American model (to ~1660 / ~1680–1760 / ~1760+).
- Mortuary **[[seriation]]** (death's head → cherub → urn and willow) as cultural chronology.
- Ceramic foodways arc from dairy/corporate use to individualized matching sets after creamware mass production.
- **[[earthfast-construction]]** and tobacco-capital logic (Carson et al.).
- African American archaeology as **[[creolization]]** (not simple [[acculturation]]): Parting Ways, [[shotgun-house]], [[colono-ware]], creole pipes, foodways continuum.
- Pedagogical dating toolkit: TPQ/TAQ, South **[[mean-ceramic-date]]**, Harrington/Binford pipestems; [[probate-inventory-analysis]] as folk taxonomy.
