# Charles L. Briggs

## Overview

American linguistic anthropologist and folklorist; co-author with [[bauman-richard|Richard Bauman]] of “Language Philosophy as Language Ideology: John Locke and Johann Gottfried Herder” in [[kroskrity-regimes-of-language-2000|*Regimes of Language* (2000)]] — a historical-semiotic reading of foundational modern language philosophies as ideological projects.

## Contributions

- With Bauman: Locke and Herder as architects of modern language ideologies (purification of language, national spirit in language).
- Broader work on metadiscourse, interviews, and medical/communicability (densify when ingested).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Close collaborator of Bauman; ethnography-of-speaking and performance networks; SAR 1994 seminar.
