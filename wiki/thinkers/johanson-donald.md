## Overview

Donald Johanson (b. 1943), then of the Cleveland Museum of Natural History, conducted fieldwork in the Afar Depression at [[hadar]], Ethiopia, recovering fossils central to *Origins*' (1977) discussion of early hominid diversity.

## Contributions

At Hadar, Johanson's team found a roughly three-million-year-old multi-individual hominid assemblage and the partial skeleton nicknamed "Lucy," among fossils of both *Homo* and [[australopithecus]]. *Origins* (1977) was published before Johanson's formal 1978 naming of *Australopithecus afarensis* — the book discusses the Hadar finds without that later taxonomic designation, a gap this wiki flags per the ethnographic/scientific date-stamping protocol.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The taxonomic status of the Hadar fossils discussed in *Origins* (1977) was formalized the following year with the naming of *Australopithecus afarensis*, superseding the book's more provisional framing.
