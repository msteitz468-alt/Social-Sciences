# Robert J. Wenke

American archaeologist; long associated with the University of Washington. Best known in this vault as lead author of the multi-edition world-prehistory textbook *[[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007|Patterns in Prehistory]]* (5th ed. with [[olszewski-deborah|Deborah I. Olszewski]], OUP 2007) and for evolutionary-theoretical reviews of cultural complexity (e.g. the 1981 *Advances in Archaeological Method and Theory* essay cited across theory debates).

## Overview

Wenke's textbook work places human prehistory in a **comparative, multi-causal frame**: competing models of cultural origins, modern-human origins, agricultural origins, and the evolution of complex societies are presented as testable against regional archaeological sequences (attributed, Wenke & Olszewski 2007 preface and chs. 3, 6–7). His 1981 review of cultural-complexity evolution is a standard cite in later discussions of evolutionary archaeology and systems approaches (as of secondary mentions in already-ingested theory sources).

## Contributions

- Co-author of *Patterns in Prehistory* — major Anglophone **[[world-prehistory]]** teaching synthesis spanning early culture through primary civilizations.
- Programmatic review of **explaining cultural complexity** (1981) — linked from post-processual and evolutionary debates in this vault.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

As a textbook author, Wenke is subject to the usual lag and selection critiques of teaching syntheses: dates and model rankings freeze mid-2000s consensus; regional specialists dispute details of every case chapter.
