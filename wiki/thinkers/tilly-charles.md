# Charles Tilly

Charles Tilly (1929–2008) was an American historical and political sociologist, a central builder of the study of **collective action**, **contentious politics**, European **state formation**, and **[[durable-inequality|durable / categorical inequality]]**.

## Overview

Five major programs ingested so far:

1. **Contention / mobilization** (*[[tilly-from-mobilization-to-revolution-1978|From Mobilization to Revolution]]*, 1978) — collective action as joint pursuit of common ends under polity-structured opportunity; revolution as **[[multiple-sovereignty]]**. Skocpol labels this "[[political-conflict-theory|political-conflict theory]]."
2. **[[durable-inequality-theory|Durable inequality]]** (*[[tilly-durable-inequality-1998|Durable Inequality]]*, 1998; Ross Lectures UCLA 1995) — large inequalities map mainly onto **[[categorical-pairs|paired categories]]**, installed by **[[exploitation]]** and **[[opportunity-hoarding]]**, cemented by **[[organizational-emulation|emulation]]** and **[[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]]**. Relational ontology: "bonds" not "essences."
3. **[[contentious-politics-theory|Dynamics of contention]]** (*[[mcadam-tarrow-tilly-dynamics-of-contention-2001|Dynamics of Contention]]* with McAdam & Tarrow, 2001) — mechanism–process synthesis across forms of struggle.
4. **Identities, boundaries, ties** (*[[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005|Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties]]*, 2005) — decade cross-connect: transactions compound into [[political-identity|identities]], [[social-boundary|boundaries]], and [[social-tie|ties]]; inventory of [[social-boundary-mechanism|boundary-change mechanisms]]; [[citizenship]] and [[protected-consultation]]; inequality ↔ [[democratization]]/[[de-democratization]]; social-movement repertoire diffusion.
5. **[[bellicist-theory-of-state-formation|State formation]]** (*[[tilly-coercion-capital-1992|Coercion, Capital, and European States]]*, 1990/1992) — "war made states, and vice versa": extraction of the means of war created state structure; [[capitalized-coercion|coercion/capital trajectories]] converging on the [[national-state]]; [[citizenship]] as bargains over war extraction; [[direct-rule]] from the French Revolution; the Third World's external-coercion divergence. Corrects the 1975 Rokkan-era volume. Study + hub: [[tilly-coercion-capital-study]] · [[hubs/studies/tilly-coercion-capital-study]].

## Contributions

- **[[political-conflict-theory|Political-conflict / mobilization theory]]** — polity + mobilization models (*FMR* 1978); organization = catness × netness; repertoires; collective violence; multiple sovereignty.
- **[[durable-inequality-theory|Durable / categorical inequality theory]]** (*DI* 1998; restated *IBST* Chs. 5–8, 10) — four mechanisms; interior/exterior category matching; organizational problem-solving as engine of inequality; politics of inequality as special case when a government is a party; democracy threatened by widespread categorical inequality.
- **Relational method** — rejects methodological individualism, phenomenological individualism, and systems theories as essence ontologies; starts from transactions → ties → networks → organizations; mechanism–process explanation (*IBST* Ch. 3).
- **[[social-boundary-mechanism|Social boundary mechanisms]]** (*IBST* Ch. 9) — precipitating (encounter, imposition, borrowing, conversation, incentive shift) and constitutive (inscription/erasure, activation/deactivation, site transfer, relocation).
- **[[political-identity]]** — identities as public representations of us–them / us–us relations; political when government is party; claim-making performances (*IBST* Chs. 4, 14).
- **[[citizenship]] / [[protected-consultation]]** — categorical rights–obligations contracts; democracy as breadth × equality × consultation × protection; de-democratization as reversal (*IBST* Chs. 11–13).
- **[[repertoire-of-contention|Social movement repertoire]]** — campaign + repertoire + WUNC; invention/diffusion via brokerage, certification, local adaptation (*IBST* Ch. 15).
- Event catalogs (France/Britain contention) and migration/niche illustrations (Mamaroneck, [[chain-migration]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Sources:** [[marx-karl|Marx]] (class interests; exploitation lineage for *DI*); Mill/utilitarian strategic tools; [[weber-max|Weber]] (closure; social movements); Oberschall/resource mobilization; Stinchcombe affinities (*DI* acknowledgments); historical France/Britain.
- **reacted_against:** Durkheimian strain; crowd psychology; relative deprivation monism; human-capital and attitude monisms of inequality; essence ontologies; dispositional nationalism (Greenfeld as foil in *IBST* Ch. 1).
- **Dialogue:** [[skocpol-theda|Skocpol]] (state autonomy vs Tilly's polity arena); Zelizer (relational monies — *DI* Ch. 2 opener); White workshop; Chris Tilly (*Work Under Capitalism*); Brubaker & Cooper (identity); Mann, Sahlins, Gould (as engaged in *IBST*).

### Mann dialogue — capitalism and national states (attributed)

[[mann-michael|Michael Mann]] (*[[mann-sources-of-social-power-vol1-1986|Sources of Social Power, Vol. I]]*, 1986) tracks the early-modern alliance of **international capitalism** and the **[[organic-national-state]]** as co-evolving master organizational processes (military revolution, class-nation coordination, multistate geopolitical civilization) — a multi-power ([[iemp-model|IEMP]]) reading adjacent to Tilly's coercion–capital–states program (*[[tilly-coercion-capital-1992|Coercion, Capital, and European States]]*, 1990/1992 — now ingested). Cross-link, not merge: Mann emphasizes overlapping power networks and despotic/infrastructural duals; Tilly emphasizes war-driven extraction, the [[capitalized-coercion|coercion/capital trajectories]], and the extraction→bargaining→[[citizenship|rights]] loop. See [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], [[bellicist-theory-of-state-formation]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Skocpol (1979): under-autonomizes the state (*FMR* line).
- *DI* self-position: working hypotheses inviting verification/refutation, not already validated generalizations.
- *IBST* Ch. 13: inequality–democracy claims labeled **conjectures**.
- Open problems Tilly names: cultural differentiation of category types; life-history preparation vs point-of-reward sorting; boundary-mechanism inventory “preliminary.”
- Policy preference (attributed, not wiki-voice): material equalization scenario; organizational redesign over tolerance education alone.

## From *Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties* (2005)

*(Attributed — Tilly.)* Cross-connections among identities, boundaries, social ties, mechanisms, democratization, citizenship, inequality, collective violence, social movements, and trust. Relational ontology reiterated; mechanism inventories for political processes and boundary change; chain migration as opportunity-hoarding site; political identities vs essence models; SM repertoire transfer mechanisms. Source: [[tilly-identities-boundaries-social-ties-2005]].

## From *Dynamics of Contention* (2001)

*(Attributed — with McAdam & Tarrow.)* Third major contention program after *FMR*: mechanism–process explanation of [[contentious-politics]] across movements, revolutions, nationalism, and democratization; relational mechanisms ([[brokerage]], [[certification]], [[category-formation]], [[identity-shift]], etc.); robust processes (actor constitution, polarization, [[scale-shift]]); democratization as net shift toward [[protected-consultation]]. Develops from and partially supersedes static boxes of earlier political-process models (including Tilly's own). Source: [[mcadam-tarrow-tilly-dynamics-of-contention-2001]]. Theory: [[contentious-politics-theory]]; Hub: [[hubs/theory/contentious-politics-theory]].
