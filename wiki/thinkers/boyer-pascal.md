# Pascal Boyer

## Overview

Anthropologist and major architect of the [[cognitive-science-of-religion|cognitive science of religion]]. In White's (2021) synthesis, Boyer is the central voice for the **by-product / naturalness** strand: religious concepts succeed in cultural transmission because they slightly violate intuitive ontological expectations while remaining learnable — [[minimally-counterintuitive-concepts|minimally counterintuitive (MCI)]] concepts.

## Contributions

- *The Naturalness of Religious Ideas* (1994) — cognitive theory of religion; naturalness thesis.
- *Religion Explained* (2001/2002) — popular synthesis: gods, spirits, ancestors as products of ordinary cognitive systems (agency, ToM, intuitive ontology), not special religious faculties.
- Supports [[fractionation-of-religion|fractionating]] "religion" into recurrent conceptual packages rather than a single essence.
- With others: domain specificity and intuitive ontology; moral religions (Baumard & Boyer 2013, per White).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Peers/rivals in White's map: [[barrett-justin-l]], [[guthrie-stewart]], [[whitehouse-harvey]], Scott Atran (primary pages may densify). Draws on evolutionary psychology and cognitive anthropology.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

MCI memory advantage: White notes mixed experimental results and more complex cognitive architecture than early models assumed. Adaptationist CSR (moralizing gods, costly ritual) competes with pure by-product framing.
