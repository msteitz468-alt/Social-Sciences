# Elijah Anderson

## Overview

American urban ethnographer of black community life, public space, and interpersonal violence. Primary wiki ingest: *[[anderson-code-of-the-street-1999|Code of the Street]]* (1999) — study [[anderson-code-of-the-street-study]]; hub [[hubs/studies/anderson-code-of-the-street-study]]. Earlier monographs (*A Place on the Corner*, 1978; *Streetwise*, 1990) established his Chicago/Philadelphia field career; *The Cosmopolitan Canopy* (2011) extends the public-space program (not yet ingested).

## Contributions

- **[[code-of-the-street]]:** informal public rules of respect and payback where civil law and trusted policing are weak (Anderson 1999).
- **[[decent-and-street]]:** dual orientations and code-switching within the same poor neighborhoods; majority decent claim (Anderson 1999 Ch 1).
- **Public ecology:** Germantown Avenue continuum from civility to street regulation; staging areas; school as street extension (1999 Introduction).
- **Role types:** decent daddy, old head, grandmother in transition; extended cases (John Turner; Robert/Rob with Herman Wrice).
- **Method:** long-form [[participant-observation]] and interviews across status settings; impressionistic multi-site design continuous with *Streetwise*.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Thanks in *Code of the Street* include [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]], Howard S. Becker, Gerald D. Suttles, Jack Katz, Marvin Wolfgang, James Short, William Labov, and a Philadelphia academic/community network. Positions himself in the urban-ethnographic line (Whyte, Thrasher, Sanchez Jankowski, Horowitz, Sullivan) while distinguishing the street code from a community-wide subculture of violence (vs Wolfgang & Ferracuti). Dialogues with structural underclass accounts ([[urban-underclass]], [[concentrated-poverty]], joblessness, [[social-isolation]]) without reducing culture to epiphenomenon. At University of Pennsylvania during the *Code* period; appears in the John Turner chapter as character reference — researcher role is part of the fieldwork.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Reception clusters (positions): structural-first critics (culture secondary); representation/stigma concerns when the title-concept is detached from majority-decent and code-switching claims; method critiques (single city, impressionistic); quantitative afterlife mixed (`partially-supported` on the study page). No Boelen-style full restudy of the Philadelphia field is ingested here.

## Historiography

Primary: Norton 1999. Prior article versions in *Atlantic Monthly*, *Public Interest*, *Annals AAPSS*, Jencks & Peterson *The Urban Underclass*, Katz & Sugrue, McCord volume. Guggenheim support for violence/street-code work. *Streetwise* (1990) is the direct predecessor; *A Place on the Corner* and *Cosmopolitan Canopy* remain collection gaps.
