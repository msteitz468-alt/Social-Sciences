# Paul Lane

Senior Lecturer in Archaeology (University of York, as of handbook bio); Africanist specializing in eastern African archaeology, landscape approaches, and the intersection of archaeology with heritage and contemporary societies. Co-editor with [[mitchell-peter]] of *[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013|The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology]]* (2013).

## Overview

Lane’s editorial and research contributions emphasize how African archaeology is *done* — methods, landscapes, heritage politics, and colonial/postcolonial research contexts — alongside regional sequences. With Mitchell he organized the 2013 handbook’s seven-part architecture (practice → origins → foragers → food production → states → modern world system).

## Contributions

- **Handbook co-editorship** with [[mitchell-peter]] (*[[mitchell-lane-oxford-handbook-african-archaeology-2013]]*).
