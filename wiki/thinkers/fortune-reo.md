# Reo Fortune

New Zealand-born anthropologist (1903–1979), trained under [[malinowski-bronislaw|Bronislaw Malinowski]]; later partner of [[mead-margaret|Margaret Mead]] on the 1931–33 New Guinea expedition that produced *[[mead-sex-and-temperament-1935|Sex and Temperament]]*. His fieldwork on **Dobu** (d'Entrecasteaux Islands, 1927–28) produced *Sorcerers of Dobu* (1932) — the sole ethnographic basis for [[benedict-ruth|Benedict]]'s [[dobu|Dobu]] chapter in *[[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Patterns of Culture]]*, where he is cited by page throughout. This page is a **network stub** grounded in the *Sex and Temperament* and *Patterns of Culture* ingests — expand when Fortune's monographs are ingested directly.

## Contributions

- ***Sorcerers of Dobu*** (1932): documented Dobuan social organization — the matrilineal *susu*, alternating-year residence, the yam-magic property system, the *wabuwabu* sharp-dealing in the Kula, and the pervasive sorcery and disease-charm complex — material [[benedict-ruth|Benedict]] recast as an institutionalized "paranoid" [[cultural-configuration|configuration]] ([[dobu]]).
- Field partnership enabling mountain and river work Mead says she could not have reached alone.
- Arapesh language analysis; men's cults and ceremonies outside Alitoa.
- Substantial ethnography among [[mundugumor]] and [[chambri|Tchambuli/Chambri]] (Mead's thinner treatment of those societies partly reflects division of labour).
