# Peter Bellwood

## Overview

Peter Bellwood is an archaeologist whose 1979 synthesis, *Man's Conquest of the Pacific*, Kirch (*On the Road of the Winds*, 2017) explicitly identifies as the prior standard account his own book was written to replace, calling it "long out of date." Bellwood is also cited by Kirch as an active scholarly voice on later theoretical debates (Bellwood 1996b, on phylogenetic-vs-reticulate models of Pacific cultural history) and as an early practitioner of settlement-pattern archaeology in the Marquesas (Bellwood 1972).

## Contributions

- ***Man's Conquest of the Pacific*** (1979) — the major synthesis of Pacific prehistory that preceded Kirch's *On the Road of the Winds*; per Kirch, the two positive published reviews of Kirch's first edition included one by Bellwood himself (in *Antiquity*).
- **Out-of-Taiwan / "Express Train" model** of the [[austronesian-expansion|Austronesian expansion]] (attributed, theoretical claim) — the leading farming-language-dispersal account holding that Austronesian-speaking populations, agriculture, and associated material culture spread rapidly out of Taiwan through Island Southeast Asia and into the Pacific, in contrast to slower, more mixed "entangled bank" alternatives; see [[express-train-vs-entangled-bank-debate]].
- Applied settlement-pattern archaeological methods in the Marquesas (Bellwood 1972), part of the broader diffusion of the settlement-pattern approach that [[green-roger|Roger Green]] introduced to Polynesia.

## Intellectual Genealogy

No direct teacher/student genealogy to Kirch or Green is recorded in this source; Bellwood is presented as a scholarly peer and predecessor-synthesist rather than part of the Bishop Museum/Harvard settlement-pattern lineage. His Out-of-Taiwan model stands as the leading position in [[express-train-vs-entangled-bank-debate]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Kirch positions his own 2017 revised edition as an update superseding Bellwood's 1979 synthesis in scope and currency, without disparaging its scholarly standing at the time; the Express Train model itself remains an active, contested position (per the debate page) rather than a superseded one, in contrast to Te Rangi Hiroa's or Heyerdahl's origin theories.
