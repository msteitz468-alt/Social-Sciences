# Milford H. Wolpoff (b. 1942)

American paleoanthropologist; principal late-20th-century advocate of **[[multiregional-evolution|multiregional evolution]]** — modern humans as the product of regional continuity from archaic populations linked by gene flow. Appears in *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) as the chief rival to Stringer's replacement reading (attributed positions as **reported by Stringer & Gamble** — opponent's synthesis, not a primary Wolpoff ingest).

## Overview

*(Densified from Stringer & Gamble 1993 engagement and named Wolpoff quotes they report; awaiting primary Wolpoff texts for balanced depth.)*

Wolpoff, with Alan Thorne and others, names and presses multiregional evolution against Out of Africa 2 / total replacement in the 1980s–1990s. S&G present him as continuity's public face — including the popular line that he sees a Neanderthal every morning in his shaving mirror, and a sharp moral-scientific critique of replacement as the "story of Cain."

## Contributions

*(As attributed via Stringer & Gamble 1993 unless noted.)*

- **Multiregional evolution (with Thorne et al.).** Regional continuity plus gene flow produces modern humans worldwide without total replacement; isolation never sufficient for allopatric speciation across the Pleistocene; only one species (*Homo sapiens*) for ~1 Ma since *erectus* Out of Africa 1 (multiregional model as summarized by S&G 1993).
- **Centre-and-edge model (Thorne & Wolpoff).** Interbreeding or gene flow between wide-ranging Ice Age human populations always sufficient to prevent full speciation while local selection maintains regional features — underpinning the claim that racial variation is ancient but within one polytypic species (via S&G 1993).
- **Australia dual-origin (with Thorne).** Australian skeletal variation as two evolutionary origins: gracile eastern island-hopping Asian route vs robust Ngandong/Java western route retaining "mark of ancient Java" frontal flattening — vs Peter Brown's *in situ* diversification (S&G 1993 report of the dispute).
- **European Neanderthal contribution.** Argues Neanderthals contribute to modern European ancestry (POSITION on [[modern-human-origins-debate]]); continuity camp reads Neanderthal face as intermediate, not specialized dead end (via S&G).
- **Limb proportions argument.** Suggests Neanderthal–Cro-Magnon limb-proportion differences reflect longer-distance walking/running from hunting-pattern or wider social-network changes — S&G reject, citing West Turkana boy (1.6 Ma) and Skhul/Qafzeh long-legged physiques as climatic adaptation instead (S&G 1993).
- **Critique of replacement rhetoric.** Replacement studies attacked as unscientific and contrary to spirit of the times: spread of humankind into lasting geographic groups with long contact and cooperation is "a more satisfying interpretation… than a scientific rendering of the story of Cain" based on one population quickly, completely, and most likely *violently* replacing all others — "making war and not love"; implications "not pleasant" (Wolpoff quoted by S&G 1993). S&G also cite his phrase **"Killer Africans"** as a framing they address when discussing coexistence interactions 45–30 ka.
- **Public continuity image.** "Shaving mirror" Neanderthal — popular continuity of Neanderthals in living Europeans (via S&G 1993).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **S&G 1993 methodological jab:** Multiregional gene-flow arguments hard to test — "Let the genes do the walking, if the bones won't play" (S&G critique of the program Wolpoff leads).
- **Fossil counters (S&G):** African primacy of modern package; Levant TL/ESR reordering; Cro-Magnons not on Neanderthal trajectory; Vindija too incomplete for intermediate claims; Zhoukoudian Upper Cave / Australian genetics against simple dual-origin Java continuity.
- **Genetics counters (S&G 1993 horizon):** mtDNA Eve ~200 ka and nuclear African primary split — multiregionalists attack calibration; S&G hold weight of then-available genetics on Out of Africa 2 side.
- **Post-2010 aDNA:** Supports limited archaic admixture but not equal multiregional contribution — record later sources when ingested; do not adjudicate here. Page remains opponent-sourced until primary Wolpoff ingest.

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012) — Wolpoff as foil

*Attributed via [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012]], [[stringer-chris]] — still opponent-sourced; not a primary Wolpoff ingest.*

**1994 Multiregional program (as quoted).** Stringer quotes a 1994 paper by [[wolpoff-milford|Milford Wolpoff]] and four co-authors asserting: earliest “modern” humans were not Africans; no evidence of specific admixture with Africans at any time, let alone replacement by them; continuous regional skeletal feature combinations from earliest to living peoples. Stringer treats these classic Multiregional claims as **comprehensively falsified** by the fossil, archaeological, and genetic evidence he presents in the book (as of Stringer 2012) — Africa’s special role and recent modern origin stand; residual archaic input is occasional hybridization, not continuous multiregional conversion of local *erectus* into local moderns.

**Saint-Césaire.** Wolpoff argued Neanderthal features of the Saint-Césaire skeleton had been overemphasized and that it was “transitional” (Brace’s Neanderthal Phase / Multiregional continuity expectations). Once Neanderthal nature was generally accepted, the find supported Replacement in western Europe rather than morphological intermediacy (Stringer’s reading).

**Floresiensis and single-species commitment.** Multiregionalists wedded to a single species *H. sapiens* over the last ~2 Ma preferred pathology / “village idiot” / dental-fillings claims against *Homo floresiensis*; Stringer says short-term profile-raising damaged their and paleoanthropology’s reputation (as of Stringer 2012). 1987 mtDNA Eve work was heavily attacked by “disgruntled multiregionalists” (generic school label in this source; Alan Thorne named on Mungo 3 assimilation/multiregional claims).
