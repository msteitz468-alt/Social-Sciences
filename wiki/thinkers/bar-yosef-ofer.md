# Ofer Bar-Yosef (1937–2020)

## Overview

Israeli–American prehistoric archaeologist; major figure in Levantine Paleolithic–Epipaleolithic research and in framing the [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] as a terminal Pleistocene forager complex on the verge of the [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic]] transition. Co-edited the two standard multi-author Natufian symposium volumes (1991; [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013|2013]]) with [[valla-francois|François R. Valla]].

## Contributions

- **Natufian synthesis and agenda-setting.** With Valla, organized the 1989 and ~2009 international meetings that defined the field’s data corpus; the 2013 preface stresses site-formation, radiocarbon scarcity, plant-preservation gaps, phytolith/starch work, and expansion beyond the traditional “homeland” ([[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]).
- **Fieldwork.** Directed Natufian excavations at Hayonim Cave; long program of SW Asian Paleolithic research (including collaboration with [[belfer-cohen-anna|Anna Belfer-Cohen]]).
- **Theoretical framing.** Processual / evolutionary-ecology readings of hunter-gatherer complexity, sedentism, and the path to food production — positions to be stated in attributed voice on theory and culture pages.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborators and co-editors include Valla, Belfer-Cohen, and a wide international network of Levantine prehistorians. Students and rivals filled as sources densify.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Core-area / homeland models and climate-driven agriculture narratives associated with Bar-Yosef’s circle have been challenged within the same volumes (e.g., Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris on multi-origin Natufian complexity; Henry on Younger Dryas; Maher et al. on climate causation — as of 2013 literature cited in the volume).

## After the Ice densification (Mithen 2003)

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven]], *[[mithen-after-the-ice-2003|After the Ice]]* 2003, chs. 4–6, 9.)*

- **“Point of no turning back”:** Mithen presents Bar-Yosef’s formulation that the Early Natufian (exemplified by ‘Ain Mallaha) is the irreversible step on the road to farming (attributed).
- **Hayonim Cave program:** Bar-Yosef and colleagues from 1964 — six ~2 m circular structures with drystone walls and paved floors; one limekiln then bone workshop with cattle-rib sickle blanks (attributed).
- **Homeland model:** Mediterranean woodland core as Natufian homeland; Late Natufian lunates then spread to Arabian deserts and Euphrates before YD-related abandonment (Mithen’s narrative using Bar-Yosef framing) (attributed).
- **Nahal Hemar:** Alon & Bar-Yosef 1983 organic textiles/baskets/linen among earliest linen evidence (via Mithen, ch. 9).

Reception of homeland and climate-path models remains contested within later Natufian symposia (see main Critiques). Source: [[mithen-after-the-ice-2003]].

## Cauvin engagement (2000)

*(Attributed — *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000|Cauvin 2000]].)* Cauvin adopts Bar-Yosef’s **[[levantine-corridor|Levantine corridor]]** as the spatial locus of earliest PPNA agriculture (Jordan–Damascus–Euphrates alluvial band). Corridor geography underwrites Cauvin’s rejection of pan-hilly-flanks simultaneous origins and his three-culture PPNA map. Later Bar-Yosef Natufian symposium work (2013 volume ingested separately) densifies forager prehistory feeding the same transition Cauvin theorizes symbolically.
