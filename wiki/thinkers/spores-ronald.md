# Ronald Spores

## Overview
American archaeologist and ethnohistorian (Vanderbilt University) whose combined archaeological survey of the Nochixtlán Valley and documentary study of colonial-era Mixtec kingdoms made him the principal authority on [[mixtec-culture|Mixtec]] political organization. A core contributor to [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983), writing most of its Mixteca Alta archaeology and Postclassic cacicazgo/ethnohistory topics.

## Contributions
- Nochixtlán Valley settlement survey ([[archaeological-survey]]) establishing the Cruz–Ramos–Las Flores–Natividad ceramic sequence for the Mixteca Alta.
- Spores argued that the Postclassic Mixtec cacicazgo was a class-stratified petty kingdom whose royal marriage alliances, documented in the [[mixtec-codices]], structured regional politics ([[ethnohistory]] + material evidence combined).

## Intellectual Genealogy
Worked in close collaboration with [[flannery-kent-v|Kent Flannery]] and [[marcus-joyce|Joyce Marcus]] on Oaxaca comparative projects.
