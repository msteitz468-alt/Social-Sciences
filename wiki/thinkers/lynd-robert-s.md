# Robert S. Lynd

## Overview

American sociologist best known, with [[lynd-helen-merrell|Helen Merrell Lynd]], for *[[lynd-middletown-1929|Middletown]]* (1929) — the landmark [[community-study|community study]] of a mid-sized Midwestern city treated "after the manner of social anthropology." Field investigation for *Middletown* was completed in 1925 under support of the Institute of Social and Religious Research; Clark Wissler contributed the Foreword framing the book as social anthropology of contemporary life. Later work includes the restudy *Middletown in Transition* (1937, with Helen Merrell Lynd) and the critical essay *Knowledge for What?* (1939) — neither yet ingested here.

## Contributions

- Co-author of *Middletown*: total-situation portrait of [[middletown]] organized by six activity areas and a binary class scheme ([[business-class-and-working-class|business class / working class]]), with 1890 as comparison horizon.
- Helped establish the American **community study** as a research genre (see [[community-study]], [[lynd-middletown-study]]).
- Later programmatic critique of value-free social science (*Knowledge for What?* — not yet ingested; note only).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Institutional setting for *Middletown*:** Institute of Social and Religious Research funding; counsel acknowledged from Clark Wissler, L. C. Marshall, William F. Ogburn, Lawrence K. Frank, Joseph Chassell, Gardner Murphy (Preface).
- **Field staff:** Faith Moors Williams, Dorothea Davis, Frances Flournoy (Preface).
- **Relation to [[chicago-school|Chicago School]]:** roughly contemporary urban/community research tradition, but *Middletown* is not a Chicago departmental product; method is explicitly anthropological-community rather than Parkian human ecology.
- **Students / trained:** not documented from this source alone (`[[unknown]]`).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Critiques of *Middletown* (representativeness of the "typical" city; underplaying of race and politics; later restudies including Caplow et al.) belong primarily on [[lynd-middletown-study]] and its Studies Hub page. Personal misconduct record: none flagged from this ingest.

## Historiography

Reputation is inseparable from *Middletown* and the sequel *Middletown in Transition*; Muncie, Indiana, became a standing laboratory for American community restudy. Primary source for this page: the 1929 volume only.
