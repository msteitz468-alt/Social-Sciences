# Cecilia L. Ridgeway

American sociologist and social psychologist; central figure in the sociology of gender and in the expectation-states / status-construction tradition. Principal synthesis of her gender program: *[[ridgeway-framed-by-gender-2011|Framed by Gender]]* (2011).

## Overview

Ridgeway argues that gender persists as a principle of inequality in modern societies because people use sex/gender as a **[[primary-frame|primary cultural frame]]** for coordinating social relations; shared **[[status-beliefs|gender status beliefs]]** then bias judgments and behavior so that positional inequalities between men and women are continually re-embedded in new organizational and interpersonal forms even as leveling forces erode older ones.

## Contributions

- **[[gender-as-primary-frame]]** — theory that sex/gender is a near-automatic framing device for social relations; deep treatment [[hubs/theory/gender-as-primary-frame]].
- **[[status-construction-theory]]** — account of how status beliefs form and spread from resource-linked encounters.
- Application and extension of **[[expectation-states-theory]]** (Berger and colleagues) to gender inequality at work and home.
- Concepts: [[primary-frame]], [[status-beliefs]], [[sex-categorization]], diffuse vs specific status beliefs, performance expectations under gender salience.

## Historiography

*Framed by Gender* (2011) is the book-length consolidation of arguments Ridgeway developed across articles from the 1990s–2000s (including a 1997 statement she cites in acknowledgments). Status-construction experiments and expectation-states applications form the empirical spine; the book is synthesis and program statement more than a single primary study.
