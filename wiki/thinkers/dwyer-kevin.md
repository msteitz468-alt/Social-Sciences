# Kevin Dwyer

American anthropologist, a principal figure of the **dialogic** strand of reflexive
anthropology. His *Moroccan Dialogues: Anthropology in Question* (1982) presented
fieldwork as a series of edited dialogues between anthropologist and informant (the
Moroccan farmer Faqir Muhammad), arguing that anthropological knowledge is produced in,
and cannot be detached from, the encounter between Self and Other.

## Overview

Dwyer belongs to the cluster of Moroccanist reflexive ethnographers alongside
[[rabinow-paul|Paul Rabinow]] (*[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977|Reflections on
Fieldwork in Morocco]]*, 1977) and [[crapanzano-vincent|Vincent Crapanzano]] (*Tuhami*,
1980). Rabinow acknowledges Dwyer among those who helped with *Reflections*. Where
Rabinow narrates the encounter retrospectively, Dwyer foregrounds the **dialogue form**
itself as the vehicle of, and check upon, ethnographic authority.

## Contributions

*(Attributed.)*

- **Dialogic anthropology.** Knowledge as jointly produced in Self–Other dialogue; the
  dialogue's structure and the informant's questions are data, not scaffolding to be
  discarded.
- Contributed to the [[writing-culture|Writing Culture]] moment's questioning of
  monologic ethnographic authority (see [[writing-culture-debate]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Part of the Morocco-centred reflexive turn of the late 1970s–1980s; adjacent to
[[reflexive-fieldwork|reflexive fieldwork]] and its critics.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The same critiques that met reflexive anthropology apply: that the dialogue is still
edited and framed by the anthropologist, so the appearance of shared authority can mask
its persistence.
