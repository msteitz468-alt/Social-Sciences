# Stewart E. Guthrie

## Overview

Anthropologist whose *Faces in the Clouds* (1993) is treated by White (2021) as a founding CSR book: religion as systematic **anthropomorphism** — attributing human-like agency and mentality to the non-human world. Earlier "A cognitive theory of religion" (1980 *Current Anthropology*).

## Contributions

- Religion as anthropomorphism: faces, agents, and minds projected onto ambiguous stimuli.
- Evolutionary-cognitive framing of why such projections are compelling and culturally recurrent.
- Precursor strand to later HADD/ADD formulations (Barrett et al.), though not identical.

## Intellectual Genealogy

CSR pioneers cluster: [[boyer-pascal]], [[lawson-e-thomas]], [[whitehouse-harvey]]. Distant foil: pure intellectualism ([[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion|Tylor/Frazer]]) vs Guthrie's perceptual/cognitive bias account.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Anthropomorphism thesis can be over-extended; later CSR fractionates agent concepts (MCI, ToM, dualism) beyond single "faces in clouds" mechanism. Still cited as charter evolutionary-cognitive work.
