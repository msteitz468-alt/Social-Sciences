# Julian H. Steward

> **Deep analysis:** [[hubs/thinkers/steward-julian-h|Thinkers Hub]]

## Overview

Julian Haynes Steward (1902–1972), American anthropologist, developed **[[cultural-ecology]]** and **[[multilinear-evolution]]** as a methodology for explaining cross-cultural regularities without Victorian unilinearity or pure particularism. *[[steward-theory-of-culture-change-1955|Theory of Culture Change]]* (1955) is the vault's primary source — a revised essay collection (most chapters from earlier papers; Chs. 2, 5, and 6 newly written) synthesizing concepts with case applications: [[great-basin-shoshoneans|Great Basin Shoshoneans]], hunting bands, Southwest lineages, irrigation civilizations, [[carrier-indians|Carrier]], and Puerto Rico.

## Contributions

- **[[multilinear-evolution]]** — empirical methodology seeking limited parallels of form, function, and sequence; explicitly not unilinear stages and not only White/Childe universal laws of culture-in-general.
- **[[cultural-ecology]]** — method for analyzing creative adaptation via technology–environment–behavior and the [[cultural-core]]; three procedures; contrast with [[human-ecology]].
- **[[levels-of-sociocultural-integration]]** — operational family / folk / state (and intermediate) levels for comparison and for analyzing nations as multi-level systems.
- **[[culture-type]]** vs **[[culture-area]]** — types defined by core features and integration level; regularities vs historical uniformities.
- **Type cases:** [[patrilineal-band]]; [[composite-hunting-band]]; family-level Great Basin; lineage→clan processes; irrigation-civilization developmental sequence (trial formulation); Puerto Rican industrial subcultures.
- **Programmatic stance** in the [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]]: reopened evolutionary questions after Boasian critique without restoring ethnocentric ladders.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Reacted against:** unilinear [[social-evolutionism]]; pure [[historical-particularism]] / relativism that forgoes cross-cultural laws; environmental determinism as caricature; Whitean culturology as sole master key.
- **Dialogued with:** [[white-leslie-a|Leslie White]] ([[universal-evolution]], *[[white-evolution-of-culture-1959|Evolution of Culture]]* 1959 — White as the culture-in-general / energy pole Steward contrasts); V. Gordon Childe; Wittfogel (irrigation); Kroeber (limited independent types; [[culture-area]]); Redfield (folk society re-read as level); Boas school on parallelism interest despite particularism. Carneiro and Brown (2007) rank White primary and Steward/Childe secondary in the mid-century resuscitation of evolutionary anthropology — a historiographic position, not Steward's self-ranking.
- **Students / orbit (from Service’s self-report):** [[service-elman-r|Elman Service]] names Steward (with [[white-leslie-a|White]]) as teacher of cultural evolution; *[[service-primitive-social-organization-1971|Primitive Social Organization]]* is dedicated to Steward and White and reworks Steward’s band types and levels into the [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]] (Service renames patrilineal → [[patrilocal-band]]; critiques pure geographical determination of band structure). [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] is placed in the same neo-evolutionary collaboration network via Service (joint *Evolution and Culture*, 1960 — not yet primary-ingested). **[[mintz-sidney-w|Sidney W. Mintz]]** trained under Steward on the Puerto Rico / *People of Puerto Rico* project (fieldwork from 1948); *[[mintz-sweetness-and-power-study|Sweetness and Power]]* (1985) is densified on this wiki from that lineage's Caribbean agro-proletarian ground. Full biographical training chains still incomplete.
- **Applied multilinear urbanism (Adams 1966):** [[adams-robert-mcc|Robert McC. Adams]] closes Ch. I of *[[adams-evolution-urban-society-1966|The Evolution of Urban Society]]* with Steward's mandate to seek recurrent phenomena and independent cause-effect sequences; uses Stewardian levels/core as tools while **parting company on ecological primacy** of the cultural core for the Urban Revolution problem. Major application of [[multilinear-evolution]] to pristine urban trajectories ([[adams-evolution-urban-society-study]]).
- **Applied cultural ecology (Sanders & Price 1968):** [[sanders-william-t|Sanders]] & [[price-barbara-j|Price]]'s *[[sanders-price-mesoamerica-1968|Mesoamerica]]* aligns "with what Steward calls multilineal evolution," takes over his [[cultural-core|core]]-vs-superstructure hierarchy and ecological-adaptation account of convergence, and — unlike Adams — pushes ecological/materialist primacy *harder* than Steward, making it the New World companion to Adams's synthesis ([[symbiotic-region]], [[state-formation]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Determinism charge — Steward denied it (open ecology; latitude cases).
- Subsistence bias / thin meaning — built into core definition.
- Second-hand comparative ethnography for world band sample.
- Later [[cultural-materialism]] ([[harris-marvin|Harris]], *[[harris-rise-anthropological-theory-1968|Rise]]* 1968 ch. 23 — attributed) redescribes Steward's [[cultural-ecology]] as applied cultural materialism and the leading concrete materialist method; processual archaeology and political ecology reworked or replaced other parts of the program.
- Ethnonyms and evaluative language (e.g. Carrier potlatch "imitation") reflect mid-century comparative hierarchy.
- **The band-model challenge (*[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]*, 1968).** Steward's [[patrilineal-band]] type (with Radcliffe-Brown's horde and [[service-elman-r|Service]]'s [[patrilocal-band]]) was the target of Part III's new fieldwork, which found band composition typically fluid and bilateral — see [[patrilocal-band-model-debate]]. Steward, kept away by illness, contributed "Causal Factors and Processes in the Evolution of Pre-farming Societies" (attributed): hunting is "not even a single causal factor" but a family of adaptations, and the nuclear family rests on "cultural complementarity rather than on some inherent or 'natural' tendency" and so "may be weakened" — a pointed contrast to the [[hunting-hypothesis]]'s more biological account. The editors called him "in many ways the founder of modern hunter-gatherer studies."

## Historiography

Primary base for this page: *Theory of Culture Change* (1955). Outstanding for denser biography and field history: *Basin-Plateau* (1938); full *People of Puerto Rico*; intellectual biographies; student networks.

### Carneiro 2003 (attributed)

[[carneiro-robert-l|Carneiro]] (*[[carneiro-evolutionism-cultural-anthropology-2003|Evolutionism in Cultural Anthropology]]*) treats Steward as co-pillar of the midcentury reemergence with White: cultural ecology and multilinear evolution as the limited-regularities path; White/Childe reclassified by Steward under “universal evolution.” Carneiro critiques Steward’s wording of multilinear evolution as a “methodology” and his ambivalence about broader laws (White’s “rivers flow downhill” chide). Steward’s abandonment of Wittfogel hydraulic primacy after Yucatan is noted; Carneiro re-reads arid-center correlations as [[circumscription|environmental circumscription]] rather than irrigation. See [[multilinear-evolution]], [[cultural-ecology]].
