# Robert D. Putnam

American political scientist and public intellectual whose work on [[civic-social-capital|social capital]] and civic engagement — especially *[[putnam-bowling-alone-2000|Bowling Alone]]* (2000) and the earlier *Making Democracy Work* (1993, Italy) — became a central reference point in late-twentieth-century sociology and political science. Harvard faculty; long civic-generation framing of U.S. mid-century participation.

## Overview

Putnam synthesized survey series, organizational membership data, and time-use evidence into a decline-of-civic-America narrative that entered public and policy discourse as well as academic debate. The wiki tracks his claims in **attributed voice**; the standing critical literature is filed under [[bowling-alone-debate]].

## Contributions

- **[[civic-social-capital]]** — community-level networks, norms of reciprocity, and trustworthiness enabling coordination (distinct from Bourdieu's convertible network resources — see [[social-capital]]).
- **[[bonding-social-capital]] / [[bridging-social-capital]]** — exclusive/inward vs inclusive/cross-cutting networks.
- **[[putnam-bowling-alone-study|Bowling Alone research program]]** — multi-indicator U.S. civic-trend analysis c. 1960s–1990s and causal decomposition (TV, sprawl, time pressures, generational replacement).
- **Italian regional performance** — *Making Democracy Work* (1993; not yet fully ingested) linked civic traditions to institutional performance.
