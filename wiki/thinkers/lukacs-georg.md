# Georg Lukács

## Overview

Georg (György) Lukács (1885–1971), Hungarian philosopher, literary critic, and Marxist theorist, is a principal founder of [[western-marxism|Western Marxism]]. His *[[lukacs-history-and-class-consciousness-1923|History and Class Consciousness]]* (1923) reconceived Marxism as **method** (dialectic of subject and object / [[concrete-totality|totality]]), developed the modern theory of [[reification]] from Marx’s [[commodity-fetishism]], and formulated [[imputed-class-consciousness|imputed class consciousness]] (*zugerechnetes Bewusstsein*) as the structural possibility of proletarian knowledge. This page is built from the 1923 essays and the 1967 self-critical preface (plus Löwy’s 2023 framing apparatus) of the English edition; later major works (aesthetics, *Young Hegel*, ontology) are not yet primary-ingested.

## Contributions

From *History and Class Consciousness* (attributed to Lukács 1919–1923):

- **Orthodoxy as method, not results.** Even if every individual Marxian thesis were disproved, “orthodox” Marxism could remain — it “refers exclusively to method” (dialectical materialism as road to truth). Opposed to Bernsteinite removal of the dialectic as the condition of opportunist “evolution without revolution.”
- **[[concrete-totality|Concrete totality]].** “The primacy of the category of totality is the bearer of the revolutionary principle in science”; the decisive difference between Marxism and bourgeois science is not (only) economic primacy but the point of view of totality. Facts become knowledge only as moments of a historical process, not as isolated “pure” data modeled on natural science.
- **[[reification]].** When the commodity form becomes the universal structuring principle of society, relations between people take on the character of things with “phantom objectivity.” Objective side: world of commodity laws confronting agents as invisible forces. Subjective side: activity becomes a commodity under non-human social laws. Extended from factory/Taylorism into bureaucracy, law, journalism; contemplative stance; time degraded to space.
- **[[class-consciousness]] / [[imputed-class-consciousness]].** Class consciousness is not the sum or average of what workers think, but the rational reactions **imputed** to a typical position in production (category of objective possibility). Proletarian class consciousness uniquely must fuse theory and practice and abolish class society itself; fate of revolution depends on “ideological maturity.”
- **Identical subject–object of history (1923).** The proletariat, becoming conscious of its situation, is cast as the socio-historical realisation of Hegel’s identical subject–object — later **retracted** in the 1967 preface as a metaphysical construction that “out-Hegels Hegel.”
- **Organisation as mediation.** The Communist Party is analysed as the organised form of class consciousness mediating theory and practice (essay of 1922); workers’ councils as political-economic overcoming of reification (in the class-consciousness/reification arguments).
- **Engagement with [[luxemburg-rosa|Rosa Luxemburg]].** Praises her totalising Marxism of accumulation/imperialism; later essay critically revises her account of the Russian Revolution (organic development, party/spontaneity, agrarian and national questions).

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Formation (as in 1967 preface):** early Marx read as sociologist via [[simmel-georg|Simmel]] and Weber; WWI return to Marx under Hegel; Syndicalism/Sorel via Ervin Szabó; wartime contact with Luxemburg; repelled by Kautskyan Social Democracy; *Theory of the Novel* in wartime despair; Russian Revolution as “window to the future.”
- **Political biography (1918–22):** joined Hungarian Communists; People’s Commissar for Education in the Hungarian Soviet Republic (1919); Vienna exile; *Communism* magazine ultra-left milieu; tension between international ultra-leftism and anti-Kun (Landler) line in the Hungarian party; Lenin criticised his anti-parliamentarism.
- **Reacted against:** Bernstein/revisionism; vulgar Marxist economism and “scientism”; contemplative bourgeois thought; (in 1923) Engels’s insufficient treatment of subject–object in history → [[lukacs-dialectics-of-nature-debate]].
- **Reception network (Löwy 2023 list, attributed):** Ernst Bloch, [[mannheim-karl|Karl Mannheim]], [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]], Walter Benjamin, Herbert Marcuse, Sartre, Merleau-Ponty, Henri Lefebvre, Lucien Goldmann, Karel Kosík, among others — via reification and totality into [[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt]] / [[critical-theory|Critical Theory]] and broader Western Marxism. Parallel charter figure: [[gramsci-antonio|Gramsci]] (hegemony path; not a pupil).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

**Comintern-era attacks (FINDING via Löwy):** Kautsky (social-democratic); Zinoviev (communist); developed critiques by László Rudas and Abram Deborin (1924). Lukács replied in *Tailism and the Dialectic* (*Chvostismus und Dialektik*, c. 1925, rediscovered in Lenin Institute archives) — unpublished; Löwy reads it as more authoritarian on party/Luxemburg than *HCC* itself.

**Lukács’s own 1967 self-criticism (POSITION):** authorised German republication with a long preface that treats *HCC* as a document of apprenticeship (1918–1930), not current orthodoxy. Major retractions:
1. Treating Marxism as theory of society only / “nature is a societal category” — strikes at Marxian ontology.
2. Missing **labour** as metabolism of society and nature → subjectivist colouring of revolution.
3. Over-extension of praxis into messianic utopianism; imputed consciousness left contemplative (“miracle” if it becomes praxis).
4. Mistaken chain of argument against Engels on experiment and industry.
5. Totality elevated **over** economic priority (Hegelian distortion) even while restoring totality against revisionism.
6. **Identical subject–object** of the proletariat = metaphysical construct; equating alienation with objectification (Hegelian error) — a crude error that also fed the book’s success.
7. Earlier harsh self-criticisms (1934 Soviet Academy; 1957 *Arguments* letter forbidding republication) more severe; 1971 *Lived Thought* more affirmative on historicism of Marxism.

**Löwy’s partial defence (POSITION):** the nature-dialectics controversy is a bypath; *HCC* is above all a political-philosophical essay on proletarian revolution against capitalist reification; Lukács’s own claim that *HCC* simply identifies alienation with objectivity is inaccurate — reification/fetishism are processes making social relations appear as things.

## Historiography

Primary English base for this wiki: Verso 2023 / Merlin 1971 Livingstone translation with 1967 preface and Löwy 2023 centenary preface. Composition: essays 1919–1922; two wholly new for the book — “Reification…” and “Towards a Methodology of the Problem of Organisation.” French full edition 1960 (Minuit) after partial *Arguments* publication against Lukács’s wishes. The book’s afterlife as charter of Western Marxism and as object of its author’s lifelong self-critique is itself a major historiographical fact — claims from 1923 and 1967 must not be collapsed.
