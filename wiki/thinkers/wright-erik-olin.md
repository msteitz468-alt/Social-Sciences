# Erik Olin Wright

## Overview

Erik Olin Wright (1947–2019) was an American sociologist and leading figure in **[[analytical-marxism]]**, known for rebuilding Marxist class analysis with survey methods and explicit microfoundations (exploitation, contradictory locations). *[[wright-class-counts-1997|Class Counts]]* (1997) is the empirical summa of his multi-country Comparative Class Analysis Project.

## Contributions

*(From [[wright-class-counts-1997|Class Counts]], attributed.)*

- **[[contradictory-class-locations]]** — 12-location class map combining property, authority, and skill/expertise among employees (the "middle class" problem).
- **[[mediated-class-locations]]** — class interests shaped by household/spouse relations as well as own job.
- **[[class-boundary-permeability]]** — mobility, friendship, and cross-class marriage as measures of how sealed class boundaries are (property usually least permeable).
- Comparative class structure, US class transformation 1960–90, self-employment revival, class–gender interactions, class consciousness and formation (Sweden/US/Japan).
- Study: [[wright-class-counts-study]] · Hub: [[hubs/studies/wright-class-counts-study]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

Analytical Marxism circle (Cohen, Elster, Roemer series); dialogue with Weberian class analysis, post-industrial theory, feminist critiques of class primacy; Braverman proletarianization thesis as foil for US structural change.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Survey operationalizations of "capitalists" mostly small employers (Wright's own limitation); underclass and race under-analyzed in *Class Counts*; feminist challenges to class primacy recorded in the book as a live debate. Further reception not yet ingested as primaries.
