# Thomas B. Larsson

## Overview

**Thomas B. Larsson** is a Swedish archaeologist (Umeå University) specializing in the Scandinavian and European Bronze Age, with particular attention to the **diffusion of religious and cosmological institutions**. He is co-author, with [[kristiansen-kristian|Kristian Kristiansen]], of *[[kristiansen-larsson-rise-bronze-age-2005|The Rise of Bronze Age Society]]* (2005).

## Contributions

- ***[[kristiansen-larsson-rise-bronze-age-2005|The Rise of Bronze Age Society]]*** (2005, with Kristiansen): Larsson's long-standing work on the transmission of Bronze Age religion (Larsson 1997) and on Scandinavian rock-art iconography (chariots, sun-symbolism) feeds the book's [[twin-rulership|cosmological]] and iconographic argument especially heavily.
- Studies of Nordic Bronze Age religious symbolism, rock art, and the material expression of cosmology.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Works within the Scandinavian Bronze Age research tradition and the [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]] / [[diffusionism|interaction]] approach shared with [[kristiansen-kristian|Kristiansen]]; draws on comparative Indo-European mythology (Dumézil, Ward, Olmsted) for the religious reconstruction.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Shares the reception of the 2005 synthesis: the [[twin-rulership|Divine Twins / twin-rulership]] and sun-cult reconstructions rest on comparative-mythological and iconographic-parallel reasoning that critics regard as under-constrained (see [[diffusion-collapse-european-prehistory-debate]]).

## Historiography

Best known through the Kristiansen collaboration; the cosmological and rock-art strands of *The Rise of Bronze Age Society* bear his imprint most clearly.
