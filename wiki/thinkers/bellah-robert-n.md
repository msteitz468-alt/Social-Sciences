# Robert N. Bellah

American sociologist of religion (1927–2013), student of [[parsons-talcott|Talcott
Parsons]] at Harvard and later Ford Professor of Sociology at Berkeley. Best known for
the concept of American **"civil religion"** (1967), the collaborative study *Habits of
the Heart* (1985), and a career-long argument for a hermeneutic, non-reductive
("symbolic realist") approach to religion and meaning.

## Overview

Bellah enters this ingest as the author of the **Foreword** to
[[rabinow-paul|Rabinow]]'s *[[rabinow-reflections-morocco-1977|Reflections on Fieldwork
in Morocco]]* (1977), which he frames in an explicitly hermeneutic register: he
underscores Rabinow's borrowing from [[ricoeur-paul|Ricoeur]] — fieldwork as "the
comprehension of the self by the detour of the comprehension of the other" — and insists
that the "self" at stake is the *cultural*, not the personal-psychological, self. Bellah
reads the book as exemplary of an interpretive social science that refuses positivist
objectivism while keeping [[verstehen|value-relevance]] disciplined.

## Contributions

*(Attributed.)*

- **Civil religion.** A public religious dimension of American political life expressed
  in shared symbols and rituals (1967).
- **Symbolic realism.** Religious symbols are to be taken as real and meaningful on
  their own terms rather than reduced to social or psychological function.
- Framing of interpretive/[[reflexive-fieldwork|reflexive]] anthropology as a legitimate
  hermeneutic human science (Foreword to Rabinow 1977).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Trained under [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]]; carried the Weberian sociology of religion in
an interpretive direction; a fellow traveler of [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]'s
[[interpretive-anthropology|interpretive]] project.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The civil-religion thesis has been contested as over-unifying American political culture;
*Habits of the Heart*'s communitarian diagnosis drew both wide readership and
methodological criticism. *(Full treatment out of scope for this ingest.)*
