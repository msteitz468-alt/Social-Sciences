# E. Thomas Lawson

## Overview

Religious-studies scholar and CSR co-founder. White (2021) cites Lawson & [[mccauley-robert-n|McCauley]] *Rethinking Religion* (1990) as a charter text, Lawson's 2000 "Towards a cognitive science of religion" for early naming of the field, and the pair's *Bringing Ritual to Mind* (2002) for cognitive foundations of ritual form. Served on [[white-claire|Claire White]]'s PhD committee (ICC Belfast).

## Contributions

- Cognitive competence approach to ritual structure (agent, action, patient; special agent rituals).
- Program-building for CSR as named field.
- By-product-compatible account of ritual cognition (White's table of by-product vs adaptation).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Collaborator: [[mccauley-robert-n]]. Network: [[whitehouse-harvey]], [[white-claire]], [[boyer-pascal]].

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Ritual form theory's formalism criticized as under-ethnographic; McCauley/Lawson program answers by specifying cognitive competence underlying ritual judgments, not full ritual ethnography.
