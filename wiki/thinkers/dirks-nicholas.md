# Nicholas Dirks

## Overview

Nicholas Dirks (b. 1950) is an American historian and anthropologist of South
Asia. He is cited in [[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich (2018)]] as the leading
exponent of the view that strong [[caste]] endogamy, as documented in the modern
era, was substantially a **British colonial-era construction** — systematized and
in significant part produced through colonial census-taking and administration —
rather than an ancient practice (see [[caste-origins-debate]]).

## Contributions

- ***Castes of Mind* (2001).** Argued that caste in its modern, rigid form is
  inseparable from colonial knowledge-production and governance — the position
  against which Reich's genetic evidence for deep pre-colonial endogamy is set
  (attributed, as of Reich 2018).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Works in the postcolonial and historical-anthropological tradition of South Asian
studies; part of the broader colonial-knowledge critique associated with
Saidian and Subaltern Studies currents (enrich from a dedicated source).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

His colonial-construction thesis stands opposite the tribal-continuity account of
[[karve-irawati|Irawati Karve]]; [[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich (2018)]] presents
genetic admixture-dating as favoring deep pre-colonial antiquity for jati
endogamy over the strong colonial-invention reading (see [[caste-origins-debate]]).
