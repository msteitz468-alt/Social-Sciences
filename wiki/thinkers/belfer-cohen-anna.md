# Anna Belfer-Cohen

## Overview

Israeli prehistoric archaeologist; major analyst of [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] variability, origins, and settlement patterning in the Mediterranean zone. Frequent collaborator of [[goring-morris-nigel|A. Nigel Goring-Morris]] and of [[bar-yosef-ofer|Ofer Bar-Yosef]] (Hayonim).

## Contributions

- **Phases and facies.** With Goring-Morris, “Breaking the Mould” (in [[bar-yosef-valla-natufian-foragers-2013]]) treats the Natufian as a cultural complex with substantial intra-/inter-regional and chronological patterning over ~3000 years, not a monolithic culture evolving solely *in situ* from local Geometric Kebaran.
- **Core–periphery.** Challenges simple expansion-from-homeland models; argues Early Natufian contemporaneity in core and semi-arid zones (with Goring-Morris).
- **Burial and symbolic interpretation.** Earlier work on Natufian burial change (ancestor cult / place attachment) is widely cited in textbook syntheses (e.g., via Wenke & Olszewski).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Hebrew University Institute of Archaeology; network with Goring-Morris, Bar-Yosef, and the broader Levantine Epipaleolithic community.
