# Robert D. Drennan

## Overview
American archaeologist (University of Pittsburgh), trained in the Michigan Oaxaca projects, later a leading quantitative comparativist of chiefdoms ([[chiefdom]]). In [[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]] (1983) he wrote the topics on ritual and ceremonial development at the hunter-gatherer and early village levels, the mountain survey zone between the Valleys of Oaxaca and Nochixtlán, and compiled the volume's radiocarbon-date appendix ([[radiocarbon-dating]]).

## Contributions
- Drennan argued (drawing on Rappaport) that ritual served to sanctify information and social convention in egalitarian and early ranked societies, tracing its escalation from preceramic dance grounds to Formative public buildings.
- Systematic radiocarbon chronology for the Oaxaca region as of 1983 (uncalibrated).

## Intellectual Genealogy
Trained under [[flannery-kent-v|Kent Flannery]] at Michigan; collaborator of [[marcus-joyce|Joyce Marcus]] and the Valley of Oaxaca survey group.
