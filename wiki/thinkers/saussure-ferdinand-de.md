# Ferdinand de Saussure (1857–1913)

Swiss linguist whose posthumous *Course in General Linguistics* (1916) founded structural linguistics and, through it, [[structuralism]] across the human sciences. This page is a scaffold created during the ingest of [[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Sahlins's *Culture and Practical Reason*]] and *[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*, which use Saussure heavily; his primary text is not yet ingested.

## Overview

*(Attributed, via Sahlins's use.)* Saussure's decisive contributions as they figure in this wiki: the **sign** as the union of signifier and signified; the **arbitrariness of the sign** (no natural bond between word and thing); the distinction of **langue** (the collective system) from **parole** (individual speech); and above all the theory of **linguistic value** — that "concepts are purely differential and defined not by their positive content but negatively by their relations with other terms in the system." His example that the value of English *sheep* differs from French *mouton* "because it has beside it a second term" (*mutton*) is Sahlins's recurrent proof that no language is "a mere nomenclature."

## Contributions

- **The differential theory of value**, which [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] transposes to economics: "economic value is Saussurean... the differential standing of a given object in a system of meaningful relationships," and Western exchange-value is the production of "a symbolically significant difference" (see [[la-pensee-bourgeoise]]). Saussure himself, Sahlins notes, modeled linguistic value on economic value — Sahlins reverses the debt.
- **The arbitrariness of the symbol** as "the indicative condition of human culture," extended by Sahlins beyond the sign to cultural propositions generally (see [[culture]], [[cultural-reason]]).
- **Synchrony/diachrony**, which Sahlins revises: against a purely synchronic model of structure he argues (crediting Hocart) for an "internal diachrony" of the categories (see [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]]).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Saussure's *Course*, compiled by his students, became the fountainhead of European structuralism, taken up by Jakobson, [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]], and — critically and at one remove — by [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]]. Formal training relations are not documented in the ingested sources; `trained_under`/`trained` left as `[[unknown]]`.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Sahlins's principal amendment is that Saussurean structure is too static: reducing structure to "tables of paired binary contraries" is "yin-yang structuralism, without a Book of Changes." Any such proportion is a partial, interested statement taken from a standpoint, not the total system of relations. Broader critiques of Saussure (from post-structuralism, pragmatics, and sociolinguistics) lie outside the ingested sources.
