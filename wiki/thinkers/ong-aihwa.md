# Aihwa Ong

## Overview

Malaysian-born anthropologist (locally-born Chinese; trained at Columbia) best known for *[[ong-spirits-of-resistance-1987|Spirits of Resistance and Capitalist Discipline]]* (1987), a landmark ethnography of Malay [[neophyte-factory-women|neophyte factory women]] in free-trade-zone electronics plants. The book links agrarian transition, transnational corporate discipline, public sexual discourse, and shopfloor [[spirits-of-resistance|spirit possession]] as cultural struggle. Later work (beyond this ingest) on flexible citizenship, neoliberalism, and Asian biopolitics is not yet held.

## Contributions

- **Industrial ethnography of gender and capital:** factory discipline as multi-scalar power (household, kampung, state, Japanese corporation) rather than shopfloor alone — [[ong-spirits-of-resistance-study]].
- **[[capitalist-discipline]]:** power effects that enforce compliance for production inside and outside the firm; dialogue with Foucault's docile bodies and Marxist labor-process theory.
- **[[spirits-of-resistance]]:** *hantu* possession episodes as indirect retaliation and claims to humanity under industrial coercion — engaged with, but distinct from, [[taussig-michael|Taussig]]'s use-/exchange-value devil reading.
- **[[neophyte-factory-women]]:** first-generation Malay female industrial labor as contradictory public symbol and new industrial subjectivity.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Trained under** (Columbia Anthropology): Joan Vincent, Clive Kessler, Robert Murphy, William Roff (as Ong acknowledges, 1987 preface).
- **Series / mentors in publication:** June Nash (SUNY Anthropology of Work series editor); readers including Nancy Scheper-Hughes, Brackette Williams, Asraf Ghani.
- **Interlocutors in the 1987 text:** [[taussig-michael|Taussig]] (1980 devil/commodity); Foucault (discipline/docile bodies); E.P. Thompson (working-class culture — Ong argues limited applicability to peripheral Malay labor); Braverman, Burawoy; Scott and peasant studies; Malaysian scholars (e.g. S. Husin Ali, Shamsul, Jamilah Ariffin; Ackerman on possession).
- **Position:** [[feminist-anthropology]] × [[anthropological-political-economy]] at the critical turn; Southeast Asian case for global factory studies.
