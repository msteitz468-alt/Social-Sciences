# Charles E. Orser, Jr.

American historical archaeologist; series editor of Plenum's *Contributions to Global Historical Archaeology*; author of *[[orser-historical-archaeology-modern-world-1996|A Historical Archaeology of the Modern World]]* (1996), the vault's primary charter for **[[global-historical-archaeology]]**. Illinois State University affiliation on the 1996 title page. Volume dedicated to **Brian Fagan** for lessons in readable archaeological prose (Orser and Fagan co-authored the 1995 textbook *Historical Archaeology*).

## Overview

Orser's 1996 program treats historical archaeology as the archaeology of the **modern world** (post-1492 interconnection), organized by four **[[four-haunts|haunts]]** (colonialism, Eurocentrism, capitalism, modernity) and a **[[mutualism-historical-archaeology|mutualist]]** epistemology of nets/relationships. Empirical pairing of [[palmares|Palmares]] (Brazil) and [[gorttoose|Gorttoose]] (Ireland) demonstrates that distant historic contexts share the same structural haunts without being formal comparanda or dual site reports.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Orser 1996 unless noted.)*

- **Global historical archaeology** as named research program and series project — multi-/interdisciplinary field seeking "the global nature of modern life."
- **Definitional intervention:** among period, method, and modern-world definitions of HA, only the last "unlocks the power of the field."
- **Four haunts** framework and **mutualism/nets** alternative to isolationist culture packages.
- **Method slogan:** "think globally, dig locally" — multiscalar questions, local excavation.
- Case work and illustration at Palmares and Gorttoose; Colono Ware and clay-pipe network readings; engagement with subaltern speech under unequal power (Ch. 7 title borrows Spivak's question without full Spivak apparatus).
- Textbook collaboration with Fagan; series editorship institutionalizing global HA.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Axes visible from the charter itself and standard reception expectations (reception monographs not yet ingested):

- Do four haunts over-unify heterogeneous modernities?
- Does 1492 periodization reinstall Eurocentrism?
- Spivak title without deep Spivak engagement.
- Balance of program polemic vs. excavation monograph standards.
- Later field may keep "global HA" while dropping "haunts" vocabulary.

## Historiography

Reputation as a principal American advocate of global/multi-scalar historical archaeology in the 1990s; series editorship as institutional marker. Vault coverage currently rests on the 1996 charter. Thinkers Hub not warranted from a single programmatic book alone.
