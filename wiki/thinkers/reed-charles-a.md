# Charles A. Reed

Zooarchaeologist / paleobiologist; contributed the major regional synthesis “The pattern of animal domestication in the prehistoric Near East” to *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*. Also left a short poem in the volume’s preface (editorial informality).

## Overview

Reed’s paper synthesizes the 1960s picture of when and in what order animals were domesticated in prehistoric SW Asia — a counterpart to botanical progenitor papers (e.g. [[zohary-daniel|Zohary]]) and to [[flannery-kent-v|Flannery]]’s ecological model in the same volume. All taxon sequences and dates are “(as of Reed 1969)” until later sources revise them.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Reed 1969 in Ucko & Dimbleby.)*

- **Domesticates as artifacts:** cultivated plants and domestic animals divert ecosystem energy “as much [as] an Oldowan pebble tool.”
- **Earliest zone:** lower hills and adjacent plains from east Mediterranean into SW Iran (wild emmer/einkorn/two-row barley; wolf, goat, sheep, pig, cattle); possible independent sheep in Dobrogea (Romania) roughly contemporary.
- **Four variables:** animal biology, human biology, environment, culture — discounts evolutionary “readiness” of animals/humans; at ~11 kya environment + culture changing together (glacier melt; geometric microliths; open sites).
- **Zeribar:** accepts warming/tree re-invasion ~11 kya **without** reviving desiccation theory of animal domestication.
- **Villages 11–10 kya:** Natufian (Mallaha), Zawi Chemi Shanidar, Ganj-Dareh — Natufians more complex culture but **no** domestic animals; Zawi Chemi domestic sheep (Perkins).
- **Ruminant preadaptation:** cellulose conversion without competing with human food; delay of domestication was **cultural readiness of man**.
- **Village life** as primary requirement for earliest ruminant/pig domestication; counterexamples of villages with milling stones but without domesticates (Mureybat, Bouqras, Natufian).
- **Palestine enigma:** rejects total pre-6500 BP absence of domestication given Beidha goats/barley and related evidence.
- **Sequence notes (as of 1969):** dog Çayönü/Jarmo; pig early Çayönü; cattle late (Argissa ~8500 BP, Asia later).

## Intellectual Genealogy

1968 London seminar network; dialogue with Bökönyi, Ducos, Clutton-Brock, Flannery, and Near Eastern excavators cited in the volume.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Near Eastern domestication chronologies have been heavily revised by calibrated radiocarbon, Ganj Dareh/Ali Kosh reanalyses, and aDNA (as of post-1969 literature) — keep Reed’s pattern as historiography of the 1969 consensus, not current wiki-voice sequence.
