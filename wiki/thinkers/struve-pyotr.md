# Pyotr Struve

Russian economist and political thinker: the leading "**legal Marxist**" of the 1890s (Marxist economics published legally against Narodnik economics), then the exemplary case of the trajectory *out* of Marxism — revisionist "critical trend" by 1899–1900, liberal Constitutional-Democrat politics after 1905. Known to the wiki through Lenin's polemics and editorial apparatus; no work of his is ingested.

## Overview and Contributions (trajectory, as documented in ingested sources)

- **Ally phase:** *Critical Remarks* (1894) attacked Narodnik economics from a Marxist direction; Lenin's [[lenin-development-of-capitalism-in-russia-1899|Development of Capitalism (1899)]] cites him (with Bulgakov and Tugan-Baranovsky) as a fellow combatant on realization theory and the home market.
- **Break:** In "Uncritical Criticism" (1900), Lenin names Struve and Tugan-Baranovsky as the Russian "new critical trend" — eclectic, part neo-Kantian, echoing Bernstein's German revisionism — against which Lenin defines orthodoxy as "critical application and further development" of Marx.
- **Retrospect:** the 1907/8 second edition of *Development of Capitalism* interpolates the verdict that Struve, Tugan-Baranovsky, and Bulgakov "tried to be Marxists in 1899" but had "turned into plain bourgeois economists."

## Critiques and Reappraisals

All ingested characterizations are Lenin's — a hostile witness documenting a break he had stakes in. Struve's own writings (Critical Remarks; his later *Vekhi* liberalism and emigre career) are un-ingested; the page should be rebuilt from primary or scholarly material when a source lands.

## Historiography

Struve is the standard case study for the "legal Marxism → liberalism" path in histories of Russian social thought (Pipes's two-volume biography is the reference work — not ingested).
