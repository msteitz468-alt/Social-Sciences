# Helen Codere

## Overview

Helen Codere (1917–2009), American anthropologist, editor of [[boas-franz|Franz Boas]]'s posthumous *[[boas-kwakiutl-ethnography-1966|Kwakiutl Ethnography]]* (1966) and author of *Fighting with Property* (1950; not yet ingested), the standard mid-century reanalysis of [[potlatch]] history. Her first field trip to the [[kwakiutl|Kwakiutl]] was in 1951, twenty-one years after Boas's final trip; her 1966 editorial introduction reconstructs Boas the fieldworker partly from the testimony of elderly Kwakiutl who remembered him.

## Contributions

- **The 1966 edition.** Assembled Boas's unfinished ethnography from the unpublished manuscript and his published corpus (~175 Kwakiutl publications, 1886–1943), with a substantial critical introduction on Boas's ethnographic aims, the fieldwork history, and the [[hunt-george|George Hunt]] collaboration. Her editorial apparatus keeps provenance visible (source notes per section; explicit flags where she substituted published material, e.g. *Primitive Art* 1927 for the missing Arts chapter).
- **Defense of Boas's method.** Codere argued (1966) Boas's ethnographic achievement was under-recognized because he never produced a final synthesis, because scholars dipped into the 57-year corpus as if homogeneous rather than using his final corrected statements, and because his "austere" formal conclusions were overlooked as not being conclusions — contrasting his reception with Malinowski's, and citing Lévi-Strauss as the exception who used Boas's formal art analyses seriously.
- **"Informal culture."** Coined the term (Codere 1959) for the commonplace low-stakes material — jokes, gossip, byplay — that ethnography systematically under-documents.
- **Myth-density argument.** Argued (1959) that the huge proportion of myth in Kwakiutl ethnography reflects myths' centrality as sanctions of social position and crest symbology, not merely the ease of dictating texts.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Editor and continuator of the Boasian Kwakiutl program; positions in the [[potlatch-interpretation-debate]] descend from her 1950 monograph.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Her editorial selections constitute a second authorial layer over the Boas–Hunt corpus (chapter framing, the Marriage section's placement, the Arts substitution) — a fact she documents herself. Assessments of *Fighting with Property* await ingestion of that work.
