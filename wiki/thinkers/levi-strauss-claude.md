# Claude Lévi-Strauss

## Overview

Claude Lévi-Strauss (1908–2009) was the central figure of twentieth-century **[[structuralism|structural anthropology]]** — the program that sought the unconscious logical structures (above all [[binary-opposition|binary oppositions]]) underlying kinship, myth, and classification. Five of his works are now primary-ingested: the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures of Kinship]]* (1949), founding text of [[alliance-theory]]; *[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]* (1955), the reflexive memoir of his Brazilian fieldwork; *[[levi-strauss-structural-anthropology-1958|Structural Anthropology]]* (1958), the method manifesto; *[[levi-strauss-savage-mind-1962|The Savage Mind]]* (1962), on the logic of "untamed" thought; and *[[levi-strauss-miel-aux-cendres-1966|Du miel aux cendres]]* (Mythologiques II, 1966/67), the honey/tobacco volume extending the [[culinary-triangle]] into infra- and meta-culinary surrounds. He also appears in this vault as the **principal polemical target** of [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]'s *[[bourdieu-outline-1977|Outline of a Theory of Practice]]*.

## Contributions

- **Structuralism** as a method: treat cultural phenomena (kinship terminologies, myths, food, totemic classifications) as sign systems whose meaning lies in relations of opposition, on the analogy of Saussurean and Jakobsonian structural linguistics. See [[structuralism]], [[binary-opposition]].
- **The [[alliance-theory|elementary structures of kinship]]** (1949): marriage rules — [[cross-cousin-marriage]], the [[exchange-of-women]] — as systems of [[restricted-exchange|restricted]] and [[generalized-exchange|generalized]] exchange generating alliance; the [[incest-taboo|incest prohibition]] as the hinge between nature and culture ([[nature-culture-distinction]]) and "the supreme rule of the gift."
- ***Tristes Tropiques*** (1955): reflexive anthropology avant la lettre — the anti-travelogue, the "three mistresses" (geology, Marxism, psychoanalysis) as the roots of the structural method, [[chieftainship-by-consent]] and the "writing lesson" among the [[nambikwara|Nambikwara]], the [[caduveo|Caduveo]] art-as-structure thesis, and "[[entropology]]."
- ***[[levi-strauss-structural-anthropology-1958|Structural Anthropology]]*** (1958): the method manifesto — the **[[atom-of-kinship|atom of kinship]]**, the structural study of **[[myth]]** (mytheme, the Oedipus analysis), the **[[effectiveness-of-symbols|effectiveness of symbols]]**, and the theory of **models** (mechanical vs statistical).
- ***[[levi-strauss-savage-mind-1962|The Savage Mind]]*** (1962): the **[[science-of-the-concrete|science of the concrete]]**; **[[bricolage]]**; the dissolution of **[[totemism]]**; **[[cold-and-hot-societies|cold vs hot societies]]**; and the closing polemic against **[[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre]]** ([[levi-strauss-sartre-debate]]). "Savage" thought is a different, equally rigorous ordering, not a pre-logical one.
- **Mythologiques**: myth as a combinatorial system transforming a small stock of oppositions across variants. *[[levi-strauss-miel-aux-cendres-1966|Du miel aux cendres]]* (Vol. II; study [[levi-strauss-miel-aux-cendres-study]]): honey **infra-culinary** / tobacco **meta-culinary**; closed transformation groups (S₁/S₃/S₋₃) with bird-nester armature; dry-season “mythology of Lent”; instruments of darkness (noise/stench/rattles); coda — logic of qualities → logic of forms without evolutionary necessity; structure grants history irreducible contingency. Vol. I *Le Cru et le Cuit* still not primary-ingested.
- **"[[primitiveness|The Concept of Primitiveness]]"** (closing essay to *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]*, 1968; his Viking Fund Medal address, 8 April 1966): recent hunter-gatherers are often "regressive rather than primitive" (devolved from richer antecedents), not windows onto the Paleolithic — a caution against [[ethnographic-analogy]] and evolutionist ranking. He also defended structural models against statistical behaviour (a ~20% cross-cousin-marriage rate suffices to "imprint the society with a given structure"), the position Hiatt contested at the symposium (see [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]] and [[primitiveness]]).

Trained for the *agrégation de philosophie*; recruited in 1934 by Célestin Bouglé to the new University of São Paulo, arriving in Brazil in 1935; conducted the Brazilian fieldwork (1935–39) that grounds *Tristes Tropiques*; fled Vichy France in 1941 (with Breton and Victor Serge aboard the *Capitaine-Paul-Lemerle*) to the New School in New York, where he met **Roman Jakobson** — the decisive encounter for structuralism — and Boas.

## Sociétés à Maisons (attributed, via Moore 2005)

Lévi-Strauss developed the **[[house-society|"house societies"]]** (*sociétés à maisons*) concept in lectures and *The Way of the Masks* (1983), building from an analysis of Franz Boas's shifting attempts to classify Kwakiutl kinship — Boas ultimately abandoned unilineal/bilateral models and resorted to the Kwakiutl term *numaym*, "resigned himself to describing it as 'a type of structure without equivalent in the archives of ethnology'" (Lévi-Strauss 1983:170, quoting Boas). Lévi-Strauss defined the house as "a corporate body holding an estate made up of both material and immaterial wealth, which perpetuates itself through the transmission of its name, its goods, and its titles down a real or imaginary line of descent," extending the concept to Yurok, medieval European noble houses, and — more speculatively — Japan, ancient Greece, Polynesia, Indonesia, Melanesia, and Africa. [[moore-cultural-landscapes-andes-2005|Jerry Moore (2005)]] applies the concept to North Coast Peruvian architecture (see [[chan-chan]], [[chimu-culture]], [[sican-culture]]).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- **Descola's revision** (*[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]*, 2005): retains structural method; overturns nature/culture hinge; restores ontological totemism alongside classificatory reading; student/lineage figure [[descola-philippe]] (see [[four-ontologies]]).


- **Bourdieu's practice critique** (documented here in primary form): structuralism mistakes "the model constructed to account for practice for the principle of practice," detemporalizes exchange, and substitutes the rule for the strategy. Where Lévi-Strauss reads cousin marriage as the realization of a structural code, Bourdieu reads it as matrimonial *strategy* generated by [[habitus]]. See [[strategy-vs-rule]], [[gift-exchange]].
- **The prescriptive/preferential dispute** with [[needham-rodney|Needham]] over what the *Elementary Structures* is about ([[prescriptive-preferential-debate]]); the **Leach** disputes over the [[kachin|Kachin]] and [[murngin|Murngin]].
- **The feminist critique**: the [[exchange-of-women]] premise (Gayle Rubin, "The Traffic in Women," 1975), extended by [[butler-judith|Judith Butler]] (*[[butler-gender-trouble-1990|Gender Trouble]]*, 1990), who argued (attributed) that the kinship system rests on repressed homosocial bonds and that the "must have" of the origin of exchange "function[s] as a performative" injunction rather than a historical inference (see [[gender-performativity]]).
- **The writing/logocentrism critique**: Derrida's *Of Grammatology* (1967) on the [[nambikwara|Nambikwara]] "writing lesson."
- **The "becomings of structuralism" reappraisal**: [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] (*[[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]*, 2009/2014) rereads Lévi-Strauss through [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]], distinguishing "two structuralisms" — an algebraic/discrete one and a dynamic, rhizomatic one emerging across the *Mythologiques* (culminating in *The Story of Lynx*). He casts the later Lévi-Strauss as a "poststructuralist" and "a philosopher of Amerindian thought," with "perpetual disequilibrium" (the unequal twins) as the deep principle — "a structuralism without structure." See [[structuralism]], [[perspectivism]], [[ontological-turn]].

## Historiography

- **Collection coverage: strong.** Five primary texts ingested (1949, 1955, 1958, 1962, Mythologiques II 1966/67). *Mythologiques* Vol. I, III, IV still outstanding for full series coverage.
- *Tristes Tropiques* is itself a historiographical object — an early reflexive text anticipating the 1980s *Writing Culture* critique; its charged comparative-religion passages on Islam, Buddhism, and Christianity are recorded in attributed voice (see [[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955]]).
- The relation to Mauss, Jakobson, and Saussure, and the full reception of structuralism, are now grounded in primary ingestion rather than refracted solely through Bourdieu's polemic.

## Clifford's reading (*The Predicament of Culture*, 1988)

*(Attributed — [[clifford-james|James Clifford]], in [[clifford-predicament-of-culture-1988|The Predicament of Culture]].)* Clifford critiques the *[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]* entropy narrative as eloquent but "too neat," resting on a "questionable Eurocentric position at the 'end' of a unified human history." Ch. 10 situates Lévi-Strauss's wartime New York exile (1941–47) as a "chronotope" of global entropy: at the École Libre des Hautes Études he attended Roman Jakobson's lectures on sound and meaning, which supplied the phonemic-analysis model for kinship structures; *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]* was researched in the New York Public Library and drafted in a Greenwich Village studio near Claude Shannon. He published a 1943 *Gazette des beaux-arts* essay praising Northwest Coast masks (with a Picasso comparison) and contributed to the surrealist journal *VVV* (1942–43), including a Malinowski obituary. Clifford reads Lévi-Strauss's period image of "the feathered Indian with the Parker pen" as registering salvage/survival and parody, missing that the contemporaneous Collier-era Indian New Deal was enabling actual tribal reorganization and emergence rather than only decline — evidence, for Clifford, that surrealist-inflected structural anthropology and salvage ethnography shared blind spots about coevalness. See [[salvage-ethnography]], [[art-culture-system]].

## From Kuper, *Invention of Primitive Society* (1988)

*(Attributed — Kuper 1988, Ch. 11.)* Alliance theory as L-S creation from reading; Kuper ranks Frazer’s cross-cousin marriage theory above Jakobson/Mauss as direct kinship source; *Elementary Structures* as transformation of Frazer (reciprocity replacing economic barter). Restricted/generalized exchange; atom of kinship; reception via Leiden, Leach, Needham, Dumont. Larger frame: another transformation of the [[primitive-society]] prototype competing with [[descent-theory]]. Source: [[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988]].

## From Leach, *Rethinking Anthropology* (1961) — as interlocutor

*(Attributed — Leach.)* Mutual non-citation of Leach’s 1945 Jinghpaw paper and *ESK* chs. 15–16 due to publication lag; LS (1953) noted similarity of structure-as-model views. Ch. 3 (1951): early English commentary — great sympathy with structural method; ethnographic inaccuracy charges (Chin/Kachin conflation; prestations; rank direction). Ch. 1: Leach’s preferred comparative program “derives from” LS against R-B typology. Ch. 6: myth analysis under LS influence (heard LS myth lecture before writing Cronus essay). Source: [[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961]].
