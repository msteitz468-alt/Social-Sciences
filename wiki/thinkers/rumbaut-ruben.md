# Rubén G. Rumbaut

U.S. sociologist of immigration and the second generation; co-author with [[portes-alejandro|Alejandro Portes]] of *[[portes-rumbaut-legacies-2001|Legacies]]* (2001) and co-director of the [[children-of-immigrants-longitudinal-study|Children of Immigrants Longitudinal Study (CILS)]].

## Overview

Rumbaut's work within *Legacies* is especially visible on ethnic identity, the psychology of the second generation ("the crucible within"), language, and Southeast Asian refugee youth in San Diego. He co-develops [[segmented-assimilation]] with Portes.

## Contributions

- Co-architect of **CILS** design and analysis ([[portes-rumbaut-legacies-study]]).
- Ethnic self-identification dynamics and [[reactive-ethnicity]].
- Psychosocial outcomes (self-esteem, depressive affect, ambition) as adaptation indicators.
- Language/bilingualism patterns in the new second generation.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Long collaboration with [[portes-alejandro]] (from *Immigrant America* conversation, Atlanta 1988, per *Legacies* acknowledgments). Training lineage not yet filled from ingested sources.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Shared with the segmented-assimilation / CILS program: site selection, measurement of identity and bilingualism, and the durability of adolescent patterns into adulthood.
