# Raúl Prebisch

Argentine economist (1901–1986), leading figure of the UN Economic Commission for Latin America (CEPAL/ECLA). In this vault he appears substantially through [[connell-raewyn|Connell]]'s *[[connell-southern-theory-2007|Southern Theory]]* (2007, ch. 7) as a case of **Southern theory in economics**: centre–periphery analysis, critique of the false universality of metropolitan economics, and the political fate of autonomy projects under later dictatorships and neoliberalism.

## Overview

Connell reports Prebisch as starting from orthodox economics, then developing a dynamic account of centre–periphery trade and industrialisation after wartime consultancy and CEPAL work. Prebisch argued that metropolitan economic theory treated metropolitan experience as universal; peripheral economies needed concepts adequate to deteriorating terms of trade and dependent industrialisation (import-substitution strategies). Connell pairs this with later Latin American debates on culture, dependency, hybridity, and the collapse of developmentalist autonomy projects — and notes that **neoliberalism is not Southern theory**.

## Contributions (as mediated by Connell 2007)

- [[centre-periphery]] framing of the world economy
- Sociology-of-knowledge point: mainstream economics as ethnoscience of the metropole framed as universal (Connell's parallel to her critique of Northern sociological theory)
- Policy-intellectual tradition feeding dependency debates and [[modernization-vs-dependency-world-systems-debate]]

## Intellectual Genealogy

From orthodoxy to CEPAL structuralism; contested by Northern economists (Connell flags blunt put-downs of "economics for developing countries"); related to but not identical with Frank/Amin delinking or [[world-systems-analysis]]. Cardoso & Faletto's dependent development is discussed by Connell as refining, not simply opposing, dependence.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Later Latin American cultural and post-dictatorship debates (as Connell reads them) move beyond pure trade-structural models. Full primary Prebisch ingest not yet in this vault.
