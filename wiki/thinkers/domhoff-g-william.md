# G. William Domhoff

## Overview

G. William (Bill) Domhoff is an American sociologist (UC Santa Cruz, Distinguished Professor Emeritus) whose career centers on **power-structure research**: mapping the US corporate community, social upper class, policy-planning network, and their domination of government. This page is grounded primarily in *[[domhoff-who-rules-america-2022|Who Rules America?]]* (8th ed. 2022), the multi-edition flagship of that program (1st ed. 1967).

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Domhoff unless noted.)*

- **Class-domination account of US power.** Corporate owners and top executives, socially organized as an upper class and organizationally through a corporate community and policy-planning network, dominate government — not as a conspiracy, but through documented network processes (special-interest, policy-planning, candidate-selection / opinion-shaping).
- **[[corporate-community|Corporate community]]** and **[[policy-planning-network|policy-planning network]]** as empirical objects (boards, foundations, think tanks, discussion groups).
- **[[power-indicators|Power indicators]]** (who benefits, who governs, who wins) as operational criteria for power claims.
- **Revision of Mills.** Shares elite-structure concern with [[mills-c-wright|Mills]]’s [[power-elite]] but recenters **class and corporate organization** over the mid-century military–political–corporate triad; military and parties treated as secondary in the US configuration (as argued in WRA 8th).
- **Foil to pluralism.** Principal empirical challenger to Dahlian community-power and pluralist images — see [[power-elite-vs-pluralism-debate]].

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Lineage:** Power-structure research after Mills and Floyd Hunter; opponent of pluralist political science (Dahl and successors).
- **Collaborators named in 8th ed. acknowledgments:** Richard L. Zweigenhaft (diversity in corporations/cabinets); Adam Schneider (long-term research assistant/co-author on databases).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

*(Positions — densify from secondary sources.)* Pluralists charge overstated elite unity and understated electoral/interest-group power; state-autonomy theorists charge understatement of state interests; some Marxists charge under-theorized capital fractions or over-focus on social upper class. Domhoff insists on multi-indicator evidence and rejects conspiracy theory explicitly.

## Historiography

First edition 1967; successive editions track corporate and party realignment through the 2020s. The 8th edition’s framing of “white nationalist Republicans” and “inclusionary Democrats” is a contemporary periodization of the party system relative to the corporate rich — record as Domhoff’s analysis of the 2020s, not timeless taxonomy.
