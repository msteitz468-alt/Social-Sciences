# Eduardo Viveiros de Castro

## Overview

Eduardo Viveiros de Castro (b. 1951) is a Brazilian anthropologist, based at the Museu Nacional (Rio de Janeiro), whose name is synonymous with **Amerindian [[perspectivism]]** and **[[multinaturalism]]** and who became a central figure of the anthropological "[[ontological-turn|ontological turn]]." A Jesuit-educated native of Rio and (in translator Peter Skafish's phrase) "virtuoso of carioca irony," his ethnographic base is fieldwork among the [[arawete|Araweté]] of the eastern Amazon, published as *From the Enemy's Point of View* (1992 [1986]). Primary-ingested here: *[[viveiros-de-castro-cannibal-metaphysics-2014|Cannibal Metaphysics]]* (2009/2014), his programmatic statement of a "post-structural anthropology."

His signature move is to treat the concepts of the peoples anthropologists study as **concepts** — genuine philosophy — rather than beliefs, worldviews, or cognitions, and thereby to enlist anthropology in what he calls "the permanent decolonization of thought" (attributed). He is a self-described structuralist "only in a Deleuzian sense": his work rereads [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] through [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]] and Guattari.

## Contributions

- **[[perspectivism|Amerindian perspectivism]]** (formulated with Tania Stolze Lima, mid-1990s; "Cosmological Deixis and Amerindian Perspectivism," *JRAI* 1998 [1996]): nonhuman beings see *themselves* as persons and see humans as animals (prey or predator); what differs across species is the **body** (a "bundle of affects," a habitus), not the soul or culture. "The point of view creates the subject."
- **[[multinaturalism]]**: the ontological corollary — one culture/soul and *many natures/bodies*, the structural inverse of Western multiculturalism (one nature, many cultures).
- **[[controlled-equivocation]]**: a theory of anthropological comparison and translation as the management of referential alterity between homonymous concepts ("controlled" as walking is a controlled way of falling).
- **The metaphysics of predation and the "cannibal cogito"**: reworking Tupinambá and Araweté cannibalism, he argues the self is constituted through the **enemy's** point of view rather than the Cartesian/Aristotelian relation of self to self — "not only a perspectivism but an 'enemyism.'"
- **Virtual/potential affinity**: affinity (the affine = the enemy) as the generic, cosmological form of relation in Amazonia, prior to and more encompassing than consanguinity — kinship as "the political economy of the universe."
- **"Two structuralisms" / becomings of structuralism**: a rereading of Lévi-Strauss's *Mythologiques* as a "poststructuralist," rhizomatic project — "a structuralism without structure."

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Forerunner reworked:** [[levi-strauss-claude|Claude Lévi-Strauss]] — whom Viveiros de Castro recasts as "a philosopher of Amerindian thought," reading structuralism as itself "a structural transformation of Amerindian thought" (with Anne-Christine Taylor's formula, "the Amerindian foundations of structuralism").
- **Philosophical resource:** [[deleuze-gilles|Gilles Deleuze]] (and Félix Guattari) — *Difference and Repetition*, *Anti-Oedipus*, *A Thousand Plateaus*; Viveiros de Castro casts perspectivism as "the becoming-Amazonian of Deleuze."
- **Interlocutors / affinities:** [[descola-philippe|Philippe Descola]] (lifelong friend and theoretical opponent — see [[ontological-turn-debate]]); [[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]] (his self-declared "impossible twin"); [[wagner-roy|Roy Wagner]] ("the invention of culture," "reverse anthropology"); [[latour-bruno|Bruno Latour]] (symmetric anthropology). He names Wagner, Strathern, and Latour the discipline's genuine "post-structuralists." Pierre Clastres (society against the state; a political philosophy of cannibalism) and Patrice Maniglier are recurring references.
- **Disputed:** Françoise Héritier (a chief inheritor of Lévi-Strauss's alliance theory), with whom he debated in Paris.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

The standing point of contention is his relation to [[descola-philippe|Descola]]: Viveiros de Castro insists "perspectivism is not, *pace* Descola, a subtype of [[animism]]... It is not a type but a concept," resisting subsumption within Descola's [[four-ontologies|four-ontologies matrix]] (see [[ontological-turn-debate]]). Critics of the wider ontological turn question whether concepts abstracted from Amazonian material generalize, and whether his Amerindian cosmology is ethnographic report or philosophical construction — a caution the wiki registers by holding all his claims in **attributed voice**. His own reflexive answer: "we cannot think like Indians; at most, we can think with them."
