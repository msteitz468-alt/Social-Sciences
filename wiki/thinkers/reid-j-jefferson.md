# J. Jefferson Reid

American archaeologist; co-developer of [[behavioral-archaeology|behavioral archaeology]] with [[schiffer-michael-b|Michael B. Schiffer]]. Schiffer’s preface to *[[schiffer-formation-processes-1987|Formation Processes of the Archaeological Record]]* (1987) credits Reid with first urging him to write the book (1982) and lists Reid among general influences on the theoretical framework.

## Overview

Southwest-focused archaeologist associated with the University of Arizona behavioral archaeology circle. Appears in the wiki primarily via Schiffer’s acknowledgments and the program’s intellectual genealogy pending dedicated sources.

## Contributions

- Co-articulation of behavioral archaeology / formation-process concerns in the 1970s–80s (attributed via Schiffer 1987 front matter and secondary reports).

## Intellectual Genealogy

Close colleague of Schiffer; part of the Arizona network that also intersects CRM and Southwest chronological debates (Hohokam) treated in Schiffer 1987.
