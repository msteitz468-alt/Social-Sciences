# Philippe Bourgois

## Overview

U.S. anthropologist of urban poverty, drug markets, labor, and social suffering. Best known for *[[bourgois-in-search-of-respect-1995|In Search of Respect: Selling Crack in El Barrio]]* (1995; 2nd ed. 2003) — multi-year [[participant-observation]] among Nuyorican crack dealers in [[el-barrio|East Harlem]]. Later work (with photographer Jeff Schonberg) on homeless heroin users in San Francisco (*Righteous Dopefiend*, 2009 — not yet ingested). Institutional base at UCSF (Anthropology, History and Social Medicine) at the time of the 2003 edition; earlier affiliations included San Francisco State and research institutes in New York.

## Contributions

- **[[bourgois-in-search-of-respect-study|In Search of Respect]]** — landmark ethnography of the crack economy as window onto class/ethnic apartheid, legal-labor exclusion, school failure, gender violence, and family crisis; study page and [[hubs/studies/bourgois-in-search-of-respect-study|Studies Hub]].
- **[[street-culture]]** — analytic framing of inner-city street culture as resistance *and* self-destruction (not freestanding [[culture-of-poverty]]).
- Dialogue with [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]] ([[cultural-capital]], symbolic power), [[wilson-william-julius|Wilson]] (concentrated poverty / joblessness), [[wacquant-loic|Wacquant]] (editing and later carceral sociology), and the culture-of-poverty debate.
- Method: long-term residential [[participant-observation]] with tape-recorded conversations; explicit politics-of-representation stance against both exoticizing and sanitizing the poor.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Interlocutors (via *Respect* acknowledgments and text):** Loïc [[wacquant-loic|Wacquant]] (intensive editorial critique); Pierre [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]] (French series; symbolic power); Eric Wolf; Robert Merton; Marc Edelman; Nancy Scheper-Hughes; William Julius [[wilson-william-julius|Wilson]]; Arthur Kleinman; Saskia Sassen; Terry Williams; and others.
- **Earlier book:** *Ethnicity at Work* (1989) on Central American banana-plantation labor — political-economy ethnography preceding the East Harlem project.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Politics of representing violence, rape, and addiction — Bourgois himself theorizes the risk of reinforcing stereotypes; critics debate whether graphic material is justified or exploitative (positions on study page).
- Gender: Ch. 6’s rape and sexual-conquest material is load-bearing and contested in feminist reception (record positions, do not adjudicate here).
- Generalizability from a small dealer network in one East Harlem microneighborhood.
- Afterlife: widely taught in urban anthropology/sociology; cited alongside [[anderson-elijah|Anderson]]’s [[code-of-the-street]] (related but distinct program).
