# Juliet Clutton-Brock (1933–2015)

British archaeozoologist; Department of Human Environment, Institute of Archaeology, London (seminar participant affiliation). Contributed “Carnivore remains from the excavations of the Jericho Tell” to *[[ucko-dimbleby-domestication-exploitation-1969|Ucko & Dimbleby 1969]]*.

## Overview

Clutton-Brock’s Jericho carnivore paper is a site-specific zooarchaeological study within the seminar’s regional evidence section — dog and other carnivores at a type early-farming tell. The volume’s editors note her sub-specific *Canis* discussion as an exception to their binomial standardization. Findings attributed and date-stamped after extraction.

## Contributions

*(Attributed — Clutton-Brock 1969; densified after claims integration.)*

- Analysis of carnivore remains from Jericho excavations.
- Methodological attention to canid taxonomy and domestication-related morphology at an early village site.

## Intellectual Genealogy

Institute of Archaeology human-environment circle; seminar cohort with [[dimbleby-g-w|Dimbleby]], [[ucko-peter-j|Ucko]], and faunal specialists (Bökönyi, Ducos, Grigson, Payne, Reed).

## Critiques and Reappraisals

Jericho sequences and early dog claims have long afterlives; 1969 identifications are a period dataset.
