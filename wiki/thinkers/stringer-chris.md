# Christopher Stringer (b. 1947)

British paleoanthropologist at the Natural History Museum, London (from 1973); principal fossil-morphology and synthesis voice of **[[recent-african-origin|Recent African Origin (RAO)]]** against **[[multiregional-evolution]]**. Co-author of *[[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|In Search of the Neanderthals]]* (1993) with [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]; sole author of the career-length synthesis *[[stringer-lone-survivors-2012|Lone Survivors]]* (2012). Deep analysis: [[hubs/thinkers/stringer-chris|Thinkers Hub]]. Live dispute: [[modern-human-origins-debate]].

## Overview

Stringer’s program argues that [[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern humans]] originated primarily in Africa and dispersed, largely replacing archaic populations including [[neanderthals]] — with the 2012 book explicitly absorbing early nuclear aDNA evidence of limited Neanderthal and [[denisovans|Denisovan]] admixture as “**mostly Out of Africa**,” still labeled RAO when archaic contribution stays modest (under ~10% as he frames it). Positions below are horizon-stamped: **as of S&G 1993** vs **as of Stringer 2012**.

## Contributions

### From *In Search of the Neanderthals* (1993, with Gamble)

- **Out of Africa 2 program.** Recent single ancestral modern population in Africa; Neanderthals not primary regional ancestors of living Europeans; Out of Africa 2 (~130–50 ka) vs *erectus* Out of Africa 1 ~1 Ma (S&G 1993).
- **Ancients vs Moderns** operational division; anatomy decoupled from behaviour (S&G 1993).
- **Levant absolute dating reordering** (TL/ESR/U-series): Skhul/Qafzeh early moderns ~100+ ka before or coeval with some Neanderthals (S&G 1993).
- **African fossil sequence** as strongest modern homeland claim; *Homo heidelbergensis* as Afro-European ancestor splitting toward Neanderthals north and moderns in Africa (S&G 1993).
- **Extinction as “whimper rather than a bang”** — competition and demography, not genocide-as-sole-mechanism (S&G 1993).
- **Genetics 1993 horizon:** mtDNA Eve and nuclear trees as support; complete-replacement genetics already hedged (S&G 1993).

### From *Lone Survivors* (2012) — densified

- **Career formation.** Switched from medicine to anthropology at 18; Bristol Ph.D. 1970 on Neanderthals; NHM Palaeontology post 1973; July 1971 tour of ten European countries measuring ~100 archaic and modern skulls; comparative modern data from William Howells (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Early multivariate results.** Neanderthal skulls no more similar to recent Europeans than to Africans, Eskimo, or Native Tasmanians; Cro-Magnons did not slot between Neanderthals and recent Europeans; early moderns clustered with modern counterparts rather than local archaics — no support for Neanderthal ancestry of Cro-Magnons or multiregional/spectrum continuity (as of Stringer 2012 recalling early-1970s analyses).
- **Program chronology.** Gravitated to Howells’s 1976 Garden of Eden/Noah’s Ark model; privately convinced Africa was main center by 1980; strong public Out of Africa stance by ~1984; RAO rose from a view “no one held in 1970” to dominant model in under thirty years (as of Stringer 2012).
- **1988 *Science* paper with Peter Andrews** set out contrasting RAO vs Multiregional models that organized subsequent debate (as of Stringer 2012 intro).
- **“Mostly Out of Africa” update.** Places his 2012 stance near Bräuer’s Out of Africa + Hybridization or a **modest** [[assimilation-model|Assimilation]] model; if archaic assimilation remains under ~10% of the genome, still RAO (as of Stringer 2012). Maps a continuum from pure replacement through Assimilation (~center) to classic Multiregionalism.
- **Genetics absorption (2012 horizon).** ~2% Neanderthal DNA in non-Africans; up to ~9% at one X-chromosome locus; Melanesians ~5% Denisovan / ~8% total archaic — “surprisingly high” to pure Out-of-Africa adherents; serial founder decline of diversity with distance from Africa challenges pan-assimilation except Melanesian hotspot (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Behavioural revision.** Revises 1993 underestimation of archaic competence (Boxgrove hunting, Schöningen spears, primary access); theory of mind / social brain; MSA symbolism (e.g. [[blombos-cave]]); anatomical ≠ behavioural modernity retained; McBrearty–Brooks gradualism vs Klein ~50 ka switch as live alternatives (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Modernity as composite.** “Modernity” was not a unique package from one African time/place/population (as of Stringer 2012) — softens single Garden of Eden behavioural packaging.
- **Regional (“racial”) features.** Shared species morphology first in Africa; regional features superimposed later largely by drift (cranial diversity declines with distance from Africa), with exceptions under strong selection (cold-adapted faces; pigmentation, lactase, malaria resistance, EDAR-linked traits as quirky genetics — all attributed, as of Stringer 2012). Not race typology.
- **Flores.** Treats [[homo-floresiensis]] as likely genuine distinct species; unconvinced on full behavioural package without excluding later moderns at Liang Bua (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Ongoing evolution.** Argues human evolution has not stopped; agriculture and medicine reshape selection (as of Stringer 2012) — against stoppage claims he associates with Gould/Jones-type arguments.

## Critiques and Reappraisals

- Multiregionalists attack total replacement, mtDNA certainty, and African primacy (ongoing).
- Stringer (2012) self-critique: 1980s struggle sometimes oversimplified multiregional views and data complexity; he once favored a rapid single-area African Garden of Eden.
- Strict 1993 minimal-gene-flow formulations revised by nuclear aDNA; he retains separate-species names pending evidence of *widespread* hybridization (as of Stringer 2012).
- Behavioural package still contested (gradual MSA vs late neural switch — [[klein-richard]]).
