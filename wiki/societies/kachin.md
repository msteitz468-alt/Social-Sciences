# Kachin

## Overview

In [[leach-edmund|Leach]]'s *[[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-1954|Political Systems of Highland Burma]]* (1954), **Kachin** names the hill populations of the Kachin Hills Area of northeast Burma (Myanmar), conventionally contrasted with valley [[shan|Shan]]. Leach insisted the category is **political and cultural**, not a fixed racial or tribal essence: language, dialect, and custom vary widely; people move between Kachin and Shan identification; kinship can override language for political affiliation (except across the Shan boundary).

**Documentation tense:** claims below are dated to Leach's sources and observation window (chiefly late 19th century–c. 1952 materials; Hpalang 1939–40), not "the present."

## Social Organization

As documented in Leach's synthesis:

- **Political poles:** hierarchical [[gumsa]] vs egalitarian [[gumlao]] ideals, with empirical communities as unstable compromises ([[gumsa-gumlao-oscillation]]).
- **Lineage and territory:** patrilineal *htinggaw*; segmentary surnames and clan names (~7–8 major aristocratic clans in myth — Marip, Lahtaw, Lahpai, Nhkum, Maran, etc.); territorial units *kahtawng* (village), *mare*, *mung*; dual territorial and lineage idioms of authority.
- **Affinity:** [[mayu-dama]] (wife-givers / wife-takers); preferred matrilateral ties; rank often mayu > dama under gumsa ideology.
- **Rank and class:** aristocrats, commoners, and *mayam* ("slaves"/bond dependents — App. III); mobility and residence strategies; ultimogeniture among chiefs as ideal (*uma du*).
- **Office:** *duwa* (chief) as ritual more than autocratic ruler; councils (*salang hpawng*); priests (*dumsa*), bards (*jaiwa*), diviners, mediums.
- **Hpalang 1939–40:** ~130 households / ~500 people in nine villages and six language groups; rival Maran and Atsi *duwa* claimants; feuds as political ammunition; festivals as status rivalry.

## Economy and Subsistence

Hill **taungya** (slash-and-burn) rice and associated crops; ecological zones (Leach's A/B/C) with different grassland/monsoon and terrace patterns; valley wet-rice generally Shan-associated and surplus-producing relative to hill deficiency. Trade and ritual wealth objects (*hpaga*); army service, market towns (e.g. Lweje near Hpalang), smuggling and frontier tolls in the colonial period. Terracing, where present, Leach treated as often military/political rather than purely economic.

## Belief and Ritual

Nats (spirits) ordered in relation to human rank — chiefs alone approach Madai in gumsa ideology; ritual action diagrams status; witchcraft discourse intersects mayu–dama. Leach redefined **ritual** as the communicative aspect of behaviour and **myth** as ritual's verbal counterpart used to justify faction — not a single coherent charter. Christian missions (Baptist, Catholic, etc.) created additional factional banners by the mid-20th century.

## Documentation History

- **Leach:** Hpalang 1939–40; Burma Army service and travel 1940–45; historical thesis 1946+; monograph 1954. Field notes lost.
- **Earlier corpus Leach reinterprets:** Hanson, Gilhodes, Carrapiett, Enriquez, Hertz, Kawlu Ma Nawng, Stevenson, colonial gazetteers and enquiry records, travellers from the 19th century.
- **Colonial production:** British administration preferred fixed gumsa chiefs; 1926 slave-release figures enter App. III; WWII reshaped access and destroyed records.

## Representation Debates

Whether gumsa/gumlao describe historical cycles or over-idealized models; whether "Kachin" over-unifies; reliability after note loss; Gluckman's "stable equilibrium" reading vs Leach's ideas/facts distinction. See [[leach-political-systems-highland-burma-study]], [[gumsa-gumlao-oscillation]], [[functionalism-and-social-change-debate]].

**From *Rethinking Anthropology* (1961):** Ordinary Jinghpaw vs Gauri vs [[lakher|Lakher]] as a controlled contrast set — same patrilineal *mayu*-type politics, different marriage institutions (indissoluble person-transfer vs intermediate vs hire-like bridewealth + easy divorce). Jinghpaw terminology as ideal [[ethnographic-algebra]]; Ch. 1 continuum pole opposite [[trobriand-islanders|Trobriand]] paternity metaphysics. Source: [[leach-rethinking-anthropology-1961]].

## Identity Cautions

Do not equate Kachin (political-cultural category) with a single language, genetic population, or archaeological culture. Jinghpaw, Atsi, Maru, Lashi, Nung and others appear as related but not identical. Boundary with [[shan]] is permeable by design in Leach's analysis — use `associated_with`, not identity. Lévi-Strauss (1949) confused Kachin with Haka Chin — Leach corrects (*Rethinking* Ch. 5).
