# Arsi Galla (Knutsson)

Rift Valley Arsi (Oromo/“Galla” in period language) analyzed by Karl Eric Knutsson in “Dichotomization and Integration” (*[[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-1969|Ethnic Groups and Boundaries]]*, 1969). **Documentation: mid-1960s southern Ethiopian materials in the 1969 symposium — not a full Oromo ethnography.**

## Overview

Knutsson situates Arsi relations with neighbours (Laki, highland Amhara/Shoa Galla, Jille, Sidamo, etc.) after Menelik’s conquest and the *pax amharica*. Processes include banned transhumance → farming, *gada* decline, Islamization, and pauperization for Rift Valley Arsi; neighbour contacts range from symbiosis to absolute frontier dichotomization to stratified multiethnic market villages (Macha).

## Social Organization

Dichotomization of ethnic groups can increase under competition (e.g. land with highlanders) even when cultural similarity is high (Jille frontier). Market villages show language rank, hypergamy, and strategies of Amharization vs Ethiopian-identity claims. Barth’s Introduction cites Knutsson for cases where **status change ≠ ethnic identity change** (Galla/Amhara).

## Economy and Subsistence

From pastoral/transhumant patterns toward agriculture under state constraints; market integration in multiethnic villages; ecological-zone settlement via lineage segments.

## Belief and Ritual

Islamization as part of post-conquest transformation; Orthodox church introduction in the wider Ethiopian frame; religion as possible diacritic (Knutsson contrasts Muslim diacritic transfer with Haaland’s Fur→Baggara path).

## Documentation History

Knutsson via Barth 1969; map/diagram of main ethnic groups and sub-groups in Ethiopia with dichotomization indicated (as printed in the essay).

## Representation Debates

“Galla” is a contested exonym; modern scholarship and self-identification prefer **Oromo**. Ethiopian imperial and later political histories reframe these materials.

## Identity Cautions

Arsi ≠ all Oromo; Knutsson’s essay is about **dichotomization and integration processes**, not a complete culture inventory. Do not merge linguistic, political, and genetic categories without attribution.
