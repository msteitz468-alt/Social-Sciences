# Coast Sámi (Eidheim's Coast Lapps)

Coastal Sámi communities of northern Norway analyzed by Harald Eidheim in “When Ethnic Identity is a Social Stigma” (*[[barth-ethnic-groups-boundaries-1969|Ethnic Groups and Boundaries]]*, 1969). **Documentation moment: 1960 West-Finnmark fjord communities as Eidheim described them — not a timeless ethnography of all Sámi.**

## Overview

Eidheim analyzes Lappish/Sámi identity as a **stigmatized** ethnic status (explicitly via Goffman 1963 [[stigma]]) managed through **public** vs **closed** interaction spheres, passing, and impression management. Local mutual classification: *finn* vs *dátza* (Lapp vs Norwegian). Field fjord: ~150 people with only about six Norwegians; administrative centre inverse proportions.

## Social Organization

Three spheres (Eidheim):

1. **Public sphere** — Norwegian-coded participants (school, shop, children); local Norwegian language; Lappish language and Lappish identity presentation excluded.
2. **Lappish closed sphere** — kin/neighbour and wider Lappish district network; Lappish as domestic and secret code outside the household.
3. **Norwegian closed sphere** — settings where Norwegian identity is displayed among those who can pass.

Public sphere: Lapp and Norwegian are **not** complementary ethnic statuses — Coast Lapps avoid behaviour pointing to Lappish identity. Code-switching when Norwegians enter; steamer micro-dynamics of public/closed alternation. Rare public ethnic solidarity incidents (e.g. bridge-building) reinforce caution afterward.

## Economy and Subsistence

Coastal mixed livelihoods in the Norwegian national economy; limited local resources; careers require Norwegian partners. Contrast with inland Finnmark (Lapp majority; reindeer minority; dairy farming bulk) where Lappish identity has more public organizational potential.

## Documentation History

Eidheim 1960 fieldwork; 1966 guest-relations article; 1969 symposium essay under Barth’s [[ethnic-boundary-theory|boundary]] framework. Barth’s Introduction flags the Coast Lapp case for minority/stigma analysis.

## Representation Debates

Sámi politics, language rights, and decolonization of “Lapp” terminology postdate and reframe this documentation. The minority-movement context is noted by Eidheim as largely external to the coastal closed sphere of his case.

## Identity Cautions

Coastal Sámi ≠ reindeer-herding inland Sámi as a single lifestyle package. Eidheim’s case is specifically **stigma in Norwegian coastal majority-coded public settings**. Visible “objective” cultural contrasts with Norwegians were weak; mother-tongue dichotomy required local valuation. Period term “Lapp” is the source’s language.
