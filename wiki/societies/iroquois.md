# Iroquois

## Overview

The **Haudenosaunee / Iroquois** confederacy nations of the northeastern woodlands appear in this wiki as the **type case** of (1) the gentile constitution in [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]] as reworked by [[engels-friedrich|Engels]] (*[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]*, ch. III) and (2) the **classificatory system of relationship** in Morgan’s *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity]]* (1871; Seneca-Iroquois as standard form). All social description below is **as of Morgan’s mid-nineteenth-century documentation** (and Engels’s 1884/1891 restatement), not the ethnographic present and not a full ethnohistory.

## Social Organization

As Engels reports Morgan (attributed, documentation ~1850s–1870s): society organized in matrilineal [[gens|gentes]] (Seneca example: eight gentes with enumerated rights — e.g. elect/depose sachem and chief, inheritance in gens, mutual aid, common burial ground, council); grouped into phratries and tribes; confederacy (League) with ongoing form into the late 17th c. in Engels’s sketch; exogamy of the gens; [[mother-right|mother-right]] descent and inheritance; pairing family easily terminable alongside classificatory kinship terminology that does not match the pairing household (brothers’ children called sons/daughters, etc.). No special public force of the [[the-state|state]] type — gentile organs in the midst of society, not above it.

## Economy and Subsistence

Horticulture (women’s farming in the classical ethnographic sketch), hunting, fishing — as cited in the evolutionary literature; not a quantified subsistence study here.

## Belief and Ritual

Common gentile cult/burial and ceremonial roles appear in Morgan’s trait list as reused by Engels; not independently verified in this ingest.

## Documentation History

Morgan’s personal and political relations with Seneca and other Haudenosaunee communities shaped *League of the Iroquois* (1851), Tonawanda schedule pretests, and *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-study|Systems]]* Part II (Seneca as classificatory type). Engels never did fieldwork; he treats Morgan as authority. Colonial situation of mid-19th-c. Iroquois nations is the production context of the ethnography.

## Representation Debates

Iroquois-as-model for primitive communism / gens democracy is a **reception construct** in Marxist and evolutionist literature. Contemporary Haudenosaunee scholarship and ethnohistory may contradict Morgan on specifics — not yet ingested; flag as gap. See [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].

## Identity Cautions

“Iroquois” collapses multiple nations (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, Seneca, and later Tuscarora) and centuries of change. Do not equate the Morgan–Engels type case with all Iroquoian-speaking peoples or with any single political present.

## Wolf 1982 ethnohistoric notes (fur trade)

In *[[wolf-europe-people-without-history-1982|Europe and the People Without History]]* (Ch. 1, Ch. 6), [[wolf-eric-r|Wolf]] uses Haudenosaunee / Iroquois involvement in the [[north-american-fur-trade|fur trade]] as a charter illustration that "people without history" isolation is false: European trade goods appear on the Niagara frontier by the 1570s; by the 1670s some Onondaga sites show almost no items of native manufacture except pipes (as Wolf synthesizes archaeological reports). Between 1670 and 1760, Wolf notes Iroquois demand for scarlet and blue Stroudwater cloth and links American trade to English industrializing valleys and Birmingham gunsmiths. Treat as **Wolf's 1982 synthesis**, not new excavation results; documentation dates remain Morgan-era for social organization sections above, contact-era for trade claims.
