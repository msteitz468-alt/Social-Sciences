# Andamanese (Great Andaman)

The peoples of the Andaman Islands in the Bay of Bengal as documented principally by [[radcliffe-brown-a-r|A. R. Radcliffe-Brown]] during fieldwork **1906–08** and published in *[[radcliffe-brown-andaman-islanders-1922|The Andaman Islanders]]* (1922), with substantial comparative use of E. H. Man's earlier South Andaman work. **Past tense of the documentation moment throughout** — not ethnographic present. Primary study: [[radcliffe-brown-andaman-islanders-study]].

## Overview

In the early twentieth century the Great Andamanese lived in small **local groups** (coast- or forest-specialized) loosely grouped into **linguistic tribes**, practicing hunting, gathering, fishing, and (coast) turtle and dugong hunting. British occupation (Penal Settlement at Port Blair from **1858**, after a brief 1789–96 settlement) and introduced disease had already drastically reduced population among groups in contact with the settlement. Radcliffe-Brown reconstructed pre-occupation organisation from residual communities and oral testimony; he estimated former total population ~**5,500** (c. 1858) vs. a few hundred "friendly" Great Andamanese in his field years.

## Social Organization

As Radcliffe-Brown reported for the reconstruction of pre-occupation / early-contact Great Andaman (fieldwork 1906–08):

- **Local group:** independent, autonomous community; land-owning / hunting-rights unit; camps of family huts; communal hut (*bud*) in some contexts; typical size often tens of persons.
- **Tribe:** loose aggregate of local groups sharing a language/name; weak regulatory role. Ten Great Andaman tribes (Jarawa set aside as Little Andaman division by language/culture). Neighbouring languages closely related; dialects within tribes common.
- **Coast (*Ar-yoto*) vs forest (*Erem-taga*):** subsistence-based division cutting across tribes (Aka-Bea terms). Coast: fish, turtle, canoes, also forest foods. Forest: pig, inland creeks; no turtle/dugong hunting.
- **Family:** man, wife, unmarried children (own or adopted); **no clans**. RB argued kinship terminology was not classificatory in the Morgan/Rivers sense.
- **Leadership:** no organised government; influence of older/respected persons and *oko-jumu* (wise men / medicine-men); RB disputed Man's stronger "chief" readings.
- **Conflict:** surprise warfare and feuds between neighbouring groups; peace-making ceremonies (aggression/passivity, weeping, weapon exchange); Aka-Bea–Jarawa feud as standing case; little knowledge of people living more than ~20 miles away.

## Economy and Subsistence

Foraging only — **no agriculture**. Forest products, wild pig (*Sus andamanensis*), roots and fruits; coast groups added reef fishing, turtle, dugong. Dogs for hunting after introduction post-1858. Iron cold-worked after wrecks/visits (known by 1771); no indigenous fire-making (fire kept continuously). Present-exchange and use-rights strong; RB described an "approach to communism" in property practice. Material culture (bows by region type, canoes/outrigger, coiled pottery, mats, ornaments) catalogued in Appendix A.

## Belief and Ritual

As documented 1906–08 and in Man's materials:

- **Initiation (*aka-op*):** sequential abstinence from chief foods; North Andaman boys' back scarification; turtle- and pig-eating ceremonies ending taboo phases; girls' first-menstruation rites and temporary flower-names.
- **Dance:** ordinary, initiation, mourning, war; inter-group dance meetings with food and presents.
- **Death and mourning:** burial, clay, name avoidance, bone recovery, ceremonial weeping on multiple occasions of attachment and separation.
- **Spirits and weather beings:** Biliku/Puluga and Tarai (and variants) associated with monsoons, storms, forbidden acts (e.g. beeswax, yams, cicada in RB's synthesis); spirits (*Lau* etc.); *oko-jumu* medicine-men; magic of protective objects (fire, fibres, bones, weapons).
- **Myths:** ancestral time; fire; flood/catastrophe; animal transformations; night/cicada; dispersion — variant-rich, not a single orthodox canon.

RB's Chs V–VI interpret these as expressions of **social sentiments** and **social values** maintaining cohesion — attributed theory, not native self-description as science.

**Production context:** Penal Settlement; Andamanese Homes (rations, medical attendance to induce friendly visits); "friendly" vs hostile (Jarawa) relations; use of Andamanese in convict tracking and trepang labour. **Depopulation (attributed to RB and his sources):** syphilis from ~1870 among contacted tribes; measles epidemic March 1877 (51 of 184 hospital cases fatal; Portman estimated half to two-thirds mortality for the Great Andaman); influenza and other introduced diseases; birth-rate collapse — RB in 1907 saw ≤12 children under five among ~500 persons; predicted possible Great Andaman extinction within ~50 years. Little Andaman less affected by settlement contact at that date.

## Representation Debates

- Type-case for "simple" [[social-structure]] and early functionalist ritual interpretation.
- Period **Negrito** racial classification and isolation/"pure culture" hypothesis in RB's Introduction — **period scientific position**, not wiki-voice identity or genetic fact.
- Man vs RB on Puluga as monotheistic "God," chieftainship, and continuous myth narratives.
- Later human-rights, Indigenous, and population-history literatures (not yet ingested) reframe colonial contact and survival of distinct groups (Great Andamanese residual communities, Onge, Jarawa, Sentinelese).

## Identity Cautions

"Andamanese" / "Andaman Islanders" is an **umbrella ethnographic label**. Great Andaman tribes, Little Andaman (Onge), Jarawa, and North Sentinelese are **not** one society. Radcliffe-Brown's monograph is primarily a **Great Andaman** reconstruction under colonial depopulation; Little Andaman and Jarawa coverage is thin. Do not equate the 1906–08 / reconstructed pre-occupation portrait with present populations without new documentation. Do not equate linguistic tribe with a single political nation or with a genetic population without attributed argument — use `associated_with`, not identity (four-way non-identity rule).
