# Open Ticket (Oregon matsutake camp)

## Overview

**Open Ticket** is [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt|Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing]]'s pseudonym for a composite commercial matsutake foraging and buying landscape in Oregon's Cascade Mountains, documented during matsutake seasons roughly 2004–2011 (with boom history from the late 1980s). It is a multi-ethnic seasonal community of pickers and buyers — including Hmong, Mien, Lao, and Khmer refugees, white veterans, Latinos, and others — working ruined industrial forests and selling into export chains toward Japan *(Tsing 2015)*.

Tsing constructs the site as a composite of an off-map early-2000s buying cluster plus characters from a nearby trading spot, to protect privacy *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Social Organization

After Forest Service rationalization of camping, pickers self-segregated ethnically in a grid camp: Mien then Hmong; half a mile away Lao then Khmer; whites in an isolated hollow — mimicking Thai refugee-camp structure *(Tsing 2015)*. Earlier free camping gave way to segregation after white litter complaints; the Service shunted pickers to a lonely access road and built a numbered grid with toilets and a distant water tank *(Tsing 2015)*.

Regulatory and spectral violence shape camp life: late-1990s asphyxiation deaths of three Hmong pickers left the campground haunted and emptied; an ambulance delayed by police-escort rules aimed only at the mushroom camp left a critically wounded picker dead — emergency access limited by discrimination, not terrain *(Tsing 2015)*.

Tsing prefers **assemblage** over coherent "culture" for Open Ticket: open-ended entanglement of ways of being in which varied trajectories gain a hold on each other and indeterminacy matters *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Economy and Subsistence

Commercial wild-mushroom foraging without wages or benefits; pickers sell what they find. Many reject "work"/wage labor — Lao picker Sai defines work as obeying a boss, while picking is "searching" for fortune *(Tsing 2015)*. **"Open ticket"** as buying practice: pickers may return the same night for the difference if prices rise after sale; buyers use it to entice early sales while competing for poundage commissions; prices roughly $2–$60/lb best grade (2004–2008), with 1993 spike memory at $600/lb *(Tsing 2015)*.

Evenings exchange not only mushrooms and money but dramatic enactments of **freedom** as pickers, buyers, and field agents separately understand it; money and mushrooms are trophies of the performance *(Tsing 2015)*. Buying competition is performative theater aimed at raising prices; sorting is public prowess (white men "juggling"; Lao women "Royal Lao dancing") *(Tsing 2015)*.

Tsing denies that Open Ticket picking/buying is itself a capitalist formation: money changes hands but does not form investment; accumulation is downstream among exporters/importers (Vancouver, Tokyo, Kobe) — a node of [[salvage-accumulation]] *(Tsing 2015)*. Pickers treat forest as extensive commons while land is national forest/private property, holding property in abeyance *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Belief and Ritual

Freedom is irregular, performative, and haunted — not economists' rational choice or political liberalism; "negotiation of ghosts on a haunted landscape" *(Tsing 2015)*. Most pickers/buyers link freedom commitments to the U.S.–Indochina War and subsequent civil wars; matsutake extends living survival of war *(Tsing 2015)*.

Ethnicized war and livelihood styles (Tsing 2015 findings):
- **White veterans** — forest as escape from crowds and flashbacks; frontier romanticism.
- **Khmer** — healing and recovery narratives layered on war trauma.
- **Hmong** — hunting and war landscapes; forest navigation; nostalgia risks (souls left in Laos).
- **Lao** — entrepreneurial sport, camouflage, buyer niches, karaoke/noodle tents; Buddhist renunciation variants among some.
- **Latino** pickers — often undocumented; hide from checks rather than sass the law (contrast with Lao refugee-citizen room for maneuver).
- **Mien** — reconstituting remembered village life in the matsutake forest.

Japanese Americans appear as a contrast group: leisure/gift matsutake rather than commercial refugee camps, indexing a different assimilation history *(Tsing 2015)*.

## Identity Cautions

Open Ticket is not a people; it is a seasonal commercial patch. Ethnic groups within it have separate histories (U.S.–Indochina War refugee trajectories, white veteran trajectories, Latino migrant labor circuits). Do not collapse them into a single culture or treat the pseudonym as a real place-name.
