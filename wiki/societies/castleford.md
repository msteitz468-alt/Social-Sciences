# Castleford

## Overview

*(As documented in [[smith-laurajane|Laurajane Smith]]'s early 2000s fieldwork, *[[smith-uses-of-heritage-2006|Uses of Heritage]]* 2006, Ch. 7 — past tense of documentation.)* **Castleford**, West Yorkshire, a small English town shaped by coal mining and other industries, undergoing deindustrialization after plant and pit closures and the aftermath of the **1984–85 miners' strike**. Smith studies how residents and the **Castleford Heritage Trust** use heritage — memory, festival, landscape markers, everyday networking — to assert community identity and political-economic legitimacy against external dismissals.

## Social Organization

*(As of Smith’s early 2000s research published 2006.)* Working-class community cohesion historically tied to coal mining and other industries; mass job losses and the aftermath of the **1984–85 miners’ strike** shook those ties. The **Castleford Heritage Trust (CHT)** and **Forum Project** organized collective remembering, networking, and agenda-setting. Festival and public history events created occasions for intergenerational teaching of work experience and solidarity. External officials could dismiss local heritage work (e.g. as “just a half dozen old ladies” — Drake, personal communication, as Smith reports).

## Economy and Subsistence

Industrial then **deindustrializing** local economy. Industrial buildings, pits, machinery, and high-density worker housing were torn down and redeveloped — “the slate, so to speak, was wiped clean” *(Smith)*. Heritage practice entangled with regeneration policy and claims that the town matters economically and culturally.

## Belief and Ritual

Not a religious ethnography. Commemorative and festive **performance** (festival; Forum Project); memory attached to landscape markers (e.g. the “black lump” coal tip in local talk); everyday practices such as shopping treated as community networking that daily “preserves” elements giving meaning to Castleford’s heritage *(Smith, attributed)*. Heritage here is change-oriented identity making, not cultural stasis.

## Documentation History

Qualitative engagement with Castleford Heritage Trust (named local collaborators in Smith’s acknowledgements). Single multi-chapter case in *Uses of Heritage* Ch. 7 — no restudy series in this vault yet.

## Representation Debates

The [[authorized-heritage-discourse|AHD]] “cannot readily recognize that Castleford has any ‘heritage’ left, as memory alone is untrustworthy without the material evidence” *(Smith)* — residents creating new heritage linked to existing memory reject this. Subaltern/community heritage vs professional dismissal (Pevsner-type “no heritage” judgments as Smith frames them). Cross-link labour heritage chapter (industrial museums), [[dissonant-heritage]], [[social-memory]], [[intangible-heritage]].
