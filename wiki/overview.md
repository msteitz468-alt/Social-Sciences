## [2026-07-09] Coverage note — Scheper-Hughes *Death Without Weeping* (1992)

Ingested the landmark critical-medical ethnography of hunger, sickness, and child death on the [[alto-do-cruzeiro|Alto do Cruzeiro]] shantytown, NE Brazil (fieldwork 1964–66; anthropological returns 1982–89). This is the vault's **first medical-anthropology primary source**, and it opens the subfield: new discipline page [[medical-anthropology]] (coverage **absent → weak**), research-program page [[critical-medical-anthropology]], and method page [[militant-anthropology]]. Concepts added — [[everyday-violence]], [[the-mindful-body]] (three bodies), [[nervoso]]/[[delirio-de-fome]] (medicalization of hunger), [[mortal-selective-neglect]], [[maternal-thinking]], [[political-economy-of-the-emotions]], [[paternalismo]]; subject-matter [[alto-do-cruzeiro]] and [[infant-mortality]]; thinker [[scheper-hughes-nancy]]; Studies Hub built. Two standing debates opened: [[mother-love-scarcity-debate]] (Scheper-Hughes vs. Nations & Rebhun) and [[militant-anthropology-debate]] (vs. D'Andrade). Kleinman's interpretive/explanatory-models program has since been added (*[[kleinman-illness-narratives-1988|The Illness Narratives]]*, 1988 — [[meaning-centered-medical-anthropology]], [[explanatory-models]], [[illness-disease-distinction]], [[illness-narrative]], [[somatization]], [[neurasthenia]], the [[explanatory-models-interview|mini-ethnography]]). Still absent in medical anthropology: Paul Farmer's structural violence, ethnomedicine, and the global-health literature. **Source-integrity note:** a mislabeled twin file (title metadata "Death without weeping" over the text of Brinton's *Women and the Economic Miracle*, Japan) was caught at intake and not ingested.
