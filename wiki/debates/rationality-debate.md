# Rationality Debate (Azande and "Primitive Thought")

## Dispute

Is the thought of peoples who maintain witchcraft, oracles, and magic **irrational**, **pre-logical**, or **rational within a different web of belief**? The [[azande|Azande]] of [[evans-pritchard-e-e|Evans-Pritchard]]'s *[[evans-pritchard-witchcraft-oracles-magic-1937|Witchcraft, Oracles and Magic]]* (1937) became the discipline's central empirical exhibit.

## Positions

### Evans-Pritchard (1937 ethnography)

Evans-Pritchard argued (attributed) that Zande [[witchcraft]] belief **does not contradict empirical knowledge of cause and effect**; the [[second-spear]] selects the socially relevant cause. Oracles and magic form a practical system for diagnosing and responding to misfortune. Apparent contradictions (failed oracles, hereditary logic vs individual accusation) are handled by further moves inside the system rather than by falsifying the whole.

### Intellectualist / "primitive mentality" foils

Earlier and concurrent traditions (Tylor–Frazer intellectualism; Lévy-Bruhl on mystical participation / pre-logical mentality) treated such systems as failed science or as differently constituted mentalities. E-P's monograph is a principal ethnographic counterweight — see also critiques of [[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion]] and [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]].

### Philosophical reception (post-1937; lightly sourced here)

Mid-to-late twentieth-century philosophy of social science used the Azande case to dispute whether rationality is universal (logic + evidence) or practice-immanent (Winch-style arguments and replies). **Those texts are not yet ingested**; this page records the debate's existence and anchors it to the primary ethnography without adjudicating philosophical sides.

## Shared Factual Ground (as of this ingest)

- E-P conducted late-1920s fieldwork and published 1937.
- The book carefully distinguishes witchcraft from sorcery and details [[poison-oracle|*benge*]] procedure.
- It is widely treated as founding modern anthropological respect for the internal coherence of African belief systems.

## Resolution Status

**Open** as a philosophical dispute; **ethnographically absorbed** as a teaching consensus that Azande practice is systematically coherent (with ongoing critique of colonial production and of overusing one case). Curator has not adjudicated Winch-era arguments.
