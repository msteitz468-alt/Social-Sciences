# Does Pre-Modern Anti-Judaism Lead Continuously to Modern Antisemitism and the Holocaust?

A standing dispute over how to periodize and connect Christian (and other) **[[anti-judaism]]**, modern racial/political **[[antisemitism]]**, and the Holocaust. Positions are recorded in their strongest form; the wiki does not adjudicate.

## Position 1 — Long continuity / teleology

Some popular and older scholarly narratives treat New Testament and patristic hostility as the root that grows, more or less continuously, into medieval persecution, modern racial antisemitism, and genocide. On this view, Goebbels’s “Jewish intellectualism” is essentially the same structure as gospel attacks on Pharisees. Nirenberg cites such juxtapositions as **provocations**, not proofs, and explicitly declines (Ch. 7, attributed) to cast Luther as architect of the Holocaust by anachronism.

## Position 2 — Radical discontinuity

Modern antisemitism is a **new** formation: racial science, nationalism, mass politics, and the modern state create something discontinuous with theological anti-Judaism. Emancipation and secularization, not medieval teaching alone, produce the distinctive modern [[jewish-question]]. Cutting the past from the present is necessary to avoid anachronism and special pleading. (Nirenberg treats pure “liberty”/cutting as having costs — blindness to continuity and to asymmetries of power.)

## Position 3 — Mask with transformation (Nirenberg)

[[nirenberg-david|Nirenberg]] (2013, attributed) rejects both heavy causal bridges and fantasies of freedom from the past. [[anti-judaism]] is a **pedagogical mask**: enduring forms that impress themselves on memory while the concepts behind the mask transform. Gospel figures of Jewish “empty wisdom” and Nazi book-burning rhetoric may be *related* without being *implied* or *caused* in a simple chain.

**Load-bearing formula (Ch. 13, attributed):**

- The West’s long history of Jewish questions **did not cause** the Final Solution and did not make it inevitable (slight variable changes imaginable).
- The Holocaust was nevertheless **inconceivable without** that history.
- Not a German *Sonderweg* of unique determination — habits widespread in Europe and the US — even though common history **cannot by itself explain** why Germany enacted genocide.

**Method:** genealogical attention to formal continuity **and** ceaseless change (Nietzsche masks; Foucault genealogy). Epilogue: past contributes **possibility** without responsibility, causality, or necessity (e.g. not claiming gospels caused genocide or that Paul is responsible for futures of “letter kills”). Risk of forgetting continuities is as real as inventing them.

## Position 4 — Myth-warrant (Cohn)

[[cohn-norman|Norman Cohn]] (*[[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-1967|Warrant for Genocide]]*, 1967, attributed) focuses the genocidal strand on the **[[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]]** and the *[[protocols-of-the-elders-of-zion|Protocols]]*: a quasi-demonological warrant that, under war/revolution/slump, prepared massacre. Continuity is strongest in the subterranean mythic underworld, not in high philosophy alone. Complementary to Nirenberg’s high-cultural thought-tools axis.

## Position 5 — Modernity / bureaucracy (Bauman)

[[bauman-zygmunt|Zygmunt Bauman]] (*[[bauman-modernity-holocaust-1989|Modernity and the Holocaust]]*, 1989, attributed) relocates explanation in modern organization, science, and the [[gardening-state]] — complementary to ideology/myth axes; full treatment on [[holocaust-and-modernity-debate]].

## Shared Factual Ground, Framed Per Side

Parties generally accept: long Christian (and other) corpora of hostile or instrumental Jewish figures; a distinctive modern racial and political antisemitism; the historical fact of the Holocaust. They divide over **which past is load-bearing**, whether intellectual history can span millennia without teleology, and whether genocide requires myth, modern organization, national culture, or some combination.

Nirenberg’s additional shared-ground claim (attributed): even “critical” modern thought (Marx, neo-Kantian fights, university disciplines) can **collude** with anti-Judaism’s cognitive claims — so the problem is not only popular prejudice.
