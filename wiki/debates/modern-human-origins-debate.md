# Modern Human Origins Debate

## Shared Factual Ground

Fossil *Homo* is documented outside Africa by the Early Pleistocene; anatomically modern *Homo sapiens* fossils appear first in Africa; Middle–Upper Paleolithic behavioral change is regionally uneven; non-African living humans carry some archaic admixture (as of aDNA literature after the 2007 textbook — date-stamp claims).

## Position: Recent African Origin (Out of Africa)

Anatomically modern humans originated in Africa and dispersed, largely replacing archaic populations elsewhere (with later recognition of limited interbreeding). Emphasizes African fossil moderns and genetic coalescence patterns (Stringer and others — attributed).

## Position: Multiregional Evolution

Modern traits evolved in multiple regions from local archaic populations connected by gene flow, so regional continuity is expected in the fossil record (Wolpoff and others — attributed).

## Position: Assimilation / Hybrid Models

African origin of modern anatomy with geographically variable archaic contribution short of full multiregional equality (various authors — attributed).

## Evidence Classes and Sticking Points

Fossils vs. archaeology vs. genetics answer different questions; the 2007 textbook surveys models before the full aDNA revolution (as of Wenke & Olszewski 2007) — **do not treat that edition as current genetic consensus**.

## Wenke & Olszewski — *Patterns in Prehistory* (2007)

Full teaching survey of the debate as of mid-2000s textbook consensus (attribute; **do not treat as current genetic consensus**). Source: [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007]].

### Model structure (as of W&O 2007)

W&O present two basic competitors: (1) **African Origins / Total Replacement / Noah’s Ark / Eve** — modern humans evolve first only in Africa within the last few hundred thousand years, then replace other hominins worldwide with little/no genetic interchange (majority confidence as of their writing); (2) **Continuity / Multiregional Evolution / Candelabra** — archaic populations outside Africa also contribute via regional continuity plus gene flow under shared hunter-forager selection, converging as one species by ~30 kya with regional physical differences (minority) (attributed, chs. 2 and 4). Multiregional models face the problem that few nonhuman species evolve convergently over a wide range; proposed answer is shared cultural niche plus gene flow (estimates as low as one migrant/generation), which many still doubt can produce simultaneous similar *H. s. sapiens* from N China to S Africa (attributed). Historiography noted: Weidenreich 1930s multiregional vs Leakey African-origins framing (attributed).

Five testable implications of Total Replacement (adapted from Frayer et al. "Paleontological Test," via W&O): earliest *H. s. sapiens* only in Africa first; no anatomical genetic-mixing intermediates outside Africa ~400–30 kya; earliest moderns outside Africa resemble African ancestors not locals, plus rapid replacement signal; no anatomical continuity across replacement horizon (e.g. Zhoukoudian 300 kya → 50 kya → today); some visible advantage explaining complete displacement (attributed). W&O stress all five are equivocal in practice and end their review **inconclusively**: reasonable scientists differ; hypotheses cannot be proven — only not disproven; "best" model = fit + parsimony (attributed). Growing consensus (as of 2007) that many pre-~300 ka hominins were not direct genetic ancestors of *Homo sapiens* is noted while leaving room for radical revision (attributed, ch. 3).

### Genetic evidence as of 2007 (superseded horizons flagged)

Cann, Stoneking & Wilson mtDNA from 147 placentas concluded all living people descend from an African "mitochondrial Eve" ~140–290 kya (probably ~200 kya), with Africa identified by greatest living mtDNA variation (attributed via W&O). Critics (Frayer et al.; Melnick & Hoelzer) attack generation-time estimates, molecular-clock constancy, and substitution rates; Frayer et al. hold there is "no statistically reliable mtDNA evidence for an African, or any other single, geographically centered origin for Eve"; pushing the clock start to ~800 kya would better fit fossils (attributed).

**Critical 2007-horizon flag:** W&O present early Neandertal mtDNA studies (Krings, Ovchinnikov, Caramelli et al.) showing >20 mtDNA differences from modern humans as strong support for Out of Africa — "Neandertals, and likely most other archaic *Homo* forms outside of Africa, did not contribute to the gene pool that constitutes us" (as of Wenke & Olszewski 2007). Later nuclear aDNA demonstrated limited Neandertal/Denisovan admixture (as of post-2010 aDNA literature); **do not promote the textbook's "no contribution" claim to wiki voice.** Even in 2007 W&O still recorded three camps: no significant Neandertal contribution; European admixture of African + Neandertal genes; regional evolution of Neandertals into modern Europeans with genic exchange (as of Wenke & Olszewski 2007).

### Fossils and archaeology (as of W&O 2007)

Herto (Middle Awash, Ethiopia; Tim White et al.), ~160–154 kya, crania intermediate between African archaic *Homo* and anatomically modern humans — offered as strong African-ancestry evidence (attributed). Klasies River Mouth (South Africa) remains with MSA tools ~125–95 kya: scholars disagree on modernity of fragmentary facial morphology (attributed). Other African candidates (Bodo, Omo, Laetoli, Kanjera, Kabwe, Border Cave, etc.) are each considered by some poorly dated or insufficiently modern; others see continuous African modernization ~200–100 kya nearly complete by ~70 kya (attributed). Richard Klein proposes the humans who actually replaced all others appeared in Africa only after ~50 kya via a genetic mutation enabling fully modern culture/language — so earlier "modern-looking" fossils could themselves have been replaced (attributed; anatomical ≠ behavioral modernity). Brooks et al. barbed bone points at Katanda (Zaire) as early as ~90 kya — cognitive superiority vs non-Africans still ambiguous per W&O (attributed).

W&O Africa summary: suggestions of archaic→sapiens transition ~160 kya, but not certain Africa is unique locus; material evidence of a decisive replacement advantage remains lacking (attributed). Australia occupied by at least 50 kya and possibly 60 kya — implications for replacement timing depend on whether total replacement began ~200 kya or after 50 kya (attributed). Multiregional advocates (Pope, Wolpoff, Thorne, Frayer et al.) see strong early Australian–Javanese similarity and shovel-shaped-incisor continuity in China (Zhoukoudian, Dali, etc.) as regional continuity (attributed). Zhoukoudian Chinese *erectus* shovel-shaped upper incisors used by some as continuity to modern Mongoloid populations (Wu & Jia via W&O — position) (attributed).

## Stringer & Gamble — *In Search of the Neanderthals* (1993)

Full mid-1990s book-length statement of the **replacement / Out of Africa 2** position, co-authored by [[stringer-chris|Chris Stringer]] (fossils/genetics) and [[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]] (archaeology). Source: [[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993]]. **All claims below are positions as of 1993** — pre-nuclear aDNA; genetic absolute calibrations and many TL/ESR dates are historical horizon, not current consensus. No adjudication in wiki voice.

### Framing (as of S&G 1993)

S&G divide later Pleistocene humans into **Ancients** (Neanderthals and anatomically pre-modern neighbours) and **Moderns** (anatomically modern *Homo sapiens*, including European Cro-Magnons), stressing that modern anatomy is **not** synonymous with modern behaviour — Moderns "literally behaved like Neanderthals for very long periods" in some regions (attributed). Two camps:

1. **Population replacement / Out of Africa 2** — anatomically modern *H. sapiens* evolve in Africa and disperse (sometime between ~130 and 50 ka in their terminology), replacing Ancients including Neanderthals; Neanderthals are an evolutionary dead end relative to living Europeans (Stringer–Andrews program and allies; Howells "Noah's Ark" framing).
2. **Regional continuity / multiregional evolution** — modern humans evolve semi-independently regionally from Ancients with continual gene flow; Neanderthals evolve into modern Europeans (Weidenreich → Coon / Brace / Wolpoff–Thorne lineage as S&G present it).

S&G hold that scientific debate over Neanderthal "insider vs outsider" status cannot be divorced from contemporary opinions about identity, heritage, and classical notions such as Aristotle's "natural slave" (attributed reflexivity). Terminology: **Out of Africa 1** = *Homo erectus* dispersal ~1 Ma; **Out of Africa 2** = modern dispersal.

### Position A — Replacement (Stringer & Gamble, strongest form)

As of S&G 1993, the replacement case is argued from four interlocking lines:

**Phylogeny and isolation.** Speciation via geographic isolation (allopatry, Mayr) is implicated in Far Eastern *erectus* vs western "archaic *H. sapiens*" and in the subsequent north–south divergence producing Neanderthals (Europe) vs Moderns (Africa). Some Neanderthal specializations are cold-adaptation and strength selection; African origin of modern morphology is linked (uncertainly) to behavioural changes reducing skeletal robusticity — language? planning depth? technology? (attributed). African middle Pleistocene Ancients (Bodo/Broken Hill grade) group with European Mauer/Petralona/Arago as *Homo heidelbergensis*, ancestral to both northern Neanderthals and African moderns, with the split under way by ~200 ka (Ehringsdorf) or earlier (attributed).

**African fossil sequence.** Africa has the strongest claim as modern homeland. Sequence as of 1993: Bodo/Broken Hill → Ngaloba/Irhoud transitional/"borderline Moderns" → Klasies River Mouth, Border Cave (contested provenience), Omo Kibish 1; consistent modern package (shorter rounder skull, smaller brow/face, chin, lighter build) only from ~100 ka; MSA context (= European Middle Palaeolithic). Early African Moderns and Levantine Skhul/Qafzeh "looked like us… but they were not us" — lacking ~100 ka of subsequent change (attributed). China (Dali/Maba) lacks African-style borderlines; Zhoukoudian Upper Cave ~25 ka looks more Cro-Magnon-like than intermediate between ancient and modern Chinese (attributed, against simple East Asian continuity).

**Levant chronology as critical test.** TL/ESR (as of early 1990s) put Qafzeh early moderns ~92 ka (TL) / 120–100 ka (ESR) — ~3× older than European Cro-Magnons and older than Kebara Neanderthal; Skhul early moderns also deep; Amud Neanderthals 50–40 ka; Tabun sequence 200–90 ka with Tabun C woman ~110 ka roughly coeval with or slightly before Qafzeh. Three explanatory options S&G record: (1) overlap zone of European Neanderthal and African modern lineages 120–40 ka without proven contemporaneity; (2) moderns first (≥100 ka), cold-adapted Neanderthals arriving ~70 ka possibly *displacing* early moderns (Zuttiyeh → Skhul/Qafzeh → Tabun/Kebara/Amud sequence); (3) single variable regional *H. sapiens* with Qafzeh and Kebara as extremes of continuous variation (multiregional-compatible). S&G favour the corridor/overlap reading against western Europe as a Neanderthal cul-de-sac; Middle East as springboard for later global dispersals (Australia 60–50 ka, western Pacific ≥32 ka, Americas 30–13 ka, Europe after ~40 ka) (attributed).

**Genetics (1993 horizon — superseded calibrations flagged).** Cann, Stoneking & Wilson 1987 mtDNA tree: African "Eve"/lucky mother ~200 ka, dispersal ~100 ka, greatest African diversity; earlier Cann–Wilson ~400 ka calibration had been multiregional-compatible. Multiregionalists attack rate constancy, chimp calibration, and PAUP's inability to prove Africa conclusively; revised calibrations ~140–130 ka (range 400–60 ka). Nuclear DNA / Cavalli-Sforza hierarchical clustering: primary African/non-African split, secondary Eurasian/SE Asian; ancestral African population ~100 ka (aligning Nei & Roychoudhury 1974). Within-region diversity clocks (Australia/NG ~40%, Americas ~20% of total variation) used to argue all living variation could arise within ~150 ka — consistent with Out of Africa 2 not multiregional ≥1 Ma origin (attributed). S&G note mtDNA has been used **very controversially** for *complete* replacement; nuclear DNA less definitive on intermixture. **Do not promote 1993 "no contribution" genetics to wiki voice** — post-2010 nuclear aDNA demonstrates limited Neanderthal/Denisovan admixture.

**Europe 40–30 ka and extinction mechanism.** Encounter between long-established Neanderthals and incoming fully modern, technologically ahead Moderns; Cro-Magnons not derived from Neanderthals (separate ≥200 ka trajectory away from Cro-Magnons in face, nose, body proportions). Occipital bulge and mandibular foramen in some Cro-Magnons do not prove Neanderthal ancestry (features also outside Neanderthal zone). Occasional morphological "hybrid" hints treated as exceptions; gene flow **minimal** (as of S&G 1993). Vindija late less-robust Neanderthals too incomplete/late for multiregional intermediate claims; Vindija–Aurignacian association excavation-uncertain. Châtelperronian and other "transitional" industries: imitation of incoming Modern technology, not independent Neanderthal invention of Upper Palaeolithic (Saint-Césaire TL ~36.3 ka; structures only after Moderns arrive elsewhere). Behavioural differences "completely different order of complexity"; replacement the only plausible explanation given absence of gradual change from one way of life to the other (attributed). Global colonization after ~40 ka too rapid and extensive for continuity models reasonably to explain (attributed). Economic competition and marginalization: Cro-Magnons expand into favourable lowlands; Neanderthals pushed to higher/less-sheltered ground → higher infant mortality, shorter lifespans; Zubrow simulation — 2% higher Neanderthal mortality → extinction ~1,000 years. Stringer: "whimper rather than a bang" — gradual displacement, **not** genocide or colonial clash (attributed; reply to Wells/Quennells imagery and to Wolpoff's "Killer Africans" / "story of Cain" framing).

**Behaviour and symbolism.** Modern anatomy and modern behaviour need not share a single centre; source of modern behaviour "less certain" than African anatomical origin (time-lag Omo ~130 ka → symbolic explosion after 40 ka). Symbolism as all-or-nothing "switch"/Pfeiffer "creative explosion," not Clark & Lindly dimmer-switch gradual accrual. Neanderthals: capacity for emulation/change (Châtelperronian, Arcy structures) but not full symbolism — "nests" vs Modern "homes"; "tool-assisted hominids" without symbolically organized culture as S&G define it (attributed). Modern behaviour spreads "rather like a virus" on pre-existing modern anatomy (~70 ka lag), then host expands 60–40 ka — evidence of replacement not continuity as mechanism of the "human revolution" (attributed).

**Book conclusion (position).** Population replacement is "the most plausible interpretation of the archaeological and anthropological record of the later Pleistocene." Africa alone can support a multiregional-style "Neanderthal phase" in modern ancestry; elsewhere the trail leads to Africa as single continental centre for physical origins (attributed). Difference without Boule/Wells moral condemnation: Neanderthals "as human as us, but… a different brand of humanity"; nothing inevitable about Modern triumph (attributed).

### Position B — Multiregional evolution (as reported by S&G 1993; strongest form)

Recorded here in strongest form from S&G's foil presentation plus named multiregional claims they quote — **not** as S&G's own view. Primary Wolpoff/Thorne texts not yet ingested.

- **Core model:** Continuity in time and space from *Homo erectus* Out of Africa 1; isolation never sufficient for allopatric speciation; gene flow among all Pleistocene human populations; only one species (*H. sapiens*) for ~1 Ma; regional lineages respond to universal forces while local selection maintains features (e.g. Neanderthal large noses → modern Europeans; Javanese *erectus* cheek bones → modern Australians). Gene flow counters local specialization ("centre and edge," Thorne & Wolpoff) — even the suggestion that only *H. sapiens* has existed since the early Ice Age (attributed via S&G).
- **Historiography of the program (as of S&G):** de Mortillet, Schwalbe, Gorjanović-Kramberger, Hrdlička, Weidenreich 1943 world-wide modern-race model from Zhoukoudian → Coon *Origin of Races* (1962; accused of racism for staggered *erectus*→*sapiens* transitions) → Brace 1964 "fate of the Classic Neanderthals" against Boule-style expulsion/"catastrophism" and "culinary revolution" (tool-driven reduction of front-tooth/face selection) → Wolpoff–Thorne multiregional evolution (attributed).
- **Neanderthal face as intermediate:** Continuity favourers read Neanderthal midface not as specialized dead end but as logical intermediate between big projecting archaic faces and small tucked modern European faces via two-stage reduction (attributed). S&G reject this, arguing overall facial reduction under the braincase produced all modern faces, which then *supplanted* Neanderthals (replacement reply).
- **Australia dual-origin (Thorne–Wolpoff):** Gracile eastern island-hopping Asian route vs robust Ngandong/Java western route retaining "mark of ancient Java" frontal flattening; Peter Brown's alternative is late Pleistocene *in situ* diversification; bulk of Australian genetic data (as of 1993) supports one basic origin (S&G synthesis of the dispute).
- **Genetics critique:** mtDNA rate not constant; pattern could fit 1 Ma *erectus* spread as well as recent moderns; PAUP cannot prove African origin from then-available data (multiregionalists via S&G).
- **Wolpoff rhetorical critique of replacement:** Continuity with long contact and cooperation is "a more satisfying interpretation… than a scientific rendering of the story of Cain" based on one population quickly, completely, and most likely *violently* replacing all others — "making war and not love"; implications "not pleasant" (Wolpoff quoted by S&G). Popular continuity image: Wolpoff "sees a Neanderthal every morning in his shaving mirror" (attributed via S&G). Limb-proportion differences attributed by Wolpoff to longer-distance walking/running and social-network change — S&G reject, citing West Turkana boy (1.6 Ma) and Skhul/Qafzeh long-legged physiques in MP context as climatic adaptation instead.
- **1990 AAAS clash:** Continuity accuses replacement of "justifying extinction"; replacement criticizes continuity for treating racial differences as original and historical; Wolpoff reported saying no one who understood fossils could accept the African Eve framing (Independent 19 Feb 1990 via S&G).

S&G's own methodological jab at multiregional gene-flow flexibility: "Let the genes do the walking, if the bones won't play" — a theory "with such supple methods is obviously very difficult to test or disprove" (S&G critique, attributed).

### Historiographic layer in S&G 1993 (not a third living camp)

**Boule–Vallois "presapiens":** Modern features had long gestation; Neanderthals a relict from an ancient common ancestor (≥1 Ma); Cro-Magnons from coexisting gracile European ancestors — Europe remains central while Neanderthals are replaced (historical position via S&G; see [[boule-marcellin]]). Distinct from both living camps. McCown & Keith Mount Carmel "progressive Neanderthal" clinal radiation is another historical reading overturned (as of S&G) by TL/ESR reordering.

### S&G reply to moral/political framing

S&G reject winners/losers and colonial metaphors; urge broadening definitions of humanity; Neanderthals "not us" but their African kin provided the basis from which we sprang; investigate the "curtain" between Neanderthals and Moderns as cultural patterning, not moral hierarchy (closing manifesto, attributed).

## Gamble — *Timewalkers* (1994): Gardeners vs Sons of Noah

[[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s ch. 8 frames the same split in rhetorical labels: **"Gardeners"** (single recent origin / "Out of Africa 2" / "Garden of Eden") favoring **replacement** of Ancients by Moderns from an already-modern source (often Africa), versus **"Sons of Noah"** (regional continuity / multiregional evolution / Howells's "neanderthal phase") tracing moderns more or less independently from regional *Homo erectus* founders with gene flow (attributed, *[[gamble-timewalkers-1994|Timewalkers]]* 1994). Gamble self-identifies with Gardeners/replacement, but defines replacement as competitive/behavioral (e.g. language advantages), "often in the nicest possible way," not necessarily violent extermination (attributed). Multiregional genealogy traced to Weidenreich; Coon's extreme independent-race form repudiated (attributed). Behavioral package for Moderns/Pioneers (Table 8.2): planning depth, art/ornament after ~40 ka, social-scale/alliance revolution enabling later [[global-colonization|colonization]] of marginal lands — interpreted through [[exaptation]], not teleological design to replace Neanderthals (attributed). **Genetics as of 1994** (mtDNA "Eve," low diversity, Cavalli-Sforza trees) are historical snapshot only — **do not promote over post-2010 nuclear aDNA**. Book-length prior statement: [[stringer-gamble-in-search-of-the-neanderthals-1993|Stringer & Gamble 1993]] (section above).

## Klein — *The Human Career* (2nd ed., 1999): "Out of Africa 2" and the behavioral turn

Richard Klein's synthesis is a leading statement of the **replacement** position, but with a
distinctive twist that shifts the debate from *anatomy* to *behavior* (all attributed, as of
Klein 1999; note the edition predates the nuclear-aDNA admixture results):

- **Terminology.** Klein names the recent-African-origin/replacement model **"Out of Africa
  2,"** to distinguish it from **"Out of Africa 1,"** the earlier ~1 Ma dispersal of *Homo
  erectus*/*ergaster*. He judges the fossil evidence to favor Out of Africa 2 over
  multiregionalism while conceding "the multiregional model cannot be dismissed."
- **Anatomy preceded behavior.** Klein's signature move: anatomically modern or near-modern
  people existed in Africa (and its SW-Asian margin — Skhul/Qafzeh, ~120–80 ka) by ~100 ka,
  but were **behaviorally** little different from the Neanderthals until ~50–40 ka. Only then
  did fully modern behavior (art, formal bone tools, exchange, structured sites) appear — a
  change Klein attributes, tentatively, to a **neural/genetic transformation** enabling fully
  modern cognition and language (see [[behavioral-modernity]]).
- **The jury-trial verdict.** "If the study of human origins were a jury trial, the verdict
  would surely be that modern humans, originating in Africa, exterminated the Neanderthals";
  he predicts this verdict "will not be reversed," while the East Asian case is weaker ("the
  jury might hang"). Demographically, a mere 1–2% rise in Neanderthal mortality would have
  extinguished them within ~1,000 years.
- **The Levant reversal.** Klein reads the Skhul/Qafzeh near-moderns (~120–80 ka) as
  *predating* the Levantine Neanderthals of Kebara/Amud (~65–47 ka), implying Neanderthals
  temporarily **displaced** early moderns in southwest Asia when Last-Glacial climate
  deteriorated — reversing the European "Neanderthals then moderns" sequence.
- **Standing problems Klein concedes.** (1) Why did modern behavior appear so abruptly at
  ~50 ka rather than gradually? (2) The **Chatelperronian** (Neanderthal-made yet carrying
  Upper-Paleolithic bone tools and ornaments at Saint-Césaire and Arcy-sur-Cure) — Klein
  reads it as acculturation/diffusion from Aurignacian moderns, but calls it "a major puzzle
  whose solution is important for closure on Out of Africa 2." (3) The **Katanda** barbed
  bone points (Semliki, ~90+ ka) are "perhaps the most serious obstacle" to his own scenario,
  since they would put modern behavior and modern anatomy together, early, in Africa.

Klein's position is thus a *behavioral* replacement model: the decisive African advantage was
cognitive, not anatomical. Critics (the MSA-gradualist camp; see [[behavioral-modernity]])
reject the abrupt ~50-ka threshold and the single-mutation mechanism. Source:
[[klein-human-career-1999]].

## Stringer — *Lone Survivors* (2012)

*Source: [[stringer-lone-survivors-2012|Chris Stringer, *Lone Survivors* (2012)]]. Full position layer as of 2012 — early nuclear aDNA horizon (Neanderthal draft genome; Denisovans). No adjudication in wiki voice. Companion layers: 1993 S&G (above), Klein 1999, Wenke 2007.*

### Continuum of models (as of Stringer 2012)

Stringer refuses a binary RAO vs Multiregional frame. He maps a **continuum**:

| Pole | Model (as he labels it) | Non-African contribution (his sketch) |
|---|---|---|
| Left | Pure Recent African Origin | Total replacement of non-African genes |
| Near left | Bräuer Out of Africa + Hybridization / “mostly Out of Africa” | Modest archaic input (<~10%) |
| Center | Smith–Trinkaus Assimilation model | Up to ~50% if archaic gene flow is the rule |
| Right | Classic Multiregionalism | Africa no special role; regional continuity |

His own stance (as of 2012): near Bräuer hybridization or **modest Assimilation**, still labeled **RAO** — “mostly Out of Africa.” Pure RAO was reinforced by fossils, archaeology, mtDNA/Y-DNA, early Neanderthal mtDNA; autosomal Neanderthal/Denisovan data **halted and partly reversed** the pure-replacement pendulum (attributed). RAO is also about shared modern **behavior**, not only morphology/genes; **modernity is a composite** assembled at different times and places (attributed). He revises single-area African “Garden of Eden” gradualism toward **origins (plural)** inside Africa (attributed).

### Analytical distinction: shared modern vs regional traits

Two origins must be separated (as of Stringer 2012): (1) shared physical modern traits (slender skeleton, higher/rounder braincase, smaller brows, chin); (2) regional/“racial” traits — with different origins. Models he set out to test ~1970: global Multiregional (+ Braceian), Early Neanderthal (Howell), Pre-sapiens (Boule–Keith), Campbell & Weiner Spectrum Hypothesis. Post-mtDNA landscape adds Assimilation (Smith, Trinkaus) between RAO and classic Multiregionalism (attributed).

### Multiregional position as he reports and critiques it

Weidenreich (1930s) → Coon five lineages → Brace neanderthaloid phase → Wolpoff/Thorne/Wu 1984 single-species network (see [[multiregional-evolution]]). Wolpoff et al. 1994 (quoted): earliest moderns not Africans; no specific African admixture/replacement; continuous regional skeletal feature complexes. Stringer’s position (2012): those classic claims have been “pretty comprehensively shown to be false”; modest (~5%) archaic genome does **not** restore Multiregionalism (attributed). **Saint-Césaire** Neanderthal + Châtelperronian: once Neanderthal nature accepted, supported Replacement in W Europe against continuity hopes (Wolpoff transitional counter-claim recorded) (as of Stringer 2012). **Mungo 3** Australian aDNA claims (2001; Thorne among authors) for Multiregional/Assimilation rejected on recovery-rate, protocol, and reanalysis grounds (Stringer, Cooper, Collins) (as of Stringer 2012). **[[homo-floresiensis]]** single-species Multiregional resistance vs pathology hypotheses recorded as damaging reputation-seeking (Stringer position).

### aDNA horizon (as of Stringer 2012)

- **Neanderthal genome:** draft composite (largely Vindija; also El Sidrón) confirms Out of Africa and Neanderthal distinctiveness; separation ~350 ka; non-African genomes (Europe, China, New Guinea) slightly closer to Neanderthal than African ones → estimated ~**2%** Neanderthal genetic input overall; Yotova et al. up to ~9% Neanderthal-derived DNA at one non-African X-chromosome locus (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Timing/place of admixture:** not necessarily late European contact — Stringer favors earlier/warmer (possibly Levant ~120 ka Skhul-Qafzeh/Tabun era); advantageous light-skin genes apparently did **not** transfer from European Neanderthals; early Cro-Magnon linear physiques argue against heavy cold-adapted European admixture (attributed). Currat & Excoffier model ~2% as a **limit** on hybridization effectiveness across separate events (via Stringer).
- **Denisovans:** mtDNA more divergent than Neanderthal–modern; nuclear DNA closer to Neanderthals; Melanesians ~5% Denisovan (+ earlier small Neanderthal component → ~8% total archaic in New Guinea/Bougainville estimates) (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Serial founders:** diversity declines with distance from Africa challenges pan-assimilation; Melanesia hotspot is the exception (Long et al.) (as of Stringer 2012).
- **Within Africa:** Hammer et al. ~2% archaic autosomal ancestry in some sub-Saharan samples, introgressed ~35 ka from unknown African archaic possibly separate ~700 ka (via Stringer 2012).
- **Morphology vs genome:** Stringer rejects Trinkaus’s reading of Neanderthal input in most earliest European moderns (e.g. Lapedo child); prefers ancestral modern variation/individual overlap pending better early modern samples from N Africa/Asia ~50 ka (attributed).
- **Species naming:** hybridization does not yet force lumping all later Pleistocene humans as one *H. sapiens*; morphological species differences still justify separate names (as of Stringer 2012). Clifford Jolly baboon analogy: concentrate on biology, avoid semantic traps (via Stringer).

### Behavioral modernity debates (Klein vs McBrearty–Brooks; Mellars)

Core behavioral axes (as of Stringer 2012):

1. **~35 ka European “Human Revolution”** (1987 Cambridge conference co-organized by Stringer) — later criticized as Eurocentric.
2. **Klein:** African mutations ~50 ka enhanced cognition/language; morphological modernity first, behavioral later; pre-50 ka “modern” evidence unconvincing (attributed via Stringer). Stringer **disagrees** with a unique genetic “switch” but agrees language was critical; Omo Kibish/[[herto]] frontal/parietal shape already ~100 ka earlier than Klein’s threshold (as of Stringer 2012).
3. **Mellars:** accelerated African change ~60–80 ka (blades, microliths, bone tools, beads, engraved ochre, marine fish, etc.); neurological switch + climatic fluctuations (possibly Toba) (via Stringer).
4. **McBrearty & Brooks:** gradual early assembly of modern package across African MSA ~100–250 ka; “eureka moment” quest Eurocentric (via Stringer).

Stringer (as of 2012): most checklist attributes of behavioral modernity present by ~60 ka, not always as a package; African signals intermittent (“flickering candle”) until near final exodus ~55 ka; demography (Powell, Thomas, Shennan density/migration threshold; Tasmania/Still Bay/Howiesons Poort analogies) explains discontinuity better than intelligence alone. If Neanderthals participated in symbolism (Spanish shell pendants; Châtelperronian pendants at Arcy — four interpretations: mixing, trade, acculturation, independent parallel modernization), sole genetic-MSA origin of modern behavior is wrong unless parallel mutations or hybridization (as of Stringer 2012). He revises his and Gamble’s 1993 “emulate but not fully understand” line: if Neanderthals made/used pendants, they participated in symbolism (attributed).

### Parallel evolution framing

As of Stringer’s Ch.2 framing: Neanderthals and moderns evolved in parallel (N of Mediterranean vs Africa); after false starts moderns left Africa along Asian coasts; Europe beyond modern reach until ~45 ka (updated from classic RAO’s ~35 ka). Weaver, Roseman, and Stringer: cranial differences can be explained by **genetic drift** after ~400 ka separation rather than special adaptive accounts (as of Stringer 2012).

## Mithen — *The Prehistory of the Mind* (1996): cognitive layer on origins

*(Attributed — [[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]], [[mithen-prehistory-of-the-mind-1996]]; pre-aDNA horizon.)* Mithen adopts **single African origin with replacement** of archaic *Homo sapiens*, sympathetic to **limited hybridization** with resident Early Humans (explicitly contrasts multiregional multiple-origin views). The distinctive contribution is a **cognitive architecture** layer on the debate, not a new fossil phylogeny:

1. **Anatomical modernity ≠ behavioral modernity.** *H. sapiens sapiens* appears ~100 kya (Qafzeh/Skhul, Border Cave, Klasies) but initially behaves largely like Early Humans; the major cultural transition lags ≥40 kyr.
2. **Regional cultural sparks ~60–30 kya**, not one global bang — Australia colonization, Near East blade cores, European UP package at staggered times; confident global art after ~30 kya.
3. **[[cognitive-fluidity]] as competitive edge.** Partial fluidity (social × natural-history integration) among Early Moderns ~100 kya (animal-in-grave ritual; improved hunting prediction; technical still isolated); full technical integration 60–30 kya produces Middle/Upper Palaeolithic transition and second global colonization pulse. Resident Early Humans (including [[neanderthals]]) remain Phase-2 Swiss-army-knife minds — high technical skill without art/religion package — and go extinct under competition (hybridization possible).
4. **Genetic bottleneck language as of 1996** (tiny effective population estimates Mithen reports) is **historical horizon only** — do not promote to wiki voice; later aDNA revises demography and admixture.

Pairs with [[gamble-timewalkers-1994]] colonization framing and Stringer–Gamble replacement archaeology as a *cognitive* mechanism for why Moderns eventually differed behaviorally. See [[cognitive-fluidity]] and [[hubs/theory/cognitive-fluidity]].

## Gamble — *The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe* (1999): networks, not only fossils

*(Attributed — [[gamble-clive]], [[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999]]; **as of Gamble 1999**. Pre-nuclear aDNA. No adjudication — positions only. Industry pages: [[mousterian-industry]], [[aurignacian-industry]], [[chatelperronian-industry]]; population: [[neanderthals]].)*

### Polythetic transition and the 33 Kyr hinge

Gamble treats 60–33 Kyr as a long, complicated, **polythetic** transition far from “human revolution” rhetoric — whether characterized as replacement or continuity (citing Clarke 1968; Stringer & Gamble 1993). After **33 Kyr bp** (Desbrosse & Kozlowski division) he sees structural change in regional space–time constellations **and** in locales as social occasions: settlement hierarchies and pronounced inter-/intra-regional material-culture variation, conceptualized as landscape of habit vs **social landscape** (position). Continuities (assemblage size, occupation intensity, prime-aged hunting) sit beside innovations (raw-material and shell transfer distances; ornament, sculpture, music). Major structural changes after 33 Kyr, not only at first blade industries (attributed).

### Two social systems (Neanderthal vs Cro-Magnon)

Two social systems based on distinct uses of **emotional, material, and symbolic** resources; confirms a level playing field (climate/survival/recovery) and focuses what needs explaining in Middle Palaeolithic **replacement**. Portraying the transition purely as population replacement **misrepresents its subtlety**. Differences are less Stiner-style strategy/territory rules than living within environments; **agency** creates the taskscape irrespective of fossil name-tag (position). Significant transition requires transformation of **power** and appearance of **extended networks** (system integration / action *in absentia*; release from proximity — Giddens; Rodseth et al.) (position).

### Imitation, co-residence, Iberia (recorded positions)

- **Chatelperronian–Aurignacian co-residence** in SW Europe minimally ~43–36 Kyr bp; St Césaire Neanderthal + Chatelperronian TL 36.3 Kyr; conditions for imitation/emulation of ornaments/ochre; Chatelperronian ornaments distinctively local, not pale Aurignacian copies (Harrold, Hublin et al. via Gamble).
- Network model: Chatelperronian suits wheel-shaped Neanderthal intimate/effective networks; Aurignacian “excels at extension” across a social landscape — different power construction, not a simple better/worse ranking (Gamble model).
- **Iberia (Zilhão via Gamble):** Ebro frontier ~38 Kyr; southern Iberian Mousterian/Neanderthals to ~28–30 Kyr; lacks Chatelperronian-like transitional industries; exclusive systems either side without symbolic boundary signalling; power as domination/replacement is an option not always an outcome (positions).
- **Authorship caution (Straus):** only significant Chatelperronian fossil is St Césaire; early Aurignacian makers in France/Cantabria not known from fossils; industry distinctions can blur in Spain.
- Early Aurignacian in low-density central Europe (e.g. Geißenklösterle) read as movement into rarely visited space rather than dense competitive replacement everywhere (Gamble position).

### Relation to Stringer & Gamble 1993 and Klein 1999

Gamble 1999 keeps replacement/extension of Neanderthal social systems live while shifting the explanatory load onto **network resources and release from proximity**, and while warning against pure population-replacement storytelling. It does **not** re-adjudicate African fossil phylogeny or 1993 genetics (those layers stay above). Klein’s cognitive-threshold Out of Africa 2 and S&G’s imitation-of-Moderns package remain separate positions; Gamble’s network model is an additional archaeological-social frame on the same transition.
