# The Labor Process Debate

The post-1974 dispute over how capital controls work under monopoly capitalism. Recorded here primarily from the Burawoy side (Braverman and Edwards not yet ingested — expand their positions from primary texts when available).

## Positions

- **Braverman (1974, as engaged by Burawoy):** the distinctive feature of monopoly capitalism is the destruction of skill — the separation of conception and execution, narrowing worker discretion, Taylorism as the operative ideology. His book "appeared while I was struggling to make out at Allied," and Burawoy's approach "has been largely shaped in opposition to many of the dominant themes of *Labor and Monopoly Capital*."
- **Burawoy (1979, primary):** Braverman identified a real tendency but "missed the equally important parallel tendency toward the expansion of choices within those ever narrower limits" — it is that expansion (easier rates, bidding, chiseling room) that generates consent and lets degradation proceed "without continuing crisis." Deskilling was also "uneven" and occurred "as much under competitive capitalism"; the analytic task is how conception dictates execution *after* their separation. Marx's coercion-only theory of the labor process needs the organization of consent added; the despotic → hegemonic transition tracks the passage from competitive to monopoly capitalism ([[burawoy-manufacturing-consent-study]]; [[making-out]]; [[internal-labor-market]]; [[internal-state]]).
- **Edwards (1979, as engaged by Burawoy):** the internal labor market is "bureaucratic control," rationalizing enterprise power into authority — Burawoy objects that this leaves unexplained *how* rule-following legitimizes power.
- **Doeringer & Piore (1971, as engaged by Burawoy):** internal labor markets adapt to enterprise-specific skills — hard to square, Burawoy notes, with Braverman's claim that specific skill requirements are declining.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

All parties accept that twentieth-century management transformed shop-floor organization (job classification, bidding, grievance machinery, retreat of the stopwatch); they divide over whether the change is best read as degradation and control (Braverman, Edwards), efficiency (Doeringer & Piore), or the manufacture of consent (Burawoy).
