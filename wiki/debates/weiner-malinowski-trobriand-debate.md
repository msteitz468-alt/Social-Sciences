# Weiner's Trobriand Restudy vs. Malinowski

Whether [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]]'s canonical account of [[trobriand-islanders|Trobriand]] exchange systematically misread the society by working through male informants and overlooking women's activity. [[weiner-annette-b|Annette Weiner]]'s *[[weiner-women-of-value-1976|Women of Value, Men of Renown]]* (1976), based on fieldwork in Kwaibwaga in **1971–72** — some 56 years after Malinowski's 1915–18 fieldwork — reopened the most famous site in social anthropology and argued for a substantially different picture. This is a **restudy/reinterpretation** dispute; the two accounts also document different moments (see Identity/Time Cautions below). Positions are recorded, not adjudicated.

## Shared Factual Ground

Both fieldworkers agree on much: matrilineal [[dala]] organization; the centrality of yam gardens and yam presentations through wives; ranked chieftainship financed by polygamy; the [[kula]] ring; elaborate mortuary ritual in which women are conspicuous. Malinowski himself noted (1929, p. 37) that women "conduct some parts of the ceremonial distributions themselves" and (1935) that his harvest data over-focused on men of rank — observations Weiner treats as under-developed leads rather than errors. Weiner's own account of the events immediately after death, she states, "correlate[s] very closely" with Malinowski's.

## Position A — Malinowski's Account

- Yam presentations from a wife's kin place "the wife's family under a permanent tributary obligation to the husband" (1929, p. 121) — read as **tribute**.
- In marriage exchange women figure as objects circulated between men; sex is "a service rendered by the female to the male," non-reciprocal by custom (1929) — a reading later systematized by Lévi-Strauss's [[exchange-of-women|exchange of women]].
- The father is a *tomakava* ("stranger") to his children, opening the way to an Oedipal reading of Trobriand kinship.
- **Magic** compensates for a technological/empirical "hiatus" — the gap left by inadequate control over a hostile physical environment (open-sea vs. lagoon fishing).
- The Trobriands have a **paramount chief** (term from Seligman 1910), and the village is the primary organizational unit.

## Position B — Weiner's Reinterpretation

- The yams a man receives are **not tribute but one leg of a reciprocal circuit**: he is obligated to help his wife accumulate [[womens-wealth|women's wealth]], and wife-givers monitor and enforce the return ("if he does not help her quickly... we do not want to make a garden for our sister any more"). Rank-linked presentations (*bopokala*) likewise carry "positive exchange feedback for the original donors." See [[urigubu]].
- Women are **agents, not objects**: "women use their sexuality to full advantage"; at marriage a man gains "the value of *womanness*, not women." Weiner rejects the [[exchange-of-women]] framing for the Trobriand data.
- The father is his children's most important *veyola* (kinsperson); "stranger" mistranslates *tomakava*, which applies only in dala-alignment contexts.
- **Magic** addresses a *social* hiatus — ignorance of others' minds and the danger that their interests conflict with one's own — not a technological one (evidence: fishing magic used in the *safe* lagoon when discharging an exchange obligation).
- Following [[leach-edmund|Leach]] (1958) against Powell, the **hamlet**, not the village, is the primary unit; and the "chief vs. leader" label is itself contested (Powell 1960 preferred "leader").

## Reception and Current Standing

Weiner's restudy is widely credited (attributed) with demonstrating how attention to women transforms the analysis of a classic case, and it became a founding reference for [[feminist-anthropology|the anthropology of women]] and for economic anthropology's turn to [[womens-wealth|women's wealth]] and, later, inalienable possessions. Critics note her cosmological framework ([[reproduction-regeneration]]) is interpretive and hard to test, and that some disagreements are differences of emphasis or of period rather than flat contradictions. The "tribute vs. reciprocity" reinterpretation is the most durable and widely accepted element.

## Identity / Time Cautions

The two accounts are separated by ~56 years and by changed conditions (colonial administration, the Kabisawali political movement led by John Kasaipwalova, reduced ceremonial elaboration, a cash economy inflating bundle counts). Some divergence reflects genuine reinterpretation of shared phenomena; some reflects real change between the 1915–18 and 1971–72 documentation moments. Neither account should be read as the timeless "Trobriand system."
