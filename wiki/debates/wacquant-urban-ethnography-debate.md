# Urban Ethnography of Black Men: Wacquant vs Anderson and Duneier

A methodological dispute staged in the *American Journal of Sociology* (May 2002) over how ethnographers of poor urban Black men handle morality, theory, and evidence. Recorded here as framed in Charles Lemert's 2003 foreword to *Tally's Corner*; the primary texts (Wacquant's essay and the Anderson/Duneier/Newman replies) are not yet ingested — expand from primary sources when available.

## Positions

- **Wacquant** ("Scrutinizing the Street," AJS 2002, per Lemert): [[anderson-elijah|Anderson]]'s code theory too sharply dichotomizes [[decent-and-street|decent and street]]; [[duneier-mitchell|Duneier]] "naively romanticizes" his subjects; urban ethnography has drifted into moralism.
- **Anderson and Duneier** (AJS replies, per Lemert): defend their designs and portrayals; each rejects Wacquant's reading of his evidence.
- **Lemert** (2003, POSITION): argues that neither the critic nor the criticized engaged "Liebow's interpretation — that the men are both decent and street men, both longing for respectability and unable to achieve it" — making *Tally's Corner* the unacknowledged precedent that dissolves the dichotomy Wacquant attacks ([[liebow-tallys-corner-study]]; [[shadow-values]]).
