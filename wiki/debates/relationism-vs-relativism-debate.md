# Relationism vs Relativism (Sociology of Knowledge)

Live theoretical dispute over the epistemic status of the [[sociology-of-knowledge]]. Positions stated in strongest form; no adjudication.

## Positions

### 1. Mannheimian relationism

[[mannheim-karl|Mannheim]] argues that once the social conditioning of thought is visible, science must incorporate those relations as checks rather than deny them. **[[relationism]]** holds that certain (especially historical-political) assertions can only be formulated in terms of a situational **perspective**, yet discussion still has criteria of rightness and wrongness. Relativism, on his account, arises when relational knowledge is judged by an alien ideal of eternal, unperspectivistic truth (the model of formal propositions like “2×2=4”). **Particularization** redefines the scope of claims without pure arbitrariness. He sketches routes to a new objectivity: translation among perspectives; dynamic synthesis (linked to the [[free-floating-intelligentsia]]); rejection of a hypostatized “sphere of truth as such” as empty dualism. Part III also lists a third path among “three varieties” of SoK: committed relationist mediation rather than denial of truth or eliminative purification.

### 2. Relativism charge (critics of classical SoK)

Critics hold that if all substantial social knowledge is standpoint-bound, then (a) no claim including SoK’s own can claim authority beyond a circle; (b) “relationism” is a verbal shield for relativism; (c) the program dissolves the difference between science and ideology. The charge was already anticipated in Weimar discussion (Wirth’s preface notes the tension) and recurs in mid-century American sociology’s wariness of European historicist method.

### 3. Eliminative / non-evaluative purification

A path Mannheim himself describes: assign SoK the task of discovering the “social equation” in every view and **eliminating** it to recover non-evaluative, supra-social, supra-historical truth. This preserves a classical objectivity ideal by treating social determination as bias to be removed. Mannheim does not take this as his final path for historical-political knowledge, but records it as a live option with partial justification where autonomous regularities exist.

### 4. Postwar redefinition (Berger & Luckmann and kin)

[[social-construction-of-reality|Berger & Luckmann]] (1966) redefine SoK as analysis of the **social construction of reality**, centering everyday common-sense knowledge and bracketing ultimate philosophical validity questions as not the field’s proper task. This avoids Mannheim’s full epistemological program while keeping social determination of what *passes for* knowledge. Critics still read constructionism as relativism; proponents treat bracketing as methodological, not ontological nihilism.

## Shared factual ground

- Classical SoK (Scheler naming; Mannheim radicalization) made social location of thought a research problem in interwar German and then English sociology.
- The English composite *[[mannheim-ideology-and-utopia-1936|Ideology and Utopia]]* (1936) became the default classical referent in Anglophone teaching.
- No consensus resolution: relationism, relativism charge, eliminative objectivity, and constructionist bracketing remain concurrent strategies.
