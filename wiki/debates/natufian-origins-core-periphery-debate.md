# Natufian Origins and Core–Periphery Debate

How did the [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] form, and is the Mediterranean “core” / arid “periphery” dichotomy still useful?

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

- Natufian material packages (lunates, groundstone, bone, shells, often architecture/burials) appear across the Levant in the terminal Pleistocene.
- Early definitions (Garrod, Neuville) used Carmel–Judean sites; later survey filled Negev, Sinai, Transjordan, and northern Levant.
- Geometric Kebaran precedes Natufian in much of the Mediterranean zone; Mushabian/Ramonian sequences structure the south.
- Absolute dates and assemblage purity remain uneven (admixture at el-Wad Layer B, Kebara; sparse 14C).

## Position A — Classic homeland / core-first model

**Strongest form:** The Natufian crystallized in a Mount Carmel–Galilee–Upper Jordan Valley homeland under Bølling–Allerød woodland expansion; Early Natufian base-camps there show the full package (architecture, burials, art, groundstone, bone); later stages expand into semi-arid periphery. Geometric Kebaran is the local ancestor. Used in much mid-to-late 20th-century synthesis and still a baseline contrast term in [[bar-yosef-ofer|Bar-Yosef]] school writing.

## Position B — Multi-tradition cultural complex (Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris 2013)

**Strongest form:** Core Natufian is not monolithic. Early Natufian sites likely amalgamate different Middle Epipaleolithic kin groups. Stratigraphic/lithic evidence for *in situ* evolution from local Geometric Kebaran is weak; supposed GK→Natufian continuity at el-Wad/Kebara often reflects mechanical admixture. Microburin technique and some stylistic elements originate south/east of the classic core. Settlement is not a simple permanent-base vs hunting-station dichotomy. Negev–Sinai shows *stronger* long-term continuity (Mushabian→Ramonian→Natufian→Harifian) than the Mediterranean core. Speculative reverse model: GK groups displaced southward by Mushabians regroup in the Mediterranean zone as Early Natufian, carrying southern lithic elements. Social identity and ecology jointly produce “core vs periphery,” not diffusion alone.

## Position C — Dual cores and staged expansion (Henry 2013)

**Strongest form:** Independent largely synchronous Early Natufian emergence from a broadly Geometric Kebaran substrate in a **Northern Core** (el-Wad / Carmel) and **Southern Core** (Beidha, Wadi Judayid / Ma’an Plateau), >300 km apart, both near climatic thresholds for woodland. Four expansion stages under late Bølling–Allerød and into Younger Dryas; Stage 4 Late Natufian desert colonization extraordinary in scale; Negev ornament designs link to Northern not Southern core. Harifian as mobile Late offshoot then collapse.

## Position D — Northern Levant inversion (Conard et al. 2013)

**Strongest form:** In the Anti-Lebanon foothills (Baaz, Kaus Kozah, TDASP survey), Late Natufian occupation is more intense than Early — opposite the southern heartland Early-hamlet / Late-ephemeral narrative. Pan-Levant models projecting southern periodization are unwarranted. Jeftelik (Homs Gap) Early Natufian further challenges a single-core homeland map.

## Curator’s note

*(2026-07-10)* Positions recorded from the 2013 symposium volume; no adjudication. Multi-origin + dual-core models are compatible in part (both reject simple single-homeland expansion) but differ on Geometric Kebaran continuity and southern independence.
