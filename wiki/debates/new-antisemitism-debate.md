# The New Antisemitism Debate

Is contemporary anti-Zionism — the demonization and delegitimization of Israel — a continuation of [[antisemitism]] in new clothing, or a distinct political phenomenon wrongly assimilated to it? This is one of the live definitional disputes of contemporary social science and public policy (IHRA vs. Jerusalem Declaration definitions are its institutional face).

## Position 1 — Continuity ("the new antisemitism")

[[wistrich-robert|Wistrich]] (*[[wistrich-lethal-obsession-2010|A Lethal Obsession]]*, 2010) states the strongest version: antisemitism is a single evolving demonological obsession — deicide myth → [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth|world-conspiracy myth]] → racial antisemitism → anti-Zionism — in which Israel now occupies the structural position of "the Jew among the nations." Evidence adduced: migration of the *[[protocols-of-the-elders-of-zion|Protocols]]* and blood-libel motifs into Arab and Islamist media; Soviet anti-Zionist campaigns recycling Nazi-era imagery; Hamas charter and Iranian state discourse fusing conspiracy myth with genocidal rhetoric; Western far-left/Islamist convergence ("red-green axis"). On this view demonization, double standards, and delegitimization mark where criticism becomes antisemitism.

Wistrich's fullest genealogy (ch. 14, "The Anti-Zionist Masquerade"): Nazi ideology opposed Jewish statehood as a vehicle of "world Jewry" (Hitler, Rosenberg, von Neurath 1937); Stalinist anti-Zionism (from the late 1940s) recycled the same conspiracy structure minus racial vocabulary; the post-1967 New Left adopted the Israel-as-colonial/Nazi-oppressor frame; and the 1982 Lebanon War mainstreamed the Nazi analogy in Western media. Allied voices he cites: Bernard Lewis (1976), Yoram Dinstein, Daniel Patrick Moynihan (1975), Irwin Cotler (Durban 2001 as "tipping point"), Henryk Broder, Pierre-André Taguieff (*la nouvelle judéophobie*). He qualifies the thesis explicitly: pre-state principled Jewish anti-Zionism (the Bund, classical Reform, Brit Shalom — Buber, Magnes, Hermann Cohen, Rosenzweig) is credited as free of antisemitic taint, and he disclaims labeling ordinary policy criticism antisemitic.

## Position 2 — Distinction

Brian Klug, Antony Lerman, Judith Butler, Norman Finkelstein, and others argue (each in their own register) that anti-Zionism is a position about a state and an ideology, held by many Jews; that the "new antisemitism" concept is definitionally circular (it finds what it builds into its measures); and that its deployment functions politically to delegitimize criticism of Israeli policy, diluting the term's force against real antisemitism.

The French version of this dispute (documented, hostilely, in Wistrich ch. 8–9) has its own named cast: Pascal Boniface, Esther Benbassa, Rony Brauman, Dominique Vidal, and others argued post-2000 antisemitism claims were exaggerated or instrumentalized to shield Israeli policy, against Taguieff, Finkielkraut, Bensoussan, and Bernard-Henri Lévy on the other side. Note a sourcing asymmetry: *A Lethal Obsession* itself presents Position 2 almost entirely through Wistrich's framing and quoted self-disclaimers; this page's statement of Position 2 rests on the critics' own literature (Klug 2004, Lerman, Finkelstein 2005), not on the book.

## Position 3 — Contingent overlap

Survey researchers and some historians treat the relation as empirical: Israel-related hostility correlates with classic antisemitic attitudes in some populations and not others; conspiracist and demonological content marks the antisemitic subset. Neither wholesale equation nor wholesale separation survives the data.

## Shared factual ground, framed per side

Both sides accept that classical antisemitic motifs (Protocols, blood libel) circulate widely in parts of Middle Eastern and Islamist media, and that Jews have been attacked in Europe in connection with Middle East events. They differ on whether this shows anti-Zionism *is* antisemitism (Position 1) or that antisemitism sometimes attaches itself to a distinct political conflict (Position 2).

## Status

Open; institutionalized in dueling definitions (IHRA 2016 vs. Jerusalem Declaration 2021). This wiki records positions and does not adjudicate.
