# Natufian and Younger Dryas Debate

Did the [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] cause major [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] settlement and subsistence change — and thereby the path to cultivation?

## Shared factual ground

- Global YD is a late-glacial cold reversal after Bølling–Allerød; Levantine expression is reconstructed from speleothems (e.g. Soreq), marine SSTs, pollen, Dead Sea/Lisan levels.
- Natufian occupation spans Bølling–Allerød into YD and the earliest Holocene (exact phase boundaries disputed).
- Plant remains are poorly preserved at most Natufian sites; radiocarbon samples are uneven (Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013 preface).
- Evidence-class rule: environmental proxies license `concurrent_with` until a mechanism is argued; sole `caused_by` from climate alone is rarely defensible.

## Position A — Classic YD stress → agriculture

**Strongest form:** Early Natufian flourishes under moist Bølling–Allerød woodlands; Late Natufian coincides with YD deterioration; resource stress, mobility change, and population packing push communities toward plant cultivation and eventually domestication. Underpins many textbook and Bar-Yosef-tradition models (population + resource decline; seasonality; woodland-to-grassland shift variants).

## Position B — Henry 2013: cold-driven YD, chronological disconnect

**Strongest form:** Levantine YD (~13–11 ka cal BP) was longer and less abrupt than Greenland; deterioration was more about **temperature drop (~3.5–4.5°C)** than drought. Early Natufian and the Early→Late transition (~13.7 ka cal BP) fall largely in late Bølling–Allerød — **disconnecting** climatic and cultural breakpoints at Natufian emergence and phase change. YD still matters for Late Natufian land use: cold would compress oak-nut masts and force heavier cereal storage reliance. Harifian (~12.6–12.0 ka) tracks harsh YD then collapses; possible reverse migration into Mediterranean woodlands — opposite Binford/Flannery poor-to-rich origins geography.

## Position C — Habitat mosaics and anti-uniform aridity

**Strongest form:** Levant is a mosaic; habitats respond differently. Conard et al. / Napierala: Baaz brown trout imply cool perennial highland streams; YD not a simple desertification of the Anti-Lebanon. Ashkenazi (Eynan habitats): flora/fauna do **not** support a cold-dry YD reading at Mallaha. Goring-Morris & Belfer-Cohen: little local direct deleterious YD evidence in Negev–Sinai until PPNA desertion (though they also call YD the “death knell” for Final Natufian/Harifian south — internal tension to record). Maher, Banning & Chazan (2011): “oasis or mirage?” — abrupt climate change as driver is overstated.

## Position D — Methodological skepticism (editors’ agenda)

**Strongest form (Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013 preface):** Gross-scale climate summaries cannot cleanly test climate–economy links; different studies contradict; need dated speleothem δ18O series and site-derived climatic evidence, plus phytolith/starch programs to recover plant economy.

## Position E — Cultural subsistence shifts independent of climate tracking

**Strongest form (Bar-Oz, Yeshurun & Weinstein-Evron 2013):** Specialized gazelle hunting intensification on the Carmel coastal plain rises **within Early Natufian**, not as a YD climate response. Rosen’s phytolith “Monocot Revolution”: resource re-ranking under YD without demonstrated Late Natufian cultivation.

## Roberts (1998) supporting evidence

[[roberts-neil|Roberts]] ([[roberts-holocene-1998|1998]], attributed) reports the position (Moore and Hillman 1992) that [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] climatic deterioration (c. 13,000–11,500 Cal. yr BP) reduced the availability of wild cereals and plant foods in the Near East, potentially stimulating more intensive resource-management strategies that preceded full domestication. Roberts also stresses (against V. Gordon [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]'s desiccation model) that Lake Zeribar pollen shows the early Holocene Near East grew *wetter*, not drier — bearing on the broader [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate|agricultural-origins causation debate]].

## Barker 2006 densification (*The Agricultural Revolution in Prehistory*, Ch. 10 SW Asia recap)

*(Attributed — [[barker-graeme]], *[[barker-agricultural-revolution-prehistory-2006|The Agricultural Revolution in Prehistory]]* 2006. Europe/theory-adjacent synthesis claims only from assigned ranges; not a full SW Asia primary-data densification.)*

### Position — long process, not PPNA event (Barker)

Barker objects that SW Asia data show a **very long-lived process**, not a “PPNA event”: practices with many characteristics of animal and/or plant husbandry were undertaken by the [[natufian-culture|Natufians]] ~5,000 years before the PPNA. Natufian behaviour was profoundly affected by post-LGM climatic/ecological change, **especially Younger Dryas cold/aridity** shifting gazelle ranges and contracting wheats/barleys into better-watered refugia; people had to respond to Pleistocene–Holocene landscape change and did so — some by moving, some diversifying, some intensifying toward recognizable Eurasian mixed farming. Developing reliance on domesticated cereals is treated as an irrevocable labour-time commitment poorly compatible with mobile foraging.

### Position — reversibility and no necessary trajectory

Elsewhere in the same chapter Barker stresses archaeological **reversions** (late vs early Natufians; Barbary sheep herding experiment then return to hunting in Libyan Sahara) as evidence there is no inexorable path from husbandry-like behaviours to agriculture — relevant to how YD “crisis” narratives are framed (response options plural; not all roads lead to farming).

Do not treat Barker’s recap as settling Positions A–E above; it densifies a multi-millennial Natufian–YD–PPNA process reading and anti-teleology caution. Source: [[barker-agricultural-revolution-prehistory-2006]].
