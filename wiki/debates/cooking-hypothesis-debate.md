# Cooking Hypothesis Debate

Live dispute over whether **cooking and fire control** explain the major *Homo* transition, human dietary dependence, and foundational social arrangements — or whether meat-eating, late fire, or other factors dominate. Positions in strongest form; **not adjudicated**.

Related: [[cooking-hypothesis]] · [[hunting-hypothesis]] · [[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]] · [[control-of-fire]] · [[expensive-tissue-hypothesis]]

## Position A — Cooking first (*Homo erectus* cooks)

*([[wrangham-richard|Wrangham]] 2009; [[cooking-hypothesis]].)*

Meat-eating jump-starts habilines (~2.5–2.3 Ma tools/brains) but cannot explain the second step: *Homo erectus* (~1.9–1.8 Ma) with small jaws/teeth unsuited to tough raw meat, reduced guts, near-modern body, and further encephalization. Cooking supplies the energy and soft diet those anatomies require; humans are obligate cooks. Archaeology of early fire is biased and incomplete, so **biology dates cooking**. Soft food frees time for a sexual division of labor and ties pair-bonds to camp meals.

## Position B — Hunting / meat as master adaptation

*([[hunting-hypothesis]]; Washburn & Lancaster 1968; lineage Wrangham steel-mans.)*

Hunting (and meat more broadly) is the master behavior pattern shaping intellect, emotions, cooperation, and the family via male hunting / female gathering reciprocity. Meat importance in forager diets and deep archaeological butchery support continuity from habilines. Cooking, on this view, is a later cultural add-on once humans were already human (Darwinian sequence).

## Position C — Late fire control (archaeological minimalism)

*(As Wrangham reconstructs opponents: Upper Paleolithic ~40 ka regular cooking; or Brace-type ~200–250 ka fire with delayed dental response.)*

Confident hearths cluster in the last few hundred thousand years (Abri Pataud, Hayonim, etc.). Lower Paleolithic "hints" (Swartkrans burnt bone, Chesowanja clay, Koobi Fora patches) may be natural fires. Without early secure fire, cooking cannot drive *erectus* origins; dental reduction needs other explanations or much later cooking.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

- *Homo* shows a multi-step fossil sequence with major change near 2 Ma and again later — sides disagree on causes per step.
- Cooking is ethnographically universal among known societies (Tylor line); sides disagree whether that universality is biological dependence from deep time or cultural universal of modern humans.
- Meat matters in human evolution; sides disagree whether it is *sufficient* for the *erectus* package.
- Fire leaves poor traces; sides disagree whether that licenses biological dating (A) or demands archaeological caution against early cooking (C).
