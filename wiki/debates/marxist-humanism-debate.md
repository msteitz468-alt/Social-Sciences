# Marxist Humanism vs Theoretical Anti-Humanism

## The dispute

After the Twentieth Congress of the CPSU and the crisis of Stalinist dogmatism, many Communists and Marxists turned to Marx’s **Early Works** (especially the 1844 Manuscripts) and to languages of **alienation**, **human essence**, and **socialist humanism** as the ethical and theoretical core of Marxism. [[althusser-louis|Louis Althusser]] intervened (essays collected in *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*, 1965) to draw a line of demarcation: mature Marxism is a **science of history** grounded in concepts of [[social-formation]], not a philosophical anthropology; [[theoretical-anti-humanism]] is the negative precondition of that science; humanism is an **ideology** that may have practical value but not theoretical foundation.

This page records positions; it does **not** adjudicate.

## Position 1 — Humanist Marxism (strongest form)

- Marx’s Early Works reveal the emancipatory meaning of the mature critique of political economy; *Capital* fulfills, rather than cancels, the 1844 vision of communism as “fully developed naturalism — Humanism.”
- [[alienation]] is a key critical concept for diagnosing capitalism and motivating socialism; discarding it yields technocratic or Stalinist Marxism.
- Socialist humanism (class humanism; personal humanism after dictatorship of the proletariat) is not a theoretical mistake but the living ideology and goal of the workers’ movement.
- Continuist reading: “the young Marx is part of Marxism”; genetic history from Feuerbach through 1844 to *Capital* is legitimate.

## Position 2 — Althusserian theoretical anti-humanism (strongest form)

*(Attributed — Althusser, *For Marx*.)*

- An [[epistemological-break]] ~1845 separates ideological anthropological problematics from the scientific concepts of historical and dialectical materialism.
- In the couple humanism–socialism, **socialism** is scientific; **humanism** is ideological: it designates existents without giving their essences.
- Theoretical anti-humanism is not hatred of people; it is refusal of the myth of Man as theoretical foundation. Knowledge of the human world requires reducing that myth to ashes.
- Post-1956 humanist revival is an ideological symptom of theoretical weakness and of the political crisis of the cult of personality — a liberal-ethical reaction that risks restoring pre-Marxist ideology.
- 1844 Manuscripts show Marx “furthest from Marx” — Feuerbach–Hegel synthesis on the threshold of the break, not the secret of *Capital*.

## Position 3 — Intermediate / historicist / praxis strands

- Recover totality, consciousness, hegemony, or praxis ([[western-marxism]], Gramsci, some Lukács receptions, Sartrean Marxism) without accepting either Stalinist DIAMAT or Althusser’s full science/ideology dualism and anti-humanism.
- May accept relative autonomy of superstructures and complexity of conjuncture while rejecting the claim that “Man” must be theoretically abolished.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

- Marx wrote the 1844 Manuscripts and later wrote *Capital*; the textual sequence is not in dispute.
- Post-1956 Communist and New Left cultures heavily published and discussed the Early Works.
- Althusser wrote as a Communist philosopher intervening in PCF and international Marxist debate (1960–65 essays).
- What is disputed is **theoretical status**: science vs ideology; break vs continuity; humanism as foundation vs slogan.
