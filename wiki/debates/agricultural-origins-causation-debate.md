# Agricultural Origins Causation Debate

What caused the shift from hunting-gathering to agriculture at the start of the [[holocene|Holocene]]? [[roberts-neil|Roberts]] ([[roberts-holocene-1998|1998]]) frames this as a genuine, unresolved dispute between environmental and non-environmental explanations, and argues that no single model fits all regions.

## Shared factual ground

- The [[agricultural-revolution|transition to farming]] occurred independently in several regions (Near East, Mesoamerica, China, probably highland New Guinea and parts of Africa) within a few millennia of the Holocene's onset, with different domesticate packages (see [[domestication]]).
- It was gradual (a transitional proto-agricultural stage), not a sudden "revolution," despite Childe's term.
- Early-Holocene climate changed substantially in most of these regions.

## Positions

**Environmental-determinist / oasis model.** In its strongest early form, V. Gordon [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]'s (1928) oasis hypothesis: post-glacial desiccation concentrated humans, plants, and animals around shrinking water sources, forcing domestication. A refined climatic version holds that [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] resource decline stimulated intensive management preceding domestication (Moore & Hillman 1992; see the [[natufian-younger-dryas-debate]]).

**Opportunist model.** Environmental *amelioration* — rising [[sea-level-change|sea levels]], expanding wild cereal stands — made new resources available that were opportunistically exploited (e.g. the marine shift at Franchthi Cave).

**Demographic / social model.** Binford (1968) and Cohen (1977): environmental change alone is insufficient, since comparable opportunities existed in earlier interstadials without being taken; population dynamics and social factors are the active variables.

## Shared factual ground, framed per side

Roberts' key empirical checks (attributed):
- The oasis model is **falsified for the Near East**: Lake Zeribar pollen (van Zeist) shows *increasing* early-Holocene moisture, the opposite of Childe's predicted desiccation.
- It **fits the African case better**: Sahelian-Sudanian cultivation followed Saharan [[desertification]] after ~5500 years ago.
- It **fails for Mesoamerica**: cultivation began in central Mexico, *not* in the region of most severe desiccation (the northwest) — an explicit anomaly against determinism.

## Curator's note (2026-07-10)

Roberts' own position is multi-causal and anti-determinist: environmental change is a real contributor whose weight varies by region and must be demonstrated, not assumed — consistent with the wiki's treatment of [[environmental-determinism]]. The debate remains open.

## Cauvin position — ideology / revolution of symbols (2000)

*(Attributed — [[cauvin-jacques]], *[[cauvin-birth-of-the-gods-2000]]* 2000; do not adjudicate.)*

| Position holder | Causal claim (as of Cauvin 2000) |
|---|---|
| **Cauvin** | **[[revolution-of-symbols]]** among Khiamian foragers installs new religion (Goddess + Bull) ~9500 BC; farming ~9000 BC is second step. Stratigraphy inverts materialist cause→effect order. Ecological and purely demographic prime movers rejected for the Levantine origin. |
| **Cauvin vs Childe** | Grants oriental origins of European Neolithic; rejects simplified historical materialism / environmental determinism as explanation of domestication itself. |
| **Cauvin vs Braidwood** | Reinterprets “Culture was not ready” as mental/religious readiness and *desire* to intervene in nature, not only techno-economic lag in the nuclear zone. |
| **Cauvin vs population-pressure** | PPNA large villages without pan-Near-Eastern demographic expansion; labour organisation and social regrouping precede population-pressure explanations. |
| **Cauvin on climate (Postscript)** | Younger Dryas ~10,600 BC (not 11,000) at Abu Hureyra; moderate impact; not important causal trigger of Neolithic Revolution — drives abandonment of desertifying zones. |
| **Softened uptake** | Many later writers accept **ideology-before-economy** as hypothesis while rejecting mother-goddess cult label (e.g. Mithen on Göbekli/Jerf). |

## Scott position — *Against the Grain* (2017)

*(Attributed — [[scott-james-c|James C. Scott]], *[[scott-against-the-grain-2017|Against the Grain]]* 2017. Political-scientist synthesis; not primary archaeobotany. Recorded as a position, not adjudicated.)*

| Claim cluster | Scott’s stance (as of 2017) |
|---|---|
| **Magic-moment Neolithic** | Rejected: no single fateful line from foraging to agriculture or savagery to civilization; planting is one event in deep fire-mediated niche construction from *Homo erectus* onward |
| **Tempo / lag** | ~4,000 years between key domestications and first agrarian economies based on them; ancestors did not rush into the Neolithic or earliest states |
| **Boserup “backs-to-the-wall”** | Recounted then **rejected for Mesopotamia / Fertile Crescent**: cultivation arose in abundance not scarcity; flood-retreat agriculture may invalidate pure “great toil” premise; theory “in tatters” without satisfactory replacement for why cultivation spread |
| **Why farm at all?** | Fixed-field farming’s huge drudgery vs forager labor (even in poor environments ~half time subsistence); Abu Hureyra researchers: no foragers in productive multi-seasonal locales would willingly cultivate staples; Younger Dryas + restricted mobility “pistol” **hotly contested** — Scott declines to adjudicate multi-millennial drivers |
| **Broad-spectrum then agriculture** | Accepts BSR archaeological reality; jury out on its causes/consequences; for agriculture proper 3–4 millennia later “the jury is in”: population pressure, restricted mobility of sedentists, large-game decline, deliberate **avoidance** of planting/stock as dominant mode because of work defending simplified landscapes |
| **Wetland / sequence** | Sedentism and early towns from wetland abundance, not irrigation-state package; classical “making the desert bloom by irrigation invents sedentism/state” narrative “totally wrong” for southern alluvium (Pournelle) |
| **Progress narrative** | Initial agriculture worsened well-being relative to foraging; multi-millennial process not civilizational Rubicon — densified under [[agricultural-revolution]], [[sedentism]], [[domestication]] |

**Relation to other positions on this page:** Scott is **anti-stadial** and **anti-ascent-of-man**, multi-causal by refusal of single prime movers, closer to demographic/avoidance-of-drudgery logics than to oasis desiccation or Cauvin ideology-before-economy (he does not engage Cauvin’s Goddess–Bull program as such). Wetland-abundance early sedentism is an empirical/position challenge to irrigation-state coupling more than a full rival “why plant?” theory. Adjacent (not identical) anti-linear themes: [[dawn-of-everything-debate]] — do not merge Scott with Graeber/Wengrow.
