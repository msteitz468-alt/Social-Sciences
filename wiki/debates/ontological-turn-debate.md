# The Ontological Turn: Concept vs. Type, Ontology vs. Epistemology

A live theoretical dispute within and around the anthropological "[[ontological-turn|ontological turn]]," bound up with the lifelong friendship-and-disagreement of [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Eduardo Viveiros de Castro]] and [[descola-philippe|Philippe Descola]] — two Amazonianist heirs of [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] who agree that the Western [[nature-culture-distinction|nature/culture]] dualism is provincial but disagree on how to theorize the alternatives. Positions are recorded here in their strongest form; the wiki does not adjudicate.

## Position 1 — Perspectivism is a subtype of animism (Descola)

[[descola-philippe|Descola]]'s *[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]* builds a comparative typology of **[[four-ontologies|four ontologies]]** — [[animism]], totemism, analogism, naturalism — generated by continuity/discontinuity of [[interiority-and-physicality|interiority and physicality]] between humans and nonhumans. On this scheme Amerindian [[perspectivism]] falls within **animism** (shared interiority, differentiated physicality); Descola credits Viveiros de Castro's 1998 perspectivism article as a partial source. The value of the framework is systematic comparability across regions.

## Position 2 — Perspectivism is a concept, not a type (Viveiros de Castro)

[[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]] rejects the subsumption: "perspectivism is not, *pace* Descola, a subtype of animism... It is not a type but a concept." A typology, he argues, classifies from outside and treats indigenous thought as an object to be sorted; but "the very idea of identification is an analogist idea" — Descola's classificatory method itself instantiates *one* of the four ontologies (analogism), so the frame cannot be neutral. Where Descola aimed "to rewrite *The Savage Mind* after... *The Order of Things*," Viveiros de Castro aimed "to rewrite the *Mythologiques* on the basis of... *A Thousand Plateaus*" — perspectivism as [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuzian]] concept-creation, not classification. (Attributed.)

## Position 3 — The turn overclaims (external critics)

A broader critical position (registered by the turn's opponents) holds that the difference the turn calls "ontological" is really **epistemological** — different representations of one world, not different worlds — and that reading Amazonian ethnography as metaphysics is idealist over-interpretation that romanticizes alterity and courts obscurity. Viveiros de Castro's pre-emptive answer is that the charge of "idealism" itself relies on a constructionist metaphysics (one real world, many constructions of it) that the turn exists to reject.

## Shared Factual Ground, Framed Per Side

All parties accept: (a) that nature/culture is not a cross-cultural universal; (b) that Amazonian peoples attribute personhood widely across species; (c) that Lévi-Straussian structuralism is the common inheritance. They divide on whether the right theoretical object is a **comparative typology of ontologies** (Descola), a **concept** irreducible to typology (Viveiros de Castro), or an **epistemology** that the turn has inflated into ontology (critics).
