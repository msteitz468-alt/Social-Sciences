# Oasis vs Natural-Habitat (Hilly Flanks) Agricultural Origins

Live (and historiographically layered) dispute over **where and why** food production began in western Asia — and by extension how environment and culture are related in agricultural origins. This page records positions; it does not adjudicate.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

- Food production appears in the early Holocene after a long Pleistocene foraging record.
- Wild ancestors of wheat and barley have restricted natural ranges.
- Early village sites and oasis/river sites do not map identically.
- Absolute chronology and multi-regional centers have been heavily revised since mid-century formulations.

## Position A — Oasis / propinquity (as restated by Braidwood 1959)

*(Attributed — Braidwood’s reconstruction of a theory he says held “for at least twenty-five years.”)*

After ~10,000 B.C., glacial melt; while glaciers stood, a rain belt south of the ice kept N Africa, the Nile, and the Middle East green; when ice retreated, the rain belt moved north; desiccation forced people, plants, and animals into **river valleys and oases**; forced proximity plus trial-and-error produced domestication.

**Strength as claimed by proponents (per Braidwood’s summary):** links the food-producing revolution to a large, independently known environmental change; makes “why then?” answerable.

## Position B — Natural habitat / hilly flanks (Braidwood)

*(Attributed — [[braidwood-robert]], *[[braidwood-prehistoric-men-1959|Prehistoric Men]]* 1959.)*

- Earliest traces are **not** primarily in oases and pure river lowlands.
- The **[[hilly-flanks|hilly-flanks zone]]** (~2,000–5,000 ft, winter rain) holds wild wheats **together with** wild barley and potential animal domesticates; climate then ≈ climate now.
- Domestication requires a **[[nuclear-area|nuclear area]]** (environment + cultural receptiveness), not desiccation forcing.
- [[jarmo|Jarmo]], Natufian, Karim Shahir, and peer early villages are the empirical face of the case.
- Oasis theory is “too simple for the facts.”

## Position C — Later climate models without full oasis package

*(Attributed — textbook surveys, e.g. [[wenke-olszewski-patterns-in-prehistory-2007|Wenke & Olszewski 2007]].)*

Climate returns as a contributor (Younger Dryas, Holocene stability/CO₂) without requiring classic oasis propinquity geography. Record as POSITION, not wiki-voice resolution.

## Position D — Demographic-ecological / packing (Binford line)

*(Attributed — [[binford-lewis]], *New Perspectives* 1968 and later packing restatements.)*

Critiques oasis **and** nuclear-zone settling-in / “Garden of Eden” narratives; multi-origin adaptive model driven by packing, mobility security, and resource depression rather than unique nuclear natural habitat. See [[processual-archaeology]], [[agricultural-revolution]].

## Methodological stakes

- How much weight environmental **forcing** may carry vs cultural **receptiveness**.
- Whether “nuclear area” is a useful analytic unit or a teleological western-heritage construct.
- Whether early radiocarbon (as of 1959) can pin priority among flanks, Levant, and riverine sites.

## Resolution status

**Partially-resolved** in the weak sense that pure oasis geography as sole driver is widely abandoned, while climate, demography, and social models remain open and multi-causal. Multi-regional farming further dissolves single-hearth exclusivity. Do not promote any single causal story to wiki voice.

## Ucko & Dimbleby 1969 positions (seminar densification)

*(Attributed — chapter authors; do not adjudicate.)*

- **Ucko & Dimbleby (intro):** Jarmo often read as hilly-flanks response to desiccation; Jericho as oasis response — both over-emphasize desiccation with little direct support. Würm II Mediterranean/Zagros: arid and cold (Artemisia steppe pollen); early post-glacial humidity increase. Apparent aridity can be anthropogenic (Raikes) without overall climate change. Çatalhöyük moves attention to Anatolia beyond classic Fertile Crescent.
- **van Zeist:** Near Eastern paleoenvironments — early Holocene not simply “less winter rain”; site-scale moisture vs regional climate.
- **Flannery:** for much of SW Asia, late/post-Pleistocene environmental change is **not** evidenced as forcing major subsistence shifts; prefers density-equilibrium / population-pressure and **marginal** innovation ([[broad-spectrum-revolution]], cultivation, irrigation). Challenges farming origin in densest wild cereal heartland (Harlan–Zohary).
- **Reed:** bows to Lake Zeribar warming/tree re-invasion ~11 kya without **reviving** desiccation theory of animal domestication; environment + culture changing together after glacial retreat.
- **Darlington (same volume):** single Nuclear Zone monogenesis — related to, but not identical with, Braidwood natural-habitat geography; competes with multi-origin botanical papers in the volume.
