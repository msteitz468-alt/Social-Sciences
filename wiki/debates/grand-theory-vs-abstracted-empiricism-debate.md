# Grand Theory vs. Abstracted Empiricism: the Form of Social Science

The dispute, framed most sharply by [[mills-c-wright|C. Wright Mills]] in *[[mills-sociological-imagination-1959|The Sociological Imagination]]* (1959), is over the proper **form** of social science in its postwar American ascendancy — whether knowledge of society advances chiefly through general theoretical systematization, through rigorous empirical measurement, or through a problem-centered "classic" practice that Mills claimed both dominant styles had abandoned. It is the wiki's principal statement of the theory-versus-method and (in part) qualitative–quantitative disputes; note that Mills's polemic is not a two-sided quarrel between the two named styles but a two-front attack on **both**, from a third position.

Per the Voice and Attribution Protocol, no position is adjudicated in wiki voice. Mills’s side rests on *[[mills-sociological-imagination-1959|The Sociological Imagination]]*; Position 2 on [[parsons-social-system-1951]]; Position 4 on primary Merton (*[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]*); Position 3 remains a reconstruction from the survey tradition’s general character pending Lazarsfeld primary ingest.

## Position 1 — Mills: both are withdrawals from the tasks of social science

Mills argued (attributed) that [[grand-theory]] (his label for [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]]'s *The Social System*) is "drunk on syntax, blind to semantics" — an "arid game of Concepts" pitched so high it "cannot logically get down to observation" — and that its value-consensus frame structurally excludes power and conflict from view. Its twin, [[abstracted-empiricism]] (the Lazarsfeld/Bureau survey style), suffers "the methodological inhibition": method dictates which problems can be posed, yielding "thinness of results" and a drift toward psychologism and administrative service. Both, Mills held, are "parasites living off the classic social science tradition." The alternative is the [[sociological-imagination]]: problem-driven, comparative, historical "classic" craft that connects [[personal-troubles-and-public-issues|troubles to issues]] — "Every man his own methodologist."

## Position 2 — The general-theory defense

On the Parsonsian side (stated from [[parsons-social-system-1951]], not from a reply to Mills): a general analytic scheme of [[social-system]], [[social-role|role]], and institutionalized [[value-orientation]] is the precondition of a cumulative science rather than a flight from it; the "Hobbesian problem of order" is a real theoretical problem that piecemeal empiricism cannot even formulate; and the high abstraction Mills mocks is the necessary generality of a framework meant to organize comparison across all societies. Neofunctionalists later argued Mills's plain-English "translations" caricatured the scheme by stripping its systematic connections. See [[structural-functionalism]], [[consensus-vs-conflict-debate]].

## Position 3 — The survey-method defense

On the survey side (stated from the general character of the tradition): probability sampling, explicit operationalization, and statistical analysis deliver comparability, replicability, and trend detection that interpretive "classic" essays cannot — and Mills's exemplars (Neumann on Nazi Germany, Weber on the mandarinate) are precisely the interpretive works whose claims are hardest to check. Lazarsfeld's "building-block" image holds that findings accumulate; the administrative uses Mills disdained are also what make large-scale data collection possible. Mills conceded Lazarsfeld was "among the more sophisticated spokesmen" of the style.

## Position 4 — Merton: theories of the middle range

[[merton-robert-k|Merton]] (*[[merton-social-theory-social-structure-1968|STSS]]* Ch. II; also Chs. IV–V on theory–research traffic) stated a **fourth path** that is neither total-system first nor bare description (attributed): [[middle-range-theory|theories of the middle range]] lie between working hypotheses and all-inclusive unified theory; they guide empirical inquiry with abstractions close enough for testable propositions; they are **not** logically deduced from a single total scheme though they may later be consistent with one. He recorded polarized assent and rejection of this policy, and illustrated with [[reference-group]] / [[relative-deprivation]] and [[role-set]] theory. Reciprocally, research **initiates, reformulates, deflects, and clarifies** theory — including **serendipity** (chance discovery by a prepared mind of findings not looked for). This is not identical with Mills’s “classic” craft, nor with Bureau methodism alone, nor with Parsons’s total conceptual scheme; it is the Columbia theory–research settlement’s charter.

## Shared factual ground, framed per side

All parties agree that postwar American sociology was dominated by Parsonsian theory and Bureau-style survey research, that the two rarely interacted, and that both commanded institutional resources Mills's "classic" craftsman lacked. Where Mills read this as a duopoly starving the discipline of its promise, the defenders read the same division of labor as complementary specialization — theory supplying concepts, surveys supplying disciplined evidence.

## Current standing

Mills's essay endures as the discipline's most-quoted indictment of both styles and a founding text of a more reflexive, problem-centered self-image; Gitlin's 2000 afterword judges the critique "as important and vivid... today as forty years ago," while noting its target had shifted (a contemporary Mills would aim "grand theory" at Foucault-era Theory rather than Parsons). Yet survey methodology and formal theorizing both remain central to the discipline — the dispute is `partially-resolved` in that Mills's *values* (reflexivity, the troubles-to-issues charge, distrust of method-for-its-own-sake) were widely absorbed even as the *practices* he attacked were not abandoned. Curator's note (2026-07-09): Position 4 (Merton middle-range) is now grounded in primary *STSS*; Position 2 still rests on Parsons primary without a direct reply-to-Mills text; Position 3 remains reconstruction pending Lazarsfeld primary.
