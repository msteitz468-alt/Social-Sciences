# Kinship: Biology vs Culture

Live theoretical dispute over whether kinship is fundamentally **genealogical / biogenetic connection** (birth as distinctive feature; non-birth relations "fictive," "metaphorical," or extensions) or a **cultural order of intersubjective participation** that may use procreation among other means. Primary densification here from [[sahlins-what-kinship-is-2013|Sahlins, *What Kinship Is—And Is Not*]] (2013), which reconstructs the orthodoxy in order to reverse it: **"if anything, birth is a metaphor of kinship relations."**

## Positions

### Position 1 — Genealogical / biogenetic orthodoxy

*(Attributed reconstruction — Sahlins 2013 of a tradition from Morgan through mid-century analytic kinship.)*

- **[[morgan-lewis-henry|Lewis Henry Morgan]]** (*[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems of Consanguinity]]* 1871) founded the anthropology of kinship on determination of relations as **genealogical connections**. Sahlins: few concessions since on the "essential matter of the biological basis."
- **Davis & Warner (1937):** kinship = "social relationships based on connection through birth" — including affinity (via common offspring) and adoption ("as if" reproductive family); "it makes no difference what conception of reproduction the particular culture may have."
- **Scheffler & Lounsbury (1971):** "relations of genealogical connection" are "**kinship proper**," "logically and temporally prior to any social relations of kinship." Scheffler (1976): persons related by birth are relatives "by definition"; birth is the **distinctive feature** of the primary category of relatives (genitor/genetrix/offspring chains). Sahlins notes Scheffler unusually refuses the word "biological" while retaining birth as criterion.
- **Gellner (1960):** kinship structure = social use of a simple, universal **physical pattern**; that substrate makes descent systems comparable with precision.
- **Extensionism:** primary kin (birth) are focal; terms extend outward; non-genealogical uses are metaphorical, "as if," or mixed biogenetic+cultural.
- **Sociobiology / evolutionary psychology** (as Sahlins groups them): non-genealogical kinship dismissed as metaphor/fictive because no biogenetic link.

**Limited concessions within orthodoxy (per Sahlins):** (a) "folk biology" — local theories of reproduction vary, but "it makes no difference"; (b) genealogical network is inflected by descent schemes — treated as analytical virtue, not challenge to the substrate.

### Position 2 — Schneider's dissolution

*(Attributed — [[schneider-david-m|David M. Schneider]], as read by Sahlins 2013.)*

Long study convinced Schneider there is **no such thing as kinship** as a distinct cultural system or cross-cultural category: Western "blood" (biogenetic substance) vs law/code is a folk model that anthropology projected onto others (*American Kinship* 1968; "What Is Kinship All About?" 1972; *A Critique of the Study of Kinship* 1984). The same *physis*/*nomos* opposition structures kinship, nationalism, and religion; kinship symbols are not domain-exclusive — hence dissolve the topic.

Sahlins's historiographic gloss: Schneider's end-of-kinship announcement had "the logical force of the famous observation of the Cretan that 'All Cretans are liars'" — it spurred the enlightening constructivist work it seemed to forbid. Sahlins roots the impasse in Parsonian culture/social dualism and argues Schneider recapitulated the American blood/in-law dualism he analyzed.

### Position 3 — Constructivist / relatedness

*(Attributed — field orthodoxy as Sahlins endorses it: Carsten, Bamford & Leach, Franklin & McKinnon, McKinnon, etc.)*

"Any relationship constituted in terms of procreation, filiation, or descent can also be made postnatally or performatively by culturally appropriate action." Constructed "biological" relationships are often preferred (brothers by compact closer than by birth). "Relatedness" (Carsten) reopened kinship after Schneider without simply restoring genealogical naturalism. Feeding, co-residence, land, name-sharing, and nurturance are first-class kinship makers.

### Position 4 — Mutuality of being (Sahlins 2013)

*(Attributed — Sahlins; see [[mutuality-of-being]].)*

1. Kinship **is** mutuality of being — intersubjective participation in each other's existence — covering procreative *and* performative constitution, consanguinity *and* affinity, interpersonal *and* descent-group forms.
2. **Birth is not pre-discursive**; procreation already entails a greater kinship matrix (ancestors as co-generators; substances as social endowments of status).
3. Therefore kinship categories are **not** representations or metaphorical extensions of birth; **birth is a metaphor of kinship** (developing McKinley).
4. Fallacy of "primary kin": abstract genitors without social identity; classificatory kinship is often present from birth, not an extension from nuclear-family cognition (polemic vs Malinowski's Trobriand "I saw classificatory kinship born").
5. Generalization: **unilineal** orders tend to build kinship into procreation; **cognatic/kindred** orders favor life-achieved mutuality.
6. Kinship–gift–magic form one ontological regime ([[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]); Viveiros **inverts** Schneider — same cross-domain analysis, opposite conclusion (kinship as cosmic intersubjectivity, not non-existence).
7. Closing line of the program (via Viveiros on Amazonia): kinship can be "what you have when you 'do without' a biological theory of relationality"; "kinship is culture, all culture."

## Shared Factual Ground

- Ethnography documents both birth-based and non-genealogical kinship (adoption, feeding, name-sharing, compact) across many societies.
- Local "theories of conception" vary widely (substance, soul, ancestral implantation, exclusion of mother or father, etc.).
- Kinship terminologies often use a genealogical *idiom* even where practice is non-genealogical — a fact each side interprets differently (orthodoxy: proof of biogenetic focus; Sahlins/McKinley: folk-biological metaphor for social positions).

## Methodological Stakes

| Tool / move | Orthodoxy | Sahlins / mutuality |
|---|---|---|
| [[genealogical-method]] | Collects "dry fact" pedigrees as substrate | Useful data technique; does not prove genealogical definition of kinship |
| Primary kin + extension | Analytic architecture | Egocentric biologism; "Welcome to America" |
| Uncontrolled comparison (Sahlins's own method) | Weak verification | Explicitly illustrative demonstration of an idea |

## Resolution Status

**Open.** The constructivist/relatedness turn is the contemporary mainstream in sociocultural anthropology; genealogical formal analysis and evolutionary approaches remain active in other subfields. Sahlins 2013 is a late culturalist synthesis, not a field consensus decree. No wiki-voice adjudication.
