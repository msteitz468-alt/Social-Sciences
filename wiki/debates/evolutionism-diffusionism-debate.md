# Evolutionism vs. Diffusionism (and the critique of conjectural history)

A foundational dispute in early anthropology over how to explain the similarities and
differences among human cultures, and whether the history of non-literate societies can be
reconstructed at all. Its pivot figure is [[rivers-w-h-r|W. H. R. Rivers]], whose public
conversion from one camp to the other — recorded inside
*[[rivers-melanesian-society-1914|The History of Melanesian Society]]* (1914) — crystallized
the issue, and whose whole enterprise was then swept away by a third position that rejected
the question.

## Position 1 — Evolutionism (independent invention)

Cultural similarities across the world arise because all human minds are alike
([[psychic-unity-of-mankind|psychic unity]]) and independently invent similar institutions as
they ascend a universal ladder of stages. [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]], Morgan, Spencer,
Frazer used the [[comparative-method]] on traveller and missionary reports to reconstruct this
ladder, treating out-of-place traits as **[[survivals]]** of earlier stages. In its strongest
form: culture-history is the working-out of a single developmental logic, and cross-cultural
resemblance needs no historical connection to explain it.

**The 1888 origin point (attributed — [[stocking-after-tylor-1995|Stocking, *After Tylor*, 1995]]).** [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]]'s 1888 "adhesions" paper is treated by Stocking as the moment the two positions' terms became explicit inside evolutionism itself: Tylor argued similar customs (avoidance, teknonymy, classificatory kinship, matrilocal residence) co-occurring across unrelated societies at above-chance rates arise independently via "the like working of men's minds under like conditions" (psychic unity). At the same meeting, Francis Galton objected that the compared cases might instead reflect "blood relationship or intercourse, direct or indirect" — shared historical origin or diffusion — which would invalidate treating them as statistically independent (**Galton's problem**); W. H. Flower raised a parallel objection about non-equivalent units of comparison. Tylor dismissed the objection. Stocking reads this exchange as the seed of the larger disciplinary cycle: diffusionary ethnology gave way to evolutionary anthropology in the 1860s, and a renewed diffusionism reasserted itself in the early twentieth century, first via [[boas-franz|Boas]] in the United States, then in Germany (Graebner) and England (Rivers). See [[comparative-method]] and [[social-evolutionism]] for the fuller record of the paper.

## Position 2 — Diffusionism (contact and migration)

Cultural similarity is better explained by the **spread** of traits between peoples than by
independent invention. In [[graebner-fritz|Graebner]]'s Kulturkreis form, trait-complexes
diffuse from centres and layer into recoverable culture-strata; in [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]]'
form, a region's present culture is analysed into successive **immigrant layers** whose fusion
produced the observed complexity. Rivers' strongest statement: "the contact of peoples and the
blending of their cultures act as the chief stimuli setting in action the forces which lead to
human progress"; isolation produces "degradation and even... disappearance." Rivers held
social structure (least mutable) to be the securest evidence for reconstructing contact
history, and criticized [[graebner-fritz|Graebner]] for treating institutions as diffusing as
easily as weapons. The hyper-diffusionist wing (Elliot Smith, Perry) pushed the position to
its limit: **all** civilization radiated from Egypt.

**Stocking's tree-vs-ladder framing.** *(Attributed — [[stocking-george-w|Stocking]] 1987.)* Stocking reads the evolutionism/diffusionism split as turning on a logical asymmetry in the underlying genealogical image: independent invention implied a "polygenesis of arts" — parallel origins with no single tree — whereas both diffusionist ethnology and Darwinian biological evolution were given temporal structure by a genealogical tree tracing every branch to a common trunk. On his account, classical evolutionists privileged independent invention over diffusion even though diffusion was conceptually the *closer* fit to the branching Darwinian model, because independent invention alone guaranteed the processual regularity (no external contact needed) that let evolutionism dispense with divine intervention. Stocking also credits [[boas-franz|Boas]]'s 1896 "Limitations of the Comparative Method" essay as the first sustained anthropological statement of this fault line, and notes an early instance of the underlying dispute already visible among the evolutionists themselves: [[pitt-rivers-augustus|Pitt Rivers]] (then Lane Fox) insisted on tracing material-culture forms back to single geographical sources rather than accepting independent invention, drawing criticism from his own friends [[tylor-edward-burnett|Tylor]] and [[lubbock-john|Lubbock]] for it.

**The 1920s heliolithic episode.** Stocking narrates the British "heliolithic" diffusionist vogue of the early 1920s — Grafton Elliot Smith and William Perry, following on from [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]] — which held that seafaring ancient Egyptians spread sun worship, monolith-building, and much else worldwide. He notes British anthropological circles were "set agog" by Perry's accounts before the theory was eclipsed, and flags it as a "diffusionist fantasy" in fact foreshadowed by pre-Prichardian biblical anthropology (Stocking's own framing, not the diffusionists'). This episode fed directly into the functionalist reaction below: [[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]] broke from his mentor Rivers's diffusionist re-readings of the Andaman Islands and Western Australia fieldwork data he had originally analyzed in evolutionary terms, deciding the whole "independent invention versus borrowing" question no longer interested him, and redirecting inquiry toward synchronic functional explanation instead — a pivotal episode, on Stocking's account, in the shift from this debate toward Position 3.

## Position 3 — Functionalism (reject conjectural history)

[[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]] and [[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]] rejected the
premise shared by both prior camps — that the unobservable past of non-literate peoples can be
reconstructed from present-day survivals. In its strongest form: such reconstructions are
**conjectural history**, untestable and idle; institutions must be explained by the functions
they serve in the *present* working of a society, studied through intensive fieldwork. This
position did not adjudicate evolutionism against diffusionism; it declared the whole contest
misconceived.

## Shared Factual Ground, Framed per Side

All sides agreed that cultures resemble and differ, and that both independent development and
diffusion are *possible* processes. Evolutionists foregrounded psychic unity and stage-logic;
diffusionists foregrounded migration and contact; functionalists foregrounded present function
and bracketed origins entirely. Rivers' Oceanic kinship data (see
[[oceanic-relationship-systems]]) were common evidence read three ways: as survivals of
evolutionary stages, as strata of fused peoples, or (functionalist) as a working present-day
system whose history was beside the point.

## Resolution

**Dissolved**, not settled. Functionalism displaced both evolutionism and diffusionism from
the 1920s without resolving which was right — it changed the subject. Hyper-diffusionism
collapsed under its own implausibility; classical evolutionism was already discredited. The
*questions* both older schools asked (the deep history of Oceanic and other populations) were
later reopened on entirely different, evidence-based foundations — historical linguistics,
archaeology, and ancient-DNA population genetics — which can test migration claims Rivers could
only conjecture. What survived from the old debate is method, not doctrine: Rivers'
[[genealogical-method]] and the recognition that both diffusion and independent development are
real.

## Curator's note (2026-07-09)

Rivers is easy to caricature via his hyper-diffusionist followers; the ingested text shows a
more scrupulous, self-critical thinker who ranked his own claims by evidential security and
flagged the risk of circular argument. The lasting verdict is that his *method* was sound and
his *conjectural history* was not — a distinction worth preserving against both hagiography and
demolition.
