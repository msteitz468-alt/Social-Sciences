# Interpreting the Potlatch

What is the [[potlatch]]? The Kwakiutl case built by the [[boas-franz|Boas]]–[[hunt-george|Hunt]] corpus became the type case for incompatible readings.

## Positions

**Boas — a credit and rank system.** In the 1897 account (reproduced in [[boas-kwakiutl-ethnography-1966|Codere's 1966 edition]]): "The underlying principle is that of the interest-bearing investment of property." Names and seats are validated by distributions; loans run at scaled interest; coppers work like high-denomination banknotes; destruction of property is a rivalry move within the system, not its essence. Wealth is desired "not as much [for] the possession... as the ability to give great festivals." Boas records the rivalry idiom as his informants' own: formerly bravery counted, but "nowadays, as the Indians say, 'rivals fight with property only.'"

**Mauss — agonistic total prestation.** In *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* (1925), the potlatch is the agonistic extreme of the gift system — "essentially usurious and extravagant," a total social phenomenon engaging clan, religion, economy, and law at once (see [[potlatch]]).

**Benedict — Dionysian rivalry.** *[[benedict-patterns-of-culture-1934|Patterns of Culture]]* (1934) reads the same corpus as a cultural configuration of self-glorification and shame, "megalomaniac paranoid" in tendency — the potlatch as the institutional core of a rivalry-obsessed ethos (see [[kwakiutl]], [[benedict-patterns-of-culture-study]]).

**Codere — a historical process.** [[codere-helen|Codere]]'s *Fighting with Property* (1950; not yet ingested) argued the agonistic, destructive potlatch of the classic accounts was a post-contact escalation — fur-trade wealth, catastrophic depopulation (to ~1,000 by the 1920s), and the Canadian potlatch ban reshaping the institution — so the famous rivalry potlatch is a dated phenomenon, not a timeless essence. Her 1966 editorial apparatus presses the corollary: the 57-year Boas corpus must be read chronologically, not as a homogeneous ethnographic present.

## Shared factual ground

All positions rest on the same Boas–Hunt documentary base: the blanket-currency and loan mechanics, the copper economy, marriage as privilege transfer, the informant statements framing property contest as a substitute for war ("the white man came and stopped up that stream of blood with wealth. Now we are fighting with our wealth" — speech recorded 1895). The disagreement is about what the institution *is* — economic rationality, total prestation, ethos, or historical conjuncture.
