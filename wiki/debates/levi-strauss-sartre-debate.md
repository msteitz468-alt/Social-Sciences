# Lévi-Strauss vs Sartre

The confrontation that crystallized the mid-century displacement of **existentialism** by **[[structuralism]]** in French thought: [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss's]] closing chapter of *[[levi-strauss-savage-mind-1962|The Savage Mind]]* (1962), "History and Dialectic," is a sustained polemic against Jean-Paul [[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre's]] *Critique of Dialectical Reason* (1960). The material below is recorded from Lévi-Strauss's text; Sartre's positions appear here in Lévi-Strauss's characterization and are stated in attributed voice.

## Position A — dialectical reason and History are privileged (Sartre)

On Lévi-Strauss's account, Sartre grounds anthropology in **dialectical reason** rooted in historical praxis and the (sociologized) Cartesian *cogito*, and gives **History** a near-mystical, privileged status as the medium in which humanity constitutes itself. This, Lévi-Strauss charges, relegates societies "without history" to a "repetitive, short-term dialectic... placed very near biology," a "stunted and deformed" humanity whose full humanity depends on contact with or validation by "historical humanity."

## Position B — reason is continuous; History is one code among others (Lévi-Strauss)

Lévi-Strauss argues:
- **The two reasons are continuous, not opposed.** "The opposition between the two sorts of reason is relative, not absolute... dialectical reason is... the bridge, forever extended and improved, which analytical reason throws out over an abyss." Sartre's alternation between opposing them and identifying them makes his project "contradictory in the one case and superfluous in the other."
- **History is a method, not a privileged object.** "History is a method with no distinct object corresponding to it"; a "historical fact" is constituted by the historian's selection ("there is no history without dates"; "history is never history, but history-for"). It is not tied to man or to any object — "history may lead to anything, provided you get out of it."
- **Valuing History above other codes is ethnocentric.** "The problem raised by the *Critique de la raison dialectique* is reducible to the question: under what conditions is the myth of the French Revolution possible?" To assign "the metaphysical function of Other to the Papuans" is "a sort of intellectual cannibalism much more revolting to the anthropologist than real cannibalism." History is one classification among others (like [[totemism]]); see [[cold-and-hot-societies]].
- **The Cogito is a prison.** "By sociologizing the Cogito, Sartre merely exchanges one prison for another"; "Descartes... separated Man from Society. Sartre... separates his own society from others."
- **The goal is to dissolve, not enshrine, the subject.** "The ultimate goal of the human sciences is not to constitute, but to dissolve man" (quoting Rousseau: "to study man one must learn to look from afar").

Notably, Lévi-Strauss offered a **partial self-critique**: he conceded that the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]* had "unintentionally... lent support to these erroneous ideas" by not distinguishing exchange "as it is expressed... in the praxis of groups" from the conscious rules codifying it — a concession that anticipates the practice-theoretic critique (see [[strategy-vs-rule]]).

## Shared ground

Both take **Marx** as a point of departure and accept that human meaning is never transparent to consciousness. They divide on whether **History/praxis** is the privileged route to that meaning (Sartre) or one code among many that anthropology must relativize (Lévi-Strauss).

## Relation to other disputes

Adjacent to, but not identical with, the [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]: this is specifically a dispute over the **status of historical/dialectical reason** and the humanist subject, a species of the broader structure/agency question. It also prefigures the practice turn's critique of *both* structuralism and subject-philosophy ([[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu]]; [[practice-theory]]).

## Resolution

`dissolved` rather than adjudicated: the exchange became a landmark of the moment structuralism eclipsed existentialism in France, but the two philosophies talked past each other and the "debate" faded as both programs were overtaken by post-structuralism and the practice turn. Its lasting deposit is the question it forced — whether historical consciousness is the truth of the human or one of its many codes. See [[structuralism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]].
