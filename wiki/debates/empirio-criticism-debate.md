# The Empirio-criticism Debate

The 1904–09 dispute inside Russian Marxism over whether the positivist epistemology of Ernst Mach and Richard Avenarius ("empirio-criticism") could be combined with Marxism. It is the formative philosophical controversy of Bolshevism: Lenin's *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]) was written against a bloc of prominent Bolshevik and Menshevik intellectuals, and the reflection epistemology it codified ([[reflection-theory]]) became foundational for later official [[dialectical-materialism]].

## Positions

**Bogdanov — now primary-sourced** ([[bogdanov-empiriomonism-1906]], attributed). His actual doctrine: "the contrast between the physical and the psychical series of experiences boils down to the difference between experience that is socially-organised and experience that is individually organised" — objectivity as social validity, explicitly distinguished from Kantian absolutes; "matter" and "invariable laws" as historically transient organizing forms, "truth for their times... never truth for eternity"; the substitution doctrine; and a *social-genetic* theory of cognition he presented as demanded by "the social materialism of Marx." Crucially, the primary text confirms his self-distancing that Lenin dismissed: empiriocriticism is "a necessary but passing stage," Avenarius is criticized at length (the vital-differential as "psychological Lamarckism"), and the Book Three preface states flatly "I cannot consider myself to be a 'Machist'." On the identity-of-social-being-and-consciousness thesis, the primary text carries his own clarification against Axelrod ("Ortodoks") — the formulation Lenin attacked was, on Bogdanov's account, about ideology's organizing role, not a denial that being outruns consciousness. His side of the Plekhanov exchange (the thing-in-itself "golden mean" as unresolved) is likewise now first-hand.

**The other Russian Machists** (V. Bazarov, A. Lunacharsky, P. Yushkevich, N. Valentinov; the 1908 symposium *Studies in the Philosophy of Marxism*) remain reconstructed mainly from Lenin's quotations: Yushkevich's "empirio-symbolism" (laws of nature as symbols); Lunacharsky's "god-building" conclusions — though Bogdanov's own Book Two dispute *with* Lunacharsky over Avenarius's Affectional theory (primary) already shows the camp was less unified than Lenin's collective portrait implies.

**Lenin** argued the entire current was Berkeleian subjective idealism in modern dress — "complexes of sensations" repeat Berkeley 1710 — collapsing into solipsism if consistent and into fideism in social effect ("graduated flunkeys of fideism," adopting Dietzgen's phrase). His counter-doctrine: matter as objective reality given in sensation; knowledge as reflection tested by practice; absolute truth compounded of relative truths; philosophy as a struggle of two parties (materialism vs. idealism) in which "non-partisan" middle positions always slide toward idealism. He read the "crisis in physics" ("matter has disappeared") as epistemological, not physical, coining "physical idealism" for the Mach–Poincaré–Pearson–Duhem school.

**Mach and Avenarius themselves** (with disciples Petzoldt, Willy, Carstanjen, Kleinpeter, Cornelius) held the elements doctrine as an anti-metaphysical unification of physics and psychology; their school's own splits — over the pre-human earth (Avenarius's "potential central term," Petzoldt's a priori "unique determination," Willy's "worm" argument) — were exhibited by Lenin as reductio evidence.

**Plekhanov — now primary-sourced** ([[plekhanov-materialismus-militans-1908]], attributed). His own three-letter polemic against Bogdanov (1908–10): Bogdanov stands "outside Marxism" by definition, since Marxism rests on dialectical materialism — Plekhanov refuses him the title "comrade"; Mach oscillates self-contradictorily between materialist naturalist and idealist philosopher; Bogdanov's objectivity-as-socially-organized-experience falls to the hobgoblin/witch-trial reductio (social coordination cannot constitute objectivity); and Bogdanov's claimed independence from Mach is rejected — the same contested point Bogdanov's own text asserts and Lenin's dismisses, now documented from all three principals. Crucially, Plekhanov defends his **hieroglyph** (sign, not copy) theory of sensation, traced to Sechenov, with his own 1905/06 admission that the terminology was "ambiguous" — the doctrine Lenin's M&E called "an obvious mistake" (recorded in this edition's own apparatus): the two chief anti-Machists prosecuted the same enemy with *different* epistemologies ([[reflection-theory]]). Lenin's further correction of Plekhanov's Carstanjen misreading is in [[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]. A dating nuance from his own 1908 *Fundamental Problems* ([[plekhanov-fundamental-problems-of-marxism-1908]], attributed): Plekhanov's final systematic work, published at the fight's peak, contains only a brief protest against giving Marxism "a new basis" (neo-Kantianism, Machism) — the extended anti-Bogdanov framing in that volume is Riazanov's 1928 editorial preface, not Plekhanov's text. His fuller anti-Machist polemics (the *Materialismus Militans* letters) remain un-ingested.

## Shared ground

All parties claimed fidelity to Marx and to modern science; the dispute was over what the new physics licensed epistemologically, and over who owned the Marxist inheritance — with an organizational subtext (Bogdanov was Lenin's chief rival inside the Bolshevik faction; the editorial apparatus of the 1909/1920 editions frames the book against Bogdanov's "proletarian culture" movement as well).

## Resolution status

`dissolved` — the dispute was not settled philosophically but ended politically: Bogdanov's group was expelled from the Bolshevik centre (1909), and after 1917 Lenin's position became state doctrine, removing the debate from the field rather than resolving it. Philosophy of science outside the Soviet orbit largely developed along lines closer to the Machist sensibility (conventionalism, logical positivism) without engaging this exchange; that afterlife is not yet documented from ingested sources (as of 2026-07-16).

## Bogdanov's later answer (primary, attributed)

With [[bogdanov-essays-in-tektology-1980]] and full Book 1 ([[bogdanov-tektology-book1-1996]]) ingested, Bogdanov's post-1909 trajectory is primary-documented: [[tektology]] restates his monism as a "universal organizational science" rather than a philosophy, argues *against* Kantian subjectivism about unifying schemes (organizing "forms" arise from historically changing modes of organizing experience, constrained by "the structure of things" — complicating Lenin's subjectivist reading), and openly declares dialectics an "insufficiently dynamic" embryo of tektology. Book 1 Addendum 3 replies directly to Nevsky's essay (appended to Lenin's book 2nd ed.) — empty schema, barbarian terms, Machist idealism via elements/complexes — by distinguishing ordinary scientific element/complex analysis from Mach's sensual decomposition and by denying "gnoseology" as scholasticism. Gorelik's apparatus (secondary, attributed) holds that Lenin's polemic caused tektology's neglect by making critics unable to distinguish "Bogdanov the philosopher" from "Bogdanov the scientist."

## Retrospective stratum: Lenin's post-1909 reading notes (attributed — [[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]])

Lenin's private notebooks show him continuing to track the Mach/Avenarius literature well after the 1908 polemic, not opening a new position. Bibliographic jottings from 1912–16 record his reading of F. Raab's *Die Philosophie von R. Avenarius* (Leipzig, 1912) and a Suter dissertation on Avenarius (1910), alongside a note on A. Aliotta's *La reazione idealistica contro la scienza* (1912, read via a Segond review) surveying the wider "idealist reaction" — German and French neo-criticism, Mach/Avenarius empirio-criticism, neo-Hegelianism, Bergsonian intuitionism, and Anglo-American pragmatism — as a connected front. On a quoted Machian analogy comparing the "ego" (as focus of sensation-threads) to money as "the focal point in the network of social connections," Lenin's marginal comment was blunt: "muddle... incorrect... not 'in the same way'... incorrect." Separately, conspecting Hegel's account of Semblance (Schein), he wrote the marginal aside "cf. Machism!!," registering a perceived structural parallel between Hegel's Semblance and Machist "elements." These are reading annotations continuous with the 1908 argument, not a revised philosophical position; none were published in Lenin's lifetime.

## Reflexivity

Both principals are now primary-sourced: Lenin's polemic ([[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]]) and Bogdanov's *Empiriomonism* itself ([[bogdanov-empiriomonism-1906]], Rowley trans.), plus Bogdanov's later system ([[bogdanov-essays-in-tektology-1980]]; [[bogdanov-tektology-book1-1996]]). Mach's and Avenarius's own texts, and the minor Russian Machists, are still reconstructed from the principals' quotations. Where Lenin's rendering and Bogdanov's text diverge (the "Machist" label; the identity thesis's meaning), both readings are recorded above — the divergence is itself part of the debate's record.
