# Smash or Capture the State Debate

## Shared ground

What should a socialist or proletarian movement **do** with the existing [[the-state|state]]? The positions below largely agree that modern states concentrate coercive power and that class structure matters to politics; they disagree on whether that apparatus can be inherited, must be destroyed and replaced, or must be abolished without replacement. Each position in strongest form; **no wiki-voice verdict.**

Primary ingest anchoring this page: [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]], *[[lenin-state-and-revolution-1917|State and Revolution]]* (1917), and — as of 2026-07-16 — Kautsky's own *[[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918|The Dictatorship of the Proletariat]]* (1918), so position 2 is now stated from its author's text. Bernstein and anarchist primary texts remain un-ingested.

## Positions

### 0. The pre-history — Plekhanov (1885) against conspiratorial seizure

Before the smash/capture dispute crystallized, [[plekhanov-our-differences-1885|Our Differences]] (attributed) stated the founding Russian Marxist position on revolutionary power: against Tkachov-Tikhomirov Blanquism, class self-emancipation over secret-society seizure — commune disintegration "cannot be stopped by any 'seizures of power' by any secret society" — and the "Peruvian despotism" warning that a minority-seizure socialism would produce paternalist despotism rather than emancipation. Every later position in this debate (Lenin's insurrection-with-majority doctrine, Kautsky's Bonapartism/Jesuitism argument, the Menshevik two-stage orthodoxy) descends from or reacts against this 1885 statement — including Plekhanov's own 1917 use of it against Lenin.

### 1. Smash and replace — Lenin (1917), reading Marx

[[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] argues that Marx’s lesson of 1848–51 and of the Paris Commune is that the working class cannot simply lay hold of the ready-made state machinery: it must **[[smash-the-state-machine|smash]]** the bureaucratic-military machine and replace it with a [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] (Commune-type organs: armed people, recallable officials at workers’ wages, working body). The bourgeois democratic republic remains class dictatorship of the bourgeoisie. The proletarian state then [[withering-away-of-the-state|withers]] after class abolition. Recognition of class struggle without the dictatorship of the proletariat is treated as incomplete Marxism.

**Strongest form:** Only destruction of the separated armed and bureaucratic apparatus can prevent the new rulers from being captured by the old machine; the Commune proved both the necessity of smash and the outline of the replacement form.

### 2. Capture / use the democratic state — Second International reformism (as opponent)

As Lenin constructs his target (especially Karl **Kautsky** and reformist Social Democracy): the existing state can be taken over or gradually democratized; emphasis falls on parliamentary majorities, mass parties, and administrative continuity; “smash” is rejected or postponed; dictatorship of the proletariat is redefined, muted, or left to the future. Lenin’s specific charges (attributed to his reading of Kautsky’s anti-Bernstein work, *The Social Revolution*, *The Road to Power*, and the 1912 clash with Pannekoek): Kautsky admits the proletariat will take power but slurs over *destruction* of the machine; treats the question of forms as secondary; when pressed, opposes the idea that the victorious proletariat must smash the state machine.

**Strongest form, now from Kautsky's own text** ([[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918]], attributed). "No Socialism without democracy": democracy is not a bourgeois shell but the school in which the proletariat attains the "maturity" socialism requires, and the only reliable gauge of relative class strength. Marx's 1875 "dictatorship of the proletariat" names *a condition, not a form of government* — the Paris Commune, which Engels expressly called the dictatorship of the proletariat, rested on universal suffrage; "Marx must not be cited by those who support dictatorship in preference to democracy." As a form of government, class dictatorship is impossible (a class can rule but not govern); it becomes dictatorship of a party, then of a fraction — in peasant Russia, either a *peasants'* dictatorship or rule by a shrinking, exclusionary minority. Minority power held against the majority must become "Jesuitism" or "Bonapartism"; civil war, its inevitable by-product, is socialist construction's "deadly foe"; and the suppression of press and opposition creates an information vacuum in which even the government cannot verify its own decrees' operation. Parliament — not soviets restricted by class franchise — is the only organized body able to control a modern bureaucracy. Disfranchising capitalists is either superfluous (if they are a tiny minority, universal suffrage exposes it) or futile (if they are not, exclusion from voting does not touch their social power). Kautsky's verdict on Russia: "the last of middle-class, and not the first of Socialist revolutions."

**Lenin's 1918 direct reply — *[[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918|The Proletarian Revolution and the Renegade Kautsky]]* (attributed).** Lenin frames Kautsky's post-Soviet-Constitution critique — that disfranchising capitalists is "arbitrary" and undemocratic — as itself evidence of position 2's core move: judging the transition by formal-legal, cross-class democratic criteria rather than by which class the state serves. He counters that Kautsky's own 1907 German occupational statistics (35 million wage-earners/salaried vs. 17 million "independents") undercut Kautsky's claim that a workers' majority party could still be a population minority, and that Kautsky wrongly lumped all "independents" (including non-labor-hiring Russian peasants, who retained the franchise) with the disfranchised — which Lenin calls "the counter-revolutionary whining of a bourgeois." He credits Marx with attaching "enormous significance to smashing the bureaucratic machine" and faults Kautsky for ignoring bourgeois lawyers and bureaucrats as a distinct class interest — restating position 1's core charge (that position 2 mistakes administrative continuity for neutrality) with a named, now-primary-sourced opponent.

### 3. Immediate abolition — anarchism (as framed in the dispute)

No transitional state: political authority is destroyed or “blown up” without a proletarian dictatorship. Engels (*On Authority*, via Lenin) counters that revolution is “the most authoritarian thing there is,” that technical and social life require some authority, and that the Commune can be blamed for too little use of armed authority. Lenin claims Marxists share hostility to the *bourgeois* state with anarchists but reject abolition of the *proletarian* transitional state. Anarchist primary dossier **not yet ingested**.

### 4. Structural counterpoint — revolution builds the state (historical sociology)

Separate research question: regardless of revolutionary ideology, successful [[social-revolution|social revolutions]] have often produced more centralized, bureaucratic states (e.g. Skocpol’s comparative argument in *[[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-1979|States and Social Revolutions]]*). This neither proves nor refutes Lenin’s *normative* programme; it challenges empirical expectations that “smash” yields lasting anti-statism. See [[state-autonomy]], [[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-study]].

### 2b. Democracy and humanity against revolutionary terrorism — Kautsky (1919)

From Kautsky's own *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919|Terrorism and Communism]]* (attributed), beyond the 1918 *Dictatorship* pamphlet:

- **Terrorism is not the essence of revolution** — popular myth; planned state terror fails (Jacobin sequence → Thermidor; cadre depletion; mass apathy).
- **1871 Commune ≠ Soviet license** — Commune not a Soviet Republic; seated opposition; almost no terror practice; DoP as "government of the people through the people" = democracy; Marx/Engels cited against terror.
- **Civilisation thesis** — 19th-c. proletarian humanisation drops terror from the socialist programme; world war reversed that; adapt power to conditions, do not socialise-by-terror in undeveloped Russia.
- **Soviet methods** — expropriation without organisation; free-press abolition fosters corruption; despotism is the essence of dictatorship; White terror does not justify Red; hostages of Mensheviks/Left SRs.
- **Closing** — world revolution only through democracy and humanity, not cannons and destruction of political adversaries.

**Strongest form:** Socialist construction requires democratic majority compulsion and open criticism; minority dictatorship and planned terror destroy the moral and economic preconditions of socialism.

### 1b. Smash guaranteed by terror and party dictatorship — Trotsky (1920)

[[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]]'s *[[trotsky-terrorism-and-communism-1920|Terrorism and Communism]]* (1920) is the civil-war *practitioner's* restatement of position 1, aimed at Kautsky rather than at anarchists. Core moves (all attributed):

- **Against the "balance of power" / 51% fetish:** Parliamentary majorities and "balance of power" talk are opportunistic formulae that lag behind the collapse of productive forces and the imperialist war; the revolutionary party is a colossal historical factor at psychological turning points; waiting for a trustworthy democratic majority repudiates the dictatorship and Marxism.
- **Democracy as class form:** "Either democracy or civil war" is a false binary; formal democracy is a natural-law metaphysics that both protests and consoles; even a proletarian parliamentary majority would not abolish the real determinants of civil war; the Constituent Assembly was not an arbitrary break but was swept aside by imperialism and war once soviets had already "constituted" power.
- **[[red-terror]] as means of dictatorship:** Who aims at the end cannot reject the means; White Guards can only be terrorized or crushed; principled repudiation of terrorism is repudiation of proletarian supremacy; hostages, executions, and press suppression are revolutionary self-defence, with Marx's Commune writings enlisted against Kautsky.
- **Party and soviets:** "The dictatorship of the soviets became possible only by means of the dictatorship of the party"; this is not accidental "substitution" but expression of the class's fundamental interests by its Communist vanguard; Mensheviks and SRs "have disappeared" after suppression — a material criterion of line.
- **Labour front:** The same authoritarian state principle extends to [[militarization-of-labour]] — the road to socialism as highest intensification of the state before withering.

**Strongest form:** In an epoch of imperialist collapse and civil war, formal democracy is the enemy's weapon; proletarian power must create its majority dynamically and break the enemy's will by all forms of violence, including systematic terror and compulsory labour organization.

### 5. The live 1917–18 application — Soviets vs. the Constituent Assembly

Beyond the general theoretical dispute, the debate had an immediate institutional stake in Russia: whether Soviet power should give way to, share power with, or dissolve the Constituent Assembly elected on pre-October party lists (attributed; [[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]). Lenin's "Theses on the Constituent Assembly" (Pravda, Dec 26, 1917) held that "the interests of this revolution stand higher than the formal rights of the Constituent Assembly," and warned that "a few Bolshevik leaders" had strayed toward treating the Assembly's standing as a matter of formal legal right — an internal echo of position 2 inside the Bolshevik party itself. When the Assembly's Right-SR majority (met Jan 5, 1918) refused to recognize the Declaration of Rights of the Working and Exploited People or Soviet power, the Bolshevik/Left-SR delegations withdrew, calling the majority "in fact a bourgeois and counter-revolutionary party," and the CEC decree of Jan 6/19, 1918 declared the Assembly "hereby dissolved" as a "screen for the struggle of the counter-revolutionaries to overthrow Soviet power." Lenin contrasted the Taurida Palace (site of the Assembly) as "moribund bourgeois parliamentarism" against the Smolny Institute's "plain, proletarian Soviet apparatus." This episode is the debate's clearest historical test case of "smash" applied not to the bourgeois state in the abstract but to a still-live rival institution claiming democratic legitimacy — record the SR/Menshevik objection to forcible dissolution as the position-2-adjacent counter-position, not yet primary-sourced from their side. Trotsky 1920 (above) restates the dissolution as historically inevitable under imperialism and war, and denies that civil war conditions allow a wartime demand for new Constituent elections.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

| Fact-like ground | How sides frame it |
|---|---|
| Marx wrote that the next revolution must break the bureaucratic-military machine (*Brumaire*; Commune writings) | Lenin: binding general lesson. Reformists: context-bound or secondary to democratic capture. |
| Paris Commune (1871) used recallable officials and abolished the standing army | Lenin: model. Reformists: heroic failure, not template. Anarchists: still too state-like / too little force (Engels’s own ambivalence). |
| Second International parties entered wartime governments 1914 | Lenin: social-chauvinist betrayal. Defenders: national democratic responsibility (primary sources pending). |
| Post-1917 Soviet state did not wither in the short or medium run | Critics: falsification of withering/DoP democracy. Defenders: civil war, isolation, backwardness blocked antecedents of the theory. |
| Pannekoek (1912) argued destruction of the state machine; Kautsky opposed | Lenin: unmasks Kautsky. Kautsky (1918, own text): parliament is the only organized check on bureaucracy; destroying it hands power to an uncontrollable apparatus. |
| Constituent Assembly dissolved Jan 1918 | Lenin: revolution's interests above formal rights. Kautsky (own text): even the Bolsheviks' Nov 1917 decree named their government provisional "pending the Constituent Assembly"; Lenin's invalid-election argument then flipped into rejecting *any* assembly by universal suffrage — evidence the dictatorship narrowed from class to party to fraction. |
