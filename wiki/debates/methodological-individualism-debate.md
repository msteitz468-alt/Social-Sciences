# Methodological individualism vs. social realism

## Shared ground

How should social science explain collective patterns — rates, institutions, norms, structures? Parties agree that individuals act and that societies exhibit regularities; they disagree on **whether those regularities constitute an irreducible explanatory level** and on **what counts as an adequate explanation**. This page records positions from primary sources as ingested; no wiki-voice adjudication.

## Positions (strongest form each)

### 1. Durkheimian social realism — *Rules*, *Suicide*, *Division of Labor*

[[durkheim-emile|Durkheim]] argues that [[social-fact|social facts]] are ways of acting, thinking, and feeling **external** to the individual and endowed with **coercive power**; they include morphological “ways of being.” Sociology’s object is this order of facts; if only individuals existed, sociology would collapse into psychology (*[[durkheim-rules-of-sociological-method-1895|Rules]]* Ch. I–II; *Suicide* Preface). Method: [[treat-social-facts-as-things|treat social facts as things]]; explain a social fact by **prior social facts**, especially the constitution of the **inner social environment** ([[social-morphology]], [[dynamic-density]]), not by individual motives or utility alone (Ch. V). Function and efficient cause must be investigated separately. In the 1901 Preface he denies a substantial collective soul while maintaining the *sui generis* character of collective phenomena (attributed).

### 2. Methodological individualism (as rival pole)

The opposing program (named later as “methodological individualism”; classical rivals in Durkheim’s field include psychological and utilitarian reductions and imitation theories such as Tarde’s) holds that **adequate explanations of social phenomena terminate in individuals** — their beliefs, desires, actions, and relations. Collective terms are summaries or outcomes of individual-level processes; “society” is not an additional causal substance. (Recorded as the **structural opposite** Durkheim writes against; full primary ingest of canonical individualist manifestos still thin in this wiki.)

### 3. Emergence / non-reductive dependence (later bridge)

A later analytic line (flagged by Lukes’s 2013 Introduction as a live framing — secondary) treats social properties as **dependent on** individuals yet **not eliminable** into individual descriptions (emergence). This is a **position in the afterlife of the debate**, not Durkheim’s own vocabulary.

### 4. Interpretive / action-theoretic starting point — Weberian strand

[[weber-max|Weber]]’s [[interpretive-sociology]] starts from subjectively meaningful [[social-action]] and [[verstehen]], without Durkheim’s exterior “thing” rule as the first commandment. Not pure psychological reduction (ideal types of action and Herrschaft are sociological), but a different entry point — see [[weber-interpretive-sociology-debate]].


### 5. Neither structural sociology nor strong MI — Giddens (*Constitution*, 1984)

[[giddens-anthony|Giddens]] (*[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]*, Ch. 4 Critical Notes) argues that “structural sociology” and methodological individualism **are not alternatives** such that to reject one is to accept the other. Only corporeal individuals are agents; collectivities are not. But “what the individual is” cannot be taken as obvious — it requires the stratification model of the agent ([[discursive-consciousness]], [[practical-consciousness]], unconscious) and the [[duality-of-structure]]. Structure must not be equated with constraint; history is the temporality of human practices fashioning and fashioned by structural properties. See [[structuration-theory]]; [[structure-vs-agency-debate]].

| Text / episode | Role |
|---|---|
| *Rules* (1895/1901) | Codifies social-fact definition, treat-as-things, social explanation |

## Resolution status

`open` — core dispute of social science method; vocabularies (emergence, multilevel models, mechanisms) shift, but the Durkheimian non-reduction claim and individualist reduction claim remain live poles.
