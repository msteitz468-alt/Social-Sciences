# Unilinear Kinship Evolution Debate

Dispute over whether human kinship and political organization passed through a **single universal sequence** of forms — as in [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]]’s and [[engels-friedrich|Engels]]’s schemes — or whether kinship systems are historically particular, multilinear, or otherwise not stages of one ladder.

`resolution_status: resolved-by-consensus` refers to the **abandonment of the classical unilinear ladder as literal world history** in mainstream anthropology; it does **not** mean every subordinate claim (e.g. links between property and gender hierarchy) is dead. Record positions; do not sneer.

## Position 0 — Maine patriarchal theory (pre-Morgan charter)

Holders: [[maine-henry-sumner|Henry Sumner Maine]] (*[[maine-ancient-law-1861|Ancient Law]]*, 1861).

Claims (strongest form): comparative jurisprudence establishes the **[[patriarchal-theory|Patriarchal Theory]]** — primeval society organized under absolute power of the eldest male ascendant; unit of ancient society is the **Family**, of modern society the **Individual**; ascending Family → Gens → Tribe → Commonwealth; progressive societies move **[[status-to-contract|from Status to Contract]]**. Evidence chiefly Roman, Hindu, Slavonic legal antiquities. Everyman intro already notes M’Lennan–[[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]] matriarchal critiques of this position.

## Position 1 — Morgan–Engels unilinear sequence

Holders: Morgan; Engels (*[[engels-origin-of-the-family-1884|Origin of the Family]]*); later Marxist orthodoxy.

Claims (strongest form): classificatory kinship systems preserve evidence of prior marriage customs including [[group-marriage]]/[[punalua]]; [[descriptive-system-of-relationship|descriptive]] systems follow pair marriage; [[mother-right]] precedes father-right in gentile society; the [[gens]] was the universal pre-state form; [[monogamy]] and the [[the-state|state]] arise with [[private-property]] and civilization ([[savagery-barbarism-civilization]]).

**From *[[morgan-systems-consanguinity-1871|Systems]]* primary (1871):** two radical systems; conjectural custom series (promiscuity → communal family → Hawaiian custom → tribal reform → pair marriage → property/civilized family); Malayan → Turanian → Ganowanian stage ordering of classificatory forms; Asiatic origin of American Indians from Turanian–Ganowanian identity. **From Engels:** historical-materialist mechanisms (production, property, class) totalizing Morgan’s ladder via *Ancient Society*. **Inverts Maine’s patriarchal priority** on the mother-right step.

## Position 2 — Victorian rivals

Holders: Bachofen (religious lever for mother-right); McLennan (exogamy/endogamy, bride-capture, polyandry focus).

Claims: alternative universal or near-universal schemes competing with Morgan (and against Maine’s patriarchal priority) on the same evolutionist terrain. Engels’s 1891 preface narrates this as a research history culminating in Morgan.

## Position 3 — Particularist / anti-stage rejection

Holders: Boasian tradition and much twentieth-century social anthropology.

Claims: no universal group-marriage stage; kinship terms do not decode a single past; matriliny ≠ primitive universal; each society’s kinship has its own history; “savagery/barbarism” vocabulary is ethnocentric.

**From *[[lowie-primitive-society-1920|Primitive Society]]* (Lowie 1920; 1947 reissue) — full social-organization charter of this position:** (1) “mottled diversity” of family, [[sib]], associations, rank, and territory replaces Morgan’s single sib-pattern ladder; (2) bilateral individual family is universal for the ethnographic present and chronologically prior to the sib; sibless simpler cultures are common; (3) no world-historical promiscuity/[[group-marriage]] stage and no true matriarchate; mother-sib not necessary prior to father-sib; (4) independent development occurs but stage-series laws are formally abjured; diffusion “lays the axe to the root” of historical laws; (5) social forms are incommensurable for objective progress ranking (“mock-law of social evolution”). See [[lowie-primitive-society-study]] · [[hubs/studies/lowie-primitive-society-study]] · [[shreds-and-patches]] · [[historical-particularism]].

## Position 4 — Multilinear / limited-sequence revision

Holders: mid-20th-c. neo-evolutionists and comparative anthropologists (details as sources arrive).

Claims: some recurrent pathways (e.g. state formation correlates) without one kinship ladder for all humanity.

## Position 5 — Feminist partial retention

Holders: various Marxist and feminist theorists (as ingested later).

Claims: Engels’s insight that monogamy, property, and women’s oppression co-vary can be kept as a research hypothesis without Morgan’s full prehistoric sequence.

## Stocking's account (Victorian Anthropology, 1987)

*(Attributed — [[stocking-george-w|Stocking]] 1987.)* Stocking's narrative deepens Position 2. [[mclennan-john-ferguson|McLennan]]'s scheme in *Primitive Marriage* (1865) ran promiscuity → polyandry → matriliny → patriliny/monogamy, with polyandry explained as a consequence of female infanticide creating a male demographic surplus, and exogamy (McLennan's own coinage) derived from marriage by capture — itself read as a symbolic residue of that same scarcity of women. Stocking records that this sequence was contested from the outset by figures within the evolutionist camp itself, not only by later critics: [[darwin-charles|Darwin]] and, later, Edward Westermarck argued against primitive promiscuity on grounds of sexual jealousy observed among primates, favoring an original patriarchal or pair-bonded condition instead; [[maine-henry-sumner|Maine]] made the same argument from the jurisprudential side, holding his own patriarchal theory better fit the biological evidence. McLennan defended his sequence by insisting animal-behavior data could not be directly transposed to human inquiry ("the inquiry is, remember, a human one").

Stocking's own retrospective verdict (attributed, not adopted here as wiki voice) is unusually blunt: he judges that the whole apparatus of universal evolutionary stages built on "primitive promiscuity," "matriarchy," female infanticide, marriage by capture, and polyandry was "called into question very quickly after their formulation and now seems perhaps to have been ... largely a fantasy of the Victorian male anthropological imagination." He also complicates a purely ideological reading of the matriarchy debate (that it was engineered to make patriarchy evolution's goal rather than its origin once Maine's timeless-patriarchy argument failed): Bachofen's *Mutterrecht* (1861) was independent of, not a response to, Maine, and Maine himself treated growing legal equality for women as a genuine "law of development." See [[morgan-mclennan-debate]] for the parallel priority dispute between Maine and McLennan.

## Shared factual ground (framed per side)

- Kinship terminologies vary systematically and were first mapped at scale by Morgan.
- Matrilineal systems exist and are well documented (including Haudenosaunee/[[iroquois]] cases).
- States are not universal; stateless societies exist.
- What these facts **mean** for deep human history is exactly the dispute.
