# Generation vs. Verification of Theory

A methodological dispute over the **primacy of purpose** in sociological research: whether the discipline's canons and prestige structure properly center **testing** existing theory or **discovering** theory from data. Framed programmatically by [[glaser-barney-g|Glaser]] and [[strauss-anselm-l|Strauss]] in *[[glaser-strauss-discovery-grounded-theory-1967|The Discovery of Grounded Theory]]* (1967); related to but not identical with Mills's two-front attack in the [[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]].

No adjudication in wiki voice.

## Position 1 — Glaser & Strauss: restore generation

Glaser and Strauss argued (attributed) that postwar sociology concentrated on improving methods for **verifying** theory while "grand theory" and logico-deductive schemes remained influential, so many researchers equated "theory" with a priori systems of little research relevance. Merton, they claimed, reached grounded *modification* of theory via serendipity but not purposeful **discovery** of grounded theory. Blumer, they argued, diagnosed the theory–data gap but threw weight toward verification problems rather than specifying generation procedures.

They do not deny verification's necessity; they deny its exclusive primacy and call for canons of sampling, coding, credibility, and presentation suited to **discovery**. Comparative analysis, [[theoretical-sampling]], and the [[constant-comparative-method]] are their procedural answer. Fit and work reverse the speculative license: theory should fit the data. The Epilogue attacks Merton's 1964 capsule (theory outrunning research; qualitative demoted; speculative generation illustrated by apt cases) as the position they mean to overcome.

## Position 2 — Verification-first tradition

Stated as the foil reconstructed from *Discovery*'s polemic (primary rebuttals not yet ingested): science advances by stating hypotheses clearly and testing them with accurate indicators, sampling, reliability, and significance tests; generation without such canons is exploratory at best and risks impressionism. Quantitative verification methods are the gold standard for "proof." Flexible qualitative work is labelled unsystematic; flexible quantitative work, sloppy. Merton's 1964 pages (as quoted by G&S) associate systematic empirical investigation with quantitative designs (e.g., panel analysis for social process) and contrast the sociologist with the "literary observer."

## Position 3 — Mills's adjacent third way

[[mills-c-wright|Mills]] (*[[mills-sociological-imagination-1959|Sociological Imagination]]*, 1959) attacked both [[grand-theory]] and [[abstracted-empiricism]] as withdrawals from classic craft. Overlaps the generation pole (theory should engage empirical problems) without prescribing GT procedures. See [[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]].

## Position 4 — Tavory & Timmermans: abductive generation (2014)

[[tavory-iddo|Tavory]] and [[timmermans-stefan|Timmermans]] (*[[tavory-timmermans-abductive-analysis-2014|Abductive Analysis]]*) argue (attributed) that the generation–verification debate was held hostage by a Popperian firewall between discovery and justification and by a false choice between **induction** ([[grounded-theory]]) and **favorite-theory deduction** ([[extended-case-method]]). Peircean [[abduction]] is the logic that introduces new ideas; induction only strengthens existing framings; deduction tests what is already assumed. They further argue that even when GT practitioners privately admit theory-ladenness, research design and coding packages still reify process and sideline theory — “abductive GT” rebranding is insufficient. Generation requires cultivated surprise against **multiple** existing theories, method-driven defamiliarization, systematic variation, [[consequences-in-action]], and a [[community-of-inquiry]]. Program page: [[abductive-analysis]].

## Shared factual ground, framed per side

Parties agree that mid-century American sociology invested heavily in verification methodology and that a theory–research gap was widely diagnosed (Blumer, Merton, Mills). They disagree on whether flexible generation methods can be rigorous, whether qualitative data can be "systematic," and how theory should enter research design (a priori scheme vs emerging from data vs multiple theories as abduction background). Position 4 shares Position 1’s generation priority but rejects tabula-rasa induction.

## Current standing

`open` — qualitative methodology widely institutionalised generation language (GT's success is itself a reception fact); hypothesis-testing remains core in quantitative traditions. Later GT internal debates and the abductive-analysis program further complexify Position 1.
