# Race, Genomics, and Average Population Differences

Whether human genetic variation supports any biologically meaningful notion of
"race," and how science should treat the possibility of **average** genetic
differences among populations in complex traits (including cognition and
behavior). The dispute is simultaneously **empirical** (what does the genome
show?), **conceptual** (is "race" or "ancestry" the right vocabulary?), and
**ethical/normative** (what should be studied, published, and inferred?). It
resurfaced sharply with the ancient-DNA revolution and with
[[reich-who-we-are-2018|Reich (2018)]], ch. 11, "The Genomics of Race and
Identity." Positions are recorded here in their strongest form; this page does
not adjudicate.

## Position A — Social construction / no biological race

Race is a social classification with no biological reality. **Ashley Montagu**
(*Man's Most Dangerous Myth: The Fallacy of Race*, 1942) set this tone.
**Richard Lewontin** (1972) partitioned protein-variation across seven "races"
and found ~85% of variation lies *within* populations and only ~15% *among*
them, concluding that racial classification is "of virtually no genetic or
taxonomic significance." **Frank Livingstone**: "there are no races, there are
only clines." Held across mainstream anthropology and much of genetics; the
inconsistent, nation-specific definitions of a category like "black" are offered
as evidence that race lacks biological coherence.

**[[cavalli-sforza-luigi-luca|Cavalli-Sforza]] (2000, ch. 1)** states a closely
allied geneticist position from inside population genetics (not identical to
Montagu’s cultural constructionism): the main genetic differences are between
*individuals*, not between “races”; between-population differences are small and
(for visible traits) mostly climate responses; pure races are an illusion from
surface traits; continuous variation and isolation-by-distance make traditional
boundaries arbitrary; “the variation between two random individuals within any
one population is 85 percent as large as that between two individuals randomly
selected from the world’s population”; therefore “abandon any attempt at racial
classification along the traditional lines.” He still allows practical ancestry
uses (disease, drugs) and endogamy-unit micro-variation without reinstating race
typology. Racism he defines genetically as belief in biological superiority of a
race (glossing Lévi-Strauss), distinct from broader cultural intolerance.

## Position B — Ancestry realism (prepare for average differences)

Associated here with **Reich (2018)**. Its strongest form: the Lewontin
consensus is correct that (i) most variation is within groups and (ii) no single
trait cleanly separates populations — but it has "morphed" into an *orthodoxy*
that average biological differences are so modest they can be ignored and should
not be studied. Reich argues (attributed): nontrivial average genetic
differences across populations already exist for many traits (pigmentation,
bodily dimensions, starch/lactose digestion, altitude adaptation, disease risk);
traits governed by many mutations (height; probably cognition/behaviour) can
develop large average differences under natural selection just as single-gene
traits can (citing selection on height, infant head size, and educational-
attainment polygenic scores); given divergence times "far from negligible," it
is "a bad bet" to assume no average cognitive/behavioural differences exist; and
refusing to build "a rational framework for discussing human differences" cedes
the ground to pseudoscience. Reich holds that "ancestry" is a precise vocabulary,
not a race euphemism, and — crucially — that acknowledging average differences
does **not** license inferring their content from racial stereotypes, and must
not weaken the commitment to equal rights (his analogy: accepted male–female
biological differences coexisting with equal-rights aspirations).

## Position C — Hereditarian racial-stereotype claims

The view that substantial differences exist **and correspond to traditional
racial stereotypes**. **Nicholas Wade** (*A Troublesome Inheritance*, 2014):
enduring civilizational institutions "rest on a bedrock of genetically shaped
human behavior." **Gregory Cochran, Jason Hardy & Henry Harpending** (2006):
Ashkenazi Jewish IQ as a product of medieval occupational selection.
**James Watson**: public and private assertions of genetically-based racial
intelligence differences. Reich (2018) records these at length in order to
reject them: he states they have "no merit" and "no scientific evidence," calls
the Cochran–Hardy–Harpending selection story contradicted by a founder-bottleneck
explanation (see [[founder-event]]), and labels the jump from "differences are
plausible" to "I know what they are and they match stereotypes" as what makes
such claims racist. This position is included as a recorded stance, not endorsed.

## Position D — Precautionary / restrict group-genetic claims

That the risks of studying group genetic differences outweigh the benefits and
warrant restraint or restriction. **Duana Fullwiley**: geneticists' "admixture
technology" and "ancestry" language is a euphemistic reversion to biological
race. **Jacqueline Stevens**: proposed prohibiting publication of claims tying
genetics to race/ethnicity/nationality absent authorization by a standing
committee. Also the anthropologists who challenged Reich's disease-mapping work
as "flirting with racism." The concern: such work sits on a slippery slope to the
historical misuses (the slave trade, eugenic sterilization, the Holocaust).

## Shared factual ground, framed per side

All sides accept Lewontin's arithmetic (most variation within groups) and that
race categories are historically loaded and nation-specific. They divide on what
follows: Position A reads it as dissolving biological race; Position B reads it as
compatible with real average differences that science must be free to study;
Position C reads it as cover for stereotype-confirming claims; Position D reads
the whole terrain as too dangerous to traverse. A second shared fact, emphasized
by Reich, cuts against biological nationalism of every stripe: ancient DNA shows
present populations are recent products of **repeated mixture** (see
[[admixture]]), so no population is "pure" — dismantling the Nazi "Aryan"/Corded
Ware myth, Hindutva claims of no external contribution to India, and the
West-Eurasian-ancestry rationale invoked in the Rwandan genocide.

## Curator's note (2026-07-10)

Reich's argument, adapted into a March 2018 *New York Times* op-ed, drew open
letters from groups of geneticists and social scientists disputing his framing of
"average differences." The empirical facts Reich reports (specific polygenic-score
and selection studies) flow to the relevant pages in attributed, date-stamped
voice; his **normative thesis** — that science should expect and prepare for
average cognitive/behavioural differences — is recorded here as Position B, not
asserted in wiki voice. The wiki takes no side on whether Position B's forecast
is correct; it notes only that Positions A–D disagree about evidence, framing, and
prudence, and that Position C's specific stereotype-matching claims are, on
Reich's own account and that of his critics, unsupported by data.
