# The Russian Question

The defining theoretical dispute of the Trotskyist movement, 1937–40, and a genuine problem in political sociology: how to classify a society with abolished private capital but extreme political domination. Documented here from [[service-trotsky-2009]].

## Position 1 — Degenerated workers' state (Trotsky)

In *The Revolution Betrayed* (1937), [[trotsky-leon|Trotsky]] argued the USSR remained a workers' state because natural resources and the major economic sectors were state-owned and the old propertied classes had been destroyed; but the "unstable equilibrium" created by Russian backwardness and the small 1917 proletariat let a "bureaucratic stratum" consolidate Bonapartist power under [[stalin-joseph|Stalin]]. The bureaucracy was a parasitic caste, not a class; the remedy was a *political* revolution against it, not a new *social* revolution: "No devil ever yet voluntarily cut off its own claws." Trotsky accepted "totalitarian" as description of Stalin's USSR while insisting it was not a category of Marxist analysis, and he explicitly rejected individual terror against Stalin as a method. In 1939–40 he conceded a conditional: if the USSR emerged from the war intact under Stalin, it would be necessary "in retrospect" to judge it "a precursor of a new exploiting regime" — entertaining, without adopting, his critics' thesis.

## Position 2 — Bureaucratic collectivism (Shachtman; Rizzi)

Dissident Trotskyists — Max Shachtman within Marxist categories, the Italian Bruno Rizzi more radically — argued the Soviet bureaucracy had become a ruling *class* extracting surplus through collective state ownership: a new form of class society, neither capitalist nor a workers' state. The 1939–40 events (Nazi–Soviet Pact, invasion of Poland, Winter War) made "unconditional defense of the USSR" untenable for this faction; Shachtman led the "Minority" out of the American Socialist Workers Party in 1940. The lineage runs back to Jan Machajski — whom Trotsky had known in Siberian exile — who argued before 1905 that the radical intelligentsia would come to dominate the workers in whose name it ruled.

## Position 3 — Managerial revolution (Burnham)

James Burnham pushed the new-class analysis beyond Marxism entirely: a managerial stratum was gaining independent class power in Soviet *and* Western economies alike, and Marxist class categories could not capture it (developed after his 1940 break as *The Managerial Revolution*). His open letter to Trotsky (1 February 1940) also attacked the epistemology holding the orthodox position together — the doctrine of "class truth" — as "the road of Plato's Philosopher Kings, of prophets and Popes and Stalins." The parallel philosophical front (Sidney Hook's 1937 critique of Engels's dialectics) cost Trotsky his ablest Anglophone intellectuals: Hook, Burnham, and Shachtman all departed by 1940. Robert Service's judgment — that Trotsky "lacked the competence" to answer the philosophical case and retreated to moral polemic (*Their Morality and Ours*, 1938) — is Service's position.

## Shared ground and afterlife

All sides agreed the Stalinist outcome demanded theoretical explanation and that the bureaucracy was the central phenomenon (a diagnosis Trotsky had begun in the 1923 *New Course* critique of "apparatus bureaucratism" — see [[bureaucracy]]). The dispute dissolved rather than resolved: the USSR's 1991 collapse removed the object, while the analytical problem migrated into mainstream sociology (theories of the new class, state socialism, and totalitarianism). No position is adjudicated here.

## Carr 1979 (related classification claims)

[[carr-edward-hallett|Carr]] (*[[carr-russian-revolution-lenin-stalin-1979]]*, Ch. 19) does not enter the 1937–40 Trotskyist "Russian Question" under that name, but offers a related classification: Soviet planned economy is **not** [[state-capitalism]] in any meaningful sense; it is the "material, economic half" of socialism **and** a hybrid "revolution from above" that failed Marx's and Lenin's criteria of proletarian self-emancipation. That pairing — socialist economic forms + non-emancipatory political means — is adjacent to Position 1 (degenerated workers' state) without adopting Trotsky's caste-vs-class vocabulary. Record as Carr's historiographical position, not a fourth formal pole of the 1937–40 debate.
