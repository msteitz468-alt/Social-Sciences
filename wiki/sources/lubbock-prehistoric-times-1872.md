# Pre-Historic Times (Lubbock, 1865/1872)

## Overview

[[lubbock-john|John Lubbock]]'s *Pre-Historic Times* is a founding-era charter for prehistoric archaeology as a science linking geology and history. First published 1865; this ingest uses the **1872** enlarged London edition (Elibron facsimile). Its load-bearing conceptual move is the division of prehistoric archaeology into **four epochs**, of which the two stone ages receive the durable labels **[[palaeolithic]]** ("Paleolithic" period of the Drift, coeval with extinct fauna) and **[[neolithic]]** (later polished Stone Age without metal except gold ornaments), followed by Bronze and Iron Ages — building on and popularizing the northern European three-age system of Thomsen/Nilsson for an English-reading public, while extending the case for the deep [[antiquity-of-man|antiquity of man]].

The book pairs material-culture synthesis (bronze, stone, megaliths, Swiss *Pfahlbauten*, Danish *kjøkkenmøddinger*, American earthworks, Quaternary fauna, caves, river-drift gravels) with long comparative chapters on "modern savages" as ethnographic analogy for prehistoric lifeways — classic founding-era method, with all the sampling and evolutionary bias that later anthropology rejected.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Pre-Historic Times* is the English-language charter that made the [[three-age-system]] and the [[palaeolithic]]/[[neolithic]] cut public scientific vocabulary. Its archaeological chapters are second-hand synthesis of northern European and French/English Palaeolithic research, valuable for history of the discipline more than as primary site evidence. The ethnographic half is founding-era comparative method at full Victorian volume: travellers' reports, living-fossil analogy, progress moral. For this wiki, promote only period **labels** and historiographic facts toward wiki voice where independently standard; keep Lubbock's racial rankings, progress sermon, and case ethnography strictly attributed. Pairs with [[antiquity-of-man]] (Stocking) as the archaeological sister of Darwinian deep time, and with [[social-evolutionism]] / [[degenerationism]] as the sociocultural program Stocking judged less durable than the period terms.
