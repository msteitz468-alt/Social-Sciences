# Darkness in El Dorado (Tierney, 2000)

Patrick Tierney, *Darkness in El Dorado: How Scientists and Journalists Devastated the Amazon* (New York: W. W. Norton, 2000; paperback ed. with afterword dated July 2001). Investigative polemic arguing that research and media practices — especially those associated with [[chagnon-napoleon|Napoleon Chagnon]], geneticist James V. Neel, naturalist/politician Charles Brewer-Carías, filmmaker Timothy Asch, and related press expeditions — harmed the [[yanomamo|Yanomami]] and distorted scientific and public knowledge about them.

**Ingest mode:** polemic / artifact (CLAUDE.md Source-Type Handling). Primary wiki product is the updated debate page [[chagnon-tierney-yanomami-ethics-debate]]. This page is bibliographic record + structured claim inventory. **No `studies/` or hub page** — the book is not a landmark empirical research design; it is a public brief inside a standing ethics controversy.

### FACTS (high-confidence bibliographic / institutional — still attributed where single-sourced to Tierney)

- Tierney acknowledges reliance on **R. Brian Ferguson's** warfare analysis; names Leslie Sponsel, Terence Turner, Kenneth Good, Giovanni Saffirio among readers/contacts; credits Leda Martins's Chagnon dossier; cites Smithsonian NAA tapes of the 1968 expedition and Eileen Welsome (*The Plutonium Files*) for the AEC bridge chapter.
- **Chagnon 1995 Brazil visit** via *Veja* journalistic permit; blood-vacutainer incident with FUNAI/health officials as Tierney and photographer Antonio Mari recount; Indigenous Council of Roraima / Martins dossier politics.
- **Asch–Chagnon films** named and discussed: *Yanomama: A Multidisciplinary Study*, *The Feast*, *The Ax Fight*, *Magical Death*; later Nova *Warriors of the Amazon*.
- **Haximu (1993):** garimpeiro massacre of Yanomami (Tierney: 12 victims after inflated early body counts) — a real atrocity whose *media and political uses* Tierney disputes.
- **AAA** Executive Board resolution of **15 November 2000** (full text in Appendix 2) authorizing ethics/human-rights review process related to the allegations.
- Paperback **Afterword (July 2001)** documents pre-publication email controversy ("e-mail heard 'round the world") and lists thematic updates (first contact, fierce-people politics, unokai data critique, measles clarification).

### THESES (Tierney — quarantine on debate page)

Core interpretive architecture (see debate charge table for counters):

1. **"Napoleonic Wars":** Chagnon's 1964 arrival with steel/guns/motors intensified the wars that made *The Fierce People* famous; method is political intervention (Ferguson framework).
2. **Theory-driven ferocity:** Neel's dominance/ILA genetic program selected Yanomami and shaped Chagnon's assignment; ethnography served sociobiological gospel.
3. **1968 epidemic:** Live Edmonston B protocols, vaccinator movements, and research/film priorities are causally implicated in catastrophic measles mortality (Tierney's strongest and most contested cluster; afterword walks back intentional-genocide media framing while keeping structural-harm claims).
4. **Blood-for-steel / AEC:** Virgin-soil sampling under Atomic Energy Commission agendas without adequate consent.
5. **Film as social engineering:** Feasts, fights, and first contacts choreographed; post-production afterlives include death, fission, and *xawara* blame.
6. **Unokai ratios:** Public "3× children / 2× wives" claim collapses under reanalysis Tierney offers.
7. **FUNDAFACI / Brewer:** Science–media–mining power bloc; Siapa project as private kingdom; expulsion 1993 as reckoning.
8. **Representation politics:** Fierce-people caricature enabled territorial harm; missionaries/NGOs attacked while miners welcomed the message.
9. **Closing (afterword):** Not intentional genocide; yes structural harm from expeditions that spread disease, warfare, and cultural chaos among a vulnerable people — and a call for disciplinary reform and medical attention to remote groups.

### QUOTES (load-bearing, for debate/source use)

> "Every time we are making a contact, we are spoiling them." — Charles Brewer-Carías, as Tierney quotes the Venevision / first-contact material (Ch. 1).

> "Are you Chagnon?" — first man to return at Mokarita-teri after dispersing in terror at Tierney's arrival (close of Ch. 2) — Tierney's irony that the ethnographer had become the dangerous spirit.

> Afterword clarification: media reduced the book to "Scientist killed Amazon Indians to test race theory"; Tierney states those were **not** his conclusions about the measles epidemic, and that the epidemic was "a small part of the book."

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Darkness in El Dorado* is best used in this wiki as a **map of a controversy**, not as a substitute ethnography of the Yanomami. Its lasting wiki value is threefold: (1) it forces explicit cross-links among representation, methods reactivity, genetics consent, film ethics, and gold-frontier politics; (2) it preserves a dense (if adversarial) citation trail to missions, journalists, Yanomami speakers, and archives that later work must answer; (3) its afterword already shows the polemic adjusting under fire — a reminder that the book's public life is part of the debate object. For subject-matter claims about mid-1960s Yanomamö social organization, prefer [[chagnon-yanomamo-fierce-people-study]] and later independent sources; for the ethics afterlife, prefer this page + [[chagnon-tierney-yanomami-ethics-debate]].
