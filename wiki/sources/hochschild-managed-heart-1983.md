## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Managed Heart* is a hybrid theoretical-empirical program that founded the research vocabulary of **[[emotional-labor|emotional labor]]**. Part One builds a private "emotional system" — [[emotion-work]], [[surface-and-deep-acting]], [[feeling-rules]], gift exchange of feeling — then Part Two shows its **[[commercialization-of-feeling|transmutation]]** under corporate service labor, with flight attendants (Delta as high-demand benchmark) and bill collectors as poles. Appendices supply emotion-theory scaffolding and 1970 Census occupational estimates (~38% of jobs; over half of employed women).

**Relation to already-ingested work:** extends [[mills-c-wright|Mills]]'s [[personality-market]] and Marxian [[alienation]] into a specified labor process of feeling; critiques [[goffman-erving|Goffman]]'s surface-acting strength and deep-acting gap while remaining dramaturgically kin. **Voice:** theoretical claims and single-study findings attributed throughout. **Hub:** Studies Hub built in-session (selection criteria met: founded a research vocabulary and subfield). Thinkers Hub deferred (single monograph).
