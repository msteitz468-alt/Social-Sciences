# Primitive Classification (Durkheim & Mauss, 1903)

## Overview

[[durkheim-emile|Émile Durkheim]] and [[mauss-marcel|Marcel Mauss]]'s *Année Sociologique* essay **« De quelques formes primitives de classification »** (1903) — English *[[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-1903|Primitive Classification]]* (Needham 1963). Subtitle: contribution to the study of **[[collective-representations|collective representations]]**. Load-bearing thesis (attributed): the **classificatory function** is not a spontaneous individual faculty; the first classes of things were classes of men; logical hierarchy is another aspect of social hierarchy; notions of space, time, hierarchy, number, class, and related cognitive categories are products of society. Comparative cases: Australian moiety/clan systems, Zuñi and Sioux regional classifications, Chinese correlative cosmology. Research-act analysis: [[durkheim-mauss-primitive-classification-study]]. Critical apparatus in this edition: [[needham-rodney|Rodney Needham]]'s Introduction (major demolition of the causal argument while salvaging residual value for classification studies).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Primitive Classification* is a short *Année Sociologique* essay that does three things at once: (1) it isolates **classification** as a sociological problem distinct from individual psychology; (2) it offers a comparative morphology of Australian, Zuñi/Siouan, and Chinese systems in which social divisions are claimed to model logical divisions; (3) it programs a sociology of the categories of the understanding later fulfilled in part by *Elementary Forms*. The Needham English edition is not neutral packaging — its Introduction is the major demolition of the **causal** demonstration (correspondence failures, petitio, source misuse, circularity of faculty-from-model) while salvaging the **analytical object** (symbolic classification) and residual research charter (structural concordance; inventory of categories). For the wiki, the correct filing is dual: attributed core theses on the study and concept pages; contested causal claim on the debate page; Durkheimian precursor layer on [[sociology-of-knowledge]] without equating 1903 with Mannheim or Berger & Luckmann. Hub study page was not warranted — depth lives on the studies/ page plus the existing Elementary Forms Studies Hub.
