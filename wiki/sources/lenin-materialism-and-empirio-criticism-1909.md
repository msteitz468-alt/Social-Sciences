# Materialism and Empirio-criticism (1909)

Lenin's one book of systematic philosophy: a polemic against the Russian Machists (Bogdanov, Bazarov, Lunacharsky, Yushkevich, Valentinov and the 1908 symposium *Studies in the Philosophy of Marxism*), arguing that empirio-criticism is Berkeleian subjective idealism in modern dress. Ingested from the combined 45-volume Collected Works file (Vol. 14 segment, incl. "Ten Questions to a Lecturer"); ~106k words, 5-agent extraction.

## Content and claims

- **Epistemology:** the reflection theory of knowledge — sensations as "copies, photographs, images, mirror-reflections"; matter as "objective reality given in sensation"; the criterion of practice; absolute truth compounded of relative truths. Codified on [[reflection-theory]].
- **The polemic:** genealogy of Machism from Berkeley/Hume/Fichte; solipsism and fideism charges; the immanentists as "comrades-in-arms"; the disciples' trajectory toward theism. Positions recorded on [[empirio-criticism-debate]].
- **Philosophy of science:** the "crisis in modern physics" ("matter has disappeared") read as epistemological; "physical idealism" coined; "with each epoch-making discovery... materialism has to change its form" (quoting Engels).
- **Historical materialism defended:** against Bogdanov's "identity of social being and social consciousness" — "social consciousness reflects social being"; against Blei's empirio-critical critique of Marx; "parties in philosophy" as class ideology ([[historical-materialism]], [[dialectical-materialism]]).
- **Supplement:** Chernyshevsky praised as Russia's one integral materialist, short of Marx–Engels dialectics.

## Cross-note: continuity or revision against the *Philosophical Notebooks* (1914–15)

Lenin's private 1914–15 Bern notebooks ([[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]]) complicate this book's copy-theory formulation. Where MEC states sensations are "copies, photographs, images, mirror-reflections" of matter, Lenin noted privately (1915, on Aristotle) that cognition "is not a simple, immediate act, a dead mirroring, but one which is complex, split into two, zig-zag-like, which includes in it the possibility of the flight of fantasy from life." Whether this is a genuine revision of the 1909 reflection theory or a compatible elaboration of it is an open question — both readings are recorded here rather than adjudicated; see [[reflection-theory]]. The notebooks also show Lenin continuing to track the Avenarius/Mach literature and reviews of it through 1912–16, well past this book's 1909 publication — the philosophical fight documented here did not end with the book.

## Citation provenance note

Lenin's deployments of "the materialist Plekhanov" can now be partially traced: the hieroglyph passage he criticizes is verbatim in [[plekhanov-notes-on-ludwig-feuerbach-1892]] (1892, Note 9); the "ichthyosaurus"/"object existed long before the subject" passages he cites from "Plekhanov's *L. Feuerbach*" were not found in the ingested transcription of that apparatus — an unresolved citation gap, flagged rather than assumed.

## Reliability

A partisan primary source for the history of Marxist philosophy and of Bolshevism's internal disputes; not a reliable guide to Mach, Avenarius, or Bogdanov's own doctrines, which it quotes selectively and tendentiously. The 1920 second-edition apparatus adds a political layer (anti-Proletkult framing).
