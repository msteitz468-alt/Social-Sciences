# History and Class Consciousness (Lukács, 1923)

Georg Lukács, *Geschichte und Klassenbewusstsein* (1923); English *History and Class Consciousness: Studies in Marxist Dialectics* (Livingstone tr.). Charter text of [[western-marxism|Western Marxism]] and of the modern theory of [[reification]], written from within early communist politics and Hegelian-Marxist philosophy.

**All theoretical claims are attributed to Lukács (1923) or to his 1967 self-criticism**, never asserted as wiki-voice findings. Empirical/historical illustrations (party history, Russian Revolution, accumulation debates) are positions and interpretations, not independent historiography. **No `studies/` page** — theoretical essay collection, not a landmark empirical research act.

## Claims extracted (integration summary)

### Bibliographic / composition
- Essays 1919–1922; two wholly new for the book: Reification…; Organisation methodology. Vienna Christmas 1922 original preface; Budapest March 1967 self-critical preface; Löwy Paris May 2023 centenary preface.

### Method and totality
- Orthodoxy = **method** (dialectical materialism), not acceptance of every Marxian thesis; even total empirical refutation of results need not end orthodoxy.
- [[concrete-totality]]: primacy of totality as bearer of the revolutionary principle in science; facts only within historical process; natural-science “pure” facts mirror capitalist [[reification]].
- Bernstein/revisionism: remove dialectic → opportunist evolution without revolution.

### Class consciousness
- [[class-consciousness]] = rational reactions **imputed** (*zugerechnet*) to a production position — not sum/average of psychological thoughts ([[imputed-class-consciousness]]).
- False consciousness: dual (subjectively right / objectively false); concrete investigation as stage of totality.
- Only bourgeoisie and proletariat as pure classes; petty bourgeoisie/peasantry limited or borrowed consciousness.
- Proletarian superiority = see society as coherent whole; theory–practice coincide; fate of revolution depends on ideological maturity.
- Party as organised form of class consciousness; workers’ councils as overcoming of reification.

### Reification
- Commodity as **central structural problem** of capitalist society in all aspects (not economics alone).
- Essence: relation between people takes on character of a thing / “phantom objectivity.”
- Objective + subjective sides; Taylorism into the “soul”; bureaucracy, law, journalism; contemplative stance; time→space.
- Part II: antinomies of bourgeois thought (Kant, German idealism) as intellectual expression of reified society.
- Part III: proletariat as (1923) identical subject–object of history — **retracted 1967**.

### Historical materialism / politics
- Changing function of historical materialism with social structure / dictatorship.
- Legality/illegality as pure tactics; cretinism of legality vs romanticism of illegality.
- Luxemburg: praised for totalising accumulation Marxism; criticised on Russian Revolution (organic development, party, agrarian/national questions).
- Organisation: mediation of theory and practice; CP vs Menshevik institutionalisation of crisis.

### Dialectics of nature
- 1923: Engels *Anti-Dühring* omits subject–object interaction central to revolutionary dialectic → [[lukacs-dialectics-of-nature-debate]].
- 1967: retracts “nature is a societal category” as strike at Marxian ontology; revises polemic on experiment/industry.

### 1967 self-criticism (major)
- Missing labour as metabolism society–nature; imputed consciousness left contemplative; totality elevated over economics; identical subject–object = out-Hegeling Hegel; alienation equated with objectification (crude error that also fed success).

### Reception (Löwy, attributed)
- Influence: Bloch, [[mannheim-karl|Mannheim]], [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]], Benjamin, Marcuse, Sartre, Merleau-Ponty, Lefebvre, Goldmann, et al.
- Attacks: Kautsky; Zinoviev; Rudas; Deborin 1924.
- *Tailism and the Dialectic* (c. 1925) unpublished defence; more authoritarian on party/Luxemburg than *HCC* (Löwy).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*History and Class Consciousness* is the single densest charter text for [[western-marxism]] in this collection: it converts Marx’s [[commodity-fetishism]] into a general theory of [[reification]], makes [[concrete-totality|totality]] the methodological badge of Marxism against Second International “scientism,” and introduces [[imputed-class-consciousness]] as the structural category of proletarian knowledge. It is also a document of a failed revolutionary wave (1917–23) and of a philosopher later at war with his own book. The wiki must therefore hold **two Lukácses** in tension — the 1923 revolutionary Hegelian-Marxist and the 1967 self-critic who authorises republication only as apprenticeship literature — without collapsing either into wiki-voice truth. Cross-links to [[frankfurt-school|Frankfurt]] / [[critical-theory]] now rest on a primary reification source rather than hand-waved genealogy; the [[lukacs-dialectics-of-nature-debate]] records the orthodoxy fight that often displaced reification in early reception. Outstanding complementary ingests: Gramsci primary depth, Korsch, *Tailism and the Dialectic*, and Lukács’s later ontology/aesthetics.
