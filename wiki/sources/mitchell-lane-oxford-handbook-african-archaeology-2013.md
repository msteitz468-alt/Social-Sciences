# The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology (Mitchell & Lane, eds., 2013)

## Bibliographic Record

Peter Mitchell and Paul Lane (eds.), *The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology* (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2013). ~70 authored chapters + figures/tables/contributors/index. Editors: [[mitchell-peter]], [[lane-paul]].

Continent-scale multi-author handbook covering theory and method of African archaeology, hominin origins, hunter-gatherer diversity, food production and metallurgy, towns and states, and Africa in the modern world system (Ottoman, Atlantic, colonial, diaspora). Designed as the field’s reference scaffold rather than a single-author narrative; chapters are specialist syntheses with independent bibliographies. **No `studies/` page** (handbook, not a primary research act). Complements [[phillipson-african-archaeology-2005]] (deep-time single-author survey) and [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]] (urbanism/state comparative).

## Key Claims Extracted

All claims **attributed to named chapter authors** (or Mitchell & Lane as editors); scientific horizon **~2010–2012** unless noted.

1. **Africa as triple evolutionary cradle (editors).** Darwin’s African intuition confirmed for hominin divergence, genus *Homo*, and *H. sapiens*; “behavioural modernity” crystallised first in Africa before effective extra-continental dispersal (Mitchell & Lane Ch. 1 — attributed synthesis).
2. **Method before narrative.** Part II precedes chronological Parts III–VII because a “what happened when” story without construction history is misleading (editors).
3. **Comparative African themes (editors).** [[cattle-before-crops]] pastoral pathways; complex crop adoption; possible independent ferrous metallurgy ([[sub-saharan-iron-origins-debate]]); urbanism/complexity without only hierarchical coercive states; deep Indian Ocean connections; Kopytoff internal frontiers.
4. **Practice history (Connah Ch. 2).** African archaeology’s colonial, nationalist, and settler-ideological uses (Great Zimbabwe denial; Stone Age bias) are content for reflexivity.
5. **Cross-disciplinary methods.** Oral structural analysis (Schmidt); linguistics (Blench — four African language phyla; reconstruction caveats); genetics (MacEachern — sampling bias, Last Paragraphs Problem; TMRCA claims date-stamped); migration models (Ashley — anti-Hamitic empty-lands critique).
6. **Deep time.** Oldowan ~2.6 mya ([[gona]]); Acheulean Mode 2 summary; recent-African-origin genetics majority (as of ~2012); hunting vs scavenging as **position**; modernity checklists contested (Barham “beyond modernity”).
7. **Southern MSA.** [[still-bay-industry]] ~70–75 kya pre-dates [[howiesons-poort-industry]] ~59–65 kya; bone tools, beads, compound adhesives, projected weapons arguments at [[blombos-cave]], [[sibudu-cave]] (Lombard Ch. 26).
8. **Food production.** Pottery ≠ Neolithic (Barich); early Nabta cattle **contested** (di Lernia vs optimistic readings); five African plant-domestication centres (Fuller & Hildebrand); Sahel cattle-before-crops then crisis then iron-age complexity (Breunig).
9. **Bantu (de Maret Ch. 43).** ~500 languages; Cameroon Grassfields homeland; **no metallurgy in initial Proto-Bantu lexicon**; stream models open; SMA/EIA packages **associated_with** early Bantu-speaking communities — not pots=people=language=genes; multi-process “Bantuization.”
10. **Iron (Mapunda Ch. 42).** Mid-first millennium cal. BC consensus dates; external vs independent invention **open**; early anomalous dates tentative.
11. **Urbanism/state.** Sinclair heterarchy + operational urbanism; Monroe power strategies vs neo-evolution; Egypt geographically African / indigenous state formation vs Dynastic Race (Shaw); trade often intensifier (aligned with Connah’s productive-land frame).
12. **Swahili (LaViolette).** Indigenous African origins consensus (as of 2013); Tana/TIW from 6th c.; Islamic practice mid-8th c. Shanga; gold/ivory articulation with Zimbabwe Plateau.
13. **[[zimbabwe-culture]] (Pikirayi).** Etic construct: Toutswe–Mapungubwe–Great Zimbabwe–Torwa–Mutapa sequence; Mapungubwe demise ~1300 not simply environmental.
14. **Modern world system.** Ottoman, Atlantic slavery/diaspora, colonial encounters — historical archaeology of African agency within global systems (Chs 66–70).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Oxford Handbook of African Archaeology* is the vault’s **multi-author operating system** for African archaeology: it refuses a narrative-only continental story and leads with how the field is constructed (methods, heritage politics, colonial residues). Relative to already-ingested [[phillipson-african-archaeology-2005]] and [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]], the handbook adds (1) specialist depth and bibliography density per region/theme, (2) explicit genetics–linguistics–material non-identity hygiene (especially de Maret on Bantu; MacEachern on genes), (3) southern MSA behavioural-complexity densification beyond Phillipson’s survey grain, and (4) historical-archaeology coverage of Ottoman, Atlantic, and colonial encounters largely outside the two textbooks’ cores. Chronologies and genetic claims are **date-stamped to a ~2012 secondary horizon** and must be densified by post-2013 aDNA, Bayesian dating, and site monographs. Outstanding monographic gaps remain: Mitchell’s own *Archaeology of Southern Africa* (correct text still needed) and the *Oxford Handbook*’s regional companions when acquired.
