# The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe (Gamble, 1999)

[[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s major late-1990s pan-European synthesis: Palaeolithic societies are **not** unknowable. Against [[childe-v-gordon|Childe]]'s judgement that reconstruction of Palaeolithic social life is doomed by meagre data, Gamble rebuilds a social archaeology from the **individual**, **networks** (intimate / effective / extended), and **performance**, using a three-level analytic frame — **locales, rhythms, regions** — applied to ~500,000–21,000 BP European evidence. Succeeds and replaces *The Palaeolithic Settlement of Europe* (1986).

## Core Argument (attributed, Gamble 1999)

1. **Childe was wrong about the data** — the problem is approach (group/ecology/typology), not evidence quality relative to knowledge of the times.
2. Start from the **individual** and **interaction**, not band/tribe social typologies or pure ecological adaptation.
3. Three personal **networks** (intimate, effective, extended) are maintained by different **resources** (emotional, material, symbolic) and leave archaeological signatures at different scales.
4. Analytic bridge: **locales** (where co-presence happens), **rhythms** (acquiring, making, walking, performing, attending), **regions** (nine-region European frame).
5. Before ~60–40 ka, European Palaeolithic social life is largely **local** (intimate/effective); later Upper Palaeolithic **extends** social life via symbolic resources and longer-distance networks — a transformation of **power**, not merely better hunting technology.
6. Aim: raise the curtain on Palaeolithic people as neither **stomach-led** nor **brain-dead**.
