## Core Moves (attributed — Tilly)

1. Social processes start from **interpersonal transactions** that compound into identities, boundaries, and durable ties — not from essences or pure individual attitudes.
2. **Relational mechanisms** (including mechanism–process accounts of democratization and contention) explain political outcomes better than disposition monisms.
3. **[[durable-inequality-theory|Durable inequality]]** is restated and extended: categorical inequality, exploitation, opportunity hoarding, emulation, adaptation; changing forms; knowledge inequality.
4. **Social boundary mechanisms** transform how categories organize social life (inscription, erasure, activation, etc. — extract from Ch. 9).
5. **Citizenship** is a bounded political relation of rights/obligations; exclusion and boundary work are constitutive.
6. **Democratization and de-democratization** interact with categorical inequality; political identities are relational claim-making performances.
7. **Social movement repertoire** invents, diffuses, and transforms via brokerage, certification, local adaptation, and related mechanisms.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Identities, Boundaries, and Social Ties* is Tilly’s mid-2000s portfolio of relational social science. It does not replace *Durable Inequality* (1998) or *From Mobilization to Revolution* (1978); it **cross-connects** them. Transactions compound into identities, boundaries, and durable ties. The distinctive new analytic payloads for this vault are the **social-boundary mechanism inventory** (Ch. 9), the full **citizenship / protected-consultation** operationalization of democracy, the explicit **de-democratization** frame, and the **political-identity** / social-movement repertoire diffusion chapters. Inequality chapters largely restate DI with extensions on knowledge inequality and changing resource bases — treat as re-statement, not independent discovery. Self-reuse is authorially flagged.

Gaps: *Trust and Rule*, *Social Movements 1768–2004*, *Contentious Politics* (Tarrow), *Coercion, Capital, and European States* still uningested; Massey *Categorically Unequal* remains on the Outstanding line with this volume.
