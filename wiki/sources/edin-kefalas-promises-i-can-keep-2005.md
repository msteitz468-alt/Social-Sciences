# Promises I Can Keep (Edin & Kefalas, 2005 / 2011 preface)

Kathryn Edin and Maria Kefalas’s multi-site qualitative study of why low-income women in Philadelphia and Camden put motherhood before marriage. Fieldwork centers on 162 unmarried mothers below the poverty line across eight neighborhoods, stratified by race/ethnicity and age.

## Key claims (extracted)

1. **Puzzle:** Survey explanations of marriage–childrearing decoupling leave a large residual; women’s own logics underused (Intro).
2. **Design:** N=162; eight Philly/Camden neighborhoods; class-comparable race comparison including white high-poverty industrial villages (Intro, App. A).
3. **Class not race:** Similar worldviews across African American, white, and Puerto Rican mothers in comparable poverty contexts (authors’ FINDING).
4. **Semi-planned fertility:** Rapid courtship; male desire for babies; conception neither pure accident nor pure design (Ch.1).
5. **Pregnancy as test:** Can he become a family man in time? (Ch.2).
6. **Breakup forces:** Crime/jail, addiction, infidelity, abuse dominate mothers’ accounts over pure money shortage (Ch.3).
7. **High marriage bar:** Economic + relational standards; better unwed motherhood than doomed marriage; title thesis (Ch.4).
8. **Being there:** Emic mothering standard under constraint (Ch.5; [[being-there-mothering]]).
9. **Redemptive motherhood:** Children as meaning and turning point; authors’ caveat on perception vs outcomes (Ch.6; [[redemptive-motherhood]]).
10. **Theory engagement:** Wilson MMPI best of three standard accounts but modest; marriage criteria changed more than men’s economies (Conclusion).
11. **2011 frame:** Instability/complexity (78%/18%/4% urban nonmarital kindergarten figures via Fragile Families apparatus); spread beyond “truly disadvantaged.”

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Promises* is the wiki’s first primary ethnography of contemporary U.S. low-income family formation. It dual-files as source + study ([[edin-kefalas-promises-study]]) and feeds [[marriage]], [[nonmarital-childbearing]], [[culture-of-poverty]] (as foil), [[male-marriageable-pool-index]] (as refinement of Wilson), and [[concentrated-poverty]] (Philly/Camden site ecology). The load-bearing intellectual move is **not** “the poor reject marriage” but **high reverence + high bar + available motherhood as meaning**, under sex-ratio and labor-market constraint. Evidence class is ethnographic depth with purposive multi-neighborhood design — pair with demographic sources (Fragile Families, vital statistics) for scale.
