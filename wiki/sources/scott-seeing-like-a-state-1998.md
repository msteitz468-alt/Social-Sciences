# Seeing Like a State (Scott 1998)

Scott's theory of state [[legibility]] and the failure of [[high-modernism|high-modernist]] schemes, built on four case families: scientific forestry and cadastral mapping; planned cities (Le Corbusier vs. Jane Jacobs); Soviet collectivization (Lenin vs. Luxemburg); Tanzanian ujamaa villagization — closing with agricultural simplification and the defense of [[metis|mētis]] (practical knowledge).

## Argument Summary

All attributed to Scott 1998:

1. **[[legibility|Legibility]].** The modern state's core project is rendering society and nature readable from the center — cadastre, census, standard measures, surnames, official language. State simplifications are transformative maps: "a state cadastral map... creates such a system through its ability to give its categories the force of law." Parable: German scientific forestry's *Normalbaum* monocultures, followed a century later by *Waldsterben*.
2. **[[high-modernism|High modernism]].** A muscle-bound, unscientific faith in rational redesign, aesthetic as much as technical. The four-element recipe for catastrophe: legibility (capacity) + high-modernist ideology (desire) + authoritarian state (determination) + prostrate civil society (terrain). Representative institutions are the strongest historical check.
3. **Cases.** Le Corbusier's plan-as-despot vs. [[jacobs-jane|Jacobs]]'s functional order (Brasília via Holston: "brasilite," the unplanned city at 75% of population); Lenin's vanguard blueprint vs. [[luxemburg-rosa|Luxemburg]]/Kollontai's revolution-as-learning; Soviet collectivization — a production failure but an appropriation/control success ("second serfdom"; the 1933–34 "collectivization and procurement famine"; deaths estimated 3–4M to 20M+ as of 1998); Tanzanian ujamaa villagization 1973–76 (≥5M relocated, ~70% of the population in villages in three years by Nyerere's own report; Ethiopia's Dergue as harsher parallel); monocrop agronomy vs. polyculture, landraces, and shifting cultivation (Richards, Howard, Carson, van der Ploeg as his witnesses).
4. **[[metis|Mētis]].** Practical, adaptive knowledge that formal order parasitizes ("formal order... is always... parasitic on informal processes"), cannot codify, and — via Taylorism, contract farming, and state simplification — deliberately destroys. Conclusion: mētis-friendly institutions (democracy, common law, language) and four planning rules of thumb (small steps, reversibility, plan on surprises, plan on human inventiveness).
5. **Disclaimers Scott built in:** not anti-state, not romantic about the local, not pro-market (capitalism "just as much an agency of homogenization"); legibility is morally neutral (the 1941 Amsterdam map served deportation; the same capacity underwrites public health and equality before law).
