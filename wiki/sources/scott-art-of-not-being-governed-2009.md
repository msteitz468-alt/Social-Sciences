## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Art of Not Being Governed* is Scott’s fullest statement of highland [[state-evasion]] as a political ecology. **[[zomia]]** (van Schendel’s term) is cast as a ~2.5 million km² massif of refuge and relative statelessness above the padi plains — a [[shatter-zone]] and **state effect**, not an evolutionary leftover. The padi state concentrates **manpower and grain**; hills concentrate **friction of terrain**, escape agriculture ([[swidden]], roots), escape social structure (“jellyfish,” fission), orality, and ethnolinguistic amphibiousness. Civilizational labels (cooked/raw, civilized/barbarian) recode **state-subject** vs **not-a-state-subject**. Conclusion: hill peoples are **post–irrigated rice, postsedentary, postsubject, perhaps postliterate** — reactive purposeful statelessness. Scope largely ends ~1945 when distance-demolishing technologies enable the “last great enclosure.”
