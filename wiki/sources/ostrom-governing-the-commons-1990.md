# Governing the Commons (Ostrom, 1990)

Elinor Ostrom, *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action* (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1990). Landmark argument that **common-pool resources (CPRs)** need neither sole Leviathan nor privatization: under specified conditions, appropriators can craft and sustain **self-governing institutions**. Critiques Hardin’s tragedy of the commons, the prisoner’s dilemma framing, and Olson’s logic of collective action as over-applied metaphors; develops institutional analysis and **design principles** from long-enduring and failed cases.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Governing the Commons* is the wiki’s charter primary for self-organized CPR governance. Ch.1 dismantles Hardin/PD/Olson as sole policy metaphors (Leviathan vs privatization) and offers Alanya as an empirical alternative. Ch.2 defines CPRs (exclusion difficulty + subtractability), the three puzzles (supply, commitment, monitoring), and multi-level rules. Ch.3 induces eight [[design-principles-cpr|design principles]] from alpine, Japanese, Spanish, and Philippine cases. Ch.4 shows polycentric institutional change ending California groundwater pumping races. Ch.5 contrasts failures/fragilities by principle absence. **Ch.6 incomplete in this scan.** Limits: OCR two-column noise; secondary-case base; no Theory Hub this pass (theory + study pages carry the program).
