# Analytical Archaeology (Clarke 1968; 2nd ed. Chapman rev. 1978)

The classic British charter of the **[[processual-archaeology|New Archaeology]]**: a programmatic, systems-theoretic reconstruction of archaeology as an autonomous analytical discipline with its own hierarchical entities, models, and procedures. [[clarke-david-l|David L. Clarke]] argued that archaeological data form nested systems (attribute → artefact → type → assemblage → culture → culture group → technocomplex) best treated with explicit models, [[polythetic-classification|polythetic]] classification, and feedback-controlled procedure rather than intuitive culture-history. The 1978 second edition, revised by Bob Chapman after Clarke's death, is the text ingested here.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Analytical Archaeology* is the vault's **British processual charter**: where *[[binford-binford-new-perspectives-1968|New Perspectives]]* demonstrates social-process research via case studies, Clarke supplies the **entity ontology and systems metalanguage** — polythetic hierarchy, technocomplex, procedure-as-feedback, and the claim that archaeology is a discipline in its own right. Relative to ingested sources: reforms [[archaeological-culture]] beyond Childe's people-equation without abolishing the culture unit; densifies [[processual-archaeology]] with a Cambridge systems wing distinct from Binford's anthropology-as-frame; anticipates quantitative handbooks (Doran & Hodson; Hodder & Orton) that Chapman used to justify dropping the first edition's methods chapters. Standing cautions for the wiki: keep Clarke's ethnology bridges in attributed probability voice; enforce four-way non-identity; date claims to 1968 program / 1978 text as appropriate.
