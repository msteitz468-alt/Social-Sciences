# Power: A Radical View (2nd ed.)

Steven Lukes, *Power: A Radical View*, 2nd expanded edition (Basingstoke: Palgrave Macmillan, 2005; 1st ed. 1974). Classic statement of the **three-dimensional view of power** — decision-making, agenda-setting/nondecision-making, and preference-shaping — framed as theoretically and politically radical, essentially contested, and operational. Second edition adds a long Introduction, Ch. 2 (power, freedom, reason — including Foucault critique), and Ch. 3 (defence of three-dimensional power, real interests, false consciousness).

## What the book is

A contribution to mid-century US debates on community and national power (Mills, Hunter, Dahl pluralism, Bachrach & Baratz) that argues power is most effective when least observable. One-dimensional (pluralist decisionism), two-dimensional (nondecisions/agenda control), and three-dimensional (securing compliance by shaping wants) maps; domination as a species of power (capacity, not only exercise); engagement with Foucault, Bourdieu, and liberal/republican freedom.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Power: A Radical View* (2nd ed.) is the wiki’s charter primary for the **three-dimensional** analysis of power in political sociology. Ch. 1 (1974) maps one-/two-/three-dimensional views against pluralist decisionism and Bachrach–Baratz nondecisions; prizes Crenson’s air-pollution study; ties interest conceptions (liberal/reformist/radical) to each map. The 2005 Introduction corrects the 1974 formula: power is **capacity**, not only exercise; PRV’s topic is **domination** as one species. Ch. 2 engages Foucault (productive micro-power, willing compliance) while rejecting ultra-radical dissolution of freedom/rationality. Ch. 3 defends [[real-interests]] as purpose-relative counterfactuals and reformulates [[false-consciousness]] as **power to mislead**. All claims remain attributed; the concept is essentially contested by design. Limits: no studies/ page; Dahl/Bachrach/Baratz/Crenson primaries not yet ingested; Thinkers Hub deferred.
