# Essays in Tektology (Gorelik trans.)

The third counter-source of the re-balance program: Bogdanov's mature system in his own words, ending the situation where the wiki's Bogdanov rested entirely on Lenin's 1909 polemic. ~139k words, 5-agent extraction.

## Contents (all attributed)

- **Bogdanov's text:** the full Essays — "What Is Organizational Science?", basic concepts and mechanisms (conjunction, ingression, disingression, selection), stability and the law of the minimum, divergence/convergence, egression/degression, crises C/D, and the "organizational dialectic" with its explicit critique of the Marxist triad. Codified on [[tektology]].
- **The 1912 and 1921 prefaces:** the program's self-dating, the Plenge priority note, the claim that postwar/revolutionary reconstruction made tektology timely.
- **Gorelik's introduction (secondary scholarship):** Bogdanov biography (A. A. Malinovskii, 1873–1928; physician; the Essays' publication history), reception history (Marxist attacks; Lenin's rejection of empirio-monism as the cause of neglect; Stalin-era suppression; Soviet rehabilitation from the 1960s), and the anticipation thesis with its own stated limits.

Primary for Bogdanov's system; the reception/anticipation story is Gorelik's advocacy and is recorded as attributed scholarship. Bogdanov's empirical illustrations are a theoretical work's illustrations, never findings — his divergence-derived claims about "races" and the sexual division of labor are period speculation, flagged against the four-way non-identity rule.
