# How to Observe Morals and Manners (Martineau, 1838)

Harriet Martineau's systematic manual on the **observation of morals and manners** — a founding English methodological treatise for a science of society. Published London: Charles Knight and Co., 1838, as the second volume in a planned series of observational handbooks. This ingest uses the Project Gutenberg plain-text of the 1838 edition.

## Claims extracted (high-value summary)

~127 claims across three ranges (A–C). Load-bearing clusters:

1. **Method system** — philosophical / moral / mechanical requisites; no peremptory decision; no on-spot generalization; cumulative multi-observer program; query list + journal + note-book; defer dogmas.
2. **[[things-before-discourse]]** — institutions and records first; discourse as commentary; five departments.
3. **Evaluative and ethical theory** — happiness criterion; conscience from influences; circumstances form prevalent vices; untrammelled sympathy.
4. **Religion program** — [[licentious-ascetic-moderate-religion]]; churches, clergy, superstitions; suicide character/number as religious-sentiment indicators (anticipatory relation to later [[moral-statistics]] / [[durkheim-suicide-study]]).
5. **Liberty and progress indicators** — feudal residual, police origin, class gradation, servants, metropolis vs provinces, press freedom, free education, persecution for opinion; fraternity end-state; charity hierarchy (relief → prevention); arts for many vs luxury.
6. **Domestic program** — tenure and classes, health/registers, marriage compact imperfect, woman's condition as domestic-morals test, children as "breathings" of home atmosphere.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*How to Observe* is a **method book with an implicit social theory**: morals and manners are products of gigantic general influences; they can be read from objectified social products more reliably than from talk; the observer's mind and heart are part of the instrument. It sits in the founding era beside [[tocqueville-democracy-in-america-1835|Tocqueville]] (travel-based comparative political sociology) and before Martineau's own transmission of [[comte-positive-philosophy-martineau-1853|Comte]] — so the English public first received from her a *how to observe society* manual, then a *positive philosophy* condensation. For this wiki it supplies the first dedicated **[[social-observation]]** method page, the priority rule **[[things-before-discourse]]**, and a religion typology, and it thickens the pre-Durkheim history of treating **[[suicide]]** as a social indicator. Thin areas after this ingest: *Society in America* (empirical companion) not yet ingested; secondary historiography of Martineau as "first woman sociologist" not primary-sourced; statistical operationalization arrives later with [[moral-statistics]] and Durkheim.
