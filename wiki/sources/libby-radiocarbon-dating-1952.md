# Radiocarbon Dating (Libby 1952 / 2nd ed. 1955; Phoenix 1965)

Willard F. Libby's founding monograph on the physical basis, laboratory practice, and first large catalogue of **[[radiocarbon-dating|radiocarbon (carbon-14) dates]]**. Chapter VII is by archaeologist **[[johnson-frederick|Frederick Johnson]]** on the significance of the dates for archaeology, geology, and pollen analysis. This wiki uses the 1965 Phoenix printing of the 1955 second edition (copyright 1952 and 1955), including the "Notes Added in 1965 Printing" addenda.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Radiocarbon Dating* is the charter text of archaeology's first absolute-dating revolution on organic materials. Libby supplies the nuclear-chemical argument (cosmic-ray production, exchange reservoir, half-life, laboratory assay) and the first large public date list; Frederick Johnson supplies the anthropological assessment of what those ages do to relative chronologies. For this wiki the ingest grounds [[radiocarbon-dating]] in primary method prose rather than only secondary reception (Clark, Childe, Renfrew, Reich), locks the [[libby-half-life]] / conventional-BP distinction and early Egyptian discrepancy as period-documented, and records the AAA/GSA Committee on Carbon 14 as the institutional bridge between Chicago chemistry and field archaeology. All ch. VI ages are uncalibrated Libby-scale results; calibration and the "second radiocarbon revolution" remain the Renfrew-stream layer already on disk.
