# Çatalhöyük Excavations: The 2009–2017 Seasons (Hodder ed. 2022)

Final Hodder-directed main excavation report for Neolithic East Mound work 2009–2017 (North and South Areas) plus Gdańsk (GDN) later-phase work. Primary aims: settlement layout and social geography; art/symbolism in secure architectural context; Bayesian dating of contemporaneity and occupational continuity; house and burial variability vs social differentiation.

## Volume Synthesis Note

**Primary excavation-report type.** *[[hodder-catalhoyuk-excavations-2009-2017|Çatalhöyük Excavations: The 2009–2017 Seasons]]* (Hodder ed. 2022; CRP Series Volume 12 / BIAA Monograph 56) is the **final Hodder-directed main East Mound excavation report** for 2009–2017 North and South Areas plus GDN later-phase work — multi-author unit/building catalogues, not a single-voice theory book. Companion specialist volumes 13–15 cover materials; this volume owns architecture, phasing, methods, conservation, geophysics, and Bayesian North chronology.

**What it adds (attributed).** Hodder Ch.1 restates the long project aim (symbolism in full socio-economic context), maps four excavation phases, and summarises house typology (**history houses** and other types — Hodder & Pels 2010), neighbourhood foci, revised population bands, and aDNA framing **(as of Lazaridis et al. 2016; Feldman et al. 2019; Chyleński et al. 2019 / Vol.13 Ch.18)**. Ch.2 (Farid/Hodder/Lukas) freezes mature [[reflexive-fieldwork]] / [[interpretation-at-the-trowels-edge]] practice: interpretive category + probability, 2014 hybrid Feature Sheets, priority tours, integrated DB, diaries/sketches, multivocal report quotation with selection-bias caveats. Ch.3 revises South/North level phasing; **Bayesian modelling (Bayliss et al. Ch.19, as of 2022)** supplies North 25-year time slices, a B.5–B.1 break, and B.131 after B.77 where stratigraphy alone was ambiguous — levels ≠ absolute contemporaneity. Building chapters (Taylor, Tung, et al.) supply load-bearing patterns for [[catalhoyuk-shrine-house-debate]]: **B.7 = Mellaart Shrine 8** southern annexes with oven/de-husking/internal-partition evidence; **B.43** double-leopard ‘shrine’ stacked on domestic B.162–160 with southern ovens, dirty floors, storage annexe; house-size variability without shrine status; Red Shrine remnant Sp.581. Conservation (Lingle) and display policy are first-class constraints on what was dug.

**Wiki contribution.** Densifies [[hodder-ian]] final field phase and Bayesian collaboration; hardens Position B on the shrine–house debate with named buildings; operationalises method concepts at report scale; feeds [[catalhoyuk]] chronology and building biography (concurrent densification). Absolute chronological claims must stay date-stamped **(as of Bayliss et al. / Hodder ed. 2022)**. No per-building wiki pages; no new `studies/` page required unless later curated as a methods exemplar.

**Relation to *Leopard’s Tale* (2006).** The 2006 synthesis remains the public interpretive narrative of mid-project results; the 2022 volume supplies the primary architectural and dating evidence that extends (and where Bayesian models revise) that narrative’s chronological scaffolding.
