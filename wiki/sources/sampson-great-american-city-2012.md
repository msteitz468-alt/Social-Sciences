# Great American City (Sampson 2012)

## Bibliographic note

Robert J. Sampson, *Great American City: Chicago and the Enduring Neighborhood Effect*. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2012. Foreword by [[wilson-william-julius|William Julius Wilson]]. Ingested from the full PDF (553 pp.).

## What this source is

Landmark multi-method synthesis arguing that **neighborhood differentiation endures** in the face of globalization and “placelessness” narratives, and that a **family of neighborhood effects** operates through community-level social processes (especially **[[collective-efficacy]]**), spatial interdependence, organizational structure, and higher-order sorting — not reducible to individual selection alone. Empirically grounded in the **Project on Human Development in Chicago Neighborhoods (PHDCN)** and related Chicago Project data (~1990s–2010 revisit). Simultaneously: intellectual history of neighborhood-effects research; research-project memoir; Chicago urban sociology in the Chicago School lineage; theory of context; empirical map of community processes (inequality, disorder, civic life, altruism, migration, elite networks).

Evidence classes: community surveys; [[systematic-social-observation|systematic social observation]] (SSO); [[ecometrics]]; longitudinal cohort data; administrative records (crime, births, housing); network surveys of elites (KeyNet); natural experiments / MTO engagement; lost-letter field measure; 2010 field revisit. ## Volume Synthesis Note

*Great American City* is the major book-length architecture of contemporary neighborhood-effects research: multi-method PHDCN / Chicago Project evidence that **place endures**, that community **processes** ([[collective-efficacy]], organizations, altruism, cynicism, looking-glass disorder) mediate structure and outcomes, that neighborhoods are **spatially interdependent**, and that **selection is a social process** (“structural sorting”) rather than a nuisance that voids context. Methodologically it codifies **[[ecometrics]]** and large-scale **[[systematic-social-observation]]**. Theoretically it reworks [[chicago-school|Chicago School]] ecology without urban-village romanticism, dialogues with [[wilson-william-julius|Wilson]]’s concentration program, and challenges strong [[broken-windows]] and experimental-hegemony readings of MTO. The 2010 revisit (foreclosures, CHA, letter-drop stickiness) argues crisis reorganized but did not erase deep structure. Wiki coverage now has a primary-text basis for Positions A/D on [[neighborhood-effects-debate]] and Position 3 on [[broken-windows-debate]]; cross-city experimental monographs remain secondary via Sampson’s citations.
