# In the Society of Nature

Philippe Descola, *In the Society of Nature: A Native Ecology in Amazonia* (CUP 1994; tr. Nora Scott from *La Nature domestique*, 1986). Landmark [[achuar]] ethnography: technical resource management interwoven with a cosmology that endows "nature" with the characteristics of society. Author: [[descola-philippe]]. **Study page:** [[descola-society-of-nature-study]]. **Studies Hub:** [[hubs/studies/descola-society-of-nature-study]]. ## Claims Extracted

Principal load-bearing claims (attributed to Descola 1986/1994 unless noted):

1. **Equal heuristic weight** to quantitative and qualitative data; refuse hard line between technical and conceptual determinations (Preface).
2. Amazonian ethnography trapped between **semiological** and **ecological-reductionist** monisms; practice-centered alternative (General introduction).
3. **Household** as analytic pole of domestic socialization of nature; endogamous nexus 10–15 houses; Dravidian marriage; war mortality ~1/2 male, ~1/5 female deaths.
4. Rich **ethnoecology** (soils, *yumi*/*entza* waters, biotopes, calendar) not only utilitarian.
5. Beings ranked by **sociability/speech** (*aents*, *wakan*) — continuum, not nature/culture cut.
6. **Garden** = consanguineal world (Nunkui; plant children; gardening magic); pioneering [[swidden]]; manioc staple; large unused potential.
7. **Forest** = affinal world (game mothers/*amana*; hunting [[anent]] as *sai*); ~14.2 kg/trip; no riverine edible-game advantage once taboos applied.
8. **River** = mediating marital bed (Tsunki); fish lower soul/*anent* rank.
9. **Good life (*shiir waras*)**: ~3.5 h/day subsistence labor; diet ~3408 kcal / 104.5 g protein; under-exploitation of labor and manioc potential.
10. **Positions:** against Gross protein-limitation hyperdeterminism; against Sahlins DMP chronic underproduction generalization; against biotope-compelled actualization.
11. **Conclusion:** nature as domestic continuum; homeostasis via social labor ceilings + kinship socialization of nature.
