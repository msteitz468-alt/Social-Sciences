# The Golden Bough (Frazer, 1922 abridgement)

## Bibliographic record

Sir James George Frazer, *The Golden Bough: A Study in Magic and Religion*. Abridged edition. London: Macmillan, 1922 (first abridgement of the third edition, 12 vols., 1911–15). First edition 1890 (2 vols.); second edition 1900 (3 vols.). Ingest source: Lux Occulta Press 2009 PDF (Microsoft Word-derived text layer).

## What this source is

Landmark **armchair comparative synthesis** of magic, religion, kingship, and ritual across world ethnography and classical antiquity. Framed as solving the riddle of the Arician priesthood at Nemi (the King of the Wood who must kill his predecessor after plucking the Golden Bough), the work elaborates a vast program: [[sympathetic-magic]], the evolutionary sequence magic → religion → science, [[taboo]], the [[divine-king|killing of the divine king]], dying-and-rising vegetation gods (Adonis, Attis, Osiris, Dionysus, Demeter), the [[scapegoat]], fire-festivals, and the [[external-soul]]. Dual-file with study page [[frazer-golden-bough-study]] and Studies Hub; thinker [[frazer-james-george]] and Thinkers Hub.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Golden Bough* (1922 abridgement of the third edition) is the wiki’s charter primary for **armchair comparative religion** and the [[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion|intellectualist]] branch of Victorian evolutionism. Frazer opens with the Nemi *Rex Nemorensis* riddle and builds outward: [[sympathetic-magic]] as false science (Laws of Similarity and Contact); magic → religion → science as successive theories of thought; [[taboo]] as negative magic; the [[divine-king]] killed before decay; [[dying-and-rising-god]] and [[vegetation-spirit]] cycles (Adonis, Attis, Osiris, Dionysus, Demeter, European corn-spirits); [[scapegoat]] expulsion of evils; [[external-soul]] and the mistletoe–Golden Bough identification; the Farewell’s three-threaded web of magic, religion, and science.

The work dual-files as theoretical program and landmark research act ([[frazer-golden-bough-study]] + Studies Hub; [[frazer-james-george]] + Thinkers Hub; Theory Hub on intellectualism). Methodologically it is the emblematic case of second-hand trait-lifting under evolutionary headings — Galton uncontrolled, no fieldwork, colonial and classical source base. Substantive evolutionary claims stay in attributed voice; `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested`. What survives in current discourse is vocabulary (sympathetic magic, scapegoat, divine king, dying god as historiographic object) stripped of unilinear ranking. Gaps: full 12-volume third edition notes; Ackerman biography as secondary source; companion *Totemism and Exogamy*; later critiques (J.Z. Smith, Evans-Pritchard monographs) not yet ingested as primaries.
