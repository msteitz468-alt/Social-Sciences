# Ancient Middle Niger (McIntosh 2005)

Roderick J. McIntosh, *Ancient Middle Niger: Urbanism and the Self-Organizing Landscape* (Cambridge University Press, 2005), in Rita P. Wright’s **Case Studies in Early Societies** series. Synthesis of Middle Niger urban archaeology centered on the [[jenne-jeno|Jenné-jeno]] research program, arguing for **clustered, non-nucleated cities without centralized state-focused power** — a [[heterarchy|heterarchical]] “City without Citadel” path to complexity.

## Bibliographic note

- **Author:** Roderick J. McIntosh (Rice University; visiting archaeology, University of Pretoria). Long-term collaborator: [[mcintosh-susan-keech|Susan Keech McIntosh]] (excavations and ceramic sequence).
- **Prior McIntosh works cited in series blurb / preface:** *The Peoples of the Middle Niger* (1998); *The Way the Wind Blows* (2000); *Geomorphology and Human Palaeoecology of the Méma, Mali* (2005).
- **Genre:** Teaching-oriented case study + research synthesis; primary findings and theory intertwined — treat empirical sequence as attributed McIntosh program results; treat Pulse Model / self-organization / co-evolution as theory.

## Volume Synthesis Note

McIntosh’s book is the single best teaching synthesis of the argument that **early Middle Niger urbanism is indigenous, clustered, and heterarchical**. Empirically, it anchors the [[jenne-jeno]] ceramic/metallurgical sequence (~250 BCE–AD 1400), the [[urban-cluster]] settlement form, and systematic hinterland survey results, with [[dia-shoma|Dia]] as peer. Theoretically, it elaborates the [[pulse-model]] and [[self-organizing-landscape]] as process language for specialist accommodation under floodplain risk, and exports a Middle Niger lens to Mesopotamia, the Nile, and northern China. The vault already held Connah and handbook secondary densifications; this ingest supplies the primary program voice and a Studies Hub analysis. Standing tension — Connah’s negative-evidence caution vs McIntosh’s City without Citadel thesis — is preserved on [[heterarchy]] and the study pages without wiki-voice adjudication.
