# The Consequences of Modernity (Giddens, 1990)

[[giddens-anthony|Anthony Giddens]], *The Consequences of Modernity* (Stanford: Stanford University Press / Cambridge: Polity Press, 1990). Extended essay developing a **discontinuist** account of modernity: disembedding mechanisms ([[symbolic-tokens]], [[expert-systems]]), [[trust]] in [[abstract-systems]], the [[reflexive-modernization|reflexivity of modernity]], [[globalization]] as inherent to modern institutions, and the case for **[[radicalised-modernity]]** rather than postmodernity. Builds on [[structuration-theory]] and [[time-space-distanciation]] from *[[giddens-constitution-of-society-1984|The Constitution of Society]]* (1984).

## What the book is

A short programmatic theory text (Giddens: "essentially… an extended essay"), divided into continuous **sections** rather than numbered chapters, to keep argument flow uninterrupted. It characterises modernity’s institutional dimensions (capitalism, industrialism, surveillance, military power), the dynamics of trust and risk, the transformation of intimacy, and political orientations under high-consequence risk (utopian realism; social movements). Empirical illustrations are **illustrations**, not findings.

Not a `studies/` page candidate — pure theory / program essay.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Consequences* is the short programmatic pivot from Giddens’s 1984 structuration outline to his 1990s modernity/self-identity/politics phase. Its load-bearing moves for this wiki are: (1) **discontinuist** modernity against evolutionist and single-dynamic classical diagnoses; (2) the **disembedding / abstract-systems / trust** apparatus that operationalises extended time-space for institutional analysis; (3) **globalisation** as inherent and multi-dimensional (not reducible to world-economy or IR); (4) **radicalised modernity** as the standing position against present-tense postmodernity claims, while keeping a utopian-realist sketch of a *future* post-modern order; (5) explicit sibling engagement with Beck on risk without merging programs. All claims remain attributed theory. OCR source quality is usable with minor garble; a few mid-section jumps (juggernaut factors; social-movements map) limit quote completeness for those passages only.
