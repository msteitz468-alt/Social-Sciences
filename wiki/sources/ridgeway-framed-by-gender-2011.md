## Volume Synthesis Note

*Framed by Gender* is the book-length statement of Ridgeway's answer to the **how** of gender-inequality persistence in the contemporary United States. Gender is a **[[primary-frame|primary cultural frame]]** for coordinating social relations; shared **[[status-beliefs|gender status beliefs]]** (especially diffuse male advantage in general competence/status) make inequality categorical; interpersonal processes under the frame re-embed men and women in unequal **positions** at work and home; when new practices form, **lagging** common-knowledge beliefs get written into them — the "heart of the persistence process." Leveling forces (bureaucracy, rights, women's labor supply) compete with that reconstructive dynamic; post-1990s stall is real; equality is not impossible without ending the frame, but requires eroding diffuse status beliefs, revaluing caregiving (men in routine care), and shrinking gender's task relevance.

**Relation to already-ingested work:** complements [[doing-gender]] (sex category/accountability) with coordination and EST mechanisms; dialogues with [[durable-inequality-theory]] (categorical inequality) while stressing status beliefs and interpersonal re-inscription; links to [[second-shift]] / [[work-family-conflict]] via family-devotion vs [[ideal-worker-norm]] and [[maternal-wall]]; draws dual-structure language from Giddens/Sewell. **Voice:** theory and single-study findings attributed throughout. **Hub:** Theory Hub built in-session (selection criteria met: research program organizing a generation of gender/status work).
