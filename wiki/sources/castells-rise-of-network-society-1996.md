## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Rise of the Network Society* (Vol. I) is the **founding architectural volume** of Castells’s Information Age program: technology, economy, organization, work, culture, space, and time under a new social morphology — the [[network-society|network society]] — powered by an IT paradigm and a mode of development called [[informationalism]], without capitalism’s abolition. The 2010 preface re-enters after financial crisis and Web 2.0 (global automaton; mass self-communication) without abandoning the 1996 frame.

**What it adds to this wiki:** (1) first full [[network-society-theory]] + Theory Hub; (2) [[castells-manuel]] thinker page + study page; (3) concept kit ([[network-society]], [[informationalism]], [[mode-of-development]], [[space-of-flows]], [[space-of-places]], [[timeless-time]], [[real-virtuality]], [[network-enterprise]], [[informational-city]], [[spirit-of-informationalism]]); (4) [[flexible-employment]] phenomenon; (5) dense bridge to existing [[global-city-theory]] / [[sassen-saskia]] and [[globalization]] / [[urbanization]]; (6) Weber echo via spirit of informationalism ↔ [[protestant-ethic-thesis]].

**Tensions / limits:** Vols II–III absent (identity, movements, state, fourth world); G-7 weight on employment; grand-theory critique surface; 1990s new-economy details age without 2010 crisis frame; *The Informational City* (1989) and *Urban Question* not ingested. Complementary not identical to Sassen: process/morphology of flows vs production of control capacity in places.
