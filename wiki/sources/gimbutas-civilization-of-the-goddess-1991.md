# The Civilization of the Goddess (Gimbutas, 1991)

## Bibliographic Record

Marija Gimbutas, *The Civilization of the Goddess: The World of Old Europe*, edited by Joan Marler (San Francisco: HarperSanFrancisco, 1991). Author: [[gimbutas-marija]]. Thinkers Hub: [[hubs/thinkers/gimbutas-marija]].

Pan-European synthesis of Neolithic and Copper Age Europe (~6500–3500 BCE, later in the west/north) as **[[old-europe|Old Europe]]** — a goddess-centered, artistically sophisticated, largely peaceful agricultural world — disrupted and hybridized by steppe pastoralist (**Kurgan**) intrusions that Gimbutas equates with Indo-Europeanization. ## Key Claims Extracted

**Split: THESES (Gimbutas) vs reported FACTS as she states them.** Nothing below is wiki-voice adjudication.

### THESES (quarantined — debates / attributed)

- **Civilization redefinition:** artistic creation, nonmaterial values, sex balance — not warrior hierarchy (Preface).
- **Old Europe** as gynocentric, Goddess-worshipping, largely peaceful Neolithic–Copper Age Europe before IE.
- **Goddess religion:** four-group typology under one Nature deity; rejects Venus/fertility-charm readings; Paleolithic continuity.
- **Sacred script:** non-administrative writing ~5300–4300 BCE; disappears with Kurgan-related collapse.
- **Matristic/gylany society:** no IE-type chiefdoms; rejects mirror-image matriarchy.
- **Three Kurgan waves** (I c. 4400–4300, II c. 3500, III ~3000 BCE) = Indo-Europeanization; dual symbolic structures hybridize with IE prevailing and OE as undercurrent.

### FACT inventories (still “Gimbutas reports,” date-stamped 1991)

- Culture-group distributions and calibrated chronologies for SE/central, northern, Adriatic, and western Neolithic sequences.
- Cucuteni megasite sizes; Varna gold inventory; Rudna Glava / Ai Bunar mines; temple models (Cascioarele, Achilleion, Sabatinivka).
- Winn’s Vinča sign catalogue (210 signs, 29 sites); Turdaș/Tărtăria find history.
- Steppe site inventories (S’ezzhee, Dereivka, Suvorovo, Usatovo, Mikhailovka, etc.) and burial-type contrasts (barrow/pit vs OE cemeteries).

### METHOD

- Multidisciplinary archaeology + mythology + linguistics + early historic data.
- Calibrated radiocarbon (B.C. vs b.c. convention stated in Preface).
- Culture-historical ethnic labeling and pan-European synthesis.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Civilization of the Goddess* is Gimbutas’s late **total package**: the first half maps Neolithic Europe as a single civilizational field with regional styles; the second half argues that field was religiously Goddess-centered, socially matristic, literate in a sacred script, and destroyed/hybridized by steppe Kurgan waves she equates with Indo-Europeanization. The Preface’s redefinition of “civilization” is load-bearing — without it, Neolithic towns and art do not count as civilization against Renfrew-style or urban-state criteria.

For this wiki the book’s value is dual and must stay split: (1) it is the **primary Position A voice** for the classic Kurgan multi-wave narrative and for the Old Europe brand that Anthony later narrows and that aDNA partially reweights demographically; (2) its Goddess–peace–gylany package is a **contested interpretive system** filed on [[old-europe-goddess-civilization-debate]], not a finding. Four-way non-identity is mandatory when she equates cultures, languages, and peoples. Chronologies are as of early-1990s calibration.
