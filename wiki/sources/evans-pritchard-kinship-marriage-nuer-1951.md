## Volume Synthesis Note

*Kinship and Marriage among the Nuer* is the domestic middle volume of the Nuer trilogy. Three load-bearing moves: (1) **local community as marriage-derived kinship mesh** (*buth* vs *mar*; village cases); (2) **cattle bridewealth as jural instrument** creating pater-status and scaffolding affinity into kinship (ideal ~40 / usual 20–30 / floor ~16); (3) **union typology** with simple legal marriage as standard — especially [[ghost-marriage]] (legal husband = ghost) distinguished from true [[levirate]] and widow-concubinage. Completes the political skeleton of *[[evans-pritchard-nuer-1940|The Nuer]]* and pairs with *[[evans-pritchard-nuer-religion-1956|Nuer Religion]]* on ghosts. Outstanding for deeper restudy comparison remains later Sudan ethnography.
