# Tristes Tropiques (1955)

Lévi-Strauss's celebrated reflexive account of his Brazilian fieldwork and his vocation as an anthropologist, opening with the famous anti-travelogue line "I hate travelling and explorers." It reports on four indigenous groups — the [[caduveo|Caduveo]] (facial/body art), the [[bororo|Bororo]] (village plan and dual/triadic organization), the [[nambikwara|Nambikwara]] (nomadic bands, chieftainship, writing), and the [[tupi-kawahib|Tupi-Kawahib]] — while meditating on culture contact, the destruction of indigenous worlds, and the melancholy of a discipline built on vanishing others. As a text it is a primary source both for the four societies (dated to 1935–1939) and for the history and reflexivity of anthropology.

## Key Claims Extracted

Ethnographic particulars are attributed field observations dated 1935–39; philosophical theses are attributed authorial reflection.

- **Ethnography (1935–39):** four Brazilian groups — [[caduveo|Caduveo]] (facial/body art read as symbolic resolution of social contradiction), [[bororo|Bororo]] (circular village plan encoding moiety/clan structure and cosmology; death as "anticultural"), [[nambikwara|Nambikwara]] ([[chieftainship-by-consent]]; the "writing lesson"), [[tupi-kawahib|Tupi-Kawahib]] (a "last untouched" band dissolving at contact).
- **Method/roots of structuralism:** the anti-travelogue opening ("I hate travelling and explorers"); the "three mistresses" — geology, Marxism, psychoanalysis — as the shared model of the [[structuralism|structural method]] ("to understand is to reduce one type of reality to another; true reality is never the most manifest").
- **Political anthropology:** [[chieftainship-by-consent]] — power resting on consent and generosity, not coercion (the Nambikwara chief as "banker").
- **Reflexivity/critique:** the ethnographer's paradox; cannibalism vs Western "anthropémie" (incarceration); Rousseau as "the most ethnographic of philosophers"; "[[entropology]]" and "the world began without man and it will end without him."
- **Charged material (handle as attributed positions):** the closing comparative-religion polemic on Islam, Buddhism, and Christianity is opinionated and superseded by nothing in the text — recorded in strict attributed voice, never wiki voice.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Tristes Tropiques* is dual-valued for the wiki: a primary source for four indigenous societies (dated to the fieldwork moment, against the ethnographic-present trap) and a primary source for the **history and reflexivity of anthropology**. Composed 1954–55, a decade after the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]*, it supplies the autobiographical and methodological backstory of [[structuralism]] — showing that the structural method's roots (geology, Marx, Freud) predate Lévi-Strauss's encounter with linguistics — and is an early reflexive text anticipating the 1980s *Writing Culture* critique of ethnographic authority. Its melancholy over vanishing worlds ("entropology") and its critique of exoticism make it, with the *Elementary Structures*, the collection's anchor for structuralism. The book is the complete French Plon 1955 edition; the opening travelogue/regional-geography chapters were extracted lightly, the ethnographic and reflexive chapters in full.
