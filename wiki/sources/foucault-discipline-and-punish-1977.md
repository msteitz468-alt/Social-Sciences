## Volume Synthesis Note

*Discipline and Punish* is the technical charter of Foucault's mid-1970s analytics of power applied to penality and the body. Its arc runs: sovereign **torture** (scaffold as political ritual; four rules; political economy of the body) → reformist **punishment** as semio-technique of signs (the path not taken) → **discipline** as arts of distribution, training, examination, and panoptic diagram → **prison** as concentration of discipline within law, producer of delinquency, node of a carceral archipelago that naturalizes the power to punish. The book's most portable contributions to this vault are (1) a full architecture of [[disciplinary-power]] complementary to *History of Sexuality*'s [[biopower]] program; (2) [[panopticism]] and the examination as power-knowledge rituals; (3) the reverse of institutional "failure" that feeds [[instrument-effects]] readings (Ferguson); (4) an exemplary [[genealogy]] of the present. Tensions with existing pages: productive power vs. Marxist instrumental state; genealogical discontinuity vs. social-historical continuity accounts; panoptic metaphor stretch in later surveillance studies — recorded on the study and theory pages, not adjudicated in wiki voice. Studies Hub deep analysis built in-session; Thinkers Hub (Foucault) and Theory Hub (analytics of power) remain warranted open densifications now that both charter texts are in the vault.
