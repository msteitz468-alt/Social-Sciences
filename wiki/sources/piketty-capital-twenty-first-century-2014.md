# Capital in the Twenty-First Century (Piketty, 2013/2014)

Bibliographic record for [[piketty-thomas|Thomas Piketty]]'s *Capital in the Twenty-First Century* (*Le capital au XXIe siècle*, 2013; Goldhammer English 2014). Landmark historical and theoretical synthesis of the long-run dynamics of wealth and income inequality: the capital/income ratio β, the capital share α = r × β, the second fundamental law β = s/g, and the central tendency **r > g** as a structural force favoring capital concentration under low-growth patrimonial capitalism — with policy lessons for the social state, progressive income taxation, a global progressive tax on capital, and public debt.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Capital in the Twenty-First Century* is a **founding public synthesis** of the collaborative top-incomes and historical-wealth-accounts program: multi-century β series, top shares, inheritance arithmetic, and a political diagnosis centered on **[[r-greater-than-g]]**. It rejects both Marxist apocalypse (growth and knowledge diffusion avoided it) and Kuznets automatic harmony (mid-century compression was largely war- and policy-driven). The return of high private capital/income ratios under slow growth frames a risk of [[patrimonial-capitalism]], complicated by the distinct US path of [[supermanagers|supermanager]] labor-income inequality.

**What it adds to this wiki:** (1) first full [[piketty-capital-dynamics]] research-program page; (2) [[piketty-thomas]] thinker page; (3) study + **Studies Hub**; (4) concept kit (β, fundamental laws, r > g, patrimonial capitalism/middle class, supermanagers, progressive global wealth tax, Kuznets curve as foil); (5) methods [[historical-wealth-accounts]] and [[top-income-shares]]; (6) phenomena [[wealth-inequality]] and [[income-inequality]]; (7) institutions [[social-state]] and [[progressive-taxation]]; (8) debate scaffold [[piketty-capital-debate]]; (9) bridges to [[economic-sociology]], [[social-mobility]], and classical interlocutors (Marx).
