## Volume Synthesis Note

*Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity* (1999) is Rappaport's posthumous summa: religion is central to the continuing evolution of life and co-extensive with the invention of language and culture; ritual is the main component that constructs religious conceptions and has been central in humanity's adaptation. The book systematically restates and expands the formal ritual theory of *[[rappaport-ecology-meaning-religion-1979|Ecology, Meaning, and Religion]]* across fourteen chapters, then closes with adaptive architecture ([[cybernetics-of-the-holy]]) and a diagnosis of modern desecration (idolatry, lies of oppression, diabolical lies, law–meaning dissonance) toward "post-modern science and natural religion."

**What it adds to the wiki:** (1) book-length restatement of [[liturgical-order]] (five features; self-referential vs canonical; time/hierarchy/communitas); (2) full sanctity architecture — sacred vs sanctified, USP, grounds of unquestionableness — plus [[deutero-truth]]; (3) [[logos-rappaport]] / comparative logoi; (4) [[cybernetics-of-the-holy]] as closed adaptive loop; (5) densified [[sanctity]], [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]], [[adaptive-structure]], thinker + Thinkers Hub; (6) Maring illustrations still date-stamped to 1960s fieldwork (with notes on later discontinuities). **What it leaves thin:** primary Peirce/Austin/Otto monographs; specialist critiques of comparative logoi; reception after 1999.
