# The History of Melanesian Society (Rivers, 1914)

Two-volume work by [[rivers-w-h-r|W. H. R. Rivers]], product of the Percy Sladen Trust
Expedition to Melanesia. Volume I presents a regional survey of systems of relationship
and social organisation collected by the [[genealogical-method|genealogical method]];
Volume II offers a theoretical reconstruction of Melanesian culture-history that marks
Rivers' public conversion from [[social-evolutionism|evolutionism]] to
[[diffusionism|diffusionist]] ("historical") analysis. A landmark of British kinship
studies and a methodological reference point; its substantive historical conclusions are
largely superseded. See the study page [[rivers-melanesian-society-study]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The History of Melanesian Society* is two books with one spine. **Volume I** is a
disciplined regional survey of kinship and social organisation, collected by the
[[genealogical-method]] and graded island by island for reliability — Rivers at his most
scrupulous, separating recorded fact from inference and naming every informant. **Volume II**
is a theoretical edifice built on that data, and it records, in real time, Rivers' conversion
from the "current English [evolutionary] school" to a [[diffusionism|historical/diffusionist]]
analysis: a morphological comparison of relationship systems that persuaded him Melanesian
society is "complex" — the fused product of successive immigrant peoples — followed by a
linguistic comparison and a full migration reconstruction (kava-people, betel-people,
sitting-interment people). The work's lasting value is inverted relative to its ambition: the
*method* (genealogical collection, morphological analysis of terminology, evidence-grading)
became permanent equipment, while the *conjectural history* was abandoned within two decades by
the functionalists who inherited his fieldwork standards. Two tensions with the existing wiki
are worth flagging. First, Rivers is the **apostate** of [[social-evolutionism]] and the
British anchor of the [[diffusionism]] its adjacent pages ([[historical-particularism]],
[[structural-functionalism]]) define themselves against. Second, his reconstruction repeatedly
runs culture, language, and physical type together (sorting Pacific peoples into "higher"
immigrant and "lower" aboriginal strata in period racial vocabulary) — a standing violation of
the four-way non-identity rule, handled here as attributed authorial period voice, never wiki
voice. A **Studies-Hub** analysis is warranted (the work founded a method and anchors a standing
debate) but was not built in this session given the two-volume scope; flagged for user decision.
