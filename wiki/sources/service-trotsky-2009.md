# Trotsky: A Biography (Robert Service, 2009)

Full-length archival biography of Lev Davidovich Bronstein (Leon Trotsky, 1879–1940): Ukrainian Jewish upbringing, revolutionary apprenticeship, 1905 Petersburg Soviet, emigration and journalism, 1917 seizure of power, Red Army command in the Civil War, the Left Opposition and defeat by [[stalin-joseph|Stalin]], exile (Turkey, France, Norway, Mexico), the Fourth International, and assassination in 1940. For this wiki the source feeds Trotsky as a Marxist *thinker* — [[permanent-revolution]], his debates with [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] and [[luxemburg-rosa|Luxemburg]], his sociology of Soviet bureaucracy — plus the historiography of his posthumous reputation.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Service's biography is a sustained revisionist case against the Deutscher tradition: its culminating verdict is that Trotsky was "close to Stalin in intentions and practice... no more likely than Stalin to create a society of humanitarian socialism," that he "revelled in terror," and that his followers "invented a man and a leader who bore only an erratic kinship to Lev Davidovich Trotsky." Service also deflates the originality of Trotsky's theory — the "proletarian government" idea from Parvus, the backwardness analysis from liberal Westernizing historians (Chicherin), the USSR analysis owing "much to the Mensheviks." All such verdicts are recorded across the wiki strictly as Service's positions.

For this wiki the volume's chief yields are: (1) the genealogy and content of [[permanent-revolution]]; (2) Trotsky's two-phase bureaucracy analysis (1923 *New Course*; 1937 *The Revolution Betrayed*) and the [[russian-question-debate]] it provoked among his own followers — a genuine episode in the sociology of state socialism ending in Burnham's managerialism; (3) documented-by-drafts evidence of memoir self-fashioning, a methods lesson in autobiography as source; (4) the "Trotsky and the Jews" material on assimilationist Marxism and the antisemitic "Judeo-Bolshevism" trope. Tension with existing holdings: Lenin pages built from Lenin's primary texts now carry Service's hostile-witness framing of the Lenin–Trotsky relation — registers kept separate. Trotsky's own works are not yet ingested; every theoretical claim on the new pages is currently mediated by an adversarial biographer, which is the collection's most significant gap on this figure.
