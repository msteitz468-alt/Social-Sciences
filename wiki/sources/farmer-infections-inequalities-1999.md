# Infections and Inequalities (Farmer, 1999)

Bibliographic record for [[farmer-paul|Paul Farmer]]'s *Infections and Inequalities: The Modern
Plagues* (University of California Press, 1999; updated paperback 2001). A physician-anthropologist's
programmatic argument that infectious disease among the poor — tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS — is patterned by
**[[structural-violence|structural violence]]**, and that social scientists and epidemiologists routinely
make **[[immodest-claims-of-causality|immodest claims of causality]]** that mistake the effects of poverty
and inequality for cultural difference or individual agency. Methodological signature: **[[biosocial-analysis|biosocial analysis]]**
linking molecular epidemiology to history, ethnography, and political economy. Principal field sites:
**[[do-kay|Do Kay]]** and Haiti's Central Plateau; the MDR-TB project in Carabayllo, Lima, Peru.
