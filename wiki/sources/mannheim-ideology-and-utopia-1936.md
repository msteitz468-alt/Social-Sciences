# Ideology and Utopia (Mannheim, 1936)

Karl Mannheim, *Ideology and Utopia: An Introduction to the Sociology of Knowledge*, translated by Louis Wirth and Edward Shils (London: Routledge & Kegan Paul, 1936). Foundational English-language statement of classical **[[sociology-of-knowledge]]** (*Wissenssoziologie*). Composite volume: Parts II–IV from German *Ideologie und Utopie* (1929); Part V from the 1931 encyclopaedia article "Wissenssoziologie"; Part I written for this English edition. Preface by [[wirth-louis|Louis Wirth]].

## What the book is

A theoretical treatise arguing that thought is existentially bound to social location (*Seinsverbundenheit*), developing the paired concepts **[[ideology]]** and **[[utopia|utopian mentality]]**, proposing **[[relationism]]** (not crude relativism) as the sociology of knowledge's epistemic stance, and assigning a synthesizing role to the **[[free-floating-intelligentsia|socially unattached intelligentsia]]**. It is the primary classical source against which Berger & Luckmann later redefined the field toward everyday knowledge ([[social-construction-of-reality]]).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Ideology and Utopia* is the English classical charter of the [[sociology-of-knowledge]]. Its decisive architecture is fourfold: (1) **[[ideology]]** escalated from particular interest-unmasking to total and then **general** total ideology (own standpoint included); (2) **[[utopia]]** as the order-shattering twin of ideology, morphologized into four modern mentalities; (3) **[[relationism]]** and particularization as the epistemic answer to sterile relativism after mutual unmasking ([[relationism-vs-relativism-debate]]); (4) **[[free-floating-intelligentsia]]** and styles of thought as the political-sociological path toward synthesis without pretending politics is natural science.

For the wiki, the book fills the classical gap left when Berger & Luckmann were ingested first: their redefinition toward everyday knowledge and [[social-construction-of-reality]] now has its primary foil and ancestor on disk.
