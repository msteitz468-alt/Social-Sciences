# Islam, Archaeology and History: Gao Region (Mali) ca. AD 900–1250 (Insoll 1996)

Timothy Insoll’s BAR monograph on the **Gao region** of the Middle Niger (modern Mali), focusing on roughly **AD 900–1250** — the period in which Arabic sources and material evidence together document an urban entrepôt drawn into the Islamic and trans-Saharan world. Complements the later programmatic *[[insoll-archaeology-of-islam-1999|The Archaeology of Islam]]* (1999) with a **regional case**: excavation, survey historiography, trade assemblages, and the archaeology of Islamization at [[gao-ancien]] and [[gao-saney]].

## Relation to Insoll 1999

The 1996 Gao monograph is the **empirical West African anchor** for the 1999 program: everyday trade goods, burial practice, urban form, and Islamization as process rather than monument checklist. Read 1996 for case findings (attributed, date-stamped); 1999 for cross-regional theory.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Insoll 1996 is the **fieldwork spine** for his later 1999 *Archaeology of Islam* program, applied to the Gao Middle Niger entrepôt (ca. AD 900–1250). It re-synthesizes poorly documented colonial cemetery digs at Gao-Saney, adds a 1993 stratigraphic sequence at Gao Ancien, and argues that Islamization here is (1) **trade-vectored**, (2) **socially stratified** (Islamized quartier vs rural lag), and (3) **materially multi-domain** (burial, brick, imports, mosque) rather than monument-only. Chronology and import provenances are mid-1990s science — later campaigns may revise absolute dates. Four-way non-identity is load-bearing: archaeological Gao is not Songhay ethnicity or empire.
