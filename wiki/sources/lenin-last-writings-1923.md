# Lenin's Last Writings, 1922–1923

Lenin's final dictations, combining the last published articles (NEP, the state apparatus, the theory of the revolution) with the suppressed "Letter to the Congress." Single-agent extraction of two targeted segments from the combined Collected Works file (Vols. 33 and 36).

## Load-bearing content (all attributed)

- **"On Co-operation":** under NEP, co-operatives plus proletarian state power are "all that is necessary and sufficient" for socialism — "a radical modification in our whole outlook on socialism"; the shift to "cultural work" among the peasantry ([[new-economic-policy]], [[peasantry-as-class]]).
- **"Our Revolution":** against Sukhanov and Second-International "pedantry" — Marxism's "revolutionary dialectics" permits the reversal of the customary historical sequence: seize power first, build the cultural prerequisites after ([[historical-materialism]], [[dialectical-materialism]]).
- **Rabkrin articles:** the Soviet state apparatus as "a survival of the past... slightly anointed with Soviet oil"; "Better fewer, but better"; "first, to learn, secondly, to learn, and thirdly, to learn" ([[bureaucracy]], [[the-state]]).
- **Letter to the Congress (Testament):** characterizations of the CC leaders — Stalin's "unlimited authority" and the January 4, 1923 addition recommending his removal as General Secretary ("Stalin is too rude..."); Trotsky "personally perhaps the most capable man in the present C.C." but with "excessive self-assurance"; Bukharin "the favourite of the whole Party" who "never fully understood" dialectics; the Stalin–Trotsky relation as the chief split risk ([[stalin-joseph]], [[trotsky-leon]]).
- **The nationalities dictation:** the Georgian affair; the "Great-Russian chauvinist... in substance a rascal and a tyrant, such as the typical Russian bureaucrat is"; oppressor-nation internationalism as compensatory inequality; political responsibility laid on Stalin and Dzerzhinsky ([[the-state]], [[bureaucracy]]).

## Reliability

The Testament's transmission history (secret dictation, restricted congress reading in 1924, suppression until 1956) makes provenance part of any claim built on it; the versions ingested are the canonical post-1956 texts as printed by Progress Publishers. The last articles were published in Pravda in Lenin's lifetime and are textually uncontroversial.
