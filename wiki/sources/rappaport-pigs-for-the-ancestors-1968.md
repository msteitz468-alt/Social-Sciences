## Volume Synthesis Note

*Pigs for the Ancestors* (1968; enlarged 1984) is the vault's primary source for **systems-ecology ethnography of ritual** and for the [[tsembaga|Tsembaga Maring]] case. The 1968 core (Ch.1–6 + Apps 1–10) argues that the multi-year cycle culminating in the [[kaiko]] regulates people–pig–garden relations and articulates local ecosystems with a regional Maring system of war, alliance, and exchange ([[ritual-regulation]]). Measurement (herd 169→~60 across the 1962–63 kaiko; energy ratios; diet; [[carrying-capacity]] estimates) is the warrant against Homans-style internalist ritual theory. Chapter 6 supplies [[cognized-model]] / [[operational-model]] and the homeostat/transducer framing.

The **1984 Epilogue** is a second theoretical book: answers to Friedman (vulgar materialism), Sahlins (practical reason), McArthur (nutrition), Ellen/Bennett (units), and others — see [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]]. Appendix 11 reworks nutrition and softens 1968 nutritional emphasis. This ingest used the 1984 DJVU text layer (~169k words); some appendix tables are OCR-gappy; main-text arguments are complete.

**What it adds to the wiki:** a Melanesian horticultural society page; a landmark study + Studies Hub; a thinker + Thinkers Hub for Rappaport; four concepts (cognized/operational models, kaiko, ritual regulation); densification of [[cultural-ecology]] and [[carrying-capacity]]; an open multiparty debate page. **What it leaves thin:** full Maring comparative ethnography beyond Tsembaga; post-1984 ecological anthropology and political ecology critiques; Rappaport's later religion monographs.
