# The Elementary Structures of Kinship (1949)

Claude Lévi-Strauss's first major work and the founding text of **alliance theory** in kinship studies. It argues that the incest prohibition is the threshold between nature and culture, and that positive marriage rules (especially cross-cousin marriage) are systems of **reciprocal exchange of women** that generate alliance and social solidarity. "Elementary structures" are systems that positively prescribe or preferentially designate a category of spouse; "complex structures" leave spouse selection to other mechanisms. See the study page [[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-study]] for analysis of the argument as a research act.

## Key Claims Extracted

All attributed to Lévi-Strauss (theoretical work, attributed voice throughout):

- The [[incest-taboo|incest prohibition]] is the positive **rule of the gift** and the hinge between nature and culture ([[nature-culture-distinction]]); "a rule obliging the mother, sister or daughter to be given to others."
- Positive marriage rules realize two elementary forms — [[restricted-exchange]] (direct/reciprocal) and [[generalized-exchange]] (indirect/cyclical) — the theoretical core of [[alliance-theory]].
- Which form develops depends on the **harmonic/disharmonic** relation of descent and residence rules; generalized exchange is a "speculation on credit" tending toward anisogamy, hypergamy, and caste endogamy.
- **Alliance, not descent**, is the generative principle of kinship: "it is men who exchange women" (the [[exchange-of-women]] premise, later heavily contested).
- Cross-cousin marriage ([[cross-cousin-marriage]]) is the test case: cross vs parallel cousins are biologically equidistant yet socially opposite, so the rule is social, not biological.
- Method: a [[structuralism|structuralist]] analysis on the model of structural linguistics; formal/algebraic modelling legitimate (Weil's group-theoretic Murngin appendix).
- Cases: Australian section systems and the [[murngin|Murngin]] (restricted exchange); [[kachin|Kachin]] and Gilyak (generalized exchange); Chinese and Indian systems; the transition to complex structures (modern Western kinship).

## Volume Synthesis Note

The *Elementary Structures* is the founding text of [[alliance-theory]] and the applied face of anthropological [[structuralism]]. It adds to the wiki the first primary-ingested Lévi-Strauss, filling a gap the thinker page had flagged: the vault's account of structuralism had been refracted entirely through [[bourdieu-pierre|Bourdieu's]] polemic. Its argument — kinship as a system of communication, women circulating like words — both extends [[mauss-the-gift-1925|Mauss's]] reciprocity and sets up the two great critiques the wiki tracks: the practice critique ([[strategy-vs-rule]]) and the feminist critique ([[exchange-of-women]]). Tensions with already-ingested material: it stands opposite the **descent theory** of the ingested British Africanists ([[radcliffe-brown-forde-african-systems-kinship-marriage-1950|African Systems of Kinship and Marriage]]), the alliance/descent contrast being the axis of mid-century kinship studies. The ingested edition covers all 29 chapters; the formal chapters XXIV/XXVI/XXVII (asymmetric cycles) were sampled rather than exhaustively extracted, their argument being recapitulated in the Conclusion.
