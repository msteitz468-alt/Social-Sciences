# Terrorism and Communism: A Reply to Karl Kautsky (1920)

*Leon Trotsky.* Workers Party of America Eng. ed. 1920 (*Dictatorship vs Democracy*); Verso 2007 with foreword by Slavoj Žižek and preface by H.N. Brailsford.

Classic Bolshevik defence of revolutionary dictatorship and [[red-terror]] against [[kautsky-karl|Karl Kautsky]], and the fullest primary statement on this wiki of Trotsky's 1920 [[militarization-of-labour]] programme. Companion polemic to Lenin's *[[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918|The Proletarian Revolution and the Renegade Kautsky]]* (1918) and to Kautsky's own *[[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918|The Dictatorship of the Proletariat]]* (1918).

## Volume Synthesis

Trotsky's book is not a constructive essay on socialist institutions so much as a **civil-war ethics and state theory**: parliamentary "balance of power" and metaphysical democracy are denounced as the crooked mirror of a class society in collapse; the [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] must guarantee itself by force; [[red-terror]] is the continuation of insurrection when the overthrown class resists with foreign aid; the Paris Commune is reclaimed via Marx against Kautsky's "humane" contrast; and the same authoritarian state principle is extended from the military to the **labour front** — compulsory service, labour armies, production-unions, one-man management, single plan. The theoretical load-bearing claim is that free-labour productivity is a liberal prejudice when applied to the bourgeois→socialist transition: *who compels whom, for what purpose* decides productivity and legitimacy.

For the wiki, the ingest (a) gives Trotsky's own voice on terror and labour where Service biography and Lenin's trade-union polemic had been secondary or one-sided; (b) deepens [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]] with the democracy-vs-dictatorship and Constituent Assembly arguments from the war commissar's side; (c) creates [[red-terror]] and [[militarization-of-labour]] as first-class concepts. **Balance note:** Kautsky's *[[kautsky-terrorism-and-communism-1919]]* (1919) is now ingested — rebalance any page that still relies only on Trotsky's paraphrase of Kautsky on terror.
