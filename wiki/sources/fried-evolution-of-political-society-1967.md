# The Evolution of Political Society (Fried, 1967)

[[fried-morton-h|Morton H. Fried]]'s *The Evolution of Political Society: An Essay in Political Anthropology* (Random House, 1967) is the classic mid-century statement of the **[[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model|egalitarian → rank → stratified → state]]** sequence — an alternate neo-evolutionary cut of political organization to Service’s [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model|band–tribe–chiefdom]] model. Fried defines levels by **status scarcity** and **access to basic resources**; treats [[stratified-society|stratified society]] as intrinsically unstable; and centers the **[[pristine-state|pristine]] / [[secondary-state|secondary]] state** distinction in origin theory.

## Volume Synthesis Note

EPS is a **thesis essay**, not a literature survey. Fried presents a few concepts — egalitarian, rank, stratified, state; ranking vs stratification; pristine vs secondary — and tests them in comparative and conjectural-historical context against synchronic functionalism and political-science universalization of the state.

**Definitions first.** Political vocabulary is unstandardized; definitions are tools. Authority channels behavior without sanction threat; power uses sanctions. Law is Austinian (rule enforced by determinate locus of power); custom is habitual practice. Political organization manages public policy; government is its administrative apparatus. Legitimacy blends ideology with power. Culture is conventional responses primarily by symbolic learning.

**Egalitarian.** As many prestige positions per age-sex grade as capable persons; reciprocal economy (Polanyi/Sahlins); open access to basic resources; no law; situational authority; sharing as human hinge versus primate non-sharing. Living cases often occupy marginal habitats — Pleistocene extrapolation is hazardous.

**Rank.** Limited valued statuses without necessary resource stratification; redistribution paramount; chiefs rich for what they dispense; kinship ideology peaks; relatively stable; still no law. Explicit critique of **tribe** as evolutionary stage (most tribes secondary); chiefdom carries evolutionary load in Service–Sahlins schemes; external polity is feast as well as war.

**Stratified.** Differential access to basic resources; almost unfindable ethnographically without state institutions; unstable — state formation implicit once stratification exists. Origins list: population pressure, residence shifts, resource contraction, subsistence/market shifts, managerial maturation — **not** war or slavery as initiators.

**State.** Power organized superior to kinship; maintains order of stratification; force paramountcy plus ideology. Pristine = autochthonous in political vacuum; secondary = stimuli/models from pre-existing states. Mesopotamia/Egypt/Indus/China as pristine candidates (China/Indus personal lean); classical West mostly secondary.

**Method and limits.** Comparative second-hand ethnography; conjectural reconstruction; flag secondary situations. Theory offered as crude target. No `studies/` page.
