# Origins (Leakey & Lewin, 1977)

An illustrated popular-science synthesis of human evolution, co-authored by the
paleoanthropologist **[[leakey-richard|Richard E. Leakey]]** — then director of the
National Museums of Kenya and leader of the [[koobi-fora|Koobi Fora / East Turkana]]
research programme — and the science journalist **[[lewin-roger|Roger Lewin]]**. Published
by Macdonald and Jane's (London) with the Rainbird design group; ~272 pp, ten chapters,
heavily illustrated (100 colour plates, 140 monochrome photographs, maps, and
reconstructions).

*Origins* is simultaneously three things, which the Voice and Attribution Protocol keeps
separate on the pages it feeds:
1. a **secondary synthesis** of mid-1970s paleoanthropology for a general audience;
2. **primary reportage** of the authors' (chiefly Richard Leakey's) own East Turkana
   fossil discoveries and their preferred interpretations — hence `source_type: mixed`;
3. a **theoretical argument** — the book's polemical spine — that human beings were made
   by *cooperation, food-sharing, and the sexual division of labour*, and that the
   then-popular "innate aggression / killer ape" thesis of [[ardrey-robert|Robert Ardrey]]
   and [[lorenz-konrad|Konrad Lorenz]] is both bad biology and dangerous ideology.

## What It Contributes to the Wiki

Origins is the vault's **first primary-ish paleoanthropology monograph**, opening the
fossil (as opposed to archaeogenetic) stream flagged thin on the
[[biological-anthropology]] discipline page. It anchors:

- the East African fossil **sites** — [[koobi-fora]], [[olduvai-gorge]], [[hadar]],
  [[omo-river]] — none of which had pages before;
- the hominin **species/culture** pages — [[australopithecus]], [[homo-habilis]],
  [[homo-erectus]], and the now-superseded [[ramapithecus]];
- the **[[leakey-richard|Leakey]]** family and their rivals/collaborators
  ([[johanson-donald|Johanson]], [[dart-raymond|Dart]]);
- the **[[food-sharing-hypothesis]]** (Glynn Isaac's home-base model, which the authors
  champion) as the named alternative to the [[hunting-hypothesis|Man-the-Hunter thesis]];
- the **[[human-aggression-killer-ape-debate]]** — the sociobiology-adjacent dispute over
  whether human aggression is an innate evolutionary inheritance.

## Reliability and Voice

This is an argument in a popular register by a working scientist who is a party to the
disputes he describes. Treat accordingly:

- The authors' big claims (cooperation-not-aggression; food-sharing as the human-making
  adaptation) are **attributed** to Leakey & Lewin, never wiki voice.
- Fossil identifications and dates are **date-stamped 1977** and flagged where later
  superseded (Ramapithecus; the KBS-tuff/skull-1470 date).
- The killer-ape material is recorded as **positions** on the debate page — each side in
  its strongest form — not adjudicated in wiki voice.
- Living hunter-gatherers (!Kung, Hadza) appear as **ethnographic analogy** for the
  Pleistocene past; the analogy is flagged as a hypothesis-generator, not evidence.
