# The Impending Catastrophe and How to Combat It (1917)

Lenin's economic program on the eve of October: diagnosis of the autumn 1917 collapse and the five "control measures" — bank amalgamation/nationalisation, syndicate nationalisation, abolition of commercial secrecy, compulsory syndication, consumers' societies. Single-agent extraction (CW Vol. 25 segment).

## Load-bearing content (all attributed)

- **The core theoretical move:** "imperialism is merely monopoly capitalism"; the war has produced "state-monopoly capitalism," and "socialism is merely the next step forward from state-capitalist monopoly... made to serve the interests of the whole people" — culminating in the famous formulation that state-monopoly capitalism is "a complete material preparation for socialism, the threshold of socialism, a rung on the ladder of history between which and the rung called socialism there are no intermediate rungs." Codified on [[state-capitalism]].
- **Reactionary-bureaucratic vs revolutionary-democratic regulation** — the same machinery, different class direction (Germany/USA as the former).
- **"War is the continuation of politics":** control measures would transform the war's class character.
- **Companion articles:** "Power to the Soviets" as radical reshaping of the state apparatus, not a cabinet change ([[smash-the-state-machine]]); bourgeois "freedom of the press" as advertising-capital dominance, with the press-requisition proposal.

## Reliability

Primary programmatic source; the sabotage diagnosis and all figures (war-profit estimates, circulation ratios) are Lenin's polemical assertions. Cross-referenced by Lenin himself from "Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?" ([[lenin-collected-works-vol-26-1964]]).
