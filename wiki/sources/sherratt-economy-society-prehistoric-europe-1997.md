## Volume Synthesis Note

*Economy and Society in Prehistoric Europe* is the charter collection for Sherratt’s **Secondary Products Revolution** and for a European prehistory that refuses the false choice between pure demography and pure symbols. The fourth millennium BC is the structural hinge: secondary animal products, wheeled vehicles, plough agriculture, elite consumables (textiles, alcohol, metals), and Near Eastern urban peripheries reorganize temperate Europe after two millennia of hoe horticulture. Relative to already-ingested Childe, Braidwood, and Renfrew–Bahn textbook layers, this volume supplies the classic **second revolution** thesis, the Hungarian survey empirics, the megalith-as-conversion program, and a carefully non-identitarian IE archaeology (with E.S. Sherratt). Weaknesses for current research: pre-aDNA genetics, pre-lipid-residue dairying chronology, and essay-by-essay absolute-date conventions — all date-stamped, not silently updated.
