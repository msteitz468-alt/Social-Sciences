# Europe in the Neolithic (Whittle, 1996)

Alasdair Whittle, *Europe in the Neolithic: The Creation of New Worlds* (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1996). Cambridge World Archaeology. ISBN 0-521-44476-4 (hbk) / 0-521-44920-0 (pbk). Dedicated to Elisabeth. Succeeds and replaces *Neolithic Europe: a survey* (1985).

## Overview

A continental-scale interpretive survey of European Neolithic societies **c. 7000–2500 BC** (to ~3000 BC in SE Europe). Whittle challenges conventional demic/farming-package narratives, arguing (attributed) that Neolithic society was **rooted in forager values of sharing and integration** across the continent; settling down and adopting farming were **piecemeal and slow**; only gradually did new attitudes emerge to time and the past, to ancestors and the dead, to nature, and to community. The Neolithic is framed as the **creation of new worlds** of belief and social relations as much as of economy.

## Key Arguments (attributed — to densify from claims)

1. **Indigenous continuity:** In nearly all areas, largely indigenous people created Neolithic histories (Preface / Ch.1).
2. **Forager social ethic persists:** Sharing, cooperation, mutuality remain central; little established hierarchy within groups.
3. **Slow, piecemeal process:** Settling down and farming adoption are gradual; people do not instantly become “mixed farmers.”
4. **Sacred and ancestral:** Spirits, ancestors, descent, and origins structure values more than secular surplus logic.
5. **LBK as local adaptation (Ch.6 position):** Against pure colonisation orthodoxy for central European longhouse populations.
6. **Regional individuality:** Many Neolithics, not one European Neolithic; Brittany monument sequence as distinctive coastal history.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Whittle 1996 is the vault’s **continental indigenous-lean synthesis** for European Neolithic social archaeology — the counterpart, on the Position B side of [[farming-spread-europe-debate]], to demic programs densified from Renfrew, Bellwood, and classical genetics. Three moves structure the contribution:

1. **Values before package.** The Neolithic is redefined as beliefs about descent, ancestors, time, and sacred community, continuous with Mesolithic sharing ethics yet marked by a new conceptual order (time, beginnings, categorisation). Farming and sedentism are piecemeal; cereals and cattle are re-read (drink; partners) rather than as instant mixed-farming revolution. This sits beside — and partly allies with — [[bradley-richard|Bradley]]’s monument-first / dual-Neolithic arguments without being identical to them.

2. **Indigenous creation of regional Neolithics.** SE tells ([[karanovo]], [[starcevo-koros-cris-culture]], [[vinca-culture]], [[varna]]); central European [[longhouse]] / [[linear-pottery-culture]] as inland-forager **acculturation** (Ch. 6 load-bearing); Atlantic megaliths and TRB “abstract funerals”; Mediterranean [[impresso-cardial-culture]] via sea-borne acculturation and lower chronology lean. Colonisation is reported for orthodoxy and allowed as limited special cases, not as the primary continental engine.

3. **Anti-hierarchy and anti-kurgan/IE packages (1996).** Varna, copper, and Iberian plaques/idols toward ancestral identity and regeneration; Baden carts and secondary products as accents; Gimbutas kurgan and Renfrew early demic IE rejected as necessary explanations. **Pre-aDNA** — post-2010 EEF demography is supersession pressure on the demographic half of the thesis; keep ideology and slow-process arguments as attributed positions.
