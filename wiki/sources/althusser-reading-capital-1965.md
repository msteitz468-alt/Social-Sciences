## Bibliographic note

English *Reading Capital*: New Left Books abridged 1970 (Brewster); Verso reprints; **this ingest uses Verso 2015 Complete Edition** (ISBN 978-1-78478-141-5 PB), restoring all six parts in original seminar order, tr. Ben Brewster and David Fernbach. Seminar held late January–early April 1965 at ENS Ulm under Althusser; written-up contributions published November 1965 with *Pour Marx*.

## Volume synthesis

*Reading Capital* is the **co-charter** of [[structural-marxism]] with *[[althusser-for-marx-1965|For Marx]]*. Where *For Marx* installs overdetermination, the epistemological break, structure-in-dominance, theoretical practice, and theoretical anti-humanism in essay form, *Reading Capital* demonstrates those theses on the text of *Capital* and rebuilds the basic concepts of [[historical-materialism]]. Load-bearing moves (all attributed, by part-author): [[symptomatic-reading]] and [[knowledge-effect]] (Althusser Part One); residual anthropological discourse in mature Marx (Rancière Part Two); process of exposition as work of concepts (Macherey Part Three); [[differential-historical-time]], anti-historicism, object of *Capital*, and [[structural-causality]] / *Darstellung* (Althusser Part Four); [[mode-of-production]] as [[combination-combinatory|combination]] of dual connections, [[bearer-trager|bearers]], reproduction, and transition as structured [[dislocation-decalage|dislocation]] (Balibar Part Five); plan/articulations of the volumes (Establet Part Six). The book is **not** the ISA essay and does not ground interpellation. Multi-authorship and edition history (1965 full / 1968 abridged / complete restoration) are part of the transmission politics Balibar’s Presentation records.
