## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Civilizing Process* is Elias's charter mixed work: Part One sociologizes the words civilization/*Kultur*; Part Two serializes manners standards (table, body, bedroom, sex, aggression) to track rising shame/repugnance thresholds; Part Three reconstructs European (esp. French) paths from free warrior competition to dual monopolies of force and tax, royal balancing, and courtization; Part Four couples figurational change to self-constraint and unplanned order; the 1968 Postscript restates method against static system sociology and *homo clausus*. The ingest treats the work as both theoretical-work and primary historical-comparative study (`source_type: mixed`; study + Studies Hub warranted). Theory Hub and Thinkers Hub also warranted and built in-session. Standing debate: teleology, Eurocentrism, violence anomaly, source bias.
