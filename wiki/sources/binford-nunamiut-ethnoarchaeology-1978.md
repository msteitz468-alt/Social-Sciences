# Nunamiut Ethnoarchaeology (Binford 1978)

The monograph in which [[binford-lewis|Lewis Binford]] built [[middle-range-theory-archaeology|archaeological middle-range theory]] from live observation. Framed by the problem set on his 1969 voyage home from Bordeaux — the [[archaeological-record|archaeological record]] is static and contemporary, the past is dynamic, and inference between them needs warranted bridging linkages — the book studies the [[nunamiut|Nunamiut]] as a functioning caribou-hunting system and traces how their butchering, transport, caching, and consumption decisions generate the [[faunal-analysis|faunal assemblages]] and site structures an archaeologist would later excavate. Its enduring instruments are the **[[utility-index|utility indices]]** (meat, marrow, bone-grease; the Modified General Utility Index) and the demonstration that a single group produces many different assemblage types. The research act is analyzed on the study page **[[binford-nunamiut-ethnoarchaeology-study]]**; deep treatment in **[[hubs/studies/binford-nunamiut-ethnoarchaeology-study|the Studies Hub]]**.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Nunamiut Ethnoarchaeology* is not really an ethnography of the Nunamiut — it is a methodological treatise that uses them as a controlled, observable dynamic system. Its argument is that archaeology's inference conventions ("fabrication model" reasoning, self-evident bone-to-diet readings) fail against the documented complexity of a living hunting economy, and that the remedy is instrument-building: theory-independent bridges (like the utility indices, validated against 277+ caribou at 111 kill-butchering locations) between behaviour and its static traces. Read alongside *[[binford-in-pursuit-of-the-past-1983|In Pursuit of the Past]]* (1983), which restates the same program for a general audience, and *[[binford-binford-new-perspectives-1968|New Perspectives]]* (1968), which set the processual charter this monograph operationalizes. Together the three trace one arc: the 1968 call for explanation → the 1970s recognition that explanation needs inference → the 1978 construction of the inferential apparatus.
