# Shang Civilization

K. C. Chang's 1980 Yale monograph reconstructs **Shang civilization** as a living social
system — royal capital, economy, ruling apparatus, and inter-polity network — from the
"five doors" of traditional historiography, bronze studies, oracle bones, archaeology, and
theoretical models. Part I rebuilds Shang society from the An-yang (Yinxu) record; Part II
extends the picture to Cheng-chou (Erh-li-kang / Early Shang) and other regional sites, then
addresses absolute chronology, language and physique, and Shang origins relative to Hsia.

The book is the focused Shang companion to Chang's multi-edition *Archaeology of Ancient
China*; later regional syntheses ([[liu-chen-archaeology-of-china-2012|Liu & Chen 2012]])
revise its monocentric tendencies and chronology while retaining many of its institutional
and urban-network insights in attributed form.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Chang's 1980 monograph is the **social-system companion** to his multi-edition prehistory
synthesis. Where *[[chang-archaeology-ancient-china-1963|The Archaeology of Ancient China]]*
(1963) ordered Chinese prehistory for anglophone anthropology, *Shang Civilization* rebuilds one
civilization from the inside — Five Doors of evidence, Part I society from An-yang, Part II
geography and origins.

**What it adds to the wiki.** (1) Dense, attributed reconstruction of [[anyang|Anyang]] as a
multi-nodal royal capital (Hsiao-t'un, Hsi-pei-kang, Fu Hao, sacrifice tallies) beyond textbook
sketches. (2) Institutional vocabulary — *[[tsu-lineage|tsu]]*/*yi*, [[fang-polity|*fang*]],
interior/exterior domain — still used in late-Shang network debates. (3) Method pages for
[[oracle-bone-inscription]] and [[scapulimancy]] as Shang epistemic doors. (4) Explicit
**Erlitou ≠ Shang** sequence and horizontal Three Dynasties sketch feeding
[[erlitou-xia-dynasty-debate]] and [[chinese-civilization-origins-debate]]. (5) Comparative
epilogue placing Shang beside Adams-style urban-society comparison.

**What later work revises.** Absolute chronology (traditional 1766–1122; Chang's ~1700/1300/1100
round figures) and any residual Shang-centric framing are superseded by calibrated multi-regional
syntheses ([[liu-chen-archaeology-of-china-2012|Liu & Chen 2012]],
[[shelach-lavi-archaeology-early-china-2015|Shelach-Lavi 2015]]). Race/physique discussions in Ch.7
are retained only as dated scholarly positions under four-way non-identity. Bronze-ritual monopoly
and cycle models of state formation on [[state-formation]] now cite Liu & Chen as primary; Chang
1980 remains the classic English network anatomy of the late Shang court.

**OCR caveat.** Ingest used FineReader letter-spaced text; claims were reconstructed carefully.
Specialist Chinese reports and post-1980 epigraphy remain outstanding for further densification.
