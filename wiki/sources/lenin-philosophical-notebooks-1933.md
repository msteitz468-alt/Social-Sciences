# Philosophical Notebooks (CW Vol. 38)

Lenin's private philosophical workshop: conspectuses of Marx–Engels's *Holy Family* (1895), Feuerbach's *Leibniz* (1895) and *Lectures on the Essence of Religion* (1909), Hegel's *Science of Logic* (Bern, Sept–Dec 1914 — the famous notebooks), *Lectures on the History of Philosophy* and *Philosophy of History* (1915), Lassalle's *Heraclitus*, Aristotle's *Metaphysics* (1915), the independent fragment **"On the Question of Dialectics"** (1915), and marginalia on contemporary books (Plekhanov's *Fundamental Questions of Marxism*, Deborin, Shulyatikov, Rey, Plenge, Hilferding, Noël, Plekhanov's *Chernyshevsky*). Never published by Lenin; canonized posthumously. With this, the Lenin Collected Works line planned for this wiki is complete.

## What the notebooks contain (Lenin's private notes, dated)

- **Reading Hegel materialistically (1914–15):** "Turn it round: Logic and the theory of knowledge must be derived from 'the development of all natural and spiritual life'"; logic coincides with the theory of knowledge; the "logic of *Capital*" sketch (commodity as "Sein"); the **16 elements of dialectics**; and the aphorism that one cannot fully understand *Capital* "without having thoroughly studied and understood the whole of Hegel's Logic. Consequently, half a century later none of the Marxists understood Marx!!"
- **"On the Question of Dialectics" (1915):** the splitting of a single whole and cognition of its contradictory parts as the essence of dialectics; unity of opposites relative, struggle absolute; development as self-movement vs. the "lifeless" increase/decrease conception; knowledge as spiral, not straight line; idealism not nonsense but "a one-sided, exaggerated... development of one of the facets of knowledge into an absolute" — "a sterile flower... on the living tree of... human knowledge."
- **Against "dead mirroring":** cognition as "the eternal, endless approximation of thought to the object"; the approach of mind to a thing "is not a simple, immediate act, a dead mirroring, but one which is complex, split into two, zig-zag-like, which includes in it the possibility of the flight of fantasy from life." Raw material for the standing question of continuity vs. tension with the 1908 copy-theory of [[lenin-materialism-and-empirio-criticism-1909]] — recorded, not adjudicated ([[reflection-theory]]).
- **The Plekhanov verdicts:** dialectics is "the theory of knowledge of (Hegel and) Marxism... to which Plekhanov, not to speak of other Marxists, paid no attention"; Plekhanov wrote ~1,000 pages on dialectics but "nil!!" on Hegel's *Logic*; his unity-of-opposites treated as "a sum-total of examples"; NB marks on *Fundamental Questions of Marxism*; and the Feuerbach-derived criticism turned back on Plekhanov's own *Chernyshevsky* book. First-hand documentation of the Lenin–Plekhanov epistemological divergence already dual-documented on [[reflection-theory]] ([[plekhanov-georgi]]).
- **Other aphorisms:** "Intelligent idealism is closer to intelligent materialism than stupid materialism"; "Clericalism killed what was living in Aristotle and perpetuated what was dead"; Lassalle "drowns Heraclitus in Hegel"; Shulyatikov's class-reduction of philosophy — "what nonsense!"; Rey as "shamefaced materialism."
- **Early stratum (1895):** the *Holy Family* and Feuerbach-*Leibniz* conspectuses show the 25-year-old Lenin's reading ("cf. electrons!" on the monads) — two decades before the Bern notebooks; the two strata are not one doctrine.

## Reception note

The notebooks were assembled into "Leninist dialectics" by Soviet philosophy after 1925 — private, exploratory, often tentative notes ("So, it seems to me, Feuerbach is to be understood?") canonized as doctrine. The editorial apparatus performing that canonization is extracted as a separate register. The Plenge marginalia preserve, for refutation, a specimen of antisemitic anti-Marx polemic in pre-WWI German academia ("a revolutionary Jew... re-tailored the garment borrowed from our great philosophy") — kept verbatim as reception history on [[marx-karl]].

## Reliability

Private notes are the strongest possible evidence for what Lenin read and privately thought, and the weakest for "Lenin's doctrine" — he never authorized any of it. Conspectus quotes are Lenin's selections from German editions, at double remove. Dating is per notebook and is load-bearing (1895 ≠ 1914–15). The scholarly dispute over whether the Hegel notebooks revise or extend the 1908 epistemology is recorded as an open question, with the raw passages on both sides extracted.
