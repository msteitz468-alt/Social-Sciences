## Volume Synthesis Note

*Ethnic Groups and Boundaries* is the charter text of **organizational / constructivist ethnicity** in anthropology. Its enduring move is to treat the ethnic group as a category of **ascription** maintained by **boundary processes**, demoting shared culture from definitional primary to variable content of an “organizational vessel.” Two opening discoveries structure the program: boundaries persist despite personnel flow; and inter-ethnic interaction often *constitutes* social systems rather than dissolving difference. Case essays supply positive mechanisms (Haaland pastoralization; Pathan performance failure; Eidheim stigma spheres), stability under dominance (Siverts), multi-ethnic neighbourhood ecology (Izikowitz), stratified dichotomization (Knutsson), and a crucial **negative case** (Blom: cultural diversity without ethnicity).

For this wiki the book is a pillar of postwar social anthropology alongside Leach’s multi-ethnic Burma and Goffman’s stigma/interaction apparatus (both in the intellectual network of the volume). It feeds the standing [[primordialism-constructivism-ethnicity-debate]] and supplies lineage for later boundary sociology ([[symbolic-boundaries]], [[things-of-boundaries]]). Period ethnonyms and mid-1960s political contexts are documentation facts — not timeless labels.
