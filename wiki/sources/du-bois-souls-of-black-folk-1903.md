# The Souls of Black Folk (Du Bois, 1903)

W. E. B. Du Bois's *The Souls of Black Folk: Essays and Sketches* (Chicago: A. C. McClurg, 1903) — founding text of the sociology of race in the United States and of African American letters' classical modern idiom. Ingested from Project Gutenberg eBook #408 (complete body).

## What the book is

Fourteen linked essays (plus Forethought and Afterthought) on the meaning of being Black in America at the opening of the twentieth century. Du Bois's framing sentence — "the problem of the Twentieth Century is the problem of the color-line" — and his concepts of [[double-consciousness]], [[the-veil]], and [[the-color-line]] organize the whole. The book moves from the spiritual condition of the post-Emancipation "American Negro," through a history of the Freedmen's Bureau and a candid critique of Booker T. Washington (→ [[washington-du-bois-debate]]), into education and the [[talented-tenth]], then into empirical sketches of the Black Belt peasantry, race relations between "sons of master and man," the [[black-church]], personal elegy, biography (Alexander Crummell), a short story ("Of the Coming of John"), and the [[sorrow-songs]] as America's only indigenous music. All substantive claims are **attributed** to Du Bois (1903) unless independently converged from other ingested sources.

## Reliability and voice

- **Genre hybridity.** Theoretical chapters (I, III, V–VI, IX) as attributed theory; Black Belt chapters (VII–VIII) as date-stamped observation (not ethnographic present); literary chapters (XI, XIII) as literature used for social analysis, not as survey data.
- **Self-positioning.** Du Bois writes as "bone of the bone… of them that live within the Veil" — positionality is explicit and load-bearing.
- **Prior publication.** Several essays revised from magazines; 1903 book form is the ingested unit.
- **Violence and race discourse.** Slavery aftermath, lynching, rape charges as social facts of the color-line recorded at full fidelity in attributed voice.
- **Talented Tenth.** Phrase appears in ch. VI; related 1903 essay *The Talented Tenth* (not this volume) flagged for later thickening.
- **Washington position.** Staged from Du Bois's reconstruction pending direct Washington ingest.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Souls* gives the wiki its first classical American race-sociology node and corrects a collection bias toward European founders who theorized "society" while treating race as stage, type, or residual. The conceptual cluster (color-line / Veil / double-consciousness) is durable; the Black Belt debt monography and Freedmen’s Bureau balance sheet are historical findings attributed to 1903 observation and documentary reconstruction; the Washington debate page holds leadership conflict without adjudicating. Gaps left thin: *The Philadelphia Negro* (empirical design precursor), *Black Reconstruction*, the separate Talented Tenth essay, Washington’s own corpus, and women’s experience as equal analytical subject. File companion *Du Bois Essays* in `raw/` remains queued.
