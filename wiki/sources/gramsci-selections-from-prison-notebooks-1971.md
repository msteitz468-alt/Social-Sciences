# Selections from the Prison Notebooks (Gramsci, 1971)

Antonio Gramsci, *Selections from the Prison Notebooks*, edited and translated by Quintin Hoare and Geoffrey Nowell Smith (1971). The standard Anglophone gateway to the *Quaderni del carcere* (1929–1935) — fountainhead of modern [[hegemony]] theory, [[organic-intellectuals]], [[passive-revolution]], [[war-of-position]], and the [[philosophy-of-praxis]].

**All theoretical claims are attributed to Gramsci (or to the editors when they gloss)**, never asserted as wiki-voice findings. Historical reconstructions in the General Introduction are **editorial secondary narrative**, date-stamped and attributed to Hoare/Nowell Smith. **No `studies/` page** — theoretical notebooks selection, not a landmark empirical research act. **Thinkers Hub** warranted and built in-session: [[hubs/thinkers/gramsci-antonio]].

## Claims extracted (integration summary)

### Editorial apparatus (Hoare General Introduction — secondary)
- Biography 1891–1937: Sardinia, Turin, *Ordine Nuovo*/factory councils 1919–20, PCI Livorno 1921, leadership 1924–26, arrest 1926, notebooks 1929–35, death 1937.
- Political field: Bordiga / Tasca / Togliatti; Comintern pressures; fascism as reading context for opaque notes.
- Intellectual formation: Labriola (philosophy of praxis phrase), Croce (to overcome), Salvemini, Pastore; censorship euphemism practice.

### Intellectuals and education (Gramsci)
- Organic vs traditional intellectuals; all men are intellectuals but not all have the function; intellectuals as hegemony deputies (consent + coercion).
- Civil society = private organisms; political society = State/direct domination.
- School as elaboration of intellectual strata; class-encoded school types; work as educational principle.

### Italian history / subaltern / passive revolution
- Subaltern history fragmentary/episodic; State unifies dominant classes.
- Risorgimento: Moderates' hegemony; Action Party failure; Piedmont = dictatorship without hegemony; *trasformismo*.
- Passive revolution from 1859-Preface principles; criterion not programme; linked to war of position; Europe-as-passive-revolution sketch.

### Modern Prince
- Party as modern prince / collective will; politics as autonomous science; relations of force (structural, political, military).
- Anti-economism; spontaneity + conscious leadership; historical bloc language; economic-corporate phase.

### State and civil society
- Organic crisis / crisis of authority; "old is dying and the new cannot be born."
- War of manoeuvre vs war of position; East (gelatinous civil society) vs West (trenches).
- State = political society + civil society = hegemony protected by the armour of coercion.
- Caesarism (progressive/reactionary); statolatry; collective man / social conformism.

### Americanism and Fordism
- New type of worker/man; Taylorism; high wages as force+persuasion; sexual/moral regulation.
- **Exact:** "Hegemony here is born in the factory and requires for its exercise only a minute quantity of professional political and ideological intermediaries."
- Americanism as possible passive revolution vs explosion.

### Philosophy of praxis / Problems of Marxism
- Philosophy of praxis as integral historicist Marxism; absolute historicism; orthodoxy as self-sufficiency.
- Common sense (*senso comune*) vs good sense; every person a philosopher; renovate mass common sense.
- Against "primitive infantilism" of immediate structure→ideology expression; Bukharin *Popular Manual* as positivist sociology not theory of praxis.
- Historical bloc as feeling→understanding→knowledge cohesion of intellectuals and people-nation.
