# Ecology, Meaning, and Religion (Rappaport, 1979)

Roy A. Rappaport, *Ecology, Meaning, and Religion* (North Atlantic Books, Richmond, CA, 1979). Essay collection bridging systems ecology of ritual with formal and evolutionary treatments of meaning, sanctity, and ritual form. Ingested 2026-07-10 from OCR PDF in `raw/`.

**Relation to prior vault coverage:** *[[rappaport-pigs-for-the-ancestors-1968|Pigs for the Ancestors]]* (1968/1984) and [[hubs/thinkers/rappaport-roy-a|Thinkers Hub]] already cover the Tsembaga case and systems-ecology program. This volume is the primary-ingested source for (a) "On Cognized Models" as a freestanding concept paper, (b) the formal/performative analysis of ritual ("The Obvious Aspects of Ritual"), (c) hierarchical adaptive structure and maladaptation ("Adaptive Structure and Its Disorders"), (d) sanctity, ultimate sacred postulates, and evolutionary treatment of lies ("Sanctity and Lies in Evolution"), and (e) Rappaport's extended reply to Friedman on functionalism and ecological units.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Ecology, Meaning, and Religion* (1979) is Rappaport's mid-career bridge between the systems ecology of *Pigs* and the general theory of ritual and sanctity completed later in *Ritual and Religion in the Making of Humanity* (1999, not yet ingested). The Preface states the volume's thesis in dual-enterprise form: anthropology's objective (lawful/causal) and interpretive (meaningful) projects are complementary, not alternatives — a species that lives by meanings in a physical world subject to causal law needs both.

**What it adds to the wiki:** (1) primary text for [[cognized-model]] / [[operational-model]] as a freestanding method ("On Cognized Models"), including hierarchical sanctity structure and reference values vs goal ranges; (2) formal theory of [[liturgical-order]], acceptance vs belief, indexical/canonical messages, and [[ultimate-sacred-postulates]] ("Obvious Aspects of Ritual"); (3) hierarchical [[adaptive-structure]] and maladaptation-as-anomaly taxonomy ("Adaptive Structure and Its Disorders"); (4) [[sanctity]] as unquestionableness and evolutionary treatment of lies/desecration ("Sanctity and Lies"); (5) densified Friedman reply and population-vs-culture unit argument ("Ills of Functionalism") for [[ecological-anthropology-ritual-regulation-debate]]; (6) early island-ecosystems paper (Polynesian stratification via ecological control) and the 1967 *Ethnology* ritual-regulation précis. **What it leaves thin:** full Peirce/Austin primary literature; Tillich theology beyond citation; post-1979 reception; 1999 summa.

**Voice:** all program claims remain attributed to Rappaport; no promotion of ritual-regulation or adaptive-structure theses to wiki-voice findings. No `studies/` page for the volume (essay collection; *Pigs* remains the landmark study).
