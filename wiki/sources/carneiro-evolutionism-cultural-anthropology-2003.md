# Evolutionism in Cultural Anthropology: A Critical History (Carneiro, 2003)

Westview Press critical history of cultural evolutionism from Spencer/Tylor/Morgan through Boasian anti-evolutionism to mid- and late-twentieth-century neo-evolutionism (White, Steward, Sahlins, Service, processual archaeology, population/warfare/trade determinants). Author: [[carneiro-robert-l|Robert L. Carneiro]] (American Museum of Natural History), a principal late-twentieth-century evolutionist and formulator of [[circumscription|circumscription theory]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

Carneiro 2003 is the wiki’s fullest **partisan critical history** of cultural evolutionism: classical foundations (Spencer’s formal definition; Tylor/Morgan; comparative-method principles; survivals; psychic unity; non-caricature unilinearity), anti-evolution ascendancy (Boas, British diffusionism, functionalism, “barren landscape”), midcentury reemergence (White, Steward, Childe, Darwin centennial, Sahlins–Service *Evolution and Culture*), late midcentury issues ([[general-and-specific-evolution]], Service stages, processual archaeology, “neo-evolutionism” label), features of process (stages, scale analysis, directionality, Darwinian models), determinants (Service vs Harris; population pressure; warfare; trade; ideology; agency), and current attitudes (post-processual resistance; archaeological carriers; sociologists).

**What it adds:** (1) systematic quotation-backed defense of the classicals against Boasian caricature; (2) Carneiro’s own theoretical synthesis — Spencerian restriction of “evolution,” uni/multilinear complementarity, political unilinearity band→state, population-warfare-[[circumscription]] package; (3) dense map of neo-evolutionist personnel and debates usable to densify existing theory/thinker/debate pages; (4) thinker page for Carneiro and concept pages for circumscription and general/specific evolution.

**Voice hygiene:** all program claims remain attributed. Carneiro writes *as* an evolutionist rehabilitating the tradition — treat historiographic verdicts (“perjury,” “too valid to abandon”) as Position 5 on [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]], not wiki-voice field consensus.
