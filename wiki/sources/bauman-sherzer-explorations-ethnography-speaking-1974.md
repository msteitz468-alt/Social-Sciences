# Explorations in the Ethnography of Speaking (Bauman & Sherzer, eds., 1974)

Edited collection marking the **second exploratory stage** of the [[ethnography-of-speaking|ethnography of speaking]]: first fruits of fieldwork undertaken under the Hymes–Gumperz program, from the Austin conference of April 1972. Editors [[bauman-richard|Richard Bauman]] and [[sherzer-joel|Joel Sherzer]] organize substantive case studies (Sections II–V) plus programmatic extensions (Section VI). Companion volume in this wiki’s linguistic-anthropology spine to [[gumperz-hymes-directions-sociolinguistics-1972]] and [[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974]].

## Framing (Preface + Introductions, attributed — Bauman & Sherzer)

- Something missing from both grammars and ethnographies: **patterns and functions of speech** in social life; ethnography as the method to recover them.
- Field named by Hymes 1962; developed via Gumperz & Hymes 1964 *Ethnography of Communication* and 1972 *Directions*.
- 1972 Austin conference gathered researchers doing *ethnographies of speaking* as such; this book unifies revised papers.
- Sections II–V: case studies (N America, S America, Europe, Africa, Asia, Pacific); Section VI: state of the art and extensions.
- **Speech community** as organization of diversity; resources for speaking; ground rules; performance as nexus of resources and competence in situations.
- Second-edition intro (later printing) reviews subsequent monographs, performance turn, and applied ethnography of speaking.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Explorations* is the empirical counterpart to the programmatic Hymes/*Directions* spine already in the vault. Its load-bearing contributions for this wiki: (1) **speech community as organization of diversity**; (2) **performance** as analysis nexus (resources × competence × situation); (3) a comparative **case library** (Warm Springs, Antigua, Malagasy, Quaker, Wolof, Māori, Cuna, Jewish narrative, Chamula, etc.) proving the program is field-researchable; (4) §VI moves from ethnography toward **ethnology** (Grimshaw), **writing** (Basso), and **ways of speaking** (Hymes).
