## Volume Synthesis Note

This volume is a **program-state snapshot** of world-systems analysis at the Fernand Braudel Center’s twenty-fifth anniversary (conference 2001; print 2004), not a founding monograph. Part I restates the [[capitalist-world-economy]] as a polarizing historical system and debates its near future — Amin’s global apartheid and five monopolies; Chase-Dunn’s path through hegemonic obstacles toward global democracy; Arrighi’s hegemony–antisystemic reassessment with Gramscian passive revolution; von Werlhof’s feminist alchemical critique; van der Linden’s long-run [[proletarian-internationalism]]. Part II historicizes [[structures-of-knowledge]]: Collins on millennial intellectual networks; Mamdani on African studies as settler representation; Santos on [[lazy-reason]] and cognitive justice; Abu-Lughod on US provincialism and globalization “babble”; Aymard on Braudel vs Wallerstein; Wallerstein on post-1945 concept fashions and systemic transition; Trouillot on [[north-atlantic-universals]].

**What it adds to this wiki:** (1) first full [[world-systems-analysis]] theory page + Theory Hub seed; (2) thinker network (Wallerstein, Arrighi, Amin, Chase-Dunn, Santos, Trouillot, Abu-Lughod, Mamdani) and concept kit ([[core-periphery]], [[semiperiphery]], [[antisystemic-movements]], [[world-system-hegemony]], [[longue-duree]], etc.); (3) live [[modernization-vs-dependency-world-systems-debate]] linked to ingested [[modernization-theory]]; (4) knowledge-layer bridge to decolonial/Southern epistemologies without promoting political theses to wiki voice.

**Tensions / limits:** MWS Vols. I–IV and Arrighi’s *Long Twentieth Century* remain uningested — empirical reconstruction of the long sixteenth century and systemic cycles is stub-depth; many contributor monographs pending; prognostic essays (global socialism, terminal crisis) are **positions**. Aymard’s Braudel-vs-Wallerstein note and Abu-Lughod’s residual-Eurocentrism critique of *MWS* I are internal program tensions to preserve.
