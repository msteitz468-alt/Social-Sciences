# Tally's Corner (Liebow, 1967)

Participant-observation study of about two dozen Black streetcorner men at the "New Deal Carry-out" corner in Washington, D.C.'s Second Precinct, fieldwork January 1962–July 1963, done for Hylan Lewis's NIMH-funded Child Rearing Study and submitted as Liebow's Catholic University anthropology dissertation. Study analysis: [[liebow-tallys-corner-study]]; deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/liebow-tallys-corner-study]]; author: [[liebow-elliot]].

## Reception Apparatus (2003 edition)

Lemert: over a million copies sold; lineage through Rodman's value stretch, Merton's anomie, Stack's *All Our Kin* ("in many ways the feminist sequel"), and an unacknowledged Du Bois resonance; contrast with the Moynihan Report's reception ("evoke, not provoke"). Wilson: Liebow "perhaps the first scholar" to center how labor-market failure erodes the job search; conditions for low-skilled Black men worse by 2003 (computerization, internationalization, employer statistical discrimination — Holzer 1996; Wilson 1996).
