# Re-Constructing Archaeology (Shanks & Tilley, 1987 / 2nd ed. 1992)

Critical charter for a **reflexive social archaeology**: challenge to traditional and 'new' archaeology; past–present mediation; positivism and value-freedom critique; museum presentation and a **[[redemptive-aesthetic|redemptive aesthetic]]**; archaeology as **[[fourfold-hermeneutic|fourfold hermeneutic]]** and dialectical practice; social ontology (agency, structure, ideology, material culture as objectification); two extended material-culture analyses (middle Neolithic TRB ceramics outside a Swedish passage grave; design of contemporary British and Swedish beer cans). Co-authors [[shanks-michael|Michael Shanks]] and [[tilley-christopher|Christopher Tilley]]. Frame: [[post-processual-archaeology]]; debate [[processual-postprocessual-debate]]. Companion volume: *[[shanks-tilley-social-theory-and-archaeology-1987|Social Theory and Archaeology]]* (1987). ### Pref. 2nd ed. + Intro + past/present + positivism (R1)
- Ethics and values *within* archaeological work; archaeology as material practice in the present (not abstract epistemology).
- Context cannot be fixed once and for all — interpretive decision of present purpose (beer-can dispersal as illustration).
- Polemical style defended; knee-jerk labels recorded (nihilist, Stalinist, Kossinna analogy, Boasian particularist).
- Commodified/allochronic time; multiple pasts; text/rhetoric/storytelling; spectacular archaeology and CRM as cultural capitalism.
- 'New' archaeology as positivism: naturalism, phenomenalism, hypothetico-deductive prestige; middle-range as empiricism (as they characterize it).

### Value-freedom + museums (R2)
- Value-freedom imports unrecognized capitalist values; separation of facts/values is disabling ideology.
- Schools as ideology: economic optimization, systems homeostasis-as-pathology, cultural evolution's politicized time, mathematization.
- Notes toward **critical archaeology** — make values explicit; reflexivity; rhetoric; truth as practical.
- Museum modes: commodity/chronometric (Newcastle); aesthetic/*Homo Artifex* (British Museum); narrative/information (Museum of London); period-room simulacrum and shop-front (York); heritage myth (Beamish); hero-archaeologist (Jorvik).
- **[[redemptive-aesthetic]]**: heterogeneity; democratized authorship; open construction; montage/irony; non-permanent; community use.

### Hermeneutics + social archaeology (R3)
- Hermeneutic circle (Bauman/Gadamer/Heidegger as they use them); hermeneutics not an alternative methodology.
- **[[fourfold-hermeneutic]]**: (i) contemporary discipline; (ii) present social participation; (iii) alien culture meaning frames; (iv) transcending past and present — expanding Giddens's [[double-hermeneutic]].
- Dialectics beyond pure hermeneutics; theoretical vs real object; data as resistances; knowledge as production.
- Object of [[social-archaeology]]: MC patterning ↔ practices/strategies/structures; anti-essentialism; agency (stratified model); structuration; power/ideology/contradiction; MC as objectification.

### Style + TRB (R4)
- Seven style accounts critiqued (normative, drift, adaptation, interaction, motor habit, information exchange, isochrestic).
- Alternative: style as social practice; three levels (habituated consciousness; restructuring reality; insertion of ideology).
- **Attributed case:** Fjälkinge No. 9 — ~70 TRB vessels, phases, bounded designs, six spatial regions, dramaturgical tomb reading (single-analysis findings + ideological interpretation).

### Beer cans (R5)
- Multi-variable design analysis Britain vs Sweden; beer vs lager; Swedish strength classes I–III; PCA/PCO clustering.
- Political economy of drink, disciplinary power, Bratt system, medicalization, brewing industry, news and advertising.
- Synthesis (theoretical): can design as ideological mediation of consumption vs social constraint; artifact context as dispersal.

### Conclusions + Appendix (R6)
- Scepticism vs scientism; archaeology as hermeneutically informed dialectical practice; radical pluralism; feminist and Indigenous stakes.
- Four criticisms (subjectivity, relativism, fiction, impotency) and materialist replies; contingent objectivity; "not just anything goes."
- Named reception (*NAR* 22; Kristiansen; Watson; Renfrew, Trigger, Wenke opposed as they report); 1990s programme points; relation of RCA to STA.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Re-Constructing Archaeology* is the vault's **practice-facing twin** to *[[shanks-tilley-social-theory-and-archaeology-1987|Social Theory and Archaeology]]*. Where STA densifies metatheory (theory-as-practice, [[logic-of-contingency]], politics of theory), RCA densifies **critique of positivism and value-freedom**, **museum/heritage aesthetics**, **[[fourfold-hermeneutic|hermeneutic architecture]]**, **[[social-archaeology]] ontology**, and two extended material-culture encounters (prehistoric style-as-ideology; modern design under constraint). Together with Hodder's 1999 field-method charter and Tilley's 1994 landscape phenomenology, the Anglophone post-processual wing is now primary-documented on three complementary axes: **metatheory/politics**, **critical practice + MC analysis**, and **recursive excavation method**, plus a later **landscape embodiment** strand.
