# Interaction Ritual Chains (Collins 2004)

[[collins-randall|Randall Collins]]'s *Interaction Ritual Chains* (Princeton University Press, 2004) is the mature statement of his **interaction ritual (IR) theory**: a radical microsociology in which situations, not individuals, are the starting point; successful rituals generate **[[emotional-energy|emotional energy (EE)]]**, solidarity, and membership symbols; and chains of such encounters over time produce selves, stratification, markets, thought, sexuality, and historical shifts in manners. It develops the IR micro-mechanism already present in *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology]]* (1975) into a general research program.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Interaction Ritual Chains* is best read as a single argument in two parts. **Part I** states the mechanism: situation-first radical microsociology (Ch. 1); the mutual-focus / emotional-entrainment model with variable ingredients and outcomes (Ch. 2); [[emotional-energy|EE]] as long-term stratified currency (Ch. 3); IR markets that both embed and motivate material markets, with EE proposed as rational choice's missing common denominator (Ch. 4); thinking as internalized IR in network-charged symbols (Ch. 5). **Part II** applies the same engine to sexual interaction (Ch. 6), late-modern [[situational-stratification|situational stratification]] (Ch. 7), historical private/carousing rituals (Ch. 8), and the cult of individuality and inwardness as social products (Ch. 9) — closing with the formula that human beings are **EE-seekers**.

Relative to already-ingested *[[collins-conflict-sociology-1975|Conflict Sociology]]*, this book is the micro wing detached and expanded: the 1975 IR propositions become a general theory; EE is the new master concept; the micro–macro promissory note is better specified (chains + markets) without being closed as a settled finding. Relative to [[rational-choice-theory|Coleman's rational choice]], Collins offers a rival micro-foundation rather than a friendly amendment. Relative to [[collective-effervescence|Durkheimian collective effervescence]], he localizes, stratifies, and conflictualizes what functionalist receptions had globalized.
