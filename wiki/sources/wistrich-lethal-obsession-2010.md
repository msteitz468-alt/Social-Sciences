# A Lethal Obsession (Wistrich, 2010)

Robert Wistrich's ~1,100-page synthesis of antisemitism from antiquity to the twenty-first century, centered on the argument (attributed) that a "lethal obsession" — genocidal, conspiracist Jew-hatred — has migrated from Christian Europe through Nazism and Soviet anti-Zionism into contemporary Islamist and Western anti-Zionist discourse.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*A Lethal Obsession* is at once a reference archive and a brief. As an archive it is the most comprehensive single documentation of post-1945 antisemitic discourse this wiki holds — country by country (post-Soviet Russia, Eastern Europe, Austria, Germany, France, Britain, the Americas, the Arab and Muslim world, Iran), with named actors, dated statements, and the survey/incident record. As a brief it argues one thesis relentlessly: that a single "lethal obsession" runs from Christian deicide theology through the Protocols and Nazism into Soviet anti-Zionism and contemporary Islamist and Western anti-Zionist discourse, with Israel as the "collective Jew." The archive layer feeds this wiki's factual pages in attributed voice; the thesis layer is quarantined as Position 1 of [[new-antisemitism-debate]].

The book extends [[cohn-norman|Cohn]]'s *Warrant for Genocide* framework — the conspiracy myth as genocidal warrant — forward by half a century and eastward into the Middle East, and the wiki's Cohn material should be read as its intellectual scaffolding. Its main tensions with the wiki's other holdings: where [[nirenberg-david|Nirenberg]] refuses a continuous-hatred teleology (anti-Judaism as thought-habit, not a single transmitted obsession), Wistrich asserts precisely such a continuity; that tension is the live axis of both [[anti-judaism-continuity-debate]] and [[new-antisemitism-debate]]. Methodologically, the book's evidence class is almost entirely textual/discursive — it demonstrates the circulation of motifs abundantly, but circulation-to-causation inferences (e.g., the Mufti's role, incitement → violence) are its weakest, most contested links.
