# Exit, Voice, and Loyalty (Hirschman, 1970)

Bibliographic record for Albert O. Hirschman's *Exit, Voice, and Loyalty: Responses to Decline in Firms, Organizations, and States* (Harvard University Press, 1970). Founding statement of the [[exit-voice-loyalty]] framework: when firms, organizations, or states decline, members and customers may **[[exit-option|exit]]** or exercise **[[voice-option|voice]]**; **[[loyalty]]** delays exit and can activate voice. Author: [[hirschman-albert]]. Theory Hub: [[hubs/theory/exit-voice-loyalty]].

Written at the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences; dedicated to Eugenio Colorni (1909–1944). Origin: a paragraph on Nigerian rail transport in *Development Projects Observed* (1967) that a critic charged with hidden assumptions.

## Central Argument

*(Attributed — Hirschman.)* Economists have neglected **repairable lapses** in performance — random losses of maximizing energy, continuously regenerated as [[organizational-slack|slack]]/entropy in surplus-producing societies. Two internal recuperation mechanisms of equal rank respond to quality decline: **exit** (market/leave) and **voice** (political/protest). Their interaction is the book's subject: exit can drain the most quality-conscious members and *atrophy* voice; limited competition can *comfort* lazy monopoly; loyalty holds exit at bay and activates voice; public goods make full exit impossible; no stable optimal mix exists because each mechanism itself decays.

## Volume Synthesis Note

EVL is a short book with a long afterlife: a middle-range apparatus for asking how organizations recover from decline without assuming either perfect markets or purely moral rescue. What it adds to this wiki:

1. **A second charter text** next to [[olson-logic-collective-action-1965|Olson 1965]] in the economics–politics border zone — different dependent variable (recuperation after lapse vs group formation for a collective good), explicit citation of Olson on few-buyer voice, shared public-goods language in Ch. 7.
2. **Concept kit** — [[exit-option]], [[voice-option]], [[loyalty]], [[organizational-slack]] — portable across firm, party, school, nation, and public enterprise.
3. **Design stance** — not a fixed optimal mix but diagnosis of which feedback is aroused vs which the organization hears; remedies include switching members between modes, not only stiffening the dominant one.
4. **Tensions with already-ingested sources:** complements [[collective-action-theory]] and [[rational-choice-theory]] without being absorbed by them; revises Hotelling–Downs as received in political science; frames Friedman-style exit preference as disciplinary bias rather than neutral efficiency.
