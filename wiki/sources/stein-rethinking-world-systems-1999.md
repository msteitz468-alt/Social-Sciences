# Rethinking World-Systems (Stein 1999)

Gil J. Stein's monograph reframing the archaeological study of interregional interaction
in early complex societies. It mounts a sustained critique of the uncritical application
of [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]] models to prehistory and offers two
alternative frameworks — the [[trade-diaspora|trade-diaspora]] and [[distance-parity|distance-parity]]
models — tested against Stein's own excavations at [[hacinebi|Hacınebi Tepe]] in the
[[uruk-expansion|Uruk-period]] Anatolian periphery. The wiki's fullest statement of
[[uruk-world-system-debate|Position 2]] in the Uruk world-system debate.

## Core Contributions to the Wiki

- The book's argument enters the wiki as **[[uruk-world-system-debate|Position 2]]** against
  [[algaze-guillermo|Algaze's]] [[algaze-uruk-world-system-2005|Uruk world-system]]: the
  [[distance-parity|distance-parity model]] (core power decays with transport cost, yielding
  symmetry in far peripheries) and the archaeological [[trade-diaspora|trade-diaspora]]
  concept (a power-neutral replacement for "colony").
- Chapters 1–4 supply the theoretical critique of [[world-systems-analysis|world-systems]]
  applied to prehistory and a survey of the wider critical literature (Skocpol, Sahlins,
  Kohl, Edens, Chase-Dunn & Hall) — all in attributed/position voice.
- Chapter 5 supplies the **method**: paired eight-point archaeological correlate sets for
  distinguishing symmetric from asymmetric interaction (see [[distance-parity]]).
- Chapter 7 (Hacınebi) is the empirical core — see [[hacinebi]] and [[stein-hacinebi-study]].

## Reliability

Hybrid primary excavation report + theoretical program: the empirical chapters rest on
Stein's own Hacınebi dig, so evidence and interpretation share an author — a standard
excavation-monograph caution, mitigated by the convergence of independent evidence classes
(ceramics, fauna, INAA, radiocarbon, archaeobotany). Read as one side of the still-open
[[uruk-world-system-debate]], not a neutral synthesis.
