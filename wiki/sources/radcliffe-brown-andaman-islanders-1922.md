## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Andaman Islanders* (1922) is both a primary ethnography of Great Andaman social life at colonial crisis and a method charter for British social anthropology. Descriptive chapters (I–IV + App. A) reconstruct local-group organisation without clans, ceremonial passages, spirit/weather belief, and variant myths, under conditions of catastrophic depopulation after the 1858 Penal Settlement. Interpretative chapters (V–VI), drafted c. 1910 after fieldwork, argue that customs have **meanings** as parts of a present **system** ("social physiology"), that **social function** is contribution to solidarity/cohesion via maintenance of **sentiments**, and that comparison must be of **social systems or types**, not isolated traits — against conjectural history and English intellectualist explanations of belief. The book thus supplies the structural/sentiments pole of 1922 functionalism alongside Malinowski's *Argonauts* needs/imponderabilia pole. Production limits (Little Andaman language barrier; post-field interpretation; dependence on Man; period Negrito framing) are first-class content for historiography, not reasons to thin the record.
