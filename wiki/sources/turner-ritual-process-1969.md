# The Ritual Process: Structure and Anti-Structure (Victor Turner, 1969)

Turner's most influential book, based on the 1966 Morgan Lectures. It opens with two
chapters of Ndembu ethnography — the Isoma fertility ritual and the paradoxes of
twinship (Wubwang'u) — analyzed through the color triad and processual method developed
in *The Forest of Symbols*, then crosses what Turner calls the "threshold" from African
ritual to a general theory of social process. Chapters 3–5 develop the paired concepts
of **structure** and **communitas** (the unstructured, egalitarian, "anti-structural"
bond of persons in the liminal state), argue that all societies alternate between the
two, and trace communitas across religious orders, millenarian movements, marginals,
"the powers of the weak," and rituals of status reversal. The book carried "liminality"
and "communitas" out of anthropology into history, religious studies, theatre, and
literary criticism.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Ritual Process* is the book in which Turner "crosses the threshold" — his own image — from
Ndembu ethnography to a general theory of social process. Chapters 1–2 (Isoma, twinship) are of a
piece with *[[turner-forest-of-symbols-1967|The Forest of Symbols]]*: the same color triad, the
same [[symbolic-exegesis]], the same [[ndembu]] fieldwork. The break comes in chapters 3–5, where
[[liminality]] — a phase Turner had already isolated — becomes the seedbed of **[[communitas]]**,
and communitas becomes one pole of a dialectic that Turner claims runs through *all* societies:
"maximization of communitas provokes maximization of structure." The book's power and its
vulnerability are the same fact: it lifts concepts defined in a seclusion lodge into claims about
the Franciscans, the Sahajiya, and the 1960s counterculture, on the strength of secondary reading
rather than fieldwork. That reach is what carried the terms into history, theology, and
performance studies, and it is what critics fasten on ([[communitas-scale-debate]]). Read
alongside [[gluckman-max|Gluckman]]'s [[ritual-of-rebellion|rituals of rebellion]], chapter 5
shows the [[manchester-school|Manchester School]]'s conflict-functionalism being reworked into a
processual dialectic: status reversal not as a safety-valve for a fixed order but as communitas
scouring and regenerating structure.
