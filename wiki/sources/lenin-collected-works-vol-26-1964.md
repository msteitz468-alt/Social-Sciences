# Lenin, Collected Works Vol. 26 (selections)

The seizure-of-power volume: Lenin's texts from the insurrection debate through October and the dissolution of the Constituent Assembly. 4-agent extraction over three targeted slices (⚠️ partial — volume's minor material skipped).

## Load-bearing content (all attributed)

- **"Marxism and Insurrection"** (Sept 1917, private CC letter): insurrection as an art, attributed to Marx; the three conditions distinguishing Marxist insurrection from Blanquism (advanced class, popular upsurge, enemy vacillation) ([[social-revolution]], [[vanguard-party]]).
- **"The Crisis Has Matured"** (Sept 29): peasant revolt as proof of national crisis; Lenin's resignation threat to the CC over delay.
- **"Can the Bolsheviks Retain State Power?"** (Oct 1917): the six pleas of Novaya Zhizn rebutted; state as "a class concept"; six advantages of Soviets as new state apparatus; the 240,000-member governance argument; accounting-and-control, work-books, "he who does not work, neither shall he eat" ([[the-state]], [[bureaucracy]], [[state-capitalism]]).
- **October 25–26 documents:** "the workers' and peasants' revolution... has been accomplished"; the Decree on Peace (annexation defined, secret treaties abolished) and Decree on Land with the 242-mandate Peasant Mandate ([[peasantry-as-class]]).
- **Constituent Assembly cluster** (Dec 1917–Jan 1918): Theses on the Constituent Assembly ("the interests of this revolution stand higher than the formal rights of the Constituent Assembly"), the Declaration of Rights of the Working and Exploited People, the dissolution decree and speech ([[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]], [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]).
- **Brest-Litovsk theses tail** (Jan 1918): the three party factions (revolutionary war / Trotsky's "neither war nor peace" / Lenin's separate peace) — recorded on [[trotsky-leon]]; "the interests of socialism are higher than the interests of the right of nations to self-determination."
- **"How to Organise Competition?"**: accounting-and-control as the essence of socialist transformation; includes Lenin's punitive catalogue against "idlers" (up to summary shooting "in a fourth place") — retained verbatim where load-bearing, as primary evidence of early-Soviet coercion doctrine.

## Reliability

The volume mixes public interventions, state documents, and private letters published years later — check each text's publication history (recorded in claims) before treating it as contemporary public discourse. Soviet editorial apparatus is teleological.
