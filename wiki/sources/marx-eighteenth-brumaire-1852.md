# The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte (Marx, 1852)

[[marx-karl|Karl Marx]], *The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte* (German original 1852; English public-domain translation as ingested). A short book-length analysis of the French Second Republic from the February Revolution of 1848 through Louis Bonaparte’s coup d’état of 2 December 1851 — named after the original 18 Brumaire (9 November 1799) on which Napoleon I seized power. Ingested from the DigiCat 2022 plain-text in `raw/`.

## What the text is

A **mixed** work: empirical-historical reconstruction of a concrete political cycle **and** a theoretical intervention in [[historical-materialism|historical materialism]] — especially on class fractions, the peasantry, the [[lumpenproletariat]], and the **relative autonomy** of the executive [[the-state|state]] under [[bonapartism|Bonapartism]]. It is not a programmatic manifesto (contrast [[marx-engels-communist-manifesto-1848|the *Manifesto*]]) and not a critique of political economy (contrast [[marx-capital-volume-1-1867|*Capital* I]]). Every interpretive claim drawn from it is **attributed** to Marx.

## Reliability and register

Written under the pressure of events (as later prefaces state — not in this file), the *Brumaire* is simultaneously journalism, polemic, and theory. Its class maps of 1848–51 France and its account of the coup are **Marx’s reconstructions**; later historians contest details of class alliances and peasant politics. The theoretical payload — especially the independence of the executive when the bourgeoisie loses capacity or will to rule politically — is a **position** within Marxism and political sociology, not a wiki-voice law of states. The translation line is older public-domain English; key phrases (e.g. “sack of potatoes,” “men make their own history”) may differ slightly from modern scholarly translations (Fowkes et al.).
