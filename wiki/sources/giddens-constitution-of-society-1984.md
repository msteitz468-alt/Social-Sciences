# The Constitution of Society (Giddens, 1984)

[[giddens-anthony|Anthony Giddens]], *The Constitution of Society: Outline of the Theory of Structuration* (Cambridge: Polity Press, 1984). Foundational Critical Turn statement of **[[structuration-theory|structuration theory]]** — Giddens's program for reconciling structure and agency via the **[[duality-of-structure|duality of structure]]**. Primary base for this vault's Giddens apparatus.

## What the book is

A systematic theoretical outline. Giddens argues that structure is both medium and outcome of the practices it recursively organizes; agents are knowledgeable and capable, yet act under conditions they did not choose and produce unintended consequences that feed back into structural properties. The book synthesizes and reworks resources from Goffman, Garfinkel/ethnomethodology, Freud (critically), time-geography (Hägerstrand), Foucault (critically), Durkheim, Marx, Weber, and Parsons — always in service of the structuration frame.
