### Part I — Discovery of prehistory (attributed, Renfrew 2007)
- Dual sense of prehistory: deep time before writing + the discipline that studies it; past always seen through a modern "mirror."
- Concept only ~two centuries old; Ussher 4004 BCE blocked deep human time; Thomsen Three Age; uniformitarian geology; Frere at Hoxne; 1859 antiquity of man + Darwin.
- Word "prehistory": Wilson 1851; Lubbock 1865 paleolithic/neolithic.
- Culture-history to 1940: Montelius *ex Oriente lux*; Kossinna; Childe's modified diffusion and Neolithic/Urban Revolutions; colonial diffusion critiques (Great Zimbabwe, Australia).
- Libby radiocarbon; dendrochronology calibration; first radiocarbon revolution shocks (Britain, Vinča); collapse of automatic *ex Oriente lux* for European megaliths and Balkan metallurgy.
- Clark *World Prehistory* (1961) as first coherent radiocarbon-based global outline; postcolonial national archaeologies; WAC; heritage politics (Ayodhya, Bamiyan).

### Part II — Prehistory of mind (attributed)
- **[[sapient-paradox]]:** genetic/cognitive modernity of *H. sapiens* far precedes sedentism, farming, urbanism, writing (~30k+ year lag after "modern" package).
- **[[speciation-phase]]** vs **[[tectonic-phase]]:** biological capacity formation vs multi-trajectory cultural construction after ~60 kya (as of Renfrew 2007).
- Cognitive archaeology: past ways of thought from material remains; two subfields map onto the two phases; mind embodied/extended/distributed; constitutive symbols (weight, value) via [[material-engagement]]; Donald stages + material-symbolic revision; [[external-symbolic-storage]].
- Community: sedentism as decisive turn (Natufian precedence); houses/property; group-oriented monuments as active memory (Orkney, Wessex, Stonehenge labor).
- Worldly goods: intrinsic value → commodity nexus → money/coinage; inequality paths; Varna, jade axes; Indus exception.
- Cosmos: Stonehenge/Newgrange alignments; divine kingship; Teotihuacan microcosm; royal burials; "early secular states" (Harappan caution); immanent religion hard to see archaeologically.
- Writing as ESS opening theoretic thought; alphabet vs Chinese path; money as parallel non-literary material engagement; Prospect: comparative program, genetics agenda, politics of heritage/looting.
