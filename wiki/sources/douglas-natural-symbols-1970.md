## Volume Synthesis Note

*Natural Symbols* is a **comparative theoretical program-statement** that founds [[grid-group-analysis]]. Its enduring moves are (1) the negative thesis that there are no free-standing [[natural-symbols|natural symbols]] of the body, only socially naturalized selections; (2) the two dimensions of [[grid-and-group]] as generators of cosmology and ritual style; (3) the correlation of [[body-symbolism|bodily control]] with social control; (4) the relocation of [[anti-ritualism]] from spiritual progress narrative to social-environment variable, via analogy to Bernstein's [[restricted-and-elaborated-codes]]. Edition history matters: 1970 launches, 1973 Pelican clarifies axes (scatter vs quadrants), 1996 intro historiographically frames the book as Douglas's long-term quarry and admits diagram simplification.

For this wiki the book pairs with *[[douglas-purity-and-danger-1966|Purity and Danger]]* without collapsing into it: purity/pollution answers "what is dirt?"; grid/group answers "why do symbolic environments vary?" Later risk/cultural-theory works are successor literature, not yet ingested. Reflexivity: British Catholic intellectual production; 1960s anti-structure occasion; Africanist secondary sample plus Western religious sociology — not a global probability sample.

**Reliability:** PDF text layer complete through most of Ch.10; final sentence truncated in extract. Theoretical claims stay attributed. Ethnographic illustrations are Douglas's uses of others' field reports.
