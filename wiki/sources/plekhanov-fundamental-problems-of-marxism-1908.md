# Fundamental Problems of Marxism (1908)

Plekhanov's late, compact codification of dialectical materialism — written in the empirio-criticism fight's peak year, and the companion piece to the ingested [[plekhanov-monist-view-of-history-1895|Monist View]] (1895): the founder's first and last systematic statements now bracket his work. ~47k words, 2-agent extraction.

## The doctrine (attributed)

- **The Spinozism thesis** (heavily verbatim-grounded): Feuerbach's humanism is "nothing else than Spinozism which has shed its theological lumber," and "this Spinozism... was the philosophy which Marx and Engels adopted... the Spinozism of Marx and Engels is materialism in its most modern form" — with the accompanying insistence on the *unity, not identity*, of thought and being (against Hegel/Schelling and against Conrad Schmidt's conflation).
- **The five-term formula** — the historically canonical expansion of base/superstructure: (1) state of the productive forces; (2) economic relations conditioned by them; (3) the socio-political regime on that foundation; (4) the psychology of social man, determined partly directly by economics and partly by the whole regime; (5) ideologies reflecting that psychology. Called by Plekhanov "a monistic formula... impregnated with materialism" ([[base-and-superstructure]]).
- **Ideology and art:** the Romanticist trinity (Hugo/Delacroix/Berlioz) as one psychology across unrelated arts; the Taine critique continued from 1895.
- **"Sudden Changes in Nature and History":** the quantity→quality leaps essay (water, chrysalis, 1789) against gradualist evolutionism — the anti-Tikhomirov polemic.
- **"Dialectic and Logic":** formal logic's three laws valid within limits, dialectic as the "logic of contradiction" for motion and transition; Marx's 1873 *Capital* afterword as anchor.
- **On the Machist controversy:** notably, Plekhanov's own 1908 essay contains only a brief protest against attempts to "provide a new basis" for Marxism (neo-Kantianism, Machism); the extended anti-Bogdanov framing is **Riazanov's 1928 preface**, not Plekhanov's text — an attribution distinction that matters for [[empirio-criticism-debate]].

## The Riazanov layer (1928, secondary, attributed)

The editor corrects the author in his own edition: Plekhanov's Feuerbach-as-Marx's-foundation formulation judged "not perfectly correct" against the Theses on Feuerbach; a de Vries mutation note supplemented via Timiryazev; Planck's quantum theory added as post-1908 confirmation of leaps. With the Monist View edition's apparatus, the wiki now holds two dated strata of Soviet editorial correction of the "father of Russian Marxism" — drift evidence spanning 1928 and the post-1938 canon.

## Lenin's marginalia (per [[lenin-philosophical-notebooks-1933]])

CW 38 preserves Lenin's own "NB" marks and marginal notes ("REMARKS IN BOOKS") on a copy of this very book — a distinct evidentiary layer from Lenin's other citations of Plekhanov. Lenin marked passages on: dialectics vs. Feuerbach and Dietzgen's definitions, and Marx and Engels's "elaboration of a correct method" against Feuerbach's failure to appreciate Hegel's dialectic; the case against vulgar evolutionism ("neither nature nor history makes leaps"), citing Hegel's dismissal of gradualist "theory of development" as "ridiculous and untenable"; the geographical-environment and productive-forces passage, including hunting tribes' inability to sustain large political organizations at low productive-force levels; Plekhanov's quotation of Engels on base/superstructure and necessity vs. accident in history ("Men make their history themselves, but not as yet with a collective will..."); and Plekhanov's five-term base/superstructure schema itself. Notably, Lenin's famous complaint that none of the Marxists understood *Capital* without having studied the whole of Hegel's *Logic* — the "logic of Capital" aphorism — does **not** appear among the marginalia on this book; it occurs elsewhere in the notebooks, in Lenin's own 1914–15 conspectus of Hegel (see [[marx-karl]]), a negative finding worth recording rather than assuming.

## Reliability

The founder's final systematic word, in a Soviet edition already engaged in curating him. Two supplementary essays are 1890s polemics reprinted in 1908. Herero reference and period ethnographic vocabulary present in quoted sources — attributed material only.
