**Principal attributed architecture:**

1. **Terminology** — “conflict” = structural oppositions (personnel change, pattern constant); “contradiction” = principles forcing radical pattern change; surface strife = competition/dispute/quarrel; “cohesion” = underlying process.
2. **Property** — nested estates of administration and production; not feudal; not pure communism/individualism; status-embedded exchange; subsistence + prestige economies.
3. **Stateless order** — cross-cutting memberships (Cheyenne; [[plateau-tonga|Plateau Tonga]]/Colson); joking as political arbitration; no permanent factions.
4. **State scale** — Anuak headmanship → Shilluk symbolic kingship → authoritative chiefship → large kingships (Lozi, Buganda, Ruanda, West African cities); rebellion vs revolution; frailty of authority.
5. **Law** — plural meanings; hierarchy of social-control words; adjudication vs mediation; law and custom; [[intrinsic-penalties]]; restatement of [[bohannan-gluckman-debate]] (anti–cultural solipsism).
6. **Mystical control + method** — witchcraft/ancestors/divination; **[[extended-case-method]]** (Mitchell Yao; Turner social drama) vs apt illustrations.
7. **Equilibrium and science** — repetitive equilibrium with leeway; radical colonial change; anthropology as cumulative science; Eskimo song-contest closing exercise.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Politics, Law and Ritual* is Gluckman’s mid-career **teaching synthesis** of Manchester political and legal anthropology: denser and more systematic than the 1955 BBC lectures, less primary than the Barotse judicial monographs. Its load-bearing wiki contributions are (a) the public **extended-case** program with Mitchell/Turner exemplars, (b) the pluralist legal-vocabulary program that frames the Bohannan debate for students, (c) the estates-of-administration land-tenure model, and (d) the morphological scale of African polities from headman to city-state. Equilibrium language is retained but redefined against stasis charges; radical change under colonial economy is treated as open-system, not denied. The book is **not** a substitute for Colson’s Tonga monographs, Mitchell’s *Yao Village*, or Turner’s *Schism and Continuity* — those remain primary for their cases.
