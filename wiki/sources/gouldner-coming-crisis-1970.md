# The Coming Crisis of Western Sociology (Alvin W. Gouldner, 1970)

[[gouldner-alvin-w|Alvin W. Gouldner]]'s major sociology-of-sociology: a historically informed critique of Western Academic Sociology and of [[structural-functionalism|Parsonian structural-functionalism]] as its dominant theoretical synthesis, ending in a program for [[reflexive-sociology|reflexive sociology]]. Gouldner argues that Academic Sociology and Marxism form the two major "halves" of Western sociology, both approaching crisis under welfare-state and New Left conditions; Functionalism's "entropy" and the rise of rival infrastructures (dramaturgy, ethnomethodology, exchange theory, New Left) are symptoms. John Leonard (*New York Times*) called it "the most important book in this field since C. Wright Mills's *The Sociological Imagination*."

## Volume Synthesis Note

Gouldner's *Coming Crisis* is the wiki's load-bearing **critical-turn sociology of sociology**. Three moves organize the book and the pages it created:

1. **Infrastructure theory.** Social theories ride on [[domain-assumptions]], sentiments, and personal reality ([[theoretical-infrastructure]]); their careers are not settled by logical refutation alone. [[methodological-dualism]] protects the theorist from the scrutiny applied to subjects. Exit path: [[reflexive-sociology]] as work ethic and historical mission ("not merely how to work but how to live"), with knowledge as *awareness* rather than Positivist *information*.

2. **Historical model and binary fission.** Western sociology institutionalizes only after fission into Academic Sociology (west, middle-class reform) and Marxism (east, opposition). Four periods culminate in Parsonsian [[structural-functionalism]]; a fifth opens under Welfare-State polycentrism. Utilitarian culture is the long matrix; anomie is its "normal pathology."

3. **Parsons as case; entropy as diagnosis.** Chs. 5–9 are a close critique of [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]] (systems wholeness, moralistics of order, power/property, Welfare-State drift and partial Marx convergence). Ch. 10 diagnoses Functionalism's **entropy** and maps rival infrastructures ([[goffman-erving|Goffman]], ethnomethodology, Homans/Blau exchange, New Left). Ch. 11 sketches a Plato-to-Parsons conservative infrastructure; Ch. 12 mirrors the crisis in Soviet Academic Sociology; the Epilogue applies the method to Gouldner himself (including projection admission).

**What it adds to the wiki:** named pages for Gouldner, reflexive sociology, domain assumptions, theoretical infrastructure, methodological dualism, and the coming-crisis debate; deep second-order content on [[structural-functionalism]] and [[parsons-talcott]] complementary to the concurrent *Social System* primary ingest; links into [[sociology]], [[positivism]], [[anomie]], [[goffman-erving]], [[conflict-theory]].

**Tensions with other holdings:** Gouldner's Parsons is polemical — prefer [[parsons-social-system-1951]] / [[hubs/theory/structural-functionalism]] for program self-description. His Goffman is infrastructure diagnosis, not primary dramaturgy. Crisis-as-metamorphosis is only partly confirmed by later historiography (`status: absorbed` on Functionalism; pluralized "reflexivity"). East–West convergence remains a 1970 thesis, not wiki-voice fact.
