# Prehistoric Men (Braidwood, 3rd ed. 1959)

[[braidwood-robert|Robert J. Braidwood]]'s popular museum synthesis of human prehistory — Chicago Natural History Museum Popular Series — carrying the **[[hilly-flanks|hilly-flanks / natural-habitat]]** thesis of the **food-producing revolution**, the **[[nuclear-area|nuclear area]]** construct, the **[[incipient-cultivation|era of incipient cultivation and animal domestication]]**, and the multidisciplinary type-case of **[[jarmo|Jarmo]]** (Iraqi Kurdistan). Complements Childe's [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] vocabulary while rejecting the simple **oasis** environmental-stimulus story and equating neither tool-making traditions nor fossil "races" with archaeological cultures.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Prehistoric Men* is the mid-century **public charter** of Braidwood’s farming-origins program: a readable Old World sequence that climaxes not in the Paleolithic art chapter but in the **food-producing revolution** and the move from **[[hilly-flanks|hilly flanks]]** villages to riverine **civilization**. Relative to already-ingested Childe (*Man Makes Himself*, *What Happened in History*), it keeps the revolution metaphor while relocating geography (natural habitat vs oasis) and stretching tempo (incipient era). Relative to later Binford packing models, it is the nuclear-zone foil. The book is weak as primary excavation evidence (dates uncalibrated; Jarmo/Jericho/Natufian later revised) and strong as a dated position in the historiography of agricultural origins — hence the debate page [[oasis-natural-habitat-debate]] rather than wiki-voice adoption of hilly-flanks causality.
