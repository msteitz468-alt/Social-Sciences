# The Evolution of Culture (White, 1959)

Leslie A. White's *The Evolution of Culture: The Development of Civilization to the Fall of Rome* (McGraw-Hill, 1959; Left Coast Press reprint 2007) is the major book-length statement of White's **[[universal-evolution|universal cultural evolution]]** and of **[[culturology]]** as the science of culture-as-a-system. It synthesizes his earlier essays (energy and culture; history vs. evolution vs. functionalism; diffusion vs. evolution) into a two-part developmental account: **primitive culture** on a human-energy base, then the **[[agricultural-revolution|Agricultural Revolution]]** and the rise of civil society, **[[state-church]]**, class stratification, and theology-vs-science up to the fall of Rome.

## Volume Synthesis Note

White's 1959 volume is a **treatise on structure, function, and evolution of sociocultural systems** (Carneiro & Brown), not episodic world history. Part One (~three-quarters) builds the human-energy baseline: [[symboling]] as the human threshold; culture as extrasomatic continuum; [[culturology]]; the [[energy-law-of-cultural-evolution]] and formulas `E×T→P` / `E(H×N)×T→P`; social organization from anthropoid family through incest/exogamy, kinship, clans/moieties, classes, custom/ethics/law, kinship economy and myth-as-philosophy. Part Two becomes fully evolutionary: the [[agricultural-revolution|Agricultural Revolution]] as qualitative energy unlock (plants/animals), civil society on property relations, the [[state-church]] as integrative/coercive mechanism, commercial higher economies, theology as class instrument and science as emerging specialized tradition — arc closed at Rome's political collapse (culture not lost) with a teaser of the unfinished Fuel Revolution.

White's polemical frame is anti-anti-evolution: Boas/Laufer, *Kulturkreis*/Schmidt, and functionalist nonevolution. He **rejects "neoevolutionism"** as a label and claims principle-continuity with Tylor 1881 while updating demonstration. Steward's rival taxonomy names this pole **[[universal-evolution]]** against [[multilinear-evolution]]. Within the vault this source supplies the White primary that Steward 1955 and the [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]] previously cited only second-hand.

**What this source does not replace:** White's *Science of Culture* (1949) essay base; the unpublished Fuel Revolution / *Modern Capitalist Culture* industrial arc; Steward's ecological case monographs; primary Near Eastern excavation reports. All theoretical claims stay attributed; ethnographic and archaeological illustrations are synthesis, not new fieldwork.
