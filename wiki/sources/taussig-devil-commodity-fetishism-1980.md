## Volume Synthesis Note

Taussig's book is the wiki's first major **cultural-Marxist ethnography of proletarianisation**: devil contracts and baptism of money in Cauca, Tio rites in Bolivian tin mines, both read as mediations of use-value vs exchange-value and as ethnographic entry points to Marx's commodity fetishism. It densifies [[anthropological-political-economy]] from the meaning/magic side (adjacent to Wolf's relational history and Mintz's sugar circuit) and gives plantation pages a Colombian agribusiness case to set beside Caribbean slavery. The 2010 anniversary apparatus (*Sun Gives*, 2009 preface) is a documented self-reanalysis (Bataille, storytelling, neo-liberal short contracts) — keep it distinct from the 1980 core. Collection gaps: June Nash's mine monographs; Taussig *Shamanism, Colonialism, and the Wild Man*; later occult-economies literature.
