# The Dobe Ju/'hoansi (Lee, 4th ed. 2013)

[[lee-richard-b|Richard B. Lee]]'s longitudinal teaching ethnography of the [[juhoansi|Ju/'hoansi]] of the Dobe (Botswana) and Nyae Nyae (Namibia) areas — Case Studies in Cultural Anthropology series. Fourth edition updates political evolution, land, education, health (including HIV/AIDS), and adds **Chapter 13: Tsumkwe at 50** (2010–11 social survey). Complements *[[lee-kung-san-1979|The !Kung San]]* (1979 research monograph).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Dobe Ju/'hoansi* (4th ed.) is the **longitudinal teaching twin** of *[[lee-kung-san-1979|The !Kung San]]* (1979). Where the monograph freezes a research apparatus on 1960s–70s ecology, economy, demography, and conflict, the case study carries students through ethnonym change, life-cycle ([[complaint-discourse]]), neighbors, and — critically — **transformation to 2011**: Botswana land and schooling struggles, Namibian Tsumkwe settlement and conservancy politics, HIV, and a 2010–11 Tsumkwe survey showing mixed subsistence and continued valuation of bush life. Appendix B densifies the [[kalahari-debate]] with Ju oral history (*n//a k aishe*) that Lee argues was missing from both isolationist and revisionist camps. Appendix A reprints the Christmas ox essay that made [[insulting-the-meat]] famous.

What it adds beyond 1979: dated change narrative; Tsumkwe survey numbers; Rosenberg aging chapter; oral-history App. B. What it does not replace: the quantitative apparatus and homicide case archive of the 1979 monograph. Wilmsen 1989 remains the outstanding primary for full revisionist densification.
