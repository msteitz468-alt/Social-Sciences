## Volume Synthesis Note

*Durable Inequality* is a **mechanism charter** for stratification: large durable inequalities map onto paired categories because resource controllers solve organizational problems with categorical distinctions. Two installing mechanisms ([[exploitation]], [[opportunity-hoarding]]) and two reinforcing ones ([[organizational-emulation|emulation]], [[organizational-adaptation|adaptation]]) do the work; matching [[interior-exterior-categories|interior organizational categories]] to exterior pairs multiplies durability and lowers cost. The book is programmatically **relational** (bonds over essences) and **anti-mentalist** (beliefs reinforce more than they initiate). Empirical content is illustrative — stature gaps, chain migration (Roccasecca–Mamaroneck vs Lyon), ethnic niches, gender at work and household–firm edges, South Africa, Catholic Emancipation, professions, health care — not a single sample design. Politics is the special case when a government is a party; democracy is threatened by widespread categorical inequality. Intervention leverage is organizational redesign (break exterior–interior matching; alter referral networks), not hire-point antidiscrimination alone or tolerance education. What it adds to this wiki: first full inequality program for Tilly beside [[political-conflict-theory]]; opportunity-hoarding and categorical-inequality concept kit; Theory Hub depth page. Tensions: working hypotheses by Tilly's own admission; reception and formal tests not yet ingested; race/gender interchangeability claim softened by his own structural contrast of how those categories cut social life.
