# Reproduction in Education, Society and Culture (Bourdieu & Passeron, 1970/1990)

[[bourdieu-pierre|Pierre Bourdieu]] and [[passeron-jean-claude|Jean-Claude Passeron]], *Reproduction in Education, Society and Culture*, trans. Richard Nice (London: Sage, 2nd ed. 1990; French *La reproduction*, Éditions de Minuit, 1970). The founding statement of the *cultural reproduction* program in the sociology of education and the fullest development of the theory of [[symbolic-violence]]. See the study analysis [[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-study]] and its deep treatment [[hubs/studies/bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-study|Studies Hub]].

## What the book argues (attributed)

Book I builds, as numbered propositions, a general theory of [[symbolic-violence]]: all [[pedagogic-action]] is "the imposition of a [[cultural-arbitrary]] by an arbitrary power," succeeding only through misrecognized [[pedagogic-authority]] and durable [[pedagogic-work]] that produces a [[habitus]]. Book II shows the French educational system performing four functions inseparably — communication, inculcation of legitimate culture, [[self-elimination|selection]], and legitimation — so as to reproduce the class distribution of [[cultural-capital]] ([[cultural-reproduction]]) while concealing that it does so ([[relative-autonomy-of-the-educational-system]]). The Appendix's "translation not redistribution" analysis shows post-war expansion moving the whole distribution of chances upward without narrowing relative class gaps.
