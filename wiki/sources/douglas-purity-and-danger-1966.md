## Volume Synthesis Note

*Purity and Danger* is a **comparative theoretical program-statement**, not a site monograph. Its enduring move is to treat dirt, pollution, and taboo as effects of **classification and social structure** — "Where there is dirt there is system" — against medical-materialist, evolutionary-survivals, and piecemeal accounts. The 2002 preface is historiographically essential: it restates the dirt thesis, situates sleeper reception and anti-racist intent, bridges to risk politics, and **repudiates the 1966 Leviticus chapter** as a major mistake (circular anomaly reading; weak social-system link; wrong theology of abominable creatures), pointing to later covenant/body–altar work. Integration must keep the 1966 Ch. 3 argument as a **historical position** of the book that made it famous, not as Douglas's standing biblical view.

For this wiki the book is a pillar of symbolic/structural anthropology of religion alongside Durkheimian classification ([[primitive-classification]], [[sacred-and-profane]]) and a reworking of [[taboo]] after Frazer/Robertson Smith. Successor programs (grid/group; cultural theory of risk) should be filed separately when those sources are ingested — *Natural Symbols* remains on the outstanding list.
