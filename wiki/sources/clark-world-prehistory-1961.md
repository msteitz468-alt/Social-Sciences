# World Prehistory: An Outline (Clark, 1961)

[[clark-grahame|Grahame Clark]]'s first modern-style **global** prehistoric textbook — a single-frame outline of human biological and cultural development from the Pleistocene through the threshold of continuous written history in each region. Complements his methodological charter *[[clark-archaeology-and-society-1957|Archaeology and Society]]* (1939/1957) and the European economic demonstration *Prehistoric Europe: The Economic Basis* (1952) by applying the **[[economic-prehistory|economic–ecological approach]]** at world scale. Trigger later called this lineage the first modern global prehistory textbook; this ingest is the 1961 *An Outline* text (1967 reprint), not the 1969 *A New Outline* revision.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*World Prehistory: An Outline* (1961) is the vault's first **global** prehistoric teaching synthesis — Clark's ecological–economic program applied continent by continent rather than as an Old-World “main stream” alone. Relative to already-ingested Childe (*What Happened in History*, *Man Makes Himself*, *Dawn*, *Danube*), this book owns the **[[world-prehistory]]** genre frame, full New World and Australasian chapters, Maglemosian/[[mesolithic]] depth, and a cautious early-[[radiocarbon-dating]] outline practice. Relative to *[[clark-archaeology-and-society-1957|Archaeology and Society]]*, it is demonstration-at-scale rather than method charter.

It remains a **period document**: pre-calibration absolute chronology; residual [[ex-oriente-lux]] structure for farming and European BA (Wessex–Mycenae); adaptive-race and progress language in Ch. 1 and the Retrospect; culture-historical migration labels (Battle-axe, Beaker, Aryans, Palaeoindians) that must not be equated with languages or genetic populations. Filename and IA metadata saying “A New Outline (1969)” are wrong for this PDF — the text is the 1961 outline (1967 reprint). Later revisions (1969, 1977) would re-ingest as separate sources if acquired.
