# The Savage Mind (1962)

Lévi-Strauss's treatise on **la pensée sauvage** — "untamed"/"savage" thought — arguing it is not pre-logical or need-driven but a rigorous [[science-of-the-concrete|science of the concrete]]: a systematic classification of the sensible world. It introduces **[[bricolage]]** (the bricoleur vs the engineer), dissolves **[[totemism]]** into a general logic of classification, works out how classification runs from the universal to the proper name, and closes with the famous polemic against **[[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre]]** on history and dialectical reason (see [[levi-strauss-sartre-debate]]).

## Key Claims Extracted

All attributed to Lévi-Strauss:

- **The [[science-of-the-concrete|science of the concrete]]:** "savage" thought is a rigorous classification of the sensible world — folk taxonomies far exceed practical need ("animals and plants are... deemed to be useful or interesting because they are first of all known"), against Malinowski's "rumbling of their stomachs." Magic and science are "two parallel modes of acquiring knowledge," not stages.
- **[[bricolage]]:** mythical thought is "intellectual bricolage" — the bricoleur makes do with "whatever is at hand" (a closed, heterogeneous set), working with signs where the engineer works with concepts.
- **The dissolution of [[totemism]]:** "totemism" is not one institution but a **logic of classification**; natural species encode social differences as a *homology of two systems of differences* (species chosen for their conceptual, not nutritional, value); **totem and caste** are inverse transformations ("castes naturalize a true culture falsely, totemic groups culturalize a false nature truly").
- **Cold vs hot societies:** societies that ritually annul historical change vs those that internalize it as their motor — replacing "peoples without history."
- **History and dialectic (vs [[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre]]):** dialectical and analytical reason are continuous, not opposed; History is one code among others, not privileged; valuing it above others is ethnocentric (the "myth of the French Revolution"); "the ultimate goal of the human sciences is not to constitute but to dissolve man."

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Savage Mind* extends [[structuralism]] from social organization (the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]*) and method (*[[levi-strauss-structural-anthropology-1958|Structural Anthropology]]*) into **cognition and classification** — the claim that "the savage mind... is not... a mind of savages" but a universal mode of thought coexisting with scientific reason. It supplies the vault's [[bricolage]] and [[science-of-the-concrete]] pages, completes the reinterpretation of [[totemism]] the totemism page had flagged as pending, and, in its closing chapter, stages the era-defining **structuralism-vs-existentialism** confrontation with [[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre]] that displaced him as the prestige position of the French field. With the 1958 and prior volumes it makes the collection's coverage of structuralism strong; only *Mythologiques* remains outstanding.
