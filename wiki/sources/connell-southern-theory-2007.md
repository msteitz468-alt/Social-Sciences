# Southern Theory (Connell, 2007)

[[connell-raewyn|Raewyn Connell]]'s intervention in the sociology of knowledge and global social theory. Argues that metropolitan social science presents itself as universal while embedding North Atlantic viewpoints; recovers social theorising from the global periphery as *theory*, not only as local data for Northern frameworks. ## Core argument (attributed)

Connell proposes a path for social theory that can serve democratic purposes on a world scale. Dominant genres, she argues, picture the world from the global **[[metropole]]** while claiming universality. **"Southern theory"** marks **relations** of knowledge/power between metropolitan and peripheral intellectuals — not a bounded map of states — and asserts that the majority world produces theory (contra Hountondji's colonial data/theory division of labour). Social thought is **placed** (Connell writes from Australia; Indigenous land is centre, not margin).

## Claims by part (ingest summary)

### Introduction
- Democratic purpose; ambiguous democracy of social science; planetary-scale knowledge
- Three reasons for the title (relations; periphery produces theory; place)
- Dumont's one-universal-science default; Gulbenkian *Open the Social Sciences* as Eurocentric (Wallerstein chair, Connell's reading)
- Universalism from privilege serves hegemony; modernity-as-North-Atlantic-self-creation as "gigantic lie"
- Alternative founding fathers (e.g. Ibn Khaldun claim) inadequate without changing production terms

### Ch. 1 — Empire and the creation of a social science
- Founding-fathers curriculum as **internalist** canon myth; classical-period sociologists lacked it (Giddings→Smith; Branford→Condorcet; Ward's 37 notables without Marx/Weber)
- *L'Année sociologique*: minority of reviews on modern industrial West; majority on ancient/colonial/global surveys
- Organising idea: **[[global-difference]]**; progress constitutive; ethnographic dividend of empire; race/gender/sexuality core early topics
- Comparative method maps colonial domination; grand ethnography; social evolution naturalises difference
- WWI ruptures progress worldview; only US academic sociology flourishes 1920–50; new object = disorder *within* metropole
- Classical canon as reconstruction (Parsons *Structure of Social Action*; later Marx graft); export of reconstructed discipline

### Ch. 2 — Modern general theory
- Cases: [[coleman-james]] *Foundations* (1990); [[giddens-anthony]] *Constitution* (1984); [[bourdieu-pierre]] *Logic of Practice* (1980/1990)
- Shared pattern: universal ambition; reading from centre; weak/no theory of empire; ethnosociology of metropole
- Coleman: neoliberal natural person; featureless dance floor; slavery as rational-choice puzzle; colonies unmentioned
- Giddens: duality from Euro-American antinomies; tribal/class-divided/class grid; imperialism unnamed
- Bourdieu: practical toolkit richest empirically yet still Northern standpoint problems (as Connell argues)
- Four Northern moves + *terra nullius* of theory

### Ch. 3 — Imagining globalisation
- Metropolitan globalisation sociology reifies "global society" from Northern concepts; often fails to name the metropole; erases colonialism
- Methodological projection of peripheral data into Northern frames; [[world-systems-analysis|Wallerstein]] names metropole/system but (Connell) reifies system and closes gender/race

### Ch. 4 — Discovery of Australia
- Settler colony: data mine of "most primitive" (Spencer & Gillen → Durkheim) → 1950–80 "branch office" sociology ("X in Australia")
- Cultural cringe; Indigenous presence drops out then returns as "ethnic minority"
- Connell: *none of this defines a distinctive Australian school of sociology* — rich periphery can link to other peripheral projects (Bulbeck) without inventing a national Southern school
- Autobiographical note: own earlier metropolitan-theory-first pattern

### Ch. 5 — Indigenous knowledge and African Renaissance
- Akiwowo 1986 *asuwada* indigenous sociology export project; Makinde; Lawuyi & Taiwo critique
- [[hountondji-paulin]]: extroversion, ethnophilosophy critique, endogenous knowledge
- African Renaissance rhetoric (Mbeki et al.); Plaatje *Native Life* as land/domination classic

### Ch. 6 — Islam and Western dominance
- al-Afghani *Refutation*; Al-e Ahmad **Gharbzadegi** (Westoxication) — machine dependency, desire, ulama, orientalism-before-Said
- Shariati: martyrdom, multi-part domination, *tawhid*, public intellectual practice; post-revolution defeat of that project (as Connell frames)

### Ch. 7 — Dependency, autonomy and culture
- [[prebisch-raul]]/CEPAL centre–periphery; ISI; false universality of economics
- Cardoso & Faletto dependent development; vs Frank/Amin delinking; cultural dependency (Dorfman/Mattelart)
- Dictatorships destroy autonomy models; **neoliberalism ≠ Southern theory**; hybridity / García Canclini; gender (Montecino)

### Ch. 8 — Power, violence and the pain of colonialism
- Subaltern Studies (Guha; archive as elite knowledge; dominance without hegemony)
- Early vs later Chatterjee (Connell prefers early modes-of-power work)
- Veena Das: Partition, Bhopal, limits of meaning
- [[nandy-ashis]] *Intimate Enemy*: colonialism remakes British culture/masculinity; Gandhi; critique of developmentalist science-state
- Psycho-cultural insight needs institutional analysis (Connell)

### Ch. 9 — Silence of the land
- Modern social theory silences land/place after early political economy (Smith/Ricardo) abstracted it away
- Yolngu / Yirrkala / Williams: land as *part of* social order; *terra nullius*; Mabo; Dodson on land rights and social justice
- Central Desert art as social knowledge; **[[dirty-theory]]**

### Ch. 10 — Social science on a world scale
- Mutual learning; studying up the metropole-apparatus; multi-centred Southern networks (currently low periphery–periphery connection)
- Mainstream sociology as ethnosociology of metropolitan society
- Permanent revolution of corrigibility vs protected metropolitan theory
- Four democratic ends: compassion; contesting untruth; movement-relevant knowledge; democracy in knowledge production
- Neoliberal constriction of social knowledge; path possible, not guaranteed

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Southern Theory* is the geopolitics-of-knowledge panel of Connell's career, continuous with her gender work's refusal of unmarked universal categories. It does not offer a single alternative grand theory; it offers a **program**: historicise Northern theory's imperial conditions; read Southern texts as theory; keep generalisation dirty and corrigible; build multi-centred links; tie social science to democratic self-knowledge against neoliberal spin. For this vault it densifies [[connell-raewyn]] and [[hubs/thinkers/connell-raewyn]], creates the [[southern-theory]] program page, and supplies attributed critiques of [[coleman-james]], [[giddens-anthony]], [[bourdieu-pierre]], and [[wallerstein-immanuel]]'s Gulbenkian panel — without treating Connell's readings as wiki-voice verdicts on those theorists' entire corpora.
