# Anthropology: Culture Patterns & Processes (Kroeber, 1963 / 1948 chs.)

A. L. [[kroeber-alfred-l|Kroeber]]'s *Culture Patterns & Processes* is a Harbinger Books abridgement of six chapters from his *Anthropology* (revised edition, 1948): the discipline-definition chapter plus the major theoretical treatments of **language**, the **nature of culture**, **patterns**, **culture processes**, and **culture change**. It is the vault's primary ingest of Kroeber's mature culture theory — [[superorganic]], [[ethos-and-eidos]], [[culture-pattern]], [[stimulus-diffusion]], and [[acculturation]].

## The Argument

Attributed to Kroeber throughout:

- **Anthropology's object.** Science of *groups* of humans and their works; dual participation in biological and social-historical sciences; distinctive focus on the **interrelation** of organic and sociocultural factors — not physiology or individual psychology alone.
- **Society vs culture.** Culture is the learned, transmitted mass of speech, knowledge, beliefs, customs, arts, ideals — what humans have that other social species lack. Society can exist without culture (ants, birds); no cultureless human society is known.
- **[[superorganic]].** Culture is organic-plus and superindividual: acquired by learning; historically continuous; explained primarily by other cultural facts; not a dualist "soul" free of organic life.
- **Language.** Genetic families via sound correspondences; type classification separate; language, race, and nationality vary independently; no ranking of languages as higher/lower; change largely unconscious; diffusion and parallelism both operate.
- **[[ethos-and-eidos]] and values.** Content vs form; eidos as describable appearance; ethos as pervading quality and values orientation; morals relative as folkways (Sumner lineage) without making standards meaningless.
- **[[culture-pattern]].** Universal schemes (weak), systemic patterns (diffusable plans), total-culture physiognomy, style patterns; genius and simultaneous invention cluster when patterns are "fruitful."
- **Processes.** Persistence via habit; invention rarely pure necessity; [[stimulus-diffusion]] as idea-plus-local-reinvention; cultural loss and isolation effects; no inherent force guaranteeing progressive accumulation.
- **Change and [[acculturation]].** Internal (subsistence, fashion, revolution, religious redirection) vs external (diffusion, acculturation); resistance and marginality; nativist revivals; modern technological uniformization and its costs.

## Reflexivity

This is a **theoretical textbook abridgement**: every claim is Kroeber's mid-century synthesis, and ethnographic cases are illustrations. The full *Anthropology* and California monographs would change the empirical weight. Production context: senior Boasian codifying culture theory for U.S. students after WWII, against residual race science and unilinear evolutionism, still largely silent on colonial power as structure. OCR of the IA PDF is noisy; section numbers match the 1948 parent.
