# Ancient North America: The Archaeology of a Continent (Fagan, 1991)

Brian Fagan's *Ancient North America* (1st ed. 1991) is a single-author survey of the
archaeology of the continent north of Mexico, organized region by region from the first
settlement of the Americas to European contact. Written for students and general readers, it
was for two decades a standard English-language regional synthesis. This wiki ingests it
primarily for its coverage of the **western and northern culture areas** — Arctic, Subarctic,
Northwest Coast, Plains, Great Basin, California, and the Southwest — which the collection's
existing North American sources (Milner on the Eastern Woodlands; Fagan & Durrani's world
prehistory) leave thin.

## Reliability and Reflexivity

- **Dated on peopling and chronology.** The 1991 edition treats Clovis-first as orthodoxy and
  predates Monte Verde's acceptance and the aDNA revolution. Its first-Americans chapters are
  recorded as the pre-1997 state of the field; current claims live on
  [[peopling-of-the-americas]] and [[peopling-of-the-americas-debate]].
- **Regional syntheses are the value.** Arctic, Northwest Coast, Great Basin, and Southwest
  coverage is less superseded and supplies pages the wiki otherwise lacks — date-stamped
  "(as of Fagan 1991)" and flagged where later work has moved on.
- **Author.** [[fagan-brian|Brian Fagan]] is a prolific synthesizer and popularizer of
  archaeology (see also [[fagan-great-journey-1987]], [[fagan-durrani-people-of-the-earth-2022]]);
  this is secondary synthesis, not primary field reporting.
