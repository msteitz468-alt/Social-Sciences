# Lenin: A Biography (Robert Service, 2000)

Full-length archival biography of Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov (**Lenin**, 1870–1924): mixed-status Ulyanov–Blank family in Simbirsk, Alexander Ulyanov's execution and Volodya's radicalisation, St Petersburg and Siberian exile, *Iskra*/Bolshevik faction-building in European emigration, 1905 and the second emigration, wartime defeatism, sealed-train return and the October seizure of power, Brest-Litovsk, Civil War and the Cheka, NEP, the succession struggle and Testament, death and mausoleum cult. For this wiki the source **rebalances** a Lenin thinker page built almost entirely from Lenin's own primary texts: it supplies life-course, formation, personality, archival governance detail, and a scholarly (revisionist, anti-hagiographic) historiographic frame — while all Service interpretive verdicts stay in **attributed** voice.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Service’s biography is a sustained archival case against hagiography (Soviet and Trotskyist) and against several Western simplifications: pure-doctrine Lenin (Harding), pure-psychopath Lenin (Pipes), late liberal reformer (Lewin/Cohen), wholly alien-to-Russia (Solzhenitsyn/Volkogonov), and Jewish-determinist ancestry theses (Soloukhin). Its load-bearing claims for this wiki are: (1) **formation** — deep-narrow Classics schooling, Alexander’s hanging, Chernyshevski and terrorist-stream influences alongside Marxism; (2) **personality-as-politics** — “political warrior,” family/women as organisational infrastructure, chronic illness; (3) **1917–21 pivots** — April, October, Brest, NEP as personal strategical shifts without which the USSR would not have been consolidated as it was; (4) **one-party state as improvisation within assumptions** — not a 1902 flowchart but 1918–19 elaboration of vanguard/leadership/infallibility; (5) **Testament and cult** — archival texture of the Stalin–Krupskaya clash, succession, mausoleum politics.

Tension with existing holdings is productive rather than contradictory: the wiki already holds dense **primary** Lenin; Service supplies the life and a hostile-to-legend secondary frame. Theoretical claims on doctrine pages remain grounded in Lenin’s own texts; Service’s character and “malignancy” verdicts stay quarantined as positions. Missing counterweights still include Lih (2011) and Lewin *Last Struggle* as independent secondary ingests.
