# Negara: The Theatre State in Nineteenth-Century Bali (Geertz, 1980)

Landmark political-anthropological monograph by [[geertz-clifford|Clifford Geertz]]. Argues that the classical Balinese **[[negara]]** was a **[[theatre-state]]**: power organized primarily as spectacle, ceremony, and the display of an **[[exemplary-center|exemplary center]]**, not as bureaucratic command or monopolized coercion. Major empirical densification of [[interpretive-anthropology]] after *[[geertz-interpretation-of-cultures-1973|The Interpretation of Cultures]]* (1973); companion Bali corpus with *[[geertz-peddlers-princes-1963|Peddlers and Princes]]* (1950s Tabanan) and *[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]* (cockfight as text). ## Volume Synthesis Note

*Negara* is Geertz’s major **political** densification of the interpretive program: culture as public symbol applied to the form of the classical state itself. The book’s enduring analytic products are the **[[theatre-state]]** type, the **[[exemplary-center]]** doctrine, **[[sinking-status]]** as gentry rank engine, and the closing **poetics of power** (“make inequality enchant”; “They were what there was”). Methodologically it models reconstructive historical anthropology when annalistic materials are thin — nineteenth-century Bali as ethnographic model for Indic Indonesian polities, against Oriental despotism and *dorpsrepubliek* couplets. Empirically it reorganizes Tabanan materials already used for *Peddlers and Princes* into a pre-*puputan* court anatomy, with *subak* anti-Wittfogel and port-of-trade chapters that keep economic domains only loosely under court command. The notes apparatus is half the scholarly project (source criticism, colonial feudalization irony). Contested reception centers on idealism vs material domination; keep claims attributed and case-scoped. Completes the vault’s primary Geertz Bali set for religion/economy (*Peddlers*), ritual text (*Deep Play*), and polity (*Negara*); still outstanding: *Works and Lives*, *Social History of an Indonesian Town*, *Kinship in Bali*.
