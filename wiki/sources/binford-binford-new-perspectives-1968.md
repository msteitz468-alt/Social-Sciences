# New Perspectives in Archeology (Binford & Binford, eds., 1968)

Edited volume that served as a public charter for **[[processual-archaeology|processual archaeology / the New Archaeology]]**: explicit theory, hypothesis-testing, social organization from material remains, and analysis of variability within and among sites. Grew from a 1965 AAA symposium organized by [[binford-lewis|Lewis R. Binford]] and [[binford-sally-r|Sally R. Binford]]. L.R. Binford's **Archeological Perspectives** states the program against culture-historical limits; **Post-Pleistocene Adaptations** reframes Mesolithic/Neolithic change in systemic adaptive terms. Case studies (Longacre, Hill, Deetz, Flannery & Coe, Struever, et al.) operationalize social inference.

## Claims Extracted

### Program (Preface + L.R. Binford, Archeological Perspectives)
- 1965 AAA Denver symposium; departure from conventional studies; dialogue with cultural anthropologists.
- Three aims of archaeology: culture history, lifeways, **cultural process** — with despair over the third.
- Traditional method inadequate: cannot "read" the record by a priori principles; facts do not speak for themselves; remains as data for **testing propositions**.
- Culture as non-additive **system**; multivariate attributes; style vs function.
- Social organization in principle recoverable; limits are methodological naiveté, not the record alone.
- Shared anthropological aim: explain differences and similarities among cultural systems; **arguments of relevance** link observations to past systems.
- "New archaeology" as structural change in the field's theoretical practice (volume framing).

### Part I other papers (attributed per author)
- **Spaulding:** explanation, covering-law style monism; archaeology and anthropology interrelation.
- **Deetz:** residence/descent inference from ceramic attribute patterning; levels of behavior.
- **S.R. Binford:** Mousterian variability — functional tool-kits vs culture-historical *fossiles directeurs*; adaptive MP→UP model.
- **Sackett:** Upper Paleolithic method/theory; critique of fossile model; systemic goals.

### Part II — single occupation (attributed)
- **Longacre (Carter Ranch):** ceramic design distributions as residence/social units under systemic culture model.
- **Hill (Broken K):** hypothesis tests on room function; multi-independent tests.
- **Cowgill (Teotihuacan):** computer clustering of surface data.
- **Longacre & Ayres (Apache wickiup):** ethnoarchaeological lesson — material structure to activity.
- **Williams (Birhor):** ethnographic control on settlement heterogeneity.

### Part III — among units (attributed)
- **Winters:** Late Archaic trade/value systems (Indian Knoll).
- **Whallon:** ceramic style and matrilocality/interaction (NY Owasco–Iroquois).
- **Hole:** Deh Luran social inference 8000–4000 B.C.
- **Flannery & Coe:** Formative Mesoamerica microenvironments; 1968 postscript retracts redistribution→stratification link.
- **Struever:** Illinois Valley Woodland subsistence-settlement; Hopewell.

### Post-Pleistocene + Discussion
- **L.R. Binford:** critique of Mesolithic systematics and environmental stimulus–response / Childe oasis / Braidwood nuclear-zone explanations; alternative demographic–ecological adaptive model of multi-origin agriculture (attributed 1968).
- **Discussants:** Lee, DeVore, Fried, Dozier, Aberle, Harris — feedback from social anthropology (endorse dialogue; caution on over-inference, descent reconstruction, etc.).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*New Perspectives* is the vault's **processual charter collection**: it turns Taylor's conjunctive critique and W&P's "archaeology as anthropology" into a multi-author demonstration that social and adaptive questions are researchable with explicit theory and tests. Relative to ingested sources: challenges [[culture-historical-archaeology]] trait-list and *fossile directeur* practice; extends [[conjunctive-approach|Taylor]] toward systemic process explanation; densifies [[binford-lewis]] beyond the *Man the Hunter* analogy critique; reframes [[neolithic-revolution]] debates in adaptive-systems language without deleting Childe's problem.
