# The Emergence of Civilisation (Renfrew, 1972)

## Bibliographic Record

Colin Renfrew, *The Emergence of Civilisation: The Cyclades and the Aegean in the Third Millennium BC* (London: Methuen, 1972; this ingest: Oxbow Books paperback 2011, ISBN 978-0-97740-946-4, with 2010 Preface and Introduction by Renfrew; Foreword by John Cherry). Author: [[renfrew-colin]]. Dedicated to the memory of [[childe-v-gordon|V. Gordon Childe]].

Programmatic British [[processual-archaeology|processual]] synthesis: Aegean (especially Cycladic) Early Bronze Age culture *sequence* (Part I) plus culture *process* via interacting subsystems and the [[multiplier-effect]] (Part II). Argues **local autonomy** of Minoan–Mycenaean origins against diffusion/migration from the Near East. ## Key Claims Extracted

**Split: THESES (Renfrew) vs reported FINDINGS as he states them.** Nothing below is wiki-voice adjudication of 1972 chronology or population figures.

### THESES (quarantine / attributed)

1. **Autonomy:** Aegean civilisation was not brought ready-made by migration or diffusion; essential change is endogenous third-millennium process (Ch. 21).
2. **Third millennium formative:** foundations of Minoan–Mycenaean palace societies (ranking, polyculture, metallurgy, ships, trade, burial/religion) laid in EBA out of Neolithic communities.
3. **Multiplier effect:** take-off when positive feedback couples ≥2 culture subsystems (subsistence, craft, social, symbolic/projective, trade); single prime movers (irrigation, metallurgy, redistribution, population alone) insufficient.
4. **Mediterranean polyculture:** early third-millennium olive/vine (with cereals) enables hill-slope micro-environments, surplus, redistribution, craft nuclei — palace “in microcosm.”
5. **Anti–Ex Oriente lux for Aegean process:** chronological priority of Egypt/Sumer does not prove culture-process dependence; third-millennium East Mediterranean contacts too sparse to explain palace emergence; intra-Aegean contacts more important.
6. **Migration theory untenable** as origin of civilisation formula (limited movements allowed; Lefkandi/Kastri may mark small NW Anatolian groups only).
7. **Civilisation** as polythetic / multi-dimensional artificial environment (not reducible to city or writing alone).
8. **Trade** favours innovation (including local innovation under trade conditions); third-millennium commercial colonial scale rejected; four external-trade models; Melian obsidian vs Spondylus prestige-chain contrast.
9. **Local metallurgy** with Neolithic antecedents; EB2 boom not simple Anatolian import package.
10. **2010 softens** Ch. 14 densities, simple redistribution, and ‘chiefdom’ label while holding polyculture, EBA economic foundations, and multiplier/systems frame.

### FINDINGS / DESCRIPTIVE (as Renfrew reports — date-stamped 1972)

- Cycladic culture sequence: [[grotta-pelos-culture]] → [[keros-syros-culture]] → [[phylakopi-i-culture]] via Phylakopi stratigraphy + cemetery seriation.
- Diagnostics: Grotta-Pelos cists/burnished wares/Plastiras figurines; Keros-Syros sauceboats/folded-arm figurines/Syros graves; Phylakopi I duck vase/Melian bowl/kernos.
- Melian obsidian dominates Aegean Neolithic (trace-element); direct-access model when Melos uninhabited.
- EB2 “international spirit”; Lerna House of the Tiles sealings; fortifications (mainland/Cyclades; Crete exception).
- Absolute framework ~3200–2100 BC for third millennium (1972 calibration horizon).
- Appendix 1 Cycladic site gazetteer to summer 1969.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Emergence* is the wiki’s flagship **British processual case study of civilisation origins**: culture-historical densification of the Cycladic EBA (still foundational nomenclature) coupled to a systems model that relocates explanation from Oriental irradiation to **subsystem coupling**. Read together with *[[renfrew-before-civilization-1973|Before Civilization]]* (radiocarbon autonomy of Europe) and *[[renfrew-archaeology-and-language-1987|Archaeology and Language]]* (Anatolian IE hypothesis): same author, different load-bearing claims — do not collapse into one “Renfrew package.” Pair culture-sequence pages with later specialist synthesis (Broodbank, Doumas, intensive survey) before treating 1972 population figures as current. The [[multiplier-effect]] and [[mediterranean-polyculture]] concepts are the portable theoretical apparatus; the three Cycladic cultures are the portable culture-history apparatus.
