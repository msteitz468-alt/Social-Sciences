# Settling the Earth: The Archaeology of Deep Human History (Gamble, 2013)

[[gamble-clive|Clive Gamble]]'s major 2013 synthesis of **deep human history** organized around **six [[terrae|Terrae]]** (homonym worlds of settlement) and the **evolution of human imagination** — the capacity to **[[going-beyond|go beyond]]** co-presence and to build societies where people **lived apart yet stayed in touch**. Succeeds and densifies the colonization frame of *[[gamble-timewalkers-1994|Timewalkers]]* (1994) and the network social archaeology of *[[gamble-palaeolithic-societies-europe-1999|The Palaeolithic Societies of Europe]]* (1999) with a fuller integration of Quaternary climate drivers, archaeogenetics, and cognitive / social-brain models (as of 2013).

## Core Argument (attributed, Gamble 2013)

1. Prefer **deep history** over "prehistory" — everything about the human past is history (attributed).
2. Organize ~10 Ma of hominin settlement into **six Terrae** whose boundaries are drawn by geography and social reach, not only by skull shape or tool mode (attributed; Table 1.1 / Figure 1.1).
3. Two growth measures — **encephalisation** and **global settlement** — are **not** in lockstep: major brain growth 2 Ma–200 ka with technology and range often stagnant; Renfrew's **[[sapient-paradox|sapient paradox]]** as second disconnect (attributed).
4. Global settlement required the imaginative ability to **go beyond** co-presence and to **[[kinshipping|kinship]]** people outside the genetic pool — societies of people living apart yet staying in touch (attributed).
5. Social archaeology of deep time is read through **FACE** (Fragmentation, Accumulation, Consumption, Enchainment) and **instruments vs containers** technology (attributed glossary / ch. 1).
6. Climate and tectonics are drivers and frames (variability selection, tectonic trail, bio-tidal Old World); archaeogenetic phylogeography is a veneer that must be handled carefully for deep time (attributed, chs 2–3).
