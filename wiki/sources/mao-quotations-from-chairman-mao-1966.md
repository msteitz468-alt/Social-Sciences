# Quotations from Chairman Mao Tse-tung (1966)

*Quotations from Chairman Mao Tse-tung* — internationally known as the **Little Red Book** — is a pocket anthology of excerpts from [[mao-zedong|Mao Zedong]]'s writings and speeches, published by the Foreign Languages Press, Peking, and mass-distributed in China and abroad in the mid-1960s–1970s. It is not an empirical study, not a systematic treatise, and not a scholarly edition. It is a **political catechism**: selected maxims organized for memorization, study sessions, and application "with specific problems in mind" (Lin Biao foreword).

This wiki treats it in **artifact mode**. The book is evidence for (a) how Maoist doctrine was packaged for mass political education, (b) which theses the Cultural Revolution–era apparatus elevated as core, and (c) the rhetorical form of personality-cult ideology. It is **not** independent evidence that those theses are true.

Lin Biao's foreword asserts that "Comrade Mao Tse-tung is the greatest Marxist-Leninist of our era," that Mao Tse-tung's thought is "Marxism-Leninism of the era in which imperialism is heading for total collapse," and that once grasped by the masses it becomes a "spiritual atom bomb of infinite power." That is **Lin Biao's (1966) position** — recorded as cult-era framing, not as wiki evaluation.

## FACTS / THESES / QUOTES split (ingest discipline)

**FACTS (descriptive, wiki-voice OK):** publication history; chapter structure; that billions of copies were produced and the red pocket format became an international icon; that quotations are drawn from named Mao texts with FLP page refs; that Lin Biao signed the Dec 1966 foreword.

**THESES (always attributed):** all doctrinal content — Party leadership, permanent class struggle under socialism, mass line, people's war, paper-tiger strategy, practice-first epistemology, peasantry as historical motor in Chinese feudal society, etc. Quarantined on theory/concept/debate pages.

**QUOTES (load-bearing samples, with anchors):** retained on those pages with chapter or source-title attribution as printed in the anthology.

## Reliability and register

Treat every political, historical, and philosophical claim as **Mao's (or Lin Biao's) thesis as packaged in 1964–66**. Cross-check against full *Selected Works* texts and against non-Maoist historiography before promoting anything toward wiki voice. The anthology's function was to **arm minds**, not to report findings. Social-scientific use: ideology and political culture; comparative revolutionary doctrine; peasant-revolution theory (as program, not as ethnography); the sociology of political education and cult production.
