## Argument Summary

Scott generalizes the *Weapons of the Weak* triangulation method into a comparative theory of discourse under domination. Core moves (all attributed):

1. **The transcript pair.** The [[public-transcript]] is the open, coerced joint performance between dominant and subordinate; the [[hidden-transcript]] is site- and audience-specific offstage discourse on both sides. Neither is simply "true" — their discrepancy measures domination's grip on public discourse.
2. **Domination as theater.** Elites must perform their own legitimacy (affirmation, concealment, euphemization, stigmatization, unanimity); subordinates perform deference that licenses no inference of belief (Newby, Patterson, Goffman, Hochschild mobilized).
3. **Against hegemony.** Chapter 4 distinguishes thick (consent) from thin (naturalized inevitability) theories of [[hegemony]]/[[false-consciousness]] and rejects both; genuine hegemony requires prospective mobility or total atomization, conditions nearly never met. Gramsci inverted: subordinates freer in thought, more constrained in action. Filed at [[hegemony-resistance-debate]].
4. **Sites and disguise.** The hidden transcript requires sequestered social sites and carriers; it reaches the public stage disguised — euphemism, grumbling, gossip, rumor, spirit possession, trickster tales (Brer Rabbit), world-upside-down prints, carnival. Against the Gluckman/Turner safety-valve reading, Scott argues ritual reversal is contested terrain elites tried to suppress, and revolts repeatedly erupted from it (Romans, 1580).
5. **[[infrapolitics|Infrapolitics]].** Material, status, and ideological domination each pair open resistance with a disguised counterpart; aggregated petty acts have macro effects; infrapolitics is "real politics," the elementary form of political life.
6. **Rupture.** The first public declaration of the hidden transcript (Mrs. Poyser; Douglass; Lagos 1988; Ceaușescu's last rally 1989) is electric, irrevocable, and personally authenticating; [[charisma]] is reframed as recognition of a collectively rehearsed text, explaining why elites and social scientists are surprised by sudden mass defiance.
