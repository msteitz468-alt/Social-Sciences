# Order and Rebellion in Tribal Africa (Gluckman 1963)

Collected essays of [[gluckman-max|Max Gluckman]] with a long autobiographical **Introduction** (1962) reviewing his formation, Zululand and Barotseland fieldwork, Rhodes-Livingstone Institute, and responses to critics of the conflict-and-cohesion programme (including partial engagement with [[leach-edmund|Leach]] on equilibrium). Reprints the Frazer Lecture on **[[ritual-of-rebellion]]**, Bemba succession, village headman / [[intercalary-role]], Barotse **[[reasonable-man]]**, and three Malinowski critiques.

Companion densification beyond *[[gluckman-custom-and-conflict-1955|Custom and Conflict]]* and *[[gluckman-politics-law-ritual-1965|Politics, Law and Ritual]]*.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Order and Rebellion* is the **essay archive of Gluckman’s middle career**: the Introduction is the densest single autobiographical source for training (Wits → Zululand → RLI → Manchester), the APS Zulu chapter’s “rebels not revolutionaries” thesis, the Bemba predictive succession exercise, and the defence of [[ritual-of-rebellion]] against Wilson, Gibson, and psychological “catharsis” readings. The volume binds Manchester conflict-and-cohesion to concrete case-work (headman, chief/NC, Barotse courts) and ends with a sustained critique of [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]]’s culture-contact / social-change programme (one-entry field; three cultural realities) — load-bearing for [[functionalism-and-social-change-debate]]. Relative to *Custom and Conflict*, it supplies the **primary Frazer Lecture text** and the intercalary / reasonable-man essays; relative to *Politics, Law and Ritual*, it is denser on intellectual genealogy and Malinowski, lighter as textbook synthesis. Partial Leach engagement in the Introduction (accepts some oscillation analogy; keeps equilibrium vocabulary) is the volume’s bridge to later debate recorded on the Leach–Gluckman equilibrium line.
