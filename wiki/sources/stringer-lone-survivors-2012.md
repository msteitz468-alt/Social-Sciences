# Lone Survivors (Stringer, 2012)

[[stringer-chris|Christopher Stringer]]'s single-author synthesis of modern human origins — fossil, archaeological, and genetic — written after three decades as a lead advocate of **[[recent-african-origin|Recent African Origin]]**. Updates and softens pure-replacement formulations in light of early nuclear aDNA (Neanderthal and Denisovan admixture, as of 2010–2012) while retaining African primary origin of **[[anatomically-modern-humans|anatomically modern humans]]**. Central to [[modern-human-origins-debate]]; densifies [[neanderthals]], [[denisovans]], [[behavioral-modernity]], and related dating/genetics methods.

## Core Argument (attributed, Stringer 2012)

1. **African primacy retained.** Anatomically modern *Homo sapiens* originated in Africa; living non-Africans are essentially a subset of African genetic variation, with serial founder decline of diversity with distance from Africa.
2. **Classic RAO updated, not abandoned.** Pure total-replacement genetics of the 1980s–90s is revised by nuclear aDNA: non-Africans carry ~2% Neanderthal ancestry (higher at some loci); Melanesians add Denisovan ancestry (~5%; ~8% total archaic as of 2012 figures). Stringer labels modest archaic input “mostly Out of Africa,” still RAO when under ~10% of the genome — near Bräuer hybridization / modest Assimilation, not classic Multiregionalism.
3. **Anatomy ≠ behaviour; modernity is composite.** Early African and Levantine moderns can look modern without the full later behavioural package; MSA symbolism (e.g. Blombos) and gradualist vs late-switch (Klein) accounts remain live; “modernity” was assembled at multiple times and places, not a single Garden of Eden event.
4. **Archaic competence revised upward.** Hunting, fire, care, pigments, and limited marine use among Neanderthals and earlier *Homo* are acknowledged more than in 1993 hard-replacement rhetoric; sophisticated language still tied to early modern African social complexity.
5. **Regional (“racial”) features are late.** Shared species morphology first; regional traits largely drift (with selection exceptions); not deep multiregional inheritance from local *erectus*.
6. **Other humans and sole survival.** Neanderthals, Denisovans, and possibly *Homo floresiensis* document a multi-human Pleistocene; *H. sapiens* as “lone survivors” is contingent, not teleological. Human evolution continues.
