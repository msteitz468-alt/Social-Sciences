# The Theory of the Leisure Class (Veblen, 1899)

Thorstein Veblen's *The Theory of the Leisure Class: An Economic Study of Institutions*
(New York: Macmillan, 1899) — the founding text of American [[institutional-economics|institutional economics]]
and the origin of the concepts [[conspicuous-consumption]], [[conspicuous-leisure]], and
[[conspicuous-waste]]. Ingested from the Duke Classics 2012 e-text of the first edition.

## What the book is

A satirical-analytic account of how a [[leisure-class|leisure class]] arises and how the
motive of [[pecuniary-emulation|pecuniary emulation]] reshapes consumption, taste, dress,
sport, religion, and learning across an entire society. Veblen treats economic life as an
evolution of **institutions** — settled *habits of thought* inherited from earlier cultural
stages — and argues that the modern leisure class conserves archaic, "predatory" habits that
retard industrial and scientific adaptation. Method is evolutionary and comparative:
Veblen reasons by analogy from "barbarian" and "savage" culture to Gilded-Age America. That
analogy is a hypothesis-generator in his hands, not evidence, and is flagged as such on the
pages it feeds.

## Structure

Fourteen chapters in two movements. Chapters 1–7 build the core apparatus — the origin of
the leisure class, pecuniary emulation, conspicuous leisure, conspicuous consumption, the
pecuniary standard of living, the pecuniary canons of taste, and dress. Chapters 8–14 turn
to the *dynamics and survivals*: industrial exemption and conservatism, the conservation of
archaic traits, modern survivals of prowess (sport), the belief in luck, devout observances
(religion), the non-invidious interests (workmanship, the parental bent, idle curiosity),
and the higher learning as an expression of pecuniary culture.

## Reliability and voice

Every claim here is Veblen's theoretical position, rendered in **attributed voice**. Two
standing cautions travel with the material:

1. **Ethnographic analogy, not evidence.** Veblen's "barbarian/savage" stages are an
   armchair evolutionary scheme of 1899; the wiki records his claims about them as his
   analogical reasoning, never as ethnographic fact.
2. **Dated race-science.** The ch. 9 ethnic-type typology is reproduced as an attributed,
   date-stamped ("Veblen 1899") historical position and flagged as superseded race theory.
