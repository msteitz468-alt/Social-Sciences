# Democracy in America (Tocqueville, 1835/1840)

Alexis de Tocqueville's two-volume comparative analysis of American democracy,
based on his 1831–32 tour of the United States with Gustave de Beaumont. Volume 1
(1835) analyzes American political institutions and the sovereignty of the people;
Volume 2 (1840) turns to the effects of the *equality of social conditions* on
American thought, feeling, mores, and political life. Written from a French
vantage — Tocqueville reads America to understand the democratic future he believed
was providentially advancing across the Christian world. This ingest uses the
Penguin Classics edition (Bevan translation, Kramnick introduction), which also
carries the *Two Essays on America*.
