# Social Theory and Archaeology (Shanks & Tilley, 1987)

Programmatic charter for a **critical social archaeology**: theory as practice (not methodology); critique of processual social archaeology's **[[logic-of-contingency|logic of necessity]]** and proposal of a **logic of contingency**; material culture as active **signifying practice**; reconception of archaeological time against abstract chronology; rejection of evolutionary stage models and systems prime-movers; archaeology as present **political and moral practice**. Co-authors [[shanks-michael|Michael Shanks]] and [[tilley-christopher|Christopher Tilley]]. Frame: [[post-processual-archaeology]]; debate [[processual-postprocessual-debate]]. Companion volume: *[[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987|Re-Constructing Archaeology]]* (1987/1992) ✅. No `studies/` page (theoretical-work, not empirical landmark). Theory Hub densification: [[hubs/theory/post-processual-archaeology]].

### Programmatic claims (Preface–Ch.1)
- After 150 years of empiricism, only rudimentary understanding of the archaeological past (S&T 1987).
- "No amount of excavation, survey, ethnoarchaeological work or so-called 'middle-range' empiricism will cure the perceived fundamental isolation of past from present" — need conceptual tools to reinscribe past into present.
- Positivism and functionalism as twin pillars of 'new' archaeology; archaeology still deeply empiricist and antitheoretical.
- All archaeology ought to be **cultural critique** — practice produced in and contributing to the present; archaeological discourse is a form of power and study of power.
- Reject unified all-encompassing theoretical framework; Cambridge 1986 seminars; comments from Giddens, Hodder, Rowlands.
- David Clarke's metaphysics/epistemology/logic + predepositional→interpretative levels summarized then displaced by metatheoretical questions about what theory *is*.
- Rationalization since 1960s reduced largely to **methodology** (detective/therapist images; method as pharmacopoeia); theory must not be reduced to method.
- Theory (*theoria*) is reflection, critique, performance — not separate from practice; past and present mediated in archaeological practice; meaning deferred; interpretation opens difference rather than closing a complete past.

### Social archaeology critique and contingency (Ch.2)
- Traditional narrative archaeology (Piggott, Burgess as read by S&T): common-sense categories, anonymous societies, individual occluded.
- Processual systems social archaeology (Renfrew, variables, boxes-and-arrows, adaptation, mortuary role theory, middle-range predicting deposition without social process): **13-point summary** of shared problems (reified variables, functionalism, occlusion of individual, statics/dynamics split, neo-evolutionary facilitation).
- Via Laclau & Mouffe (1985): two assumptions = **logic of necessity** — (1) social as intelligible totality; (2) history has rational substratum.
- **[[logic-of-contingency]]**: refuse reductionist/essentialist social logics; no hierarchy of determination; primacy of the political; social order as open field of relations; archaeology as discursive mediation of past and present.

### Individual, practice, ideology (Ch.3)
- Mauss on variable category of the person; naming ≠ autonomous ego; capitalist subject historically specific.
- Practice and structure (Giddens structuration as S&T use it); power as effects/resources/interests not only ranking typology.
- Ideology as signifying practice constituting subjects to **reproduce** rather than transform the social totality (Althusserian framing as adapted); dual character; not mere false consciousness.
- Subject as **trace** within social field; material culture as primary object tackled as signifying practice.

### Material culture (Ch.4)
- Traditional type/culture/cognition and new-archaeology style/function packages both inadequate for specificity of form.
- Function nearly redundant as explanation of particular designs; style not passive ethnic reflection.
- Material culture as **symbolic order in practice** — resources drawn on in political relations and ideology; actively involved in the social world.
- Translation of the past, not recovery of original meaning; past speaks in signifying residues; archaeologist articulates in present speech.

### Time and social change (Chs.5–6)
- Critique of chronology as abstract/spatialized time; Bailey dualities; capitalism's chronometry; "Choosing a past, that is constituting a past, is choosing a future."
- Systems homeostasis and prime-movers (population, exchange, environment) fail to explain particularity.
- Neo-evolutionary stage models (band/tribe/chiefdom/state) ethnocentric, essentialist, progress-laden.
- Structural Marxism / Friedman–Rowlands epigenetic models discussed and critiqued; beyond evolution toward contingent historical conjunctures, contradiction, ideology.
- **Stability rather than social change needs explaining** (Appendix 5.1; Ch.6 theme).

### Politics of theory (Ch.7) and Appendix theses
- Critically self-conscious archaeology; WAC controversy framed as exposing exterior politics vs neutrality ideology.
- Gero & Root on *National Geographic*; Leone on Shaker museum; archaeology as cultural capital of elite.
- No neutral algorithm for evaluating archaeologies; validity hinges on intention, interest, politics, morality.
- Archaeology is nothing if it is not **critique**; value-committed; argue *through* the medium of the past to detach power of truth from present social order.
- Appendix formalizes six domains: archaeology and theory; social and individual; material culture; time; social change; form and politics of theory — including call for archaeological **poetics**/rhetoric of how the past is written.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Social Theory and Archaeology* is a vault **primary charter** for the critical/theoretical wing of [[post-processual-archaeology]], complementary to Hodder's later **field-method** charter (*[[hodder-archaeological-process-1999|The Archaeological Process]]* 1999) and to Tilley's later **landscape phenomenology** (*[[tilley-phenomenology-of-landscape-1994|A Phenomenology of Landscape]]* 1994). Where Hodder 1999 densifies recursive excavation practice, S&T 1987 densifies the **metatheoretical and political** program: theory-as-practice, contingency vs necessity, material culture as signifying practice, anti-evolutionism, and archaeology as moral/political writing of the past. Companion *[[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987|Re-Constructing Archaeology]]* (ingested 2026-07-10) densifies practice-facing critique (positivism, value-freedom, museums, fourfold hermeneutic, style/beer-can analyses).
