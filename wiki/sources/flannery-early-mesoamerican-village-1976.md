# The Early Mesoamerican Village (Flannery, ed., 1976)

Edited volume that became the **charter for multi-scalar / household archaeology** in Formative [[mesoamerica|Mesoamerica]]: analysis from activity area and house through village, valley catchment and settlement system, to interregional exchange and ritual networks. Editor **[[flannery-kent-v|Kent V. Flannery]]** frames the program against "telephone booth" trenching and normative trait-list generalization; contributing authors operationalize sampling, household clusters, site catchment, nearest-neighbor analysis, stylistic interaction, and exchange.

## Claims Extracted

### Ch. 1 — Research strategy (Flannery)
- Scope: Formative villages, southern Mexico + western Guatemala; ~**1500–500 B.C.**
- Permanent wattle-and-daub villages become widespread; “primary village farming communities” make [[mesoamerica]] definable as culture area (attributed Flannery).
- Credibility gap: goals (settlement survey, ecology, social organization, population, trade) vs practice (telephone-booth pits, arbitrary levels, mound-fill seriation).
- Parable: R.M.A. / G.S. / S.G.S. as composite of real practices.
- Program debt to [[binford-lewis]] (regional + probability sampling) and Struever (subsistence-settlement, population, social structure).
- **Multi-scalar units:** activity area → house → [[household-cluster]] → courtyard group → residential ward → village → maximum subsistence-settlement unit (valley) → interregional exchange and ritual networks.
- Critique of normative “typical site/phase” framework; variation as information.
- Dual goals: procedures at each scale + model of Early Formative society.

### Ch. 2 — Household level
- Early Formative house: rectangular wattle-and-daub, door on long side; comparative plans Oaxaca, Tehuacán, Valley of Mexico, Gulf, Guatemala Pacific.
- **Winter:** household cluster = house + associated pits, burials, middens, features; Tierras Largas type cases.
- Flannery & Winter: activity areas; universal vs specialist vs regional activities; hypothesized male/female work areas.
- Guatemalan Pacific Coast household cluster comparison (Salinas La Blanca / Cuadros).

### Ch. 3 — Community level
- Shallow small sites enable horizontal village “anatomy.”
- Intensive surface collection cases (incl. [[san-jose-mogote]]).
- **Winter / [[tierras-largas]]:** ~3.7% random 2×2 m quadrats; first probability-sample Formative village maps; ~8–12 household clusters.
- Deep sites: transect samples / fortuitous sections (limits noted).
- Courtyard groups and residential wards (Flannery); Whalen zoning at Tomaltepec (status zones + cemetery).
- **Marcus:** literature survey of village sizes; **momentary population** vs phase-total houses; modal small hamlets dominate; upper size limits discussed regionally.

### Ch. 4 — Catchment ([[site-catchment-analysis]])
- Vita-Finzi & Higgs lineage adapted; Flannery critiques circular weighting vs maize productivity.
- **Rossmann:** San Lorenzo / Potrero Nuevo / Tenochtitlán catchments.
- **Flannery:** empirical Oaxaca ([[san-jose-mogote]], Fábrica San José) + Tehuacán (Las Canoas; Coxcatlán seasonal annex).
- **Zarky:** statistical Ocós catchments (small radii; estuary/salt selection).

### Ch. 5 — Regional sampling
- **Plog:** efficiency of simple random / stratified / systematic / stratified systematic unaligned × quadrats vs transects on Oaxaca survey blocks (Valdeflores, Zaachila, Etla).
- **Flannery:** “The Trouble with Regional Sampling” — jungle, site-size pyramid, dual survey strategies; temper on pure designs.

### Ch. 6–7 — Regional systems
- Settlement **system** vs **pattern** distinction (Flannery).
- Linear stream / riverside settlement rules; opposite-bank neighbors; Etla social spacing ~5 km (attributed).
- Reynolds double-dependent Markov models (Grijalva).
- **Earle:** nearest-neighbor analysis — Valley of Mexico hierarchical aggregation; Gulf Coast / Olmec major-center regular spacing; chiefdom-scale territorial model (attributed).

### Ch. 8 — Growth
- **Winter:** [[tierras-largas]] stable ~8–12 households across phases vs [[san-jose-mogote]] ~10–15× growth; nearly half valley growth 1500–500 BCE from SJM; modal hamlet size; late changes not local population pressure.
- **Brumfiel:** eastern Valley of Mexico test — population pressure should be demonstrated not assumed; TF pressure better as **effect** of state than cause via growth (attributed).

### Ch. 9 — Style / interaction
- **Plog:** gravity model and design-element coefficients (BR, Pearson’s *r*); expected distance/interaction correlations often fail; design similarity **cannot be assumed** to measure interaction; caution for Deetz–Longacre-type inferences.
- **Pyne:** 595 vessels, fire-serpent vs were-jaguar motifs; non-random by household/residential area; descent-group hypothesis; “deity” label contested (Marcus).

### Ch. 10 — Exchange
- Reciprocity, redistribution, pooling models for Formative commodities.
- **Pires-Ferreira:** obsidian NAA spheres (Guadalupe Victoria, El Chayal, Barranca, Zinapécuaro); San Lorenzo–Oaxaca links and breakdowns.
- Winter & Pires-Ferreira: household-level obsidian at Tierras Largas vs San José Mogote (pooling vs individual access — as reported in volume).
- Shell networks; iron-ore mirrors via Mössbauer groups (Oaxaca sources; exports to San Lorenzo, La Venta, Morelos, Nochixtlán).

### Ch. 11 — Religious networks
- **Flannery:** contextual analysis of ritual paraphernalia Formative Oaxaca; platforms; exotics (stingray spines, shark teeth, shell, mirrors).
- **Drennan:** ultimate sacred propositions + rituals of sanctification (Rappaport); sedentism/ranking increase sanctification demand; Olmec-style symbols as sanctification technology under internal social evolution (hypothesis); San Lorenzo end as possible desanctification spiral not mere natural catastrophe (hypothesis).

### Ch. 12 — Conclusion (Flannery)
- Closes parable; self-critique (all three characters in every archaeologist); program summary as “prayer for an endangered species” (careful multi-scalar village archaeology).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Early Mesoamerican Village* is this vault’s **household- and multi-scalar archaeology charter for Formative Mesoamerica**. Relative to already-ingested sources: it operationalizes [[processual-archaeology]] at village scale the way [[binford-binford-new-perspectives-1968]] does as symposium manifesto; it densifies [[flannery-kent-v]] and [[marcus-joyce]] from textbook mentions to primary program text; it supplies the foundational cases for [[household-archaeology]], [[site-catchment-analysis]], and probability-based [[archaeological-survey]] / [[settlement-pattern-archaeology]] in the New World Formative. Load-bearing attributed results include Winter’s household cluster and Tierras Largas sample; Marcus’s momentary-population caution; Plog’s negative results on style-as-interaction; Brumfiel’s pressure-as-effect inversion; Pires-Ferreira’s exchange networks; Drennan’s sanctification reading of Olmec-style spread. Empirical figures are **1976-state** and date-stamped; later Oaxaca monographs and archaeometry refine numbers without erasing the program architecture.
