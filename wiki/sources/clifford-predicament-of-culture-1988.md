# Clifford — *The Predicament of Culture* (1988)

Essay collection (chapters written 1979–1986) treating ethnography as a historically
situated form of writing and collecting. Companion volume to [[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]:
where that collection was a manifesto, this is [[clifford-james|Clifford]]'s own
historical-critical program worked through cases — [[griaule-marcel|Griaule]],
[[leiris-michel|Leiris]], [[segalen-victor|Segalen]], [[cesaire-aime|Césaire]],
the MoMA "Primitivism" show, [[said-edward|Said]]'s *Orientalism*, and the Mashpee trial.

## Volume Synthesis Note

The book is [[clifford-james|Clifford]]'s own historical-critical program: where
*Writing Culture* was a collective manifesto, *Predicament* argues through cases that
ethnographic authority, the culture concept, and "authenticity" are historical
constructions — and that identity is conjunctural, hybrid, and invented without being
false. Its four-mode authority scheme (ch. 1) is now the wiki's fullest statement of
[[ethnographic-authority]]; its Paris chapters give the vault its first coverage of the
French interwar ethnographic milieu (Mauss's Institut, Dakar–Djibouti, *Documents*,
Musée de l'Homme); chs. 9–10 charter the [[art-culture-system]] and the critique of
[[salvage-ethnography]]; ch. 12 supplies the Mashpee case now anchoring
[[mashpee-tribal-identity-debate]]. Tension with already-ingested sources: Clifford's
"saving fiction" reading of *Argonauts* sharpens (without contradicting) the
Malinowski study pages; his Said critique adds the anthropological counter-position to
the [[orientalism]] cluster. Everything Clifford asserts about ethnography is itself a
Writing Culture-era position — kept in attributed voice throughout.
