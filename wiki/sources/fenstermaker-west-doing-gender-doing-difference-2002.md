## Core Contribution

In **attributed voice**: West and Zimmerman argued that gender is not a role, a trait, or an optional "display," but a routine, methodical, recurring *accomplishment* — "an emergent feature of social situations: both as an outcome of and a rationale for various social arrangements and as a means of legitimating one of the most fundamental divisions of society." They distinguished **sex** (socially agreed biological criteria), **[[sex-category|sex category]]** (everyday membership via identificatory displays), and **gender** (managing conduct in light of normative conceptions for one's sex category), tying the last to Garfinkelian **[[accountability]]**: to do gender is to engage in behavior *at the risk of gender assessment*, and under the omnirelevance of sex category doing gender is unavoidable. West and Fenstermaker later proposed that race and class are accomplished through analogous mechanisms ("[[doing-difference]]"), a claim the 1995 *Gender & Society* symposium contested — especially on power, history, and the simultaneity of oppressions (see [[doing-difference-debate]]).
