# Partial Connections

[[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]'s *Partial Connections* (1991; AltaMira updated edition 2004/5) is a short methodological–theoretical monograph on **scale, complexity, and comparison** after the reflexive / [[writing-culture|Writing Culture]] moment. It develops the image of **[[partial-connections|partial connections]]** (parts that relate without composing a single whole), pairs it with [[haraway-donna|Donna Haraway]]'s cyborg and Cantor's dust / fractal imagery, and reopens cross-cultural comparison for Melanesia without restoring holist totalities. Study: [[strathern-partial-connections-study]]. Companion prior ingest: *[[strathern-gender-of-the-gift-1988|The Gender of the Gift]]* (1988).

Edition ingested: AltaMira Press (Rowman & Littlefield), Updated Edition, 2005 printing; original 1991. Main text dated Manchester 1987/1989 acknowledgements; 2004 Preface reflects on form, amplitude/excess of information, and reception.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Partial Connections* is Strathern’s compact method manifesto between *The Gender of the Gift* (1988) and *After Nature* (1992). Where GOG rebuilds Melanesian sociality (dividual, gift/commodity, gender of analysis), PC rebuilds **how anthropology can still compare** after the Writing Culture dismantling of representation and of autonomous comparative constructs. The load-bearing move is shared imagery across writing and comparison: **scale/proportion**, **remainder/cut**, **[[partial-connections]]**, **[[postplural]]** perception, Haraway’s cyborg, Cantor’s dust, and Melanesian self-scaling images (trees, flutes, figure–ground). Wagner’s [[fractal-person]] is reciprocal provocation, not mere citation.
