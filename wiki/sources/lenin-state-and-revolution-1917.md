# The State and Revolution (Lenin, 1917/1918)

V. I. [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]], *The State and Revolution: The Marxist Theory of the State and the Tasks of the Proletariat in the Revolution* (written August–September 1917; first edition 1918; second edition preface 17 Dec 1918). Ingested from the 2014 Haymarket annotated English edition (Todd Chretien).

## What the text is

A short theoretical–political pamphlet reconstructing what Lenin presents as Marx and Engels’s theory of the [[the-state|state]] and of the [[proletariat|proletariat]]’s tasks toward state power. It is not a study and reports no original data. Method (Lenin’s own statement): re-establish “what Marx really taught on the subject of the state” by long quotations so that “Kautskyism” can be documentarily exposed. Everything drawn onto this wiki is **attributed** to Lenin (or to Marx/Engels as he quotes them), never stated in wiki voice as established finding.

## Core argument (attributed summary)

Lenin holds that:

1. The state is a product of the irreconcilability of [[class-struggle|class antagonisms]] (via Engels, *Origin*), not a neutral reconciler of classes — and that “petit-bourgeois” and Kautskyite distortions either deny the irreconcilability or concede class rule while omitting the need to **destroy** the state apparatus.
2. The distinctive means of the state are “**special bodies of armed men**” (Lenin’s synthesis of Engels), plus prisons, bureaucracy, taxes — a public power alienated from society and growing with imperialism and world war (“state-monopoly capitalism”; hinterlands as “military convict prisons”).
3. The bourgeois state is **abolished** / **smashed** by revolution; only the proletarian state (a semi-state) **[[withering-away-of-the-state|withers away]]**. The phrase “withering away” does not apply to the bourgeois state.
4. From the *Brumaire* and the Commune: previous revolutions perfected the bureaucratic-military machine; the proletariat must **[[smash-the-state-machine|smash]]** it, not “simply lay hold of the ready-made state machinery.”
5. The replacement form is the [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]] — proletariat organized as the ruling class — of which the Paris Commune is the first historical sketch (armed people, recallable officials at workers’ wages, working not merely parliamentary body).
6. Against anarchists (*On Authority*): revolution is “the most authoritarian thing there is”; a transitional political form is required until social relations that birthed the state are destroyed.
7. Against “opportunists” (esp. Kautsky): recognition of class struggle is incomplete without recognition of the dictatorship of the proletariat; Kautsky is charged with slurring over smash, treating seizure of the existing state as enough, and opposing smash language when pressed by Pannekoek (1912).
8. Economic basis (*Gotha Critique*): lower phase of communism still carries “bourgeois right”; the state withers only as classes and the need for special coercion disappear in the higher phase (“from each according to his ability, to each according to his needs”).

## Production context (reflexivity)

Written underground after the July 1917 days, during dual power and world war, from the notebook *Marxism and the State*. Chretien (via Sawer and others) situates a 1916 Lenin–Bukharin dispute on whether to “utilize” or smash the imperialist state; by May 1917 Lenin reportedly told Krupskaya he no longer disagreed with Bukharin on the state. Chapter 7 on 1905/1917 was never written. The gap between the pamphlet’s Commune-democratic programme and post-October Soviet state practice is a standing historiographical problem — **not adjudicated from this source alone**.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*State and Revolution* is the short, canonical statement of revolutionary Marxist state theory for the Bolshevik and later communist tradition. For this wiki it supplies: (1) a tightly attributed reconstruction of Marx/Engels on the state that can be cross-checked against already-ingested *Manifesto*, *Brumaire*, and *Origin*; (2) the strategic pair **smash** + **dictatorship of the proletariat** + **withering away** as a single programme; (3) a named polemic target (Kautsky / Second International) that anchors [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]; (4) a contrast foil for Weberian, Domhoffian, and Skocpolian state theory already on the wiki — different questions (legitimate force; US power structure; structural conditions of social revolution) that must not be merged with Lenin’s strategic doctrine. All load-bearing claims remain in attributed voice.
