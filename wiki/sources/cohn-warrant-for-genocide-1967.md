# Warrant for Genocide

Norman Cohn, *Warrant for Genocide: The Myth of the Jewish World Conspiracy and the Protocols of the Elders of Zion* (London: Eyre & Spottiswoode, 1967; Serif 1996/2005 with Foreword to the Serif Edition). Study: [[cohn-warrant-for-genocide-study]]. Thinker: [[cohn-norman]].

## What This Source Is

A landmark historical study of how the **myth of a Jewish world-conspiracy** — culminating in the forged *[[protocols-of-the-elders-of-zion|Protocols of the Elders of Zion]]* — was assembled, disseminated, and used as ideological justification for massacre and attempted [[genocide]]. Cohn's central thesis (attributed): the deadliest form of [[antisemitism]] that issues in extermination is less about ordinary interest-conflict or even "racial prejudice as such" than a **quasi-demonological conviction** that Jews everywhere form a conspiratorial body set on ruining and dominating mankind; under conditions of war, revolution, and slump that myth became a **warrant for genocide**.

Cohn is best known for *The Pursuit of the Millennium* (1957); this book is his major modern-history study, prompted (Serif foreword) by 1945 access to Nazi/proto-Nazi libraries and SS under interrogation, and by the gap he still saw at the Eichmann trial (1961).

## Key Claims Extracted (attributed to Cohn unless marked FACT)

### Core thesis
- Genocidal antisemitism springs from a **quasi-demonological** [[jewish-world-conspiracy-myth]], not from ordinary conflicts of interest alone.
- Forgotten "subterranean" writers built a myth that masters of a great European nation later used as a **warrant for genocide**.
- WWI, revolution, and the slump were enabling crises; without the myth, Cohn argues, those disasters alone would not have produced Auschwitz.

### Textual apparatus (largely FACT as Cohn documents)
- The Protocols are a **forgery**; their main literary source is Maurice Joly's 1864 *Dialogue aux Enfers entre Machiavel et Montesquieu* (anti-Napoleon III satire) — Cohn reports ~160 parallel passages, roughly two-fifths of the text, order often preserved.
- Additional streams: Goedsche/"Rabbi's Speech" lineage; messianic/Antichrist frames (Nilus); anti-Witte and Russian political uses.
- First major international unmasking: *The Times* (Philip Graves), 1921.

### Political career (FACT chronology as Cohn reconstructs; causal weight THESIS)
- Russian launch among *pogromshchiki*; Kishinev 1903; Union of the Russian People / Black Hundreds; White-army editions during the civil war; Cohn reports **over 100,000** Jews killed in civil-war massacres in this context.
- German breakthrough 1920 (Müller von Hausen / Gottfried zur Beek); massive print runs; fusion with *völkisch* [[scientific-racism|racism]] turns conspiracy fantasy into an **extermination** program in Cohn's reading.
- Hitler's thinking "permeated" by the Protocols by 1923 (*Mein Kampf*, Gemlich letter, later table-talk/prophecy chain as Cohn cites).
- Nazi propaganda used the myth at every stage from party rise through terror, war, genocide, and delay of surrender (Ch. 9 periodization).
- Berne trial (1933–35; appeal 1937) litigated authenticity; Cohn records later publishers misrepresenting the appeal.
- Transnational "antisemitic international" carried the text into the Americas and wartime collaboration régimes; Céline (*Bagatelles pour un massacre*, 1937) saw clearly, Cohn argues, that the Protocols were a warrant for genocide — "and that is precisely what they became."

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Warrant for Genocide* gives the wiki a primary scholarly spine for the **Protocols** and for conspiracy-myth [[antisemitism]] as a distinct causal strand in Holocaust ideology — complementary to Bauman's modernity/bureaucracy thesis already ingested. It does **not** replace social histories of perpetrators (Browning) or monocausal eliminationism (Goldhagen); it documents how a forgery's career supplied language, enemies, and apocalyptic permission structures. Appendices preserve the documentary apparatus (Rabbi's Speech; Joly parallels) that later denials still try to obscure. Post-1945 survivals Cohn notes (Stalinist variants, samizdat, Western fringe) keep the myth's afterlife visible without promoting it to contemporary wiki-voice sociology.
