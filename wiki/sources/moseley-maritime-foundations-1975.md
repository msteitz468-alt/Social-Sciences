# Moseley — The Maritime Foundations of Andean Civilization (1975)

Charter statement of the [[maritime-foundations-hypothesis]]: that Cotton Preceramic coastal Peruvian populations built sedentary communities and monumental corporate architecture on a maritime (not agricultural) subsistence base, positioning marine resources as the economic foundation from which Andean civilization arose.

Core claims: the maritime (not agricultural) subsistence base of Preceramic coastal complexity; the **preadaptation** argument (plants + work force + mobilizing institutions predate agricultural dependence); **corporate authority precedes irrigation** (inverting the hydraulic model); **industrial cultigens** (cotton, gourd) drive Preceramic farming; the **demographic deviation** feedback model. Empirical spine: [[ancon|Ancón–Chillón]] excavations plus the northern mound sites, with uncalibrated 1975 radiocarbon and twined-textile seriation.
