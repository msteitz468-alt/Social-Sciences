## Volume Synthesis Note

*Ancient Law* is the charter English statement of **historical comparative jurisprudence** as a method for social theory. Maine rejects pure Law-of-Nature and original-contract starting points, reconstructs law as developing from judgment and custom through codes, then through **[[legal-fictions|fictions]], equity, and legislation**. The theoretical centre is Ch. V: comparative evidence for the **[[patriarchal-theory|Patriarchal Theory]]** and the formula that progressive societies move **[[status-to-contract|from Status to Contract]]** — from family-fixed personal conditions to individual agreement. Later chapters support the thesis through wills (universal succession before modern testation), property (joint ownership and village community before separate occupancy theories), contract (late and ceremonial), and delict/crime (torts and compositions before true criminal law). The book is a founding-era node for [[social-evolutionism]] and kinship debates, and a method classic for [[comparative-method]], without being ethnography.
