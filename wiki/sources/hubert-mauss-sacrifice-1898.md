# Sacrifice: Its Nature and Functions (Hubert & Mauss, 1898)

Founding essay of the Durkheimian sociology of [[sacrifice]]. Hubert and Mauss argue (attributed voice throughout — this is a theoretical program) that sacrifice is a **unitary social institution**: a scheme of **consecration** that establishes communication between the [[sacred-and-profane|sacred and profane]] through a **victim** as intermediary, rather than a search for one original motive (gift, homage, abnegation, communion).

## Edition

- French original: *Essai sur la nature et la fonction du sacrifice*, *L'Année sociologique* II (1898).
- English: *Sacrifice: Its Nature and Functions*, trans. W. D. Halls, foreword E. E. Evans-Pritchard; University of Chicago Press 1964 / 1981 reprint (this ingest).

## Claims Integrated

### Edition and reception
- French original *Année sociologique* II (1898), pp. 29–158; Halls English 1964/1981 with Evans-Pritchard foreword; *sacrifiant* → English **sacrifier** (Halls coinage).
- EP: grammar of the rite superb; conclusions rather lame; part of Oxford *Année* translation series.

### Definitions and roles (attributed Hubert & Mauss)
- Ch. I definition: religious act that, through consecration of a victim, modifies the moral person or related objects.
- Conclusion definition: communication between sacred and profane worlds through mediation of a destroyed victim.
- Roles: [[sacrifier]] / sacrificer / victim; personal vs objective sacrifices; consecration ≠ sacrifice; vegetable offerings count when destroyed.

### Scheme and functions
- Procedural scheme (Vedic foundation): entry → victim consecration/identification → immolation → disposition → exit; religiosity **curve**.
- General functions: [[sacralization]] vs [[desacralization]]/expiation; same elements, opposite ends; impurity as consecration.
- Special functions: building, vows, first-fruits, agrarian spirit rites; conversation with Mannhardt/Frazer without origin reduction.
- Sacrifice of the god: limit case of victim–divinity identity (Aztec, soma/Purusha, dying gods, Mass).

### Social function
- Sacred things are social things; religious ideas exist as social facts; abnegation nourishes collective forces; victim redeems sacrifier; contractual element with gods who need the profane share.

### Foils
- Tylor gift→homage→abnegation (no mechanism); Robertson Smith totemic communion (arbitrary unifying principle); Frazer god-sacrifice/scapegoat lines engaged as supplements.
