# Coral Gardens and Their Magic (Malinowski, 1935)

Bronislaw Malinowski's two-volume Trobriand agriculture monograph: **Vol. I** institutional ethnography of gardens, harvest gifts, land tenure, and method; **Vol. II** ethnographic theory of language and the magical word. Author: [[malinowski-bronislaw]]. Companion: *[[malinowski-argonauts-1922|Argonauts]]* (1922). Society: [[trobriand-islanders]]. Study: [[malinowski-coral-gardens-study]] · [[hubs/studies/malinowski-coral-gardens-study]].

Edmund Leach's 1965 introduction (Vol. I Indiana reprint) frames Vol. I as Malinowski's demonstration of **functionalist analysis** with particular reference to **land tenure**, and Vol. II as a technical linguistics work only loosely connected except via shared magic texts.

### Combined highlights
- **Gardens as political economy:** taytu year; chieftainship financed by harvest gifts; food as wealth/power medium.
- **Urigubu customary law:** ~half produce to sister’s husband; polygamy multiplies chiefly receipts; gubakayeki personal plots; display sanctions.
- **Magic and work:** distinct but interleaved; towosi organises; vilamalia acts on appetite/village; no confusion of technique and spell (Malinowski).
- **Method (Ch. XI):** constructive field-work; **invisible facts** (land tenure paradigmatic); charters of institutions; self-critique of communism/individualism fallacy.
- **Land tenure (Ch. XII):** sub-clan title vs gardening-team use; residence fundamental; nigada loans; rank via surrender not conquest; Four Doctrines.
- **Language (Vol. II):** context of situation; dual coefficients; force without *mana* noun; Formulae 1–44.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Together the two volumes complete Malinowski’s Trobriand **agriculture** program: Vol. I is the institutional, calendrical, legal, and methodological ethnography; Vol. II is the linguistic and spell apparatus. Leach’s split (functional land-tenure demo vs technical linguistics) is useful but understates the shared *towosi*/garden-magic object. With *Argonauts*, the wiki now holds exchange (Kula) + gardens (Coral Gardens) as dual type-cases under **1915–18** documentation. Remaining Malinowski gaps: *Sexual Life of Savages*, functionalist essays, *Diary*.
