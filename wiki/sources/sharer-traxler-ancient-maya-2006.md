# The Ancient Maya (Sharer & Traxler, 6th ed. 2006)

The standard comprehensive Anglophone reference on **pre-Columbian Maya civilization** — setting, methods and models, calendar and writing, Preclassic origins through Postclassic reformulation, economy, social/political organization, ideology, and the Spanish Conquest — by [[sharer-robert-j|Robert J. Sharer]] with [[traxler-loa-p|Loa P. Traxler]] (Stanford University Press). Densifies the wiki after [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston, *The Maya* (2015)]].

## Framing (Preface / edition history, attributed)

- Sixth edition thoroughly rewritten and expanded from Sharer’s fourth/fifth; earlier editions preserved Morley–Brainerd core while adding research.
- Spans ~2,500 years from origins of complex society in [[mesoamerica]] to the Spanish Conquest.
- Integrates archaeological, epigraphic, ethnohistoric, and ethnographic evidence; chapter-by-chapter bibliographic summaries (apparatus).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Ancient Maya* (6th ed. 2006) is the densifying comprehensive reference after [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]]. Load-bearing contributions to this vault: (1) full Preclassic→Postclassic political arc with dynastic tables (Tikal–Calakmul “Great Game,” Petexbatun, Usumacinta, western and southeastern polities); (2) multi-causal Terminal Classic as century-plus process with regional triggers, explicitly rejecting “end of the Maya”; (3) settlement-density and population apparatus feeding overpopulation debates; (4) economy chapters rejecting pure-swidden models and tracking trade-route shifts; (5) social/political organization (households, *k’uhul ajaw*, state models); (6) decipherment historiography (Proskouriakoff, Knorosov, Morley lineage of the handbook itself). Weaknesses for this wiki: mid-2000s horizon (pre-LIDAR demography; drought literature now thicker); ethnohistoric analogy from Yucatan/highlands; OCR noise on some table figures. Pair with Coe & Houston for mid-2010s epigraphy; both remain synthesis layers, not primary excavation reports.
