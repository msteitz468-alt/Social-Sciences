## Key Claims Extracted

**Split: THESES (Dickinson) vs FINDINGS as he reports them.** Mid-1990s secondary horizon.

### THESES (attributed)

1. Accepts Renfrew’s (1972: 7) definition that peak Aegean BA qualifies as **civilisation**; asks how it arose, why it collapsed, what legacy to later Greek civilisation.
2. **Anti-invasion / anti-colonisation** as primary culture-change engines; origin legends not to be taken at face value; Egyptian or “northern nomad” colonisation of Greece lacks rational motivation.
3. **Anti-“influence-as-disease”:** contact does not automatically transmit superior culture; MH mainland long in contact with Aegean before perceptible change.
4. **Anti-mono-catastrophe:** earthquakes/climate/Thera alone insufficient; circular when destructions are the only evidence; Thera dissociated from Second Palace end.
5. Prefer **cultural process** explanations (resources, status competition, exchange, ideology; interactions within Aegean and abroad); particular events as catalysts.
6. **Economy:** land remains basis; craft specialists mostly part-time farmers; only palace/elite-maintained specialists full-time; **anti-total palace redistribution** (softens Renfrew/Halstead extremes).
7. **Polyculture:** middle path between Renfrew’s early olive/vine package and hard sceptics.
8. **Trade:** anti-Aegeocentric; gifts vs commerce; temper Renfrew’s “international spirit”; don’t overestimate Mycenaean eastern role (Ahhiyawa = Mycenae unlikely); oxhide/Ulu Burun as international system evidence.
9. **Religion:** reject Evans/Frazer mother-goddess monism; multi-local gods; limited literacy of belief without texts; Dark Age rupture for Mycenaean cult.
10. **Collapse / legacy:** Mycenaean systems collapse as cumulative process; dual Aegean collapses vs Near East; Dark Age as true birth of “the Greeks” (his framing).

### FINDINGS / DESCRIPTIVE (as of Dickinson 1994)

- Tripartite EM/EC/EH, MM/MC/MH, LM/LC/LH terminology as Procrustean but still required; assemblage names preferred to pure culture labels for some EH phases.
- Absolute chronology contested (Warren–Hankey vs Manning high LBA / Thera ~1630); traditional Near Eastern synchronisms weakened.
- Crafts survey: pottery through weapons/armour; seals/writing palace-tied and collapse with palaces.
- Burial: status-filtered sample; tholos vs chamber tomb trajectories; children’s underrepresentation.
- Settlement hierarchy and Linear B land/wool economy sketches.
