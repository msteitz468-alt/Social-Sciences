# The Prehistory of the Mind (Mithen, 1996)

[[mithen-steven|Steven Mithen]]'s major mid-1990s synthesis of **cognitive archaeology** with evolutionary psychology and developmental modularity theories. The book proposes a **cathedral** model of mind evolution: a nave of generalized intelligence, chapels of specialized intelligences (social, natural-history, technical, linguistic), and finally **[[cognitive-fluidity|cognitive fluidity]]** — the cross-domain flow of knowledge that Mithen argues underwrites art, religion, and science in modern humans. Subtitled *A Search for the Origins of Art, Religion and Science*.

## Core Argument (attributed, Mithen 1996)

1. The modern mind has a **modular / multi-intelligence architecture** (Fodor, Gardner, Cosmides & Tooby, Karmiloff-Smith, Rozin) that cannot be explained by continuous brain-size increase alone.
2. Prehistory shows three architectural phases: (I) **generalized intelligence**; (II) **specialized intelligences** with poor cross-domain access — Early Human / Neanderthal profile; (III) **cognitive fluidity** — uniquely (or first fully) in anatomically modern *Homo sapiens*.
3. The Upper Palaeolithic cultural explosion (art, religion, complex tools, colonization) is the archaeological signature of cognitive fluidity, not merely of new bodies or climates — staggered regional **sparks** ~60–30 kya, not one global bang.
4. **Language** (social → general-purpose) is the principal evolutionary mechanism that opened walls between specialized intelligences; consciousness expands from social prediction to multi-domain database.
5. Art, religion, and science are **exaptive** products of fluid minds; agriculture is an Epilogue consequence once fluid minds meet Holocene plant/animal opportunities (four mental propensities × climate; Natufian point of no return).
