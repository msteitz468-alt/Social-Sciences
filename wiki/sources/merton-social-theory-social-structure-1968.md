## Coverage and Reliability

Theoretical essays and codifications, not a continuous ethnography or survey monograph. Empirical illustrations (bank run, American Soldier relative deprivation, Rovere influentials, 17th-century English science) are evidence *as Merton uses them* — attributed to Merton's framing, not re-filed as independent primary findings without their original studies. Chapter VI (anomie) and the science essays have independent article origins (1938; 1930s–40s) and warrant study-level treatment where the field treats them as landmarks.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Social Theory and Social Structure* is the postwar American charter that this collection’s functionalism holdings had been missing on the **Merton** side of the Parsons–Merton settlement. Where *[[parsons-social-system-1951|The Social System]]* supplies total-system architecture, STSS supplies (1) a **research policy** — [[middle-range-theory]] — positioned against both premature total systems and raw description; (2) a **codification of functional analysis** that builds in [[dysfunction]], net balance, functional alternatives, and unit-specification, rejecting anthropological postulates of unity, universal functionalism, and indispensability; (3) a cluster of still-live middle-range theories — anomie/strain typology ([[merton-social-structure-and-anomie-study]]), [[reference-group]] / [[relative-deprivation]] / [[anticipatory-socialization]], [[role-set]] / [[status-set]], [[self-fulfilling-prophecy]], local/cosmopolitan influentials; (4) a founding statement of the **[[scientific-ethos|ethos of science]]** and a Weberian extension of the Protestant-ethic affinity logic to science (Ch. XX); (5) a paradigm appraisal of [[sociology-of-knowledge]] and mass communications.

Eight parallel extractors covered Prefaces through Part IV (~24,650 body lines). Integration created thinker + Theory Hub + Thinkers Hub, middle-range theory summary + Theory Hub, eleven concept pages (plus study page for the anomie essay), and updated [[structural-functionalism]], [[anomie]], [[bureaucracy]], [[sociology-of-knowledge]], [[parsons-talcott]], and the grand-theory / functional-change / Protestant-ethic debate pages.

Tensions with existing holdings: Merton’s anomie must not be collapsed into Durkheim 1897 or Gouldner’s utilitarian-pathology reading; his functional analysis is an internal reform of functionalism, not a conflict-theory substitute; middle-range theory is a **fourth** position relative to Mills’s dual attack, not identical with “classic” craft or Bureau methodism. Flagged debts: Lazarsfeld primary; full sociology-of-science secondary literature; Cloward–Ohlin and later strain revisions; Merton biography beyond STSS self-presentation.
