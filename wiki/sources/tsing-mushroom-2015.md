# Tsing, *The Mushroom at the End of the World* (2015)

Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing's *The Mushroom at the End of the World: On the Possibility of Life in Capitalist Ruins* (Princeton, 2015) is a multi-locale, multi-species ethnography that follows matsutake mushrooms and the people, forests, and commercial chains that entangle them. Tsing argues (attributed) that in an era of **precarity** and **capitalist ruins**, collaborative survival is best noticed in **patchy assemblages** of humans and nonhumans, and that capital amasses wealth in part by **salvage accumulation** — appropriating value produced in unplanned, often non-capitalist or peri-capitalist patches.

The book earns a [[tsing-mushroom-study|study page]] and Studies Hub analysis as a reference point for multi-species ethnography, salvage capitalism, disturbance ecology in the social sciences, and livelihood under precarity.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Mushroom at the End of the World* is the wiki’s first full multi-species ethnography charter and the second Tsing primary after *Friction*. Where *Friction* tracks frictional human universals on Indonesian frontiers, *Mushroom* asks how life and wealth work *after* progress narratives fail — in ruined industrial forests and irregular livelihoods. The book’s portable vocabulary ([[salvage-accumulation]], [[precarity]], [[arts-of-noticing]], [[contamination-as-collaboration]], [[patchiness]], [[unintentional-design]]) links economic anthropology of supply chains to ecological multi-species history without reducing either to the other.

For the wiki, the densest subject-matter page is [[open-ticket-oregon]] (seasonal commercial patch, not a people). Japan material densifies [[japanese-people]] as a second documentation layer (2004–2011) beside Benedict’s 1940s national-character construct. Methodologically, [[multi-species-ethnography]] and an extended [[ethnography-of-global-connection]] now sit alongside each other under [[tsing-anna-lowenhaupt]].

**What this source does well:** multi-locale + multi-species design; capitalism-without-progress analytic; gift/commodity chain that operationalizes salvage; careful pseudonym ethics; formal experiment matched to patchiness thesis.

**What it leaves thin:** *Diamond Queen* (1993) still uningested; quantitative trade volumes anonymized; secondary critique literature; Thinkers Hub still deferred (two monographs, no secondary biography).

**Tensions with existing pages:** Complements Marxian [[alienation]] with an ecological-asset sense — keep registers distinct. Complements Appadurai gift/commodity blur with a chain-temporal model (hours of pure inventory). Complements Castells/Sennett flexible-employment pages with forager non-wage precarity. Scalability (*Mushroom*) ≠ scale-making (*Friction*) — related, not identical.
