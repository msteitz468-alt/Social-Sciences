# Ancient Egypt: Anatomy of a Civilization (Kemp 2018)

## Volume Synthesis Note

Kemp’s 2018 *Anatomy* is a **civilization-scale social archaeology** of Egypt: culture as mental filter; dominance hierarchy and deference; identity without race science; early-state ideology (king lists, *ma’at*, names, gods); provider-state bureaucracy and model communities; New Kingdom temple corporatism and empire; “economic man” beside redistribution; Amarna as urban microcosm from Kemp’s own fieldwork. ~282 claims across 4 extractors.

For the wiki this **opens an Egyptology spine** previously thin: thinker + Amarna + Kahun stubs; densifies Weberian [[bureaucracy]] and [[the-state]] with a long Egyptian case; extends [[temple-economy]] beyond Mesopotamia; grounds [[maat]]; feeds [[state-formation]] and [[urbanization]]. No `studies/` page — multi-edition synthetic treatise, not a single research act (Amarna project monographs remain future primary digests).
