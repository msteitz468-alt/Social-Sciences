# Cannibal Metaphysics: For a Post-Structural Anthropology

Eduardo Viveiros de Castro, *Cannibal Metaphysics: For a Post-Structural Anthropology*, trans. and ed. Peter Skafish (Minneapolis: Univocal, 2014). Original: *Métaphysiques cannibales: Lignes d'anthropologie post-structurale* (Paris: PUF, "MétaphysiqueS," 2009).

A programmatic, speculative-philosophical statement of **[[perspectivism|Amerindian perspectivism]]** and **[[multinaturalism]]** as the basis for a "post-structural anthropology" and a "permanent decolonization of thought." The book reads Amazonian ethnography through, and against, [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]]'s [[structuralism]] and [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]] & Guattari's philosophy, in sustained counterpoint with [[descola-philippe|Descola]].

## Claims Extracted

All claims are **attributed** (theoretical work; ethnographic material is deployed as philosophical argument, not fresh finding).

- **Anthropology as philosophy / decolonization of thought.** "Every nontrivial anthropological theory is a version of an indigenous practice of knowledge"; anthropology's mission is "the permanent decolonization of thought"; native ideas are to be treated as **concepts**, not beliefs. → [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo]], [[ontological-turn]].
- **Perspectivism.** Nonhumans see themselves as persons and see humans as animals; difference is bodily/perspectival, not cultural; "the point of view creates the subject"; "a perspective is not a representation... a point of view is in the body." → [[perspectivism]].
- **Multinaturalism.** One soul/culture, many bodies/natures — the inverse of multiculturalism; "variation as nature." → [[multinaturalism]].
- **Controlled equivocation.** Translation as the management of referential alterity between homonyms; "one meaning, multiple referents." → [[controlled-equivocation]].
- **Deleuze & Guattari, anthropologized.** *Capitalism and Schizophrenia* read as secretly Amazonian; multiplicity, rhizome, intensive/extensive, becoming, disjunctive synthesis appropriated; "the rhizome is alliance, uniquely alliance." → [[deleuze-gilles]].
- **Metaphysics of predation / cannibal cogito.** Tupinambá and [[arawete|Araweté]] cannibalism as "transmutation of perspectives"; the self constituted through the **enemy's** point of view — "not only a perspectivism but an 'enemyism.'" → [[arawete]].
- **Transversal shamanism.** The shaman as cosmopolitical diplomat crossing species-perspectives; "shamanism is the continuation of war by other means."
- **Virtual/potential affinity.** Affinity (= the enemy) as the generic cosmological relation; "affines are enemies, and enemies are thus affines"; kinship as "the political economy of the universe."
- **Becomings of structuralism.** "Two structuralisms"; the *Mythologiques* as rhizomatic — "a structuralism without structure"; "Lévi-Strauss with Deleuze" as "monstrous alliance." → [[structuralism]], [[levi-strauss-claude]].
- **The Descola dispute.** "Perspectivism is not, *pace* [[descola-philippe|Descola]], a subtype of [[animism]]... not a type but a concept." → [[ontological-turn-debate]], [[four-ontologies]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Cannibal Metaphysics* is a programmatic, speculative-philosophical work: it argues that anthropology can *do* metaphysics, and that the metaphysics worth doing is one built from the concepts of Amazonian peoples. Its three linked theses — [[perspectivism]] (nonhumans are persons who differ by body), [[multinaturalism]] (one culture, many natures), and virtual affinity (the affine-enemy as the primary relation) — are welded to a method, [[controlled-equivocation|controlled equivocation]], and a genealogy that reads [[levi-strauss-claude|Lévi-Strauss]] through [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]] to produce a "post-structural anthropology." The book culminates in the "cannibal cogito": against a Western selfhood grounded in the Friend/self-relation, an Amerindian selfhood grounded in incorporating the [[arawete|enemy's]] point of view.

For this vault the work is doubly important. It supplies the **Viveiros de Castro pole** of the [[ontological-turn]] to sit beside the already-ingested **Descola pole** (*[[descola-beyond-nature-and-culture-2005|Beyond Nature and Culture]]*), and it makes their long disagreement — perspectivism as a *concept* vs. as a *subtype of animism* — explicit and citable ([[ontological-turn-debate]]). It also seeds the vault's first pages on [[deleuze-gilles|Deleuze]] and [[wagner-roy|Roy Wagner]] as anthropological interlocutors. Tension with existing content is productive rather than contradictory: where the [[structuralism]] pages present a program that is "absorbed," this source documents one live afterlife of it.

The standing caution (in `reliability_notes`) governs downstream use: the Amazonian material here is Viveiros de Castro's own philosophical synthesis of prior Amazonianist ethnography (his [[arawete|Araweté]] fieldwork and colonial Tupinambá sources), not raw data — it must not be laundered into wiki voice on society/culture pages. Not primary-ingested: his Araweté monograph (*From the Enemy's Point of View*, 1992) and *A inconstância da alma selvagem* (2002), both priority acquisitions.
