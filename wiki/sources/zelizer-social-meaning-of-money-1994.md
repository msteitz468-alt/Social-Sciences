## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Social Meaning of Money* is a hybrid theoretical–historical program that founded the research vocabulary of **[[multiple-monies|multiple monies]]** and **[[earmarking]]** in contemporary [[economic-sociology|economic sociology]]. Chapter 1 reconstructs the classical utilitarian/Simmelian model of homogeneous "colorless" money (also Marx and Weber as foils) and states five counter-claims; Chapters 2–6 deliver U.S. historical cases (c. 1870–1930) in three strategic domains where classical theory expected monetization to flatten personal life — domestic monies, gift monies, and charitable/welfare cash; Chapter 7 synthesizes; the 2017 afterword adds reception history, the **[[relational-work]]** label, hostile-worlds critique, and policy applications.

**Relation to already-ingested work:** direct challenge to the Simmel layer on [[money-economy]] and *[[simmel-philosophy-of-money-1900|Philosophy of Money]]*; kin to [[embeddedness]] and [[gift-exchange]]; densifies [[family]] monetary contests alongside Hochschild work–family material; seeds subfield page [[economic-sociology]]. **Voice:** theoretical claims and historical findings attributed throughout. **Hub:** Studies Hub built in-session (selection criteria met: founded a research vocabulary and program in economic sociology of money).
