# Configurations of Culture Growth (Kroeber, 1944)

A. L. [[kroeber-alfred-l|Kroeber]]'s *Configurations of Culture Growth* (1944) is his monumental empirical demonstration of the [[superorganic]] thesis: a systematic comparative survey of the "spasmodic bursts" in which civilizations reach their highest achievements in philosophy, science, philology, sculpture, painting, drama, literature, music, and national power. Kroeber's central claim is that high cultural productivity — including the appearance of "genius" — clusters in time and place because it expresses the growth of *culture patterns*, not the free unfolding of individual wills (see [[genius-clustering]], [[growth-configuration]]). The book anchors his dispute with Spengler's organicist cyclical history ([[kroeber-spengler-debate]]). This is the vault's primary ingest of Kroeber's culture-growth program; his mature culture theory is ingested separately as *[[kroeber-culture-patterns-processes-1963|Culture Patterns & Processes]]*.

## The Argument

*Claims attributed to Kroeber throughout.*

- **The problem.** Cultures develop their highest intellectual and aesthetic achievements *spasmodically* — in clustered bursts (florescences) that grow, culminate, and decline. Kroeber investigates the **growth configurations** of these bursts (their patterns in time, space, and degree) empirically and comparatively, deliberately setting aside their *content* and their *causes*: "a clearer and surer understanding of how cultures behave historically seems antecedent to why."
- **Genius is a medium, not a cause.** Great cultural products cluster around great men because personalities are the medium through which culture patterns express themselves; but "the subject of this inquiry is not who expresses, but what is expressed." Newton's *Principia* required both Newton's genius *and* the arrived-at state of science — the clustering of genius is evidence of the [[superorganic]] growth of patterns.
- **Growth configurations recur.** Across civilizations and activities, florescences show recurrent features: definite durations, single or multiple peaks, geographic shift, and characteristic rise–culmination–exhaustion curves. Patterns can be exhausted and a growth end without the "death" of the whole culture.
