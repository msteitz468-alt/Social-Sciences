# The Nuer (Evans-Pritchard 1940) — Source

> Study analysis: [[evans-pritchard-nuer-study]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/evans-pritchard-nuer-study|Studies Hub — The Nuer]]. Author: [[evans-pritchard-e-e|E. E. Evans-Pritchard]].

## Bibliographic Record

E. E. Evans-Pritchard, *The Nuer: A Description of the Modes of Livelihood and Political Institutions of a Nilotic People* (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1940). Ethnography of the [[nuer|Nuer]] of the southern Sudan, based on fieldwork of 1930–1936. The first of Evans-Pritchard's Nuer trilogy (followed by *Kinship and Marriage among the Nuer*, 1951, and *Nuer Religion*, 1956).

## Structure

Six chapters: **Introductory** (the Nuer and their country; fieldwork conditions) · **Interest in Cattle** (the cattle idiom of social life) · **Oecology** (transhumant pastoralism; villages and camps; Nuer–Dinka relations) · **Time and Space** (oecological time and structural time; structural distance) · **The Political System** (tribe, segmentary opposition, feud, leopard-skin chief, "ordered anarchy") · **The Lineage System** and **The Age-Set System** (agnatic lineages as the structural skeleton of political groups; age-sets).

## Claims Extracted

Extracted across six chapters (attributed to Evans-Pritchard throughout): the **cattle idiom** (cattle as pivot of kinship, ritual, value, identity; ox-names; symbiosis; colour vocabulary); **oecology** (transhumance, villages/camps, millet, fishing, rinderpest, simple material culture, Nuer–Dinka relations); **structural time and distance** (oecological vs structural time; the cattle-clock; the forty-miles/twenty-miles inversion); **the political system** (segmentary opposition, fission/fusion, "ordered anarchy," the feud, the leopard-skin chief, prophets); **the lineage system** (agnatic clans and lineages as "conceptual skeleton"; lineage ≠ political group; diel/rul/jaang; Dinka adoption; exogamy); **the age-set system** (gar initiation; age-sets are "not regiments," not politically interdependent); and the Summary's definition of **social structure** and its refusal of a Radcliffe-Brownian functional reading. Fieldwork-condition material (the "Nuerosis"; method forced by hostility) feeds the study and method pages.
