# Writing Women's Worlds: Bedouin Stories

[[abu-lughod-lila|Lila Abu-Lughod]]'s *Writing Women's Worlds* (1993) is a narrative ethnography of [[awlad-ali|Awlad ʿAli]] Bedouin women in Egypt's Western Desert. Companion to *[[abu-lughod-veiled-sentiments-1986|Veiled Sentiments]]* (1986): where the earlier book analyzed dual discourses of honor and poetry, this volume **writes against culture** by organizing women's stories, conversations, songs, and arguments around five anthropological themes without typifying "Bedouin culture." Study page: [[abu-lughod-writing-womens-worlds-study]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/abu-lughod-writing-womens-worlds-study|Studies Hub]].

## Reliability and use

Primary ethnographic evidence for [[awlad-ali]] **as of the 1980s return fieldwork** (esp. 1986–89), continuous with the 1978–80 community of *Veiled Sentiments*. Write society claims in the past tense of the documentation moment. Interpretive program ([[writing-against-culture]], [[tactical-humanism]], feminist ethnography stance) is **Abu-Lughod's analysis**, not wiki-voice social theory. Do not collapse five thematic chapters into timeless "Bedouin culture" generalizations — the book's method is precisely against that.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Writing Women's Worlds* is a **narrative ethnography** that answers the late-1980s crisis of representation and feminist ethnography debates by **[[writing-against-culture|writing against culture]]**: five chapters titled with social-science institutions (patrilineality, polygyny, reproduction, patrilateral parallel-cousin marriage, honor and shame) filled with named Awlad ʿAli women's stories, songs, and arguments from 1986–89 returns rather than typifying "Bedouin culture." The introduction's **[[tactical-humanism]]** names the limited political wager of familiar humanist narrative against othering, while "For Whom?" states that critical ethnography is a project for the West and may not serve hosts.

**What this ingest adds:** companion study + Studies Hub to *Veiled Sentiments*; concepts [[writing-against-culture]], [[tactical-humanism]], [[patrilateral-parallel-cousin-marriage]]; densified [[awlad-ali]] 1980s cases (Migdim, Gateefa, Kamla); links into [[feminist-anthropology]], [[writing-culture-debate]], [[life-history-method]], [[honor-and-modesty]], [[marriage]].

**What it does not settle:** post-1989 community change (see 2016 *Veiled Sentiments* afterword); later "saving Muslim women" public debates; quantitative rates of polygyny or FBD marriage (method refuses those typifications).

**Production context is content:** NEH 1986; Fulbright 1987; IAS Gender Seminar (Haraway, Butler, Scott, Keller…); Santa Fe Fox seminar; halfie / father-mediated / 1989 bridal-return positionality; tape ethics and host censorship of some songs.
