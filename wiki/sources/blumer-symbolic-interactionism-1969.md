# Symbolic Interactionism: Perspective and Method (Blumer, 1969)

Herbert Blumer's principal statement of [[symbolic-interactionism|symbolic interactionism]] as a distinctive sociological perspective *and* as a methodological program. The volume gathers his scattered articles on interactionist theory and on social-science method, capped by a long original introductory essay that formulates the three premises, the nature of group life and objects, and the procedures of exploration and inspection.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Blumer's 1969 volume is the **methodological charter** of named [[symbolic-interactionism|symbolic interactionism]]: one long original statement (Ch. 1) plus eleven reprinted essays that stage the same fight on different fronts — against factor explanations, system sociology, attitude psychology, variable analysis, operationalism, and aggregate public-opinion polling. The three premises, root images ([[joint-action|joint action]], [[self-indication]], [[social-object|objects]]), and [[exploration-and-inspection|exploration/inspection]] pair are load-bearing for the theory and method pages; the sensitizing/definitive contrast (Ch. 8) and the variable essay (Ch. 7) are the most cited methods pieces beyond the premises.

**What this source adds to the wiki.** (1) A primary-text base for Blumer as thinker and for SI as a *named* school, not only as Mead reception. (2) Explicit method pages and concepts (exploration/inspection, joint action, self-indication, social object, sensitizing concept) that earlier Mead/Goffman/Cooley ingests could only anticipate. (3) A Theory Hub analysis of SI's architecture, rivals, and survival. (4) A clear Blumer position on the [[structure-vs-agency-debate]]. (5) A critical but not dismissive appraisal of Thomas & Znaniecki that updates the Polish Peasant documentary lineage.

**Tensions with already-ingested sources.** Blumer's pedagogical Mead overlaps and sometimes simplifies the wiki's *Mind, Self, and Society* apparatus (and the authorship debate). His critique of attitude as concept sits beside Thomas & Znaniecki's [[attitude-and-value]] program — he rejects their "laws of social becoming" while keeping the subjective factor and human documents. Berger & Luckmann remain a bridge *outward* from Meadian social psychology, not a Blumerian branch. Goffman *Asylums* remains the wiki's main SI-adjacent ethnography; Blumer supplies the methodological self-description that *Asylums* only partly enacts under different labels.

**Production context.** Mid-century American sociology's quantification turn; Blumer writes as Chicago-trained naturalist against protocolized survey science. OCR text complete through the public-opinion essay (~p. 208); page headers bleed into body lines.
