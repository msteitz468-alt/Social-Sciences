# The Children of Aataentsic (Trigger, 1976/1987)

Bruce Trigger's two-volume ethnohistory of the Huron (Wendat) confederacy from Ontario prehistory to the 1660 aftermath of its 1649 destruction — the full-scale scholarly work behind the synthesis later popularized in *[[trigger-natives-and-newcomers-1985|Natives and Newcomers]]* (1985). Widely treated as the founding monument of Canadian ethnohistory: it insists that Indian actions be interpreted as rational, interest-driven behaviour from within Huron culture, and that precontact change (known archaeologically) is the indispensable baseline for measuring European impact. Analyzed as a research act at [[trigger-children-of-aataentsic-study]].
