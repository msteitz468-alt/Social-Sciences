# The Maya (Coe & Houston, 9th ed. 2015)

The standard concise Anglophone synthesis of **Maya civilization** — geography, Preclassic origins, Classic city-states and dynasties, Terminal Classic “collapse,” Postclassic kingdoms, contact-period life, religion/writing/calendar, and living Maya peoples — by [[coe-michael-d|Michael D. Coe]] and [[houston-stephen|Stephen Houston]] (Thames & Hudson, Ancient Peoples and Places). First published 1966; this ingest uses the **2015 ninth edition**, the first fully co-authored with Houston.

## Framing (Preface, attributed — Coe & Houston)

- Written for students, travelers, and the general public as a pocketable yet accurate account of Maya civilization and its ruins.
- Hieroglyphic decipherment lets the Maya “express themselves in their own voice”; Houston joins to represent a younger epigraphic generation.
- Subsequent editions track new sites, inscriptions, and dating while keeping the single-volume format.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Maya* (2015) is the best single-volume entry point for the wiki’s Mesoamerican civilization gap flagged after the Fagan textbook ingest. Its load-bearing moves: (1) Preclassic “proto-Classic” and Mirador-scale urbanism rewrite Classic-centric origins; (2) post-decipherment dynastic history (Emblem Glyphs, Tikal–Calakmul “Great Game,” named kings and wars) replaces Morley empire and Thompson non-historical script eras; (3) Terminal Classic is regionally split — southern multi-causal collapse vs northern florescence — with living Maya continuity insisted upon from the first page; (4) writing, calendar, and *k'uhul ajaw* ideology are first-class, not afterthoughts. Weaknesses for this wiki: survey compression of specialist debates; ethnohistoric analogy from Yucatan/highlands back to Classic; pre-LIDAR demography; drought literature as of mid-2010s. Sharer & Traxler *The Ancient Maya* remains the natural densifying reference next.

**Not created (deliberate):** separate pages for every secondary site (Yaxchilan, Dos Pilas, Uxmal, Tulum, etc.), full Mayan language-family culture page, or Thinkers Hub biographies — survey depth does not meet hub criteria; Coe hub better awaits *Breaking the Maya Code*.
