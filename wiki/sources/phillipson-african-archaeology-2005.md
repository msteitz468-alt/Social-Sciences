# African Archaeology (Phillipson, 2005)

## Bibliographic Record

David W. Phillipson, *African Archaeology*, 3rd edition (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2005). ~309 body pages + Bibliographic guide/references (p. 310–) / Index (p. 369–). Author: [[phillipson-david-w]].

Continent-wide archaeological survey from the emergence of hominids through the second millennium CE in sub-Saharan Africa. Emphasises Africa’s centrality in human biological and cultural origins; integrates stone-artefact “modes” (after [[clark-grahame|Grahame Clark]]), regional sequences, farming origins, iron-working, Bantu linguistic archaeology, and late precolonial polities. Companion continental synthesis to [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]] (which focuses on urbanism and state formation over the last ~6,000 years).

## Key Claims Extracted

All claims **attributed to Phillipson 2005** unless noted as his citation of others.

1. **Africa as cradle.** Humankind first evolved in Africa ~5.0–2.5 Ma; archaeology is the main primary source for most of the deep past; “prehistory” is an imperfect label where oral tradition and outsider texts fill uneven literacy (Ch. 1).
2. **Methods package.** Linguistics, oral tradition, and ethnoarchaeology complement material remains for recent periods only; Clark’s five [[lithic-technological-modes|lithic modes]] organise technology without false period boxes.
3. **Oldowan / Acheulean.** Mode-1 Oldowan: unstandardised flakes/cores from ~2.5 Ma; mode-2 Acheulean from ~1.6 Ma to ~350–250 ka; [[sangoan-industry|Sangoan]] as final Acheulean manifestation (not a forest industry).
4. **Regional diversification.** MSA/LSA (mode 3→5) ~250–10 ka: increasing regional specialisation while remaining hunter-gatherers; African mode-5 microliths (incl. [[howiesons-poort-industry]]) and anatomical modernity antedate Europe’s Upper Palaeolithic package; “Both humanity itself and cultural/anatomical modernity were African developments.”
5. **Sedentism and farming.** Early Holocene Nile–Sahara sedentism and specialised foraging precede widespread herding/cultivation; “Neolithic” refused as a uniform African stage; indigenous and introduced crops/animals both matter regionally ([[nabta-playa]], [[dhar-tichitt]], Kintampo, etc.).
6. **Iron without bronze.** Most of sub-Saharan Africa lacks a distinct Bronze/Copper Age; iron is the first metal widely used; farming and iron often co-appear south of the equator.
7. **Chifumbaze / Bantu.** Early Iron Age farming package of eastern–central–southern Africa framed as [[chifumbaze-complex]]; **associated with** early Bantu-speaking communities — not equated (four-way non-identity).
8. **Second millennium.** Local resource bases and political centralisation often precede long-distance trade as prime mover (West African kingdoms, interlacustrine, western Bantu); [[great-zimbabwe]] as capital controlling territories/resources with Indian Ocean links; elite/trade research bias acknowledged.
9. **Epilogue.** African archaeology is of world-wide relevance; indigenous scholarship and multi-disciplinary collaboration (incl. genetics) are rising; publication quantity/quality pressures noted (as of 2005).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*African Archaeology* (3rd ed.) is the wiki’s primary **deep-time continental survey** of Africa — from Plio-Pleistocene hominids through MSA/LSA, Holocene food production, iron-age farming expansions, and second-millennium polities to the colonial threshold. Where [[connah-african-civilizations-2016]] densifies **urbanism and states** over roughly the last six millennia, Phillipson supplies the **lithic and economic prehistory** that Connah largely presupposes, and a pan-African frame for modernity origins that aligns with [[recent-african-origin]] / African-assembly readings of [[behavioral-modernity]] against Eurocentric Upper Palaeolithic checklists. Methodologically the book is a textbook-handbook synthesis (no `studies/` page): all chronologies and culture-historical labels are **date-stamped to a ~2003 secondary horizon** and must be densified by site monographs, later absolute dating, and post-2005 aDNA (e.g. [[reich-who-we-are-2018]] on [[bantu-expansion]]). Standing cautions enforced at ingest: industry ≠ people ≠ language ≠ genes; ethnographic analogy for rock art flagged; elite/trade visibility bias for late periods explicit in the source itself.
