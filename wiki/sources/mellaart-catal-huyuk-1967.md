# Çatal Hüyük: A Neolithic Town in Anatolia (Mellaart 1967)

James Mellaart’s popular-scholarly monograph on the 1961–1965 excavations at [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] (Konya Plain, central Anatolia), published in the *New Aspects of Archaeology* series (general editor Mortimer Wheeler; Thames & Hudson / McGraw-Hill, 1967). The book made the site world-famous: dense mudbrick “town,” elaborate “shrines,” wall-paintings, plaster reliefs, goddess imagery, secondary burial, and early farming economy. It remains the baseline primary synthesis that later Hodder-project work renegotiates.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Mellaart 1967 is the **founding public and scholarly image** of Çatalhöyük: a seventh-millennium Anatolian “town” with roof-access cellular plan, a high fraction of elaborately decorated “shrines,” goddess-and-bull iconography, secondary burial with vulture excarnation, early textiles and metals, and Helbaek/Perkins economic specialist results. Extraction (~218 claims across three ranges) confirms the book is a complete illustrated monograph (~55k words), not a partial conversion.

For this wiki the ingest **anchors the baseline that Hodder restudied**. Site page [[catalhoyuk]] now carries a full Mellaart densification alongside existing Hodder/Mithen/Cauvin/Roberts layers; [[mellaart-catal-huyuk-study]] and Studies Hub treat the 1961–65 campaign as a research act with `replication_status: reanalyzed-contested`; [[catalhoyuk-shrine-house-debate]] holds shrine dualism vs practice continuum as open positions; [[mother-goddess]] gains primary-type-image history from Mellaart rather than only Cauvin. Absolute dates stay attributed to the mid-1960s C-14 framework. Cranial “race” typology is quarantined. Hacılar enters as a comparandum stub.

**What this source does not finish:** virgin soil, workshop quarter, calibrated chronology, modern zooarchaeology/aDNA, or a non-dualist building biography — those remain Hodder-project and future specialist monographs.
