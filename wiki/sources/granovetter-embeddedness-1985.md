# Economic Action and Social Structure: The Problem of Embeddedness (Granovetter, 1985)

Mark S. Granovetter, "Economic Action and Social Structure: The Problem of Embeddedness," *American Journal of Sociology* 91, no. 3 (November 1985): 481–510. Reprinted as Appendix D in *[[granovetter-getting-a-job-1974|Getting a Job]]* 2nd ed. (1995). Charter theoretical statement of the **[[new-economic-sociology|new economic sociology]]** and of the network reading of [[embeddedness]].

## Bibliographic note

Full text extracted from GAJ 2nd ed. Appendix D (cache lines ~8171–9540). Drafts written at IAS and Harvard; support from IAS, Guggenheim, NSF SPI 81-65055; early readers include Wayne Baker, Albert Hirschman, Charles Perrow, Theda Skocpol, [[harrison-white|Harrison White]], among others (fn 1).

## Source type

`source_type: theoretical-work`. Programmatic essay: critiques under-socialized (classical/neoclassical atomized self-interest) and over-socialized (Wrong 1961; Parsonsian internalized norms) conceptions of action; argues both ironically share atomization and neglect **ongoing structures of social relations**; proposes embeddedness as the middle path; illustrates via trust/malfeasance and a critique of Oliver Williamson's markets-and-hierarchies program.

## Core argument (attributed — Granovetter 1985)

1. **Definition:** "the behavior and institutions to be analyzed are so constrained by ongoing social relations that to construe them as independent is a grievous misunderstanding."
2. **Against the modernization break:** level of embeddedness is *lower* in nonmarket societies than substantivists claim, has *changed less* with modernization than they believe, but has *always been and remains more substantial* than formalists and economists allow.
3. **Trust and malfeasance:** networks of past dealings (more than general reputation, contracts, or generalized morality) mainly produce trust in economic life; the same trust enables large-scale malfeasance (vulnerability, teams, coalitions).
4. **Markets and firms:** anonymous markets "virtually nonexistent"; order and disorder flow more from **relation structures** than from org form alone; vertical integration "pressures" are not functionalist guarantees (critique of Williamson).
5. **GAJ link:** cites *Getting a Job* (1974) — interfirm mobility and spanning contacts transmit employee information across firm boundaries.
6. **Program:** treat markets as central sociological objects; broaden rational-action analysis with social structure; Weberian economic action as social action → [[new-economic-sociology]].

## Reliability and limits

Theoretical essay with selective empirical illustrations; not a hypothesis test. "Embeddedness" later stretched across incompatible senses (Krippner and others) — semantic history on the concept page. Ingest path is book appendix, not independent AJS OCR.

## Volume synthesis note

Read with *Getting a Job* as the labor-market exemplar the essay retrospectively theorizes: Ch. 1 of GAJ already names job-finding as "heavily embedded in other social processes"; the 1985 paper generalizes that insight against economics and mid-century sociology alike.
