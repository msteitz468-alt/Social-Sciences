# Structural Anthropology (1958)

The programmatic anthology of [[structuralism|structural anthropology]] — Lévi-Strauss's methodological manifesto, gathering the essays in which he set out the structural method for kinship, language, myth, magic, art, and social organization. It contains the field-defining statements: the **[[atom-of-kinship|atom of kinship]]** (the avunculate within a four-term structure), the structural-linguistics model, **"The Structural Study of Myth"** (the Oedipus analysis), **"The Effectiveness of Symbols"** ([[effectiveness-of-symbols|symbolic efficacy]]), and the "Social Structure" essay on mechanical vs statistical models.

## Key Claims Extracted

All attributed to Lévi-Strauss:

- **The [[atom-of-kinship|atom of kinship]]:** the elementary unit of kinship is not the biological family but a structure of four terms (brother, sister, father/husband, son/nephew) linked by two pairs of correlative oppositions; the avunculate is intelligible only within it. Against Radcliffe-Brown's "elementary family" as the building block — "it is not the families (isolated terms) which are truly 'elementary,' but... the relations between those terms."
- **The structural-linguistics model:** kinship, myth, ritual are "special languages" analysable like phonology (Troubetzkoy's four operations: shift to the unconscious infrastructure; relations not terms; the concept of system; general laws). See [[structuralism]], [[binary-opposition]].
- **The structural study of [[myth]]:** myth is language with a third temporal register; its units are **mythemes** ("gross constituent units") read as *bundles of relations*; "the purpose of myth is to provide a logical model capable of overcoming a contradiction" (the Oedipus autochthony analysis); a myth is "all its versions."
- **The [[effectiveness-of-symbols|effectiveness of symbols]]:** the Cuna childbirth song cures because "the shaman provides the sick woman with a language" for otherwise inexpressible states; the "shamanistic complex" (shaman, patient, group); the psychoanalysis analogy.
- **"Social Structure":** social structure is not empirical reality but **models** built from it; **mechanical vs statistical**, conscious vs unconscious models; society as communication at three levels (women/kinship, goods/economics, messages/language) — the "order of orders."
- **"Do Dual Organizations Exist?":** apparent moiety dualism (diametric) often masks a more complex **concentric/triadic** reality; native models are "rationalizations," not the real structure.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Structural Anthropology* is the keystone of the wiki's structuralism holdings: where the *[[levi-strauss-elementary-structures-1949|Elementary Structures]]* applied the method to kinship and *[[levi-strauss-tristes-tropiques-1955|Tristes Tropiques]]* narrated its origins, this volume states the **method itself** across every domain. It grounds four concept pages the vault previously lacked ([[atom-of-kinship]], [[effectiveness-of-symbols]], and the myth/models apparatus) and gives [[structuralism]] its programmatic core. Read with *[[levi-strauss-savage-mind-1962|The Savage Mind]]* (1962), which extends the same logic to classification and cognition, it completes the theoretical spine of Lévi-Strauss's structuralism in this collection (only *Mythologiques* now remains absent).
