# Pauketat — *Chiefdoms and Other Archaeological Delusions* (AltaMira, 2007)

Programmatic theoretical monograph (Issues in Eastern Woodlands Archaeology series, edited by Emerson & Pauketat) attacking the neo-evolutionary [[chiefdom]] concept and the [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]] as "archaeological delusions," and proposing **[[historical-processualism]]** in their place. Structure: Intro; 1 Principles and Principals; 2 A Crisis in Mississippian Archaeology; 3 Breaking the Law of Cultural Dominance; 4 Parsing Mississippian Chiefdoms; 5 The X Factor; 6 Yoffee's Rule and Cahokia; 7 What Constitutes Civilization?; 8 Truth, Justice, and the Archaeological Way.

## Claims

- **The program:** [[historical-processualism]] named and stated — anti-institutional ontology ("the state is always in a state of becoming"), trajectories not types as comparative units, ordinary practice causing chiefly ascent, the "X factor" (Phillips, Ford & Griffin 1951, repurposed) as migration-driven cultural fusion. → new theory page.
- **Chiefdom concept dismantled:** genealogy (Oberg/Service/Fried; Polynesian perfection; simple/complex; pristine/secondary), 1970s redistribution falsification, "return chiefdom to a purely descriptive usage." → [[chiefdom]], [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]] (Law of Cultural Dominance verbatim), [[chiefly-cycling]] (Anderson tautology critique).
- **Yoffee's Rule** rejected as "an arbitrary yardstick"; Lekson's counter-corollary; Yoffee, Fish & Milner's four early-state criteria applied to Cahokia; Yoffee's pers.-comm. concession (city yes, archaic state doubtful). → [[yoffee-norman]], [[cahokia-population-urbanism-debate]], [[state-formation]].
- **Cahokia empirics:** metroplex anatomy (Cahokia + East St. Louis + St. Louis), Lohmann-phase crux of the Milner dispute, Richland complex, replacement/ruralization, Mound 72 dietary isotopes, refined Pax Cahokiana (violence at places not people, 1050–1200). → [[cahokia]].
- **Site chapters:** Etowah radiocarbon problem + Wilbanks/Morning Star reading; Moundville 1225 redesign + hegemony-mechanism positions; Toltec (Rolingson vs. Nassaney); Spiro as Cahokia-influenced periphery; comparative Chaco/Paquimé/Paso de la Amada/San Lorenzo/Monte Albán/Uruk figures. → [[etowah]], [[moundville]], [[toltec-mounds]], [[spiro]], [[chaco-canyon]], [[mississippian-culture]] (Mississippianization/ethnoscape; wall-trench positions).

**Register warning:** Ch. 1–2 and 8 stage debates through fictional composite characters ("Dr. Science," "the Southern Pragmatist," "Uncertain Graduate Student" — device from Flannery 1976); their dialogue is Pauketat's rhetorical staging, never reportage of named scholars. Pauketat reports the Cahokia debate has "turned political" (citation refusals, blocked collections access) and is himself a faction member — all programmatic claims held in attributed voice.
