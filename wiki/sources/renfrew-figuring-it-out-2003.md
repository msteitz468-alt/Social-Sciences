# Figuring It Out (Renfrew 2003)

Colin Renfrew's *Figuring It Out: What Are We? Where Do We Come From? The Parallel Visions of Artists and Archaeologists* (Thames & Hudson, 2003; ISBN 0-500-05114-3) is a cognitive-archaeology and material-culture program statement written as a dialogue between **prehistoric archaeology** and **contemporary visual art**. Expanded from the six **2001 Rhind Lectures** (Society of Antiquaries of Scotland, 11–13 May, National Museum of Scotland; lecture title *Art as Archaeology and Archaeology as Art: The construction of the world through material culture*), it asks Gauguin's Tahiti questions and argues that archaeologists and modern artists converge on understanding the human condition through **active engagement with the material world**.

## Central Theses (attributed — Renfrew 2003)

- **Parallel visions:** contemporary artists and archaeologists share a research program into what it is to be human, pursued through material action and material traces rather than beauty-as-representation alone; the task of ascribing meaning to material culture is "the same," "no analogy."
- **[[sapient-paradox]]:** anatomically and (largely) cognitively modern *Homo sapiens* is present tens of millennia before sedentism, farming, cities, metallurgy, and writing — the delay is the puzzle a material-engagement account must solve.
- **[[material-engagement]] / material-symbolic stage:** new forms of mind and society arise from sustained interaction with artefacts; material culture is **constitutive** of symbolic relations (property, value, deity images), not a passive reflection of pre-existing ideas.
- **Revision of [[donald-merlin|Merlin Donald]]:** insert **material-symbolic** culture between mythic and theoretic; pre-writing ESS as "analogue" storage.
- **Archaeology of now / baneful signs:** writing and modern sign systems are powerful [[external-symbolic-storage]] but also reshape (and can dominate or dematerialize) experience.
- **Epistemic stance:** realist about the past; active interpreter of material remains.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Figuring It Out* is the vault's first primary densification of Renfrew's **cognitive + material-engagement** program in his own 2000s voice (beyond Renfrew & Bahn textbook pedagogy and secondary Mithen/Johnson layers). Three concepts now carry the load: the **sapient paradox** (problem), **material engagement** (mechanism), and **external symbolic storage** (Donald term expanded archaeologically). The Donald revision (material-symbolic phase) is the book's sharpest theoretical contribution relative to *Origins of the Modern Mind*. The art–archaeology parallel is method and rhetoric as much as theory — it should not be flattened into "Renfrew liked sculpture," but kept as a claim about shared tasks of meaning-ascription. Coverage gap remaining: Malafouris MET monographs; primary Donald; full *Prehistory: The Making of the Human Mind* (2007) if a further Renfrew densify is wanted.
