# Masculinities (R. W. Connell, 1995; 2nd ed. 2005)

R. W. Connell's *Masculinities* is the founding synthesis of the critical
sociology of men and masculinities. It reframes masculinity not as a fixed
character type or a "sex role" but as a **configuration of practice within a
structure of gender relations**, and introduces the concept of
[[hegemonic-masculinity|hegemonic masculinity]] — the culturally exalted
pattern of masculinity that legitimates the [[patriarchal-dividend|patriarchal
dividend]] men collectively draw from the subordination of women — alongside a
relational typology of **subordinated, complicit, and marginalized**
masculinities. The book couples this framework ([[gender-relations-theory|
theory of gender relations]]) with a life-history study of four groups of
Australian men and a historical-political argument about masculinity in world
history.

Note: **"emphasized femininity" does not appear in this book** — it belongs to Connell's *[[connell-gender-and-power-1987|Gender and Power]]* (1987), now ingested.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Masculinities* is best read as the second panel of a diptych whose first panel (*[[connell-gender-and-power-1987|Gender and Power]]*, 1987) states the structural theory of gender; this volume fills in the diversity of masculinities that the structural theory "demanded." With both panels ingested, the vault holds the full framework — relational theory ([[gender-relations-theory]]), [[hegemonic-masculinity]] + [[emphasized-femininity]], [[patriarchal-dividend]], [[gender-order]], [[cathexis]], [[body-reflexive-practice]], and the empirical anchor ([[connell-masculinities-lifehistory-study]]).

The book sits in productive tension with the vault's existing gender coverage. Where [[hochschild-arlie-russell|Hochschild]]'s [[gender-strategy]] and [[patriarchal-alchemy]] work at the micro level of the couple and the household economy of emotion, Connell works at the level of structure and the field of masculinities — the two are complementary scales of one problem (the household division of labour is Connell's "production" structure seen up close). Where the vault's [[practice-theory|Bourdieusian practice theory]] supplies [[habitus]] as the mechanism of embodied disposition, Connell's [[body-reflexive-practice]] is a parallel, independently derived attempt to solve the body/practice problem for gender. And the critique of [[social-role|sex-role theory]] directly engages the vault's Parsonian [[structural-functionalism]] material, extending the standing consensus/conflict tension into the domain of gender. The chief caution the book itself insists on: every theoretical claim here is Connell's, in attributed voice, and the empirical base is four small Australian groups — a foundational framework, not a settled body of findings.
