## Volume Synthesis Note

This 2013 symposium volume is the primary densification source for Levantine [[natufian-culture|Natufian]] research after the 1991 Bar-Yosef & Valla corpus. It does **not** offer an editorial grand synthesis (preface explicitly refuses one); instead it multiplies site-level datasets and frames open theoretical disputes.

**What it adds to the wiki.** (1) A full **culture page densification** replacing textbook-thin chronology (~12.5–10.5 kya) with calibrated schemes (~15.5–11 ka cal BP range across Henry/Grosman). (2) **Site pages** for Eynan, el-Wad, Hayonim, Wadi Hammeh 27, Raqefet, Hilazon with chapter-level architecture, burial, fauna, and exchange detail. (3) Two standing **debates**: origins/core–periphery (Belfer-Cohen & Goring-Morris multi-tradition complex vs classic homeland vs Henry dual cores) and Younger Dryas causation (classic stress models vs Henry disconnect/cold-not-drought vs habitat-mosaic counter-cases). (4) Method/concept densification for [[sedentism]], [[younger-dryas]], [[commensalism-archaeology]], [[broad-spectrum-revolution]], [[epipaleolithic]], and the Natufian path into [[agricultural-revolution]].

**Tensions with already-ingested sources.** Wenke & Olszewski (2007) and Mithen (1996) remain useful for classroom framing and cognitive “point of no return,” but their dates and uniform YD→farming narratives are now explicitly positioned against 2013 multiproxy and multi-author contestation. Henry’s specialized gazelle/cereal model and Bar-Oz et al.’s cultural gazelle preference both push back on pure [[broad-spectrum-revolution]] prime-mover stories.

**Coverage gaps still open.** aDNA; full 1991 volume; Barker/Cauvin/Bellwood agricultural-origins monographs; systematic starch programs the preface requests.
