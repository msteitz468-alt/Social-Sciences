# The Gender of the Gift

[[strathern-marilyn|Marilyn Strathern]]'s *The Gender of the Gift* (1988) is a landmark theoretical ethnography of Melanesia that re-reads gift exchange, gender, and personhood against Western anthropological and feminist categories. ## Reliability and use

Comparative Melanesian synthesis grounded in Strathern's Hagen fieldwork and a wide ethnographic literature. **Attributed voice** for all theoretical claims (dividual, gift/commodity inverse, domination). Do not treat Melanesia as a single society. Distinct from Mauss *[[mauss-the-gift-1925|The Gift]]* — builds on and transforms the gift concept.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Gender of the Gift* is a dual intervention: Part One dismantles Highlands sexual-antagonism, domestic/public, and exploitation frames by alternating anthropological and feminist questions; Part Two builds an apparatus (gift/commodity root metaphors, [[dividual]], [[mediated-unmediated-exchange]], gender as alternation of sociality) for reading Melanesian gift economies; the conclusion redefines domination as particular acts of excess and comparison as controlled fiction. It does not settle prevalence of inequality; it refuses Western universals as neutral tools.
