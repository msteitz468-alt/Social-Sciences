# Legacies: The Story of the Immigrant Second Generation (Portes & Rumbaut, 2001)

[[portes-alejandro|Alejandro Portes]] and [[rumbaut-ruben|Rubén G. Rumbaut]]'s major report on the **Children of Immigrants Longitudinal Study (CILS)** and the theory of [[segmented-assimilation|segmented assimilation]]. The book combines (1) real-name-fictionalized family vignettes, (2) census/CPS portraits of the immigrant-stock population, (3) the CILS student panel and parental survey in southern Florida and southern California, and (4) a policy-oriented conclusion contrasting nativism, forceful assimilationism, and selective acculturation.

Study object: [[portes-rumbaut-legacies-study]]. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/portes-rumbaut-legacies-study|Studies Hub]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Legacies* is simultaneously a **theory book** (ch. 3 [[segmented-assimilation]]), a **methods and data book** ([[children-of-immigrants-longitudinal-study|CILS]] design and questionnaires), and a **policy polemic** (ch. 10 against nativism and forceful assimilationism). Its lasting contribution to the wiki is the closed loop between first-generation [[modes-of-incorporation]], intergenerational acculturation type ([[selective-acculturation]] / [[dissonant-acculturation]] / [[consonant-acculturation]]), and adolescent language, identity, family climate, and school outcomes.

Empirically, the CILS portrait is **optimistic at the mean** (English mastery; dropout rates below native-parentage district averages) and **pessimistic at the tails and trajectories** (nationality risk sets; time-in-U.S. erosion of drive; reactive identity under hostile politics; Mexican case as potential anchor of downward path). The two "paradoxes" (Southeast Asian grades; Cuban public schools) are load-bearing for the theory: they show that residual nationality effects after individual controls still map onto reception history and community structure.

For the collection: first major primary ingest on contemporary U.S. immigration / second generation; seeds [[segmented-assimilation-debate]] against classical and (uningested) neo-assimilation positions. Adult CILS waves and Alba/Nee-type counters are natural follow-ons.
