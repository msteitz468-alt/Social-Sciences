# Reflections on Fieldwork in Morocco (Rabinow 1977)

Short reflexive book (University of California Press "Quantum Books" series; ~160 pp.)
by American anthropologist [[rabinow-paul|Paul Rabinow]], recounting his doctoral
fieldwork in and around **Sefrou** and the Middle Atlas Berber village of
**[[sidi-lahcen-lyussi]]**, Morocco (1968–1969), as a sustained meditation on what
fieldwork *is*. Its organizing thesis, borrowed from Paul Ricoeur, is that ethnographic
understanding is **"the comprehension of the self by the detour of the comprehension of
the other"** — data are not collected but **intersubjectively and dialogically
constructed** between anthropologist and informant, both of whom are historically
situated. Written from within the [[interpretive-anthropology|Geertzian interpretive]]
milieu (Rabinow was a student in the Chicago Sefrou project) yet pushing past it toward
the reflexive/dialogical turn later crystallized in *[[clifford-marcus-writing-culture-1986|Writing Culture]]*.

See [[rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]] for analysis of the research act; deep
treatment in [[hubs/studies/rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]].

## Key claims extracted (attributed to Rabinow 1977 unless noted)

- **Facts are made, not found.** Ethnographic data are "doubly mediated" — by the
  anthropologist's presence and by the "doubling of consciousness" that questioning forces
  on the informant. → [[reflexive-fieldwork]], [[rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]]
- **Fieldwork is intersubjective construction of liminal communication**; understanding is
  a "moving ratio" that "never reaches identity." → [[reflexive-fieldwork]], [[liminality]]
- **Symbolic violence** against informants' integrity is "inherent in the structure of the
  situation" (the Sultan-exile extraction via Ali). → [[rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]]
- **The informant as "insider's outsider"** — the best informants (Ali, Malik) are marginal
  figures able to objectify their own culture for a foreigner. → [[sidi-lahcen-lyussi]]
- **Against "the primitive"**: "There is no 'primitive.' There are other men, living other
  lives"; Otherness is "not an ineffable essence, but rather the sum of different historical
  experiences." → [[othering]]
- **Hermeneutic method** after [[ricoeur-paul|Ricoeur]]: "the comprehension of the self by
  the detour of the comprehension of the other"; culture as [[webs-of-significance|webs of
  significance]] both parties spin (after [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]). → [[reflexive-fieldwork]]
- **Fieldwork setting** (findings): 1968–69 doctoral fieldwork under [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]]'s
  Sefrou project; the Middle Atlas village of [[sidi-lahcen-lyussi]] (~900 people, four saintly
  lineages, active saint's *musem*); a sequence of interlocutors — Maurice Richard, Ibrahim,
  Ali, Malik (Abd al-Malik ben Lahcen), Driss ben Mohammed. → [[sidi-lahcen-lyussi]],
  [[rabinow-reflections-morocco-study]]
- **Foreword** ([[bellah-robert-n|Bellah]]): frames the book hermeneutically; the "self"
  comprehended is the *cultural*, not psychological, self; Weberian [[verstehen|value-neutrality]]
  as a moral norm. → [[bellah-robert-n]]
- **Bibliographic** (findings): self-cited theoretical foundations —
  [[berger-peter-l|Berger]] & [[luckmann-thomas|Luckmann]], [[geertz-clifford|Geertz]],
  [[ricoeur-paul|Ricoeur]], [[schutz-alfred|Schutz]], Sartre, Duvignaud; companion monograph
  *Symbolic Domination* (1975).
