## Volume Synthesis Note

This volume is the wiki’s primary carrier for Spivak’s landmark essay and its first-wave scholarly afterlife. It does three jobs at once: (1) stabilizes the **textual object** by printing both 1988 and 1999 versions plus Spivak’s 2010 self-commentary; (2) documents **reception as positions** rather than a single correct reading; (3) extends the question into slavery, war graves, migrant reproductive labor, and indigenous Latin America without claiming those extensions are the same “subaltern.”

For the wiki’s two-layer design, the volume is almost pure **knowledge-layer** input (theory, concept, thinker, debate, study) with subject-matter cases only as attributed illustrations (*sati* discourse; Bhubaneswari; FDWs; EZLN). It strengthens [[postcolonialism]] beyond the Said/*Orientalism* anchor already in the Theory Hub, and creates the standing debate page the field’s classroom life requires. Gaps remaining: full Guha monographs, Bhabha’s *Location of Culture*, and a Thinkers Hub Spivak biography (study hub built; thinker hub deferred pending broader corpus).
