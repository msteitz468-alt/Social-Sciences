## Volume Synthesis Note

*Catching Fire* is the vault's first primary charter for **biological-anthropological** diet-and-origins theory. It installs a named [[cooking-hypothesis]] program that splits human evolution into two dietary revolutions — meat (plus pounding) for habilines; cooking for *Homo erectus* — and ties both to the [[expensive-tissue-hypothesis]] and to a social package ([[cooking-and-pair-bonding]], reworked [[sexual-division-of-labor]]).

Relative to already-ingested material, the book densifies the forager and hunting neighborhood from the *other* direction: *[[lee-devore-man-the-hunter-1968|Man the Hunter]]* and the [[hunting-hypothesis]] supplied the 1960s meat-and-sharing charter and the feminist counter-story ([[man-the-hunter-woman-the-gatherer-debate]]); Wrangham accepts meat's early role, rejects it as sufficient for the *erectus* package, and makes **cooking** the missing condition for long male hunts and household exchange. Food studies already hold Mintz's historical-anthropological commodity circuit (*[[mintz-sweetness-and-power-1985|Sweetness and Power]]*); Wrangham supplies deep-time biology and nutrition rather than Atlantic political economy.

What to watch in later ingests: specialist revision of fire dates (Gesher Benot Ya’aqov and Lower Paleolithic claims); peer-reviewed cooking-energy literature; competing social-evolutionary packages (cooperative breeding, grandmother hypothesis); gender critique of the "protection racket" / patriarchy thesis. Theory Hub and Thinkers Hub remain warranted-but-deferred until more of Wrangham's academic corpus is primary-ingested.
