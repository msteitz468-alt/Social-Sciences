Bibliographic record for [[sassen-saskia|Saskia Sassen]]'s *The Global City: New York, London, Tokyo*, second edition (Princeton University Press, 2001; first edition 1991). ## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Global City* (2nd ed.) is both a **founding theoretical program** and a **comparative empirical study** of New York, London, and Tokyo. The 2001 edition retests the 1991 framework against 1990s evidence and a decade of critique. Core move: economic globalization does not dissolve cities; it reconstitutes a subset of them as production sites for specialized services and finance that make cross-border control possible. Dispersal and centralization are joint. Leading cities form networks, not a single capital. Growth dynamics press toward high/low valuation and informalization without requiring middle-class disappearance or sole globalization causation.

**What it adds to this wiki:** (1) first full [[global-city-theory]] + Theory Hub; (2) landmark [[sassen-global-city-study]] + Studies Hub; (3) concept kit ([[global-city]], [[producer-services]], [[practices-of-global-control]], [[transnational-urban-system]], [[spatial-polarization]]); (4) live [[global-city-model-debate]]; (5) bridge from Chicago/Wilson/Massey urban sociology to globalization and economic restructuring; (6) dense update to [[globalization]] and [[urbanization]] phenomena.

**Tensions / limits:** three Northern financial centers; official stats undercount informal work; state/politics of global finance underdeveloped (author’s concession); polarization magnitude still contested; world-city network measurement literature and Sassen’s earlier/later monographs not yet ingested. Adjacent but not identical to [[world-systems-analysis]] (cities as scales vs world-economy as unit).
