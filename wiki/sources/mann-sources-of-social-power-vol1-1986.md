# The Sources of Social Power, Volume I (Mann 1986)

[[mann-michael|Michael Mann]]'s first volume of a multi-volume history and theory of power: from the rejection of unitary "society" through the [[iemp-model|IEMP model]] (ideological, economic, military, political power networks) to a narrative of agrarian power from prehistory to A.D. 1760. Companion volumes (industrial societies; general theory) are outside this ingest.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Mann’s Vol. I is a **program-founding synthesis**: a neo-Weberian organizational theory of power (overlapping [[power-networks]]; [[iemp-model|IEMP]]) applied to a secondary-source history of agrarian societies from the evasion of power in prehistory to the eve of industrial England. Its load-bearing contributions to this wiki are theoretical and conceptual, not new primary facts about Mesopotamia or Rome. The anti-totality thesis reorganizes how [[the-state]], [[state-formation]], class, and religion are linked without reducing them to a single master unit (contra nation-state sociology, pure [[historical-materialism]], and early-modern readings of [[world-systems-analysis]] as already fully transnational). The empirical spine — [[social-caging]] and [[multi-power-actor-civilization]] origins; [[empire-of-domination]] and [[compulsory-cooperation]]; Roman territorial empire; transcendent Christian ecumene; European intensive→coordinating→[[organic-national-state]] path with [[normative-pacification]] of Christendom — must stay in **attributed voice** and mid-1980s date-stamps on subject-matter pages.

What the volume adds relative to already-ingested comparative-historical work: a clean **four-source checklist** and the [[despotic-power]]/[[infrastructural-power]] dualism for [[political-sociology]]; a class *phase* model tied to infrastructures rather than mode-of-production succession alone; and an explicit **accident + pattern** account of the “European miracle” that refuses both teleological progress and single-factor late starts. Tensions with Tilly (capital–coercion–states — *Coercion, Capital* still outstanding) and Wallerstein are recorded as positions, not adjudicated. Gaps: Vols. II–IV; gender/household; non-Eurasian depth; post-1986 archaeology/aDNA cannot be backfilled from this source.
