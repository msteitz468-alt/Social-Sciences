# Directions in Sociolinguistics: The Ethnography of Communication (Gumperz & Hymes, eds., 1972)

The founding anthology of the **ethnography of communication** / **ethnography of
speaking** as a coordinated research program. Where Hymes's [[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974|Foundations in Sociolinguistics]] (1974) states the charter as continuous essays, *Directions* assembles the program's empirical and theoretical range across nineteen chapters and an editor's Introduction. It is the volume in which several distinct traditions — the [[ethnography-of-speaking|ethnography of speaking]], [[interactional-sociolinguistics|interactional sociolinguistics]] ([[code-switching|code-switching]]), [[ethnomethodology|ethnomethodology]] and [[conversation-analysis|conversation analysis]], [[variationist-sociolinguistics|variationist sociolinguistics]], and [[restricted-and-elaborated-codes|Bernstein's code theory]] — appear side by side as "directions" out of a shared interactional turn in the study of language.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Directions in Sociolinguistics* is best understood not as a single argument but as a
**map of a field at the moment of its formation**. Its unifying theme, stated in
[[gumperz-john-j|Gumperz's]] Introduction, is the "interactional approach to language
behavior" — the claim that linguistic and social categories are mutually constitutive and
knowable only through situated use, against the "correlational" sociolinguistics that matches
independent linguistic and social facts after the fact. But the volume's lasting importance
is that it gathered, under that banner, several traditions that would soon become distinct
subfields: the [[ethnography-of-speaking|ethnography of speaking]] ([[hymes-dell|Hymes]] ch.1),
[[interactional-sociolinguistics|interactional sociolinguistics]] and
[[code-switching|code-switching]] (Blom & Gumperz ch.14), [[ethnomethodology]] and
[[conversation-analysis|conversation analysis]] (Garfinkel, Sacks, Schegloff, chs.10–12),
[[variationist-sociolinguistics|variationist sociolinguistics]] ([[labov-william|Labov]] ch.19),
Fishman's [[domain-language-choice|domains]] and [[diglossia]] (ch.15), and
[[restricted-and-elaborated-codes|Bernstein's code theory]] (ch.17).

For this wiki the volume does three things. First, it **establishes Gumperz** as a primary
figure (thinker page and [[hubs/thinkers/gumperz-john-j|Thinkers Hub]] biography) alongside
the already-ingested [[hymes-dell|Hymes]], filling a gap the
[[linguistic-anthropology|linguistic-anthropology]] discipline page had explicitly flagged
(Gumperz, Labov, and conversation-analysis primary texts). Second, it supplies the primary
1972 statements behind concepts previously held only through [[hymes-foundations-in-sociolinguistics-1974|Hymes's
1974 *Foundations*]] — so most of its contribution to the ethnography-of-speaking pages is
additive depth, not new scaffolding. Third, it opens two standing debates onto the wiki: the
[[deficit-difference-debate|deficit-vs-difference dispute]] over language, class, and
educability (Bernstein / Labov), and — through the [[blom-gumperz-hemnesberget-study|Hemnesberget
study]] — the now-contested status of the situational/metaphorical code-switching model.

Alongside the programmatic chapters, the volume's ethnographic case studies seeded a set of
contributor and society pages: [[dundes-alan|Alan Dundes]] et al. on Turkish verbal dueling,
[[mitchell-kernan-claudia|Claudia Mitchell-Kernan]] on [[signifying]], Ethel Albert on speech
and rank among the [[rundi|Barundi]], [[frake-charles-o|Charles Frake]] on litigation among
the [[yakan|Yakan]], [[ervin-tripp-susan|Susan Ervin-Tripp]] on sociolinguistic rules, and
[[friedrich-paul|Paul Friedrich]] on Russian [[power-and-solidarity|T/V address]].

The tension worth recording against already-ingested sources is the volume's own internal
one: **Gumperz's interactional pole vs. Labov's and Fishman's correlational/quantitative
pole**. The wiki holds both, attributed, rather than harmonized — the two are genuinely
different research programs sharing a subject, and the Introduction's polemical framing of
"correlational" work should not be mistaken for the wiki's verdict on it.
