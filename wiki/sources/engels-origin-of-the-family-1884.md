# The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State (Engels, 1884)

Friedrich Engels, *Der Ursprung der Familie, des Privateigentums und des Staats* (Hottingen-Zurich, 1884; substantially revised fourth edition, Stuttgart, 1891/1892). Written March–May 1884 after Engels found Marx’s précis of Lewis H. Morgan’s *Ancient Society* (1877); presented as fulfilling Marx’s uncompleted plan to restate Morgan in light of the materialist conception of history. Ingested from the Marxists Internet Archive English text (West/MEW-based).

## What the text is

A short comparative-evolutionary monograph in nine chapters plus two authorial prefaces. It is **not** a field study: ethnographic cases (especially the [[iroquois|Iroquois]]) are taken second-hand from Morgan; Greek, Roman, Celtic, and Germanic material is classical and early-medieval literary history. Every stage sequence, origin of monogamy, and theory of the state is an **attributed** Engels (or Morgan-via-Engels) claim. The book is a hinge between Victorian kinship evolutionism and Marxist theories of the family, private property, and the state.

## Reliability and register

- **Genre:** programmatic historical-materialist reconstruction, not HRAF ethnography. Morgan’s factual material is filtered through Engels’s economic and political conclusions; Engels explicitly claims responsibility for conclusions not expressly Morgan’s.
- **Supersession:** twentieth-century anthropology largely abandoned unilinear savagery→barbarism→civilization and the group-marriage sequence; matriliny ≠ primitive universal mother-right. Record as positions; see [[unilinear-kinship-evolution-debate]].
- **Edition:** fourth-edition revisions (Kovalevsky, family history review) are in the text; do not treat 1884-only formulations as final where 1891 revises them.
- **Sensitive material:** discussions of slavery, prostitution, sexual coercion, and conquest violence are first-class content of Engels’s argument and are retained in attributed form on relevant pages.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Origin* is the hinge between Victorian kinship evolutionism and Marxist theories of gender, property, and the state. Engels’s load-bearing moves are: (1) the **dual production** thesis (means of life + human beings) that expands historical materialism’s object beyond industrial political economy; (2) the **Morgan scaffold** (savagery–barbarism–civilization; family sequence; gens) with Engels’s own economics and classical/Germanic cases; (3) the **monogamy–property–women’s oppression** package (“world-historical defeat”; first class oppression); (4) the **state origin** formula (product of irreconcilable class antagonisms; territory + special public force; normally class organ; exceptional relative independence; withering-away horizon). What the book adds to this wiki is a first systematic treatment of kinship and pre-state political forms on the conflict-tradition side, and a bridge page set for anthropology that the collection had lacked (Morgan, Bachofen, gens, mother-right, Iroquois-as-type-case). What it must not launder into wiki voice is the unilinear ladder itself — mainstream anthropology abandoned that as world history while retaining fragments (clan analysis, property–gender hypotheses) as research programs. Tension with already-ingested sources: Comte’s natural sex hierarchy vs Engels’s historical-defeat thesis (different mechanisms, both attributed); Weber’s means-based state definition vs Engels’s origin-and-function definition (complementary problem-spaces, not the same claim); *Brumaire* relative autonomy as French case vs *Origin* as general exception list.
