# Systems of Consanguinity and Affinity of the Human Family (Morgan, 1871)

## Bibliographic record

Lewis Henry Morgan, *Systems of Consanguinity and Affinity of the Human Family* (Washington: Smithsonian Institution, 1871; Smithsonian Contributions to Knowledge, vol. 17). Reprint used for ingest: University of Nebraska Press, 1997, introduction by Elisabeth Tooker. CIP: GN487.M67 1997; kinship / family.

## What this source is

Landmark **comparative kinship study** that founded kinship terminology as a systematic anthropological object. Morgan distinguishes **descriptive** (Aryan–Semitic–Uralian) from **classificatory** systems (Ganowanian / American Indian, Turanian, Malayan), tabulates schedules of ~200 kin types across many nations, and advances a **conjectural evolutionary solution** (family forms → terminologies) plus an Asiatic-origin argument for American Indians. Dual-file with study page [[morgan-systems-consanguinity-study]] and Studies Hub.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Systems* is the wiki’s charter primary for **kinship as a field**: schedule method, descriptive/classificatory binary, Seneca type case, and the conjectural evolutionary series Engels later materializes. It upgrades Morgan from Engels-mediated figure to primary-sourced methodologist while keeping evolutionary claims in attributed voice and marking Ganowanian/Turanian/Malayan as period constructs. Studies Hub and Thinkers Hub built in-session. Gaps: *Ancient Society*, *League* full text, Trautmann biography, later formal kinship monographs.
