# Pauketat — *Cahokia: Ancient America's Great City on the Mississippi* (Viking, 2009)

Popular-audience synthesis of Cahokia archaeology by Timothy R. Pauketat ([[pauketat-timothy]]), the field's principal maximalist interpreter. Argues that Cahokia was a true city born in a rapid "big bang" around 1050 CE, with mass spectacle, human sacrifice (Mound 72), long-distance emigration/missionization, and political-religious centralization exceeding the "chiefdom" label.

## Claims

Load-bearing extractions (all attributed to Pauketat unless marked [FACT]):

- **Big bang thesis:** Old Cahokia (village of 1,000+) abruptly razed ~1050 CE and New Cahokia built as a planned city — his framing of Wittry's Tract 15A/15B stratigraphy [FACT: Old Cahokia houses 900–1050 beneath New Cahokia construction]. Population 10,000–16,000 + 20,000–30,000 hinterland (his extrapolations). → [[cahokia]], [[cahokia-population-urbanism-debate]].
- **Mound 72 in full** (Fowler 1967–71; Rose's forensic detail on the 39 executed under Feature 229's false floor; the 53-woman pit; beaded falcon burial) [FACT layer] with Pauketat's "political theater" reading (attributed). → [[cahokia]].
- **Chunkey** as material tracer of post-1050 Cahokian expansion; Perino typology, Thebes Gap sourcing (Norris 2005), Emerson's PIMA flintclay sourcing of exported sculptures [FACT] + soft-power missionization reading (attributed). → [[chunkey]], [[spiro]].
- **Mississippian origins at Cahokia** (his mother-culture framing) and the Mesoamerican-connection positions (Brown 2005 consensus quote; Kehoe minority; Hall's shared-background middle view; sole Mesoamerican artifact = Spiro obsidian chip [FACT]). → [[mississippian-culture]].
- **Hinterland and diaspora:** Emerson's replacement hypothesis; Alt's immigration evidence at Halliday; Vacant Quarter (Williams); "Pax Cahokiana" collapse-violence hypothesis (hedged). → [[cahokia]], [[mississippian-culture]].
- **State-in-production claim** and anti-[[chiefdom]] stance. → [[state-formation]], [[chiefdom]].
- **Excavation history / reflexivity:** Brackenridge 1811; Moorehead 1921 vs. the natural-formation claim; Holder's McCarthy-era firing; Wittry's Woodhenge; FAI-270 project; Emerson's gender-historiography point on goddess figurines. → [[cahokia]], [[moundbuilder-myth]].

Ethnographic analogies (Ho-Chunk Red Horn saga via Radin 1908; Sagard's 1630 Iroquois reincarnation ritual; Plains Sun Dance and adoption nose-cutting; Natchez funerary rites) are used heavily and flagged as speculative bridging throughout — never promoted past attributed voice.
