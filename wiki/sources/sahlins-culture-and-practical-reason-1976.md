# Culture and Practical Reason (Sahlins 1976)

Marshall Sahlins's programmatic statement that culture is a **symbolic scheme** irreducible to practical/utilitarian logic — the theoretical counterpart to the substantivist economics of *[[sahlins-stone-age-economics-1972|Stone Age Economics]]* (1972). The book opposes two "paradigms of anthropological theory": **practical reason** (which explains cultural form as the effect of material/biological utility — including Marxist historical materialism and Steward/Harris cultural ecology and materialism) and **cultural reason** (which holds that material forces are organized by a meaningful cultural order). Its polemical targets are utilitarianism in all its guises; its positive claim is that even Western capitalist "economy" is a symbolic order — argued through the analysis of the American food and clothing systems ("La Pensée Bourgeoise").

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Culture and Practical Reason* is the theoretical hinge between Sahlins's economic anthropology (*[[sahlins-stone-age-economics-1972|Stone Age Economics]]*, whose "economy as a category of culture" it universalizes) and his historical anthropology (*[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*, whose symbolic-order premise it establishes). Its single argument — that [[cultural-reason]] encompasses [[practical-reason]], so that "it is culture which constitutes utility" — is directed against every materialist and utilitarian program the wiki already holds: [[historical-materialism]], [[structural-marxism]], [[cultural-materialism]], and [[cultural-ecology]]. Its most-cited move, "[[la-pensee-bourgeoise]]," turns the anthropological gaze back on the West, reading American food and clothing as a symbolic/totemic code and coining "bourgeois totemism" (see [[totemism]]). The book thus completes the wiki's picture of the mid-century **practical-reason vs. cultural-reason** split (see [[disciplines/anthropology|anthropology]]) from the culturalist side, as [[harris-marvin|Harris]]'s *Rise of Anthropological Theory* states it from the materialist side — the two are direct antagonists. Tension with already-ingested sources is sharpest against Harris's cultural materialism and against the [[rappaport-roy-a|Rappaport]]/[[cultural-ecology|ecological]] program, which Sahlins names "ecology fetishism."
