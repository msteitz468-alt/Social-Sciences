# Street Corner Society: The Social Structure of an Italian Slum

Bibliographic record for [[whyte-william-foote|William Foote Whyte]]'s *Street
Corner Society* (1943; ingested from the 4th edition, 1993). The analysis of the
research act lives on the [[whyte-street-corner-society-study|study page]] and the
[[hubs/studies/whyte-street-corner-society-study|Studies Hub]] deep analysis; the
community documented lives on [[cornerville]].

## What It Is

A book-length [[participant-observation]] ethnography of "Cornerville" (the North
End of Boston), based on Whyte's 1936–1940 Harvard Junior Fellowship fieldwork.
Structure: Introduction; Part I (Corner Boys and College Boys — the Nortons; Chick
Morelli's club; social mobility); Part II (Racketeers and Politicians — the policy
racket and police; the Cornerville S. and A. Club; ward politics); Part III
(Conclusion). The 1955 second edition added **Appendix A, "On the Evolution of
Street Corner Society,"** Whyte's canonical account of his own fieldwork; the 1993
fourth edition added a "fifty years later" section (with the Boelen rejoinder) and
**Appendix B**, Angelo Ralph Orlandella's insider memoir.

## Coverage

- **Discipline / era:** sociology and social anthropology, fieldwork-revolution
  era; a founding case of urban participant observation.
- **Region / period:** the North End of Boston, late Depression (1936–40).
- **Method:** immersed participant observation plus, for the larger club,
  systematic positional mapmaking and pair-event/set-event interaction tabulation.

## Reliability Notes

See frontmatter. In brief: a primary ethnographic source whose descriptive
findings are strong for the fieldwork moment but pseudonymized and, since 1992,
contested as to production (Italian-language competence, member-checking, informant
ethics). The methodological appendices are indispensable but are self-authored
defenses and must be read as such. Sociograms in the original (S. & A. Club
informal-organization charts; "fixing a pinch"/"park fence" diagrams) are diagrams
not captured in the ingested text.
