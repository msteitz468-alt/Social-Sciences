# States and Social Revolutions (Skocpol, 1979)

Bibliographic record for Theda Skocpol's *States and Social Revolutions: A Comparative Analysis of France, Russia, and China* (Cambridge University Press, 1979). The founding statement of the **state-centered, structural theory of [[social-revolution]]** and a charter text of comparative-historical sociology. Author: [[skocpol-theda]]. Study page: [[skocpol-states-social-revolutions-study]]; Studies Hub deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/skocpol-states-social-revolutions-study]].

Dedicated "for Bill" (Bill Skocpol). Written out of Skocpol's Harvard dissertation, supervised in the milieu of [[moore-barrington|Barrington Moore Jr.]]'s comparative-historical program. The book argues that the great social revolutions are best explained not by ideology or the intentions of revolutionary actors but by the structural conjunction of (a) old-regime **state** crises under international/military pressure and (b) agrarian class structures that gave peasants autonomy and leverage against landlords — with revolutionary outcomes shaped above all by **state-building** in the wake of collapse.
