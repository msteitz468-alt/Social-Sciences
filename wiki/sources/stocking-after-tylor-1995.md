# After Tylor: British Social Anthropology 1888–1951 (Stocking, 1995)

[[stocking-george-w|George W. Stocking, Jr.]]'s intellectual and institutional history of British anthropology across the decades between the heyday of [[social-evolutionism|social evolutionism]] and the establishment of [[structural-functionalism]] in the 1930s–40s — the sequel to *[[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Victorian Anthropology]]* (1987). Taking as its starting point [[tylor-edward-burnett|E. B. Tylor]]'s 1888 comparative essay "On a Method of Investigating the Development of Institutions" (the "adhesions" paper, the first statistical [[comparative-method|cross-cultural correlation]] study), it traces: the tension between the evolutionary paradigm and missionary ethnography ([[fison-lorimer|Fison]] in Australia, [[codrington-robert-henry|Codrington]] in Melanesia); the heterodox Scottish evolutionists ([[lang-andrew|Andrew Lang]], [[robertson-smith-william|W. Robertson Smith]]); the birth of an academic fieldwork tradition ([[spencer-baldwin|Baldwin Spencer]] and [[gillen-frank|Gillen]] in Central Australia, [[haddon-a-c|Haddon]] and the [[torres-strait-expedition-study|Torres Strait Expedition]]); the crisis in evolutionary theory around 1900 (the "**Frazerian moment**" — [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]], [[westermarck-edward|Westermarck]], [[marett-r-r|Marett]]); the diffusionist alternative ([[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]], [[elliot-smith-grafton|Elliot Smith]], [[perry-w-j|Perry]], [[hocart-a-m|Hocart]]); the fieldwork-to-functionalism transition ([[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]]); the delimitation of social anthropology ([[radcliffe-brown-a-r|Radcliffe-Brown]]); and the institutional consolidation under colonial and Rockefeller patronage.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*After Tylor* completes the arc Stocking began in *[[stocking-victorian-anthropology-1987|Victorian Anthropology]]*: where the earlier volume showed how sociocultural evolutionism was assembled around 1860, this one traces its dissolution and the emergence of the fieldwork-and-functionalism tradition that displaced it. Stocking's central interpretive moves — all held here in **attributed voice** — reshape several pages in this wiki. First, the "**Frazerian moment**": the crisis of evolutionary anthropology around 1900, as [[frazer-james-george|Frazer]]'s magic→religion→science scheme, [[westermarck-edward|Westermarck]]'s biological critique of primitive promiscuity, and [[marett-r-r|Marett]]'s emotionalist "pre-animism" pulled the paradigm apart from within (feeding [[social-evolutionism]], [[cultural-evolutionism-debate]], [[intellectualist-theory-of-magic-and-religion]]). Second, the **rehabilitation of [[diffusionism]]**: Stocking argues British diffusionism ([[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]] → [[elliot-smith-grafton|Elliot Smith]]/[[perry-w-j|Perry]] heliolithic school → [[hocart-a-m|Hocart]]) was a serious paradigmatic alternative, since repressed in disciplinary memory, not a mere aberration.

Third, and most consequential for this wiki's existing coverage, Stocking's **historicist deflation of the "invention of fieldwork."** He argues [[malinowski-bronislaw|Malinowski]]'s *Argonauts* charter was built on [[rivers-w-h-r|Rivers]]'s 1912 "intensive study" formulation (itself distilled from the [[torres-strait-expedition-study|Torres Strait Expedition]] tradition), and reads *Argonauts* as a "euhemerist myth" divinizing "the Ethnographer" — an analysis that supplies [[ethnographic-authority]], [[ethnographic-realism]], and [[ethnographic-present]] with their historicist genealogy. This sits in productive tension with, rather than contradiction of, [[kuper-anthropology-and-anthropologists-2015|Kuper's]] "1922 baseline" account on [[british-social-anthropology]]: Kuper narrates the school from its founding monographs forward; Stocking reconstructs the 1888–1922 prehistory those monographs mythologized. Fourth, Stocking treats **institutionalization as content** — Rockefeller/IAI funding, colonial demand for "practical anthropology," and the [[indirect-rule|Dual Mandate]] context — while explicitly bracketing the question of functionalism's actual influence on colonial *policy* (see [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]]). His own methodological candor (tentative psychobiography of Malinowski's diary; explicit scope-limiting) is recorded in `reliability_notes`.
