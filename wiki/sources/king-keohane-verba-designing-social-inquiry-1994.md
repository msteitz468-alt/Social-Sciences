# Designing Social Inquiry: Scientific Inference in Qualitative Research

King, Keohane & Verba (1994) — known universally as **KKV** — is a programmatic methods text arguing that qualitative and quantitative research share **one logic of inference**, and that qualitative work is improved by adopting the design discipline of quantitative (regression-style) analysis. Extraction feeds the study page, three thinker pages, and a cluster of research-design concept pages; the book's contested reception feeds the qualitative–quantitative debate and a dedicated KKV-response debate page.
