# The Birth of the Gods and the Origins of Agriculture (Cauvin, 2000)

[[cauvin-jacques|Jacques Cauvin]]'s mature synthesis of Near Eastern **neolithisation**, translated by Trevor Watkins. Defines the [[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]] as first and foremost a **restructuring of human mentality** expressed in new religious ideas and symbols — the **[[revolution-of-symbols|revolution of symbols]]** — rather than as an ecological or techno-economic response. Survey from Natufian villages through PPNB diffusion to Mediterranean, Cyprus, Jezirah, and pastoral nomadism, ending when village farming and developed religious ideology are established across the Near East and already spreading toward Europe (~6300 BC in Cauvin's calibrated frame).

## Core Argument (attributed, Cauvin 2000)

1. Near Eastern neolithisation is a **cultural** process in which a **revolution of symbols** (~9500 BC Khiamian) — woman/Goddess and bull as dominant symbols of a new religion — **precedes** the farming economy (~9000 BC).
2. Farming is the **second step**, not the first: material-infrastructure explanations (ecology, demography, Oasis/hilly-flanks settling-in) cannot generate the symbolic change from which agriculture follows as released initiative.
3. PPNA diversity (Sultanian, Aswadian, Mureybetian) along the **Levantine corridor** is the local cradle; PPNB then spreads as a culturally unified package (including skull cult and perfected cultivation/herding).
4. Diffusion requires specified human behaviours and motivations, not a bare label; later Great Exodus carries the Neolithic package to Anatolia, southern Levant, Mediterranean littoral, Cyprus, Jezirah, and pastoral zones.
5. The Goddess–Bull symbolic system seeds later Near Eastern and east-Mediterranean mythic thought (read forward to Çatalhöyük and Bronze Age theologies — analogical and historiographic, not identity).

## Volume Synthesis Note

Cauvin’s English *Birth of the Gods* is the single most explicit **ideology-before-economy** program in the wiki’s agricultural-origins layer. It keeps Childe’s Neolithic Revolution vocabulary and productive-economy criterion while relocating the Revolution’s *cause* to a [[revolution-of-symbols|revolution of symbols]] among [[khiamian|Khiamian]] foragers (~9500 BC): Woman/[[mother-goddess|Goddess]] and Bull as dominant symbols of a new religion, read forward through [[mureybet|Mureybet]] bucrania to Mellaart’s [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]] “explicit theology.” Farming (~9000 BC) among the PPNA corridor triad ([[sultanian-culture|Sultanian]], [[aswadian-culture|Aswadian]], [[mureybetian-culture|Mureybetian]]) is the **second step**; PPNB then spreads a culturally unified package (rectangular architecture, herding, [[skull-cult|skull cult]]) in a “Great Exodus” to Anatolia, southern Levant, Mediterranean, [[khirokitia|Cyprus]], Jezirah, and pastoral nomadism.

**What it adds to the wiki:** (1) a full attributed position on [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]] against oasis, hilly-flanks settling-in, and demographic prime movers; (2) culture-historical resolution of PPNA into three corridor cultures; (3) sites [[mureybet]], [[jerf-el-ahmar]], [[nevali-cori]], [[cafer-hoyuk]], [[khirokitia]] as network nodes; (4) Postscript date-stamps (YD ~10,600 BC; Göbekli Mureybetian industry; Halula cattle ~7700 BC correcting Cyprus chronology).

**Tensions with already-ingested sources:** Mithen (*After the Ice*; *Prehistory of the Mind*) accepts ideology-before-economy / ceremonial gatherings while rejecting mother-goddess cult labels at Göbekli; Bar-Yosef & Valla 2013 densify Natufian complexity and YD debates that Cauvin simplifies; Braidwood’s nuclear-zone program is re-read rather than discarded; multi-regional domestication outside SW Asia is outside Cauvin’s scope — do not launder his Near East radiation story into global monogenesis. Absolute dates and site inventories are **as of Cauvin 1997–2000** (pre-aDNA; pre-full Göbekli publication).
