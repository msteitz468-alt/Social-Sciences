# The Archaeology of Islam (Insoll 1999)

Timothy Insoll’s programmatic synthesis of **[[islamic-archaeology|Islamic archaeology]]** as a **social archaeology of a living world religion**: Islam as a cross-cultural superstructure of belief whose substructure of practice and material culture is highly diverse; Muslim communities should nonetheless leave archaeologically recognizable signatures if “Islam is more than a religion — a way of life.” Published in the Blackwell **Social Archaeology** series (series editor [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]]).

## Volume Synthesis Note

Insoll 1999 is the vault’s **program charter for Islamic archaeology as social archaeology**. Extraction (~292 claims across four ranges) confirms a complete monographic synthesis (~103k words / 143 pp): not an excavation report, but a multi-regional case review organized by life domain.

**Core load.** (1) Superstructure of belief / substructure of diverse material practice — one Islam archaeologically approachable without essentializing an “ideal Muslim.” (2) Way-of-life scope: mosque, domestic privacy, diet, pilgrimage, dress, magic, war, art/trade, burial, settlement/landscape. (3) Dual scales — structuring principles vs cultural diversity — with Annales mix-and-match as heuristic. (4) Field critique: art-historical subservience, top-heavy elite bias, neglected fauna/botany/everyday ceramics, isolation from theory, superposition neglect of Islamic levels. (5) Ideals vs realities: exceptions expected to outweigh orthodoxy; ideal material suites rarely complete. (6) Developmental not instantaneous conquest material culture (Spain/Córdoba; ninth-century turning point hypothesis). (7) Minimum settlement recognition: mosque + Muslim burials; mix-and-match against orientalizing “Islamic city” isnads.

**Voice.** All program theses and case readings attributed to Insoll 1999 or his cited specialists. Living-religion ethnography treated as analogy privilege, not proof. Four-way non-identity: Muslim religious identity ≠ ethnicity ≠ language ≠ genes; “Islamic art” vs “art of Muslims” kept as Insoll’s distinction.

**What this source does not finish:** post-1999 field standards and CRM explosion; Grabar primary texts; regional monographs (Insoll’s own West African fieldwork beyond citation); aDNA/bioarchaeology of Islamic periods; dedicated debate pages for early-Islam archaeology vs Qur’anic historicity.
