# Tsing, *Friction* (2005)

Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing's *Friction: An Ethnography of Global Connection* (Princeton, 2005) is a multi-locale ethnography of how global capitalist, scientific, and activist universals are made and remade in the forests and politics of South Kalimantan (Indonesian Borneo) and Indonesian national nature-loving milieus. Tsing argues (attributed) that global connection works through **friction** — the awkward, unstable, creative and destructive engagements of difference — not through frictionless flow.

The book earned a [[tsing-friction-study|study page]] and [[hubs/studies/tsing-friction-study|Studies Hub]] analysis as a reference point for ethnographies of globalization, scale, frontiers, and collaboration.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Friction* is the wiki’s first full charter ingest for the **ethnography of global connection** and for Tsing’s program vocabulary (friction, frontier, appearances, scale-making, engaged universals). It densifies contemporary cultural/political/economic anthropology of Southeast Asian resource frontiers and Indonesian environmental politics, and supplies a methodological counterweight to seamless-flow globalization discourse already partially represented via Sassen, Castells, and Wallerstein-volume material on [[globalization]].

**What this source does well for the wiki:** multi-locale method page; concept cluster portable beyond Indonesia; society page for Meratus with anti-ethnographic-present discipline; Studies Hub for a landmark 2005 ethnography; links to development/environment neighbors ([[ferguson-james]], [[escobar-arturo]]) without collapsing programs.

**What it leaves thin:** *Diamond Queen* (1993) gender/politics depth; secondary critique literature; English-edition pagination for citation hygiene; corporate interior of mining/logging firms (access failed in fieldwork). *Mushroom* (2015) multi-species extension now densified at [[tsing-mushroom-2015]] / [[tsing-mushroom-study]].

**Tensions with existing pages:** Complements rather than replaces [[world-systems-analysis]] and [[global-city-theory]] macros; converses with [[north-atlantic-universals]] without identity (Tsing’s travel/engagement vs Trouillot’s North Atlantic conceptual history); nature-loving is not the same object as US wilderness ideology though lineages cross.
