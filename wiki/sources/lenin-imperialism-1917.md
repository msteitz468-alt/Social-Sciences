## Core argument (attributed)

Lenin argued that:

1. **Concentration** of production, at a certain stage, leads to **monopoly** (cartels, trusts, combines). Free competition’s apex was roughly the 1860s–70s; after 1873 cartels spread; by the boom of the 1890s and crisis of 1900–03 cartels become foundations of economic life — capitalism transformed into imperialism (~1900 as conventional turning zone).
2. Banks cease to be mere intermediaries and become monopolies that control industry through credit, holdings, and **personal union** with industry and the state; bank capital fused with industrial capital is **[[finance-capital|finance capital]]** (Hilferding’s definition, which Lenin amends to stress monopoly).
3. Typical of the new stage is the **export of capital** (not only commodities), generating a rentier/parasitic stratum and “usurer” states, while dividing the world first figuratively (capital spheres) and then actually (territories and combines).
4. **Five essential features** of imperialism: (i) monopolies decisive in economic life; (ii) finance capital and financial oligarchy; (iii) capital export of pronounced importance; (iv) international monopolist associations sharing the world; (v) completed territorial division of the globe among the great powers (thereafter only redivision).
5. Briefest definition: imperialism is the **monopoly stage of capitalism**. Fuller: domination of monopolies and finance capital; capital export; division among trusts begun; partition of territories completed.
6. Against **Kautsky**: imperialism is not merely a preferred *policy* of industrial capital annexing agrarian regions detachable from economics; “ultra-imperialism” (peaceful joint exploitation by united finance capital) is empty abstraction / reactionary consolation — finance capital **increases** unevenness and contradictions; under capitalism disparities of productive forces vs colonial division have no remedy other than **war**.
7. Monopoly generates a tendency to **stagnation and decay** (parasitism, rentier state) without precluding rapid uneven growth; imperialism can create a **labor aristocracy** (echoing Marx/Engels on Britain) and bonds with **social-chauvinism**/opportunism.
8. Historically, imperialism is **transitional / moribund** capitalism: socialization of production within a private-property shell; intensified contradictions drive a transitional period toward a higher social-economic order.

## Method (as Lenin states)

- Deliberate use of **bourgeois** statistical and descriptive sources while declaring Marxism “refuted” by their authors.
- Censorship constraint → Aesopian substitutions.
- Polemical targets: Kautsky, social-chauvinism, free-trade nostalgia as reformist ideal under monopoly.
