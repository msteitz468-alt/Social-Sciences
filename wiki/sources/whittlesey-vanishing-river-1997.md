# Vanishing River (Whittlesey, Ciolek-Torrello & Altschul, eds., 1997)

Overview, synthesis, and conclusions volume of the **Lower Verde Archaeological Project (LVAP)** — multi-year Bureau of Reclamation–sponsored CRM research by Statistical Research, Inc., in the lower Verde River valley, central Arizona (Tonto National Forest; Horseshoe and Bartlett project areas). The printed book synthesizes environment, research history, Yavapai and Western Apache ethnohistory/archaeology, Euroamerican history, agricultural economics, ceramic chronology, site structure, settlement and demography, critiques of Hohokam core–periphery models, CRM “big project” practice, and landscape reconstructions. Companion CD-ROM holds site catalogues and specialist volumes.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Vanishing River* is the print overview of a Bureau of Reclamation–sponsored CRM project that turned a reservoir-impact mitigation into a regional synthesis of the Arizona Transition Zone. Its intellectual spine is twofold: (1) a **landscape** framework (Whittlesey) that integrates formal, historical, and relational dimensions across prehistoric farming, Apache/Yavapai use, and Euroamerican hydraulic development; (2) an empirical **rejection of simple Hohokam core–periphery** explanations for the lower Verde, grounded in ceramic percentages, ballcourt investment, mortuary diversity, settlement demography, and agricultural modeling. The volume is also a primary document of 1990s big-CRM practice (Altschul): APE limits, contract mods, CD-ROM data publication, and sponsor-imposed research questions.

What it adds to the wiki: first dense coverage of [[sinagua-culture|Sinagua]] construct history, [[yavapai|Yavapai]] and [[western-apache|Western Apache]] ethnohistory/archaeology (including [[skeleton-cave|Skeleton Cave]]), lower Verde type sites ([[scorpion-point-village|Scorpion Point]], [[roadhouse-ruin|Roadhouse Ruin]]), and the Southwestern sense of [[hohokam-core-periphery-model|core–periphery]] as distinct from world-systems [[core-periphery]]. Gaps: CD-ROM Volumes 1–3 not ingested; post-1997 chronology and tribal collaboration protocols not updated here; Salado remains under-paged beyond Ch. 4 research history.
