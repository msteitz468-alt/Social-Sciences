# The Dawn of Everything (Graeber & Wengrow, 2021)

## Overview

[[graeber-david|David Graeber]] and [[wengrow-david|David Wengrow]]'s *The Dawn of Everything: A New History of Humanity* (2021) is a mixed theoretical–synthetic work: a sustained critique of the standard "origins of inequality" narrative (Rousseauan fall / Hobbesian pacification) and of stage-evolutionary big history, offered together with a reconstructed deep-time account of political creativity among foragers, early farmers, and early urbanites. The authors argue (attributed) that before and after agriculture, human societies experimented with a "carnival parade of political forms"; that farming did not lock in hierarchy; that many early cities lacked kings; and that three basic freedoms (to move away, to disobey, to create new social arrangements) and three elementary forms of domination (control of violence, control of knowledge, charismatic politics) better organize the comparative record than band→tribe→chiefdom→state ladders.

Evidence classes: material (secondary archaeological synthesis), ethnographic (second-hand), textual/archival (Enlightenment and Indigenous diplomatic literature). Not primary excavation or long-term fieldwork. Production context: ten years of co-writing; Graeber's anarchist anthropology and Wengrow's Near Eastern / comparative archaeology; written for a wide public as well as specialists.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Dawn of Everything* is best filed in this wiki as a **mixed theoretical–synthetic intervention**: it reorganizes already-published archaeology and ethnography under a political anthropology that privileges contingency, seasonality, and institutional experiment over stage laws. Its permanent wiki value is less any single site claim (those remain specialist-owned and contested) than the **concept apparatus** it deposits — [[indigenous-critique]], [[three-basic-freedoms]], [[three-elementary-forms-of-domination]], [[schismogenesis]] (as comparative engine), [[play-farming]] — and the **debate surface** it opens against [[social-evolutionism]], [[band-tribe-chiefdom-model]], [[egalitarian-rank-stratified-state-model]], and popular big history.

For this vault's two-layer design, the book sits almost entirely in the **knowledge layer** (theories, concepts, thinkers, debates, history of the disciplines). Subject-matter pages (sites, societies, cultures) should receive only **attributed, date-stamped G&W readings**, never silent promotion to wiki voice. The standing [[dawn-of-everything-debate]] is the correct home for reception and case-level pushback.

Compared with already-ingested neighbours: it *uses* [[sahlins-marshall|Sahlins]] (affluence, culture as antithesis, schismogenesis via Sahlins) and *attacks* the neoevolutionary ladders of [[service-elman-r|Service]] and [[fried-morton-h|Fried]] without replacing them by a new unilinear scheme. It is closer in genre to public-facing program books (cf. Wrangham *Catching Fire* in this vault) than to monographic excavation reports — footprint kept deliberate: no Studies Hub, no Theory Hub yet (2021 program has not organized a generation of specialist research the way processualism did), Thinkers Hubs deferred pending *Debt* and regional monographs.

**What it does not cover for us:** primary aDNA; systematic HRAF-style coding; full Enlightenment historiography; the specialist site monographs it synthesizes. Those remain acquisition priorities if the debate is to be adjudicated rather than only recorded.

## Reflexivity / reliability

- **Genre hybrid:** big-history popular book + research synthesis + political argument. Filed as `source_type: mixed`.
- **Authorship:** Graeber died September 2020; Wengrow's acknowledgements stress co-equal intellectual partnership over a decade.
- **Reception:** Major public success; specialist pushback on archaeological selectivity, ethnographic framing, and Enlightenment-reception history — [[dawn-of-everything-debate]].
- **Voice rule for this wiki:** G&W's counter-narrative is **attributed** throughout; do not promote their readings of Çatalhöyük, Teotihuacan, Cahokia, Indus, Wendat, etc. to wiki voice without independent convergence from ingested specialist sources.
