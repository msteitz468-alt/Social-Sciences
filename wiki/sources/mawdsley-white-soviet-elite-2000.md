## Research design (source-level)

Mawdsley and White define the Soviet political elite as **full and candidate members of the Communist Party Central Committee** — the regime's own institutional definition of the leading circle. Some **1,932** individuals held CC status between 1917 and 1991. The book is a long-run collective biography (prosopography) of that population, organized by period chapters that track composition, recruitment, turnover, job-slot mix, and the CC's decision-making role under successive leaders.

Core analytic concepts (authors' and borrowed):
- **[[job-slot-system|Job-slot system]]** (after Robert V. Daniels): CC membership as automatic adjunct of holding a ranked office.
- **[[nomenklatura]]**: the appointments system and the social group selected through it.
- **Elite generations / cohorts**: revolutionary, early Stalinist, late Stalinist, post-Stalin; birth cohorts (pre-1901; 1901–20; 1921–40).
- **Circular flow of power** (Daniels): apparatus control → regional appointments → congress delegations → CC election → apparatus leadership.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Mawdsley and White deliver a full-period **collective biography** of the Soviet political elite defined as CPSU Central Committee full and candidate members (N=1,932; 22 CCs). The analytic payoff is institutional rather than purely biographical: the **[[job-slot-system]]** (after Daniels) explains how the CC became a map of ranked party-state offices; the **[[nomenklatura]]** explains how those offices were filled; turnover and four birth-band **generations** explain renewal, stagnation, and three "elite revolutions" (1917; Purges; Gorbachev). Chapters 7–8 treat representativeness, privilege, the new-class debate, and post-1991 continuity/power conversion as empirical questions.

**What this adds to the wiki:** a Soviet party-state counterpart to US [[power-elite]] / [[mills-power-elite-study]] research; operational content for [[nomenklatura]], [[job-slot-system]], [[soviet-central-committee]], [[new-class]], [[democratic-centralism]], and [[circular-flow-of-power]]; a method exemplar for [[collective-biography]] plus elite interviews and [[archival-research]].

**Tensions / cautions:** do not equate Mawdsley–White's CC elite with Mills's corporate–military–political power elite; do not dump Soviet recruitment into vault concept [[elite-recruitment]] (Anthony IE archaeology); Purge findings are attributed personnel-system interpretations as well as body counts; post-1991 continuity magnitudes remain debateable outside this source.
