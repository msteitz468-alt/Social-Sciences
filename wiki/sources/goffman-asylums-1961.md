## Volume Synthesis Note

*Asylums* is four free-standing essays sharing an inmate-world problem — how total institutions remake the self — approached through ideal type (Essay 1), moral career (Essay 2), underlife (Essay 3), and medical-service ideology (Essay 4). The ethnographic core is a 1955–56 year at St. Elizabeths under NIMH Laboratory sponsorship, with comparative literature carrying the total-institution type beyond the asylum. For the wiki, this is the first Goffman ingest and the first full postwar institutional ethnography: it densifies [[symbolic-interactionism]] with institutional self-theory ([[total-institution]], [[mortification-of-the-self]], [[moral-career]], [[secondary-adjustment]], [[underlife]], [[medical-model]]) while remaining date-stamped to mid-century custodial practice. Reception as anti-psychiatry is recorded as position, not as the book's self-description. *Presentation of Self* and *Stigma* remain queued; Thinkers Hub for Goffman deferred until those are ingested.
