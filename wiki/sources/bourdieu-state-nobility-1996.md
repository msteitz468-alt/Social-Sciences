# The State Nobility (Bourdieu, 1989/1996)

Pierre Bourdieu's summa on elite formation: a study of how the French *grandes écoles*
consecrate a "state nobility" — a ruling class whose domination is legitimated no longer by
blood or by God but by academically certified "intelligence." Building on and extending
*[[bourdieu-distinction-1984|Distinction]]*, it argues that the school has taken over the
Church's medieval role of sanctifying social divisions, converting inherited (economic and
cultural) privilege into apparently personal grace through **[[rites-of-institution|rites of
institution]]**, and it gives the fullest statement of the **[[field-of-power]]** and of the
shift from a **family** to a **school-mediated [[modes-of-reproduction|mode of
reproduction]]**.
