## Volume Synthesis Note

Barker 2006 is the vault’s fullest **anti-progress, multi-path, forager-centred** global synthesis of agricultural origins, complementary and often **opposed** to [[bellwood-first-farmers-2005]] on mechanism of spread. Where Bellwood organizes comparative archaeology around farming–language–demic correlations after homeland origins, Barker organizes around **why foragers decided** (or stumbled) into husbandry under Holocene conditions: stress-preservation without hindsight; mutualistic domestication; Pleistocene behavioural pre-adaptations without Neolithic cosmologies; independent regional beginnings; ideology (animism→theism) co-equal with economy. Demic packages are staged region by region and weakened; exchange and mobility are treated as baseline features of modern humans rather than Neolithic novelties.

**What it adds:** (1) systematic forager ethnography and world-view chapter feeding [[hunter-gatherer]] and [[animism]]; (2) method chapter on how we know foragers from farmers; (3) dense mid-2000s regional surveys (SW Asia process length; Asia parallels; Americas multi-center; Africa cattle-before-crops and Bantu complexity; Europe *ex oriente lux* critique); (4) an explicit challenger position on [[early-farming-dispersal-hypothesis]] / [[demic-diffusion]] for the [[agricultural-origins-causation-debate]].

**Limitations / dating:** Secondary synthesis as of ~2005 literature; classical and early aDNA genetics **as of Barker 2006** — Europe especially has been revised by genome-wide aDNA after 2010 (often strengthening demic signals relative to Richards/Sykes-era readings Barker used). Absolute crop/animal dates and some African/Chinese chronologies have also moved. Use attributed voice and date-stamps; do not promote mid-2000s genetic fractions to wiki voice.
