# The Mind and Society (Pareto, *Trattato*; English 1935)

Vilfredo Pareto’s *Trattato di Sociologia generale* (1916), in the four-volume English edition *The Mind and Society* (Harcourt, Brace, 1935), edited by Arthur Livingston. The work is the major statement of Pareto’s general sociology: logico-experimental method; non-logical conduct; residues and derivations; social system and equilibrium; circulation of elites.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Pareto’s *Trattato* (English *The Mind and Society*, 1935) is the major classical statement of a **logico-experimental general sociology** distinct from Comtean encyclopedism, Spencerian evolutionism, Durkheimian social-fact monography, and Marxian historical materialism. Vol. I fixes method and the centrality of **[[non-logical-conduct]]**, then shows how writers and doctrines **logicalize** that conduct and smuggle non-experimental principles under scientific dress — isolating constant element *a* and variable *b*. Vol. II names *a* **[[residues]]** and develops the six-class taxonomy, with load-bearing dualism of **[[combination-instinct|Class I combinations]]** (progressive) and **[[group-persistence|Class II group-persistences]]** (conservative cement). Vol. III develops **[[derivations]]** (assertion, authority, accords, verbal proofs) and properties linking residues/derivations to utility and equilibrium. Vol. IV builds the **[[paretian-social-equilibrium|social system and equilibrium]]**, then the political-economic machinery of **[[circulation-of-elites]]**: force vs cunning (**[[lions-and-foxes]]**), **[[speculators-and-rentiers]]**, and modern **[[demagogic-plutocracy]]**, with historical waves of crystallization and free initiative.

For this wiki the work scaffolds classical elite theory and ideology-as-justification vocabulary beside (not under) [[positivism]] and [[social-evolutionism]]. All doctrinal content is **attributed voice**. Historical cases are illustrations only. The standing analytic hinge is the **[[truth-utility-distinction]]**: experimental truth and social utility of doctrines are independent and may conflict.
