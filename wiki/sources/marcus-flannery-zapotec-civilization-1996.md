# Zapotec Civilization (Marcus & Flannery 1996)

Joyce Marcus and Kent V. Flannery, *Zapotec Civilization: How Urban Society Evolved in Mexico's Oaxaca Valley* (London: Thames and Hudson, 1996). ISBN 0-500-05078-3. Foreword by series editors Colin Renfrew and Jeremy A. Sabloff. 302 illustrations (16 colour).

A full-sequence synthesis of Formative-through-Classic Oaxaca: from Late Ice Age foragers through Archaic collectors, early villages, ranked chiefdoms centered on [[san-jose-mogote|San José Mogote]], the Monte Albán **synoikism** (urban relocation c. 500 BCE), state formation and expansion, Classic "golden age," and a closing **action-theory** account of social evolution without unilinear stages. Builds on and supersedes for narrative purposes the editors' earlier seminar volume *[[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]]* (1983) and feeds later comparative arguments in *[[flannery-marcus-creation-inequality-2012|The Creation of Inequality]]* (2012).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Zapotec Civilization* is the public, continuous narrative of the Oaxaca long-term program: ethnohistoric Zapotec as direct-historical bridge; Archaic risk and domestication; Formative prestige→rank→chiefly warfare and writing; Monte Albán as **synoikism**; state institutions and an expansion triad (colonize / conquer / diplomatically engage Teotihuacan); Classic multipolar defense; and a closing **action-theory** refusal of unilinear stages. Relative to *[[flannery-marcus-cloud-people-1983|The Cloud People]]* (1983 seminar topics), it sacrifices multi-author density for a single actor-centered story usable as a world-archaeology case. Relative to Coe & Koontz’s survey chaptering, it supplies primary population ranges, founding scenarios, and theoretical machinery. All population figures and ethnic equations stay attributed; four-way non-identity remains mandatory for “Zapotec” across the deep sequence.
