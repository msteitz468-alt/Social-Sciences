# The Theory of Communicative Action, Volume 2: Lifeworld and System

Bibliographic record for [[habermas-jurgen|Jürgen Habermas]]'s *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns*, Band 2 (1981; Eng. trans. Thomas McCarthy, 1987). Volume 2 turns from the action-theoretic foundations of Volume 1 to a two-level theory of society: it reconstructs the [[lifeworld]] concept via Mead and Durkheim, distinguishes [[system-and-lifeworld|system from lifeworld]], analyzes the [[steering-media|steering media]] of money and power, critiques [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]], and culminates in the thesis of the [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld|colonization of the lifeworld]]. Feeds the [[theory-of-communicative-action]] theory page and the concept cluster below.

**Volume 1 not ingested** — see `reliability_notes`. Treat [[communicative-action]] and communicative rationality as documented here from Volume 2's back-references, not from their primary Volume 1 statement.

## Key Claims Extracted

All in attributed voice (Habermas's theses). Developed on the linked pages.

- **Paradigm shift.** Mead and Durkheim prepared "a paradigm shift from purposive activity to communicative action"; normative authority passes from ritual to reasoned agreement — the **[[linguistification-of-the-sacred]]**. → [[communicative-action]]
- **Two-level society.** "Conceive of societies simultaneously as systems and lifeworlds": social integration (lifeworld, communicative) vs. system integration (economy/state, functional). → [[lifeworld]], [[system-and-lifeworld]]
- **Steering media.** Money and power are delinguistified media that "replace" communication and "uncouple" system from lifeworld. → [[steering-media]]
- **Parsons critique.** Parsons subordinated action theory to systems theory and produced a "too harmonious" theory of modernity. → [[parsons-talcott]]
- **Weber/Marx reconstruction.** Weber's "loss of meaning/freedom" and Marx's reification reinterpreted via the system/lifeworld frame; the labor theory of value "economistically foreshortens" critique. → [[theory-of-communicative-action]]
- **Colonization.** System imperatives colonize communicatively structured lifeworld domains, producing pathologies; **[[juridification]]** is the test case; new social movements the predicted resistance. → [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld]]

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Theory of Communicative Action*, Vol. 2 enters the vault as the charter of Habermas's mature social theory, and it converses directly with pages already here. Its architecture is a single descent: from the reconstruction of [[communicative-action]] out of Mead and Durkheim (the [[linguistification-of-the-sacred]]), through the two-level theory of [[system-and-lifeworld]] and its [[steering-media]], the immanent critique of [[parsons-talcott|Parsons]] and reworking of [[weber-max|Weber]] and [[marx-karl|Marx]], to the diagnostic climax of the [[colonization-of-the-lifeworld]] and its test case, [[juridification]].

Its chief contribution to the wiki is a **reconstruction of [[critical-theory]] on communicative rather than instrumental reason** — the explicit alternative to the [[frankfurt-school|first generation]]'s *[[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate|Dialectic of Enlightenment]]* (Habermas argues [[adorno-theodor-w|Adorno]] and [[horkheimer-max|Horkheimer]] "generalized the reification of consciousness into an expression of instrumental reason"). It also supplies a sustained sociological counterweight to [[structural-functionalism]] and Luhmannian systems theory, while conceding to them a "system" level that classical [[historical-materialism]] could not theorize. It pairs naturally with the concurrently-ingested *[[habermas-structural-transformation-public-sphere-1989|Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere]]*: the public sphere is, in effect, an institutional site where lifeworld communication resists colonization. **Standing limitation:** Volume 1 is absent, so communicative rationality, the validity-claims typology, and the action-typology are documented here only from Volume 2's back-references. A hub-level treatment (Thinkers Hub for Habermas; Theory Hub for TCA) is warranted but deferred pending Volume 1.
