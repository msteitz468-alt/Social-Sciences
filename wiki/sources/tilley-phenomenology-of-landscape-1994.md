# A Phenomenology of Landscape (Tilley, 1994)

Christopher Tilley's founding Anglophone statement of **phenomenological landscape archaeology**: places, paths, and monuments as lived, bodily, and mnemonic structures rather than dots on a functional distribution map. Part I builds theory and ethnographic analogy; Part II applies the framework to Mesolithic locales and Neolithic monuments in three contrasting British regions. ### Programmatic claims (Intro–Ch.1)
- Key question: why these locations for habitation and monuments.
- Rejects exclusive environmental-functional explanation (Binford, Jarman et al. cited as examples of the approach).
- Stresses symbolics of landscape perception + social memory without polarizing economy vs culture.
- Three study areas; three Neolithic monument traditions; Mesolithic mainly surface scatters.
- "Middle-range" gap between site plan and distribution map (mole vs aircraft).
- Parallel trajectories of new geography and new archaeology; later humanized space.
- Phenomenology (Heidegger dwelling; Merleau-Ponty body — as Tilley presents them); place vs abstract space; path and narrative.

### Ethnographic conceptual background (Ch.2)
- Landscape significance cross-cuts ecology and subsistence dichotomies.
- Landscape as **mythopoesis** (metaphor, allegory, synecdoche).
- Cases: Aboriginal Dreaming/tracks; Mistassini Cree; Koyukon; Melanesian paths and ancestral stones; Tewa tetrads — all as Tilley's secondary synthesis.
- Enculturation of land as ongoing symbolic process.

### Regional empirics (Ch.3–5) and synthesis (Ch.6)
- SW Wales: coastal affinity; outcrops as natural megaliths; chamber rows and Carn Ingli focus.
- Black Mountains: long-axis vision; approach/exit; Arthur's Stone → Hay Bluff; monument makes landscape visible.
- Cranborne Chase: ceremonial core; Penbury Knoll; 39 barrows; Dorset Cursus walk; Hambledon bone/enclosure complex; continuity vs hiatus position.
- Synthesis: Mesolithic place-from-landscape → Neolithic landscape-from-monuments; double ancestral powers; freeze perspective as power; west→east gradient.

## Volume Synthesis Note
