# Method and Theory in American Archaeology (Willey & Phillips 1958)

Gordon R. Willey and Philip Phillips's charter for **American** archaeological method and for a hemisphere-scale **historical-developmental** synthesis of New World prehistory. Combines revised versions of their 1953 and 1955 *American Anthropologist* papers with a new introduction on archaeology's relation to anthropological theory. Dedicated to James A. Ford; special thanks to Spaulding and Rouse.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Method and Theory* is the vault's **American culture-history charter**, complementary to Childe's European stream (*Danube* / *Dawn*). Part I fixes everyday Americanist vocabulary — component, phase, horizon, tradition — and names descriptive work [[culture-historical-integration]] while reserving [[processual-interpretation]] for explanatory regularities (a pre-New Archaeology use of “processual”). Part II offers a hybrid historical-developmental five-stage map of the hemisphere, with Classic/Postclassic “civilization” limited to Middle America and the central Andes. The book is theoretical-methodological, not a single research act: **no `studies/` page**. Willey Thinkers Hub deferred pending Virú settlement and handbook corpus.

**Reliability:** all stage content, absolute dates, and culture equations are attributed “(as of Willey & Phillips 1958)”; four-way non-identity enforced on unit and stage pages; civilizational ranking of Classic treated as their evaluative position, not wiki voice.
