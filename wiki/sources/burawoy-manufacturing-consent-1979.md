# Manufacturing Consent (Burawoy, 1979)

Burawoy's ten months (July 1974–May 1975) as a machine operator at "Allied Corporation," South Chicago — Donald Roy's "Geer Company" of 1944–45 — asking why workers work as hard as they do. ## Key Claims (attributed)

Consent is manufactured at the point of production: playing [[making-out]] generates consent to its rules; the [[internal-labor-market]] individualizes; the [[internal-state]] makes industrial citizens; imported consciousness has negligible direct effect on output (N=185 regressions; Zambian comparison). Periodization: despotic → hegemonic organization of work with the passage from competitive to monopoly capitalism; against Braverman, degradation proceeds "without continuing crisis" because choice expands within narrowing limits.
