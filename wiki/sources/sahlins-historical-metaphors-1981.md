# Historical Metaphors and Mythical Realities (Sahlins, 1981)

First statement of Sahlins's historical anthropology: the monograph that coins the **[[structure-of-the-conjuncture]]** and works out, through the death of Captain Cook and the four decades that followed (to the 1819 abolition of the tabu system), how a cultural order reproduces itself in action and is thereby transformed. The programmatic formula: "the transformation of a culture is a mode of its reproduction" — Hawaiians acted on their inherited categories (Cook as the god Lono; Europeans assimilated to chiefship and divinity), and in so acting placed those categories at the risk of practical revaluation.

## Claims Extracted

All framework claims attributed to Sahlins. Pages updated (1 created, 9 updated):

- **[[structure-of-the-conjuncture]]** — the coining passage ("Practice, rather, has its own dynamics — a 'structure of the conjuncture'..."); the reproduction→transformation question; interest vs. sense; sign as type/token; the two mechanisms of conceptual revaluation (referential, instrumental); the closing "practice of the structure / structure of the practice" dialectic. Now primary for the 1981 formulation.
- **[[taboo]]** — Hawaiian tabu as constitutive-of-structure; chiefly pragmatic tabu and the semantic drift toward proprietary interdiction (modern KAPU = "no trespassing"); gendered asymmetry of sanctions; decades of transgression before the 1819 *ʻai noa*; abolition as ritual act / prolonged death ceremonies of Kamehameha; Hocart on tabu vs. close-season.
- **[[hawaiians]]** — new 1981 section: men/women/chiefs' differential engagement with Europeans (*ʻimi haku*, *wāwahi*, gendered trade goods, commoner–chief schismogenic cleavage); secularization of the *haole*; the king's affines (Kaʻahumanu faction) vs. collateral line (Kekuaokalani); 1819 crisis dates and Battle of Kuamoʻo; 1829–34 counter-noa inversions.
- **[[sahlins-obeyesekere-cook-debate]]** — the 1981 evidential backbone (Makahiki calendrical correlation, hānaipū rite, "the Makahiki in an historical form") plus Sahlins's own 1981 cautions (no "Lono" designation at Kauai 1778; Lono-return myth a late development); Cortés/Moctezuma analogy.
- **[[mytho-praxis]]** — 1981 anticipation: anti-Malinowski charter critique, Johansen's "kinship I," genus–species descent logic, Maori-cosmology vs. Hawaiian-epic contrast.
- **[[stranger-king]]** — the Paao legend as Hawaiian charter; "a chief is a shark that travels on land"; usurper appropriation of the deposed line's ranking woman; the Vancouver cliff-jump anecdote.
- **[[mana]]** — mana ≠ tabu; defying tabu as sign of mana; "European mana was still the decisive fact of Hawaiian life"; Kamehameha's inheritance of Cook's mana; mana as solar brilliance / *kapu moe*.
