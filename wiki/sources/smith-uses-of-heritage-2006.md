# Uses of Heritage (Laurajane Smith, 2006)

[[smith-laurajane|Laurajane Smith]]'s programmatic retheorization of **heritage**: challenges the Western material/monumental definition of heritage as old, aesthetic, tangible fabric that promotes consensual views of past and present; develops an alternative framed by memory, performance, identity, intangibility, dissonance, and place. Coins and elaborates the **[[authorized-heritage-discourse|Authorized Heritage Discourse (AHD)]]**; argues that heritage is a **cultural process** (not a thing); deploys multi-sited qualitative research (English country houses; Riversleigh World Heritage / [[waanyi|Waanyi]] country; industrial social-history museums; [[castleford|Castleford]] community heritage; Indigenous control politics). Anchors [[critical-heritage-studies]]; densifies [[heritage]], [[heritage-management]], [[dissonant-heritage]], [[intangible-heritage]].

No standalone `studies/` page for the volume as a whole (mixed theoretical-work + multi-case ethnography; cases feed society/site/concept pages rather than a single landmark-study object).

### Load-bearing extracts (attributed)

- **“There is, really, no such thing as heritage”** — discourse constitutes the object; AHD privileges monumentality, time depth, expert judgement, consensus, nation building.
- **Heritage as process** — experience, identity, intangibility, memory, performance, place, dissonance; all heritage ultimately intangible; sites as props/theatres of memory.
- **Management as governance** — Venice / World Heritage / Burra / UNESCO intangible as authorizing institutions; technical management regulates values.
- **Country house (n=454, 2004)** — banality of grandiloquence; active but mostly AHD-aligned identity work; ~21% want servants/slavery/workers interpretation.
- **Riversleigh** — WHS natural/scientific vs Waanyi country; Australian landscape AHD; control of meanings.
- **Labour museums (n=273)** — Beamish, NCMM, Tolpuddle; remembering work/class; reactionary nostalgia ~2%.
- **Castleford** — community heritage after deindustrialization/miners’ strike; AHD cannot see heritage without fabric.
- **Indigenous control** — heritage as resource of power; control integral to meaningful identity; repatriation symbolism (e.g. Lake Mungo, Kennewick as deployed).
- **Conclusion** — people more active/mindful than assumed; all heritage dissonant and political; practice consequences of definitions.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Uses of Heritage* (2006) is the vault’s primary densification of **[[critical-heritage-studies]]** and the coinage site for **[[authorized-heritage-discourse]]**. It retheorizes [[heritage]] away from fabric inventories toward cultural process, and retheorizes [[heritage-management]] as governance of memory, identity, and place under expert discourse. Empirically it is multi-sited qualitative work (UK country houses and industrial museums; Castleford; Riversleigh/Waanyi; Indigenous politics US/Australia) — single-author, interview/survey-based findings stay in **attributed voice**. It balances earlier vault material that treated heritage mainly via looting ethics ([[renfrew-loot-legitimacy-2000]]), museum internationalism ([[cuno-who-owns-antiquity-2008]]), textbook management ([[renfrew-bahn-archaeology-2012]]), and post-processual museum critique ([[shanks-tilley-reconstructing-archaeology-1987]]) by supplying a named discourse concept and community/Indigenous use cases. Gaps remaining: Meskell UNESCO ethnography still outstanding; Smith’s 2004 *Archaeological Theory and the Politics of Cultural Heritage* not ingested; secondary CHS literature after 2006 thin.
