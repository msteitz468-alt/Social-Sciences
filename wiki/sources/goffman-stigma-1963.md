## Volume Synthesis Note

*Stigma* is Goffman's systematic microsociology of **spoiled identity**: not a second St. Elizabeths ethnography but a conceptual redescription of clinical and popular stigma literature under a scheme of **social information**. The progressive five-chapter design moves from virtual/actual social identity and the three stigma types, through information control (passing, covering), to ego-identity politics and a careful boundary with **deviance**. Core contributions now on the wiki: relational stigma definition; discredited/discreditable; own and wise; courtesy stigma; moral-career patterns beyond the mental hospital; symbols and disidentifiers; normal and stigmatized as **perspectives** rather than two piles of persons.

**Relation to already-ingested work:** *Asylums* supplies institutional careers and mortification; *Stigma* generalizes identity threat to ordinary mixed contacts. Together they cover two of three mid-career pillars; *Presentation of Self* (dramaturgy) remains the main gap for a Goffman Thinkers Hub. **Voice:** theoretical throughout — all substantive claims attributed. **Hub:** Studies Hub built in-session (selection criteria met: founded a research vocabulary and subfield of stigma studies).
