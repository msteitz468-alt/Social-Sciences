## Volume Synthesis Note

Hodder’s 1999 volume is the **method charter** of mature post-processual archaeology: it accepts the charge that interpretive theory had not remade the trench, and answers with a reflexive package grounded at Çatalhöyük. The double argument is epistemic and political. Epistemically, interpretation already constitutes data at the trowel’s edge; linear description→interpretation practice contradicts what theory concedes about theory-ladenness; reasoning is a hermeneutic spiral of accommodative fitting (Haddenham) that still allows data to break wholes. Politically, global heritage networks and multivocal claims make closed professional authority untenable; the same digital and commercial systems can emancipate or dominate. Natural sciences and computers are not rejected — they are folded into relational, recursive practice. The book is framed as provisional progress, not a finished universal method.
