# The Rules of Sociological Method (Durkheim, 1895/1901)

Émile Durkheim's *Les règles de la méthode sociologique* (1895; 2nd ed. 1901) — the programmatic method treatise that codifies **[[social-fact|social facts]] as things**, the normal/pathological distinction relative to **[[social-types|social types]]**, the separation of **cause and function**, and comparative **demonstration of sociological proof**. This ingest uses the Halls translation as revised in Lukes's 2013 Palgrave edition, with subsequent methodological texts and debates.

## What the book is

A short manifesto for an **objective, specific, and methodical** sociology: (1) define the **[[social-fact|social fact]]** (exteriority + constraint; ways of acting *and* morphological ways of being); (2) **[[treat-social-facts-as-things|treat social facts as things]]** — discard prenotions, define by external characteristics, prefer solid facts; (3) distinguish **[[normal-and-pathological|normal from pathological]]** relative to a social type and developmental phase (crime as normal); (4) constitute **[[social-types|social species/types]]** via morphological classification (middle ground between historical nominalism and philosophical monism); (5) explain social facts by **prior social facts** — separate **efficient cause** from **function**; locate causes in the **inner social environment** ([[social-morphology|social morphology]] / volume and dynamic density); (6) demonstrate by **comparative method** / concurrent variation (indirect experiment). The 1901 Preface answers reification and anti-psychology charges. Subsequent texts engage Marxism, ethnology, history (Seignobos), political economy, psychology/philosophy, and letter-form clarifications of social facts' psychological character.

Not a `studies/` page: the work is a **theoretical-method program**, not a limited-problem empirical monograph (contrast *Suicide*). Deep program analysis: [[hubs/theory/social-realism|Theory Hub — social realism]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Rules of Sociological Method* is the **method spine** of Durkheimian [[social-realism|social realism]]: a short book whose six chapters fix sociology’s object ([[social-fact]]), observational discipline ([[treat-social-facts-as-things]]), diagnostic vocabulary ([[normal-and-pathological]] relative to [[social-types]]), explanatory dualism (cause ≠ function; social by social via [[social-morphology]]), and proof procedure ([[comparative-method|concomitant variations]]). The 1901 Preface and the subsequent debates/letters in the Lukes volume are not optional apparatus — they show the program under fire (reification, anti-psychology, history, economics, Marxism) and Durkheim’s reformulations (representations, psychology *sui generis*, 1895 religion watershed under Robertson Smith).

Relative to already-ingested Durkheim: *Division of Labor* enacts the method before naming it; *Suicide* is the flagship application to rates; *Elementary Forms* (concurrent/related ingest) radicalizes collective representations and the religious matrix the 1895 watershed announced. Against other classical programs in this wiki, *Rules* is the clearest French counterpart to Weber’s interpretive method statements and to Comte’s positive-method program — keeping the demand for science while rejecting encyclopedism and individualist reduction ([[methodological-individualism-debate]]).
