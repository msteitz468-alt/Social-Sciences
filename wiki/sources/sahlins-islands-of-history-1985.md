# Islands of History (Sahlins 1985)

Marshall Sahlins's major statement of **historical anthropology**: five essays arguing that "history is culturally ordered" and that "cultural schemes are historically ordered" — the synthesis of structure and event. Its running case is the Pacific first-contact encounter, above all **Captain Cook in Hawaii** and the **Fijian stranger-king**, worked as demonstrations of the **[[structure-of-the-conjuncture|structure of the conjuncture]]**, **mytho-praxis**, and the distinction between **performative and prescriptive** cultural orders. The primary source for the Sahlins side of the [[sahlins-obeyesekere-cook-debate|Sahlins–Obeyesekere debate]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Islands of History* is Sahlins's synthesis of structure and event: "history is culturally ordered... cultural schemes are historically ordered," and "the transformation of a culture is a mode of its reproduction." Where *[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]* established that culture is a symbolic order, *Islands* sets that order in motion — through the [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]], in which inherited categories are "put at risk" and revalued when they meet contingency. The five essays supply the wiki's densest Polynesian ethnohistory ([[hawaiians]], [[fijians]], [[maori]]) and four durable analytic concepts ([[mytho-praxis]], [[heroic-history]], [[stranger-king]], [[performative-and-prescriptive-structures]]). It is the primary text on the Sahlins side of the [[sahlins-obeyesekere-cook-debate|Cook debate]] and the direct source [[sewell-william-jr|Sewell]]'s [[eventful-sociology|eventful sociology]] builds on. Its running polemic is against **world-system theory**'s denial of indigenous cultural autonomy and against structural-Marxists Hindess and Hirst — continuous with the anti-utilitarian argument of *Culture and Practical Reason*. Tension with already-ingested sources: it presses the [[eventful-sociology|eventful]] program's "structure of the conjuncture" toward a *singular, purely symbolic* conception of structure that Sewell explicitly reverses (a prior "conjuncture of structures"; resources as well as schemas) — recorded on [[sahlins-marshall]] and [[structure-of-the-conjuncture]].
