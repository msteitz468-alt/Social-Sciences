## Volume Synthesis Note

*Distinction* is the large-n empirical pillar of Bourdieu’s cultural sociology: it takes the practice-theory apparatus (habitus, capital, field) and shows — via a 1963 + 1967–68 French survey and a vast secondary-data apparatus — that taste is a system of classification homologous to social space. Relative to already-ingested *Reproduction* (school mechanism) and *Outline*/*Logic* (philosophical anthropology of practice), this source supplies the **lifestyle topology**, the **three-pole class-habitus of taste** (aesthetic disposition / cultural goodwill / taste for necessity), **reconversion** under structural change, and the **Kantian postscript** that makes pure aesthetics a misrecognized class ethos. Methodologically it is frank about oversampling, farmer exclusion, and the questionnaire’s weak hold on modality — which should keep all findings France- and period-stamped. The wiki’s cultural-capital and habitus pages now rest on education + practice + taste legs rather than promissory notes about *Distinction*.
