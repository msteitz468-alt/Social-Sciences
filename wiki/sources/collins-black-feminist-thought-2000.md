# Black Feminist Thought (Collins 2000, 2nd ed.)

Patricia Hill Collins's *Black Feminist Thought* is a programmatic statement of Black
feminism as **critical social theory**: knowledge produced by and for African-American
women that documents their collective standpoint and works toward Black women's
empowerment and social justice. First published 1990; this ingest is the substantially
revised second edition (2000), which adds sexuality and nation as forms of oppression,
develops the **domains-of-power** framework, and extends the analysis transnationally.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Black Feminist Thought* argues a single connected thesis: that African-American women, from
their location in intersecting oppressions, have produced a collective body of critical
social theory whose purpose is empowerment and social justice. The 2nd edition's advances
over the 1990 original are structural — it adds **sexuality** and **nation** as axes of
oppression, consolidates the **matrix of domination** into an explicit four-domains-of-power
architecture, and opens a preliminary **transnational** frame — while preserving the first
edition's core (standpoint, controlling images, self-definition, the ethic-of-caring
epistemology).

For this wiki the book is a **theoretical work**, not an empirical study: its interviews,
oral histories, literature, and music are illustrations of a standpoint, and its claims are
filed in attributed voice. It anchors the [[intersectionality]] and [[standpoint-theory]]
lineages and connects to already-ingested gender sociology through the
[[doing-difference-debate]] (Collins's "difference without power" critique) and adjacent
pages on [[doing-gender]] and gender theory. It is the collection's first major entry in
**contemporary Black feminist / critical race sociology**; the coverage note below flags
that adjacent primary sources (Crenshaw, Davis, Lorde, hooks, the Combahee River Collective)
remain un-ingested, so much of this page's genealogy currently rests on Collins's own
citations rather than on those works directly.
