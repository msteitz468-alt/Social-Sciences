## Volume Synthesis Note

Lefebvre’s charter for critical spatial theory: space is a social product analyzed through a dialectical triad (spatial practice / representations of space / representational spaces = perceived / conceived / lived), historicized from absolute through historical to capitalist abstract space, with differential space as the horizon seeded by abstract space’s contradictions. Methodologically he seeks a unitary theory of physical, mental, and social fields against specialist fragmentation and semiological “reading” of space. Politically he binds Gramscian hegemony to space and insists that revolution without a new space is incomplete; class struggle alone differentiates planetary abstract space. Harvey’s Afterword supplies biography and reception: POS as the 1968–74 urban sequence’s culmination and a “detonating” political enquiry for anglophone geography.
