## Volume Synthesis Note

*Shamanism, Colonialism, and the Wild Man* is the wiki’s densest single treatment of **colonial terror as cultural production** and of **shamanic healing as colonial image-work**. Part One reconstructs the Putumayo rubber boom through Casement, Hardenburg, and company speech as a [[culture-of-terror]] in a [[space-of-death]], organized by [[epistemic-murk]] and a colonial mirror of savagery ([[wild-man]], [[mimesis]]). Part Two follows highland–lowland [[yage|yagé]] circuits (1969–85 fieldwork) where montage nights, envy, and [[implicit-social-knowledge]] mobilize the same image pool to heal — including white colonists — and closes by circulating Putumayo power back into the [[cauca-valley-afro-colombians|Cauca]] world of the 1980 devil book. Methodologically the book is Benjaminian: against historicist narcotic, for montage learned from terror and shamanism. Read as a diptych with [[taussig-devil-commodity-fetishism-1980]]. Collection gaps: Casement primaries; recent Putumayo historiography; *Mimesis and Alterity*; society pages for Inga/Siona/Cofán disaggregated from Taussig’s montage; June Nash for Bolivia balance.
