## Volume Synthesis

Avrich sets the rising inside the transition crisis from [[war-communism]] to the [[new-economic-policy]], which [[lenin-vladimir|Lenin]] treated as the gravest since 1917. He records both poles of the contemporary controversy — Bolshevik White-conspiracy charges versus libertarian martyrdom for free soviets — and argues for a third: a **spontaneous** mass revolt of sailors and garrison that emigres hoped to exploit (Secret Memorandum real and detailed) but **did not engineer**, with no material aid delivered in time. The military narrative covers failed first ice assault, Oranienbaum executions, and final crush 16–18 March under Tukhachevsky. Programmatically Avrich reads the rebels as **anarcho-populist**: free soviets of left multi-tendency workers/sailors, not liberal multipartyism; anti–War Communism economics; closest affinity to SR Maximalists. NEP, he holds, was **already decided** before the outbreak (Politburo 8 Feb; draft 24 Feb) and was **hastened** not caused by Kronstadt; concurrently the Tenth Congress used the rising as cudgel for faction ban and intensified monopoly of power. Closing position: defeat of Kronstadt and smothering of left opposition made "toilers' democracy" a dead letter and totalitarianism a **likely eventuality** (not strict inevitability) — attributed Avrich, with Serge quoted on multiple "germs" in Bolshevism.
