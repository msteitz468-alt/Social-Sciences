# On the Road of the Winds (Kirch 2017)

Patrick Vinton Kirch, *On the Road of the Winds: An Archaeological History of the Pacific
Islands before European Contact*, Revised and Expanded Edition (Oakland: University of
California Press, 2017; first edition 2000). ISBN 9780520292819.

The standard single-author synthesis of Oceanic prehistory, spanning ~50,000 years from the
Pleistocene settlement of Sahul to the eve of European contact. Where
*[[kirch-evolution-polynesian-chiefdoms-1984|The Evolution of the Polynesian Chiefdoms]]*
(1984) treated Polynesia as a laboratory for chiefdom evolution, *Road of the Winds*
enlarges the frame to the entire Pacific: Near Oceania (Australia, New Guinea, the Bismarcks
and Solomons — settled deep in the Pleistocene) and Remote Oceania (everything beyond the
main Solomons, settled only after the mid-Holocene Lapita/Austronesian dispersal). Kirch
organizes the book as a history of the discipline (Ch. 1), an island-biogeography primer
(Ch. 2), regional culture-historical syntheses (Chs. 3–8), and a closing comparative essay
on "big structures and large processes" (Ch. 9).
