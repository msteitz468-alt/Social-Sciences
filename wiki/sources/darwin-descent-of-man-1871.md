# The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex (Darwin, 1871)

Charles Darwin's *The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex* (2 vols., 1871) is the founding application of evolutionary theory to humankind: (1) whether man is descended from some pre-existing form; (2) the manner of his development; (3) the value of the differences between the so-called races of man. Part I treats human descent, mental continuity with animals, the evolution of the intellectual and [[moral-sense|moral faculties]], genealogy, and [[race|races]]. Part II develops [[sexual-selection]] at encyclopedic length across the animal kingdom and then applies it to secondary sexual characters of man and to racial differentiation. The book is a **theoretical synthesis**, not a primary study: it earns no `studies/` page. It feeds [[biological-anthropology]], [[darwin-charles]], [[sexual-selection]], [[natural-selection]], [[monogenism]]/[[polygenism]], [[race]], [[antiquity-of-man]], and the Victorian evolutionist network ([[tylor-edward-burnett]], [[lubbock-john]], [[mclennan-john-ferguson]], [[huxley-thomas-henry]], [[spencer-herbert]]).

## Claims Extracted

All claims are Darwin’s, in attributed voice (theoretical synthesis; “hardly any original facts in regard to man”). Extraction: 6 parallel agents (vol. I Intro–IV; V–VI; VIII–X; vol. I endnotes; vol. II XII–XVI; XVII–XVIII) + main-thread Ch VII (races) and Ch XIX–XXI (man + conclusion). Zoological catalogues compressed to principles.

**Part I — Descent of man.**
- *Aims & antiquity* (Intro): three objects (descent; manner of development; value of racial differences); [[antiquity-of-man]] taken as granted (Lyell, Lubbock); Huxley on man–ape morphological interval; Haeckel/Wallace predecessors; sexual-selection preview for race.
- *Evidence of descent* (ch. I): homology, embryology, rudiments as three classes of facts; community of descent.
- *Mental powers* (ch. II–III): no fundamental mental difference man vs higher mammals (degree not kind); language origin; tools; beauty; spirits/religion via Tylor, McLennan, Lubbock; [[moral-sense]] from social instincts + intellect + praise/blame.
- *Manner of development* (ch. IV): variability, use/disuse, Malthusian checks, [[natural-selection]] on man; erect posture suite; limits of NS.
- *Intellect & morals in primeval and civilised times* (ch. V): intellect under NS; tribe-level selection for moral qualities; within-tribe altruism paradox; civilisation checks elimination of the weak (Greg/Wallace/Galton) yet sympathy must not be suppressed; progress not invariable; all civilised nations once barbarous (vs [[degenerationism]]).
- *Genealogy* (ch. VI): man in Primates (Catarhine/Old World stem); Africa probable birthplace; missing links not decisive; deep vertebrate genealogy to Ascidian-like form.
- *Races* (ch. VII): species criteria applied and rejected; sub-species rank; evolutionary [[monogenism]] ends monogenist–[[polygenism]] dispute; extinction via tribe/race competition (period construct); external race traits not from climate/NS → [[sexual-selection]].

**Part II — Sexual selection.**
- *Principles* (ch. VIII): SS vs NS; primary vs [[secondary-sexual-characters]]; male combat + female choice; inheritance laws; sex ratios.
- *Lower animals–mammals* (ch. IX–XVIII): principles and exemplars across insects, fishes, birds (female aesthetic choice central), mammals; bridge via Quadrumana ornaments.
- *Man* (ch. XIX–XX): sex differences parallel Quadrumana; law of battle; race-relative beauty; interferences (promiscuity, infanticide, betrothal, slavery of women); hairlessness and skin colour via SS; SS “by far the most efficient” for external racial differences.
- *Conclusion* (ch. XXI): main conclusion descent from lower form; hairy-quadruped reconstruction; moral-sense and language–brain feedback summaries; irreligion charge answered.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Descent* is a single argument in two unequal parts. Part I places man inside evolutionary natural history: bodily and mental continuity, a naturalized [[moral-sense]], and races as diverging sub-species of one stock. Part II arms [[sexual-selection]] at a length Darwin himself called “inordinate,” then returns to man to explain sex differences and racial exteriors that utility-driven [[natural-selection]] cannot. The book is the founding application of Darwinism to humankind for [[biological-anthropology]], while remaining a Victorian theoretical synthesis that mines travellers and evolutionist ethnologists rather than producing primary ethnographic or fossil data.

Against the wiki’s holdings, *Descent* supplies the biological pole of founding-era evolutionary thought (alongside Tylor’s cultural pole in *[[tylor-primitive-culture-1871|Primitive Culture]]*, same year), re-grounds [[monogenism]]/[[polygenism]] in evolutionary terms, and is a root text both for evolutionary ethics and for the contested afterlife labeled [[social-darwinism]] (ch. V — quote carefully: Darwin refuses to abandon sympathy). It earns no `studies/` page (theory, not empirical design) but warrants a [[hubs/thinkers/darwin-charles|Thinkers Hub]] analysis. Sensitive race and sex-selection-of-man material is fully extracted in attributed voice and must not migrate into wiki-voice findings on [[race]] or sex difference.
