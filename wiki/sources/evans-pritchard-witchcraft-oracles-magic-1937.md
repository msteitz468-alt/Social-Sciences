## Volume Synthesis Note

Evans-Pritchard's monograph is a **system ethnography of misfortune**: [[witchcraft|*mangu*]] as organic hereditary psychic injury (distinct from sorcery), explained as [[second-spear|secondary cause]] alongside empirical processes; [[poison-oracle|*benge*]] and lesser oracles as daily decision technology; [[magic|*ngua*]] as counter-witchcraft including leechcraft, witch-doctors, vengeance-magic, and closed associations. The three form a triangle that peaks at death and vengeance. Methodologically the book and App. IV model theory-informed [[participant-observation]], language mastery, and situational analysis. Theoretically it is the primary ethnographic exhibit for the [[rationality-debate]] and the founding type-case of modern witchcraft studies. Colonial production (Anglo-Egyptian Sudan survey; bans on direct vengeance; settlement policy) is part of the system observed, not mere background. The 1976 abridgement is pedagogically complete on core arguments; the full 1937 and later restudies remain desirable.
