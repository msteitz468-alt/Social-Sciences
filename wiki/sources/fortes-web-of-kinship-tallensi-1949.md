## Bibliographic Record

Meyer Fortes, *The Web of Kinship among the Tallensi: The Second Part of an Analysis of the Social Structure of a Trans-Volta Tribe* (London: Oxford University Press for the International African Institute, 1949; 2nd impression 1957; 3rd impression 1967, Anthropological Publications, Oosterhout / OUP). Dedicated to Sonia L. Fortes, co-fieldworker. Foreword dated Oxford 1948. Completes the investigation of Tale social structure begun in *Dynamics of Clanship* (1945) by carrying the analysis into family life and the social ties that grow directly out of marriage and parenthood — “the domain of kinship, in the current usage of social anthropology.”

**Principal attributed findings integrated:**

1. **Warp/woof architecture** — *Dynamics* lineage framework vs *Web* kinship starting from the individual; lineage and domestic organization interpenetrate like blood and tissue (political-jural-ritual vs economic-reproductive ends).
2. **Cognatic vs agnatic** — corporate agnatic ties vs personal cognatic ties; kindred-as-unit rejected; *doyam* vs *deen*.
3. **Patriliny’s ritual coefficient** — genitor/pater; legitimate paternity; conception theory; inheritance and *bɔyar*.
4. **Uterine *soog* and complementary filiation** — non-corporate maternal web; witchcraft/*tyuk*; centrifugal matrilateral force vs centripetal patriliny.
5. **Joint family / homestead *yir*** — architecture maps structure; 1934 N=61 developmental cycle; fission and reintegration.
6. **Marriage and affines** — corporate sexual rights; co-wives/*dug*; wife’s paternal claims; *poyasama*.
7. **Parenthood and generations** — absolute moral bond; generational tension; first-born taboos; *Yin*; ancestors as parent images; GP–GC foil.
8. **Siblings** — *mabiirat*, *soog*, *sunzo*, *nyeer*, twins, levirate/sororate grades.
9. **Extra-clan web** — *ahab*/*ahay* avunculate; gifts/fosterage/ritual; father’s sister limited; socio-spatial field (Tongo sample).
10. **Functions of kinship (Ch. XII)** — irreducible articulating principle; moral mechanism of interdependence; positions vs Malinowski, with R-B and Firth.
