# A Forest of Kings (Schele & Freidel, 1990)

Popular-scholarly **narrative political history** of the ancient Maya by epigrapher–art historian [[schele-linda|Linda Schele]] and archaeologist [[freidel-david|David Freidel]] (William Morrow, 1990). Reconstructs Preclassic through Terminal Classic (and Early Postclassic northern) political and ritual history from hieroglyphic texts, iconography, and excavation — the breakthrough book that brought “Maya kings as history” to a general audience after the decipherment generation’s technical advances.

**Relation to vault sources:** Upstream of the handbook synthesis in [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015]] and the dynastic chronicle in [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008]]; peer popular program to *The Blood of Kings* (Schele & Miller 1986). Historiography of the decipherment context densified in [[coe-breaking-the-maya-code-2012]]. **All narrative reconstructions and causal claims are attributed to Schele & Freidel (1990)** unless independently corroborated.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*A Forest of Kings* (1990) is the vault’s primary **popular-scholarly narrative** of lowland Maya political history from the decipherment generation — the book that taught a wide audience to expect named kings, dated wars, and dynastic strategy rather than a timeless ceremonial civilization. Where [[martin-grube-chronicle-maya-kings-2008|Martin & Grube 2008]] delivers reign-level chronicle under a formalized [[overkingship]] model, and [[coe-houston-the-maya-2015|Coe & Houston 2015]] / [[sharer-traxler-ancient-maya-2006|Sharer & Traxler 2006]] synthesize settlement and collapse science, Schele & Freidel own the **story arc and cosmological frame**: Wacah Chan / bloodletting kingship ideology; Preclassic *ahau* at [[cerros]]; Early Classic Tikal–Uaxactun war; seventh-century “star wars”; Palenque succession theology; Yaxchilan *cahalob*; Copan–Quirigua rupture; Chichen cosmopolitan/*multepal* reading; Terminal Classic as crisis of literate divine kingship.

**What this ingest adds:** source + study pages; [[freidel-david]] thinker; [[cerros]] site; densification of [[schele-linda]], [[classic-maya]], [[classic-maya-collapse-debate]], major Classic sites, and kingship concepts with **(as of 1990)** date-stamps. Load-bearing attributed spine matches later M&G arcs (378, 562, 695, 738, southern fall) while preserving S&F-specific positions (reject Teotihuacan usurpation of Tikal; reject sequential pure-Maya/pure-Toltec Chichen; female kings at Palenque; narrative cosmology).

**Limits:** interpretive FLAG — reconstructed monologues are devices; nicknames and some political details superseded; no post-1990 paleoclimate/LIDAR. Pair always with Martin & Grube for updated dynastic facts and with C&H/S&T for handbook breadth.
