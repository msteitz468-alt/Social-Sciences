# Outline of a Theory of Practice (Bourdieu, 1972/1977)

Pierre Bourdieu's founding systematic statement of [[practice-theory|practice theory]] — the book in which [[habitus]], [[doxa]], [[symbolic-capital|symbolic capital]], [[symbolic-violence|symbolic violence]], and the [[strategy-vs-rule|strategy-not-rule]] critique of structuralist objectivism receive their first extended theoretical elaboration. Translated by Richard Nice from *Esquisse d'une théorie de la pratique* (1972), revised. See the deep treatment at [[hubs/theory/practice-theory|Theory Hub — Practice Theory]] and the thinker summary [[bourdieu-pierre|Pierre Bourdieu]].

## What This Source Is

A theoretical monograph built on ethnographic materials from [[kabyle|Kabyle]] Algeria (and, secondarily, Béarn). Its argument is epistemological before it is substantive: it seeks the prerequisites for "a truly scientific discourse about human behaviour, that is, an adequate theory of practice which must include a theory of scientific practice" (translator's foreword). The book is organized in four chapters:

1. **The objective limits of objectivism** — the critique of the structuralist/legalist model of practice; the move "from the mechanics of the model to the dialectic of strategies"; the *sense of honour* (*nif*/*h'urma*); the fallacies of the rule; the case study of parallel-cousin marriage and matrimonial strategies.
2. **Structures and the habitus** — the false dilemma of mechanism vs finalism; the definition of [[habitus]] as "systems of durable, transposable dispositions"; the dialectic of objectification and embodiment.
3. **Generative schemes and practical logic** — the synoptic illusion of the calendar; the "economy of logic" and polythetic practical logic; the body as geometer; Kabyle ritual (union/separation, thresholds, rites of passage).
4. **Structures, habitus, power** — [[doxa]], orthodoxy, and heterodoxy; [[symbolic-capital]]; [[symbolic-violence|modes of domination]] (the shift from personal to objectified/institutionalized domination).

## Relation to the Rest of the Bourdieu Corpus in This Vault

*Outline* is the earliest and most programmatic of the three Bourdieu primary bases now ingested: it precedes *[[bourdieu-logic-of-practice-1990|The Logic of Practice]]* (1980/1990), which rewrites and extends the same apparatus, and *[[bourdieu-passeron-reproduction-1990|Reproduction]]* (with [[passeron-jean-claude|Passeron]], 1970), which applies an early version to the French educational system. Where *Logic* anchored the practice-theory pages before, *Outline* now supplies the founding formulations of the core concepts and the anti-structuralist polemic in its sharpest early form.

## Reliability

See `reliability_notes`. A theoretical work in artifact-neutral terms: strongly argued, deliberately anti-positivist, and self-consciously reflexive about its own (colonial, wartime) conditions of production. Its Kabyle ethnography is not presented with systematic method sections; it functions as illustration of the theoretical apparatus and should not be treated as an independent evidentiary base for Kabyle society beyond what the [[kabyle]] page attributes to Bourdieu's fieldwork of the period.
