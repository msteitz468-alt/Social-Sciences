# Dynamics of Contention (McAdam, Tarrow & Tilly, 2001)

Programmatic statement of the **[[contentious-politics-theory|dynamics-of-contention / mechanism–process program]]** by [[mcadam-doug|Doug McAdam]], [[tarrow-sidney|Sidney Tarrow]], and [[tilly-charles|Charles Tilly]]. Dissatisfied with compartmentalization among studies of strikes, wars, revolutions, social movements, and other political struggle, the authors shift from static single-actor models (including their own earlier work) toward **dynamic interaction**, and from general laws for whole event families toward **recurrent causal mechanisms and processes** within smaller episodes.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Dynamics of Contention* is this vault's primary charter for the **McAdam–Tarrow–Tilly mechanism–process program**. Relative to Tilly's *From Mobilization to Revolution* (already ingested), DOC keeps polity and repertoire heritage but **abandons covering-law and static-box ambitions** — including self-critique of the classic social-movement agenda the co-authors helped build. The book's wager is that brokerage, certification, attribution of threat/opportunity, social appropriation, category formation, identity shift, object shift, and related mechanisms recur across wars, revolutions, movements, nationalism, and democratization, while large-scale outcomes diverge by sequence, combination, and context. Empirically it is a **secondary-literature comparative synthesis** (fifteen major episodes), not new fieldwork; theoretically it is a research-program manifesto. For the wiki it seeds [[contentious-politics]] and a cluster of mechanism concepts, densifies [[mcadam-doug]], [[tarrow-sidney]], and [[tilly-charles]], and links democratization/revolution pages to protected-consultation and anti-general-law readings. Adjacent later programs — [[strategic-action-fields-theory]], Tilly *IBST* boundary/identity work — re-embed pieces of this vocabulary.
