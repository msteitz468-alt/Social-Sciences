# Notes to Engels's Ludwig Feuerbach (1892/1905)

The text Lenin mined for "the materialist Plekhanov" in M&E — now first-hand, so those borrowings stop being Lenin-mediated. ~23k words, 1-agent extraction.

## Content (attributed)

- **The hieroglyph formulation in situ** (1892, Note 9): "Our sensations are in their way hieroglyphs which inform us of what is taking place in reality. The hieroglyphs do not resemble the events conveyed by them. But they can with complete fidelity convey... the relations existing between them" — the exact sentence the later dispute turned on. Sign-fidelity of *relations*, not resemblance of *contents*: the original is more precise than either side's later gloss ([[reflection-theory]]).
- **Kantian causality answered** by "experiment and industry" (converging with Engels's alizarin argument); Sechenov quoted for realist epistemology.
- **The 1905 preface theses:** motion as "contradiction in action"; dialectics vs formal logic; a direct swipe at Machism/solipsism — his mature restatement two years before the Bogdanov letters.
- **Animism and dualism:** the mind/matter problem traced (via Engels) to "savage" spirit-explanations of dreams and death — the same primitive-religion genealogy his *Monist View* and Bogdanov's *Empiriomonism* each retell differently ([[ideology]]).

## Provenance notes

The hieroglyph self-correction is *not* here — it lives in [[plekhanov-materialismus-militans-1908]] (1905/06, acknowledged there); this text carries the uncorrected 1892 original. The "ichthyosaurus"/"object existed long before the subject" passages Lenin attributes to "Plekhanov's *L. Feuerbach*" are not in this transcription — flagged unresolved rather than silently assumed to be here.
