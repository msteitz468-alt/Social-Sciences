# Dialectic of Enlightenment (Horkheimer & Adorno, 1947)

## Bibliographic Record

*Dialectic of Enlightenment* (*Dialektik der Aufklärung*) is the programmatic
text of first-generation [[critical-theory|Critical Theory]], co-authored by
[[horkheimer-max|Max Horkheimer]] and [[adorno-theodor-w|Theodor W. Adorno]]
during their wartime exile in Los Angeles. Drafts circulated in 1944 under the
title *Philosophische Fragmente* (mimeographed by the Institut für
Sozialforschung, published by Querido in Amsterdam); the book proper appeared as
*Dialektik der Aufklärung* in 1947. The authors added a new preface for the
1969 reissue but declined to revise the text, granting it "the status of
documentation." It is dedicated to Friedrich Pollock. The ingested edition is
the Verso Classics paperback (John Cumming translation).

The book comprises: a **Preface/Introduction**; the central essay **"The Concept
of Enlightenment"**; two **Excursuses** — **I: "Odysseus or Myth and
Enlightenment"** and **II: "Juliette or Enlightenment and Morality"**; **"The
Culture Industry: Enlightenment as Mass Deception"**; **"Elements of
Anti-Semitism: Limits of Enlightenment"** (seven theses); and a closing set of
aphoristic **"Notes and Drafts."**

## Central Thesis

The book advances one thesis in two directions (attributed throughout to
Horkheimer & Adorno): **"Myth is already enlightenment; and enlightenment
reverts to mythology."** Enlightenment — understood not as a historical period
but as the whole program of demythologizing, calculating, dominating reason from
Homer through Bacon to positivism — carries domination in its structure from the
start, and, carried to its logical end, turns against the thinking subject and
reverts to a new barbarism. See [[dialectic-of-enlightenment]] for the thesis,
[[instrumental-reason]] and [[domination-of-nature]] for its mechanism,
[[mimesis]] for its repressed other, and [[culture-industry]] and
[[false-projection]] for its two demonstrations in mass culture and anti-Semitism.

## Reliability and Genre Notes

This is a **theoretical work** and a genre hybrid — philosophy fused with
sociology, psychoanalysis, and political economy, written in a deliberately
"fragmentary" essayistic form the authors defend as itself resistant to the
"identity thinking" they attack. Its empirical materials (Hollywood cinema,
American commercial radio, jazz arrangement, opinion polling, Sade's *Juliette*,
the Odyssey, Nazi propaganda) are **illustrations deployed for the argument, not
independent findings**; per the wiki's source-type handling they do not become
evidence on subject-matter pages. The book's most contested move — that
instrumental reason and the Enlightenment are implicated in fascism and the
Holocaust — is recorded as a position on
[[dialectic-of-enlightenment-debate]], not asserted in wiki voice.
