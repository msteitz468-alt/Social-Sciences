## Volume Synthesis Note

*Body & Soul* is the wiki’s first full carnal ethnography and the principal U.S. urban demonstration of Bourdieu’s learning-by-body thesis. It dual-serves: (1) a method charter for [[carnal-sociology]] / observant participation — body as tool, theoretically armed immersion, communicating-vessels writing; (2) a craft-world monography of the [[woodlawn-boys-club|Woodlawn Boys Club]] (1988–92) that theorizes [[pugilistic-habitus]], [[bodily-capital]], gym-as-sanctuary, and the local pro fight market (Studio 104; Golden Gloves). Structurally it links [[practice-theory]] to Chicago urban sociology while polemically recusing disorganization and underclass frames — aligning more with Wacquant’s later *Urban Outcasts* program than with Wilson’s residual underclass vocabulary, even though Wilson collaboration occasioned the entry. Compared with already-ingested urban ethnographies ([[whyte-street-corner-society-study|Whyte]], [[anderson-code-of-the-street-study|Anderson]]), the distinctive move is **apprenticeship combat** as epistemic instrument, not only hanging around. Gaps remaining: secondary critiques; *Urban Outcasts* / carceral monographs; multi-site gym comparison; gender of the carnal method.
