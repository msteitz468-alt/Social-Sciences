# In Search of the Indo-Europeans (Mallory 1989 / 1991 pb)

Bibliographic record for J. P. Mallory, *In Search of the Indo-Europeans: Language, Archaeology and Myth* (Thames & Hudson, 1989; first paperback edition 1991). See [[mallory-j-p]]. Principal late-1980s English survey of the steppe/Pontic-Caspian case for Proto-Indo-European origins; densifies [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]] Position A alongside [[gimbutas-marija|Gimbutas]] and later [[anthony-david-w|Anthony]] (2007).

## Overview

Mallory argues that Proto-Indo-European is a reconstructible linguistic entity whose speakers can be approached through the combined evidence of comparative linguistics, linguistic palaeontology, comparative mythology (including Dumézilian tripartition), and archaeological cultures of the Pontic-Caspian steppe and its margins. He surveys early IE peoples of Asia and Europe, reconstructs PIE culture and religion from the lexicon and myth, reviews homeland criteria and rivals, then proposes a Pontic-Caspian archaeological sequence culminating in the Yamnaya cultural-historical area and subsequent expansions. The book is deliberately a **survey of the state of knowledge** as much as a single homeland solution; the Foreword states he has not written it merely to propose yet another homeland, while still defending mainstream steppe-oriented conclusions against Renfrew and Gamkrelidze–Ivanov.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Mallory 1989 is the vault’s **missing English survey layer** between Renfrew 1987 (Position B program) and Anthony 2007 (Position A archaeological refinement). Its load-bearing moves are methodological as much as geographic: IE is linguistic first; homeland is a temporal slice of continuum not a singularity; multi-criterion constraints (neighbours, palaeontology, substrates, archaeology) converge on Pontic-Caspian without pots=people absolutism; Renfrew is rejected on timing, place, and results; Yamnaya cultural-historical area anchors late PIE archaeology; expansion uses migration + bilingualism + social permeability with explicit anti-teleology. Ch.9 supplies the field’s reflexivity on Aryan misuse. Post-2015 aDNA (Reich) and Anthony’s wool/wheel termini/franchising must be layered *on top of* Mallory, not read back into 1989. Four-way non-identity is Mallory’s own Foreword rule as much as the wiki’s.
