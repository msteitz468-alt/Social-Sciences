# Racism without Racists (Bonilla-Silva, 2018)

Bibliographic record for [[bonilla-silva-eduardo|Eduardo Bonilla-Silva]], *Racism without Racists: Color-Blind Racism and the Persistence of Racial Inequality in America*, 5th edition (Rowman & Littlefield, 2018). The book that named and anatomized [[colorblind-racism|color-blind racism]] as the dominant post–civil rights racial ideology in the United States. Analyzed as a research act in [[bonilla-silva-racism-without-racists-study]].

## Overview

Bonilla-Silva's central argument (attributed): the United States sustains a system of racial domination that no longer requires avowed racists. In the "[[the-new-racism|new racism]]" era, white privilege is reproduced through covert, institutional, and apparently nonracial mechanisms, and is legitimated by a color-blind racial ideology organized around four interpretive frames ([[colorblind-racism|abstract liberalism, naturalization, cultural racism, and minimization of racism]]) and a distinctive style of racial talk. Racism, on his account, is best understood not as individual prejudice but as a [[racialized-social-system|racialized social system]].
