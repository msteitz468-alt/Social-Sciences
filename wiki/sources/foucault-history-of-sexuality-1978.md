## Key Claims Extracted

All in attributed voice (Foucault's theses). Full development on the linked pages.

- **Against the repressive hypothesis** — the modern West did not silence sex but subjected it to an "incitement to discourse"; the nineteenth century "multiplied" rather than reduced sexualities. → [[the-repressive-hypothesis]], [[discourse-foucauldian]]
- **Scientia sexualis vs. ars erotica** — the West alone built a *science* of sexuality, on the ritual of confession. → [[scientia-sexualis]], [[confession]]
- **Analytics of power** — power is relational, productive, "everywhere," exercised not possessed, comes "from below," intentional-yet-nonsubjective; "where there is power, there is resistance." → [[analytics-of-power]], [[power-knowledge]]
- **The deployment of sexuality** — sexuality is a "historical construct," produced through four strategic figures (hysterical woman, masturbating child, Malthusian couple, perverse adult), and applied first to the bourgeoisie itself. → [[deployment-of-sexuality]]
- **Biopower** — modern power shifted from the sovereign "right to take life" to a "power to foster life," organized at two poles (the body's disciplines, the population's regulation), with sex as the pivot. → [[biopower]]
- **Method** — a genealogy / "history of the present." → [[genealogy]]
- **Framing debate** — Foucault vs. the Freudo-Marxian (Reich/Marcuse) repression thesis. → [[repressive-hypothesis-debate]]

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The History of Sexuality* Vol. I is the hinge of Foucault's mid-1970s turn to power, and it enters this vault as a theoretical charter rather than an empirical study. Its single argumentative arc runs: the received story of Victorian *repression* (Part One) is displaced by a history of *incitement to discourse* (Part Two), which the West organized as a *science of sexuality* grounded in *confession* (Part Three); this requires a new *analytics of power* — productive, not juridical (Part Four, "Method") — that reveals *sexuality itself as a deployment*, produced through four strategic figures and rooted first in the bourgeois "class body" (Part Four, "Domain"/"Periodization"); and the whole is finally situated within *biopower*, the modern administration of life, with sex as the pivot between body and population (Part Five).

Its most distinctive contribution to the wiki is a **theory of power that is not repression** — a direct counterweight to the Marxian and Freudo-Marxian frameworks already ingested. It converses pointedly with [[marcuse-herbert|Marcuse]]'s [[repressive-desublimation]] (both hold that liberalized sexuality can serve domination, but Foucault denies the underlying repression model), and it offers the [[power-knowledge]] and [[biopower]] vocabulary that later reshaped criminology, the sociology of the body, science studies, and governmentality research. Tensions with existing pages: Foucault's productive-power thesis stands against the conflict-theoretic and Marxist accounts of the state as an instrument of class domination (recorded, not adjudicated, in [[repressive-hypothesis-debate]]); and his radical constructionism about "sexuality" sits against biological and psychological accounts. A hub-level treatment (Thinkers Hub for Foucault; Theory Hub for the analytics of power / biopower) is warranted by the figure and the program, but is deliberately deferred until *Discipline and Punish*, the later *History of Sexuality* volumes, and the Collège de France lectures are ingested — a single introductory volume cannot responsibly carry a graduate-level biography of the whole corpus.
