# First Farmers: The Origins of Agricultural Societies (Bellwood, 2005)

Peter Bellwood, *First Farmers: The Origins of Agricultural Societies* (Wiley-Blackwell, 2005). ## Reliability and Reflexivity

- **Synthesis, not primary data.** Regional chronologies and genetic claims reflect the literature Bellwood commanded as of ~2003–04; post-2010 aDNA has substantially revised European and other farmer–forager admixture fractions — treat ch. 11 as historiography of classical population genetics, not current science.
- **Programmatic thesis.** The early farming dispersal hypothesis is Bellwood's research program; competing positions (indigenous Mesolithic adoption, elite dominance language shift, steppe IE, entangled-bank Austronesian models) are recorded on debate pages, not adjudicated here.
- **Four-way non-identity.** Bellwood often argues *correlations* among material culture, language families, and genes at the scale of early expansions; the wiki keeps culture ≠ language ≠ genes ≠ people and records his correlations as attributed claims with `associated_with` where needed.
- **Race language.** The book occasionally uses older "racial" vocabulary for phenotypic variation; wiki pages rephrase in contemporary population-genetic / skeletal terms where possible and attribute when quoting the 2005 framing.
