## Bibliographic Record

M. Fortes and E. E. Evans-Pritchard (eds.), *African Political Systems* (London: published for the International African Institute by Oxford University Press, 1940). Collaborative comparative volume of eight African political systems, framed as an experiment in comparative politics and a reference for anthropology and (explicitly) colonial administration under Indirect Rule.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*African Political Systems* is the British structural-functional charter for comparative political anthropology. Its enduring architecture is the **Group A / Group B** contrast, Radcliffe-Brown’s force definition of the political, and the editors’ thesis that **mystical values of office** integrate political communities beyond utility and colonial coercion. The eight essays remain classic case sketches; the Nuer and Tallensi Group B analyses became research programs of their own. The book’s administrative entanglement (Indirect Rule) and later reanalysis of segmentary models ([[segmentary-lineage-model-debate]]) are part of its afterlife, not reasons to omit it. In this vault it opens multi-region African political coverage and links the sociology of [[the-state]] to anthropological stateless-order analysis.

## Coverage Note

First substantial **comparative African political anthropology** charter in the collection. Feeds societies across southern, east, west, and central Africa; the Group A/B typology; segmentary / acephalous analysis; chieftainship and kingship; mystical legitimation of office. Nuer deep treatment shared with concurrent *The Nuer* monograph ingest — this source's Nuer chapter is the comparative condensation only.
