# Terrorism and Communism: A Contribution to the Natural History of Revolution (Kautsky)

*Karl Kautsky.* German 1919; English National Labour Press 1920, trans. W. H. Kerridge.

The Social-Democratic classic against Bolshevik methods of [[red-terror]] and the Soviet form of [[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]. ## Volume Synthesis

Kautsky's starting point is Social Democracy's attitude to Bolshevik methods. Because Bolshevism claimed the 1871 Paris Commune (with Marx's sanction) as precursor, he builds a double historical parallel: (1) French Revolution / first Parisian revolutionary episodes → origin and **failure** of systematic terrorism; (2) second Commune (1871) → a regime that, on his reading, was **not** a Soviet Republic, seated opposition, practised almost no terrorism, and whose socialism was cautious workshop association under democratic forms — "government of the people through the people." Chapter VII supplies the ethical-sociological core: industrial civilisation and the proletariat humanise conduct; planned state terror is a regression; the world war reversed that humanisation and revived pre-Marxist power-theory (Blanqui/Bakunin). Chapter VIII applies the critique to Soviet Russia: expropriation without organisation produces chaos; "dictatorship of the proletariat" as motto absolves anti-Marxist methods; personal/factory dictatorship is incompatible with democracy (including Soviet democracy); free press is the only check on corruption, and despotism is the essence of dictatorship; terror is the **inevitable result of Communist methods**, not a contingent excess; White terror does not justify Red; Mensheviks and Left SRs held as hostages; closing thesis that world revolution proceeds only through **democracy and humanity**, not cannons and destruction of adversaries.
