# The Development of the Monist View of History (1895)

Plekhanov's masterwork and the founding theoretical text of Russian Marxism — the book Lenin said "reared a whole generation of Russian Marxists," ingested to end Plekhanov's status as the last major figure of the Lenin-era cluster documented only through others' eyes. ~115k words, 4-agent extraction.

## The argument (attributed)

A history-of-ideas staircase, each step credited and shown insufficient: **French materialism** (Holbach, Helvétius — man as product of environment, but environment explained by "opinion": a circle); **Restoration historians** (Guizot, Mignet, Thierry — constitutions rooted in social relations, social relations in "the state of property," but property unexplained); **utopian socialists** (human nature as criterion); **German idealism** (Hegel — the right method, dialectics, on an inverted basis); and **Marx**, "the genius" who grounds the state of property in the relations men "of necessity enter... in the social production of their life" — a discovery Plekhanov "boldly place[s] on a par with the discovery of Copernicus."

Load-bearing doctrine:
- **"Dialectical materialism" named and defended** (footnote): "We use the term 'dialectical materialism' because it alone can give an accurate description of the philosophy of Marx... The expression 'economic materialism' is extremely inappropriate. Marx never called himself an economic materialist." The book's sustained use popularized the term ([[dialectical-materialism]]).
- **The mediated prime mover:** productive forces determined "in the long run" by geographic environment, but once social relations exist "their further development takes place according to its own inner laws" — geography acts "through the social environment." Economy itself is "far from being a prime cause... a consequence, a 'function' of the productive forces" ([[historical-materialism]], [[base-and-superstructure]]).
- **Freedom as known necessity:** the kingdom-of-necessity/kingdom-of-freedom staircase ending "I am a god when I know. Tantum possumus, quantum scimus."
- **Against subjective sociology:** the Bruno Bauer → Lavrov → Mikhailovsky genealogy; objectivity relocated to "the relations which exist outside me"; against heroes-vs-crowd, "the kingdom of reason" comes "only when the 'crowd' itself becomes the hero of historical action" — the self-consciousness of the producers ([[class-consciousness]]).
- **Anti-Kareyev:** the dualist body/soul "synthesis" mocked; economy and social psychology as "two sides of one and the same phenomenon."
- **The appendices:** the anti-Mikhailovsky "triad" polemic (dialectics owes nothing to the triad), the same 1877 Marx letter on Russia's path that Lenin deployed in 1894, and the methodological self-defense.

## Reliability

The founding statement of Russian "orthodox" Marxism by its founder — a partisan text in the same anti-Narodnik war as [[lenin-what-the-friends-of-the-people-are-1894]] (same targets: Mikhailovsky, Kareyev; convergent arguments, independently documented). The Soviet edition's corrective apparatus is itself evidence of doctrinal drift between 1895 Plekhanov and post-1938 orthodoxy — extracted as a distinct register and useful precisely for that. Mikhailovsky's and Kareyev's positions remain reconstructed from their opponents.
