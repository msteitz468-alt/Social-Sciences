## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Power Elite* is the apex book of Mills's American stratification trilogy: after *White
Collar*'s propertyless middle class, the command posts above them. Its durable analytic
products for this wiki are the [[power-elite]] concept (positional, three-hierarchy, anti-
conspiracy, anti-simple-ruling-class), the tier map ([[middle-levels-of-power]] vs top vs
[[mass-society]]), the scoped critique of the [[theory-of-balance]], the mid-1950s
[[military-ascendancy]] thesis, and the moral-political couplet [[organized-irresponsibility]]
/ [[higher-immorality]]. Methodologically it is synthetic institutional analysis under
conditions of elite inaccessibility — not survey ethnography of the top. The standing dispute
is [[power-elite-vs-pluralism-debate]] (`reanalyzed-contested` on the study page). Alan Wolfe's
Afterword is filed as reception: corporate-elite richness and the democracy question hold; several
1950s trajectories aged. Deep analysis: [[hubs/studies/mills-power-elite-study]]. Thinkers Hub
for Mills remains deferred until *The Sociological Imagination* is ingested.
