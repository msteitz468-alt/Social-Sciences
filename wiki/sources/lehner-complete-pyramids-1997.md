# The Complete Pyramids (Lehner 1997)

Mark Lehner’s Thames & Hudson synthetic survey of Egyptian pyramids from Predynastic origins through Late Antiquity: ideology and complex form, exploration history, a whole-pyramid catalogue (Abydos–Saqqara–Giza–Abusir–Lisht and later), and a construction/labour/economy section (“The Living Pyramid”). Anchors the wiki’s Old Kingdom monument and labour archaeology spine.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Lehner 1997 is the wiki’s **first dedicated pyramid-corpus and construction-labour synthesis** by the principal modern settlement archaeologist of Giza. Where Kemp *Anatomy* theorizes the provider state and model communities, and Wilkinson narrates political rise and fall, Lehner supplies (1) a whole-catalogue sequence from Predynastic mound and mastaba antecedents through Nubian/Late Antiquity pyramids, (2) the architectural package of the [[pyramid-complex]], and (3) the “Living Pyramid” program — quarries, ramps, survey, workforce organization (crew–gang–phyle), estates/*hut ka*, and pyramid towns including early GPMP results at [[heit-el-ghurab]].

~597 claims across 8 OCR extractors. All construction-mechanism, labour-status, and “first centralized nation state” claims stay **attributed**. OCR noise on captions and tables is flagged in claims; dimensions kept approximate where uncertain. No `studies/` page (synthetic illustrated reference).
