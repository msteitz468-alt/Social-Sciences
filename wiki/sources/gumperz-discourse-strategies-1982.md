# Discourse Strategies (Gumperz 1982)

John J. [[gumperz-john-j|Gumperz]]'s programmatic monograph — the founding text of [[interactional-sociolinguistics]] and volume 1 of the CUP series of that name (companion volume: *Language and Social Identity*, ed. Gumperz, 1982, not ingested).

## Argument

Communication requires knowledge "considerably beyond... grammatical competence"; participation is sustained by [[conversational-inference]] guided by [[contextualization-cue|contextualization cues]] (both concepts canonically defined here). Against Chomskyan idealization, variationist survey method ([[labov-william]]), and — with more sympathy — the [[ethnography-of-speaking]] and [[conversation-analysis]]. [[communicative-competence]] is redefined interactionally; the Postscript ties unevenly distributed contextualization conventions to stereotyping and social inequality in gatekeeping encounters. Chapter provenance (preface): most chapters consolidate 1976–79 papers (Ch. 4 "completed in 1976"; Ch. 5 with Hannah Kaltman; Ch. 6 partly with Deborah Tannen; Ch. 9 from *Language in Society* 1978).

## Evidence base

Ten years of field studies (India, Europe, US, per preface): the [[gail-valley-slovenes|Gail Valley]] Slovenian–German language-shift study (1970s; fieldwork largely by Sarah Wickander); participant-recorded [[code-switching]] corpora (Gail Valley, Delhi Hindi–English, Chicano Spanish–English); Indian vs. Western English prosody elicitation (two principal informants — flagged "preliminary" by the author); interethnic "crosstalk" recordings in British institutional settings (airport cafeteria "Gravy" case; London counseling interview with a Pakistani-born teacher); classroom playback studies of Black English intonation; a Bay Area sermon and the late-1960s "we will kill Richard Nixon" rally speech (indictment dismissed on a technicality). Funding: NIMH MH26831, NSF; completed at IAS Princeton under NEH.
