# All Our Kin: Strategies for Survival in a Black Community

Bibliographic record for [[stack-carol|Carol B. Stack]]'s *All Our Kin* (1974). Analysis of the research act lives on the [[stack-all-our-kin-study|study page]] and the [[hubs/studies/stack-all-our-kin-study|Studies Hub]]; the community documented lives on [[the-flats]].

## What It Is

A book-length [[participant-observation]] ethnography of kinship, exchange, and domestic organization among the Black urban poor in "The Flats," the poorest section of a pseudonymous Midwestern Black community ("Jackson Harbor"). Stack's core move is to replace deficit/disorganization models of the Black family (culture-of-poverty and Moynihan-adjacent framings) with an account of **cooperative domestic networks** built through **swapping**, **personal kindreds**, and **child-keeping**.

**Structure (chapters):** Introduction (method and entrée) · 1 The Flats · 2 Black Urban Poor · 3 Swapping ("What Goes Round Comes Round") · 4 Personal Kindreds ("All Our Kin") · 5 Child-Keeping ("Gimme a Little Sugar") · 6 Domestic Networks ("Those You Count On") · 7 Women and Men ("I'm Not in Love with No Man Really") · 8 Conclusion (with John R. Lombardi) · Appendix A (AFDC case history survey) · Appendix B (interview topic outline) · Notes · Bibliography · Index.

## Coverage

- **Discipline / era:** cultural anthropology and urban sociology in the critical-turn era (published 1974; fieldwork late 1960s).
- **Region / period:** Midwestern U.S. Black urban community; residents largely descendants of Southern migration (1930s–40s) along a Chicago–South rail line; fieldwork c. 1968–early 1970s.
- **Method:** immersed participant observation (Stack lived with kin of the Waters network; son Kevin present in field); resident assistants co-designed interview topics; AFDC case-record survey; residence life histories; genealogical mapping of domestic networks.

## Reliability Notes

See frontmatter. Primary ethnographic source: strong for the fieldwork moment and for emic exchange/kinship categories; pseudonymized; white outsider positionality addressed in the Introduction (Stack rejected black-establishment sponsorship; entered via a university student from The Flats). Statistical claims from the AFDC survey are case-file measures of child-keeping and residence, not full community census. Charts/tables in the original edition may be incomplete in the ingested epub text conversion.
