# Anthropology and Anthropologists (Kuper, 2015)

Adam Kuper’s ethnographic-style history of **[[british-social-anthropology|British social anthropology]]** from the Malinowski / Radcliffe-Brown founding moment (baseline **1922**) through mid-century institutionalization, the colonial setting, postwar routinization, Leach and neo-structuralism, and the 1970s paradigm crisis that dissolved the specifically British project into a cosmopolitan discipline. Fourth edition; first appeared 1973 as *Anthropologists and Anthropology*.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Kuper supplies the vault’s **full-length institutional and intellectual history of the British school** as a single object — the companion to his later *[[kuper-invention-of-primitive-society-1988|Invention of Primitive Society]]* (1988), which targets the kinship-theory core rather than the profession’s sociology. Distinctive contributions of the 2015 text:

1. **Periodization:** 1922 baseline (Rivers’s death + founding monographs + *Dual Mandate*); ~fifty-year life of a distinctive British movement; 1970s crisis as structural, not merely generational.
2. **Faction map:** Malinowski vs Radcliffe-Brown parties; LSE / Oxford / Cambridge / Manchester sites; Fortes–Leach Cambridge dualism as lived structure for research students.
3. **Colonial chapter as debate position:** elective affinity and loose symbiosis (Malinowski–Indirect Rule; IAI/Rockefeller; RLI; applied vs purist tracks) without endorsing consistent spy-collaboration — filed as Position 9 on [[anthropology-and-colonialism-debate]].
4. **Neo-structural and crisis chapters:** Leach between Malinowski and Lévi-Strauss; Needham’s repudiation; Douglas; end of Empire and object crisis; Americanization and *Writing Culture*; cosmopolitan “new realism.”
5. **Appendix 1** maps the secondary literature (Stocking, Young’s Malinowski, Mills, Asad volume, biographies of Leach/Douglas/Gellner) — a ready historiographic checklist for the wiki.

**Tensions with prior holdings are productive:** primary ethnographies (*Argonauts*, *Andaman Islanders*, APS, *Highland Burma*, *Purity and Danger*) remain findings-in-context; Kuper supplies second-order claims about professional formation, rivalry, and colonial setting. Treat “tribe,” “obituary,” and personality judgments as **attributed**. Complements Asad 1973 (colonial-encounter charter) and Stocking (Victorian prehistory) without replacing them.

**Not covered / thin:** detailed South African / Australian regional historiographies beyond Appendix 2 sketches; full primary corpus of Goody and Gellner; Kuper’s own African ethnography; reception history of the 2015 edition itself.

**No `studies/` page** (history-of-discipline, not a primary empirical research act). **No Thinkers Hub** for Kuper (two historiographical books densify the summary; African ethnography and culture monographs still outstanding). **No Theory Hub** (the object is a national school’s history, not a single research program’s claim architecture — though [[british-social-anthropology]] and [[structural-functionalism]] carry the theory load).
