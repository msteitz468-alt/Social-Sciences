# The Interpretation of Cultures (Geertz, 1973)

Programmatic essay collection that crystallized **[[interpretive-anthropology]]** and **[[thick-description]]** as the leading late-twentieth-century alternative to functionalist, structuralist, and reductive definitions of culture. Geertz gathers fifteen years of work “only those of my essays which bear, directly and explicitly, on the concept of culture,” arguing for a narrowed semiotic concept against Tylor’s “most complex whole.”

Author: [[geertz-clifford]]. Landmark empirical essay within the volume: *[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]* (Ch. 15).

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Interpretation of Cultures* converts Geertz’s Indonesia (and Morocco) fieldwork capital into a general theory of culture as public meaning. Ch.1 supplies the method constitution ([[thick-description]], [[webs-of-significance]]); Ch.4–5 the religion engine ([[models-of-and-for]]); Ch.15 the worked example (*[[geertz-deep-play-study|Deep Play]]*). Read with *Religion of Java* and *Agricultural Involution* for the ethnographic base; with Swidler/Sewell for the program’s critical afterlife. For the vault this is the primary charter of [[interpretive-anthropology]].
