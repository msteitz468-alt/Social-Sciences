# The Discovery of Grounded Theory (Glaser & Strauss, 1967)

Barney G. Glaser and Anselm L. Strauss's methodological manifesto for **generating** theory from systematic qualitative (and, they insist, quantitative) research rather than verifying logico-deductive theory. Directed primarily at sociologists but claimed as useful for any social-phenomena researcher. The program name [[grounded-theory|grounded theory]] enters the discipline from this book.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Discovery* is the **charter text** of grounded theory as a named research program: a frank polemic plus a kit of strategies for closing the theory–research gap from the *generation* side. Part I defines comparative analysis as the general generation method, then specifies [[theoretical-sampling]], the substantive→formal pathway, the [[constant-comparative-method]], and an accounting scheme for assessing comparative studies. Part II refuses a qualitative ghetto — library caches and quantitative secondary analysis are first-class generation media; Lazarsfeld-style elaboration is repurposed for denser theory. Part III defends credibility without verification-canon monopoly, states four properties of applicable theory, roots theorizing in disciplined insight, and closes with an Epilogue assault on speculative theory's immunity licenses (via Merton 1964 on anomie).

**What this source adds to the wiki.** (1) A full method-theory node ([[grounded-theory]] + Theory Hub) bridging Chicago qualitative craft and Columbia methodological literacy. (2) Named methods ([[theoretical-sampling]], [[constant-comparative-method]]) and scope concepts ([[substantive-theory]], [[formal-theory-grounded]], [[theoretical-saturation]], [[theoretical-sensitivity]]). (3) A standing methodological debate page ([[generation-vs-verification-debate]]) that intersects Mills's [[grand-theory-vs-abstracted-empiricism-debate]] without collapsing into it. (4) Dense links to [[symbolic-interactionism]], [[chicago-school]], Blumerian [[sensitizing-concept]] / [[exploration-and-inspection]], and [[comparative-method]].

**Tensions with already-ingested sources.** Mills (1959) attacks grand theory and abstracted empiricism from a classic-craft stance; G&S supply **procedures** and refuse to abandon scientific generation language. Blumer (1969 manifesto, earlier essays) shares hostility to premature operationalism and imported schemes; G&S are more willing to codify stages and to treat quantitative data as generative. Parsonsian [[structural-functionalism]] / [[grand-theory]] appears as foil for a priori schemes. Merton is both foil (serendipity; 1964 capsule) and Columbia training reference — no Merton primary ingest yet for his own reply. Dying monographs remain the missing empirical base behind the illustrations.

**Production context.** PHS nursing grant NU-00047; hospital dying research as strategic site. Joint Columbia–Chicago authorship is methodological branding. OCR reprint is complete; illustrations must not be filed as society findings without the monographs.
