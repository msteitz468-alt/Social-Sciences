## Volume Synthesis Note

Young displaces the distributive paradigm of justice toward structural conditions of **domination** and **oppression** (self-determination and self-development). Oppression is diagnosed via **five faces**; liberation often requires a **politics of difference** and a **heterogeneous public** rather than impartial assimilation. Later chapters develop impartiality critique, body/identity politics, affirmative action and the myth of merit, and **city life** as a normative ideal of being-together-in-difference. Epilogue extends toward international justice.
