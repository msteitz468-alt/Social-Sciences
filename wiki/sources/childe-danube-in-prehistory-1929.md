## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Danube in Prehistory* is the vault's charter text for **[[archaeological-culture]]** and for **[[culture-historical-archaeology]]** as a working research program. The Preface definition — culture as a regularly associated trait complex, assumed to be the material expression of a people, with race reserved for exclusive skeletal association — is the load-bearing conceptual product; the twenty chapters then apply that unit across the Danube corridor from Palaeolithic residues to late Bronze Age urnfields and limited Scythian impact.

The book's second product is the **corridor thesis**: for more than two millennia the Danube channelled south-eastern (Aegean, Anatolian, Syrian) civilizing influences north and west as far as Britain; in Childe's Period VI the flow inverted as East-Central European movements pressed toward Troy and Macedonia, while southern energy re-entered Central Europe via the Adriatic and Alpine fringe. Absolute dates and many migration stories are 1929 constructions — Childe's own Epilogue marks chronological choices as provisional and sometimes reversible — but the culture labels and the problem of correlating material packages across space remain the everyday language of European prehistory.

Relative to already-ingested material: densifies [[corded-ware-culture]] and [[bell-beaker-culture]] with pre-aDNA culture-historical definitions beside Reich 2018 genetic readings; anchors [[neolithic]] semantic history in Childe's farming-corridor narrative; supplies the archaeological counterpart to anthropological [[diffusionism]] without collapsing into hyper-diffusionism. Outstanding Childe corpus (*Dawn*, *Man Makes Himself*, *What Happened in History*) will densify Neolithic/Urban Revolutions and popular evolutionary synthesis.
