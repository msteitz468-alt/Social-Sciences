# What Happened in History (Childe, 1942 / rev. 1954)

[[childe-v-gordon|V. Gordon Childe]]'s popular Old-World synthesis from [[palaeolithic|Palaeolithic]] "savagery" through the fall of Rome — the book that carried the **[[neolithic-revolution|Neolithic Revolution]]** and **[[urban-revolution|Urban Revolution]]** into general intellectual currency, framed as progressive economic revolutions increasing population and [[social-surplus|social surplus]]. Complements *[[childe-danube-in-prehistory-1929|The Danube in Prehistory]]* (regional culture-history) and *[[childe-dawn-european-civilization-1958|The Dawn of European Civilization]]* (continental culture maps) with a long-run materialist narrative of the "main stream" of civilization (Mesopotamia/Egypt → Greece/Rome → Atlantic Europe). Coining depth for the Revolutions is shared with *[[childe-man-makes-himself-1936|Man Makes Himself]]* (1936); this volume extends the frame through classical antiquity.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*What Happened in History* is the vault's long-run public face of Childe's materialist prehistory: two economic revolutions ([[neolithic-revolution]], [[urban-revolution]]) organized by [[social-surplus]] concentration, told as the "main stream" from alluvial Bronze Age cities through Greek Iron Age innovations to Rome's discontinuous collapse. Relative to already-ingested Childe: *Danube* and *Dawn* own culture definition and European culture maps; this book (with *Man Makes Himself*) owns the Revolution vocabulary and the progressive Old-World narrative that mid-century social science absorbed. Absolute chronology and diffusion stories are period constructions — attribute, date-stamp, and keep four-way non-identity. Closing thesis ("Progress is real if discontinuous") is a position, not wiki-voice law.
