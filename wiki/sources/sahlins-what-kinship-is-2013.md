# What Kinship Is—And Is Not (Sahlins 2013)

Marshall Sahlins's late statement on **what kinship is**: kinfolk are persons who participate intrinsically in each other's existence — **[[mutuality-of-being|mutuality of being]]**. Chapter 1 ("What Kinship Is—Culture") develops that definition across performative/constructed and procreative forms; Chapter 2 ("What Kinship Is Not—Biology") argues against the biogenetic/genealogical orthodoxy (Morgan → Scheffler–Lounsbury → folk-biology "primary" kinship). Central interlocutors: [[schneider-david-m|David M. Schneider]] (inverted via [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]), Janet Carsten / "relatedness" literature, Scheffler & Lounsbury, Gellner, Davis & Warner.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*What Kinship Is—And Is Not* is Sahlins's late definitional statement for kinship studies, continuous with the culturalist arc from *[[sahlins-culture-and-practical-reason-1976|Culture and Practical Reason]]* (culture constitutes utility) through historical anthropology (*[[sahlins-islands-of-history-1985|Islands of History]]*) into a pure **ontology of relatedness**. Where *Stone Age Economics* made kinship distance the map of [[reciprocity]], this book makes **mutuality of being** the definition of kinship itself and places kinship in one ontological regime with [[gift-exchange]], magic, and witchcraft (via [[viveiros-de-castro-eduardo|Viveiros de Castro]]). Its polemical target is the genealogical/biogenetic orthodoxy from [[morgan-lewis-henry|Morgan]] through Scheffler–Lounsbury and Gellner, and the "fictive kinship" dismissal shared with sociobiology; its historiographic hinge is [[schneider-david-m|Schneider]]'s failed closure of kinship studies, inverted by Viveiros and filled by Sahlins's mutuality formula. Methodologically self-aware (Frazerian uncontrolled comparison; exemplify, don't verify), it is weak as statistical proof and strong as concept-work. Tension with already-ingested sources: densifies the same culturalist Sahlins who attacked practical reason, now applied to kinship's oldest problem; links gift/*[[hau]]* and *[[mana]]* (already primary via SAE / Islands) into a single intersubjective regime. The generalization that **unilineal** orders build kinship into procreation while **cognatic** orders favor life-achieved mutuality connects to [[descent-theory]] and Amazonian personhood ([[personhood]], Viveiros).
