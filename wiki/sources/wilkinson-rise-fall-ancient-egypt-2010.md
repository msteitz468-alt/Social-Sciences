# The Rise and Fall of Ancient Egypt (Wilkinson 2010)

Toby Wilkinson’s single-volume narrative of Egyptian political history from Predynastic origins through the death of Cleopatra (30 BCE), emphasizing divine kingship, state power, and cycles of unity and collapse. Major loci densified: [[giza]], [[memphis]], [[thebes-egypt]], [[amarna]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

Wilkinson 2010 is a **popular chronological political history** of Egypt from Predynastic origins to Cleopatra (30 BCE), framed by durable **divine kingship** ideology and repeated unity/fragmentation cycles. ~315 claims across 5 extractors. Complements Kemp *Anatomy* (2018): Wilkinson = narrative skeleton and late periods (TIP–Ptolemaic) largely outside Kemp’s Amarna-centred edition; Kemp = bureaucracy, model towns, settlement archaeology.

Wiki footprint: thinker + Giza/Memphis/Thebes stubs; densified state/maat/Amarna/bureaucracy/temple-economy/state-formation; links into Kemp spine. No studies/ page. All interpretive rise/fall and “tyranny/golden age” language stays attributed.
