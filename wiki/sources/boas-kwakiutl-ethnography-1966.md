# Kwakiutl Ethnography (Boas, ed. Codere, 1966)

Helen Codere's edition of Franz Boas's unfinished summary ethnography of the Kwakiutl (Kwakwaka'wakw), assembled from a previously unpublished manuscript and passages of his published Kwakiutl corpus, with her substantial critical introduction on Boas's ethnographic method and the history of the Kwakiutl work.

## Volume Synthesis Note

Codere's edition fixes the unfinished Boas summary ethnography into eleven chapters plus appendixes, splicing the unpublished manuscript with the 1897 potlatch monograph, the 1909 introduction, the 1930 religion texts, the 1935 mythology conclusions, and the 1927 *Primitive Art* chapter — every splice flagged in her source notes. Read against the vault's existing Kwakiutl coverage, it grounds and complicates Benedict's Dionysian synthesis (the ceremonial "frenzy" is documented as managed theater; Boas judged the people "at heart... not warlike") and supplies the primary mechanics beneath Mauss's potlatch type (blanket credit, scaled interest, coppers). Its most wiki-productive threads: the numaym as positions-not-people, the text-collection method and the Hunt collaboration as co-authorship, and Boas's own diffusionist reasoning about the hamatsa complex — historical particularism practiced rather than programmatized.
