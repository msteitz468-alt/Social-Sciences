## Volume Synthesis Note

This collection completes the classical Du Bois node begun with *Souls*. The **Talented Tenth** essay supplies the statistical and historical program that *Souls* ch. VI only sketched (occupation tables, cascade model, Washington/Tuskegee staffing paradox). **Conservation of Races** adds the 1897 Academy race-ideal doctrine and dual American/Negro identity structure that feeds double-consciousness without replacing it. The paired **Economic Revolution** / **Religion in the South** lectures give political-economy depth (peonage, group economy, industry-and-disfranchisement settlement, Atlanta 1906) and a historical ethics of Southern Christianity for the [[black-church]] page. **Black North** opens the subject-matter layer on Northern urban Black life at the 1900 horizon — incomplete in this reprint, and still awaiting *The Philadelphia Negro*. Gaps remaining: *Philadelphia Negro*, *Black Reconstruction*, Washington’s own corpus, Niagara/NAACP years.
