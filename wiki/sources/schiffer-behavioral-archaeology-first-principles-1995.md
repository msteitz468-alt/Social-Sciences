## Volume Synthesis Note

*Behavioral Archaeology: First Principles* (1995) is Schiffer’s authorized charter collection: reprinted and lightly framed papers from the 1972–mid-1980s program core, plus a new personal history (Ch. 1) and a previously unpublished 28-principle conceptual inventory (Ch. 23). It does **not** replace *Formation Processes of the Archaeological Record* (1987) — FPAR remains the handbook expansion of identification, environmental processes, and case applications — but it supplies what the 1987 ingest deliberately deferred: the four-strategies disciplinary redefinition, behavioral-chain method, synthetic model of inference in charter form, Pompeii-premise polemic in Schiffer’s own wording, Broken K reanalysis as program text, performance-characteristic experiment with Skibo, and the program’s self-codified ontology (activity as basic unit; culture non-explanatory; archaeologist as formation process).

For the wiki, the volume densifies [[behavioral-archaeology]] from a formation-process-centered summary into a full research-program page and warrants the Theory Hub analysis. New concept page [[behavioral-chain]]; new thinkers [[rathje-william]] and [[skibo-james]]; heavy updates to systemic/archaeological context, formation processes, Pompeii premise, refuse types, old wood, ethnoarchaeology, experimental archaeology, use-wear analysis, and [[hill-broken-k-pueblo-study]]. Voice rule held: all program claims attributed; Schiffer–Binford disputes recorded as positions.

**Coverage note:** Outstanding roadmap item 27 asked for densification of *Behavioral Archaeology* (1976). This 1995 volume reprints the charter chapters of that program (including four strategies and synthetic model material from the 1976 book) and is the practical densification vehicle; a separate ingest of the 1976 monograph alone would be largely redundant for program architecture though useful for page-level citation purity of 1976-only passages.
