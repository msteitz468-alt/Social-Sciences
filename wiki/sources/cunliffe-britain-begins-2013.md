# Britain Begins (Cunliffe, 2013)

## Bibliographic Record

Barry Cunliffe, *Britain Begins* (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2013). First edition; copyright 2013. Author: [[cunliffe-barry]].

A single-author narrative synthesis of the deep history of the peoples of Britain and Ireland — from origin myths and island formation, through Mesolithic settlement, Neolithic transformation, Bronze Age connectivity, Iron Age conflict, Roman integration, post-Roman migrations, and the Age of the Northmen (to c. AD 1100). Cunliffe’s programme continues his Atlantic and European syntheses (*[[cunliffe-ancient-celts-1997|The Ancient Celts]]* 1997; *Facing the Ocean* 2001; *Europe Between the Oceans* 2008) with Britain and Ireland as the focal theatre.

`source_type: mixed` — theoretical/synthetic narrative + secondary presentation of primary archaeological, textual, environmental, and genetic evidence; not itself a landmark single excavation, so no `studies/` page.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Britain Begins* is Cunliffe’s single-author deep history of Britain and Ireland as an **Atlantic and North Sea theatre**: structural geography (shelf, Gulf Stream, Doggerland) sets the stage; origin myths (classical through antiquarian to scientific) are treated as part of the knowledge problem, not mere preface. Empirically the book stitches Mesolithic recolonization, rapid Neolithization, Beaker connectivity and metallurgy, LBA–EIA warrior and agrarian intensification, hillfort Iron Age, Roman provincial integration, post-Roman migrations, and Scandinavian raiding/settlement through Cnut and the Norman elite takeover.

Load-bearing interpretive programme (all attributed): **selective mobility** — humans are mobile by nature; the magnitude of migration vs exchange vs indigenous development is **horizon-specific**, not a single invasion-or-continuity rule ([[britain-invasion-continuity-debate]]). That programme rejects culture-historical folk waves for Hallstatt/Celtic packages while accepting substantial mobility for Beaker Phase 1 (small-scale), fifth-century Germanic settlement, and Viking-Age land-takes (especially Northern Isles), and elite-only impact for 1066. Four-way non-identity is enforced throughout: pots, Celtic speech, classical ethnonyms, and genes are kept analytically separate.

Relation to *[[cunliffe-ancient-celts-1997|The Ancient Celts]]* (1997): shared multi-thread method and anti-equation hygiene; *Britain Begins* is the Insular long-run narrative rather than a pan-European Celtic ethnography. Genetics appear as of 2013 (modern DNA, early isotopes/aDNA) — later aDNA revolutions must be layered with date-stamps, not silently merged. Outstanding densifiers remain *Facing the Ocean* and *Europe Between the Oceans*.
