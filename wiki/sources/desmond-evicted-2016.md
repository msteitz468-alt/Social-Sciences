# Evicted (Desmond 2016)

## Bibliographic note

Matthew Desmond, *Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City*. New York: Crown Publishers, 2016. Ingested from the Crown/Penguin Random House epub.

## What this source is

Landmark mixed **relational ethnography** + survey/administrative analysis of **eviction** and the **low-income private rental market** in **Milwaukee**, Wisconsin, principally **May 2008–December 2009** (with survey window 2009–2011). Desmond follows eight tenant families (black and white; with and without children) and landlords (notably Sherrena Tarver on the North Side and Tobin Charney in a South Side trailer park), arguing that **housing is implicated in the creation of poverty** — not merely a backdrop to jobs, welfare, parenting, and incarceration. Forced displacement (formal court eviction, informal eviction, landlord foreclosure, building condemnation) is treated as a **common, consequential process** binding poor tenants to landlords who extract rent under extreme rent-burden.

Evidence classes: ethnographic (participant observation with landlords and tenants; digital audio); survey (Milwaukee Area Renters Study); eviction-court observation/survey; court and administrative records; secondary literature. Study page: [[desmond-evicted-study]]; Studies Hub: [[hubs/studies/desmond-evicted-study]]. Thinker: [[desmond-matthew]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Evicted* is the public-sociology charter of contemporary U.S. **eviction research**: multi-sited [[relational-ethnography]] of Milwaukee landlords and tenants (2008–09) plus MARS survey and court study, arguing that **housing markets and forced displacement create poverty** rather than merely reflecting it. Core measurement claim — more than 1 in 8 renters forced out in two years, nearly half via [[informal-eviction]] — reframes court dockets as undercounts. Concepts [[disposable-ties]], [[housing-cost-burden]], and [[nuisance-property-ordinance]] travel with the study; policy positions (counsel rights; universal vouchers) are Epilogue advocacy, recorded as positions. For this wiki the ingest fills the housing-extraction layer beside Wilson, Massey & Denton, and Anderson on [[underclass-and-segregation-debate]] without adjudicating their primary-cause dispute.
