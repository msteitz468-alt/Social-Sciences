## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Logic of Practice* is Bourdieu's fullest statement of the theory of practice: a double critique of objectivist structuralism and subjectivist philosophies of the free subject, organized around [[habitus]], [[practical-sense]], and the recovery of practice's irreversible time. Book I builds the conceptual architecture (hexis, doxa, illusio, symbolic capital, modes of domination, objectivity of the subjective); Book II demonstrates it on Béarn inheritance strategies and Kabyle kinship, honor, agrarian ritual, and domestic space. The work is theoretical-first with ethnographic second-order use of wartime Algeria fieldwork — not a monographic ethnography in the *Argonauts* sense, so no `studies/` page was created. Both Theory Hub and Thinkers Hub criteria were met and both analyses were built in-session.

Gaps remaining in the Bourdieu cluster: *Outline of a Theory of Practice*, *Distinction*, *Reproduction* (with Passeron), and the Algeria monographs (*Travail et travailleurs*, *Le Déracinement*). Field topology is thinner here than in later field monographs; cultural capital is presupposed more than developed.
