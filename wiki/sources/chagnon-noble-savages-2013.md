# Noble Savages (Chagnon, 2013)

Napoleon A. Chagnon, *Noble Savages: My Life Among Two Dangerous Tribes—the Yanomamö and the Anthropologists* (New York: Simon & Schuster, 2013; first hardcover February 2013). Career memoir and public defense arguing that long-term fieldwork among the [[yanomamo|Yanomamö]] produced scientifically important findings about warfare, demography, and reproductive competition, and that a second "tribe" — cultural anthropologists, missions, and advocacy networks — mounted a politicized campaign culminating in Patrick Tierney's *[[tierney-darkness-el-dorado-2000|Darkness in El Dorado]]* (2000).

**Ingest mode:** polemic / artifact (CLAUDE.md Source-Type Handling). Primary wiki product is densification of **Position A** on [[chagnon-tierney-yanomami-ethics-debate]]. This page is bibliographic record + structured claim inventory. **No `studies/` or hub page** — the book is not a new landmark research design; it is a public brief and life-narrative inside a standing ethics controversy.

### THESES (Chagnon — quarantine on debate / thinker pages)

Core interpretive architecture (see debate page for counters already on file from Tierney):

1. **"Two tribes":** Yanomamö fieldwork vs a second dangerous tribe of cultural anthropologists, missions, and advocates who punish unpopular findings about human aggression.
2. **Forbidden fruit:** Evolutionary/biological questions about sociality are legitimate science; cultural anthropology's biophobia and culturology blocked them.
3. **Women, revenge, and war:** Fights over women and revenge for prior killings organize much violence; not protein scarcity alone (Harris) or pure culture.
4. **Unokai reproductive success:** Military success correlates with more wives and offspring; Darwinian anthropology, not a "gene for warfare" slogan.
5. **Salesians as political monopoly:** Mission access control, shotgun politics, disease at concentrated settlements, and press campaigns interdicted independent demography.
6. **ABA six charges as activist template:** Racism, genocide complicity, faked data, disease concealment, sensational press, micro-reservation causal blame — all denied.
7. **Darkness as anti-sociobiology witch hunt:** Central measles-genocide claim false (Tooby/CDC etc.); scandal thrived on press appetite and AAA process bias; twelve Task Force-issue charges answered point by point (see debate densify section).
8. **AAA process failure:** Peacock "inquiry"/investigation, open-mike theater, tainted Final Report, membership twice overruled leadership; Hill private "sleaze" admission vs public proceeding.
9. **Science vs advocacy schism:** Postmodern/militant anthropology (Scheper-Hughes, D'Andrade, Gross as he cites) redefined the profession as denunciation of colleagues; 2010 "anti-science" long-range plan as continuation.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Noble Savages* is best used in this wiki as the **Position-A memoir counterpart** to Tierney's *Darkness in El Dorado*, not as a substitute ethnography of the Yanomamö. Its lasting wiki value is threefold: (1) it supplies Chagnon's own strongest-form answers to the charge clusters already tabled from Tierney (measles/Edmonston B, film staging, steel/methods, unokai, Brewer/FUNDAFACI, representation politics); (2) it adds institutional chronology the 1968 teaching ethnography lacks — ABA 1988–89, Salesian/OCAI fights, Hashimo commission, AAA Peacock/Task Force/referenda, named defense network; (3) it frames the whole career as a science-vs-advocacy morality play (Alexander/Wilson dedication; "two tribes" title).
