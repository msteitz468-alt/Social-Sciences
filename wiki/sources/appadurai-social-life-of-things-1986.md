# The Social Life of Things (Appadurai, ed., 1986)

Landmark critical-turn collection reorienting anthropology (and adjacent history/archaeology) toward **things-in-motion**: commodities as objects with social lives, trajectories, and culturally constructed exchangeability. Editor [[appadurai-arjun|Arjun Appadurai]]'s introduction and [[kopytoff-igor|Igor Kopytoff]]'s "cultural biography of things" are the program statements; eight case chapters apply the lens to Solomon Islands valuables, Muria Gond consumption, Varna prehistoric wealth, medieval relics, oriental carpets, Somali/northeast-African qat, French Revolutionary cloth, and Indian cloth/swadeshi.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*The Social Life of Things* (1986) is the critical-turn charter for a **comparative anthropology of commodities** that refuses three traps at once: (1) confining the commodity to industrial capitalism; (2) reifying gift vs commodity as mutually exclusive moral worlds; (3) analyzing exchange only as institutional form without following **things-in-motion**. Appadurai’s politics-of-value program ([[regimes-of-value]], paths/diversions, [[tournaments-of-value]], demand as social control, knowledge/authenticity politics) and Kopytoff’s [[cultural-biography-of-things]] / [[commoditization]]–[[singularization]] dialectic supply the toolkit; eight case chapters stress-test it across Melanesia, central India, Chalcolithic Europe, medieval Christendom, the oriental-carpet trade, northeast African qat, Revolutionary France, and colonial–nationalist India.

For the wiki, SLOT densifies [[economic-anthropology]]’s post-formalist–substantivist value front and links existing Marx/Simmel/Mauss pages without merging them: Marx’s commodity and [[commodity-fetishism]] remain production-centered; Simmel’s [[value]] remains the exchange-source philosophy Appadurai reuses; Mauss’s [[gift-exchange]] remains obligatory total prestation — Appadurai’s blur is recorded as a **position**. Material and historical cases ([[varna]], relics, cloth, carpets, qat) keep evidence classes separate. No single empirical *study* page: the volume is a multi-author program, not one research act.
