# Antisemitism in America (Dinnerstein, 1994)

Leonard Dinnerstein's *Antisemitism in America* (Oxford University Press, 1994) is the first comprehensive scholarly survey of antisemitism in the United States, from colonial beginnings through 1992. Dinnerstein (University of Arizona historian; earlier author of the standard study of the Leo Frank case) argues that American antisemitism derives from the Christian theological heritage carried to the colonies, was structurally milder than its European counterpart, crested between 1919 and 1945, and declined sharply after World War II — except, he argues, among African Americans (his most contested chapter).
