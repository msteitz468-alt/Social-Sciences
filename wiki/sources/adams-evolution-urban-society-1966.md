# The Evolution of Urban Society (Adams, 1966)

## Bibliographic Record

Robert McC. Adams, *The Evolution of Urban Society: Early Mesopotamia and Prehispanic Mexico* (Chicago: Aldine Publishing Company, 1966). Originally the **Lewis Henry Morgan Lectures**, University of Rochester, 1965. Comparative analysis of the two then best-documented independent ("pristine") urban trajectories under a [[multilinear-evolution|multilinear evolutionary]] framework associated with [[steward-julian-h|Julian Steward]].

[[adams-robert-mcc|Adams]] argues that early Mesopotamia and prehispanic central Mexico are **slightly variant examples of a single processual pattern** culminating in stratified, politically centralized urban society — from temple-centered theocratic polities through militaristic differentiation to extended trade-and-tribute conquest states — rather than incommensurable culture-historical uniquenesses. See the study page for design, findings, and standing: [[adams-evolution-urban-society-study]].

## Key Claims Extracted

All claims attributed to Adams 1966 (mid-1960s evidence horizon). Full extraction: ~408 claims across 5 ranges in `scratchpad/adams_eus_cache/claims/`.

1. **Single processual pattern.** Early Mesopotamia and prehispanic central Mexico are slightly variant examples of one urban developmental course — fundamental similarity without one-for-one formal or even functional identity.
2. **Multilinear mandate.** Comparative recovery of recurrent cause-and-effect sequences (Steward) is preferable to pure particularism; Old World / New World pair minimizes diffusion confounds.
3. **Urban Revolution reworked.** Prioritize functionally related institutional cores (stratification + polity) over Childe's mixed trait-list; city as container of process; Food-Producing Revolution is a distinct process type.
4. **Ramp vs step.** Style/monumentality often "step" (Uruk–Teotihuacan plateau); social differentiation "ramp" (Mesopotamia ~A; Mexico pulsed ~B).
5. **[[theocratic-to-militaristic-sequence]].** Theocratic polities → militaristic polities → conquest (trade-and-tribute) states; homotaxial pairs Uruk–Teotihuacan, Early Dynastic–Tula, Akkadian–Aztec.
6. **Surplus as gross deployable wealth.** Institutionally mobilized (Polanyi/Orans); not automatic per-capita efficiency prime mover.
7. **Kin → class.** Corporate kin land control encapsulated by private elite estates; class = objective access without required class-consciousness.
8. **Wittfogel rejected as prime mover.** Irrigation can induce stratification as capital investment but population pressure and irrigation are not independent causes of the Urban Revolution.
9. **Imperial termini.** Akkadian and Aztec as loosely knit trade-and-tribute systems; "empire" often unsuitable; rates differ, essentials parallel.
