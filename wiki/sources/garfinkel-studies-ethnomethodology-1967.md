# Studies in Ethnomethodology (Garfinkel, 1967)

Harold Garfinkel's *Studies in Ethnomethodology* (Prentice-Hall, 1967) collects eight
studies written between roughly 1954 and 1967 and, in its opening chapter, names and
programmatically launches **[[ethnomethodology]]** as a distinct enterprise within
sociology. The book is the founding text of the field.

This is the `sources/` bibliographic record. The research program is analyzed on the
theory page **[[ethnomethodology]]** (deep treatment: [[hubs/theory/ethnomethodology|Theory Hub]]);
the author on **[[garfinkel-harold]]** (deep treatment: [[hubs/thinkers/garfinkel-harold|Thinkers Hub]]);
the Agnes case on **[[garfinkel-agnes-study]]**.

## Contents

1. What is ethnomethodology?
2. Studies of the routine grounds of everyday activities (the "breaching" demonstrations)
3. Common sense knowledge of social structures: the documentary method of interpretation in lay and professional fact finding
4. Some rules of correct decisions that jurors respect
5. Passing and the managed achievement of sex status in an intersexed person, Part 1 (the "Agnes" case)
6. "Good" organizational reasons for "bad" clinic records
7. Methodological adequacy in the quantitative study of selection criteria and selection practices in psychiatric outpatient clinics (with Egon Bittner)
8. The rational properties of scientific and common sense activities

## Core Contribution

In **attributed voice**: Garfinkel argued that social order is not secured by internalized
norms (the Parsonian answer) but is an ongoing *practical accomplishment* — members produce
orderly settings and make them "accountable" (observable-and-reportable) in one and the same
activity. He held that accounts and expressions are irremediably [[indexicality|indexical]]
and [[reflexivity-of-accounts|reflexive]], and that the seen-but-unnoticed
[[background-expectancies]] of everyday life, exposed by [[breaching-experiment|breaching demonstrations]],
are the proper topic of sociology. Against the model of the actor as a "cultural dope," he
insisted members are practical methodologists whose [[documentary-method-of-interpretation|documentary method]]
and [[etcetera-principle|ad hoc reasoning]] cannot be idealized away — a claim he turned
against the coding and measurement practices of conventional sociology (Ch. 6–7). The
book founds [[ethnomethodology]] and, through the UCLA circle, the conversation-analysis
lineage.

## Reliability and Reading Notes

See `reliability_notes` in the frontmatter. The prose is the central reading hazard:
Garfinkel's technical vocabulary is designed to defamiliarize the taken-for-granted, and
his sentences resist paraphrase. Extractions preserve his key terms verbatim and mark
every theoretical claim as attributed voice.
