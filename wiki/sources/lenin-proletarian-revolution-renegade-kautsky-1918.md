# The Proletarian Revolution and the Renegade Kautsky (1918)

The canonical dictatorship-vs-democracy polemic: Lenin's book-length answer to Kautsky's critique of Bolshevik rule, finished as the German revolution broke out (postscript dated Nov 10, 1918). 2-agent extraction (CW Vol. 28 segment).

## Load-bearing content (all attributed)

- **The dictatorship definition:** "rule based directly upon force and unrestricted by any laws" — the revolutionary dictatorship of the proletariat as "rule won and maintained by the use of violence... unrestricted by any laws." Marx's Gotha Programme transition passage as the textual anchor ([[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]]).
- **Democracy as class question:** bourgeois democracy "a paradise for the rich and a snare and deception for the exploited"; Soviet democracy "a million times more democratic"; dictatorship abolishes democracy for the class it targets, not necessarily for the ruling class (the slaveholder-democracy analogy).
- **Engels deployed:** the Bebel letter ("so long as the proletariat still needs the state... in order to hold down its adversaries"), the Civil War in France introduction, "a revolution is certainly the most authoritarian thing there is" ([[withering-away-of-the-state]], [[engels-friedrich]]).
- **The Kautsky dispute itself:** dueling imperialism definitions; Soviets as "combat organisation" vs. state organs; the Constituent Assembly dispersal defended by the five-Congress radicalization table (Bolshevik share 13%→66%); the franchise/"pure democracy" polemic; "What Is Internationalism?" against defencism ([[kautsky-karl]], [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]).
- **Agrarian defense:** the full Decree on Land against Kautsky's selective quotation; nationalisation vs socialisation as Marxist categories; the two-stage alliance theory (whole peasantry, then poor peasants) with the "Chinese Wall" warning ([[peasantry-as-class]], [[permanent-revolution]]).

## Reliability

The dyad is now closed on one side: Kautsky's *The Dictatorship of the Proletariat* is ingested ([[kautsky-dictatorship-of-the-proletariat-1918]]), and [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]] states position 2 from Kautsky's own text. Comparison confirms Lenin's quotations track Kautsky's argument in outline, with two caveats: John H. Kautsky (1964) flags inaccuracies in the English translation through which Lenin's quotations circulate, and Lenin systematically strips Kautsky's grounding arguments (the maturity theory, the Commune-suffrage reading of Marx). Kautsky's *Terrorism and Communism* (1919) and Trotsky's reply remain un-ingested.
