## Core load-bearing positions (attributed summary)

### Boycott and the Third Duma

Lenin argued that **active boycott** is correct only under conditions of a broad revolutionary upswing moving toward assault on the old regime; for the **Third Duma** (autumn 1907) those conditions did not hold — Social-Democrats should **participate**, use the Duma as a platform of exposure, and reject Cadet “national opposition” coalitions with the Black-Hundred–Octobrist order.

### Stuttgart (1907)

Lenin reported and defended International decisions rejecting “socialist colonial policy,” moving against trade-union **neutrality**, and framing anti-militarism as using war crisis to hasten overthrow of the bourgeoisie (against pure Hervéism and pure parliamentary-opportunist pacifism, as he frames the poles).

### *Preface to Twelve Years*

Lenin presented Bolshevism’s struggle against the Right Marxist tendency as continuous from legal Marxism through Economism to Menshevism, and defended the organisational line of *[[lenin-what-is-to-be-done-1902|What Is to Be Done?]]* against anachronistic critique.

### Agrarian programme (1905–07 monograph)

Lenin argued that Russian agrarian revolution faces **two bourgeois paths** (often labelled Prussian landlord vs American farmer-type evolution); that the peasant mass programme points toward **[[land-nationalisation|nationalisation]]** of the land (transfer of rent; “clearing of estates”); that **[[municipalisation]]** (as in Maslov / Stockholm dualism) is theoretically and politically flawed, including via repudiation of Marx’s **[[absolute-rent|absolute rent]]**; and that the Social-Democratic agrarian programme must match the political scope of democratic dictatorship of proletariat and peasantry. A **1917 Postscript** re-reads nationalisation under state-monopoly capitalism as a step toward socialism (attributed; later frame).

### Trade-union neutrality

Across Stuttgart reportage, the Voinov preface, and *Trade-Union Neutrality*, Lenin argued for closest alignment of unions with the Party / against the neutrality slogan (see [[trade-union-neutrality]]).
