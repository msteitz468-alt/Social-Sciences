# Anti-Judaism: The Western Tradition

David Nirenberg, *Anti-Judaism: The Western Tradition* (New York: W. W. Norton, 2013). Thinker: [[nirenberg-david]]. Central concept: [[anti-judaism]]. Companion phenomenon page: [[antisemitism]] (seeded from Cohn/Bauman; densified from this source).

## What This Source Is

A long-span **intellectual history** of how Western (and, Nirenberg argues, Islamic) cultures have used figures of **Judaism, Jews, and “Judaizing”** as tools for thinking about the world — about power, money, scripture, law, flesh, letter, reason, revolution, and modernity — often in societies with few or no living Jews. Nirenberg’s central thesis (attributed): anti-Judaism is not only hatred of Jews but a **pedagogical and conceptual habit** that gives enduring form to key questions in the history of thought; the “mask” of anti-Judaism persists while the meanings behind it transform.

The book is **not** a complete history of Jewish life, nor a monocausal genealogy from the gospels to the Holocaust. Nirenberg explicitly rejects both easy continuity and easy discontinuity, treating anti-Judaism as a Nietzschean “mask” that conceals ceaseless transformation while still constraining later possibilities of thought and of Jewish existence.

## Key Claims (from extraction; all attributed to Nirenberg unless noted)

### Project and method
1. **Thinking with Judaism.** Diverse cultures—including many with no living Jews—have used figures of Judaism to make sense of the world; the book asks what work that thinking did and how it constrained later possibilities of thought and of Jewish existence.
2. **Judaism as category.** Following Marx’s insight into the *Judenfrage*, “Judaism” names not only a people/religion but attributes with which non-Jews criticize their world (money, mediation, law, letter, formalism).
3. **Anti-Judaism as basic tool, not closet.** Anti-Judaism is one of the basic tools with which the Western edifice was constructed; systems generate “Judaism” from their own entrails.
4. **Mask method.** Continuity of pedagogical form with ceaseless transformation of content (Nietzschean masks); rejects both gospel-to-Holocaust teleology and radical presentist cutting.
5. **Three deviations.** Focus on non-Jews’ thought; ideas granted power against pure interests; continuity + change.
6. **Special pleading.** For critical history of ideas—not for or against Jews, Judaism, or Israel.

### Ancient → Christian → Islamic
7. Pre-Christian anti-Judaism occupied “tiny islands” later enlarged by projection; Egyptian Manetho tradition and Alexandrian politics are early workshops, not full ideology in the modern sense.
8. Paul and the gospels supply enduring pedagogies (letter/spirit; “to Judaize”; Pharisee hypocrisy; dualisms) that later ages retool.
9. Patristic anti-Judaism often arises from **Christian–Christian** conflict (Marcion, Chrysostom’s Judaizing disease, Ambrose) more than from living Jewish pressure; Augustine’s protected exile creates a political paradox of preservation-with-subordination.
10. Early Islamic materials use Jewish enmity and *tahrif*/hypocrite≈Judaizer as structuring principles; Nirenberg frames potential as internal to Islamic thought-worlds, not “essential antisemitism.”

### Medieval → early modern
11. Medieval “Jew-love” of princes, fiscality, moneylending, ritual-murder/host-desecration charges, and expulsion as power-extension show anti-Judaism as a language of **politics**, including “Jewish politics” without real Jews.
12. Spain 1391→Inquisition: mass conversion produces anxiety that Christian–Jew difference is vanishing; *limpieza de sangre* and purity discourse; Spain “Judaized” after expelling Jews.
13. Luther moves from mild 1523 pamphlet to 1543 elimination program; Nirenberg declines anachronistic Holocaust-architect claims while tracking hermeneutic draining of Augustinian witness.

### Shakespeare → Enlightenment → Revolution
14. *Merchant of Venice* as chronic conversion drama; commerce/contract/theater as Judaizing; conversion poetics and “relief-fantasy” as forms of anti-Semitism (Adorno/Horkheimer cited).
15. Confessional-age politics (Hobbes, Hebraism, English Civil Wars without living Jews) **translate** rather than overthrow anti-Judaism into modern political thought.
16. Spinoza, Voltaire, Kant: Enlightenment “revolts against Judaism” retool older figures (Pharisee, letter, carnality); Kant’s “euthanasia of Judaism” as limit case of critical de-Judaization.
17. French Revolution: Jews as citizenship limit-case (~32 Assembly debates; 1791 emancipation); regeneration/conversion logic; Jacobins less murderously anti-Jewish toward real Jews than Terror was bloody overall; Burke and Fichte as counter-revolutionary and German-philosophical uses of Jewish figures.

### Philosophy → modernity → epilogue
18. German philosophy (Kant→Hegel→Schopenhauer→Heine) repeatedly “Judaizes” progressive steps; Heine’s Venice prophecy of a tempest on Jewish heads if Satan/pantheism conquers.
19. Marx 1844: fundamental insight into Judaism-as-category of mediation/money; critical failure in elevating emancipation *from Judaism* and confusing figural/real Jews.
20. Weber–Sombart dispute over capitalism’s “Jewish” spirit; Nazi propaganda of Jewish capital; academic disciplines’ collusion (Jewish formalism, mathematics, higher criticism).
21. **Holocaust formula:** inconceivable without the deep history of Jewish questions; not caused by it; not inevitable; not explained by German uniqueness alone.
22. Epilogue: Arendt rejects pure scapegoating but clings to co-responsibility; anti-Judaism as theory-laden sense-making framework; Horkheimer/Adorno shift to *Dialectic of Enlightenment*; Auerbach’s *Figura*/*Mimesis* as method model; risk of forgetting continuities equals risk of inventing them.

## Volume Synthesis Note
