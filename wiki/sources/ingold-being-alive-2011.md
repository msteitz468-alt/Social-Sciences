# Being Alive: Essays on Movement, Knowledge and Description (Tim Ingold, 2011)

A collection of nineteen essays elaborating Ingold's claim that to understand life we
must follow it **along its lines of movement and growth** through a world that is not a
surface of objects but a fluid, weather-suffused **[[meshwork]]** of interwoven paths.
The essays develop, in their sharpest form, the vocabulary that the wiki also draws from
*[[ingold-lines-2007|Lines]]* and *[[ingold-making-2013|Making]]*: materials against
materiality, wayfaring against transport, the meshwork against the network, the
weather-world against the landscape-as-surface, and knowing as growth rather than
assembly.

It is here that Ingold stages his best-known head-on argument with **Actor-Network
Theory** — the essay "When ANT meets SPIDER" — contrasting the network of connected
actants with the spider's web of lines spun by a **skilled, developmentally embodied,
responsive** practitioner (SPIDER).

## Claims extracted

Extracted via 5 range-partitioned subagents (OCR text). Core content:

- **Materials against materiality** (Ch. 2): the critique of "materiality" in material-
  culture studies (contra Tilley, Godelier, Renfrew) in favour of a "world of materials"
  (Gibson's medium/substance/surface) — the seed of the [[hylomorphism]] argument.
- **Culture on the ground** (Ch. 3): pedestrian perception, boots vs. bare feet; the
  ground as felt, not merely stood on.
- **Skill** (Ch. 4 "Walking the Plank"): sawing/skill as continuous corrective attention
  (feeds [[enskilment]]); a dispute with Connerton on repetition (automaticity vs.
  heightened attention).
- **Rethinking the animate** (Ch. 5): animacy as life-in-movement, not an added property
  — Ingold's re-definition of animism.
- **When ANT meets SPIDER** (Ch. 7): the explicit critique of Actor-Network Theory —
  **SPIDER** ("Skilled Practice Involves Developmentally Embodied Responsiveness") vs.
  the network of actants — the key source for the [[meshwork]] page.
- **The weather-world** (Chs. 8–11): the [[weather-world]] as medium (earth/sky/wind/
  weather); "landscape or weather-world?"; four objections to "soundscape."
- **Against space** (Ch. 12) and **stories against classification** (Ch. 13): place vs.
  abstract space; [[wayfaring]] vs. transport as ways of knowing; the taxonomic-tree /
  genealogical critique (linking *[[ingold-lines-2007|Lines]]*).
- **The textility of making** (Ch. 17): the anti-[[hylomorphism|hylomorphic]] manifesto —
  the seed essay of *[[ingold-making-2013|Making]]*.
- **Anthropology is not ethnography** (Ch. 19): anthropology as forward-looking
  [[correspondence]] *with* people vs. descriptive ethnography *of* them
  ([[knowing-from-the-inside]]).

Notable: Ingold here states he **regrets his earlier emphasis on "dwelling" and prefers
"wayfaring."** All claims attributed to Ingold.
