# The Dictatorship of the Proletariat (1918)

Kautsky's critique of Bolshevik rule, written August 1918 — the pamphlet that opened the Kautsky–Lenin–Trotsky pamphlet war and, in John H. Kautsky's 1964 framing, "a milestone" where communism and democratic socialism definitively parted ways. This ingest supplies the missing side of a dyad the wiki previously held only through Lenin's hostile quotation ([[lenin-proletarian-revolution-renegade-kautsky-1918]]). 2-agent extraction, ~39k words complete.

## Kautsky's argument (attributed)

- **"No Socialism without democracy":** socialism is a means to the abolition of all exploitation and oppression, and democracy an equally indispensable means — "Socialism without democracy is unthinkable," though democracy without socialism is possible.
- **Marx interpretation:** the 1875 Gotha Programme "dictatorship of the proletariat" describes "a condition, not a form of government"; the Paris Commune — Engels's own exemplar of the dictatorship — rested on universal suffrage; "Marx must not be cited by those who support dictatorship in preference to democracy." This is the direct interpretive counter to Lenin's "rule unrestricted by any laws" definition ([[dictatorship-of-the-proletariat]], [[smash-or-capture-the-state-debate]]).
- **Class vs party:** a class can rule but not govern; dictatorship "of a class" as a form of government is impossible — it becomes dictatorship of a party, then of part of a party; in a peasant-majority Russia, Soviet class representation logically yields a dictatorship *of the peasants* or, via exclusion, of a shrinking minority.
- **The maturity theory:** democracy is the school in which the proletariat attains the organizational and intellectual "ripeness" socialism requires, and the instrument for measuring relative class strength; minority seizure leads to "Jesuitism" or "Bonapartism," and civil war is the deadliest enemy of socialist construction.
- **On Russia:** the revolution is "the last of middle-class, and not the first of Socialist revolutions"; its durable legacy will be secured peasant private property and a coming peasant–worker antagonism, not socialism; the Bolsheviks staked everything on a European revolution that failed to arrive. The information vacuum under dictatorship (no free press to verify decrees' operation) is itself analyzed as a structural feature.
- **Predictions:** capitalism will re-arise in Russia; dictatorship of a party can become — quoting Lenin's own April 1918 words on one-person "dictatorial power" — dictatorship of a single person; the Revolution's achievements survive only "if dictatorship is opportunely replaced by democracy."

## The introduction (John H. Kautsky, 1964 — secondary scholarship)

Documents Kautsky's pre-1914 stature (via Shachtman: literary executor of Marx and Engels, 35 years editing *Die Neue Zeit*, Lenin's pre-war writings "studded with laudatory references"); his isolation even within the USPD over the anti-Bolshevik stand; the 1930/31 retrospective; and an adjudicating 1964 judgment — that on Russia's unripeness "Kautsky's interpretation of Marx's materialist conception of history was right and Lenin's was wrong," while both men's categories fit an industrializing agrarian country poorly. Recorded as attributed scholarly positions, not wiki-voice verdicts.
