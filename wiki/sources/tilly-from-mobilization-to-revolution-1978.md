## Volume Synthesis Note

*From Mobilization to Revolution* is a **program charter**, not a single-case monograph: stone-soup synthesis of Michigan conflict-research memoranda and NSF France/Britain event catalogs into a portable kit — interests, organization, mobilization, opportunity; polity members vs challengers; repertoires; violence as claim–counterclaim; revolution as multiple sovereignty. It is doggedly anti-Durkheimian (strain/pathology) and oriented to Marxian interest problems while using Millian strategic tools critically. What it adds to this wiki: (1) first full Tilly page and political-conflict theory opposite Skocpol's already-ingested state-centered program; (2) concept kit for contentious politics *avant la lettre*; (3) method appendices documenting event-catalog operationalization. Tensions: Skocpol's state-autonomy critique stands as a live rival position; later Tilly (contentious politics, state formation) will refine without replacing this 1978 baseline. OCR limits appendix table precision — qualitative coding rules are solid; many cell values are not.
