# Empiriomonism (1904–06)

The primary text Lenin's *Materialism and Empirio-criticism* attacked — completing the counter-source program: the [[empirio-criticism-debate]]'s Machist side can now be stated from Bogdanov's actual words. ~217k words, 6-agent extraction.

## Bogdanov's doctrine (attributed)

- **The core formula** (the thesis Lenin assailed, verbatim in Books One and Three): "the contrast between the physical and the psychical series of experiences boils down to the difference between experience that is socially-organised and experience that is individually organised" — the physical as *socially organized experience*, objectivity as social validity explicitly distinguished from Kantian absolutes.
- **Not a Machist, by his own insistence:** empiriocriticism called "a necessary but passing stage"; sustained critiques of Avenarius (the vital-differential as static, "psychological Lamarckism," the Affectional theory) and the flat statement "I cannot consider myself to be a 'Machist'" (Book Three preface). His self-account: from natural-scientific materialism, through rejected neo-Kantianism, to a *social-genetic* theory of cognition demanded by "the social materialism of Marx."
- **Truth within epochs:** "matter" and "invariable laws" as historically transient organizing forms — "truth for their times... never truth for eternity"; the direct source of Lenin's eternal-truths counterattack, here with Bogdanov's own Engels-vs-Plekhanov staging.
- **Substitution** and **psychical/social selection**; **historical monism** (technology-primary; ideology as organizing adaptations; the identity-of-social-being-and-consciousness thesis with Bogdanov's own clarification against Axelrod/"Ortodoks"); the class-genetic derivation of empiriomonism itself from machine-production labor experience.
- **Against Plekhanov ("Bel'tov"):** the thing-in-itself "golden mean" critiqued as unresolved; the "reaction" theory of ideology as eclectic.

## The apparatus (secondary, attributed)

Rowley's publication history and translation notes; Bogdanov's 1924 autobiography (Tula gymnasium "like a military barracks or prison," Moscow expulsion 1894, exile, the 1905 Soviet arrest and Kresty imprisonment, the 1923 GPU arrest); Bazarov's memorial essay (the Tula friendship, the three-stage periodization — 1899 historical view of nature → empiriomonism → tektology — and Bukharin's graveside speech); the editor's postscript on the Institute of Haematology directorship and the fatal 1928 transfusion experiment.
