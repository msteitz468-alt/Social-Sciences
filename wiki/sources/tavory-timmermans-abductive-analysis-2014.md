## What this source is

A **method-theory monograph** reframing qualitative analysis through C. S. Peirce’s [[abduction|abduction]] and semiotics. Positions [[abductive-analysis]] against pure inductivism of classic/textbook [[grounded-theory]] and single-favorite-theory [[extended-case-method]]. Core attributed program: cultivate empirical surprise against **multiple** theorizations; use method for defamiliarization and revisiting; build sets via variation; explain via [[consequences-in-action]]; warrant claims in a [[community-of-inquiry]].

Not a `studies/` landmark ethnography; no Studies Hub page. Primary home: [[abductive-analysis]].

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Abductive Analysis* is the collection’s first major **contemporary method-theory** monograph on qualitative theory construction after the 1967 GT charter. It does three structural jobs for the wiki:

1. **Peirce enters the knowledge layer** not as a full primary ingest but as the philosophical engine of a living research program (abduction, semiotics, community of inquiry) — upgrading [[pragmatism]] beyond Mead-only coverage.
2. **Triangulates the theory–data method wars**: GT induction, Burawoy ECM deduction/extension, and abductive analysis as a third path — recorded on [[generation-vs-verification-debate]] without adjudicating winners.
3. **Promotes method concepts** (consequences-in-action; community criteria of fit/plausibility/relevance) that other qualitative sources can link to when ingested.

Limitations of this ingest: OCR source; empirical illustrations are second-hand; Burawoy, Peirce, and Strauss/Corbin manuals remain un-ingested primaries. Hub pages not warranted (not a classic study; not a multi-generation theory school on the GT scale yet — method page is the right resolution).
