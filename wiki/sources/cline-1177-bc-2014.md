# 1177 B.C.: The Year Civilization Collapsed (Cline, 2014)

## Bibliographic Record

Eric H. Cline, *1177 B.C.: The Year Civilization Collapsed* (Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2014). Turning Points in Ancient History series (Barry Strauss, series editor). ~230 pages of body + notes/bibliography/index. Author: [[cline-eric-h]].

Cline synthesizes the rise and interconnectedness of Late Bronze Age (LBA) polities from Greece and Anatolia through the Levant, Cyprus, Egypt, and Mesopotamia, then surveys the multi-decade destructions and systemic collapse conventionally anchored to **1177 BCE** (eighth year of Ramses III; Sea Peoples invasion of Egypt). Core thesis (attributed): no single cause — a **"perfect storm"** of earthquakes, drought/famine, invaders (including but not limited to Sea Peoples), internal rebellions, trade-network disruption, and systems effects in a globalized interdependent world.

## Key Claims Extracted

All claims **attributed to Cline 2014** or to scholars he cites.

1. **Multi-civ system collapse.** Object is a globalized multi-civilization system (not single-empire collapse like Gibbon/Diamond cases).
2. **1177 BCE shorthand.** Year 8 of Ramses III = convenient marker for a process ~1225–1130 BCE (cf. AD 476 for Rome).
3. **Sea Peoples.** Modern label for named Egyptian groups (Peleset, Tjekker, Shekelesh, Shardana, Danuna, Weshesh, etc.); multi-origin; not monocausal destroyers; multiphase (Merneptah 1207 + Ramses III 1177 + later).
4. **International system peak.** Amarna Great Powers diplomacy; Uluburun multi-origin cargo ~1300; Ugarit/Hattusa archives; tin strategic (Bell: tin≈oil).
5. **Destructions uneven.** Ugarit ~1190–85 violent end; Hattusa emptied then burned (Kashka after internal weakness); Mycenaean palaces lose writing/*wanax*; Egypt survives weakened; some Mesopotamian damage Elamite.
6. **Perfect storm.** Earthquakes (storm ~1225–1175), drought/famine (textual + proxies), invaders/migrants, revolts, trade cuts — multiplier and domino effects; Renfrew systems-collapse features; complexity/hyper-coherence with Cline’s own predictive-power caveat.
7. **Aftermath.** Network loss; Iron Age smaller polities; regenerations (Phoenicians, Philistines, Israelites, Greeks, Neo-Hittites); not pure void.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*1177 B.C.* is the collection’s first dedicated synthesis of the Eastern Mediterranean Late Bronze Age international system and its multi-decade end. It complements [[yoffee-myths-of-the-archaic-state-2005|Yoffee 2005]] on [[societal-collapse]] by supplying a dense multi-polity *case* (systems interdependence, Sea Peoples historiography, climate and seismic evidence classes) rather than a general theory of archaic states. Strengths for the wiki: clear attribution of positions (Sandars, Drews, Renfrew, Sherratt, Bell, Finkelstein, Yasur-Landau, excavators); insistence on four-way caution when equating Sea Peoples names, destructions, and later Philistines; and a usable debate map. Limits: secondary synthesis (~2013 literature horizon); popular-series modern analogies are rhetorical; absolute chronologies and post-2014 proxies/aDNA will need later ingest updates. No Studies Hub page — synthesis is a field reference point but not a primary research act meeting hub criteria.
