Bibliographic record for Colin Renfrew's *Archaeology and Language: The Puzzle of Indo-European Origins* (Jonathan Cape 1987; Penguin 1989). See [[renfrew-colin]]. Primary program statement of the [[anatolian-hypothesis]]; principal rival position on [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]].

## Overview

*Archaeology and Language* argues that the Indo-European language family spread with early farming from Anatolia beginning in the early Neolithic, rather than from a later Bronze Age steppe/Kurgan homeland as argued by [[gimbutas-marija|Marija Gimbutas]] and culture-historical predecessors. Renfrew rejects equating archaeological "cultures" with peoples and languages, and proposes a **processual** toolkit linking language change to demography and social organization — demic diffusion of farming as the principal large-scale replacement mechanism in Europe, with other models (élite dominance, system collapse, constrained displacement) for other cases. The book is anti-migrationist in the *culture-historical* sense while allowing demographically driven language replacement where agriculture expands.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*Archaeology and Language* is best filed as a **theoretical-work**: a programmatic synthesis, not a primary excavation or a primary reconstruction of PIE. Its lasting contribution to this wiki is threefold.

1. **Position B in full voice.** The vault previously held the Anatolian hypothesis mainly via Reich (2018) and Cavalli-Sforza (2000) secondary summaries. Renfrew’s own load-bearing arguments are now on disk: rejection of Kurgan/Corded/Beaker as pan-European IE solutions; farming as the only early enough pan-European process; demic wave of advance with explicit density and mobility parameters; Basque/Etruscan as mesolithic-survival tips; Celtic *in situ* crystallization; Indo-Iranian dual hypotheses; and the frank admission that first-farmers-spoke-IE is “sheer hypothesis.”

2. **Processual language methodology.** Three processes and three replacement models give the wiki a reusable apparatus for any archaeology–language problem, not only IE. The attack on circular linguistic palaeontology and on culture=people equations strengthens the vault’s four-way non-identity rule from inside a major IE debate.

3. **Chronological and genetic supersession expected.** The book is a 1987 Critical Turn artifact. Post-2015 aDNA (Reich layer on [[steppe-hypothesis-debate]]) reweights European demography toward later steppe-related migration without settling ultimate homeland or erasing Renfrew’s methodological critique of culture-historical equations. Anthony’s primary steppe synthesis remains the outstanding gap for Position A’s best form.

No `studies/` page (argumentative synthesis, not a research act as object). Thinkers Hub for Renfrew still deferred pending *The Emergence of Civilisation* for full program depth, but the IE/language program is no longer secondary-only.
