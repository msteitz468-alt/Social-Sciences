# Çatalhöyük: The Leopard's Tale (Hodder 2006)

Ian Hodder’s mid-project popular-scholarly synthesis of Neolithic [[catalhoyuk|Çatalhöyük]]: leopard symbolism as puzzle, settlement as “town,” house-centred social life, the invention of “history,” production/exchange, materiality and art, gender and personhood. Bridge between Mellaart’s 1960s fame-narrative and the later CRP monographs.

## Volume Synthesis Note

*[[hodder-leopards-tale-2006|The Leopard’s Tale]]* (English 2006; Turkish Yapı Kredi ed. on disk) is [[hodder-ian|Ian Hodder]]’s mid-project **popular-scholarly synthesis** of the Çatalhöyük Research Project to the mid-2000s — not a unit catalogue. Its load-bearing move is to reframe Mellaart’s fame site as a **house-centred Late Neolithic “town”** whose central puzzle is wild animal symbolism (leopards, aurochs, vultures) against a domestic sheep/plant economy, with art–bone mismatches (esp. zero leopard bones among ~24k identified fauna as of writing) as the book’s emblem.

**Argument arc (attributed).** After stakeholder and method framing (Turkish state/UNESCO conservation, local villages, goddess tourism, multi-national single-context labs), Hodder organises life into interacting spheres (domestic production, exchange, descent/ancestry, community) structured by placement rules (clean north burial platforms vs dirty south ovens; pots and clay figurines out of graves). He rejects Mellaart’s shrine/temple dualism: decorated buildings are houses with varying ceremonial intensity ([[catalhoyuk-shrine-house-debate]]). “Invention of history” names house-specific ancestral material proofs (skull retrieval, installation memory, multi-burial “dominant” houses) rather than generic ancestor pools. Gender chapters challenge strong mother-goddess social models via isotopes (no sex–diet link as of Richards/Pearson mid-2000s data), Hamilton’s burial review, and figurine re-readings (most sexless; life/death dual figurine) while keeping multivocal dialogue with goddess constituencies ([[mother-goddess]]). Long-term change is slow practice drift toward house independence and domestic production in upper levels, not a single Neolithic revolution event.

**Wiki contribution.** Densifies [[catalhoyuk]], [[hodder-ian]], the shrine–house debate, [[post-processual-archaeology]] as public synthesis of reflexive results, and [[mother-goddess]] critique positions. Absolute dates are mid-2000s framing — supersede with Bayesian North results **(as of Bayliss et al. 2022)** in *[[hodder-catalhoyuk-excavations-2009-2017]]* where they conflict. Complements already-ingested *Archaeological Process* (1999 method charter) and Mellaart 1967 (Position A primary). No separate `studies/` page required: the work is synthesis/program statement over primary excavation.

**Tensions with other ingested sources.** Stronger recovery methods reduce cattle’s dietary role vs Mellaart/Perkins; population figures (Cessford 3,500–8,000) later revised downward in Hodder 2022 Ch.1 (~1,000–4,000 Middle-period max under different assumptions). Four-way non-identity: local political kinship-with-ancestors questions outrun aDNA available as of 2006.
