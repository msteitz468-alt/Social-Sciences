# Basin of Mexico Chronology

Two period systems run through the wiki's central-Mexican pages. The **conventional Mesoamerican stages** (Formative/Preclassic, Classic, Epiclassic, Postclassic) and the **Basin of Mexico survey project's neutral scheme** introduced in [[sanders-parsons-santley-basin-of-mexico-1979|Sanders, Parsons & Santley (1979)]], which replaced developmental stage names with Horizons and numbered Intermediate phases precisely to avoid presupposing what "Formative" or "Classic" imply. Recorded here on first use per the regional-period-system rule.

## The Sanders–Parsons–Santley Scheme

Dating rests on ceramic cross-ties and radiocarbon anchors as of 1979; the authors flag phase boundaries as approximate (chronological precision: `approximate`). Later refinements (esp. post-1990 recalibrations of the Formative and the redating of the Epiclassic) have shifted several boundaries — treat the right-hand equivalences as the book's, not current consensus.

| Project phase | Dates (as of 1979) | Conventional equivalent | Markers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Horizon | 1500–1150 BCE | Early Formative | first sedentary villages; [[tlatilco]] |
| First Intermediate 1 | 1150–650 BCE | Middle Formative | village growth, southern lakeshore focus |
| First Intermediate 2 | 650–300 BCE | Late Formative | first supravillage centers; [[loma-torremote]] |
| First Intermediate 3 | 300–100 BCE | Terminal Formative | [[cuicuilco]] vs. [[teotihuacan]] rivalry |
| First Intermediate 4 | 100 BCE–100 CE | Terminal Formative/Protoclassic | Teotihuacan's explosive nucleation |
| First Intermediate 5 | 100–300 CE | Early Classic (early) | Teotihuacan consolidation |
| Middle Horizon | 300–750 CE | Classic | Teotihuacan hegemony; [[maquixco-bajo]] |
| Second Intermediate 1 | 750–950 CE | Epiclassic | Teotihuacan collapse, balkanization |
| Second Intermediate 2 | 950–1150 CE | Early Postclassic / Toltec | [[tula]] ascendancy |
| Second Intermediate 3 | 1150–1350 CE | Middle Postclassic | city-state competition |
| Late Horizon | 1350–1519 CE | Late Postclassic / Aztec | [[tenochtitlan]], [[aztec-empire]] peak; basin population maximum |

Ceramic-phase detail (Appendix C of the source) is summarized on [[sanders-basin-of-mexico-study]].
