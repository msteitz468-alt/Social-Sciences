# Eastern Woodlands Chronology

Regional period system for eastern North American prehistory as synthesized in [[milner-moundbuilders-2021|Milner, *The Moundbuilders* (2021)]]. **Period boundaries are conventional and locally variable by centuries**; recalibration of radiocarbon dates continually revises them. Early periods (Paleoindian–Early Archaic) discussed in **calibrated calendar years** (OxCal 3.4 in Milner); later periods use customary archaeological labels.

## Period Sequence (as of Milner 2021)

| Period | Approx. span (conventional / Milner) | Hallmarks (attributed — Milner) |
|---|---|---|
| Paleoindian | late Pleistocene entry; Clovis ≤~13,400 cal BP (~11,500 ¹⁴C BP) | Fluted points; mobile foragers; peopling debates |
| Early Archaic | ends ~8,900 cal BP (~8,000 ¹⁴C BP customary); lasts 2,000+ years | Mobile foragers; point-style regionalization |
| Middle Archaic | customary 6000–3000 BCE (calibrated start ~900 years earlier) | Sedentary trends; middens; Climatic Optimum; early mounds ([[watson-brake]]) |
| Late Archaic | into early first millennium BCE | Native plants; [[poverty-point]]; shell rings |
| Early Woodland | opening centuries of first millennium BCE; ~500+ years | [[adena-culture]] burial mounds |
| Middle Woodland | Midwest/SE half-millennium ending ~400 CE (NE labels sometimes to ~1000 CE) | [[hopewell-culture]] earthworks & exchange |
| Late Woodland | mid first millennium – c. 1000+ CE | Villages; maize switch; chiefdom origins; Coles Creek; Toltec |
| Mississippian | c. 11th century CE – European contact (SE) | Platform-mound [[chiefdom|chiefdoms]]; [[cahokia]], [[moundville]], [[spiro]] |
| Northern late prehistoric | ~1000 CE – contact | [[fort-ancient-culture]], [[oneota-culture]], Owasco–Iroquois, etc. |
| Contact / historic | 16th century onward | Depopulation; last mounds; Removal |

## Climatic anchors (Milner)

- **Younger Dryas:** from 12,900 years ago for ~1,200 years.
- **Holocene Climatic Optimum:** ~8,000–4,000 cal years ago — midcontinent warmer/drier, prairie expansion, river-valley productivity.
- **Medieval Warm Period:** 11th-c. northward Mississippian expansion.
- **14th-c. volatility → Little Ice Age:** midwestern chiefdom dissolution; northern conflict intensification (Milner: **unpredictability** of food production more than simple cold/dry).

## Population growth model (Milner)

Three general stages from multi-state site-file data (AL, AR, IL, KY, MS, NY, TN, WI): early increase ~**0.06%/year**; middle **stationary**; late growth again ~**0.06%/year**. Large study area rules out interregional migration as major driver of the pattern (attributed).

## Notes
