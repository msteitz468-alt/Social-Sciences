# Holocene Chronology

An environmental chronology of the [[holocene|Holocene]] and its terminal-Pleistocene prelude, after [[roberts-neil|Roberts]] ([[roberts-holocene-1998|1998]]). Dates are **calibrated calendar years before present (Cal. yr BP)** unless noted; note that the calibrated onset (11 500 Cal. yr BP in Roberts; 11 700 cal yr b2k in the later formal GSSP) differs from the older ~10 000 uncalibrated ¹⁴C-year convention (see [[radiocarbon-dating]]).

## Regional Period Systems Recorded Here

- **[[blytt-sernander|Blytt–Sernander scheme]]** (European Holocene): Preboreal · Boreal · Atlantic · Sub-Boreal · Sub-Atlantic — a climato-stratigraphic sequence, now treated as largely obsolete as a *climatic* framework but retained as descriptive shorthand.
- Godwin pollen zones (British Isles): Late-glacial I–III; Holocene IV–VIII.
- ICS formal subdivisions (2018): Greenlandian, Northgrippian, Meghalayan (bounded by the 8.2 ka and 4.2 ka events).

## Chronology

| Date (Cal. yr BP) | Event | Evidence / method |
|---|---|---|
| ~25 000–18 000 | Last Glacial Maximum; sea level >100 m below present; land bridges exposed | [[oxygen-isotope-stratigraphy]], [[ice-core-analysis]] |
| ~18 000 → | Deglaciation begins; ~9000 yr to complete in Europe/N. America | moraine and [[radiocarbon-dating]] |
| 15 000–13 000 | Bølling–Allerød / Windermere interstadial; abrupt warming (~1 °C/decade at Glanllynnau) | [[coleopteran-analysis]], [[pollen-analysis]] |
| 13 000–11 500 | [[younger-dryas|Younger Dryas]] / Loch Lomond stadial; cold reversal; thermohaline shutdown | ice cores, beetles, Barbados sea level |
| **11 500** | **Onset of the Holocene** (Roberts; ±300 yr; multi-proxy convergence) | tree-ring, [[ice-core-analysis|ice-core]], varve, U–Th calibration |
| 11 500–9000 | Protocratic phase: rapid forest re-advance; no-analogue communities | [[pollen-analysis]] |
| ~11 500–5500 | "Green [[desertification|Sahara]]" / African Humid Period; strengthened monsoons | lake levels, fossil fauna |
| 9000–5500 | Holocene thermal maximum ("climatic optimum" / Atlantic period) | thermophilous indicators |
| ~8200 | 8.2 ka abrupt cooling/arid event | Greenland ice, African lake levels |
| ~5800 | European elm decline | fine-resolution [[pollen-analysis]] |
| 6500–4500 | Subtropical desiccation forms the modern Sahara, Arabia, Thar | lake-level records |
| ~5000 (3000 BC) | Mesopotamian hydraulic civilization; earliest cities and writing | archaeology, [[state-formation]] |
| ~2600 | Sub-Boreal → Sub-Atlantic transition (Grenzhorizont) | peat stratigraphy |
| AD 1590–1850 | Little Ice Age (~1 °C cooling; alpine glacier advances) | [[dendrochronology]], documentary, glacial |
| ~AD 1950 | Onset of accelerated [[freshwater-acidification|acidification]]/[[eutrophication]]; ¹³⁷Cs/²¹⁰Pb datum; proposed [[anthropocene|Anthropocene]] marker | [[diatom-analysis]], [[lead-210-dating]] |

## Notes

Radiocarbon dates are calibrated by default; the appendix of Roberts (1998) tabulates the ¹⁴C→calendar conversion. Absolute anchors of highest confidence are [[dendrochronology|tree-ring]], [[ice-core-analysis|ice-core layer-counting]], and [[varve-analysis|varve]] chronologies, which cross-match to within a few centuries across the Pleistocene–Holocene boundary despite the ¹⁴C plateau there.
